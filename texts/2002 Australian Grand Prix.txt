<p>The <b>2002 Australian Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>LXVII Foster's Australian Grand Prix</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 3 March at the <a href="/wiki/Melbourne_Grand_Prix_Circuit" class="mw-redirect" title="Melbourne Grand Prix Circuit">Melbourne Grand Prix Circuit</a>. With 127,000 people in attendance, the race was the first of the <a href="/wiki/2002_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2002 Formula One World Championship">2002 Formula One World Championship</a> and the 18th edition of the race in Formula One. <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> won the 58-lap race from starting in second position. <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a> of <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a> finished second and <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> took third for the <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> team.
</p><p>Ferrari's <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> qualified on <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> by recording the fastest lap in qualifying. He retired at the start of the race, when he braked early for the first corner, catching out Williams driver <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a>, who hit the rear of Barrichello's car. Six drivers were involved in a separate incident. The <a href="/wiki/Safety_car" title="Safety car">safety car</a> was deployed for four laps to clear the track. McLaren's <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> led the first ten laps until an error on lap eleven allowed Michael Schumacher to pass him. Montoya then passed Schumacher for first place at the beginning of lap twelve. He kept the lead until he ran wide and Michael Schumacher overtook him to reclaim it. He led the rest of the race to take his third win in Australia and the 54th of his career.
</p><p>Following this, the first round of the season, Michael Schumacher left Australia leading the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">World Drivers' Championship</a> with ten <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Championship_points_scoring_systems" title="List of Formula One World Championship points scoring systems">points</a>. Montoya was four points behind in second and Räikkönen in third. <a href="/wiki/Jaguar_Racing" title="Jaguar Racing">Jaguar</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a> was fourth with three points and <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> was fifth with two. In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a>, Ferrari led with ten points. Williams and McLaren followed in second and third with sixteen races left in the season.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2002_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:2014_Melbourne_Grand_Prix_(From_Eureka_Skydeck).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2a/2014_Melbourne_Grand_Prix_%28From_Eureka_Skydeck%29.jpg/220px-2014_Melbourne_Grand_Prix_%28From_Eureka_Skydeck%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="131" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2a/2014_Melbourne_Grand_Prix_%28From_Eureka_Skydeck%29.jpg/330px-2014_Melbourne_Grand_Prix_%28From_Eureka_Skydeck%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2a/2014_Melbourne_Grand_Prix_%28From_Eureka_Skydeck%29.jpg/440px-2014_Melbourne_Grand_Prix_%28From_Eureka_Skydeck%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3689" data-file-height="2200" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:2014_Melbourne_Grand_Prix_(From_Eureka_Skydeck).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The <a href="/wiki/Melbourne_Grand_Prix_Circuit" class="mw-redirect" title="Melbourne Grand Prix Circuit">Melbourne Grand Prix Circuit</a> <i>(pictured in 2014)</i>, where the race was held.</div></div></div>
<p>Preparations for the event began in January 2002. At the time, it was only on the provisional calendar of the <a href="/wiki/2002_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2002 Formula One World Championship">2002 Formula One World Championship</a> due to the death of <a href="/wiki/Motorsport_marshal" title="Motorsport marshal">marshal</a> Graham Beveridge in an accident in the <a href="/wiki/2001_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="2001 Australian Grand Prix">2001 race</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-SPJan02_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SPJan02-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Formula One's governing body, the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile</a> (FIA), later confirmed it would proceed as scheduled following the publication of a coroner's report ruling Beveridge's death "avoidable".<sup id="cite_ref-GPGoAhead_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPGoAhead-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> It was the first of 17 races of 2002 and the 18th Formula One World Championship Australian race. It occurred at the 5.303&#160;km (3.295&#160;mi) <a href="/wiki/Melbourne_Grand_Prix_Circuit" class="mw-redirect" title="Melbourne Grand Prix Circuit">Melbourne Grand Prix Circuit</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Melbourne" title="Melbourne">Melbourne</a> suburb of <a href="/wiki/Albert_Park,_Victoria" title="Albert Park, Victoria">Albert Park</a> on 3 March.<sup id="cite_ref-CF1Overview_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CF1Overview-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> To improve safety, the height of the safety fences was raised to 3.8&#160;m (12&#160;ft), cages to safeguard race officials were installed and the size of access openings reduced.<sup id="cite_ref-TrackMods_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TrackMods-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Heading into the <a href="/wiki/2002_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2002 Formula One World Championship">new season</a>, several teams retained the same line-up as they had in <a href="/wiki/2001_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2001 Formula One World Championship">2001</a>, however some teams changed drivers for 2002. The <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a> team was renamed <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>, ending its 16 year involvement in <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> and the Japanese car maker <a href="/wiki/Toyota_Racing_(Formula_One_team)" title="Toyota Racing (Formula One team)">Toyota</a> debuted with drivers <a href="/wiki/Allan_McNish" title="Allan McNish">Allan McNish</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mika_Salo" title="Mika Salo">Mika Salo</a> after it spent 2001 developing the <a href="/wiki/Toyota_TF102" title="Toyota TF102">TF102</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Prost_Grand_Prix" title="Prost Grand Prix">Prost</a> team was liquidated in January 2002 after it was unable to locate money from sponsors or a buyer to remain solvent.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The two-time world champion <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> took a sabbatical and fellow Finn <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> replaced him at <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> whose <a href="/wiki/Sauber_Motorsport" title="Sauber Motorsport">Sauber</a> car was driven by <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a>, the <a href="/wiki/2001_Euro_Formula_3000_Championship" title="2001 Euro Formula 3000 Championship">2001 Euro Formula 3000</a> champion.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> At the <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a> team, the <a href="/wiki/2001_British_Formula_Three_Championship" title="2001 British Formula Three Championship">2001 British Formula Three</a> champion <a href="/wiki/Takuma_Sato" title="Takuma Sato">Takuma Sato</a> paired with <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> whom <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> replaced at Renault.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Former Prost driver <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a> drove <a href="/wiki/Jos_Verstappen" title="Jos Verstappen">Jos Verstappen</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Arrows_Grand_Prix_International" title="Arrows Grand Prix International">Arrows</a> car,<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> left <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a> to become Renault's <a href="/wiki/Test_driver" title="Test driver">test driver</a>; he was replaced by <a href="/wiki/International_Formula_3000" title="International Formula 3000">International Formula 3000</a> competitor <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At the front of the field, the press and bookmakers considered <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> the favourite to take his fifth <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">World Drivers' Championship</a>, with the <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a> predicted to be his main challenger.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher said his objective for the season was to win the championship and equal <a href="/wiki/Juan_Manuel_Fangio" title="Juan Manuel Fangio">Juan Manuel Fangio</a>'s all-time record of five titles, "Our motivation is still unchanged, our target and goals are still the same. We want to once again win both world championships and there is nothing nicer than winning with Ferrari."<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Montoya declared that he was more relaxed than he was in 2001 because he knew his team better, "I think I showed everyone out there that I am competitive and that's the important thing. It was a lot harder to win in Formula One than in <a href="/wiki/Champ_Car" class="mw-redirect" title="Champ Car">CART</a> because it took longer to understand the car, but now that's done I'm looking to do better in 2002."<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Former driver <a href="/wiki/Derek_Warwick" title="Derek Warwick">Derek Warwick</a> believed Michael Schumacher was the best driver in the sport and suspected Montoya was not emotionally mature.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Ferrari brought the <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_F2001" title="Ferrari F2001">F2001</a> in lieu of the <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_F2002" title="Ferrari F2002">F2002</a> to Australia due to performance and reliability issues. According to the Ferrari team principal <a href="/wiki/Jean_Todt" title="Jean Todt">Jean Todt</a>, the F2002 was fast in pre-season testing but the team had not the time available to be certain that it would be reliable in Australia, "We think it will be able to bring home valuable points for the championship. Next week, we will continue our on-track development of the F2002, as well as fine-tuning the F2001 for the first race."<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> During the first two practice sessions, the F2001 was fitted with a <a href="/wiki/Spoiler_(car)" title="Spoiler (car)">rear wing</a> used on the F2002 in testing; both Michael Schumacher and his teammate <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> opted for one seen at the <a href="/wiki/2001_Japanese_Grand_Prix" title="2001 Japanese Grand Prix">2001 Japanese Grand Prix</a>. The <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4-17" title="McLaren MP4-17">McLaren MP4-17</a> car featured an attachment to its lower front <a href="/wiki/Car_suspension" title="Car suspension">suspension</a> frame split to promote airflow under the front wing, and revised ailerons were equipped to the front and rear of the car. The Jordan, Arrows and Sauber teams copied the design.<sup id="cite_ref-Piola_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Piola-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice">Practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2002_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Four practice sessions were held before the Sunday race, two each on Friday and Saturday. The Friday morning and afternoon sessions each lasted an hour; the third and fourth sessions, on Saturday morning, lasted 45 minutes each.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> In the first session, which was held in variable weather leading to the fastest times late on,<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher lapped fastest with a time of 1 minute, 28.804 seconds, 0.363 seconds faster than his teammate Barrichello in second. Fisichella, <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a>, Massa, the Jaguar duo of <a href="/wiki/Pedro_de_la_Rosa" title="Pedro de la Rosa">Pedro de la Rosa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a>, Frentzen, Salo and Renault's <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> rounded out the session's top ten drivers.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Takuma_Sato.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7e/Takuma_Sato.jpg/170px-Takuma_Sato.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="227" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7e/Takuma_Sato.jpg/255px-Takuma_Sato.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7e/Takuma_Sato.jpg/340px-Takuma_Sato.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1704" data-file-height="2272" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Takuma_Sato.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Takuma_Sato" title="Takuma Sato">Takuma Sato</a> <i>(pictured in 2005)</i> crashed in the third practice session and drove the spare <a href="/wiki/Jordan_EJ12" title="Jordan EJ12">Jordan EJ12</a> car for qualifying.</div></div></div>
<p>Michael Schumacher set the day's fastest lap, a 1 minute, 27.276 seconds, in the second session. His teammate Barrichello was 0.523 seconds slower in second place. The two Williams cars of Ralf Schumacher and Montoya, <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> of the Sauber team, Salo, Räikkönen, Massa, Fisichella and Trulli were in positions three to ten.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> During the session, <a href="/wiki/Enrique_Bernoldi" title="Enrique Bernoldi">Enrique Bernoldi</a> stopped his <a href="/wiki/Arrows_A23" title="Arrows A23">Arrows A23</a> car with a broken <a href="/wiki/Transmission_(mechanics)" title="Transmission (mechanics)">gearbox</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello spun through 360 degrees across the grass and reemerged onto the circuit soon after.<sup id="cite_ref-F1NetFP2_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1NetFP2-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Montoya braked too late and returned to the track via an <a href="/wiki/Run-off_area" title="Run-off area">escape road</a> and a gravel trap. A short light rain shower made the circuit slippery and caught out <a href="/wiki/Alex_Yoong" title="Alex Yoong">Alex Yoong</a>; he beached his Minardi car in the turn one gravel trap. An engine failure ended Fisichella's session early and his teammate Sato ran across a gravel trap.<sup id="cite_ref-F1NetFP2_28-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1NetFP2-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The weather for the third session was cool, damp and overcast.<sup id="cite_ref-BBCPractice_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCPractice-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-AF1FP3_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1FP3-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Ferrari continued to be fastest with a revised unofficial lap record of 1 minute, 26.177 seconds from Michael Schumacher. Barrichello was 0.321 seconds slower in second.<sup id="cite_ref-BBCPractice_29-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCPractice-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> McLaren's Coulthard and Räikkönen, Williams' Ralf Schumacher and Montoya, <a href="/wiki/British_American_Racing" title="British American Racing">British American Racing</a> (BAR) driver <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Panis" title="Olivier Panis">Olivier Panis</a>, Sauber teammates Heidfeld and Massa along with Trulli followed in the top ten.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1FP3_30-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1FP3-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> With 13 minutes and 34 seconds to go, Sato lost control of the rear of his <a href="/wiki/Jordan_EJ12" title="Jordan EJ12">Jordan EJ12</a> car on the entry to Stewart turn and crashed into the left-hand side tyre barrier at 160&#160;km/h (99&#160;mph).<sup id="cite_ref-F1NetFP3_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1NetFP3-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-SatoCrash_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SatoCrash-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Sato was unhurt;<sup id="cite_ref-AF1FP3_30-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1FP3-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> practice was stopped for nine minutes to allow marshals to clean the track.<sup id="cite_ref-F1NetFP3_31-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1NetFP3-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Since <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_motorsport_terms#S" title="Glossary of motorsport terms">spare cars</a> could not be used in free practice, Sato missed the fourth session since his car required repairs.<sup id="cite_ref-SatoCrash_32-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SatoCrash-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the fourth session, a brief rain shower dampened the circuit, which prevented an improvement in lap times as some drivers went off the track. Hence, no driver bettered Michael Schumacher's fastest time, as Barrichello in second halved the gap to his teammate. The Williams duo of Montoya and Ralf Schumacher improved to third and fourth and the McLaren pair of Coulthard and Räikkönen fell to fifth and sixth. Heidfeld, the BAR duo of <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a> and Panis and Fisichella were seventh to tenth.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2002_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Barrichello_2002.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/36/Barrichello_2002.jpg/170px-Barrichello_2002.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="298" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/36/Barrichello_2002.jpg/255px-Barrichello_2002.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/36/Barrichello_2002.jpg/340px-Barrichello_2002.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1128" data-file-height="1975" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Barrichello_2002.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> took the fourth pole position of his career.</div></div></div>
<p>Saturday's afternoon one-hour qualifying session saw each competitor limited to twelve laps, with the starting order decided by their fastest laps. During this session, the <a href="/wiki/107%25_rule" title="107% rule">107% rule</a> was in effect, which necessitated each driver set a time within 107 per cent of the quickest lap to qualify for the race.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> The session began on a dry track until a heavy rainstorm fell halfway through, making the circuit slippery and prevented any improvement in lap time.<sup id="cite_ref-RediffQuali_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RediffQuali-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-AutosportQuali_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportQuali-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello completed two laps before it rained (the first was compromised by slower traffic);<sup id="cite_ref-QualiQuotes_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-QualiQuotes-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> he took the fourth <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> of his career and his first since the <a href="/wiki/2000_British_Grand_Prix" title="2000 British Grand Prix">2000 British Grand Prix</a> with a time of 1 minute, 25.843 seconds.<sup id="cite_ref-RediffQuali_34-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RediffQuali-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> He was joined on the <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_motorsport_terms" title="Glossary of motorsport terms">grid</a>'s front row by Michael Schumacher, who made an error on a <a href="/wiki/Curb" title="Curb">kerb</a> in the first third of a lap.<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportQuali_35-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportQuali-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FIAQuali_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAQuali-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Ralf Schumacher, third, remained in the garage for the first three minutes to avoid slower traffic.<sup id="cite_ref-QualiQuotes_36-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-QualiQuotes-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Fourth-placed Coulthard attempted to pass an unsighted Villeneuve on the outside at the final turn and they made contact. Coulthard hit the wall with his right-rear wheel and spun onto a run-off area.<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportQuali_35-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportQuali-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> A error put his teammate Räikkönen fifth.<sup id="cite_ref-FIAQuali_37-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAQuali-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Montoya in sixth lost six-tenths of a second after Fisichella slowed his first timed lap. Trulli in seventh was slowed by a Jaguar car on his first lap. Fisichella in eighth expressed satisfaction with the balance and performance of his car.<sup id="cite_ref-QualiQuotes_36-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-QualiQuotes-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Massa was the highest-placed rookie in ninth after two errors. His teammate Heidfeld qualified tenth.<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportQuali_35-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportQuali-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-QualiQuotes_36-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-QualiQuotes-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Button was the fastest driver not to qualify in the top ten after he was fourth early on. Panis completed one timed lap as he could not extract more performance from his <a href="/wiki/BAR_004" title="BAR 004">BAR 004</a> and took 12th.<sup id="cite_ref-QualiQuotes_36-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-QualiQuotes-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Excess <a href="/wiki/Understeer_and_oversteer" title="Understeer and oversteer">oversteer</a> slowed his teammate Villeneuve in 13th.<sup id="cite_ref-FIAQuali_37-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAQuali-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Salo, 14th, drove cautiously on his first timed lap. A minor <a href="/wiki/Pressure_regulator" title="Pressure regulator">fuel pressure</a> fault limited Frentzen in 15th to one untroubled lap.<sup id="cite_ref-QualiQuotes_36-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-QualiQuotes-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> McNish, 16th, had one set of tyres deducted from his qualifying allocation because Toyota mistakenly used one set assigned for the Friday practice sessions on Saturday. A stoppage caught out Bernoldi at the start of his first timed lap and he qualified 17th. Webber took 18th and used wet-weather tyres.<sup id="cite_ref-FIAQuali_37-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAQuali-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> The two <a href="/wiki/Jaguar_R3" title="Jaguar R3">Jaguar R3</a> cars had a major aerodynamic deficiency and were 19th and 20th:<sup id="cite_ref-STimesQuali_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-STimesQuali-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> A rear brake balance slowed Irvine and a handling deficiency saw de la Rosa drive the spare Jaguar.<sup id="cite_ref-QualiQuotes_36-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-QualiQuotes-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Yoong completed one timed lap for 21st.<sup id="cite_ref-FIAQuali_37-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAQuali-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Five minutes in Sato's spare Jordan EJ12 car stopped before Stewart turn with a hydraulic <a href="/wiki/Clutch" title="Clutch">clutch</a> problem that automatically selected third gear. The session was stopped for eight minutes to allow marshals to move his car. Sato returned to his garage and drove Fisichella's race car but the rain made him more than 107 per cent slower than Barrichello.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-qualifying">Post-qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2002_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Post-qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After qualifying, the Jordan team principal and owner <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Jordan" title="Eddie Jordan">Eddie Jordan</a> appealed to the stewards to allow Sato to participate in the race because he was under the 107 per cent limit during free practice. They permitted him to start under "exceptional circumstances" as in the case of previous cases affected by changeable weather.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_39-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-SunAge107_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SunAge107-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Warm-up">Warm-up</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2002_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Warm-up">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A half-hour warm-up session was held on Sunday morning in variable weather as heavy rain fell before it commenced.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_23-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-F1COMWUP_43-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1COMWUP-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> Teams used rain tyres and <a href="/wiki/Racing_setup" title="Racing setup">set-up their cars</a> against the weather of the time.<sup id="cite_ref-FIAWUP_44-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAWUP-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> With a lap of 1 minute and 41.509 seconds, Michael Schumacher was fastest with teammate Barrichello 1.382 seconds slower in second position. Coulthard, Ralf Schumacher, Montoya, Massa, Heidfeld, Fisichella, Trulli and Irvine made up positions three to ten.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> With 40 seconds to go,<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportWUP_46-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportWUP-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> Salo drove off the <a href="/wiki/Racing_line" title="Racing line">racing line</a> to allow Barrichello past into Ascari corner. He drove onto a white painted line, lost control of his Toyota and crashed into the barrier; Salo's front wing detached heading towards an escape road.<sup id="cite_ref-F1COMWUP_43-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1COMWUP-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FIAWUP_44-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAWUP-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-AutosportWUP_46-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportWUP-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2002_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The weather at the start was dry and overcast with the air temperature between 15 to 18&#160;°C (59 to 64&#160;°F) and the track temperature from 20 to 23&#160;°C (68 to 73&#160;°F).<sup id="cite_ref-F1REvent_47-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1REvent-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ITVRace_48-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ITVRace-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-F1COMRace_49-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1COMRace-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> One-hundred and twenty-seven thousand people attended the event.<sup id="cite_ref-PAReport_50-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PAReport-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> Sato drove the spare Jordan EJ12 car set up for his teammate Fisichella; after warm-up the Jordan team rectified an electrical problem that rendered its clutch inoperable.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> Before the race commenced at 14:00 local time,<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_23-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Frentzen and Bernoldi <a href="/wiki/Stall_(engine)" title="Stall (engine)">stalled</a> their stationary cars and marshals and mechanics extricated them to the pit lane.<sup id="cite_ref-MCOMRace_52-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MCOMRace-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FIARace_53-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIARace-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Ralf_Schumacher_2002.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d5/Ralf_Schumacher_2002.jpg/170px-Ralf_Schumacher_2002.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="248" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/d/d5/Ralf_Schumacher_2002.jpg 1.5x" data-file-width="206" data-file-height="300" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Ralf_Schumacher_2002.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> made contact with the rear of Barrichello's car and retired with heavy car damage at the start of the race.</div></div></div>
<p>Ralf Schumacher used the <a href="/wiki/Grip_(auto_racing)" title="Grip (auto racing)">grip</a> on the outside to pass Michael Schumacher for second position and challenged the heavily fuelled Ferrari of Barrichello for the lead.<sup id="cite_ref-IndyRace_54-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IndyRace-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-CrashRace_55-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CrashRace-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup> As Michael Schumacher turned left to provide himself with the best possible entry for Brabham corner, his teammate Barrichello switched lanes twice to try and block Ralf Schumacher, who responded by turning to the centre in anticipation that momentum would move him into the lead.<sup id="cite_ref-RW4Mar02_56-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RW4Mar02-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello braked early for Brabham turn and caught out Ralf Schumacher,<sup id="cite_ref-CrashRace_55-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CrashRace-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup> who struck the rear of Barrichello's car at about 240&#160;km/h (150&#160;mph).<sup id="cite_ref-ITVRace_48-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ITVRace-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> He launched over the Ferrari,<sup id="cite_ref-FIARace_53-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIARace-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> grazed Barrichello's helmet,<sup id="cite_ref-PAReport_50-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PAReport-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> careened 100&#160;m (330&#160;ft) and rested against the tyre wall.<sup id="cite_ref-GPComRace_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPComRace-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:0_57-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello's rear wing was removed from his car, spinning broadside to a stop and caused an eight-car accident.<sup id="cite_ref-CrashRace_55-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CrashRace-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup> His teammate Michael Schumacher and Räikkönen drove onto the grass to avoid a collision,<sup id="cite_ref-GPComRace_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPComRace-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FIARace_53-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIARace-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> as Fisichella hit the Sauber cars of Heidfeld and Massa, causing Button, Panis and McNish to get caught up in the incident. Villeneuve, Salo, Webber, Irvine, de la Rosa, Yoong and Sato negotiated their way through the crash scene.<sup id="cite_ref-GPComRace_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPComRace-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The drivers involved in the crash returned to the pit lane in anticipation that the race would be stopped and could drive their spare cars for a restart. However, the FIA race director <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Whiting" title="Charlie Whiting">Charlie Whiting</a> did not stop the race and order a restart since no driver was injured. He deployed the <a href="/wiki/Safety_car" title="Safety car">safety car</a> with the damaged cars moved and debris cleared.<sup id="cite_ref-IndyRace_54-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IndyRace-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-IHTRace_58-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IHTRace-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup> This left Coulthard in the lead, followed by Trulli, Montoya, Michael Schumacher, Irvine and de la Rosa.<sup id="cite_ref-F1REvent_47-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1REvent-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> At the conclusion of lap two, Räikkönen entered the pit lane for a 48-second pit stop to remove debris from the rear underbody of his McLaren.<sup id="cite_ref-GPComRace_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPComRace-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FIARace_53-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIARace-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-CrashRace_55-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CrashRace-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup> Webber's <a href="/wiki/Differential_(mechanical_device)" title="Differential (mechanical device)">differential</a> and <a href="/wiki/Traction_control_system" title="Traction control system">traction control</a> began to malfunction on lap three.<sup id="cite_ref-WebberAutoBio_59-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WebberAutoBio-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup> On the same lap, Frentzen ignored a red light instructing drivers to remain in the pit lane until further notice and ventured onto the circuit.<sup id="cite_ref-F1COMRace_49-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1COMRace-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-MCOMRace_52-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MCOMRace-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup> The safety car was withdrawn at the conclusion of the fifth lap and Coulthard led Trulli and Montoya.<sup id="cite_ref-F1REvent_47-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1REvent-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> Going into Whiteford turn,<sup id="cite_ref-ITVRace_48-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ITVRace-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> Montoya drew close to Trulli and slid wide on oil laid on the circuit.<sup id="cite_ref-IndyRace_54-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IndyRace-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-IHTRace_58-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IHTRace-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher used Montoya's error to overtake him for third place.<sup id="cite_ref-SpeedRace_60-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SpeedRace-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup> Further down the field, Räikkönen moved from eleventh to ninth position.<sup id="cite_ref-FIARace_53-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIARace-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Coulthard began to pull away from the rest of the field, increasing his lead to four-and-a-half seconds by lap seven.<sup id="cite_ref-F1REvent_47-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1REvent-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> That same lap, Sato overtook de la Rosa for sixth place, as Webber lost seventh position to Räikkönen.<sup id="cite_ref-FIARace_53-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIARace-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> At the back, Bernoldi rejoined the race after switching from his race car to the spare Arrows vehicle.<sup id="cite_ref-GPComRace_2-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPComRace-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Trulli blocked Michael Schumacher from passing him for second into Brabham corner on the eighth lap, allowing Montoya to gain on Schumacher; he was not close enough to affect a pass.<sup id="cite_ref-ITVRace_48-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ITVRace-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> There were overtakes further down the field: Räikkönen passed de la Rosa and Sato as Villeneuve overtook Webber for ninth.<sup id="cite_ref-FIARace_53-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIARace-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> During lap nine, Trulli lost control of the rear of his Renault on oil at the exit to Jones turn, and broke his suspension in a collision with the inside barrier.<sup id="cite_ref-ITVRace_48-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ITVRace-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-CrashRace_55-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CrashRace-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup> Because Trulli was stopped on the centre of the track,<sup id="cite_ref-F1COMRace_49-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1COMRace-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-BBCRace_61-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCRace-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup> the safety car was deployed for the second time to allow marshals to move his car.<sup id="cite_ref-F1REvent_47-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1REvent-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> When the safety car was withdrawn at the end of the 11th lap, an electrical fault distracted Coulthard, causing him to miss a gear change,<sup id="cite_ref-CrashRace_55-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CrashRace-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-RW4Mar02_56-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RW4Mar02-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> lock his brakes,<sup id="cite_ref-F1COMRace_49-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1COMRace-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Understeer_and_oversteer" title="Understeer and oversteer">understeer</a> wide onto the grass entering Prost turn.<sup id="cite_ref-ITVRace_48-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ITVRace-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> He fell to fifth.<sup id="cite_ref-F1COMRace_49-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1COMRace-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Michael Schumacher moved into the lead with Montoya second and Irvine third.<sup id="cite_ref-BBCRace_61-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCRace-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup> On the approach to Brabham corner Montoya's higher straightline speed allowed him to overtake Michael Schumacher on the outside at the end of the main straight for the lead.<sup id="cite_ref-GPComRace_2-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPComRace-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Montoya then steered onto the inside to maintain the lead from Schumacher at Jones corner.<sup id="cite_ref-CrashRace_55-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CrashRace-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup> By the 13th lap, Michael Schumacher had close to within eight-tenths of a second to affect a pass on Montoya,<sup id="cite_ref-F1COMRace_49-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1COMRace-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> which he was unable to do because of slower traffic.<sup id="cite_ref-ITVRace_48-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ITVRace-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> On lap 14,<sup id="cite_ref-FIARace_53-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIARace-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> Sato retired in the pit lane with an unrectifiable electronics issue that limited his gear selection.<sup id="cite_ref-RaceQuotes_62-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RaceQuotes-62">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher continued to pressure Montoya into an error,<sup id="cite_ref-CrashRace_55-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CrashRace-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup> and caused the latter to drive onto oil at Brabham corner.<sup id="cite_ref-GPComRace_2-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPComRace-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He overtook Montoya on the inside at the exit of Jones turn to take the lead at the start of lap 17.<sup id="cite_ref-ITVRace_48-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ITVRace-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-RW4Mar02_56-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RW4Mar02-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher began to pull away from the rest of the field as Montoya struggled to generate heat in his tyres. On lap 18, de la Rosa fell to eighth after Yoong, Webber and Villeneuve overtook him. On the following lap Frentzen was disqualified for his earlier transgression of ignoring the red light at the exit of the pit lane.<sup id="cite_ref-FIARace_53-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIARace-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>By the 22nd lap Michael Schumacher increased his lead over Montoya to 11.3 seconds with consecutive lap times in the 1 minute and 29 seconds range.<sup id="cite_ref-GPComRace_2-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPComRace-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-SpeedRace_60-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SpeedRace-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup> Räikkönen was eight-tenths of a second behind Montoya.<sup id="cite_ref-F1COMRace_49-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1COMRace-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> On the same lap, Coulthard went off the track at Prost corner. His reduced speed meant Irvine passed him for fourth soon after.<sup id="cite_ref-ITVRace_48-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ITVRace-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> On lap 23 Bernoldi was disqualified from the race because the stewards deemed him to have switched to the spare car after the race began.<sup id="cite_ref-CrashRace_55-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CrashRace-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup> Webber and Villeneuve passed Coulthard for fifth and sixth on laps 25 and 26.<sup id="cite_ref-ITVRace_48-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ITVRace-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FIARace_53-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIARace-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> Villeneuve in sixth had a rear wing failure that sent him into the tyre wall at high speed on the entry to Waite corner and retirement from his 100th race entry on lap 28.<sup id="cite_ref-F1COMRace_49-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1COMRace-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FIARace_53-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIARace-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:0_57-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup> He was unhurt. On lap 29, Yoong overtook Coulthard for sixth position.<sup id="cite_ref-FIARace_53-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIARace-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> Seven laps later, Coulthard became the race's final retirement when he pulled off to the side of track at Whiteford corner because his McLaren was stuck in sixth gear.<sup id="cite_ref-GPComRace_2-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPComRace-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-F1COMRace_49-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1COMRace-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Michael_Schumacher-I%27m_the_man_(cropped).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/Michael_Schumacher-I%27m_the_man_%28cropped%29.jpg/170px-Michael_Schumacher-I%27m_the_man_%28cropped%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="230" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/Michael_Schumacher-I%27m_the_man_%28cropped%29.jpg/255px-Michael_Schumacher-I%27m_the_man_%28cropped%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/Michael_Schumacher-I%27m_the_man_%28cropped%29.jpg/340px-Michael_Schumacher-I%27m_the_man_%28cropped%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1260" data-file-height="1704" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Michael_Schumacher-I%27m_the_man_(cropped).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> <i>(pictured in 2005)</i> took his third win at the Australian Grand Prix and the 54th of his career.</div></div></div>
<p>Webber was the first of the top six drivers to make a pit stop on lap 37.<sup id="cite_ref-ITVRace_48-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ITVRace-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> He had a problematic 34.9 second pit stop; the Minardi's fuel cover did not open automatically and a mechanic used a screwdriver to unsecure it. Webber rejoined the track in sixth position.<sup id="cite_ref-Webber5Mar02_63-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Webber5Mar02-63">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup> Montoya and Michael Schumacher made their pit stops on laps 37 and 38.<sup id="cite_ref-BBCRace_61-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCRace-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup> In the meantime, Räikkönen recorded the race's fastest lap of 1 minute, 25.841 seconds on lap 37,<sup id="cite_ref-GPComRace_2-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPComRace-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> as he sought to pass Montoya for second position after his pit stop.<sup id="cite_ref-CrashRace_55-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CrashRace-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup> He made his stop on lap 38.<sup id="cite_ref-FIARace_53-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIARace-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> As Räikkönen drove onto the track, he drew alongside Montoya and carried excess speed into Brabham corner.<sup id="cite_ref-CrashRace_55-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CrashRace-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup> He understeered wide onto the grass as he regained control of the rear of his car and fell to third, behind Montoya.<sup id="cite_ref-F1REvent_47-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1REvent-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ITVRace_48-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ITVRace-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>With the first four positions settled, attention switched to the battle for fifth between Webber and Salo.<sup id="cite_ref-FIARace_53-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIARace-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> Webber <a href="/wiki/Short_shifting" title="Short shifting">short shifted</a> to prevent unneeded car component stress and noticed Salo closing up. He went faster and the Minardi team owner <a href="/wiki/Paul_Stoddart" title="Paul Stoddart">Paul Stoddart</a> radioed that he had to defend fifth from Salo. Webber was aware of a potential revenue bonus of $25 million for Minardi if they finished ahead of Toyota in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">World Constructors' Championship</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-WebberAutoBio_59-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WebberAutoBio-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup> Salo had been aware of the distance between himself and Webber and closed up to the latter by the 57th lap.<sup id="cite_ref-CrashRace_55-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CrashRace-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup> He attempted to pass Webber; the aerodynamic <a href="/wiki/Turbulence" title="Turbulence">turbulence</a> created of airflow over Webber's car and the latter's block caused Salo to spin through 180 degrees on radiator coolant from Button's car at Whiteford turn. Salo was able to restart his car and continue in sixth.<sup id="cite_ref-F1REvent_47-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1REvent-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ITVRace_48-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ITVRace-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:2_64-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-64">&#91;64&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Michael Schumacher led the final 39 laps to take his third successive Australian Grand Prix victory and the 54th of his career.<sup id="cite_ref-IHTRace_58-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IHTRace-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup> Montoya was 18.628 seconds behind in second position. He was a further 6.4 seconds ahead of Räikkönen,<sup id="cite_ref-GPComRace_2-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPComRace-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> who took the first podium finish of his career in third.<sup id="cite_ref-IHTRace_58-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IHTRace-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup> Irvine and Webber finished fourth and fifth after starting from 19th and 18th respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-ITVRace_48-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ITVRace-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-IHTRace_58-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IHTRace-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup> Salo took the final point in sixth place, the first time a team scored points in its debut race since <a href="/wiki/JJ_Lehto" title="JJ Lehto">JJ Lehto</a> finished fifth for Sauber at the <a href="/wiki/1993_South_African_Grand_Prix" title="1993 South African Grand Prix">1993 South African Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65">&#91;65&#93;</a></sup> Yoong came seventh with a "long" brake pedal and a car optimised for wet-weather;<sup id="cite_ref-RaceQuotes_62-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RaceQuotes-62">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup> marshals mistakenly waved blue flags at Yoong because they did not know which lap he was on.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_64-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-64">&#91;64&#93;</a></sup> De la Rosa was the final classified finisher after a misfiring engine required him to undergo a lengthy pit stop. The attrition rate was high, with eight of the twenty-two starters finishing the race.<sup id="cite_ref-GPComRace_2-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPComRace-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:0_57-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2002_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The top three drivers appeared on the podium to collect their trophies and spoke to the media in a later press conference.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_23-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher called his battle with Montoya "an interesting one" and "a bit back and forward", adding "I think as well that the tyres played a little bit of a role in that; initially I struggled to get the temperature in where these guys seemed to get faster on top of temperatures but then it went the other way around, their tyres went off and my one came in so I had a nice chance to battle a little bit and finally got first position for us, which was ideal."<sup id="cite_ref-PressConf_66-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PressConf-66">&#91;66&#93;</a></sup> Montoya said he enjoyed the battle and stated Ferrari had the fastest car, "I think the conditions were not the best for the tyres. Hopefully in Malaysia it is going to be hotter, it could play into our hands a little bit."<sup id="cite_ref-PressConf_66-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PressConf-66">&#91;66&#93;</a></sup> Räikkönen said he was surprised to finish third and talked about the ease of overtaking other cars, "Some cars was more difficult and then it was helping me a lot big time because the safety car came out second time and I got behind the leaders and that was the main reason that I catch them, but it was quite a difficult and interesting race."<sup id="cite_ref-PressConf_66-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PressConf-66">&#91;66&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Mark_Webber_2010_Japan.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8d/Mark_Webber_2010_Japan.jpg/170px-Mark_Webber_2010_Japan.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="208" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8d/Mark_Webber_2010_Japan.jpg/255px-Mark_Webber_2010_Japan.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8d/Mark_Webber_2010_Japan.jpg/340px-Mark_Webber_2010_Japan.jpg 2x" data-file-width="800" data-file-height="978" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Mark_Webber_2010_Japan.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> <i>(pictured in 2010)</i> finished fifth and was given an impromptu podium ceremony after the race.</div></div></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Ron_Walker_(Australian_businessman)" title="Ron Walker (Australian businessman)">Ron Walker</a>, the chairman of the Australian Grand Prix Corporation, persuaded Webber and Stoddart to celebrate their fifth-place result with an impromptu ceremony on the podium, which led to a fine of £50,000 from the FIA president <a href="/wiki/Max_Mosley" title="Max Mosley">Max Mosley</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-AusMar12_67-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AusMar12-67">&#91;67&#93;</a></sup> In a retrospective interview for <i><a href="/wiki/The_Australian" title="The Australian">The Weekend Australian</a></i> in 2012, Stoddart called Webber's fifth-place finish "the most exciting two points in the history of Formula One".<sup id="cite_ref-AusMar12_67-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AusMar12-67">&#91;67&#93;</a></sup> Webber commented on the result, "Finishing the race fifth was unbelievable. We had people scaling catch fencing. Occupational health and safety would have gone ballistic these days. It was a unique day."<sup id="cite_ref-AusMar12_67-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AusMar12-67">&#91;67&#93;</a></sup> The result saw Webber's three-race contract with Minardi extended to the end of the season.<sup id="cite_ref-AusMar12_67-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AusMar12-67">&#91;67&#93;</a></sup> The media compared Webber's achievement to the ice skater <a href="/wiki/Steven_Bradbury" title="Steven Bradbury">Steven Bradbury</a> winning a <a href="/wiki/Gold_medal" title="Gold medal">gold medal</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Short_track_speed_skating_at_the_2002_Winter_Olympics_%E2%80%93_Men%27s_1000_metres" title="Short track speed skating at the 2002 Winter Olympics – Men&#39;s 1000 metres">men's 1000 metres short track speed skating</a> at the <a href="/wiki/2002_Winter_Olympics" title="2002 Winter Olympics">2002 Winter Olympics</a>; both men were successful after several participants crashed in their respective events.<sup id="cite_ref-Webber5Mar02_63-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Webber5Mar02-63">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-68" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-68">&#91;68&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Following their collision, their third in the past two seasons, Barrichello and Ralf Schumacher were summoned to meet the stewards, who reviewed television footage post-race. Neither driver received a penalty and were given a warning.<sup id="cite_ref-69" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-69">&#91;69&#93;</a></sup> The stewards classed the crash as "a racing incident", with neither competitor to blame.<sup id="cite_ref-PAReport_50-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PAReport-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> Ralf Schumacher argued Barrichello braked earlier than normal, saying "I was afraid to turn into the first corner because I suddenly saw cars flying next to me. I decided to go straight on and have a nice ride through the grass, which was a good decision, otherwise I guess I would have been hit."<sup id="cite_ref-PAReport_50-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PAReport-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello said he was not to blame for the accident, "If he wanted to overtake on the outside, he should have moved a lot further. I didn't get in his way."<sup id="cite_ref-PAReport_50-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PAReport-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Jackie_Stewart" title="Jackie Stewart">Jackie Stewart</a>, the three-time world champion, argued Ralf Schumacher had misjudged the braking distance for turn one,<sup id="cite_ref-70" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-70">&#91;70&#93;</a></sup> while Coulthard stated his belief Barrichello caused the accident by braking earlier than usual.<sup id="cite_ref-71" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-71">&#91;71&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Whiting's decision not to stop the race after the eight-car crash was criticised. The technical director of the Jordan team <a href="/wiki/Gary_Anderson_(motorsport)" title="Gary Anderson (motorsport)">Gary Anderson</a> called it "the most absurd thing I've seen in my life".<sup id="cite_ref-IndyRace_54-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IndyRace-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup> Fisichella believing not stopping the race  was "ridiculous",<sup id="cite_ref-RaceQuotes_62-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RaceQuotes-62">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup> and Michael Schumacher agreed it should have been stopped.<sup id="cite_ref-PressConf_66-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PressConf-66">&#91;66&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard defended the decision, saying "I've always felt that to deprive the spectators of a number of cars as a result of an incident at the first corner isn't really good for the business. But this isn't <a href="/wiki/Cinema_of_the_United_States" title="Cinema of the United States">Hollywood</a>. You don't cut out the bits you don't like. This is real. This is racing."<sup id="cite_ref-RW4Mar02_56-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RW4Mar02-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> Ralf Schumacher also backed Whiting's ruling, noting "Charlie Whiting took the right decision by not stopping the race. He had made it quite clear to us that he would not stop a race unless it was for safety reasons and that wasn't the case."<sup id="cite_ref-72" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-72">&#91;72&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Because this was the first race of the season, Michael Schumacher led the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">World Drivers' Championship</a> with ten <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Championship_points_scoring_systems" title="List of Formula One World Championship points scoring systems">points</a>. Montoya was second with six points and Räikkönen was third with four. Irvine was fourth with three points and Webber was fifth with two points.<sup id="cite_ref-GPComRace_2-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPComRace-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Ferrari took the lead of the World Constructors' Championship with ten points. Williams were second with six points and McLaren followed in third with four points. With three points, Jaguar were fourth and Minardi fifth with two points with sixteen races left in the season.<sup id="cite_ref-GPComRace_2-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPComRace-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
