<p>The <b>2005 San Marino Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>XXV Gran Premio Foster's di San Marino</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 24 April 2005 at the <a href="/wiki/Autodromo_Enzo_e_Dino_Ferrari" class="mw-redirect" title="Autodromo Enzo e Dino Ferrari">Autodromo Enzo e Dino Ferrari</a> in <a href="/wiki/Imola" title="Imola">Imola</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>. The 62-lap race was the fourth round of the <a href="/wiki/2005_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2005 Formula One season">2005 Formula One season</a>, and the 25th running of the <a href="/wiki/San_Marino_Grand_Prix" title="San Marino Grand Prix">San Marino Grand Prix</a>.
</p><p>The race was won by <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>, who extended his lead in the Drivers' Championship after his third win and fourth podium from four races. <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> finished the race in second position for the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> team, only two-tenths of a second behind Alonso having challenged him for the win late into the race. <a href="/wiki/British_American_Racing" title="British American Racing">BAR</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> crossed the line in third place, but his team were subsequently disqualified for underweight cars; third place was then awarded to <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Wurz" title="Alexander Wurz">Alexander Wurz</a>.
</p><p>Pole position was taken by McLaren driver <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a>, using the aggregate system which was in the rules at the start of the 2005 season. He led the race from the start until his retirement on lap 9 due to a driveshaft failure. Alonso took the lead and kept it except during the pit stop phases when it was held by Button, and later Schumacher when he overtook Button.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2005_San_Marino_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2005_San_Marino_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Vitantonio_Liuzzi" title="Vitantonio Liuzzi">Vitantonio Liuzzi</a> replaced <a href="/wiki/Christian_Klien" title="Christian Klien">Christian Klien</a> in the second <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a> car, for this and the next two races; after unanimous agreement between the teams, Klien was allowed to race car #37 for Red Bull in the Friday practice sessions. <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Wurz" title="Alexander Wurz">Alexander Wurz</a> drove the second <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_HighPerformanceEngines" class="mw-redirect" title="Mercedes-Benz HighPerformanceEngines">Mercedes</a> car, in place of the injured <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a>. During the first qualifying session on Saturday, Red Bull announced that they would use <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> engines for two years, beginning from the <a href="/wiki/2006_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2006 Formula One season">2006 season</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2005_San_Marino_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Räikkönen led from pole, pulling out a gap of several seconds, before his McLaren retired on lap nine with driveshaft problems. Alonso took over the lead, and was unchallenged until lap 50, when Michael Schumacher emerged from the pits just behind him. Schumacher had started 13th, and had been unable to pass <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> for 20 laps, until the Italian pitted allowing Schumacher to increase his pace. After pitting himself, he emerged in third place ahead of Trulli and started to catch race leader Alonso. He caught up with second place driver Jenson Button and overtook him, overturning a 20-second gap in 13 laps. After the second round of pit-stops he emerged from the pits seconds behind Alonso. The next 12 laps saw the two battle for the lead but Schumacher was unable to overtake Alonso who took the race victory. Third was Button, followed by Wurz's McLaren, <a href="/wiki/Takuma_Sato" title="Takuma Sato">Takuma Sato</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a>, Trulli and <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a>. Ralf was later given a 25-second penalty as he was released into the path of <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a>; this temporarily dropped him to 11th place.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During checks after the race it was found that Button's car had been under the 600&#160;kg minimum weight requirement when drained of fuel. The race stewards cleared Button, as they believed data provided by BAR-Honda was sufficient to prove that they had been operating inside the rules, but the FIA appealed sending them to court.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> They were found guilty, but the FIA's preferred penalty of having the team disqualified from the championship for the year was not carried through, and they were given a two-race ban, starting from the next round in Spain. In addition, Button's teammate Sato, who had finished 5th on the track, was disqualified from the race despite his car not being found to be underweight.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Wurz was thus promoted to third, followed by Villeneuve, Trulli, Heidfeld, <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> and Liuzzi.
</p><p>British broadcaster <a href="/wiki/ITV_(TV_network)" title="ITV (TV network)">ITV</a> attracted controversy when it cut to a commercial break during the closing moments of the race which garnered 126 complaints from viewers. Media regulator <a href="/wiki/Ofcom" title="Ofcom">Ofcom</a> ruled that ITV breached section 6.7 of the Rules on the Amount and Scheduling of Advertising.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The broadcasters also had not shown the post-race press conference segment featuring <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a>'s comments about the race.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> ITV repeated the last three laps after the race and as a result of these complaints, the server on their website crashed. An on-air apology was made by presenter Jim Rosenthal before the start of the <a href="/wiki/2005_Spanish_Grand_Prix" title="2005 Spanish Grand Prix">next race in Spain</a> two weeks later.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
