<p>The <b>1962 French Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held at <a href="/wiki/Rouen-Les-Essarts" title="Rouen-Les-Essarts">Rouen-Les-Essarts</a> on 8 July 1962. It was race 4 of 9 in both the <a href="/wiki/1962_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1962 World Championship of Drivers">1962 World Championship of Drivers</a> and the <a href="/wiki/1962_International_Cup_for_Formula_One_Manufacturers" class="mw-redirect" title="1962 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers">1962 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers</a>. The race was won by <a href="/wiki/Dan_Gurney" title="Dan Gurney">Dan Gurney</a>, his first Formula One victory, driving a <a href="/wiki/Porsche_in_Formula_One" class="mw-redirect" title="Porsche in Formula One">Porsche</a>, that company's only win as a constructor in a Formula One championship race coming after three years of racing. It was the third time that the French Grand Prix was held at Rouen, last time being <a href="/wiki/1957_French_Grand_Prix" title="1957 French Grand Prix">1957</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-IMS962_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IMS962-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1962_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Phil_Hill" title="Phil Hill">Phil Hill</a>, running second in the championship, was in the stands with a camera around his neck; a metal workers' strike in Italy meant that <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> could not take part. This left <a href="/wiki/Graham_Hill" title="Graham Hill">Graham Hill</a> the fastest on track, taking the lead at the start and also setting a new lap record. The leading pack also included <a href="/wiki/John_Surtees" title="John Surtees">John Surtees</a> (Lola), <a href="/wiki/Jim_Clark" title="Jim Clark">Jim Clark</a> (Lotus), and <a href="/wiki/Bruce_McLaren" title="Bruce McLaren">Bruce McLaren</a> (<a href="/wiki/Cooper_Car_Company" title="Cooper Car Company">Cooper</a>). These four pulled steadily away from <a href="/wiki/Jack_Brabham" title="Jack Brabham">Jack Brabham</a> (Lotus) and <a href="/wiki/Dan_Gurney" title="Dan Gurney">Dan Gurney</a> (Porsche). After only nine laps Brabham was out of the race with a broken rear suspension, while McLaren lost fourth gear and spun off the track, rejoining the race far down the field. Surtees had to stop four laps later with ignition problems, but was back on track in eighth place.<sup id="cite_ref-IMS962_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IMS962-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Hill had pulled out a twenty-second lead ahead of Clark, but on the thirtieth lap he made contact with <a href="/wiki/Jackie_Lewis" title="Jackie Lewis">Jackie Lewis</a>' Cooper when lapping him, allowing Clark to pass. Hill gave chase, netting the lap record, and re-took the lead on lap 33. On the next lap, Clark had to stop with a broken front suspension. Hill and BRM looked sure to win, but on lap 42 he stopped in a hairpin with fuel injection and throttle linkage troubles; he lost several laps and ended up dead last after also having stopped in the hairpin to pick up the engine cover that he had left behind during his earlier visit there.<sup id="cite_ref-IMS962_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IMS962-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>All of a sudden Gurney found himself in the lead, he made no mistakes and gained what was to be Porsche's only Grand Prix victory with their own car. <a href="/wiki/Tony_Maggs" title="Tony Maggs">Tony Maggs</a> had second place handed to him in what was only his second race in a V8 Cooper, while Surtees' Lola kept giving him trouble. With only fourth gear left, he was passed by <a href="/wiki/Richie_Ginther" title="Richie Ginther">Richie Ginther</a> on the 43rd lap.<sup id="cite_ref-IMS16_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IMS16-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Ginther's car had refused to start, setting him a half lap back at the start, but as cars ahead of him all began to break he drove harder and harder. His troubles were not over, however, with five laps left his throttle wire snapped and he had to control it with his hand - rather troublesome since his BRM's gear shifter was also on the right hand side.<sup id="cite_ref-IMS14_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IMS14-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> McLaren was forced to make another pit stop, but managed to get by the troubled Surtees on the very last lap. The last points scoring position went to the ever steady <a href="/wiki/Carel_Godin_de_Beaufort" title="Carel Godin de Beaufort">Carel Godin de Beaufort</a> in his uncompetitive <a href="/wiki/Porsche_718" title="Porsche 718">Porsche 718</a> from 1961.<sup id="cite_ref-IMS16_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IMS16-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Other contenders were <a href="/wiki/Jo_Bonnier" title="Jo Bonnier">Jo Bonnier</a>, who had gearbox and engine troubles. Later he stopped with fuel starvation, but got the car started and was classified as the last finisher, in tenth. <a href="/wiki/Maurice_Trintignant" title="Maurice Trintignant">Maurice Trintignant</a> had to make a number of pit stops in his <a href="/wiki/Rob_Walker_Racing_Team" title="Rob Walker Racing Team">Rob Walker</a> <a href="/wiki/Lotus_24" title="Lotus 24">Lotus 24</a>. <a href="/wiki/Trevor_Taylor_(racing_driver)" title="Trevor Taylor (racing driver)">Trevor Taylor</a> had not been able to practice and was taking it comparatively easy when his throttle return spring broke, leaving it fully open. After a pit stop, he finished eighth, six laps down.<sup id="cite_ref-IMS16_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IMS16-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> A rather confusing race ended even worse - Surtees was trying to eke his crippled car into the pits but was hindered by a wall of <a href="/wiki/National_Gendarmerie" title="National Gendarmerie">gendarmes</a>, who were refusing to move. Trintignant moved over to the left, but Taylor arrived at a high speed and rear ended Trintignant. Both cars ended up in the haybales, badly damaged, but amazingly no one was injured.<sup id="cite_ref-IMS17_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IMS17-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At the end, Phil Hill took his countryman Gurney aside and thanked him for "driving so well for him", as both of his closest competitors ended the race without any points.<sup id="cite_ref-IMS17_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IMS17-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
