<p>The <b>1960 French Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held at <a href="/wiki/Reims-Gueux" title="Reims-Gueux">Reims-Gueux</a> on 3 July 1960. It was race 6 of 10 in the <a href="/wiki/1960_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1960 World Championship of Drivers">1960 World Championship of Drivers</a> and race 5 of 9 in the <a href="/wiki/1960_International_Cup_for_Formula_One_Manufacturers" class="mw-redirect" title="1960 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers">1960 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers</a>.
</p><p>The 50-lap race was won from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> by Australian driver <a href="/wiki/Jack_Brabham" title="Jack Brabham">Jack Brabham</a>, driving a works <a href="/wiki/Cooper_Car_Company" title="Cooper Car Company">Cooper</a>-<a href="/wiki/Coventry_Climax" title="Coventry Climax">Climax</a>. Belgian driver <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Gendebien" title="Olivier Gendebien">Olivier Gendebien</a> finished second in a Cooper-Climax entered by the <a href="/wiki/British_Racing_Partnership" title="British Racing Partnership">British Racing Partnership</a>, while New Zealander <a href="/wiki/Bruce_McLaren" title="Bruce McLaren">Bruce McLaren</a> was third in the other works Cooper-Climax.
</p>
