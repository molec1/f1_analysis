<p>The <b>1985 Austrian Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held at <a href="/wiki/%C3%96sterreichring" class="mw-redirect" title="Österreichring">Österreichring</a> on 18 August 1985. It was the tenth race of the <a href="/wiki/1985_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1985 Formula One World Championship">1985 Formula One World Championship</a>. It was the 25th Austrian Grand Prix and the 24th to be held at Österreichring. The race was run over 52 laps of the 5.94-kilometre (3.69&#160;mi) circuit for a total race distance of 308.9 kilometres (191.9&#160;mi).
</p><p>The race was won by Frenchman <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/2" title="McLaren MP4/2">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Techniques_d%27Avant_Garde" title="Techniques d&#39;Avant Garde">TAG</a>, after he started from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>. Prost took his fourth victory of the season by 30 seconds from Brazilian <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> in a <a href="/wiki/Lotus_97T" title="Lotus 97T">Lotus</a>-<a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>, with Italian <a href="/wiki/Michele_Alboreto" title="Michele Alboreto">Michele Alboreto</a> third in a <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_156/85" title="Ferrari 156/85">Ferrari</a>. With the win, Prost moved level on points with Alboreto at the top of the Drivers' Championship.
</p><p>This was the last F1 race until the start of <a href="/wiki/1987_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1987 Formula One World Championship">1987</a> in which a car with a <a href="/wiki/Naturally_aspirated_engine" title="Naturally aspirated engine">naturally aspirated engine</a> was entered, <a href="/wiki/Martin_Brundle" title="Martin Brundle">Martin Brundle</a> failing to qualify his <a href="/wiki/Cosworth_DFV" title="Cosworth DFV">Cosworth</a>-powered <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_012" title="Tyrrell 012">Tyrrell</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_summary">Race summary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1985_Austrian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Race summary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Missing from the grid was <a href="/wiki/RAM_Racing" title="RAM Racing">RAM</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Manfred_Winkelhock" title="Manfred Winkelhock">Manfred Winkelhock</a> who had been killed in a sportscar race in <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> just a week before. His place was taken in the team by <a href="/wiki/Kenny_Acheson" title="Kenny Acheson">Kenny Acheson</a> for his first Formula One race since the <a href="/wiki/1983_South_African_Grand_Prix" title="1983 South African Grand Prix">1983 South African Grand Prix</a>.
</p><p>A second <a href="/wiki/Toleman" title="Toleman">Toleman</a> was driven by <a href="/wiki/Piercarlo_Ghinzani" title="Piercarlo Ghinzani">Piercarlo Ghinzani</a>.
</p><p>Before Saturday morning practice triple and defending World Champion (and <a href="/wiki/1984_Austrian_Grand_Prix" title="1984 Austrian Grand Prix">1984 Austrian Grand Prix</a> winner) <a href="/wiki/Niki_Lauda" title="Niki Lauda">Niki Lauda</a>, flanked by a very unhappy <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> team boss <a href="/wiki/Ron_Dennis" title="Ron Dennis">Ron Dennis</a>, announced to the media that he would be retiring for good from Formula One following the season ending <a href="/wiki/1985_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="1985 Australian Grand Prix">Australian Grand Prix</a> to concentrate on running his airline <a href="/wiki/Lauda_Air" title="Lauda Air">Lauda Air</a>. Dennis was reportedly unhappy as he had paid Lauda a considerable amount of money to make his F1 comeback in <a href="/wiki/1982_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1982 Formula One World Championship">1982</a> and he had unsuccessfully tried to get Lauda to continue racing into the <a href="/wiki/1986_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1986 Formula One World Championship">1986</a> season.
</p><p>A now relaxed Lauda gave his home fans something to cheer about when he qualified a season-high third. Lauda's team mate <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> captured pole position, averaging 155.478&#160;mph (250.219&#160;km/h), followed by <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> (<a href="/wiki/Williams_FW10" title="Williams FW10">Williams</a>-<a href="/wiki/Honda_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Honda F1">Honda</a>), Lauda, <a href="/wiki/Keke_Rosberg" title="Keke Rosberg">Keke Rosberg</a> (Williams) and <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a> (<a href="/wiki/Brabham_BT54" title="Brabham BT54">Brabham</a>-<a href="/wiki/BMW_M12" title="BMW M12">BMW</a>). After a troubled qualifying, Senna only qualified 14th on the grid in his <a href="/wiki/Lotus_97T" title="Lotus 97T">Lotus</a>-<a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a>.
</p><p>The race was restarted after one lap (with Niki Lauda having made a great start from third on the grid to lead Prost as the race was stopped). Mansell had got away very slowly in his Williams, but behind him <a href="/wiki/Teo_Fabi" title="Teo Fabi">Teo Fabi</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Toleman_TG185" title="Toleman TG185">Toleman</a>-<a href="/wiki/Brian_Hart_Ltd." class="mw-redirect" title="Brian Hart Ltd.">Hart</a> barely moved. <a href="/wiki/Elio_de_Angelis" title="Elio de Angelis">Elio de Angelis</a> (Lotus) dived left to avoid Fabi and was hit by the <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_156/85" title="Ferrari 156/85">Ferrari</a> of Alboreto. Fabi suffered damage as did the <a href="/wiki/Arrows_A8" title="Arrows A8">Arrows</a>-<a href="/wiki/BMW_M12" title="BMW M12">BMW</a> of Austria's "other" driver in the race <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a>. Luckily for those with damaged cars (especially championship leader Alboreto) the first lap was declared null and void and the race was completely restarted meaning those with damaged cars were permitted to start in the team spares. Lucky too was Prost who was able to change cars after his McLaren had developed a misfire. <a href="/wiki/Piercarlo_Ghinzani" title="Piercarlo Ghinzani">Piercarlo Ghinzani</a> became a non-starter in his Toleman after team mate Fabi took over the spare TG185 for the race as he had qualified 6th while Ghinzani started 19th. This left Ghinzani without a drive.
</p><p>On lap 13 <a href="/wiki/Andrea_de_Cesaris" title="Andrea de Cesaris">Andrea de Cesaris</a> survived one of the biggest crashes ever seen in Formula One when his <a href="/wiki/Ligier_JS25" title="Ligier JS25">Ligier</a>-Renault got sideways at the left hand Panorama Curve and slid onto the outside grass at high speed. The grass, wet from overnight rain caused the Ligier to initially slide sideways before his right rear hit a slight bank launching the car into a series of rolls with de Cesaris's head bouncing around freely in the cockpit. Somehow as soon as the Ligier came to a rest (thankfully the right way up), de Cesaris undid his seat belts and walked away with nothing more than a mud-splattered helmet and driving suit. The crash was the end for de Cesaris at Ligier, with team owner <a href="/wiki/Guy_Ligier" title="Guy Ligier">Guy Ligier</a> firing the Italian after he saw a replay of the crash stating "I can no longer afford to keep employing this man" referring to the constant repair bills from de Cesaris's crashes since he joined the team in <a href="/wiki/1984_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1984 Formula One World Championship">1984</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> When he returned to the pits, de Cesaris told the team that the reason for his DNF was that the car had stalled and wouldn't restart. It was reported at that point that the Ligier team had not seen a replay of the accident.
</p><p>With his 20th career victory, Prost moved into a shared lead in the World Drivers' Championship alongside Alboreto, with each having 50 points. After a string of non-finishes since his win in the second race of the season in <a href="/wiki/1985_Portuguese_Grand_Prix" title="1985 Portuguese Grand Prix">Portugal</a>, Senna drove a great race into second from a lowly (for him) 14th on the grid, with Alboreto finishing third in the spare Ferrari to retain his lead in the World Championship (now shared with Prost). <a href="/wiki/Stefan_Johansson" title="Stefan Johansson">Stefan Johansson</a> (Ferrari), Elio de Angelis, and <a href="/wiki/Marc_Surer" title="Marc Surer">Marc Surer</a> (Brabham), completed the points-scoring finishers.
</p>
