<p>The <b>2008 Belgian Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>LXIV ING Belgian Grand Prix</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 7 September 2008 at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Spa-Francorchamps" title="Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps">Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps</a> near the town of <a href="/wiki/Spa,_Belgium" title="Spa, Belgium">Spa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a>. It was the 13th race of the <a href="/wiki/2008_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2008 Formula One season">2008 Formula One season</a>. The 44-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> team, after the initial winner, <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>, was penalised for cutting a <a href="/wiki/Chicane" title="Chicane">chicane</a> and gaining an advantage over Ferrari's <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a>.
</p><p>Hamilton started from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> alongside title rival Massa. Hamilton's McLaren teammate <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> started from third next to the <a href="/wiki/2007_Belgian_Grand_Prix" title="2007 Belgian Grand Prix">2007</a> winner Kimi Räikkönen. Following a spin by Hamilton on the second lap, Räikkönen led most of the race, until rain fell on lap&#160;41 and Hamilton performed the penalised pass. Räikkönen crashed on the following lap as the rain became heavier. Massa finished second on the road after Hamilton, followed by <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> of <a href="/wiki/BMW_in_Formula_One" title="BMW in Formula One">BMW Sauber</a>.
</p><p>Hamilton received a 25-second penalty, which demoted him to third place and advanced Massa and Heidfeld to first and second positions. McLaren appealed the decision at the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile</a> (FIA) International Court of Appeal. Their case, however, was declared inadmissible, with the Court ruling that 25 second penalties cannot be challenged. The penalty prompted global press discussion, primarily from the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> and <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>, with several former drivers questioning the decision. Massa's retroactive victory, with Hamilton demoted to third, narrowed the gap in the Championship from six points to just two.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2008_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/2008_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2008 Formula One season">2008 Formula One season</a></div>
<p>Heading into the 13th race of the season, <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> was leading the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a> with 70&#160;<a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Championship_points_scoring_systems" title="List of Formula One World Championship points scoring systems">points</a>; <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> was in second place with 64&#160;points. With 57&#160;points, Ferrari's <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> was third, followed by <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a> with 55&#160;points. In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a>, Ferrari led 121&#160;points to McLaren's 113.<sup id="cite_ref-autosport1_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosport1-2">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Following the <a href="/wiki/2008_European_Grand_Prix" title="2008 European Grand Prix">2008 European Grand Prix</a> on 24 August, mid-season testing took place at Italy's <a href="/wiki/Autodromo_Nazionale_Monza" class="mw-redirect" title="Autodromo Nazionale Monza">Autodromo Nazionale Monza</a>. Ferrari concentrated on their <a href="/wiki/F1_car#Aerodynamics" class="mw-redirect" title="F1 car">aerodynamic set-up</a> during tests on Monza's long straights. Massa set the quickest times on the first day, ahead of McLaren driver <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/BMW_in_Formula_One" title="BMW in Formula One">BMW Sauber</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> was quickest on the second day. Massa spun off into the gravel at the Ascari chicane, bringing a brief halt to testing.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton was fastest on the third day.<sup id="cite_ref-monzatest3_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-monzatest3-5">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
Kubica lost control of his car and drove off the track, limiting BMW's testing time as minor repairs were performed on the underside of his car.<sup id="cite_ref-monzatest3_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-monzatest3-5">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice">Practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2008_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Three practice sessions were held before the Sunday race &#8211; two on Friday, and one on Saturday. The Friday morning and afternoon sessions each lasted 90&#160;minutes; the third session, on Saturday morning, lasted for an hour.<sup id="cite_ref-sporting_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sporting-6">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The first session was held on a dry track under cloudy skies; Massa was fastest with a time of 1:47.284, just quicker than his Ferrari teammate Räikkönen. The McLarens of Hamilton and Kovalainen, Alonso, Webber, Bourdais and Vettel completed the top eight.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Light rain during the second session grew momentarily heavier halfway through, forcing the teams to switch to their wet set-ups. Hamilton led for most of the running, before Alonso, Massa and Kovalainen set faster times late in the session on a drying track.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Räikkönen, Piquet, Coulthard, Bourdais and Webber all had off-track excursions with little damage done; a Fisichella spin at Stavelot forced the session to be halted for ten minutes while the debris was removed and water was cleared from the track.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Conditions had not improved by the following morning for the start of the final period of practice, but a drying track late in the session allowed Heidfeld to record the best time, a 1:47.876, ahead of Kovalainen, Alonso, Hamilton, Massa, Vettel and Räikkönen.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2008_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Saturday afternoon's qualifying session was divided into three&#160;parts. In the first 20-minute period, cars finishing 16th or lower were eliminated. The second qualifying period lasted for 15&#160;minutes, at the end of which the fastest ten cars went into the final period, to determine their grid positions for the race. Cars failing to make the final period were allowed to be refuelled before the race but those competing in it were not, and so carried more fuel than they had done in the earlier qualifying sessions.<sup id="cite_ref-sporting_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sporting-6">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatleft" style="width:35%;&#32;;"><style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r887775652">.mw-parser-output .quotebox{background-color:#F9F9F9;border:1px solid #aaa;box-sizing:border-box;padding:10px;font-size:88%;max-width:100%}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft{margin:0.5em 1.4em 0.8em 0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright{margin:0.5em 0 0.8em 1.4em}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.centered{margin:0.5em auto 0.8em auto}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft p,.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright p{font-style:inherit}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-title{background-color:#F9F9F9;text-align:center;font-size:larger;font-weight:bold}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:before{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" “ ";vertical-align:-45%;line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:after{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" ” ";line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .left-aligned{text-align:left}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .right-aligned{text-align:right}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .center-aligned{text-align:center}.mw-parser-output .quotebox cite{display:block;font-style:normal}@media screen and (max-width:360px){.mw-parser-output .quotebox{min-width:100%;margin:0 0 0.8em!important;float:none!important}}</style>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">I did a great lap, but it was not enough to be on pole position. Today, we lacked a bit of speed compared to our main rivals but the race is very long and, over a distance, we know we can be very competitive and therefore we are capable of fighting for the win ... <p><cite class="left-aligned" style=""><a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a>, following the third qualifying session.<sup id="cite_ref-qualquotes_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualquotes-11">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup></cite>

</div>
<p>Hamilton clinched his fifth <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> of the season with a time of 1:47.338, ahead of Felipe Massa, who qualified second 0.34&#160;seconds behind his McLaren rival.<sup id="cite_ref-qualtimes_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualtimes-12">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> A mistake-free run allowed Kovalainen to take third place on the grid, alongside Räikkönen in fourth.<sup id="cite_ref-qualquotes_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualquotes-11">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Heidfeld took fifth, ahead of Alonso, Webber and Kubica. Bourdais &#8211; after setting the quickest time in the first session &#8211; took ninth, one place ahead of his Toro Rosso teammate Vettel.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Toyota driver <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> qualified 11th after he experienced problems generating heat into his tyres; his teammate <a href="/wiki/Timo_Glock" title="Timo Glock">Timo Glock</a> experienced similar problems and lined up from 13th behind Piquet. Coulthard and Rosberg took the next two grid spots, ahead of the Honda pair of <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a>, who both believed they had maximised the performance of their cars.<sup id="cite_ref-qualquotes_11-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualquotes-11">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Force India driver <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a>, Williams driver <a href="/wiki/Kazuki_Nakajima" title="Kazuki Nakajima">Kazuki Nakajima</a> and Fisichella took the final three positions.<sup id="cite_ref-qualtimes_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualtimes-12">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Nelson Piquet was the only driver not to start with the softer compound <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_tyres" title="Formula One tyres">dry-weather tyres</a> on a track which was still drying from morning rain, with the Brazilian opting for the harder tyre available.<sup id="cite_ref-piquettyre_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-piquettyre-15">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> There was a strong chance of showers predicted during the race.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Several cars were slow away on the slippery track, most notably Kovalainen. The main beneficiaries off the line were Piquet and Trulli, who each gained five places. Trulli was hit from behind at the first corner by Bourdais, who also had a quick start. The Toyota suffered diffuser and gearbox damage from the incident, spinning later in the lap.<sup id="cite_ref-quobeltoy_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-quobeltoy-17">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Kovalainen and Heidfeld also collided at the start. This allowed Alonso to gain several places. Sebastian Vettel locked his <a href="/wiki/Brake" title="Brake">brakes</a> and ran wide, losing two places. Fisichella collided with Nakajima on the first lap and had to <a href="/wiki/Pit_stop" title="Pit stop">pit</a> to fix a broken <a href="/wiki/Formula_one_car#Aerodynamics" class="mw-redirect" title="Formula one car">front wing</a> and punctures.<sup id="cite_ref-f1byteambel_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1byteambel-18">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton led from Räikkönen who managed to pass his Ferrari teammate Felipe Massa on the Kemmel straight.<sup id="cite_ref-quobelfer_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-quobelfer-19">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On the second lap, Hamilton spun at La Source and lost his lead to Räikkönen on the next straight. Glock lost several places over the first few laps due to a lack of grip from his tyres.<sup id="cite_ref-quobeltoy_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-quobeltoy-17">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> On lap&#160;ten, Heikki Kovalainen attempted to pass Webber at the Bus Stop chicane, but hit Webber on the side, causing the Australian to spin. Although the McLaren driver later dismissed the contact as "a racing accident",<sup id="cite_ref-quobelmcl_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-quobelmcl-20">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> he was given a drive-through penalty, which he took on lap&#160;14, dropping him to 15th place.<sup id="cite_ref-bellapchart_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bellapchart-21">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Hamilton_%2B_Raikkonen_2008_Belgium.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/35/Hamilton_%2B_Raikkonen_2008_Belgium.jpg/220px-Hamilton_%2B_Raikkonen_2008_Belgium.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/35/Hamilton_%2B_Raikkonen_2008_Belgium.jpg/330px-Hamilton_%2B_Raikkonen_2008_Belgium.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/35/Hamilton_%2B_Raikkonen_2008_Belgium.jpg/440px-Hamilton_%2B_Raikkonen_2008_Belgium.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3636" data-file-height="2423" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Hamilton_%2B_Raikkonen_2008_Belgium.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Hamilton spins on the second lap of the race, allowing <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> to pass him for the lead.</div></div></div>
<p>By the ten-lap mark, Räikkönen had opened a three-second lead over Hamilton. Hamilton was the first of the leaders to pit, on lap&#160;11, followed by Räikkönen a lap later. Massa and Alonso pitted on lap&#160;13, with Heidfeld a lap after them. Heidfeld's BMW teammate Robert Kubica pitted on lap&#160;15, along with Bourdais.<sup id="cite_ref-f1pitsbel_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1pitsbel-22">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Räikkönen maintained his lead over Hamilton after the round of pit stops, followed by Massa, Alonso and Bourdais.<sup id="cite_ref-bellapchart_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bellapchart-21">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Piquet retired on lap&#160;13, after spinning off and colliding with a wall. Following the race, the Renault driver blamed damp patches on the track for the incident, adding that "It's a shame because I'm sure we could have done something today."<sup id="cite_ref-quobelren_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-quobelren-23">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> On lap&#160;17, <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> retired with a gearbox problem, suffering from a failing sixth gear and <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_engines" title="Formula One engines">engine over-revving</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-f1byteambel_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1byteambel-18">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The first nine runners continued in the same order until Räikkönen and Hamilton both took their second pit stops on lap&#160;25, with Alonso pitting on lap&#160;27, Massa on lap&#160;28, and Heidfeld on lap&#160;31. Bourdais and Kubica pitted on laps 32 and 33, respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-f1pitsbel_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1pitsbel-22">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Hamilton_%2B_Raikkonen_2008_Belgium_2.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4b/Hamilton_%2B_Raikkonen_2008_Belgium_2.jpg/220px-Hamilton_%2B_Raikkonen_2008_Belgium_2.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="146" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4b/Hamilton_%2B_Raikkonen_2008_Belgium_2.jpg/330px-Hamilton_%2B_Raikkonen_2008_Belgium_2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4b/Hamilton_%2B_Raikkonen_2008_Belgium_2.jpg/440px-Hamilton_%2B_Raikkonen_2008_Belgium_2.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2908" data-file-height="1936" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Hamilton_%2B_Raikkonen_2008_Belgium_2.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Hamilton passes Räikkönen in the closing stages, having cut the previous corner.</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:F1_belgium_2008_lap_41_hamilton_and_raikkonen_off.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d5/F1_belgium_2008_lap_41_hamilton_and_raikkonen_off.jpg/220px-F1_belgium_2008_lap_41_hamilton_and_raikkonen_off.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d5/F1_belgium_2008_lap_41_hamilton_and_raikkonen_off.jpg/330px-F1_belgium_2008_lap_41_hamilton_and_raikkonen_off.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d5/F1_belgium_2008_lap_41_hamilton_and_raikkonen_off.jpg/440px-F1_belgium_2008_lap_41_hamilton_and_raikkonen_off.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3033" data-file-height="2022" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:F1_belgium_2008_lap_41_hamilton_and_raikkonen_off.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Both drivers go off at Pouhon as rain falls down.</div></div></div>
<p>Following the second round of pit stops, Räikkönen was five seconds ahead of Hamilton, but Hamilton began to close in on Räikkönen during the last stint as a result of the tyres better suiting his McLaren Mercedes.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Heavy rain began to fall on lap&#160;41. Hamilton closed the gap on Räikkönen to under a second, still six&#160;seconds ahead of Massa. Hamilton attempted to pass Räikkönen at the Bus Stop chicane, but he cut across the corner. Consequently, Hamilton led out of the chicane but allowed Räikkönen to re-pass him halfway down the straight. Hamilton then passed him again at the La Source hairpin under braking.<sup id="cite_ref-hamwinbel_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hamwinbel-25">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Räikkönen tried to double-back on Hamilton to regain the position but the line was defended, resulting in Räikkönen touching his front wing on Hamilton's rear tyre.<sup id="cite_ref-hamwinbel_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hamwinbel-25">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> The rain started coming down more heavily and <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> spun at Fagnes corner, rejoining in front of the two frontrunners and causing Hamilton to run onto the grass.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> At the next corner, Räikkönen spun and gave the lead back to Hamilton. Räikkönen then lost control of his Ferrari through the Blanchimont corner and collided with the barrier, ending his race.<sup id="cite_ref-hamwinbel_25-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hamwinbel-25">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Hamilton was first to finish the race, having cautiously driven his car to the line in very wet conditions, followed by Massa and Heidfeld. Heidfeld rose from ninth position during the final laps due to the superior grip of the intermediate tyres put on during a pit stop two laps prior to the end of the race.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Fernando Alonso and Timo Glock similarly benefited from intermediate tyres, Alonso finishing fourth and Glock eighth, having passed Webber on the final corner. Conversely, Toro Rosso drivers Bourdais and Vettel, who were third and fourth with one lap to go, were pushed down to seventh and fifth&#160;positions due to the lack of grip of their dry-weather tyres.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2008_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Timo Glock was handed a 25-second penalty for overtaking Mark Webber under <a href="/wiki/Racing_flags#Yellow_flag" title="Racing flags">yellow flags</a> during the final lap of the race. The penalty pushed Glock from eighth to ninth place.<sup id="cite_ref-Glock_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Glock-29">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Lewis_Hamilton_2008_Belgium.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/15/Lewis_Hamilton_2008_Belgium.jpg/220px-Lewis_Hamilton_2008_Belgium.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/15/Lewis_Hamilton_2008_Belgium.jpg/330px-Lewis_Hamilton_2008_Belgium.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/15/Lewis_Hamilton_2008_Belgium.jpg/440px-Lewis_Hamilton_2008_Belgium.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3356" data-file-height="2239" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Lewis_Hamilton_2008_Belgium.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Hamilton celebrates his on-track victory. It was to be short-lived.</div></div></div>
<p>Hamilton appeared on the podium and subsequent <a href="/wiki/Press_conference" title="Press conference">press conference</a> in the winner's position. When questioned on the incident at the press conference, Hamilton defended his actions:
</p>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r960796168">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>[Räikkönen] pushed me to the point where I would either have been on the kerb and crashed into him or have to go on the escape route, so I went on the escape route. I understood I had to let him past, so I did. I got in his tow and he was ducking and diving left and right and I did the same and managed to get back to the inside of him. But then he hit me at the apex of the corner but I think I was pretty much gone from there.</p><div class="templatequotecite">—&#8201;<cite>Lewis Hamilton, <sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup></cite></div></blockquote>
<p>Two hours after the race, the FIA stewards for the race issued a statement announcing that Hamilton was guilty of contravening Article&#160;30.3&#160;(a) of the <i><a href="/wiki/Formula_One_regulations" title="Formula One regulations">2008 FIA Formula One Sporting Regulations</a></i> and Appendix&#160;L, Chapter&#160;4, Article&#160;2&#160;(g) of the <i><a href="/wiki/International_Sporting_Code" title="International Sporting Code">International Sporting Code</a></i>, which both state that cutting a chicane and gaining an advantage is subject to a drive-through penalty. As the race had finished, a 25-second penalty was added to Hamilton's time instead.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> As a result of this penalty, Massa was promoted to winner of the Grand Prix, and Hamilton was dropped down to third&#160;position.<sup id="cite_ref-hamilton_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hamilton-32">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 9 September, <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> lodged an official complaint with the FIA about the incident.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> In the appeal hearing on 22 September, McLaren alleged that they had twice been advised by race director <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Whiting" title="Charlie Whiting">Charlie Whiting</a> that Hamilton had correctly returned the position to Räikkönen.<sup id="cite_ref-whiting_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-whiting-34">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Furthermore, they presented telemetry evidence suggesting that Hamilton had not only crossed the finish line after Räikkönen, but that he was travelling 6.7&#160;km/h slower at the time.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>|url= <a rel="nofollow" class="external free" href="http://argent.fia.com/web/fia-public.nsf/917D67F70F1C5EE7C12573B7003DCEC5/$FILE/CSI%20modif%20ap%20AGO%20oct%2007%20ANG%20-%20Applic.%2001.01.08%20-%20clean.pdf">http://argent.fia.com/web/fia-public.nsf/917D67F70F1C5EE7C12573B7003DCEC5/$FILE/CSI%20modif%20ap%20AGO%20oct%2007%20ANG%20-%20Applic.%2001.01.08%20-%20clean.pdf</a>

|author= FIA
|publisher= FIA.com
|date= 2007-12-20
|accessdate= 2008-10-24}}&lt;/ref&gt; McLaren's <a href="/wiki/Lawyer" title="Lawyer">lawyer</a> Mark Phillips <a href="/wiki/Queen%27s_Counsel" title="Queen&#39;s Counsel">QC</a> attempted to convince the court that since the penalty was in the form of time added on, the case was valid.<sup id="cite_ref-whiting_34-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-whiting-34">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> They presented the previous case of <a href="/wiki/Vitantonio_Liuzzi" title="Vitantonio Liuzzi">Vitantonio Liuzzi</a> at the <a href="/wiki/2007_Japanese_Grand_Prix" title="2007 Japanese Grand Prix">2007 Japanese Grand Prix</a>, specifically the FIA's claim that the presiding chief steward at the race, Tony Scott Andrews, had admitted fault in awarding the penalty to Liuzzi.<sup id="cite_ref-tomclarencase_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tomclarencase-36">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> Andrews denied the FIA's allegation.<sup id="cite_ref-tomclarencase_36-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tomclarencase-36">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> McLaren went on to use the case as a precedent for the Spa incident. The Court stated that this analogy was invalid, since in Liuzzi's case the admissibility of his appeal was not contested.<sup id="cite_ref-hearing_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hearing-37">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 23 September, the Court of Appeal issued its decision, ruling that the appeal was inadmissible.<sup id="cite_ref-hearing_37-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hearing-37">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reaction">Reaction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2008_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Reaction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Kimi_Raikkonen_2008_Belgium_crash.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a5/Kimi_Raikkonen_2008_Belgium_crash.jpg/220px-Kimi_Raikkonen_2008_Belgium_crash.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a5/Kimi_Raikkonen_2008_Belgium_crash.jpg/330px-Kimi_Raikkonen_2008_Belgium_crash.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a5/Kimi_Raikkonen_2008_Belgium_crash.jpg/440px-Kimi_Raikkonen_2008_Belgium_crash.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2031" data-file-height="1353" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Kimi_Raikkonen_2008_Belgium_crash.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Räikkönen's damaged <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_F2008" title="Ferrari F2008">Ferrari F2008</a> is brought back to the pits after the <a href="/wiki/Finland" title="Finland">Finn</a> crashed with two laps to go.</div></div></div>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatright" style="width:21%;&#32;;"><link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r887775652"/>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">From the position where [Hamilton] was, because he cut the chicane, he was [able to pass into turn one]. But if he had would have been behind me through the chicane then it may have been a different story.</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style="">&#160;–Kimi Räikkönen<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>Other <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> drivers believed that Hamilton had gained an advantage. <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9bastien_Bourdais" title="Sébastien Bourdais">Sébastien Bourdais</a> of <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso</a> said that "the penalty is really rough but in the end it's up to you to give the position back or not. Pretty straightforward".<sup id="cite_ref-driverquote_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-driverquote-39">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> The view was reiterated by his fellow drivers <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-driverquote_39-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-driverquote-39">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> Trulli believed that "Hamilton got an advantage by cutting the chicane", saying, "Had he stayed on the road, he wouldn't have had the speed to overtake the Ferrari. In the same way at <a href="/wiki/Autodromo_Nazionale_Monza" class="mw-redirect" title="Autodromo Nazionale Monza">Monza</a> someone could cut the first chicane, catch a rival's <a href="/wiki/Drafting_(racing)" class="mw-redirect" title="Drafting (racing)">draft</a>, and overtake him under braking at Roggia".<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Former world champion <a href="/wiki/Niki_Lauda" title="Niki Lauda">Niki Lauda</a> said that he did "not understand this completely wrong decision",<sup id="cite_ref-toworldmedia_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-toworldmedia-41">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> adding "It's unbelievable how the best driver in yesterday's race makes no mistakes and only gets six&#160;points".<sup id="cite_ref-toworldmedia_41-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-toworldmedia-41">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> He called for the formation of permanent race stewards, instead of the temporary steward system currently in place. He called it "the worst judgement in the <a href="/wiki/History_of_Formula_One" title="History of Formula One">history of F1</a>",<sup id="cite_ref-toworldmedia_41-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-toworldmedia-41">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> saying, "It's absolutely unacceptable when three&#160;[stewards] influence the championship like this".<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> Three-time world champion <a href="/wiki/Jackie_Stewart" title="Jackie Stewart">Jackie Stewart</a> said that "F1 attracts the largest capital investment in sport, but it's being overseen by people who are not doing it full-time and we get inconsistent decisions".<sup id="cite_ref-jscrit_43-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jscrit-43">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The original penalty received mixed reactions in the world press. Byron Young in Britain's <i><a href="/wiki/Daily_Mirror" title="Daily Mirror">Daily Mirror</a></i> said that it was the stewards' decision "that mars sport and turns fans away, that ruins the efforts of even the best competitors, taints the day and leaves fans wondering what exactly they are 'fans' of".<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Finland" title="Finland">Finnish</a> daily newspaper <i><a href="/wiki/Helsingin_Sanomat" title="Helsingin Sanomat">Helsingin Sanomat</a></i> noted that though the Grand Prix had "crushed" Räikkönen's championship dreams, Hamilton had adhered "with the rules of racing" in giving the position back. The newspaper put Räikkönen's disappointment down to his crash, rather than the chicane incident.<sup id="cite_ref-2008BGPHS_45-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2008BGPHS-45">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> In Italy, <i><a href="/wiki/La_Gazzetta_dello_Sport" title="La Gazzetta dello Sport">La Gazzetta dello Sport</a></i> declared that the decision was justified, stating that Hamilton "should have waited at least another turn rather than attacking so soon".<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The incident and subsequent court hearing led to a clarification by the FIA, saying that drivers must wait one further corner after cutting a chicane before attempting another overtaking manoeuvre.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> Formula One driver and a director of the <a href="/wiki/Grand_Prix_Drivers%27_Association" title="Grand Prix Drivers&#39; Association">Grand Prix Drivers' Association</a> <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> said that the clarification had made the issue much clearer:
</p>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r960796168"/><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>Lewis would probably never ever have had a crack at Kimi around the outside at the first part of the Bus Stop without knowing he had the option of going onto the asphalt part. I think we've got to get on top of the chicanes going forward, and we're not too far away from that at the moment, where drivers know that if you gain a position or gain an advantage, you have to give it back a bit more.
</p><div class="templatequotecite">—&#8201;<cite>Mark Webber, <sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup></cite></div></blockquote>
