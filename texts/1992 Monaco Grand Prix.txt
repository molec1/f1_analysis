<p>The <b>1992 Monaco Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>50ème Grand Prix de Monaco</b><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 31 May 1992 at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Monaco" title="Circuit de Monaco">Circuit de Monaco</a>. It was the sixth race of the <a href="/wiki/1992_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1992 Formula One World Championship">1992 Formula One World Championship</a>.
</p><p>The 78-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/7A" title="McLaren MP4/7A">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Honda_in_Formula_One" title="Honda in Formula One">Honda</a>. Drivers' Championship leader <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> took <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> in his <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW14" title="Williams FW14">Williams</a>-<a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a> and led until lap 71, when he suspected he had a puncture and made a pit stop for new tyres. He emerged behind Senna, closed up to the Brazilian and tried to find a way past but without success, Senna holding on to win by 0.2 seconds. It was Senna's fifth Monaco Grand Prix win, equalling the record set by <a href="/wiki/Graham_Hill" title="Graham Hill">Graham Hill</a>.
</p><p>Mansell's teammate <a href="/wiki/Riccardo_Patrese" title="Riccardo Patrese">Riccardo Patrese</a> was third, half a minute behind, with the top six completed by the <a href="/wiki/Benetton_B192" title="Benetton B192">Benettons</a> of <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> and <a href="/wiki/Martin_Brundle" title="Martin Brundle">Martin Brundle</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Venturi_LC92" title="Venturi LC92">Larrousse</a> of <a href="/wiki/Bertrand_Gachot" title="Bertrand Gachot">Bertrand Gachot</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-race">Pre-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1992_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Pre-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After the first five races of the championship, <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> lead the Drivers Championship by 26 points having achieved five race wins in a row. Team-mate <a href="/wiki/Riccardo_Patrese" title="Riccardo Patrese">Riccardo Patrese</a> was second with 24 points. The Williams team was leading the Constructors Championship with 74 points; second-placed <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a> had 20, with their driver <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> in third place. Reigning World Champion <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> of <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> was only in fourth place, having accumulated eight points. However, the <a href="/wiki/Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="Monaco Grand Prix">Monaco Grand Prix</a> had only been won in the last eight years by either <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> or <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a>, but <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> was looking to achieve his first win at <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Monaco" title="Circuit de Monaco">Monaco</a>. There were no changes to the driver line-up from the previous race, however before the race <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> President <a href="/wiki/Luca_di_Montezemolo" class="mw-redirect" title="Luca di Montezemolo">Luca di Montezemolo</a> had to publicly announce <a href="/wiki/Ivan_Capelli" title="Ivan Capelli">Ivan Capelli</a>'s position within the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> team was "safe" in response to criticism from the Italian Autosprint magazine about <a href="/wiki/Ivan_Capelli" title="Ivan Capelli">Capelli</a>'s recent performances.<sup id="cite_ref-Ivan_The_Terrible_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ivan_The_Terrible-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Autosport" title="Autosport">Autosport</a> had also reported on the Thursday before the race that <a href="/wiki/Ivan_Capelli" title="Ivan Capelli">Capelli</a> was "about to be replaced" according to "Italian rumours", highlighting the fact <a href="/wiki/Gianni_Morbidelli" title="Gianni Morbidelli">Gianni Morbidelli</a> had carried out most of <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>'s testing work at the <a href="/wiki/Autodromo_Enzo_e_Dino_Ferrari" class="mw-redirect" title="Autodromo Enzo e Dino Ferrari">Imola</a> circuit before the Monaco race.<sup id="cite_ref-r&amp;r_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-r&amp;r-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> had released a statement in response to <a href="/wiki/Gianni_Morbidelli" title="Gianni Morbidelli">Morbidelli</a>'s testing of the <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_F92A" title="Ferrari F92A">Ferrari F92A</a> saying <a href="/wiki/Ivan_Capelli" title="Ivan Capelli">Capelli</a> had been "on holiday" but <a href="/wiki/Ivan_Capelli" title="Ivan Capelli">Capelli</a> denied this was the case.<sup id="cite_ref-r&amp;r_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-r&amp;r-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Team Lotus</a> also brought a second new <a href="/wiki/Lotus_107" title="Lotus 107">107</a> chassis for <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> at <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Monaco" title="Circuit de Monaco">Monaco</a> as only one <a href="/wiki/Lotus_107" title="Lotus 107">107</a> had been available for Herbert at <a href="/wiki/1992_San_Marino_Grand_Prix" title="1992 San Marino Grand Prix">San Marino</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The car had been "shaken down" at <a href="/wiki/Hethel" title="Hethel">Hethel</a> in Norfolk by <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Beretta" title="Olivier Beretta">Olivier Beretta</a> before being taken to <a href="/wiki/Monaco" title="Monaco">Monaco</a><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1992_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-qualifying_report">Pre-qualifying report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1992_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Pre-qualifying report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">The team still hadn't made a proper seat for me, so I had taken a beating inside the cockpit, but I kept my foot down and desperately tried to remember where the next corner was. I knew the tunnel was taken flat out on my first lap but as I came back into daylight at 170mph, I was being bounced around so badly, I had double vision and I vividly remember speeding toward the tyre barrier wondering if I should turn left or right. Anyway I was called back to the pits and that was my run for the day.</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style=""><a href="/wiki/Perry_McCarthy" title="Perry McCarthy">Perry McCarthy</a> sums up his Monaco pre-qualifying session.<sup id="cite_ref-McCarthy_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-McCarthy-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_racing#Pre-qualifying" title="Formula One racing">pre-qualifying</a> session on the Thursday morning lasted for one hour and started at 8:00 local time, in warm and sunny weather conditions.<sup id="cite_ref-p39_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-p39-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Michele_Alboreto" title="Michele Alboreto">Michele Alboreto</a> was the fastest pre-qualifier in the <a href="/wiki/Footwork_Arrows" title="Footwork Arrows">Footwork</a>, for the second Grand Prix in succession. <a href="/wiki/Bertrand_Gachot" title="Bertrand Gachot">Bertrand Gachot</a> also pre-qualified without drama for <a href="/wiki/Larrousse" title="Larrousse">Larrousse</a>, finishing second fastest, just over half a second slower than Alboreto.<sup id="cite_ref-p39_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-p39-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Brazilian <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Moreno" title="Roberto Moreno">Roberto Moreno</a> finished third fastest despite a slight gearbox leak, promoting the <a href="/wiki/Andrea_Moda_Formula" title="Andrea Moda Formula">Andrea Moda</a> team into the main qualifying sessions for the first time. <a href="/wiki/Andrea_Chiesa" title="Andrea Chiesa">Andrea Chiesa</a> was again the final pre-qualifier in fourth place in the <a href="/wiki/Fondmetal" title="Fondmetal">Fondmetal</a>, 0.57 of a second slower than Moreno.<sup id="cite_ref-p39_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-p39-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The second <a href="/wiki/Venturi_Automobiles" title="Venturi Automobiles">Venturi</a> <a href="/wiki/Larrousse" title="Larrousse">Larrousse</a> of <a href="/wiki/Ukyo_Katayama" title="Ukyo Katayama">Ukyo Katayama</a> was slowed by a slight oil leak before the Japanese driver crashed at Tabac in the final minutes of session, finishing with the fifth fastest time and becoming the only driver other than Moreno and <a href="/wiki/Perry_McCarthy" title="Perry McCarthy">Perry McCarthy</a> to fail to pre-qualify in 1992.<sup id="cite_ref-p37_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-p37-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> McCarthy drove three laps at the beginning of the session, still with his ill-fitting seat, before the Andrea Moda team ordered him back to the pits,<sup id="cite_ref-McCarthy_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-McCarthy-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> as the team wanted his car to be ready as a spare for Moreno, should he have needed it.<sup id="cite_ref-p37_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-p37-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice_and_qualifying_report">Practice and qualifying report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1992_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Practice and qualifying report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3><p>
Two practice sessions were held before the race; the first was held on Thursday morning, with the second held on Saturday morning. Both sessions lasted 1 hour and 45 minutes. The first practice session took place under warm and hazy weather conditions.<sup id="cite_ref-p28_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-p28-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup></p><div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Andrea_Chiesa_1992_Monaco.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1f/Andrea_Chiesa_1992_Monaco.jpg/220px-Andrea_Chiesa_1992_Monaco.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1f/Andrea_Chiesa_1992_Monaco.jpg/330px-Andrea_Chiesa_1992_Monaco.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1f/Andrea_Chiesa_1992_Monaco.jpg/440px-Andrea_Chiesa_1992_Monaco.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1024" data-file-height="683" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Andrea_Chiesa_1992_Monaco.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Andrea_Chiesa" title="Andrea Chiesa">Andrea Chiesa</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Fondmetal" title="Fondmetal">Fondmetal</a> spins in front of <a href="/wiki/Karl_Wendlinger" title="Karl Wendlinger">Karl Wendlinger</a>'s <a href="/wiki/March_Engineering" title="March Engineering">March</a> during the Thursday practice session. <a href="/wiki/Andrea_Chiesa" title="Andrea Chiesa">Chiesa</a> failed to qualify as he finished 29th fastest, 0.715 seconds slower than <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Moreno" title="Roberto Moreno">Moreno</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Andrea_Moda_Formula" title="Andrea Moda Formula">Andrea Moda</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Qualifying_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Qualifying-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup></div></div></div> 
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Riccardo_Patrese_1992_Monaco.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b2/Riccardo_Patrese_1992_Monaco.jpg/220px-Riccardo_Patrese_1992_Monaco.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b2/Riccardo_Patrese_1992_Monaco.jpg/330px-Riccardo_Patrese_1992_Monaco.jpg 1.5x, Patrese">Riccardo Patrese</a> finished Saturday qualifying second fastest despite being blocked by <a href="/wiki/Bertrand_Gachot" title="Bertrand Gachot">Bertrand Gachot</a> in the final minutes of Saturday qualifying, resulting in a "paddock confrontation" between them.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup></div></div></div><div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Michael_Schumacher_1992_Monaco.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/Michael_Schumacher_1992_Monaco.jpg/220px-Michael_Schumacher_1992_Monaco.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> finished Saturday qualifying sixth fastest.</div></div></div> <p><a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> was fastest in the first practice session, 0.883 seconds ahead of <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> in second, with <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> in third and <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> fourth.<sup id="cite_ref-p28_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-p28-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Michele_Alboreto" title="Michele Alboreto">Michele Alboreto</a> took advantage of his extra running in the earlier pre-qualifying session by finishing sixth fastest, with <a href="/wiki/Andrea_de_Cesaris" title="Andrea de Cesaris">Andrea de Cesaris</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_Racing" title="Tyrrell Racing">Tyrrell</a> in seventh and <a href="/wiki/Karl_Wendlinger" title="Karl Wendlinger">Karl Wendlinger</a>'s <a href="/wiki/March_Engineering" title="March Engineering">March</a> eighth.<sup id="cite_ref-p28_10-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-p28-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> tried out a new traction control device and electronic differential on <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_F92A" title="Ferrari F92A">F92A</a> car and he finished the session ninth fastest.<sup id="cite_ref-p28_10-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-p28-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Alesi still said the engine "needed more power and better response" though.<sup id="cite_ref-p37_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-p37-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Stefano_Modena" title="Stefano Modena">Stefano Modena</a> who qualified on the front row of the grid in the <a href="/wiki/1991_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="1991 Monaco Grand Prix">1991 Monaco Grand Prix</a> achieved the tenth fastest time and showed improvement with the <a href="/wiki/Jordan_192" title="Jordan 192">Jordan 192</a> having previously failed to qualify for two races with Jordan in 1992.<sup id="cite_ref-p39_7-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-p39-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
The <a href="/wiki/List_of_motorsport_terminology#Q" class="mw-redirect" title="List of motorsport terminology">qualifying session</a> was split into two one-hour sessions; the first was held on Thursday afternoon with the second held on Saturday afternoon. The fastest time from either sessions counted towards their final <a href="/wiki/Standing_start" title="Standing start">grid position</a>. The Thursday afternoon qualifying session was held under warm but overcast conditions.<sup id="cite_ref-p39_7-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-p39-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Mansell finished Thursday qualifying fastest with a 1:20.714.<sup id="cite_ref-p39_7-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-p39-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Senna was second fastest ahead of <a href="/wiki/Ricardo_Patrese" class="mw-redirect" title="Ricardo Patrese">Ricardo Patrese</a> in third. Berger was fourth fastest with a time of 1:22.359, but halfway through the session his <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>'s front suspension broke at Massenet causing him to crash heavily into the barriers. Berger commented: <style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r960796168">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style></p><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>I was really pushing hard and the car just didn't turn into the corner. I think something may have broken. It was a big shunt. I'm surprised I wasn't hurt.<sup id="cite_ref-p28_10-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-p28-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote><p> Jean Alesi had a spin in the session and was forced to use the spare <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_F92A" title="Ferrari F92A">Ferrari F92A</a> on used tyres, however he still finished with the fifth fastest time. Schumacher rounded out the top six with <a href="/wiki/Andrea_de_Cesaris" title="Andrea de Cesaris">Andrea de Cesaris</a> up in seventh place in the <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_020" title="Tyrrell 020">Tyrrell 020</a>B. Having <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_racing#Pre-qualifying" title="Formula One racing">pre-qualified</a> for the first time with <a href="/wiki/Andrea_Moda_Formula" title="Andrea Moda Formula">Andrea Moda</a>, <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Moreno" title="Roberto Moreno">Roberto Moreno</a> continued to show improved speed by posting a 1:25.185 in the first twenty minutes, which put him 11th on the grid at the time despite Moreno's complaints of a "down-on-power engine".<sup id="cite_ref-p37_8-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-p37-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Motoretro_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Motoretro-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> He managed no further laps in the session due to overheating problems and slipped to 20th fastest by the end of Thursday qualifying.<sup id="cite_ref-Motoretro_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Motoretro-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Andrea_Chiesa" title="Andrea Chiesa">Andrea Chiesa</a> had an engine changed on his <a href="/wiki/Fondmetal_GR01" title="Fondmetal GR01">Fondmetal GR01</a> during the session and started late, finishing with a best lap time of 1:27.140, the slowest time of Thursday qualifying.
</p><p>The weather conditions on Saturday were sunny and dry, but gradually became more overcast later into the afternoon.<sup id="cite_ref-p39_7-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-p39-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Within the first practice session on Saturday morning <a href="/wiki/Pierluigi_Martini" title="Pierluigi Martini">Pierluigi Martini</a> crashed in the tunnel, causing damage to the barrier which took 40 minutes to repair and delayed the start of Saturday Qualifying by 30 minutes.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix.com_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix.com-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FP1-eurosport_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FP1-eurosport-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Mansell went even faster on Saturday with a 1:19.495 and took his sixth pole position of the season. He later described his time as a "totally clear lap".<sup id="cite_ref-quali2_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-quali2-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Patrese improved to qualify second fastest, however he was held up by <a href="/wiki/Bertrand_Gachot" title="Bertrand Gachot">Bertrand Gachot</a> on one of his qualifying laps, and reacted by heading over to the <a href="/wiki/Larrousse" title="Larrousse">Larrousse</a> pit and trying to punch the Frenchman.<sup id="cite_ref-p39_7-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-p39-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-grandprix.com_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix.com-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Senna finished qualifying third on the grid despite spinning the rear of his car into the tyre barrier at the Mirabeau corner, damaging both his rear suspension and wing which eventually broke off in the tunnel as he returned his damaged car to the pits.<sup id="cite_ref-quali2_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-quali2-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Modena suffered a drive shaft failure early in the session and ran back to his pit to use the spare <a href="/wiki/Jordan_192" title="Jordan 192">Jordan</a>. In the spare car he failed to improve on his Friday time and dropped to 21st on the grid. He was the only driver who did not set a faster time in Saturday Qualifying. Following his crash in the morning Martini still took part in the afternoon session in the spare <a href="/wiki/Dallara" title="Dallara">Dallara</a> and qualified 18th fastest.<sup id="cite_ref-Qualifying_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Qualifying-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Having finished 20th fastest on Thursday, Moreno only managed to improve slightly on his Thursday time with a 1:24.945 due to "a string of mechanical problems" with the <a href="/wiki/Andrea_Moda_S921" title="Andrea Moda S921">S921</a> chassis.<sup id="cite_ref-p37_8-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-p37-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> With the circuit proving faster on Saturday he fell to 26th place by the end of the session. <a href="/wiki/Eric_van_de_Poele" title="Eric van de Poele">Eric van de Poele</a> set his fastest time in the final minutes of qualifying but just failed to make 26th place as he was +0.036 seconds slower than Moreno.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix.com_14-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix.com-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Formula 1 journalist <a href="/wiki/Joe_Saward" title="Joe Saward">Joe Saward</a> reported there was "rejoicing" in the Andrea Moda pit upon Moreno's qualification for the race.<sup id="cite_ref-p33_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-p33-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1992_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_report">Race report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1992_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Race report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Ayrton_Senna_1992_Monaco.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8e/Ayrton_Senna_1992_Monaco.jpg/220px-Ayrton_Senna_1992_Monaco.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8e/Ayrton_Senna_1992_Monaco.jpg/330px-Ayrton_Senna_1992_Monaco.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8e/Ayrton_Senna_1992_Monaco.jpg/440px-Ayrton_Senna_1992_Monaco.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1024" data-file-height="683" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Ayrton_Senna_1992_Monaco.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> took the lead of the race on lap 70, but had to hold off a much faster <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> after he caught up in the last three laps</div></div></div><p> The conditions for the race were overcast, but warm and dry.<sup id="cite_ref-p39_7-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-p39-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The drivers took to the track on Sunday morning for a 30-minute warm-up session and Williams driver Patrese finished fastest with Mansell down in fifth.<sup id="cite_ref-p39_7-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-p39-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Alboreto performed strongly in his <a href="/wiki/Footwork_Arrows" title="Footwork Arrows">Footwork</a> to finish the session second, but his team-mate <a href="/wiki/Aguri_Suzuki" title="Aguri Suzuki">Aguri Suzuki</a> was sent to hospital for checks after crashing his <a href="/wiki/Footwork_FA13" title="Footwork FA13">FA13</a> at Tabac.<sup id="cite_ref-p39_7-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-p39-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The Japanese driver was later pronounced fit to race.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix.com_14-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix.com-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The formation lap started at 15:30 local time. 26 cars qualified for the race, but only 25 took to the grid for the start as <a href="/wiki/Gianni_Morbidelli" title="Gianni Morbidelli">Gianni Morbidelli</a> couldn't start his car during the warm-up lap and subsequently had to start from the pit lane.
</p><p>At the start, Senna passed Patrese into the St. Devote corner. Schumacher also passed Berger into the first corner. Martini also experienced his second crash of the weekend within the opening lap by running into the barrier on the exit of <i>Mirabeau</i>. The order at the end of lap one was Mansell in first, followed by Senna, Patrese, Alesi, Schumacher and Berger. Morbidelli joined the race a couple of laps down but only managed one lap before the gearbox again caused problems and forced him to retire for good.<sup id="cite_ref-p37_8-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-p37-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Moreno had moved up to nineteenth place thanks to the six earlier retirements but was forced to retire on lap 11 in the pits due to engine problems with the <a href="/wiki/Judd_(engine)" title="Judd (engine)">Judd GV</a> <a href="/wiki/V10_engine" title="V10 engine">V10</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-p37_8-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-p37-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Patrese began to attack Senna but then began to drop back with gearbox troubles. On lap 12, Schumacher tried to pass Alesi in the <i>Loews Hairpin</i> and the two collided, and an electronic box was damaged in Alesi's car. Alesi continued to stay in fifth for another 16 laps with Schumacher getting ahead of him, before the damage forced him to retire on lap 28, this promoted Berger to fifth. <a href="/wiki/Martin_Brundle" title="Martin Brundle">Martin Brundle</a> was chasing Berger, but he made an error in <i>Nouvelle Chicane</i> and not only suffered a puncture but also damaged the Benetton's front wing and had to pit for repairs, giving sixth place to <a href="/wiki/Ivan_Capelli" title="Ivan Capelli">Ivan Capelli</a>. On lap 32 Berger had to retire when his gearbox failed.<sup id="cite_ref-p37_8-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-p37-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On lap 60 Alboreto made a mistake and spun in front of Senna's <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Honda_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Honda F1">Honda</a>, nearly causing a collision between them. In avoiding Alboreto's Footwork, Senna lost nearly 10 seconds.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix.com_14-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix.com-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatright" style="width:250px;&#32;;"><link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r887775652"/>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style=""><b><a href="/wiki/Murray_Walker" title="Murray Walker">Murray Walker</a></b>: "<a href="/wiki/Ivan_Capelli" title="Ivan Capelli">Ivan Capelli</a>. This won't of course have done his <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> position any good because although <a href="/wiki/Luca_Cordero_di_Montezemolo" title="Luca Cordero di Montezemolo">Luca Di Montezemolo</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> boss of both the passenger and the racing car divisions has said <a href="/wiki/Ivan_Capelli" title="Ivan Capelli">Capelli</a>'s position is safe; some of us doubted that statement and <a href="/wiki/Ivan_Capelli" title="Ivan Capelli">Capelli</a> has gone off and out of the race, out of fifth position, out of a possible two points for the team"<br /><b><a href="/wiki/James_Hunt" title="James Hunt">James Hunt</a></b>: "Some of us horrible cynics yes"</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style=""><a href="/wiki/BBC_Sport" title="BBC Sport">BBC F1 Commentary</a><sup id="cite_ref-BBC_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div><p><a href="/wiki/Ivan_Capelli" title="Ivan Capelli">Ivan Capelli</a> was running in fifth place despite having been lapped by <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a>, however on lap 61 he spun at Casino Square, damaging the steering arm. When he reached the swimming pool complex the damage caused him to spin and slide backwards wedging his <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> into barrier before Rascasse at a 45-degree angle. His retirement reminded the BBC F1 commentary team of the speculation regarding his future with <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, which had been prominent before the race weekend.<sup id="cite_ref-BBC_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Mansell led the race in his <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW14" title="Williams FW14">Williams FW14</a>B-<a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a> up until lap 70, but then suffered a loose wheel nut and was forced into the pits, emerging behind Senna's McLaren. Mansell, on fresh tyres, set a lap record almost two seconds quicker than Senna's and closed from 5.2 to 1.9 seconds in only two laps. The pair duelled around the circuit for the final three laps but Mansell could find no way past, finishing just two-tenths of a second behind the Brazilian.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix.com_14-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix.com-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-pg150_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg150-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> It was Senna's fifth win at Monaco, equalling Graham Hill's record. Patrese took the final podium position after just holding off Schumacher's Benetton who finished fourth.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1992_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Senna_1992_Monaco_cropped.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bf/Senna_1992_Monaco_cropped.jpg/220px-Senna_1992_Monaco_cropped.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="171" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bf/Senna_1992_Monaco_cropped.jpg/330px-Senna_1992_Monaco_cropped.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bf/Senna_1992_Monaco_cropped.jpg/440px-Senna_1992_Monaco_cropped.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1057" data-file-height="821" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Senna_1992_Monaco_cropped.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Ayrton Senna with Prince Rainier and Princess Stephanie.</div></div></div>
<p>The top three finishers appeared in <a href="/wiki/Albert_II,_Prince_of_Monaco" title="Albert II, Prince of Monaco">Prince Albert II of Monaco's</a> Royal box to collect their trophies and in the subsequent press conference with <a href="/wiki/Tony_Jardine" title="Tony Jardine">Tony Jardine</a> asking the questions.<sup id="cite_ref-race2_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-race2-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Senna admitted he knew the Williams car was "several seconds faster" than him in the last few laps and his tyres were like "driving on ice" due to having completed the whole race on the same set.<sup id="cite_ref-race2_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-race2-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Mansell expressed his "disappointment" at not winning the race, but still described the result as the most important second place in his life and complimented Senna for his fair defensive driving:
</p>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r960796168"/><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>I must compliment Ayrton because he pretty well second guessed every move I tried to do and he was very fair and he is entitled to do what he did and I think he drove fantastic and that's why he won the race I came a close second.<sup id="cite_ref-race2_20-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-race2-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>
<p>Mansell also described the sequence of events as a result of the suspected puncture on lap 71:
</p>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r960796168"/><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>Coming into the tunnel I almost lost it, the back end just went down and I knew immediately I had picked up an instant puncture. The problem was I was halfway from the pits. So I had to drive so slowly to get to the pits. The car's brakes weren't working because I was only on three wheels. I think I lost 10–15 seconds just getting it back to the pits. We then had a longer pitstop than normal and as I came out of the pit I saw Ayrton go by, and I knew then that the race was probably lost, but as you can see from the last 10 laps we gave it everything we could and more and his car was just too wide to get past.<sup id="cite_ref-race2_20-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-race2-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>
<p>Despite initial reports that Mansell incurred a puncture in the tunnel on lap 71 Goodyear later denied this was the case.<sup id="cite_ref-p34_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-p34-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Williams engineer <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Newey" title="Adrian Newey">Adrian Newey</a> later speculated the problem had "perhaps" been caused by a "loose wheel nut".<sup id="cite_ref-p34_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-p34-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The 1992 Monaco Grand Prix turned out to be the only time the Andrea Moda team qualified for a Formula One race with Roberto Moreno retiring after 11 laps with engine problems. In an interview in 2011, Moreno declared qualifying the Andrea Moda S921 at Monaco was one of his greatest motor racing memories:
</p>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r960796168"/><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>I will remember forever going out to pre-qualify the Andrea Moda in Monaco. The tyres in those days would do their best laps around the fifth or sixth laps. For us, after the fourth lap, the engine would overheat, because we didn't have cooling big enough! We could never run that long. The engine would overheat, and the oil would be too hot. I had to stop on lap 4, to not blow the engine up. So I had given it my max in pre-qualifying before I even got the best use of the tyres. It was enough to get through stage one, and the first hurdle was overcome. I got to qualifying, and I went out early in the session. In Monaco you always get the best times in the final laps, but I put the car 11th in the first twenty minutes, that was all the laps I could do. Coming into the pits, every team was out in the pits clapping at me, and that will stay in my memory forever. Suzuka 1990 was obviously a great memory, but in achievements? Qualifying an Andrea Moda is up there as well.<sup id="cite_ref-Motoretro_13-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Motoretro-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>
<p>In a feature on the BBC's Formula 1 Website in 2009 former BBC Formula One commentator <a href="/wiki/Murray_Walker" title="Murray Walker">Murray Walker</a> ranked the 1992 Monaco Grand Prix as one of the five "best ever" Formula 1 races due to what he described as the "absolute magic" battle for the lead in the last three laps between Senna and Mansell.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
