<p>The <b>1987 San Marino Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 3 May 1987 at the <a href="/wiki/Autodromo_Enzo_e_Dino_Ferrari" class="mw-redirect" title="Autodromo Enzo e Dino Ferrari">Autodromo Dino Ferrari</a>, <a href="/wiki/Imola" title="Imola">Imola</a>. It was the second race of the <a href="/wiki/1987_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="1987 Formula One season">1987 Formula One season</a>. It was the seventh <a href="/wiki/San_Marino_Grand_Prix" title="San Marino Grand Prix">San Marino Grand Prix</a> and it was held over 59 laps of the five kilometre circuit for a race distance of 297 kilometres.
</p><p>The race was won by British driver <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> driving a <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW11B" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams FW11B">Williams FW11B</a>. It was Mansell's eighth Grand Prix victory, his first (of two) at the Imola circuit. Mansell finished 27 seconds ahead of Brazilian driver <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> driving a <a href="/wiki/Lotus_99T" title="Lotus 99T">Lotus 99T</a>. Third was Italian driver <a href="/wiki/Michele_Alboreto" title="Michele Alboreto">Michele Alboreto</a> driving a <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_F1/87" title="Ferrari F1/87">Ferrari F1/87</a>. The win gave Mansell a one-point lead in the championship over French <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_summary">Race summary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1987_San_Marino_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Race summary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In Friday practice, championship contender <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a> was eliminated from the race after an accident at the Tamburello corner. A tyre of his <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW11B" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams FW11B">Williams FW11B</a> failed, which resulted in a violent impact against the wall (when the car was returned to the pits, Williams designer <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Head" title="Patrick Head">Patrick Head</a> could not confirm if the crash was a fault with the FW11B as half the rear end had been torn off by the impact, while both Head and <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> were seen talking a good look at Piquet's wreck). Whilst in his opinion he had only sustained a sore ankle, he was taken to the nearby <a href="/wiki/Bellaria-Igea_Marina" class="mw-redirect" title="Bellaria-Igea Marina">Bellaria-Igea Marina</a> hospital, and after medical checks he was forbidden to start by FIA Medical Delegate <a href="/wiki/Sid_Watkins" title="Sid Watkins">Sid Watkins</a>. For the remainder of the weekend Piquet worked as a guest commentator on Italian television. Years later he revealed that he was "never quite right" after the accident, suffering headaches and sometimes double vision and for the rest of the 1987 season secretly visited the hospital for treatment. He did this in secret for fear that he would not be allowed to race either by Williams or Watkins.
</p><p>Alarmed by reports from other drivers of excessive tyre blistering and the suggestion that Piquet's accident may have been caused by a tyre deflation, <a href="/wiki/Goodyear_Tire_and_Rubber_Company" title="Goodyear Tire and Rubber Company">Goodyear</a> withdrew all the tyres issued to teams (the original compounds taken to Imola were actually different to those supplied for a scheduled test at the circuit the week prior to the race). A replacement selection of the tyres used in the Imola test were flown in from <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a> (with some also brought in from the nearby <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> factory in <a href="/wiki/Maranello" title="Maranello">Maranello</a>) and despite the lack of Customs officials due to an Italian public holiday, the new rubber was available for all teams by the Saturday morning practice session.
</p><p>Saturday's qualifying went on smoothly, with <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> claiming pole position on his <a href="/wiki/Lotus_99T" title="Lotus 99T">Lotus 99T</a>, the first ever pole position for an <a href="/wiki/Active_suspension" title="Active suspension">active suspension</a> car, with Mansell alongside him in the front row. The second row was occupied by <a href="/wiki/Teo_Fabi" title="Teo Fabi">Teo Fabi</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Benetton_B187" title="Benetton B187">Benetton B187</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a>'s <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/3" title="McLaren MP4/3">McLaren MP4/3</a>; "home" team <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> took the whole third row with <a href="/wiki/Michele_Alboreto" title="Michele Alboreto">Michele Alboreto</a> ahead of <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a>.
</p><p>The race required two starts as <a href="/wiki/Martin_Brundle" title="Martin Brundle">Martin Brundle</a> (<a href="/wiki/Zakspeed_871" title="Zakspeed 871">Zakspeed 871</a>), <a href="/wiki/Thierry_Boutsen" title="Thierry Boutsen">Thierry Boutsen</a> (Benetton B187) and <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Cheever" title="Eddie Cheever">Eddie Cheever</a> (<a href="/wiki/Arrows_A10" title="Arrows A10">Arrows A10</a>) stalled on the original grid. <a href="/wiki/Satoru_Nakajima" title="Satoru Nakajima">Satoru Nakajima</a> started from the pit lane owing to a faulty battery in his Lotus, and <a href="/wiki/Ren%C3%A9_Arnoux" title="RenÃ© Arnoux">RenÃ© Arnoux</a> failed to take the second start after the suspension of his <a href="/wiki/Ligier_JS29B" class="mw-redirect" title="Ligier JS29B">Ligier JS29B</a> failed.
</p><p>At the second start, Senna led off the line, but Mansell overtook him on lap two, at the Tosa corner, and went on to dominate the race. Prost took second place by overtaking Senna on lap 6; the Brazilian was then engaged in a battle with the two Ferraris, and took back second when Prost surprisingly retired with an alternator failure on lap 15. Berger retired with turbo boost failure on lap 17, and by that time Alboreto passed Senna to take second place.
</p><p>On lap 22, Mansell pitted early due to a loose wheel balance weight and for three laps Alboreto led the race to the delight of the <a href="/wiki/Tifosi" title="Tifosi">Tifosi</a>. Senna retook first position with Alboreto's pit stop, then before Mansell returned to the front when the Brazilian stopped. <a href="/wiki/Riccardo_Patrese" title="Riccardo Patrese">Riccardo Patrese</a> in his <a href="/wiki/Brabham_BT56" title="Brabham BT56">Brabham BT56</a> was now holding second place but he retired when his alternator failed on lap 57. Simultaneously, Teo Fabi retired with engine problems. Fabi's race had been wrecked by front wing damage sustained on the first lap when he collided with Cheever, although his attempted fightback did produce the fastest lap.
</p><p>Mansell took a comfortable win with Senna holding second after Alboreto's turbo experienced problems. Alboreto salvaged a 3rd place finish, which would end up being the last podium <a href="/wiki/Enzo_Ferrari" title="Enzo Ferrari">Enzo Ferrari</a> personally saw his Formula One team score, as he only attended Grand Prix races in Italy, and would die 15 months later. <a href="/wiki/Stefan_Johansson" title="Stefan Johansson">Stefan Johansson</a> (McLaren MP4/3) was in fourth place whilst <a href="/wiki/Derek_Warwick" title="Derek Warwick">Derek Warwick</a>'s late race retirement after his Arrows A10 ran out of fuel, handed fifth to Brundle for what would the only time in 5 seasons (1985-1989) that a <a href="/wiki/Zakspeed" title="Zakspeed">Zakspeed</a> would finish a race in the points. Nakajima rounded off the points in sixth place, which meant he was the first Japanese driver to score a world championship point.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
