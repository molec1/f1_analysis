<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1978_Austrian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1978_Austrian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The crowds for the Austrian GP were full of <a href="/wiki/Niki_Lauda" title="Niki Lauda">Niki Lauda</a> fans; but however for them Lauda qualified only 12th as the <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Lotus</a> cars again took the front row, with <a href="/wiki/Ronnie_Peterson" title="Ronnie Peterson">Ronnie Peterson</a> on pole. The surprise in qualifying was <a href="/wiki/Jean-Pierre_Jabouille" title="Jean-Pierre Jabouille">Jean-Pierre Jabouille</a> who qualified his turbocharged <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a> third. The start saw Peterson lead into the first corner, with <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Reutemann" title="Carlos Reutemann">Carlos Reutemann</a> snatching second from <a href="/wiki/Mario_Andretti" title="Mario Andretti">Mario Andretti</a>. Andretti tried to get the place back later in the lap, but the two collided and Andretti retired after his car spun into the barriers while Reutemann lost a couple of places to <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Depailler" title="Patrick Depailler">Patrick Depailler</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jody_Scheckter" title="Jody Scheckter">Jody Scheckter</a>. On the fourth lap, a heavy rainshower hit the track and Reutemann spun off and was beached but the marshals push-started his car as it was in a dangerous position, while Scheckter crashed out, and the race was stopped.
</p><p>The race restarted after the rain relented, and once again Peterson led followed by Depailler and Lauda. As the track began to dry, Peterson started to pull away, and behind, Reutemann was on a charge and passed Lauda for third but he was black-flagged for receiving outside assistance, and Lauda crashed out soon after, leaving <a href="/wiki/Gilles_Villeneuve" title="Gilles Villeneuve">Gilles Villeneuve</a> third. The drivers changed to slicks but the top 3 remained the same and stayed so till the end; Peterson winning ahead of Depailler, with Villeneuve taking his first ever podium.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1978_Austrian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Lap leaders: <a href="/wiki/Ronnie_Peterson" title="Ronnie Peterson">Ronnie Peterson</a> 44 laps (1–18, 29–54); <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Reutemann" title="Carlos Reutemann">Carlos Reutemann</a> 4 laps (19–22); <a href="/wiki/Gilles_Villeneuve" title="Gilles Villeneuve">Gilles Villeneuve</a> 6 laps (23–28).</li>
<li>This race was the tenth and last F1 victory for <a href="/wiki/Ronnie_Peterson" title="Ronnie Peterson">Ronnie Peterson</a>, and as of <a href="/wiki/2020_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2020 Formula One World Championship">2020</a><sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=1978_Austrian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup>, the last for a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_drivers_from_Sweden" title="Formula One drivers from Sweden">Swedish driver</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Gilles_Villeneuve" title="Gilles Villeneuve">Gilles Villeneuve</a> finished on the podium for the first time in F1</li>
<li>Lotus won the Constructor's Championship with four races left.</li>
<li>Of the 26 starters, 8 drivers had been, or would go on to be world champion. Also, of the 26 starters, 21 drivers had won, or would win a Grand Prix.</li></ul>
