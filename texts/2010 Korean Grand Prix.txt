<p>The <b>2010 Korean Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>2010 Formula 1 Korean Grand Prix</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 24 October 2010 at the <a href="/wiki/Korea_International_Circuit" title="Korea International Circuit">Korea International Circuit</a> in <a href="/wiki/Yeongam_County" title="Yeongam County">Yeongam</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Jeolla_Province" title="South Jeolla Province">South Jeolla</a>, South Korea. It was the seventeenth round of the <a href="/wiki/2010_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2010 Formula One season">2010 Formula One season</a> and the first <a href="/wiki/Korean_Grand_Prix" title="Korean Grand Prix">Korean Grand Prix</a>. The 55-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>, who started from third position. <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> finished second for <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> and Alonso's teammate <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> was third.
</p><p>The race began behind the <a href="/wiki/Safety_car" title="Safety car">safety car</a> but was stopped after three laps because of a large amount of standing water on the circuit. After a 45-minute delay, the race was restarted and the safety car was withdrawn after thirteen laps. <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">Pole sitter</a> <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> maintained his lead going into the first corner. A second safety car deployment caused by the collision of <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> on lap nineteen closed up the field but Vettel retained the lead. Alonso maintained second position until a botched <a href="/wiki/Pit_stop" title="Pit stop">pit stop</a> during a third safety-car period caused him to drop to third. At the restart on lap thirty-five, Hamilton ran wide at the first corner, allowing Alonso back into second place. Vettel's engine failed eleven laps later, promoting Alonso to the lead, which he maintained to win the race.
</p><p>The victory was Alonso's fifth of the season; the result elevated him to the lead of the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_champions" class="mw-redirect" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; champions">Drivers' Championship</a>, eleven points ahead of Webber, who had dropped to second after failing to finish the race. Hamilton moved into third and Vettel's retirement demoted him to fourth. <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> remained in fifth position. In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">World Constructors' Championship</a>, McLaren reduced the points deficit to <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a> to twenty-seven points behind. With two races of the season remaining, Ferrari was a further twenty-five points adrift.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_Korean_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Grand Prix was contested by twelve teams with two drivers each. The teams (also known as <a href="/wiki/Formula_One#Constructors" title="Formula One">constructors</a>) were <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a>, <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>, <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a>, <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso</a>, <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus_(2010%E2%80%9311)" title="Team Lotus (2010–11)">Lotus</a>, <a href="/wiki/HRT_Formula_1_Team" title="HRT Formula 1 Team">Hispania</a> and <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Racing" title="Virgin Racing">Virgin</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-classification_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-classification-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_tyre" class="mw-redirect" title="Formula One tyre">Tyre</a> supplier <a href="/wiki/Bridgestone" title="Bridgestone">Bridgestone</a> provided four types of tyres to the race; two dry compounds (soft "options" and hard "primes") and <a href="/wiki/Rain_tyre" title="Rain tyre">wet-weather compounds</a> (intermediate and full wet).<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-regulations_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The soft compounds were denoted by a green stripe on their side-walls; the wet compound tyres were identified by a green line at the bottom of their central groove.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The rules of the 2010 Grands Prix races stipulated that all cars should use both types of tyre during the race unless the driver used any one of the two wet-weather compounds. Each driver was limited to eleven sets of dry tyres for the weekend.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Before the race, Red Bull driver <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> led the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_champions" class="mw-redirect" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; champions">Drivers' Championship</a> with 220 <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Championship_points_scoring_systems" title="List of Formula One World Championship points scoring systems">points</a>; he was ahead of <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a>, who were tied for second place on 206 points. <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> was in fourth with 192 points and Hamilton's teammate <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> was fifth on 189 points.<sup id="cite_ref-points_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Seventy-five points were available for the three remaining races, which meant Hamilton or Button could still win the title. Both drivers would mathematically stay in contention if Alonso or Vettel won the race and neither McLaren driver finished in a points-scoring position.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Red Bull led the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a> with 426 points; McLaren and Ferrari were second and third with 381 and 334 points respectively, while Mercedes on 176 points and Renault on 133 contended for fourth place. Red Bull, McLaren and Ferrari had so far won races in the previous sixteen rounds of the season. <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> (twice) and <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a> had finished in second place, and <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a>, Massa (twice), Kubica (once) had all finished in third.<sup id="cite_ref-points_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>With three races remaining in the Championship and a fourteen-point advantage, Webber said he would continue his pursuit of the title; "I need to keep racing hard and going for victories. Clearly, if my rivals have a rough weekend it makes it a bit harder for them and it gives me a bit more scope, but I'm not looking to abuse that because it can all go pear-shaped very fast."<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Vettel said his season had been fraught with car problems but he remained positive about his chances; "I think it's normal to have ups and downs – and sometimes you have more, sometimes less – but, as I said, the expectation was probably to be in a position to fight for the championship and I think we're in a very strong and very good position."<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Alonso urged his teammate Massa to help him in his title bid and said the Korean Grand Prix could become "decisive" for his championship rivals.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> He favoured Hamilton to win the Korean Grand Prix.<sup id="cite_ref-alonsohamfav_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-alonsohamfav-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> With Hamilton twenty-eight points behind Webber and with Button a further three points behind his teammate, McLaren's team principal <a href="/wiki/Martin_Whitmarsh" title="Martin Whitmarsh">Martin Whitmarsh</a> said his drivers would keep their focus in the season's remaining three races and produce consistent performances.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Karun_Chandhok" title="Karun Chandhok">Karun Chandhok</a>, who raced for Hispania in the first ten races of the season, said Red Bull were the favourites to win, and that he thought the circuit's three long straights would suit the McLaren cars.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Following increased hostilities between North Korea and South Korea in the wake of the <a href="/wiki/ROKS_Cheonan_sinking" title="ROKS Cheonan sinking">sinking</a> of the South Korean <a href="/wiki/Corvette" title="Corvette">corvette</a> <a href="/wiki/ROKS_Cheonan_(PCC-772)" class="mw-redirect" title="ROKS Cheonan (PCC-772)"><i>Cheonan</i></a> in March 2010, <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_Group" title="Formula One Group">Formula One Group</a> chief executive <a href="/wiki/Bernie_Ecclestone" title="Bernie Ecclestone">Bernie Ecclestone</a> said the race would be in jeopardy if political tensions continued to escalate, and it would be abandoned if the North Korean army crossed the border.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> The revival of the <a href="/wiki/French_Grand_Prix" title="French Grand Prix">French Grand Prix</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Nevers_Magny-Cours" title="Circuit de Nevers Magny-Cours">Circuit de Nevers Magny-Cours</a> was mooted as an alternative if the Korean Grand Prix was cancelled.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Although hostilities between the two nations eventually relaxed, the race was further threatened by delayed construction. On 27 September 2010, Ecclestone said he doubted the circuit would be ready despite the laying of tarmac. A final inspection of the circuit took place on 11 October;<sup id="cite_ref-BBC9034497_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC9034497-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile</a> (FIA) safety delegate <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Whiting" title="Charlie Whiting">Charlie Whiting</a> certified that the circuit was ready on 12 October.<sup id="cite_ref-Go-ahead_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Go-ahead-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> The top layer of the track was completed ten days before the race; there was not enough time for the elements to remove the oils and bitumen chemicals in the tarmac. Circuit designer <a href="/wiki/Hermann_Tilke" title="Hermann Tilke">Hermann Tilke</a> said the surface would be slippery, making the race challenging for drivers and exciting for fans.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The layout of the track received a mostly positive response from the drivers. <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a> of Force India said he felt it was a "real pleasure" to be racing on the track and praised it for having "a lot of character".<sup id="cite_ref-responsef1com_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-responsef1com-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Mercedes driver <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> said the asphalt improved during the Friday free practice sessions and that the track was "very demanding and tricky which provides a challenge that I like a lot".<sup id="cite_ref-responsef1com_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-responsef1com-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Kubica said although the first sector was "boring" he felt the entire track layout was "ten times better than <a href="/wiki/Yas_Marina_Circuit" title="Yas Marina Circuit">Abu Dhabi</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-responsef1com_18-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-responsef1com-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> The drivers raised concerns about the entry of the <a href="/wiki/Pit_lane" class="mw-redirect" title="Pit lane">pit lane</a> at turn seventeen—a corner taken at 240&#160;km/h (150&#160;mph). Kubica said, "It might be quite tight if someone's pitting" and Lotus driver <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> said he was "worried about someone going into the back of him" when he was forced to pit because his car's gears were stuck in position.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcpitlane_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcpitlane-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> He also said the drivers would raise the point with Whiting.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcpitlane_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcpitlane-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Some teams modified their cars in preparation for the event. Red Bull and Williams revised their brake ducts.<sup id="cite_ref-williamsbrakeduct_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-williamsbrakeduct-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-redbullbrakeduct_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-redbullbrakeduct-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Williams team changed their brake ducts at the front to improve airflow inside the tyres and direct the duct towards their car's central section more efficiently.<sup id="cite_ref-williamsbrakeduct_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-williamsbrakeduct-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Red Bull's brake ducts, which had been changed for the third consecutive Grand Prix, reintroduced a small fin seen at the <a href="/wiki/2010_Singapore_Grand_Prix" title="2010 Singapore Grand Prix">Singapore Grand Prix</a>; for Korea two aerodynamic fins were fitted and a larger duct was introduced.<sup id="cite_ref-redbullbrakeduct_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-redbullbrakeduct-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> McLaren modified a version of their <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_car#aerodynamics" title="Formula One car">front wings</a> first introduced in Singapore. The team added a vertical <a href="/wiki/Gurney_flap" title="Gurney flap">gurney flap</a> and an additional vertical slot at the front wing endplate to improve airflow outside the front tyres and increase the amount of <a href="/wiki/Downforce" title="Downforce">downforce</a> and therefore <a href="/wiki/Grip_(auto_racing)" title="Grip (auto racing)">grip</a> produced by the bodywork.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> The team also brought a new rear wing and tested a new revision of their F-duct system that débuted at the <a href="/wiki/2010_Japanese_Grand_Prix" title="2010 Japanese Grand Prix">Japanese Grand Prix</a> in Suzuka.<sup id="cite_ref-alonsohamfav_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-alonsohamfav-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Ferrari revised their <a href="/wiki/Diffuser_(automotive)" title="Diffuser (automotive)">diffuser</a> with curved profiles and one large middle plate designed to improve the extraction of air from the bottom of the <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_F10" title="Ferrari F10">F10</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> As in Suzuka, Toro Rosso ran an F-duct system in the Friday practice sessions.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice">Practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_Korean_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Vettel_Cor%C3%A9e_2010.jpg" class="image"><img alt="A figure, wearing a black helmet design, is driving a Formula One car that is of a blue colour design, with red, light blue and white stripes adorned on the car&#39;s front section." src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1d/Vettel_Cor%C3%A9e_2010.jpg/220px-Vettel_Cor%C3%A9e_2010.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="130" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1d/Vettel_Cor%C3%A9e_2010.jpg/330px-Vettel_Cor%C3%A9e_2010.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1d/Vettel_Cor%C3%A9e_2010.jpg/440px-Vettel_Cor%C3%A9e_2010.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2133" data-file-height="1265" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Vettel_Cor%C3%A9e_2010.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> secured his ninth <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> of the season.</div></div></div>
<p>Three practice sessions—two on Friday and a third on Saturday—were held before the main Sunday race. The Friday morning and afternoon sessions each lasted ninety minutes. The third session was held on Saturday morning and lasted an hour.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The track was dusty when the first practice sessions were held; grip was poor and some drivers were forced onto the <a href="/wiki/Run-off_area" title="Run-off area">run-off areas</a> after they slid off the track. Hamilton paced the first practice session with a time of 1 minute and 40.887 seconds; almost one-tenth of a second quicker than Kubica. Rosberg and Michael Schumacher set the third- and sixth-fastest times respectively for Mercedes; they were separated by Vettel and Button. Webber, <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> and the two Williams drivers completed the top ten; <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a> ahead of <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a>. Hispania driver <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Senna" title="Bruno Senna">Bruno Senna</a> spun at turn nine after his left rear suspension failed and stopped on the track without hitting a barrier.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Grip was improved for the second practice session, in which Webber set a time of 1:37.942—the quickest of the day—despite spinning late in the session. Alonso was second-quickest and Hamilton third. Kubica set the fourth-quickest time. Button, who was sidelined for most of the session with an overheated exhaust, was fifth. Massa, Vettel, <a href="/wiki/Vitaly_Petrov" title="Vitaly Petrov">Vitaly Petrov</a>, Rosberg and <a href="/wiki/Kamui_Kobayashi" title="Kamui Kobayashi">Kamui Kobayashi</a> composed the rest of the top ten. <a href="/wiki/Sakon_Yamamoto" title="Sakon Yamamoto">Sakon Yamamoto</a> in the other Hispania car caused the session to be suspended after spinning and stopping his car at turn sixteen.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After discussions with Whiting during a Friday night drivers' briefing, the height of an apex at turn sixteen was increased and the wall in the area was moved back. A new apex kerb at turn eighteen was built to stop dirt and stones from accumulating on the circuit, which was extensively cleaned.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> Drivers were told they could enter the pit lane from the racing line without incurring a penalty.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcpitlane_19-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcpitlane-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Bridgestone had reported heavy tyre graining during the Friday practice sessions; its Motorsport Tyre Development director <a href="/wiki/Hirohide_Hamashima" title="Hirohide Hamashima">Hirohide Hamashima</a> said drivers would face a challenge of looking after their tyres if track conditions had not changed before the race.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Kubica was quickest in the final practice session; his late lap of 1:37.354 was 0.048 seconds quicker than Hamilton's in second. Alonso and Webber were third and fourth.<sup id="cite_ref-practice3_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-practice3-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Rosberg, who was fifth,<sup id="cite_ref-practice3_29-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-practice3-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> slowed at turn fifteen during the session's closing stages; he narrowly avoided a collision with Alonso. The incident was subject to a stewards' investigation; the stewards decided not to penalise Rosberg.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Massa, Button, Hülkenberg, Schumacher and Sutil completed the top ten ahead of qualifying. Vettel was sixteenth; the result of running onto the grass.<sup id="cite_ref-practice3_29-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-practice3-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_Korean_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatright" style="width:33%;&#32;;"><style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r887775652">.mw-parser-output .quotebox{background-color:#F9F9F9;border:1px solid #aaa;box-sizing:border-box;padding:10px;font-size:88%;max-width:100%}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft{margin:0.5em 1.4em 0.8em 0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright{margin:0.5em 0 0.8em 1.4em}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.centered{margin:0.5em auto 0.8em auto}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft p,.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright p{font-style:inherit}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-title{background-color:#F9F9F9;text-align:center;font-size:larger;font-weight:bold}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:before{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" “ ";vertical-align:-45%;line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:after{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" ” ";line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .left-aligned{text-align:left}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .right-aligned{text-align:right}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .center-aligned{text-align:center}.mw-parser-output .quotebox cite{display:block;font-style:normal}@media screen and (max-width:360px){.mw-parser-output .quotebox{min-width:100%;margin:0 0 0.8em!important;float:none!important}}</style>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">It's a special feeling to be on pole – it was very close. The track is good here – in the first sector there's not so much you can do, just hit your braking points at the right time, but the second and third sectors are quite entertaining with nice flowing corners, but it's easy to make a mistake. It was a small margin for pole, but it was enough and it was a good result as yesterday we didn't have smooth running. We had a puncture in the practice, so I hadn't done too many laps on the new track, which makes it harder to get into a rhythm. You have to take every race as it comes, Japan was a good result, but now in Korea all the focus is on tomorrow's race. The car was quick today, no doubt, but you still need to get the job done and the most important thing is that you remain calm, which we all did as we knew what we had in us. So, we got the job done today, but let's see for tomorrow.</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style="">Sebastian Vettel on taking the pole position.<sup id="cite_ref-qualiquotes_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualiquotes-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>Saturday afternoon's qualifying session was divided into three parts. The first part ran for twenty minutes and eliminated the cars that finished eighteenth or lower. The second session lasted fifteen minutes and eliminated cars that finished in positions eleven to seventeen. The final session determined <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> to tenth. Cars which competed in the final session were not allowed to change tyres before the race; these started the race fitted with the tyres with which they set their quickest lap times.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> It was held in overcast weather conditions.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportquali_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportquali-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Tyre degradation on the soft compound tyres was heavy; the drivers set their laps on the hard tyres during the first session and switched to softs for the two remaining sessions.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportquali_32-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportquali-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-bbcquali_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcquali-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>With a lap of 1:35.585, Vettel set the fastest time in the final session on his second timed run at the end of the period, achieving his ninth pole position of the season—his first at the Korea International Circuit. Although he was delighted to get pole position, he felt he lost time in the middle sector after making a mistake.<sup id="cite_ref-qualipressconf_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualipressconf-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Vettel's teammate Webber was also on the front row of the grid; he recorded a lap time 0.076 seconds slower.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcquali_33-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcquali-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Webber was not happy with his first set of option tyres; he chose to do two more timed laps on a new set of option tyres in the third session.<sup id="cite_ref-qualipressconf_34-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualipressconf-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Alonso qualified third; he said his starting position had realised Ferrari's "maximum potential". He also aimed to ensure that he finished the race because rain had been forecast.<sup id="cite_ref-gpquali_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpquali-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> Alonso had been the fastest driver during most of the third session until Vettel set his pole position lap.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportquali_32-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportquali-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton set the first session's fastest time of 1:37.113; he almost did not challenge for the pole position because he avoided making contact with the wall at the pit lane entry. He fell to fourth overall in the final part of qualifying.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcquali_33-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcquali-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> In the faster of the two Mercedes, Rosberg secured fifth place; Massa in the slower Ferrari placed sixth. Massa was not happy because he would start the race on the dirty side of the grid, having encountered traffic during the third session. Button complained about the lack of grip and that he could not get his tyres to the optimum temperature; he managed seventh position but was happy to start on the clean side of the grid.<sup id="cite_ref-qualiquotes_31-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualiquotes-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Kubica recorded the eighth-fastest time and struggled with <a href="/wiki/Understeer_and_oversteer" title="Understeer and oversteer">oversteer</a>, which prevented him from setting a faster lap time. Schumacher in the slower Mercedes took ninth place ahead of Barrichello in tenth.<sup id="cite_ref-gpquali_35-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpquali-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello was angry with Schumacher because he had blocked Barrichello during the second session.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcquali_33-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcquali-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher went to Barrichello to apologise and was subsequently reprimanded by the stewards.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcquali_33-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcquali-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-gpquali_35-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpquali-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Mark_Webber_2010_Japan.jpg" class="image"><img alt="A man in his mid-thirties waving his left hand. He is wearing a white T-shirt and a dark blue hat with sunglasses on top" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8d/Mark_Webber_2010_Japan.jpg/170px-Mark_Webber_2010_Japan.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="208" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8d/Mark_Webber_2010_Japan.jpg/255px-Mark_Webber_2010_Japan.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8d/Mark_Webber_2010_Japan.jpg/340px-Mark_Webber_2010_Japan.jpg 2x" data-file-width="800" data-file-height="978" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Mark_Webber_2010_Japan.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> qualified in second alongside teammate Vettel.</div></div></div>
<p>Hülkenberg, who qualified eleventh, was the fastest driver not to advance into the final session. His best time of 1:37.620 was 1.5 seconds slower than Webber's pace in the second session.<sup id="cite_ref-qualitimes_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualitimes-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> He said eleventh place was a "good position to start from" despite losing the rear of his car in turn twelve, which caused him to abort one of his quick laps.<sup id="cite_ref-qualiquotes_31-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualiquotes-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Hülkenberg was followed in the times by the BMW Sauber drivers Kobayashi in twelfth and Heidfeld in thirteenth, followed by Sutil in the quickest of the two Force India cars. Petrov, who qualified in fifteenth,<sup id="cite_ref-qualitimes_36-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualitimes-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> was penalised five positions on the grid because he had caused an avoidable accident with Hülkenberg at the start of the previous race at Suzuka.<sup id="cite_ref-petrovpenalty_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-petrovpenalty-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Petrov was aiming for a top ten grid position to minimise the effects of his grid penalty but he spun on his final timed lap after hitting a kerb; he was unable to set a quicker lap time because his tyres had become dirty.<sup id="cite_ref-qualiquotes_31-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualiquotes-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-autosportquali_32-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportquali-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Hence, <a href="/wiki/Jaime_Alguersuari" title="Jaime Alguersuari">Jaime Alguersuari</a> inherited 15th position, ahead of his teammate <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9bastien_Buemi" title="Sébastien Buemi">Sébastien Buemi</a> who opted to run one flying lap with a light fuel load for his last run of the second session but made minor mistakes which cost him time.<sup id="cite_ref-qualiquotes_31-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualiquotes-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-qualitimes_36-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualitimes-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Vitantonio_Liuzzi" title="Vitantonio Liuzzi">Vitantonio Liuzzi</a>, Trulli and <a href="/wiki/Timo_Glock" title="Timo Glock">Timo Glock</a> were the quickest drivers unable to advance beyond the first session. Liuzzi complained the tyre wear on his soft compound tyres was so excessive it created a large amount of oversteer for half a lap. The tenth row of the grid was filled by <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> (Lotus) and <a href="/wiki/Lucas_di_Grassi" title="Lucas di Grassi">Lucas di Grassi</a> (Virgin). The two Hispania drivers completed the final two positions on the grid; Yamamoto in twenty-third was 0.8 seconds faster than his teammate Senna in twenty-fourth.<sup id="cite_ref-qualiquotes_31-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualiquotes-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-qualitimes_36-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualitimes-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Grille_de_d%C3%A9part_Cor%C3%A9e_2010.jpg" class="image"><img alt="Seven Formula One cars are shown stationary on a wet racing track." src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Grille_de_d%C3%A9part_Cor%C3%A9e_2010.jpg/220px-Grille_de_d%C3%A9part_Cor%C3%A9e_2010.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Grille_de_d%C3%A9part_Cor%C3%A9e_2010.jpg/330px-Grille_de_d%C3%A9part_Cor%C3%A9e_2010.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Grille_de_d%C3%A9part_Cor%C3%A9e_2010.jpg/440px-Grille_de_d%C3%A9part_Cor%C3%A9e_2010.jpg 2x" data-file-width="864" data-file-height="648" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Grille_de_d%C3%A9part_Cor%C3%A9e_2010.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The drivers returned to the grid under <a href="/wiki/Safety_car" title="Safety car">safety car</a> conditions after the race was suspended because of heavy rain and spray.</div></div></div>
<p>The track was wet before the race because rain had fallen the previous night. The air temperature was 20&#160;°C (68&#160;°F) and the track temperature was 18&#160;°C (64&#160;°F).<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_30-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Approximately 80,000 people attended the race.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> Ferrari discovered a water leak in Alonso's car, which was repaired in the hour before the event started.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_30-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> While on their <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_motorsport_terms#O" title="Glossary of motorsport terms">reconnaissance laps</a>, Massa, Senna and Glock left the circuit but avoided damage to their cars<sup id="cite_ref-gprace_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gprace-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> as drivers tested wet tyre compounds.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_30-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> The race was scheduled to start at 15:00&#160;<a href="/wiki/Korea_Standard_Time" class="mw-redirect" title="Korea Standard Time">Korea Standard Time</a> (<a href="/wiki/Coordinated_Universal_Time" title="Coordinated Universal Time">UTC</a>+9) but rain continued to fall. A new start time of 15:10 local time was planned.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcrace_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcrace-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> Standing water on the track caused heavy spray and impaired visibility, meaning the race would start behind the <a href="/wiki/Safety_car" title="Safety car">safety car</a>. All cars were required to use the full wet tyres.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_30-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The race began without a <a href="/wiki/Parade_lap" title="Parade lap">formation lap</a> and despite the slow speed, drivers struggled for grip on the wet surface. After three laps under the safety car, conditions were unchanged; drivers reported on the circuit's condition via radio and the race was suspended.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcrace_40-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcrace-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> Light rain prevented the resumption of the Grand Prix for over forty-five minutes until the <a href="/wiki/Motorsport_marshal" title="Motorsport marshal">marshals</a> and road sweeping trucks removed some of the excess water and dirt on the track.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_30-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Resin on the newly laid track surface had prevented water from soaking into the ground.<sup id="cite_ref-brundlebbc_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-brundlebbc-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> The rain had eased slightly during the suspension.<sup id="cite_ref-gprace_39-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gprace-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> Teams were allowed to change the set-up of their cars, which were largely optimised for dry weather conditions.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_30-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The race was restarted behind the safety car with the drivers in the positions held before the suspension. The safety car remained out for fourteen laps, during which the standing water was reduced as the cars circulated the track. Schumacher, Liuzzi and Yamamoto left the circuit during this period but were all able to rejoin. Di Grassi made a pit stop for new tyres three times under safety car and Senna made a pit stop at the end of the first lap.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_30-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Once the safety car drove into the pit lane, cars were allowed to overtake. Vettel maintained his pole-position lead going into the first turn with Webber close behind. Further back, Schumacher passed Kubica for eighth place.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcrace_40-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcrace-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> Senna and Trulli spun off onto the run-off area but both drivers were able to continue.<sup id="cite_ref-bbctvrace_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbctvrace-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> Rosberg passed Hamilton by taking the <a href="/wiki/Overtaking#Overtaking_in_racing" title="Overtaking">inside line</a> to take fourth place at the third corner.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_30-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Button tried to overtake Massa heading into turn four but ran wide. He momentarily lost the advantage and fended off Schumacher's attempts to pass him.<sup id="cite_ref-bbctvrace_42-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbctvrace-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At the end of the first racing lap, Vettel led by 2.8 seconds from Webber,<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_30-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> who was followed by Alonso, Rosberg, Hamilton, Massa, Button, Schumacher, Kubica, Hülkenberg, Sutil, Barrichello, Kobaysahi, Heidfeld, Alguersari, Liuzzi, Buemi, Glock, Petrov, Kovalainen, Yamamoto, Trulli, Senna and di Grassi.<sup id="cite_ref-lapchart_43-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapchart-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> Alonso chose a cautious approach and lost four to five seconds while building up his pace slowly in the early stages.<sup id="cite_ref-gpreport_44-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpreport-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> Sutil ran off the circuit but continued after losing his position to both Williams drivers.<sup id="cite_ref-gprace_39-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gprace-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> Webber lost grip on the next lap after running across the outside kerbing and spinning across the track onto the wall between turns twelve and thirteen; he slid back onto the circuit and collected Rosberg, forcing both drivers to retire.<sup id="cite_ref-gprace_39-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gprace-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-bbcrace_40-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcrace-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> The debris on-track from the accident called the safety car to be deployed.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcrace_40-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcrace-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> Kobayashi, Petrov, Heidfeld and Buemi all made pit stops for intermediate tyres during the safety car period.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_30-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-gprace_39-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gprace-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:GP_de_cor%C3%A9e_2010.jpg" class="image"><img alt="Four Formula One cars are turning left into a corner with spray being lifted from the ground by their tyres." src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5a/GP_de_cor%C3%A9e_2010.jpg/220px-GP_de_cor%C3%A9e_2010.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5a/GP_de_cor%C3%A9e_2010.jpg/330px-GP_de_cor%C3%A9e_2010.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5a/GP_de_cor%C3%A9e_2010.jpg/440px-GP_de_cor%C3%A9e_2010.jpg 2x" data-file-width="720" data-file-height="540" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:GP_de_cor%C3%A9e_2010.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Sebastian Vettel led <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> during the early phase of the race.</div></div></div>
<p>The safety car pulled off the track at the end of lap twenty-three and the race resumed with Vettel leading from Alonso and Hamilton. Vettel began to gradually pull clear from the rest of the field.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_30-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> On the twenty-fourth lap while battling with Senna for twenty-first place, Trulli collided with Senna after attempting a passing manoeuvre around the inside; Trulli's front wing was knocked off.<sup id="cite_ref-bbctvrace_42-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbctvrace-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> Trulli drove to his garage for repairs. Button, who was in fifth, started to place Massa under pressure on the same lap.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_30-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Kobayashi passed Yamamoto on lap twenty-six but ran wide and fell down the order,<sup id="cite_ref-gprace_39-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gprace-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> while Trulli rejoined the race.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcrace_40-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcrace-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> Di Grassi lost control of his car at turn fourteen after trying to pass Yamamoto on lap twenty-seven; he made light contact with the wall which caused him to retire.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_30-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-bbctvrace_42-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbctvrace-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Schumacher moved up a position as he passed Button for fifth position at turn three on the same lap. On lap twenty-eight, Trulli drove back to his garage to retire with a hydraulics failure that was caused by his power steering becoming heavy during the safety car period.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_30-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-quotes_45-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-quotes-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> Vettel set a new fastest lap of the race of 1:54.098, extending his lead over Alonso to 3.5 seconds. Button, who was under pressure from Hülkenberg and Kubica, made a pit stop at the end of lap twenty-eight for intermediate tyres and rejoined in fifteenth place. Kovalainen was sent into a spin by Buemi, having been passed by Petrov two laps later.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_30-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On lap thirty-one, Buemi tried to pass Glock on the outside heading into turn three but lost control of his car under braking and rammed into the side of Glock. Buemi sustained a broken left, front suspension and front wing damage, causing him to retire.<sup id="cite_ref-bbctvrace_42-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbctvrace-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> The incident called for the race's third safety car period.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcrace_40-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcrace-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> During the safety car deployment, most of the drivers made pit stops for intermediate tyres. Alonso, who was called in by Ferrari when Vettel changed his strategy, had a pit stop that proved problematic.<sup id="cite_ref-gpreport_44-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpreport-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> He arrived into his pit box sideways after locking his tyres. One of his mechanics could not fit his front right wheel because he dropped a <a href="/wiki/Lug_nut" title="Lug nut">wheel nut</a>; he was required to fit a spare wheel nut onto the tyre, causing Alonso to be stationary for longer than usual.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_30-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-pressconf_46-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconf-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> He lost third place to Hamilton.<sup id="cite_ref-gprace_39-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gprace-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Ferrari ordered Massa to reduce his car speed on his out-lap to help Alonso minimise the effects of losing second position.<sup id="cite_ref-hughesautosport_47-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hughesautosport-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> Glock drove back to his pit box where his mechanics examined his car for damage and decided to retire him from the race.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> Kubica avoided making contact with Sutil when his lollipop man stopped him before he could drive away from his pit box.<sup id="cite_ref-bbctvrace_42-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbctvrace-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> It was the second time that season Kubica and Sutil were involved in an incident in a pit lane; Kubica had made contact with Sutil when the latter was driving into his pit box in the <a href="/wiki/2010_Hungarian_Grand_Prix" title="2010 Hungarian Grand Prix">Hungarian Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> The safety car drove into the pit lane at the end of the thirty-fourth lap and Vettel led at the restart. Hamilton ran deep, heading into the first corner and allowing Alonso to retake second position and immediately came under attack from Massa. Kubica took eighth place from Hülkenberg by taking the inside line at the third corner. Sutil took to the inside line and passed Button for twelfth on lap thirty-six. Button was pushed wide onto the run-off area and fell to fifteenth position.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_30-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As Vettel maintained his lead, the stewards informed the Lotus team that Kovalainen had been caught speeding in the pit lane, for which he was served with a ten-second <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_regulations#Penalties" title="Formula One regulations">stop-and-go penalty</a> that was taken on lap thirty-nine.<sup id="cite_ref-gprace_39-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gprace-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-bbctvrace_42-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbctvrace-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> Alonso set a new fastest lap and closed the gap to 1.2 seconds behind Vettel at the start of lap thirty-seven.<sup id="cite_ref-gprace_39-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gprace-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> Further back, Sutil overtook Heidfeld for twelfth on the following lap.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_30-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Sutil tried to pass Kobayashi on lap thirty-eight at turn three but out-braked himself, allowing Kobayashi to retake the position. He passed the Kobayashi because of his higher straight line speed, but Sutil braked later and slid off the track, rejoining in fourteenth.<sup id="cite_ref-bbctvrace_42-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbctvrace-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> One lap later, Alonso was told by his team to drive less aggressively through turns seven and eight in an effort to preserve tyre life because he was spinning his tyres under acceleration.<sup id="cite_ref-gprace_39-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gprace-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-gpreport_44-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpreport-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> At the conclusion of lap thirty-nine, all of the drivers had made pit stops. Vettel was leading Alonso, who was followed by Hamilton, Massa, Schumacher, Barrichello, Petrov, Hülkenberg, Kubica, Liuzzi, Kobayashi, Heidfeld, Alguersari, Sutil, Button, Senna, Kovalainen and Yamamoto.<sup id="cite_ref-lapchart_43-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapchart-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> A dry line began to emerge on lap forty-one as Petrov lost control of the rear-end of his car between turns seventeen and eighteen; he slid sideways into the tyre barrier at the pit lane entry.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_30-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-gprace_39-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gprace-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Hamilton_Cor%C3%A9e_2010.jpg" class="image"><img alt="A figure, wearing a yellow and white helmet design, is driving a Formula One car that is of a silver and red colour scheme." src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/97/Hamilton_Cor%C3%A9e_2010.jpg/220px-Hamilton_Cor%C3%A9e_2010.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="133" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/97/Hamilton_Cor%C3%A9e_2010.jpg/330px-Hamilton_Cor%C3%A9e_2010.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/97/Hamilton_Cor%C3%A9e_2010.jpg/440px-Hamilton_Cor%C3%A9e_2010.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2131" data-file-height="1288" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Hamilton_Cor%C3%A9e_2010.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> finished second, despite running wide at the first turn on lap 35 which allowed <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> to take over second place.</div></div></div>
<p>Alonso had reduced Vettel's lead to 1.1 seconds as Alonso set the event's fastest lap of 1:50.257 on lap 42. By lap forty-four, darkness reduced visibility and Vettel reported he could not see the braking point in turn one, while Hamilton told his team the light level was "fine".<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_30-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Drivers were dazzled and blinded by the gear-shift lights on their steering wheels as a consequence.<sup id="cite_ref-brundlebbc_41-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-brundlebbc-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> Vettel lost half of his engine capacity through turn seventeen on lap 45 and felt strong vibrations.<sup id="cite_ref-vetenginefailure_50-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vetenginefailure-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> At the start of the forty-sixth lap, Vettel slowed with his engine problems and Alonso passed him around the inside to take the lead.<sup id="cite_ref-gpreport_44-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpreport-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> Vettel then pulled into a gap at the main straight wall with smoke billowing from his engine, and retired.<sup id="cite_ref-bbctvrace_42-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbctvrace-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> Sutil tried to overtake Kobayashi on lap forty-seven but he slid into the side of the Sauber; Sutil went off the track to retire with suspension damage. Kobayashi continued.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_30-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Hülkenberg spun off the track on lap fifty-two and made a pit stop for new tyres because his engineer on the pit-wall told him one of his tyres had developed a slow puncture.<sup id="cite_ref-gprace_39-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gprace-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello ran wide on the same lap and fell to seventh behind Kubica and Liuzzi.<sup id="cite_ref-bbctvrace_42-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbctvrace-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Alonso opened a gap of fourteen seconds to Hamilton, who was suffering from wear on his tyres, as the drivers struggled for grip and crossed the finish line on lap fifty-five; it was Alonso's fifth victory of the season in a time of 2'48:20.810, at an average speed of 68.349 miles per hour (109.997&#160;km/h).<sup id="cite_ref-bbcrace_40-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcrace-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-pressconf_46-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconf-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton finished in second place, 14.9 seconds behind, ahead of Massa in third.<sup id="cite_ref-bbctvrace_42-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbctvrace-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher, who explored the best places on the circuit for grip, equalled his best result of the season at the races in <a href="/wiki/2010_Spanish_Grand_Prix" title="2010 Spanish Grand Prix">Spain</a> and <a href="/wiki/2010_Turkish_Grand_Prix" title="2010 Turkish Grand Prix">Turkey</a> in fourth.<sup id="cite_ref-points_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-gpreport_44-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpreport-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> He was followed by Kubica in fifth, Liuzzi in sixth, Barrichello in seventh, Kobayashi eighth, Heidfeld ninth and Hülkenberg passed Alguersuari on the final lap to round out the points-scoring positions in tenth. Button was the last driver on the lead lap, with Kovalainen in thirteenth. Hispania teammates Senna and Yamamoto were the last of the classified finishers in fourteenth and fifteenth, which was the team's best race finish of the season.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_30-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_Korean_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatright" style="width:33%;&#32;;"><link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r887775652"/>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">It is a great feeling winning this way, as it was a very difficult victory, given the track conditions. Especially at the start, the situation on track and in terms of visibility was really precarious. That was the most worrying time, because I knew it would be an achievement just to stay on the track. We had never driven here in the wet and so there was no reference point. Webber's retirement slightly changed our approach because, all in all, it would have been sufficient to finish on the podium without taking too many risks. The final stages were particularly difficult because half the track was almost dry and the tyres were getting ruined really quickly. I witnessed both Red Bull problems from very close up: in these situations you try and stay super-concentrated, because you are coming from a high speed corner and there is no time to think.</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style="">Fernando Alonso after the race.<sup id="cite_ref-quotes_45-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-quotes-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>The top three drivers appeared on the podium to collect their trophies and spoke to the media at a later press conference. Alonso said the Grand Prix was "one of the best races of the year" for the Ferrari team and that the wet weather conditions made the victory more difficult; "I think it's one of the most tricky conditions' victories I've ever had, probably, because we started with very extreme conditions, red flag, then a couple of laps behind the safety car".<sup id="cite_ref-pressconf_46-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconf-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> Alonso said he would not alter his driving style in the final two rounds of the season.<sup id="cite_ref-pressconf_46-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconf-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton said finishing in second was "a great result" and that he was "very happy". He also said he lost time to other drivers in the final third of the lap because of braking problems.<sup id="cite_ref-pressconf_46-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconf-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> Massa said that despite the poor visibility he endured he was happy to finish third. He also said he struggled with his rear tyres, which caused him to slow.<sup id="cite_ref-pressconf_46-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconf-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Petrov said he had mixed feelings after the race, having been in seventh position before his race-ending crash on lap forty-one, "I am disappointed to end the race in the wall and not in the points, but it was encouraging to be running in the top ten".<sup id="cite_ref-quotes_45-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-quotes-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello, placed fifth before he ran wide on lap fifty-two and was passed by Kubica and Liuzzi, said, "I was lucky with the timing of the safety car and we were running in a good position right up until the closing stages. Unfortunately, I just ran out of tyre in the last five laps which cost us two places in the end."<sup id="cite_ref-quotes_45-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-quotes-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Webber said he was at fault for causing the accident between himself and Rosberg on lap nineteen; "I thought I'd managed to catch it but I lost the car and made contact with the wall. Then Nico hit me, which wrecked his race as well. It was my mistake and it wasn't my day."<sup id="cite_ref-guardiancrash_52-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guardiancrash-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup> Rosberg was puzzled over the incident; he said, "I don't understand why Webber didn't hit the brakes. It was crazy to roll back across the track like that."<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a>, who raced in Formula One for thirteen years, courted controversy when he accused Webber of crashing deliberately and claimed Webber planned to take out either Alonso or Hamilton.<sup id="cite_ref-telegraphberger_54-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-telegraphberger-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup> Red Bull team principal <a href="/wiki/Christian_Horner" title="Christian Horner">Christian Horner</a> defended Webber in an interview with <i><a href="/wiki/The_Daily_Telegraph" title="The Daily Telegraph">The Daily Telegraph</a></i>, saying it was "absurd" that Webber attempted to take out another competitor and stated the car suffered heavy damage with the collision with the wall. Webber later went to Rosberg and apologised for the crash on the Friday before the <a href="/wiki/2010_Brazilian_Grand_Prix" title="2010 Brazilian Grand Prix">Brazilian Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Fernando_Alonso_2010_Malaysia.jpg" class="image"><img alt="Fernando Alonso pictured at the 2010 Malaysian Grand Prix" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a3/Fernando_Alonso_2010_Malaysia.jpg/170px-Fernando_Alonso_2010_Malaysia.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="229" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a3/Fernando_Alonso_2010_Malaysia.jpg/255px-Fernando_Alonso_2010_Malaysia.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a3/Fernando_Alonso_2010_Malaysia.jpg/340px-Fernando_Alonso_2010_Malaysia.jpg 2x" data-file-width="800" data-file-height="1078" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Fernando_Alonso_2010_Malaysia.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Fernando Alonso won the race and became the points leader after Webber's retirement.</div></div></div>
<p>Vettel said Red Bull "did more or less, a perfect job" in the race until his retirement from an engine failure on the forty-sixth lap. Renault principal engineer Fabrice Lom apologised to Red Bull for Vettel's engine failure.<sup id="cite_ref-guardiancrash_52-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guardiancrash-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup> It was later revealed by Vettel that the engine was not to have been used in future races had it not failed.<sup id="cite_ref-vetenginefailure_50-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vetenginefailure-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> Horner subsequently said Red Bull would not be favouring Vettel or Webber for the Drivers' Championship.<sup id="cite_ref-guardiancrash_52-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guardiancrash-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup> In 2011, Red Bull advisor <a href="/wiki/Helmut_Marko" title="Helmut Marko">Helmut Marko</a> said he had considered retirement from the sport after Vettel and Webber failed to finish because he believed Red Bull had lost the possibility to win the title in 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> After Webber and Vettel retired, members of the McLaren and Ferrari teams celebrated the events. Sauber team principal <a href="/wiki/Peter_Sauber" title="Peter Sauber">Peter Sauber</a> told <i><a href="/wiki/Blick" title="Blick">Blick</a></i> it was "very unsporting" and that "they were scenes that didn't please me at all".<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup> Button said problems with his front tyres caused him to lock up under braking.<sup id="cite_ref-quotes_45-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-quotes-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> He later described his race as "sad".<sup id="cite_ref-bbcwin_58-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcwin-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup> Button also told journalists he believed he was unable to defend the Drivers' Championship though said he would not assist Hamilton unless Button was mathematically ruled out.<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Buemi and Sutil were summoned to the stewards for the roles in their respective accidents and incurred five-place grid penalties for the next race at Interlagos. Sutil was fined $10,000 (£6,400) for driving in the manner that was performed, despite knowing of a problem with his brakes.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcwin_58-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcwin-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup> Sutil said he struggled with excessive brake force, which caused him to lock his tyres; he was unhappy with his performance.<sup id="cite_ref-quotes_45-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-quotes-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> Kobayashi said he saw Sutil in his mirror and believed he would not complete the manoveure.<sup id="cite_ref-quotes_45-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-quotes-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> Buemi said his accident with Glock was caused by him braking late and said he felt "sorry" for Glock.<sup id="cite_ref-quotes_45-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-quotes-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The use of a safety car to start the race had a mixed response within the sport. Massa thanked Whiting in the media; he said he felt Whiting "did an excellent job of managing the situation, making the right decisions as to when to start the race and when to bring in the Safety Car at the right moment".<sup id="cite_ref-quotes_45-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-quotes-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher, Petrov, Sutil and Buemi also agreed with the decision to start under safety-car conditions.<sup id="cite_ref-quotes_45-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-quotes-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton said he felt the length of time under safety-car conditions was not "good for the spectators and it's not fun for the drivers".<sup id="cite_ref-quotes_45-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-quotes-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> He additionally felt the track was dry enough for a normal restart.<sup id="cite_ref-quotes_45-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-quotes-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/BBC_Sport" title="BBC Sport">BBC</a> commentator and former driver <a href="/wiki/Martin_Brundle" title="Martin Brundle">Martin Brundle</a> said he felt the race should have started earlier,<sup id="cite_ref-brundlebbc_41-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-brundlebbc-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> while Button said he thought it should have been stopped before visibility deteriorated.<sup id="cite_ref-quotes_45-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-quotes-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> Nevertheless, the Grand Prix was awarded the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_Promotional_Trophy_winners" title="List of Formula One Promotional Trophy winners">Race Promoters Trophy</a> at the FIA Gala Awards held in <a href="/wiki/Monte_Carlo" title="Monte Carlo">Monte Carlo</a> in December 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As a consequence of the race, Alonso returned to the lead of the Drivers' Championship on 231 points. Webber's failure to score dropped him to second, eleven points behind Alonso. Hamilton's second-place finish moved him ahead of Vettel, with Button still in fifth. Red Bull retained the led the Constructors' Championship despite not getting both cars to the finish. McLaren remained in second with 399 points; Ferrari had caught up to McLaren, the gap between the two teams reduced to 25 points with two races of the season remaining.<sup id="cite_ref-points_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Given Alonso's new lead, Vettel acknowledged his team had the fastest car in the season's two remaining races and said he remained confident of winning the Drivers' Championship.<sup id="cite_ref-quotes_45-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-quotes-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> After the conclusion of the championship in Vettel's favour by four points, Webber's manager <a href="/wiki/Flavio_Briatore" title="Flavio Briatore">Flavio Briatore</a> highlighted the Korean Grand Prix as one of Webber's most obvious lost opportunities in his bid to win the title.<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
