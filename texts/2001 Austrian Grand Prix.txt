<p>The <b>2001 Austrian Grand Prix</b> (officially the <b>XXV Großer A1 Preis von Österreich</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at <a href="/wiki/A1-Ring" class="mw-redirect" title="A1-Ring">A1-Ring</a> on 13 May 2001. It was the sixth round of the <a href="/wiki/2001_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2001 Formula One season">2001 Formula One World Championship</a> and the 24th running of the <a href="/wiki/Austrian_Grand_Prix" title="Austrian Grand Prix">event</a> as part of the series. <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> driving for the <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> team won the 71-lap race from a seventh-position start. <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> team finished second, with his teammate <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> third.
</p><p>Michael Schumacher won the 37th <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> of his career by setting the fastest lap in qualifying. The <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> pair of <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> overtook him at the start of the race. Montoya led the first 15 laps until Michael Schumacher attempted a pass on Montoya that put both drivers wide on lap 16. Montoya relinquished the lead to Barrichello, who held it until a <a href="/wiki/Pit_stop" title="Pit stop">pit stop</a> on the 46th lap. Coulthard took the lead by staying on the circuit three laps longer than Barrichello. He maintained it for the rest of the race to win. Michael Schumacher finished second after Barrichello complied with <a href="/wiki/Team_orders" title="Team orders">team orders</a> from Ferrari to cede the position on the final lap.
</p><p>It was Coulthard's second victory of the season and the eleventh of his career. Due to the result of the race, Coulthard was left within four <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Championship_points_scoring_systems" title="List of Formula One World Championship points scoring systems">points</a> of the leader of the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a> Michael Schumacher. Barrichello and Ralf Schumacher maintained third and fourth. <a href="/wiki/Sauber_Motorsport" title="Sauber Motorsport">Sauber</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> kept fifth. Ferrari continued to lead McLaren by 18 points in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a> and Williams maintained third – both Montoya and Ralf Schumacher failed to finish due to mechanical problems – with eleven races left in the season.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2001_Austrian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Red_Bull_Ring,_April_18,_2018_SkySat.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a9/Red_Bull_Ring%2C_April_18%2C_2018_SkySat.jpg/220px-Red_Bull_Ring%2C_April_18%2C_2018_SkySat.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a9/Red_Bull_Ring%2C_April_18%2C_2018_SkySat.jpg/330px-Red_Bull_Ring%2C_April_18%2C_2018_SkySat.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a9/Red_Bull_Ring%2C_April_18%2C_2018_SkySat.jpg/440px-Red_Bull_Ring%2C_April_18%2C_2018_SkySat.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2023" data-file-height="1518" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Red_Bull_Ring,_April_18,_2018_SkySat.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Ring" title="Red Bull Ring">A1 Ring</a>, now called the Red Bull Ring <i>(pictured in 2018)</i>, where the race was held.</div></div></div>
<p>The 2001 Austrian Grand Prix was the 6th of the 17 motor races of the <a href="/wiki/2001_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2001 Formula One World Championship">2001 Formula One World Championship</a> and the 24th edition of the <a href="/wiki/Austrian_Grand_Prix" title="Austrian Grand Prix">event</a> as part of the series. It was held at the nine-turn 4.326&#160;km (2.688&#160;mi) <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Ring" title="Red Bull Ring">A1 Ring</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spielberg,_Styria" title="Spielberg, Styria">Spielberg, Styria</a> on 13 May 2001.<sup id="cite_ref-CF1Race_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CF1Race-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Redesigned by <a href="/wiki/Hermann_Tilke" title="Hermann Tilke">Hermann Tilke</a> to comply with <a href="/wiki/List_of_motor_racing_circuits_by_FIA_Grade#Grade_One" title="List of motor racing circuits by FIA Grade">FIA Grade 1 Circuit License</a> requirements for its Formula One return in 1997, the track is of average length and provides very few low-speed sections. It allowed teams to optimise their engines and ran with little <a href="/wiki/Downforce" title="Downforce">downforce</a>. Teams also ensured that brakes and cooling systems had no excess strain put upon them because of their heavy usage over a single lap.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Following the <a href="/wiki/2000_Austrian_Grand_Prix" title="2000 Austrian Grand Prix">2000 race</a>, the Grand Prix organisers extended the length of the <a href="/wiki/Curb" title="Curb">kerbs</a> along the side of the track and the <a href="/wiki/Run-off_area" title="Run-off area">gravel traps</a> beside it. An additional row of tyres was erected to improve driver safety. The line demoting the entry to the <a href="/wiki/Pit_stop" title="Pit stop">pit lane</a> was moved from the entry to the final corner to the exit of turn eight. The changes were predicted to affect lap times since cars would have less on-track time and spend more time in their pit stalls.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After winning the preceding <a href="/wiki/2001_Spanish_Grand_Prix" title="2001 Spanish Grand Prix">Spanish Grand Prix</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> led the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a> with 36 <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Championship_points_scoring_systems" title="List of Formula One World Championship points scoring systems">points</a>, eight ahead of <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> in the leading <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> in second. The second Ferrari of <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> was third with 14 points and <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a>' <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> was two points behind in fourth. With eight points, <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> completed the top five in the standings.<sup id="cite_ref-points_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a>, Ferrari led with 50 points; McLaren stood in second place with 32 points. Williams were third with 18 points, as <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a> with 13 points and Sauber nine contended for fourth place.<sup id="cite_ref-points_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In preparation for the race, all the teams conducted in-season test sessions at various circuits across Europe. <a href="/wiki/Jaguar_Racing" title="Jaguar Racing">Jaguar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Prost_Grand_Prix" title="Prost Grand Prix">Prost</a>, <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a>, Williams and McLaren went to the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_Ricardo_Tormo" title="Circuit Ricardo Tormo">Circuit Ricardo Tormo</a> in <a href="/wiki/Valencia" title="Valencia">Valencia</a> from 1 to 3 May. Teams undertook tyre development on behalf of <a href="/wiki/Michelin" title="Michelin">Michelin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bridgestone" title="Bridgestone">Bridgestone</a>, tested their aerodynamic packages; McLaren and Benetton developed the chassis of their <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4-16" title="McLaren MP4-16">MP4/16</a> and <a href="/wiki/Benetton_B201" title="Benetton B201">B201</a> cars.<sup id="cite_ref-Testing_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Testing-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Jordan, Sauber, <a href="/wiki/British_American_Racing" title="British American Racing">British American Racing</a> (BAR) and <a href="/wiki/Arrows_Grand_Prix_International" title="Arrows Grand Prix International">Arrows</a> tested at the <a href="/wiki/Silverstone_Circuit" title="Silverstone Circuit">Silverstone Circuit</a> in <a href="/wiki/Silverstone" title="Silverstone">Silverstone</a> from 1 to 3 May, which was disrupted on the first day when <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a>'s Sauber was caught off guard by Jordan <a href="/wiki/Test_driver" title="Test driver">test driver</a> <a href="/wiki/Ricardo_Zonta" title="Ricardo Zonta">Ricardo Zonta</a> at the exit to Becketts corner. Sauber assessed <a href="/wiki/Launch_control_(automotive)" title="Launch control (automotive)">launch control</a> for the first time.<sup id="cite_ref-Testing_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Testing-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a> tested launch control and a revised electronics package at the <a href="/wiki/Fiorano_Circuit" title="Fiorano Circuit">Fiorano Circuit</a> in Italy with driver <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>, joining Ferrari who utilised the track for car development and reliability testing.<sup id="cite_ref-Testing_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Testing-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At the previous round in Spain, Coulthard <a href="/wiki/Stall_(engine)" title="Stall (engine)">stalled</a> on the starting grid and McLaren team principal <a href="/wiki/Ron_Dennis" title="Ron Dennis">Ron Dennis</a> accused him of "brain fade", a remark he later retracted. While Dennis publicly apologised to Coulthard, the relationship between the two was still strained; Coulthard spoke of his hope of continuing to score points in every race of the season.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/The_Daily_Telegraph" title="The Daily Telegraph">The Daily Telegraph</a></i> columnist Sarah Edworthy said the Austrian Grand Prix was where Coulthard had to demonstrate no driver errors. His manager <a href="/wiki/Martin_Brundle" title="Martin Brundle">Martin Brundle</a> stated Coulthard needed to prevent emotions from disrupting his consistency.<sup id="cite_ref-DTPreview_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DTPreview-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher said his focus was to respond to McLaren's challenge and hoped to be competitive in Austria, "Last year I got pushed out of the race at the first corner which was very disappointing. This year I hope things will go better for me. The car should be competitive, the track characteristics are similar to those of the last race in terms of performance."<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice">Practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2001_Austrian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Four practice sessions were held before the Sunday race, two each on Friday and Saturday. The Friday morning and afternoon sessions lasted an hour; the third and fourth sessions, on Saturday morning, lasted 45 minutes each.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The Friday practice sessions took place in dry and overcast weather and on a slippery, dusty track. Several drivers spun during the session; all avoided damage to their cars.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> McLaren's <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a>, unwell with the flu,<sup id="cite_ref-DTPreview_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DTPreview-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> set the first practice session's fastest lap of 1 minute and 11.751 seconds late on, almost three-tenths of a second faster than his teammate Coulthard in second. Barrichello, Räikkönen, Ralf Schumacher, Michael Schumacher, Arrows driver <a href="/wiki/Enrique_Bernoldi" title="Enrique Bernoldi">Enrique Bernoldi</a>, BAR's <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Panis" title="Olivier Panis">Olivier Panis</a>, the second Arrows of <a href="/wiki/Jos_Verstappen" title="Jos Verstappen">Jos Verstappen</a> and Heidfeld rounded out the session's top ten quickest drivers.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1FP1_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1FP1-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a>'s Prost had an engine failure on the start/finish straight,<sup id="cite_ref-AF1FP1_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1FP1-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> and Jordan driver <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a> had his running curtailed with a <a href="/wiki/Power_steering" title="Power steering">power steering</a> problem.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the second practice session, Coulthard recorded the day's fastest lap of 1 minute and 11.245 seconds on his final lap of practice. His teammate Häkkinen placed second and Barrichello was the highest-placed Ferrari in third, having been the fastest driver late in the session until both of the McLaren vehicles set their quickest laps. Ralf Schumacher, Michael Schumacher, Heidfeld, Frentzen, Räikkönen and the BAR duo of Paris and <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a> followed in positions four through ten.<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportFP2_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportFP2-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a>'s Benetton B201 engine failed on the approach to turn two five minutes into practice.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Verstappen later spun into the final turn gravel trap and his Arrows teammate Bernoldi lost control of his car on the way back to the pit lane.<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportFP2_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportFP2-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Saturday morning practice sessions occurred in clear and warm weather.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> With a time of 1 minute and 10.094 seconds, Michael Schumacher was fastest in the third practice session, ahead of Williams' <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a>, Häkkinen, Barrichello, Coulthard, Frentzen, Panis, Villeneuve and the Sauber duo of Räikkönen and Heidfeld. Bernoldi ran into the grass by locking his brakes, and Coulthard approached the final corner too quickly, causing him to drive deep into the corner's gravel trap.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Coulthard led the final practice session with a lap of 1 minute and 10.010 seconds.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1FP4_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1FP4-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> The Ferrari cars were second and third – Michael Schumacher ahead of Barrichello –  with Schumacher leading the time sheets until Coulthard's last lap.<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportFP4_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportFP4-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Häkkinen, Montoya, his teammate Ralf Schumacher, Frentzen, Trulli, Heidfeld and Villeneuve completed the top ten.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1FP4_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1FP4-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Verstappen's right-rear <a href="/wiki/Suspension_(vehicle)" class="mw-redirect" title="Suspension (vehicle)">suspension</a> failed at the double left Niki Lauda bend turn, spearing into a gravel trap and a wall beside the track.<sup id="cite_ref-FTodayFP4_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FTodayFP4-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Ralf Schumacher pirouetted through 180 degrees at the turn two; he continued driving.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1FP4_18-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1FP4-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Later, a <a href="/wiki/Deer" title="Deer">deer</a> emerged from the forests and caused Montoya to slow.<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportFP4_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportFP4-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> The deer ran through a gravel trap and left the circuit via a trackside barrier.<sup id="cite_ref-FTodayFP4_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FTodayFP4-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2001_Austrian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Michael_Schumacher_01_(sk).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/53/Michael_Schumacher_01_%28sk%29.jpg/220px-Michael_Schumacher_01_%28sk%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="239" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/53/Michael_Schumacher_01_%28sk%29.jpg/330px-Michael_Schumacher_01_%28sk%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/53/Michael_Schumacher_01_%28sk%29.jpg/440px-Michael_Schumacher_01_%28sk%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="991" data-file-height="1077" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Michael_Schumacher_01_(sk).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> <i>(pictured in 2007)</i> took his first <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> in Austria and the 37th of his career.</div></div></div>
<p>Saturday afternoon's one hour qualifying session saw each driver limited to twelve laps, with the starting order decided by their fastest laps. During this session the <a href="/wiki/107%25_rule" title="107% rule">107% rule</a> was in effect, requiring each driver to remain within 107 per cent of the fastest lap time to qualify for the race.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The weather was warm and overcast with a <a href="/wiki/Headwind_and_tailwind" title="Headwind and tailwind">headwind</a> slowing drivers.<sup id="cite_ref-FIAQuali_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAQuali-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> As the circuit was low on <a href="/wiki/Grip_(auto_racing)" title="Grip (auto racing)">grip</a> due to a lack of usage, drivers waited for 21 minutes before driving on it,<sup id="cite_ref-SpeedQuali_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SpeedQuali-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> leading to heavy traffic.<sup id="cite_ref-BBCQuali_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCQuali-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Notwithstanding a driver error at turn two,<sup id="cite_ref-QualiQuotes_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-QualiQuotes-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher bettered Häkkinen's unofficial lap record from the 2000 race to take his first pole position in Austria, his fifth of the season and the 37th of his career with a lap of 1 minute and 9.562 seconds. He was joined on the grid's front row by Montoya who took his maiden front row start.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Traffic on his final timed lap, an error on his third,<sup id="cite_ref-FIAQuali_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAQuali-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> and a slower pace in the final two corners put Ralf Schumacher third.<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportQuali_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportQuali-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello in fourth adjusted his car's downforce set-up. Trulli changed his engine after it failed during practice and he drove the final 15 minutes to take fifth. Sixth-placed Heidfeld made minor alterations to his <a href="/wiki/Sauber_C20" title="Sauber C20">Sauber C20</a> and experimented with <a href="/wiki/Tire-pressure_monitoring_system" title="Tire-pressure monitoring system">tyre pressures</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FIAQuali_21-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAQuali-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> The McLaren team were seventh and eighth in its worst qualifying performance since the <a href="/wiki/1997_French_Grand_Prix" title="1997 French Grand Prix">1997 French Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportQuali_26-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportQuali-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard in seventh could not improve his lap because of the headwind and a car balance that created <a href="/wiki/Understeer_and_oversteer" title="Understeer and oversteer">oversteer</a>. A minor loss of water pressure in his engine at turn three and on the back straight slowed his teammate Häkkinen in eighth on his final attempt.<sup id="cite_ref-FIAQuali_21-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAQuali-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-QualiQuotes_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-QualiQuotes-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Ninth-placed Räikkönen was slowed by Villeneuve on a timed lap and had a recurrence of gearbox shifting problems from the prior two practice sessions. Panis took tenth after confusion over tyre strategy. Frentzen in 11th accidentally engaged his pit lane limiter at his first attempt and his best lap was on his second timed run before his car's balance deteriorated. Multiple errors from an unbalanced setup and weight distribution on his <a href="/wiki/BAR_003" title="BAR 003">BAR 003</a> restricted Villenueve to 12th.<sup id="cite_ref-FIAQuali_21-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAQuali-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Jaguar's <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a> swerved into the pit lane to avoid hitting Alesi's spun car on his first timed lap and traffic left him in 13th. His teammate <a href="/wiki/Pedro_de_la_Rosa" title="Pedro de la Rosa">Pedro de la Rosa</a> took 14th on his second lap.<sup id="cite_ref-QualiQuotes_24-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-QualiQuotes-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Bernoldi  in 15th was ahead of his teammate Verstappen 16th after a spin at the final corner, which caused Michael Schumacher to swerve into a gravel trap.<sup id="cite_ref-SpeedQuali_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SpeedQuali-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Burti and Alesi qualified their Prost cars in 17th and 20th, both complaining of a lack of car grip. Alonso separated the two drivers in 18th. Benetton's <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> in 19th had a misfiring engine and he used the spare car setup for his teammate Button. Car set-up issues and an understeer left Button in 21st.<sup id="cite_ref-FIAQuali_21-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAQuali-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Tarso_Marques" title="Tarso Marques">Tarso Marques</a> in the second Minardi car was the final qualifier in 22nd;<sup id="cite_ref-BBCQuali_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCQuali-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> he spun into a gravel trap and took the team's spare car until a rear <a href="/wiki/Tuned_mass_damper" title="Tuned mass damper">damper</a> fault curtailed his running.<sup id="cite_ref-QualiQuotes_24-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-QualiQuotes-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Warm-up">Warm-up</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2001_Austrian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Warm-up">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The drivers took to the track on Sunday morning for a 30-minute warm-up session in dry weather.<sup id="cite_ref-austriangp_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-austriangp-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> All drivers fine-tuned their race set-ups against the weather of the time, set laps in their spare cars and Jaguar and Williams tested their launch control systems.<sup id="cite_ref-WarmUPFIA_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WarmUPFIA-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Both McLaren drivers improved from qualifying: Häkkinen recorded a lap of 1 minute and 11.647 seconds to go fastest. Coulthard and Frentzen were second and third and the Ferrari pair of Barrichello and Michael Schumacher fourth and fifth, the latter stopped at the exit of the pit lane with fire erupting from the rear of his car. Positions six through ten were occupied by Trulli, Räikkönen, Villeneuve, de la Rosa and Heidfeld.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> An engine problem prompted Villeneuve to switch into the spare BAR 003 car for the race.<sup id="cite_ref-WarmUPFIA_30-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WarmUPFIA-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2001_Austrian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The 71-lap race took place in the afternoon from 14:00 <a href="/wiki/Central_European_Summer_Time" title="Central European Summer Time">Central European Summer Time</a> (<a href="/wiki/UTC%2B02:00" title="UTC+02:00">UTC+02:00</a>). The weather at the start was dry and clear, with the air temperature from 18 to 19&#160;°C (64 to 66&#160;°F) and the track temperature between 18 to 32&#160;°C (64 to 90&#160;°F);<sup id="cite_ref-austriangp_29-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-austriangp-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-F1LiveRace_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1LiveRace-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> forecasts four days beforehand predicted rain showers and lower ambient and track temperatures.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> At the start, both Jordan  cars of Frentzen, Trulli, Häkkinen's McLaren and Heidfeld's Sauber were stationary on the grid with launch control system faults.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1Race_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1Race-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> That prompted several drivers to swerve to prevent a pile-up.<sup id="cite_ref-GPEncyAUT01_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPEncyAUT01-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> In the meantime, Montoya moved ahead of the slow starting Michael Schumacher for the lead. Ralf Schumacher then got past the Ferrari driver to move into second on the approach to turn one.<sup id="cite_ref-SVRace_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SVRace-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard made a fast getaway, moving from seventh to fifth after Michael Schumacher held him off into first turn.<sup id="cite_ref-CrashRace_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CrashRace-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> At the first lap's conclusion, the <a href="/wiki/Safety_car" title="Safety car">safety car</a> was deployed since <a href="/wiki/Motorsport_marshal" title="Motorsport marshal">track marshals</a> were unable to clear the grid of stranded cars in time for all of the circulating drivers to come by at racing speed.<sup id="cite_ref-F1COMRace_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1COMRace-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> Of the stalled cars, Frentzen retired with a broken <a href="/wiki/Transmission_(mechanics)" title="Transmission (mechanics)">gearbox</a>, Häkkinen did a solitary exploratory lap once the safety car was withdrawn at the end of lap three before retiring and Trulli and Heidfeld began from the pit lane.<sup id="cite_ref-SVRace_36-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SVRace-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Juan_Pablo_Montoya_-_August_2014_-_Sarah_Stierch.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/91/Juan_Pablo_Montoya_-_August_2014_-_Sarah_Stierch.jpg/170px-Juan_Pablo_Montoya_-_August_2014_-_Sarah_Stierch.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="187" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/91/Juan_Pablo_Montoya_-_August_2014_-_Sarah_Stierch.jpg/255px-Juan_Pablo_Montoya_-_August_2014_-_Sarah_Stierch.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/91/Juan_Pablo_Montoya_-_August_2014_-_Sarah_Stierch.jpg/340px-Juan_Pablo_Montoya_-_August_2014_-_Sarah_Stierch.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2398" data-file-height="2634" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Juan_Pablo_Montoya_-_August_2014_-_Sarah_Stierch.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a> <i>(pictured in 2014)</i> led the first 16 laps until he ran wide defending from Michael Schumacher.</div></div></div>
<p>Montoya maintained the lead over his teammate Ralf Schumacher in second and Michael Schumacher in third after the safety car drove into the pit lane at lap three's conclusion.<sup id="cite_ref-BBCLapChart_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCLapChart-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> On lap four, Verstappen overtook Räikkönen and Irvine to move to sixth as he set an early fastest lap of 1 minute and 14.059 seconds because he was on a light fuel load.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1Race_34-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1Race-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-F1COMRace_38-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1COMRace-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> That same lap, Fisichella joined the list of retirees by his <a href="/wiki/Race_engineer" title="Race engineer">race engineer</a> instructing him over the radio to enter the pit lane with a misfiring engine.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1Quotes_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1Quotes-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> Verstappen then bettered his own fastest lap to a 1-minute and 13.282 seconds on the following lap as he passed Coulthard for fifth.<sup id="cite_ref-LapChart_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LapChart-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> During the fifth lap,<sup id="cite_ref-AF1Race_34-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1Race-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Trulli went into final corner's gravel trap,<sup id="cite_ref-F1COMRace_38-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1COMRace-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> and Heidfeld overtook him for 18th.<sup id="cite_ref-LapChart_41-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LapChart-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> An attempt by Barrichello to pass his teammate Michael Schumacher on lap six almost resulted in contact at the second turn. That allowed Verstappen to challenge Barrichello; he could not overtake him. On lap seven, Panis passed Irvine for seventh and stopped a counterattack from the latter.<sup id="cite_ref-F1COMRace_38-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1COMRace-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Two laps later, Ralf Schumacher began to lose pressure in his rear brakes and fell back from his teammate Montoya; he struggled against Michael Schumacher and Barrichello. Bernoldi passed Irvine and Villeneuve on the same lap.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1Race_34-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1Race-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-GPEncyAUT01_35-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPEncyAUT01-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> On lap 10, Villeneuve lined up a pass on Irvine for 10th and the two made contact halfway through the first corner, causing Villenueve to spin and relinquish 11th place to de la Rosa.<sup id="cite_ref-F1LiveRace_32-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1LiveRace-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-F1COMRace_38-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1COMRace-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> Ralf Schumacher fell to seventh before he entered the pit lane at the end of the lap to retire.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1Race_34-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1Race-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-GPEncyAUT01_35-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPEncyAUT01-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> This promoted Michael Schumacher into second, his teammate Barrichello third and Verstappen fourth.<sup id="cite_ref-LapChart_41-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LapChart-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> At this point, a higher rate of tyre degradation of his Michelin compounds slowed Montoya,<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportRace_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportRace-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:1_43-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> allowing Michael Schumacher to close up and forming a group of cars composed of Schumacher, Barrichello, Verstappen, Coulthard and Räikkönen.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1Race_34-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1Race-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-CrashRace_37-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CrashRace-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Over the next four laps, Montoya held off attempts from Michael Schumacher to overtake.<sup id="cite_ref-F1COMRace_38-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1COMRace-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> On the 14th lap, Trulli was shown a <a href="/wiki/Racing_flags#Black_flag" title="Racing flags">black flag</a> to inform him he had been disqualified from the race because he ignored the red light to indicate that the pit lane was closed.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1Race_34-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1Race-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> The top six drivers were covered by two seconds at the start of lap 16.<sup id="cite_ref-F1NetRace_44-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1NetRace-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> As the field drove towards turn two,<sup id="cite_ref-SVRace_36-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SVRace-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher drew alongside Montoya on the right going into the turn and then steered left for the corner itself.<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportRace_42-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportRace-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> Montoya braked later than Michael Schumacher and went off the <a href="/wiki/Racing_line" title="Racing line">racing line</a> to block on the dirty part of the circuit, causing him to run into the gravel trap with the latter trapped to his left.<sup id="cite_ref-SVRace_36-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SVRace-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-IHTRace_45-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IHTRace-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher ran wide onto some grass;<sup id="cite_ref-F1COMRace_38-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1COMRace-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> he dropped to sixth and Montoya seventh.<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportRace_42-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportRace-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello took the lead with Verstappen second and Coulthard third.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1Race_34-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1Race-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Bernoldi retired in the garage with a <a href="/wiki/Hydraulics" title="Hydraulics">hydraulic system</a> failure on lap 17 as Burti was overtaken by his teammate Alesi for 12th.<sup id="cite_ref-GPEncyAUT01_35-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPEncyAUT01-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-LapChart_41-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LapChart-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> Six laps later, after dropping away from Barrichello, Verstappen made the first of two pit stops, rejoining the circuit in seventh.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1Race_34-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1Race-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> In the meantime, Michael Schumacher set a series of fast lap times to get closer to Panis.<sup id="cite_ref-SVRace_36-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SVRace-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> He overtook Panis around the inside for fourth on lap 25.<sup id="cite_ref-F1LiveRace_32-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1LiveRace-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:David_Coulthard_2007.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d8/David_Coulthard_2007.jpg/170px-David_Coulthard_2007.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="250" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d8/David_Coulthard_2007.jpg/255px-David_Coulthard_2007.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d8/David_Coulthard_2007.jpg/340px-David_Coulthard_2007.jpg 2x" data-file-width="500" data-file-height="734" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:David_Coulthard_2007.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> <i>(pictured in 2007)</i> took the lead from the 47th lap and maintained it for his second victory of the season and the 11th of his career.</div></div></div>
<p>On lap 26, Marques pulled off to the side of the track to retire with a gearbox failure.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1Race_34-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1Race-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-GPEncyAUT01_35-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPEncyAUT01-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> Two laps later, Michael Schumacher caught Räikkönen, <a href="/wiki/Drafting_(aerodynamics)" title="Drafting (aerodynamics)">slipstreamed</a> him and made a pass for third place going into Gosser corner with no counter-challenge.<sup id="cite_ref-SVRace_36-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SVRace-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-F1NetRace_44-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1NetRace-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> On the same lap, Alesi overtook de la Rosa for 10th and Button got ahead of Burti for 12th on the lap after.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1Race_34-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1Race-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> By lap 35, Michael Schumacher set a new official track record of 1 minute and 11.179 seconds as he drew to within a second of Coulthard in second. Panis ran wide on the entry to the final corner at the end of the following lap and gave Montoya fifth. At this point, Coulthard and Michael Schumacher lapped half a second faster than race leader Barrichello as Michael Schumacher came to within a tenth of a second of Coulthard.<sup id="cite_ref-SVRace_36-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SVRace-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-F1NetRace_44-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1NetRace-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> Two more retirements occurred during this stage of the Grand Prix: Alonso had a gearbox fault on the 39th lap and Montoya stopped on an escape road with a loss of hydraulic pressure two laps later.<sup id="cite_ref-F1COMRace_38-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1COMRace-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-AF1Quotes_40-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1Quotes-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-LapChart_41-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LapChart-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On the 44th lap, the first round of pit stops for the leaders began when Panis made a pit stop from fifth. He rejoined in sixth and Verstappen entered the pit lane on the next lap. Michael Schumacher and Räikkönen stopped at the end of lap 46. Coulthard led when Barrichello entered the pit lane on the next lap.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1Race_34-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1Race-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello emerged in second, ahead of his teammate Michael Schumacher after the latter lost grip into the final corner and ran sideways onto dirt.<sup id="cite_ref-F1LiveRace_32-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1LiveRace-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard remained on the circuit for the next three laps by setting the race's overall fastest lap on lap 48 at 1 minute and 10.843 seconds to go faster on a lighter fuel load.<sup id="cite_ref-SVRace_36-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SVRace-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-AutosportRace_42-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportRace-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> After an eight-second pit stop, Coulthard rejoined the circuit 1.3 seconds ahead of Barrichello in second.<sup id="cite_ref-F1NetRace_44-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1NetRace-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> On lap 50, de la Rosa retired from 12th with a <a href="/wiki/Transmission_(mechanics)" title="Transmission (mechanics)">transmission</a> failure.<sup id="cite_ref-LapChart_41-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LapChart-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> Two laps later, Villenueve took a ten-second stop-and-go penalty for speeding in the pit lane during his pit stop; he kept eighth.<sup id="cite_ref-F1COMRace_38-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1COMRace-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> Further down the field, Heidfeld overtook Button for 11th on the 57th lap.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1Race_34-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1Race-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-LapChart_41-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LapChart-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>By the 61st lap, Coulthard was a full second in front of Barrichello and was able to keep him out of range for an overtake notwithstanding slower traffic impeding him. Michael Schumacher was a further 1.2 seconds behind in third.<sup id="cite_ref-LapChart_41-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LapChart-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> Button became the Grand Prix's final retirement with fire emerging from the rear of his car and spun on his own oil at the final turn on lap 63.<sup id="cite_ref-CrashRace_37-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CrashRace-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-AF1Quotes_40-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1Quotes-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> Six laps later, Ferrari's team principal <a href="/wiki/Jean_Todt" title="Jean Todt">Jean Todt</a> invoked <a href="/wiki/Team_orders" title="Team orders">team orders</a> on Barrichello to allow Michael Schumacher past and improve his teammate's status in the Drivers' Championship.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_46-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello ignored repeated instructions from Todt and continued in second;<sup id="cite_ref-:0_46-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> he obeyed at the end of lap 71 by going wide exiting the final corner to yield second to Michael Schumacher.<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportRace_42-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportRace-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> After finishing second in every Austrian Grand Prix since 1997,<sup id="cite_ref-BBCLapChart_39-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCLapChart-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard increased his advantage to 2.1 seconds by managing his lead and crossed the start/finish line first after 71 laps to take his second victory of the season and the 11th of his career.<sup id="cite_ref-SVRace_36-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SVRace-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:1_43-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> Raikkonen secured the best finish of his season in fourth.<sup id="cite_ref-F1COMRace_38-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1COMRace-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> Panis took fifth and Verstappen earned his first point of 2001 in sixth. The final finishers were Irvine, Villeneuve, Heidfeld, Alesi and Burti.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1Race_34-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1Race-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2001_Austrian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Out of respect for <a href="/wiki/Paul_Morgan_(engineer)" title="Paul Morgan (engineer)">Paul Morgan</a>, the managing director of <a href="/wiki/Ilmor" title="Ilmor">Ilmor Engineering</a>, who was killed in a plane crash at the <a href="/wiki/Sywell_Aerodrome" title="Sywell Aerodrome">Sywell Aerodrome</a> in <a href="/wiki/Northampton" title="Northampton">Northampton</a> the day before the race, Coulthard refrained from spraying champagne on the podium.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> In the subsequent press conference, Coulthard spoke of the importance of driving with a heavy fuel tank, "I was able to lean the engine out early on after the first few laps and save quite a few laps of fuel as well as obviously with the safety car and that enabled us to go quite long."<sup id="cite_ref-PressConf_48-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PressConf-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher said he executed a manual start after a launch control system fault and that his strategy after the collision with Montoya was to wait until the pit stops, adding, "I didn't have much to lose. I could have stayed behind and wait all race until the pit stops. But then I wouldn't have had a chance to have a go to get back up to the lead, which at some stage I thought I could."<sup id="cite_ref-PressConf_48-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PressConf-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello stated he began on used front tyres and unused rear compounds and spoke of his belief he would have won had Coulthard not remained on track for another three laps, "It was one of those races where I had a good feeling, I had a good car, I was really driving as fast as I could behind him, but there was very little to take away and unfortunately I couldn't go to win again."<sup id="cite_ref-PressConf_48-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PressConf-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Opinions over the application of team orders within Ferrari on the final lap were mixed.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1TeamOrders_49-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1TeamOrders-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> Alonso called it "a strange decision" since it was the early stages of the season and the technical director of McLaren <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Newey" title="Adrian Newey">Adrian Newey</a> felt it went against Formula One's moral principles. Villeneuve said he was puzzled at those who expressed shock over the order due to Barrichello's status as Ferrari's second driver and the Jaguar team principal and three-time world champion <a href="/wiki/Niki_Lauda" title="Niki Lauda">Niki Lauda</a> argued it was the correct decision because of Michael Schumacher's higher placing in the Drivers' Championship.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1TeamOrders_49-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1TeamOrders-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> Ferrari held a post-race meeting with senior staff to discuss the situation.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello later insisted the orders had not demotivated him, "Of course, I was unhappy to be asked to move over. The one thing that makes my life difficult at Ferrari is nothing to do with the team structure or team orders, it is just the simple fact that Michael is a good driver."<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The finishing order outside of the top three remained provisional because BAR lodged an appeal with the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile</a> (FIA)-aligned <a href="/wiki/Royal_Automobile_Club" title="Royal Automobile Club">Royal Automobile Club</a> over its belief Räikkönen overtook Panis under yellow flag conditions necessitated for Button's retirement on lap 66.<sup id="cite_ref-IHTRace_45-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IHTRace-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup> It came about when the team's protest to the stewards was rejected following a review of video footage that resulted in the conclusion there was no incident since it was unreported by track marshals.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Craig_Pollock" title="Craig Pollock">Craig Pollock</a>, the BAR team owner, explained the appeal was lodged because the constructor sought clarity over the regulation preventing drivers from passing under yellow flag conditions.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup> BAR's appeal was heard by a panel of judges at a meeting of the FIA's International Court of Appeal in <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a> on 1 June.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup> The judges upheld the stewards' original verdict and confirmed the original result of the race. BAR stated their satisfaction with the result.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Michael Schumacher said he was upset about the incident with Montoya on lap 16 that forced both drivers to lose positions and vowed to speak to the Colombian, "I am a little bit upset obviously because there is no way he could make that corner. He just went off and took me with him. He had lost it anyway and all he could do was do something to me. Sooner or later I would have passed him anyway."<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup> Montoya responded by saying, "If he (Schumacher) thinks he has been granted by divine grace some right which allows him to overtake wherever he wants, I have news for him: he can forget it. He won't intimidate me, because I'm not psychologically fragile as other drivers."<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup> After reviewing television footage of the incident, Michael Schumacher told the press he now agreed with Montoya's perspective it was "a racing incident", adding, "It has to be said that, when you sit in the car you don't get the overview of the situation, which you can have looking from outside."<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The race result saw Coulthard reduce Michael Schumacher's advantage in the Drivers' Championship to four points. Barrichello remained in third with 18 points and he extended his advantage over the fourth-placed Ralf Schumacher to six points. Heidfeld continued to round out the top five with eight points.<sup id="cite_ref-points_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In the Constructors' Championship, Ferrari retained the same 18-point gap over McLaren in second and Williams maintained third. Sauber in fifth made up three points over Jordan in fourth with eleven races left in the season.<sup id="cite_ref-points_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
