<p>The <b>1963 Mexican Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at the <a href="/wiki/Aut%C3%B3dromo_Hermanos_Rodr%C3%ADguez" title="Autódromo Hermanos Rodríguez">Ciudad Deportiva Magdalena Mixhuca</a> in <a href="/wiki/Mexico_City" title="Mexico City">Mexico City</a> on October 27, 1963. It was race 9 of 10 in both the <a href="/wiki/1963_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1963 World Championship of Drivers">1963 World Championship of Drivers</a> and the <a href="/wiki/1963_International_Cup_for_Formula_One_Manufacturers" class="mw-redirect" title="1963 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers">1963 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Jim_Clark" title="Jim Clark">Jim Clark</a> dominated the race from pole position, a time that was 1.7 seconds faster than anybody else. Mexico was considered one of his most successful venues. His fastest lap of the race eclipsed his pole time by 0.7 seconds, and he lapped the entire field except for second and third behind him.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He would eventually score a total of five pole positions, four fastest laps and three victories at the venue in his Formula One career. This was also his sixth win, his sixth fastest lap, and his sixth pole position of the nine races completed in 1963.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>This was also the only World Championship Grand Prix where a car raced with the number 13 until <a href="/wiki/Pastor_Maldonado" title="Pastor Maldonado">Pastor Maldonado</a> selected the number as his permanent race number in 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
