<p>The <b>1964 Mexican Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at the <a href="/wiki/Aut%C3%B3dromo_Hermanos_Rodr%C3%ADguez" title="Autódromo Hermanos Rodríguez">Ciudad Deportiva Magdalena Mixhuca</a> in <a href="/wiki/Mexico_City" title="Mexico City">Mexico City</a> on October 25, 1964. It was race 10 of 10 in both the <a href="/wiki/1964_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1964 World Championship of Drivers">1964 World Championship of Drivers</a> and the <a href="/wiki/1964_International_Cup_for_Formula_One_Manufacturers" class="mw-redirect" title="1964 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers">1964 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers</a>.
</p><p>It was perhaps the most dramatic finale in the history of the World Championship. Championship points could only be scored by the first six finishers (9–6–4–3–2–1 points). Arriving to the race, three drivers had a chance of winning the title: <a href="/wiki/Graham_Hill" title="Graham Hill">Graham Hill</a> (<a href="/wiki/BRM" class="mw-redirect" title="BRM">BRM</a> P261<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>) with 39 points, <a href="/wiki/John_Surtees" title="John Surtees">John Surtees</a> (<a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>) with 34, and <a href="/wiki/Jim_Clark" title="Jim Clark">Jim Clark</a> (<a href="/wiki/Lotus_33" title="Lotus 33">Lotus 33</a>-<a href="/wiki/Coventry_Climax" title="Coventry Climax">Climax</a>) with 30. In order to win the title, Clark had to win the race, with Surtees finishing not higher than third and Hill not higher than fourth.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;a&#93;</a></sup> Surtees could only win the title by finishing first, in each case, or second, unless Hill finished as high as third.
</p><p>The race began with Clark leading from pole position with <a href="/wiki/Dan_Gurney" title="Dan Gurney">Dan Gurney</a> running second in the Brabham-Climax (Gurney had only ten points going into this race having won the French Grand Prix and scored a sixth place at the Belgian Grand Prix). Hill and <a href="/wiki/Lorenzo_Bandini" title="Lorenzo Bandini">Lorenzo Bandini</a>, Surtees's teammate, were duelling for third place, with Surtees a distant fifth, seemingly with no chance at winning the title. Then Bandini ran into the back of Hill's BRM, causing him to spin into the Armco, damaging his exhaust and lose a few places.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Thereafter Hill's car ran with a crimped exhaust pipe, causing him to lose power. The championship was now firmly in Clark's grasp. If the positions remained the same, he would be champion with four victories to Hill's two, although they would be tied on points at 39. On the penultimate lap, an oil line failed and Clark's engine seized as the Lotus crossed the line, with one lap left to go.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The positions were now Gurney–Bandini–Surtees, meaning the championship would be Hill's, so long as Surtees placed no higher. Realizing Surtees could win the title by finishing second, the Ferrari team manager frantically signalled Bandini to slow down as he passed the pits to enter the last lap and let Surtees through. Bandini dutifully did so and Surtees finished second, thus winning the World Championship of Drivers by one point over Hill (40 to 39). Meanwhile, Gurney won the <i>Grand Prix</i>, almost unnoticed.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Ferrari cars were entered by American <a href="/wiki/North_American_Racing_Team" title="North American Racing Team">NART</a> team and painted white and blue, <a href="/wiki/List_of_international_auto_racing_colors#Major_competitors" class="mw-redirect" title="List of international auto racing colors">the national colours of the teams from the United States</a>. This race was the last time Ferrari cars wore other than the traditional red colour (<a href="/wiki/Rosso_corsa" title="Rosso corsa">rosso corsa</a>) in Formula One.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><br />
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Permutations">Permutations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1964_Mexican_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Permutations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>For the first time, 3 British drivers went into the last race with a chance of winning the championship.
<ul><li>Graham Hill (39pts) needed either:
<ul><li>1st</li>
<li>3rd, with Surtees 2nd or lower</li>
<li>Clark 2nd or lower and Surtees 3rd or lower</li></ul></li>
<li>John Surtees (34pts) needed either:
<ul><li>1st</li>
<li>2nd, with Graham Hill 4th or lower</li></ul></li>
<li>Jim Clark (30pts) needed:
<ul><li>1st, with Surtees 3rd or lower and Graham Hill 4th or lower</li></ul></li>
<li>Jim Clark nearly managed this.  If his engine hadn't blown in the last laps, he would've done just enough to win the championship.</li></ul></li>
<li>In another first, three constructors were fighting for the championship.
<ul><li>Ferrari (43pts) needed either
<ul><li>1st</li>
<li>2nd, with the top BRM 3rd or lower</li>
<li>The top Lotus-Climax 2nd or lower and the top BRM in 3rd or lower</li></ul></li>
<li>BRM (42pts) needed
<ul><li>1st</li></ul></li>
<li>Lotus-Climax (37pts) needed
<ul><li>1st, with the top Ferrari 3rd or lower</li></ul></li>
<li>Again, if Jim Clark's engine hadn't blown, then Lotus-Climax would've won the International Cup for F1 Manufacturers</li></ul></li></ul>
