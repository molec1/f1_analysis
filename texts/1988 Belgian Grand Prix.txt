<p>The <b>1988 Belgian Grand Prix</b> (formally the XLVI <a href="/wiki/Champion_(spark_plug)" title="Champion (spark plug)">Champion</a> Belgian Grand Prix) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Spa-Francorchamps" title="Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps">Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps</a> on 28 August 1988. It was the eleventh race of the <a href="/wiki/1988_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1988 Formula One World Championship">1988 Formula One World Championship</a>.
</p><p>The 43-lap race was won from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> by Brazilian driver <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/4" title="McLaren MP4/4">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Honda_in_Formula_One" title="Honda in Formula One">Honda</a>. Senna's French teammate <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> was second, with Italy's <a href="/wiki/Ivan_Capelli" title="Ivan Capelli">Ivan Capelli</a> third in a <a href="/wiki/March_881" title="March 881">March</a>-<a href="/wiki/Judd_(engine)" title="Judd (engine)">Judd</a> after the <a href="/wiki/Benetton_B188" title="Benetton B188">Benetton</a>-<a href="/wiki/Ford_Motor_Company" title="Ford Motor Company">Fords</a> of local driver <a href="/wiki/Thierry_Boutsen" title="Thierry Boutsen">Thierry Boutsen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alessandro_Nannini" title="Alessandro Nannini">Alessandro Nannini</a> were disqualified for fuel irregularities.
</p><p>The win, Senna's seventh of the season and fourth in succession, gave him a three-point lead over Prost in the Drivers' Championship.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying_summary">Qualifying summary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1988_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Qualifying summary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This was the first race after the death of <a href="/wiki/Enzo_Ferrari" title="Enzo Ferrari">Enzo Ferrari</a>. <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> was forced to miss the race due to chicken pox and was replaced at <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a> by <a href="/wiki/Martin_Brundle" title="Martin Brundle">Martin Brundle</a> who actually managed to be fastest in the second, wet qualifying session. The <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/4" title="McLaren MP4/4">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Honda_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Honda F1">Hondas</a> had dominated the first qualifying session and occupied the whole front row for the 7th time in 11 races, though neither <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> nor <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> could match <a href="/wiki/Michele_Alboreto" title="Michele Alboreto">Michele Alboreto</a> on the uphill Kemmel Straight as he pushed his <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_F1/87" title="Ferrari F1/87">Ferrari</a> to 312&#160;km/h (194&#160;mph). This was the first race in 1988 where neither of the new <a href="/wiki/EuroBrun" title="EuroBrun">EuroBrun</a> cars made the field.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Riccardo_Patrese" title="Riccardo Patrese">Riccardo Patrese</a> did a fine job to be 5th on the grid in his naturally aspirated <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW12" title="Williams FW12">Williams</a>-<a href="/wiki/Judd_(engine)#CV" title="Judd (engine)">Judd</a>, but his time of 1:57.138 was still 3.420 seconds slower than pole man Senna. Local driver <a href="/wiki/Thierry_Boutsen" title="Thierry Boutsen">Thierry Boutsen</a> gave the Belgian crowd something to cheer when he put his <a href="/wiki/Benetton_B188" title="Benetton B188">Benetton</a>-<a href="/wiki/Cosworth_DFR" class="mw-redirect" title="Cosworth DFR">Ford</a> in 6th place on the grid.
</p><p>The grid was set by Friday's qualifying session as Saturday's qualifying was held in wet conditions. Martin Brundle surprised by being fastest in the Williams, but even more of a surprise was <a href="/wiki/Satoru_Nakajima" title="Satoru Nakajima">Satoru Nakajima</a> who was second fastest in his <a href="/wiki/Lotus_100T" title="Lotus 100T">Lotus</a>-Honda. Alain Prost, never a fan of driving in the wet and knowing his second place on the grid was safe, did not drive in the second session.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardis</a> of <a href="/wiki/Luis_P%C3%A9rez-Sala" title="Luis Pérez-Sala">Luis Pérez-Sala</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pierluigi_Martini" title="Pierluigi Martini">Pierluigi Martini</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stefano_Modena" title="Stefano Modena">Stefano Modena</a> in the <a href="/wiki/EuroBrun_ER188" title="EuroBrun ER188">EuroBrun</a> and <a href="/wiki/Julian_Bailey" title="Julian Bailey">Julian Bailey</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_017" title="Tyrrell 017">Tyrrell</a> all failed to qualify while Modena's team mate <a href="/wiki/Oscar_Larrauri" title="Oscar Larrauri">Oscar Larrauri</a> failed to pre-qualify.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_summary">Race summary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1988_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Race summary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Throughout 1988, Senna had copied Prost's car settings feeling that the Frenchman had a better handle on setting up the McLaren (after driving Prost's spare car in <a href="/wiki/1988_Brazilian_Grand_Prix" title="1988 Brazilian Grand Prix">Brazil</a> he found that his team mates settings actually suited his own driving style, plus they were easier on the car). At Spa Prost decided at the last minute to alter the settings on his car by taking off some wing for more straight line speed in a bid to gain an advantage. Unfortunately for the Frenchman this had a detrimental result and he was unhappy with the balance and handling of his car in the race, compared to Senna with Prost's original settings who reported no such problems. At the start, pole sitter Senna had too much wheelspin and Prost was able to take the lead. However, the first time through Eau Rouge Senna, with better downforce and grip, was clearly quicker and easily retook the lead after he slipstreamed Prost on the Kemmel Straight and out braked him at Les Combes. Senna powered off into the distance while Prost, struggling with a car that was no longer handling to his liking, settled for second place.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> managed to get his <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> up to 3rd, challenging Prost briefly before pitting on lap 3 with electrical problems. He managed to rejoin and set the fastest lap before retiring on lap 12. Due to Berger's retirement, Boutsen climbed to 4th behind Alboreto and ahead of a thrilling battle between <a href="/wiki/Alessandro_Nannini" title="Alessandro Nannini">Alessandro Nannini</a> (Benetton), Nakajima, <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a> (Lotus), and the two <a href="/wiki/Arrows_A10" title="Arrows A10">Arrows</a>-<a href="/wiki/BMW_M12" title="BMW M12">Megatrons</a> of <a href="/wiki/Derek_Warwick" title="Derek Warwick">Derek Warwick</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Cheever" title="Eddie Cheever">Eddie Cheever</a>. Piquet passed teammate Nakajima on lap 17, before the Japanese driver pitted with an acute misfire from a jammed plug, which caused him to retire. In the meantime, <a href="/wiki/Ivan_Capelli" title="Ivan Capelli">Ivan Capelli</a> climbed from ninth to sixth in his <a href="/wiki/March_Engineering" title="March Engineering">March</a>-<a href="/wiki/Judd_(engine)" title="Judd (engine)">Judd</a>.
</p><p>On lap 36, Alboreto's engine blew at Les Combes, sending him out of the race from third. On lap 38, Nannini passed Piquet for fourth at the La Source hairpin, before Capelli overtook the Brazilian driver for fifth on the penultimate lap.
</p><p>McLaren's 1-2 sealed the Constructors' Championship for the British team with five races remaining. Boutsen and Nannini came home third and fourth for Benetton, with Capelli and Piquet rounding out the top six. In a post-race interview, Prost virtually conceded the championship to Senna, who had won his fourth race in succession and had taken the championship lead for the first time.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1988_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Both Benettons were later disqualified for using irregular fuel, so the third podium spot went to Ivan Capelli. It was his first podium finish in Formula One and <a href="/wiki/March_Engineering" title="March Engineering">March</a>'s first podium finish since <a href="/wiki/Ronnie_Peterson" title="Ronnie Peterson">Ronnie Peterson</a> had won the <a href="/wiki/1976_Italian_Grand_Prix" title="1976 Italian Grand Prix">1976 Italian Grand Prix</a>. The post race disqualification of the Benettons also meant that the two Arrows of Warwick and Cheever went into the points.
</p><p>The disqualification of the Benettons was not made official until long after the season had finished, so many published records list them as having finished third and fourth. The three points Arrows gained from the disqualification brought the team to fifth in the Constructors' Championship.
</p>
