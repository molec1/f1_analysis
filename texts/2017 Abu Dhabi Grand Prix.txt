<p>The <b>2017 Abu Dhabi Grand Prix</b> (formally known as the <b>2017 Formula 1 Etihad Airways Abu Dhabi Grand Prix</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 26 November 2017 at the <a href="/wiki/Yas_Marina_Circuit" title="Yas Marina Circuit">Yas Marina Circuit</a> in <a href="/wiki/Abu_Dhabi" title="Abu Dhabi">Abu Dhabi</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_Arab_Emirates" title="United Arab Emirates">United Arab Emirates</a>. The race was the twentieth and final round of the <a href="/wiki/2017_FIA_Formula_One_World_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="2017 FIA Formula One World Championship">2017 FIA Formula One World Championship</a> and marked the ninth running of the <a href="/wiki/Abu_Dhabi_Grand_Prix" title="Abu Dhabi Grand Prix">Abu Dhabi Grand Prix</a> and the ninth time that the race has been run as a World Championship event since the inaugural season in <a href="/wiki/1950_Formula_One_season" title="1950 Formula One season">1950</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a> controlled the pace throughout the race to win from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Lewis Hamilton finished in close second place, with <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> finishing third. <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> retired due to mechanical failure, which was significant in facilitating fourth place for <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi R√§ikk√∂nen">Kimi R√§ikk√∂nen</a> in the Driver's Championship. This was also the final Grand Prix for both <a href="/wiki/Pascal_Wehrlein" title="Pascal Wehrlein">Pascal Wehrlein</a> and 11-time Grand Prix winner <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a>.
</p><p>The first <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_eSports_Series" class="mw-redirect" title="Formula One eSports Series">Formula One eSports</a> event was held in tandem with the Grand Prix, with British competitor Brendon Leigh winning the <a href="/wiki/2017_Formula_One_eSports_Series" title="2017 Formula One eSports Series">2017 competition</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2017_Abu_Dhabi_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2017_Abu_Dhabi_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>With both championships and the top places almost settled, the final race of the season would settle an intense three-way battle for sixth place between constructors <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso</a>, <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a> and <a href="/wiki/Haas_F1_Team" title="Haas F1 Team">Haas</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-forgrabs_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-forgrabs-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Toro Rosso was ahead by 4 points, but running with two recently signed drivers who were yet to score a point. Renault was then 2 points ahead of Haas. Also, fourth place in the Drivers' Championship was unsettled, contested by <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi R√§ikk√∂nen">Kimi R√§ikk√∂nen</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-forgrabs_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-forgrabs-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Ricciardo was 7 points ahead of R√§ikk√∂nen.
</p><p>The Abu Dhabi Grand Prix takes place at sunset, leading to great changes in track and air temperature. Free practices 1 and 3 are in afternoon heat, whereas free practice 2, qualifying and the race begin in the slightly cooler evening. The temperatures further evolve through the race, as it usually lasts two hours and spans the sunset.
</p><p>The weekend marked the final Formula One race for <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a>. Throughout his F1 career, Massa scored 11 wins, 16 pole positions, 15 fastest laps, 41 podiums, and 1167 career points. It was also the last Formula One race to date for <a href="/wiki/Pascal_Wehrlein" title="Pascal Wehrlein">Pascal Wehrlein</a>, as he returned to the <a href="/wiki/2018_Deutsche_Tourenwagen_Masters" title="2018 Deutsche Tourenwagen Masters">Deutsche Tourenwagen Masters</a>. This was also the last Formula One race broadcast by <a href="/wiki/NBC_Sports" title="NBC Sports">NBC Sports</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>; the rights went to <a href="/wiki/ESPN" title="ESPN">ESPN</a> for 2018.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Free_practice">Free practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2017_Abu_Dhabi_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Free practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> set the fastest lap time in FP1 for <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, just over a tenth of a second faster than <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a>' <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> in second, with <a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a> close behind in his <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton bettered Vettel's fastest lap time in FP2, followed by Ricciardo's Red Bull. Hamilton retained the fastest lap time in FP3. During this session, the lap times increased in speed. At about the half-way point, R√§ikk√∂nen's Ferrari bettered Hamilton's time and Vettel's came within a whisker. After this, Hamilton again improved his time, and <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a> improved to second, for a Mercedes one-two.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton's final fastest lap was almost 3 tenths faster than Bottas in second and over half a second faster than both R√§ikk√∂nen in third and Vettel in fourth. Red Bull set fifth and sixth and <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> set seventh and eighth.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2017_Abu_Dhabi_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>All cars were fitted with Pirelli ultrasoft tyres for Q1. Hamilton and Bottas traded lap times whilst R√§ikk√∂nen appeared to outpace Vettel in the following two positions. In Q2, Hamilton set a marginally faster time than Bottas, whilst Vettel likewise pipped R√§ikk√∂nen. In both sessions, the Red Bulls were further behind, with Ricciardo in fifth and Verstappen in sixth.
</p><p>The final qualifying round saw Bottas set a faster time than Hamilton early, with neither improving. Hamilton qualified in P2, with Vettel remaining ahead of R√§ikk√∂nen. An unexpected change to the order arose when Ricciardo set a late lap ahead of R√§ikk√∂nen taking P4. R√§ikk√∂nen was in P5 and Verstappen in P6. Further back, Renault's <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico H√ºlkenberg">Nico H√ºlkenberg</a> qualified ahead of the <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force Indias</a>, amongst which <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio P√©rez">Sergio P√©rez</a> led <a href="/wiki/Esteban_Ocon" title="Esteban Ocon">Esteban Ocon</a>. <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> completed the top-ten.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2017_Abu_Dhabi_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After the front-runners all made a good start, positions at the front of the field did not change from the start grid into the opening laps. <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> driver Bottas retained first place from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>. Hamilton followed Bottas closely, able to challenge for position at times. Hamilton later congratulated Bottas on his dominant performance, saying the former 'gave it everything, every single lap'.<sup id="cite_ref-postracePC_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-postracePC-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Bottas said that he had been "managing the pace and that way the race", and "it was a nice feeling, a really nice feeling",<sup id="cite_ref-postracePC_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-postracePC-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> suggested he could have pushed harder. Indeed, Bottas set the fastest lap of the race in the closing stages.
</p><p>Behind, Vettel remained in third place through most of the race, largely neither challenged for the podium nor mounting a convincing challenge against Hamilton's second place. R√§ikk√∂nen put his nose ahead of Ricciardo on the opening lap, but Ricciardo held fourth place until retiring due to a hydraulic failure on lap 21. Both Toro Rosso drivers spun off the track, but rejoined to complete the race as back-markers. All cars ran a one-stop strategy. <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Sainz_Jr." title="Carlos Sainz Jr.">Carlos Sainz Jr.</a>, one of the later cars to pit, was released by Renault with the left-front wheel loose. Sainz made it out of the pitlane before retiring, and Renault incurred a fine for the unsafe release. The other Renault driver <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico H√ºlkenberg">Nico H√ºlkenberg</a> incurred a 5-second stop-go penalty early in the race for gaining an advantage cutting a corner. Later serving the penalty during a scheduled pit-stop, there was a delay removing the right-rear wheel reducing his track position to 11th. He fought back up to 6th place to secure the points required to lift Renault up to sixth in the Constructors' Championship.
</p><p>The top three remained the same throughout most of the race, Hamilton taking the lead briefly between Bottas' earlier pit-stop and his own pit-stop. Bottas won the race. Hamilton finished in close second place, with Vettel finishing third. Ricciardo's retirement was significant in handing the fourth place in the Drivers' Championship to R√§ikk√∂nen. Excluding the retired Ricciardo, the top eight places were ordered identically to the starting grid. <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> had overtaken Massa for ninth. Massa finished in tenth (the last points-paying) position in the final Formula One Grand Prix of his career.
</p>
