<p>The <b>2001 Spanish Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>XLIII Gran Premio Marlboro de Espana</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 29 April 2001 at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Catalunya" class="mw-redirect" title="Circuit de Catalunya">Circuit de Catalunya</a>, <a href="/wiki/Barcelona" title="Barcelona">Barcelona</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>. It was the fifth round of the <a href="/wiki/2001_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2001 Formula One season">2001 Formula One season</a>. The 65-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> driving a <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> car after starting from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>. <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a> finished second driving a <a href="/wiki/Williams_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams F1">Williams</a> car with <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a> third for the BAR team.
</p><p>The race was Schumacher's third win of the season, and the result that he led the Drivers' Championship, eight points ahead of Coulthard and twenty-two ahead of <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a>. Ferrari extended their lead in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a>, eighteen points ahead of <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> and thirty-two ahead of Williams, with 12 races of the season remaining.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2001_Spanish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2001_Spanish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Grand Prix was contested by 22 drivers, in eleven teams of two.<sup id="cite_ref-entrylist_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-entrylist-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The teams, also known as <a href="/wiki/Formula_One#Constructors" title="Formula One">constructors</a>, were <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>, <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a>, <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a>, <a href="/wiki/British_American_Racing" title="British American Racing">BAR</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arrows_Grand_Prix_International" title="Arrows Grand Prix International">Arrows</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jaguar_Racing" title="Jaguar Racing">Jaguar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Prost_Grand_Prix" title="Prost Grand Prix">Prost</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-entrylist_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-entrylist-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Before the race, Ferrari driver <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> and McLaren driver <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> were tied for the lead of the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a> with 26 points each. Behind them in the Drivers' Championship, <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> was third on 14 points in the other Ferrari, with <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> on 12 and 7 points respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-championship_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-championship-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a>, Ferrari were leading on 40 points and McLaren were second on 30 points, with Williams third on 12 points.<sup id="cite_ref-championship_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-championship-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Following the <a href="/wiki/2001_San_Marino_Grand_Prix" title="2001 San Marino Grand Prix">San Marino Grand Prix</a> on April 15, several teams conducted testing sessions at the <a href="/wiki/Silverstone_Circuit" title="Silverstone Circuit">Silverstone</a> circuit from April 17–19. <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a> (Jordan) set the fastest time on the first day, while <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> (McLaren) topped the second and final day's running. Sauber, Ferrari and Minardi opted to test at the <a href="/wiki/Fiorano_Circuit" title="Fiorano Circuit">Fiorano</a> circuit from April 18–20. <a href="/wiki/Luca_Badoer" title="Luca Badoer">Luca Badoer</a> topped the first day's running, while Michael Schumacher was fastest on the second and final days of testing. During the testing sessions, several teams tested out <a href="/wiki/Traction_control_system" title="Traction control system">traction control</a> which was being re-introduced. The system was banned at the end of the <a href="/wiki/1993_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="1993 Formula One season">1993 season</a>, due to fears of cheating.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>There were two driver changes heading into the race. Having been in one of the two Jaguar cars since the start of the season, <a href="/wiki/Luciano_Burti" title="Luciano Burti">Luciano Burti</a> was released from the team and replaced by <a href="/wiki/Pedro_de_la_Rosa" title="Pedro de la Rosa">Pedro de la Rosa</a>. The second driver change was Burti moving to the Prost team replacing <a href="/wiki/Gast%C3%B3n_Mazzacane" title="Gastón Mazzacane">Gastón Mazzacane</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Prost attempted to replace Mazzacane with Jordan test driver <a href="/wiki/Ricardo_Zonta" title="Ricardo Zonta">Ricardo Zonta</a>, among others.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice_and_qualifying">Practice and qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2001_Spanish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Practice and qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:David_Coulthard_2007.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d8/David_Coulthard_2007.jpg/170px-David_Coulthard_2007.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="250" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d8/David_Coulthard_2007.jpg/255px-David_Coulthard_2007.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d8/David_Coulthard_2007.jpg/340px-David_Coulthard_2007.jpg 2x" data-file-width="500" data-file-height="734" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:David_Coulthard_2007.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>David Coulthard, who qualified third, despite suffering from car balance issues (picture taken in 2007, while driving at <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a>)</div></div></div>
<p>Four practice sessions were held before the Sunday race—two on Friday, and two on Saturday. The Friday morning and afternoon sessions each lasted an hour. The third and final practice sessions were held on Saturday morning and lasted 45 minutes.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The first Friday session was held in dry and sunny conditions, becoming clouder during the day.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard was fastest in the first session, with a time of 1:20.107 that was half a second faster than Barrichello. Michael Schumacher, Ralf Schumacher, Häkkinen and <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Panis" title="Olivier Panis">Olivier Panis</a> rounded out the top six.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> In the second practice session, Coulthard, was quickest with a time of 1:20.107, ahead of <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a>, Barrichello, Panis, Michael Schumacher and Häkkinen, who were within a second of Coulthard's time.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The Saturday morning sessions were again held in dry and sunny conditions.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher was quickest in the third practice session with a time of 1:18.634; Häkkinen was second fastest despite going off into the gravel late in the session. Coulthard, Barrichello, <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> and Frentzen rounded off the top six.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher again set the fastest time in the fourth practice session, with a time identical to his fastest in the third practice session that was less than five thousands of a second faster than Barrichello. Coulthard was just off both Ferrari drivers pace. The Sauber drivers were running quicker—<a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> in fourth and Heidfeld in fifth—with Trulli completing the top six in his Jordan.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Saturday's afternoon qualifying session lasted for an hour. During this session, the <a href="/wiki/107%25_rule" title="107% rule">107% rule</a> was in effect, which necessitated each driver set a time within 107% of the quickest lap to qualify for the race. Each driver was limited to twelve laps.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Michael Schumacher clinched his fourth <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> of the season with a time of 1:18.201. He was joined on the front row by Häkkinen who was less than one-tenth of a second behind.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard qualified third, though he struggled with the balance on his car, he was confident on beginning the race on the <a href="/wiki/Racing_line" title="Racing line">racing line</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello qualified fourth, half a second behind Michael Schumacher, having battled both McLaren drivers for time during the late stages of qualifying. Ralf Schumacher qualified fifth. Trulli, <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a>, Frentzen, Räikkönen and Heidfeld rounded out the top ten.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2001_Spanish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>The conditions were dry and overcast for the race with the <a href="/wiki/Air_temperature" class="mw-redirect" title="Air temperature">air temperature</a> 18&#160;°C (64&#160;°F) and the track temperature 19&#160;°C (66&#160;°F).<sup id="cite_ref-galeforcef1race_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-galeforcef1race-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> The drivers took to the track at 09:30 (<a href="/wiki/Greenwich_Mean_Time" title="Greenwich Mean Time">GMT</a> +1) for a 30-minute warm-up session.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Both Ferrari cars maintained their good pace from qualifying; Michael Schumacher had the fastest time of 1:20.680. Barrichello was fourth in the other Ferrari car; Häkkinen and Coulthard split them for second and third positions respectively, despite the latter's car suffering from an engine failure. Frentzen and Panis rounded out the top six with identical lap times.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The race started at 14:00 local time.<sup id="cite_ref-galeforcef1race_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-galeforcef1race-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard stalled his car at the start of the parade lap, forcing him to start at the back of the grid. Michael Schumacher made a good start, retaining his first position; Häkkinen behind him maintained his second position.<sup id="cite_ref-atlasf1race_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasf1race-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Frentzen, from eighth, made a poor start losing fourteen places.<sup id="cite_ref-galeforcef1race_16-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-galeforcef1race-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard sustained damage to his front wing in a minor collision, resulting in a pitstop.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcf1race_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcf1race-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> At the completion of the first lap, Michael Schumacher led from Häkkinen, Barrichello, Ralf Schumacher, Trulli, Montoya, Villeneuve, Heidfeld, <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a>, Räikkönen, Panis, <a href="/wiki/Jos_Verstappen" title="Jos Verstappen">Jos Verstappen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a>, Burti, Fisichella, <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pedro_de_la_Rosa" title="Pedro de la Rosa">Pedro de la Rosa</a>, Frentzen, <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tarso_Marques" title="Tarso Marques">Tarso Marques</a>, <a href="/wiki/Enrique_Bernoldi" title="Enrique Bernoldi">Enrique Bernoldi</a> and Coulthard.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Michael Schumacher and Häkkinen gradually began to pull away from Barrichello. On lap 3, Panis overtook Räikkönen for 10th position and Alonso dropped to 16th position having been overtaken by de la Rosa and Frentzen. As the race progressed, Michael Schumacher began to open a small lead over Häkkinen. On lap 6, Frentzen attempted to overtake de la Rosa around turn 10 for 16th position, with both retiring from the race, as a result of a collision between both drivers.<sup id="cite_ref-atlasf1race_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasf1race-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the closing laps, Häkkinen increased his deficit over Michael Schumacher to over 40 seconds, and it appeared that the McLaren driver would win the race comfortably. However, as Häkkinen started the final lap, his engine failed without warning, and was forced to coast around the circuit before pulling off at turn seven.<sup id="cite_ref-atlasf1race_18-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasf1race-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher was thus promoted into the lead, which he held to take his third victory of the season in a time of 1'31:03.305, at an average speed of 125.832 miles per hour (202.507&#160;km/h). Montoya likewise claimed the first Formula One podium of his career, with second position. Villeneuve took the final place on the podium. Trulli managed to fend off Coulthard for fourth position, with Heidfeld completing the points scoring positions in sixth. Panis and Räikkönen finished seventh and eighth respectively. Despite not finishing the race, Häkkinen was classified ninth. The lapped cars of Alesi, Burti, Verstappen, Alonso, Fisichella, Button and Marques were the last of the finishing drivers.<sup id="cite_ref-galeforcef1race_16-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-galeforcef1race-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2001_Spanish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The top three drivers appeared on the <a href="/wiki/Podium#Usage_in_motorsport" title="Podium">podium</a> and in the subsequent <a href="/wiki/Press_conference" title="Press conference">press conference</a>. Although Michael Schumacher was pleased with his race performance, he expressed sympathy over Häkkinen's retirement. After the race he said, "I simply said sorry and I wanted to apologise. It belongs to him to be sitting here, he hasn't made any mistakes and sometimes racing is hard but to some degree that's the way racing is. It's a shame for him it happened so short before the end, if it happens sometimes 20 laps in the race it's less hard to take but this way must be very shocking for him".<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>McLaren team principal <a href="/wiki/Ron_Dennis" title="Ron Dennis">Ron Dennis</a> said Coulthard's stall on the parade lap was caused by a computer glitch. He also stated that Coulthard was not to blame for the incident, despite initial criticism of the Scot, due to a lack of information regarding the problem.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Dennis also revealed the cause of Hakkinen's engine failure which was down to an hydraulic leak, leading to the clutch failing to operate.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The race result left Michael Schumacher leading the Drivers' Championship with 36 points. Coulthard was second on 28 points, fourteen points ahead of Barrichello and sixteen ahead of Ralf Schumacher.<sup id="cite_ref-championship_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-championship-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> In the Constructors' Championship, Ferrari extended their lead with 50 points, McLaren maintained second with 32 points, with Williams maintaining third with 18 points, with 12 races of the season remaining.<sup id="cite_ref-championship_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-championship-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
