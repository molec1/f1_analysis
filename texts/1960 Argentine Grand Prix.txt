<p>The <b>1960 Argentine Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held at <a href="/wiki/Aut%C3%B3dromo_Juan_y_Oscar_G%C3%A1lvez" title="Autódromo Juan y Oscar Gálvez">Buenos Aires</a> on 7 February 1960. It was race 1 of 10 in the <a href="/wiki/1960_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1960 World Championship of Drivers">1960 World Championship of Drivers</a> and race 1 of 9 in the <a href="/wiki/1960_International_Cup_for_Formula_One_Manufacturers" class="mw-redirect" title="1960 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers">1960 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers</a>. This was the last <a href="/wiki/Argentine_Grand_Prix" title="Argentine Grand Prix">Argentine Grand Prix</a> until <a href="/wiki/1972_Argentine_Grand_Prix" title="1972 Argentine Grand Prix">1972</a>, and the last to be held on the No. 4 configuration of the circuit.
</p><p>The race was won by <a href="/wiki/Bruce_McLaren" title="Bruce McLaren">Bruce McLaren</a>, his second consecutive victory, having won his first at <a href="/wiki/1959_United_States_Grand_Prix" title="1959 United States Grand Prix">Sebring</a> at the end of the <a href="/wiki/1959_Formula_One_season" title="1959 Formula One season">1959 season</a>. <a href="/wiki/Stirling_Moss" title="Stirling Moss">Stirling Moss</a> suffered a suspension failure while leading, and took over <a href="/wiki/Maurice_Trintignant" title="Maurice Trintignant">Maurice Trintignant</a>'s Cooper, but was not awarded points for third place as a result of the shared drive, a rule that had been in place since <a href="/wiki/1958_Formula_One_season" title="1958 Formula One season">1958</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Venezuelan" class="mw-redirect" title="Venezuelan">Venezuelan</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Ettore_Chimeri" title="Ettore Chimeri">Ettore Chimeri</a> participated in his only grand prix. He was killed two weeks later driving a <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_250_TR" class="mw-redirect" title="Ferrari 250 TR">Ferrari 250 TR</a> in Havana. He was the first driver from his country in Formula One, and the last until <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Cecotto" title="Johnny Cecotto">Johnny Cecotto</a> in <a href="/wiki/1983_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="1983 Formula One season">1983</a>. It was the only grand prix appearances for <a href="/wiki/Antonio_Creus" title="Antonio Creus">Antonio Creus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alberto_Rodriguez_Larreta" title="Alberto Rodriguez Larreta">Alberto Rodriguez Larreta</a> and <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Bonomi" title="Roberto Bonomi">Roberto Bonomi</a>. It was also the last grand prix appearances for the driver who scored Ferrari's first World Championship race victory, <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Froil%C3%A1n_Gonz%C3%A1lez" title="José Froilán González">José Froilán González</a>, and experienced American driver <a href="/wiki/Harry_Schell" title="Harry Schell">Harry Schell</a>, who would be killed during practice for a non-championship race at <a href="/wiki/Silverstone_Circuit" title="Silverstone Circuit">Silverstone</a>.
</p><p>Three other local drivers took part in the practice sessions for the race. The Scuderia Centro Sud Maserati (№ 10) was driven in the first practice session by <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pedro_Llano&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Pedro Llano (page does not exist)">Pedro Llano</a>, as well as by eventual race driver <a href="/wiki/Nasif_Est%C3%A9fano" title="Nasif Estéfano">Nasif Estéfano</a> and Rodriguez Larreta. In the second practice session, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Julio_Pola&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Julio Pola (page does not exist)">Julio Pola</a> shared Chimeri's № 44 Maserati. Also, <a href="/wiki/Oscar_Cabal%C3%A9n" title="Oscar Cabalén">Oscar Cabalén</a> shared Estéfano's car in the qualifying session as a reserve driver.
</p>
