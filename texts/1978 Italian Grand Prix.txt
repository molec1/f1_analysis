<p>The <b>1978 Italian Grand Prix</b> was the 14th motor race of the <a href="/wiki/1978_Formula_One_season" title="1978 Formula One season">1978 Formula One season</a>. It was held on 10 September 1978 at <a href="/wiki/Autodromo_Nazionale_di_Monza" title="Autodromo Nazionale di Monza">Monza</a>. It was marred by the death of <a href="/wiki/Ronnie_Peterson" title="Ronnie Peterson">Ronnie Peterson</a> following an accident at the start of the race.
</p><p>With three races remaining, <a href="/wiki/Mario_Andretti" title="Mario Andretti">Mario Andretti</a> (<a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Lotus</a>-<a href="/wiki/Ford_Motor_Company" title="Ford Motor Company">Ford</a>) led the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">World Drivers' Championship</a> by 12 points from his team-mate <a href="/wiki/Ronnie_Peterson" title="Ronnie Peterson">Ronnie Peterson</a>. <a href="/wiki/Niki_Lauda" title="Niki Lauda">Niki Lauda</a> (<a href="/wiki/Brabham" title="Brabham">Brabham</a>-<a href="/wiki/Alfa_Romeo_(Formula_One)" class="mw-redirect" title="Alfa Romeo (Formula One)">Alfa Romeo</a>), in third place, was 28 points behind Andretti, and, with only 9 points for a win, could not overtake him.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1978_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="First_start_and_Ronnie_Peterson_accident">First start and Ronnie Peterson accident</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1978_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: First start and Ronnie Peterson accident">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:1978_Italian_Grand_Prix_accident.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2b/1978_Italian_Grand_Prix_accident.jpg/220px-1978_Italian_Grand_Prix_accident.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2b/1978_Italian_Grand_Prix_accident.jpg/330px-1978_Italian_Grand_Prix_accident.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2b/1978_Italian_Grand_Prix_accident.jpg/440px-1978_Italian_Grand_Prix_accident.jpg 2x" data-file-width="850" data-file-height="567" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:1978_Italian_Grand_Prix_accident.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Ronnie_Peterson" title="Ronnie Peterson">Ronnie Peterson</a>'s fatal accident at the start of the race</div></div></div>
<p>The race started at 3:30<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>pm, and the starter <a href="/w/index.php?title=Gianni_Restelli&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Gianni Restelli (page does not exist)">Gianni Restelli</a> was overenthusiastic turning on the red lights before all the cars had lined up,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> that resulted in several cars in the middle of the field getting a jump on those at the front. The result was a funneling effect of the cars approaching the chicane, and the cars were tightly bunched together with little room for maneuver. <a href="/wiki/James_Hunt" title="James Hunt">James Hunt</a> was overtaken on the right-hand side by <a href="/wiki/Riccardo_Patrese" title="Riccardo Patrese">Riccardo Patrese</a>, and Hunt instinctively veered left and hit the rear right wheel of Peterson's <a href="/wiki/Lotus_78" title="Lotus 78">Lotus 78</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Vittorio_Brambilla" title="Vittorio Brambilla">Vittorio Brambilla</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hans-Joachim_Stuck" title="Hans-Joachim Stuck">Hans-Joachim Stuck</a>, <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Depailler" title="Patrick Depailler">Patrick Depailler</a>, <a href="/wiki/Didier_Pironi" title="Didier Pironi">Didier Pironi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Derek_Daly" title="Derek Daly">Derek Daly</a>, <a href="/wiki/Clay_Regazzoni" title="Clay Regazzoni">Clay Regazzoni</a> and <a href="/wiki/Brett_Lunger" title="Brett Lunger">Brett Lunger</a> all involved in the ensuing melee. Peterson's Lotus went into the barriers hard on the right-hand side and caught fire. He was trapped, but Hunt, Regazzoni and Depailler managed to free him from the wreck before he received more than minor burns. He was dragged free and laid in the middle of the track fully conscious, but with severe leg injuries. It took 20 minutes before medical help was dispatched to the scene. Brambilla, who had been hit on the head by a flying wheel and rendered unconscious, and Peterson were taken to the Niguarda hospital in nearby <a href="/wiki/Milan" title="Milan">Milan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Delayed_restart">Delayed restart</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1978_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Delayed restart">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Incidente_al_GP_Monza_1978.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d1/Incidente_al_GP_Monza_1978.jpg/220px-Incidente_al_GP_Monza_1978.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d1/Incidente_al_GP_Monza_1978.jpg/330px-Incidente_al_GP_Monza_1978.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d1/Incidente_al_GP_Monza_1978.jpg/440px-Incidente_al_GP_Monza_1978.jpg 2x" data-file-width="597" data-file-height="398" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Incidente_al_GP_Monza_1978.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The accident scene after the flames have been extinguished</div></div></div>
<p>Drivers were allowed to use spare cars, non starters included Peterson and Brambilla who were taken to hospital, Stuck was not allowed to restart due to him suffering from slight concussion, Pironi was a non starter as the Tyrrell team had one spare car and that was set up for Depailler and Lunger was a non starter as he had no spare car available.
</p><p>The race was due to be ready for a restart at 5:15<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>pm. While driving from the pit lane to the grid, <a href="/wiki/Jody_Scheckter" title="Jody Scheckter">Jody Scheckter</a>'s Wolf lost a wheel and crashed at the second Lesmo curve, bending the <a href="/wiki/Armco_barrier" class="mw-redirect" title="Armco barrier">Armco barrier</a> that was situated right next to the track. Andretti, Hunt, Lauda, <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Reutemann" title="Carlos Reutemann">Carlos Reutemann</a> and <a href="/wiki/Emerson_Fittipaldi" title="Emerson Fittipaldi">Emerson Fittipaldi</a> all went to the spot where Scheckter crashed and upon inspection of the state of the barrier, they refused to start until it was repaired, causing more delay. The barrier was repaired and ready to race.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Second_start_and_race_recap">Second start and race recap</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1978_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Second start and race recap">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Because of the amount of time clearing up the track after the shunt in the first start and the barrier being repaired plus Scheckter's crash prior to the second start. At 5:50<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>pm, it was announced that the race would take place and the distance would be shortened from 52 laps to 40 laps. The race was restarted at 6:15<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>pm, Villeneuve overtook Andretti at the restart and at the end of the lap, both drivers were side by side but Villeneuve held on to the lead and they pulled away from Jabouille who was running third with Lauda behind him. After four laps, Regazzoni in the spare Shadow went to the pits as he was having his brakes looked at. Jabouille had engine problems after six laps and Lauda took on the pursuit, but race control gave Villeneuve and Andretti a one-minute penalty as they were judged to have jumped the start. Andretti took Villenuve with only five laps remaining. With Jabouille having retired, Lauda finished third ahead of <a href="/wiki/John_Watson_(racing_driver)" title="John Watson (racing driver)">John Watson</a> (Brabham), <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Reutemann" title="Carlos Reutemann">Carlos Reutemann</a> (Ferrari), <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Laffite" title="Jacques Laffite">Jacques Laffite</a> (<a href="/wiki/Equipe_Ligier" title="Equipe Ligier">Ligier</a>-<a href="/wiki/Equipe_Matra_Sports" title="Equipe Matra Sports">Matra</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Tambay" title="Patrick Tambay">Patrick Tambay</a> (<a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Ford_Motor_Company" title="Ford Motor Company">Ford</a>). Since all of those finished less than a minute behind, Andretti and Villeneuve were dropped to sixth and seventh place. Andretti had won the championship, but with Peterson in hospital, celebrations were muted. The race was also notable for the first finish for <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a> with a creditable ninth after dodging the Peterson wreckage in the first start.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Death_of_Ronnie_Peterson">Death of Ronnie Peterson</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1978_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Death of Ronnie Peterson">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the hospital, Peterson's X-rays showed he had seven fractures in one leg and three in the other. After discussion with him, Peterson was sent to intensive care so that the surgeons could operate to stabilize the bones.<sup id="cite_ref-death_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-death-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> There was some level of dispute between the doctors regarding whether all fractures should be immediately fixed or not.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> During the night, Peterson's condition worsened, and he was diagnosed with <a href="/wiki/Fat_embolism" class="mw-redirect" title="Fat embolism">fat embolism</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ronniepeterson.se_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ronniepeterson.se-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> By morning he was in full <a href="/wiki/Kidney_failure" title="Kidney failure">kidney failure</a> due to the embolism, and was declared dead at 9:55<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>am on 11 September 1978.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ronniepeterson.se_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ronniepeterson.se-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1978_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<table class="box-Trivia plainlinks metadata ambox ambox-style" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-image"><div style="width:52px"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/f/f2/Edit-clear.svg/40px-Edit-clear.svg.png" decoding="async" width="40" height="40" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/f/f2/Edit-clear.svg/60px-Edit-clear.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/f/f2/Edit-clear.svg/80px-Edit-clear.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="48" data-file-height="48" /></div></td><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This section contains a <b><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Trivia_sections" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Trivia sections">list of miscellaneous information</a>.</b><span class="hide-when-compact"> Please <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Handling_trivia#Recommendations_for_handling_trivia" title="Wikipedia:Handling trivia">relocate</a> any relevant information into other sections or articles.</span>  <small class="date-container"><i>(<span class="date">August 2020</span>)</i></small></div></td></tr></tbody></table>
<ul><li>Lap leaders: <a href="/wiki/Jean-Pierre_Jabouille" title="Jean-Pierre Jabouille">Jean-Pierre Jabouille</a> 5 laps (1–5); <a href="/wiki/Niki_Lauda" title="Niki Lauda">Niki Lauda</a> 35 laps (6–40).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Mario_Andretti" title="Mario Andretti">Mario Andretti</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gilles_Villeneuve" title="Gilles Villeneuve">Gilles Villeneuve</a> finished 1st and 2nd on the road but were assessed a 1-minute penalty for a jump start.</li>
<li>Due to the long delay to clean up the debris from the opening lap accident, the race was shortened to 40 laps from the original 52, to avoid dusk.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Jody_Scheckter" title="Jody Scheckter">Jody Scheckter</a> spun his <a href="/wiki/Walter_Wolf_Racing" title="Walter Wolf Racing">Wolf</a> at the Curve Di Lesmo on the <a href="/wiki/Formation_lap" class="mw-redirect" title="Formation lap">formation lap</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Harald_Ertl" title="Harald Ertl">Harald Ertl</a> failed to pre-qualify his Ensign and got another chance with the German ATS of injured <a href="/wiki/Jochen_Mass" title="Jochen Mass">Jochen Mass</a>.</li>
<li>Last race: <a href="/wiki/McLaren_M23" title="McLaren M23">McLaren M23</a> (driven by <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a>)</li>
<li>Peterson's death guaranteed Andretti the Driver's Championship with two races left.</li>
<li>Last Formula One race win for <a href="/wiki/Alfa_Romeo_in_Formula_One" title="Alfa Romeo in Formula One">Alfa Romeo</a> either as a manufacturer or engine supplier.</li>
<li>This was <a href="/wiki/Niki_Lauda" title="Niki Lauda">Niki Lauda</a>'s last victory until the <a href="/wiki/1982_United_States_Grand_Prix_West" title="1982 United States Grand Prix West">1982 United States Grand Prix West</a>.</li>
<li>This was also the final time that <a href="/wiki/Brabham" title="Brabham">Brabham</a> scored a 1–2 until the <a href="/wiki/1982_Canadian_Grand_Prix" title="1982 Canadian Grand Prix">1982 Canadian Grand Prix</a>.</li></ul>
