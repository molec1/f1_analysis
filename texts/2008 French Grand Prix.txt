<p>The <b>2008 French Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>XCIV Grand Prix de France</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 22 June 2008 at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Nevers_Magny-Cours" title="Circuit de Nevers Magny-Cours">Circuit de Nevers Magny-Cours</a>, <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>. This race would be the last French Grand Prix for a decade, before returning in <a href="/wiki/2018_French_Grand_Prix" title="2018 French Grand Prix">2018</a> at <a href="/wiki/Circuit_Paul_Ricard" title="Circuit Paul Ricard">Circuit Paul Ricard</a>. It was the eighth&#160;race of the <a href="/wiki/2008_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2008 Formula One season">2008 Formula One season</a>. The 70-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> team after starting from second position. <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a>, who started from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>, finished second in the other Ferrari car; <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> was third in a <a href="/wiki/Toyota_Racing_(Formula_One_team)" title="Toyota Racing (Formula One team)">Toyota</a>.
</p><p>Räikkönen and Massa both made a clean start. <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>, who started third, was overtaken by Trulli and <a href="/wiki/BMW_Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="BMW Sauber">BMW Sauber</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a>. The front three of Räikkönen, Massa and Trulli maintained their positions through the first round of <a href="/wiki/Pit_stop" title="Pit stop">pit stops</a>. On lap 30,&#160;Räikkönen led Massa by six and a half seconds, and Trulli by 30&#160;seconds. Just before half distance, Räikkönen's right exhaust pipe broke, which caused the engine to lose power. Massa, in second place, began lapping quicker than Räikkönen, and he caught and passed him on lap&#160;39. Massa maintained his lead through the second round of pit stops, and won the race; Räikkönen finished almost 18&#160;seconds behind. Trulli fended off <a href="/wiki/McLaren_(racing)" class="mw-redirect" title="McLaren (racing)">McLaren</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a>, who challenged him in the latter stages, to take third.
</p><p>Massa's win promoted him into the lead of the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a> for the first time in his career, overtaking Kubica. Kubica was second, two points behind Massa, while Räikkönen was third. In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a>, Ferrari increased their lead to 17&#160;points ahead of BMW Sauber, McLaren a further 16&#160;points behind in third.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2008_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Grand Prix was contested by 20&#160;drivers, in ten teams of two.<sup id="cite_ref-classification_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-classification-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The teams, also known as "<a href="/wiki/Formula_One#Constructors" title="Formula One">constructors</a>", were <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, <a href="/wiki/McLaren_(racing)" class="mw-redirect" title="McLaren (racing)">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_HighPerformanceEngines" class="mw-redirect" title="Mercedes-Benz HighPerformanceEngines">Mercedes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a>, <a href="/wiki/Honda_Racing_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Honda Racing F1">Honda</a>, <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a>-Ferrari, <a href="/wiki/BMW_Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="BMW Sauber">BMW Sauber</a>, <a href="/wiki/Toyota_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Toyota F1">Toyota</a>, <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a>-Renault, <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a>-Toyota and <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso</a>-Ferrari.<sup id="cite_ref-classification_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-classification-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_tyres" title="Formula One tyres">Tyre</a> supplier <a href="/wiki/Bridgestone" title="Bridgestone">Bridgestone</a> brought two different tyre compounds to the race; the softer of the two marked by a single white stripe down one of the grooves.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Before the race, <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a> of BMW led the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a>, with 42&#160;<a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Championship_points_scoring_systems" title="List of Formula One World Championship points scoring systems">points</a>, ahead of McLaren's <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> and Ferrari's <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a>, who each had 38&#160;points. Massa's teammate <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> was fourth, ahead of Kubica's teammate <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> in fifth. In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a>, Ferrari were leading with 73 points; three points ahead of BMW Sauber with 70&#160;points; McLaren were a further 17&#160;points behind them in third.<sup id="cite_ref-autocourse2008-pg160-61_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autocourse2008-pg160-61-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Ferrari came into the race with a long series of success at the track, having won seven of the last ten races held at Magny-Cours.<sup id="cite_ref-Ferrari_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ferrari-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/2007_French_Grand_Prix" title="2007 French Grand Prix">2007</a>, Ferrari had taken a <a href="/wiki/List_of_motorsport_terminology#O" class="mw-redirect" title="List of motorsport terminology">one-two</a>, Räikkönen ahead of Massa.<sup id="cite_ref-Ferrari_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ferrari-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Massa said that it was important not to discount McLaren and BMW:
</p>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r960796168">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>In recent years, people would say that <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Grand_Prix" title="Canadian Grand Prix">Canada</a> and <a href="/wiki/Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="Monaco Grand Prix">Monaco</a> suited McLaren better than Ferrari, while it was the other way round in France and <a href="/wiki/British_Grand_Prix" title="British Grand Prix">Britain</a>, but I don't think that is really the case this year, as apart from any other factors, we have to consider the BMW team in this equation. <a href="/wiki/2008_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="2008 Monaco Grand Prix">This year in Monaco</a>, Ferrari had the whole front row of the grid, even though I think we had more fuel than McLaren and in <a href="/wiki/2008_Canadian_Grand_Prix" title="2008 Canadian Grand Prix">Canada</a> our race pace was very good too. In other races we have all been very close.<sup id="cite_ref-Ferrari_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ferrari-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>
<p>In March 2007, the <i><a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Fran%C3%A7aise_du_Sport_Automobile" class="mw-redirect" title="Fédération Française du Sport Automobile">Fédération Française du Sport Automobile</a></i> (FFSA) stated their intention to rest the Magny-Cours circuit from the Formula One world championship for the <a href="/wiki/2008_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2008 Formula One season">2008 season</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Despite this, the race was held in 2008, but the race was dropped from the Formula One calendar for 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The French Grand Prix would not return to the Formula One World Championship calendar until <a href="/wiki/2018_FIA_Formula_One_World_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="2018 FIA Formula One World Championship">2018</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At the previous race in Canada, Hamilton had crashed into Räikkönen in the pit lane, when Raikkönën braked to stop in front of the red light at the end of the pit lane. Williams driver <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> then collided into the back of Hamilton.<sup id="cite_ref-Penalty_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Penalty-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton later said that he saw the light too late and could not avoid hitting the Ferrari.<sup id="cite_ref-Penalty_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Penalty-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton and Rosberg were both given ten place grid penalties for the French Grand Prix, meaning that whatever their qualifying position, they could start no better than 11th.<sup id="cite_ref-Penalty_11-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Penalty-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> After the penalty was given, McLaren's CEO, <a href="/wiki/Martin_Whitmarsh" title="Martin Whitmarsh">Martin Whitmarsh</a>, said that he thought the penalty was "severe", citing a similar incident at <a href="/wiki/2008_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="2008 Monaco Grand Prix">Monaco</a> where Raikkönën had crashed into the back of <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Pen_McL_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pen_McL-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> There, no penalty had been given.<sup id="cite_ref-Pen_McL_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pen_McL-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> However, Rosberg said that the penalties from Canada were "deserved".<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> When Hamilton was asked whether the penalty would force him to change his approach, Hamilton said that "it doesn't really. It's a race, I'm here to win and so I approach it the same."<sup id="cite_ref-Hamilton_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hamilton-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> "It's going to be harder," he added, "coming from the back, but I don't have any doubts or any worries, I think we're going to have a very strong package this weekend, and I think the car will be as good if not better than it was in the last race. With that pace, as long as we stay out of trouble we should be able to score some good points."<sup id="cite_ref-Hamilton_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hamilton-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Renault driver <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet_Jr." title="Nelson Piquet Jr.">Nelson Piquet Jr.</a>, who was in his first year in Formula One and had scored no points prior to this race, said that he thought that the track suited him and the car much better than previous races.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> BMW Sauber had taken their first victory at the previous race, but team principal <a href="/wiki/Mario_Theissen" title="Mario Theissen">Mario Theissen</a> said that a second win was unlikely at Magny-Cours.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In technical developments, <a href="/wiki/BMW_Sauber_F1.08" title="BMW Sauber F1.08">BMW Sauber</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_F2008" title="Ferrari F2008">Ferrari</a>, <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4-23" title="McLaren MP4-23">McLaren</a> and <a href="/wiki/Toyota_TF108" title="Toyota TF108">Toyota</a> all revised their <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_car#Aerodynamics" title="Formula One car">front wings</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-BMW_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BMW-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Ferrari_tech_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ferrari_tech-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> BMW brought both their new wing as well as the version they had used for the previous race to Magny-Cours, but decided to use the revised wing, as it offered better levels of <a href="/wiki/Downforce" title="Downforce">downforce</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-BMW_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BMW-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Ferrari's wing changes aimed at improving the performance of the car's nose hole.<sup id="cite_ref-Ferrari_tech_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ferrari_tech-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> The nose hole, which had been introduced at the <a href="/wiki/2008_Spanish_Grand_Prix" title="2008 Spanish Grand Prix">Spanish Grand Prix</a>, aimed at creating greater levels of downforce, by channelling the airflow.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Williams changed their front sidepod winglets.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> At the previous race, Red Bull modified their <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_RB4" title="Red Bull RB4">RB4</a>'s bridge wing to prevent it from flexing, to comply with the latest rule clarifications.<sup id="cite_ref-RB_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RB-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> For Magny-Cours, the team revised the central section of this element, with the aim of generating greater downforce levels.<sup id="cite_ref-RB_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RB-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice">Practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2008_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Robert_Kubica_2008_France.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1b/Robert_Kubica_2008_France.jpg/220px-Robert_Kubica_2008_France.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="77" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1b/Robert_Kubica_2008_France.jpg/330px-Robert_Kubica_2008_France.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1b/Robert_Kubica_2008_France.jpg/440px-Robert_Kubica_2008_France.jpg 2x" data-file-width="502" data-file-height="176" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Robert_Kubica_2008_France.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a> led the Drivers' Championship going into the race, but only managed seventh in qualifying.</div></div></div>
<p>Three practice sessions were held before the Sunday race—two on Friday, and a third on Saturday. The Friday morning and afternoon sessions each lasted 90&#160;minutes. The third session was held on Saturday morning and lasted an hour.<sup id="cite_ref-sporting_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sporting-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> In the first practice session, which was held in dry conditions, Ferrari's Felipe Massa was quickest, ahead of the McLarens of Hamilton and Kovalainen in second and third.<sup id="cite_ref-P1_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P1-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Massa's teammate, Räikkönen, was fourth quickest.<sup id="cite_ref-P1_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P1-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> The afternoon session, which was held in very hot conditions, saw Renault's <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> record the fastest lap, ahead of the two Ferrari drivers.<sup id="cite_ref-P2_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P2-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> The hot weather conditions posed several problems for the drivers, as many of the cars ran off the road, sliding through the gravel or across the asphalt.<sup id="cite_ref-P2_26-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P2-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> At the end of the session, a new system—designed to limit the cars' speeds in potentially hazardous situations—was tested.<sup id="cite_ref-P2_26-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P2-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> In the third practice session, again held in dry conditions, Renault continued their strong practice performance with Piquet leading the final practice session.<sup id="cite_ref-P3_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P3-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> Red Bull's <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> was next quickest while <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> of Toro Rosso was third.<sup id="cite_ref-P3_27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P3-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> As was the pattern for the season, the McLaren and Ferrari drivers ran heavier fuel loads in this session in preparation for the final section of qualifying.<sup id="cite_ref-P3_27-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P3-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2008_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Saturday afternoon's qualifying session was divided into three&#160;parts. In the first 20-minute period, cars finishing 16th or lower were eliminated. The second qualifying period lasted for 15&#160;minutes, at the end of which the fastest ten cars went into the final period, to determine their grid positions for the race. Cars failing to make the final period were allowed to be refuelled before the race but those competing in it were not, and so carried more fuel than they had done in the earlier qualifying sessions.<sup id="cite_ref-sporting_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sporting-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatleft" style="width:35%;&#32;;"><style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r887775652">.mw-parser-output .quotebox{background-color:#F9F9F9;border:1px solid #aaa;box-sizing:border-box;padding:10px;font-size:88%;max-width:100%}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft{margin:0.5em 1.4em 0.8em 0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright{margin:0.5em 0 0.8em 1.4em}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.centered{margin:0.5em auto 0.8em auto}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft p,.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright p{font-style:inherit}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-title{background-color:#F9F9F9;text-align:center;font-size:larger;font-weight:bold}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:before{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" “ ";vertical-align:-45%;line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:after{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" ” ";line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .left-aligned{text-align:left}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .right-aligned{text-align:right}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .center-aligned{text-align:center}.mw-parser-output .quotebox cite{display:block;font-style:normal}@media screen and (max-width:360px){.mw-parser-output .quotebox{min-width:100%;margin:0 0 0.8em!important;float:none!important}}</style>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">I am very happy with this result, not just for myself but above all for the team. So far, the weekend is going really well but of course we have yet to tackle the most important part which is the race, where anything could happen. If I was not to win, I would obviously be a bit disappointed, but the most important thing is to finish and bring home the maximum number of points, because we really need them.</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style=""><a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a>, following the third qualifying session.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>Räikkönen clinched Ferrari's 200th <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> and his last until the <a href="/wiki/2017_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="2017 Monaco Grand Prix">2017 Monaco Grand Prix</a> 129 races later, with a time of 1:16.449.<sup id="cite_ref-Qual_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Qual-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> He was joined on the front row of the grid by teammate Massa.<sup id="cite_ref-Qual_29-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Qual-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> This would later prove to be Ferrari's last front-row lockout until the <a href="/wiki/2017_Russian_Grand_Prix" title="2017 Russian Grand Prix">2017 Russian Grand Prix</a>. Alonso qualified third after Hamilton's penalty moved the quicker McLaren driver to 13th; Toyota's Jarno Trulli qualified fourth.<sup id="cite_ref-Penalty_11-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Penalty-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Qual_29-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Qual-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Kovalainen would have started from fifth, but was given a five-place grid penalty for blocking Webber during qualifying and would start from 10th on the grid.<sup id="cite_ref-Qual_29-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Qual-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Kov_pen_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kov_pen-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Kubica, Webber, <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a>,  <a href="/wiki/Timo_Glock" title="Timo Glock">Timo Glock</a> and Piquet rounded off the top ten.<sup id="cite_ref-Qual_29-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Qual-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Heidfeld qualified 11th, with Vettel ahead of Hamilton in 12th, and Bourdais behind in 14th.<sup id="cite_ref-Qual_29-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Qual-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Rosberg was next quickest, but after his penalty demoted him to the back of the grid his teammate <a href="/wiki/Kazuki_Nakajima" title="Kazuki Nakajima">Kazuki Nakajima</a> took his place.<sup id="cite_ref-Penalty_11-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Penalty-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Qual_29-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Qual-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> The final four places went to the Honda and Force India teams, with Button qualifying ahead of Barrichello, Fisichella and Sutil.<sup id="cite_ref-Qual_29-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Qual-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello, however, was given a penalty for changing his gearbox, meaning that he started 20th on the grid, one place behind Rosberg.<sup id="cite_ref-Bar_pen_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bar_pen-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2008_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The conditions on the grid were dry before the race, although the sky was overcast; weather forecasts predicted rain near the end of the race.<sup id="cite_ref-aucom_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aucom-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Most of the frontrunners began the race on the harder compound tyre. Rain that had fallen earlier that morning had removed some of the rubber on the track, meaning that <a href="/wiki/List_of_motorsport_terminology#G" class="mw-redirect" title="List of motorsport terminology">graining</a>, when small grains of rubber come off a tyre, was likely to be a problem; out of the two tyre types, the harder would better cope with this.<sup id="cite_ref-aucom_34-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aucom-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Räikkönen made a good start, retaining his first position; Massa behind him maintained his second place.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> Alonso, who started third, was passed by both Trulli and Kubica, but re-passed Kubica at the hairpin turn exit.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_35-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> Glock also made a good start, taking sixth after passing Webber.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_35-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> Going into the first corner, Button touched Bourdais, resulting in damage to the Honda's front wing.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_35-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-GrandPrix.com_quotes_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GrandPrix.com_quotes-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton, who started 13th, passed several drivers to move into 10th by the end of lap one.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_35-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Felipe_Massa_2008_France.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cd/Felipe_Massa_2008_France.jpg/220px-Felipe_Massa_2008_France.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="73" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cd/Felipe_Massa_2008_France.jpg/330px-Felipe_Massa_2008_France.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cd/Felipe_Massa_2008_France.jpg/440px-Felipe_Massa_2008_France.jpg 2x" data-file-width="641" data-file-height="214" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Felipe_Massa_2008_France.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The race was won by <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> for <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, moving him into the lead of the Drivers' Championship for the first time in his career.</div></div></div>
<p>At the end of the first lap, Räikkönen led from Massa, Trulli, Alonso, Kubica, Glock and Webber.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_35-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> On lap five, Hamilton overtook Kovalainen to move into ninth.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_35-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> On the same lap, Button was forced to make an unscheduled pit stop, due to the damage he had sustained in his first corner incident with Bourdais.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_35-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> This dropped him to the back of the field.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_35-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> By the end of lap&#160;10, Räikkönen led Massa by 3.2&#160;seconds, Trulli a further 8.5&#160;seconds behind. On lap&#160;13, Hamilton was given a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_regulations#Penalties" title="Formula One regulations">drive-through penalty</a> for cutting the corner of Turn Seven on lap one, and gaining an advantage.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> He took the penalty immediately, and re-emerged in 13th position.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_35-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> Over the next few laps, the Ferrari drivers continued to pull out a gap on Trulli in third, lapping at around a second per lap quicker than the Toyota.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_35-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> On lap&#160;16, Räikkönen set the fastest lap of the race, a 1:16.630, stretching his lead over his teammate.<sup id="cite_ref-fastlap_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fastlap-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Further back, Alonso, who had been running fourth, made the first scheduled pit stop, resuming 12th. Button was lapped by several drivers, due to the damage his car had sustained in his first corner incident with Bourdais, and eventually retired on lap&#160;17.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_35-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Kimi_Raikkonen_2008_France.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4a/Kimi_Raikkonen_2008_France.jpg/220px-Kimi_Raikkonen_2008_France.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="78" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4a/Kimi_Raikkonen_2008_France.jpg/330px-Kimi_Raikkonen_2008_France.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4a/Kimi_Raikkonen_2008_France.jpg/440px-Kimi_Raikkonen_2008_France.jpg 2x" data-file-width="792" data-file-height="281" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Kimi_Raikkonen_2008_France.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Massa's teammate <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> had to settle for second place after being slowed by an exhaust failure.</div></div></div>
<p>On lap&#160;20, Trulli and Kubica, then in fourth, pitted.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_35-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> Over the next two laps, both Räikkönen and Massa pitted.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_35-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> By lap&#160;30, Räikkönen had opened his lead to 6.6&#160;seconds over Massa, while Trulli was over half a minute behind him in third.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_35-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> Just before half distance, Räikkönen's pace became slower, and Massa behind him caught up, passing his teammate on lap&#160;39.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_35-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> After the race, Räikkönen explained that his lack of pace was due to his car's right exhaust pipe breaking, causing the engine to lose power.<sup id="cite_ref-GrandPrix.com_quotes_36-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GrandPrix.com_quotes-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Further back, Kovalainen, who was running seventh, passed Webber to take sixth.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_35-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>By lap&#160;46, Massa had a 10-second lead over his teammate.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_35-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> On the same lap, Kubica pitted from fourth, starting the next round of pit stops.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_35-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> Trulli pitted from third on lap&#160;50, and Räikkönen and Kovalainen two laps later.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_35-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> Massa pitted on lap&#160;54, and emerged 13.4&#160;seconds ahead of Räikkönen. Trulli kept his third position, but Kovalainen, who made up several places through the pit stops to move to fourth, was closing behind him.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_35-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> On lap&#160;55, light rain started to fall.<sup id="cite_ref-aucom_34-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aucom-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Although it would continue to rain lightly for the next few laps, it was never heavy enough to be a problem to the drivers.<sup id="cite_ref-aucom_34-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aucom-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> By lap&#160;58, Kovalainen was right behind Trulli.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_35-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> A few laps later, the exhaust pipe which had broken earlier on Räikkönen's car came off completely, but Räikkönen continued to race with similar lap times.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_35-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> Kovalainen, meanwhile, continued to try to find a way past Trulli.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_35-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> One lap before the end of the race, Kovalainen attempted to pass Trulli, but ran wide as Trulli defended his position.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_35-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Massa crossed the line to win the race, with Räikkönen nearly eighteen seconds behind.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_35-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> Trulli retained third, and took his first podium since the <a href="/wiki/2005_Spanish_Grand_Prix" title="2005 Spanish Grand Prix">2005 Spanish Grand Prix</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-SkyTru_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SkyTru-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> and Toyota's first podium since the <a href="/wiki/2006_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="2006 Australian Grand Prix">2006 Australian Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-I_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-I-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> Kovalainen finished fourth, ahead of Kubica, Webber and Piquet, who took his first ever points in Formula One.<sup id="cite_ref-I_39-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-I-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> Alonso, Coulthard, Hamilton, Glock, Vettel, Heidfeld and the lapped Barrichello, Nakajima, Rosberg, Bourdais, Fisichella and Sutil were the last of the finishing drivers.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_35-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-formula1.com-officialresult_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-formula1.com-officialresult-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2008_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Jarno_Trulli_2008_France.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d5/Jarno_Trulli_2008_France.jpg/220px-Jarno_Trulli_2008_France.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="74" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d5/Jarno_Trulli_2008_France.jpg/330px-Jarno_Trulli_2008_France.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d5/Jarno_Trulli_2008_France.jpg/440px-Jarno_Trulli_2008_France.jpg 2x" data-file-width="767" data-file-height="258" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Jarno_Trulli_2008_France.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> took <a href="/wiki/Toyota_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Toyota F1">Toyota</a>'s first podium finish since the <a href="/wiki/2006_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="2006 Australian Grand Prix">2006 Australian Grand Prix</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>Massa was delighted with his race victory, saying, "A great race, a fantastic result. The win came my way because <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi</a> had a problem with his car and at the pace he was running, it would have been hard for me to beat him on the track. I would have been happy with second place but of course, the win makes me even happier."<sup id="cite_ref-GrandPrix.com_quotes_36-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GrandPrix.com_quotes-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> After the race, Räikkönen said,
</p>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r960796168"/><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>Obviously I am a bit disappointed because I had hoped to win. Unfortunately, the right exhaust pipe broke just before half-distance and the engine lost a lot of power, especially on the straight after the slow corners. After a few laps, the situation seemed better, but towards the end of the race, I ran the risk of stopping. This sort of thing can happen in racing and I have to try and look on the bright side: eight points are still a good amount and the one-two finish is a great result for the team. Luckily, I had built up quite a good lead in the first part thanks to a car that was really very competitive.<sup id="cite_ref-GrandPrix.com_quotes_36-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GrandPrix.com_quotes-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>
<p>Trulli said, "Today was a great race, hard and tough. We had a good pace, even if we had to battle with some cars that were quicker than us. I had to fight really hard but that is what people should expect both from myself and from Toyota."<sup id="cite_ref-GrandPrix.com_quotes_36-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GrandPrix.com_quotes-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton commented on his drive-through penalty: "My drive-through penalty was an extremely close call: I felt I'd got past Vettel fairly and was ahead going into the corner. But I was on the outside and couldn't turn-in in case we both crashed, then I lost the back-end and drove over the kerb."<sup id="cite_ref-GrandPrix.com_quotes_36-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GrandPrix.com_quotes-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Nelson_Piquet_2008_France.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/26/Nelson_Piquet_2008_France.jpg/220px-Nelson_Piquet_2008_France.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="79" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/26/Nelson_Piquet_2008_France.jpg/330px-Nelson_Piquet_2008_France.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/26/Nelson_Piquet_2008_France.jpg/440px-Nelson_Piquet_2008_France.jpg 2x" data-file-width="876" data-file-height="314" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Nelson_Piquet_2008_France.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet_Jr." title="Nelson Piquet Jr.">Nelson Piquet Jr.</a> took his first ever points in Formula One, in his eighth race.</div></div></div>
<p>After BMW Sauber's victory at the previous race, their director, Mario Theissen, said that "over the entire weekend here our package did not work perfectly", while driver Heidfeld said that it was a "disappointing result".<sup id="cite_ref-GrandPrix.com_quotes_36-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GrandPrix.com_quotes-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Alonso said that he felt disappointed after his poor start to the race.<sup id="cite_ref-GrandPrix.com_quotes_36-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GrandPrix.com_quotes-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Red Bull's director, <a href="/wiki/Christian_Horner" title="Christian Horner">Christian Horner</a>, said that their problems lay in the bad starts their drivers had made.<sup id="cite_ref-GrandPrix.com_quotes_36-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GrandPrix.com_quotes-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Button, who retired during the race, commented on his collision: "I got a good start and was alongside Bourdais. I thought he was going to turn in at turn one and close the door so I pulled in behind him and then unfortunately hit him in the rear as everything slowed down for the corner. I could feel that there was something broken at the front of the car as there was an air coming in from the front, but the car was driveable and I was staying with the back of the pack so I continued. We replaced the nose but the bargeboards had been pulled off and the car had become undriveable so I had to retire."<sup id="cite_ref-GrandPrix.com_quotes_36-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GrandPrix.com_quotes-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Piquet scored his first points in Formula One in the race, having failed to score up to that point.<sup id="cite_ref-I_39-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-I-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> He said that he was happy, and hoped that the team could "continue like this for the rest of the season".<sup id="cite_ref-GrandPrix.com_quotes_36-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GrandPrix.com_quotes-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Heikki_Kovalainen_2008_France.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/19/Heikki_Kovalainen_2008_France.jpg/220px-Heikki_Kovalainen_2008_France.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="79" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/19/Heikki_Kovalainen_2008_France.jpg/330px-Heikki_Kovalainen_2008_France.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/19/Heikki_Kovalainen_2008_France.jpg/440px-Heikki_Kovalainen_2008_France.jpg 2x" data-file-width="661" data-file-height="236" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Heikki_Kovalainen_2008_France.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> took fourth for <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>After the race, Massa moved into the lead of the Drivers' Championship, on 48&#160;points, taking the Championship lead for the first time in his Formula One career.<sup id="cite_ref-I_39-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-I-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> Kubica lost the lead of the Drivers' Championship, falling two points behind Massa.<sup id="cite_ref-autocourse2008-pg170-71_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autocourse2008-pg170-71-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> Räikkönen moved ahead of Hamilton, on 43&#160;points, while Hamilton was five points further behind. Heidfeld remained fifth.<sup id="cite_ref-autocourse2008-pg170-71_41-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autocourse2008-pg170-71-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> Before the race, Ferrari had been just three points ahead of BMW in the Constructors' Championship; after the race, Ferrari moved into a comfortable 17&#160;point advantage. McLaren made up one point on BMW, although they were still 16&#160;points behind.<sup id="cite_ref-autocourse2008-pg170-71_41-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autocourse2008-pg170-71-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
