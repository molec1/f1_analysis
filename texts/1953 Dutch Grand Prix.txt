<p>The <b>1953 Dutch Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_Two" title="Formula Two">Formula Two</a> race held on 7 June 1953 at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_Zandvoort" title="Circuit Zandvoort">Circuit Zandvoort</a>. It was race 3 of 9 in the <a href="/wiki/1953_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1953 World Championship of Drivers">1953 World Championship of Drivers</a>, which was run to Formula Two rules in 1952 and 1953, rather than the Formula One regulations normally used. The 90-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Alberto_Ascari" title="Alberto Ascari">Alberto Ascari</a> after he started from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>. His teammate <a href="/wiki/Giuseppe_Farina" title="Giuseppe Farina">Nino Farina</a> finished second and <a href="/wiki/Maserati_in_motorsport" title="Maserati in motorsport">Maserati</a> drivers <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Froil%C3%A1n_Gonz%C3%A1lez" title="José Froilán González">José Froilán González</a> and <a href="/wiki/Felice_Bonetto" title="Felice Bonetto">Felice Bonetto</a> came in third
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_report">Race report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1953_Dutch_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Race report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Autoraces_Dutch_Grand_Prix_Zandvoort._Winnaar_Alberto_Ascari_met_prins_Bernhard,_Bestanddeelnr_905-7658.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/56/Autoraces_Dutch_Grand_Prix_Zandvoort._Winnaar_Alberto_Ascari_met_prins_Bernhard%2C_Bestanddeelnr_905-7658.jpg/300px-Autoraces_Dutch_Grand_Prix_Zandvoort._Winnaar_Alberto_Ascari_met_prins_Bernhard%2C_Bestanddeelnr_905-7658.jpg" decoding="async" width="300" height="250" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/56/Autoraces_Dutch_Grand_Prix_Zandvoort._Winnaar_Alberto_Ascari_met_prins_Bernhard%2C_Bestanddeelnr_905-7658.jpg/450px-Autoraces_Dutch_Grand_Prix_Zandvoort._Winnaar_Alberto_Ascari_met_prins_Bernhard%2C_Bestanddeelnr_905-7658.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/56/Autoraces_Dutch_Grand_Prix_Zandvoort._Winnaar_Alberto_Ascari_met_prins_Bernhard%2C_Bestanddeelnr_905-7658.jpg/600px-Autoraces_Dutch_Grand_Prix_Zandvoort._Winnaar_Alberto_Ascari_met_prins_Bernhard%2C_Bestanddeelnr_905-7658.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3415" data-file-height="2849" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Autoraces_Dutch_Grand_Prix_Zandvoort._Winnaar_Alberto_Ascari_met_prins_Bernhard,_Bestanddeelnr_905-7658.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Ascari after winning the Grand Prix with <a href="/wiki/Prince_Bernhard_of_Lippe-Biesterfeld" title="Prince Bernhard of Lippe-Biesterfeld">Prince Bernhard of Lippe-Biesterfeld</a> to his left.</div></div></div>
<p>The Dutch Grand Prix, which had been held in August <a href="/wiki/1952_Formula_One_season" title="1952 Formula One season">the previous year</a>, moved to an earlier June calendar slot in 1953. <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> retained the same four drivers who had competed at <a href="/wiki/1953_Argentine_Grand_Prix" title="1953 Argentine Grand Prix">Buenos Aires</a>—<a href="/wiki/Alberto_Ascari" title="Alberto Ascari">Alberto Ascari</a>, <a href="/wiki/Luigi_Villoresi" title="Luigi Villoresi">Luigi Villoresi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Giuseppe_Farina" title="Giuseppe Farina">Nino Farina</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mike_Hawthorn" title="Mike Hawthorn">Mike Hawthorn</a>—while there was also a privateer Ferrari for Frenchman <a href="/wiki/Louis_Rosier" title="Louis Rosier">Louis Rosier</a>. The Scuderia's most significant competition came from the <a href="/wiki/Maserati_in_motorsport" title="Maserati in motorsport">Maserati team</a>, who came to Zandvoort with three of their four drivers from the Argentine Grand Prix: <a href="/wiki/Juan_Manuel_Fangio" title="Juan Manuel Fangio">Juan Manuel Fangio</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Froil%C3%A1n_Gonz%C3%A1lez" title="José Froilán González">José Froilán González</a> and <a href="/wiki/Felice_Bonetto" title="Felice Bonetto">Felice Bonetto</a>. Swiss driver <a href="/wiki/Toulo_de_Graffenried" title="Toulo de Graffenried">Toulo de Graffenried</a> raced in a privateer Maserati for <a href="/wiki/Enrico_Plat%C3%A9" title="Enrico Platé">Enrico Platé</a>'s team. <a href="/wiki/Gordini" title="Gordini">Gordini</a> also entered three cars for this event, with <a href="/wiki/Maurice_Trintignant" title="Maurice Trintignant">Maurice Trintignant</a> and <a href="/wiki/Harry_Schell" title="Harry Schell">Harry Schell</a> (who had shared Trintignant's car at Buenos Aires) being retained from their lineup for Argentina. <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Mieres" title="Roberto Mieres">Roberto Mieres</a> made his Grand Prix debut in the team's third car. The <a href="/wiki/Connaught_Engineering" title="Connaught Engineering">Connaught</a> works team retained <a href="/wiki/Kenneth_McAlpine" title="Kenneth McAlpine">Kenneth McAlpine</a> and <a href="/wiki/Stirling_Moss" title="Stirling Moss">Stirling Moss</a> from their lineup for the previous European race, the <a href="/wiki/1952_Italian_Grand_Prix" title="1952 Italian Grand Prix">Italian Grand Prix</a>, while fellow British driver <a href="/wiki/Roy_Salvadori" title="Roy Salvadori">Roy Salvadori</a> also drove for the team, and <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Claes" title="Johnny Claes">Johnny Claes</a> entered a privateer Connaught. <a href="/wiki/Hersham_and_Walton_Motors" title="Hersham and Walton Motors">HWM</a> also stuck with the drivers who had competed for them in Monza—<a href="/wiki/Peter_Collins_(racing_driver)" title="Peter Collins (racing driver)">Peter Collins</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lance_Macklin" title="Lance Macklin">Lance Macklin</a>—while <a href="/wiki/Ken_Wharton" title="Ken Wharton">Ken Wharton</a> completed the field in his privateer <a href="/wiki/Cooper_Car_Company" title="Cooper Car Company">Cooper</a>-<a href="/wiki/Bristol_Cars" title="Bristol Cars">Bristol</a>.
</p><p>Ascari took his fifth consecutive pole position (excluding the <a href="/wiki/1953_Indianapolis_500" title="1953 Indianapolis 500">Indy 500</a>, in which none of the European teams competed), and he was joined on the front row by Fangio in his Maserati and the second Ferrari of Farina. Villoresi in the third Ferrari started from the second row, alongside the Maserati of González, while the third row consisted of Hawthorn in the remaining works Ferrari and a pair of privateers—de Graffenried in a Maserati and Rosier in his Ferrari. The final works Maserati of Bonetto could only manage to qualify on the fifth row of the grid, starting from thirteenth.
</p><p>The race was held in very difficult conditions – the track was made slippery by loose grit. The Ferraris had better road holding and once again Alberto Ascari led from start to finish, while the main competition for second place was between his teammates Farina and Villoresi. Farina ultimately finished second, while Villoresi, who took the point for fastest lap, was forced to retire with a throttle issue. A problem with his suspension forced González to retire. Three laps later, however, he took over his teammate Felice Bonetto's car and ran out the winner of an exciting duel with Mike Hawthorn, once again depriving Ferrari of a 1-2-3. González and Bonetto shared the four points for third place. Fangio retired with a broken back axle, having been in fourth behind the leading Ferrari trio at the time. Toulo de Graffenried took the final points position in fifth, his first points since the <a href="/wiki/1951_Swiss_Grand_Prix" title="1951 Swiss Grand Prix">1951 Swiss Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Ascari's eight consecutive World Championship race victory (ignoring the Indianapolis 500) gave him a clear lead in the points standings. He was eight points clear of <a href="/wiki/Bill_Vukovich" title="Bill Vukovich">Bill Vukovich</a>, the winner at Indianapolis, while his nearest genuine rivals for the Drivers' Championship were his teammates Villoresi and Farina, who were in third and fourth, respectively. González and Hawthorn were level on points with Farina, eleven points adrift of Ascari.
</p>
