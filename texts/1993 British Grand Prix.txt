<p>The <b>1993 British Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at <a href="/wiki/Silverstone_Circuit" title="Silverstone Circuit">Silverstone</a> on 11 July 1993. It was the ninth race of the <a href="/wiki/1993_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1993 Formula One World Championship">1993 Formula One World Championship</a>.
</p><p>The 59-lap race was won from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> by <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW15C" title="Williams FW15C">Williams</a>-<a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>. It was Prost's sixth victory of the season and the 50th of his Formula One career. <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> finished second in a <a href="/wiki/Benetton_B193" title="Benetton B193">Benetton</a>-<a href="/wiki/Ford_Motor_Company" title="Ford Motor Company">Ford</a>, with teammate <a href="/wiki/Riccardo_Patrese" title="Riccardo Patrese">Riccardo Patrese</a> third. This was the 2nd race of the season to be held in the United Kingdom  after the <a href="/wiki/1993_European_Grand_Prix" title="1993 European Grand Prix">1993 European Grand Prix</a> which had been  held  back in <a href="/wiki/April" title="April">April</a> of that year at <a href="/wiki/Donington_Park" title="Donington Park">Donington Park</a> . It would be <a href="/wiki/2020_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2020 Formula One World Championship">another 27 years</a> before Britain would have two races on the calendar in a single season when Silverstone hosted both the <a href="/wiki/2020_British_Grand_Prix" title="2020 British Grand Prix">British Grand Prix</a> and the <a href="/wiki/70th_Anniversary_Grand_Prix" title="70th Anniversary Grand Prix">70th Anniversary Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> 
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1993_British_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>With <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> now racing <a href="/wiki/Champ_Car" class="mw-redirect" title="Champ Car">Indycars</a> in America, British racing fans had taken <a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a> to their hearts. Williams took 1–2 in qualifying with Prost on pole ahead of Hill, Schumacher, <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a>, Patrese and <a href="/wiki/Martin_Brundle" title="Martin Brundle">Martin Brundle</a>.
</p><p>At the start, Hill took the lead from Prost, who was also passed by Senna, meanwhile Michael Andretti in the other McLaren spun off at Copse on the first lap, while Hill pulled away at the front, Senna held up both Prost and Schumacher. Prost finally passed Senna on lap 7 but Hill was already five seconds up the road. On lap 13, Schumacher passed Senna for third and pulled away as the order settled down.
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:The_end_of_the_day_for_Damon_Hill,_retiring_while_in_the_lead_at_the_1993_British_Grand_Prix_(32873439863).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b4/The_end_of_the_day_for_Damon_Hill%2C_retiring_while_in_the_lead_at_the_1993_British_Grand_Prix_%2832873439863%29.jpg/220px-The_end_of_the_day_for_Damon_Hill%2C_retiring_while_in_the_lead_at_the_1993_British_Grand_Prix_%2832873439863%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b4/The_end_of_the_day_for_Damon_Hill%2C_retiring_while_in_the_lead_at_the_1993_British_Grand_Prix_%2832873439863%29.jpg/330px-The_end_of_the_day_for_Damon_Hill%2C_retiring_while_in_the_lead_at_the_1993_British_Grand_Prix_%2832873439863%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b4/The_end_of_the_day_for_Damon_Hill%2C_retiring_while_in_the_lead_at_the_1993_British_Grand_Prix_%2832873439863%29.jpg/440px-The_end_of_the_day_for_Damon_Hill%2C_retiring_while_in_the_lead_at_the_1993_British_Grand_Prix_%2832873439863%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1943" data-file-height="1295" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:The_end_of_the_day_for_Damon_Hill,_retiring_while_in_the_lead_at_the_1993_British_Grand_Prix_(32873439863).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Damon Hill was leading when his engine blew on lap 42.</div></div></div>
<p>Gradually Prost closed up on Hill, narrowing the gap to three seconds after the mid-race pit stops. Then, <a href="/wiki/Luca_Badoer" title="Luca Badoer">Luca Badoer</a> crashed, bringing out the Safety Car and reducing the gap between Hill and Prost to nothing. The Safety Car came in on lap 40, before Hill's engine blew two laps later. Brundle's gearbox failed on lap 54 when he was fourth. Finally, on the last lap, Senna's car ran out of fuel. Prost took his 50th Grand Prix win ahead of Schumacher, Patrese, <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Herbert" title="Johnny Herbert">Johnny Herbert</a>, Senna (who was classified fifth) and <a href="/wiki/Derek_Warwick" title="Derek Warwick">Derek Warwick</a>.
</p><p>Complimenting the <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Lotus</a> team on Herbert's fourth-place finish, <a href="/wiki/Murray_Walker" title="Murray Walker">Murray Walker</a> closed his broadcast with the words "Lotus are back!". As it turned out, the team only scored points once more.
</p>
