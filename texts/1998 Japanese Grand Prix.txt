<p>The <b>1998 Japanese Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>XXIV Fuji Television Japanese Grand Prix</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at <a href="/wiki/Suzuka_Circuit" class="mw-redirect" title="Suzuka Circuit">Suzuka</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mie_Prefecture" title="Mie Prefecture">Mie</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a> on 1 November 1998. It was the sixteenth and final round of the <a href="/wiki/1998_FIA_Formula_One_World_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="1998 FIA Formula One World Championship">1998 FIA Formula One World Championship</a>. The 51-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> driving for the <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Mercedes_AMG_High_Performance_Powertrains" title="Mercedes AMG High Performance Powertrains">Mercedes</a> team. <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a>, driving for <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, finished second with <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> third in the other McLaren. Häkkinen's win confirmed him as <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">1998 Drivers' Champion</a> as title-rival <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> retired with a punctured tyre on Lap 31.
</p><p>Schumacher started on <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> but stalled on the formation lap, meaning he was forced to start at the back of the grid. Schumacher managed to climb the field during the course of the race and eventually retired from a punctured tyre sustained from running over debris from an incident that occurred previously.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1998_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1998_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<table class="box-Expand_section plainlinks metadata ambox mbox-small-left ambox-content" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-image"><a href="/wiki/File:Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg" class="image"><img alt="[icon]" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg/20px-Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg.png" decoding="async" width="20" height="14" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg/30px-Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg/40px-Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="44" data-file-height="31" /></a></td><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This section <b>needs expansion</b>. <small>You can help by <a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=1998_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=">adding to it</a>.</small>  <small class="date-container"><i>(<span class="date">March 2014</span>)</i></small></div></td></tr></tbody></table>
<p>Heading into the final race of the season, <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> was leading the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a> with 90 points; <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> was second with 86 points.<sup id="cite_ref-points_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> A maximum of 10 points were available for the remaining race, which meant that Schumacher could still win the title. Häkkinen only needed a second-place finish to become Drivers' Champion even if Schumacher won—both drivers would be tied on points and number of victories but Häkkinen would claim the title as he would have finished second place three times, compared to Schumacher's two.<sup id="cite_ref-championshiptables_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-championshiptables-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Behind Häkkinen and Schumacher in the Drivers' Championship, <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> was third on 52 points in a McLaren, with <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a> fourth on 41 points in a Ferrari.<sup id="cite_ref-points_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a>, McLaren were leading on 142 points and Ferrari were second on 127 points, with a maximum of 16 points available.<sup id="cite_ref-points_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During the four-week break that followed the <a href="/wiki/1998_Luxembourg_Grand_Prix" title="1998 Luxembourg Grand Prix">Luxembourg Grand Prix</a>, Ferrari and McLaren performed private tests that were heavily scheduled.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Ferrari concentrated their testing at their private race track at <a href="/wiki/Mugello_Circuit" title="Mugello Circuit">Mugello</a>, while McLaren tested at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Catalunya" class="mw-redirect" title="Circuit de Catalunya">Circuit de Catalunya</a> where they were joined by <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Prost_Grand_Prix" title="Prost Grand Prix">Prost</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arrows_Grand_Prix_International" title="Arrows Grand Prix International">Arrows</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stewart_Grand_Prix" title="Stewart Grand Prix">Stewart</a>, new entrants for 1999 <a href="/wiki/British_American_Racing" title="British American Racing">BAR</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a> opted to run at <a href="/wiki/Silverstone_Circuit" title="Silverstone Circuit">Silverstone</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Because of two controversial incidents that decided the 1994 and 1997 World Championships, Schumacher was placed under strict orders from Ferrari president <a href="/wiki/Luca_di_Montezemolo" class="mw-redirect" title="Luca di Montezemolo">Luca di Montezemolo</a> not to repeat such incidents.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After having been involved since their debut in <a href="/wiki/1965_Formula_One_season" title="1965 Formula One season">1965</a>, tyre supplier <a href="/wiki/Goodyear_Tire_and_Rubber_Company" title="Goodyear Tire and Rubber Company">Goodyear</a> bowed out of Formula One, having been the sport's single tyre supplier for several seasons. Competing manufacturer <a href="/wiki/Bridgestone" title="Bridgestone">Bridgestone</a> became the sport's single tyre supplier for the <a href="/wiki/1999_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1999 Formula One World Championship">1999</a> season.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice_and_qualifying">Practice and qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1998_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Practice and qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>Two practice sessions were held before the race; the first was held on Friday that was split into two parts and the second on Saturday morning. The first session was held for a total of three hours with the second session lasting two hours.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher set the fastest time in the first practice session with a time of 1:39.823, two-tenths of a second from <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a> driver and brother <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> and <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a>. Schumacher's team-mate Irvine was fourth fastest, Häkkinen was fifth fastest with team-mate Coulthard rounding out the top six.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/List_of_motorsport_terminology#Q" class="mw-redirect" title="List of motorsport terminology">Qualifying session</a> was run as a one-hour session held on Saturday afternoon. Schumacher clinched his third consecutive <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> in his Ferrari, with a time of 1:36.293.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> He was joined on the front row by Häkkinen, who was one-tenth of a second behind, after not being able to finish his last flying lap by running off in the gravel at the Degner corner. Coulthard was third in the other McLaren. Irvine took fourth in the second Ferrari, with Frentzen taking fifth despite going off into the <a href="/wiki/Run-off_area" title="Run-off area">gravel</a> late in the session.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1998_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Mika_Hakkinen_DTM_Norisring_2009.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/11/Mika_Hakkinen_DTM_Norisring_2009.jpg/220px-Mika_Hakkinen_DTM_Norisring_2009.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="328" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/11/Mika_Hakkinen_DTM_Norisring_2009.jpg/330px-Mika_Hakkinen_DTM_Norisring_2009.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/11/Mika_Hakkinen_DTM_Norisring_2009.jpg/440px-Mika_Hakkinen_DTM_Norisring_2009.jpg 2x" data-file-width="516" data-file-height="769" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Mika_Hakkinen_DTM_Norisring_2009.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> was crowned <a href="/wiki/1998_FIA_Formula_One_World_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="1998 FIA Formula One World Championship">1998 Formula One Drivers' Champion</a> as a result of his win at Suzuka (2009 photo)</div></div></div>
<p>The start of the race was aborted with the <a href="/wiki/Prost_Grand_Prix" title="Prost Grand Prix">Prost</a> car of <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> stalling from 14th position. Before the second attempt to start the race, Schumacher's Ferrari moved forward from his starting position and stalled as he put his car into gear.<sup id="cite_ref-bbc_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> This promoted Häkkinen into pole position as Schumacher started from the back of the grid.<sup id="cite_ref-report_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-report-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At the start, Häkkinen pulled away while Irvine overtook Coulthard for second. The Ferrari driver was unable to attack the leading Finn, while Schumacher moved up the order, reaching twelfth place at the end of the first lap. The first retirement was <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Diniz" title="Pedro Diniz">Pedro Diniz</a> who spun out on lap 3 in the <a href="/wiki/Arrows_Grand_Prix_International" title="Arrows Grand Prix International">Arrows</a>. By lap four, Schumacher overtook his brother <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf</a> for seventh, but was then stuck behind the fighting former world champions <a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a>, meanwhile <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> would eventually retire with engine failure by the end of lap 14. He lost thirty seconds in the following laps over race leader Häkkinen, damaging his hopes for victory and the title.
</p><p>After all frontrunners had pitted, Schumacher emerged in third place, having put in some fast laps. On lap 28, <a href="/wiki/Esteban_Tuero" title="Esteban Tuero">Esteban Tuero</a> missed his braking point going into the final corner, crashing into the <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_Racing" title="Tyrrell Racing">Tyrrell</a> of <a href="/wiki/Tora_Takagi" class="mw-redirect" title="Tora Takagi">Tora Takagi</a>. When Schumacher passed over the debris, he suffered a slow puncture that blew up his right rear tyre three laps later, causing him to retire. This left Häkkinen to take victory and his first drivers' championship. While Eddie Irvine succeeded at keeping David Coulthard behind him, McLaren were nevertheless able to retain their lead over Ferrari in the constructors' championship.<sup id="cite_ref-bbc_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-report_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-report-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1998_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After the race, Häkkinen described the situation after the two aborted starts as relieving, saying: "When Michael was forced to start from the back of the grid it raised an enormous amount of pressure from me. The race was not as difficult as others I've had this season. But a lot of that's down to the team who kept letting me know where Eddie and Michael were."<sup id="cite_ref-bbc_11-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Eddie Irvine was quoted saying: "What happened to Michael at the start didn't change our tactics for the race, it destroyed them. When Michael went to the back I knew it was up to me. I made a fantastic start and got close to Mika at some points but just couldn't manage to get in front."<sup id="cite_ref-bbc_11-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1998_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Last Formula One race for <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_Racing" title="Tyrrell Racing">Tyrrell</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shinji_Nakano" title="Shinji Nakano">Shinji Nakano</a>, <a href="/wiki/Esteban_Tuero" title="Esteban Tuero">Esteban Tuero</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ricardo_Rosset" title="Ricardo Rosset">Ricardo Rosset</a>. The former were later renamed as <a href="/wiki/British_American_Racing" title="British American Racing">British American Racing</a> after a complete takeover by <a href="/wiki/British_American_Tobacco" title="British American Tobacco">British American Tobacco</a>.</li>
<li>Last-ever podium and points for <a href="/wiki/Goodyear_Tire_%26_Rubber_Company" class="mw-redirect" title="Goodyear Tire &amp; Rubber Company">Goodyear</a> tyres. <a href="/wiki/Bridgestone" title="Bridgestone">Bridgestone</a> became the sole tyre supplier for <a href="/wiki/1999_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1999 Formula One World Championship">the following year</a>.</li>
<li>This race was also temporarily the last for <a href="/wiki/Jos_Verstappen" title="Jos Verstappen">Jos Verstappen</a> as he joined the <a href="/wiki/Honda_Racing_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Honda Racing F1">Honda Racing</a> project in preparation for their return to Formula 1 the following year (Honda actually returned to F1 in 2000 as engine supplier for BAR). Verstappen eventually returned to F1 with <a href="/wiki/Arrows_Grand_Prix_International" title="Arrows Grand Prix International">Arrows</a> in 2000.</li></ul>
