<p>The <b>1968 <a href="/wiki/Italian_Grand_Prix" title="Italian Grand Prix">Italian Grand Prix</a></b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at the <a href="/wiki/Autodromo_Nazionale_di_Monza" title="Autodromo Nazionale di Monza">Monza Autodrome</a> on September 8, 1968. It was race 9 of 12 in both the <a href="/wiki/1968_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1968 World Championship of Drivers">1968 World Championship of Drivers</a> and the <a href="/wiki/1968_International_Cup_for_Formula_One_Manufacturers" class="mw-redirect" title="1968 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers">1968 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers</a>. The 68-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Denny_Hulme" title="Denny Hulme">Denny Hulme</a> after he started from seventh position. <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Servoz-Gavin" title="Johnny Servoz-Gavin">Johnny Servoz-Gavin</a> finished second for the <a href="/wiki/Equipe_Matra_Sports" title="Equipe Matra Sports">Matra</a> team and <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Jacky_Ickx" title="Jacky Ickx">Jacky Ickx</a> came in third.
</p><p>There was a five-week break after the previous <a href="/wiki/1968_German_Grand_Prix" title="1968 German Grand Prix">Grand Prix in Germany</a>, and before <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">F1</a> circus resumed in <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>. The season so far had seen both rainfall and tragedy. During the break, the <a href="/wiki/1968_International_Gold_Cup" title="1968 International Gold Cup">Oulton Park Gold Cup</a> attracted some of the top names, with <a href="/wiki/Jackie_Stewart" title="Jackie Stewart">Jackie Stewart</a> taking the victory, after his dominant victory at the <a href="/wiki/N%C3%BCrburgring" title="Nürburgring">Nürburgring</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-gp_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gp-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1968_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Entry">Entry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1968_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Entry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A total of 24 <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">F1</a> cars were entered for the event, making this the biggest of the season, with a couple of notable American drivers on the entry list. Italian American <a href="/wiki/Mario_Andretti" title="Mario Andretti">Mario Andretti</a>, entered in a third <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Lotus</a> and his <a href="/wiki/United_States_Auto_Club" title="United States Auto Club">USAC</a> rival, <a href="/wiki/Bobby_Unser" title="Bobby Unser">Bobby Unser</a>, replacing <a href="/wiki/Richard_Attwood" title="Richard Attwood">Richard Attwood</a> at <a href="/wiki/Owen_Racing_Organisation" class="mw-redirect" title="Owen Racing Organisation">Owen Racing Organisation</a> (<a href="/wiki/British_Racing_Motors" title="British Racing Motors">BRM</a>). Many others team increase their drivers crews. <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Servoz-Gavin" title="Johnny Servoz-Gavin">Johnny Servoz-Gavin</a> due to drive the second of <a href="/wiki/Ken_Tyrrell" title="Ken Tyrrell">Ken Tyrrell</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Matra_International" class="mw-redirect" title="Matra International">Matra International</a> cars. <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Scuderia Ferrari</a> ran a third car for rising English star, <a href="/wiki/Derek_Bell_(racing_driver)" title="Derek Bell (racing driver)">Derek Bell</a>, while <a href="/wiki/David_Hobbs_(racing_driver)" title="David Hobbs (racing driver)">David Hobbs</a> with fielded by <a href="/wiki/Honda_Racing_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Honda Racing F1">Honda Racing</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-chicanef1968_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-chicanef1968-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-gp_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gp-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1968_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The early qualifying session saw <a href="/wiki/Mario_Andretti" title="Mario Andretti">Andretti</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bobby_Unser" title="Bobby Unser">Unser</a> set the pace, recording fast time, especially as both drivers wanted to fly back to the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">US</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Hoosier_Hundred" title="Hoosier Hundred">Hoosier Hundred</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Indiana_State_Fair" title="Indiana State Fair">Indiana State Fairgrounds</a> (a 160km dirt track race), on the following day.  They then intended to fly back to <a href="/wiki/Milan" title="Milan">Milan</a> and race in the Grand Prix. The event organisers announced that if the either driver returned to the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">States</a> and race, they would be banned from competing in the Grand Prix, under an ACI ruling which forbade drivers to complete in another event within 24 hours of the start of the Grand Prix. Both <a href="/wiki/Mario_Andretti" title="Mario Andretti">Andretti</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bobby_Unser" title="Bobby Unser">Unser</a> flew back to Indiana for the Hoosier Hundred and did not return.<sup id="cite_ref-gp_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gp-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Qualifying resulted in <a href="/wiki/John_Surtees" title="John Surtees">John Surtees</a> taking <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Honda_Racing_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Honda Racing F1">Honda Racing</a> team, in their <a href="/wiki/Honda_RA301" title="Honda RA301">Honda RA301</a>, averaging a speed of 150.314&#160;mph. He was joined on the front row by <a href="/wiki/Bruce_McLaren" title="Bruce McLaren">Bruce McLaren</a> in his own <a href="/wiki/McLaren_M7A" title="McLaren M7A">McLaren M7A</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chris_Amon" title="Chris Amon">Chris Amon</a> in a <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_312" title="Ferrari 312">Ferrari 312</a>. The second row saw the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> of <a href="/wiki/Jacky_Ickx" title="Jacky Ickx">Jacky Ickx</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Lotus</a> of <a href="/wiki/Graham_Hill" title="Graham Hill">Graham Hill</a>. <a href="/wiki/Jackie_Stewart" title="Jackie Stewart">Jackie Stewart</a>, <a href="/wiki/Denny_Hulme" title="Denny Hulme">Denny Hulme</a> and <a href="/wiki/Derek_Bell_(racing_driver)" title="Derek Bell (racing driver)">Derek Bell</a> shared the third row.<sup id="cite_ref-chicanef1968_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-chicanef1968-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-gp_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gp-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1968_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The race was held over 68 laps of the <a href="/wiki/Autodromo_Nazionale_di_Monza" title="Autodromo Nazionale di Monza">Autodromo Nazionale di Monza</a>, taking place in sunny conditions, with <a href="/wiki/John_Surtees" title="John Surtees">John Surtees</a> leading straight from the start. By the end of first lap, <a href="/wiki/Bruce_McLaren" title="Bruce McLaren">Bruce McLaren</a> was ahead. McLaren stayed in front until Surtees slipstreamed back into the lead. The following lap McLaren was ahead again, while the leading <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> of <a href="/wiki/Chris_Amon" title="Chris Amon">Chris Amon</a> lost control on oil dropped by one of the <a href="/wiki/Honda_RA301" title="Honda RA301">Honda RA301s</a> and his car flew over the barriers into the trees at one of the fast Lesmo corners. Surtees also hit the wall trying to avoid the Ferrari. This put <a href="/wiki/Jo_Siffert" title="Jo Siffert">Jo Siffert</a> into second place, with <a href="/wiki/Jackie_Stewart" title="Jackie Stewart">Jackie Stewart</a> third. The Scotsman moved into second and a slipstreaming battle developed for the lead between McLaren, Stewart, Siffert and <a href="/wiki/Denny_Hulme" title="Denny Hulme">Denny Hulme</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-gp_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gp-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Bell_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bell-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>McLaren's <a href="/wiki/McLaren_M7A" title="McLaren M7A">M7A</a> had to stop for more oil on lap 35 and retired. Stewart disappeared on lap 43, when his <a href="/wiki/Cosworth_DFV" title="Cosworth DFV">Cosworth engine</a> failed. Hulme was by this stage already leading the race, and when Siffert went out with a rear suspension failure, nine laps from the end, Hulme was left to win. He won in a time of 1hr 40:14.8mins., averaging a speed of 146.284mph. There had been a lively battle behind him, between <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Servoz-Gavin" title="Johnny Servoz-Gavin">Johnny Servoz-Gavin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jacky_Ickx" title="Jacky Ickx">Jacky Ickx</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jochen_Rindt" title="Jochen Rindt">Jochen Rindt</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> of Ickx had emerged ahead, only to stop in the closing lap for more fuel. In the process, he dropped to third behind Servoz-Gavin, while Rindt had to retire with an engine failure. <a href="/wiki/Piers_Courage" title="Piers Courage">Piers Courage</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jean-Pierre_Beltoise" title="Jean-Pierre Beltoise">Jean-Pierre Beltoise</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Joakim_Bonnier" class="mw-redirect" title="Joakim Bonnier">Jo Bonnier</a> rounded out the top six, with no other finishers.<sup id="cite_ref-gp_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gp-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-formula1.com_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-formula1.com-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
