<p>The <b>2006 Australian Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>LXXI Foster's Australian Grand Prix</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at the <a href="/wiki/Melbourne_Grand_Prix_Circuit" class="mw-redirect" title="Melbourne Grand Prix Circuit">Melbourne Grand Prix Circuit</a> in <a href="/wiki/Albert_Park_and_Lake" title="Albert Park and Lake">Albert Park</a>, <a href="/wiki/Melbourne" title="Melbourne">Melbourne</a> on 2 April 2006. The 57-lap race was the 22nd <a href="/wiki/Australian_Grand_Prix" title="Australian Grand Prix">Australian Grand Prix</a> as part of the World Championship, and the 71st Australian Grand Prix overall. It was also the third round of the <a href="/wiki/2006_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2006 Formula One season">2006 Formula One season</a>; the Australian Grand Prix had been the season opener since 1996, but this race was held later due to the <a href="/wiki/2006_Commonwealth_Games" title="2006 Commonwealth Games">2006 Commonwealth Games</a> being held in Melbourne at the time of the opening round. The season was instead opened in <a href="/wiki/2006_Bahrain_Grand_Prix" title="2006 Bahrain Grand Prix">Bahrain</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a> regained its first round of the season slot for 2007.
</p><p>The race was won by <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> (Renault's third win from three races), with the <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> of <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> second. <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> finished in third place to take the last podium of his career with the <a href="/wiki/Toyota_Racing_(Formula_One_team)" title="Toyota Racing (Formula One team)">Toyota</a> team. Polesitter <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> retired from the race when his engine blew on the final lap. He eventually stopped roughly ten metres from the finish line, losing a points scoring position (fifth place) in the process.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Murray_Walker" title="Murray Walker">Murray Walker</a> made a return to the commentary box for a one-off with Australia's <a href="/wiki/Network_Ten" class="mw-redirect" title="Network Ten">Network Ten</a>.
</p><p>When <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> took the lead on lap 21 in his <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW28" title="Williams FW28">Williams</a>-<a href="/wiki/Cosworth" title="Cosworth">Cosworth</a> he became the first Australian driver to lead his home Grand Prix since <a href="/wiki/John_Bowe_(racing_driver)" title="John Bowe (racing driver)">John Bowe</a> led the early laps of the non-championship <a href="/wiki/1984_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="1984 Australian Grand Prix">1984 race</a> driving a <a href="/wiki/Ralt" title="Ralt">Ralt</a> RT4 <a href="/wiki/Ford_BDA" class="mw-redirect" title="Ford BDA">Ford</a>.
</p><p>This was the last race until the <a href="/wiki/2015_Mexican_Grand_Prix" title="2015 Mexican Grand Prix">2015 Mexican Grand Prix</a> that neither Ferrari was classified.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2006_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The race began with an unusual spin of <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a> as he completed the formation lap. He lined up at the back of the grid, however Fisichella then stalled his engine on the grid, forcing the start to be red-flagged. Fisichella had to start from the pitlane, while Montoya reclaimed his grid position.
</p><p>As in <a href="/wiki/2002_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="2002 Australian Grand Prix">2002</a>, the race had a first-lap accident. Felipe Massa was the victim in an incident involving himself, Nico Rosberg and Christian Klien. The Williams and Red Bull effectively sandwiched Massa's Ferrari as the Brazilian attempted to squeeze through a gap too small, pitching him into the wall hard and out of the race. Rosberg returned to the pits without the rear wing. Klien continued the race unharmed.
</p><p>These incidents called the Safety Car on the very first lap. Fisichella spun but continued, again in the Jones corner. Alonso passed Button when the SC was recalled two laps later, in the start/finish straight.
</p><p>On the third lap, Christian Klien had some problem with his suspension and crashed the barriers near Clark Chicane, and destroyed his car. Klien went out walking, happily. This crash had left many pieces of debris on the track and the Safety Car went out again to help the stewards clean the zone. When the race continued, Räikkönen attacked Button and gained his place. Montoya tried that as well, though unsuccessful for many laps.
</p><p>Vitantonio Liuzzi made an exciting pass on Michael Schumacher, by the Sports Centre turn.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Mark_Webber_Australia_2006.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/02/Mark_Webber_Australia_2006.jpg/220px-Mark_Webber_Australia_2006.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="113" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/02/Mark_Webber_Australia_2006.jpg/330px-Mark_Webber_Australia_2006.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/02/Mark_Webber_Australia_2006.jpg/440px-Mark_Webber_Australia_2006.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2815" data-file-height="1444" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Mark_Webber_Australia_2006.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> led his home race for <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a></div></div></div>
<p>The race continued uneventful for another 10 laps when pit stops began with Montoya, Button and Trulli. Fernando and Räikkönen stopped, giving <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> the lead of his home Grand Prix. A gearbox failure then eliminated him.
</p><p>Another uneventful stint was run until lap 32 when Montoya touched the grass in the Prost turn, but managed to return to the track. Michael Schumacher also had the same grass cross, although the German champion could not make it back and struck the barriers. He himself said that his car was not easy to drive, and that the tires were not warm enough. This incident called the Safety Car for the third time. Many drivers used this SC situation to go to pits. Räikkönen entered just before Montoya, making a queue. Thankfully for the Woking Team there was not much delay.
</p><p>On lap 35 the Safety Car was off and the race restarted. Alonso had a strategy to give an enormous gap with the SC and slow down the cars behind, and then blasted off and leave Räikkönen and Heidfeld with the trouble of Albers, creating a 3-second gap between the two leading cars.
</p><p>The 36th lap called the Safety Car once again. Vitantonio Liuzzi had jumped to the grass, crossed the road, bumped the left barrier, crossed the road again and struck the right barrier near Whiteford, leaving his car completely destroyed. The race restarted on lap 40, and Fernando did exactly the same thing and got a little advantage from Räikkönen. After the incident Liuzzi blamed Villeneuve, saying that the Canadian had screwed up one of his best races.
</p><p>Lap 39 brought another incident to the race. Tiago Monteiro abandoned due to a mechanical problem. Lap 46 took Montoya out of the race as he crossed the kerbs in the curve where Schumacher had had his accident. This caused the car to jump momentarily, causing an electrical problem which forced a total shutdown of the car, thus making him retire.
</p><p>The Australian Grand Prix was not free of incidents in the last laps. On the very final lap, in the final 2 corners, Jenson Button blew his engine, causing Fisichella right behind him to be covered in oil and struggling to control the car. Button ended up stalling the car about 10 metres from the finish line. He did so deliberately, giving up a points-scoring position, to avoid a 10 place position penalty in the next race.
</p><p>Alonso wrapped up his tenth career victory, while Räikkönen grabbed the second place on the podium and Ralf Schumacher scored what proved to be the sole podium for Toyota in the season, and ultimately, his final podium in Formula One. This was despite a drive-through penalty for speeding in the pit lane. After the race, the stewards were busy, handing out a reprimand to <a href="/wiki/Takuma_Sato" title="Takuma Sato">Takuma Sato</a> for failing to pay attention to blue flags, and a 25-second penalty to <a href="/wiki/Scott_Speed" title="Scott Speed">Scott Speed</a> for a similar offence, albeit with yellow flags. The American driver was also fined $5000 for using abusive language during a post-race hearing.
</p>
