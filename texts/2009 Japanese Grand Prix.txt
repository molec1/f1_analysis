<p>The <b>2009 Japanese Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>XXXV Fuji Television Japanese Grand Prix</b>) was the fifteenth round of the <a href="/wiki/2009_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2009 Formula One season">2009 Formula One season</a>. The race was held at the <a href="/wiki/Suzuka_Circuit" class="mw-redirect" title="Suzuka Circuit">Suzuka Circuit</a> on 4 October 2009.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> of <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a> won the race ahead of <a href="/wiki/Toyota_Racing_(Formula_One_team)" title="Toyota Racing (Formula One team)">Toyota</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> and <a href="/wiki/2008_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2008 Formula One World Championship">2008</a> World Champion <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> leading through the whole race.
</p><p>With two races remaining in the 2009 season, Vettel's win maintained his slim hopes of winning the Drivers Championship, with Championship leader <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> finishing eighth, one place behind <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a>. The result left Button and Barrichello's <a href="/wiki/Brawn_GP" title="Brawn GP">Brawn</a> team half a point away from clinching the Constructors Championship.<sup id="cite_ref-Vet,_But_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Vet,_But-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2009_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2009_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> led <a href="/wiki/Brawn_GP" title="Brawn GP">Brawn GP</a> teammate <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> by 15 points going into the race, with <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> 10 points further back driving for <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a>. <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> could no longer win the Championship.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Brawn also led Red Bull by 42½ points in the Constructors' Championship, and only needed to maintain this lead to take the trophy. <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> and <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> were 3 points apart in the Constructors' Championship in 3rd and 4th, with their lead drivers <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> also separated by 3 points in 5th and 6th in the Drivers' Championship; Ferrari led on both counts.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> won the <a href="/wiki/2008_Japanese_Grand_Prix" title="2008 Japanese Grand Prix">2008 Japanese Grand Prix</a> for <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a>, which was held at <a href="/wiki/Fuji_Speedway" title="Fuji Speedway">Fuji Speedway</a>. Alonso also won the last race at Suzuka, in <a href="/wiki/2006_Japanese_Grand_Prix" title="2006 Japanese Grand Prix">2006</a>. Other former winners lining up include <a href="/wiki/Brawn_GP" title="Brawn GP">Brawn GP</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> and <a href="/wiki/2008_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2008 Formula One season">World Champion</a> <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>. It was the first time since <a href="/wiki/1991_Japanese_Grand_Prix" title="1991 Japanese Grand Prix">1991</a> that a Schumacher (<a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael</a> or <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf</a>) was not on the grid at Suzuka.
</p><p>Eight of the drivers had not raced at Suzuka in Formula One before, owing to the <a href="/wiki/Fuji_Speedway" title="Fuji Speedway">Fuji Speedway</a> having hosted the Japanese Grand Prix in 2007 and 2008. Those drivers included <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a>, local driver <a href="/wiki/Kazuki_Nakajima" title="Kazuki Nakajima">Kazuki Nakajima</a>, <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a>, <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9bastien_Buemi" title="Sébastien Buemi">Sébastien Buemi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jaime_Alguersuari" title="Jaime Alguersuari">Jaime Alguersuari</a> and <a href="/wiki/Romain_Grosjean" title="Romain Grosjean">Romain Grosjean</a>. However, both Vettel and Sutil had driven the circuit in practice sessions before when they served as test drivers in <a href="/wiki/2006_Japanese_Grand_Prix" title="2006 Japanese Grand Prix">2006</a>.
</p><p>The 2009 Japanese Grand Prix saw <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a> all start their fiftieth race in Formula One.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice">Practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2009_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3><p>
The Friday practice sessions were dominated by heavy rain that severely reduced running time. <a href="/wiki/Toyota_Racing_(Formula_One_team)" title="Toyota Racing (Formula One team)">Toyota's</a> <a href="/wiki/Timo_Glock" title="Timo Glock">Timo Glock</a> was advised not to drive as he was suffering from a fever, and was replaced by <a href="/wiki/GP2_series" class="mw-redirect" title="GP2 series">GP2</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Kamui_Kobayashi" title="Kamui Kobayashi">Kamui Kobayashi</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup></p><div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Kamui_Kobayashi_2009_Japan_1st_Free_Practice.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c1/Kamui_Kobayashi_2009_Japan_1st_Free_Practice.jpg/220px-Kamui_Kobayashi_2009_Japan_1st_Free_Practice.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c1/Kamui_Kobayashi_2009_Japan_1st_Free_Practice.jpg/330px-Kamui_Kobayashi_2009_Japan_1st_Free_Practice.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c1/Kamui_Kobayashi_2009_Japan_1st_Free_Practice.jpg/440px-Kamui_Kobayashi_2009_Japan_1st_Free_Practice.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1280" data-file-height="853" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Kamui_Kobayashi_2009_Japan_1st_Free_Practice.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Kamui_Kobayashi" title="Kamui Kobayashi">Kamui Kobayashi</a> filled in for <a href="/wiki/Timo_Glock" title="Timo Glock">Timo Glock</a> during Friday practice.</div></div></div>
<p>The first practice session saw <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> set the early pace, with lap times rapidly falling as the track dried out. The second half of the session was dominated by local driver <a href="/wiki/Kazuki_Nakajima" title="Kazuki Nakajima">Kazuki Nakajima</a>, who narrowly missed out on setting the fastest time after McLaren's <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> set a time three tenths of a second quicker in the final phase.<sup id="cite_ref-PrReview_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PrReview-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Championship contenders <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> finished 10th, 17th and 18th respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-Pr1_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pr1-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Adrian_Sutil_2009_Japan_1st_Free_Practice.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/83/Adrian_Sutil_2009_Japan_1st_Free_Practice.jpg/220px-Adrian_Sutil_2009_Japan_1st_Free_Practice.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="146" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/83/Adrian_Sutil_2009_Japan_1st_Free_Practice.jpg/330px-Adrian_Sutil_2009_Japan_1st_Free_Practice.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/83/Adrian_Sutil_2009_Japan_1st_Free_Practice.jpg/440px-Adrian_Sutil_2009_Japan_1st_Free_Practice.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1280" data-file-height="848" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Adrian_Sutil_2009_Japan_1st_Free_Practice.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a> during the first practice session of the weekend, which was run in wet conditions.</div></div></div>
<p>The rain increased markedly for the second session, which saw very limited running in the first hour with <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso's</a> <a href="/wiki/Jaime_Alguersuari" title="Jaime Alguersuari">Jaime Alguersuari</a> and <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9bastien_Buemi" title="Sébastien Buemi">Sébastien Buemi</a> being the only drivers to set times. Owing to poor visibility and limited tyre allocations, many of the drivers elected to do only a handful of laps; Nico Rosberg did just seven in comparison to the twenty he had done in the first session, a pattern followed by many other drivers.<sup id="cite_ref-PrH_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PrH-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Heikki Kovalainen and both <a href="/wiki/Brawn_GP" title="Brawn GP">Brawn GP</a> drivers elected not to take to the circuit. <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a> went on to set the fastest lap of the session with Sebastian Vettel in second, both of them some seven seconds slower than Kovalainen's first session time.<sup id="cite_ref-Pr2_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pr2-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Saturday's third and final practice session started with contact between championship leader Button and Alguersuari that saw the Brawn driver require a replacement front wing. The session was largely dominated by Alguersuari's Toro Rosso stablemate Buemi, with fellow Red Bull compatriot Sebastian Vettel a close second until <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> set a time six hundredths of a second quicker on his final lap.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The hour-long session was dry, with Trulli's 1:31.709 almost ten seconds faster than Heikki Kovalainen's FP1 time and 17 seconds quicker than Adrian Sutil in FP2. At the end of the hour, championship contenders Vettel, Barrichello and Button were fourth, seventh and ninth respectively and separated by just over two hundredths of a second; just one second ultimately covered the top 16 drivers. Elsewhere, Timo Glock returned to his seat whilst <a href="/wiki/Romain_Grosjean" title="Romain Grosjean">Romain Grosjean</a> out-paced <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a> teammate <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>. <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull's</a> <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> crashed near the Degner Corner, damaging his car to such an extent that he was unable to take part in qualifying.<sup id="cite_ref-Webber_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Webber-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> As the damage was heavy enough to require the chassis to be re-built, Webber had to start the race from the pit lane.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2009_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Williams_duo_2009_Japan.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/99/Williams_duo_2009_Japan.jpg/220px-Williams_duo_2009_Japan.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="149" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/99/Williams_duo_2009_Japan.jpg/330px-Williams_duo_2009_Japan.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/99/Williams_duo_2009_Japan.jpg/440px-Williams_duo_2009_Japan.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2560" data-file-height="1729" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Williams_duo_2009_Japan.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kazuki_Nakajima" title="Kazuki Nakajima">Kazuki Nakajima</a> qualified in 11th and 17th places respectively.</div></div></div>
<p>With Webber unable to take part in qualifying, only four drivers would be eliminated in the first session. An early spin into the barriers by Sébastien Buemi looked as if that number would become three, but his team repaired the car in time for him to do a single fast lap. The early pace-setter was Sebastian Vettel, who exchanged blows with the Toyota of Trulli before ultimately coming out on top by the end of the session. Meanwhile, Jaime Alguersuari made the second part of qualifying for the first time in his short career, with <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kazuki_Nakajima" title="Kazuki Nakajima">Kazuki Nakajima</a>, <a href="/wiki/Romain_Grosjean" title="Romain Grosjean">Romain Grosjean</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vitantonio_Liuzzi" title="Vitantonio Liuzzi">Vitantonio Liuzzi</a> joining Webber in an early exit from qualifying.
The second session was dominated by two accidents that both resulted in red flags. The first was <a href="/wiki/Jaime_Alguersuari" title="Jaime Alguersuari">Jaime Alguersuari</a>, who speared off at the Degner Curve, the same place as Webber in the earlier practice session and the scene of Buemi's off-track excursion in the first segment of qualifying. The second accident was more serious, with <a href="/wiki/Timo_Glock" title="Timo Glock">Timo Glock</a> crashing heavily at the final corner. The session was stopped for some time as medics attended to him and his car was removed. The session resumed with eight minutes remaining and saw a final push by the Brawn drivers to make it into the top ten shootout. The final moments were marked by another off by Buemi at Spoon, which left debris on the circuit as he limped back to the pits. Glock was flown by helicopter to hospital during Q3 with a wound on his left leg, and back pain.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The times set by the Brawn drivers were threatened should someone protest following an incident where they set their best – and only – times during a yellow flag period.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> joined the growing list of drivers who crashed at the Degner Curve early in the third part of qualifying, though the damage was minimal and the circuit quickly cleared. The damage to <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9bastien_Buemi" title="Sébastien Buemi">Sébastien Buemi</a>'s car after his earlier off was deemed too great to be able to repair in time, and like Kovalainen, he failed to set a time, meaning that Q3 would be contested by eight drivers. When the track had re-opened after Kovalainen's accident, there were just five minutes remaining on the clock. <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> once again topped the timing sheets, putting him in a strong position to keep his title campaign alive, while rival championship contenders Barrichello and Button could only manage fifth and seventh respectively. <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> qualified the sole remaining Toyota in second in his team's home race, with reigning World Champion <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> in third.
</p><p>After qualifying, <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a>, <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> and <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a> were all called before the stewards on charges of speeding during the yellow-flag caused by <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9bastien_Buemi" title="Sébastien Buemi">Sébastien Buemi</a>'s off in the final moments of Q2. Buemi himself was later summoned on the charge of driving a damaged car. All of the drivers except for Heidfeld and Kubica were given five-place grid penalties.<sup id="cite_ref-gridpens_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gridpens-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2009_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> led the race from the start; aside from a brief attempt by <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> at passing him into the first corner, the <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a> driver would remain relatively unthreatened. The opening lap was clean, with the only major changes in position coming from <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9bastien_Buemi" title="Sébastien Buemi">Sébastien Buemi</a>, who nearly stalled, and <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a>, who fell from 10th down to 12th behind the <a href="/wiki/BMW_Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="BMW Sauber">BMW Sauber</a> of Robert Kubica and the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> of <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a>. Button subsequently reclaimed those positions within the opening laps.
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Sebastian_Vettel_won_2009_Japanese_GP.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/38/Sebastian_Vettel_won_2009_Japanese_GP.jpg/220px-Sebastian_Vettel_won_2009_Japanese_GP.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="141" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/38/Sebastian_Vettel_won_2009_Japanese_GP.jpg/330px-Sebastian_Vettel_won_2009_Japanese_GP.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/38/Sebastian_Vettel_won_2009_Japanese_GP.jpg/440px-Sebastian_Vettel_won_2009_Japanese_GP.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2560" data-file-height="1644" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Sebastian_Vettel_won_2009_Japanese_GP.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> won the race, leading every lap after starting from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>Hamilton slotted into second place while <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> assumed third. Further back, <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a>, having started from the pit lane, pitted three times in the opening four laps; the first two due to his headrest coming loose, and the third for new tyres after he picked up a puncture. Most of the overtaking in the early stages of the Grand Prix took place with the first wave of stops, Vettel remaining unchallenged for the lead. An incident at the Casio Triangle saw <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a> and <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> collide at relatively low speed, allowing championship leader Button to sneak through into a points position.
</p><p>The middle stint of the race was as quiet as the first, with the only passes being Kovalainen out-dragging Fisichella in the pit exit lane, and a mistake from Hamilton allowing Trulli to pass him as he emerged from the pits. A problem with the <a href="/wiki/Kinetic_Energy_Recovery_Systems" class="mw-redirect" title="Kinetic Energy Recovery Systems">KERS</a> unit on his car meant that Hamilton subsequently struggled to stay in touch with the Toyota driver. Meanwhile, the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso</a> drivers had not fared well, with a clutch problem spelling an early retirement for Buemi, whilst <a href="/wiki/Jaime_Alguersuari" title="Jaime Alguersuari">Jaime Alguersuari</a> lost control after touching the astro turf on the outside of 130R which resulted in a spin into the tyre wall and the safety car being deployed ten laps from the end.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Jarno_Trulli_2009_Japan.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e9/Jarno_Trulli_2009_Japan.jpg/220px-Jarno_Trulli_2009_Japan.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="154" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e9/Jarno_Trulli_2009_Japan.jpg/330px-Jarno_Trulli_2009_Japan.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e9/Jarno_Trulli_2009_Japan.jpg/440px-Jarno_Trulli_2009_Japan.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2500" data-file-height="1745" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Jarno_Trulli_2009_Japan.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> took second position at <a href="/wiki/Toyota_Racing_(Formula_One_team)" title="Toyota Racing (Formula One team)">Toyota</a>'s home race.</div></div></div>
<p>By the time the wreckage from Alguersuari's accident was cleared away and the track considered safe for racing once more, there were just five laps remaining. Vettel continued to put as much space between him and second place as possible, while ninth-placed Kubica threatened Jenson Button in an attempt to wrest the single championship point for eighth place away from him. Button prevailed after deciding not to challenge teammate <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> for seventh. He later protested to the stewards that <a href="/wiki/Williams_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams F1">Williams'</a> <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> had been speeding under the safety car – though Rosberg was acquitted after a stewards' hearing found mitigating circumstances.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Vettel ultimately won the race from Trulli and Hamilton with <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> in fourth. Mark Webber, although down in last and 2 laps down, took the fastest lap on the last lap by 0.002 seconds to deny Vettel a Grand Chelem. His victory and subsequent ten championship points meant Vettel stayed in contention for the drivers' title, 16 points adrift of Button, who kept a 14-point lead over Barrichello going into the <a href="/wiki/2009_Brazilian_Grand_Prix" title="2009 Brazilian Grand Prix">penultimate race</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Vettel was confident he could make up the large points difference, saying his Red Bull team had the momentum,<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> while Button admitted he would be focusing on an error-free end to the season to try to clinch his first Driver's title.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
