<p>The <b>1990 German Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at <a href="/wiki/Hockenheimring" title="Hockenheimring">Hockenheimring</a> on 29 July 1990. It was the ninth race of the <a href="/wiki/1990_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1990 Formula One World Championship">1990 Formula One World Championship</a>. The race was the 52nd <a href="/wiki/German_Grand_Prix" title="German Grand Prix">German Grand Prix</a> and the 14th to be held at the Hockenheimring. It was the 39th and last Formula One Grand Prix to be held in <a href="/wiki/West_Germany" title="West Germany">West Germany</a> prior to its re-unification with <a href="/wiki/East_Germany" title="East Germany">East Germany</a>. The race was held over 45 laps of the seven kilometre circuit for a race distance of 306 kilometres.
</p><p>The race was won by 1988 World Champion, Brazilian driver <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> driving a <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/5B" class="mw-redirect" title="McLaren MP4/5B">McLaren MP4/5B</a>. He took a six-second victory over Italian driver <a href="/wiki/Alessandro_Nannini" title="Alessandro Nannini">Alessandro Nannini</a> driving a <a href="/wiki/Benetton_B190" title="Benetton B190">Benetton B190</a> who was just two seconds in front of Senna's Austrian teammate <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a>.
</p><p>Senna's win put him back into the championship points lead, four ahead of Ferrari driver <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1990_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-qualifying_report">Pre-qualifying report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1990_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Pre-qualifying report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The participants in the Friday morning pre-qualifying sessions were slightly reshuffled prior to this event, the season having reached its mid-point. <a href="/wiki/Larrousse" title="Larrousse">Larrousse</a> had scored enough points during the previous two half-seasons to be relieved of the requirement to pre-qualify, and could automatically join the rest of the field in the main qualifying sessions from here on. A failure to score any points so far this season meant that <a href="/wiki/Equipe_Ligier" title="Equipe Ligier">Ligier</a> were forced to join the pre-qualifying pool. <a href="/wiki/Osella" title="Osella">Osella</a>, <a href="/wiki/Automobiles_Gonfaronnaises_Sportives" title="Automobiles Gonfaronnaises Sportives">AGS</a>, <a href="/wiki/EuroBrun" title="EuroBrun">EuroBrun</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Coloni" title="Scuderia Coloni">Coloni</a> and <a href="/wiki/Life_Racing_Engines" title="Life Racing Engines">Life</a> were all still required to pre-qualify.<sup id="cite_ref-Walker_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Walker-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Here at Hockenheim, Ligier were comfortably first and second in the one-hour session, with <a href="/wiki/Philippe_Alliot" title="Philippe Alliot">Philippe Alliot</a> outpacing team-mate <a href="/wiki/Nicola_Larini" title="Nicola Larini">Nicola Larini</a> in their <a href="/wiki/Cosworth_DFR" class="mw-redirect" title="Cosworth DFR">Ford</a>-powered <a href="/wiki/Ligier_JS33" title="Ligier JS33">JS33Bs</a>. Third fastest was <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Grouillard" title="Olivier Grouillard">Olivier Grouillard</a> in the Osella, and <a href="/wiki/Yannick_Dalmas" title="Yannick Dalmas">Yannick Dalmas</a> was fourth in his AGS. It was Grouillard's eighth pre-qualification from nine events, and Dalmas' third.
</p><p>Those missing out on pre-qualification included fifth-placed <a href="/wiki/Gabriele_Tarquini" title="Gabriele Tarquini">Gabriele Tarquini</a> in the other AGS, a second slower than his team-mate, and <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Moreno" title="Roberto Moreno">Roberto Moreno</a>, sixth in the EuroBrun. Coloni, having parted ways with <a href="/wiki/Subaru" title="Subaru">Subaru</a> and their uncompetitive <a href="/wiki/Flat_12" class="mw-redirect" title="Flat 12">flat 12</a> engine, appeared with the modified but untested <a href="/wiki/Coloni_C3C" class="mw-redirect" title="Coloni C3C">C3C</a>, powered by a customer <a href="/wiki/Cosworth" title="Cosworth">Cosworth</a> DFR V8 engine. <a href="/wiki/Bertrand_Gachot" title="Bertrand Gachot">Bertrand Gachot</a> spun the car during the session and damaged it, failing to pre-qualify for the ninth consecutive time this season. Also ending his involvement at this early stage for the ninth Grand Prix in a row was <a href="/wiki/Claudio_Langes" title="Claudio Langes">Claudio Langes</a> in the other EuroBrun, ahead only of <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Giacomelli" title="Bruno Giacomelli">Bruno Giacomelli</a>, who was again a far distant last in the Life.<sup id="cite_ref-Walker_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Walker-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying_report">Qualifying report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1990_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Qualifying report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After three straight wins by <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferraris</a> Alain Prost, <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Honda_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Honda F1">Honda</a> showed that they were back on top in qualifying. Honda promised McLaren a more powerful <a href="/wiki/V10_engine" title="V10 engine">V10 engine</a> at Hockenheim and they delivered with pole man Senna and second placed Berger (only 0.2 seconds slower) the only drivers to lap in the 1:40's and they did this during the first qualifying session- an indication of the McLaren-Honda car's superior pace at one of the fastest Grand Prix circuits of the year. The <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_641" title="Ferrari 641">Ferraris</a> of Prost and <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> were over 1.5 seconds slower (with the Englishman complaining of a down on power engine compared to his team mate), and the <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW13B" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams FW13B">Williams</a>-<a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renaults</a> of <a href="/wiki/Riccardo_Patrese" title="Riccardo Patrese">Riccardo Patrese</a> and <a href="/wiki/Thierry_Boutsen" title="Thierry Boutsen">Thierry Boutsen</a> occupying the 3rd row. So confident were McLaren that their times would not be beaten that both Senna and Berger did over 20 laps each and spent the entire second qualifying session working on their race set up without actually setting anything like a qualifying time, their fastest times over 6 seconds slower than the previous day.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> McLaren also introduced a new aerodynamic cowling cover for the <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/5" title="McLaren MP4/5">MP4/5B</a> at Hockenheim designed to lessen the buffeting effect on the drivers heads by diverting air to the sides of the car. This would remain on the car for the rest of the season.
</p><p>Rounding out the top 10 were <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a> (<a href="/wiki/Benetton_B190" title="Benetton B190">Benetton</a>-<a href="/wiki/Cosworth#HB" title="Cosworth">Ford</a>), <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a> (<a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_019" title="Tyrrell 019">Tyrrell</a>-<a href="/wiki/Cosworth_DFR" class="mw-redirect" title="Cosworth DFR">Ford</a>), <a href="/wiki/Alessandro_Nannini" title="Alessandro Nannini">Alessandro Nannini</a> (Benetton-Ford) who had a fiery crash during Friday qualifying at the Bremsschikane 2 after riding a curb and hitting the barrier hard enough to throw the car into the air and back onto the track causing the session to be stopped. Nannini survived the crash with little more than bruised pride. The 10th fastest qualifier was <a href="/wiki/Ivan_Capelli" title="Ivan Capelli">Ivan Capelli</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Leyton_House_CG901" title="Leyton House CG901">Leyton House</a>-<a href="/wiki/Judd_(engine)#EV" title="Judd (engine)">Judd</a>. Such was the spread in times that Capelli was some 4.151 seconds slower than Senna.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1990_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_report">Race report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1990_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Race report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The circuit's fast nature resulted in several engine failures, although the four big teams of the era took all six points-paying positions. The first driver to retire with an engine failure was <a href="/wiki/Michele_Alboreto" title="Michele Alboreto">Michele Alboreto</a> on lap 11, he was then followed out by <a href="/wiki/Mauricio_Gugelmin" class="mw-redirect" title="Mauricio Gugelmin">Mauricio Gugelmin</a> and <a href="/wiki/David_Brabham" title="David Brabham">David Brabham</a> 2 laps later along with <a href="/wiki/Pierluigi_Martini" title="Pierluigi Martini">Pierluigi Martini</a> in the only Minardi by lap 21. Also involved with the engine failures were <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a> on lap 24 (it was his first DNF of the season) and <a href="/wiki/Satoru_Nakajima" title="Satoru Nakajima">Satoru Nakajima</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_Racing" title="Tyrrell Racing">Tyrrell</a> one lap later.
</p><p>Berger got a stronger start off the line than Senna did but the Brazilian was able to hold on to the lead through the first corner. Senna lead the first lap ahead of Berger, Prost, Mansell, Patrese and Piquet. The order of the top 6 remained the same until the 11th lap when Piquet made an attempt to pass Patrese in the <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a>, but had to take an escape road and was overtaken for 6th by teammate Nannini as a result. Meanwhile Mansell in the second Ferrari missed his braking point at the Ostkurve chicane on lap 14 and broke his front wing which also damaged his car, his fourth position was not lost but he was forced to retire in the pits 2 laps later due to the damage being too great. Back upfront Nannini overtook Patrese for the lead as the Williams and Benetton teams had planned to go the race distance with no pit-stops. However, the gamble for Williams had failed since Patrese's tires were badly damaged in the closing stages and was forced to eventually pit. While Boutsen in the other Williams was eventually overtaken by Prost for fourth position and by teammate Patrese for 5th, but managed to hold on to 6th for the closing laps. Senna eventually passed Nannini on lap 34 whilst overlapping <a href="/wiki/JJ_Lehto" title="JJ Lehto">JJ Lehto</a> and eventually took the victory. <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> won the race by 6 seconds ahead of <a href="/wiki/Alessandro_Nannini" title="Alessandro Nannini">Alessandro Nannini</a> in the remaining <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> in the second <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> in the remaining <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ricardo_Patrese" class="mw-redirect" title="Ricardo Patrese">Ricardo Patrese</a> and <a href="/wiki/Thierry_Boutsen" title="Thierry Boutsen">Thierry Boutsen</a> in the Williams.
</p><p>Both <a href="/wiki/Onyx_Grand_Prix" title="Onyx Grand Prix">Onyx Grand Prix</a> team mates <a href="/wiki/JJ_Lehto" title="JJ Lehto">JJ Lehto</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gregor_Foitek" title="Gregor Foitek">Gregor Foitek</a> both got their renamed Monteverdi ORE-1Bs into the race, the last time either car would qualify for a race prior to the team's dissolution in August.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Philippe_Alliot" title="Philippe Alliot">Philippe Alliot</a> was disqualified because marshals helped his <a href="/wiki/Ligier_JS33B" class="mw-redirect" title="Ligier JS33B">Ligier JS33B</a> rejoin the race after being blocked by the spinning <a href="/wiki/Dallara_190" class="mw-redirect" title="Dallara 190">Dallara 190</a> of <a href="/wiki/Emanuele_Pirro" title="Emanuele Pirro">Emanuele Pirro</a>. Fastest lap of the race was set by Thierry Boutsen in his <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a>, his first such achievement.
</p>
