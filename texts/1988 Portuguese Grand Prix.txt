<p>The <b>1988 Portuguese Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 25 September 1988 at the <a href="/wiki/Aut%C3%B3dromo_do_Estoril" class="mw-redirect" title="Autódromo do Estoril">Autódromo do Estoril</a>, <a href="/wiki/Estoril" title="Estoril">Estoril</a>. It was the thirteenth race of the <a href="/wiki/1988_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1988 Formula One World Championship">1988 Formula One World Championship</a>. The 71-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/4" title="McLaren MP4/4">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Honda_in_Formula_One" title="Honda in Formula One">Honda</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Ivan_Capelli" title="Ivan Capelli">Ivan Capelli</a> second in a <a href="/wiki/March_881" title="March 881">March</a>-<a href="/wiki/Judd_(engine)" title="Judd (engine)">Judd</a> and <a href="/wiki/Thierry_Boutsen" title="Thierry Boutsen">Thierry Boutsen</a> third in a <a href="/wiki/Benetton_B188" title="Benetton B188">Benetton</a>-<a href="/wiki/Ford_Motor_Company" title="Ford Motor Company">Ford</a>. Prost's teammate and Drivers' Championship rival, <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a>, could only manage sixth.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1988_Portuguese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> gained their 9th front row of the year with <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> in front of <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> for the first time since <a href="/wiki/1988_French_Grand_Prix" title="1988 French Grand Prix">France</a>. Prost, whose <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/4" title="McLaren MP4/4">MP4/4</a> had a brand new, stiffer chassis, set his fastest time early in Saturday qualifying, and then sat out the rest of the session, confident that Senna could not better the time. Senna indeed qualified second. They were ahead of <a href="/wiki/Ivan_Capelli" title="Ivan Capelli">Ivan Capelli</a> in the atmospheric <a href="/wiki/March_881" title="March 881">March</a>-<a href="/wiki/Judd_(engine)#CV" title="Judd (engine)">Judd</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Maur%C3%ADcio_Gugelmin" title="Maurício Gugelmin">Maurício Gugelmin</a> in fifth behind the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> of <a href="/wiki/1988_Italian_Grand_Prix" title="1988 Italian Grand Prix">Monza</a> winner <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a>. Both Capelli and Gugelmin expressed delight with their cars, especially in the fast corners.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>After finishing third and fourth in Italy, the <a href="/wiki/Arrows_A10" title="Arrows A10">Arrows</a>-<a href="/wiki/BMW_M12" title="BMW M12">Megatrons</a> of <a href="/wiki/Derek_Warwick" title="Derek Warwick">Derek Warwick</a> (10th) and <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Cheever" title="Eddie Cheever">Eddie Cheever</a> (18th) struggled in Portugal. Both drivers complained of little grip on the rarely used Estoril circuit, which was compounded by slow turns which exposed the <a href="/wiki/Straight-4" class="mw-redirect" title="Straight-4">Straight-4</a> Megatron engine's lack of throttle response.
</p><p>After missing the previous two races in <a href="/wiki/1988_Belgian_Grand_Prix" title="1988 Belgian Grand Prix">Belgium</a> and <a href="/wiki/1988_Italian_Grand_Prix" title="1988 Italian Grand Prix">Italy</a> due to illness, <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> was back in his <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW12" title="Williams FW12">Williams</a>-Judd. He complained of a sore neck but qualified 6th, less than 0.1 behind Gugelmin.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Julian_Bailey" title="Julian Bailey">Julian Bailey</a> (<a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_017" title="Tyrrell 017">Tyrrell</a>-<a href="/wiki/Cosworth_DFZ" class="mw-redirect" title="Cosworth DFZ">Ford</a>), <a href="/wiki/Stefano_Modena" title="Stefano Modena">Stefano Modena</a> (<a href="/wiki/EuroBrun_ER188" title="EuroBrun ER188">EuroBrun</a>-Ford), and the <a href="/wiki/Zakspeed_881" title="Zakspeed 881">Zakspeeds</a> of <a href="/wiki/Piercarlo_Ghinzani" title="Piercarlo Ghinzani">Piercarlo Ghinzani</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bernd_Schneider_(racing_driver)" title="Bernd Schneider (racing driver)">Bernd Schneider</a> all failed to qualify while <a href="/wiki/Oscar_Larrauri" title="Oscar Larrauri">Oscar Larrauri</a> (EuroBrun-Ford) failed to pre-qualify.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1988_Portuguese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The first start was aborted when the <a href="/wiki/Rial_Racing" title="Rial Racing">Rial</a>-<a href="/wiki/Cosworth_DFZ" class="mw-redirect" title="Cosworth DFZ">Ford</a> of <a href="/wiki/Andrea_de_Cesaris" title="Andrea de Cesaris">Andrea de Cesaris</a> stalled on the grid. The second start was aborted after <a href="/wiki/Derek_Warwick" title="Derek Warwick">Derek Warwick</a> stalled his <a href="/wiki/Arrows_A10" title="Arrows A10">Arrows A10</a> and was hit by de Cesaris, with <a href="/wiki/Luis_P%C3%A9rez-Sala" title="Luis Pérez-Sala">Luis Pérez-Sala</a> (<a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Satoru_Nakajima" title="Satoru Nakajima">Satoru Nakajima</a> (<a href="/wiki/Lotus_100T" title="Lotus 100T">Lotus</a>). <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> led off at the third start, to the delight of the Portuguese crowd. At the start of lap 2, <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> pulled out of Senna's slipstream to pass him. Senna moved violently over on Prost and forced him towards the pit wall at over 280&#160;km/h (174&#160;mph). The Frenchman nonetheless maintained his will to pass the Brazilian, not lifting off the throttle, and passed Senna into the first corner.
</p><p>Prost was not impressed with Senna's tactics and the pair exchanged words in the McLaren camp after the race. Senna countered Prost's anger by stating that he had almost been pushed onto the grass by Prost before the first corner after the start.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Prost's lead built up to 7 seconds and would last until the flag, while Senna was being troubled by an erratic (and ultimately false) fuel readout which was telling him he was using too much. This allowed Capelli to close right up on him. On lap 22 the Italian out-braked Senna to excited acclaim and opened up a gap on him. It was the first time all season other than when Prost suffered from severe misfires at both the <a href="/wiki/1988_British_Grand_Prix" title="1988 British Grand Prix">British</a> and Italian GP's that a McLaren-Honda had been passed on track by any other car. Soon after <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> also passed the McLaren, leaving Senna exposed to the attacks of <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a>'s <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a>. The Briton was closer on the corners, but the Honda turbo was superior in the fast sectors of the track.
</p><p>Berger had claimed the fastest lap in his pursuit of Capelli, but spun off on lap 36. He had accidentally set off his fire extinguisher, with the resultant freezing of his leg with carbon dioxide causing his foot to slip off the pedals. Berger had been attempting a cockpit adjustment to his car but on the bumpy Estoril surface simply hit the wrong button. On lap 55, Mansell and Senna attempted to lap the <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_Racing" title="Tyrrell Racing">Tyrrell</a> of <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Palmer" title="Jonathan Palmer">Jonathan Palmer</a>: during this manoeuvre Mansell hit the back off the McLaren and spun into the barriers though without any damage to Senna who continued on. Maurício Gugelmin, <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a> (<a href="/wiki/Lotus_100T" title="Lotus 100T">Lotus</a>-Honda), <a href="/wiki/Michele_Alboreto" title="Michele Alboreto">Michele Alboreto</a> (<a href="/wiki/Ferrari_F1/87#88C" title="Ferrari F1/87">Ferrari</a>), <a href="/wiki/Riccardo_Patrese" title="Riccardo Patrese">Riccardo Patrese</a> (Williams-Judd) and <a href="/wiki/Thierry_Boutsen" title="Thierry Boutsen">Thierry Boutsen</a> (<a href="/wiki/Benetton_B188" title="Benetton B188">Benetton</a>-<a href="/wiki/Cosworth_DFR" class="mw-redirect" title="Cosworth DFR">Ford</a>) were hotly contesting the last point place in 6th. On lap 29 Patrese retired with a split radiator and the next lap Piquet retired with clutch and gearbox problems. On lap 57, Senna, still having problems with his fuel readout, pitted for a tyre change and was passed by Alboreto, Boutsen and Derek Warwick, falling to 6th.
</p><p>Prost won from Capelli, who at one stage was catching the McLaren, but backed off to save his engine after seeing his teammate's car sitting beside the track with a dead engine; the gap at the line was 9.5 seconds and Prost himself was marginal on fuel. It was Capelli's first podium finish in Formula One and the first time a March had finished on the podium of a Grand Prix since the <a href="/wiki/1976_Italian_Grand_Prix" title="1976 Italian Grand Prix">1976 Italian Grand Prix</a>. Fuel problems were a feature of this race: on the very last corner of the race Alboreto's engine stuttered-his car was running out of fuel despite the gauge indicating he had plenty left. Boutsen passed him to get his 5th podium of the year and Warwick claimed 4th place. The dry Ferrari of Alboreto and McLaren of Senna were classified 5th and 6th.
</p><p>Prost's 5th win of the year, and his first since France, along with Senna's 6th place, saw him remain in strong contention for his third World Championship.
</p>
