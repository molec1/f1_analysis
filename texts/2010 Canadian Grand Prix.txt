<p>The <b>2010 Canadian Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>XLVI Grand Prix du Canada</b>) was the eighth round of the <a href="/wiki/2010_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2010 Formula One season">2010 Formula One season</a>. It was held in <a href="/wiki/Montreal" title="Montreal">Montreal, Quebec</a>, Canada at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_Gilles_Villeneuve" title="Circuit Gilles Villeneuve">Circuit Gilles Villeneuve</a> on 13 June 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> This was the first Grand Prix to be held on the <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North American</a> continent since the <a href="/wiki/2008_Canadian_Grand_Prix" title="2008 Canadian Grand Prix">2008 Canadian Grand Prix</a>. The race was won by <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>, his second Canadian Grand Prix victory, ahead of teammate <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> and <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_Canadian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_Canadian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After dominating the previous three races, it was widely expected that the Montreal circuit would not play to the Red Bull's strengths, with intense media speculation that the controversial crash between <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> whilst fighting for the lead of the <a href="/wiki/2010_Turkish_Grand_Prix" title="2010 Turkish Grand Prix">Turkish Grand Prix</a> would only worsen the team's chances. However, while neither driver was prepared to accept full responsibility for causing the accident, both expressed a desire to forget about it and move on.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>None of the teams planned to introduce major updates for the race, though several brought customised packages specifically for the high-speed, low-downforce circuit. Those who had developed an "F-duct" system for the previous race were still running it in the trial phase.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Free_Practice">Free Practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_Canadian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Free Practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Button_Canada_GP_2010_(cropped).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/83/Button_Canada_GP_2010_%28cropped%29.jpg/220px-Button_Canada_GP_2010_%28cropped%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="149" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/83/Button_Canada_GP_2010_%28cropped%29.jpg/330px-Button_Canada_GP_2010_%28cropped%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/83/Button_Canada_GP_2010_%28cropped%29.jpg/440px-Button_Canada_GP_2010_%28cropped%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3384" data-file-height="2291" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Button_Canada_GP_2010_(cropped).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> set the fastest time in the first free practice session.</div></div></div>
<p>The Friday sessions passed without incident save for minor off-track excursions by several drivers and a spin by <a href="/wiki/Pedro_de_la_Rosa" title="Pedro de la Rosa">Pedro de la Rosa</a> at l'Epingle. <a href="/wiki/Lucas_di_Grassi" title="Lucas di Grassi">Lucas di Grassi</a> was the only driver who failed to set a time in the first session after stopping on the circuit. While reigning World Champion <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> set the fastest time of the first session, <a href="/wiki/Hispania_Racing" class="mw-redirect" title="Hispania Racing">Hispania Racing</a> improved dramatically, beating both the <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Racing" title="Virgin Racing">Virgins</a>, while <a href="/wiki/Karun_Chandhok" title="Karun Chandhok">Karun Chandhok</a> also beat the <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus_(2010%E2%80%9311)" title="Team Lotus (2010–11)">Lotus</a> of <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a>. The session also showed the progress of the new teams as a whole, with <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> finishing the session just over a second adrift of <a href="/wiki/Jaime_Alguersuari" title="Jaime Alguersuari">Jaime Alguersuari</a>; the difference between the new and established teams had been as great as three seconds in <a href="/wiki/2010_Bahrain_Grand_Prix" title="2010 Bahrain Grand Prix">the first race</a> of the season. Kovalainen would go on to repeat his feat in the second session, finishing just over half a second behind Alguersuari and a full second ahead of Chandhok, the next-fastest driver.
</p><p>The biggest story of the day was the extreme tyre degradation the drivers experienced while running on the super-soft compound. Several sections of the circuit had been resurfaced since Formula One's previous visit in 2008, with the net result being that they offered comparatively little grip compared to other circuits. Hirohide Hamashima, <a href="/wiki/Bridgestone" title="Bridgestone">Bridgestone</a>'s director of motosport tyre development, commented that the problem was a result of the tyres being unable to reach their optimum operating temperature.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> With several teams expecting rain at some point during the weekend, the lack of grip and unpredictable conditions made setting the car up exceptionally difficult. Sebastian Vettel was the fastest driver in the session, narrowly outclassing the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> of <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a>' <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a>. Despite setting the fastest times of the first session, the <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLarens</a> both struggled, with <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> finishing seventh and Button only managing eleventh.
</p><p>McLaren bounded back in time for the third session, with Hamilton comfortably setting the fastest time of both the session and the weekend, ahead of Webber, Alonso and Schumacher. Hamilton was sighted clipping the concrete walls lining the circuit as one point, as did Felipe Massa, but both were able to continue without damage. Pedro de la Rosa very nearly came unstuck at the same point, but Karun Chandhok was less fortunate, his <a href="/wiki/Hispania_F110" title="Hispania F110">Hispania F110</a> stopping on the circuit early on. Nico Rosberg was sidelined with a clutch problem for most of the session. The only other incident was Lucas di Grassi losing control under braking at l'Epingle and coming to rest in the gravel trap shortly after he recorded the fastest speed – 324&#160;km/h (201&#160;mph) – through the speed trap.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_Canadian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Liuzzi_Canada_GP_2010_(cropped).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7e/Liuzzi_Canada_GP_2010_%28cropped%29.jpg/220px-Liuzzi_Canada_GP_2010_%28cropped%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="149" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7e/Liuzzi_Canada_GP_2010_%28cropped%29.jpg/330px-Liuzzi_Canada_GP_2010_%28cropped%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7e/Liuzzi_Canada_GP_2010_%28cropped%29.jpg/440px-Liuzzi_Canada_GP_2010_%28cropped%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3887" data-file-height="2632" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Liuzzi_Canada_GP_2010_(cropped).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Vitantonio_Liuzzi" title="Vitantonio Liuzzi">Vitantonio Liuzzi</a> started the race from a career best fifth position.</div></div></div>
<p>To compound the problems associated with heavy graining, overnight rain washed away whatever rubber had been laid down on the circuit during the Friday practice sessions, further reducing grip. With ambient temperatures being no higher than on Friday and the forecast predicting more of the same for the race, Bridgestone projected that a one-stop strategy in the race would be impossible with the harder prime compound losing up to seven seconds over half race distance, and the super-soft option tyres losing over sixteen seconds in the same window.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> With the rules dictating that drivers must start the race on the tyres they set their best qualifying time on, tyre selection for qualifying was therefore crucial.
</p><p>On Saturday afternoon, qualifying started without rain on the track. The first qualifying session had no surprises when both drivers from <a href="/wiki/Hispania_Racing" class="mw-redirect" title="Hispania Racing">HRT</a>, <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Racing" title="Virgin Racing">Virgin Racing</a> and <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus_(2010%E2%80%9311)" title="Team Lotus (2010–11)">Lotus</a> failed to make the cut, they were joined by <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">BMW Sauber</a> pilot <a href="/wiki/Kamui_Kobayashi" title="Kamui Kobayashi">Kamui Kobayashi</a>. The second qualifying session saw <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> miss the cut as the only surprise elimination. With two more cars setting faster times, Schumacher started the race from thirteenth. Barrichello was the fastest of this group followed by Hülkenberg, Schumacher, Petrov, Buemi, Alguersuari and de la Rosa.
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Schumacher_top_Canadian_GP_2010.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2a/Schumacher_top_Canadian_GP_2010.jpg/220px-Schumacher_top_Canadian_GP_2010.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2a/Schumacher_top_Canadian_GP_2010.jpg/330px-Schumacher_top_Canadian_GP_2010.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2a/Schumacher_top_Canadian_GP_2010.jpg/440px-Schumacher_top_Canadian_GP_2010.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3272" data-file-height="2181" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Schumacher_top_Canadian_GP_2010.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> qualified in thirteenth position.</div></div></div>
<p>At the third and final session all eyes were on <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> as he had set the fastest time in both qualifying sessions. But it was <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> who grabbed the lead early on. The teams were running different tyres, making the results unpredictable. On the very last lap, Hamilton snatched the pole from Webber who could not answer. With Hamilton ending Red Bull's run of pole positions after seven races, Webber was set to start second right in front of his teammate <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a>. <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> was fourth, <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> fifth followed by <a href="/wiki/Vitantonio_Liuzzi" title="Vitantonio Liuzzi">Vitantonio Liuzzi</a>, who qualified at a career-best sixth; <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a>, <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> completed the top ten.
</p><p>On the cool down lap, Hamilton was instructed by his team to slow down and stop on the circuit because the team realised he would not have enough fuel left in his tank for a sample to be taken by the FIA. Hamilton turned his car off and let it continue rolling down the back straight of the circuit. He undid his belts and sat higher up in the cockpit of his car, relaxed until his McLaren nearly came to a stop. Hamilton then got out and started to push his still rolling car down the back straight. A group of circuit marshalls finally came to help him and he was picked up by the course car and taken to the press conference. The FIA later handed Hamilton a reprimand and fined the McLaren team $10,000 for not completing the cool down lap in the given time, with Hamilton retaining his pole position.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_Canadian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Massa_Liuzzi_1st_Lap_Incident_2010_Canada.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/65/Massa_Liuzzi_1st_Lap_Incident_2010_Canada.jpg/220px-Massa_Liuzzi_1st_Lap_Incident_2010_Canada.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/65/Massa_Liuzzi_1st_Lap_Incident_2010_Canada.jpg/330px-Massa_Liuzzi_1st_Lap_Incident_2010_Canada.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/65/Massa_Liuzzi_1st_Lap_Incident_2010_Canada.jpg/440px-Massa_Liuzzi_1st_Lap_Incident_2010_Canada.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2565" data-file-height="1710" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Massa_Liuzzi_1st_Lap_Incident_2010_Canada.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The first lap saw contact between <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vitantonio_Liuzzi" title="Vitantonio Liuzzi">Vitantonio Liuzzi</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>The race was the first of the season in which all twenty-four cars started on the grid; prior to the Montreal race, at least one car—usually from <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Racing" title="Virgin Racing">Virgin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus_(2010%E2%80%9311)" title="Team Lotus (2010–11)">Lotus</a> or <a href="/wiki/Hispania_Racing" class="mw-redirect" title="Hispania Racing">Hispania</a>—was forced to start from the pit lane with a mechanical issue of some kind. <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> was demoted from second place on the grid to seventh after <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a> found iron filings in a sample of oil taken from the gearbox used in Webber's car during qualifying. This finding, which suggested damage to the internals of the gearbox and necessitated a gearbox change under parc ferme conditions, resulted in the five-place grid penalty.
</p><p>The opening lap saw drama unfold before the field had even cleared the start gantry. While Lewis Hamilton won the drag race to the first corner, in the middle of the pack, <a href="/wiki/Vitaly_Petrov" title="Vitaly Petrov">Vitaly Petrov</a> jumped the start and was forced onto the grassy verge as he attempted to go around the outside. This resulted in a spin that forced <a href="/wiki/Pedro_de_la_Rosa" title="Pedro de la Rosa">Pedro de la Rosa</a> to take evasive action; Petrov earned two drive-through penalties in the space of one hundred metres for his efforts and spent the rest of the race fighting with the new teams. <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vitantonio_Liuzzi" title="Vitantonio Liuzzi">Vitantonio Liuzzi</a> made contact three times in one corner, with the Italian getting spun around in the process and sliding down the order. As Hamilton, Vettel and Alonso established the running order, <a href="/wiki/Kamui_Kobayashi" title="Kamui Kobayashi">Kamui Kobayashi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a> tangled on the run into the final corners. While the <a href="/wiki/Williams_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams F1">Williams</a> driver cut the chicane to avoid further contact, Kobayashi was not as lucky and he became the Wall of Champions' 2010 victim. He retired a lap later with accident damage. After avoiding the spinning Petrov at the start, Kobayashi's <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a> teammate Pedro de la Rosa joined him on the sidelines shortly thereafter and gave the team the unenviable record of eleven retirements from sixteen starts.
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Kobaysahi_crash_Canadian_GP_2010_(cropped).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/77/Kobaysahi_crash_Canadian_GP_2010_%28cropped%29.jpg/220px-Kobaysahi_crash_Canadian_GP_2010_%28cropped%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="135" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/77/Kobaysahi_crash_Canadian_GP_2010_%28cropped%29.jpg/330px-Kobaysahi_crash_Canadian_GP_2010_%28cropped%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/77/Kobaysahi_crash_Canadian_GP_2010_%28cropped%29.jpg/440px-Kobaysahi_crash_Canadian_GP_2010_%28cropped%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1058" data-file-height="650" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Kobaysahi_crash_Canadian_GP_2010_(cropped).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Kamui_Kobayashi" title="Kamui Kobayashi">Kamui Kobayashi</a> became another victim of the "Wall of Champions".</div></div></div>
<p>The predicted early round of stops passed without incident, although Red Bull elected to run their drivers on separate strategies; Mark Webber ran the harder prime compound back-to-back with a finish on the softer options, while Vettel ran the options in his middle stint and picked up the primes for the run to the finish. Every other driver except Robert Kubica had qualified on and subsequently started the race with the softer options. The tyre lottery produced an unlikely winner with <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso</a>'s <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9bastien_Buemi" title="Sébastien Buemi">Sébastien Buemi</a> inheriting the lead for a lap before his stop, the first time a Toro Rosso had led a race since <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9bastien_Bourdais" title="Sébastien Bourdais">Sébastien Bourdais</a> led three laps at the <a href="/wiki/2008_Japanese_Grand_Prix" title="2008 Japanese Grand Prix">2008 Japanese Grand Prix</a>. Elsewhere in the field, Hülkenberg proved to be his own worst enemy when he over-extended himself under brakes while attempting to pass <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> at l'Epingle and damaging his front wing in the process. He was then flagged for speeding in the pit lane when he pitted to replace the wing, robbing himself of a potential points place as he was forced to serve a drive-through penalty.
</p><p>An accident between <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> and <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a> was narrowly avoided as Schumacher emerged from the first of his scheduled stops. Schumacher refused to yield on the approach to the fourth corner and the two took a short trip across the grassy verge. The altercation damaged Kubica's undertray while the incident was investigated by the stewards. It was the first of many incidents involving Schumacher, with the Mercedes driver later tangling with <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a> and <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a>. Massa's race was marked by a perpetual battle with the <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a> drivers including several near-misses in the second corner, the scene of his first-lap tangle with Liuzzi. Massa would later force his way past Sutil as the two closed in on the Lotus of <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a>, the cars running three-abreast into turn six. His late altercation with former Ferrari teammate Schumacher required him to pit for a replacement front wing and, like Hülkenberg before him, the Brazilian driver was cited for speeding in the pit lane. Twenty seconds were added to his time after the race as punishment.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Buemi_Canada_GP_2010_(cropped).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7d/Buemi_Canada_GP_2010_%28cropped%29.jpg/220px-Buemi_Canada_GP_2010_%28cropped%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="130" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7d/Buemi_Canada_GP_2010_%28cropped%29.jpg/330px-Buemi_Canada_GP_2010_%28cropped%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7d/Buemi_Canada_GP_2010_%28cropped%29.jpg/440px-Buemi_Canada_GP_2010_%28cropped%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4132" data-file-height="2438" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Buemi_Canada_GP_2010_(cropped).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9bastien_Buemi" title="Sébastien Buemi">Sébastien Buemi</a> led a race for the first time in his career.</div></div></div>
<p>Webber's tyre strategy initially paid off but, as the race wore on, his tyres began to deteriorate rapidly. Hamilton, running second at the time, quickly reduced the Australian's lead and caught him with twenty laps to go, dragging the Ferrari of Alonso through in the process. Webber eventually pitted, emerging behind teammate Vettel in fifth place as Vettel struggled with an unspecified but serious problem that he had to nurse to the finish; the team later clarified this as being related to the gearbox. As Hamilton settled back into the lead, reigning World Champion <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> took Alonso by surprise, passing him around the back half of the circuit and positioning McLaren for their second consecutive one-two finish. Button briefly attempted a run at his teammate, narrowing Hamilton's lead to just two seconds with ten laps to go, but Hamilton responded with a fast lap that dissuaded Button from making further attempts. The top five — Hamilton, Button, Alonso, Vettel and Webber — would remain in place until the very end with Vettel stopping on the circuit just after he crossed the finish line at the end of the race. Nico Rosberg fended off a late surge from Kubica to claim sixth while Buemi finished eighth and a lap down. Liuzzi and Sutil both found their way past Schumacher on the final lap — in Sutil's case this was in the final corner — as the Mercedes driver struggled with tyres that were almost completely out of grip and leaving the seven-time World Champion scoreless in what <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> commentator <a href="/wiki/Martin_Brundle" title="Martin Brundle">Martin Brundle</a> later described as the German's "worst weekend of his career".<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Kovalainen was the best of the new teams, two laps down and fighting off Petrov for the final phase of the race, while <a href="/wiki/Karun_Chandhok" title="Karun Chandhok">Karun Chandhok</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lucas_di_Grassi" title="Lucas di Grassi">Lucas di Grassi</a> were the final cars home, four and five laps down respectively.
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:McLaren_duo_1-2_finish_2010_Canada_(cropped).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e1/McLaren_duo_1-2_finish_2010_Canada_%28cropped%29.jpg/220px-McLaren_duo_1-2_finish_2010_Canada_%28cropped%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="101" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e1/McLaren_duo_1-2_finish_2010_Canada_%28cropped%29.jpg/330px-McLaren_duo_1-2_finish_2010_Canada_%28cropped%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e1/McLaren_duo_1-2_finish_2010_Canada_%28cropped%29.jpg/440px-McLaren_duo_1-2_finish_2010_Canada_%28cropped%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2785" data-file-height="1276" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:McLaren_duo_1-2_finish_2010_Canada_(cropped).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> scored their third 1–2 of the season.</div></div></div>
<p>The race was notably short of attrition compared to previous races at the Circuit Gilles Villeneuve, which have seen the safety car deployed so often that teams factor an accident into their strategies. However, the 2010 race was so short of retirements that it boasted the greatest number of finishers in the season to date with nineteen classified drivers. In addition to the dual retirements for BMW Sauber, <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Senna" title="Bruno Senna">Bruno Senna</a> was once again the victim of a gearbox problem while <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> stopped in the pit entry on lap forty-seven with terminal brake problems. <a href="/wiki/Timo_Glock" title="Timo Glock">Timo Glock</a> retired due to a steering rack leak that crippled his <a href="/wiki/Virgin_VR-01" title="Virgin VR-01">VR-01</a> on lap fifty-five.
</p><p>The final result meant that Hamilton leapfrogged both Button and Webber in the championship standings with six points covering the top three drivers. With McLaren claiming the lead of the constructors' championship from Red Bull in Turkey, their maximum points score in Montreal placed them a further twenty points clear of the Austrian team. The race was also the first time since the <a href="/wiki/1991_United_States_Grand_Prix" title="1991 United States Grand Prix">1991 United States Grand Prix</a> that three former drivers' champions stood on the podium.
</p>
