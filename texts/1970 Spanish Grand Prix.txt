<p>The <b>1970 Spanish Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held at the <a href="/wiki/Circuito_Permanente_Del_Jarama" class="mw-redirect" title="Circuito Permanente Del Jarama">Jarama</a> circuit on 19 April 1970. It was race 2 of 13 in both the <a href="/wiki/1970_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1970 World Championship of Drivers">1970 World Championship of Drivers</a> and the <a href="/wiki/1970_International_Cup_for_Formula_One_Manufacturers" class="mw-redirect" title="1970 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers">1970 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers</a>.
</p><p>Prior to the race, the organisers of the Grand Prix sparked anger amongst the members of <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_Constructors%27_Association" title="Formula One Constructors&#39; Association">FOCA</a> when they limited the number of starters to only sixteen. To add to the chaos, none of the laps set on Friday were counted towards qualifying. On the morning before the race, the matter seemed resolved and the organisers initially reversed their decision, and those who failed to qualify looked as if they would be allowed to start. The <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="F√©d√©ration Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">Commission Sportive Internationale</a> then stepped in and forced the Spanish organisers to revert to the original limit of sixteen starters, and the cars that failed to qualify were wheeled off the grid.<sup id="cite_ref-forix_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-forix-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The race was won by defending world champion <a href="/wiki/Jackie_Stewart" title="Jackie Stewart">Jackie Stewart</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/March_701" title="March 701">March 701</a>. American driver <a href="/wiki/Mario_Andretti" title="Mario Andretti">Mario Andretti</a> took his first Formula One podium in third place.<sup id="cite_ref-ms_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ms-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The race was marred by a serious accident involving <a href="/wiki/Jackie_Oliver" title="Jackie Oliver">Jackie Oliver</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jacky_Ickx" title="Jacky Ickx">Jacky Ickx</a>. Both their cars burst into flames, and Ickx was slightly burned after his race overalls became soaked in burning fuel. He recovered sufficiently to be able to compete in the next race, at <a href="/wiki/1970_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="1970 Monaco Grand Prix">Monaco</a>. <a href="/wiki/Bruce_McLaren" title="Bruce McLaren">Bruce McLaren</a> scored his last podium, points and race finish.
</p>
