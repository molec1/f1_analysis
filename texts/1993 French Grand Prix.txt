<p>The <b>1993 French Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Nevers_Magny-Cours" title="Circuit de Nevers Magny-Cours">Magny-Cours</a> on 4 July 1993. It was the eighth race of the <a href="/wiki/1993_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1993 Formula One World Championship">1993 Formula One World Championship</a>.
</p><p>The 72-lap race was won by home favourite <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW15C" title="Williams FW15C">Williams</a>-<a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>, after he started from second position. Prost's English teammate <a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a> finished second, having started from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> and led the first 26 laps, with German <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> third in a <a href="/wiki/Benetton_B193" title="Benetton B193">Benetton</a>-<a href="/wiki/Ford_Motor_Company" title="Ford Motor Company">Ford</a>. With Prost's Brazilian rival <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> finishing fourth in his <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/8" title="McLaren MP4/8">McLaren</a>-Ford, Prost extended his lead in the Drivers' Championship to 12 points.
</p><p>The race marked Prost's 100th Formula One podium, making him the first driver to achieve the milestone.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> It was also the last Grand Prix for <a href="/wiki/Fabrizio_Barbazza" title="Fabrizio Barbazza">Fabrizio Barbazza</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1993_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1993_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>With local hero Prost taking pole in all of the previous seven races, there was a massive turnout for qualifying where the Williams were usually dominant. The Williams did take 1–2 in qualifying, but it was Hill who took his first Formula One pole ahead of Prost. The <a href="/wiki/Equipe_Ligier" title="Equipe Ligier">Ligier</a> team, in its home race, filled the second row with <a href="/wiki/Martin_Brundle" title="Martin Brundle">Martin Brundle</a> ahead of <a href="/wiki/Mark_Blundell" title="Mark Blundell">Mark Blundell</a>, and thus completed a 1-2-3-4 for <a href="/wiki/Renault" title="Renault">Renault</a>-powered cars. <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> in the <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/8" title="McLaren MP4/8">McLaren</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_F93A" title="Ferrari F93A">Ferrari</a> were on the third row, Schumacher in the <a href="/wiki/Benetton_B193" title="Benetton B193">Benetton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Jordan_193" title="Jordan 193">Jordan</a> made up the fourth, and the <a href="/wiki/Larrousse" title="Larrousse">Larrousse</a> team, also contesting its home race, took up the fifth with <a href="/wiki/%C3%89rik_Comas" title="Érik Comas">Érik Comas</a> ahead of <a href="/wiki/Philippe_Alliot" title="Philippe Alliot">Philippe Alliot</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1993_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the start, the top 5 stayed the same while Schumacher got ahead of Alesi. Hill led from Prost, Brundle, Blundell, Senna and Schumacher.
</p><p>The Williams pulled away while Brundle pulled away from Blundell who was holding up Senna and Schumacher. However, this ended when Blundell was pushed off the road and into retirement on lap 21 as he attempted to lap de Cesaris. It was time for the mid-race stops during which Prost got ahead of Hill and Senna and Schumacher closed up on Brundle.
</p><p>During the second stops, Prost stayed ahead - just by two-tenths while Senna and Schumacher got ahead of Brundle. Schumacher passed Senna when the two were going through traffic and pulled away. Prost won with Hill right behind to make it a Williams 1-2 ahead of Schumacher, Senna, Brundle and Andretti.
</p><p>Thus, at the halfway stage of the season, Prost led the World Championship with 57 points. Senna was a further 12 points behind in second with 45, Hill was third with 28, Schumacher was fourth with 24, Brundle fifth with 9, Blundell sixth with 6, Herbert seventh with 6 and Lehto eighth with 5. There were no real battles in the Constructors Championship with Williams comfortably leading with 85 points with McLaren 37 points behind in second with 48. Benetton were third with 29 and Ligier were fourth with 15.
</p><p>Following the death of former world champion <a href="/wiki/James_Hunt" title="James Hunt">James Hunt</a>, former British driver <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Palmer" title="Jonathan Palmer">Jonathan Palmer</a> moved from Pit Lane reporter and took Hunt's place in the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> commentary booth for the race. Palmer would continue to partner <a href="/wiki/Murray_Walker" title="Murray Walker">Murray Walker</a> in the booth until the end of the <a href="/wiki/1996_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1996 Formula One World Championship">1996</a> season.
</p><p>By winning the race, Prost became the first Formula One driver to reach 100 career podiums.
</p>
