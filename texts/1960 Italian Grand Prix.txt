<p>The <b>1960 Italian Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at <a href="/wiki/Autodromo_Nazionale_di_Monza" title="Autodromo Nazionale di Monza">Monza</a> on 4 September 1960. It was race 9 of 10 in the <a href="/wiki/1960_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1960 World Championship of Drivers">1960 World Championship of Drivers</a> and race 8 of 9 in the <a href="/wiki/1960_International_Cup_for_Formula_One_Manufacturers" class="mw-redirect" title="1960 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers">1960 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers</a>. The race was won by American driver <a href="/wiki/Phil_Hill" title="Phil Hill">Phil Hill</a> driving a <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_246_F1" title="Ferrari 246 F1">Ferrari 246 F1</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_summary">Race summary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1960_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Race summary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The 1960 season had been a frustrating one for <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>'s Formula 1 program as they campaigned their obsolete <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_246_F1" title="Ferrari 246 F1">Dino 246</a>, a front engined car as the rear engined design established supremacy. Due to the boycott by British teams, the championship had already been decided for <a href="/wiki/Jack_Brabham" title="Jack Brabham">Jack Brabham</a> and Ferrari had gone without a victory. Seeing an opportunity, the Italian organizers decided to maximize Ferrari's one advantage&#160;—straightline speed— by using the combined Monza road and banked oval circuit, making the fast Monza even faster.
</p><p>Citing the fragility of their cars and the dangers of the banking, the major British factory teams of the day—<a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Lotus</a>, <a href="/wiki/British_Racing_Motors" title="British Racing Motors">BRM</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Cooper_Car_Company" title="Cooper Car Company">Cooper</a>, all boycotted the event, leading to a cobbled together field of private entrants and <a href="/wiki/Formula_2" class="mw-redirect" title="Formula 2">Formula 2</a> cars.
</p><p>The race was a processional affair, with Ginther leading at the start and eventually being overtaken by Hill. The pair with teammate <a href="/wiki/Willy_Mairesse" title="Willy Mairesse">Willy Mairesse</a> raced on to a rare 1–2–3 team result for <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Scuderia Ferrari</a>. The boycott also allowed <a href="/wiki/Cooper_T51#Scuderia_Eugenio_Castellotti" title="Cooper T51">Scuderia Castellotti</a> to score its only world championship points with <a href="/wiki/Giulio_Cabianca" title="Giulio Cabianca">Giulio Cabianca</a> finishing fourth in his <a href="/wiki/Cooper_T51" title="Cooper T51">Cooper T51</a>, two laps behind Hill and ahead of Scuderia Ferrari's fourth entry, <a href="/wiki/Wolfgang_von_Trips" title="Wolfgang von Trips">Wolfgang von Trips</a> in a 1.5 L-engined (conforming to F2 regulations) <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_156_F2" class="mw-redirect" title="Ferrari 156 F2">Ferrari 156 F2</a> car.
</p><p>It was the first victory by an American driver in a Grand Prix since <a href="/wiki/James_Anthony_Murphy" class="mw-redirect" title="James Anthony Murphy">Jimmy Murphy</a> in 1921, and the first by an American in the modern era, post codification of the <a href="/wiki/History_of_Formula_One" title="History of Formula One">Formula One championship</a> in 1950. 
</p><p>It was the last Formula One World Championship victory by a front-engined car. Although the Ferguson P99 won the non-championship <a href="/w/index.php?title=1961_Gold_Cup&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="1961 Gold Cup (page does not exist)">Gold Cup</a> at Oulton Park in 1961.
</p>
