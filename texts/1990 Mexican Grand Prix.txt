<p>The <b>1990 Mexican Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at <a href="/wiki/Autodromo_Hermanos_Rodriguez" class="mw-redirect" title="Autodromo Hermanos Rodriguez">Autodromo Hermanos Rodriguez</a> on 24 June 1990. It was sixth race of the <a href="/wiki/1990_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1990 Formula One World Championship">1990 Formula One World Championship</a>, the 14th <a href="/wiki/Mexican_Grand_Prix" title="Mexican Grand Prix">Mexican Grand Prix</a> and the fifth since the Mexico City circuit returned to the Formula One calendar in 1986. It was held over 69 laps of the 4.4 kilometre circuit for a race distance of 305 kilometres.
</p><p>The race was won by reigning world champion, French driver <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> driving a <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_641" title="Ferrari 641">Ferrari 641</a>. Prost, the <a href="/wiki/1988_Mexican_Grand_Prix" title="1988 Mexican Grand Prix">1988 race</a> winner, became the second multiple-winner in Mexican Grand Prix history, joining British driver <a href="/wiki/Jim_Clark" title="Jim Clark">Jim Clark</a> who won the race in <a href="/wiki/1962_Mexican_Grand_Prix" title="1962 Mexican Grand Prix">1962</a> (non-championship), <a href="/wiki/1963_Mexican_Grand_Prix" title="1963 Mexican Grand Prix">1963</a> and <a href="/wiki/1967_Mexican_Grand_Prix" title="1967 Mexican Grand Prix">1967</a>. It was Prost's second win of the year after the <a href="/wiki/1990_Brazilian_Grand_Prix" title="1990 Brazilian Grand Prix">Brazilian Grand Prix</a>. Prost won the race by 26 seconds over his British team mate <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a>. Third was Austrian driver <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> driving a <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/5B" class="mw-redirect" title="McLaren MP4/5B">McLaren MP4/5B</a>. <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> stopped with a puncture late in the race, allowing both Prost and Berger to close to eight points behind him in the Driver's Championship.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1990_Mexican_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-qualifying_report">Pre-qualifying report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1990_Mexican_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Pre-qualifying report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>During the Friday morning pre-qualifying session, <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Grouillard" title="Olivier Grouillard">Olivier Grouillard</a> was fastest for the first time this season, in the <a href="/wiki/Osella" title="Osella">Osella</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Larrousse" title="Larrousse">Larrousse</a>-<a href="/wiki/Lola_Cars" title="Lola Cars">Lolas</a> of <a href="/wiki/%C3%89ric_Bernard" title="Éric Bernard">Éric Bernard</a> and <a href="/wiki/Aguri_Suzuki" title="Aguri Suzuki">Aguri Suzuki</a> were second and fourth, with the <a href="/wiki/EuroBrun" title="EuroBrun">EuroBrun</a> of <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Moreno" title="Roberto Moreno">Roberto Moreno</a> between them in third place.
</p><p>For the third Grand Prix in succession, the <a href="/wiki/Automobiles_Gonfaronnaises_Sportives" title="Automobiles Gonfaronnaises Sportives">AGS</a> cars missed out in fifth and sixth positions, with <a href="/wiki/Yannick_Dalmas" title="Yannick Dalmas">Yannick Dalmas</a> faster than <a href="/wiki/Gabriele_Tarquini" title="Gabriele Tarquini">Gabriele Tarquini</a> this time. AGS technical director Hugues de Chaunac left the team prior to this event, frustrated at the team's lack of progress.<sup id="cite_ref-Walker_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Walker-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Seventh again was <a href="/wiki/Bertrand_Gachot" title="Bertrand Gachot">Bertrand Gachot</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Coloni" title="Scuderia Coloni">Coloni</a>, although he was much closer to the pace on this occasion, just three tenths of a second behind Tarquini. <a href="/wiki/Claudio_Langes" title="Claudio Langes">Claudio Langes</a> was a distant eighth again after only completing the minimum three laps required by the regulations, as the team concentrated on Moreno.<sup id="cite_ref-Walker_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Walker-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Bottom of the time sheets as usual was <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Giacomelli" title="Bruno Giacomelli">Bruno Giacomelli</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Life_Racing_Engines" title="Life Racing Engines">Life</a>, only managing an out-lap before his engine failed. Team manager Sergio Barbasio confirmed that his engineers were working on converting the L190 chassis to run with the <a href="/wiki/Judd_(engine)" title="Judd (engine)">Judd</a> CV engines he now claimed to have bought from <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Lotus</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying_report">Qualifying report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1990_Mexican_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Qualifying report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>There were no driver changes as the F1 show moved to Mexico City. The weather was changeable during the weekend, catching some drivers out, with both of the Larrousse team's drivers having major accidents on the Saturday morning. Despite that, both <a href="/wiki/Aguri_Suzuki" title="Aguri Suzuki">Aguri Suzuki</a> and <a href="/wiki/%C3%89ric_Bernard" title="Éric Bernard">Éric Bernard</a> managed to qualify for the race. The <a href="/wiki/Leyton_House" class="mw-redirect" title="Leyton House">Leyton House</a> drivers <a href="/wiki/Ivan_Capelli" title="Ivan Capelli">Ivan Capelli</a> and <a href="/wiki/Maur%C3%ADcio_Gugelmin" title="Maurício Gugelmin">Maurício Gugelmin</a> both failed to qualify.  At the front of the grid, <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> took pole from <a href="/wiki/Riccardo_Patrese" title="Riccardo Patrese">Riccardo Patrese</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thierry_Boutsen" title="Thierry Boutsen">Thierry Boutsen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pierluigi_Martini" title="Pierluigi Martini">Pierluigi Martini</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a>, <a href="/wiki/Satoru_Nakajima" title="Satoru Nakajima">Satoru Nakajima</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Stefano_Modena" title="Stefano Modena">Stefano Modena</a>, with championship contender <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a>, not happy with the qualifying set up of his Ferrari so deciding instead to qualify with his race set up, 13th. Roberto Moreno was disqualified for receiving a push start in qualifying.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1990_Mexican_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_report">Race report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1990_Mexican_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Race report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Sunshine greeted the drivers on race day and at the start it was Patrese who got away from Senna, Berger, Boutsen, and Piquet. Senna and Berger both passed Patrese on lap 2, and the McLaren drivers opened a significant lead over the rest of the field. Prost was charging through the field from his poor grid position, while Piquet moved up to third. Berger's run in second place came to an early end when he had to stop for new tyres on lap 13, leaving Piquet in second but under pressure from his former team-mate Nigel Mansell, who was able to pass him soon afterwards. Prost continued his charge through the field, eventually taking second from Mansell with 15 laps to go. Prost proceeded to set off after Senna, who was beginning to slow with tyre problems. The Brazilian had gambled on tyre durability by not making a stop for fresh rubber. On lap 60, Prost got past Senna with a simple move and three laps later Senna's rear tyre exploded, forcing him to retire from his 100th Grand Prix. As he had completed over 90% of the race, he was classified as a finisher in 20th place, the lowest classified result of his career. The battle for second place between Mansell and Berger culminated in Mansell passing around the outside of Berger going into the ultra-fast Peraltada corner. Mansell thus finished second behind Prost, with Berger third from <a href="/wiki/Alessandro_Nannini" title="Alessandro Nannini">Alessandro Nannini</a>, Boutsen, and Piquet.
</p>
