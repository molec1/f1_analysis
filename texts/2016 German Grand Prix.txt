<p>The <b>2016 German Grand Prix</b> (formally known as the <b>Formula 1 Großer Preis von Deutschland 2016</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Auto_racing" title="Auto racing">motor race</a> that took place on 31 July 2016. After a one-year absence, the race returned to the <a href="/wiki/Hockenheimring" title="Hockenheimring">Hockenheimring</a> near <a href="/wiki/Hockenheim" title="Hockenheim">Hockenheim</a> in the German state of <a href="/wiki/Baden-W%C3%BCrttemberg" title="Baden-Württemberg">Baden-Württemberg</a>, which last held the race in <a href="/wiki/2014_German_Grand_Prix" title="2014 German Grand Prix">2014</a>. It was the twelfth round of the <a href="/wiki/2016_FIA_Formula_One_World_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="2016 FIA Formula One World Championship">2016 FIA Formula One World Championship</a>, and marked the seventy-sixth running of the <a href="/wiki/German_Grand_Prix" title="German Grand Prix">German Grand Prix</a>, and the sixty-second time the race has been run as a round of the Formula One World Championship.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> entered the round with a six-point lead in the World Drivers' Championship over teammate and defending race winner <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a>. Hamilton won the race and extended his lead over Rosberg to nineteen points. Their team, <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a>, further extended its lead in the World Constructors' Championship.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2016_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the week before the race, <a href="/wiki/Manor_Racing" title="Manor Racing">MRT</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Rio_Haryanto" title="Rio Haryanto">Rio Haryanto</a> was the subject of increased media scrutiny amidst reports that his primary sponsor—Indonesian petrochemical company <a href="/wiki/Pertamina" title="Pertamina">Pertamina</a>—had not met its financial obligations to the team, thus placing his future with MRT and in the sport in jeopardy.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Haryanto was ultimately able to secure the seat for the race, but his long-term future with the team remained in doubt.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Following the handing out of several controversial penalties and extensive debate over the application of amendments to the sporting regulations, the FIA repealed all of the rules restricting pit-to-car communications.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>This was the first Grand Prix that double yellow flags would be the same as a red flag in qualifying after the controversial qualifying in the Hungarian Grand Prix.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Tyre supplier <a href="/wiki/Pirelli" title="Pirelli">Pirelli</a> provided teams with the medium, soft and supersoft compounds.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
