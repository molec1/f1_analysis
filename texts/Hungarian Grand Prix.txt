<p>The <b>Hungarian Grand Prix</b> (<a href="/wiki/Hungarian_language" title="Hungarian language">Hungarian</a>: <i lang="hu">Magyar Nagydíj</i>) is a motor race held annually in <a href="/wiki/Mogyor%C3%B3d" title="Mogyoród">Mogyoród</a>, Hungary. Since 1986, the race has been a round of the <a href="/wiki/FIA" class="mw-redirect" title="FIA">FIA</a> <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> World Championship.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Origins">Origins</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Origins">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The first Hungarian Grand Prix was held on 21 June 1936 over a 5-kilometre (3.1-mile) track laid out in <a href="/wiki/N%C3%A9pliget" class="mw-redirect" title="Népliget">Népliget</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-IHT_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IHT-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> a park in <a href="/wiki/Budapest" title="Budapest">Budapest</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz" title="Mercedes-Benz">Mercedes-Benz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Auto_Union" title="Auto Union">Auto Union</a>, and the Alfa Romeo-equipped <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> teams all sent three cars and the event drew a very large crowd. However, politics and the ensuing <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">war</a> meant the end of <a href="/wiki/Grand_Prix_motor_racing" title="Grand Prix motor racing">Grand Prix motor racing</a> in the country for fifty years.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Hungaroring">Hungaroring</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Hungaroring">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A major coup by <a href="/wiki/Bernie_Ecclestone" title="Bernie Ecclestone">Bernie Ecclestone</a>, the <a href="/wiki/1986_Hungarian_Grand_Prix" title="1986 Hungarian Grand Prix">1986 Hungarian Grand Prix</a> was the first Formula One race to take place behind the <a href="/wiki/Iron_Curtain" title="Iron Curtain">Iron Curtain</a>. Held at the twisty <a href="/wiki/Hungaroring" title="Hungaroring">Hungaroring</a> in <a href="/wiki/Mogyor%C3%B3d" title="Mogyoród">Mogyoród</a> near <a href="/wiki/Budapest" title="Budapest">Budapest</a>, the race has been a mainstay of the racing calendar ever since.  It was the only current Grand Prix venue that had never seen a wet race up until the <a href="/wiki/2006_Hungarian_Grand_Prix" title="2006 Hungarian Grand Prix">2006 Hungarian Grand Prix</a>. The first Grand Prix saw 200,000 people<sup id="cite_ref-IHT_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IHT-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> spectating, although tickets were expensive at the time.  Today, the support is still very enthusiastic, particularly from <a href="/wiki/Finns" title="Finns">Finns</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-AmCham_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AmCham-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Due to the nature of the track, narrow, twisty and often dusty because of under-use, the Hungarian Grand Prix is associated with processional races, with sometimes many cars following one another, unable to pass. <a href="/wiki/Thierry_Boutsen" title="Thierry Boutsen">Thierry Boutsen</a> demonstrated this in 1990, keeping his slower <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> car in front of championship leader <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a>, unable to find a way by. Pit strategy is often crucial; in 1998, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> team changed his strategy mid-race before Schumacher built up a winning margin after all the stops had been made. Passing is a rarity here, although the 1989 race saw a bullish performance from <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> in the Ferrari, who started from 12th on the grid and passed car after car, finally taking the lead when Ayrton Senna was baulked by a slower runner. The circuit was modified slightly in 2003 in an attempt to allow more passing.
</p><p>Other notable occasions in Budapest include first Grand Prix wins for <a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a> (in 1993), <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> (in 2003, the first Grand Prix winner from <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>, and the youngest ever Grand Prix winner at the time), <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> (in an incident-packed race in 2006), and <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> (in 2008, who also became the 100th winner of a World Championship race). In 1997, Damon Hill came close to winning in the technically inferior <a href="/wiki/Arrows_Grand_Prix_International" title="Arrows Grand Prix International">Arrows</a>-<a href="/wiki/Yamaha_Motor_Corporation" class="mw-redirect" title="Yamaha Motor Corporation">Yamaha</a>, but his car lost drive on the last lap causing him to coast in second place. In 2014, Lewis Hamilton finished in third, six seconds behind winner <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a>, despite starting the race from the pit lane.
</p><p>In 2001, Michael Schumacher equalled <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a>'s then record 51 Grand Prix wins at the Hungaroring, in the drive which also secured his fourth Drivers' Championship which also matched Prost's career tally.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The 2006 Grand Prix was the first to be held here in wet conditions. Button took his first victory from 14th place on the grid.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At the <a href="/wiki/2013_Hungarian_Grand_Prix" title="2013 Hungarian Grand Prix">2013 Hungarian Grand Prix</a>, it was confirmed that Hungary would continue to host a Formula 1 race until 2021.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The track was completely resurfaced for the first time in early 2016, and it was announced the Grand Prix's deal was extended for a further 5 years, until 2026.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
In 2020 the contract was also extended one year further to 2027.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
