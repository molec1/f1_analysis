<p>The <b>1989 French Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at <a href="/wiki/Circuit_Paul_Ricard" title="Circuit Paul Ricard">Paul Ricard</a> on 9 July 1989. It was the seventh race of the <a href="/wiki/1989_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1989 Formula One World Championship">1989 Formula One World Championship</a>.
</p><p>The 80-lap race was won from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> by local driver <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/5" title="McLaren MP4/5">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Honda_in_Formula_One" title="Honda in Formula One">Honda</a>, with Englishman <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> second in a <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_640" title="Ferrari 640">Ferrari</a> and Italian <a href="/wiki/Riccardo_Patrese" title="Riccardo Patrese">Riccardo Patrese</a> third in a <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW12" title="Williams FW12">Williams</a>-<a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-race">Pre-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1989_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Pre-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In a press conference before the race, <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> announced that he would be leaving <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> at the end of the season, with the speculation being that he would join <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> in <a href="/wiki/1990_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1990 Formula One World Championship">1990</a>.
</p><p>Four drivers would make their F1 debuts at this race. Frenchman <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a>, then contesting the <a href="/wiki/1989_International_Formula_3000_Championship" title="1989 International Formula 3000 Championship">Formula 3000 Championship</a>, replaced <a href="/wiki/Michele_Alboreto" title="Michele Alboreto">Michele Alboreto</a> at <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_Racing" title="Tyrrell Racing">Tyrrell</a> when the team took on <a href="/wiki/Camel_(cigarette)" title="Camel (cigarette)">Camel</a> as its major sponsor, clashing with Alboreto's <a href="/wiki/Marlboro_(cigarette)" title="Marlboro (cigarette)">Marlboro</a> sponsorship. Another Frenchman, <a href="/wiki/%C3%89ric_Bernard" title="Éric Bernard">Éric Bernard</a>, replaced <a href="/wiki/Yannick_Dalmas" title="Yannick Dalmas">Yannick Dalmas</a> at <a href="/wiki/Larrousse" title="Larrousse">Larrousse</a>, Dalmas still suffering the effects of <a href="/wiki/Legionnaires%27_disease" title="Legionnaires&#39; disease">Legionnaires' disease</a>. <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Lotus</a> test driver <a href="/wiki/Martin_Donnelly_(racing_driver)" title="Martin Donnelly (racing driver)">Martin Donnelly</a> took the place of <a href="/wiki/Derek_Warwick" title="Derek Warwick">Derek Warwick</a> at <a href="/wiki/Arrows_Grand_Prix_International" title="Arrows Grand Prix International">Arrows</a> for this race after Warwick injured his back in a karting accident.
</p><p>Finally, McLaren test driver <a href="/wiki/Emanuele_Pirro" title="Emanuele Pirro">Emanuele Pirro</a> replaced <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Herbert" title="Johnny Herbert">Johnny Herbert</a> at <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a> after it was decided that Herbert needed more time to recover from the leg and ankle injuries he had sustained in the Formula 3000 race at <a href="/wiki/Brands_Hatch" title="Brands Hatch">Brands Hatch</a> in <a href="/wiki/1988_International_Formula_3000_Championship" title="1988 International Formula 3000 Championship">1988</a>. Benetton debuted their <a href="/wiki/Benetton_B189" title="Benetton B189">B189</a> car, with the new <a href="/wiki/Cosworth#The_HB_V8" title="Cosworth">Ford HB</a> engine, at Paul Ricard, <a href="/wiki/Alessandro_Nannini" title="Alessandro Nannini">Alessandro Nannini</a> driving this car while Pirro drove the older, <a href="/wiki/Cosworth_DFV#DFR" title="Cosworth DFV">DFR</a>-powered <a href="/wiki/Benetton_B188" title="Benetton B188">B188</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1989_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-qualifying_report">Pre-qualifying report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1989_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Pre-qualifying report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>For the first time, both <a href="/wiki/Onyx_Grand_Prix" title="Onyx Grand Prix">Onyx</a> cars made it into the main qualifying sessions, as <a href="/wiki/Bertrand_Gachot" title="Bertrand Gachot">Bertrand Gachot</a> and <a href="/wiki/Stefan_Johansson" title="Stefan Johansson">Stefan Johansson</a> came first and second in pre-qualifying. It was Gachot's first pre-qualifying success in seven attempts. <a href="/wiki/Alex_Caffi" title="Alex Caffi">Alex Caffi</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Dallara" title="Dallara">Dallara</a> was a few hundredths of a second back in third, and <a href="/wiki/Stefano_Modena" title="Stefano Modena">Stefano Modena</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Brabham" title="Brabham">Brabham</a> was the fourth and last prequalifier, marginally faster than the <a href="/wiki/Osella" title="Osella">Osella</a> of <a href="/wiki/Nicola_Larini" title="Nicola Larini">Nicola Larini</a>.
</p><p>The other entrants who failed to pre-qualify on the Friday morning were Modena's Brabham team-mate <a href="/wiki/Martin_Brundle" title="Martin Brundle">Martin Brundle</a> in sixth, the second time in a row the British driver had failed at this stage. <a href="/wiki/Volker_Weidler" title="Volker Weidler">Volker Weidler</a> was seventh in the <a href="/wiki/Rial_Racing" title="Rial Racing">Rial</a>, his seventh successive failure to pre-qualify. Both Zakspeeds again missed out, <a href="/wiki/Bernd_Schneider_(racing_driver)" title="Bernd Schneider (racing driver)">Bernd Schneider</a> eighth, and <a href="/wiki/Aguri_Suzuki" title="Aguri Suzuki">Aguri Suzuki</a> eleventh. Ninth was <a href="/wiki/Piercarlo_Ghinzani" title="Piercarlo Ghinzani">Piercarlo Ghinzani</a> in the second Osella, the Italian's seventh consecutive pre-qualifying failure, and <a href="/wiki/Pierre-Henri_Raphanel" title="Pierre-Henri Raphanel">Pierre-Henri Raphanel</a> was tenth in his <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Coloni" title="Scuderia Coloni">Coloni</a>. <a href="/wiki/Gregor_Foitek" title="Gregor Foitek">Gregor Foitek</a>'s <a href="/wiki/EuroBrun" title="EuroBrun">EuroBrun</a> and <a href="/wiki/Joachim_Winkelhock" title="Joachim Winkelhock">Joachim Winkelhock</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Automobiles_Gonfaronnaises_Sportives" title="Automobiles Gonfaronnaises Sportives">AGS</a> were bottom of the timesheets. Winkelhock left AGS after this weekend having failed to pre-qualify at any Grand Prix thus far, to be replaced by the recently-sacked <a href="/wiki/Larrousse" title="Larrousse">Larrousse</a>-<a href="/wiki/Lola_Cars" title="Lola Cars">Lola</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Yannick_Dalmas" title="Yannick Dalmas">Yannick Dalmas</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Walker_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Walker-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying_report">Qualifying report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1989_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Qualifying report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>For the second successive race, <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> narrowly beat McLaren teammate <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> to <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>, this time by 0.025 seconds. On the second row were <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> in the Ferrari and Nannini in the new Benetton, and on the third row were <a href="/wiki/Thierry_Boutsen" title="Thierry Boutsen">Thierry Boutsen</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> in the second Ferrari. The Larrousse team had also sacked <a href="/wiki/Philippe_Alliot" title="Philippe Alliot">Philippe Alliot</a> prior to the race only to then re-hire him; he responded by qualifying seventh, with the second Williams of <a href="/wiki/Riccardo_Patrese" title="Riccardo Patrese">Riccardo Patrese</a> alongside him on the fourth row. The top ten was completed by <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Palmer" title="Jonathan Palmer">Jonathan Palmer</a> in the Tyrrell and <a href="/wiki/Maur%C3%ADcio_Gugelmin" title="Maurício Gugelmin">Maurício Gugelmin</a> in the <a href="/wiki/March_Engineering" title="March Engineering">March</a>.
</p><p>The Onyxes continued their good form from pre-qualifying, with Gachot taking 11th on the grid and Johansson 13th. Debutants Donnelly, Bernard and Alesi were 14th, 15th and 16th respectively, with Pirro 24th in the older Benetton. The other two pre-qualifiers, Modena and Caffi, were 22nd and 26th respectively, Caffi edging out teammate <a href="/wiki/Andrea_de_Cesaris" title="Andrea de Cesaris">Andrea de Cesaris</a> for the last grid spot.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1989_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_report">Race report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1989_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Race report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the first start, Senna led into the first corner from Prost, while behind them, Gugelmin locked his brakes and veered into Boutsen's Williams and Berger's Ferrari. The March launched into the air and flipped upside down, also knocking off Mansell's rear wing. The race was immediately red-flagged, a shaken Gugelmin taking the restart from the pit lane along with Mansell and Donnelly.
</p><p>At the restart, Senna suffered a differential failure, leaving Prost to lead every lap of the race. Berger ran second in the early stages, ahead of Nannini, Boutsen and <a href="/wiki/Ivan_Capelli" title="Ivan Capelli">Ivan Capelli</a> in the second March, before spinning on lap 12 and eventually retiring with a clutch failure. Boutsen developed gearbox problems while Nannini suffered a suspension failure on lap 41, promoting Capelli to second for three laps before his engine failed. This left Alesi second on his debut, ahead of Patrese and Mansell, before he pitted for tyres. Alliot and Gachot also ran in the top six before Alliot suffered an engine failure and Gachot pitted with a flat battery. On lap 61, Patrese spun under pressure from Mansell, allowing the Englishman through into second.
</p><p>Prost took the chequered flag 44 seconds ahead of Mansell, with Patrese a further 22 seconds back. Alesi was fourth, seven seconds behind Patrese and the last driver on the lead lap, with Johansson scoring Onyx's first points in fifth and <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Grouillard" title="Olivier Grouillard">Olivier Grouillard</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Equipe_Ligier" title="Equipe Ligier">Ligier</a> scoring his only point for sixth. Pirro was ninth, Bernard 11th and Donnelly 12th, while Gugelmin recovered from his accident by setting the fastest race lap.
</p><p>With the win, Prost extended his lead over Senna in the Drivers' Championship to 11 points.
</p>
