<p>The <b>2016 Monaco Grand Prix</b> (formally known as the <b>Formula 1 Grand Prix de Monaco 2016</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_racing" title="Formula One racing">Formula One motor race</a> held on 29 May 2016 at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Monaco" title="Circuit de Monaco">Circuit de Monaco</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Street_circuit" title="Street circuit">street circuit</a> that runs through the <a href="/wiki/Monaco" title="Monaco">Principality of Monaco</a>. It was the sixth round of the <a href="/wiki/2016_FIA_Formula_One_World_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="2016 FIA Formula One World Championship">2016 FIA Formula One World Championship</a>, and marked the seventy-fourth time that the <a href="/wiki/Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="Monaco Grand Prix">Monaco Grand Prix</a> had been held, as well as the sixty-third time it had been held as a round of the Formula One World Championship since the series inception in <a href="/wiki/1950_Formula_One_season" title="1950 Formula One season">1950</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> was the defending race winner having won the past three Monaco Grands Prix in a row and entered the race with a thirty-nine-point lead held over <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> in the Drivers' Championship. Rosberg's team, <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a>, held a 48-point lead over Ferrari in the Constructors' Championship.
</p><p>During Saturday's qualifying, <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a>, who was driving for <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a>, achieved the first pole position of his career. In an eventful race, Mercedes's <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> took victory ahead of Ricciardo, who had lost time during a pitstop when his mechanics did not have the tyres ready for him. <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a> completed the podium for <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a>, the team's first appearance in the top three of the season and their first since the <a href="/wiki/2015_Russian_Grand_Prix" title="2015 Russian Grand Prix">2015 Russian Grand Prix</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2016_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Monaco_Port_and_Track.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4a/Monaco_Port_and_Track.jpg/220px-Monaco_Port_and_Track.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4a/Monaco_Port_and_Track.jpg/330px-Monaco_Port_and_Track.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4a/Monaco_Port_and_Track.jpg/440px-Monaco_Port_and_Track.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4368" data-file-height="2912" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Monaco_Port_and_Track.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The swimming pool and Rascasse section of the track shortly before the race weekend</div></div></div>
<p>Following their controversial crash and double retirement at <a href="/wiki/2016_Spanish_Grand_Prix" title="2016 Spanish Grand Prix">the previous race in Spain</a> two weeks earlier, particular attention was on championship leaders <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> and their drivers <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>. The team was adamant that such an incident could not be repeated, with their head of motorsport <a href="/wiki/Toto_Wolff" title="Toto Wolff">Toto Wolff</a> saying: "We cannot afford to drop the ball, so we must remain united, remain strong and hit back hard this weekend."<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> With a win, Rosberg would have been able to become the only driver other than <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> to win the <a href="/wiki/Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="Monaco Grand Prix">Monaco Grand Prix</a> four times in a row,<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> while Hamilton had suffered a string of bad results at the circuit, having won only once <a href="/wiki/2008_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="2008 Monaco Grand Prix">in 2008</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-talkingpoints_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-talkingpoints-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> At the race <a href="/wiki/2015_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="2015 Monaco Grand Prix">the year before</a>, Hamilton had lost what he felt was a certain victory by a wrong decision to pit him during a safety car period.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> received a new, revised combustion engine for his car, his third of five allowed over the course of the season without receiving a grid penalty. Power unit supplier <a href="/wiki/Honda_in_Formula_One" title="Honda in Formula One">Honda</a> stressed that they had not used any development tokens, a set of limited allowed changes to the units, but had instead only improved reliability. The team also equipped both cars with new batteries and electronics control units, set to improve the reliability of the cars as well.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> During the two-day testing session that took place after the previous Grand Prix at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Barcelona-Catalunya" title="Circuit de Barcelona-Catalunya">Circuit de Barcelona-Catalunya</a>, <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a> introduced an updated version of their power unit, which was used during the test to the satisfaction of driver <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Magnussen" title="Kevin Magnussen">Kevin Magnussen</a>, who declared that the new unit was a step forward in both power and driveability. He urged the team to pull the introduction of the power unit forward to the Monaco race, instead of <a href="/wiki/2016_Canadian_Grand_Prix" title="2016 Canadian Grand Prix">the following Grand Prix in Canada</a> as originally planned.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Renault chose to do so, however, they were only able to hand out one updated unit to both their works team and <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a>, who used them under the name <a href="/wiki/TAG_Heuer" title="TAG Heuer">TAG Heuer</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> The two units were eventually given to Magnussen at Renault and <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> at Red Bull.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The race saw the competitive début of <a href="/wiki/Pirelli" title="Pirelli">Pirelli</a>'s new ultrasoft tyre compound, which was provided alongside the supersoft and soft compounds. Per the regulations of the <a href="/wiki/2016_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2016 Formula One World Championship">2016</a> season, every driver needs to set aside one set each of the two softest compounds for the race and one set of the ultrasofts for Q3 (should they advance). The drivers have freedom of what other compounds they choose for the remaining ten out of thirteen sets.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> From this race onwards, the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile</a> allowed drivers to choose alternative helmet designs for one race weekend per season,<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> a practice usually prohibited as they have to wear the same design to make them more recognisable to spectators and television audiences. As a consequence, <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a>'s helmet featured extensive artwork by graffiti artist <a href="/wiki/Os_G%C3%AAmeos" class="mw-redirect" title="Os Gêmeos">Os Gêmeos</a>, teammate <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a> featured <a href="/wiki/Casino" title="Casino">casino-inspired</a> imagery and <a href="/wiki/Haas_F1_Team" title="Haas F1 Team">Haas</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Romain_Grosjean" title="Romain Grosjean">Romain Grosjean</a> sported a tribute to the late <a href="/wiki/Jules_Bianchi" title="Jules Bianchi">Jules Bianchi</a> on his helmet.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> The race marked a reason for celebration at <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>, as the team had started their first ever Grand Prix at Monaco <a href="/wiki/1966_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="1966 Monaco Grand Prix">fifty years earlier</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Stats_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Stats-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Going into the weekend, Nico Rosberg led the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a> with 100 points, followed by <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> with 61 points. Lewis Hamilton followed in third, a further four points behind, with <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> in fourth with 48 points, level with Daniel Ricciardo in fifth.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">constructors' standings</a>, Mercedes led with 157 points, 48 points ahead of Ferrari, with Red Bull Racing in third with 94 points.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Free_practice">Free practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2016_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Free practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Vettel_Monaco_2016.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b9/Vettel_Monaco_2016.jpg/220px-Vettel_Monaco_2016.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b9/Vettel_Monaco_2016.jpg/330px-Vettel_Monaco_2016.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b9/Vettel_Monaco_2016.jpg/440px-Vettel_Monaco_2016.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2560" data-file-height="1920" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Vettel_Monaco_2016.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> was fastest in third practice.</div></div></div>
<p>Per the regulations for the 2016 season, two ninety-minute practice and one sixty-minute session were held before qualifying on Saturday. The first two usually take place on Friday, with Monaco being the single exemption, where those sessions are held on Thursday,<sup id="cite_ref-pracreg_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pracreg-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> in order to minimise road-closure disruptions to local residents.<sup id="cite_ref-thurprac_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-thurprac-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> In the first session on Thursday morning, Lewis Hamilton topped the timesheets with a time of 1:15.537, one tenth of a second faster than his teammate Nico Rosberg. They were followed by Sebastian Vettel, almost half a second down on Hamilton, and the two Red Bull cars of Daniel Ricciardo and <a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a> respectively. <a href="/wiki/Daniil_Kvyat" title="Daniil Kvyat">Daniil Kvyat</a>, driving for <a href="/wiki/Toro_Rosso" class="mw-redirect" title="Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso</a>, in sixth was the last driver to get within a second of Hamilton's time.<sup id="cite_ref-fp1_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fp1-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> He was the only one of the front runners to set his time on the supersoft instead of the faster ultrasoft tyres.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> There were several incidents during the session: After twenty-four minutes, Felipe Massa hit the barrier in turn one, damaging his car and leading to a <a href="/wiki/Virtual_safety_car" class="mw-redirect" title="Virtual safety car">virtual safety car</a> (VSC) period. Later, Lewis Hamilton had a brake lockup at the same spot, but was able to get into the run-off area without touching the guardrail. Several other drivers had dangerous moments in turn one as well, but the most severe incident of the session came three minutes to the end: A drain cover had come loose around the first corner, puncturing Rosberg's left rear tyre and damaging <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a>'s <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4-31" title="McLaren MP4-31">McLaren MP4-31</a>, causing the practice to be <a href="/wiki/Red_flag_(racing)" class="mw-redirect" title="Red flag (racing)">red flagged</a>, ultimately ending the session.<sup id="cite_ref-fp1_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fp1-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In second practice on Thursday afternoon, Ricciardo was fastest, setting a time of 1:14.607, more than half a second clear of Hamilton in second place.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Rosberg and Verstappen, in third and fourth, were the only other drivers to get within a second of Ricciardo, while the two <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> drivers Valtteri Bottas and Felipe Massa struggled, ending the session 14th and 16th respectively. At Ferrari, Kimi Räikkönen was fastest in seventh spot. His teammate, Vettel, had an incident-packed session that eventually ended with him setting the ninth fastest time. He spun at Mirabeau corner, damaging his rear wing, only to hit the wall in turn one after coming back to the track, albeit continuing with wheel rim damage. A VSC period occurred after Romain Grosjean crashed into the outside barrier at the exit of the tunnel. Two more VSC periods ensued when <a href="/wiki/Rio_Haryanto" title="Rio Haryanto">Rio Haryanto</a> touched the inside barrier at the same spot and later after Kevin Magnussen crashed at the final corner.<sup id="cite_ref-fp2_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fp2-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the third practice session on Saturday morning, Sebastian Vettel set the fastest time, about one hundredth of a second faster than Hamilton. Nico Rosberg and Daniel Ricciardo followed, both also within two tenth of a second of Vettel. Max Verstappen was fifth fastest although he had a minor crash coming up to Casino square, clipping the wall. Toro Rosso were again quick, getting both drivers into the top ten. Renault had different fortunes, as both drivers had accidents, with <a href="/wiki/Jolyon_Palmer" title="Jolyon Palmer">Jolyon Palmer</a> hindering one of Hamilton's flying laps as he crashed at the swimming pool section.<sup id="cite_ref-fp3_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fp3-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2016_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Red_Bull_RB12_Monaco_2016.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/76/Red_Bull_RB12_Monaco_2016.jpg/220px-Red_Bull_RB12_Monaco_2016.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/76/Red_Bull_RB12_Monaco_2016.jpg/330px-Red_Bull_RB12_Monaco_2016.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/76/Red_Bull_RB12_Monaco_2016.jpg/440px-Red_Bull_RB12_Monaco_2016.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3872" data-file-height="2592" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Red_Bull_RB12_Monaco_2016.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The wreckage of <a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a>'s car is cleared off the track.</div></div></div>
<p>Qualifying consisted of three parts, 18, 15 and 12 minutes in length respectively, with six drivers eliminated from competing after each of the first two sessions.<sup id="cite_ref-pracreg_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pracreg-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> In the first part of qualifying (Q1), the session got interrupted shortly after it had started, as <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Nasr" title="Felipe Nasr">Felipe Nasr</a> came to a halt at the exit of the tunnel, caused by an engine failure. A red flag came out while the car was cleared off the track. When the running resumed, all drivers except for the two at <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a> went out on the ultrasoft tyres to set their first lap times. Max Verstappen was the last to set a representative time, but touched the guardrail at the swimming pool chicane and crashed into the wall, causing another red flag period. As the session got back under way, it were the two <a href="/wiki/Manor_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Manor F1">Manor</a> cars of Rio Haryanto and <a href="/wiki/Pascal_Wehrlein" title="Pascal Wehrlein">Pascal Wehrlein</a>, as well as Jolyon Palmer and <a href="/wiki/Marcus_Ericsson" title="Marcus Ericsson">Marcus Ericsson</a> who joined Nasr and Verstappen on the sidelines, as they were eliminated. Kevin Magnussen meanwhile barely made it into Q2, but was under investigation for exiting the pit lane while it was closed.<sup id="cite_ref-quali_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-quali-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the second part, Lewis Hamilton quickly set a fast lap time, the quickest of the weekend up to that point, about half a second ahead of his teammate Rosberg. Meanwhile, Daniel Ricciardo set his fastest lap on the harder supersoft compound tyre, meaning that he would be able to start the race on them, should it have stayed dry. On the back end, Williams struggled for pace and both Valtteri Bottas and Felipe Massa missed out on Q3 by qualifying 11th and 14th respectively. Also eliminated were <a href="/wiki/Esteban_Guti%C3%A9rrez" title="Esteban Gutiérrez">Esteban Gutiérrez</a>, Jenson Button, Romain Grosjean and Magnussen.<sup id="cite_ref-quali_28-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-quali-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Ricciardo_Monaco_2016.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/95/Ricciardo_Monaco_2016.jpg/220px-Ricciardo_Monaco_2016.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/95/Ricciardo_Monaco_2016.jpg/330px-Ricciardo_Monaco_2016.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/95/Ricciardo_Monaco_2016.jpg/440px-Ricciardo_Monaco_2016.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2560" data-file-height="1920" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Ricciardo_Monaco_2016.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> achieved the first <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> of his career.</div></div></div>
<p>As Q3 started, Lewis Hamilton stopped in the pit lane with a loss of power and was pushed back into the garage. He was eventually able to go on track and qualified third, after starting several quick laps only to abandon them to leave his final run to the last moment. He was however beaten to <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> by Ricciardo, who was almost three-tenths of a second faster than second placed Rosberg, with Vettel following in fourth almost a second slower.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a> managed fifth ahead of Kimi Räikkönen, who was to serve a five-place grid penalty for an unscheduled gearbox change, leaving him eleventh on the grid. <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Sainz_Jr." title="Carlos Sainz Jr.">Carlos Sainz Jr.</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a>, Daniil Kvyat and Fernando Alonso rounded up the top ten.<sup id="cite_ref-quali_28-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-quali-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Ricciardo's pole position was the first for a Renault-powered turbocharged car since Ayrton Senna's front-of-the-grid start at the <a href="/wiki/1986_Mexican_Grand_Prix" title="1986 Mexican Grand Prix">1986 Mexican Grand Prix</a> for <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Lotus</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Stats_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Stats-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;N 1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-qualifying">Post-qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2016_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Post-qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After qualifying, the race stewards decided not to give a penalty to Magnussen for ignoring the red light at the end of the pit lane during Q1, since it had only changed to red 0.55 seconds before he crossed the pit exit line.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Kvyat escaped penalty as well after his car failed the obligatory front floor deflection test. As the team pointed out, the irregularity was the result of an impact on track, allowing him to keep eighth on the grid.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2016_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Daniil_Kvyat_-_2016_Monaco_GP.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/89/Daniil_Kvyat_-_2016_Monaco_GP.jpg/220px-Daniil_Kvyat_-_2016_Monaco_GP.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/89/Daniil_Kvyat_-_2016_Monaco_GP.jpg/330px-Daniil_Kvyat_-_2016_Monaco_GP.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/89/Daniil_Kvyat_-_2016_Monaco_GP.jpg/440px-Daniil_Kvyat_-_2016_Monaco_GP.jpg 2x" data-file-width="5472" data-file-height="3648" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Daniil_Kvyat_-_2016_Monaco_GP.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Daniil_Kvyat" title="Daniil Kvyat">Daniil Kvyat</a> had electrical problems early in the race and later retired after a collision.</div></div></div>
<p>With rain shortly before the race, the start was taken behind the <a href="/wiki/Safety_car" title="Safety car">safety car</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-tick_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tick-39">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> forcing all drivers to start the race on the full wet tyres.<sup id="cite_ref-tyrerules_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tyrerules-40">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> As Ricciardo led the field behind the safety car, Daniil Kvyat had to come into the pit lane on lap two, complaining of technical issues. He rejoined with a new steering wheel, being a lap down on the rest of the pack already. The race started in earnest on lap seven, with Ricciardo defending his lead at the restart. Kevin Magnussen made a pit stop for intermediate tyres at the end of the lap, but his teammate Jolyon Palmer proved just how slippery it still was when he crashed out just a lap later, being caught out by a wet zebra crossing and causing a virtual safety car period. More cars came into the pitlane for intermediate tyres over the following laps. On lap eleven, Kimi Räikkönen damaged his front wing on the guardrail at the Loews hairpin, resulting in his front wing getting caught under his front tyres, blocking Grosjean's way in the process. Räikkönen retired some corners later. Meanwhile, Ricciardo built a gap at the front, being ten seconds clear of Rosberg on lap 13. One lap later, Sebastian Vettel was the first of the front runners to change to intermediate tyres.<sup id="cite_ref-tick_39-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tick-39">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> Mercedes issued team orders demanding Rosberg let Hamilton past<sup id="cite_ref-lettingpast_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lettingpast-41">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> on lap 16, but the gap had by then grown to 13 seconds.<sup id="cite_ref-tick_39-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tick-39">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:P%C3%A9rez_Monaco_2016.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/12/P%C3%A9rez_Monaco_2016.jpg/220px-P%C3%A9rez_Monaco_2016.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/12/P%C3%A9rez_Monaco_2016.jpg/330px-P%C3%A9rez_Monaco_2016.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/12/P%C3%A9rez_Monaco_2016.jpg/440px-P%C3%A9rez_Monaco_2016.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1920" data-file-height="1440" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:P%C3%A9rez_Monaco_2016.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a> recorded <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a>'s first podium finish of the season in third.</div></div></div>
<p>On lap 21, Rosberg made a pit stop for the intermediate tyres, leaving only Ricciardo and Hamilton at the front of the field on the full wet tyres. Ricciardo made the switch on lap 24, but Hamilton stayed out, opting to remain on the wet tyre until the track dried out enough to go straight to <a href="/wiki/Racing_slick" title="Racing slick">slicks</a>. In the meantime, Verstappen had fought his way back into the top ten, after having started 21st. In the subsequent laps, Ricciardo was able to reduce the gap to Hamilton in the lead, edging as close as half a second by lap 28. Behind the leading pair, Rosberg was third ahead of Pérez and Vettel. The pivotal moment of the race followed on lap 31, when Hamilton changed to slick tyres. When Ricciardo followed suit a lap later, his pit crew were not ready for him and had the wrong tyres, resulting in a long stop, handing the lead to Hamilton, who stayed narrowly ahead of him.<sup id="cite_ref-bbc_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-42">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> Sergio Pérez was another driver who benefited during the second round of stops, emerging in third. Rosberg was left in sixth after both Vettel and Alonso also came out ahead of him after the switch to dry tyres. On lap 34, Ricciardo made an attempt to repass Hamilton on track, but Hamilton successfully defended his position.<sup id="cite_ref-tick_39-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tick-39">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Rosberg_-_2016_Monaco_GP.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f0/Rosberg_-_2016_Monaco_GP.jpg/220px-Rosberg_-_2016_Monaco_GP.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f0/Rosberg_-_2016_Monaco_GP.jpg/330px-Rosberg_-_2016_Monaco_GP.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f0/Rosberg_-_2016_Monaco_GP.jpg/440px-Rosberg_-_2016_Monaco_GP.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3319" data-file-height="2213" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Rosberg_-_2016_Monaco_GP.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> started on the front row, but fell to seventh in the race with brake problems.</div></div></div>
<p>Another virtual safety car period ensued on lap 35, caused by Verstappen who crashed his car into the barrier after locking up his tyres. As the VSC ended on lap 37, Ricciardo once more failed in an attempt to regain the lead. At the chicane coming out of the tunnel, Hamilton misjudged the corner and backed Ricciardo off at the exit of the chicane, causing the latter to complain about the move on team radio. While the incident was investigated by the stewards, the decision was not to take action against Hamilton.<sup id="cite_ref-bbc_42-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-42">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> At that point, the order stood as follows: Hamilton, Ricciardo, Pérez, Vettel, Alonso, Rosberg, Hülkenberg, Sainz, Button and Gutiérrez. The top four drivers pulled away cleanly from the rest of the field behind them.<sup id="cite_ref-tick_39-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tick-39">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> On lap 50, the two Sauber cars collided at Rascasse corner, after Felipe Nasr had ignored instructions from the pit lane to let his teammate Ericsson behind him pass. Another VSC period was called while both Sauber drivers made pit stops to repair damages, only for both to retire some laps later.<sup id="cite_ref-tick_39-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tick-39">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-bbc_42-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-42">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> In front, the race carried on in the same order. On lap 68, the fourth virtual safety car period occurred when a large plastic sheet fell onto the start–finish straight.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton was able to extend his lead over Ricciardo at that point, setting the fastest lap of the race on lap 71. Three laps from the finish, rain started to fall again, although no driver changed tyres before the <a href="/wiki/Chequered_flag" class="mw-redirect" title="Chequered flag">chequered flag</a> fell. Hamilton crossed the finish line to take victory ahead of Ricciardo and Pérez.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> This win for Hamilton marked his second at Monaco, the other being in 2008. Vettel hung on to fourth, followed by Alonso in fifth.<sup id="cite_ref-tick_39-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tick-39">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> Rosberg lost his sixth place to Nico Hülkenberg on the very last lap.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2016_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the podium interviews, conducted by former Formula One driver <a href="/wiki/Martin_Brundle" title="Martin Brundle">Martin Brundle</a>, Hamilton thanked his team and expressed satisfaction about how the race went. He pointed to the problems of driving on the full wet tyres for such a long distance, saying that they were about to "drop off", meaning to lose much of their grip to the race surface, when he came in to change. He complimented Ricciardo on driving "phenomenally all weekend".<sup id="cite_ref-pressconf_46-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconf-46">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> Ricciardo himself lamented his misfortune in losing the race during his second pit stop. In regard to his team's error and their different strategy at the previous race in Spain, which he felt had cost him a chance at victory there as well,<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> he said: "Two weekends in a row I've been screwed now. It sucks. It hurts."<sup id="cite_ref-pressconf_46-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconf-46">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> He emphasised that it had not been his decision to pit at that time, but that the team had called him in and criticised them for not being ready when he arrived. Sergio Pérez on the other hand was "extremely happy" with the performance by him and the team, saying that the race was his favourite podium finish while driving for Force India.<sup id="cite_ref-pressconf_46-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconf-46">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Rosberg_Hamilton_-_2016_Monaco_GP_2.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6d/Rosberg_Hamilton_-_2016_Monaco_GP_2.jpg/220px-Rosberg_Hamilton_-_2016_Monaco_GP_2.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6d/Rosberg_Hamilton_-_2016_Monaco_GP_2.jpg/330px-Rosberg_Hamilton_-_2016_Monaco_GP_2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6d/Rosberg_Hamilton_-_2016_Monaco_GP_2.jpg/440px-Rosberg_Hamilton_-_2016_Monaco_GP_2.jpg 2x" data-file-width="5141" data-file-height="3427" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Rosberg_Hamilton_-_2016_Monaco_GP_2.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> <i>(background)</i> thanked teammate <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> for letting him pass.</div></div></div>
<p>Speaking about the moment when Nico Rosberg let him past in the early stages of the race, Hamilton told the press that he had thanked his teammate for "being a gentleman".<sup id="cite_ref-gentleman_48-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gentleman-48">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> When asked how painful it had been to be asked to let Hamilton go by, Rosberg said: "It was more painful the feeling that I had in the car. That was the worst. Around Monaco, that's not a nice feeling to have. I had no confidence at all. The second thing [team order] was just a consequence of that."<sup id="cite_ref-gentleman_48-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gentleman-48">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> Mercedes's motorsport director Toto Wolff also praised Rosberg for working for the team and moving to the side without questioning the team's decision.<sup id="cite_ref-gentleman_48-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gentleman-48">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> Rosberg was however unable to give an explanation for his slow pace during the early stages. He pointed to the possibility that his car's tyre and brake temperatures had been particularly affected by the period behind the safety car.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Following their intra-team collision, Sauber's team principal <a href="/wiki/Monisha_Kaltenborn" title="Monisha Kaltenborn">Monisha Kaltenborn</a> put blame on both drivers. The stewards penalised Ericsson for the incident, handing him a three-place grid penalty for the following race in Canada as well as three penalty points to his <a href="/wiki/FIA_Super_Licence" title="FIA Super Licence">licence</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> Felipe Nasr was adamant that the team orders he had received prior to the accident had been uncalled for, since his slower pace was due to low tyre temperatures. He also claimed that Ericsson had ignored team orders himself on two occasions in the <a href="/wiki/2015_FIA_Formula_One_World_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="2015 FIA Formula One World Championship">previous season</a>. Ericsson in turn had a different view and said that the team had told him for "seven or eight laps" that they would swap the positions of the drivers before he decided to go for the pass himself.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> Valtteri Bottas and Pascal Wehrlein also received penalties during and after the race. Bottas was handed a ten-second time penalty and two penalty points on his licence for causing a collision with Gutiérrez,<sup id="cite_ref-bottaspen_52-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bottaspen-52">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> while Wehrlein received two separate ten-second penalties: the first for exceeding the allowed lap time under VSC conditions and the second for ignoring <a href="/wiki/Racing_flags#Blue_flag" title="Racing flags">blue flags</a>. He was also given two penalty points each for both incidents.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-wehrlein1_54-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wehrlein1-54">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-wehrlein2_55-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wehrlein2-55">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Following the Grand Prix, Nico Rosberg retained first place in the Drivers' Championship with 106 points, but his lead over the second-placed driver, now Hamilton, had decreased to 24 points. Daniel Ricciardo moved up to third with 66 points, five points ahead of Räikkönen.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup> In the Constructors' standings, Mercedes extended their lead over Ferrari to 67 points, with Red Bull in third another nine points behind.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
