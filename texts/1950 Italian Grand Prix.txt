<p>The <b>1950 Italian Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held on 3 September 1950 at <a href="/wiki/Autodromo_Nazionale_di_Monza" title="Autodromo Nazionale di Monza">Autodromo Nazionale di Monza</a>. It was race 7 of 7 in  the <a href="/wiki/1950_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1950 World Championship of Drivers">1950 World Championship of Drivers</a>. In this race, <a href="/wiki/Giuseppe_Farina" title="Giuseppe Farina">Nino Farina</a> became the first World Drivers' Champion, and the only driver to win the title in his home country.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1950_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After <a href="/wiki/Juan_Manuel_Fangio" title="Juan Manuel Fangio">Juan Manuel Fangio</a>'s win at the <a href="/wiki/1950_French_Grand_Prix" title="1950 French Grand Prix">French Grand Prix</a>, Fangio had obtained 26 points, two ahead of teammate <a href="/wiki/Luigi_Fagioli" title="Luigi Fagioli">Luigi Fagioli</a> and four ahead of another teammate, <a href="/wiki/Giuseppe_Farina" title="Giuseppe Farina">Giuseppe Farina</a>.  Having already finished four times in the points (all second places), Fagioli would only be able to drop six points or not gain at all, while Fangio and Farina had only finished three times.  All three of Fangio's finishes were wins.
</p><p>To win the championship,
</p>
<ul><li>For Fangio:
<ul><li><b>Win</b> or come <b>2nd</b> to guarantee the title.</li>
<li>Come <b>3rd</b>, <b>4th</b> or <b>5th</b> with Farina <b>2nd</b> or lower.</li>
<li>With the <b>fastest lap</b> <i>only</i>, Farina <b>3rd</b> or lower.</li>
<li>Scoring <b>no points</b>, Farina would have to finish <b>3rd</b> or lower without the fastest lap, <b>4th</b> with; and Fagioli not to <b>win</b> with the fastest lap.</li></ul></li>
<li>For Fagioli:
<ul><li><b>Win</b> the race with the fastest lap, with Farina <b>3rd</b> or lower, and Fangio <b>not to score</b> any points.</li></ul></li>
<li>For Farina:
<ul><li><b>Win</b> with the fastest lap, and Fangio <b>3rd</b> or lower.</li>
<li><b>Win</b> without the fastest lap, and Fangio <b>4th</b> or lower.</li>
<li><b>2nd</b> with fastest lap, and Fangio <b>5th</b> or lower without fastest lap.</li>
<li><b>3rd</b> with fastest lap, and Fangio <b>not to score</b>.</li></ul></li></ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_report">Race report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1950_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Race report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ferrari pulled out all the stops to impress at their home circuit, producing a new unsupercharged 4½ litre engine to try to end the Alfa Romeo monopoly. <a href="/wiki/Alberto_Ascari" title="Alberto Ascari">Alberto Ascari</a> used it to achieve second place on the grid to <a href="/wiki/Juan_Manuel_Fangio" title="Juan Manuel Fangio">Juan Manuel Fangio</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Alfa_Romeo_158" class="mw-redirect" title="Alfa Romeo 158">Alfa Romeo 158</a> and then in the race behind the fast starting <a href="/wiki/Giuseppe_Farina" title="Giuseppe Farina">Nino Farina</a> (Alfa Romeo 158) before briefly leading. Sadly, the pace was too punishing for the new car and a porous block broke on lap 20 and the battle returned as usual to the Alfas. Fangio retired twice; once in his own Alfa Romeo 158 and a second time after taking over <a href="/wiki/Piero_Taruffi" title="Piero Taruffi">Piero Taruffi</a>'s. Farina led to the finish from Ascari who was now in teammate <a href="/wiki/Dorino_Serafini" title="Dorino Serafini">Dorino Serafini</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_375" class="mw-redirect" title="Ferrari 375">Ferrari 375</a> with <a href="/wiki/Luigi_Fagioli" title="Luigi Fagioli">Luigi Fagioli</a> finishing third in his Alfa Romeo 158. <a href="/wiki/Louis_Rosier" title="Louis Rosier">Louis Rosier</a> finish fourth in his <a href="/wiki/Talbot-Lago_T26C" class="mw-redirect" title="Talbot-Lago T26C">Talbot-Lago T26C</a> with <a href="/wiki/Philippe_%C3%89tancelin" title="Philippe Étancelin">Philippe Étancelin</a> fifth in his Lago-Talbot. Étancelin would become the oldest driver to ever score a world championship point with that finish. Only seven cars finished out of the 26 starters and with Farina's win and Fangio's failure to score and Fagioli's third place points removed as his worst scoring finish, Farina became the first recipient of the World Driver's Championship crown.
</p>
