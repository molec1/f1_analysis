<p>The <b>2020 Hungarian Grand Prix</b> (officially known as the <b>Formula 1 Aramco Magyar Nagydíj 2020</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_racing" title="Formula One racing">Formula One motor race</a> held on 19 July 2020 at the <a href="/wiki/Hungaroring" title="Hungaroring">Hungaroring</a> in <a href="/wiki/Mogyor%C3%B3d" title="Mogyoród">Mogyoród</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hungary" title="Hungary">Hungary</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-details_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-details-2">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The race was the third round of the <a href="/wiki/2020_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2020 Formula One World Championship">2020 Formula One World Championship</a>, the 36th running of the <a href="/wiki/Hungarian_Grand_Prix" title="Hungarian Grand Prix">Hungarian Grand Prix</a> and the 35th time the race had been run as a World Championship event since the inaugural season in <a href="/wiki/1950_Formula_One_season" title="1950 Formula One season">1950</a>. <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> was the defending race winner and the eventual winner of this Grand Prix, Hamilton’s second successive win of the 2020 season and his eighth at the Hungaroring.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2020_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Impact_of_the_COVID-19_pandemic">Impact of the COVID-19 pandemic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2020_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Impact of the COVID-19 pandemic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/2020_Formula_One_World_Championship#Impact_of_the_COVID-19_pandemic" title="2020 Formula One World Championship">2020 Formula One World Championship §&#160;Impact of the COVID-19 pandemic</a>, and <a href="/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic_in_Hungary" title="COVID-19 pandemic in Hungary">COVID-19 pandemic in Hungary</a></div>
<p>On the provisional 2020 calendar, published in 2019, the Hungarian Grand Prix was pencilled in for 2 August.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> However, due to the <a href="/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic" title="COVID-19 pandemic">COVID-19 pandemic</a>, the FIA reshuffled the calendar. The Hungarian Grand Prix was moved to 19 July swapping places with the <a href="/wiki/2020_British_Grand_Prix" title="2020 British Grand Prix">British Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Prior to the race, concerns were raised regarding Hungary's strict coronavirus restrictions, which include the threat of fines and imprisonment for personnel who do not comply.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Further to this <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Scuderia Ferrari</a> team principal <a href="/wiki/Mattia_Binotto" title="Mattia Binotto">Mattia Binotto</a> revealed he had spoken about the need to follow the <a href="/wiki/FIA" class="mw-redirect" title="FIA">FIA</a>'s own <a href="/wiki/COVID-19" class="mw-redirect" title="COVID-19">COVID-19</a> safety protocols to both of his drivers after both were found to have breached the rules on separate occasions during the first two race weekends in <a href="/wiki/Austria" title="Austria">Austria</a> with the threat of sporting sanctions (including being removed from the event entry list) by race stewards a possibility for further breaches. Binotto stated he expects the drivers to pay closer attention to these rules going forward.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Championship_standings_before_the_race">Championship standings before the race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2020_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Championship standings before the race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After the second round, the <a href="/wiki/2020_Styrian_Grand_Prix" title="2020 Styrian Grand Prix">Styrian Grand Prix</a>, <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a> led the championship by 6 points from his teammate and defending champion <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Lando_Norris" title="Lando Norris">Lando Norris</a> of <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> a further 11 points behind in third place. In the Constructors' Championship, defending champions Mercedes led the championship with 80 points, McLaren was in second with 39 points, with <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a> in third with 27 points. <a href="/wiki/Racing_Point" class="mw-redirect" title="Racing Point">Racing Point</a> was 4th with 22 points, with <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Scuderia Ferrari</a> in 5th with 19 points following a double retirement for the team at the <a href="/wiki/2020_Styrian_Grand_Prix" title="2020 Styrian Grand Prix">Styrian Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Entrants">Entrants</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2020_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Entrants">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Further information: <a href="/wiki/2020_Formula_One_World_Championship#Entries" title="2020 Formula One World Championship">2020 Formula One World Championship §&#160;Entries</a></div>
<p>The drivers and teams were the same as the season entry list with no additional stand-in drivers for the race.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a> took part in the first practice session for <a href="/wiki/Alfa_Romeo_in_Formula_One" title="Alfa Romeo in Formula One">Alfa Romeo Racing</a> in place of <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tyre_choices">Tyre choices</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2020_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Tyre choices">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Hungarian Grand Prix used the C2, C3 and C4 combination of tyres – the same compound selection as was used in the first two races of the season at the <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Ring" title="Red Bull Ring">Red Bull Ring</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice">Practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2020_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The first practice session was topped by <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> and then <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a>, both of <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a>, followed by <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a> and then <a href="/wiki/Lance_Stroll" title="Lance Stroll">Lance Stroll</a>, both of <a href="/wiki/Racing_Point_F1_Team" title="Racing Point F1 Team">Racing Point</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Pierre_Gasly" title="Pierre Gasly">Pierre Gasly</a> was unable to set a lap time due to issues with his power unit.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Second practice was run in the wet and ended with <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> fastest for <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> ahead of Bottas and <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Sainz_Jr." title="Carlos Sainz Jr.">Carlos Sainz Jr.</a>, in a session where only 13 drivers set a time.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The third practice session ended with Bottas fastest ahead of Hamilton and Pérez.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>The start of qualifying was marked by most of the cars immediately exiting the <a href="/wiki/Pit_lane" class="mw-redirect" title="Pit lane">pit lane</a>, as there was a chance of rain (as it happened, the rain was very light, and there was no need for non-<a href="/wiki/Slick_tyres" class="mw-redirect" title="Slick tyres">slick tyres</a>). Characteristically, the grip on the <a href="/wiki/Hungaroring" title="Hungaroring">Hungaroring</a> circuit increased dramatically over the session, especially in Q1. The five drivers knocked out in the session were <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Magnussen" title="Kevin Magnussen">Kevin Magnussen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Daniil_Kvyat" title="Daniil Kvyat">Daniil Kvyat</a>, <a href="/wiki/Romain_Grosjean" title="Romain Grosjean">Romain Grosjean</a>, <a href="/wiki/Antonio_Giovinazzi" title="Antonio Giovinazzi">Antonio Giovinazzi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kimi_Raikkonen" class="mw-redirect" title="Kimi Raikkonen">Kimi Raikkonen</a> - the latter two both driving for <a href="/wiki/Alfa_Romeo_in_Formula_One" title="Alfa Romeo in Formula One">Alfa Romeo</a> and achieving the worst qualifying results this season for the team by locking out the back row of the grid with Giovianzzi qualifying 19th and Raikkonen 20th and last. The <a href="/wiki/2007_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2007 Formula One World Championship">former world champion</a> Raikkonen was also knocked out in Q1 for the fourth consecutive race, the first time in his career this had happened. Conversely, both <a href="/wiki/Williams_Racing" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams Racing">Williams</a> drivers (<a href="/wiki/George_Russell_(racing_driver)" title="George Russell (racing driver)">George Russell</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nicholas_Latifi" title="Nicholas Latifi">Nicholas Latifi</a>) managed to make it out of Q1, the first time the team had achieved this since the 2018 Italian Grand Prix. 
</p><p>In Q2, the five drivers knocked out were <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a>, Russell, <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Albon" title="Alexander Albon">Alexander Albon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Esteban_Ocon" title="Esteban Ocon">Esteban Ocon</a> and Latifi - for the latter, the highest grid placement at that time in his career. Albon, in his <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a> car, struggled with handling difficulties, and failed to reach Q3. Although <a href="/wiki/Pierre_Gasly" title="Pierre Gasly">Pierre Gasly</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_AlphaTauri" title="Scuderia AlphaTauri">AlphaTauri</a> ran out of power near the end of the session, his time set earlier would be enough to reach Q3, where he would not complete a flying lap.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> took pole by over one-tenth of a second from his <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> teammate <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a>, achieving the team's first front-row lockout of the season. <a href="/wiki/Racing_Point" class="mw-redirect" title="Racing Point">Racing Point</a> managed to lock out the second row of the grid for the first time since their debut at the <a href="/wiki/2018_Belgian_Grand_Prix" title="2018 Belgian Grand Prix">2018 Belgian Grand Prix</a>; the team was delighted with third for <a href="/wiki/Lance_Stroll" title="Lance Stroll">Lance Stroll</a> and fourth for <a href="/wiki/Sergio_Perez" class="mw-redirect" title="Sergio Perez">Sergio Perez</a>. Next came the two <a href="/wiki/Ferrari" title="Ferrari">Ferraris</a> of <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Charles_Leclerc" title="Charles Leclerc">Charles Leclerc</a>, with the team's highest qualifying result of the season, and continuing Vettel's record of qualifying fifth or higher at the circuit every year since 2008. Like his teammate, <a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a> struggled in his Red Bull, and having been forecast prior to the race to be a possible contender for the win, only managed seventh, ahead of <a href="/wiki/Lando_Norris" title="Lando Norris">Lando Norris</a> and <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Sainz_Jr" class="mw-redirect" title="Carlos Sainz Jr">Carlos Sainz Jr</a>, both driving for <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> with Gasly in tenth.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2020_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-race">Pre-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2020_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Pre-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a> crashed on the way to the grid, damaging his front wing and suspension. His car was repaired prior to the formation lap and he started from his original grid position. 
</p><p>At the start of the formation lap all drivers but one were on the <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_tyres" title="Formula One tyres">intermediate tyres</a> with Magnussen on full wets. During the formation lap, both <a href="/wiki/Haas_F1_Team" title="Haas F1 Team">Haas</a> cars entered the pits and swapped from intermediate and wet tyres to slicks.
</p><p>The race started in damp conditions with further rain forecast during the race.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_report">Race report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2020_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Race report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the start of the race, <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a>, starting second in his <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a>, moved slightly before all five lights went out; Bottas said afterwards that he had reacted to lights on his steering wheel rather than the actual lights. As the movement was so small, the sensors that determine a jumped start did not pick it up, and thus Bottas was not penalised by the stewards; however, he had damaged his own launch and consequently lost four places on the first lap. With the track drying swiftly, drivers quickly came into the pits for <a href="/wiki/Slick_tyres" class="mw-redirect" title="Slick tyres">slick tyres</a> - as both Haas cars were already on the medium compound, they did not have to pit, and were in third and fourth by lap 8. By lap 9, polesitter <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> had already gained seven seconds on second placed Verstappen. Having already spun at turn one on lap 5, <a href="/wiki/Nicholas_Latifi" title="Nicholas Latifi">Nicholas Latifi</a> of <a href="/wiki/Williams_Racing" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams Racing">Williams</a> received a five second time penalty for an unsafe release in the pits, where he almost collided with the <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> of <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Sainz_Jr." title="Carlos Sainz Jr.">Carlos Sainz Jr.</a>
</p><p>On laps 9 and 10, Bottas duelled with <a href="/wiki/Charles_Leclerc" title="Charles Leclerc">Charles Leclerc</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> for sixth place, eventually getting past at turn one, with Leclerc complaining about his soft compound tyres. On lap 13, <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> in the other Ferrari lost eighth place to <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Albon" title="Alexander Albon">Alexander Albon</a> of <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a>, after running wide at Turn 12. <a href="/wiki/Pierre_Gasly" title="Pierre Gasly">Pierre Gasly</a> in his <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_AlphaTauri" title="Scuderia AlphaTauri">AlphaTauri</a> retired on lap 15, due to the engine problem that had affected him throughout the weekend. By lap 25, the top 10 ran thus: Hamilton, Verstappen, <a href="/wiki/Lance_Stroll" title="Lance Stroll">Lance Stroll</a>, Bottas, <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Magnussen" title="Kevin Magnussen">Kevin Magnussen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Romain_Grosjean" title="Romain Grosjean">Romain Grosjean</a>, Albon, Vettel, <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a>.
</p><p>On lap 30, Ferrari, betting on there being no rain, had Vettel pit for hard tyres. This became the view of all the teams in the next few laps, and all of the drivers pitted for new slick tyres. Three laps later, Leclerc was involved in another close fight, this time with <a href="/wiki/Lando_Norris" title="Lando Norris">Lando Norris</a> for 13th. After Hamilton's pit stop on lap 40, his lead over Verstappen stood at over 20 seconds. This was enough time for him to pit near the end of the race onto soft tyres, so he could set the fastest lap of the race, a 1:16:627. In the final few laps, Albon overtook Vettel for fifth place, and Sainz overtook the other Ferrari of Leclerc for tenth (Sainz would later be promoted to ninth following a penalty for Magnussen). On the final lap, Bottas had a chance to overtake Verstappen for second place; he was not, however, able to get past.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2020_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Kevin_Magnussen" title="Kevin Magnussen">Kevin Magnussen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Romain_Grosjean" title="Romain Grosjean">Romain Grosjean</a> each received a ten-second penalty for a breach of rules regarding formation lap radio messages instructing the drivers to pit and change tyres, which, in turn, contravened the rule that the driver "must drive the car alone and unaided". The penalty dropped Magnussen from ninth to tenth, and Grosjean from fifteenth to sixteenth.<sup id="cite_ref-haaspen_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-haaspen-20">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Albon" title="Alexander Albon">Alexander Albon</a> was also investigated by the stewards as it was believed that his team was illegally drying the track around his grid spot prior to the race, but was not punished as no evidence for the claims was found.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Renault protested the results of the <a href="/wiki/Racing_Point_F1_Team" title="Racing Point F1 Team">Racing Point</a> team over the legality of <a href="/wiki/Racing_Point_RP20" title="Racing Point RP20">their car</a> for the second successive race. The FIA investigation into this matter is ongoing.<sup id="cite_ref-protest_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-protest-22">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
