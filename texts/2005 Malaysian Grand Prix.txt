<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_report">Race report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2005_Malaysian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Race report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This was another dominant win from Renault, as <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> secured his first win of the season from pole position. At the start he led from <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a>, before finishing the race 20-odd seconds ahead of the Italian, claiming Toyota's first ever podium position.
</p><p>Third place was taken by <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a>. He had been trailing Alonso's team-mate <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a>, and his own teammate <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> who collided at the end of lap 35. Having been passed by the Australian, Fisichella attempted to retake the position on the inside heading into the final corner, but lost control and slid into Webber's car.
</p><p>Fourth was McLaren's Juan Pablo Montoya, after starting from 11th on the grid. He was followed home by <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a>, <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> and <a href="/wiki/Christian_Klien" title="Christian Klien">Christian Klien</a>. This made it four points finishes from four starts for <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a>. <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> suffered a puncture immediately after his pitstop, and despite setting the fastest lap was unable to make up enough positions to score any points.
</p><p>After retiring from the Australian Grand Prix a lap from the end, which caused the rules to be changed, <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> and <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Davidson" title="Anthony Davidson">Anthony Davidson</a> retired on the second lap of the race, both from engine failures. Davidson was substituting at <a href="/wiki/British_American_Racing" title="British American Racing">British American Racing</a>, as regular driver <a href="/wiki/Takuma_Sato" title="Takuma Sato">Takuma Sato</a> was forced to withdraw with a fever. Alonso's win made him the first Spaniard ever to lead the Formula 1 World Championship.
</p><p>The race ended <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>'s run of 22 consecutive podium finishes, which started at the <a href="/wiki/2003_Italian_Grand_Prix" title="2003 Italian Grand Prix">2003 Italian Grand Prix</a>.
</p><p>The race also marked Rubens Barrichello's 200th Grand Prix.
</p><p>The race also marked <a href="/wiki/Toyota_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Toyota F1">Toyota</a>'s first-ever podium in Formula One and first Asian-licensed constructor to score a podium since <a href="/wiki/1968_United_States_Grand_Prix" title="1968 United States Grand Prix">1968 United States Grand Prix</a> when <a href="/wiki/John_Surtees" title="John Surtees">John Surtees</a> drove for <a href="/wiki/Honda_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Honda F1">Honda</a> in the 3rd place.
</p>
