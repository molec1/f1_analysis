<p>The <b>2003 Austrian Grand Prix </b>(formally known as <b>A1 Grand Prix von Österreich</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 18 May 2003 at the <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Ring" title="Red Bull Ring">A1-Ring</a>. It was the sixth round of the <a href="/wiki/2003_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2003 Formula One season">2003 Formula One season</a> and the 27th <a href="/wiki/Austrian_Grand_Prix" title="Austrian Grand Prix">Austrian Grand Prix</a>. The 69-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> driving a <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> car after starting from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>. <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> finished second driving for <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> with <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> third in the other Ferrari. It was the last Austrian Grand Prix to be held until it returned to the renamed <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Ring" title="Red Bull Ring">Red Bull Ring</a> in <a href="/wiki/2014_Austrian_Grand_Prix" title="2014 Austrian Grand Prix">2014</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2003_Austrian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2003_Austrian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Grand Prix was contested by ten teams with two drivers each. The teams (also known as <a href="/wiki/Formula_One#Constructors" title="Formula One">constructors</a>) were <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a>, <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>, <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jaguar_Racing" title="Jaguar Racing">Jaguar</a>, <a href="/wiki/British_American_Racing" title="British American Racing">BAR</a>, <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Toyota_Racing_(Formula_One_team)" title="Toyota Racing (Formula One team)">Toyota</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-entrants_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-entrants-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> It was announced in January 2003 that the Austrian Grand Prix would be dropped from the Formula One calendar in 2004, three years before the contract for the race was due to expire. This was because an exit clause was enabled in response to the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a> pushing forward a ban on tobacco advertising to 1 October 2005.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Before the race, McLaren driver <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> led the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_champions" class="mw-redirect" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; champions">Drivers' Championship</a> with 32 <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Championship_points_scoring_systems" title="List of Formula One World Championship points scoring systems">points</a>, ahead of <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> (28) and <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> (25). <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> was fourth with 20 points, while <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> was a close fifth with 19 points.<sup id="cite_ref-points_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a>, McLaren was leading with 51 points, three points ahead of their rival Ferrari. Renault (34 points) and Williams (32 points) contended for fourth place and Jordan was fifth with 11 points.<sup id="cite_ref-points_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2003_Austrian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Qualifying was interesting, as there was no knowledge of what the weather would be for the race, making tyre and fuel strategy critical. <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> qualified on pole despite a huge slide on his best qualifying lap. Championship leader <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> qualified second, and was given special dispensation from the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">FIA</a> to change his cracked valve, without incurring the usual penalty for changing an engine.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2003_Austrian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> chose to start from the pitlane in the spare car, and <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> chose to start from the pitlane in his regular car. This meant Webber could not change tyres or add fuel until the race started, whereas Alonso could. His Jaguar broke this rule, and so Webber received a 10-second stop/go penalty.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Cristiano_da_Matta" title="Cristiano da Matta">Cristiano da Matta</a> had a faulty launch control, which caused 2 aborted starts, reducing the race to 69 laps. On the third (and final) formation lap, <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a>'s Sauber did not start, and he did not have time to set up the team car.
</p><p>At the start, <a href="/wiki/Jos_Verstappen" title="Jos Verstappen">Jos Verstappen</a>'s launch control broke, and he retired. This caused the safety car to be deployed. Michael eventually led Montoya and Räikkönen when the green flag came out at the end of lap 4. The field remained relatively stable for the next several laps.
</p><p>After 11 laps, it started to rain lightly, although it was not enough to force cars into the pits. Moments later, <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a>'s Renault spun at turn one and he rejoined without problems. On lap 23, Michael pitted and there was a problem with his fuel filler, possibly caused by the fact that the team had used it to fill up <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a>'s car, which had a very slow first stop. Some of the fuel that actually did come out of the nozzle hit the bodywork on the sidepods, causing a small fire. The fire was extinguished quickly, and Michael continued in the race, losing about 12 seconds and was now running in third position.
</p><p>On lap 32, everything turned into Michael's favour. Räikkönen was having engine-related performance problems, and this allowed Schumacher to catch and eventually pass him. On the straight between turns two and three, leader <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a>'s engine blew up. He made it back to the garage, and Michael was back in the lead. Alonso was on a good run despite starting from the pit lane, and was running in the top five when he spun off course at turn one, on what turned out to be his own oil caused by a blown engine in his Renault. Michael eventually set the fastest lap of the day, at an average speed of 227.894&#160;km/h.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After the second round of stops, Barrichello closed up on second-place Räikkönen, but was unable to pass him due to some good defensive moves by Kimi, despite his car being clearly faster. Michael eventually went on to win the race, his third successive of the season.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> finished fourth for BAR despite being disappointed in qualifying, and <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a>'s race was uneventful in fifth place. <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> was sixth, Webber finished a brilliant seventh despite his penalties, and Trulli was eighth for the final point. Despite causing the aborted starts earlier in the day, da Matta finished the race, a lap down in tenth position.
</p>
