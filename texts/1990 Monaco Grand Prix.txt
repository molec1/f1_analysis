<p>The <b>1990 Monaco Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 27 May 1990 at <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Monaco" title="Circuit de Monaco">Monaco</a>. It was the fourth race of the <a href="/wiki/1990_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1990 Formula One World Championship">1990 Formula One World Championship</a> and the 48th <a href="/wiki/Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="Monaco Grand Prix">Monaco Grand Prix</a>. The race was held over 78 laps of the 3.32-kilometre (2.06&#160;mi) circuit for a race distance of 259 kilometres (161&#160;mi).
</p><p>The race was won by Brazilian driver <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/5" title="McLaren MP4/5">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Honda_in_Formula_One" title="Honda in Formula One">Honda</a>. Senna started from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>, led every lap and set the fastest race lap, en route to his second consecutive Monaco win and third in all. French driver <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a> finished second in a <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_019" title="Tyrrell 019">Tyrrell</a>-<a href="/wiki/Ford_Motor_Company" title="Ford Motor Company">Ford</a>, with Senna's Austrian team-mate <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> third.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1990_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-qualifying_report">Pre-qualifying report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1990_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Pre-qualifying report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The four successful pre-qualifiers in the one-hour Thursday morning session were the same four drivers as at the previous race at <a href="/wiki/1990_San_Marino_Grand_Prix" title="1990 San Marino Grand Prix">Imola</a>, in the same order. The <a href="/wiki/Larrousse" title="Larrousse">Larrousse</a>-<a href="/wiki/Lola_Cars" title="Lola Cars">Lola</a> cars finished first and second for the third time in a row, with <a href="/wiki/%C3%89ric_Bernard" title="Éric Bernard">Éric Bernard</a> again outpacing his team-mate <a href="/wiki/Aguri_Suzuki" title="Aguri Suzuki">Aguri Suzuki</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Osella" title="Osella">Osella</a> of <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Grouillard" title="Olivier Grouillard">Olivier Grouillard</a> was third fastest, followed by <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Moreno" title="Roberto Moreno">Roberto Moreno</a> in the <a href="/wiki/EuroBrun" title="EuroBrun">EuroBrun</a>.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Automobiles_Gonfaronnaises_Sportives" title="Automobiles Gonfaronnaises Sportives">AGS</a> drivers were frozen out in fifth and sixth, with <a href="/wiki/Gabriele_Tarquini" title="Gabriele Tarquini">Gabriele Tarquini</a> considerably faster than <a href="/wiki/Yannick_Dalmas" title="Yannick Dalmas">Yannick Dalmas</a> on this occasion. Seventh was <a href="/wiki/Claudio_Langes" title="Claudio Langes">Claudio Langes</a> in the second EuroBrun, six seconds off Bernard's pace. The other two runners were far behind; <a href="/wiki/Bertrand_Gachot" title="Bertrand Gachot">Bertrand Gachot</a> was over twelve seconds adrift in the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Coloni" title="Scuderia Coloni">Coloni</a> after another engine failure, with the team now in some disarray,<sup id="cite_ref-Walker_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Walker-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> but still nearly two seconds faster than the <a href="/wiki/Life_Racing_Engines" title="Life Racing Engines">Life</a>, driven again by <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Giacomelli" title="Bruno Giacomelli">Bruno Giacomelli</a>. This time, however, the Italian managed to complete a stint of seven laps and return to the pits without breaking down. Well off the pace, Giacomelli went back out on to the circuit and completed another lap before the <a href="/wiki/W12_engine" title="W12 engine">W12 engine</a> failed.<sup id="cite_ref-Walker_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Walker-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying_report">Qualifying report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1990_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Qualifying report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>There were no surprises in qualifying with <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> taking pole from his rival <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a>. <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a> continued to impress in his <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_Racing" title="Tyrrell Racing">Tyrrell</a>, qualifying third ahead of <a href="/wiki/Riccardo_Patrese" title="Riccardo Patrese">Riccardo Patrese</a>. Row three was formed of <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> and <a href="/wiki/Thierry_Boutsen" title="Thierry Boutsen">Thierry Boutsen</a>; <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> lined up seventh alongside the Minardi of <a href="/wiki/Pierluigi_Martini" title="Pierluigi Martini">Pierluigi Martini</a>, while <a href="/wiki/Emanuele_Pirro" title="Emanuele Pirro">Emanuele Pirro</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a> rounded out the top ten. Young Australian driver <a href="/wiki/David_Brabham" title="David Brabham">David Brabham</a> qualified in 25th, thus making his first Formula One race start.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1990_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_report">Race report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1990_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Race report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the start there was an overly long delay between the red and green lights, which caused the drivers to be a little more agitated than usual, resulting in an accident between Prost and Berger at the Mirabeau corner. The track was blocked and the race had to be stopped. At the second start everything went to plan and Senna led from Prost, Alesi, Berger, Patrese and Boutsen, with Pirro being the first retirement when his engine blew on the opening lap. Eighth-placed Nigel Mansell was the centre of action in the early stages, quickly passing Martini before setting off after Boutsen, but his overtaking attempt on the Belgian was unsuccessful as he touched the back of the Williams at the seafront chicane and had to limp back to the pits for a new front wing. At the front Senna led comfortably, even more so after Prost retired on lap 30 with battery problems. On lap 35, Piquet spun approaching Loewes Hairpin and stalled his engine; he received a push start from the marshals and was disqualified as a result. While Senna maintained his lead, there was action further back where Mansell was charging through the field. First he disposed of <a href="/wiki/Derek_Warwick" title="Derek Warwick">Derek Warwick</a> and then managed to successfully pass Boutsen for fourth, as Alesi and Berger fought over second place. In the late stages Senna started to slow, which allowed Alesi and Berger to close up on him, but Senna's McLaren survived to the finish for his third career win at Monaco. Alesi was second, followed by Berger, Boutsen, <a href="/wiki/Alex_Caffi" title="Alex Caffi">Alex Caffi</a> and the only other survivor, <a href="/wiki/%C3%89ric_Bernard" title="Éric Bernard">Éric Bernard</a>, who had taken sixth place in the late stages after he had forced himself past <a href="/wiki/Gregor_Foitek" title="Gregor Foitek">Gregor Foitek</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Onyx_Grand_Prix" title="Onyx Grand Prix">Onyx</a> by pushing him into the wall. In doing so, Bernard earned his first point in Formula One. Despite not finishing the race, Foitek was classified seventh, his best F1 result.
</p>
