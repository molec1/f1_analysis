<p>The <b>2017 Monaco Grand Prix</b> (formally known as the <b>Formula 1 Grand Prix de Monaco 2017</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_racing" title="Formula One racing">Formula One motor race</a> held on 28 May 2017 at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Monaco" title="Circuit de Monaco">Circuit de Monaco</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Street_circuit" title="Street circuit">street circuit</a> that runs through the <a href="/wiki/Monaco" title="Monaco">Principality of Monaco</a>. It was the sixth round of the <a href="/wiki/2017_FIA_Formula_One_World_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="2017 FIA Formula One World Championship">2017 FIA Formula One World Championship</a>, the seventy-fifth time that the <a href="/wiki/Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="Monaco Grand Prix">Monaco Grand Prix</a> has been held, and the sixty-fourth time it has been a round of the Formula One World Championship since the inception of the series in <a href="/wiki/1950_Formula_One_season" title="1950 Formula One season">1950</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> entered the round holding a six-point lead over <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> in the World Drivers' Championship, with <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a> being third. In the World Constructors' Championship, <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> held an eight-point lead over Ferrari, with <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a> a further eighty-one points behind in third.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> started the race from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>, his first since the <a href="/wiki/2008_French_Grand_Prix" title="2008 French Grand Prix">2008 French Grand Prix</a>. In doing so, he broke <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a>'s record for the most race starts between pole positions, with one hundred and twenty-nine starts since his last pole. <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> won the race, Ferrari's first win in Monaco since <a href="/wiki/2001_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="2001 Monaco Grand Prix">2001</a>. Räikkönen finished second as this was Ferrari's first 1–2 finish since <a href="/wiki/2010_German_Grand_Prix" title="2010 German Grand Prix">2010 German Grand Prix</a>. <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> finished third. With the result, Vettel extended his Drivers' Championship lead over <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>, while in the World Constructors' Championship, Ferrari reclaimed the points lead from Mercedes.
</p>

</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2017_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2017_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> missed the race in order to participate in the <a href="/wiki/2017_Indianapolis_500" title="2017 Indianapolis 500">Indianapolis 500</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ALO_Indy_500_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ALO_Indy_500-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Alonso became the first active Formula One driver to race at the Indy 500 since <a href="/wiki/Teo_Fabi" title="Teo Fabi">Teo Fabi</a> in <a href="/wiki/1984_Indianapolis_500" title="1984 Indianapolis 500">1984</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-TSN-SC-2017-05-28_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TSN-SC-2017-05-28-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> replaced Alonso for the race.
</p><p>Prior to the national anthem, a minute's silence was observed on the grid before the race as a mark of respect to the victims of the <a href="/wiki/Manchester_Arena_bombing" title="Manchester Arena bombing">Manchester Arena bombing</a> earlier in the week.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Free_practice">Free practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2017_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Free practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Thursday morning's first practice ended with <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a>' <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> setting the quickest lap with 1:13.425, approximately 0.2 seconds quicker than his championship rival <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a>. <a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a> was third quickest and <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Daniil_Kvyat" title="Daniil Kvyat">Daniil Kvyat</a> was the fastest driver not in one of the top three teams, setting the sixth fastest time.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In second practice, Vettel was quickest with 1:12.720. Second through fourth were <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a>, and Kvyat.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The Mercedes team had a disappointing second practice as they finished eighth and tenth, off the pace of their championship rivals <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>. The session was stopped part way through for ten minutes due to a crash by <a href="/wiki/Lance_Stroll" title="Lance Stroll">Lance Stroll</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Saturday's Free Practice 3 ended with <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>'s Sebastian Vettel setting the bar at 1:12.395, besting his own time set in FP2 by 0.3 seconds to set a new lap record, whilst nearest rival and teammate <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> was 0.3 seconds behind with 1:12.740.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The session was interrupted 8 minutes in by yellow flags when <a href="/wiki/Esteban_Ocon" title="Esteban Ocon">Esteban Ocon</a> of <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a> crashed in the swimming pool section,<sup id="cite_ref-:1_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> mimicking an incident Max Verstappen had during the 2016 qualifying session. Free practice eventually resumed with 4 minutes remaining. <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> had a brake-by-wire failure and ended up retiring from the session, leaving him sixth with 1:13.392. <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> ended the session in twelfth, but incurred a 15-place grid penalty for changing the <a href="/wiki/MGU-H" class="mw-redirect" title="MGU-H">MGU-H</a> and <a href="/wiki/Turbocharger" title="Turbocharger">turbocharger</a>, which would be applied after qualifying.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2017_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Ferrari's Kimi Räikkönen qualified on pole position for the first time in 9 years; his last pole position came at the <a href="/wiki/2008_French_Grand_Prix" title="2008 French Grand Prix">2008 French Grand Prix</a>, also for Ferrari. His teammate and championship leader <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> qualified second, followed by <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a>. Vettel's closest rival, Lewis Hamilton, qualified in fourteenth; he struggled warming up the tyres and his final flying lap in Q2 was impeded by <a href="/wiki/Stoffel_Vandoorne" title="Stoffel Vandoorne">Stoffel Vandoorne</a> who had crashed towards the end of the session. Despite Vandoorne's accident, both McLarens had made it into Q3 for the first time in 2017; Jenson Button qualified in ninth place on his return, but due to his fifteen-place grid penalty would start from the back of the grid, and eventually opted to start from the pit lane.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2017_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Räikkönen led from the start until he pitted on lap 33. His teammate Vettel stayed out longer and when he eventually pitted, he came out ahead of Räikkönen and went on to win his 3rd race of the season and extended his championship lead to 25 points over Hamilton, who finished in 7th in a recovery drive after his poor qualifying. This win gave Ferrari their first 1-2 finish since the controversial <a href="/wiki/2010_German_Grand_Prix" title="2010 German Grand Prix">2010 German Grand Prix</a>. Daniel Ricciardo finished in 3rd, despite lightly hitting the wall late in the race, jumping both Bottas and Verstappen by staying out longer on the ultra-softs in a strategy akin to Sebastian Vettel. Carlos Sainz had his best finish of the year with 6th for Toro Rosso. He was followed by Hamilton, Grosjean, Massa and Magnussen, who rounded off the points. It was the first time in Haas' history that they achieved a double points finish. It was also the first time in 2017 that both Force Indias finished out of the points with a late collision between Pérez and Kvyat, while running in the points, sent Pérez to last and Kvyat out of the race. Stoffel Vandoorne looked set to score McLaren's first points of the season before hitting the wall after being forced onto the breaking up asphalt by Sergio Pérez. Jenson Button, meanwhile, retired with suspension damage on his one-off return, much like his original retirement in the <a href="/wiki/2016_Abu_Dhabi_Grand_Prix" title="2016 Abu Dhabi Grand Prix">2016 Abu Dhabi Grand Prix</a>, after colliding with Sauber's <a href="/wiki/Pascal_Wehrlein" title="Pascal Wehrlein">Pascal Wehrlein</a>, leaving Wehrlein perched up vertically on the wall. The other Sauber of <a href="/wiki/Marcus_Ericsson" title="Marcus Ericsson">Marcus Ericsson</a> retired after hitting the wall at turn 1 under the safety car. The Renault of <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a> retired early on with a gearbox failure, while running in 10th. <a href="/wiki/Lance_Stroll" title="Lance Stroll">Lance Stroll</a> was another retiree.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After the race Sergio Pérez and Jenson Button had two penalty points (each) added to their <a href="/wiki/Super_Licence" class="mw-redirect" title="Super Licence">Super Licences</a>, both for causing a collision.
</p>
