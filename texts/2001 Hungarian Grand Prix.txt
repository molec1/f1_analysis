<p>The <b>2001 Hungarian Grand Prix</b> (officially the <b>XVII Marlboro Magyar Nagydij</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at <a href="/wiki/Hungaroring" title="Hungaroring">Hungaroring</a> on 19 August 2001. It was the 13th race of the <a href="/wiki/2001_FIA_Formula_One_World_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="2001 FIA Formula One World Championship">2001 FIA Formula One World Championship</a> and the 16th edition of the <a href="/wiki/Hungarian_Grand_Prix" title="Hungarian Grand Prix">event</a> as part of the series. <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> won the 77-lap race from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>. His teammate <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> finished second and <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> was third for <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>.
</p><p>Michael Schumacher qualified on pole position by setting the fastest lap in qualifying, and he began alongside Coulthard. Barrichello made a brisk start to pass Coulthard and he tactically delayed the McLaren driver from lap 12 to provide his teammate Michael Schumacher with a significant lead over the rest of the field. Schumacher only lost the lead during the <a href="/wiki/Pit_stop" title="Pit stop">pit stop</a> phases, maintaining the lead for the majority of the Grand Prix to take his seventh victory of the season and the 51st of his career. His win confirmed him as the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">2001 Drivers' Champion</a>, as Coulthard could not pass his <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Championship_points_scoring_systems" title="List of Formula One World Championship points scoring systems">points</a> total with four races remaining in the season, and he equalled <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> for the all-time record of career race victories.
</p><p>Schumacher received praise from many in the Formula One community for his title victory and it was headline news in Germany and Italy. Barrichello's second-place finish helped Ferrari win the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a> for the third consecutive season and the 11th time overall. McLaren were 13 points ahead of <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> in third while <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a> moved one point ahead of <a href="/wiki/British_American_Racing" title="British American Racing">British American Racing</a> (BAR) for fourth.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2001_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The 2001 Hungarian Grand Prix was the 13th of the 17 motor races of the <a href="/wiki/2001_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2001 Formula One World Championship">2001 Formula One World Championship</a> and the 16th running of the <a href="/wiki/Hungarian_Grand_Prix" title="Hungarian Grand Prix">event</a> as part of the series. It took place at the 16-turn 3.975&#160;km (2.470&#160;mi) <a href="/wiki/Hungaroring" title="Hungaroring">Hungaroring</a> in <a href="/wiki/Mogyor%C3%B3d" title="Mogyoród">Mogyoród</a>, <a href="/wiki/Budapest" title="Budapest">Budapest</a> on 19 August.<sup id="cite_ref-CF1Race_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CF1Race-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-F1NetPreview_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1NetPreview-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The Hungaroring has been likened to the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Monaco" title="Circuit de Monaco">Circuit de Monaco</a> because it is narrow, making overtaking difficult. The circuit was bumpy and dusty and it did not provide a major advantage to cars installed with powerful engines.<sup id="cite_ref-F1COMPreview_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1COMPreview-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Before the race <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> led the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_champions" class="mw-redirect" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; champions">Drivers' Championship</a> with 84 <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Championship_points_scoring_systems" title="List of Formula One World Championship points scoring systems">points</a>, ahead of <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> of <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> with 47 points and <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a>' <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> with 41. <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> in the other Ferrari was fourth on 40 points, and McLaren's <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> was fifth with 19 points.<sup id="cite_ref-points_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a> Ferrari led with 124 points, 58 points ahead of McLaren. Williams were third with 56 points; <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a> with 19 points and <a href="/wiki/British_American_Racing" title="British American Racing">British American Racing</a> (BAR) with 16 points contended for fourth place.<sup id="cite_ref-points_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> A maximum of 40 points were available for the final four races, which meant Coulthard and Ralf Schumacher could still win the title. Michael Schumacher needed to win the Grand Prix, regardless of where Coulthard and Ralf Schumacher finished. In the event both Ferrari cars finished first and second, the team would secure the Constructors' Championship for the third consecutive year.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>By winning the race, Michael Schumacher would take his 51st career victory, putting him equal with four-time World Champion <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> and join Prost and <a href="/wiki/Juan_Manuel_Fangio" title="Juan Manuel Fangio">Juan Manuel Fangio</a> as the third driver to win four or more titles.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> He said the championship was not in his mindset in Hungary and was looking forward to the race, "I am excited about the possibility of becoming champion a fourth time but if I don't look it, it's because I am not there yet. How can I be excited if I am not there yet? Only having the possibility doesn't count. I will get excited when it is in my pocket."<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> In contrast, Coulthard was within four points of Michael Schumacher entering the <a href="/wiki/2001_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="2001 Monaco Grand Prix">Monaco Grand Prix</a>; he finished fifth after failing to move off the starting <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_motorsport_terms#G" title="Glossary of motorsport terms">grid</a> due to a computer glitch. Thereafter, mechanical attrition and one accident meant Coulthard scored six points in the next five races and fell 37 points behind Schumacher.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>There were two driver changes going into the race. Having been in one of the Prost cars since the opening race of the season in <a href="/wiki/2001_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="2001 Australian Grand Prix">Australia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a> completed negotiations for the release of his contract with the French constructor and joined the <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a> team for the rest of the season. Alesi's <a href="/wiki/Prost_AP04" title="Prost AP04">Prost AP04</a> was subsequently taken over by <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a>—whom Jordan sacked before the preceding <a href="/wiki/2001_German_Grand_Prix" title="2001 German Grand Prix">German Grand Prix</a>—until the conclusion of the 2001 championship.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Both drivers were allowed to familiarise themselves with their new cars in the week before the race. Frentzen undertook a 50&#160;km (31&#160;mi) shakedown test (the maximum amount of mileage allowed under the sporting regulations during the three-week summer moratorium from in-season testing) at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Nevers_Magny-Cours" title="Circuit de Nevers Magny-Cours">Circuit de Nevers Magny-Cours</a> and Alesi undertook his running at the <a href="/wiki/Silverstone_Circuit" title="Silverstone Circuit">Silverstone Circuit</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Several teams made modifications to their <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_car" title="Formula One car">cars</a> to maximise slow speed <a href="/wiki/Grip_(auto_racing)" title="Grip (auto racing)">grip</a> and traction and <a href="/wiki/Racing_setup" title="Racing setup">setup</a> for the Hungaroring's high-speed <a href="/wiki/Chicane" title="Chicane">chicanes</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-F1COMPreview_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1COMPreview-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a> introduced a revised chassis for both of its race and test <a href="/wiki/Minardi_PS01" title="Minardi PS01">PS01s</a>, which comprised a new titanium <a href="/wiki/Transmission_(mechanics)" title="Transmission (mechanics)">gearbox</a> and rear <a href="/wiki/Suspension_(vehicle)" class="mw-redirect" title="Suspension (vehicle)">suspension</a> geometry, tested by <a href="/wiki/Andrea_Piccini" title="Andrea Piccini">Andrea Piccini</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Fiorano_Circuit" title="Fiorano Circuit">Fiorano Circuit</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> McLaren mounted a secondary wing to the tail of the <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4-16" title="McLaren MP4-16">MP4/16</a>'s engine cover above its gearbox and Williams did the same as it installed cooling bodywork on the engine for the first time since the <a href="/wiki/2001_Malaysian_Grand_Prix" title="2001 Malaysian Grand Prix">Malaysian Grand Prix</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a> and Sauber teams fitted new front wings and undertrays to their vehicles while the former constructor also introduced revised <a href="/wiki/Bargeboard_(aerodynamics)" title="Bargeboard (aerodynamics)">bargeboards</a> and a rear <a href="/wiki/Diffuser_(automotive)" title="Diffuser (automotive)">diffuser</a>. Both Ferrari and <a href="/wiki/Jaguar_Racing" title="Jaguar Racing">Jaguar</a> brought a revised high-<a href="/wiki/Downforce" title="Downforce">downforce</a> configuration that was an evolution of the package they utilised at the Monaco Grand Prix.<sup id="cite_ref-FIAFriday_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAFriday-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> BAR installed a new diffuser featuring two vertical strakes in each side channel, as well as a new floor and front wing. The <a href="/wiki/BAR_003" title="BAR 003">003</a> also featured additional winglets on the engine cover that were used at the Monaco Grand Prix. Jordan brought significant updates, including a new engine cover with a deeper top section and shallower lower section and a revised shape around the rear suspension. The new bodywork was complimented by a new undertray and diffuser. Jordan added a horizontal narrowing chord winglet similar to that of the <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW23" title="Williams FW23">Williams FW23</a> ahead of the sidepod inlets. Jordan also modified the pedal arrangement for Alesi's car, as he disliked the <a href="/wiki/Left-foot_braking" title="Left-foot braking">left-foot braking</a> technique used by Frentzen and Zonta. <a href="/wiki/Arrows_Grand_Prix_International" title="Arrows Grand Prix International">Arrows</a> brought a new front wing for the <a href="/wiki/Arrows_A22" title="Arrows A22">A22</a> and installed small wings over the rain light and rear <a href="/wiki/Axle" title="Axle">axle</a> used at the Monaco Grand Prix. Prost used its double-element front wing from Monaco and added a small wing over the rear axle. Frentzen used <a href="/wiki/Brembo" title="Brembo">Brembo</a> brakes instead of the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Carbon_Industrie&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Carbon Industrie (page does not exist)">Carbon Industrie</a> components Alesi preferred.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice">Practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2001_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Four practice sessions were held before the Sunday race, two each on Friday and Saturday. The Friday morning and afternoon sessions each lasted an hour; the third and fourth sessions, on Saturday morning, lasted 45 minutes each.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Friday's two practice sessions were held in hot and clear weather.<sup id="cite_ref-FIAFriday_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAFriday-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> In the first practice session, Michael Schumacher was fastest with a lap of 1 minute,16.995 seconds, 0.288 seconds faster than his teammate Barrichello in second. The McLaren cars of Häkkinen and Coulthard, Ralf Schumacher, <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> of Sauber, Jordan's <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a>, the BAR vehicles of <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Panis" title="Olivier Panis">Olivier Panis</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> for Sauber rounded out the session's top ten drivers.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1FP1_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1FP1-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Alesi was restricted to four timed laps because he skidded into a gravel trap at turn 13.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1FP1_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1FP1-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Ralf Schumacher avoided curtailing his session after a spin into the turn one gravel trap and reversing out of it.<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportFP1_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportFP1-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard <a href="/wiki/Understeer_and_oversteer" title="Understeer and oversteer">understeered</a> into the turn 12 chicane gravel trap and pushed the McLaren's undertray through the bottom of its <a href="/wiki/Monocoque" title="Monocoque">monocoque</a> on a serrated <a href="/wiki/Curb" title="Curb">kerb</a> with five minutes left,<sup id="cite_ref-FIAFriday_13-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAFriday-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> causing a four-minute stoppage to clear carbon fibre debris.<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportFP1_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportFP1-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Michael Schumacher repeated his morning form in the second practice session with the day's fastest lap of 1 minute, 16.651 seconds. Barrichello in second closed to within 0.083 seconds of his teammate and Häkkinen third. Ralf Schumacher was fourth-fastest, ahead of Jaguar's <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a>, Alesi, <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> of Benetton, Heidfeld, Panis and Coulthard in fifth to tenth. Several drivers, including Frentzen, Fisichella and Ralf Schumacher, spun or went off the circuit during the session without damaging their cars.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> After Friday's sessions, Ferrari and Coulthard lodged a complaint with the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile</a> race director <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Whiting" title="Charlie Whiting">Charlie Whiting</a> over the height of the kerbs at turn 12. Race officials agreed to lower its height by 2.5&#160;cm (0.98&#160;in) on Saturday morning to allow cars to be propelled more smoothly through turn 12.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It continued to be hot and humid for the two practice sessions on Saturday morning.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher set the third practice session's fastest lap of 1 minute, 15.466 seconds. His teammate Barrichello remained in second. The two McLaren cars of Häkkinen and Coulthard were third and fourth. Heidfeld, Ralf Schumacher, Panis, Trulli, <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a> of the Williams team and Räikkönen were in positions six through ten. While the session passed relatively peacefully, Räikkönen stopped on track midway through practice with his car's overheating <a href="/wiki/Exhaust_system" title="Exhaust system">exhaust system</a> catching fire, which was extinguished by <a href="/wiki/Motorsport_marshal" title="Motorsport marshal">track marshals</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Coulthard was quickest in the final practice session to prevent Ferrari from leading every session with a 1-minute, 15.266 seconds lap. Michael Schumacher could not go faster after going wide onto the grass exiting turn six and later spun off the track at turn 12. He remained in second in front of his teammate Barrichello in third and the faster Heidfeld in fourth. Häkkinen, Trulli, Ralf Schumacher, Montoya, Irvine and Fisichella were fifth through tenth. Seven minutes into the session, Ralf Schumacher beached his car in a gravel trap at turn nine and Minardi's <a href="/wiki/Tarso_Marques" title="Tarso Marques">Tarso Marques</a> spun at turn nine.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2001_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Saturday afternoon's one hour qualifying session saw each driver limited to twelve laps, with the starting order decided by their fastest laps. During this session the <a href="/wiki/107%25_rule" title="107% rule">107% rule</a> was in effect, requiring each driver to remain within 107 per cent of the fastest lap time to qualify for the race.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> It remained hot and clear with the air temperature from 29 to 31&#160;°C (84 to 88&#160;°F) and a track temperature between 33 to 41&#160;°C (91 to 106&#160;°F).<sup id="cite_ref-FIAQualifying_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAQualifying-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Several drivers waited for 20 minutes before commencing their first timed laps.<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportQuali_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportQuali-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher overtook Hakkinen as the fastest driver on his first attempt,<sup id="cite_ref-DTQuali_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DTQuali-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> and set his best lap with 20 minutes to go, taking his ninth pole position of the season and the 41st of his career with a new qualifying track record of 1 minute, 14.059 seconds.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> He was joined on the grid's front row by Coulthard who was 0.801 seconds slower;<sup id="cite_ref-DTQuali_27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DTQuali-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> an oversteer put him wide at the left-hand turn 13 and Frentzen had earlier slowed him. Barrichello in third was delayed by Montoya early on after which his Ferrari's balance changed and developed more understeer due to rising track temperatures.<sup id="cite_ref-FIAQualifying_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAQualifying-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Ralf Schumacher was the highest-placed <a href="/wiki/Michelin" title="Michelin">Michelin</a> shod car in fourth,<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportQuali_26-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportQuali-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> and fifth-placed Trulli aborted one timed lap after an error.<sup id="cite_ref-FIAQualifying_25-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAQualifying-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Häkkinen had difficulty finding an ideal setup for his McLaren and took sixth. Heidfeld, seventh, aborted two of his timed laps through separate minor errors. Eighth-placed Montoya had a power understeer that caused him to spin. An understeer in Räikkönen's Sauber on his last two laps put him ninth. Villeneuve adjusted his car's setup to qualify tenth.<sup id="cite_ref-FIAQualifying_25-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAQualifying-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-AutosportQuali_26-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportQuali-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Panis in 11th was the fastest driver not to qualify in the top ten because he lost time with an electrical fault and spun because of an inoperable <a href="/wiki/Traction_control_system" title="Traction control system">traction control system</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-SatQuotes_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SatQuotes-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Alesi made it an all-French sixth row in 11th; he admitted he required additional familiarity with his car.<sup id="cite_ref-FIAQualifying_25-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAQualifying-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> The Jaguars occupied 13th and 14th places: <a href="/wiki/Pedro_de_la_Rosa" title="Pedro de la Rosa">Pedro de la Rosa</a> ahead of his teammate Irvine; the former made errors on his first two timed laps and the latter said he over-drove in an attempt to qualify in the top ten. Fisichella in the faster of the two Benetton cars in 15th reported a loss of grip.<sup id="cite_ref-SatQuotes_29-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SatQuotes-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Frentzen in a lightly fuelled car could not match his form from the morning's practice sessions and took 16th. Two engine failures in practice required <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> to drive the spare Benetton, which had poor grip and balance and left him 17th. <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> qualified his Minardi in 18th. <a href="/wiki/Luciano_Burti" title="Luciano Burti">Luciano Burti</a> of the Prost team had balance problems that caused his car to yearn from understeer and oversteer and was 19th.<sup id="cite_ref-FIAQualifying_25-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAQualifying-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> The two <a href="/wiki/Arrows_Grand_Prix_International" title="Arrows Grand Prix International">Arrows</a> of <a href="/wiki/Enrique_Bernoldi" title="Enrique Bernoldi">Enrique Bernoldi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jos_Verstappen" title="Jos Verstappen">Jos Verstappen</a> were 20th and 21st; Verstappen utilised Arrows' spare vehicle after damaging his race monocoque in a crash during practice and Bernoldi's engine cut out on his final timed lap.<sup id="cite_ref-FIAQualifying_25-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAQualifying-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-SatQuotes_29-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SatQuotes-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Tarso Marques for Minardi was the final qualifier in 22nd as he had a setup that could not be changed for increased car performance due to him replacing a sensor.<sup id="cite_ref-SatQuotes_29-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SatQuotes-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Warm-up">Warm-up</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2001_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Warm-up">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A half an hour warm-up session took place on Sunday morning in dry and hot weather.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_15-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FIAWarmUp_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAWarmUp-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> All drivers fine-tuned their race set-ups against the weather of the time, and set laps in their spare cars.<sup id="cite_ref-FIAWarmUp_32-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAWarmUp-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> While he was briefly delayed by a battery fault that affected his car's gear change mechanism,<sup id="cite_ref-FIAWarmUp_32-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAWarmUp-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard led with a lap of 1 minute and 16.915 seconds with the Ferrari duo of Michael Schumacher and Barrichello second and third. Ralf Schumacher, Häkkinen, Trulli, Heidfeld, Panis, Räikkönen and Irvine occupied positions four to ten.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1WarmUp_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1WarmUp-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Bernoldi was the only driver to lose control of his car during the session, running wide at the first corner and continued.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1WarmUp_33-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1WarmUp-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2001_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The race took place in the afternoon from 14:00 local time.<sup id="cite_ref-F1NetPreview_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1NetPreview-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The weather was hot and humid with some cloud cover, with he air temperature between 27 to 33&#160;°C (81 to 91&#160;°F) and the track temperature from 40 to 41&#160;°C (104 to 106&#160;°F);<sup id="cite_ref-F1NetRace_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1NetRace-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-GF1Temps_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF1Temps-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ITVF1Race_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ITVF1Race-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> conditions were expected to remain consistent with a light south-easterly wind and a 20 percent chance of rain was forecast.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> Tyre consistency from over the season was predicted to allow for a one-stop strategy and had the possibility of a slower car impeding the leaders for several laps. A two stop-strategy was theorised to provide drivers with a better chance of maintaining on-track position.<sup id="cite_ref-Strategy_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Strategy-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> While on a reconnaissance lap, Michael Schumacher locked his rear brakes and ran into the turn 12 gravel trap en route to the grid. The Ferrari mechanics removed most of the stones from his car and replaced the left-hand side turning vane as a precaution to ensure there was no loose bodywork and changed his tyres.<sup id="cite_ref-ObserverRace_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ObserverRace-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-AF1RaceReport_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1RaceReport-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FIARace_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIARace-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> In the meantime, Coulthard's race car had a gearbox problem and he switched to the spare McLaren for the start.<sup id="cite_ref-F1NetRace_34-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1NetRace-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Rubens_Barrichello_2010_Malaysia.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/28/Rubens_Barrichello_2010_Malaysia.jpg/170px-Rubens_Barrichello_2010_Malaysia.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="236" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/28/Rubens_Barrichello_2010_Malaysia.jpg/255px-Rubens_Barrichello_2010_Malaysia.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/28/Rubens_Barrichello_2010_Malaysia.jpg/340px-Rubens_Barrichello_2010_Malaysia.jpg 2x" data-file-width="800" data-file-height="1110" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Rubens_Barrichello_2010_Malaysia.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> <i>(pictured in 2010)</i> finished in second to help Ferrari win its third <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a> in a row.</div></div></div>
<p>Michael Schumacher made a brisk start to maintain his pole position advantage.<sup id="cite_ref-BBCWebRace_43-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCWebRace-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard in second made a slow getaway on the dirty side of the track, providing less grip, and Barrichello overtook him on his left into turn one.<sup id="cite_ref-F1NetRace_34-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1NetRace-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-CrashRace_44-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CrashRace-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> In the middle of the pack, Irvine attempted to pass Fisichella on the left;<sup id="cite_ref-AF1RaceReport_41-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1RaceReport-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FIARace_42-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIARace-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup>  he lost control of the rear of his car on some dirt and got beached in the gravel trap.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1Quotes_45-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1Quotes-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> Trulli slowed to avoid a collision with Heidfeld.<sup id="cite_ref-GPCOMRace_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPCOMRace-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Further round the lap, Marques ran off the circuit after he and Frentzen made contact in turn two; both drivers continued.<sup id="cite_ref-FIARace_42-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIARace-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-AF1Quotes_45-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1Quotes-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> Verstappen made the best getaway in the field, moving from 21st to 16th by the conclusion of the first lap, as Frentzen fell four positions over the same distance. At the end of the first lap, Michael Schumacher led his teammate Barrichello by 1.3 seconds with Coulthard a further half a second behind in third. Ralf Schumacher was fourth, Trulli fifth and Häkkinen sixth.<sup id="cite_ref-LapChart_46-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LapChart-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> As the top three began to pull away from the rest of the field, extending their deficit over Ralf Schumacher to 6.8 seconds on lap five,<sup id="cite_ref-LapChart_46-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LapChart-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> the stewards informed the Benetton team that Button had <a href="/wiki/Standing_start" title="Standing start">jumped the start</a> for which he was imposed a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_regulations#Penalties" title="Formula One regulations">ten second stop-and-go penalty</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-F1COMRace_47-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1COMRace-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> He took the penalty on the following lap.<sup id="cite_ref-BBCWebRace_43-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCWebRace-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Trulli in fifth slowed Häkkinen, Heidfeld, Montoya and Heidfeld from the eighth lap as Hakkinen attempted an unsuccessful overtake on Trulli.<sup id="cite_ref-LapChart_46-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LapChart-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-F1COMRace_47-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1COMRace-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> On lap nine, Burti became the Grand Prix's second retirement after he spun into a gravel trap at turn 14 on heavily blistered tyres reducing grip.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1RaceReport_41-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1RaceReport-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-CrashRace_44-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CrashRace-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> Alesi overtook de la Rosa at turn two for 12th on lap 10.<sup id="cite_ref-ITVF1Race_36-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ITVF1Race-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-F1COMRace_47-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1COMRace-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> At the front of the field, Barrichello began to tactically slow Coulthard by a second per lap on lap 12, allowing his teammate Michael Schumacher to lead comfortably while lapping slower traffic later on.<sup id="cite_ref-F1NetRace_34-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1NetRace-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> That lap, Bernoldi locked his rear brakes in him catching Alonso and retired by spinning into a gravel trap at turn four.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1RaceReport_41-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1RaceReport-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-AF1Quotes_45-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1Quotes-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> On lap 13, Button passed Marques to retake 18th. By his first pit stop of the race at the conclusion of the 28th lap, Michael Schumacher extended his lead to 12.8 seconds and broke <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a>'s official lap record from the <a href="/wiki/1992_Hungarian_Grand_Prix" title="1992 Hungarian Grand Prix">1992 edition</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-F1NetRace_34-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1NetRace-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-LapChart_46-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LapChart-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> His stop lasted 8.4 seconds and relinquished the lead to his teammate Barrichello. Michael Schumacher rejoined the race in third, ahead of his brother Ralf Schumacher.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1RaceReport_41-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1RaceReport-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On the 29th lap, Trulli entered the pit lane for his first pit stop. He emerged in 12th after a refuelling rig was lodged in his car for five seconds longer than planned.<sup id="cite_ref-GPCOMRace_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPCOMRace-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Ralf Schumacher entered the pit lane on the following lap and he remained in fourth place.<sup id="cite_ref-FIARace_42-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIARace-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello stayed on the track until at the end of lap 31 to delay Coulthard in second before entering the pit lane for a 9.1 second pit stop to take on 91&#160;l (20&#160;imp&#160;gal; 24&#160;US&#160;gal) of fuel. The tactic did not work as Coulthard's pit stop on the next lap was faster than Barrichello's and he overtook him. Coulthard was therefore able to drive in clear air and given an opportunity to close up to Michael Schumacher.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1RaceReport_41-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1RaceReport-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> Further back, Button overtook Alonso for 17th on lap 33.<sup id="cite_ref-LapChart_46-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LapChart-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> Two more retirements occurred during this stage of the Grand Prix: on lap 35, Button lost control of the rear of the rear of his Benetton car through the final corner, and stopped in the centre of the circuit on the start/finish line.<sup id="cite_ref-F1NetRace_34-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1NetRace-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> He remained there due to the car <a href="/wiki/Stall_(engine)" title="Stall (engine)">stalling</a>, engaged in gear,<sup id="cite_ref-F1COMRace_47-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1COMRace-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> and facing the opposite direction before marshals extricated him under <a href="/wiki/Racing_flags#Yellow_flag" title="Racing flags">yellow flag</a> conditions.<sup id="cite_ref-F1NetRace_34-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1NetRace-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Alonso spun into a gravel trap at turn one when his rear brakes failed on the 38th lap.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1RaceReport_41-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1RaceReport-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> As Coulthard lowered the gap to Michael Schumacher to 11 seconds, he could not get closer to the Ferrari driver who made a second pit stop on lap 52.<sup id="cite_ref-FIARace_42-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIARace-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Michael_Schumacher-I%27m_the_man_(cropped).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/Michael_Schumacher-I%27m_the_man_%28cropped%29.jpg/170px-Michael_Schumacher-I%27m_the_man_%28cropped%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="230" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/Michael_Schumacher-I%27m_the_man_%28cropped%29.jpg/255px-Michael_Schumacher-I%27m_the_man_%28cropped%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/Michael_Schumacher-I%27m_the_man_%28cropped%29.jpg/340px-Michael_Schumacher-I%27m_the_man_%28cropped%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1260" data-file-height="1704" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Michael_Schumacher-I%27m_the_man_(cropped).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>, who was crowned the <a href="/wiki/2001_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2001 Formula One World Championship">2001 Formula One Drivers' Champion</a> as a result of his win (pictured in 2005)</div></div></div>
<p>Barrichello made his second pit stop on the next lap, and he rejoined in third, behind his teammate Michael Schumacher.<sup id="cite_ref-FIARace_42-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIARace-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard led laps 53 and 54 and it appeared he would retain second since the deficit between him and Barrichello was 22.6 seconds.<sup id="cite_ref-CrashRace_44-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CrashRace-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-LapChart_46-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LapChart-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> During his second pit stop, the car's refuelling rig was jammed for two seconds. Coulthard emerged in third, behind Barrichello. On that lap, Trulli pulled off to the side of the track to retire with an hydraulic failure.<sup id="cite_ref-ITVF1Race_36-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ITVF1Race-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-GPCOMRace_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPCOMRace-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> On the 58th lap, Panis drove into the garage to allow his team to rectify an electrical fault with his car.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1RaceReport_41-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1RaceReport-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-LapChart_46-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LapChart-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> Behind the top three, the fifth-placed Häkkinen, who set the overall fastest lap of 1 minute and 16.723 seconds on lap 51,<sup id="cite_ref-LapChart_46-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LapChart-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> was two seconds a lap faster than Ralf Schumacher in fourth. He lined up a pass on the inside in the corners; he could not pass because of the Williams' higher straightline speed.<sup id="cite_ref-ITVF1Race_36-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ITVF1Race-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-F1COMRace_47-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1COMRace-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> Frentzen joined the list of retirees by spinning into a gravel trap on lap 64. Marques was instructed by his team via radio to stop at the side of the track at turn 12 and switch his car's engine off because of low <a href="/wiki/Pressure_regulator" title="Pressure regulator">fuel pressure</a> soon after. On the 70th lap, Fisichella's engine failed, causing him to spin into a gravel trap beside the circuit, and forcing him to retire.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1RaceReport_41-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1RaceReport-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-AF1Quotes_45-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1Quotes-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Häkkinen made the race's final pit stop for fuel on lap 71. He remained in fifth and fell back from Ralf Schumacher. Two laps later, Panis parked his car in the garage to retire after one exploratory lap.<sup id="cite_ref-FIARace_42-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIARace-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-BBCWebRace_43-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCWebRace-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> Upfront, Michael Schumacher slowed and Barrichello held off Coulthard to enable his teammate to take his seventh victory of the season,<sup id="cite_ref-CrashRace_44-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CrashRace-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> and the 51st of his career in a time of 1 hour, 41 minutes and 49.675 seconds at an average speed of 180.344&#160;km/h (112.061&#160;mph), equalling Prost's all-time wins record.<sup id="cite_ref-IHTRace_48-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IHTRace-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> He became the 2001 Drivers' Champion as Coulthard could not catch his points total with four races left in the season. Michael Schumacher also became the first driver to win consecutive Drivers' Championships with Ferrari since <a href="/wiki/Alberto_Ascari" title="Alberto Ascari">Alberto Ascari</a> in the <a href="/wiki/1953_Formula_One_season" title="1953 Formula One season">1953 season</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ObserverRace_40-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ObserverRace-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello finished 3.363 seconds behind in second to win the third Constructors' Championship in a row for Ferrari and its 11th overall.<sup id="cite_ref-ObserverRace_40-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ObserverRace-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-GPCOMRace_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPCOMRace-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard followed in third, with Ralf Schumacher fourth, Häkkinen fifth and Heidfeld sixth. The final classified finishers were Räikkönen, Montoya, Villenueve, Alesi, de la Rosa and Verstappen.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1RaceReport_41-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1RaceReport-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> The attrition rate was high with 12 of the 22 starters not finishing the race.<sup id="cite_ref-IHTRace_48-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IHTRace-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2001_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatleft" style="width:40%;&#32;;"><style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r887775652">.mw-parser-output .quotebox{background-color:#F9F9F9;border:1px solid #aaa;box-sizing:border-box;padding:10px;font-size:88%;max-width:100%}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft{margin:0.5em 1.4em 0.8em 0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright{margin:0.5em 0 0.8em 1.4em}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.centered{margin:0.5em auto 0.8em auto}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft p,.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright p{font-style:inherit}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-title{background-color:#F9F9F9;text-align:center;font-size:larger;font-weight:bold}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:before{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" “ ";vertical-align:-45%;line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:after{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" ” ";line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .left-aligned{text-align:left}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .right-aligned{text-align:right}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .center-aligned{text-align:center}.mw-parser-output .quotebox cite{display:block;font-style:normal}@media screen and (max-width:360px){.mw-parser-output .quotebox{min-width:100%;margin:0 0 0.8em!important;float:none!important}}</style>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">I love you, I love you! I don't have the right words for you at the moment. This is simply amazing. We all love it. It is so lovely to work with you guys. I love you all. I love you all.</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style=""><a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>, speaking on his team radio after winning the Drivers' Championship.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>The top three drivers appeared on the podium to collect their trophies and spoke to the media in a later press conference.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_15-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher called his fourth Drivers' Championship and his record-equalling 51st career victory "a great achievement" and said comparisons of his three other titles and equalling Juan Manuel Fangio's five titles were unimportant, "I think we want to enjoy this and each one is different and each victory feels different. It's always something special, to win a Grand Prix, to win a championship even more, so that's what counts."<sup id="cite_ref-AF1PressConf_50-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1PressConf-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello spoke of his pride at helping Ferrari securing the Constructors' Championship and admitted to having mixed feelings over falling behind Coulthard after the first round of pit stops, "The first time I was so disappointed and the second time I was so happy, I couldn't believe it myself. I think it was probably the same with DC but just the opposite. Fortunately for me, it worked out. I had a wonderful start."<sup id="cite_ref-AF1PressConf_50-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1PressConf-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard was unemotional over losing the title because he sought to win, saying, "It makes no difference to me really whether I'm second, third or fourth, whatever it happens to be. What's important is how I perform in each of the individual races and providing I know that I've given 100 per cent effort from myself, which I know isn't always the case, but that's obviously the goal, to try and achieve that, then I can't do more than that."<sup id="cite_ref-AF1PressConf_50-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1PressConf-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Michael Schumacher received praise for his fourth World Drivers' Championship and it was headline news in Germany and Italy.<sup id="cite_ref-SCHUMoreWins_51-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SCHUMoreWins-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-GPCOMChamp_52-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPCOMChamp-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup> He was officially congratulated by <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Schr%C3%B6der" title="Gerhard Schröder">Gerhard Schröder</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Chancellor_of_Germany" title="Chancellor of Germany">chancellor of Germany</a>, who wrote a letter to Schumacher saying that his driving ability brought excitement to the sport and wished him well for the <a href="/wiki/2002_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2002 Formula One World Championship">2002 season</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Gianni_Agnelli" title="Gianni Agnelli">Gianni Agnelli</a>, the head of the <a href="/wiki/Fiat_Chrysler_Automobiles" title="Fiat Chrysler Automobiles">FIAT Group</a>, said he was overjoyed with the result,<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup> and the president of Ferrari <a href="/wiki/Luca_Cordero_di_Montezemolo" title="Luca Cordero di Montezemolo">Luca Cordero di Montezemolo</a> praised the team for their work over the season.<sup id="cite_ref-GPCOMChamp_52-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPCOMChamp-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup> Button predicted Ferrari would continue to be Formula One's dominant team and Schumacher its best driver in the long-term.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup> Mansell echoed Button's belief, adding, "If Ferrari maintain the impetus they currently have and given the personnel... then I can't see anyone stopping them for the next few years, not unless someone comes up with the perfect package."<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> Around 2,000 people watched the event on a large television screen in Michael Schumacher's hometown of <a href="/wiki/Kerpen" title="Kerpen">Kerpen</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Lower_Rhine_region" title="Lower Rhine region">Lower Rhine region</a> with the town decorated in the colours of Ferrari.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup> Church bells in <a href="/wiki/Maranello" title="Maranello">Maranello</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Emilia-Romagna" title="Emilia-Romagna">Emilia-Romagna</a> town where Ferrari's headquarters are based, rang with several of the marque's road cars driving slowly and fans of the team celebrating Schumacher's accomplishment.<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Ralf Schumacher, who finished fourth, called it "the toughest race of the year" because his vehicle had understeer and oversteer, "I had to work very hard to defend my position against Mika Hakkinen, which I managed to do."<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup> Häkkinen apportioned blame onto Trulli for his fifth-place result and said that him setting a new track record was of little consolation, "During the first 29 laps of the race, I was stuck behind Trulli, who was about 1.5 seconds slower per lap than me. It was only after his first pit stop that I could start to make inroads on Ralf Schumacher, who by then was about 30 seconds ahead."<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup> Irvine said he accepted responsibility for his first lap accident that ended his running, "I made a big charge at the beginning but maybe I carried too much into the corner and paid the price. With so few races left and only four points earned from the season so far, we've got to address the issues before Spa."<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The race result left Michael Schumacher as the Drivers' Champion with 94 points. Coulthard remained in second with 51 points. Third-placed Barrichello was five points behind Coulthard. Ralf Schumacher in fourth was a further two points behind Barrichello. With 21 points, Häkkinen was fifth.<sup id="cite_ref-points_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Ferrari won the Constructors' Championship with 140 points. McLaren in second (72 points) were 13 points ahead of Williams in third. Sauber were fourth with 20 points and the team increased their advantage over BAR to four points with four races remaining in the season.<sup id="cite_ref-points_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> After his title victory, Michael Schumacher said he was still focused on winning races, and also considered helping his teammate Barrichello finished second, something which Ferrari team principal <a href="/wiki/Jean_Todt" title="Jean Todt">Jean Todt</a> reiterated.<sup id="cite_ref-SCHUMoreWins_51-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SCHUMoreWins-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
