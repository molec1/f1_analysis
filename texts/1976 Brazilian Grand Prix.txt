<p>The <b>1976 Brazilian Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held at <a href="/wiki/Aut%C3%B3dromo_Jos%C3%A9_Carlos_Pace" title="Autódromo José Carlos Pace">Interlagos</a> in <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo" title="São Paulo">São Paulo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a> on 25 January 1976. It was the opening round of the <a href="/wiki/1976_Formula_One_season" title="1976 Formula One season">1976 Formula One season</a>. The race was the fifth <a href="/wiki/Brazilian_Grand_Prix" title="Brazilian Grand Prix">Brazilian Grand Prix</a> and the fourth to be held for the World Drivers' Championship. The race was held over 40 laps of the 7.87-kilometre circuit for a total race distance of 315 kilometres.
</p><p>The race was won by defending world champion, <a href="/wiki/Niki_Lauda" title="Niki Lauda">Niki Lauda</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_312T" title="Ferrari 312T">Ferrari 312T</a>. The Austrian driver won his eighth Formula One Grand Prix by 28 seconds over French driver <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Depailler" title="Patrick Depailler">Patrick Depailler</a> in a <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_007" title="Tyrrell 007">Tyrrell 007</a>. Second place was Depailler's best finish in almost two years having finished second previously at the <a href="/wiki/1974_Swedish_Grand_Prix" title="1974 Swedish Grand Prix">1974 Swedish Grand Prix</a>. <a href="/wiki/Tom_Pryce" title="Tom Pryce">Tom Pryce</a> finished third in a <a href="/wiki/Shadow_DN5B" class="mw-redirect" title="Shadow DN5B">Shadow DN5B</a> in his second podium in six months. It would prove to be the season highlight for Pryce and for <a href="/wiki/Shadow_Racing_Cars" title="Shadow Racing Cars">Shadow Racing Cars</a>. It was their only podium for the season and Pryce would not stand on the podium again.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1976_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Summary">Summary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1976_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Summary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>For the opening round of the season, James Hunt took his first Formula 1 pole position with reigning champion Niki Lauda alongside in his Ferrari. Emerson Fittipaldi qualified fifth on his debut for his brother Wilson's Copersucar team.<sup id="cite_ref-Overview_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Overview-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Qualifying_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Qualifying-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Clay Regazzoni in the second Ferrari took the lead at the start.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Lotus teammates Andretti and Peterson collided on the first lap, both retiring as a result.<sup id="cite_ref-LATimes_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LATimes-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Lauda was still in the lead ahead of Hunt and Jarier.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Hunt crashed out due to a sticking throttle, and Jarier did the same a lap later after driving on some oil in the track from Hunt's car.<sup id="cite_ref-LATimes_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LATimes-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Fittipaldi's debut race for Copersucar failed to live up to its initial promise, the Brazilian double world champion ending up three laps down after various technical problems. Lauda thus started his title defence with victory, with Patrick Depailler second in the Tyrrell, and Tom Pryce completing the podium in the other Shadow.<sup id="cite_ref-Results_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Results-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
