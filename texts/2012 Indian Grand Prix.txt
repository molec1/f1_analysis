<p>The <b>2012 Indian Grand Prix</b> (formally known as the <b>2012 Formula 1 Airtel Indian Grand Prix</b>)<sup id="cite_ref-formal_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-formal-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 28 October 2012 at the <a href="/wiki/Buddh_International_Circuit" title="Buddh International Circuit">Buddh International Circuit</a> in <a href="/wiki/Greater_Noida" title="Greater Noida">Greater Noida</a>, <a href="/wiki/Uttar_Pradesh" title="Uttar Pradesh">Uttar Pradesh</a>, <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7Dec_calendar_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7Dec_calendar-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The race was the seventeenth round of the <a href="/wiki/2012_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2012 Formula One season">2012 championship</a>, and marked the second running of the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Grand_Prix" title="Indian Grand Prix">Indian Grand Prix</a>. 
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> started the race from pole position.<sup id="cite_ref-VET_pole_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VET_pole-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Vettel won the race for the second consecutive year,<sup id="cite_ref-VET_win_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VET_win-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> but despite leading every lap of the race, he was denied a <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_driver_records#Pole,_led_entire_race_&amp;_scored_fastest_lap" title="List of Formula One driver records">Grand Chelem</a> when <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> set the fastest lap of the race. The organisers of the race invited <a href="/wiki/Shooting" title="Shooting">shooter</a> <a href="/wiki/Gagan_Narang" title="Gagan Narang">Gagan Narang</a>, who won a bronze medal for <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a> at the <a href="/wiki/London_Olympics" title="London Olympics">London Olympics</a>, to wave the chequered flag.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2012_Indian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2012_Indian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a> was taken ill ahead of the race, forcing <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a> to run test and reserve driver <a href="/wiki/Esteban_Guti%C3%A9rrez" title="Esteban Gutiérrez">Esteban Gutiérrez</a> in his place for the first free practice session.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Pérez was able to return to the car for the second practice session. <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a> once again ran in place of <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Senna" title="Bruno Senna">Bruno Senna</a> at <a href="/wiki/Williams_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams F1">Williams</a>, while <a href="/wiki/Giedo_van_der_Garde" title="Giedo van der Garde">Giedo van der Garde</a> drove <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Caterham_F1" title="Caterham F1">Caterham</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> was the centre of controversy over the course of the weekend when they displayed the flag of the <a href="/wiki/Italian_Navy" title="Italian Navy">Italian Navy</a> on the nose of the <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_F2012" title="Ferrari F2012">F2012</a> in support of the Italian sailors held by Indian authorities over a <a href="/wiki/2012_Italian_shooting_in_the_Arabian_Sea" class="mw-redirect" title="2012 Italian shooting in the Arabian Sea">shooting incident</a> that resulted in the deaths of two Indian fishermen in February 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Tyre supplier Pirelli brought its silver-banded hard compound tyre as the harder "prime" tyre and the yellow-banded soft compound tyre as the softer "option" tyre.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>Following track invasions by stray dogs in <a href="/wiki/2011_Indian_Grand_Prix" title="2011 Indian Grand Prix">2011</a>, race organisers took precautions by installing ultrasound devices around the circuit to deter animals from accessing the circuit.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Free_practice">Free practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2012_Indian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Free practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The first practice session was run without incident. <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> set the fastest time, despite having spent most of the ninety minutes of the session in his garage. <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> was second, three-tenths of a second behind Vettel, with <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> in third, a further tenth of a second behind.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Vettel was fastest in the second session as well, setting a time one tenth of a second faster than teammate <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> and six tenths of a second faster than <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The under-used surface of the circuit caused problems for several drivers, with <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Romain_Grosjean" title="Romain Grosjean">Romain Grosjean</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Senna" title="Bruno Senna">Bruno Senna</a> all spinning throughout the session, though all three were able to continue running without forcing the suspension of the session.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Vettel was once again fastest in the third practice session, two-tenths faster than Button and Webber.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2012_Indian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a> ensured that their cars were first on track for the first part of Saturday's qualifying session. <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Senna" title="Bruno Senna">Bruno Senna</a> set the early pace while the <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bulls</a> and <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLarens</a> stayed in the garage waiting for the track to become more grippy once rubber had been laid down. <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> attempted to improve <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>'s lap time by making Massa run in front of him on the straight so he could use the slipstream to gain extra top speed. Alonso's lap, however, was not faster than Massa's and the drafting idea was not used later in qualifying, presumably because it did not work. Massa was on a quick lap after this, but accelerated too much out of a corner and spun his car round; he recovered and returned to the pits. Once the Red Bulls and McLarens had set their lap times, the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso</a> of <a href="/wiki/Jean-%C3%89ric_Vergne" title="Jean-Éric Vergne">Jean-Éric Vergne</a> was left in eighteenth and therefore not within the top seventeen slots times required to make the next qualifying session. He set a lap time, but it was only good enough to move up one place. His teammate, <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a>, was the driver who progressed to Q2 himself when he pushed Vergne back into the eighteenth qualifying slot. Once again, the <a href="/wiki/Caterham_F1" title="Caterham F1">Caterhams</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marussia_F1" title="Marussia F1">Marrusias</a> and <a href="/wiki/HRT_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="HRT F1">HRTs</a> occupied the three final rows of the grid. <a href="/wiki/Vitaly_Petrov" title="Vitaly Petrov">Vitaly Petrov</a>, whose car had needed some last minute repair just before the session had begun, qualified in nineteenth place and  his teammate <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> ended up twentieth after spinning the car round on his final lap, and running back across the track before getting stuck in the gravel. <a href="/wiki/Narain_Karthikeyan" title="Narain Karthikeyan">Narain Karthikeyan</a> qualified in twenty-third place for his home Grand Prix, just one thousandth of a second slower than <a href="/wiki/Pedro_de_la_Rosa" title="Pedro de la Rosa">Pedro de la Rosa</a>. The Marussia cars of <a href="/wiki/Timo_Glock" title="Timo Glock">Timo Glock</a> and <a href="/wiki/Charles_Pic" title="Charles Pic">Charles Pic</a> ended up twenty-first and twenty-fourth, and last, respectively. Every car excluding the Red Bulls, McLarens, Ferraris and Lotuses used the softer compound 'option' tyre in the session. <a href="/wiki/Williams_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams F1">Williams</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Pastor_Maldonado" title="Pastor Maldonado">Pastor Maldonado</a> set the session's fastest time.
</p><p>The second part of qualifying began under yellow flag conditions because Kovalainen's stricken Caterham was still being returned to the pits. Every driver set a time not long after the flag had been lifted, and many returned to the pits not long after this. There was a flurry of lap times being set at the end of the session, and it was not certain which drivers had made it through until after the chequered flag was waved as every driver but Vettel, Webber and Hamilton came back on track. <a href="/wiki/Kamui_Kobayashi" title="Kamui Kobayashi">Kamui Kobayashi</a> qualified his <a href="/wiki/Sauber_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber F1">Sauber</a> in a disappointing seventeenth place, while his teammate made it into the final session of qualifying. It was the same story for <a href="/wiki/Romain_Grosjean" title="Romain Grosjean">Romain Grosjean</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Senna" title="Bruno Senna">Bruno Senna</a> and <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> who all failed to make Q3 after their teammates did. They lined up on the grid in 11th, 13th and 14th respectively. The Force India team were disappointed at their home race, with <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Paul_di_Resta" title="Paul di Resta">Paul di Resta</a> only taking twelfth and sixteenth spots on the grid respectively. At least one of their two drivers had qualified within the top ten at each of the last ten races. Daniel Ricciardo out-qualified Vergne for the thirteenth time in 2012, put his Toro Rosso in fifteenth position on the grid. <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> completed the final lap of the session to give himself the second fastest time behind Vettel.  
</p><p>Seven teams were represented in Q3, but the <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> of <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> elected not to run and consequently took tenth place on the grid. <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pastor_Maldonado" title="Pastor Maldonado">Pastor Maldonado</a> did well to qualify their cars in eighth and ninth positions; the <a href="/wiki/Lotus_F1" title="Lotus F1">Lotus</a> of Räikkönen was in front of them in seventh. The session was split into two informal phases, with cars choosing to make two attempts at a good time. <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> made a mistake on his first flying lap and ran across turn 7, but he set lap worthy of pole position on his following attempt. <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> set a lap half a tenth slower, initially in front of Button, Alonso and Hamilton. Both Red Bulls abandoned their laps at the very end of the session, as Vettel set a too slow first sector, and Webber ran wide at turn 3 whilst running behind the McLarens. Vettel took pole position for Red Bull's third consecutive front row lock-out. It was the first time Red Bull had had three front row lock-outs in a row, and the first time any team had done since McLaren did in <a href="/wiki/2007_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2007 Formula One World Championship">2007</a>. <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> set the last lap of qualifying to move his car up to third on the grid, only two tenths behind Webber. Button qualified fourth in front of Vettel's main title rival Alonso and Massa qualified sixth. This meant that the cars lined up two by two, with Red Bulls taking front row, McLaren taking the second and Ferrari taking the third. It was only the third time in the <a href="/wiki/2012_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2012 Formula One World Championship">2012</a> season that no penalties needed to be applied before the start of the race.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2012_Indian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post_race_controversies">Post race controversies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2012_Indian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Post race controversies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>There was speculation in the media that the chaotic Indian customs regulations might have cost <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> the race. This was because <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> could not get certain crucial auto parts and equipment flown into India because of the complex paperwork associated with Indian customs.<sup id="cite_ref-alonsofrust_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-alonsofrust-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>A significant drop in race day attendance was noticed, as the number of fans attending the race dropped to 65,000 (from 95,000 in the previous year). Formula One president <a href="/wiki/Bernie_Ecclestone" title="Bernie Ecclestone">Bernie Ecclestone</a> stated that he was not worried, and that attendance is always high in the first year and tends to go down in the second.<sup id="cite_ref-attendance_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-attendance-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>There was also some discontent between Ferrari technical director <a href="/wiki/Pat_Fry" title="Pat Fry">Pat Fry</a> and Alonso, when Fry stated in Twitter that "In order to be where we wanted and where we were capable of being, we needed to be perfect today and we weren't", after Alonso qualified fifth behind the <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bulls</a> and the <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLarens</a>. Alonso apparently took this as a jibe against his driving skills, and the Ferrari management had to prevent him from responding to it publicly.<sup id="cite_ref-alonsofrust_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-alonsofrust-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
