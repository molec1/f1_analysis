<p>The <b>2008 Brazilian Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>XXXVII Grande Prêmio do Brasil</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 2 November 2008 at the <a href="/wiki/Aut%C3%B3dromo_Jos%C3%A9_Carlos_Pace" title="Autódromo José Carlos Pace">Autódromo José Carlos Pace</a>, <a href="/wiki/Interlagos" title="Interlagos">Interlagos</a>, in <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo" title="São Paulo">São Paulo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>. It was the eighteenth and final race of the <a href="/wiki/2008_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2008 Formula One season">2008 Formula One season</a>. The 71-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> after starting from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>; this was the last of Massa's 11 wins. <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> finished second in a <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> third in a Ferrari.
</p><p>Massa started the race alongside <a href="/wiki/Toyota_Racing_(Formula_One_team)" title="Toyota Racing (Formula One team)">Toyota</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a>. Massa's teammate Räikkönen began from third next to <a href="/wiki/McLaren_(racing)" class="mw-redirect" title="McLaren (racing)">McLaren</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>. Rain fell minutes before the race, delaying the start, and as the track dried Massa established a lead of several seconds. More rain late in the race made the last few laps treacherous for the drivers, but could not prevent Massa from winning the Grand Prix. <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> of <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso</a> finished in fourth place behind Alonso and Räikkönen. Hamilton passed Toyota's <a href="/wiki/Timo_Glock" title="Timo Glock">Timo Glock</a> in the final corners of the race to finish fifth, securing him the points needed to take the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a>.
</p><p>Hamilton received praise from many in the Formula One community, including former champions <a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a> and <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>. The McLaren driver also received official congratulations from Queen <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_II" title="Elizabeth II">Elizabeth II</a> and <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Prime Minister of the United Kingdom">British prime minister</a> <a href="/wiki/Gordon_Brown" title="Gordon Brown">Gordon Brown</a>. Massa's win and Räikkönen's third place helped Ferrari win the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a>. The Grand Prix was <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a>'s final race; the Scot retired after 246&#160;race starts.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2008_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Heading into the final race of the season, <a href="/wiki/McLaren_(racing)" class="mw-redirect" title="McLaren (racing)">McLaren</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> was leading the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a> with 94&#160;<a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Championship_points_scoring_systems" title="List of Formula One World Championship points scoring systems">points</a>; <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> was second with 87&#160;points, seven points behind Hamilton. A maximum of ten points were available for the final race, which meant that Massa could still win the title if Hamilton finished in sixth place or lower. Otherwise, Hamilton would be champion. In the event of a points tie, Massa would win the championship on a count-back, having more wins.<sup id="cite_ref-pointspermutations_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pointspermutations-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-sporting_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sporting-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Behind Hamilton and Massa in the Drivers' Championship, <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a> was third with 75&#160;points in a <a href="/wiki/BMW_Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="BMW Sauber">BMW</a>, and Massa's Ferrari teammate <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> was fourth with 69&#160;points.<sup id="cite_ref-pointsFIA_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pointsFIA-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a>, Ferrari were leading with 156&#160;points and McLaren–<a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_HighPerformanceEngines" class="mw-redirect" title="Mercedes-Benz HighPerformanceEngines">Mercedes</a> were second with 145&#160;points, 11&#160;points behind, with a maximum of 18&#160;points available. If the two Ferrari drivers finished in the top six, the team would secure the Constructors' Championship, even if the McLaren drivers were to finish as the top two.<sup id="cite_ref-pointspermutations_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pointspermutations-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-pointsFIA_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pointsFIA-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Prior to this race, Hamilton was criticised by many pundits for not maintaining his composure at the <a href="/wiki/2008_Japanese_Grand_Prix" title="2008 Japanese Grand Prix">2008 Japanese Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ejordon_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ejordon-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-guardiancrit_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guardiancrit-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/The_Times" title="The Times">The Times</a></i> columnist Edward Gorman said that Hamilton should win the championship, but:
</p>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r960796168">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>Alternatively Hamilton may suffer another one of his rushes of blood to the head and do something utterly unnecessary at Interlagos, just as he did in Japan eight days ago and in <a href="/wiki/2007_Brazilian_Grand_Prix" title="2007 Brazilian Grand Prix">Brazil last year</a>, and throw it all away ... Suddenly defending even a seven-point lead sounds a tall order.<sup id="cite_ref-EdwardGormancritic_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EdwardGormancritic-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>

<p>Former Formula One team owner <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Jordan" title="Eddie Jordan">Eddie Jordan</a> created controversy when he said that "if Massa tries to take him out as he did in Japan in order to steal the title then Lewis has to be ready for it,"<sup id="cite_ref-ejordon_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ejordon-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> adding "If he tries that on then Lewis has to turn his wheel into Massa to ensure he does not finish the race either&#160;– he has to take his wheel off."<sup id="cite_ref-ejordon_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ejordon-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Both Hamilton and Massa rejected the comments; Massa said "Playing dirty has never been part of my game. I don't want anything to do with it. The only thing on my mind is winning the race."<sup id="cite_ref-guardiancrit_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guardiancrit-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The weekend marked <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a>'s final race. Coulthard's <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_RB4" title="Red Bull RB4">Red Bull RB4</a> was decorated in the colours of "Wings for Life", a <a href="/wiki/Charitable_organization" title="Charitable organization">charity</a> dedicated to raising awareness of <a href="/wiki/Spinal_cord_injury" title="Spinal cord injury">spinal cord injuries</a>. Coulthard said "I'm dedicating my last race to the vision of making <a href="/wiki/Paraplegia" title="Paraplegia">paraplegia</a> curable".<sup id="cite_ref-coulthardlast_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-coulthardlast-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a> received approval from the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile</a>, Formula One's <a href="/wiki/Sport_governing_body" class="mw-redirect" title="Sport governing body">governing body</a>, to run Coulthard's car in different colours than his teammate <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-coulthardlast_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-coulthardlast-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> This was also the last Formula One race broadcast by <a href="/wiki/ITV_(TV_network)" title="ITV (TV network)">ITV</a> in the United Kingdom and <a href="/wiki/Telecinco" title="Telecinco">Telecinco</a> in Spain; the rights went to the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> and <a href="/wiki/La_Sexta" class="mw-redirect" title="La Sexta">La Sexta</a>, respectively, for 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>  Also, this was the last race for the <a href="/wiki/Honda_Racing_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Honda Racing F1">Honda</a> team before they announced its withdrawal from Formula One due to the <a href="/wiki/Financial_crisis_of_2007%E2%80%932010" class="mw-redirect" title="Financial crisis of 2007–2010">global economic crisis</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice">Practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2008_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Trulli_Brazil_2008.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fd/Trulli_Brazil_2008.jpg/170px-Trulli_Brazil_2008.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="255" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fd/Trulli_Brazil_2008.jpg/255px-Trulli_Brazil_2008.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fd/Trulli_Brazil_2008.jpg/340px-Trulli_Brazil_2008.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1977" data-file-height="2966" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Trulli_Brazil_2008.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> qualified in second for <a href="/wiki/Toyota_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Toyota F1">Toyota</a></div></div></div>
<p>Three practice sessions were held before the race; the first on Friday morning and the second on Friday afternoon. Both sessions lasted 90&#160;minutes. The third session was held on Saturday morning and lasted an hour.<sup id="cite_ref-sporting_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sporting-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The two sessions on Friday were affected by occasionally damp conditions, which made the track moderately slippery.<sup id="cite_ref-prac1_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prac1-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-prac2_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prac2-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Massa was quickest with a time of 1:12.305 in the first session, less than two-tenths of a second faster than Hamilton. Räikkönen was just off Hamilton's pace, followed by Kubica, <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>. Webber was seventh, still within a second of Massa's time.<sup id="cite_ref-prac1_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prac1-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> In the second practice session, Alonso was fastest with a time of 1:12.296, less than six-hundredths of a second quicker than second-placed Massa. <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> took third place, ahead of Räikkönen, Webber and <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a>. Hamilton only managed ninth place, locking his McLaren's wheels and struggling for grip.<sup id="cite_ref-prac2_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prac2-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Kovalainen was only quick enough for fifteenth position. The Saturday morning session was held on a much warmer track, which reached temperatures as high as 36&#160;°C (97&#160;°F).<sup id="cite_ref-prac3f1.com_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prac3f1.com-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Alonso was again quickest, posting a time of 1:12.141, narrowly faster than both McLaren drivers. Massa, Vettel, and <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> rounded out the top six positions. Räikkönen only managed twelfth, failing to improve on his times set early in the session.<sup id="cite_ref-prac3autosport.com_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prac3autosport.com-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2008_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The qualifying session on Saturday afternoon was split into three&#160;parts. The first part ran for 20&#160;minutes and eliminated the cars from qualifying that finished the session 16th or lower. The second part of qualifying lasted 15&#160;minutes and eliminated cars that finished in positions&#160;11 to 15. The final part of qualifying determined the positions from first to tenth, and decided <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>. Cars which competed in the final session of qualifying were not allowed to refuel before the race, and as such carried more fuel than in the previous sessions.<sup id="cite_ref-sporting_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sporting-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatleft" style="width:31%;&#32;;"><style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r887775652">.mw-parser-output .quotebox{background-color:#F9F9F9;border:1px solid #aaa;box-sizing:border-box;padding:10px;font-size:88%;max-width:100%}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft{margin:0.5em 1.4em 0.8em 0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright{margin:0.5em 0 0.8em 1.4em}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.centered{margin:0.5em auto 0.8em auto}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft p,.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright p{font-style:inherit}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-title{background-color:#F9F9F9;text-align:center;font-size:larger;font-weight:bold}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:before{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" “ ";vertical-align:-45%;line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:after{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" ” ";line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .left-aligned{text-align:left}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .right-aligned{text-align:right}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .center-aligned{text-align:center}.mw-parser-output .quotebox cite{display:block;font-style:normal}@media screen and (max-width:360px){.mw-parser-output .quotebox{min-width:100%;margin:0 0 0.8em!important;float:none!important}}</style>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">Tomorrow, I will be focusing on my own race: it will be a tough afternoon but I'm comfortable with the fuel strategy we chose; the guys in front are probably on a different strategy. But we're in a good position to finish in the same place as we are today&#160;– and that's got to be our aim. We don't need to do anything spectacular.</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style=""><a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>, following the third qualifying session.<sup id="cite_ref-qualquotes_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualquotes-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>Massa clinched his sixth <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> for the season, and his third consecutive pole at Interlagos, with a time of 1:12.368. He was joined on the front row of the grid by Trulli, in his best qualifying performance of the season.<sup id="cite_ref-qualautosport.com_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualautosport.com-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-qualf1.com_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualf1.com-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Räikkönen qualified third, though he was happy with beginning the race on the <a href="/wiki/Racing_line" title="Racing line">racing line</a> behind his teammate.<sup id="cite_ref-qualquotes_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualquotes-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton qualified fourth, half a second behind Massa, having battled both Ferrari drivers for time during the first two qualifying sessions. Hamilton's slow pace in the final qualifying session compared to the first two suggested he was carrying more fuel than his title challengers.<sup id="cite_ref-qualf1.com_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualf1.com-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton's teammate Kovalainen qualified fifth. Alonso, Vettel, Heidfeld, <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9bastien_Bourdais" title="Sébastien Bourdais">Sébastien Bourdais</a> and <a href="/wiki/Timo_Glock" title="Timo Glock">Timo Glock</a> rounded out the top ten. Kubica only managed 13th, having struggled with overall grip for much of the day.<sup id="cite_ref-quobrabmwq_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-quobrabmwq-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard, in his final Formula One race, qualified 14th; <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a>, in 15th, was quicker than <a href="/wiki/Honda_Racing_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Honda Racing F1">Honda</a> teammate <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> in 17th. The Williams and <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a> drivers qualified at the back of the grid, covering positions 16 to 20 with Button.<sup id="cite_ref-qualautosport.com_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualautosport.com-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2008_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Felipe_Massa_2008_Brazilian_Grand_Prix.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/10/Felipe_Massa_2008_Brazilian_Grand_Prix.jpg/220px-Felipe_Massa_2008_Brazilian_Grand_Prix.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/10/Felipe_Massa_2008_Brazilian_Grand_Prix.jpg/330px-Felipe_Massa_2008_Brazilian_Grand_Prix.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/10/Felipe_Massa_2008_Brazilian_Grand_Prix.jpg/440px-Felipe_Massa_2008_Brazilian_Grand_Prix.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3182" data-file-height="2122" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Felipe_Massa_2008_Brazilian_Grand_Prix.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Grand Prix winner <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>The conditions on the grid were damp before the race, the <a href="/wiki/Air_temperature" class="mw-redirect" title="Air temperature">air temperature</a> at 28&#160;°C (82&#160;°F);<sup id="cite_ref-commautosport_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-commautosport-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> rain or thunderstorms were expected.<sup id="cite_ref-weatherf1.com_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-weatherf1.com-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> The race was due to begin at 15:00&#160;<a href="/wiki/Time_in_Brazil" title="Time in Brazil">local time</a> (<a href="/wiki/Coordinated_Universal_Time" title="Coordinated Universal Time">UTC</a>-2), but was delayed by ten minutes when heavy rain hit the track at 14:56.<sup id="cite_ref-commautosport_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-commautosport-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Every team but one changed the tyres on both their cars from <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_tyres" title="Formula One tyres">dry-weather tyres</a> to <a href="/wiki/Rain_tyre#Intermediate_tyres" title="Rain tyre">intermediate tyres</a>. Kubica's car was the exception, remaining on the dry set-up.<sup id="cite_ref-commautosport_22-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-commautosport-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-racebrabmwq_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-racebrabmwq-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Following the formation lap, Kubica returned to the pit lane, his team changing the car's tyres to intermediates.<sup id="cite_ref-fiaracefacts_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiaracefacts-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> This meant the <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Polish</a> driver would start the race from the pit-lane.
</p><p>Massa retained his pole position lead into the first corner, followed by Trulli, Räikkönen, Hamilton and Kovalainen. Coulthard was hit from behind by <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> into turn two, spinning him around. The <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a> car then collided with Rosberg's Williams teammate <a href="/wiki/Kazuki_Nakajima" title="Kazuki Nakajima">Kazuki Nakajima</a>. This damaged the suspension and forced Coulthard to retire in his final race.<sup id="cite_ref-racebrarbq_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-racebrarbq-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-racebrarwil_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-racebrarwil-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-coulthardf1.com_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-coulthardf1.com-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Piquet spun off at the next corner, his car hitting the barriers. Kovalainen was passed by Alonso and Vettel mid-lap, dropping him to seventh.<sup id="cite_ref-commautosport_22-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-commautosport-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> The accidents of Coulthard and Piquet prompted the deployment of the <a href="/wiki/Safety_car#Formula_One" title="Safety car">safety car</a> at the end of the first lap. The track conditions began to dry early on; Force India's <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> was the first driver to stop for dry-weather tyres, <a href="/wiki/Pit_stop#Formula_One" title="Pit stop">pitting</a> at the end of lap two. He remained in 18th&#160;position.<sup id="cite_ref-bralapchart_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bralapchart-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-f1byteambra_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1byteambra-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Racing resumed on lap five when the safety car pulled into the pit-lane.<sup id="cite_ref-fiaracefacts_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiaracefacts-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Rosberg and Button both made pit stops on lap seven, each changing to dry-weather tyres. Bourdais, Glock, <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a> and Nakajima followed a lap later. By lap&#160;11, the rest of the field had changed to dry-weather tyres.<sup id="cite_ref-commautosport_22-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-commautosport-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-brazilpitsummary_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-brazilpitsummary-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Fisichella benefited from pitting the earliest for dry tyres, moving up the order to a high of fifth position.<sup id="cite_ref-bralapchart_29-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bralapchart-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Kubica_Brazil_2008_pits.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/18/Kubica_Brazil_2008_pits.jpg/170px-Kubica_Brazil_2008_pits.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="255" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/18/Kubica_Brazil_2008_pits.jpg/255px-Kubica_Brazil_2008_pits.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/18/Kubica_Brazil_2008_pits.jpg/340px-Kubica_Brazil_2008_pits.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1966" data-file-height="2949" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Kubica_Brazil_2008_pits.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>By finishing the race in eleventh place, <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a> ceded third place in the championship to Räikkönen</div></div></div>
<p>Nakajima spun on lap&#160;13, losing five seconds on the lap.<sup id="cite_ref-commautosport_22-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-commautosport-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> On lap&#160;15, Massa set a new fastest lap of 1:16.888, and extended his lead over Vettel.<sup id="cite_ref-commautosport_22-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-commautosport-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton remained behind Fisichella, and though his McLaren car appeared quicker, he was unable to pass the Force India driver until lap&#160;18.<sup id="cite_ref-commautosport_22-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-commautosport-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Glock passed Fisichella two laps later. Trulli and Bourdais collided on lap&#160;20 into turn one, sending the Toro Rosso driver across the grass. Bourdais lost six places and rejoined in 13th&#160;place.<sup id="cite_ref-fiaracefacts_25-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiaracefacts-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-racebratrq_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-racebratrq-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Trulli's Toyota teammate, Timo Glock, passed Fisichella later on in the same lap for sixth. Massa and Sebastian Vettel traded fastest lap times; Vettel's 1:14.214 on lap&#160;25 was surpassed by Massa's 1:14.161 a lap later.<sup id="cite_ref-fiaracefacts_25-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiaracefacts-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-brazilfastlaps_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-brazilfastlaps-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> However, Vettel made a pit stop soon after, having been lighter-fuelled than his Ferrari opponent.<sup id="cite_ref-brazilpitsummary_31-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-brazilpitsummary-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> He rejoined in sixth position, behind Glock.<sup id="cite_ref-bralapchart_29-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bralapchart-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Kovalainen passed Trulli and Fisichella in separate manoeuvres, gaining seventh position.<sup id="cite_ref-commautosport_22-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-commautosport-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> On lap&#160;36 Massa set the fastest lap of the race, a 1:13.736.<sup id="cite_ref-brazilfastlaps_33-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-brazilfastlaps-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Timo Glock was fuelled so he could complete the race without stopping again, when he made a pit stop on lap&#160;36.<sup id="cite_ref-commautosport_22-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-commautosport-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Massa was the first of the championship frontrunners to pit, on lap&#160;38; Alonso and Hamilton made pit stops two laps after. When Räikkönen made a pit stop on lap&#160;43, Massa had regained the lead, ahead of Alonso. Räikkönen rejoined ahead of Hamilton in third place.<sup id="cite_ref-bralapchart_29-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bralapchart-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-brazilpitsummary_31-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-brazilpitsummary-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Fisichella's stop was marred by <a href="/wiki/Formula_one_car#Transmission" class="mw-redirect" title="Formula one car">transmission</a> problems, dropping him to 18th&#160;position when he resumed.<sup id="cite_ref-commautosport_22-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-commautosport-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-racebrafiq_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-racebrafiq-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Vettel made a pit stop again on lap&#160;51, his team fuelling him to the finish. He rejoined in fifth.<sup id="cite_ref-commautosport_22-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-commautosport-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> By lap&#160;54, Massa had extended his already comfortable lead over Alonso to 9.6&#160;seconds. Vettel was closing in quickly on Hamilton, the McLaren driver needing to finish no lower than fifth to win the championship.<sup id="cite_ref-commautosport_22-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-commautosport-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatright" style="width:37%;&#32;;"><link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r887775652"/>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">ALLEN: [Massa] has done everything he needed to do and we wait now to find out who will be the World Champion of 2008. Can Hamilton do anything? Can he run it up the inside of Vettel? Only a few corners to go now, and desperation starts to creep into Lewis Hamilton.<br />
<p>BRUNDLE: Raikkonen's third and... is that Glock? Is that Glock going slowly? It is! That's Glock!<br />
</p>
ALLEN: Oh my goodness me, Hamilton's back in position again! A hundred thousand local hearts sink in the grandstand. It's handed the place back to Hamilton. He comes through, and if I'm absolutely right, I'm sure, he's going to claim fifth place, which is all he needs to do to become the 2008 Formula One World Champion.</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style=""><a href="/wiki/James_Allen_(journalist)" title="James Allen (journalist)">James Allen</a>'s and <a href="/wiki/Martin_Brundle" title="Martin Brundle">Martin Brundle</a>'s commentary of the final few corners.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>Light rain began to fall on lap&#160;63.<sup id="cite_ref-fiaracefacts_25-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiaracefacts-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Heidfeld made a pit stop and his BMW <a href="/wiki/Pit_stop#F1_pit_crew_roles" title="Pit stop">pit crew</a> changed his tyres to intermediates, echoing their strategy at the <a href="/wiki/2008_Belgian_Grand_Prix" title="2008 Belgian Grand Prix">Belgian Grand Prix</a> which had granted the German a podium position. Kovalainen made a pit stop on lap&#160;65; Alonso and Räikkönen made a pit stop a lap later. Hamilton and Vettel halted their battle for fourth position when they came into the pit lane to change onto intermediate tyres on lap&#160;66.<sup id="cite_ref-commautosport_22-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-commautosport-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-brazilpitsummary_31-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-brazilpitsummary-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Glock chose to remain on his dry-weather tyres and rose from seventh to fourth place as those ahead of him made pit stops.<sup id="cite_ref-glockprobautosport.com_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-glockprobautosport.com-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Massa made a pit stop on lap&#160;67, meaning that all of the frontrunners, with the exception of Glock, were now on intermediate tyres. The rain began to fall heavily on lap&#160;69, as Hamilton ran wide, which allowed Vettel to take fifth position. As Massa crossed the finish line to win the race, Hamilton battled Vettel for the crucial point needed to win the championship. However, Vettel and Hamilton passed Glock in the final corners, the Toyota driver struggling for grip as his dry-weather tyres slid on the wet track.<sup id="cite_ref-commautosport_22-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-commautosport-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-glockprobautosport.com_36-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-glockprobautosport.com-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Premature joy in the Ferrari garage soon turned to disappointment as Hamilton finished the race in fifth position,<sup id="cite_ref-commautosport_22-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-commautosport-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> clinching the championship by a single point and becoming Formula One's <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions#Youngest_Drivers&#39;_Champion" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">youngest championship winner</a> until Vettel in 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-f1byteambra_30-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1byteambra-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Räikkönen's third-place finish behind Alonso secured Ferrari the constructors' title. After the race, Button's car caught fire.<sup id="cite_ref-f1byteambra_30-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1byteambra-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div style="clear:both;"></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2008_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatright" style="width:37%;&#32;;"><link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r887775652"/>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">Before it started to rain I was quite comfortable, and I was just focused on having a clean race. Then it started to drizzle and I didn't want to take any risks&#160;– but Sebastian [Vettel] got past me and I was told that I had to get back in front of him. I couldn't believe it. Then at the very last corner I managed to get past Timo [Glock]&#160;– it was just amazing. This was one of the toughest races of my life, if not the toughest. I was shouting, 'Do I have it? Do I have it?' on the radio. It was only when I took the chequered flag and got to turn one that the team told me I was world champion. I was ecstatic.</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style="">Hamilton, speaking after the race about the final lap.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Hamilton_Brazil_2008_celebrations.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8b/Hamilton_Brazil_2008_celebrations.jpg/220px-Hamilton_Brazil_2008_celebrations.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8b/Hamilton_Brazil_2008_celebrations.jpg/330px-Hamilton_Brazil_2008_celebrations.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8b/Hamilton_Brazil_2008_celebrations.jpg/440px-Hamilton_Brazil_2008_celebrations.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3086" data-file-height="2057" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Hamilton_Brazil_2008_celebrations.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Hamilton celebrating with his team after the race</div></div></div>
<p>The top three finishers appeared on the <a href="/wiki/Podium#Usage_in_motorsport" title="Podium">podium</a> and in the subsequent <a href="/wiki/Press_conference" title="Press conference">press conference</a>. Massa said that he had "almost done everything perfectly", and expressed his disappointment that despite winning the race he had not won the championship.<sup id="cite_ref-pressconference_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconference-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> However, he gave his congratulations to Hamilton for his title win:
</p>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r960796168"/><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>We need to congratulate Lewis because he did a great championship and he scored more points than us, so he deserves to be champion. I know how to lose and I know how to win and as I said before it is another day of my life from which I am going to learn a lot.<sup id="cite_ref-pressconference_39-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconference-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>
<p>Massa's Ferrari teammate Kimi Räikkönen expressed disappointment in the outcome of the Drivers' Championship, but acknowledged the support of his team, saying "we won at least the team championship".<sup id="cite_ref-pressconference_39-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconference-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> Ferrari president <a href="/wiki/Luca_di_Montezemolo" class="mw-redirect" title="Luca di Montezemolo">Luca di Montezemolo</a> was reportedly so angry with the result, however, that he destroyed the television he was viewing the race on.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> McLaren boss <a href="/wiki/Ron_Dennis" title="Ron Dennis">Ron Dennis</a> praised Hamilton, saying "He just keeps delivering and, at the end of the day, he's just two years into his career. So there's a long way to go."<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Hamilton received official congratulations from Queen <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_II" title="Elizabeth II">Elizabeth II</a>, following similar plaudits from the <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Prime Minister of the United Kingdom">Prime Minister of the United Kingdom</a> <a href="/wiki/Gordon_Brown" title="Gordon Brown">Gordon Brown</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Leader_of_the_Opposition_(United_Kingdom)" title="Leader of the Opposition (United Kingdom)">Leader of the Opposition</a> <a href="/wiki/David_Cameron" title="David Cameron">David Cameron</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> Former Formula One champions also congratulated Hamilton; <a href="/wiki/1996_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="1996 Formula One season">1996 champion</a> <a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a> called the McLaren driver "one of the greatest drivers we have had in this country".<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> Multiple champion <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> praised both Hamilton and Massa, saying the Ferrari driver's performance indicated his winning abilities.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> Massa was praised for his <a href="/wiki/Sportsmanship" title="Sportsmanship">sportsmanship</a> after the race;<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Joe_Saward" title="Joe Saward">Joe Saward</a> of GrandPrix.com said "He took defeat with a grace and a style that one rarely sees in modern sport."<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Massa_Brazil_2008_Podium.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/be/Massa_Brazil_2008_Podium.jpg/170px-Massa_Brazil_2008_Podium.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="255" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/be/Massa_Brazil_2008_Podium.jpg/255px-Massa_Brazil_2008_Podium.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/be/Massa_Brazil_2008_Podium.jpg/340px-Massa_Brazil_2008_Podium.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2024" data-file-height="3035" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Massa_Brazil_2008_Podium.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Massa on the podium after the race</div></div></div>
<p>However, Eddie Jordan said that Hamilton "didn't really give himself the best chance of winning the championship&#160;– and was very lucky".<sup id="cite_ref-ejunhappy_48-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ejunhappy-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> The former team boss called McLaren's strategy "a disaster".<sup id="cite_ref-ejunhappy_48-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ejunhappy-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> GrandPrix.com expressed disbelief in the outcome: "It was a showdown so improbable that even Hollywood would not have made a film of it. The scriptwriters would have been laughed out of the studios."<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Autosport" title="Autosport">Autosport</a> magazine writer Adam Cooper called the race "epic".<sup id="cite_ref-adamcooper_50-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-adamcooper-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> After considering other Formula One title finales, Cooper concluded "nothing has ever matched what we saw [in Brazil]".<sup id="cite_ref-adamcooper_50-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-adamcooper-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Timo Glock remained certain the decision to stay on dry-weather tyres, when other teams were pitting for wet-weather tyres, was a correct one: "We were running seventh before the rain came and we would have probably finished there if it had been totally dry. Instead we finished sixth so that shows the strategy was the right one."<sup id="cite_ref-timoq&amp;a_51-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-timoq&amp;a-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> Glock added that the conditions were so poor "I didn't even know that Lewis had overtaken me until after the race".<sup id="cite_ref-timoq&amp;a_51-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-timoq&amp;a-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Kubica's finish in 11th&#160;position meant that he lost third place in the championship to Räikkönen. After the race, Kubica said "We made too many mistakes during the weekend and this is the result."<sup id="cite_ref-racebrabmwq_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-racebrabmwq-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> His team said that they had received the wrong information about track conditions at the start of the race, which led them to keep the Polish driver's car on dry-weather tyres when the rest of the field had changed to intermediates.<sup id="cite_ref-racebrabmwq_24-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-racebrabmwq-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>David Coulthard expressed his disappointment in his early exit from his final Formula One race, saying that "I'm pretty gutted, it's not how I wanted to end my career." The Scottish driver said that he had planned to perform <a href="/wiki/Doughnut_(driving)" title="Doughnut (driving)">doughnuts</a> for the crowd, a celebration discouraged in Formula One.<sup id="cite_ref-coulthardf1.com_28-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-coulthardf1.com-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard left Formula One after 15&#160;years with 246&#160;race starts and 13&#160;wins. Red Bull team principal <a href="/wiki/Christian_Horner" title="Christian Horner">Christian Horner</a> said "It's a great shame for David to be eliminated from his last Grand Prix at the first corner, but he can look back on a long and illustrious career where he's achieved a great deal."<sup id="cite_ref-coulthardf1.com_28-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-coulthardf1.com-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard continued to work for Red Bull Racing in <a href="/wiki/2009_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2009 Formula One season">2009</a> as a testing and development consultant.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
