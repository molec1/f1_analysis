<p>The <b>2003 British Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>LVI Foster's British Grand Prix</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 20 July 2003 at the <a href="/wiki/Silverstone_Circuit" title="Silverstone Circuit">Silverstone Circuit</a>, <a href="/wiki/Silverstone" title="Silverstone">Silverstone</a>, Northamptonshire, England. It was the eleventh round of the <a href="/wiki/2003_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2003 Formula One season">2003 Formula One season</a>. The 60-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> driving for <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> after starting from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>. <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a> finished second in a <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> car, and <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> third driving for <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a>, driving for <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>, started alongside Barrichello on the front row and led the first eleven laps of the race, until a track invasion by a later-to-be-<a href="/wiki/Defrocking" title="Defrocking">defrocked</a> priest, who ran along Hangar straight, running opposite to the 280&#160;km/h train of cars, wearing a saffron kilt and waving religious banners.<sup id="cite_ref-BBC1_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> As a result, the vast majority of cars pitted under safety car conditions, which led to the <a href="/wiki/Toyota_Racing_(Formula_One_team)" title="Toyota Racing (Formula One team)">Toyota</a> drivers <a href="/wiki/Cristiano_da_Matta" title="Cristiano da Matta">Cristiano da Matta</a> and <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Panis" title="Olivier Panis">Olivier Panis</a>, who had elected not to pit, leading the field until Räikkönen assumed the lead on lap 30 when Da Matta pitted.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_report">Race report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2003_British_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Race report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The race began with <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> on pole, alongside <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a>. <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> of <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Mercedes_AMG_High_Performance_Powertrains" title="Mercedes AMG High Performance Powertrains">Mercedes</a> started from third, while world champion and championship leader <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> started from fifth. Barrichello made a poor start, allowing both Trulli and Räikkönen past on an incident-free first lap. Ralf and Michael Schumacher retained their starting positions of fourth and fifth. On the sixth lap, the headrest of <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> dislodged while traversing the first corner (Copse), forcing him to <a href="/wiki/Pit_stop" title="Pit stop">pit</a> for a replacement under safety regulations, and causing a <a href="/wiki/Safety_car" title="Safety car">safety car</a> period to allow marshals to clear the track. Upon the resumption of <a href="/wiki/Racing_flags" title="Racing flags">green flag</a> racing, Barrichello closed the gap to Räikkönen before passing him on lap 11. On the following lap, a man invaded the circuit and another safety car period was necessitated. As it was close to the period when the drivers would be making their scheduled pit stops, the vast majority of cars decided to pit under the safety car. The second placed cars from the respective teams were forced to queue up in the pit lane waiting for service, causing them to drop many places. Michael Schumacher, <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> and <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a> were all outside of the top ten. Of the leading contenders, Trulli was in fourth place while both Räikkönen and Ralf Schumacher had jumped Barrichello when in the pits.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Toyota_Racing_(Formula_One_team)" title="Toyota Racing (Formula One team)">Toyotas</a> of <a href="/wiki/Cristiano_da_Matta" title="Cristiano da Matta">Cristiano da Matta</a> and <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Panis" title="Olivier Panis">Olivier Panis</a>&#160;– who had opted not to pit&#160;– were leading, while Coulthard was in third, having not required a pit stop after his earlier unscheduled headrest replacement. Räikkönen passed Trulli immediately after the restart before clearing team-mate Coulthard on the same lap. Barrichello then passed a slowing Ralf Schumacher on the 17th lap while Räikkönen also passed Panis before chasing down the leading da Matta. Ralf Schumacher was forced to pit after encountering difficulties, while at the same time Michael Schumacher was unable to pass Alonso. By the 26th lap Barrichello was still trying to pass Trulli, and the two leaders continued to extend their lead. Barrichello and Montoya eventually passed Trulli by the end of the 27th lap, before Panis fell victim to both on the 29th. Da Matta eventually ceded the lead after pitting on the 30th lap to Räikkönen. Barrichello then set the fastest lap after being cleared of traffic, taking the lead after Räikkönen pitted for the second time. Barrichello continued to cut the advantage, but Räikkönen regained the lead with a reduced margin following the Brazilian's second stop. After closing in, Barrichello passed Räikkönen after pressuring him into a mistake. Michael Schumacher eventually passed Trulli on the 46th lap, but an unforced error by Räikkönen allowed Montoya to seize second position. In the closing phase of the race, Coulthard passed both da Matta and Trulli to earn fifth place.<sup id="cite_ref-BBC2_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC2-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-BBC3_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC3-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Track_invasion">Track invasion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2003_British_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Track invasion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Silverstone_horan.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/6/67/Silverstone_horan.jpg/220px-Silverstone_horan.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="166" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/6/67/Silverstone_horan.jpg 1.5x" data-file-width="330" data-file-height="249" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Silverstone_horan.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Horan runs onto the track into the path of <a href="/wiki/Ant%C3%B4nio_Pizzonia" title="Antônio Pizzonia">Antônio Pizzonia</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Jaguar_R4" title="Jaguar R4">Jaguar R4</a>. Caption on this television screenshot indicates deployment of safety car as a result.</div></div></div>
<p>On the 11th lap, as the procession of cars exited the Becketts corner onto the Hangar straight, <a href="/wiki/Neil_Horan" title="Neil Horan">Neil Horan</a> cleared the fence wearing a kilt, waving banners with statements "Read the bible" and "The Bible is always right"<sup id="cite_ref-NewSeekers_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NewSeekers-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and ran towards the sequence of cars, forcing several cars to swerve to avoid him. He eventually returned to the grass runoff area at the side of the track after the cars had passed for the lap, and was stopped by a track marshal.
</p><p>He was later charged with aggravated trespass and pleaded guilty in a <a href="/wiki/Northampton" title="Northampton">Northampton</a> court,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> stating that he took the open gate as a sign from God, although the prosecution contended that his act was premeditated as he had already prepared the banners prior to attending the Grand Prix. He was later jailed for two months.<sup id="cite_ref-NewSeekers_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NewSeekers-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The incident prompted comparisons to the events at the <a href="/wiki/1977_South_African_Grand_Prix" title="1977 South African Grand Prix">1977 South African Grand Prix</a>, where volunteer track marshal, Frederick Jansen van Vuuren, ran across the main straight to aid a car and was hit at 170&#160;mph by <a href="/wiki/Tom_Pryce" title="Tom Pryce">Tom Pryce</a>, who couldn't see him until it was too late because of the steep crest on the straight. Both Van Vuuren and Pryce were killed by the impact. A similar incident occurred at the <a href="/wiki/2000_German_Grand_Prix" title="2000 German Grand Prix">2000 German Grand Prix</a> when a disgruntled ex-Mercedes employee walked along part of the circuit in protest before being arrested. This caused a safety car, which eliminated the lead of <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a>, driving for McLaren Mercedes. Unlike the German protester, Horan ran directly down the middle of the track, and intentionally towards oncoming cars and lurching towards some of them.
</p><p>The race led to fears that <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> bosses <a href="/wiki/Max_Mosley" title="Max Mosley">Max Mosley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bernie_Ecclestone" title="Bernie Ecclestone">Bernie Ecclestone</a>, who had been highly critical of the media and corporate facilities of Silverstone, would use the incident to drop the race from the Formula One calendar, with Ecclestone saying "It wasn't necessary – the race was exciting enough without it. But the security wasn't good enough". However, drivers and team officials defended the circuit, with Montoya stating "This was one of the best races of the year, even with the spectator. It was so much fun today," and <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a> boss <a href="/wiki/Peter_Sauber" title="Peter Sauber">Peter Sauber</a> stated "When a man sets himself on fire in the street in Paris, no one blames Paris", while <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Mercedes_AMG_High_Performance_Powertrains" title="Mercedes AMG High Performance Powertrains">Mercedes</a> boss <a href="/wiki/Ron_Dennis" title="Ron Dennis">Ron Dennis</a> said "There is no way you can prevent it happening".<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Stephen Green, the marshal who handled Horan, was later awarded the BARC Browning Medal for "outstanding bravery in tackling a track invader during the 2003 British Grand Prix at Silverstone", the second recipient after <a href="/wiki/David_Purley" title="David Purley">David Purley</a> 31 years previously.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
