<p>The <b>1995 Japanese Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>XXI Fuji Television Japanese Grand Prix</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at the <a href="/wiki/Suzuka_Circuit" class="mw-redirect" title="Suzuka Circuit">Suzuka Circuit</a>, <a href="/wiki/Suzuka,_Mie" title="Suzuka, Mie">Suzuka</a> on 29 October 1995. It was the sixteenth and penultimate race of the <a href="/wiki/1995_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1995 Formula One World Championship">1995 Formula One World Championship</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pg222-223_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg222-223-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The 53-lap race was won from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> by <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/Benetton_B195" title="Benetton B195">Benetton</a>-<a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> second in a <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/10" title="McLaren MP4/10">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Mercedes_AMG_High_Performance_Powertrains" title="Mercedes AMG High Performance Powertrains">Mercedes</a> and Schumacher's teammate <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Herbert" title="Johnny Herbert">Johnny Herbert</a> third.<sup id="cite_ref-formula1.com-officialresult_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-formula1.com-officialresult-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a>, driving for <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, started second, alongside Schumacher. However, Alesi was forced to serve a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_regulations#Penalties" title="Formula One regulations">10-second stop-and-go penalty</a> because his car moved forward before the start. Alesi climbed back up to second, before retiring on lap&#160;25. Schumacher's rival in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a>, <a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a>, started fourth amidst pressure from the British media after poor performances at previous races. Hill moved up to second because of Alesi's retirement, but spun off the track on lap&#160;40.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix-japan_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-japan-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Schumacher's win was his ninth of the season, matching the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_driver_records#Most_wins_in_a_season" title="List of Formula One driver records">record</a> set in <a href="/wiki/1992_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1992 Formula One World Championship">1992</a> by <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-nyt_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nyt-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Benetton was confirmed <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Champions</a> as <a href="/wiki/Williams_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams F1">Williams</a> could not pass its points total in the one remaining race.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix-japan_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-japan-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1995_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1995_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Heading into the penultimate race of the season, <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> had already won the season's <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a>, having clinched the title at the previous race, the <a href="/wiki/1995_Pacific_Grand_Prix" title="1995 Pacific Grand Prix">Pacific Grand Prix</a>. Schumacher led the championship with 92&#160;points; <a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a> was second with 59&#160;points. A maximum of 20&#160;points were available for the remaining two races, which meant that Hill could not catch Schumacher. Although the Drivers' Championship was decided, the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a> was not. Benetton were leading on 123&#160;points and <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a> were second with 102&#160;points heading into the 16th&#160;race, with a maximum of 32&#160;points available.<sup id="cite_ref-gp-schumi_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gp-schumi-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In the week leading up to the race, Hill was criticised by the British media after poor performances in previous races; there was continued speculation that Williams were going to replace him with <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a> or <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> for the <a href="/wiki/1996_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="1996 Formula One season">1996 season</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-hill_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hill-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-F1Ypg203_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1Ypg203-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Despite the rumours, Williams team boss <a href="/wiki/Frank_Williams_(Formula_One)" title="Frank Williams (Formula One)">Frank Williams</a> gave Hill "an unequivocal vote of confidence" heading into the race.<sup id="cite_ref-pg218_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg218-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Damon_Hill_juillet_1995.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b6/Damon_Hill_juillet_1995.jpg/170px-Damon_Hill_juillet_1995.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="253" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b6/Damon_Hill_juillet_1995.jpg/255px-Damon_Hill_juillet_1995.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b6/Damon_Hill_juillet_1995.jpg/340px-Damon_Hill_juillet_1995.jpg 2x" data-file-width="341" data-file-height="507" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Damon_Hill_juillet_1995.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a> was criticised by the British media after poor performances</div></div></div>
<p>There were two driver changes heading into the race. Having been in one of the two <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a> cars since the fifth&#160;race of the season at <a href="/wiki/1995_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="1995 Monaco Grand Prix">Monaco</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jean-Christophe_Boullion" title="Jean-Christophe Boullion">Jean-Christophe Boullion</a> was released from the team and replaced by <a href="/wiki/Karl_Wendlinger" title="Karl Wendlinger">Karl Wendlinger</a>. The Austrian was given another chance to prove himself after suffering an accident at the <a href="/wiki/1994_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="1994 Monaco Grand Prix">1994 Monaco Grand Prix</a>, which left him in a coma for weeks.<sup id="cite_ref-gp-wendlinger_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gp-wendlinger-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The second driver change was <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a>'s return to <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> after missing the Pacific Grand Prix because of an operation for <a href="/wiki/Appendicitis" title="Appendicitis">appendicitis</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix-hakkinenappendicitis_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-hakkinenappendicitis-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice_and_qualifying">Practice and qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1995_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Practice and qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Two practice sessions were held before the race; the first was held on Friday morning and the second on Saturday morning. Both sessions lasted 1&#160;hour and 45&#160;minutes with weather conditions dry throughout.<sup id="cite_ref-atlas-japanmain_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlas-japanmain-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher was fastest in the first session, posting a time of 1:40.410, two-tenths of a second quicker than Häkkinen. The Williams and <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> cars occupied the remaining top six positions; Williams drivers Hill and <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> third and fifth respectively. The Ferrari cars were fourth and sixth fastest; <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a> ahead of Berger. Häkkinen lapped faster than Schumacher in the second practice session with a time of 1:40.389. <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a> took second place in the <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a> car, three-tenths of a second behind Häkkinen. Hill was third in the Williams, two-tenths behind Häkkinen, with Schumacher fourth behind Hill. The Ferrari cars were fifth and eighth; Alesi in front of Berger. Frentzen's Sauber and Coulthard's Williams split the Ferrari drivers.<sup id="cite_ref-pg222-223_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg222-223-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Despite both the Williams cars going off into the <a href="/wiki/Run-off_area" title="Run-off area">gravel</a>, Hill and Coulthard made the top&#160;10.<sup id="cite_ref-atlas-japan2ndquali_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlas-japan2ndquali-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

</div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/List_of_motorsport_terminology#Q" class="mw-redirect" title="List of motorsport terminology">qualifying session</a> was split into two one-hour sessions; the first was held on Friday afternoon with the second held on Saturday afternoon. The fastest time from either sessions counted towards their final <a href="/wiki/Standing_start" title="Standing start">grid position</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-atlas-japanmain_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlas-japanmain-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher clinched his tenth career <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>, in his <a href="/wiki/Benetton_B195" title="Benetton B195">Benetton B195</a>, with a time of 1:38.023. He was joined on the front row by Alesi, who was eight-tenths of a second behind.<sup id="cite_ref-pg222-223_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg222-223-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-alesi-careerreview_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-alesi-careerreview-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher was particularly pleased with the performance of his Benetton, saying that "I have rarely had such a good car&#160;... I think I can be confident for the race".<sup id="cite_ref-F1Ypg202_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1Ypg202-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Alesi was satisfied about his performance, but worried about a mechanical problem which had caused him to crash on Friday, accusing the Ferrari team of withholding information from him.<sup id="cite_ref-F1Ypg202_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1Ypg202-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Alesi was scheduled to leave Ferrari for Benetton in a swap with Schumacher at the end of the season, and the relationship between him and the team was becoming increasingly strained.<sup id="cite_ref-F1Ypg54-55_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1Ypg54-55-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Häkkinen was third in the McLaren, with Hill fourth, a second slower than Schumacher. Despite Häkkinen's best qualifying effort of the season alongside his <a href="/wiki/1995_Belgian_Grand_Prix" title="1995 Belgian Grand Prix">Belgium</a> third place,<sup id="cite_ref-formula1.com-hakkinen1995_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-formula1.com-hakkinen1995-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> his teammate, <a href="/wiki/Mark_Blundell" title="Mark Blundell">Mark Blundell</a>, had a disappointing qualifying session. In the first part of qualifying, Blundell crashed into the wall, meaning he could not set a time as his car was too badly damaged. Blundell had his second crash of the weekend at the 130R&#160;corner in Saturday practice, which was more serious than the first. Following medical advice, Blundell did not participate in the second qualifying session. He was unable to set a time, leaving him at the back of the grid.<sup id="cite_ref-pg217_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg217-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Aguri_Suzuki" title="Aguri Suzuki">Aguri Suzuki</a> crashed his <a href="/wiki/Equipe_Ligier" title="Equipe Ligier">Ligier</a> during Saturday qualifying; he was unable to start the race since he was in a hospital with a broken rib.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix-japan_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-japan-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1995_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The track surface was damp for most of the race, which meant that lap times were slower than the previous days' qualifying sessions.<sup id="cite_ref-pg222-223_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg222-223-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The drivers took to the track at 09:30&#160;<a href="/wiki/Japan_Standard_Time" title="Japan Standard Time">JST</a> (<a href="/wiki/Greenwich_Mean_Time" title="Greenwich Mean Time">GMT</a> +9) for a 30-minute warmup session. Despite underperforming in qualifying, both Williams cars performed better in the wet weather warmup session; Hill had the fastest time of 2:00.025. Coulthard was third in the other Williams car; Schumacher split them in second position. Alesi completed the top four, eight-tenths of a second behind Hill.<sup id="cite_ref-pg222-223_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg222-223-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Though 24&#160;cars qualified for the race, only 22 took the start: Suzuki was unable to start because of his crash in qualifying and <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Moreno" title="Roberto Moreno">Roberto Moreno</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Forti" title="Forti">Forti</a> car suffered a gearbox problem.<sup id="cite_ref-pg218_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg218-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-bbc-1_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-1-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>  For the first time since the Japanese Grand Prix was held at Suzuka in <a href="/wiki/1987_Japanese_Grand_Prix" title="1987 Japanese Grand Prix">1987</a>, tickets for the race did not sell out, despite the fact that three Japanese drivers entered the race.<sup id="cite_ref-F1Ypg206_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1Ypg206-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The race started at 14:00&#160;JST. All of the drivers opted to start on <a href="/wiki/Rain_tyre" title="Rain tyre">wet weather tyres</a> as the track was damp from the morning rain.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix-japan_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-japan-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher, from pole position on the grid, held onto the lead into the first corner. Alesi, who started alongside Schumacher, was judged to have jumped the start, and served a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_regulations#Penalties" title="Formula One regulations">10-second stop-and-go penalty</a> on lap&#160;three,<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix-japan_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-japan-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-alesi-careerreview_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-alesi-careerreview-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> from which he returned to the race in tenth place.<sup id="cite_ref-pg222-223_1-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg222-223-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Alesi's teammate Berger also jumped the start and received the same penalty. <a href="/wiki/Gianni_Morbidelli" title="Gianni Morbidelli">Gianni Morbidelli</a>, near the back of the field in one of the <a href="/wiki/Footwork_Arrows" title="Footwork Arrows">Footwork</a> cars, spun at the first corner on lap&#160;one after being hit from behind by Wendlinger's Sauber. Morbidelli <a href="/wiki/Stall_(engine)" title="Stall (engine)">stalled</a> his car in the process, forcing him to retire from the race.<sup id="cite_ref-pg218_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg218-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> On lap&#160;seven, Alesi stopped at the <a href="/wiki/Pit_stop" title="Pit stop">pits</a> to change to dry weather <a href="/wiki/Slick_tire" class="mw-redirect" title="Slick tire">slick tyres</a>, as the track was beginning to dry.<sup id="cite_ref-alesi-careerreview_15-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-alesi-careerreview-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-atlas-f1japanresult_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlas-f1japanresult-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> On returning to the race, he began to make his way through the field constantly recording fastest laps; the first of which was 1:54.416, five&#160;seconds faster than the remainder of the field.<sup id="cite_ref-atlas-f1japanresult_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlas-f1japanresult-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-autosport-alesi_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosport-alesi-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher made a pit stop on lap&#160;10 for slicks, handing the lead to Häkkinen for a lap before he too pitted. Alesi's progress was interrupted when he spun attempting to pass <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Lamy" title="Pedro Lamy">Pedro Lamy</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a> for 15th&#160;place,<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix-japan_3-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-japan-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-alesi-careerreview_15-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-alesi-careerreview-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> but he made his way up to second by lap&#160;10, overtaking Hill around the outside in the final <a href="/wiki/Chicane" title="Chicane">chicane</a> to take the place. Alerted by Alesi's pace on the slick tyres, the other drivers came into the pits to change to slick tyres.<sup id="cite_ref-pg219_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg219-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Michael_Schumacher_1994_San_Marino_Gran_Prix.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2a/Michael_Schumacher_1994_San_Marino_Gran_Prix.jpg/170px-Michael_Schumacher_1994_San_Marino_Gran_Prix.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="259" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/2/2a/Michael_Schumacher_1994_San_Marino_Gran_Prix.jpg 1.5x" data-file-width="200" data-file-height="305" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Michael_Schumacher_1994_San_Marino_Gran_Prix.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>, the <a href="/wiki/1995_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="1995 Formula One season">1995 Formula One Drivers' Champion</a> (pictured in 1994)</div></div></div>
<p>The two Jordan cars collided on lap&#160;15. <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> spun in the final chicane when he attempted to brake later than his teammate Irvine. Barrichello hit a wall, which damaged his car's rear wing and caused him to retire from the race. Irvine was involved in another collision at the chicane on lap&#160;20 when Frentzen hit him from behind. Irvine continued without damage, but Frentzen had to pit for a new front wing.<sup id="cite_ref-pg219_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg219-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> At the front, Alesi was lapping faster than Schumacher, even though Schumacher was on dry tyres.<sup id="cite_ref-atlas-f1japanresult_22-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlas-f1japanresult-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Alesi was only six&#160;seconds behind Schumacher when his <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_412_T2" title="Ferrari 412 T2">Ferrari 412T2</a> suffered an apparent <a href="/wiki/Differential_(mechanical_device)" title="Differential (mechanical device)">differential</a> failure on lap&#160;25.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix-japan_3-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-japan-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-alesi-careerreview_15-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-alesi-careerreview-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> It was later discovered that the problem was a <a href="/wiki/Driveshaft" class="mw-redirect" title="Driveshaft">driveshaft</a> failure, possibly as a result of his earlier spin.<sup id="cite_ref-pg219_24-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg219-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher made a pit stop for a second time on lap&#160;31, returning to the race in second place behind Hill. Schumacher set the fastest lap of the race on lap&#160;33, and regained his lead on the next lap when Hill made his pit stop.<sup id="cite_ref-atlas-f1japanresult_22-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlas-f1japanresult-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Behind them, Häkkinen and Coulthard were third and fourth respectively before their pit stops, but Coulthard pitted six laps later than Häkkinen and returned to the track in third&#160;place, one place ahead of the Finn.<sup id="cite_ref-pg221_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg221-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Herbert" title="Johnny Herbert">Johnny Herbert</a> was fifth in the second Benetton car after the second round of pit stops, with Irvine rounding out the point-scoring places in sixth.<sup id="cite_ref-pg222-223_1-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg222-223-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-bbc-2_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-2-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At this stage, the rain began to fall again, but only at the Spoon Curve end of the track. The Williams drivers were second and third until Hill ran off the track at Spoon Curve two&#160;laps after his pit stop. He damaged his front wing in the process and returned to the track in fourth.<sup id="cite_ref-atlas-f1japanresult_22-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlas-f1japanresult-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Hill returned to the pits to let his pit crew replace the damaged wing. He rejoined fifth, but was then given a ten-second stop-and-go penalty for speeding in the pitlane.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix-japan_3-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-japan-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard made the same mistake as his teammate by running through the gravel trap at the Spoon Curve but looked like he was going to escape with only minor damage. However, as he braked for 130R, the next corner, the gravel which had entered his <a href="/wiki/Ground_effect_in_cars" class="mw-redirect" title="Ground effect in cars">sidepods</a> flew out, causing him to lose control and get his car stuck in the gravel trap.<sup id="cite_ref-atlas-f1japanresult_22-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlas-f1japanresult-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Hill was told by his team on the radio to speed up as he had not yet taken his stop-and-go penalty, but later that lap he spun off at Spoon Curve and retired from the race without having taken the penalty.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix-japan_3-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-japan-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Blundell, Irvine and Frentzen also left the track at Spoon Curve but all finished the race.<sup id="cite_ref-atlas-f1japanresult_22-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlas-f1japanresult-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> With his closest challenger out, Schumacher won the race after 53&#160;laps to secure his ninth victory of the season in a time of 1:36:52.930.<sup id="cite_ref-pg222-223_1-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg222-223-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-suzuka-halloween_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-suzuka-halloween-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> The win, along with Herbert's third place and the retirements of Hill and Coulthard, gave Benetton the 1995 Constructors' Championship.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix-japan_3-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-japan-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Häkkinen finished second in his McLaren, 20&#160;seconds behind Schumacher. Irvine was fourth in his Jordan with <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Panis" title="Olivier Panis">Olivier Panis</a> fifth in his Ligier. <a href="/wiki/Mika_Salo" title="Mika Salo">Mika Salo</a> took sixth&#160;place and the final point in his <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_Racing" title="Tyrrell Racing">Tyrrell</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-suzuka-halloween_27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-suzuka-halloween-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> Despite starting last, Blundell finished in seventh, just 1.6&#160;seconds behind Salo.<sup id="cite_ref-pg221_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg221-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> The delayed Frentzen, <a href="/wiki/Luca_Badoer" title="Luca Badoer">Luca Badoer</a>, Wendlinger, Lamy and <a href="/wiki/Taki_Inoue" title="Taki Inoue">Taki Inoue</a> completed the finishers.<sup id="cite_ref-pg222-223_1-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg222-223-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1995_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatright" style="width:25em;&#32;;"><link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r887775652"/>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">"It's a really great feeling now, because I fulfilled the promises that I made to the team at the beginning of the season to get both titles [Drivers' and Constructors']."</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style="">—<a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>, commenting on <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a>'s 1995 season.<sup id="cite_ref-pg221_25-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg221-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>This was Schumacher's last win for Benetton, as he moved to the Ferrari team for the <a href="/wiki/1996_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="1996 Formula One season">1996 season</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-gp-schumiferrarimove_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gp-schumiferrarimove-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Herbert reiterated Schumacher's opinion by stating that Benetton did "a fantastic job".<sup id="cite_ref-pg221_25-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg221-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Hill was disappointed about the race and the season as a whole; he said afterwards:
</p>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r960796168">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>Just when you think that it couldn't get any worse, it does. There is no easy way out of this, you just have to keep pressing on. The easiest thing to do is to give up, and it would probably be less painful that way, but that is not an option. While we were in the race we were competitive and I was in with a shout, I suppose, all the time I was on the track. But things took a massive turn for the worse, I am afraid. I drove through the rain and the second time I spun off I think it was oil rather than rain. It is not a glorious end to the season but the ingredients are all there and there is no reason why we should not get into the winning habit again.<sup id="cite_ref-pg221_25-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg221-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>
<p>As a result of Hill not taking his 10-second stop-and-go penalty because of his retirement, Williams were fined <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">$</a>10,000 by Formula One's <a href="/wiki/Sport_governing_body" class="mw-redirect" title="Sport governing body">governing body</a>, the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile</a> (FIA).<sup id="cite_ref-pg221_25-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg221-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> In an interview with <i><a href="/wiki/Motor_Sport_(magazine)" title="Motor Sport (magazine)">Motor Sport</a></i> magazine in 2008, Hill said that the 1995 season, as a whole, "went down, mentally, and it all just got to me". He also said he believed that it was in 1995 that Frank Williams and <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Head" title="Patrick Head">Patrick Head</a> decided to replace him for the 1997 season.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/1980_Formula_One_season" title="1980 Formula One season">1980 Formula One World Champion</a> <a href="/wiki/Alan_Jones_(Formula_1)" class="mw-redirect" title="Alan Jones (Formula 1)">Alan Jones</a> praised Alesi's performance, saying that it "will go down as one of the great drives in Grand Prix racing".<sup id="cite_ref-alesi-careerreview_15-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-alesi-careerreview-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Alesi stated that if his driveshaft had not failed, he would "have fought for it, all the way to the end". Alesi added that he believed he did not jump the start, but admitted that "the car crept forwards by a few centimetres" because of the downhill slope of the grid.<sup id="cite_ref-pg219_24-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg219-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> In an interview with <i><a href="/wiki/Autosport" title="Autosport">Autosport</a></i> magazine in 2009, Alesi said that he went to see the race director before the race to see how he could avoid a penalty at the downhill start and that he was "totally fed up" with the penalty decision given.<sup id="cite_ref-autosport-alesi_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosport-alesi-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Berger also questioned his penalty, claiming that his car did not move before the green light went on.<sup id="cite_ref-F1Ypg203_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1Ypg203-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Classification">Classification</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1995_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Classification">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
