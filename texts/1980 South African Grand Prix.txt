<p>The <b>1980 South African Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held on 1 March 1980 at <a href="/wiki/Kyalami" title="Kyalami">Kyalami</a> in <a href="/wiki/Gauteng" title="Gauteng">Gauteng</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a>. It was the third round of the <a href="/wiki/1980_Formula_One_season" title="1980 Formula One season">1980 Formula One season</a>. The race was the twenty-sixth <a href="/wiki/South_African_Grand_Prix" title="South African Grand Prix">South African Grand Prix</a> and the fourteenth to be held at Kyalami. The race was held over 78 laps of the 4.104-kilometre circuit for a total race distance of 320 kilometres.
</p><p>The race was won by French driver <a href="/wiki/Ren%C3%A9_Arnoux" title="René Arnoux">René Arnoux</a> driving a <a href="/wiki/Renault_RE20" title="Renault RE20">Renault RE20</a>. It was Arnoux' second World Championship victory adding to his win at the previous race the <a href="/wiki/1980_Brazilian_Grand_Prix" title="1980 Brazilian Grand Prix">1980 Brazilian Grand Prix</a>. Arnoux won by 34 seconds over fellow French driver <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Laffite" title="Jacques Laffite">Jacques Laffite</a> driving a <a href="/wiki/Ligier_JS11/15" class="mw-redirect" title="Ligier JS11/15">Ligier JS11/15</a>. Laffite's <a href="/wiki/Equipe_Ligier" title="Equipe Ligier">Ligier</a> teammate <a href="/wiki/Didier_Pironi" title="Didier Pironi">Didier Pironi</a> was third, completing an all-French podium. This was the first race since the <a href="/wiki/1968_United_States_Grand_Prix" title="1968 United States Grand Prix">1968 United States Grand Prix</a> to have three drivers from the same country on the podium; all three were also driving French-built cars.
</p><p>The race was additionally notable because of two accidents during qualifying: French driver <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> broke his wrist when he crashed his <a href="/wiki/McLaren_M29" title="McLaren M29">McLaren M29</a> at the Esses after a suspension failure, while Swiss driver <a href="/wiki/Marc_Surer" title="Marc Surer">Marc Surer</a> badly injured his legs when he crashed the new <a href="/wiki/ATS_D4" class="mw-redirect" title="ATS D4">ATS D4</a> at Crowthorne Corner at the end of the straight. Neither started the race, with Prost also missing the following race and Surer the next three races.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> ATS had only just downsized from two entries to one, but with Surer injured the former #2 driver Dutchman <a href="/wiki/Jan_Lammers" title="Jan Lammers">Jan Lammers</a> rejoined the team. The new ATS D4 was too heavily damaged to be repaired and leaving Lammers to try and fail to qualify an <a href="/w/index.php?title=ATS_D3&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="ATS D3 (page does not exist)">ATS D3</a>.
</p><p>In common with the previous race in Brazil, altitude gave the turbo-charged Renaults a dominant edge in speed. Third on the grid, <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a> was almost two seconds behind in qualifying in his <a href="/wiki/Brabham_BT49" title="Brabham BT49">Brabham BT49</a>. <a href="/wiki/Jean-Pierre_Jabouille" title="Jean-Pierre Jabouille">Jean-Pierre Jabouille</a> and Arnoux led for much of the race until Jabouille punctured. The Ligiers climbed into the podium positions as championship leader <a href="/wiki/Alan_Jones_(racing_driver)" title="Alan Jones (racing driver)">Alan Jones</a> retired his <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW07B" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams FW07B">Williams FW07B</a>. Piquet finished fourth ahead of <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Reutemann" title="Carlos Reutemann">Carlos Reutemann</a> in the second Williams. The final point was claimed by <a href="/wiki/Jochen_Mass" title="Jochen Mass">Jochen Mass</a> in his <a href="/wiki/Arrows_A3" title="Arrows A3">Arrows A3</a>.
</p><p>Thirteen cars finished the race although <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Depailler" title="Patrick Depailler">Patrick Depailler</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Alfa_Romeo_179" title="Alfa Romeo 179">Alfa Romeo 179</a> was too far behind to be classified. <a href="/wiki/Geoff_Lees_(racing_driver)" title="Geoff Lees (racing driver)">Geoff Lees</a> was classified 13th as he crashed his <a href="/w/index.php?title=Shadow_DN11&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Shadow DN11 (page does not exist)">Shadow DN11</a> late in the race.
</p><p>Arnoux became the new championship points leader, five points up on Jones and nine ahead of Piquet. Similarly <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a> now led the constructors points over <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a>.
</p>
