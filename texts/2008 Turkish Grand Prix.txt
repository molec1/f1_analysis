<p>The <b>2008 Turkish Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>IV Petrol Ofisi Turkish Grand Prix</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 11 May 2008 at the <a href="/wiki/Istanbul_Park" class="mw-redirect" title="Istanbul Park">Istanbul Racing Circuit</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tuzla,_Istanbul" title="Tuzla, Istanbul">Tuzla</a>, <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>. It was the fifth&#160;race of the <a href="/wiki/2008_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2008 Formula One season">2008 Formula One season</a>. The 58-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> team after starting from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>. <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> finished second in a <a href="/wiki/McLaren_(racing)" class="mw-redirect" title="McLaren (racing)">McLaren</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> third in the second Ferrari.
</p><p>Massa claimed pole, with teammate Räikkönen fourth, the two Ferrari cars sandwiching the McLarens of <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> and Hamilton. At the first corner Räikkönen clipped Kovalainen's rear tyre and gave him a puncture. The safety car was deployed on the first lap, after a collision, but only remained out for one lap. During the course of the race, Hamilton, intending to make one more pit stop than both Ferrari drivers, was faster than Massa due to carrying a lighter fuel load and overtook him on lap&#160;24. After Hamilton had made his third pit stop, he rejoined in second behind Massa but in front of the Championship leader, Räikkönen. Massa won the race, with Hamilton 3.779&#160;seconds behind, and Räikkönen a further half-second behind. The two <a href="/wiki/BMW_Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="BMW Sauber">BMW Sauber</a> cars of <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> took fourth and fifth.
</p><p>In the week running up to the grand prix, the <a href="/wiki/Super_Aguri" class="mw-redirect" title="Super Aguri">Super Aguri</a> team had withdrawn from Formula One, due to financial problems, leaving the sport with only ten teams. Massa's victory was his third consecutive pole position and victory in <a href="/wiki/Turkish_Grand_Prix" title="Turkish Grand Prix">Turkey</a>, having also won the race from pole in <a href="/wiki/2006_Turkish_Grand_Prix" title="2006 Turkish Grand Prix">2006</a> and <a href="/wiki/2007_Turkish_Grand_Prix" title="2007 Turkish Grand Prix">2007</a>. <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> celebrated his 257th&#160;Grand Prix start, breaking <a href="/wiki/Riccardo_Patrese" title="Riccardo Patrese">Riccardo Patrese</a>'s previous record of 256. Due to the race result, Räikkönen's lead in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a> was lowered to seven points. Massa rose to second from fourth, whilst Hamilton dropped to third, both drivers tying on 28&#160;points but separated by Massa's two wins thus far to Hamilton's one. In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a>, Ferrari increased their lead to 22&#160;points ahead of BMW Sauber, with McLaren a further two&#160;points behind in third.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2008_Turkish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Grand Prix was contested by 20&#160;drivers, in ten teams of two. The teams, also known as "<a href="/wiki/Formula_One#Constructors" title="Formula One">constructors</a>", were <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, <a href="/wiki/BMW_Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="BMW Sauber">BMW Sauber</a>, <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a>, <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a>, <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Toyota_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Toyota F1">Toyota</a>, <a href="/wiki/Toro_Rosso" class="mw-redirect" title="Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso</a>, <a href="/wiki/Honda_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Honda F1">Honda</a>, <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a> and <a href="/wiki/McLaren_(racing)" class="mw-redirect" title="McLaren (racing)">McLaren</a>. This was two drivers fewer than in previous rounds of the <a href="/wiki/2008_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2008 Formula One season">2008 season</a>, due to the withdrawal of the <a href="/wiki/Super_Aguri" class="mw-redirect" title="Super Aguri">Super Aguri</a> team from the sport following the previous race.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_Classification_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA_Classification-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Going into the race, <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> was leading the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a>, on 29&#160;<a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Championship_points_scoring_systems" title="List of Formula One World Championship points scoring systems">points</a>; nine&#160;points ahead of <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>, with Räikkönen's teammate, <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a>, in fourth 11&#160;points behind. BMW Sauber drivers <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> were third and fifth, on 19 and 16&#160;points respectively. In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a> Ferrari were leading on 47&#160;points; 12&#160;points ahead of BMW Sauber on 35&#160;points, with McLaren just one further point behind them.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The race was also <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a>'s 257th&#160;Grand Prix, which broke the record for the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_driver_records#Races_entered_and_started" title="List of Formula One driver records">number of Grand Prix starts</a> previously held by <a href="/wiki/Riccardo_Patrese" title="Riccardo Patrese">Riccardo Patrese</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Barrichello_off_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Barrichello_off-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The particular Grand Prix at which he broke this record was disputed, as there was some disagreement about what constituted a "start", such as the 2002 Spanish Grand Prix, where Barrichello entered but did not actually take part in the race on Sunday.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello and Honda decided to make Turkey the site for their celebrations, issuing a statement that said, "In view of the lack of consensus regarding which specific race marks Rubens's 257th Grand Prix, Rubens will celebrate the milestone of 257 Grand Prix appearances at the Turkish Grand Prix".<sup id="cite_ref-Barrichello_off_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Barrichello_off-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> His Honda team gave him a special anniversary livery and helmet, both of which prominently featured the number 257.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Patrese said that he would feel some sadness at having his record broken.<sup id="cite_ref-Patrese_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Patrese-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Aguri_Suzuki_2008_Super_GT.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7d/Aguri_Suzuki_2008_Super_GT.jpg/220px-Aguri_Suzuki_2008_Super_GT.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="276" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7d/Aguri_Suzuki_2008_Super_GT.jpg/330px-Aguri_Suzuki_2008_Super_GT.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7d/Aguri_Suzuki_2008_Super_GT.jpg/440px-Aguri_Suzuki_2008_Super_GT.jpg 2x" data-file-width="800" data-file-height="1003" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Aguri_Suzuki_2008_Super_GT.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Super_Aguri" class="mw-redirect" title="Super Aguri">Super Aguri</a>'s founder, <a href="/wiki/Aguri_Suzuki" title="Aguri Suzuki">Aguri Suzuki</a>. It was announced that the team would withdraw from <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> with immediate effect before the Turkish Grand Prix.</div></div></div>
<p>The Super Aguri team had been in financial trouble since a proposed takeover deal backed by equity firm <a href="/wiki/Dubai_International_Capital" title="Dubai International Capital">Dubai International Capital</a>, under the "Magma Group" banner, failed to take effect.<sup id="cite_ref-superaguri_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-superaguri-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Early in the week before the Grand Prix, after team owner <a href="/wiki/Aguri_Suzuki" title="Aguri Suzuki">Aguri Suzuki</a> revealed the extent of his difficulties, the team's trucks were blocked from entering the paddock by the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile</a> (FIA), Formula One's governing body. This decision was taken by the FIA after being informed by Honda's Formula One team CEO, <a href="/wiki/Nick_Fry" title="Nick Fry">Nick Fry</a>, that Super Aguri, who used Honda engines and Honda-derived chassis, would not be racing at Istanbul Park.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Suzuki then admitted that his financial troubles could not be solved by existing primary sponsor <a href="/wiki/SS_United_Group_Oil_%26_Gas_Company" title="SS United Group Oil &amp; Gas Company">SS United Group Oil &amp; Gas Company</a>, leaving the team with no choice but to withdraw from Formula One as of May 6. Super Aguri had competed in Formula One since the <a href="/wiki/2006_Bahrain_Grand_Prix" title="2006 Bahrain Grand Prix">2006 Bahrain Grand Prix</a>. After scoring its first championship points after 22&#160;race starts at the <a href="/wiki/2007_Spanish_Grand_Prix" title="2007 Spanish Grand Prix">2007 Spanish Grand Prix</a>, the team finished ninth overall in the <a href="/wiki/2007_Formula_One_season#Constructors" class="mw-redirect" title="2007 Formula One season">2007 Constructors' Championship</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Super_Aguri_announce_withdrawal_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Super_Aguri_announce_withdrawal-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Toro Rosso team's plan to introduce its new car, the <a href="/wiki/Toro_Rosso_STR3" title="Toro Rosso STR3">STR3</a>, at this race were postponed due to a lack of spare parts.<sup id="cite_ref-TR_old_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TR_old-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> This was due to an accident suffered by driver Sébastien Bourdais during testing prior to the previous race, which had resulted in the chassis sustaining heavy damage.<sup id="cite_ref-TR_old_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TR_old-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Toro Rosso had already used an updated version of its 2007 car, the <a href="/wiki/Toro_Rosso_STR2" title="Toro Rosso STR2">STR2</a>, for the first four races of the season, and were now forced to race with it in Turkey as well.<sup id="cite_ref-TR_old_14-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TR_old-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> The team's other driver, <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a>, said that he was "disappointed" that the new car was not ready.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> However, this was the last race in which the STR2 competed, as the STR3 subsequently made its début at the <a href="/wiki/2008_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="2008 Monaco Grand Prix">next race</a>.
</p><p>Ferrari decided not to use a new nose for their car that had made its début at the <a href="/wiki/2008_Spanish_Grand_Prix" title="2008 Spanish Grand Prix">previous race</a> due to the low-<a href="/wiki/Downforce" title="Downforce">downforce</a> nature of the Turkish circuit.<sup id="cite_ref-Ferrari_nose_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ferrari_nose-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> According to team manager <a href="/wiki/Luca_Baldisserri" title="Luca Baldisserri">Luca Baldisserri</a>, "The cars used by Kimi [Räikkönen] and Felipe [Massa] are basically to the same specification as those that were used in Spain, with the exception that at Istanbul Park we will not use the slotted nose. This is because this solution is much more efficient at medium to high downforce levels, whereas Turkey requires less downforce. The new nose worked very well, but as we said when we gave it its race debut in Barcelona, we would only use it at circuits where we deem it will bring us an advantage over a more conventional component."<sup id="cite_ref-Ferrari_nose_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ferrari_nose-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>McLaren's <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> was passed fit to race in the days leading up to the race.<sup id="cite_ref-Kov_crash_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kov_crash-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> At the previous race, Kovalainen had suffered a serious crash, although he escaped uninjured apart from minor concussion.<sup id="cite_ref-Kov_crash_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kov_crash-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Kovalainen said that he felt ready to race again: "I arrived here today feeling confident that I would pass the FIA test as I feel 100% and if I had not felt 100% I would have told the team already and stayed at home. My preparation for this weekend has gone well and we have done a lot of fitness training."<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice">Practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2008_Turkish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Three practice sessions were held before the Sunday race&#160;– two on Friday, and a third on Saturday. The Friday morning and afternoon sessions each lasted 90&#160;minutes. The third session was held on Saturday morning and lasted an hour.<sup id="cite_ref-sporting_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sporting-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> In the first free practice session, several large damp patches around the circuit&#160;– caused by late track cleaning rather than rain&#160;– dissuaded drivers from attempting laps and left the frustrated teams sitting in the pits waiting for the surface to dry as low track temperatures made the situation worse.<sup id="cite_ref-P1_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P1-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> When the cars did take to the track, many of the drivers either went off the road or spun.<sup id="cite_ref-P1_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P1-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Räikkönen only did three laps, before retiring with a gearbox problem, causing him to finish last.<sup id="cite_ref-P1_21-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P1-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Massa set the session's fastest time, ahead of McLaren duo <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> and Hamilton.<sup id="cite_ref-P1_21-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P1-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> After the session had ended, Fisichella was given a three-place grid penalty by the stewards for exiting the pit lane whilst the light at its exit was still red.<sup id="cite_ref-Fisichella_penalty_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Fisichella_penalty-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The second free practice session was held in dry conditions, but the track was still damp from earlier drizzle.<sup id="cite_ref-P2_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P2-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> After 30 minutes, Red Bull's <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> crashed at turn six, his car sustaining serious damage.<sup id="cite_ref-P2_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P2-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Webber was unhurt, although the crash meant that he finished the session in 19th&#160;position as he was unable to complete any further laps.<sup id="cite_ref-P2_23-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P2-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> The session was briefly stopped as a result while the marshals cleared away the debris.<sup id="cite_ref-P2_23-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P2-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> In this session, Räikkönen set the fastest time, with Hamilton next up.<sup id="cite_ref-P2_23-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P2-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Massa was third, while <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> of Red Bull broke up the Ferrari-McLaren domination by setting the fourth-fastest time, ahead of Kovalainen.<sup id="cite_ref-P2_23-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P2-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> The third and final practice session was held on the Saturday morning in dry conditions, but with damp patches on the track from earlier rain.<sup id="cite_ref-P3_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P3-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> As was the pattern for the season, the championship contenders appeared to work on preparations for the final part of qualifying and the race in this session and thus carry heavier fuel loads.<sup id="cite_ref-P3_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P3-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Webber set the fastest time in the session, with Renault's <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> in second, and Coulthard in third. Massa was fifth, Hamilton seventh, Räikkönen 11th and Kovalainen 12th.<sup id="cite_ref-P3_24-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P3-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2008_Turkish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Following the withdrawal of the Super Aguri team's two entries, minor changes were made to the qualifying procedure.<sup id="cite_ref-SA_Qualy_change_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SA_Qualy_change-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> In the first qualifying session, which would run for 20&#160;minutes, the five slowest cars (a reduction from the previous number of six) would be eliminated, leaving 15&#160;drivers to continue into the second session. In the second session, which would last 15&#160;minutes, the five slowest runners (again reduced from six) were to once again be eliminated, leaving the ten fastest drivers to compete in session three, as had previously been the case.<sup id="cite_ref-SA_Qualy_change_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SA_Qualy_change-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> The time taken for each session remained the same.<sup id="cite_ref-SA_Qualy_change_25-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SA_Qualy_change-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> The third session would determine the positions from first to tenth, and would decide <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>. Cars which failed to make the final session could refuel before the race, so ran lighter in first and second sessions. Cars which competed in the final session of qualifying were not allowed to refuel before the race, and as such carried more fuel than in the previous sessions.<sup id="cite_ref-sporting_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sporting-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">"I am very happy to have taken a third consecutive pole at this circuit: it is really motivating and it would be fantastic to do the same again tomorrow in the race."</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style=""><a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a>, after qualifying.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>Qualifying was a two-way battle between Ferrari and McLaren, with BMW Sauber not showing as strong a pace as they had in previous qualifying sessions, and Alonso not on as light a fuel load as he had in <a href="/wiki/2008_Spanish_Grand_Prix" title="2008 Spanish Grand Prix">Spain</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Qualy_review_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Qualy_review-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> Massa qualified on pole with a time of&#160;1:27.617, ahead of Kovalainen and Hamilton.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_Qualifying_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA_Qualifying-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton elected not to run a lap on the softer option of the two tyre compounds, feeling that they were running low on grip in the later stages of the lap.<sup id="cite_ref-Qualy_review_27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Qualy_review-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> He completed his final lap of the session on a "scrubbed" set of the harder tyre compound and it got him third place.<sup id="cite_ref-Qualy_review_27-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Qualy_review-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> Afterwards Hamilton said that he felt that his tyre choice had been incorrect.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> However, he changed his mind the following day, having made an examination of the relevant telemetry data.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Championship leader Räikkönen qualified&#160;fourth, ahead of Kubica and Webber. Alonso, Trulli, Heidfeld and Coulthard completed the top 10. <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> driving for Williams qualified&#160;11th, ahead of Barrichello and his teammate <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a>. Vettel qualified 14th, followed by <a href="/wiki/Timo_Glock" title="Timo Glock">Timo Glock</a> for Toyota.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_Qualifying_28-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA_Qualifying-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Nakajima qualified in&#160;16th, ahead of <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet_Jr." title="Nelson Piquet Jr.">Nelson Piquet Jr.</a> (Renault) and Bourdais.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_Qualifying_28-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA_Qualifying-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Afterward Bourdais said that his low position was due to being held up by both Force India drivers: "Bad traffic, basically the Force India guys, ruined my afternoon: on my first run I was held up by Sutil as early as Turn 3 and on the second, I came up behind Fisichella in Turn&#160;8".<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Fisichella qualified&#160;19th but his three-place penalty saw him drop behind teammate Sutil to 20th and last place on the grid.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_Qualifying_28-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA_Qualifying-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2008_Turkish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Felipe_Massa_2008_Algarve.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8c/Felipe_Massa_2008_Algarve.jpg/220px-Felipe_Massa_2008_Algarve.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="192" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8c/Felipe_Massa_2008_Algarve.jpg/330px-Felipe_Massa_2008_Algarve.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8c/Felipe_Massa_2008_Algarve.jpg/440px-Felipe_Massa_2008_Algarve.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3134" data-file-height="2732" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Felipe_Massa_2008_Algarve.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> won the race, after starting from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>.</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:234px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Giancarlo_Fisichella_over_Kazuki_Nakajima_istanbul_2008.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/eb/Giancarlo_Fisichella_over_Kazuki_Nakajima_istanbul_2008.jpg/232px-Giancarlo_Fisichella_over_Kazuki_Nakajima_istanbul_2008.jpg" decoding="async" width="232" height="155" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/eb/Giancarlo_Fisichella_over_Kazuki_Nakajima_istanbul_2008.jpg/348px-Giancarlo_Fisichella_over_Kazuki_Nakajima_istanbul_2008.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/eb/Giancarlo_Fisichella_over_Kazuki_Nakajima_istanbul_2008.jpg/464px-Giancarlo_Fisichella_over_Kazuki_Nakajima_istanbul_2008.jpg 2x" data-file-width="600" data-file-height="400" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Giancarlo_Fisichella_over_Kazuki_Nakajima_istanbul_2008.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> flies over <a href="/wiki/Kazuki_Nakajima" title="Kazuki Nakajima">Kazuki Nakajima</a></div></div></div>
<p>At the start, Hamilton immediately overtook Kovalainen to claim second behind Massa.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Räikkönen had a bad start and was squeezed into the first corner by Kovalainen.<sup id="cite_ref-Autosport_commentary_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Autosport_commentary-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> The two cars made light contact, with Räikkönen's front wing touching Kovalainen's left rear tyre. This resulted in a puncture for the McLaren driver which forced him to make an unscheduled pit stop on the second lap, which dropped him to 18th and last position.<sup id="cite_ref-Autosport_commentary_33-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Autosport_commentary-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FIA_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Räikkönen damaged his front wing but continued the entire race distance without changing the nose section of his car. Meanwhile, Kubica took advantage to move up into third position, whilst Räikkönen was also passed by Alonso.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_34-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Further back, Vettel collided with Sutil and both were forced to pit, Vettel with a puncture and Sutil for a new front wing.<sup id="cite_ref-Toro_Rosso_quotes_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Toro_Rosso_quotes-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Massa_Attack_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Massa_Attack-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Sutil's teammate Fisichella and Nakajima collided at the first turn as well,<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_34-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> after Fisichella braked too late and could not avoid the Williams driver.<sup id="cite_ref-Force_India_start_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Force_India_start-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Fisichella retired on the spot but Nakajima, despite losing both his front and rear wing in the incident, was able to make it back to his pit box before retiring.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_34-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> The incident resulted in the safety car being deployed.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_34-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatright" style=";"><link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r887775652"/>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">"I thought he [Hamilton] was light&#160;– He pushed me hard"</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style=""><a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a>, speaking after the race.<sup id="cite_ref-Massa_Attack_36-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Massa_Attack-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>The safety car was withdrawn at the end of the second lap and the race got underway again with Massa in the lead.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_34-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Räikkönen, meanwhile, overtook Alonso for fourth place.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_34-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Between lap&#160;four and lap&#160;eleven, Massa and Hamilton exchanged fastest laps and the McLaren driver managed to hold the gap between him and the leader at one and a half seconds.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_34-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> The two drivers left third-placed Kubica behind, who in turn was being caught quickly by Räikkönen.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_34-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Further back, Kovalainen passed Sutil on lap 12 and Vettel on lap 17.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_34-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton then began to close the gap between himself and Massa, posting consecutive quick lap times and was within seven–tenths of a second by lap fifteen.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_34-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Alonso was the first of the leading runners to make a pit stop on lap 15, dropping to tenth.<sup id="cite_ref-Autosport_commentary_33-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Autosport_commentary-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton pitted one lap later and dropped to sixth, while Massa stayed out for another three&#160;laps.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_34-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Both he and Kubica pitted at the same time, leaving Räikkönen temporarily in the lead. On lap 20, he set the fastest lap of the race, a 1:21.506, before he himself pitted at the end of lap 21, as did the then second-placed Heidfeld.<sup id="cite_ref-FL_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FL-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FIA_34-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Räikkönen rejoined ahead of Kubica, while Heidfeld came out ahead of both Alonso and Webber, to elevate himself to fifth.<sup id="cite_ref-Autosport_commentary_33-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Autosport_commentary-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FIA_34-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Massa was now back in the lead, but with Hamilton just 0.8&#160;seconds behind.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_34-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Running lighter on fuel with a view to making an additional pit stop to the Ferrari driver, Hamilton was able to close the gap on Massa and on lap&#160;24, he overtook him at turn&#160;twelve.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_34-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton now led, with Massa second and Räikkönen third.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_34-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Hamilton_2008_Singapore_GP_1.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4b/Hamilton_2008_Singapore_GP_1.jpg/220px-Hamilton_2008_Singapore_GP_1.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="332" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4b/Hamilton_2008_Singapore_GP_1.jpg/330px-Hamilton_2008_Singapore_GP_1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4b/Hamilton_2008_Singapore_GP_1.jpg/440px-Hamilton_2008_Singapore_GP_1.jpg 2x" data-file-width="667" data-file-height="1006" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Hamilton_2008_Singapore_GP_1.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> finished second.</div></div></div>
<p>Hamilton soon broke away from Massa, lapping over a second quicker than the Ferrari driver.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_34-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Further back, Bourdais spun off at turn twelve, with a rear suspension failure that put him out of the race.<sup id="cite_ref-Autosport_commentary_33-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Autosport_commentary-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FIA_34-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Bourdais_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bourdais-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> The top five in the race were now Hamilton, Massa, Räikkönen, Kubica and Heidfeld.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_34-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Over the next six laps, Hamilton stretched his lead to over six seconds before pitting on lap&#160;32.<sup id="cite_ref-Autosport_commentary_33-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Autosport_commentary-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> He rejoined the race in third, 2.6&#160;seconds behind second-placed Räikkönen.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_34-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Further back, on lap 34, Kovalainen passed Glock, only to be immediately re-passed.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_34-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> He did succeed up the main straight, on the same lap.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_34-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Two laps later, he passed Button.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_34-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Eight laps after Hamilton's pit stop, Ferrari called Massa in for his final stop of the afternoon, making Räikkönen the leader of the race once more.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_34-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> This lead, however, only lasted for a couple of laps as the Finn pitted on lap&#160;43, also making his final stop of the afternoon.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_34-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> On lap&#160;43, Hamilton led by 14.4&#160;seconds from Massa with Räikkönen a further seven&#160;seconds behind.<sup id="cite_ref-Autosport_commentary_33-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Autosport_commentary-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Two laps later Hamilton was forced to make his third and final pit stop.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_34-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> The McLaren driver rejoined the track behind Massa but crucially ahead of championship leader Räikkönen.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_34-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> The top three would remain this way for the remainder of the race.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_34-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Further back in fourth and fifth were the two BMW Saubers of Kubica and Heidfeld and behind them were Alonso, Trulli, Webber, Rosberg and Coulthard, with Kovalainen pursuing closely behind them.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_34-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Kovalainen passed Coulthard on lap 48, and then on the next lap passed Rosberg.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_34-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> However, Rosberg immediately overtook Kovalainen to regain his position, and Kovalainen was forced to pit on lap 50.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_34-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> After his final pit stop, Kovalainen rejoined the circuit in 13th position.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_34-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Felipe Massa won the race, making it his third consecutive win at the circuit, 3.7&#160;seconds ahead of Hamilton, with Räikkönen a further half-second behind in third.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_Classification_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA_Classification-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FIA_34-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> The two BMWs of Kubica and Heidfeld finished fourth and fifth respectively while further back Alonso, Webber and Rosberg completed the top eight.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_34-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard finished ninth, Trulli&#160;10th and Button&#160;11th.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_Classification_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA_Classification-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> After his puncture at the start, Kovalainen had managed to recover to 12th by the end of the race, passing Glock in the closing stages.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_34-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Race_report_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Race_report-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> Glock, Barrichello, Piquet, Sutil and Vettel were the last of the classified finishers.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_Classification_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA_Classification-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2008_Turkish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatright" style="width:40%;&#32;;"><link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r887775652"/>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">I am so thrilled with today's result, and I think this is the best race ever for me. It's not about winning, it's about feeling that you extract 100% from yourself and the car and I did that today. Before the race our prediction was that all being well I would finish fifth, so second is such a bonus. I was not happy with my performance yesterday and perhaps if I had managed to get the car on pole I could have won. However, we knew that we would have to use a three stop strategy as a precaution as we had some issues with our tyres.</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style=""><a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>, speaking after the race.<sup id="cite_ref-GrandPrix.com_quotes_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GrandPrix.com_quotes-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div><p>
The race victory was Massa's third win in Turkey in as many years, having won in both <a href="/wiki/2006_Turkish_Grand_Prix" title="2006 Turkish Grand Prix">2006</a> and <a href="/wiki/2007_Turkish_Grand_Prix" title="2007 Turkish Grand Prix">2007</a>. The top three finishers appeared in the subsequent <a href="/wiki/Press_conference" title="Press conference">press conference</a>. Massa said that: <style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r960796168">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style></p><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>It was a very tough race but the team work magnificently, giving me a great car. When Hamilton came underneath me at an incredible pace, I preferred not to take too many risks: the pit wall informed me that he was clearly running a lighter fuel load even if we were not certain he was on a three stop strategy. When that became clear, we were calmer, knowing we had everything in place to go for the win.<sup id="cite_ref-GrandPrix.com_quotes_40-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GrandPrix.com_quotes-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>
<p><a href="/wiki/Ron_Dennis" title="Ron Dennis">Ron Dennis</a>, McLaren's team principal, commented on Hamilton's drive: "Today we saw a truly phenomenal drive from Lewis [Hamilton], in which he optimised a three-stop strategy that we were forced to adopt as a consequence of concerns we had with his tyres."<sup id="cite_ref-GrandPrix.com_quotes_40-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GrandPrix.com_quotes-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton said that he felt that McLaren had closed the gap on Ferrari, and that he was "very excited" about the <a href="/wiki/2008_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="2008 Monaco Grand Prix">next race in Monaco</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-GrandPrix.com_quotes_40-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GrandPrix.com_quotes-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Räikkönen said: "It was a difficult weekend for me but six points are definitely better than nothing. At the start, I was almost alongside Heikki but then he slowed and I had to brake too to avoid a heavy crash. We touched just enough to damage the end plate of the front wing on my car: it was not a very serious problem, but it definitely didn't help my race."<sup id="cite_ref-GrandPrix.com_quotes_40-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GrandPrix.com_quotes-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> Räikkönen also said that he decided not to change his front wing, which was damaged after his collision with Kovalainen, the process would have taken too long.<sup id="cite_ref-Raikkonen_front_wing_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Raikkonen_front_wing-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Kimi_Raikkonen_2008_(square).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/40/Kimi_Raikkonen_2008_%28square%29.jpg/220px-Kimi_Raikkonen_2008_%28square%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="220" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/40/Kimi_Raikkonen_2008_%28square%29.jpg/330px-Kimi_Raikkonen_2008_%28square%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/40/Kimi_Raikkonen_2008_%28square%29.jpg/440px-Kimi_Raikkonen_2008_%28square%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="964" data-file-height="964" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Kimi_Raikkonen_2008_(square).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> finished third.</div></div></div>
<p>Fisichella, who had collided with Nakajima on the first lap, which caused both drivers to retire, blamed Bourdais: "I made a good start but then under braking Bourdais changed direction twice and I couldn't brake in time and went into the back of the Williams."<sup id="cite_ref-GrandPrix.com_quotes_40-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GrandPrix.com_quotes-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> Nakajima said that, "I don't know exactly what happened; I just went into the first corner following the guy in front. I didn't change my line and I was suddenly hit from behind."<sup id="cite_ref-GrandPrix.com_quotes_40-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GrandPrix.com_quotes-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Bourdais commented on his retirement: "Something broke at the back of the car... Going into Turn 12, I braked at the usual point and the car went sideways, I felt the right rear corner of the car drop and it spun me round."<sup id="cite_ref-GrandPrix.com_quotes_40-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GrandPrix.com_quotes-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Räikkönen's lead in the Drivers' World Championship was reduced by two points to seven, ahead of Massa and Hamilton, the latter two on 28&#160;points each. Kubica was in fourth, on 24&#160;points, followed by his teammate Heidfeld on 20&#160;points. In the Constructors' Championship, the Ferrari team increased its lead with a total of 66&#160;points, whilst BMW Sauber were second with 44&#160;points. McLaren were third with 42&#160;points, Williams fourth with 13&#160;points, and Red Bull were fifth, on ten.<sup id="cite_ref-Championship_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Championship-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
