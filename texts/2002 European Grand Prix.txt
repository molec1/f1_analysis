<p>The <b>2002 European Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>XLVI Allianz Grand Prix of Europe</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 23 June 2002 at the <a href="/wiki/N%C3%BCrburgring" title="Nürburgring">Nürburgring</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nurburg" class="mw-redirect" title="Nurburg">Nurburg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rhineland-Palatinate" title="Rhineland-Palatinate">Rhineland-Palatinate</a>, Germany. It was won by <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a>, his first win since his victory at the <a href="/wiki/2000_German_Grand_Prix" title="2000 German Grand Prix">2000 German Grand Prix</a>. His team mate <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> finished second in another dominating performance by the team. <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Mercedes_AMG_High_Performance_Powertrains" title="Mercedes AMG High Performance Powertrains">Mercedes</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> finished third. This was the first race at the modified <a href="/wiki/N%C3%BCrburgring" title="Nürburgring">Nürburgring</a> circuit, as the first chicane was replaced by the <i>Mercedes Arena</i> corners.
</p><p>The victory was Barrichello's second of his career and the first of the 2002 season; the result moved him up to fourth in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_champions" class="mw-redirect" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; champions">Drivers' Championship</a> but was tied with <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> on points. <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> extended his lead to be forty-six points ahead of <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> with Montoya a further three points behind. Schumacher tied the all-time held by <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> with his 106th podium finish. Ferrari's one-two finish allowed them to draw further ahead of Williams in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a> and McLaren maintained third position. <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a> extended their points advantage over <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a> in the battle for fourth place, with eight races remaining in the season.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2002_European_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2002_European_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Grand Prix was contested by eleven teams with two drivers each. The teams, also known as <a href="/wiki/Formula_One#Constructors" title="Formula One">constructors</a>, were <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>, <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a>, <a href="/wiki/British_American_Racing" title="British American Racing">BAR</a>, <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jaguar_Racing" title="Jaguar Racing">Jaguar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arrows_Grand_Prix_International" title="Arrows Grand Prix International">Arrows</a>, <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Toyota_Racing_(Formula_One_team)" title="Toyota Racing (Formula One team)">Toyota</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Entries_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Entries-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Going into the race, Ferrari driver <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> led the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a> with 70 <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Championship_points_scoring_systems" title="List of Formula One World Championship points scoring systems">points</a>, ahead of Williams teammates <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> and <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a> who were tied for second place on 27 points. <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> was a close fourth on 26 points, and <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> was fifth on 16 points.<sup id="cite_ref-2002points_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2002points-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a>, Ferrari were leading with 86 points, ahead of Williams in second on 54 points. McLaren were third with 33 points, while Renault (twelve) and Sauber (eight) battled for fourth place.<sup id="cite_ref-2002points_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2002points-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Ferrari and Michael Schumacher had dominated the championship, with Ralf Schumacher winning the <a href="/wiki/2002_Malaysian_Grand_Prix" title="2002 Malaysian Grand Prix">Malaysian Grand Prix</a> and Coulthard won the <a href="/wiki/2002_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="2002 Monaco Grand Prix">Monaco Grand Prix</a>. Barrichello and Montoya had finished in second, and <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> finished in third.<sup id="cite_ref-2002points_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2002points-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Nuerburgring_Luft_2011_03.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/06/Nuerburgring_Luft_2011_03.jpg/220px-Nuerburgring_Luft_2011_03.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="293" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/06/Nuerburgring_Luft_2011_03.jpg/330px-Nuerburgring_Luft_2011_03.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/06/Nuerburgring_Luft_2011_03.jpg/440px-Nuerburgring_Luft_2011_03.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3240" data-file-height="4320" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Nuerburgring_Luft_2011_03.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The Mercedes Arena corners (pictured in 2011) were created to increase the chance of overtaking.</div></div></div>
<p>Michael Schumacher had won the previous two European Grands Prix held at the Nürburgring and wanted to concentrate on the potential challenge from the Williams and McLaren teams at the race rather than worry about a possible disqualification from the <a href="/wiki/2002_Austrian_Grand_Prix" title="2002 Austrian Grand Prix">Austrian Grand Prix</a> where he was given victory after Barrichello was ordered by Ferrari to allow Schumacher past.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> After retiring from the <a href="/wiki/2002_Canadian_Grand_Prix" title="2002 Canadian Grand Prix">Canadian Grand Prix</a> with an engine failure which cost him a chance to challenge for victory, Montoya said he had recovered from his disappointment and he was hoping to build on his podium finish from the previous year's European Grand Prix. He also said that Williams had addressed his engine problems.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Räikkönen was looking forward to racing at the Nürburgring where it was the home race of McLaren's engine supplier Mercedes-Benz and was hoping to secure a good finish for the German fans.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After the previous race held at the Nürburgring (the <a href="/wiki/2001_European_Grand_Prix" title="2001 European Grand Prix">2001 European Grand Prix</a>) the layout of the circuit was changed, its first major change since it was reopened in 1984.<sup id="cite_ref-review2_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-review2-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The chicane which previously formed the first corner was replaced by a sharp right-hand hairpin and included a tight left and right-hand corner and the changes were designed to make overtaking opportunities more common.<sup id="cite_ref-autoweekchanges_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autoweekchanges-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The asphalt was widened by 25 meters to facilitate any overtaking manoeuvres.<sup id="cite_ref-reviews1_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-reviews1-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The changes increased the length of the circuit by 645 yards (590 metres) and the number of laps of the Grand Prix were reduced from 67 to 60.<sup id="cite_ref-autoweekchanges_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autoweekchanges-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Jaguar team principal and former World Champion <a href="/wiki/Niki_Lauda" title="Niki Lauda">Niki Lauda</a> felt the new layout would secure a better view for spectators watching in the grandstands.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The new section received mixed reviews from the drivers and critics. Ralf Schumacher said it was "a nice part of the circuit"  though he found it difficult to find the ideal braking point and felt overtaking would still be difficult.<sup id="cite_ref-reviews1_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-reviews1-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Jordan driver <a href="/wiki/Takuma_Sato" title="Takuma Sato">Takuma Sato</a> noted although the surface was bumpy upon entry he felt being unsighted in the first corner would be "a good feeling".<sup id="cite_ref-review2_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-review2-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Retired driver and columnist for <i><a href="/wiki/Bild" title="Bild">Bild</a></i> <a href="/wiki/Hans-Joachim_Stuck" title="Hans-Joachim Stuck">Hans-Joachim Stuck</a> described it as "sexiest the new corner on the Formula One circuit" and that it was made for aggressive drivers. Coulthard was more vocal in his criticism saying the bumpiness of the section was "ridiculous" and preferred the challenge of the former first corner chicane, while Montoya felt the section was "too slow".<sup id="cite_ref-reviews1_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-reviews1-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Some teams made modifications to their cars in preparation for the event. Ferrari brought new electronic bi-directional telemetry and steering wheels and Michael Schumacher evaluated a new engine cover which was not used in the Sunday race. Renault introduced a new aerodynamic package and a traction control system and McLaren brought a new revision of their aerodynamic package. BAR débuted a revised Honda V10 engine specification for the race and Jordan made minor alterations to their front wings.<sup id="cite_ref-fiafriday_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiafriday-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice_and_qualifying">Practice and qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2002_European_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Practice and qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>Four practice sessions were held before the Sunday race, two each on Friday and Saturday. The Friday morning and afternoon sessions each lasted an hour; the third and fourth sessions, on Saturday morning, lasted 45 minutes each.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The Saturday morning practice and afternoon qualifying sessions began 45 minutes earlier from their usual start times of 09:00 <a href="/wiki/Central_European_Summer_Time" title="Central European Summer Time">CEST</a> (<a href="/wiki/Coordinated_Universal_Time" title="Coordinated Universal Time">UTC</a>+2), 10:15 and 14:00 to accommodate the final two quarter-final matches of the <a href="/wiki/2002_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2002 FIFA World Cup">2002 FIFA World Cup</a> held in South Korea and Japan on the same day.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Conditions for the Friday practice sessions were warm and overcast.<sup id="cite_ref-fiafriday_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiafriday-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello set the fastest time of the first practice session with a lap time of 1 minute and 33.665 seconds, one-tenth of a second faster than his Ferrari teammate Michael Schumacher. <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> (with a time of 1:34.924) was third fastest, ahead of <a href="/wiki/Allan_McNish" title="Allan McNish">Allan McNish</a> and <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a>. Coulthard, Frentzen, <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mika_Salo" title="Mika Salo">Mika Salo</a> and Montoya rounded out the top ten fastest drivers of the session.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Enrique_Bernoldi" title="Enrique Bernoldi">Enrique Bernoldi</a> lost the rear-end of his Arrows at the Ford Kurve in the 36th minute of the session and his car's right side made light contact with a tyre wall;<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> this required a <a href="/wiki/Racing_flags#Yellow_flag" title="Racing flags">yellow-flag</a> to be shown as <a href="/wiki/Motorsport_marshal" title="Motorsport marshal">marshals</a> were needed to remove Bernoldi's car from the track.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> In the second practice session, Coulthard was the fastest driver with a time of 1:31.886 despite going off into a <a href="/wiki/Run-off_area" title="Run-off area">gravel trap</a> at Dunlop Kurve; Räikkönen finished with the third quickest time. The Ferrari drivers second and fourth; Michael Schumacher ahead of Barrichello. They were followed by the two Williamses of Montoya (who complained of understeer) and Ralf Schumacher. <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> were seventh and eighth for Renault with Salo and Panis completing the top ten.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It remained warm and overcast for the Saturday morning practice sessions.<sup id="cite_ref-fiasaturday_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiasaturday-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher set the fastest time of the third session of 1:30.658 which was recorded at the end of the session though he lost control of the rear-end of his Ferrari after clipping a kerb at the Veedol-S chicane early in the session.<sup id="cite_ref-fiasaturday_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiasaturday-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> His teammate Barrichello was second fastest. Ralf Schmacher was third fastest, ahead of his Williams teammate Montoya. Heidfeld secured the fifth fastest time ahead of Räikkönen. Massa, Button, Coulthard and Trulli followed in the top ten.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> In the final practice session, Michael Schumacher was unable to improve his time but remained fastest; Barrichello finished with the third quickest lap. Heidfeld was running quicker and was fourth, ahead of Ralf Schumacher and Salo. Coulthard, Montoya, Trulli and Button completed the top ten ahead of qualifying.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> de la Rosa had a hydraulic leak and fire in his car which prevented him from setting a lap time.<sup id="cite_ref-fiasaturday_17-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiasaturday-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">I am very pleased with today's pole, especially because it came as a bit of a surprise. After this morning's practice sessions we were not expecting anything higher than the second row, actually. We changed the car quite a lot from this morning but in my first run I made a mistake, the second went better and on my third run I got the pole. I still don't know where the speed came from and I must admit that luck also played a part in my result. Tomorrow is going to be a hard race where I think the tyres will be a key factor.</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style="">Juan Pablo Montoya on taking the pole position.<sup id="cite_ref-satquotes_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-satquotes-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup></cite>

<p>Saturday's afternoon qualifying session lasted for an hour. Each driver was limited to twelve laps, with the starting order decided by the drivers' fastest laps. During this session, the <a href="/wiki/107%25_rule" title="107% rule">107% rule</a> was in effect, which necessitated each driver set a time within 107% of the quickest lap to qualify for the race.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The session was held on a dry track with cloudy weather conditions; the track temperature ranged between 23–26&#160;°C (73–79&#160;°F). Montoya clinched his third consecutive pole position and the seventh of his career with a time of 1:29.906 which was set thirteen minutes before the session ended.<sup id="cite_ref-fiasaturday_17-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiasaturday-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> He was happy to take the pole position despite having set a target to be on the second row.<sup id="cite_ref-fiasaturday_17-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiasaturday-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Montoya was joined on the front row of the <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_motorsport_terms#G" title="Glossary of motorsport terms">grid</a> by teammate Ralf Schumacher who was 0.009 seconds off Montoya's pace and did not change his car's set-up.<sup id="cite_ref-fiasaturday_17-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiasaturday-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher qualified in third and his race car was stuck in gear on his first out lap and switched to the spare Ferrari car for the remainder of the session as his mechanics were unable to rectify the gearbox issue. He made two minor mistakes at the final two corners of the circuit on his final run after pushing hard which prevented him from improving his lap time.<sup id="cite_ref-satquotes_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-satquotes-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello progressively changed his car's balance over the one-hour qualifying period and secured fourth.<sup id="cite_ref-fiasaturday_17-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiasaturday-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard changed his set-up to run faster in the circuit's middle section which compromised performance in the first sector but took fifth.<sup id="cite_ref-satquotes_21-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-satquotes-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Räikkönen dropped one of his wheels on the turn thirteen kerbs which restricted him to sixth.<sup id="cite_ref-fiasaturday_17-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiasaturday-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> The two Renault drivers filled the fourth row of the grid with Trulli ahead of Button.<sup id="cite_ref-fiasaturday_17-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiasaturday-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Trulli was happy with his starting position although Button had understeer from his second qualifying run and made a minor change to his rear anti roll-bar in an attempt to go quicker but was delayed by slower cars. Heidfeld felt vibrations after <a href="/wiki/Flat_spot" title="Flat spot">flat-spotting</a> his front-right tyre on his first run which restricted him to ninth. Salo rounded out the top ten qualifiers and reported his car handled best on his first run but it deteriorated afterwards.<sup id="cite_ref-satquotes_21-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-satquotes-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Massa improved the balance on his Sauber but had excessive oversteer through turns two and four and managed eleventh. Panis took twelfth. He was ahead of McNish in the slower of the two Toyotas and his car developed intermittent issues with his electronics. Sato in the faster Jordan followed in 14th place and was disappointed with his performance as he struggled with an excessive amount of oversteer throughout qualifying.<sup id="cite_ref-fiasaturday_17-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiasaturday-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> He was followed in the time sheets by Frentzen who was suffering from understeer which caused him to lose time in the circuit's first sector.<sup id="cite_ref-satquotes_21-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-satquotes-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> de la Rosa drove the spare Jaguar monocoque for two laps and did two further laps in his repaired race car; he said his race car felt better balance but was delayed on his final run which restricted him to 16th.<sup id="cite_ref-satquotes_21-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-satquotes-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fiasaturday_17-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiasaturday-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Irvine said he picked up understeer in the one-hour session and had difficulty of accelerating out of corners;<sup id="cite_ref-satquotes_21-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-satquotes-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> these issues meant he could only take 17th. He was in front of Fisichella in the slower Jordan.<sup id="cite_ref-fiasaturday_17-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiasaturday-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Villeneuve managed 19th and was unable to find a suitable car set-up and his car had become slower.<sup id="cite_ref-satquotes_21-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-satquotes-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> The two Minardi drivers qualified in the 20th and 22nd positions with <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> quicker than <a href="/wiki/Alex_Yoong" title="Alex Yoong">Alex Yoong</a>; they were separated by Bernoldi who had balance problems.<sup id="cite_ref-fiasaturday_17-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiasaturday-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2002_European_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The drivers took to the track at 09:30 local time for a 30-minute warm-up session,<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_11-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> in overcast weather conditions. Barrichello maintained his form by setting the fastest lap in the closing moments of the session, with a lap of 1:32.671, three-tenths of a second faster than teammate Michael Schumacher who traded the fastest time with Barrichello during the session. Coulthard was third fastest and Räikkönen rounded out the top four; both drivers were more than one second off Barrichello's pace.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The race started at 14:00 local time. The conditions on the grid were overcast before the race; the track temperature ranged between 28–31&#160;°C (82–88&#160;°F) and rain was forecast.<sup id="cite_ref-f1comrace_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrace-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> When the race started, Montoya and Ralf Schumacher drew alongside each other heading into the first corner,<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> with Ralf Schumacher passing Montoya for the lead by taking the outside line.<sup id="cite_ref-f1comrace_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrace-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard, who started fifth, made a quick getaway but slid wide allowing Barrichello and Michael Schumacher to reclaim their starting positions.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_26-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Both Jordan drivers collided with Fisichella spinning into Sato at the first corner; the pair dropped to the rear-end of the field and both made pit stops for repairs at the end of the first lap.<sup id="cite_ref-f1comrace_25-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrace-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_26-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher got ahead of Montoya for third on the same lap but Montoya momentarily reclaimed the position,<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_26-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> while Barrichello overtook Ralf Schumacher for the lead on the straight heading into turn eight. Michael Schumacher eventually moved ahead of Montoya by the end of lap one.<sup id="cite_ref-f1comrace_25-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrace-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> At the end of the first lap, Barrichello led Ralf Schumacher by one second, with Michael Schumacher a further two seconds behind in third. Montoya in fourth was followed by Coulthard, Räikkönen, Button, Trulli, Massa and Heidfeld.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_26-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Barrichello started to pull away from Ralf Schumacher who was being caught by Michael Schumacher. Elsewhere in the field, Bernoldi moved ahead of Irvine for 14th position and Villeneuve overtook Yoong and de la Rosa to move into 18th.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_26-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher moved into second place after he passed Ralf Schumacher at the RTL Kurve on the third lap and began to close the gap to Barrichello.<sup id="cite_ref-f1comrace_25-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrace-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Panis moved ahead of Salo for twelfth and Villeneuve overtook Webber for 17th. Villeneuve ran wide during the course of lap four but retained his 17th position.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_26-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Trulli ran deep into turn one and fell down from eighth to twelfth place on the following lap, while Barrichello and Michael Schumacher continued to pull away from Ralf Schumacher.<sup id="cite_ref-f1comrace_25-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrace-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Trulli regained eleventh position after passing Panis on lap six while Salo in 13th spun and lost six positions. Webber made mistake and ran wide on lap seven and dropped behind de la Rosa and Salo to 19th position, while Yoong took a drive-through penalty because he jumped the start.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_26-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> By lap eight, Michael Schumacher was catching teammate Barrichello and was 1.4 seconds behind the Brazilian driver,<sup id="cite_ref-f1comrace_25-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrace-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> and was right behind him by the tenth lap. Trulli had moved back up into ninth on the same lap after overtaking McNish and Heidfeld, while Bernoldi was passed by Frentzen for 13th place.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_26-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Fisichella's Jordan lost parts of its bodywork as he drove down the main straight on lap eleven having bounced heavily on the kerbs and grass lining the circuit earlier in the race.<sup id="cite_ref-f1comrace_25-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrace-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> de la Rosa passed Irvine for 16th on the same lap. Bernoldi lost 14th position after he was overtaken by Villeneuve on the 13th lap, while Frentzen ran off the circuit and was passed by Villeneuve and Bernoldi; the latter also drove off the circuit on the following lap.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_26-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Räikkonen ran wide at the Yokohama-S and struggled to drive his car from the outside of the track allowing Button to take advantage to move into sixth on lap 17.<sup id="cite_ref-f1comrace_25-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrace-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Ferrari and Williams teams were employing different strategies – the Ferrari team were planning a two-stop strategy whereas the Williams team were only planning for one stop. Michael Schumacher made a pit stop on lap 24 and exited in front of Ralf Schumacher.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_26-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
