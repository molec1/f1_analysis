<p>The <b>1982 Swiss Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held at <a href="/wiki/Dijon-Prenois" title="Dijon-Prenois">Dijon-Prenois</a> in France on 29 August 1982.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The race, contested over 80 laps, was the fourteenth race of the <a href="/wiki/1982_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="1982 Formula One season">1982 Formula One season</a> and was won by <a href="/wiki/Keke_Rosberg" title="Keke Rosberg">Keke Rosberg</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW08" title="Williams FW08">Williams</a>-<a href="/wiki/Ford_Motor_Company" title="Ford Motor Company">Ford</a>. <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> finished second in a <a href="/wiki/Renault_RE30" title="Renault RE30">Renault</a>, having started from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>, while <a href="/wiki/Niki_Lauda" title="Niki Lauda">Niki Lauda</a> was third in a <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/1" title="McLaren MP4/1">McLaren</a>-Ford. This was the only win of the season for eventual World Champion Rosberg.
</p><p>This was the first World Championship <a href="/wiki/Swiss_Grand_Prix" title="Swiss Grand Prix">Swiss Grand Prix</a> since <a href="/wiki/1954_Swiss_Grand_Prix" title="1954 Swiss Grand Prix">1954</a> (despite it not being held in <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a>), and the last running of the event to date. Switzerland had banned motor racing after the <a href="/wiki/1955_Le_Mans_disaster" title="1955 Le Mans disaster">1955 Le Mans disaster</a>. Switzerland lifted the ban on motor racing in 2007.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1982_Swiss_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><b>First win:</b> <a href="/wiki/Keke_Rosberg" title="Keke Rosberg">Keke Rosberg</a>.</li>
<li>First win by a Finnish driver.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Patrick_Tambay" title="Patrick Tambay">Patrick Tambay</a>, Ferrari's only entry after team leader <a href="/wiki/Didier_Pironi" title="Didier Pironi">Didier Pironi</a>'s career-ending crash three weeks earlier, was unable to start due to a pinched nerve in his back.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> This was the last of only three times Ferrari team did not start a World Championship race in which they entered (see also <a href="/wiki/1950_French_Grand_Prix" title="1950 French Grand Prix">1950 French Grand Prix</a> and <a href="/wiki/1982_Belgian_Grand_Prix" title="1982 Belgian Grand Prix">1982 Belgian Grand Prix</a>). Ferrari withdrew their entry so late that <a href="/wiki/Chico_Serra" title="Chico Serra">Chico Serra</a> was not allowed to start as the reserve driver.<sup id="cite_ref-TV198219_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TV198219-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>The chequered flag was shown after 81 laps when the organizers / flag stand missed the leading car on lap 80.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
