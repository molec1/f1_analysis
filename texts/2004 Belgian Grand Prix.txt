<p>The <b>2004 Belgian Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>Belgian Grand Prix</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held on 29 August 2004, at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Spa-Francorchamps" title="Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps">Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps</a>, near the town of <a href="/wiki/Spa,_Belgium" title="Spa, Belgium">Spa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a>. It was the 14th race of the <a href="/wiki/2004_Formula_One_Season" class="mw-redirect" title="2004 Formula One Season">2004 Formula One Season</a>. The race was contested over 44 laps and was won by <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a>, taking his and <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>'s only race win of the season from tenth place on the grid. Second place for <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> won his seventh world championship, after beating third-placed <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> started from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> alongside Schumacher. The race saw many changes of the lead, but following several fortuitous <a href="/wiki/Safety_car" title="Safety car">safety cars</a>, Räikkönen was leading the race for the final few laps. <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> triggered a pile-up at the start, eliminating four cars and damaging several others, although he did admit to his mistake later. The race saw <a href="/wiki/Christian_Klien" title="Christian Klien">Christian Klien</a> score his first championship points, and <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Panis" title="Olivier Panis">Olivier Panis</a> and the Jaguar team their last.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2004_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2004_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/2004_Formula_One_Season" class="mw-redirect" title="2004 Formula One Season">2004 Formula One Season</a></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> had sealed the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a> at <a href="/wiki/2004_Hungarian_Grand_Prix" title="2004 Hungarian Grand Prix">the previous race</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> but the battle for second in the Championship, between <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a> and <a href="/wiki/British_American_Racing" title="British American Racing">BAR</a>, was still fierce.
</p><p>Heading into the 14th race of the season, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>, driving for the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> team, was leading the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a> by 38 points from teammate <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a>. <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a>, driving for <a href="/wiki/British_American_Racing" title="British American Racing">BAR</a>, was in third place, but only Schumacher and Barrichello could mathematically win the championship.
</p><p>However, the biggest story was the controversy surrounding Button's drive for 2005. Ten days before the Hungarian Grand Prix, Button chose to leave BAR and signed a two-year contract to return to <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> This was surprising, as Button was enjoying his best season to date, while Williams had been struggling.<sup id="cite_ref-contract_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-contract-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> BAR, however, insisted they had the right to exercise their option to keep Button. Button's management argued that the BAR option was not valid because it contained a clause allowing him to leave if BAR risked losing their Honda engines. They felt the new contract signed in the summer for Honda to supply engines to BAR was not definitive, and thus Button was free to move.<sup id="cite_ref-contract_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-contract-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-BARcontract_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BARcontract-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>With regard to the Spa circuit itself, the race did not take place in 2003 so that modifications to the <i>Bus Stop</i> chicane could be completed. Pundits were very critical of the changes to the chicane: namely, a sweeping right-hand bend has been introduced just before it. This left a huge piece of green-coloured tarmac, causing <a href="/wiki/Martin_Brundle" title="Martin Brundle">Martin Brundle</a> to be very scathing of it during qualifying:
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice_and_qualifying">Practice and qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2004_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Practice and qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Four practice sessions were held before qualifying – two 60-minute sessions on Friday, 27 August, and two 45-minute sessions on Saturday, 28 August.<sup id="cite_ref-regs_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regs-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> All teams, with the exception of Ferrari, <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a>, McLaren, and <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>, were permitted to run three drivers on Friday.<sup id="cite_ref-regs_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regs-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Davidson" title="Anthony Davidson">Anthony Davidson</a>, the third driver for BAR, set the quickest time in the first practice session, 1:45.104.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Räikkönen set the fastest time in practice two.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Saturday practice one was cancelled due to fog,<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> and Barrichello set the quickest time in Saturday practice two, shortened from 45 to 30 minutes as a result of the fog that had cancelled practice one.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Saturday's second practice session saw two major excursions from <a href="/wiki/Ant%C3%B4nio_Pizzonia" title="Antônio Pizzonia">Antônio Pizzonia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gianmaria_Bruni" title="Gianmaria Bruni">Gianmaria Bruni</a>, the latter causing the session to be red-flagged.<sup id="cite_ref-Grand_Prix_Qualifying_Live_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Grand_Prix_Qualifying_Live-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Jarno_Trulli_2004_Belgium_2.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6f/Jarno_Trulli_2004_Belgium_2.jpg/220px-Jarno_Trulli_2004_Belgium_2.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6f/Jarno_Trulli_2004_Belgium_2.jpg/330px-Jarno_Trulli_2004_Belgium_2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6f/Jarno_Trulli_2004_Belgium_2.jpg/440px-Jarno_Trulli_2004_Belgium_2.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1024" data-file-height="768" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Jarno_Trulli_2004_Belgium_2.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> took <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a> team.</div></div></div>
<p>Qualifying was split into two sections. In the first season, dubbed "pre-qualifying", each driver took turns to record one lap at a time. The order the cars ran in was the reverse order to the classified results at the <a href="/wiki/2004_Hungarian_Grand_Prix" title="2004 Hungarian Grand Prix">2004 Hungarian Grand Prix</a>. For example, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> won the race, so would be the last car to run in pre-qualifying. The second qualifying session (dubbed "qualifying") repeated this process (with the drivers running in reverse order to the pre-qualifying results), but with the caveat that the drivers would not be allowed to alter their fuel loads between then and the race. The fastest time in the second session would take pole. As in 2003, setups and fuel loads could not be altered between the end of the second qualifying session and the race.<sup id="cite_ref-regs_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regs-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> In pre-qualifying, <a href="/wiki/Ricardo_Zonta" title="Ricardo Zonta">Ricardo Zonta</a>, driving for <a href="/wiki/Toyota_Racing_(Formula_One_team)#2002–2004:_Early_years" title="Toyota Racing (Formula One team)">Toyota</a>, spun off at <i>Pouhon</i> corner, leading him to collide with the tyre wall.<sup id="cite_ref-Grand_Prix_Qualifying_Live_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Grand_Prix_Qualifying_Live-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The second part of qualifying started as being very wet but quickly dried out, enabling <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> to switch to intermediate tyres,<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> enabling him to set the fastest lap time. Schumacher qualified second. The conditions did not suit <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a>, who had run on wet tyres when the track was ready for intermediates, and <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a>, who did his qualifying lap on intermediates as it began to rain.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2004_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Trulli's Renault had a good start from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>, but second-placed Schumacher's <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> did not, and he lost positions to <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>'s Renault and <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a>'s <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>. At <i>La Source</i>, <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Jaguar_Racing" title="Jaguar Racing">Jaguar</a> collided with Barrichello's Ferrari, causing Webber to lose his front wing and Barrichello to suffer rear wing damage. In a separate incident, Räikkönen's McLaren made contact with <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a>, which caused Massa to lose his front wing. In a third incident, <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Panis" title="Olivier Panis">Olivier Panis</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Toyota_Racing_(Formula_One_team)#2002–2004:_Early_years" title="Toyota Racing (Formula One team)">Toyota</a> made contact. Going into <i>Eau Rouge</i>, Webber was side-by-side with <a href="/wiki/Takuma_Sato" title="Takuma Sato">Takuma Sato</a>'s <a href="/wiki/British_American_Racing" title="British American Racing">BAR</a>, and they collided in the middle of the corner whilst they were overtaken by Montoya's <a href="/wiki/Williams_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams F1">Williams</a>. The contact broke Sato's left rear suspension and Webber's front right suspension. Sato consequently spun in the middle of the track. The rest of the field attempted to avoid the spinning BAR, but in doing so, <a href="/wiki/Zsolt_Baumgartner" title="Zsolt Baumgartner">Zsolt Baumgartner</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gianmaria_Bruni" title="Gianmaria Bruni">Gianmaria Bruni</a>, both driving for <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a>, made contact, putting Bruni out of the race and getting him hit by <a href="/wiki/Giorgio_Pantano" title="Giorgio Pantano">Giorgio Pantano</a>'s Jordan. Sato, Webber, Bruni, and Pantano were out of the race. A small fire on Bruni's car, caused by the contact with Pantano, forced the <a href="/wiki/Safety_car" title="Safety car">safety car</a> to come out. During this time, Button, Massa, Barrichello, Heidfeld, Baumgartner, and <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Panis" title="Olivier Panis">Olivier Panis</a> stopped for repairs caused by debris. Massa and Barrichello stopped twice.
</p><p>The safety car came in at the end of lap four and Trulli led Alonso and Coulthard. Räikkönen overtook Schumacher for fifth position and Button overtook <a href="/wiki/Ricardo_Zonta" title="Ricardo Zonta">Ricardo Zonta</a>'s Toyota. Schumacher continued to be slow, losing 1.1 seconds to Räikkönen in the middle sector on lap five, enabling Montoya to overtake him around the outside of the <i>Bus Stop</i> chicane on lap five. On lap six, Räikkönen overtook his teammate, Coulthard, for third, going up the inside at the <i>Les Combes</i> chicane. On lap eight, Barrichello overtook Baumgartner and Heidfeld, putting him into 14th place. <a href="/wiki/Christian_Klien" title="Christian Klien">Christian Klien</a>, in the sole remaining Jaguar, was the first driver to make a scheduled <a href="/wiki/Pit_stop" title="Pit stop">pit stop</a>, on lap nine, and race leader Trulli made a pit stop on lap 10, rejoining in ninth place. Alonso now led the race, but on lap 12 he had an oil leak and spun twice at <i>Les Combes</i>, losing the lead to Räikkönen. He managed to rejoin, but he then had a further spin at <i>Rivage</i> for the same reason and could not rejoin. On the same lap Coulthard had a rear tyre de-lamination, but he made it back to pit lane. Räikkönen stopped at the end of lap 13. Montoya now led the race from Schumacher, until Montoya pitted on lap 15. Montoya left the pit lane behind Massa, losing time while overtaking him. Schumacher pitted one lap later and leapfrogged Montoya. New leader Pizzonia came into the pits on lap 17. Räikkönen now led from Button in second, Trulli in third, and Schumacher in fourth, but Schumacher was able to overcome Trulli on lap 19.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a> ran wide on lap 20 and lost parts of his front wing. On the same lap, Montoya attempted to repeat his earlier overtake on Trulli, but the pair collided, putting Trulli into a spin. Trulli lost several places, and Montoya lost fourth place to his teammate, Pizzonia. Button pitted on lap 21, rejoining in seventh, behind the battling Fischella and Barrichello. Barrichello then took fifth place on lap 22, as Trulli made a second pit stop, having been passed by Panis. Button was also able to pass Fisichella for sixth position on lap 23, as Barrichello pitted. Räikkönen pitted on lap 29, rejoining in second position, while Schumacher still had to stop. Montoya pitted on the same lap as Räikkönen. On lap 31, Button suffered a right rear tyre de-lamination similar to Coulthard's, at approximately 205&#160;mph (330&#160;km/h),<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> pitching him into a spin. As a result, he crashed into the Minardi of Baumgartner, who was being lapped, putting both drivers out of the race and bringing out a <a href="/wiki/Safety_car" title="Safety car">safety car</a>. Schumacher, Pizzonia, and Heidfeld took the opportunity to pit behind the safety car.
</p><p>Still behind the safety car, on lap 32, Pizzonia retired due to a gearbox problem. Räikkönen led the race from Schumacher, Montoya, Barrichello, and Zonta, who started last. At the restart on lap 34, Klien overtook Panis for eighth place and one point, as Coulthard overtook Trulli for tenth place. One lap later, Coulthard overtook Panis for ninth. On lap 36, Montoya's rear right tyre de-laminated, forcing him to retire.
</p><p>On lap 38, Coulthard attempted to overtake Klien for seventh place, but contact between the two drivers meant Coulthard needed a new front wing after it broke loose and became stuck over his own rear wing.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (April 2013)">clarification needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> The resulting debris caused the safety car to come out again. The safety car came in at the end of lap 41, but just four corners later, fourth-placed Zonta's engine blew up spectacularly, putting him out of the race. On the penultimate lap, Coulthard overtook Panis for seventh.
</p><p>Räikkönen won the race, his first and only victory of 2004. Schumacher finished second, with teammate Barrichello third. The result gave Schumacher his seventh World Drivers' Championship.
</p>
