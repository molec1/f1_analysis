<p>The <b>2020 Tuscan Grand Prix</b> (officially known as the <b>Formula 1 Pirelli Gran Premio della Toscana Ferrari 1000 2020</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 13 September 2020 at the <a href="/wiki/Mugello_Circuit" title="Mugello Circuit">Autodromo Internazionale del Mugello</a> in <a href="/wiki/Scarperia_e_San_Piero" title="Scarperia e San Piero">Scarperia e San Piero</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tuscany" title="Tuscany">Tuscany</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>. The race was the ninth round of the <a href="/wiki/2020_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2020 Formula One World Championship">2020 Formula One World Championship</a>. The race was won by <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> team, with teammate <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a> in second; Mercedes scored their third 1–2 finish of the season. <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Albon" title="Alexander Albon">Alexander Albon</a> of <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a>-<a href="/wiki/Honda_in_Formula_One" title="Honda in Formula One">Honda</a> scored his first podium in Formula One with a third place finish, becoming the first <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_drivers_from_Thailand" title="Formula One drivers from Thailand">Thai driver</a> to do so. The race was the first race since the <a href="/wiki/2016_Brazilian_Grand_Prix" title="2016 Brazilian Grand Prix">2016 Brazilian Grand Prix</a> to have two <a href="/wiki/Racing_flags#Red_flag" title="Racing flags">red flags</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> This event, along with the preceding <a href="/wiki/2020_Italian_Grand_Prix" title="2020 Italian Grand Prix">Italian Grand Prix</a>, also marked the first time that back to back Formula One Grands Prix were red flagged since the <a href="/wiki/2011_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="2011 Monaco Grand Prix">2011 Monaco Grand Prix</a> and the <a href="/wiki/2011_Canadian_Grand_Prix" title="2011 Canadian Grand Prix">2011 Canadian Grand Prix</a>. Lewis Hamilton's win was the 90th of his career and he joined <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> as the only drivers with 90 or more victories. The race is also notable for having three standing starts due to the two red flags.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2020_Tuscan_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The race was held as an event celebrating Ferrari's 1000th race in the <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One World Championship</a> and the safety car used a red livery, instead of its usual silver colour, to mark the occasion.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The Mugello Circuit hosted a World Championship Formula One race for the first time.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Impact_of_the_COVID-19_pandemic">Impact of the COVID-19 pandemic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2020_Tuscan_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Impact of the COVID-19 pandemic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/2020_Formula_One_World_Championship#Impact_of_the_COVID-19_pandemic" title="2020 Formula One World Championship">2020 Formula One World Championship §&#160;Impact of the COVID-19 pandemic</a>, and <a href="/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic_in_Italy" title="COVID-19 pandemic in Italy">COVID-19 pandemic in Italy</a></div>
<p>The opening rounds of the <a href="/wiki/2020_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2020 Formula One World Championship">2020</a> championship were heavily affected by the <a href="/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic" title="COVID-19 pandemic">COVID-19 pandemic</a>. Several Grands Prix were cancelled or postponed after the aborted <a href="/wiki/2020_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="2020 Australian Grand Prix">opening round</a> in <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>, prompting the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile</a> to draft a new calendar. While the previous eight events were held <a href="/wiki/Behind_closed_doors_(sport)" title="Behind closed doors (sport)">behind closed doors</a>, organizers announced that up to 2,880 spectators would be allowed for the Tuscan Grand Prix.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Entrants">Entrants</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2020_Tuscan_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Entrants">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Further information: <a href="/wiki/2020_Formula_One_World_Championship#Entries" title="2020 Formula One World Championship">2020 Formula One World Championship §&#160;Entries</a></div>
<p>The drivers and teams were the same as those on the season entry list with no additional stand-in drivers for either the race or practice.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tyres">Tyres</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2020_Tuscan_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Tyres">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2020_Tuscan_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The race was marked by several incidents. On the first lap, <a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pierre_Gasly" title="Pierre Gasly">Pierre Gasly</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Romain_Grosjean" title="Romain Grosjean">Romain Grosjean</a> collided, resulting in Gasly and Verstappen retiring from the race and Räikkönen having to change his front wing. A separate incident at the same corner involving <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Sainz_Jr." title="Carlos Sainz Jr.">Carlos Sainz Jr.</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lance_Stroll" title="Lance Stroll">Lance Stroll</a> led to front wing damage for <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> as he ran into Sainz. These incidents together brought out the safety car. On lap 6 the safety car pulled in, but an <a href="/wiki/Accordion_effect" title="Accordion effect">accordion effect</a><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> led the midfield drivers to accelerate up to racing speed before the leaders did and were forced to brake, triggering a collision involving Sainz, <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Magnussen" title="Kevin Magnussen">Kevin Magnussen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Antonio_Giovinazzi" title="Antonio Giovinazzi">Antonio Giovinazzi</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Nicholas_Latifi" title="Nicholas Latifi">Nicholas Latifi</a>. All four drivers retired from the race and the red flag was brought out. During the red flag period, <a href="/wiki/Esteban_Ocon" title="Esteban Ocon">Esteban Ocon</a> was forced to retire due to a brake failure; <a href="/wiki/George_Russell_(racing_driver)" title="George Russell (racing driver)">George Russell</a> had earlier reported Ocon's brakes being on fire. The second red flag came on lap 42, after <a href="/wiki/Lance_Stroll" title="Lance Stroll">Lance Stroll</a> appeared to suffer a tyre failure at turn 9 and went into the barrier.
</p><p>By finishing in ninth place former World Champion <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> scored his first points of the season. The race marked the first time since the <a href="/wiki/2016_Brazilian_Grand_Prix" title="2016 Brazilian Grand Prix">2016 Brazilian Grand Prix</a> where there were two red flags.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> A poor restart after the second red flag meant <a href="/wiki/George_Russell_(racing_driver)" title="George Russell (racing driver)">George Russell</a> slipped from ninth to 12th and last. He eventually finished 11th leaving him out of the points.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
