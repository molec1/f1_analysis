<p>The <b>1997 Australian Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 9 March 1997 at the <a href="/wiki/Melbourne_Grand_Prix_Circuit" class="mw-redirect" title="Melbourne Grand Prix Circuit">Melbourne Grand Prix Circuit</a> in <a href="/wiki/Albert_Park_and_Lake" title="Albert Park and Lake">Albert Park</a>, <a href="/wiki/Melbourne" title="Melbourne">Melbourne</a>. It was the first race of the <a href="/wiki/1997_FIA_Formula_One_World_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="1997 FIA Formula One World Championship">1997 FIA Formula One World Championship</a>. It was the second Grand Prix to be hosted in Melbourne. The 58-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> driver <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> after starting from fourth position. <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> finished second for the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> and Coulthard's teammate <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> was third.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1997_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1997_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Two new teams came into Formula One in 1997: <a href="/wiki/Stewart_Grand_Prix" title="Stewart Grand Prix">Stewart</a> and <a href="/wiki/MasterCard_Lola" title="MasterCard Lola">Lola</a>. <a href="/wiki/Footwork_Arrows" title="Footwork Arrows">Footwork</a> reverted to their old name of <a href="/wiki/Arrows_Grand_Prix_International" title="Arrows Grand Prix International">Arrows</a> and acquired <a href="/wiki/Yamaha_Motor_Company" title="Yamaha Motor Company">Yamaha</a> engines, while <a href="/wiki/Equipe_Ligier" title="Equipe Ligier">Ligier</a> were bought by <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> and changed their name to <a href="/wiki/Prost_Grand_Prix" title="Prost Grand Prix">Prost Grand Prix</a>. <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_Racing" title="Tyrrell Racing">Tyrrell</a> acquired <a href="/wiki/Ford_Motor_Company" title="Ford Motor Company">Ford</a> engines.
</p><p>The change that dominated the drivers line up was <a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a>'s surprise sacking from <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a> having just won the World Championship. He joined <a href="/wiki/Tom_Walkinshaw" title="Tom Walkinshaw">Tom Walkinshaw</a> and the newly purchased Arrows team. In the week up to the race, there were rumours, which proved to be unfounded, of Hill having left Arrows due to the poor performance of the car.<sup id="cite_ref-itv-qualifying-3_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-itv-qualifying-3-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Diniz" title="Pedro Diniz">Pedro Diniz</a> brought significant sponsorship backing, and was hired as Hill's teammate. Williams retained <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a> and replaced Hill with <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a>.
</p><p>Villeneuve was the <a href="/wiki/Bookmaker" title="Bookmaker">bookmaker</a>'s favourite heading into the new season. He said that being the favourite put "extra pressure, but it's good pressure [on me]".<sup id="cite_ref-itv-qualifying-1_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-itv-qualifying-1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-itv-qualifying-4_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-itv-qualifying-4-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> retained <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a>, <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a> kept <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> and McLaren retained <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> and <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a> team had two new drivers in <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a>, previously at <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a>, brother of Michael. The new <a href="/wiki/Prost_Grand_Prix" title="Prost Grand Prix">Prost Grand Prix</a> team kept <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Panis" title="Olivier Panis">Olivier Panis</a> and signed Japanese rookie driver <a href="/wiki/Shinji_Nakano" title="Shinji Nakano">Shinji Nakano</a>. <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a> kept <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Herbert" title="Johnny Herbert">Johnny Herbert</a> and the loss of Frentzen saw <a href="/wiki/Peter_Sauber" title="Peter Sauber">Peter Sauber</a> sign Ferrari <a href="/wiki/Test_driver" title="Test driver">test driver</a> <a href="/wiki/Nicola_Larini" title="Nicola Larini">Nicola Larini</a>. Tyrrell retained <a href="/wiki/Mika_Salo" title="Mika Salo">Mika Salo</a> for a third year and added <a href="/wiki/Jos_Verstappen" title="Jos Verstappen">Jos Verstappen</a> to the team from Arrows. <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a> with <a href="/wiki/V8_engine" title="V8 engine">V8</a> <a href="/wiki/Brian_Hart_Ltd." class="mw-redirect" title="Brian Hart Ltd.">Hart</a> engines signed <a href="/wiki/Ukyo_Katayama" title="Ukyo Katayama">Ukyo Katayama</a> and <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italian</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a>. <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Panis" title="Olivier Panis">Olivier Panis</a> were both making their 50th race entry, and <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Herbert" title="Johnny Herbert">Johnny Herbert</a> was making his 100th race entry.
</p><p>The new <a href="/wiki/Stewart_Grand_Prix" title="Stewart Grand Prix">Stewart Grand Prix</a> team had signed <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> and <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Danish</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Jan_Magnussen" title="Jan Magnussen">Jan Magnussen</a>. <a href="/wiki/Mastercard_Lola" class="mw-redirect" title="Mastercard Lola">Lola</a> recruited ex-<a href="/wiki/Footwork_Arrows" title="Footwork Arrows">Footwork Arrows</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Ricardo_Rosset" title="Ricardo Rosset">Ricardo Rosset</a> and former Benetton test driver <a href="/wiki/Vincenzo_Sospiri" title="Vincenzo Sospiri">Vincenzo Sospiri</a>.
</p><p>Bridgestone also made their first official appearance in Formula 1, breaking Goodyear's reign as a sole tyre supplier which began in 1992. They provided tyres for Minardi, Arrows, Prost, Stewart and Lola. Previously, the company had produced Formula One tyres at the 1976 and 1977 Japanese Grand Prix for Japanese entrants such as Kazuyoshi Hoshino's Heros Racing and Kojima.
</p><p>The British television coverage switched to <a href="/wiki/ITV_(TV_network)" title="ITV (TV network)">ITV</a> for the 1997 season and beyond, after 18 years of regular coverage for the BBC. Former driver <a href="/wiki/Martin_Brundle" title="Martin Brundle">Martin Brundle</a> joined <a href="/wiki/Murray_Walker" title="Murray Walker">Murray Walker</a> in the commentary box.
</p><p>In the build-up to the weekend, Michael Schumacher said that the circuit "wasn't particularly special", resulting in some criticism from locals.<sup id="cite_ref-itv-qualifying-2_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-itv-qualifying-2-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> There were also protests in the lead-up to the race, with protestors pouring diesel on to the track the week before the race.<sup id="cite_ref-itv-qualifying-2_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-itv-qualifying-2-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> A strike also meant that there was no tram shuttle service running, with spectators forced to catch buses to the track. <a href="/wiki/Jeff_Kennett" title="Jeff Kennett">Jeff Kennett</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Premier_of_Victoria" title="Premier of Victoria">Premier of Victoria</a>, labelling them "bloody minded" and that they will have to "incur the wrath of the community".<sup id="cite_ref-itv-qualifying-2_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-itv-qualifying-2-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice_and_qualifying">Practice and qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1997_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Practice and qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Canadian <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a> took his fourth career <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> with a lap of 1:29.369, while <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a> could only manage a 1:31.121 to fill the remaining spot on the front row. Only six drivers managed to qualify within 3 seconds of the pole position time. The session was red flagged with just over two minutes remaining after a collision between <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nicola_Larini" title="Nicola Larini">Nicola Larini</a> on the straight between turns 10 and 11. This resulted in many of the cars effectively having to complete a one-lap sprint to post a lap time before the end of the session. Damon Hill struggled during the session, only just qualifying inside of the 107% limit due to an oil leak which hampered the lap times. Both of the <a href="/wiki/Mastercard_Lola" class="mw-redirect" title="Mastercard Lola">Lola</a> cars failed to make the qualifying limit and were over ten seconds slower than Villeneuve in their first and only F1 qualifying session, as the team had to withdraw from the <a href="/wiki/1997_Brazilian_Grand_Prix" title="1997 Brazilian Grand Prix">next race</a> due to a lack of funds. Pedro Diniz was also outside of the 107% time, but he was permitted to race as he had set a time in practice within the 107% time.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1997_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On the parade lap, Damon Hill's throttle jammed, leaving him stranded on the track and causing him to retire from the race. At the first corner, <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a> misjudged his braking, hitting both Villeneuve and <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Herbert" title="Johnny Herbert">Herbert</a> - all three retired from the race. Williams adopted a two-stop strategy, while most other teams were going for one-stop races. <a href="/wiki/Jos_Verstappen" title="Jos Verstappen">Jos Verstappen</a> spun off on lap two while attempting to overtake Ukyo Katayama. Frentzen quickly built up a lead: 2.7secs on the first lap, 3.7s on the second, 5.3s on the third and 7.2s on the fourth. Both of the Jordan cars soon retired from the race, Ralf Schumacher suffering a gearbox problem and Fisichella spinning off the track while passing Barrichello. By lap 12, however, Frentzen eased off, and for the next six laps the gap between first and second stayed at 17-18s. Frentzen pitted on lap 18 and rejoined third.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a> retired from the race after running out of fuel, to the fury of his <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a> team, who had been trying to call him into the pits for 5 laps. In the laps that followed he was able to close up on Coulthard and Schumacher. Frentzen lost time in traffic, struggling with his brakes. Coulthard and Schumacher pitted in mid-race and so Frentzen moved ahead again and ran very quickly for a few laps before he began to fade again. On lap 40 he came in for his second stop. The gap to Coulthard was only 23secs, and with the time in the pitlane being around 22-24secs it was unclear whether he could emerge ahead. In the end, his pit stop was delayed for several seconds by a right rear tire problem, allowing Coulthard and Schumacher to move ahead. At the front <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> continued to keep away from these incidents to lead the race, followed by <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> and Frentzen.  Frentzen closed up on Coulthard and Schumacher who were by then running together but Schumacher had to make an unscheduled fuel stop towards the end of the race, promoting Frentzen to second. Large quantities of dust had been coming from Frentzen's brakes for some time and with three laps to go a brake disc failed, sending him into the gravel trap at the end of the start/finish straight. Coulthard went on to take his second career win. It was <a href="/w/index.php?title=McLaren%27s&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="McLaren&#39;s (page does not exist)">McLaren's</a> first win since <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> won the <a href="/wiki/1993_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="1993 Australian Grand Prix">1993 Australian Grand Prix</a>. It was also their first win with <a href="/wiki/Mercedes_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Mercedes F1">Mercedes</a> as an engine supplier, and Mercedes' first victory as an engine manufacturer since the <a href="/wiki/1955_Italian_Grand_Prix" title="1955 Italian Grand Prix">1955 Italian Grand Prix</a>.
</p>
