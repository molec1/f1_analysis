<p>The <b>1959 Italian Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held at <a href="/wiki/Autodromo_Nazionale_di_Monza" title="Autodromo Nazionale di Monza">Monza</a> on 13 September 1959. It was race 8 of 9 in the <a href="/wiki/1959_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1959 World Championship of Drivers">1959 World Championship of Drivers</a> and race 7 of 8 in the <a href="/wiki/1959_International_Cup_for_Formula_One_Manufacturers" class="mw-redirect" title="1959 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers">1959 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers</a>. It was the 29th <a href="/wiki/Italian_Grand_Prix" title="Italian Grand Prix">Italian Grand Prix</a> and the 24th to be held at <a href="/wiki/Autodromo_Nazionale_di_Monza" title="Autodromo Nazionale di Monza">Monza</a>. The race was held over 72 laps of the five kilometre circuit for a total race distance of 414 kilometres.
</p><p>The race was won by British driver <a href="/wiki/Stirling_Moss" title="Stirling Moss">Stirling Moss</a> driving a <a href="/wiki/Cooper_T51" title="Cooper T51">Cooper T51</a> for the privateer <a href="/wiki/Rob_Walker_Racing_Team" title="Rob Walker Racing Team">Rob Walker Racing Team</a>. Moss won by 46 seconds over American driver <a href="/wiki/Phil_Hill" title="Phil Hill">Phil Hill</a> driving a <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_246_F1" title="Ferrari 246 F1">Ferrari Dino 246</a> for <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Scuderia Ferrari</a>. Championship points leader Australian <a href="/wiki/Jack_Brabham" title="Jack Brabham">Jack Brabham</a> finished third in <a href="/wiki/Cooper_Car_Company" title="Cooper Car Company">works</a> entered Cooper T51, expanding his points lead, but not sufficiently to prevent a championship showdown with Moss and Ferrari driver <a href="/wiki/Tony_Brooks_(racing_driver)" title="Tony Brooks (racing driver)">Tony Brooks</a> at the <a href="/wiki/1959_United_States_Grand_Prix" title="1959 United States Grand Prix">United States Grand Prix</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_report">Race report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1959_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Race report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This race was won on the weight of the cars, with <a href="/wiki/Stirling_Moss" title="Stirling Moss">Stirling Moss</a> and team manager <a href="/wiki/Rob_Walker_(motorsport)" class="mw-redirect" title="Rob Walker (motorsport)">Rob Walker</a> gambling on running the whole race without a tyre change in the little lightweight <a href="/wiki/Cooper_Car_Company" title="Cooper Car Company">Cooper</a> - although they substituted knock-on wheels for bolt-ons in case a pit stop was necessary. Stirling drove a careful race, relying on the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> crew needing to pit. <a href="/wiki/Tony_Brooks_(racing_driver)" title="Tony Brooks (racing driver)">Tony Brooks</a> made a good start but a piston failure eliminated him on the first lap. <a href="/wiki/Graham_Hill" title="Graham Hill">Graham Hill</a> and then <a href="/wiki/Dan_Gurney" title="Dan Gurney">Dan Gurney</a> led, but lost their advantages through clumsy pit-stop action. Moss continued to win at an average speed of 124&#160;mph, a track record. <a href="/wiki/Phil_Hill" title="Phil Hill">Phil Hill</a> was second for Ferrari on their home track, ahead of a Ferrari 4-5-6 in the order Gurney, <a href="/wiki/Cliff_Allison" title="Cliff Allison">Cliff Allison</a> and <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Gendebien" title="Olivier Gendebien">Olivier Gendebien</a>.
</p><p>Moss' win closed the championship gap to only 5½ points behind <a href="/wiki/Jack_Brabham" title="Jack Brabham">Jack Brabham</a> with Brooks eight points behind Brabham. The combined efforts of Brabham, Moss, <a href="/wiki/Maurice_Trintignant" title="Maurice Trintignant">Maurice Trintignant</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bruce_McLaren" title="Bruce McLaren">Bruce McLaren</a> and <a href="/wiki/Masten_Gregory" title="Masten Gregory">Masten Gregory</a> secured the constructors championship for the <a href="/wiki/Cooper_Car_Company" title="Cooper Car Company">Cooper Car Company</a>.
</p>
