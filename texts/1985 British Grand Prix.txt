<p>The <b>1985 British Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>XXXVIII Marlboro British Grand Prix</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at <a href="/wiki/Silverstone_Circuit" title="Silverstone Circuit">Silverstone</a> on 21 July 1985. It was the eighth race of the <a href="/wiki/1985_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1985 Formula One World Championship">1985 Formula One World Championship</a>.
</p><p>The 65-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/2" title="McLaren MP4/2">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Techniques_d%27Avant_Garde" title="Techniques d&#39;Avant Garde">TAG</a>, after he started from third position. <a href="/wiki/Michele_Alboreto" title="Michele Alboreto">Michele Alboreto</a> finished second in a <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_156/85" title="Ferrari 156/85">Ferrari</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Laffite" title="Jacques Laffite">Jacques Laffite</a> third in a <a href="/wiki/Ligier_JS25" title="Ligier JS25">Ligier</a>-<a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>. The win, Prost's third of the season, moved him to within two points of Alboreto at the top of the Drivers' Championship.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1985_British_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Silverstone, a circuit located on a former World War II airfield located between London and <a href="/wiki/Birmingham,_England" class="mw-redirect" title="Birmingham, England">Birmingham</a> in England was known for being the fastest circuit used by Formula One; it was even faster than Monza and the Österreichring. The circuit's layout back then was quite different and simpler compared to the current layout used from 2010 onwards. Silverstone alternated the British Grand Prix with Brands Hatch and each circuit would host it every 2nd year.
</p><p>Qualifying saw <a href="/wiki/Keke_Rosberg" title="Keke Rosberg">Keke Rosberg</a> underline Silverstone's incredibly high speed laps by taking pole in his <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW10" title="Williams FW10">Williams</a> with a lap of 1:05.591. At an average speed of 160.9&#160;mph (259&#160;km/h), it stood as the fastest qualifying lap in F1 history for 17 years. Alongside Rosberg on the front row was <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Brabham_BT54" title="Brabham BT54">Brabham</a>, while <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> in the <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/2" title="McLaren MP4/2">McLaren</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Lotus_97T" title="Lotus 97T">Lotus</a> were on the second row. <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> in the second Williams and championship leader <a href="/wiki/Michele_Alboreto" title="Michele Alboreto">Michele Alboreto</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_156/85" title="Ferrari 156/85">Ferrari</a> filled the third row, while on the fourth were <a href="/wiki/Andrea_de_Cesaris" title="Andrea de Cesaris">Andrea de Cesaris</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Ligier_JS25" title="Ligier JS25">Ligier</a> and <a href="/wiki/Elio_de_Angelis" title="Elio de Angelis">Elio de Angelis</a> in the second Lotus. <a href="/wiki/Teo_Fabi" title="Teo Fabi">Teo Fabi</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Toleman_TG185" title="Toleman TG185">Toleman</a> and <a href="/wiki/Niki_Lauda" title="Niki Lauda">Niki Lauda</a> in the second McLaren completed the top ten.
</p><p>At the start of the race, Senna made a superb start and passed Prost, the slow-starting Piquet and Rosberg to take the lead, while Mansell moved up to third and de Cesaris to fifth. Behind them, four cars were eliminated at the first corner, Copse, as <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Tambay" title="Patrick Tambay">Patrick Tambay</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Renault_RE60" title="Renault RE60">Renault</a> lost control and was hit by the second Ferrari of <a href="/wiki/Stefan_Johansson" title="Stefan Johansson">Stefan Johansson</a>, which also caused the <a href="/wiki/Osella" title="Osella">Osella</a> of <a href="/wiki/Piercarlo_Ghinzani" title="Piercarlo Ghinzani">Piercarlo Ghinzani</a> and the <a href="/wiki/RAM_Racing" title="RAM Racing">RAM</a> of <a href="/wiki/Philippe_Alliot" title="Philippe Alliot">Philippe Alliot</a> to crash into each other.
</p><p>Back at the front, de Cesaris passed Prost and then Mansell to be third, before Prost repassed the Ligier on lap 9. Prost later admitted he was apprehensive about being behind the Ligier thanks to the unpredictable de Cesaris' reputation for crashing. Mansell dropped out on lap 18 with a clutch failure, before team-mate Rosberg suffered a broken exhaust four laps later. Senna continued to lead, with Prost now second and Lauda up to third ahead of de Cesaris, Piquet and Alboreto. Prost and Lauda's McLaren-TAG/Porsche MP4/2B's performance through high-speed corners was far superior to that of any other car competing that year – something that was a feature of all of the original Silverstone's 8 corners (10 if the 3-turn sequence of the Woodcote chicane is counted).
</p><p>Alboreto passed Piquet for fifth, before moving up to fourth on lap 42 when de Cesaris's clutch failed. By then, Senna and Prost were running nose-to-tail. The Frenchman finally passed the Brazilian on lap 58, Lauda's electrics failing on the same lap. Then Senna's fuel injection failed, promoting Alboreto to second, while <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Laffite" title="Jacques Laffite">Jacques Laffite</a> in the second Ligier moved up to third ahead of Piquet.
</p><p>The chequered flag was erroneously shown at the end of lap 65, one lap early, with Prost having lapped the entire field. Halfway around what was meant to have been the final lap, Laffite ran out of fuel, which annoyed Piquet – he would have thus taken the final podium place had the error not been made. The final points were taken by <a href="/wiki/Derek_Warwick" title="Derek Warwick">Derek Warwick</a> in the second Renault and <a href="/wiki/Marc_Surer" title="Marc Surer">Marc Surer</a> in the second Brabham.
</p><p>In the Drivers' Championship, Prost moved to within two points of Alboreto, while in the Constructors' Championship McLaren moved up to second, 18 points behind Ferrari.
</p>
