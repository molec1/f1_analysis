<p>The <b>2006 Turkish Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Auto_racing" title="Auto racing">motor race</a>, held on 27 August 2006. The 58-lap race, which was the fourteenth round of the <a href="/wiki/2006_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2006 Formula One season">2006 Formula One season</a>, and the second <a href="/wiki/Turkish_Grand_Prix" title="Turkish Grand Prix">Turkish Grand Prix</a>, was held at <a href="/wiki/Istanbul_Park" class="mw-redirect" title="Istanbul Park">Istanbul Park</a> in <a href="/wiki/Tuzla,_Istanbul" title="Tuzla, Istanbul">Tuzla</a>, <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>. <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a>, driving for the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> team, took his first pole position, and then his first race victory. <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>, who drove for <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a> finished the race in second position and Massa's teammate <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> occupied the final position on the podium. The race was also the first Grand Prix meeting for future quadruple world champion <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a>, who was assigned a Friday test driver role for the <a href="/wiki/BMW_Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="BMW Sauber">BMW Sauber</a> team. Vettel received a $1,000 fine for speeding in the pit-lane only 6 seconds after he got out onto the track.
</p><p>As a consequence of the race, Fernando Alonso extended his lead in the Drivers' Championship to 12 points from Michael Schumacher. With victory, Felipe Massa retained third place in the standings, and despite closing the gap between himself and Schumacher to 34 points, it was still near impossible for him to surpass his teammate's points total. In the Constructors' Championship, Ferrari closed the gap to leaders Renault to just two points. <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> remained in third position, 69 points behind the top two teams.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2006_Turkish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2006_Turkish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Prior to the Grand Prix weekend, <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> led the <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> World Drivers' Championship on 100 points. He held a ten-point lead over his main rival <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>, despite retiring from the preceding race in <a href="/wiki/2006_Hungarian_Grand_Prix" title="2006 Hungarian Grand Prix">Hungary</a>. <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> teammates, Schumacher and <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> lay second and third in the standings on 90 and 52 points respectively, ahead of the second Renault of <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> and the <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> of <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a>, both on 49 points.
</p><p>Renault led the Constructors' Championship with 149 points, seven clear of Ferrari, while McLaren were in third position with 89 points. They were ahead of <a href="/wiki/Honda_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Honda F1">Honda F1</a>, who sat in fourth with 52 after picking up their first and to be only victory since their return, three weeks before in Hungary. BMW Sauber completed the top five with 26 points.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Friday_drivers">Friday drivers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2006_Turkish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Friday drivers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

These drivers drove on Friday but did not compete in qualifying or the race.

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice_and_qualifying">Practice and qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2006_Turkish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Practice and qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Jarno_Trulli_2006_Turkey.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/17/Jarno_Trulli_2006_Turkey.jpg/220px-Jarno_Trulli_2006_Turkey.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/17/Jarno_Trulli_2006_Turkey.jpg/330px-Jarno_Trulli_2006_Turkey.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/17/Jarno_Trulli_2006_Turkey.jpg/440px-Jarno_Trulli_2006_Turkey.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1024" data-file-height="685" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Jarno_Trulli_2006_Turkey.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> qualified thirteenth, but started in twelfth because of his teammate's ten place grid penalty.</div></div></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> set the fastest time in the first free practice session, despite only completing five laps; ahead of <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a>, who were both just within half a second of Räikkönen's time. In the bottom half of the order, <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> finished the session 17th and <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> 23rd. Neither of the <a href="/wiki/Williams_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams F1">Williams</a> left the garage during the session.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> impressed many as he set the fastest time in the second free practice session, on his first Grand Prix weekend appearance.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The following day in qualifying, however, it was Felipe Massa who set the fastest time, taking the first pole position of his career, ahead of Schumacher by one tenth of a second. The two Renaults occupied the next row with Alonso in front of Fisichella again. Fifth spot was initially taken up by <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Toyota_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Toyota F1">Toyota</a>, before he was demoted to fifteenth for a gearbox change. This was also true for <a href="/wiki/Christijan_Albers" title="Christijan Albers">Christijan Albers</a>, who was taken from a good sixteenth place qualifying for the <a href="/wiki/Midland_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Midland F1">Midland</a> team, to the back of the grid. Only four hundredths of a second separated <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a>, Button and Räikkönen qualified from sixth to eighth before all moving up a place after Ralf Schumacher's penalty. <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> completed the top ten fastest times, with <a href="/wiki/Christian_Klien" title="Christian Klien">Christian Klien</a> completing the top ten on the starting grid.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2006_Turkish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Kimi_Raikkonen_2006_Turkey.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9d/Kimi_Raikkonen_2006_Turkey.jpg/220px-Kimi_Raikkonen_2006_Turkey.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9d/Kimi_Raikkonen_2006_Turkey.jpg/330px-Kimi_Raikkonen_2006_Turkey.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9d/Kimi_Raikkonen_2006_Turkey.jpg/440px-Kimi_Raikkonen_2006_Turkey.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1024" data-file-height="685" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Kimi_Raikkonen_2006_Turkey.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> retired early due to collision damage.</div></div></div>
<p>The start of the race was affected by a multi-car accident entering the first corner. Massa, Michael Schumacher, Alonso and Fisichella all battled for position entering the first corner, with Fisichella spinning, causing a <a href="/wiki/Chain_reaction" title="Chain reaction">chain reaction</a> behind him. During the chain reaction, <a href="/wiki/Scott_Speed" title="Scott Speed">Scott Speed</a> collided with the McLaren of Räikkönen, causing one of Räikkönen's rear tyres to puncture. Räikkönen pitted for a new set of tyres, but soon faced a similar problem due to damaged bodywork; because of this he went straight off the outside of turn 4 and into a barrier, on the next lap. <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a>, Fisichella, Speed and Ralf Schumacher all needed repairs from the incident, and the <a href="/wiki/MF1_Racing" class="mw-redirect" title="MF1 Racing">Midland</a> of <a href="/wiki/Tiago_Monteiro" title="Tiago Monteiro">Tiago Monteiro</a> was forced to retire.
</p><p>On lap 13, <a href="/wiki/Vitantonio_Liuzzi" title="Vitantonio Liuzzi">Vitantonio Liuzzi</a>, who had climbed to seventh place, lost control entering the first turn and spun, stalling the engine. The car was left stranded on the racing line at the exit of the corner, and the <a href="/wiki/Safety_Car" class="mw-redirect" title="Safety Car">Safety Car</a> was deployed. Massa, Schumacher, Alonso, Button, Kubica and Webber all came in to make pit stops whilst the Safety Car circulated. The first four drivers stayed at the front, but Webber and Kubica dropped to near the back of the pack. Alonso, however, was able to jump Schumacher, as his title rival was forced to 'stack' behind Massa before the mechanics could set to work on his car. The final 15 laps of the race were focused on Alonso and Michael Schumacher, who fought for second place behind Massa as part of their championship battle. Schumacher pursued Alonso for the last laps, but was unable to catch Alonso until the final corners of the final lap. Schumacher was right on the gearbox of Alonso exiting the last corner, but did not get past.
</p><p>Thus, Massa took his first career Formula One victory, making it the second Grand Prix in a row with a first-time winner, following Button's win in Hungary three weeks earlier. It was the first time this had happened since 2003, when Räikkönen and Fisichella won their first victories at the consecutive <a href="/wiki/2003_Malaysian_Grand_Prix" title="2003 Malaysian Grand Prix">Malaysian</a> and <a href="/wiki/2003_Brazilian_Grand_Prix" title="2003 Brazilian Grand Prix">Brazilian</a> Grands Prix. Drivers' Championship rivals Alonso and Schumacher completed the podium. Button, de la Rosa, Fisichella, Ralf Schumacher and <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> completed the points-scorers and the cars on the lead lap.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2006_Turkish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The podium display after the race caused controversy when winner Felipe Massa received the trophy from <a href="/wiki/Mehmet_Ali_Talat" title="Mehmet Ali Talat">Mehmet Ali Talat</a>, who was referred to as the "President of the <a href="/wiki/Turkish_Republic_of_Northern_Cyprus" class="mw-redirect" title="Turkish Republic of Northern Cyprus">Turkish Republic of Northern Cyprus</a>". This breakaway area of the island of <a href="/wiki/Cyprus" title="Cyprus">Cyprus</a> is only recognized by <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>. The government of the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Cyprus" class="mw-redirect" title="Republic of Cyprus">Republic of Cyprus</a> filed an official complaint with the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">FIA</a>, the body governing the <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> world championship. After investigating the incident, the FIA fined the organizers of the Grand Prix $5 million on September 19, 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
