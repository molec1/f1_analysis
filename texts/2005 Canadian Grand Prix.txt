<p>The <b>2005 Canadian Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 12 June 2005 at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_Gilles_Villeneuve" title="Circuit Gilles Villeneuve">Circuit Gilles Villeneuve</a> in <a href="/wiki/Montreal" title="Montreal">Montreal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">Quebec</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>. The 70-lap race was the eighth round of the <a href="/wiki/2005_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2005 Formula One season">2005 Formula One season</a>, the 43rd running of the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Grand_Prix" title="Canadian Grand Prix">Canadian Grand Prix</a>, and the 37th running as a round of the World Championship. It set a ratings record and was the most watched F1 race in history. It was also the first of two consecutive <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North American</a> rounds. The race was won by <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a>, taking his third win from four races. The two <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> cars completed the podium, with <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> in second place and <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> in third. Both <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renaults</a> failed to finish the race, but the team maintained their lead in the Constructors' Championship; their driver, <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> also kept his lead in the Drivers' Championship, despite the gap between himself and nearest rival Räikkönen closing by ten points.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2005_Canadian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice_and_qualifying">Practice and qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2005_Canadian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Practice and qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Christian_Klien" title="Christian Klien">Christian Klien</a> returned for the first of four confirmed races in the <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a> car, with <a href="/wiki/United_States_of_America" class="mw-redirect" title="United States of America">American</a> <a href="/wiki/Scott_Speed" title="Scott Speed">Scott Speed</a> filling in the Friday testing role.
</p><p>After retiring on the last lap of the <a href="/wiki/2005_European_Grand_Prix" title="2005 European Grand Prix">European Grand Prix</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> was the 11th driver on the track in qualifying, and he managed to line up 7th on the grid. <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> suffered a <a href="/wiki/Gearbox" class="mw-redirect" title="Gearbox">gearbox</a> failure before the start of his qualifying lap, and was forced to abort his run. <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> managed to take <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> second, and both <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a> drivers on the second row.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2005_Canadian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Pedro_de_la_Rosa_2005_Canadian_GP.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2e/Pedro_de_la_Rosa_2005_Canadian_GP.jpg/220px-Pedro_de_la_Rosa_2005_Canadian_GP.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2e/Pedro_de_la_Rosa_2005_Canadian_GP.jpg/330px-Pedro_de_la_Rosa_2005_Canadian_GP.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2e/Pedro_de_la_Rosa_2005_Canadian_GP.jpg/440px-Pedro_de_la_Rosa_2005_Canadian_GP.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2048" data-file-height="1371" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Pedro_de_la_Rosa_2005_Canadian_GP.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Pedro_de_la_Rosa" title="Pedro de la Rosa">Pedro de la Rosa</a> driving for <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> during Friday practice as third driver.</div></div></div>
<p>Barrichello, who would have been last on the grid, decided to start the race from pitlane, putting extra fuel in his car. At race start, both Button and Schumacher got away slowly, and <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> took off well to move into first and second by the first corner. The <a href="/wiki/Team_McLaren" class="mw-redirect" title="Team McLaren">McLarens</a> of <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a> and Räikkönen were also able to pass Schumacher, moving into 4th and 5th behind Button. Further back in the field, <a href="/wiki/British_American_Racing" title="British American Racing">BAR</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Takuma_Sato" title="Takuma Sato">Takuma Sato</a> made a small mistake, but did not lose a large amount of time. On the second lap, Fisichella was able to widen the gap back to Alonso, while local driver <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a> was forced to pit for a new front wing, leaving him at the back of the field. As the race continued, the Renault's pulled further away from the pack, and Barrichello was constantly moving up the field, up to 15th position by the end of lap 8. <a href="/wiki/Narain_Karthikeyan" title="Narain Karthikeyan">Narain Karthikeyan</a> had a spin after a mistake in turn 1, allowing <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a> teammate <a href="/wiki/Tiago_Monteiro" title="Tiago Monteiro">Tiago Monteiro</a> past.
</p><p>Michael Schumacher became the first driver to take his scheduled pitstop, refuelling on lap 12 to drop from 6th to 12th position. Three laps later, Button and <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> showed their hands, also entering the pits for more fuel. Button dropped from 3rd to 7th, leaving the two Renaults to lead from the two McLarens. On lap 21, the first of the two-stoppers pitted, with <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> and Klien all entering pitlane. The following lap, Sato entered his garage, apparently retiring from the race with gearbox trouble. Lap 24 saw the first of the leaders pit, with championship contenders Alonso and Räikkönen refuelling, before both of their teammates pitted on the following lap. On exiting the pits, Montoya went too fast on cold <a href="/wiki/Tire" title="Tire">tyres</a>, leaving the track and allowing Alonso to retain 2nd position. Karthikeyan hit the wall at the back end of the circuit, forcing him to retire from the race. <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a>, up to 7th position through a very long first stint, pitted on lap 28, and fed back into the race 9th, just ahead of Massa, Heidfeld and Ralf Schumacher. On the following lap, Webber went wide onto the grass, and in attempting to make up time, braked too late into the hairpin, running very wide and losing 3 positions. Barrichello became the final driver to pit on lap 31, dropping from 8th to 12th.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Lap4_Canada2005.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/14/Lap4_Canada2005.jpg/220px-Lap4_Canada2005.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="150" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/14/Lap4_Canada2005.jpg/330px-Lap4_Canada2005.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/14/Lap4_Canada2005.jpg/440px-Lap4_Canada2005.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2048" data-file-height="1393" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Lap4_Canada2005.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The battle for fifth place continues on lap 4 as <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> leads <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>, and just behind <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> attempts a move on <a href="/wiki/Takuma_Sato" title="Takuma Sato">Takuma Sato</a> for seventh.</div></div></div>
<p>After following close behind for several laps, and appearing to be quicker, Alonso passed teammate Fisichella for the lead on lap 33, but Fisichella obviously had a technical problem, as Montoya easily passed him on the same lap. Fisichella entered the pits to retire at the end of the lap, with hydraulic system problems. On lap 34, both Button and Michael Schumacher took their second stops, retaining 4th and 5th positions. On lap 35, Alonso led from Montoya, Räikkönen, Button, Schumacher and <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a>. However, on lap 39, Alonso ran wide at the exit of turn 4 and hit the outside wall, damaging his <a href="/wiki/Suspension_(vehicle)" class="mw-redirect" title="Suspension (vehicle)">suspension</a> and forcing him to retire from the race. This left Montoya in the lead, followed by Räikkönen. After following Massa for several laps, Heidfeld's <a href="/wiki/BMW_in_Formula_One" title="BMW in Formula One">BMW</a> <a href="/wiki/Engine" title="Engine">engine</a> failed, allowing Ralf Schumacher into 7th and Webber to 8th. Takuma Sato rejoined the race 24 laps behind, after the B.A.R team fixed his car in order to gain him a slightly better starting position in qualifying for the following <a href="/wiki/2005_United_States_Grand_Prix" title="2005 United States Grand Prix">grand prix</a>.
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Fisichella_%2B_Alonso_2005_Canada.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d4/Fisichella_%2B_Alonso_2005_Canada.jpg/220px-Fisichella_%2B_Alonso_2005_Canada.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d4/Fisichella_%2B_Alonso_2005_Canada.jpg/330px-Fisichella_%2B_Alonso_2005_Canada.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d4/Fisichella_%2B_Alonso_2005_Canada.jpg/440px-Fisichella_%2B_Alonso_2005_Canada.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1024" data-file-height="683" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Fisichella_%2B_Alonso_2005_Canada.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The two <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a> drivers led the race in its early stages, with <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> ahead of <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>. Both eventually failed to finish.</div></div></div>
<p>On lap 49, the gap from Montoya to Räikkönen was reduced to around 3 seconds. Button, under pressure from Michael Schumacher, made a mistake at the final chicane, hitting the inside kerb heavily, which forced him into the "wall of champions" on the outside. Button was forced to retire from the race, handing 3rd position to Schumacher, and causing the deployment of the <a href="/wiki/Safety_car" title="Safety car">safety car</a> as Button's car was in a dangerous position. Every car took the opportunity to pit besides Montoya, who was forced to stay out an extra lap while Räikkönen was refuelled. On exiting the pits, Montoya re-entered the race under the safety car, which had just led the train of cars through turns 1 and 2. Montoya slipped into 2nd, slightly ahead of <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> on track, although the Red Bull Racing driver had been lapped. Before the restart, Räikkönen led from Montoya, Michael Schumacher, Trulli, Webber, Massa, Barrichello and Ralf Schumacher. As the safety car re-entered the pits, Webber made a mistake into turn 1, allowing both Massa and Barrichello through, although the <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a> driver was forced wide and Barrichello moved into 5th. Soon after, it was announced that Montoya was under investigation by the stewards, and he was <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_regulations#Flags" title="Formula One regulations">black-flagged</a> (disqualified) from the race for exiting the pits while a red light was showing (as the safety car was on the pit straight), making it his 2nd consecutive disqualification in Canada.
</p><p>As the race entered the closing stages, Michael Schumacher closed in on Räikkönen, who was troubled by a misaligned <a href="/wiki/Steering_wheel" title="Steering wheel">steering wheel</a>. Trulli appeared to be having trouble with his brakes, and retired when they failed, leaving Barrichello in a comfortable 3rd position. However, Barrichello made a small error, allowing Massa to close up behind, but Webber was also close to Massa. Meanwhile, Takuma Sato appeared to have a mechanical failure which forced him to spin at the hairpin, before the rear of his car caught fire due to overheating. Räikkönen was able to retain his 1-second gap back to Schumacher to take the race win, with Barrichello more than 30 seconds behind in 3rd position. Webber was close to Massa into the final corner, but was unable to pass, leaving Massa to take a valuable 4th position and 5 championship points. Completing the pointscorers were Ralf Schumacher, Coulthard and Klien. The result meant Räikkönen reduced the gap in the championship standings to just 22 points, and the double-podium for Ferrari allowed them to close up in the constructors championship.
</p>
