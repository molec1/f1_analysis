<p>The <b>1999 Austrian Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 25 July 1999 at the <a href="/wiki/A1-Ring" class="mw-redirect" title="A1-Ring">A1-Ring</a> in <a href="/wiki/Spielberg,_Styria" title="Spielberg, Styria">Spielberg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Austria" title="Austria">Austria</a>. It was the ninth race of the <a href="/wiki/1999_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="1999 Formula One season">1999 Formula One season</a>.
</p><p>After <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> broke his leg in the previous race at Silverstone, <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>'s title attention turned to his teammate <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a>. <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> nudged teammate <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> off the track on the first lap, pushing Häkkinen to the back. When Coulthard failed to hold off Irvine during the sole round of pitstops, the man from <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">Northern Ireland</a> wrapped up his 2nd <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> win. Häkkinen fought through the field to finish 3rd. It was Ferrari's first win in Austria since <a href="/wiki/1970_Austrian_Grand_Prix" title="1970 Austrian Grand Prix">1970</a>. <a href="/wiki/Mika_Salo" title="Mika Salo">Mika Salo</a> substituted for Schumacher, while <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Diniz" title="Pedro Diniz">Pedro Diniz</a> scored his last ever world championship points at this race.
</p>
