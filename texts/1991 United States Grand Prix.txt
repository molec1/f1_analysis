<p>The <b>1991 United States Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>XXVIII Iceberg United States Grand Prix</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on March 10, 1991 in <a href="/wiki/Phoenix,_Arizona" title="Phoenix, Arizona">Phoenix, Arizona</a>. It was the first race of the <a href="/wiki/1991_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1991 Formula One World Championship">1991 Formula One World Championship</a>. The 81-lap race was won from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> by <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/6" title="McLaren MP4/6">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Honda_in_Formula_One" title="Honda in Formula One">Honda</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> second in a <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_642" title="Ferrari 642">Ferrari</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a> third in a <a href="/wiki/Benetton_B190" title="Benetton B190">Benetton</a>-<a href="/wiki/Ford_Motor_Company" title="Ford Motor Company">Ford</a>.
</p><p>The race marked the respective debuts of future double World Champion <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a> team. It was also the first F1 World Championship race in which ten points were awarded for a win rather than nine, as part of a revised scoring system introduced for 1991. However, it was also to be the last United States Grand Prix until <a href="/wiki/2000_United_States_Grand_Prix" title="2000 United States Grand Prix">2000</a>, due to poor attendances.
</p><p>In the two previous years, the championship had been decided when Senna and Prost tangled at <a href="/wiki/Suzuka_Circuit" class="mw-redirect" title="Suzuka Circuit">Suzuka</a>. In <a href="/wiki/1989_Japanese_Grand_Prix" title="1989 Japanese Grand Prix">1989</a>, their collision as team-mates secured Prost's third World Championship; in <a href="/wiki/1990_Japanese_Grand_Prix" title="1990 Japanese Grand Prix">1990</a>, with Prost driving for Ferrari and still in title contention, it handed Senna his second crown. Controversy regarding the nature of the 1990 incident had created great anticipation for the rematch.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1991_United_States_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-qualifying_report">Pre-qualifying report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1991_United_States_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Pre-qualifying report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Several teams were required to participate in the Friday morning pre-qualifying sessions during 1991, in order to reduce the field to thirty cars for the main qualifying sessions on Friday afternoon and Saturday. At the midway point of the season, the pre-qualifying group was to be reassessed, with the more successful teams (over a twelve-month period consisting of the second half of 1990 and the first half of 1991) being allowed to avoid pre-qualifying thereafter, and less successful teams being required to pre-qualify from mid-season onwards.
</p><p>Eight cars were to take part in pre-qualifying sessions during the first half of the 1991 season, after 1990 ended with no pre-qualifying sessions at the last two Grands Prix, due to the withdrawal of the <a href="/wiki/EuroBrun" title="EuroBrun">EuroBrun</a> and <a href="/wiki/Life_Racing_Engines" title="Life Racing Engines">Life</a> teams. Pre-qualifying was necessary again in 1991 due to the arrival of two new entrants: <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Modena_(racing_team)" title="Modena (racing team)">Modena</a>, which were entirely new teams, running two cars each. Another new name, <a href="/wiki/Fondmetal" title="Fondmetal">Fondmetal</a>, was essentially a continuation of the defunct <a href="/wiki/Osella" title="Osella">Osella</a> team and was running a single car. Also involved in pre-qualifying were <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Coloni" title="Scuderia Coloni">Coloni</a>, running one car, and <a href="/wiki/BMS_Scuderia_Italia" title="BMS Scuderia Italia">Scuderia Italia</a>, running two cars. These were the two least successful surviving teams over the past two half seasons. The fastest four cars would go through to the main qualifying sessions.<sup id="cite_ref-Walker_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Walker-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Jordan had signed veteran Italian driver <a href="/wiki/Andrea_de_Cesaris" title="Andrea de Cesaris">Andrea de Cesaris</a>, plus last season's Coloni driver <a href="/wiki/Bertrand_Gachot" title="Bertrand Gachot">Bertrand Gachot</a>, who had much experience in pre-qualifying sessions. Their car was the all-new <a href="/wiki/Jordan_191" title="Jordan 191">191</a> powered by a <a href="/wiki/Cosworth" title="Cosworth">Cosworth</a> V8 engine. Modena's drivers were Italian <a href="/wiki/Nicola_Larini" title="Nicola Larini">Nicola Larini</a>, another driver well used to pre-qualifying, and Belgian debutant <a href="/wiki/Eric_van_de_Poele" title="Eric van de Poele">Eric van de Poele</a>. They would be driving the <a href="/wiki/Lamborghini_V12" title="Lamborghini V12">Lamborghini V12</a>-engined <a href="/wiki/Lambo_291" title="Lambo 291">Lambo 291</a>, so named for the team's strong <a href="/wiki/Lamborghini" title="Lamborghini">Lamborghini</a> connections.<sup id="cite_ref-Walker_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Walker-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Fondmetal had retained the French Osella driver <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Grouillard" title="Olivier Grouillard">Olivier Grouillard</a>, and were starting the season with the Cosworth V8-powered <a href="/w/index.php?title=Osella_FA1M&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Osella FA1M (page does not exist)">Fondmetal FA1M-E</a>, effectively last year's Osella, which itself was an update of their 1989 car.<sup id="cite_ref-Hodges_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hodges-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Coloni had brought the <a href="/wiki/Coloni_C4" title="Coloni C4">C4</a>, a modified version of the <a href="/wiki/Coloni_C3" title="Coloni C3">C3</a> used in 1989 and 1990,<sup id="cite_ref-Hodges_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hodges-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> which also had a Cosworth V8 engine. Their driver was the 1990 <a href="/wiki/British_Formula_3000" title="British Formula 3000">British Formula 3000</a> champion <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Chaves" title="Pedro Chaves">Pedro Chaves</a>, from Portugal. Scuderia Italia were campaigning a <a href="/wiki/Dallara" title="Dallara">Dallara</a> chassis as usual, the all-new <a href="/wiki/Dallara_F191" title="Dallara F191">F191</a>, powered by <a href="/wiki/Judd_(engine)" title="Judd (engine)">Judd</a> V10 engines. The team retained Italian <a href="/wiki/Emanuele_Pirro" title="Emanuele Pirro">Emanuele Pirro</a>, and hired Finnish driver <a href="/wiki/JJ_Lehto" title="JJ Lehto">JJ Lehto</a>, who had driven for <a href="/wiki/Onyx_(racing_team)" class="mw-redirect" title="Onyx (racing team)">Onyx</a> in 1990.<sup id="cite_ref-Walker_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Walker-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In pre-qualifying in Phoenix, the Dallaras were easily the fastest cars in the session, with Pirro half a second faster than Lehto. Nearly two seconds slower in third was Larini in the Modena Lambo, and the fourth pre-qualifier was Gachot in the Jordan. Missing out in fifth place was de Cesaris in the other Jordan, who made a gear selection error on a faster lap, just ahead of Chaves, who crashed the Coloni during the session. Grouillard was seventh in the Fondmetal, complaining of a lack of grip, but bottom of the time sheets, nearly five seconds adrift of Grouillard, was van de Poele in the other Lambo. The Belgian had encountered a protester on the track during one run, after which his engine failed.<sup id="cite_ref-Walker_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Walker-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying_report">Qualifying report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1991_United_States_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Qualifying report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:AlainProst_Ferrari_1991.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/94/AlainProst_Ferrari_1991.jpg/220px-AlainProst_Ferrari_1991.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="330" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/94/AlainProst_Ferrari_1991.jpg/330px-AlainProst_Ferrari_1991.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/94/AlainProst_Ferrari_1991.jpg/440px-AlainProst_Ferrari_1991.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1250" data-file-height="1875" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:AlainProst_Ferrari_1991.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> in his <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_642" title="Ferrari 642">Ferrari 642</a> on the opening lap.</div></div></div>
<p>Prior to arriving in Phoenix, the 1991 McLaren chassis had had only had one brief test session, and the two race cars were completed about 4 am Friday, six hours before the first practice session.  Working to prepare the new car, <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> claimed he had never gotten totally comfortable with the increasing complexity of the sport since helping <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Lotus</a> introduce the first active suspension car in <a href="/wiki/1987_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="1987 Formula One season">1987</a>, and he still found it hard to embrace the huge role of computers in achieving a proper setup. "Friday, to understand and interpret things properly, I worked with the engineers into the evening," Senna said. "It has been a long time since I did that. The engineers and I talked our way around the circuit, then we compared this with what the computer predicted. It was great because the computer confirmed almost everything, and it also showed where there was room for improvement."<i></i>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferraris</a> of Alain Prost and <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a> were expected to be the strongest team, but their V-12s and 7-speed semi-automatic gearboxes were not well suited to the tight turns and short straights of the street circuit. As it turned out, they were not the only ones plagued by gearbox problems throughout the race. To add insult to injury, Alesi, who had clinched the fastest time on Friday, ended his qualifying session clipping the tire barrier with his rear right wheel, ensuring he would qualify no higher than fifth place.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1991_United_States_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_report">Race report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1991_United_States_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Race report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Start_1991_USA.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/43/Start_1991_USA.jpg/220px-Start_1991_USA.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/43/Start_1991_USA.jpg/330px-Start_1991_USA.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/43/Start_1991_USA.jpg/440px-Start_1991_USA.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2302" data-file-height="1537" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Start_1991_USA.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The start of the race, with <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> leading the field.</div></div></div>
<p>On Sunday, Prost began his second season at Ferrari alongside Senna on the front row. At the start, he fell in behind the Brazilian, with <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> slipping ahead of <a href="/wiki/Riccardo_Patrese" title="Riccardo Patrese">Riccardo Patrese</a>. Alesi and <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> followed, then <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a>, <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Moreno" title="Roberto Moreno">Roberto Moreno</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stefano_Modena" title="Stefano Modena">Stefano Modena</a> and <a href="/wiki/Emanuele_Pirro" title="Emanuele Pirro">Emanuele Pirro</a>. A lap later, Alesi, in his first race for Ferrari, swept past Patrese, but by then Senna was pulling away. After ten laps, he had a lead of ten seconds over Prost.
</p><p>Behind Senna, Patrese was involved in two successive three-way battles. First, after getting back by Alesi for fourth on lap 16, Patrese closed on Mansell who was immediately behind Prost. By lap 22, Patrese was close enough to attack his <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a> team-mate but overshot onto the escape road, as Mansell swerved to avoid him. Upon rejoining, Patrese quickly latched onto Alesi and Berger, as the new three-car train now covered fourth through sixth places. Patrese had gotten past Berger when, suddenly, two of the top six runners retired on consecutive laps. On lap 35, Mansell pulled over when the Williams's new semi-automatic gearbox failed, and on the next time around, Berger's race ended as well.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Jean_alesi_1991.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/58/Jean_alesi_1991.jpg/220px-Jean_alesi_1991.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="145" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/58/Jean_alesi_1991.jpg/330px-Jean_alesi_1991.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/58/Jean_alesi_1991.jpg/440px-Jean_alesi_1991.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4384" data-file-height="2880" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Jean_alesi_1991.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Prost's teammate <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a> is pursued by <a href="/wiki/Riccardo_Patrese" title="Riccardo Patrese">Riccardo Patrese</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW14" title="Williams FW14">Williams FW14</a>.</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Ayrton_Senna_1991_USA_3.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b9/Ayrton_Senna_1991_USA_3.jpg/220px-Ayrton_Senna_1991_USA_3.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b9/Ayrton_Senna_1991_USA_3.jpg/330px-Ayrton_Senna_1991_USA_3.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b9/Ayrton_Senna_1991_USA_3.jpg/440px-Ayrton_Senna_1991_USA_3.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3575" data-file-height="2383" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Ayrton_Senna_1991_USA_3.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> took a comfortable win in his <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/6" title="McLaren MP4/6">McLaren MP4/6</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>Patrese passed Alesi for the second time, and Alesi pitted for new <a href="/wiki/Goodyear_Tire_and_Rubber_Company" title="Goodyear Tire and Rubber Company">Goodyear</a> tires on lap 43. He rejoined in seventh. Three laps later, Prost was being hounded by Patrese, and he also pitted, ceding second spot to the Williams. When the Ferrari crew had problems changing his right rear tire, the Frenchman dropped to seventh behind Modena's <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_Racing" title="Tyrrell Racing">Tyrrell</a>-Honda.
</p><p>On lap 48, Senna pitted without giving up the lead. Like Mansell, Patrese was having problems with the gearbox in his Williams, and when it selected neutral midway through Turn Seven, it caused him to spin out of second place. The car stopped on the outside of the track, perpendicular to the racing line. Piquet and <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> (making his F1 debut) barely managed to avoid him as they passed, but before Patrese could get out of the car, Moreno, in the second Benetton, went straight across the bow of the Williams, removing the nose of Patrese's car and the right front wheel of the Benetton. Neither driver was injured.  Patrese and Moreno's cars remained on the course throughout the race, and <a href="/wiki/Bertrand_Gachot" title="Bertrand Gachot">Bertrand Gachot</a>, driving the first race for the new <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan team</a> and challenging <a href="/wiki/Satoru_Nakajima" title="Satoru Nakajima">Satoru Nakajima</a>, spun later after swerving to avoid them.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Bertrand_Gachot_1991_USA.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/61/Bertrand_Gachot_1991_USA.jpg/220px-Bertrand_Gachot_1991_USA.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/61/Bertrand_Gachot_1991_USA.jpg/330px-Bertrand_Gachot_1991_USA.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/61/Bertrand_Gachot_1991_USA.jpg/440px-Bertrand_Gachot_1991_USA.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1229" data-file-height="819" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Bertrand_Gachot_1991_USA.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The race marked the debut of the <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a> team in F1. <a href="/wiki/Bertrand_Gachot" title="Bertrand Gachot">Bertrand Gachot</a> was classified tenth after a late-race engine failure prevented him from finishing.</div></div></div>
<p>With Patrese out, Senna led Piquet, who was having to hold off Alesi, by over a minute. Alesi squeezed up to second on lap 53, while four laps later Prost disposed of Modena. Then on lap 70, Prost went from fourth to second in one move at turn five. With Alesi and Piquet running second and third, Piquet's <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a> pulled alongside Alesi– who had set the race's fastest lap while Prost was in the pits– in Turn Four, but could not get by. Down the straight, however, Piquet passed Alesi for second place. Prost also went by his new teammate on the left, and then sliced back to the right, between Alesi and Piquet, beating Piquet to the apex of Turn Five and regaining second place. By now Senna led by 40 seconds.
</p><p>Gearbox troubles dropped Alesi to fifth before he retired less than 10 laps from the finish, leaving third place to Piquet who, like the two Tyrrells following, had never pitted for tires. Martini was pressing <a href="/wiki/Satoru_Nakajima" title="Satoru Nakajima">Satoru Nakajima</a> for fifth, only to have his engine let go after 75 laps. A similar problem removed Gachot from eighth on the same lap, promoting <a href="/wiki/Aguri_Suzuki" title="Aguri Suzuki">Aguri Suzuki</a> to sixth. The race ended a lap short of the planned 82 as the two-hour limit was reached, Senna taking the win having led every lap.
</p><p>After the race, Senna assessed his new car's performance: "I had small problems with the gearbox, and the car balance was not optimum, so it was very difficult to drive. I'm looking forward to running a proper circuit with proper conditions and see what the car can do."
</p>
