<p>The <b>1999 Monaco Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>LVII Grand Prix Automobile de Monaco</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 16 May 1999 at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Monaco" title="Circuit de Monaco">Circuit de Monaco</a> in <a href="/wiki/Monte_Carlo" title="Monte Carlo">Monte Carlo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Monaco" title="Monaco">Monaco</a>. It was the fourth race of the <a href="/wiki/1999_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="1999 Formula One season">1999 Formula One season</a>. The 78-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> after starting from second position. It was Schumacher's 16th win with Ferrari, breaking the record held by <a href="/wiki/Niki_Lauda" title="Niki Lauda">Niki Lauda</a>. His team-mate <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a> finished second with <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> third for the <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> team.
</p><p>The race was Schumacher's second win of the season, his fourth at Monaco, and the result meant that he extended his lead in the Drivers' Championship, to eight points over Irvine and twelve over Häkkinen. Ferrari extended their lead in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a>, twenty-four points ahead of McLaren and twenty-eight ahead of <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a> with 12 races of the season remaining.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1999_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1999_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Grand Prix was contested by eleven teams, each of two drivers.<sup id="cite_ref-entrants_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-entrants-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The teams, also known as <a href="/wiki/Formula_One#Constructors" title="Formula One">constructors</a>, were <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arrows_Grand_Prix_International" title="Arrows Grand Prix International">Arrows</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stewart_Grand_Prix" title="Stewart Grand Prix">Stewart</a>, <a href="/wiki/Prost_Grand_Prix" title="Prost Grand Prix">Prost</a>, <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a> and <a href="/wiki/British_American_Racing" title="British American Racing">BAR</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-entrants_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-entrants-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_tyre" class="mw-redirect" title="Formula One tyre">Tyre</a> supplier <a href="/wiki/Bridgestone" title="Bridgestone">Bridgestone</a> brought four different tyre types to the race: two dry compounds, the extra soft and the soft, and two <a href="/wiki/Rain_tyre" title="Rain tyre">wet-weather compounds</a>, the intermediate and full wet.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Going into the race, Ferrari driver <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> led the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a> with 16 points, ahead of <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a> on 12 points and <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> on 10 points. <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a> was fourth with 10 points while <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> was fifth on 10 points. In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a> Ferrari were leading with 28 points, McLaren and Jordan were second and third with 16 and 13 points respectively, while Williams with 7 and Stewart with 6 points contended for fourth place. Ferrari had so far dominated the championship, winning two out of the three previous races, with Häkkinen winning the <a href="/wiki/1999_Brazilian_Grand_Prix" title="1999 Brazilian Grand Prix">Brazilian Grand Prix</a>. Championship contenders Frentzen and <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> had each gained one second-place finish, and Ralf Schumacher and <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> had achieved third place podium finishes.
</p><p>Following the <a href="/wiki/1999_San_Marino_Grand_Prix" title="1999 San Marino Grand Prix">San Marino Grand Prix</a> on 2 May, several teams conducted testing sessions at circuits around the world. Ferrari and Minardi headed for <a href="/wiki/Fiorano_Circuit" title="Fiorano Circuit">Fiorano</a> where testing for the set-up around the Monaco circuit took place. McLaren and Prost tested at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Nevers_Magny-Cours" title="Circuit de Nevers Magny-Cours">Circuit de Nevers Magny-Cours</a> running over the course of three days, while Prost elected to test for one day. Jordan tested at the <a href="/wiki/Lurcy-L%C3%A9vis" title="Lurcy-Lévis">Lurcy-Lévis</a> test track with driver <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Gilbert-Scott" title="Andrew Gilbert-Scott">Andrew Gilbert-Scott</a> performing aerodynamic mapping runs.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice_and_qualifying">Practice and qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1999_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Practice and qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Mika_Hakkinen_DTM_Norisring_2009.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/11/Mika_Hakkinen_DTM_Norisring_2009.jpg/170px-Mika_Hakkinen_DTM_Norisring_2009.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="253" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/11/Mika_Hakkinen_DTM_Norisring_2009.jpg/255px-Mika_Hakkinen_DTM_Norisring_2009.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/11/Mika_Hakkinen_DTM_Norisring_2009.jpg/340px-Mika_Hakkinen_DTM_Norisring_2009.jpg 2x" data-file-width="516" data-file-height="769" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Mika_Hakkinen_DTM_Norisring_2009.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Häkkinen achieved his fourth pole position in four races, and the 14th in his career.</div></div></div>
<p>Four practice sessions were held before the race; two one-hour sessions on Thursday and two 45 minutes sessions on Saturday.<sup id="cite_ref-faq_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-faq-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The Friday sessions were held in dry and cloudy conditions. Irvine was the fastest driver in the first session, with a time of 1:18.910 that was less than six-tenths of a second faster than Michael Schumacher. Jordan driver <a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a> was less than two-tenths of a second off Michael Schumacher's pace, with Barrichello, <a href="/wiki/Alessandro_Zanardi" class="mw-redirect" title="Alessandro Zanardi">Alessandro Zanardi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a> rounding out the top six.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In the second practice session, Michael Schumacher was fastest with a time of 1:22.718, ahead of Häkkinen, <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Panis" title="Olivier Panis">Olivier Panis</a>, Irvine, <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> and Coulthard.
</p><p>Saturday's afternoon qualifying session lasted for an hour. During this session, the <a href="/wiki/107%25_rule" title="107% rule">107% rule</a> was in effect, which necessitated each driver set a time within 107% of the quickest lap to qualify for the race. Each driver was limited to twelve laps.<sup id="cite_ref-faq_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-faq-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Häkkinen achieved his fourth pole position of the season, his second at the Circuit de Monaco, with a time of 1:20.547. He was joined on the front row of the grid by Michael Schumacher, who was less than one-tenth of a second behind.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1999_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The conditions for the race were dry with the <a href="/wiki/Air_temperature" class="mw-redirect" title="Air temperature">air temperature</a> 19&#160;°C (66&#160;°F) and the track temperature 34&#160;°C (93&#160;°F).<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The drivers took to the track at 09:30 (<a href="/wiki/Greenwich_Mean_Time" title="Greenwich Mean Time">GMT</a> +1) for a 30-minute warm-up session.<sup id="cite_ref-faq_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-faq-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Both Ferrari drivers maintained their good pace from qualifying; Michael Schumacher set the fastest time, a 1:23.792. Irvine was second in the other Ferrari car. Both McLaren drivers were just off Irvine's pace—Häkkinen ahead of Coulthard—while Hill and Ralf Schumacher rounded out the top six.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Both Ferraris gained a position at the start, with <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> taking the lead from <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a> and <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a>. The order remained this way until lap 35 when Coulthard's car began to slow, eventually causing him to retire. Further bad luck befell <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> a few laps later when Häkkinen went straight on at Mirabeau from oil left after <a href="/wiki/Toranosuke_Takagi" title="Toranosuke Takagi">Toranosuke Takagi</a>'s blown engine. <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a> moved into 2nd place and stayed there.
</p><p>On the podium, the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland" title="Republic of Ireland">Republic of Ireland</a>'s flag was erroneously flown for Irvine instead of the British one.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1999_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The race result left Michael Schumacher extending his lead in the Drivers' Championship with 26 points. Irvine's second place ensured that he maintained second position in the Championship with 18 points, four points ahead of Häkkinen and five ahead of Frentzen. In the Constructors' Championship, Ferrari extended their lead to 44 points, McLaren maintained second with 20 points, with Jordan maintaining third with 16 points, with 12 races of the season remaining.
</p>
