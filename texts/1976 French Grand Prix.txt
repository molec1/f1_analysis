<p>The <b>1976 French Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_Paul_Ricard" title="Circuit Paul Ricard">Circuit Paul Ricard</a> in <a href="/wiki/Le_Castellet,_Var" title="Le Castellet, Var">Le Castellet</a>, near <a href="/wiki/Marseille" title="Marseille">Marseille</a> in <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>, on 4 July 1976. It was the eighth race of the 1976 World Championship of F1 Drivers and the 1976 International Cup for F1 Constructors. It was the 54th <a href="/wiki/French_Grand_Prix" title="French Grand Prix">French Grand Prix</a> and the fourth to be held at Paul Ricard. The race was held over 54 laps of the 5.8-kilometre (3.6&#160;mi) circuit for a race distance of 313 kilometres (194&#160;mi).
</p><p>The race was won by eventual 1976 world champion <a href="/wiki/James_Hunt" title="James Hunt">James Hunt</a> driving a <a href="/wiki/McLaren_M23" title="McLaren M23">McLaren M23</a>. Hunt won by twelve seconds over the <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_P34" title="Tyrrell P34">Tyrrell P34</a> of <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Depailler" title="Patrick Depailler">Patrick Depailler</a>. It was Hunt's second win for the year and his third career Grand Prix victory, although at this stage his appeal against disqualification from the Spanish Grand Prix win had yet to be upheld. Eleven seconds further back in third was <a href="/wiki/John_Watson_(racing_driver)" title="John Watson (racing driver)">John Watson</a> driving a <a href="/wiki/Penske_PC4" title="Penske PC4">Penske PC4</a>. It was the first podium finish for both Watson and Penske, although the result only came after <a href="/wiki/Ronnie_Peterson" title="Ronnie Peterson">Ronnie Peterson</a>'s <a href="/wiki/March_761" class="mw-redirect" title="March 761">March 761</a> stopped with three laps to go and after Watson was disqualified for a rear wing irregularity and subsequently reinstated upon appeal.
</p><p>Fourth place was taken by young Brazilian driver <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Carlos_Pace" class="mw-redirect" title="José Carlos Pace">Carlos Pace</a> driving a <a href="/wiki/Brabham_BT45" title="Brabham BT45">Brabham BT45</a> ahead of <a href="/wiki/Mario_Andretti" title="Mario Andretti">Mario Andretti</a> (<a href="/wiki/Lotus_77" title="Lotus 77">Lotus 77</a>) and the <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_P34" title="Tyrrell P34">Tyrrell P34</a> of <a href="/wiki/Jody_Scheckter" title="Jody Scheckter">Jody Scheckter</a>.
</p><p>Reigning champion <a href="/wiki/Niki_Lauda" title="Niki Lauda">Niki Lauda</a> retired his <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_312T2" class="mw-redirect" title="Ferrari 312T2">Ferrari 312T2</a> with an engine failure while leading. While Hunt would eventually prove to be the challenger to Lauda for the 1976 championship Lauda's points lead at this time was over the Tyrrell pair of <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Depailler" title="Patrick Depailler">Patrick Depailler</a> (26 points) and Scheckter (24 points).
</p>
