<p>The <b>1987 German Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at the <a href="/wiki/Hockenheimring" title="Hockenheimring">Hockenheimring</a> on 26 July 1987. It was the eighth round of the <a href="/wiki/1987_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="1987 Formula One season">1987 Formula One season</a>. It was the 49th <a href="/wiki/German_Grand_Prix" title="German Grand Prix">German Grand Prix</a> and the eleventh to be held at the Hockenheimring. It was held over 44 laps of the seven kilometre circuit for a race distance of 298.760&#160;km (185.812&#160;mi).
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-race">Pre-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1987_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Pre-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the week leading up to the German Grand Prix a number of teams tested at the Hockenheim circuit. During testing <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> had a very lucky escape when he had a rear tyre failure at over 300&#160;km/h (186&#160;mph) on the long straight leading to the Bremsschikane. The rear corner of his <a href="/wiki/Lotus_99T" title="Lotus 99T">Lotus 99T</a> was destroyed and parts were reported to be hard to find as most ended up flying off into the forest. As a result of the crash <a href="/wiki/Goodyear_Tire_and_Rubber_Company" title="Goodyear Tire and Rubber Company">Goodyear</a> took the precaution of immediately flying in new compound tyres from their plant in <a href="/wiki/Akron,_Ohio" title="Akron, Ohio">Akron</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> in time for the Grand Prix weekend.
</p><p>The tyre failure was determined to be the result of Senna picking up a puncture late on his previous lap. However the Lotus' <a href="/wiki/Active_suspension" title="Active suspension">active suspension</a> system compensated for that and kept the car at its correct ride height, masking the problem from Senna. This caused many to question the system as the general belief was that with a passively suspended car Senna would have known he had a puncture and would not have continued to drive at high speed.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1987_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The race was won by eventual 1987 World Champion, <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a> driving a <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW11B" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams FW11B">Williams FW11B</a>. It was his first win of the season and his third win in the German Grand Prix having previously won for <a href="/wiki/Brabham" title="Brabham">Brabham</a> in <a href="/wiki/1981_German_Grand_Prix" title="1981 German Grand Prix">1981</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> in <a href="/wiki/1986_German_Grand_Prix" title="1986 German Grand Prix">the previous year</a>. Piquet won by over a minute and a half from Swedish driver <a href="/wiki/Stefan_Johansson" title="Stefan Johansson">Stefan Johansson</a> driving a <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/3" title="McLaren MP4/3">McLaren MP4/3</a>, who coasted over the finish line on three wheels due to a tyre puncture suffered just past the pits on his last lap. The Swede's second place was the 50th podium finish for the <a href="/wiki/Porsche" title="Porsche">Porsche</a>-designed <a href="/wiki/Techniques_d%27Avant_Garde" title="Techniques d&#39;Avant Garde">TAG</a> turbo engine. Piquet inherited the win after engine failure claimed his team-mate, <a href="/wiki/British_people" title="British people">Briton</a> <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a>, and reigning champion, <a href="/wiki/Frenchman" class="mw-redirect" title="Frenchman">Frenchman</a> <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> (McLaren MP4/3). <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> finished third in his <a href="/wiki/Lotus_99T" title="Lotus 99T">Lotus 99T</a>.
</p><p>Just seven cars were classified at the end of the race, as the long straights took their toll on engine reliability. Naturally aspirated cars finished as high as fourth place with Frenchman <a href="/wiki/Philippe_Streiff" title="Philippe Streiff">Philippe Streiff</a> leading home a team one-two in the <a href="/wiki/Jim_Clark" title="Jim Clark">Jim Clark</a>/<a href="/wiki/Colin_Chapman" title="Colin Chapman">Colin Chapman</a> Trophy standings for <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_Racing" title="Tyrrell Racing">Tyrrell</a> as <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Palmer" title="Jonathan Palmer">Jonathan Palmer</a> finished in fifth place. In sixth was French driver <a href="/wiki/Philippe_Alliot" title="Philippe Alliot">Philippe Alliot</a> driving a <a href="/wiki/Lola_LC87" title="Lola LC87">Lola LC87</a> for the new <a href="/wiki/Larrousse" title="Larrousse">Larrousse</a> team. It was Alliot's second top six finish in Formula One and Larrousse's first world championship point, although the Constructor's Championship point would be credited to the chassis designers, <a href="/wiki/Lola_Cars" title="Lola Cars">Lola Cars</a>.
</p><p>Piquet's win vaulted him into the championship lead for the first time in 1987, putting him four points ahead of Senna and nine ahead of Mansell.
</p>
