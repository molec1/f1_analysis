<p>The <b>2002 Italian Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at <a href="/wiki/Autodromo_Nazionale_di_Monza" title="Autodromo Nazionale di Monza">Monza</a> on 15 September 2002. It was the fifteenth race of the <a href="/wiki/2002_FIA_Formula_One_World_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="2002 FIA Formula One World Championship">2002 FIA Formula One World Championship</a>.
</p><p>The 53-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_F2002" title="Ferrari F2002">Ferrari</a>. Teammate <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> finished second, 0.25 seconds behind, to complete a Ferrari 1-2 on the team's home soil. <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a> finished third in a <a href="/wiki/Jaguar_R3" title="Jaguar R3">Jaguar</a>-<a href="/wiki/Cosworth" title="Cosworth">Cosworth</a>, achieving both his and the Jaguar team's last F1 podium finish.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2002_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2002_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Heading into the Italian Grand Prix, both the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_champions" class="mw-redirect" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; champions">Drivers'</a> and <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championships</a> were already settled, with Ferrari driver <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> having claimed the Drivers' Championship four races earlier in <a href="/wiki/2002_French_Grand_Prix" title="2002 French Grand Prix">France</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-2002points_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2002points-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and Ferrari securing the Constructors' Championship two races later in <a href="/wiki/2002_Hungarian_Grand_Prix" title="2002 Hungarian Grand Prix">Hungary</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2002points_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2002points-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After the <a href="/wiki/2002_Belgian_Grand_Prix" title="2002 Belgian Grand Prix">Belgian Grand Prix</a> on 1 September, all teams (except <a href="/wiki/Arrows_Grand_Prix_International" title="Arrows Grand Prix International">Arrows</a>) conducted mid-season testing at the Autodromo Nazionale di Monza between 3–6 September to prepare for the upcoming race at the circuit. Michael Schumacher was fastest on the first day, ahead of BAR test driver <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Davidson" title="Anthony Davidson">Anthony Davidson</a> which was held in wet weather conditions, with intermittent dry spells.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Ralf Schumacher set the fastest times on the second where the rain continued into the morning before stopping meaning the track dried up as the session progressed.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The first full day of dry weather conditions was held on the third day where <a href="/wiki/Pedro_de_la_Rosa" title="Pedro de la Rosa">Pedro de la Rosa</a> was the fastest driver.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> was the fastest driver on the final day of testing. <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a> caused a <a href="/wiki/Racing_flags#Red_flag" title="Racing flags">red flag</a> to be shown when he lost control of his car at the Lesmo corners, and removed his front wing after colliding with the barrier.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Due to the configuration of the Autodromo Nazionale di Monza, with its high average speed, the teams set up their cars to produce the minimum amount of downforce possible.<sup id="cite_ref-fiafriday_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiafriday-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Ferrari introduced a new qualifying-specification engine but the team installed a previous engine specification for the race on Sunday.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Williams and BAR introduced revised engine specifications for the qualifying session, while Renault debuted new front wings for the race.<sup id="cite_ref-fiafriday_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiafriday-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fiasaturday_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiasaturday-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice_and_qualifying">Practice and qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2002_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Practice and qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Four practice sessions were held before the Sunday race, two each on Friday and Saturday. The Friday morning and afternoon sessions each lasted an hour; the third and fourth sessions, on Saturday morning, lasted 45 minutes each.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Conditions were sunny and dry for the Friday practice sessions.<sup id="cite_ref-fiafriday_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiafriday-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatright" style="width:33%;&#32;;"><style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r887775652">.mw-parser-output .quotebox{background-color:#F9F9F9;border:1px solid #aaa;box-sizing:border-box;padding:10px;font-size:88%;max-width:100%}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft{margin:0.5em 1.4em 0.8em 0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright{margin:0.5em 0 0.8em 1.4em}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.centered{margin:0.5em auto 0.8em auto}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft p,.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright p{font-style:inherit}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-title{background-color:#F9F9F9;text-align:center;font-size:larger;font-weight:bold}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:before{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" “ ";vertical-align:-45%;line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:after{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" ” ";line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .left-aligned{text-align:left}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .right-aligned{text-align:right}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .center-aligned{text-align:center}.mw-parser-output .quotebox cite{display:block;font-style:normal}@media screen and (max-width:360px){.mw-parser-output .quotebox{min-width:100%;margin:0 0 0.8em!important;float:none!important}}</style>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">It was a good fight with Ralf and Michael and I am pleased to be on pole. We knew we would be at ease on this track from the start and we haven't needed to change the car too much during the weekend. The tyres are very consistent and we have found a good balance for our car. I hope for my first win this season and for my second here at Monza and in Formula One overall. It is going to be a good race.</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style="">Juan Pablo Montoya on taking the pole position.<sup id="cite_ref-saturdayquotes_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-saturdayquotes-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>Saturday's afternoon qualifying session lasted for an hour. Each driver was limited to twelve laps, with the starting order decided by the drivers' fastest laps. During this session, the <a href="/wiki/107%25_rule" title="107% rule">107% rule</a> was in effect, which necessitated each driver set a time within 107% of the quickest lap to qualify for the race.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The Williams and Ferrari teams chose to experiment with tyre set-ups by using hard compound tyres with the aim of gaining better race performance.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcquali_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcquali-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Montoya eclipsed the fastest lap speed recorded in Formula One (259.01 km/h, set by <a href="/wiki/Keke_Rosberg" title="Keke Rosberg">Keke Rosberg</a> at the <a href="/wiki/1985_British_Grand_Prix" title="1985 British Grand Prix">1985 British Grand Prix</a>) to clinch his seventh pole position of the season with a time of one minute and 20.264 seconds and a speed of 259.83 km/h.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> His record would remain unbroken until <a href="/wiki/2018_Italian_Grand_Prix" title="2018 Italian Grand Prix">2018</a>, when <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> set a faster pole lap. As of August 2020, Räikkönen's record still stands. He felt his lap time was "pretty good" and gained a large amount of time at the Lesmo corners which contributed to a fast middle sector time.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Montoya was joined on the front row of the grid by Michael Schumacher who was 0.257 seconds off Montoya's pace and was happy with his lap time despite him taking time to get optimum tyre temperature. Ralf Schumacher qualified third and reported making a mistake at the Lesmo corners on his third timed run.<sup id="cite_ref-fiasaturday_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiasaturday-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello set his fastest lap time on his second timed run to secure fourth position. Räikkonen initially secured fifth in his McLaren but was stripped of his fastest qualifying lap as a result of a collision with Sato's Jordan at the Roggia chicane, which caused the session to be stopped temporarily;<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> as a result, Irvine in the Jaguar was promoted to fifth with Räikkonen sixth.<sup id="cite_ref-raikkonenpenalty_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-raikkonenpenalty-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard took seventh in the other McLaren, with de la Rosa alongside him on the fourth row in the second Jaguar. Villeneuve took ninth in the BAR, with Salo rounding out the top ten in his Toyota.
</p><p>Trulli was eleventh in the Renault, joined on the sixth row by Fisichella in the other Jordan. McNish was thirteenth in the second Toyota, followed by the two Saubers of Massa and Heidfeld. Panis was sixteenth in the second BAR, with Button seventeenth in the second Renault.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcquali_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcquali-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Despite the incident with Räikkonen, Sato took eighteenth and said "That was the most difficult qualifying session of my career".<sup id="cite_ref-saturdayquotes_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-saturdayquotes-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The two Minardis lined up on the back row of the grid, Webber ahead of the returning Yoong.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcquali_13-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcquali-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2002_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The race started at 14:00 local time. The weather conditions on the grid were dry and sunny before the race; the track temperature ranged between 33–34&#160;°C (91–93&#160;°F).<sup id="cite_ref-f1comrace_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrace-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Ralf Schumacher switched to the spare Williams because his regular car developed a fuel pressure problem.<sup id="cite_ref-sunquotes_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sunquotes-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> At the start, Montoya drove to the right to block Michael Schumacher, allowing teammate Ralf Schumacher to run alongside him heading into the Rettifilo chicane. Montoya ran wide to prevent a collision with Ralf, who drove over the chicane and thus took the lead illegally.<sup id="cite_ref-sunquotes_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sunquotes-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-gprace_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gprace-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Williams contacted FIA race director <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Whiting" title="Charlie Whiting">Charlie Whiting</a> who replied that Ralf was required to cede the lead back to Montoya.<sup id="cite_ref-gprace_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gprace-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> McNish made the best start in the field, moving from thirteenth to seventh by the end of the first lap, while Trulli made up four positions over the same distance.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> At the end of the first lap, the order of the top ten drivers was Ralf Schumacher, Montoya, Barrichello, Michael Schumacher, Räikkönen, Irvine, McNish, Salo, Panis and Villeneuve.<sup id="cite_ref-lapchart_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapchart-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Ralf Schumacher began to pull away from Montoya. Further down the field Villenueve got ahead of Heidfeld to take over tenth, while Button passed Massa to move into thirteenth position and Yoong overtook teammate Webber for eighteenth. Ralf Schumacher set a new <a href="/wiki/Fastest_lap" title="Fastest lap">fastest lap</a>, a 1:26.230 on lap three, but was later eclipsed by Barrichello. Villeneuve was overtaken by Trulli on the same lap and Webber reclaimed eighteenth from Yoong.<sup id="cite_ref-f1comrace_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrace-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Williams received a reply to order Ralf Schumacher to cede first position to Montoya which was relayed to Schumacher by the Senior Operations Engineer of Williams <a href="/wiki/Sam_Michael" title="Sam Michael">Sam Michael</a> at the end of lap four.<sup id="cite_ref-sunquotes_19-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sunquotes-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-gprace_20-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gprace-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Afterwards Ralf Schumacher pulled over to the side of the track at the Rettifilo with smoke billowing from his engine and became the first retirement of the race at the start of lap five.<sup id="cite_ref-f1comrace_18-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrace-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> His teammate Montoya thus temporarily inherited the lead but was blinded by the smoke, allowing Barrichello to move into the inside line, and after running in Montoya's slipstream, he passed the Williams driver for the lead under braking going into the Rettifilo.<sup id="cite_ref-gprace_20-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gprace-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Kimi Räikkönen retired during the 30th lap due to engine failure, for the fifth time of the season.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2002_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Juan Pablo Montoya's pole position lap was, at the time, the fastest lap ever recorded by a Formula One car, with an average speed of 259.827&#160;km/h.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> was demoted to sixth on the grid after causing a collision with <a href="/wiki/Takuma_Sato" title="Takuma Sato">Takuma Sato</a> during qualifying.</li>
<li>At the start, <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> cut the chicane on the first corner of the first lap to take the lead from teammate <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a>. When Ralf's engine blew up on lap 5, Montoya's view was blocked and <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> passed him for the lead. Soon, Montoya was passed by <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> and stayed third until he retired with damage to the chassis of his car.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2008)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup></li>
<li>This was the only race of the 2002 season that featured a car on the podium which was not a Ferrari, Williams or McLaren.</li>
<li>This was also the final podium, and points finish for <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a>.</li>
<li>This was also the final podium for <a href="/wiki/Jaguar_Racing" title="Jaguar Racing">Jaguar</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pedro_de_la_Rosa" title="Pedro de la Rosa">Pedro de la Rosa</a> collided at the Ascari chicane. As a result, Massa was handed a 10-grid place penalty for causing a collision and forced to sit out the <a href="/wiki/2002_United_States_Grand_Prix" title="2002 United States Grand Prix">next race</a> where he was replaced by 2003 <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a>.</li>
<li>This race saw the first use of the Head and Neck Support device (<a href="/wiki/HANS_device" title="HANS device">HANS device</a>) which was to be mandated for the <a href="/wiki/2003_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2003 Formula One season">2003 season</a>.</li></ul>
