<p>The <b>1957 Monaco Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held on 19 May 1957 at <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Monaco" title="Circuit de Monaco">Monaco</a>. It was race 2 of 8 in the <a href="/wiki/1957_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1957 World Championship of Drivers">1957 World Championship of Drivers</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_report">Race report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1957_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Race report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Despite a hesitant start, Moss led away on the first lap from Collins, Fangio, and Hawthorn. On lap 4 coming out of the tunnel, there was mayhem. Moss went straight through the chicane, sending debris from the wrecked barrier crashing onto the circuit.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Collins crashed through the <a href="/wiki/Quay" class="mw-redirect" title="Quay">quayside</a> barriers trying to avoid it.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Fangio and Brooks slowed to make their way through the carnage. Brooks' effort was for nought, being hit by Mike Hawthorn's Ferrari, which lost a wheel.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Fangio took the lead from Brooks' damaged car and held it to the chequered flag.
</p><p>On lap 96, with nine laps to go,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> von Trips lost a certain third place when his engine blew up. Brabham inherited it, but he in turn lost the place when the engine in his <a href="/wiki/Cooper_T43" title="Cooper T43">Cooper T43</a>-<a href="/wiki/Coventry-Climax" class="mw-redirect" title="Coventry-Climax">Climax</a><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> cut out at Casino five laps from the end.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> He coasted to the harbour and pushed the car home for 6th place.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1957_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Lap Leaders: <a href="/wiki/Stirling_Moss" title="Stirling Moss">Stirling Moss</a> 4 laps (1-4); <a href="/wiki/Juan_Manuel_Fangio" title="Juan Manuel Fangio">Juan Manuel Fangio</a> 101 laps (5-105).</li>
<li>Shared Drives:
<ul><li>Car #24: <a href="/wiki/Wolfgang_Graf_Berghe_von_Trips" class="mw-redirect" title="Wolfgang Graf Berghe von Trips">Wolfgang von Trips</a> (92 laps) and <a href="/wiki/Mike_Hawthorn" title="Mike Hawthorn">Mike Hawthorn</a> (3 laps).</li>
<li>Car #34: <a href="/wiki/Giorgio_Scarlatti" title="Giorgio Scarlatti">Giorgio Scarlatti</a> (42 laps) and <a href="/wiki/Harry_Schell" title="Harry Schell">Harry Schell</a> (22 laps).</li></ul></li>
<li>Grand Prix debut for: <a href="/wiki/Ivor_Bueb" title="Ivor Bueb">Ivor Bueb</a>, <a href="/wiki/Masten_Gregory" title="Masten Gregory">Masten Gregory</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Stuart_Lewis-Evans" title="Stuart Lewis-Evans">Stuart Lewis-Evans</a>.</li>
<li>Career Firsts: <a href="/wiki/Tony_Brooks_(racing_driver)" title="Tony Brooks (racing driver)">Tony Brooks</a> and <a href="/wiki/Masten_Gregory" title="Masten Gregory">Masten Gregory</a> (points and podium); <a href="/wiki/Stuart_Lewis-Evans" title="Stuart Lewis-Evans">Stuart Lewis-Evans</a>  (points); <a href="/wiki/Vanwall" title="Vanwall">Vanwall</a> (podium).</li>
<li>First podium finish for an American in Formula One (excluding Indianapolis 500) (<a href="/wiki/Masten_Gregory" title="Masten Gregory">Masten Gregory</a>).</li></ul>
