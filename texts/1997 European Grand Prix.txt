<p>The <b>1997 European Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>XLII European Grand Prix</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 26 October 1997 at the <a href="/wiki/Circuito_Permanente_de_Jerez" class="mw-redirect" title="Circuito Permanente de Jerez">Circuito Permanente de Jerez</a>, Spain. Originally scheduled as the Grand Prix of Portugal at the <a href="/wiki/Aut%C3%B3dromo_do_Estoril" class="mw-redirect" title="Autódromo do Estoril">Estoril</a> circuit, it was moved when Estoril's management had financial difficulties. It was the 17th and final race of the <a href="/wiki/1997_FIA_Formula_One_World_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="1997 FIA Formula One World Championship">1997 FIA Formula One World Championship</a>. The 69-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> in a <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>, his first Formula One race victory. His teammate <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> finished second and <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a> took third, which was sufficient for him to win the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">World Championship</a>. As of 2020<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=1997_European_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup>, these are Williams's last Drivers' and Constructors' World Championships. This was also the final Grand Prix for <a href="/wiki/Ukyo_Katayama" title="Ukyo Katayama">Ukyo Katayama</a>, <a href="/wiki/Norberto_Fontana" title="Norberto Fontana">Norberto Fontana</a> and 10-time Grand Prix winner <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>, driving for <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, had led the championship by a single point ahead of Villeneuve going into the race. During the race Villeneuve and Schumacher collided while battling for the lead and the resulting damage to Schumacher's car forced him to retire. The blame for the incident was later attributed to Schumacher by the sport's governing body, the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">FIA</a>, and he was stripped of his second-place finish in the championship. Schumacher's tactics were widely criticised by the media, including publications based in his home country of Germany, and in Ferrari's home country of Italy.
</p><p>Following the race Williams and McLaren were accused of colluding to decide the finishing order. Villeneuve stated that "it was better to let them through and win the World Championship". The FIA determined there was no evidence to support the claims, and dismissed the accusations.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1997_European_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1997_European_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Originally scheduled as the Grand Prix of Portugal at the Estoril Circuit, the race was moved to the Jerez Circuit when Estoril's management had financial difficulties.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The race was added to the schedule after Japan because engine suppliers <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a> did not want their last Formula One race to be in Japan.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Heading into the final race of the season, two drivers were still in contention for the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">World Drivers' Championship</a>. <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> driver Michael Schumacher was leading with 78 points; <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> driver Jacques Villeneuve was second with 77 points, one point behind Schumacher. Behind Schumacher and Villeneuve in the Drivers' Championship, <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a> was third on 41 points in the other Williams, with <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a> and <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> on 36 and 30 points respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-points_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Villeneuve had won two more races than Schumacher during the season, meaning that in the event of a tie on points, the rules stated Villeneuve would be world champion.
</p><p>Villeneuve had to finish the race in a points-scoring position (points were awarded for drivers finishing in sixth place or higher) and ahead of Schumacher to become World Drivers' Champion. Schumacher would be world champion if he finished ahead of Villeneuve, or if Villeneuve failed to score any points by finishing lower than sixth or not completing the race.
</p><p>In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">World Constructors' Championship</a>, Williams led with 118 points and Ferrari were second on 100. Williams had therefore clinched the constructors' title before the race, as even if Ferrari finished first and second in the race, thereby taking 16 points, and both Williams cars failed to score a point, they could not beat Williams.<sup id="cite_ref-points_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Following the Japanese Grand Prix on 12 October, the teams conducted testing sessions at various locations around the world. Williams, Benetton, <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a> and McLaren conducted at a test session at the <a href="/wiki/Silverstone_Circuit" title="Silverstone Circuit">Silverstone Circuit</a>. Williams, Sauber and Jordan tested using 1998-spec cars, in preparation for the <a href="/wiki/1998_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1998 Formula One World Championship">1998</a> season. <a href="/wiki/Arrows_Grand_Prix_International" title="Arrows Grand Prix International">Arrows</a> conducted testing at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Nevers_Magny-Cours" title="Circuit de Nevers Magny-Cours">Circuit de Nevers Magny-Cours</a> circuit, performing wet weather tyre development with tyre suppliers <a href="/wiki/Bridgestone" title="Bridgestone">Bridgestone</a> with the circuit flooded to simulate wet weather conditions. <a href="/wiki/Prost_Grand_Prix" title="Prost Grand Prix">Prost</a> conducted testing at <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Barcelona-Catalunya" title="Circuit de Barcelona-Catalunya">Circuit de Catalunya</a> and performed tyre testing for Bridgestone, as well as testing their 1998-specification car. Ferrari remained at the Suzuka Circuit testing an electronic differential used by driver <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a> at the previous race.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice_and_qualifying">Practice and qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1997_European_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Practice and qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Four practice sessions were held before the Sunday race—two on Friday from 11:00 to 12:00 and 13:00 to 14:00 <a href="/wiki/Central_European_Summer_Time" title="Central European Summer Time">CEST</a> (<a href="/wiki/UTC" class="mw-redirect" title="UTC">UTC</a>+2), and two on Saturday morning held from 09.00 to 09.45 and from 10.15 to 11.00 <a href="/wiki/Central_European_Summer_Time" title="Central European Summer Time">CEST</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pg220_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg220-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> In the first two practice sessions, Prost driver <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Panis" title="Olivier Panis">Olivier Panis</a> set the fastest time with a lap of 1 minute and 22.735 seconds, one-tenths of a second faster than Arrows driver <a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a>. Villeneuve and <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> were third and fourth. <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> and Coulthard, set the fifth and eighth-fastest times respectively for McLaren; they were separated by Frentzen and Alesi. Michael Schumacher and <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> completed the top ten.<sup id="cite_ref-europe1997_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-europe1997-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the final two practice sessions, Coulthard set the fastest lap with a time of 1:20.738; Häkkinen finished with the second-fastest time. The Williams drivers were quicker—Villeneuve in third and Frentzen in sixth. Panis slipped to fourth ahead of <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a>, their best times one-tenth of a second apart. They were ahead of Stewart driver <a href="/wiki/Jan_Magnussen" title="Jan Magnussen">Jan Magnussen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shinji_Nakano" title="Shinji Nakano">Shinji Nakano</a> for Prost, Hill and Alesi.<sup id="cite_ref-europe1997_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-europe1997-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_racing#Qualifying_sessions" title="Formula One racing">qualifying</a> session was held on Saturday afternoon and lasted one-hour between 13:00 to 14:00 <a href="/wiki/Central_European_Summer_Time" title="Central European Summer Time">CEST</a>, each driver was allowed up to twelve timed laps, with their fastest lap used to determine their grid position. Cars were timed using a TAG Heuer timing system, which measured to an accuracy of one-thousandth of a second.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> At the end of the session, the three fastest drivers had all set the same laptime, the first time this had happened in the history of the World Championship.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a> was first to set a time of 1:21.072, fourteen minutes into the one hour session. A further fourteen minutes later, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> posted an identical time. With nine minutes of the session remaining, <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a> crossed the line, again with a time of 1:21.072. Under the regulations, in the event of drivers setting equal times in qualifying, the order in which the times were set is considered, with the first driver to set the time given precedence. Villeneuve was awarded pole position on the starting grid for the race, with Schumacher second and Frentzen third. Fourth place on the grid went to the reigning World Champion, <a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a>, in his <a href="/wiki/Arrows_Grand_Prix_International" title="Arrows Grand Prix International">Arrows</a>, with a time of 1:21.130, 0.058 seconds behind the time of the leading three. Hill had been on course to get pole position but had to slow towards the end of the lap because of yellow flags due to an incident involving <a href="/wiki/Ukyo_Katayama" title="Ukyo Katayama">Ukyo Katayama</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1997_European_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The race took place in the afternoon from 14:00 <a href="/wiki/Central_European_Time" title="Central European Time">CET</a> (<a href="/wiki/UTC" class="mw-redirect" title="UTC">UTC</a>+1), in dry and sunny weather. Villeneuve started the race in pole position, with Schumacher in second. Just a few moments before the start of the race, a blue liquid came out of Villeneuve's Williams. However, this did not influence his car during the race. Schumacher's getaway at the start was better than Villeneuve's and he had taken the lead by the time they reached the first corner. Schumacher led 40 of the first 47 laps of the race.<sup id="cite_ref-nytimes_ap_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nytimes_ap-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Frentzen also got a better start than Villeneuve and overtook him. Under the orders of the Williams team, on lap eight Frentzen let teammate Villeneuve past. Schumacher made his first pit-stop on lap 22 and Villeneuve made his first stop the following lap. Both retained their positions. During the first round of pitstops the McLarens swapped places with <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> leading <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> and Frentzen dropped to fifth position behind both of them. The order of the leaders after the second round of pit stops on lap 43 and lap 44 remained the same but with Villeneuve closer to Schumacher.
</p><p>Villeneuve went into lap 48 less than a second behind Schumacher. Partway through the lap he attempted to overtake Schumacher at the <i>Dry Sack</i> corner. Braking later than Schumacher, Villeneuve held the inside line and was ahead on the track when Schumacher turned in on him resulting in a collision. ITV's then pit lane reporter <a href="/wiki/James_Allen_(Formula_One_commentator)" class="mw-redirect" title="James Allen (Formula One commentator)">James Allen</a> has noted that onboard footage shows Schumacher twitching his steering wheel left before turning right into Villeneuve.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Martin_Brundle" title="Martin Brundle">Martin Brundle</a>, in the commentary box alongside <a href="/wiki/Murray_Walker" title="Murray Walker">Murray Walker</a>, immediately saw that Schumacher's move had been deliberate, saying, "That didn't work Michael. You hit the wrong part of him, my friend".<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The right-front wheel of Schumacher's Ferrari hit the left <a href="/wiki/Radiator_(engine_cooling)" title="Radiator (engine cooling)">radiator</a> pod of Villeneuve's Williams—unlike the <a href="/wiki/1994_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="1994 Australian Grand Prix">1994 collision with Hill</a> where Schumacher inflicted damage on Hill's suspension—and caused Schumacher to retire. Villeneuve described the incident after the race "The car felt very strange. The hit was very hard. It was not a small thing."<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> He continued but the damage to the mounts on his car's <a href="/wiki/Automotive_battery" title="Automotive battery">battery</a> meant he was slower than the cars behind him.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At the time of the incident there were 22 laps of the race remaining. The slower pace of Villeneuve's car meant that on the last lap, he had been caught by both McLarens, Häkkinen having regained second place from Coulthard under <a href="/wiki/Team_orders" title="Team orders">team orders</a>. After the race Villeneuve stated "I did not fight then. It was better to let them through and win the World Championship. It is a good exchange."<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> in fourth place (in what turned out to be his final Grand Prix) was also catching Villeneuve but he did not pass before crossing the finish line. The final margin between Villeneuve and Berger was 0.116 seconds. Third place meant Villeneuve finished ahead of Schumacher in the Drivers' Championship by three points, and became World Champion. Häkkinen's victory was the first of his career.<sup id="cite_ref-nytimes_ap_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nytimes_ap-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1997_European_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="Schumacher.2FVilleneuve_collision"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Schumacher/Villeneuve_collision">Schumacher/Villeneuve collision</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1997_European_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Schumacher/Villeneuve collision">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Circuit_Jerez_Dry_Sac.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e6/Circuit_Jerez_Dry_Sac.jpg/220px-Circuit_Jerez_Dry_Sac.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="229" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e6/Circuit_Jerez_Dry_Sac.jpg/330px-Circuit_Jerez_Dry_Sac.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e6/Circuit_Jerez_Dry_Sac.jpg/440px-Circuit_Jerez_Dry_Sac.jpg 2x" data-file-width="470" data-file-height="490" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Circuit_Jerez_Dry_Sac.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The <i>Curva Dry Sack</i> corner, the scene of <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Villeneuve</a> and <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Schumacher's</a> collision on Lap 48.</div></div></div>
<p>Before the race at Jerez, the president of the FIA, <a href="/wiki/Max_Mosley" title="Max Mosley">Max Mosley</a>, had promised to issue penalties to anyone who tried to influence the outcome of the championship.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix_13-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Race officials issued a statement following the race stating that they had "unanimously concluded it was a racing accident and no further action is necessary".<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> However, Schumacher was subsequently summoned to a disciplinary hearing by the FIA and on 11 November 1997, it was announced that Schumacher would be disqualified from the 1997 World Championship. This meant he lost his second place in the overall standings to <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a> but he would retain his race victories and other results and would not be fined or face any further punishment in the following season. Max Mosley stated that the panel "concluded that although the actions were deliberate they were not premeditated".<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher was also ordered to take part in a road-safety campaign during the 1998 season.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Ferrari also escaped unpunished despite Article 123 of the FIA International Sporting Code stating: "The entrant shall be responsible for all acts or omissions on the part of the driver." Under this rule, which has never been invoked for a driving incident, Ferrari could have been punished for failing to control its driver. When questioned on the subject Max Mosley said that the World Council had decided not to invoke Article 123. Another question which the World Council failed to address was whether or not there would be any public sanction against the stewards at Jerez, who had dismissed the Schumacher-Villeneuve incident, which, has been claimed, was a dereliction of their duty.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Media_reaction">Media reaction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1997_European_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Media reaction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The German newspapers were among the many from across Europe that attacked Schumacher. <i><a href="/wiki/Bild" title="Bild">Bild</a></i> said "Schumacher was to blame for the crash". "He played for high stakes and lost everything – the World Championship and his reputation for fair play. There is no doubt that he wanted to take out Villeneuve." The <i><a href="/wiki/Frankfurter_Allgemeine" class="mw-redirect" title="Frankfurter Allgemeine">Frankfurter Allgemeine</a></i> called him "a kamikaze without honour" and commented that the "monument is starting to crack because the foundations are faulty". A German TV station asked fans for their views and received the views of 63,081 votes. 28% said they could not support Schumacher any longer.<sup id="cite_ref-Newspaper_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Newspaper-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In Italy there was widespread condemnation of Schumacher. The daily newspaper <i>l'Unità</i> called for him to be fired by Ferrari. "Schumacher ought to face charges in a Spanish court for the grave deed he committed" it reported. "The driver covered himself, Ferrari and Italian sport as a whole with shame. We are waiting for Ferrari to announce that it is throwing out Michael Schumacher and hiring a new driver who is more intelligent, has more wisdom and a real sense of morality."<sup id="cite_ref-Newspaper_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Newspaper-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><i><a href="/wiki/La_Repubblica" title="La Repubblica">La Repubblica</a></i> reported that "seeing a world title vanish after waiting 18 years is sad enough. But to see it go up in smoke with the move from Michael Schumacher is unfortunately much worse. It's shameful." <i><a href="/wiki/Gazzetta_dello_Sport" class="mw-redirect" title="Gazzetta dello Sport">Gazzetta dello Sport</a></i> said that if Ferrari had won the title it would have been "<i>a title to hide</i>" and said that it preferred to go on waiting for the day when "our passion for Ferrari has a happy ending."<sup id="cite_ref-Newspaper_19-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Newspaper-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Even <i><a href="/wiki/La_Stampa" title="La Stampa">La Stampa</a></i>, the newspaper owned by the <a href="/wiki/Agnelli_family" title="Agnelli family">Agnelli family</a>, which also controls Ferrari, said "His image as a champion was shattered, like a glass hit by a stone."<sup id="cite_ref-Newspaper_19-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Newspaper-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the British newspapers, Schumacher's manoeuvre against <a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a> at the <a href="/wiki/1994_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="1994 Australian Grand Prix">1994 Australian Grand Prix</a> was used as a comparison in many of the reports. The <i><a href="/wiki/Daily_Mail" title="Daily Mail">Daily Mail</a></i> reported that Schumacher had now "lost the last vestige of his reputation of being a sportsman" and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Times" title="The Times">The Times</a></i> wrote that Schumacher had "sacrificed his reputation by an act of such cynicism that it lost him the right to any sympathy".<sup id="cite_ref-Newspaper_19-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Newspaper-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the Australian telecast of the race, former world champion <a href="/wiki/Alan_Jones_(racing_driver)" title="Alan Jones (racing driver)">Alan Jones</a> stated that there was “no doubt in his mind that Schumacher deliberately drove into the side of him to have him off, ala Damon Hill at Adelaide”, while co-commentator <a href="/wiki/Darrell_Eastlake" title="Darrell Eastlake">Darrell Eastlake</a> said that it's "not the way the world championship should have been decided and viewers around the world would be disappointed with that sort of act” and his "credibility falls away a bit”.<sup id="cite_ref-Nine_Network_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Nine_Network-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Ferrari hosted a press conference on the Tuesday after Jerez during which Schumacher admitted that he had made a mistake but said it was a misjudgement rather than a deliberate attempt to take out Villeneuve. "I am human like everyone else and unfortunately I made a mistake," he said. "I don't make many but I did this time."<sup id="cite_ref-Newspaper_19-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Newspaper-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> In their respective books on Schumacher, James Allen and Swiss motorsports author Luc Domenjoz have both since expressed the view that Villeneuve went into the corner too fast, and without Schumacher turning into him would have overshot the turn.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Jerez_circuit">Jerez circuit</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1997_European_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Jerez circuit">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 12 December 1997, the World Motorsport Council ruled that the mayor of <a href="/wiki/Jerez" class="mw-redirect" title="Jerez">Jerez</a>, Pedro Pacheco, disrupted the podium ceremony. Originally, the verdict was that no further Formula One races would occur at the track. However, when Mr Pacheco appeared before the WMSC, this ruling was rescinded.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The people chosen to present the trophies were dependent on the race order, with Daimler-Benz chairman <a href="/wiki/J%C3%BCrgen_Schrempp" class="mw-redirect" title="Jürgen Schrempp">Jürgen Schrempp</a> only willing to make a presentation to a McLaren-Mercedes driver. As the McLarens of Häkkinen and Coulthard passed Villeneuve's Williams on the last lap, this would have meant he could present either the trophy for first or second position or the winning constructor trophy. There was some confusion due to the late changes in position and whilst the Mayor and the president of the region presented trophies, Schrempp did nothing. FIA president Max Mosley later announced "The disruption caused embarrassment and inconvenience to those presenting the trophies and therefore, no further rounds of the FIA Formula 1 World Championship will be held at the Jerez circuit."<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> As of 2020<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=1997_European_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup>, this race was the last time that <a href="/wiki/Circuito_Permanente_de_Jerez" class="mw-redirect" title="Circuito Permanente de Jerez">Jerez</a> hosted a Formula One Grand Prix, although it has held many pre-season test days.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Collusion_allegations">Collusion allegations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1997_European_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Collusion allegations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatright" style="width:250px;&#32;;"><style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r887775652">.mw-parser-output .quotebox{background-color:#F9F9F9;border:1px solid #aaa;box-sizing:border-box;padding:10px;font-size:88%;max-width:100%}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft{margin:0.5em 1.4em 0.8em 0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright{margin:0.5em 0 0.8em 1.4em}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.centered{margin:0.5em auto 0.8em auto}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft p,.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright p{font-style:inherit}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-title{background-color:#F9F9F9;text-align:center;font-size:larger;font-weight:bold}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:before{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" “ ";vertical-align:-45%;line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:after{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" ” ";line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .left-aligned{text-align:left}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .right-aligned{text-align:right}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .center-aligned{text-align:center}.mw-parser-output .quotebox cite{display:block;font-style:normal}@media screen and (max-width:360px){.mw-parser-output .quotebox{min-width:100%;margin:0 0 0.8em!important;float:none!important}}</style>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style=""><b><a href="/wiki/Murray_Walker" title="Murray Walker">Murray Walker</a></b>: A case of champagne from Ferrari to Sauber for Norberto Fontana. Because the Argentinian newcomer, up from Formula 3, really, really, helped Michael Schumacher on his way there.<br /><b><a href="/wiki/Martin_Brundle" title="Martin Brundle">Martin Brundle</a></b>: What engines have they got in that Sauber, Murray? Isn't it a Ferrari?<br /><b><a href="/wiki/Murray_Walker" title="Murray Walker">Murray Walker</a></b>: Well it is, yes. Martin, you are a cynical chap.</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style="">— <a href="/wiki/ITV_Sport" title="ITV Sport">ITV F1 commentary</a></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>On 8 November, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Times" title="The Times">The Times</a></i> newspaper published an article accusing Williams and McLaren of colluding to decide the finishing order at the end of the Grand Prix.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> The article's claims were based on recordings of the radio transmissions made by the two teams.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> The FIA World Council rejected the claims when the matter was brought before them. FIA president <a href="/wiki/Max_Mosley" title="Max Mosley">Max Mosley</a> stated "It is quite clear that the result of the race was not fixed. There was no arrangement between McLaren and Williams that Mika Häkkinen was going to win. They were able to demonstrate very clearly that was not the case."<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2006, <a href="/wiki/Norberto_Fontana" title="Norberto Fontana">Norberto Fontana</a> claimed in an interview with the Argentinian newspaper <i>Olé</i> that a few hours before the Grand Prix the Ferrari team director <a href="/wiki/Jean_Todt" title="Jean Todt">Jean Todt</a> visited the <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a> motorhome and told the <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Swiss</a> team, which used Ferrari engines at the time, that the Saubers must block <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a> if they were in a position to do so in order to help <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> win the World Championship.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> The Sauber team owner and manager at the time, <a href="/wiki/Peter_Sauber" title="Peter Sauber">Peter Sauber</a> has however denied these allegations and said "Ferrari never expressed the desire that we should obstruct an opponent of Schumacher on the track."<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2014 David Coulthard gave an interview to Charles Bradley in <i><a href="/wiki/Autosport" title="Autosport">Autosport</a></i> where he claimed that indeed an agreement between McLaren and Williams was in place. "Ron [Dennis] had made that deal with Frank [Williams], which none of us knew anything about, that if we helped Williams in their quest to beat Ferrari they wouldn't get in the way of helping McLaren. Ron would probably still deny it today. That's what happened."<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
