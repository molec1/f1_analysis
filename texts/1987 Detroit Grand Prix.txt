<p>The <b>1987 Detroit Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on June 21, 1987, in <a href="/wiki/Detroit" title="Detroit">Detroit</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michigan" title="Michigan">Michigan</a>. It was the sixth <a href="/wiki/Detroit_Grand_Prix" title="Detroit Grand Prix">Detroit Grand Prix</a>. It was held over 63 laps of the four kilometre circuit for a race distance of 253 kilometres.
</p><p>The race was won by <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Lotus_99T" title="Lotus 99T">Lotus 99T</a> with its computer-controlled <a href="/wiki/Active_suspension" title="Active suspension">active suspension</a>. It was the second win in succession for Senna after his victory at <a href="/wiki/1987_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="1987 Monaco Grand Prix">Monaco</a>. The Lotus rode the bumpy street circuit far better than the competition allowing Senna to take care of his tyres and cruise to a 33-second victory over the <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW11B" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams FW11B">Williams FW11B</a> of <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a> with reigning world champion <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> finishing third in his <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/3" title="McLaren MP4/3">McLaren MP4/3</a>.
</p><p>Poleman <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a>, the only driver ever to lap the 4.023&#160;km (2.5&#160;mi) Detroit circuit in under 1:40 during qualifying, led the race until his stop for tyres, but soon after began to fall away with cramp in his right leg making it hard to push the brake pedal. He later said that he had almost retired due to the pain but managed to finish in 5th place, one lap down on Senna.
</p><p>Prost tried to convince <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> via the radio that his tyres were OK and he did not need to stop, but he gave in to orders and pitted for fresh tyres. It was there that <a href="/wiki/Goodyear_Tire_and_Rubber_Company" title="Goodyear Tire and Rubber Company">Goodyear</a> technicians discovered Prost was correct and he had not needed to stop, though this did not alter the call for Piquet to stop as Prost had a reputation of being easier on his tyres than most.
</p><p>Senna's back-to-back victories gave him a two-point lead in the championship over Prost, but it would be a brief surge. Senna would not win again this year. It would also be the last win in the very long history of <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Team Lotus</a> which began with their first win in the <a href="/wiki/1961_United_States_Grand_Prix" title="1961 United States Grand Prix">1961 United States Grand Prix</a>.  The next occasion on which a car bearing the Lotus name would win a Grand Prix was the <a href="/wiki/2012_Abu_Dhabi_Grand_Prix" title="2012 Abu Dhabi Grand Prix">2012 Abu Dhabi Grand Prix</a>, won by <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> driving for the <a href="/wiki/Lotus_F1" title="Lotus F1">Lotus F1</a> team.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Summary">Summary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1987_Detroit_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Summary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1987_Detroit_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> repeated his <a href="/wiki/1986_Detroit_Grand_Prix" title="1986 Detroit Grand Prix">1986 win in Detroit</a> and won his second 1987 race in a row and the second win for a car with <a href="/wiki/Active_suspension" title="Active suspension">active suspension</a>. The win in <a href="/wiki/1987_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="1987 Monaco Grand Prix">Monaco</a> three weeks before had proven that <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Lotus</a>' new system worked, and on the bumpy Detroit circuit, it was even more of an advantage. Superior tyre wear allowed Senna to run the entire race without stopping, and he came home over half a minute ahead of the <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW11B" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams FW11B">Williams</a>-<a href="/wiki/Honda_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Honda F1">Honda</a> of eventual Driver's Champion <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a>.
</p><p>Normally, the Detroit race was immediately preceded or followed by the <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Grand_Prix" title="Canadian Grand Prix">Canadian Grand Prix</a>. In 1987, however, <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_du_Sport_Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale du Sport Automobile">FISA</a> had demanded improvements to the circuit in Montreal, including new pits. When it was determined that these changes could not be completed in time, along with sponsorship problems, the race was cancelled and there would be no Canadian race for only the second time since <a href="/wiki/1967_Formula_One_season" title="1967 Formula One season">1967</a>.
</p><p>On Friday, <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a>'s Williams was fastest in both qualifying sessions, ahead of Senna's Lotus and Piquet, in the second Williams. It rained on Friday night, but the track was dry for the afternoon session on Saturday. In the final session, Senna briefly took the top spot, but Mansell took it back and ended up more than a second quicker for his fourth pole in five races on the season. American <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Cheever" title="Eddie Cheever">Eddie Cheever</a> was sixth for <a href="/wiki/Arrows_Grand_Prix_International" title="Arrows Grand Prix International">Arrows</a>, just behind <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a>'s <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/3" title="McLaren MP4/3">McLaren MP4/3</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1987_Detroit_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>It rained again on Saturday night and Sunday morning, but, after a soaked warm-up on race morning, the start was dry. The first three got away from the grid in order, while Cheever jumped up to fourth and <a href="/wiki/Teo_Fabi" title="Teo Fabi">Teo Fabi</a> went from eighth to fifth in the <a href="/wiki/Benetton_B187" title="Benetton B187">Benetton B187</a>, followed by <a href="/wiki/Michele_Alboreto" title="Michele Alboreto">Michele Alboreto</a> (<a href="/wiki/Ferrari_F1/87" title="Ferrari F1/87">Ferrari F1/87</a>), Prost, <a href="/wiki/Thierry_Boutsen" title="Thierry Boutsen">Thierry Boutsen</a> (Benetton B187) and <a href="/wiki/Stefan_Johansson" title="Stefan Johansson">Stefan Johansson</a> (McLaren MP4/3). On lap three, Piquet went wide in a corner and picked up debris that led to a slow puncture.  Cheever and Fabi got by Piquet before he was forced to pit.  Three laps later, after repeated attempts to get around Cheever, Fabi put his front wing into Cheever's rear tire, puncturing it and breaking the nose of his Benetton B187. Fabi came around to the pits, driving without a nosecone, but retired the vehicle there.  Speaking with CBS' Dave Despain directly after, Fabi confirmed the only problem was a missing wing. Cheever made it to the pits around the same time, losing a lap and rejoining in 19th position.
</p><p>By lap ten, Mansell was five seconds ahead of Senna, with Alboreto another 23 seconds back in third. Suddenly, Senna felt his brake pedal go soft entering a turn and he narrowly avoided hitting the wall. He decided to back off and allow the brakes to cool, dropping three seconds per lap from his times. Primarily concerned about staying ahead of Alboreto, Senna got a break when the Ferrari F1/87's gearbox failed on lap 25, handing third place to Prost. On the next lap, Senna began to go after Mansell.
</p><p>On lap 26, Mansell's lead was 18.8 seconds over Senna, but he was beginning to experience cramps in his right leg. A stop for tyres on lap 34 took 18 seconds when the right rear wheel nut refused to seat properly. Holding the brakes on much longer than normal made the Englishman's cramp even worse. Prost, now in second, was struggling with brake and gearbox problems as he stopped for tyres.
</p><p>Senna set the race's fastest lap on lap 39 at 1:40.464, faster than his qualifying time. Realizing that he was faster on his original tyres than the others were on new ones, he decided to finish the race without stopping to change tyres. The Lotus crew emerged in the pit lane for a time as if preparing for a stop, but it became clear that the Brazilian was not in fact coming in. By that time, he was nearly a minute ahead.
</p><p>Mansell, by this time, was exhausted, his head rolling from side to side in the cockpit. He said after the race that every time he passed the pits, he thought of stopping. On lap 53, Piquet and Prost passed him, and on lap 56, <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> did as well. His perseverance gained him two points for fifth, a lap down, while Cheever took the final point.
</p><p>Senna eased up toward the end, and with three laps to go the skies were threatening rain. It never became an issue, however, as the rain started to fall after the finish, with Senna crossing the line thirty-three seconds ahead. It was the sixth win of his career, but his last in 1987 and the last for the Lotus team, which eventually folded in 1994. Senna said that his tyres were able to last the entire distance for two reasons: the laps he slowed to cool the brakes, and the smooth ride given by the active suspension.
</p>
