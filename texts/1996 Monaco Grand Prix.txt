<p>The <b>1996 Monaco Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>LIV Grand Prix Automobile de Monaco</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Monaco" title="Circuit de Monaco">Monaco</a> on 19 May 1996. It was the sixth race of the <a href="/wiki/1996_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="1996 Formula One season">1996 Formula One season</a>. The race was run in wet weather, causing significant attrition and setting <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_race_records" title="List of Formula One race records">a record</a> for the fewest cars to be running at the end of a Grand Prix race, with the three podium finishers being the only cars left. 
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Olivier_Panis" title="Olivier Panis">Olivier Panis</a> scored his sole career Formula One victory, earning the last ever Formula One victory for the <a href="/wiki/Equipe_Ligier" title="Equipe Ligier">Ligier</a> team, and the first ever for engine manufacturer <a href="/wiki/Mugen_Motorsports" title="Mugen Motorsports">Mugen Motorsports</a>, after switching to slick tyres in a well-timed pitstop. Second place went to <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> in a <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Mercedes_AMG_High_Performance_Powertrains" title="Mercedes AMG High Performance Powertrains">Mercedes</a>. <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Herbert" title="Johnny Herbert">Johnny Herbert</a> was the last finisher in a <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a>, scored his only points of the season. 
</p><p>This was the final win by a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_drivers_from_France" title="Formula One drivers from France">French Formula One driver</a> until <a href="/wiki/Pierre_Gasly" title="Pierre Gasly">Pierre Gasly</a> won the <a href="/wiki/2020_Italian_Grand_Prix" title="2020 Italian Grand Prix">2020 Italian Grand Prix</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1996_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice_and_qualifying">Practice and qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1996_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Practice and qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> had taken pole position but had caused controversy on his slowing down lap when he impeded <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> right at the end of the session. Coming out of the tunnel Schumacher was cruising slowly, acknowledging the crowd, while Berger was on a fast lap. Schumacher tried to get out of Berger's way but the Austrian had to spin at high speed to avoid the Ferrari, entering the chicane backwards.
</p><p>The Ligiers were 14th and 17th in qualifying, both below their expectations after the team had mis-firing issues, preventing a top six position.<sup id="cite_ref-acclimatisation-9_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-acclimatisation-9-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_day">Race day</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1996_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Race day">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Olivier_Panis" title="Olivier Panis">Olivier Panis</a> was fastest in the warm-up.<sup id="cite_ref-acclimatisation-8_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-acclimatisation-8-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a> was 18th after running a wet-setup in preparation for the expected rain.<sup id="cite_ref-acclimatisation-4_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-acclimatisation-4-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Between the warm-up session and the race, heavy rain fell at the circuit, and an additional 15-minute session was added to allow the drivers to get used to the changed conditions as it was the first time rain had fallen over the race weekend.<sup id="cite_ref-acclimatisation-1_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-acclimatisation-1-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-acclimatisation-3_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-acclimatisation-3-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-acclimatisation-5_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-acclimatisation-5-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The session took place at 13:15&#160;<a href="/wiki/Central_European_Summer_Time" title="Central European Summer Time">CEST</a> (<a href="/wiki/Greenwich_Mean_Time" title="Greenwich Mean Time">GMT</a> +2).<sup id="cite_ref-acclimatisation-12_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-acclimatisation-12-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Several drivers went off during the session, including <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Lamy" title="Pedro Lamy">Pedro Lamy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Diniz" title="Pedro Diniz">Pedro Diniz</a> and <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a>, but all continued.<sup id="cite_ref-acclimatisation-6_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-acclimatisation-6-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-acclimatisation-7_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-acclimatisation-7-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-acclimatisation-10_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-acclimatisation-10-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The only two drivers to escape with damage were <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Andrea_Montermini" title="Andrea Montermini">Andrea Montermini</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-acclimatisation-11_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-acclimatisation-11-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-acclimatisation-13_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-acclimatisation-13-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a> suffered a puncture at the end of the session, but was able to return to the pitlane.<sup id="cite_ref-acclimatisation-14_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-acclimatisation-14-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Several drivers opted to skip the session. The <a href="/wiki/Footwork_Arrows" title="Footwork Arrows">Footwork</a> team chose not to participate as they did not have any spare parts, and any crash would have marked the end of their weekend.<sup id="cite_ref-acclimatisation-15_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-acclimatisation-15-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Häkkinen was fastest, setting his time before his crash, with Alesi, <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> and <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Herbert" title="Johnny Herbert">Johnny Herbert</a> behind. The two <a href="/wiki/Williams_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams F1">Williams</a> cars were 7th and 8th, Villeneuve ahead of <a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a> with Schumacher behind them in 9th.<sup id="cite_ref-acclimatisation-16_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-acclimatisation-16-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The race started at 14:30&#160;CEST.<sup id="cite_ref-acclimatisation-2_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-acclimatisation-2-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> On the warm-up lap, Montermini crashed his Forti coming out of the tunnel, and the team's lack of a spare car meant the Italian was unable to start, leaving 21 cars on the grid. Hill overtook Schumacher into Ste Devote, while further back, <a href="/wiki/Jos_Verstappen" title="Jos Verstappen">Jos Verstappen</a>, who had opted to start the race on slicks, slid straight into the wall. The two Minardis were then eliminated when they tangled coming out of the first corner. Hill began to pull away while polesitter Schumacher lost control coming out of Lower Mirabeau and hit the wall. Coming into the Rascasse, Barrichello spun and retired. After five laps, there were only 13 cars remaining as <a href="/wiki/Ukyo_Katayama" title="Ukyo Katayama">Ukyo Katayama</a> (accident), <a href="/wiki/Ricardo_Rosset" title="Ricardo Rosset">Ricardo Rosset</a> (accident) and Diniz (transmission) retired. A significant gap began to open between the leaders and <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a> in fourth. A queue of eight cars formed behind the slow Ferrari. Berger retired from third place on the 10th lap with gearbox trouble leaving 12 cars, while <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a> damaged his front wing trying to pass Irvine, dropping to second last, ahead of Luca Badoer.
</p><p>On lap 31, <a href="/wiki/Martin_Brundle" title="Martin Brundle">Martin Brundle</a> spun off, which left only 11 cars in the race. Three laps later, Irvine was passed when Panis forced his way through at the Loews hairpin. Irvine lost control, became stuck and had undone his seatbelts before he restarted his car with the assistance of the marshals. Hill, meanwhile, had briefly lost the lead to Alesi when he made a pit stop on lap 30 to change to slicks as the track began to dry, but regained it a lap later when he overtook the Frenchman (who was still on wet tyres) on the track. Alesi made his pit stop shortly afterwards, allowing Hill to extend his lead to nearly 30 seconds and continue untroubled at the front until the 40th lap, when a failed oil pump caused his engine to blow coming out of the tunnel, his first retirement of the season. Alesi then led for 20 laps, before his suspension failed, handing the lead to Panis. <a href="/wiki/Luca_Badoer" title="Luca Badoer">Luca Badoer</a> was running six laps down in the Forti when he collided with Villeneuve at Mirabeau on lap 66, retiring both drivers.
</p><p>The race did not run its full distance as the two-hour time limit came into effect. Panis was leading <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> by a small margin with only five other cars behind them. Irvine spun at the same point that his teammate Schumacher had crashed, and as he tried to rejoin, he was hit by <a href="/wiki/Mika_Salo" title="Mika Salo">Mika Salo</a>, who was in turn hit by Häkkinen. All three cars retired, leaving only four cars circulating, with Frentzen running last. The German decided to pull into the pits on the final lap as he was running last anyway, and everyone else had already seen the checkered flag. Panis thus won his one and only Grand Prix, taking Ligier's first win in 15 seasons. Frentzen, Salo and Häkkinen were classified in the final points positions with Irvine credited with seventh place.
</p>
