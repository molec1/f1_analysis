<p>The <b>1953 Italian Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_Two" title="Formula Two">Formula Two</a> race held on 13 September 1953 at <a href="/wiki/Autodromo_Nazionale_di_Monza" title="Autodromo Nazionale di Monza">Monza</a>. It was the ninth and final race in the <a href="/wiki/1953_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1953 World Championship of Drivers">1953 World Championship of Drivers</a>, which was run to Formula Two rules in 1952 and 1953, rather than the <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> regulations normally used. This made it the last World Championship race to run under the Formula Two regulations. The 80-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Maserati_in_motorsport" title="Maserati in motorsport">Maserati</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Juan_Manuel_Fangio" title="Juan Manuel Fangio">Juan Manuel Fangio</a> after he started from second position. <a href="/wiki/Giuseppe_Farina" title="Giuseppe Farina">Nino Farina</a> finished second for the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> team and his teammate <a href="/wiki/Luigi_Villoresi" title="Luigi Villoresi">Luigi Villoresi</a> came in third.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_report">Race report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1953_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Race report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The initial part of the race was a four-way battle between <a href="/wiki/Alberto_Ascari" title="Alberto Ascari">Alberto Ascari</a>, <a href="/wiki/Giuseppe_Farina" title="Giuseppe Farina">Giuseppe Farina</a>, <a href="/wiki/Juan_Manuel_Fangio" title="Juan Manuel Fangio">Juan Manuel Fangio</a> and <a href="/wiki/Onofre_Marim%C3%B3n" title="Onofre Marimón">Onofre Marimón</a>. With five drivers running together on the last lap, the race saw a spectacular finish with Ascari and Farina ahead of Fangio approaching the last corner. Ascari made a mistake and spun. To avoid him, Farina pulled to the grass but recovered later. Fangio pounced on this window of opportunity and took a famous win.
</p>
