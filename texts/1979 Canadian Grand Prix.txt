<p>The <b>1979 Canadian Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 30 September 1979 at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_Gilles_Villeneuve" title="Circuit Gilles Villeneuve">Circuit Île Notre-Dame</a>, <a href="/wiki/Montreal" title="Montreal">Montreal</a>.
</p><p>During practice <a href="/wiki/Niki_Lauda" title="Niki Lauda">Niki Lauda</a> announced his retirement from Formula One. The <a href="/wiki/Brabham" title="Brabham">Brabham</a> team, who had replaced their <a href="/wiki/Alfa_Romeo_in_Formula_One" title="Alfa Romeo in Formula One">Alfa Romeo</a>-engined <a href="/wiki/Brabham_BT48" title="Brabham BT48">BT48</a> with the <a href="/wiki/Cosworth_DFV" title="Cosworth DFV">Cosworth DFV</a>-engined <a href="/wiki/Brabham_BT49" title="Brabham BT49">BT49</a>, recruited Argentine newcomer <a href="/wiki/Ricardo_Zunino" title="Ricardo Zunino">Ricardo Zunino</a> as Lauda's replacement.
</p><p>The organizers would not let the Alfa Romeo factory team compete unless they pre-qualified. They refused to do so but a compromise was reached where one of their drivers would be allowed to take part in practice. The other, <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Giacomelli" title="Bruno Giacomelli">Bruno Giacomelli</a>, was not allowed to enter the race.
</p><p>The race turned into a close duel between <a href="/wiki/Alan_Jones_(racing_driver)" title="Alan Jones (racing driver)">Alan Jones</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gilles_Villeneuve" title="Gilles Villeneuve">Gilles Villeneuve</a> that continued the entire race.
</p>
