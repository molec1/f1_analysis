<p>The <b>2013 Korean Grand Prix</b> (formally known as the <b>2013 Formula 1 Korean Grand Prix</b>)<sup id="cite_ref-formal_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-formal-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Auto_racing" title="Auto racing">motor race</a> that was held on 6 October 2013 at the <a href="/wiki/Korea_International_Circuit" title="Korea International Circuit">Korea International Circuit</a> in <a href="/wiki/Yeongam_County" title="Yeongam County">Yeongam</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_calender_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA_calender-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The race was the fourteenth round of the <a href="/wiki/2013_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2013 Formula One season">2013 season</a>, and marked the fourth and final running of the <a href="/wiki/Korean_Grand_Prix" title="Korean Grand Prix">Korean Grand Prix</a>.
</p><p>The race which was contested over 55 laps was won by <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a>, driving for <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a>. He dominantly won the race to claim his fourth consecutive victory of the year.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He achieved a Grand Chelem for the second race in a row, becoming the first driver since <a href="/wiki/Jim_Clark" title="Jim Clark">Jim Clark</a> in 1963 to achieve the feat in consecutive race weekends.
</p><p>The result meant that Vettel extended his lead to 77 points over Alonso and could seal his fourth consecutive championship in the next race in Japan if results went his way. Räikkönen leapfrogged Hamilton to go third, 28 points behind Alonso while Hamilton's result meant he was six points behind Räikkönen's score. Behind them, <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a>, who retired after a fire due to a collision with <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a>, kept fifth while Rosberg also stayed in sixth.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Reliable_sources/Perennial_sources" title="Wikipedia:Reliable sources/Perennial sources"><span title="The material near this tag may rely on an unreliable source (November 2019)">deprecated source</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>  On lap 38 a fire truck appeared on track on its driver's own accord during a safety car period after Webber's car caught fire.
</p><p>Despite major circuit renovations including a complete modification to the exit of the pit lane, this was the last Korean Grand Prix in Formula One to date.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
