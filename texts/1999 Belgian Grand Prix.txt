<p>The <b>1999 Belgian Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>LVII Foster's Belgian Grand Prix</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 29 August 1999 at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Spa-Francorchamps" title="Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps">Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps</a> in <a href="/wiki/Francorchamps" title="Francorchamps">Francorchamps</a>, <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a>. It was the twelfth race of the <a href="/wiki/1999_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="1999 Formula One season">1999 Formula One season</a>. The 44-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> for the <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> team, from a second position start. <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> finished second in the other McLaren, with <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a> third in a <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a>.
</p><p>Häkkinen's second place promoted him into the lead of the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a>, overtaking <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a>. Irvine was second, one point behind Häkkinen, while Coulthard was third. In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a>, McLaren were promoted into the lead, nine points ahead of Ferrari, with Jordan a further fifty-nine behind.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1999_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1999_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Grand Prix was contested by 22 drivers, in eleven teams of two. The teams, also known as <a href="/wiki/Formula_One#Constructors" title="Formula One">Constructors</a>, were <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arrows_Grand_Prix_International" title="Arrows Grand Prix International">Arrows</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stewart_Grand_Prix" title="Stewart Grand Prix">Stewart</a>, <a href="/wiki/Prost_Grand_Prix" title="Prost Grand Prix">Prost</a>, <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a> and <a href="/wiki/British_American_Racing" title="British American Racing">BAR</a>.
</p><p>Before the race, Ferrari driver <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a> was leading the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a> on 56 points; McLaren driver <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> was second on 54 points. Behind them in the Drivers' Championship, <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> was third on 36 points in the other McLaren, with <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> on 36 and 32 points respectively. In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a>, Ferrari were leading on 94 points and McLaren were second on 90 points, with Jordan third on 42 points.
</p><p>Following the <a href="/wiki/1999_Hungarian_Grand_Prix" title="1999 Hungarian Grand Prix">Hungarian Grand Prix</a> on 15 August, the teams conducted testing sessions at the <a href="/wiki/Silverstone_Circuit" title="Silverstone Circuit">Silverstone</a> circuit on 17–19 August. Häkkinen set the fastest time on the first, second and final days of testing.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Ferrari traveled to their testing circuit at <a href="/wiki/Mugello_Circuit" title="Mugello Circuit">Mugello</a> on 18–20 August, running Schumacher on the final day after being cleared by doctors to test. After completing 20 laps, Schumacher suffered from ankle pain preventing him completing a physical training programme. Ferrari later announced that temporary replacement <a href="/wiki/Mika_Salo" title="Mika Salo">Mika Salo</a> would continue to race for the team.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Several teams announced changes to their driver line-ups for the following season. Benetton confirmed that the team was retaining their driver line-up of <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Wurz" title="Alexander Wurz">Alexander Wurz</a>, with an option for 2001.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Sauber announced that it would sign up Salo on a two-year contract and retain <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Diniz" title="Pedro Diniz">Pedro Diniz</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Jordan confirmed that it was signing Prost driver <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> on a two-year contract, replacing <a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice_and_qualifying">Practice and qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1999_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Practice and qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Jacques_Villeneuve_2002.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/db/Jacques_Villeneuve_2002.jpg/170px-Jacques_Villeneuve_2002.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="255" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/db/Jacques_Villeneuve_2002.jpg/255px-Jacques_Villeneuve_2002.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/db/Jacques_Villeneuve_2002.jpg/340px-Jacques_Villeneuve_2002.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1000" data-file-height="1500" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Jacques_Villeneuve_2002.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Jacques Villeneuve (pictured in 2002) suffered a heavy crash during Saturday afternoon's qualifying session.</div></div></div>
<p>Four practice sessions were held before the Sunday race—two on Friday, and two on Saturday. The Friday morning and afternoon sessions each lasted an hour. The third and final practice sessions were held on Saturday morning and lasted 45 minutes.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The Friday sessions were held in dry and sunny conditions. Häkkinen was quickest in the first session, with a time of 1:54.396 that was less than half a second faster than Coulthard. Williams driver <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> was just off Coulthard's pace; Salo, Irvine and <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a> rounded out the top six; within 1.3 seconds of Häkkinen's time. In the second practice session, Coulthard was fastest with a time of 1:53.577, ahead of Häkkinen, Fisichella, Frentzen, Hill and Schumacher.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Mika_Hakkinen_DTM_Norisring_2009.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/11/Mika_Hakkinen_DTM_Norisring_2009.jpg/170px-Mika_Hakkinen_DTM_Norisring_2009.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="253" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/11/Mika_Hakkinen_DTM_Norisring_2009.jpg/255px-Mika_Hakkinen_DTM_Norisring_2009.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/11/Mika_Hakkinen_DTM_Norisring_2009.jpg/340px-Mika_Hakkinen_DTM_Norisring_2009.jpg 2x" data-file-width="516" data-file-height="769" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Mika_Hakkinen_DTM_Norisring_2009.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> (pictured in 2009) took his tenth pole position of the season.</div></div></div>
<p>Saturday's afternoon qualifying session lasted for an hour. During this session, the <a href="/wiki/107%25_rule" title="107% rule">107% rule</a> was in effect, which necessitated each driver set a time within 107% of the quickest lap to qualify for the race. Each driver was limited to twelve laps. Häkkinen clinched his tenth <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> of the season with a time of 1:50.329. He was joined on the front row by Coulthard, who was one-tenth of a second behind. Frentzen qualified third, though be believed he could have gone faster due to two separate red flag incidents.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>BAR drivers <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ricardo_Zonta" title="Ricardo Zonta">Ricardo Zonta</a> both suffered massive, high-speed accidents at the fast Eau Rouge sweep during the qualifying session.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Both accidents caused the session to be suspended.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1999_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The conditions were dry for the race with the <a href="/wiki/Air_temperature" class="mw-redirect" title="Air temperature">air temperature</a> 20&#160;°C (68&#160;°F) and the track temperature 25&#160;°C (77&#160;°F).<sup id="cite_ref-race_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-race-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The drivers took to the track at 09:30 (<a href="/wiki/Central_European_Summer_Time" title="Central European Summer Time">UTC</a> +2) for a 30-minute warm-up session.
</p><p>Coulthard took the lead from Häkkinen at the start by going around the outside of La Source, the McLaren team-mates making light contact.<sup id="cite_ref-race_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-race-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> After emerging ahead, Coulthard led every lap of the race to claim his second victory of the season, ten seconds ahead of his team-mate. Häkkinen refused to shake Coulthard's hand after the race.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
