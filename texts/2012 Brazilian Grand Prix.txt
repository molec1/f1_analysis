<p>The <b>2012 Brazilian Grand Prix</b> (formally known as the <b>Formula 1 Grande Prêmio Petrobras do Brasil 2012</b>)<sup id="cite_ref-formal_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-formal-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> that took place at the <a href="/wiki/Aut%C3%B3dromo_Jos%C3%A9_Carlos_Pace" title="Autódromo José Carlos Pace">Autódromo José Carlos Pace</a> in <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo" title="São Paulo">São Paulo</a>, Brazil, on 25 November 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-7Dec_calendar_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7Dec_calendar-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The race was the twentieth and final round of the <a href="/wiki/2012_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2012 Formula One season">2012 Formula One World Championship</a>, and marked the forty-first running of the <a href="/wiki/Brazilian_Grand_Prix" title="Brazilian Grand Prix">Brazilian Grand Prix</a>. The race was won by <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> driving for <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>, scoring the last victory of his Formula One career.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> As of 2020<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=2012_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup>, it remains McLaren's most recent victory.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It was at this race that <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> won the <a href="/wiki/2012_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2012 Formula One season">2012</a> <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">World Drivers' Championship</a>, his third title in a row. <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> finished second in both the race and the championship, and had the provisional championship lead at a late stage of the race, before Vettel moved up the order to have a three-point cushion. The race also marked the final one for <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>, after he announced his retirement for the second time. It was also the last race for <a href="/wiki/HRT_Formula_1_Team" title="HRT Formula 1 Team">HRT</a>, as they collapsed before the start of 2013. This would also prove to be the last Grand Prix for <a href="/wiki/Vitaly_Petrov" title="Vitaly Petrov">Vitaly Petrov</a>, <a href="/wiki/Timo_Glock" title="Timo Glock">Timo Glock</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pedro_de_la_Rosa" title="Pedro de la Rosa">Pedro de la Rosa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Narain_Karthikeyan" title="Narain Karthikeyan">Narain Karthikeyan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Senna" title="Bruno Senna">Bruno Senna</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2012_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2012_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Alonso_2012_brazil.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/93/Alonso_2012_brazil.jpg/220px-Alonso_2012_brazil.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/93/Alonso_2012_brazil.jpg/330px-Alonso_2012_brazil.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/93/Alonso_2012_brazil.jpg/440px-Alonso_2012_brazil.jpg 2x" data-file-width="800" data-file-height="536" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Alonso_2012_brazil.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> needed to overcome a 13-point deficit to <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>The 2012 Brazilian Grand Prix marked <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>'s final race before his second retirement and was also the last race for <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Senna" title="Bruno Senna">Bruno Senna</a><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/HRT_Formula_1_Team" title="HRT Formula 1 Team">HRT Formula 1 Team</a>. HRT in their history did not score a point, and withdrew because of financial issues.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Tyre supplier <a href="/wiki/Pirelli" title="Pirelli">Pirelli</a> provided teams with early prototypes of their <a href="/wiki/2013_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2013 Formula One season">2013</a> compounds during Friday practice for testing and review.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Pirelli brought its silver-banded hard compound tyre as the harder "prime" tyre and the white-banded medium compound tyre as the softer "option" tyre, as opposed to the <a href="/wiki/2011_Brazilian_Grand_Prix" title="2011 Brazilian Grand Prix">previous year</a> when they brought the medium and soft compound dry tyres.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In order to become world champion for the third time, <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> needed to defend a 13-point advantage over <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> meaning the Spaniard needed at least third place even if the German failed to score. Conversely, a fourth place was sufficient for Vettel even if Alonso won.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Free_practice">Free practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2012_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Free practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>During free practice on Friday and Saturday, it was <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> and <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> who set the pace, closely followed by championship contender <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Vettel</a> and his <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a> teammate <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-whtimes.co.uk_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-whtimes.co.uk-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> While Hamilton topped the time sheets in both Friday sessions, <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> was quickest during the third session on Saturday morning.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> had a difficult first free practice, trailing Vettel by 0.465 seconds, twice as much as in previous races. However, he was able to lap faster than the German on the harder compound tyres in the second session. <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> looked stronger during the long run trials with <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> putting in the most consistently fast lap times, 0.1 seconds ahead of Hamilton.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> With high temperatures, tyre degradation was a concern for the teams in the paddock<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> with Hamilton commenting that "it felt almost as though I was sliding around the track with the tyres melting".<sup id="cite_ref-whtimes.co.uk_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-whtimes.co.uk-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p><cite class="left-aligned" style=""><a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> commenting on taking pole position.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup></cite>

<p>After the very warm practice sessions, qualifying on Saturday was run under difficult weather conditions. Qualifying one (Q1) started on a damp track and therefore on intermediate tyres, while by Q2 the track was almost dry and slick tyres were used. In Q1 <a href="/wiki/Romain_Grosjean" title="Romain Grosjean">Romain Grosjean</a> collided with the slower running <a href="/wiki/HRT_F1_Team" class="mw-redirect" title="HRT F1 Team">HRT</a> of <a href="/wiki/Pedro_de_la_Rosa" title="Pedro de la Rosa">Pedro de la Rosa</a> and lost his front wing. While he succeeded in nursing the car back into the pit lane, he was unable to change his tyres and was therefore left in a disappointing 18th position on the grid. <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>'s last ever qualifying did not go well either. He took 14th position, almost half a second down on his teammate, admitting he had perhaps compromised his setup too much for an expected wet race.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLarens</a> were fastest all weekend and easily took the front row, with <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> securing his last pole position with the team. Title contenders <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Vettel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Alonso</a> both struggled, taking 4th and 8th position on the grid respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Pastor_Maldonado" title="Pastor Maldonado">Pastor Maldonado</a> was handed a 10-place grid penalty for missing the weighbridge in the second part of qualifying. He dropped from sixth to 16th, moving <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Alonso</a> one place up to seventh.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2012_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-race">Pre-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2012_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Pre-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Start_2012_brazil_GP.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/Start_2012_brazil_GP.jpg/220px-Start_2012_brazil_GP.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/Start_2012_brazil_GP.jpg/330px-Start_2012_brazil_GP.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/Start_2012_brazil_GP.jpg/440px-Start_2012_brazil_GP.jpg 2x" data-file-width="800" data-file-height="536" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Start_2012_brazil_GP.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Start of the race</div></div></div>
<p>Title contender <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Alonso</a> and <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> were hoping for damp conditions during the race, stating: "Wet races are a bit more unpredictable so we need some kind of damp race with many things happening, because we know that in normal conditions, fighting for the championship will be very difficult".<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
<a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> discounted rumours he might try to help Alonso win the title by forcing <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Vettel</a> into retirement, stating "I've always been an honest person and an honest driver. And that will continue to be the case. My limit is the limit of the regulations, and I will stay true to this limit".<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_report">Race report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2012_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Race report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>With only light rain just 10 minutes before the start, all teams opted to start on slick tyres. <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Vettel</a> got away poorly, dropping to seventh position, while his rival <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Alonso</a> moved up into fifth. Vettel and <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Senna" title="Bruno Senna">Bruno Senna</a> collided at turn 4 in a racing incident, which saw Vettel spin while Senna ended up hitting the rear of <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Pérez</a>. Vettel suffered damage to his left sidepod but was able to carry on, in 22nd position. However, the reigning world champion began to move up the order, as there was close racing for position at the front of the field as well.  <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Massa</a> helped his teammate overtake <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> on lap two going into the Senna-S, giving the Spaniard the crucial third position he would need to win the title. But Alonso lost the position again to the fast running <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a> of <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a> after running wide on lap four. Maldonado crashed out on lap two, when he lost control of his car on the kerb through the inside of turn 3, and crashed into the tyre barrier on the exit of the corner.
</p><p>Rain began to fall during the next few laps and the first cars made pit stops for intermediate tyres, including the two title contenders, while <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Button</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Hülkenberg</a> stayed out. This proved to be the right decision since the rain decreased shortly afterwards and  cars on intermediates were forced to pit for slick tyres once again. Hülkenberg continued his strong performance by overtaking Button for the lead at the end of lap 18 (their official time differential at the finish line at the end of that lap was 0.000).
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Hamilton_2012_brazil_retire.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d0/Hamilton_2012_brazil_retire.jpg/220px-Hamilton_2012_brazil_retire.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d0/Hamilton_2012_brazil_retire.jpg/330px-Hamilton_2012_brazil_retire.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d0/Hamilton_2012_brazil_retire.jpg/440px-Hamilton_2012_brazil_retire.jpg 2x" data-file-width="800" data-file-height="536" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Hamilton_2012_brazil_retire.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> retires from the lead of the race on lap 54</div></div></div>
<p>With the two cars in front leading the field by almost a minute, debris on the track brought out the <a href="/wiki/Safety_car" title="Safety car">safety car</a> on lap 23. At this point <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Alonso</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Vettel</a> were running in fourth and fifth place. At the restart on lap 29, <a href="/wiki/Kamui_Kobayashi" title="Kamui Kobayashi">Kamui Kobayashi</a> took fifth position from Vettel. The damage on the Red Bull car slowed Vettel down considerably more in dry conditions, and <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Massa</a> helped his teammate by overtaking the German shortly after.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Meanwhile, at the front, <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Hamilton</a> took second from his teammate and was able to pass <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Hülkenberg</a> when the German half-spun. Hülkenberg pursued the <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> but slid into him on lap 54, leaving Hamilton out of the race with damage to the left front suspension and himself with a drive-through penalty, handing <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Button</a> the lead.
</p><p>When the rain started again, <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Vettel</a> was one of the first to pit for intermediates, but since his radio had failed the team was not ready for him, causing a long delay. <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> timed <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Massa's</a> pit stop well, letting <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Alonso</a> take second position from him. Vettel took sixth position from <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> which proved sufficient to retain the title even with Alonso finishing second.
</p><p>The race ended after <a href="/wiki/Paul_di_Resta" title="Paul di Resta">Paul di Resta</a> crashed on the start/finish straight shortly before the end, bringing out the <a href="/wiki/Safety_car" title="Safety car">safety car</a> once again until the conclusion of the race. <a href="/wiki/Caterham_F1" title="Caterham F1">Caterham's</a> <a href="/wiki/Vitaly_Petrov" title="Vitaly Petrov">Vitaly Petrov</a> took 11th position which meant his team overtook <a href="/wiki/Marussia_F1" title="Marussia F1">Marussia</a> in the Constructors' Championship. Former world champion <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> had an eventful race as well, almost crashing into <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Vettel</a> after the start and later trying to use an escape road only to find it to be a dead end. He later stated the way had been open 11 years prior, having made the same manoeuver at the <a href="/wiki/2001_Brazilian_Grand_Prix" title="2001 Brazilian Grand Prix">2001 race</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> He also had a close contest with the retiring <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>, who finished seventh after recovering from an early puncture, but gave up sixth to Vettel in order to help him extend his small point advantage over <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2012_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2012 Formula One season">championship</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2012_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatright" style="width:37%;&#32;;"><link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r887775652"/>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">"I think everything that could go wrong went wrong. As a matter of fact, though, I think we always kept believing. [...] I think they tried everything to make it even harder for us, not just the others but also the circumstances: as I said, with the damage on the car, losing radio, in these conditions, when communication is so crucial, stopping just a lap too early, not having the tyres ready because communication wasn't there. Where do you start, really? I think you guys had your show and we had to really fight until the end."</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style=""><a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> during the post-race press conference.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>The race received widespread acclaim, with <i><a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">The Guardian</a></i> calling it a "rollercoaster".<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Three-time world champions <a href="/wiki/Niki_Lauda" title="Niki Lauda">Niki Lauda</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a> stated that they had never seen a race like it.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-nytimes_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nytimes-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> German magazine <i><a href="/wiki/Der_Spiegel" title="Der Spiegel">Der Spiegel</a></i> deemed the race "spectacular" and "historic" due to the record-breaking 147 successful overtaking manoeuvres carried out during the race.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> himself called it his "toughest race ever".<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At age 25, <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Vettel</a> became the youngest triple world champion of the sport, six years younger than <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> had been in <a href="/wiki/1991_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="1991 Formula One season">1991</a>. On drawing level with Senna, Vettel commented: "To win that third title here, where one of my greatest idols, Ayrton Senna, was from, it is very difficult to imagine I join him and other great names by winning three successive titles".<sup id="cite_ref-nytimes_28-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nytimes-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a>'s team principal <a href="/wiki/Christian_Horner" title="Christian Horner">Christian Horner</a> praised his driver, saying "Sebastian has driven better than ever this season and has fought his way back into this championship, he's never given up and you saw that in today's race".<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The <i><a href="/wiki/Daily_Mirror" title="Daily Mirror">Daily Mirror</a></i> lauded <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a> designer <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Newey" title="Adrian Newey">Adrian Newey</a> for making the decision to change the engine mapping on Vettel's car after reviewing photos of the damage sustained in the first lap accident. Newey was quoted saying: "So we photographed it going past, saw huge crease in exhaust, which usually means it will soon crack and the bodywork catch fire. [...] So we changed the engine mapping to keep the exhaust as cool as we could, even though it meant lost performance".<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatright" style="width:25%;&#32;;"><link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r887775652"/>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">"I didn't win the title, but I won so much respect from everybody."</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style=""><a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> after the race.<sup id="cite_ref-nytimes_28-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nytimes-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>Championship runner-up <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> expressed pride for his team, going so far as to call the <a href="/wiki/2012_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2012 Formula One season">2012 season</a> "the best season of my career". During the podium interview with <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a>, he asserted: "Obviously we lost the championship now, but I don't think, as you said, that we lost here in Brazil, we lost in some races where we were a little bit unlucky. But this is a sport, but when you do something with your heart, when you do something with 100 per cent, you have to be proud of your team, happy for them, and we will try next year."<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> The press later pointed out Alonso's "grace after the race", contrasting him to Vettel who had criticized the competition's "dirty tricks" during his post-race press conference.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Speaking about his victory, <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> congratulated his team, saying: "This is the perfect way to end the season. We have had ups and downs and to end on a high bodes well for 2013."<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> However, his victory in this race would turn out to be his last in Formula One.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Upon his second retirement from the sport, Michael Schumacher commented on the three years at <a href="/wiki/Mercedes_GP" class="mw-redirect" title="Mercedes GP">Mercedes GP</a>: "I have tried that mission to end successful. It didn't work this time but I'm quite happy to finish from here and go for a different life again."<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Commenting on the outcome of the season, former team owner <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Jordan" title="Eddie Jordan">Eddie Jordan</a> said he believed Alonso would have deserved the title more, considering the "less competitive machine".<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> columnist and former Formula One driver <a href="/wiki/Jaime_Alguersuari" title="Jaime Alguersuari">Jaime Alguersuari</a> took a different viewpoint, saying the German deserved the title, having gone into the last race with "the most difficult position [...] – he had everything to lose".<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversies">Controversies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2012_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Controversies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Three days after the race, <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> announced they were contemplating filing an appeal with the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">FIA</a> due to video footage allegedly showing <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> overtaking <a href="/wiki/Jean-%C3%89ric_Vergne" title="Jean-Éric Vergne">Jean-Éric Vergne</a> under yellow flags. Should the allegations have proven valid, the FIA could have added 20 seconds to Vettel's finishing time, demoting him to 8th in the race result, handing the driver's title to <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Alonso</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> Just two days later however, Ferrari opted not to appeal the result after receiving additional information from the FIA.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> Formula One CEO <a href="/wiki/Bernie_Ecclestone" title="Bernie Ecclestone">Bernie Ecclestone</a> had previously stated that he considered Ferrari's allegations "a shame" and "a joke".<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After losing 10th place in the Constructors' Championship to <a href="/wiki/Caterham_F1" title="Caterham F1">Caterham</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marussia_F1" title="Marussia F1">Marussia</a>'s director of engineering <a href="/wiki/Nikolai_Fomenko" title="Nikolai Fomenko">Nikolai Fomenko</a> accused driver <a href="/wiki/Charles_Pic" title="Charles Pic">Charles Pic</a> of deliberately letting <a href="/wiki/Vitaly_Petrov" title="Vitaly Petrov">Vitaly Petrov</a> pass him due to Pic having already signed a contract with Caterham for <a href="/wiki/2013_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2013 Formula One season">next season</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> However, no actions were taken following these accusations.
</p><p>In August 2018, Red Bull team principal <a href="/wiki/Christian_Horner" title="Christian Horner">Christian Horner</a> revealed that Sebastian Vettel felt that his teammate Mark Webber was responsible for his bad start by pushing him towards the wall in the first turn of the race.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
