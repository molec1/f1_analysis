<p>The <b>2015 Japanese Grand Prix</b> (formally known as the <b>2015 Formula 1 Japanese Grand Prix</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Auto_racing" title="Auto racing">motor race</a> that was held on 27 September 2015 at the <a href="/wiki/Suzuka_Circuit" class="mw-redirect" title="Suzuka Circuit">Suzuka Circuit</a> in <a href="/wiki/Suzuka,_Mie" title="Suzuka, Mie">Suzuka</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. The race was the fourteenth round of the <a href="/wiki/2015_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2015 Formula One World Championship">2015</a> World Championship, and marked the forty-first running of the <a href="/wiki/Japanese_Grand_Prix" title="Japanese Grand Prix">Japanese Grand Prix</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> entered the race as the <a href="/wiki/2014_Japanese_Grand_Prix" title="2014 Japanese Grand Prix">defending winner</a> of the Grand Prix and <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a> leader with a 41-point lead over his <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> teammate <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a>. <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> came into the event eight points further back in third. Mercedes led the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a> over Ferrari by 153 points, with <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> a further 110 points behind Ferrari.
</p><p>Hamilton won the race, having overtaken Rosberg at the start, who fell back to fourth, but recovered to finish second. Rosberg's deficit in the Drivers' Championship therefore increased to 48 points. Sebastian Vettel finished third for Ferrari. This was the first race in which all cars were classified as finishers since the <a href="/wiki/2011_European_Grand_Prix" title="2011 European Grand Prix">2011 European Grand Prix</a>, and would not be replicated again until the <a href="/wiki/2016_Chinese_Grand_Prix" title="2016 Chinese Grand Prix">2016 Chinese Grand Prix</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>For the third year in a row, <a href="/wiki/Pirelli" title="Pirelli">Pirelli</a> opted to bring its two hardest dry weather compounds for this event, the orange-banded hard compound as the "prime" selection, while the white-banded medium tyre used as the "option" selection. The two wet-weather tyres, the green-banded intermediate and blue-banded full wet tyres, were also available to use as they are at all events. Pirelli cited the nature of the track and the high lateral energy loads experienced in the corners, in particular 130R – typically taken at full throttle and top speed in dry weather racing – as reasons for the hardest tyres being used. The suppliers expected a performance difference of 0.6–0.8 seconds per lap between the compounds.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Japanese Grand Prix was one of only two events in the 2015 season to have only one <a href="/wiki/Drag_reduction_system" title="Drag reduction system">drag reduction system</a> (DRS) zone, the other being <a href="/wiki/2015_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="2015 Monaco Grand Prix">Monaco</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The DRS zone at Suzuka was in its traditional spot, on the start/finish straight between turn 18 and turn 1, with the detection point just before the Casio Triangle complex.<sup id="cite_ref-formal_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-formal-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:H%C3%BClkenberg_Japan_2015.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/01/H%C3%BClkenberg_Japan_2015.jpg/220px-H%C3%BClkenberg_Japan_2015.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="146" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/01/H%C3%BClkenberg_Japan_2015.jpg/330px-H%C3%BClkenberg_Japan_2015.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/01/H%C3%BClkenberg_Japan_2015.jpg/440px-H%C3%BClkenberg_Japan_2015.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4715" data-file-height="3138" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:H%C3%BClkenberg_Japan_2015.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a> served a three-place grid penalty carried over from the <a href="/wiki/2015_Singapore_Grand_Prix" title="2015 Singapore Grand Prix">Singapore Grand Prix</a>.</div></div></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a> served a three-place grid penalty following qualifying after being deemed at-fault for a collision with Williams driver <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> at the <a href="/wiki/2015_Singapore_Grand_Prix" title="2015 Singapore Grand Prix">previous event</a> in Singapore.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a> equipped both of their cars with new turning vanes for the Grand Prix in order to improve the airflow underneath the car and therefore create more downforce. The new device had previously only been used by <a href="/wiki/Daniil_Kvyat" title="Daniil Kvyat">Daniil Kvyat</a> during the previous race in Singapore.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> After a poor performance in Singapore, <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> introduced a revised rear wing endplate,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> while <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> brought a new front wing to Suzuka.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>This was the first running of the Japanese Grand Prix after <a href="/wiki/Jules_Bianchi" title="Jules Bianchi">Jules Bianchi</a>'s crash at the previous edition, which proved fatal when Bianchi succumbed to his injuries nine months following the accident.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Marussia_F1" title="Marussia F1">Manor Marussia</a> team announced that they would mark the occasion in a "very private way".<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> In the wake of the accident, the organisers of the Japanese Grand Prix installed a large crane in place of the tractor that Bianchi hit.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Lotus_F1" title="Lotus F1">Lotus</a> team's equipment arrived late in Suzuka, while team members were also kept from entering their hospitality unit after cash-flow problems caused the unit not to be paid for in time.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Going into the weekend, <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> was leading the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">World Drivers' Championship</a> on 252 points, 41 ahead of his Mercedes teammate <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a>. <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> of <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> was 8 more points behind on 203, followed by <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a> on 107 and 101 points respectively. In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">World Constructors' Championship</a>, Mercedes was leading Ferrari by 153 points, with <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> an additional 112 points behind in third.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Free_practice">Free practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Free practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Massa_Japan_2015.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3e/Massa_Japan_2015.jpg/220px-Massa_Japan_2015.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="146" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3e/Massa_Japan_2015.jpg/330px-Massa_Japan_2015.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3e/Massa_Japan_2015.jpg/440px-Massa_Japan_2015.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4186" data-file-height="2786" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Massa_Japan_2015.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> pictured during the first free practice session, held in rainy conditions on Friday morning.</div></div></div>
<p>Per the regulations for the 2015 season, three practice sessions were held; there were two 90-minute sessions on Friday and another one-hour session before qualifying on Saturday.<sup id="cite_ref-pracreg_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pracreg-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> In first practice, <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Sainz_Jr." title="Carlos Sainz Jr.">Carlos Sainz Jr.</a> set the fastest time with a 1:49.434 in rainy conditions, about half a second ahead of second placed Daniil Kvyat. Eight drivers chose not to set a time, with complaints about aquaplaning in the later stage of the session. The first fifty minutes of the session saw most drivers only drive installation laps. While the first timed laps were set on full wet tyres, the two Williams cars were the first to go out on intermediate tyres. Nico Rosberg ran with the power unit that he had used briefly at the <a href="/wiki/2015_Italian_Grand_Prix" title="2015 Italian Grand Prix">Italian Grand Prix</a> before it was changed due to a coolant leak. He switched back to the one he used in the previous race in Singapore for the rest of the weekend. <a href="/wiki/Jolyon_Palmer" title="Jolyon Palmer">Jolyon Palmer</a> again took over for <a href="/wiki/Romain_Grosjean" title="Romain Grosjean">Romain Grosjean</a> at Lotus, but he did not set a timed lap.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>While the rain had stopped at the beginning of the second session, the track was still damp and all drivers initially ran on intermediate tyres. Daniil Kvyat eventually set the fastest time at 1:48.277, just 0.023 seconds ahead of Nico Rosberg, with Lewis Hamilton in third, half a second down on Kvyat. Sebastian Vettel set the fifth fastest time while driving the most timed laps of the session at nineteen. Sainz, fastest in first practice, heard "strange noises" from his power unit, but eventually finished seventh fastest. Felipe Massa was the only Williams driver to run, as Valtteri Bottas saved wet-weather-tyres. After more than half an hour, more rain fell and a number of drivers went out to get more running on the full-wet tyres. This included <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>, who had been forced to wait forty minutes to start his running due to an unspecified issue with the power unit.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The third session on Saturday was held in dry conditions. The two Mercedes drivers Nico Rosberg and Lewis Hamilton set the fastest times, with Rosberg being about three-tenths of a second quicker than Hamilton. Red Bull driver <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> was third, half a second down on Rosberg's 1:33.995 lap time. Kvyat in the other Red Bull did not have a good session, ending up eleventh fastest after running wide at both Spoon and the Degner corners. Behind Ricciardo, the Williams cars led the two Ferrari drivers in a session where drivers did a lot of laps to make up for the limited running on Friday.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Hamilton_Japan_2015.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/aa/Hamilton_Japan_2015.jpg/220px-Hamilton_Japan_2015.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="125" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/aa/Hamilton_Japan_2015.jpg/330px-Hamilton_Japan_2015.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/aa/Hamilton_Japan_2015.jpg/440px-Hamilton_Japan_2015.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4454" data-file-height="2526" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Hamilton_Japan_2015.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> qualified second but went on to win the race.</div></div></div>
<p>Qualifying consisted of three parts, 18, 15 and 12 minutes in length respectively, with five drivers eliminated from competing after each of the first two sessions.<sup id="cite_ref-pracreg_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pracreg-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The first part of qualifying (Q1) was disrupted towards the end, when <a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso</a> came to a halt at the hairpin, preventing a number of drivers from improving on their lap times. <a href="/wiki/Marcus_Ericsson" title="Marcus Ericsson">Marcus Ericsson</a>, whose first timed lap had been hampered by a spin, was one of the affected drivers and was eliminated in 17th place.<sup id="cite_ref-qualf1fan_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualf1fan-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> also did not make it into Q2. While his second run was also affected by Verstappen, he lost time in his first due to a failure of communication concerning his engine mode.<sup id="cite_ref-mclarenqual_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mclarenqual-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Also eliminated were the second <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a> of <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Nasr" title="Felipe Nasr">Felipe Nasr</a> and both Manor Marussia cars.<sup id="cite_ref-qualf1fan_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualf1fan-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Just as in Q1, the two Mercedes cars set the fastest times in Q2 as well. Verstappen was unable to continue and therefore only four of the running cars were to be eliminated. Those were Hülkenberg, the second Toro Rosso of Sainz, <a href="/wiki/Pastor_Maldonado" title="Pastor Maldonado">Pastor Maldonado</a> and Fernando Alonso, who said his lap was likely the best he had ever driven at the circuit.<sup id="cite_ref-qualf1fan_19-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualf1fan-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-mclarenqual_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mclarenqual-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The top ten drivers contested Q3 for pole position and it was the two Mercedes drivers who set the fastest times once more, with Rosberg beating teammate Hamilton by only 0.076 seconds. Valtteri Bottas put his Williams in third while Massa lost fourth on the grid to the Ferrari of Sebastian Vettel due to a mistake towards the end of the lap. Behind Massa, Kimi Räikkönen took sixth place with what he described as an "average" lap.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> The positions remained unchanged as the drivers were prevented from setting better lap times, when Daniil Kvyat crashed into the barriers at turn ten, bringing out the <a href="/wiki/Racing_flags#Red_flag" title="Racing flags">red flag</a>. He had touched the grass and over-corrected, and his car rolled over on impact, but Kvyat was unharmed. With only 36 seconds of the session remaining after the red flags were lifted, it was impossible to set new fast laps.<sup id="cite_ref-qualf1fan_19-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualf1fan-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Lewis Hamilton was disappointed, since he had been one and a half tenths up on his teammate on his second fast lap when the red flags came out.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Ricciardo_Japan_2015.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/ff/Ricciardo_Japan_2015.jpg/220px-Ricciardo_Japan_2015.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/ff/Ricciardo_Japan_2015.jpg/330px-Ricciardo_Japan_2015.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/ff/Ricciardo_Japan_2015.jpg/440px-Ricciardo_Japan_2015.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4311" data-file-height="2874" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Ricciardo_Japan_2015.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A first lap puncture dropped <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> down the field, he finished fifteenth.</div></div></div>
<p>At the start of the race, Lewis Hamilton was able to pass teammate Nico Rosberg, who had less power available due to a temperature problem.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Rosberg lost two more positions to Vettel and Bottas going into turn two, as he "had to avoid collision" with Hamilton.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-bbcrace_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcrace-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Further back, Daniel Ricciardo and Felipe Massa touched, resulting in flat tyres for both drivers, with Sergio Pérez also having to go into the pit lane with a puncture. Hamilton led Vettel by 1.6 seconds after the first lap, increasing his advantage over the next couple of laps. Alonso, who had moved up the order at the start, lost ninth position to Sainz on lap four. Meanwhile, Rosberg tried to pass Bottas for third, but was told to hold off as his engine was overheating. By lap eight, Daniil Kvyat had moved up into 15th position, having started from the pit lane. Fernando Alonso was the first to come into the pit lane for a scheduled stop on lap nine. By lap eleven, Rosberg was told to resume his attempts to overtake Bottas, who in turn went into the garage for his first stop, rejoining in sixth place.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcrace_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcrace-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Sebastian Vettel was the next one of the front runners to pit, coming in on lap 13, while teammate Kimi Räikkönen followed a lap later. Rosberg made a pit stop on lap 15 and emerged behind Bottas, overtaking him two laps later. Meanwhile, Hamilton went into the pit lane for a tyre change on lap 16. By lap 18, Hamilton was leading Vettel by 8.1 seconds, with Rosberg a further 3.8 seconds behind. Max Verstappen entered the point scoring ranks with a pass on Fernando Alonso into turn one on lap 26. Two laps later, his Toro Rosso teammate Sainz damaged his front wing when hitting a bollard on his way into the pit lane, leading to a front wing change.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcrace_25-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcrace-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At the second round of pit stops, Räikkönen managed to get ahead of Bottas, who made pit stops simultaneously with Nico Rosberg on lap 29. Another two laps later, Vettel made a pit stop and emerged behind Rosberg, who claimed second, about ten seconds down on Hamilton, who made a pit stop on lap 32. Bottas was briefly challenged by the Lotus of Romain Grosjean, but Grosjean went off track on lap 33, while <a href="/wiki/Will_Stevens" title="Will Stevens">Will Stevens</a> received a five-second penalty for speeding in the pit lane. On lap 43, Marcus Ericsson overtook Pérez for 12th and two laps later, Verstappen moved ahead of teammate Sainz for ninth. Pérez lost another position to Kvyat on lap 45. Another lap later, Stevens went into a spin while fighting for position with <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Rossi" title="Alexander Rossi">Alexander Rossi</a>, who finished ahead of his more experienced teammate for the second race in a row. By lap 49, Kvyat moved ahead of Ericsson as well to claim 13th position. Two laps from the end, Felipe Nasr became the first retirement of the race, albeit he would be classified in last, four laps behind the winner. Lewis Hamilton took victory comfortably, finishing 18.9 seconds ahead of Rosberg.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcrace_25-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcrace-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the podium interviews, conducted by Kai Ebel, a German TV reporter, Lewis Hamilton stressed that it was important to him to "strike back" after his retirement in Singapore. Later on in the press conference, he commented on equalling <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a>'s number of victories: "Also knowing that this would be the race that I would equal Ayrton, who won here and who had quite an interesting [sic] here. So, yeah, quite an emotional day. But to be honest I'm not a teary guy, so I'm just full of joy and happiness and light and I'm really grateful for [...] all the people who have helped me get to where I am today and this team, because without them I would not be here." Nico Rosberg acknowledged that "it's going the wrong way" in terms of his championship hopes as he would have needed a victory and declared: "Just need to try to win next time out." Sebastian Vettel lamented his chances at second place, as he said that he could have stayed in front of Rosberg had he made a pit stop a lap earlier during the second round of stops, However, he stated that "with hindsight, it's always easy so, nevertheless, it's a great day".<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton followed up on his delight at matching Senna later on, saying: "For me to win here at a race where I loved watching Ayrton drive and to match his wins, it doesn't feel real at the moment." Senna had won his three world championship titles at Suzuka.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>While successful on track, the Mercedes team got into a discussion with Formula One chief executive <a href="/wiki/Bernie_Ecclestone" title="Bernie Ecclestone">Bernie Ecclestone</a> over the TV coverage time their drivers received during the Grand Prix. Over the ninety-minute race, Mercedes cars were visible for only six minutes, leading to their non-executive chairman <a href="/wiki/Niki_Lauda" title="Niki Lauda">Niki Lauda</a> saying: "I was watching TV all day long, and funny enough I saw Saubers and a lot of Honda cars, but I don't know why [...]. Somebody must do the filming here. I have to ask what's wrong with him. I want to see Bernie next week and ask him what is the reason."<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Speaking about hitting the pit bollard at his stop, Carlos Sainz Jr. said it had been a "rookie error" due to his urgency to overtake Pastor Maldonado during the pit stops.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Daniil Kvyat, who finished 13th after starting in the pit lane complained about the race, saying: "It was pretty boring for me out there this afternoon because a combination of circumstances meant I had to just sit back and was unable to attack. [...] In the end, I did manage to overtake some people, but it wasn't great. It's frustrating and annoying, but there was nothing I could do."<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Alonso_Japan_2015.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/25/Alonso_Japan_2015.jpg/220px-Alonso_Japan_2015.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="146" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/25/Alonso_Japan_2015.jpg/330px-Alonso_Japan_2015.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/25/Alonso_Japan_2015.jpg/440px-Alonso_Japan_2015.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4928" data-file-height="3280" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Alonso_Japan_2015.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A radio message from <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> sparked controversy at <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>Friction emerged at McLaren over the race weekend. Fernando Alonso publicly criticised the power unit provided by local company <a href="/wiki/Honda" title="Honda">Honda</a>. When being overtaken by Max Verstappen on lap 27, Alonso yelled "GP2 engine, GP2!" on the team radio, comparing his power unit with those of Formula One's <a href="/wiki/GP2_Series" title="GP2 Series">feeder series</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-radiotrans_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-radiotrans-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Alonso elaborated after the race, saying: "I feel embarrassed when I'm racing sometimes because it's frustrating when you see the other cars making mistakes, going off the racing line, getting sideways. You look in the mirror on the straight to look for them and they are already side by side with you. The deficit we have on power is like another category."<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Team CEO <a href="/wiki/Ron_Dennis" title="Ron Dennis">Ron Dennis</a> in turn criticised Alonso for his outspoken words, saying: "It doesn't show the professionalism I would like all our drivers to show."<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Jenson Button had expounded the problems of the power unit on team radio as well, lamenting the fact that other cars had been able to pass him into 130R corner.<sup id="cite_ref-radiotrans_32-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-radiotrans-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> The row in the team, which <i><a href="/wiki/The_Daily_Telegraph" title="The Daily Telegraph">The Daily Telegraph</a></i> described as being a "complete meltdown", came amidst speculation about the future of both drivers. Ron Dennis admitted publicly to not having dealt with the question of Jenson Button's role in <a href="/wiki/2016_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2016 Formula One World Championship">2016</a> earlier, following much debate over the driver's future.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> However, speculation ended when Button was confirmed as a McLaren driver for 2016 just five days later.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> After the race, Alonso had also left his future with the team open, but made it clear a day later that he intended to end his career with the team, writing: "No one should have any doubt that I have three years with McLaren and my career in F1 will end with this team, hopefully winning everything." About his comments on the team radio he said: "We keep working hard. We all want to win and sometimes transmit the team radios, but it should be private chats."<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As a result of the race, Hamilton extended his lead over teammate Nico Rosberg in the Drivers' Championship to 48 points, with Sebastian Vettel a further eleven points behind in third. Mercedes cemented their lead in the Constructors' standings, now leading Ferrari by 506 to 337 points, with Williams in third, an additional 129 points behind.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
