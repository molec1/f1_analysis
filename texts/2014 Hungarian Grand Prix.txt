<p>The <b>2014 Hungarian Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>Formula 1 Pirelli Magyar Nagydíj 2014</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Auto_racing" title="Auto racing">motor race</a> held on 27 July 2014 at the <a href="/wiki/Hungaroring" title="Hungaroring">Hungaroring</a> in <a href="/wiki/Mogyor%C3%B3d" title="Mogyoród">Mogyoród</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hungary" title="Hungary">Hungary</a>. It was the eleventh round of the <a href="/wiki/2014_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2014 Formula One season">2014 Formula One season</a> and the 30th <a href="/wiki/Hungarian_Grand_Prix" title="Hungarian Grand Prix">Hungarian Grand Prix</a>, and the 29th time it had been held as a round of the World Championship. The 70-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a> team after starting from fourth position. <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> finished second in a <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> third in a <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a>.
</p><p>The race was only the third <a href="/wiki/Hungarian_Grand_Prix" title="Hungarian Grand Prix">Hungarian Grand Prix</a> to be rain-affected, after the <a href="/wiki/2006_Hungarian_Grand_Prix" title="2006 Hungarian Grand Prix">2006</a> and <a href="/wiki/2011_Hungarian_Grand_Prix" title="2011 Hungarian Grand Prix">2011</a> races. Thunderstorms and heavy rain soaked the track at around lunchtime, forcing the drivers to start on intermediates before the track dried out later on in the race. This race was the most recent podium finish for <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2014_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2014_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Grand Prix was contested by eleven teams, each of two drivers. The teams, also known as <a href="/wiki/Formula_One#Constructors" title="Formula One">constructors</a>, were <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes-Benz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lotus_F1" title="Lotus F1">Lotus</a>, <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>, <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso</a>, <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marussia_F1" title="Marussia F1">Marussia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Caterham_F1" title="Caterham F1">Caterham</a>. <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_tyres" title="Formula One tyres">Tyre</a> supplier <a href="/wiki/Pirelli" title="Pirelli">Pirelli</a> brought four different tyre types to the race: two dry compounds, the medium "primes" and the soft "options" and two <a href="/wiki/Rain_tyre" title="Rain tyre">wet-weather compounds</a>, the intermediate and full wet.<sup id="cite_ref-preview_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-preview-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Drag_reduction_system" title="Drag reduction system">drag reduction system</a> (DRS) had two activation zones for the race; one was on the start/finish straight from the final to first corners, and the second from turns 1 and 2.<sup id="cite_ref-preview_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-preview-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Going into the race, Mercedes driver <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> led the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a> with 190 points, ahead of teammate <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> on 176 points and <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> on 106. <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> was fourth with 97 points while <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a> was fifth on 91 points.<sup id="cite_ref-preview_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-preview-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a> Mercedes were leading with 366 points, Red Bull and Williams were second and third with 188 and 121 points respectively, while Ferrari with 116 and Force India on 98 points rounded out the top five positions.<sup id="cite_ref-preview_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-preview-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Mercedes had so far dominated the championship, winning nine out of the ten previous races, with Ricciardo winning the <a href="/wiki/2014_Canadian_Grand_Prix" title="2014 Canadian Grand Prix">Canadian Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-preview_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-preview-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Magnussen" title="Kevin Magnussen">Kevin Magnussen</a> and Bottas had each gained second-place finishes, and Vettel, Alonso, <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a> had achieved third place podium finishes.<sup id="cite_ref-preview_3-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-preview-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Several teams made technical changes to their cars for the Grand Prix. Marussia changed one of their <a href="/wiki/Marussia_MR03" title="Marussia MR03">MR03</a> chassis for <a href="/wiki/Max_Chilton" title="Max Chilton">Max Chilton</a> following balance and electrical issues that arose during the <a href="/wiki/2014_Austrian_Grand_Prix" title="2014 Austrian Grand Prix">Austrian Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Williams introduced a small wing section, aimed at increasing the amount of <a href="/wiki/Downforce" title="Downforce">downforce</a>, and therefore grip, produced by the bodywork.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Lotus brought new front wing endplates, which were designed to scavenge airflow more efficiently.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice_and_qualifying">Practice and qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2014_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Practice and qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Three practice sessions were held before the race; two 90 minute sessions on Friday and one lasting an hour on Saturday.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton was the fastest driver in the first practice session, ahead of Rosberg and Ferrari driver <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a>. Chilton's Marussia car was afflicted by an oil leak, resulting in a fire;<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> this restricted him to completing only five timed laps, and he was 21st overall.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton was fastest in the second session, ahead of Rosberg, Vettel and Alonso&#160;— although Hamilton had struggled with tyre grip throughout the session.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The third session on Saturday morning saw Hamilton again fastest, ahead of Rosberg, Ricciardo and Vettel.
</p><p>The qualifying session on Saturday afternoon was split into three parts. The first part ran for 18 minutes and eliminated the cars from qualifying that finished the session 17th or lower. During this session, the 107% rule was in effect, which necessitated each driver set a time within <a href="/wiki/107%25_rule" title="107% rule">107%</a> of the quickest lap to qualify for the race. The second part of qualifying lasted 15 minutes and eliminated cars that finished in positions 11 to 16. The final part of qualifying determined the positions from first to tenth, and decided pole position.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Rosberg achieved his sixth <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> of the season, and his first at the Hungaroring, with a time of 1:22.715.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> He was joined on the front row by Vettel. Hamilton's car caught fire during qualifying before he could post a time, and so he started the race from the back row.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2014_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The race started at 14:00 local time. The track was wet before the race, as thunderstorms had hit the area throughout the day. The air temperature ranged between 20 to 21&#160;°C (68 to 70&#160;°F), with the track temperature between 27 to 28&#160;°C (81 to 82&#160;°F).<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>All the runners began the race on intermediate tyres.<sup id="cite_ref-race_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-race-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> At the start of the race, <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> led into the first corner, while <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a> overtook <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> on the outside of turn one to take second position. <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> also overtook Vettel shortly after turn one to take third, but Vettel repassed him later on in the lap. Having started from the pit lane, <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> had not been permitted to participate in the formation lap. As a result, his brakes were cold, and on the first lap he spun off at turn two. The front left of his car lightly scraped against the barriers, mildly damaging the front wing, but otherwise his car sustained no serious damage and Hamilton rejoined the track, albeit last in 22nd position.
</p><p>By lap 9, Rosberg had pulled out an advantage over the field and Hamilton had made his way up to 13th, but a crash by <a href="/wiki/Marcus_Ericsson" title="Marcus Ericsson">Marcus Ericsson</a> meant that the safety car was deployed. All the drivers pitted under the safety car&#160;– all switching to dry tyres except the two McLarens who put on more intermediates&#160;–  but the timing of the safety car's deployment meant that the top four runners (Rosberg, Bottas, Vettel and Alonso) missed the pit entry and had to do the entire lap behind the safety car before they could pit, costing them a considerable amount of time to the cars behind. Following the pit stops, Rosberg had moved down from 1st to 4th, and Bottas lost out massively to move down from 2nd to 11th, while Hamilton maintained 13th. <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> benefited greatly from the safety car timing and was now leading the race. The safety car stayed out for longer than usual as <a href="/wiki/Romain_Grosjean" title="Romain Grosjean">Romain Grosjean</a> lost control of his car and crashed under safety car conditions, but racing resumed on lap 14 and <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> quickly passed Ricciardo for the lead. The following lap Rosberg was overtaken by <a href="/wiki/Jean-%C3%89ric_Vergne" title="Jean-Éric Vergne">Jean-Éric Vergne</a>, demoting him to fifth, while Hamilton overtook four cars in one lap to move up to ninth. Having done only two laps on the intermediates, Button pitted from the lead for dry tyres on lap 16 as it became clear that the track was drying. McLaren's decision to put him on a second set of intermediates at his first stop had cost him greatly and he rejoined down the order in 16th.
</p><p>On lap 17 <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a> retired after colliding with his teammate <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a>, though Pérez avoided any major damage. At this point Hamilton was up to seventh behind Vettel and only a second back from Rosberg, who was still fifth and stuck behind Vergne. Alonso was excelling in the conditions and had moved up to third by lap 18. On lap 23 Pérez spun at the final corner and crashed into the pit wall, meaning that Force India had suffered their first double retirement of the season. This brought out another safety car, prompting Ricciardo and Massa to pit from first and second, rejoining in sixth and seventh behind Alonso, Vergne, Rosberg, Vettel and Hamilton, all of whom stayed out under the safety car. The safety car came in on lap 27, and Alonso proceeded to pull out a lead over a train of cars reaching from second to fifth. The train of cars was led by Vergne, who was still holding up Rosberg, closely followed by Vettel and Hamilton. On lap 33 Rosberg pitted for softs from third and rejoined in 13th, while Vettel spun at the final corner&#160;– in the same place as Perez did&#160;– but miraculously managed to avoid contact with the wall. This allowed Hamilton to catch up to Vergne, and the following lap he overtook Vergne on the outside at turn four. On lap 39 Alonso pitted for soft tyres, and a lap later Hamilton pitted for medium tyres, rejoining in fourth and fifth respectively. This promoted Ricciardo back into the lead of the race. Rosberg had been making up positions but had been jumped by Hamilton in the pit stops and was down in ninth.
</p><p>By lap 47, Rosberg had made further progress and was now almost a second behind Hamilton, with no cars separating them. Hamilton was given team orders to let Rosberg past, as Rosberg was on the softer tyre and had one more stop to make, while Hamilton was on a two stop strategy and did not have to pit again. However, Hamilton did not concede the place, replying on the radio: "I'm not slowing down for Nico. If he gets close enough to overtake, he can overtake me."<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton was aware that he was effectively racing Rosberg at this point, and he did not want to lose a considerable amount of time by lifting to let his title rival through, as Rosberg was still over a second behind him.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Race-leader Ricciardo and Rosberg pitted for soft tyres on laps 54 and 56 respectively, rejoining in fourth and seventh. By lap 62, Alonso, Hamilton and Ricciardo were running very closely in first, second and third, with Hamilton 0.4 seconds behind Alonso and Ricciardo 0.4 seconds behind Hamilton. Meanwhile, Nico Rosberg was running fourth, over twenty seconds behind Ricciardo, but was rapidly closing the gap by up to three seconds a lap on his newer soft tyres, setting the fastest lap of the race while doing so. On lap 63, Alonso cut the chicane, but avoided any penalty and maintained the race lead. Ricciardo passed Hamilton around the outside of turn two on lap 67, and then the following lap passed Alonso at turn one for the race lead with two laps to go. Alonso continued to defend his position from Hamilton, but both of their tyres had fallen off of their respective operating cliffs&#160;– having both done close to 30 laps on their tyres&#160;– and by the final lap Rosberg had caught up to the back of them. Rosberg attempted to overtake Hamilton on the outside into turn two, but Hamilton defended and held position. Ricciardo crossed the line to win his second race of the season by 5.2 seconds, with Alonso coming second and Hamilton and Rosberg close behind in third and fourth.<sup id="cite_ref-race_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-race-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2014_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The top three drivers appeared on the podium to collect their trophies, and were interviewed by a selected media delegate before the subsequent press conference.
</p><p>The standings barely changed in both the drivers and constructors championships with only one change in both. With a 3rd place, <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> closed the gap on <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> to eleven points.
</p><p>After the race, Mercedes' non-executive chairman <a href="/wiki/Niki_Lauda" title="Niki Lauda">Niki Lauda</a> said that he agreed with Hamilton's decision to ignore team orders, and stated that Mercedes had panicked in issuing the orders.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Both Mercedes cars suffered from rear brake problems at certain points during the race, with Rosberg's brakes overheating behind the first safety car and Hamilton encountering a similar problem in the middle stages of the race. Hamilton's car also suffered from a fuel pressure problem shortly after the rear brake problem.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Upon analysing Hamilton's car after the race, Mercedes discovered that the fuel pressure issue had led to a considerable loss in engine power shortly after his second pitstop on lap 40. This cost Hamilton around 0.5 seconds per lap for the rest of the race - potentially costing him victory.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>This race marked the most recent podium finish for <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>. He was the most recent <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_drivers_from_Spain" title="Formula One drivers from Spain">Spanish driver</a> to finish on the podium until <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Sainz,_Jr." class="mw-redirect" title="Carlos Sainz, Jr.">Carlos Sainz, Jr.</a> achieved third at the <a href="/wiki/2019_Brazilian_Grand_Prix" title="2019 Brazilian Grand Prix">2019 Brazilian Grand Prix</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
