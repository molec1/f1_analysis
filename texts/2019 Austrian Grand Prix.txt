<p>The <b>2019 Austrian Grand Prix</b> (formally known as the <b>Formula 1 myWorld Großer Preis von Österreich 2019</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Auto_racing" title="Auto racing">motor race</a> held on 30 June 2019 at the <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Ring" title="Red Bull Ring">Red Bull Ring</a> in <a href="/wiki/Spielberg,_Styria" title="Spielberg, Styria">Spielberg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Austria" title="Austria">Austria</a>. The race was the 9th round of the <a href="/wiki/2019_FIA_Formula_One_World_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="2019 FIA Formula One World Championship">2019 FIA Formula One World Championship</a>. The race marked the 33rd running of the <a href="/wiki/Austrian_Grand_Prix" title="Austrian Grand Prix">Austrian Grand Prix</a> and the 32nd time it had been held as a round of the Formula One World Championship since the series inception in <a href="/wiki/1950_Formula_One_season" title="1950 Formula One season">1950</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Details_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Details-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The victory of <a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing_RB15" title="Red Bull Racing RB15">Red Bull Racing RB15</a> was the first win for a <a href="/wiki/Honda_in_Formula_One" title="Honda in Formula One">Honda</a>-powered F1 car since <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2006_Hungarian_Grand_Prix" title="2006 Hungarian Grand Prix">2006 Hungarian Grand Prix</a> and he also became the first non-Mercedes driver to win a race in 2019.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2019_Austrian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Championship_standings_before_the_race">Championship standings before the race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2019_Austrian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Championship standings before the race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Heading into the race <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> had a 36-point advantage over team mate <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a> in the Drivers' Championship. In the Constructors' Championship  <a href="/wiki/Mercedes_in_Formula_One" class="mw-redirect" title="Mercedes in Formula One">Mercedes</a> held a 140-point advantage over <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Entrants">Entrants</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2019_Austrian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Entrants">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Further information: <a href="/wiki/2019_Formula_One_World_Championship#Entries" title="2019 Formula One World Championship">2019 Formula One World Championship §&#160;Entries</a></div>
<p>The drivers and teams entered were the same as those on the season entry list with no additional stand-in drivers for either the race or practice.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Penalties">Penalties</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2019_Austrian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Penalties">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Carlos_Sainz_Jr." title="Carlos Sainz Jr.">Carlos Sainz Jr.</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Albon" title="Alexander Albon">Alexander Albon</a> were required to start from the back of the grid for exceeding their quotas for power unit components.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Magnussen" title="Kevin Magnussen">Kevin Magnussen</a> received five-place grid penalties for exceeding their quota for power unit components and for a gearbox change, respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Free_practice">Free practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2019_Austrian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Free practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> set the fastest time in first practice with <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> second. <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a> was third ahead of <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>’s <a href="/wiki/Charles_Leclerc" title="Charles Leclerc">Charles Leclerc</a> in fourth.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Second practice was eventful as Vettel spun and both Bottas and <a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a> crashed. Leclerc was fastest ahead of Bottas, <a href="/wiki/Pierre_Gasly" title="Pierre Gasly">Pierre Gasly</a> and Hamilton.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In third practice Leclerc was fastest again ahead of Hamilton, Bottas and Vettel.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

Additionally, he was required to start from the pit lane for changing his front wing under parc fermé conditions.</li></ul>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_summary">Race summary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2019_Austrian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Race summary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Whilst <a href="/wiki/Charles_Leclerc" title="Charles Leclerc">Charles Leclerc</a> started the race well from pole position, <a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a> encountered an issue with his anti-stall, dropping from 2nd down to 8th place by turn 2. <a href="/wiki/Lando_Norris" title="Lando Norris">Lando Norris</a> briefly battled <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> for 3rd place, and <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> ran as high as 4th. Räikkönen held the position until lap 7, when he was passed by <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a>, who had started 9th. By lap 9, Verstappen had recovered after his poor start, passing Räikkönen and Norris. <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Magnussen" title="Kevin Magnussen">Kevin Magnussen</a> pitted on lap 12, dropping him to the back, but was handed a drive-through penalty soon afterwards after the stewards found he had over-stepped his grid line at the beginning of the race and started too far forward. He would only recover one place during the race, and eventually crossed the finish line in 19th.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> pitted on lap 22, with Bottas emerging in clear air in 4th, but with Vettel stuck behind Norris, Räikkönen and <a href="/wiki/Pierre_Gasly" title="Pierre Gasly">Pierre Gasly</a> in 8th after a communication issue saw his pit-stop take longer than expected. Leclerc pitted on the following lap, handing the lead of the race to Hamilton. At this stage of the race, the Mercedes cars were suffering from the high air and track temperatures. Hamilton repeatedly ran wide at turn 1, driving over the sausage kerb. This eventually caused damage to his front wing, forcing him to pit for a replacement on lap 31 and dropping him to 5th place. Verstappen inherited the lead, but pitted on the following lap; Leclerc thus regained 1st place. 
</p><p>Verstappen emerged from the pits in 4th place, and began his charge towards the front. He passed Vettel on Lap 50, on the straight after turn 2. Vettel subsequently pitted on the following lap. Verstappen then passed Bottas into turn 2 on lap 56. Finally, he caught up with Leclerc at the front, passing him into turn 2 with three laps to go. On lap 69, Verstappen took a wide line through the corner on the inside of Leclerc, making contact and resulting in Leclerc leaving the track and driving over the kerb. Verstappen held on to his lead until the chequered flag, and the stewards later deemed his overtake legal. Vettel passed Hamilton on the final lap to take 4th place.
</p><p>Verstappen's victory marked the first win for a <a href="/wiki/Honda_in_Formula_One" title="Honda in Formula One">Honda</a>-powered F1 car since <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2006_Hungarian_Grand_Prix" title="2006 Hungarian Grand Prix">2006 Hungarian Grand Prix</a>. The race was also the first race in the season not to be won by a <a href="/wiki/Mercedes_in_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Mercedes in F1">Mercedes</a> driver, as <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a> finished in third and <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> fifth. <a href="/wiki/Antonio_Giovinazzi" title="Antonio Giovinazzi">Antonio Giovinazzi</a> scored his first ever career points in Formula One by finishing tenth place.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> This also marked the first race in which every driver saw the chequered flag since the <a href="/wiki/2016_Japanese_Grand_Prix" title="2016 Japanese Grand Prix">2016 Japanese Grand Prix</a>.
</p>
