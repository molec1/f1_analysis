<p>The <b>1992 Japanese Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>XVIII Fuji Television Japanese Grand Prix</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at <a href="/wiki/Suzuka_Circuit" class="mw-redirect" title="Suzuka Circuit">Suzuka</a> on 25 October 1992. The race, contested over 53 laps, was the fifteenth race of the <a href="/wiki/1992_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1992 Formula One World Championship">1992 Formula One World Championship</a> and was won by <a href="/wiki/Riccardo_Patrese" title="Riccardo Patrese">Riccardo Patrese</a> driving a <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW14" title="Williams FW14">Williams</a>-<a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> second for the <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> team and <a href="/wiki/Martin_Brundle" title="Martin Brundle">Martin Brundle</a> third for <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a>. This was Patrese's sixth and final Grand Prix victory. This was the final win by an <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italian</a> driver until <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> won the <a href="/wiki/2003_Brazilian_Grand_Prix" title="2003 Brazilian Grand Prix">2003 Brazilian Grand Prix</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-race">Pre-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1992_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Pre-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Two driver changes took place before the race: <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> replaced <a href="/wiki/Ivan_Capelli" title="Ivan Capelli">Ivan Capelli</a> with their test driver <a href="/wiki/Nicola_Larini" title="Nicola Larini">Nicola Larini</a>, while <a href="/wiki/Karl_Wendlinger" title="Karl Wendlinger">Karl Wendlinger</a> left <a href="/wiki/March_Engineering" title="March Engineering">March</a> to join <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a>'s preparatory program ahead of the Swiss team's debut in 1993. His place was taken by <a href="/wiki/Jan_Lammers" title="Jan Lammers">Jan Lammers</a>, making his first F1 start for over 10 years.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1992_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying_report">Qualifying report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1992_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Qualifying report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In qualifying, <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a>' <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> took his 13th pole position of the season, equalling the record set by <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> in <a href="/wiki/1988_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1988 Formula One World Championship">1988</a> and <a href="/wiki/1989_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1989 Formula One World Championship">1989</a>. Team-mate <a href="/wiki/Riccardo_Patrese" title="Riccardo Patrese">Riccardo Patrese</a> was alongside him on the front row, while the <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLarens</a> of Senna and <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> filled the second row. <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a> was fifth, followed by the two <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Lotuses</a> of <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Herbert" title="Johnny Herbert">Johnny Herbert</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a>. The top ten was completed by <a href="/wiki/%C3%89rik_Comas" title="Érik Comas">Érik Comas</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Equipe_Ligier" title="Equipe Ligier">Ligier</a>, <a href="/wiki/Andrea_de_Cesaris" title="Andrea de Cesaris">Andrea de Cesaris</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_Racing" title="Tyrrell Racing">Tyrrell</a> and <a href="/wiki/Thierry_Boutsen" title="Thierry Boutsen">Thierry Boutsen</a> in the second Ligier.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1992_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_report">Race report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1992_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Race report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Mansell made a fast start and at the end of the first lap led Patrese by three seconds. Senna held on to third before becoming the race's first retirement on lap 3 with an engine failure. Meanwhile, Larini, who had qualified 11th, stalled on the grid and fell to last, while Boutsen's gearbox failed on lap 4. <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Grouillard" title="Olivier Grouillard">Olivier Grouillard</a> in the second Tyrrell spun off and crashed at Spoon curve on lap 7.
</p><p>Berger made an early pit stop and rejoined the race in sixth place, behind Schumacher and the two Lotuses. On lap 13, Schumacher retired with gearbox failure, his only mechanical retirement of the year; Herbert's gearbox also failed two laps later. After the mid-race pit stops for tyres, Mansell retained a comfortable lead over Patrese, while Berger moved ahead of Häkkinen into third and <a href="/wiki/Martin_Brundle" title="Martin Brundle">Martin Brundle</a>, who had only qualified 13th in the second Benetton, moved up to fifth ahead of Comas and de Cesaris. <a href="/wiki/Maur%C3%ADcio_Gugelmin" title="Maurício Gugelmin">Maurício Gugelmin</a> in the second Jordan had also spun off the track and crashed at 130R leaving debris on the track by lap 23.
</p><p>On lap 36, Mansell slowed and Patrese moved ahead. The following lap, Comas retired with an engine failure on the same. The two Venturi Larousse cars collided at the chicanes as they were both on lap 40 at the time as <a href="/wiki/Bertrand_Gachot" title="Bertrand Gachot">Bertrand Gachot</a> hit his Japanese teammate <a href="/wiki/Ukyo_Katayama" title="Ukyo Katayama">Ukyo Katayama</a> and went off into the gravel trap, who managed to carry on and pit in for fresh tyres (despite making contact). On lap 45, both Mansell and Häkkinen suffered engine failures of their own; this moved Berger and Brundle into second and third respectively. Patrese cruised to his sixth and final Grand Prix victory, finishing 13 seconds ahead of Berger with Brundle a further minute back; the top six was completed by de Cesaris, <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a> in the second Ferrari and <a href="/wiki/Christian_Fittipaldi" title="Christian Fittipaldi">Christian Fittipaldi</a>, who scored his first point in Formula One and the only point of the season for the <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a> team.
</p>
