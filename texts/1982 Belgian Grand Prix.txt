<p>The <b>1982 Belgian Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at <a href="/wiki/Circuit_Zolder" title="Circuit Zolder">Zolder</a> on 9 May 1982. It was the fifth round of the <a href="/wiki/1982_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="1982 Formula One season">1982 Formula One season</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying_and_death_of_Gilles_Villeneuve">Qualifying and death of Gilles Villeneuve</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1982_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Qualifying and death of Gilles Villeneuve">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Canadian driver <a href="/wiki/Gilles_Villeneuve" title="Gilles Villeneuve">Gilles Villeneuve</a> was killed in an accident during the final qualifying session. At the time of the crash, his teammate <a href="/wiki/Didier_Pironi" title="Didier Pironi">Didier Pironi</a> had set a time 0.1s faster than Villeneuve for sixth place. Contemporary and more recent writers say that he was attempting to improve his time on his final lap. Some suggest that he was specifically aiming to beat Pironi due to bitterness at being passed by him two weeks earlier in the closing stages of the <a href="/wiki/1982_San_Marino_Grand_Prix" title="1982 San Marino Grand Prix">San Marino Grand Prix</a>, when Villeneuve believed Pironi had been ordered to remain behind him.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Villeneuve's biographer Gerald Donaldson quotes Ferrari <a href="/wiki/Race_engineer" title="Race engineer">race engineer</a> <a href="/wiki/Mauro_Forghieri" title="Mauro Forghieri">Mauro Forghieri</a> as saying that the Canadian, although pressing on in his usual fashion, was returning to the pits on his last set of qualifying tyres when the accident occurred.<sup id="cite_ref-GD1_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GD1-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> If so, he would not have set a time on that lap.
</p><p>With eight minutes of the session left, Villeneuve came over the rise after the first chicane and found <a href="/wiki/Jochen_Mass" title="Jochen Mass">Jochen Mass</a> in the <a href="/wiki/March_Engineering" title="March Engineering">March</a> travelling much more slowly through <i>Butte</i>, the left-handed bend before the <i>Terlamenbocht</i> corner. Mass saw Villeneuve approaching at high speed and moved to the right to let him through on the <a href="/wiki/Racing_line" title="Racing line">racing line</a>. At the same instant Villeneuve also moved right to pass Mass. The Ferrari hit the back of the March and was launched into the air at a speed estimated at 200&#8211;225&#160;km/h (120&#8211;140&#160;mph). It was airborne for over 100 metres before nosediving into the ground and disintegrating as it somersaulted along the edge of the track. Villeneuve, still strapped to his seat but without his helmet, was thrown a further 50 metres from the wreckage into the <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_motorsport_terms#Catch_fence" title="Glossary of motorsport terms">catch fencing</a> on the outside edge of <i>Terlamenbocht</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-GD1_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GD1-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Several drivers stopped and rushed to the scene. <a href="/wiki/John_Watson_(racing_driver)" title="John Watson (racing driver)">John Watson</a> and <a href="/wiki/Derek_Warwick" title="Derek Warwick">Derek Warwick</a> pulled Villeneuve, his face blue, from the catch fencing.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The first doctor arrived on the scene within 35 seconds to find that Villeneuve was not breathing, although his pulse continued throughout; he was <a href="/wiki/Intubate" class="mw-redirect" title="Intubate">intubated</a> and ventilated before being transferred to the circuit medical centre and then by helicopter to University St Raphael Hospital where a fatal <a href="/wiki/Cervical_fracture" title="Cervical fracture">fracture of the neck</a> was diagnosed.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Villeneuve was kept alive on <a href="/wiki/Life_support" title="Life support">life support</a> while his wife travelled to the hospital and the doctors consulted with specialists worldwide. He died at 9:12 that evening.<sup id="cite_ref-GD1_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GD1-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Ferrari team withdrew from the race after the accident and left the circuit. The final eight minutes of the qualifying were run after the crash debris had been removed. No drivers improved their times, leaving the Renaults of <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ren%C3%A9_Arnoux" title="René Arnoux">René Arnoux</a> on the front row of the grid.<sup id="cite_ref-Lang1_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lang1-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Abbreviated_race_summary">Abbreviated race summary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1982_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Abbreviated race summary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>John Watson won the race after taking the lead on the penultimate lap from <a href="/wiki/Keke_Rosberg" title="Keke Rosberg">Keke Rosberg</a>, who was struggling on worn tires. <a href="/wiki/Niki_Lauda" title="Niki Lauda">Niki Lauda</a> finished third on the road but was disqualified when his car was found to be underweight in post-race scrutineering,<sup id="cite_ref-Lang1_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lang1-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> and so the final podium place went to <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Cheever" title="Eddie Cheever">Eddie Cheever</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1982_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Didier Pironi and the whole Ferrari team withdrew and did not compete in the race, the second of only three times Ferrari team did not start a World Championship race in which they entered (see also <a href="/wiki/1950_French_Grand_Prix" title="1950 French Grand Prix">1950 French Grand Prix</a> and <a href="/wiki/1982_Swiss_Grand_Prix" title="1982 Swiss Grand Prix">1982 Swiss Grand Prix</a>). Pironi would be the only Ferrari driver to take part in the subsequent Monaco, Detroit (USA) and Canada rounds.</li>
<li>This race marked the first and only points finish for <a href="/wiki/Chico_Serra" title="Chico Serra">Chico Serra</a>, and last points to <a href="/wiki/Fittipaldi_Automotive" title="Fittipaldi Automotive">Fittipaldi</a>.</li>
<li>First podium: <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Cheever" title="Eddie Cheever">Eddie Cheever</a></li></ul>
