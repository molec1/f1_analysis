<p>The <b>2010 Italian Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>LXXXI Gran Premio Santander d'Italia</b>) was the fourteenth round of the <a href="/wiki/2010_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2010 Formula One season">2010 Formula One season</a>. It was held in <a href="/wiki/Autodromo_Nazionale_di_Monza" title="Autodromo Nazionale di Monza">Autodromo Nazionale di Monza</a>, <a href="/wiki/Monza" title="Monza">Monza</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a> on 12 September 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> won the race from pole position, taking Ferrari's first win on home soil since <a href="/wiki/2006_Italian_Grand_Prix" title="2006 Italian Grand Prix">2006</a> and their last until <a href="/wiki/2019_Italian_Grand_Prix" title="2019 Italian Grand Prix">2019</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On the Wednesday before the race, <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> were summoned before the <a href="/wiki/FIA_World_Motor_Sport_Council" title="FIA World Motor Sport Council">FIA World Motor Sport Council</a> to answer charges related to fixing a race outcome through the use of <a href="/wiki/Team_orders" title="Team orders">team orders</a> – banned since 2003 under Article 39.1 of the sporting regulations following an infamous incident at the <a href="/wiki/2002_Austrian_Grand_Prix" title="2002 Austrian Grand Prix">2002 Austrian Grand Prix</a> – and subsequently bringing the sport into disrepute at the <a href="/wiki/2010_German_Grand_Prix#Post-race" title="2010 German Grand Prix">German Grand Prix</a>. Although Ferrari were fined US$100,000 at the time of the incident, the special meeting of the WMSC opened the door for further sanctions. The tribunal eventually decided against punishing the team further, with a promise to review the ban on team orders, a decision that was received negatively by fans.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Following increased speculation over <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a> and <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> appearing to use flexible bodywork – banned under the sporting regulations – the FIA announced its intention to intensify testing procedures for the second time, having increased lateral load-bearing tests on the front wings at <a href="/wiki/2010_Belgian_Grand_Prix" title="2010 Belgian Grand Prix">Spa-Francorchamps</a>. Speculation within the Belgian paddock suggested that both teams had been using flexible floors to lower the front wings closer to the roadway, giving rise to the FIA's plan for stricter testing at Monza.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> On the Wednesday before the race, <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> admitted to changing the floor of the <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4-25" title="McLaren MP4-25">MP4-25</a> to meet the increased load tests.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> led the drivers' championship and <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> went into this race 3 points behind, since <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> did not score in <a href="/wiki/2010_Belgian_Grand_Prix" title="2010 Belgian Grand Prix">Belgium</a>. They were over a race win behind Hamilton and Webber. <a href="/wiki/Sakon_Yamamoto" title="Sakon Yamamoto">Sakon Yamamoto</a> took <a href="/wiki/Karun_Chandhok" title="Karun Chandhok">Karun Chandhok</a>'s seat again for the Italian Grand Prix.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The first qualifying session saw all six cars from the new teams knocked out, with <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus_(2010%E2%80%9311)" title="Team Lotus (2010–11)">Lotus</a> leading among them in 18th. His teammate <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> was 19th and then came <a href="/wiki/Vitantonio_Liuzzi" title="Vitantonio Liuzzi">Vitantonio Liuzzi</a>, whose <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a> had had technical issues. <a href="/wiki/Timo_Glock" title="Timo Glock">Timo Glock</a> had originally been ahead of Liuzzi in his <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Racing" title="Virgin Racing">Virgin</a>, but he was handed a five place grid penalty for changing his gearbox, putting him at the very back. <a href="/wiki/Lucas_di_Grassi" title="Lucas di Grassi">Lucas di Grassi</a> was 21st in the other Virgin ahead of <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Senna" title="Bruno Senna">Bruno Senna</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sakon_Yamamoto" title="Sakon Yamamoto">Sakon Yamamoto</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Hispania_Racing" class="mw-redirect" title="Hispania Racing">HRTs</a>.
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Italian_GP_starting_grid_(Alonso_and_Button).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/28/Italian_GP_starting_grid_%28Alonso_and_Button%29.jpg/220px-Italian_GP_starting_grid_%28Alonso_and_Button%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="134" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/28/Italian_GP_starting_grid_%28Alonso_and_Button%29.jpg/330px-Italian_GP_starting_grid_%28Alonso_and_Button%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/28/Italian_GP_starting_grid_%28Alonso_and_Button%29.jpg/440px-Italian_GP_starting_grid_%28Alonso_and_Button%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="968" data-file-height="590" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Italian_GP_starting_grid_(Alonso_and_Button).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> started on the front row.</div></div></div>
<p>There were no real surprises in the second qualifying session either, as the two <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">BMW Saubers</a> of <a href="/wiki/Kamui_Kobayashi" title="Kamui Kobayashi">Kamui Kobayashi</a> (13th) and <a href="/wiki/Pedro_de_la_Rosa" title="Pedro de la Rosa">Pedro de la Rosa</a> (17th) were eliminated along with the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rossos</a> of <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9bastien_Buemi" title="Sébastien Buemi">Sébastien Buemi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jaime_Alguersuari" title="Jaime Alguersuari">Jaime Alguersuari</a>, the Force India of <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a> and <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a>. Also eliminated was <a href="/wiki/Vitaly_Petrov" title="Vitaly Petrov">Vitaly Petrov</a>, who qualified his <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a> 15th but was given a five place grid drop for blocking Glock in the first session.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> scraped into the top 10 and qualified tenth in his <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a>, just behind <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a> in the other Renault. It was a good performance from the other Williams of <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a>, who out qualified his teammate to qualify eighth. <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> was probably pleased with seventh in the Mercedes, but then came the championship contenders. A mistake put <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> sixth, and it was a tough day for his <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a> team as <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> could only qualify fourth in the sister <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_RB6" title="Red Bull RB6">RB6</a>. The two <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLarens</a> of <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> had chosen completely different car set ups, and it did not work out for Hamilton who could qualify only fifth. It was looking good for <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> in front of their home crowd, and <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> qualified third. It was their day as Button was pipped to pole by <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>, the 19th pole of the Spaniard's career.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the start of the race, Jenson Button had a fantastic start, jumping his <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> from second past Fernando Alonso's <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>. Alonso, who was alongside his teammate Felipe Massa through the turn one chicane, just clipped the rear of Button's diffuser, but there was only light damage to the McLaren. As the pack headed down to the second chicane of turns three and four, Button's teammate <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>, who was in fourth, tried to pull a move on Massa, but there was contact between the two. Although Lewis tried to carry on, the front track rod was broken and he pulled up at the next corner. Massa managed to carry on unscathed. <a href="/wiki/Kamui_Kobayashi" title="Kamui Kobayashi">Kamui Kobayashi</a> did start the race but retired his <a href="/wiki/BMW_Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="BMW Sauber">BMW Sauber</a> on the first lap with a gearbox failure.
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Heikki_Kovalainen_2010_Italy_(Cropped).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/09/Heikki_Kovalainen_2010_Italy_%28Cropped%29.jpg/220px-Heikki_Kovalainen_2010_Italy_%28Cropped%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="164" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/09/Heikki_Kovalainen_2010_Italy_%28Cropped%29.jpg/330px-Heikki_Kovalainen_2010_Italy_%28Cropped%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/09/Heikki_Kovalainen_2010_Italy_%28Cropped%29.jpg/440px-Heikki_Kovalainen_2010_Italy_%28Cropped%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="988" data-file-height="737" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Heikki_Kovalainen_2010_Italy_(Cropped).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> was slower than teammate <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> for much of the weekend, and finished the race 18th.</div></div></div>
<p>Meanwhile, <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a> was going backwards from his fourth place grid slot, and he was ninth at the end of the first lap. He soon re-passed <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> at the second chicane on lap 6, with a calm and collected move on the seven times world champion. A hydraulic failure made <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Senna" title="Bruno Senna">Bruno Senna</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Hispania_Racing" class="mw-redirect" title="Hispania Racing">HRT</a> the race's third retirement on lap 11.
</p><p>Alonso was harrying Button all the way, and drama occurred further back on lap 20 as Webber's teammate <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a>, who had also had a bad start, dropping from 6th to 7th and was harrying <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a> in the <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a> to take the place back, complained of an engine issue in the Red Bull's <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a> power unit. Webber sailed past and Vettel was just dropping back into the clutches of compatriot Schumacher when he suddenly regained power, and began to close in on Hülkenberg and Webber.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Massas_Monza_2010_(cropped).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/57/Massas_Monza_2010_%28cropped%29.jpg/220px-Massas_Monza_2010_%28cropped%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="116" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/57/Massas_Monza_2010_%28cropped%29.jpg/330px-Massas_Monza_2010_%28cropped%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/57/Massas_Monza_2010_%28cropped%29.jpg/440px-Massas_Monza_2010_%28cropped%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="560" data-file-height="296" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Massas_Monza_2010_(cropped).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> finished third, days after <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> were spared any further sanction in relation to events at the <a href="/wiki/2010_German_Grand_Prix" title="2010 German Grand Prix">German Grand Prix</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>Button made his pit stop on lap 36, and by staying out for one lap longer Alonso was rejoin ahead of Button after his stop, although his teammate Massa actually held 1st for two laps before his own pit-stop relegated him back to 3rd. It was very tight as Alonso exited the pits, but the Spaniard just managed to stay ahead by holding the racing line as the two were side-by-side through turns one and two. <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> was the race's next retirement in the <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus_(2010%E2%80%9311)" title="Team Lotus (2010–11)">Lotus</a> with a gearbox failure on lap 46.
</p><p>After even more off track adventures for Hülkenberg, he was finally passed by Webber with just four laps to go, and the Aussie was visibly frustrated by then. On the same lap, <a href="/wiki/Lucas_di_Grassi" title="Lucas di Grassi">Lucas di Grassi</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Racing" title="Virgin Racing">Virgin</a> suffered a suspension failure. After his phantom technical failure, the Red Bull mechanics had decided to leave Vettel's stop to the very last lap, which they did, and the gamble paid off handsomely as the German emerged from the pits still in fourth place, ahead of his teammate.
</p><p>Alonso crossed the line in first place to the delight of the <a href="/wiki/Tifosi#Formula_1" title="Tifosi">Tifosi</a>, with Button still less than three seconds in arrears. Massa was third to complete a fantastic double home podium for the Scuderia, while Vettel drove a brilliant recovery drive to bring the Red Bull home fourth, holding off a late challenge from <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a>, who had had a quiet race for Mercedes. Webber, Hülkenberg and <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a> were all in close attendance, while Schumacher and <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a>'s Williams rounded out the points.
</p>
