<p>The <b>1975 Belgian Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held at <a href="/wiki/Zolder" class="mw-redirect" title="Zolder">Zolder</a> on 25 May 1975. It was race 6 of 14 in both the <a href="/wiki/1975_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1975 World Championship of Drivers">1975 World Championship of Drivers</a> and the <a href="/wiki/1975_International_Cup_for_Formula_One_Manufacturers" class="mw-redirect" title="1975 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers">1975 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> It was the 33rd <a href="/wiki/Belgian_Grand_Prix" title="Belgian Grand Prix">Belgian Grand Prix</a> and the second to be held at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_Zolder" title="Circuit Zolder">Circuit Zolder</a>. The race was held over 70 laps of the four kilometre venue for a race distance of 280 kilometres.
</p><p>The race was won by Austrian driver <a href="/wiki/Niki_Lauda" title="Niki Lauda">Niki Lauda</a> driving a <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_312T" title="Ferrari 312T">Ferrari 312T</a>, his second victory for the year after winning <a href="/wiki/1975_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="1975 Monaco Grand Prix">Monaco</a> two weeks prior. Lauda led 65 of the 70 laps, taking a 19-second victory over South African driver <a href="/wiki/Jody_Scheckter" title="Jody Scheckter">Jody Scheckter</a> in a <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_007" title="Tyrrell 007">Tyrrell 007</a>. Argentinian driver <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Reutemann" title="Carlos Reutemann">Carlos Reutemann</a> drove his <a href="/wiki/Brabham_BT44B" class="mw-redirect" title="Brabham BT44B">Brabham BT44B</a> to third place. The win put Lauda into the lead of the championship for the first time in 1975, passing previous leader <a href="/wiki/Emerson_Fittipaldi" title="Emerson Fittipaldi">Emerson Fittipaldi</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1975_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_summary">Race summary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1975_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Race summary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> arrived at <a href="/wiki/Circuit_Zolder" title="Circuit Zolder">Circuit Zolder</a> eager for the fray and buoyed up by their Monaco victory. <a href="/wiki/Niki_Lauda" title="Niki Lauda">Niki Lauda</a> dutifully gained pole after a technical gremlin held up early leader <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Carlos_Pace" class="mw-redirect" title="José Carlos Pace">Carlos Pace</a> and Saturday's qualifying was rained out. <a href="/wiki/Vittorio_Brambilla" title="Vittorio Brambilla">Vittorio Brambilla</a> was third on the grid and <a href="/wiki/Tony_Brise" title="Tony Brise">Tony Brise</a> stunned on his debut race for <a href="/wiki/Graham_Hill" title="Graham Hill">Graham Hill</a>, gaining a fourth-row start ahead of Championship leader <a href="/wiki/Emerson_Fittipaldi" title="Emerson Fittipaldi">Emerson Fittipaldi</a>. <a href="/wiki/Ronnie_Peterson" title="Ronnie Peterson">Ronnie Peterson</a> suffered the unfortunate experience of being given a parking ticket during the practice session – after having parked his car in a dangerous position at the edge of the track, rather than risk a puncture by pulling into the gravel trap. <a href="/wiki/Mario_Andretti" title="Mario Andretti">Mario Andretti</a> was missing from the field because he was competing at the <a href="/wiki/1975_Indianapolis_500" title="1975 Indianapolis 500">Indianapolis 500</a>.
</p><p>Pace took the lead whilst Brambilla and Regazzoni duelled. <a href="/wiki/Jochen_Mass" title="Jochen Mass">Jochen Mass</a> and <a href="/wiki/John_Watson_(racing_driver)" title="John Watson (racing driver)">John Watson</a> collided – the German retired whilst Watson returned to the pits with a damaged nosecone. <a href="/wiki/Alan_Jones_(racing_driver)" title="Alan Jones (racing driver)">Alan Jones</a> also had to retire after a collision with <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Laffite" title="Jacques Laffite">Jacques Laffite</a>. <a href="/wiki/Arturo_Merzario" title="Arturo Merzario">Arturo Merzario</a> was out with a burnt out clutch. Pace's fiery start was now causing problems with brakes and cold tyres and he was having to drop back.
</p><p>Brambilla took the lead until lap six when he was passed by Lauda. <a href="/wiki/Tony_Brise" title="Tony Brise">Tony Brise</a> spun at the chicane and retired shortly after with piston failure. <a href="/wiki/Jody_Scheckter" title="Jody Scheckter">Jody Scheckter</a> was storming through the field to be in second place by lap ninth. Brambilla's brakes were fading and he dropped down the order. <a href="/wiki/Jean-Pierre_Jarier" title="Jean-Pierre Jarier">Jean-Pierre Jarier</a> spun into the catch-fencing, <a href="/wiki/James_Hunt" title="James Hunt">James Hunt</a> retired with a broken gear-linkage. <a href="/wiki/Clay_Regazzoni" title="Clay Regazzoni">Clay Regazzoni</a> had risen to third, but then dived into the pits to change a blistered tyre. Lauda led Scheckter comfortably and the race settled down into a procession.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Carlos_Pace" title="Carlos Pace">Carlos Pace</a> had handling problems and dropped down the order when he lost third gear. By lap 49, <a href="/wiki/Ronnie_Peterson" title="Ronnie Peterson">Ronnie Peterson</a> crashed into the catch fencing, whilst Brambilla surrendered third place to change a blistered tyre, resuming but retiring with brake problems.
</p><p>Fittipaldi was also having brake problems, leaving him helpless against the assault of Regazzoni and <a href="/wiki/Tom_Pryce" title="Tom Pryce">Tom Pryce</a> as he dropped from fifth to seventh in the last six laps.
</p>
