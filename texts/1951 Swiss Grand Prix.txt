<p>The <b>1951 Swiss Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held at the <a href="/wiki/Bremgarten_Circuit" class="mw-redirect" title="Bremgarten Circuit">Bremgarten</a> on 27 May 1951. It was race 1 of 8 in the <a href="/wiki/1951_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1951 World Championship of Drivers">1951 World Championship of Drivers</a>. The race saw the Formula One debut of <a href="/wiki/British_people" title="British people">British</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Stirling_Moss" title="Stirling Moss">Stirling Moss</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1951_Swiss_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Swiss Grand Prix, the first event of the 1951 World Championship due to the absence of Monaco from the calendar, saw the <a href="/wiki/Alfa_Romeo_in_Formula_One" title="Alfa Romeo in Formula One">Alfa Romeo team</a> continue their dominance of the previous season. All four of their drivers occupied positions on the front two rows of the grid; the highest non-Alfa qualifier was <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Luigi_Villoresi" title="Luigi Villoresi">Luigi Villoresi</a>, who was alongside <a href="/wiki/Juan_Manuel_Fangio" title="Juan Manuel Fangio">Fangio</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nino_Farina" class="mw-redirect" title="Nino Farina">Farina</a> on the front row.
</p><p>The race took place in the rain, with Fangio initially leading from Farina. Ferrari's <a href="/wiki/Piero_Taruffi" title="Piero Taruffi">Piero Taruffi</a> also challenged for the lead, having started from sixth on the grid. Fangio pitted, handing Nino Farina the lead for the 24th lap of the race. However, Farina's decision not to make a pitstop did not pay off, as Fangio was able to retake the lead on lap 29. Fangio maintained the lead for the remainder of the race, eventually winning by nearly a minute from Taruffi, who had overtaken Farina on the penultimate lap. This was Taruffi's first podium in just his second championship race. The remaining Alfa drivers‚Äî<a href="/wiki/Consalvo_Sanesi" title="Consalvo Sanesi">Consalvo Sanesi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Toulo_de_Graffenried" title="Toulo de Graffenried">Toulo de Graffenried</a>‚Äîcompleted the points paying positions, ahead of Ferrari's <a href="/wiki/Alberto_Ascari" title="Alberto Ascari">Alberto Ascari</a>, who competed despite suffering from burns from the previous weekend's Formula 2 race in Genoa. <a href="/wiki/Stirling_Moss" title="Stirling Moss">Stirling Moss</a>, driving for <a href="/wiki/Hersham_and_Walton_Motors" title="Hersham and Walton Motors">HWM</a>, was in seventh, but ran out of fuel on the final lap, therefore yielding the position to <a href="/wiki/Louis_Chiron" title="Louis Chiron">Louis Chiron</a>, who was driving a <a href="/wiki/Maserati_in_motorsport" title="Maserati in motorsport">Maserati</a> for <a href="/wiki/Enrico_Plat%C3%A9" title="Enrico Plat√©">Enrico Plat√©</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
