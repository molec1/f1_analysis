<p>The <b>1972 Italian Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at <a href="/wiki/Autodromo_Nazionale_di_Monza" title="Autodromo Nazionale di Monza">Monza</a> on 10 September 1972. It was race 10 of 12 in both the <a href="/wiki/1972_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1972 World Championship of Drivers">1972 World Championship of Drivers</a> and the <a href="/wiki/1972_International_Cup_for_Formula_One_Manufacturers" class="mw-redirect" title="1972 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers">1972 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers</a>.
</p><p>Before the race, the Monza circuit was modified with the addition of two chicanes, one before the Curva Grande and one at the site of the old Curva Vialone, in order to reduce speeds in the interests of safety.
</p><p>The 55-lap race was won by Brazilian driver <a href="/wiki/Emerson_Fittipaldi" title="Emerson Fittipaldi">Emerson Fittipaldi</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/Lotus_72" title="Lotus 72">Lotus</a>-<a href="/wiki/Ford_Motor_Company" title="Ford Motor Company">Ford</a>, after he started from sixth position. With the win, Fittipaldi sealed the Drivers' Championship, becoming the youngest ever champion at 25 years and 273 days until superseded by <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> at 24 years and 59 days in <a href="/wiki/2005_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2005 Formula One season">2005</a>; Lotus also secured the Manufacturers' Cup. Englishman <a href="/wiki/Mike_Hailwood" title="Mike Hailwood">Mike Hailwood</a> finished second in a <a href="/wiki/Surtees_TS9" title="Surtees TS9">Surtees</a>-Ford, with New Zealander <a href="/wiki/Denny_Hulme" title="Denny Hulme">Denny Hulme</a> third in a <a href="/wiki/McLaren_M19A" title="McLaren M19A">McLaren</a>-Ford.
</p><p>The race marked the last win for American tyre manufacturer <a href="/wiki/Firestone_Tire_and_Rubber_Company" title="Firestone Tire and Rubber Company">Firestone</a> in Formula One. It was also the last race in which 1964 World Champion <a href="/wiki/John_Surtees" title="John Surtees">John Surtees</a> competed.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1972_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Team Lotus</a> was forced to run only one car because Emerson Fittipaldi's <a href="/wiki/Lotus_72" title="Lotus 72">Lotus 72D</a> was seriously damaged in an accident while being transported to Monza. Fittipaldi raced another 72D, but with specifications very close to the <a href="/wiki/1970_Formula_One_season" title="1970 Formula One season">1970</a> model.</li></ul>
