<p>The <b>1963 Italian Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held at <a href="/wiki/Autodromo_Nazionale_di_Monza" title="Autodromo Nazionale di Monza">Monza</a> on September 8, 1963. It was the seventh of ten races in both the <a href="/wiki/1963_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1963 World Championship of Drivers">1963 World Championship of Drivers</a> and the <a href="/wiki/1963_International_Cup_for_Formula_One_Manufacturers" class="mw-redirect" title="1963 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers">1963 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers</a>. At this race, <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scottish</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Jim_Clark" title="Jim Clark">Jim Clark</a> clinched the World Championship crown with three races to go, the first time anyone had done so.
</p><p>The organizers had planned to run on the full 10 km circuit but the very bumpy (and in some places ruined) nature of the banked concrete curves provoked much criticism and also caused accidents. Therefore, at the drivers' request, for the next day it was decided to revert to the 5.75 km road layout.
</p><p>This race was Scuderia Ferrari's 100th start in a World Championship event as a team.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<ul><li><span class="citation wikicite" id="endnote_1"><b><a href="#ref_1">^1</a></b></span>  – Only 20 cars were permitted to take the start. Chris Amon's practice accident left him hospitalised him, leaving him unable to take the start. This would have promoted Mário de Araújo Cabral, who was 21st in qualifying, onto the starting grid. However, on race day, Giancarlo Baghetti, who was 25th quickest in qualifying, lined up on the starting grid. A race report from the time speculated that this was as a result of the race organisers arranging for Baghetti to start so there could be an Italian driver in the race.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1963_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Jim Clark became the first driver to win the Drivers' World Championship with 3 races left to go.  Lotus-Climax also won the Constructors' Championship.</li>
<li>This was the last ever Formula One race with a distance of more than 300 miles.</li></ul>
