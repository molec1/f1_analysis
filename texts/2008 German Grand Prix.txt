<p>The <b>2008 German Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>LXIX Großer Preis Santander von Deutschland</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 20 July 2008 at the <a href="/wiki/Hockenheimring" title="Hockenheimring">Hockenheimring</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hockenheim" title="Hockenheim">Hockenheim</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>. It was the 10th&#160;race of the <a href="/wiki/2008_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2008 Formula One season">2008 Formula One season</a> and was contested over 67&#160;laps. It was won by <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> for the <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> team after starting from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>. <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet_Jr." title="Nelson Piquet Jr.">Nelson Piquet Jr.</a> finished second for <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> third for <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>.
</p><p>Hamilton maintained his startline advantage and led until he made his first pit stop on lap&#160;18. As other cars made their pit stops, Hamilton regained the lead on lap&#160;22. On lap&#160;36, <a href="/wiki/Timo_Glock" title="Timo Glock">Timo Glock</a> crashed, and the race was neutralized by the deployment of the <a href="/wiki/Safety_car" title="Safety car">safety car</a>. Hamilton, on a two-stop strategy, did not stop to get more fuel during this period, while all the cars around him did.  Thus, when he did eventually stop on lap&#160;50, he rejoined the race in fifth position. In the closing stages of the race, Hamilton first overtook his teammate <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a>, then Massa, and finally Piquet, whilst also gaining a position due to <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a>'s late pit stop. This allowed him to take the lead again on lap&#160;60, which he maintained to win the race.
</p><p>The victory was Hamilton's second consecutive win, having won the preceding <a href="/wiki/2008_British_Grand_Prix" title="2008 British Grand Prix">British Grand Prix</a> at <a href="/wiki/Silverstone_Circuit" title="Silverstone Circuit">Silverstone</a>. The win also put him ahead of his two main rivals in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> (who finished sixth) and Massa, who were on equal points with him before the race. After the race he was four points ahead of Massa, and seven ahead of Räikkönen. In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a>, McLaren drew closer to the two teams ahead of them, <a href="/wiki/BMW_in_Formula_One" title="BMW in Formula One">BMW Sauber</a> and Ferrari. Ferrari still led by 15&#160;points from McLaren, and 12&#160;from BMW, whose drivers &#8211; <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> and <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a> &#8211; finished fourth and seventh respectively.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2008_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Grand Prix was contested by 20&#160;drivers, in ten teams of two.<sup id="cite_ref-Classification_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Classification-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The teams, also known as "<a href="/wiki/Formula_One#Constructors" title="Formula One">constructors</a>", were <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Mercedes_AMG_High_Performance_Powertrains" title="Mercedes AMG High Performance Powertrains">Mercedes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>, <a href="/wiki/BMW_in_Formula_One" title="BMW in Formula One">BMW Sauber</a>, <a href="/wiki/Honda_in_Formula_One" title="Honda in Formula One">Honda</a>, <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a>, <a href="/wiki/Toyota_Racing_(Formula_One_team)" title="Toyota Racing (Formula One team)">Toyota</a>, <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> and <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Classification_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Classification-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Prior to the race, <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> led the Drivers' Championship jointly, all with 48&#160;points. Behind them was <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a> in fourth with 46&#160;points for BMW Sauber, and his teammate <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> in fifth with 36 points.<sup id="cite_ref-autocourse-pg180-181_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autocourse-pg180-181-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> In the Constructors' Championship, Ferrari were leading with 96&#160;points, 14&#160;points ahead of BMW Sauber, who at this stage were ahead of McLaren, despite Hamilton tying for the lead of the Drivers' World Championship. This was due to Heikki Kovalainen's low point score of 24. Toyota were fourth, with 25&#160;points, ahead of Red Bull by one point, who in turn were one further point ahead of Renault.<sup id="cite_ref-autocourse-pg180-181_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autocourse-pg180-181-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Hamilton had won the preceding <a href="/wiki/2008_British_Grand_Prix" title="2008 British Grand Prix">British Grand Prix</a> by 68&#160;seconds from Heidfeld and Honda's <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a>. Of his championship rivals, Räikkönen had finished fourth, Kubica had retired, and Massa had had a nightmare of a race, spinning five times and finishing 13th.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In the run-up to the German Grand Prix, Hamilton had tested the <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4-23" title="McLaren MP4-23">McLaren MP4-23</a>'s new front wing &#8211; introduced at the British Grand Prix &#8211; extensively in the team's simulator, and then during a pre-race test session at the Hockenheim circuit.<sup id="cite_ref-McLaren_tech_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-McLaren_tech-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Having honed the car's setup to his liking after initially finding the handling difficult, he used it in the race.<sup id="cite_ref-McLaren_tech_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-McLaren_tech-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Kovalainen was not as comfortable with the car's handling and ran a greater front wing angle to produce more <a href="/wiki/Downforce" title="Downforce">downforce</a>, at the expense of having to run larger radiators due to the reduced airflow produced over the car.<sup id="cite_ref-McLaren_tech_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-McLaren_tech-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> This led to a slight reduction in his car's overall performance.<sup id="cite_ref-McLaren_tech_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-McLaren_tech-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>  In other technical developments, Ferrari introduced revised <a href="/wiki/Bargeboard_(aerodynamics)" title="Bargeboard (aerodynamics)">bargeboards</a>, exhaust exits and an altered windscreen for its <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_F2008" title="Ferrari F2008">F2008</a> car.<sup id="cite_ref-Tech_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Tech-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> BMW Sauber added <a href="/wiki/Turning_vane" class="mw-redirect" title="Turning vane">turning vanes</a> to the top of its <a href="/wiki/BMW_Sauber_F1.08" title="BMW Sauber F1.08">F1.08</a>'s nose, while Toyota removed some bodywork from its <a href="/wiki/Toyota_TF108" title="Toyota TF108">TF108</a>'s exhaust exits.<sup id="cite_ref-Tech_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Tech-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Honda also introduced new bargeboards for its <a href="/wiki/Honda_RA108" title="Honda RA108">RA108</a> chassis.<sup id="cite_ref-Tech_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Tech-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>  All of these changes were carried out with the aim of improving each car's aerodynamic performance.<sup id="cite_ref-Tech_6-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Tech-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice">Practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2008_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Three practice sessions were held before the Sunday race &#8211; two on Friday, and a third on Saturday. The Friday morning and afternoon sessions each lasted 90&#160;minutes. The third session was held on Saturday morning and lasted an hour.<sup id="cite_ref-sporting_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sporting-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> During the first practice session the track was wet following early-morning rain, and shortly after the session started, further precipitation made the track even wetter, although it did begin to dry out as the session progressed.<sup id="cite_ref-P1_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P1-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton was fastest in this session ahead of his teammate Kovalainen.<sup id="cite_ref-P1_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P1-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The Ferraris of Massa and Räikkönen came third and fifth respectively, with Renault's <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> sandwiched between them in fourth place.<sup id="cite_ref-P1_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P1-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Kubica spun off the track and <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9bastien_Bourdais" title="Sébastien Bourdais">Sébastien Bourdais</a> had a mechanical problem with his flywheel sensor towards the end of the session.<sup id="cite_ref-TR18_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TR18-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Both ended up at the bottom of the timesheets as a result of these problems.<sup id="cite_ref-P1_8-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P1-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Friday's second session was held in dry conditions.<sup id="cite_ref-P2_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P2-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton again set the fastest time. Massa and Räikkönen improved to second and third positions, pushing Kovalainen down to fourth.<sup id="cite_ref-P2_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P2-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> set the fifth-fastest time, after earlier having electrical and clutch problems with the car.<sup id="cite_ref-RB18_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RB18-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Alonso, <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a>, Kubica, Heidfeld and <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> made up the rest of the top ten.<sup id="cite_ref-P2_10-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P2-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the final practice session on Saturday &#8211; which was held in dry conditions &#8211; Kovalainen was fastest, ahead of Massa and teammate Hamilton.<sup id="cite_ref-P3_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P3-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Alonso continued his strong practice form, finishing fourth, with Toro Rosso's <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> making it to fifth position. Kubica once again was at the bottom of the table, this time due to a transmission problem.<sup id="cite_ref-P3_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P3-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The qualifying session on Saturday afternoon was split into three&#160;parts. The first part ran for 20&#160;minutes, and cars that finished the session 16th or lower were eliminated from qualifying. The second part of qualifying lasted 15&#160;minutes and eliminated cars that finished in positions&#160;11 to 15. The final part of qualifying determined the positions from first to tenth, and decided <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>. Cars which failed to make the final session could refuel before the race, so ran lighter in those sessions. Cars which competed in the final session of qualifying were not allowed to refuel before the race, and as such carried more fuel than in the previous sessions.<sup id="cite_ref-sporting_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sporting-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2008_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatright" style="width:27.5%;&#32;;"><style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r887775652">.mw-parser-output .quotebox{background-color:#F9F9F9;border:1px solid #aaa;box-sizing:border-box;padding:10px;font-size:88%;max-width:100%}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft{margin:0.5em 1.4em 0.8em 0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright{margin:0.5em 0 0.8em 1.4em}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.centered{margin:0.5em auto 0.8em auto}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft p,.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright p{font-style:inherit}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-title{background-color:#F9F9F9;text-align:center;font-size:larger;font-weight:bold}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:before{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" “ ";vertical-align:-45%;line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:after{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" ” ";line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .left-aligned{text-align:left}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .right-aligned{text-align:right}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .center-aligned{text-align:center}.mw-parser-output .quotebox cite{display:block;font-style:normal}@media screen and (max-width:360px){.mw-parser-output .quotebox{min-width:100%;margin:0 0 0.8em!important;float:none!important}}</style>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">"What a lap! My first run during [the third session of qualifying] wasn’t bad, but my final lap was very smooth and I expected to set an even better time. The wind made today's conditions quite tricky and sometimes it was difficult to stay on the racing line, particularly in the stadium section &#8211; where the wind seemed to change a couple of times. The team did a really good job to adjust to these conditions and to prepare the car perfectly. We’ve been fast and consistent all weekend, we have good pace, and I'm confident for tomorrow."</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style=""><a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>, following the third qualifying session.<sup id="cite_ref-QQ_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-QQ-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>Hamilton took pole position for the ninth time in his career with a lap time of 1:15.666, having beaten Massa by two-tenths of a second in his final lap of the third session.<sup id="cite_ref-QQQ_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-QQQ-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Kovalainen, coming back after an error during his first run, qualified third, with <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> taking fourth position.<sup id="cite_ref-QQQ_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-QQQ-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Alonso and Räikkönen were close together in fifth and sixth positions, with just 0.006&#160;seconds separating them.<sup id="cite_ref-QQQ_14-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-QQQ-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Kubica took a comfortable seventh, half a second quicker in the third session than eighth-placed Webber.<sup id="cite_ref-QQQ_14-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-QQQ-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Vettel and Coulthard completed the top ten.<sup id="cite_ref-QQQ_14-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-QQQ-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Timo_Glock" title="Timo Glock">Timo Glock</a> took 11th place on the grid for Toyota, seven places behind his teammate Trulli. He was immediately in front of compatriots Heidfeld and Rosberg. <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> and Bourdais took 14th and 15th positions respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-QQQ_14-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-QQQ-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Both Heidfeld and Button later said that they suffered from tyre problems during the session.<sup id="cite_ref-QQ_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-QQ-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Completing the field, <a href="/wiki/Kazuki_Nakajima" title="Kazuki Nakajima">Kazuki Nakajima</a> was 16th, Nelson Piquet Jr. 17th, <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> 18th, whilst the Force Indias of <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a> and <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> brought up the rear of the grid in 19th and 20th positions.<sup id="cite_ref-QQQ_14-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-QQQ-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Piquet later complained that he was blocked during his first-session run by Vettel: "During my lap, Vettel was in front of me, which unsettled me a little, although the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">FIA</a> felt that he did not impede me."<sup id="cite_ref-QQ_13-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-QQ-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Kovalainen was, however, penalized by the stewards and fined <a href="/wiki/Euro" title="Euro">€</a>5,000 for using a refuelling system during qualifying which had not been approved by the FIA.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2008_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Hamilton_2008_Singapore_GP_1.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4b/Hamilton_2008_Singapore_GP_1.jpg/220px-Hamilton_2008_Singapore_GP_1.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="332" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4b/Hamilton_2008_Singapore_GP_1.jpg/330px-Hamilton_2008_Singapore_GP_1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4b/Hamilton_2008_Singapore_GP_1.jpg/440px-Hamilton_2008_Singapore_GP_1.jpg 2x" data-file-width="667" data-file-height="1006" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Hamilton_2008_Singapore_GP_1.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Lewis Hamilton won the race from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>, overcoming a late race <a href="/wiki/Safety_car" title="Safety car">safety car</a> period in the process.</div></div></div>
<p>At the start, Hamilton, Massa and Kovalainen all maintained their grid positions. Hamilton quickly began to pull away from the other cars, at the rate of about half a second per lap.<sup id="cite_ref-Grandprix.com_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Grandprix.com-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Behind the leading trio, Kubica passed Räikkönen off the grid, and went on to pass both Alonso and Trulli at the <a href="/wiki/Hairpin_turn" title="Hairpin turn">hairpin</a> (turn 4), when Alonso's own attempted pass on Trulli delayed both drivers.<sup id="cite_ref-BBC_Sport_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC_Sport-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> The fight for fourth position continued in the early stages of the race. Kubica ran wide at the final corner on lap four, impeding Trulli and therefore allowing Alonso to attempt an overtaking manoeuvre on the Toyota down the main straight. However, Trulli held on to his position, and Alonso was instead passed by Räikkönen, moving him down to sixth position. At the back of the field, Nakajima spun his Williams but recovered back onto the track and continued in the race.<sup id="cite_ref-Autosport_commentary_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Autosport_commentary-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At the first round of pit stops, Hamilton had a lead of nearly 20&#160;seconds over Massa, and fuelled longer than all the other drivers in the points-scoring positions when he pitted on lap&#160;18. Coming out of the pits, Hamilton made an error and was passed by Trulli. The two drivers then nearly collided at the hairpin, with Hamilton holding back for the rest of the lap waiting for Trulli to pit.<sup id="cite_ref-Autosport_commentary_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Autosport_commentary-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Trulli's teammate Glock stayed out on the track longer than all of the other drivers except Barrichello and Piquet, meaning that when he pitted, he was actually in third position and rejoined the race in eighth. Hamilton regained the lead by lap&#160;22 after Massa, Kovalainen, Trulli and Räikkönen made their pit stops, and had in the region of an 11&#160;second lead over Massa at the half-way stage of the race.<sup id="cite_ref-Autosport_commentary_20-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Autosport_commentary-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Behind the leaders, fourth-placed Kubica was beginning to catch Kovalainen in third, while Trulli, Räikkönen, Alonso and Vettel battled over the final four point-scoring places.<sup id="cite_ref-Autosport_commentary_20-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Autosport_commentary-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On the 36th lap, Glock's right-rear suspension failed coming out of the final corner.<sup id="cite_ref-Autosport_commentary_20-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Autosport_commentary-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> He spun through 180 degrees before hitting the pit wall with a strong rearward impact, with <a href="/wiki/Debris" title="Debris">debris</a> flying across the track.<sup id="cite_ref-BBC_Sport_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC_Sport-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> The car, pointing the wrong way, rolled to a stop on the grass. A dazed Glock extracted himself before being treated at the medical centre. The safety car was deployed after the accident.<sup id="cite_ref-Autosport_commentary_20-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Autosport_commentary-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> When the pit lane opened on lap&#160;38, most drivers headed in, including all of the leaders except Hamilton.<sup id="cite_ref-Autosport_commentary_20-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Autosport_commentary-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> McLaren believed that Hamilton had enough fuel to stay out, the plan being for him to then rapidly pull away from his heavily-fuelled rivals, after the safety car went in. Hamilton needed to build up enough of a lead to rejoin still in first position, following his final pit stop.<sup id="cite_ref-GrandPrix_quotes_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GrandPrix_quotes-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> This would prove to be very risky, as Massa rejoined in fourth position, fuelled to the finish.<sup id="cite_ref-BBC_Sport_19-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC_Sport-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Piquet, on a one-stop strategy after his poor qualifying performance, had fortuitously pitted immediately prior to the safety car's deployment and was also fuelled to the end of the race.<sup id="cite_ref-BBC_Sport_19-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC_Sport-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Autosport_commentary_20-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Autosport_commentary-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Promoted to third place from 14th beforehand following the pit stops, he was set to take the lead once Hamilton and Heidfeld (who also had not pitted under the safety car conditions) pitted for their final stops.<sup id="cite_ref-autocourse-pg187_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autocourse-pg187-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Further back, Vettel squeezed past Alonso at the pit exit, pushing him over the white line separating the pit lane exit from the track. Alonso complained over his radio but no action was taken against either driver.<sup id="cite_ref-Autosport_commentary_20-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Autosport_commentary-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Meanwhile, Räikkönen dropped several places to 11th, having had to queue behind Massa at the second pit stops.<sup id="cite_ref-Autosport_commentary_20-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Autosport_commentary-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Nelson_Angelo_Piquet.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ec/Nelson_Angelo_Piquet.jpg/220px-Nelson_Angelo_Piquet.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="309" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ec/Nelson_Angelo_Piquet.jpg/330px-Nelson_Angelo_Piquet.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ec/Nelson_Angelo_Piquet.jpg/440px-Nelson_Angelo_Piquet.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1851" data-file-height="2598" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Nelson_Angelo_Piquet.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet_Jr." title="Nelson Piquet Jr.">Nelson Piquet</a> finished in second position, despite qualifying in 17th.</div></div></div>
<p>When the safety car came in on lap&#160;42, Hamilton led, while Heidfeld and Piquet were running second and third.<sup id="cite_ref-Autosport_commentary_20-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Autosport_commentary-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Behind them were Massa, Kubica, Kovalainen, Trulli, Vettel, Alonso, Webber, Rosberg and Räikkönen.<sup id="cite_ref-Grandprix.com_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Grandprix.com-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Webber's Red Bull had been dripping oil for a couple of laps after picking up debris from Glock's crash, and on lap&#160;40 he pulled out of the race.<sup id="cite_ref-Autosport_commentary_20-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Autosport_commentary-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-autocourse-pg188_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autocourse-pg188-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Räikkönen quickly passed Rosberg, and then overtook the squabbling Vettel and Alonso on the way down to the hairpin, while Hamilton desperately tried to pull away at the front.<sup id="cite_ref-Autosport_commentary_20-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Autosport_commentary-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Räikkönen passed Trulli two&#160;laps later, while Kovalainen overtook Kubica for fifth place. Coulthard and Barrichello collided on the 48th&#160;lap with both cars pitting for repairs.<sup id="cite_ref-Autosport_commentary_20-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Autosport_commentary-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello retired three&#160;laps later due to damage sustained as a result of the incident. Hamilton pitted on lap&#160;50, coming out behind Heidfeld, Piquet, Massa and Kovalainen.<sup id="cite_ref-BBC_Sport_19-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC_Sport-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Autosport_commentary_20-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Autosport_commentary-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Kovalainen let his teammate past at the hairpin one lap later, meaning that Hamilton was now fourth. On lap&#160;52, Heidfeld set a new fastest lap time of 1:15.987. He was the only driver to go under one minute 16 seconds during the Grand Prix.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> At the end of the following lap, Heidfeld entered the pits with a sizeable gap over Piquet, returning to the field comfortably ahead of Kovalainen, but behind Hamilton.<sup id="cite_ref-autocourse-pg188_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autocourse-pg188-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Hamilton's speed allowed him to quickly catch Massa in second position, with Piquet in the lead by a further three&#160;seconds. On lap&#160;57, Hamilton passed Massa at the hairpin. Massa appeared to have the inside line covered, but he was not fully committed to the line into the braking zone, giving Hamilton the chance to take second position.<sup id="cite_ref-BBC_Sport_19-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC_Sport-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Three&#160;laps later, he used a similar overtaking move to overtake Piquet, although the Renault driver put up a greater fight than Massa had.<sup id="cite_ref-BBC_Sport_19-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC_Sport-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton led easily to the finish, although Piquet kept within six&#160;seconds of him.<sup id="cite_ref-BBC_Sport_19-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC_Sport-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Heidfeld  threatened a slowing Massa in the last laps, while Räikkönen was able to pass Kubica for sixth place on lap&#160;60.<sup id="cite_ref-Autosport_commentary_20-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Autosport_commentary-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Hamilton took the chequered flag first, while Piquet enthusiastically celebrated his drive from 17th to second, just 5.5&#160;seconds behind.<sup id="cite_ref-Autosport_commentary_20-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Autosport_commentary-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Massa held on to take third ahead of Heidfeld, Kovalainen, Räikkönen, and Kubica.<sup id="cite_ref-Autosport_commentary_20-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Autosport_commentary-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Vettel secured eighth &#8211; the last points-scoring position &#8211; for Toro Rosso, ahead of Trulli in ninth, and Rosberg in 10th.<sup id="cite_ref-Autosport_commentary_20-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Autosport_commentary-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Alonso finished 11th, ahead of Bourdais.<sup id="cite_ref-Autosport_commentary_20-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Autosport_commentary-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard was 13th, Fisichella 14th, Nakajima 15th, Sutil 16th and Button was last of the classified finishers in 17th.<sup id="cite_ref-Autosport_commentary_20-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Autosport_commentary-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> A record of sixteen drivers finished on the lead lap, beating the previous highest number of fourteen at the <a href="/wiki/1999_Belgian_Grand_Prix" title="1999 Belgian Grand Prix">1999 Belgian Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-autocourse-pg190-191_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autocourse-pg190-191-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div style="clear:both;"></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2008_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Following the podium ceremony, in which two Brazilian drivers participated for the first time since the <a href="/wiki/1991_Belgian_Grand_Prix" title="1991 Belgian Grand Prix">1991 Belgian Grand Prix</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-autocourse-pg187_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autocourse-pg187-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> the top three finishers appeared in the obligatory press conference. Hamilton said that the decision to not make a pit stop during the safety car period was the team's decision: "We had two very comfortable, two very decent stints and the team opted for me to stay out. I guess they thought I could pull out a gap but it was a 23 second gap I needed and I only had seven laps or something, so I don't know how that worked out."<sup id="cite_ref-Press_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Press-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton added that he felt that things looked good for the rest of the season, stating that "if we [McLaren] can continue with this momentum we have got we are looking very good".<sup id="cite_ref-Press_26-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Press-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Ron_Dennis" title="Ron Dennis">Ron Dennis</a>, McLaren's team principal, also commented on the pit stop decision:
</p>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r960796168">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>The deployment of the Safety Car presented us with a difficult decision: should we or should we not bring Lewis in for a pitstop? We decided that, since he still had quite a lot of fuel on board, we would leave him out until lap 50. We'd expected the track to be cleared of debris a little faster than it was, which would have allowed the Safety Car to come in a bit earlier. As a result, Lewis was unable to rejoin the race in the lead.<sup id="cite_ref-GrandPrix_quotes_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GrandPrix_quotes-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>
<p>Piquet was delighted with his drive to second after qualifying 17th, saying:
</p>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r960796168"/><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>I am obviously very happy! After qualifying yesterday I was thinking that my weekend was over, but we opted for an aggressive strategy in the race and the team made some great decisions when the safety car came out. Then I had to concentrate to keep my pace up and look after my tyres as much as possible towards the end of the race. I knew that Lewis was much faster than me, so I did not want to take any pointless risks. This second place today is a great reward for the whole team.<sup id="cite_ref-Official_F1_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Official_F1-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Felipe_Massa_2008_Algarve.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8c/Felipe_Massa_2008_Algarve.jpg/220px-Felipe_Massa_2008_Algarve.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="192" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8c/Felipe_Massa_2008_Algarve.jpg/330px-Felipe_Massa_2008_Algarve.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8c/Felipe_Massa_2008_Algarve.jpg/440px-Felipe_Massa_2008_Algarve.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3134" data-file-height="2732" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Felipe_Massa_2008_Algarve.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> was third after being unable to match Hamilton's pace.</div></div></div>
<p>Although Piquet's result was fortuitous, Renault Engineering Director, <a href="/wiki/Pat_Symonds" title="Pat Symonds">Pat Symonds</a>, subsequently praised him for stepping up to the job of staying with the leaders in the final stint of the race.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Meanwhile, Massa commented that his car was not in good order during the race: "It was just slow with difficult stability and also on braking I think I had a little problem as well and lost a lot of performance. I didn’t have the car to fight. I tried but it was not possible."<sup id="cite_ref-Press_26-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Press-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Teammate Räikkönen also struggled with low grip and hot brakes for the duration of the race, while Kovalainen and Kubica had problems with their tyres.<sup id="cite_ref-autocourse-pg188_23-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autocourse-pg188-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Fisichella was penalised 25&#160;seconds for unlapping lead cars during the safety car period, which dropped him from 14th to 16th position.<sup id="cite_ref-Fisichella_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Fisichella-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> The stewards issued the following statement about the decision: "While the safety car was deployed, the driver of car 21 [Fisichella] chose to enter the pits before 'Lapped cars may now overtake' was shown on the timing monitors. Having done so the driver of car 21 on leaving the pitlane should then not have unlapped himself."<sup id="cite_ref-Fisichella_29-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Fisichella-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The treatment of Glock after his crash caused Toyota some concern.<sup id="cite_ref-Yahoo_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Yahoo-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Toyota did not think that normal FIA procedure had been followed. After crashes, drivers are normally taken out of the car in their seat, whereas Glock was unstrapped and then hauled out by the marshals.<sup id="cite_ref-Yahoo_30-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Yahoo-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Glock stayed in hospital overnight for observation, but was released the next day with no injuries.  Toyota subsequently confirmed that the failed suspension component had already completed a full race distance at the previous Grand Prix, and resolved to adjust its checking procedures.<sup id="cite_ref-autocourse-pg187_22-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autocourse-pg187-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The new gap between Hamilton and Massa in the Drivers' Championship after the race stood at four&#160;points in Hamilton's favour.<sup id="cite_ref-autocourse-pg190-191_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autocourse-pg190-191-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> In the Constructors' Championship, McLaren somewhat caught up on Ferrari, although the gap between the two teams was still sizeable at 19&#160;points. McLaren were ten points behind BMW coming into the race and the deficit was reduced to three due to the race result.<sup id="cite_ref-autocourse-pg190-191_25-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autocourse-pg190-191-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
