<p>The <b>1959 Monaco Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Monaco" title="Circuit de Monaco">Circuit de Monaco</a> on 10 May 1959. It was race 1 of 9 in the <a href="/wiki/1959_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1959 World Championship of Drivers">1959 World Championship of Drivers</a> and race 1 of 8 in the <a href="/wiki/1959_International_Cup_for_Formula_One_Manufacturers" class="mw-redirect" title="1959 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers">1959 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers</a>. It was also the 17th <a href="/wiki/Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="Monaco Grand Prix">Monaco Grand Prix</a>. The race was held over 100 laps of the three kilometre circuit for a race distance of 315 kilometres.
</p><p>The race was won by Australian racer <a href="/wiki/Jack_Brabham" title="Jack Brabham">Jack Brabham</a> driving a <a href="/wiki/Cooper_T51" title="Cooper T51">Cooper T51</a> for the factory <a href="/wiki/Cooper_Car_Company" title="Cooper Car Company">Cooper Car Company</a> team. It was the first win for Brabham, a future three-time world champion. It was the first World Championship Grand Prix victory by an Australian driver. It was also the first win for the factory Cooper team. Coopers had won races previously in the hands of <a href="/wiki/Rob_Walker_Racing_Team" title="Rob Walker Racing Team">Rob Walker Racing Team</a>. Brabham finished 20 seconds ahead of British driver <a href="/wiki/Tony_Brooks_(racing_driver)" title="Tony Brooks (racing driver)">Tony Brooks</a> driving a <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_246_F1" title="Ferrari 246 F1">Ferrari 246</a>. A lap down in third was the Cooper T51 of French driver and <a href="/wiki/1958_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="1958 Monaco Grand Prix">1958 Monaco Grand Prix</a> winner <a href="/wiki/Maurice_Trintignant" title="Maurice Trintignant">Maurice Trintignant</a> of the Rob Walker Racing Team.
</p>
