<p>The <b>1988 French Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 3 July 1988 at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_Paul_Ricard" title="Circuit Paul Ricard">Circuit Paul Ricard</a>, <a href="/wiki/Le_Castellet,_Var" title="Le Castellet, Var">Le Castellet</a>. The race, contested over 80 laps, was the seventh race of the <a href="/wiki/1988_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="1988 Formula One season">1988 Formula One season</a> and was won by <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/4" title="McLaren MP4/4">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Honda_in_Formula_One" title="Honda in Formula One">Honda</a>, with teammate <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> second and <a href="/wiki/Michele_Alboreto" title="Michele Alboreto">Michele Alboreto</a> third in a <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_F1/87#F1/87/88C" title="Ferrari F1/87">Ferrari</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1988_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The two <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/4" title="McLaren MP4/4">McLarens</a> lined up in their usual front row places, but, for the first time this season, <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> was ahead of <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a>, the Frenchman beating the Brazilian by almost half a second to take his first pole since the <a href="/wiki/1986_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="1986 Monaco Grand Prix">1986 Monaco Grand Prix</a> and in doing so stopping his teammate from a record breaking 7th pole in succession. The two <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_F1/87#F1/87/88C" title="Ferrari F1/87">Ferraris</a> filled the second row, <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> beating <a href="/wiki/Michele_Alboreto" title="Michele Alboreto">Michele Alboreto</a> by over 1.1 seconds, though neither driver believed they could race with the McLarens simply because of fuel consumption. On the third row were the two <a href="/wiki/Naturally_aspirated_engine" title="Naturally aspirated engine">normally-aspirated</a> <a href="/wiki/Benetton_B188" title="Benetton B188">Benettons</a> of <a href="/wiki/Thierry_Boutsen" title="Thierry Boutsen">Thierry Boutsen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alessandro_Nannini" title="Alessandro Nannini">Alessandro Nannini</a>, while the <a href="/wiki/Lotus_100T" title="Lotus 100T">Lotuses</a> of <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a> and <a href="/wiki/Satoru_Nakajima" title="Satoru Nakajima">Satoru Nakajima</a> filled the fourth row, Piquet being the last driver to qualify under 1:10. The top ten was completed by the <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW12" title="Williams FW12">Williams</a> of <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> (over 2.7 seconds slower than Prost and almost 4 seconds slower than his <a href="/wiki/1987_French_Grand_Prix" title="1987 French Grand Prix">1987</a> pole time) and the <a href="/wiki/March_881" title="March 881">March</a> of <a href="/wiki/Ivan_Capelli" title="Ivan Capelli">Ivan Capelli</a>, who had recovered after fracturing his left foot in a crash during practice in <a href="/wiki/1988_Detroit_Grand_Prix" title="1988 Detroit Grand Prix">Detroit</a> two weeks before.
</p><p>Meanwhile, <a href="/wiki/Equipe_Ligier" title="Equipe Ligier">Ligier</a> had a disastrous weekend on their home turf with both <a href="/wiki/Ren%C3%A9_Arnoux" title="Ren√© Arnoux">Ren√© Arnoux</a> and <a href="/wiki/Stefan_Johansson" title="Stefan Johansson">Stefan Johansson</a> failing to qualify, though they did have a celebration of sorts over the weekend as Arnoux turned 40 the day after the race. <a href="/wiki/Julian_Bailey" title="Julian Bailey">Julian Bailey</a> also failed to qualify in his <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_017" title="Tyrrell 017">Tyrrell</a>, while <a href="/wiki/Piercarlo_Ghinzani" title="Piercarlo Ghinzani">Piercarlo Ghinzani</a> qualified 22nd in his <a href="/wiki/Zakspeed_881" title="Zakspeed 881">Zakspeed</a> but was excluded for missing a weight check, thus promoting <a href="/wiki/Oscar_Larrauri" title="Oscar Larrauri">Oscar Larrauri</a>'s <a href="/wiki/EuroBrun_ER188" title="EuroBrun ER188">EuroBrun</a> to the last grid spot. Larrauri qualifying was to the displeasure of many of those towards the front of the grid as the Argentine in the slower than most EuroBrun-<a href="/wiki/Cosworth_DFZ" class="mw-redirect" title="Cosworth DFZ">Ford Cosworth</a> had developed a reputation of being a mobile chicane in both qualifying and races and most felt that would be a problem on the shortest track on the F1 calendar.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_summary">Race summary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1988_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Race summary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Prost led away from Senna and the two Ferraris, with Piquet passing the two Benettons for fifth. Despite worries in the McLaren pit about overly high fuel consumption (the fuel readouts in the cars were showing less fuel used than what the Honda telemetry was telling the team), Ron Dennis was reportedly prepared to let his drivers run out of fuel at the expense of a perfect season if it taught them to be more conservative given their obvious advantage over the field, though ultimately the onboard readouts were proved correct. Prost maintained a two-second advantage over Senna until the mid-race pit stops, while an early stop for by Berger on lap 22 allowed teammate Alboreto through into third. While both McLarens were racing each other out front, they were still pulling away from the field at sometimes a second per lap.
</p><p>Senna made his pit stop three laps before Prost, enabling him to take the lead thanks to a slow stop for Prost with a sticking front wheel, but traffic on the Mistral Straight (including a less than helpful Piquet who most likely enjoyed seeing Senna lose the lead to Prost at close quarters) and a gearbox problem allowed Prost to close right up to him and then overtake him on lap 61 as they lapped <a href="/wiki/Alex_Caffi" title="Alex Caffi">Alex Caffi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pierluigi_Martini" title="Pierluigi Martini">Pierluigi Martini</a> at the Signes corner. Meanwhile, Berger reclaimed third only for Alboreto to pass him again during the pit stops, while Mansell suffered his seventh consecutive retirement, dropping out on lap 49 with suspension problems though he had been dropping back for a number of laps with power loss from his <a href="/wiki/Judd_(engine)#CV" title="Judd (engine)">Judd</a> <a href="/wiki/V8_engine" title="V8 engine">V8 engine</a>. After returning to the pits and getting himself cleaned up, the Englishman left the circuit profoundly fed up with the performance of the Williams, fuelling ongoing speculation in the press that he would leave the team at the end of the year to join Ferrari after the Italian team had announced they would not be keeping Alboreto for 1989. Despite the rumours turning out to be correct, Mansell continued to deny he was joining Ferrari.
</p><p>In the closing laps, Prost pulled away as Senna's gearbox troubles continued, losing several gears. The Brazilian eventually finished nearly 32 seconds behind the Frenchman, though he was still nearly 35 seconds ahead of Alboreto, the last man on the lead lap. Berger was fourth ahead of Piquet, who also suffered gearbox problems in the later stages of the race; the final point went to the Benetton of Nannini. France was the first time since the <a href="/wiki/1987_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="1987 Monaco Grand Prix">1987 Monaco Grand Prix</a> that Alboreto had finished in front of his teammate in a race where they both finished. During the race Alboreto had the advantage of an almost perfectly balanced car, while Berger complained of severe understeer, something not needed at the high speed Signes corner at the end of the Mistral.
</p><p>The fastest lap of the race, a 1:11.737 set by Prost on lap 45, was over two seconds slower than the 1:09.548 set by Piquet in a Honda-powered Williams the <a href="/wiki/1987_French_Grand_Prix" title="1987 French Grand Prix">previous year</a> ‚Äì a consequence of the restrictions imposed on <a href="/wiki/Turbocharger" title="Turbocharger">turbocharged</a> engines this season with the major difference being the speeds attained on the now 1&#160;km long Mistral Straight. In 1987 the fastest turbo cars (Honda powered) were timed at 325&#160;km/h (202&#160;mph) on the straight. In 1988 the top speed was recorded at 311&#160;km/h (193&#160;mph).
</p><p>For Prost it was his third win in his home Grand Prix after winning in both <a href="/wiki/1981_French_Grand_Prix" title="1981 French Grand Prix">1981</a> at <a href="/wiki/Dijon-Prenois" title="Dijon-Prenois">Dijon</a> (his first Grand Prix victory) and <a href="/wiki/1983_French_Grand_Prix" title="1983 French Grand Prix">1983</a> at the full length Paul Ricard. On both occasions he had been driving for the factory <a href="/wiki/Equipe_Renault" class="mw-redirect" title="Equipe Renault">Renault</a> team.
</p>
