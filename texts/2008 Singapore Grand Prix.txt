<p>The <b>2008 Singapore Grand Prix</b>, formally known as the <b>2008 Formula 1 SingTel Singapore Grand Prix</b>, was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> race held on 28 September 2008 at 20:00 <a href="/wiki/Singapore_Standard_Time" title="Singapore Standard Time">SST</a> at the newly built <a href="/wiki/Marina_Bay_Street_Circuit" title="Marina Bay Street Circuit">Marina Bay Street Circuit</a> in <a href="/wiki/Marina_Bay,_Singapore" title="Marina Bay, Singapore">Marina Bay</a>, <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a>. It was the fifteenth race of the <a href="/wiki/2008_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2008 Formula One season">2008 Formula One season</a>, the 800th Formula One World Championship race overall, the first ever Formula One race held at night. This was also the first time Singapore hosted a Formula One race, as the last Singapore Grand Prix was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_Libre" title="Formula Libre">Formula Libre</a> event in <a href="/wiki/1973_Singapore_Grand_Prix" title="1973 Singapore Grand Prix">1973</a>.
</p><p>The 61-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a> team from 15th on the grid. <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> claimed second in his <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a> followed by <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren's</a> <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> of <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> started from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>. <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a> leader Hamilton was second while reigning world champion <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> qualified third. They continued in this order until Renault's <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet,_Jr." class="mw-redirect" title="Nelson Piquet, Jr.">Nelson Piquet, Jr.</a> spun out and crashed on lap 14, bringing out the safety car. The leading drivers all pitted when the pit lane was opened. Massa prematurely left the pit box with his fuel hose still attached and dropped to last place.
</p><p>Alonso, who had pitted before the safety car was deployed, subsequently took the lead and won the race. Nico Rosberg managed to finish second despite incurring a stop-go penalty and Hamilton completed the podium in third. <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing's</a> <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> finished 7th, scoring the final points of his career. <a href="/wiki/Kazuki_Nakajima" title="Kazuki Nakajima">Kazuki Nakajima</a> also scored his final points. The result saw Hamilton extend his lead in the Driver's standings to 7 points over Massa, who failed to score. McLaren also took over the lead in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a> by one point from Ferrari.
</p><p>In September 2009, Renault F1 admitted to an <a href="/wiki/FIA_World_Motor_Sport_Council" title="FIA World Motor Sport Council">FIA World Motor Sport Council</a> meeting that Piquet had deliberately crashed per instructions from Renault team principal <a href="/wiki/Flavio_Briatore" title="Flavio Briatore">Flavio Briatore</a> and chief engineer <a href="/wiki/Pat_Symonds" title="Pat Symonds">Pat Symonds</a>, in the hope of helping Alonso win. The Renault team were handed a disqualification from F1, which was suspended for two years pending any further rule infringements. Briatore was banned from all FIA-sanctioned events for life, while Symonds was banned for five years.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> However,  Briatore and Symonds sued the FIA in French courts, and on January 5, 2010, the Tribunal de Grande Instance overturned the ban which had been put in place on both men.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2008_Singapore_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2008_Singapore_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In early 2007, talks of staging a Formula One Grand Prix in Singapore began, with the focus on arranging this to be a night race in order to cater Formula One's audience in Europe.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The race was approved in May<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and construction for the grand prix complex began in August 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The lighting system consisted of nearly 1600&#160;light projectors, separated by four meters and suspended 10&#160;meters above the track. The system generated a total of 3000&#160;<a href="/wiki/Lux" title="Lux">lux</a> intensity, approximately four times intensity used in standard  <a href="/wiki/Soccer-specific_stadiums" class="mw-redirect" title="Soccer-specific stadiums">football stadium</a>. The whole system required total of 108,423 meter cables and was connected to twelve twin-powered <a href="/wiki/Electrical_generator" class="mw-redirect" title="Electrical generator">generators</a>, generating estimated power of three <a href="/wiki/Watt" title="Watt">megawatts</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At the <a href="/wiki/2008_Italian_Grand_Prix" title="2008 Italian Grand Prix">previous race in Italy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> took his and <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso's</a> maiden win in the wet race. Vettel became the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_driver_records#Youngest_winners" title="List of Formula One driver records">youngest driver to win a Formula One race</a>, aged 21 years and 73 days. <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari's</a> <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> finished in sixth place, narrowing <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton's</a> lead in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a> to a single point. They were followed by <a href="/wiki/BMW_Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="BMW Sauber">BMW Sauber</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a> and Massa's teammate <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a>. In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a>, Ferrari were in the lead with 134 points, followed by <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Mercedes_Benz" class="mw-redirect" title="Mercedes Benz">Mercedes</a> and BMW Sauber.
</p><p>Due to the unusual time of the race, teams and drivers had to adapt their working hours by waking up at around midday local time, starting work around late afternoon, and staying up until 4am. Most drivers and teams praised the track after carrying out track inspections as well as arrangements undertaken by the track authorities.<sup id="cite_ref-thurs-pc_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-thurs-pc-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-bourdais-pre_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bourdais-pre-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-theissen_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-theissen-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> However, concerns were raised about high bumps on kerbs of the Turn 10&#160;<a href="/wiki/Chicane" title="Chicane">chicane</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-thurs-pc_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-thurs-pc-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-bourdais-pre_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bourdais-pre-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-alonso-pre_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-alonso-pre-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Concerns were also expressed on safety measures needed in case of rain during the race.<sup id="cite_ref-thurs-pc_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-thurs-pc-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice">Practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2008_Singapore_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Chicane_Kerbs_Singapore_GP.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/91/Chicane_Kerbs_Singapore_GP.jpg/220px-Chicane_Kerbs_Singapore_GP.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/91/Chicane_Kerbs_Singapore_GP.jpg/330px-Chicane_Kerbs_Singapore_GP.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/91/Chicane_Kerbs_Singapore_GP.jpg/440px-Chicane_Kerbs_Singapore_GP.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3558" data-file-height="2374" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Chicane_Kerbs_Singapore_GP.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The controversial Turn 10 chicane kerbs</div></div></div>
<p>Hamilton set the fastest time in the first practice session on 26 September. He was followed by the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferraris</a> of Massa and Räikkönen and Hamilton's teammate Kovalainen. <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull's</a> <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> suffered a crash at Turn 18, damaging his front suspension. A number of drivers faced problems at the final corner of the circuit, including <a href="/wiki/Honda_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Honda F1">Honda</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> who crashed backwards into the barriers. Toyota's <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> also spun at this corner and subsequently made his way into the pits by driving in the wrong direction. Trulli was fined <a href="/wiki/Euro" title="Euro">€</a>10,000 for his misconduct.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Renault's <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> was fastest in the second practice session, displacing Hamilton to second position with his final flying lap. The two were followed by Kovalainen, Massa and <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a>. During the session, <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso</a>'s <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9bastien_Bourdais" title="Sébastien Bourdais">Sébastien Bourdais</a> almost collided with <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld's</a> <a href="/wiki/BMW_Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="BMW Sauber">BMW Sauber</a> as he exited from the pit lane into the first corner. In separate incidents, <a href="/wiki/Timo_Glock" title="Timo Glock">Timo Glock</a> lost his front wing when he crashed into wall at Turn 7, <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> faced gearbox problems and Massa mistakenly took a wrong turn towards the escape road off the track.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Alonso was again the fastest man on the track in the final practice session, with Hamilton and Massa second and third respectively. Numerous drivers faced problems with bumps on the track, narrowly avoiding accidents. Fisichella damaged his <a href="/wiki/Spyker_F8-VII" title="Spyker F8-VII">Force India VJM01's</a> front wing and suspension after it launched off the kerbs at the Turn 10 chicane.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2008_Singapore_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatright" style="width:28%;&#32;;"><style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r887775652">.mw-parser-output .quotebox{background-color:#F9F9F9;border:1px solid #aaa;box-sizing:border-box;padding:10px;font-size:88%;max-width:100%}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft{margin:0.5em 1.4em 0.8em 0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright{margin:0.5em 0 0.8em 1.4em}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.centered{margin:0.5em auto 0.8em auto}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft p,.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright p{font-style:inherit}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-title{background-color:#F9F9F9;text-align:center;font-size:larger;font-weight:bold}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:before{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" “ ";vertical-align:-45%;line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:after{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" ” ";line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .left-aligned{text-align:left}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .right-aligned{text-align:right}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .center-aligned{text-align:center}.mw-parser-output .quotebox cite{display:block;font-style:normal}@media screen and (max-width:360px){.mw-parser-output .quotebox{min-width:100%;margin:0 0 0.8em!important;float:none!important}}</style>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">I think first of all the car was just perfect, you know, so nice to drive smoothly and then I managed to do a perfect lap and that always helps when you have a good car and you don't make any single mistakes in whatever corner you go. That is always a great achievement.</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style=""><a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a>, following his fifth pole of the year.<sup id="cite_ref-pressconference1_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconference1-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>Räikkönen topped the first qualifying session, ahead of Kovalainen and Hamilton. <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet,_Jr." class="mw-redirect" title="Nelson Piquet, Jr.">Nelson Piquet, Jr.</a>, Sébastien Bourdais, Rubens Barrichello, <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a> and Giancarlo Fisichella were eliminated in this session. Fisichella was unable to record a time during the session after he crashed into barriers at Turn 3 due to a punctured tyre.<sup id="cite_ref-qquotes_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qquotes-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> During his flying lap, Barrichello was impeded by Nick Heidfeld and subsequently entered the pits to abandon his lap. Heidfeld was dropped three grid places for blocking and Barrichello was fined €10,000 for an unsafe pit lane entry as he did not use the deceleration zone.<sup id="cite_ref-heidfeld_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-heidfeld-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The second qualifying session was led by Felipe Massa, with Kovalainen and Räikkönen in second and third place. Hamilton barely managed to reach the third session, after a driving mistake during his penultimate run.<sup id="cite_ref-qquotes_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qquotes-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Fernando Alonso was eliminated early in this session when his car suffered a fuel feed problem before he could set a lap time.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> The other drivers who failed to reach the top ten were Jarno Trulli, <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> and both Red Bulls of Mark Webber and <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a>.
</p><p>Massa took the pole position in the final session, with Hamilton, Räikkönen and Kubica completing first two rows of the grid. Massa secured his position by clocking lap time six-tenths faster than Hamilton, describing it as his best qualifying result of the season.<sup id="cite_ref-qquotes_21-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qquotes-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Kovalainen, managing fifth position, rued his tyre selections in the final session.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Kovalainen, Heidfeld, <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Vettel</a> and Glock occupied the third and fourth rows of the grid while the <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams'</a> of Rosberg and <a href="/wiki/Kazuki_Nakajima" title="Kazuki Nakajima">Nakajima</a> completed the top ten.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2008_Singapore_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:2008_Singapore_Grand_Prix_pre-race.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a5/2008_Singapore_Grand_Prix_pre-race.JPG/220px-2008_Singapore_Grand_Prix_pre-race.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="124" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a5/2008_Singapore_Grand_Prix_pre-race.JPG/330px-2008_Singapore_Grand_Prix_pre-race.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a5/2008_Singapore_Grand_Prix_pre-race.JPG/440px-2008_Singapore_Grand_Prix_pre-race.JPG 2x" data-file-width="3264" data-file-height="1840" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:2008_Singapore_Grand_Prix_pre-race.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Just before the race</div></div></div>
<p>The race began at 20:00 <a href="/wiki/Singapore_Standard_Time" title="Singapore Standard Time">SST</a>. During the warm-up lap, Piquet spun out at Turn 23, but immediately recovered and took his position on the grid. At the start, Massa, championship rival Hamilton and Räikkönen all got through the first sequence of corners safely and retained their positions. Kubica and Kovalainen made contact at Turn 3, causing Kovalainen to lose two places to Vettel and Glock. Heidfeld and Alonso cut the first corner but they did not receive any penalty. Massa began to edge away from Hamilton, who in turn was pulling away from Räikkönen. Jarno Trulli had begun the race on a heavy fuel load, and soon was holding up a number of cars with Williams's Nico Rosberg immediately behind him. It was not until the seventh lap that Rosberg finally passed Trulli's Toyota, and soon Nakajima and Alonso got through as well.
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:2008_Singapore_Grand_Prix_Massa_fuel.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/19/2008_Singapore_Grand_Prix_Massa_fuel.jpg/220px-2008_Singapore_Grand_Prix_Massa_fuel.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="124" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/19/2008_Singapore_Grand_Prix_Massa_fuel.jpg/330px-2008_Singapore_Grand_Prix_Massa_fuel.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/19/2008_Singapore_Grand_Prix_Massa_fuel.jpg/440px-2008_Singapore_Grand_Prix_Massa_fuel.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3264" data-file-height="1840" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:2008_Singapore_Grand_Prix_Massa_fuel.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> mechanics work on <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a>'s car during the safety car period while teammate <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> waits behind him. The <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazilian</a> was released with the fuel hose still attached to his car and nearly collided with <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>After ten laps, Massa had built up a lead of over three seconds to Hamilton and Räikkönen was a further seven seconds behind Hamilton. On lap twelve, Alonso was the first driver who came into the pits.<sup id="cite_ref-gpupdate.net_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpupdate.net-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> He switched his super-soft tyres for the soft compound tyres and rejoined at the back of the field. Two laps later, teammate <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet,_Jr." class="mw-redirect" title="Nelson Piquet, Jr.">Nelson Piquet, Jr.</a> <a href="/wiki/Renault_Formula_One_crash_controversy" title="Renault Formula One crash controversy">lost control of his car and hit the wall at Turn 17</a>, blaming his hard tyres for the accident.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> This resulted in the first <a href="/wiki/Safety_car" title="Safety car">safety car</a> period of the race.<sup id="cite_ref-gpupdate.net_26-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpupdate.net-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> The two Red Bull cars and <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> managed to come into the pits before it was closed. With very little fuel left, Rosberg and Kubica had no choice but to pit despite the pit lane being closed. Barrichello's Honda then had a mechanical failure and became the second retirement of the race. After all the cars were behind the safety car, the pit lane was opened and nearly all the drivers pitted.
</p><p>In the pits, Ferrari prematurely released Massa with the fuel hose still attached to the car. The Brazilian was also released into the path of Adrian Sutil, who narrowly avoided the Ferrari. Massa went the full length of the pitlane before stopping just before the exit. Massa rejoined at the back of the field after the Ferrari mechanics ran down the pitlane and removed the hose from the car. Cars who had either pitted before the safety car or were on one stop strategies were now in front. Rosberg now led from Trulli, Fisichella, Kubica, Alonso, Webber, Coulthard and Hamilton.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Singapore_grand_prix_2008_alonso_win.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/88/Singapore_grand_prix_2008_alonso_win.jpg/220px-Singapore_grand_prix_2008_alonso_win.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="137" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/88/Singapore_grand_prix_2008_alonso_win.jpg/330px-Singapore_grand_prix_2008_alonso_win.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/88/Singapore_grand_prix_2008_alonso_win.jpg/440px-Singapore_grand_prix_2008_alonso_win.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2313" data-file-height="1437" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Singapore_grand_prix_2008_alonso_win.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Despite qualifying down in fifteenth, <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> took full advantage of pitting before the <a href="/wiki/Safety_car" title="Safety car">safety car</a> period and his rivals' misfortunes, to take victory at the inaugural Singapore Grand Prix.</div></div></div>
<p>Rosberg started to pull away from the heavily fuelled Trulli and Fisichella's <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a>, who held up most of the field behind him. In the next few laps, Felipe Massa was given a drive through penalty for an unsafe release from the pits while Rosberg and Kubica were given 10 second stop-go penalties for refuelling when the pitlane was closed. The Ferrari driver served his penalty and rejoined the track still in last place. Kubica came into the pits from fourth and dropped behind Massa. After building a lead of over fifteen seconds to Trulli, Rosberg served his penalty a lap later but only lost three positions. He rejoined the circuit in fourth behind Alonso and Jarno Trulli took over the lead.
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:2008_Singapore_Grand_Prix_podium.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3b/2008_Singapore_Grand_Prix_podium.jpg/220px-2008_Singapore_Grand_Prix_podium.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="135" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3b/2008_Singapore_Grand_Prix_podium.jpg/330px-2008_Singapore_Grand_Prix_podium.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3b/2008_Singapore_Grand_Prix_podium.jpg/440px-2008_Singapore_Grand_Prix_podium.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2118" data-file-height="1302" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:2008_Singapore_Grand_Prix_podium.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The podium, (left to right): <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Rosberg</a> celebrates his best Grand Prix finish up to that point, <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Alonso</a> takes the top step of the podium for the first time since the <a href="/wiki/2007_Italian_Grand_Prix" title="2007 Italian Grand Prix">2007 Italian Grand Prix</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Hamilton</a> settles for third to extend his lead in the Drivers' Championship.</div></div></div>
<p>A lap later, Fisichella came in from second to make his only pit stop, moving Alonso and Rosberg into second and third. Trulli led the race for four more laps before making his only stop of the night, handing the lead to Alonso. He soon pulled away from Rosberg who pitted from second on lap 41, rejoining in seventh. Alonso made his final stop a lap later but still rejoined in the lead, just ahead of second and third placed Coulthard and Hamilton. Coulthard lost momentum and the McLaren overtook the Red Bull at turn seven. At the end of the lap, both cars pitted and Coulthard nearly left with his fuel hose attached.
</p><p>After 45 laps, Alonso led Timo Glock by just over six seconds and Rosberg by a further eleven seconds in third. The Toyota driver pitted two laps later, rejoining in fifth. Four laps later, Trulli began to slow down and soon retired after a mechanical failure. On the same lap, Räikkönen made his second and final stop of the race and rejoined in fifth. A lap later, Massa spun at Turn 18 and lightly tapped the tyre wall without sustaining much damage. Moments later, Sutil fell victim to the same corner and damaged his front suspension, becoming the fifth retirement of the night. The incident brought out the second safety car period of the race.
</p><p>The safety car withdrew from the circuit with nine laps remaining and Alonso pulled away from Rosberg and Hamilton. Glock, Räikkönen, Vettel, Heidfeld and Coulthard (ultimately, claiming points for the final time) were in the remaining points positions. Rosberg was coming under pressure from Hamilton, who was looking to capitalize on Massa's incidents. With four laps remaining, Räikkönen bounced off the kerbs at the Turn 10 chicane and crashed into the barriers while chasing Glock, promoting Nakajima into the points. Alonso maintained his lead over Rosberg and took his first win for Renault since his return to the team. Rosberg held on to second and Hamilton settled for third. Massa finished in 13th place and the points difference between himself and Hamilton increased to seven points and McLaren took over the lead in the Constructors' Championship. With teammate Räikkönen failing to finish, this was the first time the Ferrari team had failed to score any points in a race since the <a href="/wiki/2006_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="2006 Australian Grand Prix">2006 Australian Grand Prix</a>. Räikkönen recorded his tenth fastest lap of the season during the race, tying his own record that he shared with <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>. Heidfeld broke a Schumacher record, being classified for a 25th consecutive race.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2008_Singapore_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatleft" style="width:28%;&#32;;"><link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r887775652"/>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">A first podium of the season and first victory as well and I am extremely happy. I cannot believe it right now, I think I need a couple of days to realise we won a race this year. It seems impossible all through the season to be close to the top guys and here suddenly we have been competitive from Friday. Obviously we started at the back and the first safety car helped me a lot and I was able to win the race.</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style=""><a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>, speaking after his first win of the year.<sup id="cite_ref-pressconference2_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconference2-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>Fernando Alonso credited his success to the team's strategy and bit of luck. He said that the team chose a two-stop strategy with a short initial stint as opposed to a one-stop strategy, considering the difficulties of overtaking on the track and possibility of brake problems.<sup id="cite_ref-postconf_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-postconf-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Nico Rosberg was pleased with his career best result,<sup id="cite_ref-postconf_30-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-postconf-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> although he recalled when he thought his efforts for a good finish might have been in vain after being given a stop-and-go penalty. He mentioned that he had to come in for the pit stops on the same lap that the safety car came out, and admitted that he was annoyed with the penalty.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton said that he tried to get close to Rosberg towards the final laps, after the second safety car period. However, seeing that overtaking was extremely risky, he decided against it, considering that neither of the Ferraris was going to score.<sup id="cite_ref-postconf_30-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-postconf-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Ferrari team principal <a href="/wiki/Stefano_Domenicali" title="Stefano Domenicali">Stefano Domenicali</a> said that the reason for Massa's pit incident was not the electronic signalling system but a human error. He defended the electronic system saying that the system saves any reaction time for the driver as opposed to a standard "lollipop" system, and considering it was a human error, the incident may have happened with a lollipop as well.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Toyota filed a protest against Toro Rosso for releasing Sebastian Vettel in Timo Glock's path during their first pit stops under the safety car.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> The stewards, however, rejected Toyota's plea.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a>, Lewis Hamilton's third place moved him seven points clear of Felipe Massa on 84 points. With both Ferrari drivers failing to score, McLaren took over the lead in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a> on 135 points. Fernando Alonso's victory meant that Renault overtook Toyota for fourth place in the constructors standings.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reactions">Reactions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2008_Singapore_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Reactions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The international media and many of those directly involved in Formula One praised the staging of the SingTel Singapore Grand Prix highly. Fears which were previously expressed about the weather, visibility and narrow nature of the circuit itself all proved unfounded. Organisation of the event itself was widely deemed to be impeccable as the ushering, transport, and security services all ran like clockwork. However, the drivers did complain about how bumpy the track was, particularly off the racing line, which made overtaking difficult on a street circuit hemmed in by barriers.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Sports supremo and Formula One's commercial rights holder <a href="/wiki/Bernie_Ecclestone" title="Bernie Ecclestone">Bernie Ecclestone</a> heralded the Singapore Grand Prix as the "<i>new jewel in the Formula One sports crown</i>", a phrase often used to describe the <a href="/wiki/Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="Monaco Grand Prix">Monaco Grand Prix</a> circuit.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> He described Singapore as an example to follow and would make people reconsider their opinions of Asia.<sup id="cite_ref-sport.sg.msn.com_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sport.sg.msn.com-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r960796168">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>For years I've been saying go East, not West. When you think about it, most parts of Asia where people have visited, they sort of put everything in one basket – 'Singapore is the same as India, Malaysia and Thailand'. That's how they feel because they don't know any better. Hopefully this will open people's eyes and they'll say, my God, Singapore really is alive and well.</p><div class="templatequotecite">—&#8201;<cite>Commercial rights holder <a href="/wiki/Bernie_Ecclestone" title="Bernie Ecclestone">Bernie Ecclestone</a><sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup></cite></div></blockquote>
<p>Several other prominent Formula One personalities were quoted as saying,
</p>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r960796168"/><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>The guys here have done an absolutely unbelievable job. The effort that has gone into this circuit in the last 12 months is nothing short of phenomenal. I think of the spectacle of a night race, but not only have we got a night race, we have also got a great circuit, a really challenging circuit. They have just raised the bar considerably for a new circuit and the spectacle of racing at night in a big city such as Singapore is really exciting.</p><div class="templatequotecite">—&#8201;<cite>Red Bull racing team principal <a href="/wiki/Christian_Horner" title="Christian Horner">Christian Horner</a><sup id="cite_ref-sport.sg.msn.com_38-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sport.sg.msn.com-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup></cite></div></blockquote>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r960796168"/><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>It has a good chance of challenging Monaco for being the jewel in the crown of Formula One. That is the most accurate thing to say. They have great weather, a very good track and the grandstands packed. I think there is a lot of enthusiasm out there.</p><div class="templatequotecite">—&#8201;<cite>Williams team boss Sir <a href="/wiki/Frank_Williams_(Formula_One)" title="Frank Williams (Formula One)">Frank Williams</a><sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup></cite></div></blockquote>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r960796168"/><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>It is not just a new experience, it is a real big step in the history of grand prix racing because it has been done so well. <p>Locally, Sunday's F1 night race on the local <a href="/wiki/MediaCorp_TV_Channel_5" class="mw-redirect" title="MediaCorp TV Channel 5">MediaCorp TV Channel 5</a> drew more viewers than the <a href="/wiki/2008_Summer_Olympics" title="2008 Summer Olympics">Beijing Olympics</a> <a href="/wiki/2008_Summer_Olympics_opening_ceremony" title="2008 Summer Olympics opening ceremony">opening ceremony</a> in the previous month, becoming the highest-rated programme for the year. The average ratings for Sunday's race were 17.8 per cent, or 789,000 viewers, as compared to 17.3 per cent (765,000 viewers) for the opening of the Beijing Games. The world's inaugural F1 race under floodlights is reported to have been watched by an estimated global TV audience of about 400 million.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="FIA_investigation">FIA investigation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2008_Singapore_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: FIA investigation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Renault_Formula_One_crash_controversy" title="Renault Formula One crash controversy">Renault Formula One crash controversy</a></div>
<p>During the race, <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault F1</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet,_Jr." class="mw-redirect" title="Nelson Piquet, Jr.">Nelson Piquet, Jr.</a> crashed on Lap 14, which at the time he attributed to a simple mistake. At the time, his Renault teammate <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> was the only driver to have <a href="/wiki/Pit_stop" title="Pit stop">pitted</a> for fuel, and he later went on to win the race. After Piquet, Jr. left Renault in August 2009, allegations surfaced that this crash had been deliberate, to give an advantage to Alonso. Following a <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile</a> (FIA) investigation in which Piquet, Jr. stated he had been ordered by Renault team principal <a href="/wiki/Flavio_Briatore" title="Flavio Briatore">Flavio Briatore</a> and engineer <a href="/wiki/Pat_Symonds" title="Pat Symonds">Pat Symonds</a> to stage the crash, on 4 September 2009 Renault were charged with conspiracy and race fixing, and were due to face the <a href="/wiki/FIA_World_Motor_Sport_Council" title="FIA World Motor Sport Council">FIA World Motor Sport Council</a> in Paris on 21 September 2009. Initially, Renault and Briatore stated they would take legal action against Piquet, Jr. for making false allegations; before the 21 September meeting, however, Renault announced they would not contest the charges and that Briatore and Symonds had left the team.<sup id="cite_ref-Telegraph17Sep09QnA_45-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Telegraph17Sep09QnA-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-BBC17Sep09Blame_46-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC17Sep09Blame-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
