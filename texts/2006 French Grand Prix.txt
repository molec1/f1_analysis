<p>The <b>2006 French Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>XCII Grand Prix de France</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Nevers_Magny-Cours" title="Circuit de Nevers Magny-Cours">Circuit de Nevers Magny-Cours</a>, near <a href="/wiki/Magny-Cours" title="Magny-Cours">Magny-Cours</a>, <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> on 16 July 2006. The 70-lap race was the eleventh round of the <a href="/wiki/2006_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2006 Formula One season">2006 Formula One season</a>, the 57th <a href="/wiki/French_Grand_Prix" title="French Grand Prix">French Grand Prix</a> as part of the World Championship, and the 92nd overall. This race also marked the centenary of the <a href="/wiki/1906_French_Grand_Prix" title="1906 French Grand Prix">first French Grand Prix in 1906</a>.
</p><p>This race was a scene of yet another record breaking milestone for <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>, who became the first driver in <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">F1</a> history to win the same <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_Grands_Prix" title="List of Formula One Grands Prix">Grand Prix</a> on eight occasions (having previously won the <a href="/wiki/French_Grand_Prix" title="French Grand Prix">French Grand Prix</a> in <a href="/wiki/1994_French_Grand_Prix" title="1994 French Grand Prix">1994</a>, <a href="/wiki/1995_French_Grand_Prix" title="1995 French Grand Prix">1995</a>, <a href="/wiki/1997_French_Grand_Prix" title="1997 French Grand Prix">1997</a>, <a href="/wiki/1998_French_Grand_Prix" title="1998 French Grand Prix">1998</a>, <a href="/wiki/2001_French_Grand_Prix" title="2001 French Grand Prix">2001</a>, <a href="/wiki/2002_French_Grand_Prix" title="2002 French Grand Prix">2002</a> and <a href="/wiki/2004_French_Grand_Prix" title="2004 French Grand Prix">2004</a>). Schumacher also achieved his 22nd career <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_driver_records#Hat_Trick_(pole,_win_&amp;_fastest_lap_in_same_race)" title="List of Formula One driver records">hat trick</a> (pole position, win &amp; fastest lap at the same race), also a record. <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a> at the team's home race, finished second, whilst Schumacher's <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> team-mate, <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a>, completed the podium by finishing in third position.
</p><p>This was the first time that neither Honda was classified. It was also the 68th and final pole position of Michael Schumacher's career. Schumacher held the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_polesitters" title="List of Formula One polesitters">record for the most pole positions</a> until <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> surpassed it at the <a href="/wiki/2017_Italian_Grand_Prix" title="2017 Italian Grand Prix">2017 Italian Grand Prix</a>. 
</p>
