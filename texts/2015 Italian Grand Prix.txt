<p>The <b>2015 Italian Grand Prix</b> (formally known as the <b>Formula 1 Gran Premio d'Italia 2015</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Auto_racing" title="Auto racing">motor race</a> held on 6 September 2015 at the <a href="/wiki/Autodromo_Nazionale_di_Monza" title="Autodromo Nazionale di Monza">Autodromo Nazionale di Monza</a> in <a href="/wiki/Monza" title="Monza">Monza</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>. The race was the twelfth round of the <a href="/wiki/2015_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2015 Formula One season">2015 season</a>, and marked the 85th running of the <a href="/wiki/Italian_Grand_Prix" title="Italian Grand Prix">Italian Grand Prix</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> of <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> was the defending race winner, and entered with a 28-point lead over teammate <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> a further 39 points behind. Mercedes lead the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a> by 184 points over <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> a further 81 points behind in third.
</p><p>Hamilton completed the second <a href="/wiki/Grand_Slam_(Formula_One)" class="mw-redirect" title="Grand Slam (Formula One)">Grand Slam</a> of his career by winning the race, taking pole position, setting the fastest lap and leading every lap of the race. He finished 25 seconds ahead of Vettel, while <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> finished third for Williams. Rosberg retired with three laps to go due to an engine failure, resulting in Hamilton extending his lead in the Drivers' Championship to 53 points. Hamilton's victory was confirmed a few hours after the race following an investigation from race stewards after it was discovered that the left-rear tyre on his Mercedes was below <a href="/wiki/Pirelli" title="Pirelli">Pirelli</a>'s recommended minimum tyre pressure standards in a random check but due to procedural inconsistencies, no sanctions were imposed against either Mercedes driver or the team.
</p><p>This race marked the 41st and final podium finish for <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Tyre supplier <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_tyres" title="Formula One tyres">Pirelli</a> brought its white-banded medium compound tyre as the harder "prime" tyre and the yellow-banded soft compound as the softer "option" compound, as opposed to the <a href="/wiki/2014_Italian_Grand_Prix" title="2014 Italian Grand Prix">previous year</a> where the company brought the hard compound as the prime and medium compound as the option.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a>'s Head of Track Engineering, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Giampaolo_Dall%27Ara&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Giampaolo Dall&#39;Ara (page does not exist)">Giampaolo Dall'Ara</a>, praised the updated choice, summing up that it will challenge teams to make time in the corners in addition to going for pure top speed, putting on a better show for the fans.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> However, Pirelli had received criticism from several drivers and commentators following tyre failures on <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a>'s and <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a>'s cars at the <a href="/wiki/2015_Belgian_Grand_Prix" title="2015 Belgian Grand Prix">Belgian Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-vettel2_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vettel2-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Prior to the Italian Grand Prix, Formula One commercial rights holder <a href="/wiki/Bernie_Ecclestone" title="Bernie Ecclestone">Bernie Ecclestone</a> called for the drivers to keep their concerns to themselves, saying: "We’re not going to let them [Pirelli] go. They're doing a good job."<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:2015_Italian_Grand_Prix.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2c/2015_Italian_Grand_Prix.jpg/220px-2015_Italian_Grand_Prix.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2c/2015_Italian_Grand_Prix.jpg/330px-2015_Italian_Grand_Prix.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2c/2015_Italian_Grand_Prix.jpg/440px-2015_Italian_Grand_Prix.jpg 2x" data-file-width="960" data-file-height="720" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:2015_Italian_Grand_Prix.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> reintroduced their snub noses for the race.</div></div></div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Lotus_F1" title="Lotus F1">Lotus</a> team, who had their cars impounded by bailiffs following Belgium two weeks earlier, managed to get their cars back in time for the Grand Prix.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Despite their financial troubles, the team said it had enough funds for the rest of the season.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Meanwhile, contract negotiations were high in the agenda prior to the weekend, with <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> announcing that the team would retain <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a> for <a href="/wiki/2016_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2016 Formula One World Championship">2016</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> while <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a> signed a new two-year deal with <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> An additional talking point was the future of the Italian Grand Prix at Monza, with the event at risk to be dropped from the calendar.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> After the race, <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> driver Sebastian Vettel spoke out in favour of the venue, saying: "If we take this race away from the calendar for any shitty money reasons, you are basically ripping our hearts out".<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In terms of technical developments, Ferrari upgraded their engine for their home race by using three of their development tokens while Mercedes spent all seven of their remaining engine tokens.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> As a result of this, Mercedes became the first team in 2015 to use all their tokens, with both Honda and Ferrari having four tokens remaining and Renault having twelve tokens left for later in the season. Because of these changes both Mercedes cars were on their third powerplants, whilst both Ferrari cars were on their fourth power units. Additionally, <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso</a>, <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a> and <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> received grid penalties for exceeding their parts usage, forcing them to start at the back of the grid.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> McLaren went back to using their snub nose design, which they had replaced earlier in the season ahead of the <a href="/wiki/2015_Austrian_Grand_Prix" title="2015 Austrian Grand Prix">Austrian Grand Prix</a>, in order to achieve better aerodynamics and lower drag for the high-speed Monza circuit.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Prior to the race, <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> was leading the World Championship on 227 points, 28 clear of teammate Rosberg. Sebastian Vettel was in third place, another 39 points adrift.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> In the Constructors' standings, <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> were in front on 426 points, with Ferrari and Williams second and third on 242 and 161 points respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Free_practice">Free practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Free practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Per the regulations for the 2015 season, three practice sessions were held; there were two 90-minute sessions on Friday and another one-hour session before qualifying on Saturday.<sup id="cite_ref-pracreg_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pracreg-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> In what <i><a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">The Guardian</a></i> described as an "as dominant as ever" performance, Mercedes and Lewis Hamilton lead the times in both practice sessions on Friday, with Nico Rosberg in second on both occasions.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> During the first session on Friday morning, Hamilton led his teammate by half a second, with Sebastian Vettel in third, another full second behind. <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> was the fastest driver in a <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>-powered car, and trailed Hamilton by more than two seconds. <a href="/wiki/Jolyon_Palmer" title="Jolyon Palmer">Jolyon Palmer</a> again replaced <a href="/wiki/Romain_Grosjean" title="Romain Grosjean">Romain Grosjean</a> in the Lotus during the first practice and ended the session half a second off his teammate's pace in fifteenth. <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Sainz_Jr." title="Carlos Sainz Jr.">Carlos Sainz Jr.</a> briefly brought out a <a href="/wiki/Racing_flags#Red_flag" title="Racing flags">red flag</a> when he got stuck in the gravel after spinning out at Parabolica.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:2015_Italian_Grand_Prix_(3).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ce/2015_Italian_Grand_Prix_%283%29.jpg/220px-2015_Italian_Grand_Prix_%283%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="293" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ce/2015_Italian_Grand_Prix_%283%29.jpg/330px-2015_Italian_Grand_Prix_%283%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ce/2015_Italian_Grand_Prix_%283%29.jpg/440px-2015_Italian_Grand_Prix_%283%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="720" data-file-height="960" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:2015_Italian_Grand_Prix_(3).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A view into the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> garage on Saturday</div></div></div>
<p>At the second practice session on Friday afternoon, Ferrari was able to reduce the gap to Mercedes, with Vettel third a little more than seven-tenths of a second behind Hamilton. Nico Rosberg was just 0.021 seconds off his teammate's time of 1m24.279s, even leading through the first two sectors but losing fastest time through the third and final sector. The two Force India cars of <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a> and Nico Hülkenberg split the two Ferrari drivers, with <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> finishing in sixth. <a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a> was the second Toro Rosso driver of the day to spin out into the gravel, but he was able to continue after losing control of his car at the Ascari chicane. There were technical problems for both Daniel Ricciardo and <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a>. While the Australian had to cut his first run short due to a hydraulical issue, Button was summoned back into the pit lane after only three laps with an unspecified problem.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the third session on Saturday morning, Lewis Hamilton once more finished fastest, but this time it was Sebastian Vettel in second, 0.264s behind Hamilton's time of 1m24.544s. The session started on a slightly wet track caused by rain over the night, but it soon dried up. Ricciardo's session was marred by another technical problem, this time an engine failure to his newly equipped power unit.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> His teammate <a href="/wiki/Daniil_Kvyat" title="Daniil Kvyat">Daniil Kvyat</a> did not have a productive session either as he spent most of the time in the garage, setting only seventeenth best time.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Hamilton_Monza_2015.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fd/Hamilton_Monza_2015.jpg/220px-Hamilton_Monza_2015.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="248" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fd/Hamilton_Monza_2015.jpg/330px-Hamilton_Monza_2015.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fd/Hamilton_Monza_2015.jpg/440px-Hamilton_Monza_2015.jpg 2x" data-file-width="850" data-file-height="960" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Hamilton_Monza_2015.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> qualified on pole position and went on to win the race.</div></div></div>
<p>Qualifying consisted of three parts, 18, 15 and 12 minutes in length respectively, with five drivers eliminated from competing after each of the first two sessions.<sup id="cite_ref-pracreg_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pracreg-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Before qualifying began on Saturday afternoon, Mercedes was forced to change the power unit in Nico Rosberg's car back to the one used in Belgium, which had already run for six full races, after finding an issue with the new specification unit.<sup id="cite_ref-rosbergengine_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rosbergengine-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Meanwhile, Lotus was forced to borrow tyre blankets from other teams, as their own had been rendered unusable by rain during the night.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> When the session began, Nico Hülkenberg was one of the first out on track, but his fast lap was disrupted by <a href="/wiki/Marcus_Ericsson" title="Marcus Ericsson">Marcus Ericsson</a>, who was later penalised for the incident.<sup id="cite_ref-ericsson_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ericsson-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Both Daniel Ricciardo and Max Verstappen spent most of the session in the pit lane. While Ricciardo was able to get out and set a lap sufficient to see him proceed to the second part of qualifying (Q2), Verstappen went out only to see his engine cover blown off at Curva Grande, scattering the track with debris, though at the very end of the session, so no other cars were affected. Next to Verstappen, both <a href="/wiki/Marussia_F1" title="Marussia F1">Manor Marussia</a> and McLaren drivers were eliminated.<sup id="cite_ref-fanqual_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fanqual-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During the first timed runs in Q2, Lewis Hamilton was seven-tenths of a second quicker than teammate Rosberg, while the Ferrari cars split the pair to place provisional second and third. Nevertheless, both Mercedes drivers did not go out again. Sebastian Vettel set a second timed lap to improve his time, now a little more than a tenth behind Hamilton. Many drivers only set one timed lap towards the end of the session, with <a href="/wiki/Pastor_Maldonado" title="Pastor Maldonado">Pastor Maldonado</a>, <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Nasr" title="Felipe Nasr">Felipe Nasr</a>, Carlos Sainz Jr. and Daniil Kvyat unable to move on into Q3, while Ricciardo did not set a time, leaving him fifteenth for the time being.<sup id="cite_ref-fanqual_34-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fanqual-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>When Q3 was contested by the top ten drivers, Nico Rosberg was the first to set a time, but Hamilton immediately went faster, setting a time of 1:23.397, which eventually secured him pole position. Again, both Ferrari drivers went faster than Rosberg, being about three-tenths of a second behind Hamilton. During the last timed runs, Räikkönen moved ahead of Vettel to qualify on the front row, while Rosberg improved his time, but stayed in fourth on the grid. He would later refer to his old power unit as the main reason for his qualifying performance.<sup id="cite_ref-rosbergengine_31-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rosbergengine-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Nico Hülkenberg posted a time good enough for ninth, but his car came to a halt at the pit lane entrance, after his team had miscalculated the fuel put into his car.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> The two Williams of Massa and Bottas qualified fifth and sixth respectively, with Hülkenberg's teammate Pérez behind them in seventh, ahead of Romain Grosjean. Marcus Ericsson rounded up the top ten, but was later dropped to twelfth on the grid for impeating Hülkenberg in Q1.<sup id="cite_ref-ericsson_33-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ericsson-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fanqual_34-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fanqual-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> It was Hamilton's eleventh pole position in twelve races in the season so far.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Toro Rosso's Max Verstappen was handed a drive-through penalty to be served within the first three laps of the race due to an unsafe pit release during qualifying.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Kimi Räikkönen expressed delight with his front row performance, saying that the team was surprised by the strong result and calling it "our best qualifying of the year as a team".<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Sebastian_Vettel_in_his_Ferrari_SF15-T_(21256804649).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2f/Sebastian_Vettel_in_his_Ferrari_SF15-T_%2821256804649%29.jpg/220px-Sebastian_Vettel_in_his_Ferrari_SF15-T_%2821256804649%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="146" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2f/Sebastian_Vettel_in_his_Ferrari_SF15-T_%2821256804649%29.jpg/330px-Sebastian_Vettel_in_his_Ferrari_SF15-T_%2821256804649%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2f/Sebastian_Vettel_in_his_Ferrari_SF15-T_%2821256804649%29.jpg/440px-Sebastian_Vettel_in_his_Ferrari_SF15-T_%2821256804649%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4288" data-file-height="2848" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Sebastian_Vettel_in_his_Ferrari_SF15-T_(21256804649).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> finished second for <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>As the race got under way, Kimi Räikkönen's Ferrari remained motionless on the spot and only started after all other cars had passed the Finn. Meanwhile, Hamilton got through the first chicane in front of Vettel, but his teammate Rosberg had to avoid Räikkönen's car and lost two places to the two Williams of Felipe Massa and Valtteri Bottas. Both Lotus drivers were forced to retire before the end of the second lap, Grosjean due to contact with another car, while Maldonado damaged his chassis when he went over a kerb. While Räikkönen charged through the field to get back up to twelfth place by lap three, Lewis Hamilton quickly increased his advantage over Vettel and moved out of the one-second <a href="/wiki/Drag_reduction_system" title="Drag reduction system">DRS</a> window. Nico Rosberg was unable to pass Bottas on track and was told by his team to cool his brakes. Further back, the Red Bull sponsored cars moved up the order as well, with Carlos Sainz Jr., Daniel Ricciardo and Daniil Kvyat fighting for tenth position. Sainz yielded his place on lap 11, when he needed to go into the pit lane for a five-second penalty handed to him for cutting the chicane at the start.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> By lap 14, Hamilton led Vettel by over eight seconds, with Rosberg still behind both Williams in fifth place, pushing Bottas by enabling DRS on the long straights. Behind the German were the two Force India cars of Sergio Pérez and Nico Hülkenberg.<sup id="cite_ref-racereportguard_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-racereportguard-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On lap 18, Rosberg was the first of the front runners to come in, attempting to "undercut" the two Williams, who were forced to pit shortly after. Massa came in a lap later and Bottas on lap 22, both emerging from the pit lane behind the Mercedes of Rosberg, who was now third. By lap 26, both Hamilton and Vettel had made pit stops as well, as Hamilton was leading the Ferrari by more than twelve seconds. By that point, Räikkönen, who had yet to stop, was in third place but easily overtaken by Rosberg on lap 28. On lap 29, Daniel Ricciardo, also still on his original tyres, moved past Pérez into sixth position, only for the Mexican to retrieve it a lap later. At the end of lap 29, Räikkönen made a pit stop but almost collided with <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Merhi" title="Roberto Merhi">Roberto Merhi</a> at the pit entrance. After his stop, the Finn emerged tenth, but quickly moved up the order again, taking eighth from Marcus Ericsson on lap 32. By lap 36, Nico Rosberg had moved within four seconds of Sebastian Vettel in front of him, while Räikkönen moved into seventh at the expense of Nico Hülkenberg.<sup id="cite_ref-racereportguard_40-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-racereportguard-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:2015_Italian_Grand_Prix_(2).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1e/2015_Italian_Grand_Prix_%282%29.jpg/220px-2015_Italian_Grand_Prix_%282%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1e/2015_Italian_Grand_Prix_%282%29.jpg/330px-2015_Italian_Grand_Prix_%282%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1e/2015_Italian_Grand_Prix_%282%29.jpg/440px-2015_Italian_Grand_Prix_%282%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="960" data-file-height="720" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:2015_Italian_Grand_Prix_(2).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> finished third for <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>By lap 45, Vettel and Rosberg encountered backmarkers on track, which allowed Rosberg to move closer to his compatriot, trailing him by less than three seconds. On lap 49, Mercedes told Hamilton to increase his speed, without giving the driver an explanation. With Hamilton leading Vettel by 23 seconds, the call came unexpected. It later proved to be caused by the fact that Hamilton's left rear tyre had too little pressure at the start, leading the team to fear for a time penalty after the race. <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> retired from the race on lap 50 due to loss of power.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> One lap later, Räikkönen moved into sixth place by overtaking the Force India of Sergio Pérez. With one lap to go, Rosberg was moving ever closer to Vettel in front, when his engine failed, forcing him to retire, handing Felipe Massa the final spot on the podium, who defended the position from teammate Bottas over the final lap. Lewis Hamilton finished the race to take victory on what he himself described as "the best [weekend] I've ever driven".<sup id="cite_ref-guardianrace_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guardianrace-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> He finished more than 25 seconds ahead of Vettel.<sup id="cite_ref-racereportguard_40-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-racereportguard-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> with a grand slam of pole position, fastest lap, win and every lap led.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Podium_Italian_Grand_Prix_2015.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0c/Podium_Italian_Grand_Prix_2015.jpg/220px-Podium_Italian_Grand_Prix_2015.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="227" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0c/Podium_Italian_Grand_Prix_2015.jpg/330px-Podium_Italian_Grand_Prix_2015.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0c/Podium_Italian_Grand_Prix_2015.jpg/440px-Podium_Italian_Grand_Prix_2015.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1490" data-file-height="1536" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Podium_Italian_Grand_Prix_2015.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The podium celebrations</div></div></div>
<p>For more than two hours after the race, Hamilton's victory was still up for debate, as his left rear tyres was found to have had 0.3psi less tyre pressure than regulated.<sup id="cite_ref-guardianrace_42-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guardianrace-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-pressconf_43-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconf-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> While Lewis Hamilton stated in the post-race press conference that he had not been aware of the issue,<sup id="cite_ref-pressconf_43-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconf-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> Williams officials <a href="/wiki/Pat_Symonds" title="Pat Symonds">Pat Symonds</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rob_Smedley" title="Rob Smedley">Rob Smedley</a> called for Hamilton's disqualification, with the latter pointing out that it was "a safety issue".<sup id="cite_ref-bbcracereport_44-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcracereport-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> The stewards eventually decided not to take any further action, stating that: "In making this determination regarding the pressure, the stewards noted that the tyre warming blankets had been disconnected from their power source, as is normal procedure, and the tyres were significantly below the maximum permitted tyre blanket temperature at the time of the FIA's measurement on the grid and at significantly different temperatures from other cars measured on the grid. Further, the stewards are satisfied that the team followed the currently specified procedure, supervised by the tyre manufacturer for the safe operation of the tyres. Therefore, the stewards decide to take no further action."<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At the podium interview, conducted by film director <a href="/wiki/George_Lucas" title="George Lucas">George Lucas</a>, Lewis Hamilton expressed gratitude to his team and the fans at the venue. Felipe Massa, asked about his last lap fight with teammate Bottas, stated: "I’m getting old for that! I even said to the team I’m getting old! The last three laps of the race I was fighting with my team-mate. It was very difficult but we managed to be here." During the post-race press conference, Hamilton expressed surprise over his championship lead, since he had not been made aware of Rosberg's retirement at that point. When asked about the tyre pressure issue, Sebastian Vettel defended Hamilton, saying: "I think it’s not fair to hand that question to Lewis because he doesn’t know what’s going on [...]. In a lot of respect and fairness he did a very good job today and you have to accept that."<sup id="cite_ref-pressconf_43-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconf-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Following the race, Red Bull team principal <a href="/wiki/Christian_Horner" title="Christian Horner">Christian Horner</a> said that Mercedes had made a "worrying step forward" with their new engine specification, admitting that the team was "in a league of their own at the moment".<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> Nico Rosberg, who had raced with the old specification version of the power unit, described the race as a "massive step in the wrong direction".<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> After the race, the team revealed that they had turned his engine up in order to help him catch Vettel in the closing stages.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> The team later admitted that using the new power unit at the Italian Grand Prix had been "a risk".<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As a result of the race, Lewis Hamilton extended his lead in the championship to 53 points over teammate Rosberg.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> His second-place finish meant that Sebastian Vettel moved closer to Rosberg, now trailing him by 21 points.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> In the Constructors' Championship, Mercedes now led the field on 451 points, with Ferrari and Williams in second and third, on 270 and 188 points respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
