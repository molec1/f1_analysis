<p>The <b>2007 British Grand Prix</b> was the ninth race of the <a href="/wiki/2007_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2007 Formula One season">2007 Formula One season</a>. It was held on 8 July 2007 at the <a href="/wiki/Silverstone_Circuit" title="Silverstone Circuit">Silverstone Circuit</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-official_f1_page_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-official_f1_page-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The race was won by <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> after overtaking pole position driver <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> during the first round of pit stops.<sup id="cite_ref-Race_results_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Race_results-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Second place was taken by <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> and Hamilton was third.
</p><p>Following British driver Lewis Hamilton's win at the <a href="/wiki/2007_Canadian_Grand_Prix" title="2007 Canadian Grand Prix">2007 Canadian Grand Prix</a>, circuit director Richard Phillips reported that ticket sales had "gone through the roof". Phillips added, "we haven't seen this level of interest since <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Mansell</a>-mania in the late 80s and early 90s".<sup id="cite_ref-Hamilton_Hype_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hamilton_Hype-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2007_British_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2007_British_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the run up to the race <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> presented a legal case against <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">English</a> engineer <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Stepney" title="Nigel Stepney">Nigel Stepney</a> and a <a href="/wiki/McLaren_(racing)" class="mw-redirect" title="McLaren (racing)">McLaren</a> employee, after a search warrant revealed evidence of theft of technical information from the <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italian</a> team.<sup id="cite_ref-Ferrari_name_Stepney_and_McLaren_engineer_in_theft_allegations_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ferrari_name_Stepney_and_McLaren_engineer_in_theft_allegations-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> McLaren indicated that they would fully cooperate with the investigation, and after a thorough internal investigation the team concluded that "no Ferrari intellectual property has been passed to any other members of the team or incorporated into its cars." The <a href="/wiki/FIA" class="mw-redirect" title="FIA">FIA</a> are launching their own investigation into the matter, but stress that their investigation will only involve matters of the Formula One rules and regulations.<sup id="cite_ref-McLaren:_no_Ferrari_elements_in_team_or_car_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-McLaren:_no_Ferrari_elements_in_team_or_car-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In another element of the pre-race activities, the <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Red Bull F1">Red Bull</a> cars of <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> sported a <a href="/wiki/Livery" title="Livery">livery</a> of fan-submitted <a href="/wiki/Image" title="Image">images</a> as part of a one-off event for the charity <i>Wings for Life</i>. More than 30,000 fans pledged money and uploaded images to the team's website, and each fan has selected a spot on either car to have their image placed. The goal was to raise <a href="/wiki/Euro" title="Euro">€</a>1 million.<sup id="cite_ref-Red_Bull_to_race_30,000_faces_at_Silverstone_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Red_Bull_to_race_30,000_faces_at_Silverstone-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> This would be repeated again at the <a href="/wiki/2012_British_Grand_Prix" title="2012 British Grand Prix">2012 British Grand Prix</a>, but the number would be reduced to 25,000 fans.<sup id="cite_ref-Red_Bull_to_race_25,000_faces_for_Wings_for_Life_charity_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Red_Bull_to_race_25,000_faces_for_Wings_for_Life_charity-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Toyota_at_Silverstone_07.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4c/Toyota_at_Silverstone_07.jpg/220px-Toyota_at_Silverstone_07.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4c/Toyota_at_Silverstone_07.jpg/330px-Toyota_at_Silverstone_07.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4c/Toyota_at_Silverstone_07.jpg/440px-Toyota_at_Silverstone_07.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1024" data-file-height="683" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Toyota_at_Silverstone_07.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> set the fastest time of the second test day in his <a href="/wiki/Toyota_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Toyota F1">Toyota</a> <a href="/wiki/Toyota_TF107" title="Toyota TF107">TF107</a>.</div></div></div><p>Preparations for the race began on 19 June, when the teams began testing at Silverstone. During the first day's testing <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> set the fastest time, focusing on his race setup for the Grand Prix. In contrast the two top constructors, <a href="/wiki/McLaren_(racing)" class="mw-redirect" title="McLaren (racing)">McLaren</a> and <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, set about testing new parts and developments on their cars. This testing was undertaken by their respective test drivers, <a href="/wiki/Pedro_de_la_Rosa" title="Pedro de la Rosa">Pedro de la Rosa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Luca_Badoer" title="Luca Badoer">Luca Badoer</a>. <a href="/wiki/Spyker_F1" title="Spyker F1">Spyker</a> brought a new <a href="/wiki/Chassis" title="Chassis">chassis</a> to the test and raced it for the first time, with <a href="/wiki/Adri%C3%A1n_Vall%C3%A9s" title="Adrián Vallés">Adrián Vallés</a> and <a href="/wiki/Giedo_van_der_Garde" title="Giedo van der Garde">Giedo van der Garde</a> taking the car around the track for 64 laps. <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> ended his test session early after complaining of back problems.<sup id="cite_ref-Silverstone_day_one_-_Ralf_sets_the_pace_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Silverstone_day_one_-_Ralf_sets_the_pace-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In day two of testing it was <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a>'s turn to take the <a href="/wiki/Toyota_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Toyota F1">Toyota</a> car around Silverstone, and managed to set the fastest time before the session was slowed down by showers. While Ferrari continued to test new parts to their car, this time with <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> taking to the seat, McLaren stuck with <a href="/wiki/Pedro_de_la_Rosa" title="Pedro de la Rosa">Pedro de la Rosa</a> and worked on the race set up and <a href="/wiki/Aerodynamics" title="Aerodynamics">aerodynamics</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Silverstone_day_two_-_Trulli_keeps_Toyota_on_top_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Silverstone_day_two_-_Trulli_keeps_Toyota_on_top-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Day three saw Toyota drop down from taking the fastest times to take the fifth fastest time on a moderately damp track. <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> was the fastest man on the track after 87 laps of the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">British</a> circuit testing various aerodynamic and mechanical adjustments to the car. Although delayed through an <a href="/wiki/Oil" title="Oil">oil</a> system problem Alonso finished off aerodynamics work and continued to set up the car for the coming British Grand Prix, finishing with a time slower than second placed <a href="/wiki/Williams_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams F1">Williams</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a>. The leading <a href="/wiki/GP2_Series" title="GP2 Series">GP2</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Timo_Glock" title="Timo Glock">Timo Glock</a> took to the circuit with <a href="/wiki/BMW_Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="BMW Sauber">BMW Sauber</a> up against ex-rival <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Angelo_Piquet" class="mw-redirect" title="Nelson Angelo Piquet">Nelson Piquet Jr.</a> who was driving with <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a>. Piquet Jr. made the fourth fastest time, more than a second faster than Glock.<sup id="cite_ref-Massa_tops_final_day_at_Silverstone_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Massa_tops_final_day_at_Silverstone-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice">Practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2007_British_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The first Friday practice was dominated as per many of the previous Grand Prix of the season, with Ferrari and McLaren taking the top 4 times. Lewis Hamilton took the top time at his home Grand Prix, and the two Ferraris split Alonso from his team mate.<sup id="cite_ref-Friday_practice_1_results_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Friday_practice_1_results-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Both McLaren drivers spun their cars at different parts of the test, and they were not the only to do so. Other such drivers included <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a>, <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a>, <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a>, Trulli and Massa; who all spun their vehicles on the greasy, but dry track.<sup id="cite_ref-Practice_1_-_Hamilton_makes_the_perfect_start_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Practice_1_-_Hamilton_makes_the_perfect_start-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> In the end Rosberg managed to set a faster time than the two competitive <a href="/wiki/BMW_Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="BMW Sauber">BMWs</a>, and Trulli - who boasted a fastest time at Silverstone during testing in the last month - only just managed to set a faster time than <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Davidson" title="Anthony Davidson">Anthony Davidson</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Super_Aguri_F1" title="Super Aguri F1">Super Aguri</a>. Davidson outpaced his <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">British</a> counterpart <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> in the works <a href="/wiki/Honda_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Honda F1">Honda</a> by half a second.<sup id="cite_ref-Friday_practice_1_results_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Friday_practice_1_results-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Button, who suffered a minor back injury in a shunt in the <a href="/wiki/2007_United_States_Grand_Prix" title="2007 United States Grand Prix">United States Grand Prix</a>, decided not to partake in the second Friday practice session in order to avoid aggravating the injury.<sup id="cite_ref-Back_injury_puts_Button_out_of_second_practice_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Back_injury_puts_Button_out_of_second_practice-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Toyota_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Toyota F1">Toyota</a> returned to previous testing form in the second session, putting their drivers in with the McLaren drivers, both of them outpacing Alonso. Schumacher even managed to run exactly the same time as Hamilton. Ferrari meanwhile got the two fastest times of the session, with Räikkönen a full half a second faster than his <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazilian</a> team-mate. <a href="/wiki/Williams_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams F1">Williams</a> were close up behind Alonso, as <a href="/wiki/BMW_Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="BMW Sauber">BMW</a> were running lower in the field than usual.<sup id="cite_ref-Friday_practice_2_results_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Friday_practice_2_results-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Although Kubica was not complaining, <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> said that "[the car] was difficult to drive, so we have to figure out why."<sup id="cite_ref-Friday_practice_-_selected_driver_quotes_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Friday_practice_-_selected_driver_quotes-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Christian_Klien" title="Christian Klien">Christian Klien</a> got a drive during the practice to replace Button in the car and help set it up. Both of the works Hondas were beaten by both of the Super Aguri cars and Davidson improved on his time and position to tenth.<sup id="cite_ref-Friday_practice_2_results_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Friday_practice_2_results-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Drivers from many different teams were running off the Copse corner, with eight drivers running off eleven times. The only incident of the afternoon was at the Copse, where <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a> ran his car onto the tarmac, then spun into the tyre barrier. He was unharmed and the damage to the car was insignificant.<sup id="cite_ref-Friday_practice_-_selected_driver_quotes_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Friday_practice_-_selected_driver_quotes-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Practice_2_-_Raikkonen_and_Ferrari_on_top,_as_Toyota_surprise_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Practice_2_-_Raikkonen_and_Ferrari_on_top,_as_Toyota_surprise-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On the Saturday practice before the qualifying, the top drivers broke the 1 minute 20 barrier, with Massa, Alonso and Räikkönen all setting fastest laps in the high 1 minute 19s. Räikkönen took the fastest time, faster than rival Alonso who had set a very similar time to Massa.<sup id="cite_ref-Saturday_practice_results_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Saturday_practice_results-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton's final time on low fuel was disrupted by <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Wurz" title="Alexander Wurz">Alexander Wurz</a> sending his Williams off the track, resulting in a yellow flag.<sup id="cite_ref-Raikkonen_edges_Alonso_ahead_of_qualifying_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Raikkonen_edges_Alonso_ahead_of_qualifying-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Toyota and Williams were the closest to the top of the pack - as BMW continued their bad run. Nick Heidfeld improved from his bad run from the day before to take a joint eighth fastest time, but team-mate Kubica found himself only able to achieve a sixteenth fastest time. Davidson out paced his team-mate for the third time of the weekend, and he achieved a tenth fastest time - something he has never converted to a qualifying session.<sup id="cite_ref-Saturday_practice_results_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Saturday_practice_results-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2007_British_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The first <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_racing#Qualifying_sessions" title="Formula One racing">qualifying</a> session was disrupted near the end by Anthony Davidson, who spun his Super Aguri while attempting to warm the tyres, bringing out the yellow flags. This also meant that he could not complete a good hot lap and left him near the back of the grid. This also forced Sato and Button to slow down on their hot laps, and the three drivers could not improve on their previous times in the closing stages of the session.<sup id="cite_ref-Hamilton_snatches_last-minute_pole_at_Silverstone_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hamilton_snatches_last-minute_pole_at_Silverstone-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> The six drivers eliminated in the first round were <a href="/wiki/Christijan_Albers" title="Christijan Albers">Albers</a>, Sato, Sutil, Davidson, Button and Rosberg (for the first time this season, but due to an engine issue).<sup id="cite_ref-Qualifying_results_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Qualifying_results-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> The fastest time of the session  was taking by Alonso.
</p><p>The second session was quite uneventful, with Alonso taking the fastest time again. Near the end of the session the six fastest drivers decided not to set any more times. This was a gamble for the two BMW drivers, as by the end of the session Heidfeld had been pushed down to tenth position. The six drivers who were eliminated from the final session were Liuzzi, Speed, Barrichello, Wurz, Coulthard and Webber.<sup id="cite_ref-Qualifying_results_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Qualifying_results-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The two McLaren drivers were the first two drivers out on the track for the initial fuel burning phase, and initially, Alonso was the fastest on the track. After the first set of pit stops it looked like Alonso was going to take the pole. On the last set of laps, Alonso was on a faster time than he was before, but Räikkönen was on a faster. Both of them completed their laps and Räikkönen came out on top, despite an error coming out of the final corner.<sup id="cite_ref-Hamilton_snatches_last-minute_pole_at_Silverstone_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hamilton_snatches_last-minute_pole_at_Silverstone-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton who had been sitting in fourth did not look like he was very competitive during qualifying. In the last few seconds of the session he set the fastest lap and put himself on pole position, and he was the only one to break into the 1 minute 19 second margin.<sup id="cite_ref-Qualifying_results_21-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Qualifying_results-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2007_British_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Hamilton_leading_Silverstone_2007.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ee/Hamilton_leading_Silverstone_2007.jpg/300px-Hamilton_leading_Silverstone_2007.jpg" decoding="async" width="300" height="155" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ee/Hamilton_leading_Silverstone_2007.jpg/450px-Hamilton_leading_Silverstone_2007.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ee/Hamilton_leading_Silverstone_2007.jpg/600px-Hamilton_leading_Silverstone_2007.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1024" data-file-height="528" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Hamilton_leading_Silverstone_2007.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Hamilton leads the early stages of the Grand Prix, ahead of winner <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a>.</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Kimi_Raikkonen_2007_Britain.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d3/Kimi_Raikkonen_2007_Britain.jpg/220px-Kimi_Raikkonen_2007_Britain.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d3/Kimi_Raikkonen_2007_Britain.jpg/330px-Kimi_Raikkonen_2007_Britain.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d3/Kimi_Raikkonen_2007_Britain.jpg/440px-Kimi_Raikkonen_2007_Britain.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1600" data-file-height="1071" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Kimi_Raikkonen_2007_Britain.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Räikkönen got past Hamilton at the first round of pit stops and Alonso during the second to take his third win of the season.</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Fernando_Alonso_2007_Britain.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a0/Fernando_Alonso_2007_Britain.jpg/220px-Fernando_Alonso_2007_Britain.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a0/Fernando_Alonso_2007_Britain.jpg/330px-Fernando_Alonso_2007_Britain.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a0/Fernando_Alonso_2007_Britain.jpg/440px-Fernando_Alonso_2007_Britain.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1600" data-file-height="1071" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Fernando_Alonso_2007_Britain.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Alonso led the race during his middle stint, but had to settle for second place.</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Lewis_Hamilton_2007_Britain.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/86/Lewis_Hamilton_2007_Britain.jpg/220px-Lewis_Hamilton_2007_Britain.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/86/Lewis_Hamilton_2007_Britain.jpg/330px-Lewis_Hamilton_2007_Britain.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/86/Lewis_Hamilton_2007_Britain.jpg/440px-Lewis_Hamilton_2007_Britain.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1600" data-file-height="1071" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Lewis_Hamilton_2007_Britain.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>After starting from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>, Hamilton led until his first stop.  He lost ground to Räikkönen and Alonso from that moment onwards, and finished third.</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Podio.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9e/Podio.jpg/220px-Podio.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="255" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9e/Podio.jpg/330px-Podio.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9e/Podio.jpg/440px-Podio.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1230" data-file-height="1428" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Podio.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Podium: First Räikkönen, second Alonso and third Hamilton.</div></div></div>
<p>Felipe Massa stalled on the grid, leading to a second formation lap and causing the <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazilian</a> to start from the pit lane, joining <a href="/wiki/Takuma_Sato" title="Takuma Sato">Takuma Sato</a>, who also started from the pits. Hamilton led from the start with Räikkönen closely following and Alonso a few seconds behind them. Hamilton led the race until lap sixteen, when he made his stop and rejoined in fifth; his car briefly lurched forward with the fuel hose attached when Hamilton reacted to the 'lollipop' board being turned over. Räikkönen stopped three laps later and came out of the pit in front of Hamilton to take the race lead. Another three laps later Alonso took a good pit stop and came out in front of both of the previous race leaders.<sup id="cite_ref-British_race_analysis_-_Ferrari_resurgence_gathers_pace_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-British_race_analysis_-_Ferrari_resurgence_gathers_pace-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As Alonso was short-fuelled in the first set of pit stops in order to jump into the race lead, it meant that he had to stop quite early compared to his rival. McLaren's plan was to use the superior pace of a lighter car to build up a large gap between him and Räikkönen before the second set of pit stops, but the traffic played into Räikkönen's hands and when Alonso had to pit Räikkönen resumed the race lead. He was able to make his final stop and rejoined without losing first position.<sup id="cite_ref-British_race_analysis_-_Ferrari_resurgence_gathers_pace_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-British_race_analysis_-_Ferrari_resurgence_gathers_pace-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Behind the leaders, Massa was forcing his way through the pack to make up as many places as possible from the back, and finished in fifth behind a defensive <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a>. <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> took sixth, while <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> brought the two Renaults home in seventh and eighth. <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> were taking advantage of being quite far back by investing in a one stop strategy and they finished ninth and tenth respectively. <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> had a good race taking back quite a few places from the start - with points a possibility, but after a bad first pit stop he had to fight back to gain twelfth. His team-mate, Wurz, finished one place behind him in thirteenth after having tried to overtake <a href="/wiki/Scott_Speed" title="Scott Speed">Scott Speed</a> and putting the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> out of the race with broken suspension.<sup id="cite_ref-Raikkonen_and_Ferrari_unstoppable_at_Silverstone_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Raikkonen_and_Ferrari_unstoppable_at_Silverstone-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>David Coulthard took a good eleventh position; his team-mate, Webber, retired due to a full <a href="/wiki/Hydraulics" title="Hydraulics">hydraulics</a> failure. <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a> retired after a large <a href="/wiki/Engine" title="Engine">engine</a> failure going towards the Stowe corner. Toyota suffered the worst problems as both of their cars went out of the race, Ralf Schumacher retiring with a wheel fixation problem, and his team mate, Trulli retiring with handling problems. Anthony Davidson, after a good weekend of practice and an unlucky qualifying session, had to retire his Super Aguri car when he felt vibrations from the underside of the car.<sup id="cite_ref-British_race_analysis_-_Ferrari_resurgence_gathers_pace_22-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-British_race_analysis_-_Ferrari_resurgence_gathers_pace-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Liuzzi retired his car with gearbox failure but was ultimately still classified in 16th place.
</p><p>Räikkönen continued from the second stint of pit stops to take the win a couple of seconds ahead of Alonso.<sup id="cite_ref-Race_results_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Race_results-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> It was Räikkönen's second straight win after being victorious at the <a href="/wiki/2007_French_Grand_Prix" title="2007 French Grand Prix">French Grand Prix</a> the week before. Alonso, finishing second, told the Spanish <a href="/wiki/News_media" title="News media">press</a> that he was adamant that he would catch up with Hamilton in the drivers championship saying that, "With normal races, sooner or later I will close the gap. I must always be one step higher than him on the podium."<sup id="cite_ref-Alonso_confident_of_closing_gap_to_Hamilton_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Alonso_confident_of_closing_gap_to_Hamilton-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> The British driver finished third to lose two points in the championship to the chasing Alonso, but also achieving his ninth podium out of nine race starts,<sup id="cite_ref-Race_results_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Race_results-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> equalling <a href="/wiki/Jim_Clark" title="Jim Clark">Jim Clark</a>'s record.<sup id="cite_ref-f1fbgpstatsfacts_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1fbgpstatsfacts-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Following the race <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> chief <a href="/wiki/Luca_di_Montezemolo" class="mw-redirect" title="Luca di Montezemolo">Luca di Montezemolo</a> complained that although Räikkönen had now won more races than any other driver in 2007, he was not leading the championship. Di Montezemolo said: "This is unsporting, it’s wrong: F1 shouldn’t be a sport for calculators, it should be for winners."<sup id="cite_ref-f1fbgpstatsfacts_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1fbgpstatsfacts-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
