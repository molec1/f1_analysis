<p>The <b>1980 United States Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on October 5, 1980 at the <a href="/wiki/Watkins_Glen_International" title="Watkins Glen International">Watkins Glen Grand Prix Race Course</a> in <a href="/wiki/Watkins_Glen,_New_York" title="Watkins Glen, New York">Watkins Glen, New York</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> This event was also referred to as the <b>United States Grand Prix East</b> in order to distinguish it from the <a href="/wiki/1980_United_States_Grand_Prix_West" title="1980 United States Grand Prix West">United States Grand Prix West</a> held on March 30, 1980 in <a href="/wiki/Long_Beach,_California" title="Long Beach, California">Long Beach, California</a>.
</p><p>It was the fourteenth and final race of the <a href="/wiki/1980_Formula_One_season" title="1980 Formula One season">1980 Formula One season</a>. The race was the 30th <a href="/wiki/United_States_Grand_Prix" title="United States Grand Prix">United States Grand Prix</a>, the 20th and last to be held at Watkins Glen and the last to be held for nine years. The race was held over 59 laps of the 5.435-kilometre (3.377&#160;mi) circuit for a total race distance of 320.67 kilometres (199.26&#160;mi).
</p><p>The race was won by the new World Champion, Australian driver <a href="/wiki/Alan_Jones_(racing_driver)" title="Alan Jones (racing driver)">Alan Jones</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW07B" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams FW07B">Williams FW07B</a>. It was Jones' fifth World Championship Formula One victory of the season and the sixth of the seven Grands Prix (including <a href="/wiki/1980_Spanish_Grand_Prix" title="1980 Spanish Grand Prix">Spain</a> and <a href="/wiki/1980_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="1980 Australian Grand Prix">Australia</a>) he would win in his career defining season. Jones won by four seconds over his Argentinian team mate <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Reutemann" title="Carlos Reutemann">Carlos Reutemann</a> with French driver <a href="/wiki/Didier_Pironi" title="Didier Pironi">Didier Pironi</a> finishing third in his <a href="/wiki/Ligier_JS11/15" class="mw-redirect" title="Ligier JS11/15">Ligier JS11/15</a>. This was also the final race for <a href="/wiki/1972_Formula_One_season" title="1972 Formula One season">1972</a> and <a href="/wiki/1974_Formula_One_season" title="1974 Formula One season">1974</a> World Champion <a href="/wiki/Emerson_Fittipaldi" title="Emerson Fittipaldi">Emerson Fittipaldi</a>, since this circuit was the same one where he scored his first victory 10 years earlier in <a href="/wiki/1970_United_States_Grand_Prix" title="1970 United States Grand Prix">1970</a>. It was also the final race for <a href="/wiki/1979_Formula_One_season" title="1979 Formula One season">1979</a> World Champion <a href="/wiki/Jody_Scheckter" title="Jody Scheckter">Jody Scheckter</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Summary">Summary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1980_United_States_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Summary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p><a href="/wiki/Frank_Williams_(Formula_One)" title="Frank Williams (Formula One)">Frank Williams</a>' first Championship season, his eleventh as an owner, ended in style with another victory by team leader and new Driver's Champion <a href="/wiki/Alan_Jones_(racing_driver)" title="Alan Jones (racing driver)">Alan Jones</a>, but this was the last Grand Prix to be held on the historic Watkins Glen course in the wooded hills at the foot of <a href="/wiki/Seneca_Lake,_New_York" class="mw-redirect" title="Seneca Lake, New York">Seneca Lake</a>. The Glen's demise came about when attempts to resurface the track proved not to be enough, runoff areas became too short for the speeds produced, and attendance was not what it had been, for even this race had been in doubt until just a month before.
</p><p>As the track's last Grand Prix weekend got under way, the big surprise of qualifying was the <a href="/wiki/Alfa_Romeo_in_Formula_One" title="Alfa Romeo in Formula One">Alfa Romeo</a> of Italian <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Giacomelli" title="Bruno Giacomelli">Bruno Giacomelli</a>, who was quickest in both Friday's and Saturday's sessions, averaging 130.314&#160;mph (209.720&#160;km/h). Giacomelli took the only pole of his 69-race career, and the first for Alfa Romeo since 1951, by three-quarters of a second over a tightly-bunched group including <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Brabham" title="Brabham">Brabham</a>, <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Reutemann" title="Carlos Reutemann">Carlos Reutemann</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a>, <a href="/wiki/Elio_de_Angelis" title="Elio de Angelis">Elio de Angelis</a>' <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Lotus</a> and Jones in the second Williams.
</p><p>Race day threatened rain, but none appeared. <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a>, completing his first F1 season had a heavy crash at Turn 10 during Saturday morning's practice. He hit his head on his McLaren's steering wheel and suffered a concussion, forcing him to miss the race. This enabled Dutchman <a href="/wiki/Jan_Lammers" title="Jan Lammers">Jan Lammers</a> to start the race, and Prost, already having suffered 8 previous mechanical failures during the season chose to leave McLaren for the factory <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a> team. At the start, Giacomelli got off the line well and led into the first turn. Jones rocketed forward from fifth on the grid to second entering Turn One, but he could not hold his line through the turn. He, along with Giacomelli's teammate <a href="/wiki/Andrea_de_Cesaris" title="Andrea de Cesaris">Andrea de Cesaris</a> swung wide onto the dirt, then collected himself and continued, finishing the first lap in fourteenth place.
</p><p>At the end of a lap, Giacomelli had opened a gap back to Piquet, followed by Reutemann, <a href="/wiki/Didier_Pironi" title="Didier Pironi">Didier Pironi</a>, de Angelis and <a href="/wiki/H%C3%A9ctor_Rebaque" title="Héctor Rebaque">Héctor Rebaque</a>. In two more laps, the Alfa was ahead by two and a half seconds. Meanwhile, Jones was battling his way back through the field, and on lap 7, he was already poking around the gearbox of <a href="/wiki/John_Watson_(racing_driver)" title="John Watson (racing driver)">John Watson</a>'s <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>, trying to take seventh place. Watson held him off until the tenth lap, and, once by, Jones quickly pulled away after de Angelis and Pironi.
</p><p>Giacomelli continued to set an even pace, and his lead was ten seconds on lap 21, with Reutemann now threatening Piquet, whose tires were beginning to go off. Jones had caught up to de Angelis, and on the next lap, got around him for fifth.
</p><p>Reutemann's pressure, and the Brabham's increasing lack of grip, finally got to Piquet on lap 26 as he spun off in turn one. The off-road excursion damaged a skirt, and he retired after reaching the pits. Three laps later, Jones passed Pironi for third, as the <a href="/wiki/Equipe_Ligier" title="Equipe Ligier">Ligier</a> was also suffering from tire wear.
</p><p>The only car between Jones and the leader now was teammate Reutemann, but with the Driver's Championship already in Jones' hands, there would be no team orders. Understeer induced by a worn left front tire was plaguing the Argentinian, however, and Jones easily overcame him on lap 30 and set his sights on Giacomelli, now 12 seconds ahead. The red and white Alfa Romeo coasted to a halt in the Boot on lap 32 with electrical trouble. Once in the lead, Jones went faster and faster, finally doing a 1:34.068 on lap 44, better than his qualifying time.
</p><p>When Watson pitted to replace a broken shock absorber on his McLaren, crowd favorite <a href="/wiki/Mario_Andretti" title="Mario Andretti">Mario Andretti</a> moved up to the final points position. A set of new tires allowed <a href="/wiki/Ren%C3%A9_Arnoux" title="René Arnoux">René Arnoux</a> to catch and pass the American, but a broken skirt soon slowed the <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>, and Andretti was able to regain the sixth spot and score his only point of the season.
</p><p>The Williams team celebrated the climax to a year in which they had taken the Driver's and Constructor's Championships by waving a huge <a href="/wiki/Union_Flag" class="mw-redirect" title="Union Flag">Union Flag</a> at their one-two finish. The race saw not only the end of Formula One at Watkins Glen, but former champions <a href="/wiki/Emerson_Fittipaldi" title="Emerson Fittipaldi">Emerson Fittipaldi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jody_Scheckter" title="Jody Scheckter">Jody Scheckter</a>'s last Grand Prix and Mario Andretti's last race for <a href="/wiki/Colin_Chapman" title="Colin Chapman">Colin Chapman</a> and Lotus. This Grand Prix would also witness the final occasion where a privateer entry would be classified as a finisher with <a href="/wiki/Rupert_Keegan" title="Rupert Keegan">Rupert Keegan</a> finishing in 9th place in a <a href="/wiki/RAM_Racing" title="RAM Racing">RAM Racing</a>-entered Williams FW07.
</p>
