<p>The <b>1984 French Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held at <a href="/wiki/Dijon-Prenois" title="Dijon-Prenois">Dijon</a> on 20 May 1984. It was race 5 of 16 in the <a href="/wiki/1984_FIA_Formula_One_World_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="1984 FIA Formula One World Championship">1984 FIA Formula One World Championship</a>.
</p><p>This was the final Formula One race to be held at the 3.801&#160;km (2.362&#160;mi) Dijon-Prenois circuit, as it was deemed too short by governing body <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_du_Sport_Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale du Sport Automobile">FISA</a>. Fittingly in France, Frenchman <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Tambay" title="Patrick Tambay">Patrick Tambay</a>, in the all-French team (French car, French engine, French tyres and French fuel) scored the <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Equipe Renault</a> team's last pole position.
</p><p>Double World Champion <a href="/wiki/Niki_Lauda" title="Niki Lauda">Niki Lauda</a> won the race in his <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/2" title="McLaren MP4/2">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Techniques_d%27Avant_Garde" title="Techniques d&#39;Avant Garde">TAG</a>, his first win in France since <a href="/wiki/1975_French_Grand_Prix" title="1975 French Grand Prix">1975</a>. Tambay finished second in his <a href="/wiki/Renault_RE50" title="Renault RE50">Renault RE50</a>, with the <a href="/wiki/Lotus_94T" title="Lotus 94T">Lotus</a>-Renault of <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> third. Lauda's team-mate and World Championship leader, <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a>, could only manage seventh after he was forced to pit to change a loose wheel.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Andrea_de_Cesaris" title="Andrea de Cesaris">Andrea de Cesaris</a> failed to qualify his <a href="/wiki/Ligier_JS23" title="Ligier JS23">Ligier</a>, but nonetheless started the race and finished tenth. De Cesaris' Friday qualifying time was disallowed when his car's onboard fire extinguisher was found to be empty, before rain in the Saturday session prevented him from setting a time fast enough to make the grid. Ligier took the bizarre step of withdrawing their second car, driven by <a href="/wiki/Fran%C3%A7ois_Hesnault" title="François Hesnault">François Hesnault</a> (who had qualified 14th), in order to allow de Cesaris to start 26th and last.
</p>
