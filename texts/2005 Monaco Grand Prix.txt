<p>The <b>2005 Monaco Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>LXIII Grand Prix de Monaco</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 22 May 2005 at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Monaco" title="Circuit de Monaco">Circuit de Monaco</a> in <a href="/wiki/Monte_Carlo" title="Monte Carlo">Monte Carlo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Monaco" title="Monaco">Monaco</a>. The 78-lap race was the sixth round of the <a href="/wiki/2005_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2005 Formula One season">2005 Formula One season</a> and the 63rd running of the <a href="/wiki/Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="Monaco Grand Prix">Monaco Grand Prix</a>. It was won by polesitter and <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a>. <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> completed the race in second position whilst his teammate, <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a>, completed the podium by finishing in third place.
</p><p>This was the first <a href="/wiki/Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="Monaco Grand Prix">Monaco Grand Prix</a> held after the death of <a href="/wiki/Prince_Rainier_III" class="mw-redirect" title="Prince Rainier III">Prince Rainier III</a>. His death was the reason why the <a href="/wiki/Monegasque_Princely_Family" class="mw-redirect" title="Monegasque Princely Family">Princely Family</a> did not attend the Grand Prix.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2005_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice">Practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2005_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In practice, <a href="/wiki/Christijan_Albers" title="Christijan Albers">Christijan Albers</a> lost his car out coming from turn 4 and crashed. <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a>, <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> crashed as they were coming up high from Beau Rivage. Montoya was judged to be the cause of this incident and thus was forced to start the race from the back of the grid.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2005_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Polesitter Kimi Räikkönen led the field from the start of the race, and though he was at first closely followed by <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>, he was more than five seconds ahead by the twentieth lap. Meanwhile, <a href="/wiki/Narain_Karthikeyan" title="Narain Karthikeyan">Narain Karthikeyan</a> retired with a hydraulic failure. The race progressed otherwise uneventfully, with <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> followed by <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a>, and Mark Webber, until the twenty-third lap, when Christijan Albers spun his <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a> into a wall at Mirabeau, blocking about two-thirds of the width of the track. David Coulthard, arriving behind him, swerved and successfully stopped his <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a> without damage; however, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> plowed into Coulthard, breaking off his own nosecone and damaging the Red Bull's suspension beyond repair. As more drivers reached the blocked turn, the Safety Car was deployed so that marshals could remove Albers' car.
</p><p>Both <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a> drivers pitted immediately, but Räikkönen - acting on instructions from McLaren chief strategist <a href="/wiki/Neil_Martin_(motorsport)" title="Neil Martin (motorsport)">Neil Martin</a> - continued on in what was to prove a winning move.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Though this seemingly put Räikkönen in a bad spot, as all of his close competitors had pitted, he fought back with a series of brilliant laps that would give him a 34.7-second lead by the time he pitted on lap 42. Alonso, whose car was substantially slower as it was full of fuel, and whose rear tyres were wearing rapidly, was unable to catch up, and Räikkönen, after pitting, returned to the track still 13 seconds ahead. He would go on to win the race having led every lap of it.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> worked his way up from sixth grid position, passing his own teammate in the pits after <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a> called him in a lap earlier whilst Alonso was badly holding the two Williams drivers up.  After Heidfeld's stop, a superb overtaking manoeuvre into the Nouvelle chicane put him ahead of Alonso.  Webber tried to follow suit a lap later, but on the first attempt Alonso cut the corner and stayed in front. On the second attempt Alonso cut the corner again, this time clearly intentionally, but Webber eventually succeeded in overtaking him and claiming his first career podium in Formula One and scoring Williams last double podium finish until the <a href="/wiki/2014_Abu_Dhabi_Grand_Prix" title="2014 Abu Dhabi Grand Prix">2014 Abu Dhabi Grand Prix</a> over nine years later. Alonso straggled into fourth, his rear tyres almost entirely bald. Montoya advanced from sixteenth on the grid to an eventual fifth, finishing on Alonso's tail, followed by Michael and Ralf Schumacher, who crossed the line almost side-by-side.
</p><p>Both <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a> cars ran with the <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Wars:_Episode_III_%E2%80%93_Revenge_of_the_Sith" title="Star Wars: Episode III – Revenge of the Sith">Star Wars: Episode III – Revenge of the Sith</a></i> livery and, for this race, the Red Bull Racing pit crew dressed up as <a href="/wiki/Stormtrooper_(Star_Wars)" title="Stormtrooper (Star Wars)">Imperial Stormtroopers</a>. It didn't help the team's fortunes, as this was the first race where they failed to score points.
</p>
