<p>The <b>1961 Dutch Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 22 May 1961 at <a href="/wiki/Circuit_Park_Zandvoort" class="mw-redirect" title="Circuit Park Zandvoort">Zandvoort</a>. It was race 2 of 8 in both the <a href="/wiki/1961_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1961 World Championship of Drivers">1961 World Championship of Drivers</a> and the <a href="/wiki/1961_International_Cup_for_Formula_One_Manufacturers" class="mw-redirect" title="1961 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers">1961 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers</a>.
</p><p>Taking place one week after the <a href="/wiki/1961_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="1961 Monaco Grand Prix">Monaco Grand Prix</a>, there was not time for <a href="/wiki/Innes_Ireland" title="Innes Ireland">Innes Ireland</a> to heal from his injury in the previous race, so he was replaced by <a href="/wiki/Trevor_Taylor_(racing_driver)" title="Trevor Taylor (racing driver)">Trevor Taylor</a>. The front row was taken up by three <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferraris</a>. <a href="/wiki/Wolfgang_von_Trips" title="Wolfgang von Trips">Wolfgang von Trips</a> took the lead from the start and led every lap. <a href="/wiki/Phil_Hill" title="Phil Hill">Phil Hill</a> was second but was soon pressured by <a href="/wiki/Jim_Clark" title="Jim Clark">Jim Clark</a>, who had started from the fourth row. The two would trade second place often with the Ferrari quicker on the straight and the Lotus faster in the corners. This continued until about 20 laps from the end when Clark's handling allowed the Ferrari to pull away. Fourth place was contested between <a href="/wiki/Stirling_Moss" title="Stirling Moss">Stirling Moss</a> and <a href="/wiki/Richie_Ginther" title="Richie Ginther">Richie Ginther</a>, with Moss passing Ginther on the final lap. The race was the first of nine races in Formula 1 history in which every car was classified as a finisher. In addition, no drivers made any pit stops.<sup id="cite_ref-LangVol1_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LangVol1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
