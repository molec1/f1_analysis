<p>The <b>2004 United States Grand Prix</b>, officially the <b>XXXIII Foster's United States Grand Prix</b>, was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 20 June 2004 at the <a href="/wiki/Indianapolis_Motor_Speedway" title="Indianapolis Motor Speedway">Indianapolis Motor Speedway</a> in <a href="/wiki/Indianapolis,_Indiana" class="mw-redirect" title="Indianapolis, Indiana">Indianapolis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Indiana" title="Indiana">Indiana</a>. It was the ninth round of the <a href="/wiki/2004_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2004 Formula One season">2004 Formula One season</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> started from pole position in his Ferrari ahead of teammate <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>. However, following a start-line incident between five cars, four of whom retired as a result, Schumacher overtook Barrichello on the safety car restart on lap six, and despite a threat from Barrichello after the final pit stops, Schumacher held on to take his eighth win of the season. <a href="/wiki/Takuma_Sato" title="Takuma Sato">Takuma Sato</a> became only the second Japanese driver to achieve a podium finish.
</p><p>The race saw two serious accidents caused by <a href="/wiki/Michelin" title="Michelin">Michelin</a> tire failures in what would be a precedent for the <a href="/wiki/2005_United_States_Grand_Prix" title="2005 United States Grand Prix">2005 United States Grand Prix</a>, at which all Michelin-shod cars withdrew over safety concerns. First, <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a> crashed into the barriers alongside the end of the pit straight on lap nine. Later, <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> suffered the same fate, but in the most dangerous part of the track, causing him to hit the wall at a ninety-degree angle rearwards. The impact left Schumacher with spinal fractures and concussion, which prevented him from racing until the <a href="/wiki/2004_Chinese_Grand_Prix" title="2004 Chinese Grand Prix">2004 Chinese Grand Prix</a>, three months later.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2004_United_States_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2004_United_States_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Heading into round nine, the season had so far belonged to <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>, driving for <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, winning all but one race, in <a href="/wiki/2004_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="2004 Monaco Grand Prix">Monaco</a>, owing to a crash with <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a>, and so had 70 points out of a possible 80. However, <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a>, Schumacher's teammate at Ferrari, was only 16 points behind him, having taken six podiums and two other points finishes. In the Constructors' Championship, however, Ferrari were dominating, with 124 points: more than double that of second-placed <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1_Team" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1 Team">Renault</a>, the only other team to win a Grand Prix that year so far, with <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> triumphing in Monaco. Third-placed <a href="/wiki/British_American_Racing" title="British American Racing">BAR</a> had a quick car, but suffered reliability problems. Lead driver <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a>, like his future teammate Barrichello, picking up six podiums and two other points finishes. However, of the eight races so far, <a href="/wiki/Takuma_Sato" title="Takuma Sato">Takuma Sato</a>, in the other BAR, had suffered five engine failures, three of which had occurred in the preceding three Grands Prix in Monaco, <a href="/wiki/2004_European_Grand_Prix" title="2004 European Grand Prix">Europe</a>, and <a href="/wiki/2004_Canadian_Grand_Prix" title="2004 Canadian Grand Prix">Canada</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> and <a href="/wiki/Toyota_Racing_(Formula_One_team)" title="Toyota Racing (Formula One team)">Toyota</a> had been disqualified from the last event in Canada for illegal brake ducts, even though, in Williams's case, this proved to be a manufacturing error and had not given them any aerodynamic or cooling gain.<sup id="cite_ref-itv_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-itv-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice_and_qualifying">Practice and qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2004_United_States_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Practice and qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Alonso_US-GP_2004.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1d/Alonso_US-GP_2004.jpg/220px-Alonso_US-GP_2004.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="125" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1d/Alonso_US-GP_2004.jpg/330px-Alonso_US-GP_2004.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1d/Alonso_US-GP_2004.jpg/440px-Alonso_US-GP_2004.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2871" data-file-height="1626" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Alonso_US-GP_2004.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> during free practice.</div></div></div>
<p>Four practice sessions were held before qualifying - two 60-minute sessions on Friday and two 45-minute sessions on Saturday.<sup id="cite_ref-regs_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regs-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> All of the teams, with the exception of Ferrari, Williams, <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>, and Renault, were permitted to run three drivers on Friday.<sup id="cite_ref-regs_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regs-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Rubens Barrichello topped the timesheets in practices one, two, and three, with times of 1:11.354,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> 1:10.365,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> and 1:10.911<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> respectively. Jenson Button, driving for BAR, however, prevented Barrichello from a clean sweep in the second Saturday session: his time of 1:10.056 was 0.143 quicker than second-placed Michael Schumacher. Third was Takuma Sato, Button's teammate, whilst Barrichello languished in fourth.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> However, despite the pace of the Ferraris, their <a href="/wiki/Bridgestone" title="Bridgestone">Bridgestone</a> tires had <a href="/wiki/Graining" title="Graining">grained</a> in the long runs, meaning that balls of rubber would form on the surface of the tire, reducing mechanical grip.<sup id="cite_ref-itv_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-itv-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Mclaren_racing_usgp_2004.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/76/Mclaren_racing_usgp_2004.jpg/220px-Mclaren_racing_usgp_2004.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/76/Mclaren_racing_usgp_2004.jpg/330px-Mclaren_racing_usgp_2004.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/76/Mclaren_racing_usgp_2004.jpg/440px-Mclaren_racing_usgp_2004.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2700" data-file-height="1800" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Mclaren_racing_usgp_2004.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> mechanics push <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a>'s <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4-19" title="McLaren MP4-19">MP4-19</a> into the garage during qualifying.</div></div></div>
<p>Qualifying was split into two sections. In the first session, dubbed "pre-qualifying", each driver took turns to record one lap at a time. The order the cars ran in was the reverse order to the classified results at the <a href="/wiki/2004_Canadian_Grand_Prix" title="2004 Canadian Grand Prix">2004 Canadian Grand Prix</a>. For example, Michael Schumacher won the race, so would be the last car to run in pre-qualifying. The second qualifying session (dubbed "qualifying") repeated this process (with the drivers running in reverse order to the pre-qualifying results), but with the caveat that the drivers would not be allowed to alter their fuel loads between then and the race. The fastest time in the second session would take pole. As in 2003, setups and fuel loads could not be altered between the end of the second qualifying session and the race.<sup id="cite_ref-regs_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regs-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Rubens Barrichello started from pole from teammate Michael Schumacher after setting a time of 1:10:223. Michael Schumacher started second, making it an all-Ferrari front row. BAR locked out the second row, with Takuma Sato lining up ahead of team leader Jenson Button. Williams locked out row three, with Juan Pablo Montoya in fifth, ahead of <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a>. A gearbox issue prevented Jarno Trulli in the Renault from setting a lap, meaning he started last. <a href="/wiki/Jean_Todt" title="Jean Todt">Jean Todt</a> had told the media that Barrichello would be allowed to race Schumacher and would not be forced to concede to him, as in the <a href="/wiki/2002_Austrian_Grand_Prix" title="2002 Austrian Grand Prix">2002 Austrian Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-itv_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-itv-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2004_United_States_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As the formation lap began, Juan Pablo Montoya's Williams failed to start. Montoya jumped out of the car and ran to the pits to use the spare car; it was set up for him as he was higher up on the grid than his teammate.<sup id="cite_ref-itv_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-itv-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Montoya started the race from the pit lane.
</p><p>Rubens Barrichello led into the first corner from pole position. Michael Schumacher retained second place. <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> started in ninth place on the grid, but went around the outside of the similarly fast-starting <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> in the McLaren, and then used the inside line at turn two to pass Takuma Sato and move into third place. Jenson Button dropped from fourth to sixth, whilst Ralf Schumacher dropped to seventh. Jarno Trulli moved up to eleventh place, having started last, partially helped by a big crash in turn two. A gear selection issue for <a href="/wiki/Cristiano_da_Matta" title="Cristiano da Matta">Cristiano da Matta</a> meant that he lost momentum, and an unsighted <a href="/wiki/Christian_Klien" title="Christian Klien">Christian Klien</a> ploughed into the back of him and speared across the track, hitting <a href="/wiki/Giorgio_Pantano" title="Giorgio Pantano">Giorgio Pantano</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a>. <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> in the other Jordan attempted to avoid the chaos, and squeezed <a href="/wiki/Gianmaria_Bruni" title="Gianmaria Bruni">Gianmaria Bruni</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a> onto the kerb, terminally damaging the suspension of the Minardi. The safety car was deployed, and remained out until the end of lap five.<sup id="cite_ref-gp_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gp-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:RalfSchumacher-2004-06-20-Accident-01-KuyperHoffman.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d3/RalfSchumacher-2004-06-20-Accident-01-KuyperHoffman.JPG/220px-RalfSchumacher-2004-06-20-Accident-01-KuyperHoffman.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d3/RalfSchumacher-2004-06-20-Accident-01-KuyperHoffman.JPG/330px-RalfSchumacher-2004-06-20-Accident-01-KuyperHoffman.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d3/RalfSchumacher-2004-06-20-Accident-01-KuyperHoffman.JPG/440px-RalfSchumacher-2004-06-20-Accident-01-KuyperHoffman.JPG 2x" data-file-width="3072" data-file-height="2048" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:RalfSchumacher-2004-06-20-Accident-01-KuyperHoffman.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> is attended to by Professor <a href="/wiki/Sid_Watkins" title="Sid Watkins">Sid Watkins</a> after impacting the wall out the outside of turn thirteen on lap ten.</div></div></div>
<p>As the safety car came in, Michael Schumacher overtook Barrichello for the lead of the race. On lap nine, Fernando Alonso, in a <a href="/wiki/Michelin" title="Michelin">Michelin</a>-shod Renault, crashed out from third place at the end of the main straight after his right rear tire deflated under braking. He lost control of the car and it crashed nose-first into the barriers along the track, destroying the front end of the Renault and some polystyrene boards at turn one. Double yellow flags were deployed, meaning the drivers were not allowed to overtake in that zone. At the end of lap ten, the Williams of Ralf Schumacher, also on Michelin tires, suffered a tire failure in turn thirteen, the only banked corner at the time in Formula One, backing the car into the wall at a ninety-degree angle. The car skidded several hundred metres down the race track before coming to rest. Schumacher sat motionless in the car for two minutes, unable to talk to his team over the radio, as the radio had been smashed in the incident. The safety car was deployed, and so many people opted to make an early pit stop. Michael Schumacher pitted, as did his teammate Barrichello, but Barrichello had to queue behind Schumacher, and also struggled to get going from the stop, costing him track position to Kimi Räikkönen, who also pitted. As he left the pit lane, <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> ran over a piece of debris from Alonso's Renault. Michael Schumacher now led the race from Takuma Sato, Jenson Button, and Juan Pablo Montoya, none of whom had made a pit stop. Kimi Räikkönen was now fifth, ahead of Barrichello in sixth, Trulli in seventh place, and <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Panis" title="Olivier Panis">Olivier Panis</a> in the last points position in eighth.
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Jenson_button_usgp_2004_ontrack.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9b/Jenson_button_usgp_2004_ontrack.jpg/220px-Jenson_button_usgp_2004_ontrack.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9b/Jenson_button_usgp_2004_ontrack.jpg/330px-Jenson_button_usgp_2004_ontrack.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9b/Jenson_button_usgp_2004_ontrack.jpg/440px-Jenson_button_usgp_2004_ontrack.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1800" data-file-height="1200" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Jenson_button_usgp_2004_ontrack.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a>'s BAR retired due to a gearbox problem.</div></div></div>
<p>The safety car pulled in at the end of lap nineteen, and as the BARs had not stopped, they were pressuring Michael Schumacher's much heavier Ferrari: the top three were within two seconds of each other for a long period of time. Takuma Sato was very quick through the first and second sectors of the lap, but Michael Schumacher's Ferrari had good traction out of turn eleven, the only overtaking opportunity, and so Sato was unable to pass. On lap 24, Button was the first BAR to make a pit stop. Button's arrival in his pit box was messy, causing him to lose a couple of seconds whilst his mechanics readjusted their positions. Sato pitted one lap later, and had a clean stop, rejoining in eleventh place, just in front of Button in twelfth. Sato had good traction, however, out of turn seven on his out lap, and overtook <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Jaguar_Racing" title="Jaguar Racing">Jaguar</a> to move up to tenth. On the same lap, he overtook David Coulthard through turn thirteen, finishing off the move into turn one on lap twenty seven. Mark Webber ran wide at turn one, gifting a place to Button. However, BAR's reliability problems continued: Button retired at the end of lap twenty seven with a gearbox issue. On lap 29, Sato repeated his move on Webber on <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a>, and moved into a points position. A few corners later, he passed Nick Heidfeld and moved into seventh.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Sauber_usgp_2004.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7e/Sauber_usgp_2004.jpg/220px-Sauber_usgp_2004.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7e/Sauber_usgp_2004.jpg/330px-Sauber_usgp_2004.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7e/Sauber_usgp_2004.jpg/440px-Sauber_usgp_2004.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3072" data-file-height="2048" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Sauber_usgp_2004.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> battles <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> for eighth place.</div></div></div>
<p>Räikkönen made his second stop of the race at the end of lap 29, rejoining in eleventh place, behind Webber. On lap 31, he moved past Webber, and into tenth place, behind David Coulthard. One lap later, the McLarens swapped positions, as Räikkönen moved into ninth. However, Räikkönen had to make yet another pit stop at the end of lap thirty four due to an electronics problem on his car. The stop lasted fifteen seconds and he rejoined last. Montoya made his first pit stop at the end of lap 35, rejoining in sixth.
</p><p>Michael Schumacher now led the race by thirteen seconds from Rubens Barrichello, and continued to pull away. Jarno Trulli, now in third place, drove sharply over the kerb at turn eight and lost a piece of his car on lap 38. Nick Heidfeld pitted at the end of lap 38 and rejoined the race in eleventh. Takuma Sato, meanwhile, had caught Olivier Panis's Toyota, and overtook him on lap 40 for fourth place. Schumacher pitted at the end of lap 42 with a fifteen-second gap to his teammate, and Barrichello assumed the lead. Schumacher's 10.3-second stop fuelled him to get to the end of the race. Barrichello then pushed hard, attempting to leapfrog Schumacher. Heidfeld retired on lap 45, and Sato pitted on the same lap for his second and final pit stop. Sato rejoined in sixth place, behind Montoya. Jarno Trulli pitted on lap 46 and rejoined in fifth place, behind Olivier Panis, although that quickly changed when Panis pitted at the end of lap 47. Panis rejoined behind Sato, and Coulthard pitted on the same lap for McLaren, rejoining in ninth.
</p><p>Fisichella, on Bridgestone tires, then suffered a left rear puncture on lap 49. He took the opportunity to make his final fuel stop, rejoining near the back of the field. Barrichello had been pushing hard, and so when he finally stopped for fuel on lap 51, the gap between the two Ferraris had been cut to approximately two seconds - Barrichello had cut the gap by approximately thirteen seconds. On lap 52, Barrichello went up the inside of Schumacher in turn four, but Schumacher cut across the front of his teammate. The two cars nearly collided, and Barrichello then, contrary to Todt's earlier statements, was told to remain behind Schumacher.<sup id="cite_ref-itv_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-itv-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The battle between the two Ferraris meant that Montoya and Trulli were catching them. Sato was, in turn, catching Trulli for fourth place. David Coulthard pitted on lap 55, and, similarly to Button, had a messy arrival. Montoya pitted on lap 56, but as he left the pit lane, he was disqualified from the race due to him changing cars too late on the grid; it was his second disqualification in as many races. Mark Webber's engine expired on lap 60, and he coasted down the pit straight, pulling off at turn one. Takuma Sato went down the inside of Jarno Trulli on lap 61, and although they both ran on the grass, Sato moved up into third position. In attempting to recover from the excursion, Trulli spun. With eight laps to go, Fisichella retired with a hydraulics problem.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Baumgartner_usgp_2004.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/51/Baumgartner_usgp_2004.jpg/220px-Baumgartner_usgp_2004.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/51/Baumgartner_usgp_2004.jpg/330px-Baumgartner_usgp_2004.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/51/Baumgartner_usgp_2004.jpg/440px-Baumgartner_usgp_2004.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2781" data-file-height="1854" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Baumgartner_usgp_2004.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Zsolt_Baumgartner" title="Zsolt Baumgartner">Zsolt Baumgartner</a> became the first Hungarian driver in Formula One to score a point.</div></div></div>
<p>Michael Schumacher held off the threat of Barrichello to win the race, his eighth win of 2004. Barrichello finished second, and Takuma Sato became only the second Japanese driver to achieve a podium finish after <a href="/wiki/Aguri_Suzuki" title="Aguri Suzuki">Aguri Suzuki</a>, a feat not repeated until <a href="/wiki/Kamui_Kobayashi" title="Kamui Kobayashi">Kamui Kobayashi</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2012_Japanese_Grand_Prix" title="2012 Japanese Grand Prix">2012 Japanese Grand Prix</a>. Jarno Trulli finished fourth, Panis fifth, Räikkönen sixth, Coulthard seventh, and <a href="/wiki/Zsolt_Baumgartner" title="Zsolt Baumgartner">Zsolt Baumgartner</a> rounded out the points scorers.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2004_United_States_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Christian Klien did not claim responsibility for the crash on lap one, claiming that he had "nowhere to go".<sup id="cite_ref-GP.com_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GP.com-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello was disappointed to have conceded the lead on the safety car restart and criticised the Ferrari technicians for indecision regarding his first pit stop, which lost him position to Kimi Räikkönen.<sup id="cite_ref-GP.com_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GP.com-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Juan Pablo Montoya was frustrated to have lost points for the second race running due to a disqualification beyond his control. However, he said that the pace of the car was positive and allowed Williams to push on for the rest of the season.<sup id="cite_ref-GP.com_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GP.com-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
