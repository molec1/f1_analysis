<p>The <b>1990 United States Grand Prix</b> was the opening motor race of the <a href="/wiki/1990_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1990 Formula One World Championship">1990 Formula One World Championship</a> held on March 11, 1990, in <a href="/wiki/Phoenix,_Arizona" title="Phoenix, Arizona">Phoenix, Arizona</a>. It was the 32nd <a href="/wiki/United_States_Grand_Prix" title="United States Grand Prix">United States Grand Prix</a> since the <i>American Grand Prize</i> was first held in <a href="/wiki/1908_American_Grand_Prize" title="1908 American Grand Prize">1908</a>, and the 25th under Formula One regulations since the first United States Grand Prix was held at Sebring, Florida in <a href="/wiki/1959_United_States_Grand_Prix" title="1959 United States Grand Prix">1959</a>. It was the second to be held on the <a href="/wiki/Phoenix_street_circuit" title="Phoenix street circuit">streets of Phoenix</a> and ran over 72 laps of the 4&#160;km-circuit.
</p><p>The race was won by Brazilian <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/5B" class="mw-redirect" title="McLaren MP4/5B">McLaren MP4/5B</a> by eight seconds over French driver <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a> in his comparatively under-funded <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_018" title="Tyrrell 018">Tyrrell 018</a>. Throughout the race, the pair enthralled fans with some fabulous and daring passing for the lead.
</p><p>It was Alesi's first podium finish, with Belgian driver <a href="/wiki/Thierry_Boutsen" title="Thierry Boutsen">Thierry Boutsen</a> in a <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW13B" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams FW13B">Williams FW13B</a> coming home third.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1990_United_States_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The United States Grand Prix in the dry desert city of Phoenix was moved to the start of the season in March to avoid the intense 100+ °F heat the city experiences in summer, although the previous year's race was run in June because it was given at such short notice. Swiss driver <a href="/wiki/Gregor_Foitek" title="Gregor Foitek">Gregor Foitek</a> made his Formula One race debut for the Brabham team. He had attempted to qualify for races during the <a href="/wiki/1989_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="1989 Formula One season">1989 season</a>, failing to make the grid in eleven qualifying attempts for <a href="/wiki/EuroBrun" title="EuroBrun">EuroBrun</a> and one for <a href="/wiki/Rial_Racing" title="Rial Racing">Rial</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1990_United_States_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-qualifying_report">Pre-qualifying report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1990_United_States_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Pre-qualifying report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Several teams were required to participate in the Friday morning pre-qualifying sessions during 1990, in order to reduce the field to thirty cars for the main qualifying sessions on Friday afternoon and Saturday. At the midway point of the season, the pre-qualifying group was to be reassessed, with the more successful, points-scoring teams being allowed to avoid pre-qualifying, and unsuccessful teams being required to pre-qualify from mid-season onwards.
</p><p>Nine cars were required to take part in pre-qualifying sessions during the first half of the 1990 season, reduced from thirteen at the end of the 1989 season. The disappearance of the <a href="/wiki/Zakspeed" title="Zakspeed">Zakspeed</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rial_Racing" title="Rial Racing">Rial</a> teams reduced the numbers, as did the reduction of the <a href="/wiki/Osella" title="Osella">Osella</a> and <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Coloni" title="Scuderia Coloni">Coloni</a> teams from two cars each to one car each. In contrast, <a href="/wiki/EuroBrun" title="EuroBrun">EuroBrun</a> expanded from one car to two, and were joined by the only new team on the entry lists, <a href="/wiki/Life_Racing_Engines" title="Life Racing Engines">Life Racing Engines</a>, who ran a single car. The fastest four cars would go through to the main qualifying sessions.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Larrousse" title="Larrousse">Larrousse</a> team again fielded <a href="/wiki/Lola_Cars" title="Lola Cars">Lola</a>-<a href="/wiki/Lamborghini" title="Lamborghini">Lamborghinis</a>, driven by <a href="/wiki/%C3%89ric_Bernard" title="Éric Bernard">Éric Bernard</a> and ex-Zakspeed driver <a href="/wiki/Aguri_Suzuki" title="Aguri Suzuki">Aguri Suzuki</a>, both of whom had driven odd races for the team before. <a href="/wiki/Automobiles_Gonfaronnaises_Sportives" title="Automobiles Gonfaronnaises Sportives">AGS</a> continued with <a href="/wiki/Yannick_Dalmas" title="Yannick Dalmas">Yannick Dalmas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gabriele_Tarquini" title="Gabriele Tarquini">Gabriele Tarquini</a>, while EuroBrun hired ex-Coloni driver <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Moreno" title="Roberto Moreno">Roberto Moreno</a> and newcomer <a href="/wiki/Claudio_Langes" title="Claudio Langes">Claudio Langes</a>. Coloni brought in ex-Rial and <a href="/wiki/Onyx_Grand_Prix" title="Onyx Grand Prix">Onyx</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Bertrand_Gachot" title="Bertrand Gachot">Bertrand Gachot</a> to drive the <a href="/wiki/Coloni_C3" title="Coloni C3">C3B</a> with its new <a href="/wiki/Subaru_1235" title="Subaru 1235">Subaru</a>-badged engine, while the sole Osella FA1M was to be driven by ex-<a href="/wiki/Equipe_Ligier" title="Equipe Ligier">Ligier</a> man <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Grouillard" title="Olivier Grouillard">Olivier Grouillard</a>. The new team, Life, hired another Grand Prix newcomer, <a href="/wiki/Gary_Brabham" title="Gary Brabham">Gary Brabham</a> to drive their L190 car with its unusual <a href="/wiki/W12_engine" title="W12 engine">W12 engine</a>. The chassis had initially been built for the previous season by the <a href="/wiki/First_Racing" title="First Racing">First Racing</a> team, which ultimately did not take part in Formula One.<sup id="cite_ref-Walker_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Walker-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During the pre-qualifying session on Friday morning, Moreno was fastest by four tenths of a second in his <a href="/wiki/EuroBrun_ER189" title="EuroBrun ER189">EuroBrun ER189B</a>, already a great improvement over their form during 1989, despite the car being overweight. The Larrousse-Lola <a href="/wiki/Lola_LC89" title="Lola LC89">LC89Bs</a> of Bernard and Suzuki were second and fourth, this marking the first pre-qualification for Suzuki after failing at every event for Zakspeed last season. Sandwiched between the Lolas in third was Grouillard in the Osella.<sup id="cite_ref-Walker_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Walker-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The unsuccessful runners included Tarquini and Dalmas in the two <a href="/wiki/AGS_JH24" title="AGS JH24">AGS JH24</a> cars, two seconds behind Suzuki, and debutant Langes in the second EuroBrun, a couple of seconds adrift of Dalmas. The other two cars struggled, as Brabham in the Life suffered electrical failure after four laps, the team having been unable to sufficiently test their new car and engine; while Gachot's Coloni broke a gear selector rod on its first lap.<sup id="cite_ref-Walker_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Walker-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying_report">Qualifying report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1990_United_States_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Qualifying report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Unexpected rain on Saturday meant that the grid was decided entirely by times from Friday's session. <a href="/wiki/Pirelli" title="Pirelli">Pirelli</a>'s soft qualifying tires caught <a href="/wiki/Goodyear_Tire_and_Rubber_Company" title="Goodyear Tire and Rubber Company">Goodyear</a> off guard, and the Italian manufacturer put five of its teams in the top ten positions, including Jean Alesi, who was fourth in a Tyrrell. In a surprise move, team manager <a href="/wiki/Ken_Tyrrell" title="Ken Tyrrell">Ken Tyrrell</a> had signed with Pirelli two days before the race, after 18 years with Goodyear. <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a>'s pole-winning McLaren was the only car in the first two rows sporting Goodyears. <a href="/wiki/Pierluigi_Martini" title="Pierluigi Martini">Pierluigi Martini</a> put <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a> on the front row for the only time in the team's history, less than seven-hundredths off Berger's pole time. In addition, <a href="/wiki/Andrea_de_Cesaris" title="Andrea de Cesaris">Andrea de Cesaris</a> (<a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Italia" class="mw-redirect" title="Scuderia Italia">Scuderia Italia</a>), <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Grouillard" title="Olivier Grouillard">Olivier Grouillard</a> (<a href="/wiki/Osella" title="Osella">Osella</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Moreno" title="Roberto Moreno">Roberto Moreno</a> (<a href="/wiki/EuroBrun" title="EuroBrun">EuroBrun</a>) all scored what proved to be their team's best grid position of the year. <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> could only manage fifth, while <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a>, in his first race for <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, was seventh. <a href="/wiki/Philippe_Alliot" title="Philippe Alliot">Philippe Alliot</a> was excluded when a mechanic worked on the car outside of the pit area during Friday's practice.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1990_United_States_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_report">Race report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1990_United_States_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Race report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Martin_Donnelly_(racing_driver)" title="Martin Donnelly (racing driver)">Martin Donnelly</a> did not take the dummy grid due to gearbox failure. (Some sources consider this a DNS.)
</p><p>Race day was cool with a chance of rain.  Berger quickly pulled ahead of Martini and moved over in front of him, but Alesi, whose F1 career had begun in his home country of <a href="/wiki/1989_French_Grand_Prix" title="1989 French Grand Prix">France</a> the previous summer, made an even quicker start, passed them both and outbraked Berger into the first corner. He immediately began pulling away, and led by 2.4 seconds after one lap.
</p><p>Senna passed de Cesaris for third, and closed on Berger, who was giving up a half second a lap to Alesi. The Brazilian got by his team-mate when, on lap 9, Berger hit a bump under braking, backed into the tire wall and damaged his rear wing. He pitted for a new wing, and later recorded the fastest lap of the race, but eventually retired with a clutch problem.
</p><p>When Berger spun, Senna was 8.2 seconds behind Alesi. Not knowing whether the Tyrrell's Pirelli tires would last, Senna was reluctant to push too hard early on. After 30 laps, Alesi remained in the lead, but on lap 34, Senna passed him on the inside.  The Tyrrell held the outside line on the exit and retook the lead on the next turn.  At the same point, one lap later, Senna took the lead again, and this time he held it.
</p><p>After several attempts to regain the lead, Alesi decided to conserve his tires, and let Senna go. Meanwhile, <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a> had progressed from sixth on the grid to third by lap 17. Brake trouble caused him to flatspot his tyres, however, and on lap 28 he pitted for new ones. This allowed <a href="/wiki/Thierry_Boutsen" title="Thierry Boutsen">Thierry Boutsen</a>'s <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a> through, and though he battled a problem with his engine intermittently cutting out, he maintained third place to the flag. Piquet had to settle for fourth in his first drive for <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a>.
</p><p>Prost had fallen back to ninth at the start with gearbox problems, and, while he advanced to fourth place by lap 17, his gearbox troubles proved terminal on lap 21. In the other Ferrari, <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> retired from fifth on lap 49, when his clutch disintegrated and pierced the oil tank. The engine seized and caught fire, sending Mansell into a spin.
</p><p>Senna built his lead over Alesi to 28.5 seconds, but backed off slightly when his engine began sounding less than healthy. <a href="/wiki/Stefano_Modena" title="Stefano Modena">Stefano Modena</a> and <a href="/wiki/Satoru_Nakajima" title="Satoru Nakajima">Satoru Nakajima</a>, in a Brabham and the second Tyrrell, respectively, finished the scoring in fifth and sixth places. It was the 21st win of <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a>'s career and the first of six for the season. Alesi's podium finish was the first of his career.
</p><p>The first two finishers were full of praise for one another after the race. Senna said he knew Alesi had the ingredients to be World Champion, and Alesi gushed, "He is my hero and has been for many years." Other winners in the race were Ken Tyrrell, with two cars in the points; and Pirelli, with three points finishers.
</p>
