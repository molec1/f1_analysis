<p>The <b>2011 Bahrain Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>2011 Formula 1 Gulf Air Bahrain Grand Prix</b>) was scheduled to be the opening round of the <a href="/wiki/2011_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2011 Formula One season">2011 Formula One season</a>. Planned to be held on 13 March 2011 at the <a href="/wiki/Bahrain_International_Circuit" title="Bahrain International Circuit">Bahrain International Circuit</a> in <a href="/wiki/Sakhir" title="Sakhir">Sakhir</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bahrain" title="Bahrain">Bahrain</a>, it was postponed on 21 February 2011 due to <a href="/wiki/Bahraini_protests_of_2011%E2%80%9313" class="mw-redirect" title="Bahraini protests of 2011â€“13">civil unrest</a>. On 3 June, it was announced that the race would be held on 30 October, thus making it round 17 of the 20 to be held during 2011. Following controversy over the reinstatement of the race, organisers of the Grand Prix abandoned their bid to host a race in 2011.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Postponement">Postponement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Bahrain_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Postponement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On 14 February 2011, civil unrest broke out in Bahrain as part of <a href="/wiki/Arab_Spring" title="Arab Spring">a series of protests</a> across North Africa and the Middle East.<sup id="cite_ref-BBC12490286_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC12490286-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> As a result of the unrest, medical staff due to attend the practice session in the Bahrain <a href="/wiki/GP2_Asia_Series" title="GP2 Asia Series">GP2 Asia Series</a> race were redeployed to hospitals in <a href="/wiki/Manama" title="Manama">Manama</a>, forcing the cancellation of the Thursday practice session.<sup id="cite_ref-BBC9400392_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC9400392-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Later that day, it was announced that the whole weekend would be cancelled at the request of the local motoring federation.<sup id="cite_ref-autosport89525_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosport89525-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Chief executive of the Bahrain International Circuit Prince <a href="/wiki/Salman_ibn_Hamad_ibn_Isa_Al_Khalifa" class="mw-redirect" title="Salman ibn Hamad ibn Isa Al Khalifa">Salman ibn Hamad ibn Isa Al Khalifa</a>, who is also the <a href="/wiki/Crown_prince" title="Crown prince">crown prince</a> of Bahrain stated that everything possible would be done to ensure that the race went ahead.  <a href="/wiki/Bernie_Ecclestone" title="Bernie Ecclestone">Bernie Ecclestone</a>, CEO of Formula One Management and Formula One Administration, stated that he hoped that talks with Al Khalifa would ease his fears that the event might be cancelled. Vice President of the <a href="/wiki/Bahrain_Centre_for_Human_Rights" title="Bahrain Centre for Human Rights">Bahrain Centre for Human Rights</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nabeel_Rajab" title="Nabeel Rajab">Nabeel Rajab</a> said that it would be hard to bring a quick end to the protests.<sup id="cite_ref-BBC9399251_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC9399251-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Ecclestone stated that a decision on the race would be made by 23 February, saying that if the situation had not calmed down by then, the event would probably be cancelled.<sup id="cite_ref-Metro190211_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Metro190211-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> However, protesters were quoted stating that "the only reason" the Crown Prince was willing to talk with the protestors was for the sake of holding the race.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 21 February 2011, it was announced that the race had been postponed due to <a href="/wiki/2011_Bahraini_protests" class="mw-redirect" title="2011 Bahraini protests">the ongoing protests</a> and the championships rescheduled to begin in <a href="/wiki/2011_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="2011 Australian Grand Prix">Australia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The organisers were later given until 1 May to decide whether they want to proceed with the race at a later date.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The Bahrain race officials released a statement one day later that they were hopeful of rescheduling the Grand Prix.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Provisional_reinstatement_and_cancellation">Provisional reinstatement and cancellation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Bahrain_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Provisional reinstatement and cancellation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Bernie_Ecclestone.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/26/Bernie_Ecclestone.jpg/220px-Bernie_Ecclestone.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="255" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/26/Bernie_Ecclestone.jpg/330px-Bernie_Ecclestone.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/26/Bernie_Ecclestone.jpg/440px-Bernie_Ecclestone.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1449" data-file-height="1682" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Bernie_Ecclestone.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Bernie Ecclestone at the <a href="/wiki/2006_Bahrain_Grand_Prix" title="2006 Bahrain Grand Prix">2006 Bahrain Grand Prix</a></div></div></div>
<p>On 2 May 2011 <a href="/wiki/Bernie_Ecclestone" title="Bernie Ecclestone">Bernie Ecclestone</a> extended the deadline to 3 June.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Autosport later reported that the FIA was exploring the possibility of moving the <a href="/wiki/2011_Indian_Grand_Prix" title="2011 Indian Grand Prix">Indian Grand Prix</a> to the final race of the season with a provisional date of 4 December, and holding the Bahrain Grand Prix on the Indian Grand Prix's original date of 30 October.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At a meeting of the <a href="/wiki/World_Motor_Sport_Council" class="mw-redirect" title="World Motor Sport Council">World Motor Sport Council</a> on 3 June, FIA members unanimously voted to reinstate the <a href="/wiki/Bahrain_Grand_Prix" title="Bahrain Grand Prix">Bahrain Grand Prix</a> to the calendar on the planned date of 30 October.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The decision was controversial, with <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> team principal <a href="/wiki/Ross_Brawn" title="Ross Brawn">Ross Brawn</a> stating that a December finale was unacceptable,<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> while <a href="/wiki/Human_rights" title="Human rights">human rights</a> interest groups and activists criticised the FIA for the reinstatement in light of the ongoing political upheaval in the country.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> expressed also his concerns over the human rights conditions and stated that he would have hoped for the sport to have taken a firmer stance on the affair.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Several other drivers expressed a willingness to race on the condition that their safety could be guaranteed amid reports that widespread protests were being planned for the day of the race.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> In response to this, FIA president <a href="/wiki/Jean_Todt" title="Jean Todt">Jean Todt</a> promised that the sport's governing body would monitor the situation in Bahrain carefully, leaving open the possibility of a cancellation should the country deteriorate ahead of the race,<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> while commercial rights holder Bernie Ecclestone called for a second vote to take place, restoring the Indian Grand Prix to its original October date and moving the Bahrain Grand Prix back to the season finale in December.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> According to former <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="FÃ©dÃ©ration Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">FIA</a> president <a href="/wiki/Max_Mosley" title="Max Mosley">Max Mosley</a>, the rescheduling of the race would require the unanimous agreement of the teams.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> It had been reported that the <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_Teams_Association" title="Formula One Teams Association">Formula One Teams Association</a> (FOTA) was opposed to rescheduling the race to 30 October on logistical grounds, but were willing to discuss an end-of-season berth for the race instead.<sup id="cite_ref-Autosport92061_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Autosport92061-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> On 8 June, Ecclestone stated that he felt the race would not go ahead because the FIA had overlooked Rule 66 of the Sporting Code, which states that "no amendments can be made to the arrangements for a championship after entries open without the agreement of all competitors."<sup id="cite_ref-BBC13694628_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC13694628-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> The FIA later asked Ecclestone to submit a new calendar proposal<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> after they were informed by FOTA that holding the Bahrain Grand Prix on 30 October was "impractical".<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>One week after the WMSC voted to return the race to the calendar, organisers for the Bahrain Grand Prix officially abandoned their bid to return to the calendar.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
