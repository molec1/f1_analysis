<p>The <b>2000 Hungarian Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>XVI Marlboro Magyar Nagydíj</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 13 August 2000 at the <a href="/wiki/Hungaroring" title="Hungaroring">Hungaroring</a>, near <a href="/wiki/Budapest" title="Budapest">Budapest</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hungary" title="Hungary">Hungary</a>. It was the twelfth race of the <a href="/wiki/2000_FIA_Formula_One_World_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="2000 FIA Formula One World Championship">2000 FIA Formula One World Championship</a> and the 18th <a href="/wiki/Hungarian_Grand_Prix" title="Hungarian Grand Prix">Hungarian Grand Prix</a>. The 77-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/15" title="McLaren MP4/15">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Mercedes_AMG_High_Performance_Powertrains" title="Mercedes AMG High Performance Powertrains">Mercedes</a>, after starting from third position. <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> finished second in a <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_F1-2000" title="Ferrari F1-2000">Ferrari</a>, having started from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>, with Häkkinen's teammate <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> third.
</p><p>Häkkinen beat Schumacher and Coulthard off the start line and went on to lead every lap, except for the first round of pit stops. The Finn's eventual margin of victory was eight seconds, with Schumacher holding off Coulthard for second. The win, Häkkinen's third of the season, gave him the lead of the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a> for the first time in 2000, two points ahead of Schumacher and six ahead of Coulthard, while McLaren took the lead of the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a> by one point from Ferrari.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2000_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Formula_One_tyre" class="mw-redirect" title="Formula One tyre">Tyre</a> supplier <a href="/wiki/Bridgestone" title="Bridgestone">Bridgestone</a> brought two different tyre compounds to the race; the Soft and the Extra Soft dry compound tyres.<sup id="cite_ref-preview_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-preview-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Going into the race, Ferrari driver <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> led the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a> with 56 <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Championship_points_scoring_systems" title="List of Formula One World Championship points scoring systems">points</a>, ahead of McLaren teammates <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> and <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a>, who were tied for second on 54 points. <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> was fourth with 46 points while <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> was fifth on 18 points.<sup id="cite_ref-pointsprerace_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pointsprerace-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a>, Ferrari were leading with 102 points, four points ahead of their rivals McLaren in second. Williams on 22 points and Benetton with 18 points contended for third place, while BAR were fifth on 12 points.<sup id="cite_ref-pointsprerace_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pointsprerace-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> McLaren and Ferrari had so far dominated the championship, winning the previous eleven races. Championship participants Fisichella and Barrichello had each gained second place podium finishes while <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> and <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a> had each achieved third place podium finishes.<sup id="cite_ref-pointsprerace_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pointsprerace-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Following the <a href="/wiki/2000_German_Grand_Prix" title="2000 German Grand Prix">German Grand Prix</a> on 30 July, six teams conducted mid-season testing at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_Ricardo_Tormo" title="Circuit Ricardo Tormo">Circuit Ricardo Tormo</a> from 3–5 August. McLaren test driver <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Panis" title="Olivier Panis">Olivier Panis</a> was fastest on the first day, ahead of Häkkinen. <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Diniz" title="Pedro Diniz">Pedro Diniz</a>'s Sauber car was afflicted with an oil leak, limiting his team's testing time as the leak was repaired.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard was fastest on the second day. Fisichella set the fastest times on the final day of testing. His teammate <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Wurz" title="Alexander Wurz">Alexander Wurz</a> spun off and collided with the tyre barrier. His car's wishbone struck his right leg and was taken to the circuit's medical center before a transfer to a local hospital.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Wurz was passed fit to compete in the race the day after his accident.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Ferrari opted to spend five days testing at the <a href="/wiki/Fiorano_Circuit" title="Fiorano Circuit">Fiorano Circuit</a> and concentrated on car development, practice starts, aerodynamic testing and race distance simulations with their test driver <a href="/wiki/Luca_Badoer" title="Luca Badoer">Luca Badoer</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-test1_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-test1-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> He was joined by Barrichello on the second day and Michael Schumacher from the fourth day onwards.<sup id="cite_ref-test1_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-test1-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Badoer and Michael Schumacher spent two further days at the circuit performing shakedowns of the <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_F1-2000" title="Ferrari F1-2000">Ferrari F1-2000</a> car.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After consecutive retirements in the previous three races which included first lap collisions in <a href="/wiki/2000_Austrian_Grand_Prix" title="2000 Austrian Grand Prix">Austria</a> and Germany, Michael Schumacher said that his objective in Hungary was to avoid any incidents on the first lap and to finish in a points-scoring position. He was also confident that Ferrari would perform well at the circuit.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello revealed that he received backing from Ferrari president <a href="/wiki/Luca_Cordero_di_Montezemolo" title="Luca Cordero di Montezemolo">Luca di Montezemolo</a> to challenge for more victories and the championship despite the latter's comments to the press about Barrichello assisting Michael Schumacher's title aspirations.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Prost's <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a> was passed fit in the days leading up to the race.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> At the previous race, Alesi had suffered a serious crash which involved a collision with Sauber driver <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Diniz" title="Pedro Diniz">Pedro Diniz</a>, although he escaped uninjured apart from abdominal pains and suffered from dizziness and vomiting.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Prost had their test driver <a href="/wiki/St%C3%A9phane_Sarrazin" title="Stéphane Sarrazin">Stéphane Sarrazin</a> ready to replace Alesi should the latter had suffered a relapse.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Alesi said he felt ready to race again: "It took a few days before I really started to recover, but now I sleep and feel much better".<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Some teams made technical changes to their cars for the Grand Prix. McLaren introduced a revised aerodynamic for their <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/15" title="McLaren MP4/15">MP4/15</a> chassis, aimed at increasing the amount of <a href="/wiki/Downforce" title="Downforce">downforce</a>, and therefore <a href="/wiki/Grip_(auto_racing)" title="Grip (auto racing)">grip</a>, produced by the bodywork. They also brought revised nose wings.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> BAR fitted their cars with one-off components that were produced to help optimise the performance of the monocoque's cooling systems.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Ferrari introduced an aerodynamic set-up similar to that used at the <a href="/wiki/2000_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="2000 Monaco Grand Prix">Monaco Grand Prix</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-fia4aug2000_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fia4aug2000-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> and the team debuted a modified version of the <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_F1-2000" title="Ferrari F1-2000">F1-2000</a>'s chimneys.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Minardi arrived with new radiator intakes and exits to rectify temperature issues with their <a href="/wiki/Fondmetal" title="Fondmetal">Fondmetal</a> V10 engines.<sup id="cite_ref-fia4aug2000_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fia4aug2000-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice">Practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2000_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Michael_Schumacher-I%27m_the_man_(cropped).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/Michael_Schumacher-I%27m_the_man_%28cropped%29.jpg/170px-Michael_Schumacher-I%27m_the_man_%28cropped%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="230" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/Michael_Schumacher-I%27m_the_man_%28cropped%29.jpg/255px-Michael_Schumacher-I%27m_the_man_%28cropped%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/Michael_Schumacher-I%27m_the_man_%28cropped%29.jpg/340px-Michael_Schumacher-I%27m_the_man_%28cropped%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1260" data-file-height="1704" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Michael_Schumacher-I%27m_the_man_(cropped).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> took his fourth pole position of the 2000 season.</div></div></div>
<p>Four practice sessions were held before the Sunday race—two on Friday, and two on Saturday. The Friday morning and afternoon sessions each lasted an hour. The third and final practice sessions were held on Saturday morning and lasted 45 minutes.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> The Friday practice sessions took place in dry conditions. Michael Schumacher set the first session's fastest time, at 1 minute and 20.198 seconds, almost six-tenths of a second faster than teammate Barrichello. Jaguar's <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a> was third fastest, ahead of Ralf Schumacher, Fisichella and BAR driver <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a>. <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a>, Diniz, <a href="/wiki/Mika_Salo" title="Mika Salo">Mika Salo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> completed the top ten.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> In the second practice session, Coulthard set the quickest lap of the day, a 1:18.792; Häkkinen finished with the second fastest time. The two Ferrari drivers were third and fourth—Michael Schumacher ahead of Barrichello. Trulli was running quicker finishing fifth fastest, ahead of Fisichella and Williams drivers Ralf Schumacher and Button. Frentzen and Irvine followed in the top ten.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> The weather remained dry for the Saturday practice sessions. Barrichello was fastest in the third practice session, with a time of 1:18.268. Coulthard was second fastest and was one thousands of a second off Barrichello's pace. Frentzen continued his strong practice form, setting the third fastest time, ahead of Michael Schumacher, Häkkinen and Ralf Schumacher. Sauber driver <a href="/wiki/Mika_Salo" title="Mika Salo">Mika Salo</a>, Button, Trulli and Fisichella took the final top ten places.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> In the final practice session, Michael Schumacher was fastest setting a time of 1:17.395, ahead of Coulthard and Barrichello. Frentzen set the fourth fastest time, narrowly faster than Ralf Schumacher and Häkkinen. Trulli, Fisichella, Salo and Diniz completed the top ten ahead of qualifying.<sup id="cite_ref-fiabulletin_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiabulletin-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2000_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatleft" style="width:33%;&#32;;"><style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r887775652">.mw-parser-output .quotebox{background-color:#F9F9F9;border:1px solid #aaa;box-sizing:border-box;padding:10px;font-size:88%;max-width:100%}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft{margin:0.5em 1.4em 0.8em 0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright{margin:0.5em 0 0.8em 1.4em}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.centered{margin:0.5em auto 0.8em auto}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft p,.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright p{font-style:inherit}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-title{background-color:#F9F9F9;text-align:center;font-size:larger;font-weight:bold}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:before{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" “ ";vertical-align:-45%;line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:after{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" ” ";line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .left-aligned{text-align:left}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .right-aligned{text-align:right}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .center-aligned{text-align:center}.mw-parser-output .quotebox cite{display:block;font-style:normal}@media screen and (max-width:360px){.mw-parser-output .quotebox{min-width:100%;margin:0 0 0.8em!important;float:none!important}}</style>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">"I am delighted to be on pole. We came here confident and I hope we will leave here tomorrow with the same confidence. My first target is to finish and then we are in a good position to win. As for the start, I think we have improved after practising last week. But you also need a bit of luck with so much horsepower and a small clutch. Getting it right is a fine edge. This is a demanding circuit to drive with no rest for the driver with the variety of corners and some bumps. This makes it difficult to find the best set up for the race but I am happy with my car in race trim. Qualifying is one thing, but the race is another and I want to get a good result. The real answer will come tomorrow."</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style="">Michael Schumacher, commenting on taking pole position.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>Saturday's afternoon one hour qualifying session saw each driver was limited to twelve laps, with the starting order decided by their fastest laps. During this session, the <a href="/wiki/107%25_rule" title="107% rule">107% rule</a> was in effect, which necessitated each driver set a time within 107 per cent of the quickest lap to qualify for the race.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> The session was held in dry weather.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher clinched his 28th <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> of his career, and his fourth at the circuit, with a time of 1:17.514. He was joined on the front row of the <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_motorsport_terms#G" title="Glossary of motorsport terms">grid</a> by Coulthard, who was three-tenths of a second off Michael Schumacher's pace. Häkkinen qualified third, and stated that his team made changes to his car's set-up having been unhappy with his car in previous sessions.<sup id="cite_ref-fiabulletin_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiabulletin-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Ralf Schumacher qualified fourth, and said he was pleased with revisions to his car's aerodynamics.<sup id="cite_ref-fiabulletin_23-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiabulletin-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello struggled with the handling of his car taking fifth position and stated that Coulthard prevented him from setting a faster lap time. Frentzen, Fisichella, Button, Salo and Irvine rounded out the top ten positions.<sup id="cite_ref-fiabulletin_23-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiabulletin-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Wurz missed out on qualifying in the top ten by two-tenths of a second. Trulli qualified 12th having struggled with oversteer, ahead of Diniz, Alesi, Arrows driver <a href="/wiki/Pedro_de_la_Rosa" title="Pedro de la Rosa">Pedro de la Rosa</a> and Villeneuve. <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Herbert" title="Johnny Herbert">Johnny Herbert</a> for Jaguar qualified 17th despite spinning late in the session, and was followed by Zonta, <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jos_Verstappen" title="Jos Verstappen">Jos Verstappen</a>. The Minardi drivers qualified last; <a href="/wiki/Marc_Gen%C3%A9" title="Marc Gené">Marc Gené</a> outqualified his teammate <a href="/wiki/Gast%C3%B3n_Mazzacane" title="Gastón Mazzacane">Gastón Mazzacane</a> by two-tenths of a second.<sup id="cite_ref-motorsport_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-motorsport-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Warm-up">Warm-up</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2000_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Warm-up">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The drivers took to the track at 09:30 <a href="/wiki/Central_European_Summer_Time" title="Central European Summer Time">Central European Summer Time</a> (<a href="/wiki/Coordinated_Universal_Time" title="Coordinated Universal Time">UTC</a>+2) for a 30-minute warm-up session.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_19-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard maintained his good performance from qualifying and set the fastest time, a 1:19.261. The Ferrari cars were second and third—Michael Schumacher faster than Barrichello. Häkkinen completed the top four, 1.2 seconds behind teammate Coulthard.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2000_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The race started at 14:00 local time.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_19-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> The conditions on the grid were dry before the race; the air temperature was 32&#160;°C (90&#160;°F) and the track temperature ranged between 34 to 44&#160;°C (93 to 111&#160;°F);<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> conditions were expected to remain consistent throughout the race.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Whilst the grid was forming up, Mazzacane's car was afflicted with a gearbox problem and he was forced to start the race with his <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_motorsport_terms#S" title="Glossary of motorsport terms">spare car</a>. Herbert also planned to use his spare car as his regular car developed a leak which was fixed before the start. Häkkinen accelerated faster than Michael Schumacher and Coulthard off the line, getting ahead of both drivers going into the first corner. Coulthard then withstood Ralf Schumacher's attempts to pass him for third position. Heading into the <a href="/wiki/Chicane" title="Chicane">chicane</a>, Villeneuve and de la Rosa collided.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Villeneuve pitted for a new front wing followed by Verstappen who pitted for a new left rear tyre. At the end of the first lap, Häkkinen led from Michael Schumacher, Coulthard, Ralf Schumacher, Barrichello, Frentzen, Fisichella, Irvine, Wurz, Salo, Diniz, Trulli, Alesi, Herbert, Zonta, Heidfeld, Verstappen, Gené, Mazzacane, Villeneuve and de la Rosa.<sup id="cite_ref-lapbylap_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapbylap-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Häkkinen began to gradually pull away from Michael Schumacher as the former set consecutive fastest laps.<sup id="cite_ref-lapbylap_33-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapbylap-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Fisichella spun off from seventh place on lap eight, losing eight positions and Irvine moved into Fisichella's former position. Alesi pitted on lap ninth and re-emerged at the rear of the field after repairs to his car's steering. Villeneuve passed de la Rosa for 21st position. Two laps later, Alesi drove to his garage and became the first retirement of the race. Fisichella ran wide on lap 12 and was passed by Herbert for 13th. Three laps later, Fisichella made his first pit stop for repairs to his car's brakes and came out in 19th position. Häkkinen's lead over Michael Schumacher was seven seconds by lap 19.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_32-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard was a further three seconds behind the Michael Schumacher and was drawing ahead from Ralf Schumacher.<sup id="cite_ref-lapbylap_33-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapbylap-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Villeneuve moved into 18th position after passing Fisichella and Zonta by lap 20. Heidfeld became the race's second retirement when he stalled after making the first scheduled pit stop on lap 22. Two laps later, Irvine, who had been running seventh, made his first pit stop and dropped to 11th.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_32-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Mika_Haekkinen_2006.jpg" class="image"><img alt="Head and shoulders of a man in his late thirties with blonde hair and grey eyes, facing to the right. He is wearing a black polo neck sweater." src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/53/Mika_Haekkinen_2006.jpg/170px-Mika_Haekkinen_2006.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="230" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/53/Mika_Haekkinen_2006.jpg/255px-Mika_Haekkinen_2006.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/53/Mika_Haekkinen_2006.jpg/340px-Mika_Haekkinen_2006.jpg 2x" data-file-width="549" data-file-height="743" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Mika_Haekkinen_2006.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Mika Häkkinen won the race from third position after passing Michael Schumacher and McLaren teammate <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>Ralf Schumacher took his first pit stop on lap 28, emerging in seventh position. Barrichello pitted one lap later. Race leader Häkkinen took his pit stop on lap 31 and came out behind teammate Coulthard. Häkkinen regained the lead after Coulthard made his pit stop on lap 32, who came out in third position. Fisichella retired with further brake problems on lap 32. Häkkinen set the fastest lap of the race, a 1:20.028 on lap 33, as he continued to stretch his lead over Michael Schumacher. Coulthard, who was on fresh tyres, gradually began to close the gap to Michael Schumacher by lap 39. Michael Schumacher increased the gap when Coulthard lost two seconds; the result of being held up by Genè who was later issued a 10-second stop-go penalty. Barrichello pitted for the second time on lap 47. Michael Schumacher and Ralf Schumacher made their pit stops on lap 50, one lap ahead of Coulthard.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_32-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Häkkinen pitted on lap 53 and remained in the lead, having built a 20-second lead over Michael Schumacher.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_32-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-lapbylap_33-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapbylap-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Frentzen became the last driver to make a scheduled pit stop on lap 56.<sup id="cite_ref-lapbylap_33-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapbylap-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> At the end of lap 57, with the scheduled pit stops completed, the running order was Häkkinen, Michael Schumacher, Coulthard, Barrichello, Ralf Schumacher, Frentzen, Button, Trulli, Diniz, Irvine, Salo, Wurz, Herbert, Villeneuve, Verstappen, Zonta, Gené, Mazzacane and de la Rosa.<sup id="cite_ref-lapbylap_33-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapbylap-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Herbert spun while battling for 13th position with Villeneuve.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_32-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Diniz retired from the race when his engine failed on lap 63.<sup id="cite_ref-lapbylap_33-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapbylap-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Herbert came under pressure from Verstappen on lap 67 and suffered his second spin, losing the position to Verstappen.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_32-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Herbert retired on lap 69 as the result of gearbox problems. Mazzacane pulled off the track on lap 70 and retired because of an engine failure.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Trulli managed to close the gap to Button and passed him for seventh on lap 74, while Button lost another position to Irvine one lap later.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_32-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Häkkinen crossed the finish line on lap 77 to win his third victory of the season in a time of 1'45:33.869, at an average speed of 108.097 miles per hour (173.965&#160;km/h). Michael Schumacher finished second 7.9 seconds behind, ahead of Coulthard in third, Barrichello in fourth, Ralf Schumacher in fifth with Frentzen rounding out the points scoring positions in sixth. Trulli, Irvine, Button, Salo and Wurz filled the next five positions albeit one lap behind the race winner. Villeneuve, Verstappen, Zonta, Gené and de la Rosa were the last of the classified finishers.<sup id="cite_ref-results_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-results-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2000_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatright" style="width:33%;&#32;;"><link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r887775652"/>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">"I knew that the start would be crucial, because it's almost impossible to overtake on this circuit. Normally the perfect start doesn't happen more than once a year and after my get away two weeks ago at Hockenheim I thought that my quota had been used up. Once I took the lead I was comfortable throughout the race and the entire team has done a fantastic job the whole weekend to optimise the car. I'm extremely happy to win and also to take the lead in the World Championship."</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style="">Mika Häkkinen, speaking after the race.<sup id="cite_ref-racequotes_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-racequotes-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>The top three drivers appeared on the podium to collect their trophies and in the subsequent <a href="/wiki/News_conference" class="mw-redirect" title="News conference">press conference</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_19-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Häkkinen stated that his good start was instigated by modifications made to his car's engine.<sup id="cite_ref-gpafterace_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpafterace-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> His win was praised by the Vice President of <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_motorsport" title="Mercedes-Benz in motorsport">Mercedes-Benz Motorsport</a> <a href="/wiki/Norbert_Haug" title="Norbert Haug">Norbert Haug</a>. "Mika had a great race," he said. "His victory may have looked easy, but it was tough to achieve and in my view this was one of his best drives ever."<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher said that although he was unable to catch Häkkinen, he was happy to finish in second position.<sup id="cite_ref-gpafterace_37-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpafterace-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard said that he believed that his car's suffered from balance issues before taking his first pit stop which accounted for his lack of pace. He also added that spending time behind back-markers during the second stint hindered his attempts to overtake Michael Schumacher but admitted that third position was his best possible result.<sup id="cite_ref-gpafterace_37-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpafterace-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After Ferrari's victory at the previous race, their team's technical director <a href="/wiki/Ross_Brawn" title="Ross Brawn">Ross Brawn</a> said that "Our pitstops and our race strategy went well, but we just weren't quick enough.",<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> while di Montezemolo urged the mechanics and engineers of Ferrari to concentrate on rectifying the issue of wheel-spin and also praised Häkkinen for his recent trend of good starts.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello said that he blamed his poor qualifying performance for his fourth-place finish.<sup id="cite_ref-gpafterace_37-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpafterace-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Ralf Schumacher and Frentzen were pleased to finish in fifth and sixth places respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-gpafterace_37-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpafterace-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Fisichella, who retired from the race from a brake problem, said that the reoccurring problem caused damage to his car and forced his later retirement.<sup id="cite_ref-gpafterace_37-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpafterace-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Gené placed blame upon faulty radio communication to his team as the reason for his stop-go penalty and said that he did not receive the <a href="/wiki/Racing_flags#Blue_flag" title="Racing flags">blue flag</a> until the last moment.<sup id="cite_ref-gpafterace_37-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpafterace-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Jaguar's technical director <a href="/wiki/Gary_Anderson_(motorsport)" title="Gary Anderson (motorsport)">Gary Anderson</a> was angry with Gené after the race as he believed the Spaniard's driving cost Irvine the chance to take a points-scoring position.<sup id="cite_ref-andersonangry_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-andersonangry-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> "I don't understand why the blue flags weren't waved because it was plain for all to see." said Anderson.<sup id="cite_ref-andersonangry_41-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-andersonangry-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As a consequence of the race, Häkkinen moved into the lead of the Drivers' Championship, on 64 points, taking the championship lead for the first time in the 2000 season. Michael Schumacher lost the lead of the Drivers' Championship, falling two points behind Häkkinen. Coulthard maintained third place with 58 points, nine points ahead of Barrichello and forty-one in front of Fisichella.<sup id="cite_ref-pointsprerace_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pointsprerace-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> In the Constructors' Championship, McLaren took over the lead of the Constructors' Championship with 112 points, pushing Ferrari on 111 points to second place. Williams increased the gap to Benetton to 6 points, with Jordan jumping ahead of BAR for fifth on 12 points.<sup id="cite_ref-pointsprerace_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pointsprerace-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Despite McLaren taking the lead of both championships, their team principal <a href="/wiki/Ron_Dennis" title="Ron Dennis">Ron Dennis</a> acknowledged that he expected both his drivers would have the advantage in the most of the five remaining races although he believed that being complacent would reduce McLaren's chances of success.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
