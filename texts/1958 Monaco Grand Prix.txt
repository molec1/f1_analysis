<p>The  <b>1958 Monaco Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 18 May 1958 at <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Monaco" title="Circuit de Monaco">Monaco</a>. It was race 2 of 11 in the <a href="/wiki/1958_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1958 World Championship of Drivers">1958 World Championship of Drivers</a> and race 2 of 10 in the <a href="/wiki/1958_International_Cup_for_Formula_One_Manufacturers" class="mw-redirect" title="1958 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers">1958 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers</a>. The race was the 16th <a href="/wiki/Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="Monaco Grand Prix">Monaco Grand Prix</a> and was held over 100 laps of the three kilometre circuit for a total race distance of 314 kilometres.
</p><p>The race was won by French driver <a href="/wiki/Maurice_Trintignant" title="Maurice Trintignant">Maurice Trintignant</a> in the second and final Grand Prix victory of his long career. The win was the second consecutive victory for the privateer <a href="/wiki/Rob_Walker_Racing_Team" title="Rob Walker Racing Team">Rob Walker Racing Team</a>. Trintignant took the teams newly acquired <a href="/wiki/Cooper_T45" class="mw-redirect" title="Cooper T45">Cooper T45</a> to a twenty-second victory over Italian driver <a href="/wiki/Luigi_Musso" title="Luigi Musso">Luigi Musso</a> driving a <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_246_F1" title="Ferrari 246 F1">Ferrari Dino 246</a> with Musso's British teammate <a href="/wiki/Peter_Collins_(racing_driver)" title="Peter Collins (racing driver)">Peter Collins</a> (Ferrari Dino 246) was third.
</p><p>Trintignant's win put doubt in the superiority of front-engined cars. Musso's second place put him into a four-point championship lead over Moss and Trintignant.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_report">Race report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1958_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Race report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Vanwall" title="Vanwall">Vanwall</a> and <a href="/wiki/BRM" class="mw-redirect" title="BRM">BRM</a> returned after their Argentine absence and so <a href="/wiki/Stirling_Moss" title="Stirling Moss">Stirling Moss</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jean_Behra" title="Jean Behra">Jean Behra</a> and <a href="/wiki/Harry_Schell" title="Harry Schell">Harry Schell</a> all went back to their regular drives. In Moss's place at the <a href="/wiki/Rob_Walker_Racing_Team" title="Rob Walker Racing Team">Rob Walker Racing Team</a> with the new 2000 cc engined <a href="/wiki/Cooper_T45" class="mw-redirect" title="Cooper T45">Cooper T45</a> was French driver <a href="/wiki/Maurice_Trintignant" title="Maurice Trintignant">Maurice Trintignant</a>. The race winning <a href="/wiki/Cooper_T43" title="Cooper T43">Cooper T43</a> could not even qualify for the small 16-car Monaco grid, <a href="/wiki/Ron_Flockhart_(racing_driver)" title="Ron Flockhart (racing driver)">Ron Flockhart</a> was first reserve for the race.
</p><p>Missing from the field was the previous year's winner and reigning World Champion, <a href="/wiki/Juan_Manuel_Fangio" title="Juan Manuel Fangio">Juan Manuel Fangio</a> who surprisingly did not have a full-season drive. Fangio himself was in America, preparing for an ultimately disappointing <a href="/wiki/1958_Indianapolis_500" title="1958 Indianapolis 500">Indianapolis 500</a> campaign with the Dayton-Walther team. Future Formula One impresario <a href="/wiki/Bernie_Ecclestone" title="Bernie Ecclestone">Bernie Ecclestone</a> entered a <a href="/wiki/Connaught_Engineering" title="Connaught Engineering">Connaught</a> B-Type in his first appearance but he failed to qualify, as did two substitute drivers. Also failing to make the grid was 58-year-old <a href="/wiki/Louis_Chiron" title="Louis Chiron">Louis Chiron</a> in his final appearance at a Grand Prix. The Monaco local had won the Grand Prix 27 years earlier but Chiron and his <a href="/wiki/Maserati_250F" title="Maserati 250F">Maserati 250F</a> did not make it past qualifying.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Tony_Brooks_(racing_driver)" title="Tony Brooks (racing driver)">Tony Brooks</a> took pole position in his <a href="/w/index.php?title=Vanwall_VW_5&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Vanwall VW 5 (page does not exist)">Vanwall VW 5</a> but Behra won the start. He led until his brakes failed in his <a href="/wiki/BRM_P25" title="BRM P25">BRM P25</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Mike_Hawthorn" title="Mike Hawthorn">Mike Hawthorn</a> swept by in the <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_246_F1" title="Ferrari 246 F1">Ferrari Dino 246</a>. Moss (Vanwall VW 5) had been duelling with him throughout the race until he retired on lap 38, and Hawthorn followed suit on lap 47 with a broken fuel pump. <a href="/wiki/Graham_Hill" title="Graham Hill">Graham Hill</a> (<a href="/wiki/Lotus_12" title="Lotus 12">Lotus 12</a>) retired from fourth place in his first race on lap 69, after briefly leading. Vanwall pair Brooks and <a href="/wiki/Stuart_Lewis-Evans" title="Stuart Lewis-Evans">Stuart Lewis-Evans</a> retired, leaving Trintignant to lead home the Ferraris by some 20 seconds giving Rob Walker his second race win of the year. Behind the Ferraris of <a href="/wiki/Luigi_Musso" title="Luigi Musso">Luigi Musso</a> and <a href="/wiki/Peter_Collins_(racing_driver)" title="Peter Collins (racing driver)">Peter Collins</a> was <a href="/wiki/Jack_Brabham" title="Jack Brabham">Jack Brabham</a> in the factory Cooper T45, although three laps down and still looking a long way from becoming the next years champion. Schell in his BRM P25 was a further six laps behind Brabham picking up the final points after <a href="/wiki/Wolfgang_von_Trips" title="Wolfgang von Trips">Wolfgang von Trips</a> had an engine failure in the closing stages in his Ferrari Dino 246. The only other car still circulating was the second Lotus 12 of <a href="/wiki/Cliff_Allison" title="Cliff Allison">Cliff Allison</a>, 13 laps behind Trintignant.
</p><p>Trintignant's win put to the sword the belief that rear-engined Cooper victory achieved by <a href="/wiki/Stirling_Moss" title="Stirling Moss">Stirling Moss</a> in <a href="/wiki/1958_Argentine_Grand_Prix" title="1958 Argentine Grand Prix">Argentina</a> was a one-off freak circumstance win and the smaller British cars would be treated more seriously, although ultimately a Cooper would not win again until <a href="/wiki/1959_Formula_One_season" title="1959 Formula One season">1959</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Rob_Walker_Racing_Team" title="Rob Walker Racing Team">Rob Walker Racing Teams</a> pair of early 1958 victories would remain the best ever performance by a privateer team until the rise of <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_Racing" title="Tyrrell Racing">Tyrrell Racing</a> in the late 1960s.
</p>
