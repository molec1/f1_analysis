<p>The <b>1999 German Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 1 August 1999 at the <a href="/wiki/Hockenheimring" title="Hockenheimring">Hockenheimring</a> near <a href="/wiki/Hockenheim" title="Hockenheim">Hockenheim</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>. It was the tenth race of the <a href="/wiki/1999_FIA_Formula_One_World_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="1999 FIA Formula One World Championship">1999 FIA Formula One World Championship</a>. With <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> out injured, <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a> took a second successive victory as he chased the championship, aided by stand-in team-mate <a href="/wiki/Mika_Salo" title="Mika Salo">Mika Salo</a> moving over to give him the lead. In the early laps <a href="/wiki/Finland" title="Finland">Finnish</a> drivers ran first and second. However, <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> ultimately crashed out on lap 25 due to a tyre failure, allowing <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a> to finish third in his home Grand Prix.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a> was again rumoured to be leaving <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> when he allegedly retired a healthy car. Hill claimed that his <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a> had brake problems.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a> gave his winner's trophy to <a href="/wiki/Mika_Salo" title="Mika Salo">Mika Salo</a>, who was leading towards the end of the race and moved over on team orders.
</p>
