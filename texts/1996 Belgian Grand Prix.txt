<p>The <b>1996 Belgian Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>LIV Grand Prix de Belgique</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 25 August 1996 at <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Spa-Francorchamps" title="Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps">Spa-Francorchamps</a>. It was the thirteenth race of the <a href="/wiki/1996_FIA_Formula_One_World_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="1996 FIA Formula One World Championship">1996 FIA Formula One World Championship</a>.
</p><p>The 44-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_F310" title="Ferrari F310">Ferrari</a>. Schumacher had crashed heavily in Friday practice,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> but recovered to qualify third before taking his second win of the season. <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a>, who had started from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>, finished second in his <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW18" title="Williams FW18">Williams</a>-<a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> third in a <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/11" title="McLaren MP4/11">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Mercedes_AMG_High_Performance_Powertrains" title="Mercedes AMG High Performance Powertrains">Mercedes</a>. Villeneuve's teammate and Drivers' Championship leader, <a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a>, finished fifth.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1996_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<table class="box-Trivia plainlinks metadata ambox ambox-style" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-image"><div style="width:52px"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/f/f2/Edit-clear.svg/40px-Edit-clear.svg.png" decoding="async" width="40" height="40" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/f/f2/Edit-clear.svg/60px-Edit-clear.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/f/f2/Edit-clear.svg/80px-Edit-clear.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="48" data-file-height="48" /></div></td><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This article contains a <b><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Trivia_sections" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Trivia sections">list of miscellaneous information</a>.</b><span class="hide-when-compact"> Please <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Handling_trivia#Recommendations_for_handling_trivia" title="Wikipedia:Handling trivia">relocate</a> any relevant information into other sections or articles.</span>  <small class="date-container"><i>(<span class="date">August 2020</span>)</i></small></div></td></tr></tbody></table>
<ul><li>The start of the race saw the two <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Saubers</a> of <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Herbert" title="Johnny Herbert">Johnny Herbert</a> eliminated immediately when they collided at the La Source hairpin, following an incident that also involved <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Panis" title="Olivier Panis">Olivier Panis</a>' Ligier and <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a>'s Jordan. Panis also retired on the spot; Barrichello was able to continue, pitting to repair his suspension, although it eventually failed altogether on lap 30.</li>
<li>On lap 10, <a href="/wiki/Jos_Verstappen" title="Jos Verstappen">Jos Verstappen</a> pitted with a sticking throttle. The <a href="/wiki/Footwork_Arrows" title="Footwork Arrows">Footwork Arrows</a> pit crew found no damage and sent Verstappen back out, only for the Dutchman to crash almost immediately. Team boss <a href="/wiki/Tom_Walkinshaw" title="Tom Walkinshaw">Tom Walkinshaw</a> confirmed after the race that the throttle problem had not recurred, and that the crash was caused by a faulty wheel. The incident brought out the <a href="/wiki/Safety_car" title="Safety car">safety car</a> for seven laps, during which time all the drivers besides the <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLarens</a> of <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> and <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> (both running a one-stop strategy) made pit stops.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a>, leading the race when the safety car came out, missed his pit stop on lap 13. As a result, he lost the lead to <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>, who eventually won the race by 5.6 seconds from Villeneuve. The Canadian driver later explained that he had misunderstood the radio instruction to come in, due to the confusion brought about by the deployment of the safety car.</li>
<li>As a further consequence of Villeneuve's error, teammate <a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a> was instructed to pit by the Williams engineers, only to then be told to stay out just as he was heading into the pit lane. Hill was driving the spare Williams following a misfire in the Sunday morning warm-up session. By the time he finally got to make his pit stop, he had fallen to 13th, but he recovered to finish fifth.</li>
<li>Running in fourth place at half distance, <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> spun off in his Benetton while trying to pass <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a>'s Ferrari, an error which dropped him to 12th. After setting a string of fastest laps he recovered to sixth by the end of the race, thanks in part to Irvine's retirement with gearbox problems. Berger's Benetton teammate <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a> finished fourth after Coulthard had spun off into retirement and crashed on lap 38.</li></ul>
