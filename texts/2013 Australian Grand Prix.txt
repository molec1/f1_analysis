<p>The <b>2013 Australian Grand Prix</b> (formally known as the <b>2013 Formula 1 Rolex Australian Grand Prix</b>)<sup id="cite_ref-Official_name_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Official_name-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Auto_racing" title="Auto racing">motor race</a> that was held on 17 March 2013 as the opening round of the <a href="/wiki/2013_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2013 Formula One season">2013 World Championship</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_calender_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA_calender-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The race was held at the <a href="/wiki/Melbourne_Grand_Prix_Circuit" class="mw-redirect" title="Melbourne Grand Prix Circuit">Melbourne Grand Prix Circuit</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Melbourne" title="Melbourne">Melbourne</a> suburb of <a href="/wiki/Albert_Park,_Victoria" title="Albert Park, Victoria">Albert Park</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> It was the 78th race in the combined history of the <a href="/wiki/Australian_Grand_Prix" title="Australian Grand Prix">Australian Grand Prix</a> – which dates back to the <a href="/wiki/1928_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="1928 Australian Grand Prix">100 Miles Road Race</a> of 1928 – and the 18th time the event has been held at the Melbourne Grand Prix Circuit. The race was won by <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> with <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> coming second and <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> coming third.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>This was the last time a Formula One race was won by a driver from a team other than Mercedes, Ferrari or Red Bull until <a href="/wiki/Pierre_Gasly" title="Pierre Gasly">Pierre Gasly</a> won the <a href="/wiki/2020_Italian_Grand_Prix" title="2020 Italian Grand Prix">2020 Italian Grand Prix</a> driving for <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_AlphaTauri" title="Scuderia AlphaTauri">Scuderia AlphaTauri</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2013_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2013_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Following the collapse of <a href="/wiki/HRT_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="HRT F1">HRT F1</a> in December 2012,<sup id="cite_ref-HRT_out_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HRT_out-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> the grid for the season was reduced to twenty-two entries, necessitating changes to the structure of qualifying. The three-round knockout system introduced in <a href="/wiki/2006_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2006 Formula One World Championship">2006</a> remained in place, but only six cars, instead of seven, were eliminated during the first period of qualifying, with six more eliminated at the end of the second period. The third qualifying period remains unchanged with the ten fastest drivers all advancing to the final ten minutes of qualifying.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During the winter off-season, the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile</a> introduced new rules restricting the use of the <a href="/wiki/Drag_Reduction_System" class="mw-redirect" title="Drag Reduction System">Drag Reduction System</a> during free practice and qualifying, limiting its use to the circuit's designated DRS zones.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> However, during Friday practice, the race stewards encountered a technical glitch in the telemetry system that controlled the availability of DRS, meaning that the system would be completely unrestricted. This forced the stewards to rely on drivers to use the system honestly, threatening penalties to any driver caught using DRS outside the designated areas.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The same error meant that the stewards would be unable to notify drivers via telemetry of <a href="/wiki/Racing_flags" title="Racing flags">warning flags</a>, instead relying on marshalls around the circuit to display them and teams to warn drivers up upcoming flags during the race.
</p><p>Tyre supplier <a href="/wiki/Pirelli" title="Pirelli">Pirelli</a> provided teams with the super-soft and medium compound tyres for the race, compared to the soft and medium selection offered in 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2013_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:2013AusF1GPQ.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d6/2013AusF1GPQ.jpg/220px-2013AusF1GPQ.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="140" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d6/2013AusF1GPQ.jpg/330px-2013AusF1GPQ.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d6/2013AusF1GPQ.jpg/440px-2013AusF1GPQ.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2136" data-file-height="1356" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:2013AusF1GPQ.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>New Mercedes teammates, Hamilton and Rosberg, negotiating turn 2 during a waterlogged qualifying session.</div></div></div>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Q1">Q1</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2013_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Q1">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Prior to the first qualifying session of the season, Melbourne had experienced a record heatwave and no rain for almost the entire month of March.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> These dry conditions, however, did not prevail with precipitation occurring over free practice, turning into heavy rainfalls just before qualifying. After an initial thirty-minute postponement from the official 17:00 (local time) start, the first session of qualifying eventually started and was held in wet conditions with all drivers on wet tyres. Conditions were tricky, resulting in some drivers taking off-field excursions. <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> lost control of his car during turn 2 and slid into the wall. He was able to reverse back onto the circuit and continue, albeit with minor damage to his rear wing. <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> also spun into the wall after turn 14, escaping with only a broken front wing. As the track dried out, however, drivers switched to intermediate tyres and lap times began dropping for over the remainder of the session. <a href="/wiki/Esteban_Guti%C3%A9rrez" title="Esteban Gutiérrez">Esteban Gutiérrez</a> spun at the same place as Massa had previously, stalling the engine. The incident ended his qualifying session and prejudiced the lap times of others drivers.
</p><p>At the end of Q1, six drivers were eliminated. <a href="/wiki/Pastor_Maldonado" title="Pastor Maldonado">Pastor Maldonado</a> qualified seventeenth after going off the circuit, whilst Gutiérrez's crash meant that the Mexican was unable to continue and was subsequently eliminated in eighteenth place. <a href="/wiki/Marussia_F1" title="Marussia F1">Marussia's</a> <a href="/wiki/Jules_Bianchi" title="Jules Bianchi">Jules Bianchi</a> (on his Formula One debut) and <a href="/wiki/Max_Chilton" title="Max Chilton">Max Chilton</a> qualified nineteenth and twentieth respectively, ahead of the <a href="/wiki/Caterham_F1" title="Caterham F1">Caterhams</a> of <a href="/wiki/Giedo_van_der_Garde" title="Giedo van der Garde">Giedo van der Garde</a> and <a href="/wiki/Charles_Pic" title="Charles Pic">Charles Pic</a>. The latter failed to set a lap time within <a href="/wiki/107%25_rule" title="107% rule">107%</a> of the fastest time, set by <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a>, meaning his participation in the race depended on dispensation from the race stewards.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Q2">Q2</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2013_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Q2">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>With debris from several accidents during Q1 scattered across the track, the break between Q1 and Q2 was extended to allow marshalls to clean up. Over this period, however, rain intensified forcing a postponement of the session. Marshalls resorted to brooms in a bid to remove puddles forming on the poorly drained public road surface. With qualifying and the race being run late in the afternoon to accommodate European television audiences, twilight approached and the stewards tried to schedule a restart at 18:30 and then finally 18:50. The rain continued, albeit very lightly, and stewards ultimately chose to suspend qualifying.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> The final two sessions (Q2 and Q3) were thus rescheduled for Sunday morning.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Q2 session was scheduled to begin at 11:00 (local time) while the weather was cool and cloudy with an air temperature of only 15&#160;°C (59&#160;°F). The track remained damp from overnight rain and was also cool 13&#160;°C (55&#160;°F); humidity was hovering around 63%. In the end, it was <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jean-%C3%89ric_Vergne" title="Jean-Éric Vergne">Jean-Éric Vergne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a> and <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a> who failed to make it through to Q3.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Q3">Q3</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2013_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Q3">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The Red Bulls locked out the front row with <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> on pole and <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> on second.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2013_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The race began at 17:00 local time, with <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a> unable to take part in the race due to worries over a fuel feed problem in his <a href="/wiki/Sauber_C32" title="Sauber C32">Sauber C32</a>. Sebastian Vettel got a perfect start from pole and went into the first corner unchallenged, but the same could not be said of teammate Mark Webber who started alongside him, as a problem with an ECU meant that he could not use his KERS off the start line and he ended up dropping down to seventh.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> The chief beneficiaries of this were the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferraris</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> moving from fourth to second and <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Alonso</a> to third after overtaking <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> around the outside of Turn 3. <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> moved up to fifth ahead of Nico Rosberg and Webber and on the second lap took fourth from Hamilton by passing him at Turn 11. His <a href="/wiki/Lotus_F1_Team" class="mw-redirect" title="Lotus F1 Team">Lotus</a> teammate <a href="/wiki/Romain_Grosjean" title="Romain Grosjean">Romain Grosjean</a> went backwards from eighth to eleventh, losing places to <a href="/wiki/Paul_di_Resta" title="Paul di Resta">Paul di Resta</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> and <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a>.
</p><p>All the jostling behind Vettel helped him to gain a two-second lead by the end of the first lap. That was soon erased out by Massa and Alonso who rapidly caught up. Jenson Button made his first pit stop to switch from the super-softs to the medium tyres after just four laps and was followed by many others. Webber lost even further time and places during this period with a slow pitstop due to an issue with his jacks. The leaders elected to stay longer than the others, Vettel waiting for seven laps before pitting, Massa eight and Alonso and Räikkönen two laps further. The quartet rejoined in the same order, but more spread out due to Vettel and Massa gaining an extra lap on the new set of tyres. Hamilton and Rosberg, having realized that they did not have the pace of those in front, ran till lap 13 and 14 respectively in order to attempt a two-stop strategy.
</p><p>This left Sutil and <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a>, two of the drivers who elected to start the race on mediums out in front ahead of Vettel, Massa, Alonso and Räikkönen. Pérez was unable to make the mediums last and was quickly dispatched by the quartet on fresher mediums. Sutil was a totally different prospect. Vettel was unable to pass him despite having closed rapidly and this soon helped bring Massa and Alonso back on to his tail. Neither of them were able to make any direct impression. Räikkönen began to rapidly close in as well. Neither Webber nor Grosjean had got out of the midfield after their poor starts, with the former in ninth and the latter in eleventh.
</p><p>On lap 20, Alonso decided to make an early second pitstop for new tyres. Sutil and Vettel came in a lap later, but the lap on fresh tyres was enough for the Spanish driver to jump both Germans. Massa stayed out for too long and dropped to the back of the group. Räikkönen, Hamilton and Rosberg now held the top three places, all three of them attempting two-stop strategies. Alonso was fourth, Vettel fifth having passed Sutil and Massa dropped to seventh. The two Mercedes started to experience a drop-off in their lap times but Räikkönen was still able to drive at a consistent speed despite having pitted for the tyres four laps before the Mercedes drivers. The tyre issue became academic for Rosberg when he retired close to half-distance with an electrical failure. Hamilton was now vulnerable to the drivers with newer tyres. Alonso attacked and passed Hamilton on lap 32 despite his best efforts to defend. The Briton flatspotted his tyre and had to pit immediately and rejoined in sixth place just ahead of di Resta and Webber.
</p><p>With a clear track in front of him, Alonso now started to rapidly close in on Räikkönen's 16 second lead. The Finn responded by making his second pitstop on lap 34, rejoining in fifth. During this stint, Alonso was able to drop Vettel and build a five-second gap over the German driver, who in turn was building a similar gap to Sutil. Massa, like Vettel earlier, was held up behind Sutil unable to pass despite having a faster car. The frustrated Brazilian made his final pit stop early as a result on lap 36. Vettel followed suit to cover him next lap, whereas Alonso, now having the cushion of a gap was able to wait until lap 39. On the same lap, after releasing Button from 11th place, <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> was forced to retire with a broken exhaust. Sutil now led from Räikkönen and Alonso but could do little to keep them behind with older tyres. Räikkönen took full advantage when Alonso got held up behind Sutil as the Finn had a seven-second gap to the Spaniard. Hamilton decided that a two-stop strategy was not going to work once Vettel and Massa passed him. He made a third stop for fresh tyres, again rejoining in front of di Resta and Webber.
</p><p>When Sutil made his final pitstop on lap 46 while running third, he had to switch to the super-soft tyres as he had started on the mediums unlike the leaders. He rejoined fifth, but quickly found out that the super-softs wore out rapidly. Sutil was defenseless when first Hamilton and then a recovering Webber attacked him on lap 51. Despite the newer tyres, neither Alonso nor Vettel were able to make an impression on leader Räikkönen. Räikkönen hammered the point home by setting the fastest lap of the race on lap 56, which was the 23rd lap on the same set of tyres. Alonso backed off and settled for second after almost crashing into the back of <a href="/wiki/Charles_Pic" title="Charles Pic">Charles Pic</a> while lapping the <a href="/wiki/Caterham_F1_Team" class="mw-redirect" title="Caterham F1 Team">Caterham</a> driver.
</p><p>Räikkönen won from Alonso and Vettel completed the podium. Massa was fourth after having to back off in the latter stages to conserve his tyres. Hamilton fifth ahead of Webber. Sutil, despite the badly worn super-softs, still held on for seventh ahead of his teammate di Resta. The battle for ninth was ultimately won by Button, with Grosjean taking the final point, holding off Pérez and <a href="/wiki/Jean-%C3%89ric_Vergne" title="Jean-Éric Vergne">Jean-Éric Vergne</a>. <a href="/wiki/Esteban_Guti%C3%A9rrez" title="Esteban Gutiérrez">Esteban Gutiérrez</a> was the best placed rookie in 13th, one place ahead of <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a>. <a href="/wiki/Jules_Bianchi" title="Jules Bianchi">Jules Bianchi</a> was 15th, comfortably ahead of his teammate and the two <a href="/wiki/Caterham_F1" title="Caterham F1">Caterhams</a>. Ferrari took the lead in the Constructor's Championship, with Lotus-Renault second and <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull-Renault</a> third.
</p><p>The win was the twentieth of Räikkönen's career, tying him with Finnish compatriot <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a>. It was also his last until the <a href="/wiki/2018_United_States_Grand_Prix" title="2018 United States Grand Prix">2018 United States Grand Prix</a>.
</p>
