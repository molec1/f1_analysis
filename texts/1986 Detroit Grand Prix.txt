<p>The <b>1986 Detroit Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on June 22, 1986, in <a href="/wiki/Detroit" title="Detroit">Detroit</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michigan" title="Michigan">Michigan</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Summary">Summary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1986_Detroit_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Summary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Lotus</a>-<a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a> star <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> started on the pole and came away with a hard-earned win, the fourth of his career. The young Brazilian charged through the field after dropping to eighth with a deflating tire for his first USGP victory. The race saw six lead changes among five drivers, and the victory for Senna began a streak that would see him take five United States Grand Prix wins in six years.
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Gerhard_Berger_1986_Detroit.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/67/Gerhard_Berger_1986_Detroit.jpg/220px-Gerhard_Berger_1986_Detroit.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="124" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/67/Gerhard_Berger_1986_Detroit.jpg/330px-Gerhard_Berger_1986_Detroit.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/67/Gerhard_Berger_1986_Detroit.jpg/440px-Gerhard_Berger_1986_Detroit.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1600" data-file-height="899" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Gerhard_Berger_1986_Detroit.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> driving for <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a> at the race.</div></div></div>
<p>American <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Cheever" title="Eddie Cheever">Eddie Cheever</a>, in his only F1 race of the season, was taking <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Tambay" title="Patrick Tambay">Patrick Tambay</a>'s seat in the <a href="/wiki/Carl_Haas" title="Carl Haas">Carl Haas</a> <a href="/wiki/Lola_Cars" title="Lola Cars">Lola</a>, as Tambay had not fully recovered from his <a href="/wiki/1986_Canadian_Grand_Prix" title="1986 Canadian Grand Prix">Montreal</a> injuries. Haas had attempted to get the legendary <a href="/wiki/Mario_Andretti" title="Mario Andretti">Mario Andretti</a>, but, apparently because of a <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_du_Sport_Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale du Sport Automobile">FISA</a>-<a href="/wiki/Champ_Car" class="mw-redirect" title="Champ Car">CART</a> feud, Andretti's application for an <a href="/wiki/FIA_Super_Licence" title="FIA Super Licence">FIA Super Licence</a> was denied (allegedly Haas then tried for <a href="/wiki/Michael_Andretti" title="Michael Andretti">Michael Andretti</a> on Mario's suggestion but his bid for the license was also denied). Cheever, who was racing in the <a href="/wiki/1986_World_Sportscar_Championship" title="1986 World Sportscar Championship">1986 World Sportscar Championship</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Tom_Walkinshaw_Racing" title="Tom Walkinshaw Racing">TWR</a> <a href="/wiki/Jaguar_Cars" title="Jaguar Cars">Jaguar</a> team, already had the required licence and had no such issues qualified tenth, out-qualifying temporary teammate <a href="/wiki/Alan_Jones_(racing_driver)" title="Alan Jones (racing driver)">Alan Jones</a> by almost 3 seconds. His race ended at just over half-distance with a broken wheel peg.
</p><p>As expected, the drivers had difficulty finding grip in Friday's sessions, as the temporary street circuit needed time to get some rubber down. <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a>-<a href="/wiki/Honda_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Honda F1">Honda</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> was the only one under 1:40, putting in two outstanding laps on race tires. On a gorgeous Saturday, Mansell and Senna bided their time, waiting until halfway through the one-hour session to take the track. Senna came out on qualifiers, managed to get a clear track, and clocked a course record 1:38.301. Mansell was balked on his run on the fragile qualifying tires when <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> crashed in the chicane, and Senna had the pole by more than half a second.
</p><p>Sunday was hot and humid with a chance of rain. At the start, Senna led Mansell into the first corner, and <a href="/wiki/Ren%C3%A9_Arnoux" title="René Arnoux">René Arnoux</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Equipe_Ligier" title="Equipe Ligier">Ligier</a>-Renault jumped ahead of <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a> in the second Williams.
</p><p>Beginning lap 3, Senna missed a gear entering Turn One, and Mansell's Williams shot by into the lead.
</p><p>By the end of lap 5, Mansell's lead was 4.3 seconds, but that was as big as it got. His rear brake pads were too cool and had become glazed, and Senna was able to draw back up to him. By lap seven, he was right on his gearbox, and on lap 8, he retook the lead.
</p><p>Senna immediately began to draw away, while Mansell slipped back through the field. On lap 14, with a 6.5 second lead, Senna suddenly ducked into the pits with a slowly deflating right rear tire. Despite a quick change, he re-entered in eighth place, twenty seconds behind the new leader, René Arnoux.
</p><p>Arnoux held the lead for just three laps before having to pit for new rubber. His Ligier teammate <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Laffite" title="Jacques Laffite">Jacques Laffite</a>, running with harder compound <a href="/wiki/Pirelli" title="Pirelli">Pirelli</a> rear tires, moved to the top of the score sheet. At age 42, it was Laffite's first time to lead a race in more than three years,
and the last time in his career.
</p><p>After his stop on lap 14, Senna had begun to carve his way back to the front, overtaking, not back markers, but the best drivers of the era. He passed  the struggling <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferraris</a> of <a href="/wiki/Michele_Alboreto" title="Michele Alboreto">Michele Alboreto</a> on lap 15, and <a href="/wiki/Stefan_Johansson" title="Stefan Johansson">Stefan Johansson</a> on lap 17. He then passed Arnoux when he made his tire stop on lap 18, Prost's <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Techniques_d%27Avant_Garde" title="Techniques d&#39;Avant Garde">TAG</a> on lap 28, and Mansell on lap 31. Piquet passed Laffite to become the race's fifth different leader. Senna reached second just 1.7 seconds behind Piquet by passing Laffite, who then pitted for tires.
</p><p>With the two of them in front on their own, and Piquet leading by up to 3.5 seconds, Senna was content for the time being to follow his countryman. After eight laps in the lead, Piquet pitted for tires.  Senna regained first place and then also pitted.  Piquet's 18.4 second stop was even slower than teammate Mansell's had been, but when he returned to the track, the two-time champion got the bit between his teeth. Running second behind Senna, Piquet set the fastest lap of the race on lap 41, but with a pit stop ten seconds quicker than Piquet's, Senna was able to retain the lead.
</p><p>On the very next lap, the 42nd, Piquet pushed a bit too much, and crashed hard at the left-hand corner before the last chicane. He ended up in the tire barrier, unhurt, but with his car in a very precarious position. The crane, present for just that reason, was unable to move the car. Arnoux, now 16.6 seconds behind in second, took a second a lap off Senna's lead for five laps while a yellow was displayed in the corner where Piquet had crashed. As soon as the yellow flag was gone, Arnoux went wide and hit Piquet's abandoned Williams! The Frenchman decided that his car was not damaged, and attempted to rejoin the field, but did so right in front of <a href="/wiki/Thierry_Boutsen" title="Thierry Boutsen">Thierry Boutsen</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Arrows_Grand_Prix_International" title="Arrows Grand Prix International">Arrows</a>-<a href="/wiki/BMW_in_Formula_One" title="BMW in Formula One">BMW</a>, sidelining both cars.
</p><p>Very uncharacteristic of a street race, these three&#160;– Piquet, Arnoux and Boutsen&#160;– were the only drivers all afternoon to retire due to accidents.
</p><p>With 17 laps to go, Senna led Prost by 27 seconds, but the McLaren's TAG engine was cutting out under braking. Laffite was able to close as Prost struggled, and quickly the Ligier moved by into second place. Senna came home 30 seconds in front for his first victory in the United States, and the only American win for the Renault.
</p>
