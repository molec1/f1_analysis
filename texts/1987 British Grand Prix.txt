<p>The <b>1987 British Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>XL Shell Oils British Grand Prix</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 12 July 1987 at the <a href="/wiki/Silverstone_Circuit" title="Silverstone Circuit">Silverstone Circuit</a>, <a href="/wiki/Silverstone" title="Silverstone">Silverstone</a>. It was the seventh race of the <a href="/wiki/1987_FIA_Formula_One_World_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="1987 FIA Formula One World Championship">1987 FIA Formula One World Championship</a>. It was the 42nd <a href="/wiki/British_Grand_Prix" title="British Grand Prix">British Grand Prix</a> and the 23rd to be held at Silverstone. The race was held over 65 laps of the 4.78-kilometre (2.97&#160;mi) circuit for race distance of 311 kilometres (193&#160;mi).
</p><p>The race was won by local driver <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW11" title="Williams FW11">Williams</a>-<a href="/wiki/Honda_in_Formula_One" title="Honda in Formula One">Honda</a>. In one of his most memorable Formula One performances, Mansell hunted down Brazilian team-mate <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a>, who had started from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>, with three laps remaining. It was Mansell's third win of the season, and his second consecutive British Grand Prix victory. <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> finished third in his <a href="/wiki/Lotus_99T" title="Lotus 99T">Lotus</a>-Honda, one lap behind.
</p><p>The win moved Mansell into second place in the Drivers' Championship, level on points with Piquet and one point behind Senna.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_summary">Race summary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1987_British_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Race summary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1987_British_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As usual, <a href="/wiki/Honda_in_Formula_One" title="Honda in Formula One">Honda</a>-powered cars dominated the qualifying sessions, with <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a> taking pole position and <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> third in his <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Lotus</a> despite being a full second slower than Mansell. <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> was fourth in his <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>, while the <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benettons</a> of <a href="/wiki/Thierry_Boutsen" title="Thierry Boutsen">Thierry Boutsen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Teo_Fabi" title="Teo Fabi">Teo Fabi</a> were fifth and sixth, and the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferraris</a> of <a href="/wiki/Michele_Alboreto" title="Michele Alboreto">Michele Alboreto</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> seventh and eighth. Completing the top ten were <a href="/wiki/Andrea_de_Cesaris" title="Andrea de Cesaris">Andrea de Cesaris</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Brabham" title="Brabham">Brabham</a> and <a href="/wiki/Stefan_Johansson" title="Stefan Johansson">Stefan Johansson</a> in the second McLaren.
</p><p>During qualifying, <a href="/wiki/Piercarlo_Ghinzani" title="Piercarlo Ghinzani">Piercarlo Ghinzani</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Equipe_Ligier" title="Equipe Ligier">Ligier</a> ran out of fuel in front of the pits. His mechanics jumped the pit wall, refuelled him on the track and then push-started him, a clear violation of the rules leading to Ghinzani's exclusion from the remainder of the event. Before the incident, the Italian had set a time which would have put him 19th on the grid.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1987_British_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the start, Prost was the quickest and took the lead, only to be passed by Piquet at Maggotts; Mansell soon followed his teammate. The race then developed into a battle between the two Williams drivers, with Piquet leading most of the time.
</p><p>On lap 35, Mansell was around two seconds behind his teammate. Both Williams drivers were scheduled to complete the race without a tyre change, but Mansell and the team elected to make a stop to change tyres. Mansell rejoined the race some 29 seconds behind Piquet, with 28 laps remaining. On fresh rubber, Mansell began an epic charge, breaking the lap record eight times to the delight of the over 100,000 strong British crowd.
</p><p>By lap 62 Mansell was right on Piquet's tail, and on lap 63 the Englishman overtook his teammate. Shortly after crossing the finish line, Mansell's car slowed down and was engulfed by the crowd. Initially it was thought that he had run out of fuel, but he had actually blown up the engine, out of the stress of running the last 6 laps on "Q" mode (which gives the engine +100hp), and risking running out of fuel at any moment (his fuel display was reading "minus 2.5 laps"). In fact that incident was the last straw for the patience of the Honda management, since it had – again – threatened their easily attainable 1-2 result. Honda would switch their supply of engines from Williams to McLaren for 1988, while Piquet would sign for Lotus – also running Hondas – in the following weeks.<sup id="cite_ref-Honda_engines_report_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Honda_engines_report-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Senna finished third, one lap down, with a further lap back to teammate <a href="/wiki/Satoru_Nakajima" title="Satoru Nakajima">Satoru Nakajima</a> in fourth. <a href="/wiki/Derek_Warwick" title="Derek Warwick">Derek Warwick</a> was fifth in the <a href="/wiki/Arrows_A10" title="Arrows A10">Arrows</a>-<a href="/wiki/BMW_M12" title="BMW M12">Megatron</a>, with Fabi completing the top six.
</p>
