<p>The <b>1982 British Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>XXXV Marlboro British Grand Prix</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held at <a href="/wiki/Brands_Hatch" title="Brands Hatch">Brands Hatch</a> on 18 July 1982. It was the tenth race of the <a href="/wiki/1982_FIA_Formula_One_World_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="1982 FIA Formula One World Championship">1982 FIA Formula One World Championship</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The 76-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Niki_Lauda" title="Niki Lauda">Niki Lauda</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/1" title="McLaren MP4/1">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Ford_Motor_Company" title="Ford Motor Company">Ford</a>, after he started from fifth position. <a href="/wiki/Didier_Pironi" title="Didier Pironi">Didier Pironi</a> finished second in a <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_126C" title="Ferrari 126C">Ferrari</a>, while teammate <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Tambay" title="Patrick Tambay">Patrick Tambay</a> achieved his first podium finish by coming third. <a href="/wiki/Derek_Warwick" title="Derek Warwick">Derek Warwick</a> stunned and delighted the British fans by taking his <a href="/wiki/Toleman" title="Toleman">Toleman</a>, a team who frequently failed to qualify for Grand Prix races during 1981 and 1982, through the field into second place before being forced to retire. Pironi took over the lead of the Drivers' Championship from Lauda's teammate, <a href="/wiki/John_Watson_(racing_driver)" title="John Watson (racing driver)">John Watson</a>, who spun off on the third lap.
</p>
