<p>The <b>2010 Singapore Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>2010 Formula 1 SingTel Singapore Grand Prix</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 26 September 2010 at the <a href="/wiki/Marina_Bay_Street_Circuit" title="Marina Bay Street Circuit">Marina Bay Street Circuit</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marina_Bay,_Singapore" title="Marina Bay, Singapore">Marina Bay, Singapore</a>. It was the fifteenth race of the <a href="/wiki/2010_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2010 Formula One season">2010 Formula One season</a>, and the third Singapore Grand Prix held as part of the Formula One World Championship. The 61-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> who started in <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>. <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> finished second for <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a>, his teammate <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> was third.
</p><p>Alonso held off Vettel to maintain his start line advantage on the first lap. <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>, who started third, fought off teammate <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> to retain his start position. The top two remained the same through the first and only round of <a href="/wiki/Pit_stop" title="Pit stop">pit stops</a>. Webber's team, Red Bull, opted to pit him early allowing him to move ahead of Hamilton. The race was neutralised by the deployment of the <a href="/wiki/Safety_car" title="Safety car">safety car</a> after <a href="/wiki/Kamui_Kobayashi" title="Kamui Kobayashi">Kamui Kobayashi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Senna" title="Bruno Senna">Bruno Senna</a> crashed on lap 31. Hamilton retired after a collision with Webber shortly after the race restarted on lap 36. Alonso maintained his lead throughout the remainder of the race despite pressure from Vettel.
</p><p>The race was Alonso's second consecutive victory and his fourth of the 2010 season; he had started from pole position to win the <a href="/wiki/2010_Italian_Grand_Prix" title="2010 Italian Grand Prix">Italian Grand Prix</a> two weeks earlier. The win moved Alonso into second place in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">World Drivers' Championship</a>, eleven points behind leader Webber. Hamilton's retirement dropped him to third, while Vettel's second-place finish moved him into fourth ahead of <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a>. In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a> Red Bull extended its lead over McLaren to twenty-four points. Ferrari was a further forty points behind, with four races remaining in the season.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_Singapore_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The 2010 Singapore Grand Prix was the fifteenth round of the <a href="/wiki/2010_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2010 Formula One season">2010 Formula One season</a> after taking a two-week break from the previous race in <a href="/wiki/2010_Italian_Grand_Prix" title="2010 Italian Grand Prix">Italy</a>. The event was held at the <a href="/wiki/Marina_Bay_Street_Circuit" title="Marina Bay Street Circuit">Marina Bay Street Circuit</a> in <a href="/wiki/Marina_Bay,_Singapore" title="Marina Bay, Singapore">Marina Bay, Singapore</a> on 26 September 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The Grand Prix was contested by twelve teams with two drivers each. The teams (also known as <a href="/wiki/Formula_One#Constructors" title="Formula One">constructors</a>) were: <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a>, <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>, <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso</a>, <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus_(2010%E2%80%9311)" title="Team Lotus (2010–11)">Lotus</a>, <a href="/wiki/HRT_Formula_1_Team" title="HRT Formula 1 Team">Hispania</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a> and <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Racing" title="Virgin Racing">Virgin</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-gpraceresults_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpraceresults-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_tyre" class="mw-redirect" title="Formula One tyre">Tyre</a> supplier <a href="/wiki/Bridgestone" title="Bridgestone">Bridgestone</a> brought four different tyre types to the race: two dry compounds (super soft "options" and medium "primes") and two <a href="/wiki/Rain_tyre" title="Rain tyre">wet-weather compounds</a> (intermediate and full wets).<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-regulations_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The soft compounds were denoted by a green stripe on their side-walls; the wet compound tyres were identified by a green line at the bottom of their central groove.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The rules of 2010 Grand Prix races stipulated that all cars should use both types of tyre during a race unless the driver used any one of two wet-weather compounds. Each driver was limited to eleven sets of dry tyres for the weekend.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Before the race, Red Bull driver <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> led the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a> with 187&#160;<a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Championship_points_scoring_systems" title="List of Formula One World Championship points scoring systems">points</a>. He was ahead of <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> in second with 182 points and <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> in third on 166 points. Hamilton's teammate <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> was fourth with 165 points, and <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> was fifth on 163 points.<sup id="cite_ref-points_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Red Bull was leading the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a> with 350&#160;points; McLaren and Ferrari were second and third with 347 and 290 points respectively, while Mercedes on 158 and Renault with 127 points contended for fourth position.<sup id="cite_ref-points_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Red Bull, McLaren, and Ferrari had won the previous fourteen races of the season (with victories being taken by Alonso, Button, Vettel, Hamilton and Webber). <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> (twice), <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a> had finished in second place, and <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> (three times), Massa (twice) and Kubica (once) had all finished in third.<sup id="cite_ref-points_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Singapore was a critical race in the championship as all five drivers had a chance to take the lead with a victory. The situation was made difficult for Alonso as he had used his allocation of eight engines following the Italian Grand Prix, and to replace one would incur a ten-place grid penalty. His rivals each had at least one untouched engine in reserve; Webber had two.<sup id="cite_ref-mscompreview_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mscompreview-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Nevertheless, Alonso set himself the target to score points in Singapore.<sup id="cite_ref-mscompreview_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mscompreview-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> He also acknowledged that his car was not the fastest in the field and felt he needed five podiums in the season's five remaining races.<sup id="cite_ref-singaporedrivers_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-singaporedrivers-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Webber stated that he expected Singapore to be a tough weekend for him after he had retired from the event in 2008 and 2009, but felt there was still some time before a driver clinched the championship.<sup id="cite_ref-mscompreview_10-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mscompreview-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> After crashing out of the first lap of the Italian Grand Prix, Hamilton said he would push hard to stay in championship contention: "I'll take each race as it comes, but I'll also be making sure I get to the finish of the next five races, that's more important than anything. I go to Singapore to win."<sup id="cite_ref-mscompreview_10-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mscompreview-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton felt Alonso was the favourite to win the Grand Prix and viewed the latter as his main rival for the Drivers' Championship which was shared by Red Bull team principal <a href="/wiki/Christian_Horner" title="Christian Horner">Christian Horner</a>. Button himself believed Alonso would have an extra advantage as the latter's teammate Massa was out of contention for the title.<sup id="cite_ref-singaporedrivers_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-singaporedrivers-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Turn_5,_Marina_Bay_Street_Circuit,_first_practice_for_2010_Singapore_Grand_Prix.JPG" class="image"><img alt="The fifth corner of the Marina Bay Street Circuit viewed behind a metal fence" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e3/Turn_5%2C_Marina_Bay_Street_Circuit%2C_first_practice_for_2010_Singapore_Grand_Prix.JPG/220px-Turn_5%2C_Marina_Bay_Street_Circuit%2C_first_practice_for_2010_Singapore_Grand_Prix.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e3/Turn_5%2C_Marina_Bay_Street_Circuit%2C_first_practice_for_2010_Singapore_Grand_Prix.JPG/330px-Turn_5%2C_Marina_Bay_Street_Circuit%2C_first_practice_for_2010_Singapore_Grand_Prix.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e3/Turn_5%2C_Marina_Bay_Street_Circuit%2C_first_practice_for_2010_Singapore_Grand_Prix.JPG/440px-Turn_5%2C_Marina_Bay_Street_Circuit%2C_first_practice_for_2010_Singapore_Grand_Prix.JPG 2x" data-file-width="4000" data-file-height="3000" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Turn_5,_Marina_Bay_Street_Circuit,_first_practice_for_2010_Singapore_Grand_Prix.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The fifth turn of the Marina Bay Street Circuit, one of the areas resurfaced in an effort to reduce bumpiness</div></div></div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Marina_Bay_Street_Circuit" title="Marina Bay Street Circuit">Marina Bay Street Circuit</a> was resurfaced between turns three and seven, along with turns thirteen to seventeen, after drivers complained about its bumpiness following the <a href="/wiki/2009_Singapore_Grand_Prix" title="2009 Singapore Grand Prix">2009 Singapore Grand Prix</a>. The Singapore Sling <a href="/wiki/Chicane" title="Chicane">chicane</a> was adjusted to allow for a more gradual curve to exist which enabled a safer exit for drivers.<sup id="cite_ref-mscompreview_10-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mscompreview-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The outside wall at turn 21 was brought closer towards the race track to cover up half of a drain which was previously exposed to cars,<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> and the pit lane was resurfaced to allow for a smoother exit for drivers rejoining the circuit.<sup id="cite_ref-mscompreview_10-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mscompreview-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton, a vocal critic of the circuit's condition, described it as "dangerous" because it had remained uneven, and said the Singapore Sling chicane was "the worst corner I have ever driven in Formula One" despite the safety modifications made to it.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> The organisers of the Singapore Grand Prix introduced a new machine to clean the surface of the track after drivers had problems with a dusty and slippery track surface in the previous year's race.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Two days before the first practice session, drivers expressed concerns over low visibility caused by rain showers combined with the glare from the circuit lighting.<sup id="cite_ref-mscompreview_10-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mscompreview-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>There were two driver changes heading into the Grand Prix. Hispania Racing announced that <a href="/wiki/Sakon_Yamamoto" title="Sakon Yamamoto">Sakon Yamamoto</a>, one of the team's drivers since the tenth race of the season, was to be replaced for the weekend by the team's reserve driver <a href="/wiki/Christian_Klien" title="Christian Klien">Christian Klien</a> because he was suffering from <a href="/wiki/Foodborne_illness" title="Foodborne illness">food poisoning</a>. Several journalists noticed a seemingly healthy Yamamoto in the paddock, suggesting that he had been dropped for sponsorship reasons instead.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Team principal <a href="/wiki/Colin_Kolles" title="Colin Kolles">Colin Kolles</a> confirmed that the arrangement would be for one race and Yamamoto would return to the cockpit at <a href="/wiki/2010_Japanese_Grand_Prix" title="2010 Japanese Grand Prix">Suzuka</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Pedro_de_la_Rosa" title="Pedro de la Rosa">Pedro de la Rosa</a> was summarily dropped by Sauber and replaced by <a href="/wiki/Pirelli" title="Pirelli">Pirelli</a> test driver <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a>, who last drove for the Sauber team in <a href="/wiki/2009_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2009 Formula One World Championship">2009</a>. The change was made because of de la Rosa's poor performance relative to that of teammate <a href="/wiki/Kamui_Kobayashi" title="Kamui Kobayashi">Kamui Kobayashi</a>, and Heidfeld's knowledge of the Marina Bay Street Circuit and the <a href="/wiki/Yas_Marina_Circuit" title="Yas Marina Circuit">Yas Marina Circuit</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Several teams made modifications to their cars in preparation for the event. McLaren, Red Bull, and Williams all revised their front wings. McLaren's revision was designed to separate <a href="/wiki/Airflow" title="Airflow">airflow</a> into two separate channels, with both directing airflow around the outside of the car's front tyres.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Red Bull's design, used in the Friday practice sessions, had two vertical slots to the rear of its endplate along with another to avoid creating a vortex when it operated with the planes and endplate. A previous design, debuted at the <a href="/wiki/2010_British_Grand_Prix" title="2010 British Grand Prix">British Grand Prix</a>, was used both in qualifying and in the race.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> In addition, the team used a revised <a href="/wiki/Diffuser_(automotive)" title="Diffuser (automotive)">diffuser</a> to allow for better management of airflow to the top of the diffuser's side section.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Williams's design debuted for the first time and was designed to make the tyre act like a diffuser which took air in to improve its efficiency.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice">Practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_Singapore_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Adrian_Sutil_2010_Canada.jpg" class="image"><img alt="Adrian Sutil at the 2010 Canadian Grand Prix" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cd/Adrian_Sutil_2010_Canada.jpg/170px-Adrian_Sutil_2010_Canada.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="255" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cd/Adrian_Sutil_2010_Canada.jpg/255px-Adrian_Sutil_2010_Canada.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cd/Adrian_Sutil_2010_Canada.jpg/340px-Adrian_Sutil_2010_Canada.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2336" data-file-height="3504" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Adrian_Sutil_2010_Canada.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a>, who damaged his Force India's suspension in a crash during the second practice session.</div></div></div>
<p>Three practice sessions—two on Friday and a third on Saturday—were held before the main Sunday race. The Friday afternoon and evening sessions each lasted ninety minutes. The third session was held on Saturday afternoon and lasted an hour.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The first practice session was initially held on a wet track with a clear sky after rainstorms earlier in the day left standing water on parts of the circuit, and high humidity slowed the drying process. These conditions meant drivers used intermediate tyres first before dry tyres were utilised in the session's closing minutes.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Webber set the fastest time of the first session with a late lap of 1 minute and 54.589 seconds, one-tenth of a second faster than <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> in second who was briefly quickest before Webber set his lap. <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a>, with a lap of 1:58.827, was third fastest, ahead of Vettel and <a href="/wiki/Jaime_Alguersuari" title="Jaime Alguersuari">Jaime Alguersuari</a>. Button, <a href="/wiki/Vitantonio_Liuzzi" title="Vitantonio Liuzzi">Vitantonio Liuzzi</a>, <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9bastien_Buemi" title="Sébastien Buemi">Sébastien Buemi</a>, Kubica, and <a href="/wiki/Vitaly_Petrov" title="Vitaly Petrov">Vitaly Petrov</a> rounded out the session's top ten fastest drivers.<sup id="cite_ref-practice1_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-practice1-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the second practice session, when the track was still damp in some sections, Vettel set the fastest time of the night, a lap of 1:46.660 on super-soft tyres; Webber finished with the second fastest lap. Button drove aggressively and battled Vettel for the fastest time throughout before he ended with the third quickest lap, half a second off Vettel's pace. Alonso, who was fourth fastest, ran wide at turn 17 while on a quick lap and slid down the access road at the next corner; he stalled while attempting to rejoin the track and abandoned his car requiring <a href="/wiki/Motorsport_marshal" title="Motorsport marshal">marshals</a> to push his Ferrari away from the track.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcpractice2_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcpractice2-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton, Barrichello and Massa were in positions five to seven. The two Mercedes drivers were eighth and tenth; with <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> ahead of Schumacher, separated by Kubica. Sutil's Force India was launched into the air when he hit a <a href="/wiki/Curb" title="Curb">kerb</a> at the Singapore Sling chicane and his front-left suspension was broken upon landing requiring him to miss half the session,<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> and Alguersuari's left-rear tyre made contact with a barrier but he avoided major damage to his car.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcpractice2_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcpractice2-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Sutil was fined US$10,000 (£6,300) by the stewards because he attempted to drive back to the pit lane in his damaged car and did not stop at a safe position on the circuit.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Rain hit the circuit on Saturday afternoon, ending three hours before the session started, resulting in several damp patches on the circuit though the air temperature remained hot. Different sections of the circuit dried out at different rates making it difficult for drivers to tell precisely how wet or dry certain corners were. The teams ran intermediate tyres before switching to the super-soft tyre as the session progressed. Some drivers ran deep into turn five.<sup id="cite_ref-practicethree_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-practicethree-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-autosportp3_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportp3-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Vettel was the fastest driver of the session with a time of 1:48.028 set in the last twenty minutes of the one-hour period. The time was four-tenths faster than championship rival Alonso. Hamilton, Massa, Rosberg, Webber, <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a>, Kubica, Sutil and Buemi completed the top ten ahead of qualifying.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportp3_28-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportp3-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Hispania Racing driver <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Senna" title="Bruno Senna">Bruno Senna</a>'s right-rear tyre got onto a white line at the Singapore Sling chicane and spun.<sup id="cite_ref-practicethree_27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-practicethree-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> Senna later brushed a wall entering the <a href="/wiki/Esplanade_Bridge" title="Esplanade Bridge">Esplanade Bridge</a> in the final minute and stopped his car, causing the <a href="/wiki/Racing_flags#Yellow_flag" title="Racing flags">yellow-flags</a> to be shown; drivers were unable to improve on their lap times because of the limited time available.<sup id="cite_ref-practicethree_27-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-practicethree-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-autosportp3_28-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportp3-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_Singapore_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Saturday evening's qualifying session was divided into three parts. The first part ran for twenty minutes and eliminated the cars that finished eighteenth or lower. The second sessions lasted fifteen minutes and eliminated cars in positions eleven to seventeen. The final session ran for ten minutes and determined <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> to tenth. Cars which competed in the final session were not allowed to change tyres before the race; these started the race fitted with the tyres with which they set their quickest lap times.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_7-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> It was held in dry weather conditions.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Alonso was fastest in the first and third sessions, and clinched his second consecutive pole position with a lap time of 1:45.390 which he set on his first run of the third session. He was restricted to one timed lap in the second session because of an engine mapping problem,<sup id="cite_ref-gpqualireport_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpqualireport-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> which meant Ferrari immediately re-programmed Alonso's engine upon discovering the issue.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcquali_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcquali-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Alonso was joined on the front row of the grid by Vettel, who recorded a lap 0.067&#160;seconds slower, and felt he could have taken pole position as he misjudged a gap while following Schumacher and brushed a wall exiting the Singapore Sling chicane.<sup id="cite_ref-gpqualireport_30-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpqualireport-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton qualified third and was happy with his starting position despite losing <a href="/wiki/Downforce" title="Downforce">downforce</a> on the track's final sector while running in teammate Button's tow. Button secured fourth and pushed hard in the final session which meant he had slight damage to his rear tyres on his first run, and could not get the optimum tyre temperature in the first section on his second run.<sup id="cite_ref-qualiquotes_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualiquotes-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Webber managed fifth and admitted that he was struggling to find a good rhythm when driving the circuit.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcquali_31-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcquali-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello qualified in sixth. The two Mercedes drivers took seventh and ninth; Rosberg ahead of Schumacher.<sup id="cite_ref-qualitimes_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualitimes-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Rosberg believed that he should have performed better in qualifying than in the practice session, as he felt the soft tyres lacked <a href="/wiki/Grip_(auto_racing)" title="Grip (auto racing)">grip</a>; Schumacher was satisfied with his qualifying performance. The Mercedes drivers were separated by Kubica, in the faster Renault, who was happy with his lap time despite his car sliding from a lack of grip.<sup id="cite_ref-qualiquotes_32-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualiquotes-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Kobayashi rounded out the top ten qualifiers.<sup id="cite_ref-qualitimes_33-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualitimes-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Felipe_Massa_2010_Singapore.jpg" class="image"><img alt="Felipe Massa driving a red Ferrari Formula One car in Singapore" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ae/Felipe_Massa_2010_Singapore.jpg/220px-Felipe_Massa_2010_Singapore.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="99" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ae/Felipe_Massa_2010_Singapore.jpg/330px-Felipe_Massa_2010_Singapore.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ae/Felipe_Massa_2010_Singapore.jpg/440px-Felipe_Massa_2010_Singapore.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3668" data-file-height="1653" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Felipe_Massa_2010_Singapore.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> used his ninth engine of the season after his gearbox failed in qualifying.</div></div></div>
<p>Alguersuari, who qualified eleventh, was the fastest driver not advancing to the final session. His best time of 1:47.666 was 1.8 seconds slower than Vettel's pace in the second session.<sup id="cite_ref-qualitimes_33-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualitimes-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Alguersuari's time was followed by Hülkenberg, who was backed up by Sutil on his first run in the second session, and described his car's balance like "night and day".<sup id="cite_ref-qualitimes_33-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualitimes-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> However, Hülkenberg was demoted five positions on the grid after the Williams team changed his gearbox after the race at Monza.<sup id="cite_ref-practice1_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-practice1-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> As a consequence, Petrov inherited 12th position having pushed hard on a set of option tyres. He had lost control of his car after he went deep, under braking, heading for turn five (a corner that was damp from the earlier rain shower), with his right-rear wheel hitting the wall.<sup id="cite_ref-qualiquotes_32-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualiquotes-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Petrov's crash meant he took no further part in qualifying.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcquali_31-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcquali-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> He was ahead of Buemi, in the slower of the two Toro Rossos, who lost a large amount of grip and got stuck in traffic.<sup id="cite_ref-qualiquotes_32-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualiquotes-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Heidfeld took 14th ahead of his fellow countryman Sutil in 15th.<sup id="cite_ref-qualitimes_33-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualitimes-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Liuzzi had a problem with his front brakes on the first run of the second session and managed 16th.<sup id="cite_ref-qualiquotes_32-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualiquotes-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Timo_Glock" title="Timo Glock">Timo Glock</a>, <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lucas_di_Grassi" title="Lucas di Grassi">Lucas di Grassi</a> were the quickest drivers unable to advance beyond the first part of qualifying. The eleventh row of the grid was filled by <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> and Klien. Senna took 23rd.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcquali_31-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcquali-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Massa was unable to set a timed lap as his gearbox seized without warning forcing him to stop on the circuit; Ferrari chose to replace his engine and gearbox as a precaution. Massa's engine change was his ninth of the season which incurred a ten-place grid penalty, but since he started from 24th position, the penalty was nullified.<sup id="cite_ref-Massa_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Massa-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_Singapore_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Fernando_Alonso_2010_Malaysia.jpg" class="image"><img alt="Fernando Alonso at the 2010 Malaysian Grand Prix" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a3/Fernando_Alonso_2010_Malaysia.jpg/170px-Fernando_Alonso_2010_Malaysia.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="229" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a3/Fernando_Alonso_2010_Malaysia.jpg/255px-Fernando_Alonso_2010_Malaysia.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a3/Fernando_Alonso_2010_Malaysia.jpg/340px-Fernando_Alonso_2010_Malaysia.jpg 2x" data-file-width="800" data-file-height="1078" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Fernando_Alonso_2010_Malaysia.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> won his fourth race of the season in Singapore and achieved the first Formula One Grand Chelem since 2004.</div></div></div>
<p>The race began at 20:00 <a href="/wiki/Singapore_Standard_Time" title="Singapore Standard Time">Singapore Standard Time</a> (<a href="/wiki/Coordinated_Universal_Time" title="Coordinated Universal Time">UTC</a>+8). The conditions on the grid were dry and cloudy before the race; the air temperature ranged between 29 to 32&#160;°C (84 to 90&#160;°F) and the track temperature was 31&#160;°C (88&#160;°F); no rain was predicted during the race. Most drivers started on the super-soft compound tyre.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-f1racing_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1racing-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Alguersuari started from the pit lane as his team discovered a coolant leak in his car.<sup id="cite_ref-sunquotes_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sunquotes-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> As the five red lights went out to signal the start of the race, Alonso maintained his pole position advantage heading into the first corner and withstood Vettel's attempt to pass him. Button got an early advantage over teammate Hamilton, but Hamilton fought him to retain third place.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello fell from sixth to eighth place. Kubica passed Rosberg at the start but the German regained sixth position in the same lap.<sup id="cite_ref-gpracenotes_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpracenotes-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Further down the field, the early momentum was broken when Heidfeld, attempting to overtake both Force India cars at turn seven, hit Sutil's rear, damaging his front wing.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-sunquotes_35-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sunquotes-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> Liuzzi subsequently went into Heidfeld's rear-end after the latter squeezed him towards the wall, causing front wing damage to his car.<sup id="cite_ref-sunquotes_35-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sunquotes-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> Heidfeld made a pit stop for a replacement front wing at the end of lap one,<sup id="cite_ref-gpracenotes_36-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpracenotes-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> while Massa made his sole pit stop for the medium compound tyres.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcwebrace_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcwebrace-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Liuzzi pulled over to the inside of the circuit on the third lap, at turn ten, with left-rear suspension damage resulting from contact with the wall. His retirement triggered the deployment of the <a href="/wiki/Safety_car" title="Safety car">safety car</a> as marshals were required to push his car away from the track.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-independentf1_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-independentf1-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> Webber was the only top running driver to pit for the medium compound tyres during the safety car period on lap four.<sup id="cite_ref-gpracenotes_36-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpracenotes-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Red Bull had told Webber to enter the pit lane, assuring him that it was the right decision, though he questioned the move.<sup id="cite_ref-sunquotes_35-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sunquotes-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> Several drivers towards the rear also chose to pit. Racing resumed at the end of lap five when the safety car pulled into the pit lane.<sup id="cite_ref-gpracenotes_36-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpracenotes-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Webber immediately pushed hard and passed Glock for tenth position and a line of cars began to form behind Glock. Alonso began to pull away from Vettel as he set consecutive <a href="/wiki/Fastest_lap" title="Fastest lap">fastest laps</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_2-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Webber passed Kobayashi at turn five to move into ninth on lap seven, and withstood an attempt to Kobayashi to reclaim ninth heading into turn seven.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcwebrace_37-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcwebrace-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Hülkenberg and Petrov's wheels made contact and both drivers went off the circuit on the same lap;<sup id="cite_ref-gpracenotes_36-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpracenotes-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Hülkenberg managed to move ahead of Petrov with Massa taking advantage to pass Petrov. Webber caught Schumacher on lap 11 and passed the latter on the same lap at turn five after Schumacher ran wide.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_2-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Vettel was told by his team to cool his brakes on the same lap as Alonso continued to extend his lead.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcwebrace_37-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcwebrace-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Sutil got ahead of Glock for eleventh place three laps later and started to pull away from the queue behind Glock. Hülkenberg ran wide at turn seven on the same lap but did not lose any positions. Hülkenberg passed Glock in the first sector of the track for twelfth on lap&#160;16.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_2-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Sebastian_Vettel_2010_Japan.jpg" class="image"><img alt="Sebastian Vettel at the 2010 Japanese Grand Prix" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8b/Sebastian_Vettel_2010_Japan.jpg/170px-Sebastian_Vettel_2010_Japan.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="247" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8b/Sebastian_Vettel_2010_Japan.jpg/255px-Sebastian_Vettel_2010_Japan.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8b/Sebastian_Vettel_2010_Japan.jpg/340px-Sebastian_Vettel_2010_Japan.jpg 2x" data-file-width="600" data-file-height="873" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Sebastian_Vettel_2010_Japan.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> finished second and moved up into fourth in the Drivers' Championship.</div></div></div>
<p>Vettel and Alonso traded fastest laps as they pushed hard in their efforts to build up a sufficient lead over Webber to maintain first and second positions after their pit stops.<sup id="cite_ref-gpracenotes_36-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpracenotes-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Massa, Petrov, Buemi and Alguersuari all passed Glock on lap 17 thus eliminating the queue of cars.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_2-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> By lap&#160;20, Alonso was leading Vettel by 3.2 seconds; he, in turn, was a further 11 seconds in front of Hamilton. Button was in fourth, 20 seconds behind Alonso, with Rosberg 3.3 seconds behind the World Champion in fifth.<sup id="cite_ref-gpracenotes_36-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpracenotes-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Webber changed his engine settings to give him a higher top speed in an attempt to get past Barrichello and decrease the time gap to Hamilton on lap 22. He avoided colliding with an Armco barrier at the entry of turn eighteen one lap later. Hamilton was told to increase his pace on the 24th lap to try to stay ahead of Webber after the pit stop phase. But his car had a large amount of <a href="/wiki/Understeer_and_oversteer" title="Understeer and oversteer">oversteer</a>, as his rear, super-soft, compound tyres began to deteriorate, causing his lap times to drop off the leader's pace.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_2-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-bbcwebrace_37-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcwebrace-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Trulli drove to the pit lane and was pushed by his mechanics into the Lotus garage to retire with a hydraulic issue on lap&#160;28. Hamilton made the first scheduled stop for tyres on lap&#160;29 and re-emerged in eighth position behind Webber.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_2-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Alonso and Vettel made simultaneous pit stops on the following lap; Alonso retained his lead despite Vettel's pit crew completing their pit stop first; Vettel avoided stalling and drove away in second gear.<sup id="cite_ref-gpracenotes_36-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpracenotes-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> With newer tyres which provided more grip, Vettel recorded faster lap times than Alonso to close the time gap as the two came across slower cars.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_2-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-bbcwebrace_37-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcwebrace-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Kobayashi attempted to pass around the outside of Schumacher on lap&#160;31 but the two made contact with Schumacher subsequently hitting a barrier.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_2-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-gpracenotes_36-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpracenotes-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Kobayashi damaged his Sauber, losing control of his rear-end, and crashed at the exit of turn 18 after his front wing was knocked off entering the corner. Senna was slow to react and buried his Hispania nose-first into the barrier alongside Kobayashi. These incidents called for the race's second safety car period to allow removal of both cars from the circuit which were on the racing line. Klien was pushed into his garage to retire with a hydraulic problem on lap&#160;34. The safety car pulled into the pit lane at the end of lap&#160;35 and the race resumed with Alonso leading.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_2-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton got a run on Webber who was caught behind di Grassi and lined up an overtaking manoeuvre on Webber,<sup id="cite_ref-gpracenotes_36-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpracenotes-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-independentf1_38-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-independentf1-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> and overtook him on the Raffles Boulevard straight. Webber's front-right tyre hit Hamilton's left-rear tyre at turn seven. He escaped without significant damage, while Hamilton had a punctured tyre and pulled off the circuit to retire for the second consecutive race.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcwebrace_37-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcwebrace-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> However, Webber felt vibrations on the front of his Red Bull car.<sup id="cite_ref-independentf1_38-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-independentf1-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> His tyre, which made contact with Hamilton's, had been pushed five millimetres (0.20&#160;in) off its normal mounting on the rim,<sup id="cite_ref-webbertyre_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-webbertyre-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> and Button drew closer to Webber who managed to maintain a good pace.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_2-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> On lap&#160;37, Schumacher ran into the rear of Heidfeld's car and limped back to the pit lane to replace his damaged front wing creating sparks as it was dragged along the surface of the track,<sup id="cite_ref-independentf1_38-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-independentf1-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> while Heidfeld retired after hitting a barrier. Button was urged by his McLaren mechanics on lap&#160;42 to push Webber whose tyres were older, while Vettel gradually closed the gap with Alonso.<sup id="cite_ref-gpracenotes_36-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpracenotes-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Hülkenberg, in ninth, made an error on lap&#160;44 which allowed Massa to momentarily take advantage, but he fought back to retain ninth position.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_2-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Kubica sustained a right-rear puncture on lap&#160;46 and made a pit stop on the same lap, rejoining in 13th.<sup id="cite_ref-gpracenotes_36-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpracenotes-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> 
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Mark_Webber_2010_Japan.jpg" class="image"><img alt="Mark Webber at the 2010 Japanese Grand Prix" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8d/Mark_Webber_2010_Japan.jpg/170px-Mark_Webber_2010_Japan.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="208" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8d/Mark_Webber_2010_Japan.jpg/255px-Mark_Webber_2010_Japan.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8d/Mark_Webber_2010_Japan.jpg/340px-Mark_Webber_2010_Japan.jpg 2x" data-file-width="800" data-file-height="978" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Mark_Webber_2010_Japan.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> finished third after stopping early for tyres and increased his championship lead.</div></div></div>
<p>Upfront, Vettel had reduced the gap between himself and Alonso to one second by lap&#160;50 as the pair continued to trade the fastest lap time.<sup id="cite_ref-gpracenotes_36-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpracenotes-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-bbcwebrace_37-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcwebrace-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Glock started to slow on the circuit and drove to the pit lane to retire.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_2-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> It was later confirmed that his retirement was the result of an issue with his car's hydraulic system.<sup id="cite_ref-sunquotes_35-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sunquotes-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> With the advantage of having newer tyres which gave him more grip, Kubica passed Alguersuari, Buemi, his teammate Petrov, Massa, Hülkenberg, and Sutil (who was holding up a queue of five cars) within nine laps. Vettel continued to pressure Alonso in the closing laps as the pair began to encounter slower cars.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_2-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Kovalainen and Buemi made contact in the final sector of the lap, with the latter spinning around in front of the Toro Rosso, who was quick to avoid a head-on collision. Kovalainen's car suffered a cracked fuel tank pressure release valve and he limped back to the pit lane, the rear of his Lotus catching fire during the final few turns. Kovalainen aborted the pit entry but did not make it much further as his car was consumed by the fire. Kovalainen stopped on the main straight and vacated his car to extinguish the fire himself after members of the Williams team handed him a fire extinguisher.<sup id="cite_ref-sunquotes_35-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sunquotes-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> As Kovalainen was off the racing line, yellow flags were waved in the final corners instead of a safety car deployment.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcwebrace_37-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcwebrace-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Vettel drew to within two-tenths of a second behind Alonso as the final lap started, and came across Sutil, Hülkenberg, Petrov and Massa; both drivers managed to get past Petrov, and Alonso maintained the first position heading into the final sector of the track.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_2-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The yellow flag for Kovalainen's car meant that Vettel's final chance of an overtake, into the final corner, was denied, and Alonso took the <a href="/wiki/Racing_flags#The_chequered_flag" title="Racing flags">checkered flag</a> on lap&#160;61 to win the race, 0.293 seconds ahead of Vettel.<sup id="cite_ref-gpraceresults_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpraceresults-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Alonso secured his first <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_driver_records#Pole,_win,_fastest_lap,_and_led_every_lap" title="List of Formula One driver records">Grand Chelem</a> (leading the entire race from pole position with the fastest lap) of his career as well as the first since Schumacher achieved the feat at the <a href="/wiki/2004_Hungarian_Grand_Prix" title="2004 Hungarian Grand Prix">2004 Hungarian Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-gpracenotes_36-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpracenotes-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-espnalonsowins_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-espnalonsowins-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> Webber was third, 1.2 seconds ahead of Button in fourth. Rosberg, Barrichello and Kubica took the next three positions.<sup id="cite_ref-gpraceresults_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpraceresults-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Sutil held off Hülkenberg in the final sector of the track for eighth and ninth on the line with Massa in tenth.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_2-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Petrov, Alguersuari, Schumacher and Buemi finished one lap behind Alonso, with di Grassi and Kovalainen (despite his retirement) the last of the classified finishers.<sup id="cite_ref-gpraceresults_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpraceresults-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_Singapore_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The top three drivers appeared on the podium to collect their trophies and spoke to the media in a later press conference.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_7-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Alonso said that his victory "meant a lot" for him to remain in contention for the Drivers' Championship and thanked his team for their efforts during the weekend. Alonso added that Ferrari would give "100&#160;per cent" in the season's four remaining races and said the battle remained "very tight" despite scoring the most points out of all the drivers in the previous five races.<sup id="cite_ref-pressconference_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconference-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> Vettel said that he was hoping that Alonso would make a mistake but acknowledged that it was difficult to overtake on the circuit. He also said that attempting to overtake Alonso in the final stages of the race was "the only chance" that he had of winning the race and hoped that the remaining tracks would suit his Red Bull car.<sup id="cite_ref-pressconference_41-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconference-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> Webber stated that he was "very happy" with his third-place finish and said the entire race weekend was the toughest of the year on him. He also said that he did not feel comfortable throughout the weekend and that he was staying composed through certain sections of the race.<sup id="cite_ref-pressconference_41-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconference-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Alonso's win has been regarded as one of the best of his Formula One career.<sup id="cite_ref-espnalonsowins_40-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-espnalonsowins-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-saunderscrashnet_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-saunderscrashnet-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> Former Formula One driver <a href="/wiki/Martin_Brundle" title="Martin Brundle">Martin Brundle</a> described it as: "one of those races in which two drivers elevate themselves on to a separate plane from their rivals." It reminded him of the <a href="/wiki/2000_Japanese_Grand_Prix" title="2000 Japanese Grand Prix">2000 Japanese Grand Prix</a> where Schumacher won that year's world championship from his main rival <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-brundlebbc_43-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-brundlebbc-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> Will Saunders of <i>crash.net</i> said in 2014 that Alonso: "had little right to wrestle a performance of such magnitude out of either himself or the Ferrari, yet he found another level that day." After the eventual conclusion of the championship in Vettel's favour by four points, Saunders said: "Alonso's performance in Singapore showed he would have been an equally deserving championship victor."<sup id="cite_ref-saunderscrashnet_42-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-saunderscrashnet-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/ESPN" title="ESPN">ESPN</a></i> listed it as one of Alonso's top ten races while driving for Ferrari.<sup id="cite_ref-espnalonsowins_40-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-espnalonsowins-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The stewards immediately investigated the accident between Webber and Hamilton on lap 37&#160;and took no further action.<sup id="cite_ref-independentf1_38-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-independentf1-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton later reviewed the incident on video and was adamant that he was not at fault saying that he was "unlucky" for the second consecutive Grand Prix.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> Webber stated the contact nearly forced him to retire and compared the accident to Hamilton's collision with Massa at the Italian Grand Prix.<sup id="cite_ref-pressconference_41-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconference-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> Brundle felt Hamilton should have given Webber more space and agreed with the stewards' decision.<sup id="cite_ref-brundlebbc_43-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-brundlebbc-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> Bridgestone director of motorsport tyre development <a href="/wiki/Hirohide_Hamashima" title="Hirohide Hamashima">Hirohide Hamashima</a> stated Webber was "very lucky" to make the finish and that if left hand high-speed corners were installed on the track, the tyre would have moved more frequently and lost its pressure. Horner had also admitted that Webber had been lucky with his tyre lasting for a long distance.<sup id="cite_ref-webbertyre_39-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-webbertyre-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Schumacher and Heidfeld's accident on lap 37 was investigated by the stewards who took no further action after deciding it was a "racing incident".<sup id="cite_ref-independentf1_38-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-independentf1-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> Heidfeld felt Schumacher had braked too late and hoped that he would have had a better finishing position.<sup id="cite_ref-sunquotes_35-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sunquotes-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher himself said that he was unhappy with his first race in Singapore but enjoyed the second half of the race.<sup id="cite_ref-sunquotes_35-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sunquotes-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/BBC_Sport" title="BBC Sport">BBC</a> pundit and former team principal, <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Jordan" title="Eddie Jordan">Eddie Jordan</a> was highly critical of Schumacher arguing that the latter should have been sacked by Mercedes to prevent him from being "slaughtered" in the event he was unable to win another race.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> Mercedes team principal <a href="/wiki/Ross_Brawn" title="Ross Brawn">Ross Brawn</a> came to Schumacher's defense and said his team was "happy" with his contribution.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> Motorsport pundits criticised Kovalainen's decision to stop his car on the pit straight after it had caught fire and argued that he was "irresponsible". Kovalainen defended his action arguing that it had not been safe to drive his Lotus into the pitlane.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> Nevertheless, Kovalainen's fire was voted the <a href="/wiki/Autosport_Awards#Moment_of_the_Year" title="Autosport Awards">Moment of the Year</a> by readers of <i><a href="/wiki/Autosport" title="Autosport">Autosport</a></i> magazine in December 2010 at the Autosport Awards held in London.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Sutil was given a 20-second time penalty by the stewards as he was deemed to gain an advantage by taking the outside line at turn seven on the first lap. This demoted him from eighth to tenth and promoted Hülkenberg from ninth to eighth and Massa from tenth to ninth.<sup id="cite_ref-sutilpenalty_49-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sutilpenalty-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> Following a protest from Force India, Hülkenberg was issued a 20-second time penalty after the stewards deemed him to have gained an advantage by corner-cutting. This ruling demoted Hülkenberg to tenth while Massa moved to eighth and Sutil inherited ninth. Force India withdrew an appeal of Sutil's penalty following the decision,<sup id="cite_ref-hulkenbergpenalty_50-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hulkenbergpenalty-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> and Williams technical director <a href="/wiki/Sam_Michael" title="Sam Michael">Sam Michael</a> said that his team had accepted the stewards' judgement.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> The stewards elected not to penalise Senna after examining a video of his collision with the stationary Kobayashi.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup> Kobayashi admitted that he was at fault and Senna believed the marshals were late reacting to Kobayashi's accident and felt his own crash was unavoidable.<sup id="cite_ref-sunquotes_35-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sunquotes-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As a consequence of the race, Webber increased his lead in the World Drivers' Championship. Alonso's victory moved him into second place, eleven points behind Webber. Hamilton's retirement dropped him to third place, nine points behind Alonso. Vettel's second-place finish moved him to fourth place, ahead of Button.<sup id="cite_ref-points_9-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In the World Constructors' Championship, Red Bull drew further ahead of McLaren who was now twenty-four points behind. Ferrari remained in third on 319 points, forty points behind McLaren. Mercedes increased their points advantage over Renault to thirty-five with four races remaining in the season.<sup id="cite_ref-points_9-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
