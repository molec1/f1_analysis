<p>The <b>1971 United States Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on October 3, 1971, at the <a href="/wiki/Watkins_Glen_International" title="Watkins Glen International">Watkins Glen Grand Prix Race Course</a> in <a href="/wiki/Watkins_Glen,_New_York" title="Watkins Glen, New York">Watkins Glen, New York</a>. It was race 11 of 11 in both the <a href="/wiki/1971_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1971 World Championship of Drivers">1971 World Championship of Drivers</a> and the <a href="/wiki/1971_International_Cup_for_Formula_One_Manufacturers" class="mw-redirect" title="1971 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers">1971 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The 59-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_Racing" title="Tyrrell Racing">Tyrrell</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Fran%C3%A7ois_Cevert" title="François Cevert">François Cevert</a> after he started from fifth position. <a href="/wiki/Jo_Siffert" title="Jo Siffert">Jo Siffert</a> finished second for the <a href="/wiki/British_Racing_Motors" title="British Racing Motors">BRM</a> team and <a href="/wiki/March_Engineering" title="March Engineering">March</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Ronnie_Peterson" title="Ronnie Peterson">Ronnie Peterson</a> came in third.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Summary">Summary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1971_United_States_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Summary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Jackie_Stewart" title="Jackie Stewart">Jackie Stewart</a>'s domination in 1971 clinched his second Driver's Championship with three races remaining, but the final round belonged to his <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_Racing" title="Tyrrell Racing">Tyrrell</a> teammate, <a href="/wiki/Fran%C3%A7ois_Cevert" title="François Cevert">François Cevert</a>. The Frenchman took the lead from Stewart on lap 14 and went on to claim his only career win, the first GP victory for a French driver since <a href="/wiki/Maurice_Trintignant" title="Maurice Trintignant">Maurice Trintignant</a> in 1958.
</p>
<blockquote>
<p><i>As usual, the American race attracted a large field of entrants, despite it being the last race of the year and both Championships having long been wrapped up. It seemed nearly every spare works F1 car and quite a few independents, as well, were present to try for a share of the $267,000 in prize money, easily the richest purse in F1. Unfortunately, the two most popular American drivers, <a href="/wiki/Mario_Andretti" title="Mario Andretti">Mario Andretti</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mark_Donohue" title="Mark Donohue">Mark Donohue</a>, who was fresh from a stunning third place finish in <a href="/wiki/1971_Canadian_Grand_Prix" title="1971 Canadian Grand Prix">Canada</a> in his Formula One debut, were committed to drive on Sunday in a USAC race which had incomprehensibly been rescheduled to the GP weekend after a previous rainout. The two drivers qualified, Andretti in a <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> and Donohue in a <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> shared with <a href="/wiki/David_Hobbs_(racing_driver)" title="David Hobbs (racing driver)">David Hobbs</a>, hoping for more rain in New Jersey and the chance to return for the race on Sunday.</i>
</p><p><i>Since the previous year's race, the course had been resurfaced, widened and, most significantly, lengthened by a mile to 3.377 miles with an entirely new section at the southwest corner called the "Boot" or "Anvil". The pits were also moved from the north end straight back before the right angle turn known as "The 90," which now became Turn One.</i>
</p>
</blockquote>
<p>Friday was sunny and hot (105&#160;°F (41&#160;°C)), and Stewart jumped immediately to the top of the charts with a time of 1:42.844, as the times were recorded to a thousandth of a second for the first time. On Saturday, with the temperature now 110° and both <a href="/wiki/Goodyear_Tire_and_Rubber_Company" title="Goodyear Tire and Rubber Company">Goodyear</a>'s and <a href="/wiki/Firestone_Tire_and_Rubber_Company" title="Firestone Tire and Rubber Company">Firestone</a>'s qualifying tires breaking down after a few laps, <a href="/wiki/Emerson_Fittipaldi" title="Emerson Fittipaldi">Emerson Fittipaldi</a> pipped Stewart's time from the day before, but the Scot returned to the track and grabbed the pole by .017 of a second. <a href="/wiki/Denny_Hulme" title="Denny Hulme">Denny Hulme</a> joined them on the front row in his McLaren, followed by <a href="/wiki/Clay_Regazzoni" title="Clay Regazzoni">Clay Regazzoni</a>'s Ferrari, Cevert and the soon-to-be-absent Andretti. American <a href="/wiki/Peter_Revson" title="Peter Revson">Peter Revson</a> qualified nineteenth in the third Tyrrell. It was his only race for Tyrrell and his first Grand Prix since 1964.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1971_United_States_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Sunday was dry in both upstate New York and Trenton, and word came that the USAC race would go on, dismaying both the crowd and the organizers, who were robbed of seeing two of the country's best road racers. At the start, Hulme jumped into the lead, ahead of Cevert and Stewart, but by the end of the first lap, Stewart led Hulme, Cevert, Regazzoni, <a href="/wiki/Jo_Siffert" title="Jo Siffert">Jo Siffert</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jacky_Ickx" title="Jacky Ickx">Jacky Ickx</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chris_Amon" title="Chris Amon">Chris Amon</a> and Fittipaldi.
</p><p>At first, Stewart was able to open a gap back to the following group, now headed by his teammate, but after ten laps, his tires began to go off and the gap closed. The Scot realized that Cevert's Goodyears were holding up much better in the heat, and when Cevert closed up right behind him, he waved him by on lap 14. Hulme was now struggling with a terrible vibration in his tires and was passed, first by Ickx, then Regazzoni and Siffert. On lap 15, American <a href="/wiki/Sam_Posey" title="Sam Posey">Sam Posey</a>, in his first Grand Prix, retired from a fine run with a blown piston in his <a href="/wiki/Surtees" title="Surtees">Surtees</a>. By the time Ickx could get around Stewart on lap 17, Cevert's lead was 5.7 seconds.
</p>
<blockquote>
<p><i>At about half-distance, Cevert finally began to struggle with the same understeer that had plagued Stewart much earlier. Ickx was closing, and his Firestones were getting better as the race went on. On lap 43, the Belgian set the fastest lap of the race, and the gap was down to 2.2 seconds. Then, on lap 49, the Ferrari's alternator fell off, punching a hole in the gearbox and spilling oil all over the track! Hulme hit the oil and spun into the barrier, bending his front suspension. He was standing beside the track when Cevert came by and also hit the barrier, but kept going, now 29 seconds in the lead!</i>
</p>
</blockquote>
<p>Jo Siffert was now in second place and 33 seconds clear of <a href="/wiki/Ronnie_Peterson" title="Ronnie Peterson">Ronnie Peterson</a>. With four laps to go, however, Siffert began to run low on fuel. The Swede took huge chunks off the gap as Siffert jerked the <a href="/wiki/British_Racing_Motors" title="British Racing Motors">BRM</a> from side to side, trying to use every remaining drop of fuel. Cevert coasted home, taking both hands off the wheel to wave as he crossed the line, and Siffert weaved his way around to hold second place by four seconds over Peterson's <a href="/wiki/March_Engineering" title="March Engineering">March</a>.
</p><p>After taking the checkered flag, Cevert gave a nod to his teammate. "I feel pretty good with a $50,000 win. I followed Stewart in the beginning and was flagged on ahead. Jackie Stewart is a very sensible driver and a very good teacher. He let me go through." While it was the first race on the expanded Watkins Glen track, it was the third year in a row that The Glen had rewarded a driver with his first career victory.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1971_United_States_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>The win, naturally, was the high point of Cevert's career, which would end at The Glen <a href="/wiki/1973_United_States_Grand_Prix" title="1973 United States Grand Prix">two years later</a>, just as he was set to take over the Number One drive at Tyrrell from the retiring Stewart.</li>
<li>This was Jo Siffert's last race, as he was killed violently 3 weeks later in a <a href="/wiki/1971_World_Championship_Victory_Race" title="1971 World Championship Victory Race">non-championship race</a> at Brands Hatch in the UK.</li>
<li>It was also <a href="/wiki/Jo_Bonnier" title="Jo Bonnier">Jo Bonnier</a>'s last race, as he would die next year at the <a href="/wiki/1972_24_Hours_of_Le_Mans" title="1972 24 Hours of Le Mans">24 Hours of Le Mans</a>.</li></ul>
