<p>The <b>2008 Hungarian Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>Formula 1 ING Magyar Nagydíj 2008</b><sup id="cite_ref-programmecover_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-programmecover-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 3 August 2008, at the <a href="/wiki/Hungaroring" title="Hungaroring">Hungaroring</a> in <a href="/wiki/Mogyor%C3%B3d" title="Mogyoród">Mogyoród</a>, near <a href="/wiki/Budapest" title="Budapest">Budapest</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hungary" title="Hungary">Hungary</a>. It was the 11th race of the <a href="/wiki/2008_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2008 Formula One season">2008 Formula One season</a>. Contested over 70&#160;laps, the race was won by <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> for the <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> team, from a second position start. <a href="/wiki/Timo_Glock" title="Timo Glock">Timo Glock</a> finished second in a <a href="/wiki/Toyota_Racing_(Formula_One_team)" title="Toyota Racing (Formula One team)">Toyota</a> car, with <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> third in a <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>. It was Kovalainen's first Formula One victory, which made him the sport's 100th driver to win a World Championship race, and it was Glock's first podium finish. It also turned out to be the only F1 race Kovalainen ever won.
</p><p>The majority of the race consisted of a duel between <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a>, who drove for McLaren and Ferrari, respectively. Hamilton started from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> but was beaten at the first corner by Massa, who passed him around the outside. The two championship rivals began a battle for the lead that was resolved when Hamilton sustained a <a href="/wiki/Flat_tire" title="Flat tire">punctured tyre</a> just over halfway through the race, giving Massa a lead of more than 20&#160;seconds over Kovalainen. The Ferrari's engine, however, failed with three laps remaining, allowing the McLaren driver to win. Räikkönen set the race's <a href="/wiki/Fastest_lap" title="Fastest lap">fastest lap</a> in the other Ferrari, but was hampered by a poor qualifying performance and was stuck behind <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> (<a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>) and Glock in turn for almost all of the race.
</p><p>As a consequence of the race, Hamilton extended his lead in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">World Drivers' Championship</a> to five&#160;points over Räikkönen, with Massa a further three behind. <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a>, who finished eighth after finding his <a href="/wiki/BMW_in_Formula_One" title="BMW in Formula One">BMW Sauber</a> car uncompetitive at the Hungaroring, slipped to 13&#160;points behind Hamilton, ahead of teammate <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> and Kovalainen. In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">World Constructors' Championship</a>, McLaren passed BMW Sauber for second&#160;position, 11&#160;points behind Ferrari.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2008_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Grand Prix was contested by 20&#160;drivers in ten teams of two.<sup id="cite_ref-pg200-201_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg200-201-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The teams, also known as "<a href="/wiki/Formula_One#Constructors" title="Formula One">constructors</a>", were <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Mercedes_AMG_High_Performance_Powertrains" title="Mercedes AMG High Performance Powertrains">Mercedes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>, <a href="/wiki/Honda_in_Formula_One" title="Honda in Formula One">Honda</a>, <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a>-Ferrari, <a href="/wiki/BMW_in_Formula_One" title="BMW in Formula One">BMW Sauber</a>, <a href="/wiki/Toyota_Racing_(Formula_One_team)" title="Toyota Racing (Formula One team)">Toyota</a>, <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a>-Renault, <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a>-Toyota and <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso</a>-Ferrari.<sup id="cite_ref-pg200-201_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg200-201-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Before the race, Ferrari was in the lead of the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a> with 105&#160;points, 16 ahead of BMW Sauber and a further three in front of McLaren. Vying for fourth place were Toyota, Red Bull and Renault, all within two points of each other but more than 60 points behind McLaren.<sup id="cite_ref-pg190-191_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg190-191-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> (McLaren) led with 58&#160;<a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Championship_points_scoring_systems" title="List of Formula One World Championship points scoring systems">points</a>, ahead of Ferrari teammates <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a>, who were on 54 and 51&#160;points respectively. BMW drivers <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a> (48) and <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> (41) were fourth and fifth in the championship, followed by <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a>, who had scored 28&#160;points in the second McLaren.<sup id="cite_ref-pg190-191_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg190-191-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Following the <a href="/wiki/2008_German_Grand_Prix" title="2008 German Grand Prix">German Grand Prix</a> on July 20, the teams conducted testing sessions at the <a href="/wiki/Circuito_Permanente_de_Jerez" class="mw-redirect" title="Circuito Permanente de Jerez">Jerez</a> circuit from July 22–25.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Each team was limited to 30,000 kilometres (19,000&#160;mi) of testing during the 2008 calendar year, a reduction compared with previous seasons.<sup id="cite_ref-sporting_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sporting-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> (Toro Rosso) set the fastest time of the first and second days, while <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> (Red Bull) topped the third day's running, and <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> was fastest on the final day of testing. Several teams tested using <a href="/wiki/Bridgestone" title="Bridgestone">Bridgestone</a> <a href="/wiki/Slick_tyre" class="mw-redirect" title="Slick tyre">slick tyres</a>, as preparation for the switch from grooved to slick tyres for the <a href="/wiki/2009_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2009 Formula One season">2009 season</a>, and <a href="/wiki/BMW_in_Formula_One" title="BMW in Formula One">BMW Sauber</a> tested a <a href="/wiki/Kinetic_Energy_Recovery_Systems" class="mw-redirect" title="Kinetic Energy Recovery Systems">Kinetic Energy Recovery System</a>, also for the following year.<sup id="cite_ref-test1_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-test1-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-test2_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-test2-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-test3_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-test3-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-test4_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-test4-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Among the other teams, Force India's test driver, <a href="/wiki/Vitantonio_Liuzzi" title="Vitantonio Liuzzi">Vitantonio Liuzzi</a>, tested the team's new "seamless-shift" <a href="/wiki/Transmission_(mechanics)" title="Transmission (mechanics)">gearbox</a> ahead of the system's race début later in the year, while <a href="/wiki/Timo_Glock" title="Timo Glock">Timo Glock</a> of Toyota took part after a heavy crash at the German Grand Prix.<sup id="cite_ref-test2_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-test2-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-test3_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-test3-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-test4_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-test4-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Fiat_Luca_cordero_di_Montezemolo.jpg" class="image"><img alt="Upper body of a man in his sixties with neatly combed, brown but greying hair, wearing a smart grey suit and navy tie with small white spots. <p>In the week leading up to the race, a meeting between the teams at Ferrari's headquarters in <a href="/wiki/Maranello" title="Maranello">Maranello</a> resulted in the formation of a new representative body, the <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_Teams_Association" title="Formula One Teams Association">Formula One Teams Association</a> (FOTA), which was led by Ferrari president <a href="/wiki/Luca_di_Montezemolo" class="mw-redirect" title="Luca di Montezemolo">Luca di Montezemolo</a>. McLaren team principal <a href="/wiki/Ron_Dennis" title="Ron Dennis">Ron Dennis</a> said the establishment of FOTA was intended to encourage greater co-operation between the teams, particularly in framing new sporting and technical regulations, and to act as a counterweight to the sport's existing governing body, the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile</a> (FIA) and the company responsible for its commercial management, <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_Management" class="mw-redirect" title="Formula One Management">Formula One Management</a> (FOM).<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On July 31, the day before the event's first free practice sessions took place, McLaren confirmed the team would retain Kovalainen for 2009 alongside Hamilton, while the organisers of the Hungarian Grand Prix signed a deal with <a href="/wiki/Bernie_Ecclestone" title="Bernie Ecclestone">Bernie Ecclestone</a>, the president of FOM, to continue hosting the race until 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-IB2_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IB2-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Several teams made technical changes to their cars for the Grand Prix. Ferrari altered the <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_F2008" title="Ferrari F2008">F2008</a> chassis's cooling system and bodywork following high brake wear and engine water temperatures at the German Grand Prix. McLaren and Force India introduced revised <a href="/wiki/Aerodynamics" title="Aerodynamics">aerodynamic</a> packages for their <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4-23" title="McLaren MP4-23">MP4-23</a> and <a href="/wiki/Force_India_VJM01" class="mw-redirect" title="Force India VJM01">VJM01</a> chassis, aimed at increasing the amount of <a href="/wiki/Downforce" title="Downforce">downforce</a>, and therefore grip, produced by the bodywork. Force India also brought its seamless-shift gearbox to the event.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Ferrari, Honda and Toyota also débuted raised engine covers, nicknamed "shark-fins" for the way they stretched toward the rear wing, and Honda introduced a new rear suspension package.<sup id="cite_ref-IB2_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IB2-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-TBT_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBT-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The sport's sole tyre supplier, Bridgestone, provided two specifications of grooved dry tyres for the race, designated Soft (also referred to as the "prime" tyre) and Super Soft (also referred to as the "option" tyre). The Super Soft compound was distinguished by a white stripe in one of the tyre's grooves. As was the case for all of the 2008 Grands Prix, the rules stipulated that all cars should use both types of tyre during the course of the race, and each driver was limited to seven sets of dry tyres for the weekend.<sup id="cite_ref-pg200-201_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg200-201-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-sporting_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sporting-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice">Practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2008_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Three practice sessions were held before the Sunday race—two on Friday from 10:00 to 11:30 and 14:00 to 15:30 local time, and a third on Saturday morning between 11:00 and 12:00.<sup id="cite_ref-sporting_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sporting-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The first practice session took place in dry conditions. The ambient temperature was between 27–28&#160;°C (81–82&#160;°F), and the track temperature ranged from 31–34&#160;°C (88–93&#160;°F) during the hour-long period.<sup id="cite_ref-pg200-201_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg200-201-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Massa set the session's fastest time with a lap of 1&#160;minute and 20.981&#160;seconds, almost four-tenths of a second ahead of his teammate Räikkönen. The two McLaren drivers were third and fourth, Kovalainen ahead of Hamilton. <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet_Jr." title="Nelson Piquet Jr.">Nelson Piquet Jr.</a> set the fifth and eighth-fastest times respectively for Renault; they were separated by Glock and Kubica. Their teammates, Heidfeld and Toyota driver <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a>, completed the top ten.<sup id="cite_ref-pg200-201_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg200-201-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Vettel's Toro Rosso car was afflicted by a hydraulics problem;<sup id="cite_ref-TBT_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBT-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> this restricted him to completing only four timed laps, and he was slowest overall.<sup id="cite_ref-pg200-201_1-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg200-201-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Sebastian_Vettel_2008.jpg" class="image"><img alt="Head, arms and torso of a man in his early twenties with slightly tousled blond hair. <p>The second practice session was held in similar weather to the first; the only difference was a slightly higher peak track temperature of 37&#160;°C (99&#160;°F).<sup id="cite_ref-pg200-201_1-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg200-201-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> During this session, Hamilton set the quickest lap time of the day, a 1:20.554; Kovalainen finished with the third-fastest time.<sup id="cite_ref-pg200-201_1-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg200-201-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The Renault drivers were again quick—Piquet in second and Alonso fourth—although the team's Executive Director of Engineering, <a href="/wiki/Pat_Symonds" title="Pat Symonds">Pat Symonds</a>, admitted both cars were running with slightly lower fuel loads than normal, improving their performance.<sup id="cite_ref-TBT_15-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBT-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Räikkönen and Massa slipped to fifth and sixth respectively, their best times one-thousandth of a second apart. They were ahead of Heidfeld, Kubica, Trulli and Williams driver <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pg200-201_1-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg200-201-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Vettel's car was still suffering from the hydraulics problem and he completed just five laps, again setting the slowest time of the session.<sup id="cite_ref-pg200-201_1-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg200-201-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-TBT_15-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBT-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Saturday's weather was again dry for the third and final practice session, with ambient temperature between 27–29&#160;°C (81–84&#160;°F) and track temperature from 32–36&#160;°C (90–97&#160;°F).<sup id="cite_ref-pg200-201_1-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg200-201-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton again set the fastest time, a 1:20.228, quicker than his best on Friday. This put him immediately ahead of Massa, Glock, Kovalainen and Piquet.<sup id="cite_ref-pg200-201_1-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg200-201-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Heidfeld was much happier with the <a href="/wiki/Racing_setup" title="Racing setup">setup</a> of his car and set the sixth-fastest time, but Kubica suffered a mechanical problem that restricted him to 18th&#160;position.<sup id="cite_ref-TBT_15-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBT-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Vettel had a trouble-free session and set the eighth-fastest time, one position behind teammate <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9bastien_Bourdais" title="Sébastien Bourdais">Sébastien Bourdais</a>. Räikkönen and Rosberg completed the top ten ahead of qualifying.<sup id="cite_ref-pg200-201_1-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg200-201-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Lewis_Hamilton_2.jpg" class="image"><img alt="Head and shoulders of a man in his twenties, who has close-cropped black hair and is showing his teeth as he smiles while signing something unseen. <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2008_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Saturday afternoon's qualifying session was divided into three&#160;parts. In the first 20-minute period, cars finishing 16th or lower were eliminated. The second qualifying period lasted 15&#160;minutes, at the end of which the fastest 10 cars went into the final period to determine their grid positions for the race. Cars failing to make the final period were allowed to be refuelled before the race, but those competing in it were not and carried more fuel than they had done in the earlier qualifying sessions to see them through the first part of the race.<sup id="cite_ref-sporting_6-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sporting-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The session was held in dry weather slightly hotter than any of the free practice sessions; the ambient temperature ranged between 30 and 31&#160;°C (86 and 88&#160;°F), while track temperature ranged between 38 and 41&#160;°C (100 and 106&#160;°F).<sup id="cite_ref-pg200-201_1-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg200-201-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatright" style="width:40%;&#32;;"><style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r887775652">.mw-parser-output .quotebox{background-color:#F9F9F9;border:1px solid #aaa;box-sizing:border-box;padding:10px;font-size:88%;max-width:100%}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft{margin:0.5em 1.4em 0.8em 0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright{margin:0.5em 0 0.8em 1.4em}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.centered{margin:0.5em auto 0.8em auto}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft p,.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright p{font-style:inherit}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-title{background-color:#F9F9F9;text-align:center;font-size:larger;font-weight:bold}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:before{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" “ ";vertical-align:-45%;line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:after{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" ” ";line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .left-aligned{text-align:left}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .right-aligned{text-align:right}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .center-aligned{text-align:center}.mw-parser-output .quotebox cite{display:block;font-style:normal}@media screen and (max-width:360px){.mw-parser-output .quotebox{min-width:100%;margin:0 0 0.8em!important;float:none!important}}</style>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">"The team has done a fantastic job of continually improving the car over the past few weeks, so I'm really proud of what we've achieved today. It's great to have locked out the front row with Heikki—we've been threatening to do it for a number of races, so to achieve it at a track where it's tricky to pass is really satisfying. There's a great harmony within the team at the moment and we really deserved this. I couldn't ask to be in a better position, we've both got good strategies for tomorrow and we'll be challenging for the win."</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style=""><a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>, commenting on taking <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>Hamilton set the fastest time in the first and final parts of the session, which clinched him <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> with a lap of 1:20.899.<sup id="cite_ref-pg200-201_1-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg200-201-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Although he was pleased with the handling of his McLaren—he said that he had never been more comfortable in the car—he believed that he could have recorded a faster lap, as he made a slight mistake going into Turn Five.<sup id="cite_ref-qualifying_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualifying-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton was joined on the front row by his teammate Kovalainen, who recorded a lap time 0.241&#160;seconds slower and was fuelled for an additional two laps in the race.<sup id="cite_ref-pg194_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg194-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Massa set the session's fastest time of 1:19.068 during its second part, but was delayed by other cars, which prevented him from heating his tyres sufficiently to achieve the maximum grip possible. He dropped to third overall in the final part of qualifying. Räikkönen was on a heavier fuel load than his teammate but made a mistake on his final flying lap that restricted him to sixth&#160;place.<sup id="cite_ref-qualifying_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualifying-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> That left him behind Kubica and Glock on the grid. Kubica achieved his competitive time despite handling problems, which led him to describe his lap as his best so far of the season,<sup id="cite_ref-pg194_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg194-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> while Glock recorded the best qualifying result of his career thus far.<sup id="cite_ref-qualifying_18-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualifying-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Alonso qualified in seventh&#160;position with Piquet in tenth on a heavy fuel load; the Renault teammates were split by Webber and Trulli.<sup id="cite_ref-qualifying_18-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualifying-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> As the Soft tyres were expected to perform better in the race than the Super Softs, the McLaren drivers' use of one fewer set of the Soft tyres than their Ferrari counterparts during the qualifying session suggested Hamilton and Kovalainen might have had a tyre performance advantage in the race. This was because the Soft tyre was the fastest tyre choice over the course of a single lap, despite the theoretical performance advantage of the Super Soft; Ferrari used one more set of Soft tyres than McLaren before realising this was the case.<sup id="cite_ref-ASpg40_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ASpg40-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Vettel was the fastest driver not to advance into the final session, qualifying 11th; his best time of 1:20.131 was just over a second slower than Massa's pace in the second session.<sup id="cite_ref-pg200-201_1-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg200-201-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> His teammate, Bourdais, set the 14th-fastest lap, but was penalised five positions on the grid for impeding Heidfeld during the first part of qualifying, a delay which limited the BMW Sauber driver to the 16th-fastest time.<sup id="cite_ref-TBT_15-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBT-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> The Toro Rosso drivers were split before Bourdais' penalty by <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a>—who found his Honda's revised suspension a significant improvement—and <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a>, who believed the Hungaroring did not suit the handling characteristics of his <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_RB4" title="Red Bull RB4">Red Bull RB4</a> chassis.<sup id="cite_ref-TBT_15-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBT-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-qualifying_18-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualifying-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Rosberg made it into the second part of qualifying, but did not complete any laps thereafter after his Williams car developed a hydraulics problem.<sup id="cite_ref-qualifying_18-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualifying-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Kazuki_Nakajima" title="Kazuki Nakajima">Kazuki Nakajima</a> (Williams), <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> (Honda) and Force India drivers <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> and <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a> failed to advance beyond the first part of qualifying, and thus completed the final rows of the grid. In the first part of qualifying (the only section in which all drivers took part), the entire field was covered by just under three&#160;seconds.<sup id="cite_ref-pg200-201_1-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg200-201-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2008_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The race took place in the afternoon from 14:00 local time, in dry and sunny weather, with an ambient temperature of between 30 and 31&#160;°C (86 and 88&#160;°F), and a track temperature ranging from 40 to 43&#160;°C (104 to 109&#160;°F). The race-day attendance was 84,000.<sup id="cite_ref-official_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-official-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> As usual, the race was broadcast worldwide, with the <a href="/wiki/Formula_One#Television" title="Formula One">"World Feed"</a> coverage being produced by FOM. Every driver except Coulthard started on the Soft compound tyres.<sup id="cite_ref-pg200-201_1-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg200-201-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Massa made a good start on his only remaining new set of Soft tyres, moving ahead of Kovalainen from the grid and drawing alongside Hamilton into the first corner. Hamilton held the inside line for the turn, but Massa braked later than the McLaren driver and passed him around the outside. Behind the leading three in the run down to the first corner, Glock moved ahead of Kubica, while Alonso overtook Räikkönen.<sup id="cite_ref-ASpg40_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ASpg40-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello made the best start in the field, moving from 17th to 13th&#160;place at the end of the first lap, while Vettel made a poor start and lost four places over the same distance. At the completion of the first&#160;lap, Massa led from Hamilton, Kovalainen, Glock, Kubica, Alonso, Räikkönen, Webber, Trulli, Piquet, Coulthard, Heidfeld, Barrichello, Button, Vettel, Bourdais, Rosberg, Nakajima, Fisichella and Sutil.<sup id="cite_ref-pg200-201_1-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg200-201-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Heikki_Kovalainen_portrait(cropped).jpg" class="image"><img alt="Head and shoulders of a man in his twenties as he speaks into a microphone. He is wearing a bright red, Vodafone-branded baseball cap which almost obscures his pale blond hair, and a white T-shirt which bears the Mercedes-Benz, Vodafone and Mobil 1 logos." src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/58/Heikki_Kovalainen_portrait%28cropped%29.jpg/170px-Heikki_Kovalainen_portrait%28cropped%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="225" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/58/Heikki_Kovalainen_portrait%28cropped%29.jpg/255px-Heikki_Kovalainen_portrait%28cropped%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/58/Heikki_Kovalainen_portrait%28cropped%29.jpg/340px-Heikki_Kovalainen_portrait%28cropped%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="522" data-file-height="692" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Heikki_Kovalainen_portrait(cropped).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> benefited from problems that afflicted pace-setters Hamilton and <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> to take the only win of his Formula One career.</div></div></div>
<p>Massa and Hamilton immediately began to pull clear of Kovalainen.<sup id="cite_ref-ASpg40_20-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ASpg40-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> On lap&#160;3, Button overtook his teammate Barrichello for 13th position, but both Honda drivers were stuck behind Heidfeld, who was carrying a heavier fuel load than them.<sup id="cite_ref-TBT_15-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBT-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> As the race progressed, Massa began to open a small lead over Hamilton, who put his McLaren into a "fuel-saving mode", attempting to jump ahead of Massa later in the race by making a pit stop after the Ferrari driver.<sup id="cite_ref-pg195_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg195-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> In addition, the high track temperature was to the Ferrari chassis's advantage, as it was easier on its tyres than the McLaren and was able to run them at an <a href="/wiki/Operating_temperature" title="Operating temperature">operating temperature</a> of up to 10&#160;°C (18&#160;°F)  lower, resulting in less tyre wear. By lap&#160;18, Massa had a lead of 3.5&#160;seconds over Hamilton, who in turn was almost 8&#160;seconds ahead of Kovalainen. Glock was a further 3&#160;seconds behind the second McLaren driver, but was drawing ahead of Kubica in fifth, who was finding his BMW Sauber difficult to drive in race conditions with a lack of grip and stability under braking, and was holding up a group of cars behind him.<sup id="cite_ref-ASpg43_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ASpg43-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Massa, Kubica and Webber were the first three drivers to make pit stops by coming in on lap&#160;18.<sup id="cite_ref-pg200-201_1-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg200-201-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The McLaren mechanics timed Massa's stop to estimate the amount of fuel he received, and when Hamilton made his own first stop on the next lap, they fuelled him to run for three&#160;laps longer than the Ferrari in the second stint of the race.<sup id="cite_ref-pg195_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg195-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Kovalainen took over the lead of the race for two&#160;laps before his pit stop on lap&#160;21 returned it to Massa.<sup id="cite_ref-pg200-201_1-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg200-201-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Piquet was the last of the leading runners to make a pit stop, on lap&#160;25, allowing him to jump ahead of Kubica, Trulli and Webber.<sup id="cite_ref-ASpg43_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ASpg43-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Further down the order, Vettel made an unscheduled pit stop on lap&#160;20 and retired two&#160;laps later with an overheating engine.<sup id="cite_ref-pg200-201_1-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg200-201-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-TBT_15-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBT-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> By the end of lap&#160;26, all of the leading drivers on two-stop strategies had taken their pit stops. The race order was Massa leading from Hamilton, Kovalainen, Glock, Coulthard (who was yet to pit), Alonso, Räikkönen, Piquet, Trulli, Kubica, Webber, Heidfeld, Button, Barrichello, Bourdais, Rosberg, Nakajima, Fisichella and Sutil.<sup id="cite_ref-pg200-201_1-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg200-201-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Timo_Glock_2327895375_cropped.jpg" class="image"><img alt="Head and shoulders of a man in his twenties with dark brown hair and eyes. He is wearing a red-and-white baseball cap which displays the Panasonic Toyota Racing logo at the front, and the number 12, with his signature superimposed upon it, on the left side. He is also wearing white racing overalls with red stripes, upon the collar of which the Denso logo is embroidered." src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e0/Timo_Glock_2327895375_cropped.jpg/220px-Timo_Glock_2327895375_cropped.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="200" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/e/e0/Timo_Glock_2327895375_cropped.jpg 1.5x" data-file-width="270" data-file-height="245" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Timo_Glock_2327895375_cropped.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Timo_Glock" title="Timo Glock">Timo Glock</a> also recorded the best result of his career, by finishing in second place.</div></div></div>
<p>Hamilton rejoined the race following his first pit stop 2.6&#160;seconds behind Massa, and needed to stay within approximately 3.5&#160;seconds of the Ferrari driver to gain track position after the second round of pit stops.<sup id="cite_ref-ASpg43_23-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ASpg43-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Massa began to pull away again, easing the gap open to 4&#160;seconds by lap&#160;32, while Hamilton locked his front-left wheel as he tried to keep up with the Ferrari, <a href="/wiki/List_of_motorsport_terminology#F" class="mw-redirect" title="List of motorsport terminology">flat-spotting</a> the tyre in the process. The two continued to set fastest laps as they pulled away from the rest of the field.<sup id="cite_ref-ASpg44_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ASpg44-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> On lap&#160;29, Coulthard made his first pit stop, dropping to 12th&#160;place as a result. Button, Barrichello, Bourdais, Rosberg, Nakajima, Fisichella and Sutil also made their first pit stops at this stage of the race.<sup id="cite_ref-pg200-201_1-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg200-201-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Three of these drivers experienced delays during their pit stops which dropped them down the running order: Barrichello and Bourdais suffered flash fires, while Rosberg's fuel hose jammed, losing him time.<sup id="cite_ref-pg198_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg198-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At the front of the field, Massa continued to pull away gradually from Hamilton; the gap between the two had risen to 5&#160;seconds by the end of lap&#160;40. On the following lap, Hamilton's front-left tyre <a href="/wiki/Flat_tire" title="Flat tire">deflated</a> approaching Turn Two; the resultant slow lap back to the pit lane and stop for a replacement tyre dropped him to tenth place.<sup id="cite_ref-pg195_22-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg195-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Massa now had a 23-second lead over Kovalainen and slowed his pace accordingly, adjusting the performance of the engine to place it under less mechanical stress.<sup id="cite_ref-ASpg44_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ASpg44-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> He made his final pit stop on lap&#160;44, allowing Kovalainen to take the lead until his own stop four laps later, handing Massa back his lead.<sup id="cite_ref-pg195_22-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg195-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> On lap&#160;41, Heidfeld made his only pit stop, dropping from 11th to 12th position. In the following laps, the other drivers made their second stops, except Nakajima, who switched to a one-stop strategy at his first visit to his pit box.<sup id="cite_ref-pg200-201_1-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg200-201-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-TBT_15-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBT-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Behind the leading trio of Massa, Kovalainen and Glock, Räikkönen moved ahead of Alonso despite running off the road just before his pit stop; after he exited the pit lane, Piquet fended off Trulli as they battled for position. The pit stop sequence allowed Hamilton to move back up the order, to sixth&#160;place behind Alonso.<sup id="cite_ref-ASpg44_24-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ASpg44-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Further back, Bourdais suffered another flash fire on lap&#160;45, and made another visit to the pit lane one lap later to have <a href="/wiki/Fire_extinguisher" title="Fire extinguisher">fire extinguisher</a> foam cleaned off his helmet visor.<sup id="cite_ref-pg198_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg198-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Rosberg was the final scheduled driver to make a pit stop, on lap&#160;58. The majority of the drivers ran with Soft tyres for the first two stints of the race, then switched to the Super Soft compound for the final stint.<sup id="cite_ref-pg200-201_1-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg200-201-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Felipe_Massa_out_2008_Hungarian_GP.jpg" class="image"><img alt="A figure in a red racing suit and boots, and wearing a helmet with a dark blue and light green design, walks away from a stationary red open-wheel racing car, which is parked next to the concrete wall separating the pit lane from the track. He is holding both his hands to his head. He has already removed his left racing glove." src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/23/Felipe_Massa_out_2008_Hungarian_GP.jpg/220px-Felipe_Massa_out_2008_Hungarian_GP.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="87" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/23/Felipe_Massa_out_2008_Hungarian_GP.jpg/330px-Felipe_Massa_out_2008_Hungarian_GP.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/23/Felipe_Massa_out_2008_Hungarian_GP.jpg/440px-Felipe_Massa_out_2008_Hungarian_GP.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2592" data-file-height="1024" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Felipe_Massa_out_2008_Hungarian_GP.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Massa walks away from his car, having suffered an engine failure while leading with three laps to go.</div></div></div>
<p>At the conclusion of lap&#160;59, with the scheduled pit stops completed, the running order was Massa leading from Kovalainen, Glock, Räikkönen, Alonso, Hamilton, Piquet, Trulli, Kubica, Webber, Heidfeld, Coulthard, Button, Nakajima, Rosberg, Fisichella, Sutil, Barrichello and Bourdais.<sup id="cite_ref-pg200-201_1-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg200-201-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Running in <a href="/wiki/List_of_motorsport_terminology#C" class="mw-redirect" title="List of motorsport terminology">clean air</a> without a car immediately in front of him for the first time, Räikkönen set the fastest lap of the race at 1:21.195, on lap&#160;61, as he closed the nine-second gap to Glock at a rate of one second per lap. Hamilton caught Alonso at a similar rate, but his rear Super Soft tyres began to overheat and he was unable to make any further impression after closing the gap to 1.5&#160;seconds.<sup id="cite_ref-ASpg44_24-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ASpg44-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> On lap&#160;62, Sutil suffered a puncture caused by a brake failure and became the second retirement of the race.<sup id="cite_ref-TBT_15-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBT-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> In the closing laps, Kovalainen reduced his deficit to Massa to 15&#160;seconds, but the Ferrari driver appeared to be in command of the race. However, as Massa started lap&#160;68, his engine failed without warning, forcing him to retire from the lead with three&#160;laps remaining.<sup id="cite_ref-ASpg44_24-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ASpg44-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Kovalainen was thus promoted into the lead, which he held to take the first victory of his Formula One career in a time of 1'37:27.067, at an average speed of 117.309 miles per hour (188.791&#160;km/h). Kovalainen was the 100th driver to win a Formula One World Championship race.<sup id="cite_ref-pg200-201_1-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg200-201-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Glock likewise claimed the best result of his career, and first podium finish, with second&#160;position. Räikkönen took the final place on the podium despite a failure in his car's rear suspension during the final few laps.<sup id="cite_ref-ASpg44_24-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ASpg44-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Alonso and Piquet finished on either side of Hamilton, who was in fifth position, leading Symonds to describe the race as his team's best of the year so far.<sup id="cite_ref-pg197_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg197-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Trulli finished seventh, ahead of Kubica, who was extremely disappointed with the uncompetitive performance of his car at the Grand Prix closest to his home country of <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Poland</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Offline_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Offline-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> His teammate, Heidfeld, finished in tenth place between the two Red Bull drivers, both of whom were also disappointed by their team's performance.<sup id="cite_ref-TBT_15-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TBT-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Button, Nakajima, Rosberg and Fisichella filled the next places, a lap behind the leader, while Barrichello was two laps down in 16th&#160;position after the delay at his first pit stop. Massa was classified in 17th&#160;place, ahead of Bourdais, who was the final finisher.<sup id="cite_ref-pg200-201_1-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg200-201-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2008_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatright" style="width:40%;&#32;;"><link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r887775652"/>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">"There have been various incidents this year and we have been in the position after Saturday quite a few times to fight for the victory, but always something has gone wrong and it hasn't functioned perfectly. Today obviously I knew Massa and Lewis were both very fast at the beginning of the race but halfway through the race I felt it was starting to work for me a little bit better and then at the end I just tried to put pressure on Massa and hoped something would happen and obviously it looked like he had a mechanical failure, so it all worked fine for me today and I am very, very happy about it. All the hard work that the whole team has put in the last few months, through difficult times, we just kept pushing and it is very respectable and I am very, very glad to score my first victory."</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style=""><a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a>, speaking during the post-race FIA <a href="/wiki/Press_conference" title="Press conference">press conference</a> for the podium finishers.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>Although Kovalainen was delighted with his first Formula One victory, he attributed much of the credit for his win to luck. After the race he said, "I feel a bit sorry for Felipe and Lewis. They both drove great races, but I know how it feels when things go wrong—I've had a few similar moments this year. I tried to put pressure on Felipe, especially during the last stint. I felt something might happen if I did that, you never know, but I still found it hard to believe when I saw his Ferrari on fire."<sup id="cite_ref-pg197_26-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg197-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> As of 2019, the race remains Kovalainen's only Formula One win.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Glock was pleased with second position and spoke of how he had dedicated extra training to improve his race starts. He said he had focussed on not making any mistakes in the closing laps as Räikkönen closed, instead of maintaining the gap.<sup id="cite_ref-ASpg43_23-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ASpg43-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-pg197_26-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg197-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Räikkönen described his race as "frustrating and boring" due to the amount of time he spent stuck behind slower cars.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The podium finishers were overshadowed in the media by coverage of the ill fortune of both the weekend's pace-setters, Hamilton and Massa.<sup id="cite_ref-pg197_26-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg197-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Massa in particular was praised for his performance. The Ferrari team principal, <a href="/wiki/Stefano_Domenicali" title="Stefano Domenicali">Stefano Domenicali</a>, said it was, "the best race of his career. It was fantastic the way he managed the race."<sup id="cite_ref-IB1_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IB1-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Journalist <a href="/wiki/Mark_Hughes_(journalist)" title="Mark Hughes (journalist)">Mark Hughes</a> described it as "almost certainly his best race to date",<sup id="cite_ref-ASpg44_24-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ASpg44-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> and colleague Simon Arron termed it as "one of the finest afternoons of his F1 career".<sup id="cite_ref-pg197_26-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg197-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Both drew attention to his controlled aggression at the first corner of the race, followed by his relentless, mistake-free pace. Arron, in particular, noted that Massa's first-corner passing move was more typical of Hamilton's attacking style than his own, and was a "defining moment" in Massa's championship campaign.<sup id="cite_ref-pg195_22-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg195-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton himself later expressed surprise that Massa had been able to overtake him in such a manner, and warned his rival that "it won't happen again".<sup id="cite_ref-ev_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ev-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Hughes described the Grand Prix as "a throwback race", in that the leaders had suffered from unreliability, and the winner had not been in contention on speed alone; It was a situation reminiscent of earlier times in the sport, when the cars were generally less reliable.<sup id="cite_ref-ASpg40_20-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ASpg40-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Bridgestone director of motorsport <a href="/wiki/Hirohide_Hamashima" title="Hirohide Hamashima">Hirohide Hamashima</a> said Hamilton's puncture was probably caused by debris, although as a result of the damage the tyre had sustained, the precise nature of the failure was impossible to determine. He also stated that Hamilton's tyre was more vulnerable to debris damage because he had flat-spotted it earlier in the race.<sup id="cite_ref-ASpg44_24-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ASpg44-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Massa said he had no prior indication of his engine failure.<sup id="cite_ref-pg197_26-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg197-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> The problem was later traced to a <a href="/wiki/Connecting_rod" title="Connecting rod">connecting rod</a> failure caused by an impurity in the component material. An identical problem caused Räikkönen to retire from the following race, the <a href="/wiki/2008_European_Grand_Prix" title="2008 European Grand Prix">2008 European Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As a consequence of the race, Hamilton extended his lead in the Drivers' Championship to five&#160;points ahead of Räikkönen, who moved ahead of Massa in the standings. Kubica maintained fourth place; Kovalainen's win moved him to within three points of Heidfeld in fifth. In the Constructors' Championship, Ferrari continued to lead, but McLaren jumped ahead of BMW Sauber for second position. Behind Toyota, Renault moved ahead of Red Bull.<sup id="cite_ref-pg200-201_1-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg200-201-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Despite his increased lead, Hamilton acknowledged that he expected the Ferrari drivers to be formidable opponents in the season's seven remaining races.<sup id="cite_ref-ev_32-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ev-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> After the eventual conclusion of the championship in Hamilton's favour by one&#160;point, the Hungarian Grand Prix was highlighted as one of Massa's most obvious lost opportunities in his bid to win the title.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
