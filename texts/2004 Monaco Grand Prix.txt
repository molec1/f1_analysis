<p>The <b>2004 Monaco Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>LXII Grand Prix de Monaco</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 23 May 2004, at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Monaco" title="Circuit de Monaco">Circuit de Monaco</a>; contested over 77&#160;laps, it was the sixth race of the <a href="/wiki/2004_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2004 Formula One season">2004 Formula One season</a>. The race was won by the <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a>; this was his only victory in Formula One. The <a href="/wiki/British_American_Racing" title="British American Racing">BAR</a> driver, <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> finished in second position, one second behind Trulli. <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> took the third and final podium spot for <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>.
</p><p>In fine conditions Trulli beat Button to the first corner off the <a href="/wiki/List_of_motorsport_terminology#G" class="mw-redirect" title="List of motorsport terminology">grid</a>, and the fast starting <a href="/wiki/Takuma_Sato" title="Takuma Sato">Takuma Sato</a> beat <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> to fourth place before retiring at the end of the second lap. On lap three the <a href="/wiki/Safety_car" title="Safety car">safety car</a> was deployed due to a collision between <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> and <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a>, with the race resuming on lap seven. The two Renault drivers, Trulli and <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> remained close together at the front of the race until the pit stops which briefly saw Michael Schumacher lead the race. Alonso retired after crashing trying to lap <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a>, and a second safety car period ensued. After briefly leading, Michael Schumacher retired behind the safety car following a collision with <a href="/wiki/Juan-Pablo_Montoya" class="mw-redirect" title="Juan-Pablo Montoya">Juan-Pablo Montoya</a>, who subsequently finished fourth. Schumacher's retirement elevated Button to second, and Trulli to the lead. Button subsequently reduced Trulli's lead, and the pair raced closely until the finish.
</p><p>The race was Trulli's sole victory of his F1 career. The retirement of <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>, the defending <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Champion</a>, brought to an end his run of five successive victories from the inaugural race of the season. The race result had no bearing on the Drivers' Championship standings, and despite his retirement, Schumacher departed Monaco as the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Championship_points_scoring_systems" title="List of Formula One World Championship points scoring systems">points</a> leader, ahead of Barrichello and Button. Trulli's victory placed him ten points ahead of his teammate Alonso. Ferrari maintained their lead in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a>, 36&#160;points ahead of Renault and 48 ahead of BAR, with 12&#160;races of the season remaining.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2004_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice_and_qualifying">Practice and qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2004_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Practice and qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Schumacher_at_Monaco_2004.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/50/Schumacher_at_Monaco_2004.jpg/220px-Schumacher_at_Monaco_2004.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="143" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/50/Schumacher_at_Monaco_2004.jpg/330px-Schumacher_at_Monaco_2004.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/50/Schumacher_at_Monaco_2004.jpg/440px-Schumacher_at_Monaco_2004.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3369" data-file-height="2186" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Schumacher_at_Monaco_2004.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> during free practice</div></div></div>
<p>Four practice sessions were held before the qualifying session, two on the Thursday before the race, and two on Saturday morning before the qualifying session.<sup id="cite_ref-GP1_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GP1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The driver's championship leader, the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>, was fastest in all four practice sessions, and in the final session set a record lap time for the Monaco circuit, of 1:14.014. Schumacher's record time was set in response to a fast lap time from Jarno Trulli.<sup id="cite_ref-Aut1_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Aut1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The Jaguar driver <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> suffered an engine failure in the final practice session, and for changing his engine suffered an automatic 10 place grid penalty for the subsequent qualifying session, a penalty also suffered by <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a>, for an engine failure in the first practice session.<sup id="cite_ref-Aut1_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Aut1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Bridgestone" title="Bridgestone">Bridgestone</a> tyres used by Schumacher were inferior to the <a href="/wiki/Michelin" title="Michelin">Michelin</a> tyres used by the Renault, BAR and Williams teams at the Monaco weekend.<sup id="cite_ref-GuardMay2304_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GuardMay2304-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> In the weeks before the Monaco race Bridgestone had worked with the Ferrari team on tyres that could perform a fast single lap during qualifying and the opening laps of the race.<sup id="cite_ref-GuardMay2304_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GuardMay2304-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher had set his fast practice times on soft tyres, but after his tyres had struggled on a slippery track, Schumacher and the other Bridgestone teams returned to hard tyres.<sup id="cite_ref-GuardMay2304_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GuardMay2304-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2004_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Schmacher_and_Sato_at_Monaco_2004.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/23/Schmacher_and_Sato_at_Monaco_2004.jpg/220px-Schmacher_and_Sato_at_Monaco_2004.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="142" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/23/Schmacher_and_Sato_at_Monaco_2004.jpg/330px-Schmacher_and_Sato_at_Monaco_2004.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/23/Schmacher_and_Sato_at_Monaco_2004.jpg/440px-Schmacher_and_Sato_at_Monaco_2004.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3376" data-file-height="2185" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Schmacher_and_Sato_at_Monaco_2004.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> and <a href="/wiki/Takuma_Sato" title="Takuma Sato">Takuma Sato</a>, in qualifying, en route to setting the fifth and eighth best times respectively</div></div></div>
<p>Michael Schumacher had won pole at four of the last five races.<sup id="cite_ref-GuardMay2304_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GuardMay2304-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> In recent years the Monaco Grand Prix had rarely been won from pole position, the last winner from pole before 2004 had been <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> at the <a href="/wiki/1998_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="1998 Monaco Grand Prix">1998 Monaco Grand Prix</a> and Michael Schumacher at the <a href="/wiki/1994_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="1994 Monaco Grand Prix">1994 Monaco Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-GuardMay2304_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GuardMay2304-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The first four fastest times in qualifying were set by cars with Michelin tyres.<sup id="cite_ref-Aut2_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Aut2-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The Sauber team decided to use the soft Bridgestone tyre and their performance had suffered, with their drivers qualifying 10th and 16th.<sup id="cite_ref-Aut2_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Aut2-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Renault driver Jarno Trulli set his pole time of 1m13.985s, it was the fastest ever recorded around the Monaco circuit, and was the first pole position of his career. His time was 0.411 faster than the next driver.<sup id="cite_ref-Aut2_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Aut2-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Aut3_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Aut3-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Trulli had previously started on the front row at Monaco in 2000.<sup id="cite_ref-Aut3_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Aut3-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Of his first pole Trulli said that "I'm pretty impressed over my lap time because I couldn't believe I lapped under the 14s...I put in a very impressive lap. In Monaco, being on pole, which is something I have chased for such a long time, I'm so pleased."<sup id="cite_ref-Aut2_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Aut2-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The BAR driver <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> started the race alongside Trulli on the front row, although the Williams driver <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> had qualified second, he was demoted to twelfth for changing his engine.<sup id="cite_ref-GuardMay2304_3-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GuardMay2304-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Ralf Schumacher had qualified on pole at Monaco at the previous year's race.<sup id="cite_ref-Aut3_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Aut3-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Trulli's Renault teammate <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> qualified third, with Michael Schumacher eventually qualified fifth. Michael Schumacher was moved to fourth following his brother Ralf's demotion.<sup id="cite_ref-GuardMay2304_3-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GuardMay2304-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Takuma Sato qualified seventh after a fast time in the first sector of the circuit, but a driving error damaged his chances of a quick lap.<sup id="cite_ref-Aut2_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Aut2-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatleft" style="width:28%;&#32;;"><style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r887775652">.mw-parser-output .quotebox{background-color:#F9F9F9;border:1px solid #aaa;box-sizing:border-box;padding:10px;font-size:88%;max-width:100%}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft{margin:0.5em 1.4em 0.8em 0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright{margin:0.5em 0 0.8em 1.4em}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.centered{margin:0.5em auto 0.8em auto}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft p,.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright p{font-style:inherit}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-title{background-color:#F9F9F9;text-align:center;font-size:larger;font-weight:bold}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:before{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" “ ";vertical-align:-45%;line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:after{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" ” ";line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .left-aligned{text-align:left}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .right-aligned{text-align:right}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .center-aligned{text-align:center}.mw-parser-output .quotebox cite{display:block;font-style:normal}@media screen and (max-width:360px){.mw-parser-output .quotebox{min-width:100%;margin:0 0 0.8em!important;float:none!important}}</style>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">"...Where we will end up from our situation is the big question. Monaco is a long race, it's not optimum where we start from, we're not very happy about that, but what can you do?"</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style=""><a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>, following the qualifying session.<sup id="cite_ref-Aut2_4-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Aut2-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>Following the qualifying session, a mini-bus carrying the session's three fastest drivers was hit by a car. No one was hurt in the incident. Trulli described the accident as "...a big surprise...Maybe they thought we had not taken enough risks already on the track. But I don't think so. Not during qualifying in Monte Carlo."<sup id="cite_ref-GuardMay2304_3-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GuardMay2304-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2004_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The race began with Trulli on pole and <a href="/wiki/British_American_Racing" title="British American Racing">BAR</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> behind; Renault's <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> and Michael Schumacher occupied the second row. After two aborted starts (<a href="/wiki/Olivier_Panis" title="Olivier Panis">Olivier Panis</a> stalled his <a href="/wiki/Toyota_Racing_(Formula_One_team)" title="Toyota Racing (Formula One team)">Toyota</a> and Trulli's Renault leaked coolant onto the track) the parade lap began; Panis stalled again and started the race from the pits. As the race began, BAR's <a href="/wiki/Takuma_Sato" title="Takuma Sato">Takuma Sato</a> made an excellent start, moving from eighth to fourth in seconds. Presently Sato's engine began smoking; on the third lap, it exploded spectacularly and released an enormous cloud of smoke, in which <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> collided with <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>’s <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> and overturned. Both drivers were unhurt but out of the race, as was Sato.
</p><p>Out came the yellow flags, and the race proceeded under the safety car until the eighth lap, at which point Alonso fought Trulli for the lead, followed by Button. When the race restarted, <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a> moved past <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> to take sixth position, and Trulli set three consecutive fastest laps, but was only able to increase his lead to 1.2 seconds over Alonso. The leaders began their first pit stops on lap 18, and by <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>'s stop on lap 26, Trulli led from Alonso, with Schumacher now in third ahead of both <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> and Button.
</p><p>On lap 28, Räikkönen retired with mechanical troubles, joining <a href="/wiki/Jaguar_Racing" title="Jaguar Racing">Jaguar's</a> <a href="/wiki/Christian_Klien" title="Christian Klien">Christian Klien</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a>, as well as <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan's</a> <a href="/wiki/Giorgio_Pantano" title="Giorgio Pantano">Giorgio Pantano</a> and <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Gianmaria_Bruni" title="Gianmaria Bruni">Gianmaria Bruni</a> on the sidelines.
</p>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatright" style="width:28%;&#32;;"><link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r887775652"/>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">"...I've been waiting so long to win and it came at the best race in Monaco...In the end I just took no risks. I was just doing what I had to do and just responding to the lap times and there was no point taking any risks. Everything was under control...It has been a faultless weekend and it is the best way to win – being on top all weekend"</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style=""><a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a>, following his victory in the race.<sup id="cite_ref-Aut4_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Aut4-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>In an attempt to lap <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a>, who was down in 11th position, Alonso tried to pass him offline around the outside in the tunnel and crashed heavily. Alonso was enraged by this incident, and publicly accused Ralf of dangerous driving. The safety car was immediately deployed, and all of the front-runners (except Michael Schumacher and Montoya) took the opportunity to pit. While following the safety car, Schumacher locked his left front tyre in the tunnel in an apparent attempt to generate heat in his brake pads and discs ahead of the restart. Montoya, directly behind, moved to the inside trying to avoid running into the back of Schumacher. However, as Schumacher continued there was no space between his car and the barrier for Montoya's and he clipped Montoya's left front tyre with his right rear, spun sideways and hit the barriers. This accident ended Schumacher's hopes for a sixth consecutive victory and a perfect season. Upon investigations, the stewards declined to apportion blame to either driver.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As the order settled down towards the end of the race, Trulli led from Button, with Barrichello in third. The top three were a lap in front of the rest of the field, and the only drivers with a chance of winning the race. Barrichello needed to pit with 22 laps remaining, and rejoined too far behind the top two in order to make a challenge. From then on, it was a straight fight between Trulli and Button, but <a href="/wiki/Monaco" title="Monaco">Monaco</a> is notoriously the hardest circuit to make a passing move on. Therefore, Button having no chance of passing, Trulli took the win by close to half a second.
</p><p>Having won the first Grand Prix of his career, Trulli was left literally speechless, cancelling interviews and having teammate Alonso conduct interviews on his behalf.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
