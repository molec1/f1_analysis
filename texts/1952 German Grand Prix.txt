<p>The <b>1952 German Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_Two" title="Formula Two">Formula Two</a> race held on 3 August 1952 at the  <a href="/wiki/N%C3%BCrburgring" title="Nürburgring">Nürburgring Nordschleife</a>. It was race 6 of 8 in the <a href="/wiki/1952_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1952 World Championship of Drivers">1952 World Championship of Drivers</a>, in which each Grand Prix was run to Formula Two rules rather than the Formula One regulations normally used. The 18-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Alberto_Ascari" title="Alberto Ascari">Alberto Ascari</a> after he started from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>. His teammates <a href="/wiki/Giuseppe_Farina" title="Giuseppe Farina">Giuseppe Farina</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rudi_Fischer" title="Rudi Fischer">Rudi Fischer</a> finished in second and third places.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1952_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Maserati_in_motorsport" title="Maserati in motorsport">Maserati</a> factory team finally appeared with their new car, the <a href="/wiki/Maserati_A6GCM" title="Maserati A6GCM">A6GCM</a>, which was driven by <a href="/wiki/Felice_Bonetto" title="Felice Bonetto">Felice Bonetto</a>. Also racing A6GCMs were the Escuderia Bandeirantes drivers <a href="/wiki/Gino_Bianco" title="Gino Bianco">Bianco</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eitel_Cantoni" title="Eitel Cantoni">Cantoni</a>. <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> once again entered the successful trio of <a href="/wiki/Alberto_Ascari" title="Alberto Ascari">Alberto Ascari</a>, <a href="/wiki/Giuseppe_Farina" title="Giuseppe Farina">Nino Farina</a> and <a href="/wiki/Piero_Taruffi" title="Piero Taruffi">Piero Taruffi</a>, while there were privateer Ferrari entries for <a href="/wiki/Rudi_Fischer" title="Rudi Fischer">Rudi Fischer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rudolf_Schoeller" title="Rudolf Schoeller">Rudolf Schoeller</a> of Ecurie Espadon, <a href="/wiki/Roger_Laurent" title="Roger Laurent">Roger Laurent</a> of Ecurie Francorchamps, and <a href="/wiki/Piero_Carini" title="Piero Carini">Piero Carini</a> of Scuderia Marzotto. <a href="/wiki/Jean_Behra" title="Jean Behra">Jean Behra</a> returned to action for the <a href="/wiki/Gordini" title="Gordini">Gordini</a> team, having recovered from his shoulder injury. He replaced <a href="/wiki/Birabongse_Bhanudej" title="Birabongse Bhanudej">Prince Bira</a>, and was partnered by teammates <a href="/wiki/Robert_Manzon" title="Robert Manzon">Robert Manzon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Maurice_Trintignant" title="Maurice Trintignant">Maurice Trintignant</a>. <a href="/wiki/Hersham_and_Walton_Motors" title="Hersham and Walton Motors">HWM</a> entered three cars, with regular <a href="/wiki/Peter_Collins_(racing_driver)" title="Peter Collins (racing driver)">Peter Collins</a> joined by the Belgian pairing of <a href="/wiki/Paul_Fr%C3%A8re" title="Paul Frère">Paul Frère</a> and <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Claes" title="Johnny Claes">Johnny Claes</a>, while Australian <a href="/wiki/Tony_Gaze" title="Tony Gaze">Tony Gaze</a> drove a privateer HWM. <a href="/wiki/Bill_Aston" title="Bill Aston">Bill Aston</a> drove an <a href="/wiki/Aston_Butterworth" title="Aston Butterworth">Aston Butterworth</a>, and the field was completed by a plethora of privateer German cars (<a href="/wiki/Veritas_(automobile)" title="Veritas (automobile)">Veritas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alex_von_Falkenhausen_Motorenbau" title="Alex von Falkenhausen Motorenbau">AFM</a> and <a href="/wiki/BMW_in_Formula_One" title="BMW in Formula One">BMW</a>).
</p><p>Ferrari were once again fastest in qualifying, with Ascari and Farina being joined on the front row of the grid by the Gordinis of Trintignant and Manzon. The remaining works Ferrari driver, Taruffi, started from the second row, alongside the Ecurie Espadon-entered Ferrari of Fischer and <a href="/wiki/Paul_Pietsch" title="Paul Pietsch">Paul Pietsch</a> in a Veritas. Bonetto's works Maserati made the third row, along with the Gordini of Jean Behra, and a pair of local entrants: <a href="/wiki/Hans_Klenk" title="Hans Klenk">Hans Klenk</a>'s Veritas, and <a href="/wiki/Willi_Heeks" title="Willi Heeks">Willi Heeks</a> in an AFM.
</p><p>The race turned out to be rather a processional event, with Ascari leading Farina all the way in the first 16 laps. Two laps from home, he had to dive into the pits for oil, emerging 10 seconds behind Farina-which he rattled off on the next lap, catching Farina just a mile from home to win by several seconds after an otherwise dull race. Piero Taruffi had been running in third behind his teammates, but he lost the position to Rudi Fischer towards the end of the race when he encountered problems due to his suspension breaking. Fischer's podium and Taruffi's fourth place-finish ensured that it was a Ferrari 1-2-3-4. Manzon, who had been running in fourth for much of the first half of the race, between Taruffi and Fischer, was forced to retire when a wheel fell off his car. This meant that his teammate Behra was left to take the final points in fifth position in his Gordini, ahead of Roger Laurent's Ferrari. Felice Bonetto, of the factory Maserati team, was disqualified for receiving a push start after his first lap spin.
</p><p>Ascari, who had taken his fourth consecutive victory, along with a fourth consecutive fastest lap, had now scored the maximum of 36 points for the season, as only a driver's four best results counted. As a result, he clinched the world championship, making him the first driver to win the championship with two races left to go.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The date was 3 August, the earliest anyone would claim the Championship until <a href="/wiki/Jim_Clark" title="Jim Clark">Jim Clark</a> seized the crown on 1 August in <a href="/wiki/1965_Formula_One_season" title="1965 Formula One season">1965</a>, also at the <a href="/wiki/1965_German_Grand_Prix" title="1965 German Grand Prix">Nürburgring</a>. Ascari's teammates, Taruffi and Farina, remained in second and third, respectively, in the Drivers' Championship, while Swiss driver Fischer's second podium of the season raised him up to fourth in the standings.
</p>
