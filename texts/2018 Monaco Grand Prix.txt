<p>The <b>2018 Monaco Grand Prix</b> (formally known as the <b>Formula 1 Grand Prix de Monaco 2018</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_racing" title="Formula One racing">Formula One motor race</a> that was held on 27 May 2018 at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Monaco" title="Circuit de Monaco">Circuit de Monaco</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Street_circuit" title="Street circuit">street circuit</a> that runs through the <a href="/wiki/Monaco" title="Monaco">Principality of Monaco</a>. It was the 6th round of the <a href="/wiki/2018_FIA_Formula_One_World_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="2018 FIA Formula One World Championship">2018 FIA Formula One World Championship</a>, the 76th time the <a href="/wiki/Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="Monaco Grand Prix">Monaco Grand Prix</a> had been held, and the 65th time it had been a round of the Formula One World Championship since the inception of the series in <a href="/wiki/1950_Formula_One_season" title="1950 Formula One season">1950</a>.
</p><p>The race was won by Daniel Ricciardo in Red Bull, with Sebastian Vettel in Ferrari coming second. Lewis Hamilton in Mercedes finished third, maintaining the championship lead over Vettel. Mercedes also led in the Constructors' Championship ahead of Ferrari.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2018_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Tyres">Tyres</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2018_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Tyres">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The race marked the competitive début of <a href="/wiki/Pirelli" title="Pirelli">Pirelli</a>'s new hypersoft tyre compound.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Chassis_updates">Chassis updates</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2018_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Chassis updates">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Following controversy over the use of winglets above <a href="/wiki/Halo_(safety_device)" title="Halo (safety device)">halo</a>-mounted rear view mirrors at the previous round in Spain, the FIA banned the winglets ahead of the race weekend.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Penalties">Penalties</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2018_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Penalties">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Romain Grosjean was handed a three-place grid penalty for causing a collision on the opening lap of the <a href="/wiki/2018_Spanish_Grand_Prix" title="2018 Spanish Grand Prix">Spanish Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-GRO_3_places_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GRO_3_places-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Max Verstappen received a 5 place grid penalty for an unscheduled gearbox change, and a 10 place grid penalty for use of a third MGU-K, although this made no difference to his starting position, as he already was at the back of the grid.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice">Practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2018_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> was fastest across all three practice sessions, improving his time in each successive session.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In FP3, Max Verstappen crashed heavily at the Turn 16, meaning his car was damaged beyond repair for qualifying.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2018_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a> was unable to compete in qualifying as his mechanics were unable to repair the car in time after a crash in the final practice session.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> set a new qualifying lap record to take pole position for the race.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Red Bull capped both ends of the grid, Ricciardo starting first and Verstappen starting last. Pierre Gasly made Q3 for the second time, however his teammate, Brendon Hartley, after showing promise with P7 in FP3, qualified in P16 after being held up in traffic as well as being unhappy with the balance in his car.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2018_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Ricciardo</a> led going into the first corner with the top 6 staying in the same order. On lap 28, Ricciardo, still leading the race, complained of a loss of power to the team.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Ricciardo was left to manage a wounded car for the remaining 50 laps of the race, with what was later revealed to be an <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_engines" title="Formula One engines">MGU-K</a> failure (giving him 25% less horsepower than usual), and with only six out of eight gears functioning.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Sergey_Sirotkin_(racing_driver)" title="Sergey Sirotkin (racing driver)">Sergey Sirotkin</a> was given a 10-second stop-go penalty after his tyres were not fitted to the car at the 3 minute signal.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On lap 53 <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> retired at <a href="/wiki/Sainte-D%C3%A9vote_Chapel" title="Sainte-Dévote Chapel">Sainte-Dévote</a> due to gearbox problems, his first retirement of the 2018 season.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> A <a href="/wiki/Safety_car" title="Safety car">virtual safety car</a> came out on lap 70 when <a href="/wiki/Charles_Leclerc_(racing_driver)" class="mw-redirect" title="Charles Leclerc (racing driver)">Charles Leclerc</a>'s left front brake disc failed just before the Nouvelle Chicane, causing him to crash into the back of <a href="/wiki/Brendon_Hartley" title="Brendon Hartley">Brendon Hartley</a>. Both drivers were forced to retire due to damage.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>This was the first Monaco Grand Prix since <a href="/wiki/2009_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="2009 Monaco Grand Prix">2009</a> where a (full) <a href="/wiki/Safety_car" title="Safety car">safety car</a> did not make an appearance, although there was a brief virtual safety car period.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post_race">Post race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2018_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Post race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After the race <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Hamilton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Alonso</a> both complained that this was "one of the most boring races ever"; drivers were lapping several seconds a lap slower than they could have been to conserve tires and avoid making a second pit-stop.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Alonso also pointed to the spread out field, "as there were barely any yellow flags or safety cars."<sup id="cite_ref-:2_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
