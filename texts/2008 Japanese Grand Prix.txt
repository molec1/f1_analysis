<p>The <b>2008 Japanese Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>XXXIV Fuji Television Japanese Grand Prix</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 12 October 2008, at the <a href="/wiki/Fuji_Speedway" title="Fuji Speedway">Fuji Speedway</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oyama,_Shizuoka" title="Oyama, Shizuoka">Oyama</a>, Japan. It was the 16th race of the <a href="/wiki/2008_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2008 Formula One season">2008 Formula One season</a>. The 67-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a> team from fourth position on the starting grid. <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a> finished second for <a href="/wiki/BMW_Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="BMW Sauber">BMW Sauber</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> third for <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>, the eventual <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Champion</a>, led the Championship going into the race, and started from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> alongside Räikkönen. Hamilton's <a href="/wiki/McLaren_(racing)" class="mw-redirect" title="McLaren (racing)">McLaren</a> teammate <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> began from third, next to Alonso. At the first corner Hamilton braked late, forcing Räikkönen wide. Hamilton was later given a penalty, and was criticised by the British racing press for overaggressive driving. Ferrari driver <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a>, Hamilton's principal Championship rival, was penalised after an incident on lap two in which he touched Hamilton's car, causing it to spin. The incident dropped Hamilton to the back of the field, from where he was unable to regain a <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Championship_points_scoring_systems" title="List of Formula One World Championship points scoring systems">points scoring position</a>. Massa later collided with <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9bastien_Bourdais" title="Sébastien Bourdais">Sébastien Bourdais</a> of <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso</a>. Bourdais was penalised after the race, and demoted from sixth to tenth position. The penalty prompted widespread criticism from the racing media and ex-drivers.
</p><p>The victory was Alonso's second consecutive win; he started from 15th on the grid to win the <a href="/wiki/2008_Singapore_Grand_Prix" title="2008 Singapore Grand Prix">Singapore Grand Prix</a> two weeks prior. Kubica held off a determined attack from Räikkönen in the closing laps to take second place. Massa's seventh place narrowed his gap to Hamilton in the Drivers' Championship to five points. Ferrari established a seven-point lead over the McLaren team in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a>, with two races of the season remaining.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2008_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Grand Prix was contested by 20&#160;drivers, in ten teams of two.<sup id="cite_ref-classification_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-classification-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The teams, also known as "<a href="/wiki/Formula_One#Constructors" title="Formula One">constructors</a>", were <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, <a href="/wiki/McLaren_(racing)" class="mw-redirect" title="McLaren (racing)">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_HighPerformanceEngines" class="mw-redirect" title="Mercedes-Benz HighPerformanceEngines">Mercedes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a>, <a href="/wiki/Honda_Racing_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Honda Racing F1">Honda</a>, <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a>, <a href="/wiki/BMW_Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="BMW Sauber">BMW Sauber</a>, <a href="/wiki/Toyota_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Toyota F1">Toyota</a>, <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a>, <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a> and <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-classification_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-classification-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Prior to the race, McLaren driver <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> led the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a> with 84&#160;<a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Championship_points_scoring_systems" title="List of Formula One World Championship points scoring systems">points</a>, and Ferrari driver <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> was second with 77&#160;points. Behind them in the Drivers' Championship, <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a> was third with 64&#160;points in a BMW Sauber, and Massa's Ferrari teammate <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> was fourth with 57&#160;points. Kubica's teammate <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> was fifth with 56&#160;points.<sup id="cite_ref-pointsFIA_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pointsFIA-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a>, McLaren–Mercedes were leading with 135&#160;points, one point ahead of their rivals Ferrari, whom they had overtaken at the previous race. BMW Sauber were third with 120&#160;points. In the battle for fourth place, Renault had 51&#160;points, five points ahead of Toyota.<sup id="cite_ref-pointsFIA_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pointsFIA-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>A botched <a href="/wiki/Pit_stop" title="Pit stop">pit stop</a> at the <a href="/wiki/2008_Singapore_Grand_Prix" title="2008 Singapore Grand Prix">Singapore Grand Prix</a> had demoted Massa from first position to the back of field. With three races remaining in the Championship and a seven-point deficit, Massa remained confident about his title chances: "If you look at what happened to me in Singapore where my gap went from one point to seven so suddenly, then you have to consider it could easily go the other way as well."<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton emphasised the value that a conservative racing strategy could hold for his title chances:
</p>

I hate driving for points, but I think we can all see the benefit of that approach at the moment.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>

<p><a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>'s victory at the Singapore Grand Prix was his first Formula One win since rejoining the Renault team, after driving for McLaren in 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-gpsingapore_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpsingapore-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> After qualifying in 15th and making an early pit stop, Alonso had managed to jump to the front of the race when his teammate <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet_Jr." title="Nelson Piquet Jr.">Nelson Piquet Jr.</a> crashed. Left with no opportunity to pit, the frontrunners had to delay their stops until the damage was cleared, allowing the heavily fuelled Alonso to move to the front once they did so.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Though Alonso questioned whether his Renault team could match the pace of Ferrari and McLaren at Fuji, he said "We must remain focussed and try to repeat our level of performance from Singapore to fight at the front."<sup id="cite_ref-preren_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-preren-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Renault's technical director <a href="/wiki/Bob_Bell_(motorsport)" title="Bob Bell (motorsport)">Bob Bell</a> said that the team's improvement from the start of the season was encouraging, but "we recognise that we're not going to overhaul McLaren and Ferrari this season".<sup id="cite_ref-bobbell_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bobbell-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Bell said that the objective for the last races was to win more Grands Prix and, importantly, secure fourth position in the Constructors' Championship from Toyota.<sup id="cite_ref-bobbell_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bobbell-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile</a> (FIA), the sport's <a href="/wiki/Sport_governing_body" class="mw-redirect" title="Sport governing body">governing body</a>, launched the Formula One component of their <a href="/wiki/Make_Cars_Green" title="Make Cars Green">Make Cars Green</a> campaign at the Japanese Grand Prix. For the weekend, the grooves in the <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_tyres" title="Formula One tyres">tyres</a> were painted green to promote <a href="/wiki/Environmentally_friendly" title="Environmentally friendly">environmentally friendly</a> driving.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> FIA president <a href="/wiki/Max_Mosley" title="Max Mosley">Max Mosley</a> said "The FIA is determined to ensure that future investment in motor sport will also help drive the development of technologies that will benefit the public at large."<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> As is normal for a Formula One race, <a href="/wiki/Bridgestone" title="Bridgestone">Bridgestone</a> brought two different tyre compounds to the race; the softer of the two marked by a single white stripe down one of the grooves.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice">Practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2008_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Kazuki_Nakajima_2008_Japan_free_practice.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/40/Kazuki_Nakajima_2008_Japan_free_practice.jpg/220px-Kazuki_Nakajima_2008_Japan_free_practice.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="136" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/40/Kazuki_Nakajima_2008_Japan_free_practice.jpg/330px-Kazuki_Nakajima_2008_Japan_free_practice.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/40/Kazuki_Nakajima_2008_Japan_free_practice.jpg/440px-Kazuki_Nakajima_2008_Japan_free_practice.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2560" data-file-height="1583" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Kazuki_Nakajima_2008_Japan_free_practice.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Home driver <a href="/wiki/Kazuki_Nakajima" title="Kazuki Nakajima">Kazuki Nakajima</a> qualified ahead of teammate <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a>, in 14th.</div></div></div>
<p>Three practice sessions were held before the Sunday race—two on Friday, and a third on Saturday. The Friday morning and afternoon sessions each lasted 90&#160;minutes. The third session was held on Saturday morning and lasted an hour.<sup id="cite_ref-sporting_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sporting-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> The Friday sessions were held in dry and sunny conditions.<sup id="cite_ref-prac1_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prac1-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-prac2_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prac2-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton was quickest in the first session, with a time of 1:18.910 that was less than two-tenths of a second faster than Massa. McLaren driver <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> was just off Massa's pace; Räikkönen, Alonso and Piquet rounded out the top six, still within a second of Hamilton's time.<sup id="cite_ref-prac1_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prac1-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> In the second practice session, <a href="/wiki/Timo_Glock" title="Timo Glock">Timo Glock</a>, of Toyota, was fastest with a time of 1:18.383, ahead of Alonso, Hamilton, Massa, Räikkönen and Red Bull driver <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a>. The top seventeen drivers set times within a second of Glock's fastest lap, indicating a competitive field.<sup id="cite_ref-prac2_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prac2-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> The Saturday morning session was held on a damp track, where grip was poor and many drivers were forced to use the <a href="/wiki/Run-off_area" title="Run-off area">run-off areas</a> after sliding off the track. Kubica was quickest with his final lap of the session at 1:25.087; Glock, Piquet, <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> of BMW, <a href="/wiki/Kazuki_Nakajima" title="Kazuki Nakajima">Kazuki Nakajima</a> of Williams, Red Bull driver <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a>, and Massa rounded out the top seven positions. Hamilton managed only eleventh, but was ahead of Kovalainen and Räikkönen, in 16th and 17th&#160;positions, respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-prac3_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prac3-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Both <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a> and <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> of Force India spent most of the session in their garage, suffering numerous mechanical problems.<sup id="cite_ref-prac3_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prac3-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2008_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The qualifying session on Saturday afternoon was split into three&#160;parts. The first part ran for 20&#160;minutes, and cars that finished the session 16th or lower were eliminated from qualifying. The second part of qualifying lasted 15&#160;minutes and eliminated cars that finished in positions&#160;11 to 15. The final part of qualifying determined the positions from first to tenth, and decided <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>. Cars which failed to make the final session could refuel before the race, so ran lighter in those sessions. Cars which competed in the final session of qualifying were not allowed to refuel before the race, and as such carried more fuel than in the previous sessions.<sup id="cite_ref-sporting_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sporting-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatleft" style="width:35%;&#32;;"><style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r887775652">.mw-parser-output .quotebox{background-color:#F9F9F9;border:1px solid #aaa;box-sizing:border-box;padding:10px;font-size:88%;max-width:100%}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft{margin:0.5em 1.4em 0.8em 0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright{margin:0.5em 0 0.8em 1.4em}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.centered{margin:0.5em auto 0.8em auto}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft p,.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright p{font-style:inherit}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-title{background-color:#F9F9F9;text-align:center;font-size:larger;font-weight:bold}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:before{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" “ ";vertical-align:-45%;line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:after{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" ” ";line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .left-aligned{text-align:left}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .right-aligned{text-align:right}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .center-aligned{text-align:center}.mw-parser-output .quotebox cite{display:block;font-style:normal}@media screen and (max-width:360px){.mw-parser-output .quotebox{min-width:100%;margin:0 0 0.8em!important;float:none!important}}</style>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">A shame. Today I was the quickest man on track but at the wrong moment. In [the third session], I never had the right amount of grip from both sets of tyres that I used. On my first run I was a bit cautious on my "out" lap and so suffered in the first sector, making a mistake at Turn&#160;3. On the second run, I did the opposite and found myself without grip at the end of the lap. So, I've ended up fifth on the grid, which is definitely not an easy place to be.</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style=""><a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a>, following the third qualifying session.<sup id="cite_ref-japferq_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-japferq-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>Hamilton clinched his sixth pole position of the season with a lap time of 1:18.404. He was joined on the front row by Räikkönen, who was fastest for most of the final session.<sup id="cite_ref-japqual_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-japqual-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Provisionally sitting in third as the session drew to a close, Massa was pushed back to fifth as Kovalainen and Alonso put in last-minute laps to fill the second row of the grid.<sup id="cite_ref-japqualitv_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-japqualitv-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Kubica took sixth place, ahead of both Toyota cars of <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> and Glock and the Toro Rossos of <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> and <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9bastien_Bourdais" title="Sébastien Bourdais">Sébastien Bourdais</a>. Coulthard bettered teammate Webber when he qualified 11th; Piquet split the Red Bull drivers in 12th.<sup id="cite_ref-japqualgp_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-japqualgp-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> The only Japanese driver in the field, Nakajima, managed 14th ahead of his Williams teammate <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a>. Heidfeld could only achieve 16th place, ten places behind his BMW teammate Kubica. Heidfeld spent the majority of the first session struggling with the <a href="/wiki/Racing_setup" title="Racing setup">set-up</a> of his car.<sup id="cite_ref-japqualgp_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-japqualgp-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> The Honda cars of <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> filled the ninth row in front of the constructor's home crowd.<sup id="cite_ref-japqualitv_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-japqualitv-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> The Force Indias qualified last; Sutil comfortably outqualified his teammate Fisichella by 0.8&#160;seconds, to sit in 19th&#160;place.<sup id="cite_ref-japqual_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-japqual-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2008_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Robert_Kubica_leads_2008_Japanese_GP.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c3/Robert_Kubica_leads_2008_Japanese_GP.jpg/220px-Robert_Kubica_leads_2008_Japanese_GP.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="144" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c3/Robert_Kubica_leads_2008_Japanese_GP.jpg/330px-Robert_Kubica_leads_2008_Japanese_GP.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c3/Robert_Kubica_leads_2008_Japanese_GP.jpg/440px-Robert_Kubica_leads_2008_Japanese_GP.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1280" data-file-height="837" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Robert_Kubica_leads_2008_Japanese_GP.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a> leads from <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> early in the race.</div></div></div>
<p>The conditions on the grid were dry before the race. The <a href="/wiki/Air_temperature" class="mw-redirect" title="Air temperature">air temperature</a> was 16&#160;°C (61&#160;°F) and the track temperature was 21&#160;°C (70&#160;°F);<sup id="cite_ref-commautosport_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-commautosport-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> conditions were expected to remain consistent throughout the race.<sup id="cite_ref-weatherf1.com_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-weatherf1.com-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Most of the frontrunners began the race on the harder compound tyre; only Massa was using the softer option.<sup id="cite_ref-commbbc_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-commbbc-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Räikkönen accelerated faster than Hamilton off the line, getting ahead of him down the first straight, but the McLaren driver pulled into the Ferrari's <a href="/wiki/Drafting_(aerodynamics)" title="Drafting (aerodynamics)">slipstream</a>, before swerving to overtake Räikkönen in the inside of the corner.<sup id="cite_ref-japmclr_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-japmclr-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Going into the first corner, Hamilton badly locked-up his front wheels while braking and ran wide; although there was no contact, Räikkönen was also forced wide in avoidance. This was then followed by contact with Kovalainen, which  finally forced  Räikkönen off the track. All three drivers dropped back down the field as a result of the incident.<sup id="cite_ref-commautosport_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-commautosport-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-gpreport_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpreport-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Kubica took the lead ahead of Alonso and Kovalainen after the corner, avoiding collisions as other cars locked their tyres on the cold track.<sup id="cite_ref-gpreport_26-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpreport-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fiaracefacts_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiaracefacts-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> Fisichella drove into the back of Barrichello, scattering <a href="/wiki/Carbon_fiber_reinforced_polymer" title="Carbon fiber reinforced polymer">carbon fibre</a> over the track.<sup id="cite_ref-gpreport_26-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpreport-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard collided with Bourdais and then Piquet, sustaining suspension damage and sliding into the barriers after turn two.<sup id="cite_ref-japrbur_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-japrbur-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Nakajima left the track trying to avoid Coulthard, although he managed to rejoin after losing his <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_car#Wings" title="Formula One car">front wing</a>. The Japanese driver made a <a href="/wiki/Pit_stop" title="Pit stop">pit stop</a> at the end of the lap for a new wing.<sup id="cite_ref-japanpitsummary_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-japanpitsummary-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Sebastien_Bourdais_and_Felipe_Massa_2008_Japan.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a2/Sebastien_Bourdais_and_Felipe_Massa_2008_Japan.jpg/220px-Sebastien_Bourdais_and_Felipe_Massa_2008_Japan.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="115" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a2/Sebastien_Bourdais_and_Felipe_Massa_2008_Japan.jpg/330px-Sebastien_Bourdais_and_Felipe_Massa_2008_Japan.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a2/Sebastien_Bourdais_and_Felipe_Massa_2008_Japan.jpg/440px-Sebastien_Bourdais_and_Felipe_Massa_2008_Japan.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2560" data-file-height="1341" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Sebastien_Bourdais_and_Felipe_Massa_2008_Japan.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9bastien_Bourdais" title="Sébastien Bourdais">Sébastien Bourdais</a> and <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> battling for position early in the race</div></div></div>
<p>At the end of the first lap, Kubica led from Alonso, Kovalainen, Trulli, Massa, Hamilton and Räikkönen. On lap two, Massa braked late into the chicane at turn&#160;10, briefly letting Hamilton past, before running over the kerbs on the exit from the corner and hitting the McLaren driver's car.<sup id="cite_ref-massadenial_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-massadenial-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton was spun round by the contact and made a pit stop at the end of the lap for new tyres and more fuel, which dropped him to 18th place.<sup id="cite_ref-commbbc_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-commbbc-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-F1penalties_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1penalties-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Massa continued in seventh place. On lap eight Räikkönen passed Trulli into turn&#160;10 to take fourth position.<sup id="cite_ref-commautosport_22-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-commautosport-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> In third place, more than a second ahead of the Ferrari driver, Kovalainen set a new <a href="/wiki/Fastest_lap" title="Fastest lap">fastest lap</a> of 1:19.258 on lap&#160;12 to which Räikkönen responded with a 1:19.193 four laps later.<sup id="cite_ref-fiaracefacts_27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiaracefacts-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> Two more drivers joined Coulthard in retirement before the first round of fuel stops: Glock made a pit stop on lap five and again on lap six, before retiring because of handling difficulties resulting from a broken seat fixing; Sutil retired on lap nine with a <a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/puncture" class="extiw" title="wikt:puncture">puncture</a> caused by running over carbon fibre shards.<sup id="cite_ref-gpreport_26-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpreport-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fiaracefacts_27-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiaracefacts-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On lap&#160;17, Massa and Hamilton were given <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_regulations#Penalties" title="Formula One regulations">drive-through penalties</a>, Massa for colliding with Hamilton and Hamilton for forcing Räikkönen off the track into turn one.<sup id="cite_ref-fiaracefacts_27-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiaracefacts-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton took his penalty immediately.<sup id="cite_ref-commautosport_22-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-commautosport-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Massa made a pit stop for fuel and tyres on the next lap, before coming into the pit lane again on lap 20 to serve his penalty, which dropped him to 14th place, one place ahead of Hamilton. Meanwhile, Kubica and Räikkönen made pit stops on lap&#160;17 for tyres and fuel.<sup id="cite_ref-japanpitsummary_29-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-japanpitsummary-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> On the same lap, Kovalainen pulled over to the side of the track with engine problems and retired from the race.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Alonso made a pit stop on lap&#160;18, emerging ahead of Kubica to take the provisional lead, with cars in front still to pit.<sup id="cite_ref-fiaracefacts_27-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiaracefacts-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> Trulli, Vettel and Bourdais made pit stops over the following six laps.<sup id="cite_ref-japanpitsummary_29-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-japanpitsummary-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Fisichella retired from the race with gearbox problems on lap&#160;21.<sup id="cite_ref-gpreport_26-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpreport-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fiaracefacts_27-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiaracefacts-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> Piquet took his first pit stop on lap&#160;28, emerging ahead of Bourdais as Alonso opened the gap on Kubica to 7.8&#160;seconds.<sup id="cite_ref-commbbc_24-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-commbbc-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Massa overtook Button to take 12th position one lap later.<sup id="cite_ref-fiaracefacts_27-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiaracefacts-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Kimi_Raikkonen_and_Nelsinho_Piquet_2008_Japan.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/77/Kimi_Raikkonen_and_Nelsinho_Piquet_2008_Japan.jpg/220px-Kimi_Raikkonen_and_Nelsinho_Piquet_2008_Japan.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="153" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/77/Kimi_Raikkonen_and_Nelsinho_Piquet_2008_Japan.jpg/330px-Kimi_Raikkonen_and_Nelsinho_Piquet_2008_Japan.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/77/Kimi_Raikkonen_and_Nelsinho_Piquet_2008_Japan.jpg/440px-Kimi_Raikkonen_and_Nelsinho_Piquet_2008_Japan.jpg 2x" data-file-width="800" data-file-height="557" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Kimi_Raikkonen_and_Nelsinho_Piquet_2008_Japan.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet_Jr." title="Nelson Piquet Jr.">Nelson Piquet</a> closes in on <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> late in the race.</div></div></div>
<p>Alonso lapped consistently in the low 1:19 range, setting the new fastest lap of the race on lap&#160;41, a 1:19.101,<sup id="cite_ref-autocourse2008-pg247_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autocourse2008-pg247-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> to extend his lead over Kubica to more than 12&#160;seconds.<sup id="cite_ref-fiaracefacts_27-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiaracefacts-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> Räikkönen was five seconds behind Kubica in third.<sup id="cite_ref-fiaracefacts_27-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiaracefacts-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> Alonso made a pit stop for the second time on lap&#160;43 and changed to the softer compound tyre.<sup id="cite_ref-japanpitsummary_29-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-japanpitsummary-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Kubica, complaining of <a href="/wiki/Understeer" class="mw-redirect" title="Understeer">understeer</a> over the team radio, made his second stop on lap&#160;46, two laps ahead of Räikkönen.<sup id="cite_ref-commbbc_24-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-commbbc-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-japanpitsummary_29-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-japanpitsummary-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Trulli, Bourdais, Vettel and Piquet made pit stops over the next five laps, their teams giving them sufficient fuel to finish the race.<sup id="cite_ref-japanpitsummary_29-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-japanpitsummary-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> As Bourdais exited the pit-lane in seventh place on lap&#160;51, Massa, who was eighth, but yet to make his final pit stop, attempted to pass him and the two cars collided at the first corner. Massa spun, but rejoined the race behind Bourdais.<sup id="cite_ref-commautosport_22-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-commautosport-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Three laps later, the stewards announced that they were investigating the incident, and would make their decision after the race.<sup id="cite_ref-commbbc_24-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-commbbc-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> On lap&#160;52, Räikkönen attempted to pass Kubica on the approach to turn one after <a href="/wiki/Drafting_(aerodynamics)" title="Drafting (aerodynamics)">drafting</a> behind him up the straight, but Kubica drove right as a blocking manoeuvre, braked late, and defended his position.<sup id="cite_ref-commautosport_22-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-commautosport-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> On the following lap Kubica attempted to replicate his block, but Räikkönen <a href="/wiki/List_of_motorsport_terminology#O" class="mw-redirect" title="List of motorsport terminology">out-braked</a> him into turn one, and the two drew alongside.<sup id="cite_ref-japbmwr_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-japbmwr-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Kubica held the inside line on the turn three left-hander, and drove the <a href="/wiki/Racing_line" title="Racing line">racing line</a> as Räikkönen left the track at the <a href="/wiki/Run-off_area" title="Run-off area">run-off area</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-japferr_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-japferr-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> Räikkönen then rejoined behind the BMW driver.<sup id="cite_ref-japferr_35-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-japferr-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> Kubica faced similar challenges from Räikkönen over the next two laps into turn one, but he successfully defended his position.<sup id="cite_ref-commautosport_22-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-commautosport-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Fernando_Alonso_won_2008_Japanese_GP.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6d/Fernando_Alonso_won_2008_Japanese_GP.jpg/220px-Fernando_Alonso_won_2008_Japanese_GP.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="104" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6d/Fernando_Alonso_won_2008_Japanese_GP.jpg/330px-Fernando_Alonso_won_2008_Japanese_GP.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6d/Fernando_Alonso_won_2008_Japanese_GP.jpg/440px-Fernando_Alonso_won_2008_Japanese_GP.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2560" data-file-height="1205" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Fernando_Alonso_won_2008_Japanese_GP.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> celebrates his second consecutive race victory.</div></div></div>
<p>Massa made a pit stop on lap&#160;53, and rejoined behind Heidfeld in tenth.<sup id="cite_ref-lapchart_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapchart-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> He subsequently set the fastest lap of the race on lap&#160;55, a 1:18.426.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Meanwhile, Piquet was able to close the gap on Räikkönen to under a second,<sup id="cite_ref-commbbc_24-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-commbbc-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> before losing time by running wide at turn five on lap&#160;60.<sup id="cite_ref-commautosport_22-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-commautosport-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> Massa passed Heidfeld for ninth on the same lap, and began closing in on Webber.<sup id="cite_ref-fiaracefacts_27-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiaracefacts-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> On lap&#160;65, Massa drafted down the straight and attempted to pass Webber,<sup id="cite_ref-fiaracefacts_27-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiaracefacts-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> who defended his position by driving to the right.<sup id="cite_ref-commautosport_22-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-commautosport-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Crossing the track boundary into the end of the pit-lane, Massa managed to pass Webber, and out-braked him into turn one, to take eighth place.<sup id="cite_ref-gpreport_26-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpreport-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Alonso crossed the finish line on lap&#160;67 to take his second win of the season, five seconds ahead of Kubica.<sup id="cite_ref-classification_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-classification-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Räikkönen was third, ahead of Piquet, Trulli, Bourdais, Vettel and Massa.<sup id="cite_ref-classification_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-classification-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Webber took ninth on the line, ahead of Heidfeld, who struggled with a heavy car and failed to improve on a poor qualifying performance.<sup id="cite_ref-f1quotes_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1quotes-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> Rosberg finished in front of Hamilton, in 11th. Barrichello and Button took the next two positions; both drivers blamed their Honda cars for their uncompetitive performance at the Fuji circuit.<sup id="cite_ref-f1quotes_39-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1quotes-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> Nakajima finished last, in 15th, unable to recover after his forced pit-stop early in the race.<sup id="cite_ref-f1quotes_39-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1quotes-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div style="clear:both;"></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2008_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatright" style="width:28%;&#32;;"><link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r887775652"/>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">Well, again difficult to believe&#160;... I cannot believe it right now but obviously back to back wins is a very nice feeling and the team did a great job to improve the car. We are now maybe just behind Ferrari and McLaren and this is completely amazing.</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style=""><a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>, speaking after the race.<sup id="cite_ref-pressconference_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconference-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>The top three finishers appeared in the subsequent <a href="/wiki/Press_conference" title="Press conference">press conference</a> where Alonso said that the decision to run a shorter second <a href="/wiki/List_of_motorsport_terminology#S" class="mw-redirect" title="List of motorsport terminology">stint</a> than Kubica (between the first and second pit stops) was his decision: "Sometimes you can do it, sometimes you can't but today the car was perfect and I was able to do it."<sup id="cite_ref-pressconference_40-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconference-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> Alonso added that he had confidence in the car to perform at the remaining Grands Prix: "The feeling I have now is that we can do anything".<sup id="cite_ref-pressconference_40-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconference-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> Kubica said that his second-placed finish was better than his win at the <a href="/wiki/2008_Canadian_Grand_Prix" title="2008 Canadian Grand Prix">Canadian Grand Prix</a> earlier in the season.<sup id="cite_ref-pressconference_40-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconference-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> He added that to finish on the podium after BMW Sauber's failure to improve the car from the beginning of the season was a "great result for the team in, I think, a very difficult moment".<sup id="cite_ref-pressconference_40-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconference-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> Räikkönen said that he was "a bit disappointed because being in first place in the first corner but then being pushed out didn't help and being in the front could have given us a better result but anyhow, that's racing".<sup id="cite_ref-pressconference_40-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconference-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> He added that Kovalainen's impact with him at the first corner had caused handling difficulties in his car, which left him unable to improve on his third-placed finish.<sup id="cite_ref-pressconference_40-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconference-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Forty minutes after the race, Bourdais received a 25-second penalty from the stewards for his collision with Massa on lap&#160;50.<sup id="cite_ref-commbbc_24-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-commbbc-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-bourdaispenalty_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bourdaispenalty-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> This demoted him from sixth to tenth, and promoted Massa to seventh, giving him one more Championship point.<sup id="cite_ref-bourdaispenalty_41-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bourdaispenalty-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> Bourdais blamed Massa for the incident:
</p>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r960796168"/><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>I did everything I could not to run into him and he just squeezed and turned and behaved like I didn't exist, like I wasn't there. What am I supposed to do? ... It's just a little bit of respect, you give each other room and then everything goes right, but if you don't for sure it's going to be an incident.<sup id="cite_ref-bourdaisreaction_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bourdaisreaction-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Kubica_Warszawa.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fc/Kubica_Warszawa.jpg/220px-Kubica_Warszawa.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="313" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fc/Kubica_Warszawa.jpg/330px-Kubica_Warszawa.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/f/fc/Kubica_Warszawa.jpg 2x" data-file-width="351" data-file-height="500" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Kubica_Warszawa.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a> described his second place as better than <a href="/wiki/2008_Canadian_Grand_Prix" title="2008 Canadian Grand Prix">his win in Canada</a> earlier in the season.</div></div></div>
<p>Massa denied responsibility, and agreed with the stewards' decision: "I think there's little to say: I had already entered the turn and he hit me from behind, spinning me round."<sup id="cite_ref-gpquotes_43-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpquotes-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> The penalty was largely criticized in the media. GrandPrix.com called the penalty "bizarre", saying that Bourdais "could not just disappear".<sup id="cite_ref-gpreport_26-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpreport-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/James_Allen_(Formula_One_commentator)" class="mw-redirect" title="James Allen (Formula One commentator)">James Allen</a> of <a href="/wiki/ITV_(TV_network)" title="ITV (TV network)">ITV</a> said that in light of FIA race director <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Whiting" title="Charlie Whiting">Charlie Whiting's</a> announcement prior to the race, which indicated that cars exiting the pit-lane would have <a href="/wiki/Priority_(right_of_way)" class="mw-redirect" title="Priority (right of way)">right of way</a>, the penalty was "ridiculous".<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> French sporting newspaper <i><a href="/wiki/L%27%C3%89quipe" title="L&#39;Équipe">L'Équipe</a></i> criticized the stewards for their intervention, saying that both drivers held their lines and the collision was just a racing incident.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Mark_Blundell" title="Mark Blundell">Mark Blundell</a>, who drove in Formula One for five years, called for former drivers to be part of the stewards meetings which award penalties: "You can examine pieces of paper, graphs, telemetry, but you don't know what's going on in a driver's brain until you've experienced it."<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> Blundell's suggestion eventually became fact - the 2010 season introduced a system whereby a former F1 driver acted as a steward during each Grand Prix.
</p><p>Lewis Hamilton was criticized for his aggressive drive into the first corner by much of the British press. <a href="/wiki/Edward_Gorman" class="mw-redirect" title="Edward Gorman">Edward Gorman</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Times" title="The Times">The Times</a></i> described Hamilton's move as "impetuosity and untamed aggression", adding that Hamilton "gambled with a kamikaze attempt to get past Räikkönen".<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC's</a> Andrew Benson said that "Hamilton is still in a strong position but the Englishman will have to cut out the mistakes that have characterised his season if he is not to lose the championship for the second year in a row."<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> In Italy, <i><a href="/wiki/La_Gazzetta_dello_Sport" title="La Gazzetta dello Sport">La Gazzetta dello Sport</a></i> said that Hamilton's start suffered from "his usual excessive aggression".<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton said "I made a mistake and I paid for it."<sup id="cite_ref-bourdaispenalty_41-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bourdaispenalty-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Massa's touch on Hamilton's car on lap two was labelled by the McLaren driver "as deliberate as it could be".<sup id="cite_ref-gpreport_26-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpreport-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Massa rejected the allegation, saying "I had two wheels on the gravel. I could not stop the car and I was on the gravel because he pushed me into the gravel."<sup id="cite_ref-massadenial_30-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-massadenial-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Media coverage of the incident suggested that though the contact was Massa's fault, it was unintentional.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> Simon Arron, writing for <i><a href="/wiki/The_Daily_Telegraph" title="The Daily Telegraph">The Daily Telegraph</a></i>, said Hamilton's accusation of deliberate contact was "unworthy and unwise", adding that the contact was simply a racing incident.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> The gap between the drivers in the Drivers' Championship after the race stood at five points, in Hamilton's favour, with two races remaining.<sup id="cite_ref-pointsFIA_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pointsFIA-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> In the Constructors' Championship, McLaren's failure to score points, combined with Ferrari's third and seventh, moved Ferrari to a seven-point lead.<sup id="cite_ref-pointsFIA_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pointsFIA-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
