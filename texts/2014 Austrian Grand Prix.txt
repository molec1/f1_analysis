<p>The <b>2014 Austrian Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>Formula 1 Grosser Preis Von Österreich 2014</b>)<sup id="cite_ref-formal_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-formal-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Auto_racing" title="Auto racing">motor race</a> held on 22 June 2014 at the <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Ring" title="Red Bull Ring">Red Bull Ring</a> in <a href="/wiki/Spielberg,_Styria" title="Spielberg, Styria">Spielberg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Austria" title="Austria">Austria</a>. It was the eighth round of the <a href="/wiki/2014_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2014 Formula One season">2014 season</a> and marked the 28th running of the <a href="/wiki/Austrian_Grand_Prix" title="Austrian Grand Prix">Austrian Grand Prix</a> and the 27th time it had been held as a round of the Formula One World Championship. It was the first Austrian Grand Prix held since <a href="/wiki/2003_Austrian_Grand_Prix" title="2003 Austrian Grand Prix">2003</a>. The 71-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> after starting from third position.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> His teammate <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> finished second with <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a> third for the <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> team.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> started the race from pole position, his first since the <a href="/wiki/2008_Brazilian_Grand_Prix" title="2008 Brazilian Grand Prix">2008 Brazilian Grand Prix</a> and the last of his career.<sup id="cite_ref-Q_result_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Q_result-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Massa had one previous participation in an Austrian Grand Prix, back in 2002, while from the last Austrian Grand Prix to be held before 2014, which was in 2003, only three drivers (Fernando Alonso, Kimi Räikkönen, and Jenson Button) returned to drive in the 2014 Austrian Grand Prix.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2014_Austrian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2014_Austrian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Grand Prix was contested by eleven teams, each of two drivers. The teams, also known as <a href="/wiki/Formula_One#Constructors" title="Formula One">constructors</a>, were <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lotus_F1" title="Lotus F1">Lotus</a>, <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>, <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso</a>, <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marussia_F1" title="Marussia F1">Marussia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Caterham_F1" title="Caterham F1">Caterham</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-preview_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-preview-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_tyres" title="Formula One tyres">Tyre</a> supplier <a href="/wiki/Pirelli" title="Pirelli">Pirelli</a> brought four different tyre compounds for the race; two dry compounds, the soft "primes" and the supersoft "options", and two <a href="/wiki/Rain_tyre" title="Rain tyre">wet-weather compounds</a>, the intermediate and full wet.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Drag_reduction_system" title="Drag reduction system">drag reduction system</a> (DRS) had two activation zones for the race; one was on the start/finish straight from the final to first corners, and the second on the straight between turns 2 and 3.<sup id="cite_ref-preview_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-preview-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Going into the race, Mercedes driver <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> led the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a> with 140 points, ahead of teammate <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> on 118 points and <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> on 79. <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> was fourth on 69 points while <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> was fifth on 60 points. In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a> Mercedes were leading with 258 points and Red Bull were second on 139 points. Ferrari with 87 points and Force India with 77 points contended for third place, with McLaren fifth on 66 points.<sup id="cite_ref-preview_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-preview-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Mercedes had so far dominated the championship, winning six out of the previous seven races, with Ricciardo winning the <a href="/wiki/2014_Canadian_Grand_Prix" title="2014 Canadian Grand Prix">Canadian Grand Prix</a>. Championship competitor <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Magnussen" title="Kevin Magnussen">Kevin Magnussen</a> had gained one second-place finish, while <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a>, Vettel (twice), <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a> and Alonso had achieved third place podium finishes.<sup id="cite_ref-preview_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-preview-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice_and_qualifying">Practice and qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2014_Austrian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Practice and qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> was the fastest in the first free practice session with a lap time of 1:11.295.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Teammate <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> was the fastest in the second free practice session with a lap time of 1:09.542.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a> was the fastest in the third free practice session with a lap time of 1:09.848.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> became the first driver other than Hamilton and Rosberg to claim pole position in 2014 with a Q3 lap time of 1:08.759.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> He was joined on the front row by teammate Valtteri Bottas; it was the team's first front row lockout since <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> did so, at the <a href="/wiki/2003_German_Grand_Prix" title="2003 German Grand Prix">2003 German Grand Prix</a> at <a href="/wiki/Hockenheimring" title="Hockenheimring">Hockenheim</a>.
</p><p>Hamilton failed to post a time in Q3, ending up ninth on the grid after <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a>'s penalty was applied. On his first flying lap in Q3 – having been 0.4 seconds faster than Bottas' provisional pole time of 1:08.846 through the first two sectors – Hamilton ran wide at the final corner and had his lap time deleted for exceeding track limits. On his second and final lap Hamilton locked his rear brakes under braking for turn 2 and spun.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> failed to reach Q3, although his teammate <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> did.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2014_Austrian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the start, Massa led the field into the first corner, with Rosberg passing Bottas, but being repassed the following straight. Hamilton gained five places on the first lap, after passing two cars on the run into the first corner and two further cars heading towards turn 2, before taking another spot from <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> at turn 7. As a result, he ended the first lap only one position and 0.9 seconds behind his teammate, despite starting ninth. <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> once again suffered from technical problems, losing drive on the first lap and attempting to retire on lap two. However, his car then regained drive and he was able to continue, albeit a lap down. After damaging his front wing halfway through the race, he later retired from <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a>'s home Grand Prix on lap 34.
</p><p>At the front, the Mercedes cars were able to pass the <a href="/wiki/Williams_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams F1">Williams</a> cars by stopping earlier and undercutting them during the pitstop phases. Rosberg passed both Williams' during the first stops, and Hamilton took second position during the second round, despite suffering two slow pit stops. Sergio Pérez once again drove a good race with a different tyre strategy, only running the super soft compound for the last 16 laps and successfully challenging Kevin Magnussen for sixth place, despite starting sixteenth following a grid penalty.
</p><p>For much of the race both Mercedes cars had to manage overheating brake issues. On lap 55 Hamilton was held up by backmarkers, losing around a second. During the final laps, Hamilton was slowly gaining on teammate Rosberg, and despite having lost a total of 1.9 seconds to his teammate through slower pit stops he began the final lap 1.1 seconds behind. However, he was not close enough to his teammate to challenge him and backed off at the end of the lap. Rosberg took the chequered flag for his third win of the season.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2014_Austrian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As a result of the race, <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> extended his championship lead to 29 points, the largest it had been all season.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> admitted he was frustrated by his slow pit stops, but said that second was still a "good result" considering his qualifying position.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>, who managed to finish closely behind <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> in the Williams and with a smaller gap to Mercedes than usual, later described the race as his best of the season so far.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The race was a remarkable one for Mercedes Team Principal <a href="/wiki/Toto_Wolff" title="Toto Wolff">Toto Wolff</a>, who in being a shareholder of both Mercedes and his former team Williams effectively held some ownership of the 1st, 2nd, 3rd, and 4th placed cars in the race. This remarkable achievement was sweetened by it coming at the Austrian's home race.
