<p>The <b>1997 French Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>LXXXIII French Grand Prix</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held at <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Nevers_Magny-Cours" title="Circuit de Nevers Magny-Cours">Circuit de Nevers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Magny-Cours" title="Magny-Cours">Magny-Cours</a>, <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> on 29 June 1997. It was the eighth race of the <a href="/wiki/1997_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1997 Formula One World Championship">1997 Formula One World Championship</a>.
</p><p>The 72-lap race was won from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> by <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_F310" title="Ferrari F310">Ferrari</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a> second in a <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW19" title="Williams FW19">Williams</a>-<a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a> third in the other Ferrari. The win, Schumacher's third of the season and second in succession, gave him a 14-point lead in the Drivers' Championship over <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a>, who finished fourth in the other Williams-Renault.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_summary">Race summary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1997_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Race summary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>At the start, Michael Schumacher led away from pole with Frentzen in the Williams behind him all the way.  It was a bad start for Damon Hill, who lost his wing at the first corner after going off and didn't feature much after that.
</p><p>The race was relatively uneventful until towards the end when a nearby thunderstorm caused rain around the circuit. Some drivers pitted for wet tyres whilst others stayed on their dry tyres. Several drivers spun under the conditions, including Michael Schumacher. Schumacher maintained his big lead, however
</p><p>In the end, Michael crossed the line well ahead of Frentzen but on the last couple of laps a huge battle was going on between Ralf Schumacher, David Coulthard and Jean Alesi. At the same time Jacques Villeneuve was reeling in Eddie Irvine for the final podium spot.
</p><p>Ralf Schumacher spun, losing sixth place, but as mentioned in the notes, Michael Schumacher (who had lapped him), allowed him back through at the final corner. At the time, commentator Murray Walker said that it was a silly move. However, on the last lap Alesi pushed Coulthard straight off, costing Coulthard fifth place, thus giving Ralf Schumacher the final point because he had unlapped himself.
</p><p>Meanwhile, at the last corner, Villeneuve had caught Irvine. In a desperate lunge at the final corner he spun off, rejoined, and held off Alesi at the flag.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1997_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Pedro_Diniz" title="Pedro Diniz">Pedro Diniz</a> outqualified team-mate <a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a> for the first time.</li>
<li>Michael allowed his brother Ralf to overtake him and unlap himself on the last corner. Ralf subsequently gained the last point of the day as a result, rising from 7th to 6th after Jean Alesi and David Coulthard made contact, with Coulthard stuck in the gravel.</li>
<li>Jarno Trulli switched from Minardi to Prost replacing the injured <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Panis" title="Olivier Panis">Olivier Panis</a>.</li>
<li>Trulli's seat at Minardi went to Tarso Marques.</li>
<li>Norberto Fontana replaced Gianni Morbidelli at Sauber. Morbidelli had suffered from a broken arm.</li></ul>
