<p>The <b>2000 German Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>LXII Großer Mobil 1 Preis von Deutschland</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 30 July 2000 at the <a href="/wiki/Hockenheimring" title="Hockenheimring">Hockenheimring</a> near <a href="/wiki/Hockenheim" title="Hockenheim">Hockenheim</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>. It was the eleventh round of the <a href="/wiki/2000_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2000 Formula One season">2000 Formula One season</a> and the 62nd <a href="/wiki/German_Grand_Prix" title="German Grand Prix">German Grand Prix</a>. The 45-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> after starting from 18th position. <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> finished second for the <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> team with teammate <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> third.
</p><p>Coulthard started from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> alongside <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>. Coulthard's teammate Häkkinen began from third. At the first corner Michael Schumacher moved to the left and <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> collided with him and both drivers retired. Häkkinen took the lead of the race which he held until an intruder penetrated circuit limits on lap 25 causing drivers to make pit stops under <a href="/wiki/Safety_car" title="Safety car">safety car</a> conditions. Barrichello, meanwhile, had gained thirteen positions to run fifth until the first safety car period. Häkkinen retook the lead after Coulthard pitted on lap 27. Barrichello stayed out on dry slick tyres, taking the lead which he held to clinch the first victory of his Formula One career. It was also the first victory for a Brazilian driver in F1 since <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> won at <a href="/wiki/Adelaide_Street_Circuit" title="Adelaide Street Circuit">Adelaide</a> in <a href="/wiki/1993_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="1993 Australian Grand Prix">1993</a>.
</p><p>Barrichello's victory was considered popular amongst the Formula One paddock as it came after a setback during his career. The race result meant Häkkinen and Coulthard were tied for second place but the points advantage to Michael Schumacher was reduced to two points. Barrichello remained a further eight points behind the McLaren drivers. In the Constructors' Championship McLaren remained four points behind the Ferraris, who were 80 points ahead of <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> with six races of the season remaining. The track intruder, named as 47-year-old Frenchman Robert Sehli, later apologised for his actions and was fined by the track's management.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2000_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Grand Prix was contested by eleven teams, each of two drivers.<sup id="cite_ref-entrants_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-entrants-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The teams, also known as <a href="/wiki/Formula_One#Constructors" title="Formula One">constructors</a> were, <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jaguar_Racing" title="Jaguar Racing">Jaguar</a>, <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a>, <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Prost_Grand_Prix" title="Prost Grand Prix">Prost</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arrows_Grand_Prix_International" title="Arrows Grand Prix International">Arrows</a>, <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a> and <a href="/wiki/British_American_Racing" title="British American Racing">BAR</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-entrants_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-entrants-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_tyre" class="mw-redirect" title="Formula One tyre">Tyre</a> supplier <a href="/wiki/Bridgestone" title="Bridgestone">Bridgestone</a> brought four different tyre types to the race: two dry compounds, the soft and the medium, and two <a href="/wiki/Rain_tyre" title="Rain tyre">wet-weather compounds</a>, the intermediate and full wet.<sup id="cite_ref-preview_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-preview-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Going into the race, Ferrari driver <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> led the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a> with 56 <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Championship_points_scoring_systems" title="List of Formula One World Championship points scoring systems">points</a>, ahead of <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> on 50 points and <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> on 48 points. <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> was fourth with 36 points whilst <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> was fifth with 18 points.<sup id="cite_ref-points_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a> Ferrari were leading with 92 points, McLaren and Williams with 88 points and 19 points were second and third respectively, whilst Benetton with 18 points and BAR with 12 points contended with fourth place.<sup id="cite_ref-points_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Ferrari and McLaren had so far dominated the championship winning ten out of the ten previous races. Championship competitors Barrichello and Fisichella had each gained second-place finishes, whilst <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> and <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a> had achieved third place podium finishes.<sup id="cite_ref-points_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Following the <a href="/wiki/2000_Austrian_Grand_Prix" title="2000 Austrian Grand Prix">Austrian Grand Prix</a> on 16 July, the teams conducted testing sessions at three circuits from 18–21 July to prepare for the Grand Prix. McLaren, Benetton, Jordan, Jaguar, Sauber and BAR went to <a href="/wiki/Silverstone_Circuit" title="Silverstone Circuit">Silverstone</a> over three days. <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Panis" title="Olivier Panis">Olivier Panis</a>, McLaren's test driver, set the fastest time on the first day of testing.<sup id="cite_ref-testgp_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-testgp-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Jaguar test driver <a href="/wiki/Luciano_Burti" title="Luciano Burti">Luciano Burti</a> crashed at Stowe corner where his car's <a href="/wiki/Suspension_(vehicle)" class="mw-redirect" title="Suspension (vehicle)">suspension</a>, front and rear wings, and sidepod were damaged. The resulting incident caused a brief halt to testing and Jaguar shipped a <a href="/wiki/List_of_motorsport_terminology#S" class="mw-redirect" title="List of motorsport terminology">spare car</a> for the next day's testing.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Panis remained fastest on the second day.<sup id="cite_ref-testgp_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-testgp-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Trulli damaged his suspension and rear wing, resulting in repairs which limited his team's testing time. Burti suffered another incident when he lost his right rear wheel.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Fisichella topped the third and final day's running.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Ferrari test driver <a href="/wiki/Luca_Badoer" title="Luca Badoer">Luca Badoer</a> spent four days at the <a href="/wiki/Fiorano_Circuit" title="Fiorano Circuit">Fiorano Circuit</a> where he concentrated on testing engine and aerodynamic development whilst Michael Schumacher did practice starts and component testing on the fourth day.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Jaguar's <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a> was passed fit in the days leading up to the race. He arrived at the previous race ill with a suspected bout of appendicitis and withdrew at the end of the Friday practice sessions. He was replaced by Burti.<sup id="cite_ref-irvinefitness_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-irvinefitness-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Irvine later travelled to a hospital in London where he was diagnosed with a swollen intestine.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Irvine said he felt ready to race again: "I'm looking forward to Hockenheim. I have been keeping tabs on the team's Silverstone test this week and we're all encouraged by what has been achieved."<sup id="cite_ref-irvinefitness_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-irvinefitness-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Jordan's new car, the <a href="/wiki/Jordan_EJ10" title="Jordan EJ10">EJ10B</a>, was also introduced that weekend; the team had used their primary 2000 car, the EJ10, for the previous ten races. Originally due to be introduced at the previous race in Austria, the car was required to undergo <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile</a> (FIA) safety tests on its bodywork and Jordan wanted to develop more spare parts for the EJ10B, delaying the car's race début.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice">Practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2000_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:David_Coulthard_2009.jpg" class="image"><img alt="Upper body of a man in his late thirties with a shaved, greying beard, wearing a T-shirt with light blue and dark red lines. <p>Four practice sessions were held before the Sunday race—two on Friday, and two on Saturday. The Friday morning and afternoon sessions each lasted an hour. The third and final practice sessions were held on Saturday morning and lasted 45 minutes.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The Friday sessions were held in dry and cloudy conditions, becoming damp during the day, resulting in the track surface becoming slippery.<sup id="cite_ref-fiafriday_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiafriday-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher set the first session's fastest time with a lap of 1 minute and 43.532 seconds, almost six-tenths of a second faster than Häkkinen. Barrichello was just off Häkkinen's pace with Coulthard setting the fourth fastest time. Frentzen and BAR driver <a href="/wiki/Ricardo_Zonta" title="Ricardo Zonta">Ricardo Zonta</a> followed in fifth and sixth positions respectively. Fisichella, <a href="/wiki/Mika_Salo" title="Mika Salo">Mika Salo</a>, Ralf Schumacher and Herbert completed the top ten fastest drivers in the session. Williams driver <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> crashed into the pit lane at the end of the barriers and lost his <a href="/wiki/Downforce#Front" title="Downforce">front wing</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> In the second practice session, Michael Schumacher did not manage to improve his lap time but was still quickest. Frentzen was running quicker and was second fastest. The two McLaren drivers were running slower—Häkkinen in third and Coulthard fifth–although both drivers were undertaking race set-up and brake performance testing. They were separated by Barrichello. Trulli was sixth fastest, ahead of Zonta. Villeneuve, Fisichella and Salo followed in the top ten.<sup id="cite_ref-fiafriday_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiafriday-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Saturday morning sessions were held in damp weather conditions with intermittent rain.<sup id="cite_ref-fiasaturday_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiasaturday-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Häkkinen set the third session's fastest time, a 1:44.144, one-tenth of a second quicker than <a href="/wiki/Pedro_de_la_Rosa" title="Pedro de la Rosa">Pedro de la Rosa</a>. Coulthard was third fastest, ahead of Frentzen and Salo. Trulli, Barrichello, Michael Schumacher, Villeneuve and Fisichella rounded out the top ten fastest drivers.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> In the final practice session, Häkkinen set the fastest time of the day, a 1:41.658; Coulthard finished with the third-fastest time. The Ferrari drivers were again quick—Michael Schumacher in second and Barrichello in fourth. Frentzen slipped to fifth, with Fisichella sixth fastest and was happy with his car's feel. Button was seventh fastest, in front of Salo. Villeneuve and Trulli completed the top ten ahead of qualifying.<sup id="cite_ref-fiasaturday_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiasaturday-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Fisichella's car suffered an engine failure in the closing seconds of the session and a crash by Michael Schumacher after the session concluded meant that the German was forced to use his team's spare car for qualifying.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2000_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatleft" style="width:35%;&#32;;"><style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r887775652">.mw-parser-output .quotebox{background-color:#F9F9F9;border:1px solid #aaa;box-sizing:border-box;padding:10px;font-size:88%;max-width:100%}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft{margin:0.5em 1.4em 0.8em 0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright{margin:0.5em 0 0.8em 1.4em}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.centered{margin:0.5em auto 0.8em auto}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft p,.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright p{font-style:inherit}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-title{background-color:#F9F9F9;text-align:center;font-size:larger;font-weight:bold}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:before{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" “ ";vertical-align:-45%;line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:after{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" ” ";line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .left-aligned{text-align:left}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .right-aligned{text-align:right}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .center-aligned{text-align:center}.mw-parser-output .quotebox cite{display:block;font-style:normal}@media screen and (max-width:360px){.mw-parser-output .quotebox{min-width:100%;margin:0 0 0.8em!important;float:none!important}}</style>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">A perfect result. There was a bit of a rush at the start as everybody went out on the track to take advantage while it was still dry. I was probably able to read the conditions better than everybody else and managed to be very quick in the middle sector. I had my second run towards the end because the surface can dry very quickly and I wanted to make sure I was in a position to take advantage and defend my pole position if necessary. The race tomorrow will be very close but I want the ten points.</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style="">David Coulthard, following the qualifying session.<sup id="cite_ref-qualifyingquotes_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualifyingquotes-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>Saturday's afternoon one hour qualifying session saw each driver limited to twelve laps, with the starting order decided by their fastest laps. During this session, the <a href="/wiki/107%25_rule" title="107% rule">107% rule</a> was in effect, which necessitated each driver set a time within 107 per cent of the quickest lap to qualify for the race.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The session was held in damp weather with intermittent rain.<sup id="cite_ref-fiasaturday_15-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiasaturday-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-temp_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-temp-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard achieved his second pole position of the season, his first at the Hockenheimring, with a time of 1:45.697. He was joined on the front row of the <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_motorsport_terms#G" title="Glossary of motorsport terms">grid</a> by Michael Schumacher who was 1.3 seconds slower than Coulthard.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcquali_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcquali-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Fisichella qualified third, though he was happy with his performance despite using his team's T-car after suffering a spin on his first run.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> He was later fined $5,000 for not placing his car at the pit-lane weighbridge.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Häkkinen qualified fourth, three hundredths of a second slower than Fisichella. Häkkinen later admitted that he was cautious about going off the race track due to the weather conditions.<sup id="cite_ref-qualifyingquotes_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualifyingquotes-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> de la Rosa qualified fifth giving Arrows their best qualifying performance of the season. Trulli and Wurz were satisfied with their sixth and seventh place qualifying positions.<sup id="cite_ref-fiasaturday_15-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiasaturday-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Herbert in the quicker of the two Jaguars took eighth.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcquali_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcquali-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Villeneuve secured ninth using his team's spare car after spinning on track which disrupted Frentzen's running.<sup id="cite_ref-fiasaturday_15-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiasaturday-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Irvine rounded out the top ten.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcquali_20-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcquali-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Verstappen had problems starting his engine, resulting in a lack of qualifying laps but managed to clinch eleventh. Zonta qualified twelfth having used a new engine and could not set a faster lap time after being blocked by <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a>. He was ahead of Alesi's teammate <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcquali_20-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcquali-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Ralf Schumacher qualified in 14th, quicker than teammate Button in 16th. They were separated by Salo.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcquali_20-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcquali-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Frentzen spent the majority of qualifying 107% outside of the pole sitters time but took 17th; his first quick time was disallowed after cutting the <a href="/wiki/Chicane" title="Chicane">chicane</a> to set a faster lap and to run in clean air.<sup id="cite_ref-fiasaturday_15-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiasaturday-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello used his teammate's car after his developed oil-leak issues and qualified 18th.<sup id="cite_ref-fiasaturday_15-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiasaturday-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Sauber's <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Diniz" title="Pedro Diniz">Pedro Diniz</a> took 19th, in front of Alesi in 20th. The Minardi drivers qualified at the back of the grid; <a href="/wiki/Gast%C3%B3n_Mazzacane" title="Gastón Mazzacane">Gastón Mazzacane</a> in 21st and <a href="/wiki/Marc_Gen%C3%A9" title="Marc Gené">Marc Gené</a> in 22nd.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcquali_20-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcquali-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Warm-up">Warm-up</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2000_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Warm-up">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The drivers took to the track at 09:30 <a href="/wiki/Central_European_Summer_Time" title="Central European Summer Time">Central European Summer Time</a> (<a href="/wiki/Greenwich_Mean_Time" title="Greenwich Mean Time">GMT</a>+2) for a 30-minute warm-up session.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_12-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> David Coulthard maintained his good performance from qualifying by setting the fastest time, a 1:44.065; Häkkinen was second in the other McLaren car. de la Rosa and Michael Schumacher completed the top four.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> The session was disrupted by incidents as Coulthard and de la Rosa struck the barriers—both incidents required <a href="/wiki/Motorsport_marshal" title="Motorsport marshal">marshals</a> to collect debris—whilst Villeneuve and Verstappen collided going into the circuit's first chicane.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2000_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Mika_Hakkinen_DTM_Norisring_2009.jpg" class="image"><img alt="Head and shoulders of a man in his forties with blonde hair and grey eyes. He is wearing a white shirt which bears the Mercedes-Benz and AMG logos, and is holding a microphone in both of his hands." src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/11/Mika_Hakkinen_DTM_Norisring_2009.jpg/170px-Mika_Hakkinen_DTM_Norisring_2009.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="253" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/11/Mika_Hakkinen_DTM_Norisring_2009.jpg/255px-Mika_Hakkinen_DTM_Norisring_2009.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/11/Mika_Hakkinen_DTM_Norisring_2009.jpg/340px-Mika_Hakkinen_DTM_Norisring_2009.jpg 2x" data-file-width="516" data-file-height="769" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Mika_Hakkinen_DTM_Norisring_2009.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> (pictured in 2009) took the lead at the start of the race, but fell to second after his second pit stop.</div></div></div>
<p>The race started at 14:00 local time. The conditions for the start of the race was dry, but became damp and wet as the race progressed. The <a href="/wiki/Air_temperature" class="mw-redirect" title="Air temperature">air temperature</a> was 21&#160;°C (70&#160;°F) and the track temperature ranged from 21 to 25&#160;°C (70 to 77&#160;°F).<sup id="cite_ref-temp_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-temp-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-f1comrace_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrace-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> During the <a href="/wiki/Parade_lap" title="Parade lap">parade lap</a> Button's engine did not start and he was forced to start from the back of the grid.<sup id="cite_ref-gprace_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gprace-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> Häkkinen accelerated faster than teammate Coulthard and Michael Schumacher off the line, getting ahead of both drivers going into the first corner. Michael Schumacher moved to his left with Fisichella hitting him from behind and both drivers went off into the turn one barriers.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello made the best start in the field, moving from 18th to 10th place at the end of the first lap.<sup id="cite_ref-gprace_27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gprace-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> At the completion of the first lap, Häkkinen led from Coulthard, Trulli, de la Rosa, Irvine, Herbert, Verstappen, Villeneuve, Zonta, Barrichello, Ralf Schumacher, Wurz, Heidfeld, Salo, Diniz, Frentzen, Gené, Alesi, Button and Mazzacane.<sup id="cite_ref-lapchart_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapchart-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Häkkinen began to maintain his lead from teammate Coulthard.<sup id="cite_ref-lapchart_29-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapchart-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> On lap two Herbert moved into fifth position after Irvine dropped to seventh place when Verstappen overtook him. Further down the field Barrichello continued to gain positions when he passed both BAR drivers for eighth. The McLaren drivers managed to maintain a gap to Trulli who set the fastest lap of the race, 1:46.321.<sup id="cite_ref-lapchart_29-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapchart-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Irvine lost a further position to Barrichello on lap three, as Frentzen claimed 14th from Diniz.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_28-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> On lap four, Frentzen made up a further position by passing Heidfeld for 13th. On the same lap Verstappen locked up his tyres to avoid a collision with Herbert.<sup id="cite_ref-f1comrace_26-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrace-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> This allowed Barrichello to pass Verstappen for sixth position in the run up to the Clark chicane on lap five. Herbert lost fifth position to Barrichello on lap six with Frentzen continuing to move up the field by passing Ralf Schumacher and Wurz for eleventh.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_28-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello began setting consecutive fastest laps as he closed the gap to de la Rosa.<sup id="cite_ref-lapchart_29-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapchart-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Zonta lost 10th position when he was passed by Frentzen on lap seven. On the next lap, Verstappen suffered a worrying moment when half of his engine cover was shed from its chassis. Frentzen managed to gain a further four positions in the next four laps.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_28-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Further down, Ralf Schumacher took Zonta for eleventh place and Diniz passed teammate Salo for 14th position.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_28-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-lapchart_29-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapchart-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello caught de la Rosa by lap twelve and passed him to take fourth and started to come under pressure from Frentzen.<sup id="cite_ref-lapchart_29-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapchart-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Herbert pulled over to the side of the track with gearbox problems on lap 13.<sup id="cite_ref-gprace_27-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gprace-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello passed Trulli for third position at the second corner two laps later.<sup id="cite_ref-f1comrace_26-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrace-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello became the first driver to make a <a href="/wiki/Pit_stop" title="Pit stop">pit stop</a> by coming in on lap 17. Frentzen made his pit stop one lap later and re-joined in sixth place.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_28-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> By lap 20, Häkkinen had a lead of 1.4 seconds over Coulthard, who in turn was almost 22 seconds ahead of Trulli. de la Rosa was a further 2.1 seconds behind Trulli, and was being caught by Barrichello in fifth who set a new fastest lap, a 1:44.300.<sup id="cite_ref-lapchart_29-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapchart-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Villeneuve overtook Irvine to claim eighth place on lap 22.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_28-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Barrichello_2002.jpg" class="image"><img alt="Head, arms and torso of a man in his thirties with his right arm slightly bending. He is wearing a scarlet red racing overalls which displays the Vodaphone, Shell, Bridgestone and Ferrari logos at the front with a white background embroiled on the bottom top." src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/36/Barrichello_2002.jpg/170px-Barrichello_2002.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="298" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/36/Barrichello_2002.jpg/255px-Barrichello_2002.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/36/Barrichello_2002.jpg/340px-Barrichello_2002.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1128" data-file-height="1975" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Barrichello_2002.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> (pictured in 2002) took the first win of his Formula One career.</div></div></div>
<p>On lap 25, a man appeared beside the barriers of the circuit on the straight heading towards turn 2. He ran across the track to avoid being caught by marshals. The incident prompted the deployment of the <a href="/wiki/Safety_car" title="Safety car">safety car</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-gprace_27-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gprace-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> Teams immediately brought their drivers into the pit lane to take advantage of the scenario with Trulli and de la Rosa the first to make pit stops. McLaren called in Häkkinen to the pit lane on lap 27 and Coulthard remained out on worn tyres. The Scot pitted on the following lap and emerged in sixth place.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_28-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> On the same lap, the man was caught and escorted off the circuit.<sup id="cite_ref-f1comrace_26-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrace-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Racing resumed on lap 29 when the safety car pulled into the pit lane.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_28-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Trulli immediately came under pressure from Barrichello and withstood the Brazilian's attempts to pass him. Ralf Schumacher spun at turn 3 on lap thirty and Verstappen was forced wide in avoidance. Later on Alesi collided with Diniz and struck the barriers, having lost his car's two left wheels.<sup id="cite_ref-f1comrace_26-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrace-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> The safety car was once again deployed as <a href="/wiki/Carbon-fiber-reinforced_polymer" class="mw-redirect" title="Carbon-fiber-reinforced polymer">carbon fibre</a> was scattered across the grass.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_28-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-f1comrace_26-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrace-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The safety car was withdrawn at the end of lap 31 and the race got underway again with Häkkinen in the lead. Wurz, meanwhile, immediately pulled off the circuit with a gearbox failure with Salo almost colliding with the Benetton.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_28-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Light rain began to fall on lap 33. Button pitted on the following lap and his Williams pit crew changed his dry tyres to wets. Gené became the race's seventh retirement with a failed engine. All drivers, apart from Barrichello, Coulthard, Frentzen and Zonta, pitted for wet tyres.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_28-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Villeneuve spun after minor contact with teammate Zonta but managed to continue.<sup id="cite_ref-f1comrace_26-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrace-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Trulli was issued with a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_regulations#Penalties" title="Formula One regulations">ten-second stop-go penalty</a> on lap 37 because he was observed overtaking Barrichello under <a href="/wiki/Racing_flags#Yellow_flag" title="Racing flags">yellow flags</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-gprace_27-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gprace-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> He took the penalty immediately and rejoined in 11th.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_28-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Zonta was also issued with a penalty but spun into the tyre wall at turn 12 and retired.<sup id="cite_ref-f1comrace_26-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrace-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard became the final driver to have pitted on the 38th lap and rejoined in fifth.<sup id="cite_ref-lapchart_29-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapchart-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Button then passed de la Rosa for sixth place.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_28-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At the completion of lap 39, with the pit stops completed, the running order was Barrichello, Häkkinen, Frentzen, Salo, Coulthard, Button, de la Rosa, Verstappen, Ralf Schumacher, Villeneuve, Trulli, Heidfeld, Mazzacane and Irvine.<sup id="cite_ref-lapchart_29-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapchart-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Frentzen retired with a gearbox failure on lap 40 as Coulthard moved into third after passing Salo.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_28-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Heidfeld became the final retirement of the race with an alternator failure on lap 40.<sup id="cite_ref-gprace_27-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gprace-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> Three laps later Button caught Salo and overtook him to clinch fourth position. It began to rain more heavily by lap 44 whilst Barrichello opened a gap to 11.5 seconds, and won the race after 45 laps to secure the first victory of his Formula One career in a time of 1'25:34.418,<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_28-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> at an average speed of 133.807 miles per hour (215.341&#160;km/h).<sup id="cite_ref-results_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-results-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Häkkinen finished second in his McLaren 7.4 seconds behind Barrichello, with teammate Coulthard third. Button clinched his best result of the 2000 season with fourth, ahead of Salo in fifth and de la Rosa rounded out the points-scoring positions in sixth. Ralf Schumacher, Villeneuve, Trulli, Irvine and Mazzacane filled the next five positions, with Heidfeld the last of the classified runners despite his alternator failure.<sup id="cite_ref-results_30-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-results-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2000_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatright" style="width:33%;&#32;;"><link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r887775652"/>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">"I just feel great. On the last lap, I heard the guys telling me I was going to win, but it's such a long lap here. Also, it's been such a long time (since my last victory) that I had almost lost the taste. And I can promise you that it was getting more and more tricky today, because the rain was falling in separate places. Yesterday was such a bad day that I was just thinking to myself that this one had to be mine. I told myself it could come good, so let's make it as simple as possible. But I still can't believe it.</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style="">Rubens Barrichello, speaking after the race.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>The top three drivers appeared on the podium to collect their trophies and in the subsequent <a href="/wiki/News_conference" class="mw-redirect" title="News conference">press conference</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_12-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello's maiden Formula One victory was very popular amongst spectators and team personnel,<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> because it came after a setback earlier in his career, not least a serious accident during practice for the <a href="/wiki/1994_San_Marino_Grand_Prix" title="1994 San Marino Grand Prix">1994 San Marino Grand Prix</a> that left him unconscious.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello dedicated his victory to fellow Brazilian and three-time World Champion <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> who had helped him during the early phase of his career.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> He also revealed that it was his decision to stay out on dry tyres as he believed he would have an advantage on the straights and the chicanes,<sup id="cite_ref-pressconf_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconf-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> although he flat-spotted a tyre in the closing stages of the Grand Prix which reduced his visibility.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Häkkinen said that he felt "in control" during the first phase of the event, although he admitted that he was conservative on the wet tyres and could have secured victory on dry tyres.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard revealed that he utilised tactics performed by Michael Schumacher at the start after he sought clarification on the rules regarding such manoeuvres.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> He additionally commented that he was unable to talk to his team via radio in the forest sections which caused him to stay out for an additional lap when Häkkinen pitted.<sup id="cite_ref-pressconf_35-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconf-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Button was delighted with his then career best finish of fourth position and praised his team for the timing to the switch to wet-weather tyres.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> Salo described his race as "hard" because of him opting to have a high downforce set-up meaning he was slower than his rivals on the straights.<sup id="cite_ref-quotes_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-quotes-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> Additionally he revealed that towards the end of the race, he did not have oil in his engine with temperatures continuously rising.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> de la Rosa scored points for the second time in the season, having taken fifth at the <a href="/wiki/2000_European_Grand_Prix" title="2000 European Grand Prix">European Grand Prix</a>. de la Rosa thought that the Grand Prix was "strange" though he was happy with the effort of his team.<sup id="cite_ref-quotes_40-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-quotes-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher, who retired on the first lap, after Fisichella collided with him, accused the latter of causing the incident. "I am out of the race not because of David (Coulthard) but because of Fisichella." he said.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> Fisichella however said that he was maintaining his racing line and believed that drivers should choose their preferred racing line before he described his Grand Prix as a "waste".<sup id="cite_ref-quotes_40-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-quotes-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher's manager <a href="/wiki/Willi_Weber" title="Willi Weber">Willi Weber</a> rejected reports that the accident was part of a conspiracy.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The majority of media attention, however, was focussed on the intruder who penetrated the circuit's barriers on lap 25. He was revealed to be man named Robert Sehli,<sup id="cite_ref-sehliapology_44-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sehliapology-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> a 47-year-old from France who worked for <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz" title="Mercedes-Benz">Mercedes-Benz</a> in a production factory at Le Mans.<sup id="cite_ref-intrudergpcom_45-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-intrudergpcom-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> He also had three children.<sup id="cite_ref-intruderatlasf1_46-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-intruderatlasf1-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> Sehli informed the press that he was protesting against his dismissal from his job after 22 years on health grounds.<sup id="cite_ref-intruderatlasf1_46-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-intruderatlasf1-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> Additional information revealed that he planned to protest 15 seconds before the start of the formation lap but was prevented by marshals who dragged him off the circuit.<sup id="cite_ref-intrudergpcom_45-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-intrudergpcom-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> Sehli had attempted to organise something similar at the <a href="/wiki/2000_French_Grand_Prix" title="2000 French Grand Prix">French Grand Prix</a> before the FIA Photographers' Delegate stopped him in the pit lane ten laps before the race ended.<sup id="cite_ref-gprace_27-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gprace-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-intrudergpcom_45-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-intrudergpcom-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> He was released on a <a href="/wiki/Deutsche_Mark" title="Deutsche Mark">DM</a> 2,000 ($945) bail the Monday after the German Grand Prix.<sup id="cite_ref-intruderatlasf1_46-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-intruderatlasf1-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> Retired Formula One driver <a href="/wiki/Hans-Joachim_Stuck" title="Hans-Joachim Stuck">Hans-Joachim Stuck</a> said that Sehli had "succeeded in avenging himself on Mercedes."<sup id="cite_ref-intruderatlasf1_46-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-intruderatlasf1-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> Ferrari technical director <a href="/wiki/Ross_Brawn" title="Ross Brawn">Ross Brawn</a> said that Sehli's actions were "very, very dangerous" and that similar intrusions "should never be allowed to happen again."<sup id="cite_ref-bbcintruder_47-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcintruder-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> However the Vice-President of Mercedes-Benz Motorsport <a href="/wiki/Norbert_Haug" title="Norbert Haug">Norbert Haug</a> criticised the police's approach towards Sehli calling it a "scandal".<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> The Hockenheimring track owners Hockenheimring GmbH announced that it filed a trespassing charge on Sehli.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcintruder_47-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcintruder-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> He was later awarded compensation from Mercedes-Benz and apologised for the track invasion.<sup id="cite_ref-sehliapology_44-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sehliapology-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> On 16 December Sehli won a court case against Mercedes-Benz who were ordered to pay F91,000 for "dismissing him without any conclusive reasons".<sup id="cite_ref-selhicomp_49-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-selhicomp-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> He was however fined £600 by Hockenheimring GmbH for breaching circuit limits.<sup id="cite_ref-selhicomp_49-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-selhicomp-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The race result meant that Michael Schumacher's lead in the Drivers' Championship was reduced to two points. Häkkinen moved into joint second, level on points with team-mate Coulthard; both were eight points ahead of Barrichello, with Fisichella remaining a distant fifth.<sup id="cite_ref-points_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> In the Constructors' Championship, McLaren reduced Ferrari's lead to four points. Williams, with 22 points, increased the gap to their rivals Benetton to four points, whilst BAR maintained fifth place on 12 points, with six races of the season remaining.<sup id="cite_ref-points_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
