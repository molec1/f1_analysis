<p>The <b>1955 Argentine Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held at <a href="/wiki/Aut%C3%B3dromo_Oscar_Alfredo_G%C3%A1lvez" class="mw-redirect" title="Autódromo Oscar Alfredo Gálvez">Buenos Aires</a> on January 16, 1955. It was race 1 of 7 in the <a href="/wiki/1955_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1955 World Championship of Drivers">1955 World Championship of Drivers</a>.
</p><p>The race was won from third on the grid by <a href="/wiki/Juan_Manuel_Fangio" title="Juan Manuel Fangio">Juan Manuel Fangio</a> for <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a>.  <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> drivers <a href="/wiki/Giuseppe_Farina" title="Giuseppe Farina">Nino Farina</a> and <a href="/wiki/Maurice_Trintignant" title="Maurice Trintignant">Maurice Trintignant</a> finished both second and third in two three-way shared drives with <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Froil%C3%A1n_Gonz%C3%A1lez" title="José Froilán González">José Froilán González</a> and <a href="/wiki/Umberto_Maglioli" title="Umberto Maglioli">Umberto Maglioli</a> respectively. The high temperatures of the Argentinian summer proved to be very taxing for both drivers and cars. Fangio and <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Mieres" title="Roberto Mieres">Roberto Mieres</a> were the only two drivers able to complete the race without handing their car to another driver.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1955_Argentine_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><b>Shared Drives:</b>
<ul><li>Car #12: <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Froil%C3%A1n_Gonz%C3%A1lez" title="José Froilán González">José Froilán González</a> (60 laps), <a href="/wiki/Giuseppe_Farina" title="Giuseppe Farina">Nino Farina</a> (20 laps), and <a href="/wiki/Maurice_Trintignant" title="Maurice Trintignant">Maurice Trintignant</a> (16 laps). They shared the 6 points for second place.</li>
<li>Car #10: <a href="/wiki/Giuseppe_Farina" title="Giuseppe Farina">Nino Farina</a> (50 laps), <a href="/wiki/Umberto_Maglioli" title="Umberto Maglioli">Umberto Maglioli</a> (22 laps), and <a href="/wiki/Maurice_Trintignant" title="Maurice Trintignant">Maurice Trintignant</a> (22 laps). They shared the 4 points for third place.</li>
<li>Car #8: <a href="/wiki/Hans_Herrmann" title="Hans Herrmann">Hans Herrmann</a> (30 laps), <a href="/wiki/Karl_Kling" title="Karl Kling">Karl Kling</a> (30 laps), and <a href="/wiki/Stirling_Moss" title="Stirling Moss">Stirling Moss</a> (34 laps). They shared the 3 points for fourth place.</li>
<li>Car #28: <a href="/wiki/Harry_Schell" title="Harry Schell">Harry Schell</a> (50 laps), and <a href="/wiki/Jean_Behra" title="Jean Behra">Jean Behra</a> (38 laps).</li>
<li>Car #22: <a href="/wiki/Luigi_Musso" title="Luigi Musso">Luigi Musso</a> (50 laps), <a href="/wiki/Sergio_Mantovani" title="Sergio Mantovani">Sergio Mantovani</a> (20 laps), and <a href="/wiki/Harry_Schell" title="Harry Schell">Harry Schell</a> (13 laps).</li>
<li>Car #20: <a href="/wiki/Sergio_Mantovani" title="Sergio Mantovani">Sergio Mantovani</a> (30 laps), <a href="/wiki/Jean_Behra" title="Jean Behra">Jean Behra</a> (14 laps), and <a href="/wiki/Luigi_Musso" title="Luigi Musso">Luigi Musso</a> (10 laps).</li>
<li>Car #26: <a href="/wiki/Clemar_Bucci" title="Clemar Bucci">Clemar Bucci</a> (30 laps), <a href="/wiki/Harry_Schell" title="Harry Schell">Harry Schell</a> (14 laps), and <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Menditeguy" title="Carlos Menditeguy">Carlos Menditeguy</a> (10 laps).</li>
<li>Car #36: <a href="/wiki/Eugenio_Castellotti" title="Eugenio Castellotti">Eugenio Castellotti</a> (20 laps) and <a href="/wiki/Luigi_Villoresi" title="Luigi Villoresi">Luigi Villoresi</a> (15 laps).</li></ul></li>
<li>This race holds the unusual distinction of being the hottest race ever for a Formula One event, 40&#160;°C (about 104&#160;°F). This record has since been tied twice, at the <a href="/wiki/1984_Dallas_Grand_Prix" title="1984 Dallas Grand Prix">1984 Dallas Grand Prix</a> and the <a href="/wiki/2005_Bahrain_Grand_Prix" title="2005 Bahrain Grand Prix">2005 Bahrain Grand Prix</a>.</li>
<li>The extreme heat led to a scoring nightmare of 16 driver substitutions. Only <a href="/wiki/Juan_Manuel_Fangio" title="Juan Manuel Fangio">Juan Manuel Fangio</a> and <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Mieres" title="Roberto Mieres">Roberto Mieres</a> finished the race without a relief driver.</li>
<li>Fangio suffered severe burns to his leg which, for the entire duration of the race, was rubbing against the chassis frame which was being heated by the exhaust. It took him 3 months to recover; his next race in Monaco was not until late May. It left a permanent scar on his leg later in life.</li>
<li>Grand Prix debut for: <a href="/wiki/Eugenio_Castellotti" title="Eugenio Castellotti">Eugenio Castellotti</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alberto_Uria" title="Alberto Uria">Alberto Uria</a>. Only Grand Prix appearance for <a href="/wiki/Jes%C3%BAs_Iglesias" title="Jesús Iglesias">Jesús Iglesias</a>.</li>
<li>Last Grand Prix appearance for: <a href="/wiki/Pablo_Birger" title="Pablo Birger">Pablo Birger</a>, <a href="/wiki/Clemar_Bucci" title="Clemar Bucci">Clemar Bucci</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Sergio_Mantovani" title="Sergio Mantovani">Sergio Mantovani</a>.</li></ul>
