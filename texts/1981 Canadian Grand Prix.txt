<p>The <b>1981 Canadian Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held at <a href="/wiki/Circuit_Gilles_Villeneuve" title="Circuit Gilles Villeneuve">Circuit Île Notre-Dame</a>, <a href="/wiki/Montreal" title="Montreal">Montreal</a> on 27 September 1981. It was the fourteenth race of the <a href="/wiki/1981_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1981 Formula One World Championship">1981 Formula One World Championship</a>.
</p><p>The race was run in wet conditions, and ended after 63 of the scheduled 70 laps due to the two-hour time limit.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Frenchman <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Laffite" title="Jacques Laffite">Jacques Laffite</a> won in a <a href="/wiki/Ligier_JS17" title="Ligier JS17">Ligier</a>-<a href="/wiki/Equipe_Matra_Sports" title="Equipe Matra Sports">Matra</a>, with Northern Ireland's <a href="/wiki/John_Watson_(racing_driver)" title="John Watson (racing driver)">John Watson</a> second in a <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/1" title="McLaren MP4/1">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Ford_Motor_Company" title="Ford Motor Company">Ford</a> and local hero <a href="/wiki/Gilles_Villeneuve" title="Gilles Villeneuve">Gilles Villeneuve</a> third in a <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_126C" title="Ferrari 126C">Ferrari</a>. Brazilian <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a> finished fifth in his <a href="/wiki/Brabham_BT49" title="Brabham BT49">Brabham</a>-Ford, having started from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>, while Drivers' Championship rival, Argentine <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Reutemann" title="Carlos Reutemann">Carlos Reutemann</a>, could only manage tenth in his <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW07" title="Williams FW07">Williams</a>-Ford. Piquet thus moved within one point of Reutemann with one race to go, while the win gave Laffite an outside chance of the title. Despite both Reutemann and Australian teammate <a href="/wiki/Alan_Jones_(racing_driver)" title="Alan Jones (racing driver)">Alan Jones</a> failing to score, Williams clinched the Constructors' Championship.
</p><p>This would turn out to be Laffite's final F1 victory, as well as the last for the Ligier team for fifteen years, until the <a href="/wiki/1996_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="1996 Monaco Grand Prix">1996 Monaco Grand Prix</a>. It was also the last time the Canadian Grand Prix was held in the fall, as the race would move to June <a href="/wiki/1982_Canadian_Grand_Prix" title="1982 Canadian Grand Prix">the following year</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-race">Pre-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1981_Canadian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Pre-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Prior to the race, <a href="/wiki/Alan_Jones_(racing_driver)" title="Alan Jones (racing driver)">Alan Jones</a> announced that he was retiring from the sport after clinching the title <a href="/wiki/1980_Formula_One_season" title="1980 Formula One season">the previous year</a>. Rumors were also spreading around in paddock that <a href="/wiki/Niki_Lauda" title="Niki Lauda">Niki Lauda</a> had tested for <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> at <a href="/wiki/Donington_Park" title="Donington Park">Donington Park</a> and that he was planning a comeback.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1981_Canadian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nelson Piquet clinched pole with a time of 1:29.221, with rival <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Reutemann" title="Carlos Reutemann">Carlos Reutemann</a> alongside him on the front row. The top ten were completed by: Jones, Prost, Mansell, Rebaque, de Angelis, Arnoux, Watson and Laffite respectively.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1981_Canadian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The race start was marred with bad weather. Immediately after the start, Jones took the lead after a minor collision with Reutemann which left Reutemann behind. Toward the middle of the pack, Arnoux and Pironi had a collision with resulted in Arnoux spinning out of the race. On lap 7, Jones spun and Piquet had to take evasive action, resulting in both drivers dropping down the pecking order. Prost took the lead with Laffite, who had climbed from tenth, second. Prost's lead did not last long, as Laffite overtook him on lap 13, and kept the lead to the end of the race.
</p><p>Villeneuve had a minor accident that damaged his front wing. The wing flew up only to be stuck up in the air, blocking Villeneuve's race-line vision. With his forward vision impaired, he used his peripheral vision to navigate using the yellow track markers as reference. After racing this way for minutes he used vibration on the ribbed curbing,  to the point it flew off the car.  This way he managed to keep control until the end of the race, without a front wing and under the rain.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Prost was eventually overtaken by Villeneuve and Watson, before retiring with an accident on lap 48. Watson was able to catch and pass Villeneuve a few laps later and the two finished second and third respectively, with Villeneuve clinching his third and last podium of the year on home soil. Bruno Giacomelli of Alfa Romeo, who had started fifteenth, came home fourth, with pole-man Piquet coming in behind him.
</p>
