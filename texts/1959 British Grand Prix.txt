<p>The <b>1959 British Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held at the <a href="/wiki/Aintree_Circuit" class="mw-redirect" title="Aintree Circuit">Aintree Circuit</a> on 18 July 1959. It was race 5 of 9 in the <a href="/wiki/1959_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1959 World Championship of Drivers">1959 World Championship of Drivers</a> and race 4 of 8 in the <a href="/wiki/1959_International_Cup_for_Formula_One_Manufacturers" class="mw-redirect" title="1959 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers">1959 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers</a>. It was the 14th <a href="/wiki/British_Grand_Prix" title="British Grand Prix">British Grand Prix</a> and the third to be held at the <a href="/wiki/Aintree_Motor_Racing_Circuit" title="Aintree Motor Racing Circuit">Aintree Motor Racing Circuit</a>, a circuit mapped out in the grounds of the <a href="/wiki/Aintree_Racecourse" title="Aintree Racecourse">Aintree Racecourse</a> horse racing venue. The race was held over 75 laps of the four kilometre circuit for a race distance of 362 kilometres.
</p><p>The race was won by Australian <a href="/wiki/Jack_Brabham" title="Jack Brabham">Jack Brabham</a> taking his second Grand Prix victory in a works <a href="/wiki/Cooper_T51" title="Cooper T51">Cooper T51</a>. Brabham dominated the race, leading all 75 laps to win by 22 seconds over British driver <a href="/wiki/Stirling_Moss" title="Stirling Moss">Stirling Moss</a> driving a <a href="/wiki/British_Racing_Partnership" title="British Racing Partnership">British Racing Partnership</a> entered <a href="/wiki/BRM_P25" title="BRM P25">BRM P25</a>. It was the first time a BRP entry finished in the top three. Brabham's <a href="/wiki/Cooper_Car_Company" title="Cooper Car Company">Cooper Car Company</a> teammate, New Zealader <a href="/wiki/Bruce_McLaren" title="Bruce McLaren">Bruce McLaren</a> finished in third place, just 0.2 seconds behind Moss, having lost second place late in the race. <a href="/wiki/Harry_Schell" title="Harry Schell">Harry Schell</a> finished fourth for the <a href="/wiki/Owen_Racing_Organisation" class="mw-redirect" title="Owen Racing Organisation">Owen Racing Organisation</a> BRM team a lap behind Brabham.
</p><p>The British Grand Prix had the biggest entry of the season outside the Indianapolis 500 with 30 cars competing and 24 starting the race, all despite the absence of Ferrari. Strikes in Italy trapped the team at home, leaving the British teams to fight over the race. Ferrari's new lead driver <a href="/wiki/Tony_Brooks_(racing_driver)" title="Tony Brooks (racing driver)">Tony Brooks</a> was given a release and started the race in a <a href="/wiki/Vanwall" title="Vanwall">Vanwall</a> but was the first to retire with misfire after 13 laps having started in a lowly 17th after winning the <a href="/wiki/1959_French_Grand_Prix" title="1959 French Grand Prix">French Grand Prix</a> a few weeks earlier.
</p><p>The win saw Brabham expand his points lead over Brooks to 13 points. Moss and McLaren moved into fourth place just half a point behind the absent <a href="/wiki/Phil_Hill" title="Phil Hill">Phil Hill</a>.
</p><p>On the last lap of this race, McLaren became the youngest driver to set a fastest lap in Formula One, aged 21 years and 322 days. It was another 44 years before <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> relieved him of that achievement with fastest lap in the 2003 Canadian GP. He was a day younger aged 21 years and 321 days.
</p>
