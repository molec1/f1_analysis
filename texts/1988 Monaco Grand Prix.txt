<p>The <b>1988 Monaco Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>46e Grand Prix de Monaco</b><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 15 May 1988 at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Monaco" title="Circuit de Monaco">Circuit de Monaco</a>, <a href="/wiki/Monte_Carlo" title="Monte Carlo">Monte Carlo</a>. It was the third race of the <a href="/wiki/1988_FIA_Formula_One_World_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="1988 FIA Formula One World Championship">1988 FIA Formula One World Championship</a>.
</p><p>The 78-lap race was won by Frenchman <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/4" title="McLaren MP4/4">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Honda_in_Formula_One" title="Honda in Formula One">Honda</a>. Prost took his fourth and final Monaco win after Brazilian teammate <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> crashed out late on while leading comfortably. Austrian driver <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> finished second in a <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_F1/87#F1/87/88C" title="Ferrari F1/87">Ferrari</a>, with Italian teammate <a href="/wiki/Michele_Alboreto" title="Michele Alboreto">Michele Alboreto</a> third.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1988_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1988_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Honda_in_Formula_One" title="Honda in Formula One">Hondas</a> continued their dominant form from the previous race at <a href="/wiki/1988_San_Marino_Grand_Prix" title="1988 San Marino Grand Prix">Imola</a>, as <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> took pole position by some 1.4 seconds from team-mate <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a>, with a further 1.2 seconds back to the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> of <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> in third. Berger's team-mate <a href="/wiki/Michele_Alboreto" title="Michele Alboreto">Michele Alboreto</a> was fourth, while <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> was fifth in the <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a>, the quickest of the <a href="/wiki/Naturally_aspirated_engine" title="Naturally aspirated engine">naturally-aspirated</a> cars, albeit some 3.6 seconds behind Senna. Completing the top ten were <a href="/wiki/Alessandro_Nannini" title="Alessandro Nannini">Alessandro Nannini</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Derek_Warwick" title="Derek Warwick">Derek Warwick</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Arrows_Grand_Prix_International" title="Arrows Grand Prix International">Arrows</a>, <a href="/wiki/Riccardo_Patrese" title="Riccardo Patrese">Riccardo Patrese</a> in the second Williams, <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Cheever" title="Eddie Cheever">Eddie Cheever</a> in the second Arrows and <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Palmer" title="Jonathan Palmer">Jonathan Palmer</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_Racing" title="Tyrrell Racing">Tyrrell</a>, while among the non-qualifiers was <a href="/wiki/Satoru_Nakajima" title="Satoru Nakajima">Satoru Nakajima</a>, whose <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Lotus</a> was powered by the same Honda <a href="/wiki/Turbocharger" title="Turbocharger">turbo</a> engine as the McLaren.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1988_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the start, Senna led away, while Berger overtook Prost when the Frenchman momentarily could not engage second gear. Behind them, a variety of accidents occurred at Sainte-Dévote: <a href="/wiki/Alex_Caffi" title="Alex Caffi">Alex Caffi</a> hit the wall in his <a href="/wiki/Dallara" title="Dallara">Dallara</a>; <a href="/wiki/Philippe_Streiff" title="Philippe Streiff">Philippe Streiff</a>, who had started 12th in his <a href="/wiki/Automobiles_Gonfaronnaises_Sportives" title="Automobiles Gonfaronnaises Sportives">AGS</a>, retired when an accelerator cable broke; and World Champion <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a> collided with Cheever, which forced the Brazilian to retire at the end of the first lap and thus end a disastrous weekend for the Lotus team.
</p><p>The running order of Senna, Berger, Prost, Mansell, Alboreto and Nannini was maintained until lap 33 when Alboreto took Mansell off at the Swimming Pool, ending the Englishman's race. Nannini then suffered a gearbox failure on lap 39. On lap 51, Patrese collided with <a href="/wiki/Philippe_Alliot" title="Philippe Alliot">Philippe Alliot</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Lola_LC88" title="Lola LC88">Lola</a> while trying to lap him; Alliot retired immediately.
</p><p>On lap 54, Prost passed Berger for second on the run to Sainte-Dévote, though he was some 50 seconds behind Senna. In an effort to put some pressure on his team-mate, he started trading fastest laps with him. With 11 laps remaining, McLaren team boss <a href="/wiki/Ron_Dennis" title="Ron Dennis">Ron Dennis</a> radioed Senna to slow down to ensure a safe 1-2 finish, allowing Prost to gain six seconds.<sup id="cite_ref-Rubython2011_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rubython2011-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On lap 67, Senna lost concentration at Portier, causing him to spin his McLaren into the barrier and damage the car's front suspension. Immediately afterwards, he went to his home in Monaco to contemplate losing a race that he had dominated from the first time he took to the track for free practice on Thursday morning; the McLaren team did not even hear from him until that evening, when he walked into the pits as they were packing up.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Prost thus took his fourth Monaco win in five years, with Berger some 20 seconds behind and Alboreto a further 21 seconds back. Warwick finished fourth after a race-long battle with Palmer, while Patrese recovered from his collision with Alliot to take the final point, passing the other Lola of <a href="/wiki/Yannick_Dalmas" title="Yannick Dalmas">Yannick Dalmas</a> on the last lap. Patrese's point was also the first-ever World Championship point scored by a Judd-powered car and his first point for Williams.
</p>
