<p>The <b>2017 Russian Grand Prix</b> (Russian: <span lang="ru">Гран-при России 2017 года</span>, <small>romanized:&#160;</small><i lang="ru-Latn" title="Russian-language romanization">Gran-pri Rossii 2017 goda</i>), officially known as the <b>2017 Formula 1 VTB Russian Grand Prix</b>,<sup id="cite_ref-formal_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-formal-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_racing" title="Formula One racing">Formula One motor race</a> that took place on 30 April 2017 as the fourth round of the <a href="/wiki/2017_FIA_Formula_One_World_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="2017 FIA Formula One World Championship">2017 FIA Formula One World Championship</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-prov._calendar_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prov._calendar-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The fifty-two lap race was held at the <a href="/wiki/Sochi_Autodrom" title="Sochi Autodrom">Sochi Autodrom</a>, marking the fourth running of the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Grand_Prix" title="Russian Grand Prix">Russian Grand Prix</a> as a round of the Formula One World Championship. <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> started the race from pole, with his teammate <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> in second in Ferrari's first front-row lockout since the <a href="/wiki/2008_French_Grand_Prix" title="2008 French Grand Prix">2008 French Grand Prix</a>. <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a> won the race by 0.617 seconds over Vettel, the smallest winning margin since the <a href="/wiki/2016_Abu_Dhabi_Grand_Prix" title="2016 Abu Dhabi Grand Prix">2016 Abu Dhabi Grand Prix</a>.
</p><p>Going into the race, Vettel led <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> in the World Drivers' Championship by seven points, with Bottas third; they remained in the same order after the race. In the World Constructors' Championship, <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> was in the lead ahead of <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> before the race, with <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a> third; Mercedes was ahead of Ferrari after the race.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2017_Russian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Driver_changes">Driver changes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2017_Russian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Driver changes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Further information: <a href="/wiki/2017_Formula_One_World_Championship#Teams_and_drivers" title="2017 Formula One World Championship">2017 Formula One World Championship §&#160;Teams and drivers</a></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Sergey_Sirotkin_(racing_driver)" title="Sergey Sirotkin (racing driver)">Sergey Sirotkin</a> made his first appearance of the season with <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>, driving in place of <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a> in the first free practice session.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2017_Russian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The initial start was aborted, as <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> was unable to start, pulling up in the opening to the pit lane because of engine issues. Team radio from the initial parade lap suggested issues with the <a href="/wiki/MGU-K" class="mw-redirect" title="MGU-K">MGU-K</a> electronic power supplementary unit leading to a loss of hybrid power.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-AlonsoDNS_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AlonsoDNS-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Without the energy recovery system (ERS), the <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MCL32" title="McLaren MCL32">MCL32</a> would be uncompetitive and racing would put an unnecessary wear upon the rest of the power unit. Alonso's McLaren teammate <a href="/wiki/Stoffel_Vandoorne" title="Stoffel Vandoorne">Stoffel Vandoorne</a> had already been given a 15-place grid penalty, having exceeded the limit of four exchanges of power unit per driver and car throughout the season stipulated by the <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_regulations" title="Formula One regulations">2017 regulations</a>. With Alonso's car already being at least partially on its fourth power unit of the season on only race 4 out of 20, in the light of the charge failure, a decision was made to retire Alonso from the race in order to preserve the car.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Alonso was unable to even complete the formation lap, suffering from a total car failure before being able to return to the pitlane.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Sainz_Jr." title="Carlos Sainz Jr.">Carlos Sainz Jr.</a> was given a 3-place penalty as a result of causing a collision with <a href="/wiki/Lance_Stroll" title="Lance Stroll">Lance Stroll</a> at the <a href="/wiki/2017_Bahrain_Grand_Prix" title="2017 Bahrain Grand Prix">Bahrain Grand Prix</a>.
</p><p>Because of Alonso's car obstructing the track, the start was aborted by the FIA's race director <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Whiting" title="Charlie Whiting">Charlie Whiting</a>, and the cars were sent on an additional formation lap. The race was accordingly shortened by a lap. <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a> attained his maiden victory after taking first place at the start, passing both Ferraris before turn 2 – aided by starting from the clean side of the grid, and picking up the slipstream behind Vettel. Hamilton attempted the same, but was squeezed on both sides by the Ferrari of Räikkönen, now running 3rd – and Ricciardo of Red Bull, challenging for 4th from 5th. This caused him to be held up and hemmed into his starting grid position, where he would stay for the remainder of the race. Vettel meanwhile pulled away from tight racing of the main straight, and pursued Bottas. Vettel briefly led the race when Bottas went into the pits following his one-stop strategy, but then lost his position again after his own pit stop. He again spent the final third of the race gradually closing the distance between himself and Bottas, coming to within 0.7 seconds just a few laps before the end. Lapped traffic, particularly the delayed Williams of Massa, however, ultimately prevented him from overtaking the race leader, leading to a secure second place for the German. <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> rounded off the podium. He once again set a late fastest lap of 1:36.844 on lap 49, recording not only the fast lap of the race, but also the fastest ever lap at the circuit. At the chequered flag, he was followed by <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>, finishing a distant 4th. Hamilton had struggled with overheating throughout the race.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Non-finishers were <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>, who failed to make it to the grid with a hybrid-charge failure, as well as <a href="/wiki/Jolyon_Palmer" title="Jolyon Palmer">Jolyon Palmer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Romain_Grosjean" title="Romain Grosjean">Romain Grosjean</a> who crashed on the first lap, causing a safety car deployment. <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> retired with brake problems while running in 7th. <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> was running in 6th when he obtained a slow puncture and had to pit for new tyres, falling to 9th behind the Force Indias of <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a> and <a href="/wiki/Esteban_Ocon" title="Esteban Ocon">Esteban Ocon</a> as well as the Renault of <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>There were very few on-track overtakes after the safety car period.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Despite making up two places before turn 2, <a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a>'s Red Bull did not advance further, finishing in 5th, a minute behind Bottas, once again showing <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull's</a> struggles to keep up with <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a>. The team's problems were compounded by the retirement of Daniel Ricciardo, their third mechanical failure in four races.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
