<p>The <b>1987 Belgian Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 17 May 1987 at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Spa-Francorchamps" title="Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps">Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spa,_Belgium" title="Spa, Belgium">Spa</a>. Contested over 43 laps, the race was the 45th <a href="/wiki/Belgian_Grand_Prix" title="Belgian Grand Prix">Belgian Grand Prix</a>, the 33rd to be held at Spa and the fourth since the circuit was redeveloped in 1979, and the third race of the <a href="/wiki/1987_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="1987 Formula One season">1987 Formula One season</a>.
</p><p>The race was won by France's <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> driving a <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/3" title="McLaren MP4/3">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Techniques_d%27Avant_Garde" title="Techniques d&#39;Avant Garde">TAG</a>. This was Prost's second victory of the 1987 season and his second in the Belgian Grand Prix (after <a href="/wiki/1983_Belgian_Grand_Prix" title="1983 Belgian Grand Prix">1983</a>), as well as his 27th Grand Prix victory overall, equalling <a href="/wiki/Jackie_Stewart" title="Jackie Stewart">Jackie Stewart</a>'s all-time record. Prost's Swedish team-mate <a href="/wiki/Stefan_Johansson" title="Stefan Johansson">Stefan Johansson</a> finished second, the only other driver on the same lap, giving McLaren their first 1-2 finish since the previous year's <a href="/wiki/1986_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="1986 Monaco Grand Prix">Monaco Grand Prix</a>. Italy's <a href="/wiki/Andrea_de_Cesaris" title="Andrea de Cesaris">Andrea de Cesaris</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/Brabham_BT56" title="Brabham BT56">Brabham</a>-<a href="/wiki/BMW_in_Formula_One" title="BMW in Formula One">BMW</a>, finished third despite having to push his car over the line as he had run out of fuel.
</p><p>The win gave Prost a five-point lead over Johansson in the Drivers' Championship. <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> was three points further back; a first-lap collision with <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Lotus_99T" title="Lotus 99T">Lotus</a> had ultimately led to his retirement from the race, after which he angrily confronted the Brazilian driver in the Lotus garage.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_summary">Race summary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1987_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Race summary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Qualifying runs saw the two <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW11B" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams FW11B">Williams FW11Bs</a> take the front row, with <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> on the pole, nearly 1.5 seconds faster than <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a>. Certainly, Piquet was still suffering the results of his accident at Imola. <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> took the third place in his <a href="/wiki/Lotus_99T" title="Lotus 99T">Lotus 99T</a> ahead of the two <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_F1/87" title="Ferrari F1/87">Ferrari F1/87s</a> of <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> and <a href="/wiki/Michele_Alboreto" title="Michele Alboreto">Michele Alboreto</a>.
</p><p>The race required two starts. On the first start Mansell took the lead ahead of Senna, Piquet and Alboreto. At the back of the grid <a href="/wiki/Ren%C3%A9_Arnoux" title="René Arnoux">René Arnoux</a> (<a href="/wiki/Ligier_JS29B" class="mw-redirect" title="Ligier JS29B">Ligier JS29B</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Andrea_de_Cesaris" title="Andrea de Cesaris">Andrea de Cesaris</a> (Brabham BT56) tangled, whilst <a href="/wiki/Thierry_Boutsen" title="Thierry Boutsen">Thierry Boutsen</a> (<a href="/wiki/Benetton_B187" title="Benetton B187">Benetton B187</a>) hit Berger's spinning Ferrari F1/87. A more serious accident befell <a href="/wiki/Philippe_Streiff" title="Philippe Streiff">Philippe Streiff</a> who crashed heavily at the <a href="/wiki/Eau_Rouge_corner" class="mw-redirect" title="Eau Rouge corner">Eau Rouge</a> before the wreckage was hit by Tyrrell teammate <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Palmer" title="Jonathan Palmer">Jonathan Palmer</a>; both were unhurt but their <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_DG016" title="Tyrrell DG016">Tyrrell DG016s</a> were reduced to scrap. By virtue of qualifying 23rd to his team mate's 24th, and ironically by crashing first, Streiff was given the spare car for the restart forcing Palmer out of the race.
</p><p>On the second start, Senna led Mansell away, but during lap one the Briton tried to overtake the Brazilian. The two controversially tangled, leading to the retirement of the Lotus 99T. Mansell rejoined the race at the back, until the damage sustained in the collision finally forced him to retire on lap 17. Mansell subsequently visited the Lotus garage where harsh words were exchanged and punches were thrown.
</p><p>Berger retired on lap three with a broken piston whilst on lap 10 second place <a href="/wiki/Michele_Alboreto" title="Michele Alboreto">Michele Alboreto</a>'s wheel bearing broke and <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a> retired with a broken turbo pipe, handing the lead to <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> from <a href="/wiki/Teo_Fabi" title="Teo Fabi">Teo Fabi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Stefan_Johansson" title="Stefan Johansson">Stefan Johansson</a>. The pitstops changed little in the situation, and he maintained this to win easily by 25 seconds, despite concerns with his fuel consumption due to a faulty gauge.
</p><p>This was Prost's 27th win, equalling <a href="/wiki/Jackie_Stewart" title="Jackie Stewart">Jackie Stewart</a>'s record with team-mate Johansson making it a <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> 1-2. <a href="/wiki/Andrea_de_Cesaris" title="Andrea de Cesaris">Andrea de Cesaris</a> drove superbly to finish third for <a href="/wiki/Brabham" title="Brabham">Brabham</a> despite having to push his car over the line with <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Cheever" title="Eddie Cheever">Eddie Cheever</a> (<a href="/wiki/Arrows_A10" title="Arrows A10">Arrows A10</a>), <a href="/wiki/Satoru_Nakajima" title="Satoru Nakajima">Satoru Nakajima</a> (Lotus 99T) and Arnoux taking the remaining points. With the setbacks to the Tyrrell drivers, the Jim Clark points were won by the <a href="/wiki/Lola_LC87" title="Lola LC87">Lola LC87</a> of <a href="/wiki/Philippe_Alliot" title="Philippe Alliot">Philippe Alliot</a>.
</p>
