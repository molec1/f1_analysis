<p>The <b>2003 San Marino Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>XXIII Gran Premio Foster's di San Marino</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held on 20 April 2003 at the <a href="/wiki/Autodromo_Enzo_e_Dino_Ferrari" class="mw-redirect" title="Autodromo Enzo e Dino Ferrari">Autodromo Enzo e Dino Ferrari</a>, <a href="/wiki/Imola" title="Imola">Imola</a>, <a href="/wiki/Emilia-Romagna" title="Emilia-Romagna">Emilia-Romagna</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>, with the race taking place on Easter Sunday. It was the fourth round of the <a href="/wiki/2003_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2003 Formula One season">2003 Formula One season</a>  The 62-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> team after starting from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>. <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a>, driving for <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>, finished second with <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> third in the other Ferrari. The remaining points-scoring positions were filled by <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> (<a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a>), <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> (McLaren), <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> (<a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>), <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a> (Williams) and <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> (<a href="/wiki/British_American_Racing" title="British American Racing">BAR</a>). Schumacher's victory for Ferrari was his and the team's first of the season.
</p><p>Schumacher and his brother Ralf raced despite the death of their mother Elisabeth before the Grand Prix. The brothers led the field at the start with Ralf leading having overtaken Michael and held the lead until the first round of pit stops.
</p><p>As a consequence of the race, Räikkönen increased his lead in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">World Drivers' Championship</a>, over teammate <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> to 13 points with Schumacher climbing to third. In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">World Constructors Championship</a>, McLaren increased their lead to 16 points with Ferrari overtaking <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a> for second.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2003_San_Marino_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2003_San_Marino_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Heading into the fourth race of the season, <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> was leading the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">World Drivers' Championship</a> with 24 points, teammate <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> was second on 15 points, 9 points behind Räikkönen. Behind Räikkönen and Coulthard in the Drivers' Championship, <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> was third on 14 points in a <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> on 10 and 9 points respectively. In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">World Constructors' Championship</a>, McLaren were leading on 39 points and Renault were second on 23 points, with Ferrari on 16 points.<sup id="cite_ref-dukevideof1_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dukevideof1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Autodromo_aerea_poster.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fc/Autodromo_aerea_poster.jpg/250px-Autodromo_aerea_poster.jpg" decoding="async" width="250" height="167" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fc/Autodromo_aerea_poster.jpg/375px-Autodromo_aerea_poster.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fc/Autodromo_aerea_poster.jpg/500px-Autodromo_aerea_poster.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2362" data-file-height="1575" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Autodromo_aerea_poster.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>An aerial view of the <a href="/wiki/Autodromo_Enzo_e_Dino_Ferrari" class="mw-redirect" title="Autodromo Enzo e Dino Ferrari">Autodromo Enzo e Dino Ferrari</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>At the preceding <a href="/wiki/2003_Brazilian_Grand_Prix" title="2003 Brazilian Grand Prix">Brazilian Grand Prix</a>, confusion caused by a red flag at the end of the race led to Räikkönen being declared the winner. An investigation by Formula One's governing body the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">FIA</a> in the days following the race proved that Fisichella had been the actual victor. The investigation discovered that Fisichella started his 56th lap when the red flag was shown and the results were rolled back to 54 laps.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Under the countback rule, the driver leading two laps before a race is stopped is declared the winner. During a meeting organised by the teams, the countback rule would be placed under review having highlighted potential problems.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Fifteen minutes before the start of the first practice session, a ceremony was held in which Räikkönen and McLaren team principal <a href="/wiki/Ron_Dennis" title="Ron Dennis">Ron Dennis</a> presented their winners' trophies to Fisichella and Jordan team principal <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Jordan" title="Eddie Jordan">Eddie Jordan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Ferrari originally planned to debut its new car the <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_F2003-GA" title="Ferrari F2003-GA">F2003-GA</a> at Imola. However, issues with reliability led to the decision to race the <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_F2002" title="Ferrari F2002">F2002</a>. Ferrari stated the F2002 was still a competitive car and believed racing the F2003-GA would be "risky"<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> despite testing the car at Mugello and Fiorano.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice_and_qualifying">Practice and qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2003_San_Marino_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Practice and qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Three practice sessions were held before the race; the first was held from 11:00 to 12:00 local time on Friday, and two 45 minute timed sessions were also held on Saturday from 09:00 to 09:45 and 10:15 to 11:00 local time.<sup id="cite_ref-f1rules_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1rules-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/List_of_motorsport_terminology#Q" class="mw-redirect" title="List of motorsport terminology">Qualifying session</a> was run as a one-lap session and took place on Friday and Saturday afternoon. The cars were run one at a time; the Friday running order was determined with the Championship leading heading out first. The Saturday running order was determined by times set in Friday afternoon qualifying with the fastest heading out last and the slowest running first. The lap times from the Friday afternoon session did not determine the grid order.<sup id="cite_ref-f1rules_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1rules-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Williams drivers Ralf Schumacher and <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a> set the pace in the Friday free practice, which took place in dry and sunny conditions, with a time of 1:21.335. Montoya was less than sixth hundredths of a second behind, ahead of <a href="/wiki/Jaguar_Racing" title="Jaguar Racing">Jaguar</a> driver Mark Webber, Coulthard, Alonso and <a href="/wiki/British_American_Racing" title="British American Racing">BAR</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a>. Michael Schumacher clipped the kerb at the Variante Bassa chicane,<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> damaging the Ferrari's left rear suspension.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the Saturday afternoon qualifying session, Schumacher clinched his second <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> of the season with a time of 1:22.327. He was joined on the front row by his brother <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a>, who was 14 thousands of a seconds behind. <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> was third in the other Ferrari, with Montoya fourth. Webber took fifth, with Räikkönen taking sixth. <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Jos_Verstappen" title="Jos Verstappen">Jos Verstappen</a> crashed into the wall at the Variane Alta chicane becoming the first driver to not set a competitive lap time under the new qualifying format.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2003_San_Marino_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The race took place in the afternoon and started at 14:00 local time, in dry and clear weather.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcf1sanmarino_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcf1sanmarino-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> As usual, the race was broadcast worldwide, with the "<a href="/wiki/Formula_One#Television" title="Formula One">World Feed</a>" coverage being produced by host broadcaster <a href="/wiki/RAI" title="RAI">RAI</a>. Michael Schumacher, from pole position on the grid, lost the lead from Ralf Schumacher before the first corner. Webber, from fifth, made a poor start dropping down to eleventh. Räikkonen managed to gain one position for fifth with teammate Coulthard making the best start and gained four places to go into eighth position.<sup id="cite_ref-gpresults_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpresults-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Webber's teammate <a href="/wiki/Ant%C3%B4nio_Pizzonia" title="Antônio Pizzonia">Antônio Pizzonia</a> stalled on the grid.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcf1sanmarino_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcf1sanmarino-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Trackside marshals pushed Pizzonia's car to the exit of the pitlane where Jaguar mechanics managed to restart the car's engine enabling Pizzonia rejoin at the back of the field.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2003_San_Marino_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Ralf and Michael Schumacher raced despite the death of their mother Elisabeth just hours before the race with the pair flying on a private jet to <a href="/wiki/Cologne" title="Cologne">Cologne</a> to be at her side.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> They sported black armbands and no champagne was sprayed on the podium as a mark of respect.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Ralf also sported a black stripe on his racing helmet.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> The Schumacher brothers left the circuit immediately after the podium celebrations and Ferrari team principal <a href="/wiki/Jean_Todt" title="Jean Todt">Jean Todt</a> took Michael's place at the post-race <a href="/wiki/News_conference" class="mw-redirect" title="News conference">press conference</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Race stewards Tony Scott-Andrews, Roger Peart and Giuseppe Musiconi formally excused the Schumacher brothers from the pre and post-race formalities.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At the subsequent post-race press conference, Todt revealed that Schumacher made the decision to compete in the Grand Prix with Ferrari giving their full support to Schumacher.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Mercedes motorsport vice-president <a href="/wiki/Norbert_Haug" title="Norbert Haug">Norbert Haug</a> expressed sympathy for the Schumacher brothers while also praising them for their performance during the race.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As a consequence of the race, Räikkönen increased his lead in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">World Drivers' Championship</a>, over teammate <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> to 13 points with Schumacher climbing to third. In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">World Constructors Championship</a>, McLaren increased their lead to 16 points with Ferrari overtaking <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a> for second.
</p>
