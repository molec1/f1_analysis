<p>The <b>1980 British Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>XXXIII Marlboro British Grand Prix</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held at <a href="/wiki/Brands_Hatch" title="Brands Hatch">Brands Hatch</a> on 13 July 1980. It was the eighth round of the <a href="/wiki/1980_Formula_One_season" title="1980 Formula One season">1980 Formula One season</a>. The race was held over 76 laps of the 4.207-km (2.614-mile) circuit for a total race distance of 319.73&#160;km (198.67 miles).
</p><p>The race was won by Australian driver, <a href="/wiki/Alan_Jones_(racing_driver)" title="Alan Jones (racing driver)">Alan Jones</a> driving a <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW07B" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams FW07B">Williams FW07B</a>. The win was Jones' eighth Formula One Grand Prix victory and his fourth of the year. Including the non-championship <a href="/wiki/1980_Spanish_Grand_Prix" title="1980 Spanish Grand Prix">Spanish Grand Prix</a> it was Jones' third victory in a row as he built his charge towards becoming the 1980 World Drivers' Champion. Jones won by eleven seconds over the man becoming his arch-rival, Brazilian driver <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a> driving a <a href="/wiki/Brabham_BT49" title="Brabham BT49">Brabham BT49</a>. Third, and the only other car to finish on the lead lap, was Jones' <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams Grand Prix Engineering</a> teammate, Argentinian driver <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Reutemann" title="Carlos Reutemann">Carlos Reutemann</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1980_British_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1980_British_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the two weeks between the <a href="/wiki/1980_French_Grand_Prix" title="1980 French Grand Prix">French</a> and British Grands Prix, <a href="/wiki/Brabham" title="Brabham">Brabham</a> decided to replace <a href="/wiki/Ricardo_Zunino" title="Ricardo Zunino">Ricardo Zunino</a> with Mexican driver <a href="/wiki/H%C3%A9ctor_Rebaque" title="Héctor Rebaque">Héctor Rebaque</a>, while the <a href="/wiki/Shadow_Racing_Cars" title="Shadow Racing Cars">Shadow</a> team closed down. There were still 27 cars on the entry list, as <a href="/wiki/RAM_Racing" title="RAM Racing">RAM Racing</a> entered year-old <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW07" title="Williams FW07">Williams FW07s</a> for <a href="/wiki/Rupert_Keegan" title="Rupert Keegan">Rupert Keegan</a> and South African female racer <a href="/wiki/Desir%C3%A9_Wilson" title="Desiré Wilson">Desiré Wilson</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-f1rejects_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1rejects-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1980_British_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>For the third consecutive race, a <a href="/wiki/Ligier_JS11" title="Ligier JS11">Ligier</a> driver took pole position, <a href="/wiki/Didier_Pironi" title="Didier Pironi">Didier Pironi</a> setting a time some 5.8 seconds faster than the pole time set by <a href="/wiki/Ronnie_Peterson" title="Ronnie Peterson">Ronnie Peterson</a> at the <a href="/wiki/1978_British_Grand_Prix" title="1978 British Grand Prix">previous Grand Prix at Brands Hatch</a> two years before. Pironi's teammate <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Laffite" title="Jacques Laffite">Jacques Laffite</a> was alongside on the front row, while the works <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW07" title="Williams FW07">Williams</a> filled the second row with championship leader <a href="/wiki/Alan_Jones_(racing_driver)" title="Alan Jones (racing driver)">Alan Jones</a> ahead of <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Reutemann" title="Carlos Reutemann">Carlos Reutemann</a>. On the third row were <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Brabham_BT49" title="Brabham BT49">Brabham</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Giacomelli" title="Bruno Giacomelli">Bruno Giacomelli</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Alfa_Romeo_179" title="Alfa Romeo 179">Alfa Romeo</a>, and on the fourth were <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> in the <a href="/wiki/McLaren_M29" title="McLaren M29">McLaren</a> and <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Depailler" title="Patrick Depailler">Patrick Depailler</a> in the second Alfa Romeo. <a href="/wiki/Mario_Andretti" title="Mario Andretti">Mario Andretti</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Lotus_81" title="Lotus 81">Lotus</a> and <a href="/wiki/Derek_Daly" title="Derek Daly">Derek Daly</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_010" title="Tyrrell 010">Tyrrell</a> completed the top ten.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Renault_RE20" title="Renault RE20">Renaults</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_312T" title="Ferrari 312T">Ferraris</a> had issues with their <a href="/wiki/Michelin" title="Michelin">Michelin</a> tyres,<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Jean-Pierre_Jabouille" title="Jean-Pierre Jabouille">Jean-Pierre Jabouille</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ren%C3%A9_Arnoux" title="René Arnoux">René Arnoux</a> could only manage 13th and 16th respectively for the French team, while <a href="/wiki/Gilles_Villeneuve" title="Gilles Villeneuve">Gilles Villeneuve</a> and reigning champion <a href="/wiki/Jody_Scheckter" title="Jody Scheckter">Jody Scheckter</a> struggled to 19th and 23rd respectively for the Italian outfit, and were both out-qualified by Keegan's private Williams. Wilson failed to qualify in the other private Williams.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1980_British_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the start, Pironi led with Laffite holding off Jones and Piquet passing Reutemann for fourth. Pironi held a comfortable lead until lap 19, when he suffered a deflating front tyre followed by a long pit stop to replace it. Laffite led until he too suffered a deflating tyre, causing him to spin off into catch fencing at Hawthorn Bend on lap 31. Thereafter, Jones retained a comfortable advantage over Piquet and Reutemann, while Pironi made a charge from the back of the field, reaching fifth before suffering another tyre failure on lap 64. It was later established that the Ligiers' problems were caused by their wheel rims cracking.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Jones eventually took the chequered flag 11 seconds ahead of Piquet, for his fourth victory of the season and his third in succession (including the <a href="/wiki/1980_Spanish_Grand_Prix" title="1980 Spanish Grand Prix">Spanish Grand Prix</a>, which was subsequently stripped of its World Championship status). Reutemann, the last man on the lead lap, finished 2.2 seconds behind Piquet, with the minor points going to Daly, his Tyrrell teammate <a href="/wiki/Jean-Pierre_Jarier" title="Jean-Pierre Jarier">Jean-Pierre Jarier</a>, and Prost.
</p><p>In the Drivers' Championship, Jones doubled his lead over Piquet to six points, while in the Constructors' Championship Williams moved 18 points clear of Ligier.
</p><p>This race turned out to be the last for Depailler, who lost his life three weeks later while testing at <a href="/wiki/Hockenheimring" title="Hockenheimring">Hockenheim</a> ahead of the <a href="/wiki/1980_German_Grand_Prix" title="1980 German Grand Prix">German Grand Prix</a>.
</p>
