<p>The <b>2011 Japanese Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>XXXVII Japanese Grand Prix</b><sup id="cite_ref-manipef1_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-manipef1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Auto_racing" title="Auto racing">motor race</a> that was held on 9 October 2011 at the <a href="/wiki/Suzuka_Circuit" class="mw-redirect" title="Suzuka Circuit">Suzuka Circuit</a> in <a href="/wiki/Suzuka,_Mie" title="Suzuka, Mie">Suzuka</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-WMSC031110_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WMSC031110-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> It was the fifteenth round of the <a href="/wiki/2011_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2011 Formula One season">2011 Formula One season</a> and the 37th time the <a href="/wiki/Japanese_Grand_Prix" title="Japanese Grand Prix">Japanese Grand Prix</a> had been held. The 53-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a>, after he started from second on the grid. <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> finished in second place for <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Scuderia Ferrari</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> completed the podium, with third, for <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Race_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Race-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Vettel had started the race in <a href="/wiki/Pole_position#Formula_One" title="Pole position">pole position</a> alongside Button, whom he had marginally outqualified. The two drivers were the only two within mathematical contention for the title. Button attempted to overtake Vettel at the start of the race, yet was pressured towards the grass by Vettel which resulted in him losing his second position to third-placed starter <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>. Vettel was passed by Button in the second pit-stop phase, and was then passed by Alonso in the third. Hamilton slipped back from second to fifth, predominantly in the pit-stops; debris from a collision between himself and <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> caused a safety car period in the race. The second Red Bull of <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> finished in fourth position.
</p><p>As a consequence of the race, Vettel secured the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">World Drivers' Championship</a> for the second year in succession,<sup id="cite_ref-Race_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Race-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> having only required one point prior to the weekend to be declared World Champion.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>  Button remained in second place on the standings after his victory, extending the gap over third-placed Alonso to eight points. In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">World Constructors' Championship</a>, Red Bull's championship lead over McLaren was cut to 130&#160;points, with Ferrari a further 96&#160;points behind in third position.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Sebastian_Vettel_2011_Sebastian_Vettel_in_Yokohama.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2f/Sebastian_Vettel_2011_Sebastian_Vettel_in_Yokohama.jpg/220px-Sebastian_Vettel_2011_Sebastian_Vettel_in_Yokohama.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="277" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2f/Sebastian_Vettel_2011_Sebastian_Vettel_in_Yokohama.jpg/330px-Sebastian_Vettel_2011_Sebastian_Vettel_in_Yokohama.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2f/Sebastian_Vettel_2011_Sebastian_Vettel_in_Yokohama.jpg/440px-Sebastian_Vettel_2011_Sebastian_Vettel_in_Yokohama.jpg 2x" data-file-width="800" data-file-height="1009" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Sebastian_Vettel_2011_Sebastian_Vettel_in_Yokohama.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> required just a single championship point to secure his second successive world title in <a href="/wiki/Suzuka_Circuit" class="mw-redirect" title="Suzuka Circuit">Japan</a>.</div></div></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> entered the race knowing that either a points finish or <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a>'s failure to win, would see him crowned <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">the sport's</a> youngest double World Champion. He led the championship on 309 points, 124 ahead of Button, with only 125 still available. <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> may have been out of the Championship hunt, but were both within 3 points of Button, on 184 and 182 respectively. <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> occupied fifth spot on 168 points. Red Bull led the Constructors' Championship with 491 points, ahead of <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> on 353, with <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> no longer in Championship contention after <a href="/wiki/2011_Singapore_Grand_Prix" title="2011 Singapore Grand Prix">Singapore</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Prior to the race weekend, it was announced that Button had signed a new multi-year deal with <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> after recent rumours surrounding his future.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Button wore a special helmet for the weekend which featured a design in the style of the Japanese flag; he was to auction the helmet off afterwards to raise money for those caught in unfortunate circumstances during the times of the tsunami earlier that year.
</p><p>Tyre supplier Pirelli brought its white-banded medium compound tyre as the harder "prime" tyre and the yellow-banded soft compound as the softer "option" compound, as opposed to the previous year where Bridgestone brought the silver-banded hard compound as the prime.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Just like the race in Singapore, a single DRS (Drag Reduction System) Zone was used in the race. The detection point was located at the exit of the high speed turn 15, better known as 130R, while the DRS activation point was 30m after the turn 18. Hence, the DRS was activated from the final turn down the start/finish straight up to turn 1.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/1980_Formula_One_season" title="1980 Formula One season">1980</a> World Champion, <a href="/wiki/Alan_Jones_(racing_driver)" title="Alan Jones (racing driver)">Alan Jones</a>, was the drivers' representative on the steward's panel for the weekend.<sup id="cite_ref-Race_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Race-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice">Practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Kamui_Kobayashi_supporters_2011_Japan.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d5/Kamui_Kobayashi_supporters_2011_Japan.jpg/220px-Kamui_Kobayashi_supporters_2011_Japan.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="114" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d5/Kamui_Kobayashi_supporters_2011_Japan.jpg/330px-Kamui_Kobayashi_supporters_2011_Japan.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d5/Kamui_Kobayashi_supporters_2011_Japan.jpg/440px-Kamui_Kobayashi_supporters_2011_Japan.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1600" data-file-height="826" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Kamui_Kobayashi_supporters_2011_Japan.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Kamui_Kobayashi" title="Kamui Kobayashi">Kamui Kobayashi</a> helped to raise awareness of the <a href="/wiki/2011_T%C5%8Dhoku_earthquake_and_tsunami" title="2011 Tōhoku earthquake and tsunami">Japanese earthquake and subsequent tsunami</a> which occurred earlier in the year.</div></div></div>
<p>Three practice sessions were held before the race on Sunday; the first two were held on Friday, and ran from 10:00 to 11:30, and 14:00 to 15:30 local time, respectively. The third free practice session was held on Saturday morning, before qualifying, from 10:30 until 11:30.
</p><p>The first free practice session was held under clear skies and bright sunlight kept <a href="/wiki/Suzuka_Circuit" class="mw-redirect" title="Suzuka Circuit">Suzuka</a> with a steady track temperature of 30&#160;°C (86&#160;°F) and air temperature of 22&#160;°C (72&#160;°F).<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Pastor_Maldonado" title="Pastor Maldonado">Pastor Maldonado</a> skidded off the track during the session, rejoined but later parked his car in an escape road, missing out on the rest of the session. At the end of the session, whilst trying to set the fastest time, <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> crashed his <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a> at Degner.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> He finished the session in third position behind the two McLarens of <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>; Button on top by a tenth of a second.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Vettel's teammate <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> eventually finished the session fifth, behind <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>. During the session, <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a> filled in at <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a> for <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Karun_Chandhok" title="Karun Chandhok">Karun Chandhok</a> filled in for <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> at <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus_(2010%E2%80%9311)" title="Team Lotus (2010–11)">Lotus</a>.
</p><p>The second free practice session was held in an air temperature of 23&#160;°C (73&#160;°F), and track temperature of 37&#160;°C (99&#160;°F).<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Button again topped the timesheets ahead of Alonso and Vettel.<sup id="cite_ref-FP2_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FP2-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Senna" title="Bruno Senna">Bruno Senna</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kamui_Kobayashi" title="Kamui Kobayashi">Kamui Kobayashi</a> both spun during the session, but both drivers rejoined. <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> was less fortunate, as he crashed at the first Degner curve, having run wide on entry. He was later joined on the sidelines by teammate Maldonado, as his car ground to a halt, also at Degner. After the session, Hamilton, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>, <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9bastien_Buemi" title="Sébastien Buemi">Sébastien Buemi</a>, Kovalainen and Senna were called before the stewards for ignoring yellow warning flags, but no penalties were given.<sup id="cite_ref-FP2_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FP2-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the Saturday practice session, Button was fastest again, by half a second ahead of Hamilton and almost nine tenths ahead of third-placed Vettel. Alonso, Webber and Massa were separated a tenth and a half in fourth, fifth and sixth places. Senna crashed on the exit of Spoon Curve during the session, causing a short red flag period.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Bruno_Senna_2011_Japan_FP1.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3a/Bruno_Senna_2011_Japan_FP1.jpg/220px-Bruno_Senna_2011_Japan_FP1.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3a/Bruno_Senna_2011_Japan_FP1.jpg/330px-Bruno_Senna_2011_Japan_FP1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3a/Bruno_Senna_2011_Japan_FP1.jpg/440px-Bruno_Senna_2011_Japan_FP1.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2560" data-file-height="1707" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Bruno_Senna_2011_Japan_FP1.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Bruno_Senna" title="Bruno Senna">Bruno Senna</a> qualified in ninth position.</div></div></div>
<p>Saturday afternoon's qualifying session was divided into three parts. In the first 20-minute period, cars finishing 18th or lower were eliminated. The second qualifying period lasted 15 minutes, at the end of which the fastest 10 cars went into the final 10-minute period to determine their starting positions for the race. This session was held in dry weather conditions and the ambient temperature was 30&#160;°C (86&#160;°F), while track temperature was higher than any of the practice sessions at 36&#160;°C (97&#160;°F).
</p><p>In the first qualifying session, <a href="/wiki/Sauber_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber F1">Sauber's</a> <a href="/wiki/Kamui_Kobayashi" title="Kamui Kobayashi">Kamui Kobayashi</a> topped the timesheets to the delight of his local fans. He along with many other drivers had decided to use the soft compound tyres to elevate themselves into the second session. <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a>, <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> and <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> along with <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> had used the medium compound tyres to get into the second part. Schumacher's <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> teammate, <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> failed to set a lap time because of a hydraulics failure, and would start the race from twenty-third. It was the first time in <a href="/wiki/2011_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2011 Formula One World Championship">2011</a> that Rosberg had failed to make the final session. He would be joined on the back row by <a href="/wiki/Hispania_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Hispania F1">HRT</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Vitantonio_Liuzzi" title="Vitantonio Liuzzi">Vitantonio Liuzzi</a> – who also experienced technical problems. Liuzzi's teammate, <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a>, would start twenty-second behind the two <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus_(2010%E2%80%9311)" title="Team Lotus (2010–11)">Lotuses</a> and then the two <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Racing" title="Virgin Racing">Virgins</a>, where <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> and <a href="/wiki/J%C3%A9r%C3%B4me_d%27Ambrosio" title="Jérôme d&#39;Ambrosio">Jérôme d'Ambrosio</a> outqualified their respective teammates.
</p><p>Every driver used the option tyres in the second session apart from Pérez, who was prevented from setting a flying lap with a problem similar to Rosberg's. The Toro Rossos occupied the row in front of him, <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9bastien_Buemi" title="Sébastien Buemi">Sébastien Buemi</a> ahead of <a href="/wiki/Jaime_Alguersuari" title="Jaime Alguersuari">Jaime Alguersuari</a> by two tenths of a second; while there was a very similar story at Williams, with <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> in front of <a href="/wiki/Pastor_Maldonado" title="Pastor Maldonado">Pastor Maldonado</a> on the seventh row. In the dying moments of the session, Kobayashi, <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Senna" title="Bruno Senna">Bruno Senna</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vitaly_Petrov" title="Vitaly Petrov">Vitaly Petrov</a> all set lap times quick enough to progress into the top ten. This meant that the Force Indias of <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a> and <a href="/wiki/Paul_di_Resta" title="Paul di Resta">Paul di Resta</a> would have to start eleventh and twelfth respectively. <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> set the fastest time of the session with <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> not far behind.
</p>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatleft" style="width:40%;&#32;;"><style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r887775652">.mw-parser-output .quotebox{background-color:#F9F9F9;border:1px solid #aaa;box-sizing:border-box;padding:10px;font-size:88%;max-width:100%}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft{margin:0.5em 1.4em 0.8em 0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright{margin:0.5em 0 0.8em 1.4em}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.centered{margin:0.5em auto 0.8em auto}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft p,.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright p{font-style:inherit}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-title{background-color:#F9F9F9;text-align:center;font-size:larger;font-weight:bold}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:before{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" “ ";vertical-align:-45%;line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:after{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" ” ";line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .left-aligned{text-align:left}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .right-aligned{text-align:right}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .center-aligned{text-align:center}.mw-parser-output .quotebox cite{display:block;font-style:normal}@media screen and (max-width:360px){.mw-parser-output .quotebox{min-width:100%;margin:0 0 0.8em!important;float:none!important}}</style>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">"He [Button] slowed down to get his gap and I was coming up to the last corner, trying to make sure that I had a gap between me and him. It wasn't that big and just as I was coming into the chicane I looked in my mirror and I saw Mark diving up the inside of me, and then I saw… I didn't even see Michael but as I gave Mark room, Michael nearly crashed me on the left, so it was… quite dangerous."</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style=""><a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>, commenting on the incident at the final chicane on his out-lap before he didn't manage to start his flying lap in time.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Jenson_Button_2011_Japan_FP1.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c3/Jenson_Button_2011_Japan_FP1.jpg/220px-Jenson_Button_2011_Japan_FP1.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="94" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c3/Jenson_Button_2011_Japan_FP1.jpg/330px-Jenson_Button_2011_Japan_FP1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c3/Jenson_Button_2011_Japan_FP1.jpg/440px-Jenson_Button_2011_Japan_FP1.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2560" data-file-height="1099" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Jenson_Button_2011_Japan_FP1.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> set the second fastest time in qualifying, marginally behind pole position holder, <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>At the start of the third session, Kobayashi appeared on the prime tyres, and after setting two sectors of a flying lap he returned to the pits. Hamilton set the initial pace, he was followed by Button, Vettel, Webber and then <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a>. Seven of the cars left the pits about two minutes before the end of the session; the latest cars to leave being Hamilton, Schumacher and then <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a>. Hamilton, possibly not realising he had little time in which to cross the start/finish line, was making a gap to Button in front and changing dials on his steering wheel.<sup id="cite_ref-ridiculousQ3_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ridiculousQ3-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> As he approached the final chicane, Webber shot up his inside and bolted to try to get across the line in time – which he did – Hamilton backed off, surprised by what had happened and also by Schumacher cutting straight across the chicane on the outside of him.<sup id="cite_ref-ridiculousQ3_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ridiculousQ3-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Both Hamilton and Schumacher crossed the line after the flag had fallen and missed the chance to set a flying lap. Once all the flying laps had been completed, Vettel took his twelfth pole of the season, putting him in a prime position to clinch the title the following day. The only other championship contender, Button, lined up second on the grid, 0.009 seconds off Vettel's time,<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> his first front row start since the <a href="/wiki/2011_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="2011 Monaco Grand Prix">Monaco Grand Prix</a>. Hamilton was third, while Massa out-qualified teammate <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> for only the third time in <a href="/wiki/2011_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2011 Formula One World Championship">2011</a>. Behind the two Ferraris was Webber in sixth place, who was disappointed after making an error in not opening his DRS between the hairpin and Spoon corner.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>  Kobayashi was seventh, his highest ever qualifying place, because he had started a flying lap, unlike Schumacher, who lined up eighth on the grid. The two Renaults had also made an attempted to save tyres, and did not leave the garage, starting ninth and tenth.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:2011_Japanese_GP_opening_lap.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/78/2011_Japanese_GP_opening_lap.jpg/220px-2011_Japanese_GP_opening_lap.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="76" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/78/2011_Japanese_GP_opening_lap.jpg/330px-2011_Japanese_GP_opening_lap.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/78/2011_Japanese_GP_opening_lap.jpg/440px-2011_Japanese_GP_opening_lap.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2560" data-file-height="887" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:2011_Japanese_GP_opening_lap.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> led both the <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4-26" title="McLaren MP4-26">McLarens</a> after the first corner on the opening lap.</div></div></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> made a better start off the line than <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> who also had a good start compared to some cars behind him. Button attempted to overtake down the inside, but Vettel covered aggressively and Button lifted off after being half on the grass which allowed <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> to take him down the outside of Turn 1.<sup id="cite_ref-fullreport_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fullreport-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-grandprix_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>  Button asked his race engineer soon afterwards "He's got to get a penalty for that, hasn't he?".<sup id="cite_ref-fullreport_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fullreport-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Martin_Brundle" title="Martin Brundle">Martin Brundle</a> thought the move was "harsh but not worth a penalty", while Vettel said that he had not seen Button because Button was behind him.<sup id="cite_ref-Race_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Race-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Meanwhile, home favourite <a href="/wiki/Kamui_Kobayashi" title="Kamui Kobayashi">Kamui Kobayashi</a> lost his seventh-place qualifying slot, slipping to twelfth when his anti-stall kicked in at the start.<sup id="cite_ref-fullreport_20-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fullreport-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Conversely, <a href="/wiki/Paul_di_Resta" title="Paul di Resta">Paul di Resta</a> made a good start to take eighth place.
</p><p>Lap 6 saw <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> take <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> teammate <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> into Turn 1, for fourth place; Massa made no attempt to defend the position.<sup id="cite_ref-fullreport_20-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fullreport-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton was overtaken by Button on lap 9, after developing what the team believed to be a right-rear puncture (judging by their telemetry) at the time, before later discovering it was just heavy degradation.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton attempted to compensate for the puncture by slowing through 130R before pitting and changing to the option tyres. The other front runners pitted within the next couple of laps, with Vettel remaining in the lead after the first pit-stop phase. Both <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bulls</a> pitted together for the second time each on lap 20, a 10.3 second gap only just ensured that Vettel left the pits as soon as Webber began to enter them. The good pace and low tyre-degradation of the <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> proved quite useful for Button, as he took the net lead on the next lap after making his pit-stop, leaving Vettel a second behind him.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Also on this lap, Hamilton and Massa made contact for the fourth time in <a href="/wiki/2011_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2011 Formula One World Championship">2011</a>, after Massa went down the outside of Hamilton on the approach to the chicane. Hamilton, seemingly unaware of Massa being there, went to take his normal line, breaking off a small endplate on the Ferrari's front wing. Hamilton blamed his wingmirrors for the contact, saying that they vibrated too much down the straights.<sup id="cite_ref-HamCrit_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HamCrit-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton dived into the pits immediately afterwards, but a bad pit stop lost him more time putting him behind Massa and Webber, who had managed to pass Massa.
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Jenson_Button_2011_Japan_Race.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/34/Jenson_Button_2011_Japan_Race.jpg/220px-Jenson_Button_2011_Japan_Race.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="146" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/34/Jenson_Button_2011_Japan_Race.jpg/330px-Jenson_Button_2011_Japan_Race.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/34/Jenson_Button_2011_Japan_Race.jpg/440px-Jenson_Button_2011_Japan_Race.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1600" data-file-height="1065" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Jenson_Button_2011_Japan_Race.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> took the lead on lap 23, passing <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> during the pitstops</div></div></div>
<p>The awkwardly placed piece of debris that had resulted from the Massa-Hamilton collision caused the safety car to emerge on lap 24. Button slowed the pack up on the exit of 130R before restarting. Vettel pitted on lap 34, to begin his final stint on the prime tyres. Within the next three laps Webber, Massa, Hamilton and race leader Button all made their stops, all retaining position. This was until lap 38, when Hamilton overtook Massa in the DRS zone for net fifth place. Heavy traffic had slowed Vettel down on his out-lap, and Alonso moved into net second place after his stop. This left Schumacher in the lead, already being on the prime tyres, he had a longer stint before switching to the options at the end. He spent three laps in the lead in total, the first time he had led a race since the <a href="/wiki/2006_Japanese_Grand_Prix" title="2006 Japanese Grand Prix">2006 Japanese Grand Prix</a>. When he pitted, he had got the undercut on Massa, putting Schumacher into sixth on options and Massa in seventh on primes. Schumacher's pace on the options meant that he initially challenged Hamilton for fifth but later backed off.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Sebastian_Vettel_won_2011_Formula_One_World_Drivers_Championship.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/91/Sebastian_Vettel_won_2011_Formula_One_World_Drivers_Championship.jpg/220px-Sebastian_Vettel_won_2011_Formula_One_World_Drivers_Championship.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/91/Sebastian_Vettel_won_2011_Formula_One_World_Drivers_Championship.jpg/330px-Sebastian_Vettel_won_2011_Formula_One_World_Drivers_Championship.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/91/Sebastian_Vettel_won_2011_Formula_One_World_Drivers_Championship.jpg/440px-Sebastian_Vettel_won_2011_Formula_One_World_Drivers_Championship.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1280" data-file-height="853" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Sebastian_Vettel_won_2011_Formula_One_World_Drivers_Championship.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a>'s third-place finish meant that he won the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">World Drivers' Championship</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>Vettel began to get closer to Alonso at this stage, diving down the outside of Turn 1 on a couple of occasions. He showed his frustration at having lost two places in the pits and at being unable to regain them when he wildly gesticulated after a <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Racing" title="Virgin Racing">Virgin</a> got in his way whilst chasing Alonso. Throughout the final eight laps, Vettel was reminded by the team that he did not need to win the race, to win the Championship in Japan<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> so he held position, as did teammate Webber who was in fourth. A large battle for the final points scoring positions began on lap 46, when <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a> went down the inside of 130R to pass Kobayashi for ninth. Teammate di Resta was falling back on worn out tyres though, with <a href="/wiki/Vitaly_Petrov" title="Vitaly Petrov">Vitaly Petrov</a> taking eleventh from him only a few corners later. Petrov would then pass both Sutil and Kobayashi to finish the race in ninth place. <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> would get ahead of both the <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force Indias</a> to claim the final points scoring position, overtaking Sutil around Dunlop on the penultimate lap. To worsen Kobayashi's race, he would eventually be taken by both Force Indias too, placing him a lowly thirteenth. Kobayashi's teammate, <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a>, made great use of a two stop strategy, by saving his tyres coming from seventeenth to eighth on race day. Force India just missed out with eleventh- and twelfth-place finishes. <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus_(2010%E2%80%9311)" title="Team Lotus (2010–11)">Lotus</a> had a relatively successful day, finishing with both cars on the lead lap for the first time since their debut at <a href="/wiki/2010_Bahrain_Grand_Prix" title="2010 Bahrain Grand Prix">Bahrain in 2010</a>. This completed the most cars on the lead lap of a race ever, in Formula One.
</p><p>Button took his third victory of the season, and fifth consecutive podium, just ahead of Alonso, who was only 1.1 seconds behind Button by the time he took the chequered flag. Vettel became <a href="/wiki/2011_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2011 Formula One World Championship">2011</a> Drivers' World Champion with four races remaining, by finishing third, taking his fourteenth podium from fifteen races. Vettel also became the youngest double World Champion, and one of only nine drivers in the <a href="/wiki/History_of_Formula_One" title="History of Formula One">sport's history</a> to successfully defend their title. Mark Webber's solid fourth place, only 8 seconds off the lead, ensured that <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a> had already amassed more points than in their <a href="/wiki/2010_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2010 Formula One season">previous year's</a> World title with 518 points. Hamilton finished fifth for the second race in a row, followed by Schumacher, Massa, Pérez, Petrov and Rosberg, who completed the ten points scoring positions.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatright" style="width:40%;&#32;;"><link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r887775652"/>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">"To win the championship here is fantastic. There are so many things you want to say at this moment but it's hard to remember all of them. I am just so thankful to everyone in the team. We have got so many people here at the track but also at Milton Keynes working day in day out. Not only Friday, Saturday, Sunday, but also Monday to Friday, every day, pushing hard to build those two cars and to fight for a lot of points and to fight for the championship. We found ourselves in a very, very strong position and it is great to achieve the goal we set ourselves going into this year already now. There are so many people it is hard to name them all to thank, but I think one person that really stands out this year is the person I spend most of my time with during the year. It's my trainer Tommi Parmakoski. Also, regards to his family. I think back in Finland they have a great son with a great heart. He was the one not allowing me at any stage this year to lose the grip, start to fly, or think about things that are not in our control".</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style=""><a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a>, speaking in the FIA press conference upon claiming the championship.<sup id="cite_ref-PostRaceConference_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PostRaceConference-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>The top three finishers appeared on the <a href="/wiki/Podium#Usage_in_motorsport" title="Podium">podium</a> and in the subsequent <a href="/wiki/Press_conference" title="Press conference">press conference</a>. Button was delighted to have won in <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>, a place which was particularly special to him, because his girlfriend <a href="/wiki/Jessica_Michibata" title="Jessica Michibata">Jessica Michibata</a> was born there, and because he had driven for the Japanese <a href="/wiki/Honda_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Honda F1">Honda</a> team during his career.<sup id="cite_ref-ButtonWinJapan_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ButtonWinJapan-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r960796168">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>"It feels like a home win, and it's a very emotional win, not just for myself, but the whole team, and especially my close-knit little team of (trainer) Mikey (Collier), the old man (father John) and (girlfriend) Jessica (Michibata)".<sup id="cite_ref-ButtonWinJapan_26-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ButtonWinJapan-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Sebastian_Vettel_2011_Japan.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/14/Sebastian_Vettel_2011_Japan.jpg/220px-Sebastian_Vettel_2011_Japan.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/14/Sebastian_Vettel_2011_Japan.jpg/330px-Sebastian_Vettel_2011_Japan.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/14/Sebastian_Vettel_2011_Japan.jpg/440px-Sebastian_Vettel_2011_Japan.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1920" data-file-height="1280" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Sebastian_Vettel_2011_Japan.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> was praised for his success in <a href="/wiki/2011_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2011 Formula One World Championship">2011</a> by many other <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">World Champions</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>Button was later quoted as saying: "This is one of the most perfect circuits in racing with one of the most special crowds. It means a lot to me", about his win. <a href="/wiki/Martin_Brundle" title="Martin Brundle">Martin Brundle</a> congratulated Button on his race win, and applauded his recent form and driving throughout the weekend.<sup id="cite_ref-BrundleReport_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BrundleReport-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> Brundle was not the only one to notice Button's driving, with <a href="/wiki/1996_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1996 Formula One World Championship">1996</a> World Champion, <a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a> saying: "Button is now in the sweet spot of his career – he has become like a fine wine".<sup id="cite_ref-ButtonReaction_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ButtonReaction-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r960796168"/><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>"It gives us, as a team, a lot of motivation. It is good, as Sebastian said, to see three different cars within, I think, about three seconds. It shows how competitive Formula One is at the moment. There are a lot of fans here. They are so supportive of the sport that we are in and I think we have all tried to do a little bit to help them, to plant a good memory in their mind as this has been a tough year for Japan. This is a special race and a very special crowd. I think we need to thank them for supporting us. But, lastly, it should be Seb that we talk about, as this guy has done a great job this year. However hard we have tried we haven't been able to touch him in a lot of races so congratulations".</p><div class="templatequotecite">—&#8201;<cite><a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> in the FIA <a href="/wiki/Press_conference" title="Press conference">press conference</a> for podium finishers after the race.<sup id="cite_ref-PostRaceConference_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PostRaceConference-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup></cite></div></blockquote>
<p>Button's teammate Hamilton however, did not get such a positive reception after his race. Martin Brundle criticised the amount of collisions that he was having.<sup id="cite_ref-ButtonReaction_28-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ButtonReaction-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Even Hamilton himself described his race as "shocking",<sup id="cite_ref-HamCrit_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HamCrit-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> before admitting that he was "gobsmacked" by what had happened.
</p><p>Many drivers congratulated Vettel on his second world title,<sup id="cite_ref-HamCrit_23-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HamCrit-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> including <a href="/wiki/Jackie_Stewart" title="Jackie Stewart">Jackie Stewart</a> who described Vettel as "the most mature 24-year-old racing driver" he had ever seen.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Niki_Lauda" title="Niki Lauda">Niki Lauda</a> was also highly complimentary of Vettel, going as far as to say that he was capable of breaking <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>'s record of seven world titles. <a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a> however, had the opposite view, describing Vettel's chances of beating the record as "very unlikely".<sup id="cite_ref-VetUnlikely_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VetUnlikely-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> He did, however, praise Vettel's abilities.<sup id="cite_ref-VetUnlikely_31-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VetUnlikely-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Although, Vettel and Button were not the only ones receiving praise, <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Newey" title="Adrian Newey">Adrian Newey</a>, Red Bull's chief technical officer, was congratulated by <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a> left <a href="/wiki/Suzuka_Circuit" class="mw-redirect" title="Suzuka Circuit">Suzuka</a> with a 130-point advantage over <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> in the Constructors' Championship, with a maximum of 172 points remaining at the last four races. Therefore, if Red Bull were to clinch the title at the <a href="/wiki/2011_Korean_Grand_Prix" title="2011 Korean Grand Prix">Korean Grand Prix</a>, they would have to either score more than McLaren, or lose out to them by no more than a single point.
</p>
