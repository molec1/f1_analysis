<p>The <b>2004 Italian Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 12 September 2004 at the <a href="/wiki/Autodromo_Nazionale_di_Monza" title="Autodromo Nazionale di Monza">Autodromo Nazionale di Monza</a>. Ferrari took a 1–2 in front of the delighted <a href="/wiki/Tifosi" title="Tifosi">Tifosi</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> ahead of teammate <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>. Both cars had to make their way through the field from the back, Barrichello having made an early pitstop after choosing the wrong tyres at the start and Schumacher having spun on the first lap. This was the last race for <a href="/wiki/Giorgio_Pantano" title="Giorgio Pantano">Giorgio Pantano</a>.
</p><p>The race was perhaps best known for speed records set during the race. In the first part of qualifying (which did not count towards grid positions), Juan Pablo Montoya lapped Monza in his <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW26" title="Williams FW26">Williams FW26</a> at an average speed of 262.242&#160;km/h (162.9&#160;mph), the fastest lap ever, at the time, in the history of Formula One which was subsequently surpassed by <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> at the <a href="/wiki/2018_Italian_Grand_Prix" title="2018 Italian Grand Prix">2018</a> edition. The next day in the race, Montoya's teammate <a href="/wiki/Ant%C3%B4nio_Pizzonia" title="Antônio Pizzonia">Antônio Pizzonia</a> reached a top speed of 369.9&#160;km/h (229.9&#160;mph), the fastest speed ever recorded in Formula One at the time (it was to be exceeded by Montoya in <a href="/wiki/2005_Italian_Grand_Prix" title="2005 Italian Grand Prix">2005</a>).
</p><p>In the <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a> pit garage, the car of <a href="/wiki/Gianmaria_Bruni" title="Gianmaria Bruni">Gianmaria Bruni</a> caught fire after fuel escaped from the hose onto the hot bodywork during a routine pit-stop, and it was put out without any serious injury. Bruni inhaled some of the extinguishant and was having trouble breathing and so the team decided to retire the car.<sup id="cite_ref-gpe_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpe-2">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>This race was <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Scuderia Ferrari</a>'s 700th start in a World Championship event as a team.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
