<p>The <b>1988 Australian Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at the <a href="/wiki/Adelaide_Street_Circuit" title="Adelaide Street Circuit">Adelaide Street Circuit</a> on 13 November 1988. It was the sixteenth and final race of the <a href="/wiki/1988_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1988 Formula One World Championship">1988 Formula One World Championship</a>, and the last race for which <a href="/wiki/Turbocharger" title="Turbocharger">turbocharged</a> engines would be eligible until the <a href="/wiki/2014_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="2014 Australian Grand Prix">2014 Australian Grand Prix</a>.
</p><p>The 82-lap race was won by Frenchman <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/4" title="McLaren MP4/4">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Honda_in_Formula_One" title="Honda in Formula One">Honda</a>. It was Prost's seventh victory of the season, and the McLaren team's fifteenth, a record for a constructor in a single season that would stand until 2014. Prost's Brazilian teammate and new World Champion <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> finished second, having started from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>, while Senna's compatriot and outgoing champion <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a> came third in a <a href="/wiki/Lotus_100T" title="Lotus 100T">Lotus</a>-Honda, achieving what would turn out to be the final podium finish for the original Team Lotus.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1988_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As had become normal in 1988, qualifying was the domain of the McLaren-Hondas. Senna and Prost easily led the time sheets on both Friday and Saturday, with Prost initially fastest on Friday with a 1:18.179 lap, 0.153 ahead of his teammate. The pair traded pole laps in the second qualifying session, with Senna doing his usual act of snatching pole on the last lap of qualifying with a 1:17.748 lap, only 0.132 in front of his teammate, but 1.7 seconds faster than the next fastest car, Nigel Mansell in the Williams-Judd. During qualifying Senna was hampered by a sprained left wrist having injured it during a game of beach soccer in <a href="/wiki/Bali" title="Bali">Bali</a> where he had taken a small holiday after winning the championship in <a href="/wiki/1988_Japanese_Grand_Prix" title="1988 Japanese Grand Prix">Japan</a>. His injury was such that there was speculation the teams test driver <a href="/wiki/Emanuele_Pirro" title="Emanuele Pirro">Emanuele Pirro</a> would drive in his place, but the new World Champion refused to be sidelined and took his place in Adelaide, though he admitted that driving the <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/4" title="McLaren MP4/4">MP4/4</a> on a tight, bumpy street circuit while nursing a sprained wrist was hard. Prost on the other hand had spent his time between <a href="/wiki/Suzuka_Circuit" class="mw-redirect" title="Suzuka Circuit">Suzuka</a> and Adelaide playing golf at a resort in the Australian state of <a href="/wiki/Queensland" title="Queensland">Queensland</a>. There was also speculation that Honda would run their <a href="/wiki/V10_engine" title="V10 engine">V10 engines</a> (intended for <a href="/wiki/1989_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1989 Formula One World Championship">1989</a>) in Adelaide instead of the <a href="/wiki/V6_engine" title="V6 engine">V6</a> turbo. However, team boss <a href="/wiki/Ron_Dennis" title="Ron Dennis">Ron Dennis</a> explained that racing the V10 was never part of the 1988 plan, and that with the RA168-E proving so dominant, Honda wanted to finish the turbo era on the highest possible note.
</p><p>Third on the grid on both days was <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> in his atmospheric <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW12" title="Williams FW12">Williams</a>-<a href="/wiki/Judd_(engine)#CV" title="Judd (engine)">Judd</a>, though he was 1.7 seconds slower than Senna. Mansell was ahead of <a href="/wiki/1987_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="1987 Australian Grand Prix">1987</a> pole winner <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> in his turbocharged <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_F1/87#F1/87/88C" title="Ferrari F1/87">Ferrari</a>. Fifth was Nelson Piquet, finally finding some balance in his Lotus on the only street circuit he liked, despite a couple of spins in qualifying. Mansell's teammate <a href="/wiki/Riccardo_Patrese" title="Riccardo Patrese">Riccardo Patrese</a> lined up sixth.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Gabriele_Tarquini" title="Gabriele Tarquini">Gabriele Tarquini</a> (<a href="/wiki/Enzo_Coloni_Racing_Car_Systems" class="mw-redirect" title="Enzo Coloni Racing Car Systems">Coloni</a>), <a href="/wiki/Julian_Bailey" title="Julian Bailey">Julian Bailey</a> (<a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_017" title="Tyrrell 017">Tyrrell</a>), <a href="/wiki/Pierre-Henri_Raphanel" title="Pierre-Henri Raphanel">Pierre-Henri Raphanel</a> (<a href="/wiki/Larrousse" title="Larrousse">Larrousse</a>-<a href="/wiki/Lola_Cars" title="Lola Cars">Lola</a> making his first appearance in F1 in place of <a href="/wiki/Yannick_Dalmas" title="Yannick Dalmas">Yannick Dalmas</a> who had contracted <a href="/wiki/Legionaire%27s_Disease" class="mw-redirect" title="Legionaire&#39;s Disease">Legionaire's Disease</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Bernd_Schneider_(racing_driver)" title="Bernd Schneider (racing driver)">Bernd Schneider</a> (<a href="/wiki/Zakspeed_881" title="Zakspeed 881">Zakspeed</a>) all failed to qualify. The <a href="/wiki/Osella_FA1L" title="Osella FA1L">Osella</a> of <a href="/wiki/Nicola_Larini" title="Nicola Larini">Nicola Larini</a> with its ancient "<a href="/wiki/Alfa_Romeo_in_Formula_One" title="Alfa Romeo in Formula One">Osella V8</a>" turbocharged engine (which started life as the Alfa Romeo 890T in <a href="/wiki/1983_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1983 Formula One World Championship">1983</a> and was actually the most powerful car in the 1988 field with approximately 700&#160;bhp (522&#160;kW; 710&#160;PS)) failed to pre-qualify.
</p><p>For the most part in 1988, qualifying and race times had generally been faster than those set in <a href="/wiki/1987_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1987 Formula One World Championship">1987</a>, showing the advances in development despite the leading turbo powered cars having approximately 300&#160;bhp (224&#160;kW; 304&#160;PS) less than they had the previous year. In Adelaide the drop in power meant a big difference to the top speeds on the 900-metre-long <a href="/wiki/Jack_Brabham" title="Jack Brabham">Brabham Straight</a> and times were slower as a result. In 1987 the faster cars were topping 320&#160;km/h (199&#160;mph) on the straight, while in 1988 those speeds were down to 300&#160;km/h (186&#160;mph) or less. Senna's 1988 pole time was 0.481 slower than Berger's time in 1987 while Berger himself, driving an updated version of his 1987 Ferrari, was 2.25 seconds slower than his 1987 pole time of 1:17.267.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1988_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Prost led off the start from Senna, Berger, Piquet, and Mansell in his last race for <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> before joining Ferrari in 1989. By lap 4, Prost's lead over Senna was 5.5 seconds, with Berger close behind the Brazilian. <a href="/wiki/Michele_Alboreto" title="Michele Alboreto">Michele Alboreto</a>'s last race for Ferrari ended in retirement shortly after the start, when he collided with <a href="/wiki/Alex_Caffi" title="Alex Caffi">Alex Caffi</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Dallara" title="Dallara">Dallara</a>.
</p><p>Berger passed Senna on lap 5 at the Racetrack Hairpin, a tight second-gear right hander at the end of the Brabham Straight, and began a determined drive, catching and passing Prost at the same place on lap 14. He had a three-second lead when he came up to lap <a href="/wiki/Stefano_Modena" title="Stefano Modena">Stefano Modena</a> in the <a href="/wiki/EuroBrun" title="EuroBrun">EuroBrun</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ren%C3%A9_Arnoux" title="René Arnoux">René Arnoux</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Equipe_Ligier" title="Equipe Ligier">Ligier</a> on lap 23. Arnoux appeared to ignore his mirrors and the blue flags instructing him to let Berger past, causing the Ferrari's left front wheel to ride the Ligier's rear right wheel and send both cars into a spin. Berger retired immediately with suspension damage, putting Prost back into the lead with Senna second.
</p><p>Behind the McLarens, Piquet was maintaining third ahead of the Williams pair of Patrese and Mansell. The Italian opened a small gap on his teammate and closed on Piquet, making several attempts to pass the Brazilian. However, he spun on lap 53, letting Mansell past. The Englishman was no more successful in trying to pass Piquet, and he retired on lap 66 when his brakes failed and he spun into the wall. By lap 59 Prost was putting in a succession of fast laps, extending his lead to over 30 seconds and lapping the whole field up to the fifth-placed <a href="/wiki/Rial_Racing" title="Rial Racing">Rial</a> of <a href="/wiki/Andrea_de_Cesaris" title="Andrea de Cesaris">Andrea de Cesaris</a>.
</p><p>On lap 46, <a href="/wiki/Pierluigi_Martini" title="Pierluigi Martini">Pierluigi Martini</a> spun his <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a> at the chicane and was almost collected by <a href="/wiki/Stefan_Johansson" title="Stefan Johansson">Stefan Johansson</a>'s Ligier. <a href="/wiki/Maur%C3%ADcio_Gugelmin" title="Maurício Gugelmin">Maurício Gugelmin</a> slowed his <a href="/wiki/March_Engineering" title="March Engineering">March</a> to avoid the pair, only to be hit from behind by <a href="/wiki/Satoru_Nakajima" title="Satoru Nakajima">Satoru Nakajima</a>'s Lotus, putting both cars out. This crash also had an effect on Prost who was the first car upon the scene. The McLaren ran over some debris which broke off part of the front end wing plate, causing the car to understeer for the rest of the race, though Prost was able to play with his car's onboard settings to compensate and would set the fastest race lap later in the race.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Alessandro_Nannini" title="Alessandro Nannini">Alessandro Nannini</a> spun his <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a>; <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Palmer" title="Jonathan Palmer">Jonathan Palmer</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_Racing" title="Tyrrell Racing">Tyrrell</a> suffered transmission problems; and <a href="/wiki/Arrows_Grand_Prix_International" title="Arrows Grand Prix International">Arrows</a> drivers <a href="/wiki/Derek_Warwick" title="Derek Warwick">Derek Warwick</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Cheever" title="Eddie Cheever">Eddie Cheever</a> both suffered engine failures. Driveshaft trouble accounted for the EuroBruns of Modena and <a href="/wiki/Oscar_Larrauri" title="Oscar Larrauri">Oscar Larrauri</a>, while Caffi succumbed to clutch failure in his Dallara.
</p><p>At the finish, only eleven cars were classified, of which just seven saw the chequered flag: de Cesaris, Johansson and <a href="/wiki/Philippe_Alliot" title="Philippe Alliot">Philippe Alliot</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Larrousse" title="Larrousse">Larrousse</a>-<a href="/wiki/Lola_Cars" title="Lola Cars">Lola</a> all ran out of fuel in the closing laps, while <a href="/wiki/Philippe_Streiff" title="Philippe Streiff">Philippe Streiff</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Automobiles_Gonfaronnaises_Sportives" title="Automobiles Gonfaronnaises Sportives">AGS</a> suffered an electrical failure. Prost led home Senna in yet another McLaren 1–2, with Piquet third and Patrese fourth. De Cesaris' misfortune handed fifth place to <a href="/wiki/Thierry_Boutsen" title="Thierry Boutsen">Thierry Boutsen</a> in the Benetton despite a spin and a stuttering engine, with <a href="/wiki/Ivan_Capelli" title="Ivan Capelli">Ivan Capelli</a> taking the final point for sixth in his March despite a puncture.
</p><p>In the last race in which cars with <a href="/wiki/Turbocharger" title="Turbocharger">turbocharged</a> engines were allowed to compete until <a href="/wiki/2014_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2014 Formula One World Championship">2014</a>, all three podium positions were taken by cars powered by Honda turbos.
</p>
