<p>The <b>2001 French Grand Prix</b> (officially the <b>Mobil 1 Grand Prix de France</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Nevers_Magny-Cours" title="Circuit de Nevers Magny-Cours">Circuit de Nevers Magny-Cours</a>, <a href="/wiki/Magny-Cours" title="Magny-Cours">Magny-Cours</a>, <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> on 1 July 2001 before a crowd of 120,717 people. It was the tenth race of the <a href="/wiki/2001_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2001 Formula One World Championship">2001 Formula One World Championship</a> and the 51st running of the <a href="/wiki/French_Grand_Prix" title="French Grand Prix">event</a> as part of the series. The 72-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> after starting from second position. <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> finished second for the <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> team with <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> third in the other Ferrari.
</p><p>Ralf Schumacher led the opening 23 laps before he made his first <a href="/wiki/Pit_stop" title="Pit stop">pit stop</a> and Michael Schumacher assumed the lead after the first round of pit stops. His nearest championship rival <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> of the <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> outfit served a ten-second stop-and-go penalty on lap 32 for an earlier transgression of speeding at the pit lane exit. Michael Schumacher pulled away from the slower car of Ralf Schumacher who baulked his teammate <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a>, until his second pit stop on the 44th lap. Montoya led laps 46 to 50 before he ceded the lead to Michael Schumacher, who maintained it to take his fifth victory at the French Grand Prix, his sixth victory of the season and the 50th of his career.
</p><p>The result enabled Michael Schumacher to increase his <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a> lead over Coulthard in second to 31 points. Ralf Schumacher's second-place finish moved him past Barrichello for third. Although he retired from the event, Montoya kept fifth. Ferrari further extended their <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a> advantage to 52 points over the McLaren team in second. Williams remained in third place with 43 points while <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a> broke its tie with <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a> to move into a clear fourth with seven rounds left in the season.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2001_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Circuit_de_Nevers_Magny-Cours-Northeast_side.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0c/Circuit_de_Nevers_Magny-Cours-Northeast_side.jpg/220px-Circuit_de_Nevers_Magny-Cours-Northeast_side.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0c/Circuit_de_Nevers_Magny-Cours-Northeast_side.jpg/330px-Circuit_de_Nevers_Magny-Cours-Northeast_side.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0c/Circuit_de_Nevers_Magny-Cours-Northeast_side.jpg/440px-Circuit_de_Nevers_Magny-Cours-Northeast_side.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2560" data-file-height="1920" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Circuit_de_Nevers_Magny-Cours-Northeast_side.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Nevers_Magny-Cours" title="Circuit de Nevers Magny-Cours">Circuit de Nevers Magny-Cours</a> <i>(pictured in 2009)</i>, where the race was held.</div></div></div>
<p>The 2001 <a href="/wiki/French_Grand_Prix" title="French Grand Prix">French Grand Prix</a> was the tenth of seventeen scheduled <a href="/wiki/Open-wheel_car" title="Open-wheel car">single seater</a> races of the <a href="/wiki/2001_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2001 Formula One World Championship">2001 Formula One World Championship</a> and the 51st running of the event as part of the series. It took place at the fifteen-turn 4.251&#160;km (2.641&#160;mi) <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Nevers_Magny-Cours" title="Circuit de Nevers Magny-Cours">Circuit de Nevers Magny-Cours</a> in the commune of <a href="/wiki/Magny-Cours" title="Magny-Cours">Magny-Cours</a>, <a href="/wiki/Burgundy" title="Burgundy">Burgundy</a> on 1 July 2001.<sup id="cite_ref-CF1FRA2001_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CF1FRA2001-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The layout of the circuit provided an mixture of corners taken at low and high speeds with four long and short <a href="/wiki/Straight_(racing)" title="Straight (racing)">straights</a>. It had a flat and smooth racing surface that had no single deformation around the track to disrupt the stability of the cars and the series' two <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_tyres" title="Formula One tyres">tyre</a> suppliers <a href="/wiki/Bridgestone" title="Bridgestone">Bridgestone</a> and <a href="/wiki/Michelin" title="Michelin">Michelin</a> brought soft compound tyres to maximise <a href="/wiki/Grip_(auto_racing)" title="Grip (auto racing)">mechanical grip</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Before the race, <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> led the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a> with 68 <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Championship_points_scoring_systems" title="List of Formula One World Championship points scoring systems">points</a> ahead of <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>'s <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> (44 points) in second. <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> (26 points) in the second Ferrari was third and <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> in fourth was one point behind in the battle for the position. The other Williams driver <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a> completed the top five in the standings with 12 points.<sup id="cite_ref-points_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a> Ferrari led with 94 points, with a 41-point gap over their nearest rivals McLaren in second. With 37 points, Williams were in third position and <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a> (15 points) and <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a> (13 points) contended for fourth place.<sup id="cite_ref-points_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In preparation for the Grand Prix, the Ferrari team tested its chassis, the <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_F2001" title="Ferrari F2001">F2001</a>, the only constructor to do so. The outfit conducted a six-hour and 45-minute shakedown session on 28 June at the <a href="/wiki/Fiorano_Circuit" title="Fiorano Circuit">Fiorano Circuit</a> with their test driver, <a href="/wiki/Luca_Badoer" title="Luca Badoer">Luca Badoer</a>, who drove 45&#160;km (28&#160;mi) to test the functionality of the car electrical system and also concentrated on <a href="/wiki/Launch_control_(automotive)" title="Launch control (automotive)">launch control system</a> practice starts.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> After an FIA World Motor Sport Council meeting in Paris on 27 June 2001, all of the eleven teams unanimously agreed that starting from this event, any driver who <a href="/wiki/Stall_(engine)" title="Stall (engine)">stalled</a> his car on the <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_motorsport_terms#G" title="Glossary of motorsport terms">grid</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Parade_lap" title="Parade lap">formation lap</a> would be barred from using the <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_motorsport_terms#S" title="Glossary of motorsport terms">spare car</a>. The rule was revised to state that any switch into the spare car could only be undertaken fifteen seconds before the commencement of the formation lap and it would avoid any driver incurring a <a href="/wiki/Pit_stop" title="Pit stop">pit lane</a> start.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Coulthard had won the <a href="/wiki/2000_French_Grand_Prix" title="2000 French Grand Prix">2000 French Grand Prix</a> and spoke of his anticipation that McLaren would be within four-tenths of a second of Ferrari depending on the amount of tyre degradation, "So, in qualifying, again, unless the tyre that we have available to us works well on our car there, I don't think we have something in the set-up that we can change. It's probably something a bit more fundamental."<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> He said that he did not want to be drawn into discussion over the Drivers' Championship and sought to extract his best performance over the rest of the season, " I concentrate on the future because you can't change the past. What we can influence is the future and we won't be affected by the situation that we are in."<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher stated his acknowledgement that Coulthard was his main rival for the Drivers' Championship,<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> and took a race-by-race approach, "The year is long and there are still eight races to go and I still have to calculate for David in particular. McLaren could still be very strong in Magny-Cours"<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Some teams made modifications to their <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_car" title="Formula One car">cars</a> for the race. The <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a> engine manufacturer débuted an revised specification of its <a href="/wiki/V10_engine" title="V10 engine">V10 power unit</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Benetton_B201" title="Benetton B201">Benetton B201s</a> of <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a>. This however did not increase the overall top speed of the cars and they remained slow throughout the race meeting.<sup id="cite_ref-Piola_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Piola-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> All of the primary teams did not bring any major aerodynamic improvements to the Magny-Cours track as the <a href="/wiki/Prost_Grand_Prix" title="Prost Grand Prix">Prost</a> outfit installed revised its <a href="/wiki/Suspension_(vehicle)" class="mw-redirect" title="Suspension (vehicle)">suspension</a> geometry and modified the bodywork of the <a href="/wiki/Prost_AP04" title="Prost AP04">AP04</a> chassis. The <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a> team mounted a new front wing on the <a href="/wiki/Sauber_C20" title="Sauber C20">C20</a> that featured an V-shaped wing to support it and the <a href="/wiki/British_American_Racing" title="British American Racing">British American Racing</a> (BAR) squad introduced a new rear suspension design that was developed by its partner <a href="/wiki/Honda_in_Formula_One" title="Honda in Formula One">Honda</a> on <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Panis" title="Olivier Panis">Olivier Panis</a>' <a href="/wiki/BAR_003" title="BAR 003">003</a> chassis.<sup id="cite_ref-Piola_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Piola-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice">Practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2001_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Four practice sessions were held before the Sunday race—two on Friday, and two on Saturday. The Friday morning and afternoon sessions each lasted an hour. The third and final practice sessions on Saturday morning ran for 45 minutes.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Weather conditions were hot and clear for the two practice sessions on Friday.<sup id="cite_ref-FIAFriday_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAFriday-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> In the first practice session, McLaren's <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> was fastest with a lap of 1 minute and 15.889 seconds, 0.155 seconds faster than Michael Schumacher in second. Coulthard and Barrichello were third and fourth, with the Williams of Ralf Schumacher and Montoya fifth and sixth. <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> of the Jordan team, Panis, <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a> and BAR driver <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a> rounded out the session's top ten drivers.<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportFP1_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportFP1-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Several drivers spun on the dusty track and into the <a href="/wiki/Run-off_area" title="Run-off area">gravel traps</a> during the session.<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportFP1_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportFP1-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Ralf Schumacher's running ended early due to a water leak that was possibly caused by a damaged pipe in the car's sidepod from hitting debris off <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a>'s Sauber.<sup id="cite_ref-FIAFriday_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAFriday-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Coulthard was quicker in the second practice session and recorded the day's fastest lap of 1 minute and 14.935 seconds. <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a> had increased pace in his <a href="/wiki/Jaguar_Racing" title="Jaguar Racing">Jaguar</a> and he went second and Villeneuve was third. Fourth was Häkkinen and the Williams pair of Ralf Schumacher and Montoya placed fifth and sixth.  Michael Schumacher, Jaguar's <a href="/wiki/Pedro_de_la_Rosa" title="Pedro de la Rosa">Pedro de la Rosa</a>, Trulli and Barrichello were in positions seven to ten.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1FP2_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1FP2-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Jos_Verstappen" title="Jos Verstappen">Jos Verstappen</a>'s engine failed in his <a href="/wiki/Arrows_A22" title="Arrows A22">Arrows A22</a> on the pit lane straight and he took over his teammate <a href="/wiki/Enrique_Bernoldi" title="Enrique Bernoldi">Enrique Bernoldi</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Arrows_Grand_Prix_International" title="Arrows Grand Prix International">Arrows</a> car for the final 20 minutes of the session.<sup id="cite_ref-FIAFriday_14-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAFriday-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-AF1FP2_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1FP2-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> An unstable rear caused Panis to pirouette into the gravel trap at the Adelaide <a href="/wiki/Hairpin_turn" title="Hairpin turn">hairpin</a>. As Räikkönen exited the pit lane, the front wing on his car detached, causing him to <a href="/wiki/Understeer_and_oversteer" title="Understeer and oversteer">oversteer</a> through the gravel trap at Estoril corner and shattering the front wing.<sup id="cite_ref-FIAFriday_14-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAFriday-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It continued to be hot and clear for Saturday morning's two practice sessions.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher increased the performance of his car and was fastest in the third practice session with a lap of 1 minute and 13.729 seconds. He was 0.406 seconds clear of Coulthard in second and third was Ralf Schumacher. Barrichello was fourth-fastest, ahead of Montoya and Trulli in fifth and sixth places. Frentzen, Panis, Villeneuve and Räikkönen followed in the top ten.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1FP3_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1FP3-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> No incidents were reported during the session although Häkkinen did not record a timed lap.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1FP3_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1FP3-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Although he did not improve his lap time Michael Schumacher remained quickest in the fourth practice session, with Ralf Schumacher in second. The two McLarens were third and fourth – Coulthard ahead of Häkkinen – with Trulli improving to fifth and Barrichello fell to sixth. Montoya was seventh-fastest, Sauber's <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> placed eighth, Irvine came ninth and Räikkönen duplicated his third practice session result in tenth ahead of qualifying.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2001_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Ralf_Schumacher_2002.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d5/Ralf_Schumacher_2002.jpg/170px-Ralf_Schumacher_2002.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="248" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/d/d5/Ralf_Schumacher_2002.jpg 1.5x" data-file-width="206" data-file-height="300" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Ralf_Schumacher_2002.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> <i>(pictured in 2002)</i>, who took the first <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> of his career in his <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a>-<a href="/wiki/BMW_in_Formula_One" title="BMW in Formula One">BMW</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>Saturday's afternoon one hour qualifying session saw each driver was limited to twelve laps, with the <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_motorsport_terms#G" title="Glossary of motorsport terms">grid</a> order decided by their fastest laps. During this session, the <a href="/wiki/107%25_rule" title="107% rule">107% rule</a> was in effect, which necessitated each driver to set a time within 107 per cent of the quickest lap to qualify for the race.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Weather conditions for qualifying were hot and sunny.<sup id="cite_ref-FIAQuali_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAQuali-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Ralf Schumacher twice surpassed the qualifying track record held by the <a href="/wiki/1992_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1992 Formula One World Championship">1992 World Champion</a> <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> to claim the first <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> of his career with a 1-minute and 12.989 seconds lap on his second timed lap.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcquali_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcquali-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> He was joined on the <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_motorsport_terms#G" title="Glossary of motorsport terms">grid</a>'s front row by Michael Schumacher who was ten-hundredths of a second slower. He was distracted by the Ferrari technical director <a href="/wiki/Ross_Brawn" title="Ross Brawn">Ross Brawn</a> over the radio and was later baulked by Trulli on his third timed lap.<sup id="cite_ref-satquotes_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-satquotes-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-SVQuali_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SVQuali-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard qualified third on his fourth timed run with three minutes left and spoke of his feeling he could have improved further as he ran over gravel scattered by <a href="/wiki/Luciano_Burti" title="Luciano Burti">Luciano Burti</a>'s Prost at turn 13 causing him to make a minor mistake.<sup id="cite_ref-FIAQuali_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAQuali-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-AutosportQuali_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportQuali-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> His teammate Häkkinen had minor engine vibrations and an error on his final timed lap put him fourth. Trulli and Frentzen took fifth and seventh for the Jordan team. Trulli was pleased with the balance of his car and later apologised to Michael Schumacher for slowing the Ferrari driver while Frentzen was baulked by Panis. They were separated by Montoya's slower Williams who was pleased with how his car felt but was annoyed with Alesi who blocked Montoya during his last timed lap towards its conclusion.<sup id="cite_ref-FIAQuali_21-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAQuali-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-satquotes_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-satquotes-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>A handling balance problem that made his car anxious under braking and launched over the kerbs at the turn 13/14 <a href="/wiki/Chicane" title="Chicane">chicane</a> restricted Barrichello to eighth.<sup id="cite_ref-satquotes_23-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-satquotes-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-AutosportQuali_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportQuali-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Heidfeld made minor adjustments to improve the vehicle's balance and took ninth. Villeneuve used the first half an hour to adjust his front and rear flaps and took tenth, although he lost time through turn one. Panis took 11th after heavy traffic slowed his final timed lap. Irvine, 12th, spun twice during the session and Räikkönen in 13th expressed disappointment of the balance of his car.<sup id="cite_ref-FIAQuali_21-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAQuali-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> 14th-placed De La Rosa was kept from the circuit due to an electrical fault at his part of the garage and engine overheating and driver errors affected his on-track running. Burti's car was altered at midday and pirouetted his car on his third timed lap as he qualified 15th. Fisichella and Button of the Benetton outfit took 16th and 17th: Button was prevented from setting a faster lap because he changed the setting of his <a href="/wiki/Differential_(mechanical_device)" title="Differential (mechanical device)">differential</a> exiting Estroil corner.<sup id="cite_ref-satquotes_23-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-satquotes-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-SVQuali_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SVQuali-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Verstappen in 18th could not adapt the Arrows A22 to the track and he was ahead of 19th-placed Alesi spun at high-speed leaving the Imola chicane. Bernoldi was unable to extract additional speed from his car and took 20th.<sup id="cite_ref-FIAQuali_21-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAQuali-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-AutosportQuali_25-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportQuali-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> The two <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardis</a> qualified at the rear of the field in 21st and 22nd places: <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> was slowed on his final timed lap by Bernoldi, and his teammate <a href="/wiki/Tarso_Marques" title="Tarso Marques">Tarso Marques</a> had a large amount of understeer that affected the handling of his chassis, which could not be rectified.<sup id="cite_ref-satquotes_23-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-satquotes-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-SVQuali_24-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SVQuali-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-qualifying">Post-qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2001_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Post-qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the pre-race drivers meeting on Saturday evening, the drivers deliberated the best method to avoid potential crashes at the exit of the pit lane, which was changed at the request of all drivers for the 2001 race, from the exit of the Estroil turn to its entry.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Warm-up">Warm-up</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2001_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Warm-up">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The drivers took to the track at 09:30 <a href="/wiki/Central_European_Summer_Time" title="Central European Summer Time">Central European Summer Time</a> (<a href="/wiki/UTC%2B02:00" title="UTC+02:00">UTC+2</a>) for a 30-minute warm-up session in warm and dry weather conditions.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_13-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FIAWarmUp_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAWarmUp-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> All drivers fine-tuned their race set-ups against the weather conditions of the time, undertook laps in their spare cars and Barrichello changed the setting of his rear wing.<sup id="cite_ref-FIAWarmUp_28-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAWarmUp-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Although he spun towards the conclusion of the session,<sup id="cite_ref-FIAWarmUp_28-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAWarmUp-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Häkkinen drove better than in qualifying and he was fastest with a lap of 1 minute and 15.428 seconds, which was one-thousands of a second faster than Michael Schumacher in second.<sup id="cite_ref-FIAWarmUp_28-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAWarmUp-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello's car was corrected from having an unbalanced car with an change of electronic settings after a technical meeting and he placed third and Coulthard was fourth.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Fifth was Trulli, Räikkönen placed sixth and Panis came seventh. De La Rosa, Heidfeld and Irvine were in positions eight to ten going into the race.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_29-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2001_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The race took place in the afternoon from 14:00 local time.<sup id="cite_ref-CF1FRA2001_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CF1FRA2001-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Weather conditions at the start were hot and clear.<sup id="cite_ref-ITVF1Race_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ITVF1Race-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> The air temperature ranged between 25 and 29&#160;°C (77 and 84&#160;°F) and the track temperature was from 40 to 51&#160;°C (104 to 124&#160;°F).<sup id="cite_ref-frenchgp_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-frenchgp-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> The hot weather increased the possibility of mechanical attrition.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Approximately 120,717 people attended the event.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> A two-stop strategy was the standard for the French Grand Prix and every pit stop took less time because of pit lane layout.<sup id="cite_ref-CooperAnalysis_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CooperAnalysis-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Häkkinen could not get off the grid at the start of the <a href="/wiki/Parade_lap" title="Parade lap">formation lap</a> because an assembled component in his car's gearbox was incorrectly fitted. His car was pushed back into the <a href="/wiki/Pit_lane" class="mw-redirect" title="Pit lane">pit lane</a> where the mechanics were unable to rectify the problem and Häkkinen did not take the start. De la Rosa's throttle failed during the formation lap and drove back to the pit lane and started one lap behind the rest of the field.<sup id="cite_ref-GPFRA01_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPFRA01-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> When the five red flags extinguished to signal the start of the race,<sup id="cite_ref-F1NetRace_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1NetRace-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> Ralf Schumacher made a brisk start to maintain his pole position advantage going into the first corner and retained despite an error at the Adelaide hairpin.<sup id="cite_ref-F1LiveFRARace_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1LiveFRARace-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher in second was slower off the line due to a minor glitch between the launch control system and <a href="/wiki/Clutch" title="Clutch">clutch</a> that was changed after the warm-up session though he held off Coultard on the first lap.<sup id="cite_ref-CrashRace_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CrashRace-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:2_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Barrichello had an fast gateway, rising from eighth to fifth by the end of the first lap. Räikkönen moved up four positions over the same distance.<sup id="cite_ref-FIARace_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIARace-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> Montoya passed Trulli and the manoeuvre caused the latter to lose momentum and allow Barrichello to overtake him.<sup id="cite_ref-CrashRace_40-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CrashRace-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> At the end of the first lap, Ralf Schumacher led by 0.3 seconds from Michael Schumacher, who was in turn was followed by Coulthard, Montoya, Barrichello, Trulli, Frentzen, Villeneuve, Räikkönen, Heidfeld, Irvine, Panis, Burti, Verstappen, Bernoldi, Fisichella, Button, Alesi, Marques and Alonso.<sup id="cite_ref-lapchart_43-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapchart-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> Ralf Schumacher extended his advantage to three-tenths of a second from Michael Schumacher with Coulthard close behind. Further down the field Panis passed Irvine to take over eleventh position, while Fisichella was overtaken by teammate Button for 16th.<sup id="cite_ref-FIARace_42-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIARace-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> Villeneuve in eighth became the Grand Prix's first retirement when his engine lost power and he went into the gravel trap at Estroil corner on lap six.<sup id="cite_ref-GPFRA01_37-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPFRA01-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-F1COMRace_44-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1COMRace-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> At the front of the field, Ralf Schumacher, driving with an unbalanced car that caused him to lock his tyres into the Adelaide hairpin,<sup id="cite_ref-CooperAnalysis_36-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CooperAnalysis-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> increased his lead over Michael Schumacher to 1.8 seconds by the 17th lap, who in turn, was 1.7 seconds in front of Coulthard in third. Montoya was a further 1.7 seconds adrift in fourth and he maintained a consistent gap ahead of Barrichello in fifth.<sup id="cite_ref-lapchart_43-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapchart-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>That lap, Bernoldi retired with an engine failure.<sup id="cite_ref-GPFRA01_37-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPFRA01-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Irvine was challenging Panis for ninth though he was not able to pass and he fell back slightly before the first round of pit stops.<sup id="cite_ref-F1COMRace_44-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1COMRace-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> In the meantime, the top five drivers were covered by a gap of ten seconds as all of them lapped in the 1 minute and 16 second range and began to lap slower drivers for the first time.<sup id="cite_ref-ITVF1Race_31-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ITVF1Race-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-BBCLapwatch_45-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCLapwatch-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello was the first lead driver to make a pit stop on the 21st lap. He rejoined the track in seventh place.<sup id="cite_ref-FIARace_42-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIARace-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> That lap, Irvine overtook Panis around the inside at Lycee corner for ninth.<sup id="cite_ref-F1NetRace_38-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1NetRace-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> Three laps later, Ralf Schumacher made his first pit stop from the lead. His crew had trouble fitting the car's right-rear tyre because Ralf Schumacher engaged first gear before the mechanic fitted it. He lost 2.8 seconds and returned to the circuit in fourth.<sup id="cite_ref-CooperAnalysis_36-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CooperAnalysis-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-GPFRA01_37-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPFRA01-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> MIchael Schumacher led the 26th lap and increased his pace before his own stop for fuel, which took 7.7 seconds.<sup id="cite_ref-CooperAnalysis_36-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CooperAnalysis-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> He emerged in third, ahead of Ralf Schumacher but behind Coulthard and Montoya.<sup id="cite_ref-CrashRace_40-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CrashRace-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> Montoya led the next three laps as Michael Schumacher began to draw closer to him.<sup id="cite_ref-ITVF1Race_31-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ITVF1Race-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> On lap 30, Montoya made his first pit stop and he rejoined the track in fifth place.<sup id="cite_ref-FIARace_42-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIARace-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>After the pit stops, Michael Schumacher led Ralf Schumacher by 7.2 seconds, who in turn, was 1.1 seconds ahead of the third-placed Coulthard. Barrichello was another two seconds behind in fourth and the rest of the top six was completed by Montoya and Trulli.<sup id="cite_ref-lapchart_43-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapchart-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> On lap 31,<sup id="cite_ref-BBCLapwatch_45-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCLapwatch-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> the stewards informed the McLaren team that Coulthard had incurred a ten-second stop-and-go penalty because he was observed going 5&#160;km/h (3.1&#160;mph) above the 80&#160;km/h (50&#160;mph) pit lane speed limit because he disengaged his pit lane speed limiter before crossing the white line denoting the pit lane exit.<sup id="cite_ref-CooperAnalysis_36-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CooperAnalysis-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> He took the penalty on the following lap.<sup id="cite_ref-ITVF1Race_31-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ITVF1Race-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard lost 26.8 seconds and he fell from third to fifth. On lap 35, Verstappen passed Alonso to move into 16th.<sup id="cite_ref-lapchart_43-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapchart-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello made his second pit stop one lap later in Ferrari's aim to get him in clean air and move past Ralf Schumacher.<sup id="cite_ref-F1NetRace_38-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1NetRace-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> He returned in fourth place.<sup id="cite_ref-lapchart_43-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapchart-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> As Michael Schumacher continued to pull away from Ralf Schumacher,<sup id="cite_ref-lapchart_43-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapchart-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> it became apparent that the latter was delaying his teammate Montoya. The Williams team attempted to <a href="/wiki/Team_orders" title="Team orders">order</a> Ralf Schumacher to cede second place to Montoya though he did not do so because of radio communication problems with his earpiece and Montoya lost time.<sup id="cite_ref-FIARace_42-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIARace-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At the end of lap 42, Irvine drew to the right of Frentzen and out-braked him at the Imola chicane as Frentzen cut the corner to retain eighth. Irvine gesticulated at the Jaguar gantry on the pit lane to signal that Frentzen performed an illegal manoeuvre. Frentzen then ceded the position to Irvine to avoid incurring a penalty.<sup id="cite_ref-ITVF1Race_31-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ITVF1Race-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-F1NetRace_38-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1NetRace-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> On lap 44, the technical director of the Williams team <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Head" title="Patrick Head">Patrick Head</a> went to his outfit's gantry on the pit wall to instruct Ralf Schumacher to enter the pit lane earlier than scheduled.<sup id="cite_ref-FIARace_42-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIARace-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher made his second pit stop for tyres on the next lap and rejoined the circuit in second place, three seconds behind Montoya, who assumed the lead.<sup id="cite_ref-F1NetRace_38-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1NetRace-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> Montoya increased his pace to hold a five-second advantage when he entered the pit lane for his second scheduled stop on the 50th lap.<sup id="cite_ref-CooperAnalysis_36-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CooperAnalysis-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-GPFRA01_37-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPFRA01-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> He emerged in fourth position, ahead of Ralf Schumacher as Michael Schumacher returned to the lead.<sup id="cite_ref-FIARace_42-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIARace-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> Montoya pulled off to the side of the track with smoke bellowing from his engine on lap 53, ending his race. Barrichello entered the pit lane for his third and final stop on the next lap. It took 7.7 seconds and he emerged in third position.<sup id="cite_ref-F1COMRace_44-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1COMRace-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> On lap 56, Irvine stopped on the grass at the right-hand side of the Adelaide hairpin with a pneumatic valve system failure.<sup id="cite_ref-GPFRA01_37-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPFRA01-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-F1LiveFRARace_39-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1LiveFRARace-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-AutosportRace_47-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportRace-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Coulthard drew close to Barrichello at the Adelaide hairpin on lap 58 after the latter ran wide. He dropped back from Barrichello through Estroil corner three laps later. Button pirouetted through 720 degrees into the gravel trap at the Adelaide hairpin on the 63rd lap though he continued.<sup id="cite_ref-F1NetRace_38-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1NetRace-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-F1LiveFRARace_39-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1LiveFRARace-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-F1COMRace_44-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1COMRace-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> Further down the field, de la Rosa overtook Alonso for 15th position,<sup id="cite_ref-lapchart_43-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapchart-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> and Alonso was called into the pit lane to retire with suspected engine problems located on the Minardi telemetry equipment.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_34-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> On lap 68, Coulthard to pass Barrichello on the left-hand side of the track on the run to the Adelaide hairpin while the two <a href="/wiki/Racing_flags#Blue_flag" title="Racing flags">lapped</a> Alesi's slower car though he could not complete the manoeuvre as Barrichello accelerated faster than him at the corner.<sup id="cite_ref-ITVF1Race_31-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ITVF1Race-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:2_41-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> On lap 71, an oversteer and a rear tyre lock sent Button into a spin through 180 degrees into a gravel trap and retired with a fuel pressure pick-up problem.<sup id="cite_ref-GPFRA01_37-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPFRA01-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-F1LiveFRARace_39-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1LiveFRARace-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> Upfront, Michael Schumacher was told over the radio to reduce his pace to avoid excessive strain on his car,<sup id="cite_ref-CrashRace_40-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CrashRace-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> and he maintained the lead to take his fifth victory at the French Grand Prix, his sixth of the season and the 50th of his career.<sup id="cite_ref-IDTRace_49-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IDTRace-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> Ralf Schumacher was 10.399 seconds behind in second and Barrichello completed the podium finishers in third. Off the podium, Coulthard finished fourth, Trulli fifth and Heidfeld completed the points-scoring positions in sixth.<sup id="cite_ref-GPFRA01_37-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPFRA01-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2001_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The top three drivers appeared on the podium to collect their trophies and spoke to the media in a later press conference.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_13-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher said he was delighted to take the 50th victory of his career though he explained his priority was to win the race and focus on securing the Drivers' Championship, "People seem to misunderstand my feeling about these statistics – I always said they were second priority but they do mean something to me."<sup id="cite_ref-:3_50-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> Ralf Schumacher expressed his happiness to finish second after his pit stop fumble, "If I would have come out in front of Michael I think it would have been difficult to hold him behind me. I am actually happy to sit here in second today because it was a disaster and really difficult to drive."<sup id="cite_ref-:3_50-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello expressed his surprise over finishing in third place, " Qualifying was horrible for me, I never really knew what was going on. The car was oversteering in some places, understeering in other places. This morning the car felt a lot better and I was going really well on my first stint, saving a lot of fuel at that time, and Ross came on the radio and said 'would you like to try a three stop' and I said 'I think so, it's a good idea, because then I can be quick on the track the whole time and by doing stops I might only finish in front of the Jordans, so let's try.'"<sup id="cite_ref-:3_50-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Coulthard learned about his ten-second stop-and-go penalty over the radio and admitted his surprise and disappointment over incurring it as he believed he had exited the pit lane, "The fact is the penalty for speeding cost me points. I was just a bit too keen with the button. It has cost me dearly", and, "I knew I had a penalty, at first I didn't know what for. Then the team explained it to me. I wasn't gutted because you never know what can happen during the rest of the race. I knew I had to keep pushing."<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> Häkkinen said he felt nothing about failing to start the race, "If I'd led the race and then retired, there would have been something to feel. But as it is, there is nothing. I know how to win races and am certain I will again in the future."<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup> According to Trulli, who finished in fifth, the Jordan team required additional work to increase the <a href="/wiki/Jordan_EJ11" title="Jordan EJ11">EJ11</a>'s pace, "I knew that today was going to be a good chance to score points and I kept pushing hard. The car felt perfect from the beginning and the lap times were good but it is difficult to keep close to the three top teams."<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The result enabled Michael Schumacher (on 78 points) to increase his Drivers' Championship lead over Coulthard in second to 31 points. Ralf Schumacher's second-place finish moved him past Barrichello for third. Montoya remained in fifth place despite he retirement.<sup id="cite_ref-points_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Ferrari further extended its advantage in the Constructors' Championship to 52 points over the McLaren team in second. Williams remained in third place with 43 points while Heidfeld's sixth-place finish meant Sauber broke its tie with Jordan to move into a clear fourth position with seven rounds left in the season.<sup id="cite_ref-points_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
