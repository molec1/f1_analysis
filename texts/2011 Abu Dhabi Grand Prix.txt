<p>The <b>2011 Abu Dhabi Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>Etihad Airways Abu Dhabi Grand Prix</b>) was the eighteenth and penultimate round of the <a href="/wiki/2011_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2011 Formula One season">2011 Formula One season</a>. It was held on 13 November 2011 at the <a href="/wiki/Yas_Marina_Circuit" title="Yas Marina Circuit">Yas Marina Circuit</a> on Yas Island, a man-made island on the outskirts of <a href="/wiki/Abu_Dhabi" title="Abu Dhabi">Abu Dhabi</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_Arab_Emirates" title="United Arab Emirates">United Arab Emirates</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-WMSC031110_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WMSC031110-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> It was the third running of the <a href="/wiki/Abu_Dhabi_Grand_Prix" title="Abu Dhabi Grand Prix">Abu Dhabi Grand Prix</a>. As is customary with the race, it was the only <a href="/wiki/Twilight" title="Twilight">twilight</a> Grand Prix of the <a href="/wiki/2011_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2011 Formula One season">2011 Formula One season</a>, with a start time of 17:00 <a href="/wiki/UTC%2B04:00" title="UTC+04:00">local time</a>.
</p><p>The race was won by <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>. Second was <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> coming third in the other <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a>, having won both of the previous iterations of the race for <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a>-<a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a>, had been in pole position, but retired after a puncture in the first lap, near the second corner, which was his first retirement since the <a href="/wiki/2010_Korean_Grand_Prix" title="2010 Korean Grand Prix">2010 Korean Grand Prix</a>, where he encountered a problem with his engine. As a result, this prevented him from equalling <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>'s record of 13 wins in one season, but Vettel did equal <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a>'s 1992 record of 14 pole positions in one season. This was also the only race of 2011 in which neither Red Bull finished on the podium, as Vettel's teammate <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> finished fourth.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Abu_Dhabi_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Abu_Dhabi_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Vitantonio_Liuzzi" title="Vitantonio Liuzzi">Vitantonio Liuzzi</a> returned to <a href="/wiki/HRT_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="HRT F1">Hispania</a> for the race, having been replaced by <a href="/wiki/Narain_Karthikeyan" title="Narain Karthikeyan">Narain Karthikeyan</a> for the Indian Grand Prix to make way for him to drive in front of his home crowd.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Williams_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams F1">Williams</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Pastor_Maldonado" title="Pastor Maldonado">Pastor Maldonado</a> took a ten-place grid penalty for exceeding his engine quota for the season.<sup id="cite_ref-Maldonado_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Maldonado-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Drivers are entitled to use up to eight engines over the course of the season without penalty, but Maldonado's eighth and final engine was damaged beyond repair when he retired from the <a href="/wiki/2011_Indian_Grand_Prix" title="2011 Indian Grand Prix">Indian Grand Prix</a>.
</p><p>In the week before the race, it was announced that the teams would trial several new compounds of tyre rubber in Friday practice, to offer tyre supplier <a href="/wiki/Pirelli" title="Pirelli">Pirelli</a> more feedback on their planned 2012 tyre compounds.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Tyre supplier Pirelli brought its white-banded medium compound tyre as the harder "prime" tyre and the yellow-banded soft compound as the softer "option" compound, as opposed to the previous year where Bridgestone brought the red-banded super-soft compound as the option.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/2011_Formula_Renault_3.5_Series_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2011 Formula Renault 3.5 Series season">Formula Renault 3.5 Series</a> champion <a href="/wiki/Robert_Wickens" title="Robert Wickens">Robert Wickens</a> and runner-up <a href="/wiki/Jean-%C3%89ric_Vergne" title="Jean-Éric Vergne">Jean-Éric Vergne</a> drove during free practice; Vergne drove for <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> while Wickens drove for <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Racing" title="Virgin Racing">Virgin</a> in place of <a href="/wiki/J%C3%A9r%C3%B4me_d%27Ambrosio" title="Jérôme d&#39;Ambrosio">Jérôme d'Ambrosio</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/2011_GP2_Series_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2011 GP2 Series season">GP2 Series</a> champion <a href="/wiki/Romain_Grosjean" title="Romain Grosjean">Romain Grosjean</a> also drove during free practice, for <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The circuit included two <a href="/wiki/Drag_Reduction_System" class="mw-redirect" title="Drag Reduction System">DRS</a> zones, each with their own independent detection points.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The first detection point was located just before the Turn 7 hairpin, with its corresponding activation point positioned halfway down the long back straight; the second detection point was set after the exit of Turn 9, with its activation point adjacent to the secondary pit lane at Turn 10.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The drivers' representative on the stewards panel for the Grand Prix weekend was <a href="/wiki/Derek_Warwick" title="Derek Warwick">Derek Warwick</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Free_practice">Free practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Abu_Dhabi_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Free practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The first practice session saw <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> set the fastest lap time, one tenth of a second ahead of <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>. Hamilton had set the early pace, using Pirelli's experimental development tyres, but Webber and <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> surpassed him, and Button set his fastest lap time as the session drew to a close. <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> drivers <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> and <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> experienced several off-track excursions between them. Romain Grosjean set a faster lap time than teammate <a href="/wiki/Vitaly_Petrov" title="Vitaly Petrov">Vitaly Petrov</a>, though Petrov was forced to abandon his car during his final run with engine troubles. <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> was also forced out with technical problems, failing to set a lap time.
</p><p>The second session was known for two separate incidents at Turn 1; Vettel spun and became stuck in the outside barrier early in the session, while Alonso crashed at the same place. Hamilton went on to set the fastest time, two-tenths faster than Button's fastest time in the first session.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Abu_Dhabi_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Qualifying began with dusk approaching in Abu Dhabi, with Vettel looking to equal Nigel Mansell's record of 14 poles in a season. In the first session Rubens Barrichello had an oil leak, therefore unable to set a lap time, but was allowed to race. He joined the Lotuses, HRTs and Virgins in failing to make it to the second part of qualifying. In the second qualifying session, which took place as the sun went down, there was a red flag mid way, due to a loose bollard, which Massa drove over but escaped without damage. When the bollard was removed the session resumed. Sauber, Renault, Toro Rosso, and the remaining Williams car of Pastor Maldonado were all knocked out. Pastor Maldonado had been given a 10 place grid penalty for a ninth engine, so started in 23rd, just in front of his teammate on the back row of the grid. Hamilton led most of the final qualifying session, only to be beaten to pole position by Vettel, on Vettel's last lap. The order then followed with Button in 3rd, Webber 4th, then Alonso, Massa, Rosberg, Schumacher, Sutil, and di Resta, having not set a time in order to save tyres for the race.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Abu_Dhabi_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Vettel led into the first corner, but an unexplained puncture on his left rear tyre caused him to spin off the track at Turn 2 and suffer his first retirement of the season. Hamilton led the race afterwards, and won the race mostly untroubled. Alonso finished second after he overtook Button on lap 1, Button eventually finished third. Webber dropped to behind Alonso at the start, but remained fourth after Vettel's retirement. He battled with Button throughout the race, but to no avail, eventually finishing fourth. He was followed by <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a>, the two <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a>, the two <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force Indias</a>, and the two <a href="/wiki/Sauber_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber F1">Saubers</a> - with Kobayashi taking the final point and Pérez missing out in eleventh. <a href="/wiki/J%C3%A9r%C3%B4me_d%27Ambrosio" title="Jérôme d&#39;Ambrosio">Jérôme d'Ambrosio</a>, <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9bastien_Buemi" title="Sébastien Buemi">Sébastien Buemi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> were the other retirements of the race.
</p>
