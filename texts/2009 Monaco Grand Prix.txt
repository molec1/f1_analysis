<p>The <b>2009 Monaco Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Auto_racing" title="Auto racing">motor race</a> that was held on 24 May 2009 at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Monaco" title="Circuit de Monaco">Circuit de Monaco</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Monaco" title="Monaco">Monaco</a>. The race, which was contested over 78 laps, was the sixth round of the <a href="/wiki/2009_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2009 Formula One season">2009 Formula One season</a>. It was won by <a href="/wiki/Brawn_GP" title="Brawn GP">Brawn GP</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a>, with his teammate <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> second, and <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> third.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>This year a <i>peace and sport</i> initiative was introduced on this Grand Prix under the High Patronage of <a href="/wiki/Albert_II,_Prince_of_Monaco" title="Albert II, Prince of Monaco">Albert II, Prince of Monaco</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9bastien_Bourdais" title="Sébastien Bourdais">Sébastien Bourdais</a> scored his last ever world championship points at this race.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2009_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2009_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The first five races of the championship ended with <a href="/wiki/Brawn_GP" title="Brawn GP">Brawn</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> leading the Drivers Championship by 14 points from teammate <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a>, having claimed 41 points out of a possible 45. Their Brawn GP team was leading the Constructors Championship with 68 points; second-placed <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a> had 38.5, with their driver <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> third place in the drivers championship. Reigning World Champion <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> of <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> was only in seventh place, having accumulated nine points.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Red Bull had a new two-tier diffuser ready for their <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_RB5" title="Red Bull RB5">RB5</a> car in Monaco, with chief designer <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Newey" title="Adrian Newey">Adrian Newey</a> believing it could be a real benefit to the team. The <a href="/wiki/Austria" title="Austria">Austrian</a> <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull" title="Red Bull">drinks company</a> team had won the <a href="/wiki/2009_Chinese_Grand_Prix" title="2009 Chinese Grand Prix">2009 Chinese Grand Prix</a> . They had finished third and fourth in the previous race at <a href="/wiki/2009_Spanish_Grand_Prix" title="2009 Spanish Grand Prix">Barcelona</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Force India had prepared several important upgrades for the <a href="/wiki/Force_India_VJM02" title="Force India VJM02">VJM-02</a>, focused upon giving high <a href="/wiki/Downforce" title="Downforce">downforce</a> by modifying the front and rear wings. This was to improve performance to score their first points, having not scored points before in a Formula One race. Most other teams, including <a href="/wiki/Toyota_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Toyota F1">Toyota</a> and <a href="/wiki/BMW_Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="BMW Sauber">BMW Sauber</a>, had modified cars to give good downforce and high mechanical grip; important for the Monaco circuit's low speed and twisting, slow corners.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the run up to the race, <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> issued a statement declaring that "Ferrari does not intend to enter its cars in the next Formula One World Championship." if "the introduction of two different sets of regulations based on arbitrary technical rules and economic parameters." were to take place.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> This was in reference to proposed regulations by the FIA <a href="/wiki/World_Motor_Sport_Council" class="mw-redirect" title="World Motor Sport Council">World Motor Sport Council</a> to introduce a Budget Cap for 2010, and allow technical freedom to those teams operating under it.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a> Formula One team made a similar statement, announcing "If the decisions announced by the World Council on the 29th of April 2009 are not revised, we have no choice but to withdraw from the FIA Formula One World Championship at the end of 2009."<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> These statements were the start of the <a href="/wiki/FIA%E2%80%93FOTA_dispute" title="FIA–FOTA dispute">FIA–FOTA dispute</a> over the 2010 regulations. During the event, scenes for the 2010 film <i><a href="/wiki/Iron_Man_2" title="Iron Man 2">Iron Man 2</a></i> were shot, which features an historic Monaco Grand Prix race.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Prior to the race the drivers complained about a billboard for <a href="/wiki/Martini_(vermouth)" title="Martini (vermouth)">Martini</a> featuring model Jessiqa Pace was distracting and in the line of sight of drivers as they exit Loews hairpin.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice_and_qualifying">Practice and qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2009_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Practice and qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Jenson_Button_2009_Monaco.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f8/Jenson_Button_2009_Monaco.jpg/220px-Jenson_Button_2009_Monaco.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="224" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f8/Jenson_Button_2009_Monaco.jpg/330px-Jenson_Button_2009_Monaco.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f8/Jenson_Button_2009_Monaco.jpg/440px-Jenson_Button_2009_Monaco.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2092" data-file-height="2133" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Jenson_Button_2009_Monaco.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> took <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> and won the race, extending his championship lead.</div></div></div>
<p>The early practice sessions at Monaco are traditionally run on the Thursday of the week so that the roads can be opened on the Friday for public access. As such, two practice sessions were held on the Thursday, with the third on Saturday morning, followed shortly by qualifying.
</p><p>The Thursday practice sessions began with Brawn GP establishing its dominance once again; Rubens Barrichello was fastest by three-tenths of a second from countryman <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> with a time of 1:17.199, and the two McLarens of Lewis Hamilton and Heikki Kovalainen following shortly thereafter. The Brawns continued their form in the second session, placing third and fourth behind <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> – who topped practice for the ninth time this season with a 1:15.243 – and Hamilton once again. A similar story held true for the Saturday session, with Jenson Button narrowly being beaten by <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a>.
</p><p>The first qualifying session was marked by <a href="/wiki/2008_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="2008 Monaco Grand Prix">2008 winner</a> Lewis Hamilton crashing at Mirabeau and damaging his suspension, putting him out of qualifying and gradually knocking him down the order until he would finish sixteenth, ahead of the <a href="/wiki/BMW_Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="BMW Sauber">BMW Saubers</a> of <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> and <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a> and the Toyotas of <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> and <a href="/wiki/Timo_Glock" title="Timo Glock">Timo Glock</a>. The second session saw the departure of the two Force Indias, both Toro Rossos and <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet,_Jr." class="mw-redirect" title="Nelson Piquet, Jr.">Nelson Piquet, Jr.</a>'s Renault while Finland's Kimi Räikkönen and Heikki Kovalainen topped the timing sheets. <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> had two times disallowed for cutting the corners at the Swimming Pool Complex and Nouvelle Chicane. The third and final session was dominated by Rubens Barrichello and Sebastian Vettel until a late lap from Jenson Button saw the championship leader take his fourth pole from six starts this season with Kimi Räikkönen the highest-placed KERS-equipped car in second place.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2009_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Kimi_Raikkonen_2009_Monaco.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d6/Kimi_Raikkonen_2009_Monaco.jpg/220px-Kimi_Raikkonen_2009_Monaco.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="213" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d6/Kimi_Raikkonen_2009_Monaco.jpg/330px-Kimi_Raikkonen_2009_Monaco.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d6/Kimi_Raikkonen_2009_Monaco.jpg/440px-Kimi_Raikkonen_2009_Monaco.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1426" data-file-height="1378" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Kimi_Raikkonen_2009_Monaco.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> took <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari's</a> first podium finish of <a href="/wiki/2009_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2009 Formula One World Championship">2009</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>At the start, Button maintained his lead as Barrichello passed Räikkönen on the approach to the first corner. Both Brawn cars and Sebastian Vettel started the race on the super-soft tyres;<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> the Ferraris and Williams started on the soft compound. This provided an advantage, as the super-soft tyres – which had proven to be the better of the two all weekend – started to lose grip exponentially after twelve laps as championship contender Sebastian Vettel proved when he started losing up to four seconds per lap on leader Button, because of the difficulty in overtaking at Monaco, this greatly helped the Brawn GP drivers and Kimi Räikkönen (the only three drivers ahead of Vettel when his tyres started to lose grip), because for several minutes all cars behind him found themselves unable to overtake, opening a huge gap between third and fourth, even for several laps after Vettel had been overtaken.
</p><p>Sébastien Buemi had an accident running into Nelson Piquet Jr. on lap 10 during a passing attempt at Sainte Devote,<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> while Vettel slid into the barriers under brakes at the same spot several laps later. Button maintained a fifteen-second lead over teammate Barrichello for most of the race, who had a smaller gap over the Ferraris of Kimi Räikkönen and Felipe Massa; Massa raised the ire of the stewards after crossing the chicane at the Swimming Pool Complex twice, though no penalty was awarded as both occasions were due to driver error.
</p><p>In the late stages of the race, Heikki Kovalainen crashed out at the Swimming Pool, spinning and colliding with the barriers. Kazuki Nakajima also crashed out on the last lap of the race at Mirabeau. Robert Kubica was the only other retirement, his race ending with brake problems. In the final phase of the race, the Ferraris pitted for the super-soft tyres, discovering the same graining problem as everyone else over longer stints. The distance between Button and Barrichello halved over the final few laps, though Button was deliberately slowing to avoid encountering backmarkers who were fighting for position. He won by seven-and-a-half seconds from Barrichello, with the Ferraris of Räikkönen and Massa third and fourth.
</p><p>After the end of the race, Button mistakenly parked his car in <a href="/wiki/Parc_ferm%C3%A9" title="Parc fermé">parc fermé</a> in the pit lane as is normal for other Grands Prix, rather than on the main straight with the other two podium finishers as is the norm for Monaco. As a result, he had to run down the start/finish straight to the podium.
</p><p>With his win, Button scored the  third-best  start to a season in Formula One history, with five victories and one  third place. Only <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a>  in <a href="/wiki/1992_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1992 Formula One World Championship">1992</a> and <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> in <a href="/wiki/1994_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1994 Formula One World Championship">1994</a> recorded better starts, with each  taking five victories and one second place in the first six rounds. Also, his victory in the <a href="/wiki/Brawn_GP" title="Brawn GP">Brawn</a>-<a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_HighPerformanceEngines" class="mw-redirect" title="Mercedes-Benz HighPerformanceEngines">Mercedes</a>  marked the first time in modern F1 history that a single engine has won  three races in a row<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> – engines in the  1950s lasted most of a season, sometimes more than one season. It was also the first time since <a href="/wiki/2006_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2006 Formula One World Championship">2006</a> that a driver had won three races in succession. Michael Schumacher was the last to do so, as he won the <a href="/wiki/2006_United_States_Grand_Prix" title="2006 United States Grand Prix">United States</a>, <a href="/wiki/2006_French_Grand_Prix" title="2006 French Grand Prix">French</a>  and <a href="/wiki/2006_German_Grand_Prix" title="2006 German Grand Prix">German</a> Grands Prix.
</p>
