<p>The <b>1993 Brazilian Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at <a href="/wiki/Aut%C3%B3dromo_Jos%C3%A9_Carlos_Pace" title="Autódromo José Carlos Pace">Interlagos</a> on 28 March 1993. It was the second race of the <a href="/wiki/1993_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1993 Formula One World Championship">1993 Formula One World Championship</a>.
</p><p>The 71-lap race was won by local hero <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/8" title="McLaren MP4/8">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Ford_Motor_Company" title="Ford Motor Company">Ford</a>, with English driver <a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a> second in a <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW15C" title="Williams FW15C">Williams</a>-<a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a> and German <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> third in a <a href="/wiki/Benetton_B193" title="Benetton B193">Benetton</a>-Ford.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Senna was the last Brazilian driver to win his home race until <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> in <a href="/wiki/2006_Brazilian_Grand_Prix" title="2006 Brazilian Grand Prix">2006</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1993_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1993_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Brazil was Senna's home race but he was able to do nothing to prevent the Williamses being 1–2 in qualifying with Prost on pole ahead of Hill. Senna was third ahead of Schumacher, Andretti and Patrese.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1993_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the start, Senna got ahead of Hill but Andretti hit Berger with both crashing hard in the tyre barrier in turn 1. Both of them along with Brundle and <a href="/wiki/Fabrizio_Barbazza" title="Fabrizio Barbazza">Fabrizio Barbazza</a>'s Minardi were out. The order was: Prost, Senna, Hill, a fast starting <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a>, Schumacher, and Lehto.
</p><p>Schumacher passed Alesi on lap 2, but his teammate Patrese only lasted until the fourth lap when his suspension failed. While Prost built up a substantial lead, Senna was under pressure from Hill who took second on lap 11. On lap 25, Senna was issued a stop-go penalty for lapping a backmarker under yellow flags and dropped behind Schumacher. There was a rainstorm, and many drivers pitted for <a href="/wiki/Rain_tyre" title="Rain tyre">wet tyres</a>, including Senna, Schumacher and Hill, while Prost stayed out on <a href="/wiki/Slick_tyres" class="mw-redirect" title="Slick tyres">slick tyres</a>. Several drivers spun, with both <a href="/wiki/Ukyo_Katayama" title="Ukyo Katayama">Ukyo Katayama</a> and <a href="/wiki/Aguri_Suzuki" title="Aguri Suzuki">Aguri Suzuki</a> crashing on the start–finish straight on lap 27, the Footwork partially blocking the circuit and bringing out the <a href="/wiki/Safety_car" title="Safety car">safety car</a> – the first time this had been seen in Formula 1. Fittipaldi spun at the first corner on lap 30 with his car stopped in the middle of the track. With the rain coming down harder, Prost lost control and, unable to avoid Fittipaldi's car, crashed into him, taking them both out of the race. As the wreckage was cleared, the new safety car controlled the field, Hill led Senna, Schumacher (who had lost time during his pit stop because his car fell off the jack), Alesi, <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Herbert" title="Johnny Herbert">Johnny Herbert</a>, and Lehto. They were followed by <a href="/wiki/Alessandro_Zanardi" class="mw-redirect" title="Alessandro Zanardi">Alessandro Zanardi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Philippe_Alliot" title="Philippe Alliot">Philippe Alliot</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mark_Blundell" title="Mark Blundell">Mark Blundell</a>, <a href="/wiki/Derek_Warwick" title="Derek Warwick">Derek Warwick</a>, rookie <a href="/wiki/Luca_Badoer" title="Luca Badoer">Luca Badoer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Karl_Wendlinger" title="Karl Wendlinger">Karl Wendlinger</a>, <a href="/wiki/Andrea_de_Cesaris" title="Andrea de Cesaris">Andrea de Cesaris</a>, <a href="/wiki/%C3%89rik_Comas" title="Érik Comas">Érik Comas</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Michele_Alboreto" title="Michele Alboreto">Michele Alboreto</a>.
</p><p>It began to dry and everyone stopped for dry tyres. Johnny Herbert had pitted for slicks right when the safety car pulled back into the pits, and he was to move up to third place due to this early change back to slicks.
</p><p>Just after the tire stops Senna passed Hill for the lead and pulled away. Behind, Schumacher and Alesi had stop-go penalties for passing under yellow flags, Schumacher dropping to fifth and Alesi going down to ninth. Schumacher passed Blundell and then Herbert to take third. Senna won from Hill, Schumacher, Herbert, Blundell and <a href="/wiki/Alessandro_Zanardi" class="mw-redirect" title="Alessandro Zanardi">Alessandro Zanardi</a>.
</p>
