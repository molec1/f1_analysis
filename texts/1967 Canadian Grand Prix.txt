<p>The <b>1967 Canadian Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Auto_racing" title="Auto racing">motor race</a> held at <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Tire_Motorsport_Park" title="Canadian Tire Motorsport Park">Mosport Park</a> in <a href="/wiki/Bowmanville" title="Bowmanville">Bowmanville</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ontario" title="Ontario">Ontario</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> on August 27, 1967. The 90-lap contest was race 8 of 11 in both the <a href="/wiki/1967_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1967 World Championship of Drivers">1967 World Championship of Drivers</a> and the <a href="/wiki/1967_International_Cup_for_Formula_One_Manufacturers" class="mw-redirect" title="1967 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers">1967 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers</a>. It was won by <a href="/wiki/Jack_Brabham" title="Jack Brabham">Jack Brabham</a> driving for his own <a href="/wiki/Brabham" title="Brabham">Brabham</a> team. This was the first <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Grand_Prix" title="Canadian Grand Prix">Canadian Grand Prix</a> to have World Championship status.
</p><p>Having already changed his battery on the grid, losing six laps, local driver <a href="/wiki/Al_Pease" title="Al Pease">Al Pease</a> spun his private <a href="/wiki/All_American_Racers" title="All American Racers">Eagle</a>-<a href="/wiki/Coventry_Climax" title="Coventry Climax">Climax</a> and stalled out on the circuit during the race, and suffered another flat battery. He ran back to the pits for a new one, ran back to the car, fitted the battery himself and continued. He was still running at the finish, albeit 43 laps down on Brabham.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1967_Canadian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Jo_Siffert" title="Jo Siffert">Jo Siffert</a> qualified 13th, but was unable to start his car on the grid and withdrew from the race as a result.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Eppie_Wietzes" title="Eppie Wietzes">Eppie Wietzes</a> was disqualified after attempting to receive outside assistance whilst suffering an ignition failure. He was driving a factory <a href="/wiki/Lotus_49" title="Lotus 49">Lotus 49</a>, but qualified 17th (16th due to Siffert's withdrawal) and was 8.4 seconds off <a href="/wiki/Jim_Clark" title="Jim Clark">Jim Clark</a>'s pace.</li>
<li>Last podium finish for <a href="/wiki/Dan_Gurney" title="Dan Gurney">Dan Gurney</a> and his team, <a href="/wiki/All_American_Racers" title="All American Racers">Eagle</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Last victory for the Repco engine and for a tube-frame Brabham.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup></li></ul>
