<p>The <b>1952 Italian Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_Two" title="Formula Two">Formula Two</a> race held on 7 September 1952 at <a href="/wiki/Autodromo_Nazionale_di_Monza" title="Autodromo Nazionale di Monza">Monza</a>. It was the eighth and final round of the <a href="/wiki/1952_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1952 World Championship of Drivers">1952 World Championship of Drivers</a>, in which each Grand Prix was run to Formula Two rules rather than the Formula One regulations normally used. The 80-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Alberto_Ascari" title="Alberto Ascari">Alberto Ascari</a> after he started from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>. <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Froil%C3%A1n_Gonz%C3%A1lez" title="José Froilán González">José Froilán González</a> finished second for the <a href="/wiki/Maserati_in_motorsport" title="Maserati in motorsport">Maserati</a> team and Ascari's teammate <a href="/wiki/Luigi_Villoresi" title="Luigi Villoresi">Luigi Villoresi</a> came in third.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_report">Race report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1952_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Race report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Due to the dominance of the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari team</a> throughout <a href="/wiki/1952_Formula_One_season" title="1952 Formula One season">1952</a>, the World Drivers' Championship had already been clinched a month prior to the season-ending Italian Grand Prix. Nevertheless, Ferrari entered five drivers for their home race, with their <a href="/wiki/1952_Dutch_Grand_Prix" title="1952 Dutch Grand Prix">Dutch Grand Prix</a> trio—World Champion <a href="/wiki/Alberto_Ascari" title="Alberto Ascari">Alberto Ascari</a>, <a href="/wiki/Giuseppe_Farina" title="Giuseppe Farina">Nino Farina</a> and <a href="/wiki/Luigi_Villoresi" title="Luigi Villoresi">Luigi Villoresi</a>—being joined by <a href="/wiki/Piero_Taruffi" title="Piero Taruffi">Piero Taruffi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Andr%C3%A9_Simon_(racing_driver)" title="André Simon (racing driver)">André Simon</a>, both of whom had competed for the Scuderia at various points of the season. There were also a number of privateer Ferraris, including the Ecurie Espadon pairing of <a href="/wiki/Rudi_Fischer" title="Rudi Fischer">Fischer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hans_Stuck" title="Hans Stuck">Stuck</a>, as well as <a href="/wiki/Charles_de_Tornaco" title="Charles de Tornaco">Charles de Tornaco</a> of <a href="/wiki/Ecurie_Francorchamps" title="Ecurie Francorchamps">Ecurie Francorchamps</a>, <a href="/wiki/Louis_Rosier" title="Louis Rosier">Louis Rosier</a> and <a href="/wiki/Peter_Whitehead_(racing_driver)" title="Peter Whitehead (racing driver)">Peter Whitehead</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Maserati_in_motorsport" title="Maserati in motorsport">works Maserati team</a> appeared for the first and only time in the 1952 World Championship, running three cars for <a href="/wiki/Felice_Bonetto" title="Felice Bonetto">Felice Bonetto</a>, <a href="/wiki/Franco_Rol" title="Franco Rol">Franco Rol</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Froil%C3%A1n_Gonz%C3%A1lez" title="José Froilán González">José Froilán González</a>. Also running <a href="/wiki/Maserati_A6GCM" title="Maserati A6GCM">A6GCMs</a> were the Escuderia Bandeirantes trio of <a href="/wiki/Gino_Bianco" title="Gino Bianco">Bianco</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eitel_Cantoni" title="Eitel Cantoni">Cantoni</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chico_Landi" title="Chico Landi">Landi</a>, while <a href="/wiki/Enrico_Plat%C3%A9" title="Enrico Platé">Enrico Platé</a>'s drivers—<a href="/wiki/Toulo_de_Graffenried" title="Toulo de Graffenried">Toulo de Graffenried</a> and debutant <a href="/wiki/Alberto_Crespo" title="Alberto Crespo">Alberto Crespo</a>—ran the older <a href="/wiki/Maserati_4CL_and_4CLT" title="Maserati 4CL and 4CLT">4CLT/48</a> equipped with the team's own revised engines. <a href="/wiki/Gordini" title="Gordini">Gordini</a> retained their previous driver lineup of <a href="/wiki/Jean_Behra" title="Jean Behra">Behra</a>, <a href="/wiki/Robert_Manzon" title="Robert Manzon">Manzon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Maurice_Trintignant" title="Maurice Trintignant">Trintignant</a>, while <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Claes" title="Johnny Claes">Johnny Claes</a> drove a privateer <a href="/wiki/Simca-Gordini" class="mw-redirect" title="Simca-Gordini">Simca-Gordini</a> at Monza. <a href="/wiki/Hersham_and_Walton_Motors" title="Hersham and Walton Motors">HWM</a> entered a pair of cars for <a href="/wiki/Peter_Collins_(racing_driver)" title="Peter Collins (racing driver)">Peter Collins</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lance_Macklin" title="Lance Macklin">Lance Macklin</a>, with Australian <a href="/wiki/Tony_Gaze" title="Tony Gaze">Tony Gaze</a> running a privateer HWM. The <a href="/wiki/Connaught_Engineering" title="Connaught Engineering">Connaught team</a>, absent since the <a href="/wiki/1952_British_Grand_Prix" title="1952 British Grand Prix">British Grand Prix</a>, returned to the Championship with a three-car entry consisting of <a href="/wiki/Stirling_Moss" title="Stirling Moss">Stirling Moss</a> (who had driven for <a href="/wiki/English_Racing_Automobiles" title="English Racing Automobiles">ERA</a> at the previous event), <a href="/wiki/Dennis_Poore" title="Dennis Poore">Dennis Poore</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kenneth_McAlpine" title="Kenneth McAlpine">Kenneth McAlpine</a>.
</p><p>For this event, only 24 cars were allowed to take the start, meaning that 11 of the 35 drivers who had entered the race failed to qualify. These included all of the HWMs, three of the privateer Ferraris, and both of the Enrico Platé-entered Maseratis. Ascari took his third consecutive pole position (and his fifth of the season), and the front row was completed by his teammates Villoresi and Farina, and the Gordini of Trintignant. The Maserati of González started from the second row, alongside the remaining works Ferraris of Taruffi and Simon, and the Gordini of Robert Manzon. Row three consisted of Stirling Moss in the leading Connaught, Frenchman <a href="/wiki/%C3%89lie_Bayol" title="Élie Bayol">Élie Bayol</a> in the sole <a href="/wiki/O.S.C.A." title="O.S.C.A.">OSCA</a>, Behra in the third and final works Gordini, and <a href="/wiki/Mike_Hawthorn" title="Mike Hawthorn">Mike Hawthorn</a> in his privateer <a href="/wiki/Cooper_Car_Company" title="Cooper Car Company">Cooper</a>-<a href="/wiki/Bristol_Cars" title="Bristol Cars">Bristol</a>. The remaining works Maseratis of Bonetto and Rol were only able to make the fourth row of the grid, starting from 13th and 16th, respectively.
</p><p>José Froilán González emerged in first place at the start of the race, ahead of Ascari in second. The Argentine remained in the lead for the first 36 laps of the race, until a slow pit stop allowed the Ferraris of Ascari and Villoresi to pass him for first and second, respectively. Ascari held the lead for the remainder of the race, and, in so doing, took his sixth consecutive World Championship race victory. González caught up with Villoresi and passed him to take second place in his only Championship race of the season. Villoresi completed the podium by taking his second consecutive third-place finish. Farina was not far behind in fourth place, while the second Maserati of Felice Bonetto took the final points position in fifth, finishing a lap down on the leaders. The remaining works Ferraris of Simon and Taruffi finished in sixth and seventh place, respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As Taruffi finished outside the points, he was unable to overtake Nino Farina in the Drivers' Championship standings. The Ferrari team monopolised the top three positions, with World Champion Alberto Ascari ahead of teammates Farina and Taruffi.
</p>
