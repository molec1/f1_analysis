<p>The <b>1994 Japanese Grand Prix</b> (officially the <b>XX Fuji Television Japanese Grand Prix</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 6 November 1994 at the <a href="/wiki/Suzuka_Circuit" class="mw-redirect" title="Suzuka Circuit">Suzuka Circuit</a>, <a href="/wiki/Suzuka,_Mie" title="Suzuka, Mie">Suzuka</a>. It was the fifteenth and penultimate race of the <a href="/wiki/1994_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1994 Formula One World Championship">1994 Formula One World Championship</a>. In wet conditions, the 50-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW16" title="Williams FW16">Williams</a>-<a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>, after he started from second position. Hill's Drivers' Championship rival <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> finished second in his <a href="/wiki/Benetton_B194" title="Benetton B194">Benetton</a>-<a href="/wiki/Ford_Motor_Company" title="Ford Motor Company">Ford</a>, having started from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a> third in his <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_412_T1" title="Ferrari 412 T1">Ferrari</a>. The win left Hill just one point behind Schumacher in the Drivers' Championship with one race remaining. This also proved to be the last Grand Prix for <a href="/wiki/%C3%89rik_Comas" title="Érik Comas">Érik Comas</a>. This was also the last time in Formula 1 history when the race was split in two parts due to race stoppage and final classification has been set by aggregate time.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1994_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Going into the race, <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> led the Drivers' Championship with 86 points, five ahead of rival <a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> on 81.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher felt he was "very confident" about the race,<sup id="cite_ref-interviews_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-interviews-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> and Hill similarly declared that he was "positive".<sup id="cite_ref-interviews_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-interviews-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>There were several changes of driver for this race: <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Herbert" title="Johnny Herbert">Johnny Herbert</a> moved from <a href="/wiki/Equipe_Ligier" title="Equipe Ligier">Ligier</a> to <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a> after just one race for the French team, replacing <a href="/wiki/Jos_Verstappen" title="Jos Verstappen">Jos Verstappen</a>. His place at Ligier was taken by <a href="/wiki/Franck_Lagorce" title="Franck Lagorce">Franck Lagorce</a>. <a href="/wiki/Eric_Bernard" class="mw-redirect" title="Eric Bernard">Eric Bernard</a> lost his seat at <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Lotus</a> to <a href="/wiki/Mika_Salo" title="Mika Salo">Mika Salo</a> who had been racing in Japanese <a href="/wiki/Formula_3000" title="Formula 3000">Formula 3000</a>, and likewise <a href="/wiki/Simtek" title="Simtek">Simtek</a> hired <a href="/wiki/Taki_Inoue" title="Taki Inoue">Taki Inoue</a> on a one-race deal, replacing <a href="/wiki/Domenico_Schiattarella" title="Domenico Schiattarella">Domenico Schiattarella</a>. Finally <a href="/wiki/JJ_Lehto" title="JJ Lehto">JJ Lehto</a> had returned to <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a> to replace <a href="/wiki/Andrea_de_Cesaris" title="Andrea de Cesaris">Andrea de Cesaris</a> after the Italian's sudden retirement from Formula One.
</p><p>The race started in torrential rain, and as a result, several cars spun out of the race by aquaplaning, including Schumacher's team-mate Herbert on lap 4, Lagorce, the <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardis</a> of <a href="/wiki/Pierluigi_Martini" title="Pierluigi Martini">Pierluigi Martini</a> and <a href="/wiki/Michele_Alboreto" title="Michele Alboreto">Michele Alboreto</a>, and all three Japanese drivers by the end of lap 3 (with both <a href="/wiki/Ukyo_Katayama" title="Ukyo Katayama">Ukyo Katayama</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hideki_Noda" title="Hideki Noda">Hideki Noda</a> being injured in separate crashes). Lehto also retired at the start with an engine failure. As did <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> in the second Ferrari with battery problems by lap 11.
</p><p>On lap 13, <a href="/wiki/Gianni_Morbidelli" title="Gianni Morbidelli">Gianni Morbidelli</a> crashed his <a href="/wiki/Footwork_Arrows" title="Footwork Arrows">Footwork</a> at one of the Esses at the first sector. Shortly afterwards, <a href="/wiki/Martin_Brundle" title="Martin Brundle">Martin Brundle</a> spun his <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> off the track and crashed at the same spot, and as he bounced off the tyre barriers, hit a track marshal who was moving Morbidelli's car off the gravel trap. The marshal suffered a broken leg, adding to the huge list of injuries of the 1994 season, and the race was immediately stopped, as both Brundle and Morbidelli were fortunately able to escape uninjured. <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> soon retired in the pits with transmission problems by lap 17, Blundell was also forced to retire from 10th position when his engine failed on lap 27, which ended an appalling weekend for Tyrrell.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> This left 13 runners, and there were no further retirements for the remaining 23 laps.
</p><p>As the rain eased, it was decided to run the remainder of the race, with around one hour to the time limit, on aggregate corrected time. Schumacher had been leading by 6.8 seconds when the red flag was shown, but Hill had a bigger lead (10.1 seconds) at the chequered flag, and thus took the win by 3.3 seconds on aggregate. This remains the last instance of aggregate race time being used in Formula One to determine the winner.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
