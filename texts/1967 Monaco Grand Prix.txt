<p>The <b>1967 Monaco Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Monaco" title="Circuit de Monaco">Monaco</a> on May 7, 1967. It was race 2 of 11 in both the <a href="/wiki/1967_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1967 World Championship of Drivers">1967 World Championship of Drivers</a> and the <a href="/wiki/1967_International_Cup_for_Formula_One_Manufacturers" class="mw-redirect" title="1967 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers">1967 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers</a>, albeit four months after <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Rodr%C3%ADguez_(racing_driver)" title="Pedro Rodríguez (racing driver)">Pedro Rodríguez's</a> unexpected victory at <a href="/wiki/Kyalami" title="Kyalami">Kyalami</a>. The 100-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Brabham" title="Brabham">Brabham</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Denny_Hulme" title="Denny Hulme">Denny Hulme</a> after he started from fourth position. <a href="/wiki/Graham_Hill" title="Graham Hill">Graham Hill</a> finished second for the <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Lotus</a> team and <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Chris_Amon" title="Chris Amon">Chris Amon</a> came in third.
</p><p>The race was overshadowed by the fatal accident suffered by Ferrari driver <a href="/wiki/Lorenzo_Bandini" title="Lorenzo Bandini">Lorenzo Bandini</a> in the late stages of the race.
</p><p>Between these races, the usual pre-season races had produced some unusual results, with <a href="/wiki/Dan_Gurney" title="Dan Gurney">Dan Gurney</a> winning at <a href="/wiki/Brands_Hatch" title="Brands Hatch">Brands Hatch</a>, in the <a href="/wiki/1967_Race_of_Champions" title="1967 Race of Champions">Race of Champions</a> in his <a href="/wiki/Anglo_American_Racers" class="mw-redirect" title="Anglo American Racers">Eagle</a>-<a href="/wiki/Weslake" title="Weslake">Weslake</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Mike_Parkes" title="Mike Parkes">Mike Parkes</a> taking the <a href="/wiki/1967_BRDC_International_Trophy" title="1967 BRDC International Trophy">BRDC International Trophy</a> for Ferrari.
</p><p>The straight after the Gasworks hairpin was lengthened by moving the 'Start and Finish' closer to Ste-Devote.<sup id="cite_ref-monaco-grandprix.org_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-monaco-grandprix.org-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1967_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Entry">Entry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1967_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Entry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A total of 17 <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> cars were entered for the event. The field was bolstered by a pair of <a href="/wiki/Formula_Two" title="Formula Two">Formula Two</a> <a href="/wiki/Equipe_Matra_Sports" title="Equipe Matra Sports">Matras</a>. The Monaco circuit with its tight layout, gave the 3-litre cars no advantage, thus many top teams entered their drivers in 2 or 2.5-litre cars. In fact the Formula Two Matras were powered by 1.6-litre <a href="/wiki/Cosworth" title="Cosworth">Cosworth</a> engines. <a href="/wiki/Honda_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Honda F1">Honda</a> was back with <a href="/wiki/John_Surtees" title="John Surtees">John Surtees</a>, with a V12 engine and the <a href="/wiki/Anglo_American_Racers" class="mw-redirect" title="Anglo American Racers">Anglo American Racers</a> were at Monaco for the first time, with their Eagle-Weslake.<sup id="cite_ref-monaco-grandprix.org_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-monaco-grandprix.org-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1967_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Jack_Brabham" title="Jack Brabham">Jack Brabham</a> took <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> for <a href="/wiki/Brabham" title="Brabham">Brabham Racing Organisation</a>, in their <a href="/wiki/Brabham_BT19" title="Brabham BT19">Brabham-Repco BT19</a>, averaging a speed of 80.779&#160;mph, around the 1.954 miles (3.145&#160;km) course. Brabham was joined on the front row by <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari's</a> <a href="/wiki/Lorenzo_Bandini" title="Lorenzo Bandini">Lorenzo Bandini</a>. The next row featured Surtees in the Honda and <a href="/wiki/Denny_Hulme" title="Denny Hulme">Denny Hulme</a> in the second <a href="/wiki/Brabham" title="Brabham">Brabham</a>. The third row was an all Scottish affair, with <a href="/wiki/Jim_Clark" title="Jim Clark">Jim Clark</a> (<a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Lotus</a>-<a href="/wiki/Coventry_Climax" title="Coventry Climax">Climax</a>) ahead of <a href="/wiki/Jackie_Stewart" title="Jackie Stewart">Jackie Stewart's</a> <a href="/wiki/British_Racing_Motors" title="British Racing Motors">BRM</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_start_through_lap_81">Race start through lap 81</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1967_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Race start through lap 81">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The opening few laps were eventful – Bandini going into the lead. Brabham's <a href="/wiki/Repco" title="Repco">Repco</a> engine blew up almost immediately, at Spélugues cruve, and he spun in front of <a href="/wiki/Bruce_McLaren" title="Bruce McLaren">Bruce McLaren</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jo_Siffert" title="Jo Siffert">Jo Siffert</a> who collided taking avoiding action. Only Siffert damaged his car and had to pit for repairs. Brabham continued, but was losing oil from Mirabeau to the port, whilst Clark had to take to the escape road after slipping on Brabham's oil.<sup id="cite_ref-monaco-grandprix.org_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-monaco-grandprix.org-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-formula1967_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-formula1967-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-wheelsofitaly1_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wheelsofitaly1-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> (Brabham retired at Mirabeau with a blown engine as a result.)
</p><p>On lap two Clark went off and dropped to the rear of the field, while Hulme and Stewart managed to pass Bandini into the lead after he too slipped on Brabham's oil. Hulme stayed in front until the sixth lap when Stewart swept past, until his crownwheel and pinion broke on lap 14. Hulme re-took the lead. The race settled down with Bandini second, McLaren third, after the departure of Surtees, with an engine failure. Clark's heroic battle from 14th up to fourth ended with broken shock absorber on lap 43. This promoted <a href="/wiki/Chris_Amon" title="Chris Amon">Chris Amon</a> to fourth.<sup id="cite_ref-formula1967_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-formula1967-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-wheelsofitaly1_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wheelsofitaly1-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the second half of the race, Bandini began to close in on Hulme. McLaren was holding Amon at bay until he was forced into the pits to change a battery. This dropped him behind Amon and <a href="/wiki/Graham_Hill" title="Graham Hill">Graham Hill</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-formula1967_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-formula1967-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-wheelsofitaly1_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wheelsofitaly1-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Piers Courage in the BRM had spun out on the hill just out of Sainte Devote by lap 65 and retired immediately after pulling off the track.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lap_82_accident_and_finish">Lap 82 accident and finish</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1967_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Lap 82 accident and finish">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On lap 82 disaster struck. Bandini's chase ended in horror when he clipped the chicane and hit a hidden mooring head, with the car turning over and exploding into flames amongst the straw bales. Bandini was trapped underneath his car. The rescue operation was hopelessly inadequate, the intervention was very slow and precious minutes passed before the fire was extinguished and Bandini was rescued and rushed to hospital. The rescue was not helped by a <a href="/wiki/Helicopter" title="Helicopter">helicopter</a> carrying a television camera crew, as it hovered at low level, the <a href="/wiki/Vertical_draft" title="Vertical draft">downdraught</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Helicopter_rotor" title="Helicopter rotor">rotor</a> blades fanned what remained of the fire, which reignited with a new ferocity.<sup id="cite_ref-monaco-grandprix.org_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-monaco-grandprix.org-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-formula1967_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-formula1967-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-wheelsofitaly1_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wheelsofitaly1-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Williams_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Williams-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Meanwhile, Hulme continued to lead the race to the finish unchallenged. With just eight laps to go, Amon suffered a puncture and dropped to third, with second going to Hill.<sup id="cite_ref-formula1967_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-formula1967-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-wheelsofitaly1_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wheelsofitaly1-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1967_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Bandini suffered horrendous burns and died of these injuries three days later - the tragedy overshadowing Hulme's first victory on one of the world's most difficult circuits. When the news broke, many of the star drivers were travelling to the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> to qualify for the <a href="/wiki/1967_Indianapolis_500" title="1967 Indianapolis 500">Indianapolis 500</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-monaco-grandprix.org_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-monaco-grandprix.org-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-formula1967_3-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-formula1967-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-wheelsofitaly1_4-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wheelsofitaly1-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> This was the last Monaco Grand Prix that was to run for 100 laps.
</p><p>Following the sad events of this race, straw bales were banned from Grand Prix circuits. The development of <a href="/wiki/Fire-retardant" class="mw-redirect" title="Fire-retardant">fire-retardant</a> fuel systems and <a href="/wiki/Nomex" title="Nomex">flameproof clothing</a> for drivers and marshals was accelerated, and never again would a TV camera crew be allowed to fly a helicopter low over a burning car.<sup id="cite_ref-Williams_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Williams-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
