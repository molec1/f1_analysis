<p>The <b>1950 British Grand Prix</b>, formally known as <b>The Royal Automobile Club Grand Prix d'Europe Incorporating The British Grand Prix</b>,<sup id="cite_ref-prog_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prog-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Auto_racing" title="Auto racing">motor race</a> held on 13 May 1950 at the <a href="/wiki/Silverstone_Circuit" title="Silverstone Circuit">Silverstone Circuit</a> in <a href="/wiki/Silverstone" title="Silverstone">Silverstone</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>. It was the first World Championship Formula One race, as well as the fifth <a href="/wiki/British_Grand_Prix" title="British Grand Prix">British Grand Prix</a>, and the third to be held at Silverstone after motor racing resumed after <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a>. It was the first race of seven in the <a href="/wiki/1950_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1950 World Championship of Drivers">1950 World Championship of Drivers</a>.
</p><p>The 70-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Giuseppe_Farina" title="Giuseppe Farina">Giuseppe Farina</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Alfa_Romeo_in_Formula_One" title="Alfa Romeo in Formula One">Alfa Romeo</a> team, after starting from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>, with a race time of 2:13:23.6 and an average speed of 146.378&#160;km/h. <a href="/wiki/Luigi_Fagioli" title="Luigi Fagioli">Luigi Fagioli</a> finished second in another <a href="/wiki/Alfa_Romeo_in_Formula_One" title="Alfa Romeo in Formula One">Alfa Romeo</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Reg_Parnell" title="Reg Parnell">Reg Parnell</a> third in a third <a href="/wiki/Alfa_Romeo_in_Formula_One" title="Alfa Romeo in Formula One">Alfa Romeo</a>.
</p><p>The race followed the non-championship <a href="/wiki/1950_Pau_Grand_Prix" title="1950 Pau Grand Prix">Pau Grand Prix</a> and <a href="/wiki/1950_San_Remo_Grand_Prix" title="1950 San Remo Grand Prix">San Remo Grand Prix</a> (both won by <a href="/wiki/Juan_Manuel_Fangio" title="Juan Manuel Fangio">Juan Manuel Fangio</a>), the <a href="/wiki/1950_Richmond_Trophy" title="1950 Richmond Trophy">Richmond Trophy</a> (won by <a href="/wiki/Reg_Parnell" title="Reg Parnell">Reg Parnell</a>) and the <a href="/wiki/1950_Paris_Grand_Prix" title="1950 Paris Grand Prix">Paris Grand Prix</a> (won by <a href="/wiki/Georges_Grignard" title="Georges Grignard">Georges Grignard</a>).
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1950_British_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a href="/wiki/File:RAF_Silverstone_1945.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c4/RAF_Silverstone_1945.png/200px-RAF_Silverstone_1945.png" decoding="async" width="200" height="304" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c4/RAF_Silverstone_1945.png/300px-RAF_Silverstone_1945.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c4/RAF_Silverstone_1945.png/400px-RAF_Silverstone_1945.png 2x" data-file-width="449" data-file-height="683" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:RAF_Silverstone_1945.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/RAF_Silverstone" title="RAF Silverstone">RAF Silverstone</a> airport in 1945</div></div></div>
<p>Held on 13 May at <a href="/wiki/Silverstone_Circuit" title="Silverstone Circuit">Silverstone Circuit</a>, and also designated as the <a href="/wiki/European_Grand_Prix" title="European Grand Prix">Grand Prix of Europe</a> for 1950, this very first World Championship round was attended by <a href="/wiki/George_VI" title="George VI">George VI</a>, <a href="/wiki/Queen_Elizabeth_The_Queen_Mother" title="Queen Elizabeth The Queen Mother">Queen Elizabeth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Princess_Margaret,_Countess_of_Snowdon" title="Princess Margaret, Countess of Snowdon">Princess Margaret</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Louis_Mountbatten,_1st_Earl_Mountbatten_of_Burma" title="Louis Mountbatten, 1st Earl Mountbatten of Burma">Earl</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/Edwina_Mountbatten,_Countess_Mountbatten_of_Burma" title="Edwina Mountbatten, Countess Mountbatten of Burma">Countess Mountbatten of Burma</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DundeeET_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DundeeET-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Participants">Participants</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1950_British_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Participants">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In all, there were 22 competing, 21 qualified for the race, and 11 classified. Numbers 7 and 13 were not assigned.<sup id="cite_ref-Nye-p39_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Nye-p39-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The Alfa Romeo factory team arrived at the circuit with four <a href="/wiki/Alfa_Romeo_158/159_Alfetta" title="Alfa Romeo 158/159 Alfetta">158s</a> for Fangio, Farina, Fagioli &amp; as a gesture to the English hosts, driver Reg Parnell.<sup id="cite_ref-Nye-p39_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Nye-p39-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> decided not to take part but there were a handful of <a href="/wiki/Maserati_in_motorsport" title="Maserati in motorsport">Maseratis</a>, one of them a factory car for Monegasque driver <a href="/wiki/Louis_Chiron" title="Louis Chiron">Louis Chiron</a>. <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ambrosiana" title="Scuderia Ambrosiana">Scuderia Ambrosiana</a> prepared two cars for <a href="/wiki/David_Hampshire" title="David Hampshire">David Hampshire</a> and <a href="/wiki/David_Murray_(racing_driver)" title="David Murray (racing driver)">David Murray</a>, <a href="/wiki/Enrico_Plat%C3%A9" title="Enrico Platé">Enrico Platé</a> entered two drivers of aristocratic origin, <a href="/wiki/Prince_Bira" class="mw-redirect" title="Prince Bira">Prince Bira</a> of Siam and Baron <a href="/wiki/Toulo_de_Graffenried" title="Toulo de Graffenried">Toulo de Graffenried</a>. <a href="/wiki/Joe_Fry" title="Joe Fry">Joe Fry</a> entered a private Maserati and <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Milano" title="Scuderia Milano">Scuderia Milano</a> entered <a href="/wiki/Felice_Bonetto" title="Felice Bonetto">Felice Bonetto</a>, but he did not arrive. These cars were raced in Italian <a href="/wiki/Rosso_Corsa" class="mw-redirect" title="Rosso Corsa">Rosso Corsa</a> livery. <a href="/wiki/Talbot-Lago" title="Talbot-Lago">Talbot-Lago</a> sent over two factory cars in the traditional French pale blue colour to be driven by <a href="/wiki/Yves_Giraud-Cabantous" title="Yves Giraud-Cabantous">Yves Giraud-Cabantous</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eug%C3%A8ne_Martin" title="Eugène Martin">Eugène Martin</a>. Other private Talbots were entered by <a href="/wiki/Louis_Rosier" title="Louis Rosier">Louis Rosier</a>, <a href="/wiki/Philippe_Etancelin" class="mw-redirect" title="Philippe Etancelin">Philippe Etancelin</a> and Belgian <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Claes" title="Johnny Claes">Johnny Claes</a>, in a yellow car. The rest of the field was made up of local machinery, which included four <a href="/wiki/English_Racing_Automobiles" title="English Racing Automobiles">E.R.A.s</a> and two <a href="/wiki/Alta_Car_and_Engineering_Company" title="Alta Car and Engineering Company">Altas</a>, in <a href="/wiki/British_racing_green" title="British racing green">British racing green</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DundeeET_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DundeeET-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_race">The race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1950_British_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: The race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Farina was fastest in qualifying and the other three Alfas were alongside him on the front row. The second row consisted of <a href="/wiki/Prince_Bira" class="mw-redirect" title="Prince Bira">"B. Bira"</a> in a Maserati and the two factory Talbots. In accordance with the standard at the time, the rest of the grid consisted of rows of four and three alternating, up to the sixth row. <a href="/wiki/Felice_Bonetto" title="Felice Bonetto">Felice Bonetto</a> was the only driver who did not take part in qualifying and would not take part in the race. On 13 May, 21 drivers from 9 countries were represented at the old Silverstone airport, 4 from France, 2 from Italy, 1 each from Belgium, Ireland, Monaco, Argentina, Thailand and Switzerland. The UK was represented by 9 drivers. The race drew 200,000 spectators. At the start of the race, Farina took the lead with Fagioli and Fangio in pursuit, while Cabantous got a poor start and lost 4 positions. In the early laps they switched around between themselves several times to keep everyone amused. Fangio retired with engine troubles caused by a broken oil pipe and so Farina led Fagioli home by 2.5 seconds with Parnell a distant third despite hitting a hare during the race. The nearest challenger was Giraud-Cabantous two laps down, Bira having retired with a fuel problem. Crossley and Murray duelled at the back before retiring, de Graffenried had done so on lap 34, while Chiron was demoted to the role of viewer 10 laps earlier. <a href="/wiki/Giuseppe_Farina" title="Giuseppe Farina">Giuseppe Farina</a> led for 63 laps (1–9, 16–37 and 39–70). <a href="/wiki/Luigi_Fagioli" title="Luigi Fagioli">Luigi Fagioli</a> led for 6 laps (10–15). <a href="/wiki/Juan_Manuel_Fangio" title="Juan Manuel Fangio">Juan Manuel Fangio</a> led for 1 lap (38). <a href="/wiki/Joe_Fry" title="Joe Fry">Joe Fry</a> drove car #10 for the first 45 laps, then <a href="/wiki/Brian_Shawe-Taylor" title="Brian Shawe-Taylor">Brian Shawe-Taylor</a> took over for 19 laps for a total 64 laps, distance 297.536&#160;km. <a href="/wiki/Peter_Walker_(driver)" class="mw-redirect" title="Peter Walker (driver)">Peter Walker</a> drove car #9 for 2 laps, then <a href="/wiki/Tony_Rolt" title="Tony Rolt">Tony Rolt</a> drove for and additional 3 laps, totaling 5 laps, a distance of 23.245&#160;km. <a href="/wiki/Giuseppe_Farina" title="Giuseppe Farina">Giuseppe Farina</a> achieved the fastest lap of the race, with a 1:50.6 on Lap 2.<sup id="cite_ref-DundeeET_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DundeeET-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
