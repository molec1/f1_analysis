<p>The <b>2020 Italian Grand Prix</b> (officially known as the <b>Formula 1 Gran Premio Heineken d'Italia 2020</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Auto_racing" title="Auto racing">motor race</a> that was held on 6 September 2020 at the <a href="/wiki/Autodromo_Nazionale_di_Monza" title="Autodromo Nazionale di Monza">Autodromo Nazionale di Monza</a> in <a href="/wiki/Monza" title="Monza">Monza, Italy</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The race was the eighth round in the <a href="/wiki/2020_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2020 Formula One World Championship">2020 Formula One World Championship</a>.
</p><p>The race was won by <a href="/wiki/Pierre_Gasly" title="Pierre Gasly">Pierre Gasly</a> of <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_AlphaTauri" title="Scuderia AlphaTauri">AlphaTauri</a>-<a href="/wiki/Honda_in_Formula_One" title="Honda in Formula One">Honda</a>, who took his first Formula One win and became the first <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_drivers_from_France" title="Formula One drivers from France">French Formula One driver</a> to win a race since <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Panis" title="Olivier Panis">Olivier Panis</a> won the <a href="/wiki/1996_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="1996 Monaco Grand Prix">1996 Monaco Grand Prix</a>. Gasly started the race in tenth, but gained positions due to a well-timed pit-stop prior to a <a href="/wiki/Safety_car" title="Safety car">safety car</a>, sent to retrieve the broken car of <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Magnussen" title="Kevin Magnussen">Kevin Magnussen</a>. <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>, who led the race until this point, was given a penalty for entering the pit lane when it was closed, passing the lead to Gasly, who defended from <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Sainz_Jr." title="Carlos Sainz Jr.">Carlos Sainz Jr.</a> in the closing stages of the race. <a href="/wiki/Racing_Point_F1_Team" title="Racing Point F1 Team">Racing Point</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Lance_Stroll" title="Lance Stroll">Lance Stroll</a> completed the podium.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> This was the first race since the <a href="/wiki/2012_Hungarian_Grand_Prix" title="2012 Hungarian Grand Prix">2012 Hungarian Grand Prix</a> to not have a Red Bull, Mercedes, or Ferrari driver on the podium and the first not to be won by a driver from those teams since the <a href="/wiki/2013_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="2013 Australian Grand Prix">2013 Australian Grand Prix</a>.
</p><p>It was the first instance of there being two standing starts since the <a href="/wiki/2001_Belgian_Grand_Prix" title="2001 Belgian Grand Prix">2001 Belgian Grand Prix</a>, following a 2018 change in the regulations to allow for standing restarts after a red flag.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>This race was the last for both <a href="/wiki/Claire_Williams" title="Claire Williams">Claire Williams</a> and <a href="/wiki/Frank_Williams_(Formula_One)" title="Frank Williams (Formula One)">Frank Williams</a>, as they stepped down from their positions at <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams Racing</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2020_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Impact_of_the_COVID-19_pandemic">Impact of the COVID-19 pandemic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2020_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Impact of the COVID-19 pandemic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/2020_Formula_One_World_Championship#Impact_of_the_COVID-19_pandemic" title="2020 Formula One World Championship">2020 Formula One World Championship §&#160;Impact of the COVID-19 pandemic</a>, and <a href="/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic_in_Italy" title="COVID-19 pandemic in Italy">COVID-19 pandemic in Italy</a></div>
<p>The opening rounds of the <a href="/wiki/2020_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2020 Formula One World Championship">2020</a> championship were heavily affected by the <a href="/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic" title="COVID-19 pandemic">COVID-19 pandemic</a>. Several Grands Prix were cancelled or postponed after the aborted <a href="/wiki/2020_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="2020 Australian Grand Prix">opening round</a> in Australia, prompting the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile</a> to draft a new calendar. However, the <a href="/wiki/Italian_Grand_Prix" title="Italian Grand Prix">Italian Grand Prix</a> was not impacted by this change and kept its original date.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Entrants">Entrants</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2020_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Entrants">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Further information: <a href="/wiki/2020_Formula_One_World_Championship#Entries" title="2020 Formula One World Championship">2020 Formula One World Championship §&#160;Entries</a></div>
<p>The drivers and teams were the same as the season entry list with no additional stand-in drivers for the race.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Roy_Nissany" title="Roy Nissany">Roy Nissany</a> drove for <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> in the first practice session, replacing <a href="/wiki/George_Russell_(racing_driver)" title="George Russell (racing driver)">George Russell</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tyres">Tyres</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2020_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Tyres">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_tyres" title="Formula One tyres">Formula One tyres</a></div>
<p>Pirelli brought the C2, C3 and C4 tyres for the race weekend.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Regulation_changes">Regulation changes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2020_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Regulation changes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Prior to the race, the governing body, the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile</a>, issued a technical directive banning qualifying-specific engine modes from the Italian Grand Prix onwards. The ban was initially planned for the <a href="/wiki/2020_Belgian_Grand_Prix" title="2020 Belgian Grand Prix">2020 Belgian Grand Prix</a> but it was delayed by one race.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice">Practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2020_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The first practice session was interrupted briefly when <a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a> crashed at the Ascari chicane. The session ended with <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a> fastest ahead of <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> teammate <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Albon" title="Alexander Albon">Alexander Albon</a> third fastest.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The second practice session ran without major incidents and concluded with Hamilton fastest, followed by Bottas and <a href="/wiki/Lando_Norris" title="Lando Norris">Lando Norris</a> of <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Bottas was again fastest in third practice, followed by <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Sainz_Jr." title="Carlos Sainz Jr.">Carlos Sainz Jr.</a> of <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> and his teammate Norris. The session was briefly red flagged after <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> stopped his <a href="/wiki/Renault_R.S.20" title="Renault R.S.20">Renault R.S.20</a> due to a mechanical failure.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2020_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying_report">Qualifying report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2020_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Qualifying report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> took pole, 0.069s ahead of <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> teammate <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a>. Hamilton's lap time of 1:18.887 is the fastest Formula One lap in history, with an average speed of 264.362&#160;km/h (164.267&#160;mph).<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>’s <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Sainz_Jr." title="Carlos Sainz Jr.">Carlos Sainz Jr.</a> took third with <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a> fourth for <a href="/wiki/Racing_Point_F1_Team" title="Racing Point F1 Team">Racing Point</a>. <a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a> was fifth in his <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a> followed by <a href="/wiki/Lando_Norris" title="Lando Norris">Lando Norris</a>. The rest of the top ten were <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lance_Stroll" title="Lance Stroll">Lance Stroll</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Albon" title="Alexander Albon">Alexander Albon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pierre_Gasly" title="Pierre Gasly">Pierre Gasly</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_AlphaTauri" title="Scuderia AlphaTauri">AlphaTauri</a>. <a href="/wiki/Daniil_Kvyat" title="Daniil Kvyat">Daniil Kvyat</a> finished 11th, with <a href="/wiki/Esteban_Ocon" title="Esteban Ocon">Esteban Ocon</a> 12th and <a href="/wiki/Charles_Leclerc" title="Charles Leclerc">Charles Leclerc</a> 13th for <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>. <a href="/wiki/Alfa_Romeo_in_Formula_One" title="Alfa Romeo in Formula One">Alfa Romeo</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> managed 14th with <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Magnussen" title="Kevin Magnussen">Kevin Magnussen</a> 15th for <a href="/wiki/Haas_F1_Team" title="Haas F1 Team">Haas</a>. Out in the first part of qualifying were <a href="/wiki/Romain_Grosjean" title="Romain Grosjean">Romain Grosjean</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Antonio_Giovinazzi" title="Antonio Giovinazzi">Antonio Giovinazzi</a> and both <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> drivers. The final part of Q1 was marked by numerous cars starting their final flying lap at close distance, hampering each other's performance.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2020_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_report">Race report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2020_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Race report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Lewis Hamilton successfully retained the lead position on the start, while his teammate Valtteri Bottas gradually dropped to sixth place over the first two laps while reporting problems with his car. Carlos Sainz pulled away quickly to take Valterri Bottas off the start claiming second. The McLaren driver went on to pull a gap to the cars behind. During lap six the left-rear brake on Sebastian Vettel's <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_SF1000" title="Ferrari SF1000">SF1000</a> overheated to the point that the brake assembly caught fire before disintegrating and flying off the car at the start of lap seven. This caused Vettel to miss a couple of corners (in the process he smashed through the polystyrene chicane direction indicator boxes at turn 1) before limping to the pits to retire. 
</p><p>On lap 18, Kevin Magnussen's <a href="/wiki/Haas_F1_Team" title="Haas F1 Team">Haas</a> suffered a power unit failure and his car stranded to the side of the pit lane entrance. At the end of the next lap, Pierre Gasly elected to make his pitstop. The <a href="/wiki/Safety_car" title="Safety car">safety car</a> was deployed shortly afterwards and the pit lane was closed to allow marshals to safely remove the stranded Haas from the track. Both Hamilton and Antonio Giovinazzi made a pit stop shortly after the safety car had been deployed despite the pit lane being closed. They were both given 10-second stop-and-go penalties for this infraction. When the pit lane was re-opened two laps later, most of the field entered to make their pit stop. This allowed Gasly to move into third place behind Hamilton and <a href="/wiki/Lance_Stroll" title="Lance Stroll">Lance Stroll</a>, the only driver who did not stop. The safety car was withdrawn at the end of the next lap, allowing normal race conditions to resume on lap 24. Hamilton held on to the lead ahead of Stroll and Gasly. Charles Leclerc crashed heavily after the restart in the Parabolica corner as the car lost its rear end, entered the gravel trap and collided with the barrier, significantly damaging the tyre barriers and his car in the process. The safety car was deployed initially, but the <a href="/wiki/Red_flag_(racing)" class="mw-redirect" title="Red flag (racing)">red flags</a> were brought out shortly afterwards to suspend the race to allow repairs to be made to the tyre barriers.  
</p><p>The race resumed later with a standing restart on lap 28. Stroll went wide at turn four dropping to fifth place in the process, with Gasly inheriting second place. Hamilton served his stop-and-go penalty at the end of the lap while Giovinazzi served his one lap later dropping them to the back of the field. This allowed Gasly to take the lead of the race ahead of Kimi Räikkönen and Carlos Sainz, Jr. Over the next 25 laps Gasly held off Räikkönen, who gradually fell back to an eventual 13th place finish, and Sainz to secure victory. Stroll meanwhile fought back to third place to complete the podium. 
</p><p>All podium finishers took their second podiums of their career, with Gasly taking his first <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_race_winners" class="mw-redirect" title="List of Formula One race winners">Formula One victory</a> and giving <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_AlphaTauri" title="Scuderia AlphaTauri">AlphaTauri</a> their first win as a constructor and second win as a team, over 12 years since Vettel won the <a href="/wiki/2008_Italian_Grand_Prix" title="2008 Italian Grand Prix">2008 Italian Grand Prix</a> when the team was known as <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso</a>. Gasly's victory was the first for a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_drivers_from_France" title="Formula One drivers from France">French driver in Formula One</a> since <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Panis" title="Olivier Panis">Olivier Panis</a> at the <a href="/wiki/1996_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="1996 Monaco Grand Prix">1996 Monaco Grand Prix</a> and the 80th F1 World Championship race win for a French driver overall. The win also made <a href="/wiki/Honda_in_Formula_One" title="Honda in Formula One">Honda</a> the first engine manufacturer to win with two different teams in the sport's V6 turbo-hybrid era.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> The result marked the first time that <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a> all failed to score a podium finish since the <a href="/wiki/2012_Hungarian_Grand_Prix" title="2012 Hungarian Grand Prix">2012 Hungarian Grand Prix</a>. It was also the first time since Räikkönen won the <a href="/wiki/2013_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="2013 Australian Grand Prix">2013 Australian Grand Prix</a> driving for <a href="/wiki/Lotus_F1" title="Lotus F1">Lotus</a> that the race winner did not drive for Ferrari, Mercedes or Red Bull.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As <a href="/wiki/Claire_Williams" title="Claire Williams">Claire</a> and <a href="/wiki/Frank_Williams_(Formula_One)" title="Frank Williams (Formula One)">Frank Williams</a> ceased their involvement with the Williams team after this Grand Prix,<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> tributes<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (September 2020)">clarification needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> were paid to the pair from around the paddock, including from <a href="/wiki/George_Russell_(racing_driver)" title="George Russell (racing driver)">George Russell</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nicholas_Latifi" title="Nicholas Latifi">Nicholas Latifi</a>, the Williams drivers, as they crossed the line.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
