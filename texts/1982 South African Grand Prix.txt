<p>The <b>1982 South African Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held at <a href="/wiki/Kyalami" title="Kyalami">Kyalami</a> on 23 January 1982. It was the first race of the <a href="/wiki/1982_FIA_Formula_One_World_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="1982 FIA Formula One World Championship">1982 FIA Formula One World Championship</a>.
</p><p>The prelude to the race was notable for a <a href="/wiki/Strike_action" title="Strike action">strike</a> action by the <a href="/wiki/Grand_Prix_Drivers%27_Association" title="Grand Prix Drivers&#39; Association">Grand Prix Drivers' Association</a>, led by <a href="/wiki/Niki_Lauda" title="Niki Lauda">Niki Lauda</a> and <a href="/wiki/Didier_Pironi" title="Didier Pironi">Didier Pironi</a>, in protest at the new <a href="/wiki/FIA_Super_Licence" title="FIA Super Licence">superlicence</a> conditions imposed by <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_du_Sport_Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale du Sport Automobile">FISA</a>, which would have tied the drivers to a single team for up to three years. A late compromise was reached and the race went ahead. The drivers were subsequently fined between <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">US$</a>5,000 and US$10,000 and handed suspended race bans; however, the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">FIA</a> Court of Appeal later reduced the penalties and criticised FISA's handling of the dispute.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Turbocharger" title="Turbocharger">Turbocharged</a> cars took the first six positions on the grid. Despite <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> suffering a puncture while leading, he was able to recover to win the race. Lauda, in his first race after two years out of F1, finished fourth. It was the final podium F1 finish for Carlos Reutemann, who retired from Grand Prix racing after the next race in Brazil,<sup id="cite_ref-C+SC30_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-C+SC30-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> and his place at Williams was taken by Mario Andretti for the following race, at Long Beach.
</p>
