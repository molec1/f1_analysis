<p>The <b>1954 French Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held at <a href="/wiki/Reims-Gueux" title="Reims-Gueux">Reims</a> on 4 July 1954, the same date as the <a href="/wiki/1954_FIFA_World_Cup_Final" title="1954 FIFA World Cup Final">1954 Football World Cup Final</a>. It was race 4 of 9 in the <a href="/wiki/1954_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1954 World Championship of Drivers">1954 World Championship of Drivers</a>. The 61-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Juan_Manuel_Fangio" title="Juan Manuel Fangio">Juan Manuel Fangio</a> after he started from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>. His teammate <a href="/wiki/Karl_Kling" title="Karl Kling">Karl Kling</a> finished second and <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Robert_Manzon" title="Robert Manzon">Robert Manzon</a> came in third. This race was held the same day as the <a href="/wiki/1954_FIFA_World_Cup_Final" title="1954 FIFA World Cup Final">1954 FIFA World Cup Final</a> in <a href="/wiki/Bern" title="Bern">Bern</a>, <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a>, but that event took place later in the day from this Grand Prix.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_report">Race report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1954_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Race report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The long-awaited <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_W196" title="Mercedes-Benz W196">Mercedes W196</a> with its straight-8 fuel-injection engine made its debut with <a href="/wiki/Juan_Manuel_Fangio" title="Juan Manuel Fangio">Juan Manuel Fangio</a> transferring from <a href="/wiki/Maserati_in_motorsport" title="Maserati in motorsport">Maserati</a> to join an otherwise all-German line-up of <a href="/wiki/Hans_Herrmann" title="Hans Herrmann">Hans Herrmann</a>, <a href="/wiki/Karl_Kling" title="Karl Kling">Karl Kling</a> and pre-war driver <a href="/wiki/Hermann_Lang" title="Hermann Lang">Hermann Lang</a>. It was a dominant return with Fangio recording a practice lap of 124.31&#160;mph. He and Kling led away and continued to race side by side around the Rheims track. The <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> drivers simply couldn't cope with the pace. <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Froil%C3%A1n_Gonz%C3%A1lez" title="José Froilán González">José Froilán González</a> retired after 12 laps and <a href="/wiki/Mike_Hawthorn" title="Mike Hawthorn">Mike Hawthorn</a>'s car blew up spectacularly. Former Ferrari double World Champion (1952 &amp; 1953) <a href="/wiki/Alberto_Ascari" title="Alberto Ascari">Alberto Ascari</a> drove a Maserati, as the <a href="/wiki/Lancia_D50" title="Lancia D50">Lancia D50</a> was not yet ready for racing, and lasted only 1 lap after starting on the outside of the front row. Herrmann set fastest lap before retiring, but Fangio and Kling continued their duel until the last lap when team orders were put in force and Fangio led Kling over the line by a mere 0.1 seconds-half a car length. Only six cars finished the gruelling race.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1954_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><b>F1 Debut:</b> Jacques Pollet, Karl Kling, <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Continental_AG" title="Continental AG">Continental</a> tyre.</li>
<li>Debut win for <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Continental_AG" title="Continental AG">Continental</a> in Formula One.</li>
<li>Excluding the Indianapolis 500, this was the first time a non-Italian constructor won a Formula One World Championship race.</li>
<li>The Thillois hairpin had been changed from the year before. The original corner's surface was laid out with bricks; the new corner bypassed this hairpin with a new smooth tarmac surface.</li></ul>
