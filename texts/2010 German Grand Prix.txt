<p>The <b>2010 German Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>Formula 1 Großer Preis Santander von Deutschland 2010</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 25 July at the <a href="/wiki/Hockenheimring" title="Hockenheimring">Hockenheimring</a> in <a href="/wiki/Hockenheim" title="Hockenheim">Hockenheim</a>, <a href="/wiki/Baden-W%C3%BCrttemberg" title="Baden-Württemberg">Baden-Württemberg</a>, Germany. It was the eleventh round of the <a href="/wiki/2010_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2010 Formula One season">2010 Formula One season</a> and the seventy-first <a href="/wiki/German_Grand_Prix" title="German Grand Prix">German Grand Prix</a>. The 67-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> after he started from second position. His teammate <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> finished second, and <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> came in third.
</p><p>Although Vettel and Alonso were on the grid's front row, both drivers were passed by Massa at the start of the race. Alonso and Massa traded the fastest times in the first twelve laps until a sequence of <a href="/wiki/Pit_stop" title="Pit stop">pit stops</a>. Massa struggled with tyre temperatures and Alonso challenged him on the 21st lap but fended off his efforts to take the lead. Massa pulled away slightly from Alonso until traffic slowed him and reduced the time deficit between the two drivers. Alonso took over the first position after Ferrari invoked <a href="/wiki/Team_orders" title="Team orders">team orders</a> on Massa to allow Alonso past. Massa resisted pressure from Vettel while Alonso maintained the lead for the rest of the race to take the 23rd victory of his career.
</p><p>Ferrari's team orders resulted in a fine of $100,000 by the race stewards but avoided further punishment from the <a href="/wiki/FIA_World_Motor_Sport_Council" title="FIA World Motor Sport Council">FIA World Motor Sport Council</a> in September 2010. Alonso's victory put him within 13 points of Vettel in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a>, while the leader <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> extended his advantage over teammate <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> by two points. Red Bull slightly reduced the deficit to <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a>, while Ferrari moved further ahead of <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a>, with eight races left in the season.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The 2010 German Grand Prix was the eleventh scheduled race of the <a href="/wiki/2010_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2010 Formula One season">2010 Formula One season</a> and was held on 25 July 2010 at the <a href="/wiki/Hockenheimring" title="Hockenheimring">Hockenheimring</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hockenheim" title="Hockenheim">Hockenheim</a>, <a href="/wiki/Baden-W%C3%BCrttemberg" title="Baden-Württemberg">Baden-Württemberg</a>, Germany.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The Grand Prix was contested by twelve teams with two drivers each. The teams (also known as <a href="/wiki/Formula_One#Constructors" title="Formula One">constructors</a>) were: <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a>, <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>, <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso</a>, <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus_(2010%E2%80%9311)" title="Team Lotus (2010–11)">Lotus</a>, <a href="/wiki/HRT_Formula_1_Team" title="HRT Formula 1 Team">Hispania</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a> and <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Racing" title="Virgin Racing">Virgin</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-results_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-results-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_tyre" class="mw-redirect" title="Formula One tyre">Tyre</a> supplier <a href="/wiki/Bridgestone" title="Bridgestone">Bridgestone</a> brought four types of tyre to the race; two dry compounds (super-soft "options" and hard "primes") and two <a href="/wiki/Rain_tyre" title="Rain tyre">wet-weather compounds</a> (intermediate and full wet).<sup id="cite_ref-f1racing_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1racing-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-regulations_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Bridgestone selected those compounds in an attempt to improve the excitement of Formula One after a large amount of degradation and graining was observed during the <a href="/wiki/2010_Canadian_Grand_Prix" title="2010 Canadian Grand Prix">Canadian Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The soft compounds were denoted by a green stripe on their side-walls; the wet compound tyres were identified by a green line at the bottom of their central groove.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Before the race, McLaren driver <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> led the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a> with 145 <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Championship_points_scoring_systems" title="List of Formula One World Championship points scoring systems">points</a>, twelve points ahead of teammate <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> in second who was five points in front of <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> in third. Webber's teammate <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> was fourth on 121 points and <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> was fifth on 98 points.<sup id="cite_ref-points_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> McLaren were leading the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a> with 278 points; Red Bull and Ferrari were second and third with 249 and 165 points, while Mercedes (126 points) and Renault (89) contended for fourth place.<sup id="cite_ref-points_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> McLaren and Red Bull had dominated the championship, while Alonso won the season-opening <a href="/wiki/2010_Bahrain_Grand_Prix" title="2010 Bahrain Grand Prix">Bahrain Grand Prix</a>. His teammate <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a> had finished in second place, and <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> (three times) Massa and Kubica (once) had taken third-place finishes.<sup id="cite_ref-points_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After the previous German Grand Prix (at the <a href="/wiki/N%C3%BCrburgring" title="Nürburgring">Nürburgring</a>) in July 2009, the future of the race was in doubt after the government of Baden-Württemberg and the town of Hockenheim withdrew their funding for the event. Planned discussions between the owner of Formula One's commercial rights <a href="/wiki/Bernie_Ecclestone" title="Bernie Ecclestone">Bernie Ecclestone</a> and the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Ministers-President_of_Baden-W%C3%BCrttemberg" title="List of Ministers-President of Baden-Württemberg">Minister-President of Baden-Württemberg</a> <a href="/wiki/G%C3%BCnther_Oettinger" title="Günther Oettinger">Günther Oettinger</a> to renegotiate the expenses for hosting the event had ended after Ecclestone made controversial comments regarding <a href="/wiki/Adolf_Hitler" title="Adolf Hitler">Adolf Hitler</a>, and the organisers of the Nürburgring circuit announced that they would not hold the race in 2010 because of a commitment to hold the event in 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> An agreement was reached in September which would see the event run until 2018 with both circuit's management and <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_Group" title="Formula One Group">Formula One Management</a> agreeing to cover any losses incurred.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Sakon_Yamamoto_2010_Germany_(cropped).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a7/Sakon_Yamamoto_2010_Germany_%28cropped%29.jpg/220px-Sakon_Yamamoto_2010_Germany_%28cropped%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="120" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a7/Sakon_Yamamoto_2010_Germany_%28cropped%29.jpg/330px-Sakon_Yamamoto_2010_Germany_%28cropped%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a7/Sakon_Yamamoto_2010_Germany_%28cropped%29.jpg/440px-Sakon_Yamamoto_2010_Germany_%28cropped%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3652" data-file-height="1994" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Sakon_Yamamoto_2010_Germany_(cropped).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>After replacing <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Senna" title="Bruno Senna">Bruno Senna</a> at the <a href="/wiki/2010_British_Grand_Prix" title="2010 British Grand Prix">British Grand Prix</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sakon_Yamamoto" title="Sakon Yamamoto">Sakon Yamamoto</a> replaced <a href="/wiki/Karun_Chandhok" title="Karun Chandhok">Karun Chandhok</a> in <a href="/wiki/Hispania_Racing" class="mw-redirect" title="Hispania Racing">Hispania Racing</a>'s second car.</div></div></div>
<p>Despite taking victory in the season-opening Bahrain Grand Prix, Alonso faced pressure heading into the second half of the season; he was forty-seven points behind Hamilton in the Drivers' Championship after scoring four points in the last two races. Alonso had been largely affected by <a href="/wiki/Safety_car" title="Safety car">safety car</a> periods in <a href="/wiki/2010_European_Grand_Prix" title="2010 European Grand Prix">Valencia</a> and <a href="/wiki/2010_British_Grand_Prix" title="2010 British Grand Prix">Silverstone</a> despite his team having narrowed the performance gap on their championship rivals. However, Alonso remained upbeat about his title chances and said his team had more motivation to succeed despite his recent results.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton, the defending race winner at the Hockenheimring, spoke of his feeling the race would provide an indication on form for the second half of the year and believed McLaren should have a successful car balance to be competitive at the track.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Several teams made alterations to their cars in preparation for the event. Mercedes introduced a new version of their car's rear wing which was designed to work more efficiently with their F-duct system.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Ferrari brought a refinement to their exhaust system and modified their diffuser's side channels.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Red Bull introduced minor improvements to their car's F-duct system and both of their drivers used a front wing specification which courted controversy at the preceding British Grand Prix.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> McLaren used a blown <a href="/wiki/Diffuser_(automotive)" title="Diffuser (automotive)">diffuser</a> which was discarded after the Friday practice sessions at the previous Grand Prix and was changed for the German Grand Prix. The diffuser been moved outwards but had a longer inner section, the pipe was cut off at an angle, and the carbon materials for its side channel were changed.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Sauber came to the circuit with their car carrying several aerodynamic upgrades which included a modified diffuser.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>There was one driver change heading into the race. <a href="/wiki/Karun_Chandhok" title="Karun Chandhok">Karun Chandhok</a> was dropped from the Hispania Racing team and was replaced by <a href="/wiki/Sakon_Yamamoto" title="Sakon Yamamoto">Sakon Yamamoto</a> while <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Senna" title="Bruno Senna">Bruno Senna</a> reclaimed his race seat after sitting out the British Grand Prix. Hispania released a statement which said Yamamoto was given another opportunity to race after impressing at the previous race and that Chandhok remained part of the team.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice">Practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Lewis_hamilton_hockenheim_2010.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a4/Lewis_hamilton_hockenheim_2010.jpg/220px-Lewis_hamilton_hockenheim_2010.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="124" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a4/Lewis_hamilton_hockenheim_2010.jpg/330px-Lewis_hamilton_hockenheim_2010.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a4/Lewis_hamilton_hockenheim_2010.jpg/440px-Lewis_hamilton_hockenheim_2010.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4908" data-file-height="2761" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Lewis_hamilton_hockenheim_2010.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> crashed in the first practice session.</div></div></div>
<p>Three practice sessions—two on Friday and a third on Saturday—were held before the main Sunday race. The Friday morning and afternoon sessions each lasted ninety minutes. The third session was held on Saturday morning and lasted an hour.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The first session was initially held in wet-weather conditions before less rain fell and drivers were required to adapt to changing levels of <a href="/wiki/Grip_(auto_racing)" title="Grip (auto racing)">grip</a>. Full wet tyres were used in the session's opening period before the field utilised intermediate tyres in its closing minutes.<sup id="cite_ref-gp1_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gp1-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> The conditions prevented the teams from collecting data on how the dry-compound tyres would perform.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a> was the fastest driver with a time of 1:25.701 seconds, ahead of Massa who spun several times and had the fastest time until Sutil's lap. Jenson Button was third-fastest despite reporting an handling imbalance, ahead of <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> (who spun at high-speed at turn three after driving into a large amount of standing water) <a href="/wiki/Vitaly_Petrov" title="Vitaly Petrov">Vitaly Petrov</a>, Rosberg and <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9bastien_Buemi" title="Sébastien Buemi">Sébastien Buemi</a>. <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vitantonio_Liuzzi" title="Vitantonio Liuzzi">Vitantonio Liuzzi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pedro_de_la_Rosa" title="Pedro de la Rosa">Pedro de la Rosa</a> rounded out the session's top-ten drivers. Hamilton drove over standing water at the exit of the turn three left-hander twenty minutes before the session ended and slid sideways into the turn four tyre barrier, heavily damaging his car.<sup id="cite_ref-gp1_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gp1-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Most of the track dried up despite a light rain shower before the second practice session and drivers recorded more lap times to make up for the lack of running in the first session. Some drivers also went off the circuit. Alonso set the fastest lap of the day with a time of 1:16.265; Massa finished with the third-fastest time. The two Red Bull drivers were second and fourth (with Vettel ahead of Webber). All of the top four quickest drivers set their fastest lap times on the super-soft dry compound tyre. Rosberg was fifth-fastest (despite damaging his front wing after he collided with a kerb bollard), ahead of his Mercedes teammate <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> in sixth. Hamilton, Kubica, Barrichello and Hülkenberg followed in the top ten.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> A heavy rain shower hit the circuit on Saturday morning which stopped before the final practice session started. Drivers were initially forced to use intermediate tyres before switching later to dry-weather conditions and the teams worked on car set-up for qualifying. Vettel set the fastest lap, a 1:15.503; Webber finished with the third-fastest time. They were separated by the Ferrari drivers in second and fourth (with Alonso ahead of Massa). Rosberg duplicated his second-session result in fifth. Hamilton, Schumacher, Barrichello, Kubica and Hülkenberg completed the top ten ahead of qualifying. Sutil was unable to record a lap time when his Force India's driveshaft broke though he managed to return to his garage and lost 45 minutes of running.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Vettel_Sebastian_-_Red_Bull_-_2010_-_Hockenheimring.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4e/Vettel_Sebastian_-_Red_Bull_-_2010_-_Hockenheimring.jpg/220px-Vettel_Sebastian_-_Red_Bull_-_2010_-_Hockenheimring.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4e/Vettel_Sebastian_-_Red_Bull_-_2010_-_Hockenheimring.jpg/330px-Vettel_Sebastian_-_Red_Bull_-_2010_-_Hockenheimring.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4e/Vettel_Sebastian_-_Red_Bull_-_2010_-_Hockenheimring.jpg/440px-Vettel_Sebastian_-_Red_Bull_-_2010_-_Hockenheimring.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1515" data-file-height="1137" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Vettel_Sebastian_-_Red_Bull_-_2010_-_Hockenheimring.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> took his sixth pole position of his season.</div></div></div>
<p>Saturday afternoon's qualifying session was divided into three parts. The first part ran for twenty minutes and eliminated the cars that finished eighteenth or lower. The second part of qualifying lasted fifteen minutes and eliminated cars that finished in positions eleven to seventeen. The final session ran for ten minutes and determined <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> to tenth. Cars which competed in the final session were not allowed to change tyres before the race. They started the race fitted with the tyres used when they set their quickest lap times.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The session was held in dry weather conditions but dark clouds brought a risk of further rain showers. With limited dry running in the final practice session grip levels rose as qualifying progressed.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Vettel set the fastest time in the final session which clinched him the sixth pole position of his season (and his third consecutive) with a lap of 1:13.791 which was recorded on his final timed lap. Although he was happy to start on the pole he said his lap was not "100% perfect" as he drove off the circuit after he pushed hard. Vettel was joined on the <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_motorsport_terms#G" title="Glossary of motorsport terms">grid</a>'s front row by Alonso who was 0.002 seconds after Alonso had recorded the fastest times in the first and second sessions. Massa took third despite running wide on his final timed lap but was optimistic on a track where he felt comfortable.<sup id="cite_ref-gpquali_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpquali-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Webber, fourth, ran wide at the Nordkurve right-hand corner on his final lap when he turned in late and cut the kerb which cost him three seconds and he opted to abandon his lap.<sup id="cite_ref-qualiquotes_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualiquotes-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> The two McLaren cars lined up on the grid's third row (with Button ahead of Hamilton). Button was happy with his lap whilst Hamilton said his qualifying pace was poor despite the McLaren drivers recording the fastest speeds on the circuit's main straight. Kubica qualified seventh and felt he could have set a faster lap time as he was held up in the track's final sector. Barrichello in eighth experienced <a href="/wiki/Understeer_and_oversteer" title="Understeer and oversteer">understeer</a> in the final session and lost his car's balance.<sup id="cite_ref-qualiquotes_26-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualiquotes-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Rosberg and Hülkenberg rounded out the top ten qualifiers.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Schumacher was the fastest driver not to advance to the final session, qualifying eleventh; his best time of 1:15.026 was almost two seconds slower than Alonso's fastest time in the second session.<sup id="cite_ref-qualiresults_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualiresults-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> He had been one tenth of a second quicker than his teammate Rosberg in the first session; Rosberg's lap caused Schumacher to be eliminated from qualifying and the latter stated he had brake problems.<sup id="cite_ref-gpquali_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpquali-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-qualiquotes_26-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualiquotes-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Kamui_Kobayashi" title="Kamui Kobayashi">Kamui Kobayashi</a> qualified in 12th was slowed by traffic, and his tyre temperatures dropped which resulted in a loss of grip. He was followed by Petrov in the 13th position. Sutil was afflicted with a lack of grip in his Force India which caused him to slide sideways.<sup id="cite_ref-qualiquotes_26-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualiquotes-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Sutil was penalised five positions because this team changed his gearbox following his driveshaft failure in the final practice session. As a result, de la Rosa inherited 14th, and was ahead of the Toro Rosso drivers (<a href="/wiki/Jaime_Alguersuari" title="Jaime Alguersuari">Jaime Alguersuari</a> in front of Buemi).<sup id="cite_ref-:0_28-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> was the quickest driver who was unable to advance beyond the first session, and was followed by his Lotus teammate <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-qualiresults_29-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualiresults-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Timo_Glock" title="Timo Glock">Timo Glock</a> was penalised five positions on the grid twice because his team changed his gearbox and fitted a seventh-gear ratio that was undeclared on Friday.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Senna took over the 20th place. Liuzzi exited turn 15 to complete his second timed lap, ran over a wet patch of astroturf causing him to lose control of the back-end of his Force India, sending him across the track to make heavy contact with the front of his car. He veered back towards the track and some grass and narrowly avoided collecting Glock.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_28-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Sakon Yamamoto had a shifting issue on his fastest lap time and took 23rd. <a href="/wiki/Lucas_di_Grassi" title="Lucas di Grassi">Lucas di Grassi</a> had a gearbox problem in his car that was unable to be rectified and completed the field.<sup id="cite_ref-qualiquotes_26-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualiquotes-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> He later incurred a five-place grid penalty for the gearbox change but it had no effect on his starting position.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_28-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The race took place in the afternoon from 14:00 <a href="/wiki/Central_European_Summer_Time" title="Central European Summer Time">Central European Summer Time</a> (<a href="/wiki/Coordinated_Universal_Time" title="Coordinated Universal Time">UTC+2</a>). The conditions on the grid were cloudy before the start; there was an air temperature between 21 to 25&#160;°C (70 to 77&#160;°F) with an track temperature ranging from 28 to 35&#160;°C (82 to 95&#160;°F) and no rain was expected.<sup id="cite_ref-f1racing_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1racing-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> The top ten qualifiers started on the super-soft compound tyre.<sup id="cite_ref-:5_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:5-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> As the five red lights went out to signal the start of the race, Vettel released his clutch pedal, found that he had poor acceleration driving off his starting position, but avoided <a href="/wiki/Stall_(engine)" title="Stall (engine)">stalling</a> his engine.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> He drove right towards Alonso in an attempt to put the latter towards the pit lane wall heading into the first corner. Alonso was on the <a href="/wiki/Racing_line" title="Racing line">racing line</a> and overtook Vettel. However, neither driver took the lead as Massa had made a fast start and passed both drivers around the outside.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_31-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-gpracenotes_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpracenotes-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-bbcwebrace_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcwebrace-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> Button avoided hitting the back of Vettel's car by swerving and braking early for the first corner.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_33-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Further round, Alguersuari committed to the braking zone late at the turn six hairpin, drove into the rear of his teammate Buemi, which removed Buemi's rear wing and his own front wing, and debris was littered across the track. Hamilton passed Webber for fourth place on the same lap. At the end of the first lap, the order was: Massa, Alonso, Vettel, Hamilton, Webber, Button, Kubica, Schumacher, Rosberg and Kobayashi.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_31-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Schumacher_hockenheim_2010.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f6/Schumacher_hockenheim_2010.jpg/220px-Schumacher_hockenheim_2010.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f6/Schumacher_hockenheim_2010.jpg/330px-Schumacher_hockenheim_2010.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f6/Schumacher_hockenheim_2010.jpg/440px-Schumacher_hockenheim_2010.jpg 2x" data-file-width="5184" data-file-height="3456" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Schumacher_hockenheim_2010.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> took two points at his home Grand Prix.</div></div></div>
<p>Elsewhere, Force India committed an error when bringing their drivers in early. Liuzzi made contact with Sutil which damaged his front wing. The team had been expecting Liuzzi to pit before Sutil, but the latter was the first to arrive into the team's pit stall. It was only after both drivers had returned to the circuit that the team realised they had accidentally placed Liuzzi's tyres on Sutil's car and Sutil's tyres on Liuzzi's.<sup id="cite_ref-:6_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:6-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Buemi was pushed back into his garage and became the race's first retirement with accident damage on the second lap.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcwebrace_35-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcwebrace-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton was running close behind Vettel but despite his car's straight-line speed advantage, he was unable to affect an overtaking manoeuvre. Hamilton later dropped further away from Vettel.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_31-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Trulli became afflicted with an gearbox issue which meant he was unable to select second gear, and his mechanics pushed his <a href="/wiki/Lotus_T127" title="Lotus T127">Lotus T127</a> back into his garage where they attempted to rectify the issue without success.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_31-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:2_33-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Alonso recorded lap times faster than teammate Massa which reduced the gap to 1.1 seconds by the conclusion of lap six.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcwebrace_35-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcwebrace-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> Trulli was released from his garage by his team but drove slowly around the circuit and elected to retire on the next lap.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_31-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Massa responded to Alonso's quick pace with his fastest lap time to increase the gap by four-tenths of a second. Button ran wide at the first corner and fell further behind sixth-place driver Webber. Hamilton set a slow time which allowed Webber to close the gap to the former. Vettel became the first driver to make a scheduled <a href="/wiki/Pit_stop" title="Pit stop">pit stop</a> for the hard-compound tyres on the 12th lap, and rejoined in sixth in front of Kubica. It was part of a strategy by Red Bull to place him into clear air in the hopes that he could make up some time on the leading Ferrari cars.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_31-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-bbcwebrace_35-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcwebrace-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> Alguersuari drove alongside Yamamoto over the start/finish line and he made a pass for 19th entering the first corner on the same lap.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_31-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Ferrari responded to Red Bull's decision by bringing Alonso and Massa in for their stops in quick succession, handing the lead to Button.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcwebrace_35-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcwebrace-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> Alonso rejoined ahead of Vettel, and was followed by Webber, but he exited the pit lane among a pack of slower cars and was immediately challenged by Kobayashi. Massa began to struggle with tyre temperatures and locked-up at turn two, and lost the balance of his car at the turn six hairpin.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_31-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-bbcwebrace_35-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcwebrace-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Alguersuari overtook Glock for the 18th position on lap 17, and started to reduce the gap between himself and Senna. Button, who was still to make a pit stop, set faster lap times than other cars despite being on a worn set of soft-compound tyres. The battle between the two Ferrari drivers allowed Vettel to quickly close the gap. Alguersuari passed di Grassi to move into 16th place on the 21st lap.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_31-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Alonso and Massa had caught up to slower cars by the same lap, which allowed the former to attempt a pass on the Brazilian by driving on the inside line heading into the turn six hairpin. But Massa held a quicker line leaving the corner which allowed him to defend his position.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcwebrace_35-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcwebrace-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> Alonso attempted to pass again heading into the following turn but backed out of the manoeuvre. He was angry and told his team "This is ridiculous" because he saw Massa placing him in danger of being challenged by both the Red Bull cars; it prompted intense discussion regarding the matter on the Ferrari pit wall.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_31-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Yamamoto pulled over to the side of the track when his engine cut out on lap 19 while battling with both Force India drivers.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_31-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:2_33-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Felipe_Massa_Hockenheim_2010_(cropped).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/90/Felipe_Massa_Hockenheim_2010_%28cropped%29.jpg/220px-Felipe_Massa_Hockenheim_2010_%28cropped%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="112" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/90/Felipe_Massa_Hockenheim_2010_%28cropped%29.jpg/330px-Felipe_Massa_Hockenheim_2010_%28cropped%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/90/Felipe_Massa_Hockenheim_2010_%28cropped%29.jpg/440px-Felipe_Massa_Hockenheim_2010_%28cropped%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2706" data-file-height="1373" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Felipe_Massa_Hockenheim_2010_(cropped).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> controversially let teammate Fernando Alonso past into the lead.</div></div></div>
<p>After passing slower cars, Vettel began to resume his battle with the Ferrari drivers.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_31-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Button made a pit stop on lap 22 and reemerged in fifth position behind his teammate Hamilton. The Ferrari cars began to set faster lap times than the rest of the field as Massa started to pull away from Alonso and held a lead of three seconds over his teammate.<sup id="cite_ref-gpracenotes_34-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpracenotes-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Alonso responded to Massa's newfound pace by recording a new <a href="/wiki/Fastest_lap" title="Fastest lap">fastest lap</a> of the race, a 1:18.075 on lap 28, having backed out to see how fast his car was in <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_motorsport_terms#C" title="Glossary of motorsport terms">clean air</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-bbcwebrace_35-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcwebrace-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> before the two drivers began to trade fastest times over the next three laps. Webber attempted to place pressure on Button in a battle for fifth place,<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_31-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> although Button was using a defensive strategy by using his McLaren's straight-line speed advantage.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcwebrace_35-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcwebrace-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> De La Rosa caught up to Hülkenberg and passed him at the turn six hairpin on lap 35. Three laps later, Hamilton was instructed by McLaren to conserve fuel usage in the event they could use an aggressive fuel saving mode towards the end of the race.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_31-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>By lap 39, Alonso had reduced the time deficit between himself and Massa as the latter was lapping the two Virgin cars.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_31-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> At the start of lap 40, Massa held a 1.2 second lead over Alonso, who in turn, was 5.2 seconds in front of Vettel. Hamilton in fourth place was 18.1 seconds behind, but was maintaining a steady gap to teammate Button in fifth.<sup id="cite_ref-gpracenotes_34-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpracenotes-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Webber was told by Red Bull over the radio to increase the gap to Button, because his car was suffering from an issue with its oil consumption,<sup id="cite_ref-bbcwebrace_35-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcwebrace-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> and was told that braking harder into the track's turns would possibly assist in moving some oil around the inside of his car. Petrov made an error which allowed Kobayashi to take advantage but Petrov held off the challenge.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_31-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> A radio transmission from Massa's <a href="/wiki/Race_engineer" title="Race engineer">race engineer</a> <a href="/wiki/Rob_Smedley" title="Rob Smedley">Rob Smedley</a> was intercepted on lap 48, with Smedley telling Massa, "OK, so, Fernando (Alonso) is faster than you. Can you confirm you understood that message?" Based on Smedley's tone, commentators surmised that it had been a coded message from engineer to driver telling Massa to move over and allow Alonso through.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcwebrace_35-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcwebrace-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Alonso_hockenheim_2010.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5b/Alonso_hockenheim_2010.jpg/220px-Alonso_hockenheim_2010.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="124" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5b/Alonso_hockenheim_2010.jpg/330px-Alonso_hockenheim_2010.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5b/Alonso_hockenheim_2010.jpg/440px-Alonso_hockenheim_2010.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4856" data-file-height="2732" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Alonso_hockenheim_2010.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> took the 23rd victory of his career.</div></div></div>
<p>Massa was slow to accelerate leaving the turn six hairpin on lap 49, giving Alonso the opening he needed to take the lead. Smedley was later heard apologising to Massa over the radio.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcwebrace_35-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcwebrace-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> De La Rosa made his solitary pit stop of the event for super-soft compound tyres on lap 51. Di Grassi, at turn one on the same lap,<sup id="cite_ref-gpracenotes_34-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpracenotes-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> hit a kerb damaging his car's rear suspension, which rendered him unable to continue in the race.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_33-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> He was pushed into his garage by his team to retire. Vettel pressured Massa by setting consecutive fastest laps to reduce the time gap between them. Petrov was on course to score one point but was told by his team to shift early into seventh gear on lap 58 otherwise his maximum <a href="/wiki/Revolutions_per_minute" title="Revolutions per minute">revolutions per minute</a> would be changed to a lower setting.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_31-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>De La Rosa attempted to lap Kovalainen around the inside of the second corner on the 60th lap but the two cars made contact.<sup id="cite_ref-:5_32-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:5-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> De La Rosa sustained damage to a section of his front wing while Kovalainen drove to his garage and vacated his car to become the Grand Prix's final retirement on lap 61.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_31-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Vettel continued to push hard in his effort to pass Massa for second place, while Hamilton was told he was no longer required to conserve fuel on the 63rd lap. Button ran wide at the first corner on the penultimate lap of the race but continued in fifth place. Alonso maintained the lead throughout the remainder of the event and crossed the start/finish line on lap 67 to secure the 23rd victory of his Formula One career. Massa finished second 4.1 seconds behind, and Vettel was third. Hamilton secured fourth, with teammate Button in fifth. Webber reduced his pace to finish in sixth place. Kubica, Rosberg, Schumacher and Petrov rounded out the points-scoring positions. Kobayashi, Barrichello, Hülkenberg, De La Rosa, Alguersuari, Liuzzi, Sutil, Glock and Senna were the final classified finishers.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_31-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The top three drivers appeared on the podium to collect their trophies and spoke to the media at a later press conference.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Alonso described his race weekend as "good overall" and felt the one-two finish for Ferrari was a deserving and strong result. He said he felt that the team's motivation would drop in the event of poor finishes and stated that Ferrari would get overexcited after finishing strongly and did not see a reason to be pessimistic for the next race of the season. Massa said it was a "very good" race for his team and that his tyres behaved better than he had expected. He felt he was not in the battle for the championship because he had lost a large number of valuable points and said every person recognised that he was able to win races and be competitive. Vettel said that despite losing two positions at the start of the race, he felt comfortable in his car and was happy to clinch a podium position at his home Grand Prix. Although he said he would have preferred to have scored more points, he felt third place was the best result his team could secure, a finish that Red Bull could be proud of.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Fourth-place finisher Hamilton said it had been a tough race for him because his car did not feel as good as he hoped. He had also hoped the gap would be closer than it was in qualifying. Webber stated that he enjoyed the first part of the event and was looking forward to the next race of the season.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_33-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Kovalainen was issued with an reprimand for his role in the collision with de la Rosa. He admitted that he was at fault for causing the incident because he was allowing another car to pass him and had not seen de la Rosa approaching him.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> Force India's chief operating officer <a href="/wiki/Otmar_Szafnauer" title="Otmar Szafnauer">Otmar Szafnauer</a> said the confusion resulting in the error of fitting the wrong tyres for both their drivers was an example of cause and effect. He wanted to disregard the issue and look forward to the next few races.<sup id="cite_ref-:6_36-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:6-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> The team received a warning from the stewards and avoided disqualification because they had recognised their mistake immediately.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Ferrari's use of <a href="/wiki/Team_orders" title="Team orders">team orders</a>, which resulted in Massa relinquishing the lead to Alonso on lap 49, risked the team's exclusion from the race results.<sup id="cite_ref-:3_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> Team principal <a href="/wiki/Stefano_Domenicali" title="Stefano Domenicali">Stefano Domenicali</a>, Ferrari manager <a href="/w/index.php?title=Massimo_Rivola&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Massimo Rivola (page does not exist)">Massimo Rivola</a>, and Alonso and Massa were summoned by the stewards because they were considered have transgressed Article 39.1 of the 2010 <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile</a> (FIA) Sporting Regulations, and were charged with violating Article 151c of the FIA's sporting code.<sup id="cite_ref-:4_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> The team was issued a $100,000 (£64,700) fine for the rule infringements,<sup id="cite_ref-:3_40-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> and the stewards decided that the result of the event would be unchanged.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> Ferrari was referred to the <a href="/wiki/FIA_World_Motor_Sport_Council" title="FIA World Motor Sport Council">FIA World Motor Sport Council</a> for further deliberation.<sup id="cite_ref-:4_41-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> The team was heavily criticised by the Formula One paddock. Red Bull team principal <a href="/wiki/Christian_Horner" title="Christian Horner">Christian Horner</a> called it "the clearest team order I've ever seen."<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/BBC_Sport" title="BBC Sport">BBC</a> pundit and former team owner <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Jordan" title="Eddie Jordan">Eddie Jordan</a> was more vocal stating the orders were "unlawful", felt it was "theft" and prevented the chance of having a race-winning battle between the two Ferrari drivers. He felt the two cars should have been disqualified.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher argued that Ferrari were concentrating on securing the Drivers' Championship.<sup id="cite_ref-:7_45-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:7-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Domenicali insisted there were no team orders and that it was Massa who chose to allow Alonso past. Alonso reaffirmed Domenicalli's statement and admitted he was surprised when Massa slowed and claimed that he felt Massa had a gearbox issue.<sup id="cite_ref-:7_45-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:7-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> FIA reporter <a href="/w/index.php?title=Lars_Osterlind&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Lars Osterlind (page does not exist)">Lars Osterlind</a> investigated the incident and sent a 160-page report to the World Motor Sport Council which concluded that Alonso should be given a five-second time penalty which would hand the victory to Massa. He also wanted a suspended penalty on the loss of Ferrari's Drivers' and Constructors' points scored in the event.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> At the World Motor Sport Council meeting held in Paris on 8 September, the tribunal elected to uphold the $100,000 fine imposed on Ferrari, decided that no further action would be taken against the team, and that Formula One's Sporting Working Group would review the rule that prohibited team orders.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> In December, the FIA announced a reversion on the rule barring team orders because it was difficult to enforce.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> Parallels were made between the race and the <a href="/wiki/2002_Austrian_Grand_Prix" title="2002 Austrian Grand Prix">2002 Austrian Grand Prix</a> where Barrichello was ordered by Ferrari to concede the event's victory to Schumacher on its final lap.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The result extended Hamilton's lead in the Drivers' Championship to 14 points over Button. Vettel's third-place finish tied him with Webber in third place on 136 points, and Alonso's victory put him within 13 points of the Red Bull drivers.<sup id="cite_ref-points_7-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> McLaren maintained their lead in the Constructors' Championship although Red Bull's finish of third and sixth meant they reduced the gap by one point. Ferrari's one-two result allowed them to reduce the points deficit to Red Bull slightly. Mercedes and Renault remained in fourth and fifth positions, with eight races left in the season.<sup id="cite_ref-points_7-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
