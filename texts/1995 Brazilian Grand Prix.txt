<p>The <b>1995 Brazilian Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>XXIV Grande Prêmio do Brasil</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 26 March 1995 at the <a href="/wiki/Aut%C3%B3dromo_Jos%C3%A9_Carlos_Pace" title="Autódromo José Carlos Pace">Autódromo José Carlos Pace</a>, <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo" title="São Paulo">São Paulo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>. It was the first&#160;round of the <a href="/wiki/1995_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="1995 Formula One season">1995 Formula One season</a>. The 71-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a> team after starting from second position. <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> finished second in a <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> third in a <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>. <a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a>, who started the race from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>, spun out while leading on lap&#160;30 with an apparent <a href="/wiki/Transmission_(mechanics)" title="Transmission (mechanics)">gearbox</a> problem, which was later found to be a <a href="/wiki/Suspension_(vehicle)" class="mw-redirect" title="Suspension (vehicle)">suspension</a> failure. Schumacher's win came despite Benetton encountering steering problems with his car during Friday practice, leading to him crashing heavily and necessitating steering component changes for the rest of the event. Despite Schumacher's victory, Hill proved to be faster during the race, and seemed to be on course for a comfortable victory before his sudden retirement.
</p><p>Other notable performances came from Berger, who took the final podium position despite being delayed during one of his routine pit stops due to a problem with a loose wheel nut; <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a>, who finished fourth for the <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> team despite its new car proving to be uncompetitive in pre-season testing; and <a href="/wiki/Mika_Salo" title="Mika Salo">Mika Salo</a>, who drove strongly in the first half of the race to run third in his first Grand Prix for the <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_Racing" title="Tyrrell Racing">Tyrrell</a> team, only to suffer from a cramp and drop back to seventh place at the finish. Behind Häkkinen, the other points-scoring finishers were <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a> in the second Ferrari and <a href="/wiki/Mark_Blundell" title="Mark Blundell">Mark Blundell</a>, who drove the second McLaren. Blundell was standing in for regular driver <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> in the second McLaren until the team could produce a wider chassis in which to accommodate him, as the car's initial cockpit design had proved to be too narrow for him to drive comfortably.
</p><p>Several hours after the conclusion of the race, Schumacher and Coulthard were <a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/excluded" class="extiw" title="wikt:excluded">excluded</a> from the race result as the chemical "fingerprint" of fuel samples from their cars taken after qualifying and the race did not match the specified sample lodged with the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile</a> (FIA) prior to the event. Berger was thus declared the winner, but the race stewards' decision to exclude them was overturned at an appeal hearing on April 13. Schumacher and Coulthard were reinstated in first and second places respectively, but the two teams did not receive their respective <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">constructors' points</a>. The Ferrari team was unhappy with the decision made at the appeal hearing; Berger called the sport "a joke". The rule concerning the legality of fuels had been changed for the 1995 season, as had the new standardised equipment used for refuelling during the race, the drivers' weighing-in procedure and the conditions of the drivers' <a href="/wiki/FIA_Super_Licence" title="FIA Super Licence">racing licences</a>; all of these changes produced controversies which at times threatened to overshadow the race, as did the excessively bumpy condition of the track. The race also marked the first Brazilian Grand Prix to take place since the death of Brazilian triple World Champion <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> the previous year; his passing was commemorated in various ways throughout the event.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1995_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1995_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/1995_Formula_One_season#Background" class="mw-redirect" title="1995 Formula One season">1995 Formula One season §&#160;Background</a></div>
<p>The race was the first&#160;round of the <a href="/wiki/1995_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="1995 Formula One season">1995 Formula One season</a> after a four&#160;month break since the last race of the <a href="/wiki/1994_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="1994 Formula One season">1994 season</a> in <a href="/wiki/1994_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="1994 Australian Grand Prix">Australia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ms-hilton-pg157-61_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ms-hilton-pg157-61-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Over the winter months, the <a href="/wiki/Aut%C3%B3dromo_Jos%C3%A9_Carlos_Pace" title="Autódromo José Carlos Pace">Autódromo José Carlos Pace</a> race track was <a href="/wiki/Road_surface#Asphalt_paving" title="Road surface">resurfaced</a> in an attempt to reduce its bumpiness. The drivers were unhappy with the resurfacing work, though, as the track became more bumpy than in the <a href="/wiki/1994_Brazilian_Grand_Prix" title="1994 Brazilian Grand Prix">previous year</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-bbc-1_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>  <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a> driver <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> described the track as "bumpy as hell, quite unbelievable".<sup id="cite_ref-pg87_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg87-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>  He was also concerned about his stamina for the duration of the 71-lap race, as he had suffered from a bout of <a href="/wiki/Tonsillitis" title="Tonsillitis">tonsillitis</a> in the run-up to the event which had disrupted his physical training.<sup id="cite_ref-pg87_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg87-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Local <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> was even more vocal in his criticism of the track's condition, declaring that the circuit organisers were "a bunch of idiots.  The track is three times bumpier than before.  It is not by applying layer upon layer of tarmac that they will improve matters."<sup id="cite_ref-F1Ypg83_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1Ypg83-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a> claimed that the bumping was so bad that he was close to passing out; the <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a> car handled badly over the bumps throughout the weekend.<sup id="cite_ref-F1Ypg83_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1Ypg83-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The race marked the first Brazilian Grand Prix to be held since the death of three-time champion <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> the previous year in a racing accident at the <a href="/wiki/1994_San_Marino_Grand_Prix" title="1994 San Marino Grand Prix">1994 San Marino Grand Prix</a>.  As such, various tributes were paid to him throughout the weekend, including a parade lap on the back of a truck with all the drivers waving Brazilian flags; a display from the <a href="/wiki/Brazilian_Air_Force" title="Brazilian Air Force">Brazilian Air Force</a> which resulted in a large "S" trail in the sky; and the renaming of a local highway to <i><a href="/wiki/Brazilian_Highway_System" title="Brazilian Highway System">Rodovia</a> Ayrton Senna</i>.  The São Paulo city authorities had planned to rename the circuit after Senna, but the family of <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Carlos_Pace" class="mw-redirect" title="José Carlos Pace">Carlos Pace</a>, the existing dedicatee, objected.<sup id="cite_ref-F1Ypg77_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1Ypg77-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello also sported a special helmet design as a personal tribute to his compatriot.<sup id="cite_ref-review_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-review-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> As a result of Senna's death, the event was lower-key than in recent years: fewer spectators turned out to watch practice and <a href="/wiki/List_of_motorsport_terminology#Q" class="mw-redirect" title="List of motorsport terminology">qualifying</a>, but the race itself was watched by a capacity crowd.<sup id="cite_ref-postcard_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-postcard-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The threat of a drivers' boycott over the terms of their 1995 FIA <a href="/wiki/FIA_Super_Licence" title="FIA Super Licence">Super Licences</a>, which allowed the FIA to demand promotional appearances and forbade the drivers from criticising the championship, was defused by the governing body prior to the race, ensuring full driver participation in the championship.<sup id="cite_ref-F1Ypg83_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1Ypg83-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-pg90_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg90-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Although the Super License issue was resolved with 14&#160;teams and 28&#160;drivers on the official 1995 entry list, the <a href="/wiki/Larrousse" title="Larrousse">Larrousse</a> team with drivers <a href="/wiki/%C3%89ric_Bernard" title="Éric Bernard">Éric Bernard</a> and <a href="/wiki/Christophe_Bouchut" title="Christophe Bouchut">Christophe Bouchut</a> did not make an appearance at the circuit for any of the on-track sessions.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-bbc-larrousse_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-larrousse-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> This was due to the team running short of money: in the period prior to the event, with French government aid not forthcoming and a 1995 chassis not yet built, team owner <a href="/wiki/G%C3%A9rard_Larrousse" title="Gérard Larrousse">Gérard Larrousse</a> elected to miss the first two rounds of the season in the hope of competing from the <a href="/wiki/1995_San_Marino_Grand_Prix" title="1995 San Marino Grand Prix">San Marino Grand Prix</a> onwards.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>The construction of some of the cars was only just completed prior to the beginning of the season; the <a href="/wiki/Footwork_FA16" title="Footwork FA16">Footwork FA16</a> and <a href="/wiki/Simtek_S951" title="Simtek S951">Simtek S951</a> chassis arrived at the event with virtually no testing, having been completed shortly beforehand.<sup id="cite_ref-pg90_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg90-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-pg88_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg88-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Of the initial 1995 drivers, <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Diniz" title="Pedro Diniz">Pedro Diniz</a> was the only "complete" <a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/rookie" class="extiw" title="wikt:rookie">rookie</a>, having not been officially entered in any other <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_races" class="mw-redirect" title="List of Formula One races">Formula One race meetings</a>, while <a href="/wiki/Andrea_Montermini" title="Andrea Montermini">Andrea Montermini</a> started his first race after failing to qualify for the <a href="/wiki/1994_Spanish_Grand_Prix" title="1994 Spanish Grand Prix">1994 Spanish Grand Prix</a> due to injury. <a href="/wiki/Mika_Salo" title="Mika Salo">Mika Salo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Domenico_Schiattarella" title="Domenico Schiattarella">Domenico Schiattarella</a> had competed in two races, with <a href="/wiki/Taki_Inoue" title="Taki Inoue">Taki Inoue</a> competing in one race the previous season.
</p><p>At the front of the field, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> and <a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a> and Williams cars respectively were the favourites to battle for the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a>, with Schumacher anticipating a "struggle" for the championship.<sup id="cite_ref-ms-hilton-pg157-61_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ms-hilton-pg157-61-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Bernard_Dudot" title="Bernard Dudot">Bernard Dudot</a>, <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault Sport's</a> Chief Engineer, said that he believed Benetton was less well-prepared than Williams, as the former team had changed its engine supplier to Renault, whereas Williams had been in partnership with the company since <a href="/wiki/1989_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1989 Formula One World Championship">1989</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-F1Ypg76_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1Ypg76-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Hill arrived at the event with the psychological advantage of having set the fastest time at the final pre-season testing session at the <a href="/wiki/Aut%C3%B3dromo_do_Estoril" class="mw-redirect" title="Autódromo do Estoril">Autódromo do Estoril</a>, 0.35 seconds faster than Schumacher's best around the circuit. The Williams team had also completed 2,500 miles of testing with its <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW17" title="Williams FW17">FW17</a> chassis, significantly more than Benetton; the team had encountered some reliability problems with its own <a href="/wiki/Benetton_B195" title="Benetton B195">B195</a> chassis.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Heading into the new season, attention also focused on the McLaren team and its driver <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a>. He was initially announced as the partner of <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a>; however, a week before the first race, McLaren announced that Mansell would not compete in the first two races of the new season, as he could not fit into the <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/10" title="McLaren MP4/10">MP4/10</a> car.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix-mansellmiss_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-mansellmiss-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Mansell's arrival at McLaren was due to demand from the team's sponsors, including <a href="/wiki/Philip_Morris_USA" title="Philip Morris USA">Philip Morris</a>, whose brand, <a href="/wiki/Marlboro_(cigarette)" title="Marlboro (cigarette)">Marlboro</a>, was on the car, who wanted to enjoy the publicity associated with the <a href="/wiki/1992_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1992 Formula One World Championship">1992</a> World Champion's return even if it meant him performing worse than teammate Häkkinen.<sup id="cite_ref-autosport-mansell1995_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosport-mansell1995-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> His place at McLaren for the first two races was taken by the team's <a href="/wiki/Test_driver" title="Test driver">test driver</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mark_Blundell" title="Mark Blundell">Mark Blundell</a>, as McLaren worked to build a wider <a href="/wiki/Monocoque" title="Monocoque">monocoque</a> to accommodate Mansell.<sup id="cite_ref-pg88_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg88-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-grandprix-mansellmiss_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-mansellmiss-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> McLaren were also concerned about the standard refuelling equipment provided for 1995 by suppliers <a href="/wiki/Intertechnique" class="mw-redirect" title="Intertechnique">Intertechnique</a>, having suffered a major leak in a test of the new rig outside of its factory. Intertechnique had redesigned the fuel equipment, which was used by all of the teams, in the wake of a <a href="/wiki/Pit_stop" title="Pit stop">pit lane</a> fire suffered by driver <a href="/wiki/Jos_Verstappen" title="Jos Verstappen">Jos Verstappen</a> during the previous year's <a href="/wiki/1994_German_Grand_Prix" title="1994 German Grand Prix">German Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pg90_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg90-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The new fuel rigs, in addition to being half the size of the 1994, also featured longer nozzles, and were designed to lock onto the car before any fuel could begin to flow.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix-newfuelrig_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-newfuelrig-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Intertechnique traced the problem to a faulty valve within the equipment, which caused 10 kilograms (22&#160;lb) of fuel to leak, and modified the parts accordingly.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix-intertechniqueresponse_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-intertechniqueresponse-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Intertechnique and the FIA advised the teams to refuel the cars slowly and carefully during pitstops to avoid any further leaks, a situation that was considered unsatisfactory by many of the team principals and mechanics.<sup id="cite_ref-TP_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TP-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> A new regulation for 1995 also mandated the teams' fuel suppliers to send samples of their fuel to the FIA prior to each Grand Prix, which the governing body would use as a reference when conducting fuel tests at the event itself. Thus only fuel approved by the FIA in advance was allowed; any deviations from the samples on file was not permitted. As before, all cars were subject to routine fuel tests as part of the scrutineering process throughout the weekend to ensure compliance with the sport's technical regulations.<sup id="cite_ref-autosport-anger_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosport-anger-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> The rule change had been proposed by the <a href="/wiki/Elf_Aquitaine" title="Elf Aquitaine">Elf</a> oil company in 1993 and unanimously supported by the other F1 suppliers in order to speed up the fuel-testing process.<sup id="cite_ref-fia-exclusion_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fia-exclusion-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Controversy also surrounded the new <a href="/wiki/Ligier_JS41" title="Ligier JS41">Ligier JS41</a> car, with rival team owners comparing it to the Benetton B195 car because of their similar design, the only apparent difference being the engine in each car.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix-ligiercontroversy_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-ligiercontroversy-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> The Ligier team was being run in 1995 by <a href="/wiki/Tom_Walkinshaw" title="Tom Walkinshaw">Tom Walkinshaw</a>, who had been Benetton's Engineering Director the previous season.<sup id="cite_ref-pg87_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg87-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Commenting on the design similarities, Walkinshaw said:
</p>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r960796168">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>Mechanically it [the JS41] is totally different [from the B195] and structurally it is quite different as well.  Aerodynamically, it's as close as we can make it to being the same.  I don't know how you would end up with anything else if you take a core of engineers who have been working on the Benetton.  Of course the damn thing looks the same.  But if you go into the detail of the car, there is nothing interchangeable.<sup id="cite_ref-pg74_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg74-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>
<p>One of the rule revisions stated that the minimum weight limit of 595 kilograms (1,312&#160;lb) applied to both car and driver together. Prior to the first session of the season, all of the drivers were weighed to establish a reference weight to be used on occasions when the two were weighed separately, or if the driver was unavailable to be weighed. As such, a small competitive advantage could be established if the driver attempted to register a weight as heavy as possible, so their actual weight when driving the car would be lower.<sup id="cite_ref-pg87_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg87-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Williams Technical Director <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Head" title="Patrick Head">Patrick Head</a> estimated that a weight penalty of 7 kilograms (15&#160;lb) could cost 14&#160;seconds over the course of 70&#160;laps of the circuit.<sup id="cite_ref-pg33_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg33-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> In the drivers' weigh-in, Schumacher weighed in at 77 kilograms (170&#160;lb), compared to 69 kilograms (152&#160;lb) at the beginning of the 1994 season.<sup id="cite_ref-ms-hilton-pg157-61_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ms-hilton-pg157-61-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Sauber driver <a href="/wiki/Karl_Wendlinger" title="Karl Wendlinger">Karl Wendlinger</a> gained the most weight compared to 1994, gaining 22 pounds (10.0&#160;kg). Wendlinger's teammate Frentzen and <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_Racing" title="Tyrrell Racing">Tyrrell</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Ukyo_Katayama" title="Ukyo Katayama">Ukyo Katayama</a> added the least amount of weight, gaining 3 pounds (1.4&#160;kg).<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix-weight_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-weight-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>  When Schumacher was weighed after the race, his weight had decreased to 71.5 kilograms (158&#160;lb), although this weight, when combined with that of his car, still left it above the limit, at 599 kilograms (1,321&#160;lb).<sup id="cite_ref-pg87_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg87-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>  Schumacher explained the weight gain as a consequence of a fitness regime over the winter that had converted excess fat to muscle, and also admitted to eating and drinking heavily, in addition to refraining from using the toilet, prior to the weigh-in.<sup id="cite_ref-pg87_4-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg87-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-pg33_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg33-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice_and_qualifying">Practice and qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1995_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Practice and qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Two practice sessions were held before the race; the first was held on Friday morning, with the second held on Saturday morning. Both were held in damp conditions due to overnight rain with the track drying towards the conclusion of the respective sessions.<sup id="cite_ref-FP1-eurosport_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FP1-eurosport-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> Both sessions lasted 1&#160;hour and 45&#160;minutes.<sup id="cite_ref-F1Ypg216-217_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1Ypg216-217-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Qualifying was split into two one-hour sessions; the first was held on Friday afternoon with the second held on Saturday afternoon. The fastest time from either of the two qualifying sessions counted towards their final <a href="/wiki/Standing_start" title="Standing start">grid position</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-quali2_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-quali2-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the first&#160;practice session, Hill was fastest with a time of 1:21.664, two&#160;thousands of a second ahead of teammate Coulthard. Schumacher was third, with the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> cars of <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a>, who had been the leading two for most of the session, close behind.<sup id="cite_ref-FP1-eurosport_27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FP1-eurosport-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> Blundell in the McLaren was sixth, with teammate Häkkinen finishing the session in 14th position, the Finnish driver complaining of <a href="/wiki/Steering" title="Steering">steering</a> problems with the car.<sup id="cite_ref-pg92-93_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg92-93-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FP1-hakkinensteering-eurosport_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FP1-hakkinensteering-eurosport-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> The two Ferrari drivers used different pedal layouts in the <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_412_T2" title="Ferrari 412 T2">412T2</a> chassis: Berger's car was equipped with two pedals and a hand-operated clutch, whereas Alesi preferred the traditional three-pedal system.<sup id="cite_ref-pg88_13-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg88-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatright" style="width:40%;&#32;;"><style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r887775652">.mw-parser-output .quotebox{background-color:#F9F9F9;border:1px solid #aaa;box-sizing:border-box;padding:10px;font-size:88%;max-width:100%}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft{margin:0.5em 1.4em 0.8em 0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright{margin:0.5em 0 0.8em 1.4em}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.centered{margin:0.5em auto 0.8em auto}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft p,.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright p{font-style:inherit}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-title{background-color:#F9F9F9;text-align:center;font-size:larger;font-weight:bold}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:before{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" “ ";vertical-align:-45%;line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:after{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" ” ";line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .left-aligned{text-align:left}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .right-aligned{text-align:right}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .center-aligned{text-align:center}.mw-parser-output .quotebox cite{display:block;font-style:normal}@media screen and (max-width:360px){.mw-parser-output .quotebox{min-width:100%;margin:0 0 0.8em!important;float:none!important}}</style>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">"Michael's done a good job to show that he can get over setbacks like that and I've learnt from last year never to underestimate the guy so, the only thing I could say is with reference to the grid is that at least we've got him surrounded as far as the British are concerned, we've got two behind him, one alongside and one ahead."</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style=""><a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a>, commenting on Schumacher's recovery after his Friday crash.<sup id="cite_ref-bbc-2_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-2-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>Hill set a provisional <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> time of 1:20.081 during the first qualifying session, ahead of Berger, Coulthard, Alesi, Häkkinen and Schumacher.<sup id="cite_ref-pg92-93_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg92-93-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The session was disrupted after Schumacher crashed heavily into the tyre barrier at turn&#160;eight, a corner normally taken at a speed of 140 miles per hour (230&#160;km/h).<sup id="cite_ref-bbc-riderschumi_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-riderschumi-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-pg85_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg85-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> As a precaution, Benetton opted to keep Schumacher's teammate, <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Herbert" title="Johnny Herbert">Johnny Herbert</a>, in the garage while the team found out what was wrong with the car. This meant that Herbert did not set a time in the session, while Schumacher completed only two of his twelve allotted laps.<sup id="cite_ref-pg92-93_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg92-93-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Speaking of the incident, Schumacher said that "there was a little movement in the steering wheel at the previous corner and at the next it went completely. I spun the car backwards into the tyre barrier and the safety structure behind my head did a good job of protecting me. For sure I am not very happy about this, but I believe that the team will spend a lot of time investigating the situation for tomorrow and they will find the problem."<sup id="cite_ref-pg85_33-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg85-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> The problem was determined to be a steering failure, specifically a joint in the car's <a href="/wiki/Steering_column" title="Steering column">steering column</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-bbc-riderschumi_32-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-riderschumi-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-pg85_33-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg85-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Replacement steering components were sourced from São Paulo by Benetton engineer <a href="/wiki/Tim_Wright_(engineer)" title="Tim Wright (engineer)">Tim Wright</a>, and the team did not encounter any problems over the remainder of the weekend.<sup id="cite_ref-pg85_33-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg85-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Gerhard_Berger_1991USA.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Gerhard_Berger_1991USA.jpg/220px-Gerhard_Berger_1991USA.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="322" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Gerhard_Berger_1991USA.jpg/330px-Gerhard_Berger_1991USA.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Gerhard_Berger_1991USA.jpg/440px-Gerhard_Berger_1991USA.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2888" data-file-height="4224" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Gerhard_Berger_1991USA.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a>, who was the leading <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> driver on the starting grid (picture taken in <a href="/wiki/1991_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1991 Formula One World Championship">1991</a>, while driving for <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>)</div></div></div>
<p>The second practice session was held in wet but drying weather conditions.<sup id="cite_ref-pg92-93_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg92-93-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> During the session, Schumacher went off the track at the exit of turn&#160;five, damaging his car's front wing in the process.<sup id="cite_ref-bbc-riderschumi_32-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-riderschumi-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-grandprix-brazil_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-brazil-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> However, he still set the fastest time of the session (1:23.607)<sup id="cite_ref-pg92-93_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg92-93-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> after a change to his car's <a href="/wiki/Ride_height" title="Ride height">ride height</a> proved beneficial to its performance.<sup id="cite_ref-pg87_4-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg87-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The top six was completed by Berger, Hill, Häkkinen, Alesi, and <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Panis" title="Olivier Panis">Olivier Panis</a>' Ligier.<sup id="cite_ref-pg92-93_1-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg92-93-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Schumacher also set the fastest time in the second qualifying session with a lap of 1:20.382, but it was not good enough to beat Hill's fastest time overall set in the first session.<sup id="cite_ref-pg92-93_1-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg92-93-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ms-hilton-pg157-61_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ms-hilton-pg157-61-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Hill made a set-up change that he turned out not to be happy with, but maintained his pole position, his first since the <a href="/wiki/1994_British_Grand_Prix" title="1994 British Grand Prix">1994 British Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pg88_13-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg88-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> The Williams and Benetton formation continued on the second row, as Coulthard was third, with Herbert qualifying fourth after a late <a href="/wiki/List_of_motorsport_terminology#F" class="mw-redirect" title="List of motorsport terminology">flying lap</a>, equalling his best-ever qualifying position, achieved at the <a href="/wiki/1994_Italian_Grand_Prix" title="1994 Italian Grand Prix">1994 Italian Grand Prix</a> for the Lotus team.<sup id="cite_ref-bbc-walkerherbert_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-walkerherbert-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> However, a hole was punched in the bottom of Herbert's monocoque chassis during the session as a result of damage, leading to a night of repairs in order for him to be able to take the start in his designated race car.<sup id="cite_ref-pg88_13-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg88-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> The work required to repair both Benetton cars caused Schumacher's race engineer, <a href="/wiki/Pat_Symonds" title="Pat Symonds">Pat Symonds</a>, to describe the weekend as "one of the hardest [...] I can remember for many years".<sup id="cite_ref-R&amp;R_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-R&amp;R-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>  It also meant that three British drivers started in the top four, leading Hill to joke that "the English [sic] have got Michael surrounded".<sup id="cite_ref-pg87_4-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg87-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Behind the leading four, the two Ferrari cars were fifth and sixth, Berger qualifying in front of Alesi. Both were optimistic on their race prospects, despite the fact that the former suffered an engine failure during the session.<sup id="cite_ref-pg88_13-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg88-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> The two McLaren cars of Häkkinen and Blundell were seventh and ninth, the two split by <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a> in a Jordan car.<sup id="cite_ref-pg92-93_1-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg92-93-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Irvine set his time despite spinning off during the session; his teammate Barrichello suffered the season's first engine failure during a timed session on Friday and then <a href="/wiki/Transmission_(mechanics)" title="Transmission (mechanics)">gearbox</a> problems on Saturday, before also spinning off during that day's qualifying session, restricting him to a disappointing 16th&#160;position on the grid at his home race.<sup id="cite_ref-pg88_13-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg88-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Katayama and Salo proved evenly-matched for Tyrrell, qualifying in 11th and 12th respectively, behind Panis. The <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a> drivers were also close, with <a href="/wiki/Pierluigi_Martini" title="Pierluigi Martini">Pierluigi Martini</a> and <a href="/wiki/Luca_Badoer" title="Luca Badoer">Luca Badoer</a> taking 17th and 18th&#160;positions on the grid respectively, separated from the Tyrrell drivers by <a href="/wiki/Gianni_Morbidelli" title="Gianni Morbidelli">Gianni Morbidelli</a> (<a href="/wiki/Footwork_Arrows" title="Footwork Arrows">Footwork</a>), Frentzen, <a href="/wiki/Aguri_Suzuki" title="Aguri Suzuki">Aguri Suzuki</a> (Ligier) and Barrichello. Wendlinger took 19th&#160;position, the Sauber cars handling particularly badly on the bumpy track,<sup id="cite_ref-pg90_9-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg90-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> ahead of <a href="/wiki/Bertrand_Gachot" title="Bertrand Gachot">Bertrand Gachot</a> and Montermini (both <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Racing" title="Pacific Racing">Pacific</a> drivers), who sandwiched Inoue in the second Footwork. At the back of the grid, the two <a href="/wiki/Forti" title="Forti">Forti</a> and <a href="/wiki/Simtek" title="Simtek">Simtek</a> cars covered positions 23&#160;to&#160;26.<sup id="cite_ref-pg92-93_1-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg92-93-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Schiattarella, in a Simtek, was last out of the four positions, having caused one of the two <a href="/wiki/Racing_flags#Red_flag" title="Racing flags">red flags</a> in qualifying, crashing on the exit of turn&#160;three at the beginning of the second qualifying session.<sup id="cite_ref-quali2-2_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-quali2-2-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> The Simtek team's testing time was limited on Friday as the mandatory onboard <a href="/wiki/Fire_extinguisher" title="Fire extinguisher">fire extinguishers</a> for its cars failed to arrive in time; a similar problem with the late arrival of parts affecting Martini's Minardi.<sup id="cite_ref-R&amp;R_36-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-R&amp;R-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> The times in the second session were generally quicker, with only Hill, Badoer and Wendlinger setting their quickest lap times during the first session.<sup id="cite_ref-pg92-93_1-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg92-93-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1995_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The conditions for the race were dry, with a capacity crowd of some 40,000 fans turning out to watch the race.<sup id="cite_ref-pg87_4-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg87-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The drivers took to the track on Sunday morning for a 30-minute warm-up session. Both Williams cars maintained their good performance from qualifying; Coulthard was fastest with teammate Hill in second. Schumacher completed the top three positions.<sup id="cite_ref-pg92-93_1-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg92-93-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Herbert and Katayama both suffered from gearbox problems with their race cars, and elected to start the race with their teams' spare cars.<sup id="cite_ref-R&amp;R_36-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-R&amp;R-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> The Simtek team did not take part in the session, as both drivers waited for stronger suspension components flown out from the United Kingdom to be fitted to the cars for the race.<sup id="cite_ref-ASpg32_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ASpg32-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> Minutes before the race started, a <a href="/wiki/News_release" class="mw-redirect" title="News release">press release</a> from the FIA was given to all media and team personnel, notifying them that the <a href="/wiki/Chromatography" title="Chromatography">chromatographic</a> analysis of the Elf fuel samples taken from Schumacher's Benetton and the Coulthard's Williams during qualifying did not match the sample provided by the fuel company before the start of the season for reference purposes, thus rendering both cars illegal.<sup id="cite_ref-pg90_9-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg90-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-autosport-anger_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosport-anger-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Both teams were fined <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">$</a>30,000 and raced under <a href="/wiki/Appeal" title="Appeal">appeal</a>, as the standard penalty for the offence was disqualification from the event.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix-weight_26-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-weight-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-bbc-fuel_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-fuel-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> Neither Schumacher nor Coulthard were informed of the situation, as the teams did not want to unsettle their drivers before the race start.<sup id="cite_ref-pg90_9-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg90-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Hill and Herbert's races were not affected by the judgement, despite using the same type of fuel.<sup id="cite_ref-TP_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TP-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Samples from Ferrari's <a href="/wiki/Agip" title="Agip">Agip</a> and McLaren's <a href="/wiki/Mobil" title="Mobil">Mobil</a> fuel were also tested; both were in accordance with the regulations.<sup id="cite_ref-pg90_9-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg90-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Though 26&#160;cars qualified for the race, only 25 took the start: Martini retired on the <a href="/wiki/Parade_lap" title="Parade lap">parade lap</a>, his Minardi car suffering a gearbox problem.<sup id="cite_ref-pg92-93_1-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg92-93-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-bbc-martini_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-martini-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:JohnnyHerbert2006.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/56/JohnnyHerbert2006.JPG/220px-JohnnyHerbert2006.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="402" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/5/56/JohnnyHerbert2006.JPG 1.5x" data-file-width="280" data-file-height="511" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:JohnnyHerbert2006.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Johnny_Herbert" title="Johnny Herbert">Johnny Herbert</a>, who dropped to tenth&#160;place in the opening laps of the Grand Prix (picture taken in <a href="/wiki/2006_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2006 Formula One World Championship">2006</a> while an employee of the <a href="/wiki/Midland_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Midland F1">Midland F1</a> team)</div></div></div>
<p>Hill had a bad start from pole position, allowing Schumacher to overtake him into turn&#160;one.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix-brazil_34-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-brazil-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Panis, who started from tenth&#160;position in a Ligier, spun at the first&#160;corner after a nudge from Katayama, hitting the wall in the process and retiring from the race.<sup id="cite_ref-pg91_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg91-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> Herbert, racing the spare Benetton chassis, dropped three places by the end of lap&#160;one, dropping behind Häkkinen and the two Ferrari cars.<sup id="cite_ref-pg92-93_1-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg92-93-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-pg91_42-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg91-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> Blundell also made a poor start, dropping to fourteenth position after his gearbox changed from first to third gear as he accelerated away from the grid.<sup id="cite_ref-pg91_42-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg91-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> At the end of the first lap, the race order was Schumacher leading Hill, from Coulthard, Häkkinen, Berger, Alesi, Herbert, Irvine, Katayama and Salo, with the two Tyrrell drivers switching places on the next lap.<sup id="cite_ref-pg92-93_1-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg92-93-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> At the start of lap&#160;three, Hill attempted to overtake Schumacher heading into turn&#160;five, but Schumacher closed the door on Hill. Hill lost the advantage for a moment, with teammate Coulthard drawing alongside, but unable to get past with Hill on the <a href="/wiki/Overtaking#Overtaking_in_racing" title="Overtaking">inside line</a> into turn&#160;six.<sup id="cite_ref-review_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-review-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Henceforth, Schumacher and Hill began to gradually pull away from Coulthard, who realised that he could not match the pace of the leaders and settled into his own rhythm, albeit one that was still comfortably faster than the cars behind him.<sup id="cite_ref-ASpg34_43-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ASpg34-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> By lap&#160;five, Herbert had dropped down to tenth&#160;position, unhappy with the handling of the spare car and having been overtaken by Irvine, Salo and Katayama.<sup id="cite_ref-pg92-93_1-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg92-93-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-R&amp;R_36-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-R&amp;R-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> On lap&#160;ten, Schumacher lapped last-placed Diniz for the first time; the presence of slower cars a constant factor for the front-runners thereafter.<sup id="cite_ref-pg92-93_1-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg92-93-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> On the same lap Frentzen retired from 12th&#160;position with electrical failure. He was joined by Schiattarella two laps later after his Simtek car developed a steering problem.<sup id="cite_ref-pg92-93_1-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg92-93-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Irvine made a <a href="/wiki/Pit_stop" title="Pit stop">pit stop</a> earlier than expected on lap&#160;fifteen, retiring from the race with a gearbox actuator problem.<sup id="cite_ref-pg91_42-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg91-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> On the same lap, Katayama spun at the fourth&#160;corner, <a href="/wiki/Stall_(engine)" title="Stall (engine)">stalled</a> his car in the process and retired from the race. The two retirements meant Herbert moved back up to eighth position.<sup id="cite_ref-bbc-lap15_44-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-lap15-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> Alesi was the first of the leading drivers to make a scheduled pit stop on lap&#160;17,<sup id="cite_ref-pg92-93_1-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg92-93-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> rejoining the track in eleventh place.<sup id="cite_ref-review_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-review-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello, Irvine's teammate, retired a lap later, driving into the pit lane slowly with a gearbox problem.<sup id="cite_ref-pg92-93_1-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg92-93-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> On the same lap, Verstappen retired his Simtek car with a clutch problem.<sup id="cite_ref-pg92-93_1-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg92-93-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Benetton and Williams teams were employing different pit stop strategies – the Benetton team were planning a three&#160;stop strategy whereas the Williams team were only planning for two&#160;stops.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix-brazil_34-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-brazil-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher made a pit stop on lap&#160;18, but was momentarily baulked by the slowing Jordan car of Barrichello as he was entering the pitlane.<sup id="cite_ref-bbc-lap16_45-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-lap16-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher exited the stop behind Berger, but overtook the Austrian driver at the beginning of the next lap.<sup id="cite_ref-pg92-93_1-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg92-93-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Hill, who had remained close behind Schumacher before the German made a pit stop, made his own stop on lap&#160;21, exiting the pitlane in front of Schumacher.<sup id="cite_ref-pg92-93_1-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg92-93-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard took the lead of the race for one lap, before pitting and dropping behind the two leaders and the yet-to-stop Häkkinen and Berger.<sup id="cite_ref-pg92-93_1-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg92-93-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Häkkinen and Berger were the final drivers of the leading teams to pit, however, the McLaren mechanics had trouble getting the fuel hose off the Finn's car when he stopped on lap&#160;23, losing him ten&#160;seconds.<sup id="cite_ref-bbc-mika_46-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-mika-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> Several other teams experienced problems with attaching and detaching the refuelling nozzles, and occasional small sprays of fuel were observed, reinforcing the problem experienced by McLaren with the equipment before the race meeting.<sup id="cite_ref-TP_20-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TP-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Berger, adopting a strategy of pitting late, was the only driver not to have stopped by lap&#160;24 and thus was third, with Hill, Schumacher, Coulthard, Alesi and Salo the other drivers in the top six places.<sup id="cite_ref-pg92-93_1-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg92-93-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Salo's stop had been faultless, gaining him significant time on those drivers who had been running in front of him.<sup id="cite_ref-salo_47-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-salo-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> By this stage Gachot had retired his Pacific car after it got stuck in fifth gear.<sup id="cite_ref-R&amp;R_36-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-R&amp;R-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Berger made his stop on lap 27, but had problems leaving the pit lane after a communication problem between him and his mechanics nearly led to him leaving the pit lane with a loose <a href="/wiki/Lug_nut" title="Lug nut">wheel nut</a> on the front right-hand wheel. The <a href="/wiki/Pit_stop#List_of_Formula_One_Pit_Crew" title="Pit stop">pit "lollipop"</a>, signalling the driver when to leave, was raised and lowered again in quick succession, preventing Berger from leaving until the wheel nut was sufficiently tightened. The miscommunication cost Berger 13&#160;seconds,<sup id="cite_ref-bbc-berger_48-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-berger-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> dropping him back to seventh&#160;position.<sup id="cite_ref-pg92-93_1-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg92-93-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Alesi fell behind Salo and Häkkinen after the first&#160;set of pit&#160;stops, Salo overtaking him on lap&#160;25, with Häkkinen moving past three&#160;laps later as Alesi was held up behind the lapped Montermini.<sup id="cite_ref-pg92-93_1-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg92-93-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-review_7-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-review-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Damon_Hill_juillet_1995.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b6/Damon_Hill_juillet_1995.jpg/170px-Damon_Hill_juillet_1995.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="253" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b6/Damon_Hill_juillet_1995.jpg/255px-Damon_Hill_juillet_1995.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b6/Damon_Hill_juillet_1995.jpg/340px-Damon_Hill_juillet_1995.jpg 2x" data-file-width="341" data-file-height="507" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Damon_Hill_juillet_1995.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a> (pictured at the <a href="/wiki/1995_French_Grand_Prix" title="1995 French Grand Prix">1995 French Grand Prix</a>), who spun out of the race on lap&#160;30 from the lead with suspected gear box failure</div></div></div>
<p>Once in front of Schumacher, Hill was able to gradually extend his lead to 3.4&#160;seconds by lap&#160;30 despite carrying a heavier fuel load for one fewer planned pit stop.<sup id="cite_ref-pg91_42-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg91-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> However, he lost second gear during the course of that lap, and then spun off at turn&#160;two on lap&#160;31, retiring from the race with a suspected gearbox seizure.<sup id="cite_ref-review_7-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-review-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-pg91_42-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg91-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> On the same lap, Herbert also retired from the race after suffering damage in a collision with Suzuki, who himself had to make an unscheduled pit stop to replace his car's damaged nosecone.<sup id="cite_ref-R&amp;R_36-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-R&amp;R-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-pg91_42-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg91-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher thus assumed the lead as a result of Hill's retirement, leading by 11.5&#160;seconds from Coulthard. Salo was third in the remaining Tyrrell, 39.4&#160;seconds behind Coulthard. Häkkinen and the two Ferrari cars completed the top six&#160;positions.<sup id="cite_ref-pg92-93_1-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg92-93-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-bbc-positions_49-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-positions-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher made his second&#160;pit stop on lap&#160;37, exiting in second position behind Coulthard.<sup id="cite_ref-pg92-93_1-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg92-93-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Salo and Häkkinen, both performing much better than had been expected prior to the race,<sup id="cite_ref-ASpg35_50-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ASpg35-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> battled over third&#160;position until lap&#160;39, when Salo, suffering from cramp and driving with one hand, spun at the final bend. He made a pit stop soon after for fresh tyres, dropping to eighth&#160;position.<sup id="cite_ref-review_7-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-review-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-pg91_42-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg91-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> Häkkinen, having moved up to third, made his second&#160;stop on lap&#160;43, exiting behind the two Ferrari cars, Berger moving in front of Alesi as the latter made his stop on the following lap, rejoining in seventh.<sup id="cite_ref-pg92-93_1-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg92-93-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard made a pit stop from the lead on lap&#160;47, the gap between him and Schumacher not big enough to allow him to maintain the lead.<sup id="cite_ref-pg91_42-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg91-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher set the fastest lap of the race, 1:30.921, extending his lead to such an extent that when he made his own final stop on lap&#160;52, he maintained the lead.<sup id="cite_ref-ASpg36_51-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ASpg36-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> After his final stop, Schumacher gradually opened his leading margin 11&#160;seconds, winning the race after 71 laps in a time of 1:48:49.972s, with Coulthard finishing second in his Williams. The Ferrari cars of Berger – who made his final stop on lap&#160;49 without losing a place<sup id="cite_ref-pg92-93_1-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg92-93-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> – and Alesi were third and fifth respectively, but off the pace as Schumacher lapped them both in the closing stages. Häkkinen, despite losing part of his rear wing after his car was struck by a bird, split them both to take fourth position.<sup id="cite_ref-pg91_42-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg91-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> In the closing stages, Morbidelli and Blundell both overtook Salo, only for the Footwork driver to retire on lap&#160;62 with a blocked fuel line.<sup id="cite_ref-pg91_42-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg91-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> Blundell, who had earlier changed his steering wheel and was driving without the use of third gear,<sup id="cite_ref-R&amp;R_36-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-R&amp;R-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> thus took the final point in sixth place, ahead of Salo, Suzuki, Montermini – who completed the race distance despite struggling with a broken floor on his chassis, taking the Pacific team's first race finish – and Diniz.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix-brazil_34-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-brazil-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-R&amp;R_36-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-R&amp;R-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> The race saw a high attrition rate; only ten out of the 25 starters finished the race.<sup id="cite_ref-pg92-93_1-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg92-93-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Of the other late retirements, Wendlinger suffered a broken battery cable on lap&#160;41, <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Moreno" title="Roberto Moreno">Roberto Moreno</a> and Badoer retired on lap&#160;47 due to a spin and gearbox problem respectively, and Inoue's car caught fire on lap&#160;48.<sup id="cite_ref-pg92-93_1-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg92-93-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-pg91_42-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg91-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> The Japanese driver was in any case due to receive the <a href="/wiki/Racing_flags#Black_flag" title="Racing flags">black flag</a> for receiving an illegal push-start from the marshals following a spin on the circuit.<sup id="cite_ref-R&amp;R_36-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-R&amp;R-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

</p>
</div>
<p>Moments after his spin, Hill was interviewed by the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a>, revealing that his spin was due to a gearbox problem; the Williams car losing second gear before apparently seizing completely. Hill also said that he was "very, very disappointed" with the result, and that "we could have beaten him [Schumacher] today".<sup id="cite_ref-F1Ypg77_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1Ypg77-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-bbc-hillinterview_54-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-hillinterview-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup> Further post-race analysis conducted by the Williams team, however, found that the problem was actually a broken left-rear <a href="/wiki/Suspension_(vehicle)" class="mw-redirect" title="Suspension (vehicle)">suspension</a> <a href="/wiki/Overhead_valve" class="mw-redirect" title="Overhead valve">pushrod</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pg91_42-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg91-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Schumacher felt lucky to win the race, conceding that "Damon was a little bit quicker than me...he ended up going off and that's racing, but I don't think I could have caught him." He also believed that his good start was fortunate, as it was the first one he had made in the new Benetton car; the team had not practised starts previously due to being short of time. He also specified his evasion of Barrichello's slowing car in the pit-lane entry while making his first stop as a crucial moment of the race.<sup id="cite_ref-F1Ypg77_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1Ypg77-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard admitted that he had been affected by his tonsillitis: "I wasn't able to match Michael and Damon's pace so I settled for running my own race".<sup id="cite_ref-F1Ypg77_6-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1Ypg77-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Berger, meanwhile, was frustrated to finish a lap down on Schumacher, saying that "He was faster everywhere: down the straights, going in and out of the corners. What can you do in these conditions? Okay, the car's roadholding is not bad. We must work very hard now. But at least I finished, that's something."<sup id="cite_ref-F1Ypg77_6-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1Ypg77-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After the Grand Prix, Schumacher's Benetton and Coulthard's Williams cars were <a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/excluded" class="extiw" title="wikt:excluded">excluded</a> from the race classification as a result of the illegal fuel sample "fingerprints", and Berger declared the new victor.<sup id="cite_ref-review_7-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-review-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fia-exclusion_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fia-exclusion-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-grandprix-weight_26-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-weight-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Further samples of both cars' fuel were taken after the race; these also did not match the specified sample.<sup id="cite_ref-pg90_9-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg90-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Patrick Head was surprised by this outcome as he believed that "there was no question of disqualification until the fuel samples had been returned to Europe for further analysis".<sup id="cite_ref-pg90_9-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg90-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In addition to Benetton and Williams, fuel samples were taken from the Ferrari (<a href="/wiki/Agip" title="Agip">Agip</a>) and McLaren (<a href="/wiki/Mobil" title="Mobil">Mobil</a>) cars during the event, all of which were declared legal after FIA analysis.<sup id="cite_ref-fia-exclusion_22-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fia-exclusion-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> After an appeal was made by the two teams, the drivers were reinstated into the race classification at the hearing on April 13 (after the <a href="/wiki/1995_Argentine_Grand_Prix" title="1995 Argentine Grand Prix">Argentine Grand Prix</a> had taken place); however, the teams did not receive <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' points</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pg90_9-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg90-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The two teams were fined a further $200,000 during the hearing.<sup id="cite_ref-ms-hilton-pg157-61_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ms-hilton-pg157-61-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> It was generally agreed that the illegal fuel did not offer a performance advantage or break any of the regulations relating to chemical composition, only that it did not precisely match the sample that had previously been lodged with the FIA.<sup id="cite_ref-pg90_9-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg90-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Niki_Lauda" title="Niki Lauda">Niki Lauda</a>, an advisor to Ferrari, was not happy with the decision made at the appeal:
</p>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r960796168"/><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>I cannot separate car and driver completely. If this is a new rule, you can build an illegal car and let the team pay for victory. The whole thing is only commercial and has nothing to do with sport any more. It's like scoring a half-goal in soccer – it is not possible. Either you score a goal or not. The decision for me is the biggest defeat for the FIA, who cannot govern the sport any longer.<sup id="cite_ref-ms-hilton-pg157-61_2-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ms-hilton-pg157-61-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>
<p>Berger also commented on the situation, saying "I no longer understand anything. Formula One has become a joke."<sup id="cite_ref-ms-hilton-pg157-61_2-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ms-hilton-pg157-61-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> In the lead-up to the San Marino Grand Prix, Schumacher was quoted saying that Berger should "concentrate on racing instead of thinking how he can criticise me. I have never understood how someone can celebrate a victory like that [Brazil] one lap down and winning after someone else has been disqualified." Berger responded to Schumacher's comments by saying:
</p>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r960796168"/><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>I never criticised Schumacher. I only criticised the decision [to reinstate him]. I can live with Schumacher being angry. I was declared the winner by the FIA Stewards so I have every reason to open the champagne.<sup id="cite_ref-ms-hilton-pg157-61_2-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ms-hilton-pg157-61-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>
<p><a href="/wiki/Max_Mosley" title="Max Mosley">Max Mosley</a>, the president of the FIA, also responded to Schumacher's comments saying that his comments "reflect poorly on the sport and show a lack of adult attitude". Mosley also commented on the weight issue surrounding Schumacher saying that "it is a pity that it became a matter for public discussion, whether he drank a huge amount of water, didn't go to the loo or had a heavy helmet. It is just a pity he didn't take care that it didn't happen".<sup id="cite_ref-ms-hilton-pg157-61_2-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ms-hilton-pg157-61-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Elf was also unhappy with the way in which the episode had been handled, the company's Commercial Director <a href="/w/index.php?title=Michel_Bonnet&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Michel Bonnet (page does not exist)">Michel Bonnet</a> declaring that "We believe the FIA acted in a high-handed manner in disqualifying our two cars so quickly. It should have declared the results provisional and waited for the results of further analysis. Acting the way it did, the FIA has affected the image of large industrial companies, of top F1 teams and of famous drivers. The fact that we have been treated as cheats by a red team – probably red with embarrassment at having finished one lap behind our cars – and by Gerhard Berger is a serious slur against us." Elf also threatened to take the matter to a tribunal if the cars were not reinstated.<sup id="cite_ref-F1Ypg90_55-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1Ypg90-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup> Company representative <a href="/w/index.php?title=Valerie_Jorquera&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Valerie Jorquera (page does not exist)">Valerie Jorquera</a> also highlighted the need for a consistent chromatographic analysis of fuel samples, drawing attention to the fact that the samples provided during the event had been analysed by a different company than the reference sample, and that the differences may have been caused by the use of different analytical machines and methods.<sup id="cite_ref-autosport-anger_21-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosport-anger-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> The incident was seen as unfortunate for the sport, which was trying to make a fresh start after <a href="/wiki/Allegations_of_cheating_in_the_1994_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="Allegations of cheating in the 1994 Formula One season">several controversies the previous year</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-F1Ypg82_56-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1Ypg82-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> From the San Marino Grand Prix onwards, an FIA mobile fuel laboratory accompanied the teams to each event to permit more detailed testing at the track, in addition to ensuring consistency between events.<sup id="cite_ref-autosport-anger_21-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosport-anger-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
