<p>The <b>2010 Turkish Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>2010 Formula 1 Turkish Grand Prix</b>) was a Formula One motor race held on 30 May 2010 at the <a href="/wiki/Istanbul_Park" class="mw-redirect" title="Istanbul Park">Istanbul Park</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tuzla,_Istanbul" title="Tuzla, Istanbul">Tuzla</a>, Turkey. It was the seventh round of the <a href="/wiki/2010_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2010 Formula One season">2010 Formula One season</a> and the sixth <a href="/wiki/Turkish_Grand_Prix" title="Turkish Grand Prix">Turkish Grand Prix</a>. The 58-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> who started from second position. His teammate <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> finished second, and <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> took third.
</p><p>Webber clinched the <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> and maintained his lead at the start of the race with Hamilton in second who fended off a challenge from <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> in the other Red Bull. The order was maintained until the first sequence of <a href="/wiki/Pit_stop" title="Pit stop">pit stops</a> when Hamilton lost second place after his crew had trouble with fitting one of his tyres correctly. Webber conserved fuel on lap 40 which allowed Vettel to challenge him for the lead but the two collided. Vettel retired and the crash promoted Hamilton and Button to first and second. Hamilton and Button were instructed to conserve fuel for the remainder of the race but the latter had not been giving a target lap time and attempted to overtake Hamilton on lap 48 although the former retained the lead which he held for the remaining ten laps to win his first race of the season.
</p><p>Hamilton's victory made him the fifth different driver in seven races to win a Grand Prix in 2010. The result extended Webber's advantage over second place in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a>, now occupied by Button, by five points. Hamilton's victory elevated him to third place, while Alonso fell from third to fourth. Vettel's retirement demoted him from second to fifth. McLaren's 1–2 finish reduced Red Bull's lead in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a> to one point. Ferrari fell from second to third after a poor result, with 12 races left of the season.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_Turkish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Istanbul_Park_aerial.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/90/Istanbul_Park_aerial.jpg/220px-Istanbul_Park_aerial.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="123" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/90/Istanbul_Park_aerial.jpg/330px-Istanbul_Park_aerial.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/90/Istanbul_Park_aerial.jpg/440px-Istanbul_Park_aerial.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3798" data-file-height="2115" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Istanbul_Park_aerial.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Istanbul_Park" class="mw-redirect" title="Istanbul Park">Istanbul Park</a>, where the race was held.</div></div></div>
<p>The 2010 Turkish Grand Prix was the seventh scheduled round of the <a href="/wiki/2010_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2010 Formula One season">2010 Formula One season</a> after taking a two-week break from the previous race of the season in <a href="/wiki/2010_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="2010 Monaco Grand Prix">Monaco</a>. It was held on 30 May 2010 at the <a href="/wiki/Istanbul_Park" class="mw-redirect" title="Istanbul Park">Istanbul Park</a> circuit in <a href="/wiki/Tuzla,_Istanbul" title="Tuzla, Istanbul">Tuzla</a>, near <a href="/wiki/Istanbul" title="Istanbul">Istanbul</a>, Turkey.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The Grand Prix was contested by twelve teams with two drivers each. The teams (also known as <a href="/wiki/Formula_One#Constructors" title="Formula One">constructors</a>) were <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a>, <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>, <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a>, <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso</a>, <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus_(2010%E2%80%9311)" title="Team Lotus (2010–11)">Lotus</a>, <a href="/wiki/HRT_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="HRT F1">Hispania</a> and <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Racing" title="Virgin Racing">Virgin</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-results_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-results-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_tyre" class="mw-redirect" title="Formula One tyre">Tyre</a> supplier <a href="/wiki/Bridgestone" title="Bridgestone">Bridgestone</a> brought four different tyre types to the race: two dry compounds (soft "options" and hard "primes") and two <a href="/wiki/Rain_tyre" title="Rain tyre">wet-weather compounds</a> (intermediate and full wet).<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Before the race Red Bull drivers <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> were tied for the lead of the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_champions" class="mw-redirect" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; champions">Drivers' Championship</a> with 78 <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Championship_points_scoring_systems" title="List of Formula One World Championship points scoring systems">points</a> each, with Ferrari driver <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> in third with 75 points. <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> was fourth on 70 points and <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> was fifth on 61 points.<sup id="cite_ref-points_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Champions</a> Red Bull were leading with 156 points, Ferrari and McLaren were second and third with 136 and 129 points respectively, while Mercedes (78 points) and Renault (65) contended for fourth place.<sup id="cite_ref-points_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Ferrari, McLaren and Red Bull had so far clinched the six previous victories of the season. Championship competitors Massa and <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a> had both secured second place podium finishes with <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> having both achieved third place podium finishes.<sup id="cite_ref-points_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Webber dominated the previous two rounds in <a href="/wiki/2010_Spanish_Grand_Prix" title="2010 Spanish Grand Prix">Spain</a> and Monaco and clinched the <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> in both events. Despite the form, Webber said Red Bull were still wary of their rivals, saying that they had a good foundation and position but no person knew who would be challenging for the title late in the season, but did not feel it would between two people.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Ferrari came into the event with a long series of success at the track, having won three of the last five races held at Istanbul Park. The team identified the event as their 800th Grand Prix and their cars carried a logo celebrating the achievement on their engine covers.<sup id="cite_ref-telegraphferrari_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-telegraphferrari-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Alonso was upbeat about their chances in the race, saying that compared to the Spanish Grand Prix, he felt their situation was a little better due to the fact Istanbul Park's characteristics are different and did not require the maximum amount of aerodynamic downforce, but a lower level which was suited to the <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_F10" title="Ferrari F10">F10</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-telegraphferrari_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-telegraphferrari-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Alonso also believed it would be a "surprise" if the Red Bull team finished behind him.<sup id="cite_ref-telegraphferrari_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-telegraphferrari-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton claimed that McLaren would be competitive in Turkey and hoped that he would secure his first victory of the season.<sup id="cite_ref-hurrietprerace_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hurrietprerace-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Several teams made modifications to their <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_cars" class="mw-redirect" title="Formula One cars">cars</a> in preparation for the event. Red Bull introduced a revised version of its <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_RB6" title="Red Bull RB6">RB6</a> chassis for Vettel as his previous monocoque had a small defect which created handling difficulties in Monaco.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The team tested a version of McLaren's <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4-25#F-duct" title="McLaren MP4-25">F-duct</a> system (which increases the top speed of a car) during the Friday practice sessions but removed it for qualifying and the race because both of McLaren's drivers found the device difficult to operate.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Ferrari modified their F-duct system so that it would allow their drivers to operate the system by using their left leg.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Mercedes arrived at the circuit by reverting to a previous specification of suspension and introduced a longer wheelbase on both their cars as well as a new version of their F-duct system.<sup id="cite_ref-hurrietprerace_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hurrietprerace-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Williams decided to revert to an old specification of their front wing following accidents involving their drivers in Monaco, though the team brought new brake ducts.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Lotus débuted a new rear wing specification which improved forward balance of their chassis.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Virgin managed to procure a longer-wheelbase version of the <a href="/wiki/Virgin_VR-01" title="Virgin VR-01">VR-01</a> for <a href="/wiki/Lucas_di_Grassi" title="Lucas di Grassi">Lucas di Grassi</a> after the <a href="/wiki/2010_eruptions_of_Eyjafjallaj%C3%B6kull" title="2010 eruptions of Eyjafjallajökull">2010 eruptions of Eyjafjallajökull</a> prevented them from having two cars prepared in time for the previous race.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice">Practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_Turkish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Three practice sessions were held before the Sunday race—two on Friday, and a third on Saturday. The Friday morning and afternoon sessions each lasted 90 minutes. The third session was held on Saturday morning and lasted an hour.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The Friday morning practice session was held on a dusty track scattered with debris and several drivers spun off the track.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcpractice_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcpractice-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton set the fastest time of the first session, at 1 minute and 28.653 seconds; his late lap was almost one second quicker than teammate Button. The two Mercedes drivers ended third and fourth quickest; <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> ahead of Rosberg. Vettel and Webber set the fifth and eighth fastest times respectively for Red Bull; they were separated by the Renault cars of Kubica and <a href="/wiki/Vitaly_Petrov" title="Vitaly Petrov">Vitaly Petrov</a>. Alonso and <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a> rounded out the session's top ten drivers.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportfree1_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportfree1-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> The session was disrupted with two minutes remaining when Sutil ran wide at turn eight, which caused him to spin backwards and crash into a barrier, breaking his front wing and both front tyres.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> The session was prematurely ended due to the limited time running.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportfree1_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportfree1-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the second practice session, Button set the quickest lap of the day, a 1:28.280, which was nearly one tenth of a second quicker than Webber. Vettel was third fastest followed by Hamilton and Alonso in fourth and fifth. Both Mercedes cars were slower in the session with Rosberg sixth and Schumacher seventh. Kubica, Petrov and Massa followed in the top ten.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Webber stopped outside the exit of turn two with an engine failure and engaged in an argument with <a href="/wiki/Motorsport_marshal" title="Motorsport marshal">marshals</a> on preventing the car from moving backwards.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcpractice_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcpractice-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> In the final practice session, Vettel was fastest with a time of 1:27.086. This made him three tenths of a second faster than Rosberg in second place. Hamilton set the third quickest lap despite spinning sideways into the turn eight <a href="/wiki/Run-off_area" title="Run-off area">gravel trap</a>. He was ahead of Webber who was afflicted with a throttle problem. Kubica, Alonso, Schumacher, Button, Massa and Petrov completed the top ten ahead of qualifying.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_Turkish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Mark_Webber_2014_WEC_Fuji.jpg" class="image"><img alt="A man in his late thirties wearing a red, white and grey jacket and a white baseball cap with sponsors logos." src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/20/Mark_Webber_2014_WEC_Fuji.jpg/170px-Mark_Webber_2014_WEC_Fuji.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="227" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/20/Mark_Webber_2014_WEC_Fuji.jpg/255px-Mark_Webber_2014_WEC_Fuji.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/20/Mark_Webber_2014_WEC_Fuji.jpg/340px-Mark_Webber_2014_WEC_Fuji.jpg 2x" data-file-width="800" data-file-height="1067" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Mark_Webber_2014_WEC_Fuji.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> <i>(pictured in 2014)</i> had his third consecutive <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> of the season.</div></div></div>
<p>The qualifying session held on Saturday afternoon was split into three parts. The first part ran for 20 minutes and eliminated the cars that finished the session 18th or lower. The second session lasted 15 minutes and eliminated cars that finished in positions eleven to 17. The final session ran for ten minutes and determined pole position to tenth. Cars which competed in the final session of qualifying were not allowed to change tyres before the race, and as such started the race on the tyres that they set their quickest lap on.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The session was held in dry weather conditions.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Webber set the fastest time in the final session, and took his third successive pole position with a lap of 1:26.295. He was joined on the grid's front row by Hamilton, who recorded a lap time 0.148 seconds slower. Vettel set the fastest times in the first two sessions, though mistakes on his first two runs due to a <a href="/wiki/Anti-roll_bar" title="Anti-roll bar">roll-bar</a> failure in the final session prevented him from recording a faster lap. He dropped to third overall in the final session.<sup id="cite_ref-gpquali_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpquali-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-bbcquali_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcquali-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> A car setup error caused Button to run too low to the ground through turn eight and was restricted to fourth.<sup id="cite_ref-quotes_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-quotes-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher qualified in fifth and was happy with his starting position despite spinning at turn eight on his final qualifying lap.<sup id="cite_ref-gpquali_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpquali-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-quotes_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-quotes-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Rosberg qualified sixth and despite wanting to start higher up the field, he was confident about his race chances Kubica had problems when running the soft compound tyre managing seventh but was happy with his lap in the final session.<sup id="cite_ref-quotes_23-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-quotes-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He was ahead of Massa in the faster of the two Ferrari cars. Petrov secured ninth in the slower Renault car. Kobayashi rounded out the top ten fastest qualifiers.<sup id="cite_ref-gpquali_21-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpquali-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Sutil was the fastest driver not to advance into the final session in eleventh; his best time of 1:27.525 was eight tenths of a second slower than Vettel's pace in the second session.<sup id="cite_ref-bbctimes_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbctimes-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Alonso recorded the twelfth fastest time and could not secure a position in the final session as his car touched a white line under braking on the circuit, causing his car to slide. Nevertheless, Alonso thought that the error did not cost him time.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcquali_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcquali-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> He was followed by <a href="/wiki/Pedro_de_la_Rosa" title="Pedro de la Rosa">Pedro de la Rosa</a> in the other Sauber car in 13th, who in turn, was ahead of <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9bastien_Buemi" title="Sébastien Buemi">Sébastien Buemi</a> for Toro Rosso and Williams driver <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a>. This formation continued on the eighth row of the grid, which was occupied by <a href="/wiki/Jaime_Alguersuari" title="Jaime Alguersuari">Jaime Alguersuari</a> in the second Toro Rosso and <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a> in the other Williams.<sup id="cite_ref-bbctimes_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbctimes-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Vitantonio_Liuzzi" title="Vitantonio Liuzzi">Vitantonio Liuzzi</a> failed to progress beyond the first session; the Force India driver qualified in 18th. The Lotus cars of <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> lined up in 19th and 20th respectively. <a href="/wiki/Timo_Glock" title="Timo Glock">Timo Glock</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Senna" title="Bruno Senna">Bruno Senna</a> lined up on the eleventh row of the grid, with their teammates <a href="/wiki/Lucas_di_Grassi" title="Lucas di Grassi">Lucas di Grassi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Karun_Chandhok" title="Karun Chandhok">Karun Chandhok</a> qualifying at the rear of the field.<sup id="cite_ref-bbctimes_24-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbctimes-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_Turkish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The race commenced at 15:00 <a href="/wiki/Eastern_European_Summer_Time" title="Eastern European Summer Time">Eastern European Summer Time</a> (<a href="/wiki/Coordinated_Universal_Time" title="Coordinated Universal Time">UTC</a>+3). The conditions on the grid were warm and sunny before the race; there was an air temperature between 28 to 29&#160;°C (82 to 84&#160;°F) with an track temperature ranging between 39 to 49&#160;°C (102 to 120&#160;°F).<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Di Grassi changed his engine and began from the <a href="/wiki/Pit_lane" class="mw-redirect" title="Pit lane">pit lane</a> because his team rectified an issue with his engine oil system which was discovered one hour beforehand.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_25-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-sundayquotes_26-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sundayquotes-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> As the five red lights went out to signal the start of the race, Webber maintained his pole position advantage heading into the first corner.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_25-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton had <a href="/wiki/Wheelspin" title="Wheelspin">wheelspin</a> when he moved out of his starting position and abandoned the use of his second clutch.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> Vettel made a brisk start to pass Hamilton for second around the inside of turn one but Hamilton reclaimed the position by passing Vettel around the outside at the third corner.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcwebrace_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcwebrace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher also made a fast getaway and overtook Button for fourth place but the latter retook the position at turn twelve after <a href="/wiki/Drafting_(aerodynamics)" title="Drafting (aerodynamics)">slipstreaming</a> in Schumacher's tow on the back straight.<sup id="cite_ref-gpracenotes_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpracenotes-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> De la Rosa ran wide at turn two allowing Buemi to pass him heading into the third turn. However, Buemi went wide allowing Hülkenberg to take advantage of Buemi's error. Buemi attempted to re-pass Hülkenberg around the inside at turn seven but Hülkenberg made contact with Buemi, puncturing the right rear tyre of Buemi's car which forced both drivers to make early pit stops.<sup id="cite_ref-sundayquotes_26-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sundayquotes-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> At the end of the first lap, Webber led Hamilton by 0.4 seconds,<sup id="cite_ref-bbcwebrace_28-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcwebrace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> who in turn was followed by Vettel, Button, Schumacher, Rosberg, Kubica, Massa, Petrov, Sutil, Kobayashi, Alonso, de la Rosa, Alguersuari, Hülkenberg, Liuzzi, Trulli, Kovalainen, Senna, Barrichello, Glock, Chandhok, di Grassi and Buemi.<sup id="cite_ref-fialapchart_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fialapchart-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Sebastian_Vettel_2010_Japan.jpg" class="image"><img alt="A man in his early twenties wearing a navy blue baseball cap with sponsors logos and a T-shirt jacket. He is holding a microphone in his right hand." src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8b/Sebastian_Vettel_2010_Japan.jpg/170px-Sebastian_Vettel_2010_Japan.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="247" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8b/Sebastian_Vettel_2010_Japan.jpg/255px-Sebastian_Vettel_2010_Japan.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8b/Sebastian_Vettel_2010_Japan.jpg/340px-Sebastian_Vettel_2010_Japan.jpg 2x" data-file-width="600" data-file-height="873" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Sebastian_Vettel_2010_Japan.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> collided with teammate Webber on the race's 40th lap.</div></div></div>
<p>The first four drivers opened a gap from the rest of the field,<sup id="cite_ref-gpracenotes_29-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpracenotes-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> as Webber set the <a href="/wiki/Fastest_lap" title="Fastest lap">fastest lap</a> of the race so far on lap two with a time of 1:33.685.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcwebrace_28-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcwebrace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton attempted an overtake manoeuvre on Webber heading into turn twelve on lap three but was unable to get close to affect a pass. Alonso passed de la Rosa for twelfth place on the same lap and began to battle Kobayaski for eleventh. Hamilton attempted to pass Webber for a second time at turn twelve on lap four but could not get close enough.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_25-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> This was because Hamilton's car was more effective than Webber's; it allowed Webber to pull away through turn eight but Hamilton could close the gap between himself and Webber in the back straight by employing his car's F-duct.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_25-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-bbcwebrace_28-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcwebrace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Hülkenberg passed di Grassi and Chandhok in turn eight to move up into 21st place by lap six, while Webber, Hamilton and Vettel traded the fastest lap with Hamilton quickest on the same lap.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_25-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> McLaren became aware of a higher than expected fuel consumption by the tenth lap and they ordered both their drivers to change engine modes for fuel conservation.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Kobayashi made a pit stop on lap eleven which released Alonso into <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_motorsport_terms" title="Glossary of motorsport terms">clean air</a>; Alonso made his pit stop on the following lap for hard tyres and rejoined ahead of Kobayashi. The next few laps saw a large amount of activity in the pit lane. Vettel made his pit stop from third position on lap 15, and rejoined in front of Rosberg, while Button pushed hard in an attempt to move ahead of Vettel. Hamilton and Webber both made their pit stops on the following lap; Webber emerged in front as Hamilton's pit crew were slow to fit Hamilton's left-rear tyre. Hamilton fell to third place behind Vettel.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_25-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Button thus inherited the lead but reported to his team via radio that he was losing rear <a href="/wiki/Grip_(auto_racing)" title="Grip (auto racing)">grip</a> in his tyres after pushing hard. He made a pit stop on lap 18 and re-emerged in fourth. Hamilton attempted to pass Vettel around the outside heading into turn 12 on lap 18 but was unable to complete the manoveure after running deep into the corner.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcwebrace_28-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcwebrace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> By the end of the 19th lap, all of the leading drivers had taken their pit stops. The running order was Webber leading with Vettel, Hamilton and Button in close attendance, then a 15-second gap back to Schumacher, Rosberg and Kubica. Massa in eighth was being caught by Petrov in ninth, and the two were followed by Alonso, Kobayashi, Sutil, de la Rosa, Alguersuari, Liuzzi, Hülkenberg, Kovalainen, Barrichello, Trulli, Glock, Senna, Buemi, di Grassi and Chandhok.<sup id="cite_ref-fialapchart_30-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fialapchart-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> The top nine drivers remained in the same positions in which they had qualified, and as the race continued, there was a threat of rain forecast by meteorological service <a href="/wiki/M%C3%A9t%C3%A9o-France" title="Météo-France">Météo-France</a> which intensified by hot weather and heavy clouds brewing to the west of the circuit. Trulli pulled off to the side of the track with an hydraulic failure, becoming the first retirement on lap 35.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_25-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Trulli's teammate Kovalainen retired the lap after with a <a href="/wiki/Power_steering" title="Power steering">power steering</a> failure in turn eight which was followed by him not being able to operate his gearbox, clutch and throttle and his car was pushed into the Lotus garage.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_25-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-sundayquotes_26-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sundayquotes-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Hamilton_2008_Singapore_GP_1.jpg" class="image"><img alt="A man in his early twenties wearing a white T-shirt and a red baseball cap with sponsors logos. He is holding a microphone in his left hand." src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4b/Hamilton_2008_Singapore_GP_1.jpg/170px-Hamilton_2008_Singapore_GP_1.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="256" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4b/Hamilton_2008_Singapore_GP_1.jpg/255px-Hamilton_2008_Singapore_GP_1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4b/Hamilton_2008_Singapore_GP_1.jpg/340px-Hamilton_2008_Singapore_GP_1.jpg 2x" data-file-width="667" data-file-height="1006" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Hamilton_2008_Singapore_GP_1.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> <i>(pictured in 2008)</i> took his first victory of the year.</div></div></div>
<p>On lap 39, Webber asked Red Bull to advise Vettel to reduce his speed but his request was rejected due to the quick pace and the close distance between themselves and the McLaren cars.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Webber entered fuel-saving mode on lap 40 which lost him performance while Vettel had saved one kilogram of fuel and opted to run on a faster engine setting.<sup id="cite_ref-vettelwebbercrash_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vettelwebbercrash-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Vettel got a run exiting turn eleven and turned left to the <a href="/wiki/Racing_line" title="Racing line">inside lane</a> and drew alongside Webber on the back straight.<sup id="cite_ref-gpracenotes_29-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpracenotes-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> As the pair approached turn twelve, they made contact at high-speed which sent Vettel spinning through 360 degrees twice. Both drivers ended on a run-off area and Vettel almost hit Webber again as Hamilton and Button overtook them. Vettel retired with a punctured right-rear tyre and Webber sustained damage to his front wing endplate.<sup id="cite_ref-vettelwebbercrash_33-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vettelwebbercrash-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> The expected rain only manifested as a very light drizzle on lap 41, as Webber made a pit stop for a replacement front wing two laps later. Hamilton was informed by McLaren to conserve fuel on lap 44 as the drivers drove cautiously through turn one because of the light rain.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_25-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Senna drove to the pit lane to retire with a fuel pressure issue.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_25-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-sundayquotes_26-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sundayquotes-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Button was instructed to conserve fuel although he was not given a target lap time because the team's chief engineer Phil Prew felt Button would not pass his teammate.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton had been told by his race engineer Andy Latham that Button would not overtake him.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> McLaren asked both drivers to drive cautiously through turn eight where there was a high risk for problems with their front-right tyres.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_31-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Button drew alongside teammate Hamilton on lap 48 heading into turn twelve and passed Hamilton (who drove on the inside line) around the outside of the corner. Hamilton reclaimed the lead on the following lap after getting a run on the pit straight and narrowly avoided a collision with Button at turn one.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcwebrace_28-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcwebrace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-gpracenotes_29-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpracenotes-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>  Hamilton began to pull away from teammate Button. Sutil passed Kobayashi around the outside for tenth on lap 52 while Button was ordered to conserve his tyres and fuel on the same lap.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_25-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Chandhok became the final retirement of the race when he entered the pit lane with a <a href="/wiki/Fuel_pump" title="Fuel pump">fuel pump</a> failure on lap 53.<sup id="cite_ref-sundayquotes_26-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sundayquotes-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Alonso attempted to overtake Petrov around the outside of turn one on lap 54 but was unable to get ahead.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_25-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Alonso tried again going into turn three and Petrov (who had worn tyres) made an error allowing Alonso to move into eighth. Both drivers made contact and Petrov sustained a punctured tyre.<sup id="cite_ref-sundayquotes_26-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sundayquotes-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Petrov made a pit stop for soft tyres on lap 56 and emerged in 15th. Hamilton maintained his lead throughout the remainder of the race and took the <a href="/wiki/Racing_flags#The_chequered_flag" title="Racing flags">checkered flag</a> on lap 58 to secure his first win of the season, 2.6 seconds in front of teammate Button.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportrace_25-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportrace-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Webber was third, ahead of Schumacher who equalled his best result of the season, having finished fourth at the Spanish Grand Prix as well. Rosberg held off Kubica to take fifth, followed by Massa, Alonso, Sutil and Kobayashi. De la Rosa was close behind teammate Kobayashi in eleventh. Alguersuari, Liuzzi, Barrichello, Petrov, Buemi, Hülkenberg, Glock, di Grassi and Chandhok (despite his mechanical issues) were the final classified finishers.<sup id="cite_ref-fialapchart_30-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fialapchart-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton's victory made him the fifth different driver in seven races to win a Grand Prix in 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_Turkish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The top three drivers appeared on the podium to collect their trophies and in a later press conference.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_14-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton said the race was "quite exciting" and dedicated the victory to his father Anthony. He stated that he was unfortunate up until the race but was happy that he could help McLaren achieve their second 1–2 finish of the season. Button said his second-place result was pleasing despite him starting from fourth position, and stated the pace of his McLaren was good. He felt the event was "fun", and praised his team for improving his car which allowed them to challenge Red Bull. Webber revealed that he had been confident that he would be able to take victory having fended off both McLaren cars for the previous 40 laps. He also stated the first stint of the race was "very interesting" and had expected it to be an "interesting race" but revealed he was not as comfortable on the hard-compound tyre.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Much of the post-race debate centred on the Red Bulls' clash at the front of the field on lap 40. Both Webber and Vettel blamed each other for the crash.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Team principal <a href="/wiki/Christian_Horner" title="Christian Horner">Christian Horner</a> implied Webber remained partially responsible for failing to give Vettel enough room on the run to the corner,<sup id="cite_ref-vettelwebbercrash_33-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vettelwebbercrash-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> and he was noticeably frustrated and angry at both of his drivers for wasting a potential 1–2 finish.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> Red Bull motorsport adviser <a href="/wiki/Helmut_Marko" title="Helmut Marko">Helmut Marko</a> insisted the team did not favour Vettel through their strategy and also offered the perspective that Vettel had to attack for fear of backing off into the clutches of Hamilton behind.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> McLaren chief engineer <a href="/wiki/Tim_Goss" title="Tim Goss">Tim Goss</a> said the crash had prevented Red Bull from securing a 1–2 finish, but understood why Webber switched to a different engine mode as he noted that one of Vettel's engines had failed earlier in the season. He felt that the battle between both McLaren and Red Bull drivers for both championships would continue unabated.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> Red Bull held a meeting at their factory in <a href="/wiki/Milton_Keynes" title="Milton Keynes">Milton Keynes</a> four days after the race, and all people in attendance believed the situation had been dealt with.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> The incident between Webber and Vettel is widely seen by the media as a key moment in the rivalry between the two drivers.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Mercedes maintained they had achieved what the car was capable of with fourth and fifth-place finishes for Schumacher and Rosberg respectively. Massa said it was a "very boring" and "difficult" race from his point of view because he was behind Kubica for the duration of the race whom he could not overtake. Alonso was disappointed with the team's performance in what Ferrari identified as their 800th start, yet he looked forward to upgrades in the following races. <a href="/wiki/James_Key_(Formula_One)" title="James Key (Formula One)">James Key</a>, the technical director of BMW Sauber, stated he was happy with the double finish and the one point the team picked up, courtesy of Kobayashi. Despite setting the race's fastest lap, Petrov was disappointed to not finish in a points-scoring position. He said there were many positives he could take from the event and hoped to achieve a stronger result in the Canadian round.<sup id="cite_ref-sundayquotes_26-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sundayquotes-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The result extended Webber's lead in the Drivers' Championship to five points over Button who moved into second place. Hamilton's victory promoted him to third, while Alonso's eighth-place finish demoted him third to fourth. Vettel's fell from second to fifth.<sup id="cite_ref-points_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> McLaren's 1–2 result allowed them to assume the lead of the Constructors' Championship with a one-point advantage over Red Bull. Ferrari's poor finish dropped them from second to third, while Mercedes remained in fourth position with 100 points. Renault retained fifth on 73 points, with 12 races left in the season.<sup id="cite_ref-points_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> After their strong finish, Hamilton hoped McLaren's result would give them further momentum to challenge Red Bull for the championship.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
