<p>The <b>1950 Monaco Grand Prix</b>, formally titled the <i><b>Prix de Monte-Carlo et XI<sup><small>e</small></sup> Grand Prix Automobile</b></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-prog_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prog-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held on 21 May 1950 at <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Monaco" title="Circuit de Monaco">Monaco</a>. It was race two of seven in the <a href="/wiki/1950_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1950 World Championship of Drivers">1950 World  Championship of Drivers</a>. The 100-lap race was held at an overall distance of 318.1&#160;km (197.1&#160;mi) and was won by <a href="/wiki/Juan_Manuel_Fangio" title="Juan Manuel Fangio">Juan Manuel Fangio</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Alfa_Romeo_in_Formula_One" title="Alfa Romeo in Formula One">Alfa Romeo</a> team after starting from pole position. <a href="/wiki/Alberto_Ascari" title="Alberto Ascari">Alberto Ascari</a> finished second for <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> and <a href="/wiki/Louis_Chiron" title="Louis Chiron">Louis Chiron</a> finished third for <a href="/wiki/Maserati_in_motorsport" title="Maserati in motorsport">Maserati</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1950_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>After two qualifying sessions, on Thursday and Saturday, which <a href="/wiki/Charles_Pozzi" title="Charles Pozzi">Charles Pozzi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yves_Giraud-Cabantous" title="Yves Giraud-Cabantous">Yves Giraud-Cabantous</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pierre_Levegh" title="Pierre Levegh">Pierre Levegh</a> and <a href="/wiki/Clemente_Biondetti" title="Clemente Biondetti">Clemente Biondetti</a> did not start, the race was dominated from start to finish by <a href="/wiki/Juan_Manuel_Fangio" title="Juan Manuel Fangio">Juan Manuel Fangio</a>, who scored his first ever victory in a World Championship event, driving an <a href="/wiki/Alfa_Romeo_(Formula_One)" class="mw-redirect" title="Alfa Romeo (Formula One)">Alfa Romeo</a>. The starting grid consisted of alternating rows of three and two, starting with three on the front row and continuing up to two on the 8th row. The first two rows of the grid (positions 1 to 5) were made up of the fastest five drivers from the Thursday qualifying session, with the remaining positions based on the other 16 drivers' times in the second session on the Saturday. This format meant that <a href="/wiki/Luigi_Villoresi" title="Luigi Villoresi">Luigi Villoresi</a> started 6th, despite his time being fast enough for 2nd place on the grid. 
</p><p>Due to an accident in practice, <a href="/wiki/Alfredo_Pi%C3%A0n" class="mw-redirect" title="Alfredo Piàn">Alfredo Piàn</a> did not start the race, with <a href="/wiki/Peter_Whitehead_(racing_driver)" title="Peter Whitehead (racing driver)">Peter Whitehead</a> another non-starter. The race was marred by a large pile-up during the first lap, when a wave from the harbour flooded the track at Tabac Corner. <a href="/wiki/Giuseppe_Farina" title="Giuseppe Farina">Nino Farina</a> in 2nd, spun and crashed while Fangio managed to escape the chaos. Those who were behind them tried to stop or avoid the carnage, but eight more drivers (from a field of 19 drivers) crashed and retired. None of them was injured, but <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Froil%C3%A1n_Gonz%C3%A1lez" title="José Froilán González">José Froilán González</a>, who damaged his <a href="/wiki/Maserati_in_motorsport" title="Maserati in motorsport">Maserati</a> in the pile-up but was subsequently running second, crashed during the second lap. His car caught fire and he suffered burns. The race went on with many cars going off at Tabac Corner, nearly causing other accidents. <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Luigi_Villoresi" title="Luigi Villoresi">Luigi Villoresi</a> charged his way from the back of the field after being delayed by the pile-up, but did not finish.
</p><p>Harry Schell's Cooper was the first rear-engined car to start in a championship race. Chiron's 3rd place finish made him the only <a href="/wiki/Monaco" title="Monaco">Monegasque</a> driver to score points in <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> until <a href="/wiki/Charles_Leclerc_(racing_driver)" class="mw-redirect" title="Charles Leclerc (racing driver)">Charles Leclerc</a> finished 6th in the <a href="/wiki/2018_Azerbaijan_Grand_Prix" title="2018 Azerbaijan Grand Prix">2018 Azerbaijan Grand Prix</a> some 68 years later.
</p>
