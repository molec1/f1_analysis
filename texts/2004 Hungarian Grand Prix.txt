<p>The <b>2004 Hungarian Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>XX Marlboro Magyar Nagydíj</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 15 August 2004 at the <a href="/wiki/Hungaroring" title="Hungaroring">Hungaroring</a>, <a href="/wiki/Budapest" title="Budapest">Budapest</a>, Hungary. It was the thirteenth race of the <a href="/wiki/2004_FIA_Formula_One_World_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="2004 FIA Formula One World Championship">2004 FIA Formula One World Championship</a> and the 20th <a href="/wiki/Hungarian_Grand_Prix" title="Hungarian Grand Prix">Hungarian Grand Prix</a>. The 70-lap race was won from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> by <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_F2004" title="Ferrari F2004">Ferrari</a>, with teammate <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> second and <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> third in a <a href="/wiki/Renault_R24" title="Renault R24">Renault</a>.
</p><p>The win was Michael Schumacher's twelfth of the season and his seventh in succession. The result meant that Schumacher increased his lead in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_champions" class="mw-redirect" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; champions">Drivers' Championship</a> to 38 points over Barrichello. <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a>, who finished fifth in the race in his <a href="/wiki/BAR_006" title="BAR 006">BAR</a>-<a href="/wiki/Honda_in_Formula_One" title="Honda in Formula One">Honda</a>, remained in third but was mathematically eliminated from the championship. Ferrari's one-two finish meant that they secured their sixth consecutive <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2004_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2004_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Heading into the thirteenth race of the season, Ferrari driver <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> led the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a> with 110 <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Championship_points_scoring_systems" title="List of Formula One World Championship points scoring systems">points</a>, ahead of teammate <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> on 74 points and <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> on 65. <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> was fourth with 46 points with his Renault teammate <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> in fifth place on 39 points.<sup id="cite_ref-points_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Ferrari were leading the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a> with 184 points; Renault (85 points) and BAR (76) contended for second place with Williams in fourth on 47 points and McLaren were a further ten points adrift in fifth place.<sup id="cite_ref-points_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Ferrari had dominated the championship; Michael Schumacher had won eleven races for the team, while Trulli had clinched the victory in the <a href="/wiki/2004_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="2004 Monaco Grand Prix">Monaco Grand Prix</a>. Barrichello, Button, Alonso, <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> had finished in second and third positions during the season.<sup id="cite_ref-points_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>There was one driver change heading into the race. <a href="/wiki/Cristiano_da_Matta" title="Cristiano da Matta">Cristiano da Matta</a> was dropped by Toyota and was replaced by the team's third driver <a href="/wiki/Ricardo_Zonta" title="Ricardo Zonta">Ricardo Zonta</a>. Da Matta was dropped because of his poor performance relative to teammate <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Panis" title="Olivier Panis">Olivier Panis</a>, but remained at the team as a driver and would perform marketing work while Toyota test driver <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Briscoe" title="Ryan Briscoe">Ryan Briscoe</a> assumed Zonta's former position.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
