<p>The <b>2010 Bahrain Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 14 March 2010 at the <a href="/wiki/Bahrain_International_Circuit" title="Bahrain International Circuit">Bahrain International Circuit</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sakhir" title="Sakhir">Sakhir</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bahrain" title="Bahrain">Bahrain</a>. It was the seventh <a href="/wiki/Bahrain_Grand_Prix" title="Bahrain Grand Prix">Bahrain Grand Prix</a> and the opening round of the <a href="/wiki/2010_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2010 Formula One season">2010 Formula One season</a>. It was the first time since <a href="/wiki/2006_Bahrain_Grand_Prix" title="2006 Bahrain Grand Prix">2006</a> that Bahrain had hosted the opening round and the race took place on a lengthened layout of the track.<sup id="cite_ref-new_layout_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-new_layout-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The race was won by <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>, his first as a <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> driver.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> His new teammate, <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> ensured a good start to the year for the team by finishing second. <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> completed the podium by finishing in third position.
</p><p>All of the race had been led by polesitter and <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a>, until lap 33 when a gearbox problem forced him to concede the lead to Alonso. This meant that he was eventually overtaken by Massa and Hamilton too, meaning that he finished in fourth place. Vettel's teammate, <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> qualified sixth before finishing the race in eighth place. <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>'s first race of his return to Formula One for the <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> resulted in him finishing sixth, one place behind teammate <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a>. Reigning world champion <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> completed his first race for the <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> team by finishing seventh.
</p><p>The three new teams – <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus_(2010%E2%80%9311)" title="Team Lotus (2010–11)">Lotus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hispania_Racing" class="mw-redirect" title="Hispania Racing">Hispania</a> and <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Racing" title="Virgin Racing">Virgin</a> – did not have a very successful debut race. Their six cars occupied the last six places in qualifying, and only one, the Lotus of <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a>, finished the race, although <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a>'s Lotus was also classified in last place. As a consequence of the race, <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> and <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> led their respective championships with maximum points.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_Bahrain_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_Bahrain_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The race saw the debut of three new teams: <a href="/wiki/Hispania_Racing" class="mw-redirect" title="Hispania Racing">Hispania</a>, <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Racing" title="Virgin Racing">Virgin</a> and the new Malaysian-owned <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus_(2010%E2%80%9311)" title="Team Lotus (2010–11)">Lotus</a> (not a direct descendant to <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Team Lotus</a>, even though it is part owned by the same company which at present owns <a href="/wiki/Lotus_Cars" title="Lotus Cars">Lotus Cars</a>), as well as the return of <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> after Daimler AG's take over of <a href="/wiki/Brawn_GP" title="Brawn GP">Brawn GP</a> after the end of the <a href="/wiki/2009_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2009 Formula One World Championship">2009</a> season, and of <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a>, having competed as BMW the previous year. New drivers <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Karun_Chandhok" title="Karun Chandhok">Karun Chandhok</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Senna" title="Bruno Senna">Bruno Senna</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lucas_di_Grassi" title="Lucas di Grassi">Lucas di Grassi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vitaly_Petrov" title="Vitaly Petrov">Vitaly Petrov</a> also take part in their maiden races. Hülkenberg joined <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a>, Petrov joined <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a> and di Grassi became part of the Virgin team, while Hispania had an all-rookie line-up of Senna and Chandhok. Petrov became Russia's first ever Formula One World Championship driver; Chandhok became India's second following <a href="/wiki/Narain_Karthikeyan" title="Narain Karthikeyan">Narain Karthikeyan</a>; Senna is a nephew of the legendary three-time Formula One world champion <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a>.
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Heikki_Kovalainen_2010_Bahrain.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/01/Heikki_Kovalainen_2010_Bahrain.jpg/220px-Heikki_Kovalainen_2010_Bahrain.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/01/Heikki_Kovalainen_2010_Bahrain.jpg/330px-Heikki_Kovalainen_2010_Bahrain.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/01/Heikki_Kovalainen_2010_Bahrain.jpg/440px-Heikki_Kovalainen_2010_Bahrain.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3888" data-file-height="2592" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Heikki_Kovalainen_2010_Bahrain.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus_(2010%E2%80%9311)" title="Team Lotus (2010–11)">Lotus Racing</a> was one of three new teams to debut in Bahrain.</div></div></div>
<p>2009 World Drivers' Champion <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> made his debut for <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> after changing teams from 2009 Constructors' Champion team Brawn in November 2009. Seven-time World Champion <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> made his return to <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> with Mercedes, <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> returned for <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> after his head injury at the <a href="/wiki/2009_Hungarian_Grand_Prix" title="2009 Hungarian Grand Prix">2009 Hungarian Grand Prix</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Timo_Glock" title="Timo Glock">Timo Glock</a> returned with the Virgin team following his accident at the <a href="/wiki/2009_Japanese_Grand_Prix" title="2009 Japanese Grand Prix">2009 Japanese Grand Prix</a>.
</p><p>All the previous winners of the event were present: <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> won the <a href="/wiki/2009_Bahrain_Grand_Prix" title="2009 Bahrain Grand Prix">2009 Bahrain Grand Prix</a>, with the Ferrari pair of <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> and <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> were both two-time winners at this circuit. Schumacher won the first event in 2004.
</p><p>The race also saw a return to the banning of refueling of the cars during the race, a practice which had been allowed since the <a href="/wiki/1994_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="1994 Formula One season">1994 Formula One season</a>. Additionally a new <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Championship_points_scoring_systems" title="List of Formula One World Championship points scoring systems">points scoring system</a> to decide the World Drivers' Championship was implemented, the most radical revamp of the system since the formation of the World Championship in <a href="/wiki/1950_Formula_One_season" title="1950 Formula One season">1950</a>. The 10-8-6-5-4-3-2-1 system used from <a href="/wiki/2003_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2003 Formula One World Championship">2003</a> to <a href="/wiki/2009_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2009 Formula One World Championship">2009</a> was replaced by a new system which awards points to the top ten finishers on a 25-18-15-12-10-8-6-4-2-1 basis.
</p><p>At 24 drivers, this was the largest grid at a Grand Prix since <a href="/wiki/1995_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1995 Formula One World Championship">1995</a>.
</p><p>The race was also the first to feature a revised stewards' panel under new FIA regulations, featuring a former Formula One driver.  The driver in Bahrain was four-time World Champion <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice_and_qualifying">Practice and qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_Bahrain_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Practice and qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Nico_Rosberg_2010_Bahrain.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a1/Nico_Rosberg_2010_Bahrain.jpg/220px-Nico_Rosberg_2010_Bahrain.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="125" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a1/Nico_Rosberg_2010_Bahrain.jpg/330px-Nico_Rosberg_2010_Bahrain.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a1/Nico_Rosberg_2010_Bahrain.jpg/440px-Nico_Rosberg_2010_Bahrain.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3629" data-file-height="2061" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Nico_Rosberg_2010_Bahrain.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> set the fastest time in the second free practice session for <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a>.</div></div></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a> set the fastest time for the <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a> team in the first session of free practice on Friday morning. <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> (<a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>) was second, ahead of <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a> (<a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>), <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> in the other Ferrari, and the two <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> drivers, <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>. On his return to Formula One, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> set the tenth-fastest time for <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a>, two positions behind teammate <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a>. Of the new teams, both <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus_(2010%E2%80%9311)" title="Team Lotus (2010–11)">Lotus</a> drivers and <a href="/wiki/Timo_Glock" title="Timo Glock">Timo Glock</a> set times, but <a href="/wiki/Lucas_di_Grassi" title="Lucas di Grassi">Lucas di Grassi</a> did not complete a full lap in the second <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Racing" title="Virgin Racing">Virgin</a> car. The <a href="/wiki/Hispania_Racing" class="mw-redirect" title="Hispania Racing">Hispania</a> team was still completing its two chassis when the session started, but <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Senna" title="Bruno Senna">Bruno Senna</a> was able to complete two installation laps before it ended.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In session 2 of free practice, <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> set the fastest time, with Hamilton in second and Schumacher came third. Senna was struggling to match the pace of the fastest <a href="/wiki/GP2_Asia_Series" title="GP2 Asia Series">GP2 Asia Series</a> drivers, but eventually did so, four seconds off the pace of the Virgins.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2010)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>In the Saturday free practice session, Alonso in the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> set the fastest time of 1:54.099, 0.269&#160;seconds faster than <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> in the Mercedes. Hispania's <a href="/wiki/Karun_Chandhok" title="Karun Chandhok">Karun Chandhok</a> failed to participate in the practice session due to a hydraulic problem.<sup id="cite_ref-BBC8565704_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC8565704-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Elsewhere, the Virgin of Glock lost its left-front wheel in the middle of the session, with the problem being attributed to an under-torqued airgun.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Felipe_Massa_Ferrari_during_Bahrain_2010_GP.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/Felipe_Massa_Ferrari_during_Bahrain_2010_GP.jpg/220px-Felipe_Massa_Ferrari_during_Bahrain_2010_GP.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="121" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/Felipe_Massa_Ferrari_during_Bahrain_2010_GP.jpg/330px-Felipe_Massa_Ferrari_during_Bahrain_2010_GP.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/Felipe_Massa_Ferrari_during_Bahrain_2010_GP.jpg/440px-Felipe_Massa_Ferrari_during_Bahrain_2010_GP.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3888" data-file-height="2135" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Felipe_Massa_Ferrari_during_Bahrain_2010_GP.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> qualified second for <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, behind polesitter <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>The first qualifying session saw the six drivers from the three new teams eliminated, with Timo Glock – the fastest of the newcomers – 2.7&#160;seconds adrift of <a href="/wiki/Jaime_Alguersuari" title="Jaime Alguersuari">Jaime Alguersuari</a> in eighteenth and the only driver from the established teams to be eliminated. Despite a crippling hydraulics problem that saw him unable to take part in any of the free practice sessions, Hispania's Karun Chandhok was able to qualify in 24th and last place with a lap time ten seconds slower than the fastest driver, Ferrari's Fernando Alonso.
</p><p>Reigning World Champion Jenson Button narrowly avoided elimination in the second qualifying period, pushing former <a href="/wiki/Brawn_GP" title="Brawn GP">Brawn GP</a> teammate <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> out of the top ten and into elimination. Barrichello's <a href="/wiki/Williams_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams F1">Williams</a> teammate <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a> was also eliminated, as were both <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Saubers</a> of <a href="/wiki/Pedro_de_la_Rosa" title="Pedro de la Rosa">Pedro de la Rosa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kamui_Kobayashi" title="Kamui Kobayashi">Kamui Kobayashi</a>, the second <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso</a> of <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9bastien_Buemi" title="Sébastien Buemi">Sébastien Buemi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Vitantonio_Liuzzi" title="Vitantonio Liuzzi">Vitantonio Liuzzi</a> and rookie driver <a href="/wiki/Vitaly_Petrov" title="Vitaly Petrov">Vitaly Petrov</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> took pole late in the third session, edging out the Ferraris of Massa and Alonso, with <a href="/wiki/2008_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2008 Formula One World Championship">2008</a> World Champion Lewis Hamilton in fourth. Mercedes's Nico Rosberg and seven-time World Champion Michael Schumacher were fifth and seventh respectively, with Sebastian Vettel's Red Bull teammate <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> splitting them. Jenson Button could only manage eighth place, while ninth and tenth were taken by <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a> and Adrian Sutil in the remaining Renault and Force India.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_Bahrain_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Fernando_Alonso_2010_Bahrain.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c5/Fernando_Alonso_2010_Bahrain.jpg/220px-Fernando_Alonso_2010_Bahrain.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="100" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c5/Fernando_Alonso_2010_Bahrain.jpg/330px-Fernando_Alonso_2010_Bahrain.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c5/Fernando_Alonso_2010_Bahrain.jpg/440px-Fernando_Alonso_2010_Bahrain.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1279" data-file-height="580" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Fernando_Alonso_2010_Bahrain.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> won on the occasion of his first race for the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> team.</div></div></div>
<p>The first corner of the first lap saw Mark Webber's engine release copious amounts of oil smoke, triggering a sequence of events that saw Adrian Sutil and Robert Kubica spin around and fall to the back of the field. The first lap was otherwise clean, with <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> quickly converting his pole position into the race lead. <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> got past teammate <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a>, to get second, and both Mercedes drivers also gained a place, with <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> fourth ahead of <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> beating <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> to sixth spot. There was little position-changing otherwise, with <a href="/wiki/Vitaly_Petrov" title="Vitaly Petrov">Vitaly Petrov</a> in the second Renault the biggest mover, up to eleventh from seventeenth on the grid.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Felipe_Massa_2010_Bahrain.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/98/Felipe_Massa_2010_Bahrain.jpg/220px-Felipe_Massa_2010_Bahrain.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="91" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/98/Felipe_Massa_2010_Bahrain.jpg/330px-Felipe_Massa_2010_Bahrain.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/98/Felipe_Massa_2010_Bahrain.jpg/440px-Felipe_Massa_2010_Bahrain.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1825" data-file-height="755" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Felipe_Massa_2010_Bahrain.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Massa, in his first race back after his accident in <a href="/wiki/2009_Hungarian_Grand_Prix" title="2009 Hungarian Grand Prix">Hungary in 2009</a>, finished second behind new teammate Alonso to score Ferrari their first 1-2 finish since the <a href="/wiki/2008_French_Grand_Prix" title="2008 French Grand Prix">2008 French Grand Prix</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>It was a race of attrition, with the first major incident of the race being the retirement of Hispania's Karun Chandhok, who, after just a handful of laps in qualifying, hit a bump he did not know existed and retired with a damaged front wing. Virgin Racing's Lucas di Grassi joined him on the sidelines shortly thereafter when his Virgin VR-01's hydraulics — a chronic problem throughout the off-season — gave up. Fellow rookie Nico Hülkenberg was lucky to avoid a similar fate to Chandhok when he missed a corner on the run down to turn seventeen and skipped over the circuit. Sauber's Kamui Kobayashi fell victim to hydraulic failure, and was joined a lap later by Petrov who damaged his front-right suspension when he hit a kerb too hard; at the time of his retirement, Petrov had been the highest-placed rookie and had been racing Rubens Barrichello for tenth place and the final championship point on offer. Renault later clarified the issue as being an unanticipated mechanical fault on both cars that was traced back to Petrov's preference for a lower ride height than teammate Kubica who went unscathed. Timo Glock in the second Virgin also retired after losing third and fifth gears, while Bruno Senna's debut for Hispania ended when his engine overheated at the end of the main straight. The six drivers retired during the first seventeen laps. At the front, Vettel and the two Ferraris quickly pulled out a sizeable gap to the rest of the field.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Lewis_Hamilton_2010_Bahrain.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/19/Lewis_Hamilton_2010_Bahrain.jpg/220px-Lewis_Hamilton_2010_Bahrain.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="136" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/19/Lewis_Hamilton_2010_Bahrain.jpg/330px-Lewis_Hamilton_2010_Bahrain.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/19/Lewis_Hamilton_2010_Bahrain.jpg/440px-Lewis_Hamilton_2010_Bahrain.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3888" data-file-height="2401" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Lewis_Hamilton_2010_Bahrain.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> finished behind the two Ferrari drivers in third position.</div></div></div>
<p>The first round of pit stops also proved to be the only round of stops, with Vettel stretching out enough of a lead to prevent the Ferraris from leap-frogging them. Elsewhere, good work from the McLaren crew allowed both their drivers to gain a place in the stops; Hamilton on Rosberg, and <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> on Webber. The list of retirements grew on lap 23 when Pedro de la Rosa in the second BMW Sauber was also struck by a hydraulic issue.
</p><p>Vettel continued to lead comfortably, two seconds ahead of Alonso and a further three ahead of Massa. However, he began to noticeably slow down during the latter part of the race due to a problem that was later identified within the team as a problem with a spark plug, and was quickly passed by the two Ferraris and Hamilton, and spent the rest of the race trying to hold Rosberg at bay. Alonso went on to win the race, joining <a href="/wiki/Juan_Manuel_Fangio" title="Juan Manuel Fangio">Juan Manuel Fangio</a>, <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Baghetti" title="Giancarlo Baghetti">Giancarlo Baghetti</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mario_Andretti" title="Mario Andretti">Mario Andretti</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> as the only men to win for Ferrari on their debut. Massa finished second on his return to full-time racing after his injury at the <a href="/wiki/2009_Hungarian_Grand_Prix" title="2009 Hungarian Grand Prix">2009 Hungarian Grand Prix</a>. Hamilton completed the podium, while Vettel successfully fended Rosberg off long enough to salvage fourth. Schumacher followed his teammate home for sixth, with Button seventh, Webber eighth, Vitantonio Liuzzi placing ninth and Barrichello claiming the final point on offer. Kubica recovered from his first-corner spin to claim eleventh while Sébastien Buemi and Jarno Trulli also retired; Buemi's Toro Rosso was struck by electrical issues, while Trulli added his name to the growing list of drivers taken down by hydraulics issues. As they each retired having completed 46 laps, they were classified as finishers as they completed ninety percent of the winner's race distance. Heikki Kovalainen finished fifteenth in the second Lotus, meaning that Lotus became the only entrant of the new teams to have a car finish the race.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_Bahrain_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>There was criticism after the race of the "new" Formula One, with it being echoed by Formula One personalities. McLaren team boss <a href="/wiki/Martin_Whitmarsh" title="Martin Whitmarsh">Martin Whitmarsh</a> stated that the F1 community had to "work together to improve it."<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Former driver and <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> pundit <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> said that two pitstops could be made mandatory as it "would also mean more potential for mistakes (and, by extension, spectator interest) in the pits." Coulthard was critical also of changes made by former <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">FIA</a> president <a href="/wiki/Max_Mosley" title="Max Mosley">Max Mosley</a>, as the changes were made during his time in charge.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>One person to disagree with the criticism, though, was <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a>, who said that "after a few races, I am sure the good drivers, the top drivers, will like it [the new format]."<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The new section of the circuit used by Formula One for the first time also drew criticism from both drivers and fans alike, with most claiming the added corners offered little in the way of passing opportunities. For the next Bahrain Grand Prix in <a href="/wiki/2012_Bahrain_Grand_Prix" title="2012 Bahrain Grand Prix">2012</a>, Formula One would revert to using the original Grand Prix Circuit.
</p>
