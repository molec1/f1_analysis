<p>The <b>1994 Italian Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>Pioneer 65<sup>o</sup> Gran Premio d'Italia</b><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held on 11 September 1994 at the <a href="/wiki/Autodromo_Nazionale_di_Monza" title="Autodromo Nazionale di Monza">Autodromo Nazionale di Monza</a>, <a href="/wiki/Monza" title="Monza">Monza</a>. It was the twelfth race of the <a href="/wiki/1994_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1994 Formula One World Championship">1994 Formula One World Championship</a>.
</p><p>The 53-lap race was won by British driver <a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW16" title="Williams FW16">Williams</a>-<a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>, with Austria's <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> second in a <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_412_T1" title="Ferrari 412 T1">Ferrari</a> and Finland's <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> third in a <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/9" title="McLaren MP4/9">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Peugeot" title="Peugeot">Peugeot</a>. Frenchman <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a> took <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> in the other Ferrari and led before suffering a gearbox failure on lap 15.
</p><p>The win enabled Hill to move to within 11 points of <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> in the Drivers' Championship. Schumacher was banned for this race and the following race in <a href="/wiki/1994_Portuguese_Grand_Prix" title="1994 Portuguese Grand Prix">Portugal</a> for his actions at the <a href="/wiki/1994_British_Grand_Prix" title="1994 British Grand Prix">British Grand Prix</a>; his place at <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a> was taken by Finland's <a href="/wiki/JJ_Lehto" title="JJ Lehto">JJ Lehto</a>, who had been his teammate earlier in the season.
</p><p>The day after the race, <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Lotus</a> went into receivership; however, they would compete in the remaining races of the 1994 season. Lotus had brought an upgraded <a href="/wiki/Mugen_Motorsports" title="Mugen Motorsports">Mugen</a> engine to Monza, allowing <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Herbert" title="Johnny Herbert">Johnny Herbert</a> to qualify in a season-best fourth place, but hopes of a points finish were ended by a first-corner collision with <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1994_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1994_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Grand Prix was originally cancelled on 12 August 1994 when local officials refused a demand to cut down 123 trees for reasons related to safety.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The trees in question were located at the Lesmo corners which lacked suitable run off-areas. After the announcement, <a href="/wiki/Gianni_Letta" title="Gianni Letta">Gianni Letta</a>, an Italian cabinet under-secretary went to Cannes to meet with FIA president <a href="/wiki/Max_Mosley" title="Max Mosley">Max Mosley</a> to discuss the issue. The meeting, also attended by Ferrari driver and representative to the drivers <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a>, agreed that changes to the shape of the curve would reduce its speed.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1994_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The two <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferraris</a> got off the line well heading into turn 1 with <a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a> going alongside the Ferrari cars. <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a> locked up causing him to hit the <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Lotus</a>-<a href="/wiki/Mugen_Motorsports" title="Mugen Motorsports">Mugen</a> of <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Herbert" title="Johnny Herbert">Johnny Herbert</a>. Herbert spun around clipping the right rear of <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> which caused a blockage in the road and led to the race being stopped. Coulthard took Hill's spare car, and Herbert sacrificed his starting position to get in his spare as well. At the second start, both Ferraris got away well, but going into Curva Grande, <a href="/wiki/Jos_Verstappen" title="Jos Verstappen">Jos Verstappen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alessandro_Zanardi" class="mw-redirect" title="Alessandro Zanardi">Alessandro Zanardi</a> tangled which forced <a href="/wiki/Gianni_Morbidelli" title="Gianni Morbidelli">Gianni Morbidelli</a> off the track and sliding into the outer wall. <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a> kept extending his lead over teammate <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> until he suffered clutch problems while exiting his pitbox. Berger inherited the lead but he himself had problems in the pitlane getting stuck behind <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Panis" title="Olivier Panis">Olivier Panis</a> and losing out track position to both <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a>. On lap 30, <a href="/wiki/Michele_Alboreto" title="Michele Alboreto">Michele Alboreto</a>'s gearbox failed at the Variante Ascari spraying oil on the track. As a result, <a href="/wiki/Pierluigi_Martini" title="Pierluigi Martini">Pierluigi Martini</a> spun off on lap 32, and then <a href="/wiki/Mark_Blundell" title="Mark Blundell">Mark Blundell</a> on lap 40. These retirements promoted <a href="/wiki/Ukyo_Katayama" title="Ukyo Katayama">Ukyo Katayama</a> to 5th place before he spun out and crashed at the second Lesmo six laps later. <a href="/wiki/David_Brabham" title="David Brabham">David Brabham</a> became the final retirement when a puncture had given his Simtek terminal damage, he retired just one lap after Katayama. The two Williams led a 1-2 race until the last corner of the race when Coulthard ran out of fuel, thus gifting Berger a second place, Häkkinen third, Barrichello fourth and Brundle fifth in the second McLaren. Coulthard was still classified in sixth as all other runners were a lap down.
</p>
