<p>The <b>2011 Turkish Grand Prix</b>, formally the <b>2011 Formula 1 DHL Turkish Grand Prix</b>,<sup id="cite_ref-DHL_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DHL-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 8 May 2011 at <a href="/wiki/Istanbul_Park" class="mw-redirect" title="Istanbul Park">Istanbul Park</a> in <a href="/wiki/Tuzla,_Istanbul" title="Tuzla, Istanbul">Tuzla</a>, near <a href="/wiki/Istanbul" title="Istanbul">Istanbul</a>, <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> It was the fourth round of the <a href="/wiki/2011_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2011 Formula One season">2011 Formula One season</a>. The 56-lap race was won by the championship leader, <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> after starting from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>. His teammate <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> finished in second place, and <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> completed the podium in third position.<sup id="cite_ref-Race_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Race-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As a consequence of the race, Vettel extended his lead in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">World Drivers' Championship</a> to 34&#160;points over <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>, who finished the race in fourth position. Webber's second-place finish moved him into third place in the championship, 4&#160;points behind Hamilton in third, and nine ahead of <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a>, who was sixth in Turkey. In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">World Constructors' Championship</a>, Red Bull extended their championship advantage to 43&#160;points over McLaren, with Ferrari a further 40&#160;points behind in third position.
</p><p>It was the last Turkish Grand Prix until <a href="/wiki/2020_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2020 Formula One World Championship">2020</a>, after the <a href="/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic" title="COVID-19 pandemic">COVID-19 pandemic</a> caused disruption to the schedule.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Turkish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Turkish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>With Turkey being the first European race of the season, several teams introduced upgrades to their cars in order to aid with their respective performances on the grid. <a href="/wiki/Williams_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams F1">Williams</a> introduced improvements to help with the handling of their car, having suffered a testing start to the season with no points at the first three races.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Racing" title="Virgin Racing">Virgin Racing</a>'s aerodynamic updates were bit-part, as only <a href="/wiki/Timo_Glock" title="Timo Glock">Timo Glock</a> received the package with <a href="/wiki/J%C3%A9r%C3%B4me_d%27Ambrosio" title="Jérôme d&#39;Ambrosio">Jérôme d'Ambrosio</a>'s package due to be ready for the <a href="/wiki/2011_Spanish_Grand_Prix" title="2011 Spanish Grand Prix">Spanish Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Turk_upgrades_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Turk_upgrades-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Hispania_Racing" class="mw-redirect" title="Hispania Racing">Hispania Racing</a> set their sights on trying to overhaul Virgin Racing, by introducing updates to their <a href="/wiki/Hispania_F111" title="Hispania F111">car</a> for the second successive race.<sup id="cite_ref-Turk_upgrades_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Turk_upgrades-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Other teams to introduce packages were <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>, <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> as they all strived to move closer on overall pace to <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a>, who were confident that they had rectified the <a href="/wiki/Kinetic_Energy_Recovery_Systems" class="mw-redirect" title="Kinetic Energy Recovery Systems">KERS</a> problems that had hampered their performance over the first few races.<sup id="cite_ref-Turk_upgrades_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Turk_upgrades-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Several teams made driver changes for the first practice session. <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a> substituted for <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a> at <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a>, while <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> drove in the place of <a href="/wiki/Jaime_Alguersuari" title="Jaime Alguersuari">Jaime Alguersuari</a> at <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Scuderia Toro Rosso</a>. <a href="/wiki/Karun_Chandhok" title="Karun Chandhok">Karun Chandhok</a> took part once again, replacing <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> at <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus_(2010%E2%80%9311)" title="Team Lotus (2010–11)">Lotus</a>.
</p><p>Tyre supplier <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_tyres" title="Formula One tyres">Pirelli</a> brought its silver-banded hard compound tyre as the harder "prime" tyre and the yellow-banded soft compound as the softer "option" compound. This was the same tyre selection that Bridgestone had chosen to bring to the Turkish Grand Prix for the past two years.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Before the race, <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> led the World Drivers' Championship with 68 points, 21 ahead of his nearest rival at the time - <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> - who was on 47 points. Hamilton had cut Vettel's lead from 24 to 21 points after winning his first race of the year, and also the first Vettel did not win, in <a href="/wiki/2011_Chinese_Grand_Prix" title="2011 Chinese Grand Prix">China</a>. <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> was third in the standings with 38 points, 1 ahead of <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> who had scored his first podium of the year when he drove from 18th to 3rd in China, three weeks previously. <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso's</a> <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> was still relatively slow compared to what it would be later in the year - and Ferrari hadn't been on the podium yet - he was fifth in the standings with just 26 points, 2 ahead of teammate <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Massa</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a> were the only team over 100 points and therefore lead the Constructors' Championship on 105. <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> were the only other team to have a podium at all three races and were in second place on 85 points. Ferrari had less than half of Red Bull on 50 points, but were still in third place. <a href="/wiki/Lotus_Renault_GP" class="mw-redirect" title="Lotus Renault GP">Renault</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes GP</a> were fourth and fifth with 32 and 16 points respectively.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Free_practice">Free practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Turkish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Free practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The opening session was run in wet conditions – the first competitive session in which <a href="/wiki/Pirelli" title="Pirelli">Pirelli</a>'s wet weather tyres had been used in the season<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> – where <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> was quickest by 1.4 seconds ahead of the <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> cars of <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> in second and third places respectively, with the only other drivers within two seconds of Alonso being the <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renaults</a> of <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vitaly_Petrov" title="Vitaly Petrov">Vitaly Petrov</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Kamui_Kobayashi" title="Kamui Kobayashi">Kamui Kobayashi</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a>. However, due to the conditions, many teams opted for conservative running with <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> running the fewest laps; their pairing of <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> completing five laps between them. Championship leader <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> caused a red flag during the session after spinning into the barrier; he put his right-rear wheel over the kerb at the exit of Turn 8 and thus ended the session – as well as his day's running – in 17th place.
</p><p>The second session was held in drying conditions, where Button was marginally quickest ahead of Rosberg, the only drivers to record a sub-1:27 lap time. After sitting out the first session save for an exploratory lap, Hamilton ran 31 laps in the second session and finished third ahead of Schumacher and <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a>, who ran mainly long-distance stints during the session.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Morning pacesetter Alonso was eleventh after a hydraulic problem limited track running, while on track, he had a spin at Turn 6.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Pastor_Maldonado" title="Pastor Maldonado">Pastor Maldonado</a> crashed his <a href="/wiki/Williams_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams F1">Williams</a> into the barrier, having run wide on the exit of Turn 8, and spun out on a damp patch. <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Racing" title="Virgin Racing">Virgin Racing</a>'s Jérôme d'Ambrosio later received a five-place grid penalty for ignoring yellow flags in the area of Maldonado's accident.<sup id="cite_ref-DAM_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DAM-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The third session saw the top four places taken by the Red Bull and Mercedes cars; Vettel finishing as the fastest driver ahead of Schumacher by 0.001 seconds. Webber was almost four tenths adrift in third, just edging out Rosberg in fourth, after recovering from a high-speed spin at Turn 8 during the session, while McLaren wound up fifth and sixth with Button getting the better of Hamilton.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Turkish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The first session started with <a href="/wiki/Kamui_Kobayashi" title="Kamui Kobayashi">Kamui Kobayashi</a> having a mechanical fault on his first flying lap, meaning that he was unable to post a qualifying time, but the race stewards ultimately decided to allow him to take part in the race.<sup id="cite_ref-Reps_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Reps-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> By the end of the session, both <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus_(2010%E2%80%9311)" title="Team Lotus (2010–11)">Lotuses</a> were eliminated, with <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> ending the session half a second behind <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a>'s Williams, who had been 17th. D'Ambrosio outqualified his teammate Glock on-track but with his grid penalty, he would start the race from last place. <a href="/wiki/Vitantonio_Liuzzi" title="Vitantonio Liuzzi">Vitantonio Liuzzi</a> also outqualified Glock, with <a href="/wiki/Narain_Karthikeyan" title="Narain Karthikeyan">Narain Karthikeyan</a> recording the slowest time, 1.7 seconds within the <a href="/wiki/107%25_rule" title="107% rule">107%</a> benchmark.
</p><p>The second session saw Vettel return to the top of the timesheets, recording the first instance of a sub-1:26 lap time all weekend. Rosberg also broke into the 1:25s as he ended the second part in second place ahead of Hamilton and Webber. A late lap from Nick Heidfeld saw him qualify for the final session at the expense of <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a>. <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a> outqualified his <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a> teammate <a href="/wiki/Paul_di_Resta" title="Paul di Resta">Paul di Resta</a> as they ended the session in twelfth and thirteenth places, with di Resta escaping a penalty for missing the weighbridge.<sup id="cite_ref-Reps_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Reps-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Maldonado and <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a> were next on the grid ahead of the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso</a> pair, with <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9bastien_Buemi" title="Sébastien Buemi">Sébastien Buemi</a> getting the better of <a href="/wiki/Jaime_Alguersuari" title="Jaime Alguersuari">Jaime Alguersuari</a> by three tenths.
</p><p>The third period saw the Red Bull cars only attempt a single run at the start of the session, in order to save tyres for the race, with Vettel ending the session over four tenths of a second clear from Webber; it was Vettel's fifth consecutive pole, the first driver to record five in succession since Fernando Alonso did so in <a href="/wiki/2006_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2006 Formula One World Championship">2006</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Rosberg finished third, moving ahead of Lewis Hamilton on his final run, with Alonso in fifth having not improved on his first run time. <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> qualified in sixth place for McLaren, followed by Vitaly Petrov in the Renault and Schumacher in the second Mercedes in eighth. Heidfeld and <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> completed the top ten, with Massa failing to record a time, abandoning his only run in Q3 due to a mistake and elected to save tyres for the race.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Turkish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>All the drivers started except for Timo Glock, who lost fifth gear prior to the race. Sebastian Vettel led the race from the start, with a lead of 6 seconds by the time he took his first pitstop on lap 10.<sup id="cite_ref-Race_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Race-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Behind him there were battles, with Fernando Alonso and Mark Webber fighting for second place in the closing laps, the victory falling to Webber giving Red Bull a one-two result.<sup id="cite_ref-Race_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Race-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Behind Webber and Alonso, Hamilton and Button fought for fourth place, with Hamilton getting the upper hand, and Nico Rosberg later slipping in between the pair, taking fifth from Button when Button's tyres were degrading massively on the last stint. Paul di Resta made a bad pit exit with a loose wheel, and retired from the race.
</p><p>The race, which had the most pitstops for a Grand Prix race ever (over 80) and the most overtaking moves since 1983,<sup id="cite_ref-f1_racing_report_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1_racing_report-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> extended Sebastian Vettel's lead in the world championship standings to 93 points to second placed Hamilton's 59. In the constructors' standings, Red Bull increased their tally to 148 points to McLaren's 105, with Ferrari in third place with 65.<sup id="cite_ref-f1_racing_report_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1_racing_report-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
