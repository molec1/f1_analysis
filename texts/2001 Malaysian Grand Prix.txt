<p>The <b>2001 Malaysian Grand Prix</b> (officially the <b>III Petronas Malaysian Grand Prix</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at the <a href="/wiki/Sepang_International_Circuit" title="Sepang International Circuit">Sepang International Circuit</a> in <a href="/wiki/Sepang_District" title="Sepang District">Sepang</a>, <a href="/wiki/Selangor" title="Selangor">Selangor</a>, Malaysia in front of a crowd of 75,000 spectators on 18 March 2001. It was the second round of the <a href="/wiki/2001_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2001 Formula One World Championship">2001 Formula One World Championship</a> and the third edition of the <a href="/wiki/Malaysian_Grand_Prix" title="Malaysian Grand Prix">event</a> as part of the series. The race was won from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> by <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>, driving for <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>. His teammate <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> finished second and <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>'s <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> was third.
</p><p>The race was delayed and shortened from 56 to 55 laps because <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a> team was out of position on the starting <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_motorsport_terms#G" title="Glossary of motorsport terms">grid</a>. When it did start, Michael Schumacher led the first two laps until he and Barrichello skidded on oil left by <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Panis" title="Olivier Panis">Olivier Panis</a>' <a href="/wiki/British_American_Racing" title="British American Racing">British American Racing</a> (BAR) car, as a tropical monsoon begin to fall. They ran into a <a href="/wiki/Run-off_area" title="Run-off area">gravel trap</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> took the lead for one lap until he and several drivers <a href="/wiki/Aquaplaning" title="Aquaplaning">aquaplaned</a> in the deteriorating weather. Coulthard led for 11 laps after a phase of <a href="/wiki/Pit_stop" title="Pit stop">pit stops</a> for <a href="/wiki/Rain_tyre" title="Rain tyre">wet-weather tyres</a> before Michael Schumacher passed him on lap 16. Schumacher led for the remainder of the race to take his second victory of the season and in Malaysia and the 46th of his career.
</p><p>Michael Schumacher's victory was his sixth in a row from pole position, bettering a record jointly held by former world champions <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alberto_Ascari" title="Alberto Ascari">Alberto Ascari</a>. The result left him ten <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Championship_points_scoring_systems" title="List of Formula One World Championship points scoring systems">points</a> clear of Barrichello and Coulthard, who were tied in second, in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a>. <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a> of Jordan moved past <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> for fourth. In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a>, Ferrari further extended its advantage over second-place McLaren while Sauber fell to fourth behind Jordan with fifteen races left in the season.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2001_Malaysian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Bird-eye_view_of_Sepang_F1_Circuit_-_panoramio.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c9/Bird-eye_view_of_Sepang_F1_Circuit_-_panoramio.jpg/220px-Bird-eye_view_of_Sepang_F1_Circuit_-_panoramio.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c9/Bird-eye_view_of_Sepang_F1_Circuit_-_panoramio.jpg/330px-Bird-eye_view_of_Sepang_F1_Circuit_-_panoramio.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c9/Bird-eye_view_of_Sepang_F1_Circuit_-_panoramio.jpg/440px-Bird-eye_view_of_Sepang_F1_Circuit_-_panoramio.jpg 2x" data-file-width="5152" data-file-height="3864" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Bird-eye_view_of_Sepang_F1_Circuit_-_panoramio.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The <a href="/wiki/Sepang_International_Circuit" title="Sepang International Circuit">Sepang International Circuit</a> <i>(pictured in 2016)</i>, where the race was held.</div></div></div>
<p>The 2001 Malaysian Grand Prix was the 2nd of the 17 races in the <a href="/wiki/2001_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2001 Formula One World Championship">2001 Formula One World Championship</a> and the 3rd edition of the <a href="/wiki/Malaysian_Grand_Prix" title="Malaysian Grand Prix">event</a> as part of the series. It was held at the 15-turn 5.543&#160;km (3.444&#160;mi) <a href="/wiki/Sepang_International_Circuit" title="Sepang International Circuit">Sepang International Circuit</a> in <a href="/wiki/Sepang_(town)" title="Sepang (town)">Sepang</a>, <a href="/wiki/Selangor" title="Selangor">Selangor</a> on 18 March.<sup id="cite_ref-CF1Race_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CF1Race-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> A race sanctioned by the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile</a> (FIA), motorsport's governing body, had been held in Malaysia since the 1960s, with the first editions being run in <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a>, then part of the Malaysian Federation, before moving to the <a href="/wiki/Shah_Alam_Circuit" title="Shah Alam Circuit">Shah Alam Circuit</a>. With the arrival of the Formula One Grand Prix in <a href="/wiki/1999_Malaysian_Grand_Prix" title="1999 Malaysian Grand Prix">1999</a>, the race was moved to the purpose-built Sepang International Circuit, where all editions were held until <a href="/wiki/2017_Malaysian_Grand_Prix" title="2017 Malaysian Grand Prix">2017</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After winning the season-opening <a href="/wiki/2001_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="2001 Australian Grand Prix">Australian Grand Prix</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> led the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a> with ten <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Championship_points_scoring_systems" title="List of Formula One World Championship points scoring systems">points</a>, ahead of <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>'s <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> with six and <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> in the second Ferrari with four. <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a> team was fourth with three points and <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a> was fifth on two points.<sup id="cite_ref-points_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a>, Ferrari led with 14 points; McLaren were in second on six points. Sauber was in third place with four points and Jordan in fourth had two points.<sup id="cite_ref-points_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The press and bookmakers considered Michael Schumacher the favourite to win the Malaysian Grand Prix, his team Ferrari had won every single running of the race up to that point: <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a> in 1999 and Schumacher in <a href="/wiki/2000_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2000 Formula One World Championship">2000</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Some of the media attention focussed on the possibility of Michael Schumacher taking his sixth consecutive victory, which would have made him the first driver since the two-time world champion <a href="/wiki/Alberto_Ascari" title="Alberto Ascari">Alberto Ascari</a> in <a href="/wiki/1952_Formula_One_season" title="1952 Formula One season">1952</a> to achieve the feat.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> He said he would not over relax after his victory in Melbourne: "Every year we say here at Ferrari that it is our best car ever and this time, like Rubens, I feel it is true. But we have experience and we know things can go up and down very quickly so we are not going to be complacent or over-confident. That is not our style."<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Following the death of <a href="/wiki/Motorsport_marshal" title="Motorsport marshal">track marshal</a> Graham Beveridge, who was struck in the chest by a wheel from the car of <a href="/wiki/British_American_Racing" title="British American Racing">British American Racing</a> (BAR) driver <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a> at the Australian Grand Prix,<sup id="cite_ref-F1TodayMarshals_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1TodayMarshals-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> the organisers of the Malaysian race worked to better protect trackside personnel and drivers; they did not request assistance from Formula One officials.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Circuit officials moved back the kerbs at turns five and six, and moved guard rails at turns two and 15 back by 2&#160;m (6.6&#160;ft). FIA race director and safety delegate <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Whiting" title="Charlie Whiting">Charlie Whiting</a> approved both modifications.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Safety marshals were urged by <a href="/wiki/Clerk_of_the_course" title="Clerk of the course">clerk of the course</a> to be extra attentive while observing vehicles and to maintain care for fellow marshals.<sup id="cite_ref-F1TodayMarshals_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1TodayMarshals-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> This came as executive director of the Malaysian Grand Prix and former driver <a href="/wiki/Philippe_Gurdjian" title="Philippe Gurdjian">Philippe Gurdjian</a> presented a six-point advisory plan to all 700 circuit personnel including track marshals, firefighters and paramedics to improve safety.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>All <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_car" title="Formula One car">cars</a> ran with the same rear wings as in Melbourne since the revised <a href="/wiki/Downforce" title="Downforce">downforce</a> regulations caused aerodynamicists to reclassify the Sepang International Circuit as a "high-downforce track".<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> After competing the season's first round without a spare vehicle, half of the personnel working for the <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a> team returned to its bases in Italy and the United Kingdom to assemble, test and transport a spare car to Malaysia so that its drivers <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tarso_Marques" title="Tarso Marques">Tarso Marques</a> had a back-up plan if either one had a problem.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Ferrari, McLaren and <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a> ran some aerodynamic modifications on their vehicles while Williams, Sauber, <a href="/wiki/Prost_Grand_Prix" title="Prost Grand Prix">Prost</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arrows_Grand_Prix_International" title="Arrows Grand Prix International">Arrows</a> and Minardi all ran to the same specification as in Australia. McLaren installed larger brake cooling ducts to their <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4-16" title="McLaren MP4-16">MP4/16s</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jaguar_Racing" title="Jaguar Racing">Jaguar</a> on their <a href="/wiki/Jaguar_R2" title="Jaguar R2">R2s</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice">Practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2001_Malaysian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Four practice sessions were held before the Sunday race—two on Friday, and two on Saturday. The Friday morning and afternoon sessions each lasted an hour. The third and final practice sessions on Saturday morning ran for 45 minutes.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Conditions for the two Friday practice sessions were clear, hot and humid with several cars struggling for <a href="/wiki/Grip_(auto_racing)" title="Grip (auto racing)">grip</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FIAFriday_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAFriday-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-SVFridayFP_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SVFridayFP-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello led the first session with a 1-minute 39.502 seconds lap, 0.621 seconds faster than his teammate Michael Schumacher in second. Jordan driver <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a>, Coulthard, Irvine, <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a> of Jordan, McLaren's <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Panis" title="Olivier Panis">Olivier Panis</a> for BAR, Jaguar's <a href="/wiki/Luciano_Burti" title="Luciano Burti">Luciano Burti</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> of Sauber followed in positions three to ten.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Williams driver <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a> did not complete a lap because of a <a href="/wiki/Fuel_pump_(engine)" class="mw-redirect" title="Fuel pump (engine)">fuel pump</a> failure and the Arrows car of <a href="/wiki/Jos_Verstappen" title="Jos Verstappen">Jos Verstappen</a> stopped at turn three with an engine sensor fault. His teammate <a href="/wiki/Enrique_Bernoldi" title="Enrique Bernoldi">Enrique Bernoldi</a> stopped at turn four with a <a href="/wiki/Clutch" title="Clutch">clutch</a> hydraulics problem about half a minute later.<sup id="cite_ref-FIAFriday_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAFriday-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the second practice session, Trulli recorded the day's fastest lap at 1 minute 38.846 seconds. He was 0.083 seconds ahead of Michael Schumacher in second. Barrichello, Coulthard, Irvine, Häkkinen, Frentzen, Burti, Panis and <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> of the Williams team rounded out the session's top ten drivers.<sup id="cite_ref-SVFridayFP_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SVFridayFP-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Early in the session, Frentzen confronted the Sauber team principal <a href="/wiki/Peter_Sauber" title="Peter Sauber">Peter Sauber</a>, who was angered at comments made by the former regarding the fast acceleration of his team's vehicles exiting the corners in the Australian Grand Prix. Both engaged in a verbal argument, which was ended when Jordan team owner <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Jordan" title="Eddie Jordan">Eddie Jordan</a> pulled Frentzen away from Peter Sauber.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Automotive_battery" title="Automotive battery">Battery</a> problems sidelined Montoya and two fuel pump failures limited Alonso's running to the final 20 minutes. His teammate Marques had an engine problem and Benetton's <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> set his first lap times after missing the first session due to an overheating <a href="/wiki/Transmission_(mechanics)" title="Transmission (mechanics)">gearbox</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FIAFriday_20-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAFriday-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It continued to be clear, hot and humid for the two Saturday morning practice sessions.<sup id="cite_ref-F1COMSatPractice_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1COMSatPractice-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> With a time of 1 minute 36.814 seconds, Coulthard was fastest in the third session, followed by Barrichello in second and his Ferrari teammate Michael Schumacher in third. Häkkinen, Ralf Schumacher, Heidfeld,. Frentzen, Montoya, Panis and Irvine were in positions four through ten.<sup id="cite_ref-SVSatPractice_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SVSatPractice-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> In the final session, Barrichello led with a lap of 1 minute 36.188 seconds. Second was Ralf Schumacher and Häkkinen third. Fourth was Michael Schumacher, Coulthard fifth and Frentzen improved to sixth. Trulli, Irvine, Panis and Sauber's <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> occupied seventh to tenth places.<sup id="cite_ref-SVSatPractice_26-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SVSatPractice-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Benetton's <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> ran into a <a href="/wiki/Run-off_area" title="Run-off area">gravel trap</a> beside the circuit and Alonso stopped with smoke billowing from the rear of his car because of debris accumulation overheating his engine.<sup id="cite_ref-F1COMSatPractice_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1COMSatPractice-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2001_Malaysian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Enrique_Bernoldi_2007_Curitiba.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f7/Enrique_Bernoldi_2007_Curitiba.jpg/170px-Enrique_Bernoldi_2007_Curitiba.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="195" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f7/Enrique_Bernoldi_2007_Curitiba.jpg/255px-Enrique_Bernoldi_2007_Curitiba.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f7/Enrique_Bernoldi_2007_Curitiba.jpg/340px-Enrique_Bernoldi_2007_Curitiba.jpg 2x" data-file-width="800" data-file-height="919" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Enrique_Bernoldi_2007_Curitiba.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Enrique_Bernoldi" title="Enrique Bernoldi">Enrique Bernoldi</a> <i>(pictured in 2007)</i> had all of his lap times deleted because his <a href="/wiki/Arrows_A22" title="Arrows A22">Arrows A22</a> was found to have an illegal front wing and bodywork parts on the step plane.</div></div></div>
<p>Saturday afternoon's one-hour qualifying session saw each driver limited to twelve laps, with the <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_motorsport_terms#G" title="Glossary of motorsport terms">grid</a> order decided by each's fastest lap. During this session, the <a href="/wiki/107%25_rule" title="107% rule">107% rule</a> was in effect, which necessitated each driver to set a time within 107 per cent of the quickest lap to qualify for the race.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Weather conditions were hot and sunny.<sup id="cite_ref-FIAQuali_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAQuali-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher completed two timed laps with new tyres and another two on used front wheels; he lost two-tenths of a second on his first attempt.<sup id="cite_ref-FIAQuali_28-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAQuali-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> He took his sixth consecutive pole position—becoming the first driver since Häkkinen in <a href="/wiki/1999_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1999 Formula One World Championship">1999</a> to achieve the feat—his third in Malaysia and the 34th of his career with a time of 1 minute 35.220 seconds.<sup id="cite_ref-DTQuali_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DTQuali-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-AutosportQuali_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportQuali-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> He was joined on the grid's front row by his teammate Barrichello who had lost time in the first two turns due to a strengthening wind.<sup id="cite_ref-QualiQuotes_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-QualiQuotes-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-SVQuali_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SVQuali-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Ralf Schumacher initially led and traded pole position with both Ferrari drivers before falling to third.<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportQuali_30-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportQuali-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Häkkinen had a car balance problem that left him in fourth.<sup id="cite_ref-FIAQuali_28-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAQuali-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Trulli in fifth had a lack of acclimatisation due to an electrical fault and could not fine-tune his <a href="/wiki/Racing_setup" title="Racing setup">car's setup</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DTQuali_29-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DTQuali-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-QualiQuotes_31-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-QualiQuotes-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Montoya, sixth, spun during the session, as Villeneuve, who said later he thought he drove too aggressively, took seventh.<sup id="cite_ref-QualiQuotes_31-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-QualiQuotes-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard, eighth, admitted to over-driving in the middle sector during his final attempt, losing him time. Excess <a href="/wiki/Understeer_and_oversteer" title="Understeer and oversteer">understeer</a> put Frentzen, who took ninth, off the track. Panis in tenth lost four-tenths of a second on his last timed lap.<sup id="cite_ref-FIAQuali_28-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAQuali-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-AutosportQuali_30-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportQuali-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Heidfeld was the fastest driver not to qualify in the top ten after driving on worn tyres on his first two timed laps and new front tyres on his third.<sup id="cite_ref-QualiQuotes_31-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-QualiQuotes-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> He was followed by Irvine in 12th, who had understeer going into the second corner and Villenueve slowed him into the final corner.<sup id="cite_ref-JagQuali_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JagQuali-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a> was the faster of the two Prost cars in 13th and spoke of his belief that the team had made progress from its pre-season testing form.<sup id="cite_ref-FIAQuali_28-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAQuali-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-DTQuali_29-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DTQuali-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> After a change of left-rear <a href="/wiki/Suspension_(vehicle)" class="mw-redirect" title="Suspension (vehicle)">suspension</a> wishbone, Räikkönen took 14th; he ran wide due to an understeer and was prevented from doing a fourth lap because he was called to the <a href="/wiki/Truck_scale" title="Truck scale">weighbridge</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FIAQuali_28-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAQuali-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-QualiQuotes_31-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-QualiQuotes-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> A light fuel road and a changed car balance put Burti 15th.<sup id="cite_ref-JagQuali_33-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JagQuali-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Fisichella switched to the spare <a href="/wiki/Benetton_B201" title="Benetton B201">B201</a> car setup for his teammate Button due to a minor chassis technical issue on his race car and he took 16th. An oversteer in the fast-speed corners before a setup alteration to improve his car's balance put Button 17th. A lack of engine power and understeer left Verstappen in 18th.<sup id="cite_ref-FIAQuali_28-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAQuali-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-AutosportQuali_30-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportQuali-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> His teammate Bernoldi was blocked by Räikkönen on his final timed lap and was 19th. <a href="/wiki/Gast%C3%B3n_Mazzacane" title="Gastón Mazzacane">Gastón Mazzacane</a> in the slower Prost car had his setup changed and improved his best time to go 20th-fastest.<sup id="cite_ref-DTQuali_29-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DTQuali-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-QualiQuotes_31-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-QualiQuotes-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Understeer on Marques' first two attempts and setup improvements to correct it put him 21st. His teammate Alonso could not drive his race car because its gearbox and engine was replaced. He took the spare Minardi and was 22nd.<sup id="cite_ref-FIAQuali_28-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAQuali-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-QualiQuotes_31-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-QualiQuotes-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Penalties">Penalties</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2001_Malaysian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Penalties">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>31 minutes into qualifying, the stewards deleted all of Bernoldi's lap times because the FIA technical delegate discovered the dimension of the <a href="/wiki/Arrows_A22" title="Arrows A22">Arrows A22</a>'s front wing and parts of its bodywork lying on the step plane were less than a respective 100&#160;mm (10&#160;cm) and 50&#160;mm (5.0&#160;cm) above the plane of reference.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> The penalty promoted Mazzacane to 19th, Marques to 20th, Alonso to 21st and dropped Bernoldi to 22nd.<sup id="cite_ref-StatsQuali_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-StatsQuali-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Warm-up">Warm-up</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2001_Malaysian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Warm-up">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A 30-minute warm-up session was held on Sunday morning, in hot and humid weather and on a saturated track created by a tropical thunderstorm, prompting most teams run the <a href="/wiki/Rain_tyre" title="Rain tyre">intermediate tyres</a> on their cars.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_19-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-AF1WarmUp_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1WarmUp-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> A dry line appeared towards the session's end and the top three runners set their fastest times this way.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello was fastest with a lap of 1 minute 49.763 seconds. He was followed by Coulthard, Trulli, Räikkonen, Verstappen, Frentzen, Michael Schumacher, Heidfeld, Panis and Villeneuve.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1WarmUp_37-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1WarmUp-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Alonso stopped on the start/finish straight with a fuel pump failure and he took the spare car originally setup for his teammate Marques. Irvine's engine cut out, causing him to drive the spare Jaguar. Fisichella spun twice and his teammate Button was observed driving on the dry compound tyres for three laps towards the end of warm-up.<sup id="cite_ref-FIAWarmUp_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAWarmUp-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2001_Malaysian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The race took place in the afternoon from 14:00 MST and in front of a crowd of 75,000 spectators.<sup id="cite_ref-F1LiveRace_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1LiveRace-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> The weather at the start was overcast but hot and humid.<sup id="cite_ref-F1RacingNetRace_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1RacingNetRace-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-F1COMRace_43-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1COMRace-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> The air temperature ranged from 32 to 33&#160;°C (90 to 91&#160;°F) and the track temperature 41 to 44&#160;°C (106 to 111&#160;°F);<sup id="cite_ref-F1RacingNetRace_42-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1RacingNetRace-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-AtlasF1Review_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AtlasF1Review-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> forecasts four days earlier predicted conditions to be consistent with sunny skies.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> Every driver, except for Burti, began on the soft compound tyres.<sup id="cite_ref-GPCOMRace_45-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPCOMRace-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> Five minutes before the <a href="/wiki/Pit_stop" title="Pit stop">pit lane</a> was officially closed, Michael Schumacher's race car had an oil leak,<sup id="cite_ref-F1RacingNetRace_42-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1RacingNetRace-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-GPCOMRace_45-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPCOMRace-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> prompting him to drive the spare Ferrari that was setup for his teammate Barrichello.<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportRace_46-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportRace-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> He avoided a pit lane start because he exited the area one minute before it closed.<sup id="cite_ref-F1COMRace_43-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1COMRace-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On the <a href="/wiki/Parade_lap" title="Parade lap">formation lap</a>, Frentzen's engine misfired due to a computer-hardware error that limited his <a href="/wiki/Rev_limiter" title="Rev limiter">rev limiter</a> to 4,000 rpm as he drove away from his starting slot.<sup id="cite_ref-AtlasF1Review_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AtlasF1Review-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-IndyRace_47-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IndyRace-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> Just before the race was due to start,<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportRace_46-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportRace-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> Fisichella was out of position because he missed his starting slot and was stranded in the middle of the grid.<sup id="cite_ref-AtlasF1Review_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AtlasF1Review-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He drove to his original starting position and was perpendicular across the track with not enough steering lock and rolled between the two cars ahead and behind him.<sup id="cite_ref-GPCOMRace_45-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPCOMRace-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-AutosportRace_46-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportRace-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> Because series regulations barred drivers from reversing, this resulted in an aborted start. A new start time of 14:10 MST was planned and the race was shortened from 56 to 55 laps since drivers were required to do a second formation lap.<sup id="cite_ref-F1RacingNetRace_42-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1RacingNetRace-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> During this lap Montoya ran to his garage to take the spare vehicle because his race car had an engine problem.<sup id="cite_ref-AtlasF1Review_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AtlasF1Review-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Barrichello_2002.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/36/Barrichello_2002.jpg/170px-Barrichello_2002.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="298" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/36/Barrichello_2002.jpg/255px-Barrichello_2002.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/36/Barrichello_2002.jpg/340px-Barrichello_2002.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1128" data-file-height="1975" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Barrichello_2002.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> <i>(pictured in 2002)</i>, who finished in second, had a 72-second pit stop due to a tyre mix-up early in the race.</div></div></div>
<p>Michael Schumacher, from the pole position, made a brisk getaway to maintain his startline advantage going into the first corner.<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportRace_46-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportRace-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> His teammate Barrichello made a slow start and fell from second to fourth as Trulli was to the left of him and Ralf Schumacher to his right.<sup id="cite_ref-AtlasF1Review_2-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AtlasF1Review-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Ralf Schumacher slid before turn one and he defended his position at the corner.<sup id="cite_ref-GPCOMRace_45-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPCOMRace-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> That caused the left-front corner of Barrichello's Ferrari and the rear of Ralf Schumacher's Williams car to connect; Schumacher spun 180 degrees in front of the pack, sending him to the rear of the field. In doing so, Irvine was sent into a half-spin by Verstappen as Räikkönen drove near the pit lane wall with a broken transmission.<sup id="cite_ref-GPCOMRace_45-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPCOMRace-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-CrashRace_48-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CrashRace-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> Elsewhere, Häkkinen slowed to avoid piling into Ralf Schumacher. He fell from fourth to eighth as his teammate Coulthard moved from eighth to fourth after going into the grass to avoid the incidents.<sup id="cite_ref-AtlasF1Review_2-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AtlasF1Review-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-GPCOMRace_45-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPCOMRace-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> Verstappen moved from 18th to sixth by the end of the first lap by driving on the inside,<sup id="cite_ref-:0_49-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> as Ralf Schumacher fell from third to 20th over the same distance. At the end of the first lap, Michael Schumacher led his teammate Barrichello in second by 1.3 seconds, with Trulli a further 1.8 seconds behind in third.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As the two Ferraris pulled away from the rest of the field on lap two, Panis' engine failed, pitching his car backwards into the gravel trap. Leaking oil pooled on the track and dripped onto his hot <a href="/wiki/Exhaust_system" title="Exhaust system">exhaust</a>, where it ignited.<sup id="cite_ref-GPCOMRace_45-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPCOMRace-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-AutosportRace_46-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportRace-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-IndyRace_47-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IndyRace-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> Fisichella overtook his teammate Button for 11th as Ralf Schumacher moved from 20th to 14th.<sup id="cite_ref-AtlasF1Review_2-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AtlasF1Review-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> An unexpected monsoon shower began to fall on lap three.<sup id="cite_ref-BBCWebRace_51-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCWebRace-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-StatsF1Race_52-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-StatsF1Race-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup> The two Ferraris continued to pull further away from the rest of the field, although they were unaware of Panis' deposited oil. Both drivers slid on the oil and into the gravel trap, and navigated back onto the track in third and seventh.<sup id="cite_ref-AtlasF1Review_2-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AtlasF1Review-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-GPCOMRace_45-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPCOMRace-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> Trulli took the lead with Coulthard in second. None of the leaders made pit stops to switch to wet-weather compound tyres immediately because half of the track was dry while the final <a href="/wiki/Hairpin_turn" title="Hairpin turn">hairpin</a> was flooded. On lap four, Montoya, Bernoldi, Heidfeld and Villeneuve <a href="/wiki/Aquaplaning" title="Aquaplaning">aquaplaned</a> into a gravel trap and retired. Race officials deployed the <a href="/wiki/Safety_car" title="Safety car">safety car</a> on the lap though several cars, including Trulli, Coulthard and Barrichello, aquaplaned into the turn 15 hairpin.<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportRace_46-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportRace-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During the safety car period, several drivers made pit stops for rain tyres. Although he had no radio communication with his team, Coulthard exited the pit lane leading while Ferrari had a fraught stop.<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportRace_46-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportRace-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> After Ferrari technical director <a href="/wiki/Ross_Brawn" title="Ross Brawn">Ross Brawn</a> called both Michael Schumacher and his teammate Barrichello for their first pit stops,<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportRace_46-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportRace-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher sat behind Barrichello for 1 minute and 12 seconds because the team was missing Barrichello's front-right wheel and debris had to be removed from his sidepods. The duo emerged in 10th and 11th. Rain stopped falling by lap seven although there was a large amount of <a href="/wiki/Water_stagnation" title="Water stagnation">standing water</a> as the safety car remained on track for the next three laps.<sup id="cite_ref-GPCOMRace_45-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPCOMRace-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-CrashRace_48-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CrashRace-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard led from Frentzen and Verstappen at the lap-11 restart.<sup id="cite_ref-F1LiveRace_40-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1LiveRace-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> He began to pull away from the rest of the field as Verstappen slipstreamed Frentzen on the back straight and out-braked him for second at the turn 15 hairpin.<sup id="cite_ref-F1RacingNetRace_42-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1RacingNetRace-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-F1COMRace_43-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1COMRace-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> On lap 12, Frenzten fell to fifth when Häkkinen and Ralf Schumacher passed him.<sup id="cite_ref-StatsF1Race_52-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-StatsF1Race-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello radioed Brawn about dirt on the track and that his teammate Michael Schumacher should take care.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_49-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> During the lap, Schumacher, on a light fuel load,<sup id="cite_ref-CrashRace_48-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CrashRace-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> went onto the dirt to pass Barrichello,<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> who went to the left of Trulli at turn five and was blocked by him.<sup id="cite_ref-GPCOMRace_45-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPCOMRace-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Michael Schumacher overtook Frentzen, Ralf Schumacher and Häkkinen off the <a href="/wiki/Racing_line" title="Racing line">racing line</a> on the 13th lap. He approached Verstappen in second on the next lap though Verstappen twice blocked his path.<sup id="cite_ref-GPCOMRace_45-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPCOMRace-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> Further back, his teammate Barrichello passed Ralf Schumacher and Häkkinen took fourth.<sup id="cite_ref-AtlasF1Review_2-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AtlasF1Review-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> On the 15th lap, Michael Schumacher overtook Verstappen on the inside for second and began to draw closer to Coulthard,<sup id="cite_ref-F1LiveRace_40-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1LiveRace-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> who drove with an understeer.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_49-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> He passed Coulthard on the right for the lead on the start/finish straight linking the final and first corners on lap 16.<sup id="cite_ref-GPCOMRace_45-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPCOMRace-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> That lap, Barrichello overtook Verstappen and Coulthard to advance to second as Michael Schumacher began to pull away by five seconds. It appeared at this point that Ferrari would win the race comfortably barring a mechanical issue. Häkkinen and Ralf Schumacher were the first two drivers to enter the pit lane and switch from the full wet compound tyres to the dry compound tyres on lap 17. Barrichello made his second stop four laps later, emerging in fourth. Coulthard fell to 40 seconds behind the Ferraris by the time of his second stop on lap 25. He rejoined the race in third, more than 20 seconds behind Barrichello.<sup id="cite_ref-AtlasF1Review_2-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AtlasF1Review-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Michael_Schumacher-I%27m_the_man_(cropped).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/Michael_Schumacher-I%27m_the_man_%28cropped%29.jpg/170px-Michael_Schumacher-I%27m_the_man_%28cropped%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="230" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/Michael_Schumacher-I%27m_the_man_%28cropped%29.jpg/255px-Michael_Schumacher-I%27m_the_man_%28cropped%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/Michael_Schumacher-I%27m_the_man_%28cropped%29.jpg/340px-Michael_Schumacher-I%27m_the_man_%28cropped%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1260" data-file-height="1704" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Michael_Schumacher-I%27m_the_man_(cropped).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> <i>(pictured in 2005)</i> led the majority of the race to better <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alberto_Ascari" title="Alberto Ascari">Alberto Ascari</a>'s joint record of five victories in a row from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> with his sixth.</div></div></div>
<p>In the meantime, Ralf Schumacher overtook Frentzen for fifth on lap 24 and Häkkinen for fourth four laps later.<sup id="cite_ref-AtlasF1Review_2-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AtlasF1Review-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> On lap 27 Mazzacane delayed Häkkinen, who was in the act of lapping him. When Häkkinen got past, Button followed through for 14th.<sup id="cite_ref-F1COMRace_43-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1COMRace-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-StatsF1Race_52-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-StatsF1Race-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup> Light rain began to fall again on lap 28 though it was not heavy enough to affect the race.<sup id="cite_ref-BBCWebRace_51-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCWebRace-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher led by 67 seconds when he made his second pit stop on lap 30 for fuel to complete the race and a front wing adjustment.<sup id="cite_ref-F1COMRace_43-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1COMRace-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-BBCWebRace_51-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCWebRace-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> He rejoined 28 seconds ahead of his teammate Barrichello in second. Further back, Verstappen lost fourth to Ralf Schumacher on lap 31. Fisichella in ninth became the race's final retiree with no fuel pressure at the pit lane entrance the lap after.<sup id="cite_ref-F1COMRace_43-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1COMRace-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-AtlasF1Review_2-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AtlasF1Review-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Around this time, Häkkinen duelled Verstappen for fifth though he could not pass and allowed Frentzen to gain on the pair. Verstappen defended fifth by out-braking Häkkinen at the turn 15 hairpin on lap 38; the manoeuvre put Häkkinen wide and allowed Frentzen to overtake him for sixth.<sup id="cite_ref-F1RacingNetRace_42-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1RacingNetRace-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-AtlasF1Review_2-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AtlasF1Review-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-CrashRace_48-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CrashRace-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello made his final pit stop three laps later and he remained in second.<sup id="cite_ref-F1LiveRace_40-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1LiveRace-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> Verstappen entered the pit lane for the third time on the 43rd lap to take on enough fuel to complete the race.<sup id="cite_ref-CrashRace_48-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CrashRace-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The track was entirely dry by lap 47 as Coulthard lowered the deficit behind Barrichello to 4.5 seconds. One lap later, Häkkinen set the race's overall <a href="/wiki/Fastest_lap" title="Fastest lap">fastest lap</a>, at 1 minute 40.962 seconds, as he pursued Ralf Schumacher in fifth.<sup id="cite_ref-BBCWebRace_51-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCWebRace-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> However, he did not have enough traction exiting the corners to pass Ralf Schumacher, whose more powerful engine gave him a straight-line speed advantage on the main straights, but emitted a large amount of carbon brake dust each time he braked into the heavy braking zones.<sup id="cite_ref-F1RacingNetRace_42-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1RacingNetRace-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-F1COMRace_43-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1COMRace-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher maintained the lead to take his second victory of 2001 and in Malaysia in a time of 1 hour, 47 minutes, 34.801 seconds, averaging 170.030&#160;km/h (105.652&#160;mph).<sup id="cite_ref-IHTRace_54-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IHTRace-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup> It was also the 46th of his career, and he broke <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a>'s and Ascari's joint record of five victories in a row from pole position with his sixth.<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportRace_46-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportRace-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello was 23.660 seconds behind in second and Coulthard came third.<sup id="cite_ref-AtlasF1Review_2-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AtlasF1Review-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Frentzen's two-stop strategy put him fourth despite locking rear wheels.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_49-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> Ralf Schumacher in fifth held off Häkkinen in sixth. Outside of the points-scoring positions, Verstappen, Trulli, Alesi, Burti, Button and Mazzacane followed in positions seven to twelve.<sup id="cite_ref-AtlasF1Review_2-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AtlasF1Review-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-AutosportRace_46-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportRace-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> The Minardi cars were the final two finishers, Alonso ahead of Marques; the latter was delayed by a right-rear <a href="/wiki/Flat_tire" title="Flat tire">puncture</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2001_Malaysian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The top three drivers appeared on the podium to collect their trophies and spoke to the media in a later press conference.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_19-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher called the race "exciting" and said it reminded him of his victory at the <a href="/wiki/1996_Spanish_Grand_Prix" title="1996 Spanish Grand Prix">1996 Spanish Grand Prix</a>, "It was probably the same story, but honestly it's great being somehow dominant, but we know that won't be the case for every coming race now. It was special conditions, special circumstances, and we used them well for us."<sup id="cite_ref-PressConf_56-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PressConf-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello said that he made an extra pit stop to have debris removed from his car, adding, "I think we have a good car, a fantastic car actually, the best car I have ever driven, like I have said. Once I came on the radio to say that I saw some bits of the car flying, and that's basically was some of the barge boards and maybe the brake duct or something."<sup id="cite_ref-PressConf_56-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PressConf-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard stated he was unsurprised at Ferrari's faster speed, "They clearly have a car advantage at this time, very similar to the sort of advantage we probably had in '98, and that allows you, if you make the right calls, to really drive at the pace you want to relative to others. So it's quite clear to everyone that they're the class of the field at the moment and we have a lot of work to do to catch up."<sup id="cite_ref-PressConf_56-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PressConf-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Ralf Schumacher said he felt the first lap collision with Barrichello prevented him from finishing on the podium and noted it was the second collision the latter had with a German driver in the past two races, "He should learn the rules. It is a racing accident but it difficult to understand how it happened because the car in front usually has the priority in the corner."<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello argued that Ralf Schumacher had not provided him with enough space to negotiate through, "If he comes around the outside, he leaves some room for a line. But I had no room or line and I had to let the car roll. He was in front and suddenly he shut the door and that was where we touched."<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Ron_Dennis" title="Ron Dennis">Ron Dennis</a>, the McLaren team principal, said he was certain the decision to run his cars on the wet tyres was correct, "Are we disappointed? Not really, but we are more frustrated. Today was one of those days when you have to make a decision and we made ours to go on wets and they (Ferrari) took a risk and went on intermediates."<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup> Brawn stated Ferrari had information from testing at the <a href="/wiki/Fiorano_Circuit" title="Fiorano Circuit">Fiorano Circuit</a> that the intermediate tyres operated efficiently in deep standing water, "We knew there were dry parts of the track and we knew there were wet parts of the track and we knew the tyres were very good in the morning [warm-up] as well. That was what we ran this morning. We knew it was very good and we knew it would go into the dry."<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Fisichella apologised to the Benetton team over the radio for missing his starting position that led to the start being aborted.<sup id="cite_ref-FisiLGDS_61-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FisiLGDS-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup> He said that he was focused on ensuring his car was setup to his liking and only saw the vehicle ahead of him. The Benetton team principal <a href="/wiki/Flavio_Briatore" title="Flavio Briatore">Flavio Briatore</a> did not reprimand Fisichella, but praised him for moving to seventh place before retiring.<sup id="cite_ref-FisiLGDS_61-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FisiLGDS-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup> The result extended Michael Schumacher's lead in the Drivers' Championship to 10 points over Barrichello and Coulthard, tied for second place. Frentzen moved past his compatriot Heidfeld for fourth.<sup id="cite_ref-points_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In the Constructors' Championship, Ferrari further increased their advantage over McLaren to 19 points while Jordan passed Sauber for third and Williams in fifth registered their first points of the season with Ralf Schumacher's fifth-place finish with fifteen races left in the season.<sup id="cite_ref-points_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
