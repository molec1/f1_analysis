<p>The <b>1980 Italian Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 14 September 1980 at the <a href="/wiki/Autodromo_Enzo_e_Dino_Ferrari" class="mw-redirect" title="Autodromo Enzo e Dino Ferrari">Autodromo Dino Ferrari</a> in <a href="/wiki/Imola" title="Imola">Imola</a>, Italy. It was the twelfth race of the 1980 World Championship of F1 Drivers and the 1980 International Cup for F1 Constructors.
</p><p>This was the first Formula One World Championship race to be held at Imola (the circuit having hosted the non-championship <a href="/wiki/1979_Dino_Ferrari_Grand_Prix" title="1979 Dino Ferrari Grand Prix">Dino Ferrari Grand Prix</a> the previous year). It was the only Italian Grand Prix since <a href="/wiki/1948_Italian_Grand_Prix" title="1948 Italian Grand Prix">1948</a> not to be held at <a href="/wiki/Autodromo_Nazionale_di_Monza" title="Autodromo Nazionale di Monza">Monza</a>, which was undergoing refurbishment at the time. The event would return to Monza in 1981, but Imola would remain on the F1 calendar until 2006, hosting the <a href="/wiki/San_Marino_Grand_Prix" title="San Marino Grand Prix">San Marino Grand Prix</a>.
</p><p>The race was held over 60 laps of the 5-kilometre (3.1&#160;mi) circuit for a total race distance of 300 kilometres (186.4&#160;mi).
</p><p>The race was won by Brazilian driver <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/Brabham_BT49" title="Brabham BT49">Brabham</a>-<a href="/wiki/Ford_Motor_Company" title="Ford Motor Company">Ford</a>. Piquet won by nearly half a minute from Australian driver <a href="/wiki/Alan_Jones_(racing_driver)" title="Alan Jones (racing driver)">Alan Jones</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW07" title="Williams FW07">Williams</a>-Ford, with Jones' Argentinian team-mate <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Reutemann" title="Carlos Reutemann">Carlos Reutemann</a> third. The win was Piquet's third of the season and second in succession, and it gave him the lead of the Drivers' Championship by one point from Jones.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Manfred_Winkelhock" title="Manfred Winkelhock">Manfred Winkelhock</a> made his debut, substituting for the injured <a href="/wiki/Jochen_Mass" title="Jochen Mass">Jochen Mass</a> at <a href="/wiki/Arrows_Grand_Prix_International" title="Arrows Grand Prix International">Arrows</a>. A collision with <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Lotus_81" title="Lotus 81">Lotus</a> during qualifying resulted in both drivers failing to make the grid. <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> debuted their first <a href="/wiki/Turbocharger" title="Turbocharger">turbo</a> car, the <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_126C" title="Ferrari 126C">126C</a>, but <a href="/wiki/Gilles_Villeneuve" title="Gilles Villeneuve">Gilles Villeneuve</a> started the race in his regular <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_312T" title="Ferrari 312T">312T5</a>. Regardless, after a heavy crash in practice at the flat-out right hander before Tosa, reigning World Champion <a href="/wiki/Jody_Scheckter" title="Jody Scheckter">Jody Scheckter</a> announced his retirement from the sport.
</p><p>The front row of the grid was occupied by the <a href="/wiki/Renault_RE20" title="Renault RE20">Renaults</a> of <a href="/wiki/Ren%C3%A9_Arnoux" title="René Arnoux">René Arnoux</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jean-Pierre_Jabouille" title="Jean-Pierre Jabouille">Jean-Pierre Jabouille</a>, although Piquet led by lap 4 and was never headed. On lap 6, Villeneuve crashed his Ferrari heavily at the corner which now bears his name, following a puncture. Villeneuve escaped unhurt, but the incident also caused the retirement of <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Giacomelli" title="Bruno Giacomelli">Bruno Giacomelli</a>, who ran over some of the debris in his <a href="/wiki/Alfa_Romeo_179" title="Alfa Romeo 179">Alfa Romeo</a>. Jones moved up to second on lap 29 after running as low as seventh. Behind Reutemann, <a href="/wiki/Elio_de_Angelis" title="Elio de Angelis">Elio de Angelis</a> finished fourth in his Lotus, with <a href="/wiki/Keke_Rosberg" title="Keke Rosberg">Keke Rosberg</a> fifth in his <a href="/wiki/Fittipaldi_Automotive" title="Fittipaldi Automotive">Fittipaldi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Didier_Pironi" title="Didier Pironi">Didier Pironi</a> sixth in his <a href="/wiki/Ligier_JS11" title="Ligier JS11">Ligier</a>.
</p><p>Jones' and Reutemann's podium finishes enabled the Williams team to secure their first Constructors' Championship with two races remaining.
</p>
