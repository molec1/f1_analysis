<p>The <b>2002 Belgian Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Spa-Francorchamps" title="Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps">Spa-Francorchamps</a> on 1 September 2002. It was the fourteenth race of the <a href="/wiki/2002_FIA_Formula_One_World_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="2002 FIA Formula One World Championship">2002 FIA Formula One World Championship</a>.
</p><p>The race was won by <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_F2002" title="Ferrari F2002">Ferrari</a>. Schumacher took <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>, led the whole race except during the pit stops, and set the fastest race lap. With the win, he became the first driver to win ten Formula One Grands Prix in a single season, surpassing the record jointly held by himself and <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a>. Team-mate <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> finished second, with <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a> third in a <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW24" title="Williams FW24">Williams</a>-<a href="/wiki/BMW_in_Formula_One" title="BMW in Formula One">BMW</a>.
</p><p>Two months after the race, it was announced that the Belgian Grand Prix would not be held in <a href="/wiki/2003_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2003 Formula One World Championship">2003</a> due to a row over tobacco advertising.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Spa would return to the F1 calendar in <a href="/wiki/2004_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2004 Formula One World Championship">2004</a>, however, when the Bus Stop chicane was modified significantly.
</p>
