<p>The <b>1994 San Marino Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>14° Gran Premio di San Marino</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 1 May 1994 at the <a href="/wiki/Autodromo_Enzo_e_Dino_Ferrari" class="mw-redirect" title="Autodromo Enzo e Dino Ferrari">Autodromo Enzo e Dino Ferrari</a>, located in <a href="/wiki/Imola" title="Imola">Imola</a>, Italy. It was the third race of the <a href="/wiki/1994_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1994 Formula One World Championship">1994 Formula One World Championship</a>.
</p><p>Fatalities and injuries at this Grand Prix proved to be a major turning point in both the 1994 season, and in the development of Formula One itself, particularly with regard to safety. The race weekend was marked by the deaths of Austrian driver <a href="/wiki/Roland_Ratzenberger" title="Roland Ratzenberger">Roland Ratzenberger</a> and of three-time world champion <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> in separate accidents. Other incidents saw driver <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> injured and several mechanics and spectators injured. They were the first fatalities in the Formula One World Championship since the <a href="/wiki/1982_Canadian_Grand_Prix" title="1982 Canadian Grand Prix">1982 Canadian Grand Prix</a>, and the first with two driver deaths since the <a href="/wiki/1960_Belgian_Grand_Prix" title="1960 Belgian Grand Prix">1960 Belgian Grand Prix</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>, driving for <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a>, won the race despite contact with <a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a> (who dropped to the back of the field and battled back to finish sixth). <a href="/wiki/Nicola_Larini" title="Nicola Larini">Nicola Larini</a>, driving for <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, scored the first points of his career when he achieved a podium finish in second position. <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> finished third in a <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>.
</p><p>The race led to an increased emphasis on safety in the sport as well as the reforming of the <a href="/wiki/Grand_Prix_Drivers%27_Association" title="Grand Prix Drivers&#39; Association">Grand Prix Drivers' Association</a> after a 12-year hiatus, and the changing of many track layouts and car designs. Since the race, numerous regulation changes have been made to slow Formula One cars down and new <a href="/wiki/Race_track" title="Race track">circuits</a> incorporate large run-off areas to slow cars before they collide with a wall. Senna was given a <a href="/wiki/State_funeral" title="State funeral">state funeral</a> in his home town of <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo" title="São Paulo">São Paulo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>, where around 500,000 people lined the streets to watch the coffin pass. Italian prosecutors charged six people with manslaughter in connection with <a href="/wiki/Death_of_Ayrton_Senna" title="Death of Ayrton Senna">Senna's death</a>, all of whom were later acquitted. The case took more than 11 years to conclude due to an appeal and a retrial following the original verdict of not guilty.
</p><p>As a result of increased standards in safety following this race, there was a 20-year gap between the deaths of Ratzenberger and Senna, and the crash of <a href="/wiki/Jules_Bianchi" title="Jules Bianchi">Jules Bianchi</a> at the <a href="/wiki/2014_Japanese_Grand_Prix" title="2014 Japanese Grand Prix">2014 Japanese Grand Prix</a> which led to his death the following year.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1994_San_Marino_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1994_San_Marino_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Heading into the third round of the season, <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> was leading the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">World Drivers' Championship</a> with 20 points; <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> was second on seven points, 13 points behind Schumacher. Behind Schumacher and Barrichello was <a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a> in third place on six points, tied on points with <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a>. Berger's teammate <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a> was fifth on four points.<sup id="cite_ref-points_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">World Constructors' Championship</a>, Benetton were leading on 20 points and Ferrari were second on ten points, with Jordan third on seven points.<sup id="cite_ref-points_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>There were two driver changes heading into the race. <a href="/wiki/JJ_Lehto" title="JJ Lehto">JJ Lehto</a> replaced <a href="/wiki/Jos_Verstappen" title="Jos Verstappen">Jos Verstappen</a> at Benetton, the latter having replaced Lehto for the opening two races of the season due to an injury sustained by Lehto in pre-season testing. Jordan's <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a>, still serving a three race suspension, was replaced with <a href="/wiki/Andrea_de_Cesaris" title="Andrea de Cesaris">Andrea de Cesaris</a> for this race.<sup id="cite_ref-report_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-report-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1994_San_Marino_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Friday">Friday</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1994_San_Marino_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Friday">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Rubens_Barrichello_(GP_San_Marino_1994)_-_01.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/19/Rubens_Barrichello_%28GP_San_Marino_1994%29_-_01.jpg/220px-Rubens_Barrichello_%28GP_San_Marino_1994%29_-_01.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="124" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/19/Rubens_Barrichello_%28GP_San_Marino_1994%29_-_01.jpg/330px-Rubens_Barrichello_%28GP_San_Marino_1994%29_-_01.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/19/Rubens_Barrichello_%28GP_San_Marino_1994%29_-_01.jpg/440px-Rubens_Barrichello_%28GP_San_Marino_1994%29_-_01.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1024" data-file-height="576" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Rubens_Barrichello_(GP_San_Marino_1994)_-_01.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> crashed heavily at <i>Variante Bassa</i> during the first qualifying session on Friday.</div></div></div>
<p>On Friday, 29 April, during the first qualifying session to determine the starting order for the race,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a>, a driver for Jordan, hit a kerb at the <i>Variante Bassa</i> corner at 225&#160;km/h (140&#160;mph), launching him into the air.<sup id="cite_ref-hamiltonbook_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hamiltonbook-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> He hit the top of the tyre barrier, and was knocked unconscious by an impact measured at <a href="/wiki/G-force" title="G-force">95 g</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello's car rolled several times after landing before coming to rest upside down. Medical teams treated him at the crash site after he swallowed his tongue, and he was taken to the circuit's medical centre before being transferred to <a href="/wiki/Ospedale_Maggiore_di_Bologna" title="Ospedale Maggiore di Bologna">Maggiore Hospital</a> in <a href="/wiki/Bologna" title="Bologna">Bologna</a> by helicopter for routine tests and observation to be carried out, Barrichello suffered a sprained wrist and broken nose, he did not continue for the rest of the weekend.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> He returned to the race meeting the next day, although his broken nose and a plaster cast on his arm forced him to sit out the rest of the race weekend.<sup id="cite_ref-review_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-review-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Ten years after the incident, <a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a>, who drove for the <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams-Renault</a> team at the time, described the feeling after the crash: "We all brushed ourselves off and carried on qualifying, reassured that our cars were tough as tanks and we could be shaken but not hurt."<sup id="cite_ref-damonhill_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-damonhill-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Despite a spin, Senna was the fastest driver at the end of Friday's session with a time of 1:21.548, almost five-tenths of a second faster than Schumacher and Berger. Senna's teammate <a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a> was seventh, having spun himself, over 1.6 seconds behind Senna.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Saturday_qualifying_and_death_of_Roland_Ratzenberger">Saturday qualifying and death of Roland Ratzenberger</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1994_San_Marino_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Saturday qualifying and death of Roland Ratzenberger">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Ratzenberger.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d8/Ratzenberger.jpg/200px-Ratzenberger.jpg" decoding="async" width="200" height="135" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d8/Ratzenberger.jpg/300px-Ratzenberger.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d8/Ratzenberger.jpg/400px-Ratzenberger.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3496" data-file-height="2363" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Ratzenberger.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Roland_Ratzenberger" title="Roland Ratzenberger">Roland Ratzenberger</a> was fatally injured in qualifying after crashing due to a front-wing failure.</div></div></div>
<p>Twenty minutes into the final qualifying session, Austrian driver <a href="/wiki/Roland_Ratzenberger" title="Roland Ratzenberger">Roland Ratzenberger</a> failed to negotiate the <i>Villeneuve curva</i> in his <a href="/wiki/Simtek" title="Simtek">Simtek</a>; he subsequently hit the opposing concrete barrier wall almost head-on and was critically injured. Although the survival cell remained largely intact, the force of the impact inflicted a <a href="/wiki/Basal_skull_fracture" class="mw-redirect" title="Basal skull fracture">basal skull fracture</a>. Ratzenberger, in his first season as a Formula One driver, had run over a kerb at the <i>Acque Minerali</i> chicane on his previous lap, the impact of which is believed to have damaged his front wing. Rather than return to the pitlane, he continued on another fast lap. Traveling at 314&#160;km/h (195&#160;mph), his car suffered a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_car#Wings" title="Formula One car">front wing</a> failure leaving him unable to control it.<sup id="cite_ref-herald_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-herald-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Roland_Ratzenberger_accident.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1a/Roland_Ratzenberger_accident.jpg/220px-Roland_Ratzenberger_accident.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="101" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1a/Roland_Ratzenberger_accident.jpg/330px-Roland_Ratzenberger_accident.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1a/Roland_Ratzenberger_accident.jpg/440px-Roland_Ratzenberger_accident.jpg 2x" data-file-width="710" data-file-height="326" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Roland_Ratzenberger_accident.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The aftermath of <a href="/wiki/Roland_Ratzenberger" title="Roland Ratzenberger">Roland Ratzenberger</a>'s fatal crash.</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:142px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Circuit_Imola_1992_Villeneuve_(vectorized).svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9e/Circuit_Imola_1992_Villeneuve_%28vectorized%29.svg/140px-Circuit_Imola_1992_Villeneuve_%28vectorized%29.svg.png" decoding="async" width="140" height="132" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9e/Circuit_Imola_1992_Villeneuve_%28vectorized%29.svg/210px-Circuit_Imola_1992_Villeneuve_%28vectorized%29.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9e/Circuit_Imola_1992_Villeneuve_%28vectorized%29.svg/280px-Circuit_Imola_1992_Villeneuve_%28vectorized%29.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="516" data-file-height="485" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Circuit_Imola_1992_Villeneuve_(vectorized).svg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The Villeneuve kink, location of Ratzenberger's fatal crash.</div></div></div><p>The session was stopped while doctors attended to Ratzenberger. After initially being taken by ambulance to the on-circuit medical centre, he was airlifted to Maggiore Hospital shortly after, the second driver to be admitted there during the weekend. The session was restarted approximately 25&#160;minutes later, but several teams—including Williams and Benetton—took no further part.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Later in hospital, it was announced that Ratzenberger had died as a result of his multiple injuries. His death marked the first Formula One race weekend fatality since the <a href="/wiki/1982_Canadian_Grand_Prix" title="1982 Canadian Grand Prix">1982 Canadian Grand Prix</a> when <a href="/wiki/Riccardo_Paletti" title="Riccardo Paletti">Riccardo Paletti</a> was killed. It had been eight years since <a href="/wiki/Elio_de_Angelis" title="Elio de Angelis">Elio de Angelis</a> died testing a <a href="/wiki/Brabham" title="Brabham">Brabham</a> car at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_Paul_Ricard" title="Circuit Paul Ricard">Circuit Paul Ricard</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-review_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-review-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Professor <a href="/wiki/Sid_Watkins" title="Sid Watkins">Sid Watkins</a>, then head of the Formula One on-track medical team, recalled in his memoirs <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a>'s reaction to the news, stating that "Ayrton broke down and cried on my shoulder."<sup id="cite_ref-Hamilton234_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hamilton234-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Watkins tried to persuade Senna not to race the following day, asking "What else do you need to do? You have been world champion three times, you are obviously the quickest driver. Give it up and let's go fishing." Senna replied, "Sid, there are certain things over which we have no control. I cannot quit, I have to go on."<sup id="cite_ref-Hamilton234_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hamilton234-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Senna had qualified on <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>, having not set a lap time following Ratzenberger's death.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> He was joined on the front row by Schumacher and Berger qualified in 3rd. Damon Hill was able to improve on his disastrous Friday session before the red flag, improving his time by one second and qualifying fourth as a result. A time posted by Ratzenberger before his fatal crash would have been sufficient for entry into the race starting from the 26th and final position on the grid. Two drivers did not qualify: Pacific's <a href="/wiki/Paul_Belmondo" title="Paul Belmondo">Paul Belmondo</a>, and the injured Barrichello, who was unable to race.<sup id="cite_ref-report_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-report-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/ESPN_SpeedWorld" title="ESPN SpeedWorld">ESPN SpeedWorld</a>'s race coverage on race day reported that Belmondo was offered the final position on the grid, but that he declined to take it out of respect for Ratzenberger, and on the grounds that he had not earned that race spot and Ratzenberger had.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><br />
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_day">Race day</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1994_San_Marino_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Race day">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span id="Ratzenberger_fatal_accident_aftermath.2C_drivers.27_meeting.2C_GPDA_reformation"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Ratzenberger_fatal_accident_aftermath,_drivers'_meeting,_GPDA_reformation">Ratzenberger fatal accident aftermath, drivers' meeting, GPDA reformation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1994_San_Marino_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Ratzenberger fatal accident aftermath, drivers&#039; meeting, GPDA reformation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Ratzenberger's death the previous day had one lasting legacy. On 1 May 1994, during the customary drivers' briefing, the remaining drivers agreed to the reformation of the <a href="/wiki/Grand_Prix_Drivers%27_Association" title="Grand Prix Drivers&#39; Association">Grand Prix Drivers' Association</a>, with Senna, <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> and <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> as its first directors. The association subsequently pressed for improvements to car and circuit safety in the wake of Imola and other serious crashes during the 1994 season; for <a href="/wiki/2003_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2003 Formula One World Championship">2003</a>, the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">FIA</a> mandated the use of the <a href="/wiki/HANS_device" title="HANS device">HANS device</a>, designed to prevent the type of injury suffered by Ratzenberger.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="First_start_crash">First start crash</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1994_San_Marino_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: First start crash">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Racing_accident_at_1994_Imola_start_(J.J._Lehto_and_Pedro_Lamy).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/25/Racing_accident_at_1994_Imola_start_%28J.J._Lehto_and_Pedro_Lamy%29.jpg/220px-Racing_accident_at_1994_Imola_start_%28J.J._Lehto_and_Pedro_Lamy%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="117" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/25/Racing_accident_at_1994_Imola_start_%28J.J._Lehto_and_Pedro_Lamy%29.jpg/330px-Racing_accident_at_1994_Imola_start_%28J.J._Lehto_and_Pedro_Lamy%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/25/Racing_accident_at_1994_Imola_start_%28J.J._Lehto_and_Pedro_Lamy%29.jpg/440px-Racing_accident_at_1994_Imola_start_%28J.J._Lehto_and_Pedro_Lamy%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="600" data-file-height="318" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Racing_accident_at_1994_Imola_start_(J.J._Lehto_and_Pedro_Lamy).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Pedro_Lamy" title="Pedro Lamy">Pedro Lamy</a> ramming his <a href="/wiki/Lotus_107C" class="mw-redirect" title="Lotus 107C">Lotus 107C</a> in the back of <a href="/wiki/JJ_Lehto" title="JJ Lehto">JJ Lehto</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Benetton_B194" title="Benetton B194">Benetton B194</a>.</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Pedro_Lamy_at_1994_San_Marino_Gran_Prix.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/29/Pedro_Lamy_at_1994_San_Marino_Gran_Prix.jpg/220px-Pedro_Lamy_at_1994_San_Marino_Gran_Prix.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="196" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/29/Pedro_Lamy_at_1994_San_Marino_Gran_Prix.jpg/330px-Pedro_Lamy_at_1994_San_Marino_Gran_Prix.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/29/Pedro_Lamy_at_1994_San_Marino_Gran_Prix.jpg/440px-Pedro_Lamy_at_1994_San_Marino_Gran_Prix.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1200" data-file-height="1071" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Pedro_Lamy_at_1994_San_Marino_Gran_Prix.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Pedro_Lamy" title="Pedro Lamy">Pedro Lamy</a> leaving his wrecked <a href="/wiki/Lotus_107C" class="mw-redirect" title="Lotus 107C">Lotus 107C</a> after hitting <a href="/wiki/JJ_Lehto" title="JJ Lehto">JJ Lehto</a>'s stalled <a href="/wiki/Benetton_B194" title="Benetton B194">Benetton B194</a>, causing an early safety car.</div></div></div>
<p>The race took place in the afternoon from 14:00pm <a href="/wiki/Central_European_Summer_Time" title="Central European Summer Time">CEST</a> (<a href="/wiki/UTC" class="mw-redirect" title="UTC">UTC</a>+2), in dry and sunny weather. <a href="/wiki/Michele_Alboreto" title="Michele Alboreto">Michele Alboreto</a> was supposed to start 15th but started from the pitlane in the spare <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a> due to his race car developing a problem. At the start of the race, <a href="/wiki/JJ_Lehto" title="JJ Lehto">JJ Lehto</a> stalled his Benetton on the grid. <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Lamy" title="Pedro Lamy">Pedro Lamy</a>, starting from further back on the grid, had his view of the stationary Benetton blocked by other cars and his <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Lotus</a> hit the back of Lehto's Benetton, causing bodywork and tyres to fly into the air. Parts of the car went over the safety fencing designed to protect spectators at the startline causing minor injuries to a <a href="/wiki/Police_officer" title="Police officer">police officer</a> and eight spectators.<sup id="cite_ref-Times_tragic_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Times_tragic-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Further back, <a href="/wiki/Martin_Brundle" title="Martin Brundle">Martin Brundle</a> had a good start, overtaking two cars as well as Lehto, propelling him from thirteenth to tenth place.
</p><p>The incident between Lehto and Lamy caused the <a href="/wiki/Safety_car" title="Safety car">safety car</a> to be deployed, with all the remaining competitors holding position behind it while travelling at a reduced speed.<sup id="cite_ref-report_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-report-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> During this period, as a result of travelling at slower speeds, tyre temperatures dropped. At the drivers' briefing before the race, Senna, along with <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a>, had expressed concern that the safety car (itself only reintroduced in Formula One in 1993 and only the third time used since then, the other occurrences being the <a href="/wiki/1993_Brazilian_Grand_Prix" title="1993 Brazilian Grand Prix">1993 Brazilian Grand Prix</a> and the <a href="/wiki/1993_British_Grand_Prix" title="1993 British Grand Prix">1993 British Grand Prix</a>) did not go fast enough to keep tyre temperatures high.<sup id="cite_ref-Times_tragic_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Times_tragic-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Senna was also worried by a procedure introduced at the <a href="/wiki/1994_Pacific_Grand_Prix" title="1994 Pacific Grand Prix">1994 Pacific Grand Prix</a>, whereby the safety car would lead the grid on the <a href="/wiki/Formation_lap" class="mw-redirect" title="Formation lap">formation lap</a>, rather than letting the race leader choose the pace of the formation lap.<sup id="cite_ref-LDRA_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LDRA-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> The procedure was removed for this race. The safety car chosen for the event, an <a href="/wiki/Opel_Vectra" title="Opel Vectra">Opel Vectra</a>, traveled very slowly on the track, even when the reduced speeds of a safety car period were factored in, and Senna pulled alongside it several times, urging the driver to increase his speed. It was later learned the car's brakes had been overwhelmed and started fading on the first lap, and thus the driver had to reduce his speed to avoid the possibility of the safety car itself causing an accident.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> During the safety car, <a href="/wiki/%C3%89rik_Comas" title="Érik Comas">Érik Comas</a> and <a href="/wiki/%C3%89ric_Bernard" title="Éric Bernard">Éric Bernard</a> made contact such that Comas' car experienced a vibration. Comas pitted to have the problem evaluated by his <a href="/wiki/Larrousse" title="Larrousse">Larrousse</a> pit crew.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span id="Restart.2C_Senna_crash_and_red_flag"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Restart,_Senna_crash_and_red_flag">Restart, Senna crash and red flag</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1994_San_Marino_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Restart, Senna crash and red flag">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Death_of_Ayrton_Senna" title="Death of Ayrton Senna">Death of Ayrton Senna</a></div>

<p>Once the track was reported clear of debris, the safety car was withdrawn and the race restarted on lap five. <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Palmer" title="Jonathan Palmer">Jonathan Palmer</a>, commentating alongside <a href="/wiki/Murray_Walker" title="Murray Walker">Murray Walker</a> for the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a>, remarked how quick Schumacher was, as his time in the warm-up session on Sunday morning gave rise to speculation that he was going to make one pit stop and, therefore, race with a heavier car than Senna, who was planning to make two, as was conventional. Martin Brundle had told BBC <i><a href="/wiki/Grandstand_(TV_programme)" title="Grandstand (TV programme)">Grandstand</a></i> presenter <a href="/wiki/Steve_Rider" title="Steve Rider">Steve Rider</a> that <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> were going to make two stops. 
</p><p>On the 7th lap, the second lap at racing speeds after the stoppage, Senna was leading Michael Schumacher. Going at a speed of 305&#160;km/h (190&#160;mph), Senna's car could not take the turn at the Tamburello left corner, and, continuing in a straight line, struck the unprotected, concrete barrier that carried an advertising banner for <i>Pilotissimi</i>, the <a href="/wiki/Trading_card" title="Trading card">collectible cards</a> of racing drivers, at a speed of 211&#160;km/h (131&#160;mph), reduced as a result of the driver's effort to brake.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> As was subsequently ascertained, Senna was killed instantly.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> When track officials examined the wreckage of Senna's racing car, they found a furled Austrian flag. Senna had planned to raise it after the race, in honour of Ratzenberger.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At 14:17 local time, a red flag was shown to indicate the race was stopped and FIA race doctor <a href="/wiki/Sid_Watkins" title="Sid Watkins">Sid Watkins</a> arrived at the scene to treat Senna. When a race is stopped under a red flag, cars must slow down and make their way back to the pit lane or starting grid unless notified of a restart. This protects <a href="/wiki/Marshal#Racing_and_other_competitions" title="Marshal">race marshals</a> and medical staff at the crash scene, and allows easier access for medical cars to the incident. Approximately 10 minutes after Senna's crash, the <a href="/wiki/Larrousse" title="Larrousse">Larrousse</a> team, which had been "concentrating on fixing [Comas's] car and didn't realise Senna had crashed",<sup id="cite_ref-:0_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> sent Comas to the end of the pit lane for release despite the circuit being closed under red flags.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Comas described "a big confusion about whether (he) could rejoin", and that eventually the pit lane marshal allowed him onto the race course.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_6-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Marshals frantically waved him down as he approached the scene of the accident travelling at close to full speed.<sup id="cite_ref-Eurosport_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Eurosport-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Eurosport commentator <a href="/wiki/John_Watson_(racing_driver)" title="John Watson (racing driver)">John Watson</a> described the incident as "the most ridiculous thing I have ever seen at any time in my life".<sup id="cite_ref-Eurosport_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Eurosport-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Comas avoided hitting any of the people or cars that were on the circuit, but after seeing Senna's accident scene, Comas was so distressed that he withdrew from the race, and didn't speak of what he witnessed for more than 10 years.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_6-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The pictures shown on the world feed, supplied by host broadcaster <a href="/wiki/RAI" title="RAI">RAI</a>, of Senna being treated were considered by the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a>, the corporation responsible for broadcasting the San Marino Grand Prix live to viewers in the United Kingdom, to be too upsetting for general viewing at the time (around 13:20 GMT), and the BBC abandoned RAI's feed to focus on their own camera in the pit lane.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> BBC commentator <a href="/wiki/Murray_Walker" title="Murray Walker">Murray Walker</a> has frequently talked about how upsetting it was to have to talk to viewers whilst avoiding mentioning the images shown on RAI. Referring to the number of times the incident was replayed on the world feed, Ferrari team principal Jean Todt stated that "even if you didn't want to watch it, you could barely fail to".<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Waiting on pit lane, Martin Brundle reported that shortly after Senna's crash, televisions in garages were being switched off, but that reports were that Senna was okay.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_6-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Senna was lifted from the wrecked Williams, and after approximately fifteen minutes of on-site medical attention, was airlifted directly to Maggiore Hospital, becoming the third and final driver to be admitted there during the weekend. Medical teams continued to treat him during the flight. Thirty-seven minutes after Senna's fatal crash, at 2:55&#160;pm local time, the race was restarted.<sup id="cite_ref-restarted_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-restarted-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Second_start">Second start</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1994_San_Marino_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Second start">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Podium_of_1994_San_Marino_Gran_Prix.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a8/Podium_of_1994_San_Marino_Gran_Prix.jpg/220px-Podium_of_1994_San_Marino_Gran_Prix.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="161" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a8/Podium_of_1994_San_Marino_Gran_Prix.jpg/330px-Podium_of_1994_San_Marino_Gran_Prix.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a8/Podium_of_1994_San_Marino_Gran_Prix.jpg/440px-Podium_of_1994_San_Marino_Gran_Prix.jpg 2x" data-file-width="736" data-file-height="540" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Podium_of_1994_San_Marino_Gran_Prix.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Nicola_Larini" title="Nicola Larini">Nicola Larini</a>, Michael Schumacher and Mika Häkkinen (left to right) at the podium.</div></div></div>
<p>The race was restarted from the beginning of lap 6. The first five laps would be added to the second part of the race and the overall result would be decided on aggregate. The race ran to a total of 58 laps, five from the first section and 53 from the second section.
</p><p>On the second formation lap, <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a> stalled his Sauber whilst attempting to leave the grid and was forced to start from the pitlane. The other cars started from the grid in the order they were at the point the race was stopped. Michael Schumacher had a poor start and Gerhard Berger took the lead on track (but Schumacher still led the race overall due to the amount of time he was ahead of Berger before the race was stopped). Hill, from third, made contact attempting to overtake Schumacher at the Tosa corner, dropping Hill to the back of the field and was forced to make a pitstop in order to fit a new nose cone. Hill battled back to finish in sixth position.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Schumacher took the lead on track on lap 12 when Berger ran wide, before relinquishing the race lead overall to Berger when he made his first pitstop, confirming that his pace both before and after the red flag was down to him running a three-stop strategy, therefore racing with a lighter car. Berger pitted at the end of lap 15 for his first of two scheduled stops, before retiring a lap later with handling problems. Häkkinen led his first ever laps of a Formula One World Championship race, before pitting at the end of lap 18. Following the first series of pit stops, Schumacher resumed the race lead on aggregate, overtaking <a href="/wiki/Nicola_Larini" title="Nicola Larini">Nicola Larini</a> on track at Tosa. Schumacher's extra pace as a result of his lighter fuel loads meant he was able to pull out enough of a gap to Häkkinen which enabled him to make an extra pit stop. Häkkinen's pace was very slow, allowing Larini to leapfrog him when the two-stoppers made their final pit stops.
</p><p>On lap 48, <a href="/wiki/Michele_Alboreto" title="Michele Alboreto">Michele Alboreto</a> came in for a pit stop, but as he left, the rear-right wheel came loose from the Minardi as it left the pit lane, striking two Ferrari and two Lotus mechanics, who were left needing hospital treatment.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Christian_Fittipaldi" title="Christian Fittipaldi">Christian Fittipaldi</a>, battling for 5th with <a href="/wiki/Ukyo_Katayama" title="Ukyo Katayama">Ukyo Katayama</a>'s Tyrrell &amp; <a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a>'s Williams, would eventually retire his Footwork with brake problems on lap 55.
</p><p>Towards the end of the race, Häkkinen's pace was so slow that <a href="/wiki/Karl_Wendlinger" title="Karl Wendlinger">Karl Wendlinger</a> was catching him in the <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a>, aiming to give Sauber their first podium finish. However, Häkkinen was able to resist Wendlinger's challenge and finish in third place, with Wendlinger fourth. <a href="/wiki/Ukyo_Katayama" title="Ukyo Katayama">Ukyo Katayama</a> finished fifth for <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_Racing" title="Tyrrell Racing">Tyrrell</a> and Hill was able to battle back to finish sixth, the last of the points-scorers. Michael Schumacher won the race ahead of Larini and Häkkinen, giving him a maximum 30 points after 3 rounds of the <a href="/wiki/1994_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="1994 Formula One season">1994 Formula One season</a>. It was the only podium finish of Larini's career, and the first of just two occasions when he scored world championship points. <a href="/wiki/Karl_Wendlinger" title="Karl Wendlinger">Karl Wendlinger</a> rode back to the pits on Häkkinen's McLaren after Wendlinger's car broke down on the slowing-down lap. At the podium ceremony, out of respect for Roland Ratzenberger, who had died the day before, no champagne was sprayed.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_6-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1994_San_Marino_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the press conference following the race, Schumacher said that he "can't feel satisfied, can't feel happy" with his win following the events that had occurred during the race weekend. Two hours and 20 minutes after Schumacher crossed the finish line, at 6:40&#160;pm local time, Dr. Maria Teresa Fiandri announced that Ayrton Senna had died. The official time of death was given, however, as 2:17&#160;pm local time, meaning that Senna had been killed instantly.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> The autopsy recorded the cause of death as head injuries likely caused by an impact from a wheel and suspension.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/BBC_Television" title="BBC Television">BBC Television</a> commentator <a href="/wiki/Murray_Walker" title="Murray Walker">Murray Walker</a> described it as "the blackest day for Grand Prix racing that I can remember".<sup id="cite_ref-BBConthisday_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBConthisday-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 3 May, the FIA called a meeting at the request of the Italian Automobile Club to review the events of the Weekend.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Later on, the governing body announced new safety measures for the next round in <a href="/wiki/1994_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="1994 Monaco Grand Prix">Monaco</a> which included the entry and exit of the pitlane to be controlled by a curve to force cars to run at a reduced speed, no team mechanic would be allowed onto the pit lane surface except for pit stops and a draw would be arranged to determine the order in which cars make pit stops and be limited to emergencies with cars not taking on new tyres or allowed to refuel.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Senna was given a <a href="/wiki/State_funeral" title="State funeral">state funeral</a> in São Paulo, Brazil on 5 May 1994. Approximately 500,000 people lined the streets to watch the coffin pass.<sup id="cite_ref-BBConthisday_32-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBConthisday-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Senna's rival <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> was among the pallbearers.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> The majority of the Formula One community attended Senna's funeral; however the president of the sport's governing body, the FIA, <a href="/wiki/Max_Mosley" title="Max Mosley">Max Mosley</a> attended the funeral of Ratzenberger instead which took place on 7 May 1994 in <a href="/wiki/Salzburg" title="Salzburg">Salzburg</a>, Austria.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Drivers Gerhard Berger and Johnny Herbert were present as well.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_6-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Mosley said in a press conference ten years later, "I went to (Ratzenberger's) funeral because everyone went to Senna's. I thought it was important that somebody went to his."<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:GrandPrix_Circuit_San_Marino_Changes.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cb/GrandPrix_Circuit_San_Marino_Changes.svg/220px-GrandPrix_Circuit_San_Marino_Changes.svg.png" decoding="async" width="220" height="178" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cb/GrandPrix_Circuit_San_Marino_Changes.svg/330px-GrandPrix_Circuit_San_Marino_Changes.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cb/GrandPrix_Circuit_San_Marino_Changes.svg/440px-GrandPrix_Circuit_San_Marino_Changes.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="518" data-file-height="419" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:GrandPrix_Circuit_San_Marino_Changes.svg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The layout of the circuit was changed after the two fatal accidents at the 1994 event.</div></div></div>
<p>The 1994 Imola layout, which had been in place since 1981,<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> was never again used for a Formula One race. The circuit was heavily modified following the race, including a change at Tamburello—also the scene of major accidents for <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> (<a href="/wiki/1989_San_Marino_Grand_Prix" title="1989 San Marino Grand Prix">1989</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a> (<a href="/wiki/1987_San_Marino_Grand_Prix" title="1987 San Marino Grand Prix">1987</a>)—from a high speed corner to a much slower <a href="/wiki/Chicane" title="Chicane">chicane</a>. The FIA also changed the regulations governing Formula One car design, to the extent that the 1995 regulations required all teams to create completely new designs, as their 1994 cars could not be adapted to them.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> The concern raised at the drivers briefing the morning of the race, by Senna and Berger, would lead to the <a href="/wiki/Grand_Prix_Drivers%27_Association" title="Grand Prix Drivers&#39; Association">Grand Prix Drivers' Association</a> reforming at the following race, the <a href="/wiki/1994_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="1994 Monaco Grand Prix">1994 Monaco Grand Prix</a>. The GPDA, which was founded in 1961, had previously disbanded in 1982. The primary purpose of it reforming was to allow drivers to discuss safety issues with a view to improve standards following the incidents at Imola. The front two grid slots at the Monaco Grand Prix that year, which were painted with Brazilian and Austrian flags, were left clear in memory of the two drivers who had lost their lives, while both Williams and Simtek entered only one car each. Additionally, a minute of silence was observed before the race.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Severe injuries to F1 drivers in May 1994 did not end with the San Marino Grand Prix. Two weeks after Imola, <a href="/wiki/Karl_Wendlinger" title="Karl Wendlinger">Karl Wendlinger</a> suffered a shunt in practice at Monaco that left him comatose for several weeks with brain injuries and ended his 1994 season. Pedro Lamy suffered season ending broken bone injuries in a crash during a testing session at Silverstone on 24 May.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In October 1996 FIA set about researching a driver restraint system for head-on impacts, in conjunction with McLaren and Mercedes-Benz. Mercedes contacted the makers of the <a href="/wiki/HANS" class="mw-redirect" title="HANS">HANS</a> (Head and Neck Support) device, with a view to adapting it for Formula One. The HANS device was first released in 1991 and was designed to restrain the head and neck in the event of an accident to avoid basal skull fracture, the injury which killed Ratzenberger. Initial tests proved successful, and at the <a href="/wiki/2000_San_Marino_Grand_Prix" title="2000 San Marino Grand Prix">2000 San Marino Grand Prix</a> the final report was released which concluded that the HANS should be recommended for use. Its use was made compulsory from the start of the 2003 season.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Senna was the last driver for twenty years to die in a Formula One accident, until the death of <a href="/wiki/Jules_Bianchi" title="Jules Bianchi">Jules Bianchi</a> in 2015 from injuries sustained at the <a href="/wiki/2014_Japanese_Grand_Prix" title="2014 Japanese Grand Prix">2014 Japanese Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> However, three trackside marshals were killed during those years as a direct result of such crashes: <a href="/wiki/Paolo_Gislimberti" title="Paolo Gislimberti">Paolo Gislimberti</a> at the <a href="/wiki/2000_Italian_Grand_Prix" title="2000 Italian Grand Prix">2000 Italian Grand Prix</a>, Graham Beveridge at the <a href="/wiki/2001_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="2001 Australian Grand Prix">2001 Australian Grand Prix</a> and Mark Robinson at the <a href="/wiki/2013_Canadian_Grand_Prix" title="2013 Canadian Grand Prix">2013 Canadian Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Trial">Trial</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1994_San_Marino_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Trial">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Death_of_Ayrton_Senna" title="Death of Ayrton Senna">Death of Ayrton Senna</a></div>
<p>Italian prosecutors brought legal proceedings against six people in connection with <a href="/wiki/Death_of_Ayrton_Senna" title="Death of Ayrton Senna">Senna's death</a>. They were <a href="/wiki/Frank_Williams_(Formula_One)" title="Frank Williams (Formula One)">Frank Williams</a>, <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Head" title="Patrick Head">Patrick Head</a> and <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Newey" title="Adrian Newey">Adrian Newey</a> of <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a>; Fedrico Bendinelli representing the owners of the Autodromo Enzo e Dino Ferrari; Giorgio Poggi as the circuit director and <a href="/wiki/Roland_Bruynseraede" title="Roland Bruynseraede">Roland Bruynseraede</a> who was race director and sanctioned the circuit.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> The trial verdict was given on 16 December 1997, clearing all six defendants of manslaughter charges.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> The cause of Senna's accident was established by the court as the steering column breaking.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> The column had been cut and <a href="/wiki/Welding" title="Welding">welded</a> back together at Senna's request in order for him to be more comfortable in the car.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Following the court's decision, an appeal was lodged by the state prosecutor against Patrick Head and Adrian Newey. On 22 November 1999, the appeal absolved Head and Newey of all charges, stating that no new evidence had come to light (there was missing data from the black box recorder on Senna's car due to damage, and 1.6 seconds of video from the onboard camera of Senna's car was unavailable because the broadcaster switched to another car's camera just before the accident), and so under Article 530 of the Italian Penal Code, the accusation had to be declared as "non-existent or the fact doesn't subsist".<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> This appeal result was annulled in January 2003, as the <a href="/wiki/Court_of_Cassation_(Italy)" class="mw-redirect" title="Court of Cassation (Italy)">Court of Cassation</a> believed that Article 530 was misinterpreted,<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> and a retrial was ordered. On 27 May 2005, Newey was acquitted of all charges while Head's case was "timed out" under a statute of limitations.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> The Italian Court of Appeal, on 13 April 2007, stated the following in the verdict numbered 15050: "It has been determined that the accident was caused by a steering column failure. This failure was caused by badly designed and badly executed modifications. The responsibility of this falls on Patrick Head, culpable of omitted control". Even being found responsible for Senna's accident, Patrick Head was not arrested, as the verdict was delivered past the Italian statute of limitation for manslaughter.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Launch_control_controversy">Launch control controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1994_San_Marino_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Launch control controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Liverpool_Data_Research_Associates" title="Liverpool Data Research Associates">Liverpool Data Research Associates</a> (LDRA) were called in to investigate allegations of cheating using banned driving aids, such as <a href="/wiki/Traction_control" class="mw-redirect" title="Traction control">traction control</a> and <a href="/wiki/Launch_control_(automotive)" title="Launch control (automotive)">launch control</a>, both prohibited at the start of the year. The top three cars of <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nicola_Larini" title="Nicola Larini">Nicola Larini</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> were investigated and their teams were asked to surrender their systems' source code to the company.<sup id="cite_ref-LDRA_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LDRA-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Larini's team, <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, complied in light of allegations that they were cheating, but Schumacher and Häkkinen's teams, <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a> and <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> refused, claiming copyright reasons.<sup id="cite_ref-LDRA_18-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LDRA-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> After being fined $100,000 by the <a href="/wiki/FIA" class="mw-redirect" title="FIA">FIA</a>, both teams complied eight days after the race.<sup id="cite_ref-LDRA_18-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LDRA-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> LDRA discovered that McLaren were running a programme that permitted <a href="/wiki/Automatic_gearbox" class="mw-redirect" title="Automatic gearbox">automatic gearshifts</a> but the car was declared legal.<sup id="cite_ref-LDRA_18-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LDRA-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Benetton">Benetton</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1994_San_Marino_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Benetton">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Benetton sent an alternative suggestion to the company on 10 May 1994, accepted by LDRA five days later.<sup id="cite_ref-LDRA_18-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LDRA-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Tests on the <a href="/wiki/Benetton_B194" title="Benetton B194">car</a> were to be carried out on 28 June 1994, but were cancelled.<sup id="cite_ref-LDRA_18-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LDRA-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> The tests eventually took place on 6 July 1994. LDRA found the tests unsatisfactory.<sup id="cite_ref-LDRA_18-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LDRA-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Benetton therefore complied with the original request, the source code, on 18 July 1994.<sup id="cite_ref-LDRA_18-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LDRA-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Analysis of the software found that it included <a href="/wiki/Launch_control_(automotive)" title="Launch control (automotive)">launch control</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-LDRA_18-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LDRA-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> a banned aid. Benetton stated that "it can only be switched on by recompilation of the code."<sup id="cite_ref-LDRA_18-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LDRA-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> However LDRA found this to be untrue; launch control could be switched on by connecting a computer to the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Gearbox_control_unit&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Gearbox control unit (page does not exist)">gearbox control unit</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-LDRA_18-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LDRA-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Benetton conceded that this was possible but this "came as a surprise to them".<sup id="cite_ref-LDRA_18-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LDRA-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> To switch the system on, the user is presented with a menu with 10 visible options. "Launch Control" was not visibly listed as an option, however, should the user scroll down to option 13, launch control could be enabled.<sup id="cite_ref-LDRA_18-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LDRA-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
