<p>The <b>1962 Monaco Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held at <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Monaco" title="Circuit de Monaco">Monaco</a> on 3 June 1962. It was race 2 of 9 in both the <a href="/wiki/1962_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1962 World Championship of Drivers">1962 World Championship of Drivers</a> and the <a href="/wiki/1962_International_Cup_for_Formula_One_Manufacturers" class="mw-redirect" title="1962 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers">1962 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers</a>. The 100-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Cooper_Car_Company" title="Cooper Car Company">Cooper</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Bruce_McLaren" title="Bruce McLaren">Bruce McLaren</a> after he started from third position. <a href="/wiki/Phil_Hill" title="Phil Hill">Phil Hill</a> finished second for the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> team and his teammate <a href="/wiki/Lorenzo_Bandini" title="Lorenzo Bandini">Lorenzo Bandini</a> came in third.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1962_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Lotus</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Jim_Clark" title="Jim Clark">Jim Clark</a> claimed the first pole position of his career, ahead of <a href="/wiki/British_Racing_Motors" title="British Racing Motors">BRM</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Graham_Hill" title="Graham Hill">Graham Hill</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cooper_Car_Company" title="Cooper Car Company">Cooper</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Bruce_McLaren" title="Bruce McLaren">Bruce McLaren</a> and <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Willy_Mairesse" title="Willy Mairesse">Willy Mairesse</a>.
</p><p>Mairesse got an excellent start from fourth on the grid and led into the first corner, the Gasworks hairpin, but braked too late and skidded around it. The concertina effect of the cars behind trying to avoid Mairesse's skidding car led to a collision further behind which eliminated <a href="/wiki/Dan_Gurney" title="Dan Gurney">Dan Gurney</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maurice_Trintignant" title="Maurice Trintignant">Maurice Trintignant</a> and <a href="/wiki/Richie_Ginther" title="Richie Ginther">Richie Ginther</a>.
</p><p>McLaren and Graham Hill both passed Mairesse on the exit of Gasworks hairpin; the New Zealander ended the first lap in the lead from the Englishman. Meanwhile, Mairesse spun at the station hairpin on the first lap, dropping down the order.
</p><p>McLaren led until the seventh lap when he was passed by Graham Hill; the BRM driver held the lead all the way until lap 92, when an engine failure handed the lead back to McLaren who held on to win ahead of Ferrari's <a href="/wiki/Phil_Hill" title="Phil Hill">Phil Hill</a>, who recovered from a spin earlier in the race. Hill's Ferrari team mate <a href="/wiki/Lorenzo_Bandini" title="Lorenzo Bandini">Lorenzo Bandini</a> completed the podium places.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1962_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>A marshal, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ange_Baldoni&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Ange Baldoni (page does not exist)">Ange Baldoni</a>, was killed by the dislodged right rear wheel of Richie Ginther's BRM when it collided with the Lotuses of Maurice Trintignant, Innes Ireland and Trevor Taylor, as well as Dan Gurney's Porsche at the Gasometer hairpin on lap 1.</li>
<li>This was Jim Clark's first pole position in Formula One.</li>
<li>The organisers guaranteed two spots on the grid for each of the 5 works teams, leaving six grid spots for the remaining entries to fight over. This explains why some drivers with faster qualifying times failed to qualify whereas drivers with slower times qualified.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
