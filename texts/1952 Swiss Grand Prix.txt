<p>The <b>1952 Swiss Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_Two" title="Formula Two">Formula Two</a> race held on 18 May 1952 at <a href="/wiki/Bremgarten_Circuit" class="mw-redirect" title="Bremgarten Circuit">Bremgarten Circuit</a>. It was the first round of the <a href="/wiki/1952_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1952 World Championship of Drivers">1952 World Championship of Drivers</a>, in which each Grand Prix was run to Formula Two rules rather than the Formula One regulations normally used.
</p><p>Pre-<a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">WWII</a> Grand Prix great <a href="/wiki/Rudolf_Caracciola" title="Rudolf Caracciola">Rudolf Caracciola</a> crashed heavily during a support sports car race. He survived with a broken leg, but this crash effectively ended his racing career. He was driving a Mercedes 300SL; his brakes locked up going into a corner and the car skidded off the road and hit a tree.
</p><p>Italian driver Piero Taruffi scored his only win in a World Championship race, driving for Ferrari.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1952_Swiss_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>With the withdrawal of <a href="/wiki/Alfa_Romeo_in_Formula_One" title="Alfa Romeo in Formula One">Alfa Romeo</a> from the World Championship, <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> were left as the sole competitive team under the existing regulations. It was therefore decided to run the Championship to Formula Two regulations.
</p><p>The works Ferrari team brought three drivers to the Swiss Grand Prix, namely <a href="/wiki/Giuseppe_Farina" title="Giuseppe Farina">Farina</a>, <a href="/wiki/Piero_Taruffi" title="Piero Taruffi">Taruffi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Andr%C3%A9_Simon_(racing_driver)" title="André Simon (racing driver)">Simon</a>. Regular Ferrari drivers <a href="/wiki/Alberto_Ascari" title="Alberto Ascari">Alberto Ascari</a> and <a href="/wiki/Luigi_Villoresi" title="Luigi Villoresi">Luigi Villoresi</a> were both unavailable, the former due to his participation in the <a href="/wiki/1952_Indianapolis_500" title="1952 Indianapolis 500">Indianapolis 500</a>, and the latter because of his having had a road accident. Also running Ferraris were <a href="/wiki/Rudi_Fischer" title="Rudi Fischer">Rudi Fischer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Peter_Hirt" title="Peter Hirt">Peter Hirt</a> of <a href="/wiki/Ecurie_Espadon" class="mw-redirect" title="Ecurie Espadon">Ecurie Espadon</a>, and veteran Frenchman <a href="/wiki/Louis_Rosier" title="Louis Rosier">Louis Rosier</a>. <a href="/wiki/Gordini" title="Gordini">Gordini</a> also had a three-car team for this race, consisting of <a href="/wiki/Robert_Manzon" title="Robert Manzon">Robert Manzon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Birabongse_Bhanudej" title="Birabongse Bhanudej">B. Bira</a> and the debutant <a href="/wiki/Jean_Behra" title="Jean Behra">Jean Behra</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Hersham_and_Walton_Motors" title="Hersham and Walton Motors">HWM</a> team, returning to the World Championship for the first time since the <a href="/wiki/1951_Swiss_Grand_Prix" title="1951 Swiss Grand Prix">previous race at Bremgarten</a>, fielded the all-British quartet of <a href="/wiki/George_Abecassis" title="George Abecassis">Abecassis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Peter_Collins_(racing_driver)" title="Peter Collins (racing driver)">Collins</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lance_Macklin" title="Lance Macklin">Macklin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Stirling_Moss" title="Stirling Moss">Moss</a>. <a href="/wiki/Maserati_in_motorsport" title="Maserati in motorsport">Maserati</a> had planned to enter defending World Drivers' Champion <a href="/wiki/Juan_Manuel_Fangio" title="Juan Manuel Fangio">Juan Manuel Fangio</a> and fellow Argentinian <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Froil%C3%A1n_Gonz%C3%A1lez" title="José Froilán González">José Froilán González</a>, but this did not come into fruition. Completing the field were the sole <a href="/wiki/Alex_von_Falkenhausen_Motorenbau" title="Alex von Falkenhausen Motorenbau">AFM</a> entry of <a href="/wiki/Hans_Stuck" title="Hans Stuck">Hans Stuck</a> and a number of privately run cars representing various constructors.
</p><p>Former Alfa Romeo driver Nino Farina took pole position, alongside Taruffi and Manzon on the front row of the grid. Simon and Fischer started from the second row, in front of Collins, Behra and <a href="/wiki/Toulo_de_Graffenried" title="Toulo de Graffenried">Toulo de Graffenried</a>, who was driving an <a href="/wiki/Enrico_Plat%C3%A9" title="Enrico Platé">Enrico Platé</a>-entered Maserati.
</p><p>Polesitter Farina led the race until his car broke down. His Ferrari teammate assumed the lead, which he held for the remainder of the race. Moss was impressively running in third place in the early stages, behind Farina and Taruffi, before he had to stop. The main battle was between Behra and Simon, for second place (once Farina had retired). When Behra had to stop, due to his exhaust pipe having fallen off, Farina, who had taken over Simon's car, assumed second place. However, further problems meant that he once again had to retire, on lap 51, handing second to local driver Rudi Fischer. The Swiss driver took his first Championship podium, being the only driver not to be lapped by Taruffi, who took his first (and only) World Championship race victory. Behra completed the podium, taking third on debut, while <a href="/wiki/Ken_Wharton" title="Ken Wharton">Ken Wharton</a> (fourth) and <a href="/wiki/Alan_Brown_(racing_driver)" title="Alan Brown (racing driver)">Alan Brown</a> (fifth) took the first points finishes for <a href="/wiki/Frazer_Nash" title="Frazer Nash">Frazer Nash</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cooper_Car_Company" title="Cooper Car Company">Cooper</a>, respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
