<p>The <b>2020 Austrian Grand Prix</b> (officially known as the <b>Formula 1 Rolex Großer Preis von Österreich 2020</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Auto_racing" title="Auto racing">motor race</a> that was held on 5 July 2020 at the <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Ring" title="Red Bull Ring">Red Bull Ring</a> in <a href="/wiki/Spielberg,_Styria" title="Spielberg, Styria">Spielberg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Austria" title="Austria">Austria</a>. The race was the opening round of the <a href="/wiki/2020_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2020 Formula One World Championship">2020 Formula One World Championship</a>, and the 34th running of the <a href="/wiki/Austrian_Grand_Prix" title="Austrian Grand Prix">Austrian Grand Prix</a> (the 33rd as part of the World Championship since <a href="/wiki/1950_Formula_One_season" title="1950 Formula One season">1950</a>) as well as the first of two consecutive races to be held at the Red Bull Ring.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> entered the round as the defending <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">World Drivers' Champion</a> and his team, <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> as the defending <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">World Constructors' Champion</a>. <a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a> was the defending race winner, having won the <a href="/wiki/2018_Austrian_Grand_Prix" title="2018 Austrian Grand Prix">2018</a> and <a href="/wiki/2019_Austrian_Grand_Prix" title="2019 Austrian Grand Prix">2019</a> Austrian Grands Prix.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The race was won by Mercedes driver <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a> with <a href="/wiki/Charles_Leclerc" title="Charles Leclerc">Charles Leclerc</a> in second for <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lando_Norris" title="Lando Norris">Lando Norris</a> finishing third for <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> – his first podium in Formula One. Norris became the 3rd youngest driver ever to finish on the podium.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2020_Austrian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Impact_of_the_COVID-19_pandemic">Impact of the COVID-19 pandemic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2020_Austrian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Impact of the COVID-19 pandemic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/2020_Formula_One_World_Championship#Impact_of_the_COVID-19_pandemic" title="2020 Formula One World Championship">2020 Formula One World Championship §&#160;Impact of the COVID-19 pandemic</a>, and <a href="/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic_in_Austria" title="COVID-19 pandemic in Austria">COVID-19 pandemic in Austria</a></div>
<p>The originally scheduled calendar for the <a href="/wiki/2020_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2020 Formula One World Championship">2020</a> championship was heavily affected by the <a href="/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic" title="COVID-19 pandemic">COVID-19 pandemic</a>. Several Grands Prix were cancelled or postponed after the aborted <a href="/wiki/2020_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="2020 Australian Grand Prix">opening round</a> in Australia, prompting the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile</a> to draft a new calendar. The start of the championship was delayed until 5 July, with the <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Ring" title="Red Bull Ring">Red Bull Ring</a> hosting the <a href="/wiki/Austrian_Grand_Prix" title="Austrian Grand Prix">Austrian Grand Prix</a> as the opening round of the championship. Organisers of the race signed a contract to host a second round at the circuit on 12 July (a week after the first race) to be known as the <a href="/wiki/Styrian_Grand_Prix" class="mw-redirect" title="Styrian Grand Prix">Styrian Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The back-to-back Austrian races would mark the first time that a country hosts back-to-back races in the same season since <a href="/wiki/1995_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1995 Formula One World Championship">1995</a> when Japan hosted the <a href="/wiki/1995_Pacific_Grand_Prix" title="1995 Pacific Grand Prix">Pacific</a> and <a href="/wiki/1995_Japanese_Grand_Prix" title="1995 Japanese Grand Prix">Japanese</a> Grands Prix.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;c&#93;</a></sup> This would also mark the first time in the sport's history that the same venue and circuit layout would have hosted back-to-back World Championship races and the first time that a F1 race weekend was held <a href="/wiki/Behind_closed_doors_(sport)" title="Behind closed doors (sport)">behind closed doors</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The race was the first season opener held in Europe since the <a href="/wiki/1966_Formula_One_season" title="1966 Formula One season">1966 season</a> began with the <a href="/wiki/1966_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="1966 Monaco Grand Prix">Monaco Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>  Also due to being in July the race also broke the long standing record for the latest season opener (in terms of month and date) in a calendar year breaking the record set by the <a href="/wiki/1951_Swiss_Grand_Prix" title="1951 Swiss Grand Prix">1951 Swiss Grand Prix</a> which was held on 27 May 1951 as the opener of that season.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Entrants">Entrants</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2020_Austrian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Entrants">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Further information: <a href="/wiki/2020_Formula_One_World_Championship#Entries" title="2020 Formula One World Championship">2020 Formula One World Championship §&#160;Entries</a></div>
<p>Twenty drivers representing ten teams entered the race.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_AlphaTauri" title="Scuderia AlphaTauri">Scuderia AlphaTauri</a> made their debut as a constructor having previously competed under the name <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Scuderia Toro Rosso</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Esteban_Ocon" title="Esteban Ocon">Esteban Ocon</a> made a return to the championship with <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Nicholas_Latifi" title="Nicholas Latifi">Nicholas Latifi</a> made his competitive debut with <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Philip_Morris_International" title="Philip Morris International">Mission Winnow</a>, the title sponsor of <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, was banned from the race as it did not comply with local laws governing tobacco sponsorship.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tyres">Tyres</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2020_Austrian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Tyres">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_tyres" title="Formula One tyres">Formula One tyres</a></div>
<p>Pirelli brought C2, C3 and C4 (designated hard, medium and soft respectively) compounds of tyres for teams to use at the event.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> In recognition of the need to cut costs and to give Pirelli achievable targets due to the congested calendar all drivers received identical tyre allocations getting two sets of hards, three sets of mediums and eight sets of softs.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice">Practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2020_Austrian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>All three practice sessions ended with <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> in first ahead of <a href="/wiki/Mercedes_in_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Mercedes in F1">Mercedes</a> teammate <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a>. <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a> was third fastest in the first and third sessions while <a href="/wiki/Racing_Point_F1_Team" title="Racing Point F1 Team">Racing Point</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a> came third in the second session. The first two sessions ran without major incidents, however, the third practice session was temporarily <a href="/wiki/Red_flag_(racing)" class="mw-redirect" title="Red flag (racing)">red flagged</a> when <a href="/wiki/Nicholas_Latifi" title="Nicholas Latifi">Nicholas Latifi</a> crashed at turn one.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2020_Austrian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> finished 1–2 in qualifying with <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Bottas</a> taking pole ahead of his team-mate <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Hamilton</a>, despite Bottas going into the gravel in his last Q3 attempt, with <a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a> 3rd in his <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing_RB16" title="Red Bull Racing RB16">Red Bull RB16</a>, 0.5 seconds behind the Mercedes pair. <a href="/wiki/Lando_Norris" title="Lando Norris">Lando Norris</a> qualified a career best 4th in his McLaren ahead of Verstappen's Red Bull team-mate <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Albon" title="Alexander Albon">Alexander Albon</a> in 5th with <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a> of <a href="/wiki/Racing_Point_F1_Team" title="Racing Point F1 Team">Racing Point</a> in 6th. <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> struggled in the session with <a href="/wiki/Charles_Leclerc" title="Charles Leclerc">Charles Leclerc</a> only qualifying in 7th place, nearly one second off pole position, while his-teammate <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> finished in 11th place, therefore failing to make it into Q3. The rest of the top 10 after Leclerc was rounded out by <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Sainz_Jr." title="Carlos Sainz Jr.">Carlos Sainz Jr.</a> in 8th in the second McLaren, <a href="/wiki/Lance_Stroll" title="Lance Stroll">Lance Stroll</a> 9th in the second Racing Point and <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> in 10th for <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>.
</p><p>Earlier in Q1 the <a href="/wiki/Alfa_Romeo_in_Formula_One" title="Alfa Romeo in Formula One">Alfa Romeo Racing</a> team saw both their drivers eliminated with <a href="/wiki/Antonio_Giovinazzi" title="Antonio Giovinazzi">Antonio Giovinazzi</a> and his team-mate <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> qualifying in 18th and 19th places respectively behind the <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> of <a href="/wiki/2018_Formula_2_Championship" title="2018 Formula 2 Championship">2018 Formula 2</a> champion <a href="/wiki/George_Russell_(racing_driver)" title="George Russell (racing driver)">George Russell</a> and only ahead of the other Williams car of debutant <a href="/wiki/Nicholas_Latifi" title="Nicholas Latifi">Nicholas Latifi</a> who qualified 20th (last) on the grid.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-qualifying_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualifying-22">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Post-qualifying, Hamilton was summoned to the stewards for allegedly ignoring yellow flags shown during Bottas's error, but was cleared of any wrongdoing after stewards concluded that "conflicting signals were shown to the driver".<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> However, prior to the race start, Lewis Hamilton was summoned to the stewards for a review of the decision and given a three-place grid penalty after all, following a protest by Red Bull over the FIA's original decision to clear the Mercedes driver of any wrongdoing, presenting new evidence from Hamilton's 360-degree onboard camera.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2020_Austrian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_report">Race report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2020_Austrian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Race report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_laps">Early laps</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2020_Austrian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Early laps">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The opening lap proceeded without incident, with the only change in position amongst the top ten being <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> claiming tenth place from <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a>. <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Lando_Norris" title="Lando Norris">Lando Norris</a>, running in third place, was overtaken by <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Albon" title="Alexander Albon">Alexander Albon</a> on lap three and then by <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> soon after. Hamilton later entered the podium positions by passing Albon on lap nine.
</p><p>The first incident of the race came on lap eleven when the <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a> of <a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a>, running in second place, began to slow with a suspected electrical issue. He was able to return to the pits where his mechanics attempted to correct the problem, however this was unsuccessful and he was forced to retire from the race.<sup id="cite_ref-RBR_retirements_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RBR_retirements-26">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> The ninth-placed <a href="/wiki/Racing_Point" class="mw-redirect" title="Racing Point">Racing Point</a> of <a href="/wiki/Lance_Stroll" title="Lance Stroll">Lance Stroll</a> began to suffer power loss on lap 16, allowing Vettel and Ricciardo to close in. However, Ricciardo was soon forced to pull into the pits and retire with suspected overheating issues. On lap 21, Stroll entered the pits and retired from the race, having fallen behind Vettel and the two <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_AlphaTauri" title="Scuderia AlphaTauri">AlphaTauris</a>. At the same time, 16th-placed <a href="/wiki/Romain_Grosjean" title="Romain Grosjean">Romain Grosjean</a> spun onto the gravel at turn three. He dropped to the back of the field but was able to continue, entering the pits at the end of the lap to become the first driver to change tyres.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Mid-race">Mid-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2020_Austrian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Mid-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On lap 25, 11th-placed <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Magnussen" title="Kevin Magnussen">Kevin Magnussen</a> went straight on at turn two having suffered a brake failure, bringing out the first <a href="/wiki/Safety_car" title="Safety car">safety car</a> of the race.<sup id="cite_ref-Haas_retirements_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Haas_retirements-27">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> All cars still remaining (bar Grosjean) entered into the pits for their first tyre changes. The order of the top ten after the first round of pit stops was Bottas, Hamilton, Albon, Norris, Pérez, Leclerc, Sainz Jr., Vettel, Gasly and Kvyat. The safety car period ended on lap 31. At the restart, Vettel attempted an overtake on the inside of <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Sainz_Jr." title="Carlos Sainz Jr.">Carlos Sainz Jr.</a> at turn two. The cars made contact and Vettel's <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> was pitched into a spin, dropping him to 15th place. On lap 33 Pérez made it past Norris to take fourth place.
</p><p>Both <a href="/wiki/Mercedes_in_Formula_One" class="mw-redirect" title="Mercedes in Formula One">Mercedes</a> drivers, running first and second, were warned by their engineers to avoid driving over the kerbs. It emerged that the vibrations were damaging the cars' gearboxes, and it was feared this would cause a failure leading to immediate retirement.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Running 15th, Grosjean suffered a brake failure on lap 50, but was able to make it to the pits to retire.<sup id="cite_ref-Haas_retirements_27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Haas_retirements-27">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> At the same time, 13th-placed <a href="/wiki/George_Russell_(racing_driver)" title="George Russell (racing driver)">George Russell</a> lost fuel pressure and pulled onto the grass on the inside of turn three, deploying the second safety car of the race. Eight of the fourteen remaining drivers elected to pit for a second tyre change, including Albon who changed to soft-compound tyres, whilst the leading Mercedes cars stayed out on their hard-compound tyres. The order of the top ten was now Bottas, Hamilton, Pérez, Albon, Norris, Leclerc, Gasly, Ocon, Sainz and Giovinazzi.
</p><p>The safety car period ended on lap 54. However, almost immediately after the restart, the right-front wheel of <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Alfa_Romeo_in_Formula_One" title="Alfa Romeo in Formula One">Alfa Romeo</a> detached from the car and he was forced to stop on the pit straight, bringing out a third safety car. Shortly prior to the safety car deployment, Albon passed Pérez for third place. Racing resumed on lap 61. Shortly after the restart, Albon attempted an overtake on Hamilton for second place around the outside of turn three. The cars made contact, causing Albon to spin into the gravel. He was able to continue but returned to the track last of the running cars in 13th place. Shortly afterwards, Sainz passed both <a href="/wiki/Esteban_Ocon" title="Esteban Ocon">Esteban Ocon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pierre_Gasly" title="Pierre Gasly">Pierre Gasly</a> to take sixth place.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Closing_laps">Closing laps</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2020_Austrian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Closing laps">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Fifth-placed <a href="/wiki/Charles_Leclerc" title="Charles Leclerc">Charles Leclerc</a> began to move towards the front, overtaking <a href="/wiki/Lando_Norris" title="Lando Norris">Lando Norris</a> on lap 64 and then Pérez on lap 66 to take third place. On the same lap, Hamilton received a five-second time penalty, having been judged by the stewards to have caused the earlier collision with Albon. Pérez then received the same penalty for having sped in the pit lane. On lap 68, Albon pulled over at turn six with an electrical failure and Norris passed Pérez for fourth place at turn two. On the penultimate lap, eleventh-placed <a href="/wiki/Daniil_Kvyat" title="Daniil Kvyat">Daniil Kvyat</a> suffered a puncture which broke his suspension, the ninth retirement of the race. Sainz then passed Pérez for fifth place on the final lap.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a> crossed the line, having led every lap of the race, to take the eighth win of his Formula One career and his second win at the <a href="/wiki/Austrian_Grand_Prix" title="Austrian Grand Prix">Austrian Grand Prix</a>, having won the <a href="/wiki/2017_Austrian_Grand_Prix" title="2017 Austrian Grand Prix">2017 edition</a>. Hamilton crossed the line second, but Leclerc took second after Hamilton's penalty was applied. On the final lap, a late push from Norris set the <a href="/wiki/Fastest_lap" title="Fastest lap">fastest lap</a> of the race and reduced his gap to Hamilton to 4.8 seconds, promoting him to third and a place on the podium for the first time in his career, while Hamilton was classified fourth and Sainz fifth. Pérez's sixth-place finish was unaffected by his time penalty. Gasly took points for AlphaTauri with seventh place on the team's first race since their rebranding, and Ocon finished eighth on his return to the sport after a year out. <a href="/wiki/Antonio_Giovinazzi" title="Antonio Giovinazzi">Antonio Giovinazzi</a> and Vettel rounded out the points positions, with debutant <a href="/wiki/Nicholas_Latifi" title="Nicholas Latifi">Nicholas Latifi</a> being the only driver to finish the race outside the points in eleventh place.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Norris became the third-youngest driver to finish on the podium in <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> history (behind Verstappen and Stroll) and the youngest <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_drivers_from_the_United_Kingdom" title="Formula One drivers from the United Kingdom">Briton</a> to do so.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> His fastest lap (1:07.475), the first of his Formula One career, was identical to the fastest lap set by Max Verstappen at the <a href="/wiki/2019_Austrian_Grand_Prix" title="2019 Austrian Grand Prix">2019 Austrian Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
