<p>The <b>2016 Belgian Grand Prix</b> (formally known as the <b>2016 Formula 1 Belgian Grand Prix</b>)<sup id="cite_ref-Spa-Francorchamps_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Spa-Francorchamps-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Auto_racing" title="Auto racing">motor race</a> held on 28 August 2016 at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Spa-Francorchamps" title="Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps">Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps</a> in <a href="/wiki/Spa,_Belgium" title="Spa, Belgium">Spa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a>. It was the thirteenth round of the <a href="/wiki/2016_FIA_Formula_One_World_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="2016 FIA Formula One World Championship">2016 FIA Formula One World Championship</a>, and marks the seventy-second running of the <a href="/wiki/Belgian_Grand_Prix" title="Belgian Grand Prix">Belgian Grand Prix</a> and the fifty-ninth time the race was held at Spa-Francorchamps.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> driver and <a href="/wiki/2015_Belgian_Grand_Prix" title="2015 Belgian Grand Prix">defending</a> race winner <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> entered the race leading the World Drivers' Championship by nineteen points ahead of teammate <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a>. In the World Constructors' Championship, Mercedes held a lead of 159 points. <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a> was lying second, having passed <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> in the standings at the <a href="/wiki/2016_German_Grand_Prix" title="2016 German Grand Prix">previous round in Germany</a>.
</p><p>Nico Rosberg won this Grand Prix, with <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> and Lewis Hamilton, second and third respectively. Hamilton received a multi-grid penalty before the start of the race. Hamilton retained the championship lead, with Rosberg closing the gap to nine points. <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a> and both <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferraris</a> were involved in an incident at the start of the race.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/List_of_red-flagged_Formula_One_races" title="List of red-flagged Formula One races">race was stopped</a> on lap 9 after <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Magnussen" title="Kevin Magnussen">Kevin Magnussen</a> crashed heavily at Raidillon, suffering a 12.5G impact, causing structural damage to the armoured collision barrier and tyre wall which required repairs, before the race could resume under <a href="/wiki/Racing_flags#Green_flag" title="Racing flags">green flag</a> conditions. Magnussen suffered a cut on his ankle but no serious injury.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2016_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2016_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Driver_changes">Driver changes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2016_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Driver changes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p><a href="/wiki/Esteban_Ocon" title="Esteban Ocon">Esteban Ocon</a> made his Formula One début, replacing <a href="/wiki/Rio_Haryanto" title="Rio Haryanto">Rio Haryanto</a> at <a href="/wiki/Manor_Racing" title="Manor Racing">MRT</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ocon_in_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ocon_in-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Haryanto stayed with the team, filling a testing and reserve role.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Tyres">Tyres</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2016_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Tyres">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Tyre supplier <a href="/wiki/Pirelli" title="Pirelli">Pirelli</a> provided teams with the super-soft, soft and medium compounds for the races. Drivers were also supplied with two sets of "prototype" soft tyres for use in the Friday practice sessions, which were designed to provide similar lap times and performance to the existing soft compound whilst being more durable when striking kerbs and going beyond track limits.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The prototype tyres were introduced to allow Pirelli to gain data on their performance ahead of their competitive début in <a href="/wiki/2016_Malaysian_Grand_Prix" title="2016 Malaysian Grand Prix">Malaysia</a>, where they will replace the existing soft compound.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Upgrades">Upgrades</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2016_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Upgrades">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Both <a href="/wiki/Honda_in_Formula_One" title="Honda in Formula One">Honda</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> introduced upgrades to their power units for the race, while <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a> brought their first updates of the season for the <a href="/wiki/Sauber_C35" title="Sauber C35">C35 chassis</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Penalties">Penalties</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2016_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Penalties">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p><a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marcus_Ericsson" title="Marcus Ericsson">Marcus Ericsson</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> took grid penalties for exceeding their season allocation of engine components; Hamilton received fifteen places for using his sixth turbocharger and associated Motor Generator Unit, while Ericsson was demoted ten places for replacing his turbocharger,<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> and Alonso thirty-five places for introducing his sixth complete power unit. Hamilton received a second set of penalties when Mercedes introduced a seventh turbocharger and MGU-H ahead of the second free practice session;<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> and a third set for further changes ahead of the final practice session, thereby guaranteeing that he would start the race from last place. Mercedes deliberately took the penalties as they sought to stockpile new components for the remainder of the season, deeming the Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps to be a venue where the potential for overtaking offset the penalties.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Esteban_Guti%C3%A9rrez" title="Esteban Gutiérrez">Esteban Gutiérrez</a> received a five-place grid penalty for dangerous driving during the final practice session when <a href="/wiki/Pascal_Wehrlein" title="Pascal Wehrlein">Pascal Wehrlein</a> was forced to take evasive action and cross the grassy verge on the inside of Raidillon to avoid contact.
</p><p>On race day, Mercedes removed a seal on the gearbox of Hamilton's car so he could take a new gearbox at the next race, giving the world champion an additional five place grid penalty. The penalty was initially incorrectly issued and originally designated Hamilton to start from the pit lane.<sup id="cite_ref-Hamilton_gearbox_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hamilton_gearbox-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
