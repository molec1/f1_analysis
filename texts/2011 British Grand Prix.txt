<p>The <b>2011 British Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>2011 Formula 1 Santander British Grand Prix<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup></b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 10 July 2011 at the <a href="/wiki/Silverstone_Circuit" title="Silverstone Circuit">Silverstone Circuit</a> in Northamptonshire and <a href="/wiki/Buckinghamshire" title="Buckinghamshire">Buckinghamshire</a>, England, and won by <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
Changes to the circuit mean that the race was previously classified as being in <a href="/wiki/Northamptonshire" title="Northamptonshire">Northamptonshire</a>, but the movement of the start-finish line means that the race is now officially classed as being in <a href="/wiki/Buckinghamshire" title="Buckinghamshire">Buckinghamshire</a>.  It was the ninth race of the <a href="/wiki/2011_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2011 Formula One season">2011 season</a>, and saw the introduction of a ban on off-throttle blown diffusers,<sup id="cite_ref-OTBD_ban_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OTBD_ban-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> the practice of forcing the engine to continue to produce exhaust gasses to generate downforce when drivers are not using the throttle.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_British_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_British_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The circuit underwent a major reconfiguration for the <a href="/wiki/2010_British_Grand_Prix" title="2010 British Grand Prix">2010 British Grand Prix</a>, with the addition of the "Arena" loop bypassing the old Abbey, Bridge and Priory corners before re-joining at Brooklands. The 2011 race saw the second stage of this reconfiguration used for the first time, with the pits and the start/finish line moved to the straight between Club Corner and the new Abbey. The new pit facility was deliberately designed to be challenging for drivers, and to minimise the amount of time spent in the pits. The entry lane branches off from the circuit on the approach to Vale and Club – one of the slowest sections of the circuit – allowing a pitting driver to continue to accelerate while others are forced to slow for the corner. The pit lane itself does not run parallel to the main straight, but rather is angled at five degrees to make the corner at the entry to the pit lane slightly faster. <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a> team principal <a href="/wiki/Christian_Horner" title="Christian Horner">Christian Horner</a> noted that the pits are lower than the circuit proper, and that he believed that drivers would have to treat the corner at the entry to the pit lane as they would a corner on the circuit.<sup id="cite_ref-New_Pits_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-New_Pits-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Following complaints from <a href="/wiki/Grand_Prix_motorcycle_racing" title="Grand Prix motorcycle racing">MotoGP riders</a> in <a href="/wiki/2010_British_motorcycle_Grand_Prix" title="2010 British motorcycle Grand Prix">2010</a>, the entry to the new Abbey has been re-surfaced to remove a large bump situated on the racing line.<sup id="cite_ref-New_Pits_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-New_Pits-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The reconfiguration was not without controversy, as the lowered pit lane meant that the pit garages were obscured from the view of the spectators by a retaining wall. <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_Management" class="mw-redirect" title="Formula One Management">Formula One Management</a> reversed the intended pit lane order, placing the <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a>, <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> and <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> pit garages behind the retaining wall, and moving the <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus_(2010%E2%80%9311)" title="Team Lotus (2010–11)">Team Lotus</a>, <a href="/wiki/HRT_Formula_1_Team" title="HRT Formula 1 Team">Hispania</a> and <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Racing" title="Virgin Racing">Virgin</a> teams' garages to the visible end of the pit lane. The change in the allocation of garages was believed to be for the benefit of corporate guests in the Formula One "Paddock Club", a corporate suite housed above the far end of the pit lane obscured from view.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After experimenting with two Drag Reduction System (DRS) zones in <a href="/wiki/2011_Canadian_Grand_Prix" title="2011 Canadian Grand Prix">Montreal</a> and <a href="/wiki/2011_European_Grand_Prix" title="2011 European Grand Prix">Valencia</a>, the FIA announced that the sport would return to using only the one DRS zone at Silverstone, with the activation point placed before Aintree corner, allowing drivers to deploy the system for the length of the Wellington Straight.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Tyre supplier <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_tyres" title="Formula One tyres">Pirelli</a> brought its silver-banded hard compound tyre as the harder "prime" tyre and the yellow-banded soft compound as the softer "option" compound. This was the same tyre selection that Bridgestone had chosen to bring to the British Grand Prix for the previous two years.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 30 June, it was announced that <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Scuderia Toro Rosso</a> testing and reserve driver <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> would replace <a href="/wiki/Narain_Karthikeyan" title="Narain Karthikeyan">Narain Karthikeyan</a> at <a href="/wiki/HRT_Formula_1_Team" title="HRT Formula 1 Team">HRT Formula 1 Team</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> With <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> driving for <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a>, Ricciardo's debut marks the first time that two Australian drivers have competed in the same race since <a href="/wiki/Alan_Jones_(racing_driver)" title="Alan Jones (racing driver)">Alan Jones</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vern_Schuppan" title="Vern Schuppan">Vern Schuppan</a> raced in the <a href="/wiki/1977_Austrian_Grand_Prix" title="1977 Austrian Grand Prix">1977 Austrian Grand Prix</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice">Practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_British_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The first ninety-minute session of the weekend was deemed wet. The initial lap times were slow, but as a dry line began to appear around the circuit, the drivers switched to Pirelli's intermediate compound, and lap times began to improve significantly. <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> set the fastest time of the session, but experienced fuel problems and stopped on the circuit. <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> finished second ahead of <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> third. The rain returned late in the session, and <a href="/wiki/Kamui_Kobayashi" title="Kamui Kobayashi">Kamui Kobayashi</a> crashed heavily on the pit straight. <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a> were able to repair Kobayashi's car in time for the second session, where he finished third overall. The session was once again wet, with conditions worse than in the morning session; almost half of the ninety minutes had passed before any driver took to the circuit. A dry line gradually began to form around the circuit once again, prompting many drivers to start setting times. <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> finished the session fastest, nearly eight-tenths of a second clear of <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> and Kobayashi.
</p><p>The third and final practice session was held in the driest conditions of the weekend, interrupted by a brief shower early in the session. <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> finished the hour-long session fastest, six hundredths of a second faster than <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> and four-tenths quicker than team-mate Mark Webber, whose progress was hampered by an early problem with his gearbox. Michael Schumacher also experienced technical problems, this time with his floor, and finished the session eleventh.
</p>
<div style="clear:both;"></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_British_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The first qualifying period saw all twenty-four cars take to the circuit as soon as the session began in response to the imminent threat of rain; it was noted by teams and commentators alike that the old start line and Copse corner in particular were slippery. Several drivers topped the timing sheets, including <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pastor_Maldonado" title="Pastor Maldonado">Pastor Maldonado</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a>, with Webber ultimately setting the fastest time of the session. Alonso had an excursion into the gravel trap at Luffield, but emerged unscathed and went on to set his fastest time within two laps of the incident, while the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rossos</a> of <a href="/wiki/Jaime_Alguersuari" title="Jaime Alguersuari">Jaime Alguersuari</a> and <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9bastien_Buemi" title="Sébastien Buemi">Sébastien Buemi</a> were caught out on the circuit as a heavy downpour began in the final five minutes of the session. Both drivers were eliminated, with Alguersuari qualifying seventeenth and Buemi eighteenth, allowing <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> to enter Q2 for the first time since the <a href="/wiki/2011_Spanish_Grand_Prix" title="2011 Spanish Grand Prix">2011 Spanish Grand Prix</a>. <a href="/wiki/Timo_Glock" title="Timo Glock">Timo Glock</a> qualified his <a href="/wiki/Virgin_MVR-02" title="Virgin MVR-02">Virgin MVR-02</a> in twentieth place ahead of <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> in the second <a href="/wiki/Lotus_T128_(Formula_One_car)" title="Lotus T128 (Formula One car)">Lotus T128</a> and <a href="/wiki/J%C3%A9r%C3%B4me_d%27Ambrosio" title="Jérôme d&#39;Ambrosio">Jérôme d'Ambrosio</a>. The <a href="/wiki/HRT_Formula_1_Team" title="HRT Formula 1 Team">Hispanias</a> of <a href="/wiki/Vitantonio_Liuzzi" title="Vitantonio Liuzzi">Vitantonio Liuzzi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> qualified on the final row of the grid, with the Australian rookie half a second behind his team-mate.
</p><p>The rain died down in the interlude following the first qualifying period, and when the second period began, the drivers took to the circuit on a variety of tyre compounds, including both the hard and soft dry tyres and the intermediate-grade rubber owing to the varying conditions around the circuit; the old main straight and Copse remained slippery, while the Vale and Club Corner were noticeably dry. Many of the drivers were simply performing sighting laps in an attempt to decide which tyres were best-suited to the conditions, waiting for lap times to be set on all compounds to establish a base lap time. Once the first lap times were established, the drivers pitted for the dry compounds and took to the circuit once more in another race to beat the weather. Almost every driver in the session was the fastest man on circuit at some point during the session, but once the circuit finally rubbered in, Fernando Alonso and Lewis Hamilton start setting times two seconds faster than the rest of the field, led by <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a>. <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> went on to set the fastest time from Webber and Alonso, while a late lap from <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> saw him through to the final qualifying period. Adrian Sutil was eliminated in eleventh, followed by <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a>. <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> qualified thirteenth, the first time he had qualified outside the top ten at Silverstone in his career. <a href="/wiki/Vitaly_Petrov" title="Vitaly Petrov">Vitaly Petrov</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> finished the session in fourteenth and sixteenth respectively – split by the <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> of <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> – in <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>'s worst qualifying performance of the season. Heikki Kovalainen qualified seventeenth after setting just one lap time to preserve tyres for the race.
</p><p>The final period began as the previous two had, with most of the drivers venturing out as soon as the circuit was opened. The front-running drivers planned for two separate runs, but were anticipating rain later in the period, making the early laps crucial. Meanwhile, Pastor Maldonado, Kamui Kobayashi and <a href="/wiki/Paul_di_Resta" title="Paul di Resta">Paul di Resta</a> opted for just one qualifying run. Mark Webber claimed his second pole position of the season, just three-hundredths of a second ahead of Vettel on their first run before the predicted rain arrived three minutes from the end of the session.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The Ferraris of Alonso and Massa filled the second row of the grid, ahead of Button, di Resta and Maldonado. Button had been attempting a final flying lap at the end of the period, but was interrupted by the arrival of the rain and aborted the lap early. Kamui Kobayashi finished eighth ahead of <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a>, who, like Button, had his final flying run cut short by the conditions. <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> qualified tenth when he set a lap time on worn tyres before the rain arrived, denying him the opportunity to better his lap time. This session marked Sebastian Vettel's 14th consecutive front row start.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_British_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The race started under difficult conditions, with a full wet track from the Arena to Chapel, but the remaining sections being dry; as a result of this all of the teams elected to run on intermediate tyres. Before the start <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a> slid off the circuit, with Pérez damaging his car's front wing. At the start <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> beat Webber to the first corner, while <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> passed <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a>. Lewis Hamilton made up four positions on the first lap after starting from tenth. The two Renaults of <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vitaly_Petrov" title="Vitaly Petrov">Vitaly Petrov</a> had a small collision at Vale, nearly taking Vitaly Petrov out of the race. The difficult conditions meant drivers were forced to nurse the intermediate tyres through the dry sections of the circuit to preserve the integrity of the tyres through the wet stretches. This resulted in the drivers having to stay out on the circuit as long as possible until the circuit became dry enough to switch to the dry-compound tyres, or else risk making an extra stop and losing track position.
</p><p>Vettel started building up a comfortable lead ahead of Webber whilst Jenson Button struggled, first losing fourth place to Massa and then being passed by Hamilton as the 2008 World Champion climbed back up through the field. The Lotuses of <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> became the first retirements of the race, stopping within the first ten laps of the race due to gearbox issues. As the first round of scheduled pit stops approached, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> collided with <a href="/wiki/Kamui_Kobayashi" title="Kamui Kobayashi">Kamui Kobayashi</a> at Luffield, spinning the Japanese driver around. Schumacher was forced to replace his front wing and was subsequently given a ten-second stop-go penalty for causing an avoidable accident; as the new pit complex was designed in such a way that drivers would spend a minimal amount of time in the pits, the stewards decided that a stop-go penalty was more appropriate than a drive-through. Kamui Kobayashi was given a similar penalty when he pitted due to an unsafe release that saw him drive over a wheel gun. Kobayashi would then go on to retire from the Grand Prix when his engine expired. Meanwhile, <a href="/wiki/Jaime_Alguersuari" title="Jaime Alguersuari">Jaime Alguersuari</a> and <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9bastien_Buemi" title="Sébastien Buemi">Sébastien Buemi</a> were fighting up the order from their poor grid positions, and both successfully passed the struggling Renault of <a href="/wiki/Vitaly_Petrov" title="Vitaly Petrov">Vitaly Petrov</a>. At Force India, <a href="/wiki/Paul_di_Resta" title="Paul di Resta">Paul di Resta</a> was delayed in the pits when the team were expecting <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a>, and thus had Sutil's tyres ready in a similar incident to a mistake at the <a href="/wiki/2010_German_Grand_Prix" title="2010 German Grand Prix">2010 German Grand Prix</a>, forcing di Resta to wait while tyres from his own allocation were found. The error dropped di Resta well down the order, and he eventually made contact with Buemi at Copse, damaging the Swiss driver's left-rear tyre. Yellow flags were displayed as Buemi tried to limp back to the pits, but his tyre soon disintegrated and he had to retire by the side of the track.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The second round of pit stops saw Vettel and Alonso enter at the same time, but an uncharacteristic mistake from the Red Bull mechanics meant Vettel was delayed and allowing Alonso to take the lead of the race. Vettel emerged in third behind Lewis Hamilton, and struggled to pass the McLaren driver as Alonso increased his lead. Red Bull would eventually pit Vettel for a third time to allow him to run in clear air. Meanwhile, Button pitted for new tyres but retired from the race after the front right wheelnut was not attached, leaving the wheel visibly loose on the exit from the pits and continuing Button's run of poor results in his home race.
</p><p>With less than ten laps to go, Hamilton was told to start conserving fuel to finish the race. This slowed him to the point where Vettel and Webber were able to pass him and put him in danger of being passed by Felipe Massa. As the race entered the final two laps, Webber was close enough to Vettel to attempt a pass while Massa was visibly faster than Hamilton. Webber was given an order by the team not to pass Vettel, but ignored it. He was ultimately unsuccessful, and finished in third place. Behind them, Massa caught Hamilton on the final lap and attempted a pass into Vale corner. Hamilton, holding a defensive line into the corner, was unable to slow the car down in time and the two made contact. This forced Massa off the racing line through Club corner and across the line; Hamilton prevailed by two hundredths of a second, while Massa ran wide and crossed the finish line on the tarmac run-off on the outside of the corner. The stewards investigated, but no action was taken.
</p><p>Alonso won the race – Ferrari's first of the 2011 season – sixteen seconds ahead of Vettel and Webber with Hamilton fourth and Massa fifth. <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> finished sixth, five seconds ahead of Sergio Pérez in a career-best finish. Nick Heidfeld salvaged four points for Renault in eighth place and Schumacher recovered from his penalty to place ninth. Vitaly Petrov's failure to score meant that <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> took fourth place in the World Constructors' Championship. Jaime Alguersuari took the final point-scoring position in tenth, his third successive finish in the points. <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> was the nineteenth and final classified finisher on his race debut.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Diffuser_ban_and_controversy">Diffuser ban and controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_British_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Diffuser ban and controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:2011_Canadian_GP_Friday_19.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/86/2011_Canadian_GP_Friday_19.jpg/220px-2011_Canadian_GP_Friday_19.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/86/2011_Canadian_GP_Friday_19.jpg/330px-2011_Canadian_GP_Friday_19.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/86/2011_Canadian_GP_Friday_19.jpg/440px-2011_Canadian_GP_Friday_19.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2476" data-file-height="1650" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:2011_Canadian_GP_Friday_19.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The controversy covered the changing of the usage of blowing exhaust fumes through the diffuser at the rear of the car</div></div></div>
<p>The race also saw teams banned from using the "off-throttle blown diffuser" concept.<sup id="cite_ref-OTBD_ban_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OTBD_ban-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Previously, teams had mapped their engines to burn fuel when the driver lifted off the throttle. This created exhaust gasses that were routed over the diffuser at the rear of the car, generating additional downforce. This counters the loss of downforce experienced with blown diffusers when the driver lifts off the throttle. Compared to a car with a conventional blown diffuser, the additional downforce produced while off throttle improves grip under braking and in slower corners while also making the car's handling less throttle sensitive. Some estimates suggested that this allowed teams to find upwards of one second per lap. Off-throttle blown diffusers had been a source of controversy when the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">FIA</a> previously banned them at the <a href="/wiki/2011_Spanish_Grand_Prix" title="2011 Spanish Grand Prix">Spanish Grand Prix</a>, only to recant the ban twenty-four hours later pending a discussion with the sport's Technical Working Group,<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> and prompting <a href="/wiki/HRT_Formula_1_Team" title="HRT Formula 1 Team">Hispania</a> to threaten a protest of the race results. This led to speculation that engine supplier <a href="/wiki/Cosworth" title="Cosworth">Cosworth</a> had been unable to map their engines appropriately and thus could not take advantage of the off-throttle concept as none of the Cosworth-powered teams – <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Racing" title="Virgin Racing">Virgin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> and Hispania (as well as the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>-powered <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a> and <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso</a> teams) – were using off-throttle blown diffusers.<sup id="cite_ref-OTBD_ban_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OTBD_ban-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Whiting" title="Charlie Whiting">Charlie Whiting</a>, the FIA's technical delegate, ruled that off-throttle blown diffusers qualified as a movable aerodynamic part because the concept used moveable parts within the engine to manipulate relative levels of aerodynamic grip, and were therefore illegal under the FIA's long-standing ban on moveable aerodynamic parts. The ban stated that teams may only map their engines to use ten percent of available throttle power when under braking, with further restrictions to be introduced in <a href="/wiki/2012_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2012 Formula One World Championship">2012</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-OTBD_ban_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OTBD_ban-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Newey" title="Adrian Newey">Adrian Newey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a>'s technical director, admitted that his team had the most to lose from the ban on off-throttle blown diffusers because the <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_RB7" title="Red Bull RB7">Red Bull RB7</a> had been designed around the concept, in comparison to other teams who had simply applied it to their respective cars after the fact.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatleft" style="width:31%;&#32;;"><style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r887775652">.mw-parser-output .quotebox{background-color:#F9F9F9;border:1px solid #aaa;box-sizing:border-box;padding:10px;font-size:88%;max-width:100%}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft{margin:0.5em 1.4em 0.8em 0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright{margin:0.5em 0 0.8em 1.4em}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.centered{margin:0.5em auto 0.8em auto}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft p,.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright p{font-style:inherit}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-title{background-color:#F9F9F9;text-align:center;font-size:larger;font-weight:bold}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:before{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" “ ";vertical-align:-45%;line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:after{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" ” ";line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .left-aligned{text-align:left}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .right-aligned{text-align:right}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .center-aligned{text-align:center}.mw-parser-output .quotebox cite{display:block;font-style:normal}@media screen and (max-width:360px){.mw-parser-output .quotebox{min-width:100%;margin:0 0 0.8em!important;float:none!important}}</style>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">I think that the top teams will lose quite a bit, because we've had this for a little while now so you start designing the car around the systems that you have in place. It's going to be a reasonably big hit and you'll feel it everywhere: high, low speed, not so much on power but it's more under braking and high speed corners. I think the biggest problem will be is that braking and exit will be very different in corners to the feeling of the car, could be changing a lot, so that's probably the thing that you need to get used to ... there's no safety issues. We feel the circuit, we feel the car. When it's wet we have to drive the car slower because it's easier to go off because there's less grip. There's no safety issues, no.</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style=""><a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a>, on describing the effects on the car of the off-throttle blown diffuser ban.<sup id="cite_ref-Thursday_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Thursday-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>Further controversy erupted on the Friday of the Grand Prix when it was revealed that the FIA had allowed <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>-powered teams certain concessions over the ban. Where the ban prevented teams from running engine maps that simulated throttle conditions of more than 10%, Renault had applied for a special exemption to the rule on the grounds that running at 10% of full throttle conditions adversely affected the reliability of their engines. The FIA granted the concession, permitting them to run up to 50% of full throttle.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> This sparked a protest from the <a href="/wiki/Mercedes_AMG_High_Performance_Powertrains" title="Mercedes AMG High Performance Powertrains">Mercedes</a>-powered teams, in particular <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>. Red Bull Racing's <a href="/wiki/Christian_Horner" title="Christian Horner">Christian Horner</a> responded to the protest, stating that the Mercedes teams had been granted concessions of their own to manage pressure within the crank case. The FIA agreed to review the exemptions granted to the Renault-powered teams, and the concessions allowed to Renault-powered teams were revoked ahead of the final free practice session on Saturday morning;<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> however, early reports indicated that the concessions granted to Mercedes-powered teams would remain in place, with Red Bull and McLaren team principals Christian Horner and <a href="/wiki/Martin_Whitmarsh" title="Martin Whitmarsh">Martin Whitmarsh</a> seeking clarification over the matter with FIA technical delegate Charlie Whiting, who called an extraordinary meeting of the sport's Technical Working Group to resolve the dispute.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Due to the teams' dissatisfaction with the situation, the FIA has stated that it is prepared to withdraw the rule change from the German Grand Prix, restoring the <a href="/wiki/2011_European_Grand_Prix" title="2011 European Grand Prix">Valencia</a> specification of regulations, which would allow teams the freedom to run engine maps at their discretion, but require them to use the same engine maps between qualifying and the race.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> However, team principals and technical chiefs failed to come to an agreement at an extraordinary meeting on Sunday morning, with the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>-powered <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a> teams refusing to sign an agreement restoring the pre-Silverstone specifications.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> At a second meeting among the teams on Sunday afternoon, Ferrari and Sauber agreed to the terms of the proposal, abandoning the ban on blown diffusers for the 2011 season.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
