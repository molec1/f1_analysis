<p>The <b>1995 Monaco Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>LIII Grand Prix de Monaco</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 28 May 1995 at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Monaco" title="Circuit de Monaco">Circuit de Monaco</a>, <a href="/wiki/Monte_Carlo" title="Monte Carlo">Monte Carlo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Monaco" title="Monaco">Monaco</a>. It was the fifth&#160;round of the <a href="/wiki/1995_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="1995 Formula One season">1995 Formula One season</a>. The 78-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a> team after starting from second&#160;position. <a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a> finished second for <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a> after starting from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> and leading the first 23&#160;laps of the race, ahead of <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> in a <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> car. The remaining points-scoring positions were filled by <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Herbert" title="Johnny Herbert">Johnny Herbert</a> in the second Benetton, <a href="/wiki/Mark_Blundell" title="Mark Blundell">Mark Blundell</a> (<a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a> (<a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a>). Schumacher's win was his third of the season thus far and extended his lead in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">World Drivers' Championship</a> over Hill to five&#160;points. It was also <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a>'s first win in the <a href="/wiki/Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="Monaco Grand Prix">Monaco Grand Prix</a>, as Benetton's engine supplier.
</p><p>Schumacher won the race comfortably, over 30&#160;seconds ahead of Hill. Hill's speed advantage in qualifying, in which he had set a lap time almost one&#160;second faster than Schumacher, vanished in the race, enabling Schumacher to follow him closely from the start, despite carrying a heavier fuel load for a <a href="/wiki/Pit_stop" title="Pit stop">one-stop pit strategy</a>, as opposed to Hill's two. When Hill made his first pit stop, Schumacher took the lead, and stayed ahead of his rival for the remainder of the race. Hill also dropped behind the one-stopping Ferrari of <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a>, but the latter crashed on lap&#160;42 whilst attempting to avoid the lapped <a href="/wiki/Equipe_Ligier" title="Equipe Ligier">Ligier</a> of <a href="/wiki/Martin_Brundle" title="Martin Brundle">Martin Brundle</a>, who had spun in front of him. Hill's Williams teammate, <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a>, retired from the race due to a <a href="/wiki/Gearbox" class="mw-redirect" title="Gearbox">gearbox</a> failure after initially running in third place. In total, sixteen of the 26&#160;entrants failed to finish, including both drivers for the <a href="/wiki/Simtek" title="Simtek">Simtek</a> team, which subsequently ran out of money and withdrew from the sport before the <a href="/wiki/1995_Canadian_Grand_Prix" title="1995 Canadian Grand Prix">Canadian Grand Prix</a>. The race was also marked by an accident at the first corner of the first lap involving Coulthard, Alesi and Berger, which caused the track to become blocked and the race to be restarted as a result.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1995_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1995_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Heading into the fifth race of the season, <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> was leading the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a> with 24&#160;<a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Championship_points_scoring_systems" title="List of Formula One World Championship points scoring systems">points</a>, a single point ahead of <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a>. The two <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> drivers, <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a>, lay third and fourth in the rankings with 14 and 13&#160;points respectively, whilst <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> and <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Herbert" title="Johnny Herbert">Johnny Herbert</a>, in the second Williams and Benetton respectively, had scored 9&#160;points each.<sup id="cite_ref-pg118-119_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg118-119-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Just four points separated these three teams in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a>, with Ferrari (27&#160;points) leading Williams (26) and Benetton (23).<sup id="cite_ref-pg118-119_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg118-119-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> After a trying start to the season, Schumacher had dominated the preceding <a href="/wiki/1995_Spanish_Grand_Prix" title="1995 Spanish Grand Prix">Spanish Grand Prix</a>, but both championships were open, with no strong favourite for the race victory.
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Jean-Christophe_Boullion_2007_Mil_Milhas_Brasil.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/69/Jean-Christophe_Boullion_2007_Mil_Milhas_Brasil.jpg/220px-Jean-Christophe_Boullion_2007_Mil_Milhas_Brasil.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="309" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/69/Jean-Christophe_Boullion_2007_Mil_Milhas_Brasil.jpg/330px-Jean-Christophe_Boullion_2007_Mil_Milhas_Brasil.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/69/Jean-Christophe_Boullion_2007_Mil_Milhas_Brasil.jpg/440px-Jean-Christophe_Boullion_2007_Mil_Milhas_Brasil.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1101" data-file-height="1546" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Jean-Christophe_Boullion_2007_Mil_Milhas_Brasil.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Jean-Christophe_Boullion" title="Jean-Christophe Boullion">Jean-Christophe Boullion</a> (pictured in 2007) made his <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> début with the <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a> team, replacing <a href="/wiki/Karl_Wendlinger" title="Karl Wendlinger">Karl Wendlinger</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>There were two driver changes going into the event: <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> was replaced by the team's test driver, <a href="/wiki/Mark_Blundell" title="Mark Blundell">Mark Blundell</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Karl_Wendlinger" title="Karl Wendlinger">Karl Wendlinger</a> was replaced by Williams test driver and reigning <a href="/wiki/International_Formula_3000" title="International Formula 3000">International Formula 3000</a> champion <a href="/wiki/Jean-Christophe_Boullion" title="Jean-Christophe Boullion">Jean-Christophe Boullion</a>. Mansell's departure was the main talking point prior to the beginning of the race meeting, as it brought the <a href="/wiki/1992_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1992 Formula One World Championship">1992</a> World Champion's troubled relationship with McLaren to an end after just two races with the team. Mansell had made a part-time return to <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> in <a href="/wiki/1994_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1994 Formula One World Championship">1994</a> as a replacement for the late <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> at Williams, but was passed over in favour of Coulthard for 1995 and opted to sign for McLaren instead. When he tested the <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4-10" class="mw-redirect" title="McLaren MP4-10">MP4-10</a> chassis for the first time, it became apparent that it had a major handling imbalance, and, more seriously, that he did not fit comfortably in the cockpit. Mansell was thus forced to miss the opening two races of the season whilst the team built a wider <a href="/wiki/Monocoque" title="Monocoque">monocoque</a> to accommodate him. He competed in the <a href="/wiki/1995_San_Marino_Grand_Prix" title="1995 San Marino Grand Prix">San Marino</a> and Spanish Grands Prix in the wider MP4-10B, but struggled to match the pace of teammate <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> and retired his otherwise healthy car from the latter race with handling problems. Mansell and team principal <a href="/wiki/Ron_Dennis" title="Ron Dennis">Ron Dennis</a> subsequently agreed a mutual termination of their contract, and Blundell was restored to the seat that he had already occupied in the first two races. Blundell was employed on a race-by-race basis.<sup id="cite_ref-pg54-57_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg54-57-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The split marked the end of Mansell's Formula One career.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> His presence was still strong at Monaco, however, as the official tickets and publicity material featured a prominent image of his McLaren.<sup id="cite_ref-pg121_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg121-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Wendlinger had crashed heavily in practice for the previous year's <a href="/wiki/1994_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="1994 Monaco Grand Prix">Monaco Grand Prix</a>, sustaining a serious head injury from which it took the rest of the season for him to recover. Since rejoining Sauber at the beginning of the current season, he had struggled to match his pre-accident form, lapping several seconds per lap slower than teammate <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a> in the first four Grands Prix.<sup id="cite_ref-pg80-81_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg80-81-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Sauber released a statement clarifying that Wendlinger had not been sacked, and that he would still test for the team with a view to improving his performance.<sup id="cite_ref-F1Ypg112_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1Ypg112-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Boullion, who made his Formula One début as a result, was loaned to Sauber by <a href="/wiki/Frank_Williams_(Formula_One)" title="Frank Williams (Formula One)">Frank Williams</a>, who was keen to use the known quantity of his test driver to evaluate Frentzen's potential for a future race seat in his team.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Frentzen was in his second season of <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a>, but this event also marked his first race start at Monaco, as Sauber had withdrawn him from the previous year's race after Wendlinger's accident.<sup id="cite_ref-F1Ypg110_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1Ypg110-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The state of the <a href="/wiki/Simtek" title="Simtek">Simtek</a> team's finances was also a major topic of interest. On the Thursday before the race weekend, team principal <a href="/wiki/Nick_Wirth" title="Nick Wirth">Nick Wirth</a> held a <a href="/wiki/News_conference" class="mw-redirect" title="News conference">press conference</a> in which he admitted that the outfit faced imminent closure unless it received money that had been promised by various sponsors.<sup id="cite_ref-R&amp;R_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-R&amp;R-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Wirth admitted that Simtek faced a budget shortfall of several million dollars needed to complete the season, part of which was due to him being the victim of "a broken deal". He also pledged to arrange a meeting of all Simtek's financial backers over the race weekend in an attempt to raise additional capital, and that the team would not travel to the following <a href="/wiki/1995_Canadian_Grand_Prix" title="1995 Canadian Grand Prix">Canadian Grand Prix</a>, an expensive "fly-away" race, unless a rescue package was negotiated successfully.<sup id="cite_ref-loccfstc_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-loccfstc-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The team was already reduced to three gearboxes for the Monaco race meeting.<sup id="cite_ref-R&amp;R_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-R&amp;R-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Due to the configuration of the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Monaco" title="Circuit de Monaco">Circuit de Monaco</a>, with its low average speed and abundance of low-speed corners, allied to the low-grip nature of the public road surface, the teams all set their <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_car" title="Formula One car">cars</a> up to produce the maximum amount of <a href="/wiki/Downforce" title="Downforce">downforce</a> and mechanical grip possible. Ferrari introduced a revised rear wing and <a href="/wiki/Diffuser_(automotive)" title="Diffuser (automotive)">diffuser</a> design for the <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_412_T2" title="Ferrari 412 T2">412T2</a> chassis, allowing more space for downforce-generating winglets around the rear bodywork. The team also introduced two new <a href="/wiki/Suspension_(vehicle)" class="mw-redirect" title="Suspension (vehicle)">suspension</a> configurations, one used by Alesi and the other tested by Berger on his  <a href="/wiki/Spare_car" class="mw-redirect" title="Spare car">spare car</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Piola_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Piola-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> As was traditional at Monaco, Ferrari also brought an additional spare car, bringing the team's total number of chassis to four.<sup id="cite_ref-ASpg27_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ASpg27-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The Sauber team introduced new front and rear wings and a revised diffuser, in addition to a slightly lightened <a href="/wiki/Sauber_C14" title="Sauber C14">C14</a> chassis design. Benetton, <a href="/wiki/Footwork_Arrows" title="Footwork Arrows">Footwork</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_Racing" title="Tyrrell Racing">Tyrrell</a> also added additional flaps and wings to the rear bodywork of their cars, whilst the latter team also applied its "Hydrolink" suspension design to the rear of the <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_023" title="Tyrrell 023">023</a> for the first time.<sup id="cite_ref-R&amp;R_11-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-R&amp;R-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Piola_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Piola-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Lead <a href="/wiki/Forti" title="Forti">Forti</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Diniz" title="Pedro Diniz">Pedro Diniz</a> was given a new monocoque which weighed 4 kilograms (8.8&#160;lb) less than the first two <a href="/wiki/Forti_FG01" title="Forti FG01">FG01</a> tubs to have been built, and <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a> débuted a new exhaust system on its <a href="/wiki/Minardi_M195" title="Minardi M195">M195</a> to increase the power produced by the <a href="/wiki/Ford_Motor_Company" title="Ford Motor Company">Ford</a> engine.<sup id="cite_ref-R&amp;R_11-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-R&amp;R-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice_and_qualifying">Practice and qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1995_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Practice and qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Two practice sessions were held before the race; the first was held from 09:30 to 11:15 local time on Thursday, and an identically timed session was also held on Saturday. Each driver was limited to 23&#160;laps of free practice per day. The <a href="/wiki/List_of_motorsport_terminology#Q" class="mw-redirect" title="List of motorsport terminology">qualifying period</a> was split into two one-hour sessions; the first was held on Thursday afternoon from 13:00 to 14:00 local time, with the second held on Saturday afternoon at the same time. The fastest time set by each driver from either session counted towards his final <a href="/wiki/Standing_start" title="Standing start">grid position</a>. Each driver was limited to twelve&#160;laps per qualifying session.<sup id="cite_ref-F1Ypg216-217_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1Ypg216-217-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Alesi set the pace in Thursday free practice, which took place in bright and sunny weather conditions, with a time of 1:25.457. Schumacher and Hill were second and third, separated from their teammates by the two improved McLarens, with Häkkinen ahead of the returning Blundell. Berger was over two&#160;seconds slower than Alesi with the ninth-fastest lap time.<sup id="cite_ref-pg128-129_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg128-129-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher was unhappy with the handling of his car, and so the rear end of the <a href="/wiki/Benetton_B195" title="Benetton B195">B195</a> was stripped down for a precautionary check before qualifying.<sup id="cite_ref-pg121_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg121-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Further back, Frentzen set the eighteenth-fastest time after his Sauber shed its engine cover during the session.<sup id="cite_ref-ASQNIB_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ASQNIB-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Boullion fared even worse, as he crashed at the Nouvelle Chicane in a manner reminiscent of Wendlinger's accident the year before, but he was unhurt.<sup id="cite_ref-ASQSauber_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ASQSauber-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Simtek's shortage of gearboxes was also apparent: lead driver <a href="/wiki/Jos_Verstappen" title="Jos Verstappen">Jos Verstappen</a>'s unit only allowed him to complete five&#160;laps before developing problems, but teammate <a href="/wiki/Domenico_Schiattarella" title="Domenico Schiattarella">Domenico Schiattarella</a> set an encouraging 14th-fastest time.<sup id="cite_ref-R&amp;R_11-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-R&amp;R-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatright" style="width:40%;&#32;;"><style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r887775652">.mw-parser-output .quotebox{background-color:#F9F9F9;border:1px solid #aaa;box-sizing:border-box;padding:10px;font-size:88%;max-width:100%}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft{margin:0.5em 1.4em 0.8em 0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright{margin:0.5em 0 0.8em 1.4em}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.centered{margin:0.5em auto 0.8em auto}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft p,.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright p{font-style:inherit}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-title{background-color:#F9F9F9;text-align:center;font-size:larger;font-weight:bold}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:before{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" “ ";vertical-align:-45%;line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:after{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" ” ";line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .left-aligned{text-align:left}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .right-aligned{text-align:right}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .center-aligned{text-align:center}.mw-parser-output .quotebox cite{display:block;font-style:normal}@media screen and (max-width:360px){.mw-parser-output .quotebox{min-width:100%;margin:0 0 0.8em!important;float:none!important}}</style>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">"Here, unlike most of the tracks, the driver contributes 60&#160;per cent of the result against 40 for the car. This is because the qualities of the chassis are not so important on these twists and turns. Guts are more important."</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style=""><a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a>, commenting on taking provisional <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> on Thursday.<sup id="cite_ref-F1Ypg110_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1Ypg110-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>Thursday qualifying was held in similar weather conditions to practice, but was interrupted by a brief shower of rain close to the end of the session.<sup id="cite_ref-pg128-129_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg128-129-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Alesi carried his form over from free practice, nudging the crash barriers lining the circuit on more than one occasion to set a provisional <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> time of 1:23.754. Berger was also impressive, power-sliding through Casino Square, to the delight of the crowd, on his way to the third-fastest time, although he complained that Alesi had held him up on what he thought would have been his quickest lap of the session. The form of the Ferraris came as something of a surprise, as the 41T2's unique <a href="/wiki/V12_engine" title="V12 engine">V12 engine</a> configuration theoretically produced less <a href="/wiki/Torque" title="Torque">torque</a> than its rivals, a disadvantage on a circuit as tight as Monaco.<sup id="cite_ref-pg121_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg121-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Alesi said that he believed the results of the session were due to the extreme effort that he and Berger were putting into their driving overcoming any deficiencies with the car.<sup id="cite_ref-F1Ypg110_10-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1Ypg110-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The Ferraris were split by Schumacher, who felt that the rain shower had prevented him from fulfilling his potential, as he did not complete his full quota of 12&#160;laps as a result of the track conditions becoming slower. His teammate, Herbert, took provisional sixth&#160;place. Of the Williams drivers, Hill was fourth, but Coulthard admitted that he was still learning the track on his way to 11th, 2.8&#160;seconds slower than Alesi's best.<sup id="cite_ref-pg121_6-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg121-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Häkkinen continued McLaren's promising form with a provisional fifth&#160;place, but was fined $10,000 for ignoring <a href="/wiki/Racing_flags#Yellow_flag" title="Racing flags">yellow flags</a> during the season.<sup id="cite_ref-pg121_6-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg121-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Blundell was back in eighth after crashing into the barriers in the closing minutes of the session.<sup id="cite_ref-pg121_6-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg121-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Frentzen impressed by setting the seventh-fastest time in his first Monaco qualifying session, despite having to take to the escape road at the Nouvelle Chicane after encountering the rain shower whilst on a flying lap.<sup id="cite_ref-F1Ypg110_10-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1Ypg110-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> After his morning accident, Boullion drove steadily to take a provisional 21st&#160;position, complaining of a dire lack of grip from the <a href="/wiki/Sauber_C14" title="Sauber C14">C14</a> chassis.<sup id="cite_ref-F1Ypg112_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1Ypg112-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Another Monaco rookie, <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a>, out-paced his more experienced <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a> teammate, <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a>, as the pair set the ninth and 13th-fastest times respectively. They were split by Coulthard and the <a href="/wiki/Equipe_Ligier" title="Equipe Ligier">Ligier</a> drivers, with <a href="/wiki/Martin_Brundle" title="Martin Brundle">Martin Brundle</a> ahead of <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Panis" title="Olivier Panis">Olivier Panis</a>, despite the former crashing at La Rascasse and removing his rear wing as the rain fell. <a href="/wiki/Gianni_Morbidelli" title="Gianni Morbidelli">Gianni Morbidelli</a>, who took 14th&#160;place, was another driver to crash during the sudden change to wet conditions, as did Footwork teammate <a href="/wiki/Taki_Inoue" title="Taki Inoue">Taki Inoue</a> on his way to 24th&#160;position. Minardi drivers <a href="/wiki/Luca_Badoer" title="Luca Badoer">Luca Badoer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pierluigi_Martini" title="Pierluigi Martini">Pierluigi Martini</a> occupied the provisional eighth&#160;row of the grid, just ahead of Tyrrell teammates <a href="/wiki/Mika_Salo" title="Mika Salo">Mika Salo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ukyo_Katayama" title="Ukyo Katayama">Ukyo Katayama</a>, both of whom complained of poor handling over the bumps and <a href="/wiki/Understeer" class="mw-redirect" title="Understeer">understeer</a>, despite the expected benefits of the Hydrolink suspension.<sup id="cite_ref-pg128-129_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg128-129-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-R&amp;R_11-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-R&amp;R-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Towards the back of the Thursday time sheet, Schiattarella and Verstappen shared the former's chassis to set the 20th and 19th-fastest times respectively, as Verstappen's gearbox was still refusing to run smoothly. Schiattarella spun at La Rascasse and attempted to execute a spin-turn at the blind entry to the corner, almost collecting <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Moreno" title="Roberto Moreno">Roberto Moreno</a>'s Forti in the process; he was given a $20,000 fine, suspended for three races, for "creating a very dangerous situation".<sup id="cite_ref-ASQNIB_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ASQNIB-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Verstappen then took over the car and crashed it, restricting the Simtek team to a total of nine&#160;laps between its two drivers. Behind Boullion, <a href="/wiki/Andrea_Montermini" title="Andrea Montermini">Andrea Montermini</a> (<a href="/wiki/Pacific_Racing" title="Pacific Racing">Pacific</a>) set the 22nd-quickest time, ahead of Moreno, who had to share his car with Diniz after the latter's gearbox failed, restricting him to 25th&#160;place, over four&#160;seconds slower than his teammate.<sup id="cite_ref-pg128-129_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg128-129-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-R&amp;R_11-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-R&amp;R-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Occupying the last&#160;spot on the provisional grid, <a href="/wiki/Bertrand_Gachot" title="Bertrand Gachot">Bertrand Gachot</a> failed to set a time due to a sheared brake disc on his Pacific, which caused his left-rear wheel to fall off on his first out-lap, forcing him to abandon his car out on the circuit.<sup id="cite_ref-R&amp;R_11-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-R&amp;R-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Inoue_Footwork_Monaco.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/af/Inoue_Footwork_Monaco.jpg/220px-Inoue_Footwork_Monaco.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="198" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/af/Inoue_Footwork_Monaco.jpg/330px-Inoue_Footwork_Monaco.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/af/Inoue_Footwork_Monaco.jpg/440px-Inoue_Footwork_Monaco.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1024" data-file-height="921" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Inoue_Footwork_Monaco.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Taki_Inoue" title="Taki Inoue">Taki Inoue</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Footwork_Arrows" title="Footwork Arrows">Footwork</a> is transported back to the pit lane having been hit by the <a href="/wiki/Safety_car" title="Safety car">safety car</a> after Saturday morning practice.</div></div></div>
<p>After taking Friday off – a feature of the event timetable unique to Monaco – the drivers returned to action on Saturday in bright and warm conditions.<sup id="cite_ref-pg128-129_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg128-129-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Hill set the pace with a time of 1:23.468, which was the fastest lap of the weekend thus far.<sup id="cite_ref-pg121_6-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg121-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> He was almost 0.8&#160;seconds ahead of Alesi, who in turn led Coulthard, Häkkinen, Berger and Panis.<sup id="cite_ref-pg128-129_1-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg128-129-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher was back in seventh&#160;place after damaging his car's suspension in a collision with Frentzen exiting Casino Square, restricting him to 11&#160;laps only.<sup id="cite_ref-pg121_6-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg121-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Frentzen later crashed heavily after losing control under braking for Massenet, punching a hole in the Sauber's monocoque in the process.<sup id="cite_ref-ASQSauber_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ASQSauber-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Blundell also crashed his McLaren at La Rascasse, but the damage was easier to repair.<sup id="cite_ref-ASQNIB_16-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ASQNIB-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The main drama of the day occurred after the practice session had ended. Inoue had spun and stalled his Footwork, which was being towed back to the pit lane by a recovery vehicle when it was struck from behind by the <a href="/wiki/Renault_Clio" title="Renault Clio">Renault Clio</a> <a href="/wiki/Safety_car" title="Safety car">safety car</a> – which was being taken on quick demonstration laps of the circuit in the hands of rally driver <a href="/wiki/Jean_Ragnotti" title="Jean Ragnotti">Jean Ragnotti</a>, with the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile</a>'s press delegate as passenger – in the middle of the Piscine complex. The impact was sufficient to overturn the Footwork and render it a <a href="/wiki/Write-off" title="Write-off">write-off</a>: the <a href="/wiki/Footwork_FA16" title="Footwork FA16">FA16</a> sustained severe rear-end damage to the engine and gearbox, whilst the tow rope, attached to the car's crash structure behind the driver's head, pulled the roll hoop from the chassis.<sup id="cite_ref-ASInoue_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ASInoue-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Inoue, who still sitting in the cockpit with his helmet on but his seatbelts unfastened, sustained two impacts to the head, the severity of which was shown by the fact that a chunk had been taken out of his helmet. He was taken to <a href="/wiki/The_Princess_Grace_Hospital_Centre" class="mw-redirect" title="The Princess Grace Hospital Centre">The Princess Grace Hospital Centre</a> for a brain scan and, although he was found to have sustained only a slight <a href="/wiki/Concussion" title="Concussion">concussion</a>, was not permitted to take part in the afternoon qualifying session as a precaution.<sup id="cite_ref-pg121_6-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg121-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-JRCCC_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JRCCC-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> The Footwork team principal, <a href="/wiki/Jackie_Oliver" title="Jackie Oliver">Jackie Oliver</a>, was infuriated by the incident, and wrote an official letter of complaint to the <a href="/wiki/Automobile_Club_de_Monaco" title="Automobile Club de Monaco">Automobile Club de Monaco</a>, the body responsible for organising the event. He said that Inoue would have been killed had he not been wearing his helmet, and questioned the organisation of the ACM and Ragnotti's attitude: "Why was Ragnotti out there? It's just lack of discipline. I understand he had accomplished a couple of laps before at a million miles an hour with <a href="/wiki/Handbrake_turn" title="Handbrake turn">handbrake turns</a> at the Loews <a href="/wiki/Hairpin_turn" title="Hairpin turn">hairpin</a>. What was the purpose of him doing it? Was it to give officials a thrill?"<sup id="cite_ref-ASInoue_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ASInoue-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> The stewards tacitly acknowledged that the incident was not Inoue's fault by authorising him to use the team's spare car for qualifying, but this was rendered academic by his non-participation in the session.<sup id="cite_ref-pg121_6-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg121-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">"When Michael did his quick lap early in the session, I thought Benetton was seriously back in the groove again and we would be in trouble, but it all unfolded for me. My last run was the nearest to a perfect lap I think I have ever produced. Now I've got my sixth pole and I want to follow that with the sixth win for the Hill family at Monaco."</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style=""><a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a>, commenting on taking <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>, and the prospect of adding to his <a href="/wiki/Graham_Hill" title="Graham Hill">father's</a> total of Monaco wins, on Saturday.<sup id="cite_ref-pg123_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg123-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>The afternoon's final qualifying session took place in similar good weather conditions to the morning's running.<sup id="cite_ref-pg128-129_1-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg128-129-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The Ligier drivers were the first to set quick times, and then the leading drivers began to complete their first runs: Schumacher took provisional pole position with a time of 1:23.139, set twenty&#160;minutes into the session, and Berger went 0.5&#160;seconds to move up to third. Five minutes later, Hill lowered the benchmark to 1:23.294; Schumacher responded with a time of 1:22.742 eight minutes afterwards; and then Hill put pole position beyond the reach of anyone else after completing a lap in 1:22.115.<sup id="cite_ref-pg123_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg123-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ASQHill_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ASQHill-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher was unable to improve his time on his final run, and then Hill trimmed the pole time to 1:21.952 with his own last effort of the session.<sup id="cite_ref-pg123_22-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg123-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> It was a dominant performance from the Williams team leader, who went fastest in each of the circuit's three timing sectors, and set three distinct lap times which were all faster than Schumacher's best.<sup id="cite_ref-ASQHill_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ASQHill-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Whilst Hill was delighted with his performance, Schumacher found that his Benetton was more difficult to control, and he blamed the disruptive effect of his collision with Frentzen in the morning session for the 0.8&#160;second gap between him and Hill.<sup id="cite_ref-pg123_22-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg123-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Damon_Hill_juillet_1995.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b6/Damon_Hill_juillet_1995.jpg/220px-Damon_Hill_juillet_1995.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="327" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b6/Damon_Hill_juillet_1995.jpg/330px-Damon_Hill_juillet_1995.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/b/b6/Damon_Hill_juillet_1995.jpg 2x" data-file-width="341" data-file-height="507" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Damon_Hill_juillet_1995.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a> took a clear <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>, 0.8&#160;seconds ahead of second-placed <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>Behind the two leading runners, Ferrari's challenge for pole position faded during the final session. Alesi's car ground to a halt on his first out-lap of the session after losing hydraulic pressure, and he had to share Berger's chassis for the remainder of qualifying. This forced Berger to hurry through his planned programme, making three runs instead of four, before handing his car over to his teammate. The extensive adjustments required to tailor the car's pedal system from Berger to Alesi's preferred set-up meant that he left the pits with only two&#160;minutes and 38&#160;seconds of the session remaining. Alesi thus had time for just one flying lap, which was spoiled when Irvine crashed his Jordan at Tabac during the final minutes. As a result, Alesi failed to improve on his Thursday time and dropped to fifth&#160;place on the grid, half a second and one&#160;position behind Berger.<sup id="cite_ref-F1Ypg110_10-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1Ypg110-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ASQFerrari_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ASQFerrari-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> The team's problems allowed Coulthard to improve to third place with a time of 1:23.109, continuing his progress as he became more familiar with the circuit. Häkkinen was another driver to be caught out by Irvine's crash at the end of the session, restricting him to sixth&#160;place on the grid ahead of Herbert, who was satisfied to qualify only a second behind Schumacher.<sup id="cite_ref-R&amp;R_11-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-R&amp;R-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Blundell rounded out the top ten in the second McLaren, despite going up the escape road after missing his braking point at Sainte Dévote. Brundle took eighth and Irvine ninth despite his crash, which meant that all six of the British drivers qualified in the top ten.<sup id="cite_ref-ASQNIB_16-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ASQNIB-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-pg123_22-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg123-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Barrichello, outqualified by Irvine at every event thus far in 1995, set the 11th-fastest time, ahead of Panis – who complained of heavy traffic – and Morbidelli. Frentzen, like Alesi, was scheduled to take over his teammate's car after his own proved too heavily damaged to repair, but the Sauber team's programme was interrupted when Boullion crashed, also at Massanet. Frentzen thus failed to set a time and dropped to 14th on the grid, whilst Boullion had improved his time by three&#160;seconds before crashing, and took 19th&#160;place.<sup id="cite_ref-ASQSauber_17-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ASQSauber-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> They were split by the Tyrrell and Minardi drivers, in the order of Katayama, Badoer, Salo and Martini.<sup id="cite_ref-pg128-129_1-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg128-129-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Nearing the back of the grid, Schiattarella outqualified Verstappen for the first time by taking 20th&#160;position on the grid; Verstappen failed to improve on his Thursday time after another crash and further gearbox problems, and was restricted to 23rd. Gachot set the 21st-fastest time for Pacific, but on this occasion it was Montermini's turn to not set a time as a result of his car suffering a gearbox failure; he dropped to 25th&#160;place. As on Thursday, the Forti drivers again had to share a single car, as Moreno's car developed a misfire: he qualified in 24th&#160;position, two places behind Diniz.<sup id="cite_ref-R&amp;R_11-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-R&amp;R-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The grid, which was covered by just over 9.5&#160;seconds, was completed by the absent Inoue.<sup id="cite_ref-pg128-129_1-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg128-129-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1995_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On Sunday morning, a pre-race warm-up session took place at 11:00 local time, and lasted for 30&#160;minutes.<sup id="cite_ref-F1Ypg216-217_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1Ypg216-217-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> It took place in dry weather conditions.<sup id="cite_ref-pg128-129_1-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg128-129-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Alesi led his teammate Berger at the top of the standings with a time of 1:24.356; both Ferrari drivers trying their race and spare cars.<sup id="cite_ref-pg128-129_1-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg128-129-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-pg123_22-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg123-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher was third, ahead of Häkkinen, Herbert, and Hill, who was concerned about the development of understeer in the handling of his race car, particularly in the track's slower corners.<sup id="cite_ref-pg123_22-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg123-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard set the 11th-fastest time, and the Williams team, worried about its cars' pace in race trim, opted to switch its planned pit strategy from one stop to two, hoping that the cars would handle better on the relatively lighter fuel load required for such tactics.<sup id="cite_ref-F1Ypg112_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1Ypg112-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-tp_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tp-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Meanwhile, the Sauber mechanics had built up a new spare car for Frentzen (who was now driving the team's original spare car after the damage to his race car was found to be irreparable) and also repaired Boullion's damaged machine.<sup id="cite_ref-R&amp;R_11-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-R&amp;R-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ASpg25_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ASpg25-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Their Jordan counterparts had performed similar work on Irvine's car, whilst Barrichello's car suffered a broken wheel rim. Inoue was cleared to race, and set the slowest time of the session in the spare Footwork.<sup id="cite_ref-pg123_22-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg123-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As was traditional in Monaco, the race began at the relatively late time of 15:30, allowing <a href="/wiki/Rainier_III,_Prince_of_Monaco" title="Rainier III, Prince of Monaco">Prince Rainier</a> and his family to eat lunch before viewing the event from the royal box on the start-finish straight.<sup id="cite_ref-ASpg26_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ASpg26-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> The race took place in warm, sunny conditions, with an ambient temperature of 23&#160;°C (73&#160;°F), and was watched by a total of approximately 48,000 spectators.<sup id="cite_ref-F1Ypg117_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1Ypg117-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> As the drivers completed their <a href="/wiki/List_of_motorsport_terminology#O" class="mw-redirect" title="List of motorsport terminology">reconnaissance laps</a> from the pit-lane to the starting grid, an oil leak was discovered on Frentzen's Sauber, so he switched to the newly built spare car in time for the start.<sup id="cite_ref-pg123_22-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg123-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As the green light signalled the start of the race, Hill and Schumacher both made brisk starts and led into Sainte Dévote, but behind them a multiple collision between Coulthard and the two Ferraris caused the race to be <a href="/wiki/Racing_flags#Red_flag" title="Racing flags">red-flagged</a> to a halt. Coulthard was slightly slower away from the line and was challenged on the outside of the run to the first corner by Berger, and on the inside by Alesi. As the track narrowed at the entry to Sainte Devote, there was not enough space for the three cars to run side-by-side, and Alesi made contact with the rear-right quarter of Coulthard's car, which in turn pushed it into Berger. The Williams was launched up into the air and spun through 180 degrees before crashing down again and coming to a halt at the apex of the corner with broken suspension, whilst the two damaged Ferraris also made further contact with each other before coming to rest at the same point.<sup id="cite_ref-ASpg26_27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ASpg26-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-pg127_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg127-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Most of the pursuing drivers, led by Brundle, threaded their way through the stationary cars, but Irvine knocked off his <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_car#Aerodynamics" title="Formula One car">front wing</a>, Boullion sustained a broken diffuser, and Katayama, Salo, Morbidelli, Inoue and Schiattarella were all forced to come to a halt as they negotiated the obstacles.<sup id="cite_ref-R&amp;R_11-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-R&amp;R-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-pg127_29-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg127-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-VHS_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VHS-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> With the track now completely blocked, the race was halted and preparations were immediately made for a completely fresh start.<sup id="cite_ref-pg127_29-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg127-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Coulthard, Berger and Alesi's cars had been damaged beyond immediate repair; all three were thus forced to race their teams' spare cars, vindicating Ferrari's customary Monaco practice of bringing an additional car for each driver.<sup id="cite_ref-ASpg27_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ASpg27-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Berger's spare car, however, was fitted with an earlier-specification engine that did not produce quite as much power as his race car or Alesi's spare car, whilst the throttle on Coulthard's car was not fully calibrated in the rush to ready it for the restarted race.<sup id="cite_ref-R&amp;R_11-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-R&amp;R-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ASpg27_14-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ASpg27-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Irvine's Jordan and Boullion's Sauber were repaired, and the other drivers stuck at the first corner were able to restart their cars, apart from Salo and Schiattarella: the former's <a href="/wiki/Yamaha_Motor_Company" title="Yamaha Motor Company">Yamaha</a> engine overheated in the jam, requiring him to take the restart from the pit-lane in the spare Tyrrell, and the latter's Simtek was damaged by marshals attempting to move it from the scene of the accident.<sup id="cite_ref-R&amp;R_11-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-R&amp;R-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ASpg27_14-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ASpg27-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The impoverished team lacked a spare car, so Schiattarella could not take any further part in the race, and Simtek's participation in the event was ended – without either car completing a racing lap – when Verstappen's car suffered a recurrence of its persistent gearbox problems and was pushed into the pit-lane as the rest of the field embarked upon the second <a href="/wiki/Parade_lap" title="Parade lap">formation lap</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pg127_29-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg127-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At the restart, Hill and Schumacher again held their positions, whilst Coulthard led the more circumspect Ferraris through Sainte Dévote, with Alesi jumping ahead of Berger.<sup id="cite_ref-pg127_29-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg127-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> On this occasion there were no major incidents, although Katayama sustained light damage to his car's front wing as a result of a brush with Morbidelli.<sup id="cite_ref-R&amp;R_11-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-R&amp;R-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> As the drivers completed the first lap, Hill led by 0.5&#160;seconds from Schumacher,<sup id="cite_ref-pg127_29-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg127-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> who was followed in turn by Coulthard, Alesi, Berger, Herbert, Häkkinen, Irvine, Brundle, Barrichello, Blundell, Panis, Frentzen, Badoer, Morbidelli, Katayama, Martini, Boullion, Gachot, Moreno, Montermini, Salo, Diniz and Inoue.<sup id="cite_ref-pg128-129_1-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg128-129-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> In the early laps, Hill gradually built a lead of nearly two&#160;seconds over Schumacher by lap&#160;10, with the two pulling away in tandem from Coulthard, who was at this point a further eight&#160;seconds behind the Benetton.<sup id="cite_ref-ASpg27_14-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ASpg27-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ASpg28_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ASpg28-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> By this stage there had already been two retirements: Häkkinen's <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_HighPerformanceEngines" class="mw-redirect" title="Mercedes-Benz HighPerformanceEngines">Mercedes</a> engine failed due to a fuel pump drive problem on the ninth&#160;lap of the race, and Moreno spun at Sainte Dévote when a brake fluid leak caused him to lose control of his Forti a lap later.<sup id="cite_ref-R&amp;R_11-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-R&amp;R-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Morbidelli also made an early unscheduled stop to cure a serious tyre vibration caused by a section of tie-rope from a tyre-warming blanket becoming jammed in one of his rear wheels, the Footwork driver dropping to the tail of the field in the process.<sup id="cite_ref-pg127_29-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg127-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>This was the third event to take place with electronic sensors monitoring every car for jump-starts, and at this stage of the race, six drivers (Barrichello, Brundle, Montermini, Frentzen, Morbidelli and Panis) were issued with ten-second <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_motorsport_terms#S" title="Glossary of motorsport terms">stop-go penalties</a> for this offence.<sup id="cite_ref-pg127_29-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg127-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> All except Montermini came in promptly to serve their penalties – some even having to form a queue in the penalty area – but the Pacific driver failed to pit within the stipulated three laps of receiving it, whereupon he was disqualified from the race, having climbed to 17th&#160;place before belatedly serving his penalty.<sup id="cite_ref-pg128-129_1-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg128-129-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ASpg28_31-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ASpg28-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> The penalised drivers dropped back through the field as a result of losing time, with the result that after 16&#160;laps completed, the race order was: Hill, Schumacher, Coulthard, Alesi, Berger, Herbert, Irvine, Blundell, Badoer, Katayama, Brundle, Martini, Barrichello, Panis, Frentzen, Boullion, Salo, Montermini (yet to serve his penalty), Gachot, Inoue, Diniz and Morbidelli.<sup id="cite_ref-pg128-129_1-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg128-129-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1995_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The incident between Inoue and the safety car in practice was referred to the <a href="/wiki/FIA_World_Motor_Sport_Council" title="FIA World Motor Sport Council">FIA World Motor Sport Council</a>, which met one month after the race. The ACM received no punishment for the incident, and negotiated the payment of damages to Footwork directly with the team.<sup id="cite_ref-WCD_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WCD-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
