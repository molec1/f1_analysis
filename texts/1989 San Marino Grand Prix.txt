<p>The <b>1989 San Marino Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>IX Gran Premio Kronenbourg di San Marino</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at the <a href="/wiki/Autodromo_Enzo_e_Dino_Ferrari" class="mw-redirect" title="Autodromo Enzo e Dino Ferrari">Imola circuit</a> on 23 April 1989. It was the second race of the <a href="/wiki/1989_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="1989 Formula One season">1989 Formula One season</a>. The race was overshadowed by <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a>'s massive accident at Tamburello corner. The race was stopped for one hour and restarted. The race won by <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> who started from pole position.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1989_San_Marino_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-qualifying_report">Pre-qualifying report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1989_San_Marino_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Pre-qualifying report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As at the previous race in Brazil, The <a href="/wiki/Brabham" title="Brabham">Brabhams</a> were easily the fastest cars in the Friday morning session. <a href="/wiki/Stefano_Modena" title="Stefano Modena">Stefano Modena</a> was quickest on this occasion, with <a href="/wiki/Martin_Brundle" title="Martin Brundle">Martin Brundle</a> in second. <a href="/wiki/Alex_Caffi" title="Alex Caffi">Alex Caffi</a> pre-qualified his <a href="/wiki/Dallara" title="Dallara">Dallara</a> in third, with <a href="/wiki/Nicola_Larini" title="Nicola Larini">Nicola Larini</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Osella" title="Osella">Osella</a> in fourth. Only four cars went through from this session now that the injured <a href="/wiki/Philippe_Streiff" title="Philippe Streiff">Philippe Streiff</a> had been replaced at <a href="/wiki/Automobiles_Gonfaronnaises_Sportives" title="Automobiles Gonfaronnaises Sportives">AGS</a> by <a href="/wiki/Gabriele_Tarquini" title="Gabriele Tarquini">Gabriele Tarquini</a>, who had left the abortive <a href="/wiki/FIRST_(constructor)" class="mw-redirect" title="FIRST (constructor)">FIRST</a> team.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Onyx_Grand_Prix" title="Onyx Grand Prix">Onyx</a> improved on their showing in Brazil, with <a href="/wiki/Bertrand_Gachot" title="Bertrand Gachot">Bertrand Gachot</a> just missing out on pre-qualification in fifth place. The <a href="/wiki/EuroBrun" title="EuroBrun">EuroBrun</a> of <a href="/wiki/Gregor_Foitek" title="Gregor Foitek">Gregor Foitek</a> was sixth, ahead of the second Osella of <a href="/wiki/Piercarlo_Ghinzani" title="Piercarlo Ghinzani">Piercarlo Ghinzani</a>. The other Onyx of <a href="/wiki/Stefan_Johansson" title="Stefan Johansson">Stefan Johansson</a> was eighth, followed by the second AGS of <a href="/wiki/Joachim_Winkelhock" title="Joachim Winkelhock">Joachim Winkelhock</a>. Tenth was the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Coloni" title="Scuderia Coloni">Coloni</a> of <a href="/wiki/Pierre-Henri_Raphanel" title="Pierre-Henri Raphanel">Pierre-Henri Raphanel</a>, just ahead of the two <a href="/wiki/Zakspeed" title="Zakspeed">Zakspeeds</a> of <a href="/wiki/Aguri_Suzuki" title="Aguri Suzuki">Aguri Suzuki</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bernd_Schneider_(racing_driver)" title="Bernd Schneider (racing driver)">Bernd Schneider</a>, the Japanese driver outpacing his more experienced team-mate. Nearly four seconds adrift at the bottom of the time sheets was the <a href="/wiki/Rial_Racing" title="Rial Racing">Rial</a> of <a href="/wiki/Volker_Weidler" title="Volker Weidler">Volker Weidler</a>, despite the German trying all three of the team's cars.<sup id="cite_ref-Walker_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Walker-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying_report">Qualifying report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1989_San_Marino_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Qualifying report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The only change to the entry list for the San Marino Grand Prix was the arrival of <a href="/wiki/Gabriele_Tarquini" title="Gabriele Tarquini">Gabriele Tarquini</a> in the second car of the small <a href="/wiki/Automobiles_Gonfaronnaises_Sportives" title="Automobiles Gonfaronnaises Sportives">AGS</a> team, which had only run one car in <a href="/wiki/1989_Brazilian_Grand_Prix" title="1989 Brazilian Grand Prix">Brazil</a> after <a href="/wiki/Philippe_Streiff" title="Philippe Streiff">Philippe Streiff</a> had suffered career ending injuries in pre-season testing. A record 39 cars were entered for the Grand Prix,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> although only 26 were allowed to start the race.
</p><p>After their defeat in <a href="/wiki/1989_Brazilian_Grand_Prix" title="1989 Brazilian Grand Prix">Brazil</a>, <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> spent eight days testing at Imola prior to the San Marino Grand Prix. According to <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a>, they tested everything on the <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/5" title="McLaren MP4/5">McLaren MP4/5</a>, including aerodynamics, suspension, brakes and fuel consumption. It worked for Senna and <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a>, as they locked out the front row and were over 1.5 seconds faster than the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> of <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a>. <a href="/wiki/Riccardo_Patrese" title="Riccardo Patrese">Riccardo Patrese</a> showed his and <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a>' revival with fourth on the grid followed by <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> (Ferrari) with <a href="/wiki/Thierry_Boutsen" title="Thierry Boutsen">Thierry Boutsen</a> rounding out the top six.
</p><p>At <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_Racing" title="Tyrrell Racing">Tyrrell</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michele_Alboreto" title="Michele Alboreto">Michele Alboreto</a> failed to qualify for a race for the first time since the <a href="/wiki/1981_German_Grand_Prix" title="1981 German Grand Prix">1981 German Grand Prix</a>, in the new <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_018" title="Tyrrell 018">Tyrrell 018</a>. Only one 018 was available, and although team mate <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Palmer" title="Jonathan Palmer">Jonathan Palmer</a> managed to sneak onto the grid in 25th in the older model <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_017" title="Tyrrell 017">017</a>, he raced the 018.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1989_San_Marino_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_report">Race report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1989_San_Marino_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Race report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the start Ayrton Senna got away well but behind him <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> found himself just ahead of <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> but the Englishman could not find his way around Prost's <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>.  Mansell fell off a little after that and found himself battling with <a href="/wiki/Riccardo_Patrese" title="Riccardo Patrese">Riccardo Patrese</a> while on the second lap <a href="/wiki/Ivan_Capelli" title="Ivan Capelli">Ivan Capelli</a> had a nasty accident in his <a href="/wiki/March_Engineering" title="March Engineering">March</a>. On lap four, fifth-placed <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a>'s Ferrari speared off the track at the fast Tamburello corner due to a mechanical failure. Berger hit the wall at an estimated 180&#160;mph and when his car came to a rest it was covered in fuel and it immediately burst into flames. Three fire marshalls (Bruno Miniati, Paolo Verdi and Gabriele Violi) arrived on foot sixteen seconds after impact and the fire was put out ten seconds later; the fuel had also burned up in the inferno. The race was red-flagged and Berger escaped with broken ribs and second-degree burns.
</p><p>The race was restarted after half an hour and run a further 55 laps on aggregate timing. This time Prost got away much better and got past Senna while behind them Mansell made a poor start and fell behind Patrese and <a href="/wiki/Alessandro_Nannini" title="Alessandro Nannini">Alessandro Nannini</a>. On the run-down to the Tosa hairpin Senna got alongside Prost into Villeneuve and out-braked him into Tosa. The McLarens proceeded to pull away from the competition while behind them there was more drama as <a href="/wiki/Stefano_Modena" title="Stefano Modena">Stefano Modena</a> put his <a href="/wiki/Brabham" title="Brabham">Brabham</a> into the wall rather violently, escaping unhurt. Olivier Grouillard was disqualified on Lap 5 for his car being illegally worked on by his team during the one-hour delay.
</p><p>As the McLarens pulled away Mansell, Patrese, and Nannini were busy fighting over third place. It was settled in the space of three laps as Patrese retired with a timing belt failure and Mansell followed shortly afterwards with a gearbox problem. This left Nannini in third while up front Senna cruised home to victory from Prost who, in his pursuit of Senna, had suffered a spin on lap 42 at Variante Bassa. Nannini led home <a href="/wiki/Thierry_Boutsen" title="Thierry Boutsen">Thierry Boutsen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Derek_Warwick" title="Derek Warwick">Derek Warwick</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Arrows_Grand_Prix_International" title="Arrows Grand Prix International">Arrows</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Palmer" title="Jonathan Palmer">Jonathan Palmer</a> in the Tyrrell. <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Grouillard" title="Olivier Grouillard">Olivier Grouillard</a> was disqualified because <a href="/wiki/Equipe_Ligier" title="Equipe Ligier">Ligier</a> illegally repaired his car on the grid before the second start. <a href="/wiki/Thierry_Boutsen" title="Thierry Boutsen">Thierry Boutsen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alex_Caffi" title="Alex Caffi">Alex Caffi</a> were initially disqualified after a protest from Ligier because they had changed tyres in the pitlane before the second start, but were reinstated following an appeal.
</p><p>The Prost/Senna war began to build up speed after the Frenchman said that McLaren had a pre-race agreement that whoever led into the first turn should stay there, which was ironically suggested by Senna.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> In Prost's view, Senna had broken this agreement by passing him partway round the first lap after the restart.
</p>
