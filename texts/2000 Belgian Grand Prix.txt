<p>The <b>2000 Belgian Grand Prix</b> (formally, the <b>LVIII Foster's Belgian Grand Prix</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 27 August 2000 at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Spa-Francorchamps" title="Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps">Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stavelot" title="Stavelot">Stavelot</a>, <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a>. It was the thirteenth race of the <a href="/wiki/2000_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2000 Formula One season">2000 Formula One season</a>, and the 58th <a href="/wiki/Belgian_Grand_Prix" title="Belgian Grand Prix">Belgian Grand Prix</a>. The 44-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a>, who started from the <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>. <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> finished second in a <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> was third.
</p><p>The race began behind the <a href="/wiki/Safety_car" title="Safety car">safety car</a>, and when the car returned to the pit lane Häkkinen built a comfortable lead over <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a>. As the track dried and his rivals made <a href="/wiki/Pit_stop" title="Pit stop">pit stops</a>, Häkkinen maintained his lead until a lap-13 spin gave Michael Schumacher the lead for most of the remainder of the race. By the 34th lap Schumacher's tyres began to degrade; he drove off the racing line to cool them, which allowed Häkkinen to close the gap. On lap 41 Häkkinen overtook Michael Schumacher for the lead, lapping <a href="/wiki/British_American_Racing" title="British American Racing">BAR</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Ricardo_Zonta" title="Ricardo Zonta">Ricardo Zonta</a> and maintaining the lead to win. Although <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> set the <a href="/wiki/Fastest_lap" title="Fastest lap">fastest lap</a> time in the other Ferrari, he was hampered by a poor qualifying performance and retired with a fuel-pressure problem thirteen laps from the finish.
</p><p>Häkkinen's victory extended his lead in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a> to six points over Michael Schumacher, with Coulthard a further seven points behind. Barrichello's retirement dropped him to twenty-five points behind Häkkinen. In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a>, McLaren extended their lead to eight points over Ferrari with four races remaining in the season.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2000_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Grand Prix was contested by eleven teams with two drivers each.<sup id="cite_ref-entrants_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-entrants-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The teams (also known as <a href="/wiki/Formula_One#Constructors" title="Formula One">constructors</a>) were <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jaguar_Racing" title="Jaguar Racing">Jaguar</a>, <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a>, <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Prost_Grand_Prix" title="Prost Grand Prix">Prost</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arrows_Grand_Prix_International" title="Arrows Grand Prix International">Arrows</a>, <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a> and <a href="/wiki/British_American_Racing" title="British American Racing">BAR</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-entrants_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-entrants-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_tyres" title="Formula One tyres">Tyre</a> supplier <a href="/wiki/Bridgestone" title="Bridgestone">Bridgestone</a> brought four different tyre types to the race: two dry compounds (soft and medium) and two <a href="/wiki/Rain_tyre" title="Rain tyre">wet-weather compounds</a> (hard and soft).<sup id="cite_ref-preview_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-preview-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The wet-weather tyres were introduced for the race weekend in response to prospective rival tyre supplier <a href="/wiki/Michelin" title="Michelin">Michelin</a> beginning their tyre-development program during the year, resulting in Bridgestone increasing their development rate to research advances.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Before the race McLaren driver <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> led the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a> with 64 <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Championship_points_scoring_systems" title="List of Formula One World Championship points scoring systems">points</a>, ahead of <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> (62 points) and <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> (58). <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> was fourth with 49 points, and <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> was fifth with 18.<sup id="cite_ref-pointsprerace_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pointsprerace-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a> McLaren led with 112 points, one point ahead of rival Ferrari. Williams were third with 24 points, while Benetton (18 points) and Jordan (12) contended for fourth place.<sup id="cite_ref-pointsprerace_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pointsprerace-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> McLaren and Ferrari had dominated the championship, winning the previous twelve races. Barrichello and Fisichella had second-place finishes, and <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> and <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a> had finished third.<sup id="cite_ref-pointsprerace_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pointsprerace-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After the <a href="/wiki/2000_Hungarian_Grand_Prix" title="2000 Hungarian Grand Prix">Hungarian Grand Prix</a> on 13 August, five teams conducted mid-season testing at the <a href="/wiki/Silverstone_Circuit" title="Silverstone Circuit">Silverstone Circuit</a> on 15–17 August. McLaren test driver <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Panis" title="Olivier Panis">Olivier Panis</a> was fastest on the first day, ahead of Frentzen. Williams test driver <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Junqueira" title="Bruno Junqueira">Bruno Junqueira</a>'s car had a water leak, resulting in repairs which limited his team's testing time.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Panis remained the fastest on the second day. <a href="/wiki/Jos_Verstappen" title="Jos Verstappen">Jos Verstappen</a>'s Arrows car had a sensor failure, limiting his team's testing time; the car's floor had to be removed to install a new sensor.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Panis was again fastest on the final day of testing.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Ferrari opted to test the suspension and tyres of Michael Schumacher's car at the <a href="/wiki/Fiorano_Circuit" title="Fiorano Circuit">Fiorano Circuit</a>. Schumacher later moved to the <a href="/wiki/Mugello_Circuit" title="Mugello Circuit">Mugello Circuit</a>, with Barrichello conducting engine and setup tests, and Ferrari test driver <a href="/wiki/Luca_Badoer" title="Luca Badoer">Luca Badoer</a> remained at Fiorano for development work on new car components. Prost opted to test at the <a href="/wiki/Autodromo_Nazionale_Monza" class="mw-redirect" title="Autodromo Nazionale Monza">Autodromo Nazionale Monza</a> on 17–18 August with driver <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a>. Benetton conducted a five-day, one-car test at the Danielson Circuit, with test driver <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> on aerodynamic development for the first four days and <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Wurz" title="Alexander Wurz">Alexander Wurz</a> concentrating on practice starts the last day.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In September 1999 the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile</a> (FIA) released a provisional calendar for the <a href="/wiki/2000_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2000 Formula One season">2000 season</a>, dropping the Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps from the Formula One World Championship due to Belgian tobacco-advertising laws which threatened to cancel the race; several teams had tobacco sponsorship. The FIA had the revived <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Grand_Prix" title="Dutch Grand Prix">Dutch Grand Prix</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_Park_Zandvoort" class="mw-redirect" title="Circuit Park Zandvoort">Circuit Park Zandvoort</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_Grand_Prix" title="Portuguese Grand Prix">Portuguese Grand Prix</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Aut%C3%B3dromo_do_Estoril" class="mw-redirect" title="Autódromo do Estoril">Autódromo do Estoril</a> as alternatives if the Belgian Grand Prix was cancelled.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The dispute was resolved when the Belgian government exempted the race from the advertising law, and it was reinstated at the <a href="/wiki/FIA_World_Motor_Sport_Council" title="FIA World Motor Sport Council">FIA World Motor Sport Council</a> meeting in <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a> on 6 October.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Ferrari arrived at the circuit with a lighter, more-powerful version of its Tipo 049 <a href="/wiki/V10_engine" title="V10 engine">V10 engine</a> for Saturday's qualifying session, returning to the development power plant used at the Hungarian Grand Prix. They also had a revised aerodynamic package.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice">Practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2000_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Jarno_Trulli_2000_Belgium.jpg" class="image"><img alt="A figure, wearing a helmet with a white, red, yellow and blue design, is driving a Formula One that is of a yellow and black colour scheme." src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/19/Jarno_Trulli_2000_Belgium.jpg/220px-Jarno_Trulli_2000_Belgium.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="129" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/19/Jarno_Trulli_2000_Belgium.jpg/330px-Jarno_Trulli_2000_Belgium.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/19/Jarno_Trulli_2000_Belgium.jpg/440px-Jarno_Trulli_2000_Belgium.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1243" data-file-height="727" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Jarno_Trulli_2000_Belgium.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> qualified in the front row of the grid, behind <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>Four practice sessions were held before the Sunday race, two each on Friday and Saturday. The Friday morning and afternoon sessions each lasted an hour; the third and fourth sessions, on Saturday morning, lasted 45 minutes each.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Conditions were dry for the Friday morning and afternoon practice sessions.<sup id="cite_ref-fridaypractice_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fridaypractice-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard set the first session's fastest time with a lap of 1 minute, 53.398 seconds, eight-tenths of a second quicker than Michael Schumacher. Häkkinen had the third-fastest time, with <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a>, Barrichello and <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a> in the next three positions. The two Benetton drivers were seventh and eighth (with Wurz ahead of Fisichella), and the Williams cars of Ralf Schumacher and Button completed the top ten. Alesi's Prost had a fuel-pressure problem which prevented him from completing a timed lap, and he was the slowest overall.<sup id="cite_ref-fridaypractice_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fridaypractice-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> In the second practice session, due to a slow rear puncture Coulthard's first-session lap was still the fastest; Häkkinen had the second-fastest time. Jaguar driver <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Herbert" title="Johnny Herbert">Johnny Herbert</a> changed his car's balance, improving its performance and finishing third-fastest. Villeneuve moved into fourth after changes to his car's setup; Michael Schumacher slipped to fifth, and Wurz finished sixth. <a href="/wiki/Jos_Verstappen" title="Jos Verstappen">Jos Verstappen</a> was seventh-fastest, ahead of Fisichella, Barrichello and Trulli.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The weather remained dry for the Saturday-morning practice sessions.<sup id="cite_ref-fiasaturday_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiasaturday-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Häkkinen set the fastest lap of the third session at 1 minute, 51.043 seconds, quicker than his best on Friday and ahead of Frentzen, Trulli and Ralf Schumacher. Coulthard, who had an engine problem early in the session, was fifth-fastest (ahead of Button and Michael Schumacher). Alesi, Villeneuve and Sauber driver <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> rounded out the top ten.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> During the final practice session Häkkinen could not improve his time, although he remained the fastest. Button, much happier with his car's handling, set the second-fastest time. The Jordan drivers were third and fourth, with Trulli ahead of Frentzen. Ralf Schumacher and Coulthard completed the top six. Of the slower drivers, <a href="/wiki/Marc_Gen%C3%A9" title="Marc Gené">Marc Gené</a> had an anxious moment when his Minardi car shed its engine cover but was able to return to his garage.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2000_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatright" style="width:33%;&#32;;"><style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r887775652">.mw-parser-output .quotebox{background-color:#F9F9F9;border:1px solid #aaa;box-sizing:border-box;padding:10px;font-size:88%;max-width:100%}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft{margin:0.5em 1.4em 0.8em 0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright{margin:0.5em 0 0.8em 1.4em}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.centered{margin:0.5em auto 0.8em auto}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft p,.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright p{font-style:inherit}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-title{background-color:#F9F9F9;text-align:center;font-size:larger;font-weight:bold}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:before{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" “ ";vertical-align:-45%;line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:after{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" ” ";line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .left-aligned{text-align:left}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .right-aligned{text-align:right}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .center-aligned{text-align:center}.mw-parser-output .quotebox cite{display:block;font-style:normal}@media screen and (max-width:360px){.mw-parser-output .quotebox{min-width:100%;margin:0 0 0.8em!important;float:none!important}}</style>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">Obviously I'm pleased to take my second consecutive Belgian Grand Prix pole position. We found a good set-up to the car and the team did a great job. We improved the car throughout the qualifying and I'm optimistic for tomorrow's race.</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style=""><a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> on taking the <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>Saturday afternoon's one hour qualifying session saw each driver limited to twelve laps, with the starting order decided by their fastest laps. During this session the <a href="/wiki/107%25_rule" title="107% rule">107% rule</a> was in effect, requiring each driver to remain within 107 per cent of the fastest lap time to qualify for the race.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The session was held in dry weather.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Häkkinen was unhindered by slower traffic,<sup id="cite_ref-:0_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> clinched his fifth <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> of the season and his third at the circuit, with a time of 1 minute, 50.646 seconds;<sup id="cite_ref-bbcquali_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcquali-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> although he was optimistic about his race prospects, he was concerned about the start.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Häkkinen was joined in the front row of the <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_motorsport_terms#G" title="Glossary of motorsport terms">grid</a> by Trulli, who equalled his best qualifying performance of the season (at the <a href="/wiki/2000_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="2000 Monaco Grand Prix">Monaco Grand Prix</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-bbcquali_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcquali-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Trulli was also optimistic about his chances because of the Jordan team's strong record at the circuit.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Button qualified third, and said he was happy with his starting position.<sup id="cite_ref-qualiatlasf1_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualiatlasf1-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher secured fourth position, nine-tenths of a second behind Häkkinen, setting a lap time which pushed Häkkinen's teammate Coulthard into fifth. Coulthard, who had problems with <a href="/wiki/Grip_(auto_racing)" title="Grip (auto racing)">grip</a>, believed that he could have had a faster lap time. Ralf Schumacher secured sixth position after being caught in traffic during his final run, keeping the Williams driver from a quicker lap time.<sup id="cite_ref-fiasaturday_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiasaturday-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Villeneuve, in seventh, reported oversteering.<sup id="cite_ref-qualiatlasf1_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualiatlasf1-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Frentzen qualified eighth; his best lap time was disqualified after Coulthard blocked him at the Bus Stop <a href="/wiki/Chicane" title="Chicane">chicane</a>, which caused Frentzen to run onto the grass.<sup id="cite_ref-qualiatlasf1_24-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualiatlasf1-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Herbert and Barrichello rounded out the top ten drivers, with Barrichello spinning at the chicane during his third run after locking his brakes.<sup id="cite_ref-qualiatlasf1_24-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualiatlasf1-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Fisichella, eleventh, missed the top ten by three-tenths of a second on his only quick run of the session.<sup id="cite_ref-qualiatlasf1_24-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualiatlasf1-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Herbert's teammate, <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a>, qualified twelfth with tyre-grip problems. He was ahead of Zonta in the slower of the two BARs (which lost a half-second through Eau Rouge),<sup id="cite_ref-qualiatlasf1_24-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualiatlasf1-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Heidfeld in the faster Prost, Sauber's <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Diniz" title="Pedro Diniz">Pedro Diniz</a> and Arrows driver <a href="/wiki/Pedro_de_la_Rosa" title="Pedro de la Rosa">Pedro de la Rosa</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcquali_21-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcquali-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Alesi qualified 17th, despite spinning at the chicane and triggering a <a href="/wiki/Racing_flags#Yellow_flag" title="Racing flags">yellow flag</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-fiasaturday_15-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiasaturday-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Salo qualified 18th, ahead of Wurz (who had engine problems and shared the spare Benetton <a href="/wiki/Monocoque" title="Monocoque">monocoque</a> with Fisichella).<sup id="cite_ref-fiasaturday_15-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiasaturday-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Verstappen and the two Minardi drivers qualified at the back of the grid, in positions 20 to 22.<sup id="cite_ref-qualiatlasf1_24-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualiatlasf1-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Warm-up">Warm-up</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2000_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Warm-up">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The drivers took the track at 09:30 <a href="/wiki/Central_European_Summer_Time" title="Central European Summer Time">Central European Summer Time</a> (<a href="/wiki/Coordinated_Universal_Time" title="Coordinated Universal Time">UTC</a>+2) for a 30-minute warm-up<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_12-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> in wet weather, with lap times 13 seconds slower than the previous days' practice and qualifying sessions. Heavy rain fell early in the morning and a rising mist formed low on the track by dawn.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Häkkinen maintained his good performance, setting a time of 2:03.392. Michael Schumacher was the second-fastest driver; Button was third, two-thousands of a second behind Schumacher. Barrichello completed the top four, three-tenths of a second behind Häkkinen. Giancarlo Fisichella struck the barriers heavily at turn 14 after spinning sideways and was launched into the air, landing upside-down;<sup id="cite_ref-gpcomrace_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpcomrace-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> the session was suspended while <a href="/wiki/Motorsport_marshal" title="Motorsport marshal">marshals</a> cleared the track of debris. Fisichella sustained a bruised left knee,<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> and was forced to start the race with his team's <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_motorsport_terms#S" title="Glossary of motorsport terms">spare car</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-gpcomrace_26-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpcomrace-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Jacques Villeneuve damaged his car's rear end in a crash later in the session, but was able to continue.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2000_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:2000_Belgian_Grand_Prix.jpg" class="image"><img alt="Mika Häkkinen, the first black and silver car, leads a field of ten Formula One cars on a wet track." src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/47/2000_Belgian_Grand_Prix.jpg/220px-2000_Belgian_Grand_Prix.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="163" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/47/2000_Belgian_Grand_Prix.jpg/330px-2000_Belgian_Grand_Prix.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/47/2000_Belgian_Grand_Prix.jpg/440px-2000_Belgian_Grand_Prix.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1371" data-file-height="1016" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:2000_Belgian_Grand_Prix.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Häkkinen led for the first part of the race. Although it did not rain during the race, the track was wet and spray impaired the drivers' visibility.</div></div></div>
<p>The race began at 14:00 local time, with air and track temperatures at 15&#160;°C (59&#160;°F);<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> clouds, but no rain, were predicted for the race.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> The rain had stopped by the time of the race's start but there was standing water on the track,<sup id="cite_ref-:0_20-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> causing heavy spray and impairing visibility, meant that the event would start behind the <a href="/wiki/Safety_car" title="Safety car">safety car</a>; all cars except Pedro Diniz' had wet tyres.<sup id="cite_ref-review_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-review-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Minardi's <a href="/wiki/Gast%C3%B3n_Mazzacane" title="Gastón Mazzacane">Gastón Mazzacane</a> started from the pit lane, since he changed to his team's spare car after his engine failed during the warm-up.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> The race began without a <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_motorsport_terms#F" title="Glossary of motorsport terms">formation lap</a>. During the safety-car period, Diniz spun off; he was passed by Pedro de la Rosa,<sup id="cite_ref-gpcomrace_26-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpcomrace-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> who received a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_regulations#Penalties" title="Formula One regulations">ten-second stop-go penalty</a> which he served on lap 13. The safety car entered the pit lane after one lap, and the cars were allowed to overtake after crossing the start-finish line. Häkkinen maintained his lead going into the first corner, followed by Trulli, Button, Michael Schumacher and Coulthard. Herbert ran wide at the first turn, losing ninth place to Barrichello. At the end of the first racing lap, Diniz dropped to the rear of the field. De la Rosa lost 16th position on lap three after running wide at turn 18, losing two places to Alesi and Verstappen.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Häkkinen began to pull away from Trulli.<sup id="cite_ref-lapchart_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapchart-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Although Button tried to pass Trulli on the fourth lap at the Bus Stop chicane, he ran wide and lost third position to Michael Schumacher.<sup id="cite_ref-gpcomrace_26-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpcomrace-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> On that lap, Alesi was the first driver to pit for dry tyres.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_33-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> By the beginning of the fifth lap Häkkinen increased his lead over Trulli to ten seconds, ahead of Michael Schumacher, Button and Coulthard.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_33-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Button attempted to overtake Trulli on the outside of the Bus Stop chicane, but left the inside open for Schumacher to pass him for third place. Schumacher then took second place from Trulli at La Source.<sup id="cite_ref-gpcomrace_26-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpcomrace-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Button attempted to follow Schumacher down the inside, but made contact with the latter. Trulli was sent into a spin, becoming the first retirement of the race. Button lost two positions to Coulthard and Ralf Schumacher in the incident. On the next lap Michael and Ralf Schumacher were the first leaders to pit for dry tyres, as Alesi began setting faster lap times than the front-runners. Häkkinen made a pit stop from the lead on lap seven, followed by Button, and re-emerged ahead of Coulthard to retain the lead. Coulthard made his pit stop on the following lap, re-emerging in ninth position.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_33-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>All drivers made pit stops by the end of lap nine. The race order was Häkkinen, Michael Schumacher, Ralf Schumacher, Alesi, Button, Villeneuve, Barrichello, Frentzen, Coulthard, Diniz, Herbert, Irvine, Zonta, Salo, Gené, Verstappen, de la Rosa, Heidfeld, Wurz and Mazzacane.<sup id="cite_ref-lapchart_34-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapchart-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> During that lap Barrichello overtook Frentzen for seventh, whilst Verstappen and Fisichella collided after Verstappen tried to pass the slowing Benetton at the Bus Stop chicane.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_33-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Verstappen had front-wing damage, and Fisichella later retired with an electrical problem.<sup id="cite_ref-gpcomrace_26-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpcomrace-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> By the beginning of lap 13, Michael Schumacher closed his gap from Häkkinen to about 4.6 seconds after four consecutive fastest laps.<sup id="cite_ref-lapchart_34-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapchart-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Later in the lap, Häkkinen touched a damp kerb at Stavelot corner and was sent spinning sideways at high-speed into the grass;<sup id="cite_ref-:0_20-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-gpcomrace_26-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpcomrace-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher took the lead.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_33-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Nick Heidfeld was the race's third retirement when his car developed a mechanical problem during that lap.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_33-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Alesi, the first front-runner to make a scheduled pit stop on lap 18, rejoined in tenth. During the next two laps Salo passed Irvine for twelfth, whilst Barrichello made a pit stop from sixth position and came out in eleventh. By lap 22, Michael Schumacher had increased his lead over Häkkinen to eleven seconds.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_33-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Ralf Schumacher, ten seconds behind Häkkinen, maintained a six-second gap over teammate Button.<sup id="cite_ref-lapchart_34-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapchart-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher made a pit stop on that lap, emerging in third position. On lap 23 Barrichello passed Herbert for ninth position, and during the next two laps Villeneuve and Ralf Schumacher made pit stops. Button made a pit stop from fourth position on lap 26, and Häkkinen one lap later;<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_33-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Häkkinen was told by his team (on pit boards) to speed up to reduce the gap to Michael Schumacher, who had a heavier fuel load.<sup id="cite_ref-f1comrace_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrace-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> Button dropped to eighth position, and Häkkinen came out behind Michael Schumacher. Frentzen and Coulthard made their pit stops together on lap 28, with Coulthard emerging ahead of Frentzen. On that lap, Barrichello passed Alesi for sixth position.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_33-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello, setting the fastest lap of the race (1 minute, 53.803 seconds on lap 30),<sup id="cite_ref-lapchart_34-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapchart-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> had consecutive fastest laps before making his second pit stop on lap 31. However, his car's fuel pressure dropped and he was pushed by marshals into the pit lane.<sup id="cite_ref-gpcomrace_26-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpcomrace-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello and Alesi (who had a similar problem) retired, and Button inherited fourth place.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_33-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Salo was the final scheduled driver to make a pit stop, on lap 33.<sup id="cite_ref-lapchart_34-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapchart-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Ricardo_Zonta_2000_Belgium.jpg" class="image"><img alt="A figure, wearing a helmet of a dark blue design with red and yellow stripes, is driving a Formula One with an white colour scheme adorned with sponsorship. He is holding both hands on a steering wheel, turning right." src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7c/Ricardo_Zonta_2000_Belgium.jpg/220px-Ricardo_Zonta_2000_Belgium.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="136" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7c/Ricardo_Zonta_2000_Belgium.jpg/330px-Ricardo_Zonta_2000_Belgium.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7c/Ricardo_Zonta_2000_Belgium.jpg/440px-Ricardo_Zonta_2000_Belgium.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1159" data-file-height="715" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Ricardo_Zonta_2000_Belgium.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Ricardo_Zonta" title="Ricardo Zonta">Ricardo Zonta</a> was lapped by Häkkinen and <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> while they were battling for the lead.</div></div></div>
<p>At the end of lap 34, after all scheduled pit stops, the running order was Michael Schumacher, Häkkinen, Ralf Schumacher, Button, Coulthard, Frentzen, Villeneuve, Herbert, Salo, Irvine, Diniz, Zonta, Wurz, Gene, Verstappen, de la Rosa and Mazzacane.<sup id="cite_ref-lapchart_34-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapchart-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> During that lap Michael Schumacher's tyres began to degrade, and he ran off the racing line to cool them by driving through water;<sup id="cite_ref-gpcomrace_26-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpcomrace-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_33-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-f1comrace_35-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrace-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> Häkkinen gradually closed the gap.<sup id="cite_ref-lapchart_34-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapchart-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard, fifth, passed Button for fourth position on lap 37. Häkkinen tried to pass Michael Schumacher on the inside for the lead on the 40th lap on the approach to Les Combes (after <a href="/wiki/Drafting_(aerodynamics)" title="Drafting (aerodynamics)">drafting</a> behind him on the straightaway), but Schumacher blocked him to defend his position.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_20-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_33-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> The drivers made contact, with Häkkinen receiving minor damage to his front wing and forced to slow.<sup id="cite_ref-overtake_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-overtake-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> During the following lap, while both drivers were lapping Zonta, Häkkinen moved to the inside to pass Schumacher for the lead.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_33-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> He kept the lead for the rest of the race,<sup id="cite_ref-lapchart_34-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapchart-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> crossing the finish line on lap 44 for his fourth victory of the season in a time of 1'28:14.494—an average speed of 129.535 miles per hour (208.466&#160;km/h).<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher finished second, 1.1 seconds behind Häkkinen and ahead of Ralf Schumacher in third, Coulthard in fourth, Button in fifth place. Frentzen rounded out the point-scoring positions in sixth. Villeneuve, Herbert, Salo, Irvine and Diniz filled the next five positions. Zonta, Wurz, Gené and Verstappen finished a lap behind the leader, with de la Rosa and Mazzacane the last classified finishers.<sup id="cite_ref-results_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-results-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="After_the_race">After the race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2000_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: After the race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatright" style="width:33%;&#32;;"><link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r887775652"/>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">This was an enjoyable race and I'm really happy to leave Belgium with ten points. I lost the lead to Schumacher when I spun at Stavelot. I don't really know what happened but the kerbs were very slippery. After my second pit stop I was closing on Schumacher and tried to get past him at the end of the straight but that didn't work. However I was much quicker coming out of La Source. I would probably have been close enough coming up the hill to overtake him anyway but because of the backmarker I got an additional tow and was able to go past and pull away.</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style="">Mika Häkkinen after the race<sup id="cite_ref-atlasf1quotes_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasf1quotes-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>The top three drivers appeared on the podium to collect their trophies and at a later <a href="/wiki/News_conference" class="mw-redirect" title="News conference">press conference</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_12-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Häkkinen was delighted with his victory: "This was an incredible win. It was a very difficult and unusual situation including my spin which of course was not planned. The kerbs here are very slippery and once you go over one there is not much you can do. I was lucky to keep going and I was able to chase Michael. But the car got better and better."<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> The driver added that he wanted to review his overtaking manoeuvre to see if Michael Schumacher performed an illegal move.<sup id="cite_ref-gpafterrace_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpafterrace-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher said that despite improvements to his car, he was unable to match Häkkinen's overall pace.<sup id="cite_ref-atlasf1quotes_39-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasf1quotes-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> He added that he experienced no problems running off-line to cool his tyres, and was happy to run behind slower cars for straight-line speed assistance during the race's closing stages.<sup id="cite_ref-atlasf1quotes_39-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasf1quotes-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-gpafterrace_41-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpafterrace-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Ralf Schumacher said, "I'm more than happy; we have been strong here the whole weekend and my car behaved perfectly. I had a little moment of worry about six laps from the end when the throttle didn't seem to pick up properly and I thought the engine was going to stop, but it came back and all was well."<sup id="cite_ref-atlasf1quotes_39-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasf1quotes-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher added that his team was confident of securing third in the Constructors' Championship; although he could not match Häkkinen's pace, he praised the team for his car's set-up.<sup id="cite_ref-gpafterrace_41-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpafterrace-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard was disappointed with his fourth-place finish, and thought his team's decision to call him into the pit lane after his competitors cost him the victory; however, he remained confident about his championship chances.<sup id="cite_ref-gpafterrace_41-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpafterrace-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> Button was also disappointed to finish fifth: "It was a bad race for me, if not the worst. The car was working well at the start, but when I tried to pass Jarno I went in a bit late."<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> Trulli refused to criticise Button after the race, believing that Button made a mistake.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> Fisichella and Verstappen felt similarly about their lap-nine collision, with Fisichella saying that he "felt sorry" for Verstappen and describing his weekend as "disastrous".<sup id="cite_ref-gpafterrace_41-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpafterrace-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> Verstappen said that he struggled with his car's balance, which resulted in <a href="/wiki/Understeer_and_oversteer" title="Understeer and oversteer">oversteer</a>. He was "happy to go home", because "it's not been a good weekend."<sup id="cite_ref-atlasf1quotes_39-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasf1quotes-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> Frentzen said that although he was happy to score points, he had hoped for a better finish.<sup id="cite_ref-gpafterrace_41-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpafterrace-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Mika_Haekkinen_2006.jpg" class="image"><img alt="Head and shoulders of a man in his late thirties with blonde hair and grey eyes, facing to the right. He is wearing a black polo neck sweater." src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/53/Mika_Haekkinen_2006.jpg/170px-Mika_Haekkinen_2006.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="230" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/53/Mika_Haekkinen_2006.jpg/255px-Mika_Haekkinen_2006.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/53/Mika_Haekkinen_2006.jpg/340px-Mika_Haekkinen_2006.jpg 2x" data-file-width="549" data-file-height="743" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Mika_Haekkinen_2006.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Mika Häkkinen won the race from the <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> after recovering from a spin.</div></div></div>
<p>The media focused on Häkkinen overtaking Michael Schumacher for the lead on lap 41, and after the race Häkkinen went to Schumacher in the <i><a href="/wiki/Parc_ferm%C3%A9" title="Parc fermé">parc fermé</a></i> and told him not to make a move like that again. Zonta later said that although he was unaware of Häkkinen's presence, he saw Schumacher in his mirrors.<sup id="cite_ref-overtake_36-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-overtake-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Derick Allsop of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Independent" title="The Independent">The Independent</a></i> described the impact the move might have on Häkkinen's career: "Perhaps, he [Mika Häkkinen] will be recognised as a driver worthy of a place in the pantheon of the sport's heroes."<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> According to McLaren team principal Ron Dennis, "His overtaking manoeuvre I'm sure will go down as one of the greatest in Formula One history",<sup id="cite_ref-atlasf1quotes_39-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasf1quotes-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> and in February 2001 Häkkinen's move was chosen by more than 60 Formula One historians as the <a href="/wiki/MasterCard" class="mw-redirect" title="MasterCard">MasterCard</a> Priceless Moment of the 2000 F1 season.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The use of a safety car to start the race had a mixed response within the sport. Coulthard agreed with the FIA's decision: "I know there will be a debate over it but the fact is I was asked beforehand and I said that, based on the previous years we've had here, the safest thing is to have a safety-car start".<sup id="cite_ref-safetycarf1_46-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-safetycarf1-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> He added that although the safety-car start eliminated overtaking, it also prevented a major accident.<sup id="cite_ref-safetycarf1_46-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-safetycarf1-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> Ralf Schumacher also agreed with the decision to start under safety-car conditions.<sup id="cite_ref-gpafterrace_41-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpafterrace-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> However, <a href="/wiki/ITV-F1" class="mw-redirect" title="ITV-F1">ITV</a> commentator and former driver <a href="/wiki/Martin_Brundle" title="Martin Brundle">Martin Brundle</a> felt that the track was not wet enough for a safety car.<sup id="cite_ref-safetycarf1_46-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-safetycarf1-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> Journalist <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Roebuck" title="Nigel Roebuck">Nigel Roebuck</a> said that the length of time under safety-car conditions was inadequate, and raised the possibility of abandoning standing starts.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> FIA race director <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Whiting" title="Charlie Whiting">Charlie Whiting</a> consulted Coulthard, the drivers' representative, before making his decision.<sup id="cite_ref-review_31-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-review-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After the race, Häkkinen remained in the Drivers' Championship lead with 74 points. Michael Schumacher was second with 68 points, seven points ahead of Coulthard and nineteen ahead of Barrichello. Ralf Schumacher passed Fisichella for fifth place with 20 points, and Frentzen moved ahead of teammate Trulli and Salo.<sup id="cite_ref-review_31-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-review-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> In the Constructors' Championship, McLaren maintained their lead with 125 points and Ferrari remained in second with 117 points. Williams increased their lead over Benetton to twelve points, and Jordan remained fifth with 13 points.<sup id="cite_ref-review_31-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-review-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Given Häkkinen's increased lead, Michael Schumacher acknowledged that his team lacked speed against McLaren in the season's four remaining races but remained confident of winning the world championship.<sup id="cite_ref-review_31-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-review-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
