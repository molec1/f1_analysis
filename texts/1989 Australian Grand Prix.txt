<p>The <b>1989 Australian Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at <a href="/wiki/Adelaide_Street_Circuit" title="Adelaide Street Circuit">Adelaide</a> on 5 November 1989. It was the 16th and final race of the <a href="/wiki/1989_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1989 Formula One World Championship">1989 Formula One World Championship</a>. It took place in wet conditions, and was stopped and restarted following a first-lap collision. <a href="/wiki/Andrea_de_Cesaris" title="Andrea de Cesaris">Andrea de Cesaris</a> spun at the same corner on two consecutive laps. As Formula One cars are not as fast in wet conditions as they are in the dry, the race was stopped at the two-hour mark with 70 laps being completed, 11 short of the scheduled 81 laps. It was the last race for <a href="/wiki/Ren%C3%A9_Arnoux" title="René Arnoux">René Arnoux</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Cheever" title="Eddie Cheever">Eddie Cheever</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-race">Pre-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1989_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Pre-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The race weekend saw continuing fallout from the events in <a href="/wiki/1989_Japanese_Grand_Prix" title="1989 Japanese Grand Prix">Japan</a> two weeks previously, where the <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Honda_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Honda F1">Hondas</a> of <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> had taken each other out in their battle for the race lead and the World Championship with seven laps remaining. A post-race disqualification to Senna for cutting the chicane to return to the circuit saw Prost confirmed as a triple World Champion and Senna was unhappy with the sport's governing body, the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_du_Sport_Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale du Sport Automobile">Fédération Internationale du Sport Automobile</a> (FISA), and in particular its French president <a href="/wiki/Jean-Marie_Balestre" title="Jean-Marie Balestre">Jean-Marie Balestre</a> whom he accused of rigging the championship for his countryman. Senna initially threatened to boycott the event and leave Formula One altogether. However, after lengthy talks with his family and McLaren boss <a href="/wiki/Ron_Dennis" title="Ron Dennis">Ron Dennis</a>, he reluctantly showed up at Adelaide and immediately set the pace on Friday. Prost was determined to go out on a high note in his last Grand Prix for McLaren before joining <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>.
</p><p>McLaren were appealing Senna's Japanese Grand Prix disqualification. Ron Dennis said in a press conference that the appeal was not motivated against Prost (who was leaving the team) winning the championship, but simply that the team believed it had unjustly lost a race win, including the prize money as well as sponsorship bonuses from team backers such as <a href="/wiki/Marlboro_(cigarette)" title="Marlboro (cigarette)">Marlboro</a>. This meant that if Senna won in Adelaide he could still be declared champion if his disqualification from Japan was overturned. In a hearing in <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a> the week after Japan, FISA had also labeled Senna as a 'dangerous driver' (citing a number of incidents involving the <a href="/wiki/1988_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1988 Formula One World Championship">1988</a> World Champion) and gave him a six-month suspended sentence, a decision that was met with criticism from many in the F1 paddock, including rival team bosses such as <a href="/wiki/Ken_Tyrrell" title="Ken Tyrrell">Ken Tyrrell</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Most agreed the collision between the McLarens, while the culmination of the growing hostility between Prost and Senna that had been building since <a href="/wiki/1989_San_Marino_Grand_Prix" title="1989 San Marino Grand Prix">Imola</a>, was nothing more than a racing incident and that Senna was being harshly punished by the governing body of the sport. Dennis also claimed that the charges and subsequent Paris hearing against Senna were farcical and that the stewards report of the incident had been "badly changed" from the initial one given in Japan.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>In other news, after pre-qualifying his car on the Thursday, <a href="/wiki/Piercarlo_Ghinzani" title="Piercarlo Ghinzani">Piercarlo Ghinzani</a> announced his retirement from Formula One after 76 races. Ghinzani qualified 21st for his final Grand Prix. He had taken one points finish in his Formula One career, when he finished 5th at the <a href="/wiki/1984_Dallas_Grand_Prix" title="1984 Dallas Grand Prix">1984 Dallas Grand Prix</a> for Osella. At the drivers meeting before the race <a href="/wiki/Equipe_Ligier" title="Equipe Ligier">Ligier</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Ren%C3%A9_Arnoux" title="René Arnoux">René Arnoux</a> also announced his retirement from Formula One racing, at the age of 41. He qualified 26th and last for what would be his 149th start in Grand Prix racing having begun his career in <a href="/wiki/1978_Formula_One_season" title="1978 Formula One season">1978</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1989_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-qualifying_report">Pre-qualifying report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1989_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Pre-qualifying report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In his last appearance for the <a href="/wiki/Osella" title="Osella">Osella</a> team, <a href="/wiki/Nicola_Larini" title="Nicola Larini">Nicola Larini</a> was fastest in pre-qualifying for the third Grand Prix in succession, and his team-mate <a href="/wiki/Piercarlo_Ghinzani" title="Piercarlo Ghinzani">Piercarlo Ghinzani</a> pre-qualified third fastest in his final Formula One event. Both Osellas lapped inside the lap record.<sup id="cite_ref-Walker_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Walker-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Philippe_Alliot" title="Philippe Alliot">Philippe Alliot</a> was second fastest in the <a href="/wiki/Larrousse" title="Larrousse">Larrousse</a>-<a href="/wiki/Lola_Cars" title="Lola Cars">Lola</a>, with the <a href="/wiki/Onyx_(racing_team)" class="mw-redirect" title="Onyx (racing team)">Onyx</a> of <a href="/wiki/JJ_Lehto" title="JJ Lehto">JJ Lehto</a> in fourth, edging out his team-mate <a href="/wiki/Stefan_Johansson" title="Stefan Johansson">Stefan Johansson</a> by just under a tenth of a second. It was Johansson's eighth pre-qualifying failure of the season.
</p><p>Sixth was the other Lola of <a href="/wiki/Michele_Alboreto" title="Michele Alboreto">Michele Alboreto</a>, who had failed to qualify for any of the last three races of the season. Both Alliot and Alboreto left the team at the end of the season. <a href="/wiki/Bernd_Schneider_(racing_driver)" title="Bernd Schneider (racing driver)">Bernd Schneider</a> was seventh in the <a href="/wiki/Zakspeed" title="Zakspeed">Zakspeed</a>, his fourteenth failure to pre-qualify in 1989. <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Moreno" title="Roberto Moreno">Roberto Moreno</a> was eighth in his last appearance for <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Coloni" title="Scuderia Coloni">Coloni</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Oscar_Larrauri" title="Oscar Larrauri">Oscar Larrauri</a> ninth for <a href="/wiki/EuroBrun" title="EuroBrun">EuroBrun</a> in his last Formula One event. <a href="/wiki/Aguri_Suzuki" title="Aguri Suzuki">Aguri Suzuki</a> was tenth in the other Zakspeed, having failed to pre-qualify in any of the sixteen Grands Prix this season, and Zakspeed elected to pull out of Formula One at the end of the year. The <a href="/wiki/Automobiles_Gonfaronnaises_Sportives" title="Automobiles Gonfaronnaises Sportives">AGS</a> team struggled again with <a href="/wiki/Yannick_Dalmas" title="Yannick Dalmas">Yannick Dalmas</a> eleventh and <a href="/wiki/Gabriele_Tarquini" title="Gabriele Tarquini">Gabriele Tarquini</a> twelfth, although both drivers were staying with the team for 1990. Bringing up the rear, as he did in all six of the pre-qualifying sessions in which he participated this season, was <a href="/wiki/Enrico_Bertaggia" title="Enrico Bertaggia">Enrico Bertaggia</a> in the other Coloni, who like his team-mate Moreno, left the Italian team at the end of the season.<sup id="cite_ref-Walker_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Walker-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying_report">Qualifying report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1989_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Qualifying report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Friday qualifying saw Prost pip Senna to pole, with <a href="/wiki/Thierry_Boutsen" title="Thierry Boutsen">Thierry Boutsen</a> less than a tenth of a second behind Senna in third. <a href="/wiki/Pierluigi_Martini" title="Pierluigi Martini">Pierluigi Martini</a> also continued his late season qualifying form with fourth in his <a href="/wiki/Pirelli" title="Pirelli">Pirelli</a>-shod <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a>. 4th–9th were filled with Italians with Japanese Grand Prix winner <a href="/wiki/Alessandro_Nannini" title="Alessandro Nannini">Alessandro Nannini</a> fifth, <a href="/wiki/Riccardo_Patrese" title="Riccardo Patrese">Riccardo Patrese</a> sixth, <a href="/wiki/Stefano_Modena" title="Stefano Modena">Stefano Modena</a> seventh, followed by the two <a href="/wiki/Dallara" title="Dallara">Dallaras</a> of <a href="/wiki/Andrea_de_Cesaris" title="Andrea de Cesaris">Andrea de Cesaris</a> ahead of his teammate <a href="/wiki/Alex_Caffi" title="Alex Caffi">Alex Caffi</a>. British driver <a href="/wiki/Martin_Brundle" title="Martin Brundle">Martin Brundle</a> was 10th. Ferrari were struggling, with Berger 11th in his last race for the team before joining McLaren, and <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> in 16th, neither driver able to find handling balance with their V12 <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_640" title="Ferrari 640">Ferrari 640s</a>.
</p><p>Saturday was cooler, and Senna set a time below 1:17s, to take pole overall for the race. Prost did not improve and settled for 2nd, while Martini beat Nannini to third by just one tenth. The two <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW13" title="Williams FW13">Williams</a>-<a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renaults</a> were fifth and sixth with both drivers complaining of traffic on their runs, while Nigel Mansell was doing much better to qualify seventh going a second faster than he did on Friday while Berger fell to 14th. Berger was unable to better his Friday time as his car experienced engine failure on the track. He was forced to use Mansell's race car for his qualifying run which was halted when the on-board fire extinguisher was triggered (Berger could not use the spare Ferrari as it reportedly had a development engine planned for 1990 and it was strictly for Mansell's use only. The Dallaras were 9th and 10th on the grid with <a href="/wiki/Nicola_Larini" title="Nicola Larini">Nicola Larini</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Osella" title="Osella">Osella</a> in his (and the team's) highest qualifying result in 11th.
</p><p>Only 24 seconds before the end of the final qualifying session, <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Cheever" title="Eddie Cheever">Eddie Cheever</a> in his <a href="/wiki/Arrows_A11" title="Arrows A11">Arrows</a>-<a href="/wiki/Cosworth_DFR" class="mw-redirect" title="Cosworth DFR">Ford</a> caused the red flag to be shown when he heavily crashed his car at the entrance to the pit straight directly opposite the pits. Television broadcasts showed a driver's eye view of the accident, as the Arrows of Cheever and <a href="/wiki/Derek_Warwick" title="Derek Warwick">Derek Warwick</a> were carrying forward-facing cameras for the weekend. Coming out of the final hairpin onto pit straight, Cheever ran wide over the curbing and hit the concrete wall that protected the grandstand from the cars, severely damaging the left front and rear of the car and leaving a large pool of oil on the racing surface as the car came to rest lying across the middle of the track. Cheever himself was unharmed; after he threw his steering wheel away in disgust he climbed from the car, ran across the track and jumped the wall into the pits.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The four that failed to qualify were <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Palmer" title="Jonathan Palmer">Jonathan Palmer</a> in his <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_Racing" title="Tyrrell Racing">Tyrrell</a>, in what proved to be his last Grand Prix before becoming a pit lane reporter for the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> in 1990, <a href="/wiki/Luis_P%C3%A9rez-Sala" title="Luis Pérez-Sala">Luis Pérez-Sala</a> in the Minardi, who was significantly slower than teammate Martini in his last Grand Prix, and the two <a href="/wiki/Rial_Racing" title="Rial Racing">Rials</a> of <a href="/wiki/Bertrand_Gachot" title="Bertrand Gachot">Bertrand Gachot</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pierre-Henri_Raphanel" title="Pierre-Henri Raphanel">Pierre-Henri Raphanel</a>, who were two seconds slower than Sala. Despite a fourth for <a href="/wiki/Christian_Danner" title="Christian Danner">Christian Danner</a> at the <a href="/wiki/1989_United_States_Grand_Prix" title="1989 United States Grand Prix">US Grand Prix</a>, it was not enough to save the team for next season. Raphanel would also depart Formula 1 having only qualified for one race, while Gachot secured a drive for Coloni in 1990.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1989_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<table class="box-Very_long plainlinks metadata ambox ambox-style ambox-very_long" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-image"><div style="width:52px"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/f/f2/Edit-clear.svg/40px-Edit-clear.svg.png" decoding="async" width="40" height="40" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/f/f2/Edit-clear.svg/60px-Edit-clear.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/f/f2/Edit-clear.svg/80px-Edit-clear.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="48" data-file-height="48" /></div></td><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This section <b>may be <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Article_size" title="Wikipedia:Article size">too long</a> to read and navigate comfortably</b>.<span class="hide-when-compact"> Please consider <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Splitting" title="Wikipedia:Splitting">splitting</a> content into sub-articles, <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Summary_style" title="Wikipedia:Summary style">condensing</a> it, or adding <a href="/wiki/Help:Section#Subsections" title="Help:Section">subheadings</a>.</span>  <small class="date-container"><i>(<span class="date">October 2018</span>)</i></small></div></td></tr></tbody></table>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-race_2">Pre-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1989_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Pre-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Sunday was cool and overcast with rain being forecast for later in the day. After the morning warm-up session, the rain arrived just before the second <a href="/wiki/Group_A" title="Group A">Group A</a> <a href="/wiki/Touring_car_racing" title="Touring car racing">touring car</a> support race of the weekend, and an extra 30-minute session (already organised for such an occasion) was arranged for the teams to set their cars up for what would be a wet race as the weather forecast had the rain staying around all day. During the extra session a lot of drivers aquaplaned off the circuit, some on their out lap, notably Prost and Berger. Senna spun his car a full 360° over the high curb on the outside of Brewery Bend, his car emerged pointing in the right direction and he continued undamaged down the <a href="/wiki/Brabham" title="Brabham">Brabham</a> Straight. Prost and Berger, along with <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thierry_Boutsen" title="Thierry Boutsen">Thierry Boutsen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Riccardo_Patrese" title="Riccardo Patrese">Riccardo Patrese</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alessandro_Nannini" title="Alessandro Nannini">Alessandro Nannini</a> discussed not racing over safety concerns with Prost, Berger and Piquet in particular telling television interviewer <a href="/wiki/Barry_Sheene" title="Barry Sheene">Barry Sheene</a> and a worldwide television audience that the conditions were too bad to race in. The drivers' argument was that the race start should be delayed as the rain was forecast to ease within a couple of hours.
</p><p>An hour before the race the conditions significantly worsened, and Prost and Berger's proposal was being seriously considered by a lot of drivers. World Champion Senna wanted to start despite the appalling conditions. With McLaren's court action over his disqualification in Japan still pending, the championship was technically not yet settled and to keep any chance of retaining his World Drivers' Championship he had to win the race. Senna later privately confessed to a friend that he thought it was too dangerous to race but that he was a contracted driver and racing was what he was paid to do. He also said that championship or not, he believed Prost did the right thing by not taking the restart as he had nothing to gain by driving in such conditions. The drivers' arguments failed and it was agreed the race would go ahead. It also emerged that while still in his car Senna had been approached by Boutsen to get his thoughts about starting. Senna reportedly agreed the race should not go ahead, but he was bound by both his contract and the championship situation to start.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_report">Race report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1989_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Race report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The lights went out before the grid had even properly lined up, causing some confusion at the back (Eddie Cheever had been slow away on the warmup lap and was only just coming off the Brabham Straight as the lights went green). Prost got the jump on Senna at the start, but into the first corner, Senna braked significantly later, and re-took the lead, nearly hitting the Frenchman in the process. Further back, Martini fishtailed on his Pirelli rain tyres which were considered to not be as good as the <a href="/wiki/Goodyear_Tire_and_Rubber_Company" title="Goodyear Tire and Rubber Company">Goodyear</a>-shod cars around him, and Nannini overtook him for third. Other drivers who made good starts were de Cesaris and Brundle.
</p><p>On the first lap, <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Grouillard" title="Olivier Grouillard">Olivier Grouillard</a> spun off at turn 4, nearly collecting his teammate Arnoux. A number of drivers made mistakes including <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a> and Modena. But JJ Lehto's accident just after the first chicane partially blocked the road, causing the race to be stopped. While that was happening, Prost had pulled into the pits, withdrawing because of safety, before going on to criticise the race organisers for allowing the race to have been started in the first place. Before the race had started Prost stated his intention to honour his contract and start the race, but that he would pit after one lap and retire from the race. Prost, who was known not to like racing in wet conditions for reasons of safety, remained true to his word and did not contest the restart, despite the best efforts of team boss Ron Dennis to persuade him to do otherwise.
</p><p>As the cars waited on the grid, drivers argued whether the race should be restarted. The main drivers arguing for the race to be abandoned were Berger, Mansell, Patrese, Boutsen, Piquet and Nannini. Those arguing for the race to restart were Martini, Brundle, <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a> (despite suffering from bronchitis) de Cesaris and Caffi. <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_Constructors_Association" class="mw-redirect" title="Formula One Constructors Association">Formula One Constructors Association</a> (FOCA) boss <a href="/wiki/Bernie_Ecclestone" title="Bernie Ecclestone">Bernie Ecclestone</a> also pressed the race organisers to restart the race. Ecclestone also told Barry Sheene in an interview that he believed Prost would take the second start (due to his McLaren being at the end of pit lane, though Prost had no intention of getting back into his car), and that he had gone around the circuit in a course car and found the conditions had 'improved', though it was argued that there was a significant difference between a slow lap in a road car and a lap at speed in a Formula One car. Through it all, Senna remained silent sitting in his <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/5" title="McLaren MP4/5">McLaren</a>. Prost later told reporters that Ecclestone had told him they had cleared the puddles from the Brabham Straight which was where most of the cars were aquaplaning, though Prost did not believe him as it was still raining at the time.
</p><p>At the 2nd start, Larini stalled his Osella's Ford V8 before he even made his grid slot and was pushed off the circuit by the marshals to retire from the race. Alesi started from the pit lane after his car stalled on the dummy-grid and he had to be pushed into pit lane to be restarted. At the front, Martini took advantage of the gap left by Prost's absent car to get level with Senna, but the Brazilian kept the lead by cutting across him. Everyone else kept order behind, though Nannini, despite having no one in front of him with Prost's absence, was passed by the Williams' pair only a few corners after the start.
</p><p>Senna pulled away rapidly - he was almost 9 seconds in front after just the first lap, though he was also the only driver who had a clear track in front of him and was not affected by almost zero visibility. After a few laps in second place where he used better visibility and less wheelspin from his less powerful Ford V8 engine to stay ahead, Martini was passed in quick succession by both Williams' and Nannini and the trio immediately started to match Senna's now more steady rather than charging pace. They quickly left the Minardi with its inferior Pirelli wets well behind. The first retirement was Arnoux after he was pushed into a spin by Eddie Cheever and was beached on a high curb, ending his career on a low. Arnoux had actually been 2nd fastest in the wet pre-race warm-up behind Senna and had been confident of a good showing. Berger and Alliot then collided at the <a href="/wiki/East_Terrace,_Adelaide" title="East Terrace, Adelaide">East Terrace</a> bend, Berger taking out Alliot, while <a href="/wiki/Derek_Warwick" title="Derek Warwick">Derek Warwick</a> spun into the turn 7 and 8 esses when his throttle stuck open (as seen by his onboard camera which showed Warwick lucky not to be hit by his own right front wheel after hitting the concrete wall).
</p><p>Then in the space of two laps, six cars retired. Both Dallaras spun out at the same place at Brewery Bend, though de Cesaris was able to continue before spinning again later in the lap and beaching himself on a curb, while Mansell, Nannini, Piquet and Cheever all had off track excursions. However, the major accident was Senna running into the back of Brundle while lapping him and Piquet. The crash was recorded by a rear view camera mounted on the back of Brundle's <a href="/wiki/Brabham" title="Brabham">Brabham</a>, television commentator <a href="/wiki/Murray_Walker" title="Murray Walker">Murray Walker</a> describing Senna's McLaren charging into Brundle as "bearing down on him like <a href="/wiki/Jaws_(film)" title="Jaws (film)">Jaws</a>". Senna, who earlier had multiple spins on one lap before the pits hairpin (losing only 4 seconds in the process), was out with major front suspension damage after returning to the pits with his left front wheel missing, as was Brundle, and the Williams' of Boutsen and Patrese were one-two with Nannini still in third despite his off track excursion. <a href="/wiki/Ivan_Capelli" title="Ivan Capelli">Ivan Capelli</a> also retired his March.
</p><p>Five laps later, Mansell spun out at Stag Turn and then there was a major collision involving Piquet and Ghinzani. Piquet, who could see nothing but a grey wall of spray and as a consequence missed his braking markers, ran into the back of the braking Osella at speed at the hairpin at the end of the Brabham Straight and one of Ghinzani's rear tyres hit Piquet's helmet, though the triple World Champion was not injured. Ghinzani, who was lucky not to hit Martini, limped away from his broken Osella and Grand Prix racing having banged his ankle on the car's <a href="/wiki/Monocoque" title="Monocoque">monocoque</a>.
</p><p>The last retirement of the race was <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Cheever" title="Eddie Cheever">Eddie Cheever</a> (who as it turned out was driving in his 143rd and last Grand Prix) when he spun his Arrows down the East Terrace escape road and stalled his engine on lap 42. Cheever, who earlier in his career had gained a reputation for being a good wet weather driver, had driven much of his race with a piece of another car's front wing lodged in one of the Arrows' sidepods. <a href="/wiki/Satoru_Nakajima" title="Satoru Nakajima">Satoru Nakajima</a>, in one of the best drives of his career which drew praise even from those who had regularly been critical of him such as <a href="/wiki/1976_Formula_One_season" title="1976 Formula One season">1976</a> World Champion <a href="/wiki/James_Hunt" title="James Hunt">James Hunt</a>,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> set the fastest lap of the race, making his way through the field and almost catching Patrese for third, but finally settling for fourth. Nakajima had spun at the chicane on the first lap of the restart and was last by a long way at the end of the first lap. His drive surprised many as he had always been known to dislike street circuits and also had no like for racing in the rain. Third placed qualifier Martini went steadily backwards to finally finish in sixth place, three laps down on Boutsen. Martini's race generally confirmed the view that while <a href="/wiki/Pirelli" title="Pirelli">Pirelli</a>'s qualifying tyres were superior to the <a href="/wiki/Goodyear_Tire_and_Rubber_Company" title="Goodyear Tire and Rubber Company">Goodyears</a>, it was the opposite for both dry and wet weather race tyres with Goodyear holding a distinct advantage.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>During the middle stages of the race, Nannini, who had earlier passed a spinning Patrese for second place, was able to make significant inroads into Boutsen's lead and got to within a second of the leading Williams. However, as seen by the television cameras, this was mainly due to his <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a> teammate <a href="/wiki/Emanuele_Pirro" title="Emanuele Pirro">Emanuele Pirro</a> who ignored flags telling him he was about to be lapped. Pirro held Boutsen up for just over 3 laps allowing his team leader to close the gap. Boutsen eventually managed to pass the Benetton (shaking his fist in disgust soon after), while Pirro then moved over and let Nannini through. Boutsen then proceeded to drive steadily away from his <a href="/wiki/1988_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1988 Formula One World Championship">1988</a> Benetton teammate with Nannini unable to respond.
</p><p>After two hours, the race was declared finished with 70 laps having been completed out of the scheduled 81. Boutsen won his second wet race of the season followed by Nannini. Patrese finished third with Nakajima fourth having a good last race for <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Lotus</a> before joining Tyrrell in 1990. Patrese said in the post-race driver interviews that in the conditions he was only driving for third place knowing that with Mansell failing to finish, this would have allowed him to pass Mansell on points and finish a career best third in the Drivers' Championship. Pirro came home fifth in his last race for Benetton and Martini eventually came sixth, 3 laps down. The remaining survivors were the March of <a href="/wiki/Maur%C3%ADcio_Gugelmin" title="Maurício Gugelmin">Maurício Gugelmin</a> and the Brabham of <a href="/wiki/Stefano_Modena" title="Stefano Modena">Stefano Modena</a>, both finishing outside the points.
</p>

<ul><li>Note: Jonathan Palmer being eliminated in qualifying meant that only 15 drivers entered races in 1989 without failing to qualify for any of their attempts (including Martin Donnelly and Paolo Barilla who had only entered one race each and Eric Bernard who only entered two grand prix during the 1989 season). Of the other 10, Gerhard Berger missed Monaco due to injury, the same applied for Derek Warwick (French Grand Prix, subbed by Donnelly) and for Pierluigi Martini (missed the Japanese Grand Prix where Paolo Barilla stood in for him) while Nigel Mansell was disallowed from entering the Spanish Grand Prix following his incidents in Portugal. Furthermore, Jean Alesi only entered half of the races since he was only hired by the Tyrrell team for the seventh round of the 16 race schedule and missed two further rounds during the season due to his F3000 commitments. Emanuele Pirro also only started to compete from the seventh round onwards but then entered all of the remaining rounds for Benetton. This meant that only six drivers (Ayrton Senna, Alain Prost, Riccardo Patrese, Thierry Boutsen, Alessandro Nannini and Ivan Capelli) qualified for all of the 16 races of the season.</li></ul>
