<p>The <b>1950 Swiss Grand Prix</b>, formally titled the <i><b>Großer Preis der Schweiz für Automobile</b></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-prog_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prog-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held on 4 June 1950 at <a href="/wiki/Bremgarten_Circuit" class="mw-redirect" title="Bremgarten Circuit">Bremgarten</a>. It was race four of seven in the <a href="/wiki/1950_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1950 World Championship of Drivers">1950 World  Championship of Drivers</a>. The 42-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Alfa_Romeo_in_Formula_One" title="Alfa Romeo in Formula One">Alfa Romeo</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Giuseppe_Farina" title="Giuseppe Farina">Nino Farina</a> after he started from second position. His teammate <a href="/wiki/Luigi_Fagioli" title="Luigi Fagioli">Luigi Fagioli</a> finished second and <a href="/wiki/Talbot-Lago" title="Talbot-Lago">Talbot-Lago</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Louis_Rosier" title="Louis Rosier">Louis Rosier</a> came in third.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1950_Swiss_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The fourth round of the Championship took place just three weeks after the series began at <a href="/wiki/1950_British_Grand_Prix" title="1950 British Grand Prix">Silverstone</a> (with <a href="/wiki/1950_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="1950 Monaco Grand Prix">Monaco</a> and <a href="/wiki/1950_Indianapolis_500" title="1950 Indianapolis 500">Indianapolis</a> having taken place on consecutive weekends). Once again the event proved to be a battle between the <a href="/wiki/Alfa_Romeo_(Formula_One)" class="mw-redirect" title="Alfa Romeo (Formula One)">Alfa Romeo</a> factory 158s of <a href="/wiki/Giuseppe_Farina" title="Giuseppe Farina">Giuseppe Farina</a>, <a href="/wiki/Juan_Manuel_Fangio" title="Juan Manuel Fangio">Juan Manuel Fangio</a> and <a href="/wiki/Luigi_Fagioli" title="Luigi Fagioli">Luigi Fagioli</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Scuderia Ferraris</a> of <a href="/wiki/Alberto_Ascari" title="Alberto Ascari">Alberto Ascari</a>, <a href="/wiki/Luigi_Villoresi" title="Luigi Villoresi">Luigi Villoresi</a> (who had the latest model with de Dion rear suspension, twin overhead camshaft engine and 4-speed gearbox), <a href="/wiki/Raymond_Sommer" title="Raymond Sommer">Raymond Sommer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Peter_Whitehead_(racing_driver)" title="Peter Whitehead (racing driver)">Peter Whitehead</a>. There were a number of uncompetitive <a href="/wiki/Talbot-Lago" title="Talbot-Lago">Talbot-Lagos</a> and <a href="/wiki/Maserati_in_motorsport" title="Maserati in motorsport">Maseratis</a> as usual. <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Froil%C3%A1n_Gonz%C3%A1lez" title="José Froilán González">José Froilán González</a> was out of action as a result of burns he had received after the first lap accident at <a href="/wiki/1950_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="1950 Monaco Grand Prix">Monaco Grand Prix</a>. Also out of action as a result of the crash was Maserati factory driver <a href="/wiki/Franco_Rol" title="Franco Rol">Franco Rol</a>. This was the last race to be entered by pre-war racer <a href="/wiki/Eug%C3%A8ne_Martin" title="Eugène Martin">Eugène Martin</a>. It was also the first and only World Championship Grand Prix for <a href="/wiki/Nello_Pagani" title="Nello Pagani">Nello Pagani</a>, better known for his exploits in Grand Prix motorcycle racing. 
</p><p>In qualifying Fangio and Farina were well clear of Fagioli with Villoresi and Ascari sharing the second row of the 3-2-3 grid. <a href="/wiki/Peter_Whitehead_(racing_driver)" title="Peter Whitehead (racing driver)">Peter Whitehead</a>, <a href="/wiki/Franco_Rol" title="Franco Rol">Franco Rol</a>, <a href="/wiki/Reg_Parnell" title="Reg Parnell">Reg Parnell</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rudi_Fischer" title="Rudi Fischer">Rudi Fischer</a> failed to qualify. In the race, on the first lap Ascari managed to get among the Alfa Romeos but he quickly slipped back and it was left to the Alfas to battle. Fangio led early on but then Farina went ahead through a faster refuelling stop. Fagioli was unable to keep up and after both Villoresi and Ascari retired it was left to <a href="/wiki/Birabongse_Bhanudej" title="Birabongse Bhanudej">Prince Bira</a> to run fourth. He had to refuel and so <a href="/wiki/Philippe_%C3%89tancelin" title="Philippe Étancelin">Philippe Étancelin</a> in a Talbot-Lago was able to move into fourth place. Shortly afterwards, factory Talbot-Lago driver <a href="/wiki/Eug%C3%A8ne_Martin" title="Eugène Martin">Eugène Martin</a> crashed heavily and was seriously hurt when he was thrown from the car. Étancelin later went out with gearbox trouble and so Talbot-Lago factory driver <a href="/wiki/Louis_Rosier" title="Louis Rosier">Louis Rosier</a> moved into fourth. He was promoted to third when Fangio retired on lap 33 with an electrical problem. Farina became the first driver to win multiples Grands Prix, after winning the inaugural <a href="/wiki/1950_British_Grand_Prix" title="1950 British Grand Prix">World Championship Grand Prix</a>. 
</p>
