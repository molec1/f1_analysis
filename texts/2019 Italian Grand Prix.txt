<p>The <b>2019 Italian Grand Prix</b> (formally known as the <b>Formula 1 Gran Premio Heineken d'Italia 2019</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_racing" title="Formula One racing">Formula One motor race</a> held on 8 September 2019 at the <a href="/wiki/Autodromo_Nazionale_di_Monza" title="Autodromo Nazionale di Monza">Autodromo Nazionale di Monza</a> in <a href="/wiki/Monza" title="Monza">Monza, Italy</a>. The race was the 14th round of the <a href="/wiki/2019_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2019 Formula One World Championship">2019 Formula One World Championship</a> and marked the 89th running of the <a href="/wiki/Italian_Grand_Prix" title="Italian Grand Prix">Italian Grand Prix</a> and the 84th time the race had been held at Monza.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2019_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Championship_standings_before_the_race">Championship standings before the race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2019_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Championship standings before the race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> entered the round with a 65-point lead over teammate <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a> in the Drivers' Championship. In the Constructors' World Championship, Mercedes led <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> by 145 points.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Entries">Entries</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2019_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Entries">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Further information: <a href="/wiki/2019_Formula_One_World_Championship#Entries" title="2019 Formula One World Championship">2019 Formula One World Championship §&#160;Entries</a></div>
<p>The drivers and teams entered were the same as those for the previous race with no additional stand-in drivers for the race or practice.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice">Practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2019_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The first practice session was affected by rain and saw <a href="/wiki/Charles_Leclerc" title="Charles Leclerc">Charles Leclerc</a> finish the session fastest followed by <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Sainz_Jr." title="Carlos Sainz Jr.">Carlos Sainz Jr.</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lando_Norris" title="Lando Norris">Lando Norris</a>. The session was interrupted three times, firstly when <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> spun and got stuck in the gravel trap at turn 11. <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a> also crashed his car on the exit of turn 10 and finally when <a href="/wiki/Pierre_Gasly" title="Pierre Gasly">Pierre Gasly</a> beached his car on the kerbs at turn 2. The session was also filled with spins from a number of other drivers.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The second practice session was dry for the first hour before it started raining and the session once again saw Leclerc set the fastest time but this time it was <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> who was second fastest and <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> was third.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The third practice session was delayed and shortened by 10 minutes following an accident during the <a href="/w/index.php?title=2019_Monza_FIA_Formula_3_round&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="2019 Monza FIA Formula 3 round (page does not exist)">support Formula 3 race</a> and finished with Vettel fastest followed by <a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2019_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying_report">Qualifying report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2019_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Qualifying report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Q1_and_Q2">Q1 and Q2</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2019_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Q1 and Q2">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The first 18 minute qualifying session, Q1, was temporarily red-flagged with 4:34 remaining, when <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a>'s car lost power on the inside of turn 3. <a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a>, already facing grid penalties for replacing power unit components, did not set a time in the session. Verstappen and Pérez were eliminated in Q1, as were both <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> drivers, <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a> and <a href="/wiki/George_Russell_(racing_driver)" title="George Russell (racing driver)">George Russell</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Haas_F1_Team" title="Haas F1 Team">Haas</a> of <a href="/wiki/Romain_Grosjean" title="Romain Grosjean">Romain Grosjean</a>. The session finished with <a href="/wiki/Charles_Leclerc" title="Charles Leclerc">Charles Leclerc</a> fastest for <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Scuderia Ferrari</a>. In the second qualifying (Q2) session, the other Haas of <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Magnussen" title="Kevin Magnussen">Kevin Magnussen</a> was eliminated, as were both <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso</a> drivers, <a href="/wiki/Pierre_Gasly" title="Pierre Gasly">Pierre Gasly</a> and <a href="/wiki/Daniil_Kvyat" title="Daniil Kvyat">Daniil Kvyat</a>. <a href="/wiki/Alfa_Romeo_Racing" class="mw-redirect" title="Alfa Romeo Racing">Alfa Romeo</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Antonio_Giovinazzi" title="Antonio Giovinazzi">Antonio Giovinazzi</a> was the final driver eliminated in Q2, beaten by his teammate <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> for the last spot for the third qualifying session (Q3) by 0.002 seconds. <a href="/wiki/Racing_Point_F1_Team" title="Racing Point F1 Team">Racing Point</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Lance_Stroll" title="Lance Stroll">Lance Stroll</a> made it into Q3 for the first time in 2019.  <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> set the fastest time of Q2 which meant that <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> led a session for the first time in the weekend.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Q3">Q3</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2019_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Q3">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Q3, only 12 minutes long, was red-flagged at 6:35 left when Räikkönen lost control of his car and spun out wide into the tyre barriers at turn 11. After Räikkönen's car was cleared and the session resumed, two of the remaining nine drivers had still not set a time; <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Albon" title="Alexander Albon">Alexander Albon</a>, and Stroll. When the session restarted, no team wanted to be the first to send their driver out as Monza is a track where <a href="/wiki/Drafting_(aerodynamics)" title="Drafting (aerodynamics)">slipstreaming</a> is a significant advantage. As time began to wind down, speculation grew as to who would leave the pit lane first. The Q3 drivers did not make their way out of their garages until there were two minutes left in the session, with <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a> leading the pack.
</p><p>When he got to turn 1, Hülkenberg missed the turn and instead navigated through the run-off slip road past Turn 1. This action led to him being accused of deliberately taking the run-off in an effort to let other drivers past.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Stroll and <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Sainz_Jr." title="Carlos Sainz Jr.">Carlos Sainz Jr</a>, the next two drivers back, slowed their traversal of Turn 1, allowing enough time for Hülkenberg to again enter the track ahead of them.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> By the time the tight pack of drivers, led by Sainz and Hülkenberg, reached turn 3 on their out-laps, the margin of error allowing for drivers to reach the start line by the end of the Q3 session time was virtually gone. Ultimately, only Sainz and Leclerc successfully completed their out laps. All seven drivers behind them were caught out and thus crossed the line too late to have an opportunity to set a time in the second half of Q3. Sainz, the only driver who made a meaningful attempt, was unable to improve his first Q3 time enough to move out of seventh. The result meant that neither Albon or Stroll set a lap time.
</p><p>There was strong criticism of what had happened in the final minutes of Q3. Red Bull's team principal <a href="/wiki/Christian_Horner" title="Christian Horner">Christian Horner's</a> interpretation of Hülkenberg's move was that it was "obviously done on purpose", and beyond stating that the result was "all just a bit silly", interpreted the scenario with a widely reported expletive.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Mercedes team principal <a href="/wiki/Toto_Wolff" title="Toto Wolff">Toto Wolff</a> described Hülkenberg's chicane cut and the collective drivers' out-lap slowdowns as "junior class", and "not worthy of Formula 1."<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Hülkenberg, Sainz, and Stroll were called in by the race stewards to be investigated for "driving unnecessarily slowly" on their Q3 out laps. The stewards further investigated Hülkenberg for potentially "leaving the track without a justifiable reason".<sup id="cite_ref-:0_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Hülkenberg was cleared of wrongdoing with respect to leaving the track.  The stewards stated, "In this case we are unable to determine that the driver deliberately left the track." All three of the drivers were reprimanded for driving too slowly, which they admitted they did do, and which the stewards concluded "played a significant role in the backing up of cars at a critical stage of the final out lap for Q3".<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The race stewards concluded the investigation of the three drivers with the remark, "The Stewards strongly recommend that the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">FIA</a> expedite a solution to this type of situation".<sup id="cite_ref-:0_10-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2019_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_report">Race report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2019_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Race report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The top four drivers on the grid maintained their positions through turn 1 as the race began, with <a href="/wiki/Charles_Leclerc" title="Charles Leclerc">Charles Leclerc</a> turning pole position into an early race lead. <a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a>, who started 19th on the grid, made contact with <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a>, damaging his front wing and requiring an early pit stop to replace it.
</p><p>On lap 6, <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a>, running a strong 4th, lost control and spun to a stop at turn 9, his car halfway onto the grass. In an attempt to rejoin as quickly as possible, Vettel veered into the path of <a href="/wiki/Lance_Stroll" title="Lance Stroll">Lance Stroll</a>, who was in 7th. Vettel struck Stroll, damaging his own front wing, and further causing Stroll to spin out. When Stroll tried to rejoin the track, he in turn forced <a href="/wiki/Pierre_Gasly" title="Pierre Gasly">Pierre Gasly</a> to drive through the gravel run off to in an attempt to avoid Stroll. Vettel was forced to replace his front wing at the end of lap 6, leaving him in last place. Stroll was pushed down to 13th. Race stewards investigated the double-incident. They penalised Vettel with a ten second stop-go penalty, and Stroll with a drive-through penalty. Neither driver would recover to finish in the top ten.  Vettel also received three penalty points on his <a href="/wiki/FIA_Super_Licence" title="FIA Super Licence">FIA Super Licence</a> for the incident.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Pit_stops">Pit stops</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2019_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Pit stops">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>By lap 19, the top three drivers (Leclerc, <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a>, in order) were in a pit window to replace the aging soft tyres they started their races on. Hamilton was the first to do so, replacing his with medium tyres. Leclerc pitted on lap 20, but elected instead to use the longer lasting but slower hard tyre. Bottas, who also chose the medium tyre, would not pit until lap 27. All three drivers returned to racing in the same order they'd been in before their pit stops. <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Sainz_Jr." title="Carlos Sainz Jr.">Carlos Sainz</a> retired from the race on lap 27, after an error fitting his right front tyre in the pits forced him to stop near the exit to pit lane; this caused a brief <a href="/wiki/Virtual_safety_car" class="mw-redirect" title="Virtual safety car">virtual safety car</a>. Shortly after, <a href="/wiki/Daniil_Kvyat" title="Daniil Kvyat">Daniil Kvyat's</a> <a href="/wiki/Toro_Rosso_STR14" class="mw-redirect" title="Toro Rosso STR14">Toro Rosso</a> suffered a mechanical breakdown just past turn 1, causing a second virtual safety car while his car was pushed to safety.
</p><p>In the meantime, Leclerc's lead would be challenged for the remainder of the race by the <a href="/wiki/Mercedes_in_Formula_One" class="mw-redirect" title="Mercedes in Formula One">Mercedes</a> drivers behind him. After Leclerc and Hamilton's pit stops, Hamilton came close on several occasions to overtaking Leclerc, more than once less than one second behind and enabling him to use <a href="/wiki/Drag_reduction_system" title="Drag reduction system">DRS</a>. Both drivers exchanged occasional driving errors and Leclerc was warned once for pushing Hamilton off track. Hamilton consistently stayed close to Leclerc, but was unable to overtake him for the race lead. On lap 42, Hamilton locked up his tyres at turn 1, allowing Bottas to pass him into 2nd position. Bottas, who had medium tyres eight laps younger than Hamilton's, was able to challenge Leclerc for the lead just as Hamilton had done. Bottas came within one second of Leclerc on multiple occasions in the race's later stages, but, like Hamilton, he could not overtake Leclerc.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_finish">Race finish</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2019_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Race finish">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The Grand Prix ended with Leclerc claiming his second consecutive race win and Ferrari's first win in Italy since <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> won the <a href="/wiki/2010_Italian_Grand_Prix" title="2010 Italian Grand Prix">2010 edition</a> of the race.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Bottas was less than a second behind to take 2nd, and Hamilton, who made a late-race pit stop for soft tyres set the fastest lap of the race and took the last step of the podium. <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a> finished strong in 4th and 5th positions, promoting Renault into 5th in the Constructor's championship. This also meant that they were only 18 points behind fourth placed McLaren. <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Albon" title="Alexander Albon">Alexander Albon</a> managed a 6th place finish for <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a>, finishing one second behind Hulkenberg. Pérez and Verstappen, who both started at the back of the grid, ended in 7th and 8th. <a href="/wiki/Antonio_Giovinazzi" title="Antonio Giovinazzi">Antonio Giovinazzi</a> (<a href="/wiki/Alfa_Romeo_Racing" class="mw-redirect" title="Alfa Romeo Racing">Alfa Romeo</a>) finished 9th and <a href="/wiki/Lando_Norris" title="Lando Norris">Lando Norris</a> (<a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>) finished in tenth, the final points paying position.
</p>
