<p>The <b>1950 French Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 2 July 1950 at <a href="/wiki/Reims-Gueux" title="Reims-Gueux">Reims-Gueux</a>. It was race 6 of 7 in the <a href="/wiki/1950_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1950 World Championship of Drivers">1950 World Championship of Drivers</a>. The 64-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Alfa_Romeo_in_Formula_One" title="Alfa Romeo in Formula One">Alfa Romeo</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Juan_Manuel_Fangio" title="Juan Manuel Fangio">Juan Manuel Fangio</a> after he started from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>. His teammate <a href="/wiki/Luigi_Fagioli" title="Luigi Fagioli">Luigi Fagioli</a> finished second and <a href="/wiki/Peter_Whitehead_(racing_driver)" title="Peter Whitehead (racing driver)">Peter Whitehead</a> took third in a privateer Ferrari.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1950_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A total of 22 cars entered the event, four of which did not start the race. <a href="/wiki/Franco_Comotti" title="Franco Comotti">Franco Comotti</a> did not attend the event; <a href="/wiki/Eug%C3%A8ne_Chaboud" title="Eugène Chaboud">Eugène Chaboud</a> did not start in his own car, instead sharing <a href="/wiki/Philippe_%C3%89tancelin" title="Philippe Étancelin">Philippe Étancelin</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Talbot-Lago" title="Talbot-Lago">Talbot-Lago</a>; and the two <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Scuderia Ferrari</a> entries of <a href="/wiki/Luigi_Villoresi" title="Luigi Villoresi">Luigi Villoresi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alberto_Ascari" title="Alberto Ascari">Alberto Ascari</a> withdrew in practice.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Juan_Manuel_Fangio" title="Juan Manuel Fangio">Fangio</a> put in a stunning display with a 116&#160;mph practice lap. With Ferrari not starting their 3-litre cars, the main opposition was to come from the Talbots, complete with dual ignition engines with 12 spark plugs. However, they suffered from radiator problems and overheated, allowing Fangio and <a href="/wiki/Luigi_Fagioli" title="Luigi Fagioli">Fagioli</a> to lead home another Alfa demonstration run, whilst <a href="/wiki/Giuseppe_Farina" title="Giuseppe Farina">Farina</a> succumbed to fuel pump trouble. Peter Whitehead finished third despite a fractured head gasket in the last two laps.
</p><p><br />
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1950_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><b>Shared drives:</b>
<ul><li>Car #16: <a href="/wiki/Philippe_%C3%89tancelin" title="Philippe Étancelin">Philippe Étancelin</a> (first 26 laps) then <a href="/wiki/Eug%C3%A8ne_Chaboud" title="Eugène Chaboud">Eugène Chaboud</a> (33 laps). They shared the points for 5th place.</li>

<li>This was the first of only three World Championship races that <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Scuderia Ferrari</a> entered but did not start (the others being the <a href="/wiki/1982_Belgian_Grand_Prix" title="1982 Belgian Grand Prix">1982 Belgian</a> and <a href="/wiki/1982_Swiss_Grand_Prix" title="1982 Swiss Grand Prix">1982 Swiss</a> Grands Prix).</li></ul>
