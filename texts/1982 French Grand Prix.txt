<p>The <b>1982 French Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held at <a href="/wiki/Circuit_Paul_Ricard" title="Circuit Paul Ricard">Paul Ricard</a> on 25 July 1982. It was the eleventh race of the <a href="/wiki/1982_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1982 Formula One World Championship">1982 Formula One World Championship</a>.
</p><p>The 54-lap race was won from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> by <a href="/wiki/Ren%C3%A9_Arnoux" title="René Arnoux">René Arnoux</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/Renault_RE30" title="Renault RE30">Renault</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Turbocharger" title="Turbocharger">turbocharged</a> Renaults, <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_126C" title="Ferrari 126C">Ferraris</a> and <a href="/wiki/Brabham_BT50" title="Brabham BT50">Brabham</a>-<a href="/wiki/BMW_in_Formula_One" title="BMW in Formula One">BMWs</a> took up the first six grid positions, and Arnoux led home a French 1–2–3–4, with teammate <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> second and the Ferraris of <a href="/wiki/Didier_Pironi" title="Didier Pironi">Didier Pironi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Tambay" title="Patrick Tambay">Patrick Tambay</a> third and fourth respectively. However, Arnoux achieved his win in sour circumstances: he violated a pre-race agreement that if he and Prost were running first and second respectively, he would let Prost past to aid his Drivers' Championship hopes. Arnoux would leave Renault at the end of the year.
</p><p>The top six was completed by <a href="/wiki/Keke_Rosberg" title="Keke Rosberg">Keke Rosberg</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW08" title="Williams FW08">Williams</a>-<a href="/wiki/Ford_Motor_Company" title="Ford Motor Company">Ford</a> and <a href="/wiki/Michele_Alboreto" title="Michele Alboreto">Michele Alboreto</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_011" title="Tyrrell 011">Tyrrell</a>-Ford. Pironi's third place enabled him to extend his lead in the Drivers' Championship to nine points, though this would turn out to be his last finish before his career-ending accident at the next race in <a href="/wiki/1982_German_Grand_Prix" title="1982 German Grand Prix">Germany</a>.
</p><p>The eleventh lap of this race saw a big accident when <a href="/wiki/Jochen_Mass" title="Jochen Mass">Jochen Mass</a>'s <a href="/wiki/March_Engineering" title="March Engineering">March</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mauro_Baldi" title="Mauro Baldi">Mauro Baldi</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Arrows_Grand_Prix_International" title="Arrows Grand Prix International">Arrows</a> collided at Signes. Mass's car went through the catch fencing into the tyre walls, then catapulted into a spectator area and caught fire. Mass escaped with burns on his hands, while several spectators were injured. The West German driver retired from Formula One immediately after this race.
</p>
