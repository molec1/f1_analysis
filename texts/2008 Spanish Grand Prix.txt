<p>The <b>2008 Spanish Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>L Gran Premio de España Telefónica</b>, <b>50th Telefónica Spanish Grand Prix</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 27 April 2008 at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Catalunya" class="mw-redirect" title="Circuit de Catalunya">Circuit de Catalunya</a>, <a href="/wiki/Barcelona" title="Barcelona">Barcelona</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>. It was the fourth race of the <a href="/wiki/2008_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2008 Formula One season">2008 Formula One season</a>. The 66-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> team after starting from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>. <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> finished second in the other Ferrari, and <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> was third in a <a href="/wiki/McLaren_(racing)" class="mw-redirect" title="McLaren (racing)">McLaren</a>.
</p><p>Räikkönen began the race from pole position alongside <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>. Massa began from third, alongside <a href="/wiki/BMW_Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="BMW Sauber">BMW Sauber</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a>. Hamilton, the eventual <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Champion</a>, began from fifth and passed Kubica into the first corner, as Massa passed Alonso. Räikkönen maintained his lead through most of the race, leading to Ferrari's one-two finish. The <a href="/wiki/Safety_car" title="Safety car">safety car</a> was deployed several times throughout the race, including for a serious crash involving McLaren driver <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a>, though the Finn escaped with only a minor <a href="/wiki/Concussion" title="Concussion">concussion</a>.
</p><p>Prior to the race weekend, the event was put on probation by the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile</a>, the sport's <a href="/wiki/Sport_governing_body" class="mw-redirect" title="Sport governing body">governing body</a>, for racist taunting during pre-season testing at the circuit. Subsequently, no such racism was reported during the race weekend. The event was <a href="/wiki/Super_Aguri_F1" title="Super Aguri F1">Super Aguri's</a> final Grand Prix, withdrawing afterwards due to financial pressures, leaving the sport with ten teams; this was also the last race for both of the team's drivers, <a href="/wiki/Takuma_Sato" title="Takuma Sato">Takuma Sato</a> and <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Davidson" title="Anthony Davidson">Anthony Davidson</a>.  The result promoted Ferrari to the lead in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a>, 12&#160;<a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Championship_points_scoring_systems" title="List of Formula One World Championship points scoring systems">points</a> ahead of BMW Sauber and 13&#160;points ahead of McLaren. Räikkönen extended his lead in the Drivers' Championship to 29&#160;points, nine points ahead of Hamilton and ten points ahead of Kubica. Massa moved into fourth place on 18&#160;points, with 14&#160;races remaining in the season.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2008_Spanish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Grand Prix was contested by 22&#160;drivers, in 11 teams of two.<sup id="cite_ref-classification_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-classification-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The teams, also known as "<a href="/wiki/Formula_One#Constructors" title="Formula One">constructors</a>", were <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, <a href="/wiki/McLaren_(racing)" class="mw-redirect" title="McLaren (racing)">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_HighPerformanceEngines" class="mw-redirect" title="Mercedes-Benz HighPerformanceEngines">Mercedes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a>, <a href="/wiki/Honda_Racing_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Honda Racing F1">Honda</a>, <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a>, <a href="/wiki/BMW_Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="BMW Sauber">BMW Sauber</a>, <a href="/wiki/Toyota_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Toyota F1">Toyota</a>, <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a>, <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso</a> and <a href="/wiki/Super_Aguri" class="mw-redirect" title="Super Aguri">Super Aguri</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-classification_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-classification-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_tyres" title="Formula One tyres">Tyre</a> supplier <a href="/wiki/Bridgestone" title="Bridgestone">Bridgestone</a> brought two different tyre compounds to the race; the softer of the two marked by a single white stripe down one of the grooves.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Prior to the race, Ferrari driver <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> led the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a> with 19&#160;<a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Championship_points_scoring_systems" title="List of Formula One World Championship points scoring systems">points</a>, and BMW Sauber driver <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> was second with 16&#160;points. McLaren driver <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>, BMW driver <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a> and Hamilton's teammate <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> held the next three positions, all with 14&#160;points.<sup id="cite_ref-pointsFIA_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pointsFIA-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a>, BMW Sauber were leading with 30&#160;points, one point ahead of Ferrari, and two ahead of McLaren-Mercedes.<sup id="cite_ref-pointsFIA_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pointsFIA-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After Hamilton won the <a href="/wiki/2009_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="2009 Australian Grand Prix">season opener in Australia</a>, Ferrari had dominated the next two races in Malaysia and Bahrain. In the <a href="/wiki/2009_Malaysian_Grand_Prix" title="2009 Malaysian Grand Prix">Malaysian Grand Prix</a>, Ferrari driver <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> had claimed <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>, and while he later spun off and retired, his teammate Räikkönen went on to win the race.<sup id="cite_ref-malgpreport_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-malgpreport-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Ferrari's form had continued at the <a href="/wiki/2009_Bahrain_Grand_Prix" title="2009 Bahrain Grand Prix">Bahrain Grand Prix</a>, where Massa had led Räikkönen to a one-two finish.<sup id="cite_ref-bahgpreport_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bahgpreport-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Despite the promising start to the season, Ferrari team principal <a href="/wiki/Stefano_Domenicali" title="Stefano Domenicali">Stefano Domenicali</a> said the team was not celebrating prematurely:
</p>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r960796168">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>The real Ferrari is not the one we've seen at the Australian GP. The team reacted immediately and has shown the great potential of our package, which is extremely competitive. Having said that, we have to be aware. We're approaching the Spanish GP with a maximum effort, keeping our feet on the ground: we were not depressed after the first race and we are not euphoric after Bahrain. We need absolute motivation and we are aware of the fact that our competitors are getting better.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>
<p>With his team leading the Constructors' Championship, Heidfeld said BMW was outperforming expectations: "It's better than expected after the winter time, of course, but it could also be better. It can always be better."<sup id="cite_ref-heidfeld_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-heidfeld-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> BMW had opened the season with <a href="/wiki/Podium#Usage_in_motorsport" title="Podium">podium</a> finishes in each of the first three races, which team principal <a href="/wiki/Mario_Theissen" title="Mario Theissen">Mario Theissen</a> said put his team amongst the top three, along with Ferrari and McLaren.<sup id="cite_ref-bmwpreview_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bmwpreview-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After a poor race in Bahrain, where he narrowly avoided <a href="/wiki/Stall_(engine)" title="Stall (engine)">stalling</a> on the <a href="/wiki/List_of_motorsport_terminology#G" class="mw-redirect" title="List of motorsport terminology">grid</a> before colliding with Renault's <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> a lap later, Hamilton said he would return stronger in Spain: "I approach it in the same way and obviously I won't be making the same mistake again. This weekend being here, feeling fresh after a good break from racing, and making sure we come here fresh has been important. I feel good."<sup id="cite_ref-hamiltonpreview_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hamiltonpreview-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> His team said that they had made significant improvements to their car, the <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4-23" title="McLaren MP4-23">McLaren MP4-23</a>, in the three-week break, but were reserving judgement until the race to see how their performance had improved in comparison to their rivals.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Supporters_Renault_F1_2008.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/61/Supporters_Renault_F1_2008.jpg/220px-Supporters_Renault_F1_2008.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/61/Supporters_Renault_F1_2008.jpg/330px-Supporters_Renault_F1_2008.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/61/Supporters_Renault_F1_2008.jpg/440px-Supporters_Renault_F1_2008.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2048" data-file-height="1371" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Supporters_Renault_F1_2008.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> supporters at the Grand Prix</div></div></div>
<p>The event also took place under the shadow of incidents that had taken place at the same circuit during pre-season testing. At one of the test sessions, Hamilton was subjected to racist taunting by some of the spectators.<sup id="cite_ref-autocourse2008-pg125_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autocourse2008-pg125-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> In response, the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile</a> (FIA) launched a "Racing Against Racism" programme, and placed the Spanish and <a href="/wiki/2008_European_Grand_Prix" title="2008 European Grand Prix">European</a> Grands Prix on probation, the latter event being held in the Spanish city of <a href="/wiki/Valencia,_Spain" class="mw-redirect" title="Valencia, Spain">Valencia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-autocourse2008-pg125_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autocourse2008-pg125-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> During the course of the event itself, no such incidents were reported.  Hamilton was particularly unpopular with the country's Formula One fans due to his acrimonious inter-team rivalry with Spaniard Alonso during the <a href="/wiki/2007_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2007 Formula One season">previous year's championship</a>, which had resulted in the latter leaving the McLaren team and returning to Renault for 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-autocourse2008-pg125_13-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autocourse2008-pg125-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Super Aguri team had been in financial trouble since a proposed takeover deal backed by equity firm <a href="/wiki/Dubai_International_Capital" title="Dubai International Capital">Dubai International Capital</a>, under the "Magma Group" banner, failed to take effect.<sup id="cite_ref-superaguri_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-superaguri-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The team narrowly avoided folding in mid-April, and were only given confirmation to compete by team owners <a href="/wiki/Honda" title="Honda">Honda</a> on the Friday prior to the race.<sup id="cite_ref-superaguri_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-superaguri-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> This would be the team's final Grand Prix, withdrawing after two years in Formula One prior to the <a href="/wiki/2008_Turkish_Grand_Prix" title="2008 Turkish Grand Prix">Turkish Grand Prix</a>, leaving the sport with ten teams for the first time since the <a href="/wiki/2005_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2005 Formula One season">2005 season</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice">Practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2008_Spanish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Nick_Heidfeld_2008_Spain.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/07/Nick_Heidfeld_2008_Spain.jpg/220px-Nick_Heidfeld_2008_Spain.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="131" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/07/Nick_Heidfeld_2008_Spain.jpg/330px-Nick_Heidfeld_2008_Spain.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/07/Nick_Heidfeld_2008_Spain.jpg/440px-Nick_Heidfeld_2008_Spain.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1083" data-file-height="647" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Nick_Heidfeld_2008_Spain.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> was quickest in the final session of practice.</div></div></div>
<p>Three practice sessions were held before the Sunday race—two on Friday, and a third on Saturday. The Friday morning and afternoon sessions each lasted 90&#160;minutes. The third session was held on Saturday morning and lasted an hour.<sup id="cite_ref-sporting_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sporting-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> The Ferraris were fastest in the first session; Räikkönen was quickest with a time of 1:20.649, ahead of Massa, who recovered from a spin and an off-track excursion on his first two laps to take second.<sup id="cite_ref-prac1_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prac1-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton, Kubica and Kovalainen took the next three positions, ahead of home driver Alonso in sixth.<sup id="cite_ref-prac1_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prac1-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Räikkönen repeated his morning effort in the second session, with a time of 1:21.935, as the frontrunners experimented with higher fuel loads.<sup id="cite_ref-prac2_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prac2-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Renault driver <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet_Jr." title="Nelson Piquet Jr.">Nelson Piquet</a> was next quickest, ahead of Alonso, Williams driver <a href="/wiki/Kazuki_Nakajima" title="Kazuki Nakajima">Kazuki Nakajima</a>, Massa and Red Bull driver <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-prac2_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prac2-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> The McLarens had a less successful session; Kovalainen struggled with <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_car#Transmission" title="Formula One car">gearbox</a> problems, and difficulty with Hamilton's <a href="/wiki/Racing_setup" title="Racing setup">set-up</a> meant he managed 11th after suffering from excessive <a href="/wiki/Oversteer" class="mw-redirect" title="Oversteer">oversteer</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Heidfeld was quickest in the final session, his 1:21.269 benefiting from use of the quicker soft compound tyre.<sup id="cite_ref-prac3_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prac3-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Webber's teammate <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> was next quickest, ahead of Alonso, Kubica, Toyota driver <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> and <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9bastien_Bourdais" title="Sébastien Bourdais">Sébastien Bourdais</a> of Toro Rosso. Ferrari and McLaren again ran heavier cars, and neither team managed better than ninth quickest.<sup id="cite_ref-prac3_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prac3-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Mechanical problems meant Webber failed to set a time, his car coming to a halt in the pit-lane exit after two <a href="/wiki/List_of_motorsport_terminology#I" class="mw-redirect" title="List of motorsport terminology">installation laps</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-prac3_20-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prac3-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2008_Spanish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The qualifying session on Saturday afternoon was split into three&#160;parts. The first part ran for 20&#160;minutes, and cars that finished the session 17th or lower were eliminated from qualifying. The second part of qualifying lasted 15&#160;minutes and eliminated cars that finished in positions&#160;11 to 16. The final part of qualifying determined the positions from first to tenth, and decided pole position. Cars which failed to make the final session could refuel before the race, so ran lighter in those sessions. Cars which competed in the final session of qualifying were not allowed to refuel before the race, and as such carried more fuel than in the previous sessions.<sup id="cite_ref-sporting_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sporting-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatleft" style="width:31%;&#32;;"><style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r887775652">.mw-parser-output .quotebox{background-color:#F9F9F9;border:1px solid #aaa;box-sizing:border-box;padding:10px;font-size:88%;max-width:100%}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft{margin:0.5em 1.4em 0.8em 0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright{margin:0.5em 0 0.8em 1.4em}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.centered{margin:0.5em auto 0.8em auto}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft p,.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright p{font-style:inherit}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-title{background-color:#F9F9F9;text-align:center;font-size:larger;font-weight:bold}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:before{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" “ ";vertical-align:-45%;line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:after{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" ” ";line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .left-aligned{text-align:left}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .right-aligned{text-align:right}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .center-aligned{text-align:center}.mw-parser-output .quotebox cite{display:block;font-style:normal}@media screen and (max-width:360px){.mw-parser-output .quotebox{min-width:100%;margin:0 0 0.8em!important;float:none!important}}</style>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">I've been chasing pole for a while now, but for one reason or another, it always escaped me. But today I got it. There is no better place to start so I will try and make the most of that tomorrow. It will be important to get off the line well, getting through the first corner without any problems. Then it will be down to exploiting the car's potential and that is definitely good. The race is what matters and tomorrow I want to bring home a nice result.</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style=""><a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a>, following the third qualifying session.<sup id="cite_ref-qualquotes_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualquotes-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>

<p>Räikkönen clinched his first pole position of the season with a time of 1:21.813.<sup id="cite_ref-qualautosport_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualautosport-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Alonso would start alongside him on the grid, amid suspicions the Spaniard was fuelled light for the race.<sup id="cite_ref-qualgp.com_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualgp.com-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Massa, Kubica and Hamilton qualified next quickest; just 0.2&#160;seconds separated Hamilton's time from that of Räikkönen.<sup id="cite_ref-qualautosport_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualautosport-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Kovalainen would line up on the third row of the grid next to his teammate; Webber and Trulli would start from the fourth row.<sup id="cite_ref-qualbbc_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualbbc-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Heidfeld was disappointed with his ninth place, after suffering from tyre problems on his final lap; Piquet, who made it into the final session for the first time in his career, qualified just ahead of Honda driver <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> and Nakajima.<sup id="cite_ref-qualquotes_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualquotes-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Honda driver <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> and <a href="/wiki/Timo_Glock" title="Timo Glock">Timo Glock</a> of Toyota would occupy the seventh row; Williams driver <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> was outqualified by his teammate for the first time in the season, and would start from 15th, ahead of Bourdais.<sup id="cite_ref-qualbbc_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualbbc-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard failed to make it through the first session after failing to get sufficient heat into his tyres; Toro Rosso driver <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> and the two Force Indias of <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> and <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a> took positions 18 to 20.<sup id="cite_ref-qualautosport_22-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualautosport-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> The Super Aguris of <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Davidson" title="Anthony Davidson">Anthony Davidson</a> and <a href="/wiki/Takuma_Sato" title="Takuma Sato">Takuma Sato</a> would line up on the back row of the grid in their final race.<sup id="cite_ref-qualbbc_24-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualbbc-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2008_Spanish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:McLaren_Hamilton_2008_Spanish_GP.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b6/McLaren_Hamilton_2008_Spanish_GP.jpg/220px-McLaren_Hamilton_2008_Spanish_GP.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="131" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b6/McLaren_Hamilton_2008_Spanish_GP.jpg/330px-McLaren_Hamilton_2008_Spanish_GP.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b6/McLaren_Hamilton_2008_Spanish_GP.jpg/440px-McLaren_Hamilton_2008_Spanish_GP.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1700" data-file-height="1011" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:McLaren_Hamilton_2008_Spanish_GP.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> started from fifth on the grid.</div></div></div>
<p>The conditions on the grid were dry before the race. The <a href="/wiki/Air_temperature" class="mw-redirect" title="Air temperature">air temperature</a> was 23&#160;°C (73&#160;°F) and the track temperature was 38&#160;°C (100&#160;°F);<sup id="cite_ref-commautosport_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-commautosport-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> weather forecasts indicated a light wind, but otherwise calm conditions.<sup id="cite_ref-weatherf1.com_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-weatherf1.com-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Räikkönen held his lead into the first corner, as Massa passed Alonso to take second; Hamilton passed Kubica to take fourth.<sup id="cite_ref-commautosport_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-commautosport-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> However, an incident further down the field brought out the <a href="/wiki/Safety_car" title="Safety car">safety car</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-fiaracefacts_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiaracefacts-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> Sutil's car entered a spin after attempting to pass Coulthard; the resulting collision eliminated Sutil and Vettel from the Grand Prix.<sup id="cite_ref-commautosport_25-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-commautosport-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-gpreport_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpreport-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> The race restarted on lap&#160;four when the safety car peeled off, Räikkönen quickly increased his lead over Massa to more than a second.<sup id="cite_ref-fiaracefacts_27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiaracefacts-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> The gap between the Ferraris increased as Räikkönen continued to lap the quickest on track; Hamilton began to close in on Alonso, only to see the Renault pit on lap&#160;16, promoting the McLaren to third place.<sup id="cite_ref-commbbc_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-commbbc-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Ferrari_Raikkonen_2008_Spanish_GP.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b9/Ferrari_Raikkonen_2008_Spanish_GP.jpg/220px-Ferrari_Raikkonen_2008_Spanish_GP.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="113" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b9/Ferrari_Raikkonen_2008_Spanish_GP.jpg/330px-Ferrari_Raikkonen_2008_Spanish_GP.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b9/Ferrari_Raikkonen_2008_Spanish_GP.jpg/440px-Ferrari_Raikkonen_2008_Spanish_GP.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2000" data-file-height="1025" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Ferrari_Raikkonen_2008_Spanish_GP.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> took the win from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>Further down the field, several incidents reduced the field to 15&#160;cars. Piquet, after running off the track on lap&#160;five, collided with Bourdais two laps later while attempting to pass at turn&#160;10.<sup id="cite_ref-fiaracefacts_27-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiaracefacts-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> The Renault retired from the race immediately; Bourdais struggled back to the pit lane before Toro Rosso decided to retire his car.<sup id="cite_ref-fiaracefacts_27-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiaracefacts-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> The resulting debris ended Davidson's race when he retired on lap&#160;eight after sustaining radiator damage.<sup id="cite_ref-f1byteam_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1byteam-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> On lap&#160;22 Kovalainen's front left tyre suffered a rapid deflation, hurling his car into the barriers at an estimated 210 kilometres per hour (130&#160;mph), with an impact of 26&#160;<a href="/wiki/G-force" title="G-force">g</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-fiaracefacts_27-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiaracefacts-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-kov1_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kov1-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-autocourse2008-pg126_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autocourse2008-pg126-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> As the <a href="/wiki/Motorsport_marshal" title="Motorsport marshal">track marshals</a> extracted the McLaren from the barriers, the safety car was once again deployed.<sup id="cite_ref-gpreport_28-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpreport-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Heidfeld was forced to pit immediately as he was low on fuel, incurring a ten-second <a href="/wiki/List_of_motorsport_terminology#S" class="mw-redirect" title="List of motorsport terminology">stop-go penalty</a> for coming in before the pit-lane was officially re-opened.<sup id="cite_ref-fiaracefacts_27-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiaracefacts-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello and Fisichella then collided in the pit-lane on lap&#160;26, heavily damaging the Honda's <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_car#Aerodynamics" title="Formula One car">front wing</a>, and requiring Barrichello to drive the whole lap with a disintegrating car, before retiring into his team's garage.<sup id="cite_ref-gpreport_28-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpreport-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-commbbc_29-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-commbbc-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Jarno_Trulli_2008_Spain.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/08/Jarno_Trulli_2008_Spain.jpg/220px-Jarno_Trulli_2008_Spain.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="103" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/08/Jarno_Trulli_2008_Spain.jpg/330px-Jarno_Trulli_2008_Spain.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/08/Jarno_Trulli_2008_Spain.jpg/440px-Jarno_Trulli_2008_Spain.jpg 2x" data-file-width="492" data-file-height="230" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Jarno_Trulli_2008_Spain.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> dropped several places when his team mistakenly called him in for a <a href="/wiki/Pit_stop" title="Pit stop">pit stop</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>Räikkönen, Massa, Hamilton, Kubica and Alonso, having already made their pit stops, trailed the safety car until it peeled off on lap 29.<sup id="cite_ref-commbbc_29-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-commbbc-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> As Räikkönen again increased his lead over Massa to more than two seconds, Alonso pulled over to the side of the track, smoke billowing from his engine, and retired.<sup id="cite_ref-commautosport_25-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-commautosport-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Rosberg's engine also suffered a failure, and the Williams retired on lap&#160;42 from seventh place.<sup id="cite_ref-commbbc_29-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-commbbc-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Four laps later, Räikkönen set the <a href="/wiki/Fastest_lap" title="Fastest lap">fastest lap</a> of the race, a 1:21.670 (a lap record that still stands), as Massa pitted from behind him.<sup id="cite_ref-fiaracefacts_27-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiaracefacts-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> Räikkönen, Hamilton and Kubica followed into the pit-lane on lap&#160;47.<sup id="cite_ref-pitsummary_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pitsummary-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard and Glock collided on lap&#160;53 after the Toyota driver attempted to pass the Red Bull at the first corner.<sup id="cite_ref-commautosport_25-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-commautosport-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Glock sustained front wing damage, and Coulthard suffered a puncture to his left rear tyre; both pitted soon after for repairs.<sup id="cite_ref-pitsummary_33-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pitsummary-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Räikkönen took his second win of the season when he crossed the line at the end of the 66th lap, 3.2&#160;seconds ahead of the second-placed Massa.<sup id="cite_ref-classification_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-classification-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton took third, ahead of Kubica, Webber and Button, who scored Honda's first points for the season.<sup id="cite_ref-f1byteam_30-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1byteam-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Nakajima finished in seventh place, ahead of Trulli, who was running in sixth place before his team mistakenly called him into the pit-lane. Toyota had mistaken Glock's damaged car for Trulli, dropping the latter down the order after the stop.<sup id="cite_ref-classification_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-classification-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Heidfeld, recovering after his penalty, finished in ninth place ahead of Fisichella.<sup id="cite_ref-classification_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-classification-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Glock and Coulthard filled the next two positions after their late collision, ahead of Sato in 13th.<sup id="cite_ref-classification_3-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-classification-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Rosberg, Alonso, Barrichello, Kovalainen, Davidson, Bourdais, Piquet, Vettel and Sutil were the retirements from a punishing Spanish Grand Prix.<sup id="cite_ref-classification_3-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-classification-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2008_Spanish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatright" style="width:35%;&#32;;"><link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r887775652"/>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">If we had wanted we could have gone a bit faster but there is no point to push it when you don't need to. I think it was closer than we expected but saying that there were many safety cars, so without them maybe it would have been looking a little bit different. It was a good day &#8211; everything was brilliant. I am happy that Heikki [Kovalainen] seems to be okay after his accident. I cannot ask anything more as the guys did perfect work.</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style=""><a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a>, speaking after the race.<sup id="cite_ref-pressconference_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconference-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>The top three finishers appeared on the <a href="/wiki/Podium#Usage_in_motorsport" title="Podium">podium</a> and in the subsequent <a href="/wiki/Press_conference" title="Press conference">press conference</a>, where Räikkönen said his pole position start was an important factor in his victory: "This circuit is very difficult to overtake. The start is the best place and when you have a lot of things going with the safety car you might be lucky or very unlucky, so the best place to try to win is always from the front. It was good yesterday and it really helped us today."<sup id="cite_ref-pressconference_35-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconference-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Massa said that the result was a good one for his team, as they moved ahead of BMW Sauber in the Constructors' Championship: "We did our homework and brought two Ferrari's in the front &#8211; first and second &#8211; which is very important for the Championship."<sup id="cite_ref-pressconference_35-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconference-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> Although Ferrari had performed well, Massa said their speed was not significantly better than their opponents, so "we need to keep working like crazy in the factory to improve the car in every area."<sup id="cite_ref-pressconference_35-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconference-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Hamilton agreed that the start was the most important part of the race: "Obviously when we qualified fifth we knew that it would be very difficult to beat the Ferraris. But the key was to get a good start and make as many places as possible and fortunately I was able to do that."<sup id="cite_ref-pressconference_35-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconference-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> Apart from the start of the race, only two passing manoeuvres were recorded: Heidfeld's lap&#160;55 pass on Fisichella and Coulthard's lap&#160;61 pass on Sato.<sup id="cite_ref-autocourse2008-pg128_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autocourse2008-pg128-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Despite his accident, Kovalainen suffered no physical injuries, except for minor <a href="/wiki/Concussion" title="Concussion">concussion</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-kov1_31-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kov1-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> After spending the night under observation in a nearby hospital, the McLaren driver was cleared to race in the next Grand Prix, two weeks later.<sup id="cite_ref-kov1_31-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kov1-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> McLaren said that the wheel failure was due to a faulty wheel clamp which made it vulnerable to the rapid changes in load and pressure that a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_car" title="Formula One car">Formula One car</a> endures, eventually leading to the deflated tyre and Kovalainen's accident.<sup id="cite_ref-kov2_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kov2-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Kovalainen praised Formula One's safety procedures and medical staff:
</p>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r960796168"/><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>It was a serious accident and I managed to walk away. I was a bit lucky, but the safety standards the FIA has been pushing worked very well, ... The chassis, the impact, all the barriers took the impact well, the FIA medical team plus the marshals and the team took the job very well and did a fantastic job to get me out of there quickly in a short period of time ... I was able to make a recovery without any injuries. I think it is something that we must still keep working on, but the work paid off that day.<sup id="cite_ref-kov3_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kov3-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Heikki_Kovalainen_2008_Spain.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/18/Heikki_Kovalainen_2008_Spain.jpg/220px-Heikki_Kovalainen_2008_Spain.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="99" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/1/18/Heikki_Kovalainen_2008_Spain.jpg 1.5x" data-file-width="320" data-file-height="144" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Heikki_Kovalainen_2008_Spain.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> before his crash.</div></div></div>
<p>However, Mark Webber, a director of the <a href="/wiki/Grand_Prix_Drivers%27_Association" title="Grand Prix Drivers&#39; Association">Grand Prix Drivers' Association</a>, said that standards at the track should have been better. Speaking in his regular <a href="/wiki/BBC_Online" title="BBC Online">BBC Online</a> column, Webber said the "run-off on that corner is too tight and we need to have a look at it because any driver that has an error there is going to have a big crash. If Heikki's accident had happened two seconds later he would have been fine and controlled the car, but it probably happened on the worst section of that whole track."<sup id="cite_ref-webberbbc_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-webberbbc-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Räikkönen's win increased his points tally in the Drivers' Championship to 29&#160;points, nine ahead of second-placed Hamilton.<sup id="cite_ref-pointsFIA_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pointsFIA-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Kubica and Massa were one and two points behind Hamilton, respectively; Heidfeld was fifth on 16&#160;points, after failing to score.<sup id="cite_ref-pointsFIA_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pointsFIA-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In the Constructors' Championship, Ferrari took the lead with 47&#160;points, 12&#160;points ahead of BMW Sauber and 13&#160;points ahead of McLaren, with 14&#160;races remaining in the season.<sup id="cite_ref-pointsFIA_5-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pointsFIA-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
