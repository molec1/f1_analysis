<p>The <b>1976 Austrian Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at the <a href="/wiki/%C3%96sterreichring" class="mw-redirect" title="Österreichring">Österreichring</a> on 15 August 1976. It was the eleventh race of the 1976 World Championship of F1 Drivers and the 1976 International Cup for F1 Constructors.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>The 54-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/John_Watson_(racing_driver)" title="John Watson (racing driver)">John Watson</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/Penske_PC4" title="Penske PC4">Penske</a>-<a href="/wiki/Ford_Motor_Company" title="Ford Motor Company">Ford</a>. As well as being Watson's first F1 victory, it was also the only F1 victory for the <a href="/wiki/Team_Penske" title="Team Penske">Penske</a> team, coming a year after the death of their former driver <a href="/wiki/Mark_Donohue" title="Mark Donohue">Mark Donohue</a> at the same circuit. <a href="/wiki/Roger_Penske" title="Roger Penske">Roger Penske</a> would withdraw from F1 at the end of the season to concentrate on <a href="/wiki/American_open_wheel_car_racing" class="mw-redirect" title="American open wheel car racing">Indycars</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Jacques_Laffite" title="Jacques Laffite">Jacques Laffite</a> finished second in a <a href="/wiki/Ligier_JS5" title="Ligier JS5">Ligier</a>-<a href="/wiki/Equipe_Matra_Sports" title="Equipe Matra Sports">Matra</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Gunnar_Nilsson" title="Gunnar Nilsson">Gunnar Nilsson</a> third in a <a href="/wiki/Lotus_77" title="Lotus 77">Lotus</a>-Ford. Drivers' Championship challenger <a href="/wiki/James_Hunt" title="James Hunt">James Hunt</a> finished fourth in his <a href="/wiki/McLaren_M23" title="McLaren M23">McLaren</a>-Ford, having started from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>.
</p><p>Local drivers <a href="/wiki/Otto_Stuppacher" title="Otto Stuppacher">Otto Stuppacher</a> and <a href="/wiki/Karl_Oppitzhauser" title="Karl Oppitzhauser">Karl Oppitzhauser</a> had applied to enter the event, but were refused due to their lack of experience. They had entered under the ÖASC Racing Team banner, with Stuppacher bringing a <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_007" title="Tyrrell 007">Tyrrell 007</a> to the circuit, and Oppitzhauser a <a href="/wiki/March_Engineering" title="March Engineering">March</a> 761. They petitioned the other teams for support, but none was forthcoming and hence they did not participate.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>With local hero <a href="/wiki/Niki_Lauda" title="Niki Lauda">Niki Lauda</a> still in hospital following his near-fatal accident at the <a href="/wiki/1976_German_Grand_Prix" title="1976 German Grand Prix">Nürburgring</a> two weeks previously, and <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> not entering the race in protest at the reinstatement of Hunt as the winner of the <a href="/wiki/1976_Spanish_Grand_Prix" title="1976 Spanish Grand Prix">Spanish Grand Prix</a>, there was talk that the race would be cancelled. In the event, the race went ahead, but was attended by fewer spectators than usual.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>As of the <a href="/wiki/2020_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2020 Formula One World Championship">2020</a> season<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=1976_Austrian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup>, this remains the last World Championship race that Ferrari did not enter and the last time an <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_constructors#Team&#39;s_nationality" title="List of Formula One constructors">American-licensed</a> constructor won a F1 race.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> This would also be the last occasion to date that a female driver would not only qualify for but also finish a Grand Prix with <a href="/wiki/Lella_Lombardi" title="Lella Lombardi">Lella Lombardi</a> qualifying 24th and finishing four laps down in 12th place in a <a href="/wiki/RAM_Racing" title="RAM Racing">RAM Racing</a> entered Brabham.
</p>
