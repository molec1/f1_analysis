<p>The <b>2011 Canadian Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>Formula 1 Grand Prix du Canada 2011</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 12 June 2011 at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_Gilles_Villeneuve" title="Circuit Gilles Villeneuve">Circuit Gilles Villeneuve</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Montreal" title="Montreal">Montreal, Quebec</a>, Canada. It was the seventh race of the <a href="/wiki/2011_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2011 Formula One season">2011 Formula One season</a> and the 48th <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Grand_Prix" title="Canadian Grand Prix">Canadian Grand Prix</a>. The 70-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> after starting from seventh position. <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a>, who started from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>, finished second in a <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a> with teammate <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> finishing third.
</p><p>The race began behind the <a href="/wiki/Safety_car" title="Safety car">safety car</a>, and once it returned to the pits Vettel built a lead over <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>. A second safety car deployment caused by the collision of Button and teammate <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> closed the time gaps between cars, but Vettel retained the lead. By lap 26, increasingly heavy rain led to the race's suspension, before it was restarted over two hours later. Button was involved in another collision on lap 37, which led to Alonso's retirement and Button falling to last place. Over the remainder of the race, he moved from twenty-first place to first, passing Webber and <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>, who had started fourth and eighth respectively, for second place on lap 65, and Vettel on the final lap.
</p><p>The victory was Button's first of the season, and put him into second place in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">World Drivers' Championship</a>, sixty points behind leader Vettel, who had extended his lead despite finishing second. Webber remained in third, and Hamilton's retirement meant he slipped to fourth. In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">World Constructors' Championship</a> Red Bull extended their lead to 65 points from McLaren, with <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> a further 85 points behind. At over four hours the race set the record as the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_race_records#Race_records" title="List of Formula One race records">longest in Formula One history</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Canadian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Grand Prix was contested by twelve teams, each of two drivers. The teams, also known as <a href="/wiki/Formula_One#Constructors" title="Formula One">constructors</a>, were <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a>, <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>, <a href="/wiki/Williams_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams F1">Williams</a>, <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso</a>, <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus_(2010%E2%80%9311)" title="Team Lotus (2010–11)">Lotus</a>, <a href="/wiki/HRT_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="HRT F1">HRT</a> and <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Racing" title="Virgin Racing">Virgin</a>. <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_tyres" title="Formula One tyres">Tyre</a> supplier <a href="/wiki/Pirelli" title="Pirelli">Pirelli</a> brought four different tyre types to the race: two dry compounds, the soft "primes" and the super-soft "options" and two <a href="/wiki/Rain_tyre" title="Rain tyre">wet-weather compounds</a>, the intermediate and full wet.<sup id="cite_ref-Autosport_report_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Autosport_report-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Drag_reduction_system" title="Drag reduction system">drag reduction system</a> (DRS) had two activation zones for the race; one was on the straight between turns 11 and 13, and the second on the start/finish straight from the final to first corners.<sup id="cite_ref-Autosport_report_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Autosport_report-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Going into the race, Red Bull driver <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> led the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a> with 143 points, ahead of <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> on 85 points and <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> on 79.<sup id="cite_ref-RPrev_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RPrev-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> was fourth with 76 points while <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> was fifth on 69 points. In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a> Red Bull were leading with 222 points, McLaren and Ferrari were second and third with 161 and 93 points respectively, while Renault with 50 and Mercedes with 40 points contended for fourth place.<sup id="cite_ref-RPrev_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RPrev-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Red Bull and Sebastian Vettel had so far dominated the championship, winning five out of the six previous races, with Lewis Hamilton winning the <a href="/wiki/2011_Chinese_Grand_Prix" title="2011 Chinese Grand Prix">Chinese Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-RPrev_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RPrev-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Championship contenders Webber and Button had gained one second-place finish each, and Alonso, <a href="/wiki/Vitaly_Petrov" title="Vitaly Petrov">Vitaly Petrov</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> had achieved third place podium finishes.<sup id="cite_ref-RPrev_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RPrev-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Prior to the event Virgin Racing announced they would end their partnership with <a href="/wiki/Wirth_Research" title="Wirth Research">Wirth Research</a>, the company responsible for designing and developing the team's racing cars.<sup id="cite_ref-VirginWirth3_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VirginWirth3-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In order to cut costs, Wirth Research had designed the cars exclusively using <a href="/wiki/Computational_fluid_dynamics" title="Computational fluid dynamics">computational fluid dynamics</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-VirginWirth2_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VirginWirth2-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> and unlike their competitors Wirth had not utilised a <a href="/wiki/Wind_tunnel" title="Wind tunnel">wind tunnel</a>. The approach was not successful as the team had failed to qualify higher than 20th so far in the season,<sup id="cite_ref-VirginWirth3_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VirginWirth3-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> and Virgin decided to end the partnership. Virgin Racing's CEO Andy Webb, who had aims to challenge for a podium position at the inaugural <a href="/wiki/Russian_Grand_Prix" title="Russian Grand Prix">Russian Grand Prix</a>, expressed that ending the partnership would mean "that the team will take greater control of its own destiny".<sup id="cite_ref-VirginWirth_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VirginWirth-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Wirth Research would continue to develop <a href="/wiki/Virgin_MVR-02" title="Virgin MVR-02">the car</a> until the end of the 2011 season, while Virgin Racing would set up their own technical department under the control of former <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a> Director of Engineering <a href="/wiki/Pat_Symonds" title="Pat Symonds">Pat Symonds</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-VirginWirth_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VirginWirth-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Several teams made major technical updates to their cars for the Grand Prix. McLaren and Ferrari altered the brake ducts on their cars,<sup id="cite_ref-Tech_updates_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Tech_updates-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> improving the cooling to manage the high brake temperatures encountered in Montreal.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Ferrari and Mercedes revised their rear suspension layouts,<sup id="cite_ref-Tech_updates_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Tech_updates-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> while Red Bull brought a new front wing optimised for a lower <a href="/wiki/Downforce" title="Downforce">downforce</a> level.<sup id="cite_ref-Tech_updates_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Tech_updates-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Renault and Williams brought new rear wings, which were designed specifically for low-downforce circuits such as <a href="/wiki/Circuit_Gilles_Villeneuve" title="Circuit Gilles Villeneuve">Circuit Gilles Villeneuve</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice">Practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Canadian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Vettel_canada_2011.jpg" class="image"><img alt="Sebastian Vettel driving a Red Bull car at the 2011 Canadian Grand Prix" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/57/Vettel_canada_2011.jpg/220px-Vettel_canada_2011.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/57/Vettel_canada_2011.jpg/330px-Vettel_canada_2011.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/57/Vettel_canada_2011.jpg/440px-Vettel_canada_2011.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3747" data-file-height="2498" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Vettel_canada_2011.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Vettel achieved his sixth pole position in seven races, and the 21st in his career.</div></div></div>
<p>Three practice sessions were held before the race; two 90-minute sessions on Friday and one lasting an hour on Saturday.<sup id="cite_ref-SportingRegs_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SportingRegs-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> was the fastest driver in the first practice session, ahead of Alonso and Mercedes driver <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>. Vettel caused the session to be suspended after crashing into the circuit's "<a href="/wiki/Canadian_Grand_Prix#Wall_of_Champions" title="Canadian Grand Prix">Wall of Champions</a>" on the last corner, heavily damaging his car.<sup id="cite_ref-FP1_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FP1-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> After the morning session <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a>, who had suffered from <a href="/wiki/Concussion" title="Concussion">concussion</a> after a crash at the <a href="/wiki/2011_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="2011 Monaco Grand Prix">previous race</a>, withdrew from the Grand Prix.<sup id="cite_ref-DePerez_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DePerez-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Although he had passed the FIA's medical examination,<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Pérez said that "I only want to drive when I'm a hundred per cent well. I need some more time to recover".<sup id="cite_ref-DePerez_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DePerez-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> He was replaced by McLaren's reserve driver <a href="/wiki/Pedro_de_la_Rosa" title="Pedro de la Rosa">Pedro de la Rosa</a>, who had competed for Sauber in <a href="/wiki/2010_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2010 Formula One World Championship">2010</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-DePerez_14-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DePerez-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Alonso was fastest in the second session, ahead of Vettel, Massa and Hamilton –  although the McLaren driver received a puncture midway through practice.<sup id="cite_ref-FP2_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FP2-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> The session was disrupted by incidents as <a href="/wiki/Kamui_Kobayashi" title="Kamui Kobayashi">Kamui Kobayashi</a> and <a href="/wiki/J%C3%A9r%C3%B4me_d%27Ambrosio" title="Jérôme d&#39;Ambrosio">Jérôme d'Ambrosio</a> struck the barriers – both accidents requiring a suspension as <a href="/wiki/Motorsport_marshal" title="Motorsport marshal">marshals</a> cleared the track of debris – and <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a>'s Force India broke its suspension in a crash at turn 7.<sup id="cite_ref-FP2.2_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FP2.2-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> The third session on Saturday morning saw Vettel fastest, ahead of Alonso, Rosberg and Massa. A <a href="/wiki/KERS" class="mw-redirect" title="KERS">KERS</a> failure on <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_RB7" title="Red Bull RB7">RB7</a> prevented him from participating.<sup id="cite_ref-FP3_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FP3-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> A crash by de la Rosa in the final minute brought out a <a href="/wiki/Racing_flags#Red_flag" title="Racing flags">red flag</a>, and the session was prematurely ended due to the limited time remaining.<sup id="cite_ref-FP3_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FP3-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Canadian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The qualifying session on Saturday afternoon was split into three parts. The first part ran for 20 minutes and eliminated the cars from qualifying that finished the session 18th or lower. During this session, the <a href="/wiki/107%25_rule" title="107% rule">107% rule</a> was in effect, which necessitated each driver set a time within 107% of the quickest lap to qualify for the race.<sup id="cite_ref-SportingRegs_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SportingRegs-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The second part of qualifying lasted 15 minutes and eliminated cars that finished in positions 11 to 17. The final part of qualifying determined the positions from first to tenth, and decided <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-SportingRegs_12-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SportingRegs-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Vettel achieved his sixth pole position of the season, and his first at the Circuit Gilles Villeneuve, with a time of 1 minute and 13.014 seconds.<sup id="cite_ref-Q2_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Q2-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> He was joined on the front row of the grid by Alonso, with teammate Massa in third, giving Ferrari their best qualifying performance of the season.<sup id="cite_ref-Q1_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Q1-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Webber had not been able to use his car's KERS and qualified fourth, four-tenths of a second behind Vettel.<sup id="cite_ref-Q2_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Q2-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton and Button qualified in fifth and seventh respectively and McLaren blamed the slow pace on too high levels of <a href="/wiki/Downforce" title="Downforce">downforce</a> on the high-speed circuit.<sup id="cite_ref-Q2_19-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Q2-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Q_Quotes_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Q_Quotes-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> The Mercedes drivers qualified in sixth and eighth, and Heidfeld and <a href="/wiki/Vitaly_Petrov" title="Vitaly Petrov">Vitaly Petrov</a> completed the top ten. <a href="/wiki/Paul_di_Resta" title="Paul di Resta">Paul di Resta</a> was in 11th position, ahead of fellow rookies <a href="/wiki/Pastor_Maldonado" title="Pastor Maldonado">Pastor Maldonado</a> and Kobayashi.<sup id="cite_ref-Q1_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Q1-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a> struggled with grip throughout the session and was 14th;<sup id="cite_ref-Q2_19-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Q2-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> following him were <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9bastien_Buemi" title="Sébastien Buemi">Sébastien Buemi</a>, Barrichello and de la Rosa. <a href="/wiki/Jaime_Alguersuari" title="Jaime Alguersuari">Jaime Alguersuari</a> qualifying in 18th complained of insufficient grip and brake balance,<sup id="cite_ref-Q1_20-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Q1-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> and at the back of the grid were the Lotus, HRT and Virgin drivers. D'Ambrosio did not qualify after failing to set a time within 107% in the first qualifying session. However the stewards allowed him to race, having considered he was using a new chassis after an accident in Friday practice, where he had set faster lap times.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Canadian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:2011_Canadian_GP_-_Start.jpg" class="image"><img alt="Fernando Alonso attempting to overtake Vettel at the start of the race." src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/db/2011_Canadian_GP_-_Start.jpg/220px-2011_Canadian_GP_-_Start.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/db/2011_Canadian_GP_-_Start.jpg/330px-2011_Canadian_GP_-_Start.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/db/2011_Canadian_GP_-_Start.jpg/440px-2011_Canadian_GP_-_Start.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2738" data-file-height="1825" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:2011_Canadian_GP_-_Start.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Alonso attacked Vettel after the first safety car period (shortly after the safety car came in). Although it had not rained at this stage of the race, the track was still wet from earlier rain and the spray impaired drivers' visibility.</div></div></div>
<p>The track was wet before the race, as rain showers had hit the area throughout the day. The race was due to start at 13:00 <a href="/wiki/Time_in_Canada" title="Time in Canada">local time</a> (<a href="/wiki/UTC%E2%88%924" class="mw-redirect" title="UTC−4">UTC−4</a>), and heavy rain was expected to arrive an hour into the race.<sup id="cite_ref-As_it_happened_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-As_it_happened-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> The air temperature ranged between 17–19&#160;°C (63–66&#160;°F), with the track temperature between 18–20&#160;°C (64–68&#160;°F).<sup id="cite_ref-As_it_happened_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-As_it_happened-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-F1_Racing_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1_Racing-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Standing water on the track, which was causing heavy spray and impairing visibility,<sup id="cite_ref-Whiting_Q&amp;A_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Whiting_Q&amp;A-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> meant that the race would start behind the <a href="/wiki/Safety_car" title="Safety car">safety car</a> and all cars would use the <a href="/wiki/Rain_tyre" title="Rain tyre">full wet tyres</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Autosport_report_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Autosport_report-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Alguersuari would start the race from the pitlane, as he modified his Toro Rosso's <a href="/wiki/Racing_setup" title="Racing setup">set-up</a> to optimise the car for the wet conditions.<sup id="cite_ref-As_it_happened_GP_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-As_it_happened_GP-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:2011_Canadian_GP_-_Hamilton.jpg" class="image"><img alt="Lewis Hamilton driving a McLaren Formula One car with a damaged left rear tyre" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3c/2011_Canadian_GP_-_Hamilton.jpg/220px-2011_Canadian_GP_-_Hamilton.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3c/2011_Canadian_GP_-_Hamilton.jpg/330px-2011_Canadian_GP_-_Hamilton.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3c/2011_Canadian_GP_-_Hamilton.jpg/440px-2011_Canadian_GP_-_Hamilton.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3053" data-file-height="2034" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:2011_Canadian_GP_-_Hamilton.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>After his collision with Button and the pitwall, Hamilton tried to keep his car going but was forced to stop beside the track.</div></div></div>
<p>The race began behind the safety car, with no <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_motorsport_terms#F" title="Glossary of motorsport terms">formation lap</a>, and despite the slow speed drivers struggled for grip on the wet surface.<sup id="cite_ref-As_it_happened_25-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-As_it_happened-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> The safety car came into the pits after five laps, and the cars were allowed to overtake.<sup id="cite_ref-Autosport.com_report_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Autosport.com_report-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Vettel retained first place withstanding Alonso's attempts to pass, while behind Hamilton and Webber collided at the first corner –   dropping the drivers to seventh and fourteenth respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-Autosport.com_report_29-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Autosport.com_report-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Button lost fifth place mid-lap after running wide, losing two places to Schumacher and Hamilton.<sup id="cite_ref-As_it_happened_25-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-As_it_happened-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> At the end of the first racing lap, di Resta had moved up to eighth position, followed by Heidfeld and Kobayashi, who made three places off the grid.<sup id="cite_ref-As_it_happened_GP_28-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-As_it_happened_GP-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Vettel extended his lead over the next lap, while the positions behind continued to change. Kobayashi had improved to eighth and Webber recovered to twelfth, while di Resta fell back to thirteenth. Hamilton lost sixth position running wide on a failed overtaking move on Schumacher, and fell behind Button. He attempted an overtake on the pit straight, but the two drivers collided and Hamilton hit the pit wall. The accident caused Hamilton's retirement, which prompted a safety car deployment at the end of lap eight.<sup id="cite_ref-As_it_happened_GP_28-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-As_it_happened_GP-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The race was resumed on lap 13 and Button, who had changed to intermediate tyres before the safety car period, was given a <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_motorsport_terms#D" title="Glossary of motorsport terms">drive-through</a> penalty for speeding behind the safety car, emerging in 15th place.<sup id="cite_ref-Guardian_report_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Guardian_report-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Vettel began to enlarge his lead over Alonso and Massa in second and third. Alonso and the Mercedes pitted for intermediate tyres, leaving Massa in second and Kobayashi in third by lap 17.<sup id="cite_ref-Autosport_report_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Autosport_report-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello, who switched for intermediates as the safety car came in, began setting times at the same pace as the leaders, and gained 15th position as the drivers in front changed tyres.<sup id="cite_ref-As_it_happened_25-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-As_it_happened-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> On lap 19, a rain storm arrived at the circuit, forcing the drivers on intermediate tyres to switch back to full wets. Alonso and Button, who were in fifth and eighth respectively, fell to ninth and eleventh, while the drivers on the full wet tyres began pitting for fresh wet tyres.<sup id="cite_ref-Autosport_report_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Autosport_report-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The safety car was brought out on lap 20 due to the intensity of the rain,<sup id="cite_ref-As_it_happened_25-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-As_it_happened-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> and Vettel, Webber, Massa and Buemi, who had not changed tyres, went to the pitlane for fresh full wets.<sup id="cite_ref-Pit_Stop_Summary_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pit_Stop_Summary-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> After six laps under the safety car, the conditions were getting worse, and the race was suspended.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:2011_Canadian_GP_-_Race_stop.jpg" class="image"><img alt="The area is saturated with heavy rain and all cars are stopped behind the safety car." src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/be/2011_Canadian_GP_-_Race_stop.jpg/220px-2011_Canadian_GP_-_Race_stop.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/be/2011_Canadian_GP_-_Race_stop.jpg/330px-2011_Canadian_GP_-_Race_stop.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/be/2011_Canadian_GP_-_Race_stop.jpg/440px-2011_Canadian_GP_-_Race_stop.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4368" data-file-height="2912" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:2011_Canadian_GP_-_Race_stop.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Heavy rain hit the circuit forty minutes into the race. Initially the FIA tried to keep the race going behind the safety car, but the track conditions were undriveable and the race was suspended.</div></div></div>
<p>Torrential rain prevented the resumption of the Grand Prix for over two hours,<sup id="cite_ref-Guardian_report_30-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Guardian_report-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> until the rain eased at 15:50 local time. The race was restarted behind the safety car with the drivers in the positions held before the suspension.<sup id="cite_ref-Autosport.com_report_29-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Autosport.com_report-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Vettel was first, followed by Kobayashi, Massa, Heidfeld, Petrov and di Resta. Webber was in seventh place with Alonso, de la Rosa and Button behind. The safety car remained out for seven laps, during which the circuit began drying enough to be suitable for intermediate tyres,<sup id="cite_ref-BBC_report_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC_report-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> and D'Ambrosio pitted on lap 33 to change from the full wets. Vettel began to extend the lead over Kobayashi once the safety car came in on lap 35, as Massa and Heidfeld fought for Kobayashi's second place.<sup id="cite_ref-Telegraph_live_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Telegraph_live-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher led several cars into the pitlane to change to intermediate tyres,<sup id="cite_ref-Telegraph_live_33-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Telegraph_live-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> while Button, Heidfeld and di Resta were among those who pitted the next lap.<sup id="cite_ref-Pit_Stop_Summary_31-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pit_Stop_Summary-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Vettel and Karthikeyan were the only drivers not to change tyres by lap 37,<sup id="cite_ref-Pit_Stop_Summary_31-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pit_Stop_Summary-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> when Button came upon tenth placed Alonso as he exited the pitlane. As Button attempted to pass at turn 3 the two cars touched, and Alonso's Ferrari spun and beached upon a curb, bringing out the safety car.<sup id="cite_ref-Autosport_report_2-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Autosport_report-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-F1_Analysis_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1_Analysis-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> When the race resumed three laps later, Vettel, Kobayashi and Massa retained their positions, as Heidfeld, di Resta, Webber and Schumacher fought for fourth place.<sup id="cite_ref-Autosport_report_2-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Autosport_report-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Button had a punctured tyre after the collision and was in twenty-first and last place, but immediately began to make up positions and was 14th by lap 44.<sup id="cite_ref-Telegraph_live_33-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Telegraph_live-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Di Resta damaged his front wing attempting to overtake Heidfeld; the subsequent pit-stop and drive-through penalty dropped him down to last.<sup id="cite_ref-F1_Analysis_34-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1_Analysis-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher, having overtaken Webber, passed Heidfeld in fourth place,<sup id="cite_ref-As_it_happened_GP_28-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-As_it_happened_GP-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> and set the fastest lap of the race.
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:2011_Canadian_GP_-_Kobayashi-Heidfeld_crash.jpg" class="image"><img alt="Nick Heidfeld hits the rear of Kamui Kobayashi" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ca/2011_Canadian_GP_-_Kobayashi-Heidfeld_crash.jpg/220px-2011_Canadian_GP_-_Kobayashi-Heidfeld_crash.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ca/2011_Canadian_GP_-_Kobayashi-Heidfeld_crash.jpg/330px-2011_Canadian_GP_-_Kobayashi-Heidfeld_crash.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ca/2011_Canadian_GP_-_Kobayashi-Heidfeld_crash.jpg/440px-2011_Canadian_GP_-_Kobayashi-Heidfeld_crash.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3205" data-file-height="2136" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:2011_Canadian_GP_-_Kobayashi-Heidfeld_crash.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Heidfeld's retirement, after his wing became lodged beneath his car, led to the final safety car deployment of the race.</div></div></div>
<p>DRS was enabled on lap 46, as Barrichello and Rosberg were contesting eighth position. Button had caught Maldonado and Alguersuari, and passed both cars for tenth place. Mark Webber was the first driver to switch to slick tyres, and was followed by Barrichello and Buemi.<sup id="cite_ref-Pit_Stop_Summary_31-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pit_Stop_Summary-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Kobayashi and Massa, fighting for second place, were both passed by Schumacher on lap 51; Massa then passed Kobayashi for third place.<sup id="cite_ref-As_it_happened_GP_28-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-As_it_happened_GP-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> As the drivers pitted for slick tyres over the next three laps,<sup id="cite_ref-Pit_Stop_Summary_31-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pit_Stop_Summary-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Massa damaged his wing forcing him into another pitstop, while damage to Adrian Sutil's car led to his retirement. Button had risen from ninth after his pit-stop to fourth, and was catching the leading trio of Vettel, Schumacher and Webber.<sup id="cite_ref-As_it_happened_25-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-As_it_happened-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> On lap 56 Heidfeld, while battling Kobayashi for fifth place, collided with the rear of the Sauber which damaged his front wing. The wing then detached and folded under the car, and Heidfeld after losing control of his car ran down an escape road on turn 3.<sup id="cite_ref-Team_&amp;_Driver_Quotes_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Team_&amp;_Driver_Quotes-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> The debris on-track from the accident called the sixth safety car period of the race.<sup id="cite_ref-As_it_happened_GP_28-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-As_it_happened_GP-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:2011_Canadian_GP_-_Winner.jpg" class="image"><img alt="McLaren driver Jenson Button celebrating his victory in the Canadian Grand Prix" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2f/2011_Canadian_GP_-_Winner.jpg/220px-2011_Canadian_GP_-_Winner.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2f/2011_Canadian_GP_-_Winner.jpg/330px-2011_Canadian_GP_-_Winner.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2f/2011_Canadian_GP_-_Winner.jpg/440px-2011_Canadian_GP_-_Winner.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3302" data-file-height="2201" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:2011_Canadian_GP_-_Winner.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Button celebrating after winning his first race since the <a href="/wiki/2010_Chinese_Grand_Prix" title="2010 Chinese Grand Prix">2010 Chinese Grand Prix</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>Following the pitstops and safety car deployment, Petrov had gained sixth place while Barrichello, Alguersuari, Rosberg and Maldonado were in the remaining point scoring positions. Schumacher and Webber, who had contested second place before the safety car, resumed their fight as the race resumed on lap 61. Barrichello was passed by Rosberg and Alguersuari before Massa, who had been in eleventh place, overtook both Williams cars for ninth. Kobayashi lost fifth place to Petrov, and Maldonado retired by spinning off on the wet track at turn two.<sup id="cite_ref-Team_&amp;_Driver_Quotes_35-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Team_&amp;_Driver_Quotes-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> Webber, passing Schumacher using DRS, cut the chicane on lap 64 and ceded the position back to avoid a penalty. Button passed Webber, after he again cut the chicane, and Schumacher on the same lap, and gained second place.<sup id="cite_ref-BBC_report_32-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC_report-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> As Button began catching race leader Vettel, Webber overtook Schumacher for third place on lap 67. On the same lap di Resta retired because of a puncture,<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> and Massa gained eighth position from Alguersuari. The fastest lap of the race was set by Button on lap 69, setting a time of 1:16.956 as he was closing to Vettel for the race lead.<sup id="cite_ref-Fastest_Laps_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Fastest_Laps-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Vettel led by 0.9 seconds on the final lap, before he ran wide at turn six. Button passed him to take the lead, and held it to win the race.<sup id="cite_ref-Autosport_report_2-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Autosport_report-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Vettel recovered from going off-track and finished second, ahead of Webber in third and Schumacher in fourth. Petrov took fifth place, while Massa passed Kobayashi on the finish line for sixth place.<sup id="cite_ref-As_it_happened_GP_28-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-As_it_happened_GP-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Alguersuari, Barrichello and Buemi filled the final point scoring positions, and Rosberg's car had lost the front wing, dropping him to eleventh.<sup id="cite_ref-Team_&amp;_Driver_Quotes_35-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Team_&amp;_Driver_Quotes-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> De la Rosa was twelfth, and Liuzzi, d'Ambrosio, Glock, Trulli and Karthikeyan were the final classified drivers.<sup id="cite_ref-R_results_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-R_results-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Canadian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatright" style="width:29%;&#32;;"><style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r887775652">.mw-parser-output .quotebox{background-color:#F9F9F9;border:1px solid #aaa;box-sizing:border-box;padding:10px;font-size:88%;max-width:100%}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft{margin:0.5em 1.4em 0.8em 0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright{margin:0.5em 0 0.8em 1.4em}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.centered{margin:0.5em auto 0.8em auto}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft p,.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright p{font-style:inherit}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-title{background-color:#F9F9F9;text-align:center;font-size:larger;font-weight:bold}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:before{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" “ ";vertical-align:-45%;line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:after{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" ” ";line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .left-aligned{text-align:left}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .right-aligned{text-align:right}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .center-aligned{text-align:center}.mw-parser-output .quotebox cite{display:block;font-style:normal}@media screen and (max-width:360px){.mw-parser-output .quotebox{min-width:100%;margin:0 0 0.8em!important;float:none!important}}</style>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">"I enjoyed it very much coming through the field, fighting your way through the field is almost as good as winning the race. That feeling of getting one up on someone. A great race for people who are sat here, to be on the podium is a pretty exceptional result and to fight my way through from last position... It is definitely my best race."</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style=""><a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a>, speaking during the post-race press conference.<sup id="cite_ref-Button_Post-race_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Button_Post-race-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>The top three finishers appeared on the podium to collect their trophies, and in the subsequent press conference. Button felt the win was the best of his career so far, and stated that he had to fight hard to win the race: "It was really a fight. I got a drive-through for speeding behind the safety car and I had to fight my way through about three times. Eventually, on the last lap I was chasing down Seb. He ran a little bit wide onto the wet part of the circuit and I was able to take the opportunity and take the win."<sup id="cite_ref-Post-race_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Post-race-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> Button also praised his team and strategy for helping him take the victory, in particular the moves to intermediate tyres.<sup id="cite_ref-Post-race_40-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Post-race-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> Vettel expressed his disappointment of making a mistake on the final lap, after driving a faultless race up to that point. Nevertheless, he was happy with second place, as he had increased his championship lead.<sup id="cite_ref-Post-race_Vettel_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Post-race_Vettel-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> Webber said he was happy to finish third after his incident at the beginning of the race, putting his recovery from fourteenth place down to correct tyre choices.<sup id="cite_ref-Post-race_40-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Post-race-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Martin_Whitmarsh" title="Martin Whitmarsh">Martin Whitmarsh</a>, McLaren's team principal, praised Button's win, believing that it was one of the finest performances in Formula One:
</p>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r960796168">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>I think it was one of the best wins in the history of F1, let alone his. There were punctures, he had to change the front wing, and from 21st I don't know how many times he had to overtake people. He just drove fantastically well. He kept focused. He applied as much pressure as he had to get past and really did a fantastic job. This was 90 per cent him and 10 per cent the car. He did a great, great, great job.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>
<p>The stewards did not impose a penalty on Button for his collisions with Hamilton and Alonso, having judged that no driver was to blame in either incident.<sup id="cite_ref-No_action_43-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-No_action-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton agreed with the stewards that Button did not intentionally collide with him, and although he felt he was alongside Button at the time, later reflected that "he probably hadn't spotted me".<sup id="cite_ref-Team_&amp;_Driver_Quotes_35-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Team_&amp;_Driver_Quotes-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> He went on to praise his teammate's race as "an utterly fantastic performance, from a truly great driver."<sup id="cite_ref-Team_&amp;_Driver_Quotes_35-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Team_&amp;_Driver_Quotes-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> Alonso also did not place any blame on Button, saying that it was the final incident on a day that "everything went wrong".<sup id="cite_ref-Team_&amp;_Driver_Quotes_35-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Team_&amp;_Driver_Quotes-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Alonso_says_44-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Alonso_says-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Michael Schumacher admitted to having mixed feelings after the race, having been in second position until the race's last laps. The Mercedes driver stated: "I would obviously have loved to finish there [in second place] and be on the podium again. But even if it did not work out in the very end, we can be happy about the result and the big fight we put in."<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> Kobayashi, placed second at the restart, shared similar feelings to Schumacher about finishing in a lower position: "Originally I qualified 13th so then it is not bad to finish seventh, but on the other hand I started second after the long red flag and looking from there the result is disappointing."<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The usage of the safety car came under criticism from figures within the sport. Fernando Alonso disagreed with the decision to start under the safety car as he felt it prevented him from attacking Vettel,<sup id="cite_ref-Alonso_says_44-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Alonso_says-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> while Sauber technical director <a href="/wiki/James_Key_(Formula_One)" title="James Key (Formula One)">James Key</a> thought his team was put at a disadvantage when Kobayashi could not defend against the quicker cars after the fifth safety car period.<sup id="cite_ref-Team_&amp;_Driver_Quotes_35-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Team_&amp;_Driver_Quotes-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Martin_Brundle" title="Martin Brundle">Martin Brundle</a>, who competed in Formula One for twelve years, stated "the fact that the drivers came in almost immediately for intermediate tyres tells me that, on the face of it, the safety car was out too long", and felt that the safety car "should be a last resort, not a default option."<sup id="cite_ref-Brundle_47-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Brundle-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> However, he recognised that FIA race director <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Whiting" title="Charlie Whiting">Charlie Whiting</a> had to consider the driver's visibility, and inexperience using the Pirelli wet-weather tyres led to caution.<sup id="cite_ref-Brundle_47-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Brundle-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> Jarno Trulli however agreed with the FIA's decisions, in particular the length of the red flag delay: "It was right to wait that long; track conditions had to improve in order to make the cars drivable again. They did everything right."<sup id="cite_ref-Trulli_applauds_48-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Trulli_applauds-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> De la Rosa and di Resta also agreed with the decision to halt the race,<sup id="cite_ref-Team_&amp;_Driver_Quotes_35-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Team_&amp;_Driver_Quotes-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> and Whiting stating that "when it rained it was quite clear we needed to stop the race."<sup id="cite_ref-Whiting_Q&amp;A_27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Whiting_Q&amp;A-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> Whiting went on to say that the opinions of the drivers were taken into account on the decision to halt the race, although he also considered the possibility of a suspension benefiting those drivers.<sup id="cite_ref-Whiting_Q&amp;A_27-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Whiting_Q&amp;A-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>With a total time of four hours, four minutes and 39 seconds (including the two-hour suspension), the race was the longest in Formula One history. Button set records for the lowest average race winning speed, at 74.864&#160;km/h (46.518&#160;mph) and with six stops the most pit stops by the winner of a world championship race.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> The race also had the highest number of safety car deployments, with six appearances.<sup id="cite_ref-F1_Analysis_34-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1_Analysis-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
