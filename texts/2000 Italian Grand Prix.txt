<p>The <b>2000 Italian Grand Prix</b> (formally the<b> LXXI Gran Premio Campari d'Italia</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 10 September 2000 at the <a href="/wiki/Autodromo_Nazionale_di_Monza" title="Autodromo Nazionale di Monza">Autodromo Nazionale di Monza</a> near <a href="/wiki/Monza" title="Monza">Monza</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lombardy" title="Lombardy">Lombardy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>. It was the fourteenth race of the <a href="/wiki/2000_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2000 Formula One season">2000 Formula One season</a> and the 71st <a href="/wiki/Italian_Grand_Prix" title="Italian Grand Prix">Italian Grand Prix</a>. The 53-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> after starting from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>. <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> finished second in a <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> car with <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> third for the <a href="/wiki/Williams_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams F1">Williams</a> team.
</p><p>Michael Schumacher maintained his start line advantage and withstood Häkkinen's attempts to pass him going into the first corner. Further around the lap, a collision involving four cars prompted the deployment of the <a href="/wiki/Safety_car" title="Safety car">safety car</a> and a fire <a href="/wiki/Motorsport_marshal" title="Motorsport marshal">marshal</a> <a href="/wiki/Paolo_Gislimberti" title="Paolo Gislimberti">Paolo Gislimberti</a> was struck by a flying wheel. When the safety car pulled into the <a href="/wiki/Pit_lane" class="mw-redirect" title="Pit lane">pit lane</a> on lap eleven, Michael Schumacher began to immediately pull clear from Häkkinen and kept the lead until his pit stop on the 39th lap. When Häkkinen made his own pit stop three laps later, Michael Schumacher regained the lead which he held to clinch his sixth victory of the 2000 season; Häkkinen finished almost four seconds behind.
</p><p>As a consequence of the race, Schumacher reduced Häkkinen's lead in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a> to two points, with <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> a further 17 points back. <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> who was caught up in the first lap accident was mathematically ruled out of clinching the title. In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a>, McLaren's eight-point advantage going into the race was reduced to four, with three races of the season remaining. Gislimberti later died in hospital and his death caused safety measures in Formula One to be reviewed.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2000_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Grand Prix was contested by eleven teams, each of two drivers.<sup id="cite_ref-entrants_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-entrants-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The teams, also known as <a href="/wiki/Formula_One#Constructors" title="Formula One">constructors</a> were, <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jaguar_Racing" title="Jaguar Racing">Jaguar</a>, <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a>, <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Prost_Grand_Prix" title="Prost Grand Prix">Prost</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arrows_Grand_Prix_International" title="Arrows Grand Prix International">Arrows</a>, <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a> and <a href="/wiki/British_American_Racing" title="British American Racing">BAR</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-entrants_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-entrants-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_tyre" class="mw-redirect" title="Formula One tyre">Tyre</a> supplier <a href="/wiki/Bridgestone" title="Bridgestone">Bridgestone</a> brought two different tyre types to the race: two dry compounds, the medium and the hard.<sup id="cite_ref-atlasf1preview_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasf1preview-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Going into the race, McLaren driver <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> led the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a> with 74 <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Championship_points_scoring_systems" title="List of Formula One World Championship points scoring systems">points</a>, ahead of <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> on 68 points and <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> on 61 points. <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> was fourth with 49 points with <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> fifth on 20 points.<sup id="cite_ref-points_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a> McLaren were leading with 125 points, Ferrari and Williams were second and third with 117 and 30 points, respectively, while Benetton with 18 points and Jordan with 13 points contended for fourth place.<sup id="cite_ref-points_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Ferrari and McLaren had so far dominated the championship winning the previous thirteen races. Benetton driver <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> had gained one second place podium finish, while Ralf Schumacher, <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a> had achieved third place podium finishes.<sup id="cite_ref-points_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At the previous race in <a href="/wiki/2000_Belgian_Grand_Prix" title="2000 Belgian Grand Prix">Belgium</a>, the gap between Häkkinen and Michael Schumacher had extended by four points.<sup id="cite_ref-belreview_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-belreview-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Häkkinen started from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> and maintained the lead until he lost control of his car at Stavelot corner on the 13th lap. He later managed to lap faster than Michael Schumacher and passed the German while both drivers were lapping BAR driver <a href="/wiki/Ricardo_Zonta" title="Ricardo Zonta">Ricardo Zonta</a> with four laps remaining and held it to win the race.<sup id="cite_ref-belreview_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-belreview-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The overtaking manoeuvre was heralded by the worldwide press and many people involved in Formula One as "the best ever manoeuvre in grand prix racing".<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher remained confident about his title chances: "With only six points between Mika and I and four more races to go, I am still optimistic about our chances. One win or a retirement before the end of the season can change the whole picture either way."<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Over the month of July, the <a href="/wiki/Autodromo_Nazionale_di_Monza" title="Autodromo Nazionale di Monza">Autodromo Nazionale di Monza</a> race track's main straight was straightened and the Variante Goodyear and Seconda Variante <a href="/wiki/Chicane" title="Chicane">chicanes</a> were reconfigured by the race organisers to become a series of narrower corners with the exit away from the entry of turn one.<sup id="cite_ref-monzaredesign_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-monzaredesign-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The run-off areas around the two sections of the circuit were enlargened.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Some of the drivers, however, were unhappy with the modifications, though, as there were fears of a multi-car accident on the first lap.<sup id="cite_ref-gptrack_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gptrack-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard claimed that the new corner would make braking more difficult and was concerned over the number of penalties issued to other competitors.<sup id="cite_ref-gptrack_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gptrack-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> However, Michael Schumacher believed his and other teams would be less concerned with suspension damage.<sup id="cite_ref-gptrack_11-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gptrack-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a> who was the first driver to test the new circuit, said that it would be easier for drivers to pull off the track in the event of a technical issue.<sup id="cite_ref-monzaredesign_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-monzaredesign-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Following the Belgian Grand Prix on 27 August, the teams conducted a four-day testing session at the Monza circuit and concentrated on optimising their car set-ups for low downforce.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Jos_Verstappen" title="Jos Verstappen">Jos Verstappen</a> set the quickest times on the first day, ahead of <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Diniz" title="Pedro Diniz">Pedro Diniz</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard was quickest on the second day. Fisichella suffered a high speed crash going into the Ascari chicane, bringing a brief halt to testing.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> He visited Rome to undergo a medical examination and was diagnosed with an inflamed tendon in his right ankle, but was cleared to race having been advised to take five days of rest.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a> set the quickest times on the third day as rain shortened the team's running. Minardi's <a href="/wiki/Gast%C3%B3n_Mazzacane" title="Gastón Mazzacane">Gastón Mazzacane</a> suffered a high speed accident at the Ascari chicane, forcing testing to be stopped.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Ralf Schumacher was fastest on the fourth and final day of testing. Michael Schumacher's car developed a malfunction and pulled off the race track, limiting Ferrari's testing time as the car's power unit was changed.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice">Practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2000_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Michael_Schumacher_01_(sk).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/53/Michael_Schumacher_01_%28sk%29.jpg/220px-Michael_Schumacher_01_%28sk%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="239" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/53/Michael_Schumacher_01_%28sk%29.jpg/330px-Michael_Schumacher_01_%28sk%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/53/Michael_Schumacher_01_%28sk%29.jpg/440px-Michael_Schumacher_01_%28sk%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="991" data-file-height="1077" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Michael_Schumacher_01_(sk).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> (pictured in 2007) clinched <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> and the victory.</div></div></div>
<p>Four practice sessions were held before the Sunday race—two on Friday, and two on Saturday. The Friday morning and afternoon sessions each lasted an hour. The third and final practice sessions were held on Saturday morning and lasted 45 minutes.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello set the first session's fastest time with a lap of 1 minute and 25.057 seconds, three-tenths of a second ahead of <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a>. Michael Schumacher was one-tenth of a second off Trulli's pace, while Coulthard set the fourth fastest time. The two Arrows drivers were fifth and sixth fastest; <a href="/wiki/Pedro_de_la_Rosa" title="Pedro de la Rosa">Pedro de la Rosa</a> ahead of Jos Verstappen. Frentzen, Fisichella, Villeneuve and <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Wurz" title="Alexander Wurz">Alexander Wurz</a> rounded out the top ten fastest drivers of the session.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> In the second practice session, Barrichello was again fastest despite not improving his time from the first session; Michael Schumacher finished with the second fastest time. Trulli set the third fastest time, with the two McLaren drivers fourth and fifth, Häkkinen ahead of Coulthard. <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a> recorded the sixth quickest lap. de la Rosa, Diniz, Verstappen and <a href="/wiki/Mika_Salo" title="Mika Salo">Mika Salo</a> completed the top ten drivers. Alesi's Prost was afflicted by a hydraulic leak; this restricted him to three timed laps, and he was slowest overall. Wurz suffered a similar problem and set the 18th fastest time. Mazzacane spun off and did not take any further part in the session.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Saturday practice sessions were again held in dry and sunny conditions. Michael Schumacher set the fastest time of the third session, a 1:24.262. The Williams drivers were running quickly—<a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> in second and Ralf Schumacher fifth—they were separated by Coulthard and Barrichello in third and fourth. Fisichella recorded the sixth fastest lap time. Villeneuve, Häkkinen, Salo and <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Herbert" title="Johnny Herbert">Johnny Herbert</a> rounded out the top ten.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> In the final practice session, Michael Schumacher again set the fastest time, a 1:23.904; Barrichello set the third fastest time. They were separated by Häkkinen with teammate Coulthard clinching the fourth fastest time. The Williams drivers continued to run quickly with Ralf Schumacher fifth, ahead of Button in sixth. Fisichella, Zonta, Villeneuve and Irvine (who suffered a rear suspension failure but regained control of his car) completed the top ten ahead of qualifying. Mazzacane again suffered problems with his car when his engine ran out of air pressure and was forced to stop on the track while Wurz did not record any laps because of a fuel pick-up issue.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2000_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatleft" style="width:33%;&#32;;"><style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r887775652">.mw-parser-output .quotebox{background-color:#F9F9F9;border:1px solid #aaa;box-sizing:border-box;padding:10px;font-size:88%;max-width:100%}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft{margin:0.5em 1.4em 0.8em 0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright{margin:0.5em 0 0.8em 1.4em}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.centered{margin:0.5em auto 0.8em auto}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft p,.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright p{font-style:inherit}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-title{background-color:#F9F9F9;text-align:center;font-size:larger;font-weight:bold}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:before{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" “ ";vertical-align:-45%;line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:after{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" ” ";line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .left-aligned{text-align:left}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .right-aligned{text-align:right}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .center-aligned{text-align:center}.mw-parser-output .quotebox cite{display:block;font-style:normal}@media screen and (max-width:360px){.mw-parser-output .quotebox{min-width:100%;margin:0 0 0.8em!important;float:none!important}}</style>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">I must thank the team for all their efforts. It was good to be fighting my teammate for pole as it proves the car is very competitive. Obviously it is very good for the championship having us both at the front. Rubens can be a big support for me, or maybe I will be supporting him. We will have to see tomorrow. It's good to be back on the right road. I messed up my first lap, running wide at the first chicane, but the car felt quick, which is why I decided to go for a second lap, even if it was a bit of a risk. It was therefore an unusual qualifying session as I finished before the end. I must say I would rather have been in the car than sitting watching the screen. I am confident about our race set up and we have had no tyre problems. As for the first chicane at the start, it will be tight and everyone will have to be reasonable and disciplined.</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style="">Michael Schumacher, commenting on taking <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>Saturday's afternoon one hour qualifying session saw each driver limited to twelve laps, with the starting order decided by their fastest laps. During this session, the <a href="/wiki/107%25_rule" title="107% rule">107% rule</a> was in effect, which necessitated each driver set a time within 107 per cent of the quickest lap to qualify for the race.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> The session was held in dry weather.<sup id="cite_ref-temp_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-temp-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher achieved his sixth pole position of the season, his second at Monza, with a time of 1:23.770. Although he was happy with his car and tyres, he said that he did not make the best of session because of making a mistake at the first chicane during his first run.<sup id="cite_ref-atlasquali_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasquali-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-bbcquali_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcquali-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher was joined on the front row by Barrichello who recorded a lap time 0.027 seconds slower and was happy to start alongside his teammate.<sup id="cite_ref-atlasquali_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasquali-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-bbcquali_26-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcquali-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Häkkinen qualified third, though he believed he could have set a faster time as he struggled with the handling on his car and his McLaren misfired on his final two timed laps. Villeneuve qualified fourth, nearly half a second behind Michael Schumacher, and stated that he was happy with his performance.<sup id="cite_ref-atlasquali_25-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasquali-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Häkkinen's teammate Coulthard qualified fifth and was disappointed with his starting position because he encountered traffic during the session and was blocked by Frentzen. Trulli and Frentzen set the sixth and eighth fastest times respectively for Jordan;<sup id="cite_ref-bbcquali_26-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcquali-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Trulli reported no problems while Frentzen was impeded by de la Rosa.<sup id="cite_ref-atlasquali_25-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasquali-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Ralf Schumacher recorded the seventh quickest time and was disappointed in his performance.<sup id="cite_ref-fiasaturday_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiasaturday-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> De la Rosa completed the top ten fastest qualifiers.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcquali_26-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcquali-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> His teammate Verstappen qualified eleventh having been forced to use two of his team's cars when they developed hydraulic and engine problems.<sup id="cite_ref-atlasquali_25-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasquali-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Button qualified twelfth and said he overheated his tyres after running insufficient amounts of downforce.<sup id="cite_ref-fiasaturday_27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiasaturday-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> Wurz, who qualified in 13th, used the session to familiarise himself with Benetton's spare car.<sup id="cite_ref-fiasaturday_27-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiasaturday-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> He was ahead of Irvine in the faster of the two Jaguar's, who set a best time that was one-tenth of a second faster than his own teammate Johnny Herbert in 18th;<sup id="cite_ref-bbcquali_26-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcquali-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> both were disadvantaged at the lack of straightline speed.<sup id="cite_ref-fiasaturday_27-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiasaturday-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> Salo was 15th quickest for the Sauber team,<sup id="cite_ref-atlasquali_25-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasquali-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> ahead of his own teammate Diniz whose car handled badly under braking.<sup id="cite_ref-fiasaturday_27-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiasaturday-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> The pair were marginally quicker than Zonta who encountered gear selection problems in his race car, and switched to his team's spare <a href="/wiki/Monocoque" title="Monocoque">monocoque</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-fiasaturday_27-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiasaturday-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_motorsport_terms#G" title="Glossary of motorsport terms">grid</a> was completed by Alesi and <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> in the Prosts who qualified in front of the Minardis of <a href="/wiki/Marc_Gen%C3%A9" title="Marc Gené">Marc Gené</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gast%C3%B3n_Mazzacane" title="Gastón Mazzacane">Gastón Mazzacane</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcquali_26-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcquali-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Warm-up">Warm-up</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2000_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Warm-up">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Mika_Hakkinen_1_-_Cropped.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e4/Mika_Hakkinen_1_-_Cropped.JPG/170px-Mika_Hakkinen_1_-_Cropped.JPG" decoding="async" width="170" height="238" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e4/Mika_Hakkinen_1_-_Cropped.JPG/255px-Mika_Hakkinen_1_-_Cropped.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e4/Mika_Hakkinen_1_-_Cropped.JPG/340px-Mika_Hakkinen_1_-_Cropped.JPG 2x" data-file-width="546" data-file-height="764" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Mika_Hakkinen_1_-_Cropped.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> (pictured in 2011) finished second.</div></div></div>
<p>The drivers took to the track at 09:30 <a href="/wiki/Central_European_Summer_Time" title="Central European Summer Time">Central European Summer Time</a> (UTC +1) for a 30-minute warm-up session.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_18-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> It took place in dry weather conditions. Zonta set the fastest time of the session, a 1:26.448, six hundredths of a second faster than Häkkinen, in second place. Michael Schumacher had the third fastest time, ahead of Coulthard in fourth and Verstappen in fifth, with Salo rounding out the top six.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2000_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The race started at 14:00 local time.<sup id="cite_ref-temp_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-temp-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> The conditions for the race were dry with the <a href="/wiki/Air_temperature" class="mw-redirect" title="Air temperature">air temperature</a> 25&#160;°C (77&#160;°F) and the track temperature 34 and 37&#160;°C (93 and 99&#160;°F).<sup id="cite_ref-temp_24-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-temp-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-f1comrace_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrace-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Heidfeld's car was being worked on by mechanics who managed to get to the side of the track before the <a href="/wiki/Parade_lap" title="Parade lap">formation lap</a> begun to avoid incurring a penalty.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher maintained his lead going into the first corner withstanding Häkkinen's attempts to pass. Barrichello dropped to third position. Heading into the first corner, Salo and Irvine made contact, with the Finn suffering a puncture and Irvine retired from the race. Going into the chicane, Frentzen collided with Trulli and collected Barrichello and Coulthard. Frentzen's car lost its right front tyre which struck fire marshal <a href="/wiki/Paolo_Gislimberti" title="Paolo Gislimberti">Paolo Gislimberti</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-gprace_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gprace-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Behind them, de la Rosa collided with Herbert and was sent airborne. The accidents prompted the deployment of the <a href="/wiki/Safety_car" title="Safety car">safety car</a> at the end of the first lap for marshals to clear up strands of <a href="/wiki/Carbon_fibre" class="mw-redirect" title="Carbon fibre">carbon fibre</a> on the circuit and the cars in the <a href="/wiki/Run-off_area" title="Run-off area">gravel trap</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-f1comrace_30-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrace-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Both Sauber drivers, Herbert and Zonta all made pit stops for repairs.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_31-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Gislimberti suffered from head and chest injuries and was given a heart massage before being taken to Monza Hospital. Salo became the fifth driver to pit on lap eight and his mechanics fitted a new engine cover and sidepods to repair handling problems.<sup id="cite_ref-lapbylap_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapbylap-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> During the end of the safety car period, Button swerved to avoid teammate Ralf Schumacher and collided with the barriers on the back straight, sustaining damage to his car. He later went off at the Parabolica corner and became the race's seventh retirement on lap eleven.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_31-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>When the race restarted on lap twelve, Michael Schumacher led, while Häkkinen and Villeneuve were running second and third. Behind them were Ralf Schumacher, Fisichella, Wurz, Gene, Heidfeld, Zonta, Mazzacane, Diniz, Salo and Alesi.<sup id="cite_ref-lapbylap_33-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapbylap-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher began to immediately pull away from Häkkinen as he set consecutive fastest laps. Further down the field, Wurz overtook Diniz and Mazzacane for tenth position.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_31-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> By the start of lap 13, Michael Schumacher led Häkkinen by 2.1 seconds.<sup id="cite_ref-lapbylap_33-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapbylap-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Further back, Zonta passed Heidfeld to take ninth. On lap 14, Zonta moved up into seventh position after passing Gené and Wurz.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_31-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Villeneuve became the third retirement of the race when he pulled over to the side of the track with gearbox problems on the same lap.<sup id="cite_ref-f1comrace_30-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrace-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_31-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Meanwhile, Verstappen overtook Fisichella to take fourth position.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_31-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Heidfeld retired after his engine failed and spun off at Variante della Roggia chicane on lap 15.<sup id="cite_ref-gprace_32-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gprace-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-lapbylap_33-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapbylap-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> One lap later, Ralf Schumacher lost two positions after being passed by Verstappen and Zonta.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_31-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Salo passed Mazzacane to claim ninth position on lap 17. At the start of the 19th lap, Zonta tried to pass Verstappen heading into the Variante Goodyear chicane, but Verstappen moved onto an early defensive line to prevent Zonta from moving ahead.<sup id="cite_ref-f1comrace_30-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrace-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Zonta attempted to overtake Verstappen into the Variante della Rogia chicane to take third place four laps later, but was unable to complete it as he ran wide. He managed to get ahead after exiting the chicane on the same lap.<sup id="cite_ref-f1comrace_30-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrace-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_31-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Ralf_Schumacher_2002.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d5/Ralf_Schumacher_2002.jpg/170px-Ralf_Schumacher_2002.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="248" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/d/d5/Ralf_Schumacher_2002.jpg 1.5x" data-file-width="206" data-file-height="300" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Ralf_Schumacher_2002.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> (pictured in 2002) finished third for the second consecutive Grand Prix.</div></div></div>
<p>Michael Schumacher lapped consistently in the 1:26 range, setting the new fastest lap of the race on lap 22, a 1:26.428, to extend his lead over Häkkinen to 5.4 seconds, who in turn was 9.9 seconds in front of Zonta. Verstappen in fourth was a further 2.9 seconds behind, but was drawing ahead of Ralf Schumacher in fifth.<sup id="cite_ref-lapbylap_33-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapbylap-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> On lap 23, Zonta became the first front runner to make a scheduled pit stop and emerged in eleventh position. Salo continued to move up the field when he passed Wurz for sixth on lap 25. Three laps later, Zonta moved into ninth position after he overtook Mazzacane and Diniz. Salo made a pit stop for the second time on lap 29 and emerged in tenth place. Verstappen took his pit stop three laps later and came out in seventh position. Zonta made his third and final pit stop of the race on lap 36 and dropped to eighth position.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_31-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher took his pit stop on lap 39 and rejoined 13.6 seconds behind Häkkinen, who now led the race.<sup id="cite_ref-lapbylap_33-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapbylap-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Three laps later, Häkkinen made a pit stop and rejoined behind Michael Schumacher with a deficit on eleven seconds.<sup id="cite_ref-f1comrace_30-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrace-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-lapbylap_33-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapbylap-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Fisichella was the final driver to make a scheduled stop on lap 44.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_31-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-lapbylap_33-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapbylap-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Fisichella's pit stop was problematic: he stalled when he encountered a problem with his clutch system and his mechanics push-started his Benetton and he rejoined in eleventh.<sup id="cite_ref-f1comrace_30-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrace-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-gprace_32-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gprace-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At the completion of lap 45, with the scheduled pit stops completed, the race order was Michael Schumacher, Häkkinen, Ralf Schumacher, Verstappen, Wurz, Zonta, Salo, Diniz, Gené, Mazzacane, Fisichella and Alesi.<sup id="cite_ref-lapbylap_33-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapbylap-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Zonta went straight down the escape road near the Variante Goodyear chicane but retained sixth position.<sup id="cite_ref-f1comrace_30-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrace-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Häkkinen was able to close the gap to Michael Schumacher on lap 50 to five seconds, but it appeared that the German would be unchallenged.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_31-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher crossed the finish line on lap 53 to clinch his sixth victory of the season in a time of 1'27:31.368, at an average speed of 130.260 miles per hour (209.633&#160;km/h).<sup id="cite_ref-result_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-result-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Häkkinen finished in second position 3.8 seconds behind, ahead of Ralf Schumacher in third, Verstappen in fourth, Wurz in fifth and Zonta rounding out the points scoring positions in sixth. Salo, Diniz, Gené, Mazzacane and Fisichella completed the next five positions and were one lap behind the winner, with Alesi the last of the classified finishers.<sup id="cite_ref-result_34-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-result-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2000_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatright" style="width:33%;&#32;;"><link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r887775652"/>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">I have no vocabulary to express my feelings except that I am happy and exhausted. 41 wins means a lot to me but there are times when I cannot answer all your questions. This was a very emotional win, even more so than in 1998. Here we are in Italy and after some difficult races we are back on the right road, as we proved all weekend. I am still not in front in the championship but this win is a big relief. There are 500 people working with us and all of them are part of this victory.</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style="">Michael Schumacher, speaking after the race.<sup id="cite_ref-quotes_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-quotes-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>The top three drivers appeared on the podium to collect their trophies and in the subsequent <a href="/wiki/News_conference" class="mw-redirect" title="News conference">press conference</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_18-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher broke into tears when asked if matching <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a>'s number of victories meant a lot to him.<sup id="cite_ref-pressconf_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconf-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> He later regained his composure and spoke about how important it was to maintain the life of the engine at the circuit.<sup id="cite_ref-pressconf_36-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconf-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher revealed that the cause of his emotion was of him thinking about Senna's death at the <a href="/wiki/1994_San_Marino_Grand_Prix" title="1994 San Marino Grand Prix">1994 San Marino Grand Prix</a> and said that he was surprised at how the media reacted to the moment which said that Schumacher "was human after all".<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Häkkinen revealed that his team made modifications to his car at his pit stop which contributed to him setting the fastest lap of the race.<sup id="cite_ref-pressconf_36-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconf-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> He also admitted that he was unable to catch Michael Schumacher due to the presence of the two Minardis which cost him time.<sup id="cite_ref-pressconf_36-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconf-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Ralf Schumacher said he was not worried from being challenged by Verstappen and Zonta during the event because of the Williams's quick pace.<sup id="cite_ref-pressconf_36-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconf-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> He also was confident that Williams had confirmed itself as the third strongest team in Formula One.<sup id="cite_ref-quotes_35-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-quotes-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Barrichello placed blame upon Frentzen for starting the lap one accident at the <i>Variante della Roggia</i> corner. He demanded that Frentzen be banned for ten races. Barrichello also added that his helmet was damaged from his collision with de la Rosa.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> Frentzen reacted by suggesting that Barrichello braked earlier which forced him to make contact with teammate Trulli.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> FIA Race Director <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Whiting" title="Charlie Whiting">Charlie Whiting</a> defended his decision not to stop the race saying that the safety car was deployed as all cars involved were in the run-off areas and that he believed stopping the race would be dangerous. However, he admitted that he was not aware about Gislimberti's condition when making the decision.<sup id="cite_ref-whiting_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-whiting-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> Jordan team principal <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Jordan" title="Eddie Jordan">Eddie Jordan</a> believed that Whiting had made the right decision and praised the safety of the modern Formula One car for protecting drivers.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Bernie_Ecclestone" title="Bernie Ecclestone">Bernie Ecclestone</a>, the owner of Formula One's commercial rights, called for the removal of chicanes from racing circuits labelling them "silly and unnecessary".<sup id="cite_ref-whiting_40-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-whiting-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> FIA president <a href="/wiki/Max_Mosley" title="Max Mosley">Max Mosley</a> subsequently announced that safety measures would be reviewed and stated a review of the Monza track would take place.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> Mosley believed that no driver was responsible for causing the accident but stressed to competitors that it was their responsibility for being aware when bunched up at the start of a Grand Prix.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> Former driver <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Laffite" title="Jacques Laffite">Jacques Laffite</a> advocated an electronic warning system for marshals and believed that a review of chicanes should have taken place.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Gislimberti was later pronounced dead at Monza Hospital. His autopsy was released two days later and determined that the cause of death was head trauma.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> On 15 September, he was given a funeral at the San Ulderico church, <a href="/wiki/Lavis" title="Lavis">Lavis</a> and attended by several drivers, friends and colleagues.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> Hours after the race, five cars involved in the accident were impounded by Italian authorities.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> Race stewards concluded the incident was a "racing accident" with no further action being taken. Magistrate Salvatore Bellomo opened a formal investigation into the crash and interviewed drivers.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> The investigating body examined all five cars which were released back to the teams on 12 September.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> The investigation was closed in June 2001 following a technical examination which concluded that Gislimberti was killed instantly.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> As a result of Gislimberti's death, the strength of the wheel tethers was doubled to stop flying tyres being a danger to the drivers, safety officials and fans. The chassis would be strengthened and enhanced crash resistance would be tested.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>With his win, Michael Schumacher reduced Häkkinen's advantage in the Drivers' Championship to just two points. Coulthard remained in third with 61 points. Barrichello's retirement at the Grand Prix ruled out any chance of him claiming the title and Ralf Schumacher retained fifth place with 24 points.<sup id="cite_ref-points_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In the Constructors' Championship, Ferrari's victory allowed them to reduce McLaren's lead to four points. Williams remained in third place with 34 points. Benetton increased the gap over Jordan in fifth place to a seven-point advantage, with three races of the season remaining.<sup id="cite_ref-points_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
