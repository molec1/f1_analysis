<p>The <b>1999 French Grand Prix </b> (formally the <b>LXXXV Mobil 1 Grand Prix de France</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held on 27 June 1999 at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Nevers_Magny-Cours" title="Circuit de Nevers Magny-Cours">Circuit de Nevers Magny-Cours</a> near <a href="/wiki/Magny-Cours" title="Magny-Cours">Magny-Cours</a>, <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>. It was the seventh race of the <a href="/wiki/1999_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="1999 Formula One season">1999 Formula One season</a>. The 72-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a> driving a <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a> car after starting from fifth position. <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> finished second driving for <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> finishing third for the <a href="/wiki/Stewart_Grand_Prix" title="Stewart Grand Prix">Stewart</a> team. The remaining points-scoring positions were filled by <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> (<a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a>), <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> (<a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>), <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a> (Ferrari).
</p><p>As a consequence of the race, Häkkinen extended his lead in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">World Drivers' Championship</a> to eight points over Schumacher, with Irvine a further 14 behind. In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">World Constructors' Championship</a>, McLaren reduced the lead over Ferrari to six points with Williams passing <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a> for fourth position, 43 points behind Ferrari.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1999_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1999_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Heading into the seventh round of the season, <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> was leading the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">World Drivers' Championship</a> with 34 points; <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> was second on 30 points, 4 points behind Häkkinen. Behind Häkkinen and Schumacher in the Drivers' Championship, <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a> was third on 25 points in the other Ferrari, with <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> both on 13 points. In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a>, Ferrari were leading on 55 points and McLaren were second on 46 points, with <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a> third on 16 points.
</p><p>Following the <a href="/wiki/1999_Canadian_Grand_Prix" title="1999 Canadian Grand Prix">Canadian Grand Prix</a> on 13 June, the teams conducted testing sessions at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Nevers_Magny-Cours" title="Circuit de Nevers Magny-Cours">Magny-Cours</a> circuit from 16–18 June. <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> (McLaren) set the fastest time on the first day, while Eddie Irvine was fastest on the second and final day of testing.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Williams made modifications to the suspension of their cars, resulting in several spins. Among the other teams, <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a> elected to perform aerodynamic mapping work at the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Automotive_Safety_Center,_Variano&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Automotive Safety Center, Variano (page does not exist)">Automotive Safety Center</a> using their test driver <a href="/wiki/Gast%C3%B3n_Mazzacane" title="Gastón Mazzacane">Gastón Mazzacane</a>. <a href="/wiki/Arrows_Grand_Prix_International" title="Arrows Grand Prix International">Arrows</a> performed shakedown runs at the <a href="/wiki/Santa_Pod_Raceway" title="Santa Pod Raceway">Santa Pod Raceway</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 16 June, Jordan driver and 1996 World Drivers' Champion <a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a> announced that he would retire from Formula One racing at the end of the season.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Hill later said a factor in his decision was the amount of testing undertaken by the teams and his own personal performance during the season, although he considered retiring with immediate effect.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice_and_qualifying">Practice and qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1999_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Practice and qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Two practice sessions were held before the Sunday race—one on Friday from 11:00 to 14:00 local time, and a second on Saturday morning between 09:00 to 11:00. The first practice session took place in dry conditions. The ambient temperature was 24&#160;°C (75&#160;°F).<sup id="cite_ref-f1raceweekend_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1raceweekend-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The second practice session was held in overcast and wet weather conditions, with a drop in the track temperature to 19&#160;°C (66&#160;°F) and the ambient temperature to 20&#160;°C (68&#160;°F).<sup id="cite_ref-f1raceweekend_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1raceweekend-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Rubens_Barrichello_2010_Malaysia.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/28/Rubens_Barrichello_2010_Malaysia.jpg/220px-Rubens_Barrichello_2010_Malaysia.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="305" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/28/Rubens_Barrichello_2010_Malaysia.jpg/330px-Rubens_Barrichello_2010_Malaysia.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/28/Rubens_Barrichello_2010_Malaysia.jpg/440px-Rubens_Barrichello_2010_Malaysia.jpg 2x" data-file-width="800" data-file-height="1110" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Rubens_Barrichello_2010_Malaysia.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a>, who took pole position in his <a href="/wiki/Stewart_Grand_Prix" title="Stewart Grand Prix">Stewart</a> car</div></div></div>
<p>Saturday afternoon's qualifying session was held from 13:00 to 14:00 local time. Each driver was limited to twelve laps with the implementation of a <a href="/wiki/107%25_rule" title="107% rule">107% rule</a> to exclude slow drivers from competing in the race. The session was held in wet conditions; the ambient and track temperature were both 18&#160;°C (64&#160;°F).<sup id="cite_ref-f1raceweekend_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1raceweekend-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> clinched his first pole position of the season, in his Stewart, in a time of 1:38.441. He was joined on the front row by <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a>, who was four-tenths of a second behind. <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Panis" title="Olivier Panis">Olivier Panis</a> was third in his <a href="/wiki/Prost_Grand_Prix" title="Prost Grand Prix">Prost</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard took fourth despite going into the gravel and spinning off late in the session. <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a> was fifth, with Michael Schumacher completing the top six.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Due to the wet conditions, a then-record five drivers - <a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marc_Gen%C3%A9" title="Marc Gené">Marc Gené</a>, <a href="/wiki/Luca_Badoer" title="Luca Badoer">Luca Badoer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pedro_de_la_Rosa" title="Pedro de la Rosa">Pedro de la Rosa</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Toranosuke_Takagi" title="Toranosuke Takagi">Toranosuke Takagi</a> - all failed to meet the 107% time. Hill missed out by just three <a href="/wiki/Millisecond" title="Millisecond">milliseconds</a>, the slimmest-ever margin. Due to the wet conditions, and the drivers' competitiveness in practice, all five drivers were permitted to race due to "exceptional circumstances".<sup id="cite_ref-atlasf1_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasf1-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1999_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On Sunday morning, a pre-race warm up session took place at 09:30 local time, and lasted for 30 minutes. It took place in wet weather conditions, with the track temperature at 15&#160;°C (59&#160;°F) and the ambient temperature was 14&#160;°C (57&#160;°F).<sup id="cite_ref-f1raceweekend_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1raceweekend-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard set the fastest lap with a time of 1:32.091. He was followed on the timesheets by Ferrari drivers Irvine and Michael Schumacher who completed the top three positions.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The race took place in the afternoon from 14:00 local time, and started on a dry track, with an ambient temperature of 19&#160;°C (66&#160;°F) and a track temperature of 20&#160;°C (68&#160;°F).<sup id="cite_ref-f1raceweekend_5-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1raceweekend-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Rubens Barrichello managed to get away quickly to maintain first position.<sup id="cite_ref-f1gr_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1gr-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Jean Alesi, starting second, was slower than Barrichello and held up drivers after the start. David Coulthard was able to pass Alesi on lap two. Mika Häkkinen stormed up through the field from his starting position of fourteenth to ninth place by lap two.<sup id="cite_ref-atlasf1_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasf1-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Häkkinen caught and passed <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Panis" title="Olivier Panis">Olivier Panis</a> for sixth place and then caught Michael Schumacher in fifth on lap five. As this happened Herbert retired thanks to a gearbox problem. Coulthard passed Barrichello on lap six by outbraking him into the Adelaide hairpin.<sup id="cite_ref-atlasf1_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasf1-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> And then Diniz retired thanks to transmission failure on lap 7. Mika Häkkinen attempted to pass Michael Schumacher on lap nine by slipstreaming him down the long straight before the Adelaide hairpin, but was on the outside coming into Adelaide and Schumacher was able to stay just ahead. Häkkinen repeated the move on the very next lap, this time to the inside, and was able to stay in front of Schumacher. Schumacher at this point was struggling on a car that was set up for a wet race.<sup id="cite_ref-f1gr_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1gr-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>David Coulthard suffered a complete electrical failure (alternator failure) on lap 10 and retired, giving the lead back to Rubens Barrichello.<sup id="cite_ref-f1gr_10-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1gr-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Mika Häkkinen caught Heinz-Harald Frentzen on lap 12, then hounded him for three laps before passing him in a repeat of the move he had pulled on Michael Schumacher earlier.<sup id="cite_ref-atlasf1_8-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasf1-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Frentzen tried to fight back, and was able to pull alongside Häkkinen, but Häkkinen's inside line through turn six cemented his position. Behind in the midfield Irvine overtook another car. This time it was Alexander Wurz in the Benetton. On Lap 16 Häkkinen caught up with Jean Alesi quickly, but was stuck behind him for several more laps. Häkkinen used the slipstream to pull alongside Alesi on lap 19, but they both locked up into Adelaide and only barely made the corner.<sup id="cite_ref-atlasf1_8-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasf1-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Häkkinen was able to stay ahead, and began to chase down Barrichello, four and a half seconds ahead.
</p><p>Rain began to fall on lap 21. Eddie Irvine pulled into the pits just as the rain started, but Ferrari was not ready for him and his pit stop took 43 seconds (42.9) after his mechanics initially put a fresh set of dry-weather tyres on his car.<sup id="cite_ref-atlasf1_8-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasf1-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Well after the Irvine pit stop disaster happened Giancarlo Fisichella spun, without retiring. A scramble into the pits ensued, but the leaders were all able to hold their positions. One lap after Hill pitted he got a puncture, because he hit a wall. Jean Alesi spun out of third place on lap 25 in Chateaux d'Eau and retired, because he beached his car in the gravel. The safety car was then deployed due to the amount of water on the track.<sup id="cite_ref-atlasf1_8-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasf1-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> On Lap 26 Villeneuve pitted for wet tyres, and before Villeneuve pitted Zonta spun. The amount of water on the track was so great that four drivers – <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Wurz" title="Alexander Wurz">Alexander Wurz</a>, Marc Gené and <a href="/wiki/Alex_Zanardi" title="Alex Zanardi">Alessandro Zanardi</a> – spun while following the safety car.<sup id="cite_ref-f1gr_10-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1gr-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> On Lap 29 Takagi pitted for a new nose. Damon Hill retired with electrical failure while behind the safety car.<sup id="cite_ref-f1gr_10-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1gr-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The safety car came in at the end of lap 35. The restart went cleanly, with no place changes amongst the front-runners.
</p><p>Mika Häkkinen made a dive-bomb attempt to pass Rubens Barrichello under braking into Adelaide on lap 38, but he put his inside wheels up onto the curbing and spun the car a full 360 degrees.<sup id="cite_ref-atlasf1_8-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasf1-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-f1gr_10-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1gr-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Mika Häkkinen fell to seventh place. Michael Schumacher passed Heinz-Harald Frentzen through Adelaide on the next lap.<sup id="cite_ref-atlasf1_8-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasf1-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher made a very late braking move to momentarily pass Barrichello into Adelaide on lap 42, but he went very wide and Barrichello cut back under Schumacher to retake the lead. Schumacher repeated the move two laps later, but this time he didn't go wide and was able to gain first place.<sup id="cite_ref-atlasf1_8-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasf1-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Heinz-Harald_Frentzen_a.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d3/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen_a.jpg/220px-Heinz-Harald_Frentzen_a.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="196" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d3/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen_a.jpg/330px-Heinz-Harald_Frentzen_a.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d3/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen_a.jpg/440px-Heinz-Harald_Frentzen_a.jpg 2x" data-file-width="800" data-file-height="712" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Heinz-Harald_Frentzen_a.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a>. who won the race by 11 seconds (picture taken in 2006 while driving for Audi in the <a href="/wiki/Deutsche_Tourenwagen_Masters" title="Deutsche Tourenwagen Masters">DTM</a> series)</div></div></div>
<p>After 47 laps, a long train formed behind Olivier Panis in fourth, with Ralf Schumacher, Eddie Irvine, and Mika Häkkinen being held up behind him.<sup id="cite_ref-atlasf1_8-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasf1-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Ralf Schumacher got past Panis on lap 50, and then Irvine came into the pits, sticking with wet tyres. Häkkinen got a good run out of Adelaide on lap 51 and passed Panis for fifth place into Nurburgring corner. On the same lap, Michael Schumacher suffered an electrical malfunction and lost most of his eight-second lead to Rubens Barrichello.<sup id="cite_ref-f1gr_10-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1gr-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> He pitted three laps later, changing his steering wheel in addition to tyres.<sup id="cite_ref-atlasf1_8-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasf1-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Mika Häkkinen got past Heinz-Harald Frentzen for second place on lap 57, with Frentzen running wide at Adelaide. Häkkinen passed Barrichello on lap 60 to retake the lead.<sup id="cite_ref-atlasf1_8-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasf1-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Mika Häkkinen and Rubens Barrichello pitted for extra fuel on lap 66, which let Heinz-Harald Frentzen through into the lead.<sup id="cite_ref-gpenc_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpenc-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> During Frentzen's one and only pit stop to change onto wet tyres, the Jordan team had fortuitously filled his car with enough fuel to finish the race.<sup id="cite_ref-atlasf1_8-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasf1-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Ralf Schumacher passed Michael Schumacher on lap 70, with Michael Schumacher appearing to still have car trouble. Frentzen won the race after 72 laps to secure his first victory of the season in a time of 1:58.24.343. Häkkinen held position to finish second, with Barrichello third, 43 seconds behind.<sup id="cite_ref-formula1.com_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-formula1.com-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1999_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The top three finishers appeared on the <a href="/wiki/Podium#Usage_in_motorsport" title="Podium">podium</a> and in the subsequent <a href="/wiki/Press_conference" title="Press conference">press conference</a>.
</p><p>Damon Hill hinted after the race that this one could have been his last, or if he did compete at <a href="/wiki/1999_British_Grand_Prix" title="1999 British Grand Prix">Silverstone</a> in the next race he might leave Formula One afterwards.<sup id="cite_ref-atlasf1_8-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasf1-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Toranosuke_Takagi" title="Toranosuke Takagi">Toranosuke Takagi</a> was disqualified for using tyres which had been marked for his team-mate, <a href="/wiki/Pedro_de_la_Rosa" title="Pedro de la Rosa">Pedro de la Rosa</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As a consequence of the race, Häkkinen extended his lead in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">World Drivers' Championship</a> to eight points over Schumacher, with Irvine a further 14 behind. In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">World Constructors' Championship</a>, McLaren reduced the lead over Ferrari to six points with Williams passing <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a> for fourth position, 43 points behind Ferrari.
</p>
