<p>The <b>2018 French Grand Prix</b> (formally known as the <b>Formula 1 Pirelli Grand Prix de France 2018</b>)<sup id="cite_ref-formal_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-formal-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Auto_racing" title="Auto racing">motor race</a> that took place on 24 June 2018 at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_Paul_Ricard" title="Circuit Paul Ricard">Circuit Paul Ricard</a> in <a href="/wiki/Le_Castellet,_Var" title="Le Castellet, Var">Le Castellet</a>, France.<sup id="cite_ref-formal_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-formal-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The race was the eighth round of the <a href="/wiki/2018_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2018 Formula One World Championship">2018 FIA Formula One World Championship</a> and marked the first time that the <a href="/wiki/French_Grand_Prix" title="French Grand Prix">French Grand Prix</a> has been run since <a href="/wiki/2008_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2008 Formula One World Championship">2008</a>. It was the 87th running of the French Grand Prix, and the 59th time the event had been included as a round of the <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_World_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="Formula One World Championship">Formula One World Championship</a> since the inception of the series in <a href="/wiki/1950_Formula_One_season" title="1950 Formula One season">1950</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> entered the race with a one-point lead over <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> in the World Drivers' Championship. In the World Constructors' Championship, <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> led Ferrari by seventeen points. Hamilton took the lead in the Driver's Championship by winning the race, with Vettel finishing fifth.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2018_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2018_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The race returned to the calendar for the first time since <a href="/wiki/2008_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2008 Formula One World Championship">2008</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Circuit_Paul_Ricard" title="Circuit Paul Ricard">Circuit Paul Ricard</a> chosen as the venue. The circuit last hosted the French Grand Prix in <a href="/wiki/1990_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1990 Formula One World Championship">1990</a> before the event moved to the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Nevers_Magny-Cours" title="Circuit de Nevers Magny-Cours">Circuit de Nevers Magny-Cours</a> in <a href="/wiki/1991_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1991 Formula One World Championship">1991</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-France_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-France-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The race used the 5.842&#160;km (3.630&#160;mi) layout of the Circuit Paul Ricard for the first time. The layout includes a chicane on the Mistral straight as opposed to the 5.809&#160;km (3.610&#160;mi) circuit that was used nine times between <a href="/wiki/1971_Formula_One_season" title="1971 Formula One season">1971</a> and <a href="/wiki/1985_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1985 Formula One World Championship">1985</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-short_circuit_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-short_circuit-4">&#91;note 1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The race was run in June, filling a vacancy left by the <a href="/wiki/2018_Azerbaijan_Grand_Prix" title="2018 Azerbaijan Grand Prix">Azerbaijan Grand Prix</a>. The race in Azerbaijan was moved to an April date to avoid clashing with celebrations for the centenary of the <a href="/wiki/Azerbaijan_Democratic_Republic" title="Azerbaijan Democratic Republic">Azerbaijan Democratic Republic</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Drag_reduction_system">Drag reduction system</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2018_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Drag reduction system">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The circuit featured two <a href="/wiki/Drag_reduction_system" title="Drag reduction system">drag reduction system</a> (DRS) zones. The first was located along the main straight, while the second was on the Mistral Straight on the approach to the chicane.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tyres">Tyres</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2018_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Tyres">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Tyre supplier <a href="/wiki/Pirelli" title="Pirelli">Pirelli</a> provided teams with the soft, supersoft and ultrasoft compounds of tyres. They reverted to their narrow tread compound following feedback from the teams in the wake of mid-season testing at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Barcelona-Catalunya" title="Circuit de Barcelona-Catalunya">Circuit de Barcelona-Catalunya</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Free_practice">Free practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2018_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Free practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> set the fastest lap in the first free practice session, which was cut short by an accident involving <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Marcus_Ericsson" title="Marcus Ericsson">Marcus Ericsson</a>. Ericsson lost control of his <a href="/wiki/Sauber_C37" title="Sauber C37">Sauber C37</a> on the approach to Turn 11 and spun into the barrier on the outside of the corner. The car hit the tyre wall at an angle and subsequently caught fire. Ericsson was unharmed, but with two minutes remaining the session was abandoned and the damage to his car so extensive that he was unable to take part in the second free practice session. Several drivers experienced spins during the session, most notably at Turn 6 where an intermittent and gusty <a href="/wiki/Mistral_(wind)" title="Mistral (wind)">local wind</a> caught the drivers unaware as they accelerated away from the apex of the corner. Unlike Ericsson, all of the drivers avoided contact with the wall courtesy of the circuit's unique, abrasive tarmac run-off areas designed to slow down cars that left the circuit.
</p><p>Hamilton was fastest again in the second free practice session despite having his flying lap interrupted by another red flag. <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a> lost a wheel as he turned onto the Mistral Straight, prompting race officials to mount an investigation as to whether <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a> had released Pérez from the pit lane with his car in an unsafe condition. The session was restarted once Pérez's car was cleared away.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
