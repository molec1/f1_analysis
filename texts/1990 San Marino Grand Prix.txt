<p>The <b>1990 San Marino Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>10<sup>o</sup> Gran Premio di San Marino</b><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 13 May 1990 at <a href="/wiki/Autodromo_Enzo_e_Dino_Ferrari" class="mw-redirect" title="Autodromo Enzo e Dino Ferrari">Imola</a>. It was the third race of the <a href="/wiki/1990_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1990 Formula One World Championship">1990 Formula One World Championship</a>. The race was held over 61 laps of the 5.04-kilometre (3.13&#160;mi) circuit for a race distance of 307.44 kilometres (191.03&#160;mi).
</p><p>The race was won by Italian driver <a href="/wiki/Riccardo_Patrese" title="Riccardo Patrese">Riccardo Patrese</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW13" title="Williams FW13">Williams</a>-<a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>. It was Patrese's third Grand Prix victory, and his first since the <a href="/wiki/1983_South_African_Grand_Prix" title="1983 South African Grand Prix">1983 South African Grand Prix</a>. As of May&#160;2020<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=1990_San_Marino_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup> Patrese holds the record for the longest waiting period between two Grand Prix wins, at 6 years, 6 months and 28 days. The record of most Grands Prix between wins was also broken and would be retained by Patrese until the <a href="/wiki/2018_United_States_Grand_Prix" title="2018 United States Grand Prix">2018 United States Grand Prix</a> when <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> took the record.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Austrian driver <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> finished second in a <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/5" title="McLaren MP4/5">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Honda_in_Formula_One" title="Honda in Formula One">Honda</a>, with Patrese's compatriot <a href="/wiki/Alessandro_Nannini" title="Alessandro Nannini">Alessandro Nannini</a> third in a <a href="/wiki/Benetton_B190" title="Benetton B190">Benetton</a>-<a href="/wiki/Ford_Motor_Company" title="Ford Motor Company">Ford</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1990_San_Marino_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the run-up to the first European race of the 1990 World Championship, there were a few changes to the grid. Brabham replaced Swiss driver <a href="/wiki/Gregor_Foitek" title="Gregor Foitek">Gregor Foitek</a> with Australian driver <a href="/wiki/David_Brabham" title="David Brabham">David Brabham</a>, the youngest son of team founder <a href="/wiki/Jack_Brabham" title="Jack Brabham">Sir Jack Brabham</a>. Foitek moved over to the troubled <a href="/wiki/Onyx_Grand_Prix" title="Onyx Grand Prix">Onyx</a> team (part-run by his father Karl), replacing <a href="/wiki/Stefan_Johansson" title="Stefan Johansson">Stefan Johansson</a>, who was unhappy at the way the team was being run. David Brabham's older brother <a href="/wiki/Gary_Brabham" title="Gary Brabham">Gary</a> had quit the <a href="/wiki/Life_Racing_Engines" title="Life Racing Engines">Life</a> team, describing it as "totally disorganised and unprofessional",<sup id="cite_ref-Walker_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Walker-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and had been replaced by Italian veteran <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Giacomelli" title="Bruno Giacomelli">Bruno Giacomelli</a>, who had last raced in F1 in <a href="/wiki/1983_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1983 Formula One World Championship">1983</a>. Meanwhile, <a href="/wiki/Emanuele_Pirro" title="Emanuele Pirro">Emanuele Pirro</a> returned to the <a href="/wiki/BMS_Scuderia_Italia" title="BMS Scuderia Italia">Dallara</a> team, having missed the first two races of the season due to hepatitis. Several teams unveiled new cars, with the <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_019" title="Tyrrell 019">Tyrrell 019</a> – designed by <a href="/wiki/Harvey_Postlethwaite" title="Harvey Postlethwaite">Harvey Postlethwaite</a> – drawing much attention as the first F1 car to sport a "high-nose" design with downward-extending supports for the front wing.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1990_San_Marino_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-qualifying_report">Pre-qualifying report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1990_San_Marino_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Pre-qualifying report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Friday morning pre-qualifying session took shape very soon into the one-hour session, after both <a href="/wiki/Automobiles_Gonfaronnaises_Sportives" title="Automobiles Gonfaronnaises Sportives">AGS</a> cars dropped out almost immediately. The team had brought their new <a href="/w/index.php?title=AGS_JH25&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="AGS JH25 (page does not exist)">JH25</a> car to this race, but <a href="/wiki/Yannick_Dalmas" title="Yannick Dalmas">Yannick Dalmas</a> was withdrawn due to a hand injury he suffered in a testing accident, and <a href="/wiki/Gabriele_Tarquini" title="Gabriele Tarquini">Gabriele Tarquini</a>'s car failed on its first lap with a fuel pressure issue. This left seven cars in the session, three of which were uncompetitive.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Larrousse" title="Larrousse">Larrousse</a>-<a href="/wiki/Lola_Cars" title="Lola Cars">Lola</a> team also brought a new car to the Grand Prix, the <a href="/wiki/Lola_LC90" title="Lola LC90">LC90</a>. As at the previous race in Brazil, they finished first and second, with <a href="/wiki/%C3%89ric_Bernard" title="Éric Bernard">Éric Bernard</a> nearly a second faster than his team-mate <a href="/wiki/Aguri_Suzuki" title="Aguri Suzuki">Aguri Suzuki</a>. The updated <a href="/wiki/Osella" title="Osella">Osella</a> <a href="/w/index.php?title=Osella_FA1M&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Osella FA1M (page does not exist)">FA1ME</a> of <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Grouillard" title="Olivier Grouillard">Olivier Grouillard</a> was third fastest, a fraction ahead of <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Moreno" title="Roberto Moreno">Roberto Moreno</a> in the <a href="/wiki/EuroBrun" title="EuroBrun">EuroBrun</a>.
</p><p>Apart from the AGS cars, the other runners who failed to pre-qualify included <a href="/wiki/Bertrand_Gachot" title="Bertrand Gachot">Bertrand Gachot</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Coloni" title="Scuderia Coloni">Coloni</a>, which, despite revised aerodynamics and a 23kg weight reduction, was still seven seconds away from Bernard's time.<sup id="cite_ref-Walker_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Walker-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Even slower was <a href="/wiki/Claudio_Langes" title="Claudio Langes">Claudio Langes</a> in the other EuroBrun, down in sixth place. At the <a href="/wiki/Life_Racing_Engines" title="Life Racing Engines">Life</a> team, <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Giacomelli" title="Bruno Giacomelli">Bruno Giacomelli</a> drove the L190 for the first time, having replaced <a href="/wiki/Gary_Brabham" title="Gary Brabham">Gary Brabham</a>. A drivebelt failed on the Italian's very slow first lap, and the car did not reappear for the rest of the session.<sup id="cite_ref-Walker_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Walker-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying_report">Qualifying report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1990_San_Marino_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Qualifying report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In practice, <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Alessandro_Nannini" title="Alessandro Nannini">Alessandro Nannini</a> and <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Pierluigi_Martini" title="Pierluigi Martini">Pierluigi Martini</a> both crashed heavily, Martini cracking his heel and withdrawing from the race as a result. 
</p><p>In the qualifying sessions, the <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLarens</a> filled the front row, with <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> on pole and team-mate <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> alongside him. The two <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> were on the second row with <a href="/wiki/Riccardo_Patrese" title="Riccardo Patrese">Riccardo Patrese</a> ahead of <a href="/wiki/Thierry_Boutsen" title="Thierry Boutsen">Thierry Boutsen</a>, while the two <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferraris</a> made up the third row, <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> ahead of <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a>. The top ten was completed by the <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_Racing" title="Tyrrell Racing">Tyrrell</a> of <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a>, the Benettons of <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a> and Nannini, and the <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Lotus</a> of <a href="/wiki/Derek_Warwick" title="Derek Warwick">Derek Warwick</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1990_San_Marino_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_report">Race report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1990_San_Marino_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Race report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Pirro, who had qualified 21st, started from the back of the grid after his Dallara stalled at the start of the formation lap. At the start, Senna led away from Berger while Boutsen got ahead of Patrese. At Tamburello, Mansell ran wide and kicked up dust, which caused the <a href="/wiki/Leyton_House_Racing" title="Leyton House Racing">Leyton House</a> of <a href="/wiki/Ivan_Capelli" title="Ivan Capelli">Ivan Capelli</a> and the second Tyrrell of <a href="/wiki/Satoru_Nakajima" title="Satoru Nakajima">Satoru Nakajima</a> to collide with each other, while at Tosa <a href="/wiki/Martin_Donnelly_(racing_driver)" title="Martin Donnelly (racing driver)">Martin Donnelly</a> spun his Lotus, narrowly avoiding other drivers. Meanwhile, Boutsen got past Berger but was unable to close on Senna. The order remained the same until lap 3 when Senna pulled off with a broken wheel rim, allowing Boutsen to take the lead with Berger close behind. Further back, Alesi collided with Piquet at Tosa; both drivers continued.
</p><p>Boutsen led until his Renault engine blew on lap 17, which left Berger ahead of Patrese and Mansell. The Englishman passed Patrese going into Tosa, much to the delight of the Italian fans. Mansell continued to charge, despite being hit by <a href="/wiki/Andrea_de_Cesaris" title="Andrea de Cesaris">Andrea de Cesaris</a> while trying to lap him and challenged Berger for the lead. On the run up to Villeneuve, Mansell tried to go around the outside, but Berger closed the door, causing Mansell to spin dramatically. The Englishman avoided hitting anything and ended up pointing in the right direction, he continued in second place, however, dirt and debris had entered Mansell's engine, causing it to overheat and blow-up a few laps later.
</p><p>Mansell's demise left Berger ahead of Patrese, who went through into the lead on lap 51. Nannini and Prost battled over third place, with Nannini winning out. Patrese duly won his first race since the <a href="/wiki/1983_South_African_Grand_Prix" title="1983 South African Grand Prix">1983 South African Grand Prix</a>, leading home Berger, Nannini, Prost, Piquet, and Alesi. With 98 races between victories, Patrese claimed the record for most starts between wins - a record that would be taken 28 years later by <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a>, who started 113 races between winning the <a href="/wiki/2013_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="2013 Australian Grand Prix">2013 Australian Grand Prix</a> and the <a href="/wiki/2018_United_States_Grand_Prix" title="2018 United States Grand Prix">2018 United States Grand Prix</a>.
</p>
