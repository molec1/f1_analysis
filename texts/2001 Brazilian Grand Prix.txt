<p>The <b>2001 Brazilian Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>XXX Grande Prêmio Marlboro do Brasil</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 1 April 2001 at the <a href="/wiki/Aut%C3%B3dromo_Jos%C3%A9_Carlos_Pace" title="Autódromo José Carlos Pace">Autódromo José Carlos Pace</a>, <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo_(city)" class="mw-redirect" title="São Paulo (city)">São Paulo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>. It was the third race of the <a href="/wiki/2001_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2001 Formula One season">2001 Formula One season</a>. The 71-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> driver <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> after starting from fifth position. <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> finished second in a <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> with <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> third for the <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a> team.
</p><p>The race was Coulthard's first win of the season, and the result meant he reduced the lead of Schumacher in the Drivers' Championship to six points along with moving up to second place, 10 points ahead of <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a>. McLaren reduced the lead to Ferrari in the Constructors' Championship to 15 points, with 14 races of the season remaining.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2001_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2001_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Grand Prix was contested by 22 drivers, in eleven teams of two.<sup id="cite_ref-entrylist_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-entrylist-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The teams, also known as <a href="/wiki/Formula_One#Constructors" title="Formula One">constructors</a>, were <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>, <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a>, <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a>, <a href="/wiki/British_American_Racing" title="British American Racing">BAR</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arrows_Grand_Prix_International" title="Arrows Grand Prix International">Arrows</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jaguar_Racing" title="Jaguar Racing">Jaguar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Prost_Grand_Prix" title="Prost Grand Prix">Prost</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-entrylist_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-entrylist-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_tyre" class="mw-redirect" title="Formula One tyre">Tyre</a> suppliers <a href="/wiki/Bridgestone" title="Bridgestone">Bridgestone</a> and <a href="/wiki/Michelin" title="Michelin">Michelin</a> brought five different tyre types to the race: two dry compounds and three <a href="/wiki/Rain_tyre" title="Rain tyre">wet-weather tyres</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Going into the race, Ferrari driver <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> led the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a> with 20 points, ahead of <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> and <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> who were tied for second on ten points each. <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a> was fourth on five points with <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> in fifth with three points. In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a>, Ferrari were leading with 30 points; McLaren were second on eleven points. Jordan and Sauber were third and fourth with five and four points, respectively, with Williams fifth on two points. Ferrari and Michael Schumacher had so far dominated the championship, winning the previous two races. Championship competitors Barrichello and Coulthard had both achieved second- and third-place podium finishes.
</p><p>Following the <a href="/wiki/2001_Malaysian_Grand_Prix" title="2001 Malaysian Grand Prix">Malaysian Grand Prix</a> on 18 March, the teams conducted testing sessions at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Barcelona-Catalunya" title="Circuit de Barcelona-Catalunya">Circuit de Catalunya</a> between 20–23 March to prepare for the upcoming Brazilian Grand Prix at the Autódromo José Carlos Pace. Ferrari test driver <a href="/wiki/Luca_Badoer" title="Luca Badoer">Luca Badoer</a> set the quickest times on the first day, ahead of McLaren's test driver <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Wurz" title="Alexander Wurz">Alexander Wurz</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Badoer remained-fastest on the second day. <a href="/wiki/Marc_Gen%C3%A9" title="Marc Gené">Marc Gené</a>, the Williams test driver, stopped on track with a mechanical failure which brought a brief halt to testing.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher was fastest on the third day of testing. Seven suspensions were required as several drivers were afflicted by car problems or spinning off the circuit.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher stayed fastest for the final day's running.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Aut%C3%B3dromo_Jos%C3%A9_Carlos_Pace" title="Autódromo José Carlos Pace">Autódromo José Carlos Pace</a> underwent safety changes following the <a href="/wiki/2000_Brazilian_Grand_Prix" title="2000 Brazilian Grand Prix">previous year's race</a>. Twenty-one cabins were installed to protect <a href="/wiki/Motorsport_marshal" title="Motorsport marshal">marshals</a> across the track in response to the death of marshal Graham Beveridge at the <a href="/wiki/2001_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="2001 Australian Grand Prix">Australian Grand Prix</a>. Animals roaming near the circuit perimeter were rounded up and taken to another location. A guard rail was installed at the Bico do Pato curve after the speed of Formula One cars had greatly increased from 2000. The <a href="/wiki/Run-off_area" title="Run-off area">gravel trap</a> on the Reta Oposta straight was reinforced and the circuit's drainage system was repaired.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The renovation work had been disrupted on 17 March when a contractor became dissatisfied with São Paulo city administration and enquiries were initiated.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The dispute, over the delayed delivery of tables and chairs, was confirmed by race organisers and would not interfere with the Grand Prix.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello was critical of the Grand Prix citing the dissatisfaction of some drivers in past years and the quality of the local facilities.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> However, the director of the event, Carlos Roberto Montagne disputed Barrichello's claims, stating that two engineers from the United Kingdom approved the road surface.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The drivers, however, were unhappy at the work as the track still remained bumpy. McLaren driver <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> described the track as "very bumpy indeed. It seems to get worse and worse every year."<sup id="cite_ref-trackquotes_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-trackquotes-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Minardi's <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> claimed that the bumping was so bad that the drivers could have lost their vision of the circuit.<sup id="cite_ref-trackquotes_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-trackquotes-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice_and_qualifying">Practice and qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2001_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Practice and qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Four practice sessions were held before the Sunday race—two on Friday, and two on Saturday. The Friday morning and afternoon sessions each lasted an hour. The third and final practice sessions were held on Saturday morning and lasted 45 minutes.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The Friday practice sessions were held in dry weather conditions and on a dirty track which gradually cleaned up as the session progressed.<sup id="cite_ref-fiafriday_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiafriday-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher outpaced the other drivers in the first practice session, setting a lap of 1:16.832. Häkkinen was second-fastest and was initially quickest during the first quarter of the session. The formation continued with Barrichello and Coulthard third- and fourth-fastest, respectively. <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Panis" title="Olivier Panis">Olivier Panis</a> was fifth-quickest, ahead of both Jaguars of <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a> and <a href="/wiki/Luciano_Burti" title="Luciano Burti">Luciano Burti</a>. <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a>, Heidfeld and <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> rounded out the top ten fastest drivers in the session.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> In the second practice session, Coulthard set the fastest lap of the day, a 1:15.520; one second quicker than Trulli. Michael Schumacher followed with the third-fastest time. The two Williams drivers of Montoya and <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> were fourth and sixth; they were separated by Häkkinen.<sup id="cite_ref-galeforcef1fri_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-galeforcef1fri-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello was seventh-quickest and spun off when his engine lost power because of an oil pressure loss.<sup id="cite_ref-fiafriday_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiafriday-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Frentzen, Heidfeld and Irvine followed in the top ten.<sup id="cite_ref-galeforcef1fri_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-galeforcef1fri-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> It became hotter during the Saturday free practice sessions.<sup id="cite_ref-fiasaturday_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiasaturday-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Häkkinen set the pace in the third practice session, with a time of 1:14.503 with Coulthard second-quickest. Michael Schumacher and Barrichello were third- and fifth-fastest, separated by Ralf Schumacher. Montoya, Trulli, Frentzen, Heidfeld and <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> rounded out the top ten.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> In the final practice session, Montoya was quickest with a lap of 1:13.963; Ralf Schumacher finished with the fourth-fastest time. They were split by the two McLarens, Häkkinen in second and Coulthard in third. Michael Schumacher finished fifth-fastest, ahead of Frentzen. Barrichello, Räikkönen, Panis and Heidfeld completed the top ten ahead of qualifying.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Saturday's afternoon qualifying session lasted for an hour. Each driver was limited to twelve laps, with the grid order decided by the drivers' fastest laps. During this session, the <a href="/wiki/107%25_rule" title="107% rule">107% rule</a> was in effect, which necessitated each driver set a time within 107% of the quickest lap to qualify for the race.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The session was held in dry and sunny weather conditions. The air temperature ranged between 27–29&#160;°C (81–84&#160;°F) and the track temperature was between 33–39&#160;°C (91–102&#160;°F).<sup id="cite_ref-temp_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-temp-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> The hot conditions prevented the front-running teams from improving their lap times towards the end of the session.<sup id="cite_ref-fiasaturday_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiasaturday-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher clinched his seventh consecutive <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>, his third of the season and his first at the Interlagos circuit, with a time of 1:13.780.<sup id="cite_ref-atlasquali_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasquali-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> He was joined on the front row of the grid by Ralf Schumacher who set a qualifying time 0.310 seconds slower, equalling his team's best qualifying position since the <a href="/wiki/1998_Italian_Grand_Prix" title="1998 Italian Grand Prix">1998 Italian Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-penaltyralf_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-penaltyralf-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> The two became the first siblings to share the front row of the grid in a Formula One World Championship event.<sup id="cite_ref-fiasaturday_18-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiasaturday-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> After the session had ended, Ralf Schumacher's car was investigated by the stewards for an fuel irregularity and was allowed to race when the fuel was declared legal.<sup id="cite_ref-penaltyralf_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-penaltyralf-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Häkkinen and Coulthard set the third- and fifth-fastest times for McLaren, respectively, both drivers agreed that their cars had a reduced tendency to understeer.<sup id="cite_ref-fiasaturday_18-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiasaturday-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Montoya separated the McLaren drivers in the second Williams, who complained that his car was difficult to drive having spun on his first quick lap and switched to the spare Williams for the remainder of the session.<sup id="cite_ref-fiasaturday_18-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiasaturday-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello was four tenths of a second slower than Michael Schumacher in sixth place,<sup id="cite_ref-atlasquali_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasquali-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> and blamed excessive understeer which caused him to have problems with handling.<sup id="cite_ref-fiasaturday_18-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiasaturday-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> The two Jordans filled the fourth row of the grid with Trulli seventh and Frentzen eighth.<sup id="cite_ref-atlasquali_22-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasquali-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> This formation continued onto the fifth row which was occupied by Heidfeld and Räikkönen in the Saubers,<sup id="cite_ref-atlasquali_22-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasquali-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> with the former changing his set-up to move ahead of his teammate.<sup id="cite_ref-fiasaturday_18-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiasaturday-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> The sixth row was filled by the BARs of Panis and <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a> and both drivers complained they had a lack of grip in the slow-speed corners.<sup id="cite_ref-fiasaturday_18-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiasaturday-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Irvine and Burti recorded the 13th- and 14th-fastest times in their Jaguars, ahead of <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a> in the faster of the two Prosts.<sup id="cite_ref-atlasquali_22-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasquali-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Enrique_Bernoldi" title="Enrique Bernoldi">Enrique Bernoldi</a> was 16th-quickest for the Arrows team, ahead of teammate <a href="/wiki/Jos_Verstappen" title="Jos Verstappen">Jos Verstappen</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-atlasquali_22-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasquali-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> for the Benetton team secured the 18th and 20th positions, respectively,<sup id="cite_ref-atlasquali_22-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasquali-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> the pair endured poor balance and oil leaks in their engines.<sup id="cite_ref-fiasaturday_18-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiasaturday-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> They were sandwiched by Alonso in the faster Minardi car.<sup id="cite_ref-atlasquali_22-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasquali-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Gast%C3%B3n_Mazzacane" title="Gastón Mazzacane">Gastón Mazzacane</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tarso_Marques" title="Tarso Marques">Tarso Marques</a> completed the final two positions on the grid.<sup id="cite_ref-atlasquali_22-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasquali-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2001_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The conditions on the grid were dry before the race; the air temperature was 30&#160;°C (86&#160;°F) and the track temperature ranged from 36 and 40&#160;°C (97 and 104&#160;°F).<sup id="cite_ref-temp_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-temp-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-f1comrace_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrace-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> The drivers took to the track at 11:30 <a href="/wiki/Time_in_Brazil" title="Time in Brazil">BRT</a> (<a href="/wiki/Coordinated_Universal_Time" title="Coordinated Universal Time">UTC</a>-3) for a 30-minute warm-up session.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_14-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> It took place in dry and hot weather conditions.<sup id="cite_ref-warm-up_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-warm-up-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Both Ferrari cars were running quickly throughout the session; Michael Schumacher had the fastest time, a 1:15.971 and also drove his race car and Ferrari's <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_motorsport_terms#S" title="Glossary of motorsport terms">spare car</a> in the half-hour period.<sup id="cite_ref-warm-up_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-warm-up-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-atlaswarmup_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlaswarmup-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello finished with the second-quickest time. Häkkinen was third-quickest, and Ralf Schumacher rounded out the top four, with a lap four tenths of a second slower than Michael Schumacher.<sup id="cite_ref-atlaswarmup_26-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlaswarmup-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Alonso went into the gravel trap and his car rested in the tyre barriers which caused damage to his front wing.<sup id="cite_ref-warm-up_25-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-warm-up-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The race started at 14:00 local time. A significant overtaking maneuver took place very early in the race. The safety car was brought onto the track because <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> had stalled on the starting grid. When the safety car came in, F1 rookie <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a> overtook defending champion Michael Schumacher.
</p><p>Local hero Rubens Barrichello had problems before he even got on the grid and had to switch to the spare car.  He then slammed into the back of Ralf Schumacher on the restart, retiring on the spot. Ralf Schumacher limped back to the pits, but was in for a long time replacing the rear wing; he later spun off when the track was wet.
</p><p>As Juan Pablo Montoya extended his lead, the stewards informed the Jaguar team that Eddie Irvine would be served with a 10-second <a href="/wiki/List_of_motorsport_terminology#Q" class="mw-redirect" title="List of motorsport terminology">stop-go penalty</a>. Eddie Irvine took his penalty on Lap 6.
</p><p>By lap 39 Montoya was leading by more than 30 seconds over Michael Schumacher.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> However, he retired after a crash while he was lapping <a href="/wiki/Jos_Verstappen" title="Jos Verstappen">Jos Verstappen</a>. Verstappen let Montoya by, but as he pulled in behind Montoya while braking for turn 4 his <a href="/wiki/Arrows_Grand_Prix_International" title="Arrows Grand Prix International">Arrows</a> slammed violently into the back of Montoya's <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a> and both men were out of the race.
</p><p>Soon after Montoya's retirement it began to rain and thunderstorms heavily, forcing everyone to change tyres.  Some went onto full-wet tyres while others chose the intermediates.
</p><p>Michael Schumacher was fighting understeer all race, and despite planning to stop twice instead of once was only able to run at the pace of Montoya and Coulthard. However, he was right back in the hunt after he had pitted early. He still needed to pit again for fuel anyway but was able to change tyres as well. Coulthard, who had made his one and only stop, stayed out an extra lap which at that point looked costly.
</p><p>But then Schumacher, with a dry-weather set-up on his car, spun soon after exiting the pit lane, allowing Coulthard right back on his tail.
</p><p>This led to a second important overtaking move of the race, this one by <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a>. He passed Schumacher with the aid of a backmarker, rather similar to his teammate <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a>'s move on Schumacher at the <a href="/wiki/2000_Belgian_Grand_Prix" title="2000 Belgian Grand Prix">Belgian Grand Prix</a> the previous season.
</p><p>Coulthard went on to win, while Schumacher suffered another off on his way to second place. Nick Heidfeld took his first-ever podium driving for <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a>. It was also Sauber's first podium since Jean Alesi's at the <a href="/wiki/1998_Belgian_Grand_Prix" title="1998 Belgian Grand Prix">1998 Belgian Grand Prix</a>.
</p>
