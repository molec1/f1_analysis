<p>The <b>1989 Brazilian Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at <a href="/wiki/Aut%C3%B3dromo_Internacional_Nelson_Piquet" title="Autódromo Internacional Nelson Piquet">Jacarepaguá</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rio_de_Janeiro" title="Rio de Janeiro">Rio de Janeiro</a> on 26 March 1989. It was the first race of the <a href="/wiki/1989_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1989 Formula One World Championship">1989 Formula One World Championship</a>.
</p><p>The 61-lap race was won by Englishman <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_640" title="Ferrari 640">Ferrari</a>, with Frenchman <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> second in a <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/5" title="McLaren MP4/5">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Honda_in_Formula_One" title="Honda in Formula One">Honda</a> and local driver <a href="/wiki/Maur%C3%ADcio_Gugelmin" title="Maurício Gugelmin">Maurício Gugelmin</a> third in a <a href="/wiki/March_881" title="March 881">March</a>-<a href="/wiki/Judd_(engine)" title="Judd (engine)">Judd</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1989_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-qualifying_report">Pre-qualifying report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1989_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Pre-qualifying report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Several teams were required to participate in the Friday morning pre-qualifying sessions during 1989, in order to reduce the field to thirty cars for the main qualifying sessions on Friday afternoon and Saturday. At the midway point of the season, the pre-qualifying group was to be reassessed, with the more successful, points-scoring teams being allowed to avoid pre-qualifying, and unsuccessful teams being required to pre-qualify from mid-season onwards. 
</p><p>At this first Grand Prix of 1989 in Brazil, five cars were allowed to progress. The <a href="/wiki/Automobiles_Gonfaronnaises_Sportives" title="Automobiles Gonfaronnaises Sportives">AGS</a> team had expanded from one car to two, and their first car, to be driven by <a href="/wiki/Philippe_Streiff" title="Philippe Streiff">Philippe Streiff</a>, was not required to pre-qualify. However, the Frenchman had been paralysed in a midweek testing crash at the circuit, which ended his career. He was not replaced for the Grand Prix weekend, allowing an extra car to progress from the pre-qualifying session.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/FIRST_(constructor)" class="mw-redirect" title="FIRST (constructor)">FIRST</a> team withdrew before the event, as the car had failed a mandatory <a href="/wiki/FIA" class="mw-redirect" title="FIA">FIA</a> pre-season crash test. This left thirteen cars participating in the session. They included the two <a href="/wiki/Brabham" title="Brabham">Brabhams</a>, as the team had not participated in 1988, and the new <a href="/wiki/Onyx_Grand_Prix" title="Onyx Grand Prix">Onyx</a> team with their two-car entry. Also included were the two <a href="/wiki/Zakspeed" title="Zakspeed">Zakspeeds</a> and the two <a href="/wiki/Osella" title="Osella">Osellas</a>. This left five other cars: the sole single-car entry from <a href="/wiki/EuroBrun" title="EuroBrun">EuroBrun</a>, and the second cars of the four teams expanding from one car to two for 1989, namely AGS, <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Coloni" title="Scuderia Coloni">Coloni</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dallara" title="Dallara">Dallara</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rial_Racing" title="Rial Racing">Rial</a>.
</p><p>During the session, the two Brabhams of <a href="/wiki/Martin_Brundle" title="Martin Brundle">Martin Brundle</a> and <a href="/wiki/Stefano_Modena" title="Stefano Modena">Stefano Modena</a> were considerably faster than the other entrants, securing a comfortable 1–2. Third was the EuroBrun driven by debutant Swiss driver <a href="/wiki/Gregor_Foitek" title="Gregor Foitek">Gregor Foitek</a>, and fourth was the Osella of <a href="/wiki/Nicola_Larini" title="Nicola Larini">Nicola Larini</a>. The fortunate fifth fastest runner, who also went through to qualifying on this occasion, was Zakspeed's <a href="/wiki/Bernd_Schneider_(racing_driver)" title="Bernd Schneider (racing driver)">Bernd Schneider</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Walker_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Walker-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Missing out in sixth was <a href="/wiki/Alex_Caffi" title="Alex Caffi">Alex Caffi</a> in the Dallara, ahead of veteran <a href="/wiki/Piercarlo_Ghinzani" title="Piercarlo Ghinzani">Piercarlo Ghinzani</a> in the other Osella. Another newcomer, German driver <a href="/wiki/Volker_Weidler" title="Volker Weidler">Volker Weidler</a> was eighth in the Rial, with <a href="/wiki/Pierre-Henri_Raphanel" title="Pierre-Henri Raphanel">Pierre-Henri Raphanel</a>'s Coloni ninth, ahead of <a href="/wiki/Joachim_Winkelhock" title="Joachim Winkelhock">Joachim Winkelhock</a>, also competing in Formula One for the first time, in the AGS. Eleventh was the second Zakspeed of <a href="/wiki/Aguri_Suzuki" title="Aguri Suzuki">Aguri Suzuki</a>, with the Onyx drivers a little way adrift at the bottom of the time sheets, having had little time to test their new car. <a href="/wiki/Stefan_Johansson" title="Stefan Johansson">Stefan Johansson</a> was faster than his Belgian team-mate <a href="/wiki/Bertrand_Gachot" title="Bertrand Gachot">Bertrand Gachot</a>, the other driver in the session to make his Formula One debut, but was still over seven seconds slower than Brundle's time.<sup id="cite_ref-Walker_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Walker-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying_report">Qualifying report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1989_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Qualifying report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> took <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> in qualifying ahead of <a href="/wiki/Riccardo_Patrese" title="Riccardo Patrese">Riccardo Patrese</a>, making a record-breaking 177th appearance at a Grand Prix, and <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> in the new <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_640" title="Ferrari 640">Ferrari 640</a>, which featured the first semi-automatic gearbox in Formula One. For Patrese it was actually his first front row start since he started second at the <a href="/wiki/1983_European_Grand_Prix" title="1983 European Grand Prix">1983 European Grand Prix</a> at <a href="/wiki/Brands_Hatch" title="Brands Hatch">Brands Hatch</a>, a gap of 81 races. On his debut for Ferrari, Berger's teammate <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> qualified sixth. After the race Mansell joked that he was so convinced of his new car's unreliability that he had booked an early flight home.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Johnny_Herbert" title="Johnny Herbert">Johnny Herbert</a> (<a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Grouillard" title="Olivier Grouillard">Olivier Grouillard</a> (<a href="/wiki/Equipe_Ligier" title="Equipe Ligier">Ligier</a>) both qualified for their first Formula One races.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1989_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_report">Race report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1989_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Race report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the start, <a href="/wiki/Nicola_Larini" title="Nicola Larini">Nicola Larini</a> was disqualified for an illegal start. Mansell became the first man since <a href="/wiki/Mario_Andretti" title="Mario Andretti">Mario Andretti</a> in 1971 to win on his Formula One debut for Ferrari, a feat that was not matched until <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> won for Ferrari at the <a href="/wiki/2007_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="2007 Australian Grand Prix">2007 Australian Grand Prix</a>. It was also the first race ever to be won by a car with a semi-automatic gearbox. Mansell cut his hands on the trophy following the race.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He was joined on the podium by <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> and <a href="/wiki/March_Engineering" title="March Engineering">March's</a> <a href="/wiki/Maur%C3%ADcio_Gugelmin" title="Maurício Gugelmin">Maurício Gugelmin</a>, making his first appearance on the podium. Johnny Herbert, still recovering from his horrifying <a href="/wiki/Formula_3000" title="Formula 3000">Formula 3000</a> crash at <a href="/wiki/Brands_Hatch" title="Brands Hatch">Brands Hatch</a> six months earlier, finished 4th on his Formula 1 debut for Benetton, 1.123 seconds behind Gugelmin and 7.748 seconds in front of teammate <a href="/wiki/Alessandro_Nannini" title="Alessandro Nannini">Alessandro Nannini</a> who finished 6th.
</p><p>The hard luck of the story of the race was <a href="/wiki/Arrows_Grand_Prix_International" title="Arrows Grand Prix International">Arrows</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Derek_Warwick" title="Derek Warwick">Derek Warwick</a>. There was a problem fitting a rear wheel during his second stop for tyres which lost him over 25 seconds. He eventually finished in fifth place, less than 18 seconds behind Mansell suggesting that the pit stop may have cost Warwick his maiden Grand Prix victory.
</p><p>Warwick's Arrows teammate <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Cheever" title="Eddie Cheever">Eddie Cheever</a> collapsed after exiting his car following the collision involving the <a href="/wiki/Zakspeed_891" title="Zakspeed 891">Zakspeed</a> of <a href="/wiki/Bernd_Schneider_(racing_driver)" title="Bernd Schneider (racing driver)">Bernd Schneider</a> that ended his race. Arrows actually had to modify Cheever's car after he failed the <a href="/wiki/FIA" class="mw-redirect" title="FIA">FIA</a> safety check where a driver had five seconds to be able to exit their car. The new <a href="/wiki/Ross_Brawn" title="Ross Brawn">Ross Brawn</a> designed <a href="/wiki/Arrows_A11" title="Arrows A11">Arrows A11</a> was a tight fit for the tall American and he had trouble fitting into the car before practice. Schneider, whose car carried the new <a href="/wiki/Yamaha_Motor_Company" title="Yamaha Motor Company">Yamaha</a> <a href="/wiki/V8_engine" title="V8 engine">V8 engine</a>, only got into the race after Philippe Streiff's crash and the FIA had allowed five pre-qualifiers to enter the main field instead of four. Schneider did not qualify for another race until the season's penultimate round in <a href="/wiki/1989_Japanese_Grand_Prix" title="1989 Japanese Grand Prix">Japan</a> some seven months later. His new teammate <a href="/wiki/Aguri_Suzuki" title="Aguri Suzuki">Aguri Suzuki</a> ultimately failed to qualify for all 16 rounds of the 1989 season.
</p><p>This was the last Formula One race at Jacarepaguá. From 1990, the Brazilian Grand Prix would be held at a shortened <a href="/wiki/Aut%C3%B3dromo_Jos%C3%A9_Carlos_Pace" title="Autódromo José Carlos Pace">Interlagos</a> in <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo" title="São Paulo">São Paulo</a>, the home town of Ayrton Senna.
</p>
