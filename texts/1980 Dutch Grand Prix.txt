<p>The <b>1980 Dutch Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 31 August 1980 at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_Zandvoort" title="Circuit Zandvoort">Circuit Zandvoort</a> in the Netherlands. It was the eleventh race of the <a href="/wiki/1980_Formula_One_season" title="1980 Formula One season">1980 Formula One season</a>. The race was the 29th <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Grand_Prix" title="Dutch Grand Prix">Dutch Grand Prix</a>. The race was held over 72 laps of the 4.252-kilometre circuit for a total race distance of 306 kilometres.
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Start_of_1980_Dutch_Grand_Prix.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bd/Start_of_1980_Dutch_Grand_Prix.jpg/220px-Start_of_1980_Dutch_Grand_Prix.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="146" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bd/Start_of_1980_Dutch_Grand_Prix.jpg/330px-Start_of_1980_Dutch_Grand_Prix.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bd/Start_of_1980_Dutch_Grand_Prix.jpg/440px-Start_of_1980_Dutch_Grand_Prix.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3661" data-file-height="2436" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Start_of_1980_Dutch_Grand_Prix.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Drivers battling at the start behind the head of the race</div></div></div>
<p>The race was won by Brazilian driver, <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a> driving a <a href="/wiki/Brabham_BT49" title="Brabham BT49">Brabham BT49</a>. The win was Piquet's second Formula One Grand Prix victory having taken his first win earlier the same year at the <a href="/wiki/1980_United_States_Grand_Prix_West" title="1980 United States Grand Prix West">1980 United States Grand Prix West</a>. The win confirmed Piquet as being the major threat to <a href="/wiki/Alan_Jones_(racing_driver)" title="Alan Jones (racing driver)">Alan Jones</a>' charge to the world championship crown. Piquet won by twelve seconds over French driver <a href="/wiki/Ren%C3%A9_Arnoux" title="René Arnoux">René Arnoux</a> driving a <a href="/wiki/Renault_RE20" title="Renault RE20">Renault RE20</a>. Less than half a second behind in third was another French driver <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Laffite" title="Jacques Laffite">Jacques Laffite</a> (<a href="/wiki/Ligier_JS11/15" class="mw-redirect" title="Ligier JS11/15">Ligier JS11/15</a>).
</p><p>The circuit had been altered for the second time in as many years with the back straight chicane tightened significantly. There were several new combinations. <a href="/wiki/Alfa_Romeo_in_Formula_One" title="Alfa Romeo in Formula One">Alfa Romeo</a> entered a second car, a replacement after <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Depailler" title="Patrick Depailler">Patrick Depailler</a>'s death a month earlier, for Italian veteran <a href="/wiki/Vittorio_Brambilla" title="Vittorio Brambilla">Vittorio Brambilla</a>. <a href="/wiki/Geoff_Lees_(racing_driver)" title="Geoff Lees (racing driver)">Geoff Lees</a> was entered in a second <a href="/wiki/Ensign_N180" class="mw-redirect" title="Ensign N180">Ensign N180</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jochen_Mass" title="Jochen Mass">Jochen Mass</a> returned from injury in an <a href="/wiki/Arrows_A3" title="Arrows A3">Arrows A3</a>. Mass' return proved to be premature and he pulled out of the meeting. <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_Racing" title="Tyrrell Racing">Tyrrell Racing</a> test driver <a href="/wiki/Mike_Thackwell" title="Mike Thackwell">Mike Thackwell</a> stepped into the car but failed in his attempt to be the youngest ever Formula One race starter.
</p><p>Jones won the start from the second row of the grid to lead early until he pitted with damaged skirts. Laffite soon took the lead from Arnoux before both were picked-off by Piquet. Late in the race Arnoux regained second place. Behind Laffite, the second <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW07B" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams FW07B">Williams FW07B</a> of <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Reutemann" title="Carlos Reutemann">Carlos Reutemann</a> finished fourth ahead of <a href="/wiki/Jean-Pierre_Jarier" title="Jean-Pierre Jarier">Jean-Pierre Jarier</a> (driving his 100th Grand Prix) in the surviving <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_010" title="Tyrrell 010">Tyrrell 010</a> after <a href="/wiki/Derek_Daly" title="Derek Daly">Derek Daly</a> had crashed earlier after brake failure. Young rookie <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> claimed the final point debuting the new <a href="/wiki/McLaren_M30" title="McLaren M30">McLaren M30</a>, while in seventh <a href="/wiki/Gilles_Villeneuve" title="Gilles Villeneuve">Gilles Villeneuve</a> had given one of Ferrari's few 1980 highlights, running as high as third before his tyres went off.
</p><p>Jones was three laps down in eleventh, his championship now in real danger as Piquet had closed to within two points. Reutemann now led the battle for third by a point over Laffite and three over Arnoux. Williams now had one hand firmly on the constructors' trophy, leading Ligier by 25 points and Brabham by 35 with Piquet's teammates providing virtually no support to the constructor's tally.
</p>
