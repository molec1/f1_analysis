<p>The <b>1994 Monaco Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 15 May 1994 at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Monaco" title="Circuit de Monaco">Circuit de Monaco</a>, <a href="/wiki/Monte_Carlo" title="Monte Carlo">Monte Carlo</a>. It was the fourth race of the <a href="/wiki/1994_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1994 Formula One World Championship">1994 Formula One World Championship</a>, and the first following the deaths of <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> and <a href="/wiki/Roland_Ratzenberger" title="Roland Ratzenberger">Roland Ratzenberger</a> at the <a href="/wiki/1994_San_Marino_Grand_Prix" title="1994 San Marino Grand Prix">San Marino Grand Prix</a> two weeks previously.
</p><p>The 78-lap race was won from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> by <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> driving a <a href="/wiki/Benetton_B194" title="Benetton B194">Benetton</a>-<a href="/wiki/Ford_Motor_Company" title="Ford Motor Company">Ford</a>, his fourth victory from the first four races of 1994. <a href="/wiki/Martin_Brundle" title="Martin Brundle">Martin Brundle</a> finished second in a <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/9" title="McLaren MP4/9">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Peugeot" title="Peugeot">Peugeot</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> third in a <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_412_T1" title="Ferrari 412 T1">Ferrari</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1994_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1994_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After the deaths of <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> and <a href="/wiki/Roland_Ratzenberger" title="Roland Ratzenberger">Roland Ratzenberger</a> at the <a href="/wiki/1994_San_Marino_Grand_Prix" title="1994 San Marino Grand Prix">San Marino Grand Prix</a>, sweeping changes were announced by the FIA to the rules and regulations of Formula One in a bid to improve safety. The majority were scheduled to come into force after the Monaco Grand Prix, but an 80 km/h pit-lane speed limit was brought into force in time for this race.<sup id="cite_ref-wendlingertimes_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wendlingertimes-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Both Williams and <a href="/wiki/Simtek" title="Simtek">Simtek</a>, the teams for whom Senna and Ratzenberger drove, ran only one car each during the race weekend.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a> was serving the third race of his three-race ban issued to him for his part in the crash during the <a href="/wiki/1994_Brazilian_Grand_Prix" title="1994 Brazilian Grand Prix">Brazilian Grand Prix</a>. <a href="/wiki/Andrea_de_Cesaris" title="Andrea de Cesaris">Andrea de Cesaris</a> again took Irvine's place at Jordan, whilst Irvine acted as a pit-lane reporter for the ESPN television station.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Olivier_Beretta" title="Olivier Beretta">Olivier Beretta</a> became the first Monégasque to compete in the Monaco Grand Prix since <a href="/wiki/Andr%C3%A9_Testut" title="André Testut">André Testut</a> in <a href="/wiki/1959_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="1959 Monaco Grand Prix">1959</a>. This was the last time a driver from Monaco competed in his home race, until <a href="/wiki/Charles_Leclerc" title="Charles Leclerc">Charles Leclerc</a> took part in the <a href="/wiki/2018_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="2018 Monaco Grand Prix">2018 race</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice_and_qualifying">Practice and qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1994_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Practice and qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>During the first free practice session on Thursday morning, Austrian driver <a href="/wiki/Karl_Wendlinger" title="Karl Wendlinger">Karl Wendlinger</a> had a major accident at the Nouvelle Chicane. Travelling at almost 280&#160;km/h (170&#160;mph) he appeared to brake too late, and the car slid sideways into the water-filled barriers. Wendlinger was knocked unconscious and was taken initially to the Princess Grace Hospital, and later to Saint Roch Hospital in Nice. He suffered a serious head injury and remained in a <a href="/wiki/Coma" title="Coma">coma</a> for several weeks.<sup id="cite_ref-wendlingertimes_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wendlingertimes-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a>-Mercedes team decided to withdraw from the race after this incident.
</p><p>Michael Schumacher claimed the first pole position of his Grand Prix career. Mika Häkkinen qualified second, which was also the highest starting position thus far in his career.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1994_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Alboreto_at_Monaco_Grand_Prix_1994.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/59/Alboreto_at_Monaco_Grand_Prix_1994.jpg/220px-Alboreto_at_Monaco_Grand_Prix_1994.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="146" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/59/Alboreto_at_Monaco_Grand_Prix_1994.jpg/330px-Alboreto_at_Monaco_Grand_Prix_1994.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/59/Alboreto_at_Monaco_Grand_Prix_1994.jpg/440px-Alboreto_at_Monaco_Grand_Prix_1994.jpg 2x" data-file-width="655" data-file-height="434" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Alboreto_at_Monaco_Grand_Prix_1994.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Michele_Alboreto" title="Michele Alboreto">Michele Alboreto</a> finished sixth in his <a href="/wiki/Minardi_M193" title="Minardi M193">Minardi</a>, scoring his last point in Formula One.</div></div></div>
<p>As a mark of respect for Senna and Ratzenberger, the FIA decided to <a href="/wiki/Missing_man_formation#Motorsport_variant" title="Missing man formation">leave the first two grid positions empty</a> for the race and painted them with the colours of the Brazilian and Austrian flags.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> For the first time since the <a href="/wiki/1959_United_States_Grand_Prix" title="1959 United States Grand Prix">1959 United States Grand Prix</a>, there was no previous World Champion competing in the race and also no former Monaco Grand Prix winner. There were also only four previous race winners: Schumacher, Hill, Berger and Alboreto.
</p><p>At the start of the race, <a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a> crashed into the back of <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a>'s <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> just before the <i>St. Devote</i> corner. Häkkinen retired immediately, while Hill continued for a few corners before retiring with broken front suspension. <a href="/wiki/Gianni_Morbidelli" title="Gianni Morbidelli">Gianni Morbidelli</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pierluigi_Martini" title="Pierluigi Martini">Pierluigi Martini</a> also collided before <i>St. Devote</i>, putting each other out. <a href="/wiki/Eric_Bernard" class="mw-redirect" title="Eric Bernard">Eric Bernard</a> in the second <a href="/wiki/Equipe_Ligier" title="Equipe Ligier">Ligier</a> had soon spun out of the race at the Nouvelle chicane. 
</p><p>Katayama retired his Tyrrell from 6th position on lap 39 when his gearbox failed, as of which by lap 41, the engine on <a href="/wiki/Mark_Blundell" title="Mark Blundell">Mark Blundell</a>'s Tyrrell failed, leaving oil on the track at <i>St. Devote</i> retiring 2 laps after Katayama which Schumacher, leading the race, had to avoid. The second placed <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> of <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> did slip on the oil, however, and required a three-point turn to escape from the run off area beside the stricken Tyrrell. Berger returned to the track still in his second place, but dirty tyres left him vulnerable to the <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> of <a href="/wiki/Martin_Brundle" title="Martin Brundle">Martin Brundle</a>, who promptly overtook him down the outside of <i>Mirabeau</i> on the same lap. <a href="/wiki/Christian_Fittipaldi" title="Christian Fittipaldi">Christian Fittipaldi</a> in his <a href="/wiki/Footwork_Arrows" title="Footwork Arrows">Footwork</a>-<a href="/wiki/Ford_Motor_Company" title="Ford Motor Company">Ford</a> ran close behind the Ferraris of Berger and Alesi in fourth position until his first refuelling stop near the start of the race. Fittipaldi continued to run strongly in a points position until lap 47 when the gearbox failed.
</p><p>The race was led from start to finish by Schumacher, who continued his perfect start to the 1994 season with four victories in the first four races. As Schumacher also held the fastest lap, this meant he scored the first <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_driver_records#Grand_Slam" title="List of Formula One driver records">Grand Slam</a> of his career, and he was the first driver other than <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> or Ayrton Senna to win the Monaco Grand Prix since <a href="/wiki/1983_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="1983 Monaco Grand Prix">1983</a>. Brundle's second place equalled the best finish of his F1 career. <a href="/wiki/Michele_Alboreto" title="Michele Alboreto">Michele Alboreto</a> finished sixth in his Minardi to score his final point in F1.
</p>
