<p>The <b>1953 Argentine Grand Prix</b> was race 1 of 9 in the <a href="/wiki/1953_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1953 World Championship of Drivers">1953 World Championship of Drivers</a>, which was run to <a href="/wiki/Formula_Two" title="Formula Two">Formula Two</a> regulations in 1952 and 1953. The race was held in Buenos Aires on January 18, 1953, at the Autódromo Galvez (official name: <a href="/wiki/Aut%C3%B3dromo_Juan_y_%C3%93scar_G%C3%A1lvez" class="mw-redirect" title="Autódromo Juan y Óscar Gálvez">Autódromo Juan y Óscar Gálvez</a>, also known as the Autódromo 17 de Octubre) as the first official Formula One race in South America and outside of Europe. Previously, the Indianapolis 500 (part of the Formula One championship calendar from 1950 to 1960) was the only Formula One championship race held outside Europe but run to <a href="/wiki/American_Automobile_Association" title="American Automobile Association">AAA</a> regulations.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_report">Race report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1953_Argentine_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Race report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Fangio_froilan_1953.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2b/Fangio_froilan_1953.jpg/250px-Fangio_froilan_1953.jpg" decoding="async" width="250" height="217" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2b/Fangio_froilan_1953.jpg/375px-Fangio_froilan_1953.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2b/Fangio_froilan_1953.jpg/500px-Fangio_froilan_1953.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1357" data-file-height="1179" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Fangio_froilan_1953.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Local drivers <a href="/wiki/Juan_Manuel_Fangio" title="Juan Manuel Fangio">Juan Manuel Fangio</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Froil%C3%A1n_Gonz%C3%A1lez" title="José Froilán González">José Froilán González</a> during a test prior to the race</div></div></div>
<p>The inaugural Argentine Grand Prix, held in mid-January, was attended by four of the major works teams: <a href="/wiki/Maserati_in_motorsport" title="Maserati in motorsport">Maserati</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cooper_Car_Company" title="Cooper Car Company">Cooper</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gordini" title="Gordini">Gordini</a>. Former World Champion <a href="/wiki/Juan_Manuel_Fangio" title="Juan Manuel Fangio">Juan Manuel Fangio</a>, who had not competed in the Championship since clinching the <a href="/wiki/1951_Formula_One_season" title="1951 Formula One season">1951</a> title in <a href="/wiki/1951_Spanish_Grand_Prix" title="1951 Spanish Grand Prix">Spain</a>, raced for Maserati alongside fellow Argentinians <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Froil%C3%A1n_Gonz%C3%A1lez" title="José Froilán González">José Froilán González</a> and <a href="/wiki/Oscar_Alfredo_G%C3%A1lvez" title="Oscar Alfredo Gálvez">Oscar Alfredo Gálvez</a>, and Italian driver <a href="/wiki/Felice_Bonetto" title="Felice Bonetto">Felice Bonetto</a>. Ferrari lined up with the familiar trio of reigning World Champion <a href="/wiki/Alberto_Ascari" title="Alberto Ascari">Alberto Ascari</a>, <a href="/wiki/Giuseppe_Farina" title="Giuseppe Farina">Nino Farina</a> and <a href="/wiki/Luigi_Villoresi" title="Luigi Villoresi">Luigi Villoresi</a>, as well as their new signing <a href="/wiki/Mike_Hawthorn" title="Mike Hawthorn">Mike Hawthorn</a>, who had driven a privateer Cooper the previous year. The Cooper team entered the British pair of <a href="/wiki/Alan_Brown_(racing_driver)" title="Alan Brown (racing driver)">Alan Brown</a> and <a href="/wiki/John_Barber_(racing_driver)" title="John Barber (racing driver)">John Barber</a> alongside the local driver <a href="/wiki/Adolfo_Schwelm_Cruz" title="Adolfo Schwelm Cruz">Adolfo Schwelm Cruz</a>. Gordini retained their <a href="/wiki/1952_Formula_One_season" title="1952 Formula One season">1952</a> trio of <a href="/wiki/Robert_Manzon" title="Robert Manzon">Manzon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maurice_Trintignant" title="Maurice Trintignant">Trintignant</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jean_Behra" title="Jean Behra">Behra</a>, who were joined by a pair of Argentinians—<a href="/wiki/Carlos_Menditeguy" title="Carlos Menditeguy">Carlos Menditeguy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pablo_Birger" title="Pablo Birger">Pablo Birger</a>—the latter of which drove a <a href="/wiki/Simca-Gordini" class="mw-redirect" title="Simca-Gordini">Simca-Gordini</a>.
</p><p>Ascari was once again the fastest qualifier, taking his fourth consecutive World Championship pole position. His teammates Villoresi and Farini lined up third and fourth, but the returning Fangio prevented a Ferrari front row lockout by qualifying second in his Maserati. González, in the second Maserati, started from row two alongside Hawthorn, making his first appearance for Ferrari, and the Gordini of Trintignant. The remaining Gordinis of Manzon, Menditeguy and Behra made up the third row with Gálvez in his Maserati. Row four consisted of the Coopers of Brown and Schwelm Cruz, and Birger in the sole Simca-Gordini. At the back of the grid were the Maserati of Bonetto and Barber in the final Cooper.
</p><p>Due to <a href="/wiki/President_of_Argentina" title="President of Argentina">President</a> <a href="/wiki/Juan_Per%C3%B3n" title="Juan Perón">Juan Perón</a>'s decision to allow free access to the circuit, there were an excessive number of spectators and they lined the track as the race began. One of the spectators wandered onto the track, and, in order to avoid hitting him, Nino Farina was forced to swerve. Farina ultimately lost control of his car and crashed into the crowd on lap 31, killing 13 spectators. Ascari, who started from pole, led the entirety of the race, taking his seventh consecutive World Championship race victory, and, in so doing, established an early lead in the Drivers' Championship. Fangio was in second until a transmission issue forced him to retire from the race. Manzon initially inherited the position, but Villoresi ultimately took second place, a lap behind his teammate. Hawthorn had been running in third, although he was eventually overtaken by González, preventing a Ferrari 1-2-3. Hawthorn finished fourth, ahead of Gálvez, who took the final points in his first and only World Championship race.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
