<p>The <b>1990 Australian Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 4 November 1990 at <a href="/wiki/Adelaide_Street_Circuit" title="Adelaide Street Circuit">Adelaide</a>. It was the sixteenth and final race of the <a href="/wiki/1990_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1990 Formula One World Championship">1990 Formula One World Championship</a>. This was the 500th race to contribute to the World Drivers' Championship since the series had started in <a href="/wiki/1950_Formula_One_season" title="1950 Formula One season">1950</a>. The race was the 55th <a href="/wiki/Australian_Grand_Prix" title="Australian Grand Prix">Australian Grand Prix</a> and the sixth to be held on the Adelaide street circuit, which also made it the sixth AGP to be part of the Formula One World Championship. The race was held over 81 laps of the 3.78&#160;km circuit for a race distance of 306 kilometres.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1990_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying_report">Qualifying report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1990_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Qualifying report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Ayrton Senna claimed his 52nd pole position with a 1:15.671, the fastest time recorded on the Adelaide street circuit to date. Second on the grid was his <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Honda_Racing_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Honda Racing F1">Honda</a> teammate <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> who was over half a second slower. Filling out the second row were the <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_641" title="Ferrari 641">Ferraris</a> of <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a>, in his last race for the <a href="/wiki/Maranello" title="Maranello">Maranello</a> based team, and Prost, who was only 0.013 slower than Mansell. Despite brake problems with his <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_019" title="Tyrrell 019">Tyrrell</a>-<a href="/wiki/Cosworth_DFR" class="mw-redirect" title="Cosworth DFR">Ford</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a> qualified 5th in front of the <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW13" title="Williams FW13">Williams</a>-<a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a> of <a href="/wiki/Riccardo_Patrese" title="Riccardo Patrese">Riccardo Patrese</a>. During the final qualifying session it was reported by <a href="/wiki/1980_Formula_One_season" title="1980 Formula One season">1980</a> World Champion <a href="/wiki/Alan_Jones_(racing_driver)" title="Alan Jones (racing driver)">Alan Jones</a> who was acting as pit reporter for race television broadcaster <a href="/wiki/Nine_Network" title="Nine Network">Channel 9</a> that <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_Racing" title="Tyrrell Racing">Tyrrell</a> team owner <a href="/wiki/Ken_Tyrrell" title="Ken Tyrrell">Ken Tyrrell</a> believed Alesi's brake trouble to be more driver related rather than any fault with the car.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/1990_Japanese_Grand_Prix" title="1990 Japanese Grand Prix">Japanese Grand Prix</a> winner <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a> qualified his <a href="/wiki/Benetton_B190" title="Benetton B190">Benetton</a>-<a href="/wiki/Cosworth#Other_Formula_One_engines" title="Cosworth">Ford</a> in 7th with teammate <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Moreno" title="Roberto Moreno">Roberto Moreno</a> 8th and <a href="/wiki/1989_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="1989 Australian Grand Prix">1989</a> race winner <a href="/wiki/Thierry_Boutsen" title="Thierry Boutsen">Thierry Boutsen</a> 9th in his Williams. Rounding out the top 10 qualifiers was <a href="/wiki/Pierluigi_Martini" title="Pierluigi Martini">Pierluigi Martini</a> in his <a href="/wiki/Pirelli" title="Pirelli">Pirelli</a> shod <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a>-Ford.
</p><p>Australian <a href="/wiki/David_Brabham" title="David Brabham">David Brabham</a>, driving for <a href="/wiki/Brabham" title="Brabham">Brabham</a>, the team his triple World Championship winning father <a href="/wiki/Jack_Brabham" title="Jack Brabham">Jack</a> had started in <a href="/wiki/1962_Formula_One_season" title="1962 Formula One season">1962</a>, qualified for his first Australian Grand Prix in 25th position. Jack Brabham had won the non-championship Australian Grands Prix in <a href="/wiki/1955_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="1955 Australian Grand Prix">1955</a> (the first rear-engined car to do so), <a href="/wiki/1963_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="1963 Australian Grand Prix">1963</a> and <a href="/wiki/1964_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="1964 Australian Grand Prix">1964</a>. David Brabham would be the first Australian to drive in his home Grand Prix since Alan Jones had his last F1 race in <a href="/wiki/1986_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="1986 Australian Grand Prix">1986</a>.
</p><p>Italians <a href="/wiki/Michele_Alboreto" title="Michele Alboreto">Michele Alboreto</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alex_Caffi" title="Alex Caffi">Alex Caffi</a> (both <a href="/wiki/Arrows_Grand_Prix_International" title="Arrows Grand Prix International">Arrows</a>), Frenchman <a href="/wiki/Yannick_Dalmas" title="Yannick Dalmas">Yannick Dalmas</a> (<a href="/wiki/Automobiles_Gonfaronnaises_Sportives" title="Automobiles Gonfaronnaises Sportives">AGS</a>) and Belgian <a href="/wiki/Bertrand_Gachot" title="Bertrand Gachot">Bertrand Gachot</a> (<a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Coloni" title="Scuderia Coloni">Coloni</a>) all failed to qualify.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1990_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-race">Pre-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1990_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Pre-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As part of a television interview with Ayrton Senna after qualifying, former triple World Champion <a href="/wiki/Jackie_Stewart" title="Jackie Stewart">Jackie Stewart</a> caused a stir when he claimed that Senna was part of too many race accidents for a driver of his ability compared to all the World Champions of the past. A visibly annoyed Senna stated he could not believe that someone of Stewart's racing experience, someone who knew first hand that accidents are a part of motor racing, would say that he was a dangerous driver, and challenged the Scotsman to go back and check his facts.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On race morning during the drivers briefing, McLaren boss <a href="/wiki/Ron_Dennis" title="Ron Dennis">Ron Dennis</a> asked the officials for a ruling on what would happen if anyone decided to effectively straight-line the chicane after the start, asking if it was legal. <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_du_Sport_Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale du Sport Automobile">FISA</a> officials replied that any driver who did that would most likely damage their car so it was not advisable. At that point Alain Prost got up and walked out of the meeting, an action that earned him a 'yellow card' from FISA, but no fine. This puzzled many as walking out of the drivers briefing without good reason usually saw a driver fined.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Then, as per normal practice, the annual end-of-season drivers photo shoot took place prior to the race. As the race was the 500th World Championship Grand Prix there was also a photo shoot taken with several World Champions who were in attendance, including legendary five time Champion <a href="/wiki/Juan_Manuel_Fangio" title="Juan Manuel Fangio">Juan Manuel Fangio</a>. Alain Prost, still angry over the events at <a href="/wiki/Suzuka_Circuit" class="mw-redirect" title="Suzuka Circuit">Suzuka</a>, did not appear in either photo by his own choice as he did not want to appear with Ayrton Senna. This, and his public statements about his former McLaren teammate following the Japanese Grand Prix where he claimed Senna deliberately took him out at the first corner in the race to win the World Championship, lead to criticism of his mental state of mind by some including former World Champion turned <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> commentator <a href="/wiki/James_Hunt" title="James Hunt">James Hunt</a>, who at the time believed the crash at Suzuka was just an accident and that Senna had not taken Prost out on purpose (Senna revealed at Suzuka <a href="/wiki/1991_Japanese_Grand_Prix" title="1991 Japanese Grand Prix">a year later</a> that he did indeed deliberately take Prost out as payback for their crash at Suzuka <a href="/wiki/1989_Japanese_Grand_Prix" title="1989 Japanese Grand Prix">1989</a> that gave Prost the world title over Senna). Hunt, who as the <a href="/wiki/1976_Formula_One_season" title="1976 Formula One season">1976</a> Champion was in the photo shoot along with Fangio, <a href="/wiki/Sir_Jack_Brabham" class="mw-redirect" title="Sir Jack Brabham">Sir Jack Brabham</a>, <a href="/wiki/Denny_Hulme" title="Denny Hulme">Denny Hulme</a>, Stewart, and current drivers Senna and Nelson Piquet, claimed during the race commentary that Prost had been mentally beaten down by Senna after losing the championship to him in controversial circumstances and seemed to be "a driver who was under a form of mental collapse".<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Other than Prost who chose not to take part, World Championship winning drivers who were still alive but not in the photo shoot due to not being in attendance in Adelaide were <a href="/wiki/Phil_Hill" title="Phil Hill">Phil Hill</a> (<a href="/wiki/1961_Formula_One_season" title="1961 Formula One season">1961</a>), <a href="/wiki/John_Surtees" title="John Surtees">John Surtees</a> (<a href="/wiki/1964_Formula_One_season" title="1964 Formula One season">1964</a>), <a href="/wiki/Emerson_Fittipaldi" title="Emerson Fittipaldi">Emerson Fittipaldi</a> (<a href="/wiki/1972_Formula_One_season" title="1972 Formula One season">1972</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/1974_Formula_One_season" title="1974 Formula One season">1974</a>), <a href="/wiki/Niki_Lauda" title="Niki Lauda">Niki Lauda</a> (<a href="/wiki/1975_Formula_One_season" title="1975 Formula One season">1975</a>, <a href="/wiki/1977_Formula_One_season" title="1977 Formula One season">1977</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/1984_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1984 Formula One World Championship">1984</a>), <a href="/wiki/Mario_Andretti" title="Mario Andretti">Mario Andretti</a> (<a href="/wiki/1978_Formula_One_season" title="1978 Formula One season">1978</a>), <a href="/wiki/Jody_Scheckter" title="Jody Scheckter">Jody Scheckter</a> (<a href="/wiki/1979_Formula_One_season" title="1979 Formula One season">1979</a>), and the winner of the <a href="/wiki/1985_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="1985 Australian Grand Prix">inaugural race</a> in Adelaide <a href="/wiki/Keke_Rosberg" title="Keke Rosberg">Keke Rosberg</a> (<a href="/wiki/1982_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1982 Formula One World Championship">1982</a>). The most surprising absentee was Australia's 1980 World Champion Alan Jones who was actually at the circuit that weekend racing a <a href="/wiki/Ford_Sierra_RS500" class="mw-redirect" title="Ford Sierra RS500">Ford Sierra RS500</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Group_A" title="Group A">Group A</a> <a href="/wiki/Touring_car_racing" title="Touring car racing">touring car</a> support races as well as performing duties as a pit reporter for Australian host television broadcaster <a href="/wiki/Nine_Network" title="Nine Network">Channel 9</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_report">Race report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1990_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Race report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The winner for the second race in a row was Brazilian veteran Nelson Piquet in his Benetton, giving the triple World Champion back to back wins for the first time since he won the <a href="/wiki/1987_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1987 Formula One World Championship">1987</a> <a href="/wiki/1987_German_Grand_Prix" title="1987 German Grand Prix">German</a> and <a href="/wiki/1987_Hungarian_Grand_Prix" title="1987 Hungarian Grand Prix">Hungarian</a> Grands Prix while driving for <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a>-Honda. He took a 3.129 second victory over Nigel Mansell after the Englishman had tried a passing move under braking for the hairpin at the end of the Brabham Straight which almost took out both cars, Mansell somehow managed to pull his Ferrari up in time to just miss Piquet and the lapped Brabham-<a href="/wiki/Judd_(engine)#EV" title="Judd (engine)">Judd</a> of <a href="/wiki/Stefano_Modena" title="Stefano Modena">Stefano Modena</a> as they turned into the right hand hairpin.
</p><p>After making the best start, but being blocked in by the slow starting Berger, Alain Prost drove a steady, but for him unremarkable race other than a rare mistake when he ran wide at Brewery Bend late in the race while trying to stay ahead of the charging Mansell. Prost ran in 5th for most of the race, but eventually finished 3rd after Senna crashed out of the lead on lap 61 with gearbox trouble, and after Berger had also run wide at Brewery Bend and had to back off to conserve his tyres. Berger finished 4th, 9.6 seconds behind Prost. Rounding out the top six were the Williamses of <a href="/wiki/1989_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="1989 Australian Grand Prix">1989 Australian Grand Prix</a> winner <a href="/wiki/Thierry_Boutsen" title="Thierry Boutsen">Thierry Boutsen</a>, the last driver on the lead lap and in his last race for Williams, and <a href="/wiki/Riccardo_Patrese" title="Riccardo Patrese">Riccardo Patrese</a>.
</p><p>On lap 50, Senna broke Gerhard Berger's <a href="/wiki/1987_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="1987 Australian Grand Prix">1987</a> lap record of 1:20.416 (set in the turbocharged <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_F1/87" title="Ferrari F1/87">Ferrari F1/87</a>) with a time of 1:19.302. In his late race pursuit of Piquet, Mansell continued to lower the lap record and eventually lowered it to 1:18.203 on lap 75. In his efforts to stay ahead of Mansell, Piquet set his fastest race lap on lap 79 with a time of 1:18.527, and then emulated Prost and Berger's earlier feats by running wide at Brewery Bend on lap 80 which allowed Mansell to close within two seconds. Piquet's fastest lap, set on the tyres which he had started the race on, was also the second fastest lap of the race. In the post race interview with the top three finishers, Piquet said with a smile that after his lap 80 off which allowed Mansell to close up to him that he had to "drive like hell" over the last lap and a half, and that the "shit almost hit the fan", referring to Mansell's last-ditch overtaking move which almost took both cars out. After slower times in the <a href="/wiki/1988_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="1988 Australian Grand Prix">1988 race</a>, and no chance to beat it in the wet in <a href="/wiki/1989_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="1989 Australian Grand Prix">1989</a>, some 9 drivers (Mansell, Piquet, Prost, Senna, Moreno, Boutsen, Alesi, Patrese and Berger himself) would go faster than Berger's 1987 record with only Berger of that group failing to go under 1:20.000.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The victory allowed Piquet to claim third spot in the drivers championship in a countback after equalling the 43 points of Gerhard Berger (Piquet scored 2 wins compared to Berger who failed to win a race). It also allowed the <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton team</a> to secure third in the Constructors' Championship over <a href="/wiki/Williams_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams F1">Williams</a>, equaling its best finish from <a href="/wiki/1988_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1988 Formula One World Championship">1988</a>.
</p><p>The race was Riccardo Patrese's 208th Grand Prix start in what was the 500th World Championship GP held. This meant that the Italian had driven in 41.6% of all Grands Prix held since the World Championship started in <a href="/wiki/1950_Formula_One_season" title="1950 Formula One season">1950</a>. Patrese had started his F1 career with the <a href="/wiki/Shadow_Racing_Cars" title="Shadow Racing Cars">Shadow team</a> at the <a href="/wiki/1977_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="1977 Monaco Grand Prix">1977 Monaco Grand Prix</a>.
</p>
