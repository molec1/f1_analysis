<p>The <b>1990 British Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at <a href="/wiki/Silverstone_Circuit" title="Silverstone Circuit">Silverstone</a> on 15 July 1990. It was the eighth race of the <a href="/wiki/1990_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1990 Formula One World Championship">1990 Formula One World Championship</a>. It was the 45th <a href="/wiki/British_Grand_Prix" title="British Grand Prix">British Grand Prix</a> and the 26th to be held at Silverstone. The race was held over 64 laps of the four kilometre circuit for a race distance of 306 kilometres.
</p><p>French reigning World Champion <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> won his third race in succession in his <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_641" title="Ferrari 641">Ferrari 641</a> taking a 40-second win over Belgian driver <a href="/wiki/Thierry_Boutsen" title="Thierry Boutsen">Thierry Boutsen</a> driving a <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW13B" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams FW13B">Williams FW13B</a>. It was Boutsen's best result for the year to date. Brazilian driver <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> was third driving a <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/5B" class="mw-redirect" title="McLaren MP4/5B">McLaren MP4/5B</a>.
</p><p>Prost's win put him into the lead of the championship for the first time in 1990, giving him a two-point lead.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1990_British_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-qualifying_report">Pre-qualifying report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1990_British_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Pre-qualifying report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In Friday morning pre-qualifying, the <a href="/wiki/Larrousse" title="Larrousse">Larrousse</a>-<a href="/wiki/Lola_Cars" title="Lola Cars">Lolas</a> were again first and second, their fifth 1–2 of the season, with <a href="/wiki/%C3%89ric_Bernard" title="Éric Bernard">Éric Bernard</a> nearly a second faster than team-mate <a href="/wiki/Aguri_Suzuki" title="Aguri Suzuki">Aguri Suzuki</a>. As at the previous Grand Prix in <a href="/wiki/1990_French_Grand_Prix" title="1990 French Grand Prix">France</a>, third fastest was <a href="/wiki/Gabriele_Tarquini" title="Gabriele Tarquini">Gabriele Tarquini</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Automobiles_Gonfaronnaises_Sportives" title="Automobiles Gonfaronnaises Sportives">AGS</a>. This time, the fourth pre-qualifying spot went to <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Grouillard" title="Olivier Grouillard">Olivier Grouillard</a> in the sole <a href="/wiki/Osella" title="Osella">Osella</a>.
</p><p>These four were quite comfortably faster than the other runners, the fastest of which was <a href="/wiki/Robert_Moreno" title="Robert Moreno">Robert Moreno</a> in a revised <a href="/wiki/EuroBrun" title="EuroBrun">EuroBrun</a> in fifth place. Sixth was <a href="/wiki/Yannick_Dalmas" title="Yannick Dalmas">Yannick Dalmas</a> in the other AGS, his sixth failure to pre-qualify so far this season. <a href="/wiki/Claudio_Langes" title="Claudio Langes">Claudio Langes</a> was seventh in the other, unrevised EuroBrun, with <a href="/wiki/Bertrand_Gachot" title="Bertrand Gachot">Bertrand Gachot</a> a distant eighth in the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Coloni" title="Scuderia Coloni">Coloni</a> after its engine destroyed itself yet again.<sup id="cite_ref-MN18/7_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MN18/7-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Subaru" title="Subaru">Subaru</a> ended their involvement with the Coloni team after this Grand Prix, with eight consecutive failures to pre-qualify, and the team were to source new engines for the next race in <a href="/wiki/1990_German_Grand_Prix" title="1990 German Grand Prix">Germany</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Walker_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Walker-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Life_Racing_Engines" title="Life Racing Engines">Life</a> team had no pit garage in which to prepare their car, and worked on the grass near the pits.<sup id="cite_ref-MN18/7_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MN18/7-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Their car, still driven by <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Giacomelli" title="Bruno Giacomelli">Bruno Giacomelli</a>, suffered an electrical failure after five laps, and was bottom of the time sheets again. Team manager Sergio Barbasio announced that they would stick with the hopeless in-house <a href="/wiki/W12_engine" title="W12 engine">W12 engine</a>, citing a lack of time to prepare the chassis for the <a href="/wiki/Judd_(engine)" title="Judd (engine)">Judd</a> CV engines purchased from <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Lotus</a>. However, Italian sources claimed that Life had simply been unable to complete the purchase due to lack of funds.<sup id="cite_ref-MN18/7_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MN18/7-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1990_British_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_report">Race report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1990_British_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Race report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Local hero <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> led until his gearbox began to malfunction. He was overtaken (against team orders, and to Mansell's chagrin) by <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> and remained in second until his gearbox failed completely on lap 57. After retiring from the race Mansell famously threw his gloves into the crowd and announced he would retire from Formula One at the end of the season, a decision he later reversed.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Riccardo_Patrese" title="Riccardo Patrese">Riccardo Patrese</a> became the first driver ever to start 200 Grands Prix. On race day, he retired after damage was sustained in a collision with the <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a> of <a href="/wiki/Alessandro_Nannini" title="Alessandro Nannini">Alessandro Nannini</a> on lap 27, whilst his team-mate <a href="/wiki/Thierry_Boutsen" title="Thierry Boutsen">Thierry Boutsen</a> reached the podium and finished second.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/%C3%89ric_Bernard" title="Éric Bernard">Éric Bernard</a> and <a href="/wiki/Aguri_Suzuki" title="Aguri Suzuki">Aguri Suzuki</a> both scored the best results of their career up to this point. For Suzuki, it was the first points scoring finish of his career.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Ivan_Capelli" title="Ivan Capelli">Ivan Capelli</a> was the charger in the race. Starting 10th he spun early to avoid the collision between Patrese and <a href="/wiki/Alessandro_Nannini" title="Alessandro Nannini">Alessandro Nannini</a>. Then racing with a broken exhaust header he charged hard, eventually passing <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> for 3rd and for a time being the fastest driver on the track before retiring on lap 48 with a fuel leak.
</p><p>Ligier needed at least a top eight finish to avoid pre-qualification, but <a href="/wiki/Nicola_Larini" title="Nicola Larini">Nicola Larini</a> could not do better than 10th place, while teammate Philippe Alliot only managed to finish 13th.
</p><p>This would be the last motor race on the original high-speed Silverstone circuit; the day after the race, a construction crew funded by <a href="/wiki/Tom_Walkinshaw" title="Tom Walkinshaw">Tom Walkinshaw</a> immediately began work on reprofiling and incorporating the newly designed corners.
</p>
