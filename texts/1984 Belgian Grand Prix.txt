<p>The <b>1984 Belgian Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 29 April 1984. It was race 3 of 16 in the <a href="/wiki/1984_FIA_Formula_One_World_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="1984 FIA Formula One World Championship">1984 FIA Formula One World Championship</a>. It was the 42nd <a href="/wiki/Belgian_Grand_Prix" title="Belgian Grand Prix">Belgian Grand Prix</a>, and the tenth and last to be held at <a href="/wiki/Circuit_Zolder" title="Circuit Zolder">Circuit Zolder</a>. The race was held over 70 laps of the 4.26-kilometre (2.65&#160;mi) circuit for a race distance of 298.3 kilometres (185.4&#160;mi).
</p><p>The race was won by Italian driver <a href="/wiki/Michele_Alboreto" title="Michele Alboreto">Michele Alboreto</a> driving a <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_126C4" class="mw-redirect" title="Ferrari 126C4">Ferrari 126C4</a>. It was Alboreto's third Grand Prix victory and his first since joining <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Scuderia Ferrari</a> for the 1984 season and became the first <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italian</a> to win for the Prancing Horse since <a href="/wiki/Ludovico_Scarfiotti" title="Ludovico Scarfiotti">Ludovico Scarfiotti</a> won the <a href="/wiki/1966_Italian_Grand_Prix" title="1966 Italian Grand Prix">1966 Italian Grand Prix</a>. Alboreto took a 42-second victory over British driver <a href="/wiki/Derek_Warwick" title="Derek Warwick">Derek Warwick</a> driving a <a href="/wiki/Renault_RE50" title="Renault RE50">Renault RE50</a>. It was Warwick's best ever Grand Prix result, improving on the third place he had achieved at the previous race in <a href="/wiki/1984_South_African_Grand_Prix" title="1984 South African Grand Prix">South Africa</a>. Alboreto's French teammate <a href="/wiki/Ren%C3%A9_Arnoux" title="René Arnoux">René Arnoux</a> was third.
</p><p>With <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> failing to finish, his lead in the world championship tightened to five points with Warwick moving into second place ahead of <a href="/wiki/Niki_Lauda" title="Niki Lauda">Niki Lauda</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Summary">Summary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1984_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Summary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Goodyear_Tire_and_Rubber_Company" title="Goodyear Tire and Rubber Company">Goodyear</a> dominated qualifying filling six of the top seven places with <a href="/wiki/Michele_Alboreto" title="Michele Alboreto">Michele Alboreto</a> securing his first career pole position. Teammate <a href="/wiki/Ren%C3%A9_Arnoux" title="René Arnoux">René Arnoux</a> was second, followed by <a href="/wiki/Keke_Rosberg" title="Keke Rosberg">Keke Rosberg</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW09" title="Williams FW09">Williams FW09</a>-<a href="/wiki/Honda_in_Formula_One" title="Honda in Formula One">Honda</a>. <a href="/wiki/Derek_Warwick" title="Derek Warwick">Derek Warwick</a>, the first <a href="/wiki/Michelin" title="Michelin">Michelin</a> runner, was fourth ahead of <a href="/wiki/Elio_de_Angelis" title="Elio de Angelis">Elio de Angelis</a> (<a href="/wiki/Lotus_95T" title="Lotus 95T">Lotus 95T</a>-Renault) and the surprising fast <a href="/wiki/ATS_D7" title="ATS D7">ATS D7</a>-<a href="/wiki/BMW_M12" title="BMW M12">BMW</a> of <a href="/wiki/Manfred_Winkelhock" title="Manfred Winkelhock">Manfred Winkelhock</a>. The <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLarens</a> were off the pace with points leader <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> in eighth and <a href="/wiki/Niki_Lauda" title="Niki Lauda">Niki Lauda</a> in 14th. Reigning world champion <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a> qualified his <a href="/wiki/Brabham_BT53" title="Brabham BT53">Brabham BT53</a>-BMW ninth. The best of the <a href="/wiki/Pirelli" title="Pirelli">Pirelli</a> runners was <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Cecotto" title="Johnny Cecotto">Johnny Cecotto</a> in 16th in his <a href="/wiki/Toleman_TG183B" class="mw-redirect" title="Toleman TG183B">Toleman TG183B</a>-<a href="/wiki/Hart_Racing_Engines" title="Hart Racing Engines">Hart</a>. The only non-qualifier was <a href="/wiki/Philippe_Alliot" title="Philippe Alliot">Philippe Alliot</a> in his <a href="/wiki/RAM_Racing" title="RAM Racing">RAM</a>-Hart.
</p><p>Alboreto led all 70 laps and was even able to retain the lead during pit stops, despite Piquet pushing through without stopping. Warwick started well to run second for much of the race with Arnoux, Winkelhock and de Angelis pursuing. Prost and <a href="/wiki/Riccardo_Patrese" title="Riccardo Patrese">Riccardo Patrese</a> (<a href="/wiki/Alfa_Romeo_184T" title="Alfa Romeo 184T">Alfa Romeo 184T</a>) were early retirements with Cecotto dropping out on lap 1 with a broken clutch.
</p><p>Lauda's <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/2" title="McLaren MP4/2">McLaren MP4/2</a> broke its water pump at half-distance, the second such failure for the <a href="/wiki/Techniques_d%27Avant_Garde" title="Techniques d&#39;Avant Garde">TAG</a>-<a href="/wiki/Porsche_in_Formula_One" class="mw-redirect" title="Porsche in Formula One">Porsche</a> engine after Prost suffered the same fate on the warm up lap in <a href="/wiki/1984_South_African_Grand_Prix" title="1984 South African Grand Prix">South Africa</a>. Shortly afterwards Winkelhock stopped and <a href="/wiki/Andrea_de_Cesaris" title="Andrea de Cesaris">Andrea de Cesaris</a> crashed his <a href="/wiki/Ligier_JS23" title="Ligier JS23">Ligier JS23</a>-Renault. Piquet found himself third after the stops, but faded as the race neared its conclusion. Arnoux moved into third until he was caught by Rosberg. Piquet's BMW engine blew and with a lap to go Rosberg ran out of fuel, allowing Arnoux to join Alboreto and Warwick on the podium. Rosberg was classified fourth, with de Angelis fifth and <a href="/wiki/Stefan_Bellof" title="Stefan Bellof">Stefan Bellof</a> completing the top six in his <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_012" title="Tyrrell 012">Tyrrell 012</a>-<a href="/wiki/Cosworth_DFY" class="mw-redirect" title="Cosworth DFY">Ford</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">Aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1984_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The results would change months later as the impact of <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_Racing" title="Tyrrell Racing">Tyrrell Racing</a>'s disqualification from the 1984 season saw <a href="/wiki/Stefan_Bellof" title="Stefan Bellof">Stefan Bellof</a> removed from sixth position, promoting <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> (Toleman TG183B) into the points.
</p><p>The Belgian Grand Prix would return to traditional home at <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Spa-Francorchamps" title="Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps">Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps</a> in <a href="/wiki/1985_Belgian_Grand_Prix" title="1985 Belgian Grand Prix">1985</a>. The shortened version of the home of Belgian motorsport had its Formula One debut the <a href="/wiki/1983_Belgian_Grand_Prix" title="1983 Belgian Grand Prix">previous year</a> and proven instantly popular and <a href="/wiki/Gilles_Villeneuve" title="Gilles Villeneuve">Gilles Villeneuve</a>'s death two years earlier at Zolder was still fresh.
</p>
