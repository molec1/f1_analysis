<p>The <b>2000 Australian Grand Prix</b> (officially known as the <b>LXV Qantas Australian Grand Prix</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 12 March 2000 at the <a href="/wiki/Melbourne_Grand_Prix_Circuit" class="mw-redirect" title="Melbourne Grand Prix Circuit">Melbourne Grand Prix Circuit</a>, <a href="/wiki/Melbourne" title="Melbourne">Melbourne</a>. It was the first race of the <a href="/wiki/2000_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2000 Formula One season">2000 Formula One season</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-atlas-preview_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlas-preview-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The 58-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> team after starting from third position. Schumacher's new teammate for the 2000 season, <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> finished second in the other Ferrari, with <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> third for <a href="/wiki/BMW_in_Formula_One" title="BMW in Formula One">BMW</a>-<a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-formula1.com-officialresult_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-formula1.com-officialresult-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> started on <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> in a <a href="/wiki/McLaren_(racing)" class="mw-redirect" title="McLaren (racing)">McLaren</a>, with teammate <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> alongside him, but both retired with <a href="/wiki/Pneumatic_valve_springs" title="Pneumatic valve springs">pneumatic valve</a> failures.<sup id="cite_ref-telegraph2_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-telegraph2-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Three drivers made their Grand Prix debut: future world champion <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> in a BMW-Williams, <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> in a <a href="/wiki/Prost_Grand_Prix" title="Prost Grand Prix">Prost</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gast%C3%B3n_Mazzacane" title="Gastón Mazzacane">Gastón Mazzacane</a> in a <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a>. Mazzacane and Button retired from their debut race, and Heidfeld finished ninth, two laps behind.<sup id="cite_ref-formula1.com-officialresult_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-formula1.com-officialresult-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-formula1.com-article_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-formula1.com-article-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2000_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2000_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Heading into the first race of the <a href="/wiki/2000_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2000 Formula One season">2000 Formula One season</a>, many were tipping <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> and <a href="/wiki/1999_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1999 Formula One World Championship">1999</a> <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Champion</a>, <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> as the main contenders to win the 2000 Drivers' Championship.<sup id="cite_ref-atlas-homepage_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlas-homepage-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher was favourite to win the Championship according to the <a href="/wiki/Bookmaker" title="Bookmaker">bookmakers</a> and by some former Formula One drivers,<sup id="cite_ref-atlas-bookie_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlas-bookie-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-atlas-grapevine1_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlas-grapevine1-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> including Schumacher's teammate from the 1999 season, and new <a href="/wiki/Jaguar_Racing" title="Jaguar Racing">Jaguar</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-independent1_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-independent1-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The last Drivers' Champion for Ferrari, <a href="/wiki/Jody_Scheckter" title="Jody Scheckter">Jody Scheckter</a>, who did so in the <a href="/wiki/1979_Formula_One_season" title="1979 Formula One season">1979 season</a>, also tipped Schumacher to win the Championship.<sup id="cite_ref-independent2_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-independent2-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Out of the 11 teams and 22 drivers on the starting grid, three drivers made their debut. <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> joined the <a href="/wiki/Prost_Grand_Prix" title="Prost Grand Prix">Prost</a> team alongside <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-atlas-prostlineup_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlas-prostlineup-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> The two replaced Prost's 1999 season line-up of <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Panis" title="Olivier Panis">Olivier Panis</a>, who moved to McLaren as <a href="/wiki/Test_driver#Formula_One" title="Test driver">test driver</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a>, who moved to <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a> alongside <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a> following <a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a>'s retirement.<sup id="cite_ref-panis-f1racing-nov99_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-panis-f1racing-nov99-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-f1racing-feb2000preview_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1racing-feb2000preview-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> At the <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a> team, <a href="/wiki/Gast%C3%B3n_Mazzacane" title="Gastón Mazzacane">Gastón Mazzacane</a> replaced <a href="/wiki/Luca_Badoer" title="Luca Badoer">Luca Badoer</a>, who opted to continue as Ferrari test driver, a role he began in 1997.<sup id="cite_ref-atlas-mazzac_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlas-mazzac-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> The third <a href="/wiki/Rookie" title="Rookie">rookie</a> driver in the 2000 driver line-up was <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a>, who replaced <a href="/wiki/Alex_Zanardi" title="Alex Zanardi">Alessandro Zanardi</a> at <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a>. Button, at the time, became the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_driver_records#Youngest_drivers_to_start_a_race" title="List of Formula One driver records">youngest British driver ever to start a Formula One race</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-atlas-buttonannounced_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlas-buttonannounced-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Many observers and fellow drivers alike had differing opinions of Williams' choice to take Button on. <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Mika_Salo" title="Mika Salo">Mika Salo</a> stated that because of Button's inexperience, he could "hurt himself or somebody else".<sup id="cite_ref-guardian6_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guardian6-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice_and_Qualifying">Practice and Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2000_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Practice and Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Four practice sessions were held before the Sunday race—two on Friday, and two on Saturday. The Friday morning and afternoon sessions each lasted an hour. The third and final practice sessions were held on Saturday morning and lasted 45 minutes.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> In the first practice session, held on a slippery track surface, Häkkinen set the fastest lap of 1 minute and 32.768 seconds, followed by Michael Schumacher, <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a>, <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a>, <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jos_Verstappen" title="Jos Verstappen">Jos Verstappen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Wurz" title="Alexander Wurz">Alexander Wurz</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-AtlasFP1_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AtlasFP1-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> During the session, <a href="/wiki/Ricardo_Zonta" title="Ricardo Zonta">Ricardo Zonta</a> and Heidfeld spun into a <a href="/wiki/Run-off_area" title="Run-off area">gravel trap</a> beside the circuit, <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Herbert" title="Johnny Herbert">Johnny Herbert</a>'s car had a possible engine failure that cut out its <a href="/wiki/Ignition_system" title="Ignition system">ignition system</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-AtlasFP1_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AtlasFP1-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> and Michael Schumacher's driving was curtailed due to the on-board <a href="/wiki/Professional_video_camera" title="Professional video camera">television camera</a> mounted atop of his Ferrari melting a <a href="/wiki/Cable_harness" title="Cable harness">wiring loom</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Although he removed the left-rear wheel of his car in an sideways accident at the fast sweeping turn 14 midway through the second practice session because he missed the braking point, Michael Schumacher recorded the day's fastest lap of 1 minute and 32.130 seconds. Coulthard was second and Barrichello third. The rest of the top ten were Häkkinen, Villenueve, <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Diniz" title="Pedro Diniz">Pedro Diniz</a>, Frentzen, Wurz, Zonta and Irvine.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the third practice session, Michael Schumacher continued to run strongly with the fastest time of 1 minute and 30.439 seconds. Coulthard was 0.519 seconds behind in second place and Frentzen placed third. Fourth went to Barrichello and Trulli was fifth. Ralf Schumacher, Villeneuve, Herbert, <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> and Irvine followed in the top ten positions.<sup id="cite_ref-GF1TimesFP_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF1TimesFP-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Häkkinen's engine failed mid-way through his first lap, necessitating two teams of McLaren mechanics to spend 42 minutes dismantling both his and teammate's Coulthard's rear of their cars to change their engines and <a href="/wiki/Transmission_(mechanics)" title="Transmission (mechanics)">gearbox</a>es to enable driving in the second practice session later that day.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher paced the final practice session with a lap of 1 minute and 30.390 seconds, ahead of Coulthard, Frentzen, Barrichello, Ralf Schumacher, Trulli, <a href="/wiki/Pedro_de_la_Rosa" title="Pedro de la Rosa">Pedro de la Rosa</a>, Verstappen, Villeneuve and Herbert.<sup id="cite_ref-GF1TimesFP_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF1TimesFP-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> On his fourth lap, Button crashed into the turn ten right-hand side barrier at 160&#160;mph (260&#160;km/h) after clipping a <a href="/wiki/Curb" title="Curb">kerb</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-F1NetJBCrash_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1NetJBCrash-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> removing the rear wheels from his Williams and heavily damaging its bodywork.<sup id="cite_ref-F1NetJBCrash_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1NetJBCrash-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-AtlasJBCrash_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AtlasJBCrash-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> The session was halted for ten minutes as track marshals extricated his car from the circuit.<sup id="cite_ref-AtlasJBCrash_26-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AtlasJBCrash-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Saturday's afternoon qualifying session lasted for an hour. Each driver was limited to twelve laps, with the starting order decided by the drivers' fastest laps. During this session, the <a href="/wiki/107%25_rule" title="107% rule">107% rule</a> was in effect, which necessitated each driver set a time within 107% of the quickest lap to qualify for the race.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> took pole position by three-tenths from teammate <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a>, after the pair locked out a <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> front row to start the season. <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> was disappointed with third after a spin by Coulthard had caused a red flag forcing him to abandon his final attempt.<sup id="cite_ref-atlas-qualifyingreport_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlas-qualifyingreport-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard said that it "was not the intention" for him to disrupt Schumacher on his final flying lap. The top three qualifying places were identical to what they had been in the previous two years. Schumacher's new <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> teammate, <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a>, qualified fourth, with the <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordans</a> of <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> behind him on the third row. <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a> qualified seventh for <a href="/wiki/Jaguar_Racing" title="Jaguar Racing">Jaguar</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a>, <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mika_Salo" title="Mika Salo">Mika Salo</a> completing the top ten. <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> was eleventh for the <a href="/wiki/Williams_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams F1">Williams</a>-<a href="/wiki/BMW_in_motorsport" title="BMW in motorsport">BMW</a>, with rookie teammate <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> qualifying 21st for his first race, setting a lap time 1.6 seconds slower. Behind Button was another rookie, <a href="/wiki/Gast%C3%B3n_Mazzacane" title="Gastón Mazzacane">Gastón Mazzacane</a>, and in front was Jaguar's <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Herbert" title="Johnny Herbert">Johnny Herbert</a>. Button later described his qualifying session by saying "It can't get worse than this".<sup id="cite_ref-guardian7_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guardian7-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Warm-up">Warm-up</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2000_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Warm-up">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In Sunday morning's half an hour warm-up session, Barrichello paced the field with a time of 1 minute and 31.225 seconds. He was 1.573 seconds faster than Button in second place who had a small oil leak and fire in the pit lane. The two McLarens were third and fourth, with Häkkinen ahead of Coulthard. Verstappen was fifth and Fisichella sixth. <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a>, Michael Schumacher, de la Rosa and Salo completed the top ten going into the race.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2000_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>21 of the 22 cars started on the grid, with Prost driver Alesi the only car to start from the pit lane.<sup id="cite_ref-atlas-review_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlas-review-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Häkkinen converted his pole position from qualifying to lead into the first corner at the start of the race. Coulthard and Schumacher remained in second and third place respectively, but Barrichello was overtaken by Frentzen into turn one, dropping him down to fifth place. Trulli rounded out the top six places at the end of lap 1.<sup id="cite_ref-atlas-review_29-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlas-review-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> At the back of the field, Button gained six positions, moving from 21st to 15th position.<sup id="cite_ref-guardian7_27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guardian7-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-f1racing-march2000aussiereport_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1racing-march2000aussiereport-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> This was partially due to a collision between <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Marc_Gen%C3%A9" title="Marc Gené">Marc Gené</a> and rookie Heidfeld, which left Gené last in the order as he had to pit for repairs.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix.com-resultpage_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix.com-resultpage-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Jaguar driver <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Herbert" title="Johnny Herbert">Johnny Herbert</a> was the first person to retire, retiring with clutch problems on lap 2. By lap 4, the two McLarens of Häkkinen and Coulthard, along with Schumacher, were already pulling away from Frentzen's Jordan, who was five seconds behind Schumacher. On lap 7, the front suspension on the <a href="/wiki/Arrows_Grand_Prix_International" title="Arrows Grand Prix International">Arrows</a> car of <a href="/wiki/Pedro_de_la_Rosa" title="Pedro de la Rosa">Pedro de la Rosa</a> failed, sending him into a tyre barrier. Herbert's teammate, Irvine spun trying to avoid the Arrows car. Irvine could not get the Jaguar going again as it suffered an engine failure.<sup id="cite_ref-atlas-review_29-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlas-review-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Both retired from the race. The <a href="/wiki/Safety_car" title="Safety car">safety car</a> was deployed to slow the other cars down so the <a href="/wiki/Motorsport_marshal" title="Motorsport marshal">marshals</a> could clear up the wreckage.<sup id="cite_ref-f1racing-march2000aussiereport_30-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1racing-march2000aussiereport-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-guardian8_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guardian8-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>When the <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_regulations#Flags" title="Formula One regulations">green flag</a> was given at the end of lap 10, Coulthard slowed dramatically and came in for a <a href="/wiki/Pit_stop" title="Pit stop">pit stop</a> with engine problems. Coulthard returned to the track, but the problem continued and forced his retirement on lap 11. Schumacher was now promoted to second, with Frentzen in third place. Barrichello was in fourth place, with Trulli fifth. Behind the top five, <a href="/wiki/British_American_Racing" title="British American Racing">BAR</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a> was in sixth place, heading a queue of cars down to 12th place Button.<sup id="cite_ref-atlas-review_29-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlas-review-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Häkkinen retired on lap 18, with the same problem as his teammate; a double retirement almost identical to the <a href="/wiki/1999_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="1999 Australian Grand Prix">1999 race</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-f1racing-march2000aussiereport_30-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1racing-march2000aussiereport-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> With Häkkinen's retirement, Schumacher led by 16.5 seconds from Frentzen in second. Schumacher made his one and only pit stop on lap 29, giving Frentzen the lead. Schumacher rejoined the track in third, just in front of Trulli.<sup id="cite_ref-atlas-review_29-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlas-review-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-guardian8_31-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guardian8-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello was the next driver of the front running cars to pit on lap 33, but the Ferrari team switched him to a two stop strategy so that he could move ahead of Frentzen.<sup id="cite_ref-guardian8_31-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guardian8-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Trulli retired his Jordan car on lap 36 with gearbox problems. The retirements and pit stops moved Button up the order, putting the Williams driver in third place before his pit stop on lap 36.<sup id="cite_ref-atlas-review_29-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlas-review-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-f1racing-march2000aussiereport_30-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1racing-march2000aussiereport-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Frentzen also made a pit stop on lap 36, but the Jordan mechanics experienced problems while refuelling the car, costing Frentzen over ten seconds in the stop. Frentzen rejoined the track in sixth, however retired three laps later with gearbox problems.<sup id="cite_ref-f1racing-march2000aussiereport_30-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1racing-march2000aussiereport-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-guardian8_31-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guardian8-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello took the lead on lap 45, overtaking teammate Schumacher before making his second pit stop. Barrichello rejoined the track second.<sup id="cite_ref-guardian8_31-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guardian8-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The retirements of the McLaren and Jordan team cars elevated <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a>, Button's Williams teammate, into third position. Villeneuve was fourth, <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> was fifth in the <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a>, with Button sixth.<sup id="cite_ref-atlas-review_29-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlas-review-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> With 11 laps remaining Button retired from the race with an engine failure.<sup id="cite_ref-guardian7_27-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guardian7-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Ricardo_Zonta" title="Ricardo Zonta">Ricardo Zonta</a>, in a BAR, was promoted to sixth, but was overtaken by Salo with a few laps remaining.<sup id="cite_ref-atlas-review_29-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlas-review-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher won the race after 58 laps in a time 1:34:01.987, twelve seconds ahead of second placed Barrichello, who completed a Ferrari one-two finish. Ralf Schumacher was classified third, with Villeneuve close behind in fourth, giving BAR their first ever points in Formula One.<sup id="cite_ref-guardian8_31-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guardian8-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Fisichella finished fifth, with Salo initially classified as sixth in the Sauber. However, a few hours after the race, Salo was disqualified from the race for a bodywork infringement, promoting Zonta into sixth position.<sup id="cite_ref-atlas-review_29-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlas-review-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2000_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After the race, Michael Schumacher said:
</p>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r960796168">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>I was driving pretty easily from the beginning, I was saving my tyres, saving my fuel, ready for the final moments when the pit stops came and I had to attack. Unfortunately the two guys in front of me broke down. I would have preferred to race them to the end, to prove how good we really are.<sup id="cite_ref-guardian8_31-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guardian8-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>
