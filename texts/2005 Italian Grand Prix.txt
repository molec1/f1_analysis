<p>The <b>2005 Italian Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 4 September 2005 at the <a href="/wiki/Autodromo_Nazionale_di_Monza" title="Autodromo Nazionale di Monza">Autodromo Nazionale di Monza</a>, Italy. It was the fifteenth race of the <a href="/wiki/2005_FIA_Formula_One_World_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="2005 FIA Formula One World Championship">2005 FIA Formula One World Championship</a>. The 53-lap race was won from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> by Colombia's <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4-20" title="McLaren MP4-20">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Mercedes_AMG_High_Performance_Powertrains" title="Mercedes AMG High Performance Powertrains">Mercedes</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Renault_R25" title="Renault R25">Renault</a> drivers <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> and <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> second and third respectively.
</p><p>Montoya's teammate <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> achieved the highest-ever speed recorded during an F1 race, 370.1&#160;km/h (230.0&#160;mph).<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Despite this, Räikkönen could only finish fourth, thus losing ground to Alonso at the top of the Drivers' Championship. <a href="/wiki/Ant%C3%B4nio_Pizzonia" title="Antônio Pizzonia">Antônio Pizzonia</a> scored his last ever world championship points at this race.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2005_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> set the fastest time in qualifying in his <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Mercedes_AMG_High_Performance_Powertrains" title="Mercedes AMG High Performance Powertrains">Mercedes</a>, but received a 10-place grid penalty for changing his engine, demoting him to 11th on the grid and giving pole to teammate <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a>.
</p><p>Montoya led every lap, winning by 2.5 seconds from the <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a> of <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> third in the other Renault. Räikkönen climbed through the field to finish fourth, ahead of <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ant%C3%B4nio_Pizzonia" title="Antônio Pizzonia">Antônio Pizzonia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a>. Räikkönen would have had a chance of winning with a one-stop strategy, but a deflated tyre forced him to make a second pit stop. <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> also had the same problem later in the day, and Montoya was lucky to finish in the lead, as his left rear tyre began to cut with several laps remaining.
</p><p>Alonso extended his lead over Räikkönen in the Drivers' Championship to 27 points, 103 to 76, with <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> third on 55 and Montoya fourth on 50. Mathematically, the championship was now a two-horse race between Alonso and Räikkönen. Renault retained an eight-point lead over McLaren-Mercedes in the Constructors' Championship, 144 to 136, <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> remaining in third on 86 and <a href="/wiki/Toyota_Racing_(Formula_One_team)" title="Toyota Racing (Formula One team)">Toyota</a> fourth on 78.
</p><p>There were no retirements during the race, a feat that had not been achieved in Formula One with a full field since the <a href="/wiki/1961_Dutch_Grand_Prix" title="1961 Dutch Grand Prix">1961 Dutch Grand Prix</a>, and would not be achieved again until the <a href="/wiki/2011_European_Grand_Prix" title="2011 European Grand Prix">2011 European Grand Prix</a>. The <a href="/wiki/2005_United_States_Grand_Prix" title="2005 United States Grand Prix">2005 United States Grand Prix</a> is also considered to have had no retirements; however, only six cars started due to problems with the supply of <a href="/wiki/Michelin" title="Michelin">Michelin</a> tyres, which led to the mass withdrawal of all teams running on those tyres due to safety issues. Pizzonia replaced <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> for the rest of the season onwards.
</p>
