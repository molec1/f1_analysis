<p>The <b>2000 British Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>LIII Foster's British Grand Prix</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 23 April 2000 at <a href="/wiki/Silverstone_Circuit" title="Silverstone Circuit">Silverstone Circuit</a>, England. It was the fourth race of the <a href="/wiki/2000_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2000 Formula One World Championship">2000 Formula One season</a> and the 55th <a href="/wiki/British_Grand_Prix" title="British Grand Prix">British Grand Prix</a>. The 60-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> driver <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> after starting from fourth position. His teammate <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> finished second with <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> third for the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> team.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2000_British_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2000_British_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Grand Prix was contested by eleven teams, each of two drivers.<sup id="cite_ref-entrants_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-entrants-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The teams, also known as <a href="/wiki/Formula_One#Constructors" title="Formula One">constructors</a> were, <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jaguar_Racing" title="Jaguar Racing">Jaguar</a>, <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a>, <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Prost_Grand_Prix" title="Prost Grand Prix">Prost</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arrows_Grand_Prix_International" title="Arrows Grand Prix International">Arrows</a>, <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a> and <a href="/wiki/British_American_Racing" title="British American Racing">BAR</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-entrants_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-entrants-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_tyre" class="mw-redirect" title="Formula One tyre">Tyre</a> supplier <a href="/wiki/Bridgestone" title="Bridgestone">Bridgestone</a> brought four different tyre compounds to the race: two dry compounds, soft and medium, and two <a href="/wiki/Rain_tyre" title="Rain tyre">wet-weather compounds</a>, soft and hard.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Going into the race Ferrari driver <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> led the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a> with 30 points, ahead of <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> on 9 points and <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> on 8. <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> in fourth and <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> in fifth were tied on 6 points.<sup id="cite_ref-points_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a> Ferrari were leading with 39 points, McLaren and Benetton were second and third with 10 and 8 points, respectively, whilst Jordan and Williams were tied in fourth place with 7 points each.<sup id="cite_ref-points_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Ferrari and Michael Schumacher had so far dominated the championship, winning the previous three races. Championship competitors Barrichello, Fisichella and Häkkinen had gained one second-place finish each, and Ralf Schumacher, <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a> and <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> had achieved one third-place podium finish each.<sup id="cite_ref-points_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Following the <a href="/wiki/2000_San_Marino_Grand_Prix" title="2000 San Marino Grand Prix">San Marino Grand Prix</a> on 9 April the teams conducted testing sessions at the <a href="/wiki/Silverstone_Circuit" title="Silverstone Circuit">Silverstone Circuit</a> from 11–14 April to prepare for the upcoming Grand Prix at the track. The sessions were marked with intermittent rain making the track slippery. Barrichello set the quickest times on the first day, ahead of Jordan driver <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Ralf Schumacher was quickest on the second day. BAR driver <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a> hit a fox at the back of the circuit, limiting his testing time.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher was fastest on the third day.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Villeneuve's teammate <a href="/wiki/Ricardo_Zonta" title="Ricardo Zonta">Ricardo Zonta</a> crashed into a spectator enclosure at the Stowe corner suffering a cut to his right middle finger.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> BAR later revealed Zonta's crash was caused by a front suspension failure and withdrew from testing.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher was fastest on the final day of testing.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> As a result of Zonta's crash, the tyre wall at Stowe corner was extended by one tyre in height and two tyres in depth.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The gravel trap around the area was also made smoother.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In a controversial move the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile</a> (FIA)—Formula One's governing body—decided to stage the British Grand Prix in April instead of the event's traditional mid-July date, with the race taking place on Easter Sunday. No specific reason was given for the move, though the reinstatement of the <a href="/wiki/2000_Belgian_Grand_Prix" title="2000 Belgian Grand Prix">Belgian Grand Prix</a>—initially excluded from the calendar due to problems concerning the country's tobacco advertising laws—and the return of the <a href="/wiki/2000_United_States_Grand_Prix" title="2000 United States Grand Prix">United States Grand Prix</a> were suggested as possible reasons.<sup id="cite_ref-independent_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-independent-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> The owner of Formula One's commercial rights <a href="/wiki/Bernie_Ecclestone" title="Bernie Ecclestone">Bernie Ecclestone</a> had scheduled the <a href="/wiki/2000_French_Grand_Prix" title="2000 French Grand Prix">French Grand Prix</a> to be held in the month of April with the British Grand Prix in July though "internal politics" in France prevented the change.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Much of the discussion heading into the event was a revision to allow teams to use modified pit lane speed limiters. The FIA allowed them to be used provided they were "hard-coded" below a limit of 50 miles per hour (80&#160;km/h), thus preventing teams from modifying them. It was in response to preventing driver aids like traction control and launch control from being secretly implemented.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Most of the drivers agreed that the change would reduce the risk of drivers entering and exiting the pit lane. However, they expressed concern that they would not drive safely at a slow speed and the possibility that mechanics or other cars could be hit would be increased.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice_and_qualifying">Practice and qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2000_British_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Practice and qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Four practice sessions were held before the Sunday race—two on Friday, and two on Saturday. The Friday morning and afternoon sessions each lasted an hour. The third and final practice sessions were held on Saturday morning and lasted 45 minutes.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> The Friday morning and afternoon practice sessions were held on a dry track early in the first practice session but it rained throughout the rest of the day.<sup id="cite_ref-fiafriday_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiafriday-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Frentzen was fastest in the first session with a time of 1:27.683, almost half a second quicker than Jaguar driver <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a>. The McLaren drivers were third and fourth, Coulthard faster than Häkkinen. <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> was fifth, ahead of Villeneuve. Barrichello, <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Wurz" title="Alexander Wurz">Alexander Wurz</a>, Fisichella and <a href="/wiki/Marc_Gen%C3%A9" title="Marc Gené">Marc Gené</a> rounded out the top ten fastest drivers in the session. An electrical failure on Ralf Schumacher's car prevented him from setting a lap time.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> In the second practice session, Frentzen remained fastest with his lap from the first session; the circuit was too wet for him and others to improve their lap times. The session was disrupted as Coulthard's McLaren pulled out to the side of the Hangar Straight with an hydraulic problem – this required a suspension as the recovery vehicle got stuck in mud meaning a tractor had to pull it out—and Villeneuve nearly struck a marshal assisting Coulthard.<sup id="cite_ref-fiafriday_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiafriday-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The weather remained wet for the Saturday morning practice sessions.<sup id="cite_ref-fiasaturday_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiasaturday-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard was fastest in the third session, setting a time of 1:33.614; Häkkinen ended with the second-quickest lap. Michael Schumacher and Barrichello were third and fifth, respectively; they were separated by Ralf Schumacher. The Jordan drivers ended sixth and seventh with Frentzen quicker than Trulli. <a href="/wiki/Mika_Salo" title="Mika Salo">Mika Salo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Diniz" title="Pedro Diniz">Pedro Diniz</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jos_Verstappen" title="Jos Verstappen">Jos Verstappen</a> followed in the top ten.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> In the final practice session, Häkkinen ended up fastest with a time of 1:33.132, with Coulthard setting the third-quickest lap time. The pair were separated by Michael Schumacher with his teammate Barrichello fourth-fastest. Ralf Schumacher was fifth overall. Fisichella followed in sixth, having lost most of the session due to an oil leak, requiring an engine change. Frentzen, Verstappen, Trulli and Salo completed the top ten ahead of qualifying. Diniz spun off the circuit damaging his car's front suspension in a crash. Williams driver <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> spun into the <a href="/wiki/Run-off_area" title="Run-off area">gravel trap</a> at turn 14 and was subsequently hit by Irvine's Jaguar. The Williams sustained a broken front and rear suspension whilst the Jaguar's <a href="/wiki/Monocoque" title="Monocoque">monocoque</a> was punctured. Both drivers were unhurt.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> 
</p>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatleft" style="width:33%;&#32;;"><style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r887775652">.mw-parser-output .quotebox{background-color:#F9F9F9;border:1px solid #aaa;box-sizing:border-box;padding:10px;font-size:88%;max-width:100%}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft{margin:0.5em 1.4em 0.8em 0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright{margin:0.5em 0 0.8em 1.4em}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.centered{margin:0.5em auto 0.8em auto}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft p,.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright p{font-style:inherit}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-title{background-color:#F9F9F9;text-align:center;font-size:larger;font-weight:bold}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:before{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" “ ";vertical-align:-45%;line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:after{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" ” ";line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .left-aligned{text-align:left}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .right-aligned{text-align:right}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .center-aligned{text-align:center}.mw-parser-output .quotebox cite{display:block;font-style:normal}@media screen and (max-width:360px){.mw-parser-output .quotebox{min-width:100%;margin:0 0 0.8em!important;float:none!important}}</style>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">I am very happy with my first pole for Ferrari. I like these conditions and I know Silverstone well. The team gave me a good car and we timed the final laps just right. I was lucky not to have any traffic. I did not know what to expect because, last week in testing, I only drove in the wet, and only did two laps in the dry yesterday. I tried to guess what the weather conditions were going to do. The only thing to do was to keep concentrated and pray for no traffic. The team did a good job with my car, I know this track very well and I pushed right to the very limit. Michael Schumacher, today I went out early because I thought it might rain later. That is why I went out early again for my second run at a different time to the others. Starting from pole tomorrow will be important if the weather is bad for the race.</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style="">Rubens Barrichello, commenting on taking <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>Saturday's afternoon one hour qualifying session saw each driver limited to twelve laps, with the grid order decided by their fastest laps. During this session, the <a href="/wiki/107%25_rule" title="107% rule">107% rule</a> was in effect, which necessitated each driver set a time within 107 per cent of the quickest lap to qualify for the race.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> The session was held in cloudy weather conditions but on a drying race track. The air temperature was 14&#160;°C (57&#160;°F) and the track temperature was 10&#160;°C (50&#160;°F).<sup id="cite_ref-fiasaturday_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiasaturday-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello clinched his third <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> of his career, his first at the circuit, with a time of 1:25.703. He was joined on the front row of the grid by Frentzen recording a lap 0.003 seconds slower. It was also his best qualifying performance of the season. Häkkinen qualified third having been forced to ease off the throttle after making a mistake during his final quick lap. He was in front of teammate Coulthard who encountered traffic on his final qualifying runs.<sup id="cite_ref-atlasquali_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasquali-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher—who made a change to his car, only to see him make more mistakes—qualified fifth and missed starting his final lap by 0.1 seconds.<sup id="cite_ref-fiasaturday_20-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiasaturday-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-atlasquali_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasquali-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Button and Ralf Schumacher clinched sixth and seventh positions, respectively, although both drivers had mixed feelings over their performances.<sup id="cite_ref-fiasaturday_20-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiasaturday-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Verstappen initially held the pole position in the session's closing seconds but spun off the track and was demoted to eighth.<sup id="cite_ref-atlasquali_24-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasquali-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Irvine took ninth and Villeneuve rounded out the top ten fastest qualifiers.<sup id="cite_ref-atlasquali_24-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasquali-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Trulli qualified in eleventh position but was prevented from setting a quicker time as he was held up by one of the Williams.<sup id="cite_ref-atlasquali_24-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasquali-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> He was ahead of Fisichella in the faster of the two Benettons.<sup id="cite_ref-fiasaturday_20-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiasaturday-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Diniz qualified in 13th position, eight tenths of a second ahead of teammate Salo in 18th;<sup id="cite_ref-atlasquali_24-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasquali-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> both drivers disadvantaged by the timing of their runs.<sup id="cite_ref-fiasaturday_20-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiasaturday-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> The two were separated by <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Herbert" title="Johnny Herbert">Johnny Herbert</a> who encountered <a href="/wiki/Racing_flags#Yellow_flag" title="Racing flags">yellow flags</a> during the session. He was followed up by <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> in the Prosts, who sandwiched Zonta. Behind Heidfeld, <a href="/wiki/Pedro_de_la_Rosa" title="Pedro de la Rosa">Pedro de la Rosa</a> in the slower Arrows set the 19th-fastest time and made a mistake during his final qualifying run.<sup id="cite_ref-atlasquali_24-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasquali-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Wurz ran his team's spare car due to an unidentifiable problem on his race car and was 20th-quickest.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> The two Minardi drivers <a href="/wiki/Marc_Gen%C3%A9" title="Marc Gené">Marc Gené</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gast%C3%B3n_Mazzacane" title="Gastón Mazzacane">Gastón Mazzacane</a> completed the final row of the grid.<sup id="cite_ref-atlasquali_24-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasquali-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The large amount of rain during the weeks leading up to the race caused extensive damage and flooding to Silverstone's car parks which saw a large reduction in spectator numbers in qualifying from 60,000 in 1999 to 15,000 in 2000.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcsilverstone_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcsilverstone-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> The car parks were closed to all traffic except for coaches which meant some spectators walked to the circuit to attend the Grand Prix.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcsilverstone_26-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcsilverstone-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> The circuit's owners placed 300 tonnes of hardcore material in car parks and entrance routes to the circuit for the Sunday race.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcsilverstone_26-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcsilverstone-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2000_British_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The conditions on the grid were dry before the race; conditions were expected to remain consistent throughout the race.<sup id="cite_ref-f1comrace_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> The drivers were due to take to the track at 09:30 <a href="/wiki/British_Summer_Time" title="British Summer Time">BST</a> (<a href="/wiki/Coordinated_Universal_Time" title="Coordinated Universal Time">UTC</a>+1) for a 30-minute warm-up session,<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_17-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> but the session was delayed by 100 minutes due to persistent fog which prevented the medical helicopter from arriving at the track. The Drivers' Parade was cancelled because of the delays.<sup id="cite_ref-warmup_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-warmup-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> The McLaren drivers were running quicker than their pace in qualifying; Coulthard had the fastest time of 1:26.800. Häkkinen was fourth in the other McLaren, two tenths of a second behind Coulthard. de la Rosa and Ralf Schumacher split them for the second- and third-quickest times, respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Häkkinen's race car was afflicted with an sensor failure which saw him use his team's spare monocoque whilst the problem was fixed. Ralf Schumacher meanwhile downplayed his chances, predicting that a wet race would give him a better finishing position.<sup id="cite_ref-warmup_29-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-warmup-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The race started at 13:00 local time. Barrichello maintained his start line advantage going into the first corner, whilst Frentzen remained in second position. Villeneuve made the best start in the field moving from 10th to 6th by the end of the first lap, whilst Michael Schumacher lost three positions over the same distance.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> At the completion of the first lap the drivers in the top ten positions were Barrichello, Frentzen, Coulthard, Häkkinen, Button, Villeneuve, Ralf Schumacher, Michael Schumacher, Verstappen and Trulli.<sup id="cite_ref-lapchart_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapchart-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello began to maintain a one-second gap between himself and Frentzen.<sup id="cite_ref-lapchart_32-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapchart-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Ralf Schumacher passed Villeneuve at Stowe corner for sixth position on lap 2. Further back Alesi lost 13th position after being overtaken by Salo whilst Diniz lost three places as he was involved in an incident.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_31-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The leading drivers began to gradually build a gap from Trulli by lap 3.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_31-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Two laps later Wurz made up a further position when he passed Zonta for 15th.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_31-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Häkkinen ran wide on lap eight and came under pressure from Button.<sup id="cite_ref-f1comrace_28-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> By the 14th lap, Barrichello had a lead of six-tenths of a seconds over Frentzen, who in turn was nine-tenths of a second in front of Coulthard. Häkkinen was a further nine-tenths of a second behind his teammate and continued to battle Button for fourth position, who was continuing to run 1.1 seconds ahead of Ralf Schumacher.<sup id="cite_ref-lapchart_32-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapchart-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Wurz, who was pressuring Alesi in 14th, became the first driver to make a <a href="/wiki/Pit_stop" title="Pit stop">pit stop</a> to ensure he would get a clear track.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_31-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Salo and Fisichella pitted over the next three laps.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_31-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Verstappen pulled over to the side of the track with electrical problems on lap 20.<sup id="cite_ref-f1comrace_28-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_31-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Frentzen and Ralf Schumacher became the next two laps to pit on lap 24 and remerged in seventh and eighth.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_31-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Button made his pit stop one lap later and joined behind teammate Ralf Schumacher.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_31-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> de la Rosa drove to the side of the circuit with hydraulic problems and retired on lap 28.<sup id="cite_ref-f1comrace_28-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_31-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard drafted down the straight and passed Barrichello at Stowe corner to become the new race leader on lap 30.<sup id="cite_ref-f1comrace_28-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_31-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard immediately began to build a gap between himself and Barrichello.<sup id="cite_ref-lapchart_32-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapchart-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Häkkinen made his only pit stop of the race on lap 31 and changed a flat-spotted tyre. He rejoined in eighth position.<sup id="cite_ref-f1comrace_28-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-lapchart_32-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapchart-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard and Villeneuve both pitted on the 33rd lap.<sup id="cite_ref-lapchart_32-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapchart-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello spun off the track with an hydraulic issue and pitted on lap 35.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_31-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> He was pushed into his garage to retire with an gearbox problem one lap later.<sup id="cite_ref-f1comrace_28-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Michael Schumacher set a new fastest lap of the race on lap 36, a 1:26.797 as he pulled out a gap before his pit stop.<sup id="cite_ref-f1comrace_28-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_31-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Zonta retired on the next lap when he spun off.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_31-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher made his pit stop on lap 37, allowing Frentzen to take over the lead. The Jordan driver pitted on lap 42 handing the lead back to Coulthard.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_31-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> The Williams pair both pitted over the next two laps, promoting Häkkinen and Michael Schumacher into second and third positions.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_31-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher was unable to catch Häkkinen as he was held up by backmarkers. Herbert became the final driver to make a scheduled pit stop on lap 48.<sup id="cite_ref-f1comrace_28-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> At the completion of lap 49 with the scheduled pit stops completed the top ten drivers were Coulthard, Häkkinen, Michael Schumacher, Frentzen, Ralf Schumacher, Button, Villeneuve, Trulli, Fisichella and Salo.<sup id="cite_ref-lapchart_32-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapchart-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Häkkinen started to reduce the gap to Coulthard on lap 51 who had problems with his gearbox.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_31-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> On the same lap, Frentzen occurred problems as he could not change his gears and fell behind Ralf Schumacher and Button.<sup id="cite_ref-f1comrace_28-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> He drove to his garage three laps later to retire.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_31-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> On lap 56, Häkkinen set a new fastest lap of the race, a 1:26.217 as he continued close on Coulthard.<sup id="cite_ref-f1comrace_28-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Villeneuve became the event's final retirement with gearbox issues on the same lap. Coulthard held off Häkkinen in the closing laps of the race and crossed the finish line on lap 60 to win his first race of the season and his second consecutive British Grand Prix victory in a time of 1'28:50.108,<sup id="cite_ref-gprace_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gprace-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> at an average speed of 129.454 miles per hour (208.336&#160;km/h).<sup id="cite_ref-results_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-results-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Häkkinen finished second 1.4-second behind, ahead of Michael Schumacher in third, Ralf Schumacher in fourth, Button in fifth and Trulli rounded out the points scoring positions in sixth.<sup id="cite_ref-gprace_33-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gprace-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Fisichella, Salo, Wurz, Alesi and Diniz followed up in the next five positions. Herbert, Irvine, Gené and Mazzacane finished in the following four positions.<sup id="cite_ref-gprace_33-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gprace-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Villeneuve and Frentzen were the last of the classified finishers despite not managing to cross the finish line because of their retirements.<sup id="cite_ref-results_34-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-results-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2000_British_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatright" style="width:33%;&#32;;"><link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r887775652"/>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">It's a fantastic feeling to win in front of my home crowd for the second time in a row. My engineers and mechanics did a fantastic job and I would really like to thank them for all their effort and support. I managed to overtake Rubens just before my one and only pitstop. That left me with quite some time to think about what it would feel like to win. I had a gearbox problem approximately 15 laps from the end, which meant that I wasn't able to take things easy. The car felt good and I'm pleased that my challenge for the Championship has begun.</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style="">David Coulthard, speaking after the race.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>The top three drivers appeared on the podium to collect their trophies and in the subsequent <a href="/wiki/News_conference" class="mw-redirect" title="News conference">press conference</a>. Coulthard said that overtaking Barrichello gave him the advantage during the first pit stops. Coulthard additionally revealed that he used inspiration from <a href="/wiki/1992_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1992 Formula One World Championship">1992</a> Drivers' Champion <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a>'s overtake on <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a> at the <a href="/wiki/1987_British_Grand_Prix" title="1987 British Grand Prix">1987 British Grand Prix</a> to execute the similar passing manoeuvre.<sup id="cite_ref-pressconf_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconf-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> He also believed that his victory made him confident about posing a challenge for the Drivers' Championship saying "my best years are still ahead of me."<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
