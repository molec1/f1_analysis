<p>The <b>1989 Mexican Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at the <a href="/wiki/Aut%C3%B3dromo_Hermanos_Rodr%C3%ADguez" title="Autódromo Hermanos Rodríguez">Autódromo Hermanos Rodríguez</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mexico_City" title="Mexico City">Mexico City</a> on 28 May 1989. The race, contested over 69 laps, was the fourth race of the <a href="/wiki/1989_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="1989 Formula One season">1989 Formula One season</a> and was won from pole position by <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/5" title="McLaren MP4/5">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Honda_in_Formula_One" title="Honda in Formula One">Honda</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Riccardo_Patrese" title="Riccardo Patrese">Riccardo Patrese</a> second in a <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW12" title="Williams FW12">Williams</a>-<a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a> and <a href="/wiki/Michele_Alboreto" title="Michele Alboreto">Michele Alboreto</a> third in a <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_018" title="Tyrrell 018">Tyrrell</a>-<a href="/wiki/Ford_Motor_Company" title="Ford Motor Company">Ford</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1989_Mexican_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-qualifying_report">Pre-qualifying report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1989_Mexican_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Pre-qualifying report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Friday morning pre-qualifying session produced another 1–2 for the <a href="/wiki/Brabham" title="Brabham">Brabham</a> team, their third of the four Grands Prix so far. This time <a href="/wiki/Martin_Brundle" title="Martin Brundle">Martin Brundle</a> was fastest ahead of <a href="/wiki/Stefano_Modena" title="Stefano Modena">Stefano Modena</a>, with the <a href="/wiki/Dallara" title="Dallara">Dallara</a> of <a href="/wiki/Alex_Caffi" title="Alex Caffi">Alex Caffi</a> pre-qualifying in third. The fourth-placed driver was <a href="/wiki/Stefan_Johansson" title="Stefan Johansson">Stefan Johansson</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Onyx_Grand_Prix" title="Onyx Grand Prix">Onyx</a>, who went through to the main qualifying sessions for the first time this season, at the expense of his team-mate <a href="/wiki/Bertrand_Gachot" title="Bertrand Gachot">Bertrand Gachot</a>, who was fifth fastest.
</p><p>Although failing to pre-qualify, <a href="/wiki/Gregor_Foitek" title="Gregor Foitek">Gregor Foitek</a> had one of his better sessions, finishing sixth fastest in the sole <a href="/wiki/EuroBrun" title="EuroBrun">EuroBrun</a>, ahead of the <a href="/wiki/Osella" title="Osella">Osella</a> of <a href="/wiki/Nicola_Larini" title="Nicola Larini">Nicola Larini</a>, who suffered a fuel pump issue. Larini had been lined up to replace <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> at <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> had the Austrian not been fit to return to the cockpit after his <a href="/wiki/1989_San_Marino_Grand_Prix" title="1989 San Marino Grand Prix">Imola</a> accident.<sup id="cite_ref-Walker_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Walker-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Volker_Weidler" title="Volker Weidler">Volker Weidler</a> was eighth in the <a href="/wiki/Rial_Racing" title="Rial Racing">Rial</a>, also one of his better performances of the season despite having gearing problems. Next were the two <a href="/wiki/Zakspeed" title="Zakspeed">Zakspeeds</a> of <a href="/wiki/Bernd_Schneider_(racing_driver)" title="Bernd Schneider (racing driver)">Bernd Schneider</a> and <a href="/wiki/Aguri_Suzuki" title="Aguri Suzuki">Aguri Suzuki</a>, and the other Osella, driven by <a href="/wiki/Piercarlo_Ghinzani" title="Piercarlo Ghinzani">Piercarlo Ghinzani</a>. Ghinzani was excluded anyway after ignoring a weight check signal.<sup id="cite_ref-Walker_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Walker-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-gp_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gp-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> At the bottom of the time sheets were <a href="/wiki/Joachim_Winkelhock" title="Joachim Winkelhock">Joachim Winkelhock</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Automobiles_Gonfaronnaises_Sportives" title="Automobiles Gonfaronnaises Sportives">AGS</a>, and lastly <a href="/wiki/Pierre-Henri_Raphanel" title="Pierre-Henri Raphanel">Pierre-Henri Raphanel</a>, who did not post a representative lap time in the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Coloni" title="Scuderia Coloni">Coloni</a> FC188B after his strong performance in the last race at <a href="/wiki/1989_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="1989 Monaco Grand Prix">Monaco</a>. Coloni had only brought three mechanics and one car for both Raphanel and <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Moreno" title="Roberto Moreno">Roberto Moreno</a>, while waiting for their new <a href="/wiki/Coloni_C3" title="Coloni C3">C3</a> to be built, making a token effort to qualify in order to comply with the regulations and avoid a fine.<sup id="cite_ref-Walker_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Walker-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying_report">Qualifying report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1989_Mexican_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Qualifying report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In qualifying proper, World Champion <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> scored his 33rd career pole position, equalling the long-standing record held by <a href="/wiki/Jim_Clark" title="Jim Clark">Jim Clark</a>. Senna's pole time in his <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/5" title="McLaren MP4/5">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Honda_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Honda F1">Honda</a> was 0.408 slower than his pole time in <a href="/wiki/1988_Mexican_Grand_Prix" title="1988 Mexican Grand Prix">1988</a> in the turbocharged <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/4" title="McLaren MP4/4">McLaren MP4/4</a>. His <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> teammate <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> was second fastest with the <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_640" title="Ferrari 640">Ferrari 640</a> of <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> third. Mansell's teammate <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a>, returning to action in Mexico after his crash at <a href="/wiki/1989_San_Marino_Grand_Prix" title="1989 San Marino Grand Prix">San Marino</a>, was sixth, the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferraris</a> split by the <a href="/wiki/March_Engineering" title="March Engineering">March</a>-<a href="/wiki/Judd_(engine)" title="Judd (engine)">Judd</a> of <a href="/wiki/Ivan_Capelli" title="Ivan Capelli">Ivan Capelli</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW12" title="Williams FW12">Williams</a>-<a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a> of <a href="/wiki/Riccardo_Patrese" title="Riccardo Patrese">Riccardo Patrese</a>. Surprisingly, Capelli's teammate <a href="/wiki/Maur%C3%ADcio_Gugelmin" title="Maurício Gugelmin">Maurício Gugelmin</a> failed to qualify. Berger, still suffering the effects of his crash, admitted that if not for Ferrari's innovative <a href="/wiki/Semi-automatic_transmission" title="Semi-automatic transmission">semi-automatic transmission</a> (which meant he did not have to change gears as with a normal stick shift), he would not have been able to race.
</p><p>The back row of the grid was arguably the best credentialed and most experienced in Grand Prix history, consisting of seven-time winner <a href="/wiki/Ren%C3%A9_Arnoux" title="René Arnoux">René Arnoux</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Ligier_JS33" title="Ligier JS33">Ligier</a>-<a href="/wiki/Ford_Motor_Company" title="Ford Motor Company">Ford</a> and triple World Champion <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Lotus_101" title="Lotus 101">Lotus</a>-Judd.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_report">Race report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1989_Mexican_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Race report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Senna chose medium compound <a href="/wiki/Goodyear_Tire_and_Rubber_Company" title="Goodyear Tire and Rubber Company">Goodyear</a> "B" tyres for the race while Prost went for the softer C-compound tyres in the hopes of gaining a speed advantage. Despite the pole being on the dirty side of the track in Mexico, Senna made a better start and was able to lead into the first turn from Mansell, Prost, Berger, Patrese and the <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_018" title="Tyrrell 018">Tyrrell</a>-Ford of <a href="/wiki/Michele_Alboreto" title="Michele Alboreto">Michele Alboreto</a>. However, it all meant nothing as Modena spun his <a href="/wiki/Brabham_BT58" title="Brabham BT58">Brabham</a> into the Peraltada on the first lap and was tapped by the Ligier of <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Grouillard" title="Olivier Grouillard">Olivier Grouillard</a> and finished against the tyre wall. Despite the car not being in a dangerous position, the red flag was shown and the race had to be restarted.
</p><p>Senna won the restart and led Prost, a fast starting Berger, Mansell and the <a href="/wiki/Williams_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams F1">Williams</a> pair of Patrese and <a href="/wiki/Thierry_Boutsen" title="Thierry Boutsen">Thierry Boutsen</a>. Prost, with his softer tyres giving him better grip, soon moved onto the back of his teammate's car. However, Mexico would be where Prost started questioning the power of his Honda V10 compared to the ones used by Senna. For a number of laps Prost, clearly faster through the final Peraltada curve coming onto the main straight, could not make an impression on Senna despite being in his aerodynamic tow on the 1.2&#160;km long main straight. Indeed, the #1 McLaren was seen to pull away from the #2 car on the straight. Running close to his teammate eventually had a detrimental effect on Prost's tyres and he was soon into the pits for a change of rubber. The McLaren team then mistakenly gave the Frenchman another set of C tyres rather than the B's he had come in for. Prost was soon back in for another tyre change and went back into the race only seconds in front of Senna who now had nearly a lap lead over his closest championship rival. Despite being on far fresher tyres than his teammate, Prost still lost ground to Senna and was eventually lapped when the Brazilian swept past on the main straight, fuelling Prost's claims that his engines were down on power compared to Senna's. McLaren team boss <a href="/wiki/Ron_Dennis" title="Ron Dennis">Ron Dennis</a> later publicly apologised to Prost for the error in his pit stop.
</p><p>Both Ferraris ran well until Berger's race ended on lap 16 with transmission failure while Mansell's gearbox lasted until lap 43. This left the Williams of Patrese in second place with Alboreto a surprising third. This was how the top three finished with <a href="/wiki/Alessandro_Nannini" title="Alessandro Nannini">Alessandro Nannini</a> fourth in his <a href="/wiki/Benetton_B188" title="Benetton B188">Benetton B188</a>. The Benetton team had hoped to have their new <a href="/wiki/Benetton_B189" title="Benetton B189">B189</a> available in Mexico, but ongoing problems with the new Ford HB engine meant the team had to continue using their 1988 car and engines. Prost, having unlapped himself, finished fifth to be the last car on the lead lap, while Italian <a href="/wiki/Gabriele_Tarquini" title="Gabriele Tarquini">Gabriele Tarquini</a> scored the final point for sixth in his <a href="/wiki/Automobiles_Gonfaronnaises_Sportives" title="Automobiles Gonfaronnaises Sportives">AGS</a>-Ford, which proved to be his only point in Formula One.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
