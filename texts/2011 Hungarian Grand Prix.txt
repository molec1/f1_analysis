<p>The <b>2011 Hungarian Grand Prix</b>, formally the <b>XXVII Eni Magyar Nagydíj</b>, was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race that was held on 31 July 2011 at the <a href="/wiki/Hungaroring" title="Hungaroring">Hungaroring</a> in Mogyoród, near <a href="/wiki/Budapest" title="Budapest">Budapest</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hungary" title="Hungary">Hungary</a><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> It was the eleventh round of the <a href="/wiki/2011_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2011 Formula One season">2011 Formula One season</a>, and the 27th running of the <a href="/wiki/Hungarian_Grand_Prix" title="Hungarian Grand Prix">Hungarian Grand Prix</a>, and the 26th time it had been held as a round of the World Championship. The 70-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a>, in his 200th Grand Prix start, after starting from third on the grid. Championship leader <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a>, who started the race from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>, finished in second place for <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> completed the podium in third position for <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Race_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Race-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As a consequence of the race, Vettel extended his lead in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">World Drivers' Championship</a> to 85&#160;points over <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a>, who finished the race in fifth position. <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>, fourth in the race, remained in third place in the championship, 3 points behind Webber in third, and one ahead of Alonso. In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">World Constructors' Championship</a>, Red Bull's championship lead was cut to 103&#160;points over McLaren, with Ferrari a further 65&#160;points behind in third position. This was the last race for <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After being replaced by <a href="/wiki/Karun_Chandhok" title="Karun Chandhok">Karun Chandhok</a> in <a href="/wiki/2011_German_Grand_Prix" title="2011 German Grand Prix">Germany</a>, <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus_(2010%E2%80%9311)" title="Team Lotus (2010–11)">Team Lotus</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> returned to his seat alongside <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Senna" title="Bruno Senna">Bruno Senna</a> also made his first appearance in a car at a Grand Prix this season, replacing <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> in the first free practice season for <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Renault team principal <a href="/wiki/%C3%89ric_Boullier" title="Éric Boullier">Éric Boullier</a> stated that the team were assessing their testing and reserve drivers – in particular Senna and <a href="/wiki/2011_GP2_Series_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2011 GP2 Series season">GP2 championship</a> leader <a href="/wiki/Romain_Grosjean" title="Romain Grosjean">Romain Grosjean</a> – as potential replacements for Heidfeld.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2011)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Tyre supplier <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_tyres" title="Formula One tyres">Pirelli</a> brought its yellow-banded soft compound tyre as the harder "prime" tyre and the red-banded super-soft compound as the softer "option" compound, as opposed to the previous year where Bridgestone brought the white-banded medium compound as the prime.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9bastien_Buemi" title="Sébastien Buemi">Sébastien Buemi</a> received a five-place grid penalty for causing an avoidable accident with Nick Heidfeld at the <a href="/wiki/N%C3%BCrburgring" title="Nürburgring">Nürburgring</a>.
</p><p>This race was <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a>'s 200th Grand Prix; and also the 100th Grand Prix of the 2.4 litre V8 engine since it became mandatory in F1 in <a href="/wiki/2006_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2006 Formula One World Championship">2006</a>. It was the 100th Grand Prix for <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> and also for the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso</a> team.
</p><p>Heading into the weekend, <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> led the World Championship with 216 points, 77 more than teammate <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> - his lead had dropped after <a href="/wiki/2011_German_Grand_Prix" title="2011 German Grand Prix">Germany</a> for only the second time this season, but only by 3 points. After his victory the previous week in <a href="/wiki/2011_German_Grand_Prix" title="2011 German Grand Prix">Germany</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> had become the only driver other than Vettel to win multiple Grands Prix thus far in <a href="/wiki/2011_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2011 Formula One season">2011</a>; he was on 134 points, 5 behind Webber, yet 4 ahead of <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>. <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> had dropped to fifth in the standings after two retirements in the last two Grands Prix, on 109 points.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a> still seemed dominant in the Constructors' table, despite scoring their season's fewest points the previous week in Germany, and led on 355 points. <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> looked set to continue their good form and pace this weekend, although were 112 points behind Red Bull on 243. Alonso had scored the most points of any driver in the last three Grands Prix, and had put <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> a clear third on 192 points. <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lotus_Renault_GP" class="mw-redirect" title="Lotus Renault GP">Renault</a> were both over 100 points behind Ferrari on 78 and 66 respectively in fourth and fifth places. <a href="/wiki/Williams_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams F1">Williams</a>, <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus_(2010%E2%80%9311)" title="Team Lotus (2010–11)">Team Lotus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hispania_Racing" class="mw-redirect" title="Hispania Racing">HRT</a> and <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Racing" title="Virgin Racing">Virgin Racing</a> had all mathematically dropped out of title contention at the last race - although, none realistically had any chance of winning.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice">Practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In practice 1, <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> topped the leaderboard with a time of 1:23.350 following on from his win at the <a href="/wiki/2011_German_Grand_Prix" title="2011 German Grand Prix">Nürburgring</a> the weekend before, <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> came second with a time of 1:23.564 and <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> third with a time of 1:23.642.
</p><p>In second practice, Hamilton once again clocked the best time of 1:21.018, Alonso improved on his previous lap with a 1:21.259, and <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> was third fastest in 1:21.322. Both <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> and Vettel failed to impress in second practice provoking <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a> to break the curfew on working hours in order to make drastic changes to the car.
</p><p>This extra time spent working on the cars worked for Vettel because he then topped the leaderboard with a lap of 1:21.168, Alonso's fastest lap was 1:21.468 and Button clocked a lap off 1:21.639, Lewis Hamilton who topped the timings in the first two practices could only manage a 1:22.667, the seventh fastest time.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Q1 saw the three newest teams fall out again – the two closely matched <a href="/wiki/Hispania_Racing" class="mw-redirect" title="Hispania Racing">HRTs</a> of <a href="/wiki/Vitantonio_Liuzzi" title="Vitantonio Liuzzi">Vitantonio Liuzzi</a> and then <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> sandwiched by the Virgin cars of <a href="/wiki/J%C3%A9r%C3%B4me_d%27Ambrosio" title="Jérôme d&#39;Ambrosio">Jérôme d'Ambrosio</a> and <a href="/wiki/Timo_Glock" title="Timo Glock">Glock</a>. Jarno Trulli returned to his <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus_(2010%E2%80%9311)" title="Team Lotus (2010–11)">Lotus</a> car and praised the new power steering system, despite being out-qualified by teammate <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a>. <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9bastien_Buemi" title="Sébastien Buemi">Sébastien Buemi</a> was the only man to qualify on the softer compound tyres and ended up in 18th on track, but would start 23rd due to his five-place grid penalty. Buemi decided to not use any of his three sets of super-soft tyres in qualifying to save them for the race.
</p><p>In the second qualifying session, every driver set a lap on the option tyres except two. <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> still managed to take his <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> into Q3 with the sixth fastest Q2 time on the prime tyres, and <a href="/wiki/Pastor_Maldonado" title="Pastor Maldonado">Pastor Maldonado</a>, who did not set a timed lap. It was notable that <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a> and <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> needed to use options when Hamilton could use primes. <a href="/wiki/Jaime_Alguersuari" title="Jaime Alguersuari">Jaime Alguersuari</a> – a man who had come from far back to score points a few times in <a href="/wiki/2011_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2011 Formula One World Championship">2011</a>, like his teammate did at this weekend set the slowest Q2 time and dropped out on the same row as <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Williams_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams F1">Williams</a>. Neither <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a> made the top 10, <a href="/wiki/Vitaly_Petrov" title="Vitaly Petrov">Vitaly Petrov</a> in 12th and <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> in 14th. One <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a> and one <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a> failed to make the cut – <a href="/wiki/Kamui_Kobayashi" title="Kamui Kobayashi">Kamui Kobayashi</a> (13th) joined Heidfeld on row 7, and similarly <a href="/wiki/Paul_di_Resta" title="Paul di Resta">Paul di Resta</a> just missed out with 11th.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a> opted not to go out in the third part of qualifying, to save tyres and started tenth, alongside the <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> of <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>, who was again beaten by teammate <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a>, who qualified seventh. <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a> continued his improved form to start eighth. Only the 3 big teams chose to do two timed runs and they occupied the top six places. <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> had a good lap, breaking the 1 minute, 20 seconds barrier for the first time that weekend. After they had all done their first stint, Hamilton was fastest with reigning world champion and championship leader <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> only slightly slower. Vettel improved his lap time on his second run, and took his eighth pole of the season. Hamilton joined him on the front row, ahead of Button, Massa, Alonso and Webber.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Formula_1_Hungarian_Grand_Prix_2011_(4).JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/43/Formula_1_Hungarian_Grand_Prix_2011_%284%29.JPG/220px-Formula_1_Hungarian_Grand_Prix_2011_%284%29.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/43/Formula_1_Hungarian_Grand_Prix_2011_%284%29.JPG/330px-Formula_1_Hungarian_Grand_Prix_2011_%284%29.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/43/Formula_1_Hungarian_Grand_Prix_2011_%284%29.JPG/440px-Formula_1_Hungarian_Grand_Prix_2011_%284%29.JPG 2x" data-file-width="4320" data-file-height="3240" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Formula_1_Hungarian_Grand_Prix_2011_(4).JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The race started under wet conditions with all the drivers starting on intermediate tyres</div></div></div>
<p>After mixed conditions wreaked havoc in the <a href="/wiki/2011_Hungarian_GP2_round" class="mw-redirect" title="2011 Hungarian GP2 round">support races</a>, the Grand Prix started with a wet surface. At the start of the race, the track was described as, "not very wet, but slippery" by <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a>; this meant that it would be much more difficult to get heat into the tyres at the start of the race. This also provided dilemmas for the team's engineers, as the conditions were not wet enough to use the full wet tyres, which would disintegrate very quickly if used, while the dry tyres were presumed to not give enough grip. This meant that all of the drivers started on intermediates.
</p><p>Vettel easily established a lead into the first corner, with Lewis Hamilton following in second after spending the first two corners successfully defending from Button in third. The Ferraris and Mark Webber struggled to get off the line on a damp track, the Mercedes cars of Rosberg and Michael Schumacher were fourth and fifth. Alonso and Webber dropped down to sixth and seventh, with Felipe Massa down in eighth followed by Paul di Resta and Kamui Kobayashi.
</p><p>Massa took seventh from Webber early on the first lap, while Michael Schumacher and Fernando Alonso battled throughout the rest of the lap for fifth, culminating with Alonso passing Schumacher at the last corner. The Ferraris continued their recovery, as Alonso quickly dealt with Rosberg and closed in on Button, but he ran wide at Turn 3, allowing Rosberg to reclaim the position. Massa passed Schumacher, with Webber also following him through. Lewis Hamilton made repeated attempts to pass Vettel around Turns 1 and 2. The World Champion was able to hold him off until the fifth lap when he made a mistake and went wide at Turn 3, handing the lead to Hamilton, and allowing Jenson Button to catch up. Hamilton, now on a clear track was released and started to pull away from the rest of the field and was 5.5 seconds clear after ten laps. Alonso had by then taken fourth from Rosberg again, whereas his teammate Massa spun off on the slippery conditions, rejoining down in 9th. Jarno Trulli become the first retirement with a water leak.
</p><p>As the track began to dry out, Webber was the first to pit for slick tyres and immediately set quick sector times. Button changed to slicks a lap later, but the leading duo of Hamilton and Vettel as well as Alonso stayed out a lap longer. Hamilton was unaffected due to the lead he had built, but Vettel and Alonso conceded places to Button and Webber who were able to pass them before they had fully heated up their slick tyres. After the entire field had pitted for slicks, the order at the front was Hamilton leading Button, followed by the Red Bulls of Vettel and Webber with Alonso, Rosberg, di Resta, Schumacher, Massa and Kamui Kobayashi completing the top ten.
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Formula_1_Hungarian_Grand_Prix_(12)(cropped).JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/79/Formula_1_Hungarian_Grand_Prix_%2812%29%28cropped%29.JPG/220px-Formula_1_Hungarian_Grand_Prix_%2812%29%28cropped%29.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="224" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/79/Formula_1_Hungarian_Grand_Prix_%2812%29%28cropped%29.JPG/330px-Formula_1_Hungarian_Grand_Prix_%2812%29%28cropped%29.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/79/Formula_1_Hungarian_Grand_Prix_%2812%29%28cropped%29.JPG/440px-Formula_1_Hungarian_Grand_Prix_%2812%29%28cropped%29.JPG 2x" data-file-width="2560" data-file-height="2604" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Formula_1_Hungarian_Grand_Prix_(12)(cropped).JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> won on his 200th GP start, at the scene of his <a href="/wiki/2006_Hungarian_Grand_Prix" title="2006 Hungarian Grand Prix">maiden victory</a> in <a href="/wiki/2006_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2006 Formula One World Championship">2006</a>, ahead of the championship leader <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>; Button's race engineer, Dave Robson, standing between Button and Vettel, collected the Constructors' Trophy.</div></div></div>
<p>Hamilton continued to extend his lead, building up a 9-second gap to Button by lap 20 while Button seemed to be preoccupied in holding back Vettel. However, then Button steadily began to close in on Hamilton whilst simultaneously pulling away from Vettel. Hamilton went to the pits for a new set of tyres, and was still comfortably ahead after Button and Vettel had made their respective stops. While Webber and Alonso fought for fourth, Michael Schumacher and Felipe Massa battled for eighth until Schumacher retired with a gearbox problem.
</p><p>Alonso made an extremely early third pitstop for a new set of super-softs in the hopes of emerging onto a clear circuit in an attempt to undercut Webber, but Webber responded by staying out longer and opting for the soft tyres during his stop. Even though this dropped him behind Alonso, it ensured that if the race stayed dry, he would not need to pit again, unlike Alonso. Button and Vettel also chose soft tyres, but Hamilton followed Alonso's suit and ran on the super-softs which meant that he, too, would need a further stop to make it to the finish. The race was interrupted by a brief shower a few laps later, making the circuit slippery again. Hamilton spun at Turn 8, forcing Paul di Resta off the circuit and allowing Button to catch up. Hamilton and Button traded places several times, with the leader being given the first choice for pit strategy. Hamilton prevailed, and elected to return to the intermediate tyre compound. However, the circuit started to dry out again within two laps, forcing Hamilton to make another stop for dry tyres. He was also given a drive-through penalty for forcing Paul di Resta off the circuit. Elsewhere, Heikki Kovalainen become the fourth and final retirement for the same reason as his teammate, giving Lotus Racing another double failure to finish.
</p><p>This left Button leading from Vettel, as neither had pitted for the intermediates, while Hamilton dropped down to sixth behind Felipe Massa and Webber. Hamilton moved up to fifth when Massa made his final scheduled stop. Hamilton and Webber fought for fourth place, and as the two passed a massively congested midfield pack, Webber got stuck behind one of the backmarkers, allowing Hamilton the opportunity he needed to take fourth. Over half a minute in front of them, Alonso started closing in on Vettel at a rate that meant second place was a possibility by the end of the race, but then suffered another spin and he was forced to settle for third.
</p><p>Jenson Button went on to win the race, his 200th Grand Prix start and the scene of his <a href="/wiki/2006_Hungarian_Grand_Prix" title="2006 Hungarian Grand Prix">maiden victory</a> ahead of Vettel in second and Alonso. Hamilton was fourth from Webber, whilst Massa – who had out-qualified Alonso for the first time since the <a href="/wiki/2010_Belgian_Grand_Prix" title="2010 Belgian Grand Prix">2010 Belgian Grand Prix</a> – was the final car classified on the lead lap in sixth place.  It was the fifth race in a row the driver who started in eighteenth place had gone on to score points when Buemi finished eighth. Di Resta triumphed over a midfield battle for seventh, with Rosberg dropping down to ninth behind <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9bastien_Buemi" title="Sébastien Buemi">Sébastien Buemi</a>, whose teammate <a href="/wiki/Jaime_Alguersuari" title="Jaime Alguersuari">Jaime Alguersuari</a> took the final point, whereas Kamui Kobayashi's tyre-saving strategy failed to work and left him outside the points.
</p>
