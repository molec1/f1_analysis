<p>The <b>2010 Hungarian Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>XXVI Eni Magyar Nagydíj</b>) was the twelfth round of the <a href="/wiki/2010_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2010 Formula One season">2010 Formula One season</a>. It was held in <a href="/wiki/Hungaroring" title="Hungaroring">Hungaroring</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hungary" title="Hungary">Hungary</a> on 1 August 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> claimed his fourth victory of the season, and reclaimed the championship lead after <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>'s retirement from the race.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Pedro_de_la_Rosa" title="Pedro de la Rosa">Pedro de la Rosa</a> scored his last ever World Championship points at this race.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Sakon_Yamamoto" title="Sakon Yamamoto">Sakon Yamamoto</a> continued to replace <a href="/wiki/Karun_Chandhok" title="Karun Chandhok">Karun Chandhok</a> at <a href="/wiki/Hispania_Racing" class="mw-redirect" title="Hispania Racing">Hispania Racing</a>, alongside <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Senna" title="Bruno Senna">Bruno Senna</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The drivers' representative on the stewards' panel was <a href="/wiki/Derek_Warwick" title="Derek Warwick">Derek Warwick</a> for the second time this season, after he was in the role at the <a href="/wiki/2010_Spanish_Grand_Prix" title="2010 Spanish Grand Prix">Spanish Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Free_practice">Free practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Free practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a> continued their on-track dominance in the first session, with both <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> setting lap times over a second faster than their nearest challenger, the <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a> of <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> was the first driver to take to the circuit, where he was observed to take the stretch between the third and fourth turn – the scene of <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa#Injury" title="Felipe Massa">his injury</a> <a href="/wiki/2009_Hungarian_Grand_Prix" title="2009 Hungarian Grand Prix">in 2009</a> – quite slowly. After <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari's</a> dominant display in <a href="/wiki/2010_German_Grand_Prix" title="2010 German Grand Prix">Germany</a>, the Italian team was noticeably off the pace in Budapest, with <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> placing seventh and Massa finishing ninth at the end of the first session. At <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> led the way with the fourth-fastest time whilst <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> was crippled with an undisclosed technical glitch and frustrations with the balance of his car that left him eighteenth and the last driver ahead of the new teams, who were once again led by <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a>. The circuit's notoriously dusty surface triggered several spins with the most notable being <a href="/wiki/Vitaly_Petrov" title="Vitaly Petrov">Vitaly Petrov</a> who lost control in the final bend and found himself splayed across the entrance to the front straight, though he was able to return to the pits. <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a>, who were running test driver <a href="/wiki/Paul_di_Resta" title="Paul di Resta">Paul di Resta</a> in place of <a href="/wiki/Vitantonio_Liuzzi" title="Vitantonio Liuzzi">Vitantonio Liuzzi</a> were fined <a href="/wiki/Euro" title="Euro">€</a>5,000 for another tyre mix-up when di Resta used a set of tyres that were a part of Liuzzi's Saturday allocation.
</p><p>The second session was once again led by Vettel and Red Bull, though the gap to the rest of the field was considerably smaller, with Alonso half a second adrift and Webber in third ahead of Massa. The most notable performance came from Petrov who, despite his earlier spin and a time two seconds down on the leaders, placed fifth overall, just a second behind Vettel and a tenth of a second faster than teammate Kubica. Lewis Hamilton recovered from his technical problems and found a setup he was happier with to take sixth. As with the first session, the second remained incident-free. <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> was forced to sit out the bulk of the ninety minutes with hydraulics problems – a recurrent theme for all of the new teams – while Alonso tore a chunk out of one of the kerbs late in the session. Several drivers ran off the road, notably at turn seven, whilst <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a> struggled with an electrical fault.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Heavy rain was expected to interrupt the session, and while clouds started to materialise over the final few corners of the circuit, the hour-long session remained free of rain. Red Bull were once again the early pace-setters, with pundits and commentators expecting the fight for pole to be between Vettel and Webber and possibly Alonso, the only driver who had been in touch with them all weekend. The fight to avoid elimination was intense, with several drivers including Michael Schumacher, Jenson Button and Adrian Sutil exchanging fastest laps to avoid eighteenth position and join the Virgin, Lotus and Hispania drivers in the knockout zone. In the end, <a href="/wiki/Kamui_Kobayashi" title="Kamui Kobayashi">Kamui Kobayashi</a> suffered the ignominy of being eliminated in Q1; although he had been on a flying lap at the very end of the first period that, based on sector times, would have been enough to keep his qualifying session alive, he cruelly encountered traffic in the final sequence of bends. With his lap compromised, he aborted his run and returned to the pits. In his frustration he missed the scrutineering weighbridge completely, and was later given a five-place grid penalty for the transgression, shunting him back to the last row of the grid alongside <a href="/wiki/Sakon_Yamamoto" title="Sakon Yamamoto">Sakon Yamamoto</a>. Elsewhere, <a href="/wiki/Timo_Glock" title="Timo Glock">Timo Glock</a> was the fastest of the new teams; the last car to set a time in the first period, he upstaged <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> by just a few hundredths of a second.
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Jenson_Button_Hungary_2010.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/56/Jenson_Button_Hungary_2010.jpg/220px-Jenson_Button_Hungary_2010.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="243" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/56/Jenson_Button_Hungary_2010.jpg/330px-Jenson_Button_Hungary_2010.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/56/Jenson_Button_Hungary_2010.jpg/440px-Jenson_Button_Hungary_2010.jpg 2x" data-file-width="852" data-file-height="941" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Jenson_Button_Hungary_2010.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> qualified his <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> in eleventh position.</div></div></div>
<p>Where Vettel claimed the fastest time of the first period, Mark Webber was the fastest in the second, with Alonso once again in third. Renault's Vitaly Petrov was the big surprise, taking fourth place and proving that his pace in the practice sessions was genuine. Although the gap between Red Bull and everyone else remained constant at a second, it was a hard-fought battle for the minor placings as several drivers struggled to promote themselves to the final qualifying period. <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9bastien_Buemi" title="Sébastien Buemi">Sébastien Buemi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jaime_Alguersuari" title="Jaime Alguersuari">Jaime Alguersuari</a> were busy taking fastest times out of one another, but the real fight was once again for the final places in the next period, with Button, <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Williams_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams F1">Williams</a> drivers of <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a> and fighting to stay alive. Where Hülkenberg and Rosberg slipped through, Button could only manage eleventh and his former teammate Barrichello twelfth, with the reigning World Champion missing the cut by just two thousandths of a second. Adrian Sutil was able to take thirteenth alongside countryman Michael Schumacher, with Buemi, an anonymous <a href="/wiki/Vitantonio_Liuzzi" title="Vitantonio Liuzzi">Vitantonio Liuzzi</a> and Alguersuari filling up the lower midfield places.
</p><p>In the third and final session, it was reported that Petrov, <a href="/wiki/Pedro_de_la_Rosa" title="Pedro de la Rosa">Pedro de la Rosa</a> and Hülkenberg had not been expecting to advance to the final period, and were therefore out of fresh sets of the softer option tyres. Petrov and de la Rosa both opted for a one-run strategy to preserve their tyres, as did <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a> in the second Renault. Hülkenberg elected to run in two stints, but as in Germany, he discovered that his tyres were too worn to set a competitive time by the end of the session. De la Rosa experienced similar difficulties, taking ninth from Hülkenberg by just two thousandths of a second. At the business end of the grid, Sebastian Vettel set his fastest time yet, which even he could not beat on his second lap even though it was still good enough for pole, whilst Mark Webber could not improve much from his Q2 time. By the end of the session, Red Bull were once again first and second, giving the team eleven pole positions from twelve attempts in <a href="/wiki/2010_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2010 Formula One World Championship">2010</a>. Vettel himself was over a second faster than Alonso, with Webber falling just shy of the enviable mark. These times, combined with footage broadcast by <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_Group#Formula_One_Management" title="Formula One Group">Formula One Management</a> that showed both the Red Bulls and Ferraris running with front wings that were notably closer to the road than their rivals, re-ignited the debate over flexible front wings, with <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes GP</a> in particular approaching the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">FIA</a> for clarification as to the rules.
</p><p>Elsewhere, <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> took advantage of Jenson Button's early Q2 exit to qualify fifth behind <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a>, the 2008 World Champion looking to secure a good result and put as much space between himself and his teammate as possible in the championship standings. Nico Rosberg endured the hour-long session despite coming dangerously close to elimination on two occasions to line up alongside Hamilton in sixth, whilst Vitaly Petrov became the twelfth and final man to out-qualify his teammate in 2010. An early mistake on Kubica's first flying lap saw the Pole struggle, and while his second run was quicker, Petrov hit back on used rubber to take seventh by a tenth of a second at the end of the session.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<table class="box-More_citations_needed_section plainlinks metadata ambox ambox-content ambox-Refimprove" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-image"><div style="width:52px"><a href="/wiki/File:Question_book-new.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/50px-Question_book-new.svg.png" decoding="async" width="50" height="39" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/75px-Question_book-new.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/99/Question_book-new.svg/100px-Question_book-new.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="512" data-file-height="399" /></a></div></td><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This section <b>needs additional citations for <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability">verification</a></b>.<span class="hide-when-compact"> Please help <a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=2010_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit">improve this article</a> by <a href="/wiki/Help:Referencing_for_beginners" title="Help:Referencing for beginners">adding citations to reliable sources</a>. Unsourced material may be challenged and removed.</span>  <small class="date-container"><i>(<span class="date">November 2011</span>)</i></small><small class="hide-when-compact"><i> (<a href="/wiki/Help:Maintenance_template_removal" title="Help:Maintenance template removal">Learn how and when to remove this template message</a>)</i></small></div></td></tr></tbody></table>
<p>Several drivers predicted that "up to seventy percent" of the final race result would be settled in the first corner, owing to the tight and twisty nature of the Hungaroring circuit. Despite the pace of the Red Bulls in qualifying, the third-placed Fernando Alonso was able to muscle his way into second position ahead of Mark Webber by the first turn. The other big movers were Vitaly Petrov, who passed Lewis Hamilton for fifth at the first corner - though he conceded the place to the 2008 World Champion the next lap when he found he could not get heat into his tyres - and Kamui Kobayashi, who surged from 23rd on the grid to 16th. Jaime Alguersuari's race was over after just one lap, with the Ferrari engine in his Toro Rosso spewing coolant fluid across the first sector of the circuit without warning.
</p><p>Sebastian Vettel streaked away from Alonso and Webber at a rate of almost a second a lap, at least partially confirming the idea that the team would have an incredibly easy race. But a disruption occurred on lap fifteen when Vitantonio Liuzzi made contact with an unidentified driver and lost his front wing at turn eleven. This prompted the deployment of the safety car and a string of pit stops. Vettel narrowly made the pit entry at the last moment. Problems began further down the order when Nico Rosberg left his garage with a loose right-rear tyre. The tyre came free almost straight away and bounced down the pit lane through the <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a> pit box, and striking a mechanic, who was bruised. Renault were able to pit Vitaly Petrov and release him to the circuit in sequence, but the Renault lollipop man released Robert Kubica into the path of Adrian Sutil, who at the time was attempting to enter his own pit box immediately following the Renault pit. The collision end Sutil's participation. Renault themselves were fined US$50,000 for an unsafe pit release, and Kubica was served with a ten-second stop-go penalty for causing an avoidable pit lane accident.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Alonso_webber_2010_hungarian_gp.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/63/Alonso_webber_2010_hungarian_gp.jpg/220px-Alonso_webber_2010_hungarian_gp.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/63/Alonso_webber_2010_hungarian_gp.jpg/330px-Alonso_webber_2010_hungarian_gp.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/63/Alonso_webber_2010_hungarian_gp.jpg/440px-Alonso_webber_2010_hungarian_gp.jpg 2x" data-file-width="720" data-file-height="480" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Alonso_webber_2010_hungarian_gp.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> pits while <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> stays on track. Webber was able to pass Alonso after making his pit stop later in the race compared to Alonso.</div></div></div>
<p>While most of the leaders pitted in the three laps the safety car was on the circuit, Webber remained a notable exception. Having passed Vettel when the German was in the pits, Webber was now the first on the road. Red Bull began formulating a strategy so that the Australian could pit and rejoin the circuit ahead of Alonso. Vettel was later issued a drive-through penalty after violating Article 40.9 of the Sporting Regulations.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Vettel fell more than ten car lengths behind the safety car, and he was penalised. Like the controversial penalty given to Lewis Hamilton in <a href="/wiki/2010_European_Grand_Prix#Post-race" title="2010 European Grand Prix">Valencia</a>, the rule - designed to stop teams from manipulating the field for strategy purposes under safety car conditions - has rarely been broken in the modern era of the sport, and Vettel was visibly angry about being penalised for it, feeling that it had cost him certain victory. He rejoined the circuit behind Alonso in third, and would spend the rest of the race trying to find his way around the Ferrari.
</p><p>At the front of the field, Webber introduced a very simple strategy of driving as fast as he could for as long as he could, intending to dial out enough of a lead over Alonso so that he could pit without risking his position. His strategy, originally designed to secure second place for him was now offering him the very real chance of victory. Elsewhere, Renault elected to retire Kubica's car with suspected damage from the collision with Sutil so as to preserve its physical condition, whilst Lewis Hamilton retired from fourth place with a gearbox problem on the same lap. As the final retirement of the race, Hamilton's exit meant that for the first time in 2010, all three new teams would see both their cars finish the race. Lotus would once again take line honours, with Kovalainen and Trulli finishing ahead of Timo Glock, and the second Virgin of Lucas di Grassi finished between the Hispanias of Senna and Yamamoto.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Alonso_vettel_hungarian_gp_2010.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d5/Alonso_vettel_hungarian_gp_2010.jpg/220px-Alonso_vettel_hungarian_gp_2010.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d5/Alonso_vettel_hungarian_gp_2010.jpg/330px-Alonso_vettel_hungarian_gp_2010.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d5/Alonso_vettel_hungarian_gp_2010.jpg/440px-Alonso_vettel_hungarian_gp_2010.jpg 2x" data-file-width="720" data-file-height="480" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Alonso_vettel_hungarian_gp_2010.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> finished behind <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>Vettel caught Alonso and the pair battled over second while Webber continued to extend his lead. The Australian's task was made much more difficult by the presence of backmarkers on the short, tight circuit, but when he pitted on lap 42, he emerged five seconds ahead of Alonso. The only other major position change came when Rubens Barrichello - the second front-running driver who did not pit as a response to Liuzzi's safety car - made his compulsory stop from fifth position. Williams had been attempting a similar strategy to BMW Sauber with Kamui Kobayashi in Valencia by having Barrichello pit late and charge back up through the field on super-soft tyres. Unlike Webber, Barrichello was unable to preserve his position and slipped down the order to eleventh behind Schumacher. He then attempted to glide through the field with ease, but quickly became trapped behind his former teammate. Things came to a head late in the race when Barrichello was able to get close enough to pass Schumacher on the main straight, but Schumacher attempted to pin Barrichello to the wall and force him to back off. However, Schumacher's move came too late, and Barrichello was already alongside him when the Mercedes driver moved over. Barrichello very nearly made contact with the concrete pit wall and was forced across the pit exit, but took the place from Schumacher, who was placed under investigation by the stewards for dangerous driving. He was later issued a ten-place grid penalty for his actions, to be taken at the next race in <a href="/wiki/2010_Belgian_Grand_Prix" title="2010 Belgian Grand Prix">Belgium</a>.
</p><p>With Webber so far in front, he was able to coast to victory by nearly twenty seconds. Vettel could not find a way past Fernando Alonso and had to settle for third, whilst Felipe Massa was fourth for Ferrari. Petrov and Nico Hülkenberg took their career-best finishes to date with fifth and sixth places respectively, whilst Pedro de la Rosa and Kobayashi in the BMW Saubers were split by a recovering Jenson Button, all a lap down. Barrichello's late move on Schumacher netted tenth and the final points-scoring place for the Brazilian, whilst Schumacher's eleventh meant that Mercedes GP failed to score for the first time in 2010.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-Race">Post-Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Post-Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In response to repeated questions regarding the flexible front wing row that gripped the Budapest paddock, the FIA promised to invoke a clause in the rules and regulations that would allow them to place greater stresses on the front wings of both the Red Bull and Ferrari cars at the <a href="/wiki/2010_Belgian_Grand_Prix" title="2010 Belgian Grand Prix">Belgian Grand Prix</a>.
</p><p>Schumacher's aggressive driving in attempting to prevent Barrichello from overtaking drew strong criticism from commentators and former drivers, including triple World Champion <a href="/wiki/Niki_Lauda" title="Niki Lauda">Niki Lauda</a> and former Schumacher teammates <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a> and <a href="/wiki/Martin_Brundle" title="Martin Brundle">Martin Brundle</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Triple World Champion <a href="/wiki/Jackie_Stewart" title="Jackie Stewart">Jackie Stewart</a> commented "We are never more than a millimetre away from something awful happening and for Schumacher to do what he did with Rubens Barrichello is just inviting disaster. ... It was one of the most blatant abuses of another driver that I have seen. It is a terrible example from a man who has seven world titles, bully-boy tactics."<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>  Schumacher publicly defended his actions after the race and in the Stewards' hearing, but did subsequently admit that he was wrong and apologised to Barrichello.  <a href="/wiki/Derek_Warwick" title="Derek Warwick">Derek Warwick</a>, one of the stewards for the race, commented that Schumacher could have been shown the <a href="/wiki/Racing_flag#Black_flag" class="mw-redirect" title="Racing flag">black flag</a>, resulting in an instant disqualification, had there been more time left in the race for the stewards to review the video evidence.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>  Schumacher was shown a black flag earlier in his career, at the <a href="/wiki/1994_British_Grand_Prix" title="1994 British Grand Prix">1994 British Grand Prix</a>, for ignoring a 5-second stop-and-go penalty; he received a two race ban for ignoring the black flag and continuing to race rather than returning to the pits as instructed.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
