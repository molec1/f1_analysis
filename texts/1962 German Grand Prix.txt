<p>The <b>1962 German Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held at the <a href="/wiki/N%C3%BCrburgring" title="Nürburgring">Nürburgring</a> on 5 August 1962. It was race 6 of 9 in both the <a href="/wiki/1962_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1962 World Championship of Drivers">1962 World Championship of Drivers</a> and the <a href="/wiki/1962_International_Cup_for_Formula_One_Manufacturers" class="mw-redirect" title="1962 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers">1962 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers</a>. The 15-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/British_Racing_Motors" title="British Racing Motors">BRM</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Graham_Hill" title="Graham Hill">Graham Hill</a> after he started from second position. <a href="/wiki/John_Surtees" title="John Surtees">John Surtees</a> finished second for the <a href="/wiki/Lola_Cars" title="Lola Cars">Lola</a> team and <a href="/wiki/Porsche_in_motorsport" title="Porsche in motorsport">Porsche</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Dan_Gurney" title="Dan Gurney">Dan Gurney</a> came in third. The race was notable for having six different constructors taking the first six positions.<sup id="cite_ref-IMS962_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IMS962-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1962_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:1962-08-05_Graham_Hill,_Surtees,_Gurney_-_Hatzenbach.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/82/1962-08-05_Graham_Hill%2C_Surtees%2C_Gurney_-_Hatzenbach.jpg/220px-1962-08-05_Graham_Hill%2C_Surtees%2C_Gurney_-_Hatzenbach.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/82/1962-08-05_Graham_Hill%2C_Surtees%2C_Gurney_-_Hatzenbach.jpg/330px-1962-08-05_Graham_Hill%2C_Surtees%2C_Gurney_-_Hatzenbach.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/82/1962-08-05_Graham_Hill%2C_Surtees%2C_Gurney_-_Hatzenbach.jpg/440px-1962-08-05_Graham_Hill%2C_Surtees%2C_Gurney_-_Hatzenbach.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1121" data-file-height="747" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:1962-08-05_Graham_Hill,_Surtees,_Gurney_-_Hatzenbach.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Graham_Hill" title="Graham Hill">Graham Hill</a>, leading <a href="/wiki/John_Surtees" title="John Surtees">Surtees</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dan_Gurney" title="Dan Gurney">Gurney</a>. These are the positions in which the race ended.</div></div></div>
<p>After a heavy midday downpour, the race was delayed by over an hour as streams of water and mud covered parts of the track. It never dried fully, and the race was run in wet conditions.<sup id="cite_ref-IMS32_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IMS32-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Graham_Hill" title="Graham Hill">Graham Hill</a> drove masterfully in the wet conditions, followed by <a href="/wiki/John_Surtees" title="John Surtees">John Surtees</a> who was gradually proving himself a great driver.<sup id="cite_ref-IMS32_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IMS32-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He reached third position in the championship with this race, but was not to score any more points in 1962. <a href="/wiki/Dan_Gurney" title="Dan Gurney">Gurney</a>'s Porsche had less than impressive handling but he finished third after having passed <a href="/wiki/Phil_Hill" title="Phil Hill">Phil Hill</a>, whose Ferrari was doing much better than at <a href="/wiki/1962_British_Grand_Prix" title="1962 British Grand Prix">Aintree</a>. Hill, however, had to pit with oil on his visor and retired with a broken rear suspension soon thereafter. Back after a disastrous strike had kept them out of the last two races Ferrari had shown up in force with four <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_156_F1" title="Ferrari 156 F1">156s</a> built to different specifications. Hill had the newest version, with a six-speed transmission mounted fore of the engine. <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Baghetti" title="Giancarlo Baghetti">Giancarlo Baghetti</a> drove a car with the usual transmission and finished tenth, whereas <a href="/wiki/Ricardo_Rodr%C3%ADguez_(racing_driver)" title="Ricardo Rodríguez (racing driver)">Ricardo Rodríguez</a> drove last year's model with the 65 degree Tipo 188 engine - and got the best result of the team, with a sixth. <a href="/wiki/Lorenzo_Bandini" title="Lorenzo Bandini">Lorenzo Bandini</a> used a development car, with a regular nosecone, smaller radiator, and modified front and rear suspension. He crashed on the third lap, while in eleventh position.<sup id="cite_ref-IMS25_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IMS25-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Jim_Clark" title="Jim Clark">Jim Clark</a> absentmindedly forgot to turn on the fuel pump at the start, losing thirteen seconds and being in 26th place after the start. A rapid climb began, and he passed no less than seventeen cars on the opening lap. He was closing in to the leaders with three to four seconds per lap, but after a few near crashes near the middle of the race he chose to ease off the pace a bit.<sup id="cite_ref-IMS962_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IMS962-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Clark finished fourth, ahead of <a href="/wiki/Bruce_McLaren" title="Bruce McLaren">Bruce McLaren</a> in a V8 Cooper. The other V8-engined Cooper was driven in practice by <a href="/wiki/Tony_Maggs" title="Tony Maggs">Tony Maggs</a>, but a German TV-company's camera fell off <a href="/wiki/Carel_Godin_de_Beaufort" title="Carel Godin de Beaufort">de Beaufort</a>'s Porsche in practice, causing Graham Hill and Maggs to crash and total their cars.<sup id="cite_ref-IMS25_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IMS25-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Maggs ran a <a href="/wiki/Coventry_Climax" title="Coventry Climax">Climax</a>-engined backup car and finished ninth. The <a href="/wiki/Grand_Prix_Drivers%27_Association" title="Grand Prix Drivers&#39; Association">Grand Prix Drivers' Association</a>'s policy was to not carry cameras due to the safety risks, but de Beaufort was not a member.<sup id="cite_ref-IMS32_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IMS32-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Three new cars appeared - the new BRM V8-engined <a href="/wiki/Gilby_Engineering" title="Gilby Engineering">Gilby</a>, driven by <a href="/wiki/Keith_Greene" title="Keith Greene">Keith Greene</a>, retired after about half the race with gearbox problems. The Belgian Maserati-engined <a href="/wiki/Ecurie_Nationale_Belge" title="Ecurie Nationale Belge">ENB</a> finished last; this car was a hodgepodge of parts from three old <a href="/wiki/Emeryson" title="Emeryson">Emerysons</a> equipped with a sharknose-style front end. This was its only appearance, and a hard worked <a href="/wiki/Lucien_Bianchi" title="Lucien Bianchi">Lucien Bianchi</a> was only allowed to start thanks to the fact that several faster racers had not finished the minimum-required five laps. Gurney's fastest qualifying lap was 8:47.2; the ENB's fastest lap was 10:40.7, nearly two minutes slower.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Most importantly, <a href="/wiki/Jack_Brabham" title="Jack Brabham">Jack Brabham</a>'s new <a href="/wiki/Brabham_BT3" title="Brabham BT3">BT3</a> finally appeared after a marathon effort by his mechanics. He spun the <a href="/wiki/Main_bearing" title="Main bearing">main bearings</a> on the first day practice, and qualified with an engine built using parts from <a href="/wiki/Trevor_Taylor_(racing_driver)" title="Trevor Taylor (racing driver)">Trevor Taylor</a>'s car (his engine bent a valve). He started the race from the rear of the field, with the Climax engine from his <a href="/wiki/Lotus_24" title="Lotus 24">Lotus 24</a>. He climbed to ninth place by the end of the first lap, but then his throttle broke and he had to retire after nine laps. Nonetheless, Brabham was happy with the car, particularly the handling.<sup id="cite_ref-IMS25_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IMS25-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
