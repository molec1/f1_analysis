<p>The <b>1968 South African Grand Prix</b>, formally the <b>2nd AA Grand Prix of South Africa</b> (<a href="/wiki/Afrikaans" title="Afrikaans">Afrikaans</a>: <i>Tweede AA Suid-Afrikaanse Grand Prix</i>), was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held at <a href="/wiki/Kyalami" title="Kyalami">Kyalami</a> Circuit on Monday 1 January 1968. It was race 1 of 12 in both the <a href="/wiki/1968_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1968 World Championship of Drivers">1968 World Championship of Drivers</a> and the <a href="/wiki/1968_International_Cup_for_Formula_One_Manufacturers" class="mw-redirect" title="1968 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers">1968 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers</a>. The 80-lap race was won by two time <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">World Drivers' Champion</a> and <a href="/wiki/1965_Indianapolis_500" title="1965 Indianapolis 500">1965 Indianapolis 500</a> winner <a href="/wiki/Jim_Clark" title="Jim Clark">Jim Clark</a> for <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Lotus</a>-<a href="/wiki/Ford_Motor_Company" title="Ford Motor Company">Ford</a> after starting from pole position. The race is significant as not only the last Formula One race to be won by Clark, but also the last in which he ever competed, due to his <a href="/wiki/1968_Deutschland_Troph%C3%A4e" title="1968 Deutschland Trophäe">fatal crash</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Hockenheimring" title="Hockenheimring">Hockenheimring</a> in Germany three months later. At this race <a href="/wiki/Team_Gunston" title="Team Gunston">Team Gunston</a> became the first Formula One team to paint their cars in the <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_sponsorship_liveries" title="Formula One sponsorship liveries">livery of their sponsors</a> when they entered a <a href="/wiki/Privateer_(motorsport)" title="Privateer (motorsport)">private</a> <a href="/wiki/Brabham" title="Brabham">Brabham</a> for <a href="/wiki/John_Love_(racing_driver)" title="John Love (racing driver)">John Love</a><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> and an <a href="/wiki/LDS_(automobile)" title="LDS (automobile)">LDS</a> for <a href="/wiki/Sam_Tingle" title="Sam Tingle">Sam Tingle</a>.
</p><p>The first six slots on the grid were filled by either previous or future world champions. However the reigning champion, <a href="/wiki/Denny_Hulme" title="Denny Hulme">Denny Hulme</a>, only started in ninth.
</p><p>This was also <a href="/wiki/Mike_Spence" title="Mike Spence">Mike Spence</a>'s final race, as he too was killed a few months later, while practising for the <a href="/wiki/1968_Indianapolis_500" title="1968 Indianapolis 500">1968 Indianapolis 500</a>.
</p><p>Clark broke many records during the weekend, such as leading the most Grands Prix (43), having the most laps led (1,943), having the most <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_driver_records#Pole,_win_and_fastest_lap_in_same_race" title="List of Formula One driver records">perfect weekends</a> (11), achieving the most pole positions (33) and finally achieving 25 race wins, beating <a href="/wiki/Juan_Manuel_Fangio" title="Juan Manuel Fangio">Juan Manuel Fangio</a>'s 11-year-old record.
</p>
