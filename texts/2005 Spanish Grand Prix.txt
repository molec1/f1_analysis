<p>The <b>2005 Spanish Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race, held on 8 May 2005 at <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Catalunya" class="mw-redirect" title="Circuit de Catalunya">Circuit de Catalunya</a> in <a href="/wiki/Barcelona" title="Barcelona">Barcelona</a>,  <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> started on pole position ahead of <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a>, who set the fastest lap during the second qualifying session to claim the front row, leaving championship leader <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> on the second row in third.  Right from the start Räikkönen started pulling away at a blistering pace, setting the fastest lap thirteen times in only twenty four laps.  Alonso's tyres wore down and he was forced to take care of them and come home in second place, never taking the lead from Räikkönen.
</p><p>Webber had a dismal start, losing second and then third to Alonso and <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a>, respectively.  After the first round of pitstops he lost three positions and had to battle his way back to finish in sixth.  Alonso's teammate <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> moved around constantly in the field, but managed to end up in fifth from sixth on the grid.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> had a good race, ending up on the podium in third ahead of his teammate Ralf Schumacher, who fell behind after the first round of pitstops.  Meanwhile, Räikkönen's teammate <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a> had an average race, moving around in the field but still only managing a seventh place.  It was his return from a two-race absence to recover from a shoulder injury. <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> claimed the final points-paying position in eighth after starting from ninth.
</p><p>Some of the retirements included <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>, whose tyre failed on lap 47, his third retirement of the year.  <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a> also had to retire on lap 52 after a water leak resulted in his engine overheating.
</p><p>This was Räikkönen's first victory of 2005.
</p>
