<p>The <b>2013 German Grand Prix</b> (formally known as the <b>Formula 1 Großer Preis Santander von Deutschland 2013</b>)<sup id="cite_ref-formal_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-formal-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Auto_racing" title="Auto racing">motor race</a> that was held on 7 July 2013 at the <a href="/wiki/N%C3%BCrburgring" title="Nürburgring">Nürburgring</a> in <a href="/wiki/N%C3%BCrburg" title="Nürburg">Nürburg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_calender_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA_calender-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> as the ninth round of the <a href="/wiki/2013_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2013 Formula One season">2013 season</a>.
</p><p>It marked the 74th running of the <a href="/wiki/German_Grand_Prix" title="German Grand Prix">German Grand Prix</a> overall, and the 60th running of the German Grand Prix since 1950, when the racing series now known as the Formula One World Championship was created. This is the earliest a German Grand Prix has been held in a calendar year, followed by the <a href="/wiki/1926_German_Grand_Prix" title="1926 German Grand Prix">1926</a> and the <a href="/wiki/2009_German_Grand_Prix" title="2009 German Grand Prix">2009</a> editions of the race, though the <a href="/wiki/1996_European_Grand_Prix" title="1996 European Grand Prix">1996 European Grand Prix</a> was held at the Nürburgring in late April.
</p><p>The 60-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a> after starting from second position on the grid. This was the first home victory for the <a href="/wiki/German_people" class="mw-redirect" title="German people">German</a> driver. <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> finished second for <a href="/wiki/Lotus_F1_Team" class="mw-redirect" title="Lotus F1 Team">Lotus</a>. Räikkönen's teammate <a href="/wiki/Romain_Grosjean" title="Romain Grosjean">Romain Grosjean</a> finished third. A charging <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> finished fourth and also set the fastest lap driving a <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>. <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>, who started from pole position driving a <a href="/wiki/Mercedes_AMG_Petronas" class="mw-redirect" title="Mercedes AMG Petronas">Mercedes</a>, ended up finishing 5th, only a second ahead of his former teammate <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As a result of the race, <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> increased his championship lead to 34 points from <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>. <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a>'s second place moved him just 7 points behind Alonso in the standings. <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> remained fourth, while <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> also remained in 5th spot. This was also <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a>'s first points finish since <a href="/wiki/2013_Spanish_Grand_Prix" title="2013 Spanish Grand Prix">Spain</a>, and also for his teammate <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a>, since <a href="/wiki/2013_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="2013 Monaco Grand Prix">Monaco</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> This is the last Formula One Grand Prix to be held at the <a href="/wiki/N%C3%BCrburgring" title="Nürburgring">Nürburgring</a>, though the circuit is due to return to calendar to host the <a href="/wiki/Eifel_Grand_Prix" class="mw-redirect" title="Eifel Grand Prix">Eifel Grand Prix</a> in 2020.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2013_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tyre_changes">Tyre changes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2013_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Tyre changes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the aftermath of the <a href="/wiki/2013_British_Grand_Prix" title="2013 British Grand Prix">British Grand Prix</a>, in which four drivers experienced explosive punctures at some of the fastest points of the <a href="/wiki/Silverstone_Circuit" title="Silverstone Circuit">Silverstone Circuit</a>, tyre supplier <a href="/wiki/Pirelli" title="Pirelli">Pirelli</a> announced plans to alter the structure of the tyres for the German Grand Prix, ahead of a more-permanent solution to be introduced at the next race in <a href="/wiki/2013_Hungarian_Grand_Prix" title="2013 Hungarian Grand Prix">Hungary</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
The new tyres replaced the steel belts used in the rear wheels with <a href="/wiki/Kevlar" title="Kevlar">Kevlar</a> in order to lower the operating temperature by up to 10&#160;°C (18&#160;°F) and reduce the likelihood of explosive punctures.<sup id="cite_ref-kevlar_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kevlar-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In addition to this, the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile</a> (FIA) banned the practice of swapping the left- and right-side tyres around on the cars, which teams had discovered increased the life expectancy of each tyre.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Pirelli further lobbied to the FIA to make their recommendations for tyre pressure and camber settings to be made mandatory, after it was discovered that teams were deliberately using pressure and camber settings outside Pirelli's recommendations in order to maximise performance.<sup id="cite_ref-kevlar_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kevlar-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> This followed a similar incident at the <a href="/wiki/2011_Belgian_Grand_Prix" title="2011 Belgian Grand Prix">2011 Belgian Grand Prix</a>, where teams used camber settings outside Pirelli's recommendations, which created blistering on the inside edge of the tyres as they experienced higher-than-normal operating temperatures.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2013_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Q1">Q1</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2013_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Q1">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p><a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> was the fastest driver in Q1, ahead of <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a>'s Lotus. The drivers eliminated in Q1, were <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pastor_Maldonado" title="Pastor Maldonado">Pastor Maldonado</a>, <a href="/wiki/Charles_Pic" title="Charles Pic">Charles Pic</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jules_Bianchi" title="Jules Bianchi">Jules Bianchi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Giedo_van_der_Garde" title="Giedo van der Garde">Giedo van der Garde</a> and <a href="/wiki/Max_Chilton" title="Max Chilton">Max Chilton</a>.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Q2">Q2</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2013_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Q2">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>This session saw a rapid improvement in times in the closing stages which caught some teams out, such as <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> being eliminated due to a mistake on the part of the <a href="/wiki/Mercedes_AMG_Petronas" class="mw-redirect" title="Mercedes AMG Petronas">Mercedes</a> team and <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> only barely making it into Q3 by five one-hundredths of a second <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> was fastest again ahead of <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> again as well. <a href="/wiki/Jean-%C3%89ric_Vergne" title="Jean-Éric Vergne">Jean-Éric Vergne</a> was eliminated, as well as <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a>, <a href="/wiki/Esteban_Guti%C3%A9rrez" title="Esteban Gutiérrez">Esteban Gutiérrez</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a> and <a href="/wiki/Paul_di_Resta" title="Paul di Resta">Paul di Resta</a>,.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Q3">Q3</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2013_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Q3">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p><a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> took pole position with <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> in second and third, respectively.  <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Romain_Grosjean" title="Romain Grosjean">Romain Grosjean</a> qualified 4th and 5th. Alonso qualified 8th behind his teammate <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> and both opted to start the race on the medium tyre. <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> managed to qualify on the 3rd row for the second race in succession. <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a> elected not to set a time, saving their tyres for the race.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2013_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Both <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Webber</a> and Vettel had strong starts applying immediate pressure to Hamilton. Hamilton responded by trying to push Vettel off the racing line on the inside of the track which then opened the door for Webber to try to make a move on the outside, but by the end of turn one Vettel had managed to shake off Hamilton and take the race lead, with Webber moving into second and Hamilton dropping down to third. <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> also had a good start, gaining a few places. Alonso fell back slightly as did Hamilton and <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a>. Vettel kept the lead for the next few laps. Meanwhile, Massa, running in sixth place, went off the track at turn one on lap 3 after locking his rear brakes. The engine stalled and he retired from the race, caused by a gearbox failure. <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> meanwhile, who had a disastrous qualifying, barely improved on his position in the first 5 laps.
</p><p>From laps 5 to 9, drivers were pitting due to using worn softs from qualifying to switch to the medium compound tyres, with Grosjean and Räikkönen going longer than most drivers and also setting a very quick pace. Grosjean led the race before stopping on lap 13 and continued to show strong pace afterwards. Webber, on the other hand, pitted a lap later and had a serious incident with his rear-right wheel. The mechanics did not attach the wheel properly and as Webber left his pitbox, the wheel fell off and rolled down the pit lane. It hit and knocked over a cameraman, who was transported to a hospital in <a href="/wiki/Koblenz" title="Koblenz">Koblenz</a> where he was reported to have suffered broken bones and a concussion. Webber's Red Bull was pushed back to his pitbox where a new wheel was fitted and he rejoined the race, a lap behind Vettel. The two Lotuses had never lost touch with Vettel during the first half of the race and were keeping pace with him, with both Grosjean and Räikkönen setting fastest laps of the race.
</p><p>On lap 23, <a href="/wiki/Jules_Bianchi" title="Jules Bianchi">Jules Bianchi</a>'s Marussia suffered an engine failure which resulted in a fire. He pulled to the left-hand side of the track but, unusually, his car rolled back across the track to the other side where it was stopped by an advertising board. This caused an instant safety car, which allowed Webber to get back on to the lead lap. Most of the drivers pitted for fresh tyres. <a href="/wiki/Jean-%C3%89ric_Vergne" title="Jean-Éric Vergne">Jean-Éric Vergne</a>'s Toro Rosso suffered a hydraulics failure less than two laps after Bianchi's engine failure, during the safety car period. He was able to return to the pits. His team-mate <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> was struggling. He had qualified sixth but was slowly falling back into the field. <a href="/wiki/Paul_di_Resta" title="Paul di Resta">Paul di Resta</a> and <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a> of Force India also struggled during the first half of the race.
</p><p>After the safety car period, Webber began to make his way through the field before becoming stuck behind the Sauber of <a href="/wiki/Esteban_Guti%C3%A9rrez" title="Esteban Gutiérrez">Esteban Gutiérrez</a>. He was unable to pass the Mexican for more than ten laps and pitted to try to get an advantage with fresh tyres and clean air. Meanwhile, the McLaren drivers were once again having a tussle, with <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> getting past <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a> at turn one. <a href="/wiki/Pastor_Maldonado" title="Pastor Maldonado">Pastor Maldonado</a>, driving in the 600th grand prix race for the <a href="/wiki/Williams_F1_Team" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams F1 Team">Williams F1 Team</a>, was in the points in eighth, along with his team-mate and rookie <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a> in tenth. Räikkönen was leading on lap 41 after Vettel and Grosjean had pitted for the third time. Lotus waited until lap 49 to pit Räikkönen, switching him to the soft tyre, which left Vettel and Grosjean in first and second, with Alonso a threat from behind in fourth place. Grosjean was then told on the radio to not hold Räikkönen up, for championship title points reasons. It took a few laps for Räikkönen to pass Grosjean and afterwards he attempted to chase down Vettel. Alonso set the fastest lap of the race, almost a second faster than the first three drivers, and was rapidly catching Grosjean. Meanwhile, Hamilton made a move on his former team-mate Button at turn two, moving into fifth. Webber then made a move on Pérez to go into seventh.
</p><p>Räikkönen was unable to catch Vettel, who took the victory. It was his first victory at his home race and the first victory he gained in his birthday month, July. It was also his 30th career victory, becoming the 6th driver to achieve such a feat and in just his 110th start. Räikkönen finished just one second behind, with Grosjean only just holding off Alonso for third place, the latter having used too much of his soft tyres while closing down the gap to Grosjean. The podium placings were the same as at the <a href="/wiki/2012_Bahrain_Grand_Prix" title="2012 Bahrain Grand Prix">2012</a> and the <a href="/wiki/2013_Bahrain_Grand_Prix" title="2013 Bahrain Grand Prix">2013 Bahrain Grand Prix</a>. Hamilton, Button, Webber and Pérez remained fifth, sixth, seventh and eighth respectively. Rosberg, struggling to keep his Mercedes on the pace, finished ninth at his home race while <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a> took the final point for Sauber. di Resta finished eleventh, the first time he had finished outside the points during the season, with Ricciardo in twelfth, <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a> in thirteenth and Gutiérrez in fourteenth. Williams had a poor ending to their milestone race, with Maldonado and Bottas finishing fifteenth and sixteenth respectively after wheel gun problems in their pitstops. <a href="/wiki/Charles_Pic" title="Charles Pic">Charles Pic</a>, his team-mate <a href="/wiki/Giedo_van_der_Garde" title="Giedo van der Garde">Giedo van der Garde</a> and <a href="/wiki/Max_Chilton" title="Max Chilton">Max Chilton</a> were the final finishers.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post_Race">Post Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2013_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Post Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After the race Red Bull and Force India were given fines for incidents in the pits.
</p><p>Red Bull were fined €30,000 after the right-rear tyre from Mark Webber's car became detached as the Australian left his pit box. The errant tyre subsequently bounced down the pit lane before striking FOM cameraman Paul Allen. Allen, who remained conscious after the incident, was treated at the circuit's medical centre before being helicoptered to Koblenz Hospital for observation.
</p><p>Force India, meanwhile, were given a €5,000 fine after releasing Paul di Resta into the path of Toro Rosso's Jean-Éric Vergne.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
