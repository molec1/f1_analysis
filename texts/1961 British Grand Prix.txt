<p>The <b>1961 British Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race, held on 15 July 1961 at the <a href="/wiki/Aintree_Motor_Racing_Circuit" title="Aintree Motor Racing Circuit">Aintree Circuit</a>, near <a href="/wiki/Liverpool" title="Liverpool">Liverpool</a>. It was race 5 of 8 in both the <a href="/wiki/1961_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1961 World Championship of Drivers">1961 World Championship of Drivers</a> and the <a href="/wiki/1961_International_Cup_for_Formula_One_Manufacturers" class="mw-redirect" title="1961 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers">1961 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers</a>. 
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Anchor_Crossing,_British_Grand_Prix,_1961_geograph-2741605-by-John-Goldsmith.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a2/Anchor_Crossing%2C_British_Grand_Prix%2C_1961_geograph-2741605-by-John-Goldsmith.jpg/220px-Anchor_Crossing%2C_British_Grand_Prix%2C_1961_geograph-2741605-by-John-Goldsmith.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="150" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a2/Anchor_Crossing%2C_British_Grand_Prix%2C_1961_geograph-2741605-by-John-Goldsmith.jpg/330px-Anchor_Crossing%2C_British_Grand_Prix%2C_1961_geograph-2741605-by-John-Goldsmith.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a2/Anchor_Crossing%2C_British_Grand_Prix%2C_1961_geograph-2741605-by-John-Goldsmith.jpg/440px-Anchor_Crossing%2C_British_Grand_Prix%2C_1961_geograph-2741605-by-John-Goldsmith.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1932" data-file-height="1314" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Anchor_Crossing,_British_Grand_Prix,_1961_geograph-2741605-by-John-Goldsmith.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Jo Bonnier in his Porsche passing Anchor Crossing</div></div></div>  
<p>Following a wet weekend, with torrential rain affecting both qualifying and the race start, the Grand Prix was ultimately dominated by <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Scuderia Ferrari</a>, with their drivers taking all three podium positions. The race was won by German <a href="/wiki/Wolfgang_Graf_Berghe_von_Trips" class="mw-redirect" title="Wolfgang Graf Berghe von Trips">Wolfgang von Trips</a>, who had led for much of the race after starting from fourth place on the grid. This was von Trips's second but also his final Grand Prix victory as two races later he was killed in an accident during the <a href="/wiki/1961_Italian_Grand_Prix" title="1961 Italian Grand Prix">1961 Italian Grand Prix</a>. <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">Pole position</a> winner <a href="/wiki/Phil_Hill" title="Phil Hill">Phil Hill</a> drove to second place, on his way to winning the World Drivers' Championship at the end of the season, and third place was taken by Hill's American compatriot <a href="/wiki/Richie_Ginther" title="Richie Ginther">Richie Ginther</a>.
</p><p>The 1961 British Grand Prix is also notable as being the first occasion on which a <a href="/wiki/Four-wheel_drive_in_Formula_One" title="Four-wheel drive in Formula One">four-wheel drive</a> car, and the last at which a <a href="/wiki/FMR_layout" class="mw-redirect" title="FMR layout">front engined</a> car was entered for a World Championship race. These two accomplishments were achieved by the same vehicle: the experimental <a href="/wiki/Ferguson_P99" title="Ferguson P99">Ferguson P99</a>-<a href="/wiki/Coventry_Climax" title="Coventry Climax">Climax</a> run by the <a href="/wiki/Rob_Walker_Racing_Team" title="Rob Walker Racing Team">Rob Walker Racing Team</a>. Although the car was disqualified for receiving assistance on the track, in the hands of <a href="/wiki/Stirling_Moss" title="Stirling Moss">Stirling Moss</a> – who took over the car from first driver <a href="/wiki/Jack_Fairman" title="Jack Fairman">Jack Fairman</a> after his own <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Lotus's</a> brakes failed – it showed some promise. The 1961 British Grand Prix also marked the last occasion on which Moss contested a Grand Prix race on home soil, as his career was ended by an accident during a non-championship race prior to the 1962 season.
</p>
