<p>The <b>2005 Belgian Grand Prix</b> (formally the 2005 Formula 1 Belgian Grand Prix) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 11 September 2005 at <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Spa-Francorchamps" title="Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps">Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps</a> in <a href="/wiki/Spa,_Belgium" title="Spa, Belgium">Spa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a>. It was the sixteenth race of the <a href="/wiki/2005_FIA_Formula_One_World_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="2005 FIA Formula One World Championship">2005 FIA Formula One World Championship</a> and the 62nd <a href="/wiki/Belgian_Grand_Prix" title="Belgian Grand Prix">Belgian Grand Prix</a>.
</p><p>The 44-lap race was won by Finnish driver <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4-20" title="McLaren MP4-20">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Mercedes_AMG_High_Performance_Powertrains" title="Mercedes AMG High Performance Powertrains">Mercedes</a>, after he started from second position. Räikkönen's Colombian teammate, <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a>, took <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> and led until his second pit stop on lap 33; he then maintained second place until a late collision with Brazilian driver <a href="/wiki/Ant%C3%B4nio_Pizzonia" title="Antônio Pizzonia">Antônio Pizzonia</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW27" title="Williams FW27">Williams</a>-<a href="/wiki/BMW_in_Formula_One" title="BMW in Formula One">BMW</a>. Spaniard <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> thus took second in his <a href="/wiki/Renault_R25" title="Renault R25">Renault</a>, with Englishman <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> third in a <a href="/wiki/BAR_007" title="BAR 007">BAR</a>-<a href="/wiki/Honda_in_Formula_One" title="Honda in Formula One">Honda</a>.
</p><p>With three races remaining, Alonso led the Drivers' Championship by 25 points from Räikkönen, needing only six more to clinch the title. In the Constructors' Championship, McLaren reduced Renault's lead to six points.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2005_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2005_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Before the race, Renault were leading the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a> with 144 points and McLaren were second with 136 points, with Ferrari third on 58 points. In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">World Drivers' Championship</a>, Renault driver <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> was leading with 103 points; <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> was second on 76 points, 27 points behind Alonso. Behind Alonso and Räikkönen in the Drivers' Championship, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> was third on 55 points in a Ferrari, with <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> on 50 and 43 points respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice_and_qualifying">Practice and qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2005_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Practice and qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Four practice sessions were held before the Sunday race—two on Friday from 11:00 to 12:00 and 14:00 to 15:00 local time, with the final two sessions held on Saturday morning between 09:00 to 09:45 and 10:15 to 11:00.<sup id="cite_ref-f1regulations_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1regulations-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Saturday's afternoon qualifying session took place as a one-lap session held between 13:00 and 14:00. Drivers went out one at a time in the reverse order of their finishing positions at the previous race. The cars ran on the fuel that would be used for the Sunday race.<sup id="cite_ref-f1regulations_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1regulations-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> started the race from 13th place – the result of a <a href="/wiki/2005_Formula_One_season#Engine_life" class="mw-redirect" title="2005 Formula One season">ten-place grid penalty</a> given for an engine change between final practice and qualifying on the Saturday.<sup id="cite_ref-Autosport_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Autosport-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2005_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:332px;"><a href="/wiki/File:F1_Raikkonen_2005_Spa.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7e/F1_Raikkonen_2005_Spa.jpg/330px-F1_Raikkonen_2005_Spa.jpg" decoding="async" width="330" height="106" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7e/F1_Raikkonen_2005_Spa.jpg/495px-F1_Raikkonen_2005_Spa.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7e/F1_Raikkonen_2005_Spa.jpg/660px-F1_Raikkonen_2005_Spa.jpg 2x" data-file-width="900" data-file-height="290" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:F1_Raikkonen_2005_Spa.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> won the race for the second consecutive year for <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>The race took place in the afternoon from 14:00 local time. On lap 14, <a href="/wiki/Takuma_Sato" title="Takuma Sato">Takuma Sato</a> hit <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>'s car from behind, causing both to retire.<sup id="cite_ref-Autosport_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Autosport-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Ant%C3%B4nio_Pizzonia" title="Antônio Pizzonia">Antônio Pizzonia</a> crashed into <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a>—in second position at the time—shortly before the finish of the race.<sup id="cite_ref-Autosport_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Autosport-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a> was able to finish sixth by virtue of a one-stop pit strategy, while other drivers stopped as many as five times.
</p><p>This race saw the <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a> team score their final point, courtesy of <a href="/wiki/Tiago_Monteiro" title="Tiago Monteiro">Tiago Monteiro</a>, while the <a href="/wiki/British_American_Racing" title="British American Racing">BAR</a> team achieved their last podium finish through <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a>. Also, <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> scored his last, and <a href="/wiki/Toyota_Racing_(Formula_One_team)" title="Toyota Racing (Formula One team)">Toyota</a>'s first, <a href="/wiki/Fastest_lap" title="Fastest lap">fastest lap</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2005_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The race stewards ruled that Sato had caused the collision with Schumacher, and he would consequently drop ten places on the grid for the next Grand Prix in <a href="/wiki/2005_Brazilian_Grand_Prix" title="2005 Brazilian Grand Prix">Brazil</a>. They also fined Pizzonia $8,000 for his collision with Montoya.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Unusually, McLaren did not send a representative to the podium to collect the constructors' trophy, so, Räikkönen accepted it on behalf of the team.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
