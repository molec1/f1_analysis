<p>The <b>1990 Belgian Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Spa-Francorchamps" title="Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps">Spa-Francorchamps</a> on 26 August 1990. It was the eleventh race of the <a href="/wiki/1990_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1990 Formula One World Championship">1990 Formula One World Championship</a>. The race was the 48th <a href="/wiki/Belgian_Grand_Prix" title="Belgian Grand Prix">Belgian Grand Prix</a>. It was the 36th to be held at Spa-Francorchamps and the seventh since the circuit was extensively redeveloped in 1979. The race was held over 44 laps of the seven kilometre circuit for a race distance of 301 kilometres.
</p><p>Brazilian driver <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> claimed his fifth race victory for the season in his <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/5B" class="mw-redirect" title="McLaren MP4/5B">McLaren MP4/5B</a> taking a four-second win over the <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_641" title="Ferrari 641">Ferrari 641</a> driven the reigning world champion, Frenchman <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a>. It was Senna's fourth victory in the Belgian Grand Prix, and his third in succession. Senna's Austrian team mate <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> finished third, while further back <a href="/wiki/Maur%C3%ADcio_Gugelmin" title="Maurício Gugelmin">Maurício Gugelmin</a> scored a point for the <a href="/wiki/Leyton_House" class="mw-redirect" title="Leyton House">Leyton House</a> team.
</p><p>Three attempts were made to start the race, the first two being red-flagged after collisions. <a href="/wiki/Aguri_Suzuki" title="Aguri Suzuki">Aguri Suzuki</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Lola_LC90" title="Lola LC90">Lola LC90</a> was eliminated after the first start, <a href="/wiki/Paolo_Barilla" title="Paolo Barilla">Paolo Barilla</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Minardi_M190" title="Minardi M190">Minardi M190</a> after the second.
</p><p>Off-track, <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> was rumoured to be reconsidering his prior announcement to retire, and he was strongly linked with Williams. He had demanded undisputed number one status as a driver, and various assurances from engine supplier <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>, which <a href="/wiki/Frank_Williams_(Formula_One)" title="Frank Williams (Formula One)">Frank Williams</a> had told him were impossible.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Onyx_Grand_Prix" title="Onyx Grand Prix">Onyx</a> team pulled out of Formula One before the weekend of the race, for financial reasons, having competed at this level for a season and a half.<sup id="cite_ref-Walker_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Walker-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1990_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-qualifying_report">Pre-qualifying report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1990_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Pre-qualifying report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>With the withdrawal of the <a href="/wiki/Onyx_Grand_Prix" title="Onyx Grand Prix">Onyx</a> team, <a href="/wiki/Equipe_Ligier" title="Equipe Ligier">Ligier</a> were relieved of the requirement to pre-qualify, and returned to the main qualifying pool. They had only been required to pre-qualify twice, which both cars did comfortably on both occasions.
</p><p>Therefore there was slightly less pressure on the other entrants in the pre-qualifying session. <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Grouillard" title="Olivier Grouillard">Olivier Grouillard</a> was fastest in the <a href="/wiki/Osella" title="Osella">Osella</a>, the second time he had been top of the Friday morning time sheets, and the first time since round six in <a href="/wiki/1990_Mexican_Grand_Prix" title="1990 Mexican Grand Prix">Mexico</a>. Second fastest was <a href="/wiki/Yannick_Dalmas" title="Yannick Dalmas">Yannick Dalmas</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Automobiles_Gonfaronnaises_Sportives" title="Automobiles Gonfaronnaises Sportives">AGS</a>, his fifth successful pre-qualification of the season from eleven attempts. Third fastest was <a href="/wiki/Bertrand_Gachot" title="Bertrand Gachot">Bertrand Gachot</a> in the improving <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Coloni" title="Scuderia Coloni">Coloni</a>, and this was the first time in 1990 that he had pre-qualified. Fourth was <a href="/wiki/Gabriele_Tarquini" title="Gabriele Tarquini">Gabriele Tarquini</a> in the other AGS, his fourth pre-qualification this season.<sup id="cite_ref-Walker_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Walker-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Missing the cut in fifth and sixth positions were the <a href="/wiki/EuroBrun" title="EuroBrun">EuroBruns</a> of <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Moreno" title="Roberto Moreno">Roberto Moreno</a> and <a href="/wiki/Claudio_Langes" title="Claudio Langes">Claudio Langes</a>. The margin of failure was fairly narrow however, with Moreno 0.36 seconds behind Tarquini, and Langes just over a second further behind. <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Giacomelli" title="Bruno Giacomelli">Bruno Giacomelli</a> was slowest again in the <a href="/wiki/Life_Racing_Engines" title="Life Racing Engines">Life</a>, with the L190 refusing to start until the closing stages of the session. The car managed five slow laps before an electrical failure. "Nothing impressive," Giacomelli stated. He also announced that work was restarting on the chassis to allow a <a href="/wiki/Judd_(engine)" title="Judd (engine)">Judd</a> CV V8 engine to be fitted by round 13 in <a href="/wiki/1990_Portuguese_Grand_Prix" title="1990 Portuguese Grand Prix">Portugal</a>, in place of the disastrous in-house <a href="/wiki/W12_engine" title="W12 engine">W12</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-MN30/8_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MN30/8-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1990_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_report">Race report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1990_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Race report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The first start had seen two incidents. The first of these incidents was when <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a> had pushed the <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_641" title="Ferrari 641">Ferrari 641</a> of <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> off the track. Whilst the second of these incidents was when the <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_Racing" title="Tyrrell Racing">Tyrrell</a> of <a href="/wiki/Satoru_Nakajima" title="Satoru Nakajima">Satoru Nakajima</a> collided with the <a href="/wiki/Brabham" title="Brabham">Brabham</a> of <a href="/wiki/Stefano_Modena" title="Stefano Modena">Stefano Modena</a>, which caused the race to be stopped.
</p><p>The second start had seen Senna's <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> take the lead with the Williams of <a href="/wiki/Thierry_Boutsen" title="Thierry Boutsen">Thierry Boutsen</a> slicing his way into second place from fourth, whilst rival <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> had dropped down to fifth place after starting third. However, there was another first lap accident when the <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a> of <a href="/wiki/Paolo_Barilla" title="Paolo Barilla">Paolo Barilla</a> crashed heavily at Eau Rouge which left debris all over the track in that area, which caused the race to be stopped again.
</p><p>The third start however had proven to be cleaner, as Senna held on to the lead ahead of teammate <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a>, Prost, Boutsen, Patrese and <a href="/wiki/Alessandro_Nannini" title="Alessandro Nannini">Alessandro Nannini</a> in the leading <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a>. Mansell in the second <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> went into the pits on lap 11 with handling problems, although he was able to rejoin but retired eight laps later as a result. Meanwhile back upfront, Prost took second ahead of Berger but then pitted for tyres. The margin between Senna and Prost was only just two seconds when they made their second pit-stops for fresh tyres. Both the Williams cars of Patrese and Boutsen were soon forced to retire with gearbox problems by lap 22. The pit-stops of Senna and Prost had allowed Nannini to take second place (who was once again planning to go the full race distance without a pit stop). Prost was able to overtake Nannini for second, Berger attempted to do the same thing but was unable to pass until lap 41 when Nannini ran wide at La Source, which allowed Berger to take third place from the Italian. <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> took his fifth win of the season by 3.5 seconds over Prost, whilst the rest of the top six were rounded out by teammate Berger, Nannini, Piquet in the second <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mauricio_Gugelmin" class="mw-redirect" title="Mauricio Gugelmin">Mauricio Gugelmin</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Leyton_House_Racing" title="Leyton House Racing">Leyton House</a>.
</p>
