<p>The <b>2009 Hungarian Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>XXV ING Magyar Nagydíj</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 26 July 2009 at the <a href="/wiki/Hungaroring" title="Hungaroring">Hungaroring</a> in <a href="/wiki/Mogyor%C3%B3d" title="Mogyoród">Mogyoród</a>, 18&#160;km (11&#160;mi) north of <a href="/wiki/Budapest" title="Budapest">Budapest</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hungary" title="Hungary">Hungary</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> It was the tenth race of the <a href="/wiki/2009_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2009 Formula One season">2009 Formula One season</a>. The 70-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/2008_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2008 Formula One World Championship">2008</a> world champion <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> for <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_HighPerformanceEngines" class="mw-redirect" title="Mercedes-Benz HighPerformanceEngines">Mercedes</a>, after starting from fourth place on the grid. The <a href="/wiki/2007_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2007 Formula One World Championship">2007</a> world champion <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> finished second for <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> finishing third.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Championship leader <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> had a poor race to finish seventh, losing ground to Webber in the championship.
</p><p>Hamilton's win was his first since the <a href="/wiki/2008_Chinese_Grand_Prix" title="2008 Chinese Grand Prix">2008 Chinese Grand Prix</a>, which was also the last time he had appeared on the podium. The result also marked the first time that a car equipped with a <a href="/wiki/Regenerative_brake" title="Regenerative brake">regenerative brake</a> system (KERS) had won a race. Räikkönen's second place was Ferrari's best result of the season so far, but his teammate <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> suffered a serious accident during the second part of the Saturday afternoon qualifying session which left him with a fractured skull. The race also saw the debut of <a href="/wiki/World_Series_by_Renault" class="mw-redirect" title="World Series by Renault">World Series by Renault</a> racer and <a href="/wiki/2008_British_Formula_Three_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2008 British Formula Three season">2008 British Formula Three</a> champion <a href="/wiki/Jaime_Alguersuari" title="Jaime Alguersuari">Jaime Alguersuari</a>, who became the youngest Formula One driver in the championship's 59-year history, at the age of 19 years and 125 days, and the first to be born in the 1990s. He replaced the fired <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9bastien_Bourdais" title="Sébastien Bourdais">Sébastien Bourdais</a> at <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Scuderia Toro Rosso</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Telegraph_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Telegraph-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2009_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Heading into the weekend, <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> led the Drivers' Championship on 68 points for <a href="/wiki/Brawn_GP" title="Brawn GP">Brawn GP</a>, 21 points clear of <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a>. Vettel's teammate <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a>, who signed a new one-year contract before the race weekend,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> was 1.5 points behind Vettel and an equal number ahead of Button's teammate <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-tables_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tables-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Brawn GP also held the lead in the Constructors' Championship, with a lead of 19.5 points from a resurgent Red Bull Racing, who were a further 48 points clear of <a href="/wiki/Toyota_Racing_(Formula_One_team)" title="Toyota Racing (Formula One team)">Toyota</a>. Toyota held an advantage of only 1.5 points over defending champions <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Scuderia Ferrari</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-tables_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tables-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Webber won the previous race in <a href="/wiki/2009_German_Grand_Prix" title="2009 German Grand Prix">Germany</a> from pole position, to claim his first win at the 130th attempt. Despite an early drive-through penalty assessed for a dangerous manoeuvre to keep the Brawn GP car of Barrichello behind him at the start, the Australian driver had enough time in hand to rejoin out front and win by nearly ten seconds from Red Bull Racing teammate Vettel.<sup id="cite_ref-foxsportswebber_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-foxsportswebber-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatright" style="width:40%;&#32;;"><style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r887775652">.mw-parser-output .quotebox{background-color:#F9F9F9;border:1px solid #aaa;box-sizing:border-box;padding:10px;font-size:88%;max-width:100%}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft{margin:0.5em 1.4em 0.8em 0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright{margin:0.5em 0 0.8em 1.4em}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.centered{margin:0.5em auto 0.8em auto}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft p,.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright p{font-style:inherit}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-title{background-color:#F9F9F9;text-align:center;font-size:larger;font-weight:bold}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:before{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" “ ";vertical-align:-45%;line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:after{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" ” ";line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .left-aligned{text-align:left}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .right-aligned{text-align:right}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .center-aligned{text-align:center}.mw-parser-output .quotebox cite{display:block;font-style:normal}@media screen and (max-width:360px){.mw-parser-output .quotebox{min-width:100%;margin:0 0 0.8em!important;float:none!important}}</style>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">"I don't really care what all the people say, I just do my work. I do what I know, to be honest. I am here because Red Bull just asked me to, as I do Formula 3 or World Series or GP2, I don't care. I just drive the cars. That's my job and that's what I enjoy doing in life. I'm here because Red Bull just sent me a message telling me that I have to drive this car so I'm here. I don't care what other people say, that's the main thing, first of all. And the second thing is that I know what my expectation is, I know what my target is. I know that today I didn't make any mistakes and I hope tomorrow I don't and even in the race and that's how I work at the end. My target here is to drive the car and not make mistakes. Then we will talk about times and about speed. But I don't feel I am a danger, not really."</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style=""><a href="/wiki/Jaime_Alguersuari" title="Jaime Alguersuari">Jaime Alguersuari</a>, who was unfazed by the criticism directed at him for being too inexperienced for Formula One.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> was third, to earn his first podium of the 2009 season to date. Championship leader Button could only muster a fifth-place finish, after being overhauled by the one-stopping Williams of <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a>, who finished fourth having started fifteenth.<sup id="cite_ref-foxsportswebber_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-foxsportswebber-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Prior to the race, the Hungarian Grand Prix had produced different winners for each of the last seven years, with <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> (<a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_HighPerformanceEngines" class="mw-redirect" title="Mercedes-Benz HighPerformanceEngines">Mercedes</a>; <a href="/wiki/2008_Hungarian_Grand_Prix" title="2008 Hungarian Grand Prix">2008</a>), <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> (McLaren–Mercedes; <a href="/wiki/2007_Hungarian_Grand_Prix" title="2007 Hungarian Grand Prix">2007</a>), Button (<a href="/wiki/Honda_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Honda F1">Honda</a>; <a href="/wiki/2006_Hungarian_Grand_Prix" title="2006 Hungarian Grand Prix">2006</a>), <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> (McLaren–Mercedes; <a href="/wiki/2005_Hungarian_Grand_Prix" title="2005 Hungarian Grand Prix">2005</a>), <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> (Ferrari; <a href="/wiki/2004_Hungarian_Grand_Prix" title="2004 Hungarian Grand Prix">2004</a>), <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> (<a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a>; <a href="/wiki/2003_Hungarian_Grand_Prix" title="2003 Hungarian Grand Prix">2003</a>) and Barrichello (Ferrari; <a href="/wiki/2002_Hungarian_Grand_Prix" title="2002 Hungarian Grand Prix">2002</a>) taking the chequered flag. The Hungaroring had also been the scene of the maiden victories of Button, Alonso and Kovalainen.
</p><p>Much of the talk prior to the weekend was about the appointment of <a href="/wiki/Jaime_Alguersuari" title="Jaime Alguersuari">Jaime Alguersuari</a> to replace <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9bastien_Bourdais" title="Sébastien Bourdais">Sébastien Bourdais</a> at <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Scuderia Toro Rosso</a>. Alguersuari did not have any circuit testing experience in a Formula One car, and had only undertaken straight-line aerodynamic tests the week before the Grand Prix, in <a href="/wiki/Faenza" title="Faenza">Faenza</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Felipe Massa felt that Alguersuari—who had 118 prior single-seater races under his belt, with 17 wins<sup id="cite_ref-Telegraph_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Telegraph-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>—was too young and inexperienced to be driving a contemporary Formula One car.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Mark Webber and Jenson Button also expressed their fears about Alguersuari driving at the Hungaroring.<sup id="cite_ref-Alguersuari_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Alguersuari-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> However, Sebastian Vettel came to the support of the Spaniard, saying that he should take his time to build up speed.<sup id="cite_ref-Alguersuari_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Alguersuari-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice_and_qualifying">Practice and qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2009_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Practice and qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatright" style="width:40%;&#32;;"><link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r887775652"/>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">"I’ve got the full upgrade package that Lewis raced at the Nurburgring and the car now feels much better to drive. We’ve got a new floor, new front wing, updated bodywork and some small changes to the suspension. It’s a big package—and I know how hard the team has worked to get everything here. There are still components arriving at the circuit today! The grip level is so much higher now. Stability under braking is much better, and the guys have done a fantastic job to improve the balance at the rear. We still have to keep working hard to make further improvements—but this is a great starting point. I still think we need to look at the data and analyse the strength of the opposition. Of course, it would be fantastic to carry this over into qualifying and the race, but we need to be realistic: I’m sure tomorrow morning will give us a much clearer idea of the position of the Brawns and the Red Bulls. But after today’s performance, I’ll definitely be going for it."</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style=""><a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a>, who topped the first Friday practice session.<sup id="cite_ref-formula1.com-friquotes_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-formula1.com-friquotes-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>Three practice sessions were held before the race; the first was held on Friday morning and the second on Friday afternoon. Both sessions lasted 1&#160;hour and 30&#160;minutes with weather conditions dry throughout, the <a href="/wiki/Air_temperature" class="mw-redirect" title="Air temperature">air temperature</a> at 24&#160;°C (75&#160;°F), and the track temperature at 30&#160;°C (86&#160;°F).<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> for session one. Session two saw the air temperature at 28&#160;°C (82&#160;°F), and the track temperature at 45&#160;°C (113&#160;°F).<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The third session was held on Saturday morning and lasted an&#160;hour, and was also dry throughout,<sup id="cite_ref-sporting_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sporting-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> with the air temperature at 21&#160;°C (70&#160;°F), and the track temperature at 26&#160;°C (79&#160;°F).<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> McLaren were the pace-setters in both Friday practice sessions, with Kovalainen topping the first and Hamilton topping the second.<sup id="cite_ref-P1_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P1-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Apart from the upturn in McLaren's performance, the usual Friday pace-setters were also competitive, with <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a> drivers <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kazuki_Nakajima" title="Kazuki Nakajima">Kazuki Nakajima</a> both setting times that were good enough for top five positions in both sessions.<sup id="cite_ref-P1_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P1-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-P2_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P2-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> The Red Bull of Mark Webber was also in the top five in both sessions, as he continued his recent good form.<sup id="cite_ref-P1_17-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P1-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-P2_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P2-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Brawn GP implemented their usual strategy of running with fuel during Friday practice sessions. In the first session, Jenson Button was tenth and Rubens Barrichello was thirteenth. In the second session, Barrichello improved to seventh place, while Button dropped back to thirteenth. That said, Button's lap time was only 0.727 seconds off the fastest time of Hamilton, in an extremely tight session.<sup id="cite_ref-P2_18-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P2-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> was the top Toyota in both sessions, finishing up sixth and eighth, beating teammate <a href="/wiki/Timo_Glock" title="Timo Glock">Timo Glock</a>, who was fourteenth and tenth. <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a> had a mixed session, with Alonso and <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet,_Jr." class="mw-redirect" title="Nelson Piquet, Jr.">Nelson Piquet, Jr.</a> struggling to get the ideal setup for the car.<sup id="cite_ref-formula1.com-friquotes_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-formula1.com-friquotes-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Alonso was seven tenths of a second off in both sessions, while Piquet improving to just a tenth and half behind his teammate. Massa and Räikkönen posted times good enough for P7 and P8 in the first session, but were adrift in the second session, with neither driver in the top ten, which left the Ferrari drivers disappointed with the lack of pace.<sup id="cite_ref-formula1.com-friquotes_12-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-formula1.com-friquotes-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/BMW_Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="BMW Sauber">BMW Sauber</a> were another team to struggle in practice, with times some six tenths of a second off the pace, which left both drivers unhappy with the setup. <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a>'s time in the second session was their best showing, ending up in ninth position.<sup id="cite_ref-formula1.com-friquotes_12-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-formula1.com-friquotes-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a> outpaced Toro Rosso in both sessions, with both cars lapping within a second of the best times. <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> (16th and 17th) and <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a> (18th and 16th) were both confident that they could improve on those times on Saturday.<sup id="cite_ref-formula1.com-friquotes_12-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-formula1.com-friquotes-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9bastien_Buemi" title="Sébastien Buemi">Sébastien Buemi</a> found himself doing much of the setup work for the Italian outfit, improving his lap time by eight tenths of a second, between sessions. Alguersuari produced a solid performance in both sessions, but still ranked last in both sessions. Each driver completed 82 laps over the two sessions.<sup id="cite_ref-P1_17-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P1-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-P2_18-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P2-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>McLaren again topped the times during the Saturday practice session with Hamilton posting the best time. Heidfeld was a surprise second for BMW Sauber, with Nico Rosberg in third. Kovalainen was fourth, ahead of the Toro Rosso of Buemi and the Toyota of Glock.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Glock's session was far from trouble-free, as his Toyota developed a hydraulic leak during its first flying lap. He returned to the track just before the end of the session, to record the sixth best time.<sup id="cite_ref-Glock_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Glock-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Only seven tenths of a second split Heidfeld in second from Barrichello's Brawn in thirteenth. At one point, Jaime Alguersuari was as high as second position, before falling down to 18th by the end.<sup id="cite_ref-Glock_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Glock-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Both Force Indias lapped slower than the teenager, with Sutil also having an incident, misjudging turn eight and hit the barrier.
</p>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatright" style="width:40%;&#32;;"><link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r887775652"/>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">"It was quite a stressful qualifying to be honest. In Q1 we had the yellow flag from Toro Rosso I think in turn 13, so we were P15 at that moment and we had to get the lap to go through turn one. We did it at the end and then in the end in Q3 as you said the times were not working, so we were waiting in parc ferme, chatting between us drivers, asking what time you did, what time you did and try to find in which position you were. Now confirmation arrived that we are on pole. A fantastic effort from the team. We put some new stuff on the car at the Nürburgring that worked very well and here we confirmed that we made a step forward. But, obviously, it is not enough. We need to keep working but finally we found the direction to go."</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style=""><a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>, after taking <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault's</a> first <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> since the <a href="/wiki/2006_Chinese_Grand_Prix" title="2006 Chinese Grand Prix">2006 Chinese Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-formula1.com-satquotes_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-formula1.com-satquotes-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>The qualifying session on Saturday afternoon was split into three&#160;parts. The first part ran for 20&#160;minutes, and cars that finished the session 16th or lower were eliminated from qualifying. The second part of qualifying lasted 15&#160;minutes and eliminated cars that finished in positions&#160;11 to 15. The final part of qualifying determined the positions from first to tenth, and decided <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-sporting_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sporting-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Cars which failed to make the final session could refuel before the race, so ran lighter in those sessions. Cars which competed in the final session of qualifying were not allowed to refuel before the race, and as such carried more fuel than in the previous sessions.<sup id="cite_ref-sporting_15-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sporting-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Weather conditions for the session saw the air temperature at 23&#160;°C (73&#160;°F), and the track temperature at 41&#160;°C (106&#160;°F).<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Robert_kubica_2009_hungary.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/16/Robert_kubica_2009_hungary.JPG/220px-Robert_kubica_2009_hungary.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/16/Robert_kubica_2009_hungary.JPG/330px-Robert_kubica_2009_hungary.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/16/Robert_kubica_2009_hungary.JPG/440px-Robert_kubica_2009_hungary.JPG 2x" data-file-width="3872" data-file-height="2592" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Robert_kubica_2009_hungary.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a> failed to make it out of the first part of qualifying.</div></div></div>
<p>The first part of qualifying saw Rosberg top the times, with a lap of 1:20.793, towards the end of the session. Hamilton and Webber were the only other drivers to record laps in the 1:20s. Alguersuari, who was 19th with several minutes remaining, slowed out of turn 12 with a hydraulic failure that left him unable to return to the pits. He fell to 20th and last on the grid, after Sutil (who moved up to 18th) lapped quicker than he did.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> The two were joined on the sidelines by the BMW Saubers of Heidfeld and <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a>, and the second Force India of Fisichella.
</p><p>After a seven-minute break, the second part of the qualifying session got underway. With just a second covering the fifteen remaining drivers, many drivers set their quickest laps in the final few minutes of the session. As the session ended, the Ferrari of Massa, who was sixth at the time, was involved in a serious accident at turn four.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Replays showed debris striking his Schuberth helmet,<sup id="cite_ref-delay_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-delay-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> 1 inch above the left eye,<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> when he was travelling at 261&#160;km/h (162&#160;mph).<sup id="cite_ref-Massa_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Massa-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> The impact knocked him unconscious, with his car straying towards the grass verge on the inside of turn four. The onboard footage from his car showed that he had his feet on both the accelerator and the brake pedals. The Ferrari decelerated at a force of between 0.6 and 1.4<a href="/wiki/G-force" title="G-force">g</a> and impacted with the barrier at 100&#160;km/h (62&#160;mph).<sup id="cite_ref-Massa_28-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Massa-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> After the car imbedded itself in the tyre barrier, the engine could be heard still revving on the <a href="/wiki/Rev_limiter" title="Rev limiter">limiter</a>, indicating that Massa's foot was still pressing the accelerator and that he was thus unconscious. This accident came just six days after <a href="/wiki/FIA_Formula_Two_Championship_(2009%E2%80%932012)" title="FIA Formula Two Championship (2009–2012)">FIA Formula Two Championship</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Henry_Surtees" title="Henry Surtees">Henry Surtees</a> was fatally injured after being struck on the head by a wheel bouncing across the circuit at the <a href="/wiki/Brands_Hatch" title="Brands Hatch">Brands Hatch</a> track in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>, and provoked some discussion about the safety of open cockpit racing cars.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> The debris, a spring from the rear suspension damper (weighing around 800&#160;grams),<sup id="cite_ref-800g_spring_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-800g_spring-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> was suspected of having come from the car of Barrichello, who had reported that something had come loose minutes beforehand.<sup id="cite_ref-Qualifying_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Qualifying-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Brawn confirmed the part had indeed come from Barrichello's car, and changed the same part on teammate Button's car ahead of the race.<sup id="cite_ref-800g_spring_30-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-800g_spring-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> It was also subjected to an inspection during the third qualifying session, leaving Button with only one run for qualifying, due to the lengthy inspection.
</p><p>Despite Massa's accident, some drivers improved their times towards the end of the session as they had passed the zone before the accident, meaning that Massa fell to eighth position. Button and Nakajima both progressed into the third part of qualifying, with their times set despite the yellow flag at turn four for Massa's accident. Barrichello's problem meant that he and the Brawn team missed out on the top ten shootout for the first time this season. He was joined on the sidelines by Buemi, Glock, <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet,_Jr." class="mw-redirect" title="Nelson Piquet, Jr.">Nelson Piquet, Jr.</a> and Trulli.<sup id="cite_ref-Qualifying_31-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Qualifying-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Even though Massa's time was quick enough to progress to Q3, his crash ruled him out of any further participation in the session. He was slowly extricated from his damaged Ferrari, and taken to the infield medical centre, where he was stabilised before being airlifted to hospital.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> This meant that there were only nine drivers in the final part of qualifying, which was delayed by twenty minutes due to the lengthy amount of time taken to remove Massa, repair the tyre barrier and check the track for any further debris.<sup id="cite_ref-delay_26-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-delay-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>With qualifying nearing its conclusion, the official timing system malfunctioned,<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> leaving the drivers bemused in the pit lane afterwards, asking each other their times for comparisons.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> The problem was later revealed by <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_Management" class="mw-redirect" title="Formula One Management">Formula One Management</a> and <a href="/wiki/LG_Group" class="mw-redirect" title="LG Group">LG</a> as being a broken cable.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> When the timing system came back online, Fernando Alonso discovered that he had set the fastest time, recording his first pole since the <a href="/wiki/2007_Italian_Grand_Prix" title="2007 Italian Grand Prix">2007 Italian Grand Prix</a>. Joining him on the front row was Vettel, with Webber and Hamilton on row two. On row three were Rosberg, who was top when the system went down, and defending race-winner Kovalainen.<sup id="cite_ref-Qualifying_31-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Qualifying-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> When the cars' starting weights were released post-qualifying, Alonso was light compared to all those around him. He was some 17.5&#160;kg lighter than Vettel, and at the extreme, 27&#160;kg lighter than Button, who qualified eighth.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> latterly confirmed that Massa would miss the Grand Prix due to his accident. He suffered a head cut, bone damage to his skull and a concussion, and remained under observation in intensive care, in the Állami Egészségügyi Központ military hospital in Budapest.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> He underwent successful surgery for the injuries he suffered in his accident.<sup id="cite_ref-800g_spring_30-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-800g_spring-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> This meant that for the first time since the <a href="/wiki/2005_United_States_Grand_Prix" title="2005 United States Grand Prix">2005 United States Grand Prix</a>, a field of fewer than 20 drivers took part in a Grand Prix. It was also the first time that Ferrari fielded only one car in the race since the <a href="/wiki/2002_French_Grand_Prix" title="2002 French Grand Prix">2002 French Grand Prix</a> when Rubens Barrichello failed to start due to ignition problems.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2009_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The conditions on the grid were dry before the race, the <a href="/wiki/Air_temperature" class="mw-redirect" title="Air temperature">air temperature</a> at 26&#160;°C (79&#160;°F), and the track temperature at 43&#160;°C (109&#160;°F).<sup id="cite_ref-commautosport_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-commautosport-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> The formation lap for the race began at 14:00 <a href="/wiki/Central_European_Summer_Time" title="Central European Summer Time">local time</a> (<a href="/wiki/Coordinated_Universal_Time" title="Coordinated Universal Time">UTC</a>+2), with the race beginning a couple of minutes later.<sup id="cite_ref-commautosport_37-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-commautosport-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Alonso retained his pole position lead into the first corner, followed by a fast-starting Hamilton, Webber, Räikkönen and Vettel, who had made a slow start from the "dirty" side of the grid. Hamilton and Räikkönen nearly touched on the run to turn one, which in effect slowed the Finn slightly and allowed Webber to maintain third.<sup id="cite_ref-Brundle_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Brundle-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton ran slightly wide on the exit of the first corner, which allowed Webber to challenge him. The counter-effect caused Räikkönen and Vettel to stack up, with the German's Red Bull making slight contact with the rear of the Ferrari. Vettel lost further places to Rosberg and Kovalainen as a result of the incident.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> Räikkönen would latterly be investigated after the race for both of his first lap incidents.<sup id="cite_ref-Brundle_38-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Brundle-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> At the end of the first lap, Sutil came into the pits from 19th place with water pressure problems.<sup id="cite_ref-hunlapchart_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hunlapchart-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> The problem was terminal and he became the first retirement of the afternoon. Alonso started to pull away from the field, setting his fastest lap of the race (1:23.529) on lap four.<sup id="cite_ref-hungaryFLsummary_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hungaryFLsummary-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Fernando_Alonso_on_three_wheels_during_2009_Hungarian_Grand_Prix.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9c/Fernando_Alonso_on_three_wheels_during_2009_Hungarian_Grand_Prix.jpg/220px-Fernando_Alonso_on_three_wheels_during_2009_Hungarian_Grand_Prix.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9c/Fernando_Alonso_on_three_wheels_during_2009_Hungarian_Grand_Prix.jpg/330px-Fernando_Alonso_on_three_wheels_during_2009_Hungarian_Grand_Prix.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9c/Fernando_Alonso_on_three_wheels_during_2009_Hungarian_Grand_Prix.jpg/440px-Fernando_Alonso_on_three_wheels_during_2009_Hungarian_Grand_Prix.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3872" data-file-height="2592" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Fernando_Alonso_on_three_wheels_during_2009_Hungarian_Grand_Prix.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> returning to the pits, after his <a href="/wiki/Renault_R29" title="Renault R29">Renault</a> shed its right front wheel on lap thirteen.</div></div></div>
<p>Positions remained static until the start of lap five. Hamilton pressured Webber in turn one, and with the aid of the KERS button, overtook the Australian before turn two. Once clear of Webber, Hamilton started to cut into the gap that Alonso had built up at the front. Alonso's Renault was suffering from graining on its rear tyres (when the tyres shed little bits of rubber before sticking to the tread of the tyre, effectively separating the tyre from the track surface very slightly<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup>), and also a fuel pump problem, which forced him to change to a different fuel setting on the car.<sup id="cite_ref-commautosport_37-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-commautosport-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> Alonso <a href="/wiki/Pit_stop#Pit_stops_in_Formula_One" title="Pit stop">pitted</a> on lap twelve, and was stationary for 6.4 seconds. However, he was released with his front right wheel incorrectly attached, causing the wheel-mounted brake duct or "spinner" to rotate while the wheel was turning. The duct then fell off and disintegrated at turn four, as Alonso continued to go round the circuit. The wheel then parted from the car at turn nine, and Alonso drove the car back to the pits on three wheels. The missing wheel was replaced, enabling him to continue in the race, but he returned to the pits on the following lap, as the earlier fuel pump problem proved terminal.
</p><p>With Hamilton extending his lead to almost six seconds—including his fastest lap of 1:22.479 on lap sixteen<sup id="cite_ref-hungaryFLsummary_42-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hungaryFLsummary-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup>—Räikkönen and Webber both pitted on lap nineteen.<sup id="cite_ref-commautosport_37-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-commautosport-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-hungarypitsummary_45-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hungarypitsummary-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> Räikkönen was at a standstill for 8.3 seconds, while a slight problem with the fuel rig cost Webber valuable seconds. He was eventually released just as the Ferrari was trundling down the pit lane.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> The two cars just avoided a collision, which almost caused the Red Bull to career into a number of the Williams mechanics, who were standing in the pit lane at the time. Hamilton pitted the following lap,<sup id="cite_ref-hungarypitsummary_45-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hungarypitsummary-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> but was slightly delayed by Rosberg's Williams, which had entered the pit lane as Hamilton was about to exit the McLaren pit box. This promoted Kovalainen into the lead, followed by Vettel but both drivers pitted at the end of lap 21, to hand the lead back to Hamilton.<sup id="cite_ref-hungarypitsummary_45-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hungarypitsummary-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> Vettel's sluggish pit-stop cost him track position, as he filtered out behind Rosberg.<sup id="cite_ref-commautosport_37-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-commautosport-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen_2009_Hungarian_Grand_Prix.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/24/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen_2009_Hungarian_Grand_Prix.jpg/220px-Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen_2009_Hungarian_Grand_Prix.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="277" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/24/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen_2009_Hungarian_Grand_Prix.jpg/330px-Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen_2009_Hungarian_Grand_Prix.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/24/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen_2009_Hungarian_Grand_Prix.jpg/440px-Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen_2009_Hungarian_Grand_Prix.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2592" data-file-height="3264" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen_2009_Hungarian_Grand_Prix.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> finished in second position in the sole Ferrari.</div></div></div>
<p>During Hamilton's stint on a second set of super-soft tyres, he was told by race engineer Phil Prew to look after his tyres as he had a comfortable gap of 15&#160;seconds over Räikkönen. Championship leader Button, who was heavily fuelled at the start, had improved up to second by the time of his first stop on lap 25.<sup id="cite_ref-hungarypitsummary_45-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hungarypitsummary-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> Vettel was struggling with his car, and was passed for eighth position by Barrichello on lap 27. He then reported to his race engineer Guillaume Rocquelin that something was broken on his car.<sup id="cite_ref-commautosport_37-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-commautosport-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> He pitted at the end of the lap from eleventh position, having lost more places, and his pit crew routinely replaced the nosecone and took a detailed look at the rear suspension.<sup id="cite_ref-commautosport_37-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-commautosport-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-hunlapchart_40-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hunlapchart-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-hungarypitsummary_45-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hungarypitsummary-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> He rejoined a lap down before retiring on lap 30. Debutant Alguersuari made his first Formula One pit stop on lap 28, when running in thirteenth place.<sup id="cite_ref-commautosport_37-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-commautosport-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-hunlapchart_40-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hunlapchart-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-hungarypitsummary_45-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hungarypitsummary-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> Glock also moved into the points-scoring positions at this stage of the race by using the strategy of a long 32-lap first stint.<sup id="cite_ref-commautosport_37-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-commautosport-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-hungarypitsummary_45-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hungarypitsummary-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> Buemi spun at turn two on lap 38, and subsequently rejoined the circuit behind his teammate Alguersuari.<sup id="cite_ref-commautosport_37-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-commautosport-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-hunlapchart_40-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hunlapchart-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Hamilton was left unchallenged for the rest of the race, making his final pit stop on lap 46,<sup id="cite_ref-hungarypitsummary_45-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hungarypitsummary-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> and came home to win his first race as World Champion.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> This was his first victory since the <a href="/wiki/2008_Chinese_Grand_Prix" title="2008 Chinese Grand Prix">2008 Chinese Grand Prix</a>, his tenth in total, and also the first time that he had won at a circuit twice, having won at nine different circuits previously. This was also the first occasion on which a KERS car had won a Grand Prix; Räikkönen (in another KERS car) came in second, setting his fastest lap on the final lap.<sup id="cite_ref-hungaryFLsummary_42-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hungaryFLsummary-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> Webber finished third, a result which moved him into second in the championship standings past Vettel. Webber also recorded the first fastest lap of his career, with a lap of 1:21.931 on lap 65.<sup id="cite_ref-hungaryFLsummary_42-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hungaryFLsummary-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> Rosberg finished fourth for the third race in succession,<sup id="cite_ref-commautosport_37-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-commautosport-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> heading home Kovalainen, Glock, Button and Trulli rounded out the top eight point scorers. Outside the point-scoring positions were Nakajima, Barrichello (the first time that a Brawn car had finished a race outside the points), Heidfeld, Piquet, Kubica, Fisichella and the Toro Rossos of Alguersuari and Buemi.<sup id="cite_ref-hunlapchart_40-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hunlapchart-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> With Button's seventh place, his lead in the drivers' championship over Webber was cut by four points, to 18.5 points.<sup id="cite_ref-standings_49-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-standings-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2009_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatright" style="width:40%;&#32;;"><link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r887775652"/>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">"Yeah, I was able to manage them quite well but something I want to say is that yesterday was, I think, and maybe I can speak for all of us drivers, quite a sad day to see Felipe not with us here today. Hopefully I speak on behalf of all of us when I say we miss him and wish him well and wish him a speedy recovery. But back to the car, it was just fantastic. The team asked me to look after the tyres in which through all the great experiences I have had—good or bad—I was able to apply that today, so really a big thank you to all the guys and all the fans that have always given me support and never gave me up and to all my family."</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style=""><a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>, reflecting on how well his car looked after its tyres, and also the previous day's events with <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a>'s accident.<sup id="cite_ref-PC_50-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PC-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>The top three finishers appeared on the <a href="/wiki/Podium#Usage_in_motorsport" title="Podium">podium</a> and in the subsequent <a href="/wiki/Press_conference" title="Press conference">press conference</a>, where Hamilton was overjoyed at being back at the front of the field: "It’s an incredible feeling to be back here after what feels such a long time away and with such a struggle with me and my team. But as I said on the in lap I am just so proud of the guys, as I go into the factory and I see how hard everyone is pushing. Everyone wants to win just as much as I do and they never gave up. They have never given up which is something very rare to see in such a large group of people. I am very, very proud of them. We didn’t expect to win this weekend. Undoubtedly we have caught up quite a bit but we never felt we had the pace to win. But the car felt fantastic and it is incredibly special to get back up here, not only on the podium, but to get a win. It is amazing."<sup id="cite_ref-PC_50-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PC-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Räikkönen also achieved his best result of the 2009 season to date, but was unaware that the moves that he made at the start of the race were under investigation, to be looked at after the race.
</p>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r960796168">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>I don’t know about the whole thing, so if you can tell me what it’s about that would be nice...I haven’t even noticed if I touched somebody. I was in-between, I think, Mark and somebody else but I don’t know what is going on. That is the first time when you told me but I didn’t feel any touching or anything, so I cannot comment on that.<sup id="cite_ref-PC_50-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PC-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>
<p>Webber's podium finish moved him into second place in the championship standings. He was surprised, however, at the way proceedings played out, describing his result as "a little bit of a surprise. I think we expected to be a little bit quicker after our running on Friday but to be honest we knew these guys would be around us. It was a pretty difficult venue for us and we knew that we didn’t have the advantage maybe that we had in the last few events. All in all for me I am still pretty happy to get the result we did. I think we had a better chance to fight Kimi if maybe we did a slightly different pit stop and maybe chose a different tyre but that was my call. I was worried about how long the length of the stint was and it was quite difficult to know which tyre to put on but overall we still have a lot of positives. We are still up here. We haven’t been blown away by any means. We are in the hunt and we can take our car to a lot of venues and be competitive, so our guys and Renault have a lot to be proud of. We are still very much a force, so it is still a positive day for us."<sup id="cite_ref-PC_50-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PC-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Two hours after the race's conclusion, representatives of Ferrari, Red Bull and Renault were summoned to see the race stewards due to the various incidents that occurred during the race.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> Ferrari and Räikkönen were cleared of any wrongdoing in either of his incidents with race-winner Hamilton and Vettel on the first lap of the race.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Ferrari_&amp;_Red_Bull_53-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ferrari_&amp;_Red_Bull-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> Red Bull were given a reprimand for the second race running, due to the unsafe release of Webber into the direct path of Räikkönen in the pit lane, at the first round of pit stops.<sup id="cite_ref-Ferrari_&amp;_Red_Bull_53-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ferrari_&amp;_Red_Bull-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup> Red Bull had been in a similar situation at the <a href="/wiki/2009_German_Grand_Prix" title="2009 German Grand Prix">German Grand Prix</a>, when Vettel was released unsafely into the path of Kazuki Nakajima during qualifying,<sup id="cite_ref-Ferrari_&amp;_Red_Bull_53-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ferrari_&amp;_Red_Bull-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> and the team were given a <a href="/wiki/Euro" title="Euro">€</a>10,000 fine.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup> Renault received the harshest punishment of the three incidents, in the form of a suspension from the next race of the season, the <a href="/wiki/2009_European_Grand_Prix" title="2009 European Grand Prix">European Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup> The race stewards felt that Renault "knowingly released car no. 7 [Alonso] from the pitstop position without one of the retaining devices for the wheel-nuts being securely in position, this being an indication that the wheel itself may not have been properly secured. Being aware of this, [Renault] failed to take any action to prevent the car from leaving the pitlane. [Renault] failed to inform the driver of this problem or to advise him to take appropriate action given the circumstances, even though the driver contacted the team by radio believing he had a puncture, resulting in a heavy car part detaching at Turn 5, and the wheel itself detaching at Turn 9."<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup> which was in breach of Articles 3.2 and 23.1.i in the Sporting Regulations.<sup id="cite_ref-sporting_15-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sporting-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Within half an hour of the decision, Renault filed the mandatory cost of €6,000 to appeal against the decision.<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup> The International Court of Appeal heard the case on August 17; the Monday before the European Grand Prix<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup> and the court upheld bid for the suspension to be overturned. The team was fined $50,000 for the breach of the regulations in Hungary, and were allowed to race at Valencia.<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatright" style="width:40%;&#32;;"><link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r887775652"/>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">"I don't remember anything and that's why what the doctors did had to be explained to me. When I saw Rob [Smedley], he asked me if I remembered Rubens, but the last thing I remembered was when I was behind him at the end of my fast lap in Q2, and then it's blank. It's difficult to explain. I'm feeling much better now and I want to recover as soon as possible to get back behind the wheel of a Ferrari."</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style=""><a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a>, in his first interview following the accident.<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>Massa's condition continued to improve over the course of the weekend, the Brazilian having been sedated in a coma for 48 hours.<sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup> After being visited by <a href="/wiki/Ferrari" title="Ferrari">Ferrari</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fiat" title="Fiat">Fiat</a> president <a href="/wiki/Luca_Cordero_di_Montezemolo" title="Luca Cordero di Montezemolo">Luca Cordero di Montezemolo</a> and also Rubens Barrichello, doctors from the Állami Egészségügyi Központ military hospital released a statement saying that Massa could make a full recovery.<sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64">&#91;64&#93;</a></sup> Massa could be released from hospital within a week to ten days, depending on how well he progresses.<sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65">&#91;65&#93;</a></sup> Massa's doctor, Dino Altmann, also told the media that he is certain that Massa will race again, having stated that the trauma suffered by the Brazilian was not as bad as first thought, and that his condition has improved rapidly,<sup id="cite_ref-66" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-66">&#91;66&#93;</a></sup> taking his first steps since the crash, when he left the intensive care unit.<sup id="cite_ref-67" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-67">&#91;67&#93;</a></sup> Massa left hospital on August 3,<sup id="cite_ref-68" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-68">&#91;68&#93;</a></sup> returning home to Brazil to recuperate. He checked into the Sociedade Beneficente Israelita Brasileira Hospital Albert Einstein in <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo" title="São Paulo">São Paulo</a> for a series of medical tests that would allow him to recuperate at home, rather than in hospital. All the results of the tests for abnormalities came back negative, and Massa was given the all-clear to continue his recuperation.<sup id="cite_ref-69" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-69">&#91;69&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatright" style="width:40%;&#32;;"><link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r887775652"/>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">"Whoever sits in the car at the next race in Valencia, it will not be Michael Schumacher. I am not 100 per cent sure; I am 200 per cent sure. The pressure on him would be huge. He would be expected to win, but he has not driven this car. When Michael was racing he would get as close to perfection as possible. In this case, it would not be perfection; it would be a gamble—and that's not Michael's style."</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style=""><a href="/wiki/Willi_Weber" title="Willi Weber">Willi Weber</a>, on rumours that <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> would be replacing Massa.<sup id="cite_ref-70" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-70">&#91;70&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>Rumours began circulating that any one out of Ferrari advisor <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>, and the team's two test drivers <a href="/wiki/Luca_Badoer" title="Luca Badoer">Luca Badoer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Marc_Gen%C3%A9" title="Marc Gené">Marc Gené</a> would be replacing Massa for the European Grand Prix, and the succeeding events. On July 29, Schumacher agreed to drive the car until Massa was fit enough to return, subject to medical tests.<sup id="cite_ref-71" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-71">&#91;71&#93;</a></sup> However, Schumacher called off his return on August 11, due to a lingering neck injury which he had suffered six months earlier, while testing a <a href="/wiki/Honda_CBR1000RR" title="Honda CBR1000RR">Honda CBR1000RR</a> motorcycle at Circuito Cartagena.<sup id="cite_ref-72" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-72">&#91;72&#93;</a></sup> Badoer replaced Massa in Valencia,<sup id="cite_ref-73" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-73">&#91;73&#93;</a></sup> and competed in his first Grand Prix since <a href="/wiki/1999_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1999 Formula One World Championship">1999</a>. The gap of nine years, nine months and 24 days was the second-longest gap between Grands Prix competed, with only <a href="/wiki/Jan_Lammers" title="Jan Lammers">Jan Lammers</a> (ten years, three months; when he returned to Formula One in <a href="/wiki/1992_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1992 Formula One World Championship">1992</a>), having a lengthier spell between races.<sup id="cite_ref-74" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-74">&#91;74&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Hungarian Grand Prix was also the last race for Renault's Nelson Piquet, Jr. as he parted company with the team on August 3, 2009 and he would not race in a Formula One Grand Prix again.<sup id="cite_ref-75" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-75">&#91;75&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Romain_Grosjean" title="Romain Grosjean">Romain Grosjean</a> was announced as his replacement,<sup id="cite_ref-76" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-76">&#91;76&#93;</a></sup> with his <a href="/wiki/GP2_Series" title="GP2 Series">GP2 Series</a> team <a href="/wiki/Barwa_Addax" class="mw-redirect" title="Barwa Addax">Barwa Addax</a> signing up <a href="/wiki/Davide_Valsecchi" title="Davide Valsecchi">Davide Valsecchi</a> to replace him; ending his title aspirations.<sup id="cite_ref-77" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-77">&#91;77&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
