<p>The <b>1962 Italian Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held at <a href="/wiki/Autodromo_Nazionale_di_Monza" title="Autodromo Nazionale di Monza">Monza</a> on 16 September 1962. It was race 7 of 9 in both the <a href="/wiki/1962_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1962 World Championship of Drivers">1962 World Championship of Drivers</a> and the <a href="/wiki/1962_International_Cup_for_Formula_One_Manufacturers" class="mw-redirect" title="1962 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers">1962 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers</a>. The 86-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/British_Racing_Motors" title="British Racing Motors">BRM</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Graham_Hill" title="Graham Hill">Graham Hill</a> after he started from second position. His teammate <a href="/wiki/Richie_Ginther" title="Richie Ginther">Richie Ginther</a> finished second and <a href="/wiki/Cooper_Car_Company" title="Cooper Car Company">Cooper</a> driver <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">Bruce McLaren</a> came in third.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_report">Race report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1962_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Race report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Jim_Clark" title="Jim Clark">Jim Clark</a> started on pole position, but the Lotus team still looked worried as the team had gone through all of their gearboxes over the weekend. Two crates of spare parts were flown down, but nonetheless Clark and <a href="/wiki/Trevor_Taylor_(racing_driver)" title="Trevor Taylor (racing driver)">Trevor Taylor</a> started with no more spares available. After only two laps Clark pitted with the expected transmission trouble; he made it back onto the track for another ten laps but that was it.<sup id="cite_ref-IMS1062_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IMS1062-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Teammate Taylor managed 25 laps before his race ended. Hill kept stretching his lead out and finished nearly a half minute before <a href="/wiki/Richie_Ginther" title="Richie Ginther">Richie Ginther</a> in the other BRM. Ginther had been duelling with <a href="/wiki/John_Surtees" title="John Surtees">Surtees</a> throughout the race, but on lap 38 Surtees slowed down and five laps later he retired with engine troubles.<sup id="cite_ref-IMS1062_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IMS1062-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Behind these two, there was a race long three-way fight between <a href="/wiki/Dan_Gurney" title="Dan Gurney">Dan Gurney</a>'s Porsche and the <a href="/wiki/Cooper_Car_Company" title="Cooper Car Company">Coopers</a> of <a href="/wiki/Bruce_McLaren" title="Bruce McLaren">McLaren</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tony_Maggs" title="Tony Maggs">Maggs</a>. The cars swapped positions constantly, coming down the straight on the fiftieth lap three abreast. Maggs had to stop for more fuel, unlike McLaren whose car had been fitted with 170-litre (45&#160;US&#160;gal) fuel tanks to enable him to run the entire distance. Meanwhile, <a href="/wiki/Willy_Mairesse" title="Willy Mairesse">Willy Mairesse</a> in the new non-sharknose <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_156_F1" title="Ferrari 156 F1">Ferrari 156</a> had caught up to the third-place contenders and brought <a href="/wiki/Jo_Bonnier" title="Jo Bonnier">Jo Bonnier</a> and <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Baghetti" title="Giancarlo Baghetti">Giancarlo Baghetti</a> with him.<sup id="cite_ref-IMSp5_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IMSp5-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Gurney's car was pressed too hard and he retired on the 66th lap with a broken rear differential. After this, the positions stabilized a bit, although not until after Baghetti had briefly led the pack to the appreciation of local fans.<sup id="cite_ref-IMSp5_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IMSp5-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Bonnier's clutch began slipping and Baghetti also fell back. Mairesse took third on the eightieth lap, and built up a three-second lead over McLaren. <a href="/wiki/Stirling_Moss" title="Stirling Moss">Stirling Moss</a>, who was watching from the speaker tower, began betting people that McLaren would take third and he was right: in the first curve of the last lap, McLaren passed Mairesse and he took third by only 0.4 seconds.<sup id="cite_ref-IMSp5_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IMSp5-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Bonnier's clutch troubles allowed Baghetti to pass him for fifth place near the end of the race. <a href="/wiki/Phil_Hill" title="Phil Hill">Phil Hill</a> had to suffer being lapped before half of the race was over, and had to make a long pit stop with engine troubles and finished twelfth. <a href="/wiki/Ricardo_Rodr%C3%ADguez_(racing_driver)" title="Ricardo Rodríguez (racing driver)">Rodríguez'</a> Ferrari lost oil pressure, <a href="/wiki/Innes_Ireland" title="Innes Ireland">Innes Ireland</a> had a good race until something snapped in the steering, and <a href="/wiki/Masten_Gregory" title="Masten Gregory">Masten Gregory</a>'s car was overheating and then only ran in fourth gear for the last laps. The fire extinguisher in <a href="/wiki/Roy_Salvadori" title="Roy Salvadori">Roy Salvadori</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Yeoman_Credit_Racing" title="Yeoman Credit Racing">Bowmaker-Yeoman</a> Lola exploded in his face during practice.<sup id="cite_ref-IMSp5_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IMSp5-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
