<p>The <b>1976 Japanese Grand Prix</b><sup class="reference plainlinks nourlexpansion" id="ref_a"><a href="#endnote_a">1</a></sup> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at <a href="/wiki/Fuji_Speedway" title="Fuji Speedway">Fuji Speedway</a> on 24 October 1976. It was the 16th and final race of the <a href="/wiki/1976_Formula_One_season" title="1976 Formula One season">1976 Formula One World Championship</a>
</p><p>The 1976 World Championship was to be decided at the Mount Fuji circuit, with <a href="/wiki/Niki_Lauda" title="Niki Lauda">Niki Lauda</a> just three points ahead of <a href="/wiki/James_Hunt" title="James Hunt">James Hunt</a> after a season full of incidents including Lauda's near-fatal crash at the Nürburgring and subsequent missed races.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1976_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Entries">Entries</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1976_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Entries">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Further information: <a href="/wiki/1976_Formula_One_season#Teams_and_drivers" title="1976 Formula One season">1976 Formula One season §&#160;Teams and drivers</a></div>
<p>The field was almost unchanged from the previous race, but <a href="/wiki/Noritake_Takahara" title="Noritake Takahara">Noritake Takahara</a> rented the second <a href="/wiki/Surtees" title="Surtees">Surtees</a> replacing <a href="/wiki/Brett_Lunger" title="Brett Lunger">Brett Lunger</a> and <a href="/wiki/Masami_Kuwashima" title="Masami Kuwashima">Masami Kuwashima</a> replaced <a href="/wiki/Warwick_Brown" title="Warwick Brown">Warwick Brown</a> in the second <a href="/wiki/Frank_Williams_Racing_Cars" title="Frank Williams Racing Cars">Wolf-Williams</a>. However, Kuwashima was himself replaced by <a href="/wiki/Hans_Binder" title="Hans Binder">Hans Binder</a> during the meeting, after his money failed to materialize. <a href="/wiki/Maki_Engineering" title="Maki Engineering">Maki</a> resurrected its Formula One car for <a href="/wiki/Tony_Trimmer" title="Tony Trimmer">Tony Trimmer</a> while <a href="/wiki/Heros_Racing" title="Heros Racing">Heros Racing</a> entered an old Tyrrell for <a href="/wiki/Kazuyoshi_Hoshino" title="Kazuyoshi Hoshino">Kazuyoshi Hoshino</a>. <a href="/wiki/Kojima_Engineering" title="Kojima Engineering">Kojima Engineering</a> entered a locally built chassis for <a href="/wiki/Masahiro_Hasemi" title="Masahiro Hasemi">Masahiro Hasemi</a> (on <a href="/wiki/Dunlop_Rubber" title="Dunlop Rubber">Dunlop</a> tyres).
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Championship_standings_before_the_race">Championship standings before the race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1976_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Championship standings before the race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Further information: <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_points_scoring_systems" class="mw-redirect" title="List of Formula One points scoring systems">List of Formula One points scoring systems</a></div>
<p>Heading into the final race of the season it was <a href="/wiki/Niki_Lauda" title="Niki Lauda">Niki Lauda</a> who led the World Driver's Championship by three points ahead of <a href="/wiki/James_Hunt" title="James Hunt">James Hunt</a>. In the Constructors' Championship it was <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> who had an eleven point lead over <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> As this was the final race of the season with 9 points available for the win it meant that the Japanese Grand Prix would decide the Driver's championship although Ferrari had confirmed their win the Constructors in the <a href="/wiki/1976_United_States_Grand_Prix" title="1976 United States Grand Prix">previous round</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Championship_permutations">Championship permutations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1976_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Championship permutations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>For Lauda to win the Championship he needed:
</p>
<ul><li>to finish ahead of Hunt</li>
<li>to finish 3rd with Hunt 2nd</li>
<li>to finish 4th or 5th with Hunt 3rd or lower</li>
<li>to finish 6th with Hunt 4th or lower</li>
<li>Hunt to finish 5th or lower</li></ul>
<p>For Hunt to win the Championship he needed to finish:
</p>
<ul><li>1st</li>
<li>2nd with Lauda 4th or lower</li>
<li>3rd with Lauda 6th or lower</li>
<li>4th with Lauda 7th or lower</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1976_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Mario_Andretti" title="Mario Andretti">Mario Andretti</a> took pole position in the <a href="/wiki/Lotus_77" title="Lotus 77">Lotus 77</a>, with Hunt alongside him on the front row and Lauda third. Then came <a href="/wiki/John_Watson_(racing_driver)" title="John Watson (racing driver)">John Watson</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Team_Penske#Formula_One" title="Team Penske">Penske</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jody_Scheckter" title="Jody Scheckter">Jody Scheckter</a>, <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Pace" title="Carlos Pace">Carlos Pace</a>, <a href="/wiki/Clay_Regazzoni" title="Clay Regazzoni">Clay Regazzoni</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vittorio_Brambilla" title="Vittorio Brambilla">Vittorio Brambilla</a>. The top 10 was completed by <a href="/wiki/Ronnie_Peterson" title="Ronnie Peterson">Ronnie Peterson</a> and Hasemi. Trimmer failed to qualify the Maki.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1976_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On race day the weather was very wet with fog and running water at several places on the track. There were intense debates as to whether the race should be started; in the end the organisers decided to go ahead and a majority of drivers did not disagree. Some drivers, including Lauda, were not happy with the decision.
</p><p>Hunt took the lead from the start with Watson and Andretti behind. On the second lap Watson slid down an escape road and Lauda drove into the pits to withdraw, as he believed the weather conditions made the track too dangerous. He later said "my life is worth more than a title". <a href="/wiki/Larry_Perkins" title="Larry Perkins">Larry Perkins</a> made a similar decision after one lap, as did Pace and <a href="/wiki/Emerson_Fittipaldi" title="Emerson Fittipaldi">Emerson Fittipaldi</a> later in the race.
</p><p>Hunt continued to lead, behind him second place passed between Andretti and Brambilla. On lap 22 Brambilla challenged for the lead but spun out of contention before retiring 15 laps later with electrical problems. <a href="/wiki/Jochen_Mass" title="Jochen Mass">Jochen Mass</a> moved into second before crashing on the 36th lap just before turn 7, promoting <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Depailler" title="Patrick Depailler">Patrick Depailler</a> into the position with Andretti third.
</p><p>It seemed Hunt was on for an easy win, but as the track began to dry he started to lose positions. He only needed a fourth place to win the title, because of Lauda's retirement. On lap 62 Hunt fell behind Depailler and Andretti, but two laps later Depailler's left rear tyre started to deflate and he had to pit. Andretti took the lead, but then Hunt had a similar tyre problem. Hunt pitted, dropped to fifth and set off after Depailler, <a href="/wiki/Alan_Jones_(racing_driver)" title="Alan Jones (racing driver)">Alan Jones</a> and Regazzoni. Depailler overtook both drivers on lap 70 and on the next lap Hunt did the same and won the World Drivers' Championship, to his surprise. <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> won the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a> despite Lauda's retirement.
</p><p>Andretti's victory was his second in Formula One, coming five years, seven months and 18 days after his maiden win at the <a href="/wiki/1971_South_African_Grand_Prix" title="1971 South African Grand Prix">1971 South African Grand Prix</a>. As of 2020<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=1976_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup>, this is the longest period between a first and second victory of a driver in the series.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1976_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><span class="citation wikicite" id="endnote_a"><b><a href="#ref_a">^</a></b></span> In Japan, the formal name of this Formula One event was not "Japanese Grand Prix" but was "Formula One World Championship in Japan" (F1世界選手権・イン・ジャパン), because an event of the <a href="/wiki/Formula_Nippon" class="mw-redirect" title="Formula Nippon">Japanese Formula 2000 championship</a> had been named "<i>Japanese Grand Prix</i>" in 1976.</li>
<li><span class="citation wikicite" id="endnote_b"><b><a href="#ref_b">^</a></b></span> It was initially announced that the fastest lap was set by <a href="/wiki/Masahiro_Hasemi" title="Masahiro Hasemi">Masahiro Hasemi</a> on lap 25, but this was a measurement mistake, and, several days later, the circuit issued a press release to correct the fastest lap holder of the race to <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Laffite" title="Jacques Laffite">Jacques Laffite</a> with a time of 1:19.97 on lap 70.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> This press release was promptly made known in Japan, and the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Japan_Automobile_Federation&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Japan Automobile Federation (page does not exist)">Japan Automobile Federation</a> (JAF) and Japanese media corrected the record.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> But this correction was not made well known outside Japan, thus, Hasemi is credited with the fastest lap of the race in many record books.</li></ul>
