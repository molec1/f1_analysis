<p>The <b>2009 Singapore Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>2009 Formula 1 SingTel Singapore Grand Prix</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Auto_racing" title="Auto racing">motor race</a> held at the <a href="/wiki/Marina_Bay_Street_Circuit" title="Marina Bay Street Circuit">Marina Bay Street Circuit</a> in <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a> on 27 September 2009. The 61-lap race was the fourteenth round of the <a href="/wiki/2009_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2009 Formula One season">2009 Formula One season</a>. It was the second <a href="/wiki/Singapore_Grand_Prix" title="Singapore Grand Prix">Singapore Grand Prix</a> to be part of a Formula One Championship and the tenth Singapore Grand Prix overall. It was also the second <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> race to be held at night. The race was won by <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_HighPerformanceEngines" class="mw-redirect" title="Mercedes-Benz HighPerformanceEngines">Mercedes</a> driver and reigning World Champion <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>. <a href="/wiki/Toyota_Racing_(Formula_One_team)" title="Toyota Racing (Formula One team)">Toyota</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Timo_Glock" title="Timo Glock">Timo Glock</a> finished second and <a href="/wiki/2008_Singapore_Grand_Prix" title="2008 Singapore Grand Prix">2008 race</a> winner <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> took third position, making this the only race of the season with neither a Brawn nor a Red Bull driver on the podium. This was also the last race of 2009 for Glock, as he was injured during qualifying for the <a href="/wiki/2009_Japanese_Grand_Prix" title="2009 Japanese Grand Prix">Japanese Grand Prix</a> and was replaced by test driver <a href="/wiki/Kamui_Kobayashi" title="Kamui Kobayashi">Kamui Kobayashi</a>. <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> scored his last ever World Championship points at this race.
</p><p>As a consequence of the race, Championship leader <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> extended his lead by one point finishing in fifth, one position ahead of <a href="/wiki/Brawn_GP" title="Brawn GP">Brawn</a> teammate and Championship rival <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a>. Fellow rival <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> had been challenging Hamilton for the lead before a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_regulations#Penalties" title="Formula One regulations">drive-through penalty</a> for speeding in the pitlane scuppered his strategy.<sup id="cite_ref-RaceR_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RaceR-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2009_Singapore_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2009_Singapore_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> of <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a> was the only previous winner of the race but <a href="/wiki/Renault_Formula_One_crash_controversy" title="Renault Formula One crash controversy">recent controversy over last year's victory</a> surrounded the race. <a href="/wiki/2008_Singapore_Grand_Prix" title="2008 Singapore Grand Prix">Last year's</a> pole sitter <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> sat out the race with injuries sustained in the <a href="/wiki/2009_Hungarian_Grand_Prix" title="2009 Hungarian Grand Prix">Hungarian Grand Prix</a>. The previous street circuit races this year were won by <a href="/wiki/Brawn_GP" title="Brawn GP">Brawn GP</a> with <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> taking the chequered flag in <a href="/wiki/2009_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="2009 Monaco Grand Prix">Monaco</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> victorious in <a href="/wiki/2009_European_Grand_Prix" title="2009 European Grand Prix">Valencia</a>.
</p><p>Prior to the race, Button led the Championship by 14 points from teammate Barrichello, with Brawn's closest challenger <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> lying 12 points behind the Brazilian and 26 behind Button. <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> of <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> was looking for a fifth consecutive podium, having scored three quarters of his 40 2009 championship points in the previous four&#160;races.
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Romain_Grosjean_2009_Singapore.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a1/Romain_Grosjean_2009_Singapore.jpg/220px-Romain_Grosjean_2009_Singapore.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a1/Romain_Grosjean_2009_Singapore.jpg/330px-Romain_Grosjean_2009_Singapore.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a1/Romain_Grosjean_2009_Singapore.jpg/440px-Romain_Grosjean_2009_Singapore.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2816" data-file-height="1880" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Romain_Grosjean_2009_Singapore.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a> lost their title sponsor <a href="/wiki/ING_Group" title="ING Group">ING</a> and removed the stickers before Friday practice.</div></div></div>
<p>Brawn led the Constructors' Championship on 146 points (and could have clinched the Constructors' title in Singapore), whilst <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a> were 40.5 behind on 105.5. Ferrari and <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> were 3rd and 4th respectively.
</p><p>The circuit was also reprofiled slightly, including modifications to turns 1, 2, 3, 13 and 14 to aid overtaking, and also at turn 10 where high kerbs caused many accidents in <a href="/wiki/2008_Singapore_Grand_Prix" title="2008 Singapore Grand Prix">2008</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The entrance and exit to the pit lane were also changed. The cars entered the pit lane before turn 22 while the most significant change was the exit of the pits with the cars now joining after turn 1.<sup id="cite_ref-TC1_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TC1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> As a result, the total length of the circuit increased by 6 metres.
</p><p>Turns 1, 7 and 10 were now known as "Sheares", "Memorial" and "Singapore Sling" respectively. The namesake of these names are as follows: "Sheares" is named after the <a href="/wiki/Benjamin_Sheares_Bridge" title="Benjamin Sheares Bridge">Benjamin Sheares Bridge</a> that spans on the expressway above the circuit, which is in turn named after ex-President of the Republic <a href="/wiki/Benjamin_Sheares" title="Benjamin Sheares">Benjamin Sheares</a>. "Memorial" is named after the WWII War Memorial in the middle of the city, and "Singapore Sling" is the name of Singapore's <a href="/wiki/Singapore_Sling" title="Singapore Sling">signature drink</a>.
</p><p>Announced on Thursday evening (UTC) that title sponsor <a href="/wiki/ING_Group" title="ING Group">ING</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mutua_Madrile%C3%B1a" title="Mutua Madrileña">Mutua Madrileña</a> had terminated their sponsorship deals with <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a> with immediate effect.<sup id="cite_ref-Terminate_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Terminate-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>  This move followed the previous year's Singapore Grand Prix race-fixing scandal that resulted in a suspended ban for the team. Renault removed the sponsors' logos from their cars before they ran them on track. Mutua Madrileña continued however to back <a href="/wiki/2005_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2005 Formula One World Championship">2005</a> and <a href="/wiki/2006_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2006 Formula One World Championship">2006</a> World Champion <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Terminate_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Terminate-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice">Practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2009_Singapore_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Giancarlo_Fisichella_2009_Singapore.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/56/Giancarlo_Fisichella_2009_Singapore.jpg/220px-Giancarlo_Fisichella_2009_Singapore.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/56/Giancarlo_Fisichella_2009_Singapore.jpg/330px-Giancarlo_Fisichella_2009_Singapore.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/56/Giancarlo_Fisichella_2009_Singapore.jpg/440px-Giancarlo_Fisichella_2009_Singapore.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2816" data-file-height="1880" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Giancarlo_Fisichella_2009_Singapore.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> struggled in practice, and qualified in 18th place.</div></div></div>
<p>The first session of the weekend ended as the <a href="/wiki/2009_Italian_Grand_Prix" title="2009 Italian Grand Prix">previous race</a> finished with the two <a href="/wiki/Brawn_GP" title="Brawn GP">Brawns</a> on top, <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> in front of <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Pr1_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pr1-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Romain_Grosjean" title="Romain Grosjean">Romain Grosjean</a> caused the session to be stopped after losing control of the car at the same corner that <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet,_Jr." class="mw-redirect" title="Nelson Piquet, Jr.">Nelson Piquet, Jr.</a> crashed out during the race at <a href="/wiki/2008_Singapore_Grand_Prix" title="2008 Singapore Grand Prix">last year's event</a>. <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a> and <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a>  were the others who had on track incidents.<sup id="cite_ref-V_Pr2_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-V_Pr2-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> split the two <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a>'s in fourth, <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> finished the session in third and <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> in fifth. <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> continued to struggle as he finished the session P17, around 1.6s behind teammate <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-V_Pr2_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-V_Pr2-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The second session was also red flagged this time with Red Bull's Mark Webber. A big accident on the pit straight as the nose, front left wheel and the wing took the impact as a lot of debris was put onto the track.<sup id="cite_ref-SinPr_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SinPr-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> However the other Red Bull of Vettel finished the session top 0.2s ahead of Alonso.<sup id="cite_ref-Pr2_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pr2-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Kovalainen took third, Heidfeld fourth and championship leader Button finished the days final session in fifth. Jenson's teammate Barrichello struggled to take eleventh almost one second slower than Vettel. Although Webber crashed out with one hour remaining in the session<sup id="cite_ref-SinPr_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SinPr-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> he managed to sixth.
</p><p>The final practice session got under way with no cars posting a time for the best part of 15 minutes. With the first session of the weekend not being red-flagged it was Lewis Hamilton who finished top of the time sheets.<sup id="cite_ref-Pr3_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pr3-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Sebastian Vettel finished second trying to make the most of this Red Bull car as his teammate Mark Webber struggled for pace finishing down in thirteenth. The <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a> of <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> came third with Hamilton's teammate <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> in fourth.<sup id="cite_ref-Pr3_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pr3-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Rubens Barrichello finished in sixth behind the two <a href="/wiki/BMW_Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="BMW Sauber">BMW</a>'s of <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a>. Rubens's teammate and championship leader Jenson Button struggled and only managed fourteenth. The two <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a> drivers didn't look quick at all as the high down-force circuit of <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a> didn't seem to suit the car. They ended the session P16 and 17. Giancarlo Fisichella seemed to be struggling a lot being 1.2s behind teammate Räikkönen and 2.4s behind Lewis Hamilton.<sup id="cite_ref-Pr3_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pr3-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2009_Singapore_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Very early on the first session <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Romain_Grosjean" title="Romain Grosjean">Romain Grosjean</a> went down the escape round at turn 17, as his brakes had no efficiency.<sup id="cite_ref-QuH_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-QuH-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Rosberg was the one who set the early pace but Button, Vettel and Hamilton responded quickly. Both <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>'s were struggling until Räikkönen took second with three minutes remaining. This left Fisichella stuck in the bottom 5 and was eventually eliminated only managing eighteenth.<sup id="cite_ref-QR_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-QR-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> 
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Lewis_Hamilton_2009_Singapore.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/23/Lewis_Hamilton_2009_Singapore.jpg/220px-Lewis_Hamilton_2009_Singapore.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/23/Lewis_Hamilton_2009_Singapore.jpg/330px-Lewis_Hamilton_2009_Singapore.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/23/Lewis_Hamilton_2009_Singapore.jpg/440px-Lewis_Hamilton_2009_Singapore.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2816" data-file-height="1880" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Lewis_Hamilton_2009_Singapore.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> took <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> for <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>.</div></div></div><p>The others to be knocked out were <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Sutil</a> (P16), <a href="/wiki/Jaime_Alguersuari" title="Jaime Alguersuari">Alguersuari</a> (P17), Grosjean (P19) and <a href="/wiki/Vitantonio_Liuzzi" title="Vitantonio Liuzzi">Liuzzi</a> (P20).<sup id="cite_ref-QR_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-QR-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Mark Webber was the early pace setter in this 15 minute knock-out, but an amazing lap by Nico Rosberg easily secured his place into Q3. Button was struggling all through the session and ran wide and almost put the car into the wall on his final lap. He eventually finished a dismal twelfth. And with Barrichello having to drop 5 places down the grid after changing his gearbox before qualifying it wasn't looking to good for <a href="/wiki/Ross_Brawn" title="Ross Brawn">Ross Brawn</a>'s team. Barrichello left it late and jumped from eleventh to sixth to book his place in the top 10 shootout. The others to be knocked out were; <a href="/wiki/Kazuki_Nakajima" title="Kazuki Nakajima">Nakajima</a> (P11), Räikkönen (P13), <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9bastien_Buemi" title="Sébastien Buemi">Buemi</a> (P14) and <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Trulli</a> in P15.<sup id="cite_ref-QR_11-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-QR-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The top 10 drivers set their early quick laps. Hamilton took the early lead with Vettel and Rosberg close behind. With only two minutes to go it looked to be a close to who would take pole position. The top three were unchanged with Vettel very quick on track while Rosberg was going even quicker. However, Barrichello spun off and hit the wall hard, destroying much of the left side of his car, which brought out the yellow flags meaning anyone who was on a flying lap had to slow down unless they had already passed the scene of the accident. The red flag was brought out with 26 seconds remaining on the clock. As no other cars crossed the line, Hamilton took pole position ahead of Vettel, Rosberg and Mark Webber.<sup id="cite_ref-QR_11-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-QR-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Although Barrichello qualified fifth, he started the race tenth after his gearbox change.<sup id="cite_ref-Q_Report_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Q_Report-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The session was restarted with only 26 seconds left there was no time to start a flying lap so no one rejoined the circuit. This session was the third of four to be red flagged during the weekend, only the third practice session was not interrupted.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2009_Singapore_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3><p>
Approximately three hours before the scheduled race start it was announced <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> would be starting the race from the pitlane, after his team discovered insufficient ballast had been fitted to his car.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> This promoted all the cars behind him one position on the grid, significantly moving both the <a href="/wiki/Brawn_GP" title="Brawn GP">Brawn</a> cars onto the 'clean' side of the starting grid, on the racing line.</p><div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Jenson_Button_2009_Singapore_2.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2f/Jenson_Button_2009_Singapore_2.jpg/220px-Jenson_Button_2009_Singapore_2.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="100" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2f/Jenson_Button_2009_Singapore_2.jpg/330px-Jenson_Button_2009_Singapore_2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2f/Jenson_Button_2009_Singapore_2.jpg/440px-Jenson_Button_2009_Singapore_2.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3888" data-file-height="1776" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Jenson_Button_2009_Singapore_2.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> finished fifth, extending his championship lead over teammate <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> by one point.</div></div></div>
<p>The race began shortly after 8pm local time with <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> making a clean start from pole position. <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> took advantage of being on the clean side of the track to overtake <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> into the first corner. <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> attempted to also pass Vettel but was unsuccessful, giving <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> the opportunity to overtake Alonso on the outside of Turn 7. Both Alonso and Webber went off track as Webber completed the move. Stewards deemed Webber's move illegal<sup id="cite_ref-SinH_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SinH-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> and on lap seven he was told to hand the position back to Alonso, which also meant letting <a href="/wiki/Timo_Glock" title="Timo Glock">Timo Glock</a> through as the German had also passed Alonso on the first lap.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Alonso's <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a> teammate <a href="/wiki/Romain_Grosjean" title="Romain Grosjean">Romain Grosjean</a> was not having any more luck, having to retire at the end of lap three with a recurring brake problem.<sup id="cite_ref-SinH_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SinH-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>With Barrichello in seventh and Button out of the points scoring positions in 10th, Vettel looked to capitalise and try to close Button's 26 point lead in the Championship. By lap 13 he was 2.1 seconds behind Rosberg, who was in turn 2.5 seconds behind Hamilton. Vettel was the first of the leaders to stop, on lap 17. A lap later Rosberg pitted but he misjudged the level of grip on the pit exit, causing his car to cross the white line that distinguishes the pit lane from the race track,<sup id="cite_ref-Ros_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ros-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> an offence that is punished by a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_regulations#Penalties" title="Formula One regulations">drive-through penalty</a>. Rosberg was issued with the penalty on lap 20 and would have to serve penalty within three racing laps. Hamilton also pitted on lap 20.
</p><p>
The following lap, an accident involving <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> necessitated the deployment of the <a href="/wiki/Safety_Car" class="mw-redirect" title="Safety Car">Safety Car</a> to remove debris from the track. Sutil spun attempting to pass <a href="/wiki/Jaime_Alguersuari" title="Jaime Alguersuari">Jaime Alguersuari</a> on the inside of Turn 14 and, with his car facing the wrong way, attempted to rejoin the circuit as Heidfeld was taking the corner, causing a collision that forced both cars to retire, Heidfeld's first non finish since the <a href="/wiki/2007_United_States_Grand_Prix" title="2007 United States Grand Prix">2007 United States Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Sutil was later reprimanded and fined $20,000 by stewards for dangerous driving.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> </p><div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Fernando_Alonso_2009_Singapore_2.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/85/Fernando_Alonso_2009_Singapore_2.jpg/220px-Fernando_Alonso_2009_Singapore_2.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="123" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/85/Fernando_Alonso_2009_Singapore_2.jpg/330px-Fernando_Alonso_2009_Singapore_2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/85/Fernando_Alonso_2009_Singapore_2.jpg/440px-Fernando_Alonso_2009_Singapore_2.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3888" data-file-height="2171" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Fernando_Alonso_2009_Singapore_2.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> controversially dedicated his podium to <a href="/wiki/Flavio_Briatore" title="Flavio Briatore">Flavio Briatore</a> after the race.</div></div></div><p>  Most cars that had thus far not pitted for fuel and tyres did so under the Safety Car conditions, including both Brawn cars. The Safety Car did not benefit Rosberg, as Formula One regulations prevent a drive-through penalty being served under Safety Car conditions, meaning he would have to stop within two laps of the restart when the field was still closely bunched.<sup id="cite_ref-Ros_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ros-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> During this round of pit stops, Alguersuari repeated the infamous mistake made by <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> at the 2008 race by attempting to exit his pit garage with the fuel hose still attached.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The race restarted on lap 26, with Vettel now pressurising Hamilton for the lead. Rosberg served his penalty on lap 28, demoting him to 14th position. Hamilton and Vettel traded fastest laps with the gap between them hovering under a second. Behind Vettel the longer-fuelled Timo Glock was being encouraged to keep pushing as his team believed they could leap-frog the Red Bull in the pits.<sup id="cite_ref-SinH_14-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SinH-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>
Vettel pitted for his second stop on lap 39 and was clearly pushing very hard, running over a kerb on his out lap and damaging the underside of his car, having already lost his right wing mirror. However, worse was to come, as on lap 40 the stewards handed him a drive-through penalty for speeding in the pit lane.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> </p><div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Lewis_Hamilton_2009_Singapore_8.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/07/Lewis_Hamilton_2009_Singapore_8.jpg/220px-Lewis_Hamilton_2009_Singapore_8.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/07/Lewis_Hamilton_2009_Singapore_8.jpg/330px-Lewis_Hamilton_2009_Singapore_8.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/07/Lewis_Hamilton_2009_Singapore_8.jpg/440px-Lewis_Hamilton_2009_Singapore_8.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1024" data-file-height="683" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Lewis_Hamilton_2009_Singapore_8.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> was barely challenged throughout the race as he took the chequered flag.</div></div></div><p> This left Hamilton 9.2 seconds ahead of Glock, who was in turn 7.6 seconds ahead of Alonso as Vettel served his penalty on lap 43. Red Bull's afternoon went from bad to worse three laps later as Webber spun out at Turn 1 after a right-front brake failure.
</p><p>McLaren brought in both Hamilton and Heikki Kovalainen for their second stops, and Timo Glock also pitted. The Safety Car was not deemed necessary to clear Webber's car, giving Jenson Button, who had been following Kovalainen, the opportunity to put in a series of fast laps before his pit stop.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> With Barrichello running in seventh after a small problem at his second pit stop,<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Button had an opportunity to stay ahead of his teammate and extend his Championship lead. He pitted on lap 51, emerging in a net fifth position behind the impressively recovering Vettel and ahead of Barrichello.
</p><p>Button seemed to have the speed to catch Vettel, who was concerned about his brakes following the retirement of Webber as well as that of Alguersuari, of Red Bull's sister team Toro Rosso, also with brake problems.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Button closed the gap to under two seconds before he too appeared to develop brake problems.<sup id="cite_ref-Button_Boost_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Button_Boost-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Fortunately for Button, his attempts to catch Vettel had given him a ten-second cushion over Barrichello with two laps to go.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Nico_Rosberg_2009_Singapore_4.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4d/Nico_Rosberg_2009_Singapore_4.jpg/220px-Nico_Rosberg_2009_Singapore_4.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4d/Nico_Rosberg_2009_Singapore_4.jpg/330px-Nico_Rosberg_2009_Singapore_4.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4d/Nico_Rosberg_2009_Singapore_4.jpg/440px-Nico_Rosberg_2009_Singapore_4.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1024" data-file-height="683" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Nico_Rosberg_2009_Singapore_4.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> received a drive-through penalty after he crossed the white line at the pit exit.</div></div></div>
<p>Nearly two hours after the race started, Hamilton completed the final lap to take the chequered flag, ahead of Glock and Alonso. Vettel and Button held on for fourth and fifth respectively, ahead of Barrichello, Kovalainen and Robert Kubica in the final points paying position. Button extended his Championship lead by one point to 15 ahead of Barrichello, with just three races remaining. Vettel was 25 points behind Button while Webber's crash meant he was now officially out of the running for the World Championship.<sup id="cite_ref-Button_Boost_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Button_Boost-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Brawn would need 12 points from the remaining three races to clinch the Constructors' Championship, while Ferrari's poor race, with neither driver scoring points, allowed McLaren to close the gap to Ferrari to three points in the battle for third.
</p><p>Under <a href="/wiki/Bernie_Ecclestone" title="Bernie Ecclestone">Bernie Ecclestone</a>'s proposed 'winner takes all' medals system, Button would have been declared World Champion after this result, with no other driver able to match his tally of six race wins.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> The Olympics-style system was planned to be used to decide the 2009 World Champion but was dropped after pressure from teams in favour of the traditional <a href="/wiki/Formula_One#Points_system" title="Formula One">points</a> method.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="F1_Rocks_Singapore_With_LG">F1 Rocks Singapore With LG</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2009_Singapore_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: F1 Rocks Singapore With LG">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:F1_Rocks_Singapore.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5e/F1_Rocks_Singapore.jpg/220px-F1_Rocks_Singapore.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5e/F1_Rocks_Singapore.jpg/330px-F1_Rocks_Singapore.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5e/F1_Rocks_Singapore.jpg/440px-F1_Rocks_Singapore.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2560" data-file-height="1920" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:F1_Rocks_Singapore.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Singapore was the first venue for the F1 Rocks concerts.</div></div></div>
<p>The inaugural F1 Rocks concerts was held in Singapore. Headlining acts include <a href="/wiki/Beyonc%C3%A9" title="Beyoncé">Beyoncé</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Black_Eyed_Peas" class="mw-redirect" title="The Black Eyed Peas">The Black Eyed Peas</a>, <a href="/wiki/ZZ_Top" title="ZZ Top">ZZ Top</a>, <a href="/wiki/Simple_Minds" title="Simple Minds">Simple Minds</a>, <a href="/wiki/N*E*R*D" class="mw-redirect" title="N*E*R*D">N*E*R*D</a>, <a href="/wiki/No_Doubt" title="No Doubt">No Doubt</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jacky_Cheung" title="Jacky Cheung">Jacky Cheung</a> and <a href="/wiki/A*Mei" class="mw-redirect" title="A*Mei">A*Mei</a> which took place 24 to 26 September nightly at <a href="/wiki/Fort_Canning_Park" class="mw-redirect" title="Fort Canning Park">Fort Canning Park</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-F1_Rocks_Artists_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1_Rocks_Artists-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> This also included <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> doing fun challenges and it was hosted by <a href="/wiki/Lindsay_Lohan" title="Lindsay Lohan">Lindsay Lohan</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Fuel_Festival_Singapore">Fuel Festival Singapore</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2009_Singapore_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Fuel Festival Singapore">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Another massive SingaporeGP Season event Fuel Festival featured headliners <a href="/wiki/Underworld_(band)" title="Underworld (band)">Underworld</a>, <a href="/wiki/Will.i.am" title="Will.i.am">will.i.am</a>, <a href="/wiki/Peter_Hook" title="Peter Hook">Peter Hook</a> ex-<a href="/wiki/New_Order_(band)" title="New Order (band)">New Order</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bloc_Party" title="Bloc Party">Bloc Party</a>, <a href="/wiki/Guru_Josh" title="Guru Josh">Guru Josh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Darren_Emerson" title="Darren Emerson">Darren Emerson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Freaks_(band)" title="Freaks (band)">Freaks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hybrid_(Welsh_band)" class="mw-redirect" title="Hybrid (Welsh band)">Hybrid</a>, <a href="/wiki/Samantha_Ronson" title="Samantha Ronson">Samantha Ronson</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Vicious" title="Johnny Vicious">Johnny Vicious</a> and was hosted by <a href="/wiki/Lindsay_Lohan" title="Lindsay Lohan">Lindsay Lohan</a> which takes place 24 September at <a href="/wiki/Suntec_Convention_Centre" class="mw-redirect" title="Suntec Convention Centre">Suntec Convention Centre</a> as well as 25 to 27 September nightly at the National Museum of Singapore.
</p><p>Singapore GP itself is offering a showcase featuring <a href="/wiki/Backstreet_Boys" title="Backstreet Boys">Backstreet Boys</a>, <a href="/wiki/Travis_(band)" title="Travis (band)">Travis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Carl_Cox" title="Carl Cox">Carl Cox</a>, and <a href="/wiki/John_Digweed" title="John Digweed">John Digweed</a> on the 25th through 27th nightly throughout the track side areas.
</p>
