<p>The <b>2009 Spanish Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 10 May 2009 at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Catalunya" class="mw-redirect" title="Circuit de Catalunya">Circuit de Catalunya</a> in <a href="/wiki/Barcelona" title="Barcelona">Barcelona</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>. It was the fifth&#160;race of the <a href="/wiki/2009_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2009 Formula One season">2009 Formula One season</a>.
</p><p>It resulted in a one-two finish by <a href="/wiki/Brawn_GP" title="Brawn GP">Brawn GP</a> drivers <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a>, respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2009_Spanish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2009_Spanish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Brawn_GP" title="Brawn GP">Brawn GP</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> led the Drivers Championship by 12 points from teammate <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a>, who was a further point ahead of <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> of <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a>. The top six drivers were from the top three teams of Brawn, Red Bull and <a href="/wiki/Toyota_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Toyota F1">Toyota</a>.
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Brawn.GP.4.Spain.09.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e9/Brawn.GP.4.Spain.09.jpg/220px-Brawn.GP.4.Spain.09.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="94" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e9/Brawn.GP.4.Spain.09.jpg/330px-Brawn.GP.4.Spain.09.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e9/Brawn.GP.4.Spain.09.jpg/440px-Brawn.GP.4.Spain.09.jpg 2x" data-file-width="981" data-file-height="418" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Brawn.GP.4.Spain.09.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Brawn_GP" title="Brawn GP">Brawn GP</a> featured imagery from the film <i><a href="/wiki/Terminator_Salvation" title="Terminator Salvation">Terminator Salvation</a></i> on their car.</div></div></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Brawn_GP" title="Brawn GP">Brawn GP</a> led the Constructors' Championship by 22.5 points from <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a>, who led <a href="/wiki/Toyota_Racing_(Formula_One_team)" title="Toyota Racing (Formula One team)">Toyota</a> by another point. <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> were the only other team to reach double figures, having scored thirteen points.
</p><p>The 2009 Spanish Grand Prix was widely anticipated as a restart to the season, with most teams bringing new developments to their cars. Brawn GP made their first upgrades to the <a href="/wiki/Brawn_BGP_001" title="Brawn BGP 001">BGP 001</a> in an attempt to remain at the front,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> whilst Ferrari introduced a new two-tier diffuser to the <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_F60" title="Ferrari F60">Ferrari F60</a>, and reduced the weight of Kimi Räikkönen's car.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Ferrari also ran <a href="/wiki/Regenerative_brake#Kinetic_energy_recovery_systems" title="Regenerative brake">KERS</a> on both their cars, despite having reliability problems with it at previous races.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Having run KERS on <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a>'s car for the first four races, and <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a>'s car in <a href="/wiki/2009_Bahrain_Grand_Prix" title="2009 Bahrain Grand Prix">Bahrain</a>, <a href="/wiki/BMW_Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="BMW Sauber">BMW Sauber</a> raced without the system in Spain. This was due to a significant aerodynamic update to the <a href="/wiki/BMW_Sauber_F1.09" title="BMW Sauber F1.09">F1.09</a><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> and the need to get the best out of it, which is limited with KERS' weight disadvantage.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> BMW's aerodynamic update included a new nose, front wing, revised sidepods, a new rear wing and an overall lightening of the chassis.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> In addition, BMW decided not to introduce a two-tier diffuser in Spain, claiming they would not have been able to exploit the performance benefit it gives.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a> also abandoned plans to run KERS to instead focus on aerodynamic developments, but may run the system later in the year according to team principal <a href="/wiki/Vijay_Mallya" title="Vijay Mallya">Vijay Mallya</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During the <a href="/wiki/2008_Spanish_Grand_Prix" title="2008 Spanish Grand Prix">previous year's grand prix</a>, <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> suffered a large crash at turn nine of the circuit.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> After the race, <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a>, a director of the <a href="/wiki/Grand_Prix_Drivers%27_Association" title="Grand Prix Drivers&#39; Association">Grand Prix Drivers' Association</a>, said that standards at the track should have been better. Speaking in his regular <a href="/wiki/BBC_Sport" title="BBC Sport">BBC Sport</a> column, Webber said the run-off area at turn nine was too tight, which could cause an even more serious accident in the future without improvements.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> In response to this, the <a href="/wiki/FIA" class="mw-redirect" title="FIA">FIA</a> made modifications to the track, which should minimise the chances of such a severe accident happening again.<sup id="cite_ref-Safety_changes_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Safety_changes-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> The rear of the gravel trap at the turn was raised by 1.5 metres to give a gradual upward incline to slow down cars, while the gravel trap itself has been changed.<sup id="cite_ref-Safety_changes_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Safety_changes-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> An additional conveyor belt has been fitted to tyres straight on at the corner, to minimise the chances of a car penetrating the barriers again.<sup id="cite_ref-Safety_changes_13-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Safety_changes-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Also, the kerb and artificial grass behind the track at turn nine have been extended by ten metres.<sup id="cite_ref-Safety_changes_13-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Safety_changes-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice_and_qualifying">Practice and qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2009_Spanish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Practice and qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Three practice sessions were held before the race; of which two were held on Friday May 8, 2009, with the first in the morning and the second in the afternoon. Both sessions lasted 90 minutes with weather conditions sunny throughout. The third session was held on Saturday morning, lasting for 60 minutes.
</p><p>In the first practice session, <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> of <a href="/wiki/Brawn_GP" title="Brawn GP">Brawn GP</a> set the fastest time of 1:21.799.<sup id="cite_ref-P1_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P1-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> of <a href="/wiki/Toyota_Racing_(Formula_One_team)" title="Toyota Racing (Formula One team)">Toyota</a> was second, with the two <a href="/wiki/BMW_Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="BMW Sauber">BMW Sauber</a> cars of <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> third and fourth fastest.<sup id="cite_ref-P1_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P1-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> In the second practice session, <a href="/wiki/Williams_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams F1">Williams</a>' driver <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> went quickest with a time of 1.21.588.<sup id="cite_ref-P2_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P2-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> His teammate, <a href="/wiki/Kazuki_Nakajima" title="Kazuki Nakajima">Kazuki Nakajima</a>, was second, with <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> third.<sup id="cite_ref-P2_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P2-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> The third practice session saw <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> leading the way, <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> ahead of <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a>, with the two Brawn cars over half a second slower in third and fourth.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Qualifying on Saturday afternoon was split into three parts. At the end of the first session, Kimi Räikkönen was knocked out from 16th place. He later admitted his fault, believing his first qualifying lap was good enough to secure passage to the next session.<sup id="cite_ref-Raikkonen_takes_blame_for_fiasco_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Raikkonen_takes_blame_for_fiasco-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Also knocked out were the <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a>'s of <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> and <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a>, <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Toro_Rosso" class="mw-redirect" title="Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso</a> of <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9bastien_Bourdais" title="Sébastien Bourdais">Sébastien Bourdais</a>. The second part of qualifying saw the Toro Rosso of <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9bastien_Buemi" title="Sébastien Buemi">Sébastien Buemi</a>, McLaren's reigning world champion <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>, BMW's Heidfeld, Renault's <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet,_Jr." class="mw-redirect" title="Nelson Piquet, Jr.">Nelson Piquet</a>, and Williams' Nakajima all fail to progress. Jenson Button claimed pole position in the final part of qualifying, starting his lap just two seconds before the session ended to put in a time of 1.20.527, one tenth quicker than second-placed <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a>. Button's teammate <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> was third quickest, with Ferrari's Felipe Massa and Red Bull's <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> in fourth and fifth. Toyota's <a href="/wiki/Timo_Glock" title="Timo Glock">Timo Glock</a> and Jarno Trulli were sixth and seventh fastest, in front of Renault's Alonso, Rosberg in the Williams, and the BMW of Kubica.<sup id="cite_ref-Last-gasp_Button_grabs_Spain_pole_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Last-gasp_Button_grabs_Spain_pole-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2009_Spanish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p><a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> made a good start from third on the grid and took the lead at the first corner. <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> was running second, whilst the KERS-equipped Ferrari of <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> was able to gain one place from the start and was placed third ahead of Red Bull's <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a>.
</p><p>An accident on the first lap of the race caused the retirement of <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a>, <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a> and both <a href="/wiki/Toro_Rosso" class="mw-redirect" title="Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso</a> drivers. Four laps behind the safety car followed while marshals cleared debris from the track. Replays of the incident showed that <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> ran wide at turn two, forcing Trulli into the gravel, where he lost control and spun back onto the track, hitting Sutil. <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9bastien_Buemi" title="Sébastien Buemi">Sébastien Buemi</a> and <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9bastien_Bourdais" title="Sébastien Bourdais">Sébastien Bourdais</a> collided in the aftermath of the initial carnage, when Buemi braked hard to avoid Trulli, and Bourdais ran into the back of his teammate.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> had been forced onto the grass at the start by <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet,_Jr." class="mw-redirect" title="Nelson Piquet, Jr.">Nelson Piquet</a> and had ended up at the back of the field, but was able to avoid the incidents in front of him. Shortly after the safety car pulled in and the race restarted, the McLaren of <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> suffered from gearbox failure, forcing him to retire.
</p><p>Barrichello pulled away from Button consistently during the first part of the race. After the first set of pit-stops, it was evident that Barrichello was running a three-stop strategy, while Button and the other front runners were making two stops. Barrichello, however, was unable to create enough of a gap to the chasing pack before his second pit-stop, and lost the lead to Button. After all the other drivers had pitted at least once, Barrichello found himself behind third-placed Massa and fourth-placed Vettel. After the last round of pit-stops were completed, Button was first, while Barrichello had moved up to second, and Red Bull's <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> took third position – the result of a long second stint that Red Bull had opted for in order to emerge in front of the slower Ferrari of Massa.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Ferrari's problems continued as <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> experienced hydraulic failure on his car, causing him to retire on lap 17. Massa had defended his position against Vettel throughout the afternoon, but late in the race, Ferrari identified a problem in that Massa was consuming more fuel than expected. As he could potentially run out of fuel before the end of the race, Massa was instructed to slow down and conserve fuel, and as a result he lost fourth place to Vettel, then fifth place to <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> on the last lap.<sup id="cite_ref-Button_victory_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Button_victory-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Button took his fourth victory of the season, with <a href="/wiki/Brawn_GP" title="Brawn GP">Brawn GP</a> claiming a one-two finish as Barrichello crossed the line 13 seconds later. Webber took the final podium position, with teammate Vettel behind him in fourth. Alonso was fifth, Massa sixth, <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a>, who had significantly closed the gap to Massa in the final lap, had to settle for seventh but in the process breaking <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>'s record of  24 consecutive finishes, while <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> claimed the final point in eighth place.<sup id="cite_ref-Button_victory_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Button_victory-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
