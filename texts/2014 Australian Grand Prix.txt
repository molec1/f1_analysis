<p>The <b>2014 Australian Grand Prix</b> (formally known as the <b>2014 Formula 1 Rolex Australian Grand Prix</b>)<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Auto_racing" title="Auto racing">motor race</a> that was held on 16 March 2014 in <a href="/wiki/Melbourne,_Victoria" class="mw-redirect" title="Melbourne, Victoria">Melbourne</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_Calendar_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA_Calendar-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The race was contested over 58 laps of the <a href="/wiki/Melbourne_Grand_Prix_Circuit" class="mw-redirect" title="Melbourne Grand Prix Circuit">Melbourne Grand Prix Circuit</a> and was the first round of the <a href="/wiki/2014_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2014 Formula One season">2014 FIA Formula One World Championship</a>. It marked the debut of new <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> regulations which introduced 1.6 litre turbocharged <a href="/wiki/V6_engine" title="V6 engine">V6 engines</a> to the sport,<sup id="cite_ref-2014_engines_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2014_engines-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> the first time since the <a href="/wiki/1988_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="1988 Australian Grand Prix">1988 Australian Grand Prix</a> that turbocharged engines have been used in Formula One.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> It was the 79th race in the combined history of the <a href="/wiki/Australian_Grand_Prix" title="Australian Grand Prix">Australian Grand Prix</a> – which dates back to the <a href="/wiki/1928_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="1928 Australian Grand Prix">100 Miles Road Race</a> of 1928 – and the 19th time the event was held at the Melbourne Grand Prix Circuit.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Reliable_sources/Perennial_sources" title="Wikipedia:Reliable sources/Perennial sources"><span title="The material near this tag may rely on an unreliable source (November 2019)">deprecated source</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> The race also marked the thirtieth year that the Australian Grand Prix was run as a round of the Formula One World Championship.
</p><p>The race was won by German driver <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> in a <a href="/wiki/Mercedes_F1_W05" class="mw-redirect" title="Mercedes F1 W05">Mercedes F1 W05</a>. It was Rosberg's fourth Grand Prix victory and completed a rare double in the Australian Grand Prix, winning a race his father <a href="/wiki/Keke_Rosberg" title="Keke Rosberg">Keke</a> had won in <a href="/wiki/Adelaide" title="Adelaide">Adelaide</a> in <a href="/wiki/1985_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="1985 Australian Grand Prix">1985</a>, the first time the AGP was a World Championship race.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> This feat had previously been achieved by <a href="/wiki/Stan_Jones_(racing_driver)" title="Stan Jones (racing driver)">Stan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alan_Jones_(racing_driver)" title="Alan Jones (racing driver)">Alan Jones</a> and <a href="/wiki/Graham_Hill" title="Graham Hill">Graham</a> and <a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a>. Rosberg had earlier achieved the same feat in the <a href="/wiki/Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="Monaco Grand Prix">Monaco Grand Prix</a> as well.<sup id="cite_ref-Guardian-race_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Guardian-race-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Magnussen" title="Kevin Magnussen">Kevin Magnussen</a> finished second in a <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4-29" title="McLaren MP4-29">McLaren MP4-29</a> on his Formula One debut, the first podium finish in a World Championship Grand Prix by a Danish driver. Third was <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> in the second McLaren, who recorded his 50th and final Formula One podium with the result (although he did not take part in the podium ceremony as the stewards had yet to disqualify Daniel Ricciardo). <a href="/wiki/Daniil_Kvyat" title="Daniil Kvyat">Daniil Kvyat</a>, aged 19, was classified in ninth, becoming the youngest points-scorer in Formula One.<sup id="cite_ref-Dan_record_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Dan_record-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Australian driver <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> originally finished in second place for <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a>, but was later disqualified due to illegal fuel flow throughout the race.<sup id="cite_ref-Dan_DQ_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Dan_DQ-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The race marked the end of <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a>'s streak of nine race wins in a row.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Button's podium finish was the last for <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> until the <a href="/wiki/2019_Brazilian_Grand_Prix" title="2019 Brazilian Grand Prix">2019 Brazilian Grand Prix</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2014_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2014_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Tyre supplier <a href="/wiki/Pirelli" title="Pirelli">Pirelli</a> brought its white-banded medium compound tyre as the harder "prime" tyre and the yellow-banded soft compound tyre as the softer "option" tyre, as opposed to the previous year where medium and super-soft selections were provided.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2014_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> set the early pace in his debut for <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a>, while <a href="/wiki/Lotus_F1" title="Lotus F1">Lotus</a> struggled, with <a href="/wiki/Pastor_Maldonado" title="Pastor Maldonado">Pastor Maldonado</a> failing to set a time and <a href="/wiki/Romain_Grosjean" title="Romain Grosjean">Romain Grosjean</a> qualifying 21st, which became 20th when <a href="/wiki/Esteban_Guti%C3%A9rrez" title="Esteban Gutiérrez">Esteban Gutiérrez</a> was given a penalty for a gearbox change in his <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a>. <a href="/wiki/Max_Chilton" title="Max Chilton">Max Chilton</a> out-qualified his <a href="/wiki/Marussia_F1" title="Marussia F1">Marussia</a> teammate <a href="/wiki/Jules_Bianchi" title="Jules Bianchi">Jules Bianchi</a>, but missed out on a place in Q2 by one hundredth of a second. <a href="/wiki/Caterham_F1" title="Caterham F1">Caterham</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Marcus_Ericsson" title="Marcus Ericsson">Marcus Ericsson</a> was the final driver eliminated in Q1, with his teammate <a href="/wiki/Kamui_Kobayashi" title="Kamui Kobayashi">Kamui Kobayashi</a> overcoming a lack of running in Free Practice to advance to the next period.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Rain began to fall in Q2, with the slippery conditions claiming their first victim in <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a>, who spun his <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> late in the period. Räikkönen's accident interrupted the laps of several drivers, with <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> and reigning World Champion <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> both reporting that Räikkönen's accident prevented them from advancing to Q3. It was later found that Vettel was also hindered by engine problems, which had plagued him since FP3 that morning.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Sauber's <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a> finished the period in fourteenth, with Kamui Kobayashi fifteenth after <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a> spun his <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a> on his final flying lap.
</p><p>The rain intensified in the final period, with several drivers venturing out on full wet tyres. <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> drivers <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a>, and Ricciardo, started a three-way battle for pole that came down to their final laps. Hamilton prevailed, with Ricciardo qualifying a career-best second in his home race. Rosberg started from third, alongside <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Magnussen" title="Kevin Magnussen">Kevin Magnussen</a>, who qualified his McLaren fourth in his Formula One debut. After Räikkönen's Q2 accident had left him twelfth, <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> gave Ferrari a decent result when he recovered to fifth after making the wrong tyre choice early in the period and started next to the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso</a> of <a href="/wiki/Jean-%C3%89ric_Vergne" title="Jean-Éric Vergne">Jean-Éric Vergne</a>. <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a> and debutant <a href="/wiki/Daniil_Kvyat" title="Daniil Kvyat">Daniil Kvyat</a> filled the next row of the grid, ahead of the <a href="/wiki/Williams_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams F1">Williams</a> pair of <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a>, who, like Gutiérrez, was demoted five places for a gearbox change.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-qualifying">Post-qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2014_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Post-qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Kevin_Magnussen" title="Kevin Magnussen">Kevin Magnussen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> were referred to the stewards for speeding under yellow flags following <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a>'s accident, but they were cleared of any wrongdoing.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> was called to the stewards for impeding <a href="/wiki/Esteban_Guti%C3%A9rrez" title="Esteban Gutiérrez">Esteban Gutiérrez</a> in Q1, but also escaped a penalty.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Pastor_Maldonado" title="Pastor Maldonado">Pastor Maldonado</a> failed to post a lap time during qualifying. He was allowed to start the race on the stewards' discretion, who judged that he was capable of lapping within the 107% limit based on his free practice times.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2014_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The race began at 17:00 local time. <a href="/wiki/Marussia_F1" title="Marussia F1">Marussia</a> drivers <a href="/wiki/Max_Chilton" title="Max Chilton">Max Chilton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jules_Bianchi" title="Jules Bianchi">Jules Bianchi</a> stalled on the grid, with Bianchi having a problem after the formation lap and Chilton stalling at the beginning of the formation lap. <a href="/wiki/Romain_Grosjean" title="Romain Grosjean">Romain Grosjean</a> started the race from the pit lane due to car modifications under parc fermé conditions<sup id="cite_ref-grid_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grid-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> and received a drive-through penalty for leaving the garage before the 15-minute signal. Seconds after the lights had gone out, <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kamui_Kobayashi" title="Kamui Kobayashi">Kamui Kobayashi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> were involved in a first-lap collision, putting Kobayashi and Massa out of the race whilst Räikkönen's Ferrari did not suffer any major damage and was able to continue the race without further problems. It was later confirmed Kobayashi had suffered a loss of rear brakes.
</p><p>Polesitter <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>'s engine lost a cylinder at the start of the race, which resulted in him dropping to third by the first corner.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> got a good start, allowing him to pass <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> in addition to his teammate, and at the first corner Rosberg led, with Ricciardo second and Hamilton third. Hamilton continued to slip down the order with his engine running on five cylinders - having fallen from first to fifth by the end of lap one - and on the second lap he was called into the pits to retire. Defending world champion <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> also had an early retirement due to engine issues, retiring on lap three. Rosberg dominantly won the race by almost twenty-five seconds, picking up his fourth career win, and his first since the previous year's <a href="/wiki/2013_British_Grand_Prix" title="2013 British Grand Prix">British Grand Prix</a>. <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> snatched second in front of his home crowd, making him the first Australian Formula One driver to finish on the podium in <a href="/wiki/Australian_Grand_Prix" title="Australian Grand Prix">his home race</a> as a world championship round. John Smith finished on the podium in 1983, but at the time, it was not yet an official Formula One world championship round. <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Magnussen" title="Kevin Magnussen">Kevin Magnussen</a> finished third, two seconds behind Ricciardo. This made him the first Formula One debutant since <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> at the <a href="/wiki/2007_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="2007 Australian Grand Prix">2007 Australian Grand Prix</a> seven years earlier to finish on the podium.
</p><p>Magnussen's teammate <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> took fourth after starting eleventh, finishing just over 3 seconds behind his teammate and half a minute behind Rosberg. He was followed by <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> in fifth. <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a> finished in sixth despite clipping the wall and causing a puncture on the right rear tire on lap 11. <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a> finished seventh, <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> a disappointing eighth despite winning the race <a href="/wiki/2013_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="2013 Australian Grand Prix">a year before</a> and driving for <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> instead of the now struggling <a href="/wiki/Lotus_F1" title="Lotus F1">Lotus-Renault</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jean-%C3%89ric_Vergne" title="Jean-Éric Vergne">Jean-Éric Vergne</a> in ninth and debutant <a href="/wiki/Daniil_Kvyat" title="Daniil Kvyat">Daniil Kvyat</a> in an impressive tenth, becoming the youngest points-scorer in Formula One.<sup id="cite_ref-Dan_record_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Dan_record-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The Ferraris suffered some electrical problems in the race, according to the team.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post_race">Post race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2014_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Post race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> was disqualified from the race for a breach of Article 5.1.4 of the Formula One Technical Regulations, which govern the maximum allowable rate at which fuel may flow into the engine. The team were also referred to the stewards for using an unauthorised method of measuring the fuel flow. <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Magnussen" title="Kevin Magnussen">Kevin Magnussen</a> was thus promoted to second with <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> up to third. It was Button's 50th and final podium finish, but the podium ceremony took place before Ricciardo's exclusion which meant Button did not take part in the proceedings.
</p><p>Red Bull immediately announced their intention to appeal the disqualification, claiming that the sensors provided by the FIA to measure the fuel flow were unreliable.<sup id="cite_ref-Dan_DQ_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Dan_DQ-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The appeal was heard by the FIA on 14 April, but it was rejected and the disqualification was upheld. This meant that McLaren got their first double podium since the 2012 Chinese Grand Prix.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Kamui_Kobayashi" title="Kamui Kobayashi">Kamui Kobayashi</a> was referred to the stewards as well for his involvement in the first-lap collision with <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a>. The stewards decided not to take any further action against him after they determined that the accident was triggered by a mechanical failure on his <a href="/wiki/Caterham_CT05" title="Caterham CT05">Caterham</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
