<p>The <b>1996 Canadian Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_Gilles_Villeneuve" title="Circuit Gilles Villeneuve">Circuit Gilles Villeneuve</a>, <a href="/wiki/Montreal" title="Montreal">Montreal</a> on 16 June 1996. It was the eighth race of the <a href="/wiki/1996_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1996 Formula One World Championship">1996 Formula One World Championship</a>.
</p><p>The 69-lap race was won from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> by Englishman <a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW18" title="Williams FW18">Williams</a>-<a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>. Local driver <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a>, Hill's teammate and the son of Gilles, finished second, with Frenchman <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a> third in a <a href="/wiki/Benetton_B196" title="Benetton B196">Benetton</a>-Renault.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1996_Canadian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> started from the back of the grid, as his crew were still working on his car as the field set off on the warm-up lap. This started a run of mechanical problems for the reigning double World Champion.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a> became the first retirement from 4th position in the leading Ferrari on lap 2 when his suspension failed, he was then followed out by <a href="/wiki/Ukyo_Katayama" title="Ukyo Katayama">Ukyo Katayama</a> in the second <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_Racing" title="Tyrrell Racing">Tyrrell</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ricardo_Rosset" title="Ricardo Rosset">Ricardo Rosset</a> in the second <a href="/wiki/Footwork_Arrows" title="Footwork Arrows">Footwork</a> when they collided at the final chicane on lap 7 due to <a href="/wiki/Ukyo_Katayama" title="Ukyo Katayama">Katayama</a> missing his braking point. Before <a href="/wiki/Ricardo_Rosset" title="Ricardo Rosset">Rosset's</a> teammate <a href="/wiki/Jos_Verstappen" title="Jos Verstappen">Jos Verstappen</a> in the leading <a href="/wiki/Footwork_Arrows" title="Footwork Arrows">Footwork</a> retired in the pits on lap 11 when his engine failed. <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> in the second <a href="/wiki/Jordan_196" title="Jordan 196">Jordan</a> after passing <a href="/wiki/Mika_Hakkinen" class="mw-redirect" title="Mika Hakkinen">Mika Hakkinen</a> for 6th position would also be forced the retire on lap 23 in the pits after his first pit stop when his clutch failed. <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> after starting at the back of the grid due to a clutch problem, was able to fight his way up to 13th position, but would soon retire due to driveshaft failure on lap 42 which started the first of three consecutive races which both <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_F310" title="Ferrari F310">Ferrari</a>'s would fail to finish a race due to mechanical failures.
For the second successive race, <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> spun off and hit the wall while trying to pass teammate <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a> on lap 43 at turn 4. Alesi continued to third place, while further behind <a href="/wiki/Martin_Brundle" title="Martin Brundle">Martin Brundle</a>, running fourth following Berger's exit, had to pit for a new nose-cone following a collision with <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Lamy" title="Pedro Lamy">Pedro Lamy</a>'s Minardi when Lamy inadvertently hit Brundle's front wing as he attempted to rejoin the track after a spin on lap 45, dropping Brundle to seventh and handing fourth place to <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a>'s McLaren. Brundle eventually recovered to sixth place behind <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> in the sister McLaren after getting past <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Herbert" title="Johnny Herbert">Johnny Herbert</a> courtesy of a faster final pit stop.
</p>
