<p>The <b>2016 Malaysian Grand Prix</b> (formally known as the <b>2016 Formula 1 Petronas Malaysia Grand Prix</b>)<sup id="cite_ref-formal_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-formal-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Auto_racing" title="Auto racing">motor race</a> that was held on 2 October 2016 at the <a href="/wiki/Sepang_International_Circuit" title="Sepang International Circuit">Sepang International Circuit</a> in <a href="/wiki/Selangor" title="Selangor">Selangor</a>, <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a>. The race marked the thirty-fifth running of the <a href="/wiki/Malaysian_Grand_Prix" title="Malaysian Grand Prix">Malaysian Grand Prix</a>, and the eighteenth time that the race has been run as a World Championship event since the championship's inception in <a href="/wiki/1950_Formula_One_season" title="1950 Formula One season">1950</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> entered the race leading the World Drivers' Championship by eight points, having reclaimed the championship lead from teammate <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> at the <a href="/wiki/2016_Singapore_Grand_Prix" title="2016 Singapore Grand Prix">previous race</a> in Singapore. Mercedes lead <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a>-<a href="/wiki/TAG_Heuer" title="TAG Heuer">TAG Heuer</a> in the World Constructors' Championship by 220 points.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> won the race, with teammate <a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a> finishing second to secure <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a>'s first one–two finish since the introduction of hybrid engines in <a href="/wiki/2014_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2014 Formula One World Championship">2014</a>. Nico Rosberg completed the podium, extending his championship lead to twenty-three points following the retirement of Lewis Hamilton.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2016_Malaysian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2016_Malaysian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The race was moved from its March date to October to allow organisers the opportunity to upgrade the circuit. The circuit was resurfaced, while drainage was improved to decrease the possibility of standing water from forming on the circuit. Nine corners were re-profiled, introducing negative camber to emphasise mechanical, rather than aerodynamic grip, and the drop at the apex of turn two removed.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2016_Malaysian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Lewis_Hamilton_engine_failure_2016_Malaysian_GP_2.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/Lewis_Hamilton_engine_failure_2016_Malaysian_GP_2.jpg/220px-Lewis_Hamilton_engine_failure_2016_Malaysian_GP_2.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/Lewis_Hamilton_engine_failure_2016_Malaysian_GP_2.jpg/330px-Lewis_Hamilton_engine_failure_2016_Malaysian_GP_2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/Lewis_Hamilton_engine_failure_2016_Malaysian_GP_2.jpg/440px-Lewis_Hamilton_engine_failure_2016_Malaysian_GP_2.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2560" data-file-height="1707" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Lewis_Hamilton_engine_failure_2016_Malaysian_GP_2.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>'s engine failure while in the lead proved to be a decisive moment in the championship.</div></div></div>
<p>At the start, <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> collided with <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> going into turn one, causing Rosberg to go into a spin to the back of the grid while Vettel damaged his front-left suspension in the process, retiring after getting to an escape road a few corners later. On lap 8, Romain Grosjean spun into the gravel trap due to brake failure once again, prematurely ending his race. 
Later on lap 41, <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>'s engine failed while he was in the lead of the race, forcing him to retire. <a href="/wiki/Esteban_Guti%C3%A9rrez" title="Esteban Gutiérrez">Esteban Gutiérrez</a> also retired when his front-left wheel came off the car in the latter stages of the race. <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> who inherited the lead of the race when Hamilton's engine failed went on to win the race. It was the fourth win of his F1 career. <a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a> finished second, giving <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a> their first one-two since Brazil in 2013, with <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> recovering to finish third from the spin on lap 1. For the next race in Japan, Vettel was handed a three-place grid penalty for causing the collision with Rosberg.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race <span id="Budgie_Nine"></span></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2016_Malaysian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Nine Australian men, subsequently dubbed the 'Budgie Nine' by Australian media, were arrested for <a href="/wiki/Flag_desecration" title="Flag desecration">intentional insult</a> and public indecency after celebrating Australia's win by stripping to their '<a href="/wiki/Budgie_smuggler" class="mw-redirect" title="Budgie smuggler">budgie smuggler</a>' swimming trunks, decorated with the Malaysian flag, and drinking beer from their shoes.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Australian driver Daniel Ricciardo said that the nine had not realized the effect their actions would have,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> and called for them to be released.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> After three days in custody the nine were charged with the lesser offence of public nuisance and released. The briefs had been made in Australia, not Malaysia.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
