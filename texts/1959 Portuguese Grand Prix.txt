<p>The <b>1959 Portuguese Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held at <a href="/wiki/Circuito_de_Monsanto" title="Circuito de Monsanto">Monsanto</a> on 23 August 1959. It was race 7 of 9 in the <a href="/wiki/1959_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1959 World Championship of Drivers">1959 World Championship of Drivers</a> and race 6 of 8 in the <a href="/wiki/1959_International_Cup_for_Formula_One_Manufacturers" class="mw-redirect" title="1959 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers">1959 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers</a>. It was the eighth <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_Grand_Prix" title="Portuguese Grand Prix">Portuguese Grand Prix</a> and the second to be held for the Formula One World Drivers' Championship. It was the third time the race was held at Monsanto and the first for Formula One. The race was held over 62 laps of the five kilometre circuit for a total race distance of 337 kilometres.
</p><p>The race was won by British driver <a href="/wiki/Stirling_Moss" title="Stirling Moss">Stirling Moss</a>, his eleventh Grand Prix victory, driving a <a href="/wiki/Cooper_T51" title="Cooper T51">Cooper T51</a> for privateer race team <a href="/wiki/Rob_Walker_Racing_Team" title="Rob Walker Racing Team">Rob Walker Racing Team</a>. Moss finished a lap ahead of American racer <a href="/wiki/Masten_Gregory" title="Masten Gregory">Masten Gregory</a> driving a similar Cooper T51 for the factory <a href="/wiki/Cooper_Car_Company" title="Cooper Car Company">Cooper Car Company</a> team. American <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Scuderia Ferrari</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Dan_Gurney" title="Dan Gurney">Dan Gurney</a> finished third in his <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_246_F1" title="Ferrari 246 F1">Ferrari Dino 246</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_report">Race report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1959_Portuguese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Race report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Wins in France and Germany had given <a href="/wiki/Tony_Brooks_(racing_driver)" title="Tony Brooks (racing driver)">Tony Brooks</a> a healthy second place in the championship behind <a href="/wiki/Jack_Brabham" title="Jack Brabham">Jack Brabham</a>. <a href="/wiki/Stirling_Moss" title="Stirling Moss">Stirling Moss</a> improved on his poor championship position with a dominating drive, lapping the entire field. <a href="/wiki/Phil_Hill" title="Phil Hill">Phil Hill</a> and <a href="/wiki/Graham_Hill" title="Graham Hill">Graham Hill</a> crashed into each other on lap 6, whilst Brabham had an enormous somersaulting crash from which he emerged unscathed. Gurney was the only <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> in the top eight with <a href="/wiki/Maurice_Trintignant" title="Maurice Trintignant">Maurice Trintignant</a> and <a href="/wiki/Harry_Schell" title="Harry Schell">Harry Schell</a> rounding off the top five.
</p><p>With Brabham failing to finish and Brooks finishing a distant five laps down in ninth, Moss closed the gap to just 9½ points behind Brabham and 5½ points behind Brooks in the championship race.
</p>
