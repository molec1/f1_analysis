<p>The <b>1998 Spanish Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 10 May 1998 at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Barcelona-Catalunya" title="Circuit de Barcelona-Catalunya">Circuit de Catalunya</a>. It was the fifth race of the <a href="/wiki/1998_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="1998 Formula One season">1998 Formula One season</a>. The 65-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> after he started from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>. His teammate <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> finished second and <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> took third.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_summary">Race summary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1998_Spanish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Race summary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> qualified in pole position, 0.7 seconds ahead of his <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> teammate <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> in second place, with Ferrari's <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> a further 0.8 seconds behind in third. The race proved to be a formality for Häkkinen, winning the race ahead of Coulthard in second, and Schumacher in third.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Arrows_Grand_Prix_International" title="Arrows Grand Prix International">Arrows</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Diniz" title="Pedro Diniz">Pedro Diniz</a> started from the pit lane due to stalling on the warm up lap. At the start the McLarens got away well, but Schumacher made a poor start and fell back to fifth behind his teammate <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a> and <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a>. They ran in these positions until the first round of pit stops, when Irvine delayed Fisichella sufficiently for his teammate Schumacher to emerge ahead of them both and regain third. Fisichella and Irvine continued to battle until lap 28, when Fisichella attempted a passing manoeuvre around the outside of Irvine, resulting in a collision spearing them both off into the gravel trap. This led to Fisichella's Benetton teammate, <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Wurz" title="Alexander Wurz">Alexander Wurz</a>, inheriting fourth place which he held until the finish. <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> was consistently faster than his teammate <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> throughout the race weekend, unable to match his pace, even though they were in the same car. Respected ex driver and pundit <a href="/wiki/Martin_Brundle" title="Martin Brundle">Martin Brundle</a> made the comment that Häkkinen was "in a class of his own".
</p><p>During the race, Michael Schumacher and <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Esteban_Tuero" title="Esteban Tuero">Esteban Tuero</a> were given 10 second stop-go penalties for pit lane speeding.<sup id="cite_ref-Atlas_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Atlas-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Stewart_Grand_Prix" title="Stewart Grand Prix">Stewart</a> of <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> earned two points by finishing in fifth place, which were team's first of the season, this was made possible by the new engine and chassis the team used (however teammate <a href="/wiki/Jan_Magnussen" title="Jan Magnussen">Jan Magnussen</a> ran with the old chassis).<sup id="cite_ref-Atlas_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Atlas-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Reigning World Champion <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a> finished in sixth place, after Williams had their worst qualifying result since the <a href="/wiki/1989_United_States_Grand_Prix" title="1989 United States Grand Prix">1989 United States Grand Prix</a>.
</p><p>On the final lap, Williams driver <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a> passed <a href="/wiki/Prost_Grand_Prix" title="Prost Grand Prix">Prost</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> for eight place when Trulli was incorrectly shown the <a href="/wiki/Racing_flags#Blue_flag" title="Racing flags">blue flags</a> as the marshalls had mistaken the Williams for a Ferrari.<sup id="cite_ref-Atlas_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Atlas-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<blockquote>
<p>"I am truly angry because I had to give up a great battle, and it isn't fair to lose a position because they are blind and can't recognise one car from another. Of course it doesn't matter much to finish eighth or ninth, but for a racer it matters. In a case of a blue flag I didn't have an alternative". Jarno Trulli<sup id="cite_ref-Atlas_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Atlas-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
</blockquote>
<p>Post race, Fisichella was given a $7,500<a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">USD</a> fine for the collision with Irvine.<sup id="cite_ref-Atlas_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Atlas-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1998_Spanish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>This was the first race of the 1998 season in which a driver failed to qualify due to not completing a time inside 107% of the pole position time. The unfortunate driver was <a href="/wiki/Ricardo_Rosset" title="Ricardo Rosset">Ricardo Rosset</a>, who failed to qualify by 7/100ths of a second.
<ul><li>This was also the slimmest-ever margin for which a driver failed to qualify due to the rule. <a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a> missed out by only 0.003 seconds at the <a href="/wiki/1999_French_Grand_Prix" title="1999 French Grand Prix">1999 French Grand Prix</a> but was permitted to race due to "exceptional circumstances".</li></ul></li></ul>
