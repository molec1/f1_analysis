<p>The <b>2014 Italian Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>Formula 1 Gran Premio d'Italia 2014</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 7 September at the <a href="/wiki/Autodromo_Nazionale_di_Monza" title="Autodromo Nazionale di Monza">Autodromo Nazionale di Monza</a> in <a href="/wiki/Monza" title="Monza">Monza</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lombardy" title="Lombardy">Lombardy</a>. It was the 13th round of the <a href="/wiki/2014_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2014 Formula One World Championship">2014 Formula One World Championship</a> and the 65th running of the <a href="/wiki/Italian_Grand_Prix" title="Italian Grand Prix">event</a> held as part of the series. The 53-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>. His teammate <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> finished second and <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> took third.
</p><p>Hamilton claimed the 36th pole position of his career by recording the fastest lap in qualifying but a slow start dropped him behind Rosberg. He returned to second by lap ten and drew closer to teammate Rosberg. He was instructed by radio to remain at least 2&#x200b;<span class="frac nowrap"><sup>1</sup>&#8260;<sub>2</sub></span> seconds behind Rosberg but opted to ignore the message and took the lead on lap 29 when Rosberg ran off the circuit. Hamilton maintained the lead for the rest of the race to achieve his sixth victory of the season and the 28th of his career. There were three lead changes among two different drivers during the course of the race.
</p><p>The result saw Hamilton lower Rosberg's lead in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">World Drivers' Championship</a> to 22 points with <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> remaining in third position. <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a> moved to fourth position while <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> did not finish the race and fell to fifth. Mercedes increased their lead in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">World Constructors' Championship</a> to 182 points over the second-placed <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a>. Williams overtook Ferrari for third with six races left in the season.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2014_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Monza_aerial_photo.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/65/Monza_aerial_photo.jpg/220px-Monza_aerial_photo.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="146" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/65/Monza_aerial_photo.jpg/330px-Monza_aerial_photo.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/65/Monza_aerial_photo.jpg/440px-Monza_aerial_photo.jpg 2x" data-file-width="572" data-file-height="379" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Monza_aerial_photo.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The <a href="/wiki/Autodromo_Nazionale_di_Monza" title="Autodromo Nazionale di Monza">Autodromo Nazionale di Monza</a>, where the race was held.</div></div></div>
<p>The 2014 <a href="/wiki/Italian_Grand_Prix" title="Italian Grand Prix">Italian Grand Prix</a> was the 13th of the 19 round <a href="/wiki/2014_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2014 Formula One World Championship">2014 Formula One World Championship</a>, and the 65th running of the event as part of the series. It was held on 7 September at the 5.793&#160;km (3.600&#160;mi) 11-turn <a href="/wiki/Autodromo_Nazionale_di_Monza" title="Autodromo Nazionale di Monza">Autodromo Nazionale di Monza</a> in <a href="/wiki/Monza" title="Monza">Monza</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lombardy" title="Lombardy">Lombardy</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-preview_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-preview-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Drag_reduction_system" title="Drag reduction system">drag reduction system</a> (DRS) had two activation zones for the race: one was on the <a href="/wiki/Straight_(racing)" title="Straight (racing)">straight</a> between the second Lesmo corner and the Ascari <a href="/wiki/Chicane" title="Chicane">chicane</a>, and the second was on the straight linking Parabolica to the Rettifilo chicane.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_tyres" title="Formula One tyres">Tyre</a> supplier <a href="/wiki/Pirelli" title="Pirelli">Pirelli</a> brought the white-banded medium and orange-banded hard dry tyre compounds to the race.<sup id="cite_ref-:8_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:8-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Before the race <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> led the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">World Drivers' Championship</a> with 220 <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Championship_points_scoring_systems" title="List of Formula One World Championship points scoring systems">points</a>, ahead of teammate <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> in second and <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> in third. <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> was fourth on 121 points and <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a> was a further eleven points behind in fifth.<sup id="cite_ref-points_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Mercedes led the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">World Constructors' Championship</a> with 411 points, and <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a> were second on 254 points. <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> and <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> were third and fourth and <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> fifth.<sup id="cite_ref-points_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Following a collision between Hamilton and Rosberg on the second lap of the preceding <a href="/wiki/2014_Belgian_Grand_Prix" title="2014 Belgian Grand Prix">Belgian Grand Prix</a>, the third such incident surrounding the Mercedes team in 2014, which went back to qualifying for the <a href="/wiki/2014_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="2014 Monaco Grand Prix">Monaco Grand Prix</a> when Rosberg was accused of spoiling Hamilton's lap,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> the Mercedes team principal <a href="/wiki/Toto_Wolff" title="Toto Wolff">Toto Wolff</a> threatened sanctions against one driver if a similar situation happened again and insisted that both men were obliged to serve the team's interests.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton said his objective at Monza was to regain some of the lost ground in the Drivers' Championship and would not give up until the season-closing round in <a href="/wiki/2014_Abu_Dhabi_Grand_Prix" title="2014 Abu Dhabi Grand Prix">Abu Dhabi</a>: "It's as big as it's been all season so I've got a lot of work ahead of me but anything can happen in this sport."<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Rosberg was aware of his situation in the title battle and was confident of achieving a strong result: "I'm focused on taking the maximum points possible in the remaining seven races and I know the team is too, starting with a top result this weekend."<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Kamui_Kobayashi_2010_Malaysia.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/17/Kamui_Kobayashi_2010_Malaysia.jpg/170px-Kamui_Kobayashi_2010_Malaysia.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="213" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/17/Kamui_Kobayashi_2010_Malaysia.jpg/255px-Kamui_Kobayashi_2010_Malaysia.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/17/Kamui_Kobayashi_2010_Malaysia.jpg/340px-Kamui_Kobayashi_2010_Malaysia.jpg 2x" data-file-width="800" data-file-height="1002" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Kamui_Kobayashi_2010_Malaysia.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Kamui_Kobayashi" title="Kamui Kobayashi">Kamui Kobayashi</a> <i>(pictured in 2010)</i> returned to Caterham after missing the <a href="/wiki/2014_Belgian_Grand_Prix" title="2014 Belgian Grand Prix">Belgian Grand Prix</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>The Parabolica corner was altered ahead of the race. A large portion of its <a href="/wiki/Run-off_area" title="Run-off area">gravel trap</a> was replaced with asphalt, reportedly for a possible return of the <a href="/wiki/Superbike_World_Championship" title="Superbike World Championship">Superbike World Championship</a> in <a href="/wiki/2015_Superbike_World_Championship" title="2015 Superbike World Championship">2015</a> after series officials raised concerns about a lack of run-off areas on the circuit.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-whitingparabolica_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-whitingparabolica-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The change was heavily criticised by the sport's fan base on social media and certain drivers who felt it removed part of the challenge from Parabolica.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile</a> (FIA) race director <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Whiting" title="Charlie Whiting">Charlie Whiting</a>, defended the changes, saying drivers and the FIA asked for changes to be made for safety reasons but acknowledged that the corner might not provide as much of a challenge than before.<sup id="cite_ref-whitingparabolica_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-whitingparabolica-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Others joined in Whiting's defence, including <a href="/wiki/Romain_Grosjean" title="Romain Grosjean">Romain Grosjean</a>, who believed the gravel trap's removal would allow drivers to find their limit quicker because of a lack of fear of a major accident.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>There was one driver change for the race. Having driven in one of the two <a href="/wiki/Caterham_F1" title="Caterham F1">Caterham</a> cars in lieu of regular driver <a href="/wiki/Kamui_Kobayashi" title="Kamui Kobayashi">Kamui Kobayashi</a> at the preceding Belgian Grand Prix, three-time <a href="/wiki/24_Hours_of_Le_Mans" title="24 Hours of Le Mans">24 Hours of Le Mans</a> winner and <a href="/wiki/2011_Formula_Nippon_Championship" title="2011 Formula Nippon Championship">2011 Formula Nippon</a> champion <a href="/wiki/Andr%C3%A9_Lotterer" title="André Lotterer">André Lotterer</a> was replaced by Kobayashi.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Lotterer planned to race at Monza but did not do so after Caterham team principal <a href="/wiki/Colin_Kolles" title="Colin Kolles">Colin Kolles</a> ran <a href="/wiki/World_Series_Formula_V8_3.5" title="World Series Formula V8 3.5">Formula Renault 3.5 Series</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Merhi" title="Roberto Merhi">Roberto Merhi</a> in the first practice session. He told the press that he needed the maximum amount of driving time because he was a rookie.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Kobayashi said he was looking forward to testing an updated <a href="/wiki/Caterham_CT05" title="Caterham CT05">CT05</a> car and did not doubt the team's decision to change drivers.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Lotus_F1" title="Lotus F1">Lotus</a> reserve driver <a href="/wiki/Charles_Pic" title="Charles Pic">Charles Pic</a> replaced Grosjean for the first free practice session for the first time in 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/2014_Deutsche_Tourenwagen_Masters" title="2014 Deutsche Tourenwagen Masters">Deutsche Tourenwagen Masters</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Juncadella" title="Daniel Juncadella">Daniel Juncadella</a> used Pérez's <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a> vehicle for the session's first half hour,<sup id="cite_ref-:1_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Giedo_van_der_Garde" title="Giedo van der Garde">Giedo van der Garde</a> drove <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a> car.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice">Practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2014_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Three practice sessions—two on Friday and a third on Saturday—were held before the Sunday race. The Friday morning and afternoon sessions lasted 90 minutes each; the third, one-hour session was held on Saturday morning.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> In the first session, which took place in dry and sunny weather,<sup id="cite_ref-:1_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton was fastest with a time of 1 minute, 26.187 seconds, six-tenths of a second faster than <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> in second, who was fastest until Hamilton's lap, and Rosberg third. Alonso, <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Magnussen" title="Kevin Magnussen">Kevin Magnussen</a>, Vettel, <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a>, <a href="/wiki/Daniil_Kvyat" title="Daniil Kvyat">Daniil Kvyat</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a> occupied position four to ten.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> During the session several drivers noted the hard compound tyres took longer than anticipated to reach their optimum working temperature,<sup id="cite_ref-:2_17-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> and some went off the track while acquainting themselves with a low-<a href="/wiki/Downforce" title="Downforce">downforce</a> set-up. Ricciardo had DRS trouble and his session ended early when he entered the <a href="/wiki/Pit_stop" title="Pit stop">pit lane</a> with a suspected <a href="/wiki/Kinetic_energy_recovery_system" title="Kinetic energy recovery system">energy recovery system</a> problem, and Magnussen had rear-braking issues.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Rosberg set the fastest lap of the second session at 1 minute, 26.225 seconds; Hamilton was 0.061 seconds behind in second despite electrical repairs taking an hour in the garage, with Räikkönen third. His teammate Alonso was fourth-fastest, with Bottas fifth and Button sixth.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Vettel (who ran wide at Parabolica corner on his fastest lap), Magnussen, Massa and Ricciardo followed in the top ten.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Multiple drivers went off the track during the session. The Lotus duo of Grosjean and Maldonado struggled to generate heat into their tyres and had further difficulty with braking stability and steering.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> In the third session, which was held in warmer and sunnier weather, Hamilton set the fastest lap of the weekend so far of 1 minute, 25.519 seconds, ahead of Alonso in second and the Williams duo of Bottas and third and fourth. Button, Vettel, Räikkonen, Kvyat, Ricciardo and Hülkenberg were in positions five to ten. Reoccurring <a href="/wiki/Transmission_(mechanics)" title="Transmission (mechanics)">gearbox</a> problems forced Rosberg to abandon his short run programme early.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2014_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Lewis_Hamilton_October_2014.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c3/Lewis_Hamilton_October_2014.jpg/170px-Lewis_Hamilton_October_2014.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="237" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c3/Lewis_Hamilton_October_2014.jpg/255px-Lewis_Hamilton_October_2014.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c3/Lewis_Hamilton_October_2014.jpg/340px-Lewis_Hamilton_October_2014.jpg 2x" data-file-width="784" data-file-height="1094" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Lewis_Hamilton_October_2014.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> had the 36th <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> of his career, and took his sixth victory of the season the following day.</div></div></div>
<p>Saturday afternoon's <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_motorsport_terms#Q" title="Glossary of motorsport terms">qualifying session</a> was divided into three parts. The first part ran for 18 minutes, eliminating cars that finished 17th or below. The <a href="/wiki/107%25_rule" title="107% rule">107% rule</a> was in effect, requiring drivers to reach a time within 107 per cent of the quickest lap to qualify. The second session lasted 15 minutes, eliminating cars that finished 11th to 16th. The final part ran for 12 minutes and determined <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> to tenth. Cars in the final session were not allowed to change tyres, using the tyres with which they set their quickest lap times in the second session.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Qualifying took place in warm weather.<sup id="cite_ref-:3_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton was fastest in all three sessions to claim his fifth pole position of the season, the 36th of his career, and his first since the <a href="/wiki/2014_Spanish_Grand_Prix" title="2014 Spanish Grand Prix">Spanish Grand Prix</a> with a lap of 1 minute, 24.109 seconds.<sup id="cite_ref-:3_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> He was joined on the <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_motorsport_terms#G" title="Glossary of motorsport terms">grid</a>'s front row by Rosberg (using a repaired gearbox)<sup id="cite_ref-:3_24-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> who had improved on his first timed lap,<sup id="cite_ref-:4_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> but an <a href="/wiki/Understeer_and_oversteer" title="Understeer and oversteer">oversteer</a> through the Ascari chicane prevented him from achieving pole position.<sup id="cite_ref-:9_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:9-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> The two Williams duo of Bottas and Massa were third and fourth; both drivers could not improve their fastest times on their second attempts.<sup id="cite_ref-:5_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:5-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> They were ahead of McLaren's Magnussen and Button in fifth and sixth; the latter lost time through the Lesmo corners on his final timed lap.<sup id="cite_ref-:6_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:6-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Alonso was slightly slower than Vettel after his first lap,<sup id="cite_ref-:4_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> but he improved slightly for seventh.<sup id="cite_ref-:5_27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:5-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> He attributed his pace to recording similar lap times on four sets of tyres.<sup id="cite_ref-:6_28-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:6-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Vettel qualified higher than his teammate Ricciardo for the fourth time in the past six races to secure eighth. Ricciardo started ninth and Pérez tenth.<sup id="cite_ref-:4_25-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Kvyat was the fastest driver not to qualify for the final session;<sup id="cite_ref-qualyresults_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualyresults-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> he took a ten-place grid penalty for an overnight engine change, his sixth of the season.<sup id="cite_ref-enginechange_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-enginechange-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Hence, Räikkonen inherited 11th.<sup id="cite_ref-:4_25-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Räikkonen lacked car <a href="/wiki/Grip_(auto_racing)" title="Grip (auto racing)">grip</a> and locked his front tyres,<sup id="cite_ref-:6_28-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:6-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> ending his first timed lap early because cars exiting the pit lane reduced his visibility for the Rettifilo chicane.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> After preparing for a second lap, he locked his right-front wheel and drove onto a run-off area.<sup id="cite_ref-:7_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:7-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Jean-%C3%89ric_Vergne" title="Jean-Éric Vergne">Jean-Éric Vergne</a> was 12th with Hülkenberg 13th after traffic slowed his preparation for a lap at Parabolica and felt the car was loose. Sutil had problems driving but gradually improved his car's balance as he drove, and improved on his final lap to secure 14th, ahead of teammate <a href="/wiki/Esteban_Guti%C3%A9rrez" title="Esteban Gutiérrez">Esteban Gutiérrez</a>, who made a minor error on his final lap.<sup id="cite_ref-:5_27-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:5-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:6_28-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:6-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Maldonado failed to advance beyond the first session;<sup id="cite_ref-qualyresults_29-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualyresults-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> his teammate Grosjean did five laps after missing the first 13 minutes as Lotus repaired a fluid leak.<sup id="cite_ref-:5_27-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:5-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> Kobayashi <a href="/wiki/Drafting_(aerodynamics)" title="Drafting (aerodynamics)">slipstreamed</a> his teammate <a href="/wiki/Marcus_Ericsson" title="Marcus Ericsson">Marcus Ericsson</a> to pass <a href="/wiki/Jules_Bianchi" title="Jules Bianchi">Jules Bianchi</a> for 18th,<sup id="cite_ref-:5_27-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:5-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> with Bianchi not improving because he slid sideways through the Ascari chicane and other drivers were faster on the medium compound tyres.<sup id="cite_ref-:7_32-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:7-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> His teammate <a href="/wiki/Max_Chilton" title="Max Chilton">Max Chilton</a> qualified 20th and Ericsson took 22nd;<sup id="cite_ref-:5_27-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:5-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> the latter was hindered by a lack of running on the medium tyres following an engine problem in the third practice session.<sup id="cite_ref-:7_32-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:7-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2014_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The weather at the start was dry and sunny, with the air temperature 25&#160;°C (77&#160;°F) and the track temperature from 37 to 42&#160;°C (99 to 108&#160;°F).<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> A right-front wheel cooler fitted to Hamilton's car detached and crunched on the front wing, causing Mercedes to push him to his grid slot following a <a href="/wiki/Parade_lap" title="Parade lap">reconnaissance lap</a>. An inspection by Mercedes found there to be no problems for Hamilton.<sup id="cite_ref-:10_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:10-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Unlike previous years when softer tyre compounds were selected, making one pit stops was calculated to be faster than two by around 12 seconds.<sup id="cite_ref-:9_26-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:9-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Ericsson was ordered to begin from the pit lane after being penalised for driving too fast under double waved <a href="/wiki/Racing_flags#Yellow_flag" title="Racing flags">yellow flag</a> conditions in the third practice session.<sup id="cite_ref-pitlanestart_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pitlanestart-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> When the race began from its <a href="/wiki/Standing_start" title="Standing start">standing start</a> at 14:00 <a href="/wiki/Central_European_Summer_Time" title="Central European Summer Time">Central European Summer Time</a> (<a href="/wiki/UTC%2B02:00" title="UTC+02:00">UTC+02:00</a>), an incorrect mode dropped Hamilton to fourth and moving Rosberg to the lead into the Rettifilo chicane.<sup id="cite_ref-:9_26-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:9-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Wheelspin" title="Wheelspin">Wheelspin</a> dropped Bottas to 11th.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Magnussen passed Massa on the left for second going into the Rettifilo chicane. Pérez accelerated faster than Alonso and passed him before Alonso repassed Pérez on the left at Curva Grande corner.<sup id="cite_ref-:9_26-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:9-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Nico_Rosberg_Stars_and_Cars_2014_2_amk_(cropped).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/51/Nico_Rosberg_Stars_and_Cars_2014_2_amk_%28cropped%29.jpg/170px-Nico_Rosberg_Stars_and_Cars_2014_2_amk_%28cropped%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="206" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/51/Nico_Rosberg_Stars_and_Cars_2014_2_amk_%28cropped%29.jpg/255px-Nico_Rosberg_Stars_and_Cars_2014_2_amk_%28cropped%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/51/Nico_Rosberg_Stars_and_Cars_2014_2_amk_%28cropped%29.jpg/340px-Nico_Rosberg_Stars_and_Cars_2014_2_amk_%28cropped%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1890" data-file-height="2285" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Nico_Rosberg_Stars_and_Cars_2014_2_amk_(cropped).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> led 26 of the first 29 laps before an error put off the track, moving Hamilton into the lead.</div></div></div>
<p>Vettel moved to fifth by the end of the first lap but his teammate Ricciardo was put wide onto the chicane's run-off area, dropping four positions over the same distance.<sup id="cite_ref-:10_34-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:10-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:11_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:11-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Massa attempted to take second from Magnussen by braking later than he did at the start of lap two as Rosberg pulled away from the two.<sup id="cite_ref-:9_26-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:9-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Magnssen went wide at Curva Grande corner and blocked another pass from Massa.<sup id="cite_ref-:10_34-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:10-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> DRS was enabled on the next lap and Hamilton used it to attack Massa, allowing Magnussen to pull away from Massa.<sup id="cite_ref-:11_37-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:11-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Magnussen defended but ran deep entering the Rettifilo chicane, allowing Massa to slipstream him into the Variante della Roggia chicane and steered right to claim second place.<sup id="cite_ref-:9_26-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:9-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:11_37-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:11-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Magnussen entered the turn off the <a href="/wiki/Racing_line" title="Racing line">racing line</a>, spun his tyres on its exit, allowing Hamilton past for third into the first Lesmo corner with better acceleration. Massa and Hamilton pulled away from Magnussen who was being caught by Vettel, Button, Alonso and Pérez.<sup id="cite_ref-:9_26-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:9-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:10_34-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:10-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Hülkenberg lost ninth to Räikkonen on the same lap and Bottas was unable to find the space at Parabolica corner to attempt a pass. Bottas did however overtake Hülkenberg for tenth at the Rettifilo chicane. Chilton entered the Variante della Roggia chicane too fast on lap six, and hit the second set of <a href="/wiki/Curb" title="Curb">kerbs</a> at the corner, launching his airborne car into the barrier, ending his race.<sup id="cite_ref-:10_34-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:10-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Safety_car" title="Safety car">safety car</a> was not needed.<sup id="cite_ref-:12_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:12-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> Rosberg led by almost four seconds by the conclusion of the lap and moved his brake bias towards the front of his car.<sup id="cite_ref-:9_26-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:9-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> As Massa went faster Rosberg did the same in response. On the following lap, Rosberg locked his tyres into the Rettifilo chicane at 206&#160;mph (332&#160;km/h), drove onto an escape road and slalomed through obstacles to retain the lead.<sup id="cite_ref-:9_26-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:9-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> His advantage over Massa fell to two seconds.<sup id="cite_ref-:13_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:13-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton used DRS to steer left before the Rettifilo chicane and passed Massa for second on the 10th lap.<sup id="cite_ref-:9_26-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:9-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:11_37-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:11-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Hamilton began drawing closer to Rosberg but required a further few laps to pull clear from Massa; he did not attack Rosberg and stayed out of his teammate's slipstream, nursed his tyres and minimised his fuel usage.<sup id="cite_ref-:9_26-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:9-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Bottas overtook Räikkönen for ninth on lap 13;<sup id="cite_ref-:11_37-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:11-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> the next lap, he slipstreamed past Pérez on the left for ninth into the Rettifilo chicane.<sup id="cite_ref-:10_34-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:10-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:13_39-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:13-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> Bottas overcame Alonso's defence on the outside to take seventh on the main straight during lap 16.<sup id="cite_ref-:12_38-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:12-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> Two laps later,<sup id="cite_ref-:13_39-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:13-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> Bottas passed Button on his right for sixth.<sup id="cite_ref-:10_34-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:10-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:12_38-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:12-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> Red Bull elected to bring Vettel into the pit lane at the end of the same lap for hard compound tyres to try and pass Magnussen on pit stop strategy rather than duel Bottas. Pérez followed in response the following lap.<sup id="cite_ref-:9_26-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:9-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Bottas passed Magnussen for fourth at the Rettifilo chicane on lap 21. Magnussen and Alonso made their pit stops on the next lap, rejoining in ninth and eleventh,<sup id="cite_ref-:11_37-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:11-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> behind Vettel and Pérez separated them. Magnussen slid exiting the Variante della Roggia chicane trying to pass Pérez.<sup id="cite_ref-:10_34-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:10-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Daniel_at_2014_Italian_Grand_Prix_(04).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0b/Daniel_at_2014_Italian_Grand_Prix_%2804%29.jpg/170px-Daniel_at_2014_Italian_Grand_Prix_%2804%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="255" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0b/Daniel_at_2014_Italian_Grand_Prix_%2804%29.jpg/255px-Daniel_at_2014_Italian_Grand_Prix_%2804%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0b/Daniel_at_2014_Italian_Grand_Prix_%2804%29.jpg/340px-Daniel_at_2014_Italian_Grand_Prix_%2804%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2425" data-file-height="3637" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Daniel_at_2014_Italian_Grand_Prix_(04).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> finished fifth after late race move up the field</div></div></div>
<p>Massa made his pit stop from third on lap 24, and emerged in fifth. Hamilton received instructions for an engine mode change and Rosberg entered the pit lane from the lead on the next lap and handed the lead to Hamilton.<sup id="cite_ref-:11_37-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:11-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Bottas made his stop on the same lap and rejoined alongside Vettel on the main straight but was forced wide, allowing Magnussen to pass him.<sup id="cite_ref-:10_34-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:10-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton took his pit stop on the 26th lap and handed back first to teammate Rosberg.<sup id="cite_ref-:11_37-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:11-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Bottas also fell behind Pérez but re-passed the latter going into the Rettifilo chicane.<sup id="cite_ref-:10_34-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:10-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton's race engineer Peter Bonnington radioed him to stay at least 2&#x200b;<span class="frac nowrap"><sup>1</sup>&#8260;<sub>2</sub></span> seconds behind teammate Rosberg and conserve his tyres for an attack later on.<sup id="cite_ref-:11_37-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:11-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton was aware from previous experiences earlier in the season that the method to pass a driver was to do so when tyre grip was optimal otherwise it would not have been possible to draw close enough to effect a pass.<sup id="cite_ref-:9_26-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:9-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> He recorded the fastest lap at that point to move within seven-tenths of a second of his teammate Rosberg by the start of lap 28, causing debate among Mercedes pit lane staff.<sup id="cite_ref-:9_26-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:9-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:13_39-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:13-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Massa at this point was 12 seconds behind and Bonnington allowed Hamilton to attack earlier than planned. Rosberg's front brake temperatures overheated and his attempt to conserve his rear tyres by moving his brake bias towards the front of his car compromised them.<sup id="cite_ref-:9_26-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:9-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton used DRS and as Rosberg applied his brakes, he drove onto the Rettifilo chicane's run-off area and slalomed his way past some obstacles, promoting Hamilton to the lead.<sup id="cite_ref-:9_26-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:9-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:11_37-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:11-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Alonso's energy recovery system failed, triggering a complete electrical shutdown half a minute later, and stopped at the side of Rettifilo's run-off area to retire for the first time in 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-:9_26-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:9-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:10_34-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:10-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Button attempted to pass Pérez into the Rettifilo chicane but the two narrowly avoided contact.<sup id="cite_ref-:10_34-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:10-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Bottas tried to pass Magnussen into the same turn but defensive driving from Magnussen forced Bottas to cut the chicane on lap 31.<sup id="cite_ref-:14_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:14-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> On the 34th lap, Ricciardo turned right and braked later than Räikkönen for ninth into the Rettifilo chicane.<sup id="cite_ref-:10_34-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:10-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:14_40-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:14-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Hamilton began to pull away from Rosberg, leading his teammate by 4.3 seconds by the start of lap 36.<sup id="cite_ref-:11_37-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:11-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Bottas steered left into the Rettifilo chicane the following lap and Magnussen turned right, causing Bottas to mount a kerb to avoid a collision; Bottas overtook Magnussen.<sup id="cite_ref-:9_26-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:9-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Soon after, the stewards imposed a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_regulations#Penalties" title="Formula One regulations">five-second stop-and-go penalty</a> on Magnussen for putting Bottas off the track in the latter's earlier passing attempt.<sup id="cite_ref-:11_37-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:11-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Button turned right to pass Pérez into the Rettifilo chicane for seventh on lap 39. Pérez then drew alongside Button through the Curva Grande and Variante della Roggia turns. Pérez steered right and was forced wide onto the Curva di Lesmo chicane kerb in passing Button for seventh.<sup id="cite_ref-:9_26-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:9-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:10_34-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:10-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:11_37-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:11-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> On the following lap, Bottas passed Vettel at the Rettifilo chicane for fourth and his teammate Ricciardo overtook Button for seventh at the same turn.<sup id="cite_ref-:9_26-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:9-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:13_39-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:13-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> Ricciardo caused Pérez to lock his brakes before the Rettifilo chicane and moved lanes to take seventh at the Variante della Roggia chicane on lap 41.<sup id="cite_ref-:10_34-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:10-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:11_37-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:11-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:14_40-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:14-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Felipe_Massa_podium_-_2014_Italian_Grand_Prix_(02).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fe/Felipe_Massa_podium_-_2014_Italian_Grand_Prix_%2802%29.jpg/170px-Felipe_Massa_podium_-_2014_Italian_Grand_Prix_%2802%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="213" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fe/Felipe_Massa_podium_-_2014_Italian_Grand_Prix_%2802%29.jpg/255px-Felipe_Massa_podium_-_2014_Italian_Grand_Prix_%2802%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fe/Felipe_Massa_podium_-_2014_Italian_Grand_Prix_%2802%29.jpg/340px-Felipe_Massa_podium_-_2014_Italian_Grand_Prix_%2802%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="786" data-file-height="983" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Felipe_Massa_podium_-_2014_Italian_Grand_Prix_(02).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> finished third to secure his first podium result since the <a href="/wiki/2013_Spanish_Grand_Prix" title="2013 Spanish Grand Prix">2013 Spanish Grand Prix</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>Button passed Pérez at the second attempt into the Rettifilo chicane but had a slow exit after locking his tyres, allowing Pérez to retake eighth by putting Button wide into the Variante della Roggia chicane.<sup id="cite_ref-:9_26-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:9-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:10_34-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:10-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:14_40-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:14-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> Ricciardo caught and passed Magnussen on his left at the Rettifilo chicane for sixth place on lap 44 despite locking his tyres and drifting sideways. Ricciardo received a message to chase his teammate Vettel, who defended fifth from Ricciardo into the Rettifilo chicane on lap 47 but was slow leaving the turn.<sup id="cite_ref-:10_34-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:10-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:11_37-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:11-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:14_40-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:14-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> Both drivers were alongside through the Curva Grande corner and Ricciardo turned left to pass Vettel for fifth at the Variante della Roggia chicane.<sup id="cite_ref-:9_26-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:9-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:11_37-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:11-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Brake problems caused Gutiérrez to hit Grosjean at the Rettifilo chicane on the 51st lap, puncturing the former's right-rear tyre, who slowly drove into the pit lane.<sup id="cite_ref-:14_40-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:14-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:19_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:19-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Kvyat's brakes failed on lap 52, causing him to turn left onto the grass at the Rettifilo chicane to avoid hitting Räikkönen, and clipping a polystyrene marker as he rejoined the track.<sup id="cite_ref-:9_26-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:9-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:10_34-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:10-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton won the race by 3.1 seconds over Rosberg.<sup id="cite_ref-:10_34-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:10-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-results_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-results-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> Massa took his first podium result of the season in third, and his first since the <a href="/wiki/2013_Spanish_Grand_Prix" title="2013 Spanish Grand Prix">2013 Spanish Grand Prix</a>. Bottas took fourth, ahead of the Red Bull duo of Ricciardo and Vettel in fifth and sixth. Pérez, Button, Räikkönen and Magnussen rounded out the top ten after his five-second time penalty was applied. Kvyat, Hülkenberg, Vergne, Maldonado and Sutil, Kobayashi and Bianchi, Gutiérrez and Ericsson were the final classified finishers.<sup id="cite_ref-:10_34-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:10-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> It was Hamilton's sixth victory of 2014, his second at Monza, and the 28th of his career.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> There were three lead changes in the race; two drivers reached the front of the field. Hamilton led twice for a total of 27 laps, more than any other competitor.<sup id="cite_ref-results_42-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-results-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2014_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the podium ceremony following the race, Rosberg was booed by the crowd, repeating a similar incident towards him at the previous race in Belgium for a collision with Hamilton, which was the catalyst for their displeasure.<sup id="cite_ref-:15_44-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:15-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton told the press he felt uncomfortable with the fans behaving in such a manner: "I've had it here, years and years ago, and it's great personally for me to have such great support, from the Ferrari fans and the Mercedes fans. But when I was up there, it was awkward for me to hear them booing Nico because I just don't like that in sport."<sup id="cite_ref-:15_44-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:15-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> Wolff affirmed that booing should not be heard on the podium but acknowledged Formula One is an emotional sport for its fans while also saying: "It's a sport and sport should unite."<sup id="cite_ref-:16_45-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:16-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> When asked about the booing in the press conference, Rosberg replied while it was "not nice" he hoped over time the fans would forgive him for his past actions.<sup id="cite_ref-:16_45-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:16-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> At the podium interviews, conducted by former driver <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a>, Massa said he was happy to finish third and was certain luck was beside him with "a lot more to come."<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Toto_Wolff_2014.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/01/Toto_Wolff_2014.jpg/170px-Toto_Wolff_2014.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="213" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/01/Toto_Wolff_2014.jpg/255px-Toto_Wolff_2014.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/01/Toto_Wolff_2014.jpg/340px-Toto_Wolff_2014.jpg 2x" data-file-width="511" data-file-height="640" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Toto_Wolff_2014.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Toto_Wolff" title="Toto Wolff">Toto Wolff</a> denied theories that Rosberg's race-lead mistake on the 29th lap was deliberate.</div></div></div>
<p>In the hours following the race, conspiracy theories emerged on social media concerning the circumstances of Rosberg losing the lead to Hamilton on lap 29. They suggested the incident was an act of revenge for the collision between Rosberg and Hamilton at the Belgian Grand Prix two weeks prior.<sup id="cite_ref-:17_47-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:17-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> Three-time world champion <a href="/wiki/Jackie_Stewart" title="Jackie Stewart">Jackie Stewart</a> said he felt the move was "a bit too easy" and appeared to indicate the error was done purposely: "I thought [Rosberg] could have least made an effort to get round the corner. I first thought 'that's wise', because he knew it wasn't a difficult thing to believe; the second time I thought: 'Hello, what's going on here?'"<sup id="cite_ref-:18_48-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:18-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> Wolff denied allegations the mistake was deliberate, suggesting Rosberg was under pressure because of the championship battle but admitted it was uncommon for Rosberg to run wide twice: "Only a paranoid mind could come up with such an idea."<sup id="cite_ref-:18_48-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:18-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> Rosberg said Hamilton's fast pace prompted him to push hard and that there was a large probability of <a href="/wiki/Flat_spot" title="Flat spot">flat-spotting</a> a tyre and driving onto the run-off area was the safer option.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> He stated he learnt about the theory and affirmed there was "no possible reason" for him to purposely drive off the circuit.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> Television pictures apparently showed Wolff smiling moments after Rosberg's error. Wolff said the broadcast was not live and that the transmission of pit-to-car radio before Rosberg's mistake about him asking Hamilton to slow to preserve his tyres for a late race attack was inaccurate.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The stewards deemed Gutiérrez responsible for hitting Grosjean and imposed a 20-second time penalty on him although they accepted he had brake problems.<sup id="cite_ref-:19_41-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:19-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> Gutiérrez stated his brake problems had manifested in "an inconsistency that sometimes you cannot predict" and that the contact with Grosjean was "an unfortunate moment."<sup id="cite_ref-:19_41-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:19-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> Sauber team principal <a href="/wiki/Monisha_Kaltenborn" title="Monisha Kaltenborn">Monisha Kaltenborn</a> deemed the incident "unnecessary" and Grosjean said he was unsure as to what happened with Gutiérrez.<sup id="cite_ref-:19_41-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:19-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> Williams performance chief <a href="/wiki/Rob_Smedley" title="Rob Smedley">Rob Smedley</a> said the team were investigating Bottas' slow start that lost him positions. Williams believed it to be <a href="/wiki/Clutch" title="Clutch">clutch</a>-related problem.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup> Bottas stated he felt he was too assertive with his clutch and his race was hindered by a lack of acceleration leaving the chicane: "The start on the formation lap – when we always do the final checks – was a bit compromised because Lewis had a slow getaway, so that maybe hurt a little bit. But definitely the tyres were not in the optimum window."<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Alonso was convinced he could have finished fifth had his car not stopped with an energy recovery system failure, saying he felt quicker than Ricciardo and revealed his team changed their approach, prompting him to slow after his problem emerged.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup> He stated the remainder of the season was "not going to be too different" after a strenuous campaign in the past 12 rounds but that Ferrari was prepared: "It is the way at the moment but we cannot do anymore than this."<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup> Bottas told the press post-race that Magnussen's driving was "on the limit" but was unsure whether the latter deserved a penalty. Magnussen was "frustrated" upon receiving his second consecutive time-penalty and stated he would review the incident and carry over lessons into the next race of 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-:20_56-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:20-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> While initial reactions in the United Kingdom disagreed with the stewards decision,<sup id="cite_ref-:10_34-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:10-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:11_37-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:11-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:14_40-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:14-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> Smedley felt the penalty was justified.<sup id="cite_ref-:20_56-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:20-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> Red Bull team principal <a href="/wiki/Christian_Horner" title="Christian Horner">Christian Horner</a> praised Ricciardo's overtakes and called it a strength of his: "For somebody that had a question mark over his overtaking coming into the year, he [has shown it] is right up there with the best."<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The result saw Hamilton lower Rosberg's lead in the Drivers' Championship to 22 points, with Ricciardo remaining in third place on 166 points. By finishing fourth Bottas passed the non-finishing Alonso for fourth and the latter fell to fifth.<sup id="cite_ref-points_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Mercedes further extended their advantage in the Constructors' Championship to lead the second-placed Red Bull by 182 points. Williams overtook Ferrari to move into third place and McLaren were with 110 points with six races left in the season.<sup id="cite_ref-points_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
