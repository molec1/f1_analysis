<p>The <b>1988 Canadian Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 12 June 1988 at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_Gilles_Villeneuve" title="Circuit Gilles Villeneuve">Circuit Gilles Villeneuve</a>, <a href="/wiki/Montreal" title="Montreal">Montreal</a>. It was the fifth race of the <a href="/wiki/1988_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1988 Formula One World Championship">1988 Formula One World Championship</a>. The 69-lap race was won from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> by Brazilian driver <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/4" title="McLaren MP4/4">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Honda_in_Formula_One" title="Honda in Formula One">Honda</a>, with French teammate <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> second and Belgian <a href="/wiki/Thierry_Boutsen" title="Thierry Boutsen">Thierry Boutsen</a> third in a <a href="/wiki/Benetton_B188" title="Benetton B188">Benetton</a>-<a href="/wiki/Ford_Motor_Company" title="Ford Motor Company">Ford</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1988_Canadian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1988_Canadian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Canadian Grand Prix returned to the Formula One calendar after a year's absence. In the meantime, major changes had been made to the Circuit Gilles Villeneuve: the start-finish line, pit lane and facilities had been moved from the top end of the track to the bottom end, necessitating the removal of two turns, while other turns had been re-profiled slightly.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLarens</a> once again dominated qualifying, with <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> taking his fifth consecutive <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> by just under 0.2 seconds from <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferraris</a> of <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> and <a href="/wiki/Michele_Alboreto" title="Michele Alboreto">Michele Alboreto</a> filled the second row, while the <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a> of <a href="/wiki/Alessandro_Nannini" title="Alessandro Nannini">Alessandro Nannini</a> was the fastest of the <a href="/wiki/Naturally_aspirated_engine" title="Naturally aspirated engine">naturally-aspirated</a> cars in fifth, just under 2.3 seconds behind Senna. <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a> was sixth in his <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Lotus</a>, followed by <a href="/wiki/Thierry_Boutsen" title="Thierry Boutsen">Thierry Boutsen</a> in the second Benetton, <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Cheever" title="Eddie Cheever">Eddie Cheever</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Arrows_Grand_Prix_International" title="Arrows Grand Prix International">Arrows</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a>, while <a href="/wiki/Philippe_Streiff" title="Philippe Streiff">Philippe Streiff</a> put in a strong performance to take tenth, which would turn out to be the best-ever grid position for the <a href="/wiki/Automobiles_Gonfaronnaises_Sportives" title="Automobiles Gonfaronnaises Sportives">AGS</a> team. Also achieving its best-ever grid placing was <a href="/wiki/EuroBrun" title="EuroBrun">EuroBrun</a>, as <a href="/wiki/Stefano_Modena" title="Stefano Modena">Stefano Modena</a> took 15th.
</p><p>The Saturday session saw <a href="/wiki/Derek_Warwick" title="Derek Warwick">Derek Warwick</a> suffer a big accident in his Arrows. Turning into the chicane into the new start-finish straight, Warwick slid on dirt kicked up moments before by Streiff's AGS. The Arrows spun onto the inside kerb and became airborne, then bounced several times before hitting what is now known as the "Wall of Champions" at unabated speed. Warwick was briefly knocked unconscious and hurt his back; he received aid from fellow Briton Mansell, who had suffered a similar crash at <a href="/wiki/1987_Japanese_Grand_Prix" title="1987 Japanese Grand Prix">Suzuka</a> the previous year. However, Warwick was declared fit to race and took up his 16th position on the grid.
</p><p>During qualifying, Williams team owner <a href="/wiki/Frank_Williams_(Formula_One)" title="Frank Williams (Formula One)">Frank Williams</a> announced that he had concluded a deal with <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a> that would see the team have exclusive use of the French company's <a href="/wiki/V10_engine" title="V10 engine">V10 engines</a> from the <a href="/wiki/1989_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1989 Formula One World Championship">1989</a> season onwards. Williams said that "for better or worse you need an association with a major manufacturer to be successful in Formula One".
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1988_Canadian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the start, Prost led away from Senna, the Ferraris and the Benettons. After ten laps, Berger began having issues with the fuel system of his Ferrari. On lap 19, Senna passed Prost at the L'Epingle hairpin as they came up to traffic, the Brazilian driver thus taking a lead he would not lose. Nannini retired from fourth position on lap 15 with electrical trouble, while Berger retired with similar problems on lap 23. Meanwhile, Mansell passed his old rival Piquet in the Lotus, before his Judd engine failed on lap 29; teammate <a href="/wiki/Riccardo_Patrese" title="Riccardo Patrese">Riccardo Patrese</a> suffered the same fate four laps later.
</p><p>On lap 34, Alboreto retired with an engine failure, promoting Boutsen to third. With many of the front runners out, minor teams had a clear chance of scoring points. By the middle of the race Philippe Streiff had brought his AGS up to fifth place, ahead of <a href="/wiki/Andrea_de_Cesaris" title="Andrea de Cesaris">Andrea de Cesaris</a>' <a href="/wiki/Rial_Racing" title="Rial Racing">Rial</a> in sixth. However, Streiff retired on lap 41 with a rear suspension failure, while de Cesaris ran out of fuel with three laps to go. This promoted <a href="/wiki/Ivan_Capelli" title="Ivan Capelli">Ivan Capelli</a> in the <a href="/wiki/March_Engineering" title="March Engineering">March</a> to fifth and <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Palmer" title="Jonathan Palmer">Jonathan Palmer</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_Racing" title="Tyrrell Racing">Tyrrell</a> to sixth.
</p><p>Senna finished just under six seconds ahead of Prost, with Boutsen a further 45 seconds back. Piquet was fourth in the Lotus, albeit a lap down on Senna, with Capelli and Palmer completing the top six. Warwick, despite his injury, finished just outside the points in seventh, having battled with teammate Cheever until the American retired on lap 31 with a broken throttle cable.
</p><p>Senna set the fastest lap of the race on lap 53 with a time of 1:24.973. Boutsen's third place marked the first time since the <a href="/wiki/1983_Dutch_Grand_Prix" title="1983 Dutch Grand Prix">1983 Dutch Grand Prix</a> that a naturally-aspirated car had legally finished on a Formula One podium.
</p>
