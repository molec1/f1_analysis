<p>The <b>1968 German Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at the <a href="/wiki/N%C3%BCrburgring" title="Nürburgring">Nürburgring</a> on 4 August 1968. It was race 8 of 12 in both the <a href="/wiki/1968_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1968 World Championship of Drivers">1968 World Championship of Drivers</a> and the <a href="/wiki/1968_International_Cup_for_Formula_One_Manufacturers" class="mw-redirect" title="1968 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers">1968 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers</a>. The race was held in extremely wet and foggy conditions, and British driver <a href="/wiki/Jackie_Stewart" title="Jackie Stewart">Jackie Stewart</a>, racing with a broken wrist, won the race by a margin of four minutes in what is widely considered to be one of the greatest victories in the history of Formula One.<sup id="cite_ref-espn_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-espn-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The race is also notable for <a href="/wiki/Dan_Gurney" title="Dan Gurney">Dan Gurney</a>´s choice of a full face helmet, making him the first driver to do so in Grand Prix racing.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1968_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1968_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After <a href="/wiki/Jo_Siffert" title="Jo Siffert">Jo Siffert</a> had surprisingly won the <a href="/wiki/1968_British_Grand_Prix" title="1968 British Grand Prix">previous race at Brands Hatch</a>, the paddock arrived at the <a href="/wiki/N%C3%BCrburgring" title="Nürburgring">Nürburgring</a> almost unchanged. Equally unchanged was the weather: with rain over the entire weekend, this was to be the fifth wet race in a row. <a href="/wiki/BMW" title="BMW">BMW</a> entered a <a href="/wiki/Lola_Cars" title="Lola Cars">Lola</a>-built <a href="/wiki/Formula_Two" title="Formula Two">Formula Two</a> car driven by <a href="/wiki/Hubert_Hahne" title="Hubert Hahne">Hubert Hahne</a> in order to evaluate their competitiveness in Formula One.<sup id="cite_ref-gp_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gp-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice_and_qualifying">Practice and qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1968_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Practice and qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On Saturday, conditions were so poor, with visibility down to mere ten yards, that the organizers scheduled an additional practice session for Sunday morning. Still many drivers slid off the track during the morning session.<sup id="cite_ref-espn_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-espn-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Eventually, <a href="/wiki/Jacky_Ickx" title="Jacky Ickx">Jacky Ickx</a> took <a href="/wiki/Pole_positions" class="mw-redirect" title="Pole positions">pole positions</a> by a full 10 seconds from second placed <a href="/wiki/Chris_Amon" title="Chris Amon">Chris Amon</a>, both in a <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>. <a href="/wiki/Jackie_Stewart" title="Jackie Stewart">Jackie Stewart</a> in his <a href="/wiki/Matra_MS10" title="Matra MS10">Matra MS10</a> was down in sixth place on the grid.<sup id="cite_ref-gp_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gp-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> At the time, Ickx became the youngest person ever to sit on pole, a record beaten 14 years later when <a href="/wiki/Andrea_de_Cesaris" title="Andrea de Cesaris">Andrea de Cesaris</a> achieved pole position at the <a href="/wiki/1982_United_States_Grand_Prix_West" title="1982 United States Grand Prix West">1982 United States Grand Prix West</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1968_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Even with the conditions treacherous, 200,000 spectators turned up for the race on Sunday afternoon.<sup id="cite_ref-espn_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-espn-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The race turned out to be a one-man show by Scotsman <a href="/wiki/Jackie_Stewart" title="Jackie Stewart">Jackie Stewart</a>. While <a href="/wiki/Graham_Hill" title="Graham Hill">Graham Hill</a> took the lead at the start, by the end of the first lap Stewart had moved into first place and built a nine-second lead. He put his superior <a href="/wiki/Dunlop_Rubber" title="Dunlop Rubber">Dunlop</a> wet tires to great effect and by the end of lap 2, had extended his lead to 34 seconds. When the race ended after 14 laps, Stewart crossed the line more than 4 minutes in front of second placed Hill. The eventual World Champion had spun on lap 11, but was able to get out of the car, push it into the right direction and keep going before third-placed <a href="/wiki/Jochen_Rindt" title="Jochen Rindt">Jochen Rindt</a> could catch up.<sup id="cite_ref-gp_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gp-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Chris_Amon" title="Chris Amon">Chris Amon</a> had battled with Hill for 11 laps over second place, rarely having more than a second between the two, until Amon spun out of the race on the same lap as Hill did.<sup id="cite_ref-espn_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-espn-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Reactions">Reactions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1968_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Reactions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Jackie_Stewart" title="Jackie Stewart">Stewart</a> described the race as a "teeth gritting effort" in his autobiography. About the first lap he wrote:
</p>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r960796168">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>Visibility is so pathetically poor I can't even see Chris' car in front of me [...] I am simply driving into this great wall of spray. I pull out to pass him but the spray is dense and I'm driving blind.</p><div class="templatequotecite">—&#8201;<cite><a href="/wiki/Jackie_Stewart" title="Jackie Stewart">Jackie Stewart</a>, Winning Is Not Enough</cite></div></blockquote>
<p>The race has been described as Stewart's best drive ever, with the Scot later confirming he felt the same way.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
