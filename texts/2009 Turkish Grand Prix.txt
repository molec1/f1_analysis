<p>The <b>2009 Turkish Grand Prix</b> was the seventh motor race of the <a href="/wiki/2009_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2009 Formula One season">2009 Formula One season</a>. It was held on 7 June 2009 at <a href="/wiki/Istanbul_Park" class="mw-redirect" title="Istanbul Park">Istanbul Park</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tuzla,_Istanbul" title="Tuzla, Istanbul">Tuzla</a>, <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> led the Drivers' Championship by 16 points from his teammate at <a href="/wiki/Brawn_GP" title="Brawn GP">Brawn</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> going into the race,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> while Brawn GP led the Constructors' Championship by 43.5 points from <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a>.
</p><p>The race was won by <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> of Red Bull Racing completing the podium. Vettel had qualified on pole but a mistake on the first lap scuppered his chances of victory.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2009_Turkish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice">Practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2009_Turkish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In Friday practice 1, Williams, Ferrari and McLaren performed well, Nico Rosberg, Lewis Hamilton and Jarno Trulli posting the top 3 times. Brawn, Toyota, Red Bull and Renault ran mid field during the session with BMW and STR backmarking.
</p><p>In Friday practice 2, Williams, Renault and Red Bull were the most consistent performers, Heikki Kovalainen, Fernando Alonso and Robert Kubica posted the top 3 times. Ferrari, Brawn and Toyota ran mid field during the session with Force India and STR backmarking.
</p><p>After both Friday practice sessions, individual performances were quite mixed, the only consistent constructor was Williams as BMW and McLaren saw mixed results, while top running teams Brawn and Red Bull ran upper mid field for both sessions.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2009_Turkish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Nick_Heidfeld_2009_Turkey_5.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3d/Nick_Heidfeld_2009_Turkey_5.jpg/220px-Nick_Heidfeld_2009_Turkey_5.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3d/Nick_Heidfeld_2009_Turkey_5.jpg/330px-Nick_Heidfeld_2009_Turkey_5.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3d/Nick_Heidfeld_2009_Turkey_5.jpg/440px-Nick_Heidfeld_2009_Turkey_5.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3252" data-file-height="2167" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Nick_Heidfeld_2009_Turkey_5.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> qualified his <a href="/wiki/BMW_in_Formula_One#BMW_Sauber" title="BMW in Formula One">BMW Sauber</a> in eleventh position.</div></div></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> dominated the qualifying session on Saturday 6 June 2009, setting the fastest time in all three parts of qualifying to claim his second pole position of the season, and third of his career.
</p><p>The first knockout session, to eliminate the five slowest cars, claimed the scalp of reigning World Champion <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> – only the second time the Englishman had failed to progress from the first session. Also eliminated were <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet_Jr." title="Nelson Piquet Jr.">Nelson Piquet Jr.</a>, <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9bastien_Buemi" title="Sébastien Buemi">Sébastien Buemi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> and <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9bastien_Bourdais" title="Sébastien Bourdais">Sébastien Bourdais</a>. They all produced times in the 1:28's.
</p><p>In the second knockout session, the top 10, who progressed to the final shoot-out for pole, were separated by just 0.4 seconds, with <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> just sneaking through. <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kazuki_Nakajima" title="Kazuki Nakajima">Kazuki Nakajima</a>, <a href="/wiki/Timo_Glock" title="Timo Glock">Timo Glock</a>, <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a> were knocked out, Heidfeld qualifying 11th for the second race in succession.
</p><p>The third session to determine pole position was a tense battle with numerous drivers thinking they had claimed pole only for their time to be bettered.
</p><p>Vettel was on provisional pole with a time of 1:28.801, with just a few minutes of the session remaining. Vettel's team mate <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> then posted a 1:28.6, only for Button to go two-tenths of a second quicker after the chequered flag fell. Vettel, however, still had one lap left in him and went quicker again, reclaiming pole with a 1:28.316. Button's teammate <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barichello" class="mw-redirect" title="Rubens Barichello">Rubens Barichello</a> also improved on his final lap, moving up to third position, ahead of Webber, with a 1:28.5.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2009_Turkish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>History would favour Vettel, the previous four Turkish Grands Prix having been won from pole position. Button however had two more laps of fuel in qualifying, meaning he would have been on pole fuel-corrected.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Jenson_Button_2009_Turkey_2.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/65/Jenson_Button_2009_Turkey_2.jpg/220px-Jenson_Button_2009_Turkey_2.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/65/Jenson_Button_2009_Turkey_2.jpg/330px-Jenson_Button_2009_Turkey_2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/65/Jenson_Button_2009_Turkey_2.jpg/440px-Jenson_Button_2009_Turkey_2.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2046" data-file-height="1364" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Jenson_Button_2009_Turkey_2.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> won the race after taking the lead from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">polesitter</a> <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> on the first lap.</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Mark_Webber_2009_Turkey.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7d/Mark_Webber_2009_Turkey.jpg/220px-Mark_Webber_2009_Turkey.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7d/Mark_Webber_2009_Turkey.jpg/330px-Mark_Webber_2009_Turkey.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7d/Mark_Webber_2009_Turkey.jpg/440px-Mark_Webber_2009_Turkey.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2915" data-file-height="1943" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Mark_Webber_2009_Turkey.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> qualified fourth and finished second.</div></div></div>
<p>Vettel initially made a strong start from pole, beating Button into the first corner. Vettel then ran wide at the challenging Turn 9, gifting Button the lead. Button's <a href="/wiki/Brawn_GP" title="Brawn GP">Brawn</a> team-mate had a less fortunate start, a clutch problem dropping him from third on the grid to thirteenth by the end of the first lap. Webber was running third, having initially been passed by <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> before the Australian regained his position. <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> had moved up to fifth position, ahead of <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>. Reigning world champion Hamilton had lost a place on the first lap, meaning he was running in 17th position out of 20 runners.
</p><p>At the front Button began to open up a lead over Vettel but the action was in the midfield, <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> getting past both Ferraris to move up to fifth by lap six. On lap eight, Barrichello spun trying to pass <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> after a wheel-to-wheel battle from Turns 9 to 16. The Brazilian dropped to seventeenth but quickly regained two places before losing part of his front wing in a clash with <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a> on lap eleven. Barrichello pitted on lap thirteen, conversely teammate Button had serenely opened up a 5.6 second lead at the front of the race.
</p><p>The leaders began pitting for their first stops on lap 15, with Vettel the first to stop, followed by Button two laps later and Webber and Rosberg on lap 18. Significantly, Vettel was fuelled lighter on a three-stop strategy, in contrast to the other leaders two-stop strategy. Vettel was therefore able to close the gap on Button after the first stops with his lighter car, but was unable to make the pass he needed before his second stop on lap 29. Vettel exited the pits behind Webber, with all the leaders due to make one more pit stop.
</p><p>On lap 37, Hamilton was lapped by championship leader Button, who made his second stop along with Webber on lap 43. Vettel briefly ran second but had to make his third pit stop on lap 48, demoting him back to third position. Trulli emerged from the pit stops in fourth, narrowly ahead of Rosberg, while <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a> and <a href="/wiki/Timo_Glock" title="Timo Glock">Timo Glock</a> had moved into the final points paying positions as Raikkonen struggled and <a href="/wiki/Kazuki_Nakajima" title="Kazuki Nakajima">Kazuki Nakajima</a> was delayed by a left front wheel covering failing to attach in his final stop. Meanwhile, Barrichello's miserable race finally ended as he retired with a gearbox problem while running near the back of the field.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> This was the first time that a Brawn GP car was not running at the finish of a race.
</p><p>Although Vettel closed the gap to Webber to just 0.7 seconds as the chequered flag fell, there were no significant positional changes in the final laps as Button cruised to his sixth win from the first seven races, a feat only matched by <a href="/wiki/Alberto_Ascari" title="Alberto Ascari">Alberto Ascari</a>, <a href="/wiki/Juan_Manuel_Fangio" title="Juan Manuel Fangio">Juan Manuel Fangio</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jim_Clark" title="Jim Clark">Jim Clark</a> and <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> – who all won the World Championship after achieving this feat. Button also became the first Englishman to score record four consecutive victories since <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> in <a href="/wiki/F1_1992" class="mw-redirect" title="F1 1992">1992</a>, who too went on to win the title that season.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The race attendance was reported as a meager 36,000, with the venue capable of holding 150,000.
</p>
