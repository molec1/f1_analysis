<p>The <b>1997 Canadian Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at <a href="/wiki/Circuit_Gilles_Villeneuve" title="Circuit Gilles Villeneuve">Circuit Gilles Villeneuve</a> on 15 June 1997. The race was stopped early on lap 54 after a big crash involving <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Panis" title="Olivier Panis">Olivier Panis</a>, who broke his legs and would be unable to start the next seven Grands Prix. <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> won ahead of <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a> in the Benetton and <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> in the Jordan. <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> had been leading, but was delayed for over a lap by a clutch problem during his second pit stop, shortly before Panis's crash. On lap 2, local driver <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a> crashed into the wall on the exit of the final corner. This wall  would later be known as the 'Wall of Champions', after three former World Champions, including Villeneuve, crashed into it separately in the <a href="/wiki/1999_Canadian_Grand_Prix" title="1999 Canadian Grand Prix">1999 race</a>.
</p><p>It also marked the debut of <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Wurz" title="Alexander Wurz">Alexander Wurz</a>, driving for <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a> in place of his compatriot <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a>. Berger had been suffering from a sinus illness for some time and during his time off his father was killed in a light aircraft accident.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
