<p>The <b>1990 Canadian Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 10 June 1990 at <a href="/wiki/Circuit_Gilles_Villeneuve" title="Circuit Gilles Villeneuve">Circuit Gilles Villeneuve</a>. It was Race 5 of 16 in the <a href="/wiki/1990_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1990 Formula One World Championship">1990 Formula One World Championship</a>. It was the 28th <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Grand_Prix" title="Canadian Grand Prix">Canadian Grand Prix</a> and the 12th to be held at the Circuit Gilles Villeneuve. The race was held over 70 laps of the 4.390 km (2.728 mi) circuit for a race distance of 307 kilometres.
</p><p>The race was won for the second time by Brazilian driver <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> driving a <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/5B" class="mw-redirect" title="McLaren MP4/5B">McLaren MP4/5B</a>. It was Senna's third win for the season having won the season-opening <a href="/wiki/1990_United_States_Grand_Prix" title="1990 United States Grand Prix">United States Grand Prix</a> and the <a href="/wiki/1990_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="1990 Monaco Grand Prix">Monaco Grand Prix</a> just two weeks earlier. Senna won by ten seconds over fellow Brazilian <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a> who drove a <a href="/wiki/Benetton_B190" title="Benetton B190">Benetton B190</a>. Three second further back in third was British driver <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> driving a <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_641" title="Ferrari 641">Ferrari 641</a>.
</p><p>The win allowed Senna to gain a twelve-point lead in the drivers' championship over his McLaren team mate <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a>. His nearest competitive rival, Ferrari driver <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> had less than half of Senna's points.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1990_Canadian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-qualifying_report">Pre-qualifying report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1990_Canadian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Pre-qualifying report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the Friday morning pre-qualifying session, <a href="/wiki/Robert_Moreno" title="Robert Moreno">Robert Moreno</a> was fastest by over three tenths of a second in the <a href="/wiki/EuroBrun" title="EuroBrun">EuroBrun</a>, despite a continued lack of testing. <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Grouillard" title="Olivier Grouillard">Olivier Grouillard</a> was second fastest in the sole <a href="/wiki/Osella" title="Osella">Osella</a>, with the <a href="/wiki/Larrousse" title="Larrousse">Larrousse</a>-<a href="/wiki/Lola_Cars" title="Lola Cars">Lolas</a> in third and fourth. For the first time this season, <a href="/wiki/Aguri_Suzuki" title="Aguri Suzuki">Aguri Suzuki</a> was faster than his team-mate <a href="/wiki/%C3%89ric_Bernard" title="Éric Bernard">Éric Bernard</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Walker_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Walker-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In fifth place, failing to pre-qualify by a hundredth of a second, was <a href="/wiki/Gabriele_Tarquini" title="Gabriele Tarquini">Gabriele Tarquini</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Automobiles_Gonfaronnaises_Sportives" title="Automobiles Gonfaronnaises Sportives">AGS</a>, with his team-mate <a href="/wiki/Yannick_Dalmas" title="Yannick Dalmas">Yannick Dalmas</a> in sixth. It was the fourth double failure to pre-qualify for the French team. The other three entrants were a long way behind: <a href="/wiki/Bertrand_Gachot" title="Bertrand Gachot">Bertrand Gachot</a> was seventh in the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Coloni" title="Scuderia Coloni">Coloni</a>, nearly 16 seconds slower than Moreno. <a href="/wiki/Claudio_Langes" title="Claudio Langes">Claudio Langes</a> had been fired by EuroBrun prior to this event, but had been reinstated; he was eighth fastest, nearly 19 seconds slower than his team-mate Moreno.<sup id="cite_ref-Walker_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Walker-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Slowest again, and nearly 22 seconds off the pace, was <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Giacomelli" title="Bruno Giacomelli">Bruno Giacomelli</a>, who managed seven laps in the <a href="/wiki/Life_Racing_Engines" title="Life Racing Engines">Life</a> before his engine failed. The team were at this point talking to <a href="/wiki/Brabham" title="Brabham">Brabham</a> and <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Lotus</a>, hoping to purchase some used <a href="/wiki/Judd_(engine)" title="Judd (engine)">Judd</a> CV engines, the same type that EuroBrun were using.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1990_Canadian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_report">Race report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1990_Canadian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Race report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In a race with a McLaren front row, the team looked strong. Senna maintained the lead coming into the first corner with Berger second, but the Austrian was deemed to have jumped the start. A few laps into the race it was announced that Berger would have a one-minute penalty added to his race time. As a consequence, after a round of pitstops for new tyres, Senna allowed his teammate to pass him going into the hairpin so that the Austrian could set about gaining time in relation to his competitors.
</p><p>The weather conditions were moist, making for a mildly slippery track. This caused spins for many. The first of the spinners was <a href="/wiki/Pierluigi_Martini" title="Pierluigi Martini">Pierluigi Martini</a> who spun off at turn 2 on the first lap. Thierry Boutsen, the <a href="/wiki/1989_Canadian_Grand_Prix" title="1989 Canadian Grand Prix">1989 winner</a>, spun mid-race while trying to pass Prost approaching a corner, and hit the Ligier of Nicola Larini as he spun.
</p><p>Nannini spun off the track into a tyre wall. Shortly afterwards on lap 26, Jean Alesi lost control while challenging another car and spun into the same tyre barrier, ending up on top of Nannini's abandoned <a href="/wiki/Benetton_B190" title="Benetton B190">Benetton B190</a>. The Benetton was written off when hit by the <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_019" title="Tyrrell 019">Tyrrell</a>, leaving team mechanics with a massive rebuild before the next race in <a href="/wiki/1990_Mexican_Grand_Prix" title="1990 Mexican Grand Prix">Mexico</a>.
</p><p>In the end, Berger had crossed the line first but was awarded a one-minute penalty for a jumped start, which was added to his overall race time, dropping him to fourth in the final order. Following Berger's penalty, Senna took the victory, whilst Piquet finished second after a determined battle with the two Ferraris where he forced his way past Prost's Ferrari going into the hairpin. It was the Benetton driver's first podium finish since the <a href="/wiki/1988_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="1988 Australian Grand Prix">1988 Australian Grand Prix</a>. Prost was later passed at the same place by teammate Mansell who went on to finish third.
</p>
