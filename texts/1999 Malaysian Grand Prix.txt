<p>The <b>1999 Malaysian Grand Prix </b>(formally the <b>I Petronas Malaysian Grand Prix</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> race held on 17 October 1999 at the new <a href="/wiki/Sepang_International_Circuit" title="Sepang International Circuit">Sepang International Circuit</a> near <a href="/wiki/Sepang_District" title="Sepang District">Sepang</a>, <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a>. It was the fifteenth race of the <a href="/wiki/1999_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1999 Formula One World Championship">1999 Formula One World Championship</a>.
</p><p>The 56-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_F399" title="Ferrari F399">Ferrari</a>, after starting from second position. Teammate <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>, in his first race back after breaking his leg at the <a href="/wiki/1999_British_Grand_Prix" title="1999 British Grand Prix">British Grand Prix</a> at <a href="/wiki/Silverstone_Circuit" title="Silverstone Circuit">Silverstone</a>, finished second having started from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>, while <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> finished third in a <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/14" title="McLaren MP4/14">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Mercedes_AMG_High_Performance_Powertrains" title="Mercedes AMG High Performance Powertrains">Mercedes</a>. The win gave Irvine a four-point lead over Häkkinen in the Drivers' Championship with one race remaining, though it would turn out to be his 4th and last F1 victory.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1999_Malaysian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This was the first <a href="/wiki/Malaysian_Grand_Prix" title="Malaysian Grand Prix">Malaysian Grand Prix</a> since a <a href="/wiki/Formula_Holden" title="Formula Holden">Formula Holden</a> event in 1995, but the first time at Formula One world championship level. <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> returned to Formula One having recovered from his broken leg, and took pole position by nearly a second from <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> team-mate <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a>, with the McLarens of <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> third and fourth respectively. At the start, Schumacher led away from Irvine, Coulthard, Häkkinen and <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a>. On lap 4, Schumacher slowed and allowed Irvine to pass him, then proceeded to block the McLarens. Coulthard forced his way past Schumacher on lap 5 and pursued Irvine for the lead, only to retire on lap 15 with fuel pressure problems.
</p><p>Back in second place, Schumacher slowed again in order to allow Irvine to build an advantage. Then, as the first round of pit stops loomed, Schumacher accelerated the pace in order to stay ahead of Häkkinen. Realising this, McLaren gambled on giving Häkkinen half a tank of fuel, hoping it would be enough to get him out of the pits ahead of Schumacher. The gamble failed, as Schumacher stayed ahead of the Finn and proceeded to block him again, allowing Irvine to extend his lead to 20 seconds.
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Ferrari_F399_bargeboard_Museo_Ferrari.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/36/Ferrari_F399_bargeboard_Museo_Ferrari.jpg/220px-Ferrari_F399_bargeboard_Museo_Ferrari.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/36/Ferrari_F399_bargeboard_Museo_Ferrari.jpg/330px-Ferrari_F399_bargeboard_Museo_Ferrari.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/36/Ferrari_F399_bargeboard_Museo_Ferrari.jpg/440px-Ferrari_F399_bargeboard_Museo_Ferrari.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2560" data-file-height="1707" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Ferrari_F399_bargeboard_Museo_Ferrari.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Ferrari_F399" title="Ferrari F399">Ferrari F399</a>'s bargeboard</div></div></div>
<p>Irvine's lead was not big enough for him to stay ahead after his second pit stop, but Ferrari were sure that Häkkinen would have to stop again. He did, emerging in fourth place behind <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Herbert" title="Johnny Herbert">Johnny Herbert</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Stewart_Grand_Prix" title="Stewart Grand Prix">Stewart</a>. Schumacher slowed once again to allow Irvine to retake the lead, while Häkkinen forced his way past Herbert for third.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Irvine duly took the chequered flag one second ahead of Schumacher, with Häkkinen a further eight seconds back.
</p><p>Immediately after the race, the Ferraris were disqualified due to an infringement on their <a href="/wiki/Bargeboard_(aerodynamics)" title="Bargeboard (aerodynamics)">bargeboards</a>. This meant that Häkkinen and McLaren were effectively handed their respective championships by default. However, Ferrari appealed against the FIA's decision in court and both drivers were subsequently reinstated.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>With one race remaining, Irvine led the Drivers' Championship by four points over Häkkinen, 70 to 66. Similarly, Ferrari held a four-point lead over McLaren in the Constructors' Championship, 118 to 114.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1999_Malaysian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> suffered an engine failure on the formation lap.</li>
<li>This race would prove to be the last points for <a href="/wiki/Stewart_Grand_Prix" title="Stewart Grand Prix">Stewart Grand Prix</a> and <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Herbert" title="Johnny Herbert">Johnny Herbert</a>.</li>
<li>This was the third race in a row in which a driver scored his last Grand Prix victory. <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a> took his last win <a href="/wiki/1999_Italian_Grand_Prix" title="1999 Italian Grand Prix">two races prior</a> while Johnny Herbert took his last win at <a href="/wiki/1999_European_Grand_Prix" title="1999 European Grand Prix">the preceding race</a>.</li>
<li>Last-ever win for <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a> and last-ever win for British driver in a <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Scuderia Ferrari</a> team.</li></ul>
