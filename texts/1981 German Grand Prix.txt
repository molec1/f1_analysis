<p>The <b>1981 German Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held at <a href="/wiki/Hockenheimring" title="Hockenheimring">Hockenheimring</a> on 2 August 1981. It was the tenth race of the <a href="/wiki/1981_FIA_Formula_One_World_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="1981 FIA Formula One World Championship">1981 FIA Formula One World Championship</a>.
</p><p>The 45-lap race was won by Brazilian driver <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/Brabham_BT49" title="Brabham BT49">Brabham</a>-<a href="/wiki/Ford_Motor_Company" title="Ford Motor Company">Ford</a>. Frenchman <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> finished second in a <a href="/wiki/Renault_RE30" title="Renault RE30">Renault</a>, having started from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>, with compatriot <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Laffite" title="Jacques Laffite">Jacques Laffite</a> third in a <a href="/wiki/Ligier_JS17" title="Ligier JS17">Ligier</a>-<a href="/wiki/Equipe_Matra_Sports" title="Equipe Matra Sports">Matra</a>. The win, Piquet's third of the season, allowed him to move to within eight points of Drivers' Championship leader, Argentine <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Reutemann" title="Carlos Reutemann">Carlos Reutemann</a>, who retired with an engine failure.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1981_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1981_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The two <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a> turbo cars were on the front row with <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> almost half a second quicker than teammate <a href="/wiki/Ren%C3%A9_Arnoux" title="René Arnoux">René Arnoux</a>. It was the first pole position of Prost's F1 career. <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> locked out the second row with World Championship leader <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Reutemann" title="Carlos Reutemann">Carlos Reutemann</a> third and teammate <a href="/wiki/Alan_Jones_(racing_driver)" title="Alan Jones (racing driver)">Alan Jones</a> fourth. <a href="/wiki/Didier_Pironi" title="Didier Pironi">Didier Pironi</a> was fifth in his <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a> was sixth in his <a href="/wiki/Brabham" title="Brabham">Brabham</a>. The top 10 was completed by <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Laffite" title="Jacques Laffite">Jacques Laffite</a> (<a href="/wiki/Equipe_Ligier" title="Equipe Ligier">Ligier</a>), <a href="/wiki/Gilles_Villeneuve" title="Gilles Villeneuve">Gilles Villeneuve</a> (<a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>) and the two <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLarens</a> of <a href="/wiki/John_Watson_(racing_driver)" title="John Watson (racing driver)">John Watson</a> and <a href="/wiki/Andrea_de_Cesaris" title="Andrea de Cesaris">Andrea de Cesaris</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1981_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The two Williams on the second row enjoyed a better start than the Renaults of Prost and Arnoux. Arnoux was overtaken both by Reutemann and Pironi before the first corner, and by Piquet tried a move in the Ostkurve but the two cars collided, deflating Arnoux's right rear tire and causing him to pit at the end of the lap. Piquet lost a place to Jones in the process, whilst Arnoux dropped through the field. On the second lap, Pironi's engine failed, making him retire from fourth. Consequently, Prost led from Reutemann, Jones, Piquet and Laffite, and the top 5 pulled away from the rest of the pack, led by Villeneuve in sixth. Reutemann was quickly overtaken by both Jones and Piquet, beginning a 3-way fight from the lead until Piquet's tires fell off the pace and he dropped to fourth behind Reutemann.
</p><p>On Lap 21, the lead changed when Prost was overtaken by Jones as the duo were lapping Arnoux. 7 laps later, Reutemann's engine failed on Lap 28, dropping out of the race. After around 30 laps, rain started to fall on the circuit and the Renaults suffered from poor handling and third place Piquet quickly overtook Prost for second. Jones' lead was diminished when his engine began to misfire, eventually dropping behind Piquet and Prost before heading to the pits, and he eventually finished a lap behind.
</p><p>Piquet held on to his lead and finished first, in front of Prost, Laffite and <a href="/wiki/H%C3%A9ctor_Rebaque" title="Héctor Rebaque">Héctor Rebaque</a>. <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Cheever" title="Eddie Cheever">Eddie Cheever</a> finished fifth to round up the top 5, in front of <a href="/wiki/John_Watson_(racing_driver)" title="John Watson (racing driver)">John Watson</a> in sixth.
</p>
