<p>The <b>1994 Australian Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>LIX Australian Grand Prix</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motorsport" title="Motorsport">motor race</a> held on 13 November 1994 at the <a href="/wiki/Adelaide_Street_Circuit" title="Adelaide Street Circuit">Adelaide Street Circuit</a>. It was the sixteenth and final race of the <a href="/wiki/1994_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1994 Formula One World Championship">1994 Formula One World Championship</a>. The 81-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> driving for the <a href="/wiki/Williams_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams F1">Williams</a> team after starting from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>. <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> finished second in a <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> car with <a href="/wiki/Martin_Brundle" title="Martin Brundle">Martin Brundle</a> third for the <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> team.
</p><p>The race is remembered for an incident involving the two title contenders <a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a> and <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> which forced both to retire and resulted in Schumacher winning the World Drivers' Championship. Also notable was the last appearance in a Formula One Grand Prix of the first incarnation of <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Team Lotus</a>, previously seven-time Constructors' Champions. It was also the 31st and last Grand Prix victory of Nigel Mansell's Formula One career. As of 2020<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=1994_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup>, this was the last Formula One race where the number of entrants exceeded the number of places on the starting grid. This would also prove to be the last Grand Prix for <a href="/wiki/Christian_Fittipaldi" title="Christian Fittipaldi">Christian Fittipaldi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Franck_Lagorce" title="Franck Lagorce">Franck Lagorce</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michele_Alboreto" title="Michele Alboreto">Michele Alboreto</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hideki_Noda" title="Hideki Noda">Hideki Noda</a>, <a href="/wiki/David_Brabham" title="David Brabham">David Brabham</a>, <a href="/wiki/JJ_Lehto" title="JJ Lehto">JJ Lehto</a> and <a href="/wiki/Paul_Belmondo" title="Paul Belmondo">Paul Belmondo</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1994_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1994_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Heading into the final race of the season, <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a> driver Michael Schumacher was leading the Drivers' Championship with 92 points; Williams driver Damon Hill was second on 91 points, one point behind Schumacher. Williams led the Constructors' Championship with 108 points, while Benetton were 5 points behind with 103. Thus, both titles were still at stake and they would be determined in the final round.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1994_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Schumacher took the lead at the start, with Hill second behind him. The order between the two remained the same until lap 36. Hill was catching Schumacher when the Benetton driver went off the track at the <i><a href="/wiki/East_Terrace,_Adelaide" title="East Terrace, Adelaide">East Terrace</a></i> corner, hitting a wall with his right side wheels before pulling back onto the track.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportcrashes_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportcrashes-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Hill had rounded the fifth corner of the track when Schumacher pulled across the track ahead of him to the left. At the next corner Hill attempted to pass Schumacher; the two collided when Schumacher turned in. Schumacher was eliminated on the spot. Hill attempted to continue the race and pitted immediately, but retired from the race with irreparable damage to the car's front left <a href="/wiki/Double_wishbone_suspension" title="Double wishbone suspension">suspension wishbone</a>. As neither driver scored, Schumacher took the title.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> After Schumacher and Hill retired <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Mansell</a> took the lead of the race. Mansell stayed in the lead until he made his second pit stop. After Mansell pitted Berger took the lead and stayed in the lead after his second pit stop. On lap 64 Mansell overtook Berger to retake the lead of the race. <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> was running 5th in the closing stages behind teammate Brundle and Barrichello until his brakes failed on lap 77 sending him off into the wall backwards into retirement but was classified 12th. Mansell stayed in the lead for the rest of the race to win, with Berger finishing second and Brundle finishing third ahead of Barrichello, Panis and Jean Alesi in the other Ferrari completing the top 6.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1994_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Schumacher was blamed for the incident by many Formula One insiders.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> After investigation the race stewards judged it as a racing incident and took no action against Schumacher. Schumacher, at age 25 was Germany's first Formula 1 World Drivers' Champion, but under highly controversial circumstances.
</p><p>Schumacher has always maintained that the collision was a racing incident, a view that met with a large degree of media cynicism, particularly in the UK.
</p><p>Although Hill deliberately avoided becoming involved in the outcry at the time, in later years he explicitly accused Schumacher of deliberately driving into him.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Formula One commentator <a href="/wiki/Murray_Walker" title="Murray Walker">Murray Walker</a> maintained that Schumacher did not cause the crash intentionally while his co-commentators for the race, former F1 driver <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Palmer" title="Jonathan Palmer">Jonathan Palmer</a> and pit reporter <a href="/wiki/Barry_Sheene" title="Barry Sheene">Barry Sheene</a> both claimed that the crash was entirely Schumacher's fault. <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Head" title="Patrick Head">Patrick Head</a> of the Williams team  stated to <i><a href="/wiki/F1_Racing" class="mw-redirect" title="F1 Racing">F1 Racing</a></i> magazine that in 1994 "Williams were already 100% certain that Michael was guilty of foul play", but did not protest Schumacher's title because the team was still dealing with the death of <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a>; this was after Schumacher's punishment for blocking the circuit during qualifying for the <a href="/wiki/2006_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="2006 Monaco Grand Prix">2006 Monaco Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher has been blamed by the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">UK</a> public for the incident – in 2003, the BBC conducted a search for "<i>The Most Unsporting Moment</i>" in which the Adelaide incident was nominated.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Hill's 1994 season earned him the 1994 <a href="/wiki/BBC_Sports_Personality_of_the_Year" title="BBC Sports Personality of the Year">BBC Sports Personality of the Year</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
