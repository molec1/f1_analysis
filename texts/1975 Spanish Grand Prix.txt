<p>The <b>1975 Spanish Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at <a href="/wiki/Montju%C3%AFc_circuit" title="Montjuïc circuit">Montjuïc circuit</a> on 27 April 1975. It was race 4 of 14 in both the <a href="/wiki/1975_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1975 World Championship of Drivers">1975 World Championship of Drivers</a> and the <a href="/wiki/1975_International_Cup_for_Formula_One_Manufacturers" class="mw-redirect" title="1975 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers">1975 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers</a>. It is one of the most controversial and tragic race weekends in the sport's history after the death of four spectators who were hit by the crashing <a href="/wiki/Hill_GH1" title="Hill GH1">Hill GH1</a> of <a href="/wiki/Rolf_Stommelen" title="Rolf Stommelen">Rolf Stommelen</a>. It was also the race in which <a href="/wiki/Lella_Lombardi" title="Lella Lombardi">Lella Lombardi</a> became the first and so far only woman to score points towards the World Championship and future world champion <a href="/wiki/Alan_Jones_(racing_driver)" title="Alan Jones (racing driver)">Alan Jones</a> made his first start.
</p><p>It was the 21st <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Grand_Prix" title="Spanish Grand Prix">Spanish Grand Prix</a> since the race was first held in 1913. It was the fourth, and last, Grand Prix to be held on the Montjuïc street circuit. The race was shortened to 29 of its scheduled 75 laps, a race distance of 109 kilometres. The race was won by German driver <a href="/wiki/Jochen_Mass" title="Jochen Mass">Jochen Mass</a> driving a <a href="/wiki/McLaren_M23" title="McLaren M23">McLaren M23</a>. It would be the only Formula One win of his career. Mass had just a second lead over the <a href="/wiki/Lotus_72E" class="mw-redirect" title="Lotus 72E">Lotus 72E</a> of Belgian driver <a href="/wiki/Jacky_Ickx" title="Jacky Ickx">Jacky Ickx</a> when the race was declared. Argentine racer <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Reutemann" title="Carlos Reutemann">Carlos Reutemann</a> was declared third in his <a href="/wiki/Brabham_BT44B" class="mw-redirect" title="Brabham BT44B">Brabham BT44B</a>, a lap behind the race leaders after a penalty was given to <a href="/wiki/Jean-Pierre_Jarier" title="Jean-Pierre Jarier">Jean-Pierre Jarier</a>. This was the final win by a <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">German</a> driver until <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> won the <a href="/wiki/1992_Belgian_Grand_Prix" title="1992 Belgian Grand Prix">1992 Belgian Grand Prix</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_summary">Race summary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1975_Spanish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Race summary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Right from the start, the drivers who were members of the <a href="/wiki/Grand_Prix_Drivers_Association" class="mw-redirect" title="Grand Prix Drivers Association">Grand Prix Drivers Association</a> were furious that the barriers were not bolted together properly. Thus, they went on strike. Most of the sport's major players refused to take part in practice. <a href="/wiki/Jacky_Ickx" title="Jacky Ickx">Jacky Ickx</a> was not a member of the GPDA, and one of the few marquee drivers who did practice.
</p><p>Track staff worked overnight to fix the barriers, and to make sure everything would be fixed in time for qualifying on Saturday, some of the teams sent out mechanics to help. The drivers, though, still were not convinced, but the race organizers threatened legal action if no race was run. This, and rumors that the <a href="/wiki/Guardia_Civil" class="mw-redirect" title="Guardia Civil">Guardia Civil</a> would seize the cars which were in the paddock, which was at <a href="/wiki/Estadi_Ol%C3%ADmpic_Llu%C3%ADs_Companys" title="Estadi Olímpic Lluís Companys">Montjuïc Stadium</a>, forced the drivers to call off the strike.
</p><p>The defending World Champion <a href="/wiki/Emerson_Fittipaldi" title="Emerson Fittipaldi">Emerson Fittipaldi</a>, however, was still furious. He did the minimum three laps, but at a very slow pace, then pulled into the pits. The next morning, Fittipaldi announced he would not race, and went back home. Also during race day morning, <a href="/wiki/Ken_Tyrrell" title="Ken Tyrrell">Ken Tyrrell</a> went out onto the circuit with his <a href="/wiki/Wrench" title="Wrench">spanner</a> to make sure the barriers were how they should be.
</p><p>The two <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferraris</a> of <a href="/wiki/Niki_Lauda" title="Niki Lauda">Niki Lauda</a> (on pole) and <a href="/wiki/Clay_Regazzoni" title="Clay Regazzoni">Clay Regazzoni</a> qualified on the front row, but their glory would not last long. At the start, <a href="/wiki/Vittorio_Brambilla" title="Vittorio Brambilla">Vittorio Brambilla</a>'s <a href="/wiki/March_Engineering" title="March Engineering">March</a> tangled with <a href="/wiki/Mario_Andretti" title="Mario Andretti">Mario Andretti</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Parnelli" class="mw-redirect" title="Parnelli">Parnelli</a>. Andretti's car hit the back of Lauda's, sending him into Regazzoni. Lauda was out immediately, while Regazzoni took his car to the garage, where repairs were made, and Regazzoni was sent back out. <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Depailler" title="Patrick Depailler">Patrick Depailler</a> also retired on the first lap because of suspension damage, and <a href="/wiki/Wilson_Fittipaldi" class="mw-redirect" title="Wilson Fittipaldi">Wilson Fittipaldi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Arturo_Merzario" title="Arturo Merzario">Arturo Merzario</a> withdrew in protest.
</p><p>After the first-corner madness ceased, <a href="/wiki/James_Hunt" title="James Hunt">James Hunt</a> was shown as the leader. Shockingly, Andretti had managed to keep going, and was running in second. <a href="/wiki/John_Watson_(racing_driver)" title="John Watson (racing driver)">John Watson</a> was in third, <a href="/wiki/Rolf_Stommelen" title="Rolf Stommelen">Rolf Stommelen</a> was fourth, Brambilla fifth, and <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Pace" title="Carlos Pace">Carlos Pace</a> sixth.
</p><p>On lap four, the engine in <a href="/wiki/Jody_Scheckter" title="Jody Scheckter">Jody Scheckter</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_Racing" title="Tyrrell Racing">Tyrrell</a> blew, and the oil dumping onto the circuit caused <a href="/wiki/Alan_Jones_(racing_driver)" title="Alan Jones (racing driver)">Alan Jones</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mark_Donohue" title="Mark Donohue">Mark Donohue</a> to crash. Three laps later, Hunt also slipped in the oil and crashed. The top three had become Andretti, Watson, and Stommelen. Watson's car suffered from vibrations and dropped out. Andretti's rear suspension lasted only seven more laps before it failed, causing him to crash out of the lead. <a href="/wiki/Jean-Pierre_Jarier" title="Jean-Pierre Jarier">Jean-Pierre Jarier</a> and Brambilla stopped to change tyres, whilst <a href="/wiki/Tom_Pryce" title="Tom Pryce">Tom Pryce</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tony_Brise" title="Tony Brise">Tony Brise</a> tangled. Stommelen was now in the top spot, followed by Pace, <a href="/wiki/Ronnie_Peterson" title="Ronnie Peterson">Ronnie Peterson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jochen_Mass" title="Jochen Mass">Jochen Mass</a>, and Ickx. On lap 24, Peterson was out after he tangled with <a href="/wiki/Fran%C3%A7ois_Migault" title="François Migault">François Migault</a> while trying to lap the Frenchman.
</p><p>Two laps later, tragedy struck. The rear wing on Stommelen's <a href="/wiki/Embassy_Hill" title="Embassy Hill">Embassy Hill</a> broke, sending him into the barrier, ironically at the point that his own mechanics had worked on. He bounced off it and back into the road, hitting the barrier across the way, and flying over it. While trying to avoid Stommelen as he crossed the track, Pace crashed. Four people were killed by Stommelen's flying car: fireman Joaquín Benaches Morera, spectator Andrés Ruiz Villanova, and two photo-journalists, Mario de Roia and Antonio Font Bayarri.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Stommelen himself suffered a broken leg, a broken wrist and two cracked ribs.
</p><p>The race continued for another four laps, during which Mass passed Ickx for the lead. On lap 29, the race was halted with Mass the winner, Ickx second, and <a href="/wiki/Jean-Pierre_Jarier" title="Jean-Pierre Jarier">Jean-Pierre Jarier</a> crossed the line in third position. <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Reutemann" title="Carlos Reutemann">Carlos Reutemann</a> finished fourth ahead of Brambilla in fifth. <a href="/wiki/Lella_Lombardi" title="Lella Lombardi">Lella Lombardi</a> finished in sixth and became the only woman in Formula One history to score championship points. With the race being stopped before three-quarters of the scheduled race distance was reached, only half points were awarded for the first time in the history of the championship. After the race, stewards found that Jarier had overtaken in a portion of the track covered by a yellow flag caution. Jarier was given a sixty-second penalty that relegated him to fourth position.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
