<p>The <b>1999 Italian Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>70<sup>o</sup> Gran Premio Campari d'Italia</b><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 12 September 1999 at the <a href="/wiki/Autodromo_Nazionale_di_Monza" title="Autodromo Nazionale di Monza">Autodromo Nazionale di Monza</a> near <a href="/wiki/Monza" title="Monza">Monza</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>. It was the thirteenth race of the <a href="/wiki/1999_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1999 Formula One World Championship">1999 Formula One World Championship</a>, and the last held on this layout of Monza.
</p><p>The 53-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/Jordan_199" title="Jordan 199">Jordan</a>-<a href="/wiki/Mugen_Motorsports" title="Mugen Motorsports">Mugen-Honda</a>, after <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a>, seeking to defend his Drivers' Championship title, spun off while leading comfortably. <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> was second in a <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW21" title="Williams FW21">Williams</a>-<a href="/wiki/Supertec" title="Supertec">Supertec</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Mika_Salo" title="Mika Salo">Mika Salo</a> third in a <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_F399" title="Ferrari F399">Ferrari</a>. Häkkinen's rival <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a> finished sixth in the other Ferrari, thus moving level on points with the Finn at the top of the Drivers' Championship.
</p><p>Though the victory moved Frentzen to within ten points of Häkkinen and Irvine, it would turn out to be his third and last victory in Formula One, as well as the last for engine suppliers Mugen. It was also the Jordan team's only victory in dry conditions, their others coming in the rain.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1999_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1999_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Going into the race, <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> led the Drivers' Championship by a single point from <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a> and Häkkinen's team-mate <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> also still in contention. Häkkinen was expected to do well on the fast Monza circuit, and duly claimed pole position by half a second from Frentzen. Coulthard was third, while <a href="/wiki/Alex_Zanardi" title="Alex Zanardi">Alessandro Zanardi</a>, who had been having a poor year with <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a>, was fourth, just ahead of team-mate <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a>. On Ferrari's home soil, Irvine had a poor qualifying session and could only manage eighth, behind team-mate <a href="/wiki/Mika_Salo" title="Mika Salo">Mika Salo</a> in sixth and <a href="/wiki/Stewart_Grand_Prix" title="Stewart Grand Prix">Stewart</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> – who had just signed with Ferrari to replace Irvine in <a href="/wiki/2000_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2000 Formula One World Championship">2000</a> – in seventh. Completing the top ten were <a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a> in the second Jordan and <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Panis" title="Olivier Panis">Olivier Panis</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Prost_Grand_Prix" title="Prost Grand Prix">Prost</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1999_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the start, Häkkinen led away while Zanardi shot past Coulthard and Frentzen into second. Frentzen quickly re-passed Zanardi, but Coulthard fell further back, behind Schumacher and Salo. Meanwhile, at the back of the field, <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Marc_Gen%C3%A9" title="Marc Gené">Marc Gené</a> tangled with <a href="/wiki/Arrows_Grand_Prix_International" title="Arrows Grand Prix International">Arrows</a>' <a href="/wiki/Pedro_de_la_Rosa" title="Pedro de la Rosa">Pedro de la Rosa</a> at the Roggia chicane and became the first retirement, while on the second lap <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Diniz" title="Pedro Diniz">Pedro Diniz</a> both spun off at the Rettifilo chicane.
</p><p>On lap 3, Zanardi ran over a kerb and damaged the underside of his car. He managed to hold on to third place for another 15 laps, while Häkkinen and Frentzen pulled away. Barrichello passed Coulthard on lap 11 and then Salo on lap 19, while Zanardi waved Schumacher past on lap 18. On lap 24, there was more drama at the back as <a href="/wiki/Toranosuke_Takagi" title="Toranosuke Takagi">Toranosuke Takagi</a> in the second Arrows tried to overtake <a href="/wiki/Luca_Badoer" title="Luca Badoer">Luca Badoer</a> in the second Minardi at the Rettifilo, only to run into the back of Badoer and end his race.
</p><p>Barrichello overtook Zanardi on lap 26; Salo did the same at the start of lap 28. At this point, Häkkinen led Frentzen by eight seconds, with Schumacher a further two-and-a-half seconds back. But on lap 30, going into the Rettifilo, Häkkinen made a mistake changing gear – selecting first instead of second<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> – and spun off, in a virtual repeat of his unforced error at <a href="/wiki/1999_San_Marino_Grand_Prix" title="1999 San Marino Grand Prix">San Marino</a> earlier in the year. In a rare show of emotion, the Finn burst into tears at the side of the track.
</p><p>Frentzen thus inherited the lead as the front-runners began to make their pit stops. When these had been completed, Salo had moved back ahead of Barrichello and into third, while Coulthard and Irvine had both leapfrogged Zanardi and were now fifth and sixth.
</p><p>Over the closing laps, Frentzen retained a comfortable lead over Schumacher – despite the Williams driver setting the fastest lap of the race on lap 48 – while Coulthard tried unsuccessfully to find a way past Barrichello, allowing Salo to pull away from both of them. Frentzen's eventual margin of victory was 3.2 seconds, with a further eight seconds back to Salo and another six back to Barrichello. Coulthard finished half a second behind the Stewart driver, but nine ahead of Irvine, who himself held off Zanardi for the final point.
</p><p>This point moved Irvine level with Häkkinen in the Drivers' Championship on 60 points apiece, while the win put Frentzen just ten points behind on 50, with Coulthard on 48. In the Constructors' Championship, Ferrari cut McLaren's lead to six points, 108 to 102.
</p>
