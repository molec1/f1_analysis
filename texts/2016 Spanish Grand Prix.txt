<p>The <b>2016 Spanish Grand Prix</b> (formally known as the <b>Formula 1 Gran Premio de España Pirelli 2016</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Auto_racing" title="Auto racing">motor race</a> held on 15 May 2016 at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Barcelona-Catalunya" title="Circuit de Barcelona-Catalunya">Circuit de Barcelona-Catalunya</a> in <a href="/wiki/Montmel%C3%B3" title="Montmeló">Montmeló</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>. The race was the fifth round of the <a href="/wiki/2016_FIA_Formula_One_World_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="2016 FIA Formula One World Championship">2016 FIA Formula One World Championship</a>, and marked the forty-sixth running of the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Grand_Prix" title="Spanish Grand Prix">Spanish Grand Prix</a> as a round of the Formula One World Championship. It was the twenty-sixth time that the race has been held at the circuit.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> was the defending race winner and entered the round with a forty-three-point lead over teammate <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> in the Drivers' Championship. Their team, <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a>, held an eighty-one point lead over <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> in the Constructors' Championship. Hamilton took <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> during qualifying, ahead of teammate Rosberg and <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a> won the race upon his début for his new team Red Bull, having swapped his <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso</a> seat with <a href="/wiki/Daniil_Kvyat" title="Daniil Kvyat">Daniil Kvyat</a> ahead of the event. At the age of 18 years and 228 days, Verstappen became the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_driver_records#Youngest_winners" title="List of Formula One driver records">youngest ever winner</a>, the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_driver_records#Youngest_drivers_to_score_a_podium_finish" title="List of Formula One driver records">youngest driver to score a podium finish</a> and the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_driver_records#For_at_least_one_lap,_youngest_leaders" title="List of Formula One driver records">youngest ever to lead a lap</a> of a Formula One race, breaking the previous records held by <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a>. In the process he also became the first Dutchman to win a Grand Prix and the first Grand Prix winner born in the 1990s. Both Mercedes drivers retired from the race following a collision with each other on the first lap, thus marking the first Mercedes double retirement since the <a href="/wiki/2011_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="2011 Australian Grand Prix">2011 Australian Grand Prix</a> and the first time the team had not scored a point since the <a href="/wiki/2012_United_States_Grand_Prix" title="2012 United States Grand Prix">2012 United States Grand Prix</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2016_Spanish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2016_Spanish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Kvyat_Spain_2016.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0c/Kvyat_Spain_2016.jpg/220px-Kvyat_Spain_2016.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0c/Kvyat_Spain_2016.jpg/330px-Kvyat_Spain_2016.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0c/Kvyat_Spain_2016.jpg/440px-Kvyat_Spain_2016.jpg 2x" data-file-width="6000" data-file-height="4000" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Kvyat_Spain_2016.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The driver swap between <a href="/wiki/Daniil_Kvyat" title="Daniil Kvyat">Daniil Kvyat</a> <i>(pictured)</i> and <a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a> was the major talking point ahead of the race.</div></div></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Daniil_Kvyat" title="Daniil Kvyat">Daniil Kvyat</a> and <a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a> traded places ahead of the race. Verstappen joined <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a> while Kvyat returned to <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Scuderia Toro Rosso</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Red Bull's team principal <a href="/wiki/Christian_Horner" title="Christian Horner">Christian Horner</a> explained the driver swap with Verstappen's talent, saying that the 18-year-old Dutchman and <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> had the potential to become Formula One's best driver pairing.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Furthermore, he stressed that Verstappen's move to Red Bull would tie him to the team for the foreseeable future.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Daniil Kvyat meanwhile expressed surprise at the decision, saying during Thursday's press conference that he was "shocked" by his demotion, stressing that Red Bull did not give him a "real explanation".<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Following problems with his power unit in both the <a href="/wiki/2016_Chinese_Grand_Prix" title="2016 Chinese Grand Prix">Chinese</a> and <a href="/wiki/2016_Russian_Grand_Prix" title="2016 Russian Grand Prix">Russian Grand Prix</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> received a redesigned MGU-H unit, in a bid to solve the troubles which hampered him earlier.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> However, <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> were unable to guarantee that the problem would not appear again.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>With Spain being the first race in Western Europe, and a mid-season test coming up after the race, many teams introduced their first major car updates for the Grand Prix. Mercedes ran a new front wing with a "more pointy" nose during first free practice. Other changes were made to the engine cover and the rear wing. <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> made more significant changes to their <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4-31" title="McLaren MP4-31">MP4-31</a>, most notably on the front wing, which <a href="/wiki/Sky_Sports" title="Sky Sports">Sky Sports</a> commentator <a href="/wiki/Ted_Kravitz" title="Ted Kravitz">Ted Kravitz</a> called the "most intricate front wing I've ever seen".<sup id="cite_ref-development_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-development-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a> updated their <a href="/wiki/Force_India_VJM09" title="Force India VJM09">VJM09</a> to improve airflow. According to the team, they were looking for improved driveability rather than faster lap times. <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a> introduced a new chassis for <a href="/wiki/Jolyon_Palmer" title="Jolyon Palmer">Jolyon Palmer</a>. More significantly, the French manufacturer debuted a new version of their power unit for both their works team and Red Bull at the two-day test following the race. All other teams except for <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a> also made changes to their cars, with the Swiss team continuing their financial struggles.<sup id="cite_ref-development_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-development-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> They also announced that they would be forced to miss the mid-season test, being unable to field a development driver, with regulations demanding that two out of four test days be driven by young driver talents.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
Both Sauber and <a href="/wiki/Haas_F1_Team" title="Haas F1 Team">Haas</a> were allowed to use the updated version of Ferrari's 2016 power unit, which the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">works team</a> had already used in Russia two weeks earlier.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Going into the weekend, <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> led the Drivers' Championship, having taken the maximum number of 100 points from the first four races. Lewis Hamilton followed in second with 57 points, while <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> was third with 43 points. Daniel Ricciardo followed in fourth, ahead of <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> In the Constructors' standings, Mercedes was first with 157 points, 81 points clear of second-placed Ferrari. Behind them, Red Bull led fourth-placed <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> by just six points.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Sole tyre supplier Pirelli brought the hard, medium and soft tyres to the event, the first time this season to feature the orange hard tyres.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> As per the regulations of the 2016 season, every driver had to set aside one set each of the two hardest compounds for the race and one set of the soft tyres for Q3 (should they advance). The drivers had freedom of what other compounds they chose for the remaining ten out of thirteen sets.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Free_practice">Free practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2016_Spanish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Free practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Per the regulations for the <a href="/wiki/2016_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2016 Formula One World Championship">2016</a> season, two 90-minute practice sessions were held on Friday and another 60-minute session was held before qualifying on Saturday.<sup id="cite_ref-pracreg_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pracreg-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> The Ferrari drivers, Sebastian Vettel and Kimi Räikkönen, set the pace in the first session, both on the soft tyre compound. The Mercedes drivers Rosberg and Hamilton followed, albeit on the medium compound, recording more timed laps than their rivals. Both Red Bull cars followed in fifth and sixth, with Daniel Ricciardo faster than new teammate Max Verstappen. <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a> was seventh for Williams, two places ahead of teammate <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a>, who at one point spun out at turn five. Upon his return to Toro Rosso, Daniil Kvyat was unable to match the pace of his teammate <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Sainz_Jr." title="Carlos Sainz Jr.">Carlos Sainz Jr.</a>, ending up half a second behind him.<sup id="cite_ref-fp1_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fp1-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Esteban_Ocon" title="Esteban Ocon">Esteban Ocon</a> made his first free practice appearance of the season for Renault, replacing Jolyon Palmer,<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> but was unable to set a timed lap.<sup id="cite_ref-fp1_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fp1-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> He suffered a tyre failure, caused by debris on the track.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Force India's <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a> managed only nine laps as he spent much of the session in the pit lane after smoke emerged from the rear of his car.<sup id="cite_ref-fp1_21-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fp1-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Mercedes topped the time sheets in the second session, now switching to the soft compound tyres as well, with Nico Rosberg two and a half-tenths of a second clear of Kimi Räikkönen, while Lewis Hamilton was third fastest, more than seven-tenths off Rosberg's time. Räikkönen's running was interrupted for a while when he was called back into the pitlane to investigate a fuel system problem, but was able to get back out. <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> was seventh fastest for McLaren, but needed a replacement of his car's plank after running wide over artificial grass surrounding the track. His teammate, <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a>, also experienced problems, having to stop at the end of the pitlane early in the session with an electrical issue. Renault's problem from the first session continued: Jolyon Palmer, back in his car in place of Ocon, suffered a tyre failure on the start-finish straight, bringing out <a href="/wiki/Racing_flags#Red_flag" title="Racing flags">red flags</a> in the process.<sup id="cite_ref-fp2_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fp2-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Renault and tyre supplier <a href="/wiki/Pirelli" title="Pirelli">Pirelli</a> were left puzzled over the failure, as in this incident, no sign of a cut from debris was found.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Nico Rosberg was again fastest in the third practice session on Saturday morning, setting a time of 1:23.078 on his first timed run, a time that stood until the end of the session. Hamilton was second, 0.126 seconds off, followed by Sebastian Vettel just two-hundredths of a second behind him. In fourth place, Max Verstappen was faster than teammate Ricciardo for the first time, who finished fifth, ahead of Räikkönen. Upon beginning his preparation laps for qualifying, Rosberg had to come back into the garage, as a sensor on his car was malfunctioning. The team was capable of repairing the damage and he went back out for the final minutes of practice. Sergio Pérez was satisfied with the eighth fastest time, saying that the car had improved "everywhere" following the updates it had received.<sup id="cite_ref-fp3_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fp3-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2016_Spanish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen_Spain_2016.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cb/R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen_Spain_2016.jpg/220px-R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen_Spain_2016.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cb/R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen_Spain_2016.jpg/330px-R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen_Spain_2016.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cb/R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen_Spain_2016.jpg/440px-R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen_Spain_2016.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1857" data-file-height="1238" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen_Spain_2016.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> struggled in qualifying, leaving them on the third row of the grid.</div></div></div>
<p>Qualifying consisted of three parts, 18, 15 and 12 minutes in length respectively, with six drivers eliminated from competing after each of the first two sessions.<sup id="cite_ref-pracreg_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pracreg-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> In the first part of qualifying (Q1), Nico Rosberg set a time of 1:23.002, the fastest of the weekend up to that point, two-tenths of a second ahead of teammate Hamilton. <a href="/wiki/Manor_Racing" title="Manor Racing">Manor</a> and Sauber occupied the back rows of the grid. Felipe Massa's first lap was seven-tenths of a second slower than the one from his teammate Bottas and as he was unable to set another timed lap, he became the surprise elimination in Q1, behind Jolyon Palmer, who was dropped into 17th place by a late improvement from Jenson Button.<sup id="cite_ref-f1fanqual_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1fanqual-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In Q2, it was Hamilton who came out on top, exactly six-tenths of a second ahead of Rosberg. Verstappen was third fastest, ahead of Ricciardo in fifth. Kimi Räikkönen had to abandon his first fast lap after running wide in turn four, but later set a time good enough for fourth. Meanwhile, Sebastian Vettel recorded just one timed lap, saving a set of tyres and was sixth. On his return to Toro Rosso, Kvyat was eliminated in 13th place, while teammate Sainz went through to Q3. Sergio Pérez advanced as well, demoting his teammate Hülkenberg into elimination in the process, who would line up eleventh on the grid. Joining him on the sidelines for Q3 were Jenson Button in twelfth, with <a href="/wiki/Romain_Grosjean" title="Romain Grosjean">Romain Grosjean</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Magnussen" title="Kevin Magnussen">Kevin Magnussen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Esteban_Guti%C3%A9rrez" title="Esteban Gutiérrez">Esteban Gutiérrez</a> lining up behind Kvyat.<sup id="cite_ref-f1fanqual_27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1fanqual-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Lewis Hamilton and Nico Rosberg were the first out in Q3. Hamilton's early sector times put him on course to a new fastest lap of the weekend, but a brake lockup at turn ten ruined his lap and tyres. Nico Rosberg made no such mistake and claimed provisional <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>. As the two Ferrari drivers struggled to find pace, Max Verstappen claimed second place for the time being, before the drivers went out for their second laps. Lewis Hamilton's second flying lap bested Rosberg's time by almost three-tenths of a second, and saw him take the fifty-second pole of his career. The two Red Bull cars of Ricciardo and Verstappen followed, with Räikkönen and Vettel for Ferrari in fifth and sixth respectively, both more than a second behind Hamilton. Valtteri Bottas was best of the rest ahead of Sainz, Pérez and Alonso.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> It was the first appearance in Q3 for McLaren since they resumed their association with <a href="/wiki/Honda_in_Formula_One" title="Honda in Formula One">Honda</a> in <a href="/wiki/2015_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2015 Formula One World Championship">2015</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2016_Spanish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Ricciardo_Verstappen_Vettel_Spain_2016.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8f/Ricciardo_Verstappen_Vettel_Spain_2016.jpg/220px-Ricciardo_Verstappen_Vettel_Spain_2016.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8f/Ricciardo_Verstappen_Vettel_Spain_2016.jpg/330px-Ricciardo_Verstappen_Vettel_Spain_2016.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8f/Ricciardo_Verstappen_Vettel_Spain_2016.jpg/440px-Ricciardo_Verstappen_Vettel_Spain_2016.jpg 2x" data-file-width="6000" data-file-height="4000" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Ricciardo_Verstappen_Vettel_Spain_2016.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Ricciardo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Verstappen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Vettel</a> (from left to right) fighting for the lead.</div></div></div>
<p>At the start, Nico Rosberg got around the outside of Hamilton in turn one, while Räikkönen lost positions due to a slow getaway. Sebastian Vettel got past Verstappen, but was immediately repassed on the approach to turn four. At the front of the race, coming out of turn three, Hamilton tried to retake the position from Rosberg. As the latter defended his position, Hamilton went off the track and spun and they collided at turn 4, ending the race of both Mercedes drivers.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> The collision resulted in a <a href="/wiki/Safety_car" title="Safety car">safety car</a> period, with the order standing: Ricciardo, Verstappen, Sainz, Vettel and Räikkönen. The safety car came in at the end of lap four. Vettel got past Sainz on lap eight and Räikkönen tried to follow suit two laps later, but was forced off track into turn two. He completed the move one lap later at the same spot. Sainz and Button were the first to come into the pit lane for new tyres on lap 12, followed by race leader Ricciardo one lap later, making Verstappen the first Dutchman to lead a Grand Prix, before he came in another lap later. When Vettel made a pit stop on lap 16, he emerged third behind the two Red Bull drivers, all on the medium compound tyre.<sup id="cite_ref-tick_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tick-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Verstappen_Spain_2016.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2d/Verstappen_Spain_2016.jpg/220px-Verstappen_Spain_2016.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2d/Verstappen_Spain_2016.jpg/330px-Verstappen_Spain_2016.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2d/Verstappen_Spain_2016.jpg/440px-Verstappen_Spain_2016.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1372" data-file-height="915" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Verstappen_Spain_2016.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a> celebrating victory</div></div></div>
<p>By lap 20, Sebastian Vettel was closing the gap to the two Red Bulls ahead of him, coming within three seconds of Verstappen. On lap 22, <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a> retired when his power unit caught fire and he had to stop on the sidelines. While Massa had fought from 18th on the grid into tenth, his teammate Bottas was lying fifth by lap 25, ahead of Sainz. On lap 28, Ricciardo was again the first of the front runners to pit, taking on the soft compound. Vettel came in two laps later, making the same choice in tyres. This left Verstappen to lead Kimi Räikkönen, as it emerged that both would run on a different strategy, pitting only twice in contrast to the three stopping Vettel and Ricciardo. On lap 34, the gap between the two leaders was 2.1 seconds, before Verstappen came in on the following lap. Räikkönen responded one lap later, emerging behind Verstappen. On lap 39, Vettel came in for his third and final stop, while Ricciardo stayed out for another four laps. When he returned to the track, he was behind Vettel, but on fresher tyres.<sup id="cite_ref-tick_32-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tick-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>While Räikkönen closed on Verstappen at the front, Fernando Alonso's home race ended on lap 47, pulling over to the side of the track at turn three. Räikkönen managed to get into the one-second window to activate the <a href="/wiki/Drag_reduction_system" title="Drag reduction system">drag reduction system</a> (DRS), as Vettel was eight seconds behind him with Ricciardo closing on him in fourth place. By lap 57, Ricciardo was close enough to use DRS as well, but unable to pass. Three laps later, Ricciardo made an attempt at overtaking into turn one, but braking too late left him running wide, allowing Vettel back through. As the front runners lapped backmarkers, Kvyat, after scoring the first fastest lap for himself and Toro Rosso, overtook Gutiérrez for tenth place. Two laps from finish, Ricciardo suffered a puncture and was forced to pit, but retained fourth place ahead of Bottas.<sup id="cite_ref-tick_32-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tick-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> On the last lap, Renault drivers Kevin Magnussen and Jolyon Palmer collided, but both made it to the finish.<sup id="cite_ref-magnussen_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-magnussen-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> As Verstappen crossed the finish line, he became the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_driver_records#Youngest_winners" title="List of Formula One driver records">youngest ever</a> and first Dutch race winner in Formula One.<sup id="cite_ref-tick_32-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tick-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2016_Spanish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Max_Verstappen_2016_Malaysia_2.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/11/Max_Verstappen_2016_Malaysia_2.jpg/220px-Max_Verstappen_2016_Malaysia_2.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="330" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/11/Max_Verstappen_2016_Malaysia_2.jpg/330px-Max_Verstappen_2016_Malaysia_2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/11/Max_Verstappen_2016_Malaysia_2.jpg/440px-Max_Verstappen_2016_Malaysia_2.jpg 2x" data-file-width="800" data-file-height="1200" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Max_Verstappen_2016_Malaysia_2.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a> became the youngest driver to win a Formula One race, aged 18 years and 228 days.</div></div></div>
<p>At the podium interviews, conducted by singer <a href="/wiki/Pl%C3%A1cido_Domingo" title="Plácido Domingo">Plácido Domingo</a>, Verstappen expressed delight about a "great race", crediting his team with giving him a good car. Kimi Räikkönen said that while he was happy for Verstappen, he was disappointed not to have won. He blamed the lack of downforce while following as the reason why he had been unable to pass Verstappen. Vettel congratulated the winner as well, but lamented that his different strategy had not worked out in his favour. During the post-race press conference, Räikkönen stressed that had anyone offered the team two podium positions after their poor performance in qualifying, they would have taken it "happily".<sup id="cite_ref-pressconf_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconf-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> Fourth placed Ricciardo expressed bitterness over Red Bull's decision to switch him to a three-stop strategy, which eventually lost him a podium position, saying: "I'm a bit devastated. A big part of me is happy the team are on winning form but it's hard to celebrate".<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> His team later explained their decision, declaring that they had focused their strategy on Vettel, who they had considered the biggest threat.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the Grand Prix, Max Verstappen broke several records: apart from being the youngest ever driver to win a race at 18 years and 228 days, he also was the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_driver_records#Youngest_driver_to_score_a_podium_finish" title="List of Formula One driver records">youngest to stand on a Formula One podium</a>, in both instances breaking Sebastian Vettel's record set at the <a href="/wiki/2008_Italian_Grand_Prix" title="2008 Italian Grand Prix">2008 Italian Grand Prix</a> by more than two years. Vettel had been 21 years and 2 months at the time. Verstappen also became the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_driver_records#For_at_least_one_lap,_youngest_leaders" title="List of Formula One driver records">youngest driver to lead at least one lap of a Grand Prix</a>, again overcoming a record set by Vettel, at the <a href="/wiki/2007_Japanese_Grand_Prix" title="2007 Japanese Grand Prix">2007 Japanese Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-records_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-records-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> Verstappen also was the first driver born in the 1990s to win a Grand Prix.<sup id="cite_ref-stats_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-stats-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> He received considerable praise after the race, with <a href="/wiki/Sky_Sports_F1" title="Sky Sports F1">Sky Sports F1</a>'s <a href="/wiki/David_Croft_(broadcaster)" title="David Croft (broadcaster)">David Croft</a> saying that he "has shown he has everything to be a world champion".<sup id="cite_ref-records_38-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-records-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> His father <a href="/wiki/Jos_Verstappen" title="Jos Verstappen">Jos Verstappen</a>, himself a former Grand Prix driver, declared that he considered his son a better driver than himself, as race wins had eluded him during his own career in the sport.<sup id="cite_ref-records_38-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-records-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> French sport newspaper <i><a href="/wiki/L%27%C3%89quipe" title="L&#39;Équipe">L'Équipe</a></i> declared Verstappen to be "already a great" and "in the big leagues".<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> Verstappen was also the first Dutchman to win a race, with the Netherlands becoming the 22nd country to produce a race winner. He was the tenth different winner of the Spanish Grand Prix in as many past editions.<sup id="cite_ref-stats_39-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-stats-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Hamilton_Spain_2016.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4c/Hamilton_Spain_2016.jpg/220px-Hamilton_Spain_2016.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4c/Hamilton_Spain_2016.jpg/330px-Hamilton_Spain_2016.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4c/Hamilton_Spain_2016.jpg/440px-Hamilton_Spain_2016.jpg 2x" data-file-width="6000" data-file-height="4000" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Hamilton_Spain_2016.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> came under criticism for his collision with teammate <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>Following their collision, Hamilton and Rosberg were summoned to the stewards after the race but neither received a penalty, as the stewards rated their crash as a racing incident, with no driver in particular to blame. It emerged that Rosberg had chosen the wrong engine mode for the start, being down on power which led to Hamilton going for a passing manoeuvre. However, Rosberg remained convinced that he had done nothing wrong, saying: "I made it very clear I wasn't going to leave any space on the inside and I was very surprised he went for the gap".<sup id="cite_ref-merccrash1_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-merccrash1-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> While Hamilton apologised to the team after the incident, he refused to accept blame for it.<sup id="cite_ref-merccrash2_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-merccrash2-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> Opinions about the incident varied: While Mercedes's executive chairman <a href="/wiki/Niki_Lauda" title="Niki Lauda">Niki Lauda</a> blamed Hamilton for the crash,<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> former F1 driver <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Davidson" title="Anthony Davidson">Anthony Davidson</a> said on Sky Sports F1 that Rosberg's move was "very aggressive".<sup id="cite_ref-merccrash1_41-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-merccrash1-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> Three-time world champion <a href="/wiki/Jackie_Stewart" title="Jackie Stewart">Jackie Stewart</a> said after the race that Mercedes should fine Hamilton for the crash: "Hamilton is to blame. Rosberg was allowed to protect himself. You don't go for it on the first lap."<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> Mercedes's head of motorsport <a href="/wiki/Toto_Wolff" title="Toto Wolff">Toto Wolff</a> stressed after the race that the team would continue to let their drivers race against each other freely, a decision praised by former world champion <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-merccrash2_42-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-merccrash2-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> While Rosberg said after the race that he contemplated having a talk with Hamilton about the situation, Mercedes later decided that they did "not need clear-the-air talks".<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> However, it later emerged that the pair did have a conversation about the incident before the next race <a href="/wiki/2016_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="2016 Monaco Grand Prix">in Monaco</a>, which, according to Hamilton, was marked by "pure respect".<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> It was Mercedes's first double retirement since the <a href="/wiki/2011_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="2011 Australian Grand Prix">2011 Australian Grand Prix</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> and the first time they failed to score a point since the <a href="/wiki/2012_United_States_Grand_Prix" title="2012 United States Grand Prix">2012 United States Grand Prix</a>, ending a 62-race long streak, the third longest in Formula One history.<sup id="cite_ref-stats_39-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-stats-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>For his last lap collision with teammate Jolyon Palmer, Kevin Magnussen received a ten-second time penalty, dropping him one place to 15th.<sup id="cite_ref-magnussen_33-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-magnussen-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> He also received two penalty points to his <a href="/wiki/FIA_Super_Licence" title="FIA Super Licence">licence</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> Carlos Sainz received a reprimand from the stewards for leaving the pit lane one minute before it was allowed. He escaped a more severe penalty because he stopped shortly afterwards, thereby not gaining an advantage.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> Upon his return to Toro Rosso, Daniil Kvyat recorded the first fastest lap of his career, and the first for the Italian team in their 190th Grand Prix.<sup id="cite_ref-stats_39-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-stats-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>With 100 points from the first four races, Nico Rosberg remained on top of the Drivers' Championship, while Hamilton's retirement dropped him behind Räikkönen, who now trailed Rosberg by 39 points, four points ahead of Hamilton. Sebastian Vettel and Daniel Ricciardo were level on points with 48, while Verstappen's victory moved him into sixth on 38.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> In the Constructors' standings, Mercedes retained their lead on 157 points, but Ferrari closed the gap to 48 points, with Red Bull in third on 94. Toro Rosso moved ahead of Haas into fifth, while Force India took seventh from McLaren.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
