<p>The <b>2011 Belgian Grand Prix</b>, formally the <b>2011 Formula 1 Shell Belgian Grand Prix</b>,<sup id="cite_ref-Spa_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Spa-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race that was held on 28 August 2011, at <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Spa-Francorchamps" title="Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps">Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps</a> in <a href="/wiki/Spa,_Belgium" title="Spa, Belgium">Spa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> It was the twelfth round of the <a href="/wiki/2011_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2011 Formula One season">2011 Formula One season</a> and the 67th Belgian Grand Prix to be held. The 44-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a>, the drivers' championship leader, after starting from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>. Vettel's teammate <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> finished in second place, and <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> completed the podium in third position for <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Race_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Race-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As a consequence of the race, Vettel extended his lead in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">World Drivers' Championship</a> to 92&#160;points over Webber. <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>, fourth in the race, moved into third place in the championship, ten&#160;points behind Webber in second, and eight ahead of Button. In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">World Constructors' Championship</a>, Red Bull extended their championship lead to 131&#160;points over McLaren, with <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> a further 64&#160;points behind in third position.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">FIA</a> banned the use of <a href="/wiki/Drag_Reduction_System" class="mw-redirect" title="Drag Reduction System">DRS</a> through the Eau Rouge corner, since drivers had raised safety concerns similar to those held at <a href="/wiki/2011_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="2011 Monaco Grand Prix">Monaco</a>. The overtaking aid was not permitted between the entrance to the La Source hairpin and the top of the hill after Eau Rouge. The DRS zone for the race was from Raidillon, along the Kemmel Straight until Les Combes.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Tyre supplier <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_tyres" title="Formula One tyres">Pirelli</a> brought its white-banded medium compound tyre as the harder "prime" tyre and the yellow-banded soft compound as the softer "option" compound, as opposed to the previous year where Bridgestone brought the hard compound as the prime.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After completing a practice session for <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a> at the <a href="/wiki/2011_Hungarian_Grand_Prix" title="2011 Hungarian Grand Prix">Hungaroring</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Senna" title="Bruno Senna">Bruno Senna</a> was promoted to a race seat for the Belgian Grand Prix, replacing <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The race weekend also marked the twentieth anniversary of <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>'s Formula One debut at the <a href="/wiki/1991_Belgian_Grand_Prix" title="1991 Belgian Grand Prix">1991 Belgian Grand Prix</a>. Schumacher wore a special gold helmet to commemorate the occasion.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Prior to the summer break, <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> had yet again extended his lead, in the previous race, in <a href="/wiki/2011_Hungarian_Grand_Prix" title="2011 Hungarian Grand Prix">Hungary</a> to 85 points over teammate <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a>, on 234. Webber on 149, was 3 points ahead of <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> and 4 ahead of <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>. <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> was ranked fifth in the standings, on 134 points, after winning the last race. As far as Constructors were concerned, <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a> held the lead with 383 points, however <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> had closed the gap, but were still 103 points behind on 280. Third placed team <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> were on 215 points, and had a big gap to the ongoing <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a> scrap for fourth place.
</p>
<div style="clear:both;"></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Free_Practice">Free Practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Free Practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The first Friday session was declared wet following a shower shortly before the pit lane opened. The <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> cars of <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> set the early pace, with lap times well under two minutes. They remained unthreatened at the top of the time sheets for the remainder of the session; <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> was third-fastest with a time some nine seconds adrift of the Mercedes cars in the wet conditions. <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Senna" title="Bruno Senna">Bruno Senna</a>'s return to Formula One ended abruptly when the Brazilian driver spun at turn 9, damaging his <a href="/wiki/Renault_R31" title="Renault R31">Renault R31</a> enough to end his session early. <a href="/wiki/Paul_di_Resta" title="Paul di Resta">Paul di Resta</a> had a similar episode at the same corner several minutes later, forcing the session to be red-flagged while his <a href="/wiki/Force_India_VJM04" title="Force India VJM04">Force India VJM04</a> was craned away.
</p><p>The second session was similarly rain-affected, with only limited running taking place before the rain returned; however, the gaps between drivers were not as extreme as those witnessed during the morning session. <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> set the fastest time of the session, four seconds faster than Schumacher's time in the morning session. <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> was second ahead of Button and <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>. The session was free of incidents, though Renault's <a href="/wiki/Vitaly_Petrov" title="Vitaly Petrov">Vitaly Petrov</a> suffered a steering problem that saw him finish the 90-minute session in 24th and last place having done no dry running in the session.
</p><p>The final session on Saturday morning was once again declared wet; so wet that most drivers did not venture out until the final few minutes of the session. Mark Webber was once again fastest, ahead of Lewis Hamilton and <a href="/wiki/Jaime_Alguersuari" title="Jaime Alguersuari">Jaime Alguersuari</a>. <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> completed very little running and finished the session 23rd and 24th, with <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> recording just seven laps and Fernando Alonso failing to set a lap time despite doing an installation lap.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The first qualifying period began badly for Michael Schumacher, who lost a wheel on the approach to Rivage and crashed out on his warm-up lap, demoting him to 24th place on the grid, his worst starting position in his career. The circuit steadily began to dry out, and at the end of the session, Mark Webber was the fastest man on the circuit. <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> edged into 17th place at the last minute and was safe from elimination at the expense of Paul di Resta. Because of the rapidly improving lap times, the <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Racing" title="Virgin Racing">Virgin</a> of local driver <a href="/wiki/J%C3%A9r%C3%B4me_d%27Ambrosio" title="Jérôme d&#39;Ambrosio">Jérôme d'Ambrosio</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Hispania_Racing" class="mw-redirect" title="Hispania Racing">Hispanias</a> of <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vitantonio_Liuzzi" title="Vitantonio Liuzzi">Vitantonio Liuzzi</a> all set times outside 107% of Webber's lap time, but all four of them were allowed to start the race.<sup id="cite_ref-Dispensation_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Dispensation-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Formel_1_Spa_2011_Unfall_Adrian_Sutil.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8e/Formel_1_Spa_2011_Unfall_Adrian_Sutil.jpg/220px-Formel_1_Spa_2011_Unfall_Adrian_Sutil.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8e/Formel_1_Spa_2011_Unfall_Adrian_Sutil.jpg/330px-Formel_1_Spa_2011_Unfall_Adrian_Sutil.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8e/Formel_1_Spa_2011_Unfall_Adrian_Sutil.jpg/440px-Formel_1_Spa_2011_Unfall_Adrian_Sutil.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="480" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Formel_1_Spa_2011_Unfall_Adrian_Sutil.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Adrian Sutil exits his car after the accident at the top of Eau Rouge.</div></div></div>
<p>The rain began falling again ahead of the second qualifying period, leading to a dramatic drop in lap times. Adrian Sutil crashed at the top of Eau Rouge, scattering pieces of debris across the circuit. Race control was forced to red-flag the session with seven minutes remaining in order to clear the circuit. Fernando Alonso went on to set the fastest lap time of the session, while Jenson Button moved over for Lewis Hamilton to allow the <a href="/wiki/2008_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2008 Formula One World Championship">2008</a> World Champion a clear lap to qualify for the third and final period. Button ultimately qualified 13th, behind Sébastien Buemi and <a href="/wiki/Kamui_Kobayashi" title="Kamui Kobayashi">Kamui Kobayashi</a>, and ahead of Rubens Barrichello, Sutil – whose time was fast enough for 15th position despite causing the session to be stopped – <a href="/wiki/Pastor_Maldonado" title="Pastor Maldonado">Pastor Maldonado</a> and Heikki Kovalainen.
</p><p>In the final few seconds of Q2, four cars attempted to clear the Bus Stop chicane, including Lewis Hamilton and Pastor Maldonado. None of the cars was able to cross the start/finish line in time to be allowed to complete another timed lap, Hamilton passed Maldonado with an aggressive move, forcing Maldonado wide on the exit of the chicane. Replays then showed Maldonado's Williams swiping across Hamilton's McLaren on the approach to Eau Rouge after the session had ended, damaging Hamilton's car enough to force McLaren to make impromptu repairs to the front wing, and the sidepod was repaired by tape in the short break before Q3. Maldonado was later penalised five grid slots because of the incident. Hamilton received a reprimand and a warning from the stewards for being partly to blame for the incident as he turned right slightly on the approach to Eau Rouge, before Maldonado moved left, striking the McLaren's front wing and sidepod.
</p><p>McLaren was able to repair Hamilton's car in time for the final qualifying period, for which a queue of cars formed in the pitlane as a dry line formed around the circuit, allowing the drivers to use the dry slick tyres for the first time during the weekend. Lap times fell rapidly during the session, with several drivers claiming provisional pole before the flag fell. Sebastian Vettel prevailed, achieving his ninth pole of the season, ahead of Lewis Hamilton and Mark Webber. Felipe Massa out-qualified Alonso in fourth, whilst <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> took fifth place ahead of Jaime Alguersuari and Bruno Senna. Fernando Alonso finished the session eighth, with <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a> in ninth and <a href="/wiki/Vitaly_Petrov" title="Vitaly Petrov">Vitaly Petrov</a> in tenth.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the build-up to the race, several teams requested that the FIA make available several sets of additional tyres after discovering problem with blistering – a result of continued racing at excessively high temperatures, causing air bubbles to form under the topmost layer of rubber – immediately after qualifying. This would force several drivers to start the race on compromised tyres.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> However, the FIA denied the request as the damage to the tyres had been done as a result of extreme camber settings in the car set-up; under the rules set forth by the sporting regulations, teams may replace tyres if they have been damaged, but only if the damage is a result of an accident. If the tyres are damaged as a result of car set-up, the teams would not be permitted to change their tyres.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The affected drivers would have the option of changing their camber settings, but this would be in breach of <a href="/wiki/Parc_ferm%C3%A9" title="Parc fermé">parc fermé</a> regulations and would force any driver who chose to do so to start from the pit lane, effectively moving them to the back of the grid.
</p><p>The start was messy, with two separate incidents in the first corner. Sebastian Vettel started strongly, but it was Nico Rosberg who claimed the early lead while behind them, Mark Webber made a very poor start, his <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_RB7" title="Red Bull RB7">Red Bull RB7</a> dropping into anti-stall. Bruno Senna braked too late at La Source and made heavy contact with Jaime Alguersuari in the first corner, pushing the Toro Rosso into the Ferrari of Fernando Alonso. Alonso escaped unscathed, but Alguersuari was forced to retire with front suspension damage. Senna had to pit for repairs, and was subsequently given a drive-through penalty for causing an avoidable accident. As the cars filtered through the first corner, <a href="/wiki/Timo_Glock" title="Timo Glock">Timo Glock</a> made contact with Paul di Resta, earning a drive-through penalty of his own, while the <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus_(2010%E2%80%9311)" title="Team Lotus (2010–11)">Lotuses</a> spun trying to avoid the Glock-di Resta incident. Jenson Button's car was damaged by debris from Senna's Lotus, forcing him to make an unscheduled stop to replace his front wing. He also lost his right mirror, but the advantage of his pit stop was that he was able to change onto the softer option tyres, having started the race on the slower primes.
</p><p>Nico Rosberg led the field though Eau Rouge for the first time, but was powerless to fend off Sebastian Vettel when the DRS zone was activated on lap three. Rosberg spent the rest of his first stint falling victim to several other drivers, slipping down the order, whilst Schumacher was doing the exact opposite, climbing to 15th on the first lap. The pre-race controversy surrounding tyre blistering came to a head on lap five, when the first round of pit stops began. Mark Webber pitted first, followed by Vettel, briefly handing the lead back to Rosberg as Alonso and Hamilton both pitted. Alonso emerged from the pits to find Webber right behind him, and the Australian and the Spaniard raced side-by-side through Eau Rouge, with Webber prevailing. Behind them, Sébastien Buemi in the second Toro Rosso was forced to retire when he was hit from behind by Sergio Pérez, destroying his rear wing.
</p><p>Throughout the first phase of the race, two drivers steadily began to make inroads on the field. Jenson Button, who had completed his first pit stop for a new front wing, and Michael Schumacher, who had started 24th and had managed to avoid the chaos at the first corner, both began to climb up through the midfield and were ideally placed when the safety car was deployed on lap 13. Hamilton, having just passed Kobayashi, moved to the left on the racing line leading into Les Combes. However, Kobayashi had not yet relinquished the position and was still on Hamilton's outside. The Sauber and the McLaren made contact, and Hamilton spun off and hit the barrier, and he retired from the race. Kobayashi was able to continue, but <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> also stopped on the circuit after experiencing a vibration in his <a href="/wiki/Hispania_F111" title="Hispania F111">Hispania F111</a>, and retired from 16th place. The safety car triggered a second round of stops among the leaders, with Alonso taking the lead from Vettel on lap 14, only to lose it when he made his own stop three laps later.
</p><p>With the wreckage from Hamilton's accident cleared, the safety car left the circuit and racing resumed. Vettel re-established his lead ahead of Alonso and Webber, with Button making his way through the field at a rate that suggested he had the potential to win the race. He inherited the lead on lap 31, but he had no answer to Vettel's out-lap and was forced to pit himself for the final time, emerging behind Alonso and Webber. Alonso was unable to defend against Webber, nor against Button several laps later. Button briefly attempted to catch Webber, but decided against it as it meant risking an extra pit stop. Further down the order, Michael Schumacher had recovered from 24th on the grid to sixth overall, before passing Nico Rosberg on the approach to Les Combes. Sergio Pérez became the fifth and final retirement of the race when his suspension failed, and he was able to limp back to the pits.
</p><p>Vettel went on to win the race, three seconds ahead of Webber in second and nine seconds ahead of Button in third. Alonso, who had struggled all race with a lack of grip, finished fourth ahead of Schumacher and Rosberg. Adrian Sutil finished seventh, while Felipe Massa was eighth; the Brazilian driver had pitted on lap 30, but picked up a puncture on his out-lap, forcing him to re-pit immediately. <a href="/wiki/Vitaly_Petrov" title="Vitaly Petrov">Vitaly Petrov</a> finished ninth after a brake failure cost him ten seconds and eighth place on the final lap. After the controversy in qualifying, Pastor Maldonado recovered from 21st on the grid to finish tenth, achieving his first World Championship point, the first for a <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">Venezuelan</a> driver since <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Cecotto" title="Johnny Cecotto">Johnny Cecotto</a> at the <a href="/wiki/1983_United_States_Grand_Prix_West" title="1983 United States Grand Prix West">1983 United States Grand Prix West</a>. His teammate, Rubens Barrichello, finished 16th behind the Lotuses of Jarno Trulli and Heikki Kovalainen after a collision with Kobayashi at the Bus Stop chicane forced him to pit for a new front wing.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-Race">Post-Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Post-Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In light of the pre-race controversy over tyre blistering, <a href="/wiki/Pirelli" title="Pirelli">Pirelli</a> accused Red Bull Racing of putting them in an "impossible" position by lobbying for extra sets of tyres to be made available after they went outside Pirelli's recommended camber settings when setting up their cars.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Paul_Hembery" title="Paul Hembery">Paul Hembery</a>, Pirelli's motorsport director, later stated that the company would be "more cautious" when recommending settings for the <a href="/wiki/2011_Italian_Grand_Prix" title="2011 Italian Grand Prix">Italian Grand Prix</a> with a view to preventing the blistering issue from arising again.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Lewis Hamilton had initially been critical of Kamui Kobayashi following their race-ending collision on lap twelve, saying that the crash had cost him the opportunity of a podium place.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Kobayashi denied any wrongdoing, saying that he had kept as far to the side of the track as possible to allow Hamilton through and that the only way to avoid a collision was to go off the circuit. After seeing replays of the incident, Hamilton agreed with the stewards' decision, adding, "I didn't give Kobayashi enough room though, I thought I was past."<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Bruno Senna also admitted that it was his error and miscalculation of the <i>La Source</i> corner that made him collide against Jaime Alguersuari.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
