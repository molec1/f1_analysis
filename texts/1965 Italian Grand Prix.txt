<p>The <b>1965 Italian Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>XXXVI Gran Premio d'Italia</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Auto_racing" title="Auto racing">motor race</a> held at <a href="/wiki/Autodromo_Nazionale_di_Monza" title="Autodromo Nazionale di Monza">Monza</a> on 12 September 1965. It was race 8 of 10 in both the <a href="/wiki/1965_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1965 World Championship of Drivers">1965 World Championship of Drivers</a> and the <a href="/wiki/1965_International_Cup_for_Formula_One_Manufacturers" class="mw-redirect" title="1965 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers">1965 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers</a>. The race was won by <a href="/wiki/Jackie_Stewart" title="Jackie Stewart">Jackie Stewart</a> who took his first Grand Prix victory, whilst driving for the <a href="/wiki/British_Racing_Motors" title="British Racing Motors">BRM</a> team. His teammate - <a href="/wiki/Graham_Hill" title="Graham Hill">Graham Hill</a> - finished second after a closely contested race between both the pair and pole-sitter and <a href="/wiki/Jim_Clark" title="Jim Clark">Jim Clark</a>, who driving for the <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Lotus</a>-<a href="/wiki/Coventry_Climax" title="Coventry Climax">Climax</a> team, had secured the 1965 Drivers' Championship at the previous race. His fuel-pump failure with a handful of laps to go, and also the preceding retirement of <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> driver <a href="/wiki/John_Surtees" title="John Surtees">John Surtees</a>, ensured that <a href="/wiki/Dan_Gurney" title="Dan Gurney">Dan Gurney</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Brabham" title="Brabham">Brabham</a>-<a href="/wiki/Coventry_Climax" title="Coventry Climax">Climax</a> team picked up the final position on the podium.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_report">Race report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1965_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Race report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Jim_Clark" title="Jim Clark">Jim Clark</a> duelled for the lead through the first two-thirds of the race with <a href="/wiki/Graham_Hill" title="Graham Hill">Graham Hill</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jackie_Stewart" title="Jackie Stewart">Jackie Stewart</a> and <a href="/wiki/John_Surtees" title="John Surtees">John Surtees</a> (who dropped out with clutch problems), until lap 64 when his fuel pump failed. Stewart took up the lead and it was expected that he would move over to give his team leader the victory.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="Need proof of such a statement (October 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> The enthusiastic crowd were waiting for the <a href="/wiki/BRM" class="mw-redirect" title="BRM">BRM</a> cars to come over the line in formation. However, on the approach to the Parabolica on the last time, Hill moved over too far and bounced across the grass, struggling to maintain grip on the mud and gravel. Stewart therefore claimed his first win in a BRM 1-2 from Hill. <a href="/wiki/Dan_Gurney" title="Dan Gurney">Dan Gurney</a> took the final podium place and <a href="/wiki/Lorenzo_Bandini" title="Lorenzo Bandini">Lorenzo Bandini</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bruce_McLaren" title="Bruce McLaren">Bruce McLaren</a> and <a href="/wiki/Richard_Attwood" title="Richard Attwood">Richard Attwood</a> completed the placings. The race featured 42 lead changes between four drivers (all British), the most ever in a Formula One motor race.
</p>
