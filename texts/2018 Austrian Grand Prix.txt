<p>The <b>2018 Austrian Grand Prix</b> (formally known as the <b>Formula 1 Eyetime Großer Preis von Österreich 2018</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Auto_racing" title="Auto racing">motor race</a> held on 1 July 2018 at the <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Ring" title="Red Bull Ring">Red Bull Ring</a> in <a href="/wiki/Spielberg,_Styria" title="Spielberg, Styria">Spielberg</a>, Austria. The race was the 9th round of the <a href="/wiki/2018_FIA_Formula_One_World_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="2018 FIA Formula One World Championship">2018 FIA Formula One World Championship</a>, and marked the 32nd running of the <a href="/wiki/Austrian_Grand_Prix" title="Austrian Grand Prix">Austrian Grand Prix</a> and the 31st time it had been held as a round of the Formula One World Championship since the series inception in <a href="/wiki/1950_Formula_One_season" title="1950 Formula One season">1950</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> entered the round with a 14-point lead over <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> in the Drivers' Championship. In the Constructors' Championship, Mercedes held a 22-point lead over <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>. 
</p><p>The race was won by <a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a> for <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a>, marking the first time that Red Bull has won at their home Grand Prix. This race also marked the first double retirement for Mercedes since the <a href="/wiki/2016_Spanish_Grand_Prix" title="2016 Spanish Grand Prix">2016 Spanish Grand Prix</a> and the first retirement for Hamilton since the <a href="/wiki/2016_Malaysian_Grand_Prix" title="2016 Malaysian Grand Prix">2016 Malaysian Grand Prix</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2018_Austrian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2018_Austrian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The circuit included three <a href="/wiki/Drag_reduction_system" title="Drag reduction system">drag reduction system</a> (DRS) zones for the first time. These included a zone along the main straight, with the second zone on the straight from turn 1 to turns 2 and 3, and a third zone extending from turn 3 to turn 4.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2018_Austrian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In Q2, <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Sainz_Jr." title="Carlos Sainz Jr.">Carlos Sainz</a> had to avoid Vettel, who was going slowly on the racing line. Vettel was later awarded a 3-place grid penalty for the incident. Mercedes locked out the front row in Q3 and <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a> took his first pole of 2018. The Ferraris locked out the second row. <a href="/wiki/Romain_Grosjean" title="Romain Grosjean">Romain Grosjean</a> outqualified <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> to take P6.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2018_Austrian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Bottas had a poor start from pole position. Initially passed by Hamilton, Räikkönen and Verstappen, he reclaimed P2 overtaking Verstappen and Räikkönen around the outside of Turn 4. Shortly after Verstappen overtook Räikkönen for P3. <a href="/wiki/Stoffel_Vandoorne" title="Stoffel Vandoorne">Stoffel Vandoorne</a> broke his front wing at Turn 3, requiring a pitstop, dropping him to the back of the field. On lap 12, <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a>'s engine failed on the start/finish straight, causing his immediate retirement from the race. On lap 14, Bottas retired with a gearbox failure which was originally caused by hydraulic problems, causing a Virtual Safety Car. During the VSC period, Ferrari and Red Bull pitted both their cars. Mercedes however, chose not to pit Hamilton, an error for which James Vowles, Mercedes' chief strategist, was heard to apologize via team radio. Hamilton pitted on lap 25, ceding the lead to Verstappen, and rejoined in P4, ahead of Vettel, who overtook him on lap 39. On lap 54 Ricciardo retired with an exhaust problem and then <a href="/wiki/Brendon_Hartley" title="Brendon Hartley">Brendon Hartley</a> retired on the next lap with a hydraulics issue. Hamilton retired on lap 64 with a fuel pressure problem, giving Mercedes only their third double retirement since returning to F1 as a constructor in 2010 (after <a href="/wiki/2011_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="2011 Australian Grand Prix">Australia 2011</a> and <a href="/wiki/2016_Spanish_Grand_Prix" title="2016 Spanish Grand Prix">Spain 2016</a>), their first double retirement due to mechanical failures, and Hamilton's first non-finish since the 2016 Malaysian Grand Prix. Verstappen won the race, giving Red Bull their first win at the Red Bull Ring, and Verstappen his first win of the season. The <a href="/wiki/Haas_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Haas F1">Haas</a> cars finished P4 and P5, giving them their best points finish ever and Grosjean his first points of the season.
</p>
