<p>The <b>2015 Brazilian Grand Prix</b> (formally known as the <b>Formula 1 Grande Prêmio Petrobras do Brasil 2015</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Auto_racing" title="Auto racing">motor race</a> held at the <a href="/wiki/Aut%C3%B3dromo_Jos%C3%A9_Carlos_Pace" title="Autódromo José Carlos Pace">Autódromo José Carlos Pace</a> in <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo" title="São Paulo">São Paulo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a> on 15 November 2015. It was the eighteenth race of the <a href="/wiki/2015_FIA_Formula_One_World_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="2015 FIA Formula One World Championship">2015 FIA Formula One World Championship</a>, and marked the 44th running of the <a href="/wiki/Brazilian_Grand_Prix" title="Brazilian Grand Prix">Brazilian Grand Prix</a> as a round of the World Championship since its inception in <a href="/wiki/1950_Formula_One_season" title="1950 Formula One season">1950</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a>, driving for <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a>, was the defending race winner. His teammate <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> entered the race as <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">World Drivers' Champion</a>, having secured the title two races earlier in the <a href="/wiki/2015_United_States_Grand_Prix" title="2015 United States Grand Prix">United States</a>. Mercedes had also already clinched the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' title</a>, having done so at the <a href="/wiki/2015_Russian_Grand_Prix" title="2015 Russian Grand Prix">2015 Russian Grand Prix</a>.
</p><p>Rosberg took pole position in Saturday's qualifying session, the 21st of his career. He would go on to win the race from his teammate, in what many commentators perceived as a "boring" race. <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> completed the podium for <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Scuderia Ferrari</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After the <a href="/wiki/2014_Brazilian_Grand_Prix" title="2014 Brazilian Grand Prix">2014 race</a> had been held at a resurfaced circuit, causing the teams to fight with the soft tyre choice by <a href="/wiki/Pirelli" title="Pirelli">Pirelli</a>, speculations arose if drivers would be able to challenge the eleven-year old track record set by <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> in <a href="/wiki/2004_Brazilian_Grand_Prix" title="2004 Brazilian Grand Prix">2004</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> However, practice showed that lap times actually slowed due to changes to the kerbs, forcing the drivers to stay away from the insides of the corners.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Pirelli" title="Pirelli">Pirelli</a> chose the soft and medium compounds for the event.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the wake of the <a href="/wiki/November_2015_Paris_attacks" title="November 2015 Paris attacks">terrorist attacks in Paris</a> on the Friday evening of the race weekend, Formula One decided to honour the victims with all drivers wearing black armbands during the parade lap on race day as well as <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_France" title="Flag of France">the French <i>tricolore</i></a> being displayed on the truck that took the drivers around the track.<sup id="cite_ref-Paris_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Paris-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> French driver <a href="/wiki/Romain_Grosjean" title="Romain Grosjean">Romain Grosjean</a> had already worn an armband with the flag of his country during Saturday's sessions.<sup id="cite_ref-Paris_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Paris-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> However, a long planned minute of silence for the World Day of Remembrance for Road Traffic Victims was upheld and plans to dedicate the salute to the Paris attacks discarded.<sup id="cite_ref-Paris_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Paris-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After passing on using <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>'s new power unit in <a href="/wiki/2015_United_States_Grand_Prix" title="2015 United States Grand Prix">the United States</a> and <a href="/wiki/2015_Mexican_Grand_Prix" title="2015 Mexican Grand Prix">Mexico</a>, <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a> equipped <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a>'s car with the new specification unit, which earned him a ten-place grid penalty in the process.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Once he had tried the new unit during the free practice and qualifying sessions, Ricciardo lamented that the upgrade had not improved the performance.<sup id="cite_ref-Ric_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ric-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> He said: "In hindsight, it didn't give us any laptime, it's just for now a penalty for a little bit of knowledge."<sup id="cite_ref-Ric_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ric-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> During the first practice session on Friday, <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> tried out what was dubbed a "S-duct", a vent to channel airflow through the nose of the car, a feature already seen at other cars over the course of the season. However, Mercedes decided not to run the device over the whole weekend.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Going into the weekend <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> and his Mercedes team had already secured their respective World Championships. Fellow Mercedes driver <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> was second in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a>, 21 points ahead of <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Dpoint_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Dpoint-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The two were followed by Finns <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a> (<a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> (Ferrari), three points apart from each other, with <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> another six points behind in sixth.<sup id="cite_ref-Dpoint_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Dpoint-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' standings</a>, Ferrari was second on 374 points, followed by Williams with 243 points.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Free_practice">Free practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Free practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Per the regulations for the 2015 season, three practice sessions were scheduled, two 1.5-hour sessions on Friday and another one-hour session before qualifying on Saturday.<sup id="cite_ref-pracreg_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pracreg-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> was fastest in the first session on Friday morning, setting a time of 1:13.543, more than half a second clear of his teammate <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a>, who was second fastest.<sup id="cite_ref-prac_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prac-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> in third and fourth respectively were the only other drivers to lap inside one second of Hamilton.<sup id="cite_ref-prac_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prac-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The session was held in dry conditions, albeit earlier reports had indicated that thunderstorms could interrupt the weekend.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton set his fastest time on the medium compound, but was caught out twice on other laps, locking up and running wide in the Senna S.<sup id="cite_ref-prac_14-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prac-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> In the later parts of the session, he also complained about a "weird" feeling clutch, but returned to the track soon after.<sup id="cite_ref-prac_14-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prac-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a>, who was fifth fastest, spun out ten minutes before the end of the session, while eighth-placed <a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a> had done the same early in the session at turn three.<sup id="cite_ref-prac_14-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prac-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Jolyon_Palmer" title="Jolyon Palmer">Jolyon Palmer</a> replaced <a href="/wiki/Romain_Grosjean" title="Romain Grosjean">Romain Grosjean</a> at <a href="/wiki/Lotus_F1" title="Lotus F1">Lotus</a>, finishing twelfth fastest.<sup id="cite_ref-prac_14-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prac-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It was Hamilton's teammate and defending race winner Rosberg who topped the time sheets at the second session on Friday afternoon, setting a 1:12.385 lap time.<sup id="cite_ref-prac2_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prac2-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> He led Hamilton by almost half a second, with the two <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> cars of Vettel and Räikkönen about a second adrift. Slight rain saw most drivers head out early, but the conditions never became wet enough to require intermediate tyres.<sup id="cite_ref-prac2_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prac2-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> After starting on the medium compound tyres, many drivers switched to the softer compound for race simulations, only for the session to be red flagged shortly after when <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> spun at turn four, only to pull over at the side of the road with smoke coming from his car some corners later.<sup id="cite_ref-prac2_16-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prac2-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a> was sixth fastest, behind Daniel Ricciardo, suffering a spin at turn nine.<sup id="cite_ref-prac2_16-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prac2-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Despite several problems during the third session on Saturday morning, Lewis Hamilton was fastest, setting a time of 1:12.070, just over a tenth of a second in front of his teammate.<sup id="cite_ref-prac3_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prac3-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Earlier in the session, he needed to return to pit lane due to a gear-selection problem which had caused him to temporarily stop on track and later spun at turn eleven.<sup id="cite_ref-prac3_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prac3-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Vettel was again third fastest, more than six-tenths of a second behind Hamilton.<sup id="cite_ref-prac3_17-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prac3-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> He was followed by teammate Räikkönen, Valtteri Bottas and <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a>, all more than a second slower than Hamilton.<sup id="cite_ref-prac3_17-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prac3-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Nasr_Brazil_2015.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/00/Nasr_Brazil_2015.jpg/220px-Nasr_Brazil_2015.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="133" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/00/Nasr_Brazil_2015.jpg/330px-Nasr_Brazil_2015.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/00/Nasr_Brazil_2015.jpg/440px-Nasr_Brazil_2015.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4480" data-file-height="2703" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Nasr_Brazil_2015.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Local driver <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Nasr" title="Felipe Nasr">Felipe Nasr</a> was penalised after qualifying for impeding compatriot <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>Qualifying consisted of three parts, 18, 15 and 12 minutes in length respectively, with five drivers eliminated from competing after each of the first two sessions.<sup id="cite_ref-pracreg_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pracreg-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> During the first part of qualifying, all drivers used the softer compound tyres at least for their second run of timed laps, including the Mercedes pair. While Valtteri Bottas was the last driver to switch from the medium tyres, his <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> teammate <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> needed a quick lap at the end of the session to secure his participation in the second qualifying round, after he had been held up by compatriot <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Nasr" title="Felipe Nasr">Felipe Nasr</a> earlier in the session. Fernando Alonso's <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> broke down due to a loss of power before he was able to set a time, with teammate <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> likewise unable to proceed. Both <a href="/wiki/Marussia_F1" title="Marussia F1">Manor Marussia</a> drivers failed to advance, while <a href="/wiki/Pastor_Maldonado" title="Pastor Maldonado">Pastor Maldonado</a> in fifteenth was the last driver to be eliminated.<sup id="cite_ref-qual2_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qual2-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During the second part, the Mercedes drivers set only one run of timed laps as they were able to save their tyres for the race, well clear of the competition.<sup id="cite_ref-qual2_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qual2-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Lewis Hamilton was fastest, half a second ahead of Rosberg, with Sebastian Vettel between the two.<sup id="cite_ref-qual2_18-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qual2-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Romain Grosjean was eliminated after a spin at turn six.<sup id="cite_ref-qual2_18-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qual2-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> He was joined on the sidelines by both <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Saubers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Sainz_Jr." title="Carlos Sainz Jr.">Carlos Sainz Jr.</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-qual2_18-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qual2-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>When the top ten drivers took to the track for the third and final part of qualifying, Nico Rosberg was able to beat his teammate Hamilton to pole position by 0.078 seconds, securing his fifth pole in a row.<sup id="cite_ref-qual2_18-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qual2-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Third was Sebastian Vettel, ahead of Bottas, who would be demoted three places due to a penalty.<sup id="cite_ref-qual2_18-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qual2-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Following a mistake on his last timed lap, Kimi Räikkönen finished fifth fastest, ahead of <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-qual2_18-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qual2-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Albeit racing with an updated power unit, Daniel Ricciardo qualified slower than teammate <a href="/wiki/Daniil_Kvyat" title="Daniil Kvyat">Daniil Kvyat</a>, and a ten-place grid penalty for the change left him nineteenth on the grid.<sup id="cite_ref-qual2_18-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qual2-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-qual1_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qual1-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Vettel_Brazil_2015.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/95/Vettel_Brazil_2015.jpg/220px-Vettel_Brazil_2015.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="201" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/95/Vettel_Brazil_2015.jpg/330px-Vettel_Brazil_2015.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/95/Vettel_Brazil_2015.jpg/440px-Vettel_Brazil_2015.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3147" data-file-height="2871" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Vettel_Brazil_2015.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> finished third for <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>On his way to the starting grid, Carlos Sainz Jr. stopped on track and had to be pushed back to pit lane, where he eventually started, only to retire after just a few hundred metres. At the start, both Mercedes drivers got away well and Hamilton tried to overtake Rosberg for the lead around the outside of the first corner, but were unable to do so. Jenson Button made a good start, moving from 16th to 13th, while <a href="/wiki/Marcus_Ericsson" title="Marcus Ericsson">Marcus Ericsson</a> fell from 13th to 18th. Behind the leaders, the Ferrari drivers retained third and fourth place respectively. Bottas moved into fifth after moving around the outside.<sup id="cite_ref-tickguard_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tickguard-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-teltick_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-teltick-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton was able to follow Rosberg closely in the opening laps, while Daniel Ricciardo was the first driver to pit for new tyres on lap four. One lap later, Grosjean was able to use <a href="/wiki/Drag_reduction_system" title="Drag reduction system">drag reduction system</a> (DRS) to overtake Felipe Nasr for eleventh. With his back row start and an early pit stop, Ricciardo overtook the two Manor Marussia cars around lap nine.<sup id="cite_ref-tickguard_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tickguard-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Regular pit stops started on lap eleven, with Massa, Kvyat, Pérez and Grosjean being the first to come in. In addition, Nico Hülkenberg made a pit stop and exited ahead of both Massa and Kvyat, who both had been in front of him before their respective stops. Bottas came in a lap later and maintained position. Kimi Räikkönen, who had been complaining about lack of grip in his front tyres, made a pit stop on lap 13. Race leader Rosberg came in for his tyre change on lap fourteen, simultaneously with Vettel, while Hamilton stayed out for another lap. He was ultimately unable to use his additional lap to come out in front of his teammate and remained second. On lap 18, Nico Hülkenberg, who was suffering from an unstable car, overtook Maldonado for sixth place. Around lap 20, Hamilton was moving closer to Rosberg, being in DRS range for multiple laps before reporting to the garage that his tyres would not last long and eased up. On lap 22, Kvyat moved ahead of Maldonado as well and was now seventh. Lap 24 witnessed Massa go around Maldonado as well, while Felipe Nasr overtook Button for 14th.<sup id="cite_ref-tickguard_20-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tickguard-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-teltick_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-teltick-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:H%C3%BClkenberg_Brazil_2015.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/14/H%C3%BClkenberg_Brazil_2015.jpg/220px-H%C3%BClkenberg_Brazil_2015.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="108" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/14/H%C3%BClkenberg_Brazil_2015.jpg/330px-H%C3%BClkenberg_Brazil_2015.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/14/H%C3%BClkenberg_Brazil_2015.jpg/440px-H%C3%BClkenberg_Brazil_2015.jpg 2x" data-file-width="5946" data-file-height="2930" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:H%C3%BClkenberg_Brazil_2015.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Stopping just twice, <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a> finished in sixth place.</div></div></div>
<p>Daniel Ricciardo was the first driver to pit for a second time on lap 30. At the same time, a three-way fight had emerged for ninth place, with Pérez in front of Grosjean and Verstappen. On lap 32, Verstappen moved ahead of Grosjean and took another position from Pérez, who in turn lost another place to Grosjean in the same manoeuvre. Vettel made a pit stop for a second time on lap 33, prompting Rosberg to follow suit one lap later. Hamilton briefly held the lead, but made a pit stop as well soon after, emerging to set a new fastest lap of the race albeit emerging behind both Ferrari cars. Lap 36 saw a collision between Pastor Maldonado and Marcus Ericsson, but both drivers were able to continue the race. Two laps later, Maldonado was in another fight for position with the other Sauber of Felipe Nasr, moving ahead. He ultimately received a five-second penalty for his part in the incident with Ericsson.<sup id="cite_ref-tickguard_20-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tickguard-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-teltick_21-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-teltick-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>By lap 43, Romain Grosjean had managed to overtake Verstappen for tenth place. Hamilton started to lose ground on Rosberg, trailing him by 3.4 seconds on lap 44; however, he managed to close the gap in the succeeding laps. Kimi Räikkönen put in his second stop on lap 47, followed by Vettel's third just one lap later, with Rosberg reacting and changing tyres as well. Hamilton changed tyres on lap 50, and went on to set the fastest lap of the race one lap later. On lap 53, Massa moved ahead of Grosjean into eighth place, but was already reported under investigation for his pre-race tyre temperatures at that point. After serving his penalty, Maldonado managed to overtake Nasr for ninth place on lap 57. Nasr, who was utilizing a two-stop strategy, struggled with his tyres and fell back behind Ricciardo on lap 61. Ricciardo gained another position at Pérez's expense on lap 67, while Verstappen moved into tenth place one lap later, overtaking Maldonado. At the front, Hamilton's tyre started to wear off in the closing stages, enabling Rosberg to take victory 7.7 seconds ahead of his teammate.<sup id="cite_ref-tickguard_20-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tickguard-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-teltick_21-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-teltick-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen_Brazil_2015.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/42/R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen_Brazil_2015.jpg/220px-R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen_Brazil_2015.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="121" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/42/R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen_Brazil_2015.jpg/330px-R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen_Brazil_2015.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/42/R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen_Brazil_2015.jpg/440px-R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen_Brazil_2015.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4678" data-file-height="2564" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen_Brazil_2015.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>By finishing fourth, <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> moved within one point of <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a> in the Championship.</div></div></div>
<p>At the podium interviews, which were conducted by former Formula One driver <a href="/wiki/Martin_Brundle" title="Martin Brundle">Martin Brundle</a>, Nico Rosberg expressed delight about his victory, but also remembered the events of Paris which, as he put it, rendered "everything relative". Replying to Brundle's notion that driving the way he did earlier in the season would have given him a chance for the championship, Rosberg replied: "Thank you very much for that piece of advice, I could figure that out for myself!" Lewis Hamilton on the other hand lamented the fact that he was unable to follow Rosberg closely on track without damaging his tyres too much, saying that he would have otherwise had the pace to pass him. Third placed Sebastian Vettel called it "a good race", but also "not very exciting", since the Ferrari drivers had been "in no-mans land" behind the Mercedes.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The main talking point after the race was Hamilton's assertion that he would have preferred to go on a different strategy compared to his teammate to have a chance of passing him, an option denied by his team.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton lobbied for his team to allow the drivers to take more risks,<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> while his team principal, <a href="/wiki/Toto_Wolff" title="Toto Wolff">Toto Wolff</a>, insisted that the team, not the drivers, were at liberty to make calls about strategy.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Opinions on the matter were divided in the following days. <a href="/wiki/1996_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1996 Formula One World Championship">1996</a> world champion <a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a> shared Hamilton's frustration, saying: "I have some sympathy with what Lewis was saying, the drivers should be allowed to call the shots - and if he wants to try something different and basically zag when the other guy is zigging."<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Daniel Johnson, writing for <i><a href="/wiki/The_Daily_Telegraph" title="The Daily Telegraph">The Daily Telegraph</a></i>, concurred: "It was not just Hamilton's failure to win the Brazilian Grand Prix, following home an obdurate Nico Rosberg; it was the manner of it, lacking the killer move while obeying a Mercedes strategy which rendered the drivers more robots than racers."<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After the race <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> was disqualified after it was found that his tyres were too hot at the start of the race. <a href="/wiki/Pirelli" title="Pirelli">Pirelli</a> stated that the highest temperature at his right rear tyre was found to be 137&#160;°C (279&#160;°F), while a maximum temperature of 110&#160;°C (230&#160;°F) was allowed. Williams announced that they would appeal the penalty.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> However, on 19 November 2015, Williams decided to drop the appeal, stating that since a decision was unlikely to be made before the end of the year, the matter would draw attention away from the preparations for the <a href="/wiki/2016_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2016 Formula One World Championship">2016</a> season.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Following his collision with Marcus Ericsson, Pastor Maldonado came under criticism by <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a> team principal <a href="/wiki/Monisha_Kaltenborn" title="Monisha Kaltenborn">Monisha Kaltenborn</a>, who called him "very annoying", pointing to the fact that Maldonado had been involved in similar situations in the past.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Reception of the race was mainly negative. Italian newspaper <i><a href="/wiki/Corriere_dello_Sport_%E2%80%93_Stadio" title="Corriere dello Sport – Stadio">Corriere dello Sport – Stadio</a></i> called it "boring",<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> while fellow Italian paper <i><a href="/wiki/Corriere_della_Sera" title="Corriere della Sera">Corriere della Sera</a></i> wrote of "Formula One like a sleeping pill".<sup id="cite_ref-kicker_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kicker-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Sky_Sports" title="Sky Sports">Sky Sports</a> described the race as "mundane" and "soporific".<sup id="cite_ref-sky1_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sky1-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Spanish newspaper <i><a href="/wiki/Sport_(Spanish_newspaper)" title="Sport (Spanish newspaper)">Sport</a></i> highlighted Max Verstappen's "spectacular" overtaking as bringing "the only colour" into the race.<sup id="cite_ref-kicker_32-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kicker-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/The_Independent" title="The Independent">The Independent</a></i> wrote, referring to Hamilton's desperate attempts to pass Rosberg: "If there was ever a race which confirmed that Formula One should be looking at ways to facilitate overtaking, this was it."<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> In the wake of his experience, Hamilton called for Formula One to adjust rules to enhance overtaking, saying: "I guess for fans it's probably not too exciting to watch. Of course, it's always nice when you're at the front, as we have been for some time now - but still, being able to race is what... and also down the back, the rest of the field is probably what fans want to see. That's probably a change that would be looked positively on." Sebastian Vettel agreed, calling for more grip created by the tyres to make it easier to follow a car closely.<sup id="cite_ref-sky1_33-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sky1-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As a result of the race, Nico Rosberg secured second place in the Drivers' Championship ahead of Vettel. Further back, Kimi Räikkönen closed the gap to fourth placed Valtteri Bottas to just one point ahead of the <a href="/wiki/2015_Abu_Dhabi_Grand_Prix" title="2015 Abu Dhabi Grand Prix">final race of the season</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> In the Constructors' Championship, Williams and Force India secured their respective third and fifth places, with no team able to overcome them on points.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
