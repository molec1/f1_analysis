<p>The <b>1989 United States Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held in <a href="/wiki/Phoenix,_Arizona" title="Phoenix, Arizona">Phoenix, Arizona</a> on June 4, 1989. It was the fifth race of the <a href="/wiki/1989_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1989 Formula One World Championship">1989 Formula One World Championship</a> and the first United States Grand Prix to be held in Phoenix.
</p><p><br />
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1989_United_States_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In October 1988, city officials of Detroit Michigan refused to invest more money to bring the Detroit circuit up to new Formula 1 regulations, and an attempt to move the circuit to a new circuit on Belle Isle, an island in the middle of the Detroit River failed (although there was success for IndyCar's move to Belle Isle for 1992). This resulted in the cancellation of the Detroit race for the 1989 season. City officials in Phoenix, Arizona, were interested in hosting major sporting event to promote the dry desert city of Phoenix worldwide. A Formula 1 race came into play after a desperate attempt by Bernie Ecclestone not to lose the United States Grand Prix. On January 13, 1989 the Phoenix City Council approved a five-year contract with F1 to promote and run the race, with event date June 4. It was well known that Phoenix weather is very warm- around the 100's F (40 C) in June but no other slot was available because Phoenix got the race on a short notice.<sup id="cite_ref-NYT_Siano_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYT_Siano-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>  On the Thursday before qualifying, <a href="/wiki/Formula_Atlantic" title="Formula Atlantic">Formula Atlantic</a>, the support category for the weekend, had the first practice session on the new track before the Formula One cars hit the circuit for pre-qualifying at 8 o'clock on Friday morning. The Atlantic session saw a couple of problems. A manhole cover was lifted and the track surface at turn 9 at the end of the back straight (Washington Street) had begun to break up in the same way Detroit and <a href="/wiki/Fair_Park" title="Fair Park">Dallas</a> had done in the past. Overnight, quick-dry cement was used to patch up the broken surface and while dusty and bumpy, the cement held for the remainder of the weekend.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1989_United_States_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-qualifying_report">Pre-qualifying report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1989_United_States_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Pre-qualifying report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A <a href="/wiki/Brabham" title="Brabham">Brabham</a> topped the pre-qualifying session time sheets for the fifth time this season as <a href="/wiki/Martin_Brundle" title="Martin Brundle">Martin Brundle</a> was fastest. It was the same four pre-qualifiers as at the last event in <a href="/wiki/1989_Mexican_Grand_Prix" title="1989 Mexican Grand Prix">Mexico</a>, albeit in a different order, as <a href="/wiki/Alex_Caffi" title="Alex Caffi">Alex Caffi</a> took second in the <a href="/wiki/Dallara" title="Dallara">Dallara</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stefan_Johansson" title="Stefan Johansson">Stefan Johansson</a> was third in the <a href="/wiki/Onyx_Grand_Prix" title="Onyx Grand Prix">Onyx</a>, and Brundle's Brabham team-mate <a href="/wiki/Stefano_Modena" title="Stefano Modena">Stefano Modena</a> was fourth.
</p><p>Those to miss out included <a href="/wiki/Piercarlo_Ghinzani" title="Piercarlo Ghinzani">Piercarlo Ghinzani</a>, who was fifth in his <a href="/wiki/Osella" title="Osella">Osella</a>, and sixth-fastest <a href="/wiki/Pierre-Henri_Raphanel" title="Pierre-Henri Raphanel">Pierre-Henri Raphanel</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Coloni" title="Scuderia Coloni">Coloni</a>. Seventh was <a href="/wiki/Gregor_Foitek" title="Gregor Foitek">Gregor Foitek</a> in the <a href="/wiki/EuroBrun" title="EuroBrun">EuroBrun</a>, ahead of the second Osella of <a href="/wiki/Nicola_Larini" title="Nicola Larini">Nicola Larini</a>. In a better showing than in previous races, <a href="/wiki/Joachim_Winkelhock" title="Joachim Winkelhock">Joachim Winkelhock</a> was ninth in his <a href="/wiki/Automobiles_Gonfaronnaises_Sportives" title="Automobiles Gonfaronnaises Sportives">AGS</a>, followed by <a href="/wiki/Volker_Weidler" title="Volker Weidler">Volker Weidler</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Rial_Racing" title="Rial Racing">Rial</a>. Then came the two <a href="/wiki/Zakspeed" title="Zakspeed">Zakspeeds</a> of <a href="/wiki/Bernd_Schneider_(racing_driver)" title="Bernd Schneider (racing driver)">Bernd Schneider</a> and <a href="/wiki/Aguri_Suzuki" title="Aguri Suzuki">Aguri Suzuki</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Bertrand_Gachot" title="Bertrand Gachot">Bertrand Gachot</a> slowest in the other Onyx, after a <a href="/wiki/Constant-velocity_joint" title="Constant-velocity joint">CV joint</a> failed on his car, and the spare car would not start.<sup id="cite_ref-Walker_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Walker-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying_report">Qualifying report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1989_United_States_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Qualifying report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In qualifying on Friday, <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> went progressively faster and faster, eventually posting a time 1.5 seconds ahead of <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> team mate <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> and the rest of the field.
Senna's Friday time of 1:30.710 stood up through the second session and gave him his 34th career pole position, breaking <a href="/wiki/Jim_Clark" title="Jim Clark">Jim Clark</a>'s record of 33 which he had equalled in the previous race in <a href="/wiki/1989_Mexican_Grand_Prix" title="1989 Mexican Grand Prix">Mexico</a>.
</p><p>During the morning practice on Saturday, Prost spun backward into a wall and damaged the monocoque and gearbox. It was the first <a href="/wiki/Monocoque" title="Monocoque">monocoque</a> Prost had broken since joining the team in <a href="/wiki/1984_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1984 Formula One World Championship">1984</a>. Prost then had to take Senna's spare car for the race. 
</p><p>The only American driver in the field, Phoenix native <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Cheever" title="Eddie Cheever">Eddie Cheever</a>, qualified his <a href="/wiki/Arrows_A11" title="Arrows A11">Arrows</a>-<a href="/wiki/Cosworth_DFR" class="mw-redirect" title="Cosworth DFR">Ford</a> in 17th place, some 3.1 seconds slower than pole man Senna.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1989_United_States_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_report">Race report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1989_United_States_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Race report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Alessandro_Nannini" title="Alessandro Nannini">Alessandro Nannini</a> crashed his <a href="/wiki/Benetton_B188" title="Benetton B188">Benetton</a> heavily in the morning warm-up session and was forced to start the race not only in the spare car but also wearing a neck brace due to a very sore neck.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Prost got a jump on Senna at the start, but hit a bump in the straight, causing his wheels to spin and the engine to be cut momentarily by the rev limiter allowing Senna to pull ahead, but by the end of the first lap his lead was only 0.45 seconds. Nannini ran third followed by <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alex_Caffi" title="Alex Caffi">Alex Caffi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stefano_Modena" title="Stefano Modena">Stefano Modena</a>, <a href="/wiki/Martin_Brundle" title="Martin Brundle">Martin Brundle</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a>, <a href="/wiki/Andrea_de_Cesaris" title="Andrea de Cesaris">Andrea de Cesaris</a> and <a href="/wiki/Michele_Alboreto" title="Michele Alboreto">Michele Alboreto</a>.
Nannini's neck could only take 10 laps of racing before he pulled into the pits to retire. He had been third until a spin on lap four dropped him to eighth and retired after not being able to hold his head up properly and complaining of dizziness. After 16 laps, Senna's lead over Prost was 4.25 seconds. He suddenly doubled that on the next lap when Prost's engine began overheating, forcing the Frenchman to back off for a few laps in a bid to get the water and oil temperatures back to normal. Despite Senna's seemingly commanding position, Prost remained confident of winning as he had seen that his <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/5" title="McLaren MP4/5">McLaren</a> was handling better than his team mate's. He reasoned that later in the race his only problem would be getting past.
</p><p>The gap between the two McLarens varied as they worked their way through traffic, but on lap 29, Prost closed the gap when Senna suffered a misfire. The problem disappeared momentarily, with Senna doing his fastest lap of the race, but then returned, worse than before. <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> would soon retire for the 4th time out of 5 races by lap 32 with the result of an alternator failure. On lap 34, with Prost only one second back, Senna waved his team mate past as they went down the back straight and then pitted at the end of the lap.
</p><p>The Honda engine's electronic fuel injection system was acting up and after two pit stops to change the black box, battery and plugs, and with successive fastest laps in between, Senna retired on lap 44 with electrical problems (notably his 1st retirement of the season). Since joining McLaren at the start of <a href="/wiki/1988_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1988 Formula One World Championship">1988</a> it was his first ever retirement because of a <a href="/wiki/Honda_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Honda F1">Honda</a> engine failure and the first failure of their <a href="/wiki/V10_engine" title="V10 engine">V10 engine</a> under race conditions. It was also only the second time in 21 races with McLaren that a Honda engine had failed, the first being when Prost's <a href="/wiki/V6_engine" title="V6 engine">V6</a> turbo had blown up halfway through the <a href="/wiki/1988_Italian_Grand_Prix" title="1988 Italian Grand Prix">1988 Italian Grand Prix</a>, the only race of the 1988 season that McLaren did not win.
</p><p>Alex Caffi, who had started in sixth in his <a href="/wiki/Pirelli" title="Pirelli">Pirelli</a> shod <a href="/wiki/Dallara" title="Dallara">Dallara</a>-<a href="/wiki/Cosworth_DFR" class="mw-redirect" title="Cosworth DFR">Ford</a>, was up to second with Senna's retirement. A stop for new tires, after being passed by Berger (whose palms were still raw and sore from his <a href="/wiki/1989_San_Marino_Grand_Prix" title="1989 San Marino Grand Prix">Imola</a> crash only six weeks before), dropped him back two more spots to fifth. As he tried to re-lap his team mate de Cesaris, however, de Cesaris turned in, forcing Caffi into the wall and out of the race. After the race de Cesaris said that he simply did not see Caffi and did not even know about putting him into the wall until after the race. De Cesaris continued on to an 8th-place finish. Berger's <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_640" title="Ferrari 640">Ferrari</a> suffered alternator failure (meaning no power to the revolutionary <a href="/wiki/Semi-automatic_transmission" title="Semi-automatic transmission">semi-automatic transmission</a>) 9 laps after Caffi's retirement. Reporters tried to interview Berger but his mechanic closed the garage door; just before the garage door closed ESPN pit reporter John Bisignano saw Berger being splashed with water.
</p><p>Throughout the race, <a href="/wiki/Riccardo_Patrese" title="Riccardo Patrese">Riccardo Patrese</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ivan_Capelli" title="Ivan Capelli">Ivan Capelli</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Cheever" title="Eddie Cheever">Eddie Cheever</a> had been in close contact. When Capelli retired on lap 21 with a gearbox failure, Patrese and Cheever carried on the battle alone. After lap 51, the fight was for second place, with Patrese ahead. Despite a fuel pickup problem with his engine, Cheever mounted a challenge in the closing laps until his front brakes and one rear brake failed.  He finished in third place.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Stefan_Johansson" title="Stefan Johansson">Stefan Johansson</a> had managed to drag the Onyx not only through pre-qualifying but also onto the grid and drove a steady race to be running in 7th place just outside the points before having to retire on lap 50 with suspension damage from a previous puncture.
</p><p>As was predicted, the two-hour time limit was reached after 75 of the scheduled 81 laps, and Prost coasted to his only United States win (after not having won at <a href="/wiki/Watkins_Glen_International" title="Watkins Glen International">Watkins Glen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Grand_Prix_of_Long_Beach" title="Grand Prix of Long Beach">Long Beach</a>, <a href="/wiki/Caesars_Palace_Grand_Prix" title="Caesars Palace Grand Prix">Las Vegas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Detroit_Street_Circuit" class="mw-redirect" title="Detroit Street Circuit">Detroit</a> or <a href="/wiki/Fair_Park#Other_notable_events" title="Fair Park">Dallas</a>), and increased his then all-time record victory total to 36 and his first win in a naturally aspirated car (his only other season in F1 without turbo power had been his rookie season with McLaren in <a href="/wiki/1980_Formula_One_season" title="1980 Formula One season">1980</a> when the team used the <a href="/wiki/Cosworth_DFV" title="Cosworth DFV">Cosworth DFV</a> <a href="/wiki/V8_engine" title="V8 engine">V8 engine</a>). He also took the lead, by two points over Senna, in the Driver's Championship, which he eventually won. Patrese's runner-up placing was his second in a row. After struggling through practice, qualifying and warm up, and starting from 14th spot, Patrese and technical director <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Head" title="Patrick Head">Patrick Head</a> had guessed at a setup and finally got it right for the race. <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Cheever" title="Eddie Cheever">Eddie Cheever</a>'s third place was the ninth and last podium finish of his F1 career. <a href="/wiki/Christian_Danner" title="Christian Danner">Christian Danner</a> benefited from retirements ahead of him to take fourth place for <a href="/wiki/Rial_Racing" title="Rial Racing">Rial</a>.  It was his best career finish and matched the best ever finish for the team.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1989_United_States_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Before the race there was a push to reduce the number of race laps from 81 to 70 due to the expected hot weather and after practice times had revealed the race would hit the two hour mark well before the scheduled number of laps had been reached: with the track almost the same length as the <a href="/wiki/Adelaide_Street_Circuit" title="Adelaide Street Circuit">Adelaide Street Circuit</a> used for the <a href="/wiki/Australian_Grand_Prix" title="Australian Grand Prix">Australian Grand Prix</a>, the prediction was that laps times would be around the 1:15 to 1:20 mark. Qualifying times, however, had been some 10–15 seconds slower than predicted.  <a href="/wiki/Ken_Tyrrell" title="Ken Tyrrell">Ken Tyrrell</a> was the only team boss who refused to sign the document which would have allowed the race length to be reduced. In the race, <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Palmer" title="Jonathan Palmer">Jonathan Palmer</a> lost a certain 4th place when his <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_018" title="Tyrrell 018">Tyrrell</a>-Ford ran out of fuel on lap 69. Had the race been flagged after 70 laps, Palmer would have finished 4th having already been lapped by Prost instead of running out of fuel and being classified as 9th and last.
The organizers were slightly disappointed with a crowd of 31,441 <sup id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> turned out for the race on Sunday in 100 degrees Fahrenheit heat, having hoped for 40,000.
</p>
