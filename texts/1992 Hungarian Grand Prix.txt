<p>The <b>1992 Hungarian Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at <a href="/wiki/Hungaroring" title="Hungaroring">Hungaroring</a> on 16 August 1992. It was the eleventh round of the <a href="/wiki/1992_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1992 Formula One World Championship">1992 Formula One World Championship</a>. <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> clinched the Drivers' Championship by finishing the race in second position, behind <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1992_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-qualifying_report">Pre-qualifying report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1992_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Pre-qualifying report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After the previous Grand Prix in <a href="/wiki/1992_German_Grand_Prix" title="1992 German Grand Prix">Germany</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Fondmetal" title="Fondmetal">Fondmetal</a> team replaced <a href="/wiki/Andrea_Chiesa" title="Andrea Chiesa">Andrea Chiesa</a> with <a href="/wiki/Brabham" title="Brabham">Brabham</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Eric_van_de_Poele" title="Eric van de Poele">Eric van de Poele</a>. Brabham did not replace van de Poele and reduced their entry to one car. This meant the pre-qualifying pool could be reduced by one, and thus <a href="/wiki/Larrousse" title="Larrousse">Larrousse</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Bertrand_Gachot" title="Bertrand Gachot">Bertrand Gachot</a> was no longer required to pre-qualify.<sup id="cite_ref-Walker_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Walker-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The session proved practically unnecessary as again, the <a href="/wiki/Andrea_Moda_Formula" title="Andrea Moda Formula">Andrea Moda</a> team did not make a serious attempt to run their second car, driven by <a href="/wiki/Perry_McCarthy" title="Perry McCarthy">Perry McCarthy</a>. His team-mate <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Moreno" title="Roberto Moreno">Roberto Moreno</a> had used both cars to pre-qualify, and McCarthy was only allowed to leave the pits 45 seconds before the end of the session, leaving him no chance to reach the start-finish line to begin a timed lap before the chequered flag was waved.<sup id="cite_ref-Walker_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Walker-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-McCarthy_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-McCarthy-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> This being the latest in a succession of similar incidents, <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_du_Sport_Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale du Sport Automobile">FISA</a> formally warned Andrea Moda to make a proper effort to run both cars at the next Grand Prix in <a href="/wiki/1992_Belgian_Grand_Prix" title="1992 Belgian Grand Prix">Belgium</a>, or face suspension from the following event in <a href="/wiki/1992_Italian_Grand_Prix" title="1992 Italian Grand Prix">Italy</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Walker_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Walker-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The other four cars in the session thus all pre-qualified, with the Fondmetals taking the first two places as <a href="/wiki/Gabriele_Tarquini" title="Gabriele Tarquini">Gabriele Tarquini</a> outpaced van de Poele by nearly a second. <a href="/wiki/Ukyo_Katayama" title="Ukyo Katayama">Ukyo Katayama</a> was another second further back in the remaining <a href="/wiki/Larrousse" title="Larrousse">Larrousse</a>, with Moreno 1.1 seconds slower in fourth, only his second pre-qualification success this season. McCarthy was the sole entrant to fail to pre-qualify.
</p><p>After the race the Brabham team withdrew entirely, removing the necessity for further pre-qualifying sessions. To date, this pre-qualifying session was the last to be held in Formula One.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying_report">Qualifying report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1992_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Qualifying report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1992_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_report">Race report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1992_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Race report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the start, <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> passed <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>, and then Mansell lost momentum and Senna passed him with Berger following his teammate through. The order was: Patrese, Senna, Berger, Mansell, Schumacher and Brundle.
</p><p>There was a collision on the first lap between <a href="/wiki/%C3%89rik_Comas" title="Érik Comas">Érik Comas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Herbert" title="Johnny Herbert">Johnny Herbert</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thierry_Boutsen" title="Thierry Boutsen">Thierry Boutsen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gabriele_Tarquini" title="Gabriele Tarquini">Gabriele Tarquini</a> who all retired on the first lap (meaning both Ligiers of Comas and Boutsen retired on the first lap), before <a href="/wiki/Eric_van_de_Poele" title="Eric van de Poele">Eric van de Poele</a> spun into retirement at the first corner on lap 3 as both <a href="/wiki/Fondmetal" title="Fondmetal">Fondmetals</a> retired after only two laps. Mansell passed Berger on lap 8 and set off after Senna. However, as hard as he tried, the combination of the small circuit and Senna's skills in defending meant that he could not pass. Another multi-car collision came on lap 14 which eliminated <a href="/wiki/Bertrand_Gachot" title="Bertrand Gachot">Bertrand Gachot</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aguri_Suzuki" title="Aguri Suzuki">Aguri Suzuki</a>, <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Grouillard" title="Olivier Grouillard">Olivier Grouillard</a>, <a href="/wiki/Karl_Wendlinger" title="Karl Wendlinger">Karl Wendlinger</a> and <a href="/wiki/Stefano_Modena" title="Stefano Modena">Stefano Modena</a> (forcing all five drivers to retire on the same lap). Alesi in the leading Ferrari behind the leading pack spun out with the rear wheels in the gravel trap by lap 15. On lap 31, Patrese made a mistake and went wide and rejoined behind Berger. Two laps later, Mansell passed Berger to get back third. Then, a pivotal movement came on lap 39 when Patrese spun off. He rejoined in seventh, outside the points which meant that Mansell would be the world champion if results stayed the same. However, on lap 51, Mansell had to go to the pits with tyre troubles and rejoined in sixth, just ahead of Patrese. Mansell quickly passed Häkkinen but before Patrese, who was right behind could take sixth, Patrese's engine blew. He was out and would get no points.
</p><p>Mansell quickly caught and passed Brundle on lap 60 and four laps later, he was up to third when <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>'s rear wing broke on his Benetton, spinning him out and retired on lap 64. Senna, a minute at the front, made a precautionary stop just as Häkkinen passed Brundle for fourth. Mansell passed Berger for second and now had a toehold on the championship. Soon afterwards, Häkkinen tried to pass Berger and spun into Brundle's path. Brundle was forced to spin to avoid a collision and both rejoined without losing places, with Häkkinen staying ahead. Senna won ahead of new world champion Mansell, Berger, Häkkinen, Brundle and Capelli scoring his last point for Ferrari as well as the last point of his career.
</p>
