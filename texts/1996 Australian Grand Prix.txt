<p>The <b>1996 Australian Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at <a href="/wiki/Melbourne_Grand_Prix_Circuit" class="mw-redirect" title="Melbourne Grand Prix Circuit">Melbourne</a> on 10 March 1996. It was the first race of the <a href="/wiki/1996_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1996 Formula One World Championship">1996 Formula One World Championship</a>, and the first Australian Grand Prix to be held at Melbourne, taking over from <a href="/wiki/Adelaide_Street_Circuit" title="Adelaide Street Circuit">Adelaide</a>.
</p><p>The 58-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW18" title="Williams FW18">Williams</a>-<a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>. Hill's teammate <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a>, making his Formula One debut, took <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> and led for most of the race, before an oil leak enabled Hill to catch and pass him in the closing laps. <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a> finished third in a <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_F310" title="Ferrari F310">Ferrari</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1996_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1996_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>This was second grand prix in a row held in Australia, the previous race being the conclusion to the <a href="/wiki/1995_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="1995 Formula One season">1995 season</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Taki_Inoue" title="Taki Inoue">Taki Inoue</a> was scheduled to race for the <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a> team as a pay driver but when no money materialised prior to the race he was replaced by Fisichella.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Marlboro had expressed interest in Fisichella running early on.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The race was the first to use the new race-start system, still used in Formula 1 today,<sup id="cite_ref-1996_Aust_GP_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1996_Aust_GP-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> replacing the old red to green light system. Under the new system, five red lights would come on at one second intervals, starting after the last driver reached his grid box. There would then be a pre-determined pause, and then the five lights would go off simultaneously.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> This was also the first race to have a single qualifying session on Saturday afternoon; the Friday session was dropped.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1996_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a>, making his début in Formula One, took pole position.
</p><p>Both <a href="/wiki/Forti" title="Forti">Fortis</a> did not make the race due to the new <a href="/wiki/107%25_rule" title="107% rule">107% rule</a> for qualifying, which said any car that qualified 107% slower than the pole time (1:38.837 in this race) would be excluded. Incidentally, the team had logged its best result of 7th one race ago at the season-ending <a href="/wiki/1995_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="1995 Australian Grand Prix">1995 Australian Grand Prix</a><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The measure was introduced as excessively slow entrants presented potential safety hazards due to a high speed difference.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="First_start">First start</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1996_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: First start">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>It was an all-<a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a> front row with <a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a> and debutant <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a> in the blue and white <a href="/wiki/Rothmans_International#Sponsorship_of_motorsports" title="Rothmans International">Rothmans</a> cars.
In the first corner Hill was squeezed by Irvine, lost momentum and was overtaken by both Ferraris in the run down going into the third corner. Behind Hill, Alesi sliced across in front of Hakkinen and Barrichello to claim the corner and began a chain reaction of heavy braking as drivers tried to avoid colliding with one another. <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> veered left under braking and his McLaren hit the side of Herbert's Sauber. Herbert tried to avoid the car and braked heavily. Martin Brundle was behind them and unable to slow sufficiently, hitting the rear of Herbert's and Coulthard's cars and was launched into a barrel roll, ending in a sand trap at turn 3 and breaking his car in two. Brundle was unhurt. The race was halted to allow the circuit to be cleared.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-1996_Aust_GP_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1996_Aust_GP-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Second_start_and_abbreviated_race_summary">Second start and abbreviated race summary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1996_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Second start and abbreviated race summary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The race was restarted. Brundle spun off after light contact with <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Diniz" title="Pedro Diniz">Pedro Diniz</a>. The Williams dominated again, with Jacques Villeneuve leading Hill. Schumacher held on in third place, but dropped back half a minute with his second pit stop. He developed brake problems on lap 28 and retired five laps later. Irvine assumed third, despite contact Jean Alesi's Benetton on lap 6, when Alesi had attempted to pass him. Towards the end of the race Villeneuve was slowed by an oil leak, which allowed Hill to catch and pass him.
</p><p>Hill took his 14th Grand Prix victory, equaling his father <a href="/wiki/Graham_Hill" title="Graham Hill">Graham</a>'s overall number of wins. Hill took back to back Australian victories, the <a href="/wiki/1995_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="1995 Australian Grand Prix">previous race</a> being the last round of <a href="/wiki/1995_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1995 Formula One World Championship">1995</a>, in Adelaide. In the end, the podium was Hill-Villeneuve-Irvine.
</p>
