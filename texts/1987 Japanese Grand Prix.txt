<p>The <b>1987 Japanese Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at <a href="/wiki/Suzuka_Circuit" class="mw-redirect" title="Suzuka Circuit">Suzuka</a> on 1 November 1987. It was the fifteenth and penultimate race of the <a href="/wiki/1987_FIA_Formula_One_World_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="1987 FIA Formula One World Championship">1987 FIA Formula One World Championship</a>. It was the first <a href="/wiki/Japanese_Grand_Prix" title="Japanese Grand Prix">Japanese Grand Prix</a> since <a href="/wiki/1977_Japanese_Grand_Prix" title="1977 Japanese Grand Prix">1977</a>, and the first as part of the Formula One World Championship to be held at the <a href="/wiki/Honda" title="Honda">Honda</a>-owned Suzuka Circuit, which originated as a test track for Honda motorcycles and automobiles.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Soichiro_Honda" title="Soichiro Honda">Soichiro Honda</a> was extremely enthusiastic about this race, and told his racing engineers "We have to win. And we have to keep winning..."<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> aiming for a hometown victory at Honda's home track in its native Japan. Soichiro Honda had reason for optimism as four of the entrants were powered by <a href="/wiki/Honda" title="Honda">Honda</a>-made engines. The <a href="/wiki/Lotus_99T" title="Lotus 99T">Lotus 99Ts</a> of <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a>, who had won races earlier in the season and was joined on Team Lotus with national favourite <a href="/wiki/Satoru_Nakajima" title="Satoru Nakajima">Satoru Nakajima</a>, along with the dominating <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW11B" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams FW11B">Williams FW11Bs</a> driven by <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a>, who were both vying for the overall championship. However, after <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> was taken out of contention by a qualifying crash, the other three cars of Piquet, Senna and Nakajima could only qualify in 5th, 7th and 11th places respectively, with the best finish for Honda being Senna's 2nd place.
</p><p>The race was won by Austrian driver <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> driving a <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_F1/87" title="Ferrari F1/87">Ferrari F1/87</a>. It was the end of a 38-race losing streak for Formula One's most famous team and Berger's second Grand Prix victory having won the <a href="/wiki/1986_Mexican_Grand_Prix" title="1986 Mexican Grand Prix">Mexican Grand Prix the previous year</a>. Berger won by 17 seconds over Brazilian driver <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> driving a <a href="/wiki/Lotus_99T" title="Lotus 99T">Lotus 99T</a>. Third was the <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/3" title="McLaren MP4/3">McLaren MP4/3</a> of Swedish driver <a href="/wiki/Stefan_Johansson" title="Stefan Johansson">Stefan Johansson</a>.
</p><p>The World Drivers' Championship was decided during practice when British driver <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> crashed his <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW11B" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams FW11B">Williams FW11B</a> heavily at the S curves. Mansell's injuries put him out of racing for the remainder of the season, leaving Brazilian Williams driver <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a> unopposed to claim his third World Championship, adding to his victories with <a href="/wiki/Brabham" title="Brabham">Brabham</a> in <a href="/wiki/1981_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="1981 Formula One season">1981</a> and <a href="/wiki/1983_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="1983 Formula One season">1983</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1987_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The 1987 Japanese Grand Prix was the first race to be held in Japan since <a href="/wiki/James_Hunt" title="James Hunt">James Hunt</a> won in his McLaren at <a href="/wiki/Fuji_Speedway" title="Fuji Speedway">Fuji</a>, in <a href="/wiki/1977_Japanese_Grand_Prix" title="1977 Japanese Grand Prix">1977</a>. This time, the Grand Prix circus utilised the Honda-owned <a href="/wiki/Suzuka_Circuit" class="mw-redirect" title="Suzuka Circuit">Suzuka Circuit</a>. The scene was set for a tense championship deciding race between bitter Williams Honda teammates, <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a>. However, Mansell suffered a huge crash during Friday qualifying while trying to better Piquet's time which put him out of action for both the Japanese race and the subsequent <a href="/wiki/1987_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="1987 Australian Grand Prix">Australian Grand Prix</a>. As a consequence, Piquet won his third World Championship before the race even began.
</p><p>Qualifying once again demonstrated the return to form of Ferrari, as <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> obtained his second pole position of the season, with the <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_F1/87" title="Ferrari F1/87">F1/87</a> being perfectly suited to the Suzuka circuit. <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> qualified 2nd in his <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/3" title="McLaren MP4/3">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Techniques_d%27Avant_Garde" title="Techniques d&#39;Avant Garde">TAG</a> with <a href="/wiki/Thierry_Boutsen" title="Thierry Boutsen">Thierry Boutsen</a> 3rd in his <a href="/wiki/Benetton_B187" title="Benetton B187">Benetton</a>-<a href="/wiki/Ford_Motor_Company" title="Ford Motor Company">Ford</a>. Following Mansell's Friday crash, the three remaining Honda powered cars of Piquet, Senna, and Senna's teammate, local favourite <a href="/wiki/Satoru_Nakajima" title="Satoru Nakajima">Satoru Nakajima</a>, could only qualify in 5th, 7th and 11th places respectively. All drivers from 8th position and below moved up one position on the grid due to Mansell being unable to start. This meant Roberto Moreno was able to race the AGS despite having the 27th fastest qualifying time.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1987_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>At the start Berger immediately imposed his authority by building a cushion. Prost, in his McLaren, perhaps the only driver capable of challenging Berger for the victory, suffered a puncture on the first lap and, therefore, was out of contention. Prost, however, drove a superb race to climb up through the field finishing just outside the points with the consolation of having the fastest lap. Boutsen's Benetton ran second early on but could not live with the pace set by Berger, ultimately fading to fifth. Piquet spent much of the race behind Senna's Lotus but was unable to find a way past his countryman. The new world champion eventually retired in the pits with oil pouring from the rear of his Williams. At one stage <a href="/wiki/Stefan_Johansson" title="Stefan Johansson">Stefan Johansson</a> in the McLaren closed on Berger, but the Austrian driver responded and eventually romped to a seemingly effortless victory, the first Ferrari's victory since the <a href="/wiki/1985_German_Grand_Prix" title="1985 German Grand Prix">1985 German Grand Prix</a>. Ayrton Senna dramatically passed Johansson on the last lap to take second place. <a href="/wiki/Michele_Alboreto" title="Michele Alboreto">Michele Alboreto</a>, in the second Ferrari, got away very slowly at the green lights leaving him towards the rear of the field. However, the Italian drove an aggressive race to climb his way back up the order to finish an excellent fourth despite suffering from a dragging undertray causing a huge amount of sparks. Boutsen and Nakajima rounded out the points.
</p><p>Johansson's third place was the 54th and last podium finish for the <a href="/wiki/Porsche" title="Porsche">Porsche</a>-designed TAG turbo <a href="/wiki/V6_engine" title="V6 engine">V6 engine</a> which had been first used in Formula One by McLaren at the <a href="/wiki/1983_Dutch_Grand_Prix" title="1983 Dutch Grand Prix">1983 Dutch Grand Prix</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1987_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>This was the first Drivers' Championship win for a <a href="/wiki/Honda_in_Formula_One" title="Honda in Formula One">Honda</a> powered car.</li>
<li>60th podium finish for Honda.</li>
<li>150th Grand Prix start for <a href="/wiki/Arrows_Grand_Prix_International" title="Arrows Grand Prix International">Arrows</a></li>
<li>214th and final Grand Prix start for <a href="/wiki/Alfa_Romeo_in_Formula_One" title="Alfa Romeo in Formula One">Alfa Romeo</a> until the name returned with the rebranded Sauber team in 2019. Although the <a href="/wiki/Osella" title="Osella">Osella</a> team would continue to use the Alfa Romeo 890T <a href="/wiki/V8_engine" title="V8 engine">V8 engine</a> in 1988, <a href="/wiki/Alfa_Romeo" title="Alfa Romeo">Alfa Romeo</a> pulled its support from the team and the engine was badged as the "Osella V8" in 1988.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
