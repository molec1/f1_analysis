<p>The <b>1997 Japanese Grand Prix</b> (officially known as the <b>XXIII Fuji Television Japanese Grand Prix</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 12 October 1997 at the <a href="/wiki/Suzuka_Circuit" class="mw-redirect" title="Suzuka Circuit">Suzuka Circuit</a>, <a href="/wiki/Suzuka,_Mie" title="Suzuka, Mie">Suzuka</a>. It was the 16th and penultimate race of the <a href="/wiki/1997_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="1997 Formula One season">1997 Formula One season</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pg209_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg209-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The 53-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> team after starting from second position. <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a> finished second in a <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a> third in the other Ferrari.<sup id="cite_ref-formula1.com_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-formula1.com-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Irvine led much of the race before moving over to assist Schumacher's championship battle by blocking <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a> leader <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a>.
</p><p>Villeneuve started on <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> in a Williams car. Before the race, it emerged that Villeneuve had been put to the back of the grid, for having ignored <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_regulations#Flags" title="Formula One regulations">waved yellow flags</a> on two consecutive laps during a practice session for the race. Williams appealed and Villeneuve started from the pole. He drove a conservative race to finish 5th, gaining two points. After the race, Williams withdrew their appeal, meaning he lost the two points he originally earned.<sup id="cite_ref-villenenuve-grandprix_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-villenenuve-grandprix-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-villenenuve-grandprix2_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-villenenuve-grandprix2-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher's win put him in front of Villeneuve in the championship on 78 points, with Villeneuve on 77 points.<sup id="cite_ref-pg209_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg209-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> However, as a result of Frentzen finishing second, Williams clinched the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a> as Ferrari could not pass their points total with only one race remaining.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix-japan_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-japan-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>. This race was the last for <a href="/wiki/Gianni_Morbidelli" title="Gianni Morbidelli">Gianni Morbidelli</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1997_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice_and_qualifying">Practice and qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1997_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Practice and qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>For each race in the <a href="/wiki/1997_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="1997 Formula One season">1997 Formula One season</a> there were four practice sessions; two sessions on Friday and two sessions on Saturday morning. The practice sessions on Friday lasted an hour and the practice sessions on Saturday lasted 45&#160;minutes.<sup id="cite_ref-pg220_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg220-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatleft" style="width:30%;&#32;;"><style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r887775652">.mw-parser-output .quotebox{background-color:#F9F9F9;border:1px solid #aaa;box-sizing:border-box;padding:10px;font-size:88%;max-width:100%}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft{margin:0.5em 1.4em 0.8em 0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright{margin:0.5em 0 0.8em 1.4em}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.centered{margin:0.5em auto 0.8em auto}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft p,.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright p{font-style:inherit}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-title{background-color:#F9F9F9;text-align:center;font-size:larger;font-weight:bold}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:before{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" “ ";vertical-align:-45%;line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:after{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" ” ";line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .left-aligned{text-align:left}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .right-aligned{text-align:right}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .center-aligned{text-align:center}.mw-parser-output .quotebox cite{display:block;font-style:normal}@media screen and (max-width:360px){.mw-parser-output .quotebox{min-width:100%;margin:0 0 0.8em!important;float:none!important}}</style>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">"I clearly saw the yellow flag. If they had been waved in a corner, I would have slowed down. But on a straight it was not necessary."</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style=""><a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a>, commenting on not slowing down under a yellow flag zone during the first Saturday practice session.<sup id="cite_ref-pg204_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg204-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>In the first practice session on Saturday morning, an incident occurred 30 minutes into the session. <a href="/wiki/Jos_Verstappen" title="Jos Verstappen">Jos Verstappen</a> in a <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_Racing" title="Tyrrell Racing">Tyrrell</a> car pulled over to the side of the track with a fuel pick-up problem. The <a href="/wiki/Track_marshal" class="mw-redirect" title="Track marshal">track marshals</a> as a result <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_regulations#Flags" title="Formula One regulations">waved yellow flags</a> meaning that drivers should slow down at that part of the track. Despite the yellow flags, nine drivers, including <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a>, never slowed down. Villeneuve in the process, set his fastest time of the session on that lap.<sup id="cite_ref-pg204_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg204-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Villeneuve set <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> with a time of 1:36.071, half a tenth faster than Schumacher, who was second in the Ferrari setting a time of 1:36.133. Schumacher's team-mate, <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a>, qualified third, four tenths behind Villeneuve. <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> rounded out the top four, only three thousands of a second behind Irvine. The <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a> drivers were fifth and seventh; <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> ahead of <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a>. <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a> in a Williams split the two in sixth, six tenths behind Villeneuve.<sup id="cite_ref-pg204_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg204-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
