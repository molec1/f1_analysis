<p>The <b>1952 British Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_Two" title="Formula Two">Formula Two</a> race held on 19 July 1952 at <a href="/wiki/Silverstone_Circuit" title="Silverstone Circuit">Silverstone Circuit</a>. It was race 5 of 8 in the <a href="/wiki/1952_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1952 World Championship of Drivers">1952 World Championship of Drivers</a>, in which each Grand Prix was run to Formula Two rules rather than the Formula One regulations normally used.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Silverstone_1952_5.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e6/Silverstone_1952_5.jpg/220px-Silverstone_1952_5.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="95" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e6/Silverstone_1952_5.jpg/330px-Silverstone_1952_5.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e6/Silverstone_1952_5.jpg/440px-Silverstone_1952_5.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1913" data-file-height="823" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Silverstone_1952_5.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Photo from Grandstand</div></div></div>
<p>New pit facilities had been built on the straight between Woodcote and Copse corners; the original pits were located between Abbey and Woodcote.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1952_British_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Jean_Behra" title="Jean Behra">Jean Behra</a> was unable to take part in the British Grand Prix, having broken his shoulder blade at the non-championship Grand Prix de Sables d'Olonne the previous weekend. Consequently, <a href="/wiki/Maurice_Trintignant" title="Maurice Trintignant">Maurice Trintignant</a> took over Behra's <a href="/wiki/Gordini" title="Gordini">Gordini</a> T16 for Silverstone, having driven a <a href="/wiki/Simca-Gordini" class="mw-redirect" title="Simca-Gordini">Simca-Gordini</a> T15 at <a href="/wiki/1952_French_Grand_Prix" title="1952 French Grand Prix">Rouen-Les-Essarts</a>. The Gordini team also fielded regular drivers <a href="/wiki/Robert_Manzon" title="Robert Manzon">Robert Manzon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Birabongse_Bhanudej" title="Birabongse Bhanudej">Prince Bira</a>. As in the previous race, Belgian driver <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Claes" title="Johnny Claes">Johnny Claes</a> entered a privateer Simca-Gordini under the 'Ecurie Belge' moniker. <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> stuck with the same three drivers — <a href="/wiki/Alberto_Ascari" title="Alberto Ascari">Alberto Ascari</a>, <a href="/wiki/Giuseppe_Farina" title="Giuseppe Farina">Nino Farina</a> and <a href="/wiki/Piero_Taruffi" title="Piero Taruffi">Piero Taruffi</a> — who had monopolised the podium positions at the French Grand Prix. There were also a number of privateer Ferrari entrants: <a href="/wiki/Rudi_Fischer" title="Rudi Fischer">Fischer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Peter_Hirt" title="Peter Hirt">Hirt</a> for Ecurie Espadon, <a href="/wiki/Peter_Whitehead_(racing_driver)" title="Peter Whitehead (racing driver)">Peter Whitehead</a> and <a href="/wiki/Roy_Salvadori" title="Roy Salvadori">Roy Salvadori</a>. <a href="/wiki/Hersham_and_Walton_Motors" title="Hersham and Walton Motors">HWM</a> continued their policy of partnering regulars <a href="/wiki/Peter_Collins_(racing_driver)" title="Peter Collins (racing driver)">Peter Collins</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lance_Macklin" title="Lance Macklin">Lance Macklin</a> with a local driver, in this case <a href="/wiki/Duncan_Hamilton_(racing_driver)" title="Duncan Hamilton (racing driver)">Duncan Hamilton</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Connaught_Engineering" title="Connaught Engineering">Connaught</a> team ran a quartet of <a href="/wiki/Lea_Francis" class="mw-redirect" title="Lea Francis">Lea Francis</a>-engined entries — <a href="/wiki/Kenneth_McAlpine" title="Kenneth McAlpine">McAlpine</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ken_Downing" title="Ken Downing">Downing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Eric_Thompson_(racing_driver)" title="Eric Thompson (racing driver)">Thompson</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dennis_Poore" title="Dennis Poore">Poore</a> — while the remainder of the grid was made up of a series of privateers of various constructors, including <a href="/wiki/Cooper_Car_Company" title="Cooper Car Company">Coopers</a> and <a href="/wiki/Maserati_in_motorsport" title="Maserati in motorsport">Maseratis</a>.
</p><p>The three works Ferraris, led on this occasion by Farina, again qualified in the top three positions on the grid, this time being joined on the four-car front row by Manzon. The second row consisted of Downing alongside <a href="/wiki/Reg_Parnell" title="Reg Parnell">Reg Parnell</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mike_Hawthorn" title="Mike Hawthorn">Mike Hawthorn</a> in a pair of Cooper-<a href="/wiki/Bristol_Cars" title="Bristol Cars">Bristols</a>. The Connaughts of Poore and Thompson shared row three with Bira's Gordini and Hamilton in his HWM.
</p><p>Ascari took the lead at the start of the race and held onto it for the whole 85 laps, taking his third consecutive victory in the World Championship. Polesitter Nino Farina was in second place for the first 26 laps but he dropped down the field when he needed to pit to change spark plugs, eventually finishing in sixth, just outside the points. Despite making a bad start that saw him drop to ninth by the end of the first lap, fellow Ferrari driver Taruffi recovered to take second place, finishing a lap behind Ascari. Dennis Poore, who had been running in third after Farina's pit stop, needed to make a stop of his own in order to refuel his car. This allowed Hawthorn to inherit third place, which he held for the remainder of the race. He finished a lap behind Taruffi and took his first World Championship podium in just his third race. Poore took fourth, ahead of Connaught teammate Eric Thompson in the fifth and final points position.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Ascari's win, coupled with yet another fastest lap, allowed him to extend his lead in the Drivers' Championship once again. He now enjoyed an eight-point lead over fellow Ferrari driver Taruffi. Farina, having not scored any points, was seven points adrift of Taruffi.
</p>
