<p>The <b>1991 Italian Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>Coca-Cola 62<sup>o</sup> Gran Premio d'Italia</b><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at <a href="/wiki/Autodromo_Nazionale_di_Monza" title="Autodromo Nazionale di Monza">Monza</a> on 8 September 1991. It was the twelfth race of the <a href="/wiki/1991_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1991 Formula One World Championship">1991 Formula One World Championship</a>.
</p><p>The 53-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW14" title="Williams FW14">Williams</a>-<a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>, after he started from second position. <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> finished second in a <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/6" title="McLaren MP4/6">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Honda_in_Formula_One" title="Honda in Formula One">Honda</a>, having started from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> third in a <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_643" title="Ferrari 643">Ferrari</a>. This was the last time Senna, Prost, Piquet and Mansell all finished a race in the top six.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-race">Pre-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1991_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Pre-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The big news between the Belgian and Italian Grands Prix revolved around young <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>, who had made his debut for <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a> in <a href="/wiki/1991_Belgian_Grand_Prix" title="1991 Belgian Grand Prix">Belgium</a>. Schumacher had signed for <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a> while still being under contract to Jordan. After much legal wrangling the German was confirmed at Benetton, while <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Moreno" title="Roberto Moreno">Roberto Moreno</a> went the other way, taking Schumacher's place at Jordan. Elsewhere <a href="/wiki/Michael_Bartels" title="Michael Bartels">Michael Bartels</a> was back at <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Lotus</a>, as <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Herbert" title="Johnny Herbert">Johnny Herbert</a> had more commitments in <a href="/wiki/Formula_Nippon" class="mw-redirect" title="Formula Nippon">Japanese Formula 3000</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1991_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-qualifying_report">Pre-qualifying report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1991_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Pre-qualifying report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>It was a third 1–2 in a row for <a href="/wiki/Brabham" title="Brabham">Brabham</a> in the Friday morning pre-qualifying session, with <a href="/wiki/Mark_Blundell" title="Mark Blundell">Mark Blundell</a> back on top of the time sheets, eight tenths of a second faster than <a href="/wiki/Martin_Brundle" title="Martin Brundle">Martin Brundle</a>. <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Grouillard" title="Olivier Grouillard">Olivier Grouillard</a> was again third fastest for <a href="/wiki/Fondmetal" title="Fondmetal">Fondmetal</a>, his fifth pre-qualifying success of the season. <a href="/wiki/Michele_Alboreto" title="Michele Alboreto">Michele Alboreto</a> took the last pre-qualification position in the <a href="/wiki/Footwork_Arrows" title="Footwork Arrows">Footwork</a>, 1.5 seconds off Blundell's pace.
</p><p>In his best pre-qualifying performance in his four attempts so far, <a href="/wiki/Fabrizio_Barbazza" title="Fabrizio Barbazza">Fabrizio Barbazza</a> just missed out in fifth place in the <a href="/wiki/Automobiles_Gonfaronnaises_Sportives" title="Automobiles Gonfaronnaises Sportives">AGS</a>, albeit 1.6 seconds slower than Alboreto. His team-mate <a href="/wiki/Gabriele_Tarquini" title="Gabriele Tarquini">Gabriele Tarquini</a> debuted the new <a href="/w/index.php?title=AGS_JH27&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="AGS JH27 (page does not exist)">JH27</a> car, but its engine failed on the first lap. Tarquini reverted to the <a href="/w/index.php?title=AGS_JH25&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="AGS JH25 (page does not exist)">JH25B</a>, but could only manage sixth fastest. <a href="/wiki/Alex_Caffi" title="Alex Caffi">Alex Caffi</a> was a couple of tenths of a second further back in seventh in the other Footwork, while <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Chaves" title="Pedro Chaves">Pedro Chaves</a> remained stuck in the pits after the worn engine in his <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Coloni" title="Scuderia Coloni">Coloni</a> refused to start.<sup id="cite_ref-Walker_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Walker-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying_report">Qualifying report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1991_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Qualifying report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In qualifying, <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> took pole again, with title rival <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> second, the Englishman complaining of traffic on his final two hot laps. <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> was third, followed by <a href="/wiki/Riccardo_Patrese" title="Riccardo Patrese">Riccardo Patrese</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a>, Schumacher, <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a>, in his 200th Grand Prix, Moreno, and <a href="/wiki/Pierluigi_Martini" title="Pierluigi Martini">Pierluigi Martini</a>, in the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> powered <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1991_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_report">Race report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1991_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Race report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the start Senna got away well from Mansell, Berger, Patrese, and Alesi, with everyone making it through the Rettifilo double chicane. Moreno was an early casualty as he spun off on lap 2 with the Jordan's suspension breaking just before the spin also just behind the leaders.
</p><p>Patrese at the front started to charge, first he disposed of Berger, and then Mansell. Martini had spun off in the Minardi by lap 8 at Roggia behind the leaders. On lap 26 Patrese passed Senna at the Ascari chicane, but on the following lap a gearbox failure took him out. Senna now led from Mansell and Berger, with Mansell pressuring Senna for the lead, and on lap 34 he took it with a perfectly timed out-braking manoeuvre going into the Ascari chicane. Senna proceeded to pit for tyres and emerged down in fifth place, but in no mood to stay there. Senna proceeded to pass Schumacher going into Ascari, and then slipstreamed passed Berger into the first corner, and got back to second by passing arch-rival Prost going into the second chicane. Mansell meanwhile cruised to victory from Senna, Prost, Berger (despite pulling off just after the start/finish line with electrical problems), Schumacher, and Piquet, Alesi having retired with a blown engine. With four races to go Senna still led Mansell by 18 points, but this race confirmed that Mansell and Williams could beat Senna's McLaren in a straight fight.
</p>
