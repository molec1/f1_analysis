<p>The <b>2004 German Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at <a href="/wiki/Hockenheimring" title="Hockenheimring">Hockenheim</a> on 25 July 2004. It was the twelfth race of the <a href="/wiki/2004_FIA_Formula_One_World_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="2004 FIA Formula One World Championship">2004 FIA Formula One World Championship</a>.
</p><p>The 66-lap race was won by local driver <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_F2004" title="Ferrari F2004">Ferrari</a>. Schumacher took his eleventh victory of the season, equalling his record from <a href="/wiki/2002_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2002 Formula One World Championship">2002</a>, after starting from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>. Englishman <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> finished second in a <a href="/wiki/BAR_006" title="BAR 006">BAR</a>-<a href="/wiki/Honda_in_Formula_One" title="Honda in Formula One">Honda</a> despite a ten-place grid penalty for an engine change in practice, and a loose helmet strap during the race, with Spaniard <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> third in a <a href="/wiki/Renault_R24" title="Renault R24">Renault</a>. <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> of McLaren-Mercedes set the fastest lap of the race but retired on lap 14 after his rear wing gave way at Turn 1, sending his car into the barriers at high speed.
</p><p>This was the final Grand Prix for Brazilian driver <a href="/wiki/Cristiano_da_Matta" title="Cristiano da Matta">Cristiano da Matta</a>, and the final time Williams used the "Walrus" front wing.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2004_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>The first start was aborted after <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Panis" title="Olivier Panis">Olivier Panis</a> indicated a problem with his <a href="/wiki/Toyota_Racing_(Formula_One_team)" title="Toyota Racing (Formula One team)">Toyota</a>. This led to a second formation lap, and the shortening of the race by one lap.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a>'s rear wing failed during the race, leading to a massive accident.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Marc_Gen%C3%A9" title="Marc Gené">Marc Gené</a> was replaced at Williams by <a href="/wiki/Ant%C3%B4nio_Pizzonia" title="Antônio Pizzonia">Antônio Pizzonia</a>, returning to Formula One after being sacked by <a href="/wiki/Jaguar_Racing" title="Jaguar Racing">Jaguar</a> following the <a href="/wiki/2003_British_Grand_Prix" title="2003 British Grand Prix">2003 British Grand Prix</a>. Pizzonia scored his first points by finishing seventh.</li></ul>
