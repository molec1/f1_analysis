<p>The <b>1981 Monaco Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held at <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Monaco" title="Circuit de Monaco">Monaco</a> on 31 May 1981.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1981_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The start of the race was delayed after a fire in the Loews Hotel kitchens, which necessitated the fire service pumping large quantities of water to put the fires out. Water then seeped through the floors of the hotel and into the tunnel, delaying the race for an hour. <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a> led for much of the race, but crashed out late on. New race leader Alan Jones then suffered a fuel feed problem in the latter stages of the race, allowing <a href="/wiki/Gilles_Villeneuve" title="Gilles Villeneuve">Gilles Villeneuve</a> in his <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, to take his first victory since 1979 as well as becoming the first, and to this day, the only Canadian to win in Monaco as well as the only driver from the whole of North America to win in Monaco. Championship leader <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Reutemann" title="Carlos Reutemann">Carlos Reutemann</a> retired with gearbox problems, breaking a 15-race streak of consistently finishing in the points.
</p>
