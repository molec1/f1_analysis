<p>The <b>1977 Monaco Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held at <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Monaco" title="Circuit de Monaco">Monaco</a> on 22 May 1977. It was the sixth race of the 1977 World Championship of F1 Drivers and the 1977 International Cup for F1 Constructors.
</p><p>The 76-lap race was won by South African driver <a href="/wiki/Jody_Scheckter" title="Jody Scheckter">Jody Scheckter</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/Wolf_WR1" title="Wolf WR1">Wolf</a>-<a href="/wiki/Ford_Motor_Company" title="Ford Motor Company">Ford</a>. It was Scheckter's second victory of the season, and the 100th World Championship race victory for the Ford-backed <a href="/wiki/Cosworth_DFV" title="Cosworth DFV">Cosworth DFV</a> engine.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Austrian <a href="/wiki/Niki_Lauda" title="Niki Lauda">Niki Lauda</a> finished second in a <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_312T" title="Ferrari 312T">Ferrari</a>, with Argentinian teammate <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Reutemann" title="Carlos Reutemann">Carlos Reutemann</a> third.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1977_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/John_Watson_(racing_driver)" title="John Watson (racing driver)">John Watson</a> took his first career pole for <a href="/wiki/Brabham" title="Brabham">Brabham</a> with <a href="/wiki/Jody_Scheckter" title="Jody Scheckter">Jody Scheckter</a> also on the front row and <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Reutemann" title="Carlos Reutemann">Carlos Reutemann</a> third. It was Scheckter who got the best start to beat Watson to the first corner, with Reutemann running third in the early stages until he was passed by his teammate <a href="/wiki/Niki_Lauda" title="Niki Lauda">Niki Lauda</a>. Watson ran second to Scheckter until mid-race when he had to retire with gearbox trouble, allowing Lauda to close in on Scheckter but the latter was flawless and held on to take his second win of the season. Lauda had to be satisfied with second, with Reutemann completing the podium.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Shadow_Racing_Cars" title="Shadow Racing Cars">Shadow</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Riccardo_Patrese" title="Riccardo Patrese">Riccardo Patrese</a> made his first career start in the race, qualifying 15th and finishing 9th. Patrese would drive in a record 257 Grands Prix (256 starts) during his career which ended after the <a href="/wiki/1993_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1993 Formula One World Championship">1993</a> season, a career which included winning at <a href="/wiki/1982_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="1982 Monaco Grand Prix">Monaco</a> for <a href="/wiki/Brabham" title="Brabham">Brabham</a> in <a href="/wiki/1982_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1982 Formula One World Championship">1982</a>.
</p>
