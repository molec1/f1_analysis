<p>The <b>2020 British Grand Prix</b> (officially known as the <b>Formula 1 Pirelli British Grand Prix 2020</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_racing" title="Formula One racing">Formula One motor race</a> held on 2 August 2020 at the Silverstone Circuit in <a href="/wiki/Silverstone" title="Silverstone">Silverstone</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>. It was the fourth round of the <a href="/wiki/2020_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2020 Formula One World Championship">2020</a> Formula One World Championship.<sup id="cite_ref-auto_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-3">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> was the defending race winner from the <a href="/wiki/2019_British_Grand_Prix" title="2019 British Grand Prix">2019 event</a> who was aiming for his seventh British Grand Prix win.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> It was the seventy-first running of the British Grand Prix as part of the World Championship. The race was the first of two consecutive Formula One races at Silverstone with the <a href="/wiki/70th_Anniversary_Grand_Prix" title="70th Anniversary Grand Prix">70th Anniversary Grand Prix</a> due to follow a week later.<sup id="cite_ref-auto_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-3">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton won the Grand Prix, his third successive win of the 2020 season, and <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a>' fourth.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2020_British_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Impact_of_the_COVID-19_pandemic">Impact of the COVID-19 pandemic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2020_British_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Impact of the COVID-19 pandemic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/2020_Formula_One_World_Championship#Impact_of_the_COVID-19_pandemic" title="2020 Formula One World Championship">2020 Formula One World Championship §&#160;Impact of the COVID-19 pandemic</a>, and <a href="/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="COVID-19 pandemic in the United Kingdom">COVID-19 pandemic in the United Kingdom</a></div>
<p>The opening rounds of the <a href="/wiki/2020_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2020 Formula One World Championship">2020</a> championship were heavily affected by the <a href="/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic" title="COVID-19 pandemic">COVID-19 pandemic</a>. Several Grands Prix were cancelled or postponed after the aborted <a href="/wiki/2020_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="2020 Australian Grand Prix">opening round</a> in Australia, prompting the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">FIA</a> to draft a new calendar. The British Grand Prix was originally intended to be held on 19 July.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> When the calendar was redrafted following the pandemic the <a href="/wiki/2020_Hungarian_Grand_Prix" title="2020 Hungarian Grand Prix">Hungarian Grand Prix</a> and British Grand Prix had swapped dates with Hungary moving to 19 July and Silverstone taking the 2 August date, the provisional date of the Hungarian race.<sup id="cite_ref-auto_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-3">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Although there had been doubts over whether Silverstone would be able to host Formula One races due to quarantine measures proposed by the British government, hopes were raised again by <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Prime Minister of the United Kingdom">Prime Minister</a> <a href="/wiki/Boris_Johnson" title="Boris Johnson">Boris Johnson</a>, who, in a statement, said that Formula One should be given an exemption.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In the end, the British Government did give approval for Formula One to be held at Silverstone.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The addition to the calendar of the <a href="/wiki/70th_Anniversary_Grand_Prix" title="70th Anniversary Grand Prix">70th Anniversary Grand Prix</a> on 9 August, also at Silverstone, will mark the first time in twenty-seven years the United Kingdom will host two Formula One races in the same season. The last season to have two British races in a season prior to this was the <a href="/wiki/1993_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1993 Formula One World Championship">1993 season</a>, when <a href="/wiki/Donington_Park" title="Donington Park">Donington Park</a> hosted the <a href="/wiki/1993_European_Grand_Prix" title="1993 European Grand Prix">European Grand Prix</a> and Silverstone hosted the <a href="/wiki/1993_British_Grand_Prix" title="1993 British Grand Prix">British Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> It was later revealed that Silverstone offered to hold as many as 12 races in total during the season.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Both the British Grand Prix and the <a href="/wiki/70th_Anniversary_Grand_Prix" title="70th Anniversary Grand Prix">70th Anniversary Grand Prix</a> will be held behind closed doors.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Northamptonshire_Police" title="Northamptonshire Police">Northamptonshire Police</a> reiterated the importance of fans staying away from Silverstone for public health reasons during the two Grand Prix weekends, and warned that strict measures would be in place to stop unauthorised persons from entering the circuit.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Championship_standings_before_the_race">Championship standings before the race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2020_British_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Championship standings before the race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After the third round at the <a href="/wiki/2020_Hungarian_Grand_Prix" title="2020 Hungarian Grand Prix">2020 Hungarian Grand Prix</a>, defending champion <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>, on 63 points, leads the championship by five points over his teammate <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a> a further 25 points behind. Defending Constructors' Champions <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a>, with 121 points, lead the championship from <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a>, who have 55. <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> sit 14 points behind Red Bull in third, and are only one point ahead of fourth-placed <a href="/wiki/Racing_Point_F1_Team" title="Racing Point F1 Team">Racing Point</a>, who have 40 points, while <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> sit 5th on 27 points. <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> are the only team without a point, heading into the race.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Entrants">Entrants</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2020_British_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Entrants">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Further information: <a href="/wiki/2020_Formula_One_World_Championship#Entries" title="2020 Formula One World Championship">2020 Formula One World Championship §&#160;Entries</a></div>
<p>The initial driver and team race entry list was identical to the season entry list with no additional stand-in drivers for practice or the race.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> However, this changed following a positive <a href="/wiki/Severe_acute_respiratory_syndrome_coronavirus_2" title="Severe acute respiratory syndrome coronavirus 2">SARS-2 coronavirus</a> test (which  indicates  the contraction of <a href="/wiki/Coronavirus_disease_2019" title="Coronavirus disease 2019">COVID-19</a>) for <a href="/wiki/Racing_Point_F1_Team" title="Racing Point F1 Team">Racing Point</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a> who was ruled out of the Grand Prix.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> He was replaced by <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tyre_choices">Tyre choices</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2020_British_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Tyre choices">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Pirelli brought the C1, C2, C3 compound tyres for use in the race. However, the <a href="/wiki/70th_Anniversary_Grand_Prix" title="70th Anniversary Grand Prix">70th Anniversary Grand Prix</a>, which will also be held at Silverstone next weekend, will by contrast see the C2, C3 and C4 selection used. This aims to not only create strategic differences between the two races, but also to force the teams to fully make use of practice time on the second weekend, instead of relying on data from the previous week. The idea was instituted after the Mercedes team rejected an alternate plan, for a reverse starting grid to be run on the second weekend.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice">Practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2020_British_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a> set the fastest time of the first practice session on Friday, followed by the <a href="/wiki/Mercedes_F1_Team" class="mw-redirect" title="Mercedes F1 Team">Mercedes</a> of <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Racing_Point" class="mw-redirect" title="Racing Point">Racing Point</a> of <a href="/wiki/Lance_Stroll" title="Lance Stroll">Lance Stroll</a>. <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a>, in his first practice session on his return to the sport, set the ninth fastest time.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> During the session, <a href="/wiki/Alfa_Romeo_in_Formula_One" title="Alfa Romeo in Formula One">Alfa Romeo</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Antonio_Giovinazzi" title="Antonio Giovinazzi">Antonio Giovinazzi</a> spun at turn 13 and scattered gravel and tyre debris onto the track, causing the session to be <a href="/wiki/Racing_flags#Red_flag" title="Racing flags">red-flagged</a> for 12 minutes whilst the track was cleared.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> He was investigated and later issued a warning by the race stewards for failing to safely return to the pit lane.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> was unable to complete any timed laps after his <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> suffered an <a href="/wiki/Intercooler" title="Intercooler">intercooler</a> issue early in the session.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Stroll set the fastest time of the second Friday practice session, followed by the Red Bull of <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Albon" title="Alexander Albon">Alexander Albon</a> and the Mercedes of <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a>. This session was also red-flagged after Albon spun at turn 15 and collided with the barriers. He was taken to the circuit's medical centre but was later declared fit. Vettel was again forced to miss practice time when a pedal issue restricted his running.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Bottas set the fastest time of Saturday morning practice, followed by Hamilton and Verstappen. Albon was forced to miss most of the session with electrical issues, and Vettel continued to suffer brake pedal problems which curtailed his running on Friday.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2020_British_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Q1_and_Q2">Q1 and Q2</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2020_British_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Q1 and Q2">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Although there was a 60% chance of rain before qualifying, all three parts would prove to be dry. In Q1 (the first part of qualifying), <a href="/wiki/Nicholas_Latifi" title="Nicholas Latifi">Nicholas Latifi</a> spun on his last lap, exiting the slow turn 7 (Luffield); his earlier time was only good enough for last place. His <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> teammate <a href="/wiki/George_Russell_(racing_driver)" title="George Russell (racing driver)">George Russell</a> was investigated by the stewards for not slowing down under the consequent yellow flags - he received a five-place grid penalty. In the end, the five drivers eliminated in Q1 were <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Magnussen" title="Kevin Magnussen">Kevin Magnussen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Antonio_Giovinazzi" title="Antonio Giovinazzi">Antonio Giovinazzi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Romain_Grosjean" title="Romain Grosjean">Romain Grosjean</a>, and Latifi - as both Giovinazzi and Raikkonen failed to make it out of Q1, <a href="/wiki/Alfa_Romeo_in_Formula_One" title="Alfa Romeo in Formula One">Alfa Romeo</a> remained the only team to not reach Q2 (the second part of qualifying) in <a href="/wiki/2020_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2020 Formula One World Championship">2020</a>.
</p><p>Two different strategies were used in Q2, with both the <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bulls</a>, <a href="/wiki/Racing_Point_F1_Team" title="Racing Point F1 Team">Racing Points</a> and one of the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferraris</a> – that of <a href="/wiki/Charles_Leclerc" title="Charles Leclerc">Charles Leclerc</a> – trying to get into Q3 (the third part of qualifying) on the more race-suitable medium compound tyre, and the other teams using the soft compound due to the top ten qualifiers having to start on the tyre used in Q2. Early on in the session, world championship leader <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> spun at turn 7; red flags were soon waved, since Hamilton had brought gravel back onto the track with him. After the session resumed, Hamilton and his Mercedes teammate <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a> set times a second clear of the rest of the field. Not all those using the medium tyres succeeded in reaching Q3 - <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Albon" title="Alexander Albon">Alexander Albon</a> of Red Bull and Racing Point's replacement driver <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a> both failed to do so. <a href="/wiki/Lance_Stroll" title="Lance Stroll">Lance Stroll</a> in 10th and <a href="/wiki/Pierre_Gasly" title="Pierre Gasly">Pierre Gasly</a> in 11th set the same time, but since Stroll set it first, the latter in his <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_AlphaTauri" title="Scuderia AlphaTauri">AlphaTauri</a> was consigned to going out in Q2. At the end of the session, the drivers eliminated were Gasly, Albon, Hulkenberg, <a href="/wiki/Daniil_Kvyat" title="Daniil Kvyat">Daniil Kvyat</a>, and Russell.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Q3">Q3</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2020_British_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Q3">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As the cars were being sent out, there was an incident in the pitlane between Leclerc, Stroll, and <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a>, with Stroll complaining that Ferrari had released their driver in an unsafe manner - the stewards, after investigating the matter, decided no further action was necessary. In the first set of laps, Hamilton broke the lap record with a 1:24.616, with his teammate 0.15 seconds adrift. Hamilton then broke his own lap record a few minutes later, recording a 1:24:303 to secure pole position, and Bottas achieved second place, 0.3 seconds back. <a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a>, one of the last to cross the line, was third fastest, over a second behind pole position. The rest of the top 10 were: Leclerc, <a href="/wiki/Lando_Norris" title="Lando Norris">Lando Norris</a>, Stroll, <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Sainz_Jr." title="Carlos Sainz Jr.">Carlos Sainz Jr.</a>, Ricciardo, <a href="/wiki/Esteban_Ocon" title="Esteban Ocon">Esteban Ocon</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a>.
</p><p>The pole position was Hamilton's 91st overall, and his seventh around the Silverstone circuit.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2020_British_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-race_ceremonies">Pre-race ceremonies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2020_British_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Pre-race ceremonies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A 'fight against racism' ceremony was held on the track before the race. All 20 of the drivers were out in formation directly in front of the grid, all displaying anti-racism messages on their t-shirts, and with some <a href="/wiki/Taking_the_knee" class="mw-redirect" title="Taking the knee">taking the knee</a> and others standing, as video messages of their support for the anti-racism message were played.<sup id="cite_ref-ss_ceremonies_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ss_ceremonies-29">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Following the singing of the national anthem ("<a href="/wiki/God_Save_The_Queen" class="mw-redirect" title="God Save The Queen">God Save The Queen</a>"), there was a flyover by a <a href="/wiki/Spitfire" class="mw-redirect" title="Spitfire">Spitfire</a> with the message "Thank you NHS" painted on the underside of its wings, and accompanied by applause from those present, in recognition of the work done by the UK's <a href="/wiki/National_Health_Service" title="National Health Service">public health services</a> in the fight against the <a href="/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="COVID-19 pandemic in the United Kingdom">COVID-19 epidemic</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ss_ceremonies_29-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ss_ceremonies-29">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_report">Race report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2020_British_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Race report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Racing_Point_F1_Team" title="Racing Point F1 Team">Racing Point</a> team was unable to start <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a>’s power unit in the garage, forcing him out of the race prior to the formation lap.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> This was determined to have been caused by a clutch bolt having sheared off in the drivetrain and, where the bolt jammed, the engine was prevented from turning over.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_laps">Early laps</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2020_British_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Early laps">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p><a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> led from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> at the race start. On the opening lap, <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Sainz_Jr." title="Carlos Sainz Jr.">Carlos Sainz Jr.</a> and <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a>, starting seventh and eighth respectively, both made up two places by passing <a href="/wiki/Lando_Norris" title="Lando Norris">Lando Norris</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lance_Stroll" title="Lance Stroll">Lance Stroll</a>. 12th-placed <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Magnussen" title="Kevin Magnussen">Kevin Magnussen</a> hit a kerb at the inside of turn 17, running wide. <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Albon" title="Alexander Albon">Alexander Albon</a> then attempted to overtake Magnussen at turn 18, the two cars collided and Magnussen was sent through the gravel and into the barriers. He was uninjured and the <a href="/wiki/Safety_car" title="Safety car">safety car</a> was deployed in order to recover his <a href="/wiki/Haas_F1_Team" title="Haas F1 Team">Haas</a>. Albon was later issued a five-second time penalty after the race stewards deemed him responsible for the collision.
</p><p>Racing resumed on lap six. At the end of the lap, Albon became the first driver to make a pit stop to change from medium to hard-compound <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_tyres" title="Formula One tyres">tyres</a>. On lap 12, 12th-placed <a href="/wiki/Daniil_Kvyat" title="Daniil Kvyat">Daniil Kvyat</a> spun at turn 10 as a result of a right-rear tyre failure and crashed heavily into the barriers on the outside of turn 11.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-32">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Kvyat walked away from the accident and the safety car was deployed whilst his car was recovered. All drivers except Albon and <a href="/wiki/Romain_Grosjean" title="Romain Grosjean">Romain Grosjean</a> made pit stops during the safety car period, allowing Grosjean to jump to fifth place. The order of the top 10 was now Hamilton, <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Charles_Leclerc" title="Charles Leclerc">Charles Leclerc</a>, Grosjean, Sainz, Ricciardo, Norris, Stroll and <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a>, who had passed <a href="/wiki/Esteban_Ocon" title="Esteban Ocon">Esteban Ocon</a> in the pits after the <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a> suffered a slow pit stop.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Mid-race">Mid-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2020_British_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Mid-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The second safety car period ended on lap 19. Shortly after the restart, Vettel fell back behind Ocon, and Norris passed Ricciardo for seventh place. Grosjean was soon overtaken by both <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLarens</a>, and was shown the <a href="/wiki/Racing_flags#Per-bend_black/white_flag" title="Racing flags">black-and-white flag</a> for <a href="/wiki/Unsportsmanlike_conduct" title="Unsportsmanlike conduct">unsportsmanlike conduct</a> after being judged to have defended too aggressively from Sainz. 14th-placed Albon made a second pit stop on lap 30, the first driver to do so, serving his time penalty and changing to soft-compound tyres. <a href="/wiki/Antonio_Giovinazzi" title="Antonio Giovinazzi">Antonio Giovinazzi</a> was issued a five-second time penalty on lap 36 for speeding under safety car conditions. On the same lap, Grosjean fell to ninth place having been passed by Ricciardo and Stroll. He was again investigated for moving too aggressively whilst defending from Ricciardo, but no action was taken. He made his first pit stop at the end of the lap, falling to the back of the field. On lap 38, Vettel dropped out of the points positions after being passed for 10th place by <a href="/wiki/Pierre_Gasly" title="Pierre Gasly">Pierre Gasly</a>. Ocon passed Stroll for eighth place on lap 46.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Closing_laps">Closing laps</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2020_British_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Closing laps">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In the closing laps Bottas, who had been running closely behind teammate and race leader Hamilton for the entire race, began to fall back, complaining of poor visibility due to vibrations. Sainz also reported a similar issue over the radio. <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a>, last of the running cars in 17th place, suffered a front wing failure on lap 48 and returned to the pits for a replacement. On the following lap, Gasly passed Stroll for ninth place and Ricciardo overtook Norris for sixth. With three laps to go, Bottas' front-left tyre <a href="/wiki/Delamination" title="Delamination">delaminated</a>. After passing Bottas for second place, Verstappen made use of the gap to Leclerc in third and pitted for fresh tyres to safeguard against suffering a tyre failure himself and in an attempt to claim the <a href="/wiki/Fastest_lap" title="Fastest lap">fastest lap</a> of the race. Bottas slowly completed his lap to return to the pits, changed tyres and emerged in 12th place.
</p><p>With two laps to go, Albon, who had worked his way up from the back of the field and was promoted to 10th place by Bottas' pit stop, passed Stroll for ninth place. At the same time, fourth-placed Sainz suffered a front-left tyre failure. He returned to the pits and eventually fell to 14th place by the finish line. After Bottas' puncture, race leader Hamilton was warned over the radio to preserve his tyres. However this proved to be too late as, at turn eight on the final lap, Hamilton became the third driver to suffer a front-left failure. At this point, Hamilton had gained a lead of over 30 seconds after Verstappen's precautionary pit stop, although this gap rapidly fell as Hamilton slowly completed the lap. 
</p><p>Verstappen could only close the gap to six seconds by the finish line, and Hamilton took the chequered flag to claim his third consecutive victory of the season, 87th career win and his seventh at the British Grand Prix. Verstappen finished second and succeeded in claiming the fastest lap, with Leclerc completing the podium. Ricciardo and Norris finished fourth and fifth, with Ocon claiming his best finish since the <a href="/wiki/2018_Italian_Grand_Prix" title="2018 Italian Grand Prix">2018 Italian Grand Prix</a> in sixth. Gasly, Albon, Stroll and Vettel rounded out the points positions.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2020_British_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>On his lap 12 accident, Kvyat claimed that he was "very, very distract[ed]" as issues with his car required him to change settings on his steering wheel immediately prior to the crash. <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_AlphaTauri" title="Scuderia AlphaTauri">AlphaTauri</a> team principal <a href="/wiki/Franz_Tost" title="Franz Tost">Franz Tost</a> suggested that Kvyat's accident may have been down to a tyre puncture.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_32-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-32">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a> defended their decision to pit Verstappen in the closing laps, raising the possibility that he could have suffered the same tyre failure as the <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> drivers.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>The tyre failures near the end of the race were investigated by <a href="/wiki/Pirelli" title="Pirelli">Pirelli</a> (the tyre supplier) and the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">FIA</a> (the governing body) after the race. The company stated that the tyre failures were generated by two different causes: firstly, the 'extremely long' stints the cars were running on the demanding Silverstone circuit; and secondly, the increased pace of the 2020 cars which maximised the forces on the tyres, with the most stress on the front-left.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a> also protested the legality of the <a href="/wiki/Racing_Point_RP20" title="Racing Point RP20">Racing Point RP20</a>'s brake ducts for a third race in succession.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> The protest was upheld with <a href="/wiki/Racing_Point_F1_Team" title="Racing Point F1 Team">Racing Point</a> being reprimanded but the team and drivers allowed to keep their results.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
