<p>The <b>2006 Monaco Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 28 May 2006 at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Monaco" title="Circuit de Monaco">Circuit de Monaco</a>. The 78-lap race was the seventh round of the <a href="/wiki/2006_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2006 Formula One season">2006 Formula One season</a>. Prior to the race, <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> had finished on the podium in all of the previous six Grands Prix, winning three of those races. His main championship rival, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> was looking to win the race as it would equal <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a>'s record at Monaco for most wins (six).
</p><p>The race was remembered by many people for Michael Schumacher's actions during the closing stages of the qualifying session for the race, where he stopped his car in the <i>Rascasse</i> corner, thus preventing his rival Fernando Alonso improving his time and most likely taking pole off Schumacher. Schumacher maintained that this was an innocent mistake, but his actions were deemed "<i>deliberate</i>" by the race stewards and he was demoted to the back of the grid as punishment for his actions, promoting Alonso from second to pole position.
</p><p>Fernando Alonso went on to win the race, his first in Monaco. <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a> came second and <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> took full advantage of <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a>'s late hydraulic failure to finish in third, scoring <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a>'s first ever podium finish and also first ever podium for Ferrari as an engine supplier for a customer team since <a href="/wiki/2003_United_States_Grand_Prix" title="2003 United States Grand Prix">2003 United States Grand Prix</a> when <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a> was a <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> customer team but badged as <a href="/wiki/Petronas" title="Petronas">Petronas</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2006_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2006_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:CoulthardSuperman.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f2/CoulthardSuperman.jpg/220px-CoulthardSuperman.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="783" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f2/CoulthardSuperman.jpg/330px-CoulthardSuperman.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f2/CoulthardSuperman.jpg/440px-CoulthardSuperman.jpg 2x" data-file-width="587" data-file-height="2090" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:CoulthardSuperman.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a> carried the <a href="/wiki/Superman_logo" title="Superman logo">Superman logo</a> to promote the launch of <i><a href="/wiki/Superman_Returns" title="Superman Returns">Superman Returns</a></i>, and <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> went to the podium wearing the hero's cape.</div></div></div>
<p>During the break between the <a href="/wiki/2006_Spanish_Grand_Prix" title="2006 Spanish Grand Prix">Spanish Grand Prix</a> and the Monaco Grand Prix, a test session was held at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_Paul_Ricard" title="Circuit Paul Ricard">Circuit Paul Ricard</a>. <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> topped the times on the first day of testing, under a tenth of a second faster than the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> of <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> who was second fastest. <a href="/wiki/Toyota_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Toyota F1">Toyota</a> also had a promising day testing their new car, the Toyota TF106B, with <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> coming in third place, who was also less than a tenth of a second behind Alonso. <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> in the second Renault was fourth ahead of both the <a href="/wiki/McLaren_(racing)" class="mw-redirect" title="McLaren (racing)">McLarens</a> of <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Räikkönen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Montoya</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Alonso was top on day two as well, with Kovalainen second in the other Renault. <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a> was third in his <a href="/wiki/BMW_in_Formula_One" title="BMW in Formula One">BMW Sauber</a>, a second slower than the Renaults. <a href="/wiki/Christian_Klien" title="Christian Klien">Klien</a>, Trulli and Massa completed the top six respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Villeneuve got the better of the two Renaults on day three, the <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canadian</a> was over four tenths faster than his nearest rival, Heikki Kovalainen.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Toyota were fastest on day four, <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Panis" title="Olivier Panis">Olivier Panis</a> was fastest ahead of <a href="/wiki/Ricardo_Zonta" title="Ricardo Zonta">Ricardo Zonta</a> and the two McLarens of <a href="/wiki/Pedro_de_la_Rosa" title="Pedro de la Rosa">Pedro de la Rosa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gary_Paffett" title="Gary Paffett">Gary Paffett</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As well as testing in Paul Ricard, some teams, namely <a href="/wiki/Honda_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Honda F1">Honda</a> and <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a>, were testing at an Italian circuit near <a href="/wiki/Vallelunga_circuit" class="mw-redirect" title="Vallelunga circuit">Vallelunga</a>, which had recently had a new chicane added to it to help simulate Monaco's characteristics. Honda's <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Davidson" title="Anthony Davidson">Anthony Davidson</a> was top of the time sheet on day one, with <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Wurz" title="Alexander Wurz">Alexander Wurz</a>'s Williams around 4 tenths behind him.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Honda continued to their advantage over Williams on day two of testing, with <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> topping the times; Davidson was second and <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> was third.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Honda were fastest again on day three, this time <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> was fastest with Jenson Button in second and Mark Webber third.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Williams were left on their own in Vallelunga on day four, Mark Webber and <a href="/wiki/Narain_Karthikeyan" title="Narain Karthikeyan">Narain Karthikeyan</a> driving for Williams. The <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australian</a> was fastest with Karthikeyan around 4 tenths slower than him.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice">Practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2006_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Fernando Alonso showed he had intention of winning his first Monaco Grand Prix as he was fastest in the opening practice session, recording a best lap of 1:16.712, a tenth of a second faster than Honda test driver <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Davidson" title="Anthony Davidson">Anthony Davidson</a>, who recorded a personal best of 1:16.872. Underlining Renault's challenge was third-fastest Giancarlo Fisichella, who was right behind Mondini, with a best time of 1:16.88.<sup id="cite_ref-Practice_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Practice-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Alexander Wurz was fastest in the second practice session, one tenth faster than Mondini and two tenths faster than third fastest, Juan Pablo Montoya.<sup id="cite_ref-Practice_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Practice-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Wurz was very pleased with his performance in practice, "I went through my tyre choice and I have to say the way the circuit is progressing, it is coming to us just as it did at the Nurburgring, which is good. This makes me quite confident and positive that we will have a good weekend" he said.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Anthony Davidson had dislodged a marker stick in the "<i>Swimming Pool</i>" to cause the red flag at the start of the session. After the restart, Davidson got his entry to the "<i>Ste Devote</i>" corner wrong and <a href="/wiki/Understeer" class="mw-redirect" title="Understeer">understeered</a> into the tyre wall, removing the Honda’s front wing and left front wheel.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Alonso was quickest in the Saturday morning session. Michael Schumacher, who had challenged Alonso all through the session, was second, 2 tenths slower than Alonso. Fisichella was third, Coulthard fourth, <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> fifth, and Montoya sixth.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2006_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Conditions were warm and sunny for the qualifying session for the race the following day, perhaps the biggest challenge for the drivers was getting a "clear track", meaning the drivers wanted a qualifying run without any cars in their way as this can seriously compromise a driver's time with the lack of width around the track so there was a possibility of a "big-name" going out before the top ten shootout. <a href="/wiki/Vitantonio_Liuzzi" title="Vitantonio Liuzzi">Vitantonio Liuzzi</a> was the first driver to set a time in the session. By the time everyone, except Michael Schumacher, had set a time, <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> lost control of his <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> at <i>Casino Square</i>; so the <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazilian</a> would start from the back of the grid and the session was red flagged. The session restarted at 1:10pm local time and Schumacher did set a time and was safely through to the second phase. <a href="/wiki/Scott_Speed" title="Scott Speed">Scott Speed</a> had a good chance of qualifying for the second session, but an error at the chicane meant he was eliminated from qualifying. <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> made a last-ditch effort to get out of the elimination zone and did as he moved up to eighth. At the end of the first session <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> was fastest, while <a href="/wiki/Christijan_Albers" title="Christijan Albers">Christijan Albers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tiago_Monteiro" title="Tiago Monteiro">Tiago Monteiro</a>, <a href="/wiki/Takuma_Sato" title="Takuma Sato">Takuma Sato</a>, <a href="/wiki/Franck_Montagny" title="Franck Montagny">Franck Montagny</a>,
Scott Speed and Felipe Massa were eliminated.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> was the first to set a time in the second session before being beaten by the Renault of Fernando Alonso and McLaren's Kimi Räikkönen who would later battle for the fastest time for the remainder of the session. So the <a href="/wiki/2005_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="2005 Monaco Grand Prix">2005</a> pole position man, <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a>, was fastest with Fernando Alonso closely behind. <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> was the surprise addition to the final session in his <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a>. <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a>, <a href="/wiki/Christian_Klien" title="Christian Klien">Christian Klien</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vitantonio_Liuzzi" title="Vitantonio Liuzzi">Vitantonio Liuzzi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> were eliminated.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Michael_Schumacher_-_Ferrari_248_F1_-_Monaco_Grand_Prix.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cf/Michael_Schumacher_-_Ferrari_248_F1_-_Monaco_Grand_Prix.jpg/220px-Michael_Schumacher_-_Ferrari_248_F1_-_Monaco_Grand_Prix.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cf/Michael_Schumacher_-_Ferrari_248_F1_-_Monaco_Grand_Prix.jpg/330px-Michael_Schumacher_-_Ferrari_248_F1_-_Monaco_Grand_Prix.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cf/Michael_Schumacher_-_Ferrari_248_F1_-_Monaco_Grand_Prix.jpg/440px-Michael_Schumacher_-_Ferrari_248_F1_-_Monaco_Grand_Prix.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4368" data-file-height="2912" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Michael_Schumacher_-_Ferrari_248_F1_-_Monaco_Grand_Prix.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> set the fastest time, but was relegated to the back of the grid for impeding rival cars.</div></div></div>
<p>The final qualifying session was overshadowed by Michael Schumacher's actions in the dying moments of the session as his rival, Fernando Alonso, was on a flying lap and was, at sector two, over two tenths of a second faster than the <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">German</a>. However, Schumacher stopped his car in the <i>Rascasse</i> corner, which compromised Alonso's lap. When asked about the incident, Schumacher responded by saying that he "locked up the front and went wide."<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The consensus in the paddock was that it was a deliberate act and the issue was raised with the race stewards. The stewards agreed and punished Schumacher by sending him to the back of the grid, alongside his teammate, making this the first time ever in the history of the sport that two Ferrari team cars would start a race on the back row. Ferrari's Managerial Director, <a href="/wiki/Jean_Todt" title="Jean Todt">Jean Todt</a>, said that he was "disgusted" by the decision.<sup id="cite_ref-schumacher-drop_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-schumacher-drop-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Steward Joaquin Verdegay commented, "He performed some absolutely unnecessary and pathetic counter-steering, and that lasted five metres, until there was no more chances of going through the turn normally. He lost control of the car while travelling at 16km/h. That's something completely unjustifiable."<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Former driver Keke Rosberg said of the incident ""It was the cheapest, dirtiest thing I have ever seen in F1. He should leave F1 and go home" <sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Schumacher wasn't the only driver to be punished in qualifying; <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> was also punished by the stewards for blocking <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> from improving his time and the <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italian</a> lost his three fastest lap times and dropped five places on the grid.<sup id="cite_ref-fisichella-drop_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fisichella-drop-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2006_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Fernando_Alonso_-_Renault_R26_-_Monaco_Grand_Prix_2006.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/80/Fernando_Alonso_-_Renault_R26_-_Monaco_Grand_Prix_2006.jpg/220px-Fernando_Alonso_-_Renault_R26_-_Monaco_Grand_Prix_2006.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/80/Fernando_Alonso_-_Renault_R26_-_Monaco_Grand_Prix_2006.jpg/330px-Fernando_Alonso_-_Renault_R26_-_Monaco_Grand_Prix_2006.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/80/Fernando_Alonso_-_Renault_R26_-_Monaco_Grand_Prix_2006.jpg/440px-Fernando_Alonso_-_Renault_R26_-_Monaco_Grand_Prix_2006.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4368" data-file-height="2912" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Fernando_Alonso_-_Renault_R26_-_Monaco_Grand_Prix_2006.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The race was won comfortably by <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> after closest challengers <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> suffered reliability problems.</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Nick_Heidfeld_(BMW)_GP_de_Monaco_2006_(IMG_6131).JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f4/Nick_Heidfeld_%28BMW%29_GP_de_Monaco_2006_%28IMG_6131%29.JPG/220px-Nick_Heidfeld_%28BMW%29_GP_de_Monaco_2006_%28IMG_6131%29.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f4/Nick_Heidfeld_%28BMW%29_GP_de_Monaco_2006_%28IMG_6131%29.JPG/330px-Nick_Heidfeld_%28BMW%29_GP_de_Monaco_2006_%28IMG_6131%29.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f4/Nick_Heidfeld_%28BMW%29_GP_de_Monaco_2006_%28IMG_6131%29.JPG/440px-Nick_Heidfeld_%28BMW%29_GP_de_Monaco_2006_%28IMG_6131%29.JPG 2x" data-file-width="1000" data-file-height="667" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Nick_Heidfeld_(BMW)_GP_de_Monaco_2006_(IMG_6131).JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> scored two World Championship points by finishing in seventh position.</div></div></div>
<p>With Schumacher starting from the back of the grid and <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> between him and his closest rival, <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> was in a good position. Like the qualifying session the day before, the sun was out and was most certainly staying out for the entire race. Coming into first corner, Alonso led the <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a> of Webber and the two McLarens of Räikkönen and <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Montoya</a>, nearly all 22 cars escaped the first corner unscathed, only <a href="/wiki/Tiago_Monteiro" title="Tiago Monteiro">Tiago Monteiro</a> had picked up a problem, losing his front wing after a minor collision by his teammate, <a href="/wiki/Christijan_Albers" title="Christijan Albers">Christijan Albers</a>. By the end of lap one, Michael Schumacher had already passed <a href="/wiki/Takuma_Sato" title="Takuma Sato">Takuma Sato</a> after starting the race from the pit lane and Alonso led the race by 8 tenths of a second from Mark Webber. The Australian ran wide at <i>St Devote</i> and was overtaken by Räikkönen at <i>Casino Square</i> during lap two and was now catching Alonso by setting the fastest lap and by lap four Räikkönen had Alonso in his sight.
</p><p>In the mid-field, Schumacher was up in 16th place and was right behind the <a href="/wiki/Honda_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Honda F1">Honda</a> of <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a>, but he would not get past Button until the 21st lap where overtook the Briton in the Chicane. By lap 14, the leaders had already caught up with the backmarkers of the two <a href="/wiki/Super_Aguri" class="mw-redirect" title="Super Aguri">Super Aguris</a> and Monteiro's <a href="/wiki/MF1" class="mw-redirect" title="MF1">Midland</a>. Albers was given a stop-and-go penalty on lap 18 for causing an avoidable collision with his teammate and took his penalty a lap later. By lap 21, the first round of pit stops had begun, Tiago Monteiro, who had already been into the pits, was the first driver to make a scheduled stop. Räikkönen was the first of the top three drivers to pit, which was on lap 22. Alonso pitted from the lead two laps later, leaving Mark Webber in the race lead. After a fast in-lap, Webber pitted from the lead and had the potential to come out ahead of Räikkönen. But he didn't manage to come out in front of the <a href="/wiki/Finland" title="Finland">Finnish</a> driver and retained his position. By lap 34, Schumacher was up in 13th position, behind <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a>, but two laps later Schumacher came in for his first stop of the race and with a stop of 9.1 seconds, was fueled until the end of the race.
</p><p>By lap 46, <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a>, who had been off pace for the entire race, pitted in for his first and only stop of the race. Webber was having a good race for Williams that afternoon, but it was to be cut short due to an exhaust failure; as the Australian completed his 46th lap, smoke started to appear at the back of his car and blew up in flames at the exit of the pit lane. The <a href="/wiki/Safety_Car" class="mw-redirect" title="Safety Car">Safety Car</a> was deployed so marshals could get Webber's Williams off track so the rest of the field could resume racing without running into the immobile Williams. The entire field took advantage of the safety car period and pitted a lap later for new tires and fuel. About two laps under the safety car, Räikkönen had bad luck as well as a heat shield, which caught fire before in the first practice session, caught fire again, burning through the car's wiring and, judging by the on-board footage, cutting off the electricity to the engine; Räikkönen parked his McLaren at the exit of the Portier turn. Following this, the Finn opted to return to his private yacht as the race carried on.
</p><p>With the safety car coming back into the pits, Alonso restarted in first place with Juan Pablo Montoya right behind him and <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> was third; Coulthard and Schumacher were in the points as well. By lap 57, Alonso had a commanding lead, as he had made a gap of over 15 seconds to Montoya. <a href="/wiki/Christian_Klien" title="Christian Klien">Christian Klien</a> retired a lap later promoting his teammate, David Coulthard to fifth and Michael Schumacher up to sixth. <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a> got a drive through penalty on lap 59 because he overtook under safety car conditions. Schumacher was closing fast on sixth place Coulthard at about 5 five seconds a lap, lapping in the 1:15s, but the Scot was over 35 seconds ahead of him. Barrichello, who was having a good Grand Prix up to lap 63, where he was given a drive through penalty for speeding in the pit lane, thus promoting <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> up to third place and Coulthard to fourth. Barrichello came back in fifth place.
</p><p>With a podium almost certain for Trulli, his Toyota team were pleased with the new car's performance but, on lap 72, the Italian had a hydraulic failure that shut the car down on the way up to the Casino, promoting Coulthard to third and was now on course to take Red Bull's first podium and moving Trulli's teammate, <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a>, who had a very quiet Grand Prix, up into the points. By lap 75 and only a few laps to go, Michael Schumacher had closed right up on his old <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> teammate, Rubens Barrichello but despite a number of attempts, Schumacher failed to get past the Honda and finished fifth. <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> was sixth in the sister <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a>; <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> was seventh and Ralf Schumacher took the final point up for grabs in eighth.
</p><p>At the podium, Coulthard wore a <a href="/wiki/Superman" title="Superman">Superman</a> cape, given Red Bull was in that race promoting the superhero's newest film, <i><a href="/wiki/Superman_Returns" title="Superman Returns">Superman Returns</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> No champagne was sprayed due to death of <a href="/wiki/%C3%89douard_Michelin_(born_1963)" title="Édouard Michelin (born 1963)">Édouard Michelin</a> on Friday.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-Race">Post-Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2006_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Post-Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Schumacher's actions in the qualifying session would serve as ammunition for Schumacher's critics to go along with his actions in the <a href="/wiki/1994_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="1994 Australian Grand Prix">1994 Australian Grand Prix</a> and the <a href="/wiki/1997_European_Grand_Prix" title="1997 European Grand Prix">1997 European Grand Prix</a>, although to this day Schumacher himself denies any of the allegations that he "<i>parked</i>" his Ferrari on purpose to block Fernando Alonso from taking pole position off him. However, in this race, it is also regarded as one of Schumacher's best performances owing to the immense difficulty to overtake in Monaco, which didn't stop him overtaking at least 11 cars on the narrow track.
</p><p>The Grand Prix was also the last time that <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a> stepped onto a Formula One podium as well as <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a>'s first podium finish. This would also be the last Monaco Grand Prix for Montoya, <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a> and Michael Schumacher, until his return in the <a href="/wiki/2010_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2010 Formula One season">2010 Formula One season</a>.
</p>

After the session there was immediate complaint from the other teams that this has been a deliberate move by Ferrari to ensure Schumacher started in pole position, since Alonso had been on pace to beat his time. Although Schumacher insisted he had simply made an error, a stewards' inquiry resulted in his qualifying times being deleted. As a result, Schumacher opted to start from the pitlane.<sup id="cite_ref-schumacher-drop_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-schumacher-drop-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
