<p>The <b>1989 Portuguese Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>XXIII Grande Prémio de Portugal</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at the <a href="/wiki/Aut%C3%B3dromo_do_Estoril" class="mw-redirect" title="Autódromo do Estoril">Autódromo do Estoril</a> in <a href="/wiki/Estoril" title="Estoril">Estoril</a>, <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portugal</a> on 24 September 1989. It was the thirteenth race of the <a href="/wiki/1989_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1989 Formula One World Championship">1989 Formula One World Championship</a>.
</p><p>The 71-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_640" title="Ferrari 640">Ferrari</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> second in a <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/5" title="McLaren MP4/5">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Honda_in_Formula_One" title="Honda in Formula One">Honda</a> and <a href="/wiki/Stefan_Johansson" title="Stefan Johansson">Stefan Johansson</a> third in an <a href="/wiki/Onyx_Grand_Prix" title="Onyx Grand Prix">Onyx</a>-<a href="/wiki/Ford_Motor_Company" title="Ford Motor Company">Ford</a>. Prost's teammate and Drivers' Championship rival, <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a>, retired following a collision with the second Ferrari of <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a>, who had been black-flagged for reversing in the pit lane. As a result, Prost moved 24 points clear of Senna in the championship with three races remaining.
</p><p>As well as Johansson taking Onyx's only podium finish, the race also saw <a href="/wiki/Pierluigi_Martini" title="Pierluigi Martini">Pierluigi Martini</a> lead for one lap – the first and only time the <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a> team led a Formula One race – and ten drivers from ten different teams finish in the top ten places. The race was also Prost's 150th Grand Prix start and the last start for the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Coloni" title="Scuderia Coloni">Coloni</a> team, though it would continue in F1 until the end of 1991.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1989_Portuguese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-qualifying_report">Pre-qualifying report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1989_Portuguese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Pre-qualifying report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Onyx_(racing_team)" class="mw-redirect" title="Onyx (racing team)">Onyx</a> returned to the top of the Friday morning time sheets as <a href="/wiki/Stefan_Johansson" title="Stefan Johansson">Stefan Johansson</a> was fastest by half a second. His new team-mate was Finnish driver <a href="/wiki/JJ_Lehto" title="JJ Lehto">JJ Lehto</a>, who had replaced <a href="/wiki/Bertrand_Gachot" title="Bertrand Gachot">Bertrand Gachot</a> since the last race, after Gachot openly criticised the team and was fired. In his first Formula One event, Lehto just missed out on pre-qualification after a suspension failure during the session, leaving him fifth. The <a href="/wiki/Larrousse" title="Larrousse">Larrousse</a>-<a href="/wiki/Lola_Cars" title="Lola Cars">Lola</a> cars both pre-qualified again, with <a href="/wiki/Philippe_Alliot" title="Philippe Alliot">Philippe Alliot</a> second and <a href="/wiki/Michele_Alboreto" title="Michele Alboreto">Michele Alboreto</a> fourth. The other driver to go through to the main qualifying sessions was <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Moreno" title="Roberto Moreno">Roberto Moreno</a> in third place in the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Coloni" title="Scuderia Coloni">Coloni</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Walker_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Walker-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Yannick_Dalmas" title="Yannick Dalmas">Yannick Dalmas</a> had originally pre-qualified in third place in his <a href="/wiki/Automobiles_Gonfaronnaises_Sportives" title="Automobiles Gonfaronnaises Sportives">AGS</a>, but was excluded from the session and his times were deleted, after mistakenly using the wrong tyres. Also excluded was <a href="/wiki/Osella" title="Osella">Osella</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Nicola_Larini" title="Nicola Larini">Nicola Larini</a>, for missing a weight check, although he had already failed to pre-qualify, being only ninth fastest.<sup id="cite_ref-Walker_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Walker-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The other drivers who failed to proceed any further included the other Osella of <a href="/wiki/Piercarlo_Ghinzani" title="Piercarlo Ghinzani">Piercarlo Ghinzani</a>, who outpaced his team-mate in sixth, and <a href="/wiki/Oscar_Larrauri" title="Oscar Larrauri">Oscar Larrauri</a>, despite an improvement to seventh in the <a href="/wiki/EuroBrun" title="EuroBrun">EuroBrun</a>. Eighth was <a href="/wiki/Gabriele_Tarquini" title="Gabriele Tarquini">Gabriele Tarquini</a> in the other AGS, ahead of the <a href="/wiki/Zakspeed" title="Zakspeed">Zakspeeds</a> of <a href="/wiki/Aguri_Suzuki" title="Aguri Suzuki">Aguri Suzuki</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bernd_Schneider_(racing_driver)" title="Bernd Schneider (racing driver)">Bernd Schneider</a>. Slowest by nearly four seconds was the second Coloni of <a href="/wiki/Enrico_Bertaggia" title="Enrico Bertaggia">Enrico Bertaggia</a>, the third time in a row the Italian had been bottom of the time sheets.<sup id="cite_ref-Walker_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Walker-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying_report">Qualifying report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1989_Portuguese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Qualifying report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Qualifying saw <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>'s Ayrton Senna take his tenth pole position of the season, with the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> of <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> alongside him on the front row. <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> took third in the other Ferrari, with <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> fourth in the other McLaren. <a href="/wiki/Pierluigi_Martini" title="Pierluigi Martini">Pierluigi Martini</a> impressed by qualifying fifth in his <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a>, ahead of the two <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> of <a href="/wiki/Riccardo_Patrese" title="Riccardo Patrese">Riccardo Patrese</a> and <a href="/wiki/Thierry_Boutsen" title="Thierry Boutsen">Thierry Boutsen</a> in sixth and eighth respectively, with <a href="/wiki/Alex_Caffi" title="Alex Caffi">Alex Caffi</a> seventh in the <a href="/wiki/Dallara" title="Dallara">Dallara</a>. The top ten was completed by <a href="/wiki/Luis_P%C3%A9rez-Sala" title="Luis Pérez-Sala">Luis Pérez-Sala</a> in the second Minardi and <a href="/wiki/Martin_Brundle" title="Martin Brundle">Martin Brundle</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Brabham" title="Brabham">Brabham</a>. Further down the grid, <a href="/wiki/Stefan_Johansson" title="Stefan Johansson">Stefan Johansson</a> took 12th in his <a href="/wiki/Onyx_Grand_Prix" title="Onyx Grand Prix">Onyx</a> after setting the fastest time in pre-qualifying, while another pre-qualifier, <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Moreno" title="Roberto Moreno">Roberto Moreno</a>, took 15th, the best-ever grid position for the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Coloni" title="Scuderia Coloni">Coloni</a> team.
</p><p>The Minardi, Dallara, Brabham and Coloni teams all had their tyres supplied by <a href="/wiki/Pirelli" title="Pirelli">Pirelli</a>, whose special qualifying tyres were generally regarded as being superior to those of <a href="/wiki/Goodyear_Tire_and_Rubber_Company" title="Goodyear Tire and Rubber Company">Goodyear</a>. However, Goodyear's race tyres were still acknowledged as being superior to Pirelli's.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1989_Portuguese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_report">Race report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1989_Portuguese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Race report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Berger had a great start and managed to overtake Senna. Mansell was in third followed by Prost, Martini and Patrese. Berger quickly opened a lead while Senna was trying to keep Mansell behind. Then Mansell finally managed to overtake Senna and started to catch Berger. As the two Ferraris caught up with the slower cars and were starting to lap them, Mansell managed to overtake Berger. Positions at lap 24 were: Mansell, Berger, Senna and Prost. Prost was the first of the leaders to pit for new tyres from fourth position. He was quickly followed by Berger on lap 35 and then by Senna. Then came the crucial moment of the race. 
Mansell came into the pits slightly too fast, locked his tyres and missed his pit box by a few metres. Although his pit crew moved down the pit lane to try to change his tyres where he had stopped, Mansell engaged reverse gear and drove backwards the short distance into the correct spot.  After the leaders went to pit for tyres, Martini led a lap in the <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a>, the only time in the F1 history that a Minardi car was at the front leading. Mansell was down in fourth. Berger, Senna and Mansell quickly overtook Martini and Mansell closed on Senna. However, as driving a car in reverse in the pit lane was expressly forbidden (the pit crew may legally push a car backwards), Mansell was black flagged. At the start of lap 48, Mansell tried to overtake Senna, the cars collided and both drivers were out.  This damaged Senna's title chances, especially since rival <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> came in second place. The race was won by Berger ahead of Prost, with Stefan Johansson a surprising third in the Onyx; the Swede did not make a pit-stop at any stage of the race and was initially on course for fifth place until both Williams-Renault entries were pulled out with overheating issues.
