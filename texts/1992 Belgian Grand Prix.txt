<p>The <b>1992 Belgian Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Spa-Francorchamps" title="Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps">Spa-Francorchamps</a> on 30 August 1992. It was the twelfth race of the <a href="/wiki/1992_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1992 Formula One World Championship">1992 Formula One World Championship</a>.
</p><p>The 44-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/Benetton_B192" title="Benetton B192">Benetton</a>-<a href="/wiki/Ford_Motor_Company" title="Ford Motor Company">Ford</a>. This was the first Grand Prix win for a German driver since <a href="/wiki/Jochen_Mass" title="Jochen Mass">Jochen Mass</a> at the <a href="/wiki/1975_Spanish_Grand_Prix" title="1975 Spanish Grand Prix">1975 Spanish Grand Prix</a>, and the first of an eventual record 91 Grand Prix wins for Schumacher. New World Champion <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> finished second in his <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW14" title="Williams FW14">Williams</a>-<a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a> with teammate <a href="/wiki/Riccardo_Patrese" title="Riccardo Patrese">Riccardo Patrese</a> third, thus securing the Constructors' Championship for Williams.
</p><p>This race marked Scuderia Ferrari's 500th start in a World Championship event as a team.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> It also marked the last race for the <a href="/wiki/Andrea_Moda_Formula" title="Andrea Moda Formula">Andrea Moda</a> team.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> It also marked the last race win for a car with manual transmission.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-race">Pre-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1992_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Pre-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>There was no pre-qualifying session at this race after the <a href="/wiki/Brabham" title="Brabham">Brabham</a> team did not arrive due to financial problems and the impending sale of the team. This left thirty cars, the maximum allowed in the main qualifying sessions.<sup id="cite_ref-Walker_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Walker-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Emanuele_Naspetti" title="Emanuele Naspetti">Emanuele Naspetti</a> made his Grand Prix debut for the <a href="/wiki/March_Engineering" title="March Engineering">March</a> team, replacing <a href="/wiki/Paul_Belmondo" title="Paul Belmondo">Paul Belmondo</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Walker_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Walker-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1992_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying_report">Qualifying report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1992_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Qualifying report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Equipe_Ligier" title="Equipe Ligier">Ligier</a> driver <a href="/wiki/%C3%89rik_Comas" title="Érik Comas">Érik Comas</a> was injured and briefly knocked unconscious in a heavy crash during practice on Friday and was advised by doctors not to drive again over the weekend, so he was withdrawn from Qualifying. <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> encountered Comas's car on the race track, stopped to help him, and cutting off the car's engine to reduce the risk of fire. Comas later credited this with likely having saved his life.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> had a 160mph crash down the hill in the wet during practice on the entrance to Eau Rouge with the back of the car catching fire, although he was uninjured.<sup id="cite_ref-Walker_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Walker-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Aside from Comas, the other three non-qualifiers included <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Christian_Fittipaldi" title="Christian Fittipaldi">Christian Fittipaldi</a>, returning after four races missed through injury. Also failing to qualify were the two <a href="/wiki/Andrea_Moda_Formula" title="Andrea Moda Formula">Andrea Moda</a> cars, this being the first time both cars had been present in the main qualifying sessions. <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Moreno" title="Roberto Moreno">Roberto Moreno</a> could only manage 28th fastest, over five seconds slower than Fittipaldi, with <a href="/wiki/Perry_McCarthy" title="Perry McCarthy">Perry McCarthy</a> 29th after he went off the track at the 170mph <a href="/wiki/Eau_Rouge" title="Eau Rouge">Eau Rouge</a> section. McCarthy reported to his team that the car's steering had jammed, and that he suspected the <a href="/wiki/Rack_and_pinion" title="Rack and pinion">steering rack</a> was flexing. Team boss <a href="/wiki/Andrea_Sassetti" title="Andrea Sassetti">Andrea Sassetti</a> replied that the team already knew the rack was faulty because it had previously been fitted to Moreno's car, and he had reported the same problem. McCarthy quit the team, before Sassetti was subsequently arrested in the paddock on Saturday afternoon on allegations of forging documents and fraud.<sup id="cite_ref-Walker_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Walker-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-McCarthy_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-McCarthy-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-MS_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MS-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1992_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_report">Race report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1992_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Race report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the start before La Source, <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> failed to get away from sixth position and retired after an immediate transmission problem which put him out straight away. Mansell attacked and passed Senna at the end of the second lap with Patrese following suit. Then it began to rain and almost everybody pitted for wets. In the early stages of the race, Senna showed strong pace, briefly leading from the Williams. Then, when rain fell and the other front-runners made pit stops for rain tyres, the Brazilian gambled on the rain stopping and thus stayed out. He pitted late and rejoined down in 12th as Alesi went out with a puncture after colliding with Mansell at La Source. <a href="/wiki/Ivan_Capelli" title="Ivan Capelli">Ivan Capelli</a> suffered a dramatic engine failure on lap 26 as he went straight off into the gravel trap at Blanchimont whilst battling Herbert for sixth position. The track was beginning to dry and <a href="/wiki/Thierry_Boutsen" title="Thierry Boutsen">Thierry Boutsen</a> spun off on lap 28 as a result, which meant the Brazilian's gamble had failed, and the subsequent pit stop dropped him to twelfth. He then made a charge through the field, passing <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Lotus</a> for fifth on the penultimate lap. <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Herbert" title="Johnny Herbert">Johnny Herbert</a> retired once again with engine problems on lap 43, and was classified 13th. Schumacher took his first Grand Prix win for <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a> by a comfortable margin over both the Williamses of Mansell and Patrese after they both suffered engine problems in the closing laps. The Williams duo were able to finish second and third ahead of Brundle in the other Benetton, Senna in the remaining McLaren and Häkkinen in the remaining Lotus securing the final point.
</p><p>Eighteen cars were classified as finishers, the highest number for the season.
</p>
