<p>The <b>2010 European Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>2010 Formula 1 Telefónica Grand Prix of Europe</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 27 June at the <a href="/wiki/Valencia_Street_Circuit" title="Valencia Street Circuit">Valencia Street Circuit</a> in <a href="/wiki/Valencia" title="Valencia">Valencia</a>, Spain. It was the ninth round of the <a href="/wiki/2010_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2010 Formula One season">2010 Formula One season</a>. The 57-lap race was won by Red Bull driver <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> who started from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>. <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> finished second for the <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> team and his teammate <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> was third. It was Vettel's second win of the season, and the seventh of his Formula One career.
</p><p>Vettel maintained his leading heading into the first corner and resisted Hamilton's attempts to pass him. The safety car was deployed following a large accident involving Vettel's teammate <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> and <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> on the ninth lap. Hamilton passed the safety car and was later issued with a drive-through penalty for which he served on lap 27. Vettel remained the leader at the restart despite running deep in the track's final corner. Hamilton drew closer to Vettel but he was unable to get close and Vettel maintained the lead for the remainder of the race to win.
</p><p>As a consequence of the race, Hamilton extended his lead in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">World Drivers' Championship</a> to seven points ahead of Button. This ensured the pair would enter the next, and their home race – the <a href="/wiki/2011_British_Grand_Prix" title="2011 British Grand Prix">British Grand Prix</a> – as first and second in the championship. Vettel's victory elevated him to third; he was six points behind Button after surpassing the totals of teammate <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>. By gaining the most points from the race, McLaren extended their lead in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">World Constructors' Championship</a> to thirty over Red Bull.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_European_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Bridge_Valencia_Street_Circuit.jpg" title="File:Bridge Valencia Street Circuit.jpg"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/20/Bridge_Valencia_Street_Circuit.jpg/220px-Bridge_Valencia_Street_Circuit.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/20/Bridge_Valencia_Street_Circuit.jpg/330px-Bridge_Valencia_Street_Circuit.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/20/Bridge_Valencia_Street_Circuit.jpg/440px-Bridge_Valencia_Street_Circuit.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1600" data-file-height="1067" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Bridge_Valencia_Street_Circuit.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Valencia_Street_Circuit" title="Valencia Street Circuit">Valencia Street Circuit</a>, where the race was held.</div></div></div>
<p>The 2010 European Grand Prix was the ninth out of 19 scheduled rounds of the <a href="/wiki/2010_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2010 Formula One season">2010 Formula One season</a>, and was held on 27 June at the <a href="/wiki/Valencia_Street_Circuit" title="Valencia Street Circuit">Valencia Street Circuit</a> in <a href="/wiki/Valencia" title="Valencia">Valencia</a>, Spain.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_tyres" title="Formula One tyres">Tyre</a> supplier <a href="/wiki/Bridgestone" title="Bridgestone">Bridgestone</a> brought two types of tyre to the race; two soft compounds (super soft "options" and medium "primes"). The super soft tyres were identified by a green stripe on their side-walls.<sup id="cite_ref-:6_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:6-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Before the race McLaren driver <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> led the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a> with 109 <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Championship_points_scoring_systems" title="List of Formula One World Championship points scoring systems">points</a>, three points ahead of teammate <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> in second, and <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> in third. <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> was fourth on 94 points, and <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> was four points behind in fifth place.<sup id="cite_ref-points_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> McLaren were leading the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a> with 215 points; Red Bull and Ferrari were second and third with 193 and 161 points, while Mercedes (108 points) and Renault (79) contended for fourth place.<sup id="cite_ref-points_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Ferrari, McLaren and Red Bull had so far clinched the eight previous victories of the season. Championship competitors <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a> had secured second place podium finishes, and <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> (twice), Kubica and Massa (both once) had all finished in third.<sup id="cite_ref-points_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After winning the previous two races of the season which put him into the lead of the Drivers' Championship, Hamilton said he did not fear a Red Bull resurgence in the following events having declared that the McLaren team was in a good state for the second half of the season.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> However,  he did not consider himself a favourite to win the championship.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Button became concerned that his team would lose its recent momentum but said he would get the maximum amount needed from his car to help him remain in contention.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Despite being afflicted with reliability issues with his Red Bull car, Vettel said there was no reason for him or his team to panic and was looking forward to the European Grand Prix in Valencia.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Alonso was optimistic that car updates to his Ferrari would allow him to become more competitive but was calm about his expectations.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Some teams made modifications to their cars in preparation for the event. Ferrari, Mercedes and Renault introduced diffuser designs similar to the <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_RB6" title="Red Bull RB6">Red Bull RB6</a>. Ferrari's design largely changed the shape of their exhaust system, and introduced a new gearbox case for Massa's car to raise the pick-up points of its rear suspension to help him adjust to the changes. They constructed a larger radiator to deal with the additional heat.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Mercedes elected not to bring a new gearbox casing, but introduced a new rear diffuser to fully utilise the air blowing from the car's exhaust.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Renault's design had the exit of their car's exhaust which had thermal protection against high temperatures and the team introduced a new design of their gearbox casing to allow for the pick-up points for its rear suspension to be accommodated.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> After running the <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4-25#F-duct" title="McLaren MP4-25">F-duct</a> device (which increases a car's top speed) in Friday practice, Red Bull decided to use it for the remainder of the weekend.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice">Practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_European_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Three practice sessions—two on Friday and a third on Saturday—were held before the Sunday race. The Friday morning and afternoon sessions lasted ninety minutes each; the third, one-hour session was held on Saturday morning.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Conditions were hot and sunny for the Friday practice sessions. Several drivers went off the track due to the track being dusty.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Rosberg recorded the fastest lap of the first session with a time of 1 minute and 41.175 seconds, ahead of McLaren teammates Hamilton and Button. Kubica, Massa, Vettel, Webber, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>, Alonso and <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> rounded out the session's top-ten drivers.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Senna" title="Bruno Senna">Bruno Senna</a>'s left-side view mirror became detached from his <a href="/wiki/Hispania_F110" title="Hispania F110">Hispania F110</a>, which grazed his helmet, and was run over by <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9bastien_Buemi" title="Sébastien Buemi">Sébastien Buemi</a> heading into turn 12, which caused the session to be stopped to allow <a href="/wiki/Motorsport_marshal" title="Motorsport marshal">marshals</a> to clear the track of debris.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:2_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the second practice session, Alonso set the fastest lap of the day with a time of 1:39.283; Red Bull drivers Vettel and Webber finished with the second and third-fastest times. Rosberg was fourth, ahead of Hamilton and Kubica. Massa, <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a>, Button and Barrichello followed in the top-ten.<sup id="cite_ref-:3_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Massa caused the session to be disrupted after spinning in the fourth corner and narrowly avoided making contact with a barrier. The session was stopped to allow Massa's car to be removed from the centre of the track for transport to the pit lane. <a href="/wiki/Timo_Glock" title="Timo Glock">Timo Glock</a>'s lost the use of his car's fifth gear and pulled over to the side of the track at turn twelve to prematurely end his running. <a href="/wiki/Vitaly_Petrov" title="Vitaly Petrov">Vitaly Petrov</a> spun at the exit of turn 14 and reversed his car to rejoin the circuit, avoiding the need for a red-flag.<sup id="cite_ref-:3_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Vettel was the fastest driver in the final practice session with a lap of 1:38.052; Webber was third-fastest. Kubica separated the two Red Bull drivers in second, with Sutil fourth. Alonso, Barrichello, <a href="/wiki/Vitantonio_Liuzzi" title="Vitantonio Liuzzi">Vitantonio Liuzzi</a>, Massa, Button and Hamilton rounded out the top ten.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_European_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Rubens_Barrichello_2010_Europe.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d4/Rubens_Barrichello_2010_Europe.jpg/220px-Rubens_Barrichello_2010_Europe.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="145" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d4/Rubens_Barrichello_2010_Europe.jpg/330px-Rubens_Barrichello_2010_Europe.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d4/Rubens_Barrichello_2010_Europe.jpg/440px-Rubens_Barrichello_2010_Europe.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1024" data-file-height="676" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Rubens_Barrichello_2010_Europe.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> qualified ninth and finished fourth, his best result of the season.</div></div></div>
<p>Saturday afternoon's qualifying session was divided into three parts. The first part ran for twenty minutes and eliminated the cars that finished eighteenth or lower. The second session lasted fifteen minutes and eliminated cars that finished in positions eleven to seventeen. The final session ran for ten minutes and determined <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> to tenth. Cars which competed in the final session were not allowed to change tyres before the race. They started the race fitted with the tyres used when they set their quickest lap times.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Vettel set the fastest times in the second and third sessions to win his fourth pole position of the season with a time of 1:37.587. It was the Red Bull team's eighth pole in the season's nine races. He was joined on the grid's front row by Webber who recorded a lap time 0.075 seconds slower.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton qualified third but locked his rear tyres on his final timed lap heading into turn 17 and slowed, preventing him from improving his grid position. The two Ferrari drivers were fourth and fifth (with Alonso ahead of Massa); Slower cars prevented Massa from gaining optimum tyre temperature and could not improve his fastest lap. Kubica took sixth position and felt more movement on his Renault when he had the medium compound tyres equipped. Button had <a href="/wiki/Understeer_and_oversteer" title="Understeer and oversteer">oversteer</a> through turn 24 and an error in the following corner restricted him to seventh. <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a> was eighth, ahead of Williams teammate Barrichello in ninth who had a brake-warm up problem. Petrov rounded out the top ten and mistakes on his final timed lap prevented him from obtaining a better starting position.<sup id="cite_ref-:4_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Buemi was the fastest driver not to advance into the final session; his best lap time of 1.38:586 was half a second off Vettel's pace in the second session.<sup id="cite_ref-:5_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:5-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> He was disappointed as he had Barrichello ahead of him driving towards turn 25 on his final timed lap which slowed Buemi and lost him two tenths of a second.<sup id="cite_ref-:4_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Rosberg took 12th position.<sup id="cite_ref-:5_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:5-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Sutil had no <a href="/wiki/Grip_(auto_racing)" title="Grip (auto racing)">grip</a> in his car and qualified in 13th. His teammate Liuzzi clinched 14th place after an oversteer and struggled on his car's soft tyres. Schumacher was unable to get his tyres working to his preference and had a right-front brake problem restricted him to qualifying in 15th.<sup id="cite_ref-:4_18-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher's qualifying effort was criticised by the Formula One paddock.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Pedro_de_la_Rosa" title="Pedro de la Rosa">Pedro de la Rosa</a> took 16th having struggled with a lack of grip on the super soft compound tyres. <a href="/wiki/Jaime_Alguersuari" title="Jaime Alguersuari">Jaime Alguersuari</a> drove hard but a lack of top speed put him 17th.<sup id="cite_ref-:4_18-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Kamui_Kobayashi" title="Kamui Kobayashi">Kamui Kobayashi</a> failed to advance beyond the first session;<sup id="cite_ref-:5_19-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:5-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> he <a href="/wiki/Flat_spot" title="Flat spot">flat-spotted</a> his front super soft compound tyres on his first timed lap which caused him to feel vibrations in his car.<sup id="cite_ref-:4_18-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> The two Lotus cars of <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> and <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> qualified in 19th and 20th positions, ahead of both Virgin drivers <a href="/wiki/Lucas_di_Grassi" title="Lucas di Grassi">Lucas di Grassi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Timo_Glock" title="Timo Glock">Timo Glock</a> in 21st and 22nd. Both Hispania drivers lined up at the back of the field (with <a href="/wiki/Karun_Chandhok" title="Karun Chandhok">Karun Chandhok</a> ahead of Senna).<sup id="cite_ref-:5_19-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:5-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_European_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The race commenced at 14:00 <a href="/wiki/Central_European_Summer_Time" title="Central European Summer Time">Central European Summer Time</a> (UTC+2).<sup id="cite_ref-:7_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:7-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> At the start, weather conditions were sunny and hot with an air temperature of 27 to 28&#160;°C (81 to 82&#160;°F) and a track temperature between 43 to 47&#160;°C (109 to 117&#160;°F).<sup id="cite_ref-:6_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:6-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:8_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:8-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> The top ten qualifiers started on the super soft compound tyre. When the five red lights extinguished to signal the start of the race, Vettel maintained his pole position advantage heading into the second corner. Hamilton accelerated faster than Webber off the line, overtaking him heading into the second turn. Vettel fended off an challenge from Hamilton who made contact with Vettel's right wheel in the second corner and Hamilton reported a vibration in his left-front tyre.<sup id="cite_ref-:8_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:8-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:10_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:10-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> The contact damaged Hamilton's front wing.<sup id="cite_ref-:9_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:9-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Webber made contact with Button and dropped to ninth by the end of the first lap.<sup id="cite_ref-:8_22-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:8-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Petrov lost the most places, falling from tenth to 16th by the conclusion of the lap.<sup id="cite_ref-:7_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:7-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> This was due to a large amount of <a href="/wiki/Wheelspin" title="Wheelspin">wheelspin</a> delaying Petrov when he drove away from his starting position.<sup id="cite_ref-:9_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:9-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> At the end of the first lap, Vettel led from Hamilton, Alonso, Massa, Kubica, Button, Barrichello, Hülkenberg, Webber, Buemi, Schumacher, Sutil, Liuzzi, Rosberg, Petrov, Alguersuari, de la Rosa, Kobayashi, Kovalainen, di Grassi, Glock, Chandhok, Senna and Trulli.<sup id="cite_ref-:7_21-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:7-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Mark_webber_Grand_prix_de_valencia-2010_(9).JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a9/Mark_webber_Grand_prix_de_valencia-2010_%289%29.JPG/220px-Mark_webber_Grand_prix_de_valencia-2010_%289%29.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="146" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a9/Mark_webber_Grand_prix_de_valencia-2010_%289%29.JPG/330px-Mark_webber_Grand_prix_de_valencia-2010_%289%29.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a9/Mark_webber_Grand_prix_de_valencia-2010_%289%29.JPG/440px-Mark_webber_Grand_prix_de_valencia-2010_%289%29.JPG 2x" data-file-width="2862" data-file-height="1902" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Mark_webber_Grand_prix_de_valencia-2010_(9).JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> (left) was involved in a large accident with <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> on the race's ninth lap.</div></div></div>
<p>Vettel began to pull away from the rest of the field.<sup id="cite_ref-:11_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:11-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Trulli made a pit stop for a replacement nose cone at the start of the second lap after another car made contact with the rear end of his car and his front wing was removed.<sup id="cite_ref-:9_24-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:9-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Webber battled Hülkenberg for eighth position but was unable to overtake the latter.<sup id="cite_ref-:10_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:10-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Trulli made a second pit stop on the fifth lap to allow his mechanics to rectify a problem with his car's gearbox and turned off his engine to allow for repairs to occur.<sup id="cite_ref-:8_22-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:8-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:9_24-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:9-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Vettel set a <a href="/wiki/Fastest_lap" title="Fastest lap">fastest lap</a> of the race on lap five, a 1:43.055, and was three seconds ahead of Hamilton, who in turn was being caught by Alonso.<sup id="cite_ref-:10_23-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:10-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> It was reported by Red Bull on the following lap that eighth-placed Webber had no mechanical issues.<sup id="cite_ref-:11_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:11-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Webber made an early pit stop on lap seven for the medium compound tyres but his crew had a minor problem fitting his left-front wheel which meant he was stationary for 7.9 seconds, and rejoined in 18th position. Rosberg made a similar stop on the next lap but he overshot his pit stall and rejoined in 19th place.<sup id="cite_ref-:8_22-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:8-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:10_23-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:10-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On lap nine, Webber attempted to pass Kovalainen for 17th,<sup id="cite_ref-:8_22-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:8-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> by running in his <a href="/wiki/Drafting_(aerodynamics)" title="Drafting (aerodynamics)">slipstream</a> at 190 miles per hour (310&#160;km/h) on the main straight but the latter appeared to brake earlier than Webber expected, and the Red Bull made contact with Kovalainen's right-rear wheel, sending him airborne. He struck an advertising hoarding and somersaulted.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:12_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:12-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> Webber's car landed on its nose, careered into the turn 12 <a href="/wiki/Run-off_area" title="Run-off area">run-off area</a> at high speed and collided with a tyre barrier. Webber was unhurt.<sup id="cite_ref-:12_27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:12-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> The large force of the accident broke his brake pedal.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> The incident prompted the deployment of the <a href="/wiki/Safety_car" title="Safety car">safety car</a>, to allow marshals to clear debris from the area and extricate Webber's car from the run-off area.<sup id="cite_ref-:8_22-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:8-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Kubica, Button, Barrichello, Hülkenberg, Buemi, Sutil, Liuzzi, de la Rosa and Chandhok elected to make pit stops during the safety car period.<sup id="cite_ref-:11_25-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:11-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> The safety car was sent onto the circuit as the leaders completed the ninth lap but did not emerge in front of Vettel. Hamilton had the opportunity to overtake the safety car but initially hesitated prior to committing to going by and the delay meant that the safety car had just crossed the official pit-exit line.<sup id="cite_ref-:8_22-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:8-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Lewis_Hamilton_2010_Europe.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/48/Lewis_Hamilton_2010_Europe.jpg/220px-Lewis_Hamilton_2010_Europe.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="145" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/48/Lewis_Hamilton_2010_Europe.jpg/330px-Lewis_Hamilton_2010_Europe.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/48/Lewis_Hamilton_2010_Europe.jpg/440px-Lewis_Hamilton_2010_Europe.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1024" data-file-height="677" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Lewis_Hamilton_2010_Europe.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> finished the race in second place after some controversy surrounding his illegal pass on the safety car and the penalty which he incurred for it.</div></div></div>
<p>The two Ferrari cars and the Renault of Kubica were the first to queue up behind the safety car, while the rest of the field had enough notice to divert to the pitlane for their first stop. The significance of these events was that both Vettel and Hamilton were able to make their first stops before their advantage over the rest of the field was nullified by catching the safety car, while Alonso, Massa and Kubica fell down the order. Hamilton's stop was for a replacement front wing. The safety car drove into the pit lane at the end of lap 14 and the cars were allowed to overtake.<sup id="cite_ref-:8_22-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:8-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Vettel locked his tyres and ran deep driving into the final turn, and resisted Hamilton's attempt to overtake him.<sup id="cite_ref-:10_23-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:10-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:11_25-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:11-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Alonso immediately pushed hard and passed Hülkenberg for ninth place at turn 17 on the 15th lap.<sup id="cite_ref-:8_22-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:8-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> A line of cars formed behind Kobayashi while Vettel and Hamilton pulled away from the rest of the field, as the front two began trading the fastest lap time of the event.<sup id="cite_ref-:11_25-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:11-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Button gained a large amount of top speed but remained behind both Sauber cars. Schumacher passed both Virgin cars to move into the 17th position by lap 17.<sup id="cite_ref-:8_22-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:8-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Alonso made a complaint about Hamilton and asked his team to work with the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile</a> race director <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Whiting" title="Charlie Whiting">Charlie Whiting</a> to discuss the issue with the safety car. Massa attempted to pass Liuzzi around the outside heading into turn 17 but Liuzzi turned right to defend the position. Shortly afterwards, Massa made a slight mistake which allowed Alguersuari to close the gap. Hamilton started to conserve fuel and prepare for a late attack on lap 20. On the following lap, it was announced Hamilton would be investigated by the stewards for his safety car overtake for which he was given a drive-through penalty which he served on the 27th lap.<sup id="cite_ref-:8_22-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:8-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> He remained in second position due to Kobayashi holding up the rest of the field.<sup id="cite_ref-:11_25-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:11-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Massa placed further pressure upon Liuzzi but was unable to pass him.<sup id="cite_ref-:8_22-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:8-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> A glass bottle appeared on the outside of the track on lap 28 which was retrieved by a marshal three laps later to allow racing to continue unaffected. Massa made an error at the final turn on lap 35 which meant he fell behind Liuzzi in the battle for 14th place.<sup id="cite_ref-:8_22-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:8-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:11_25-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:11-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Sebastian_Vettel_2010_Japan.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8b/Sebastian_Vettel_2010_Japan.jpg/170px-Sebastian_Vettel_2010_Japan.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="247" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8b/Sebastian_Vettel_2010_Japan.jpg/255px-Sebastian_Vettel_2010_Japan.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8b/Sebastian_Vettel_2010_Japan.jpg/340px-Sebastian_Vettel_2010_Japan.jpg 2x" data-file-width="600" data-file-height="873" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Sebastian_Vettel_2010_Japan.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> won his second race of the 2010 season.</div></div></div>
<p>Vettel extended his lead to 13 seconds when Hamilton lost time due to struggling to pass the lapped cars of Senna and Glock. Glock attempted to pass Senna around the outside heading into the first corner but was unable to move ahead.<sup id="cite_ref-:8_22-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:8-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> On lap 37,<sup id="cite_ref-:10_23-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:10-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Glock again attempted to overtake Senna but the two cars collided. The incident caused a puncture on Glock's car that very nearly threw him into a wall and forced him to make a pit stop, though his crew were not ready for him and he lost several seconds as the tyres were brought out. Senna remained on the circuit with a damaged front wing, and made a pit stop on the next lap. Sutil overtook Buemi for seventh place on lap 40. Hamilton was eight tenths of a second quicker than Vettel on the same lap and reduced the gap to 11.9 seconds. Hülkenberg's car emitted blue smoke from the rear on the next lap but disappeared on the 42nd lap.<sup id="cite_ref-:8_22-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:8-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> It was announced two laps later that Button, Barrichello, Hülkenberg, Kubica, Petrov, Liuzzi, Sutil, Buemi and de la Rosa were under investigation for exceeding the speed limit during the safety car period.<sup id="cite_ref-:10_23-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:10-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Hamilton set a new fastest lap of the race on lap 47 to reduce the time deficit to Vettel to 7.9 seconds. Petrov began to challenge de la Rosa for 12th place on the same lap. Hülkenberg's right rear tyre delaminated on lap 50, causing damage to his car's exhaust system and became the race's final retirement.<sup id="cite_ref-:8_22-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:8-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Kobayashi made his only pit stop of the event on lap 53 and rejoined in ninth position behind Alonso. Button was worried about a possible post-race time penalty and pushed hard to record the overall fastest lap of the race, a 1:38.766, on the 54th lap.<sup id="cite_ref-:10_23-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:10-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:11_25-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:11-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Alonso challenged Buemi for seventh place on lap 55 but was unable to pass. Kobayashi ran close behind Alonso and passed him around the inside for eighth after Alonso ran wide at turn 17 on the following lap. Buemi was overtaken by Kobayashi at the final corner on the last lap for seventh.<sup id="cite_ref-:10_23-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:10-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Vettel maintained his lead and crossed the start/finish line on lap 57 to win the race. Hamilton finished second, with his teammate Button third.<sup id="cite_ref-:8_22-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:8-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello clinched fourth (his best result of the season),<sup id="cite_ref-points_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> ahead of Kubica, Sutil and Kobayashi. Buemi took eighth on the road, and Alonso and de la Rosa rounded out the top ten. Petrov was the first non-points scoring finisher in eleventh, followed by Rosberg, Liuzzi and Massa. Alguersuari clinched 15th, half a second ahead of Schumacher. Di Grassi, Glock, Chandhok, Senna and Trulli were the final classified finishers.<sup id="cite_ref-:8_22-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:8-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> It was Vettel's second victory of the season, and the seventh of his career.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_European_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The top three drivers appeared on the podium to collect their trophies and spoke to the media in a later press conference.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_11-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Vettel said it was good to accumulate a large number of points in the championship battle and was "very pleased" with his victory. He said it was not the easiest win he had and he felt satisfied to win on a track where his team did not expect to be dominant. Hamilton stated that he felt it was "very positive" that he remained the Drivers' Championship leader and his team McLaren were still at the top of the Constructors' Championship heading into the British Grand Prix. He hoped that he would be able to close the gap between the two Red Bull cars and challenge them. Button said that the first lap of the race was "fun", and felt that the McLaren and Red Bull teams were strong but did not want to dismiss Ferrari from the championship battle. Overall, he said the event was not the most "exciting" race, but was happy that he and Hamilton were the first two drivers leading the championship.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Fernando_Alonso_2010_Europe.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/75/Fernando_Alonso_2010_Europe.jpg/220px-Fernando_Alonso_2010_Europe.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="145" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/75/Fernando_Alonso_2010_Europe.jpg/330px-Fernando_Alonso_2010_Europe.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/75/Fernando_Alonso_2010_Europe.jpg/440px-Fernando_Alonso_2010_Europe.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1845" data-file-height="1218" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Fernando_Alonso_2010_Europe.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> claimed the race had been fixed.</div></div></div>
<p>Webber said he had minor cuts and bruises after his lap nine accident with Kovalainen. He said he was unsure about Kovalainen's mindset and thought that the driver would allow him to pass.<sup id="cite_ref-:13_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:13-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Nevertheless, he did not apportion blame to Kovalainen and said the difference between the braking capabilities of both cars caught him out.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Webber later admitted that he misjudged how early Kovalainen would brake for the corner but felt the latter had moved more than once while defending the position.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Kovalainen claimed that he had not done anything wrong and blamed Webber for causing the crash. Both drivers were transported to the circuit's medical centre.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Commentators likened the crash to the <a href="/wiki/1999_24_Hours_of_Le_Mans" title="1999 24 Hours of Le Mans">1999 24 Hours of Le Mans</a> when his <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_CLR" title="Mercedes-Benz CLR">Mercedes-Benz CLR</a> became twice airborne down the <a href="/wiki/Mulsanne_Straight" title="Mulsanne Straight">Mulsanne Straight</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:13_31-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:13-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/BBC_Sport" title="BBC Sport">BBC</a> pundit and former driver <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> said of the crash: "Motor racing has just seen one of its luckiest days. That could have been a very, very bad incident."<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Button, Barrichello, Kubica, Sutil, Buemi, de la Rosa, Petrov, Liuzzi, and Hülkenberg were all issued with five second-time penalties by the stewards for exceeding the safety car-in lap time. The finishing positions of Button, Barrichello, Kubica, Sutil and Liuzzi were unchanged, but the penalty issued to Buemi moved him from seventh to ninth, allowing Alonso to finish eighth. Rosberg inherited tenth position as de la Rosa moved to twelfth in the final race classification. Glock was awarded a 20-second time penalty for failing to observe blue flags.<sup id="cite_ref-:14_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:14-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Ninth-place finisher Alonso criticised the race stewards, and felt the race had been fixed after they took longer than expected to issue Hamilton's penalty.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> The Vice-President of Ferrari, <a href="/wiki/Piero_Ferrari" title="Piero Ferrari">Piero Ferrari</a> stated that he was "incredulous and bitter, not just for Ferrari, but for the sport as a whole, as this is not the sort of thing one expects from professionals."<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> Other teams – including Red Bull, Lotus and McLaren – rejected Alonso's accusations, with Lotus' technical director <a href="/wiki/Mike_Gascoyne" title="Mike Gascoyne">Mike Gascoyne</a> stating "changing the safety car regulations had opened up a set of scenarios that had never been anticipated before. Hamilton passing the safety car is the only recorded example of a driver doing so in the modern era of Formula One." Ferrari subsequently argued for a change of safety car rules to prevent a similar situation reoccurring.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The result extended Hamilton's lead in the World Drivers' Championship over Button to six points. Vettel's victory elevated him from fifth to third, 12 points behind Hamilton. Webber's retirement dropped him to fourth, while Alonso's eighth-place finish demoted him from fourth to fifth.<sup id="cite_ref-points_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> McLaren increased their advantage over Red Bull in the Constructors' Championship to 30 points ahead. Ferrari remained in third place, while Renault reduced the points deficit to Mercedes in the battle for fourth place.<sup id="cite_ref-points_3-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
