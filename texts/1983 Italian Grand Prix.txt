<p>The <b>1983 Italian Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held at <a href="/wiki/Autodromo_Nazionale_di_Monza" title="Autodromo Nazionale di Monza">Monza</a> on 11 September 1983. It was the thirteenth race of the <a href="/wiki/1983_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1983 Formula One World Championship">1983 Formula One World Championship</a>.
</p><p>The 52-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/Brabham_BT52" title="Brabham BT52">Brabham</a>-<a href="/wiki/BMW_in_Formula_One" title="BMW in Formula One">BMW</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Ren%C3%A9_Arnoux" title="René Arnoux">René Arnoux</a> second in a <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_126C" title="Ferrari 126C">Ferrari</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Cheever" title="Eddie Cheever">Eddie Cheever</a> third in a <a href="/wiki/Renault_RE40" title="Renault RE40">Renault</a>. Drivers' Championship leader <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> retired with a <a href="/wiki/Turbocharger" title="Turbocharger">turbo</a> failure midway through the race, allowing Piquet and Arnoux to close to within five and two points of him respectively in the championship.
</p><p>The race saw the occurrence of an unusual incident in the pit lane. After completing a pit stop, <a href="/wiki/Niki_Lauda" title="Niki Lauda">Niki Lauda</a>'s <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/1" title="McLaren MP4/1">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Techniques_d%27Avant_Garde" title="Techniques d&#39;Avant Garde">TAG</a> stalled in front of the Brabham garage. The Brabham crew, who were preparing for Piquet's stop, were joined by team owner and <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_Constructors%27_Association" title="Formula One Constructors&#39; Association">FOCA</a> chief executive <a href="/wiki/Bernie_Ecclestone" title="Bernie Ecclestone">Bernie Ecclestone</a> in giving Lauda a push start, to get him back into the race and to clear the area for Piquet.<sup id="cite_ref-motorsportmag_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-motorsportmag-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> However, the Austrian driver retired shortly after with an electrical failure.
</p><p>Another incident occurred at the end of the race, when the <i>tifosi</i> ran onto the track to celebrate Arnoux's second place with the cars still going round. <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a>, running seventh in his <a href="/wiki/Lotus_94T" title="Lotus 94T">Lotus</a>-Renault, slowed down to avoid running over any of the spectators, only to be overtaken by <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Giacomelli" title="Bruno Giacomelli">Bruno Giacomelli</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Toleman_TG183" title="Toleman TG183">Toleman</a>-<a href="/wiki/Hart_Racing_Engines" title="Hart Racing Engines">Hart</a>. Infuriated, Mansell drove the wrong way into the pit lane.<sup id="cite_ref-motorsportmag_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-motorsportmag-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
