<p>The <b>1981 Caesars Palace Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on October 17, 1981, in <a href="/wiki/Las_Vegas_Strip" title="Las Vegas Strip">Las Vegas, Nevada</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. It was the fifteenth and final race of the <a href="/wiki/1981_FIA_Formula_One_World_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="1981 FIA Formula One World Championship">1981 FIA Formula One World Championship</a>.
</p><p>The 75-lap race was won by Australian driver <a href="/wiki/Alan_Jones_(racing_driver)" title="Alan Jones (racing driver)">Alan Jones</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW07" title="Williams FW07">Williams</a>-<a href="/wiki/Ford_Motor_Company" title="Ford Motor Company">Ford</a>, with Frenchman <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> second in a <a href="/wiki/Renault_RE30" title="Renault RE30">Renault</a> and Italian <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Giacomelli" title="Bruno Giacomelli">Bruno Giacomelli</a> third in an <a href="/wiki/Alfa_Romeo_179" title="Alfa Romeo 179">Alfa Romeo</a>. Brazilian <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a> finished fifth in his <a href="/wiki/Brabham_BT49" title="Brabham BT49">Brabham</a>-Ford to take the Drivers' Championship by one point from Jones's Argentinian teammate, <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Reutemann" title="Carlos Reutemann">Carlos Reutemann</a>, who finished eighth having started from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>. This was the final win by an <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australian</a> driver until <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> won the <a href="/wiki/2009_German_Grand_Prix" title="2009 German Grand Prix">2009 German Grand Prix</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Summary">Summary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1981_Caesars_Palace_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Summary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Championship_permutations">Championship permutations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1981_Caesars_Palace_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Championship permutations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Going into this race, three drivers were in contention for the World Championship. Argentine <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Reutemann" title="Carlos Reutemann">Carlos Reutemann</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW07" title="Williams FW07">Williams</a>-<a href="/wiki/Ford_Motor_Company" title="Ford Motor Company">Ford</a>, had 49 points having won two races, while Brazilian <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/Brabham_BT49" title="Brabham BT49">Brabham</a>-Ford, had 48 having won three. Frenchman <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Laffite" title="Jacques Laffite">Jacques Laffite</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/Ligier_JS17" title="Ligier JS17">Ligier</a>-<a href="/wiki/Equipe_Matra_Sports" title="Equipe Matra Sports">Matra</a>, had an outside chance on 43, having won two races including the previous race in <a href="/wiki/1981_Canadian_Grand_Prix" title="1981 Canadian Grand Prix">Canada</a>.
</p><p>Laffite needed to win this race with Reutemann finishing no higher than fourth and Piquet no higher than third, or to finish second with neither Reutemann nor Piquet finishing in the top six. If Laffite won with Piquet third and Reutemann fourth, then all three drivers would finish on 52 points, and the Frenchman would then win the Championship on a tie-break, with the same number of wins as Piquet but more second places than the Brazilian (two to one). He would also win the Championship if he finished second with neither Reutemann nor Piquet scoring, as he and Reutemann would both have 49 points and the same numbers of wins and second places, but he would have more third places than the Argentine (three to two).
</p><p>If Laffite failed to finish first or second, then all Reutemann had to do was finish ahead of Piquet, while Piquet had to finish in the top five and ahead of Reutemann.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_setting">The setting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1981_Caesars_Palace_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: The setting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>This was the third year in succession that the United States hosted the final round of the World Championship. This time, however, it took place in Las Vegas, instead of <a href="/wiki/Watkins_Glen_International" title="Watkins Glen International">Watkins Glen</a> in upstate New York: after twenty years on the Grand Prix schedule, the organizers at Watkins Glen were unable to fulfill financial obligations for 1981.
</p><p>The track, created on the parking lot of the <a href="/wiki/Caesars_Palace" title="Caesars Palace">Caesars Palace</a> hotel, had a smooth surface and provided speeds averaging over 160 km/h or 100 mph, as well as plenty of overtaking opportunities. Unusually, however, its direction was counter-clockwise, which strained the drivers' necks. This, together with the desert heat, meant that the drivers' endurance would be tested in the extreme all weekend. Even in practice, Piquet suffered noticeably and became physically sick; he later got a 90-minute massage from <a href="/wiki/Sugar_Ray_Leonard" title="Sugar Ray Leonard">Sugar Ray Leonard</a>'s masseur to help sort out his troubled back and "Las Vegas neck".
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1981_Caesars_Palace_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a> drivers, <a href="/wiki/Alan_Jones_(Formula_1)" class="mw-redirect" title="Alan Jones (Formula 1)">Alan Jones</a> and Reutemann, were fastest from the start of the first practice with points leader Reutemann the faster of the two. Later, Jones became the only other driver to break 1:18 in qualifying, and the starting front row was all Williams. Reutemann was not expecting any help winning the Championship from teammate Jones, who explained, "I don't see how I can help him; I would not go holding up people as I am a member of the British Commonwealth (Australia, specifically) and I would consider that unsporting."
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1981_Caesars_Palace_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the race on Saturday, Jones jumped off the line into the lead, but Reutemann was quickly passed by <a href="/wiki/Gilles_Villeneuve" title="Gilles Villeneuve">Gilles Villeneuve</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Giacomelli" title="Bruno Giacomelli">Bruno Giacomelli</a>, and finished the first lap in fifth. By the end of lap two, Jones had a five-second lead. Prost passed Villeneuve on lap three, but could not get close enough to challenge Jones for the lead. Villeneuve, meanwhile, kept a line of cars behind him as he fought off the advances of Giacomelli. This allowed <a href="/wiki/Mario_Andretti" title="Mario Andretti">Mario Andretti</a> to move right on to Piquet's tail, as he desperately tried to overtake Reutemann.
</p>
<blockquote>
<p><i>The Brazilian was nearly touching the back of the Williams as they approached the last left-hander before the pits on lap 17. Piquet got around Reutemann on the inside when Reutemann, fighting for the Championship, inexplicably braked early. Piquet said, "I saw his car getting worse oversteer, then he braked very early, I think in the hope I would run into him, but I saw it and passed easily." On the next lap, Andretti also went by. Piquet passed <a href="/wiki/John_Watson_(racing_driver)" title="John Watson (racing driver)">John Watson</a> on lap 22, and put himself in a position to score points when he took over sixth place. Reutemann continued to slip backwards with gearbox trouble, having lost fourth gear as early as lap two.</i>
</p>
</blockquote>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> team was trying to decide whether to call Villeneuve in on lap 23 after he had been disqualified for lining up on the grid improperly, but when he pulled off the track with an engine fire, the point was moot. On lap 30, crowd favorite Andretti retired from fourth place with broken suspension.
</p><p>With 15 laps still to go, but a 40-second lead over Prost, Jones began pacing himself to the finish. Giacomelli was third, having worked his way back after spinning from fourth to tenth, and <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> had passed Piquet for fourth.
</p>
<blockquote>
<p><i>Piquet, in fact, was on the verge of physical exhaustion with his head visibly rolling around in the cockpit, but he still held fifth place and the two points he needed for the Championship. Piquet's condition was the only question left about how the Championship would turn out, for Reutemann, driving without fourth gear, was passed by Watson and Laffite, dropping to eighth place on lap 69.</i>
</p>
</blockquote>
<p>Laffite took sixth place and the final point from Watson on the last corner of the last lap, while Giacomelli missed taking second from Prost, on failing tires, by a few car lengths. As Jones crossed the line in first, the Williams team celebrated wildly, apparently unaffected by the fact its other driver had just lost the Championship. Piquet took fifteen minutes to recover from heat exhaustion after making it to the finish, but he had collected the two points for fifth place, and was the new World Champion.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1981_Caesars_Palace_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Paul_Newman" title="Paul Newman">Paul Newman</a> was the race director.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Alan_Jones_(racing_driver)" title="Alan Jones (racing driver)">Alan Jones</a>'s final win and podium in his last race for <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a>. It would be the last time an Australian would win a Formula One Grand Prix until <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> won the <a href="/wiki/2009_German_Grand_Prix" title="2009 German Grand Prix">2009 German Grand Prix</a>.</li>
<li>It was the final Grand Prix for <a href="/wiki/H%C3%A9ctor_Rebaque" title="Héctor Rebaque">Héctor Rebaque</a>. It would be the last time a Mexican driver would race in Formula One until <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a> started the <a href="/wiki/2011_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="2011 Australian Grand Prix">2011 Australian Grand Prix</a>.</li>
<li>This was the last pole position in <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Reutemann" title="Carlos Reutemann">Carlos Reutemann</a>'s career and also for <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a> in the sport.</li>
<li>The race marked the only career podium for <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Giacomelli" title="Bruno Giacomelli">Bruno Giacomelli</a>.</li></ul>
