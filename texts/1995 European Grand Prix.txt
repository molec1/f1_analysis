<p>The <b>1995 European Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>XL Grand Prix of Europe</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 1 October 1995 at the <a href="/wiki/N%C3%BCrburgring" title="Nürburgring">Nürburgring</a>, <a href="/wiki/N%C3%BCrburg" title="Nürburg">Nürburg</a>, Germany. It was the fourteenth race of the <a href="/wiki/1995_FIA_Formula_One_World_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="1995 FIA Formula One World Championship">1995 FIA Formula One World Championship</a> and the first to be held there since <a href="/wiki/1985_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1985 Formula One World Championship">1985</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pg206-207_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg206-207-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The 67-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a> team after starting from third position. <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a> finished second in a <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, with <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a>, who started the Grand Prix from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>, third in a <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> car.<sup id="cite_ref-formula1.com_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-formula1.com-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> This was also the last race for <a href="/wiki/Massimiliano_Papis" class="mw-redirect" title="Massimiliano Papis">Massimiliano Papis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gabriele_Tarquini" title="Gabriele Tarquini">Gabriele Tarquini</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jean-Denis_D%C3%A9l%C3%A9traz" class="mw-redirect" title="Jean-Denis Délétraz">Jean-Denis Délétraz</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a>, Coulthard's teammate, started the race in second, but dropped down to third behind Schumacher at the start. Hill remained behind Schumacher for the majority of the race, before losing his front wing in a collision with Alesi and dropping back due to the resultant <a href="/wiki/Pit_stop" title="Pit stop">pit stop</a>. He then spun off the track on lap&#160;58 when running in fourth position, leading to his retirement.<sup id="cite_ref-pg206-207_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg206-207-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Alesi looked set to win the race after starting the race with dry weather <a href="/wiki/Slick_tyre" class="mw-redirect" title="Slick tyre">slick tyres</a> on a damp track, but was held up in <a href="/wiki/Racing_flags#Blue_flag" title="Racing flags">lapped traffic</a> and overtaken by Schumacher two laps before the end of the race.<sup id="cite_ref-review_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-review-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Schumacher's win kept him at the top of the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a>, 27 points ahead of Hill. The German driver only needed a further three points to secure the title.<sup id="cite_ref-pg206-207_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg206-207-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-grandprix-322_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-322-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Benetton were now 20 points ahead of Williams in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a> following Schumacher's win.<sup id="cite_ref-pg206-207_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg206-207-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1995_European_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1995_European_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The race marked the return to the Formula One calendar for the <a href="/wiki/N%C3%BCrburgring" title="Nürburgring">Nürburgring</a> track after being off the calendar since the <a href="/wiki/1985_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="1985 Formula One season">1985 season</a>, due to commercial disputes off the circuit involving the circuit's promoters.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix-301_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-301-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Its return to the World Championship schedule was facilitated by a surge of interest in the sport throughout Germany as a result of <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>'s <a href="/wiki/1994_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1994 Formula One World Championship">1994</a> drivers' title.<sup id="cite_ref-F1Ypg190_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1Ypg190-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The previous year, the <a href="/wiki/1994_European_Grand_Prix" title="1994 European Grand Prix">European round</a> was held at the <a href="/wiki/Circuito_de_Jerez" title="Circuito de Jerez">Circuito Permanente de Jerez</a> in Spain, a late replacement for the cancelled <a href="/wiki/Argentine_Grand_Prix" title="Argentine Grand Prix">Argentine Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-nyt_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nyt-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Heading into the 14th race of the season, <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a> driver Schumacher was leading the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a> with 72 points, with <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a> second on 55. Behind them were Hill's teammate <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> on 39, Schumacher's teammate <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Herbert" title="Johnny Herbert">Johnny Herbert</a> on 38, and <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a> on 34.<sup id="cite_ref-pg198-199_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg198-199-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a>, Benetton were leading with 100 points, with Williams second on 88 and Ferrari third on 62.<sup id="cite_ref-pg198-199_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg198-199-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>There was one driver change heading into the race: <a href="/wiki/Gabriele_Tarquini" title="Gabriele Tarquini">Gabriele Tarquini</a> replaced <a href="/wiki/Ukyo_Katayama" title="Ukyo Katayama">Ukyo Katayama</a> at <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_Racing" title="Tyrrell Racing">Tyrrell</a>, the Japanese driver choosing not to race on doctors' advice following a crash at the <a href="/wiki/1995_Portuguese_Grand_Prix" title="1995 Portuguese Grand Prix">Portuguese Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix-katayama_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-katayama-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Katayama had sustained a strained neck and bruising in the crash, in which his car had rolled several times following a startline collision with <a href="/wiki/Luca_Badoer" title="Luca Badoer">Luca Badoer</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pg194_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg194-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Prior to the race weekend, Coulthard announced that he was moving to the <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> team for the <a href="/wiki/1996_FIA_Formula_One_World_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="1996 FIA Formula One World Championship">1996 season</a>. He had first signed a contract with McLaren in October 1994 before his full-time Williams drive for 1995 was confirmed, and his one-year deal with the latter was not renegotiated due to his McLaren commitment. McLaren also confirmed that <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jan_Magnussen" title="Jan Magnussen">Jan Magnussen</a> would continue in their existing roles as race and test driver for the team respectively, leaving <a href="/wiki/Mark_Blundell" title="Mark Blundell">Mark Blundell</a> without a contract for 1996.<sup id="cite_ref-pg205_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg205-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard's deal was officially confirmed on the day of the race itself.<sup id="cite_ref-pg201_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg201-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Meanwhile, Coulthard's prospective replacement, <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a>, had tested a Williams for the first time since being confirmed as Hill's teammate for 1996, running for two days at the <a href="/wiki/Autodromo_Nazionale_Monza" class="mw-redirect" title="Autodromo Nazionale Monza">Monza</a> circuit.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a> team also announced that it would keep its driver pairing of <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a> for 1996 in the week before the race.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> However, Ferrari then announced that it had bought out Irvine's contract, and that he would be partnering Schumacher at the team for 1996.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Jordan subsequently announced that <a href="/wiki/Martin_Brundle" title="Martin Brundle">Martin Brundle</a> would move from the <a href="/wiki/Equipe_Ligier" title="Equipe Ligier">Ligier</a> team to partner Barrichello instead.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Some teams brought upgrades to the race – the Williams team used the upgraded "B" versions of the <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW17" title="Williams FW17">FW17</a> chassis for the second time, after its début at the previous race in Portugal.<sup id="cite_ref-pg201_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg201-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> McLaren also brought upgrades to coincide with the home race of their engine supplier, <a href="/wiki/Mercedes_AMG_High_Performance_Powertrains" title="Mercedes AMG High Performance Powertrains">Mercedes</a>. The team opted to amalgamate the "B" and "C" versions of its troublesome <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/10" title="McLaren MP4/10">MP4/10</a> chassis, with a rear end and gearbox from the former.<sup id="cite_ref-pg204_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg204-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> In addition, the Ferrari team brought a revised rear wing and sidepods for its <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_412T2" class="mw-redirect" title="Ferrari 412T2">412T2</a> car, which both drivers agreed to be an improvement.<sup id="cite_ref-pg204_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg204-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice_and_qualifying">Practice and qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1995_European_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Practice and qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>This was the first Formula One race to be held at the Nürburgring since the <a href="/wiki/1985_German_Grand_Prix" title="1985 German Grand Prix">1985 German Grand Prix</a>, so an additional familiarisation session was held prior to the usual practice and qualifying sessions. In this session, Schumacher set a benchmark time of 1:20.418, from Hill, Coulthard, Häkkinen, <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a> (<a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a>), <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> (Ferrari) and the rest of the field.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> The drivers, of whom only Berger and Brundle had raced at Nürburgring in a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_car" title="Formula One car">Formula One car</a> before, were uninspired by the circuit: Schumacher described it as "dull" and "easy to learn, with no real challenges" and Coulthard predicted a processional race without much overtaking.<sup id="cite_ref-F1Ypg190_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1Ypg190-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Two practice sessions were held before the race; the first was held on Friday morning and the second on Saturday morning. Both sessions lasted 1&#160;hour and 45&#160;minutes with weather conditions dry in the first session, but wet for the second session.<sup id="cite_ref-atlas-europemain_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlas-europemain-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-atlas-euro2ndpractice_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlas-euro2ndpractice-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Hill set the fastest time in the first session, posting a lap of 1:19.343, three-hundredths of a second quicker than teammate Coulthard in second place.<sup id="cite_ref-pg201_12-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg201-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The Benettons were third and fifth fastest; Schumacher ahead of Herbert, with Ferrari driver <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> fourth, over a second behind Hill. Häkkinen rounded out the top six in his McLaren.<sup id="cite_ref-pg206-207_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg206-207-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The wet conditions for the second practice session meant that lap times were slower as there was less grip on the track.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Despite the wet conditions, Hill was again fastest with a time of 1:34.906. Coulthard was second, with Frentzen third in the Sauber, two seconds slower than Hill. The Ferraris were fourth and fifth, Berger in front of Alesi. Schumacher and Herbert were sixth and seventh respectively, with Barrichello eighth. <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Panis" title="Olivier Panis">Olivier Panis</a> in the Ligier and <a href="/wiki/Mika_Salo" title="Mika Salo">Mika Salo</a> in the Tyrrell rounded out the top ten, both three and a half seconds off the fastest lap time.<sup id="cite_ref-pg206-207_1-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg206-207-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Amongst the slower runners, <a href="/wiki/Andrea_Montermini" title="Andrea Montermini">Andrea Montermini</a> suffered a worrying moment when his <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Racing" title="Pacific Racing">Pacific</a> car shed its left-rear wheel, but he was able to return to his pit garage.<sup id="cite_ref-F1Ypg188-189_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1Ypg188-189-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:David_Coulthard_at_the_1995_British_GP,_Silverstone_(49713882947).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/05/David_Coulthard_at_the_1995_British_GP%2C_Silverstone_%2849713882947%29.jpg/170px-David_Coulthard_at_the_1995_British_GP%2C_Silverstone_%2849713882947%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="160" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/05/David_Coulthard_at_the_1995_British_GP%2C_Silverstone_%2849713882947%29.jpg/255px-David_Coulthard_at_the_1995_British_GP%2C_Silverstone_%2849713882947%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/05/David_Coulthard_at_the_1995_British_GP%2C_Silverstone_%2849713882947%29.jpg/340px-David_Coulthard_at_the_1995_British_GP%2C_Silverstone_%2849713882947%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1605" data-file-height="1514" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:David_Coulthard_at_the_1995_British_GP,_Silverstone_(49713882947).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> took <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> in his <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a>-<a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/List_of_motorsport_terminology#Q" class="mw-redirect" title="List of motorsport terminology">qualifying session</a> was split into two one-hour sessions; the first was held on Friday afternoon and the second on Saturday afternoon. The fastest time from either session counted towards the drivers' final <a href="/wiki/Standing_start" title="Standing start">grid positions</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-atlas-europemain_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlas-europemain-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Both sessions on Friday and Saturday were interrupted by rain, which led to little action on the track.<sup id="cite_ref-atlas-europe2ndquali_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlas-europe2ndquali-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard took his third consecutive <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> in his Williams, with a time of 1:18.738.<sup id="cite_ref-pg206-207_1-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg206-207-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-formula1.com-coulthard1995_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-formula1.com-coulthard1995-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> He was joined on the front row by teammate Hill, who was two-tenths of a second behind.<sup id="cite_ref-pg206-207_1-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg206-207-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher was third in the Benetton, four-tenths of a second slower than Coulthard, despite lapping fastest in the second part of qualifying. The time he set in the second part of qualifying was still slower than Coulthard and Hill's time set in the first part, and thus he was third quickest overall. Behind the leading three, Berger was fourth with teammate Alesi in sixth. <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a> in the Jordan split them for fifth.<sup id="cite_ref-atlas-europegrid_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlas-europegrid-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Herbert and Frentzen shared the fourth row of the grid. Despite bringing car upgrades to the event, Häkkinen and Blundell could only qualify their McLarens ninth and tenth respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-pg204_17-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg204-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Tarquini qualified in nineteenth, four places behind teammate Salo, but admitted to feeling out-of-practice by Formula One standards.<sup id="cite_ref-pg204_17-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg204-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Several drivers went off in the difficult conditions, including Panis and Montermini.<sup id="cite_ref-atlas-europe1stquali_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlas-europe1stquali-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-montermini-eurosport_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-montermini-eurosport-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> The former driver's crash was caused by a stuck <a href="/wiki/Throttle" title="Throttle">throttle</a>; the resultant damage to the car forced him to switch to the team's spare <a href="/wiki/Monocoque" title="Monocoque">monocoque</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pg204_17-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg204-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> The conditions during the first session were generally faster, with seventeen of the 24 drivers setting their fastest qualifying times in this period.<sup id="cite_ref-pg206-207_1-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg206-207-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After qualifying, attention focussed on Coulthard's outperformance of Hill for the fourth time in a row.  With only the latter able to challenge Schumacher for the championship, the question of Williams team orders was raised.  In response, Coulthard said:
</p>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r960796168">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>I'm getting rather tired of questions about team orders. I think what everyone wants to see is a motor race, and my first duty to the team is to try and win the constructors' championship. If the circumstances were different, like they were last year [when Coulthard did not compete in all of the races], I would think it entirely fair to do as the team requested [...] But I also think it's fair of Williams not to ask me to slow down if I'm quicker than Damon."<sup id="cite_ref-pg201/204_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg201/204-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>
<p>Following the session, Barrichello and <a href="/wiki/Footwork_Arrows" title="Footwork Arrows">Footwork</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Max_Papis" title="Max Papis">Massimiliano Papis</a> revealed that they expected to fail routine drugs tests they had just taken, as both had been taking decongestants, for nasal problems and a cold respectively, which contained the banned substance <a href="/wiki/Ephedrine" title="Ephedrine">ephedrine</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pg204_17-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg204-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> On Saturday, the team principals also met to discuss future changes to the sport: it was agreed that qualifying would be reduced to a single hour-long session for the <a href="/wiki/1996_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="1996 Formula One season">1996 season</a>, and a reduction from seventeen to sixteen Grands Prix was confirmed.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1995_European_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The track surface for the start was damp, but dried up as the race progressed.<sup id="cite_ref-formula1.com-europefeature_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-formula1.com-europefeature-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> The drivers were scheduled to go onto the track at 09:30&#160;<a href="/wiki/Central_European_Time" title="Central European Time">CET</a> (<a href="/wiki/Greenwich_Mean_Time" title="Greenwich Mean Time">GMT</a> +1) for a 30-minute warm-up session, however the session was delayed until 10:05 CET due to <a href="/wiki/Fog" title="Fog">fog</a> and heavy rain. Several drivers went off the track during the session due to the wet weather conditions.<sup id="cite_ref-atlas-euroWU_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlas-euroWU-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Both Williams cars maintained their good performance from qualifying; Coulthard had the fastest time of 1:38.378. Hill was third in the other Williams; Berger split them in the Ferrari for second position. Schumacher completed the top four, six-tenths of a second behind Coulthard.<sup id="cite_ref-pg206-207_1-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg206-207-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Approximately 90,000 spectators attended the circuit on race day.<sup id="cite_ref-F1Ypg187_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1Ypg187-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Coulthard was forced to use his <a href="/wiki/List_of_motorsport_terminology#S" class="mw-redirect" title="List of motorsport terminology">spare car</a>, set up for Hill, for the race itself after he spun off the track during his <a href="/wiki/List_of_motorsport_terminology#O" class="mw-redirect" title="List of motorsport terminology">reconnaissance lap</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stall_(engine)" title="Stall (engine)">stalling</a> the engine in the process.<sup id="cite_ref-R&amp;R_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-R&amp;R-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-grandprix-europe_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-europe-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> The race was scheduled to start at 14:00 CET, but Papis stalled his Footwork in the <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_regulations#Race_procedure" title="Formula One regulations">starting lights procedure</a>, resulting in the start being aborted. A new start time of 14:05 CET was planned.<sup id="cite_ref-bbc_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> Many of the teams started the race with <a href="/wiki/Rain_tyre" title="Rain tyre">wet weather tyres</a>, but the Ferrari and McLaren teams chose to fit their cars with dry weather <a href="/wiki/Slick_tyre" class="mw-redirect" title="Slick tyre">slick tyres</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix-europe_34-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-europe-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> The Ferrari mechanics also raised the ride height and increased the <a href="/wiki/Downforce" title="Downforce">downforce</a> levels on Alesi and Berger's cars on the grid, making them more competitive in the wet conditions.<sup id="cite_ref-pg205_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg205-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Coulthard, from pole position, held onto the lead into the first corner, while Hill made a bad start and was overtaken by Schumacher in the run down to the corner.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix-europe_34-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-europe-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Irvine also got past Hill, but was repassed by the Williams driver later on in the opening lap.<sup id="cite_ref-pg205_11-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg205-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Further down the order, Frentzen was judged to have jumped the start, while Papis took up his normal grid position instead of starting from the back as he should have done after stalling; both were given <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_regulations#Penalties" title="Formula One regulations">ten-second stop-and-go penalties</a> in the early stages of the race for these misdemeanours.<sup id="cite_ref-pg205_11-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg205-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Papis' teammate, <a href="/wiki/Taki_Inoue" title="Taki Inoue">Taki Inoue</a>, failed to take the start due to an electronic failure.<sup id="cite_ref-pg206-207_1-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg206-207-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatright" style="width:40%;&#32;;"><style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r887775652">.mw-parser-output .quotebox{background-color:#F9F9F9;border:1px solid #aaa;box-sizing:border-box;padding:10px;font-size:88%;max-width:100%}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft{margin:0.5em 1.4em 0.8em 0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright{margin:0.5em 0 0.8em 1.4em}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.centered{margin:0.5em auto 0.8em auto}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft p,.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright p{font-style:inherit}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-title{background-color:#F9F9F9;text-align:center;font-size:larger;font-weight:bold}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:before{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" “ ";vertical-align:-45%;line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:after{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" ” ";line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .left-aligned{text-align:left}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .right-aligned{text-align:right}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .center-aligned{text-align:center}.mw-parser-output .quotebox cite{display:block;font-style:normal}@media screen and (max-width:360px){.mw-parser-output .quotebox{min-width:100%;margin:0 0 0.8em!important;float:none!important}}</style>

<p><cite class="left-aligned" style=""><a href="/wiki/Mark_Blundell" title="Mark Blundell">Mark Blundell</a>, commenting on his race.<sup id="cite_ref-Guardian2002_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Guardian2002-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>The McLarens and Berger's Ferrari slipped back in the early laps of the race, with both McLarens overtaken by the <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Racing" title="Pacific Racing">Pacific</a> and <a href="/wiki/Forti" title="Forti">Forti</a> cars as they dropped down the order.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix-europe_34-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-europe-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Guardian2002_38-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Guardian2002-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> The McLaren chassis handled badly in the wet conditions, while the throttle response of the Mercedes engine was also too abrupt to be driven effectively in the rain.<sup id="cite_ref-pg205_11-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg205-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Berger dropped to ninth, not helped by an incorrectly pressured set of tyres that left him with a handling imbalance,<sup id="cite_ref-pg204_17-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg204-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> but Alesi moved up to fourth position, overtaking Herbert on lap five and Irvine on lap eight.<sup id="cite_ref-pg206-207_1-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg206-207-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Berger and the McLarens moved back up the order as drivers made a <a href="/wiki/Pit_stop" title="Pit stop">pit stop</a> for slick tyres. As the front-runners made their stops, Alesi ended up twenty seconds ahead of Coulthard, Schumacher and Hill.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix-europe_34-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-europe-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> By the end of lap&#160;17, most of the drivers had pitted for slick tyres, and the running order had stabilised as Alesi, Coulthard, Schumacher, Hill, Berger and Irvine occupied the top six positions.<sup id="cite_ref-pg206-207_1-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg206-207-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Frentzen was outside the points in seventh, but on the next lap he collided with <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Diniz" title="Pedro Diniz">Pedro Diniz</a>'s Forti while trying to lap him, retiring from the race as a result.<sup id="cite_ref-pg205_11-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg205-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Panis and Blundell were also out by this stage, both having spun off the track on lap&#160;15.<sup id="cite_ref-pg206-207_1-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg206-207-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Near the front of the field, Hill was faster than third-placed Schumacher and began to catch him.<sup id="cite_ref-formula1.com-europefeature_30-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-formula1.com-europefeature-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher defended his position vigorously several times, but Hill eventually passed him. However, Hill then ran wide at the final corner and Schumacher repassed him.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix-europe_34-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-europe-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard, in the other Williams, began to suffer from excessive <a href="/wiki/Oversteer" class="mw-redirect" title="Oversteer">oversteer</a> with his car's handling,<sup id="cite_ref-pg205_11-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg205-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> dropping to fourth behind Schumacher and Hill, who passed him on laps&#160;21 and 23 respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-pg206-207_1-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg206-207-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> As Schumacher and the Williams drivers battled for position, Alesi was able to extend his lead to 45&#160;seconds.<sup id="cite_ref-F1Ypg187_32-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1Ypg187-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Alesi and Schumacher made their second pit stops on lap&#160;34; Alesi took on enough fuel to finish the race, while Schumacher's was the second of three scheduled pit visits.<sup id="cite_ref-pg206-207_1-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg206-207-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-grandprix-europe_34-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-europe-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Alesi exited from his stop just in front of third-placed Hill, who attempted to overtake Alesi on lap&#160;40, the lap before his own pit stop was due to take place.<sup id="cite_ref-pg205_11-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg205-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> In the process, the two cars made contact and Hill lost his <a href="/wiki/Downforce#Front" title="Downforce">front wing</a>. As a result, he had had to pit to replace the damaged wing, losing time while driving around the track with his damaged car and rejoining behind Coulthard in fourth&#160;position.<sup id="cite_ref-pg205_11-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg205-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> On the same lap, Berger retired with terminal engine problems.<sup id="cite_ref-pg204_17-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg204-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> After his stop, Hill pushed hard in an attempt to catch Coulthard, but spun out of the race on lap&#160;58.<sup id="cite_ref-formula1.com-europefeature_30-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-formula1.com-europefeature-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-grandprix-europe_34-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-europe-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> The impact with the tyre barrier was hard enough to cause a minor crack to a bone in his leg, but he escaped serious injury.<sup id="cite_ref-pg205_11-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg205-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Michael_Schumacher_1994_San_Marino_Gran_Prix.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2a/Michael_Schumacher_1994_San_Marino_Gran_Prix.jpg/170px-Michael_Schumacher_1994_San_Marino_Gran_Prix.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="259" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/2/2a/Michael_Schumacher_1994_San_Marino_Gran_Prix.jpg 1.5x" data-file-width="200" data-file-height="305" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Michael_Schumacher_1994_San_Marino_Gran_Prix.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> (pictured in 1994) won the race by 2.6 seconds.</div></div></div>
<p>Schumacher made his final pit stop on lap&#160;52 from second place, having caught up with Alesi during his third <a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/stint" class="extiw" title="wikt:stint">stint</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-F1Ypg187_32-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1Ypg187-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> He rejoined some distance behind the Ferrari, but began charging, setting a <a href="/wiki/Fastest_lap" title="Fastest lap">fastest lap</a> of 1:21.180 on lap&#160;58, almost 1.7&#160;seconds faster than Alesi's fastest lap of the race.<sup id="cite_ref-pg206-207_1-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg206-207-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Alesi's attempts to keep the lead were not helped by <a href="/wiki/Racing_flags#Blue_flag" title="Racing flags">lapped traffic</a> getting in his way, as well as losing five&#160;seconds at the Veedol <a href="/wiki/Chicane" title="Chicane">chicane</a> by running wide onto the gravel.<sup id="cite_ref-review_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-review-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> In the closing laps, Schumacher caught Alesi, overtaking him on the outside of the chicane on lap&#160;65.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix-europe_34-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-europe-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher opened the gap to two and a half seconds, and won the race after 67&#160;laps to secure his seventh victory of the season in a time of 1:39:59.044.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix-europe_34-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-europe-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Hill, watching from the side of the track, applauded Schumacher's win; the German attempted to stop his car to give his rival a lift back to the pits, but was unable due to a slipping clutch.<sup id="cite_ref-F1Ypg187_32-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1Ypg187-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Alesi held position to finish second in his Ferrari, with Coulthard third, 35&#160;seconds behind. Completing the points-scorers, Barrichello finished fourth, ahead of Herbert and Irvine. The latter two had battled for much of the race: they collided on lap&#160;31, dropping both behind Barrichello and causing Herbert to make a pit stop for a new front wing; later, Irvine spun while attempting to lap Papis, allowing Herbert back through.<sup id="cite_ref-pg205_11-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg205-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>  Outside the points, Brundle finished seventh for Ligier, ahead of the recovering Häkkinen, whose eighth position, two laps down on Schumacher, was a major disappointment for Mercedes in its home country.<sup id="cite_ref-pg205_11-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg205-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a> drivers <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Lamy" title="Pedro Lamy">Pedro Lamy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Luca_Badoer" title="Luca Badoer">Luca Badoer</a> finished ninth and eleventh, with Salo in tenth position, the latter making an unscheduled pit stop to change a punctured tyre after colliding with <a href="/wiki/Jean-Christophe_Boullion" title="Jean-Christophe Boullion">Jean-Christophe Boullion</a> during the race;<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> the Sauber driver retired as a result of damage sustained in this incident.<sup id="cite_ref-pg206-207_1-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg206-207-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Papis, Diniz, Tarquini and Délétraz completed the field.<sup id="cite_ref-pg206-207_1-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg206-207-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>  Of the other retirements, <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Moreno" title="Roberto Moreno">Roberto Moreno</a> stopped his Forti with a driveshaft failure,<sup id="cite_ref-pg206-207_1-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg206-207-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> while Montermini endured a fraught pitstop on lap&#160;42. The Pacific team's fuel rig malfunctioned, and, in the confusion, refueller Paul Summerfield sustained a fractured left <a href="/wiki/Femur" title="Femur">femur</a> when he was hit by the car as it left its pit box.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>  Montermini consequently ran out of fuel on lap&#160;45.<sup id="cite_ref-pg206-207_1-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg206-207-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1995_European_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatright" style="width:45%;&#32;;"><link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r887775652"/>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">"Today, I decided not to risk starting on slicks because the car felt so critical in these conditions and I wanted to play safe for the championship. When I saw Damon in the wall, I thought that now I had to decide to stay in second place or to push to win and my fans pushed me to try and win the race. When I passed Jean, he was making it as difficult as possible, but there was nothing dangerous or anything. He left room on the outside and we did touch. For me, it is fantastic to win the German Grand Prix, the Belgian Grand Prix and the Monaco Grand Prix [this year] as well as this race in Germany. All four are 'home' events for me and I have been very lucky."</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style=""><a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>, commenting on the race.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>Journalist <a href="/wiki/Alan_Henry" title="Alan Henry">Alan Henry</a> described the race as the best of the season so far,<sup id="cite_ref-pg205_11-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg205-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> even of recent memory,<sup id="cite_ref-pg204_17-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg204-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> and suggested that Schumacher's performance could mark a watershed in which the German "crossed that indistinct dividing line separating the good from the great".<sup id="cite_ref-pg201_12-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg201-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher's win meant he needed only three more points from the remaining races of the season to win the Drivers' Championship, while Hill would have to win all three Grands Prix, effectively meaning that the Championship was over.<sup id="cite_ref-pg201_12-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg201-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>For his part, Hill said that "I am not going to be World Champion this year, but I'll be back. But I don't think I disgraced myself.  I put up a good fight, did everything I could to win, and it didn't come off", and pledged to do his best to win the remaining races.<sup id="cite_ref-pg201_12-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg201-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Hill later said that the car's steering had felt stiff since his collision with Alesi, making it difficult to drive, but accepted the blame for his retirement.<sup id="cite_ref-pg205_11-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg205-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-F1Ypg187_32-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1Ypg187-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> In the weeks after the race, heavy criticism was directed towards Hill, in which pundits felt that he had not been "forceful" enough in his battle with Schumacher.<sup id="cite_ref-walker-hill_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-walker-hill-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-pg209_43-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg209-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> In an interview between the European and <a href="/wiki/1995_Pacific_Grand_Prix" title="1995 Pacific Grand Prix">Pacific</a> races, Brundle said:
</p>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r960796168"/><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>Damon has to do two things. First, he has to establish himself as the No. 1 at Williams for next year so the team can give him their full support. Second, he has to re-establish himself as a racer. Maybe he needs to lose a front wheel once or twice to re-establish himself.<sup id="cite_ref-times-oct20_44-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-times-oct20-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>
<p>Schumacher said that Hill made "half-hearted attempts" to overtake during the race, which led to him "getting into trouble".<sup id="cite_ref-pg209_43-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg209-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> Alesi also blamed Hill for their collision, saying that "I do not know what happened with Damon. I saw his nose alongside me, that's all. I was sideways [turning into the corner] and he hit me. He was too optimistic as normally you cannot do anything at that corner."<sup id="cite_ref-F1Ypg187_32-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1Ypg187-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> At a <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile</a> (FIA) <a href="/wiki/FIA_World_Motor_Sport_Council" title="FIA World Motor Sport Council">World Motor Sport Council</a> meeting on October 19 to discuss driver etiquette, they opted against introducing new rules on the issue. Formula One's <a href="/wiki/Sport_governing_body" class="mw-redirect" title="Sport governing body">governing body</a> emphasised that the <a href="/wiki/International_Sporting_Code" title="International Sporting Code">International Sporting Code</a> would be enforced on the basis that drivers are free to drive as they wish "provided they do not deliberately endanger another driver or repeatedly obstruct him on a straight", following incidents during the year involving Hill and Schumacher.<sup id="cite_ref-pg211_45-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg211-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Alesi, despite his performance in the early stages of the race, was also criticised by some for losing his lead after letting Schumacher gain time on him in the final laps. Ferrari designer <a href="/wiki/John_Barnard" title="John Barnard">John Barnard</a> later blamed Alesi's hesitance in lapped traffic for the loss of time,<sup id="cite_ref-pg79_46-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg79-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> the rate of which Henry described as "inexplicable".<sup id="cite_ref-pg201_12-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg201-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Alesi blamed his inability to fend off Schumacher on his worn tyres, which had completed half a race distance as opposed to Schumacher's fresher set, and on being held up by Häkkinen and Brundle's battle for seventh position in the closing laps.<sup id="cite_ref-F1Ypg187_32-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1Ypg187-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Alesi was also given the instruction to save fuel with 15 laps to go, suggesting that his one-stop strategy was the incorrect choice.<sup id="cite_ref-F1Ypg54-55_47-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1Ypg54-55-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 2 November 1995, the FIA announced that none of the drivers who took part in the anti-doping tests at the Portuguese and European Grands Prix had tested positive, including Barrichello and Papis.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> However, FIA Formula One Safety and Medical Delegate, Professor <a href="/wiki/Sid_Watkins" title="Sid Watkins">Sid Watkins</a>, subsequently argued that as ephedrine had no effect on a driver's ability, the sport should not use exactly the same list as the <a href="/wiki/International_Olympic_Committee" title="International Olympic Committee">International Olympic Committee</a> (IOC) in any case.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
