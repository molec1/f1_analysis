<p>The <b>1962 Belgian Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held at <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Spa-Francorchamps" title="Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps">Spa-Francorchamps</a> on 17 June 1962. It was race 3 of 9 in both the <a href="/wiki/1962_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1962 World Championship of Drivers">1962 World Championship of Drivers</a> and the <a href="/wiki/1962_International_Cup_for_Formula_One_Manufacturers" class="mw-redirect" title="1962 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers">1962 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers</a>. This race was notable for being the first grand prix win for <a href="/wiki/Jim_Clark" title="Jim Clark">Jim Clark</a>, and the first of four consecutive victories at Spa for the Scotsman (despite thoroughly disliking the circuit) and <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Team Lotus</a>. It was also the first win for the famous <a href="/wiki/Lotus_25" title="Lotus 25">Lotus 25</a>, and the beginning of the famous 6-year-long rivalry between Clark and <a href="/wiki/Graham_Hill" title="Graham Hill">Graham Hill</a>. This race was held the same day as the <a href="/wiki/1962_FIFA_World_Cup_Final" title="1962 FIFA World Cup Final">1962 FIFA World Cup Final</a> in <a href="/wiki/Santiago" title="Santiago">Santiago</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">Chile</a>, but that event took place later in the day from this Grand Prix.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1962_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Ricardo Rodríguez became the youngest driver to score championship points (20 years, 123 days), a record which stood for 38 years before <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a>, aged 20 years, 67 days, broke it at the <a href="/wiki/2000_Brazilian_Grand_Prix" title="2000 Brazilian Grand Prix">2000 Brazilian Grand Prix</a>.</li>
<li>Last podium for <a href="/wiki/Phil_Hill" title="Phil Hill">Phil Hill</a>.</li>
<li>Trevor Taylor and Willy Mairesse were fighting for 2nd place until the 2 cars touched, crashed into a ditch and Mairesse's car landed upside down and caught fire. Both drivers were thrown out of their cars, but were unhurt.</li>
<li>First Belgian  Grand Prix to allocate odd numbers to vehicles.</li>
<li>First win for a car with a monocoque.</li></ul>
