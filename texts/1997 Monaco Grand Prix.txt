<p>The <b>1997 Monaco Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>LV Grand Prix de Monaco</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> race held on 11 May 1997 at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Monaco" title="Circuit de Monaco">Circuit de Monaco</a>, <a href="/wiki/Monte_Carlo" title="Monte Carlo">Monte Carlo</a>. It was the fifth race of the <a href="/wiki/1997_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1997 Formula One World Championship">1997 Formula One World Championship</a>. The 62-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_F310" title="Ferrari F310">Ferrari</a>, after starting from second position. <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> finished second in a <a href="/wiki/Stewart_SF01" title="Stewart SF01">Stewart</a>-<a href="/wiki/Ford_Motor_Company" title="Ford Motor Company">Ford</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a> third in the other Ferrari.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW19" title="Williams FW19">Williams</a>-<a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>, started from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> ahead of Schumacher. Frentzen and teammate <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a> made poor starts, and both retired from the race in separate accidents. Schumacher won by some 53 seconds from Barrichello, who scored the first podium for the Stewart team in only their fifth Grand Prix. The race had been scheduled for 78 laps, but rainy conditions meant that only 62 laps were run before the two-hour time limit was reached.
</p><p>The win enabled Schumacher to take over the lead of the Drivers' Championship from Villeneuve,<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> and Ferrari to move ahead of Williams in the Constructors' Championship.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1997_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1997_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Heading into the fifth race of the 1997 season, Williams driver <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a> led the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a> with 20 points, followed by Ferrari driver <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> on 14. Behind them were five drivers on 10 points each: Villeneuve's team-mate <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a>, <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> driver <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a>, Schumacher's team-mate <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a>, <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a>, and Coulthard's team-mate <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a>. In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a>, Williams led with 30 points, followed by Ferrari on 24 and McLaren on 20.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1997_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Qualifying saw <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a> qualify on pole in his Williams in front of Michael Schumacher in the Ferrari; third was <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a> in the other Williams. Fourth was <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a>-<a href="/wiki/Peugeot" title="Peugeot">Peugeot</a>, while the fifth place was taken by <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> in the McLaren and the sixth by <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> in the second Jordan, showing the good setup the team had found on the circuit.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1997_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The warm-up session was dry, with Williams taking first and second places. But about 30 minutes to go before the start, rain began to fall; Williams decided to run both cars with dry tyres, thinking the weather would improve, while Michael Schumacher set the car for intermediate weather conditions. During the warm up lap, the weather worsened, and at the start, Schumacher was quickest.<sup id="cite_ref-gprace_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gprace-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He led by 22 seconds on lap 5. Behind him the Jordans, which both had the car set for rain, took second and third spots, until they were both passed by <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Stewart_Grand_Prix" title="Stewart Grand Prix">Stewart</a>, who benefited from the <a href="/wiki/Bridgestone" title="Bridgestone">Bridgestone</a> wet tyres, which were better than <a href="/wiki/Goodyear_Tire_and_Rubber_Company" title="Goodyear Tire and Rubber Company">Goodyear</a>'s under those conditions.
</p><p>The start of the race was catastrophic for the <a href="/wiki/Arrows_Grand_Prix_International" title="Arrows Grand Prix International">Arrows</a> team, as <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Diniz" title="Pedro Diniz">Pedro Diniz</a>, who had opted to start the race on slick tyres, spun out after the hairpin on the opening lap, while his team-mate <a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a> was involved in a collision with <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a>'s Ferrari on the second lap, breaking his suspension. Both McLarens also retired on lap two as <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> hit the wall exiting the tunnel, and <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> ran into the back of <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a> as they passed Coulthard's car.
</p><p>Both Willams drivers went out of the race after they had to pit to change their tyres.  Frentzen hit a barrier at the chicane on lap 39, while Villeneuve hit a wall and had to retire on lap 17. Schumacher continued to build his lead until he had about 30 seconds advantage over Barrichello; then he backed off and began to maintain the gap. He made an error on lap 53 at the Sainte Devote corner, in which he went down the escape road and lost 10 seconds, but did not lose his lead. The race was stopped on lap 62 instead of the 78 scheduled because of the time limit of 2 hours, and Schumacher won with a 53-second margin over Barrichello.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Irvine finished third after overtaking <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Panis" title="Olivier Panis">Olivier Panis</a>, exacting some measure of revenge for Panis' overtaking manoeuvre that had seen him get past Irvine a year earlier. After losing third place to Irvine, Panis backed off in the closing stages and settled for fourth place. <a href="/wiki/Mika_Salo" title="Mika Salo">Mika Salo</a> finished fifth despite denting his front wing on debris left from Häkkinen's accident early on, and also despite not making a single pit stop during the race. This was the last race the feat was achieved. <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a>, who at one point was running as high as second place, finished in sixth. The race was the first win for a Ferrari driver since the <a href="/wiki/1981_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="1981 Monaco Grand Prix">1981 running</a> of the Monaco Grand Prix.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> On the podium, the wrong flag was displayed for driver Eddie Irvine. The flag displayed was that of the Republic of Ireland, instead of the Union Jack of the United Kingdom.
</p>
