<p>The <b>2000 Brazilian Grand Prix</b> (officially the <b>XXIX Grande Prêmio Marlboro do Brasil</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 26 March 2000 at the <a href="/wiki/Aut%C3%B3dromo_Jos%C3%A9_Carlos_Pace" title="Autódromo José Carlos Pace">Autódromo José Carlos Pace</a> in <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo" title="São Paulo">São Paulo</a>, Brazil. 72,000 people attended the race, which was the second round of the <a href="/wiki/2000_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2000 Formula One World Championship">2000 Formula One World Championship</a> and the 29th overall edition of the <a href="/wiki/Brazilian_Grand_Prix" title="Brazilian Grand Prix">event</a>. <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> won the 71-lap race from a third-position start. <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a> team finished in second and <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a> was third.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> of the <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> team took <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> by recording the fastest lap in qualifying. He led on the first lap of the race before Michael Schumacher passed him at the start of lap two. Thereafter, Michael Schumacher opened up a 17.6 second lead before his first of two <a href="/wiki/Pit_stop" title="Pit stop">pit stops</a> for fuel and tyres on lap 20. He retook the lead after Häkkinen retired with a loss of engine oil pressure ten laps later. <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> in the other McLaren gained on Michael Schumacher in the final 12 laps as the latter slowed to manage an <a href="/wiki/Oil_pressure" title="Oil pressure">oil pressure</a> problem. He was not close enough to make a pass for the win and Michael Schumacher took his second consecutive victory of the season, his third in Brazil and the 37th of his career.
</p><p>After the race, Coulthard was disqualified from second place due to an illegal front wing endplate. McLaren filed an appeal to the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile</a>'s International Court of Appeal, which was rejected. The disqualification advanced every driver behind Coulthard by a single position. The result extended Michael Schumacher's lead atop the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a> to 12 <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Championship_points_scoring_systems" title="List of Formula One World Championship points scoring systems">points</a>. Fisichella moved from fifth to second as <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> in the second Ferrari fell to third after not finishing the race. Ferrari further increased their lead in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a> to 18 points as Benetton advanced to second with fifteen races left in the season.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2000_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Aut%C3%B3dromo_Jos%C3%A9_Carlos_Pace,_July_3,_2018_SkySat_(cropped).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/70/Aut%C3%B3dromo_Jos%C3%A9_Carlos_Pace%2C_July_3%2C_2018_SkySat_%28cropped%29.jpg/220px-Aut%C3%B3dromo_Jos%C3%A9_Carlos_Pace%2C_July_3%2C_2018_SkySat_%28cropped%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="181" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/70/Aut%C3%B3dromo_Jos%C3%A9_Carlos_Pace%2C_July_3%2C_2018_SkySat_%28cropped%29.jpg/330px-Aut%C3%B3dromo_Jos%C3%A9_Carlos_Pace%2C_July_3%2C_2018_SkySat_%28cropped%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/70/Aut%C3%B3dromo_Jos%C3%A9_Carlos_Pace%2C_July_3%2C_2018_SkySat_%28cropped%29.jpg/440px-Aut%C3%B3dromo_Jos%C3%A9_Carlos_Pace%2C_July_3%2C_2018_SkySat_%28cropped%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1639" data-file-height="1349" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Aut%C3%B3dromo_Jos%C3%A9_Carlos_Pace,_July_3,_2018_SkySat_(cropped).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The <a href="/wiki/Aut%C3%B3dromo_Jos%C3%A9_Carlos_Pace" title="Autódromo José Carlos Pace">Autódromo José Carlos Pace</a> <i>(pictured in 2018)</i>, where the race was held.</div></div></div>
<p>The 2000 <a href="/wiki/Brazilian_Grand_Prix" title="Brazilian Grand Prix">Brazilian Grand Prix</a> was the second of the 17 motor races in the <a href="/wiki/2000_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2000 Formula One World Championship">2000 Formula One World Championship</a> and the 29th overall edition of the event. It took place at the 15-turn 4.309&#160;km (2.677&#160;mi) <a href="/wiki/Aut%C3%B3dromo_Jos%C3%A9_Carlos_Pace" title="Autódromo José Carlos Pace">Autódromo José Carlos Pace</a> in the Brazilian city of <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo" title="São Paulo">São Paulo</a> on 26 March.<sup id="cite_ref-CF1Brazil2000_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CF1Brazil2000-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-AF1Preview_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1Preview-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The high-altitude, anti-clockwise track had significant elevation changes, placing a heavy load of <a href="/wiki/G-force" title="G-force">g-force</a> on the left-hand side of drivers' necks. Teams altered their cars to run at medium to high levels of <a href="/wiki/Downforce" title="Downforce">downforce</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Damper_(flow)" title="Damper (flow)">dampers</a>, <a href="/wiki/Coil_spring" title="Coil spring">springs</a> and <a href="/wiki/Suspension_(vehicle)" class="mw-redirect" title="Suspension (vehicle)">suspensions</a> were optimised to adapt to the bumpy track surface.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1Preview_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1Preview-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Formula One's control <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_tyre" class="mw-redirect" title="Formula One tyre">tyre</a> supplier <a href="/wiki/Bridgestone" title="Bridgestone">Bridgestone</a> brought the soft and the medium dry compound tyres to the race.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1Preview_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1Preview-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After winning the preceding <a href="/wiki/2000_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="2000 Australian Grand Prix">Australian Grand Prix</a>—the first of the 17 World Championship races—<a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> led the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a> with ten <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Championship_points_scoring_systems" title="List of Formula One World Championship points scoring systems">points</a>; his teammate <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> was in second with six points, and <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> team had four points. <a href="/wiki/British_American_Racing" title="British American Racing">British American Racing</a>'s (BAR) <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a> was fourth with three points and <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> was fifth with two points.<sup id="cite_ref-points_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a> Ferrari led with a maximum amount of 16 points. Williams and BAR tied in third with four points each and Benetton were fourth on four points.<sup id="cite_ref-points_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Prior to the event the majority of the 11 teams conducted sessions at various locations across Europe to test their cars and new components. <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a>, Williams, <a href="/wiki/Jaguar_Racing" title="Jaguar Racing">Jaguar</a> and <a href="/wiki/Prost_Grand_Prix" title="Prost Grand Prix">Prost</a> elected to test at the <a href="/wiki/Silverstone_Circuit" title="Silverstone Circuit">Silverstone Circuit</a> in the United Kingdom from 14 to 16 March. Ferrari test driver <a href="/wiki/Luca_Badoer" title="Luca Badoer">Luca Badoer</a> performed shakedown runs at the <a href="/wiki/Fiorano_Circuit" title="Fiorano Circuit">Fiorano Circuit</a> in northern Italy.<sup id="cite_ref-FIABulletin_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIABulletin-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Panis" title="Olivier Panis">Olivier Panis</a> of the McLaren test team led on the first two days of testing; on day three, Williams' <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> was the fastest driver.<sup id="cite_ref-FIABulletin_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIABulletin-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The BAR, <a href="/wiki/Arrows_Grand_Prix_International" title="Arrows Grand Prix International">Arrows</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a> and <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a> teams did not test before the Grand Prix.<sup id="cite_ref-FIABulletin_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIABulletin-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Johnny_Herbert_at_Goodwood_2014_002.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8e/Johnny_Herbert_at_Goodwood_2014_002.jpg/170px-Johnny_Herbert_at_Goodwood_2014_002.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="214" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8e/Johnny_Herbert_at_Goodwood_2014_002.jpg/255px-Johnny_Herbert_at_Goodwood_2014_002.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8e/Johnny_Herbert_at_Goodwood_2014_002.jpg/340px-Johnny_Herbert_at_Goodwood_2014_002.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1438" data-file-height="1809" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Johnny_Herbert_at_Goodwood_2014_002.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Johnny_Herbert" title="Johnny Herbert">Johnny Herbert</a> <i>(pictured in 2014)</i> criticised the resurfacing of the race track and voiced fears of a major accident occurring.</div></div></div>
<p>The press and bookmakers considered Michael Schumacher the favourite to win the race.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1Preview_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1Preview-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-GuardianBets_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GuardianBets-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In response to a statement by McLaren team principal <a href="/wiki/Ron_Dennis" title="Ron Dennis">Ron Dennis</a>—who claimed Ferrari was not as competitive as McLaren—Schumacher said he was optimistic over a continuing challenge to McLaren for the rest of the season.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> McLaren driver <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> stated he foresaw the team starting on the <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_motorsport_terms#G" title="Glossary of motorsport terms">grid</a>'s front row and winning, after faulty seals on their <a href="/wiki/Pneumatic_valve_springs" title="Pneumatic valve springs">pneumatic valve systems</a> forced him and two-time world champion <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> to retire from the preceding Australian Grand Prix: "We weren't beaten fair and square in the race because we dropped out. You have to give Ferrari credit because they won when we dropped out and that's our mistake. But it means we come here believing we are competitive and still have a chance."<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Over the month of February, the Autódromo José Carlos Pace was resurfaced in an attempt to reduce its bumpiness.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1Preview_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1Preview-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The pit lane exit was moved from the entry of the Senna S <a href="/wiki/Chicane" title="Chicane">chicane</a> to the Repa Opposta <a href="/wiki/Straight_(racing)" title="Straight (racing)">straight</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> A larger run-off area was installed to the outside of Laranjinha turn and its concrete barrier was padded with tyres, after <a href="/wiki/Ricardo_Zonta" title="Ricardo Zonta">Ricardo Zonta</a> had crashed and been injured during <a href="/wiki/1999_Brazilian_Grand_Prix" title="1999 Brazilian Grand Prix">the 1999 race</a>. <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Whiting" title="Charlie Whiting">Charlie Whiting</a>, the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile</a> (FIA; Formula One's governing body) technical delegate, inspected the circuit and ordered the pit lane barriers to be moved for better driver access.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The drivers had mixed feelings over the resurfacing work. <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a> of the Jaguar team criticised the track's condition.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> His teammate <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Herbert" title="Johnny Herbert">Johnny Herbert</a> described the bumps approaching the start/finish straight as "horrendous" and raised concerns over a repeat of an accident sustained by <a href="/wiki/St%C3%A9phane_Sarrazin" title="Stéphane Sarrazin">Stéphane Sarrazin</a> in 1999.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello and his teammate Michael Schumacher, on the other hand, said the track was better for them.<sup id="cite_ref-FIAFriday_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAFriday-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Several teams altered their cars, either to refine their aerodynamic appendages or to solve reliability problems that emerged during the Australian Grand Prix.<sup id="cite_ref-Piola_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Piola-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> McLaren identified an <a href="/wiki/Air_filter" title="Air filter">air filter</a> failure that had the pneumatic valve system problem in the previous race, and modified its design to prevent a recurrence.<sup id="cite_ref-Piola_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Piola-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Cosworth" title="Cosworth">Ford-Cosworth</a> modified its engine lubrication system and the Arrows team altered the design of the <a href="/wiki/Steering_linkage" title="Steering linkage">steering linkage</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Piola_15-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Piola-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> The FIA granted Arrows permission to enter the race after changing the design of the headrests to protect the driver in its <a href="/wiki/Arrows_A21" title="Arrows A21">A21</a> car.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice">Practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2000_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Four practice sessions were held before the Sunday race, two on Friday, and two on Saturday. The Friday morning and afternoon sessions each lasted an hour. The third and final practice sessions on Saturday morning lasted 45 minutes.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> The weather was hot and clear for the Friday sessions.<sup id="cite_ref-FIAFriday_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAFriday-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello made minor changes to the <a href="/wiki/Racing_setup" title="Racing setup">aerodynamic setup</a> of his car before the first session,<sup id="cite_ref-FIAFriday_14-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAFriday-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> and was fastest with a 1-minute, 17.631 seconds lap. Häkkinen, Coulthard, Michael Schumacher, Villeneuve, Prost's <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a>, Fisichella, <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a> of the Jordan team, Sauber driver <a href="/wiki/Mika_Salo" title="Mika Salo">Mika Salo</a> and Arrows' <a href="/wiki/Jos_Verstappen" title="Jos Verstappen">Jos Verstappen</a> rounded out the session's top ten drivers.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1FP1_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1FP1-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> During the session, Alesi spun three times and removed the front wing on the third occurrence.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1FP1_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1FP1-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher's session ended early after eight laps due to a <a href="/wiki/Drive_shaft" title="Drive shaft">driveshaft</a> joint leak and Zonta was restricted to four timed laps after debris accumulation overheated his engine.<sup id="cite_ref-FIAFriday_14-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAFriday-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>With three minutes remaining in the second Friday practice session, Häkkinen bettered his 1999 <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> lap by six-tenths of a second and set the day's fastest lap of 1 minute, 15.896 seconds.<sup id="cite_ref-FIAFriday_14-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAFriday-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher was second-fastest. Coulthard, Barrichello, <a href="/wiki/Pedro_de_la_Rosa" title="Pedro de la Rosa">Pedro de la Rosa</a>'s Arrows, Alesi, Verstappen, Jordan's <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a>, Villeneuve and Fisichella took third through tenth.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> A brake and balance problem twice put Ralf Schumacher off the track. <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Wurz" title="Alexander Wurz">Alexander Wurz</a> spun and beached his Benetton in a gravel trap at Mergulho corner.<sup id="cite_ref-FIAFriday_14-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAFriday-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard spent part of the session in the pit lane as McLaren replaced his front wing after he went into the grass midway through. Verstappen tried a tyre compound that affected his car's setup and put him into a gravel trap at the end of practice.<sup id="cite_ref-FIAFriday_14-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAFriday-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It continued to be hot and dry for Saturday morning's two practice sessions.<sup id="cite_ref-FIASaturday_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIASaturday-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Teams tested different <a href="/wiki/Ride_height" title="Ride height">ride height</a> setups on their cars, creating a noticeable difference in performance. They also selected the tyre compounds to use for the rest of the weekend.<sup id="cite_ref-F1COMSatFP_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1COMSatFP-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard led the third practice session with a lap of 1 minute, 15.035 seconds, followed by his teammate Häkkinen, the Ferrari pair of Michael Schumacher and Barrichello, Frentzen, Fisichella, Trulli, Irvine, Minardi's <a href="/wiki/Marc_Gen%C3%A9" title="Marc Gené">Marc Gené</a> and Alesi.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> During the session, an engine problem for Michael Schumacher prompted his team to spend an hour and ten minutes changing it.<sup id="cite_ref-FIASaturday_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIASaturday-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> An oil leak in <a href="/wiki/Gast%C3%B3n_Mazzacane" title="Gastón Mazzacane">Gastón Mazzacane</a>'s Minardi restricted him to five timed laps, and prevented his participation in the final practice session later that day.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1SatFP_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1SatFP-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Hakkinen led the fourth session with a 1-minute, 14.159 seconds time on his final lap on a new set of tyres, with his teammate Coulthard third.<sup id="cite_ref-FIASaturday_21-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIASaturday-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> They were separated by Barrichello. His teammate Michael Schumacher was fourth. Trulli was fifth and Fisichella duplicated his third practice result in sixth. Villeneuve, Verstappen, Ralf Schumacher and Frentzen completed the top ten.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1SatFP_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1SatFP-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello spun twice as he tested a new <a href="/wiki/Spoiler_(car)" title="Spoiler (car)">rear wing</a>. Frentzen and his teammate Trulli ran into a gravel trap and both sustained minor bodywork damage to their cars.<sup id="cite_ref-FIASaturday_21-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIASaturday-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Zonta lost track time due to a <a href="/wiki/Transmission_(mechanics)" title="Transmission (mechanics)">gearbox actuator</a> fault and ran into a gravel trap.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1SatFP_24-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1SatFP-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Button's engine failed, laying oil on the <a href="/wiki/Racing_line" title="Racing line">racing line</a> between the Laranjinha and Bico do Pato corners. Salo's rear wing failed on the start/finish straight; he spun as he braked before the Senna S chicane and crashed into the wall,<sup id="cite_ref-FIASaturday_21-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIASaturday-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-F1COMSatFP_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1COMSatFP-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> but was unhurt.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1SatFP_24-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1SatFP-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2000_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Mika_Haekkinen_2006.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/53/Mika_Haekkinen_2006.jpg/170px-Mika_Haekkinen_2006.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="230" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/53/Mika_Haekkinen_2006.jpg/255px-Mika_Haekkinen_2006.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/53/Mika_Haekkinen_2006.jpg/340px-Mika_Haekkinen_2006.jpg 2x" data-file-width="549" data-file-height="743" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Mika_Haekkinen_2006.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> <i>(pictured in 2006)</i> took the 23rd <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> of his career as a result of recording the fastest lap in qualifying.</div></div></div>
<p>Saturday afternoon's one-hour qualifying session saw drivers circulate the track simultaneously. Each driver was limited to twelve laps; their fastest laps would determine the starting order for the race. During this session the <a href="/wiki/107%25_rule" title="107% rule">107% rule</a> was in effect, requiring each driver to remain within 107 per cent of the fastest lap to qualify for the race.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Heavy rain fell in the session's final &#x200b;<span class="frac nowrap"><sup>1</sup>&#8260;<sub>4</sub></span> hour and the resulting slippery track prevented drivers from improving their best laps.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1Quali_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1Quali-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> The session was <a href="/wiki/Racing_flags#Red_flag" title="Racing flags">stopped</a> three times because a heavy wind caused a 6&#160;ft (1.8&#160;m) gantry-mounted cardboard advertising <a href="/wiki/Billboard" title="Billboard">hoarding</a>, joined together by nylon ties above the start/finish straight, to detach and fall 10&#160;m (33&#160;ft) onto the track.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> Häkkinen went fastest with 15 minutes to go before the rain fell with a 1-minute, 14.111 seconds lap,<sup id="cite_ref-AF1Quali_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1Quali-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-AF1BrazilGPQ_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1BrazilGPQ-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> earning his second consecutive pole position,<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> his third in Brazil and the 23rd of his career.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1BrazilGPQ_28-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1BrazilGPQ-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Häkkinen's teammate Coulthard was second and the Ferrari duo of Michael Schumacher and Barrichello took third and fourth;<sup id="cite_ref-AF1Quali_25-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1Quali-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-SVQuali_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SVQuali-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher <a href="/wiki/Understeer_and_oversteer" title="Understeer and oversteer">understeered</a> into the grass on his second timed lap,<sup id="cite_ref-GPCOMBrazil00_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPCOMBrazil00-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-AutosportQuali_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportQuali-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> damaging his car's undertray on the corrugations of a high-mounted <a href="/wiki/Curb" title="Curb">kerb</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1SatFP_24-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1SatFP-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> He drove the spare Ferrari for the rest of qualifying.<sup id="cite_ref-FIASaturday_21-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIASaturday-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello had to abort a fast lap due to the stoppage.<sup id="cite_ref-SVQuali_30-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SVQuali-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Fisichella, fifth, praised his car's handling. Irvine in sixth was happy with his Jaguar's <a href="/wiki/Grip_(auto_racing)" title="Grip (auto racing)">grip</a> and balance.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1BrazilGPQ_28-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1BrazilGPQ-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Frentzen qualified seventh after <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Diniz" title="Pedro Diniz">Pedro Diniz</a> slowed him. Zonta, eighth, focused on aerodynamic setup to improve his car's handling.<sup id="cite_ref-FIASaturday_21-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIASaturday-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-AF1BrazilGPQ_28-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1BrazilGPQ-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Button was fourth early on before falling to ninth.<sup id="cite_ref-GPCOMBrazil00_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPCOMBrazil00-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Villeneuve qualified in tenth as traffic slowed two of his timed laps and he ran onto the grass. Ralf Schumacher was the fastest driver not to qualify in the top ten after different spring rates failed to improve the setup of his chassis.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1BrazilGPQ_28-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1BrazilGPQ-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Trulli spun into a gravel trap at the Bico de Peto <a href="/wiki/Hairpin_turn" title="Hairpin turn">hairpin</a> on his first timed lap.<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportQuali_31-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportQuali-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> He returned to the pit lane to drive the spare Jordan car; an <a href="/wiki/Electronic_control_unit" title="Electronic control unit">electronic control unit</a> fault affected its gear selection and left him 12th. Traffic slowed all of Wurz's laps and left him in 13th. <a href="/wiki/Clutch" title="Clutch">Clutch</a> issues left Verstappen in 14th.<sup id="cite_ref-FIASaturday_21-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIASaturday-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-GPCOMBrazil00_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPCOMBrazil00-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Alesi, 15th, swerved at more than 250&#160;km/h (160&#160;mph) to avoid the falling advertising hoarding; it damaged his car's front wing.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1Quali_25-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1Quali-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> De La Rosa in 16th was slowed by the rain. Car setup issues put Herbert 17th.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1BrazilGPQ_28-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1BrazilGPQ-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Gené in 18th ventured onto the track early in the session. <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> used the spare Prost car to secure 19th due to a clutch failure in his race car.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1Quali_25-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1Quali-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Diniz in 20th had a similar rear wing failure to his teammate Salo in 22nd.<sup id="cite_ref-GPCOMBrazil00_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPCOMBrazil00-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Pressure_regulator" title="Pressure regulator">Fuel pressure</a> problems led Mazzacane to drive the spare Minardi car; he separated the Sauber duo in 21st.<sup id="cite_ref-FIASaturday_21-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIASaturday-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-AF1Quali_25-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1Quali-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-qualifying">Post-qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2000_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Post-qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>During the second stoppage, Sauber announced its withdrawal from the race, citing a lack of time in Brazil to analyse and rectify the structural integrity of the rear wings on both their cars.<sup id="cite_ref-SVQuali_30-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SVQuali-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> The team returned to their <a href="/wiki/Hinwil" title="Hinwil">Hinwil</a> headquarters and found the track's bumpy surface resulted in both of their cars having higher than anticipated impact loads; Salo had a failure of the lower plane on his rear wing and Diniz's failed on the upper plane.<sup id="cite_ref-SauberBumps_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SauberBumps-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Warm-up">Warm-up</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2000_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Warm-up">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A 30-minute warm-up session was held on Sunday morning in hot and overcast weather.<sup id="cite_ref-FIAWarmUp_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAWarmUp-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> All drivers fine-tuned their race set-ups and drove their <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_motorsport_terms#S" title="Glossary of motorsport terms">spare cars</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FIAWarmUp_38-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAWarmUp-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> Häkkinen set the session's fastest lap at 1 minute, 16.343 seconds, ahead of Michael Schumacher, Coulthard, Barrichello, Verstappen, Ralf Schumacher, Gené, Fisichella and Zonta in positions two to ten.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> Ten minutes in,<sup id="cite_ref-F1NetWarmUp_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1NetWarmUp-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> Wurz <a href="/wiki/Stall_(engine)" title="Stall (engine)">stalled</a> his car at the exit of the pit lane, and the session was stopped. He required the spare <a href="/wiki/Benetton_B200" title="Benetton B200">Benetton B200</a> car for the rest of the session.<sup id="cite_ref-FIAWarmUp_38-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAWarmUp-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> Alesi's engine failed and he switched to the spare <a href="/wiki/Prost_AP03" title="Prost AP03">Prost AP03</a>, which had a rear wing failure on the bumpy start/finish straight, and caused a second stoppage.<sup id="cite_ref-FIAWarmUp_38-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAWarmUp-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-F1NetWarmUp_40-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1NetWarmUp-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After the warm-up session, and before the race, Prost examined Alesi's rear wing and found that its failure was due to older bodywork parts fitted to his car and readied its spare chassis if required. The team stated they were unconcerned over the safety of their drivers and confirmed their participation in the event.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2000_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The race began at 14:00 local time.<sup id="cite_ref-GF1Race_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF1Race-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> The weather at the start was hot and dry with the ambient temperature 22&#160;°C (72&#160;°F), the track temperature 35&#160;°C (95&#160;°F), and the humidity at 76 per cent.<sup id="cite_ref-GF1Race_42-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF1Race-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-F1COMBrazilRace_43-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1COMBrazilRace-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> Approximately 72,000 spectators attended the race.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> On the <a href="/wiki/Parade_lap" title="Parade lap">formation lap</a>, Wurz stalled his engine,<sup id="cite_ref-DF1RaceQuotes_45-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DF1RaceQuotes-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> and his stationary car delayed Alesi and Herbert; both drivers took up their starting positions.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1Report_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1Report-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Wurz began the event from the pit lane.<sup id="cite_ref-FIARace_46-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIARace-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> Häkkinen made a brisk start from the grid to lead the field going into the Senna S chicane.<sup id="cite_ref-MCOMRace_47-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MCOMRace-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard, in second, had <a href="/wiki/Wheelspin" title="Wheelspin">wheelspin</a> and was passed by Michael Schumacher, who unsuccessfully challenged Häkkinen for the lead. Coulthard retained third as Barrichello made a slow start.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1Report_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1Report-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Behind them, Irvine overtook Fisichella for fifth. Verstappen moved from 14th to 11th by the conclusion of the first lap; Button dropped from 9th to 13th over the same distance.<sup id="cite_ref-FIARace_46-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIARace-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> Trulli passed Ralf Schumacher and Villeneuve to progress into tenth on the left of the circuit.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1Report_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1Report-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Giancarlo_Fisichella_2012_WEC_Fuji.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e2/Giancarlo_Fisichella_2012_WEC_Fuji.jpg/170px-Giancarlo_Fisichella_2012_WEC_Fuji.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="255" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e2/Giancarlo_Fisichella_2012_WEC_Fuji.jpg/255px-Giancarlo_Fisichella_2012_WEC_Fuji.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e2/Giancarlo_Fisichella_2012_WEC_Fuji.jpg/340px-Giancarlo_Fisichella_2012_WEC_Fuji.jpg 2x" data-file-width="800" data-file-height="1200" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Giancarlo_Fisichella_2012_WEC_Fuji.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> <i>(pictured in 2012)</i> earned his first podium finish of the season in second.</div></div></div>
<p>As the first two drivers reached the start/finish line,<sup id="cite_ref-MCOMRace_47-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MCOMRace-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher, who was close behind Häkkinen throughout lap one,<sup id="cite_ref-F1RacingNetRace_48-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1RacingNetRace-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> steered off the racing line to pass the latter for the lead heading into the Senna S chicane.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1Report_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1Report-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello <a href="/wiki/Drafting_(aerodynamics)" title="Drafting (aerodynamics)">slipstreamed</a> Coulthard on the start/finish straight and overtook him for third.<sup id="cite_ref-F1RacingNetRace_48-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1RacingNetRace-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> He then ran wide and Coulthard retook third. Barrichello retook the position by the end of lap two as Coulthard lost the use of the first three gears due to a gearbox fault and was slower in the slow-speed corners.<sup id="cite_ref-GPCOMBrazil00_3-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPCOMBrazil00-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> At the front, Michael Schumacher extended his lead over Häkkinen to four seconds by lap four and to 15 seconds by lap 15 with a series of <a href="/wiki/Fastest_lap" title="Fastest lap">fastest laps</a>. In the meantime, Trulli passed Zonta for eighth, Button overtook Alesi for 13th and Heidfeld lost 16th to de la Rosa. Verstappen progressed from 11th to seventh by passing Villeneuve, Zonta, Frentzen and Fisichella as Alesi overtook Ralf Schumacher, Villeneuve, Zonta and Frentzen to advance to ninth.<sup id="cite_ref-FIARace_46-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIARace-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> Three drivers retired from the race during this period: Wurz and Heidfeld had separate engine failures on lap seven and nine and Alesi stopped with an electrical failure at the Bico de Pato hairpin on lap 11.<sup id="cite_ref-F1COMBrazilRace_43-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1COMBrazilRace-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-AF1Report_2-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1Report-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At the end of lap 14, Barrichello drew close to Häkkinen after the latter ran wide. He slipstreamed Häkkinen and turned left to pass him for second as he braked before the Senna S chicane to start the 15th lap.<sup id="cite_ref-F1COMBrazilRace_43-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1COMBrazilRace-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-AF1Report_2-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1Report-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-F1RacingNetRace_48-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1RacingNetRace-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> De la Rosa passed Herbert for 14th on that lap and Trulli overtook Irvine for fifth on lap 16.<sup id="cite_ref-FIARace_46-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIARace-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> That lap, Villeneuve retired with a race-long gearbox problem. The Ferrari and McLaren teams employed different pit stop strategies – the Ferrari team planned for two stops whereas the McLaren squad scheduled one stop.<sup id="cite_ref-GPCOMBrazil00_3-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPCOMBrazil00-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher led by 17.6 seconds over the rest of the field when he commenced the first round of pit stops for fuel and tyres four laps later.<sup id="cite_ref-GF1Race_42-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF1Race-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> He rejoined the track in third and Barrichello led the next two laps until his stop.<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportLapByLap_49-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportLapByLap-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> On lap 21, Irvine lost control of the rear of his car entering the Bico de Pato hairpin and crashed into a tyre barrier. Six laps later, Barrichello entered the pit lane with a <a href="/wiki/Hydraulic_motor" title="Hydraulic motor">hydraulic motor</a> problem that spread from the <a href="/wiki/Steering_wheel" title="Steering wheel">steering wheel</a> to the throttle linkage.<sup id="cite_ref-F1COMBrazilRace_43-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1COMBrazilRace-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-DF1RaceQuotes_45-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DF1RaceQuotes-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> As Barrichello exited the car to retire a small fire was extinguished by his mechanics.<sup id="cite_ref-MCOMRace_47-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MCOMRace-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Michael_Schumacher_01_(sk).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/53/Michael_Schumacher_01_%28sk%29.jpg/170px-Michael_Schumacher_01_%28sk%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="185" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/53/Michael_Schumacher_01_%28sk%29.jpg/255px-Michael_Schumacher_01_%28sk%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/53/Michael_Schumacher_01_%28sk%29.jpg/340px-Michael_Schumacher_01_%28sk%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="991" data-file-height="1077" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Michael_Schumacher_01_(sk).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> <i>(pictured in 2007)</i> took his second win of the season and 37th of his career.</div></div></div>
<p>Trulli made the first of two stops from fourth at the end of the 28th lap and he emerged in seventh.<sup id="cite_ref-FIARace_46-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIARace-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> At the front of the field, Häkkinen pulled away to lead Michael Schumacher by 12 seconds until he slowed with a loss of oil engine pressure on the 30th lap.<sup id="cite_ref-DF1RaceQuotes_45-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DF1RaceQuotes-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-AutosportLapByLap_49-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportLapByLap-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> He slowed and retired in his garage. Michael Schumacher retook the lead, with the yet-to-stop Coulthard second, Verstappen third and Fisichella fourth.<sup id="cite_ref-FIARace_46-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIARace-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard lapped to within a tenth of a second of Michael Schumacher as the latter slowed slightly due to an imbalance in all four of his tyres as Ralf Schumacher challenged Fisichella for fourth.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1Report_2-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1Report-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-MCOMRace_47-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MCOMRace-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-F1RacingNetRace_48-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1RacingNetRace-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> On lap 32, Gené retired from eleventh with an engine failure.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1Report_2-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1Report-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Verstappen and his teammate de la Rosa made their first pit stops on laps 35 and 37. Four laps later, Frentzen was the first driver on a one-stop strategy to enter the pit lane.<sup id="cite_ref-FIARace_46-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIARace-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard made his only stop on lap 43 and remained in second position. Michael Schumacher led by 48 seconds when he made his second pit stop on the 51st lap and retained the lead.<sup id="cite_ref-F1RacingNetRace_48-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1RacingNetRace-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> On the same lap, Fisichella made his only stop and kept third place.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1Report_2-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1Report-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On lap 51, Herbert retired in the pit lane with a gearbox failure.<sup id="cite_ref-GPCOMBrazil00_3-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPCOMBrazil00-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> De la Rosa lost concentration and went into a gravel trap four laps later. He rejoined without losing position.<sup id="cite_ref-F1COMBrazilRace_43-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1COMBrazilRace-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-F1RacingNetRace_48-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1RacingNetRace-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> Button battled Verstappen and overtook him for seventh on the 56th lap as Trulli made a pit stop from fourth and rejoined the track in fifth.<sup id="cite_ref-FIARace_46-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIARace-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> From the 59th lap, Coulthard began to gain on Michael Schumacher, who slowed due to an <a href="/wiki/Oil_pressure" title="Oil pressure">oil pressure</a> problem.<sup id="cite_ref-GPCOMBrazil00_3-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPCOMBrazil00-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FIARace_46-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIARace-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> He could not draw close enough to pass, and Schumacher took his second victory of the season, his third in Brazil and the 37th of his career in a time of 1 hour, 31 minutes, 35.271 seconds, averaging 200.404&#160;km/h (124.525&#160;mph) over a distance of 305.939&#160;km (190.102&#160;mi) and 71 laps.<sup id="cite_ref-CF1Brazil2000_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CF1Brazil2000-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-CollingsReport_51-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CollingsReport-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard followed 4.302 seconds later and Fisichella took third. Frentzen finished fourth, Trulli fifth and Ralf Schumacher sixth. Button, Verstappen, de la Rosa, Zonta and Mazzacane were the final finishers.<sup id="cite_ref-GPCOMBrazil00_3-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPCOMBrazil00-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The attrition rate was high; only 11 of the 20 starters finished the race.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2000_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The top three drivers appeared on the podium to collect their trophies and spoke to the media in a later press conference.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_17-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher stated that pit stop strategy helped him to win the race: "In the past, as you may remember, we could not overtake the McLaren entries, regardless of their strategy. Now we are looking a lot more competitive – which is where we wanted to be – and the season could hardly have started better than it has."<sup id="cite_ref-PressConf_53-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PressConf-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard said his second-place finish provided him the start to his championship campaign after his gearbox problems: "It must have been entertaining for those who had places to watch at the first corner. Given the circumstances, I am very happy with my six points."<sup id="cite_ref-PressConf_53-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PressConf-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> Fisichella revealed that his car had understeer and oversteer for the first 15 laps until its grip and balance improved. Nonetheless, he said he was pleased to finish third, "This is a great result and will help us to be even more competitive in the future. I am confident about the next race, when we will have some new aerodynamic parts."<sup id="cite_ref-PressConf_53-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PressConf-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:David_Coulthard_2007.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d8/David_Coulthard_2007.jpg/170px-David_Coulthard_2007.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="250" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d8/David_Coulthard_2007.jpg/255px-David_Coulthard_2007.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d8/David_Coulthard_2007.jpg/340px-David_Coulthard_2007.jpg 2x" data-file-width="500" data-file-height="734" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:David_Coulthard_2007.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> <i>(pictured in 2007)</i> was disqualified from second place due to an illegal front wing endplate on his car.</div></div></div>
<p>All of the top six finishers, except for Fisichella, were found to have excess wear on the wooden planks underneath their cars, in violation of a regulation concerning illegal plank wear. The Ferrari, McLaren, Jordan and Williams teams filed a successful appeal, claiming that the bumpy racing surface caused the wear. All four teams were reinstated after a second inspection caused a &#x200b;<span class="frac nowrap">3<span class="visualhide">&#160;</span><sup>1</sup>&#8260;<sub>2</sub></span> hour delay.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1Report_2-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1Report-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup> During the inspection, the front wing endplates on Coulthard's car were found to be 43&#160;mm (4.3&#160;cm) above the ground and not 50&#160;mm (5.0&#160;cm) as stated in the regulations<i>.</i><sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Jo_Bauer" title="Jo Bauer">Jo Bauer</a>, the FIA technical delegate, deemed this to have provided Coulthard with a competitive aerodynamic advantage and the stewards disqualified the driver six hours after the race. The technical director of McLaren <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Newey" title="Adrian Newey">Adrian Newey</a> rejected an offer from the stewards to transport Coulthard's car to Paris for a fair hearing and agreed to a set of four measurements in São Paulo. McLaren iled an appeal, saying the car's undertray and chassis was damaged and shifted by vibrations from the bumps on the racing surface.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup> On 4 April, the appeal was heard by a five-man panel at a meeting of the FIA International Court of Appeal in Paris. They rejected McLaren's appeal in the 90-minute hearing and declared the result of the race final.<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>This promoted every driver behind Coulthard one position; Button was reclassified sixth and became the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_driver_records#Youngest_drivers_to_score_points" title="List of Formula One driver records">youngest driver to score a Formula One World Championship point</a> at the age of 20 years, 2 months, 7 days old, breaking <a href="/wiki/Ricardo_Rodr%C3%ADguez_(racing_driver)" title="Ricardo Rodríguez (racing driver)">Ricardo Rodríguez</a>'s record from the <a href="/wiki/1962_Belgian_Grand_Prix" title="1962 Belgian Grand Prix">1962 Belgian Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">&#91;n 1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup> On 6 April, the organisers of the Brazilian Grand Prix were summoned to a meeting of the FIA General Assembly. They were fined $100,000 for the track's safety issues and for the three times qualifying was stopped for falling advertising hoardings. The governing body ruled it as "exceptional circumstances" and allowed the track to remain in Formula One.<sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup> The president of the FIA <a href="/wiki/Max_Mosley" title="Max Mosley">Max Mosley</a> said an increased fine or cancelling the race was not imposed because its organisers had submitted evidence to prevent either action from occurring, "Bearing in mind that we allowed the Brazilian promoters to place the signs there – and they probably didn't know what potential there was for the failures which occurred – it seemed only fair to impose the comparatively modest penalty."<sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Häkkinen spoke of his disappointment over retiring from the lead of the race, "Nothing can describe how I feel, We have been quick all weekend, right the way through, so I am not happy to be leaving Brazil without any points. We have some work to do before the start of the European season."<sup id="cite_ref-CollingsReport_51-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CollingsReport-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> Button was quoted in the press as saying he preferred to score points in the race and not in a court of appeal, "I heard the news about David as I was making my way to the airport to fly back from Brazil – and I can't say it gave me any great feeling of joy. You want to earn any success through your driving skills, not someone else's misfortune."<sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65">&#91;64&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Jordan" title="Eddie Jordan">Eddie Jordan</a>, the owner of the Jordan team, said that both his cars finishing the event demonstrated they could last a full race distance after retiring from the Australian Grand Prix with mechanical issues. Frentzen stated it was as if the team's season had commenced in Brazil and required maintenance to improve their performance, "But we are a very strong team with a good atmosphere, I get along well with Jarno and everything is fine so far."<sup id="cite_ref-66" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-66">&#91;65&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The gap in the Drivers' Championship after the race stood at 12 points in favour of Michael Schumacher with Fisichella moving from fifth to second as a result of his second-place finish. Barrichello dropped to third and Ralf Schumacher fell to fourth. Frentzen rounded out the top five.<sup id="cite_ref-points_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In the Constructors' Championship, Ferrari moved further ahead with 26 points and the Benetton team progressed from fourth to second. Jordan's first points of the season put them third while Williams and BAR were fourth and fifth with fifteen races left in the season.<sup id="cite_ref-points_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
