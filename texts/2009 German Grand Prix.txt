<p>The <b>2009 German Grand Prix</b> was the ninth race of the <a href="/wiki/2009_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2009 Formula One season">2009 Formula One season</a>. It was held on 12 July 2009 at the <a href="/wiki/N%C3%BCrburgring" title="Nürburgring">Nürburgring</a> in <a href="/wiki/N%C3%BCrburg" title="Nürburg">Nürburg</a>, Germany; the earliest German Grand Prix on the calendar (a feat since beat by the <a href="/wiki/2013_German_Grand_Prix" title="2013 German Grand Prix">2013 race</a>), since <a href="/wiki/1926_German_Grand_Prix" title="1926 German Grand Prix">1926</a>.
</p><p>Australian <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> took both his first pole position and his first Grand Prix win, with Sebastian Vettel coming in second, giving Red Bull their third 1-2 of 2009. Championship leader <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> could only manage fifth. This was the last race for <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso</a> driver <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9bastien_Bourdais" title="Sébastien Bourdais">Sébastien Bourdais</a> who was dropped in favour of test driver <a href="/wiki/Jaime_Alguersuari" title="Jaime Alguersuari">Jaime Alguersuari</a> for the <a href="/wiki/2009_Hungarian_Grand_Prix" title="2009 Hungarian Grand Prix">Hungarian Grand Prix</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2009_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2009_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the lead-up to the race, despite 6th place at the <a href="/wiki/2009_British_Grand_Prix" title="2009 British Grand Prix">British Grand Prix</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> maintained a 23-point lead over <a href="/wiki/Brawn_GP" title="Brawn GP">Brawn GP</a> teammate <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> in the Drivers' Championship. Barrichello was 2 points further in front of <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a>, winner of the British Grand Prix.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Brawn_GP" title="Brawn GP">Brawn GP</a> had a sizeable lead of 30.5 points in the Constructors' Championship with Red Bull Racing in second place. <a href="/wiki/Toyota_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Toyota F1">Toyota</a> were 30 points behind the Austrian Red Bull team, with only <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a> yet to score a point. The three former World Champions in the F1 field, <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> were 9th, 10th and 11th in the Championship, with 11, 10 and 9 points respectively.
</p><p>2008 champion <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> won the <a href="/wiki/2008_German_Grand_Prix" title="2008 German Grand Prix">2008 German Grand Prix</a> for <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_HighPerformanceEngines" class="mw-redirect" title="Mercedes-Benz HighPerformanceEngines">Mercedes</a>, at <a href="/wiki/Hockenheimring" title="Hockenheimring">Hockenheim</a>. Former <a href="/wiki/German_Grand_Prix" title="German Grand Prix">German Grand Prix</a> winners in the field for 2009 include <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> of <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> of <a href="/wiki/Brawn_GP" title="Brawn GP">Brawn GP</a>. Barrichello and Alonso have also been successful at the Nürburgring when the circuit hosted the <a href="/wiki/European_Grand_Prix" title="European Grand Prix">European Grand Prix</a>.
</p><p>The Nürburgring has the unique honour of hosting three different Formula One Grands Prix (see <a href="/wiki/List_of_races_at_the_N%C3%BCrburgring" title="List of races at the Nürburgring">List of races at the Nürburgring</a>): the <a href="/wiki/German_Grand_Prix" title="German Grand Prix">German Grand Prix</a> on the old track in the 1950s, 60s and 70s as well as on the GP track in <a href="/wiki/1985_German_Grand_Prix" title="1985 German Grand Prix">1985</a> and on the revised circuit in 2009; the <a href="/wiki/European_Grand_Prix" title="European Grand Prix">European Grand Prix</a> in <a href="/wiki/1984_European_Grand_Prix" title="1984 European Grand Prix">1984</a> and from 1995 to 2007, with the exception of <a href="/wiki/1997_Luxembourg_Grand_Prix" title="1997 Luxembourg Grand Prix">1997</a> and <a href="/wiki/1998_Luxembourg_Grand_Prix" title="1998 Luxembourg Grand Prix">1998</a>, when it was the <a href="/wiki/Luxembourg_Grand_Prix" title="Luxembourg Grand Prix">Luxembourg Grand Prix</a>. <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a>, <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> have raced all three at the circuit.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice_and_Qualifying">Practice and Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2009_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Practice and Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Qualifying saw drivers switch strategies to using wet weather tyres due to the rain and wet track. The track was dry as the drivers headed out for the first session. This yielded no surprises as the two <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rossos</a> of <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9bastien_Buemi" title="Sébastien Buemi">Sébastien Buemi</a> and <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9bastien_Bourdais" title="Sébastien Bourdais">Sébastien Bourdais</a> were eliminated along with <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a>, and the consistently underperforming <a href="/wiki/Toyota_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Toyota F1">Toyota</a> of <a href="/wiki/Timo_Glock" title="Timo Glock">Timo Glock</a> and <a href="/wiki/BMW_Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="BMW Sauber">BMW Sauber</a> of <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a>. Glock was later given a three place grid drop for blocking <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault's</a> <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>, but since he qualified 19th anyway this sent him to the back of the grid.
</p><p>In the second session, the rain began to fall. This gave us some more interesting results, with both <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a> cars going out. Both <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kazuki_Nakajima" title="Kazuki Nakajima">Kazuki Nakajima</a> said that they would have done better had it not been for the rain. They were joined in elimination by Alonso, <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a>'s Toyota and <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a>, who did well to qualify 11th in the other BMW Sauber. The German claimed he could have done even better, but the rain upset his strategy as well.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a> in the other Renault made the final session for the first time in 2009, but only managed 10th as the rain eased off again in the last ten minutes. The <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferraris</a> of <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> were disappointing, only managing 8th and 9th respectively. Räikkönen's race engineer <a href="/wiki/Chris_Dyer" title="Chris Dyer">Chris Dyer</a> said that they didn't have any soft tyres left for the last qualifying lap, which cost them very dear. <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a> did excellently in the Force India to qualify not only in the top 10 for the first time, but ahead of the Ferraris in 7th. The <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLarens</a> of <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> came 5th and 6th respectively, and both thanked the upgrades to the car for their improved performances.
</p><p>Then came the fight for pole, which was solely between the four championship contenders - the <a href="/wiki/Brawn_GP" title="Brawn GP">Brawns</a> of <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bulls</a> of <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a>. Webber was the first to cross the line in 1:32.230. Then Vettel did well to do 1:32.480, but it was a surprise for him to be beaten by his teammate. This looked to be the order, but Barrichello and Button both crossed the line in the dying seconds to split the Red Bulls, so Webber claimed the first pole position of his career. He described it as a "special day".<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2009_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> were side by side coming to the first corner where Webber clashed wheels with the Brazilian, for which he later received a drive-through penalty. The <a href="/wiki/Brawn_GP" title="Brawn GP">Brawn</a> driver still managed to jump the <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a> driver to take the lead. <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> also made a fast start in the <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> and challenged for the lead, but he made slight contact with Webber at the first corner and received a puncture, ending his chance for points. He lost a lap in the pits, ultimately finishing 18th, still one lap down. <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> also collided with <a href="/wiki/Kazuki_Nakajima" title="Kazuki Nakajima">Kazuki Nakajima</a>'s <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a> at the first corner, and the Italian had to pit for a new nosecone for his <a href="/wiki/Toyota_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Toyota F1">Toyota</a>, leaving him at the back of the field with Hamilton.
</p><p>Webber retook the lead on lap 15 as Barrichello pitted and soon after pitted but returned in the lead, which was a good job as he pitted again for the drive through penalty soon after that, which dropped him to eighth. This left <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> in the lead in the Ferrari followed by Barrichello and <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a>.
</p><p>Bourdais retired due to hydraulic problems on his lap 19, in what would ultimately be his final race at <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Scuderia Toro Rosso</a>. <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a> climbed into second before his first pitstop, but as he emerged from the pits he collided with Räikkönen's <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>. The German had to pit again for a new nosecone, and lost several places.
</p><p>Barrichello retook the lead after Massa's pitstop, but lost it again to an impressively recovering Webber in the second stops. Teammate <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> also briefly led before everything settled down with Webber in the lead.
</p><p>Räikkönen retired with a radiator problem on lap 35, briefly promoting <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> into the points, but the Italian failed to score his first points for Force India, as his pitstop allowed former teammate <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> into the points in his <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a>.
</p><p>Alonso made a late charge as <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> struggled for grip, but he could not find a way past. Despite the drive through penalty for impeding Barrichello on the first lap, Webber came home to claim his maiden victory and become this first Australian winner in almost thirty years. He was followed home by Vettel, who was over nine seconds behind. Massa came home a distant but impressive third, his first and only podium of the year as he was injured during qualifying for <a href="/wiki/2009_Hungarian_Grand_Prix" title="2009 Hungarian Grand Prix">the next race</a> and didn't race again for the rest of the season. <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> had managed to jump teammate Barrichello during the second stops and came home fifth with the Brazilian very close behind, and also closely followed by Alonso. The Spaniard had made a charge in the closing stages but was unable to find a way past the Brawns, despite setting the race's faster lap, who were struggling for grip. <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> occupied the final points paying position in an ultimately disappointing race for McLaren.
</p><p>Webber said it was an "incredible day" for him, and his sentiments were echoed by Red Bull sporting director <a href="/wiki/Christian_Horner" title="Christian Horner">Christian Horner</a>, who described his team's 1-2 finish as a "brilliant team performance".<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Webber's win was the first for an Australian driver since <a href="/wiki/1980_Formula_One_season" title="1980 Formula One season">1980</a> World Champion <a href="/wiki/Alan_Jones_(racing_driver)" title="Alan Jones (racing driver)">Alan Jones</a> won the <a href="/wiki/1981_Caesars_Palace_Grand_Prix" title="1981 Caesars Palace Grand Prix">1981 Caesars Palace Grand Prix</a>.
</p>
