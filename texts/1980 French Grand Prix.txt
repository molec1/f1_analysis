<p>The <b>1980 French Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held at <a href="/wiki/Paul_Ricard_Circuit" class="mw-redirect" title="Paul Ricard Circuit">Paul Ricard</a> on 29 June 1980. It was the seventh round of the <a href="/wiki/1980_Formula_One_season" title="1980 Formula One season">1980 Formula One season</a>. The race was the 58th <a href="/wiki/French_Grand_Prix" title="French Grand Prix">French Grand Prix</a>, or the 66th Grand Prix de l'ACF and the sixth to be held at Paul Ricard. The race was held over 54 laps of the 5.809-kilometre circuit for a total race distance of 314 kilometres.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversy">Controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1980_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Originally the race was the eighth round of the season but moved up the order after the <a href="/wiki/1980_Spanish_Grand_Prix" title="1980 Spanish Grand Prix">Spanish Grand Prix</a> was removed from the schedule as a consequence of the <a href="/wiki/FISA%E2%80%93FOCA_war" title="FISA–FOCA war">first major confrontation</a> between <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_du_Sport_Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale du Sport Automobile">FISA</a> and <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_Constructors_Association" class="mw-redirect" title="Formula One Constructors Association">FOCA</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> The controversy threatened to spill over into the French Grand Prix as well as the FISA aligned teams, primarily <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alfa_Romeo_in_Formula_One" title="Alfa Romeo in Formula One">Alfa Romeo</a>, had boycotted the Spanish race which later saw its results removed from championship considerations. The two camps came to an agreement in time for the French Grand Prix to not be affected.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Results">Results</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1980_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Results">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The race was won by <a href="/wiki/Alan_Jones_(racing_driver)" title="Alan Jones (racing driver)">Alan Jones</a> driving a <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW07B" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams FW07B">Williams FW07B</a>. The win was Jones' seventh Formula One Grand Prix victory and his third of the year. Jones was entering a period of good form having won the <a href="/wiki/1980_Spanish_Grand_Prix" title="1980 Spanish Grand Prix">Spanish Grand Prix</a> four weeks earlier. Jones won by four seconds over French driver <a href="/wiki/Didier_Pironi" title="Didier Pironi">Didier Pironi</a> driving a <a href="/wiki/Ligier_JS11/15" class="mw-redirect" title="Ligier JS11/15">Ligier JS11/15</a>. Third was Pironi's French teammate <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Laffite" title="Jacques Laffite">Jacques Laffite</a>. Laffite had led for much of the race until his tyres lost condition. The large French contingent had dominated proceedings with the all-French teams Renault and <a href="/wiki/Equipe_Ligier" title="Equipe Ligier">Ligier</a> taking up four of the top six grid positions with only Jones and his Williams teammate <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Reutemann" title="Carlos Reutemann">Carlos Reutemann</a> intervening. <a href="/wiki/Jean-Pierre_Jabouille" title="Jean-Pierre Jabouille">Jean-Pierre Jabouille</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Renault_RE20" title="Renault RE20">Renault RE20</a> broke its transmission at the start and Jones picked his way past Pironi then the second Renault of <a href="/wiki/Ren%C3%A9_Arnoux" title="René Arnoux">René Arnoux</a> before finally catching Laffite on lap 34.
</p><p>Arnoux faded to fifth behind <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a> (<a href="/wiki/Brabham_BT49" title="Brabham BT49">Brabham BT49</a>) with throttle problems with Reutemann taking the final point in sixth. The win saw Jones move back into the championship lead passing Arnoux and Piquet. Jones now led Piquet by three points and Arnoux by five. Pironi was now equal with Arnoux.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a> now led <a href="/wiki/Equipe_Ligier" title="Equipe Ligier">Ligier</a> by five points, unchanged from Monaco thanks to the efforts of all four drivers and <a href="/wiki/Brabham" title="Brabham">Brabham</a> increased the gap over Renault in third and fourth.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Shadow_Racing_Cars">Shadow Racing Cars</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1980_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Shadow Racing Cars">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This race also saw the final appearance of <a href="/wiki/Shadow_Racing_Cars" title="Shadow Racing Cars">Shadow Racing Cars</a>. <a href="/wiki/Geoff_Lees_(racing_driver)" title="Geoff Lees (racing driver)">Geoff Lees</a> and <a href="/wiki/David_Kennedy_(racing_driver)" title="David Kennedy (racing driver)">David Kennedy</a> were 25th and 27th in qualifying in the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Shadow_DN12&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Shadow DN12 (page does not exist)">Shadow DN12s</a> meaning both drivers missed the 24-car grid. The team had been sold only two months previously to Macau-based businessman and motor racing team owner <a href="/wiki/Teddy_Yip_(businessman)" title="Teddy Yip (businessman)">Teddy Yip</a>. Yip's own race team <a href="/wiki/Theodore_Racing" title="Theodore Racing">Theodore Racing</a> would emerge as a Formula One team in <a href="/wiki/1981_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="1981 Formula One season">1981</a>.
</p>
