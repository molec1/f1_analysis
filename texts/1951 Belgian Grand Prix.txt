<p>The <b>1951 Belgian Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held on 17 June 1951 at <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Spa-Francorchamps" title="Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps">Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps</a>. It was race 3 of 8 in the <a href="/wiki/1951_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1951 World Championship of Drivers">1951 World Championship of Drivers</a>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1951_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Despite there being just 13 starters representing 3 makes of car, the race attracted a record crowd. There were a further 3 entries, including 2 Maseratis, which did not attend the event. <a href="/wiki/Juan_Manuel_Fangio" title="Juan Manuel Fangio">Fangio</a> had a new suspension with special wheels, which had to be concave to make room for the brake drums. These proved an expensive novelty. At his first pit stop, they jammed and his stop lasted over 14 minutes. <a href="/wiki/Giuseppe_Farina" title="Giuseppe Farina">Farina</a>'s Alfa Romeo dominated, holding off the Ferraris of <a href="/wiki/Alberto_Ascari" title="Alberto Ascari">Ascari</a> and <a href="/wiki/Luigi_Villoresi" title="Luigi Villoresi">Villoresi</a>. A lightning stop of 39 seconds for wheel change and refuelling preserved his lead until the finish.
</p>
