<p>The <b>1950 Belgian Grand Prix</b>, formally titled the <i><b>Grand Prix Automobile de Belgique</b></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-prog_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prog-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held on 18 June 1950 at <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Spa-Francorchamps" title="Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps">Spa-Francorchamps</a>. It was race five of seven in the <a href="/wiki/1950_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1950 World Championship of Drivers">1950 World  Championship of Drivers</a>. The 35-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Alfa_Romeo_in_Formula_One" title="Alfa Romeo in Formula One">Alfa Romeo</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Juan_Manuel_Fangio" title="Juan Manuel Fangio">Juan Manuel Fangio</a> after he started from second position. His teammate <a href="/wiki/Luigi_Fagioli" title="Luigi Fagioli">Luigi Fagioli</a> finished second and <a href="/wiki/Talbot-Lago" title="Talbot-Lago">Talbot-Lago</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Louis_Rosier" title="Louis Rosier">Louis Rosier</a> came in third.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1950_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>By the time of the Belgian Grand Prix, the pace of the season was beginning to tell, with only 14 cars arriving at the Spa circuit. These included the dominant <a href="/wiki/Alfa_Romeo_(Formula_One)" class="mw-redirect" title="Alfa Romeo (Formula One)">Alfa Romeos</a> of <a href="/wiki/Giuseppe_Farina" title="Giuseppe Farina">Nino Farina</a>, <a href="/wiki/Juan_Manuel_Fangio" title="Juan Manuel Fangio">Juan Manuel Fangio</a> and <a href="/wiki/Luigi_Fagioli" title="Luigi Fagioli">Luigi Fagioli</a>. <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Scuderia Ferrari</a> was down to two 125s for <a href="/wiki/Luigi_Villoresi" title="Luigi Villoresi">Luigi Villoresi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alberto_Ascari" title="Alberto Ascari">Alberto Ascari</a>, although Ascari had a new V12 engine to try out. The factory <a href="/wiki/Talbot-Lago" title="Talbot-Lago">Talbot-Lago</a> team had three cars for <a href="/wiki/Louis_Rosier" title="Louis Rosier">Louis Rosier</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yves_Giraud-Cabantous" title="Yves Giraud-Cabantous">Yves Giraud-Cabantous</a> and <a href="/wiki/Philippe_%C3%89tancelin" title="Philippe Étancelin">Philippe Étancelin</a> (standing in for the injured <a href="/wiki/Eug%C3%A8ne_Martin" title="Eugène Martin">Eugène Martin</a>). The rest of the field was made up of Talbot-Lagos (notably one for <a href="/wiki/Raymond_Sommer" title="Raymond Sommer">Raymond Sommer</a>), a single <a href="/wiki/Alta_Car_and_Engineering_Company" title="Alta Car and Engineering Company">Alta</a> and one <a href="/wiki/Maserati_in_motorsport" title="Maserati in motorsport">Maserati</a> for <a href="/wiki/Toni_Branca" title="Toni Branca">Toni Branca</a>. This race was the final entry for <a href="/wiki/Geoffrey_Crossley" title="Geoffrey Crossley">Geoffrey Crossley</a>, the sport's high costs forcing him, like many privateers, to retire after just a handful of races.
</p><p>Farina and Fangio were fastest as usual in qualifying with Fagioli unable to match them. Sommer split the Ferraris in his old Talbot-Lago. The race would be a similar story. The Alfas went off on their own and Sommer battled with the two Ferraris. When the Alfa stopped for fuel, Sommer found himself in the unlikely position of being race leader. Unfortunately his engine blew up. Ascari took the lead but he had to stop for fuel and that meant that the Alfas went ahead again with Fangio leading Farina and Fagioli. Farina suffered transmission trouble in the closing laps and dropped to fourth behind the best of the surviving Talbot-Lagos being driven by Rosier. Ascari finished fifth.
</p>
