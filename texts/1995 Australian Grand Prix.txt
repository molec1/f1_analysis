<p>The <b>1995 Australian Grand Prix</b> (officially the LX <a href="/wiki/Electronic_Data_Systems" title="Electronic Data Systems">EDS</a> Australian Grand Prix) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held on 12 November 1995 at the <a href="/wiki/Adelaide_Street_Circuit" title="Adelaide Street Circuit">Adelaide Street Circuit</a>, <a href="/wiki/Adelaide" title="Adelaide">Adelaide</a>. The race, contested over 81 laps, was the seventeenth and final race of the <a href="/wiki/1995_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="1995 Formula One season">1995 Formula One season</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-chicanef1-australia1995main_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-chicanef1-australia1995main-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> and the eleventh and last Australian Grand Prix to be held at Adelaide before the event moved to <a href="/wiki/Melbourne" title="Melbourne">Melbourne</a> the following year. This would also prove to be the last Grand Prix for <a href="/wiki/Mark_Blundell" title="Mark Blundell">Mark Blundell</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bertrand_Gachot" title="Bertrand Gachot">Bertrand Gachot</a>, <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Moreno" title="Roberto Moreno">Roberto Moreno</a>, <a href="/wiki/Taki_Inoue" title="Taki Inoue">Taki Inoue</a> and <a href="/wiki/Karl_Wendlinger" title="Karl Wendlinger">Karl Wendlinger</a>
</p><p>In a race of attrition, all the front-running cars retired except for the <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole</a>-sitting <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW17" title="Williams FW17">Williams</a>-<a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a> of <a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a>. Hill won by two clear laps, only the second time this had been achieved in Formula One history. <a href="/wiki/Ligier_JS41" title="Ligier JS41">Ligier</a>-<a href="/wiki/Mugen_Motorsports" title="Mugen Motorsports">Mugen-Honda</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Panis" title="Olivier Panis">Olivier Panis</a> was second, with <a href="/wiki/Gianni_Morbidelli" title="Gianni Morbidelli">Gianni Morbidelli</a> achieving his best-ever F1 result with third in a <a href="/wiki/Footwork_FA16" title="Footwork FA16">Footwork</a>-<a href="/wiki/Hart_Racing_Engines" title="Hart Racing Engines">Hart</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-formula1.com-officialresult_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-formula1.com-officialresult-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Of the 23 drivers who started, only eight finished, the lowest number in the 1995 season. As of 2020<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=1995_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup> this is the last time the race winner lapped every other competitor.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The race had an attendance of 210,000 – an F1 record until 2000, when 250,000 people attended that year's <a href="/wiki/2000_United_States_Grand_Prix" title="2000 United States Grand Prix">United States Grand Prix</a> at Indianapolis.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix-australia_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-australia-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1995_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-race">Pre-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1995_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Pre-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Heading into the final round of the <a href="/wiki/1995_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="1995 Formula One season">1995 Formula One season</a>, both the Drivers' Championship and Constructors' Championship were already settled, with <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> having claimed the Drivers' Championship two rounds earlier at the <a href="/wiki/1995_Pacific_Grand_Prix" title="1995 Pacific Grand Prix">Pacific Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-gpracing192-pacific_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpracing192-pacific-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> It was Schumacher's last race with the <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a> team, before his move to <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> for the <a href="/wiki/1996_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="1996 Formula One season">1996 season</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-f1fanatic_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1fanatic-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Benetton had claimed the Constructors' Championship at the previous event, the <a href="/wiki/1995_Japanese_Grand_Prix" title="1995 Japanese Grand Prix">Japanese Grand Prix</a>, with <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a> too many points behind to be able to catch them.<sup id="cite_ref-gpracing192-japan_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpracing192-japan-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> It was announced beforehand that it would be the last Formula One event to be held at the <a href="/wiki/Adelaide_Street_Circuit" title="Adelaide Street Circuit">Adelaide Street Circuit</a>, with the Australian Grand Prix moving to <a href="/wiki/Melbourne_Grand_Prix_Circuit" class="mw-redirect" title="Melbourne Grand Prix Circuit">Melbourne Grand Prix Circuit</a> in <a href="/wiki/Melbourne" title="Melbourne">Melbourne</a> from the 1996 season.<sup id="cite_ref-f1fanatic_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1fanatic-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-grandprix-adelaideglobetrotter_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-adelaideglobetrotter-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the Friday afternoon qualifying session, <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> in his <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> car suffered a puncture in his left rear tyre heading towards Brewery Bend. This caused him to lose control, become airborne and crash heavily into a tyre barrier at 120mph. The impact caused his helmet to strike the steering wheel, fracturing his skull. Within seconds he was attended by two doctors who were stationed at the corner, who found Hakkinen unresponsive and with a blocked airway. Häkkinen later said that he was aware of what had happened immediately after the impact, but subsequently lost consciousness. Unable to establish an airway, the doctors performed an emergency <a href="/wiki/Tracheotomy" title="Tracheotomy">tracheotomy</a> before taking him to the nearby <a href="/wiki/Royal_Adelaide_Hospital" title="Royal Adelaide Hospital">Royal Adelaide Hospital</a>. Häkkinen would recover in time to race the following season.<sup id="cite_ref-interviewhakkinen_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-interviewhakkinen-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Williams' cars dominated qualifying, with <a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a> in <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> and <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> alongside him.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix-australia_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-australia-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher was third in his Benetton, with the Ferrari drivers fourth and fifth, <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> ahead of <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix-australia_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-australia-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a> rounded out the top six in his <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix-australia_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-australia-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1995_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The race took place in the afternoon from 14:00 <a href="/wiki/Australia_Central_Daylight_Time" class="mw-redirect" title="Australia Central Daylight Time">ACDT</a> (<a href="/wiki/UTC" class="mw-redirect" title="UTC">UTC</a>+10:30). Hill lost the lead to Coulthard at the start.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix-australia_3-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-australia-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher also lost ground at the start, with Berger moving into third and Alesi moving into fourth.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix-australia_3-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-australia-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher made his way back up to third, overtaking Alesi on lap one, before overtaking Berger a few laps later.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix-australia_3-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-australia-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard kept the lead until the first round of pitstops. However, he came into the pitlane too fast, locking his front tyres and running into the pitwall. He was forced to retire from the race.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix-australia_3-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-australia-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> A few laps later, Forti's <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Moreno" title="Roberto Moreno">Roberto Moreno</a> had spun and caused terminal damage to his suspension in the same place where Coulthard had crashed earlier.
</p><p>After the first round of pitstops, Schumacher and Alesi collided, with both retiring.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix-australia_3-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-australia-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher's Benetton team-mate, <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Herbert" title="Johnny Herbert">Johnny Herbert</a> took second place briefly before coming in for his first stop later than many of the other drivers, while surviving a potential accident in which he missed the pit entry and rejoined the track.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix-australia_3-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-australia-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Berger was promoted to second, but his Ferrari encountered an engine problem, forcing him to retire. This promoted Frentzen to second, but he too retired due to a gearbox problem. With many of the front-runners out, Hill led at the front, even with a 22 second botched pit stop, with Herbert second. <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a> rounded out the top three, before retiring after losing all of his pneumatic pressure.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix-australia_3-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-australia-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Herbert was still second, and looked set as a result to claim third place in the Drivers' Championship.<sup id="cite_ref-gpracing192-australia_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpracing192-australia-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> However, he was forced out of the race as his Benetton suffered a driveshaft failure.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix-australia_3-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-australia-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Panis" title="Olivier Panis">Olivier Panis</a> was now second in his <a href="/wiki/Equipe_Ligier" title="Equipe Ligier">Ligier</a> a lap behind Hill, with <a href="/wiki/Footwork_Arrows" title="Footwork Arrows">Footwork</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Gianni_Morbidelli" title="Gianni Morbidelli">Gianni Morbidelli</a> third, two laps down. With a few laps remaining, Panis' Ligier was suffering an oil leak, and Hill lapped him for a second time on his way to victory.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix-australia_3-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-australia-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Panis remained second, with Morbidelli third for his only career podium, and the first podium for the Footwork/Arrows team in six years.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix-australia_3-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-australia-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Behind the top three, <a href="/wiki/Mark_Blundell" title="Mark Blundell">Mark Blundell</a> was fourth in the sole McLaren, with <a href="/wiki/Mika_Salo" title="Mika Salo">Mika Salo</a> fifth in the <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_Racing" title="Tyrrell Racing">Tyrrell</a>. <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Lamy" title="Pedro Lamy">Pedro Lamy</a> had a mid-race spin, but recovered to take sixth in his <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a>&#160;– his only Formula One point, and Minardi's last until the <a href="/wiki/1999_European_Grand_Prix" title="1999 European Grand Prix">1999 European Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix-australia_3-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-australia-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Only eight cars finished the race, with <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Diniz" title="Pedro Diniz">Pedro Diniz</a> seventh place being <a href="/wiki/Forti" title="Forti">Forti</a>'s best Formula One finish.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix-australia_3-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-australia-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The eighth place for Pacific also equalled their best result since the <a href="/wiki/1995_German_Grand_Prix" title="1995 German Grand Prix">1995 German Grand Prix</a>. The race was televised by <a href="/wiki/Nine_Network" title="Nine Network">Channel 9</a> in Australia and by the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> in the UK.
</p><p>The race marked the end of <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Racing" title="Pacific Racing">Pacific Racing</a>, as the team went back to <a href="/wiki/International_Formula_3000" title="International Formula 3000">International Formula 3000</a> for 1996. In a last gasp effort, Pacific tried to have their test driver <a href="/wiki/Oliver_Gavin" title="Oliver Gavin">Oliver Gavin</a> in the seat, but he was not granted an FIA Super License and shareholder <a href="/wiki/Bertrand_Gachot" title="Bertrand Gachot">Bertrand Gachot</a> raced instead. It was only the second time in Formula One history that the winner won by two laps– the first time was at the <a href="/wiki/1969_Spanish_Grand_Prix" title="1969 Spanish Grand Prix">1969 Spanish Grand Prix</a> when <a href="/wiki/Jackie_Stewart" title="Jackie Stewart">Jackie Stewart</a> won.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix-australia_3-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-australia-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Hill, who had been criticised for his performances in all of the three previous races, was praised by commentator <a href="/wiki/Murray_Walker" title="Murray Walker">Murray Walker</a> for this performance, with Walker saying that, with Schumacher and Coulthard's imminent moves to Ferrari and McLaren respectively, Hill would be a strong favourite to win the title in 1996 if he could continue to perform in the way he had done so in this particular race. This would also be the last race for a V12 engine. Only Ferrari used this configuration, but would switch to a more fuel-efficient V10 engine for 1996.
</p><p>Although he failed to finish, by competing in the race Gerhard Berger became the only driver to have driven in all 11 Formula One Grands Prix held in Adelaide. He had driven for <a href="/wiki/Arrows_Grand_Prix_International" title="Arrows Grand Prix International">Arrows</a>-<a href="/wiki/BMW_in_Formula_One" title="BMW in Formula One">BMW</a> (<a href="/wiki/1985_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="1985 Australian Grand Prix">1985</a>), <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a>-BMW (<a href="/wiki/1986_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="1986 Australian Grand Prix">1986</a>), Ferrari (<a href="/wiki/1987_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="1987 Australian Grand Prix">1987</a>, <a href="/wiki/1988_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="1988 Australian Grand Prix">1988</a>, <a href="/wiki/1989_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="1989 Australian Grand Prix">1989</a>, <a href="/wiki/1993_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="1993 Australian Grand Prix">1993</a>, <a href="/wiki/1994_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="1994 Australian Grand Prix">1994</a>, 1995) and <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Honda_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Honda F1">Honda</a> (<a href="/wiki/1990_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="1990 Australian Grand Prix">1990</a>, <a href="/wiki/1991_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="1991 Australian Grand Prix">1991</a>, <a href="/wiki/1992_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="1992 Australian Grand Prix">1992</a>), winning the race for Ferrari in 1987 (as well as claiming pole position and fastest lap) and McLaren in 1992.
</p><p>The post-race concert was headlined by American rock band <a href="/wiki/Bon_Jovi" title="Bon Jovi">Bon Jovi</a>, who performed at the <a href="/wiki/Victoria_Park_Racecourse" class="mw-redirect" title="Victoria Park Racecourse">Victoria Park Racecourse</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
