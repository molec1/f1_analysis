<p>The <b>2006 Bahrain Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>III Gulf Air Bahrain Grand Prix</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at the <a href="/wiki/Bahrain_International_Circuit" title="Bahrain International Circuit">Bahrain International Circuit</a> in <a href="/wiki/Sakhir" title="Sakhir">Sakhir</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bahrain" title="Bahrain">Bahrain</a> on 12 March 2006. The 57-lap race was the opening round of the <a href="/wiki/2006_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2006 Formula One season">2006 Formula One season</a> and the third running of the <a href="/wiki/Bahrain_Grand_Prix" title="Bahrain Grand Prix">Bahrain Grand Prix</a>. It was won by the <a href="/wiki/2005_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2005 Formula One World Championship">2005</a> World Champions, <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a> team. <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> driver and polesitter <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> began his final season in Formula One (before his return with <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> in <a href="/wiki/2010_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2010 Formula One World Championship">2010</a>) with second position. <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> completed the podium after he finished in third place with the <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> team, despite starting in last position.
</p><p>The race was the first Grand Prix for future world champion <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a>, son of <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">World Champion</a> <a href="/wiki/Keke_Rosberg" title="Keke Rosberg">Keke Rosberg</a>, who raced with the <a href="/wiki/Williams_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams F1">Williams</a> team. He set the fastest lap of the race and, at the age of 20 years and 258 days, broke the record for the youngest driver to do so, which he held until <a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a> scored his first fastest lap, at age 19, at the <a href="/wiki/2016_Brazilian_Grand_Prix" title="2016 Brazilian Grand Prix">2016 Brazilian Grand Prix</a>. It was also the debut race for the <a href="/wiki/BMW_in_Formula_One" title="BMW in Formula One">BMW Sauber</a>, <a href="/wiki/Toro_Rosso" class="mw-redirect" title="Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso</a>, <a href="/wiki/Midland_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Midland F1">Midland F1</a> and <a href="/wiki/Super_Aguri" class="mw-redirect" title="Super Aguri">Super Aguri</a> teams, and drivers <a href="/wiki/Scott_Speed" title="Scott Speed">Scott Speed</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yuji_Ide" title="Yuji Ide">Yuji Ide</a>.
</p><p>It also saw the début of the 2.4 litre (146.4 cu in) naturally-aspirated V8 engines which were used by all teams except Toro Rosso, who were still using the 3.0 litre (183 cu in) V10 engines that were used from <a href="/wiki/1995_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1995 Formula One World Championship">1995</a> to <a href="/wiki/2005_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2005 Formula One World Championship">2005</a>, as the team's chassis, the <a href="/wiki/Toro_Rosso_STR1" title="Toro Rosso STR1">STR1</a>, was reused from the <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_RB1" title="Red Bull RB1">Red Bull RB1</a> from the 2005 season.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2006_Bahrain_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Trulli_Toyota_1.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b8/Trulli_Toyota_1.jpg/220px-Trulli_Toyota_1.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="329" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b8/Trulli_Toyota_1.jpg/330px-Trulli_Toyota_1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b8/Trulli_Toyota_1.jpg/440px-Trulli_Toyota_1.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2592" data-file-height="3872" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Trulli_Toyota_1.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Toyota_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Toyota F1">Toyota</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> before the start of the race.</div></div></div>
<p>The race was the first run under the new <a href="/wiki/2006_Formula_One_season#Qualifying" class="mw-redirect" title="2006 Formula One season">2006 qualifying rules</a>, in which the one-hour session is split into three 'knock out' parts. The first part eliminated <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> (rear wishbone failure) and <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a>, who was caught out by the red flag after Räikkönen's accident and failed to set a competitive time, as well as the MF1's and the two Super Aguris. The second part saw fewer surprises, with the expected runners lining up for the final top 10 part. The result was that the Ferraris swept the front row.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2006_Bahrain_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the start of the race, <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> moved up to second in the first lap, only for <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> to begin to pull away. Alonso was involved in a near collision with Schumacher's teammate <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a>, who spun at the first corner. Massa was never a threat after this point, not helped by a delay in his pit stop to change tyres the same lap. Alonso's teammate <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> also played little part in the race due to an engine mapping problem which restricted power. He retired on the 21st lap due to hydraulic failure. To the surprise of few, <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> quickly moved his way up the field, only being delayed by <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a> and Fisichella for a short period of time, before passing both men.
</p><p>Further down the field, <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a>, who was making his Grand Prix début, touched at the first corner. Both men were significantly delayed, but recovered. Heidfeld's day continued to get worse, as an incident with <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> was investigated after the race.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a> finished 5th in his McLaren, having never been a threat throughout the race. It was a good day for Williams on a whole, as <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> finished in 6th, while Rosberg was the surprise of the race though, ending up a very respectable 7th, passing <a href="/wiki/Christian_Klien" title="Christian Klien">Christian Klien</a> after catching him in the last few laps of the race, putting Klien down to a still impressive 8th. Rosberg set the fastest lap of the race, becoming the youngest driver ever to achieve this in a Grand Prix, breaking a record held by Alonso.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Marlboro-Ferrari.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/de/Marlboro-Ferrari.jpg/220px-Marlboro-Ferrari.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="199" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/de/Marlboro-Ferrari.jpg/330px-Marlboro-Ferrari.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/de/Marlboro-Ferrari.jpg/440px-Marlboro-Ferrari.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1743" data-file-height="1580" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Marlboro-Ferrari.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> started on pole for <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> and finished the race in second position.</div></div></div>
<p>Honda suffered mixed fortunes throughout the race. Early on fans were treated to a fight between <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a>, as Button slipped down the order due to a slow getaway. Button eventually fought his way back up, twice passing Juan Pablo Montoya at the end of the straight but losing out again in pitstops. He eventually finished 4th, 0.6 seconds behind Räikkönen in 3rd. Räikkönen fought hard throughout the race, stopping only once on his way to the last podium spot. He rose 19 places in the race which is more than any driver since <a href="/wiki/Fabrizio_Barbazza" title="Fabrizio Barbazza">Fabrizio Barbazza</a> started 25th and finished sixth in the <a href="/wiki/1993_San_Marino_Grand_Prix" title="1993 San Marino Grand Prix">1993 San Marino Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-f1f2006stats_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1f2006stats-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At the second set of pit stops, Alonso was able to take advantage of staying out longer than Schumacher by coming out of the pit lane side by side with Schumacher. As they came into the first corner both cars were neck and neck, but Alonso was able to pull out in front of the former world champion. Despite coming under heavy pressure from Schumacher towards the end, the reigning champion started off the season with a well earned victory.
</p><p>After a tussle with <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> was reprimanded by stewards post-race for positioning his car in a way that forced Coulthard off the race circuit. Coulthard suffered engine failure on cool-down lap and received a 10-place grid penalty for <a href="/wiki/2006_Malaysian_Grand_Prix" title="2006 Malaysian Grand Prix">2006 Malaysian Grand Prix</a>.
</p><p>This was the first race for the <a href="/wiki/Midland_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Midland F1">Midland</a>, <a href="/wiki/BMW_in_Formula_One" title="BMW in Formula One">BMW Sauber</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso</a>, <a href="/wiki/Honda_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Honda F1">Honda F1</a> and <a href="/wiki/Super_Aguri" class="mw-redirect" title="Super Aguri">Super Aguri</a> teams, although only Super Aguri was a completely new team, the others all representing buyouts or rebrandings of existing teams. Along with Rosberg, <a href="/wiki/Scott_Speed" title="Scott Speed">Scott Speed</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yuji_Ide" title="Yuji Ide">Yuji Ide</a> were also making their Grand Prix débuts.
</p>
