<p>The <b>1993 Australian Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>LVIII Australian Grand Prix</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at <a href="/wiki/Adelaide_Street_Circuit" title="Adelaide Street Circuit">Adelaide</a> on 7 November 1993. It was the sixteenth and final race of the <a href="/wiki/1993_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1993 Formula One World Championship">1993 Formula One World Championship</a>.
</p><p>The 79-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/8" title="McLaren MP4/8">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Ford_Motor_Company" title="Ford Motor Company">Ford</a>. In his last race with McLaren before joining Williams for <a href="/wiki/1994_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1994 Formula One World Championship">1994</a>, Senna took <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> and led from start to finish, except during the pit stops. <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a>, in his final Grand Prix before retirement, finished second in his <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW15C" title="Williams FW15C">Williams</a>-<a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>, with teammate <a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a> third.
</p><p>This was the last race for cars with <a href="/wiki/Active_suspension" title="Active suspension">active suspension</a>, which was to be banned from 1994 along with other driver aids. <a href="/wiki/Riccardo_Patrese" title="Riccardo Patrese">Riccardo Patrese</a> and <a href="/wiki/Derek_Warwick" title="Derek Warwick">Derek Warwick</a> also retired from F1 after this race, Patrese having competed in a then-record 256 Grands Prix. Senna's victory would turn out to be his 41st and last victory in Formula One and the last time he would finish on the podium, score points or finish a race. It was also the last win for a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_drivers_from_Brazil" title="Formula One drivers from Brazil">Brazilian</a> driver until <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> in <a href="/wiki/2000_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2000 Formula One World Championship">2000</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1993_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1993_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Senna took his first pole position since the <a href="/wiki/1992_Canadian_Grand_Prix" title="1992 Canadian Grand Prix">1992 Canadian Grand Prix</a>, in the process breaking a run of 24 straight poles for Williams and preventing that team from achieving a clean sweep for the season. Prost was alongside on the front row, with <a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a> in the second Williams and <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Benetton_B193" title="Benetton B193">Benetton</a> on the second row, and <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> in the second <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/8" title="McLaren MP4/8">McLaren</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_F93A" title="Ferrari F93A">Ferrari</a> on the third. Berger had his Saturday qualifying times removed after doing 18 laps, above the limit of 12. The top ten was completed by <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a> in the second Ferrari, <a href="/wiki/Martin_Brundle" title="Martin Brundle">Martin Brundle</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Ligier_JS39" title="Ligier JS39">Ligier</a>, Patrese in the second Benetton, and <a href="/wiki/Aguri_Suzuki" title="Aguri Suzuki">Aguri Suzuki</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Footwork_FA14" title="Footwork FA14">Footwork</a>.
</p><p>Despite being the fastest qualifier in his <a href="/wiki/Ford_Motor_Company" title="Ford Motor Company">Ford</a> <a href="/wiki/V8_engine" title="V8 engine">V8</a>-powered McLaren, Senna was some 15&#160;km/h (9&#160;mph) slower on the 890m-long <a href="/wiki/Jack_Brabham" title="Jack Brabham">Brabham</a> Straight than the <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a> <a href="/wiki/V10_engine" title="V10 engine">V10</a>-powered Williams of Hill.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1993_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>It took three attempts to get the race underway. On the first attempt, Brundle's Ligier was left on the grid at the start of the formation lap, before <a href="/wiki/Ukyo_Katayama" title="Ukyo Katayama">Ukyo Katayama</a> stalled his <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_021" title="Tyrrell 021">Tyrrell</a> and the start was aborted. On the second attempt, <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a> missed his grid slot and stalled his <a href="/wiki/Jordan_193" title="Jordan 193">Jordan</a>. Again, the start was aborted. Katayama and Irvine were sent to the back of the grid for the third, successful, attempt.
</p><p>The top four retained their positions into the first corner, while Häkkinen made a bad start and fell behind Berger.
</p><p>While Senna pulled out a small lead, the two Williamses and Schumacher stayed together. Schumacher pitted early on lap 15 and rejoined in fourth but his engine failed on lap 20. Senna pitted on lap 24, allowing Prost to lead until his own stop five laps later, while Häkkinen's race went from bad to worse as he had a slow stop, allowing Alesi and Brundle to get ahead of him, before his brakes failed on lap 29.
</p><p>Senna pitted for the second time on lap 55, by which time the Williamses had already made their second stops, and this enabled the Brazilian to retain a healthy lead. Meanwhile, Alesi got ahead of team-mate Berger while Patrese got ahead of Brundle. On lap 61, Hill tried to catch Prost by surprise for second place. Prost, however, moved over to block, causing Hill to back off and spin, losing time but no places.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Alain_Prost_during_the_race_in_Adelaide_on_7_November_1993.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e9/Alain_Prost_during_the_race_in_Adelaide_on_7_November_1993.jpg/220px-Alain_Prost_during_the_race_in_Adelaide_on_7_November_1993.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="145" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e9/Alain_Prost_during_the_race_in_Adelaide_on_7_November_1993.jpg/330px-Alain_Prost_during_the_race_in_Adelaide_on_7_November_1993.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e9/Alain_Prost_during_the_race_in_Adelaide_on_7_November_1993.jpg/440px-Alain_Prost_during_the_race_in_Adelaide_on_7_November_1993.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1988" data-file-height="1310" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Alain_Prost_during_the_race_in_Adelaide_on_7_November_1993.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Alain Prost during the race in Adelaide on 7 November 1993.</div></div></div>
<p>Senna duly won from Prost by nine seconds, with Hill a further 24 seconds back. Alesi and Berger were fourth and fifth, one lap down, while Patrese was set to finish sixth in his 256th and last race, only for his fuel pressure to drop on the last lap and thus hand Brundle the last point.
</p><p>Prost thus signed off on his F1 career with his fourth Drivers' Championship and 99 points. Senna's win enabled him to secure second place in the standings with 73 points, just ahead of Hill on 69. Schumacher was fourth with 52, with a big gap to team-mate Patrese in fifth with 20, followed by Alesi (16), Brundle (13), Berger (12), <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Herbert" title="Johnny Herbert">Johnny Herbert</a> (11) and <a href="/wiki/Mark_Blundell" title="Mark Blundell">Mark Blundell</a> (10). In the Constructors' Championship, Williams finished with 168 points - four more than their tally from 1992, and double the tally of McLaren (84). Benetton were a close third with 72, while Ferrari (28) edged out Ligier (23) for fourth.
</p><p>With Prost not defending his title, the Williams cars would once again bear the numbers 0 and 2 for 1994. This race was also Derek Warwick's last race.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1993_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>During the podium celebrations, Senna invited both Prost and Hill up to the top step, much to the surprise of Prost. The move marked the first easing of tensions in the rivalry between the two men. Senna himself would later call the race 'the end of an era'. As was tradition in Adelaide, a rock concert was held inside the circuit following the race. For 1993 the concert was performed by <a href="/wiki/Tina_Turner" title="Tina Turner">Tina Turner</a> as part of her <a href="/wiki/What%27s_Love%3F_Tour" title="What&#39;s Love? Tour">What's Love? Tour</a> (the concert was free to anyone with a ticket to the race). At one stage during the concert, Ayrton Senna appeared on stage much to Turner's surprise. As a tribute to the Brazilian (and although she had already performed the song), Turner sang her hit "<a href="/wiki/The_Best_(song)" title="The Best (song)">The Best</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1993_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<table class="box-Trivia plainlinks metadata ambox ambox-style" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-image"><div style="width:52px"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/f/f2/Edit-clear.svg/40px-Edit-clear.svg.png" decoding="async" width="40" height="40" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/f/f2/Edit-clear.svg/60px-Edit-clear.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/f/f2/Edit-clear.svg/80px-Edit-clear.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="48" data-file-height="48" /></div></td><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This section contains a <b><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Trivia_sections" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Trivia sections">list of miscellaneous information</a>.</b><span class="hide-when-compact"> Please <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Handling_trivia#Recommendations_for_handling_trivia" title="Wikipedia:Handling trivia">relocate</a> any relevant information into other sections or articles.</span>  <small class="date-container"><i>(<span class="date">April 2020</span>)</i></small></div></td></tr></tbody></table>
<p>The race has become a memorable event due to numerous reasons:
</p>
<ul><li>Last race without <a href="/wiki/History_of_Formula_One_regulations" title="History of Formula One regulations">refuelling during races</a> until the <a href="/wiki/2010_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2010 Formula One World Championship">2010 season</a>.</li>
<li>Last victory for <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> until the <a href="/wiki/1997_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1997 Formula One World Championship">1997 season</a>.</li>
<li>Last pole position for <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> until the <a href="/wiki/1997_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1997 Formula One World Championship">1997 season</a>.</li>
<li>Last race for <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> driving for <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> surpassed <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> as <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_Grand_Prix_winners_(constructors)#Record_progression" title="List of Formula One Grand Prix winners (constructors)">the most successful F1 constructor in number of wins</a> (until the <a href="/wiki/1995_Canadian_Grand_Prix" title="1995 Canadian Grand Prix">1995 Canadian Grand Prix</a>).</li>
<li>Last race for electronic driver aids, including <a href="/wiki/Traction_control_system" title="Traction control system">traction control</a>, <a href="/wiki/Launch_control_(automotive)" title="Launch control (automotive)">launch control</a>, <a href="/wiki/Anti-lock_braking_system" title="Anti-lock braking system">ABS</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Active_suspension" title="Active suspension">active suspension</a>, which were all banned for <a href="/wiki/1994_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1994 Formula One World Championship">1994</a>, until the former two driver aids were made legal and re-introduced at the <a href="/wiki/2001_Spanish_Grand_Prix" title="2001 Spanish Grand Prix">2001 Spanish Grand Prix</a> (then re-banned again for the <a href="/wiki/2008_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2008 Formula One World Championship">2008 Formula One season</a>).</li></ul>
