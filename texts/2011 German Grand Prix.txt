<p>The <b>2011 German Grand Prix</b>, formally the <b>Formula 1 Großer Preis Santander von Deutschland 2011</b>, was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Auto_racing" title="Auto racing">motor race</a> that was held on 24 July 2011 at the <a href="/wiki/N%C3%BCrburgring" title="Nürburgring">Nürburgring</a> in <a href="/wiki/N%C3%BCrburg" title="Nürburg">Nürburg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> It was the tenth round of the <a href="/wiki/2011_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2011 Formula One season">2011 Formula One season</a>. The 60-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>, who had started from second position on the grid. <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> finished in second place, and <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a>, who had started the race from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>, completed the podium in third position for <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Race_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Race-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Webber's teammate and championship leader <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> finished fourth, ending an eleven-race streak of finishing in the podium placings.
</p><p>As a consequence of the race, Vettel's lead in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">World Drivers' Championship</a> was cut by Webber to 77&#160;points. Hamilton's victory allowed him to move into third place in the championship, five&#160;points behind Webber in second, and four ahead of Alonso. In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">World Constructors' Championship</a>, Red Bull extended their championship lead to 112&#160;points over McLaren, with Ferrari a further 51&#160;points behind in third position.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On the Thursday before the race, <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus_(2010%E2%80%9311)" title="Team Lotus (2010–11)">Team Lotus</a> announced that <a href="/wiki/Karun_Chandhok" title="Karun Chandhok">Karun Chandhok</a> would replace <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> for the race weekend.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The start line was moved 240&#160;m (790&#160;ft) closer to Turn 1 than it had been before, for the weekend. This meant that the race start could be less crucial for the drivers as the distance to the first corner was roughly half what it had been before.
</p><p>Tyre supplier <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_tyres" title="Formula One tyres">Pirelli</a> brought its white-banded medium compound tyre as the harder "prime" tyre and the yellow-banded soft compound as the softer "option" compound.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Before the race, <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> had an 80-point lead in the World Drivers' Championship - at the time, the record for the largest lead. Despite controversial team orders at the previous race in <a href="/wiki/2011_British_Grand_Prix" title="2011 British Grand Prix">Silverstone</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> stayed second in the standings - 12 points ahead of <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>, who had moved up to third in the standings on 112 points after his victory two weeks previously. <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> teammates <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> both stood on 109 points before the Grand Prix, but Hamilton was fourth through countback because he had one more second place.
</p><p>As far as the Constructors were concerned, <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a> were looking good on 328 points - 110 ahead of a <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> who had faltered somewhat at the last two races. <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> were third on 164 points, 54 behind McLaren, but 103 ahead of <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes GP</a> who were ahead of <a href="/wiki/Lotus_Renault_GP" class="mw-redirect" title="Lotus Renault GP">Renault</a> for the first time this season, after the deterioration of Renault's form had already begun. Before the race, all teams and all drivers were in Championship contention, but many were not after this race. Also, <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a>, <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> or <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> couldn't have lost their respective Constructors' Championship placings during this event.
</p><p>There were six German drivers at this race - including <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>, who had won at this track 5 times in his career.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice">Practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After Friday's two practice sessions, <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> bleakly summed up his chances of challenging Red Bull and Ferrari saying: "We won't find an answer tomorrow. We definitely can't challenge them for pole." Yet, he ended up marginally behind the pole position time, and on the front row.
</p><p>In the first free practice session, <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> set the fastest time followed by <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> and then <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a>. This started speculation that <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> would be matching <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a>'s pace in Qualifying. It seemed likely at this point that Alonso would be on the front row of the grid – however, this was eventually not the case. Lewis Hamilton was fifth, and his teammate Button down in 11th after having no <a href="/wiki/Kinetic_Energy_Recovery_Systems" class="mw-redirect" title="Kinetic Energy Recovery Systems">KERS</a> for both Friday Practice sessions. With only half a minute left in the session, <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9bastien_Buemi" title="Sébastien Buemi">Sébastien Buemi</a> made a mistake and was flung sideways across the gravel; he later missed the second Friday session because of a fuel pressure problem.
</p><p>Webber led Alonso in Free Practice 2. This session also featured an extraordinary number of lock-ups and off track excursions. It was in this session <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> left the track at Turn 7 whilst exploring the track's limits and waved to his home crowd. Another home driver, <a href="/wiki/Timo_Glock" title="Timo Glock">Timo Glock</a>, also left the track, spun on the wet grass at the bottom of a hill, and luckily escaped being beached on the high curb.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Virgin_et_HRT_nurburgring_2011.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/Virgin_et_HRT_nurburgring_2011.jpg/220px-Virgin_et_HRT_nurburgring_2011.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/Virgin_et_HRT_nurburgring_2011.jpg/330px-Virgin_et_HRT_nurburgring_2011.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/Virgin_et_HRT_nurburgring_2011.jpg/440px-Virgin_et_HRT_nurburgring_2011.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1024" data-file-height="768" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Virgin_et_HRT_nurburgring_2011.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The two <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Racing" title="Virgin Racing">Virgins</a> and the two <a href="/wiki/HRT_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="HRT F1">HRTs</a> were on the back three rows of the grid along with <a href="/wiki/Karun_Chandhok" title="Karun Chandhok">Karun Chandhok</a>, who drove <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus_(2010%E2%80%9311)" title="Team Lotus (2010–11)">Lotus</a> during the weekend, and <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9bastien_Buemi" title="Sébastien Buemi">Sébastien Buemi</a>, whose <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso's</a> fuel irregularity had left him 24th.</div></div></div> 
<p><a href="/wiki/Kamui_Kobayashi" title="Kamui Kobayashi">Kamui Kobayashi</a> was the most surprising driver to drop out in the first part of qualifying, where he only set a time fast enough for his <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a> to be in 18th place. <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> out-qualified new, temporary, <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus_(2010%E2%80%9311)" title="Team Lotus (2010–11)">Lotus</a> teammate <a href="/wiki/Karun_Chandhok" title="Karun Chandhok">Karun Chandhok</a> by eight tenths of a second. The Indian said he was happy with his lap as he had never driven the circuit as an F1 driver, run the car with low fuel and <a href="/wiki/Slicks" class="mw-redirect" title="Slicks">slicks</a>, used brakes not suited to his style, or used much <a href="/wiki/Kinetic_Energy_Recovery_Systems" class="mw-redirect" title="Kinetic Energy Recovery Systems">KERS</a> or <a href="/wiki/Drag_Reduction_System" class="mw-redirect" title="Drag Reduction System">DRS</a> before. Chandok was 21st fastest (which became 20th later), he and Kovalainen separated by <a href="/wiki/Timo_Glock" title="Timo Glock">Timo Glock</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Racing" title="Virgin Racing">Virgin</a>. The other Virgin of <a href="/wiki/J%C3%A9r%C3%B4me_d%27Ambrosio" title="Jérôme d&#39;Ambrosio">Jérôme d'Ambrosio</a> was ahead of the two very closely matched times of the <a href="/wiki/Hispania_Racing" class="mw-redirect" title="Hispania Racing">HRTs</a>; <a href="/wiki/Vitantonio_Liuzzi" title="Vitantonio Liuzzi">Vitantonio Liuzzi</a> was just ahead of new driver <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> although he would start 24th (later 23rd) due to a <a href="/wiki/Gearbox" class="mw-redirect" title="Gearbox">gearbox</a> change. Out of the fastest four teams, <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> was the only driver feeling the need to use a set of soft tyres in Q1. After qualifying was over, Sébastien Buemi was excluded due to a fuel irregularity demoting him to 24th place, therefore every driver below 16th gained a place. After Q1, on the way back to the pit lane, Timo Glock commented to his engineers on the car-to-pit radio that they were making it "difficult for him". Glock later tried to cover up his comments by saying they were meant in a joking way. Glock announced the following day he would be staying with the team until 2014.
</p><p>The remaining 17 drivers all participated in the second qualifying session, and all drivers decided to use the option tyres. The two <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso</a> cars were slowest in Q2, Buemi ahead of teammate <a href="/wiki/Jaime_Alguersuari" title="Jaime Alguersuari">Jaime Alguersuari</a>. Although, Alguersuari moved up to 16th and Kobayashi into 17th once Buemi was excluded form qualifying due to a fuel irregularity demoting him to 24th position. Mexican rookie <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a> out-qualified his teammate again with 15th spot, behind the two <a href="/wiki/Williams_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams F1">Williams</a> cars; <a href="/wiki/Pastor_Maldonado" title="Pastor Maldonado">Pastor Maldonado</a> not in Q3, yet ahead of F1 veteran <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> on the seventh row. <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Paul_di_Resta" title="Paul di Resta">Paul di Resta</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a> were very close to making the cut, but were both beaten by their teammates and stayed 12th and 11th respectively. This was only the 3rd time in 10 races that Force India driver <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a> out-qualified his rookie teammate, and <a href="/wiki/Vitaly_Petrov" title="Vitaly Petrov">Vitaly Petrov</a>'s Renault entered the top 10 for the first time since <a href="/wiki/2011_Canadian_Grand_Prix" title="2011 Canadian Grand Prix">Canada</a>. Paul di Resta later said he suffered from grip problems in Qualifying.
</p><p>For the final part of qualifying, all the remaining drivers went out on the option tyres, and all except the two <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> cars and Sutil's Force India chose to do two runs – who chose to do only two flying laps at the end of the session. <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> started the session by initially setting provisional pole, although he was quickly beaten by <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>. Ferrari looked on very good form at the last race in <a href="/wiki/2011_British_Grand_Prix" title="2011 British Grand Prix">Silverstone</a> and continue that good form by matching <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a> in practice. Alonso's pole looked like a solid time until Red Bull came out of the pit lane, <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> beating Alonso by four tenths and <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> fitting in between the two. On these four drivers last efforts, Alonso went faster but failed to improve his time and stayed third. Webber then went quicker by another tenth on a great lap, and then Hamilton posted a stunning lap – only five hundredths slower than Webber's and beating all the critics who said <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> had easily fallen behind Ferrari. This pushed Alonso down to fourth. Vettel then improved his lap, but stayed third – the first time since <a href="/wiki/2010_Italian_Grand_Prix" title="2010 Italian Grand Prix">Monza the previous year</a> he had not qualified on the front row; but, he was only 0.137 seconds behind pole position. This was Webber's second successive pole, and his third of the season. Home favourites Mercedes qualified sixth and tenth with <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> ahead of <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> – who had won at this circuit five times – for the ninth time this season. Ferrari's Felipe Massa was sixth tenths behind his teammate, as Alonso continued to outclass him, in fifth place. Jenson Button was disappointed after he qualified seventh, 1.2 seconds behind Hamilton, he stated it was due to lack of grip and understeer during his lap. Adrian Sutil (eighth), Vitaly Petrov (ninth) and Michael Schumacher rounded out the top 10. Four of the six Germans in the field qualified in the top ten at their home Grand Prix.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the start of the race, <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> made a slow start from pole position; he 'bogged down' with too low revs, allowing Hamilton to take the lead. The Ferraris were on the inside and outside of Vettel as they approached the first turn, and Alonso managed to get ahead of Vettel. Massa had made another good start but after being on the outside of Vettel at Turn 1, he eventually slipped behind <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> into sixth place. <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> had a poor start, slipping down from seventh to tenth in the first lap. Contrastly, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> had another good start moving from tenth to eighth on lap 1. Meanwhile, further back <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> and <a href="/wiki/Paul_di_Resta" title="Paul di Resta">Paul di Resta</a> made contact and dropped to the back of the field. After that, Hamilton was leading the race from Webber, Alonso, Vettel, Rosberg and Massa. Sutil was in seventh, after a good start, followed by Schumacher, Petrov and Button completing the top 10.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Nurburgring_mercedes.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1d/Nurburgring_mercedes.jpg/220px-Nurburgring_mercedes.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1d/Nurburgring_mercedes.jpg/330px-Nurburgring_mercedes.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1d/Nurburgring_mercedes.jpg/440px-Nurburgring_mercedes.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1024" data-file-height="768" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Nurburgring_mercedes.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> finished the race in eighth position.</div></div></div>
<p>On the fourth lap, Alonso ran wide at Turn 2 getting a wheel on the wet grass, forcing him onto the tarmac. This allowed Vettel to gain third place. Only a few laps later though, Alonso repassed Vettel into Turn 1. Later, on lap 16, <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> suffered an engine failure, however he was able to limp back to the pits.
</p><p>Heidfeld received a drive-through penalty for causing an avoidable accident with di Resta, but did not have time to serve it. Whilst trying to make progress through the field, he was squeezed off the track by <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9bastien_Buemi" title="Sébastien Buemi">Sébastien Buemi</a> at the chicane and crashed out. Buemi had to pit for new tyres, and was later given a five-place grid penalty at the next race, the <a href="/wiki/2011_Hungarian_Grand_Prix" title="2011 Hungarian Grand Prix">Hungarian Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Di Resta, fought his way through the field to finish in thirteenth by the end of the race.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Vitaly_Petrov" title="Vitaly Petrov">Vitaly Petrov</a> was defending very well against Button's <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> for ninth place; whilst <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> overtook Rosberg's <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> for fifth place after Massa's <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> engineer, <a href="/wiki/Rob_Smedley" title="Rob Smedley">Rob Smedley</a>, had told him it was necessary for his strategy to work. Button eventually passed Petrov and started closing on Schumacher. Before the first round of pit stops, Hamilton ran wide allowing Webber to come up the inside of him through the final corner. Hamilton instantly dived up the inside on the run down to Turn 1 and repassed Webber.
</p><p>Vettel spun at Turn 10 putting him eleven seconds behind third placed <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>, the first three positions were covered by just three seconds and Vettel was lapping half a second slower than them. At the pit stops, Webber pitted first in an attempt to get the undercut and came out behind Sutil, but managed to work it out, passing Vettel and catching Massa when Hamilton and Alonso pitted at the same time, bringing them just out of the pits as soon as Webber and Massa were braking for turn 1. Massa took the lead ahead of Webber, Hamilton and Alonso with Vettel pitting to ninth place. When Massa pitted he dropped to eighth, just in front of Vettel starting a new battle. After these stops Webber had undercut Hamilton into the lead – leading a race for the first time in the season – with Alonso in third position. During the pit stop phase the two Mercedes cars were closing on Petrov's <a href="/wiki/Lotus_Renault_GP" class="mw-redirect" title="Lotus Renault GP">Renault</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Drag_Reduction_System" class="mw-redirect" title="Drag Reduction System">DRS</a> zone leading to the Veedol Chicane. Rosberg passed Petrov on the straight and Schumacher followed him through. Schumacher later spun at exactly the same place as Vettel, falling behind both Sutil and Petrov.
</p><p>It looked as if Sutil and Button's two-stop strategies were successful as Sutil got ahead of Rosberg in the later pit stops and finished the race in sixth place. Button was also going strong – catching and passing many drivers including Rosberg for sixth at Turn 1 when Rosberg outbraked himself and ran wide. Button, like Sutil, was only passed in the pit-line and not on the track, although his back luck continued from <a href="/wiki/2011_British_Grand_Prix" title="2011 British Grand Prix">Silverstone</a> and he suffered his second successive mechanical retirement, with a hydraulics failure. Only two laps later, <a href="/wiki/Vitantonio_Liuzzi" title="Vitantonio Liuzzi">Vitantonio Liuzzi</a> became the fourth and final retirement of the race when his car had an electrical failure.
</p><p>Towards the front, Massa and Vettel started to move through the field, passing Kobayashi and Petrov. In the second pit-stop phase Webber pitted first, but was lapping slowly after stop struggling to get the new tyres up to temperature. The mechanical grip of Hamilton's McLaren in cold conditions helped him, and he got past Webber in the stops with the opposite effect of the undercut. Webber tried to overtake Hamilton on the outside of Turn 2, Hamilton kept him behind. Alonso was the last to pit, and came out in the lead, but due to his tyres not being at operating temperature, Hamilton made an easy pass at Turn 2 and got past Alonso to retake the lead.
</p><p>Hamilton was then first to pit for the medium compound tyres and got out in front of Alonso and Webber, he pulled away and took the race victory. All the drivers on three-stop strategies pitted for the primes in the last ten laps – not wanting to go on to the medium compound, which was 1.5 seconds slower per lap than the softer tyres, for too long. The battle between Massa and Vettel for fourth went to the pits on the penultimate lap, Massa had a slower pit stop than Vettel, and Vettel got out in front after he had not been able to pass on track. After the top 3, Vettel, Massa and Sutil completed the top six with Rosberg ahead of Schumacher, Kobayashi, from 17th on the grid, finished in ninth and Petrov completed the points finishers in tenth, ahead of Kobayashi's teammate <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a>. After the race, Fernando Alonso stopped his Ferrari on the circuit, and got a lift back to parc ferme on the sidepod of Mark Webber's Red Bull.
</p>
