<p>The <b>2000 Spanish Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>XLII Gran Premio Marlboro de España</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 7 May 2000 at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Barcelona-Catalunya" title="Circuit de Barcelona-Catalunya">Circuit de Catalunya</a>, <a href="/wiki/Montmel%C3%B3" title="Montmeló">Montmeló</a>, <a href="/wiki/Barcelona" title="Barcelona">Barcelona</a>, <a href="/wiki/Catalonia" title="Catalonia">Catalonia</a>, Spain. It was the fifth round of the <a href="/wiki/2000_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2000 Formula One season">2000 Formula One season</a> and the 42nd <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Grand_Prix" title="Spanish Grand Prix">Spanish Grand Prix</a>. The 65-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> who started from the second position. His teammate <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> finished second and <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> finished third in a <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> started from the <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> and held off a challenge from Häkkinen on the first lap. He maintained the lead until his first pit stop on lap 24 when a refueller was struck by his rear tyre, allowing Häkkinen to take over the lead until his pit stop two laps later. Michael Schumacher kept the lead for a further twenty-one laps as he and Häkkinen made their second pit stops together, with Häkkinen emerging in front because Schumacher experienced a slow pit stop. Häkkinen kept the lead for the remaining twenty-three laps to win the race.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The victory was Häkkinen's first of the season, and put him into second place in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_champions" class="mw-redirect" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; champions">World Drivers' Championship</a>, fourteen points behind Michael Schumacher. Coulthard's second-place finish meant that he dropped to third, and Barrichello third place moved him ahead of Ralf Schumacher. In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">World Constructors' Championship</a>, McLaren's one-two finish allowed them to move within seven points from leaders Ferrari. Williams remained in third on 15 points, with twelve races remaining in the season.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2000_Spanish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Grand Prix was contested by eleven teams with two drivers each.<sup id="cite_ref-entrants_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-entrants-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The teams (also known as <a href="/wiki/Formula_One#Constructors" title="Formula One">constructors</a>) were <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jaguar_Racing" title="Jaguar Racing">Jaguar</a>, <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a>, <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Prost_Grand_Prix" title="Prost Grand Prix">Prost</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arrows_Grand_Prix_International" title="Arrows Grand Prix International">Arrows</a>, <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a> and <a href="/wiki/British_American_Racing" title="British American Racing">British American Racing</a> (BAR).<sup id="cite_ref-entrants_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-entrants-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_tyre" class="mw-redirect" title="Formula One tyre">Tyre</a> supplier <a href="/wiki/Bridgestone" title="Bridgestone">Bridgestone</a> brought two different tyre types to the race; two dry compounds (soft and medium).<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Before the race Ferrari driver <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> led the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_champions" class="mw-redirect" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; champions">Drivers' Championship</a> with 34 <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Championship_points_scoring_systems" title="List of Formula One World Championship points scoring systems">points</a>, ahead of <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> (14 points) and <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> (twelve). <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> were tied in fourth place with nine points each.<sup id="cite_ref-points_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a> Ferrari led with 43 points, seventeen points ahead of rival McLaren in second. Williams were third with twelve points, while Benetton and Jordan with eight points each contended for fourth place.<sup id="cite_ref-points_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Ferrari had dominated the championship, winning three out of the previous four races, with Coulthard winning the <a href="/wiki/2000_British_Grand_Prix" title="2000 British Grand Prix">British Grand Prix</a>. Barrichello, Häkkinen and <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> had second-place finishes, and <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a> had finished third.<sup id="cite_ref-points_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After the British Grand Prix on 23 April, all teams conducted in-season testing at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Barcelona-Catalunya" title="Circuit de Barcelona-Catalunya">Circuit de Catalunya</a> on 25–28 April to prepare for the upcoming Spanish Grand Prix. <a href="/wiki/Jos_Verstappen" title="Jos Verstappen">Jos Verstappen</a> was fastest on the first day of testing despite spinning into the <a href="/wiki/Run-off_area" title="Run-off area">gravel</a> early in the session, ahead of McLaren test driver <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Panis" title="Olivier Panis">Olivier Panis</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher was quickest on the second day.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher remained the fastest driver on the third day of testing. The session was disrupted because several drivers stopped on the circuit with problems with their cars.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> On the final day, (which was held in wet weather conditions in the morning and during the end of the session) Michael Schumacher stayed the quickest driver on the final day.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the week leading up to the race, Coulthard was leasing the <a href="/wiki/Learjet" title="Learjet">Learjet</a> of friend <a href="/wiki/David_Murray_(Scottish_businessman)" title="David Murray (Scottish businessman)">David Murray</a> when the aeroplane developed engine trouble en route to <a href="/wiki/C%C3%B4te_d%27Azur_International_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Côte d&#39;Azur International Airport">Côte d'Azur International Airport</a> in <a href="/wiki/Nice" title="Nice">Nice</a>, and crashed while attempting an emergency landing at <a href="/wiki/Saint-Exup%C3%A9ry_International_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Saint-Exupéry International Airport">Lyon-Satolas Airport</a>, France. Coulthard, his then-girlfriend the American model Heidi Wichlinski and personal trainer/bodyguard Andy Matthews survived; Murray's personal pilot David Saunders and co-pilot Dan Worley died.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard suffered from bruises to his right rib cage and severe grazed elbows in the crash. <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">FIA</a> Formula One Safety and Medical Delegate <a href="/wiki/Sid_Watkins" title="Sid Watkins">Sid Watkins</a> declared that Coulthard was passed fit to compete in the race.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>BAR driver <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a> was passed fit in the days leading up to the race. At the <a href="/wiki/2000_Brazilian_Grand_Prix" title="2000 Brazilian Grand Prix">Brazilian Grand Prix</a>, Villeneuve suffered back injury after his seat became loose during the race and the effects became worse and it caused him to withdraw from testing in Barcelona.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> He was also required to undergo physiotherapy. BAR had their official test driver <a href="/wiki/Darren_Manning" title="Darren Manning">Darren Manning</a> available to replace Villeneuve should the need arise.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Some teams made modifications to their <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_cars" class="mw-redirect" title="Formula One cars">cars</a> in preparation for the event. Minardi débuted a new gearbox which was constructed out of titanium, the first for a Formula One team.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Prost made modifications to their cars oil system to improve the reliability of their engines and made minor aerodynamic changes to the front wing and the floor. The team also used an updated version of their engine the Peugeot A20 EV2 during the Friday practice sessions and reverted to the EV3 for the remainder of the weekend.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> BAR fitted their cars with a revised aerodynamic package which included new bargeboards and rear wings.<sup id="cite_ref-fripractice_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fripractice-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice">Practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2000_Spanish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p>Four practice sessions were held before the Sunday race, two each on Friday and Saturday. The Friday morning and afternoon sessions each lasted an hour; the third and fourth sessions, on Saturday morning, lasted 45 minutes each.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Conditions were dry and overcast for the Friday practice sessions and was held on a dirty track which was caused by rainfall in the run up to the event.<sup id="cite_ref-fripractice_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fripractice-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher set the first session's fastest time with an early lap of 1 minute and 21.982 seconds, half a second quicker than teammate Barrichello. Häkkinen had the third fastest time, ahead of Ralf Schumacher and <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a>. The two Benetton drivers were sixth and eighth (with Fisichella ahead of <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Wurz" title="Alexander Wurz">Alexander Wurz</a>); the two were separated by <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Herbert" title="Johnny Herbert">Johnny Herbert</a>. <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a> and Verstappen completed the top ten.<sup id="cite_ref-fripractice_15-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fripractice-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> In the second practice session, Michael Schumacher was unable to improve his time but remained fastest;<sup id="cite_ref-atlasfrip2_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasfrip2-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Ralf Schumacher had the second fastest time after putting on a new set of tyres towards the end of the session.<sup id="cite_ref-fripractice_15-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fripractice-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello was third-fastest, ahead of <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a>, Coulthard and <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a>. Häkkinen, <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Diniz" title="Pedro Diniz">Pedro Diniz</a>, Frentzen and Alesi followed in the top ten.<sup id="cite_ref-atlasfrip2_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasfrip2-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The weather remained dry for the Saturday morning practice sessions.<sup id="cite_ref-satfia_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-satfia-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard set the fastest lap of the third session at 1:21.370, ahead of teammate Häkkinen who was two-tenths of a second slower. Ralf Schumacher (with a time of 1:21.604) was third quickest, ahead of Michael Schumacher and Villeneuve. Fisichella and Frentzen followed in sixth and seventh. The two Arrows drivers were eighth and ninth (with Verstappen ahead of <a href="/wiki/Pedro_de_la_Rosa" title="Pedro de la Rosa">Pedro de la Rosa</a>). <a href="/wiki/Mika_Salo" title="Mika Salo">Mika Salo</a> rounded out the top ten.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> During the final practice session, Michael Schumacher used a new set of tyres and set the fastest time of 1:21.088; Coulthard finished with the second quickest lap. Barrichello was third fastest, in front of Häkkinen who struggled with a lack of <a href="/wiki/Grip_(auto_racing)" title="Grip (auto racing)">grip</a> and a loose rear end. Ralf Schumacher recorded the fifth fastest time. The Jordan drivers were sixth and seventh (with Trulli in front of Frentzen). de la Rosa, Villeneuve and Button completed the top ten ahead of qualifying.<sup id="cite_ref-satfia_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-satfia-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2000_Spanish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatright" style="width:33%;&#32;;"><style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r887775652">.mw-parser-output .quotebox{background-color:#F9F9F9;border:1px solid #aaa;box-sizing:border-box;padding:10px;font-size:88%;max-width:100%}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft{margin:0.5em 1.4em 0.8em 0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright{margin:0.5em 0 0.8em 1.4em}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.centered{margin:0.5em auto 0.8em auto}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft p,.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright p{font-style:inherit}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-title{background-color:#F9F9F9;text-align:center;font-size:larger;font-weight:bold}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:before{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" “ ";vertical-align:-45%;line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:after{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" ” ";line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .left-aligned{text-align:left}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .right-aligned{text-align:right}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .center-aligned{text-align:center}.mw-parser-output .quotebox cite{display:block;font-style:normal}@media screen and (max-width:360px){.mw-parser-output .quotebox{min-width:100%;margin:0 0 0.8em!important;float:none!important}}</style>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">It was very close today and if you got everything right then the lap would be good. Certainly it is nice to be on pole position for the first time this season. At the start I decided to do a run with two quick laps, but after that I switched to doing just one flying lap. The increase in wind and temperature made it a bit more difficult towards the end. I was not completely happy with the car, even though I had a clean run, so we made some adjustments but they did not work out the way I wanted. As for the race, the start will be important because it is easier to plan your tactics and control it in the lead. But we have proved this season we can also win from behind.</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style="">Michael Schumacher after taking the pole position.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>Saturday afternoon's one hour qualifying session saw each driver limited to twelve laps, with the <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_motorsport_terms#G" title="Glossary of motorsport terms">grid</a> order decided by their fastest laps. During this session the <a href="/wiki/107%25_rule" title="107% rule">107% rule</a> was in effect, requiring each driver to remain within 107 per cent of the fastest lap time to qualify for the race.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> The session was held in sunny and hot weather.<sup id="cite_ref-satfia_19-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-satfia-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-temp_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-temp-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher clinched the first <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> of the season and the 24th of his career, with a time of 1:20.974 on his second run. He was joined on the front row of the grid by Häkkinen who set a lap 0.076 seconds slower in the closing seconds of qualifying.<sup id="cite_ref-atlasquali_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasquali-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello qualified third, and felt that his car did not feel good having not made any changes.<sup id="cite_ref-satfia_19-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-satfia-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard secured fourth position, despite losing time early in qualifying because of a fuel pressure problem. He decided against taking his team's <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_motorsport_terms#S" title="Glossary of motorsport terms">spare car</a> set up for Häkkinen because he believed it would take up time and had to run more fuel throughout the remainder of the session.<sup id="cite_ref-satfia_19-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-satfia-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-atlasquali_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasquali-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-gprace_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gprace-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Ralf Schumacher qualified fifth and he was suffering from excessive oversteer which prevented him from setting a quicker lap time but was delighted with his starting position.<sup id="cite_ref-atlasquali_23-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasquali-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Villeneuve secured sixth position and said he achieved the best from his car.<sup id="cite_ref-satfia_19-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-satfia-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Both Jordan drivers lined up the fourth row of the grid (with Trulli ahead of Frentzen) and believed they could have a better starting position because of windy conditions.<sup id="cite_ref-satfia_19-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-satfia-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-atlasquali_23-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasquali-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> de la Rosa and Irvine rounded out the top ten fastest drivers, with de la Rosa confident of finishing in a higher position in his home country.<sup id="cite_ref-atlasquali_23-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasquali-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Later on Saturday afternoon, a fuel sample from de la Rosa's car was analysed and declared illegal by the FIA and Arrows announced that it would appeal the decision, allowing de la Rosa to retain his starting position.<sup id="cite_ref-gprace_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gprace-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Button, eleventh, reported his car felt unstable due to the windy conditions.<sup id="cite_ref-satfia_19-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-satfia-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> He was ahead of Verstappen in the slower of the two Arrows, Salo in the faster Sauber, and Fisichella.<sup id="cite_ref-atlasquali_23-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasquali-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Herbert, fifteenth, lost his front wing mounted camera after hitting a kerb in the early part of qualifying and made a minor mistake which prevented him from starting higher up the field. Diniz had excessive oversteer and managed sixteenth place.<sup id="cite_ref-satfia_19-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-satfia-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Ricardo_Zonta" title="Ricardo Zonta">Ricardo Zonta</a> managed seventeenth after he was unable to find a set-up which suited him and his fastest time was nine-tenths of a second slower than teammate Villeneuve. Alesi qualified eighteenth, ahead of Wurz in nineteenth. Heidfeld had engine problems and had to use the spare Prost <a href="/wiki/Monocoque" title="Monocoque">monocoque</a> and took 20th position. The two Minardi drivers <a href="/wiki/Marc_Gen%C3%A9" title="Marc Gené">Marc Gené</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gast%C3%B3n_Mazzacane" title="Gastón Mazzacane">Gastón Mazzacane</a> qualified at the rear of the field in positions 21 and 22.<sup id="cite_ref-atlasquali_23-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasquali-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Warm-up">Warm-up</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2000_Spanish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Warm-up">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Mika_Haekkinen_2006.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/53/Mika_Haekkinen_2006.jpg/170px-Mika_Haekkinen_2006.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="230" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/53/Mika_Haekkinen_2006.jpg/255px-Mika_Haekkinen_2006.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/53/Mika_Haekkinen_2006.jpg/340px-Mika_Haekkinen_2006.jpg 2x" data-file-width="549" data-file-height="743" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Mika_Haekkinen_2006.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> (pictured in 2006) took his first victory of the season in the race.</div></div></div>
<p>The drivers took to the track at 09:30 <a href="/wiki/Central_European_Summer_Time" title="Central European Summer Time">Central European Summer Time</a> (<a href="/wiki/Coordinated_Universal_Time" title="Coordinated Universal Time">UTC</a>+2) for a 30-minute warm-up<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_16-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> in cool and dry weather. Michael Schumacher maintained his good performance, setting a time of 1:22.855 despite going into the gravel after braking too deep and also drove the spare Ferrari. Häkkinen was the second-fastest driver; Barrichello was third and Coulthard rounded out the top four.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> After the session ended, Arrows withdrew their appeal after they accepted the FIA's findings and de la Rosa was required to start at the back of the field.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2000_Spanish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The conditions on the grid were dry before the race; the air temperature ranged between 21 to 30&#160;°C (70 to 86&#160;°F) and the track temperature was between 22 to 23&#160;°C (72 to 73&#160;°F).<sup id="cite_ref-temp_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-temp-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> The race started at 14:00 local time. A total of approximately 79,000 people attended the race.<sup id="cite_ref-gprace_24-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gprace-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Ralf Schumacher started the race in the spare Williams monocoque because his race car developed a sensor issue with his engine the previous day.<sup id="cite_ref-f1comrace_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> When the race started, Häkkinen got a better start than Michael Schumacher but the latter moved across the circuit to block Häkkinen. Ralf Schumacher also got a good start and passed Coulthard and Barrichello.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> At the exit of the first corner, Ralf Schumacher made contact with the rear of Häkkinen's car, sending him slightly sideways and both drivers were able to continue.<sup id="cite_ref-gprace_24-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gprace-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Button also made a good start, moving from eleventh to ninth by the end of the first lap, while Irvine dropped three places over the same distance.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_29-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Diniz spun off into the gravel on the first lap and retired.<sup id="cite_ref-f1comrace_28-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_29-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> At the end of the first lap, the top ten were Michael Schumacher, Häkkinen, Ralf Schumacher, Coulthard, Barrichello, Villeneuve, Frentzen, Trulli, Button and Salo.<sup id="cite_ref-lapchart_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapchart-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:David_Coulthard_2007.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d8/David_Coulthard_2007.jpg/170px-David_Coulthard_2007.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="250" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d8/David_Coulthard_2007.jpg/255px-David_Coulthard_2007.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d8/David_Coulthard_2007.jpg/340px-David_Coulthard_2007.jpg 2x" data-file-width="500" data-file-height="734" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:David_Coulthard_2007.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> (pictured in 2007) finished second.</div></div></div>
<p>Michael Schumacher began to pull away from Häkkinen.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_29-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> de la Rosa ran into the rear of Alesi at Wurth corner on lap two causing Alesi to retire, with de la Rosa sustaining a broken front wing and retired after going off at Campsa corner.<sup id="cite_ref-f1comrace_28-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_29-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Further back, Irvine passed Verstappen for twelfth position.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_29-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> The positions at the front were the same for the next sixteen laps with the gap between Michael Schumacher and Häkkinen fluctuated from 1.6 seconds to 3.4 seconds while Coulthard and Barrichello battled Ralf Schumacher for third position.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_29-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-lapchart_30-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapchart-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Button became the first driver to make a <a href="/wiki/Pit_stop" title="Pit stop">pit stop</a> on lap 18 and rejoined in front of Mazzacane.<sup id="cite_ref-f1comrace_28-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_29-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Villeneuve made his pit stop on the 21st lap, which promoted Frentzen into the points-scoring positions.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_29-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> After rejoining the circuit, his car caught fire and he pulled off to the side of the track at the exit of Seat corner.<sup id="cite_ref-gprace_24-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gprace-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-f1comrace_28-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Trulli's pit stop, which dropped him to 17th, proved problematic: he stalled his engine which required his mechanics to restart it. Ralf Schumacher made a pit stop one lap later and re-emerged in fifth.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_29-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> On lap 24, Michael Schumacher made his pit stop. As he accelerated from his pit box, his rear wheel hit the Ferrari refuller <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Stepney" title="Nigel Stepney">Nigel Stepney</a> who was dragged before being knocked over.<sup id="cite_ref-gprace_24-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gprace-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_29-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Stepney was taken to the circuit's medical center for observation and he was replaced by reserve refuller Andrea Vacari. Coulthard made a pit stop on the same lap and experienced problems with leaving his pit box because he selected second gear and re-emerged behind Ralf Schumacher.<sup id="cite_ref-gprace_24-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gprace-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Häkkinen made his pit stop on the 26th lap and emerged behind Michael Schumacher, while Verstappen retired from the race with a mechanical problem.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_29-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> All of the leaders had taken their stops by the start of lap 28, the top ten were Michael Schumacher, Häkkinen, Ralf Schumacher, Barrichello, Coulthard, Button, Frentzen, Salo, Zonta and Wurz.<sup id="cite_ref-lapchart_30-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapchart-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Häkkinen set the <a href="/wiki/Fastest_lap" title="Fastest lap">fastest lap</a> of the race on the same lap, a 1:24.470 as he started to close the gap to Michael Schumacher, as Ralf Schumacher again became under pressure by Coulthard in fourth.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_29-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-lapchart_30-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapchart-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Eleven laps later, Coulthard entered the pit lane, triggering the second round of pit stops. Ralf Schumacher and Barrichello made a pit stop on the following lap and rejoined behind Coulthard.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_29-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher and Häkkinen both made their pit stops on the 41st lap, with Häkkinen emerging in front because Michael Schumacher's pit stop was problematic. Michael Schumacher's mechanics had started refulling after putting his tyres on causing him to be stationary for ten seconds longer than usual.<sup id="cite_ref-gprace_24-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gprace-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-f1comrace_28-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Salo and Zonta went into the pit lane on lap 45, allowing Button into sixth.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_29-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Barrichello_2002.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/36/Barrichello_2002.jpg/170px-Barrichello_2002.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="298" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/36/Barrichello_2002.jpg/255px-Barrichello_2002.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/36/Barrichello_2002.jpg/340px-Barrichello_2002.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1128" data-file-height="1975" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Barrichello_2002.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> (pictured in 2002) finished third.</div></div></div>
<p>Coulthard had caught Michael Schumacher (who had air leaking from his left rear tyre) by lap 46. Coulthard attempted to pass Michael Schumacher on the following lap around the outside going into the first corner, but Schumacher drove into Coulthard's line as a blocking manoeuvre. Coulthard replicated his move on lap 48 and passed Michael Schumacher around the outside under braking going into turn one.<sup id="cite_ref-gprace_24-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gprace-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Ralf Schumacher had caught up to Michael Schumacher on lap 50.<sup id="cite_ref-lapchart_30-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapchart-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Ralf Schumacher tried to overtake Michael Schumacher on the same lap, but the latter blocked him. Ralf Schumacher was forced wide and lost momentum, allowing Barrichello to pass both drivers.<sup id="cite_ref-gprace_24-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gprace-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Ralf Schumacher attempted to reclaim the position and got alongside Barrichello with Michael Schumacher taking the inside line to emerge in front.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_29-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher entered the pit lane on the same lap for new tyres and rejoined in front of Button.<sup id="cite_ref-lapchart_30-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapchart-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> He immediately set new personal fastest laps in an effort to catch Ralf Schumacher.<sup id="cite_ref-f1comrace_28-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> However, the order was stable at the front of the field as Häkkinen had a comfortable lead over Coulthard, while Ralf Schumacher had dropped back from Barrichello.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_29-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Button pulled over to the side of the track on lap 62, with smoke billowing from his engine, and retired.<sup id="cite_ref-gprace_24-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gprace-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-f1comrace_28-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_29-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Häkkinen continued to maintain his lead and crossed the finish line on lap 65 to secure his first victory of the season in a time of 1:33.550.390 at an average speed of 122.040 miles per hour (196.404&#160;km/h). Coulthard finished second 16 seconds behind, ahead of Barrichello who clinched third, Ralf Schumacher in fourth, Michael Schumacher in fifth and Frentzen rounded out the points-scoring positions in sixth. Salo, Zonta, Fisichella, Wurz and Irvine filled the next five finishers, while Trulli, Herbert, Gené, Mazzacane, Heidfeld and Button (despite his retirement) completed the final classified finishers.<sup id="cite_ref-classification_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-classification-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2000_Spanish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatright" style="width:33%;&#32;;"><link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r887775652"/>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">I can't really find the words to explain how happy and relieved I am to have claimed my first victory this season. We have been so close before but today was the day and the team did a fantastic job. My start from second wasn't ideal, despite having a lot of traction I didn't get the clutch to bite properly. Fortunately Schumacher didn't make a great start either, so we were side by side coming into the first corner. In the end I had to give way because I was on the outside. Schumacher managed to pull away during the first couple of laps but when his tyres started to go off I was able to catch him and could start putting him under pressure. The team did a great job in the second pitstop so I was able to take the lead.</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style="">Mika Häkkinen after the race.<sup id="cite_ref-quotes_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-quotes-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>The top three drivers appeared on the podium to collect their trophies and in a later <a href="/wiki/News_conference" class="mw-redirect" title="News conference">press conference</a>. Häkkinen said that he was "pleased" to win the race and admitted that he waited until the pit stop phase to have any chance of overtaking Michael Schumacher. He said that his team had more work to do on his car and was looking forward to the next race.<sup id="cite_ref-pressconf_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconf-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard believed he made the right decision to compete in the Grand Prix despite his injuries and that the result was "the best thing that could have happened for the team and myself."<sup id="cite_ref-pressconf_33-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconf-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> He also said he was looking forward to resting over the coming days to allow his injuries to heal.<sup id="cite_ref-pressconf_33-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconf-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello admitted that the race had not been good for him until his overtake on Michael Schumacher and Ralf Schumacher and said the absence of Stepney caused his second pit stop to be slower than usual.<sup id="cite_ref-pressconf_33-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconf-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Attention focused on the battle between Ralf Schumacher and Michael Schumacher during the race. Michael Schumacher blamed his brother for instigating the move and did not understand why he was upset over the incident. Ralf Schumacher did not comment and stated that he would watch a video of the incident before he would say anything. Their father, Rolf talked to the pair in an effort to get them reconciled.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> One week after the race, Ralf Schumacher said that tensions between him and his brother had eased and that there were no hard feelings for each other, saying, "It's a lot of fun when we are out there on the track duelling against each other."<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> There was similar ill-feeling between de la Rosa and Alesi after their collision on lap two. de la Rosa accused Alesi of blocking him, and of causing the crash by not looking in his mirrors, while Alesi said de la Rosa attempted to overtake him in a corner where overtaking is difficult.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As a result of the race, Häkkinen moved up to second place in the Drivers' Championship, reducing Michael Schumacher's lead to fourteen points. Coulthard fell to third, two points behind Häkkinen. Barrichello moved up into fourth place on 13 points, and Ralf Schumacher fell to fifth overall.<sup id="cite_ref-points_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In the Constructors' Championship, Ferrari maintained their lead but McLaren's one-two finish moved to within seven points clear of the Italian team. Williams maintained third position with nine points. Frentzen's sixth-place finish allowed Jordan to move point clear of Benetton in the battle for fourth, with twelve races remaining in the season.<sup id="cite_ref-points_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
