<p>The <b>2007 French Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>XCIII Grand Prix de France</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 1 July 2007 at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Nevers_Magny-Cours" title="Circuit de Nevers Magny-Cours">Circuit de Nevers Magny-Cours</a>, <a href="/wiki/Magny-Cours" title="Magny-Cours">Magny-Cours</a>, <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>. It was the eighth race of the <a href="/wiki/2007_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2007 Formula One season">2007 Formula One season</a>. The 70-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> team after starting from third position. <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a>, who started the race from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>, finished second in the latter Ferrari, with <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> third in a <a href="/wiki/McLaren_(racing)" class="mw-redirect" title="McLaren (racing)">McLaren</a> car.
</p><p>Massa controlled most of the race from the front, but Räikkönen overtook him during the second round of <a href="/wiki/Pit_stop" title="Pit stop">pit stops</a> to take the lead.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2007_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2007_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Following the <a href="/wiki/2007_United_States_Grand_Prix" title="2007 United States Grand Prix">United States Grand Prix</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> teams headed to <a href="/wiki/Silverstone_Circuit" title="Silverstone Circuit">Silverstone</a> for a three-day test. Nine teams participated, with the exception of <a href="/wiki/Honda_Racing_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Honda Racing F1">Honda</a> and <a href="/wiki/Super_Aguri_F1" title="Super Aguri F1">Super Aguri</a>, who opted to test at the <a href="/wiki/Circuito_Permanente_de_Jerez" class="mw-redirect" title="Circuito Permanente de Jerez">Jerez circuit</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Neither <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> nor <a href="/wiki/McLaren_(racing)" class="mw-redirect" title="McLaren (racing)">McLaren</a> were fastest on the first two days at Silverstone, rather it was <a href="/wiki/Toyota_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Toyota F1">Toyota</a> that was fastest on both of the days.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> However, on the third and final day of testing <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> put Ferrari on top with a time of 1:20.805. The nearest challenger, <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> was 0.469 behind, with <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> a further 0.010 behind.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> With Ferrari fastest on the third day, both of their drivers, Massa and <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> were very confident heading into the French round of the <a href="/wiki/2007_Formula_One_Season" class="mw-redirect" title="2007 Formula One Season">season</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Off track Ferrari launched a <a href="/wiki/2007_Formula_One_espionage_controversy" title="2007 Formula One espionage controversy">criminal investigation</a> in <a href="/wiki/Modena" title="Modena">Modena</a> against their own employee <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Stepney" title="Nigel Stepney">Nigel Stepney</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Stepney's lawyer ruled out sabotage claims,<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> and Stepney said it was part of a "dirty tricks" campaign.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>There was also controversy at the rear-end of the grid, as <a href="/wiki/Spyker_F1" title="Spyker F1">Spyker</a> asked the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile</a> (FIA) to look at the new updates that were put on the Super Aguri at <a href="/wiki/Indianapolis_Motor_Speedway" title="Indianapolis Motor Speedway">Indianapolis</a> to see whether the Aguri team are receiving current Honda parts.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> On Saturday, Super Aguri's managing director Daniel Audetto said "We have rules – they [Spyker] can just protest. Tell them to protest – if I have something to complain about, I will make a protest."<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a> was back in his <a href="/wiki/BMW_Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="BMW Sauber">BMW</a> after his crash at the <a href="/wiki/2007_Canadian_Grand_Prix" title="2007 Canadian Grand Prix">Canadian Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Early on Saturday, <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> was cleared to continue in his BMW after experiencing back pains during Friday practice.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice">Practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2007_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Ferrari dominated both practice sessions on the Friday, with Räikkönen fastest in the first Practice Session and <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> was fastest in the second Practice Session. Behind the Ferraris, Alonso was third in his McLaren, but seven tenths behind, with his teammate and World Championship leader <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> sixth, but lost nearly an hour of the session due to car trouble. The two McLarens were split by <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Hamilton managed to recover from his morning trouble to post the fourth fastest time in the second Practice Session on Friday afternoon. The Ferraris were still leading, but Massa was fastest, just 0.035 seconds ahead of Räikkönen. However, one of the major surprises came from <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Scuderia Toro Rosso</a>, as <a href="/wiki/Scott_Speed" title="Scott Speed">Scott Speed</a> posted the third quickest time, with <a href="/wiki/Vitantonio_Liuzzi" title="Vitantonio Liuzzi">Vitantonio Liuzzi</a> posting the fifth quickest time. During the session, Liuzzi was involved in a bizarre incident with <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Davidson" title="Anthony Davidson">Anthony Davidson</a>; Davidson exited his garage, and smashed his Super Aguri into the side of Liuzzi's Toro Rosso; knocking his front wing off in the accident.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Alonso finished the second practice session eighth.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the final practice session on Saturday morning, Hamilton managed to beat Ferrari, with the Englishman ahead of second-placed Massa by 0.063 seconds. Hamilton and the two Ferraris completed the top three, but Alonso was again down in eighth, having missed nearly the whole of the session with a faulty brake sensor.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renaults</a> sparked a return to form with <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> fourth and fifth, both ahead of rivals BMW, who were sixth and fifteenth respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2007_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Both <a href="/wiki/Spyker_F1" title="Spyker F1">Spykers</a> and both <a href="/wiki/Super_Aguri_F1" title="Super Aguri F1">Super Aguris</a> were knocked out of the first phase of qualifying, along with <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Wurz" title="Alexander Wurz">Alexander Wurz</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Williams_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams F1">Williams</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vitantonio_Liuzzi" title="Vitantonio Liuzzi">Vitantonio Liuzzi</a>'s Toro Rosso. For Super Aguri's <a href="/wiki/Takuma_Sato" title="Takuma Sato">Takuma Sato</a>, it did not matter where he qualified, as he was docked ten places, due to overtaking <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> under yellow flags at the last Grand Prix.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Spyker's <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a> was hoping for a wet race after an unspectacular qualifying.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> At the front end of the grid, the McLarens were first and fourth, with Hamilton on top, and the Ferraris splitting them in second and third. <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> rounded out the top five.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> failed to complete a single timed lap in the second part of qualifying due to a gearbox problem, and started sixteenth.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Both <a href="/wiki/Honda_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Honda F1">Hondas</a> were knocked out also, along with <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scott_Speed" title="Scott Speed">Scott Speed</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a>. Both <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Button</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Barrichello</a> were happier with the upgraded Honda, with Button saying "the car is certainly better than the last race in Indianapolis, although the positions don't reflect that".<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton was again fastest in Part two, with teammate Alonso down in fifth. Massa, Räikkönen and <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Kubica</a> rounded out the top four.
</p><p>After topping the first two parts of qualifying, Hamilton dropped to second in the third and final part, with Massa taking pole position, just 0.070 ahead of the Englishman. Massa stated in the post-Qualifying press conference that "it looks like we [Ferrari] are back and fighting",<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> with Hamilton believing pole was possible had he not made a mistake at Turn 15.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Räikkönen qualified third as he lost time on one corner, which he called "all my fault".<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Alonso was unable to complete a single lap in the session due to a gearbox problem. He was classified tenth and stated that he'd "prefer a wet race".<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Behind Räikkönen was Kubica in fourth, but despite qualifying fifth, <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> believed there was "potential for more" from the Renault.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> Fisichella's teammate Kovalainen was sixth, with <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> seventh. <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> and Alonso rounded out the top ten.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Rosberg also had a slight gearbox problem in the final part of qualifying, which he believed cost him a few tenths coming into the final few corners.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2007_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Massa_Hamilton_France.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b9/Massa_Hamilton_France.JPG/250px-Massa_Hamilton_France.JPG" decoding="async" width="250" height="191" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b9/Massa_Hamilton_France.JPG/375px-Massa_Hamilton_France.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b9/Massa_Hamilton_France.JPG/500px-Massa_Hamilton_France.JPG 2x" data-file-width="1000" data-file-height="764" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Massa_Hamilton_France.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> line up on the front row of the starting grid</div></div></div>
<p>Three people were killed in a helicopter crash at the circuit on Saturday night. They were <a href="/w/index.php?title=Emmanuel_Longobardi&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Emmanuel Longobardi (page does not exist)">Emmanuel Longobardi</a>, a PR operative; the pilot Pierre Bennehard; and Simon McGill, a New Zealand national. A Bridgestone employee and his niece were injured.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Longobardi was a popular member of the <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> Paddock.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a> in the Spyker opted to start from the pitlane. Massa got off to the best possible start and retained his lead, but Räikkönen passed Hamilton into Turn One. At the back of the field, <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Davidson" title="Anthony Davidson">Anthony Davidson</a> hit the back of <a href="/wiki/Vitantonio_Liuzzi" title="Vitantonio Liuzzi">Vitantonio Liuzzi</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso</a>. The Toro Rosso came back onto the track and smashed into the side of the <a href="/wiki/Super_Aguri_F1" title="Super Aguri F1">Super Aguri</a>. Liuzzi said afterwards that "all we can do is wait for Silverstone and hope that the definite improvement we have made with the car pays off".<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> At the Adelaide hairpin, <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> rammed into the back of <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a>. Trulli was out, but Kovalainen continued at the back of the field. Trulli apologised to Kovalainen after the race, and declared it "a racing accident".<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> The tangle promoted Alonso up to eighth.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a> was quickly losing ground on the two <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferraris</a> and Hamilton. Alonso passed <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Rosberg</a> for seventh, and quickly closed in on <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Heidfeld</a>, but stayed behind him until he pitted on Lap 16. Alonso attempted to get past on Lap&#160;five, but ran wide, giving the position back to the German. Hamilton also pitted on Lap 16, with Massa pitting on Lap 19 and Räikkönen on Lap 21.
</p><p>Räikkönen decreased Massa's lead back down from four&#160;seconds to two&#160;seconds. Alonso passed both Heidfeld and <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Fisichella</a> in the middle section of the race. In the second round of stops, Räikkönen pitted two laps after Massa. These two laps extra gave Räikkönen the lead after his second stop, with Massa now second. Alonso pitted for the second time on lap 35. Heidfeld and Fisichella pitted several laps later, and both of them got out in front of Alonso. <a href="/wiki/Christijan_Albers" title="Christijan Albers">Christijan Albers</a> had an unusual accident, his car left the pit lane with the fuel rig attached without being detached by the pit crews.  Eventually, he drove to the side of the track and retired.
</p><p>Räikkönen won the race from teammate Massa, with Hamilton third. This marked Ferrari's first one-two of the season. Massa stated that the race win was lost "because of traffic",<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> while teammate Räikkönen stated he was "much happier with the car", as he became the first Finn to win the race.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Kubica was a lonely fourth, with Fisichella, Heidfeld, Alonso and <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Button</a> rounding out the points.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Despite it being Button's first points of 2007, he said that he was "not getting too excited about it".<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> Despite finishing seventh, Alonso was still confident about his title chances, saying that he hoped the "two points are important at the end of the season".<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
