<p>The <b>Abu Dhabi Grand Prix</b> (<a href="/wiki/Arabic_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>: <span lang="ar" dir="rtl">سباق جائزة أبوظبي الكبرى</span>&#8206;) is a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a>. It was announced in early 2007 at the Abu Dhabi F1 Festival in the <a href="/wiki/United_Arab_Emirates" title="United Arab Emirates">United Arab Emirates</a>. The first race took place on 1 November 2009, held at the <a href="/wiki/Hermann_Tilke" title="Hermann Tilke">Hermann Tilke</a> designed <a href="/wiki/Yas_Marina_Circuit" title="Yas Marina Circuit">Yas Marina Circuit</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-YasMarina2009_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-YasMarina2009-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 25 June 2008 the <a href="/wiki/FIA" class="mw-redirect" title="FIA">FIA</a> announced the provisional <a href="/wiki/2009_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2009 Formula One World Championship">2009</a> Formula One calendar including the Abu Dhabi Grand Prix as the 19th and final race of the season on 15 November. On 5 November 2008, however, it was announced that the race would be held as the season finale on 1 November, two weeks before the initially planned date, as the 17th and final race.<sup id="cite_ref-WorldMotor2009_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WorldMotor2009-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The inaugural race was <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a>'s first ever day–night race, starting at 17:00 <a href="/wiki/Time_zone" title="Time zone">local time</a>.  Floodlights used to illuminate the circuit were switched on from the start of the event to ensure a seamless transition from daylight to darkness.<sup id="cite_ref-AbuDhabi2009_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AbuDhabi2009-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Subsequent Abu Dhabi Grands Prix have also been day–night races.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Origin">Origin</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Abu_Dhabi_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Origin">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Formula 1 first came to Abu Dhabi in 2007 in the guise of the first ever Formula One Festival.<sup id="cite_ref-Countdownunderway2007_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Countdownunderway2007-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Announced in January 2007,<sup id="cite_ref-Festivalcoming2007_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Festivalcoming2007-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> the event which took place on 3 February 2007 was free, and the largest gathering of current Formula One cars and drivers outside of a Grand Prix.<sup id="cite_ref-AbuDhabi2007_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AbuDhabi2007-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> At the festival it was announced that Abu Dhabi had won the rights to host a Grand Prix from 2009 until 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-AbuDhabi2007a_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AbuDhabi2007a-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-AbuDhabi2007b_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AbuDhabi2007b-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Later that year, <a href="/wiki/Etihad_Airways" title="Etihad Airways">Etihad Airways</a> negotiated a three-year deal for them to become sponsors of the Grand Prix.<sup id="cite_ref-EtihadAirways2007_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EtihadAirways2007-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Inaugural_Grand_Prix_.282009.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Inaugural_Grand_Prix_(2009)">Inaugural Grand Prix (2009)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Abu_Dhabi_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Inaugural Grand Prix (2009)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/2009_Abu_Dhabi_Grand_Prix" title="2009 Abu Dhabi Grand Prix">2009 Abu Dhabi Grand Prix</a></div>
<p>For the <a href="/wiki/2009_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2009 Formula One World Championship">2009</a> season, the Abu Dhabi Grand Prix was added to the schedule. It was provisionally announced as being held on 15 November 2009, as the 19th and final Grand Prix of the season.<sup id="cite_ref-AbuDhabi2008_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AbuDhabi2008-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Both the Canadian Grand Prix and French Grand Prix were later removed from the provisional schedule,<sup id="cite_ref-WantsFrench2008_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WantsFrench2008-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Canadadropped2008_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Canadadropped2008-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> and as a result the Abu Dhabi Grand Prix was moved to 1 November 2009 where it would become the last of 17 meetings.<sup id="cite_ref-Chinamoves2008_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Chinamoves2008-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> In August 2009, it was announced that the start time would be 17:00 local time (13:00 UTC), and that the race would be floodlit.<sup id="cite_ref-Holt2009_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Holt2009-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The race was won by <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> for <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Vettelwins2009_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Vettelwins2009-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010">2010</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Abu_Dhabi_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2010">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/2010_Abu_Dhabi_Grand_Prix" title="2010 Abu Dhabi Grand Prix">2010 Abu Dhabi Grand Prix</a></div>
<p>For the <a href="/wiki/2010_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2010 Formula One World Championship">2010</a> season, the Abu Dhabi Grand Prix was held on the Yas Marina Circuit, for the weekend of 12, 13, 14 November 2010. The Drivers' Championship was decided in Abu Dhabi for the first time. With championship leader <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> losing out and <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> completing his second consecutive win on this track, the young German driver subsequently sealed the world championship.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2011">2011</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Abu_Dhabi_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2011">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/2011_Abu_Dhabi_Grand_Prix" title="2011 Abu Dhabi Grand Prix">2011 Abu Dhabi Grand Prix</a></div>
<p>The 2011 Grand Prix was the 18th and penultimate race of the <a href="/wiki/2011_FIA_Formula_One_World_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="2011 FIA Formula One World Championship">2011 FIA Formula One World Championship</a>, and took place on 13 November.
</p><p>The race was won by <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> in a <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Mercedes_AMG_High_Performance_Powertrains" title="Mercedes AMG High Performance Powertrains">Mercedes</a>. Second was <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> in a <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> coming third in a <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Mercedes_AMG_High_Performance_Powertrains" title="Mercedes AMG High Performance Powertrains">Mercedes</a>. <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a>, in a <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a>-<a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>, had been on pole position, but retired after a puncture on the first lap whilst going round the second corner.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2012">2012</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Abu_Dhabi_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2012">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/2012_Abu_Dhabi_Grand_Prix" title="2012 Abu Dhabi Grand Prix">2012 Abu Dhabi Grand Prix</a></div>
<p>Championship leader <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> finished the race in 3rd position after starting from the pitlane, due to his disqualification from qualifying due to not having enough fuel to return to <a href="/wiki/Parc_ferm%C3%A9" title="Parc fermé">parc fermé</a>. His main championship rival <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> finished 2nd behind the Finnish driver <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a>, who won for the first time after his return to Formula One earlier in 2012.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2013">2013</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Abu_Dhabi_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2013">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/2013_Abu_Dhabi_Grand_Prix" title="2013 Abu Dhabi Grand Prix">2013 Abu Dhabi Grand Prix</a></div>
<p>The race was won by <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a> for the third time, leading every lap. Having clinched their fourth consecutive Drivers' and Constructors' Championships respectively at the <a href="/wiki/2013_Indian_Grand_Prix" title="2013 Indian Grand Prix">Indian Grand Prix</a>, the team celebrated their achievements in <a href="/wiki/Abu_Dhabi" title="Abu Dhabi">Abu Dhabi</a> with <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> performing some doughnuts on the helipad of the <a href="/wiki/Burj_Al_Arab" title="Burj Al Arab">Burj Al Arab</a> luxury hotel in Dubai, 210&#160;m (690&#160;ft) above ground level.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2014">2014</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Abu_Dhabi_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: 2014">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/2014_Abu_Dhabi_Grand_Prix" title="2014 Abu Dhabi Grand Prix">2014 Abu Dhabi Grand Prix</a></div>
<p>The 2014 Abu Dhabi Grand Prix took place on 23 November<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> and was the concluding race of the <a href="/wiki/2014_FIA_Formula_One_World_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="2014 FIA Formula One World Championship">2014 FIA Formula One World Championship</a>.
</p><p>Double points were awarded for the race, which was won by <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>, securing his second Drivers' Championship.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2015">2015</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Abu_Dhabi_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: 2015">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/2015_Abu_Dhabi_Grand_Prix" title="2015 Abu Dhabi Grand Prix">2015 Abu Dhabi Grand Prix</a></div>
<p>The 2015 Abu Dhabi Grand Prix was held on 29 November 2015. The race was won by <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> making it three wins in a row with <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> completing the podium.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2016">2016</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Abu_Dhabi_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: 2016">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/2016_Abu_Dhabi_Grand_Prix" title="2016 Abu Dhabi Grand Prix">2016 Abu Dhabi Grand Prix</a></div>
<p>The 2016 Abu Dhabi Grand Prix was held on 27 November 2016. The race was won by <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> making it four wins in a row with <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> completing the podium and Rosberg securing his first Drivers' Championship.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2017">2017</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Abu_Dhabi_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: 2017">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/2017_Abu_Dhabi_Grand_Prix" title="2017 Abu Dhabi Grand Prix">2017 Abu Dhabi Grand Prix</a></div>
<p>The 2017 Abu Dhabi Grand Prix was held on 26 November 2017. The race was won by <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> in second and <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> completing the podium.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2018">2018</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Abu_Dhabi_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: 2018">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/2018_Abu_Dhabi_Grand_Prix" title="2018 Abu Dhabi Grand Prix">2018 Abu Dhabi Grand Prix</a></div>
<p>The 2018 Abu Dhabi Grand Prix was held on 25 November 2018. The race was won by <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> in second and <a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a> completing the podium. This was also the final career race of Fernando Alonso.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2019">2019</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Abu_Dhabi_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: 2019">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/2019_Abu_Dhabi_Grand_Prix" title="2019 Abu Dhabi Grand Prix">2019 Abu Dhabi Grand Prix</a></div>
<p>The 2019 Abu Dhabi Grand Prix was held on 1 December 2019. The race was won by six-time champion <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a> in second and <a href="/wiki/Charles_Leclerc" title="Charles Leclerc">Charles Leclerc</a> in third.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2020">2020</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Abu_Dhabi_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: 2020">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/2020_Abu_Dhabi_Grand_Prix" class="mw-redirect" title="2020 Abu Dhabi Grand Prix">2020 Abu Dhabi Grand Prix</a></div>
<p>The 2020 Abu Dhabi Grand Prix was scheduled for 29 November but the race was moved to 13 December to allow the <a href="/wiki/Bahrain_Grand_Prix" title="Bahrain Grand Prix">Bahrain Grand Prix</a> to run after their race was postponed due to the <a href="/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic" title="COVID-19 pandemic">COVID-19 pandemic</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Circuit">Circuit</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Abu_Dhabi_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Circuit">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Yas_Marina_Circuit" title="Yas Marina Circuit">Yas Marina Circuit</a></div>
<p>The Yas Marina Circuit was designed by <a href="/wiki/Hermann_Tilke" title="Hermann Tilke">Hermann Tilke</a> and is located on <a href="/wiki/Yas_Island" title="Yas Island">Yas Island</a> – a 2,550 hectares (25.5&#160;km<sup>2</sup>) island on the east coast of <a href="/wiki/Abu_Dhabi" title="Abu Dhabi">Abu Dhabi</a> – and the <a href="/wiki/2009_Abu_Dhabi_Grand_Prix" title="2009 Abu Dhabi Grand Prix">2009 Abu Dhabi Grand Prix</a> was the first major event to take place on the circuit.<sup id="cite_ref-AbuDhabi_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AbuDhabi-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
