<p>The <b>2006 San Marino Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>XXVI Gran Premio Foster's di San Marino</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at the <a href="/wiki/Autodromo_Enzo_e_Dino_Ferrari" class="mw-redirect" title="Autodromo Enzo e Dino Ferrari">Autodromo Enzo e Dino Ferrari</a> in <a href="/wiki/Imola" title="Imola">Imola</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a> on 23 April 2006. The 62-lap race was the fourth round of the <a href="/wiki/2006_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2006 Formula One season">2006 Formula One season</a>, and the 26th running of the <a href="/wiki/San_Marino_Grand_Prix" title="San Marino Grand Prix">San Marino Grand Prix</a>. It was won by <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>, who had started from pole position. It was both his and Ferrari's first win of the season. Championship leader <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> finished second for the <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a> team, whilst <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a> completed the podium with third position for <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>.
</p><p>As a consequence of the race, Schumacher improved his position in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a> from fourth to second. Alonso lengthened his lead in the standings from 14 to 15 points. <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> remained in third, 3 points behind Schumacher, whilst his teammate Montoya elevated himself ahead of Button into fifth place as a result of his podium finish. Alonso's Renault teammate, <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a>, dropped to fourth place but still helped to extend the team's lead to 18 points over McLaren in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a>. Ferrari were then 3 points behind McLaren, on a total of 30 points, 15 more than fourth-placed <a href="/wiki/Honda_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Honda F1">Honda</a>, and 20 more than fifth-placed <a href="/wiki/BMW_in_Formula_One#BMW_Sauber" title="BMW in Formula One">BMW Sauber</a>.
</p><p>It was Schumacher's seventh victory at the <a href="/wiki/San_Marino_Grand_Prix" title="San Marino Grand Prix">San Marino Grand Prix</a>, and his fifth win at Imola in six years. It is also the last time to date that the San Marino Grand Prix has been held.
</p><p>This was the last Formula One race to be held in Imola until the <a href="/wiki/2020_Emilia_Romagna_Grand_Prix" class="mw-redirect" title="2020 Emilia Romagna Grand Prix">2020 Emilia Romagna Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> 
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2006_San_Marino_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Michael Schumacher took pole position, and in doing so, broke <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a>'s record of 65 poles.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2006_San_Marino_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On the first lap, <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Alonso</a> passed <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Barrichello</a> at the Tamburello curve. Like the previous Grand Prix, the race started with an accident, this time in the Villeneuve curve. The Super Aguri team's driver <a href="/wiki/Yuji_Ide" title="Yuji Ide">Yuji Ide</a> hit <a href="/wiki/Midland_F1_Racing" title="Midland F1 Racing">MF1</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Christijan_Albers" title="Christijan Albers">Christijan Albers</a>, putting the Dutchman's car into a series of rolls that left it upside down. Albers was unhurt and Ide was able to continue after replacing his damaged front wing, although he later retired on lap 23. He was reprimanded by the stewards and warned over his future conduct. This incident also formed part of the evidence that led the FIA to withdraw Ide's <a href="/wiki/Superlicence" class="mw-redirect" title="Superlicence">superlicence</a> later in the season. The Safety Car was deployed for two laps.
</p><p>Michael Schumacher appeared to have a clear lead coming up to the first set of pit stops, although things started to go wrong after that. His car developed <a href="/wiki/Graining_(motorsport)" class="mw-redirect" title="Graining (motorsport)">graining</a> in its tyres, which slowed him down considerably. Alonso gained significantly on Schumacher, but could not pass him. The Imola circuit is renowned for being difficult to overtake on.
</p><p>The race continued normally for some laps, until <a href="/wiki/Toyota_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Toyota F1">Toyota</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> entered the pits to retire following a steering problem. <a href="/wiki/Tonio_Liuzzi" class="mw-redirect" title="Tonio Liuzzi">Tonio Liuzzi</a> had a problem and spun his <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso</a> car in the Variante Alta, a corner which had recently been revised.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Honda_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Honda F1">Honda</a> driver Rubens Barrichello started the first round of pit stops, on lap 14. His stop lasted 15 seconds, which dropped him to 13th place. <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> came into the pits on lap 20, temporarily giving <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a>'s Fernando Alonso the lead.
</p><p>Lap 30 saw Honda's <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> make his second stop. The refuelling nozzle got stuck and Button, thinking it had been removed, pulled away from the pits, tearing the nozzle from the refuelling rig. He was delayed in the pitlane while the Honda mechanics removed the nozzle from his car.
</p><p>By the halfway point of the race, <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> began experiencing problems, specifically tyre degradation. Eight laps were enough for Alonso to bring down the 10 second difference between himself and Schumacher. The duel that took place during the <a href="/wiki/2005_San_Marino_Grand_Prix" title="2005 San Marino Grand Prix">2005 San Marino Grand Prix</a> was to be repeated, this time with Schumacher in the lead.
</p><p>Lap 41 saw Alonso pit for a second time, ahead of Schumacher. Alonso's out lap was not good enough to put himself in front of Schumacher, so their positions remained the same. Alonso chased hard, but a mistake by running wide into a corner left Schumacher a clear path for the last few laps to win. Meanwhile, <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> had a driveshaft problem and retired on lap 47.
</p>
