<p>The <b>1974 Argentine Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held in <a href="/wiki/Aut%C3%B3dromo_Juan_y_Oscar_G%C3%A1lvez" title="Autódromo Juan y Oscar Gálvez">Buenos Aires</a> on 13 January 1974. It was race 1 of 15 in both the <a href="/wiki/1974_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1974 World Championship of Drivers">1974 World Championship of Drivers</a> and the <a href="/wiki/1974_International_Cup_for_Formula_One_Manufacturers" class="mw-redirect" title="1974 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers">1974 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers</a>. The 53-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Denny_Hulme" title="Denny Hulme">Denny Hulme</a> after he started from tenth position. <a href="/wiki/Niki_Lauda" title="Niki Lauda">Niki Lauda</a> finished second for the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> team and his teammate <a href="/wiki/Clay_Regazzoni" title="Clay Regazzoni">Clay Regazzoni</a> came in third.
</p><p>For the first time, Formula One's visit to the Buenos Aires circuit saw them use the long and fast No.15 configuration, rather than the previously-used No.9 layout.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix1_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> This race was also the 8th and last victory of Hulme's Formula One career and, as of the <a href="/wiki/2019_Abu_Dhabi_Grand_Prix" title="2019 Abu Dhabi Grand Prix">2019 Abu Dhabi Grand Prix</a><sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=1974_Argentine_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup>, the last for a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_drivers_from_New_Zealand" title="Formula One drivers from New Zealand">New Zealander driver</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1974_Argentine_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It had been one of the most frantic close seasons ever with only <a href="/wiki/Ronnie_Peterson" title="Ronnie Peterson">Ronnie Peterson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Denny_Hulme" title="Denny Hulme">Denny Hulme</a>, <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Reutemann" title="Carlos Reutemann">Carlos Reutemann</a> and <a href="/wiki/James_Hunt" title="James Hunt">James Hunt</a> remaining with their teams. <a href="/wiki/Emerson_Fittipaldi" title="Emerson Fittipaldi">Emerson Fittipaldi</a> moved from <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Lotus</a> to <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> to be replaced by <a href="/wiki/Jacky_Ickx" title="Jacky Ickx">Jacky Ickx</a>. McLaren now had sponsorship from <a href="/wiki/Marlboro_(cigarette)" title="Marlboro (cigarette)">Marlboro</a> and <a href="/wiki/Texaco" title="Texaco">Texaco</a>. The team also entered a third car in their old colours of <a href="/wiki/Yardley_of_London" class="mw-redirect" title="Yardley of London">Yardley</a> for <a href="/wiki/Mike_Hailwood" title="Mike Hailwood">Mike Hailwood</a>. Hailwood's place at <a href="/wiki/Surtees" title="Surtees">Surtees</a> was taken by <a href="/wiki/Jochen_Mass" title="Jochen Mass">Jochen Mass</a> where he partnered <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Carlos_Pace" class="mw-redirect" title="José Carlos Pace">Carlos Pace</a>, whilst <a href="/wiki/Peter_Revson" title="Peter Revson">Peter Revson</a> had moved from <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> to <a href="/wiki/Shadow_Racing_Cars" title="Shadow Racing Cars">Shadow</a>, where he was joined by <a href="/wiki/Jean-Pierre_Jarier" title="Jean-Pierre Jarier">Jean-Pierre Jarier</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix1_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-racingsportscars1974_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-racingsportscars1974-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Niki_Lauda" title="Niki Lauda">Niki Lauda</a> and <a href="/wiki/Clay_Regazzoni" title="Clay Regazzoni">Clay Regazzoni</a> remained teammates but would be driving for <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> instead of <a href="/wiki/British_Racing_Motors" title="British Racing Motors">BRM</a>, who now with <a href="/wiki/Motul_(company)" title="Motul (company)">Motul</a> sponsorship had three cars driven by Frenchmen <a href="/wiki/Jean-Pierre_Beltoise" title="Jean-Pierre Beltoise">Jean-Pierre Beltoise</a>, <a href="/wiki/Henri_Pescarolo" title="Henri Pescarolo">Henri Pescarolo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fran%C3%A7ois_Migault" title="François Migault">François Migault</a>. With the death of <a href="/wiki/Fran%C3%A7ois_Cevert" title="François Cevert">François Cevert</a> and the retirement of <a href="/wiki/Jackie_Stewart" title="Jackie Stewart">Jackie Stewart</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_Racing" title="Tyrrell Racing">Tyrrell</a> had an entirely new line-up in <a href="/wiki/Jody_Scheckter" title="Jody Scheckter">Jody Scheckter</a> and <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Depailler" title="Patrick Depailler">Patrick Depailler</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix1_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-racingsportscars1974_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-racingsportscars1974-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Wilson_Fittipaldi" class="mw-redirect" title="Wilson Fittipaldi">Wilson Fittipaldi</a> left Formula One to start his own team, so his place along Reutemann at <a href="/wiki/Brabham" title="Brabham">Brabham</a> was taken by <a href="/wiki/Richard_Robarts" title="Richard Robarts">Richard Robarts</a>. A privateer Brabham was run by John Goode Racing for <a href="/wiki/John_Watson_(racing_driver)" title="John Watson (racing driver)">John Watson</a> under the Hexagon of Highgate banner. <a href="/wiki/Howden_Ganley" title="Howden Ganley">Howden Ganley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hans_Joachim_Stuck" class="mw-redirect" title="Hans Joachim Stuck">Hans Stuck</a> were picked up by <a href="/wiki/March_Engineering" title="March Engineering">March Engineering</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix1_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-racingsportscars1974_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-racingsportscars1974-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Marlboro_(cigarette)" title="Marlboro (cigarette)">Marlboro</a> continued to back <a href="/wiki/Frank_Williams_(Formula_One)" title="Frank Williams (Formula One)">Frank Williams</a> and his <a href="/wiki/Frank_Williams_Racing_Cars" title="Frank Williams Racing Cars">Iso-Marlboro-Ford FW</a> for former Ferrari driver, <a href="/wiki/Arturo_Merzario" title="Arturo Merzario">Arturo Merzario</a>. While <a href="/wiki/Hesketh_Racing" title="Hesketh Racing">Hesketh Racing</a> were building their own car back in England, they entered <a href="/wiki/James_Hunt" title="James Hunt">James Hunt</a> in a year-old March, while <a href="/wiki/Graham_Hill" title="Graham Hill">Graham Hill</a> had <a href="/wiki/Lola_Cars" title="Lola Cars">Lola Cars</a> build two cars for himself and <a href="/wiki/Guy_Edwards" title="Guy Edwards">Guy Edwards</a>. <a href="/wiki/Rikky_von_Opel" title="Rikky von Opel">Rikky von Opel</a> completed the field, once again driving for <a href="/wiki/Ensign_Racing" title="Ensign Racing">Team Ensign</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix1_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-racingsportscars1974_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-racingsportscars1974-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1974_Argentine_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Peterson secured <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>, for <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">John Player Team Lotus</a>, averaging a speed of 120.542&#160;mph. However, <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Scuderia Ferrari</a> showed how much progress they had made during the winter, under the new management, led by <a href="/wiki/Luca_di_Montezemolo" class="mw-redirect" title="Luca di Montezemolo">Luca di Montezemolo</a>, with Regazzoni qualifying alongside the Swede on the front row. The second row featured the <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> of Fittipaldi and the <a href="/wiki/Shadow_Racing_Cars" title="Shadow Racing Cars">Shadow</a> of Revson, while James Hunt impressed in his <a href="/wiki/Harvey_Postlethwaite" title="Harvey Postlethwaite">Harvey Postlethwaite</a> modified <a href="/wiki/Hesketh_Racing" title="Hesketh Racing">Hesketh March</a> by taking fifth place on the grid.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix1_2-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1974_Argentine_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Peterson took an early lead from the fast starting Hunt, while Regazzoni, Revson and Hailwood all arrived at the first corner together and collided. Regazzoni and Revson spun, then Revson’s team-mate, Jarier ran into him. The cars of Mezario and Watson were also damaged in the melee, as Scheckter took to the grass in avoidance.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix1_2-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After Hunt spun later on the opening lap, Reutemann moved into second, with Fittipaldi, Hailwood, Ickx and Hulme completing the top six. By lap three, Reutemann took the lead, while Fittipaldi visited the pits to have a plug lead reattached. This promoted Hailwood to third. By this stage, Ickx had dropped behind Hulme, but soon both were past the Yardley McLaren. Peterson began to fade due to brake trouble and was overtaken by Hulme and Ickx. By now Ickx was beginning to come under pressure from the Ferrari of Niki Lauda.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix1_2-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On lap 27, the second Lotus was now in trouble, as Ickx pitted with a puncture. This left Lauda in a solid third place. The local hero, Reutemann seem to have the race under control when his <a href="/wiki/Cosworth" title="Cosworth">Cosworth</a> powered <a href="/wiki/Brabham_BT44" title="Brabham BT44">Brabham BT44</a> began to misfire and Hulme rapidly closed him down. On the penultimate lap, Hulme was past, taking the lead. As for Reutemann, his eventually ground to a halt on the last lap, running out of fuel, and was classified seventh overall. As a result, the Ferraris of Lauda and Regazzoni inherited a two-three at the finish.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix1_2-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-racingsportscars2_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-racingsportscars2-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Hulme won in a time of 1hr 41:02.010mins., averaging a speed of 117.405&#160;mph, and was 9.27 seconds ahead of Lauda. Regazzoni was a further 11.14 seconds behind. Only other drivers to complete the full race distance were Hailwood, Beltoise and Depailler.<sup id="cite_ref-racing-reference1_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-racing-reference1-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
