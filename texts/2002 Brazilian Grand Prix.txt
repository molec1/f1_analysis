<p>The <b>2002 Brazilian Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>XXXI Grande Prêmio Marlboro do Brasil</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 31 March 2002 at the <a href="/wiki/Aut%C3%B3dromo_Jos%C3%A9_Carlos_Pace" title="Autódromo José Carlos Pace">Autódromo José Carlos Pace</a>, <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo" title="São Paulo">São Paulo</a>, Brazil. It was the third round of the <a href="/wiki/2002_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2002 Formula One season">2002 Formula One season</a> and the thirty-first <a href="/wiki/Brazilian_Grand_Prix" title="Brazilian Grand Prix">Brazilian Grand Prix</a>. The 71-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> after starting from the second position. <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> finished second for the <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> team with <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> third in a <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a> started from pole position alongside Michael Schumacher. <a href="/wiki/Mika_Salo" title="Mika Salo">Mika Salo</a> scored his last ever world championship points at this race.
</p><p>Michael Schumacher's win was his second of the season, his fourth at Interlagos, and the result meant that he extended his lead in the Drivers' Championship to eight points over Ralf Schumacher and ten over Montoya. Ferrari reduced the lead of Williams in the Constructors' Championship to six points with fourteen races of the season remaining.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2002_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2002_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Grand Prix was contested by eleven teams, each of two drivers.<sup id="cite_ref-Entries_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Entries-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The teams, also known as <a href="/wiki/Formula_One#Constructors" title="Formula One">constructors</a>, were <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>, <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a>, <a href="/wiki/British_American_Racing" title="British American Racing">BAR</a>, <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jaguar_Racing" title="Jaguar Racing">Jaguar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arrows_Grand_Prix_International" title="Arrows Grand Prix International">Arrows</a>, <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Toyota_Racing_(Formula_One_team)" title="Toyota Racing (Formula One team)">Toyota</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Entries_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Entries-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_tyre" class="mw-redirect" title="Formula One tyre">Tyre</a> suppliers <a href="/wiki/Bridgestone" title="Bridgestone">Bridgestone</a> and <a href="/wiki/Michelin" title="Michelin">Michelin</a> brought two new dry compounds for the race.
</p><p>Going into the race, Ferrari driver <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> led the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a> with 14 points, ahead of Williams driver <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a> with 12 points. Montoya's teammate <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> was third on 10 points, ahead of McLaren driver <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a> for Jaguar. In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a>, Williams were leading with 22 points; eight points ahead of Ferrari with 14 points; McLaren were a further 10 points behind in third. Ferrari and Williams had so far dominated the championship, with Ralf Schumacher winning the <a href="/wiki/2002_Malaysian_Grand_Prix" title="2002 Malaysian Grand Prix">Malaysian Grand Prix</a>. Championship contender Montoya had gained second-place finishes, with Räikkonen achieving a third-place podium finishes.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Following the <a href="/wiki/2000_Malaysian_Grand_Prix" title="2000 Malaysian Grand Prix">Malaysian Grand Prix</a> on 17 March, the teams conducted testing sessions at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Catalunya" class="mw-redirect" title="Circuit de Catalunya">Circuit de Catalunya</a> from 19–22 March. Jaguar concentrated on chassis development to decide whether to race their previous chassis, the <a href="/wiki/Jaguar_R2" title="Jaguar R2">R2</a> and concentrate development on the <a href="/wiki/Jaguar_R3" title="Jaguar R3">R3</a> with their test team, when the latter suffered from poor performance.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> McLaren test driver <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Wurz" title="Alexander Wurz">Alexander Wurz</a> (McLaren) set the fastest time of the first day, while <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> (Ferrari) was fastest on the second and final days of testing, and Michael Schumacher (Ferrari) topped the third day's running.
</p><p>Ferrari's main 2002 car, the <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_F2002" title="Ferrari F2002">F2002</a>, was introduced that weekend; the team had been using a modified version of their 2001 car, the <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_F2001" title="Ferrari F2001">F2001</a>, for the opening two rounds.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Originally due to be introduced at the first race in <a href="/wiki/2002_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="2002 Australian Grand Prix">Australia</a>, problems with its performance had left the team to develop the car, delaying its debut. One chassis of the F2002 was made available to Michael Schumacher with teammate Barrichello using a modified F2001.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice_and_qualifying">Practice and qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2002_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Practice and qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Four practice sessions were held before the race: two 60 minute sessions on Friday and two 45 minute sessions on Saturday.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The Friday sessions were held in dry and sunny conditions. Michael Schumacher set the session's fastest time with a lap of 1:15.627, one second ahead of Ralf Schumacher. <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> was one-tenth of a second off Ralf Schumacher's pace, with the two Sauber drivers fourth and fifth, <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> ahead of <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> with Arrows driver <a href="/wiki/Enrique_Bernoldi" title="Enrique Bernoldi">Enrique Bernoldi</a> rounding out the top six. The session was disrupted by an incident as Räikkönen stalled after spinning off at the <i>Descida do Sol</i> corner—the incident required a suspension as <a href="/wiki/Motorsport_marshal" title="Motorsport marshal">marshals</a> cleared the track of the car.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In the second practice session, <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> set the fastest lap of the day, a 1:15.075. The two Williams drivers were second and fourth, Montoya ahead of Ralf Schumacher; they were separated by Toyota driver <a href="/wiki/Allan_McNish" title="Allan McNish">Allan McNish</a>. Michael Schumacher and Räikkönen completed the top six positions.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:J_p_montoya.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/43/J_p_montoya.jpg/220px-J_p_montoya.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="148" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/43/J_p_montoya.jpg/330px-J_p_montoya.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/43/J_p_montoya.jpg/440px-J_p_montoya.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3407" data-file-height="2291" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:J_p_montoya.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a>, who took pole position in his <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> car</div></div></div>
<p>The Saturday practice sessions were again held in dry and sunny conditions. Michael Schumacher again set the fastest lap with a time of 1:13.837. Räikkönen was the second fastest driver, edging closer to Michael Schumacher's time during the session. Barrichello was third fastest, with the Williams drivers fourth and sixth, Ralf Schumacher ahead of Montoya; they were separated by Coulthard.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In the final practice session, Ralf Schumacher set the fastest time of the session, a 1:13.543, two hundredths of a second ahead of Coulthard. The Renault drivers were running quicker, <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> ahead of <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a>. Michael Schumacher and Räikkönen rounded out the top six. The session was disrupted by incidents as Massa's car broke down—the stranded car required a suspension as marshals need to clear the track— and <a href="/wiki/Takuma_Sato" title="Takuma Sato">Takuma Sato</a> spun off and struck the safety barriers at turn 7 and lost his car's front wing.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> After the session's conclusion, the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile</a> (FIA) announced that Barrichello would have his fastest qualifying time stripped when it was determined that the Ferrari driver left the pitlane when closed. Sato was later given the same punishment for a similar offence.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Saturday's afternoon qualifying session lasted for an hour. During this session, the <a href="/wiki/107%25_rule" title="107% rule">107% rule</a> was in effect, which necessitated each driver set a time within 107% of the quickest lap to qualify for the race. Each driver was limited to twelve laps.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Montoya achieved his first pole position of the season, his first at Interlagos, with a time of 1:13.114. He was joined on the front row by Michael Schumacher who was one-tenth of a second behind. Ralf Schumacher qualified third, having battled with Montoya for pole position early in the session and was also happy with the set-up of his car.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-qualiquoites_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualiquoites-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard qualified fourth, though he was unhappy with his qualifying pace.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard's teammate Räikkönen qualified fifth having been forced to use his team's spare car when his race car developed a hydraulic problem.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Trulli, Button, Barrichello, Heidfeld and <a href="/wiki/Mika_Salo" title="Mika Salo">Mika Salo</a> rounded out the top ten. <a href="/wiki/Pedro_de_la_Rosa" title="Pedro de la Rosa">Pedro de la Rosa</a> bettered teammate Irvine when he qualified 11th; <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> split the two Jaguar drivers in 12th. Fisichella qualified 14th, with the only Canadian driver in the field, <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a>, managed 14th ahead of his BAR teammate <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Panis" title="Olivier Panis">Olivier Panis</a> in 17th; they were separated by McNish in 15th. The Arrows and Minardi drivers qualified at the back of the field, covering positions 18 to 22 with Sato.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2002_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The conditions for the race were dry with the <a href="/wiki/Air_temperature" class="mw-redirect" title="Air temperature">air temperature</a> 31&#160;°C (88&#160;°F) and the track temperature ranged between 38–40&#160;°C (100–104&#160;°F).<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> The drivers took to the track at 09:30 (<a href="/wiki/Coordinated_Universal_Time" title="Coordinated Universal Time">UTC</a> -3) for a 30-minute warm-up session.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher maintained his good form over the weekend by setting the fastest time of the session, a 1:15.866, half a second faster than de la Rosa, in second place. Räikkönen had the third fastest time, ahead of the Williams drivers in fourth and fifth—Ralf Schumacher ahead of Montoya—with Barrichello rounding out the top six.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> A crash by Bernoldi with two minutes of the session remaining caused his car to catch fire and required a <a href="/wiki/Racing_flags#Red_flag" title="Racing flags">red flag</a> as the <a href="/wiki/Medical_car" class="mw-redirect" title="Medical car">medical car</a> was deployed to the scene. As medical car driver <a href="/wiki/Alex_Ribeiro" title="Alex Ribeiro">Alex Ribeiro</a> stopped to assist Bernoldi, Heidfeld lost control and slid off the track, colliding with the car's left door just after Ribeiro opened it.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The race started at 14:00 local time. Michael Schumacher made a good start, drawing alongside Montoya into the first corner. Montoya held the outside line for the turn, but Michael Schumacher overtook around the inside when Montoya ran wide. As the leading pair headed into the first straight, Montoya was in the slipstream off Michael Schumacher's car and braked later than the Ferrari driver which resulted in Montoya losing his front wing.
</p><p>On lap 7, Fisichella's car suffered an engine failure and became the first retirement of the race.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2002_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The top three drivers appeared on the podium to collect their trophies, and in the subsequent <a href="/wiki/Press_conference" title="Press conference">press conference</a>.
</p><p>The race result left Michael Schumacher extending his lead in the Drivers' Championship with 24 points. Ralf Schumacher was second with 16 points, two points ahead of Montoya and ten ahead of Button. In the Constructors' Championship, Ferrari reduced the lead of Williams to six points, and McLaren remained third on eight points with fourteen races of the season remaining.
</p>
