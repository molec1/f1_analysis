<p>The <b>2012 Monaco Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>Formula 1 Grand Prix de Monaco 2012</b>)<sup id="cite_ref-Monaco_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Monaco-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race that took place in the principality of <a href="/wiki/Monaco" title="Monaco">Monaco</a> on 27 May 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-7Dec_calendar_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7Dec_calendar-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> It was the sixth round of the <a href="/wiki/2012_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2012 Formula One season">2012 season</a>, and the seventieth running of the <a href="/wiki/Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="Monaco Grand Prix">Monaco Grand Prix</a>. The race was supported by the <a href="/wiki/2012_GP2_Series_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2012 GP2 Series season">GP2</a>, <a href="/wiki/2012_GP3_Series_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2012 GP3 Series season">GP3</a> and <a href="/wiki/2012_Formula_Renault_3.5_Series_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2012 Formula Renault 3.5 Series season">Formula Renault 3.5</a> series.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Mark Webber's victory created a new record for Formula One in that there had never been six different winners of the opening six Grands Prix of the season before.<sup id="cite_ref-BBC_Sport_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC_Sport-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Mercedes' Nico Rosberg came in second place, his second podium in the 2012 season, and Ferrari's Fernando Alonso third and taking the Formula One championship lead by three points over Vettel and Webber.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2012_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2012_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Like the <a href="/wiki/2011_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="2011 Monaco Grand Prix">2011 Monaco Grand Prix</a>, tyre supplier <a href="/wiki/Pirelli" title="Pirelli">Pirelli</a> brought its yellow-banded soft compound tyre as the harder "prime" tyre and the red-banded super-soft compound tyre as the softer "option" tyre. It was the first time for the season that the super-soft compound made an appearance at a race weekend.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Circuit_changes">Circuit changes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2012_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Circuit changes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The circuit underwent some extensive changes in the aftermath of <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a>'s accident during qualifying for the <a href="/wiki/2011_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="2011 Monaco Grand Prix">2011 Monaco Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Circuit_changes_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Circuit_changes-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Pérez crashed heavily at the Nouvelle Chicane, suffering a concussion and a sprained thigh. He was unable to take part in the race.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> In response to this, the event organisers had the approach to the chicane smoothened by lowering the tarmac 20 centimetres (7.9&#160;in), and the barrier that Pérez hit was moved back a further 15 metres (49&#160;ft).<sup id="cite_ref-Circuit_changes_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Circuit_changes-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Other changes included the widening of the pit exit and the removal of nearby trees to allow a driver re-joining the race greater visibility. TecPro barriers were also added at Sainte Dévote and Piscine.<sup id="cite_ref-Circuit_changes_12-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Circuit_changes-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Driver_penalties">Driver penalties</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2012_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Driver penalties">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p><a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> was given a five-place grid penalty for causing an avoidable collision with Bruno Senna during the <a href="/wiki/2012_Spanish_Grand_Prix" title="2012 Spanish Grand Prix">Spanish Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-MSC_Penalty_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MSC_Penalty-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Pastor_Maldonado" title="Pastor Maldonado">Pastor Maldonado</a> was given a ten-place grid penalty for causing an avoidable collision with <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a> during the final free practice session.<sup id="cite_ref-MAL_Penalty_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MAL_Penalty-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> He then received another five-place grid penalty for a gearbox change.<sup id="cite_ref-MAL_pen_2_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MAL_pen_2-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2012_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

</p>

<p><a href="/wiki/Charles_Pic" title="Charles Pic">Charles Pic</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jean-%C3%89ric_Vergne" title="Jean-Éric Vergne">Jean-Éric Vergne</a> were the first cars to emerge from pit lane once the twenty-minute session was given the green light to begin. Pic's first flying lap was ruined when he was blocked by the slow-moving <a href="/wiki/HRT_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="HRT F1">HRT</a> of <a href="/wiki/Pedro_de_la_Rosa" title="Pedro de la Rosa">Pedro de la Rosa</a> at Rascasse, forcing the Frenchman to pit.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Sauber_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber F1">Sauber's</a> <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a> crashed heavily at the Swimming Pool, striking the outer barrier with enough force to remove a wheel. The session was red-flagged due to his crash (for the second year in succession) for six minutes while the car was removed. During replays of the incident, commentators observed Pérez shouting "power steering!" over his radio moments before the crash.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Pérez's left-front steering was damaged, leading to speculation that the crash had been a by-product of the incident with <a href="/wiki/Pastor_Maldonado" title="Pastor Maldonado">Pastor Maldonado</a> in the third free practice session.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>With eight minutes remaining Mercedes driver Michael Schumacher led teammate <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> with several drivers close behind. Defending World Champion <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> opted for a set of super-soft tyres late in the session, as his time from his stint on the slower soft tyres had left him in 16th place. Both <a href="/wiki/Lotus_F1" title="Lotus F1">Lotus</a> drivers waited late to set their first timed lap, both opting for soft tyres. However, while <a href="/wiki/Romain_Grosjean" title="Romain Grosjean">Romain Grosjean</a> set a competitive lap time, <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> was not so quick and was only half a second ahead <a href="/wiki/Caterham_F1" title="Caterham F1">Caterham's</a> <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> and in danger of being eliminated. Räikkönen switched to a set of super-soft compounds and was able to avoid elimination.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a> set the fastest time of the session while on super-softs, while at the back of the field, Kovalainen out-qualified teammate <a href="/wiki/Vitaly_Petrov" title="Vitaly Petrov">Vitaly Petrov</a> by eight-tenths of a second, with Marussia's <a href="/wiki/Timo_Glock" title="Timo Glock">Timo Glock</a> a further half-second behind. Pedro de la Rosa was also eliminated ahead of Pic and teammate <a href="/wiki/Narain_Karthikeyan" title="Narain Karthikeyan">Narain Karthikeyan</a>. As he did not set a lap time, Sergio Pérez was twenty-fourth and last, though this became twenty-third when Maldonado's grid penalties were applied. Prior to the session, Pérez had been the only driver on the grid who had not been eliminated in Q1 since the introduction of the knockout qualifying system.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Q2">Q2</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2012_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Q2">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p><a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Paul_di_Resta" title="Paul di Resta">Paul di Resta</a> was first out followed by <a href="/wiki/Williams_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams F1">Williams's</a> <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Senna" title="Bruno Senna">Bruno Senna</a> for the fifteen-minute session. All cars were out on track early, however <a href="/wiki/Jean-%C3%89ric_Vergne" title="Jean-Éric Vergne">Jean-Éric Vergne</a> damaged his front wing and suspension after he hit the wall under braking on the exit of the tunnel. Vergne limped back to the pits where he was subsequently eliminated.
</p><p>After the early phases of timed laps, Rosberg was fastest on 1:15.022. Six minutes of the Q2 session remained when Vettel pitted with complaints about the front of his car "jumping like a rabbit".<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> After opting for soft tyres early on, both Lotus cars eventually went for super-soft tyres along with the rest of the field. Late in the session, the first driver into the 1:14 bracket was <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> in his <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>.
</p><p>As time ran out, <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a>, was 11th and in the elimination zone, along with the Lotuses, Toro Rossos, Di Resta and Senna. Grosjean and Räikkönen were able to set laps good enough to push German <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a> and the sole remaining Sauber of <a href="/wiki/Kamui_Kobayashi" title="Kamui Kobayashi">Kamui Kobayashi</a> out of qualifying and into 11th and 12th places, respectively. Button was unable to improve on his previous lap and missed entry to the Q3 session by two-tenths of a second. It was the first time since the <a href="/wiki/2008_Brazilian_Grand_Prix" title="2008 Brazilian Grand Prix">2008 Brazilian Grand Prix</a> that Button had missed Q3 in consecutive race weekends. Behind Button were Senna, di Resta and <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a>.
</p><p>At the end of the period, just 0.967 of a second separated the top sixteen drivers.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Q3">Q3</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2012_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Q3">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The Mercedes of Nico Rosberg was the first car on track followed by <a href="/wiki/Pastor_Maldonado" title="Pastor Maldonado">Pastor Maldonado</a>. The ten-minute session was split into two informal phases: initial competitive timed laps followed by short rest at the pits, then a frantic run at the end of the session. In the first five minutes, Vettel and the two Ferrari drivers were the only drivers not to set timed laps.
</p><p>Rosberg and <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> both went out on a brand new set of super-soft tyres, while others fitted used tyres of the same compound. After the initial runs, Rosberg (who completed two timed laps) led the Lotus of Grosjean, followed by <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a>. The gap between the top three was just over one tenth of a second, meaning that no driver had the comfort of knowing his lap time would be sufficient to hold his current position.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>With three minutes left of the session, the top five cars at the time pitted and awaited for their final attack on qualifying. At this stage, both Ferraris went out onto the track for the first time in the session, along with Vettel. However, Vettel left the pit with the slower soft tyres fitted. It became clear by his slow pace that he would – as in Spain – not complete a timed lap so as to have the option of starting the race on either type of dry compound tyres.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Schumacher_pole_Mercedes_celebration_Monaco_2012.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/41/Schumacher_pole_Mercedes_celebration_Monaco_2012.png/250px-Schumacher_pole_Mercedes_celebration_Monaco_2012.png" decoding="async" width="250" height="155" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/41/Schumacher_pole_Mercedes_celebration_Monaco_2012.png/375px-Schumacher_pole_Mercedes_celebration_Monaco_2012.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/41/Schumacher_pole_Mercedes_celebration_Monaco_2012.png/500px-Schumacher_pole_Mercedes_celebration_Monaco_2012.png 2x" data-file-width="1705" data-file-height="1058" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Schumacher_pole_Mercedes_celebration_Monaco_2012.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> celebrates setting the fastest time in qualifying, but he would drop 5 grid spaces due to his collision in Spain. He later retired from the race.</div></div></div>
<p>Webber, Grosjean and Rosberg all went out again on an unused set of the red-banded super-soft compound, but their initial times remained unchallenged. All cars were by then on the track, with the exception of Vettel. As the chequered flag fell upon the session, it was Webber who was ahead of Rosberg, after having set the fastest second and third sector times. Hamilton and Massa fell short of Webber's time, leaving Schumacher, Rosberg and Grosjean the only drivers capable of taking pole from Webber,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> as they were the only drivers to still on a timed lap.
</p><p>Schumacher was the only one to eclipse Webber's time and did so by only eight hundredths of a second. It was the first time he had been fastest in qualification since his comeback after he left the sport in 2006. Unfortunately for him, he would have to start from sixth on the grid due to his penalty from the previous race. This elevated Red Bull driver Webber to pole position, the tenth of his career. The second Mercedes of Rosberg was third, with Hamilton in fourth and Grosjean in fifth, although all, along with <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>, would also gain a place due to Schumacher's penalty. Alonso was six-tenths of a second off the pole time, leading Massa, who qualified in the top ten for the first time in 2012. Kimi Räikkönen was eighth fastest, followed by Maldonado, whose ten-place grid penalty and subsequent penalty for a gearbox change would drop him from ninth to last on the grid. Vettel was promoted to ninth, and would start alongside the Force India of Nico Hülkenberg.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Post_qualifying">Post qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2012_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Post qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Schumacher was extremely happy with his best qualifying performance since his return to Formula One racing. The seven time world champion stated "It is simply a wonderful feeling to set pole after such a long time, and particularly here in Monaco. Okay, it has taken a little bit longer than I might have wanted in the second chapter of my career, but that makes it even sweeter. It's just beautiful".<sup id="cite_ref-Formula_One_Group_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Formula_One_Group-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher came into qualifying knowing that his five-place grid penalty would mean that his best possible grid position was sixth. Knowing this, in the Wednesday pre-race press conference Schumacher rather humorously stated he would have to qualify in first position. True to his word Schumacher later stated in the post-qualifying press conference "I told you guys already in the press conference, my situation is going to be pole, start the race in sixth and I'm going to win it".<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Struggling Ferrari driver Felipe Massa was upbeat about his best qualifying performance of the 2012 season saying "I am very happy with how things have gone in this Grand Prix so far. Finally, I am having fun at the wheel of the F2012".<sup id="cite_ref-Formula_One_Group_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Formula_One_Group-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Adding to Maldonado's ten-place grid penalty for colliding with Mexican Sergio Pérez in free practice three (FP3), the Williams team decided to make a gearbox change and incur a further five-place grid penalty. Pérez, who failed to set a qualifying time, also decided to take a gearbox change and was penalised with a five-place grid penalty. Maldonado started in 24th with the Mexican ahead of him in 23rd.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2012_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Pastor_Maldonado_Monaco_2012_Crash.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ed/Pastor_Maldonado_Monaco_2012_Crash.JPG/220px-Pastor_Maldonado_Monaco_2012_Crash.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ed/Pastor_Maldonado_Monaco_2012_Crash.JPG/330px-Pastor_Maldonado_Monaco_2012_Crash.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ed/Pastor_Maldonado_Monaco_2012_Crash.JPG/440px-Pastor_Maldonado_Monaco_2012_Crash.JPG 2x" data-file-width="3648" data-file-height="2736" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Pastor_Maldonado_Monaco_2012_Crash.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Pastor_Maldonado" title="Pastor Maldonado">Pastor Maldonado</a> was one driver who had to retire following the accident in the first corner.</div></div></div>
<p>The Monaco Grand Prix was the sixth of the season and began at 12:00 <a href="/wiki/UTC" class="mw-redirect" title="UTC">UTC</a> (14:00 local). Conditions for the race were sunny with an ambient temperature of 22&#160;°C (72&#160;°F) and a track temperature of 35&#160;°C (95&#160;°F).<sup id="cite_ref-formula1.com_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-formula1.com-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Rain was forecast for the day but it was unclear whether the race would be affected.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> The race begun with humidity at 58%.<sup id="cite_ref-formula1.com_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-formula1.com-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Sebastian Vettel, Jenson Button, Paul di Resta, Vitaly Petrov and Pedro de la Rosa were the only drivers to start on the slower yellow-banded soft compound tyre, with the rest of the field either opting or being forced (by qualifying rules) to start on the red-banded super-soft compound.
</p><p>As the race started both Mark Webber and Nico Rosberg got away cleanly and were first and second, respectively, as the field squabbled behind them. Fernando Alonso squeezed through the gap between Grosjean and McLaren's Lewis Hamilton, but Grosjean clipped the rear left wheel of the Ferrari which in turn pushed him to the outside of the track and into the path of fast starting Michael Schumacher. Schumacher had nowhere to go as he was already right next to the wall and as a result Grosjean's left rear wheel was forced into and over the Mercedes car's front right, which severely damaged the Lotus's suspension. This sent Grosjean into a spin and he ended up broadside across the track at the entry to turn one. Other drivers tried to avoid crashing into each other and the stricken Grosjean, however some failed. Vettel, starting from ninth, clipped the front wing of the still moving Lotus but his car was undamaged. He and a number of others took the shortcut behind the apex of the first corner to avoid the mess. Sauber's Kamui Kobayashi, who was right behind Vettel, decided to avoid Grosjean by passing him on the racing line. Before the Lotus finally came to a complete stop it rolled backwards slightly and its right rear tyre clipped the left rear of slow moving Kobayashi sending him into the air, nearly landing on Button's McLaren.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> At the very back of the field, amidst all the chaos Pastor Maldonado, starting last, braked too late and crashed into the back of de la Rosa's HRT, terminally damaging both his front wing and de la Rosa's rear wing. Maldonado retired later in the lap. In order to permit safe clearance of the debris, the safety car was deployed.
</p><p>The safety car was called in during the second lap after Grosjean and Maldonado's cars had been removed, while Kobayashi and de la Rosa made it back to the pits under their own power. Damage to de la Rosa's car forced his retirement, but following checks the Sauber was able to rejoin the race in 21st place. Schumacher was told by his team that his car seemed fine after the contact with Grosjean and he was able to continue.
</p><p>On the restart nearly all drivers held their positions. Webber led Rosberg and Hamilton in an unchanged top three from the grid. Meanwhile, Kobayashi returned to the pits where he retired due to suspension damaged from the first corner incident with the Lotus.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Webber was setting consecutive fastest laps while the stewards investigated various cars cutting the first corner to avoid Grosjean; no further action was taken.
</p><p>After 13 laps the threat of rain forced teams to not pit stop early so as not to change tyres again if it did eventually rain.<sup id="cite_ref-formula1.com_21-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-formula1.com-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Petrov pitted on lap 16 only to retire as he had an electrical problem. As the track temperature dropped both Ferraris, in fourth and fifth, started to pick up pace and set quick lap times.
</p><p>By lap 23 the sole remaining Sauber of Sergio Pérez was the only car making multiple overtaking moves, up to 15th from his 23rd place grid spot. Meanwhile, Schumacher pressured a struggling Kimi Räikkönen as the Lotus driver was told to stay out and wait for predicted rain.<sup id="cite_ref-formula1.com_21-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-formula1.com-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Thanks to Räikkönen's pace, a gap formed that allowed Rosberg to pit on lap 27, to switch to the soft compound tyre, and return to the track without any nearby traffic.
</p><p>Leaders Webber and Hamilton followed Rosberg's lead and pitted the next lap. Alonso, now in first place, stayed out for another lap during which he set consecutive fastest sectors by nearly a second before following the others and switching to the soft tyre. So quick was Alonso that he came out of the pits well ahead of Hamilton. Massa inherited the lead and pitted on the next lap. This left Vettel, who had not stopped, in the lead with a ten-second advantage over his teammate, Webber. As the race went on Vettel extended his lead over Webber and the rest of the field. Later, in the press conference, both Webber and Rosberg stated that they had struggled to warm their soft compound tyre at that time of the race whereas Vettel already had the tyres at the optimum temperature.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>On lap 38 Pérez was given a drive-through penalty for a late pit entry that had impeded Räikkönen. The same lap also saw Button, who started on the soft tyre, change for the super-soft compound. In an already poor race for Button,<sup id="cite_ref-formula1.com_21-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-formula1.com-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> his pit stop allowed the Caterham of Heikki Kovalainen to slip ahead of him as he rejoined.
</p><p>The gap to Vettel had settled to sixteen seconds as he still waited for the forecast rain.<sup id="cite_ref-http_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-http-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Massa got a radio message asking him to close the gap to Hamilton as there was a possibility that Vettel could split the two when he rejoined.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Further back there was a significant gap from sixth to seventh, where Schumacher was being followed by <a href="/wiki/Jean-%C3%89ric_Vergne" title="Jean-Éric Vergne">Jean-Éric Vergne</a>, who had pitted early, and the two Force Indias. Kovalainen held twelfth place ahead of both Button and Toro Rosso driver <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a>.
</p><p>Forty-five laps into the race Vettel finally took his only pit stop for the race. As he rejoined, teammate Webber reclaimed the lead while Vettel was able to only marginally get ahead of Hamilton.<sup id="cite_ref-http_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-http-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> As the leaders lapped backmarkers, the gap between the top six was less than six seconds. Räikkönen's engineer told him that no rain was now expected.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>By lap 48 Pérez was 2.7 seconds per lap quicker than the leaders and set a number of fastest laps. Ferrari engineer <a href="/wiki/Rob_Smedley" title="Rob Smedley">Rob Smedley</a> was attempting to keep Massa motivated in sixth when he claimed "keep our tyres fresh cause he doesn't look absolutely fantastic, old Lewis",<sup id="cite_ref-formula1.com_21-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-formula1.com-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> referring to Hamilton ahead in fifth.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Jenson_Button_at_2012_Monaco_Grand_Prix.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6b/Jenson_Button_at_2012_Monaco_Grand_Prix.JPG/220px-Jenson_Button_at_2012_Monaco_Grand_Prix.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6b/Jenson_Button_at_2012_Monaco_Grand_Prix.JPG/330px-Jenson_Button_at_2012_Monaco_Grand_Prix.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6b/Jenson_Button_at_2012_Monaco_Grand_Prix.JPG/440px-Jenson_Button_at_2012_Monaco_Grand_Prix.JPG 2x" data-file-width="3492" data-file-height="2619" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Jenson_Button_at_2012_Monaco_Grand_Prix.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> retired from the race on lap 70.</div></div></div>
<p>Schumacher began to complain of a problem on lap 59 but still continued. However, on lap 61 he let Vergne past him and continued to slow. Spots of rain started to show in some parts of the track but nothing of real concern for the drivers.<sup id="cite_ref-http_24-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-http-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> A few laps later the seven time world champion entered the pits and retired from the race with a fuel pressure problem. In consecutive laps Pic and Ricciardo both retired from the race owing to an electrical fault and a steering problem, respectively.
</p><p>With 10 laps remaining, the first six drivers were split by fewer than four seconds. On lap 69 and Vergne pitted to switch to intermediate tyres; the only driver to do so in the race. Still behind Kovalainen, Button locked up his brakes next to the swimming pool and spun. That left Pérez behind him to attack the Caterham, which Pérez succeeded in passing. Vergne's move to intermediate tyres had not worked, as he was losing over seven seconds every lap to his rivals.<sup id="cite_ref-http_24-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-http-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Two laps after Button spun, he had caught back up to Kovalainen. Button attempted an overtake on Kovalainen around the outside of turn 1. Button, who was able to brake later in his McLaren, launched down the outside of Kovalainen in the braking zone of turn 1. At the same time Kovalainen moved left and the Finn's front wing hit Button's front right tyre, causing a puncture on the McLaren, and Kovalainen damaged his front wing. A subsequent pit stop for Kovalainen put him down to 13th place, meanwhile Button retired from the race due to his puncture.
</p><p>The gap at the front remained close however; no driver was able to move ahead. In the final few laps rain still threatened,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> but each of the six leaders held their own as they lapped cars. Hamilton and Massa did drop back slightly from the four towards the end of the race. By the final lap, artificial light from cameras was becoming more prominent as daylight faded with the threat of darker rain clouds. Ultimately, Webber was able to keep Rosberg, Alonso and Vettel at bay for his second career victory at the Monte-Carlo circuit.
</p><p>In winning the Monaco Grand Prix Webber became the only Australian to win on multiple occasions at the circuit. It was Webber's first win of the season and first since his victory at the last race of the 2011 season in Brazil. In taking the victory Webber and the five victorious drivers before him set a new record for six different winners from the first six races of a Formula One season. The margin between the first four drivers was only 1.343 seconds as Rosberg followed Webber into second place and Spaniard Alonso finished in third; his 76th career podium. Red Bull teammate Vettel, using a different strategy to the top three, was fourth while Hamilton was fifth. Massa backed up his best qualifying effort of the season with his best result of the season by finishing sixth and only six seconds from the eventual winner.
</p><p>Some forty seconds off the lead was the Force India's led by di Resta, who was on the same strategy as Vettel, and teammate Hülkenberg who (like Webber and Massa) captured his best result of the season thus far. Räikkönen was ninth while Williams driver Senna picked up the final point in tenth. Importantly for the constructors title, Caterham driver Kovalainen finished in thirteenth (his highest so far) moving Caterham up into tenth place ahead of Marussia and HRT on the 2012 standings. This was despite Marussia driver Glock (14th) and HRT driver Karthikeyan (15th) finishing in their respective highest positions for the season.
</p>

This would mean he would start 24th, but when <a href="/wiki/Pastor_Maldonado" title="Pastor Maldonado">Pastor Maldonado</a> had his gearbox changed Pérez was promoted to 23rd on the grid. He received a five-place grid penalty for changing his gearbox after he was granted access to enter the race by the stewards, leaving the Sauber driver 24th overall with Maldonado promoted to 23rd ahead.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup></dd></dl>
