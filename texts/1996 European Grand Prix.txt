<p>The <b>1996 European Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at the <a href="/wiki/N%C3%BCrburgring" title="Nürburgring">Nürburgring</a> in Germany on 28 April 1996. It was the fourth race of the <a href="/wiki/1996_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1996 Formula One World Championship">1996 Formula One World Championship</a>.
</p><p>The 67-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW18" title="Williams FW18">Williams</a>-<a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>. Villeneuve beat teammate and pole-sitter <a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a> off the line and led all 67 laps, taking his first F1 victory in only his fourth race. Local driver <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> finished second in a <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_F310" title="Ferrari F310">Ferrari</a>, with <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> third in a <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/11" title="McLaren MP4/11">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Mercedes_AMG_High_Performance_Powertrains" title="Mercedes AMG High Performance Powertrains">Mercedes</a>, just ahead of Hill.
</p><p>Both Benetton cars made extremely slow starts, due to the brakes locking on. Berger was forced to pit for fresh tyres after a flat-spot was caused, while Alesi finished lap 1 in 13th place and spun while trying to recover.
</p><p>The Tyrrells were disqualified for separate infringements - Salo finished 10th but his car was found post-race to be underweight, while Katayama finished 12th but was disqualified for receiving an illegal push-start on the parade lap.
</p>
