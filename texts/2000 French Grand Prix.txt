<p>The <b>2000 French Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>LXXXVI Mobil 1 Grand Prix de France</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 2 July 2000 at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Nevers_Magny-Cours" title="Circuit de Nevers Magny-Cours">Circuit de Nevers Magny-Cours</a>, <a href="/wiki/Magny-Cours" title="Magny-Cours">Magny-Cours</a>, <a href="/wiki/Burgundy" title="Burgundy">Burgundy</a>, <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>. It was the ninth race of the <a href="/wiki/2000_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2000 Formula One season">2000 Formula One season</a> and the 86th <a href="/wiki/French_Grand_Prix" title="French Grand Prix">French Grand Prix</a>. The 72-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> driver <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> after starting from second position. His teammate <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> finished second with <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> third for the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> team.
</p><p>Coulthard started the race alongside Ferrari driver <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> who began from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>. Barrichello began from third on the grid and overtook Coulthard going into the first corner. Michael Schumacher maintained his start line advantage and kept the lead after the first round of <a href="/wiki/Pit_stop" title="Pit stop">pit stops</a>. During the course of the second stint of the race, Michael Schumacher began to struggle with tyre wear, allowing Coulthard to close the gap and passed him on lap 40. Coulthard maintained his lead through the second round of pit stops and won the race. Michael Schumacher retired on lap 59 with an engine failure promoting Häkkinen into second position. Barrichello took third, ahead of <a href="/wiki/British_American_Racing" title="British American Racing">BAR</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a> in fourth.
</p><p>The race was Coulthard's third victory of the season, his first at Magny-Cours, and the result meant that he reduced Michael Schumacher's points advantage in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a> to twelve points. Häkkinen remained third on 38 points, six ahead of Barrichello. In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a>, McLaren's one-two finish allowed them to narrow the gap to Ferrari to be six points behind, with eight races of the season remaining.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2000_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Grand Prix was contested by eleven teams, each of two drivers.<sup id="cite_ref-entrants_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-entrants-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The teams, also known as <a href="/wiki/Formula_One#Constructors" title="Formula One">constructors</a> were, <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jaguar_Racing" title="Jaguar Racing">Jaguar</a>, <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a>, <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Prost_Grand_Prix" title="Prost Grand Prix">Prost</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arrows_Grand_Prix_International" title="Arrows Grand Prix International">Arrows</a>, <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a> and <a href="/wiki/British_American_Racing" title="British American Racing">BAR</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-entrants_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-entrants-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_tyre" class="mw-redirect" title="Formula One tyre">Tyre</a> supplier <a href="/wiki/Bridgestone" title="Bridgestone">Bridgestone</a> brought two different tyre types to the race: the Soft and the Medium dry compound tyres.<sup id="cite_ref-preview_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-preview-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Going into the race, Ferrari driver <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> led the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a> with 56 <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Championship_points_scoring_systems" title="List of Formula One World Championship points scoring systems">points</a>, ahead of <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> on 34 points and <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> on 32. <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> was fourth with 28 points while <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> was fifth on 18 points.<sup id="cite_ref-points_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a>, Ferrari were leading with 84 points, McLaren and Benetton were second and third with 66 and 18 points respectively, while Williams with 15 and Jordan with ten contended for fourth place.<sup id="cite_ref-points_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Ferrari and McLaren had so far dominated the Championship, winning the previous eight races. Championship competitors Fisichella and Barrichello had gained second-place finishes while <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> and <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a> had achieved third place podium finishes.<sup id="cite_ref-points_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Following the <a href="/wiki/2000_Canadian_Grand_Prix" title="2000 Canadian Grand Prix">Canadian Grand Prix</a> on 18 June, the teams conducted testing sessions at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Nevers_Magny-Cours" title="Circuit de Nevers Magny-Cours">Circuit de Nevers Magny-Cours</a> between 21–23 June to prepare for the upcoming French Grand Prix. Coulthard set the fastest times on the first day, ahead of McLaren test driver <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Panis" title="Olivier Panis">Olivier Panis</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Arrows driver <a href="/wiki/Jos_Verstappen" title="Jos Verstappen">Jos Verstappen</a> lost control of his car after suffering a mechanical problem and crashed into the barriers at turn two. Verstappen sustained neck strain and withdrew from testing. He was later passed fit to compete in the Grand Prix.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard remained fastest on the second day. Fisichella damaged the underside of his car's chassis, limiting his testing time as Benetton made repairs to his car.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Häkkinen was quickest on the final day of testing. Michael Schumacher stopped on circuit as his Ferrari engine failed, causing his team to fit a new engine into the chassis.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Ferrari went to their private testing facility, the <a href="/wiki/Fiorano_Circuit" title="Fiorano Circuit">Fiorano Circuit</a>, on 27 June where test driver <a href="/wiki/Luca_Badoer" title="Luca Badoer">Luca Badoer</a> shook down three <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_F1-2000" title="Ferrari F1-2000">Ferrari F1-2000</a> cars and also took part in pit stop practices.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After a poor race in Canada, where he stalled on the grid before the formation lap before receiving a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_regulations#Penalties" title="Formula One regulations">ten-second stop-go penalty</a> as his mechanics worked on his car 15 seconds before the race started, Coulthard said that Ferrari could still be caught in the season's nine remaining races: "We have to look towards Magny-Cours and put this (Montreal) race behind us. The championship is not over yet and although we cannot change what happened in Canada, we can try to close the gap."<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Former World Champion and President of the <a href="/wiki/British_Racing_Drivers_Club" class="mw-redirect" title="British Racing Drivers Club">British Racing Drivers Club</a> (BRDC) <a href="/wiki/Jackie_Stewart" title="Jackie Stewart">Jackie Stewart</a> backed Coulthard to clinch the title after Häkkinen's continuing run of poor results.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher came into the race confident that his car would perform well at the circuit.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Benetton team principal <a href="/wiki/Flavio_Briatore" title="Flavio Briatore">Flavio Briatore</a> played down his team's chances saying, "We need a miracle to finish on the podium."<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice">Practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2000_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Michael_Schumacher-I%27m_the_man_(cropped).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/Michael_Schumacher-I%27m_the_man_%28cropped%29.jpg/170px-Michael_Schumacher-I%27m_the_man_%28cropped%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="230" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/Michael_Schumacher-I%27m_the_man_%28cropped%29.jpg/255px-Michael_Schumacher-I%27m_the_man_%28cropped%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/Michael_Schumacher-I%27m_the_man_%28cropped%29.jpg/340px-Michael_Schumacher-I%27m_the_man_%28cropped%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1260" data-file-height="1704" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Michael_Schumacher-I%27m_the_man_(cropped).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> (pictured in 2005) secured his fourth <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> of the season.</div></div></div>
<p>Four practice sessions were held before the Sunday race—two on Friday, and two on Saturday. The Friday morning and afternoon sessions each lasted an hour. The third and final practice sessions were held on Saturday morning and lasted 45 minutes.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> The Friday morning and afternoon practice sessions were held in dry and hot weather conditions.<sup id="cite_ref-fiafriday_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiafriday-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher set the fastest time in the first practice session, at 1 minute and 16.474 seconds, two-tenths of a second quicker than Häkkinen. Prost driver <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> was third fastest, ahead of Ralf Schumacher. The two Jaguar drivers were fifth and seventh, <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a> ahead of <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Herbert" title="Johnny Herbert">Johnny Herbert</a>. The pair were separated by Fisichella in sixth. <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pedro_de_la_Rosa" title="Pedro de la Rosa">Pedro de la Rosa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> completed the top ten fastest drivers in the session. Four drivers did not set lap times: Coulthard's car developed a mechanical fuel pump problem, Barrichello drove one installation lap to conserve tyres and both Jordan drivers decided to sit out the session.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> In the second practice session, Coulthard had a trouble-free session and was fastest with a time of 1:16.253; Häkkinen finished with the second fastest time. The Ferraris were second and fourth, Michael Schumacher was faster than Barrichello. Sauber's <a href="/wiki/Mika_Salo" title="Mika Salo">Mika Salo</a> was fifth quickest, ahead of Fisichella. Trulli, Heidfeld, Ralf Schumacher and Frentzen followed in the top ten.<sup id="cite_ref-fiafriday_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiafriday-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The weather remained dry and hot for the Saturday morning practice sessions.<sup id="cite_ref-fiasaturday_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiasaturday-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard set the fastest time of the third practice session, a 1:15.965. Häkkinen was second fastest, almost two-tenths of a second off Coulthard's pace. The two Jordan drivers were running quicker; Trulli in third and Frentzen in fourth. Both Jaguars continued to be quick with Irvine fifth and Herbert eighth. The two were separated by Michael Schumacher and Button. Barrichello and de la Rosa rounded out the top ten.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> In the final practice session, Coulthard was unable to improve his time because of an engine failure though he remained fastest. Häkkinen managed to set a quicker time and remained with the second quickest lap. Barrichello ran quicker and was third fastest, in front of Ralf Schumacher. The Jordan drivers remained fastest–Trulli was fifth and Frentzen sixth–their best times two thousands of a second apart. Irvine was seventh quickest, ahead of Michael Schumacher who concentrated on qualifying set-up. Button and Herbert completed the top ten ahead of qualifying.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<div class="quotebox pullquote floatleft" style="width:33%;&#32;;"><style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r887775652">.mw-parser-output .quotebox{background-color:#F9F9F9;border:1px solid #aaa;box-sizing:border-box;padding:10px;font-size:88%;max-width:100%}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft{margin:0.5em 1.4em 0.8em 0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright{margin:0.5em 0 0.8em 1.4em}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.centered{margin:0.5em auto 0.8em auto}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft p,.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright p{font-style:inherit}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-title{background-color:#F9F9F9;text-align:center;font-size:larger;font-weight:bold}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:before{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" “ ";vertical-align:-45%;line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:after{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" ” ";line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .left-aligned{text-align:left}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .right-aligned{text-align:right}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .center-aligned{text-align:center}.mw-parser-output .quotebox cite{display:block;font-style:normal}@media screen and (max-width:360px){.mw-parser-output .quotebox{min-width:100%;margin:0 0 0.8em!important;float:none!important}}</style>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">My first run was spot on and that is the main reason I took pole, because others seemed to improve on their later runs. The car was good so we did not need to make many changes in between the runs. So far the weekend is going well, partly because of the good work we did here last week. But you cannot rely on the testing as conditions change so quickly here, especially on the tyre side. You must be prepared to change the car according to what conditions you find. There have been some comments about the problem of the position of the pit lane exit, but with the extra speed at which the cars will leave the pits in the race, I don't think it will be a problem tomorrow.</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style="">Michael Schumacher, commenting on taking pole position.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup></cite>

</div>
<p>Saturday's afternoon one hour qualifying session saw each driver limited to twelve laps, with the starting order decided by their fastest laps. During this session, the <a href="/wiki/107%25_rule" title="107% rule">107% rule</a> was in effect, which necessitated each driver set a time within 107 per cent of the quickest lap to qualify for the race.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> The qualifying session was held in dry and hot weather.<sup id="cite_ref-fiasaturday_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiasaturday-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-temp_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-temp-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher clinched his fourth <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> of the season, his third at the circuit, with a time of 1:15.632. He was joined on the front row of the <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_motorsport_terms#G" title="Glossary of motorsport terms">grid</a> by Coulthard with his fastest time was 0.102 seconds slower and used his team's spare <a href="/wiki/Monocoque" title="Monocoque">monocoque</a> while the McLaren mechanics fitted his race car with a new fuel pump.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcquali_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcquali-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-atlasquali_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasquali-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello secured third having changed his car's set-up during the session which garnered confusion over changes in the ride height.<sup id="cite_ref-fiasaturday_16-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiasaturday-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Häkkinen took fourth and was pushed down to his position by Barrichello with the Finn struggling in the slow speed corners.<sup id="cite_ref-fiasaturday_16-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiasaturday-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-bbcquali_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcquali-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Ralf Schumacher qualified fifth and was happy with his starting position.<sup id="cite_ref-atlasquali_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasquali-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Irvine was pushed down by Ralf Schumacher to sixth in the closing stages of qualifying.<sup id="cite_ref-atlasquali_22-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasquali-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> BAR driver <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a> managed seventh.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcquali_21-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcquali-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> The Jordan drivers of Trulli and Frentzen took seventh and eighth positions respectively although both drivers were disappointed with their performance.<sup id="cite_ref-fiasaturday_16-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiasaturday-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Button rounded out the top ten fastest qualifiers.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcquali_21-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcquali-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Herbert, who took eleventh, missed out in qualifying in the top ten by nearly three-tenths of a second.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcquali_21-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcquali-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Salo took twelfth on the grid with his fastest time set when air and track temperatures were lowered by cloud cover.<sup id="cite_ref-atlasquali_22-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasquali-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> He was ahead of de la Rosa in the faster of the two Arrows.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcquali_21-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcquali-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Fisichella started from 14th position, nearly one-tenth of a second in front of Benetton teammate <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Wurz" title="Alexander Wurz">Alexander Wurz</a>; both drivers complained that their cars had a and <a href="/wiki/Grip_(auto_racing)" title="Grip (auto racing)">grip</a> issues.<sup id="cite_ref-fiasaturday_16-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiasaturday-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-atlasquali_22-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasquali-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> The two were split by <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Diniz" title="Pedro Diniz">Pedro Diniz</a> in the other Sauber and Heidfeld.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcquali_21-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcquali-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Alesi followed up in 18th.<sup id="cite_ref-atlasquali_22-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasquali-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Ricardo_Zonta" title="Ricardo Zonta">Ricardo Zonta</a> set the 19th fastest time and used BAR's spare car when an engine failure on his race car occurred.<sup id="cite_ref-fiasaturday_16-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiasaturday-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Verstappen and the two Minardi drivers <a href="/wiki/Marc_Gen%C3%A9" title="Marc Gené">Marc Gené</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gast%C3%B3n_Mazzacane" title="Gastón Mazzacane">Gastón Mazzacane</a> qualified at the rear of the grid, covering positions 20 to 22.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcquali_21-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcquali-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> After the qualifying session, but before the warm-up period, the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile</a> (FIA) safety delegate, <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Whiting" title="Charlie Whiting">Charlie Whiting</a> announced that he had banned practice starts at the pit lane exit after observing the drivers' behaviour in the area during the Saturday free practice sessions.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Warm-up">Warm-up</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2000_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Warm-up">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The drivers took to the track at 09:30 <a href="/wiki/Central_European_Summer_Time" title="Central European Summer Time">Central European Summer Time</a> (<a href="/wiki/Coordinated_Universal_Time" title="Coordinated Universal Time">UTC</a>+2) for a 30-minute warm-up session.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_13-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Both McLaren cars maintained their good pace from qualifying; Häkkinen had the fastest time of 1:19.329. Coulthard was second in the other McLaren car. Michael Schumacher set the third fastest time with Trulli rounding out the top four. Michael Schumacher drove the spare Ferrari car and his race car, making adjustments to the changeable weather conditions.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2000_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:David_Coulthard_2007.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d8/David_Coulthard_2007.jpg/170px-David_Coulthard_2007.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="250" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d8/David_Coulthard_2007.jpg/255px-David_Coulthard_2007.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d8/David_Coulthard_2007.jpg/340px-David_Coulthard_2007.jpg 2x" data-file-width="500" data-file-height="734" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:David_Coulthard_2007.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> (pictured in 2007) took his third win of the 2000 season.</div></div></div>
<p>The race started at 14:00 local time.<sup id="cite_ref-galeforcef1race_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-galeforcef1race-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> The conditions on the grid were dry before the race. The air temperature ranged from 27 to 28&#160;°C (81 to 82&#160;°F) and the track temperature was between 38 to 39&#160;°C (100 to 102&#160;°F);<sup id="cite_ref-galeforcef1race_26-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-galeforcef1race-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-f1comrace_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrace-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> conditions were expected to remain consistent throughout the race.<sup id="cite_ref-galeforcef1race_26-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-galeforcef1race-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> All drivers started on the Extra Soft dry compound tyres.<sup id="cite_ref-f1comrace_27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrace-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard managed to achieve a better start of the front runners off the line, though Michael Schumacher moved into the McLaren driver's path to defend his lead. Coulthard then moved to his right allowing Barrichello to pass him for second position. Salo made the best start in the field moving from twelfth to ninth by the end of the first lap, while Irvine lost four positions over the same distance as Ralf Schumacher dropped from fifth to seventh.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> At the completion of the first lap, the order was Michael Schumacher, Barrichello, Coulthard, Häkkinen, Villeneuve, Frentzen, Ralf Schumacher, Trulli, Salo, Irvine, Button, Herbert, de la Rosa, Heidfeld, Alesi, Fisichella, Verstappen, Zonta, Diniz, Wurz, Gené and Mazzacane.<sup id="cite_ref-lapchart_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapchart-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Michael Schumacher began to pull clear from the rest of the field and set consecutive <a href="/wiki/Fastest_lap" title="Fastest lap">fastest laps</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-lapchart_29-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapchart-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello began to hold up the McLaren drivers to allow his teammate to extend his lead. Further down the field, Trulli passed Ralf Schumacher to take seventh position on lap two. Heidfeld was overtaken by Alesi for 14th on the same lap.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_28-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> On the following lap, Ralf Schumacher retook seventh position from Trulli.<sup id="cite_ref-f1comrace_27-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrace-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> Heidfeld lost a further position to Fisichella on lap four.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_28-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Ralf Schumacher started to challenge Frentzen for sixth position on the fifth lap.<sup id="cite_ref-f1comrace_27-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrace-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> At the front of the pack, Michael Schumacher was in control, extending his lead over Barrichello to five seconds by lap ten.<sup id="cite_ref-lapchart_29-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapchart-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Button took tenth position from Irvine on lap twelve, while Heidfeld collided with Alesi,<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_28-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> sending his teammate into a spin who rejoined in 18th.<sup id="cite_ref-f1comrace_27-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrace-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> Button moved into ninth place when he passed Salo on lap 13.<sup id="cite_ref-gprace_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gprace-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Zonta, who was running 17th, suffered brake problems on lap 17 and collided with the tyre barriers which caused him to retire from the Grand Prix.<sup id="cite_ref-f1comrace_27-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrace-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_28-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>By the 18th lap, Michael Schumacher had extended his lead over Barrichello by 6.2 seconds, who in turn was continuing to hold up Coulthard in third. Häkkinen was a further 1.4 seconds behind his teammate but was drawing ahead of Villeneuve in fifth.<sup id="cite_ref-lapchart_29-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapchart-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Herbert made an unscheduled <a href="/wiki/Pit_stop" title="Pit stop">pit stop</a> due to a gearbox issue and retired two laps later. Meanwhile, on the same lap, Alesi became the first driver to pit for tyres on the same lap.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_28-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Trulli became the first front runner to pit on lap 21, followed by Villeneuve, Frentzen and Ralf Schumacher.<sup id="cite_ref-lapchart_29-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapchart-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard managed to take Barrichello on lap 22 for second place at turn five.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_28-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Häkkinen, Button, Michael Schumacher, Coulthard and Barrichello all made pit stops over the following three laps.<sup id="cite_ref-lapchart_29-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapchart-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Verstappen pulled onto the side of the pit straight to retire with gearbox problems on lap 26.<sup id="cite_ref-f1comrace_27-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrace-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_28-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard was able to close the gap on Michael Schumacher to under a second by lap 32, having set the fastest lap of the race in the process, a 1:19.479 on the 28th lap.<sup id="cite_ref-lapchart_29-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapchart-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> He attempted to overtake his rival two laps later but  Michael Schumacher defended his position.<sup id="cite_ref-gprace_30-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gprace-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard lifted off the throttle to avoid a collision and made a hand gesture towards Michael Schumacher.<sup id="cite_ref-gprace_30-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gprace-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> The manoeuvre allowed Häkkinen to close up on the two leading drivers.<sup id="cite_ref-f1comrace_27-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrace-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> On the previous lap, Mazzacane spun off at the third corner and retired.<sup id="cite_ref-f1comrace_27-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrace-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Mika_Haekkinen_2006.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/53/Mika_Haekkinen_2006.jpg/170px-Mika_Haekkinen_2006.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="230" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/53/Mika_Haekkinen_2006.jpg/255px-Mika_Haekkinen_2006.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/53/Mika_Haekkinen_2006.jpg/340px-Mika_Haekkinen_2006.jpg 2x" data-file-width="549" data-file-height="743" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Mika_Haekkinen_2006.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> (pictured in 2006) finished second.</div></div></div>
<p>Wurz locked his brakes going into turn 15, causing him to beach his car in the <a href="/wiki/Run-off_area" title="Run-off area">gravel trap</a> and retired on lap 36.<sup id="cite_ref-f1comrace_27-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrace-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-gprace_30-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gprace-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Button became the first driver to pit twice on lap 39.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_28-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard replicated his manoeuvre into turn five on Michael Schumacher on lap 40, with the later pushed to the side of the track by Coulthard who took the lead.<sup id="cite_ref-f1comrace_27-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrace-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_28-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher's right front wheel made slight contact with Coulthard's left front tyre in the process.<sup id="cite_ref-f1comrace_27-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrace-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher then immediately withstood Häkkinen's attempts to overtake him.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_28-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Frentzen and de la Rosa became the next two drivers to make pit stops on the same lap.<sup id="cite_ref-lapchart_29-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapchart-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher and Häkkinen made pit stops for the second time on lap 43 and both emerged in third and fourth respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-lapchart_29-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapchart-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello's pit stop on the 44th lap saw him drop to fifth after his team had issues with fitting his right front tyre.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_28-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard and Ralf Schumacher made pit stops over the next two laps.<sup id="cite_ref-lapchart_29-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapchart-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> At the completion of lap 48, with the frontrunners completing their pit stops, the order was Coulthard, Michael Schumacher, Häkkinen, Barrichello, Villeneuve, Trulli, Ralf Schumacher, Frentzen, Button, Fisichella, Salo, Diniz, Heidfeld, Irvine, Alesi and Gené.<sup id="cite_ref-lapchart_29-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapchart-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At the tail-end of the field, Gené and Alesi made contact on lap 52 and both drivers were sent spinning sideways. Both drivers continued in their respective positions.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_28-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Further up, Ralf Schumacher passed Trulli to clinch sixth position at turn five.<sup id="cite_ref-f1comrace_27-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrace-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> Häkkinen, meanwhile, was less than one second behind Michael Schumacher by lap 53 and continued to battle the latter for second position as his tyres degraded quicker than the McLarens.<sup id="cite_ref-lapchart_29-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapchart-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Irvine overtook Diniz for twelfth position two laps later. Michael Schumacher pulled over to the side of the track with an blown engine on lap 59.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_28-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Häkkinen was promoted to second place as a result of Michael Schumacher's retirement.<sup id="cite_ref-f1comrace_27-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrace-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard crossed the finish line on lap 72 to take his third victory of the season in a time of 1'38:05.538, at an average speed of 116.330 miles per hour (187.215&#160;km/h).<sup id="cite_ref-results_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-results-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Häkkinen finished second 14.7 seconds behind, ahead of Barrichello in third, Villeneuve in fourth, Ralf Schumacher in fifth and Trulli rounded out the points scoring positions in sixth. Frentzen, Button, Fisichella, Salo, Diniz and Heidfeld followed in the next six positions, with Irvine, Alesi and Gené the last of the classified finishers.<sup id="cite_ref-gprace_30-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gprace-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2000_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatright" style="width:33%;&#32;;"><link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r887775652"/>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">I am delighted to have won this Grand Prix and today's result puts us back where we were after Monaco – right in the championship fight. The limited amount of running which I achieved on Friday and Saturday makes the victory even more satisfying. I knew that we had a quick car and that once I settled into the race I would find an opportunity to pass Michael. It has been a tiring weekend but my mechanics, engineers and everyone on the pitwall did a great job – thank you.</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style="">David Coulthard, speaking after the race.<sup id="cite_ref-quotes_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-quotes-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>The top three drivers appeared on the podium to collect their trophies and in the subsequent <a href="/wiki/News_conference" class="mw-redirect" title="News conference">press conference</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_13-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard described his race weekend as "exhausting" because of the problems his team encountered during the event. He also apologised for the hand gestures he made towards Michael Schumacher after his first overtaking manoeuvre on the latter.<sup id="cite_ref-pressconf_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconf-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Häkkinen stated that he was happy with his second-place result and praised his team's efforts. He also spoke of how he watched Michael Schumacher's racing lines and believed he would have passed him had he not retired with an engine failure.<sup id="cite_ref-pressconf_33-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconf-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello revealed that he was ordered by Ferrari to reduce his pace in the race's closing stages as he held a large gap of 35 seconds to Villeneuve.<sup id="cite_ref-pressconf_33-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconf-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After Ferrari's victory at the previous race, their team principal <a href="/wiki/Jean_Todt" title="Jean Todt">Jean Todt</a>, said that "we seemed to be in a strong position but, of course, it is the order at the finish that counts",<sup id="cite_ref-quotes_32-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-quotes-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> while Michael Schumacher said that his car's tyres dropped in performance after taking his first pit stop.<sup id="cite_ref-quotes_32-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-quotes-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> McLaren technical director <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Newey" title="Adrian Newey">Adrian Newey</a> said that the pit stop strategy was crucial in winning the race: "The tyre wear did not spring any surprises on us and we were able to trust our estimations and co-ordinate the pit-stops to our best advantage."<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Villeneuve was happy with his strong finish and was surprised by his good start.<sup id="cite_ref-quotes_32-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-quotes-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Heidfeld, who made contact with his teammate Alesi during the race, apologised for the collision.<sup id="cite_ref-quotes_32-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-quotes-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Ralf Schumacher said that he reduced his pace to secure his fifth position.<sup id="cite_ref-quotes_32-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-quotes-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Trulli described his race as "difficult" because of the hot temperatures though he praised his mechanics for their work.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The overtaking manoeuvres made by Coulthard on Michael Schumacher received much media attention after the race. Coulthard accused Michael Schumacher of unsporting behaviour and dangerous driving, saying: "I had a clean run at Michael on the outside and equally I felt he drove me wide. You could say he had the right to do that because he had the track position, but I think it was a deliberate gesture in trying to run someone wide like that and I was very angry."<sup id="cite_ref-passschumi_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-passschumi-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher later denied any wrongdoing and citied a similar incident at the <a href="/wiki/1999_Japanese_Grand_Prix" title="1999 Japanese Grand Prix">1999 Japanese Grand Prix</a> where Coulthard pushed him wide although no objections were made. He also admitted that he did not see Coulthard's hand gesture.<sup id="cite_ref-passschumi_36-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-passschumi-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard sought clarification with the FIA over such overtakes with a source from Formula One's governing body noting: "It was hard racing – they are racing drivers aren't they?"<sup id="cite_ref-mclarenchamp_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mclarenchamp-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> GrandPrix.com said that Coulthard's overtake meant that "He is ready to stand up to Michael and he is not afraid of what will happen if Michael does not lift."<sup id="cite_ref-gprace_30-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gprace-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The race result meant that Coulthard narrowed the gap to Michael Schumacher in the Drivers' Championship to twelve points. Häkkinen remained in third place, six points ahead of Barrichello. Fisichella behind them maintained fifth position.<sup id="cite_ref-points_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> In the Constructors' Championship, McLaren's one-two finish reduced the lead of Ferrari to six points. Williams made up two points on the Benetton team, although they were still one point behind. Jordan retained fifth on eleventh points, with eight races of the season remaining.<sup id="cite_ref-points_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
