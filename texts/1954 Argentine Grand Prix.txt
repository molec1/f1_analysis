<p>The <b>1954 Argentine Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held at <a href="/wiki/Aut%C3%B3dromo_Juan_y_Oscar_G%C3%A1lvez" title="Autódromo Juan y Oscar Gálvez">Autódromo 17 de Octubre</a> in <a href="/wiki/Buenos_Aires" title="Buenos Aires">Buenos Aires</a>, <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a> on 17 January 1954. It was race 1 of 9 in the <a href="/wiki/1954_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1954 World Championship of Drivers">1954 World Championship of Drivers</a>.
</p><p>This was <a href="/wiki/Juan_Manuel_Fangio" title="Juan Manuel Fangio">Juan Manuel Fangio</a>'s first home victory, following <a href="/wiki/Alberto_Ascari" title="Alberto Ascari">Alberto Ascari</a>'s win in <a href="/wiki/1953_Argentine_Grand_Prix" title="1953 Argentine Grand Prix">1953</a>. He would repeat this with three consecutive victories in the following three years.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1954_Argentine_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>This race was run in an anti-clockwise direction, as opposed to clockwise the year before, and all subsequent races.</li>
<li>Ascari and Villoresi were not present due to the Lancia car not being ready.</li>
<li>Bayol scores his first career points.</li>
<li>Records tied or broken: Farina gets his 17th podium finish (this ties Ascari), and his 22nd race to score points (this gives him sole possession of the record).</li>
<li>First Race: Maserati 250F.</li>
<li>Farina scored the pole position at an age of 47 years and 79 days, which still is a record today.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
