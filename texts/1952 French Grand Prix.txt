<p>The <b>1952 French Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_Two" title="Formula Two">Formula Two</a> race held on 6 July 1952 at <a href="/wiki/Rouen-Les-Essarts" title="Rouen-Les-Essarts">Rouen-Les-Essarts</a>. It was race 4 of 8 in the <a href="/wiki/1952_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1952 World Championship of Drivers">1952 World Championship of Drivers</a>, in which each Grand Prix was run to Formula Two rules rather than the Formula One regulations normally used. Unusually this race was run over a duration of 3 hours, rather than a fixed distance.<sup id="cite_ref-hodges_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hodges-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1952_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Having won the previous weekend's <a href="/wiki/Grand_Prix_de_la_Marne" title="Grand Prix de la Marne">Grand Prix de la Marne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jean_Behra" title="Jean Behra">Jean Behra</a>, racing for <a href="/wiki/Gordini" title="Gordini">Equipe Gordini</a>, was among the favourites for the first French Grand Prix to be held at Rouen-Les-Essarts. Also driving for Gordini were regulars <a href="/wiki/Robert_Manzon" title="Robert Manzon">Robert Manzon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Birabongse_Bhanudej" title="Birabongse Bhanudej">Prince Bira</a>, alongside <a href="/wiki/Maurice_Trintignant" title="Maurice Trintignant">Maurice Trintignant</a>, who replaced <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Claes" title="Johnny Claes">Johnny Claes</a> from the lineup for the previous round. Claes entered the race in a <a href="/wiki/Simca-Gordini" class="mw-redirect" title="Simca-Gordini">Simca-Gordini</a> under his own 'Ecurie Belge' label, which he had used in the <a href="/wiki/1950_Formula_One_season" title="1950 Formula One season">1950</a> and <a href="/wiki/1951_Formula_One" class="mw-redirect" title="1951 Formula One">1951</a> seasons. <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> retained their lineup of <a href="/wiki/Alberto_Ascari" title="Alberto Ascari">Ascari</a>, <a href="/wiki/Giuseppe_Farina" title="Giuseppe Farina">Farina</a> and <a href="/wiki/Piero_Taruffi" title="Piero Taruffi">Taruffi</a>, who had locked out the front row of the grid in <a href="/wiki/1952_Belgian_Grand_Prix" title="1952 Belgian Grand Prix">Belgium</a>. There were also several privateer Ferrari entries: the Swiss duo of <a href="/wiki/Rudi_Fischer" title="Rudi Fischer">Rudi Fischer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Peter_Hirt" title="Peter Hirt">Peter Hirt</a>, representing Ecurie Espadon, the Italian pairing of <a href="/wiki/Franco_Comotti" title="Franco Comotti">Franco Comotti</a> and <a href="/wiki/Piero_Carini" title="Piero Carini">Piero Carini</a>, for Scuderia Marzotto, and <a href="/wiki/Louis_Rosier" title="Louis Rosier">Louis Rosier</a>. <a href="/wiki/Hersham_and_Walton_Motors" title="Hersham and Walton Motors">HWM</a> again ran regular drivers <a href="/wiki/Lance_Macklin" title="Lance Macklin">Lance Macklin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Peter_Collins_(racing_driver)" title="Peter Collins (racing driver)">Peter Collins</a>, this time alongside Frenchman <a href="/wiki/Yves_Giraud-Cabantous" title="Yves Giraud-Cabantous">Yves Giraud-Cabantous</a>. While the factory <a href="/wiki/Maserati_in_motorsport" title="Maserati in motorsport">Maserati</a> team remained absent, their new car, the <a href="/wiki/Maserati_A6GCM" title="Maserati A6GCM">A6GCM</a>, made its World Championship debut, driven by <a href="/wiki/Philippe_%C3%89tancelin" title="Philippe Étancelin">Philippe Étancelin</a> of Escuderia Bandeirantes. <a href="/wiki/Enrico_Plat%C3%A9" title="Enrico Platé">Enrico Platé</a> entered a pair of older Maseratis, the <a href="/wiki/Maserati_4CL_and_4CLT" title="Maserati 4CL and 4CLT">4CLT/48</a> model, for <a href="/wiki/Toulo_de_Graffenried" title="Toulo de Graffenried">Toulo de Graffenried</a> and <a href="/wiki/Harry_Schell" title="Harry Schell">Harry Schell</a>. Completing the grid were <a href="/wiki/Peter_Whitehead_(racing_driver)" title="Peter Whitehead (racing driver)">Peter Whitehead</a>, in a privately run <a href="/wiki/Alta_Car_and_Engineering_Company" title="Alta Car and Engineering Company">Alta</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Mike_Hawthorn" title="Mike Hawthorn">Mike Hawthorn</a>, who again took part in a <a href="/wiki/Cooper_Car_Company" title="Cooper Car Company">Cooper</a>-<a href="/wiki/Bristol_Cars" title="Bristol Cars">Bristol</a>.
</p><p>Ascari took his second consecutive pole position, with his Ferrari teammates Farina and Taruffi again joining him on the front row of the grid. The Gordini team locked out the second row, with Behra and Manzon qualifying in fourth and fifth, respectively. Their teammates Trintignant and Bira started from the third row, alongside Peter Collins in the fastest of the HWMs. The new Maserati A6GCM proved a disappointment, with Philippe Étancelin only managing to qualify on the seventh row of the grid (out of eight).
</p><p>The Ferraris once again dominated the race, with Alberto Ascari leading Farina from start to finish, thus taking his second consecutive victory in the World Championship. Despite a good start from the Gordinis of Manzon and Behra, that saw them take third and fourth place, respectively, by the end of the first lap, Piero Taruffi managed to regain third place on lap 4 and subsequently held it for the remainder of the race, ensuring that it was an all-Ferrari podium. Manzon finished fourth, a lap behind Taruffi, while his teammate Maurice Trintignant took the final points-scoring position of fifth. HWM driver Peter Collins took sixth, two laps behind Trintignant, ahead of Jean Behra, for whom seventh represented something of a recovery, having been in last place at the end of lap 3. His race had been compromised when he crashed and consequently needed to pit.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Ascari's win, and fastest lap, ensured that he took a five-point lead in the Drivers' Championship, ahead of fellow Ferrari driver Piero Taruffi. Farina's second consecutive second-place finish took him to third in the standings, one point adrift of Taruffi. <a href="/wiki/1952_Indianapolis_500" title="1952 Indianapolis 500">Indianapolis 500</a> winner <a href="/wiki/Troy_Ruttman" title="Troy Ruttman">Troy Ruttman</a> was a further four points behind in fourth, one point ahead of Gordini driver Robert Manzon.
</p>

<dl><dd><span class="citation wikicite" id="endnote_1"><b><a href="#ref_1">^1</a></b></span>  — Piero Taruffi qualified and drove the entire race in the #12 Ferrari. <a href="/wiki/Luigi_Villoresi" title="Luigi Villoresi">Luigi Villoresi</a>, who was also entered in the same car, was unable to participate due to injury.<sup id="cite_ref-statsf1results_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-statsf1results-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup></dd>
<dd><span class="citation wikicite" id="endnote_2"><b><a href="#ref_2">^2</a></b></span>  — Toulo de Graffenried qualified and drove 26 laps of the race in the #16 Maserati. <a href="/wiki/Harry_Schell" title="Harry Schell">Harry Schell</a>, whose own vehicle had already retired, took over the car for a further 8 laps before again being forced to retire.<sup id="cite_ref-espn_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-espn-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup></dd>
<dd><span class="citation wikicite" id="endnote_3"><b><a href="#ref_3">^3</a></b></span>  — Philippe Étancelin qualified and drove the entire race in the #28 Maserati. <a href="/wiki/Eitel_Cantoni" title="Eitel Cantoni">Eitel Cantoni</a> was also entered in the car, but took no part in the Grand Prix after being fired.<sup id="cite_ref-statsf1results_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-statsf1results-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup></dd>
<dd><span class="citation wikicite" id="endnote_4"><b><a href="#ref_4">^4</a></b></span>  — Chico Landi withdrew from the event prior to practice.<sup id="cite_ref-statsf1results_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-statsf1results-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup></dd>
<dd><span class="citation wikicite" id="endnote_5"><b><a href="#ref_5">^5</a></b></span>  — Rudi Fischer qualified and drove 37 laps of the race in the #36 Ferrari. He was initially due to drive the #34 <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_Tipo_500" title="Ferrari Tipo 500">Ferrari 500</a>, but engine problems in practice meant that he instead participated in a 212. <a href="/wiki/Peter_Hirt" title="Peter Hirt">Peter Hirt</a> took over the car for the remainder of the race.<sup id="cite_ref-espn_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-espn-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Rudolf_Schoeller" title="Rudolf Schoeller">Rudolf Schoeller</a>, named substitute driver for the car, was not used during the Grand Prix.<sup id="cite_ref-statsf1entrants_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-statsf1entrants-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup></dd>
<dd><span class="citation wikicite" id="endnote_6"><b><a href="#ref_6">^6</a></b></span>  — <a href="/wiki/Vittorio_Marzotto" title="Vittorio Marzotto">Vittorio Marzotto</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sergio_Sighinolfi" title="Sergio Sighinolfi">Sergio Sighinolfi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Reg_Parnell" title="Reg Parnell">Reg Parnell</a> were the designated substitute drivers for cars #38, #40 and #42, respectively. None of the three was used during the Grand Prix.<sup id="cite_ref-statsf1entrants_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-statsf1entrants-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup></dd></dl>
