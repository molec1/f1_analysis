<p>The <b>1994 European Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>XXXIX Gran Premio de Europa</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held on 16 October 1994 at the <a href="/wiki/Circuito_de_Jerez" title="Circuito de Jerez">Circuito Permanente de Jerez</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jerez_de_la_Frontera" title="Jerez de la Frontera">Jerez</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>. It was the fourteenth race of the <a href="/wiki/1994_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1994 Formula One World Championship">1994 Formula One World Championship</a>.
</p><p>The 69-lap race was won from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> by <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/Benetton_B194" title="Benetton B194">Benetton</a>-<a href="/wiki/Ford_Motor_Company" title="Ford Motor Company">Ford</a>. Schumacher, returning from a two-race ban, took his eighth victory of the season by 24.6 seconds from Drivers' Championship rival <a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW16" title="Williams FW16">Williams</a>-<a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> third in a <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/9" title="McLaren MP4/9">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Peugeot" title="Peugeot">Peugeot</a>.
</p><p>The win put Schumacher five points ahead of Hill with two races remaining, while Benetton regained the lead of the Constructors' Championship from Williams.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1994_European_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1994_European_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Argentine_Grand_Prix" title="Argentine Grand Prix">Argentine Grand Prix</a> had been due to return to the Formula One calendar on this date, but ongoing modernisation of the <a href="/wiki/Aut%C3%B3dromo_Juan_y_Oscar_G%C3%A1lvez" title="Autódromo Juan y Oscar Gálvez">Buenos Aires</a> circuit meant that this was postponed until early in the <a href="/wiki/1995_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1995 Formula One World Championship">1995</a> season.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> A race at Jerez, the first since <a href="/wiki/1990_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1990 Formula One World Championship">1990</a>, was organised in its place, and was given the title of the European Grand Prix, which had been used the previous year for the race at <a href="/wiki/Donington_Park" title="Donington Park">Donington Park</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> returned to the <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a> team following his ban from the <a href="/wiki/1994_Italian_Grand_Prix" title="1994 Italian Grand Prix">Italian</a> and <a href="/wiki/1994_Portuguese_Grand_Prix" title="1994 Portuguese Grand Prix">Portuguese Grands Prix</a>, while <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> returned to <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a>, the <a href="/wiki/1994_PPG_Indy_Car_World_Series" title="1994 PPG Indy Car World Series">1994 CART season</a> having ended the previous week. Elsewhere, <a href="/wiki/Flavio_Briatore" title="Flavio Briatore">Flavio Briatore</a> bought <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Herbert" title="Johnny Herbert">Johnny Herbert</a>'s contract from <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Lotus</a>'s administrators and transferred him to <a href="/wiki/Equipe_Ligier" title="Equipe Ligier">Ligier</a>, trading places with <a href="/wiki/%C3%89ric_Bernard" title="Éric Bernard">Éric Bernard</a>, while rookies <a href="/wiki/Hideki_Noda" title="Hideki Noda">Hideki Noda</a> and <a href="/wiki/Domenico_Schiattarella" title="Domenico Schiattarella">Domenico Schiattarella</a> joined the <a href="/wiki/Larrousse" title="Larrousse">Larrousse</a> and <a href="/wiki/Simtek" title="Simtek">Simtek</a> teams respectively, replacing <a href="/wiki/Yannick_Dalmas" title="Yannick Dalmas">Yannick Dalmas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jean-Marc_Gounon" title="Jean-Marc Gounon">Jean-Marc Gounon</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1994_European_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Schumacher took pole from Drivers' Championship rival <a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a> by 0.13 seconds, with Mansell third but half a second behind Hill. <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a> took fourth in the <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a>, followed by <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>. Herbert was seventh in the Ligier, with <a href="/wiki/Gianni_Morbidelli" title="Gianni Morbidelli">Gianni Morbidelli</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Footwork_Arrows" title="Footwork Arrows">Footwork</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> in the <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a> in the second Jordan completing the top ten.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1994_European_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the start, Hill got ahead of Schumacher, while Mansell fell to sixth behind Frentzen, Barrichello and Berger. Mansell re-passed Berger on lap 2 and Barrichello on lap 6, before the Jordan driver got by again on lap 12. Noda's debut ended with a gearbox failure after ten laps; as he slowed, he was hit by Mansell, who subsequently pitted for a new nosecone and dropped out of contention.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Schumacher overtook Hill during the first round of pit stops; both were well clear of Frentzen - who was running a one-stop strategy - with Häkkinen up to fourth and Irvine fifth. Hill briefly went ahead again during the second stops, after which Schumacher retained a comfortable lead for the rest of the race. Frentzen's strategy backfired as he fell to seventh, behind Berger and Barrichello, while Irvine moved ahead of Häkkinen and into third, only to be re-passed by the McLaren driver as a result of a quicker second stop. In the closing stages, Barrichello developed a left rear puncture, putting Frentzen back in the top six, just ahead of <a href="/wiki/Ukyo_Katayama" title="Ukyo Katayama">Ukyo Katayama</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_Racing" title="Tyrrell Racing">Tyrrell</a>.
</p><p>Schumacher's eventual margin of victory over Hill was 24.6 seconds, with another 45 seconds back to Häkkinen and a further nine back to Irvine, the last driver on the lead lap. Berger and Frentzen completed the top six, Frentzen holding off Katayama for the final point by 0.2 seconds.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> With two races remaining, Schumacher led Hill in the Drivers' Championship by five points, while Benetton moved back into the lead of the Constructors' Championship by two points from Williams.
</p><p>In the second Sauber, <a href="/wiki/Andrea_de_Cesaris" title="Andrea de Cesaris">Andrea de Cesaris</a> made his 208th and final Grand Prix start, at the time second only to <a href="/wiki/Riccardo_Patrese" title="Riccardo Patrese">Riccardo Patrese</a>. <a href="/wiki/Karl_Wendlinger" title="Karl Wendlinger">Karl Wendlinger</a> was due to return to the Swiss team at the next race in <a href="/wiki/1994_Japanese_Grand_Prix" title="1994 Japanese Grand Prix">Japan</a>, following his crash at <a href="/wiki/1994_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="1994 Monaco Grand Prix">Monaco</a> earlier in the season.
</p>
