In the inset, Stacey before the race.</div></div></div>
<p>The <b>1960 Belgian Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held at <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Spa-Francorchamps" title="Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps">Spa-Francorchamps</a> on 19 June 1960. It was race 5 of 10 in the <a href="/wiki/1960_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1960 World Championship of Drivers">1960 World Championship of Drivers</a> and race 4 of 9 in the <a href="/wiki/1960_International_Cup_for_Formula_One_Manufacturers" class="mw-redirect" title="1960 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers">1960 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers</a>. <a href="/wiki/Stirling_Moss" title="Stirling Moss">Stirling Moss</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mike_Taylor_(driver)" class="mw-redirect" title="Mike Taylor (driver)">Mike Taylor</a> were seriously injured in crashes during practice, and <a href="/wiki/Chris_Bristow" title="Chris Bristow">Chris Bristow</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alan_Stacey" title="Alan Stacey">Alan Stacey</a> were killed in accidents during the race. With the <a href="/wiki/1994_San_Marino_Grand_Prix" title="1994 San Marino Grand Prix">1994 San Marino Grand Prix</a>, it is one of two occasions in which two driver fatalities have occurred at a Formula One race meeting, and the only one where both occurred during the race itself.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice_and_race_laps_1-19">Practice and race laps 1-19</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1960_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Practice and race laps 1-19">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Practice for the event saw <a href="/wiki/Stirling_Moss" title="Stirling Moss">Stirling Moss</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mike_Taylor_(driver)" class="mw-redirect" title="Mike Taylor (driver)">Mike Taylor</a> injured in separate accidents, with Taylor suffering injuries which ended his racing career, and Moss injured seriously enough (two broken legs) to keep him out of racing for a number of months including the <a href="/wiki/1960_24_Hours_of_Le_Mans" title="1960 24 Hours of Le Mans">1960 24 Hours of Le Mans</a>.  In the race itself, the Lotus drivers Innes Ireland and Jim Clark got off to good starts before Ireland eventually spun out with clutch trouble on lap 14.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Fatal_accidents">Fatal accidents</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1960_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Fatal accidents">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On lap 20 <a href="/wiki/Chris_Bristow" title="Chris Bristow">Chris Bristow</a>, driving a year-old Cooper for the British Racing Partnership, got off line and lost control at Malmedy, crashing into a four-foot high embankment and was thrown from his car while battling for sixth place with the Ferrari of Mairesse, and landed on some barbed wire which beheaded him. 
</p><p>Five laps later, <a href="/wiki/Alan_Stacey" title="Alan Stacey">Alan Stacey</a> was hit in the face by a bird at Masta as his car crashed then somersaulted off the track and landed in a field as it went up in flames and Stacey was burned to death while still in the car on lap 25. It was the only Formula One race meeting in which two drivers were killed until the deaths of <a href="/wiki/Roland_Ratzenberger" title="Roland Ratzenberger">Roland Ratzenberger</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> at the <a href="/wiki/1994_San_Marino_Grand_Prix" title="1994 San Marino Grand Prix">1994 San Marino Grand Prix</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_conclusion">Race conclusion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1960_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Race conclusion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The race distance had been lengthened to 36 laps from 24 laps. The results highlight an unusual quirk in the rules regarding classification of non-finishers. Under modern rules, Graham Hill would have been classified third, since he completed lap 35 before the lapped <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Gendebien" title="Olivier Gendebien">Olivier Gendebien</a>. Hill then retired, in the pits, but was not classified since he did not push his car over the line after the winner took the finish (as required by the rules of the time). In fact the rule about crossing the finishing line was inconsistently applied – at the <a href="/wiki/1959_German_Grand_Prix" title="1959 German Grand Prix">1959 German Grand Prix</a>, Harry Schell was classified seventh despite only completing 49 of the race's 60 laps.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
