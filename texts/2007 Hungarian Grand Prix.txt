<p>The <b>2007 Hungarian Grand Prix</b> was the eleventh race of the <a href="/wiki/2007_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2007 Formula One season">2007 Formula One season</a> and was held on 5 August 2007 at the <a href="/wiki/Hungaroring" title="Hungaroring">Hungaroring</a> racing track in <a href="/wiki/Budapest" title="Budapest">Budapest</a>. It was also memorable for an incident in the Saturday qualifying session, between McLaren teammates <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>.
</p><p>Hamilton went into the race with a 2-point advantage in World Championship points over Alonso and was 11 points ahead of 3rd place man <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> after a chaotic <a href="/wiki/2007_European_Grand_Prix" title="2007 European Grand Prix">European Grand Prix</a>, whilst McLaren held a 27-point lead in the Constructors' Championship over their main rivals <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>. <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> scored his last ever World Championship points at this race.
</p><p>Japanese former <a href="/wiki/Super_Aguri" class="mw-redirect" title="Super Aguri">Super Aguri</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Sakon_Yamamoto" title="Sakon Yamamoto">Sakon Yamamoto</a> filled the second <a href="/wiki/Spyker_F1" title="Spyker F1">Spyker</a> car, replacing <a href="/wiki/2007_European_Grand_Prix" title="2007 European Grand Prix">European GP</a> leader, German <a href="/wiki/Markus_Winkelhock" title="Markus Winkelhock">Markus Winkelhock</a>. <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a>, the youngest Grand Prix point scorer made his second Grand Prix start replacing <a href="/wiki/Scott_Speed" title="Scott Speed">Scott Speed</a> at <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Scuderia Toro Rosso</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-f1fvetteltotororosso_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1fvetteltotororosso-3">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Controversy hit the qualifying sessions when Hamilton failed to honor an agreement to let Alonso past on track, this denied Alonso a competitive advantage when setting his lap time.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcalonsogreat2of5_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcalonsogreat2of5-4">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Alonso then retaliated by holding Hamilton up in the pit lane, to in turn deny Hamilton a chance to record a final lap time. Stewards later dropped Alonso five places down the starting grid of the race, putting him in sixth while Hamilton started in pole position. McLaren were also told they would not score any Constructor's Championship points in the race and wouldn't be presented with a winning constructor's trophy on the podium if one of their drivers were to win the race. The team stated Hamilton's hold-up was not Alonso's fault (although Alonso gave the stewards a different explanation, that he was inquiring about his tyres),<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and that Hamilton had disobeyed team orders to let Alonso pass him, which put the two drivers out of sequence for their pit stops.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> McLaren had initially expressed their intent to appeal the Constructor's points penalty but ultimately withdrew their appeal after subsequently losing all their Constructor's points as penalty for the <a href="/wiki/2007_Formula_One_espionage_controversy" title="2007 Formula One espionage controversy">espionage</a> scandal.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2007_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice">Practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2007_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/BMW_Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="BMW Sauber">BMW</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a> was the fastest driver in the first of the practice sessions with a time of 1:22.390.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> He was followed by the two <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferraris</a> of <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a>, and the two <a href="/wiki/McLaren_(racing)" class="mw-redirect" title="McLaren (racing)">McLarens</a> of <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>. Friday's second session saw the two <a href="/wiki/McLaren_(racing)" class="mw-redirect" title="McLaren (racing)">McLarens</a> of <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> in first and third, respectively, separated by <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a>. <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>'s time was 1:20.919.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In Saturday's practice session <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> took first spot with a time of 1:20.183, followed by <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> and then <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2007_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

Statements consisting only of original research should be removed.</span>  <small class="date-container"><i>(<span class="date">September 2007</span>)</i></small><small class="hide-when-compact"><i> (<a href="/wiki/Help:Maintenance_template_removal" title="Help:Maintenance template removal">Learn how and when to remove this template message</a>)</i></small></div></td></tr></tbody></table>
<p>In the first qualifying session, <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> posted the fastest time with 1:19:570, which was the quickest time recorded in the weekend so far, being the only lap under 1:20. Hamilton and the rest of the front-runners led the way, and as the session ended <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a>, the <a href="/wiki/2006_Hungarian_Grand_Prix" title="2006 Hungarian Grand Prix">2006 winner</a>, had bumped his Honda teammate <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> into the relegation zone. However, Button was soon beaten by <a href="/wiki/Vitantonio_Liuzzi" title="Vitantonio Liuzzi">Liuzzi</a>. Joining the two Hondas were <a href="/wiki/Takuma_Sato" title="Takuma Sato">Takuma Sato</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sakon_Yamamoto" title="Sakon Yamamoto">Sakon Yamamoto</a>.
</p><p>In the second session, <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> of Toyota led the pace early, but Hamilton and his teammate Alonso posted the best times. Massa and Kubica struggled to 11th and 12th places, but, Kubica's team put on new tyres and reached the top 10. Massa had worse luck; the Ferrari team weren't prepared for him to need a second run in Q2, and he was released into the pit-lane without being refuelled.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> His pit crew had to run down the pit-lane and pull him back to his pit to be refuelled, by which time his tyres had lost temperature so when he finally made it out, the lack of grip meant he was unable to improve his time. Joining Massa among the eliminated drivers were David Coulthard in 11th, Heikki Kovalainen in 12th, Alexander Wurz of Williams Toyota in 13th, 15th was Anthony Davidson, and 16th was Vitantonio Liuzzi.
</p><p>Hamilton led out of the pit-lane at the beginning of Q3, followed by teammate Alonso. Hamilton set the quickest lap time the first time around, but was soon beaten by Nick Heidfeld. Around the 9-minute mark, Trulli was the first into the pits to change tyres. Alonso was next into the pits; at the time, he was ninth. All the others followed suit, and meaningful times were soon put in. Hamilton again impressed the crowd with another time below 1:20. Alonso and Kimi Räikkönen, the only remaining Ferrari driver, <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a>, Kubica, and Trulli filed in behind. With around 2:30 remaining, Alonso again pitted for fresh tyres, along with almost every other driver. The McLaren crew held Alonso for around 20 seconds after the tyres were fitted, in a stated attempt to give him a clear track (however, this reasoning was later rejected by the stewards as most other cars on the track had also pitted). At the time, Hamilton was 'stacked up' behind, waiting for Alonso to leave. But when Alonso was released, he remained stationary for a further 10 seconds, even though the pit crew were gesturing for him to leave. Hamilton then pulled in for his final set of tyres, but the delay meant there was insufficient time for him to get back to the start-finish line before the chequered flag and the end of qualifying. Alonso started his flying lap with less than 2 seconds of the session remaining while Hamilton missed out by around 5 seconds.
</p><p>Alonso's final lap was good enough to claim pole and push Hamilton down to second. TV pictures showed <a href="/wiki/Ron_Dennis" title="Ron Dennis">Ron Dennis</a> angrily throwing his headphones down as Alonso pulled out of the pits; and after the session was over he was seen having a serious conversation with Alonso's trainer. He refused to comment on the pit-lane delay, although he later explained that Hamilton had ignored team orders to let Alonso past at the beginning of the fuel burn phase, which had put the cars out of sequence for their stops.
</p><p>Behind the McLarens of Alonso and Hamilton, third went to Heidfeld, which surprised many Ferrari and BMW fans. In fourth was Räikkönen, fifth was Rosberg. Sixth place was taken by Ralf Schumacher of Germany, seventh was the second BMW Sauber of Robert Kubica. Giancarlo Fisichella posted the eighth fastest time, with Trulli and Webber rounding out the top ten.
</p><p>Following the qualifying session, the stewards investigated an incident in Q1, when Fisichella appeared to block Yamamoto into the final corner – and they also investigated the Alonso/Hamilton incident (subsequently called <i>Pitlanegate</i> by the media).<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Fisichella was handed a five-place penalty, dropping him down to thirteenth. Alonso was also handed a five-place penalty on the grid, meaning he started the race in sixth position and promoted Hamilton to pole position. His McLaren team were told they would no be allowed to score any Constructors' World Championship points for this race,<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> which McLaren subsequently appealed.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_race">The race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2007_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: The race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<table class="box-Expand_section plainlinks metadata ambox mbox-small-left ambox-content" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-image"><a href="/wiki/File:Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg" class="image"><img alt="[icon]" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg/20px-Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg.png" decoding="async" width="20" height="14" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg/30px-Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg/40px-Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="44" data-file-height="31" /></a></td><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This section <b>needs expansion</b>. <small>You can help by <a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=2007_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=">adding to it</a>.</small>  <small class="date-container"><i>(<span class="date">December 2009</span>)</i></small></div></td></tr></tbody></table>
<p><a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> led every lap of the race (the first time anyone had done so in 2007),<sup id="cite_ref-f1fhungariangrandprixstats_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1fhungariangrandprixstats-14">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> but had to withstand near constant pressure from the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> of <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a>, which appeared more competitive than it did in qualifying and practice. Hamilton's team-mate <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> managed to climb from his 6th place grid-slot to 4th by the end of the race, just behind the <a href="/wiki/BMW_Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="BMW Sauber">BMW Sauber</a> of <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a>. Alonso spent much of the race behind the <a href="/wiki/Toyota_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Toyota F1">Toyota</a> of <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a>, only getting past by the third stint. Schumacher finished in 6th place, after repassing <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> (7th) and <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> (8th) who were on three-stop strategies compared to Schumacher's two-stop strategy. This was the last time that <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> scored in <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a>.
</p><p>Retirements from the race were <a href="/wiki/Sakon_Yamamoto" title="Sakon Yamamoto">Sakon Yamamoto</a>, who crashed in the early laps of the race on his <a href="/wiki/Spyker_F1" title="Spyker F1">Spyker</a> debut. <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vitantonio_Liuzzi" title="Vitantonio Liuzzi">Vitantonio Liuzzi</a> both suffered mechanical failure while the <a href="/wiki/Super_Aguri" class="mw-redirect" title="Super Aguri">Super Aguri</a> of <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Davidson" title="Anthony Davidson">Anthony Davidson</a> was taken out after a collision with the <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a> of <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a>. The moment <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> crossed the finish line he was celebrated becoming the first ever rookie and the only active Formula One driver to have achieved a Grand Chelem at the time. However 0.715 seconds later, <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> improved Hamilton's fastest lap, depriving him of the record.<sup id="cite_ref-hun2007fastestlapsuccession_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hun2007fastestlapsuccession-15">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
