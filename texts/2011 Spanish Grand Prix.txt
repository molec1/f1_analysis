<p>The <b>2011 Spanish Grand Prix</b>, formally the <b>Formula 1 Gran Premio de España Santander 2011</b>,<sup id="cite_ref-Santander_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Santander-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race that was held on 22 May 2011 at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Catalunya" class="mw-redirect" title="Circuit de Catalunya">Circuit de Catalunya</a> in <a href="/wiki/Barcelona" title="Barcelona">Barcelona</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> It was the fifth round of the <a href="/wiki/2011_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2011 Formula One season">2011 Formula One season</a>. The 66-lap race was won by the championship leader, <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> after starting from second on the grid. <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> finished in second place, and his teammate <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> completed the podium in third position.<sup id="cite_ref-Race_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Race-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As a consequence of the race, Vettel extended his lead in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">World Drivers' Championship</a> to 41&#160;points over Hamilton. <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a>, who started the race from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>, finished fourth in the race but he maintained third place in the championship, 10&#160;points behind second-place Hamilton, and six ahead of Button. In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">World Constructors' Championship</a>, Red Bull extended their championship lead to 47&#160;points over McLaren, with <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> a further 63&#160;points behind in third position, after only <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> reached the finish for the team, in fifth position. <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a> finished 9th and became the first driver from <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_drivers_from_Mexico" title="Formula One drivers from Mexico">Mexico</a> to score a point since <a href="/wiki/H%C3%A9ctor_Rebaque" title="Héctor Rebaque">Héctor Rebaque</a> in the <a href="/wiki/1981_Dutch_Grand_Prix" title="1981 Dutch Grand Prix">1981 Dutch Grand Prix</a>.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Spanish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Heading into the race, <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> led the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a> with 93 points, 35 more than <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> on 58 points, who had 3 more than <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> on 55 points. Other championship rivals throughout the season, <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> were fourth and fifth on 46 and 41 respectively.
</p><p>Tyre supplier <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_tyres" title="Formula One tyres">Pirelli</a> brought its silver-banded hard compound tyre as the harder "prime" tyre and the yellow-banded soft compound as the softer "option" compound. This was the same tyre selection that Bridgestone had chosen to bring to the Spanish Grand Prix for the past two years.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a>, <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a> held an advantage of 43 points over <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>, with 148 points to McLaren's 105. <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> were a further 40 points behind McLaren on 65 points, and were building a gap from <a href="/wiki/Lotus_Renault_GP" class="mw-redirect" title="Lotus Renault GP">Renault</a> – on 42 points - who were now having to fend off <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes GP</a> who were on 26 points.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> introduced a new rear wing for the race, which came under scrutiny from the stewards and was subsequently banned.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div style="clear:left;"></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice_and_qualifying">Practice and qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Spanish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Practice and qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The first practice session saw upgrades by every single team,<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> and took place in sunny conditions. Mark Webber led at the end of the first practice, 1.007 seconds ahead of his teammate Sebastian Vettel, with <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> in third place in the <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:2011_Spanish_GP_Friday_10.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5e/2011_Spanish_GP_Friday_10.jpg/220px-2011_Spanish_GP_Friday_10.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5e/2011_Spanish_GP_Friday_10.jpg/330px-2011_Spanish_GP_Friday_10.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5e/2011_Spanish_GP_Friday_10.jpg/440px-2011_Spanish_GP_Friday_10.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3888" data-file-height="2592" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:2011_Spanish_GP_Friday_10.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a>'s run of five consecutive pole positions was ended by teammate <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a></div></div></div>
<p>In the second practice session, Webber topped the timesheets again, this time with Lewis Hamilton coming second splitting the Red Bulls with Vettel in third.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the third practice session, Vettel topped the timesheets despite electrical problems that limited his running. <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a>'s car suffered a fire, causing extensive damage to his car that could not be fixed in time for the qualifying session.
</p><p>In qualifying, Mark Webber took his first pole of the season, with his teammate Vettel, who had been unable to use his KERS during the session, behind him in second. In third and fifth places were the McLarens of Hamilton and Button, split by the Ferrari of Fernando Alonso. Further down the grid Team Lotus celebrated their first ever entry to the second qualifying session on a dry track, with Heikki Kovalainen qualifying 15th, ahead of both Force Indias.<sup id="cite_ref-bbc_news_quali_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc_news_quali-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Heidfeld was unable to set a qualifying time, however, he was allowed to participate in the race due to having set competitive times in practice prior to the accident. This was a similar situation to the previous race in <a href="/wiki/2011_Turkish_Grand_Prix" title="2011 Turkish Grand Prix">Turkey</a> where <a href="/wiki/Kamui_Kobayashi" title="Kamui Kobayashi">Kamui Kobayashi</a> had not posted a qualifying time, but had been allowed entry due to his consistent lap times within the 107% time during practice.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Spanish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Fernando Alonso made a perfect start to the race, overtaking both Red Bulls and Hamilton into the first corner to the cheers of the Spanish fans, and leading the race until lap 18. However, Alonso faded after the first round of pitstops where Sebastian Vettel took the lead, a position which he held to the end of the race in spite of the efforts of Lewis Hamilton, who fought him all the way to the close, finishing only 0.6 seconds behind the winner, with his teammate Button over half a minute behind in third. Button had used a three-stop strategy instead of a four-stop strategy, like most other drivers, to get ahead of Webber and Alonso. On lap 31, Liuzzi's car stopped near the Pirelli tunnel due to a gearbox failure, forcing his retirement.
</p><p>Mark Webber came in fourth, after spending much of the race behind Fernando Alonso, only overtaking him on the 39th lap when the Spaniard pitted. Kovalainen crashed on lap 51, damaging the left side and wheels of his car. On Lap 57, Massa, struggling with his gearbox, beached his car in the Turns 6 and 7 chicane while running tenth, his game was over. Despite starting in the pits, behind all the other cars, Nick Heidfeld had a successful race, bringing his car home in the points with an eighth-place finish. After saving a set of tyres by not setting a Q3 time, Michael Schumacher finished sixth ahead of teammate Rosberg. The race was notable for Ferrari's lack of pace on the prime tyre with Alonso slipping from first to fifth in the pit stops alone. <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a> scored his first Formula One points with ninth place, after he was disqualified from seventh place at the <a href="/wiki/2011_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="2011 Australian Grand Prix">Australian Grand Prix</a>.  
</p>
