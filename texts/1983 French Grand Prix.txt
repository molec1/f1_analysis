<p>The <b>1983 French Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held at <a href="/wiki/Paul_Ricard_Circuit" class="mw-redirect" title="Paul Ricard Circuit">Paul Ricard</a> on April 17, 1983.
</p><p>French driver <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> won the race for the <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a> team, and this was the French marque's 3rd French Grand Prix win in a row and the 4th in 5 seasons. Second was the <a href="/wiki/Brabham" title="Brabham">Brabham</a>-<a href="/wiki/BMW_in_Formula_One" title="BMW in Formula One">BMW</a> of <a href="/wiki/1981_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1981 Formula One World Championship">1981</a> World Champion <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a>, with Prost's Renault teammate <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Cheever" title="Eddie Cheever">Eddie Cheever</a> finishing third. Rounding out the points finishers were <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Tambay" title="Patrick Tambay">Patrick Tambay</a> in his <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, and the non-turbo <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williamses</a> of <a href="/wiki/Keke_Rosberg" title="Keke Rosberg">Keke Rosberg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Laffite" title="Jacques Laffite">Jacques Laffite</a>. This was the final time that both <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a> cars started on the front row until the <a href="/wiki/2003_Malaysian_Grand_Prix" title="2003 Malaysian Grand Prix">2003 Malaysian Grand Prix</a>, when young Spaniard <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> started on the front row.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Summary">Summary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1983_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Summary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1983_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On home soil, Renault dominated the French Grand Prix. Alain Prost took pole position 2.3 seconds faster than second placed teammate Eddie Cheever. On the fast Circuit Paul Ricard with its 1.8&#160;km long Mistral Straight, turbo engines had a large advantage. The fastest normally-aspirated car was the 12th placed <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/1" title="McLaren MP4/1">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Cosworth_DFV" title="Cosworth DFV">Ford</a> of <a href="/wiki/1983_United_States_Grand_Prix_West" title="1983 United States Grand Prix West">Long Beach</a> runner up <a href="/wiki/Niki_Lauda" title="Niki Lauda">Niki Lauda</a>, qualifying some 4.3 seconds slower than pole-sitter Prost. After occupying the front row of the grid at Long Beach, Ferrari had difficulty with <a href="/wiki/Ren%C3%A9_Arnoux" title="René Arnoux">René Arnoux</a> qualifying 4th and Tambay qualifying 11th.
</p><p>Failing to qualify for the race were the <a href="/wiki/RAM_Racing" title="RAM Racing">RAM</a>-Fords of <a href="/wiki/Eliseo_Salazar" title="Eliseo Salazar">Eliseo Salazar</a> and debutante <a href="/wiki/Jean-Louis_Schlesser" title="Jean-Louis Schlesser">Jean-Louis Schlesser</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Osella" title="Osella">Osella</a> of <a href="/wiki/Piercarlo_Ghinzani" title="Piercarlo Ghinzani">Piercarlo Ghinzani</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1983_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As he had done in qualifying, Prost dominated the race for his first win in over 12 months, only losing the lead during the mid-race pit stops. Piquet finished second in the Brabham-BMW while Eddie Cheever finished 3rd in his Renault. Tambay managed to salvage what had been a tough weekend by finishing 4th with the Williams pair of Rosberg and Laffite finishing a lap down in 5th and 6th respectively.
</p><p>Before the race, <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Lotus</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> had had his toes accidentally run over by his own team pushing his car in the pits which caused him to need a pain killing injection. He bravely started the race but retired after just 6 laps when the pain of pushing the pedals became unbearable. Long Beach winner <a href="/wiki/John_Watson_(racing_driver)" title="John Watson (racing driver)">John Watson</a> was the races first retirement on lap 3 with engine failure in his McLaren.
</p>
