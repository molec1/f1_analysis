<p>The <b>1985 Dutch Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held at <a href="/wiki/Circuit_Zandvoort" title="Circuit Zandvoort">Circuit Zandvoort</a> on 25 August 1985. It was the eleventh round of the <a href="/wiki/1985_FIA_Formula_One_World_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="1985 FIA Formula One World Championship">1985 FIA Formula One World Championship</a> and the 34th World Championship Grand Prix to be held in the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a>. The race was held over 70 laps of the four kilometre circuit for a race distance of 298 kilometres. The race also proved to be the  25th and last Grand Prix victory for triple World Champion <a href="/wiki/Niki_Lauda" title="Niki Lauda">Niki Lauda</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/2" title="McLaren MP4/2">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Techniques_d%27Avant_Garde" title="Techniques d&#39;Avant Garde">TAG</a>. Lauda's teammate <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> was second, with Brazilian racer <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> third in his <a href="/wiki/Lotus_97T" title="Lotus 97T">Lotus</a>-<a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>. However, it was also to be the last Dutch Grand Prix for 36 years. It was planned to be re-introduced in <a href="/wiki/2020_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2020 Formula One World Championship">2020</a>, on a revised Zandvoort circuit, however the re-introduction was postponed due to the <a href="/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic" title="COVID-19 pandemic">COVID-19 pandemic</a>, which would be eventually cancelled. The race is now expected to take place in <a href="/wiki/2021_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2021 Formula One World Championship">2021</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It was also the last Grand Prix for West German <a href="/wiki/Stefan_Bellof" title="Stefan Bellof">Stefan Bellof</a>, who was killed the following week at the <a href="/wiki/1985_1000_km_of_Spa" title="1985 1000 km of Spa">1000 km of Spa</a> <a href="/wiki/World_Sportscar_Championship" title="World Sportscar Championship">World Sportscar</a> race.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_summary">Race summary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1985_Dutch_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Race summary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Tambay%27s_car_at_1985_Dutch_Grand_Prix.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cc/Tambay%27s_car_at_1985_Dutch_Grand_Prix.jpg/220px-Tambay%27s_car_at_1985_Dutch_Grand_Prix.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="146" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cc/Tambay%27s_car_at_1985_Dutch_Grand_Prix.jpg/330px-Tambay%27s_car_at_1985_Dutch_Grand_Prix.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cc/Tambay%27s_car_at_1985_Dutch_Grand_Prix.jpg/440px-Tambay%27s_car_at_1985_Dutch_Grand_Prix.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3649" data-file-height="2419" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Tambay%27s_car_at_1985_Dutch_Grand_Prix.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Tambay's Renault is being recovered after the accident during the warm-up session</div></div></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Tambay" title="Patrick Tambay">Patrick Tambay</a>, who qualified sixth, had a huge crash at nearly 200&#160;mph (322&#160;km/h) in the Sunday morning warm-up following a suspension failure on the start-finish straight. Tambay escaped shaken but unhurt, and took the start in the spare car.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Start_of_1985_Dutch_Grand_Prix.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/95/Start_of_1985_Dutch_Grand_Prix.jpg/220px-Start_of_1985_Dutch_Grand_Prix.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="146" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/95/Start_of_1985_Dutch_Grand_Prix.jpg/330px-Start_of_1985_Dutch_Grand_Prix.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/95/Start_of_1985_Dutch_Grand_Prix.jpg/440px-Start_of_1985_Dutch_Grand_Prix.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3649" data-file-height="2419" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Start_of_1985_Dutch_Grand_Prix.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Keke_Rosberg" title="Keke Rosberg">Rosberg</a> took the lead ahead of <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Senna</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Prost</a> and <a href="/wiki/Teo_Fabi" title="Teo Fabi">Fabi</a>, while <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Piquet</a>'s Brabham stalled at the start</div></div></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a> recorded his first and only pole position of the season, averaging 133.824&#160;mph (215.369&#160;km/h), the first for tyre manufacturer <a href="/wiki/Pirelli" title="Pirelli">Pirelli</a>. However, he stalled his <a href="/wiki/Brabham" title="Brabham">Brabham</a> at the start and was eventually push-started, almost a lap behind the leaders. He eventually finished eighth.
</p><p>Niki Lauda took his 25th and final Grand Prix win in his <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Techniques_d%27Avant_Garde" title="Techniques d&#39;Avant Garde">TAG</a>. His teammate <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> finished second, only 0.232 seconds behind; the two had diced for the lead over the final twelve laps of the race. <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> continued his late-season charge by finishing third in his <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Lotus</a>, albeit 48 seconds behind the McLarens; he finished just ahead of Prost's Drivers' Championship rival <a href="/wiki/Michele_Alboreto" title="Michele Alboreto">Michele Alboreto</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>. Senna's teammate <a href="/wiki/Elio_de_Angelis" title="Elio de Angelis">Elio de Angelis</a> was fifth, with <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a>' <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> taking the final point for sixth.
</p><p>Following his car destroying crash at the previous race in <a href="/wiki/1985_Austrian_Grand_Prix" title="1985 Austrian Grand Prix">Austria</a>, this was also the last time <a href="/wiki/Andrea_de_Cesaris" title="Andrea de Cesaris">Andrea de Cesaris</a> appeared in a <a href="/wiki/Equipe_Ligier" title="Equipe Ligier">Ligier</a>. It would in fact be the Italian's last race of the season.
</p>
