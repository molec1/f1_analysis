<p>The <b>2015 Bahrain Grand Prix</b> (formally known as the <b>2015 Formula 1 Gulf Air Bahrain Grand Prix</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Auto_racing" title="Auto racing">motor race</a> that was held on 19 April 2015 at the <a href="/wiki/Bahrain_International_Circuit" title="Bahrain International Circuit">Bahrain International Circuit</a> in <a href="/wiki/Sakhir" title="Sakhir">Sakhir</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bahrain" title="Bahrain">Bahrain</a>. The race was the fourth round of the <a href="/wiki/2015_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2015 Formula One season">2015 season</a> and marked the eleventh time that the <a href="/wiki/Bahrain_Grand_Prix" title="Bahrain Grand Prix">Bahrain Grand Prix</a> has been run as a round of the Formula One World Championship.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>, who was the defending race winner, came into the race with a 13-point lead over <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> after his victory a week prior in <a href="/wiki/2015_Chinese_Grand_Prix" title="2015 Chinese Grand Prix">China</a>. He took the 42nd pole position of his career during Saturday's qualifying, and his fourth in a row. In the race, Hamilton won from <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a>, taking his 36th race victory.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Bahrain_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Bahrain_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>There were tensions in the <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes team</a> prior to the Grand Prix. After the <a href="/wiki/2015_Chinese_Grand_Prix" title="2015 Chinese Grand Prix">Chinese Grand Prix</a> the week before, <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> had accused his teammate <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> of deliberately backing him into the charging <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> cars during the race, claiming he had "compromised" his race.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton in turn claimed that Rosberg was never close enough to attack him, an assertion that Rosberg rejected, saying he "did try to attack".<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The team reacted by issuing a warning to their drivers, that they would start "managing" them more if they should not be able to race for the best of the team on track.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> After comments made by Hamilton saying that his contract extension negotiations were going slow,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> rumours arose that Hamilton had asked for a clear number one status in the team, a claim denied by both driver<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> and team.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_protests">Political protests</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Bahrain_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Political protests">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>As with previous years there were protests against the race going forward. The protests highlighted the situation of the <a href="/wiki/Shia_Islam" title="Shia Islam">Shi'ite</a> majority in the <a href="/wiki/Sunni_Islam" title="Sunni Islam">Sunni</a>-ruled Arab country, demanding the release of political prisoners.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The protesters tried to use the arrival of Formula One to bring their demands into the public eye. The race came only one week after a report was published by <a href="/wiki/Amnesty_International" title="Amnesty International">Amnesty International</a> about "unabated" human rights violations in Bahrain.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In previous years, Formula One commercial rights executive <a href="/wiki/Bernie_Ecclestone" title="Bernie Ecclestone">Bernie Ecclestone</a> had maintained the view that the political situation should be kept separate from the sport, saying in <a href="/wiki/2013_Bahrain_Grand_Prix" title="2013 Bahrain Grand Prix">2013</a>: "We're not here, or we don't go anywhere, to judge how a country is run".<sup id="cite_ref-guardian1_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guardian1-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> However, in what was viewed as a "180-degree policy reversal", Formula One issued a statement ahead of the 2015 Grand Prix, stating that it "has committed itself to respecting <a href="/wiki/Human_rights_in_Bahrain" title="Human rights in Bahrain">human rights in Bahrain</a> and other countries in which it conducts business".<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Weather">Weather</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Bahrain_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Weather">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The weather for the Bahrain Grand Prix was "typically hot [and] dry",<sup id="cite_ref-weather_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-weather-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> with temperatures on Friday around 36&#160;°C (97&#160;°F) as the first practice session began.<sup id="cite_ref-fp1_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fp1-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> However, lower temperatures were expected for race day, when the race was held at night.<sup id="cite_ref-weather_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-weather-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Tyres">Tyres</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Bahrain_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Tyres">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>As in 2014, <a href="/wiki/Pirelli" title="Pirelli">Pirelli</a> announced they would be supplying teams with the white-banded medium compound tyre as the prime selection and the yellow-banded soft compound as the option selection for the event.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Free_practice">Free practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Bahrain_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Free practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Pastor_Maldonado_Bahrain_2015.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/27/Pastor_Maldonado_Bahrain_2015.jpg/220px-Pastor_Maldonado_Bahrain_2015.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="102" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/27/Pastor_Maldonado_Bahrain_2015.jpg/330px-Pastor_Maldonado_Bahrain_2015.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/27/Pastor_Maldonado_Bahrain_2015.jpg/440px-Pastor_Maldonado_Bahrain_2015.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1800" data-file-height="836" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Pastor_Maldonado_Bahrain_2015.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Pastor_Maldonado" title="Pastor Maldonado">Pastor Maldonado</a> during Friday's second free practice session</div></div></div>
<p>Per the regulations for the 2015 season, three practice sessions were held, two 1.5-hour sessions on Friday and another one-hour session before qualifying on Saturday.<sup id="cite_ref-pracreg_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pracreg-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> of Ferrari were quickest in the first free practice session on Friday afternoon, with Räikkönen the only driver to lap the course in less than 1:38. The Mercedes team focused on long runs, running their cars on high fuel loads to examine tyre wear and were therefore down in 15th and 16th places respectively. Vettel's Ferrari showed problems early in the session, but Vettel was able to run without issues towards the end. <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> spun out in turn one on the start of his first flying lap and was unable to run again as his team tried to change his car's battery. It was however an uplifting session for <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> nonetheless, since Button's teammate <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> finished in seventh place, just seven-tenths away from Räikkönen's fastest time.<sup id="cite_ref-fp1_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fp1-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> After China the week before, <a href="/wiki/Jolyon_Palmer" title="Jolyon Palmer">Jolyon Palmer</a> once again took part in the first practice session for <a href="/wiki/Lotus_F1" title="Lotus F1">Lotus</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Mercedes hit back in the second session on Friday night, with Nico Rosberg leading his teammate Lewis Hamilton by about a tenth of a second. The Ferraris were about half a second behind, with Räikkönen again the faster of the two. It was another dismal session for Jenson Button who had to park his car halfway through the session, though he was able to come back for a couple of laps towards the end of the session. A late red flag came out when Vettel, braking slowly into turn one, was clipped by the <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a> of <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a>, taking off part of his front wing endplate.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Lewis Hamilton topped the time sheets in Saturday's free practice session, with Sebastian Vettel a tenth of a second behind, while Rosberg and Räikkönen completed the top four. Few cars took to the track during the early part of the session due to high temperatures and strong winds. A brief red flag period occurred midway through practice when <a href="/wiki/Daniil_Kvyat" title="Daniil Kvyat">Daniil Kvyat</a> spun out in turn four.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Bahrain_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Qualifying consisted of three parts, 18, 15 and 12 minutes in length respectively, with five drivers eliminated from competing after each of the first two sessions.<sup id="cite_ref-pracreg_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pracreg-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> All teams, including <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> used the softer tyre compound during the first session (Q1). <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a>, who had problems with his car all during practice, was unable to set a time when his car broke down on his out lap. <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> set the fastest time, with <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a> in second. Along with Button, both <a href="/wiki/Manor_Marussia_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Manor Marussia F1">Manor Marussia</a> cars were eliminated in Q1, as well as Daniil Kvyat&#160;– who made a mistake during his final lap&#160;– and <a href="/wiki/Pastor_Maldonado" title="Pastor Maldonado">Pastor Maldonado</a>. Fernando Alonso was the first driver to reach a second qualifying session for McLaren over the course of the season.
</p>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatright" style="width:25%;&#32;;"><style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r887775652">.mw-parser-output .quotebox{background-color:#F9F9F9;border:1px solid #aaa;box-sizing:border-box;padding:10px;font-size:88%;max-width:100%}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft{margin:0.5em 1.4em 0.8em 0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright{margin:0.5em 0 0.8em 1.4em}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.centered{margin:0.5em auto 0.8em auto}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft p,.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright p{font-style:inherit}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-title{background-color:#F9F9F9;text-align:center;font-size:larger;font-weight:bold}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:before{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" “ ";vertical-align:-45%;line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:after{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" ” ";line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .left-aligned{text-align:left}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .right-aligned{text-align:right}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .center-aligned{text-align:center}.mw-parser-output .quotebox cite{display:block;font-style:normal}@media screen and (max-width:360px){.mw-parser-output .quotebox{min-width:100%;margin:0 0 0.8em!important;float:none!important}}</style>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">Neither me nor the team expected that kind of performance. We were expecting that we could get out of Q1 but to then qualify P8 was a big surprise. [...] Definitely fighting for points, I think that is the target [for the race].</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style=""><a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a>, after qualifying eighth.<sup id="cite_ref-hulkenberg_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hulkenberg-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>Hamilton once again set the time in Q2, almost a second ahead of second-placed Kimi Räikkönen, while <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> went faster than Sebastian Vettel in third. Alonso was eliminated in 14th position, ahead of <a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso</a>, who was half a second slower than <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Sainz_Jr." title="Carlos Sainz Jr.">Carlos Sainz Jr.</a>, his teammate. Sergio Pérez joined the two <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a> drivers as the last three cars not to make it into the top 10.
</p><p>All but <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> did their first run in Q3 on used tyres in order to save a set for the race, with Hamilton fastest yet again. When the cars came around for their second and final timed laps, Ricciardo crossed the line first ahead of the Ferrari drivers, with Vettel going faster than teammate Räikkönen. When the two Mercedes came across the line last, Hamilton took his fourth consecutive pole position, while <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> had to settle for third on the grid, not being able to beat Vettel's time.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> The two <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> cars filled the third row, while <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a> was delighted with eighth on the grid.<sup id="cite_ref-hulkenberg_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hulkenberg-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-qualifying">Post-qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Bahrain_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Post-qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Jenson Button was required to apply to the stewards to be allowed to race, because he did not post a lap time during the first part of qualifying. They gave him permission to start based on his satisfactory practice times.<sup id="cite_ref-Button_starts_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Button_starts-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Bahrain_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Felipe_Massa_Bahrain_2015_crop.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0c/Felipe_Massa_Bahrain_2015_crop.jpg/220px-Felipe_Massa_Bahrain_2015_crop.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="112" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0c/Felipe_Massa_Bahrain_2015_crop.jpg/330px-Felipe_Massa_Bahrain_2015_crop.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0c/Felipe_Massa_Bahrain_2015_crop.jpg/440px-Felipe_Massa_Bahrain_2015_crop.jpg 2x" data-file-width="961" data-file-height="488" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Felipe_Massa_Bahrain_2015_crop.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Being unable to start the formation lap, <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> had to start the race from pit lane.</div></div></div>
<p>Prior to the start, McLaren confirmed that they were unable to get Jenson Button's car to the grid, as the data indicated his earlier electrical issues continued.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Felipe Massa's Williams did not get off the line at the start of the formation lap, so he had to start from pit lane. The start lights went on after a long delay, due to Pastor Maldonado being in the wrong grid position.<sup id="cite_ref-racereport_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-racereport-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At the start proper, Lewis Hamilton was able to hold his lead, while his teammate Nico Rosberg tried to attack Sebastian Vettel in second place. This opened up the opportunity for the second Ferrari of Kimi Räikkönen to go around the outside of the Mercedes and take third. Hamilton tried to open up a gap in order to cover his position, should the Ferrari drivers attempt a so-called <i>undercut</i>, a strategy in which a car entered the pit lane early in order to use fresher tyres to get in front of another car without overtaking them on track. Meanwhile, Rosberg took back third from Räikkönen on lap four before doing the same with Vettel on the end of lap nine.
</p><p>As the pit stops started on lap eleven, Massa, <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Nasr" title="Felipe Nasr">Felipe Nasr</a> were the first to come in for new tyres. Hamilton had extended his advantage over Vettel to almost 8 seconds when Vettel made a pit stop&#160;– the first of the front-runners to do so&#160;– on lap 14. The undercut-strategy worked, as Rosberg emerged from the pit lane behind the Ferrari when he made a pit stop one lap later. Hamilton changed tyres on the following lap, emerging close in front of the battling Rosberg and Vettel, with the Mercedes driver taking second position in a <a href="/wiki/Drag_reduction_system" title="Drag reduction system">DRS</a>-aided move on the inside of turn one. He was now close behind teammate Hamilton, but ultimately unable to attack him for the lead. Räikkönen was the last of the top four to pit, on lap 18 and applied a different strategy, as he used the harder medium tyres, while the Mercedes and Vettel were running their middle stint on the softer tyre compound. Even with the harder tyres, he was able to close to the top three.
</p><p>Felipe Massa tried to make up places after his pit lane-start, but was caught in midfield battles. He went into the pit lane simultaneously with Felipe Nasr in front and Pastor Maldonado behind him. A swift stop by the <a href="/wiki/Lotus_F1" title="Lotus F1">Lotus</a> pit crew meant that Maldonado was able to get out ahead and gain two positions. Sauber driver <a href="/wiki/Marcus_Ericsson" title="Marcus Ericsson">Marcus Ericsson</a> was running eighth when he came into the pit lane on lap 26. A 27-second pit stop due to problems with the front left tyre meant that he fell out of reach for a points finish. Carlos Sainz Jr. retired from the race right after exiting pit lane on lap 30, due to an issue with his front left wheel. His Toro Rosso teammate Max Verstappen joined him on the sidelines on lap 34 due to an electrical failure.
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Rosberg_Bahrain_2015.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ab/Rosberg_Bahrain_2015.jpg/220px-Rosberg_Bahrain_2015.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="113" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ab/Rosberg_Bahrain_2015.jpg/330px-Rosberg_Bahrain_2015.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ab/Rosberg_Bahrain_2015.jpg/440px-Rosberg_Bahrain_2015.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1800" data-file-height="923" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Rosberg_Bahrain_2015.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> finished third for <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>On lap 34, both Hamilton and Vettel came in for their second and final planned stop, both equipping the medium tyre set. The undercut worked once more for the Ferrari, as Rosberg emerged behind Vettel one lap later. However, Vettel ran wide on the exit of the final corner on lap 36, letting Rosberg past and damaging his front wing, meaning he had to come into the pit lane for an unscheduled stop on the next lap, leaving him down in fifth position. His teammate Räikkönen made a second pit stop on lap 40, taking the softer and faster tyres, trying to catch the two Mercedes towards the end of the race. Fourteen laps before the finish, Räikkönen was fifteen seconds down on second-placed Rosberg, but closing by more than a second per lap. With the Mercedes in front having to make their way through backmarkers, Räikkönen was able to cut down the gap even faster, reaching Rosberg with three laps of the race to go. Rosberg, suffering from brake overheating over the entire course of the race, outbraked himself at turn one on lap 56, which allowed Räikkönen to take second place from him. The other Mercedes of Hamilton had brake problems as well towards the end, but was able to control the gap to the Ferrari to take his third victory of the season.
</p><p>Four-time world champion Vettel however was unable to overtake the Williams of Valtteri Bottas for fourth. The other Williams of Felipe Massa had applied the undercut well in the early part of the race to recover from his pit lane-start to eighth position, but when his tyres started to degrade he was passed by both Sergio Pérez and Daniil Kvyat and finished tenth, taking one point. The race ended spectacularly when the <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a> of Daniel Ricciardo suffered an engine blow-up on the final straight, just being able to coast his car over the finish line.<sup id="cite_ref-racereport_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-racereport-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Bahrain_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen_Bahrain_2015_cropped.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/05/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen_Bahrain_2015_cropped.jpg/220px-Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen_Bahrain_2015_cropped.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="108" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/05/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen_Bahrain_2015_cropped.jpg/330px-Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen_Bahrain_2015_cropped.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/05/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen_Bahrain_2015_cropped.jpg/440px-Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen_Bahrain_2015_cropped.jpg 2x" data-file-width="860" data-file-height="423" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen_Bahrain_2015_cropped.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> returned to the podium after an 18-month absence and was praised for his performance.</div></div></div>
<p>During the podium interviews conducted by <a href="/wiki/Jackie_Stewart" title="Jackie Stewart">Jackie Stewart</a>, Lewis Hamilton conceded it had been a tough race, saying: "The Ferraris gave us a real good run for our money and out there it was really difficult to look after the tyres in these conditions, behind backmarkers and all sorts, but fortunately I was able to keep the car together, keep the tyres as healthy as possible and pull it through."<sup id="cite_ref-press_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-press-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Kimi Räikkönen expressed disappointment with not being able to win, but said he was "very happy how the team is working" and highlighted the big step forward Ferrari had made over the winter.<sup id="cite_ref-press_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-press-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Nico Rosberg was disappointed about the brake failure costing him second place, but added that he was very happy with the car altogether.<sup id="cite_ref-press_24-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-press-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Sebastian Vettel said he felt that he had let the team down with his mistake that cost him a chance to challenge Rosberg for second position himself, but was happy with "a good recovery and still some decent points".<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After the race, particular praise from the press was directed at Räikkönen, who returned to the podium for the first time since the <a href="/wiki/2013_Korean_Grand_Prix" title="2013 Korean Grand Prix">2013 Korean Grand Prix</a>, 18 months prior. German newspaper <i><a href="/wiki/S%C3%BCddeutsche_Zeitung" title="Süddeutsche Zeitung">Süddeutsche Zeitung</a></i> called his drive a "cool application" for a contract extension after 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After Daniel Ricciardo's engine gave up on the finish straight of the last lap, he was already down to his last engine of the season after only four races. Should he needed to use another one, he would face grid penalties. This added to <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault's</a> problems, after the two Toro Rosso drivers also failed to finish and put more strain on the relationship between Red Bull and their engine provider.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Max Verstappen was quoted calling the engine "a bit of a nightmare", saying it could jeopardise the goal of fifth place for Toro Rosso in the constructors' championship.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Subsequently, team boss <a href="/wiki/Christian_Horner" title="Christian Horner">Christian Horner</a> confirmed that the teams would agree on the plan decided upon earlier that year,<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> to increase the engine limit from four to five, taking some of the pressure off Red Bull and Ricciardo.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
