<p>The <b>2011 Chinese Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>2011 Formula 1 UBS Chinese Grand Prix</b>)<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 17 April 2011 at the <a href="/wiki/Shanghai_International_Circuit" title="Shanghai International Circuit">Shanghai International Circuit</a> in <a href="/wiki/Shanghai" title="Shanghai">Shanghai</a>, <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China" class="mw-redirect" title="People&#39;s Republic of China">People's Republic of China</a>. It was the third round of the <a href="/wiki/2011_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2011 Formula One season">2011 Formula One season</a>. The 56-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> after starting from third on the grid, also becoming the first ever multiple winner of the Chinese Grand Prix. <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> finished second in a <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a>, having started from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>, and teammate <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> completed the podium, in third place having started eighteenth.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As a consequence of the race, Vettel's lead in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">World Drivers' Championship</a> was cut to 21&#160;points by Hamilton, who moved into second place in the championship. Hamilton's McLaren teammate <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a>, who finished fourth in China, fell 9&#160;points behind Hamilton in third, a single point ahead of Webber. In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">World Constructors' Championship</a>, Red Bull's championship lead was also cut from 24&#160;points to 20 by McLaren, with <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> a further 35&#160;points behind in third position after drivers <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> finished in sixth and seventh places respectively.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Chinese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Chinese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Heading into the race, <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> led the Championship on the maximum 50 points after winning the first two races. <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> was in second place in the standings after a 2nd and a 6th that put him on 26 points - just over half Vettel's total, and almost a race win behind. The other <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> of <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> and yet to reach the podium, <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a>, were third and fourth respectively, both on 22 points. <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> was fifth with 20 points, followed by <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Heidfeld</a> on 17, <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Massa</a> on 16 and <a href="/wiki/Vitaly_Petrov" title="Vitaly Petrov">Petrov</a> on 15. In both races thus far in <a href="/wiki/2011_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2011 Formula One season">2011</a> the podium had featured Vettel as the winner, a McLaren in second place and a <a href="/wiki/Lotus_Renault_GP" class="mw-redirect" title="Lotus Renault GP">Renault</a> in third.
</p><p>Tyre supplier <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_tyres" title="Formula One tyres">Pirelli</a> brought its silver-banded hard compound tyre as the harder "prime" tyre and the yellow-banded soft compound as the softer "option" compound. This was the same tyre selection that Bridgestone had chosen bring to the Chinese Grand Prix for the previous year.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the Constructors' table, <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a> led with 72 points, one and a half times <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>'s score (48), and double <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari's</a> (36). Despite having two podiums, Renault were still behind Ferrari; but only just - with 30 points - and were putting up a good fight for the time being. <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a> were fifth in the standings, with 6 points, <a href="/wiki/Toro_Rosso" class="mw-redirect" title="Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso</a> and <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a> had 4, and <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> had 2. By this point of the season, <a href="/wiki/Williams_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams F1">Williams</a> were yet to finish a race.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Chinese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Sebastian Vettel took a comfortable pole position in qualifying, with the second placed Jenson Button 0.7 seconds behind him and Button's McLaren teammate Lewis Hamilton in third. Mark Webber had a torrid session, eliminated in the first part of qualifying after electrical problems in the earlier free practice, placing him 18th on the starting grid.<sup id="cite_ref-bbc_quali_report_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc_quali_report-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Nico Rosberg in his Mercedes found himself fourth, Rosberg also recorded the fastest time of the first part of the qualifying session. Behind him were the Ferraris of Alonso and Massa, who had been unable to keep up with the pace of Red Bull or McLaren, Alonso recording a time almost a second off Vettel's pole position lap. Vitaly Petrov recorded a time in part two that got him through to the final part of qualifying, however, his car developed problems and stopped on the track, meaning he was unable to take part in the final session and would take the tenth place on the grid.<sup id="cite_ref-bbc_quali_report_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc_quali_report-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div style="clear:both;"></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Chinese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Lewis Hamilton faced a stressful situation just minutes before the start of the race; a fuel problem with his car meant that if he had started his journey to the grid just 35 seconds later, he would have had to start from the pit-lane.<sup id="cite_ref-guardian_race_report_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guardian_race_report-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton however, went on to a thrilling  three-stop race, overtaking his two-stopping rival and World Drivers' Championship leader Sebastian Vettel on lap 52 of the 56 lap race.<sup id="cite_ref-guardian_race_report_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guardian_race_report-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Vettel had had a poor start to his race, being beaten on the first lap by both McLarens, with Jenson Button initially holding the upper hand, but his teammate Hamilton putting in a series of fastest laps that would ultimately give him victory to Button's fourth-place finish.<sup id="cite_ref-bbc_race_as_it_happened_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc_race_as_it_happened-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Also Hamilton performed an overtake on his teammate in Turn 1, about the midpoint of the race to take second place.
</p><p>Mark Webber drove a spectacular race from 18th on the starting grid to bring his Red Bull to the chequered flag in 3rd position, beating Jenson Button for the final podium place on only the last lap.<sup id="cite_ref-bbc_race_as_it_happened_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc_race_as_it_happened-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Button had made a terrible mistake on the 15th lap when bringing his car into the pits, stopping in the Red Bull pit before being waved off by the Red Bull mechanics into his own McLaren pit one place ahead. The mistake cost Button time, as it did for Sebastian Vettel who was also coming into the pits and had to wait behind Button for him to realise his error; although Button entered the pits in the lead, but exited behind Vettel.<sup id="cite_ref-guardian_race_report_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guardian_race_report-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Jaime Alguersuari lost a wheel on his exit from the pits on lap 10, forcing his retirement from the race.<sup id="cite_ref-bbc_race_as_it_happened_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc_race_as_it_happened-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> He was the race's only retirement, meaning that this race set the record for the most finishers (23). The record was beaten later in the season, in <a href="/wiki/2011_European_Grand_Prix" title="2011 European Grand Prix">Valencia</a>, where all 24 starters finished the race.
</p><p>The race cut Sebastian Vettel's championship lead to 21 points from second placed Lewis Hamilton, with Red Bull constructors lead to McLaren falling to 20 points. Hamilton was ecstatic after his victory, saying "It was one of the best races I've experienced.
