<p>The <b>2009 Malaysian Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>XI Petronas Malaysian Grand Prix</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 5 April 2009 at the <a href="/wiki/Sepang_International_Circuit" title="Sepang International Circuit">Sepang International Circuit</a> in <a href="/wiki/Sepang" class="mw-redirect" title="Sepang">Sepang</a>, <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a>. It was the second race of the <a href="/wiki/2009_FIA_Formula_One_World_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="2009 FIA Formula One World Championship">2009 FIA Formula One World Championship</a>. The race was due to be contested over 56&#160;laps, but was stopped after 31&#160;laps due to torrential rain. <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a>, driving for the <a href="/wiki/Brawn_GP" title="Brawn GP">Brawn GP</a> team, was declared the winner, having started from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>. <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> was classified second for <a href="/wiki/BMW_in_Formula_One#BMW_Sauber" title="BMW in Formula One">BMW Sauber</a> with <a href="/wiki/Timo_Glock" title="Timo Glock">Timo Glock</a> third for <a href="/wiki/Toyota_Racing_(Formula_One_team)" title="Toyota Racing (Formula One team)">Toyota</a>.
</p><p>As the race did not reach the required 75% distance (42 laps) for full points to be awarded, half-points were given instead, for only the fifth time in Formula One history and the first since the <a href="/wiki/1991_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="1991 Australian Grand Prix">1991 Australian Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-reutersbwgp_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-reutersbwgp-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-timesonline_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-timesonline-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The race distance of 171.833&#160;km (106.772&#160;mi) was the fifth-shortest ever covered in a World Championship Grand Prix.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Brawn GP became only the second constructor to win their first two World Championship Grands Prix since <a href="/wiki/Alfa_Romeo_in_Formula_One" title="Alfa Romeo in Formula One">Alfa Romeo</a> won the first two ever, in <a href="/wiki/1950_Formula_One_season" title="1950 Formula One season">1950</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcclassy_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcclassy-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2009_Malaysian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2009_Malaysian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Hamilton_2009_Malaysian_GP_1.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bb/Hamilton_2009_Malaysian_GP_1.jpg/220px-Hamilton_2009_Malaysian_GP_1.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="129" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bb/Hamilton_2009_Malaysian_GP_1.jpg/330px-Hamilton_2009_Malaysian_GP_1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bb/Hamilton_2009_Malaysian_GP_1.jpg/440px-Hamilton_2009_Malaysian_GP_1.jpg 2x" data-file-width="973" data-file-height="569" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Hamilton_2009_Malaysian_GP_1.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>On the Thursday before the race, Lewis Hamilton was disqualified from the preceding <a href="/wiki/2009_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="2009 Australian Grand Prix">Australian Grand Prix</a> for misleading the stewards.</div></div></div>
<p>After winning the season-opening <a href="/wiki/2009_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="2009 Australian Grand Prix">Australian Grand Prix</a> held one week earlier, <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> led the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a> by two <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Championship_points_scoring_systems" title="List of Formula One World Championship points scoring systems">points</a> from his teammate <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> and by four points from <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a>. Trulli's teammate <a href="/wiki/Timo_Glock" title="Timo Glock">Timo Glock</a> was fourth and <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> completed the top five.<sup id="cite_ref-championship_classification_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-championship_classification-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Brawn_GP" title="Brawn GP">Brawn GP</a> led the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a> by 7 points from <a href="/wiki/Toyota_Racing_(Formula_One_team)" title="Toyota Racing (Formula One team)">Toyota</a> and by 14 points from <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>. <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> and <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso</a> were fourth and fifth.<sup id="cite_ref-championship_classification_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-championship_classification-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The race start time was moved forward two hours, from 17:00 <a href="/wiki/Malaysian_Standard_Time" class="mw-redirect" title="Malaysian Standard Time">local time</a> (09:00 <a href="/wiki/Coordinated_Universal_Time" title="Coordinated Universal Time">UTC</a>) to 15:00 local time (07:00 UTC). However, the organisers turned down the possibility of holding a night race, in line with the <a href="/wiki/2008_Singapore_Grand_Prix" title="2008 Singapore Grand Prix">2008 Singapore Grand Prix</a>, for budgetary reasons.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Three practice sessions were held before the race; the first was held on Friday morning and the second on Friday afternoon. Both sessions lasted 1&#160;hour and 30&#160;minutes with weather conditions dry throughout. The third session was held on Saturday morning and lasted an&#160;hour, and was also dry throughout.<sup id="cite_ref-sporting_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sporting-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
<a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kazuki_Nakajima" title="Kazuki Nakajima">Kazuki Nakajima</a> secured a <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> one-two in the first session with times of 1:36.260 and 1:36.305 minutes respectively. In the second session, <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> went quickest with times of 1:35.707 and 1:35.832 minutes. After 18 laps, Räikkönen's cockpit began to billow out smoke, and though Ferrari did not give an official statement, there are reports that the car's <a href="/wiki/Kinetic_energy_recovery_system" title="Kinetic energy recovery system">kinetic energy recovery system</a> (KERS) had overheated.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> took <a href="/wiki/Brawn_GP" title="Brawn GP">Brawn</a>'s second consecutive pole ahead of <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Toyota_TF109" title="Toyota TF109">Toyota</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> qualified fourth in the other <a href="/wiki/Brawn_BGP_001" title="Brawn BGP 001">Brawn</a> (third when Vettel's penalty was taken into consideration), but was demoted five places to eighth after a gearbox change.<sup id="cite_ref-Barrichello_penalty_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Barrichello_penalty-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> An error in strategy meant that Massa failed to make it through Q1. In an interview to <a href="/wiki/Rede_Globo" title="Rede Globo">Rede Globo</a>, the driver said that he and the team thought the initial time posted was enough to qualify for the second session, and refrained from recording additional times to spare the car's engine. However, this was not the case, and Massa was left in 16th place.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2009_Malaysian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:2009_Malaysian_Grand_Prix_start_(cropped).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a6/2009_Malaysian_Grand_Prix_start_%28cropped%29.jpg/220px-2009_Malaysian_Grand_Prix_start_%28cropped%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a6/2009_Malaysian_Grand_Prix_start_%28cropped%29.jpg/330px-2009_Malaysian_Grand_Prix_start_%28cropped%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a6/2009_Malaysian_Grand_Prix_start_%28cropped%29.jpg/440px-2009_Malaysian_Grand_Prix_start_%28cropped%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="428" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:2009_Malaysian_Grand_Prix_start_(cropped).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> took <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> on the starting grid, but <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> (in fourth place) made the best start and led for the first time in his career, into the first corner.</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Kubica_2009_Malaysian_GP_1.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/af/Kubica_2009_Malaysian_GP_1.jpg/220px-Kubica_2009_Malaysian_GP_1.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="113" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/af/Kubica_2009_Malaysian_GP_1.jpg/330px-Kubica_2009_Malaysian_GP_1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/af/Kubica_2009_Malaysian_GP_1.jpg/440px-Kubica_2009_Malaysian_GP_1.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1200" data-file-height="617" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Kubica_2009_Malaysian_GP_1.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a> retired on the second lap when his engine failed.</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:2009_Malaysian_Grand_Prix_last_lap.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4d/2009_Malaysian_Grand_Prix_last_lap.jpg/220px-2009_Malaysian_Grand_Prix_last_lap.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4d/2009_Malaysian_Grand_Prix_last_lap.jpg/330px-2009_Malaysian_Grand_Prix_last_lap.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4d/2009_Malaysian_Grand_Prix_last_lap.jpg/440px-2009_Malaysian_Grand_Prix_last_lap.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4272" data-file-height="2848" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:2009_Malaysian_Grand_Prix_last_lap.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Photograph showing the correct positions of <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> and <a href="/wiki/Timo_Glock" title="Timo Glock">Timo Glock</a> on the last completed lap</div></div></div>
<p>Off the line, there was a clean getaway by all drivers, apart from <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a>, who was forced to retire from engine problems after the first lap. Williams' <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> moved from fourth on the grid to lead into the first corner, followed by <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>, who had used his KERS system to good effect to move up from tenth; <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> made a poor start in his Brawn and was down to fourth. Heading into turn five, McLaren's <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> ran wide onto the track's 'marbles' (fragments of degraded tyre rubber), and spun into early retirement.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:1_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After a slow start, Button caught and passed the heavier Renault of Alonso at turn thirteen for third place and set off after Trulli. Rosberg and Trulli stopped for fuel earlier than Button, who was able to pass them during the first round of <a href="/wiki/Pit_stop" title="Pit stop">pit stops</a> and take the lead. Evidence of rain was barely noticed, but Kimi Räikkönen pitted to switch from dry to full wet tyres. However, his gamble did not pay off, as rain did not come as early as predicted, and he was forced to slow down significantly to delay the rapid wear of the wet tyres. (Without standing water on the track, wet tyres will not maintain structural integrity at high speeds, and will rapidly degrade over the course of a few laps.)<sup id="cite_ref-:0_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:1_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>By lap 19, rain had started to fall and most of the drivers pitted for wet tyres. However, at this stage there was no standing water on the track, and so the wet tyres started to wear out very quickly. <a href="/wiki/Timo_Glock" title="Timo Glock">Timo Glock</a> had been using intermediate tyres which were better suited to the conditions and moved rapidly up to third place. The other drivers followed this example and switched to intermediate tyres as well, which the majority of the field stayed on until lap 31 when the downpour finally reached the whole track, and drivers pitted for wet tyres once again.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_16-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:1_17-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> 
The conditions were proven to be so treacherous that Sebastien Buemi and Sebastian Vettel (who was in 8th position) had both spun out into retirement by lap 31 despite them being on the wet tyres right before the race was stopped.
</p><p>Due to the torrential rain, the race was stopped on the 33rd lap<sup id="cite_ref-timesonline_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-timesonline-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and the results were taken from the classification at the end of lap 31 (the penultimate fully completed in accordance with sporting regulation 42.8<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>). Half-points were subsequently awarded to the top eight. Button took his second victory of the season and five points, and it was the first time he had won back to back victories and his third career victory. The victory also meant that Brawn GP became the first team since <a href="/wiki/Alfa_Romeo_in_motorsport" title="Alfa Romeo in motorsport">Alfa Romeo</a> in <a href="/wiki/1950_Formula_One_season" title="1950 Formula One season">1950</a> to win their first two Grands Prix.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcclassy_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcclassy-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Nick Heidfeld was classified second ahead of Toyota's Timo Glock.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_17-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Heidfeld was the first driver to take a podium position with a KERS-equipped car.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2009_Malaysian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:2009_Malaysia_Red_Flag_(cropped).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/98/2009_Malaysia_Red_Flag_%28cropped%29.jpg/220px-2009_Malaysia_Red_Flag_%28cropped%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="144" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/98/2009_Malaysia_Red_Flag_%28cropped%29.jpg/330px-2009_Malaysia_Red_Flag_%28cropped%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/98/2009_Malaysia_Red_Flag_%28cropped%29.jpg/440px-2009_Malaysia_Red_Flag_%28cropped%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="885" data-file-height="578" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:2009_Malaysia_Red_Flag_(cropped).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Trulli, Barrichello, Hamilton and Webber stop their cars on the start-finish straight following the decision to red-flag the race.</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:2009_Malaysian_Grand_Prix_disgruntled_fans.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3f/2009_Malaysian_Grand_Prix_disgruntled_fans.jpg/220px-2009_Malaysian_Grand_Prix_disgruntled_fans.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3f/2009_Malaysian_Grand_Prix_disgruntled_fans.jpg/330px-2009_Malaysian_Grand_Prix_disgruntled_fans.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3f/2009_Malaysian_Grand_Prix_disgruntled_fans.jpg/440px-2009_Malaysian_Grand_Prix_disgruntled_fans.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4272" data-file-height="2848" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:2009_Malaysian_Grand_Prix_disgruntled_fans.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Crowd members leaving the stands after the race was abandoned.</div></div></div>
<p>The drivers generally backed the decision to abandon the race, citing diminishing visibility (due to the later starting time) as well as the heavy rain.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> 
</p>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r960796168">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style><blockquote class="templatequote"><p><i>"The visibility is nothing, [we] could have a serious accident if we restart."</i> — <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> before the race was officially called off<sup id="cite_ref-bbcclassy_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcclassy-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r960796168"/><blockquote class="templatequote"><p><i>"It was impossible to drive out there, it was very, very dangerous. It's the most dangerous conditions I have ever raced in,"</i> — <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a></p></blockquote>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r960796168"/><blockquote class="templatequote"><p><i>"It was way too wet out there and the decision to call it off was correct. I would obviously love to have the 10 points, but this is the best we could have done, I think, and realistically it was the right thing to do. I'm sure some people will say 'we didn't see the whole race and it's disappointing' but you have to think about the safety sometimes. When the safety car is pulling away at 20 seconds a lap, you know that it's too wet for an F1 car."</i> — <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a></p></blockquote>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r960796168"/><blockquote class="templatequote"><p><i>"It's dark now at seven o'clock so it was the right call not to make the re-start,"</i> — <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> (a director of the <a href="/wiki/Grand_Prix_Drivers%27_Association" title="Grand Prix Drivers&#39; Association">Grand Prix Drivers' Association</a>)</p></blockquote>
<p>Officials in Malaysia also confirmed that they would review the start time of the <a href="/wiki/2010_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2010 Formula One World Championship">2010</a> <a href="/wiki/2010_Malaysian_Grand_Prix" title="2010 Malaysian Grand Prix">Malaysian Grand Prix</a>. <a href="/wiki/Mokhzani_Mahathir" title="Mokhzani Mahathir">Mokhzani Mahathir</a>, chairman of the Sepang International Circuit, said he consulted <a href="/wiki/Bernie_Ecclestone" title="Bernie Ecclestone">Bernie Ecclestone</a> after the race and they had agreed to look at the timings. However, Ecclestone revealed he had no qualms about the schedule, stating:
</p>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r960796168"/><blockquote class="templatequote"><p><i>"I just have regrets about the rain, I don't see anything wrong with the start time, we just didn't know about the rain."</i><sup id="cite_ref-bbcmalaysiatime_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcmalaysiatime-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>
<p>Mokhzani suggested the possibility of implementing a lighting system (similar to that used in the <a href="/wiki/Singapore_Grand_Prix" title="Singapore Grand Prix">Singapore Grand Prix</a>) to illuminate the circuit in future races.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcmalaysiatime_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcmalaysiatime-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
