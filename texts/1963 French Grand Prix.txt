<p>The <b>1963 French Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at <a href="/wiki/Reims-Gueux" title="Reims-Gueux">Reims</a> on 30 June 1963. It was race 4 of 10 in both the <a href="/wiki/1963_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1963 World Championship of Drivers">1963 World Championship of Drivers</a> and the <a href="/wiki/1963_International_Cup_for_Formula_One_Manufacturers" class="mw-redirect" title="1963 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers">1963 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers</a>. The race was won by <a href="/wiki/Jim_Clark" title="Jim Clark">Jim Clark</a> driving a <a href="/wiki/Lotus_25" title="Lotus 25">Lotus 25</a>-<a href="/wiki/Coventry_Climax" title="Coventry Climax">Climax</a> 1.5 litre <a href="/wiki/V8_engine" title="V8 engine">V8</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_report">Race report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1963_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Race report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Jim_Clark" title="Jim Clark">Jim Clark</a> took the lead at the start from <a href="/wiki/Richie_Ginther" title="Richie Ginther">Richie Ginther</a> in the <a href="/wiki/British_Racing_Motors" title="British Racing Motors">BRM</a>. All <a href="/wiki/Graham_Hill" title="Graham Hill">Graham Hill</a>'s hard work in qualifying second despite mechanical problems in practice came to nothing when his engine died on the grid and his car had to be push started. The subsequent one-minute penalty dropped him well back. Clark led dominantly, his lead being extended when a stone pierced Ginther's radiator, forcing him into the pits. <a href="/wiki/Jack_Brabham" title="Jack Brabham">Jack Brabham</a> took second place after a strong fight with <a href="/wiki/Trevor_Taylor_(racing_driver)" title="Trevor Taylor (racing driver)">Trevor Taylor</a>, who also suffered mechanical problems.
</p><p>Brabham then began to gain significantly on Clark as the Scot's <a href="/wiki/Coventry_Climax" title="Coventry Climax">Climax</a> engine started to splutter, however this proved to be a sporadic fault and he had enough of a lead to maintain the position. Brabham himself was delayed when an ignition lead came loose,<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> handing second and third to <a href="/wiki/Tony_Maggs" title="Tony Maggs">Tony Maggs</a> and a delighted Hill. Clark was over a minute ahead of them after yet another start-to-finish victory.
</p>
