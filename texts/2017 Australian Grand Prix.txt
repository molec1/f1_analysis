<p>The <b>2017 Australian Grand Prix</b> (formally known as the <b>2017 Formula 1 Rolex Australian Grand Prix</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> that took place on 26 March 2017 in <a href="/wiki/Melbourne" title="Melbourne">Melbourne</a>. The race was contested over fifty-seven laps of the <a href="/wiki/Melbourne_Grand_Prix_Circuit" class="mw-redirect" title="Melbourne Grand Prix Circuit">Melbourne Grand Prix Circuit</a> and was the first round of the <a href="/wiki/2017_FIA_Formula_One_World_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="2017 FIA Formula One World Championship">2017 FIA Formula One World Championship</a>. The race marked the 82nd race in the combined history of the <a href="/wiki/Australian_Grand_Prix" title="Australian Grand Prix">Australian Grand Prix</a> – which dates back to the <a href="/wiki/1928_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="1928 Australian Grand Prix">100 Miles Road Race of 1928</a> – and the 22nd time the event was held at the Melbourne Grand Prix Circuit. The reigning <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">World Champion</a> <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> was the winner of the <a href="/wiki/2016_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="2016 Australian Grand Prix">2016 race</a>, but he did not defend his win as he retired from the sport after the 2016 season.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:1_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> started the race from pole, matching <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a>'s record of six pole positions in Australia.<sup id="cite_ref-quali_result_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-quali_result-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> won the race, which was his and <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari's</a> first win since the <a href="/wiki/2015_Singapore_Grand_Prix" title="2015 Singapore Grand Prix">2015 Singapore Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-race_result_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-race_result-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton finished second, with <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a> completing the podium in third on his début for Mercedes.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The race was also Ferrari's first win at the track since the <a href="/wiki/2007_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="2007 Australian Grand Prix">2007</a> edition.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2017_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Circuit_modifications">Circuit modifications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2017_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Circuit modifications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>With the technical regulations undergoing a revision for the 2017 season,<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> the FIA requested that every circuit on the calendar undergo revisions to update safety features.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Organisers of the race introduced Tecpro barriers to the run-off areas at the fastest points of the circuit, and re-profiled tyre barriers.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Driver_changes">Driver changes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2017_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Driver changes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Before the beginning of the third free practice session, <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a> announced that <a href="/wiki/Pascal_Wehrlein" title="Pascal Wehrlein">Pascal Wehrlein</a> would not start the race. Wehrlein had sustained a back injury in an accident at the <a href="/wiki/Race_of_Champions" title="Race of Champions">Race of Champions</a> during the winter pre-season. Although Wehrlein was certified as fit to take part in the Grand Prix ahead of the first practice session, he withdrew as a precaution, citing the increased physical demands of the 2017 specification of cars and the lack of sustained running during pre-season testing as compromising his ability to complete a full race distance. He was replaced by <a href="/wiki/Antonio_Giovinazzi" title="Antonio Giovinazzi">Antonio Giovinazzi</a> for the third session, qualifying and the race.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Lance_Stroll" title="Lance Stroll">Lance Stroll</a> made his Formula One début with <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Giovinazzi and Stroll were the only rookies in the field, with <a href="/wiki/Stoffel_Vandoorne" title="Stoffel Vandoorne">Stoffel Vandoorne</a> at <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> starting his first full season in the sport after a one-off appearance at the <a href="/wiki/2016_Bahrain_Grand_Prix" title="2016 Bahrain Grand Prix">2016 Bahrain Grand Prix</a> and <a href="/wiki/Esteban_Ocon" title="Esteban Ocon">Esteban Ocon</a> at <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a> starting his first full season in the sport after starting the last 9 races of the <a href="/wiki/2016_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2016 Formula One World Championship">2016</a> season.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Tyres">Tyres</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2017_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Tyres">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p><a href="/wiki/Pirelli" title="Pirelli">Pirelli</a> supplied the teams with ultrasoft, supersoft and soft tyres. Usually, teams are allowed to choose an individual selection of thirteen different sets for every driver. However, due to the selection deadline falling before the pre-season test when teams were first able to drive with the new compounds, Pirelli allocated every driver the same sets. These were seven sets of ultrasoft, four sets of supersoft and two sets of soft tyres.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
