<p>The <b>2005 Brazilian Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>XXXIV Grande Prêmio do Brasil</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on at the <a href="/wiki/Aut%C3%B3dromo_Jos%C3%A9_Carlos_Pace" title="Autódromo José Carlos Pace">Autódromo José Carlos Pace</a> in <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo_(city)" class="mw-redirect" title="São Paulo (city)">São Paulo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a> on 25 September 2005. The 71-lap race was the seventeenth round of the <a href="/wiki/2005_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2005 Formula One season">2005 Formula One season</a>. The race was won by <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a>, who took the final race victory of his career, ahead of his teammate, <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a>, who finished second. <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> became the Drivers' Champion for the first time after he finished the race in third place. Only he and Räikkönen had entered the race within mathematical contention of winning the title, and Alonso's podium ensured that he did with two rounds remaining. Alonso also qualified in pole position for the race, but the fastest lap went to Räikkönen.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2005_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2005_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As <a href="/wiki/Takuma_Sato" title="Takuma Sato">Takuma Sato</a> was subject to a ten-place grid penalty given for the accident in the <a href="/wiki/2005_Belgian_Grand_Prix#Notes" title="2005 Belgian Grand Prix">Belgian Grand Prix</a> where he crashed into <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>, Sato did not attempt a qualifying lap. <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> also had a ten-place penalty, in his case the result of an <a href="/wiki/2005_Formula_One_season#Engine_life" class="mw-redirect" title="2005 Formula One season">engine change</a> before qualifying. The Italian started from 18th on the grid.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2005_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a> won the race ahead of teammate <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a>; <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>'s first 1–2 finish since the <a href="/wiki/2000_Austrian_Grand_Prix" title="2000 Austrian Grand Prix">2000 Austrian Grand Prix</a>. <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> finished 3rd and thus became World Champion for the first time, at the time the youngest ever champion at 24 years and 59 days surpassing <a href="/wiki/Emerson_Fittipaldi" title="Emerson Fittipaldi">Emerson Fittipaldi</a>'s record of 25 years and 273 days set in <a href="/wiki/1972_Formula_One_season" title="1972 Formula One season">1972</a>. <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a> was forced to start from pit lane as a penalty for infringement of parc ferme regulations. After getting involved in an accident at the start of the race, Mark Webber was able to rejoin, over 20 laps behind the leaders and do some laps, sufficient to position himself fourth in the official qualifying order for the <a href="/wiki/2005_Japanese_Grand_Prix" title="2005 Japanese Grand Prix">subsequent Grand Prix at Suzuka</a>. The result of the Grand Prix marked the only point during the season when McLaren had more championship points than Renault. Due to a driveshaft failure, this was <a href="/wiki/Tiago_Monteiro" title="Tiago Monteiro">Tiago Monteiro</a>'s only retirement of the 2005 season.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Classification">Classification</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2005_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Classification">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Commentators have judged <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault's</a> qualifying performance as evidence that their "conservative phase" was over. Renault's <a href="/wiki/Pat_Symonds" title="Pat Symonds">Pat Symonds</a> had said that the team was not aiming to settle for a simple podium finish, rather they were aiming to win. <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC's</a> Maurice Hamilton said that <i>"the thought that Fernando Alonso might cruise to the Championship.....was dispelled in the most convincing fashion"</i>. <a href="/wiki/Team_McLaren" class="mw-redirect" title="Team McLaren">McLaren</a> CEO <a href="/wiki/Ron_Dennis" title="Ron Dennis">Ron Dennis</a> remained confident of his team's race strategy given <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a>'s strong second position, despite a major error in the qualifying lap of Kimi Räikkönen.
</p>
