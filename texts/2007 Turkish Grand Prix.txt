<p>The <b>2007 Turkish Grand Prix</b> was the twelfth race of the <a href="/wiki/2007_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2007 Formula One season">2007 Formula One season</a>. Won by <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a>, it was held on 26 August 2007 at the <a href="/wiki/Istanbul_Park" class="mw-redirect" title="Istanbul Park">Istanbul Park</a> in <a href="/wiki/Tuzla,_Istanbul" title="Tuzla, Istanbul">Tuzla</a>. It was the third time a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> race had been held here. The two previous winners of the race were <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> in <a href="/wiki/2005_Turkish_Grand_Prix" title="2005 Turkish Grand Prix">2005</a> and Massa in <a href="/wiki/2006_Turkish_Grand_Prix" title="2006 Turkish Grand Prix">2006</a>, both of whom race for <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> in 2007.
</p><p>It came following considerable controversy after the 2006 event, where the organisers were fined $5,000,000 for <a href="/wiki/Political_bias" title="Political bias">political bias</a>, due to their choice of <a href="/wiki/Mehmet_Ali_Talat" title="Mehmet Ali Talat">Mehmet Ali Talat</a> to present the winner's trophy. Talat is President of the <a href="/wiki/Turkish_Republic_of_Northern_Cyprus" class="mw-redirect" title="Turkish Republic of Northern Cyprus">Turkish Cypriot state</a>, which is not an internationally recognised government. This was seen by the <a href="/wiki/FIA" class="mw-redirect" title="FIA">governing body</a> of <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> as having compromised their neutrality.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> came into the race with a 7-point lead over <a href="/wiki/McLaren_(racing)" class="mw-redirect" title="McLaren (racing)">McLaren</a> teammate <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> after Hamilton's controversial victory in the <a href="/wiki/2007_Hungarian_Grand_Prix" title="2007 Hungarian Grand Prix">2007 Hungarian Grand Prix</a>. McLaren led <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> in the constructors' championship by 19 points despite not being allowed to score points in Hungary. However, the Ferrari had looked strong in recent weeks, and their two cars ultimately dominated the race from start to finish, Massa and Räikkönen taking first and second places respectively.   A late puncture dropped Hamilton to fifth place and cut his championship lead over Alonso to five points.
</p><p>During the press conference following the race, Massa commented that "the Istanbul Park was the track where he made his career turn-around, and finally began winning races." He also praised the track as well as the city.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2007_Turkish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2007_Turkish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> led from pole from teammate <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> who had jumped <a href="/wiki/McLaren_(racing)" class="mw-redirect" title="McLaren (racing)">McLaren</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> off the grid to take second place. <a href="/wiki/2006_Formula_One_Season" class="mw-redirect" title="2006 Formula One Season">Reigning World Champion</a> <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>'s start from fourth was even worse than teammate Hamilton's as he fell behind both <a href="/wiki/BMW_Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="BMW Sauber">BMW Saubers</a> of <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> into sixth. However, BMW's early stopping strategy led to Alonso taking both places back after the first round of pit-stops.
At the first round of stops it seemed that Räikkönen could pass Massa if he stopped later, as he did at the <a href="/wiki/2007_French_Grand_Prix" title="2007 French Grand Prix">French Grand Prix</a>. However, Massa stopped after Räikkönen to retain the lead. Hamilton stopped later than both Ferraris and caught several seconds up to them, but could not pass Räikkönen for second, as he gradually fell away. Meanwhile, Kubica's strategy had not only dropped him behind Heidfeld and Alonso, but <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault's</a> <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> too.
Massa stopped later than Räikkönen again at the second pit stop, and therefore retained the lead as Ferrari called the race between teammates off at this point. Hamilton may have emerged ahead of Räikkönen, but a puncture in his <a href="/wiki/Bridgestone" title="Bridgestone">front tyre</a> dropped him to fifth behind Heidfeld and Alonso. Damage to his front wing led to Kovalainen catching him, but the <a href="/wiki/Finland" title="Finland">Finn</a> was unable to pass Hamilton. Kubica lost a place to <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> through strategy finishing eighth.
Massa eventually won his second <a href="/wiki/2006_Turkish_Grand_Prix" title="2006 Turkish Grand Prix">consecutive</a> Turkish Grand Prix, and his fifth Grand Prix win overall, all of which came from pole position. He had secured his eighth pole in qualifying.
<a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> of <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a> was the only non-classified car, with a hydraulic failure. All the rest were classified including Adrian Sutil who retired with a fuel pressure problem. <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Scuderia Toro Rosso</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Vitantonio_Liuzzi" title="Vitantonio Liuzzi">Vitantonio Liuzzi</a> ended a run of 9 consecutive retirements stretching back to the <a href="/wiki/2007_Bahrain_Grand_Prix" title="2007 Bahrain Grand Prix">Bahrain Grand Prix</a> in April.
</p>
