<p>The <b>1986 Austrian Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held at <a href="/wiki/%C3%96sterreichring" class="mw-redirect" title="Österreichring">Österreichring</a> on 17 August 1986. It was the twelfth race of the <a href="/wiki/1986_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1986 Formula One World Championship">1986 Formula One World Championship</a>.
</p><p>The 52-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/2" title="McLaren MP4/2">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Techniques_d%27Avant_Garde" title="Techniques d&#39;Avant Garde">TAG</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_F1/86" title="Ferrari F1/86">Ferrari</a> drivers <a href="/wiki/Michele_Alboreto" title="Michele Alboreto">Michele Alboreto</a> and <a href="/wiki/Stefan_Johansson" title="Stefan Johansson">Stefan Johansson</a> second and third respectively. With Drivers' Championship challengers <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> all retiring, Prost moved into second place in the Championship, two points behind Mansell.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1986_Austrian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualification">Qualification</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1986_Austrian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Qualification">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Qualifying saw several surprises as the <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a>-<a href="/wiki/BMW_in_Formula_One" title="BMW in Formula One">BMWs</a> of <a href="/wiki/Teo_Fabi" title="Teo Fabi">Teo Fabi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> filled the front row, Fabi just under 0.2 seconds ahead, while <a href="/wiki/Riccardo_Patrese" title="Riccardo Patrese">Riccardo Patrese</a> took fourth in his <a href="/wiki/Brabham" title="Brabham">Brabham</a> despite a crash, just behind <a href="/wiki/Keke_Rosberg" title="Keke Rosberg">Keke Rosberg</a>'s <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>. The four Drivers' Championship challengers occupied fifth to eighth in the order of Alain Prost's McLaren, the two <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> of Nigel Mansell and Nelson Piquet, and Ayrton Senna's <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Lotus</a>. Completing the top ten were Michele Alboreto's Ferrari and <a href="/wiki/Derek_Warwick" title="Derek Warwick">Derek Warwick</a> in the second Brabham.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1986_Austrian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Warwick was a non-starter in bizarre circumstances. After the Englishman's car was repaired following a gearbox failure in the morning warm-up, the two Brabhams went to the grid, Patrese in the spare car following his qualifying crash. Then, as Patrese took his place on the grid, his own gearbox broke. As the Italian driver was six places ahead of Warwick, the decision was made on the grid to hand him Warwick's car for the rest of the race. Warwick later admitted that team owner <a href="/wiki/Bernie_Ecclestone" title="Bernie Ecclestone">Bernie Ecclestone</a> had to physically drag him from the car as he refused to hand it over.
</p><p>When the race got underway, local driver Berger took the lead from Fabi, while Rosberg and Patrese made slow starts and were overtaken by Prost, Mansell and Piquet. Patrese retired after two laps with an engine failure, while the same fate befell Senna after 13 laps. On lap 17, Fabi overtook Berger at the Bosch-Kurve, only for his own engine to fail seconds later. Mansell moved into second place when Prost made a pit stop for tyres, then the lead shortly afterwards when Berger pitted with a battery problem. Prost took the lead when Mansell made his own pit stop, before both Williams retired within three laps of each other, Piquet with an overheating engine and Mansell with a broken driveshaft. This left Prost around half a minute clear of team-mate Rosberg, with Alboreto up to third. Five laps from the end, Rosberg suffered an electrical failure, leaving Prost to win by a full lap from Alboreto with a further lap back to the second Ferrari of Stefan Johansson in third; the top six was completed by the two <a href="/wiki/Haas_Lola" title="Haas Lola">Haas Lolas</a> of <a href="/wiki/Alan_Jones_(racing_driver)" title="Alan Jones (racing driver)">Alan Jones</a> and <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Tambay" title="Patrick Tambay">Patrick Tambay</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Arrows_Grand_Prix_International" title="Arrows Grand Prix International">Arrows</a> of <a href="/wiki/Christian_Danner" title="Christian Danner">Christian Danner</a>. Berger, having lost four laps as a result of his battery problem, made a charge to finish close behind Danner, setting the fastest lap of the race in the process.
</p><p>With the win, Prost moved from fourth to second in the Drivers' Championship and cut Mansell's lead to two points, 55 to 53, with Senna on 48 and Piquet on 47.
</p>
