<p>The <b>1961 Italian Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 10 September 1961 at <a href="/wiki/Autodromo_Nazionale_di_Monza" title="Autodromo Nazionale di Monza">Monza</a>. It was race 7 of 8 in both the <a href="/wiki/1961_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1961 World Championship of Drivers">1961 World Championship of Drivers</a> and the <a href="/wiki/1961_International_Cup_for_Formula_One_Manufacturers" class="mw-redirect" title="1961 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers">1961 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers</a>.
</p><p>The race was marked by one of the most terrible accidents in the history of Formula One, when on the end of lap 2 at the approach to the Parabolica German driver <a href="/wiki/Wolfgang_von_Trips" title="Wolfgang von Trips">Wolfgang von Trips</a> lost control of his <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> and crashed into a fence line of spectators, killing 15 and himself.<sup id="cite_ref-50_years_ago_today:_F1’s_worst_tragedy_at_Monza_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50_years_ago_today:_F1’s_worst_tragedy_at_Monza-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The race was not stopped, allegedly to avoid the audience going home <i>en masse</i> jamming the roads around the stadium and thus impeding the rescue work for the injured. This was also the last Formula One race ever to be held on the full 10&#160;km (6.213&#160;mi) Monza circuit, with the 2 bankings and the straight between the bankings included.
</p><p>The race was won by von Trips's <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> teammate <a href="/wiki/Phil_Hill" title="Phil Hill">Phil Hill</a>, who – since von Trips was the only one who could challenge him – thus won the World Championship with one race to go. Hill's Monza win also assured Ferrari of the Constructors' Championship for 1961.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1961_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Before the running of the 1961 Italian Grand Prix, four drivers were mathematically in contention for the year's Drivers' Championship: Wolfgang von Trips, Phil Hill, <a href="/wiki/Stirling_Moss" title="Stirling Moss">Stirling Moss</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Richie_Ginther" title="Richie Ginther">Richie Ginther</a>. Realistically, however, the championship had come down to two Ferrari drivers, von Trips and Hill.
</p><p>In the Constructors' Championship, two teams were mathematically in contention: Ferrari, and <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Lotus</a>-<a href="/wiki/Coventry_Climax" title="Coventry Climax">Climax</a>. In order for Lotus to overtake Ferrari at this point, one of their drivers had to win <i>both</i> this race and the final race of the season, the <a href="/wiki/1961_United_States_Grand_Prix" title="1961 United States Grand Prix">1961 United States Grand Prix</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1961_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>37 cars attempted to qualify for the Italian Grand Prix, and 32 had sufficiently fast times to make it to the race grid. Similar to the <a href="/wiki/107%25_rule" title="107% rule">107% rule</a> of today, a rule was in place for the 1961 season enforcing a 115% cutoff against the time of the second fastest driver.
</p>
