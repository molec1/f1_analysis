</div></td></tr></tbody></table><p>The <b>2019 German Grand Prix</b> (formally known as the <b>Formula 1 Mercedes-Benz Großer Preis von Deutschland 2019</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Auto_racing" title="Auto racing">motor race</a> which was held on 28 July 2019 at the <a href="/wiki/Hockenheimring" title="Hockenheimring">Hockenheimring</a> in Germany. The race was the 11th round of the <a href="/wiki/2019_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2019 Formula One World Championship">2019 Formula One World Championship</a> and marked the 78th running of the <a href="/wiki/German_Grand_Prix" title="German Grand Prix">German Grand Prix</a>, and the 64th time the race had been run as a World Championship event since the inaugural season in <a href="/wiki/1950_Formula_One_season" title="1950 Formula One season">1950</a>.
</p><p>The race was won by <a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a> after starting second in a dramatic wet race. <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">Pole-sitter</a> <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> led the race until lap 27 when he crashed behind the <a href="/wiki/Safety_car" title="Safety car">safety car</a>, losing the lead and his front wing as well as receiving a penalty for entering the pit lane on the wrong side of a safety bollard. Whilst behind the final safety car <a href="/wiki/Daniil_Kvyat" title="Daniil Kvyat">Daniil Kvyat</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lance_Stroll" title="Lance Stroll">Lance Stroll</a> switched to dry tyres before anyone else, allowing them to finish an unexpected third and fourth respectively.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2019_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Formally known as the "Formula 1 Mercedes-Benz Großer Preis von Deutschland 2019" this was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> race held on 28 July 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-details_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-details-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The event took place at the <a href="/wiki/Hockenheimring" title="Hockenheimring">Hockenheimring</a> near <a href="/wiki/Hockenheim" title="Hockenheim">Hockenheim</a> in <a href="/wiki/Baden-W%C3%BCrttemberg" title="Baden-Württemberg">Baden-Württemberg</a>, Germany. It was the 11th round of 21 in the <a href="/wiki/2019_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2019 Formula One World Championship">2019 Formula One World Championship</a>, the 78th running of the German Grand Prix and the 64th time it was run as part of the World Championship since the inaugural <a href="/wiki/1950_Formula_One_season" title="1950 Formula One season">1950</a> season.<sup id="cite_ref-:13_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:13-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Mercedes_in_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Mercedes in F1">Mercedes</a> announced that they would be running a different livery in tribute to their heritage with the team as celebrating their 125th year of competing in motorsport, and their 200th F1 start.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Racing_Point_F1_Team" title="Racing Point F1 Team">Racing Point</a> announced that they would bring a “two step” upgrade package to the weekend and the struggling Williams team announced that they too would be bringing an upgrade package.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Haas_F1_Team" title="Haas F1 Team">Haas</a> announced that they would have to split their car setups for the second race running as a result of the inter-team crash at the previous race in <a href="/wiki/2019_British_Grand_Prix" title="2019 British Grand Prix">Britain</a> with <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Magnussen" title="Kevin Magnussen">Kevin Magnussen</a>'s car also carrying extra upgrades.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Championship_standings_before_the_race">Championship standings before the race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2019_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Championship standings before the race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Heading into the weekend it was <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mercedes_in_Formula_One" class="mw-redirect" title="Mercedes in Formula One">Mercedes</a> who held a lead of 39 and 164 points in the drivers and constructors championships respectively. The size of their leads meant that both were confirmed to still be leading their respective championships after the weekend regardless of the race result.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Entrants">Entrants</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2019_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Entrants">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Further information: <a href="/wiki/2019_Formula_One_World_Championship#Entries" title="2019 Formula One World Championship">2019 Formula One World Championship §&#160;Entries</a></div>
<p>The drivers and teams entered were the same as those on the season entry list with no additional stand-in drivers for either the race or practice.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice">Practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2019_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> was fastest in the first practice session followed by <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> teammate <a href="/wiki/Charles_Leclerc" title="Charles Leclerc">Charles Leclerc</a> in second with <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> third fastest. The session passed mostly without incident with the exception being <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Magnussen" title="Kevin Magnussen">Kevin Magnussen</a> after his <a href="/wiki/Haas_VF-19" title="Haas VF-19">car</a> failed causing a brief red flag.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Ferrari managed to get first and second in the second practice session but this time with Leclerc leading Vettel by just over 1 tenth of a second, Hamilton once again finished the session in third. The session's only notable incident was <a href="/wiki/Pierre_Gasly" title="Pierre Gasly">Pierre Gasly</a> crashing on the exit of turn 16.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2019_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_report">Race report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2019_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Race report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The race was scheduled for 67 laps with the <a href="/wiki/Formation_lap" class="mw-redirect" title="Formation lap">formation lap</a> due to start at 15:10 <a href="/wiki/Central_European_Summer_Time" title="Central European Summer Time">local time</a> (13:10 <a href="/wiki/UTC" class="mw-redirect" title="UTC">UTC</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-circuit_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-circuit-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> A rule relevant to a race which has been declared as "wet" at its start was employed for the first time at this Grand Prix. The rule states that after the formation lap and zero or more additional laps behind the safety car, the race director has three options by which to proceed: a <a href="/wiki/Standing_start" title="Standing start">standing start</a> without the <a href="/wiki/Safety_car" title="Safety car">safety car</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Rolling_start" title="Rolling start">rolling start</a> without the safety car, or suspending the race. The number of additional formation laps is subtracted from the overall race distance in all 3 instances.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_start">Race start</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2019_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Race start">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Since at race time the track conditions were considered unsuitable due to heavy rain, all 20 cars were obligated to start on the <a href="/wiki/Rain_tyre" title="Rain tyre">wet tyres</a>. After the formation lap and three additional laps behind the safety car, the race director elected to begin the grand prix with a standing start. Accordingly, the race distance was set to 64 laps, with all four laps behind the safety car at the beginning of the race officially scored as formation laps.<sup id="cite_ref-BBC_Race_p.12_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC_Race_p.12-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-BBC_Race_p.11_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC_Race_p.11-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At the standing start the <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bulls</a> driven by <a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pierre_Gasly" title="Pierre Gasly">Pierre Gasly</a> were both very slow off the starting grid and lost several places each.<sup id="cite_ref-F1_Race_live_p.1_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1_Race_live_p.1-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-StatsF1_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-StatsF1-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> took advantage, and ended up in third by the end of the first lap. Both <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> drivers had a first lap which saw them move up the order. <a href="/wiki/Charles_Leclerc" title="Charles Leclerc">Charles Leclerc</a> who started in 10th was up to 6th, and <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> had improved from 20th to 14th.<sup id="cite_ref-Lap_by_lap_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lap_by_lap-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Verstappen, who had dropped down due to his start, passed Räikkönen for third immediately before <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a> spun off coming out of turn 10, he hit the wall on the inside taking him out of the race. This caused the race's first safety car to appear on lap two.<sup id="cite_ref-BBC_Race_p.11_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC_Race_p.11-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> As the track began to dry Vettel and <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Albon" title="Alexander Albon">Alexander Albon</a> pitted for <a href="/wiki/Intermediate_tyre" class="mw-redirect" title="Intermediate tyre">intermediate tyres</a>, with the majority of the other cars doing the same on the following lap.<sup id="cite_ref-Pit_stops_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pit_stops-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> The safety car period ended on lap 5.<sup id="cite_ref-StatsF1_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-StatsF1-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Kevin_Magnussen" title="Kevin Magnussen">Kevin Magnussen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lance_Stroll" title="Lance Stroll">Lance Stroll</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lando_Norris" title="Lando Norris">Lando Norris</a>, <a href="/wiki/George_Russell_(racing_driver)" title="George Russell (racing driver)">George Russell</a> and <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a>, all of whom stayed out on their wet tyres, quickly found themselves being overtaken by those on the intermediate tyres. All ultimately pitted for intermediates, but lost out for not having done so earlier dropping them to the back of the race.<sup id="cite_ref-Pit_stops_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pit_stops-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Lap_by_lap_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lap_by_lap-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> suffered an exhaust failure on lap 14, causing a <a href="/wiki/Virtual_safety_car" class="mw-redirect" title="Virtual safety car">virtual safety car</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Guardian_live_p.3_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Guardian_live_p.3-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> On lap 18, <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Sainz_Jr." title="Carlos Sainz Jr.">Carlos Sainz</a> lost control of his <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> at turn 16, causing it to run wide and aquaplane on the slippery, dragstrip area of the track, before narrowly avoiding the barriers. This caused a local yellow flag before he was able to drive off under his own power.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Lap_22">Lap 22</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2019_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Lap 22">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>By lap 22, the track had dried sufficiently that teams further down the order decided to risk <a href="/wiki/Racing_slick" title="Racing slick">slick tyres</a>. The first driver to attempt them was Magnussen in his <a href="/wiki/Haas_F1_Team" title="Haas F1 Team">Haas</a> on soft tyres, followed shortly after by Vettel. Verstappen pitted from third position and fitted the medium tyres. <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a> then pitted on lap 27 from second and was also fitted with mediums. Almost immediately after Bottas pitted, Verstappen lost control of his car and performed a 360 degree at turn 14, but managed to recover.
</p><p>Norris's car lost power on track on lap 26, prompting a second deployment of the virtual safety car. Many teams took the opportunity to pit their drivers for dry weather tires, including Leclerc and <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>. A notable exception was Sainz; with McLaren putting on a fresh set of intermediate tyres on his car. As Hamilton was leaving the pits on soft tyres, Leclerc spun into the tyre barrier at turn 17. He tried to drive out from the gravel, but his <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_SF90" title="Ferrari SF90">Ferrari SF90</a> was beached. This ended his race, and prompted the second full safety car of the day. At the end of the lap, Hamilton, now in the lead behind the safety car, also lost control at turn 17, damaging his front wing on the wall and returning into the pit lane cutting across the track as he did so. His <a href="/wiki/Mercedes_in_Formula_One" class="mw-redirect" title="Mercedes in Formula One">Mercedes</a> team was not ready for him, and Hamilton endured a 50.3 second pit stop in which his front wing was replaced and he had switched back onto intermediate tyres. It became clear to all teams at this point that dry weather tyres were not viable, and all teams were back on intermediate tyres by the time the second safety car period ended at lap 34.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Lap_34">Lap 34</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2019_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Lap 34">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>At the beginning of lap 34, the top five drivers in order were Verstappen, <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a>, Bottas, Albon and Hamilton. Vettel, who had started last, was now in 8th. Shortly after the safety car restart, Hamilton was found to have entered the pit lane on the wrong side of a safety bollard on lap 27, and was given a five-second penalty. Meanwhile, on track, Bottas and Hamilton overtook the cars ahead of them to race second and third respectively behind Verstappen. Räikkönen avoided beaching his car in a turn 17 gravel trap on lap 39, losing numerous places. One lap later, Hülkenberg slid into another gravel trap on the same corner, and was unable to free his car. This brought out the third safety car of the day. A few drivers including race leader Verstappen pitted during this safety car period; Verstappen's stop was notable as the fastest F1 pit stop time ever at 1.88 seconds, beating the previous time of 1.91 seconds, set during the <a href="/wiki/2019_British_Grand_Prix" title="2019 British Grand Prix">2019 British Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> All drivers who pitted during this safety car kept intermediate tyres, with one notable exception: <a href="/wiki/Lance_Stroll" title="Lance Stroll">Lance Stroll</a>, who was the only driver to fit slick tyres. The third safety car period ended on lap 46.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Lap_46">Lap 46</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2019_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Lap 46">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>By this point in the race, the rain had stopped falling, and a clear dry line had formed on the track. Half the field pitted on lap 46 for dry weather tyres, including Verstappen and Bottas. Hamilton took the lead ahead of both. All the drivers remaining on intermediates, Hamilton included, pitted on lap 47 for slick tyres. Hamilton, because he had to serve his five-second penalty, was relegated to 12th position after his stop. Meanwhile, Stroll, the only driver to take slicks during the previous safety car, briefly inherited the lead of the race. Verstappen and then <a href="/wiki/Daniil_Kvyat" title="Daniil Kvyat">Daniil Kvyat</a> soon passed him within a lap leaving Stroll in 3rd.
</p><p>The next several laps of the race saw Verstappen extend his race lead to more than ten seconds. Sebastian Vettel, who had started lap 46 in 8th, began showing pace on dry tyres and moving through the midfield. Lewis Hamilton spun out at turn 1, narrowly avoiding the barriers, dropping down to 13th on lap 53. The field was frozen once again on lap 56, when Valtteri Bottas, running in 4th, slid off track and into a tyre barrier in a near identical spin to Hamilton a few laps earlier, suffering front suspension damage that would end his race. Verstappen's 10 second lead disappeared as Bottas's shunt brought out the fourth and final safety car of the race. Sebastian Vettel, who had by this point moved up to 5th position, found himself in a favorable position to attack the frontrunners heading into the final few laps.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Finish">Finish</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2019_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Finish">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The safety car caused by Bottas's crash ended on lap 60. <a href="/wiki/Pierre_Gasly" title="Pierre Gasly">Pierre Gasly</a>'s car suffered terminal damage when he collided with Albon two laps later. Verstappen extended his race lead once again, pulling over 7 seconds ahead of 2nd position by the final lap. Vettel was able to pass Sainz, Stroll, and Kvyat to finish in 2nd after starting the race in last. Kvyat, finishing 3rd, achieved Toro Rosso's first podium since Vettel's victory at the <a href="/wiki/2008_Italian_Grand_Prix" title="2008 Italian Grand Prix">2008 Italian Grand Prix</a>. Verstappen won his second race of the last three, also picking up the point for fastest lap and the spectator voted "Driver of the Day" award.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="After_the_race">After the race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2019_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: After the race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After the race, both <a href="/wiki/Alfa_Romeo_Racing" class="mw-redirect" title="Alfa Romeo Racing">Alfa Romeo</a> drivers Räikkönen and <a href="/wiki/Antonio_Giovinazzi" title="Antonio Giovinazzi">Antonio Giovinazzi</a> were penalised 30 seconds for use of driver aids at the start.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> This relegated them to 12th and 13th, respectively. The penalties moved Hamilton up to 9th and prevented Mercedes from leaving the race without points for the first time since the <a href="/wiki/2018_Austrian_Grand_Prix" title="2018 Austrian Grand Prix">2018 Austrian Grand Prix</a>. It further allowed Kubica, moved up to 10th, to earn his and <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a>'s first and what would prove to be the only point in Williams's <a href="/wiki/2019_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2019 Formula One World Championship">2019</a> campaign. An appeal against the penalties was lodged by Alfa Romeo after the race and was subsequently dismissed by the FIA World Council.
</p>
