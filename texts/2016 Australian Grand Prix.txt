<p>The <b>2016 Australian Grand Prix</b> (formally known as the <b>2016 Formula 1 Rolex Australian Grand Prix</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Auto_racing" title="Auto racing">motor race</a> that was held on 20 March 2016 in <a href="/wiki/Melbourne,_Victoria" class="mw-redirect" title="Melbourne, Victoria">Melbourne</a>. The race was contested over fifty-seven laps of the <a href="/wiki/Melbourne_Grand_Prix_Circuit" class="mw-redirect" title="Melbourne Grand Prix Circuit">Melbourne Grand Prix Circuit</a> and was the first round of the <a href="/wiki/2016_FIA_Formula_One_World_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="2016 FIA Formula One World Championship">2016 FIA Formula One World Championship</a>. The race marked the 81st race in the combined history of the <a href="/wiki/Australian_Grand_Prix" title="Australian Grand Prix">Australian Grand Prix</a> – which dates back to the <a href="/wiki/1928_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="1928 Australian Grand Prix">100 Miles Road Race</a> of 1928 – and the 21st time the event was held at the Melbourne Grand Prix Circuit. <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> was the defending race winner.
</p><p>Hamilton took the first <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> of the season and the fiftieth of his career in a qualifying session that saw the introduction of a new one-by-one elimination format that was widely criticised. His teammate <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> took victory ahead of Hamilton and <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a>. The race was stopped on lap 18 following an accident involving <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> and <a href="/wiki/Esteban_Guti%C3%A9rrez" title="Esteban Gutiérrez">Esteban Gutiérrez</a>. Mercedes used the break for a change of tyres that allowed their drivers to take a 1–2 finish after Vettel had initially led the race from the start. <a href="/wiki/Romain_Grosjean" title="Romain Grosjean">Romain Grosjean</a> finished sixth, scoring points for the <a href="/wiki/Haas_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Haas F1">Haas F1</a> team on their début, the first completely new team to do so since <a href="/wiki/2002_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2002 Formula One World Championship">2002</a>. Mercedes equalled the record for most consecutive 1–2 finishes, by achieving their fifth in a row.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2016_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2016_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Regulation_changes">Regulation changes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2016_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Regulation changes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The race saw the introduction of a new qualifying system. As before, qualifying was divided into three parts. However, instead of eliminating the slowest drivers at the end of each respective session, the slowest driver at a given point was now eliminated from contention every ninety seconds, with the countdown starting some minutes into every session.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In a further change of regulations, tyre supplier <a href="/wiki/Pirelli" title="Pirelli">Pirelli</a> now made three instead of two tyre compounds available for each Grand Prix. For the Australian Grand Prix, these were the super-soft, soft and medium compounds.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> With Pirelli providing every driver with two sets of tyres for the race (of which one must be used) and an additional one for the third part of qualifying, drivers were able to choose ten additional sets of tyres out of the three compounds available. Notably, <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> drivers <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> made different choices, with Rosberg opting for an additional set of mediums instead of soft tyres. The two <a href="/wiki/Manor_Racing" title="Manor Racing">Manor</a> drivers chose the most conservative way, taking on four sets each of the medium and soft compounds.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The race saw the competitive début of the <a href="/wiki/Haas_F1_Team" title="Haas F1 Team">Haas F1 Team</a>, and its car, the <a href="/wiki/Haas_VF-16" title="Haas VF-16">Haas VF-16</a>;<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> and the return of <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a> as a fully manufacturer-supported team after a four-year absence.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Free_practice">Free practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2016_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Free practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Per the regulations for the <a href="/wiki/2016_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2016 Formula One World Championship">2016</a> season, three practice sessions were held, two 1.5-hour sessions on Friday and another one-hour session before qualifying on Saturday.<sup id="cite_ref-pracreg_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pracreg-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Rain had fallen before the start of the first practice session, rendering the track wet and slippery, and drivers initially opted to go on their first laps on intermediate tyres. Nico Rosberg was the first to set a lap time, clocking in at 1:44.037, a time soon bettered by his Mercedes teammate Lewis Hamilton at 1:40.812. As conditions improved further, <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> set a new fastest time after about half an hour of practice. Soon after, the track had dried enough for the drivers to go out on slick tyres, with <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> setting the first time on the medium compound with 1:34.007. After around one hour of practice, Hamilton managed the best time of the session, at 1:29.725. Still, several wet spots on the track caused problems to a number of drivers, including <a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a>, who <a href="/wiki/Flat_spot#Car_tires" title="Flat spot">flat-spotted</a> his tyres while spinning in turn six, as well as <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rio_Haryanto" title="Rio Haryanto">Rio Haryanto</a>, who both had to pass through the gravel traps after slipping off the track. Shortly before the end of the session, rain returned and caught out many drivers, including Räikkönen and Ricciardo, whose <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_RB12" title="Red Bull RB12">Red Bull RB12</a> got stuck in a gravel pit at turn twelve.<sup id="cite_ref-fp1auto_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fp1auto-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fp1_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fp1-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Due to several rain showers between first and second practice, the track was again wet during the second session, which limited the drivers to using the intermediate tyres only. Hamilton was again fastest, setting a time of 1:38.841. Teammate Rosberg crashed at turn seven thirty minutes into the session, damaging a new-specification front wing that the team had deliberately not run in first practice for fear of damage. Rosberg later apologised for the incident,<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> which saw him trying to get back to pit lane, before his team told him to stop on track, calling an early end to his session.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a>, Räikkönen, Ricciardo and <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Sainz_Jr." title="Carlos Sainz Jr.">Carlos Sainz Jr.</a> all finished within one second of Hamilton's time, while <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a> was at one point on his way to a new fastest time overall, setting best times in the first two timechecks along the track, before having to slow in the last part of the lap due to heavy rain. The two Manor drivers <a href="/wiki/Pascal_Wehrlein" title="Pascal Wehrlein">Pascal Wehrlein</a> and Haryanto covered the highest number of laps, with 24 and 22 laps in 12th and 14th place respectively. Neither Renault nor <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> drivers set a time, albeit going on track. The <a href="/wiki/Sauber_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber F1">Sauber</a> cars and Max Verstappen's <a href="/wiki/Toro_Rosso" class="mw-redirect" title="Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso</a> did not leave the garage at all during the session.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Although rain fell on Saturday morning, the track had dried by the start of the final free practice session. Lewis Hamilton again set the fastest time with 1:25.624, less than two-tenths of a second in front of teammate Rosberg, with <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> in third position close behind. The fastest times had been set on the super-soft tyre compound. However, Mercedes's advantage over <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> proved more significant, up to seven-tenths of a second on the harder soft compound, the tyre most likely to be used in the race. Toro Rosso confirmed their good pace for the weekend with fourth and sixth place for Carlos Sainz and Max Verstappen respectively, being about half a second quicker than their sister team, <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a>. A major incident occurred just seconds after the beginning of the practice session, when Rio Haryanto and <a href="/wiki/Romain_Grosjean" title="Romain Grosjean">Romain Grosjean</a> collided in the pit lane. Both cars needed to equip new front wings and <a href="/wiki/Haas_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Haas F1">Haas</a> also changed the floor on Grosjean's car.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Haryanto was later issued a three-place grid penalty for the incident, as well as two penalty points added to his licence.<sup id="cite_ref-gridharyanto_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gridharyanto-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2016_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Ricciardo_2016_Australia.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/63/Ricciardo_2016_Australia.jpg/220px-Ricciardo_2016_Australia.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/63/Ricciardo_2016_Australia.jpg/330px-Ricciardo_2016_Australia.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/63/Ricciardo_2016_Australia.jpg/440px-Ricciardo_2016_Australia.jpg 2x" data-file-width="800" data-file-height="533" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Ricciardo_2016_Australia.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> qualified eighth and went on to finish fourth in the race.</div></div></div>
<p>Qualifying got under way on Saturday afternoon with new rules in place. Just as in years before, the qualifying procedure was divided into three parts, with the first part (Q1) running for 16 minutes and the second and third parts (Q2 and Q3) being 15 and 14 minutes long respectively. All twenty-two cars contested the first part, with seven drivers eliminated from further contention in each of the first two parts of qualifying, leaving eight drivers to compete for <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> in Q3. However, in a change of rules, drivers were now eliminated during the session, with the slowest runner at a given point being taken out from contention every ninety seconds, beginning seven minutes into Q1, six minutes into Q2 and five minutes into Q3.<sup id="cite_ref-pracreg_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pracreg-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The new format meant that all cars took to the track quickly in Q1, with everyone setting lap times on the super-soft compound, the fastest tyre available at the event. The two Manor drivers were first to be eliminated, having set only one timed lap each, as were both Haas cars after them, being unable to cross the finishing line in time. The same fate caught out Daniil Kvyat, who qualified 18th. Both Sauber drivers were able to go out on a second timed lap, but proved too slow to avoid being the last to go out in Q1, after Renault rookie <a href="/wiki/Jolyon_Palmer" title="Jolyon Palmer">Jolyon Palmer</a> was able to avoid elimination, setting a faster time towards the end of the session.<sup id="cite_ref-qualf1fan_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualf1fan-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Q2 started with a busy track once more, but many of the top drivers elected not to go out on a second timed run after setting sufficient lap times first time around. The eliminated drivers were determined rather quickly, with the two Renault drivers qualifying 14th and 15th respectively. The two <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLarens</a> were next to go out of contention, before Bottas was eliminated for 11th place on the grid, failing to improve on his lap time. Both <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a> drivers were the last not to make it into Q3, with Sergio Pérez beating his teammate Hülkenberg to ninth.<sup id="cite_ref-qualf1fan_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualf1fan-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The third part of qualifying determined pole position and only the two Mercedes drivers set two timed laps. After he clocked in behind both Ferrari drivers in his first run, Rosberg improved to second place on his second time out, but was unable to challenge teammate Hamilton, who took the fiftieth pole position of his career. Every other driver was limited to only one timed lap and in the end, the two Ferrari cars of Vettel and Räikkönen locked out the second row on the grid ahead of Max Verstappen and <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a>. Sainz and Ricciardo rounded up the top eight on the grid.<sup id="cite_ref-qualf1fan_19-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualf1fan-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-qualauto_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualauto-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Therefore, Q3 saw little running with the final positions determined very early on and drivers exiting their cars five minutes before the end of qualifying.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-qualifying">Post-qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2016_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Post-qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The new qualifying format was criticised immediately after the end of the session by pundits, drivers and team personnel alike. <a href="/wiki/Sky_Sports" title="Sky Sports">Sky Sports</a> commentator <a href="/wiki/Martin_Brundle" title="Martin Brundle">Martin Brundle</a> called for a swift revision of the rules, saying the procedure was "not acceptable" and calling for it to be abandoned before <a href="/wiki/2016_Bahrain_Grand_Prix" title="2016 Bahrain Grand Prix">the next race</a>. Mercedes's executive director <a href="/wiki/Toto_Wolff" title="Toto Wolff">Toto Wolff</a> and former driver <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Herbert" title="Johnny Herbert">Johnny Herbert</a> were equally critical, describing it as "rubbish" and "embarrassing". Red Bull team director <a href="/wiki/Christian_Horner" title="Christian Horner">Christian Horner</a> was apologetic, describing the format as "[not] good for Formula One."<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Niki_Lauda" title="Niki Lauda">Niki Lauda</a>, non-executive chairman at Mercedes, was in agreement, calling it "a big mistake."<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Particular criticism was aimed at the fact that many drivers did not get enough time to improve on their lap times, seeing many getting out of their cockpits while still technically in contention. <a href="/wiki/1996_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1996 Formula One World Championship">1996</a> World Champion <a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a> observed that pole-sitter Hamilton "could have waved his own chequered flag [...] with four minutes still to go."<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Nico Rosberg acknowledged that it had "not worked", while Sebastian Vettel was equally critical, describing it as "the wrong way to go."<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Lewis Hamilton joined the criticism but acknowledged the attempt to revitalise the format.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Bernie_Ecclestone" title="Bernie Ecclestone">Bernie Ecclestone</a>, the commercial rights holder of Formula One, spoke out against the format as well, declaring it to be "pretty crap".<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> On the day of the race, the teams of Formula 1 voted a proposal to go back to the qualifying format as it had been in previous seasons, which was rejected by the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">FIA</a>'s F1 Commission.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2016_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the end of the formation lap, Daniil Kvyat was unable to reach the grid due to an electrical issue.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Therefore, the field went to another formation lap and the race was shortened accordingly to 57 laps. At the start proper, Vettel got away well, passing through both Mercedes cars into the lead, followed by his teammate Räikkönen in second. As Rosberg and Hamilton went through the first corner, Hamilton was forced wide and lost additional places, falling down to sixth. <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Magnussen" title="Kevin Magnussen">Kevin Magnussen</a> suffered a puncture on the first lap and made a pit stop for new tyres, while <a href="/wiki/Esteban_Guti%C3%A9rrez" title="Esteban Gutiérrez">Esteban Gutiérrez</a> had mechanical issues at the back of the field. Hamilton was able to pass fifth-placed Massa on lap four, as Vettel was leading from Räikkonen, Rosberg and Verstappen. On lap eight, Sainz was the first of the top-ten runners to pit for new tyres. Meanwhile, Hamilton was unable to pass Verstappen for fourth, discussing alternative tactics with his team on the radio communication. Pascal Wehrlein had a good start in his debut as well, running in 14th place at that point of the race.<sup id="cite_ref-guard_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guard-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-tickbbc_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tickbbc-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Hamilton_2016_Australia.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1b/Hamilton_2016_Australia.jpg/220px-Hamilton_2016_Australia.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1b/Hamilton_2016_Australia.jpg/330px-Hamilton_2016_Australia.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1b/Hamilton_2016_Australia.jpg/440px-Hamilton_2016_Australia.jpg 2x" data-file-width="800" data-file-height="533" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Hamilton_2016_Australia.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> started in <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> and finished second in the race.</div></div></div>
<p>On the end of lap eleven, Rosberg was the first front runner to go into the pitlane and Vettel followed suit just two laps later. A quicker stop by Rosberg saw Vettel emerge just in front of him, but the Ferrari stayed ahead and he passed Hamilton for second on lap 16. On the same lap, both Räikkönen and Hamilton made pit stops as well, re-establishing the order. One lap later, <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> had a major accident as he ran into the back of Gutiérrez, being lifted into the air at 300&#160;km/h (190&#160;mph) and hitting the left-hand barrier before subsequently rolling over several times before coming to rest upside-down against the barrier at turn three, experiencing a peak force of 46G.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> While Alonso walked away with minor injuries, a safety car was deployed for a short time before the race direction decided to <a href="/wiki/Racing_flags#Red_flag" title="Racing flags">red flag</a> the race due to the huge amounts of debris across the track from both Alonso's McLaren and damaged advertising boards, causing all cars to come back into pitlane.<sup id="cite_ref-guard_31-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guard-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-tickbbc_32-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tickbbc-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>While the cars waited for the restart, work on the cars was allowed and Hamilton's front wing was changed, while Rio Haryanto ended his race. As the race resumed behind the safety car, both Ferrari drivers were running on the super-soft tyre compound, with both Mercedes now equipped with mediums, which were set to last for the rest of the race. The running order at the restart was: Vettel, Rosberg, Räikkönen, Ricciardo, Verstappen, Sainz, Hamilton, Massa, Grosjean, Bottas. Grosjean in ninth was the only driver who had not made a pit stop before the red flag was shown, which allowed him to save one pit stop and change his tyres during the break.<sup id="cite_ref-guard_31-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guard-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-tickbbc_32-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tickbbc-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Vettel led away at the restart, but his Ferrari teammate Räikkönen was forced to pull into the pit lane on lap 22 with fire coming from his airbox and subsequently retired, handing second place to Rosberg. Over the next couple of laps, Vettel was able to pull away from Rosberg on the softer tyre compound. <a href="/wiki/Marcus_Ericsson" title="Marcus Ericsson">Marcus Ericsson</a> was handed a drive-through penalty for having his team work on his car less than 15 seconds before the restart. By lap 31, the gap between Vettel and Rosberg started to come down again, while Hamilton moved ahead of Sainz into fifth as the latter made a pit stop. Two laps later, Verstappen was in as well, but a slow pit stop lost him time. On lap 35, Rosberg took the lead as Vettel made a pit stop, who also lost time due to a mistake during his stop. He emerged back on track in fourth place. Hamilton now started to pull back time on the leaders, while debutant Palmer held off the two Toro Rosso drivers in a struggle for ninth place.<sup id="cite_ref-guard_31-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guard-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-tickbbc_32-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tickbbc-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Grosjean_Barcelona_testing_2016.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/77/Grosjean_Barcelona_testing_2016.jpg/220px-Grosjean_Barcelona_testing_2016.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="62" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/77/Grosjean_Barcelona_testing_2016.jpg/330px-Grosjean_Barcelona_testing_2016.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/77/Grosjean_Barcelona_testing_2016.jpg/440px-Grosjean_Barcelona_testing_2016.jpg 2x" data-file-width="7360" data-file-height="2089" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Grosjean_Barcelona_testing_2016.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Romain_Grosjean" title="Romain Grosjean">Romain Grosjean</a> finished sixth, scoring points for <a href="/wiki/Haas_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Haas F1">Haas F1</a> on the team's début.</div></div></div>
<p>On lap 40, Ericsson started to slow on track and eventually retired. One lap later, Hamilton moved into second ahead of Ricciardo on the main straight. Lap 42 saw first Sainz and then Verstappen overtake Palmer's Renault, who fell back to eleventh. Another lap later, Ricciardo changed tyres and came out fifth behind Massa, taking fourth three laps later. At the front, Hamilton and Vettel slowly closed on Rosberg, with the gap between the two also coming down. Vettel came onto the back of Hamilton with five laps remaining, chasing him closely for several laps before making a mistake in the second to last corner of lap 55, losing his chances at second place.<sup id="cite_ref-guard_31-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guard-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-tickbbc_32-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tickbbc-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As the race ended, Nico Rosberg took victory, thirty-one years after his father <a href="/wiki/Keke_Rosberg" title="Keke Rosberg">Keke Rosberg</a> had won the <a href="/wiki/1985_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="1985 Australian Grand Prix">1985 Australian Grand Prix</a>.  Hamilton and Vettel rounded out the podium ahead of Ricciardo and Massa. With Romain Grosjean having finished in sixth, Haas F1 became the first new (and "from scratch") constructor since <a href="/wiki/Toyota_Racing_(Formula_One_team)" title="Toyota Racing (Formula One team)">Toyota</a> in 2002 to score points in its inaugural Grand Prix race entry.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;N 1&#93;</a></sup> Behind Grosjean, Hülkenberg, Bottas, Sainz and Verstappen were the other point scorers.<sup id="cite_ref-guard_31-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guard-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-tickbbc_32-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tickbbc-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> By finishing fourth, Daniel Ricciardo equalled the highest ever finish by an Australian driver at their home event, after <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> did the same in <a href="/wiki/2012_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="2012 Australian Grand Prix">2012</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;N 2&#93;</a></sup> It was Mercedes's fifth consecutive 1–2 finish, equalling a record set by Ferrari in <a href="/wiki/1952_Formula_One_season" title="1952 Formula One season">1952</a> and <a href="/wiki/2002_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2002 Formula One World Championship">2002</a> and by themselves in <a href="/wiki/2014_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2014 Formula One World Championship">2014</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-stats_35-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-stats-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2016_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the podium interviews, conducted by former Formula One driver Mark Webber, both Mercedes drivers expressed delight at their result. Hamilton in particular described his race as "great" and added that he "loved the fact that we had to come through from far behind."<sup id="cite_ref-pressconf_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconf-40">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> Sebastian Vettel meanwhile was satisfied with his start, but lamented the red flag situation and said that Ferrari had not expected Mercedes to opt for the harder tyre compound and go until the end of the race.<sup id="cite_ref-pressconf_40-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconf-40">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> He later added that he was confident that Ferrari had closed the gap to Mercedes and would be able to compete for the championship throughout the season.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> Nico Rosberg later revealed that the team had been close to retiring his car due to persisting brake issues caused by debris caught in the brake caliper. However, he was able to manage the brake temperatures to secure victory.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> Rosberg also apologised to his teammate for making contact and forcing him wide at the start.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Following an inquiry into the incident between Alonso and Gutiérrez, the stewards took no action against either driver, deeming it a "racing incident" with no driver particularly to blame.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> Fernando Alonso shared the stewards' opinion and was quick to express that he did not blame Gutiérrez.<sup id="cite_ref-sky_45-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sky-45">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> The accident was a talking point for days after the race, with former FIA president <a href="/wiki/Max_Mosley" title="Max Mosley">Max Mosley</a> pointing out that Alonso's impact might have been fatal about twenty years earlier. He praised the measures taken in terms of driver security ever since the fatal accident of <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> at the <a href="/wiki/1994_San_Marino_Grand_Prix" title="1994 San Marino Grand Prix">1994 San Marino Grand Prix</a>, saying that due to these efforts, one was now able to "expect the driver to walk away" from a violent crash.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Particular praise after the race went to both newcomers Haas and debutant Jolyon Palmer,<sup id="cite_ref-sky_45-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sky-45">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> who delivered what <i><a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">The Guardian</a></i> described as an "impressive" drive. Palmer had been able to hold off attacks by both Toro Rosso drivers for several laps in the closing stages of the race.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> Toro Rosso's Max Verstappen was very unhappy with his team's strategy in the race handling the situation, repeatedly complaining and swearing over the team radio. He lost several seconds in a rushed pitstop, after which he lamented that he had wanted to pit before his teammate Sainz. While driving behind Sainz when both where trailing Palmer, he asked for team orders to allow him to pass his teammate, which were denied, leading him to describe his team's strategy as a "joke".<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> Verstappen received criticism for his outbursts, with Sky Sports describing him as sounding "like a teenager - and a sulky one at that".<sup id="cite_ref-sky_45-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sky-45">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> He later apologised to his team for his outbursts.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
