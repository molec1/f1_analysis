<p>The <b>1999 Brazilian Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>XXVIII Grande Prêmio Marlboro do Brasil</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 11 April 1999 at the <a href="/wiki/Aut%C3%B3dromo_Jos%C3%A9_Carlos_Pace" title="Autódromo José Carlos Pace">Autódromo José Carlos Pace</a> in <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo" title="São Paulo">São Paulo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>. It was the second race of the <a href="/wiki/1999_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="1999 Formula One season">1999 Formula One season</a>. The 71-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> after starting from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>. <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> finished second in a <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> with <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a> third for the <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a> team.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Ricardo_Zonta" title="Ricardo Zonta">Ricardo Zonta</a> did not qualify for the race, after he had injured his left foot in a big crash during Saturday's practice.<sup id="cite_ref-sportinglife_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sportinglife-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1999_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1999_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Driver_Changes">Driver Changes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1999_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Driver Changes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The race marked the debut for <a href="/wiki/St%C3%A9phane_Sarrazin" title="Stéphane Sarrazin">Stéphane Sarrazin</a>, who drove the <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a> for an injured <a href="/wiki/Luca_Badoer" title="Luca Badoer">Luca Badoer</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-rejects_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rejects-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Badoer had injured his hand in a testing accident, and Sarrazin - then the test driver for <a href="/wiki/Prost_Grand_Prix" title="Prost Grand Prix">Prost</a> - was drafted in to Minardi.<sup id="cite_ref-rejects_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rejects-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As Luca Badoer returned for the following race, and he was still the test driver for Prost, it was Stéphane Sarrazin's only entry in Formula One.<sup id="cite_ref-rejects_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rejects-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1999_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the start of the race, pole sitter <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> raced off with the lead, while his McLaren teammate <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> stalled on the grid.<sup id="cite_ref-sportinglife_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sportinglife-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> McLaren at this point had been reeling from a double-DNF at the season-opening <a href="/wiki/1999_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="1999 Australian Grand Prix">Australian Grand Prix</a>, and Coulthard's failure raised eyebrows at the team.<sup id="cite_ref-sportinglife_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sportinglife-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard's car was pushed into the pit lane, where it was restarted as the leaders began lap 4.
</p><p>On lap 4, local hero <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> took the lead of the race from Mika Häkkinen after Häkkinen's car suffered a temporary transmission malfunction and was unable to select any gears. Häkkinen was also passed by <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> before his car regained the ability to select gears.<sup id="cite_ref-carenth_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-carenth-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello was able to stay in front until he pitted on lap 27.<sup id="cite_ref-BBC_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> It was the first time a <a href="/wiki/Stewart_Grand_Prix" title="Stewart Grand Prix">Stewart</a> car had led a race.<sup id="cite_ref-BBC_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The crowd of roughly 80,000<sup id="cite_ref-BBC_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> cheered wildly as "Rubinho" built a lead of about 5 seconds over Schumacher.<sup id="cite_ref-sportinglife_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sportinglife-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Alexander_Wurz" title="Alexander Wurz">Alexander Wurz</a> and <a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a> collided on lap 10, ending Hill's race.<sup id="cite_ref-sportinglife_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sportinglife-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Rubens Barrichello fell to fourth place after his pit stop, and Michael Schumacher took over the lead.<sup id="cite_ref-carenth_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-carenth-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> David Coulthard's day ended when he pulled off the track with a mechanical failure. Stéphane Sarrazin had a massive crash on the pit straight on lap 31 after suffering a wing failure, with him spinning more than six times.<sup id="cite_ref-rejects_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rejects-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> On lap 35, Barrichello passed <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a> under braking into the first corner to take third place.
</p><p>Michael Schumacher came in for a pit stop on lap 38, allowing Mika Häkkinen past. Häkkinen had been held up by Schumacher, so he began trying to build up enough of a gap so he could come out ahead of Schumacher after his own pit stop. Lap traffic delayed his progress at first, but after one lap he was able to turn in a couple of fast laps. He pitted on lap 42, and his fast laps combined with quick work by his pit crew allowed him to easily retain the lead over Schumacher.<sup id="cite_ref-carenth_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-carenth-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On lap 42, <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Diniz" title="Pedro Diniz">Pedro Diniz</a> spun off and beached his car after trying to pass another car to the inside. Rubens Barrichello's race ended on the same lap with a blown engine.<sup id="cite_ref-sportinglife_2-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sportinglife-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Eddie Irvine came in for an unscheduled pit stop on lap 55 to clear the radiators of his overheating <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, dropping him back to fifth.<sup id="cite_ref-carenth_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-carenth-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Mika Häkkinen won the race, with Michael Schumacher second. <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a> was classified third despite running out of fuel on the final lap, as the next car was a lap down. <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> finished fourth after being closely pursued by Eddie Irvine for the last few laps.
</p>
