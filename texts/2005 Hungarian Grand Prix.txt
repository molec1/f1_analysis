<p>The <b>2005 Hungarian Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> race, held on 31 July 2005 in the <a href="/wiki/Hungaroring" title="Hungaroring">Hungaroring</a> circuit at 14:00 <a href="/wiki/Central_European_Summer_Time" title="Central European Summer Time">CEST</a> (<a href="/wiki/UTC%2B2" class="mw-redirect" title="UTC+2">UTC+2</a>).
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2005_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The 2005 Hungarian Grand Prix was the thirteenth race of the <a href="/wiki/2005_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2005 Formula One World Championship">2005 Formula One season</a>, and saw the beginning of the end of <a href="/wiki/Nicotine_marketing" title="Nicotine marketing">tobacco advertising</a> in F1 due to the <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>-wide ban. <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> (<a href="/wiki/West_(cigarette)" title="West (cigarette)">West</a>) ran with tobacco livery just on Friday and <a href="/wiki/British_American_Racing" title="British American Racing">BAR</a> (<a href="/wiki/Lucky_Strike" title="Lucky Strike">Lucky Strike</a>) ran with tobacco livery on Friday and Saturday before withdrawing it for the race, while <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> (<a href="/wiki/Marlboro_(cigarette)" title="Marlboro (cigarette)">Marlboro</a>), <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a> (<a href="/wiki/Mevius" title="Mevius">Mild Seven</a>), and <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a> (<a href="/wiki/Benson_%26_Hedges" title="Benson &amp; Hedges">Benson &amp; Hedges</a>) ran full tobacco livery for the entire weekend. It was the last Formula One race to be sponsored by a tobacco company, this is also the only official <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> race weekend for <a href="/wiki/Chanoch_Nissany" title="Chanoch Nissany">Chanoch Nissany</a>, who drove for <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a> in Free Practice 1 and was around six and a half seconds behind the next slowest driver.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> surprised the paddock by taking his first <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> of the season, causing speculation that he was running a light first fuel load in order to build a gap on a circuit on which overtaking is notoriously difficult. Beside him on the front row was <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a>, nearly 9 tenths behind. <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> continued his impressive qualifying form by taking 3rd place, while <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> took fourth spot despite being the first runner out on the dirty circuit. Championship leader <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> started in sixth position.
</p><p>Race day was hot, with the air temperature at 34&#160;°C, and track temperature 47&#160;°C at the start of the race. Both Michael Schumacher and Montoya got fast starts, while Räikkönen took Trulli off the line, and ended the lap in second place, having passed his teammate as well. Further back, Alonso was squeezed into turn one by <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">German</a>'s right-rear wheel making contact with Alonso's front wing and causing it to hang unevenly. <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> braked too late into the first turn, breaking his front wing on Trulli's diffuser. Further back, <a href="/wiki/Christian_Klien" title="Christian Klien">Christian Klien</a> made contact with <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a>, with their wheels touching as they fought for position in the first turn. This sent Klien into a spectacular barrel-roll, before finishing the right way up, and unharmed. <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a>'s race then went from bad to worse, with Alonso's front wing dislodging itself between turns 10 and 11, into the path of several drivers. Most managed to avoid it, but <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a>, apparently unsighted by <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a>'s <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a>, hit the debris, causing the <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scot</a>'s right-front suspension to break and force him into retirement. At the end of lap 1, Schumacher led by 1.9 seconds over Räikkönen, who was a further 2.4 seconds ahead of Montoya. Alonso, Barrichello and <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Tiago_Monteiro" title="Tiago Monteiro">Tiago Monteiro</a> were all forced to pit at the end of the lap for repairs.
</p><p>Immediately, Räikkönen began pressuring Schumacher, the pair lapping Monteiro after just 4 laps. Räikkönen and Schumacher then began trading fastest laps, and the gap was cut down to just 0.792 seconds after 9 laps. Meanwhile, Barrichello, forced back due to his early damage, got past <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Robert_Doornbos" title="Robert Doornbos">Robert Doornbos</a>, clear superior car speed meaning the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Dutch</a> driver could offer no resistance. Räikkönen entered the pits first, on lap 11, showing that his qualifying effort was significantly helped by the light fuel load, and leaving Schumacher to attempt to open a gap before his own stop. Barrichello also made his move on <a href="/wiki/Christijan_Albers" title="Christijan Albers">Christijan Albers</a>, moving up to 14th, while Alonso finally dispatched Doornbos to move up to 16th place. Trulli became the second driver to take his scheduled fuel stop on lap 13, while Alonso made short work of Albers to move into 15th. Barrichello continued his charge, taking <a href="/wiki/Narain_Karthikeyan" title="Narain Karthikeyan">Narain Karthikeyan</a> for 13th place on lap 14. The question of how much fuel was in Schumacher's car was answered on lap 15, with the German refuelling to leave Montoya in the lead. Schumacher rejoined reasonably comfortably ahead of Räikkönen, while his brother <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> managed to leapfrog teammate Trulli, who was still suffering with a damaged diffuser.
</p><p>Montoya stretched his lead to over 16 seconds by lap 17, while Doornbos, Karthikeyan and Monteiro all made their stops from the back of the field. Montoya took his first stop on lap 22, but his lead was not large enough to allow him to stay in the lead, rejoining in 3rd place, behind Michael Schumacher and Räikkönen, and ahead of <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a>, who had yet to stop. Button took his pitstop alongside <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> on the following lap, while Räikkönen again closed in on Michael Schumacher, reducing the gap to just 0.6 seconds by lap 24. Alonso took his first scheduled stop on lap 26, rejoining the race in 14th, and still well out of the pointscoring positions. Lap 27 saw <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a> pit, the pair apparently on two-stop strategies, while <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> showed that he was fuelled heavily during qualifying, becoming the last driver to take their first stop, on lap 29, from 7th position. Meanwhile, Robert Doornbos pulled off to retire, the team citing <a href="/wiki/Hydraulics" title="Hydraulics">hydraulic</a> failure for his early demise.
</p><p>On lap 30, Michael Schumacher led by just 0.6 seconds over Räikkönen, the <a href="/wiki/Finland" title="Finland">Finn</a> apparently content to stay behind the German until their next stops. Montoya was in third position, ahead of Ralf Schumacher, Trulli, Button, Heidfeld, <a href="/wiki/Takuma_Sato" title="Takuma Sato">Takuma Sato</a>, Fisichella, Barrichello and Webber. The <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Saubers</a> of Villeneuve and <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> sat in 12th and 13th, both of their <a href="/wiki/Sauber_Petronas_Engineering" title="Sauber Petronas Engineering">Petronas</a> <a href="/wiki/Engine" title="Engine">engines</a> sounding rough. Trulli and Barrichello pitted on lap 33, followed by Michael Schumacher on lap 35. The German held just a 0.5 second lead over Räikkönen, who pitted on the following lap. The gap was enough for Räikkönen to take the position from Schumacher. Montoya then slowed considerably, and pitted at the end of lap 41 to retire from the race. This handed Räikkönen a comfortable lead over Schumacher, and pushed Ralf Schumacher into a potential podium position. Massa entered the pits on lap 42, spending several laps as the team attempted to fix the damage caused by a small fire due to a fuel spill. On lap 43, Fisichella ran wide at turn 4, losing 8th position to Webber.
</p><p>Now released from behind Schumacher, Räikkönen was able to show the full speed of his McLaren. He increased the gap to over 20 seconds by lap 44. Button made his second and final pitstop on lap 47 from 5th place, rejoining the race in 6th. Räikkönen took his final stop on the following lap, with his lead large enough to allow him to comfortably rejoin still ahead of Schumacher. Most of the field took their final stops between laps 50 and 55, with little change in the front of the field, although Webber was able to leapfrog Sato to take 7th place. Michael Schumacher made his final stop on lap 57 from a distant second place, rejoining just 4.4 seconds ahead of his brother, who was showing impressive speed in the <a href="/wiki/Toyota_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Toyota F1">Toyota</a>. By lap 62, Räikkönen had increased his lead to over half a minute, while Ralf Schumacher closed the gap to just 2.9 seconds behind Michael. Three laps later, the gap was reduced to 1.2 seconds, with all cars except the top 6 at least one lap behind the leader Räikkönen. Albers took a lengthy stop on lap 51, but rejoined the race a few laps later, while Villeneuve retired from the race on lap 57 due to yet another small engine fire on a Sauber. Fisichella took a quick "splash-and-dash" to the finish on lap 68, but was able to hold his 9th position ahead of 10th-placed Barrichello. Räikkönen took a very important comfortable victory ahead of Michael Schumacher who was able to withstand the late charge from Ralf Schumacher, who scored his first podium finish of the year, and his first for Toyota. Fourth place went to Trulli, ahead of Jenson Button, followed by the Williams' of Heidfeld and Webber, with Sato scoring his first point of the year in 8th. Alonso finished in a disappointing 11th position.
</p><p>The result meant that Räikkönen moved to within 26 points of Alonso with 6 races remaining, and a good result next race should give him a chance at the championship, given McLaren's apparent superior speed. Michael Schumacher sits in 3rd place on 55 points, while the following six drivers will all be out of the championship race if they fail to score in the <a href="/wiki/Turkish_Grand_Prix" title="Turkish Grand Prix">Turkish Grand Prix</a>. McLaren reduced the gap to Renault to just 12 points in the constructor's championship, followed by Ferrari, another 19 points in arrears. BAR took their fourth consecutive points haul of the year, but still lie a disappointing (by 2004's standards) 7th place.
</p>
