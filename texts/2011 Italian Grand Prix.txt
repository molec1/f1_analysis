<p>The <b>2011 Italian Grand Prix</b>, formally known as the <b>2011 Gran Premio Santander d'Italia</b>, was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Auto_racing" title="Auto racing">motor race</a> that was held on 11 September 2011 at the <a href="/wiki/Autodromo_Nazionale_di_Monza" title="Autodromo Nazionale di Monza">Autodromo Nazionale di Monza</a> in <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-WMSC031110_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WMSC031110-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> It was the thirteenth round of the <a href="/wiki/2011_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2011 Formula One season">2011 Formula One season</a> and was also the sixtieth time the <a href="/wiki/Italian_Grand_Prix" title="Italian Grand Prix">Italian Grand Prix</a> had been held at Monza. The 53-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a>, the World Drivers' Championship leader, after he started from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>. <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> finished in second place for <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> completed the podium in third position for <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Race_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Race-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As a consequence of the race, Vettel extended his lead in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">World Drivers' Championship</a> to 112&#160;points over Alonso, who moved up to second place in the championship. Button moved into third place in the championship, five&#160;points behind Alonso, and level on points with fourth-placed <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a>, but ahead on countback. In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">World Constructors' Championship</a>, Red Bull's championship lead was cut by McLaren to 126&#160;points, with Ferrari a further 71&#160;points behind in third position.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Tyre supplier <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_tyres" title="Formula One tyres">Pirelli</a> brought its white-banded medium compound tyre as the harder "prime" tyre and the yellow-banded soft compound as the softer "option" compound, as opposed to the previous year where Bridgestone brought the hard compound as the prime.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Regulation_changes">Regulation changes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Regulation changes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The Belgian Grand Prix saw a small controversy when several teams discovered their tyres had blistered during qualifying, but were not permitted to change their tyres ahead of the race because the damage was the result of the car set-up rather than an accident.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Tyre supplier <a href="/wiki/Pirelli" title="Pirelli">Pirelli</a> pointed to <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a>'s practice of running camber settings that were outside the recommended parameters given by Pirelli as the cause of the blistering,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> and said they would be more cautious with their recommendations for Monza to prevent the problem from arising again.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Pirelli stated that they were willing to turn to the FIA to enforce camber limits if there was any evidence of blistering after the Free Practice sessions.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Shortly before the final practice session on Saturday, the FIA announced that Pirelli's camber limits were mandatory and that any team who failed to observe them would be reported to the stewards under Article 2.3 of the sport's technical regulations for dangerous construction and would risk exclusion from the race.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Circuit_changes">Circuit changes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Circuit changes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>After experimenting with two Drag Reduction System (DRS) zones with one activation point in <a href="/wiki/2011_Canadian_Grand_Prix" title="2011 Canadian Grand Prix">Montreal</a> and <a href="/wiki/2011_European_Grand_Prix" title="2011 European Grand Prix">Valencia</a>, the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">FIA</a> reverted to a single DRS zone for <a href="/wiki/2011_British_Grand_Prix" title="2011 British Grand Prix">Silverstone</a>. At the <a href="/wiki/2011_Belgian_Grand_Prix" title="2011 Belgian Grand Prix">Belgian Grand Prix</a>, it was announced that the Italian Grand Prix would once again see two DRS zones, but this time, each zone would be independent, with one activation point for each zone.<sup id="cite_ref-Cooper_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cooper-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> One of these zones will be placed along the main straight of the circuit, with reports suggesting that the second zone would be placed along the straight between the second Curva di Lesmo and the Variante Ascari chicane.<sup id="cite_ref-Cooper_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cooper-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On Saturday morning, the FIA modified the della Roggia chicane, moving the kerbs at both corners back five metres to allow the drivers more space to navigate the chicane without being forced over the kerbs.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Team_changes">Team changes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Team changes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Nick_Heidfeld_20110220_Catalunya.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d4/Nick_Heidfeld_20110220_Catalunya.jpg/220px-Nick_Heidfeld_20110220_Catalunya.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="339" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d4/Nick_Heidfeld_20110220_Catalunya.jpg/330px-Nick_Heidfeld_20110220_Catalunya.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d4/Nick_Heidfeld_20110220_Catalunya.jpg/440px-Nick_Heidfeld_20110220_Catalunya.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1428" data-file-height="2202" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Nick_Heidfeld_20110220_Catalunya.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a> team announced that <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> would be replaced for the rest of the season, by <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Senna" title="Bruno Senna">Bruno Senna</a>.</div></div></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Bruno_Senna" title="Bruno Senna">Bruno Senna</a> replaced <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> at <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a> for the Belgian Grand Prix, though Heidfeld expressed dissatisfaction with the appointment and stated that he hoped to return to racing for them at the <a href="/wiki/2011_Singapore_Grand_Prix" title="2011 Singapore Grand Prix">Singapore Grand Prix</a> and to finish the season driving for Renault, going so far to launch legal action against the team. In the week before the Italian Grand Prix, it was announced that Heidfeld and Renault had reached a settlement, allowing Senna to complete the season in Heidfeld's place.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Senna's appointment prompted four new sponsors – Brazilian telecommunications company <a href="/wiki/Embratel" title="Embratel">Embratel</a>, oil and natural gas company <a href="/wiki/EBX_Group" title="EBX Group">OGX</a>, personal care brand <a href="/wiki/Gillette_(brand)" class="mw-redirect" title="Gillette (brand)">Gillette</a> and pharmaceutical company Auden McKenzie – to join the team.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After shelving a planned upgrade for the <a href="/wiki/2011_British_Grand_Prix" title="2011 British Grand Prix">British Grand Prix</a> in light of a technical review of the team,<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Racing" title="Virgin Racing">Virgin Racing</a> decided to re-introduce the upgrade for the Italian Grand Prix.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> The upgrade is a complete overhaul of the <a href="/wiki/Virgin_MVR-02" title="Virgin MVR-02">Virgin MVR-02</a>, including a new engine cover, sidepods, exhaust and rear floor.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Standings">Standings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Standings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Heading into the race, <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> was leading the Drivers' Championship on 259 points - a massive 92 ahead of <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a> teammate <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a>, after the pair had a 1-2 finish in <a href="/wiki/2011_Belgian_Grand_Prix" title="2011 Belgian Grand Prix">Spa</a>. <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> sat third in the standings, 10 points behind Webber on 157. The <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> duo of <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> were now occupying fourth and fifth in the standings, with 149 and 146 points respectively. Hamilton had slipped to fifth, behind Button, because of his collision at the last race with <a href="/wiki/Kamui_Kobayashi" title="Kamui Kobayashi">Kamui Kobayashi</a>.
</p><p>Red Bull led the Constructors' Championship, in a similar dominant fashion to Vettel, with 426 points. McLaren were 131 points behind on 295 points and <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> were third placed, 64 points behind McLaren. <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a> had quite a gap to Ferrari, and were fourth and fifth placed, on 98 and 68 points respectively.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Free_Practice">Free Practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Free Practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Friday practice sessions were dry, with predictions of similar weather to last the entire weekend. The first ninety-minute period was quiet, with just two drivers setting times in the first half-hour. Once the entire grid started setting times, <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> emerged as the early leader of the session. However, <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> rapidly improved his lap times, and would end the session as the fastest man on track, a second clear of teammate <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> and a further half-second ahead of Vettel. Button reported that the circuit had been resurfaced since the <a href="/wiki/2010_Italian_Grand_Prix" title="2010 Italian Grand Prix">2010 race</a>, causing the cars to slide about.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Meanwhile, several cars – most notably the <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus_(2010%E2%80%9311)" title="Team Lotus (2010–11)">Lotus</a> and <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Racing" title="Virgin Racing">Virgin</a> entries – were plagued with technical problems.
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:K_Chandhok_Monza_2011.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ab/K_Chandhok_Monza_2011.jpg/220px-K_Chandhok_Monza_2011.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="146" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ab/K_Chandhok_Monza_2011.jpg/330px-K_Chandhok_Monza_2011.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ab/K_Chandhok_Monza_2011.jpg/440px-K_Chandhok_Monza_2011.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4592" data-file-height="3056" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:K_Chandhok_Monza_2011.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Karun_Chandhok" title="Karun Chandhok">Karun Chandhok</a> drove for <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus_(2010%E2%80%9311)" title="Team Lotus (2010–11)">Team Lotus</a> in the first free practice session.</div></div></div>
<p>Vettel returned to the top of the timing sheets in the second session, just three hundredths of a second faster than Hamilton. However, Hamilton encountered <a href="/wiki/Jaime_Alguersuari" title="Jaime Alguersuari">Jaime Alguersuari</a> whilst setting his fastest lap time, denying him the fastest time of the session. Alguersuari himself was impeded by <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a>, forcing the Spanish driver to move onto the grassy verge to avoid a collision. His <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso</a> teammate <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9bastien_Buemi" title="Sébastien Buemi">Sébastien Buemi</a> also left the circuit, crashing as he exited the Parabolica. Several other drivers experienced technical difficulties that limited their running; <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> was only able to set a lap time three minutes from the end of the session after an electrical fault sidelined him, while <a href="/wiki/Kamui_Kobayashi" title="Kamui Kobayashi">Kamui Kobayashi</a> pulled over at the end of the session and <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> struggled with an undiagnosed problem in his <a href="/wiki/Mercedes_MGP_W02" title="Mercedes MGP W02">Mercedes</a>.
</p><p>Despite setting the fastest lap time in the second session, telemetry data from the speed trap on the main straight demonstrated that Vettel was noticeably slower than his rivals. <a href="/wiki/Vitaly_Petrov" title="Vitaly Petrov">Vitaly Petrov</a> was the fastest through the speed trap, recording a top speed of 347&#160;km/h (216&#160;mph); by comparison, Vettel was the slowest driver on the circuit, with a top speed of 327.9&#160;km/h (203.7&#160;mph), 19.1&#160;km/h (11.9&#160;mph) slower than Petrov.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The unique twin DRS zones around the circuit provided a challenge for the teams. The Drag Reduction System meant that drivers could run higher downforce settings than they usually would, offering more grip in the corners and unlimited DRS usage in the straights. Such a setup would invariably favour qualifying as the restricted use of DRS in the race could potentially compromise the driver's position. At the same time, a more-traditional setup would favour the race, but at the cost of qualifying position. This unique predicament forced many of the teams to run both setups in Free Practice to make a decision ahead of the race.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:2011_Italian_GP_-_Vettel.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ab/2011_Italian_GP_-_Vettel.jpg/220px-2011_Italian_GP_-_Vettel.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="102" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ab/2011_Italian_GP_-_Vettel.jpg/330px-2011_Italian_GP_-_Vettel.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ab/2011_Italian_GP_-_Vettel.jpg/440px-2011_Italian_GP_-_Vettel.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4000" data-file-height="1854" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:2011_Italian_GP_-_Vettel.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> took pole position, at a circuit where <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a> have never had a front row start before, by half a second from <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>Qualifying began in dry conditions at Monza, and the first qualifying period saw <a href="/wiki/Pastor_Maldonado" title="Pastor Maldonado">Pastor Maldonado</a> crashing to the barriers at Parabolica early on. He was able to pit for a new front wing and return to the circuit, advancing to the second period at the expense of <a href="/wiki/Jaime_Alguersuari" title="Jaime Alguersuari">Jaime Alguersuari</a>, who was eliminated alongside the <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus_(2010%E2%80%9311)" title="Team Lotus (2010–11)">Lotus</a>, <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Racing" title="Virgin Racing">Virgin</a> and <a href="/wiki/HRT_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="HRT F1">HRT</a> entries. <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> out-qualified team mate <a href="/wiki/Vitantonio_Liuzzi" title="Vitantonio Liuzzi">Vitantonio Liuzzi</a> for the first time, while <a href="/wiki/Timo_Glock" title="Timo Glock">Timo Glock</a> narrowly bested team mate <a href="/wiki/J%C3%A9r%C3%B4me_d%27Ambrosio" title="Jérôme d&#39;Ambrosio">Jérôme d'Ambrosio</a>, despite problems with his rear wing. <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> out-qualified <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> for just the second time in <a href="/wiki/2011_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2011 Formula One World Championship">2011</a>.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:P_Maldonado_Monza_2011.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7b/P_Maldonado_Monza_2011.jpg/220px-P_Maldonado_Monza_2011.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="146" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7b/P_Maldonado_Monza_2011.jpg/330px-P_Maldonado_Monza_2011.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7b/P_Maldonado_Monza_2011.jpg/440px-P_Maldonado_Monza_2011.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4592" data-file-height="3056" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:P_Maldonado_Monza_2011.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Pastor_Maldonado" title="Pastor Maldonado">Pastor Maldonado</a> crashed in Q1 breaking the car's nose, but still managed to set a fast enough time to progress to Q2, eventually qualifying fourteenth.</div></div></div>
<p>The second qualifying period was marked by the midfield teams attempting to advance to Q3. The <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bulls</a>, <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLarens</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferraris</a> easily progressed with <a href="/wiki/Vitaly_Petrov" title="Vitaly Petrov">Vitaly Petrov</a> in ninth, just one thousandth of a second behind Hamilton. Hamilton, who had initially used the prime tyres, returned to the circuit on the options, due to uncertainty of the security of his time, though Petrov remained in his garage. The remaining drivers were all competing for the final spot in Q3. <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a> briefly held tenth before being passed by <a href="/wiki/Paul_di_Resta" title="Paul di Resta">Paul di Resta</a>, and di Resta was outqualified in turn by <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Senna" title="Bruno Senna">Bruno Senna</a> by a margin of 0.006 seconds. Behind the <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force Indias</a>, were the <a href="/wiki/Williams_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams F1">Williamses</a> of <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pastor_Maldonado" title="Pastor Maldonado">Pastor Maldonado</a>, with <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9bastien_Buemi" title="Sébastien Buemi">Sébastien Buemi</a> sandwiched by the <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Saubers</a> of Pérez and Kobayashi.
</p><p>In the final ten-minute part, <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> sent both cars out together to give both drivers an opportunity to tow each other to a higher grid spot, though the strategy largely failed. <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> followed with their second runs being more crucial; Hamilton made a mistake at the Variante Roggia, which caused <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> to slow down. Jenson Button pitted after a mistake in the Parabolica, but qualified third behind Hamilton, by five hundredths of a second. There was a larger margin to Vettel though, who was half a second faster than Hamilton. It was Vettel's tenth pole of the season – joining <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> as the only other driver to have taken ten pole positions in two separate seasons – and the 25th of his career.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Alonso, Webber, Massa, Petrov, Schumacher, Rosberg and Senna – who did not set a lap time in the session – completed the first five rows of the grid.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Fernando Alonso was the first driver into the first corner, having made the best start off the line from fourth. However, his lead was short-lived; further down the field, <a href="/wiki/Vitantonio_Liuzzi" title="Vitantonio Liuzzi">Vitantonio Liuzzi</a> made contact with <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> and slid off across the grass and directly into <a href="/wiki/Vitaly_Petrov" title="Vitaly Petrov">Vitaly Petrov</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a>. The three cars retired on the spot, while <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> escaped without terminal damage, but was forced to pit for a new front wing, ruining his race. The safety car was deployed as the debris was cleared up, and when racing resumed, <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> quickly claimed the lead from Alonso. Vettel would remain unchallenged for the rest of the race, claiming his eighteenth victory. Meanwhile, <a href="/wiki/J%C3%A9r%C3%B4me_d%27Ambrosio" title="Jérôme d&#39;Ambrosio">Jérôme d'Ambrosio</a> retired on the first lap with a gearbox problem.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:M_Schumacher_Monza_2011.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/88/M_Schumacher_Monza_2011.jpg/220px-M_Schumacher_Monza_2011.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="146" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/88/M_Schumacher_Monza_2011.jpg/330px-M_Schumacher_Monza_2011.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/88/M_Schumacher_Monza_2011.jpg/440px-M_Schumacher_Monza_2011.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4592" data-file-height="3056" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:M_Schumacher_Monza_2011.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> finished fifth, having held <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> behind him for the majority of the race.</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:M_Webber_2_Monza_2011.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f8/M_Webber_2_Monza_2011.jpg/220px-M_Webber_2_Monza_2011.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="146" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f8/M_Webber_2_Monza_2011.jpg/330px-M_Webber_2_Monza_2011.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f8/M_Webber_2_Monza_2011.jpg/440px-M_Webber_2_Monza_2011.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4592" data-file-height="3056" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:M_Webber_2_Monza_2011.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> suffered <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull's</a> first retirement of <a href="/wiki/2011_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2011 Formula One World Championship">2011</a> when he attempted to take his car back to the pits following a collision with <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>Further down the grid, <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> was caught unawares by the restart and was quickly passed by <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>, whilst <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> challenged <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> for sixth place going into the first chicane. The two made contact and Massa was spun around, but was able to continue racing; for his part, Webber tried to limp back to the pits with a broken front wing, but crashed out at the Parabolica, recording <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a>'s first retirement of the season. <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a> joined the growing list of retirements several laps later with hydraulic problems, pulling his <a href="/wiki/Force_India_VJM04" title="Force India VJM04">Force India VJM04</a> over at the Ascari chicane.
</p><p>As Vettel and Alonso increased their leads, Hamilton began to threaten Schumacher for third. Schumacher was warned over the radio several times for blocking by team principal <a href="/wiki/Ross_Brawn" title="Ross Brawn">Ross Brawn</a>; Schumacher was taking a defensive line going into the Ascari chicane before cutting back over to the racing line. The battle with Hamilton slowed both drivers down enough that fifth-placed <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> could catch up to them, and things came to a head when Schumacher cut Hamilton off at the Curva Grande and forcing him onto the grass on the inside of the corner. As Hamilton backed off, Button was able to pass him. Where Hamilton's fight with Schumacher would last thirty laps, Button passed the <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> driver on his first attempt, leaving him free to pursue Alonso. Hamilton claimed fourth from Schumacher when the German made his first scheduled stop.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Sauber_C30" title="Sauber C30">Sauber C30s</a> of <a href="/wiki/Kamui_Kobayashi" title="Kamui Kobayashi">Kamui Kobayashi</a> and seventh-placed <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a> retired with near-identical gearbox problems, reducing the field to just fifteen drivers plus <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a>. Ricciardo's car had gone into anti-stall on the grid before failing to engage a gear. The car was immediately returned to the pits while repairs were carried out, and although Ricciardo returned to the circuit, he was some eight laps behind the last-placed <a href="/wiki/Timo_Glock" title="Timo Glock">Timo Glock</a> at the time of Pérez's retirement. Ricciardo would ultimately finish the race fourteen laps behind race winner Vettel, and was therefore not classified as a finisher as he had failed to complete 90% of the winner's race distance. With just fifteen drivers on the track, the <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus_(2010%E2%80%9311)" title="Team Lotus (2010–11)">Team Lotus</a> drivers of <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> were able to secure 13th and 14th place, further reinforcing the team's claim to tenth in the World Constructors' Championship.
</p><p>Vettel went on to win the race by 9.5 seconds from Jenson Button, who had caught and passed Alonso with less than ten laps to go. Once freed from behind Schumacher's Mercedes, Lewis Hamilton started catching Alonso at a rate that meant the 2008 World Champion would only be able to pass his former teammate on the last lap. Ultimately, it was not to be; Alonso completed the podium, crossing the finish line half a second ahead of Hamilton. Jenson Button scored his third consecutive second-place finish and fourth podium at Monza. After finishing fifth in <a href="/wiki/2011_Belgian_Grand_Prix" title="2011 Belgian Grand Prix">Belgium</a>, Schumacher repeated his performance with another fifth place, in front of Massa, whose race had largely been ruined by the early contact with Webber. <a href="/wiki/Jaime_Alguersuari" title="Jaime Alguersuari">Jaime Alguersuari</a> scored a career-best finish with seventh place, ahead of <a href="/wiki/Paul_di_Resta" title="Paul di Resta">Paul di Resta</a>. The four points di Resta earned for eighth place, plus the double retirement of the Saubers helped elevate <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a> to sixth in the constructors' standings. <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Senna" title="Bruno Senna">Bruno Senna</a> scored his first World Championship points in ninth place, whilst <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9bastien_Buemi" title="Sébastien Buemi">Sébastien Buemi</a> claimed the final World Championship point.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Vettel's win extended his World Championship lead to 112 points ahead of Alonso, who took advantage of Webber's retirement to move into second overall. Vettel's result meant that a win in <a href="/wiki/2011_Singapore_Grand_Prix" title="2011 Singapore Grand Prix">Singapore</a> would be enough to secure his second <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers_Champions" class="mw-redirect" title="List of Formula One World Drivers Champions">World Drivers' Championship</a> and become the sport's <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_records#Other_driver_records" title="List of Formula One records">youngest double World Champion</a>, provided Alonso does not finish second or third, and neither Button nor Webber finishes second. Button's second place moved him up to third overall in the points standings, though Hamilton's result was not enough to surpass the retired Webber, leaving him fifth overall and the last driver with a mathematical possibility of winning the championship, though several drivers<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> conceded that they would not be able to beat Sebastian Vettel.
</p><p>For causing the first corner incident, <a href="/wiki/Vitantonio_Liuzzi" title="Vitantonio Liuzzi">Vitantonio Liuzzi</a> was given a five-place grid penalty for the <a href="/wiki/2011_Singapore_Grand_Prix" title="2011 Singapore Grand Prix">Singapore Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
