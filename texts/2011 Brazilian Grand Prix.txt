<p>The <b>2011 Brazilian Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>Formula 1 Grande Prêmio Petrobras do Brasil 2011</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 27 November 2011 at the <a href="/wiki/Aut%C3%B3dromo_Jos%C3%A9_Carlos_Pace" title="Autódromo José Carlos Pace">Autódromo José Carlos Pace</a>, <a href="/wiki/Interlagos" title="Interlagos">Interlagos</a>, in <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo" title="São Paulo">São Paulo</a>, Brazil. It was the nineteenth and final round of the <a href="/wiki/2011_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2011 Formula One season">2011 Formula One season</a>. The 71-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a>. <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a>, Webber's teammate finished in second place to complete Red Bull's third 1–2 of the season; <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> finished in third position, to complete the podium for the <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> team.
</p><p>It was the 326th and final race for veteran and most experienced Formula One driver in history, <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a>, and the 256th and last race for <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a>. This would prove to be the final Grand Prix for <a href="/wiki/Vitantonio_Liuzzi" title="Vitantonio Liuzzi">Vitantonio Liuzzi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jaime_Alguersuari" title="Jaime Alguersuari">Jaime Alguersuari</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After replacing <a href="/wiki/Jaime_Alguersuari" title="Jaime Alguersuari">Jaime Alguersuari</a> during the first Friday practice session in <a href="/wiki/2011_Korean_Grand_Prix" title="2011 Korean Grand Prix">South Korea</a> and <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9bastien_Buemi" title="Sébastien Buemi">Sébastien Buemi</a> during the same session in <a href="/wiki/2011_Abu_Dhabi_Grand_Prix" title="2011 Abu Dhabi Grand Prix">Abu Dhabi</a>, <a href="/wiki/2011_Formula_Renault_3.5_Series_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2011 Formula Renault 3.5 Series season">Formula Renault 3.5</a> runner-up <a href="/wiki/Jean-%C3%89ric_Vergne" title="Jean-Éric Vergne">Jean-Éric Vergne</a> once again drove for <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso</a> on Friday morning, after a statement announced he would take the place of "whichever of the Toro Rosso drivers has the least points";<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Vergne ultimately replaced Buemi.<sup id="cite_ref-FP1_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FP1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Having completed the sufficient amount of mileage in order to acquire an <a href="/wiki/FIA_Super_Licence" title="FIA Super Licence">FIA Super Licence</a> at the Young Driver Test in <a href="/wiki/Yas_Marina_Circuit" title="Yas Marina Circuit">Abu Dhabi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jan_Charouz" title="Jan Charouz">Jan Charouz</a> took part in first practice for <a href="/wiki/HRT_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="HRT F1">HRT</a>, replacing <a href="/wiki/Vitantonio_Liuzzi" title="Vitantonio Liuzzi">Vitantonio Liuzzi</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FP1_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FP1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Romain_Grosjean" title="Romain Grosjean">Romain Grosjean</a> drove in the place of <a href="/wiki/Vitaly_Petrov" title="Vitaly Petrov">Vitaly Petrov</a> at <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a>, while <a href="/wiki/Luiz_Razia" title="Luiz Razia">Luiz Razia</a> replaced <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> at <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus_(2010%E2%80%9311)" title="Team Lotus (2010–11)">Lotus</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a> drove <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FP1_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FP1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> With Liuzzi and Trulli on the sidelines, the first practice session became the first time since the <a href="/wiki/2005_United_States_Grand_Prix" title="2005 United States Grand Prix">2005 United States Grand Prix</a> that an Italian driver had not taken part in a timed session.
</p><p>The circuit included one Drag Reduction System (DRS) zone, located along Reta Oposta.<sup id="cite_ref-DRS_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DRS-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The detection point was located in the middle of the second corner, with the activation point 70 metres (230&#160;ft) beyond the exit of Curva do Sol, creating a DRS zone of 600 metres (2,000&#160;ft). According to FIA race director <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Whiting" title="Charlie Whiting">Charlie Whiting</a>, Reta Oposta was chosen for the DRS zone because "the main straight usually gives a good enough opportunity to overtake anyway, [and] we don't want to make it too easy [to pass]".<sup id="cite_ref-DRS_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DRS-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After experimenting with a new compound of soft tyre during free practice in <a href="/wiki/2011_Abu_Dhabi_Grand_Prix" title="2011 Abu Dhabi Grand Prix">Abu Dhabi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pirelli" title="Pirelli">Pirelli</a> announced that the tyre would be used for the race in Brazil.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:2011_Brazil_GP_-_Barrichello.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/74/2011_Brazil_GP_-_Barrichello.jpg/220px-2011_Brazil_GP_-_Barrichello.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/74/2011_Brazil_GP_-_Barrichello.jpg/330px-2011_Brazil_GP_-_Barrichello.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/74/2011_Brazil_GP_-_Barrichello.jpg/440px-2011_Brazil_GP_-_Barrichello.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3639" data-file-height="2430" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:2011_Brazil_GP_-_Barrichello.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> finished fourteenth for <a href="/wiki/Williams_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams F1">Williams</a> in his 326th and final race in Formula One.</div></div></div>
<p>Vettel took pole position, his fifteenth of the season, breaking <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a>'s record from <a href="/wiki/1992_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1992 Formula One World Championship">1992</a>, for the number of pole positions in a season. Webber qualified in second place, just one tenth of a second slower than his Red Bull teammate. The two McLarens took over the second row of the grid, with Button ahead of Hamilton. <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> were on the third row of the grid, separated by half a second. <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a>, Alonso's <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> teammate, qualified seventh ahead of <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Senna" title="Bruno Senna">Bruno Senna</a> and <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> (who didn't set a time).
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:2011_Brazil_GP_-_Massa.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/67/2011_Brazil_GP_-_Massa.jpg/220px-2011_Brazil_GP_-_Massa.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/67/2011_Brazil_GP_-_Massa.jpg/330px-2011_Brazil_GP_-_Massa.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/67/2011_Brazil_GP_-_Massa.jpg/440px-2011_Brazil_GP_-_Massa.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2977" data-file-height="1995" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:2011_Brazil_GP_-_Massa.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> became the first <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> driver since <a href="/wiki/Ivan_Capelli" title="Ivan Capelli">Ivan Capelli</a> in <a href="/wiki/1992_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1992 Formula One World Championship">1992</a> to complete a season without a podium.</div></div></div>
<p>Vettel lead the race after the start, but conceded the lead to Webber when his gearbox started causing him trouble. Vettel remained second in the race though, while Webber took his first, and only, win of the season. None of the other teams could match the pace of Red Bull in the race. Button managed to complete the podium with third position after being overtaken by Alonso early on in the race, but repassing him near the end. Alonso eventually finished fourth whilst Hamilton retired with a gearbox issue. Massa completed the year with a fifth-place finish. Sutil, Rosberg, di Resta, Kobayashi and Petrov completed the points scoring positions.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Bruno_Senna" title="Bruno Senna">Bruno Senna</a> received a drive through penalty after colliding with <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> at the entry of the "Senna S" on lap 10.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Jenson Button's podium meant that he stayed second in the Drivers' Championship, while Webber's victory meant he overtook Alonso to become third. Despite both their drivers finishing well inside the points scoring positions, <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a> did not manage to overturn Renault's points total to take fifth place in the standings, and were left just four points behind. <a href="/wiki/Kamui_Kobayashi" title="Kamui Kobayashi">Kamui Kobayashi</a> scored two points for Sauber to ensure that they kept seventh place in the standings as well.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Sebastian Vettel broke his own points record from the season before with 392 and he had the biggest margin ever to second-placed driver Jenson Button with 122, nearly doubling Michael Schumacher’s gap in 2002 to Rubens Barrichello (67).</li>
<li>Red Bull-Renault also broke their own record from 2011 with a 650-point total and eclipsed Ferrari's record from 2004 with a 153-point difference to second-placed McLaren-Mercedes.</li></ul>
