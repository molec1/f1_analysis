<p>The <b>2019 Belgian Grand Prix</b> (formally known as the <b>Formula 1 Johnnie Walker Belgian Grand Prix 2019</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_racing" title="Formula One racing">Formula One motor race</a> that was held on 1 September 2019 at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Spa-Francorchamps" title="Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps">Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps</a> in <a href="/wiki/Stavelot" title="Stavelot">Stavelot</a>, <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a>. The race was the 13th round of the <a href="/wiki/2019_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2019 Formula One World Championship">2019 Formula One World Championship</a> and marked the 75th running of the <a href="/wiki/Belgian_Grand_Prix" title="Belgian Grand Prix">Belgian Grand Prix</a>, the 52nd time as a round of the World Championship and the 62nd time the race had been held at Spa-Francorchamps.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Charles_Leclerc" title="Charles Leclerc">Charles Leclerc</a> won the race, becoming the first <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_drivers_from_Monaco" title="Formula One drivers from Monaco">Monegasque driver</a> to <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_Grand_Prix_winners" title="List of Formula One Grand Prix winners">win a Formula One Grand Prix</a> followed by the <a href="/wiki/Mercedes_in_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Mercedes in F1">Mercedes</a> drivers of <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a>. Before and during the race there were several tributes in memory of French driver <a href="/wiki/Anthoine_Hubert" title="Anthoine Hubert">Anthoine Hubert</a> who had died the previous day during the <a href="/wiki/2019_Spa-Francorchamps_FIA_Formula_2_round" class="mw-redirect" title="2019 Spa-Francorchamps FIA Formula 2 round">F2 feature race</a>. <a href="/wiki/Charles_Leclerc" title="Charles Leclerc">Charles Leclerc</a>, long time friend of Hubert, dedicated the victory to him.
</p>

<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Further information: <a href="/wiki/2019_Formula_One_World_Championship#Entries" title="2019 Formula One World Championship">2019 Formula One World Championship §&#160;Entries</a></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Alexander_Albon" title="Alexander Albon">Alexander Albon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pierre_Gasly" title="Pierre Gasly">Pierre Gasly</a> swapped their seats for the race, with Albon taking Gasly's place at <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a> and Gasly returning to <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Scuderia Toro Rosso</a> to fill the seat vacated by Albon.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The rest of the entrants remained unchanged from the <a href="/wiki/2019_Hungarian_Grand_Prix" title="2019 Hungarian Grand Prix">Hungarian Grand Prix</a> although  <a href="/wiki/Nicholas_Latifi" title="Nicholas Latifi">Nicholas Latifi</a> drove in the first practice session for <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a>, replacing <a href="/wiki/George_Russell_(racing_driver)" title="George Russell (racing driver)">George Russell</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Marcus_Ericsson" title="Marcus Ericsson">Marcus Ericsson</a> did not attend the <a href="/wiki/2019_Grand_Prix_of_Portland" title="2019 Grand Prix of Portland">IndyCar Grand Prix of Portland</a> and travelled to Belgium in case he was needed to stand in for <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> who had suffered a leg injury over the summer break, but ultimately Räikkönen was able to race.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_report">Race report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2019_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Race report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The death of <a href="/wiki/Anthoine_Hubert" title="Anthoine Hubert">Anthoine Hubert</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2019_Spa-Francorchamps_FIA_Formula_2_round" class="mw-redirect" title="2019 Spa-Francorchamps FIA Formula 2 round">Formula 2 feature race</a> on Saturday loomed over the race. A <a href="/wiki/Minute_of_silence" class="mw-redirect" title="Minute of silence">minute of silence</a> was held for Hubert before the start of the race and a standing ovation was organised for lap 19, the same number as his car.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At the start of the race <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Sainz_Jr." title="Carlos Sainz Jr.">Carlos Sainz</a> suffered a power failure and did not get off the line. Whilst <a href="/wiki/Charles_Leclerc" title="Charles Leclerc">Charles Leclerc</a> got away cleanly, third placed <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> was able to attack second placed <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> on the inside of turn one, pushing the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> off the track, allowing Hamilton to take second place. However, a poor run through turn 5 and the higher top speed of the Ferrari meant that Vettel was able to recover the position on the straight that followed.  Although he briefly threatened Vettel when the Ferrari was forced wide after turn 1, <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a> settled into fourth behind Hamilton. Behind the first four, <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> was on the outside of <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a> on the start-finish straight and did not see <a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a>, who had a poor start, on inside and turned into the <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a> driver at the first corner. Both drivers suffered damage with Verstappen suffering suspension damage, causing him to crash into the barriers on the outside of turn 4.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> The collision caused the <a href="/wiki/Safety_car" title="Safety car">safety car</a> to be released.
</p><p>Due to the incident, the cars further back had to pick their way around the debris. <a href="/wiki/Lando_Norris" title="Lando Norris">Lando Norris</a>, who had started eleventh, managed to move into fifth. <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> was not so lucky as he was tagged on the rear right-hand side by <a href="/wiki/Lance_Stroll" title="Lance Stroll">Lance Stroll</a>. The Verstappen-Räikkönen incident led to the safety car being deployed and Sainz, Räikkönen and Ricciardo all made pit stops. Sainz, suffering with a power issue, had a very slow stop, as did Räikkönen when his pit crew could not secure his new front wing. Räikkönen then stopped again at the end of lap 2. Sainz continued to be slow and did not catch up the pack after his pit stop, eventually pulling off in the run off area at the final chicane at end of his second lap. This caused the safety car, which was about to be brought back into the pits, to stay out.
</p><p>The safety car came in for the start of lap 5 and the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferraris</a> managed to open a gap to <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> cars on the restart. Leclerc quickly managed to pull out of <a href="/wiki/Drag_reduction_system" title="Drag reduction system">DRS</a> range but Vettel stayed ahead of Hamilton who attacked several times before the first pit stops. Radio messages between Hamiton and his engineer revealed that Hamilton did not think Vettel was that quick. However, whilst the Mercedes cars were much stronger than the Ferraris in the second sector, they were not as fast in the first or third sectors, both of which contained the key overtaking spots of the turn 7 and turn 19 respectively.
</p><p>With Norris running in fifth and <a href="/wiki/Romain_Grosjean" title="Romain Grosjean">Romain Grosjean</a> in sixth, <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Magnussen" title="Kevin Magnussen">Kevin Magnussen</a> became embroiled in a fight with Pérez over seventh place. The two drivers battled over several laps but eventually Pérez overtook Magnussen on the Kemmel straight on lap 10 and the <a href="/wiki/Haas_F1_Team" title="Haas F1 Team">Haas</a> of Magnussen then quickly fell backwards. <a href="/wiki/Pierre_Gasly" title="Pierre Gasly">Pierre Gasly</a> overtook Magnussen towards end of Kemmel straight on the next lap with Stroll doing the same one lap later.
</p><p>Gasly pitted on lap 13 and was around a second faster than his new <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso</a> teammate <a href="/wiki/Daniil_Kvyat" title="Daniil Kvyat">Daniil Kvyat</a>, showing that <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_motorsport_terms#U" title="Glossary of motorsport terms">undercut</a> could be quite powerful. This led Ferrari to pit Vettel at end of lap 15 for medium tyres. There was a brief window for Mercedes to respond but they chose not to instead pushing on, as did Leclerc. Vettel then set a fastest lap on 17, which included an overtake on Norris, and started taking significant time out of Leclerc, Hamilton and Bottas who had still not pitted. The top three were all on one stop strategies so instead were stretching their tyres. Leclerc pitted at end of 21, Hamilton at end of 22, and Bottas at end of 23. Hamilton lost a second during his stop due to a slow left rear tyre and emerged several seconds behind Leclerc. All three had been jumped by Vettel on pit stop strategy however Leclerc then set a fastest lap on lap 23 and, along with the Mercedes cars, started catching Vettel as tyre degradation took its toll. It quickly became clear that Vettel would likely end up stopping a second time.
</p><p>At the start of lap 27, Vettel allowed Leclerc to overtake. Hamilton quickly closed on Vettel but struggled to overtake due to the sector speed differences. Eventually, on lap 32, having closed up in sector three on the previous lap, Hamilton was able to make a move into turn seven and pass Vettel but Leclerc had opened a gap of more than six seconds. When Bottas caught up to Vettel as well, Vettel pitted at end of lap 33 for soft tyres, going for the extra point for fastest lap, which he gained on lap 36.
</p><p>Behind the leaders <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Albon" title="Alexander Albon">Alexander Albon</a>, on his debut for <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a>, carried out a fine recovery race. Having taken a penalty for exceeding his quota for power unit components, he started seventeenth on the grid. When the fifteenth placed Sainz failed to get away, Albon had to take evasive action leading to a loss of position. This was rectified almost immediately in the aftermath of Verstappen-Räikkönen incident as he was able to overtake several slow and damaged cars. Pitting at end of lap 23 for soft tyres, Albon quietly gained places whilst the battle between the Mercedes and Ferraris unfolded. After passing Gasly for ninth place Albon tried to take Ricciardo on the outside of turn 10, on lap 34, but failed to overtake him. He then carried out a switch back move to pass the <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a> driver the outside of turn 11. On lap 38, Albon then passed Kvyat at start of Kemmel straight to make it up to seventh leaving him more than ten seconds behind sixth placed Pérez.
</p><p>Back with the leaders, Hamilton started making gains on Leclerc. The two drivers had to pick their way through traffic in the final laps, slowing both down. However, Hamilton managed to close to less than one second on final lap but was not close enough to make a move. As the leaders entered the second sector, <a href="/wiki/Antonio_Giovinazzi" title="Antonio Giovinazzi">Antonio Giovinazzi</a> crashed and was buried in the wall at turn 12 bringing out <a href="/wiki/Racing_flags#Yellow_flag" title="Racing flags">yellow flags</a>.  Leclerc managed to survive the last few corners to become the first driver to claim a maiden win at Spa since <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> at the <a href="/wiki/1992_Belgian_Grand_Prix" title="1992 Belgian Grand Prix">1992 Belgian Grand Prix</a>.  It was also Ferrari's first victory since Räikkönen at the <a href="/wiki/2018_United_States_Grand_Prix" title="2018 United States Grand Prix">2018 United States Grand Prix</a>, with Leclerc the Scuderia's 39th winner and <a href="/wiki/Monaco" title="Monaco">Monaco</a> the 23rd nation to win an F1 race.  Bottas finished third with Vettel in fourth.
</p><p>Lando Norris, who had run a quiet race after his first lap jump to fifth, stopped on start line on his final lap and ended up classified eleventh.  At the end of their penultimate lap, Albon had closed onto the back of Pérez at the final chicane which led to an unusual turn 1 as the two drivers slowed down excessively to try and get DRS for the Kemmel straight.  Pérez stayed ahead so Albon was able to attack with DRS after Raidillon but had to go onto the grass partway down the Kemmel straight to pass the <a href="/wiki/Racing_Point_F1_Team" title="Racing Point F1 Team">Racing Point</a> car.  The incident was investigated by the stewards after the race but no further action was taken.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>  With Albon finishing fifth, Pérez was sixth, Kvyat seventh, <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a> eight, Gasly ninth and Stroll tenth.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-bbc.co.uk_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc.co.uk-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Leclerc dedicated his win to <a href="/wiki/Anthoine_Hubert" title="Anthoine Hubert">Anthoine Hubert</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-bbc.co.uk_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc.co.uk-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>  The podium finishers carried out a notably muted celebration and did not spray champagne on the podium in respect to Hubert.
</p>
