<p>The <b>2005 Bahrain Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 3 April 2005 at the <a href="/wiki/Bahrain_International_Circuit" title="Bahrain International Circuit">Bahrain International Circuit</a> in <a href="/wiki/Sakhir" title="Sakhir">Sakhir</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bahrain" title="Bahrain">Bahrain</a>. The 57-lap race was the third round of the <a href="/wiki/2005_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2005 Formula One season">2005 Formula One season</a> and the second running of the <a href="/wiki/Bahrain_Grand_Prix" title="Bahrain Grand Prix">Bahrain Grand Prix</a>, since its <a href="/wiki/2004_Bahrain_Grand_Prix" title="2004 Bahrain Grand Prix">inception the year before</a>.
</p><p>The race was won by polesitter and championship leader <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a> team. <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> finished in second place in a <a href="/wiki/Toyota_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Toyota F1">Toyota</a> car and <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> completed the podium in third position for <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2005_Bahrain_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2005_Bahrain_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The race was only the second Formula One grand prix held in the <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a>, bringing the challenges of high temperatures and dusty conditions. The race was held the day after <a href="/wiki/Pope_John_Paul_II" title="Pope John Paul II">Pope John Paul II</a> died, with several teams and drivers offering their respects, most notably Ferrari, who raced with blackened nose cones as a mark of respect. The celebrations on the podium were also muted.
</p><p>The circuit had been modified slightly from <a href="/wiki/2004_Bahrain_Grand_Prix" title="2004 Bahrain Grand Prix">2004</a>, with turn 4 in particular being widened on the exit.
</p><p>Before the race, <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a> injured his shoulder. This led to him being replaced by <a href="/wiki/Pedro_de_la_Rosa" title="Pedro de la Rosa">Pedro de la Rosa</a> at this race, and <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Wurz" title="Alexander Wurz">Alexander Wurz</a> at the next one in <a href="/wiki/2005_San_Marino_Grand_Prix" title="2005 San Marino Grand Prix">San Marino</a>, before his return at the <a href="/wiki/2005_Spanish_Grand_Prix" title="2005 Spanish Grand Prix">Spanish Grand Prix</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2005_Bahrain_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>First qualifying on Saturday resulted in few surprises– for the first few drivers, the circuit was still somewhat sandy, providing poor grip, while the later runners performed well, with <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> taking provisional pole with a lap time of 1:29.848.
</p><p>Final qualifying on Sunday morning again passed without major incident, with Alonso taking pole position, and <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> taking second, driving the brand-new <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_F2005" title="Ferrari F2005">F2005</a> car. <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a>, having had gearbox problems on Friday and Saturday, qualified 15th, and elected to change his engine, resulting in him starting from the back of the grid.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2005_Bahrain_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Race afternoon brought the highest ever recorded temperatures experienced at a grand prix, with air temperature of 42.5°C and track temperatures of 56&#160;°C (about 132&#160;°F). This surpasses the record temperatures of the <a href="/wiki/1955_Argentine_Grand_Prix" title="1955 Argentine Grand Prix">1955 Argentine Grand Prix</a> and the <a href="/wiki/1984_Dallas_Grand_Prix" title="1984 Dallas Grand Prix">1984 Dallas Grand Prix</a>. <a href="/wiki/Christian_Klien" title="Christian Klien">Christian Klien</a> failed to get away from his seventh position on the grid for the formation lap. His car was pushed into the pits but could not be restarted, and he became the first of eight retirements.
</p><p>The leaders made a clean start, with Alonso first to turn one. Schumacher moved from his second grid slot across to the clean side of the track, ahead of <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a>, who made a strong challenge to pass Schumacher in the first two corners, without success. Barrichello made an aggressive start, moving up to tenth by the end of lap one.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a>'s engine began to smoke during lap two, and he pulled into the pits to retire. However, as he applied the pit lane speed limiter, he felt power return, and was waved through by his team. But the resurgence was short-lived, and he was back in the pits on lap four to retire. On lap three, Narain Karthikeyan's car suffered an electrical failure that looked similar to Christian Klien's.
</p><p>Schumacher continued to closely pursue Alonso until lap 12, when the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">world champion</a> overshot turn nine, and performed a 270° turn in the run-off area. At the end of the lap, Schumacher coasted back to the pits, making this his first mechanical retirement since the <a href="/wiki/2001_German_Grand_Prix" title="2001 German Grand Prix">2001 German Grand Prix</a>– a remarkable run of 58 consecutive grands prix. It later emerged that the car's hydraulics had failed, meaning he could not downshift to use engine braking for corners. Therefore, Trulli now took second place, 2.7 seconds behind Alonso, with <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> in third.
</p><p>On lap 18, <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> in fourth place made the first scheduled pit stop of the front-runners, and rejoined in 12th place. Alonso, Trulli and then Webber all pitted over the next few laps, in what appeared to be the now fairly standard three-stop pattern. After the pit stops, Alonso retained the lead, followed by Trulli, Webber, <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a>, Ralf, and Barrichello.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> was the next retirement, with a blown engine on lap 25, although it took him around half a lap to pull off the track to stop. He was shortly followed by <a href="/wiki/Takuma_Sato" title="Takuma Sato">Takuma Sato</a>, whose front brakes had been smoking for a while, and who spun and then retired in the pits on lap 27.  His teammate <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a>'s brakes also appeared to be giving off more dust than usual, as Button fought to keep <a href="/wiki/Pedro_de_la_Rosa" title="Pedro de la Rosa">Pedro de la Rosa</a> from taking his seventh place. De la Rosa was making his first start for <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>, replacing the sidelined <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a>. De la Rosa finally managed to pass Button on lap 33, outbraking him at turn one.
</p><p>The following lap, Webber lost control out of turn eight, spun on the entrance to turn nine, and allowed Räikkönen and Ralf Schumacher to pass– on a circuit with very forgiving run-off areas, the spin upset the balance of Webber's car and flat-spotted his tyres.
</p><p>In the next few laps, before the second pit stops, the closest fight was between Barrichello in sixth, and de la Rosa in seventh. De la Rosa continually pressured the remaining <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, but initially he only succeeded in pushing himself too far, running wide at turn one, and allowing Button to close up behind him. Eventually, though, he was able to take sixth place in the final corner.
</p><p>Alonso put in several fast laps to extend his lead, and pitted on lap 41. The other drivers also pitted without incident, except Button, who stalled his <a href="/wiki/British_American_Racing" title="British American Racing">BAR</a>-<a href="/wiki/Honda_Racing_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Honda Racing F1">Honda</a>. After several attempts at restarting the engine, he almost took his rear jack with him back into the race– only to retire at the end of the pit-lane due to a clutch failure, making this the second race in a row where both BAR drivers had retired. After the second round of pit stops, Alonso still led the race, followed by Trulli, Räikkönen, Ralf Schumacher, Webber, de la Rosa, Barrichello, and <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a>.
</p><p>In the closing stages, the main fight was between Webber and de la Rosa for fifth place. Webber defended strongly, but de la Rosa eventually overcame him two laps from the end of the race. <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a> retired from ninth place into the pits on the penultimate lap, while Barrichello slipped further and further back, allowing Massa to take seventh place and score points in his <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a> team's 200th Grand Prix, and <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> took eighth place on the last lap, making Ferrari scoreless for the first time since the <a href="/wiki/2003_Brazilian_Grand_Prix" title="2003 Brazilian Grand Prix">2003 Brazilian Grand Prix</a>.
</p><p>Alonso won the race by a comfortable 13.4 seconds from Trulli, bringing <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a> engines their 100th World Championship Grand Prix win.
</p>
