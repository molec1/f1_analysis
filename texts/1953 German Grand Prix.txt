<p>The <b>1953 German Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_Two" title="Formula Two">Formula Two</a> race held on 2 August 1953 at the <a href="/wiki/N%C3%BCrburgring" title="Nürburgring">Nürburgring Nordschleife</a>. It was race 7 of 9 in the <a href="/wiki/1953_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1953 World Championship of Drivers">1953 World Championship of Drivers</a>, which was run to Formula Two rules in 1952 and 1953, rather than the <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> regulations normally used.
</p><p>This race was won by <a href="/wiki/Giuseppe_Farina" title="Giuseppe Farina">Nino Farina</a> in a <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, just over 1 minute ahead of <a href="/wiki/Juan_Manuel_Fangio" title="Juan Manuel Fangio">Juan Manuel Fangio</a>. This was Farina's last victory in Formula One.
</p><p>Ferrari driver <a href="/wiki/Alberto_Ascari" title="Alberto Ascari">Alberto Ascari</a> clinched his second consecutive World Drivers' Championship as neither <a href="/wiki/Mike_Hawthorn" title="Mike Hawthorn">Mike Hawthorn</a> nor Juan Manuel Fangio won the race and now couldn't beat his points total with two races left.
</p><p>During his tour of Europe that saw Japan's 19-year old Crown Prince <a href="/wiki/Akihito" title="Akihito">Akihito</a> visit the <a href="/wiki/Coronation_of_Queen_Elizabeth_II" class="mw-redirect" title="Coronation of Queen Elizabeth II">Coronation of Queen Elizabeth II</a>, he also attended the German Grand Prix.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
