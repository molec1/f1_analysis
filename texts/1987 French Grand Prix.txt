<p>The <b>1987 French Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 5 July 1987 at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_Paul_Ricard" title="Circuit Paul Ricard">Circuit Paul Ricard</a>, <a href="/wiki/Le_Castellet,_Var" title="Le Castellet, Var">Le Castellet</a>. It was the sixth race of the <a href="/wiki/1987_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="1987 Formula One season">1987 Formula One season</a>. It was the 65th <a href="/wiki/French_Grand_Prix" title="French Grand Prix">French Grand Prix</a> and the eleventh to be held at Paul Ricard, and the second to be held on the shortened version of the circuit. It was held over 80 laps of the 3.813-kilometre (2.369&#160;mi) circuit for a race distance of 305.040 kilometres (189.543&#160;mi).
</p><p>The race was won by British driver <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> driving a <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW11B" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams FW11B">Williams FW11B</a>. It was Mansell's second win of the year and his second victory in the French Grand Prix. Mansell finished seven seconds ahead of team mate Brazilian two-time World Champion <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a>. Reigning champion Frenchman <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> driving a <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/3" title="McLaren MP4/3">McLaren MP4/3</a> finished third.
</p><p>Brazilian <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Lotus</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a>'s fourth place kept him in the championship lead by a single point over Prost and three ahead of Piquet.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_summary">Race summary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1987_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Race summary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1987_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>For the first time since the <a href="/wiki/1986_Mexican_Grand_Prix" title="1986 Mexican Grand Prix">1986 Mexican Grand Prix</a>, a non-<a href="/wiki/Honda_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Honda F1">Honda</a> powered car was on the front row in the shape of the <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/3" title="McLaren MP4/3">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Techniques_d%27Avant_Garde" title="Techniques d&#39;Avant Garde">TAG</a> of <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a>, who qualified second. The pole position was taken once again by <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> in his <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW11B" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams FW11B">Williams</a>-Honda, while the second row was occupied by <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Lotus_99T" title="Lotus 99T">Lotus</a>-Honda and <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a>'s Williams. The fast nature of the circuit clearly suited the turbo cars: the best placed non-turbo driver was <a href="/wiki/Ivan_Capelli" title="Ivan Capelli">Ivan Capelli</a> in the <a href="/wiki/March_871" title="March 871">March 871</a> in 22nd place, about six seconds off the pace and 50&#160;km/h (31&#160;mph) slower on the Mistral Straight than the Williams-Hondas.
</p><p>Showing the advances in engine development, aerodynamics and tyres, on his pole lap Mansell was timed at 325&#160;km/h (202&#160;mph) on the Mistral. Even though the Mistral used in 1987 was some 800 metres shorter than when Formula One last used the full circuit at Paul Ricard in <a href="/wiki/1985_French_Grand_Prix" title="1985 French Grand Prix">1985</a>, the sheer acceleration and top speed of the Williams-Hondas was comparable to the top speeds seen at the circuit from the <a href="/wiki/1985_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1985 Formula One World Championship">1985</a> season cars.
</p><p>During qualifying <a href="/wiki/Arrows_Grand_Prix_International" title="Arrows Grand Prix International">Arrows</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Derek_Warwick" title="Derek Warwick">Derek Warwick</a> gave a view on the strength of the turbocharged Honda RA167-E <a href="/wiki/V6_engine" title="V6 engine">V6 engine</a> and the acceleration advantage Williams had over the field since the opening round in <a href="/wiki/1987_Brazilian_Grand_Prix" title="1987 Brazilian Grand Prix">Brazil</a>. He noted that on one lap he entered the Mistral approximately 50 metres ahead of Piquet's Williams. Despite the known power of the <a href="/wiki/BMW_M12" title="BMW M12">Megatron</a> (formerly BMW) turbo, Warwick claimed by the time they got to Signes just over one kilometre later the acceleration of the Honda had seen Piquet around 100 metres ahead of the <a href="/wiki/Arrows_A10" title="Arrows A10">Arrows A10</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1987_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the lights, <a href="/wiki/Michele_Alboreto" title="Michele Alboreto">Michele Alboreto</a> jumped the start, whilst his <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> teammate <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> stalled. <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> led away and was joined by teammate <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a> at the front after he passed Prost on the Mistral Straight. <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Cheever" title="Eddie Cheever">Eddie Cheever</a> retired his <a href="/wiki/Arrows_A10" title="Arrows A10">Arrows A10</a> on lap one after accidentally setting off the fire extinguisher whilst attempting to adjust the turbo boost. <a href="/wiki/Andrea_de_Cesaris" title="Andrea de Cesaris">Andrea de Cesaris</a> (<a href="/wiki/Brabham_BT56" title="Brabham BT56">Brabham BT56</a>) then collided with <a href="/wiki/Stefan_Johansson" title="Stefan Johansson">Stefan Johansson</a>, the McLaren requiring a pit stop for a damaged nosecone; the debris of his car caused some trouble to Mansell who ran into them.
</p><p>Mansell, Piquet and Prost were engaged in a gripping battle for the lead, separated by just two seconds. On lap 19, Piquet spun and Prost passed him for second. Meanwhile, Senna was holding onto a creditable fourth place. On lap 30, Piquet pitted for new tyres, followed two laps later by Senna. Meanwhile, <a href="/wiki/Thierry_Boutsen" title="Thierry Boutsen">Thierry Boutsen</a> (<a href="/wiki/Benetton_B187" title="Benetton B187">Benetton B187</a>) retired with electrical failure. Both Mansell and Prost came in for new tyres on lap 36, handing the lead to Piquet. Mansell completed his stop and closed rapidly on Piquet, breaking fastest lap records. On lap 46, at the Le Beausset corner, Piquet made an error and Mansell passed him on the inside to take a lead he would not lose. On lap 65 Piquet made a second stop which went wrong when the engine stalled, delaying him by an additional eight seconds. After exiting the pits, he caught and passed Prost (who was dealing with an electrical problem) on lap 67.
</p><p>Piquet was about 20 seconds behind Mansell, but on the final laps he was gaining at two seconds a lap. With seven laps to go, the gap was reduced to just 13 seconds, but Mansell paced himself to win by 7.7 seconds. Johansson had fought courageously up to sixth place after two pit stops to repair damages on his McLaren, but retired just five laps from the chequered flag.
</p><p>In his home race, Prost took the final podium place some 48 seconds behind Piquet. Senna finished fourth ahead of <a href="/wiki/Teo_Fabi" title="Teo Fabi">Teo Fabi</a>'s Benetton B187 in fifth. <a href="/wiki/Philippe_Streiff" title="Philippe Streiff">Philippe Streiff</a> drove a superb race to take his first World Championship point and the victory for the <a href="/wiki/1987_Formula_One_season#Jim_Clark_Trophy_(for_normally_aspirated_runners)" class="mw-redirect" title="1987 Formula One season">Jim Clark Trophy</a> in his <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_DG016" title="Tyrrell DG016">Tyrrell</a>-<a href="/wiki/Cosworth_DFZ" class="mw-redirect" title="Cosworth DFZ">Ford</a>.
</p>
