<p>The <b>2004 Australian Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 7 March 2004 at the <a href="/wiki/Melbourne_Grand_Prix_Circuit" class="mw-redirect" title="Melbourne Grand Prix Circuit">Melbourne Grand Prix Circuit</a>. <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> won the race for <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> from pole position in very dominant fashion, with his teammate <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> finishing behind him in second. This one-two finish gave Ferrari a strong 9 point lead in the constructors' standings after just one race. <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a>, <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a> and <a href="/wiki/British_American_Racing" title="British American Racing">BAR</a> each had both cars finish in the points, while <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>, a team that had enjoyed success in years preceding this, only managed one point, with <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> finishing a lapped 8th.<sup id="cite_ref-McLaren_knows_its_weaknesses_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-McLaren_knows_its_weaknesses-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2004_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2004_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Qualifying resulted in a <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> one-two, with <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a> third on the grid for <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a>. <a href="/wiki/Gianmaria_Bruni" title="Gianmaria Bruni">Gianmaria Bruni</a>, <a href="/wiki/Christian_Klien" title="Christian Klien">Christian Klien</a> and <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Panis" title="Olivier Panis">Olivier Panis</a> all failed to set a qualifying time.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2004_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the start,  Alonso was up and away and ahead of Button and looking for a way to deal with Montoya while Jarno Trulli went from ninth on the grid to be fifth out of the first corner. He was aided in his task by Montoya, who went howling down to Turn 1, braked just a hint too late and Montoya was jumped by the <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a> of <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> as he tried to stay ahead of the surging Alonso. He went off and Alonso had to put some wheels on the grass to avoid a disaster. Montoya went back to seventh. That condemned the Colombian to an afternoon stuck in traffic and put paid to any challenge there might have been for Alonso. Behind all this there were a few wheels off the grass as others sorted themselves out (notably both Saubers) while Takuma Sato bumped the rear end of Trulli's Renault, slightly (but significantly) damaging both cars.
</p><p>The Ferraris were gone already and as the afternoon developed all that Fernando Alonso could do was to watch the rears of the two red cars as they disappeared from his view. It did not take long. By the fourth lap they were two seconds ahead. By the eighth lap they were five seconds clear and after that Alonso had nothing to do. No one else could keep up with him. <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> had a long battle for position in the midfield with <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan's</a> <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a>. Fisichella passed Heidfeld, who later dropped out of the race with a transmission failure. Montoya attempted to regain the place by going around the outside of the Spaniard into the first turn, but outbraked himself and ran wide. This dropped him behind his teammate <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a>, who'd qualified 8th. Despite repassing Ralf Schumacher on-track, the Colombian ended up behind him again by the race's end in fifth place. The race proved that <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> once again had a dominant car, with <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> winning from teammate <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> in Ferrari's first one-two since <a href="/wiki/2002_Japanese_Grand_Prix" title="2002 Japanese Grand Prix">Japan 2002</a>, while the rest of the field was over 20 seconds behind. Schumacher led every one of the 58 race laps.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> At the start, Montoya was jumped by the <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a> of <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>.
</p><p>Fernando Alonso gave Renault a podium with third place, while <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> got <a href="/wiki/British_American_Racing" title="British American Racing">BAR</a> off the mark with sixth. <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> finished 7th in the Renault and was the first lapped runner. <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> seemed to be less competitive than in recent years, with <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> becoming the first retirement of the year, dropping out with an engine problem, and with <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> picking up just 1 point in eighth place.<sup id="cite_ref-McLaren_knows_its_weaknesses_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-McLaren_knows_its_weaknesses-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
