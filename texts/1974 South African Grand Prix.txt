<p>The <b>1974 South African Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>XX Lucky Strike Grand Prix of South Africa</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held at <a href="/wiki/Kyalami" title="Kyalami">Kyalami</a> on 30 March 1974. It was race 3 of 15 in both the <a href="/wiki/1974_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1974 World Championship of Drivers">1974 World Championship of Drivers</a> and the <a href="/wiki/1974_International_Cup_for_Formula_One_Manufacturers" class="mw-redirect" title="1974 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers">1974 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_summary">Race summary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1974_South_African_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Race summary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It was initially uncertain that the South African Grand Prix would go ahead due to the <a href="/wiki/1973_oil_crisis" title="1973 oil crisis">1973 oil crisis</a>, but it did so, albeit at the end of March rather than at the start of the month. <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Lotus</a> stunned the paddock with an innovative car which used four pedals and an electric clutch.
</p><p>However, practice was overshadowed by an accident which killed <a href="/wiki/Peter_Revson" title="Peter Revson">Peter Revson</a>. While driving his <a href="/wiki/Shadow_DN3" title="Shadow DN3">Shadow</a>-Ford in a test session before the race, Revson suffered a front suspension failure on the outside of Barbecue Bend and crashed heavily into the <a href="/wiki/Armco_barrier" class="mw-redirect" title="Armco barrier">Armco barrier</a>, the car bursting into flames. Revson died instantly, and the Shadow team withdrew from the race.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Niki_Lauda" title="Niki Lauda">Niki Lauda</a> took pole by a fraction of a second from <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Pace" title="Carlos Pace">Carlos Pace</a>. The two Lotus cars tangled shortly after the start, the incident also involving <a href="/wiki/Jochen_Mass" title="Jochen Mass">Jochen Mass</a> and <a href="/wiki/Henri_Pescarolo" title="Henri Pescarolo">Henri Pescarolo</a> whilst <a href="/wiki/Tom_Bels%C3%B8" title="Tom Belsø">Tom Belsø</a>'s race lasted no more than a few hundred yards due to clutch failure. Lauda led a train of cars consisting of <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Reutemann" title="Carlos Reutemann">Carlos Reutemann</a>, <a href="/wiki/Clay_Regazzoni" title="Clay Regazzoni">Clay Regazzoni</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jody_Scheckter" title="Jody Scheckter">Jody Scheckter</a> and <a href="/wiki/James_Hunt" title="James Hunt">James Hunt</a>, whose <a href="/wiki/Hesketh_Racing" title="Hesketh Racing">Hesketh</a> was suffering vibration problems.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Mike_Hailwood" title="Mike Hailwood">Mike Hailwood</a> caught and passed Scheckter when he missed a gear, and then passed Reutemann on lap 9. On lap 75, nearly at the finish, Lauda was forced to retire with ignition problems and low oil pressure, handing the lead to Reutemann. This was Reutemann's first win, the first for an Argentinian driver since <a href="/wiki/Juan_Manuel_Fangio" title="Juan Manuel Fangio">Juan Manuel Fangio</a> won the <a href="/wiki/1957_German_Grand_Prix" title="1957 German Grand Prix">1957 German Grand Prix</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Brabham" title="Brabham">Brabham</a>'s first since the <a href="/wiki/1970_South_African_Grand_Prix" title="1970 South African Grand Prix">1970 South African Grand Prix</a>. <a href="/wiki/Jean-Pierre_Beltoise" title="Jean-Pierre Beltoise">Jean-Pierre Beltoise</a> fought his way up through the field to 2nd, holding off a determined challenge from Hailwood who took the final podium place. Beltoise's 2nd place would turn out to be the last ever podium finish for a BRM.
</p>
