<p>The <b>2001 European Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>XLV Warsteiner Grand Prix of Europe</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 24 June 2001 at the <a href="/wiki/N%C3%BCrburgring" title="Nürburgring">Nürburgring</a>, <a href="/wiki/N%C3%BCrburg" title="Nürburg">Nürburg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>. It was the ninth race of the <a href="/wiki/2001_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2001 Formula One season">2001 Formula One season</a>. It is also the last race held on this layout of the Nürburgring, before the circuit was modified in 2002. The 67-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> driving for <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> after starting from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>. <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a> finished second driving for <a href="/wiki/Williams_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams F1">Williams</a>, with <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> third driving for <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>.
</p><p>The race was Michael Schumacher's fifth win of the season, his third at the Nürburgring, and the result meant that he extended his lead in the Drivers' Championship to 24 points over Coulthard and 42 ahead of <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a>. Ferrari maintained their lead in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a>, 41 points ahead of McLaren and 57 ahead of Williams, with 8 races of the season remaining.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2001_European_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2001_European_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Grand Prix was contested by 22 drivers, in eleven teams of two.<sup id="cite_ref-entrylist_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-entrylist-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The teams, also known as <a href="/wiki/Formula_One#Constructors" title="Formula One">constructors</a>, were <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>, <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a>, <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a>, <a href="/wiki/British_American_Racing" title="British American Racing">BAR</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arrows_Grand_Prix_International" title="Arrows Grand Prix International">Arrows</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jaguar_Racing" title="Jaguar Racing">Jaguar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Prost_Grand_Prix" title="Prost Grand Prix">Prost</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-entrylist_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-entrylist-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Before the race, Ferrari driver <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> led the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a> with 58 points; McLaren driver <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> was second on 40 points. Behind them in the Drivers' Championship, <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> was third on 24 points in the other Ferrari, with <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> on 22 and 8 points respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-championship_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-championship-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a>, Ferrari were leading on 82 points and McLaren were second on 48 points, with Williams third on 28 points.<sup id="cite_ref-championship_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-championship-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Following the <a href="/wiki/2001_Canadian_Grand_Prix" title="2001 Canadian Grand Prix">Canadian Grand Prix</a> on June 10, the teams conducted testing sessions at the <a href="/wiki/Silverstone_Circuit" title="Silverstone Circuit">Silverstone</a> circuit from June 12–14.<sup id="cite_ref-testing_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-testing-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> (Jordan) set the fastest time on the first day with <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Panis" title="Olivier Panis">Olivier Panis</a> (BAR) topping the second day's running, setting a time of 1:22.803 and test driver <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Wurz" title="Alexander Wurz">Alexander Wurz</a> (Benetton) was fastest on the final day, setting a time of 1:22.081, over six-tenths of a second faster than Häkkinen.<sup id="cite_ref-testing_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-testing-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>There was one driver change heading into the race. Having missed the previous Grand Prix due to a <a href="/wiki/Headache" title="Headache">headache</a> and dizziness resulting from a crash at the <a href="/wiki/2001_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="2001 Monaco Grand Prix">Monaco Grand Prix</a>, Jordan driver <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a> was declared fit to race by the FIA Medical Delegate <a href="/wiki/Sid_Watkins" title="Sid Watkins">Sid Watkins</a> and returned to his seat taken over by temporary replacement <a href="/wiki/Ricardo_Zonta" title="Ricardo Zonta">Ricardo Zonta</a>. Similarly, Jaguar driver <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a> was suffering from a strained neck and Sauber driver <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> was suffering from headaches; both were passed fit to race.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In technical developments, Benetton announced that the team would use <a href="/wiki/Traction_control" class="mw-redirect" title="Traction control">traction control</a> on both their cars, which were tested during the Friday practice sessions.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice_and_qualifying">Practice and qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2001_European_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Practice and qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Four practice sessions were held before the Sunday race—two on Friday, and two on Saturday. The Friday morning and afternoon sessions each lasted an hour. The third and final practice sessions were held on Saturday morning and lasted 45 minutes.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The Friday practice sessions were held in overcast conditions, clearing up later in the day. Coulthard was fastest in the first session, with a time of 1:16.888, one-tenth of a second ahead of his teammate Häkkinen. The two Ferrari drivers were third and fourth; Michael Schumacher ahead of Barrichello. Trulli set the fifth fastest time, with <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> and Panis, sixth and seventh fastest respectively, their best times one-thousandth of a second apart. Panis' teammate <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a> was eighth fastest. Heidfeld and Ralf Schumacher, completed the top ten. Irvine's Jaguar car was afflicted with a problem on his out-lap; this prevented him from setting a timed lap.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the second practice session, Häkkinen set the quickest lap of the day, a 1:16.408; Coulthard finished with the second fastest time. The Williams drivers were running quicker—Ralf Schumacher in third and <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a> in sixth—they were separated by the Ferrari pair of Michael Schumacher and Barrichello, in fourth and fifth respectively. They were ahead of Trulli, Heidfeld, <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a> and Panis.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Saturday morning practice sessions were again held in overcast conditions, albeit with scattered sunshine. In the third practice session, Michael Schumacher set the session's fastest time with a lap of 1:16.308, almost three-tenths of a second faster than Ralf Schumacher. The McLaren drivers ran slower—Häkkinen ahead of Coulthard. Barrichello and Montoya rounded off the top six positions.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In the final practice session, Ralf Schumacher was fastest, setting a time of 1:15.355, almost four tenths of a second faster than Montoya. Barrichello set the third fastest time, with Häkkinen and Coulthard fourth and fifth respectively. Michael Schumacher, who set the sixth fastest time, suffered from an hydraulic problem, thus limited his running.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Saturday's afternoon qualifying session lasted for an hour. During this session, the <a href="/wiki/107%25_rule" title="107% rule">107% rule</a> was in effect, which necessitated each driver set a time within 107 per cent of the quickest lap to qualify for the race. Each driver was limited to twelve laps.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher clinched his seventh <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> of the season, with a time of 1:14.960. He was joined on the front row by Ralf Schumacher, who was two tenths of a second behind.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Montoya qualified in third ahead of Barrichello, who took fourth after struggling with his car's power steering early in the session. Coulthard and Häkkinen filled the third row of the grid, their best times six thousands of a second apart. The Jordan and Sauber drivers rounded out the top ten positions. Villeneuve qualified in 11th, was quicker than his teammate Panis in 13th. The Arrows and Minardi drivers qualified at the back of the grid, covering positions 18 to 22, with <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2001_European_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Schumacher_Europe_2001.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/07/Schumacher_Europe_2001.jpg/170px-Schumacher_Europe_2001.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="116" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/07/Schumacher_Europe_2001.jpg/255px-Schumacher_Europe_2001.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/07/Schumacher_Europe_2001.jpg/340px-Schumacher_Europe_2001.jpg 2x" data-file-width="784" data-file-height="537" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Schumacher_Europe_2001.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>, who took <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> and won the race by 4.1 seconds</div></div></div>
<p>The conditions were dry and sunny for the race with the air temperature ranging from 21 to 22&#160;°C (70 to 72&#160;°F) and the track temperature between 30 and 32&#160;°C (86 and 90&#160;°F);<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> conditions were expected to remain consistent, although a 20% chance of rain was forecast.<sup id="cite_ref-galeforcef1race_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-galeforcef1race-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> The drivers took to the track at 09:30 (<a href="/wiki/Greenwich_Mean_Time" title="Greenwich Mean Time">GMT</a> +1) for a 30-minute warm-up session.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Both Ferrari cars maintained their good performance from qualifying; Barrichello had the fastest time of 1:18.209; Michael Schumacher was second in the other Ferrari car. Ralf Schumacher was just off Michael Schumacher's pace, with Irvine rounding out the top four.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The race started at 14:00 local time.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_6-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> A total of approximately 150,000 spectators attended the race.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Whilst on a <a href="/wiki/List_of_motorsport_terminology#O" class="mw-redirect" title="List of motorsport terminology">reconnaissance lap</a>, Michael Schumacher, driving a spare Ferrari car, failed to make the grid for which he commandeered a motor scooter back to the pit lane and took his racing car to the grid.<sup id="cite_ref-gprace_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gprace-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Tarso_Marques" title="Tarso Marques">Tarso Marques</a>, from 22nd on the grid, stalled his Minardi car at the start of the formation lap.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcf1race_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcf1race-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> At the start, Michael Schumacher, Ralf Schumacher and Montoya all maintained their grid positions. Behind the leading trio, Barrichello made the worst start of the grid, moving from 4th to 7th place. <a href="/wiki/Jos_Verstappen" title="Jos Verstappen">Jos Verstappen</a>, from 19th on the grid, made the best start, moving up five places to 14th position.<sup id="cite_ref-atlasf1race_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasf1race-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> At the completion of the first lap, Michael Schumacher led from Ralf Schumacher, Montoya, Coulthard, Häkkinen, Trulli, Barrichello, Heidfeld, Räikkönen and Frentzen.<sup id="cite_ref-galeforcef1race_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-galeforcef1race-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Michael Schumacher and Ralf Schumacher were the first two lead drivers to make pitstops by coming in on Lap 28.<sup id="cite_ref-nytimesf1_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nytimesf1-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> As Michael Schumacher entered the pitlane, he ran wide forcing him to cut in front of Ralf Schumacher.<sup id="cite_ref-galeforcef1race_15-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-galeforcef1race-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> As both drivers exited the pitlane, Ralf Schumacher cut to the left of the pitlane, crossing over the white line upon exiting.<sup id="cite_ref-nytimesf1_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nytimesf1-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As Michael Schumacher extended his lead, the stewards informed the Williams team that Ralf Schumacher would be served with a 10-second <a href="/wiki/List_of_motorsport_terminology#Q" class="mw-redirect" title="List of motorsport terminology">stop-go penalty</a>. Ralf Schumacher took his penalty on Lap 39 and dropped from 2nd to 4th position.<sup id="cite_ref-galeforcef1race_15-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-galeforcef1race-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2001_European_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatright" style="width:40%;&#32;;"><link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r887775652"/>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">"Nothing better can happen than winning your home Grand Prix. We have had a superb weekend, we got pole position, we got the win, we had a nice race again together, Ralf and myself, until the stop and go, so it was quite an entertaining weekend - a tough weekend as well. Because in the end Juan Pablo was coming and pushing, so in this respect, we are very delighted to have finished where we did."</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style="">Michael Schumacher, speaking during the post-race FIA <a href="/wiki/Press_conference" title="Press conference">press conference</a> for the podium finishers.<sup id="cite_ref-pressconference_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconference-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>The top three drivers appeared on the <a href="/wiki/Podium#Usage_in_motorsport" title="Podium">podium</a> and in the subsequent <a href="/wiki/Press_conference" title="Press conference">press conference</a>. Michael Schumacher said was delighted with his race victory. He also revealed that Ferrari were doubtful about racing the spare car which was to be used for one installation lap.<sup id="cite_ref-pressconference_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconference-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The race result left Michael Schumacher extending his lead in the Drivers' Championship with 68 points. Coulthard's was second on 44 points, eighteen points ahead of Barrichello and nineteen ahead of Ralf Schumacher. In the Constructors' Championship, Ferrari maintained their lead with 94 points, McLaren maintained second with 53 points, and Williams remained third on 37 points, with 8 races of the season remaining.
</p>
