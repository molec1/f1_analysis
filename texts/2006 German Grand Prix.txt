<p>The <b>2006 German Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>Mobil 1 Grand Prix of Germany</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Auto_racing" title="Auto racing">motor race</a> held at the <a href="/wiki/Hockenheimring" title="Hockenheimring">Hockenheimring</a> on 30 July 2006. The 67-lap race was the twelfth round of the <a href="/wiki/2006_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2006 Formula One season">2006 Formula One season</a> and was won by <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>. The Grand Prix weekend got off to a controversial start when the mass damper system fitted by Renault was deemed legal by the FIA appointed stewards, despite the FIA banning the use of these devices. The FIA appealed against the steward's decision, but Renault then withdrew the system after Friday practice to avoid further sanctions.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> took pole position, but it proved artificial as McLaren had inadvertently not put enough fuel as intended in his car before qualifying. In the race, Räikkönen's early pitstop left him unable to challenge at the front, and the way was left clear for Ferrari to score a dominant one-two. Perhaps due to the damper issue, Renault were not competitive; it was the first time in <a href="/wiki/2006_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2006 Formula One World Championship">2006</a> that neither of their cars finished the race on the podium.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Sakon_Yamamoto" title="Sakon Yamamoto">Sakon Yamamoto</a> made his Formula One début at the Grand Prix, starting from pit lane after changing chassis after the qualifying session. He was not the only one to suffer changes after qualifying, as <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> and <a href="/wiki/Christijan_Albers" title="Christijan Albers">Christijan Albers</a> both had to change engines, incurring ten-place penalties. A nightmare weekend for Albers was summed up with his disqualification, along with team-mate <a href="/wiki/Tiago_Monteiro" title="Tiago Monteiro">Tiago Monteiro</a>, as the <a href="/wiki/Midland_F1_Racing" title="Midland F1 Racing">Midlands</a> were disqualified after the race for having illegally flexing rear wings.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The race also saw the last appearance by <a href="/wiki/1997_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1997 Formula One World Championship">1997</a> champion <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a>, who blamed the split on the "lack of assurances about his short-term future with BMW Sauber".<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a> was promoted internally at BMW to drive at the Hungaroring because Villeneuve was still recovering from the after-effects of his crash in Germany, and went on to race in all the remaining Grands Prix.
</p>
