<p>The <b>1990 French Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at <a href="/wiki/Paul_Ricard_Circuit" class="mw-redirect" title="Paul Ricard Circuit">Paul Ricard</a> on 8 July 1990. It was the seventh race of the <a href="/wiki/1990_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1990 Formula One World Championship">1990 Formula One World Championship</a>. It was the 68th <a href="/wiki/French_Grand_Prix" title="French Grand Prix">French Grand Prix</a> and the 14th and last to be held at Paul Ricard until 2018. It was held over 80 laps of the four kilometre circuit for a race distance of 305 kilometres. This race was held the same day as the <a href="/wiki/1990_FIFA_World_Cup_Final" title="1990 FIFA World Cup Final">1990 FIFA World Cup Final</a> in <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">Rome</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>, but that event took place later in the day from this Grand Prix.
</p><p>The race almost saw one of the most remarkable upsets in Formula One history with the <a href="/wiki/Leyton_House_Racing" title="Leyton House Racing">Leyton House Racing</a> team of Italian driver <a href="/wiki/Ivan_Capelli" title="Ivan Capelli">Ivan Capelli</a> and Brazilian driver <a href="/wiki/Maur%C3%ADcio_Gugelmin" title="Maurício Gugelmin">Maurício Gugelmin</a> running first and second for an extended period of the race in their <a href="/wiki/Leyton_House_CG901" title="Leyton House CG901">Leyton House CG901s</a>. French driver <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> claimed the lead late in the race to take the win in his <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_641" title="Ferrari 641">Ferrari 641</a> by eight seconds over Capelli. Brazilian driver <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> finished third in his <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/5B" class="mw-redirect" title="McLaren MP4/5B">McLaren MP4/5B</a>.
</p><p>The win, Prost's third for the season, closed the gap to championship points leader Senna to just three points.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1990_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-qualifying_report">Pre-qualifying report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1990_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Pre-qualifying report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the Friday morning pre-qualifying session, it was a return to the top two places for the <a href="/wiki/Larrousse" title="Larrousse">Larrousse</a>-<a href="/wiki/Lola_Cars" title="Lola Cars">Lola</a> drivers, their fourth 1–2 of the season. <a href="/wiki/%C3%89ric_Bernard" title="Éric Bernard">Éric Bernard</a> was over a second faster than his team-mate <a href="/wiki/Aguri_Suzuki" title="Aguri Suzuki">Aguri Suzuki</a>, who in turn was seven tenths faster than the next fastest driver. For the first time this season, at their home race, both <a href="/wiki/Automobiles_Gonfaronnaises_Sportives" title="Automobiles Gonfaronnaises Sportives">AGS</a> drivers pre-qualified, with <a href="/wiki/Gabriele_Tarquini" title="Gabriele Tarquini">Gabriele Tarquini</a> third and <a href="/wiki/Yannick_Dalmas" title="Yannick Dalmas">Yannick Dalmas</a> fourth. It was the first successful pre-qualification for Tarquini this season, and only the second for Dalmas.<sup id="cite_ref-Walker_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Walker-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In fifth place, missing out by just under seven hundredths of a second, was <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Grouillard" title="Olivier Grouillard">Olivier Grouillard</a> in the sole Osella, the first time he had failed to pre-qualify in 1990. Sixth was <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Moreno" title="Roberto Moreno">Roberto Moreno</a> in his <a href="/wiki/EuroBrun" title="EuroBrun">EuroBrun</a>, some way adrift of Grouillard and nearly five seconds off Bernard's pace. It was the second time this season Moreno had failed to pre-qualify. Seventh, and much closer to his team-mate this time, was <a href="/wiki/Claudio_Langes" title="Claudio Langes">Claudio Langes</a> in the other EuroBrun. The other two runners were not remotely competitive; <a href="/wiki/Bertrand_Gachot" title="Bertrand Gachot">Bertrand Gachot</a> suffered an engine failure on his first lap in his <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Coloni" title="Scuderia Coloni">Coloni</a>, still with the heavy, fragile and underpowered <a href="/wiki/Subaru_1235" title="Subaru 1235">Subaru</a> engine,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> while <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Giacomelli" title="Bruno Giacomelli">Bruno Giacomelli</a> failed to leave the pits in the <a href="/wiki/Life_Racing_Engines" title="Life Racing Engines">Life</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Walker_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Walker-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1990_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_report">Race report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1990_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Race report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In one of the most remarkable turnarounds in Grand Prix history the Leyton House cars of Ivan Capelli and Maurício Gugelmin ran first and second for almost two-thirds of the race. Neither car had qualified for the previous race in <a href="/wiki/1990_Mexican_Grand_Prix" title="1990 Mexican Grand Prix">Mexico</a>, but on the smooth surface of Paul Ricard with its 1.1&#160;km long Mistral Straight the team were able to exploit their highly efficient aerodynamic package, as well as being the only team to attempt to race without stopping for fresh tyres. Gugelmin stopped while third late in the race on whilst Capelli led until three laps from home when his engine encountered problems and dropped to second.
</p><p>Berger took the lead at the start followed by pole sitter Mansell, Senna, Nannini, Patrese, Prost, Boutsen, Piquet and <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a>. Later in the race when the leaders pitted, Capelli took the lead being followed by teammate Gugelmin. Prost overtook Gugelmin on lap 54 and Gugelmin's engine blew on lap 57. Mansell was in 8th position battling for 7th with the <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> of <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> after his second pit-stop, but was eventually forced to retire on lap 73 with engine troubles (Mansell would be classified 18th). <a href="/wiki/Alessandro_Nannini" title="Alessandro Nannini">Alessandro Nannini</a> overtook Senna for third place but eventually retired 3 laps after Mansell with electrical problems (Nannini would be classified 16th). Prost overtook Capelli for the lead on lap 77 of 80 and went on to win in front of his home crowd at the last French Grand Prix held at Paul Ricard before the race was moved to Magny-Cours in <a href="/wiki/1991_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1991 Formula One World Championship">1991</a> for many years until it eventually returned to Paul Ricard in <a href="/wiki/2018_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2018 Formula One World Championship">2018</a>.
</p><p>Prost's win was the 42nd of his career, his third French Grand Prix in succession, his fifth French GP overall (and his fourth at Paul Ricard), and the 100th Grand Prix victory for <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>. Prost won ahead of Capelli, Senna, Piquet, Berger and Patrese rounding out the top 6. It would also prove to be the third and last podium finish for Ivan Capelli and the only podium finish for the Leyton House Racing team.
</p>
