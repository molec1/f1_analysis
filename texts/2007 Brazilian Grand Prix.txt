<p>The <b>2007 Brazilian Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>XXXVI Grande Prêmio do Brasil</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Auto_racing" title="Auto racing">motor race</a> held at the <a href="/wiki/Aut%C3%B3dromo_Jos%C3%A9_Carlos_Pace" title="Autódromo José Carlos Pace">Autódromo José Carlos Pace</a> in São Paulo, Brazil on 21 October 2007. The 71-lap race was the seventeenth and final round of the <a href="/wiki/2007_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2007 Formula One season">2007 Formula One season</a>. The race was won by <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a>, who consequently won the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">2007 World Drivers' Championship</a>. His teammate <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> finished the race second, whilst <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> completed the podium by finishing in third position. <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>, who had held a four-point advantage over <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>, and a seven-point lead over Räikkönen, prior to the race was slowed by a gearbox problem early in the race. He recovered to finish seventh, losing the championship to Räikkönen by a single point; teammate Alonso also ended up finishing just a single point behind. This was also the last race for <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> before he moved on to <a href="/wiki/Deutsche_Tourenwagen_Masters" title="Deutsche Tourenwagen Masters">Deutsche Tourenwagen Masters</a> the following year.
</p><p>This was the first time since the <a href="/wiki/2003_Brazilian_Grand_Prix" title="2003 Brazilian Grand Prix">2003 Brazilian Grand Prix</a> that <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> failed to finish his home Grand Prix. It was also the first double retirement for both Honda since the <a href="/wiki/2006_French_Grand_Prix" title="2006 French Grand Prix">2006 French Grand Prix</a> and Renault since the <a href="/wiki/2006_Hungarian_Grand_Prix" title="2006 Hungarian Grand Prix">2006 Hungarian Grand Prix</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a>'s first retirement in his Formula One career.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2007_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2007_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Repair_of_the_circuit">Repair of the circuit</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2007_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Repair of the circuit">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>For the event, the largest-scale repairs in the last 35 years were carried out at the <a href="/wiki/Aut%C3%B3dromo_Jos%C3%A9_Carlos_Pace" title="Autódromo José Carlos Pace">Autódromo José Carlos Pace</a>, to fundamentally solve problems with the track surface.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The existing asphalt was entirely replaced.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> At the same time, the pit lane entrance was enhanced to improve safety.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> To facilitate the work, the circuit was closed and no events were held in the five months immediately preceding the race.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Driver_change">Driver change</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2007_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Driver change">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p><a href="/wiki/Alexander_Wurz" title="Alexander Wurz">Alexander Wurz</a> retired from <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> at the end of the <a href="/wiki/2007_Chinese_Grand_Prix" title="2007 Chinese Grand Prix">Chinese GP</a>. He was replaced at <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a>-<a href="/wiki/Toyota_Racing_(Formula_One_team)" title="Toyota Racing (Formula One team)">Toyota</a> by the Japanese driver <a href="/wiki/Kazuki_Nakajima" title="Kazuki Nakajima">Kazuki Nakajima</a>, son of former F1 driver <a href="/wiki/Satoru_Nakajima" title="Satoru Nakajima">Satoru</a>. As a result, three Japanese drivers were entered into a Grand Prix for the first time since the <a href="/wiki/1995_Japanese_Grand_Prix" title="1995 Japanese Grand Prix">1995 Japanese Grand Prix</a>.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Standings">Standings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2007_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Standings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Prior to the race, championship leader <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> (107 points) was 4 points ahead of second placed driver, <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> (103 points). <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> (100 points) was 7 points behind the leader. This was the  first three-way   title fight in the final race of the F1 season since the <a href="/wiki/1986_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="1986 Australian Grand Prix">1986 Australian Grand Prix</a>, which saw <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a>  become champion, ahead of <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice">Practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2007_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the Friday practice session, the Ferrari drivers topped the timesheet with Kimi Räikkönen leading the first practice in the wet session.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> In the second practice session, Lewis Hamilton led McLaren to a 1–2 finish, with the track improving over time.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Stewards fined McLaren, Honda and Super Aguri €15,000 each for tyre rule infringement. Lewis Hamilton, Jenson Button and Takuma Sato used two sets of wet tyres, more than the one set permitted during the first practice. In addition, each driver had to surrender one set of wet tyres to avoid gaining any advantage.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2007_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The first session of qualifying saw the two <a href="/wiki/Spyker_F1" title="Spyker F1">Spykers</a> of <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sakon_Yamamoto" title="Sakon Yamamoto">Sakon Yamamoto</a> plus the two <a href="/wiki/Super_Aguri_F1" title="Super Aguri F1">Super Aguris</a> of <a href="/wiki/Takuma_Sato" title="Takuma Sato">Takuma Sato</a> and <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Davidson" title="Anthony Davidson">Anthony Davidson</a> eliminated, along with <a href="/wiki/Kazuki_Nakajima" title="Kazuki Nakajima">Kazuki Nakajima</a>, who was making his debut in the <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a>, replacing the retiring <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Wurz" title="Alexander Wurz">Alexander Wurz</a>. <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> also went out in the <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a>.
</p><p>In session two, the two <a href="/wiki/Honda_Racing_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Honda Racing F1">Hondas</a> of <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> and the two <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rossos</a> of <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vitantonio_Liuzzi" title="Vitantonio Liuzzi">Vitantonio Liuzzi</a> went out along with <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> in his last race for Renault and <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> in the last race of his career in the <a href="/wiki/Toyota_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Toyota F1">Toyota</a>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> made the top ten for Williams, and qualified on row 5 alongside <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull's</a> <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a>. They lined up just behind <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Toyota_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Toyota F1">Toyota</a> and <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a>'s <a href="/wiki/BMW_Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="BMW Sauber">BMW Sauber</a>. Kubica's teammate <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> was sixth, and <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> took 5th in the second Red Bull. <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> took 2nd and 4th, with championship leader <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> ahead of <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>. Their fellow championship contender <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> qualified third, while his <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> teammate <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> took the ninth pole position of his career at his home Grand Prix.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2007_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a href="/wiki/File:2007_Brazilian_GP_4_drivers_at_start.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6d/2007_Brazilian_GP_4_drivers_at_start.jpg/300px-2007_Brazilian_GP_4_drivers_at_start.jpg" decoding="async" width="300" height="132" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6d/2007_Brazilian_GP_4_drivers_at_start.jpg/450px-2007_Brazilian_GP_4_drivers_at_start.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6d/2007_Brazilian_GP_4_drivers_at_start.jpg/600px-2007_Brazilian_GP_4_drivers_at_start.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1280" data-file-height="562" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:2007_Brazilian_GP_4_drivers_at_start.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> drivers took the lead from the <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLarens</a> at the start of the race.</div></div></div>
<p>Only 21 cars were aligned on the grid, because of problems on the <a href="/wiki/Spyker_F1" title="Spyker F1">Spyker</a> of <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a>, who started the race from the pit lane. At the start, the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferraris</a> of <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> were faster, with the Finn passing <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> on the outside. At the third corner, <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> passed Hamilton on the inside and then, on the Reta Oposta, defended himself from the attack of the Briton by taking the inside line. Hamilton locked up his brakes and went off the track while trying to regain the position. He rejoined the race in 8th place, just behind <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Toyota_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Toyota F1">Toyota</a>. Also in the first lap, <a href="/wiki/Vitantonio_Liuzzi" title="Vitantonio Liuzzi">Liuzzi</a> lost his front wing and was forced to enter the pits for repairs. In the first corner on the second lap, <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Fisichella</a> went off the track, and was hit on the back from <a href="/wiki/Sakon_Yamamoto" title="Sakon Yamamoto">Sakon Yamamoto</a> while re-entering on the track. <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> jumped the start and was given a drive-through penalty for this.
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Nico_Rosberg_overtaking_Heidfeld_2007_Brazil.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/63/Nico_Rosberg_overtaking_Heidfeld_2007_Brazil.jpg/220px-Nico_Rosberg_overtaking_Heidfeld_2007_Brazil.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="143" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/63/Nico_Rosberg_overtaking_Heidfeld_2007_Brazil.jpg/330px-Nico_Rosberg_overtaking_Heidfeld_2007_Brazil.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/63/Nico_Rosberg_overtaking_Heidfeld_2007_Brazil.jpg/440px-Nico_Rosberg_overtaking_Heidfeld_2007_Brazil.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1280" data-file-height="830" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Nico_Rosberg_overtaking_Heidfeld_2007_Brazil.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>One of the highlights of the race was an intense battle between <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> and <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>Hamilton was trying to make up for the positions he lost: on lap 2 he passed Trulli and went into 7th, and 4 laps later he passed <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> to take the 6th place. Just minutes later, he suffered a gearbox problem: on the Reta Oposta, he failed to find gears and slowed down, to be passed by most cars on the track. After going slowly for about 30&#160;seconds, Hamilton managed to reset the computer of his car, and get it going again: he was in 18th place by then, and with the Ferraris pulling away he needed at the very least 7th place but more likely 5th place to clinch the World Championship.
</p><p>At the front no position was changed: by lap 15, the Ferraris were almost 12&#160;seconds ahead of Alonso, third. By that lap, Hamilton climbed to 11th, having passed Barrichello, <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a>, <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Davidson" title="Anthony Davidson">Anthony Davidson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Takuma_Sato" title="Takuma Sato">Takuma Sato</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kazuki_Nakajima" title="Kazuki Nakajima">Kazuki Nakajima</a>, and having taken advantage from the retirement of <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a>, who was running in fifth position.
</p><p>After 20 laps, the front runners began stopping for the first time: the first was <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a>, who went for 3 stops; on the same lap Massa entered the pits. Räikkönen pitted on lap 22, carrying a bit more fuel than his teammate, to try to overtake him after the second stop. Alonso, Trulli, Vettel and Hamilton pitted on lap 22, with the Spaniard keeping hards, while Hamilton switched his strategy to a 3-stopper (a rather risky strategy on the track which features the longest pit lane of the whole season): he carried less fuel than Alonso and switched to soft tyres. Heidfeld was the last to stop, pitting on lap 25.
</p><p>After the first round of pit stops, Massa was leading with 3.3&#160;seconds on Räikkönen, followed by Alonso, 17&#160;seconds behind. Hamilton was 13th, again behind Barrichello, 50&#160;seconds behind Massa. On lap 32, Nakajima entered the pits for his first stop, but he ran wide while entering his stand, injuring two mechanics in the process. They suffered no serious injuries.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Heikki_Kovalainen_watching_2007_Brazil_GP.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/44/Heikki_Kovalainen_watching_2007_Brazil_GP.jpg/220px-Heikki_Kovalainen_watching_2007_Brazil_GP.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="227" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/44/Heikki_Kovalainen_watching_2007_Brazil_GP.jpg/330px-Heikki_Kovalainen_watching_2007_Brazil_GP.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/44/Heikki_Kovalainen_watching_2007_Brazil_GP.jpg/440px-Heikki_Kovalainen_watching_2007_Brazil_GP.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1280" data-file-height="1322" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Heikki_Kovalainen_watching_2007_Brazil_GP.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Kovalainen (in chair at front of picture) retired (DNF) a race for the first time in Formula One.</div></div></div>
<p>On lap 34 Kubica, who was light on fuel, passed Alonso to take the third position, only to stop 5 laps later; he rejoined in seventh place. Hamilton made his second stop on lap 37, switching to hards again; on the same lap <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a>, who finished his first 16 consecutive races in Formula One, ended his race spinning on turn 3 and crashing into the barriers. This also meant that Kovalainen lost the opportunity to become the first driver to finish all of his races in his first season.
</p><p>On lap 43, the second round of pit stops began: the first to enter was <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> from 8th, followed by Trulli (then 6th) a lap later. Meanwhile, Massa made an error and ran wide at the Descida do Lago, with Räikkönen closing on him. The Brazilian pitted on lap 50, followed by Alonso on lap 52 and Räikkönen on lap 53. The Finn set record lap times in the process, which meant he rejoined the race into the lead, 2&#160;seconds ahead of Massa. At that point, Räikkönen was in position to become world champion; third was Kubica (who still had to stop a third time), fourth Alonso, then came Heidfeld, <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a>, Trulli, Hamilton (more than a minute behind Räikkönen), Coulthard and Nakajima. Hamilton pitted on lap 57, rejoining in 9th. On lap 59 Kubica pitted dropping to 6th; in the same lap Hamilton passed Coulthard to take the 8th position, setting the then fastest lap.
</p><p>In the final laps, Räikkönen and Massa led with a comfortable margin over Alonso, and Hamilton was lapped. On lap 61 Rosberg tried to pass Heidfeld on the first corner: both ran wide. Rosberg passed Heidfeld, with Kubica passing both to take the 4th. On lap 70, Rosberg tried a similar move, overtaking Kubica. Hamilton gained 7th place when Trulli made a pit stop, but this was not enough, as he needed at least a 5th place to gain the title.
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Kimi_Raikkonen_won_2007_Brazil_GP.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/87/Kimi_Raikkonen_won_2007_Brazil_GP.jpg/250px-Kimi_Raikkonen_won_2007_Brazil_GP.jpg" decoding="async" width="250" height="79" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/87/Kimi_Raikkonen_won_2007_Brazil_GP.jpg/375px-Kimi_Raikkonen_won_2007_Brazil_GP.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/87/Kimi_Raikkonen_won_2007_Brazil_GP.jpg/500px-Kimi_Raikkonen_won_2007_Brazil_GP.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1280" data-file-height="402" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Kimi_Raikkonen_won_2007_Brazil_GP.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Räikkönen celebrating victory.</div></div></div>
<p>At the chequered flag, Räikkönen was first, winning the race and the World Championship by one point, taking his first victory in Brazil, having been a runner-up on three consecutive occasions. He performed the seemingly impossible task of overturning Hamilton's 17 point advantage over him in two races. Massa was second in the other Ferrari, the fourth double (1–2 finish) of the year for the Italian team. Third, and last on the podium, was Alonso. The other drivers in the points were Rosberg on 4th, Kubica on 5th, Heidfeld on 6th, Hamilton on 7th and Trulli on 8th.
</p><p>The race gained an average of nine million viewers in the United Kingdom on <a href="/wiki/ITV1" class="mw-redirect" title="ITV1">ITV1</a>, which was the highest for Formula One in seven years. Viewer numbers peaked at 10.4 million in the last 15&#160;minutes.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2007_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After the  race, the result had been thrown into doubt following an inquiry into the cars of <a href="/wiki/BMW_Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="BMW Sauber">BMW Sauber</a>  and <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a>.  Race stewards investigated alleged irregularities with the cars' fuel.  The stewards decided not to penalize the teams based on a lack of  conclusive evidence, but the <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>  team appealed against this ruling. The appeal was finally rejected by  the FIA on 16 November 2007. Had the FIA accepted the appeal, although  it would be unprecedented, the title could have been handed to <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton stated that he wouldn't have favored winning the championship by this means.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2007_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="New_railway_station">New railway station</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2007_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: New railway station">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 17 October, <a href="/wiki/Companhia_Paulista_de_Trens_Metropolitanos" title="Companhia Paulista de Trens Metropolitanos">Companhia Paulista de Trens Metropolitanos</a> (CPTM) began to operate the new station of the <a href="/wiki/Line_9_(CPTM)" title="Line 9 (CPTM)">Line C</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aut%C3%B3dromo_(CPTM)" title="Autódromo (CPTM)"><i>Autódromo</i></a>, near the circuit.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> The Line C had been extended to improve the access between the center of <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo" title="São Paulo">São Paulo</a> and southern region of the <a href="/wiki/Greater_S%C3%A3o_Paulo" title="Greater São Paulo">Greater São Paulo</a> including the circuit.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> As a result, 7,100 spectators used this new station on Sunday, 21 October alone, and 18,000 or more spectators in total from Friday to Sunday.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Television">Television</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2007_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Television">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In Spain, television broadcasters <a href="/wiki/Telecinco" title="Telecinco">Telecinco</a> and <a href="/wiki/TV3_(Catalonia)" title="TV3 (Catalonia)">TV3</a> had a total average audience  of 9.0 million viewers, with a market share of 77.8% in Asturias and 69.2% in Madrid.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
