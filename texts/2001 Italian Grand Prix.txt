<p>The <b>2001 Italian Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>LXXII Gran Premio Campari d'Italia</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 16 September 2001 at the <a href="/wiki/Autodromo_Nazionale_di_Monza" title="Autodromo Nazionale di Monza">Autodromo Nazionale di Monza</a>, <a href="/wiki/Monza" title="Monza">Monza</a>, Italy. It was the fifteenth round of the <a href="/wiki/2001_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2001 Formula One season">2001 Formula One season</a> and the 72nd Italian Grand Prix. The 53-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a> after starting from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>. <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> finished second in a <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> with Montoya's teammate <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> third.
</p><p>Montoya maintained his start-line advantage and led until he was passed by Barrichello on the ninth lap. Barrichello pulled away from Montoya and held the lead until his one and only <a href="/wiki/Pit_stop" title="Pit stop">pit stop</a> on lap 19 which proved problematic because of a faulty refuelling rig. Montoya was utilising a one-stop strategy and made a pit stop on lap 29, which allowed his teammate Ralf Schumacher to lead for six laps. Barrichello regained the lead on lap 36, until Montoya took over the position on lap 42 when Barrichello made a pit stop for the second time. Barrichello started to reduce the gap between himself and Montoya but was unable to challenge the Williams driver who won his first Formula One victory.
</p><p>The result meant Montoya moved up into fifth place in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a>, 83 points behind leader <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> who clinched the title two races beforehand. Barrichello's third position finish allowed him to close the gap to <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a>. Williams' strong finish meant the gap between themselves and McLaren was reduced to eight points with two races remaining in the season.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2001_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Grand Prix was contested by eleven teams with two drivers each. The teams (also known as <a href="/wiki/Formula_One#Constructors" title="Formula One">constructors</a>) were <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>, <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a>, <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a>, <a href="/wiki/British_American_Racing" title="British American Racing">BAR</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arrows_Grand_Prix_International" title="Arrows Grand Prix International">Arrows</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jaguar_Racing" title="Jaguar Racing">Jaguar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Prost_Grand_Prix" title="Prost Grand Prix">Prost</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-entrylist_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-entrylist-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Before the race, both the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_champions" class="mw-redirect" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; champions">Drivers' Championship</a> and <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a> were already settled, with Ferrari driver <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> having claimed the Drivers' Championship two rounds earlier in the <a href="/wiki/2001_Hungarian_Grand_Prix" title="2001 Hungarian Grand Prix">Hungarian Grand Prix</a> and Ferrari took the Constructors' Championship at the same event, with McLaren too many <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Championship_points_scoring_systems" title="List of Formula One World Championship points scoring systems">points</a> behind to be able to catch them.<sup id="cite_ref-points_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After the <a href="/wiki/2001_Belgian_Grand_Prix" title="2001 Belgian Grand Prix">Belgian Grand Prix</a> on 2 September, the teams conducted mid-season testing at various European race circuits between 4–7 September to prepare for the Italian Grand Prix at the Autodromo Nazionale di Monza. Ferrari, Williams, BAR, Jordan and Minardi tested at the <a href="/wiki/Mugello_Circuit" title="Mugello Circuit">Mugello Circuit</a>. <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> for Ferrari set the fastest times on the first and second days.<sup id="cite_ref-septesting_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-septesting-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The second day of testing was disrupted on six occasions after BAR test driver <a href="/wiki/Takuma_Sato" title="Takuma Sato">Takuma Sato</a> went off the circuit, suffered an <a href="/wiki/Electronic_control_unit" title="Electronic control unit">electronic control unit</a> failure and had a malfunctioning anti-stall system. BAR driver <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Panis" title="Olivier Panis">Olivier Panis</a> and Minardi driver <a href="/wiki/Alex_Yoong" title="Alex Yoong">Alex Yoong</a> both had problems with their clutch and gearboxes.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> On the final day Michael Schumacher set the fastest lap of 1 minute and 24.226 seconds, four-tenths ahead of Panis.<sup id="cite_ref-septesting_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-septesting-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Ferrari test driver <a href="/wiki/Luca_Badoer" title="Luca Badoer">Luca Badoer</a> spent three days at his team's private test track the <a href="/wiki/Fiorano_Circuit" title="Fiorano Circuit">Fiorano Circuit</a> where he did practice starts as well as testing of launch control and traction control.<sup id="cite_ref-septesting_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-septesting-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After the <a href="/wiki/September_11_attacks" title="September 11 attacks">September 11 attacks</a> on New York and Washington D.C., Formula One's governing body, the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile</a> (FIA) announced that the Italian Grand Prix would go ahead as scheduled.<sup id="cite_ref-gpahead_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpahead-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Ferrari president <a href="/wiki/Luca_Cordero_di_Montezemolo" title="Luca Cordero di Montezemolo">Luca di Montezemolo</a> said that his team would approach the race as a normal racing event instead of a traditional Ferrari festival. Furthermore, di Montezemolo stated Formula One should continue its normal schedule and not cancel races.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Additionally, the <a href="/wiki/Automobile_Club_d%27Italia" title="Automobile Club d&#39;Italia">Automobile Club d'Italia</a> urged fans and spectators to behave "in keeping with the gravity of the situation and in collective participation in the pain of American citizens."<sup id="cite_ref-gpahead_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpahead-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Podium celebrations were also cancelled and all pre-race ceremonies including a flypast by the <a href="/wiki/Frecce_Tricolori" title="Frecce Tricolori">Italian Tricolour Arrows</a> display team were called off.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Three teams altered their car's liveries as a mark of respect. Ferrari stripped their cars of all advertising and painted their nose cones black.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Jaguar fitted black engine covers to their <a href="/wiki/Jaguar_R2" title="Jaguar R2">R2</a> cars on Saturday morning,<sup id="cite_ref-practice3_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-practice3-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> and Jordan sponsor <a href="/wiki/Deutsche_Post" title="Deutsche Post">Deutsche Post</a> replaced its branding with the flag of the United States on the Jordan cars engine cover on Sunday morning.<sup id="cite_ref-atlaswarmup_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlaswarmup-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher was reluctant to take part in the race and said in 2002 that he felt it was a "bad sign" to be driving after the September 11 attacks.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>There were two driver changes heading into the race. The Minardi team replaced regular driver <a href="/wiki/Tarso_Marques" title="Tarso Marques">Tarso Marques</a> with Yoong. Yoong received backing from the <a href="/wiki/Magnum_Corporation" class="mw-redirect" title="Magnum Corporation">Magnum Corporation</a> and was granted a <a href="/wiki/FIA_Super_Licence" title="FIA Super Licence">super licence</a> after a two-day test at the Mugello Circuit. He became the first Malaysian driver to compete in Formula One. Marques was kept on as the team's test and reserve driver, and assisted with developing the <a href="/wiki/Minardi_PS02" title="Minardi PS02">Minardi PS02</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Czech <a href="/wiki/Formula_3000" title="Formula 3000">Formula 3000</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Tom%C3%A1%C5%A1_Enge" title="Tomáš Enge">Tomáš Enge</a> (who had been signed as the test driver for Prost) replaced <a href="/wiki/Luciano_Burti" title="Luciano Burti">Luciano Burti</a> who had been recovering from a concussion and bruising he sustained in an accident at the previous race in Belgium.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Due to the configuration of the Autodromo Nazionale di Monza, with its high average speed, the teams set up their cars to produce the minimum amount of downforce possible. Williams introduced a revised <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW23" title="Williams FW23">FW23</a> specification for Montoya after one was used by Ralf Schumacher in Belgium.<sup id="cite_ref-fiafriday_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiafriday-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Williams equipped the earlier aerodynamic package on Ralf Schumacher's car on Sunday morning.<sup id="cite_ref-fiawarmup_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiawarmup-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Benetton brought a new front wing and Ferrari debuted a new engine specification with improved aerodynamics.<sup id="cite_ref-fiafriday_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiafriday-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice">Practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2001_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Four practice sessions were held before the Sunday race, two each on Friday and Saturday. The Friday morning and afternoon sessions each lasted an hour; the third and fourth sessions, on Saturday morning, lasted 45 minutes each.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> The first practice session was moved from the usual start time from 11:00 <a href="/wiki/Central_European_Summer_Time" title="Central European Summer Time">Central European Summer Time</a> (<a href="/wiki/UTC%2B02:00" title="UTC+02:00">UTC+02:00</a>) to 10:50 to comply with a planned silence for one minute that was observed at 12:00 throughout Europe.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher set the pace in the first practice session, which took place in dry weather conditions,<sup id="cite_ref-fiafriday_16-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiafriday-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> with a time of 1:25.524; <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> had the second-fastest time. Barrichello was third fastest, ahead of <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> (who crashed his car at Variante Ascari) in fourth. <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> recorded the fifth fastest time, with <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> in sixth. <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a>, who was seventh fastest, collided with a barrier at Variante Goodyear which removed his front-right wheel. <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pedro_de_la_Rosa" title="Pedro de la Rosa">Pedro de la Rosa</a> rounded out the session's top ten fastest drivers. Arrows driver <a href="/wiki/Jos_Verstappen" title="Jos Verstappen">Jos Verstappen</a> did not set a lap time because he collided with a tyre barrier at the Curva di Lesmo early in the session.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> In the second practice session, where a brief rain shower hit the circuit early in the session, Ralf Schumacher set the fastest time of the day, a 1:24.667; Montoya had the second fastest time despite going off the circuit during the session. Michael Schumacher (with a time of 1:25.131) was third fastest, ahead of de la Rosa. Barrichello, Häkkinen and Coulthard filled the next three positions. Despite not going onto the circuit, Heidfeld was the eighth fastest driver. Alesi and <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a> (who slowed in his car and went off the circuit and slid wide of the track and avoided colliding with the barriers) followed in the top ten.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Montoya took the fastest lap of the third practice session, a 1:25.558 which was held on a damp track. Barrichello was second fastest with a time 0.336 slower than Montoya. de la Rosa was running quickly and set the third fastest lap, a 1:26.542. BAR drivers Villeneuve and Panis set the fourth and fifth fastest times. Räikkönen, <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a>, Verstappen, <a href="/wiki/Enrique_Bernoldi" title="Enrique Bernoldi">Enrique Bernoldi</a> and Heidfeld rounded out the top ten. <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a>, Yoong, <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a>, Michael Schumacher, Coulthard, Häkkinen and <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> did not set lap times during the session.<sup id="cite_ref-practice3_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-practice3-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The circuit dried up for the final practice session where Michael Schumacher had the fastest lap of the weekend so far with a 1:23.178; Barrichello had the fastest time when circuit conditions improved but fell to fourth. The two Ferrari drivers were separated by Montoya and Trulli. Coulthard was fifth quickest with Ralf Schumacher sixth. Heidfeld, Häkkinen, de la Rosa and Räikkönen rounded out the top ten drivers ahead of qualifying. Enge spun but regained control of his car.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2001_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Michael_Schumacher_2010_Malaysia.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/80/Michael_Schumacher_2010_Malaysia.jpg/170px-Michael_Schumacher_2010_Malaysia.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="281" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/80/Michael_Schumacher_2010_Malaysia.jpg/255px-Michael_Schumacher_2010_Malaysia.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/80/Michael_Schumacher_2010_Malaysia.jpg/340px-Michael_Schumacher_2010_Malaysia.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1280" data-file-height="2117" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Michael_Schumacher_2010_Malaysia.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> <i>(pictured in 2010)</i>, the 2001 Drivers' Champion, qualified third.</div></div></div>
<p>Saturday afternoon's one hour qualifying session saw each driver was limited to twelve laps, with the starting order decided by the drivers' fastest laps. During this session the <a href="/wiki/107%25_rule" title="107% rule">107% rule</a> was in effect, requiring each driver to remain within 107 per cent of the fastest lap time to qualify for the race.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> The session was held in dry weather conditions.<sup id="cite_ref-fiasaturday_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiasaturday-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Montoya clinched his second consecutive <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> and his third of the season with a time of 1:22.216.<sup id="cite_ref-dtquali_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dtquali-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Although he was happy with the feel of his car, he did not feel confident that he could take pole position. Montoya was joined on the front row by Barrichello who recorded a lap 0.312 seconds slower and also went off into a gravel trap during qualifying. Michael Schumacher qualified third and admitted to making a mistake on his third run which prevented him from setting a quicker time. Ralf Schumacher had balance issues which meant his car alternated between <a href="/wiki/Understeer_and_oversteer" title="Understeer and oversteer">understeer and oversteer</a> en route to fourth position. He also managed three clear runs before his fourth was disrupted by a <a href="/wiki/Racing_flags#Yellow_flag" title="Racing flags">yellow flag</a>. Trulli qualified fifth and was delighted with his car and tyres.<sup id="cite_ref-fiasaturday_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiasaturday-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-dtquali_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dtquali-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard qualified in sixth position and felt his car's balance was good although felt it difficult to push hard and achieve ideal <a href="/wiki/Grip_(auto_racing)" title="Grip (auto racing)">grip</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-fiasaturday_23-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiasaturday-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Häkkinen took seventh and ran wide at the exit of Curva di Lesmo on his final timed run and crashed heavily into a barrier which resulted in a large amount of damage to the front and left side of his car. Häkkinen was unhurt but the crash caused qualifying to be suspended for thirteen minutes.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The two Sauber drivers were eighth and ninth with Heidfeld in front of Räikkönen;<sup id="cite_ref-dtquali_24-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dtquali-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Heidfeld was happy with his position after losing track time on Friday while Räikkönen complained that he had been blocked by Fisichella who was on an out lap. de la Rosa rounded out the top ten qualifiers and was happy after Jaguar engineer Humphrey Corbett corrected an issue with understeer on de la Rosa's car.<sup id="cite_ref-satquotes_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-satquotes-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Button took eleventh position and reported that his Benetton was well balanced. Frentzen took twelfth and said his qualifying position was more significant than his fourth-place finish in the Belgian Grand Prix.<sup id="cite_ref-fiasaturday_23-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiasaturday-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Irvine struggled with brake balance which shifted towards the rear of his Jaguar which meant he managed thirteenth. He also was unable to set a faster time after <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Panis" title="Olivier Panis">Olivier Panis</a> blocked him by driving to the first corner apex and stopped his car. Fisichella used his Benetton team's <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_motorsport_terms#S" title="Glossary of motorsport terms">spare car</a> because his race car developed a hydraulic failure in practice but had an engine issue in the spare car which restricted him to fourteenth.<sup id="cite_ref-dtquali_24-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dtquali-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-satquotes_26-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-satquotes-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> BAR drivers <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a> and Panis took the fifteenth and seventeenth positions and both reported that there was a lack of aerodynamic grip but mechanical grip was fine. The two were separated by Alesi in the slower of the two Jordan cars and described his session as "the worst qualifying session I could imagine on a circuit which love" because he had brake balance and handling issues.<sup id="cite_ref-fiasaturday_23-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiasaturday-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Verstappen and Bernoldi took the eighteenth and nineteenth positions for the Arrows team. Enge set the twentieth fastest time in his first Formula One qualifying session,<sup id="cite_ref-dtquali_24-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dtquali-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> and had two engine problems in both his race car and the spare monocoque tuned for Frentzen.<sup id="cite_ref-satquotes_26-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-satquotes-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> The two Minardi drivers qualified at the rear of the field with <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> ahead of Yoong; both drivers stopped on their out laps with gearbox actuator failures and were required to share the spare Minardi car.<sup id="cite_ref-fiasaturday_23-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiasaturday-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Warm-up">Warm-up</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2001_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Warm-up">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The drivers took to the track at 09:30 local time for a 30-minute warm-up session.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_18-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> It took place in sunny and warm weather conditions.<sup id="cite_ref-fiawarmup_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiawarmup-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher maintained his good performance, setting a time of 1:26.029. Coulthard was the second-fastest driver. Montoya was third, two-tenths of a second behind Schumacher. Barrichello completed the top four fastest drivers. After the end of the warm-up but before the race, a second minute of silence was held at the end of the Drivers' Parade.<sup id="cite_ref-atlaswarmup_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlaswarmup-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2001_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Rubens_Barrichello_2010_Malaysia.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/28/Rubens_Barrichello_2010_Malaysia.jpg/170px-Rubens_Barrichello_2010_Malaysia.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="236" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/28/Rubens_Barrichello_2010_Malaysia.jpg/255px-Rubens_Barrichello_2010_Malaysia.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/28/Rubens_Barrichello_2010_Malaysia.jpg/340px-Rubens_Barrichello_2010_Malaysia.jpg 2x" data-file-width="800" data-file-height="1110" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Rubens_Barrichello_2010_Malaysia.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> <i>(pictured in 2010)</i> led 15 laps of the race.</div></div></div>
<p>The race started at 14:00 local time. The race was held in sunny weather conditions; the air temperature was 19&#160;°C (66&#160;°F) and the track temperature 26&#160;°C (79&#160;°F).<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> During the buildup to the race, Michael Schumacher attempted to organise a pact that would see no overtaking at the first two chicanes. The plan failed, with Villeneuve, Benetton team principal <a href="/wiki/Flavio_Briatore" title="Flavio Briatore">Flavio Briatore</a> and Arrows owner <a href="/wiki/Tom_Walkinshaw" title="Tom Walkinshaw">Tom Walkinshaw</a> refusing to accept the pact.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher had been keen to avoid any accidents at the start, due to a combination of the effects of the 11 September 2001 attacks earlier that week; the death of fire marshal <a href="/wiki/Paolo_Gislimberti" title="Paolo Gislimberti">Paolo Gislimberti</a> in a pile-up at the beginning of the previous year's race; and the horrific accident in the previous day's <a href="/wiki/ChampCar" class="mw-redirect" title="ChampCar">ChampCar</a> race in Germany, in which former Formula One driver <a href="/wiki/Alex_Zanardi" title="Alex Zanardi">Alex Zanardi</a> was critically injured, resulting in the amputation of both legs.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Heidfeld lost hydraulic pressure on the grid; he was required to use the spare Sauber monocoque and start from the <a href="/wiki/Pit_lane" class="mw-redirect" title="Pit lane">pit lane</a>. Fisichella did the same because he had a fuel leak in his car.<sup id="cite_ref-gprace_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gprace-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> When the race started, Montoya maintained his pole position advantage going into the first corner, with Barrichello in second and Michael Schumacher in third. Ralf Schumacher passed Michael Schumacher at the Variante Goodyear chicane but Michael challenged Ralf for the position at the exit of Variante della Roggia and got ahead heading into the Curve di Lesmo.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Further back, Trulli was hit by Button and was sent into a spin and became the first retirement of the race. Button made a pit stop at the end of the lap for a replacement front wing.<sup id="cite_ref-gprace_31-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gprace-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Irvine made a good start, rising from thirteenth to seventh by the end of the first lap, while Häkkinen made a poor gateway after going through the chicane to avoid making contact with other drivers and fell to thirteenth. Verstappen made the best start, moving from nineteenth to eighth.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_32-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> At the end of the first lap, Montoya led by half a second from Barrichello, who in turn was followed by Michael Schumacher, Ralf Schumacher, Coulthard, de la Rosa, Irvine, Verstappen, Räikkönen, Alesi, Villeneuve, Bernoldi, Häkkinen, Panis, Frentzen, Alonso, Enge, Heidfeld, Yoong, Fisichella and Button.<sup id="cite_ref-lapchart_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapchart-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Barrichello and Michael Schumacher started to maintain the gap between themselves and Montoya, and started to pull away from Ralf Schumacher.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_32-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Bernoldi passed Villeneuve for tenth position on lap two, while Irvine dropped to ninth one lap later after he was overtaken by Verstappen and Räikkönen. He later fell behind Alesi, Bernoldi and Villeneuve on the fourth lap. Button's engine failed which caused him to retire on lap five. Irvine lost a further three positions to Häkkinen, Panis and Frentzen one lap later. Coulthard became the third retirement of the Grand Prix after an engine failure on lap seven, which meant Verstappen inherited fifth and Räikkönen moved into sixth.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_32-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Montoya had a blister on one of his rear tyres which caused him to slide under braking,<sup id="cite_ref-pressconf_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconf-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> and was slow exiting the Variante della Roggia chicane which allowed Barrichello to take the lead on the ninth lap. Three laps later, Alesi passed Räikkönen for sixth while Häkkinen moved into tenth after passing Bernoldi on lap 13.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_32-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Irvine retired on lap 14 because he lost horsepower in his car when a cylinder in his engine stopped working.<sup id="cite_ref-gprace_31-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gprace-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-sunquotes_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sunquotes-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> Verstappen fell to seventh after he was passed by Alesi and Räikkönen on lap 16. Yoong spun at Curva di Lesmo and fell to the back of the field.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_32-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Juan_Pablo_Montoya.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ad/Juan_Pablo_Montoya.jpg/170px-Juan_Pablo_Montoya.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="252" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ad/Juan_Pablo_Montoya.jpg/255px-Juan_Pablo_Montoya.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ad/Juan_Pablo_Montoya.jpg/340px-Juan_Pablo_Montoya.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1228" data-file-height="1818" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Juan_Pablo_Montoya.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a> <i>(pictured in 2008)</i> won his first Grand Prix after starting from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>Michael Schumacher battled for Montoya for third position until he became the first of the leading drivers to make a scheduled pit stop on lap 18, and rejoined the track in fourth place.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_32-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> The Williams and Ferrari teams were employing different pit stop strategies – the Williams team were planning a one-stop strategy whereas the Ferrari team were planning for two stops.  Barrichello (on a lighter fuel load) was able to quickly extend his lead over Montoya to ten seconds by his first pit stop on lap 19. However, Barrichello's pit stop proved problematic: his refuelling rig failed to work which meant he was stationary for around six to seven seconds longer than planned.<sup id="cite_ref-gprace_31-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gprace-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello exited the stop in third position, ahead of teammate Michael Schumacher.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_32-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Häkkinen lost second during the course of the 19th lap, and then lost all gears on the previous lap which forced him to retire.<sup id="cite_ref-sunquotes_35-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sunquotes-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> Verstappen retired on the 26th lap because his engine stopped working.<sup id="cite_ref-gprace_31-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gprace-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> At the front of the field, Ralf Schumacher moved into the lead of the race when Montoya made his pit stop on lap 28,<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_32-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> where his pit crew made an adjustment to his front wing to rectify an issue Montoya had with oversteer,<sup id="cite_ref-gprace_31-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gprace-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> and he rejoined in third place with a deficit of 5.5 seconds.<sup id="cite_ref-lapchart_33-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapchart-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Frentzen retired from ninth position with a gearbox failure on lap 29.<sup id="cite_ref-gprace_31-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gprace-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Villeneuve made a pit stop from sixth on lap 34 and retained the position.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_32-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Ralf Schumacher opened a gap of 6.1 seconds over Barrichello by his one and only pit stop on lap 35 and rejoined in fourth.<sup id="cite_ref-lapchart_33-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapchart-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> de la Rosa was the last one-stop driver to pit on lap 36 from fifth, which he maintained upon rejoining the track. Michael Schumacher made his second pit stop on lap 40, and dropped behind Ralf Schumacher. Montoya moved back into the lead position when Barrichello made a pit stop on lap 41 and rejoined right behind Ralf Schumacher.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_32-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> At the completion of lap 42, with the scheduled pit stops completed, the order was Montoya, Ralf Schumacher, Barrichello, Michael Schumacher, de la Rosa, Villeneuve, Räikkönen, Alesi, Panis, Fisichella, Heidfeld, Bernoldi, Enge, Alonso and Yoong.<sup id="cite_ref-lapchart_33-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapchart-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Further down the field, Enge passed Bernoldi for twelfth on lap 43.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_32-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello ran in the <a href="/wiki/Drafting_(aerodynamics)" title="Drafting (aerodynamics)">slipstream</a> of Ralf Schumacher down the start/finish straight and Schumacher cut the Variante Goodyear chicane after attempting to defend his position, allowing Barrichello to take over second on lap 47. Yoong retired from the race on the same lap after he spun his car into a gravel trap.<sup id="cite_ref-gprace_31-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gprace-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-f1comrace_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrace-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Bernoldi became the final retirement of the race when his car developed a crankshaft sensor problem on the same lap.<sup id="cite_ref-f1comrace_36-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrace-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Barrichello closed the gap between himself and Montoya but was unable to catch up to the Colombian who crossed the finish line on lap 53 to clinch his first Formula One victory.<sup id="cite_ref-f1comrace_36-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrace-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> in a time of 1'16:58.393 at an average speed of 239.321 kilometres per hour (148.707&#160;mph). Barrichello finished in second position 5.1 seconds behind Montoya, Ralf Schumacher came in third, Michael Schumacher took fourth, de la Rosa took his best result of the season after he finished fifth and Villeneuve rounded out the points-scoring positions in sixth. Räikkönen was the final driver on the lead lap in a close seventh place, and Alesi, Panis, Fisichella, Heidfeld, Enge and Alonso were the last of the classified finishers.<sup id="cite_ref-chicanef1results_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-chicanef1results-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="After_the_race">After the race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2001_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: After the race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The top three drivers appeared on the podium to collect their trophies and in a later press conference. Montoya said he was "so happy" and "pleased" that he won his first race which was the first for a Colombian driver.<sup id="cite_ref-pressconf_34-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconf-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> He also said that he was not frustrated on not achieving his first victory in the past fourteen races as he was not expecting to win during the season.<sup id="cite_ref-pressconf_34-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconf-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello said that he felt that Ferrari put on "a good show" despite his slow pit stop from a fuel rig problem on lap 19. He also believed that his two-stop strategy was the right move and described his weekend as "one of my best".<sup id="cite_ref-pressconf_34-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconf-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> When asked if his car was inconsistent during the Grand Prix, Ralf Schumacher said this was not the case and stated although he had problem with his tyres he felt the Williams finish of first and third was "a great achievement".<sup id="cite_ref-pressconf_34-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconf-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Alesi attacked Briatore's role in preventing the drivers from abiding by the pact that was to see no overtaking in the first two chicanes and told the Italian and French media that some team principals threatened their drivers if they did not race. Nevertheless, he was relieved that the race passed without any major incident and said the reason why the majority of the drivers supported Michael Schumacher was that he "behaved like a perfect leader".<sup id="cite_ref-pactatlasf1_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pactatlasf1-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> Villeneuve said to British television station <a href="/wiki/ITV_(TV_channel)" title="ITV (TV channel)">ITV</a> that he felt discussing a no overtaking pact at the race circuit was not the appropriate place: "We are race car drivers, Because we signed contracts before the season and everyone was happy to be a race car driver and to earn millions of dollars. Because we knew a year ago that there would be a race at Monza and nobody complained."<sup id="cite_ref-pactatlasf1_40-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pactatlasf1-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> FIA president <a href="/wiki/Max_Mosley" title="Max Mosley">Max Mosley</a> stated that he disagreed with the pact and said that the drivers should have raised the concerns in the months leading up to the Grand Prix.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The result allowed Michael Schumacher to extend his lead in the Drivers' Championship to 50 points over Coulthard. Barrichello's second-place finish allowed him to narrow the gap to Coulthard to be three points behind. Ralf Schumacher remained in fourth position, while Montoya's victory meant he moved into fifth.<sup id="cite_ref-points_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Ferrari remained in the lead of the Constructors' Championship with an 80-point advantage over McLaren, who's points advantage over Williams had reduced to eight points. Sauber maintained fourth place, while BAR took over fifth position.<sup id="cite_ref-points_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
