<p>The <b>1966 Italian Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held at <a href="/wiki/Autodromo_Nazionale_di_Monza" title="Autodromo Nazionale di Monza">Monza</a> on September 4, 1966. It was race 7 of 9 in both the <a href="/wiki/1966_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1966 World Championship of Drivers">1966 World Championship of Drivers</a> and the <a href="/wiki/1966_International_Cup_for_Formula_One_Manufacturers" class="mw-redirect" title="1966 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers">1966 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers</a>. The race was the 36th <a href="/wiki/Italian_Grand_Prix" title="Italian Grand Prix">Italian Grand Prix</a> and the 32nd to be held at Monza. The race was held over 68 laps of the five kilometre circuit for a race distance of 391 kilometres.
</p><p>The race was won by Italian driver <a href="/wiki/Ludovico_Scarfiotti" title="Ludovico Scarfiotti">Ludovico Scarfiotti</a> driving a <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_312" title="Ferrari 312">Ferrari 312</a> in his only Grand Prix victory. Scarfiotti led home his British team mate <a href="/wiki/Mike_Parkes" title="Mike Parkes">Mike Parkes</a> by five seconds. Parkes only just defeated New Zealand racer <a href="/wiki/Denny_Hulme" title="Denny Hulme">Denny Hulme</a> in his <a href="/wiki/Brabham_BT20" title="Brabham BT20">Brabham BT20</a>, the pair separated by less than half a second.
</p><p>While series points leader <a href="/wiki/Jack_Brabham" title="Jack Brabham">Jack Brabham</a> stopped with an oil leak in his <a href="/wiki/Brabham_BT19" title="Brabham BT19">Brabham BT19</a> on lap seven, he secured his third world championship, and the unique achievement of becoming world champion in a car of his own make, when his only remaining points rival <a href="/wiki/John_Surtees" title="John Surtees">John Surtees</a> stopped with a fuel leak in his <a href="/wiki/Cooper_T81" title="Cooper T81">Cooper T81</a> 24 laps later.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_report">Race report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1966_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Race report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:1966_Italian_GP_-_Ludovico_Scarfiotti%27s_Ferrari_312_F1-66.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f4/1966_Italian_GP_-_Ludovico_Scarfiotti%27s_Ferrari_312_F1-66.jpg/220px-1966_Italian_GP_-_Ludovico_Scarfiotti%27s_Ferrari_312_F1-66.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="296" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f4/1966_Italian_GP_-_Ludovico_Scarfiotti%27s_Ferrari_312_F1-66.jpg/330px-1966_Italian_GP_-_Ludovico_Scarfiotti%27s_Ferrari_312_F1-66.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f4/1966_Italian_GP_-_Ludovico_Scarfiotti%27s_Ferrari_312_F1-66.jpg/440px-1966_Italian_GP_-_Ludovico_Scarfiotti%27s_Ferrari_312_F1-66.jpg 2x" data-file-width="800" data-file-height="1077" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:1966_Italian_GP_-_Ludovico_Scarfiotti%27s_Ferrari_312_F1-66.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Race winner <a href="/wiki/Ludovico_Scarfiotti" title="Ludovico Scarfiotti">Ludovico Scarfiotti</a> inside his <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_312" title="Ferrari 312">Ferrari 312</a> before the start</div></div></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Jack_Brabham" title="Jack Brabham">Jack Brabham</a> was odds-on favourite for the Championship – only <a href="/wiki/John_Surtees" title="John Surtees">John Surtees</a> could stop him and only then by winning all three remaining races. He was prevented from doing so by an impressive performance from his former employers <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> at their home track. <a href="/wiki/Honda_Racing_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Honda Racing F1">Honda</a> had brought a new 370&#160;bhp 3 litre V12 engine for <a href="/wiki/Richie_Ginther" title="Richie Ginther">Richie Ginther</a>, whilst <a href="/wiki/Dan_Gurney" title="Dan Gurney">Dan Gurney</a> had his <a href="/wiki/Weslake" title="Weslake">Weslake</a> engine and <a href="/wiki/Graham_Hill" title="Graham Hill">Graham Hill</a> had the <a href="/wiki/BRM" class="mw-redirect" title="BRM">BRM</a> H16. <a href="/wiki/Lorenzo_Bandini" title="Lorenzo Bandini">Lorenzo Bandini</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mike_Parkes" title="Mike Parkes">Mike Parkes</a> led from the start, whilst Hill's engine gave up on the first lap, followed in retirement by <a href="/wiki/Jackie_Stewart" title="Jackie Stewart">Jackie Stewart</a> on lap 5 with a fuel leak, and then Brabham on lap 8 with an engine failure which coated the car in oil. <a href="/wiki/Jim_Clark" title="Jim Clark">Jim Clark</a> in the H16 <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Lotus</a> was moving strongly through the field. On lap 17, <a href="/wiki/Richie_Ginther" title="Richie Ginther">Richie Ginther</a> had a horrific accident, crashing heavily into the trees and being very fortunate to escape with his life. Once Clark had pitted, <a href="/wiki/Ludovico_Scarfiotti" title="Ludovico Scarfiotti">Ludovico Scarfiotti</a>, Parkes, Surtees and Hill disputed the lead until Surtees withdrew with a split petrol tank which was pouring it all over his tyres and ending his championship chances. <a href="/wiki/Denny_Hulme" title="Denny Hulme">Denny Hulme</a> had fought through the field to third place. But the race was Scarfiotti's, as he became the first Italian to win for Ferrari at <a href="/wiki/Autodromo_Nazionale_di_Monza" title="Autodromo Nazionale di Monza">Monza</a> since <a href="/wiki/Alberto_Ascari" title="Alberto Ascari">Alberto Ascari</a> in 1952. Parkes completed Ferrari's joy as he pipped Hulme by 0.3 seconds to claim second spot. Amidst all the celebrations, Jack Brabham was crowned champion. Apart from three <a href="/wiki/North_American_Racing_Team" title="North American Racing Team">NART</a> entries in <a href="/wiki/1969_Formula_One_season" title="1969 Formula One season">1969</a> this race was the last time a Ferrari car was entered by a <a href="/wiki/Privateer_(motorsport)" title="Privateer (motorsport)">privateer team</a> when <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Baghetti" title="Giancarlo Baghetti">Giancarlo Baghetti</a> drove a private car entered by the British <a href="/wiki/Reg_Parnell_Racing" title="Reg Parnell Racing">Reg Parnell</a> team.
</p>
