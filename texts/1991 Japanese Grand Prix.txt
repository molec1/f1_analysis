<p>The <b>1991 Japanese Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>XVII Fuji Television Japanese Grand Prix</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at <a href="/wiki/Suzuka_Circuit" class="mw-redirect" title="Suzuka Circuit">Suzuka</a> on 20 October 1991. It was the fifteenth round of the <a href="/wiki/1991_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="1991 Formula One season">1991 Formula One season</a>. The 53-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> after he started from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>. His teammate <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> finished second and <a href="/wiki/Riccardo_Patrese" title="Riccardo Patrese">Riccardo Patrese</a> was third for the <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> team. 
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-race">Pre-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1991_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Pre-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Formula One moved to Japan with the fight for the title still open; this was the fifth year in a row that the title would be decided at the Suzuka circuit. Both championship contenders <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> knew exactly what they needed to do; Mansell had to win with help and Senna needed to beat Mansell. There were several changes to the driver lineup, the most notable being at Leyton House where <a href="/wiki/Ivan_Capelli" title="Ivan Capelli">Ivan Capelli</a> had been replaced by young Austrian <a href="/wiki/Karl_Wendlinger" title="Karl Wendlinger">Karl Wendlinger</a>, with Capelli being freed up as a spare driver for Ferrari if <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> decided not to enter. It was the first time the Leyton House/March team had not entered with Capelli as a named driver since their return to the grid in 1987, during which time he had partnered <a href="/wiki/Mauricio_Gugelmin" class="mw-redirect" title="Mauricio Gugelmin">Mauricio Gugelmin</a> in every race from 1988 onwards. Elsewhere, AGS had run out of money and did not make the voyage to Japan, while fellow stragglers Coloni had hired local driver <a href="/wiki/Naoki_Hattori" title="Naoki Hattori">Naoki Hattori</a>. <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Herbert" title="Johnny Herbert">Johnny Herbert</a> was back behind the wheel of the <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Lotus</a> after having missed a few races in the second half of the season due to <a href="/wiki/Formula_Nippon" class="mw-redirect" title="Formula Nippon">Japanese Formula 3000</a> commitments.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1991_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-qualifying_report">Pre-qualifying report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1991_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Pre-qualifying report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>With the withdrawal of <a href="/wiki/Automobiles_Gonfaronnaises_Sportives" title="Automobiles Gonfaronnaises Sportives">AGS</a> from Formula One prior to this event, and the return of the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Coloni" title="Scuderia Coloni">Coloni</a> team after missing the last grand Prix, the pre-qualifying pool was reduced to six cars.
</p><p>A <a href="/wiki/Brabham" title="Brabham">Brabham</a> topped the time sheets for the seventh time this season as <a href="/wiki/Martin_Brundle" title="Martin Brundle">Martin Brundle</a> was fastest, over a second ahead of <a href="/wiki/Alex_Caffi" title="Alex Caffi">Alex Caffi</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Footwork_Arrows" title="Footwork Arrows">Footwork</a>. It was only the second time in seven attempts that the Italian had pre-qualified. His team-mate <a href="/wiki/Michele_Alboreto" title="Michele Alboreto">Michele Alboreto</a> was third fastest, just under a tenth of a second slower. The fourth and final pre-qualifying position went to <a href="/wiki/Gabriele_Tarquini" title="Gabriele Tarquini">Gabriele Tarquini</a> for <a href="/wiki/Fondmetal" title="Fondmetal">Fondmetal</a>, the second time in as many attempts that he had pre-qualified for his new team.
</p><p>For the first time in 1991, the other Brabham failed to pre-qualify, as <a href="/wiki/Mark_Blundell" title="Mark Blundell">Mark Blundell</a> suffered an oil leak in his <a href="/wiki/Yamaha_Motor_Corporation" class="mw-redirect" title="Yamaha Motor Corporation">Yamaha</a> engine during the session. His time was only good enough for fifth place. Coloni had hired the 1990 Japanese <a href="/wiki/Formula_Three" title="Formula Three">Formula Three</a> champion, <a href="/wiki/Naoki_Hattori" title="Naoki Hattori">Naoki Hattori</a>, to replace <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Chaves" title="Pedro Chaves">Pedro Chaves</a> for the last two races of the season, but the car broke down before Hattori was able to post a representative time.<sup id="cite_ref-Walker_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Walker-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying_report">Qualifying report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1991_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Qualifying report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>There were a number of accidents during the qualifying sessions, including young sensation <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> and Ferrari driver <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a>. The biggest of these accidents was the one that befell <a href="/wiki/%C3%89ric_Bernard" title="Éric Bernard">Éric Bernard</a> who broke his ankle and would not take any further part in the race. At the end of the qualifying sessions it was a McLaren front row with <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> ahead of Ayrton Senna.  Mansell was third followed by the unhappy <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a>, <a href="/wiki/Riccardo_Patrese" title="Riccardo Patrese">Riccardo Patrese</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pierluigi_Martini" title="Pierluigi Martini">Pierluigi Martini</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gianni_Morbidelli" title="Gianni Morbidelli">Gianni Morbidelli</a>, Michael Schumacher, and <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a>. Senna was right where he wanted to be, while Mansell knew he had his work cut out for him.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1991_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_report">Race report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1991_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Race report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the start of the race Berger got away well and Senna blocked Mansell the way that Mansell had done to him in <a href="/wiki/1991_Portuguese_Grand_Prix" title="1991 Portuguese Grand Prix">Portugal</a>.  Elsewhere Jean Alesi's day ended on the first lap in a cloud of smoke, his Ferrari engine having blown. At the end of lap 1 the order was Berger, Senna, Mansell, Patrese, and Prost. There was a huge accident on lap two when <a href="/wiki/Andrea_de_Cesaris" title="Andrea de Cesaris">Andrea de Cesaris</a> spun his Jordan while leading other cars, and the resulting accident took Wendlinger, <a href="/wiki/Emanuele_Pirro" title="Emanuele Pirro">Pirro</a>, and <a href="/wiki/JJ_Lehto" title="JJ Lehto">Lehto</a> with him. At the front Berger pulled off into the lead while Mansell hounded Senna. It all ended on lap 10 when Mansell made a mistake on the first corner and spun off. His Williams was stuck in the gravel and Mansell was out of championship contention.
</p><p>With the news of Mansell's retirement, Senna and Berger both went off into the distance, with Berger waving his recrowned world champion teammate through. The two ran in formation for the rest of the race with Senna letting Berger through on the last lap to win his first race for McLaren.
</p><p>Patrese finished third followed by Prost, <a href="/wiki/Martin_Brundle" title="Martin Brundle">Martin Brundle</a> scoring the last points in the history of the Brabham team, and <a href="/wiki/Stefano_Modena" title="Stefano Modena">Stefano Modena</a> in the Tyrrell. Senna had won his third world championship.
</p><p>This was the last time Senna, Prost, Piquet and Mansell raced together.
</p>
