<p>The <b>2015 Belgian Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>2015 Formula 1 Shell Belgian Grand Prix</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Auto_racing" title="Auto racing">motor race</a> held on 23 August 2015 at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Spa-Francorchamps" title="Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps">Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps</a> in <a href="/wiki/Spa,_Belgium" title="Spa, Belgium">Spa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a>. It was the eleventh round of the <a href="/wiki/2015_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2015 Formula One season">2015 Formula One season</a>, and the 71st <a href="/wiki/Belgian_Grand_Prix" title="Belgian Grand Prix">Belgian Grand Prix</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> of <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> entered the event as the overall Drivers' Championship leader, 21 points ahead of his teammate <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> and 42 points ahead of <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a>. Mercedes led the Constructors' Championship by 147 points over Ferrari, while <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> entered the event in third, a further 85 points adrift. Having won the <a href="/wiki/2014_Belgian_Grand_Prix" title="2014 Belgian Grand Prix">2014 edition</a>, <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> was the defending race winner.
</p><p>Lewis Hamilton won the race for Mercedes, extending his championship lead over Rosberg, who finished second, to 28 points. <a href="/wiki/Romain_Grosjean" title="Romain Grosjean">Romain Grosjean</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lotus_F1" title="Lotus F1">Lotus</a> secured a podium finish for the first time since the <a href="/wiki/2013_United_States_Grand_Prix" title="2013 United States Grand Prix">2013 United States Grand Prix</a>, benefitting from a controversial tyre failure on Sebastian Vettel's Ferrari late in the race.
</p><p>The race was celebrated by Scuderia Ferrari as their 900th Grand Prix participation. <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> was handed a record 105-place grid penalty for a multitude of changes to their <a href="/wiki/Honda_in_Formula_One#Honda_as_an_engine_supplier" title="Honda in Formula One">Honda</a> power units.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Pirelli" title="Pirelli">Pirelli</a>, Formula One's sole tyre supplier since <a href="/wiki/2011_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2011 Formula One World Championship">2011</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> supplied the ten teams with four compounds of tyres for the event. The two dry selections were the white-banded medium compound as the "prime" tyre, while the yellow-banded soft compound was provided as the softer "option" choice.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The green-banded intermediate weather tyre and the blue-banded full wet compounds were brought to all events.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> As a consequence of <a href="/wiki/Konstantin_Tereshchenko" title="Konstantin Tereshchenko">Konstantin Tereshchenko</a>'s crash in a <a href="/wiki/GP3_Series" title="GP3 Series">GP3 Series</a> race in <a href="/wiki/2014_GP3_Series_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2014 GP3 Series season">2014</a>, new asphalt was laid down on the inside of the final corner, replacing the patch of grass that was there before.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> In addition, a new kerb was added at the exit of the famous Eau Rouge corner, on the apex of turn four. The kerb was however removed on Saturday after the FIA discussed the matter with the drivers. At turn one, the La Source hairpin, the exit kerb was shortened to make it easier to rejoin the track in the event of a driver running wide.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Mercedes_in_Spa_2015.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f9/Mercedes_in_Spa_2015.JPG/220px-Mercedes_in_Spa_2015.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f9/Mercedes_in_Spa_2015.JPG/330px-Mercedes_in_Spa_2015.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f9/Mercedes_in_Spa_2015.JPG/440px-Mercedes_in_Spa_2015.JPG 2x" data-file-width="3800" data-file-height="2850" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Mercedes_in_Spa_2015.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The new spoon shaped rear wing introduced on the <a href="/wiki/Mercedes_F1_W06_Hybrid" title="Mercedes F1 W06 Hybrid">Mercedes F1 W06 Hybrid</a></div></div></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Scuderia Ferrari</a> identified this race as their 900th Grand Prix participation.<sup id="cite_ref-ferrari_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ferrari-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Driving for the Scuderia was <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a>, the most successful active driver at <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Spa-Francorchamps" title="Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps">Spa</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-ferrari_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ferrari-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> who extended his contract with the team for an additional year in the week prior to the race.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> introduced a new low-downforce rear wing for Spa, featuring a spoon-shaped design, similar to that of the <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4-21" title="McLaren MP4-21">McLaren MP4-21</a> in <a href="/wiki/2006_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2006 Formula One World Championship">2006</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The aim was to find a balance between low-downforce for the long straights on the Spa circuit while at the same time retaining enough downforce for the more twisting second sector of the track.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a> on the other hand tested two different rear wings during Friday's free practice sessions. While <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> ran with a very low-downforce wing, <a href="/wiki/Daniil_Kvyat" title="Daniil Kvyat">Daniil Kvyat</a> tested a "more Spa-specific set-up", which was ultimately used by both drivers in qualifying and the race to prevent tyre degradation.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> came to Spa with a new specification power unit, aiming to reach the level of Ferrari's unit with the introduced changes.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> However, the team's racing director <a href="/wiki/%C3%89ric_Boullier" title="Éric Boullier">Éric Boullier</a> warned that it might take until <a href="/wiki/2015_Singapore_Grand_Prix" title="2015 Singapore Grand Prix">Singapore</a> until the gains could be visible and expected a "difficult" Belgian Grand Prix.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> In suit with the new power unit the team introduced a new engine cover. Further updates to the <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4-30" title="McLaren MP4-30">McLaren MP4-30</a> included an "unprecedented" tray design for the front of the floor, as well as lower sidepods and a new position for the radiator that cools the electronic recovery system.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Going into the weekend, <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> led the Drivers' Championship with 202 points, 21 ahead of second placed <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a>. <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> for Ferrari was a further 21 points behind Rosberg, having closed the gap by winning the <a href="/wiki/2015_Hungarian_Grand_Prix" title="2015 Hungarian Grand Prix">previous round in Hungary</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> In the Constructors' Championship, Mercedes with 383 points were leading Ferrari by 147 points, while third placed <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> were a further 85 points behind.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Regulation_changes">Regulation changes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Regulation changes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In an attempt to limit the influence of pit walls on their drivers during race starts, new regulations were introduced for the start procedure from the Belgian Grand Prix onwards. Drivers were now only allowed to adjust one clutch level after leaving the pit garage for the starting grid.<sup id="cite_ref-startmclaren_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-startmclaren-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> The response from drivers was mixed, with Nico Rosberg commenting that the new start procedures would be challenging,<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> while <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> opined that the changes were not significant,<sup id="cite_ref-startmclaren_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-startmclaren-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> and Daniel Ricciardo hoped the new procedure might help his team.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Lotus_legal_troubles">Lotus legal troubles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Lotus legal troubles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Lotus_F1" title="Lotus F1">Lotus team</a> experienced legal difficulties over the Spa weekend, as a legal battle with former test driver <a href="/wiki/Charles_Pic" title="Charles Pic">Charles Pic</a>, concerning the time the team had granted Pic at the wheel of their <a href="/wiki/2014_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2014 Formula One World Championship">2014</a> car. With the case in arbitration court, Lotus faced the threat of their cars being impounded and unable to move following the conclusion of the Grand Prix weekend.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> The bailiffs returned the cars to Lotus and the team was eventually able to travel to Italy,<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> after <a href="/wiki/Bernie_Ecclestone" title="Bernie Ecclestone">Bernie Ecclestone</a>, chief executive of the <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_Group" title="Formula One Group">Formula One Group</a>, "stepped in to ensure staff were paid", while their financial problems continued to question the team's future.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Free_practice">Free practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Free practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Per the regulations for the <a href="/wiki/2015_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2015 Formula One World Championship">2015</a> season, three practice sessions were held, two 90-minute sessions on Friday and another one-hour session before qualifying on Saturday.<sup id="cite_ref-pracreg_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pracreg-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> In the first practice session on Friday morning, Nico Rosberg was fastest after initial trouble with his power unit, a quarter of a second ahead of his Mercedes teammate Lewis Hamilton. Daniel Ricciardo (Red Bull) was third, just five hundredths of a second slower than Hamilton, with Kimi Räikkönen another tenth down in fourth. Fifty minutes into the session, a red flag came out when <a href="/wiki/Pastor_Maldonado" title="Pastor Maldonado">Pastor Maldonado</a> lost traction at the rear end of his <a href="/wiki/Lotus_E23_Hybrid" title="Lotus E23 Hybrid">Lotus E23 Hybrid</a> at the exit of Les Combes corner and crashed into the tyre barriers, damaging the front-right of his car. The session was stopped for ten minutes for the <a href="/wiki/Motorsport_marshal" title="Motorsport marshal">track marshals</a> to repair the barriers.<sup id="cite_ref-fp1_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fp1-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Speaking about his accident, Maldonado said: "It was very unlucky because I nearly saved the car. But anyway, it happened. This track is always very difficult when you have a moment or whatever, especially at that point where the second sector is quite narrow. But this can happen. Now we can't change that. We need to look forward."<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> In the second Lotus, <a href="/wiki/Romain_Grosjean" title="Romain Grosjean">Romain Grosjean</a> was again replaced by <a href="/wiki/Jolyon_Palmer" title="Jolyon Palmer">Jolyon Palmer</a>, who finished practice 17th.<sup id="cite_ref-fp1_26-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fp1-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Another accident marred the second practice session on Friday afternoon, when a rear tyre on Nico Rosberg's Mercedes blew, causing him to spin in the run-up to Blanchimont corner. Rosberg was able to avoid hitting the tyre barriers, but the session was nevertheless red-flagged in order to clear the track of the car. Prior to this incident, Rosberg had set the fastest time of the session with 1:49.687, three-tenths of a second faster than teammate Hamilton, in second. Ricciardo was again third, a further 0.4 seconds adrift, ahead of fellow Red Bull driver Daniil Kvyat, one second behind Rosberg. The Williams cars of <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> were 14th and 16th respectively, ahead of the two McLaren drivers, who were faster only than the two <a href="/wiki/Marussia_F1" title="Marussia F1">Manor Marussia</a> cars.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Rosberg's tyre failure was later deemed the result of an "external cut", excluding the possibility of "any structural integrity issues".<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Rosberg however remained worried about the reliability of the tyres for Sunday's race, saying: "The problem is that we don't really understand it. There are theories but there is no real evidence, so that's a bit worrying for sure."<sup id="cite_ref-tyre_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tyre-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>World champion Lewis Hamilton bounced back in the third session on Saturday morning, being quickest, almost half a second faster than Rosberg. In "an uneventful session", the two Ferrari drivers were third and fourth, with Vettel leading Räikkönen. Meanwhile, the <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a> drivers <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a> ended the session in fifth and eighth respectively, separated by the Red Bull drivers. The only nuisance in third practice occurred when Romain Grosjean almost spun having to go around Kimi Räikkönen in turn 16, complaining to his team about a "very, very dangerous" manoeuvre.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Qualifying consisted of three parts, 18, 15 and 12 minutes in length respectively, with five drivers eliminated from competing after each of the first two sessions.<sup id="cite_ref-pracreg_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pracreg-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Valtteri Bottas was first and last out on track during the first part of qualifying (Q1), starting the lap that saved him from elimination in the very last moments of the session. Excluded from further participation were both Manor Marussia cars, both McLaren drivers and <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Nasr" title="Felipe Nasr">Felipe Nasr</a>. <a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a> succeeded in progressing into Q2 despite complaining about a loss of power on his car.<sup id="cite_ref-f1fanqual_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1fanqual-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The second part of qualifying saw Kimi Räikkönen come to a halt on track due to a loss of oil pressure. The session was briefly red-flagged to clear the track and when it resumed, both Mercedes drivers decided not to head out again, since their first times where sufficient to advance to Q3. Max Verstappen did not compete in Q2 at all due to the problems on his car, leaving him in provisional 14th (bar his penalty) and Räikkönen in 15th on the grid.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> The other three drivers to be eliminated were <a href="/wiki/Marcus_Ericsson" title="Marcus Ericsson">Marcus Ericsson</a> for Sauber, Daniil Kvyat and Nico Hülkenberg. Hülkenberg's time was just three-tenths of a second slower than that of Sebastian Vettel in tenth and he became the only driver of a Mercedes-powered car not to compete for pole position.<sup id="cite_ref-f1fanqual_34-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1fanqual-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Hamilton_pole_Belgium_2015.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/12/Hamilton_pole_Belgium_2015.jpg/220px-Hamilton_pole_Belgium_2015.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/12/Hamilton_pole_Belgium_2015.jpg/330px-Hamilton_pole_Belgium_2015.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/12/Hamilton_pole_Belgium_2015.jpg/440px-Hamilton_pole_Belgium_2015.jpg 2x" data-file-width="6016" data-file-height="4016" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Hamilton_pole_Belgium_2015.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> celebrates taking pole position.</div></div></div>
<p>Lewis Hamilton was the first Mercedes driver to set a timed lap in Q3, being almost half a second faster than Rosberg in Q2. While Rosberg succeeded in bettering his times as well, he nevertheless ended up 0.458 seconds behind his teammate and second on the grid. This handed Lewis Hamilton his tenth pole position in eleven races, meaning that he secured the FIA Pole Position Trophy at this early stage of the season.<sup id="cite_ref-qual_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qual-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> The next fastest car, Valtteri Bottas for Williams was 1.3 seconds off Hamilton's time of 1:47.197. Fourth fastest was Grosjean, who delivered what the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> called a "stunningly impressive" lap. Sergio Pérez put in a surprising effort as well, finishing fifth for Force India. Meanwhile, Sebastian Vettel, who had won the previous race in Hungary, managed only ninth fastest after a mistake at the final chicane.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcqual_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcqual-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After qualifying, Hamilton stated his performance was mainly due to improvements he made in the middle sector of the lap, saying: "Sector two has been probably in the past a bit of a weak point. I knew the lines but could never really put the corners together. Definitely on those last two laps that was a very, very strong area for me."<sup id="cite_ref-tyre_32-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tyre-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Indeed, he gained most of his time on Rosberg in this sector, containing the majority of the circuit's corners.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcqual_37-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcqual-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> He became the first driver since <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> – who achieved the feat in <a href="/wiki/2000_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2000 Formula One World Championship">2000</a>–<a href="/wiki/2001_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2001 Formula One World Championship">2001</a><sup id="cite_ref-bbcqual_37-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcqual-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> – to secure six consecutive pole positions, while the last driver to score six consecutive poles in one season was <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> in <a href="/wiki/1999_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1999 Formula One World Championship">1999</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-qual_36-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qual-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Following their poor qualifying performance and grid penalties, Jenson Button expected a "lonely race" for McLaren, saying: "I actually enjoyed driving the car, and when you cross the finishing line and you see the time you think 'That's not bad'. But then when you see where you are, a second off the guy in front of you, it's a massive margin, it hurts."<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Penalties">Penalties</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Penalties">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Jenson_Button_Belgium_2015.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c9/Jenson_Button_Belgium_2015.jpg/220px-Jenson_Button_Belgium_2015.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c9/Jenson_Button_Belgium_2015.jpg/330px-Jenson_Button_Belgium_2015.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c9/Jenson_Button_Belgium_2015.jpg/440px-Jenson_Button_Belgium_2015.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3264" data-file-height="2448" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Jenson_Button_Belgium_2015.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> expected a "lonely race" after a poor qualifying and a grid penalty.</div></div></div>
<p>McLaren, who were expected to benefit from the start changes,<sup id="cite_ref-startmclaren_19-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-startmclaren-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> were set to start the race from the back of the grid. After they had already been allowed to use one additional power unit in the 2015 season, the team had used their sixth power unit for the Hungarian Grand Prix,<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> and now equipped both cars with a seventh, heavily revised version of their power unit for Belgium on Friday. McLaren made another power unit change on Saturday,<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> since a mid-season change in regulations meant that penalties could no longer result in in-race time penalties.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-McLaren_penalty_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-McLaren_penalty-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> The cumulative 105-place grid penalty set a new record in Formula One.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Max Verstappen received a ten-place grid penalty after he equipped his sixth power unit on Friday.<sup id="cite_ref-F1Fanatic_44-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1Fanatic-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> After Lotus changed the gearbox on Romain Grosjean's car on Saturday, Grosjean was handed a five-place grid penalty, moving him from fourth to ninth on the grid.<sup id="cite_ref-autosport.com_45-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosport.com-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> For the same reason, Kimi Räikkönen received a five-place grid penalty, which only set him back two places due to penalties of other drivers.<sup id="cite_ref-ReferenceA_46-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ReferenceA-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:H%C3%BClkenberg_Belgium_2015.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/69/H%C3%BClkenberg_Belgium_2015.jpg/220px-H%C3%BClkenberg_Belgium_2015.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/69/H%C3%BClkenberg_Belgium_2015.jpg/330px-H%C3%BClkenberg_Belgium_2015.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/69/H%C3%BClkenberg_Belgium_2015.jpg/440px-H%C3%BClkenberg_Belgium_2015.jpg 2x" data-file-width="6016" data-file-height="4016" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:H%C3%BClkenberg_Belgium_2015.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a> was unable to start the race.</div></div></div>
<p>Prior to the race start, Nico Hülkenberg returned to the pit garage after reporting a loss of power on his Force India, but he eventually made it to the grid. His problem returned during the formation lap and when his car stalled on the starting grid, the starting procedure was aborted and another formation lap commenced.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcrace_47-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcrace-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Sainz_Jr." title="Carlos Sainz Jr.">Carlos Sainz Jr.</a> experienced a similar problem during the second formation lap and was called into the pit lane. He was eventually sent out after the race started.<sup id="cite_ref-guardrace_48-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guardrace-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> The race distance was therefore shortened to 43 laps.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcrace_47-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcrace-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At the start, Nico Rosberg did not get away well, falling back to fifth behind Pérez, Ricciardo and Bottas, but overtook the latter for fourth by the end of the lap. Fernando Alonso did also start well for McLaren, moving up to twelfth, while Lewis Hamilton retained his lead, pulling clear of Pérez's Force India early on. On lap two, Pastor Maldonado became the second retirement of the race when he stopped his car due to a power failure. On lap six, Daniil Kvyat took eighth place from Felipe Massa, while Romain Grosjean had quick first laps as well, overtaking the second Williams of Bottas for sixth on lap eight.<sup id="cite_ref-guardrace_48-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guardrace-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Bottas_in_spa_2015.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/eb/Bottas_in_spa_2015.JPG/220px-Bottas_in_spa_2015.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/eb/Bottas_in_spa_2015.JPG/330px-Bottas_in_spa_2015.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/eb/Bottas_in_spa_2015.JPG/440px-Bottas_in_spa_2015.JPG 2x" data-file-width="3800" data-file-height="2849" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Bottas_in_spa_2015.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>At his first pit stop, <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a>' mechanics fitted his car with three soft-compound tyres and one medium-compound tyre. The erroneous tyre – the right-rear – resulted in Bottas serving a drive-through penalty.</div></div></div>
<p>An early pit stop by Ricciardo on lap eight forced the hand of Force India, who brought in Pérez one lap later, but Pérez still came out behind the Red Bull. Several more drivers came in over the next couple of laps, including Bottas and Grosjean, with the Williams taking back his position during the stops only to be overtaken again on lap ten. Bottas was also set for a penalty, as his team had simultaneously equipped him with two different types of tyre compounds. He would later serve a drive through penalty.<sup id="cite_ref-guardrace_48-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guardrace-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> Lap eleven saw a risky overtaking manoeuvre by Max Verstappen, who went around the outside of Nasr at Blanchimont to take eleventh place.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcrace_47-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcrace-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> Following these first stops, the order at the front was Hamilton, Rosberg, Vettel, Räikkönen and Ricciardo, with the first four yet to stop. Rosberg was the first to do so on lap 13, coming out slightly ahead of Pérez, who had overtaken Ricciardo a lap earlier. Pérez carried his momentum into Eau Rouge and up to turn seven, but ultimately failed to get the better of the Mercedes. Hamilton and Vettel made pit stops for new tyres on laps 14 and 15 respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-guardrace_48-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guardrace-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Over the next laps, Rosberg steadily closed on Hamilton in front without being able to catch his teammate. Lap 18 saw Romain Grosjean gain another position at Ricciardo's expense, moving into fourth. Just two laps later, a <a href="/wiki/Drag_reduction_system" title="Drag reduction system">DRS</a> aided move on Pérez handed him third place. On lap 21, Daniel Ricciardo's Red Bull came to a sudden stop at the exit of the 'Bus Stop' chicane, leading to a <a href="/wiki/Safety_car#Virtual_Safety_Car_.28VSC.29" title="Safety car">Virtual Safety Car</a> period, during which many drivers made pit stops for new tyres, among them Grosjean, Massa and Verstappen.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcrace_47-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcrace-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> By lap 29, Kvyat had made a pit stop for new soft tyres, being able to go fast until the end of the race, while Vettel decided to try and finish the race without pitting for new tyres a second time. Kvyat's newer tyres allowed him to overtake Bottas, Räikkönen, Massa and Pérez to eventually finish fourth. Sainz, Jr. eventually retired from the race on lap 34.<sup id="cite_ref-guardrace_48-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guardrace-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> Vettel's strategy however did not work out. By lap 42, less than two laps from the finish, his right rear tyre exploded while on the Kemmel straight, handing Grosjean the final podium position.<sup id="cite_ref-vettel1_49-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vettel1-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> Vettel ended his race in the pit lane, but was classified down in twelfth place. Unchallenged by his teammate in second, Lewis Hamilton finished the race to take his 39th Grand Prix victory.<sup id="cite_ref-guardrace_48-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guardrace-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Grosjean_third_in_Spa.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/Grosjean_third_in_Spa.JPG/220px-Grosjean_third_in_Spa.JPG" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/Grosjean_third_in_Spa.JPG/330px-Grosjean_third_in_Spa.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/Grosjean_third_in_Spa.JPG/440px-Grosjean_third_in_Spa.JPG 2x" data-file-width="3800" data-file-height="2850" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Grosjean_third_in_Spa.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The post-race podium ceremony</div></div></div>
<p>At the podium interviews, conducted by former Formula One driver <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a>, Lewis Hamilton said that he felt "fairly relaxed at the front", naming looking after the tyre wear as his only concern during the race. Nico Rosberg conceded that he "just completely messed up the start" and added that Hamilton "did a great job, so deserved to win". Romain Grosjean was delighted with his tenth career podium, speaking of "an incredible weekend" and claimed that the podium "has the feel of a race win". Asked about his title aspirations, Hamilton insisted that it was "definitely way too early" to speak about winning the world title. During the post-race press conference, Grosjean described the moment of Vettel's tyre failure as "a bit of a scary moment", saying that it was "very unfortunate he had that puncture".<sup id="cite_ref-conf_50-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-conf-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Roberto_Merhi_Belgium_2015.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2b/Roberto_Merhi_Belgium_2015.jpg/220px-Roberto_Merhi_Belgium_2015.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="128" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2b/Roberto_Merhi_Belgium_2015.jpg/330px-Roberto_Merhi_Belgium_2015.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2b/Roberto_Merhi_Belgium_2015.jpg/440px-Roberto_Merhi_Belgium_2015.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2580" data-file-height="1500" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Roberto_Merhi_Belgium_2015.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Roberto_Merhi" title="Roberto Merhi">Roberto Merhi</a> finished ahead of teammate <a href="/wiki/Will_Stevens" title="Will Stevens">Will Stevens</a> for the fourth race in succession.</div></div></div>
<p>Following the race, Sebastian Vettel voiced harsh criticism of tyre supplier Pirelli, concerning not only his own but also Nico Rosberg's tyre failure on Friday. He was quoted saying: "I think it is a sort of thing that keeps going around and no-one mentions. It is unacceptable. If Nico tells us he did not go off the track, he didn't go off the track, why should he lie to us? It is the same with me, I didn't go off the track – it is out of the blue the tyre exploded [...]." He saw the safety of the drivers at risks, asserting that "[i]f it happens 200 metres earlier, I am not standing here now."<sup id="cite_ref-vettel2_51-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vettel2-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> Pirelli's Motorsport Director <a href="/wiki/Paul_Hembery" title="Paul Hembery">Paul Hembery</a> reacted to Vettel's accusations by saying: "I am not going to criticise Sebastian. It is a hot moment and I don't want to enter into a war of words over that – it is pointless for everybody. We were concerned when we saw the number of laps that were going to be done. Nobody really suggested they were going to do a one-stop race and it was a bit of a surprise – if anything people were talking about doing three stops rather than two."<sup id="cite_ref-vettel2_51-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vettel2-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> Nico Rosberg however backed up his compatriot Vettel, telling the press: "The exploding tyres is poor. It shouldn't happen. That it keeps on happening in other categories as well and with me on Friday. Both of us were so lucky. If it had happened a couple of metres later or earlier, we would have one of the biggest shunts ever. They need to figure out something to make it safer."<sup id="cite_ref-vettel1_49-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-vettel1-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> He also called for immediate action considering the <a href="/wiki/2015_Italian_Grand_Prix" title="2015 Italian Grand Prix">Italian Grand Prix</a> – the race following Spa-Francorchamps – due to its nature as the fastest race of the year.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup> Pirelli reacted by demanding a maximum stint length for their tyres, a suggestion the company had already put in at the end of <a href="/wiki/2013_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2013 Formula One World Championship">2013</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> The day after the race, Pirelli announced that they had found cuts on other tyres from other teams, mainly on the rear tyres, and that they were still searching for the cause of the damages. However, the company reasserted their stance that while Rosberg's failure was caused by external damage, Vettel's was due to "pure wear".<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup> Vettel received additional support from Coulthard, who wrote that Vettel was "right to tackle Pirelli" over the issue of tyres,<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup> as well as Coulthard's former teammate Mika Häkkinen.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> Vettel once again defended the team's decision to try a one-stop race on the Tuesday after the race, saying: "Our strategy was never risky, at any point. The team is not to blame."<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup> When the tyre supplier released the findings of their own investigation into the matter, the company needed to overturn their claim that the failure was caused by wear, since the report proved that the damage to Vettel's tyre was caused by debris.<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Max_Verstappen_Belgium_2015.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a1/Max_Verstappen_Belgium_2015.jpg/220px-Max_Verstappen_Belgium_2015.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a1/Max_Verstappen_Belgium_2015.jpg/330px-Max_Verstappen_Belgium_2015.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a1/Max_Verstappen_Belgium_2015.jpg/440px-Max_Verstappen_Belgium_2015.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3264" data-file-height="2448" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Max_Verstappen_Belgium_2015.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a> was praised for his overtake on <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Nasr" title="Felipe Nasr">Felipe Nasr</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>Max Verstappen received particular praise for his overtake on Felipe Nasr through Blanchimont. Former driver <a href="/wiki/Allan_McNish" title="Allan McNish">Allan McNish</a> wrote: "This is the first time in my life I have seen someone go around the outside of someone at Blanchimont. That was big[.]"<sup id="cite_ref-bbcreport_59-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcreport-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup> At the FIA Prize Giving ceremony on 4 December 2015, Verstappen received an award for <i>Action of the Year</i>, acknowledging his manoeuvre.<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup> Jenson Button on the other hand called McLaren's race an "embarrassment", after he had suffered from a lack of battery power throughout the race, finishing only ahead of the two Manor Marussia drivers.<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup> Lotus disclosed that Maldonado's retirement was in part caused by "heavy contact with the kerbs",<sup id="cite_ref-maldonado_62-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-maldonado-62">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup> with the intensity of impact being as high as 17&#160;<i>g<sub>0</sub></i> (170&#160;m/s<sup>2</sup>).<sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup> Technical director Nick Chester felt it was "a big shame" that Maldonado, who had been running ahead of eventual third placed Grosjean when he retired, was unable to contribute to a "strong result" for the team.<sup id="cite_ref-maldonado_62-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-maldonado-62">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup> Lotus later revealed that the retirement was caused by damage to the clutch control system sustained when he went off the track at Eau Rouge. At the same time, the team applauded Grosjean for a "faultless" drive.<sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64">&#91;64&#93;</a></sup> Additional praise for Grosjean came from motorsport journalist <a href="/wiki/Mark_Hughes_(journalist)" title="Mark Hughes (journalist)">Mark Hughes</a>, who felt that Grosjean was "still seriously under-rated" in Formula One.<sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65">&#91;65&#93;</a></sup> Williams launched an internal investigation into the tyre mix-up at Valtteri Bottas's first pit stop, feeling that it might have cost him a podium position.<sup id="cite_ref-66" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-66">&#91;66&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As a result of the race, Lewis Hamilton extended his championship lead over Rosberg to 28 points, the biggest margin it had been all season up to that point. Vettel's tyre failure cost him points as he was now 67 points off the lead in the standings.<sup id="cite_ref-67" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-67">&#91;67&#93;</a></sup> In the Constructors' Championship, Mercedes extended their lead over Ferrari to 184 points, while Lotus moved into fifth place at the expense of Force India.<sup id="cite_ref-68" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-68">&#91;68&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
