<p>The <b>2007 Gran Premio de España Telefónica</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race, fourth round of the <a href="/wiki/2007_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2007 Formula One season">2007 Formula One season</a>, won by <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a>. It was held on 13 May 2007 at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Catalunya" class="mw-redirect" title="Circuit de Catalunya">Circuit de Catalunya</a> in Spain.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2007_Spanish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2007_Spanish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Prior to the Spanish Grand Prix, the Circuit de Catalunya went through some circuit revisions to encourage overtaking and make the racing more exciting and also to improve safety standards.<sup id="cite_ref-Circuit_Revisions_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Circuit_Revisions-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The revisions included a <a href="/wiki/Chicane" title="Chicane">chicane</a> placed at the final two corners. "Although I have always really enjoyed the final two corners and their speed, the new chicane at the end of the track will hopefully lead to more overtaking during the race, which is great for the spectators," <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> commented.<sup id="cite_ref-Circuit_Revisions_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Circuit_Revisions-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Because of the revisions, the fastest lap of the race, (recorded by Massa), was fractionally over six seconds longer than the fastest lap of the race in 2006, (also recorded by Massa).
</p><p>Just over a week before the weekend of the grand prix, there was an organised test session at the track<sup id="cite_ref-Four_day_tests_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Four_day_tests-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> for four days, with primary focus on aerodynamic set-ups. However, due to heavy rain on the Tuesday,<sup id="cite_ref-Rain_hits_Tuesday_test._3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rain_hits_Tuesday_test.-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> many teams abandoned their initial test schedule and instead looked to focus on wet-weather set-ups. Both <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> and <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a> took advantage of these conditions and scored first and second fastest times respectively on both Tuesday and Wednesday.<sup id="cite_ref-Ferrari_on_top_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ferrari_on_top-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The wet weather eventually led to a fourth day of testing, in which Red Bull driver <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> broke Ferrari's run and set the fastest time by 0.2 seconds.<sup id="cite_ref-Coulthard_sets_fastest_test_time._5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Coulthard_sets_fastest_test_time.-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The aerodynamic focus was evident in the unveiling of two new and radical front aero designs by both the <a href="/wiki/Honda_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Honda F1">Honda</a><sup id="cite_ref-Honda_grows_elephant_ears._6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Honda_grows_elephant_ears.-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a><sup id="cite_ref-McLaren_debut_radical_front_wing._7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-McLaren_debut_radical_front_wing.-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> teams. Both teams would be understandably reluctant to release figures to support their reasoning for using such radical winglets, although McLaren's consistent speed throughout the testing is noted by only having out-paced championship rivals Ferrari once.<sup id="cite_ref-Coulthard_sets_fastest_test_time._5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Coulthard_sets_fastest_test_time.-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Honda, however, ended up half a second down on the nearest car on the final day of testing where they unveiled their "elephant ear"<sup id="cite_ref-Honda_grows_elephant_ears._6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Honda_grows_elephant_ears.-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> winglets. McLaren punctuated their need for aero testing over rain testing by withdrawing on the rained-out Tuesday.<sup id="cite_ref-Rain_hits_Tuesday_test._3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rain_hits_Tuesday_test.-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice">Practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2007_Spanish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> headed the first practice session in his <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>, just 0.4seconds ahead of team-mate <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>, who was in front of his home crowd. <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> was third in his <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, but his team-mate <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> was demoted to fifth in the closing moments, as <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a> took fourth in his <a href="/wiki/BMW_Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="BMW Sauber">BMW</a>. <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Davidson" title="Anthony Davidson">Anthony Davidson</a> finished sixth in his <a href="/wiki/Super_Aguri_F1" title="Super Aguri F1">Super Aguri</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-Davidson_pleased_with_Aguri_pace_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Davidson_pleased_with_Aguri_pace-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> with the <a href="/wiki/Toyota_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Toyota F1">Toyotas</a> of <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> in 7th and 8th. <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> rounded out the top ten.<sup id="cite_ref-Practice_1:_Lewis_heads_McLaren_1-2_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Practice_1:_Lewis_heads_McLaren_1-2-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Alonso turned the tables on Hamilton in the second practice session, with Alonso three tenths ahead of the rest of the field, but Hamilton was back in fifth. McLaren team boss <a href="/wiki/Ron_Dennis" title="Ron Dennis">Ron Dennis</a> said that they had made the "best possible start to the weekend".<sup id="cite_ref-Renault_plays_down_practice_speed_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Renault_plays_down_practice_speed-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renaults</a> of <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> and <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> produced a late surge to finish 2nd and 3rd, ahead of both Ferraris, who were down in 4th and 6th. However, Renault's engineering director <a href="/wiki/Pat_Symonds" title="Pat Symonds">Pat Symonds</a> played down the times, saying they were "done with a lower fuel load" and "are therefore unrepresentatively high in the timesheets".<sup id="cite_ref-McLaren_happy_with_perfect_Friday_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-McLaren_happy_with_perfect_Friday-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Behind the top three teams, Nico Rosberg was again in the top ten in his <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a>, with engineering director <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Head" title="Patrick Head">Patrick Head</a> saying they've been "playing catch up today",<sup id="cite_ref-Williams_makes_up_for_lost_time_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Williams_makes_up_for_lost_time-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> after a high-speed crash by Rosberg during the Barcelona test.<sup id="cite_ref-Second_crash_at_Spanish_GP_track_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Second_crash_at_Spanish_GP_track-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Although out of the top six, <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> said his eighth place "doesn't reflect how competitive we are",<sup id="cite_ref-Heidfeld:_There&#39;s_more_pace_to_come_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Heidfeld:_There&#39;s_more_pace_to_come-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> with <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Webber</a> and <a href="/wiki/Scott_Speed" title="Scott Speed">Scott Speed</a> rounding out the top ten.<sup id="cite_ref-Practice_2:_Alonso_thrills_home_fans_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Practice_2:_Alonso_thrills_home_fans-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It was the second McLaren one-two of the weekend as Hamilton took first ahead of Alonso in Saturday practice. Behind them, both BMWs were in front of both Ferraris. They were split by <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a>. Anthony Davidson put in another good practice performance with eighth in his <a href="/wiki/Super_Aguri_F1" title="Super Aguri F1">Super Aguri</a>, with Nico Rosberg and Heikki Kovalainen rounding out the top ten.<sup id="cite_ref-Sat_practice:_Lewis_gears_up_for_pole_bid_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sat_practice:_Lewis_gears_up_for_pole_bid-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2007_Spanish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Fernando Alonso looked likely to take pole at his home Grand Prix. Alonso crossed the line at the end of Q3 to take pole, unaware that Felipe Massa still had one lap in hand. The Brazilian pipped pole from the home hero by three-one hundredths of a second.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2007_Spanish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Lewis_Hamilton_Spain_2007_(crop).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e8/Lewis_Hamilton_Spain_2007_%28crop%29.jpg/300px-Lewis_Hamilton_Spain_2007_%28crop%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="300" height="111" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e8/Lewis_Hamilton_Spain_2007_%28crop%29.jpg/450px-Lewis_Hamilton_Spain_2007_%28crop%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e8/Lewis_Hamilton_Spain_2007_%28crop%29.jpg/600px-Lewis_Hamilton_Spain_2007_%28crop%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1323" data-file-height="489" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Lewis_Hamilton_Spain_2007_(crop).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>'s second place put him into the lead of the championship.</div></div></div>
<p>The first start was aborted when <a href="/wiki/Toyota_Racing_(Formula_One_team)" title="Toyota Racing (Formula One team)">Toyota</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> stalled on the grid, forcing another formation lap and decreasing the race length to 65 laps. The race got underway shortly thereafter with both <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> and World Champion <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> going into the corner ahead of their team-mates <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> respectively. This order was short-lived as Alonso and Massa touched briefly going into the first corner, sending Alonso over the gravel with him returning to the track behind Räikkönen's <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> in fourth.<sup id="cite_ref-Massa_Takes_Dominate_Win_in_Spain._17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Massa_Takes_Dominate_Win_in_Spain.-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The first lap saw the first of eight retirements, that being Austrian <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Wurz" title="Alexander Wurz">Alexander Wurz</a> as he was caught unaware by Toyota driver <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> braking suddenly to avoid <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a>, as the Italian returned to the track after a minor off.<sup id="cite_ref-Massa_Takes_Dominate_Win_in_Spain._17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Massa_Takes_Dominate_Win_in_Spain.-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Six laps later, as Massa was building a lead over Hamilton, Australian <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> retired when the hydraulic problems that plagued him during qualifying returned. A lap later, Trulli, who had started from the pit lane after stalling on the grid, also pulled into the pits and retired due to a failing fuel line.
</p><p>On lap nine the electrics on Kimi Räikkönen's Ferrari failed, leaving him to coast to the pits from half way around the track. <a href="/wiki/Scott_Speed" title="Scott Speed">Scott Speed</a>'s tyre failed spectacularly on the main straight shortly afterwards, leaving debris that remained there for the rest of the race.
</p><p>As the first round of pit-stops took place, a fire briefly lit into full blaze on the back the remaining Ferrari of race leader Felipe Massa. The car was undamaged and after all the cars had stopped he was still some ten seconds ahead of Briton Lewis Hamilton. The remaining Toro Rosso of <a href="/wiki/Vitantonio_Liuzzi" title="Vitantonio Liuzzi">Vitantonio Liuzzi</a> suffered similar hydraulic problems to those befell him in qualifying.
</p><p>Italian <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a> driver, <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a>, battled with the <a href="/wiki/Honda_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Honda F1">Honda</a> of <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a>, passing the Brazilian after the second stops. While most drivers negotiated their second stops smoothly, <a href="/wiki/BMW_in_Formula_One" title="BMW in Formula One">BMW Sauber</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> lost a probable fourth position, which would have been his fourth in as many races, when his right front wheel was not properly secured during the pit stop. He coasted around the circuit for another stop and returned to the track more than a lap down.
</p><p>More drama followed the Hondas as drivers Barrichello and <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> touched as the latter exited the pits. Button was forced to return to the pits for a new front wing<sup id="cite_ref-Massa_Takes_Dominate_Win_in_Spain._17-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Massa_Takes_Dominate_Win_in_Spain.-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> whilst Barrichello raced on, futilely attempting to catch Fisichella's Renault.
</p><p>Through all the retirements and bungled pit-stops, Red Bull driver <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> raced to what became a strong fifth position despite losing third gear in the team's new quick shift gearbox. Despite Williams driver <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Rosberg's</a> best attempts, he was unable to pass the Scot. This was Red Bull's strongest result so far this season and highlighted the improving performance of the <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Newey" title="Adrian Newey">Adrian Newey</a> designed <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_RB3" title="Red Bull RB3">RB3</a> chassis.
</p><p>Renault's day went sour after problems with the refuelling rig meant that both Fisichella and Kovalainen had to pit again forcing Heikki down to 7th of what would have been a probable 5th and pushed Giancarlo out of the points altogether. He still remained ahead of the Hondas of Button and Barrichello and the Super Aguri for Anthony Davidson.
</p><p>Ralf Schumacher retired with 20 laps remaining in the race. On American SPEED Channel coverage, commentator Bob Varsha joked that Schumacher was pitting from "somewhere around <a href="/wiki/Andorra" title="Andorra">Andorra</a>" alluding to the German's very slow pace all day.
</p><p>Massa went on to win the race by almost seven seconds<sup id="cite_ref-2007_Spanish_Grand_Prix._18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2007_Spanish_Grand_Prix.-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> over the <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> pair of Hamilton and Alonso, respectively second and third. This result meant that Hamilton, after a Formula One career of only four races, led the championship. He is the youngest driver to do so, taking the record from McLaren team founder, <a href="/wiki/Bruce_McLaren" title="Bruce McLaren">Bruce McLaren</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Massa_Takes_Dominate_Win_in_Spain._17-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Massa_Takes_Dominate_Win_in_Spain.-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Pole</a> <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a> made up for his team-mate's gearbox induced retirement by continuing the team's run of fourth-place finishes, while <a href="/wiki/Aguri_Suzuki" title="Aguri Suzuki">Aguri Suzuki</a>'s Japanese <a href="/wiki/Super_Aguri" class="mw-redirect" title="Super Aguri">Super Aguri</a> team notched up their first ever points with an eighth place by <a href="/wiki/Takuma_Sato" title="Takuma Sato">Takuma Sato</a>. "I just cannot describe how happy we are and a point means much more than anything else for us and this is an absolutely fantastic result" Sato went on to say.<sup id="cite_ref-Historic_First_Point_for_Super_Aguri._19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Historic_First_Point_for_Super_Aguri.-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
