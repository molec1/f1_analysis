<p>The <b>2001 United States Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>XXX SAP United States Grand Prix</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Auto_racing" title="Auto racing">motor race</a> held on September 30, 2001 at <a href="/wiki/Indianapolis_Motor_Speedway" title="Indianapolis Motor Speedway">Indianapolis Motor Speedway</a> in <a href="/wiki/Indianapolis,_Indiana" class="mw-redirect" title="Indianapolis, Indiana">Indianapolis, Indiana</a> in the United States. The 73-lap race was the sixteenth and penultimate round of the <a href="/wiki/2001_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2001 Formula One season">2001 Formula One season</a> and was won by <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a>. It was the first international sport/race event held in the United States after the <a href="/wiki/September_11,_2001_attacks" class="mw-redirect" title="September 11, 2001 attacks">September 11, 2001 attacks</a>. Adding to the victory was the fact that the win also occurred two days after Häkkinen's 33rd birthday. It was Häkkinen's final victory and podium of his career. This was also the final live TV commentary for British veteran commentator <a href="/wiki/Murray_Walker" title="Murray Walker">Murray Walker</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Summary">Summary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2001_United_States_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Summary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Finland" title="Finland">Finland</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> overcame some early race-day adversity and won the second United States Grand Prix at Indianapolis by 11 seconds over newly crowned World Champion and pole-sitter <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> before a season-high crowd estimated at 175,000. "This Grand Prix is definitely one of my important victories," Häkkinen said. "Because I rate <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Monaco" title="Circuit de Monaco">Monaco</a>, <a href="/wiki/Silverstone_Circuit" title="Silverstone Circuit">Silverstone</a> and <a href="/wiki/Indianapolis_Motor_Speedway" title="Indianapolis Motor Speedway">Indianapolis</a>, I think, as the Grands Prix a Grand Prix driver wants to win. It's something special. So this is something I'm never going to forget." It was the twentieth win of Häkkinen's career, and the last before fulfilling his stated plan to take a year off from racing, which wound up being retirement at the end of the season.
</p><p>After a qualifying battle with Schumacher on Saturday and securing a front-row starting position, Häkkinen dug himself a hole on a cool but sunny race day: first, he slid off the track into the guardrail at the end of the infield straight in the morning warm-up, damaging his <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>'s suspension and requiring a heroic job by his crew to get the car ready to race. Three hours later, he learned that his failure to wait for the green light to begin the warm-up session had caused his best qualifying lap to be taken away, dropping him from second to fourth position on the grid.
</p><p>At the start, <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a> pulled his <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a> from third spot around the outside of Michael Schumacher's <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> into Turn 1. Side by side through the initial right-hander, the two avoided contact by inches as Schumacher closed the door and seized the advantage into the left-handed Turn 2. It soon became clear, however, that the fastest car on the track was the Ferrari of <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a>, ostensibly carrying a light fuel load and on a two-stop strategy. The Brazilian took second from Montoya beginning Lap 3, then passed Schumacher two laps later for the lead.
</p><p>While Barrichello was making his way to the front, three of the field's youngest drivers, <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a>
teammates <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a>, along with <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a>, entered the braking zone for Turn 1 three-wide on the second lap. Räikkönen became the meat in the sandwich and had his front wing broken by contact with Trulli. He retired immediately after pitting for repairs, while Heidfeld and Trulli were undamaged and both eventually ended up in the points.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Usgp_2001_ims_montoya_leads_schumacher.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2a/Usgp_2001_ims_montoya_leads_schumacher.jpg/300px-Usgp_2001_ims_montoya_leads_schumacher.jpg" decoding="async" width="300" height="201" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2a/Usgp_2001_ims_montoya_leads_schumacher.jpg/450px-Usgp_2001_ims_montoya_leads_schumacher.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2a/Usgp_2001_ims_montoya_leads_schumacher.jpg/600px-Usgp_2001_ims_montoya_leads_schumacher.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3396" data-file-height="2280" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Usgp_2001_ims_montoya_leads_schumacher.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a> leads <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> through the infield section during the 2001 United States Grand Prix</div></div></div>
<p>Once in the lead, Barrichello quickly opened up a considerable gap back to Schumacher, and by his first pit stop on Lap 27, he led by 12.5 seconds. He returned from the pits in fifth place, behind the two McLarens, as Schumacher returned to the lead. Montoya, meanwhile, was flying as his <a href="/wiki/Michelin" title="Michelin">Michelin</a> tires began to gain traction and provide him an advantage. On Lap 34, just a lap after nearly losing control in Turn 4, Montoya took the lead by outbraking Schumacher down the inside of Turn 1 as they approached the <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a> of <a href="/wiki/Alex_Yoong" title="Alex Yoong">Alex Yoong</a>. "I was trying to get close enough coming into the straight," Montoya said later, "and that time I knew I was going to get close enough because of traffic. I just went for it." Michael Schumacher said later, "I don't know where he came from." Almost immediately, Montoya built a 2.3-second advantage, recording the fastest lap of the race before pitting on Lap 36 and returning in fifth. Once again, Schumacher led, this time from Häkkinen, with neither car having visited the pits.
</p><p>Just two laps later, immediately after <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> had spun and stalled his Williams in Turn 6, the crowd on the front straight groaned as Montoya moved over to the pit wall and rolled to a stop. "It (the Williams) was really good," Montoya said a few minutes later, at the back of his garage. "The car was really competitive. I wanted to really go for the win here, and it's a big disappointment we couldn't finish the race. I think the engine was running really strong. I don't know, I think it was a hydraulic (problem) because I lost all the gears and everything. When I stopped, the engine was still running."
</p><p>When Schumacher stopped on Lap 39, the McLarens were briefly first and second ahead of Barrichello.
<a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> surrendered second place by pitting on Lap 42 and returned in fourth, while Häkkinen took control of the race by staying out until Lap 46. At Häkkinen's stop, Barrichello led again until his second stop on Lap 50, when Häkkinen took the lead for good, having won the strategy battle with the World Champions.
</p><p>As Barrichello returned from his second stop in second place, he seemed to be the only one with a
chance to challenge Häkkinen. Both Williams were gone, and the teammates of the two front-runners were showing no signs of mounting an attack, but it seemed the Brazilian might indeed have the speed to chase down the Finn's McLaren. The gap dropped steadily, down to 2.2 seconds on Lap 61, until smoke started to appear intermittently at the back of Barrichello's car. The engine note on the long front straight each time by made it clear that the usually reliable Ferrari was on its last legs. On Lap 71, with Coulthard closing in, Schumacher could hope no longer that his teammate would limp home in second. The German took second from Barrichello, and on the next lap, Coulthard took third, just a few turns before the Ferrari's engine failed and spun on the infield straight.
</p><p>It was a disappointing end for Barrichello, whose chance for second place in the Driver's Championship slipped further away, but a tremendously popular and rewarding win for Mika Häkkinen. Having endured a frustrating and unproductive season, not a Championship contender for the first time in four years, and one race away from a voluntary "sabbatical" from racing, Häkkinen basked in the crowd's glow, for what would prove to be the last time.
</p><p>Jarno Trulli's three points for fourth place moved Jordan ahead of fellow <a href="/wiki/Honda_Racing_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Honda Racing F1">Honda</a>-powered <a href="/wiki/British_American_Racing" title="British American Racing">BAR</a> in the Constructor's Championship, a result that endured after the <a href="/wiki/2001_Japanese_Grand_Prix" title="2001 Japanese Grand Prix">final race in Japan</a>. <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a> finished fifth, his first time in the points since making the podium in <a href="/wiki/2001_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="2001 Monaco Grand Prix">Monaco</a>, and Nick Heidfeld's sixth place was a fitting reward for a fine weekend.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2001_United_States_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>This race saw both <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> drivers finished on the podium for the only time of the season.</li>
<li>This was the <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> team's first victory in the United States since <a href="/wiki/1991_United_States_Grand_Prix" title="1991 United States Grand Prix">1991</a>.</li>
<li>At the start of the race, <a href="/wiki/Tom%C3%A1%C5%A1_Enge" title="Tomáš Enge">Tomáš Enge</a> did not get away and stalled on the dummy grid.</li>
<li>Since the race was less than three weeks after the <a href="/wiki/September_11,_2001_attacks" class="mw-redirect" title="September 11, 2001 attacks">September 11, 2001 attacks</a>, members of some teams took alternate air routes from Europe (through <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia,_Pennsylvania" class="mw-redirect" title="Philadelphia, Pennsylvania">Philadelphia</a> or <a href="/wiki/Chicago,_Illinois" class="mw-redirect" title="Chicago, Illinois">Chicago</a>), apparently for safety reasons.</li></ul>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Usgp_2001_ims_jordan_gp_garage_flag.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/62/Usgp_2001_ims_jordan_gp_garage_flag.jpg/300px-Usgp_2001_ims_jordan_gp_garage_flag.jpg" decoding="async" width="300" height="201" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/62/Usgp_2001_ims_jordan_gp_garage_flag.jpg/450px-Usgp_2001_ims_jordan_gp_garage_flag.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/62/Usgp_2001_ims_jordan_gp_garage_flag.jpg/600px-Usgp_2001_ims_jordan_gp_garage_flag.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3407" data-file-height="2280" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Usgp_2001_ims_jordan_gp_garage_flag.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A tribute to the US by <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan Grand Prix</a></div></div></div>
<ul><li>Also in response to the terrorist attacks, many of the teams and drivers had special US tributes on their cars and helmets. Jordan's Jarno Trulli added to the standard American flag theme with the words, "Peace no war." The Jordan cars had American flags prominently displayed on the airboxes (see photo).</li>
<li>Prior to the race, <a href="/wiki/John_Mellencamp" title="John Mellencamp">John Mellencamp</a> performed his song "Peaceful World," since the race was less than 3 weeks after September 11.</li>
<li>In the race, <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a>'s Jordan carried the number "200" on its sidepods, commemorating his 200th (and penultimate) Grand Prix.</li>
<li>Several hours after the race, Jarno Trulli had his fourth-place result taken away for having too much wear on the plank under his Jordan, but the team's appeal of the decision was accepted four weeks later and the result was reinstated.</li>
<li>This was the final race <a href="/wiki/Murray_Walker" title="Murray Walker">Murray Walker</a> commentated after 23 years of Formula 1 commentary. He stated that it was particularly emotional given the context of the terrorist attacks, saying "Hopefully [the race] will have done something to lift the spirits of America. It's certainly lifted mine." <sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> <sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
