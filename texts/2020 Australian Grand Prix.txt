<p>The <b>2020 Australian Grand Prix</b> (formally known as the <b>Formula 1 Rolex Australian Grand Prix 2020</b>)<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_racing" title="Formula One racing">Formula One motor race</a> that was due to be held on 15 March 2020 in <a href="/wiki/Melbourne" title="Melbourne">Melbourne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Victoria,_Australia" class="mw-redirect" title="Victoria, Australia">Victoria</a>. The race was to be contested at the <a href="/wiki/Albert_Park_Circuit" title="Albert Park Circuit">Albert Park Circuit</a> and was intended to be the first round of the <a href="/wiki/2020_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2020 Formula One World Championship">2020 Formula One World Championship</a>. The race was cancelled due to the <a href="/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic" title="COVID-19 pandemic">COVID-19 pandemic</a>.
</p><p>The race would have been the 85th race in the combined history of the <a href="/wiki/Australian_Grand_Prix" title="Australian Grand Prix">Australian Grand Prix</a> — which dates back to the <a href="/wiki/1928_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="1928 Australian Grand Prix">100 Miles Road Race</a> of 1928 — as well as the 25th time the event had been held at the Albert Park circuit and the 36th time the Australian Grand Prix had been a part of the Formula One World Championship. This was the first Formula One race to be cancelled since the <a href="/wiki/2011_Bahrain_Grand_Prix" title="2011 Bahrain Grand Prix">2011 Bahrain Grand Prix</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> which was cancelled due to <a href="/wiki/Bahraini_uprising_of_2011" title="Bahraini uprising of 2011">civil unrest</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> entered the round as the defending <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">World Drivers' Champion</a> and his team, <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a>, was the defending <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">World Constructors' Champions</a>. His team-mate <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a> was the defending race winner.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2020_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Australian Grand Prix was officially confirmed as the first of twenty-two races of the <a href="/wiki/2020_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2020 Formula One World Championship">2020 Formula One World Championship</a> at an <a href="/wiki/FIA_World_Motor_Sport_Council" title="FIA World Motor Sport Council">FIA World Motor Sport Council</a> meeting in Paris in December 2019. The race was due to take place at the sixteen-turn, 5.303&#160;km (3.295&#160;mi) <a href="/wiki/Albert_Park_Circuit" title="Albert Park Circuit">Albert Park Circuit</a> in <a href="/wiki/Melbourne" title="Melbourne">Melbourne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Victoria_(Australia)" title="Victoria (Australia)">Victoria</a> on 15 March 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-calendar_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-calendar-7">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Entrants">Entrants</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2020_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Entrants">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Further information: <a href="/wiki/2020_Formula_One_World_Championship#Entries" title="2020 Formula One World Championship">2020 Formula One World Championship §&#160;Entries</a></div>
<p>Ten teams each with two drivers entered the race.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_AlphaTauri" title="Scuderia AlphaTauri">Scuderia AlphaTauri</a> were due to make their debut as a constructor having previously competed under the name <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Scuderia Toro Rosso</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Esteban_Ocon" title="Esteban Ocon">Esteban Ocon</a> was due to return to the championship, replacing <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a> at <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Nicholas_Latifi" title="Nicholas Latifi">Nicholas Latifi</a> was scheduled to make his competitive debut with <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a>, taking the seat previously filled by <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> However, these events were all postponed by the cancellation of the race.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Philip_Morris_International" title="Philip Morris International">Mission Winnow</a>, the title sponsor of <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, was banned from the race as it did not comply with local laws governing tobacco sponsorship.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Impact_of_the_COVID-19_pandemic">Impact of the COVID-19 pandemic</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2020_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Impact of the COVID-19 pandemic">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/2020_Formula_One_World_Championship#Impact_of_the_COVID-19_pandemic" title="2020 Formula One World Championship">2020 Formula One World Championship §&#160;Impact of the COVID-19 pandemic</a>, and <a href="/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic_in_Australia" title="COVID-19 pandemic in Australia">COVID-19 pandemic in Australia</a></div><p>The weeks before the Grand Prix saw several major sporting events either cancelled or postponed as a result of the <a href="/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic" title="COVID-19 pandemic">COVID-19 pandemic</a>, which was declared a <a href="/wiki/Pandemic" title="Pandemic">pandemic</a> by <a href="/wiki/World_Health_Organisation" class="mw-redirect" title="World Health Organisation">World Health Organisation</a> three days before the race. Motorsport events affected included the <a href="/wiki/2020_Chinese_Grand_Prix" class="mw-redirect" title="2020 Chinese Grand Prix">Chinese Grand Prix</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-china_postponed_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-china_postponed-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> the <a href="/wiki/2019%E2%80%9320_FIA_World_Endurance_Championship" title="2019–20 FIA World Endurance Championship">World Endurance Championship</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> the <a href="/wiki/2020_MotoGP_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2020 MotoGP season">MotoGP World Championship</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> the <a href="/wiki/World_Touring_Car_Cup" title="World Touring Car Cup">World Touring Car Cup</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> the <a href="/wiki/2019-20_Formula_E_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2019-20 Formula E season">Formula E championship</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> the <a href="/wiki/2020_World_Rally_Championship" title="2020 World Rally Championship">World Rally Championship</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> the Japanese <a href="/wiki/2020_Super_Formula_Championship" title="2020 Super Formula Championship">Super Formula championship</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> and the <a href="/wiki/IndyCar_Series" title="IndyCar Series">IndyCar Series</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> The Victorian Department of Health announced that the Australian Grand Prix would go ahead as planned,<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> but <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> and <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_AlphaTauri" title="Scuderia AlphaTauri">AlphaTauri</a> expressed concern as both teams were based in Italy, which had suffered <a href="/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic_in_Italy" title="COVID-19 pandemic in Italy">one of the worst outbreaks of the virus</a> outside China.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> As the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Australia" class="mw-redirect" title="Government of Australia">Australian government</a> did not initially implement a travel ban for Italy the way it had for China, Iran and South Korea,<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;b&#93;</a></sup> Ferrari and AlphaTauri were concerned over the ability of their staff to leave the quarantine zone established in northern Italy. <a href="/wiki/Ross_Brawn" title="Ross Brawn">Ross Brawn</a>, the managing director of the sport, announced that Grands Prix would not go ahead if a team were blocked from entering a host nation, but added that a race could take place if a team voluntarily chose not to enter a host nation.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Organisers of the <a href="/wiki/2020_Bahrain_Grand_Prix" title="2020 Bahrain Grand Prix">Bahrain Grand Prix</a>, which was scheduled to take place one week after the Australian race, announced that spectators would not be permitted to attend the event. Organisers of the Australian Grand Prix opted against similar measures, instead moving to minimise contact between spectators and competitors.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> The rule was also applied to competitors in support categories, including the <a href="/wiki/2020_Supercars_Championship" title="2020 Supercars Championship">Supercars Championship</a>, <a href="/wiki/2020_Australian_S5000_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="2020 Australian S5000 Championship">S5000 Championship</a> and the <a href="/wiki/2020_TCR_Asia_Pacific_Cup" title="2020 TCR Asia Pacific Cup">TCR Asia-Pacific Cup</a>, which was to be held as a non-championship round of the <a href="/wiki/2020_TCR_Australia_Touring_Car_Series" class="mw-redirect" title="2020 TCR Australia Touring Car Series">TCR Australia Series</a>.
</p><p>Five crew members, four from <a href="/wiki/Haas_F1_Team" title="Haas F1 Team">Haas</a> and one from <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>, were entered into quarantine upon arriving in Melbourne when they displayed flu-like symptoms.<sup id="cite_ref-andrews_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-andrews-29">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> All five of them were tested for COVID-19 and the results came out negative for the Haas members but positive for the McLaren member. McLaren made the announcement on Thursday evening to withdraw from the race.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-31">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> A photographer later entered isolation as well.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Victoria Premier <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Andrews" title="Daniel Andrews">Daniel Andrews</a> was criticised for allowing the Grand Prix to go ahead, but responded by saying that cancelling the race would be a disproportionate reaction to the advice the <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Victoria" class="mw-redirect" title="Government of Victoria">state government</a> had been given.<sup id="cite_ref-andrews_29-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-andrews-29">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> Formula One drivers <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> were also critical of the decision to hold the race, citing the <a href="/wiki/National_Basketball_Association" title="National Basketball Association">National Basketball Association</a>'s decision to indefinitely <a href="/wiki/Suspension_of_the_2019%E2%80%9320_NBA_season" title="Suspension of the 2019–20 NBA season">suspend</a> its <a href="/wiki/2019%E2%80%9320_NBA_season" title="2019–20 NBA season">2019–20 season</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Daniel Andrews announced that spectators would be banned from attending if the Grand Prix were to go ahead, before the race was cancelled on the Friday morning a few hours before the Formula One cars were due to commence their first practice session.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-34">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> It subsequently emerged that only three teams—<a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a>, its sister team Scuderia AlphaTauri and <a href="/wiki/Racing_Point" class="mw-redirect" title="Racing Point">Racing Point</a>—were willing to compete if the race went ahead.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> After the cancellation, a further fourteen team members from McLaren were put into quarantine.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>All support category events were also cancelled. These had conducted practice and qualifying sessions on the Thursday, along with a singular race for the <a href="/wiki/Porsche_Carrera_Cup_Australia" class="mw-redirect" title="Porsche Carrera Cup Australia">Porsche Carrera Cup Australia</a> series. A two-seater <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a> also performed some demonstration runs early on the Friday morning.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Attempt_to_reschedule">Attempt to reschedule</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2020_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Attempt to reschedule">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Shortly after the cancellation, organisers announced that they planned to reschedule the race for later in the year. Several more Grands Prix were cancelled or postponed and the start of the championship delayed until July. A new calendar with eight races was eventually published, but the Australian Grand Prix was not included; however, Liberty Media announced that they intended to hold as many as fifteen races. In June 2020, federal tourism minister <a href="/wiki/Simon_Birmingham" title="Simon Birmingham">Simon Birmingham</a> announced that the Australian government expected that the country's borders would be closed to international travel until 2021.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
