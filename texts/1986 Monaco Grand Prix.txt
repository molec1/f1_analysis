<p>The <b>1986 Monaco Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held at <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Monaco" title="Circuit de Monaco">Monaco</a> on 11 May 1986. It was the fourth race of the <a href="/wiki/1986_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1986 Formula One World Championship">1986 Formula One World Championship</a>.
</p><p>The 78-lap race was won from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> by Frenchman <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/2" title="McLaren MP4/2">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Techniques_d%27Avant_Garde" title="Techniques d&#39;Avant Garde">TAG</a>. This was Prost's third consecutive Monaco win. His Finnish teammate <a href="/wiki/Keke_Rosberg" title="Keke Rosberg">Keke Rosberg</a> finished second, with Brazilian <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> third in a <a href="/wiki/Lotus_98T" title="Lotus 98T">Lotus</a>-<a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>.
</p><p>This was also the last Grand Prix for Italian <a href="/wiki/Elio_de_Angelis" title="Elio de Angelis">Elio de Angelis</a>, before his fatal testing accident at <a href="/wiki/Circuit_Paul_Ricard" title="Circuit Paul Ricard">Paul Ricard</a> four days later.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1986_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><b>Lap leaders:</b> <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> 71 (1-34, 42-78), <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> 7 (35-41)</li>
<li>The Monaco circuit had been modified from the year before: the chicane after the tunnel now had three turns and was renamed the Nouvelle Chicane.</li>
<li>Patrick Tambay had a spectacular accident when he and Martin Brundle tangled at Mirabeau, and Tambay's Lola went right over Brundle's Tyrrell, caught six feet of air and barrel-rolled into the protective Armco right next to some spectators, and in the process almost went over the Armco into a bar next to the track. Tambay walked away from the accident unscathed.</li></ul>
