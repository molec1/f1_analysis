<p>The <b>2005 Australian Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at the <a href="/wiki/Melbourne_Grand_Prix_Circuit" class="mw-redirect" title="Melbourne Grand Prix Circuit">Melbourne Grand Prix Circuit</a> on 6 March 2005. It was the first round of the <a href="/wiki/2005_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2005 Formula One season">2005 Formula One season</a>. The 58-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> after he started from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>. <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> finished second for the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> team and Fisichella's team-mate <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> came in third.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2005_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice">Practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2005_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The first practice session of the 2005 Australian Grand Prix was dominated by <a href="/wiki/Vitantonio_Liuzzi" title="Vitantonio Liuzzi">Vitantonio Liuzzi</a> who was appearing at his first grand prix for Red Bull at their first race. Twenty-two cars participated with reserve drivers <a href="/wiki/Pedro_de_la_Rosa" title="Pedro de la Rosa">Pedro de la Rosa</a> for McLaren, <a href="/wiki/Ricardo_Zonta" title="Ricardo Zonta">Ricardo Zonta</a> for Toyota, <a href="/wiki/Robert_Doornbos" title="Robert Doornbos">Robert Doornbos</a> for Jordan and Liuzzi for Red Bull. Neither of the Minardis ran. <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a> was fourth fastest while Rubens Barrichello was only 12th. <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> didn't participate in this session. The only casualty was <a href="/wiki/Narain_Karthikeyan" title="Narain Karthikeyan">Narain Karthikeyan</a> for Jordan whose engine blew up after 6 laps.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Pedro_de_la_Rosa" title="Pedro de la Rosa">Pedro de la Rosa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> put their McLarens fastest in the second session just ahead of <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> and <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>. <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a> was left fifth with a happy <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> sixth. The main drama was <a href="/wiki/Vitantonio_Liuzzi" title="Vitantonio Liuzzi">Vitantonio Liuzzi</a>'s spin at turn three which left him in the gravel and he had to abandon the car. <a href="/wiki/Narain_Karthikeyan" title="Narain Karthikeyan">Narain Karthikeyan</a> was hit with a fine with him speeding in the pit lane during his first ever outing in the last practice session with a speed of 86.2&#160;km/h his fine was US$6750. Once again both Minardis didn't take part.
</p><p>After the overnight rain in Australia the track was wet for the start of the third practice session with the Ferraris of <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> and Rubens Barrichello leading the way ahead of <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> in the McLaren. Both Minardis got to run but <a href="/wiki/Christijan_Albers" title="Christijan Albers">Christijan Albers</a> spun on his final lap at Turn 3. Neither of the Sauber, Williams or Renault drivers set lap times but <a href="/wiki/Christian_Klien" title="Christian Klien">Christian Klien</a> and <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a> did go out as did <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> but spun twice on his only lap.
</p><p>The final session got underway and the dampness of the track led to times all over the place but <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> did well to fight for fastest with the Finn coming out best. <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a> put in a last gasp lap for 3rd ahead of <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> and Jenson Button. Another drama for <a href="/wiki/Narain_Karthikeyan" title="Narain Karthikeyan">Narain Karthikeyan</a> as he spun at turn nine and had to abandon his car before it briefly caught fire.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2005_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Prior to the session several teams' weather forecasts predicted a rain storm after 20 minutes. To begin with the track was merely damp after the early morning rain. The session was run the reverse order of the <a href="/wiki/2004_Brazilian_Grand_Prix" title="2004 Brazilian Grand Prix">2004 Brazilian Grand Prix</a> as <a href="/wiki/Christijan_Albers" title="Christijan Albers">Christijan Albers</a> shaded teammate <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Friesacher" title="Patrick Friesacher">Patrick Friesacher</a> to set fastest time. Despite spinning in Turn 12 on his out lap, <a href="/wiki/Narain_Karthikeyan" title="Narain Karthikeyan">Narain Karthikeyan</a> went 5 seconds faster than the Minardis with his teammate following behind.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> had been an early retirement in Interlagos the previous year so he had to go out fifth and quickly went fastest with a 1m 41.512. Williams drivers <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> beat Button's time with 1m 39.717 and a 1m 36.717 respectively. Christian Klien moved to second place before <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> put his Toyota on pole with a 1m 35.270.
</p><p>A disappointing 1m 38.320 for <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> in the Red Bull, the Scot was complaining of poor balance. <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a> was the first man to put on the dry tyre but it looked like he'd got it wrong as he spun wildly on his out lap. He brought his Sauber home for 3rd place before <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> put in a 1m 33.171 to take fastest time before the rain.
</p><p>The torrential rain arrived just as <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> started his flying lap on the dry tyre, he had to abort his lap and had to settle for 20th. <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> switched to his wet <a href="/wiki/Bridgestone" title="Bridgestone">Bridgestone</a> tyre and the 7 time world champion could only do a 1m57.931 to put him 18th when all the drivers finished.
</p><p>The session had to be stopped when <a href="/wiki/Takuma_Sato" title="Takuma Sato">Takuma Sato</a> in the BAR crashed at Turn 8 on his out lap. When the session resumed the conditions had slightly improved and Ralf Schmacher's lap put him 17th. Fernando Alonso settled for 14th and Rubens Barrchello went 12th.
</p><p>Last out were Juan Pablo Montoya and Kimi Räikkönen, both McLaren team-mates but both had to fight for 10th and 11th fastest on the list.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2005_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The first attempt to start the race was <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_regulations#Flags" title="Formula One regulations">yellow flagged</a>, due to the stalled <a href="/wiki/Team_McLaren" class="mw-redirect" title="Team McLaren">McLaren</a> of <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a>, who would eventually start the abbreviated race (57 laps from 58) in pit lane. When the red lights did finally go out, front row starters Fisichella and <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> protected their positions and led the rest of the field through the first lap. Starting third in his home grand prix, <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a>– in his <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a> debut– was outsprinted to the first corner by <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a>. <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> each moved up three spots on the first lap, showing more of their cars' true potential than what was seen in the rain-soaked qualifying. Sato made the best start, moving from last place to 14th. <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a> had the worst start– his first in the <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a>– as he dropped five positions on the opening lap after losing forward <a href="/wiki/Momentum" title="Momentum">momentum</a> in a first-corner position skirmish.
</p><p>As Fisichella and Trulli raced away at the front, Coulthard began to gradually fall back, holding up Webber, <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> (also making his Williams debut), <a href="/wiki/Christian_Klien" title="Christian Klien">Christian Klien</a>, <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a> and Barrichello. Several seconds further back was Villeneuve, struggling to hold off a charging Alonso, who was himself just ahead of <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> (in his first start for <a href="/wiki/Toyota_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Toyota F1">Toyota</a>). Close behind were <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a>, Sato, the elder Schumacher, and Räikkönen, who doggedly pursued the champion but could not find a way past. The four rookies were a little further back: the two <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordans</a> of <a href="/wiki/Tiago_Monteiro" title="Tiago Monteiro">Tiago Monteiro</a> and <a href="/wiki/Narain_Karthikeyan" title="Narain Karthikeyan">Narain Karthikeyan</a> led the <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a> duo of <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Friesacher" title="Patrick Friesacher">Patrick Friesacher</a> and <a href="/wiki/Christijan_Albers" title="Christijan Albers">Christijan Albers</a>.
</p><p>Alonso passed Villeneuve, only to have the <a href="/wiki/Canadians" title="Canadians">Canadian</a> retake the position moments later. But just before the first round of pit stops, Alonso would finally find a way around the former champion, saving any podium hopes for the young <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spaniard</a>. While passing backmarkers on lap 15, Coulthard and Webber nearly collided with one another; Webber briefly went onto the grass, but no serious damage was done. After lap 17, unable to pull out of the pits due to a gearbox problem, Albers retired his Minardi, which had lost second gear as early as the formation lap. This was the only mechanical retirement of the afternoon.
</p><p>Fisichella remained firmly in command after his first pit stop, although he briefly relinquished the lead while refueling. Barrichello gained the most in the pits, as he moved up from eighth to fourth place; Alonso continued his hard charge, gaining four positions as well. However, Trulli's Toyota slowly began dropping back, getting passed again and again; it would later turn out to be a blistered rear tyre, which would affect him for the remainder of the race. Teammate Ralf Schumacher had a problem of his own, and was forced to pit twice in quick succession to tighten a loose safety harness. Räikkönen was able to get by the elder Schumacher into tenth (his starting grid position) and pull away from the champion in pursuit of Heidfeld.
</p><p>After Michael Schumacher's second stop, he emerged from the pitlane just ahead of Heidfeld, who thought he saw an opening going into the third turn. Schumacher, who momentarily lost sight of Heidfeld's Williams in his mirrors, closed the door on his fellow <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">German</a>, forcing him onto the grass. With no traction on the grass, Heidfeld braked in vain, sliding into the side of the F2004M, pushing both cars into the gravel. Heidfeld's race was finished; although Schumacher was able to get his <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> back on track, nevertheless he retired in the pits soon thereafter, due to collision damage.
</p><p>Montoya went onto the grass briefly at Turn Eight as he prepared to make his second call to pitlane; this, plus another off-track excursion while tangling with a backmarking rookie, cost him valuable time. When he later lost part of his rear deflector, Montoya eased up to finish the race and to preserve his <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_HighPerformanceEngines" class="mw-redirect" title="Mercedes-Benz HighPerformanceEngines">Mercedes-Benz</a> power plant for the next race. Teammate Räikkönen also lost a significant portion of his deflector, which became embedded under his side barge board; mechanics were later seen removing it during a pit stop.
</p><p>After the second round of stops, the final order of finish was nearly determined. While most of the field slowed to conserve engines, Alonso continued pushing hard on Barrichello's heels. Barrichello, despite battling a brake balance problem, was able to answer the challenge, and held off Alonso for second. Fisichella, who flawlessly managed the gap to his nearest opponent all race long, easily took the chequered flag for his second career victory, with his only other victory coming for <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a> in <a href="/wiki/2003_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2003 Formula One World Championship">2003</a>. He never put a foot wrong, and his R25 chassis, although not seriously challenged, performed flawlessly to claim the inaugural race of the season. Teammate Alonso clocked the fastest lap of the race, and was noticeably the fastest car on track for most of the event.
</p><p>Both <a href="/wiki/British_American_Racing" title="British American Racing">BARs</a> pulled into the pits on the final lap of the race; by not officially finishing the event, they effectively exempted themselves from the new two-race engine rule. By taking advantage of this loophole in 2005 regulations, they were entitled to replace the cars' <a href="/wiki/Honda_Racing_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Honda Racing F1">Honda</a> engines in <a href="/wiki/2005_Malaysian_Grand_Prix" title="2005 Malaysian Grand Prix">Malaysia</a> without incurring any penalty. The loophole was immediately closed, as a car was in future required to have a genuine technical problem to be entitled to a new engine.
</p>
