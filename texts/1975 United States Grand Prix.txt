<p>The <b>1975 United States Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on October 5, 1975, at the <a href="/wiki/Watkins_Glen_International" title="Watkins Glen International">Watkins Glen Grand Prix Race Course</a> in <a href="/wiki/Watkins_Glen,_New_York" title="Watkins Glen, New York">Watkins Glen, New York</a>. It was race 14 of 14 in both the <a href="/wiki/1975_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1975 World Championship of Drivers">1975 World Championship of Drivers</a> and the <a href="/wiki/1975_International_Cup_for_Formula_One_Manufacturers" class="mw-redirect" title="1975 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers">1975 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> It was the 25th <a href="/wiki/United_States_Grand_Prix" title="United States Grand Prix">United States Grand Prix</a> since the first <i>American Grand Prize</i> was held in <a href="/wiki/1908_American_Grand_Prize" title="1908 American Grand Prize">1908</a> and the 18th since the first United States Grand Prix at <a href="/wiki/Riverside_International_Raceway" title="Riverside International Raceway">Riverside</a> in <a href="/wiki/1958_United_States_Grand_Prix" class="mw-redirect" title="1958 United States Grand Prix">1958</a>.
</p><p>The race was won by the new world champion, Austrian driver <a href="/wiki/Niki_Lauda" title="Niki Lauda">Niki Lauda</a> driving a <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_312T" title="Ferrari 312T">Ferrari 312T</a>. Lauda took his fifth win for the season by a four-second margin over outgoing world champion, Brazilian <a href="/wiki/Emerson_Fittipaldi" title="Emerson Fittipaldi">Emerson Fittipaldi</a> in a <a href="/wiki/McLaren_M23" title="McLaren M23">McLaren M23</a>. Fittipaldi's West German teammate <a href="/wiki/Jochen_Mass" title="Jochen Mass">Jochen Mass</a> finished third. The second place allowed Fittipaldi to confirm runner's up position in the points race after a half-season long battle with Argentine <a href="/wiki/Brabham" title="Brabham">Brabham</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Reutemann" title="Carlos Reutemann">Carlos Reutemann</a>, although Fittipaldi's McLaren team would fall one point short of overhauling Brabham in the Constructors battle to be second behind <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Summary">Summary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1975_United_States_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Summary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> had already taken eight poles, five wins, the Drivers' Championship and the Constructors' Championship in 1975, but had never won the United States Grand Prix. Nor had any driver ever won the American race in the year he claimed the title. New world champion <a href="/wiki/Niki_Lauda" title="Niki Lauda">Niki Lauda</a> took pole position and won the race to interrupt both of these streaks.
</p><p>There was controversy before practice began. The <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Grand_Prix" title="Canadian Grand Prix">Canadian Grand Prix</a> had been cancelled and the organizers had arrived in the paddock with a writ to freeze the prize money over legal wrangles. There were also disputes with the <a href="/wiki/Grand_Prix_Drivers%27_Association" title="Grand Prix Drivers&#39; Association">Grand Prix Drivers' Association</a> over transfer fees and wages.
</p><p>The track had been modified for this race by the addition of the "<a href="/wiki/Jody_Scheckter" title="Jody Scheckter">Scheckter</a> chicane" at the bottom of the hill entering the esses. After <a href="/wiki/Fran%C3%A7ois_Cevert" title="François Cevert">François Cevert</a>'s fatal crash there two years earlier, the corner was deemed to be too fast. Named for the <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_Racing" title="Tyrrell Racing">Tyrrell</a> driver who suggested it, the chicane was expected to add nearly five seconds to the lap times.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Mark_Donohue" title="Mark Donohue">Mark Donohue</a> had been fatally injured in practice for the <a href="/wiki/1975_Austrian_Grand_Prix" title="1975 Austrian Grand Prix">Austrian Grand Prix</a>, and <a href="/wiki/John_Watson_(racing_driver)" title="John Watson (racing driver)">John Watson</a> had replaced Donohue on the <a href="/wiki/Penske_Racing" class="mw-redirect" title="Penske Racing">Penske</a> team. Penske fielded the brand new PC3 for Watson, Although bearing the Penske name The Englishman used the car in practice, but due to a technical failure was forced to switch to the old PC1, which was in the paddock as a demonstrator, for the race. Lauda was quickest from the start, as the drivers got accustomed to the new layout. <a href="/wiki/Vittorio_Brambilla" title="Vittorio Brambilla">Vittorio Brambilla</a> briefly registered in with quickest time on Friday, just one hundredth of a second better than Lauda.  Lauda's engine developed a vibration, but the Austrian used the spare car to beat the <a href="/wiki/March_Engineering" title="March Engineering">March</a>'s time by almost a second. On Saturday, it was <a href="/wiki/Emerson_Fittipaldi" title="Emerson Fittipaldi">Emerson Fittipaldi</a> who briefly held the pole at 1:42.360, but Lauda answered his challenge as well, ending the discussion at 1:42.003. <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Reutemann" title="Carlos Reutemann">Carlos Reutemann</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jean-Pierre_Jarier" title="Jean-Pierre Jarier">Jean-Pierre Jarier</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mario_Andretti" title="Mario Andretti">Mario Andretti</a> and Brambilla took the rest of the top six positions on the grid.
</p><p>On race days, Watson's Penske suffered electrical problems in the morning warmup. As he was being towed in, the crew retrieved the display car from the First National Citibank podium in the paddock and prepared it to enter the race. <a href="/wiki/Lella_Lombardi" title="Lella Lombardi">Lella Lombardi</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Frank_Williams_Racing_Cars" title="Frank Williams Racing Cars">Williams</a> suffered an electrical failure, and her teammate <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Laffite" title="Jacques Laffite">Jacques Laffite</a> was unable to race after mistaking visor cleaning fluid for his eyedrops.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Lombardi tried to use Laffite's car, but she didn't fit.
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Tom_pryce_watglen_75_full.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/91/Tom_pryce_watglen_75_full.jpg/250px-Tom_pryce_watglen_75_full.jpg" decoding="async" width="250" height="167" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/91/Tom_pryce_watglen_75_full.jpg/375px-Tom_pryce_watglen_75_full.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/91/Tom_pryce_watglen_75_full.jpg/500px-Tom_pryce_watglen_75_full.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4319" data-file-height="2879" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Tom_pryce_watglen_75_full.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Tom_Pryce" title="Tom Pryce">Tom Pryce</a> in a <a href="/wiki/Shadow_DN5" title="Shadow DN5">Shadow DN5</a> during the race.</div></div></div> 
<p>Lauda led the field away from the grid and through the new chicane for the first time, followed by Fittipaldi, Jarier, Brambilla, Reutemann and Andretti. <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Pace" title="Carlos Pace">Carlos Pace</a> and <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Depailler" title="Patrick Depailler">Patrick Depailler</a> collided on lap 2, both retiring from the race as a result.
</p><p>The gap between Lauda's Ferrari and Fittipaldi's <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> settled at about one second. Mass, who had moved up to sixth, suddenly lost three places to Andretti, <a href="/wiki/James_Hunt" title="James Hunt">James Hunt</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ronnie_Peterson" title="Ronnie Peterson">Ronnie Peterson</a> when he accidentally switched off his engine. On the next lap, with Mass now immediately in front of him, <a href="/wiki/Clay_Regazzoni" title="Clay Regazzoni">Clay Regazzoni</a> smashed his nose against the rear wheel of the McLaren and lost more than a lap as he pitted for a new one. On lap 10, Reutemann's engine expired and Andretti's front suspension collapsed. This left Lauda and Fittipaldi twelve seconds ahead of Jarier, who was five seconds clear of a group containing Hunt, Brambilla, Mass, Peterson and Scheckter.
</p><p>Lauda's teammate Regazzoni was caught by the leaders on lap 18, after his lengthy pit stop. He let Lauda by, but held up Fittipaldi for six laps, despite blue flags being waved to indicate that he should be let through. Eventually Regazzoni was <a href="/wiki/Racing_flags#Black_flag" title="Racing flags">black flagged</a> and brought in for a warning on lap 24; Ferrari manager <a href="/wiki/Luca_di_Montezemolo" class="mw-redirect" title="Luca di Montezemolo">Luca di Montezemolo</a> instructed Regazzoni to continue the race, but withdrew him from the race in protest just after four laps.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Back on the track, Fittipaldi was now 15 seconds behind Lauda, Jarier had retired with a seized rear wheel bearing, Brambilla dropped back to seventh suffering from loose seat supports, and Hunt, Mass, Peterson and Scheckter were battling for third. Mass overtook Hunt on lap 33. With nine laps to go, Peterson also passed Hunt, who was struggling with his gear selection and brake balance. Three laps from the finish, Mass's brakes began to fade, and Peterson closed, but locked up his left front tire under braking. The resulting flat spot slowed him enough for Hunt to retake fourth on the last lap. Lauda took the win by just under five seconds.
</p><p>This was the final race for <a href="/wiki/Tony_Brise" title="Tony Brise">Tony Brise</a> and <a href="/wiki/Embassy_Hill" title="Embassy Hill">Embassy Racing with Graham Hill</a>. On the evening of 29 November 1975, double-world champion <a href="/wiki/Graham_Hill" title="Graham Hill">Graham Hill</a> was piloting a <a href="/wiki/Piper_PA-23" title="Piper PA-23">Piper Aztec</a> light aircraft from France to London. His passengers were team manager Ray Brimble, driver <a href="/wiki/Tony_Brise" title="Tony Brise">Tony Brise</a>, designer Andy Smallman and mechanics Terry Richards and Tony Alcock. They were returning from <a href="/wiki/Circuit_Paul_Ricard" title="Circuit Paul Ricard">Circuit Paul Ricard</a> where they had been testing the GH2 car being prepared for 1976. They were due to land at Elstree airfield before onward travel to London to attend a party. Shortly before 10pm, the plane hit trees beside a golf course at <a href="/wiki/Arkley" title="Arkley">Arkley</a> in thick fog. In the ensuing crash and explosion, everyone on board was killed.<sup id="cite_ref-BBC_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-NYTimes2_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYTimes2-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> As the team now only consisted of the deputy team manager and two mechanics, it was impossible to continue, and so the team was closed down.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
