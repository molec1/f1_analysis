<p>The <b>1986 German Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held at <a href="/wiki/Hockenheimring" title="Hockenheimring">Hockenheimring</a> on 27 July 1986. It was the tenth race of the <a href="/wiki/1986_FIA_Formula_One_World_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="1986 FIA Formula One World Championship">1986 FIA Formula One World Championship</a>.
</p><p>The 45-lap race was won by Brazilian driver <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW11" title="Williams FW11">Williams</a>-<a href="/wiki/Honda_in_Formula_One" title="Honda in Formula One">Honda</a>. Compatriot <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> was second in a <a href="/wiki/Lotus_98T" title="Lotus 98T">Lotus</a>-<a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>, with Englishman <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> third in the other Williams-Honda. Frenchman <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> was running third in his <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/2" title="McLaren MP4/2">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Techniques_d%27Avant_Garde" title="Techniques d&#39;Avant Garde">TAG</a> in the closing laps when he ran out of fuel, dropping him to sixth and thus allowing Mansell to extend his lead in the Drivers' Championship to seven points.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-race">Pre-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1986_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Pre-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the run-up to the race, <a href="/wiki/Keke_Rosberg" title="Keke Rosberg">Keke Rosberg</a> announced that he would be retiring from Formula One at the end of the season, while <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Lotus</a> announced that they would be using <a href="/wiki/Honda_in_Formula_One" title="Honda in Formula One">Honda</a> engines in 1987, following <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>'s withdrawal from the sport, with Japanese driver <a href="/wiki/Satoru_Nakajima" title="Satoru Nakajima">Satoru Nakajima</a> replacing <a href="/wiki/John_Crichton-Stuart,_7th_Marquess_of_Bute" title="John Crichton-Stuart, 7th Marquess of Bute">Johnny Dumfries</a>. Meanwhile, <a href="/wiki/Equipe_Ligier" title="Equipe Ligier">Ligier</a> drafted in <a href="/wiki/Philippe_Alliot" title="Philippe Alliot">Philippe Alliot</a> to replace the injured <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Laffite" title="Jacques Laffite">Jacques Laffite</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1986_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>The McLarens of Keke Rosberg and Alain Prost both ran out of fuel on the final lap. Rosberg was running second, just behind Nelson Piquet's Williams, when his car stopped just after the Ostkurve chicane. Prost was running third, behind Piquet and Ayrton Senna's Lotus, when his car came to a halt on the finishing straight. The Frenchman got out of his car and tried to push it over the finish line, to great applause from the crowd, but was unsuccessful. Both drivers were passed by Nigel Mansell's Williams and René Arnoux's Ligier, who thus finished third and fourth respectively. Rosberg was eventually classified fifth with Prost sixth, as Rosberg had been ahead of Prost at the start of the final lap (although Prost had made it further around the final lap than Rosberg did), while the seventh-placed car, Derek Warwick's Brabham, was a lap behind.</li>
<li>Piquet and Senna were also running out of fuel at the end of the race: Piquet had to turn his Honda engine's boost way down after the Ostkurve chicane in order to get his car to the finish, while Senna had to swerve sideways on the finishing straight to get the last drops of fuel into his car's Renault engine.</li>
<li>The grid saw the first all-McLaren front row since <a href="/wiki/Peter_Revson" title="Peter Revson">Peter Revson</a> and <a href="/wiki/Denny_Hulme" title="Denny Hulme">Denny Hulme</a> at the <a href="/wiki/1972_Canadian_Grand_Prix" title="1972 Canadian Grand Prix">1972 Canadian Grand Prix</a>. It was also Keke Rosberg's only pole position with the team, and the fifth and last pole of his F1 career.</li></ul>
