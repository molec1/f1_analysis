<p>The <b>1995 Italian Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>Pioneer 66<sup>o</sup> Gran Premio d'Italia</b><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 10 September 1995 at the <a href="/wiki/Autodromo_Nazionale_di_Monza" title="Autodromo Nazionale di Monza">Autodromo Nazionale di Monza</a>, <a href="/wiki/Monza" title="Monza">Monza</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>. It was the twelfth race of the <a href="/wiki/1995_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1995 Formula One World Championship">1995 Formula One World Championship</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pg190-191_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg190-191-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The 53-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Herbert" title="Johnny Herbert">Johnny Herbert</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/Benetton_B195" title="Benetton B195">Benetton</a>-<a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>, after starting from eighth position. <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> was second in a <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/10" title="McLaren MP4/10">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Mercedes_AMG_High_Performance_Powertrains" title="Mercedes AMG High Performance Powertrains">Mercedes</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a> third in a <a href="/wiki/Sauber_C14" title="Sauber C14">Sauber</a>-<a href="/wiki/Ford_Motor_Company" title="Ford Motor Company">Ford</a>, achieving both his and the Sauber team's first F1 podium finish.<sup id="cite_ref-formula1.com_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-formula1.com-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1995_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Pole-sitter <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> spun off on the formation lap exiting the Ascari chicane, and retired in the pitlane as the grid formed for the start. However, the race was stopped after a first lap collision at the same spot (on dust he had dragged onto the corner when attempting to rejoin) involving <a href="/wiki/Massimiliano_Papis" class="mw-redirect" title="Massimiliano Papis">Max Papis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jean-Christophe_Boullion" title="Jean-Christophe Boullion">Jean-Christophe Boullion</a>, <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Moreno" title="Roberto Moreno">Roberto Moreno</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Andrea_Montermini" title="Andrea Montermini">Andrea Montermini</a>; resulting in a blocked track. Coulthard was able to take the restart from pole again (in a spare car set up for <a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a>), whilst Moreno and Montermini failed to take the restart due to a lack of spare cars. 
</p><p>Coulthard led until his wheel bearing failed, leaving <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> in the lead. Behind, Hill and <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> had their second major collision of the season; the previous one having happened at the <a href="/wiki/1995_British_Grand_Prix" title="1995 British Grand Prix">British Grand Prix</a>. As Hill attempted to lap <a href="/wiki/Taki_Inoue" title="Taki Inoue">Taki Inoue</a>'s Footwork, Hill crashed into the back of Schumacher when braking for the second chicane, causing both to retire. Schumacher ran over to the Williams to confront Hill whilst the British driver sat in his cockpit, but was immediately pulled away by marshals. Schumacher later apologised to Hill when Inoue admitted the incident was his fault, as he had slid in front of Hill while being passed by Schumacher, causing Hill to take evasive action and inadvertently run into the back of Schumacher's car. Hill was subsequently given a one race suspended ban for his part in the collision.<sup id="cite_ref-independentracereport_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-independentracereport-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After the pitstops the Ferraris were running first and second. However Berger suffered a bizarre retirement when a TV camera on <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a>'s rear wing flew off and destroyed Berger's suspension. Alesi looked set to win his second Grand Prix but subsequently retired with a wheel bearing failure with just 8 laps to go.<sup id="cite_ref-independentracereport_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-independentracereport-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Alesi had also retired from the lead the previous year.
</p><p>This succession of retirements handed a second victory to <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Herbert" title="Johnny Herbert">Johnny Herbert</a>, and then best-ever results to <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a> - the first podium finish for the Sauber team in F1. Papis was on course for his first points finish, until he was overtaken by Boullion on the final lap.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
