<p>The <b>2000 Malaysian Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>II Petronas Malaysian Grand Prix</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 22 October 2000 at the <a href="/wiki/Sepang_International_Circuit" title="Sepang International Circuit">Sepang International Circuit</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Sepang" class="mw-redirect" title="Sepang">Sepang</a>. It was the 17th and final race of the <a href="/wiki/2000_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2000 Formula One season">2000 Formula One season</a>, and the second <a href="/wiki/Malaysian_Grand_Prix" title="Malaysian Grand Prix">Malaysian Grand Prix</a>. The 56-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> after starting from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>. <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> finished second for the <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> team with Michael Schumacher's teammate <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> third.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a>, driving for McLaren, started from second position alongside Michael Schumacher. However, Häkkinen was forced to serve a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_regulations#Penalties" title="Formula One regulations">ten-second stop-and-go penalty</a> because he was judged to have jumped the start. Häkkinen climbed back up to fourth place, his final finishing position. Coulthard, who started third, moved into the lead until the first round of <a href="/wiki/Pit_stop" title="Pit stop">pit stops</a>. Michael Schumacher and teammate Barrichello traded the leading during their second stops with the former retaining the lead. Michael Schumacher held off a challenge from Coulthard in the closing stages of the Grand Prix to secure victory.
</p><p>Michael Schumacher's win was his ninth of the season, matching the record set by himself in <a href="/wiki/1995_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="1995 Formula One season">1995</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> in <a href="/wiki/1992_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="1992 Formula One season">1992</a>. Schumacher also tied Mansell's record of 108 points. Ferrari was confirmed as <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Champions</a> as McLaren could not pass its points total in the final race. Coulthard's second-place finish helped to secure him third position in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">World Drivers' Championship</a>. The Grand Prix was <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Diniz" title="Pedro Diniz">Pedro Diniz</a> and <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Herbert" title="Johnny Herbert">Johnny Herbert</a>'s final race; the Englishman retired after 160 race starts.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2000_Malaysian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Grand Prix was contested by eleven teams, each of two drivers.<sup id="cite_ref-entrants_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-entrants-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The teams, also known as <a href="/wiki/Formula_One#Constructors" title="Formula One">constructors</a>, were <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jaguar_Racing" title="Jaguar Racing">Jaguar</a>, <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a>, <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Prost_Grand_Prix" title="Prost Grand Prix">Prost</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arrows_Grand_Prix_International" title="Arrows Grand Prix International">Arrows</a>, <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a> and <a href="/wiki/British_American_Racing" title="British American Racing">BAR</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-entrants_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-entrants-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_tyre" class="mw-redirect" title="Formula One tyre">Tyre</a> supplier <a href="/wiki/Bridgestone" title="Bridgestone">Bridgestone</a> brought four different tyre types to the race: one dry compound, the Soft and three <a href="/wiki/Rain_tyre" title="Rain tyre">wet-weather compounds</a>, the Intermediate, Heavy Wet and the Soft wet tyres.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Heading into the final race of the season, Ferrari driver <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> had already won the season's <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a>, having clinched the title at the previous race, the <a href="/wiki/2000_Japanese_Grand_Prix" title="2000 Japanese Grand Prix">Japanese Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher led the championship with 98 <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Championship_points_scoring_systems" title="List of Formula One World Championship points scoring systems">points</a>; McLaren driver <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> was second on 86 points. A maximum of 10 points were available for the remaining race, which meant Häkkinen could not catch Schumacher.<sup id="cite_ref-championships_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-championships-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Behind Michael Schumacher and Häkkinen in the Drivers' Championship, <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> was third on 67 points, ahead of <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> in fourth on 58 points.<sup id="cite_ref-championships_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-championships-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello was still able to clinch third place if he took victory in the race and Coulthard finished outside of the top six.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Although the Drivers' Championship was decided, the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a> was not. Ferrari were leading on 156 points, and McLaren were second on 143 points, with a maximum of 16 points available.<sup id="cite_ref-championships_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-championships-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Prior to the event, protests over the imprisonment of Malaysian Deputy Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Anwar_Ibrahim" title="Anwar Ibrahim">Anwar Ibrahim</a> threatened to disrupt the race. The campaign group, known as Free Anwar,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> were reported in the media that they would use the Grand Prix in an attempt to bid for securing Ibrahim's release. However, the director of the campaign Raja Petra Kamarudin subsequently announced the event would be left untouched.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The weekend marked Jaguar driver <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Herbert" title="Johnny Herbert">Johnny Herbert</a> and Sauber's <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Diniz" title="Pedro Diniz">Pedro Diniz</a> final race. It was announced shortly beforehand that Herbert would move to the <a href="/wiki/Champ_Car" class="mw-redirect" title="Champ Car">CART series</a> and he confirmed that he rejected an offer to take up an testing position for the Williams team.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Herbert said going into the weekend: "It's going to be a bit emotional getting out of the car for the last time and out of Formula One. But I'm really looking forward to the challenges that lie ahead of me."<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Diniz's future however was uncertain as he was rumored to be moving to Prost after undergoing extensive negotiations with the team.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Following the Japanese Grand Prix on 8 October, three teams conducted testing sessions around European race tracks between 10–13 October to prepare for the upcoming Grand Prix. Ferrari opted to spend the four days with test driver <a href="/wiki/Luca_Badoer" title="Luca Badoer">Luca Badoer</a> where he tested new mechanical components on the <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_F1-2000" title="Ferrari F1-2000">F1-2000</a> car at the <a href="/wiki/Fiorano_Circuit" title="Fiorano Circuit">Fiorano Circuit</a> on a wet/dry track.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> He later moved to the <a href="/wiki/Mugello_Circuit" title="Mugello Circuit">Mugello Circuit</a> where he tried out brake testing,<sup id="cite_ref-test1_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-test1-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> car set-ups, tyre evaluation,<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> endurance runs for the car's engines and continued trying out new car components.<sup id="cite_ref-test1_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-test1-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Benetton cancelled a test in Barcelona during the period and decided to run at the <a href="/wiki/Silverstone_Circuit" title="Silverstone Circuit">Silverstone Circuit</a> one week later using new <a href="/wiki/Supertec" title="Supertec">Supertec</a> engines angled at 110° degrees.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice">Practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2000_Malaysian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Michael_Schumacher-I%27m_the_man_(cropped).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/Michael_Schumacher-I%27m_the_man_%28cropped%29.jpg/170px-Michael_Schumacher-I%27m_the_man_%28cropped%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="230" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/Michael_Schumacher-I%27m_the_man_%28cropped%29.jpg/255px-Michael_Schumacher-I%27m_the_man_%28cropped%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/Michael_Schumacher-I%27m_the_man_%28cropped%29.jpg/340px-Michael_Schumacher-I%27m_the_man_%28cropped%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1260" data-file-height="1704" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Michael_Schumacher-I%27m_the_man_(cropped).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> (pictured in 2005) the <a href="/wiki/2000_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2000 Formula One season">2000 Formula One Drivers' Champion</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>Four practice sessions were held before the Sunday race—two on Friday, and two on Saturday. The Friday morning and afternoon sessions each lasted an hour. The third and final practice sessions were held on Saturday morning and lasted 45 minutes.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> The Friday morning and afternoon sessions were held in hot and dry weather conditions.<sup id="cite_ref-fripractice_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fripractice-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher set the fastest lap of the first practice session, at 1 minute and 40.290 seconds, seven-tenths of a second quicker than teammate Barrichello. Coulthard was third quickest despite spending the majority of the session afflicted with an clutch problem. Häkkinen set the fourth fastest time, ahead of <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a> and Villeneuve. <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a>, Herbert, <a href="/wiki/Ricardo_Zonta" title="Ricardo Zonta">Ricardo Zonta</a> and Wurz completed the top ten fastest drivers in the session.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> In the second practice session, Häkkinen set the fastest time of the day, a 1:40.262; Coulthard had a trouble free session and was third fastest. The Ferrari drivers remained quick—Michael Schumacher in second and Barrichello in fourth—although the latter started to experience flu symptoms.<sup id="cite_ref-fripractice_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fripractice-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> was fifth quickest, ahead of Ralf Schumacher. Zonta ran quicker during the session and was seventh fastest. The Benetton drivers Fisichella and Wurz, along with Jordan driver <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a> rounded out the top ten.<sup id="cite_ref-fripractice_17-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fripractice-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The weather remained hot and dry for the Saturday morning practice sessions.<sup id="cite_ref-fiasaturday_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiasaturday-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Wurz was the fastest driver in the third practice session with a time of 1:38.318, three-thousands of a second quicker than Häkkinen. Coulthard set the third fastest time. The two Ferraris ran slower than the previous session; Michael Schumacher was fourth with Barrichello sixth. They were separated by Irvine. Zonta, Villeneuve and Ralf Schumacher followed in the top ten.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> An electronic engine sensor failure prevented Frentzen from setting a lap time.<sup id="cite_ref-fiasaturday_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiasaturday-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> In the final practice session, Coulthard was the quickest driver, setting a time of 1:38.109 despite going into the gravel traps in the session. Häkkinen finished with the fourth fastest time. Michael Schumacher and Wurz ended the session with the second and third fastest times respectively. Barrichello was fifth quickest, ahead of Jaguar drivers Herbert and Irvine. Button was seventh after having changed his car's engine. Villeneuve and Trulli completed the top ten ahead of qualifying.<sup id="cite_ref-fiasaturday_19-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiasaturday-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2000_Malaysian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatright" style="width:33%;&#32;;"><style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r887775652">.mw-parser-output .quotebox{background-color:#F9F9F9;border:1px solid #aaa;box-sizing:border-box;padding:10px;font-size:88%;max-width:100%}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft{margin:0.5em 1.4em 0.8em 0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright{margin:0.5em 0 0.8em 1.4em}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.centered{margin:0.5em auto 0.8em auto}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft p,.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright p{font-style:inherit}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-title{background-color:#F9F9F9;text-align:center;font-size:larger;font-weight:bold}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:before{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" “ ";vertical-align:-45%;line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:after{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" ” ";line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .left-aligned{text-align:left}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .right-aligned{text-align:right}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .center-aligned{text-align:center}.mw-parser-output .quotebox cite{display:block;font-style:normal}@media screen and (max-width:360px){.mw-parser-output .quotebox{min-width:100%;margin:0 0 0.8em!important;float:none!important}}</style>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">This session was not as easy as it might have looked. I only did a few laps, because we did not have any more time after deciding to wait as the track is dirty here. It was better to save the tyres. The car was handling very well. We slowly evolved and improved it over the past two days and we had the optimum balance for qualifying, as well as for the long runs in the race. My first run was not spot on. Then we tried changing the rear wing for the second run, but it was the wrong decision, so we went back to the original settings for my final attempt. Apart from a small mistake in the first sector, it was just right. With the championship decided, this is the ideal situation because there is no pressure. We race because we love it and you just drive to the limit for the fun of it. But we are still racing for the Constructors' title and that is very important for us, although I think it would take a miracle for us not to score those three points.</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style="">Michael Schumacher, commenting on taking <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>Saturday's afternoon one hour qualifying session saw each driver limited to twelve laps, with the starting order decided by their fastest laps. During this session, the <a href="/wiki/107%25_rule" title="107% rule">107% rule</a> was in effect, which necessitated each driver set a time within 107 per cent of the quickest lap to qualify for the race.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> The session was held in dry and hot weather. Michael Schumacher clinched his ninth <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> of the season, his second at the circuit, with a time of 1:37.397. He was joined on the front row of the <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_motorsport_terms#G" title="Glossary of motorsport terms">grid</a> by Häkkinen who recorded a lap time 0.463 seconds slower than the pole sitter.<sup id="cite_ref-atlasquali_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasquali-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard qualified third and suffered a worrying moment when a fire on his McLaren caused petrol to seep into his eye and admitted the incident could have escalated if not dealt with quickly.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello, who was still suffering from flu, qualified fourth having been pushed from second position in the closing seconds of the session.<sup id="cite_ref-fiasaturday_19-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiasaturday-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Wurz qualified fifth, in his best qualifying performance of the season.<sup id="cite_ref-fiasaturday_19-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiasaturday-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Villeneuve secured the sixth fastest time despite having to walk to the <a href="/wiki/Pit_lane" class="mw-redirect" title="Pit lane">pit lane</a> when his BAR's engine was turned off on his final run.<sup id="cite_ref-atlasquali_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasquali-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Irvine made changes to his set-up and secured seventh although he believed he could have been quicker as he backed off through turn 15.<sup id="cite_ref-fiasaturday_19-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiasaturday-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-atlasquali_22-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasquali-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Ralf Schumacher lost four-tenths of a second on his fastest lap and managed eighth.<sup id="cite_ref-fiasaturday_19-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiasaturday-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Trulli and Frentzen rounded out the top ten qualifying positions for Jordan.<sup id="cite_ref-fiasaturday_19-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiasaturday-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Zonta missed qualifying in the top ten by two-tenths of a second and lost time with a braking issue. Herbert, in his final Formula One race, qualified twelfth and struggled with understeer following an engine change. He was ahead of Fisichella, de la Rosa and Verstappen.<sup id="cite_ref-atlasquali_22-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasquali-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> qualified 16th and encountered traffic during the session which prevented him from setting a faster lap time.<sup id="cite_ref-atlasquali_22-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasquali-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> The Sauber, Prost and Minardi drivers qualified at the rear of the grid, covering positions 17 to 22.<sup id="cite_ref-atlasquali_22-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlasquali-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Warm-up">Warm-up</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2000_Malaysian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Warm-up">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The drivers took to the track at 10:30 <a href="/wiki/Malaysian_Standard_Time" class="mw-redirect" title="Malaysian Standard Time">Malaysian Standard Time</a> (<a href="/wiki/Greenwich_Mean_Time" title="Greenwich Mean Time">GMT</a> +8) for a 30-minute warm-up session. It took place in warm and dry weather conditions. Zonta set the fastest time, a 1:40.032. Both McLaren drivers maintained their good pace from qualifying—Häkkinen was second, just off Zonta's pace; Coulthard had the fourth-fastest time. They were separated by Michael Schumacher.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2000_Malaysian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:David_Coulthard_2009.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/84/David_Coulthard_2009.jpg/170px-David_Coulthard_2009.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="227" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/84/David_Coulthard_2009.jpg/255px-David_Coulthard_2009.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/84/David_Coulthard_2009.jpg/340px-David_Coulthard_2009.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1536" data-file-height="2048" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:David_Coulthard_2009.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> (pictured in 2009) finished second.</div></div></div>
<p>The race started at 15:00 local time. The conditions for the race were dry and cloudy with the <a href="/wiki/Air_temperature" class="mw-redirect" title="Air temperature">air temperature</a> 32&#160;°C (90&#160;°F) and the track temperature 35&#160;°C (95&#160;°F).<sup id="cite_ref-galeforcef1race_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-galeforcef1race-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Häkkinen, who started alongside Michael Schumacher, jumped the start and moved into the lead. Coulthard managed to accelerate faster off the line and passed Michael Schumacher for second position heading into the first corner. Further down the field, de la Rosa, Heidfeld and Diniz collided at the first corner and collected Prost driver <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a>, who was spun around by Diniz. De la Rosa, Diniz and Heidfeld became the race's first retirements whilst Alesi managed to continue. Both Minardi drivers were forced wide in avoidance. On the same lap, Ralf Schumacher was forced onto the grass while attempting to pass Irvine, with Trulli damaging his front wing after making contact with the Jaguar. Trulli made a pit stop for a new front wing, whilst Verstappen spun off.<sup id="cite_ref-gprace_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gprace-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> The resulting incidents caused the deployment of the <a href="/wiki/Safety_car" title="Safety car">safety car</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The safety car was withdrawn at the end of the second lap and the race got underway again with Häkkinen in the lead. The Finn allowed teammate Coulthard to move into the lead and was passed by both Ferrari drivers later on the same lap.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_28-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> At the competition of the third lap, Coulthard led from Michael Schumacher, Barrichello, Häkkinen, Wurz, Villeneuve, Irvine, Frentzen, Herbert, Button. <a href="/wiki/Mika_Salo" title="Mika Salo">Mika Salo</a>, Fisichella, Ralf Schumacher, <a href="/wiki/Marc_Gen%C3%A9" title="Marc Gené">Marc Gené</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gast%C3%B3n_Mazzacane" title="Gastón Mazzacane">Gastón Mazzacane</a>, Verstappen, Alesi and Trulli.<sup id="cite_ref-lapchart_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapchart-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard began to immediately pull clear of Michael Schumacher as he began setting consecutive fastest laps.<sup id="cite_ref-lapchart_29-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapchart-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> On lap four, Häkkinen was given a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_regulations#Penalties" title="Formula One regulations">ten-second stop-and-go penalty</a> for jumping the start. Zonta moved into ninth position after overtaking Button and Herbert. Button lost a further position to Salo on the same lap. Meanwhile, Verstappen started to make up positions when he claimed sixteenth position from Gené. Häkkinen took his penalty on lap five, and re-emerged in 18th. Frentzen went off the track and subsequently made a pit stop for repairs on the same lap. Verstappen overtook Ralf Schumacher to claim 14th position.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_28-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Coulthard set a new fastest lap of the race, a 1:40.679 on lap seven and extended his lead from Michael Schumacher to 3.6 seconds, who in turn was 2.6 seconds ahead of teammate Barrichello. Wurz was a further 4.2 seconds behind Barrichello but was gradually drawing ahead of Villeneuve in fifth.<sup id="cite_ref-lapchart_29-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapchart-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Verstappen continued to gain positions after passing Fisichella for eleventh. Frentzen became the event's fourth retirement with electrical issues on lap seven.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_28-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Trulli passed Mazzacane for 16th position one lap later.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_28-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-lapchart_29-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapchart-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Salo took Herbert for eighth position on lap 9, while Ralf Schumacher's car began to develop mechanical issues.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_28-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> On the tenth lap, Coulthard ran off the track at turn six,<sup id="cite_ref-gprace_27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gprace-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> with debris penetrating his left-hand radiator.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_28-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Further down the field, Gené lost 13th position to Alesi, as Trulli made a pit stop for further repairs to his car. By the start of lap twelve, Coulthard had built a 5.6-second gap over Michael Schumacher, with Verstappen taking Button for tenth position. Häkkinen moved into 14th place after passing Ralf Schumacher and Gené within the next two laps.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_28-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-lapchart_29-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapchart-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Salo and Alesi became the first drivers to make scheduled pit stops on lap 16. Coulthard made a pit stop one lap later to remove the debris from his radiator and rejoined in sixth.<sup id="cite_ref-gprace_27-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gprace-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_28-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Button became the race's fifth retirement because of an engine failure on lap 19.<sup id="cite_ref-gprace_27-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gprace-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher continued to set consecutive fastest laps, as he built an 8.8-second gap to Barrichello by his first pit stop on lap 24.<sup id="cite_ref-lapchart_29-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapchart-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello took over the lead for one lap before his pit stop on the following lap handing it back to Michael Schumacher.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_28-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> By the end of lap 26, all of the leading drivers on two-stop strategies had taken their pit stops. The race order was Michael Schumacher, Coulthard, Barrichello, Herbert, Verstappen, Villeneuve, Häkkinen, Wurz, Irvine, Zonta, Fisichella, Salo, Gené, Ralf Schumacher, Mazzacane, Alesi and Trulli.<sup id="cite_ref-lapchart_29-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapchart-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Barrichello_2002.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/36/Barrichello_2002.jpg/170px-Barrichello_2002.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="298" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/36/Barrichello_2002.jpg/255px-Barrichello_2002.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/36/Barrichello_2002.jpg/340px-Barrichello_2002.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1128" data-file-height="1975" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Barrichello_2002.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> (pictured in 2002) finished third.</div></div></div>
<p>Häkkinen passed Villeneuve on lap 28 although he went off the track in the process. Herbert made his only pit stop on the same lap although he stalled and rejoined in twelfth place.<sup id="cite_ref-gprace_27-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gprace-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_28-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Gené, Mazzacane, Ralf Schumacher and Verstappen made pit stops between laps 28 and 29.<sup id="cite_ref-lapchart_29-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lapchart-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Villeneuve tried to retake fourth position from Häkkinen on the 30th lap but overshot the manoeuvre. Häkkinen made a pit stop on lap 35, emerging behind Villeneuve and Irvine. Coulthard managed to close to within 2 seconds of Michael Schumacher when he made his second pit stop on lap 38. Michael Schumacher made a pit stop on the following lap and remained in the lead. Barrichello became the leader for one lap and made his pit stop on lap 41. This allowed Michael Schumacher to reclaim the first position.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_28-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> By the start of lap 45, Coulthard had closed to within two seconds of Michael Schumacher.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_28-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Ralf Schumacher was called into his garage by his team to retire on the 45th lap.<sup id="cite_ref-gprace_27-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gprace-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> Zonta pulled off the race track with an blown engine two laps later.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_28-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On the 49th lap, Herbert's car suffered a suspension failure, losing both his rear wheels and struck the tyre barriers at turn 4. Track marshals were required to extract Herbert from his car.<sup id="cite_ref-gprace_27-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gprace-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> Mazzacane was the final retirement of the Grand Prix and the season when his engine failed on lap 51.<sup id="cite_ref-fiarace_28-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiarace-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher managed to hold off Coulthard and crossed the finish line on lap 56 to secure his ninth victory of the season in a time of 1'35:54.235, at an average speed of 120.648 miles per hour (194.164&#160;km/h).<sup id="cite_ref-results_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-results-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard finished second in his McLaren, 0.7 seconds behind Michael Schumacher, Barrichello was third in the other Ferrari, with Häkkinen in fourth, Villeneuve in fifth and Irvine rounding out the points-scoring positions in sixth.<sup id="cite_ref-results_30-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-results-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Ferrari's strong result gave them the 2000 Constructors' Championship.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Wurz, Salo, Fisichella, Verstappen and Alesi filled the next five positions, with Trulli and Mazzacane the last of the classified finishers.<sup id="cite_ref-results_30-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-results-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2000_Malaysian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatright" style="width:33%;&#32;;"><link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r887775652"/>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">We wanted to win the Constructors' Championship and we have done it, not just by getting three points but by winning and by coming third. It was a very tight and tough race and DC was pushing me all the way, so it was tough physically and on the car. I did not know Mika had jumped the start as I was concentrating on the lights. I did not have a very good start and struggled to get off the line and so I was third as David out-braked me round the outside. He was pulling away and I was concerned, because I could not go any faster. At that point he was definitely quicker than me. I was hoping he would pit early. He did and our strategy did the rest. David put me under pressure at the end, but I had looked after the last set of tyres so that I could have defended myself if I had needed to fight him off. Then I just concentrated on not making a mistake.</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style="">Michael Schumacher, speaking after the race.<sup id="cite_ref-quotes_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-quotes-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>The top three drivers appeared on the podium to collect their trophies and in the subsequent <a href="/wiki/News_conference" class="mw-redirect" title="News conference">press conference</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_16-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher said that he and his team were delighted at clinching the victory and securing the Constructors' Championship.<sup id="cite_ref-pressconf_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconf-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> He also added that he took victory with the use of team strategy after he realised Coulthard was quicker than him.<sup id="cite_ref-pressconf_33-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconf-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard apologised to Michael Schumacher over the comments made by him about the German throughout the season. However, he also added he would not be retracting any comments made about his rival's driving style but the way in which they were expressed.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello described his race as "lonely" because of the lack of on track action he experienced. He also said that the hot temperatures he experienced worrying him mentally due to his illness.<sup id="cite_ref-pressconf_33-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconf-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>McLaren team principal <a href="/wiki/Ron_Dennis" title="Ron Dennis">Ron Dennis</a> congratulated Ferrari and Michael Schumacher for their achievement of clinching the Drivers' and Constructors' Championships.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher said he hoped that his success over the season would commence a new era of success with the Ferrari team.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Similarly, their technical director <a href="/wiki/Ross_Brawn" title="Ross Brawn">Ross Brawn</a> said he believed that the team could make further improvements in the following seasons: "We've got to build from this success and I'm sure we will do and get all the support from Italy and Ferrari that we will need to continue in this vein."<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Ferrari held an event at the <a href="/wiki/Mugello_Circuit" title="Mugello Circuit">Mugello Circuit</a> in front of 50,000 people where they celebrated their success and thanked the team's fanbase for their continued support.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> Häkkinen stated that had he not received his stop-and-go penalty, he believed he could have won the race. He added that his car was stationary before the starting procedure was initiated, and admitted that his car moved before the sequence was completed.<sup id="cite_ref-quotes_32-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-quotes-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Despite his accident, Herbert suffered no physical injuries, except for a bruised left knee.<sup id="cite_ref-herbertretired_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-herbertretired-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> He expressed disappointment in his late exit in his final Formula One race, saying that "I'm pretty disappointed to have a good race ruined by a failure such as that."<sup id="cite_ref-herbertretired_39-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-herbertretired-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> Herbert left Formula One after 12 years with 161 race starts and 3 wins. Jaguar technical director <a href="/wiki/Gary_Anderson_(motorsport)" title="Gary Anderson (motorsport)">Gary Anderson</a> traced the suspension failure to a broken link on the rear right suspension rod.<sup id="cite_ref-herbertretired_39-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-herbertretired-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> Herbert continued to participate in motor racing in 2001 driving in the <a href="/wiki/24_Hours_of_Le_Mans" title="24 Hours of Le Mans">24 Hours of Le Mans</a> and the <a href="/wiki/American_Le_Mans_Series" title="American Le Mans Series">American Le Mans Series</a>. He later abandoned plans to drive in CART and became a test driver for the Arrows team in February 2001.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> Diniz, meanwhile, could not find a race seat and became a shareholder in the Prost team to assist in helping the outfit become more competitive.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
