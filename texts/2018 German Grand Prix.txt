</div></td></tr></tbody></table><p>The <b>2018 German Grand Prix</b> (formally known as the <b>Formula 1 Emirates Großer Preis von Deutschland 2018</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Auto_racing" title="Auto racing">motor race</a> held on 22 July 2018 at the <a href="/wiki/Hockenheimring" title="Hockenheimring">Hockenheimring</a> in Germany. The race was the 11th round of the <a href="/wiki/2018_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2018 Formula One World Championship">2018 Formula One World Championship</a> and marked the 77th running of the <a href="/wiki/German_Grand_Prix" title="German Grand Prix">German Grand Prix</a>, and the 63rd time the race had been run as a World Championship event since the inaugural season in <a href="/wiki/1950_Formula_One_season" title="1950 Formula One season">1950</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> won the Grand Prix from 14th on the grid, leading a <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> 1–2 ahead of teammate <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a> after race leader and <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole sitter</a> <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> crashed out in light rain conditions. The race was not without controversy as Hamilton was seen to abort a pit stop late in the race, cutting across the infield to rejoin the circuit which was against the <a href="/wiki/International_Sporting_Code" title="International Sporting Code">international sporting code</a>. He was reprimanded for the rules violation but was allowed to keep his race win. As a result of the race, Mercedes and Hamilton advanced to first place from second in the Constructors' and the Drivers' championship standings respectively.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2018_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Formally known as the "Formula 1 Emirates Großer Preis von Deutschland 2018" this was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> race which was held on 22 July 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-details_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-details-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> This was the first <a href="/wiki/German_Grand_Prix" title="German Grand Prix">German Grand Prix</a> since <a href="/wiki/2016_German_Grand_Prix" title="2016 German Grand Prix">2016</a> and it took place at the <a href="/wiki/Hockenheimring" title="Hockenheimring">Hockenheimring</a> near <a href="/wiki/Hockenheim" title="Hockenheim">Hockenheim</a> in <a href="/wiki/Baden-W%C3%BCrttemberg" title="Baden-Württemberg">Baden-Württemberg</a>, Germany. It was the 11th round of 21 in the <a href="/wiki/2018_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2018 Formula One World Championship">2018 Formula One World Championship</a>. The race marked the 77th running of the German Grand Prix and it was the 63rd time it was run as part of the World Championship since the inaugural <a href="/wiki/1950_Formula_One_season" title="1950 Formula One season">1950</a> season.<sup id="cite_ref-:13_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:13-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Several teams brought car upgrades to this race. <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> brought a new exhaust system and a new rear wing. <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a> brought an updated front wing and <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> brought an updated aerodynamic package.<sup id="cite_ref-:10_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:10-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Championship_standings_before_the_race">Championship standings before the race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2018_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Championship standings before the race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Before the race, <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> driver, <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> lead the World Drivers' Championship, with <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> in second place, eight points behind. In the World Constructors' Championship, <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> held a twenty–point lead over second placed <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Entrants">Entrants</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2018_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Entrants">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Further information: <a href="/wiki/2018_Formula_One_World_Championship#Entries" title="2018 Formula One World Championship">2018 Formula One World Championship §&#160;Entries</a></div>
<p>There were no stand in drivers for the race but <a href="/wiki/Nicholas_Latifi" title="Nicholas Latifi">Nicholas Latifi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Antonio_Giovinazzi" title="Antonio Giovinazzi">Antonio Giovinazzi</a> drove in the first practice session instead of <a href="/wiki/Esteban_Ocon" title="Esteban Ocon">Esteban Ocon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Marcus_Ericsson" title="Marcus Ericsson">Marcus Ericsson</a> for <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a> respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:2_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tyres">Tyres</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2018_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Tyres">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_tyres" title="Formula One tyres">Formula One tyres</a></div>
<p>Tyre supplier <a href="/wiki/Pirelli" title="Pirelli">Pirelli</a> selected the medium, soft and ultrasoft tyres for the Grand Prix weekend. Of the three the ultrasoft was the least durable but offered the most grip with the medium tyres being the most durable but offering the least grip.<sup id="cite_ref-:15_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:15-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In addition to this Pirelli brought the <a href="/wiki/Intermediate_tyre" class="mw-redirect" title="Intermediate tyre">intermediate</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wet_tyre" class="mw-redirect" title="Wet tyre">full wet</a> tyres for light and heavy standing water conditions respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice">Practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2018_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As stated in the sporting regulations, three practice sessions were held, the first two were held on the Friday and were each ninety minutes in length with the last practice session taking place on the Saturday, before qualifying, lasting 60 minutes in length.<sup id="cite_ref-:4_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The first practice session passed without incident and ended with <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> being fastest for <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The second practice session also passed without incident and ended with the other Red Bull of <a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a> being fastest with <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> finishing a close second in both practice sessions.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Across the first two practice sessions it was the Red Bulls which set the fastest overall lap, marginally ahead of <a href="/wiki/Mercedes_in_Formula_One" class="mw-redirect" title="Mercedes in Formula One">Mercedes</a> with the Red Bull cars also appearing to have the fastest race pace, around 0.2 seconds quicker than the race pace of Mercedes' and <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>'s, although Mercedes and Ferrari would rarely reveal their full potential during practice sessions.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> This meant that despite Red Bull being fastest Mercedes and Ferrari were still considered favourites for the Grand Prix. However Red Bull driver <a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a> still thought that his team would be able to compete with the Mercedes and Ferraris over the course of the race weekend, meanwhile Hamilton also prophesied that <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastain Vettel</a> and Ferrari would be the team to beat.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Further back <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a> and <a href="/wiki/Stoffel_Vandoorne" title="Stoffel Vandoorne">Stoffel Vandoorne</a> both complained that they had poor first and second practice sessions with Vandoone calling it his "worst Friday for a long time."<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> however had a positive day with the team pleased with the benefits brought on by their new front wing.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Whereas the first two practice sessions were dry, final practice took place in heavy rain and as a result only nine drivers set a lap time as the heavy rain would cause the session to provide little useful data.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> The session ended with <a href="/wiki/Charles_Leclerc_(racing_driver)" class="mw-redirect" title="Charles Leclerc (racing driver)">Charles Leclerc</a> quickest in his <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:3_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2018_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As per the regulations, qualifying consisted of three parts, lasting 18, 15 and 12 minutes in length and entitled Q1, Q2 and Q3 respectively, with five drivers being eliminated after each of the first two sessions. The drivers who made it into Q3 would have to start the race on the tyres they had used to set their fastest lap time in Q2.<sup id="cite_ref-:4_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying_report">Qualifying report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2018_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Qualifying report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:FIA_F1_Austria_2018_Nr._5_Vettel.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7d/FIA_F1_Austria_2018_Nr._5_Vettel.jpg/220px-FIA_F1_Austria_2018_Nr._5_Vettel.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="124" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7d/FIA_F1_Austria_2018_Nr._5_Vettel.jpg/330px-FIA_F1_Austria_2018_Nr._5_Vettel.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7d/FIA_F1_Austria_2018_Nr._5_Vettel.jpg/440px-FIA_F1_Austria_2018_Nr._5_Vettel.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3644" data-file-height="2050" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:FIA_F1_Austria_2018_Nr._5_Vettel.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> (pictured in his <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_SF71H" title="Ferrari SF71H">car</a> at the <a href="/wiki/2018_Austrian_Grand_Prix" title="2018 Austrian Grand Prix">Austrian Grand Prix</a>) got <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>Qualifying started in overcast conditions with an air temperature of 21&#160;°C (70&#160;°F), a relatively cool track temperature of 27&#160;°C (81&#160;°F) and high humidity.<sup id="cite_ref-:5_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:5-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Although the track was dry enough for <a href="/wiki/Slick_tyres" class="mw-redirect" title="Slick tyres">slick tyres</a> the track was still damp in places, this along with the rain earlier in the day meant that a high level of track evolution was expected.<sup id="cite_ref-Track_evolution_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Track_evolution-24">&#91;note 1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:23_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:23-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> There was also a high chance of rain latter in the session.<sup id="cite_ref-:7_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:7-25">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Q1">Q1</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2018_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Q1">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>During the first part of qualifying, <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> set the fastest time for <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>. <a href="/wiki/Esteban_Ocon" title="Esteban Ocon">Esteban Ocon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pierre_Gasly" title="Pierre Gasly">Pierre Gasly</a>, <a href="/wiki/Brendon_Hartley" title="Brendon Hartley">Brendon Hartley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lance_Stroll" title="Lance Stroll">Lance Stroll</a> and <a href="/wiki/Stoffel_Vandoorne" title="Stoffel Vandoorne">Stoffel Vandoorne</a> were eliminated as they finished in the bottom five of the classification. World Championship contender <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> set a time fast enough to progress into Q2, but suffered a hydraulics issue later in the session. He attempted to bring the car back into the pit lane, but after communication with his race engineer, he was forced to stop his car on track. This meant that he would be unable to drive for the remainder of qualifying, effectively leaving him 14th on the grid.<sup id="cite_ref-:5_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:5-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:23_23-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:23-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:7_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:7-25">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:6_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:6-26">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:8_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:8-27">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:9_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:9-28">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:21_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:21-29">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Q2">Q2</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2018_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Q2">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Because Hamilton could not get his car back to the pits it meant that he would be unable to participate in Q2, it was also expected that <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> would not run in Q2 as he would be starting from the back of the grid regardless of where he qualified. This meant that two of the five drivers who would get eliminated in Q2 were determined before the session had gotten underway. Q2 started uneventfully, however around eight minutes into the session <a href="/wiki/Marcus_Ericsson" title="Marcus Ericsson">Marcus Ericsson</a> made a mistake at the Sachs curve spinning into the gravel trap. As he made it out of the run-off area, he left some gravel on the track. This caused the <a href="/wiki/Racing_flags#Red_flag" title="Racing flags">red flag</a> to be waved after several drivers complained about the gravel on the track. After the gravel had been cleared there were still seven minutes of the session left. At the session end positions eleven to fifteen were taken by Alonso, Sirotkin, Ericsson, Hamilton and Ricciardo, meaning that they were eliminated from qualifying. All of the drivers who had gotten into Q3 had set their times on the ultrasoft tyres meaning they would all have to start on that tyre.<sup id="cite_ref-:5_21-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:5-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:7_25-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:7-25">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:6_26-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:6-26">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:8_27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:8-27">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:21_29-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:21-29">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:22_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:22-30">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Q3">Q3</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2018_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Q3">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>As usual in Q3 there was time for 2 attempts to set the fastest lap time. <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> was the first person to set a lap time with 1:11.880, which was a qualifying track record. But <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> broke that time almost immediately setting a time of 1:11.539. The session ended without interruption or major errors with Vettel in first followed by <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a>, Räikkönen, <a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Magnussen" title="Kevin Magnussen">Kevin Magnussen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Romain_Grosjean" title="Romain Grosjean">Romain Grosjean</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Sainz_Jr." title="Carlos Sainz Jr.">Carlos Sainz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Charles_Leclerc" title="Charles Leclerc">Charles Leclerc</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a> in that order.<sup id="cite_ref-:5_21-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:5-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:6_26-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:6-26">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:8_27-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:8-27">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:9_28-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:9-28">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:22_30-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:22-30">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post_qualifying">Post qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2018_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Post qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> described his qualifying lap as one of his "best moments" in Formula One with his pole making him the favourite for the race.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Charles_Leclerc" title="Charles Leclerc">Charles Leclerc</a> was also pleased with his qualifying position of ninth place after he suffered from brake problems in qualifying with <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Magnussen" title="Kevin Magnussen">Kevin Magnussen</a> also pleased with his qualifying which gave <a href="/wiki/Haas_F1_Team" title="Haas F1 Team">Haas</a> their best ever qualifying result in fifth and sixth.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> After spinning in Q2, <a href="/wiki/Marcus_Ericsson" title="Marcus Ericsson">Marcus Ericsson</a> said he was disappointed with his qualifying but also said that he thought they would be able to get into the top ten for the race. Another driver who thought they could get into the points after a disappointing qualifying was <a href="/wiki/Esteban_Ocon" title="Esteban Ocon">Esteban Ocon</a> who would start the race in fifteenth after he only got one session of dry running in practice limiting his preparation for qualifying with <a href="/wiki/Stoffel_Vandoorne" title="Stoffel Vandoorne">Stoffel Vandoorne</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lance_Stroll" title="Lance Stroll">Lance Stroll</a> both also putting their poor qualifying performances down to bad balance in their respective cars. <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso</a> also suffered from a disappointing qualifying, however they were optimistic heading into the race stating that they had some of the best long run pace from the other midfield teams.<sup id="cite_ref-:25_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:25-34">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2018_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The race was scheduled to start at 15:10 <a href="/wiki/Central_European_Summer_Time" title="Central European Summer Time">local time</a> (13:10 <a href="/wiki/UTC" class="mw-redirect" title="UTC">UTC</a>) and was scheduled to last 67 laps with an upper time limit of 2 hours per the 2018 regulations.<sup id="cite_ref-:13_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:13-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_report">Race report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2018_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Race report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:FIA_F1_Austria_2018_Nr._44_Hamilton.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6a/FIA_F1_Austria_2018_Nr._44_Hamilton.jpg/220px-FIA_F1_Austria_2018_Nr._44_Hamilton.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="124" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6a/FIA_F1_Austria_2018_Nr._44_Hamilton.jpg/330px-FIA_F1_Austria_2018_Nr._44_Hamilton.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6a/FIA_F1_Austria_2018_Nr._44_Hamilton.jpg/440px-FIA_F1_Austria_2018_Nr._44_Hamilton.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4086" data-file-height="2298" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:FIA_F1_Austria_2018_Nr._44_Hamilton.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> (pictured in his <a href="/wiki/Mercedes_AMG_F1_W09_EQ_Power%2B" title="Mercedes AMG F1 W09 EQ Power+">car</a> at the <a href="/wiki/2018_Austrian_Grand_Prix" title="2018 Austrian Grand Prix">Austrian Grand Prix</a>) won the race.</div></div></div>
<p>The race started in dry conditions although there was a high chance of rain latter in the race. As they went through turn 1 it was <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> who led for <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> followed by <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a>. By the start of the second lap Vettel led Bottas by 1.5 seconds, meanwhile <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> had climbed to thirteenth but <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Riccciardo</a> had failed to make up any ground from nineteenth. On lap three Räikkönen informed his team over his radio that he was starting to get some rain on his visor. Of the front runners Räikkönen was the first to make a pitstop on lap 14 switching onto the soft tyres (the middle tyre of the three on offer). On the same lap Hamilton overtook <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Magnussen" title="Kevin Magnussen">Kevin Magnussen</a> to take fifth. The race leaders kept in formation until lap 26 when the race leader, Vettel, made his pitstop and rejoined in fourth place before Bottas pitted himself on the following lap promoting Vettel back into third behind Räikkönen. With the leaders on lap 29 the race saw its first retirement in the form of Ricciardo, the Australian pulling over between turns 10 and 11 with what appeared and later transpired to be power loss, however this did not disrupt the rhythm of the race as his car was recovered quickly. On the same lap Ricciardo's teammate <a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a> also made his first pit stop, this meant that it was Räikkönen who led followed by Vettel, Hamilton (yet to make his compulsory pitstop) and Bottas. However, after a couple of laps Vettel slowed down after complaining of overheating tyres from being with a couple of seconds from Räikkönen and on lap 39 they switched positions on the run down to turn 6 at the same time as Bottas' race engineer telling him over the radio that they expected it to start raining in eight minutes (around 5 laps) time and on the same lap Hamilton made a pitstop onto the ultrasoft tyres.<sup id="cite_ref-:10_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:10-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:15_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:15-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:14_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:14-39">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:12_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:12-40">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:17_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:17-41">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:18_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:18-42">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:19_43-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:19-43">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:16_44-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:16-44">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On lap 44 it started to rain and on the following lap <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> and <a href="/wiki/Charles_Leclerc" title="Charles Leclerc">Charles Leclerc</a> came into the pits to put on the <a href="/wiki/Rain_tyre" title="Rain tyre">intermediate</a> tyres and <a href="/wiki/Pierre_Gasly" title="Pierre Gasly">Pierre Gasly</a> changed to a set of <a href="/wiki/Rain_tyre" title="Rain tyre">full wet</a> tyres. Two laps later Verstappen also made a pitstop onto the intermediate tyres.<sup id="cite_ref-:10_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:10-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:16_44-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:16-44">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> On lap 46 Vettel mounted the kerb at the final corner and lost a small portion of his front wing.<sup id="cite_ref-:14_39-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:14-39">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> Then on lap 52, braking into turn 12, Vettel locked the rear brakes and went straight on into the barriers, taking himself out of the race, this caused a <a href="/wiki/Safety_car" title="Safety car">safety car</a> so that they could recover Vettel's car.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:18_42-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:18-42">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> As a result of the safety car Valtteri Bottas decided to make a pitstop on lap 53, however because of the late call <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> didn't have the tyres ready and therefore Bottas was in his pitbox for almost 20 seconds (pitstops times are usually 2–3 seconds). On the same lap Hamilton was also told to pit, he started to enter the pitlane but aborted the pitstop after a miscommunication with his engineer, Hamilton then cut back on to the track essentially cutting across the final corner. Räikkönen then made a pitstop on the following lap onto the ultra soft tyres handing the race lead to Hamilton and second to Bottas.<sup id="cite_ref-:19_43-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:19-43">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> On lap 52 it stopped raining and <a href="/wiki/Sergey_Sirotkin_(racing_driver)" title="Sergey Sirotkin (racing driver)">Sergey Sirotkin</a> pulled over to the side of the track to retire and on lap 54 his teammate <a href="/wiki/Lance_Stroll" title="Lance Stroll">Lance Stroll</a> retired in the pits with a brake failure. On lap 58 the race resumed and although Hamilton retained the lead fairly easily through turn 1 Bottas attacked Hamilton going into turn 7 with what ex-F1 driver turned analyist <a href="/wiki/Jolyon_Palmer" title="Jolyon Palmer">Jolyon Palmer</a> considered an unusually high level of aggression from Bottas, however Hamilton managed to hold him off.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> An investigation also started into <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Sainz_Jr." title="Carlos Sainz Jr.">Carlos Sainz</a> for overtaking while under safety car conditions. On lap 61 Bottas was told that he wasn't allowed to try and overtake Hamilton, behind Bottas was Räikkönen around 1.2 seconds back followed by Verstappen another 1.5 seconds behind. On the following lap there was confirmation that Sainz would be given a 10-second time penalty for overtaking behind the safety car. Meanwhile, <a href="/wiki/Romain_Grosjean" title="Romain Grosjean">Romain Grosjean</a> charged through the pack making up 4 places in the final 10 laps.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> The race was won by Hamilton with Bottas and Räikkönen completing the podium. Although Sainz was tenth at the finish line he was demoted to twelfth following the application of his ten-second penalty.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_53-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-53">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post_race">Post race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2018_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Post race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:1988-GP-Deutschland.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4e/1988-GP-Deutschland.jpg/220px-1988-GP-Deutschland.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="95" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4e/1988-GP-Deutschland.jpg/330px-1988-GP-Deutschland.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4e/1988-GP-Deutschland.jpg/440px-1988-GP-Deutschland.jpg 2x" data-file-width="930" data-file-height="400" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:1988-GP-Deutschland.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The pit entry (pictured at the <a href="/wiki/1988_German_Grand_Prix" title="1988 German Grand Prix">1988 German Grand Prix</a>). Hamilton was on the pit entry (on the inside of the grass) and cut across the grass to rejoin the main track which was against the rules.</div></div></div>
<p>Approximately 90 minutes after the race finished <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> was summoned to the <a href="/wiki/Motorsport_marshal#Off-track_workers" title="Motorsport marshal">stewards</a> for crossing the line between the pit entrance and the track when he aborted his pitstop on lap 53 which, according to the <a href="/wiki/International_Sporting_Code" title="International Sporting Code">international sporting code</a>, is prohibited.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup>  A five-second penalty was considered by pundits as the most likely penalty as was given to <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> at the <a href="/wiki/2016_European_Grand_Prix" title="2016 European Grand Prix">2016 European Grand Prix</a> for a similar incident, this would have dropped him to second.<sup id="cite_ref-:20_55-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:20-55">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup> However, after about 15 minutes the stewards gave Hamilton a reprimand meaning he got to keep his race win. The stewards justified their lack of time penalty by saying that because Hamilton cut across the pit entry line in such a way that he did not present a danger to anyone and because the change of direction was carried out safely a reprimand would best reflect the severity of the infringement of the rules.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Hamilton described his race win as a "dream" and said that he felt that this was a potential turning point in his championship campaign.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a>, meanwhile, admitted that the crash which took him out of the race was his fault, but he said that overall he was happy with how the weekend had progressed as it appeared that <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> still had the quickest car, something which <a href="/wiki/Mercedes_in_Formula_One" class="mw-redirect" title="Mercedes in Formula One">Mercedes</a> team principal <a href="/wiki/Toto_Wolff" title="Toto Wolff">Toto Wolff</a> also picked up on.<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:24_59-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:24-59">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup> Another driver who had a strong performance was <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a> who finished in seasons best position of fifth, a result which he admitted he was very happy with, his teammate <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Sainz_Jr." title="Carlos Sainz Jr.">Carlos Sainz</a> finished a disappointing twelfth, however on the whole <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a> were pleased with their result.<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup> Another team with mixed fortune were the <a href="/wiki/Haas_F1_Team" title="Haas F1 Team">Haas</a> cars, <a href="/wiki/Romain_Grosjean" title="Romain Grosjean">Romain Grosjean</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Magnussen" title="Kevin Magnussen">Kevin Magnussen</a> were among those who switched to intermediates and back again during the rain period and although Grosjean managed to get through the field from tenth to sixth in the closing laps his teammate Magnussen was unable to replicate this finishing eleventh, with team principal <a href="/wiki/Guenther_Steiner" title="Guenther Steiner">Guenther Steiner</a> appearing to be neither pleased nor disappointed with the result.<sup id="cite_ref-:24_59-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:24-59">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Brendon_Hartley" title="Brendon Hartley">Brendon Hartley</a> was also pleased with his result following a difficult first half of his <a href="/wiki/2018_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2018 Formula One World Championship">2018</a> season.<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup> His teammate, <a href="/wiki/Pierre_Gasly" title="Pierre Gasly">Pierre Gasly</a> however suffered from a poor strategy call causing him to finish fourteenth, although the team were neither pleased nor disappointed with their result team boss <a href="/wiki/Franz_Tost" title="Franz Tost">Franz Tost</a> said that the weekend gave them optimism for future Grands Prix after the team showed strong pace.<sup id="cite_ref-:24_59-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:24-59">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a> were also very pleased with their performance with <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a> finishing seventh, one place ahead of <a href="/wiki/Esteban_Ocon" title="Esteban Ocon">Esteban Ocon</a> who had started a disappointing fifteenth but finished eighth.<sup id="cite_ref-:25_34-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:25-34">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:24_59-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:24-59">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a> had a mixed day with <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> retiring but with <a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a> finishing in fourth, although there was a chance for them to finish on the podium Verstappen said that he felt positively about how the weekend had progressed.<sup id="cite_ref-:24_59-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:24-59">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Sauber_Motorsport" title="Sauber Motorsport">Sauber</a> also had a mixed day with <a href="/wiki/Marcus_Ericsson" title="Marcus Ericsson">Marcus Ericsson</a> finishing in the points but teammate <a href="/wiki/Charles_Leclerc" title="Charles Leclerc">Charles Leclerc</a> had a disappointing race coming last of the finishers in fifteenth.<sup id="cite_ref-:24_59-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:24-59">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> and <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> both had bad weekends, although both Williams' cars were running relatively well, poor reliability meant that both cars were forced to retire, the second and third retirements for the car all season, at McLaren meanwhile <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> also had to retire with mechanical problems and although teammate <a href="/wiki/Stoffel_Vandoorne" title="Stoffel Vandoorne">Stoffel Vandoorne</a> did manage to finish the race he still had a poor weekend with the team's sporting director <a href="/wiki/Gil_de_Ferran" title="Gil de Ferran">Gil de Ferran</a> stating that he felt that the car and both drivers had the ability to finish in the points.<sup id="cite_ref-:24_59-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:24-59">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Championship_standings_after_the_race">Championship standings after the race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2018_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Championship standings after the race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the drivers' Championship, <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> took first place from <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a>, further back <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a> overtook <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> for fourth place. <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a> and <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Sainz_Jr." title="Carlos Sainz Jr.">Carlos Sainz</a> swapped positions with Perez climbing to tenth from twelfth and Sainz dropping two positions. <a href="/wiki/Romain_Grosjean" title="Romain Grosjean">Romain Grosjean</a> jumped two positions to thirteenth knocking <a href="/wiki/Pierre_Gasly" title="Pierre Gasly">Pierre Gasly</a> and <a href="/wiki/Charles_Leclerc_(racing_driver)" class="mw-redirect" title="Charles Leclerc (racing driver)">Charles Leclerc</a> down one position each to fourteenth and fifteenth respectively. <a href="/wiki/Marcus_Ericsson" title="Marcus Ericsson">Marcus Ericsson</a> also took seventeenth place from <a href="/wiki/Lance_Stroll" title="Lance Stroll">Lance Stroll</a> with the rest of the positions being unchanged.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Championship_standings_63-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Championship_standings-63">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the constructors Championship the only changes in the standings were <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> who took the lead from <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> and <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a> who took fifth place from <a href="/wiki/Haas_F1_Team" title="Haas F1 Team">Haas</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Championship_standings_63-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Championship_standings-63">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
