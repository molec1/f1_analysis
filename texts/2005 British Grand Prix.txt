<p>The <b>2005 British Grand Prix</b> (formally the LVIII Foster's British Grand Prix) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> race held in <a href="/wiki/Silverstone_Circuit" title="Silverstone Circuit">Silverstone Circuit</a> on 10 July 2005 at 13:00 <a href="/wiki/British_Summer_Time" title="British Summer Time">BST</a> (<a href="/wiki/UTC%2B1" class="mw-redirect" title="UTC+1">UTC+1</a>). It was the eleventh race of the <a href="/wiki/2005_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2005 Formula One season">2005 Formula One season</a> and the last race for then <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Friesacher" title="Patrick Friesacher">Patrick Friesacher</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2005_British_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>For the second consecutive race, <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> took <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> while his championship rival, <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a>, was demoted ten places on the grid following an engine failure. Räikkönen, who had originally qualified second with a time just 0.027 seconds slower than Alonso's, suffered this engine failure during Saturday free practice. This promoted <a href="/wiki/British_American_Racing" title="British American Racing">BAR</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a>, in his home race, to the front row, with the top ten being completed by <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a> in the second McLaren, <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Toyota_Racing_(Formula_One_team)" title="Toyota Racing (Formula One team)">Toyota</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> in the second Renault, <a href="/wiki/Takuma_Sato" title="Takuma Sato">Takuma Sato</a> in the second BAR, <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> in the second Toyota, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> in the second Ferrari, and <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a>. <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Tiago_Monteiro" title="Tiago Monteiro">Tiago Monteiro</a> started at the rear of the grid after failing to set a time, following an engine failure during Friday practice.
</p><p>Before the race, a <a href="/wiki/Moment_of_silence" title="Moment of silence">minute of silence</a> was held as a mark of respect for those who had lost their lives in the <a href="/wiki/7_July_2005_London_bombings" title="7 July 2005 London bombings">London bombings</a> three days earlier.<sup id="cite_ref-f1rejects_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1rejects-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The weather was hot, with air temperature at 30&#160;°C, and the track temperature at 45&#160;°C as the cars completed the formation lap. Sato stalled as he came to the grid, but race director <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Whiting" title="Charlie Whiting">Charlie Whiting</a> nonetheless started the race, with the <a href="/wiki/Safety_car" title="Safety car">safety car</a> being deployed on lap 2 to allow the marshals to safely return the BAR to the pit lane. Sato would eventually rejoin the race, two laps behind the leaders. Montoya made a fast start, passing Button off the grid and then overtaking Alonso for the lead into Becketts. After the safety car period, Montoya retained the lead until the first round of pit stops, although Alonso remained no more than a second and a half behind as he and the Colombian traded fastest laps. Button held third, while Barrichello and Fisichella passed a slow-starting Trulli, who in turn was holding up Michael Schumacher. Räikkönen, already up four places, was thus able to close up behind Schumacher and Trulli, but was unable to overtake them until the pit stops.
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Juan_Pablo_Montoya_2005_Britain.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/38/Juan_Pablo_Montoya_2005_Britain.jpg/220px-Juan_Pablo_Montoya_2005_Britain.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="120" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/38/Juan_Pablo_Montoya_2005_Britain.jpg/330px-Juan_Pablo_Montoya_2005_Britain.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/38/Juan_Pablo_Montoya_2005_Britain.jpg/440px-Juan_Pablo_Montoya_2005_Britain.jpg 2x" data-file-width="800" data-file-height="436" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Juan_Pablo_Montoya_2005_Britain.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The race was won by <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a>, his first victory for <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>Montoya made his first pit stop on lap 21, a lap earlier than planned due to traffic.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Alonso followed on lap 23, rejoining the race almost side-by-side with Montoya, who again held his line. Fisichella led for the next two laps, setting the fastest lap in the process, before making his first stop. On lap 28, with every driver except Sato having pitted, Montoya led Alonso by three seconds, followed by Fisichella, Button, Barrichello, Räikkönen, Michael Schumacher and Trulli.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Fernando_Alonso_2005_Britain.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4f/Fernando_Alonso_2005_Britain.jpg/220px-Fernando_Alonso_2005_Britain.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="150" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4f/Fernando_Alonso_2005_Britain.jpg/330px-Fernando_Alonso_2005_Britain.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4f/Fernando_Alonso_2005_Britain.jpg/440px-Fernando_Alonso_2005_Britain.jpg 2x" data-file-width="762" data-file-height="518" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Fernando_Alonso_2005_Britain.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> took pole position, but had to give best to Montoya in the race.</div></div></div>
<p>On lap 32 Barrichello, on a three-stop strategy, made his second stop. This enabled Räikkönen, now the fastest man on the track, to close up behind Button. Montoya responded to his team-mate's pace, and to Alonso, by setting back-to-back fastest laps on laps 40 and 41, increasing his lead over the Spaniard to over six seconds. On lap 43, Räikkönen took fourth when Button made his second stop, easily retaining this position after his own stop two laps later. Montoya pitted on lap 44, putting Alonso back in front, before Barrichello made his third stop on lap 45. On lap 46 Fisichella, on course for his first podium since winning the opening race of the season in <a href="/wiki/2005_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="2005 Australian Grand Prix">Australia</a>, made his second stop, but <a href="/wiki/Stall_(engine)" title="Stall (engine)">stalled</a> as he tried to leave the pits, promoting Räikkönen to third.
</p><p>Alonso led for five laps before pitting on lap 49, but lost time trying to lap Trulli. This meant that he did not have a big enough lead to make his stop and rejoin the race in front of Montoya, though he was comfortably ahead of Räikkönen. In the end, the Colombian took his first win for McLaren by 2.7 seconds. Räikkönen set the fastest race lap on the final circuit to finish less than 12 seconds behind Alonso, while Fisichella ended up 3.5 seconds behind the Finn. Button finished a distant fifth, ahead of Michael Schumacher and Barrichello, while Ralf Schumacher edged out Toyota team-mate Trulli for the final point.
</p><p>There was only one retirement in the race, Jordan's <a href="/wiki/Narain_Karthikeyan" title="Narain Karthikeyan">Narain Karthikeyan</a> dropping out on lap 11 with an electrical failure.
</p><p>The result allowed Alonso to increase his lead over Räikkönen in the Drivers' Championship by two points, 77 to 51. Michael Schumacher remained in third on 43 points, while Montoya moved up to sixth with 26. In the Constructors' Championship, McLaren reduced the deficit to Renault by three points, 102 to 87, Ferrari remaining in third on 74.
</p>
