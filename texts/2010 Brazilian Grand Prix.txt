<p>The <b>2010 Brazilian Grand Prix</b> (officially the <b>Formula 1 Grande Prêmio Petrobras do Brasil 2010</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at the <a href="/wiki/Aut%C3%B3dromo_Jos%C3%A9_Carlos_Pace" title="Autódromo José Carlos Pace">Autódromo José Carlos Pace</a> in the city of <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo" title="São Paulo">São Paulo</a> on 7 November 2010. It was the 18th round of the <a href="/wiki/2010_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2010 Formula One World Championship">2010 Formula One World Championship</a> and the 38th <a href="/wiki/Brazilian_Grand_Prix" title="Brazilian Grand Prix">Brazilian Grand Prix</a> to be held as part of the series. <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> won the 71-lap race from a second position start. His teammate <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> finished second and <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> of <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> third.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> team took the only <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> of his career by recording the fastest lap in qualifying. Vettel and Webber overtook Hülkenberg for the first two positions at the start of the race. Vettel was able to maintain the lead until his first <a href="/wiki/Pit_stop" title="Pit stop">pit stop</a> to switch tyres and Webber led for two laps until he made his own pit stop. Vettel thereafter was able to maintain first position through negotiation of slower traffic for the rest of the race to take his fourth victory of the season and the ninth of his career. Webber was 4.2 seconds behind in second as Alonso drew closer to the former in the final ten laps, albeit not close enough to pass and finished third.
</p><p>The race result reduced Alonso's lead in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">World Drivers' Championship</a> to eight <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Championship_points_scoring_systems" title="List of Formula One World Championship points scoring systems">points</a> over Webber. Vettel's victory moved him from fourth to third, past <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> for <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>. <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a>, the <a href="/wiki/2009_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2009 Formula One World Championship">2009 World Champion</a>, was mathematically eliminated from retaining the championship with a fifth-place finish. With its cars finishing first and second, Red Bull won its first <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">World Constructors' Championship</a> since the team purchased <a href="/wiki/Jaguar_Racing" title="Jaguar Racing">Jaguar</a> prior to <a href="/wiki/2005_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2005 Formula One World Championship">2005</a> as McLaren could not overtake its points total with one race remaining in the season.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Aut%C3%B3dromo_Jos%C3%A9_Carlos_Pace,_July_3,_2018_SkySat_(cropped).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/70/Aut%C3%B3dromo_Jos%C3%A9_Carlos_Pace%2C_July_3%2C_2018_SkySat_%28cropped%29.jpg/220px-Aut%C3%B3dromo_Jos%C3%A9_Carlos_Pace%2C_July_3%2C_2018_SkySat_%28cropped%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="181" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/70/Aut%C3%B3dromo_Jos%C3%A9_Carlos_Pace%2C_July_3%2C_2018_SkySat_%28cropped%29.jpg/330px-Aut%C3%B3dromo_Jos%C3%A9_Carlos_Pace%2C_July_3%2C_2018_SkySat_%28cropped%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/70/Aut%C3%B3dromo_Jos%C3%A9_Carlos_Pace%2C_July_3%2C_2018_SkySat_%28cropped%29.jpg/440px-Aut%C3%B3dromo_Jos%C3%A9_Carlos_Pace%2C_July_3%2C_2018_SkySat_%28cropped%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1639" data-file-height="1349" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Aut%C3%B3dromo_Jos%C3%A9_Carlos_Pace,_July_3,_2018_SkySat_(cropped).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The <a href="/wiki/Aut%C3%B3dromo_Jos%C3%A9_Carlos_Pace" title="Autódromo José Carlos Pace">Autódromo José Carlos Pace</a> (<i>pictured in 2018</i>), where the race was held</div></div></div>
<p>The round was the 18th of the 19 races in the <a href="/wiki/2010_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2010 Formula One World Championship">2010 Formula One World Championship</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-CF1Overview_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CF1Overview-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and the 38th <a href="/wiki/Brazilian_Grand_Prix" title="Brazilian Grand Prix">Brazilian Grand Prix</a> held as part of the series.<sup id="cite_ref-FIAPreview_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAPreview-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> It was held at the 15-turn 4.309&#160;km (2.677&#160;mi) anti-clockwise <a href="/wiki/Aut%C3%B3dromo_Jos%C3%A9_Carlos_Pace" title="Autódromo José Carlos Pace">Autódromo José Carlos Pace</a> in <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo" title="São Paulo">São Paulo</a> on 7 November.<sup id="cite_ref-FIAPreview_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAPreview-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_tyre" class="mw-redirect" title="Formula One tyre">Tyre</a> supplier <a href="/wiki/Bridgestone" title="Bridgestone">Bridgestone</a> provided the super soft green-banded and medium dry compounds and the intermediate and full-wet green-line central groove banded <a href="/wiki/Rain_tyre" title="Rain tyre">wet-weather compounds</a> tyres to the race.<sup id="cite_ref-F1RacingEvent_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1RacingEvent-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>For the 2010 race, the organisers installed a 225&#160;m (738&#160;ft) long moveable steel and foam barrier to the outside of the Subida dos Boxes corner to absorb <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_car" title="Formula One car">car</a> impacts and drag it along as opposed to deflecting it back onto the track.<sup id="cite_ref-SoftWall_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SoftWall-6">&#91;N 1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-SoftWalls_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SoftWalls-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> 2&#160;m (6.6&#160;ft)-wide of <a href="/wiki/Artificial_turf" title="Artificial turf">artificial turf</a> replaced the grass at Descida do Lago corner, at the exit to the Curva do Laranjinha corner and turn eight. New <a href="/wiki/Curb" title="Curb">kerbs</a> on the exit to Curva do Laranjinha, the Mergulho and Junção corners were fitted.<sup id="cite_ref-FIAPreview_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAPreview-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The white lines denoting the boundaries of the track were coated with an anti-skid paint to improve adhesion in wet-weather.<sup id="cite_ref-SoftWalls_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SoftWalls-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Before the race, <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> led the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">World Drivers' Championship</a> with 231 <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Championship_points_scoring_systems" title="List of Formula One World Championship points scoring systems">points</a>, ahead of <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> in second with 220 points and <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> of <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> in third with 210 points. Webber's teammate <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> was fourth with 206 points and McLaren's <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> was fifth with 189 points.<sup id="cite_ref-points_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-7">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> 50 points were available for the final two races, which meant Alonso could claim the title in Brazil if he won the race and Webber finished fifth or lower.<sup id="cite_ref-championshipsenario_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-championshipsenario-8">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Red Bull led the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">World Constructors' Championship</a> with 426 points; McLaren and Ferrari were second and third with a respective 399 and 374 points. <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> on 188 points and <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a> with 143 points battled for fourth place.<sup id="cite_ref-points_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-7">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Red Bull had to score 16 points more than McLaren to win the Constructors' Championship in Brazil.<sup id="cite_ref-championshipsenario_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-championshipsenario-8">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At the <a href="/wiki/2010_Korean_Grand_Prix" title="2010 Korean Grand Prix">previous race in Korea</a> Alonso won ahead of Hamilton and Alonso's team-mate Massa. Of his championship rivals, Webber retired after he spun and hit Rosberg, Vettel's engine failed with ten laps to go and Button scored no points in 12th place.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Ferrari team principal <a href="/wiki/Stefano_Domenicali" title="Stefano Domenicali">Stefano Domenicali</a> said the team would be circumspect for the season's final two events due to what happened that year.<sup id="cite_ref-TimesPreview_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TimesPreview-10">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Alonso, the pre-race favourite,<sup id="cite_ref-DTPreview_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DTPreview-11">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> for his part said he would not alter his approach in Brazil and anticipated Red Bull would be strong there. His teammate <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> said he expected to win the race and confirmed he would help Alonso's World Drivers' Championship chances.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton said he would be satisfied if his teammate Button assisted his title ambitions, an act which McLaren team principal <a href="/wiki/Martin_Whitmarsh" title="Martin Whitmarsh">Martin Whitmarsh</a> affirmed would not occur.<sup id="cite_ref-TimesPreview_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TimesPreview-10">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Both drivers acknowledged the championship duel would be a difficult one.<sup id="cite_ref-DTPreview_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DTPreview-11">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Christian_Klien_(13994163352).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7f/Christian_Klien_%2813994163352%29.jpg/170px-Christian_Klien_%2813994163352%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="256" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7f/Christian_Klien_%2813994163352%29.jpg/255px-Christian_Klien_%2813994163352%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7f/Christian_Klien_%2813994163352%29.jpg/340px-Christian_Klien_%2813994163352%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1424" data-file-height="2144" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Christian_Klien_(13994163352).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Christian_Klien" title="Christian Klien">Christian Klien</a> (<i>pictured in 2014</i>) took over <a href="/wiki/Sakon_Yamamoto" title="Sakon Yamamoto">Sakon Yamamoto</a>'s race seat at <a href="/wiki/HRT_Formula_1_Team" title="HRT Formula 1 Team">Hispania Racing</a> for the event.</div></div></div>
<p>Some Formula One pundits suggested the Red Bull team would adopt a strategy in which Vettel would help Webber's title bid.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Webber courted controversy when he suggested Red Bull would support his teammate Vettel over him: "It's obvious isn't it? Of course when young, new chargers come onto the block, that's where the emotion is. That's the way it is."<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> His team principal <a href="/wiki/Christian_Horner" title="Christian Horner">Christian Horner</a> believed Webber's words were taken out of context and that the driver was supported by the team and its owner <a href="/wiki/Dietrich_Mateschitz" title="Dietrich Mateschitz">Dietrich Mateschitz</a> in the championship battle.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Vettel remarked: "If Mark needs help then he should take the medical car", and said he received no preferential treatment at Red Bull due to a car that was capable of winning races.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>There was one change of driver before the race. The day before the first practice session, <a href="/wiki/HRT_Formula_1_Team" title="HRT Formula 1 Team">Hispania Racing</a> for unexplained reasons announced <a href="/wiki/Christian_Klien" title="Christian Klien">Christian Klien</a> would drive in lieu of <a href="/wiki/Sakon_Yamamoto" title="Sakon Yamamoto">Sakon Yamamoto</a> whom Klien had also deputised for at the <a href="/wiki/2010_Singapore_Grand_Prix" title="2010 Singapore Grand Prix">Singapore Grand Prix</a> two months prior.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a> cancelled a first practice session outing for third driver <a href="/wiki/Paul_di_Resta" title="Paul di Resta">Paul di Resta</a> because the team wanted to provide <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vitantonio_Liuzzi" title="Vitantonio Liuzzi">Vitantonio Liuzzi</a> with additional track acclimatisation in its battle with <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> for sixth in the World Constructors' Championship.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Some teams made changes to their cars for the race. Ferrari and Williams modified their <a href="/wiki/Disc_brake" title="Disc brake">brake ducts</a> as teams aimed to optimise aerodynamic efficiency in the final races of the year.<sup id="cite_ref-ferraribrakeducts_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ferraribrakeducts-19">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-williamsbrakeducts_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-williamsbrakeducts-20">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Ferrari's alterations added a small fin to the front brake ducts to extract additional <a href="/wiki/Downforce" title="Downforce">downforce</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ferraribrakeducts_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ferraribrakeducts-19">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Williams' design was designed to recover as much downforce as possible with the installation of fins on the rear brake ducts and to receive air extracted from the <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW32" title="Williams FW32">FW32</a>'s exhausts.<sup id="cite_ref-williamsbrakeducts_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-williamsbrakeducts-20">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> The team also installed a new engine in <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a>'s car.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice">Practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Per the regulations for the 2010 season, three practice sessions were held, two 90-minute sessions on Friday morning and afternoon and another 60-minute session on Saturday morning.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-22">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> In the first practice session, Vettel was fastest with a lap of 1 minute, 12.328 seconds, followed by his teammate Webber, the McLaren pair of Hamilton and Button, who tested aerodynamic adjustments to their <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4-25" title="McLaren MP4-25">MP4-25</a> cars, Renault's <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a>, Mercedes' <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a>, Barrichello, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> for Mercedes, Sutil and <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> of <a href="/wiki/Sauber_Motorsport" title="Sauber Motorsport">Sauber</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ITVFP1_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ITVFP1-23">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> During the session <a href="/wiki/Vitaly_Petrov" title="Vitaly Petrov">Vitaly Petrov</a> lost control of his Renault cresting a hill to the Ferradura turn and damaged its front-right corner in a collision with the outside tyre wall.<sup id="cite_ref-ITVFP1_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ITVFP1-23">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Not long after <a href="/wiki/Kamui_Kobayashi" title="Kamui Kobayashi">Kamui Kobayashi</a> spun at the same corner and loosened his Sauber's right-rear tyre from its rim against a barrier.<sup id="cite_ref-ITVFP1_23-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ITVFP1-23">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Alonso's high-mileage engine failed two laps earlier than anticipated and Ferrari changed engines.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Vettel duplicated his first practice result in the second session with the day's fastest lap, a 1-minute, 11.938 seconds. His teammate Webber was 0.104 seconds slower in second. The Ferrari duo of Alonso and Massa were third and fifth; they were separated by Hamilton. Kubica, Button, Heidfeld and the Mercedes pair of Rosberg and Schumacher followed in the top ten.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Massa's session ended after one hour with a disengaged <a href="/wiki/Clutch" title="Clutch">clutch</a> caused by an electrical fault from running wide and mounting a kerb at the Senna S <a href="/wiki/Chicane" title="Chicane">chicane</a>. He stopped at the side of the circuit on the Reta Oposta <a href="/wiki/Straight_(racing)" title="Straight (racing)">straight</a> between the Senna S chicane and the Descica do Lago corner.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher attempted to pass <a href="/wiki/Jaime_Alguersuari" title="Jaime Alguersuari">Jaime Alguersuari</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso</a> car on the inside into the Senna S chicane and the two made contact at its <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_motorsport_terms#A" title="Glossary of motorsport terms">apex</a>. Schumacher appeared to suddenly <a href="/wiki/Brake_test" title="Brake test">brake test</a> Alguersuari. Soon after Kobayashi avoided contact with the pit lane wall after veering out of the slipstream of <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a>'s slower <a href="/wiki/Lotus_F1" title="Lotus F1">Lotus</a> braking for the Senna S chicane.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Rain briefly fell in São Paulo on the night of 5 November and returned the next morning. Weather forecasts suggested more rain would fall, albeit not to the same intensity than in qualifying in <a href="/wiki/2010_Japanese_Grand_Prix" title="2010 Japanese Grand Prix">Japan</a> and Korea.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> This created a damp track, prompting drivers to use wet-weather tyres. Several drivers tested their cars to see how they would behave in qualifying with five minutes to go.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Kubica used the intermediate tyres to lap fastest at 1 minute, 19.191 seconds, three-tenths of a second ahead of Vettel in second. Hamilton, Massa, Alonso, Petrov, Toro Rosso driver <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9bastien_Buemi" title="Sébastien Buemi">Sébastien Buemi</a>, Rosberg, Button and Barrichello made up positions three to ten. During the session Button lacked front-end <a href="/wiki/Grip_(auto_racing)" title="Grip (auto racing)">grip</a> and his teammate Hamilton made two errors.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Saturday afternoon's <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_motorsport_terms#Q" title="Glossary of motorsport terms">qualifying session</a> was split into three parts. The first session ran for 20 minutes, eliminating cars that finished 18th or lower. The second session lasted 15 minutes, eliminating cars that finished 11th to 17th. The final session ran for ten minutes and determined <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> to tenth. Cars in the final session were not allowed to change tyres, using the tyres with which they set their quickest lap times.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-22">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> The first two sessions and the first minutes of the final session were run on a damp circuit, and as such, drivers used intermediate compound tyres.<sup id="cite_ref-PAQuali_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PAQuali-33">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> After lap times were 108 per cent slower than in dry weather,<sup id="cite_ref-F1RacingEvent_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1RacingEvent-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> every driver changed to dry weather tyres with five minutes to go when a dry line emerged and increased grip.<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportReview_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportReview-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Nico_Hulkenberg_2010_Malaysia.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/38/Nico_Hulkenberg_2010_Malaysia.jpg/170px-Nico_Hulkenberg_2010_Malaysia.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="229" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/38/Nico_Hulkenberg_2010_Malaysia.jpg/255px-Nico_Hulkenberg_2010_Malaysia.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/38/Nico_Hulkenberg_2010_Malaysia.jpg/340px-Nico_Hulkenberg_2010_Malaysia.jpg 2x" data-file-width="800" data-file-height="1076" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Nico_Hulkenberg_2010_Malaysia.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a> took the first <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> of his career and the <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> team's first since the <a href="/wiki/2005_European_Grand_Prix" title="2005 European Grand Prix">2005 European Grand Prix</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>Williams driver <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a> ran more front wing angle than Barrichello,<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportReview_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportReview-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> and used super soft tyres earlier than the fastest teams.<sup id="cite_ref-GPQuali_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPQuali-34">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-BBCQuali_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCQuali-35">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> His final lap of 1 minute, 14.470 seconds earned him the first pole position of his career and the Williams team's first since the <a href="/wiki/2005_European_Grand_Prix" title="2005 European Grand Prix">2005 European Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-HULPole_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HULPole-37">&#91;N 2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-MCOMQuali_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MCOMQuali-38">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> He was joined on the <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_motorsport_terms#Grid" title="Glossary of motorsport terms">grid</a>'s front row by Vettel and Webber took third after traffic slowed both drivers.<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportReview_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportReview-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton qualified fourth because he could not extract temperature in his tyres and another vehicle slightly delayed him at Arquibancas corner.<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportReview_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportReview-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-BBCQuali_35-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCQuali-35">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Alonso was fastest in the first session;<sup id="cite_ref-BBCQuali_35-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCQuali-35">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> he fell to fifth in the final session from losing time going onto a damp area and losing tyre temperature. Barrichello, sixth, lost 17 seconds on his first lap out of the pit lane with Hamilton ahead of him and going wide.<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportReview_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportReview-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Going wide at Junçao corner on dry tyres,<sup id="cite_ref-GPQuali_34-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPQuali-34">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> and a car with a low downforce setup put Kubica seventh.<sup id="cite_ref-MCOMQuali_38-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MCOMQuali-38">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-QualiQuotes_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-QualiQuotes-39">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher in eighth ran onto a damp patch towards the end of the third session to allow the Red Bull cars past and lost tyre temperature. A lack of grip left Massa ninth.<sup id="cite_ref-QualiQuotes_39-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-QualiQuotes-39">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Petrov, tenth, made the final session for the first time since the <a href="/wiki/2010_Hungarian_Grand_Prix" title="2010 Hungarian Grand Prix">Hungarian Grand Prix</a> three months prior and was the highest-placed rookie driver.<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportReview_1-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportReview-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-MCOMQuali_38-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MCOMQuali-38">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Button was the fastest driver not to progress to the final session after Massa demoted him to 11h in the closing seconds of the second session;<sup id="cite_ref-PAQuali_33-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PAQuali-33">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> a lack of grip on a set of damaged intermediate tyres and brake and tyre temperature slowed him. Tyre wear left Kobayashi 12th.<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportReview_1-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportReview-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-GPQuali_34-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPQuali-34">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-QualiQuotes_39-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-QualiQuotes-39">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Rosberg set the 13th-fastest lap and was slower than his teammate Schumacher for the fourth time in 2010, attributing the result to Buemi slowing his fastest lap.<sup id="cite_ref-PAQuali_33-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PAQuali-33">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-MCOMQuali_38-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MCOMQuali-38">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Alguersuari was the faster Toro Rosso driver in 14th, qualifying ahead of his teammate Buemi in 15th for the fourth race in a row.<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportReview_1-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportReview-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Heidfeld was 16th-quickest, feeling he changed tyres too early.<sup id="cite_ref-QualiQuotes_39-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-QualiQuotes-39">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Liuzzi in 17th lost control of his car and spun into the path of Sutil and Kobayashi.<sup id="cite_ref-GPQuali_34-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPQuali-34">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-MCOMQuali_38-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MCOMQuali-38">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Sutil failed to advance beyond the first session with a lack of grip on his final timed lap that left him in 18th. <a href="/wiki/Timo_Glock" title="Timo Glock">Timo Glock</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Racing" title="Virgin Racing">Virgin</a> team in 19th found switching to a second set of intermediate tyres slowed him. Kovalainen and his Lotus teammate <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> were 20th and 21st after traffic prevented the duo from improving their laps. <a href="/wiki/Lucas_di_Grassi" title="Lucas di Grassi">Lucas di Grassi</a> for Virgin in 22nd was seven-tenths of a second slower than Glock.<sup id="cite_ref-QualiQuotes_39-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-QualiQuotes-39">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> The Hispania cars of Klien and <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Senna" title="Bruno Senna">Bruno Senna</a> occupied the grid's final row:<sup id="cite_ref-PAQuali_33-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PAQuali-33">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Klien lost time on his final timed lap due to rain,<sup id="cite_ref-QualiQuotes_39-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-QualiQuotes-39">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> and Senna was seven-tenths of a second slower since he completed a single lap on the damp track and spun towards the end of the first session.<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportReview_1-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportReview-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-PAQuali_33-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PAQuali-33">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-MCOMQuali_38-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MCOMQuali-38">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-qualifying">Post-qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Post-qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After the session, Buemi and Sutil each took a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_regulations#Penalties" title="Formula One regulations">five-place grid penalty</a> because the stewards deemed them to have caused separate collisions with Glock and Kobayashi at the preceding Korean Grand Prix. Both drivers were required to start from 20th and 22nd, respectively. This moved Heidfeld to 15th, Liuzzi 16th, Glock 17th, Trulli 18th, Kovalainen 20th and di Grassi 21st.<sup id="cite_ref-MCOMQuali_38-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MCOMQuali-38">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Rosberg reported the incident of Buemi impeding him in the second session to the stewards, who rejected the complaint after scrutiny.<sup id="cite_ref-BBCQuali_35-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCQuali-35">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The 71-lap race took place in the afternoon from 14:00 <a href="/wiki/Time_in_Brazil" title="Time in Brazil">Brasilia Time</a> (<a href="/wiki/UTC%E2%88%9202:00" title="UTC−02:00">UTC−02:00</a>).<sup id="cite_ref-CF1Overview_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CF1Overview-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The weather at the start was dry and clear, with the air temperature between 24 to 25&#160;°C (75 to 77&#160;°F) and the track temperature from 47 to 51&#160;°C (117 to 124&#160;°F);<sup id="cite_ref-MTotalRace_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MTotalRace-41">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-AutosportReview_1-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportReview-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> conditions were expected to remain consistent throughout the race,<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportLiveRace_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportLiveRace-42">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> and no rain was forecast.<sup id="cite_ref-ESPNRace_43-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ESPNRace-43">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> Klien stopped his car at the exit to the pit lane and failed to start due to fluctuating <a href="/wiki/Pressure_regulator" title="Pressure regulator">fuel pressure</a>. Trackside equipment moved his car to the pit lane, where the Hispania Racing mechanics repaired it.<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportLiveRace_42-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportLiveRace-42">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-RaceQuotes_44-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RaceQuotes-44">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> When the race commenced,<sup id="cite_ref-ESPNRace_43-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ESPNRace-43">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> Hülkenberg <a href="/wiki/Wheelspin" title="Wheelspin">spun his tyres</a>, allowing Vettel to pass him on the inside for the field into the Senna S chicane. Webber held off Hamilton on the outside for third place.<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportReview_1-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportReview-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> At the exit to the Descica do Lago corner Webber lined up a pass on Hülkenberg on the Reta Oposta straight.<sup id="cite_ref-ITVF1Race_45-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ITVF1Race-45">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> An <a href="/wiki/Understeer_and_oversteer" title="Understeer and oversteer">oversteer</a> stopped Hülkenberg from accelerating early and his braking early let Webber into second place. Hamilton, in an unbalanced car, was able to fend off Alonso on the inside at the exit of Descica do Lago turn for fifth and continued to do so for the rest of the lap.<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportReview_1-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportReview-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-F1RacingEvent_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1RacingEvent-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Fernando_Alonso_at_Puma_Store_-_Singapore_GP_2010.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3a/Fernando_Alonso_at_Puma_Store_-_Singapore_GP_2010.jpg/170px-Fernando_Alonso_at_Puma_Store_-_Singapore_GP_2010.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="266" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3a/Fernando_Alonso_at_Puma_Store_-_Singapore_GP_2010.jpg/255px-Fernando_Alonso_at_Puma_Store_-_Singapore_GP_2010.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3a/Fernando_Alonso_at_Puma_Store_-_Singapore_GP_2010.jpg/340px-Fernando_Alonso_at_Puma_Store_-_Singapore_GP_2010.jpg 2x" data-file-width="360" data-file-height="564" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Fernando_Alonso_at_Puma_Store_-_Singapore_GP_2010.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> (<i>pictured at the <a href="/wiki/2010_Singapore_Grand_Prix" title="2010 Singapore Grand Prix">Singapore Grand Prix</a></i>) finished third and remained in the lead of the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">World Drivers' Championship</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>Behind the first four drivers, Schumacher fell to tenth due to a driver error putting him onto the grass.<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportReview_1-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportReview-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Kubica moved from seventh to sixth and Button advanced from eleventh to ninth.<sup id="cite_ref-ESPNRace_43-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ESPNRace-43">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> Petrov made a slow start, mounting a kerb at the exit of the Senna S Chicane to avoid colliding with Alguersuari and fell to 22nd.<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportReview_1-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportReview-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-RaceQuotes_44-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RaceQuotes-44">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> Towards the end of lap one, Hamilton was slow out of Junção turn, allowing Alonso to challenge him on the main straight, but Hamilton retained fourth place at the Senna S chicane. Alonso tried again and took fourth from Hamilton after Descica do Lago turn. Alonso immediately began to gain on Hülkenberg.<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportReview_1-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportReview-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> On lap three, Schumacher passed Button before the Senna S chicane for ninth place.<sup id="cite_ref-ESPNRace_43-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ESPNRace-43">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> Alonso closed up to Hülkenberg and began to pressure him for third position.<sup id="cite_ref-GPCOMRace_46-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPCOMRace-46">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At the start of lap four, Alonso steered right to attempt an overtake on Hülkenberg; the latter blocked Alonso into the Senna S chicane;<sup id="cite_ref-BBCWebRace_47-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCWebRace-47">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> Hülkenberg ran with his rear wing at a high angle, making him vulnerable to a pass and it required him to steer left and brake later than Alonso.<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportReview_1-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportReview-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> On lap five, Alonso again failed to pass Hülkenberg on the outside into the Senna S chicane. This allowed Hamilton to close up to Alonso, albeit not close enough to pass him.<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportLiveRace_42-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportLiveRace-42">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ESPNRace_43-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ESPNRace-43">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> On the seventh lap, Alonso <a href="/wiki/Drafting_(aerodynamics)" title="Drafting (aerodynamics)">slipstreamed</a> Hülkenberg,<sup id="cite_ref-ESPNRace_43-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ESPNRace-43">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> who ran wide at the entry to the Descica do Lago turn and Alonso passed him on the inside cresting a hill towards Ferradura corner for third position.<sup id="cite_ref-ITVF1Race_45-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ITVF1Race-45">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> The time Alonso lost behind Hülkenberg was ten seconds,<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportReview_1-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportReview-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> dropping him 11 seconds behind Vettel.<sup id="cite_ref-F1RacingEvent_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1RacingEvent-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On lap eight, Hamilton made an unsuccessful overtake on the outside of Hülkenberg for fourth into the Senna S chicane.<sup id="cite_ref-GPCOMRace_46-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPCOMRace-46">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> This was due to a lack of tyre grip,<sup id="cite_ref-MCOMRace_48-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MCOMRace-48">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> and he sought to conserve his tyres since he did not want to overheat them in the aerodynamic turbulence created by the airflow over the rear of Hülkenberg's car.<sup id="cite_ref-ESPNRace_43-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ESPNRace-43">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> In his first lap out of aerodynamic turbulence, Alonso was unable to close up to the Red Bull cars;<sup id="cite_ref-BBCWebRace_47-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCWebRace-47">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> Vettel opted to avoid tyre strain and losing grip in case of a <a href="/wiki/Safety_car" title="Safety car">safety car</a> deployment.<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportReview_1-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportReview-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Hülkenberg blocked Hamilton from passing him on the inside at the start of the 11th lap,<sup id="cite_ref-BBCWebRace_47-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCWebRace-47">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> notwithstanding Hamilton's more powerful engine.<sup id="cite_ref-ITVF1Race_45-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ITVF1Race-45">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> They drew alongside going into Descica do Lago turn as Hülkenberg maintained fourth.<sup id="cite_ref-ESPNRace_43-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ESPNRace-43">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Mark_Webber_2010_Japan.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8d/Mark_Webber_2010_Japan.jpg/170px-Mark_Webber_2010_Japan.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="208" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8d/Mark_Webber_2010_Japan.jpg/255px-Mark_Webber_2010_Japan.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8d/Mark_Webber_2010_Japan.jpg/340px-Mark_Webber_2010_Japan.jpg 2x" data-file-width="800" data-file-height="978" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Mark_Webber_2010_Japan.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> started from third and improved to a second-place finish</div></div></div>
<p>At the conclusion of the same lap, Button, separated by slower cars,<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportReview_1-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportReview-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> made the race's first pit stop to switch onto the medium compound tyres,<sup id="cite_ref-BBCWebRace_47-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCWebRace-47">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> emerging in 18th position.<sup id="cite_ref-F1RacingEvent_2-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1RacingEvent-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Button's faster pace meant he was followed in due course by:<sup id="cite_ref-ITVF1Race_45-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ITVF1Race-45">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> Massa, Barrichello, Hülkenberg, Kubica, Heidfeld, Alguersuari, Liuzzi and Buemi over the next seven laps.<sup id="cite_ref-GPCOMRace_46-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPCOMRace-46">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> Massa and Barrichello each had <a href="/wiki/Lug_nut" title="Lug nut">wheel nut</a> installation problems, requiring them to make a second pit stop. Both drivers rejoined behind Button.<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportReview_1-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportReview-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton made his first stop for the medium compound tyres on lap 21. He emerged in sixth position, narrowly ahead of his teammate Button.<sup id="cite_ref-ESPNRace_43-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ESPNRace-43">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> At the front of the field, Alonso entered the pit lane to switch to the medium compound tyres on the 25th lap, continuing in third position. Vettel followed suit on the end of the lap, and relinquished the lead to his teammate Webber on laps 25 and 26 until the latter's own stop on lap 26.<sup id="cite_ref-GPCOMRace_46-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPCOMRace-46">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> After the pit stops, the gap to Vettel and his teammate Webber was less than three seconds.<sup id="cite_ref-F1RacingEvent_2-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1RacingEvent-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Alonso was a further 10 seconds behind in third place,<sup id="cite_ref-BBCWebRace_47-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCWebRace-47">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> and 10 in front of Hamilton in fourth.<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportReview_1-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportReview-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On lap 30, Button overtook the yet-to-pit Kobayashi on the inside into the Senna S chicane for fifth position.<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportLiveRace_42-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportLiveRace-42">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello attempted to pass Alguersuari for 13th on the outside at the same corner five laps later and the two made contact. Barrichello sustained a front-left puncture and slowed en route to the pit lane for super soft tyres. He rejoined the race one lap behind Vettel.<sup id="cite_ref-GPCOMRace_46-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPCOMRace-46">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> Four laps later, Rosberg overtook Kobayashi on the inside into the Senna S chicane for sixth place.<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportLiveRace_42-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportLiveRace-42">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-GPCOMRace_46-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPCOMRace-46">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> At the front, the Red Bull duo of Vettel and Webber appeared they would remain in first and second. Red Bull radioed Webber to lower the performance of his overheating engine from having less water temperature. Nevertheless, slower traffic allowed him to close up to Vettel to 1.5 seconds. In the meantime, di Grassi entered the Virgin team's garage on the 44th lap to rectify a worsening rear <a href="/wiki/Car_suspension" title="Car suspension">suspension</a> fault.<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportReview_1-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportReview-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He rejoined the race four laps later.<sup id="cite_ref-GPCOMRace_46-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPCOMRace-46">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Sebastian_Vettel_2010_Japan.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8b/Sebastian_Vettel_2010_Japan.jpg/170px-Sebastian_Vettel_2010_Japan.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="247" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8b/Sebastian_Vettel_2010_Japan.jpg/255px-Sebastian_Vettel_2010_Japan.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8b/Sebastian_Vettel_2010_Japan.jpg/340px-Sebastian_Vettel_2010_Japan.jpg 2x" data-file-width="600" data-file-height="873" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Sebastian_Vettel_2010_Japan.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> (<i>pictured at the <a href="/wiki/2010_Japanese_Grand_Prix" title="2010 Japanese Grand Prix">Japanese Grand Prix</a> one month prior</i>) took his fourth victory of the season and the ninth of his career.</div></div></div>
<p>Lap 51 saw the sole safety car deployment.<sup id="cite_ref-BBCWebRace_47-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCWebRace-47">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> Liuzzi lost control of his car on a kerb to the outside of the second Senna S chicane due to a suspected front suspension failure. He crashed into a barrier to the track's inside at the bottom of a hill before the exit of the turn.<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportReview_1-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportReview-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-AutosportLiveRace_42-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportLiveRace-42">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-GPCOMRace_46-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPCOMRace-46">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> Liuzzi was unhurt;<sup id="cite_ref-ESPNRace_43-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ESPNRace-43">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> a <a href="/wiki/Pull-rod_suspension" title="Pull-rod suspension">suspension rocker</a> penetrated his car's left-front monocoque and touched his foot.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> His car was deemed to be in a dangerous position and a recovery tractor moved it.<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportReview_1-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportReview-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-BBCWebRace_47-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCWebRace-47">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> Under safety car conditions, several drivers made pit stops to replace worn tyres.<sup id="cite_ref-F1RacingEvent_2-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1RacingEvent-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-GPCOMRace_46-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPCOMRace-46">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> McLaren called Hamilton and Button into the pit lane for a second pit stop to switch to a new set of tyres in an attempt to move up the field.<sup id="cite_ref-ITVF1Race_45-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ITVF1Race-45">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> Both drivers lost no positions. Mercedes asked Rosberg to make a pit stop to challenge Button at the <a href="/wiki/Rolling_start" title="Rolling start">rolling restart</a>; a miscommunication between Rosberg's <a href="/wiki/Race_engineer" title="Race engineer">race engineer</a> <a href="/wiki/Jock_Clear" title="Jock Clear">Jock Clear</a> and the Mercedes mechanics over which type of tyre to use meant they readied the mediums instead of the super softs Clear had requested. Ultimately, Rosberg's mechanics fitted an old set of tyres; he completed an additional lap before they installed the super soft tyre compound on his car.<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportReview_1-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportReview-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Racing resumed at the conclusion of the 55th lap when the safety car was withdrawn when Liuzzi's car was removed from the track.<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportLiveRace_42-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportLiveRace-42">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-MCOMRace_48-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MCOMRace-48">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> Vettel led as lapped drivers separated him, Webber and Alonso in second and third. The first three lapped faster than they had done before the safety car and prevented Hamilton and Button from gaining further positions.<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportReview_1-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportReview-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Alonso managed the wear on his tyres to allow for a challenge to Webber, who was distanced by his teammate Vettel with a sequence of faster lap times.<sup id="cite_ref-ITVF1Race_45-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ITVF1Race-45">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> On lap 65, Kobayashi passed Alguerusari for tenth place.<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportReview_1-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportReview-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Not long after the stewards informed the Sauber team that Heidfeld was deemed to have ignored <a href="/wiki/Racing_flags#Blue_flag" title="Racing flags">blue flags</a> instructing him to allow faster cars past and imposed a drive-through penalty. He took the penalty on the 66th lap and lost 14 seconds.<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportLiveRace_42-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportLiveRace-42">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Alonso closed to within six seconds of race leader Vettel,<sup id="cite_ref-GPCOMRace_46-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPCOMRace-46">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> as Red Bull did not invoke <a href="/wiki/Team_orders" title="Team orders">team orders</a> to instruct Vettel to hand the victory to Webber and improve his teammate's position in the World Drivers' Championship.<sup id="cite_ref-NYTReport_50-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYTReport-50">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> Vettel finished first in a time of 1 hour, 33 minutes, 11.803 seconds for his fourth victory of the season and the ninth of his career.<sup id="cite_ref-MCOMRace_48-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MCOMRace-48">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-NYTReport_50-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYTReport-50">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> The win, along with Webber's second-place finish won Red Bull the 2010 World Constructors' Championship since no other team could pass its points total with one race of the season left. It was Red Bull's first since the team joined Formula One in <a href="/wiki/2005_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2005 Formula One World Championship">2005</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-NYTReport_50-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYTReport-50">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> Alonso took third with the McLaren pair of Hamilton and Button finished fourth and fifth, almost one second separating the two drivers.<sup id="cite_ref-F1RacingEvent_2-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1RacingEvent-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher allowed Rosberg past after the safety car was withdrawn as his teammate had a new set of tyres and was better able to challenge Button; the two ended the race in sixth and seventh. Hülkenberg, Kubica and Kobayashi rounded out the top ten. Alguersuari, Sutil, Buemi, Barrichello, Massa, Petrov, Heidfeld, Kovalainen, Trulli, Glock, Senna and Klien (the latter registered his first finish since the <a href="/wiki/2006_German_Grand_Prix" title="2006 German Grand Prix">2006 German Grand Prix</a>) were the final classified finishers.<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportReview_1-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportReview-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The top three drivers appeared on the podium to collect their trophies and spoke to the media in a later press conference.<sup id="cite_ref-FIAPreview_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAPreview-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-regulations_22-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-22">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Vettel said it was important for him to pull away from Hülkenberg after he passed him: "The car felt fantastic. All throughout the race I was able to hold the gaps as I planned, so I could control the race from there. With the safety car in the end it was the right choice not to try to pull away too much, to have some tyres left."<sup id="cite_ref-PressConf_51-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PressConf-51">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> Webber agreed the start was the most important aspect of the race and said of the importance: "Most of the races are decided as we know pretty much on the Saturday or the first lap. You can follow each other around but eventually... in the old days you could play with the strategy a little bit, change the fuel loads and have a look at going long or a bit shorter."<sup id="cite_ref-PressConf_51-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PressConf-51">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> Alonso believed he attempted to perform to the best of his ability and a higher starting position would have allowed him to pass a Red Bull car at the start: "We are very close in race pace, maybe one or two-tenths quicker some laps, one or two-tenths slower some of the laps, so when you lose 12 seconds probably it is over."<sup id="cite_ref-PressConf_51-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PressConf-51">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Jenson_Button_2010_Malaysia.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ad/Jenson_Button_2010_Malaysia.jpg/170px-Jenson_Button_2010_Malaysia.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="231" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ad/Jenson_Button_2010_Malaysia.jpg/255px-Jenson_Button_2010_Malaysia.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ad/Jenson_Button_2010_Malaysia.jpg/340px-Jenson_Button_2010_Malaysia.jpg 2x" data-file-width="800" data-file-height="1088" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Jenson_Button_2010_Malaysia.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> (<i>pictured at the Malaysian Grand Prix seven months prior</i>) was mathematically eliminated from the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">World Drivers' Championship</a> with a fifth-place finish.</div></div></div>
<p>Afterwards, the Red Bull team celebrated their first World Constructors' Championship.<sup id="cite_ref-GuardianRace_52-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GuardianRace-52">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> Christian Horner commented on how Red Bull was regarded as "a party team" after they purchased <a href="/wiki/Jaguar_Racing" title="Jaguar Racing">Jaguar</a> in 2005: "In six years, this team has come from a team that no-one took seriously – that everyone thought was a party team – to the 2010 F1 constructors' champions. We have finished ahead of teams with far more experience and heritage than ourselves – we took them on and we won, thanks to the tremendous dedication of every single team member, the incredible support from Red Bull and the vision and unfaltering commitment from Mr. Mateschitz."<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Newey" title="Adrian Newey">Adrian Newey</a>, the team's technical director, thanked aerodynamicist <a href="/wiki/Peter_Prodromou" title="Peter Prodromou">Peter Prodromou</a> and designer Rob Marshall for their work to the <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_RB6" title="Red Bull RB6">RB6</a> in <a href="/wiki/Milton_Keynes" title="Milton Keynes">Milton Keynes</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup> Vettel stated Red Bull's Constructors' Championship win was special to him because he had visited the team's factory in Milton Keynes in 2005 and was intrigued by the experience: "Looking up to Formula One and now to be part of the team and part of the driver line-up to give them their first championship is incredible".<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Hülkenberg said he was happy to finish the race in eighth position and promoted Williams to sixth in the Constructors' Championship.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup> After he finished fifth, Button was mathematically prevented from retaining the World Drivers' Championship.<sup id="cite_ref-NYTReport_50-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYTReport-50">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> He said he would enter the final race of the season in <a href="/wiki/2010_Abu_Dhabi_Grand_Prix" title="2010 Abu Dhabi Grand Prix">Abu Dhabi</a> with no concerns and that he would use his experience from the season to improve for <a href="/wiki/2011_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2011 Formula One World Championship">2011</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-RaceQuotes_44-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RaceQuotes-44">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> His teammate Hamilton commented on his prospects of title success in the season-finale: "In Abu Dhabi I'll be doing everything I can to pull off the win I need, and hoping the other guys hit problems. As always, we won't give up and we'll keep on pushing. We've seen many times before that almost anything can happen in the last race of the season. It'll take a miracle, but miracles can happen."<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The race result meant Webber lowered Alonso's lead in the World Drivers' Championship to eight points. Vettel's victory elevated him ahead of Hamilton to third place as Button maintained fifth place.<sup id="cite_ref-points_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-7">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> In the World Constructors' Championship, Red Bull finished first with 469 points. McLaren were second with 421 points and Ferrari were another 32 points behind in third position. Mercedes secured fourth place from Renault with one race left in the season.<sup id="cite_ref-points_7-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-7">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
