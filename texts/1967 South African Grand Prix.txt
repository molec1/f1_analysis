<p>The <b>1967 South African Grand Prix</b>, formally the <b>1st AA Grand Prix of South Africa</b> (<a href="/wiki/Afrikaans" title="Afrikaans">Afrikaans</a>: <i>Eerste AA Suid-Afrikaanse Grand Prix</i><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>), was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at <a href="/wiki/Kyalami" title="Kyalami">Kyalami</a> on 2 January 1967. It was race 1 of 11 in both the <a href="/wiki/1967_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1967 World Championship of Drivers">1967 World Championship of Drivers</a> and the <a href="/wiki/1967_International_Cup_for_Formula_One_Manufacturers" class="mw-redirect" title="1967 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers">1967 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers</a>. The 80-lap race was won by Cooper driver <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Rodr%C3%ADguez_(racing_driver)" title="Pedro Rodríguez (racing driver)">Pedro Rodríguez</a> after he started from fourth position. <a href="/wiki/John_Love_(racing_driver)" title="John Love (racing driver)">John Love</a> finished second in a privately entered Cooper and <a href="/wiki/Honda_in_Formula_One" title="Honda in Formula One">Honda</a> driver <a href="/wiki/John_Surtees" title="John Surtees">John Surtees</a> came in third.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_report">Race report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1967_South_African_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Race report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>This was the first use of the <a href="/wiki/Kyalami" title="Kyalami">Kyalami</a> circuit near Johannesburg in a Formula One race. There were some changes in the driver line-ups: <a href="/wiki/John_Surtees" title="John Surtees">John Surtees</a> was driving for <a href="/wiki/Honda_in_Formula_One" title="Honda in Formula One">Honda</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mike_Spence" title="Mike Spence">Mike Spence</a> signed for <a href="/wiki/British_Racing_Motors" title="British Racing Motors">BRM</a> whilst <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Rodr%C3%ADguez_(racing_driver)" title="Pedro Rodríguez (racing driver)">Pedro Rodríguez</a> was on trial for <a href="/wiki/Cooper_Car_Company" title="Cooper Car Company">Cooper</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Denny_Hulme" title="Denny Hulme">Denny Hulme</a> led <a href="/wiki/Jack_Brabham" title="Jack Brabham">Jack Brabham</a> away from the start, but the Australian soon spun, handing second place to Surtees, but by lap 21 had managed to regain second. Further down the field, Rhodesian privateer racer <a href="/wiki/John_Love_(racing_driver)" title="John Love (racing driver)">John Love</a> reached third place in a four-cylinder Cooper Climax. On lap 41, Brabham retired, followed by <a href="/wiki/Dan_Gurney" title="Dan Gurney">Dan Gurney</a> on lap 44. On lap 59, Hulme had to pit for more brake fluid, handing the lead to Love. His drive was halted as with just seven laps left he had to pit to take on more fuel. Rodríguez took his first win in Formula One for Cooper from Love in second place and Surtees in third.
</p>
