<p>The <b>1987 Australian Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held at <a href="/wiki/Adelaide_Street_Circuit" title="Adelaide Street Circuit">Adelaide</a> on 15 November 1987. It was the sixteenth and final race of the <a href="/wiki/1987_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1987 Formula One World Championship">1987 Formula One World Championship</a>.
</p><p>The 82-lap race was won by Austrian driver <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a>, who started from pole position and led every lap in his <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_F1/87" title="Ferrari F1/87">Ferrari</a>. Brazilian <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> finished second in his <a href="/wiki/Lotus_99T" title="Lotus 99T">Lotus</a>-<a href="/wiki/Honda_in_Formula_One" title="Honda in Formula One">Honda</a> but was subsequently disqualified, thus promoting Berger's Italian teammate <a href="/wiki/Michele_Alboreto" title="Michele Alboreto">Michele Alboreto</a> to second and the <a href="/wiki/Benetton_B187" title="Benetton B187">Benetton</a>-<a href="/wiki/Ford_Motor_Company" title="Ford Motor Company">Ford</a> of Belgian <a href="/wiki/Thierry_Boutsen" title="Thierry Boutsen">Thierry Boutsen</a> to third.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1987_Australian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> started from pole position despite being ill during qualifying. <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> was still recovering from his accident in the previous race in <a href="/wiki/1987_Japanese_Grand_Prix" title="1987 Japanese Grand Prix">Japan</a> and so <a href="/wiki/Riccardo_Patrese" title="Riccardo Patrese">Riccardo Patrese</a>, who had already signed for <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> to partner Mansell in 1988, was given permission by <a href="/wiki/Brabham" title="Brabham">Brabham</a> owner <a href="/wiki/Bernie_Ecclestone" title="Bernie Ecclestone">Bernie Ecclestone</a> to stand in for the Englishman in this race; he was replaced at Brabham by <a href="/wiki/1987_International_Formula_3000_Championship" title="1987 International Formula 3000 Championship">Formula 3000</a> champion <a href="/wiki/Stefano_Modena" title="Stefano Modena">Stefano Modena</a>, making his Formula One debut.
</p><p>At the green light, it was <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a>, in his last race for Williams before moving to <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Lotus</a> in <a href="/wiki/1988_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1988 Formula One World Championship">1988</a>, who got away best of all, darting past Berger to take the lead into the first chicane whilst <a href="/wiki/Alessandro_Nannini" title="Alessandro Nannini">Alessandro Nannini</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a> was out immediately after crashing into the wall on the exit. A confident Berger, fresh from his victory in the previous race in <a href="/wiki/1987_Japanese_Grand_Prix" title="1987 Japanese Grand Prix">Japan</a>, re-passed Piquet at Wakefield Corner and began to make a break from the field.
</p><p>Early retirements included <a href="/wiki/Philippe_Streiff" title="Philippe Streiff">Philippe Streiff</a> spinning off in his <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_Racing" title="Tyrrell Racing">Tyrrell</a> and debutant Modena stopping in the pits due to exhaustion. The Italian drew the attention of BBC commentators <a href="/wiki/Murray_Walker" title="Murray Walker">Murray Walker</a> and <a href="/wiki/James_Hunt" title="James Hunt">James Hunt</a> for his efforts, as well as his highly superstitious manner; Modena wouldn't let anyone touch the car once strapped in and drove with one glove inside out.
</p><p>The battle for second between Piquet, <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michele_Alboreto" title="Michele Alboreto">Michele Alboreto</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> changed little until lap 35 when Piquet pitted for tyres and dropped to 6th. Seven laps later, Prost found himself baulked by former team-mate <a href="/wiki/Rene_Arnoux" class="mw-redirect" title="Rene Arnoux">Rene Arnoux</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Ligier" title="Ligier">Ligier</a> on the pits straight and Alboreto slipped through. Senna, meanwhile, decided gung-ho was best and powered past both; 4th to 2nd in one move.
</p><p>Attrition kicked in as the race continued with brakes in particular being a big issue. <a href="/wiki/Teo_Fabi" title="Teo Fabi">Teo Fabi</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a> was the first brake-related retirement on lap 46. Shortly after, <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> lost both cars within five laps as Prost and team-mate <a href="/wiki/Stefan_Johansson" title="Stefan Johansson">Stefan Johansson</a> spun off at Stag Turn and Wakefield Corner respectively, again with brake issues. Piquet's brakes also failed on lap 58 leaving Berger, Senna and Alboreto as the top three.
</p><p>Senna made a late charge, trading fastest laps with the Austrian, but Berger had enough in hand to respond, despite having what appeared to be a dragging under-tray and took his second consecutive victory by just under 35 seconds. Senna finished second but was later disqualified when post race scrutineering revealed oversized brake ducts on his Lotus. Alboreto was promoted up to second to make it a Ferrari 1-2, the first since Alboreto and Johansson finished 1-2 in the <a href="/wiki/1985_Canadian_Grand_Prix" title="1985 Canadian Grand Prix">1985 Canadian Grand Prix</a>. Third across the line was the Benetton of <a href="/wiki/Thierry_Boutsen" title="Thierry Boutsen">Thierry Boutsen</a>. Of the races seven finishers (the Brabham of <a href="/wiki/Andrea_de_Cesaris" title="Andrea de Cesaris">Andrea de Cesaris</a> and the Williams of Patrese were classified as finishers despite not running at the end), Alboreto was the only driver on the same lap as Berger.
</p><p>The first non-turbo car to finish was the Tyrrell of <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Palmer" title="Jonathan Palmer">Jonathan Palmer</a> in 4th place. Frenchman <a href="/wiki/Yannick_Dalmas" title="Yannick Dalmas">Yannick Dalmas</a> finished 5th in his <a href="/wiki/Larrousse" title="Larrousse">Larrousse</a> (but wasn't awarded points as the team had only entered one car for the season) with triple Australian Grand Prix winner <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Moreno" title="Roberto Moreno">Roberto Moreno</a> (<a href="/wiki/1981_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="1981 Australian Grand Prix">1981</a>, <a href="/wiki/1983_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="1983 Australian Grand Prix">1983</a> and <a href="/wiki/1984_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="1984 Australian Grand Prix">1984</a>) scoring a point in his Formula One debut with <a href="/wiki/Automobiles_Gonfaronnaises_Sportives" title="Automobiles Gonfaronnaises Sportives">AGS</a> by finishing 6th. The last car to cross the finish line was the <a href="/wiki/Zakspeed" title="Zakspeed">Zakspeed</a> of <a href="/wiki/Christian_Danner" title="Christian Danner">Christian Danner</a>, 3 laps down in 7th.
</p>
