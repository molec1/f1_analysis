<p>The <b>1985 German Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held at <a href="/wiki/N%C3%BCrburgring" title="Nürburgring">Nürburgring</a> on 4 August 1985. It was the ninth race of the <a href="/wiki/1985_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="1985 Formula One season">1985 Formula One season</a>.
</p><p>This was the first German Grand Prix that was held on the new GP-Strecke section of the track that replaced the old <a href="/wiki/S%C3%BCdschleife" title="Südschleife">Südschleife</a> section that had not been used since 1970. It was also the first time in nine years the German GP was held at Nürburgring; previously the German GP was held on the dangerous and very long Nordschleife section of the track up until <a href="/wiki/1976_German_Grand_Prix" title="1976 German Grand Prix">1976</a>, and at that race <a href="/wiki/Niki_Lauda" title="Niki Lauda">Niki Lauda</a> had a near-fatal accident in his Ferrari that forced the Formula One circus to move to the safer, shorter and faster <a href="/wiki/Hockenheimring" title="Hockenheimring">Hockenheimring</a>. The GP-Strecke would not host another Grand Prix until it hosted the <a href="/wiki/1995_European_Grand_Prix" title="1995 European Grand Prix">1995 European Grand Prix</a>, and would not host the German Grand Prix again until <a href="/wiki/2009_German_Grand_Prix" title="2009 German Grand Prix">2009</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Michele_Alboreto" title="Michele Alboreto">Michele Alboreto</a> won the race, his fifth and last F1 victory. This event was the first occasion on which an <a href="/wiki/Onboard_camera" title="Onboard camera">onboard camera</a> was used in a race; one was attached to <a href="/wiki/Fran%C3%A7ois_Hesnault" title="François Hesnault">François Hesnault</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a>. That car was not eligible for championship points, and this race marked the last time, as of the end of the <a href="/wiki/2019_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2019 Formula One World Championship">2019 season</a>, that an F1 team entered three cars for a race.
</p><p>As it was the German Grand Prix (and that the team still only had 1 new car built) <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_Racing" title="Tyrrell Racing">Tyrrell</a> team boss <a href="/wiki/Ken_Tyrrell" title="Ken Tyrrell">Ken Tyrrell</a> gave his <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">German</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Stefan_Bellof" title="Stefan Bellof">Stefan Bellof</a> use of the Renault powered <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_014" title="Tyrrell 014">Tyrrell 014</a> for the weekend instead of the car's usual driver <a href="/wiki/Martin_Brundle" title="Martin Brundle">Martin Brundle</a> who was given Bellof's <a href="/wiki/Cosworth_DFV" title="Cosworth DFV">Cosworth</a> powered <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_012" title="Tyrrell 012">012</a> to drive. To get around a <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_du_Sport_Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale du Sport Automobile">FISA</a> rule that a team and driver can not run more than 2 engines in the one chassis during a season (with the 012 running the Cosworth and 014 the Renault), Tyrrell simply switched the drivers from one car to the other but not their car numbers meaning Bellof drove in car #3 and Brundle in car #4. Bellof qualified the <a href="/wiki/Turbo" class="mw-redirect" title="Turbo">turbo</a> powered car 19th while Brundle was 26th and last with the Cosworth V8, some 10.2 seconds of Fabi's pole time and 6.4 seconds slower than his teammate in his usual car, something Brundle was reported to be not happy with.
</p><p>This was to be the last Grand Prix for <a href="/wiki/Manfred_Winkelhock" title="Manfred Winkelhock">Manfred Winkelhock</a>: the German fatally crashed his <a href="/wiki/Porsche_962" title="Porsche 962">Porsche 962C</a> sportscar at the <a href="/wiki/1985_1000_km_of_Mosport" title="1985 1000 km of Mosport">1000 km of Mosport</a> in Canada the following week.
</p>
