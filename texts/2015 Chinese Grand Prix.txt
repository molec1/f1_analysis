<p>The <b>2015 Chinese Grand Prix</b> (formally known as the <b>2015 Formula 1 Chinese Grand Prix</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Auto_racing" title="Auto racing">motor race</a> that was held on 12 April 2015 at the <a href="/wiki/Shanghai_International_Circuit" title="Shanghai International Circuit">Shanghai International Circuit</a> in <a href="/wiki/Shanghai" title="Shanghai">Shanghai</a>, <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>. The race was the third round of the <a href="/wiki/2015_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2015 Formula One season">2015 season</a>, and marked the twelfth time that the <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Grand_Prix" title="Chinese Grand Prix">Chinese Grand Prix</a> has been run as a round of the Formula One World Championship.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> was the defending race winner and went into the weekend with a three-point lead in the world championship over <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a>, who had surprised the field by taking victory in the previous round at <a href="/wiki/2015_Malaysian_Grand_Prix" title="2015 Malaysian Grand Prix">Malaysia</a>. Hamilton took pole position during Saturday's qualifying, the 41st of his career and the third in a row. He went on to win the race from his teammate <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Chinese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Chinese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a>'s surprise victory two weeks prior in <a href="/wiki/2015_Malaysian_Grand_Prix" title="2015 Malaysian Grand Prix">Malaysia</a>, eyes were on <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> whether they would be able to continue their good race pace in China and challenge the <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> for the win. As lower track temperatures were expected for the race, a serious competitiveness of Ferrari was cast into doubt, as they had benefited from lower tyre degradation in the heat of Malaysia.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> All ten teams taking part in the <a href="/wiki/2015_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2015 Formula One season">championship</a> participated in the race, with <a href="/wiki/Marussia_F1" title="Marussia F1">Marussia</a> hoping to finally be able to run both cars continually, after <a href="/wiki/Will_Stevens" title="Will Stevens">Will Stevens</a> had failed to start in Malaysia.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As in 2014, <a href="/wiki/Pirelli" title="Pirelli">Pirelli</a> announced they would be supplying teams with the white-banded medium compound tyre as the prime selection and the yellow-banded soft compound as the option selection for the event.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Free_practice">Free practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Chinese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Free practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Per the regulations for the 2015 season, three practice sessions were held, two 1.5-hour sessions on Friday and another one-hour session before qualifying on Saturday.<sup id="cite_ref-pracreg_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pracreg-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> World champion <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> took a clean sweep and topped the time sheets in all three sessions by a considerable margin. The first session saw <a href="/wiki/Lotus_F1" title="Lotus F1">Lotus</a> test driver <a href="/wiki/Jolyon_Palmer" title="Jolyon Palmer">Jolyon Palmer</a> participate in place of regular <a href="/wiki/Romain_Grosjean" title="Romain Grosjean">Romain Grosjean</a>. He finished six-tenths of a second behind his teammate <a href="/wiki/Pastor_Maldonado" title="Pastor Maldonado">Pastor Maldonado</a> and was one of several drivers to have off track experiences during the session. Both <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> drivers ran wide on different parts of the track, as did <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a>, who spun his <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> in turn 14 towards the end of the session. Hamilton was over half a second clear of his teammate <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> on Friday morning, with the Ferraris more than a second behind.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Vettel_perez_2015_china.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e2/Vettel_perez_2015_china.jpg/220px-Vettel_perez_2015_china.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e2/Vettel_perez_2015_china.jpg/330px-Vettel_perez_2015_china.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e2/Vettel_perez_2015_china.jpg/440px-Vettel_perez_2015_china.jpg 2x" data-file-width="5184" data-file-height="3456" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Vettel_perez_2015_china.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Sparks caused by titanium plates underneath the cars returned to Formula One in the <a href="/wiki/2015_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2015 Formula One World Championship">2015</a> season.</div></div></div>
<p>Hamilton continued his strong performance during the second session on Friday afternoon, beating Malaysia winner Sebastian Vettel for the top spot, Vettel finishing almost half a second down. Most teams ran with both dry tyre compounds. Mercedes appeared more dominant on the harder tyres, one second ahead of the rest of the grid. <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a> continued to struggle with the same brake problems they had endured in Malaysia two weeks before. The left rear brake of <a href="/wiki/Daniil_Kvyat" title="Daniil Kvyat">Daniil Kvyat</a> started to smoke after a pit stop, leading the team to investigate the matter further.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Felipe Massa spun yet again in turn 14, hitting the barrier with his front wing meaning the session was <a href="/wiki/Racing_flags#Red_flag" title="Racing flags">red-flagged</a> for a while. The session was further disrupted by the appearance of a local man running across the main straight, jumping into pit lane before being stopped by security after entering the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> garage, where he apparently stated he wanted to "try a car".<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Hamilton was once again fastest during third practice on Saturday morning, even while suffering from overheating under his seat. The two Mercedes cars were separated by two-tenths of a second with the two Ferraris of Vettel and <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> behind, another half a second down. Red Bull had a slight upturn in fortune, with <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> and Daniil Kvyat finishing fifth and sixth respectively. <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Honda_in_Formula_One" title="Honda in Formula One">Honda</a> had another problematic session as <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> stopped the car just after starting his installation lap. Teammate <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> was called into the pit lane as well, citing a similar problem with the engine, but was able to continue later in the session.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Felipe Massa experienced smoke in his cockpit both on Friday and Saturday, apparently caused by the new titanium plates underneath the cars which also create sparks when driving over bumps on the track.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Chinese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Ricciardo_2015_China_pits.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/60/Ricciardo_2015_China_pits.jpg/220px-Ricciardo_2015_China_pits.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/60/Ricciardo_2015_China_pits.jpg/330px-Ricciardo_2015_China_pits.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/60/Ricciardo_2015_China_pits.jpg/440px-Ricciardo_2015_China_pits.jpg 2x" data-file-width="5184" data-file-height="3456" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Ricciardo_2015_China_pits.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> out-qualified teammate <a href="/wiki/Daniil_Kvyat" title="Daniil Kvyat">Daniil Kvyat</a> for the third successive race, and qualified seventh.</div></div></div>
<p>Qualifying consisted of three parts, 18, 15 and 12 minutes in length respectively, with five drivers eliminated from competing after each of the first two sessions.<sup id="cite_ref-pracreg_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pracreg-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In the session, which started at 15:00 <a href="/wiki/Time_in_China" title="Time in China">local time</a> (<a href="/wiki/UTC%2B08:00" title="UTC+08:00">UTC +8</a>) on Saturday afternoon, Lewis Hamilton took the third out of three possible pole positions of the season, narrowly beating his teammate by 0.04 seconds. <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> was frustrated about the result, lamenting that his team had put unnecessary pressure on him by telling him to go faster during his last warm-up lap, saying "[w]e need to look at that as that was not ideal to put me under pressure like that".<sup id="cite_ref-telegraph_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-telegraph-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Mercedes were the only team to use the medium tyre in the first qualifying session (Q1), with all other teams using the faster soft tyre. Five drivers were eliminated in Q1, four of them being the two <a href="/wiki/Marussia_F1" title="Marussia F1">Marussia</a> and McLaren cars, all of which had yet failed to go through to Q2. They were joined by the <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a> of <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a>, who was to start 16th on the grid.
</p><p>The second session saw Hamilton post the fastest time, even though his car lost contact to pit lane and his seat overheating once more. He was three-tenths clear of his teammate Rosberg, with Sebastian Vettel in third. Daniil Kvyat suffered engine problems and failed to make it into Q3, as did the second Force India of <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a>, both <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso</a> drivers and the <a href="/wiki/Lotus_F1" title="Lotus F1">Lotus</a> of Pastor Maldonado.
</p><p>During Q3, contested by the top ten, Hamilton just beat his teammate to pole position, with a time set during his first timed lap. Both Ferrari drivers ran their first lap on used tyres in order to save one set for the race. In their second outing, Vettel was able to get ahead of the two Williams cars of Felipe Massa and <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a>, while teammate Kimi Räikkönen struggled with the handling of his car in the first part of the track to finish sixth on the grid.<sup id="cite_ref-telegraph_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-telegraph-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Chinese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Kvyat_stranded_car_china_2015.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ab/Kvyat_stranded_car_china_2015.jpg/220px-Kvyat_stranded_car_china_2015.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="124" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ab/Kvyat_stranded_car_china_2015.jpg/330px-Kvyat_stranded_car_china_2015.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ab/Kvyat_stranded_car_china_2015.jpg/440px-Kvyat_stranded_car_china_2015.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4000" data-file-height="2250" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Kvyat_stranded_car_china_2015.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Daniil_Kvyat" title="Daniil Kvyat">Daniil Kvyat</a> retired from the race on lap 17.</div></div></div>
<p>When the race started at 14:00 <a href="/wiki/Time_in_China" title="Time in China">local time</a> (<a href="/wiki/UTC%2B08:00" title="UTC+08:00">UTC +8</a>), Kimi Räikkönen managed to pass both Williams cars on the first lap to move up to fourth, while Nico Rosberg was able to fend off Sebastian Vettel for second. On the second lap, <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Sainz_Jr." title="Carlos Sainz Jr.">Carlos Sainz Jr.</a> spun his Toro Rosso and dropped down the order. <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a> became the first retirement on lap ten, when he parked his Force India in the gravel after his gearbox failed. During the pit stops, Ferrari attempted an undercut by pitting Sebastian Vettel early on lap 13, a strategy in which a car running behind is pitting early in order to use a fast out-lap on fresh tyres to jump ahead of a rival without having to overtake them on track. However this was unsuccessful as Mercedes reacted by pitting Rosberg the following lap, allowing Rosberg to maintain track position over Vettel. On lap 17, Daniil Kvyat retired from the race when his Red Bull started to smoke.
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Ericsson_Maldonado_China_2015.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9c/Ericsson_Maldonado_China_2015.jpg/220px-Ericsson_Maldonado_China_2015.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9c/Ericsson_Maldonado_China_2015.jpg/330px-Ericsson_Maldonado_China_2015.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9c/Ericsson_Maldonado_China_2015.jpg/440px-Ericsson_Maldonado_China_2015.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4000" data-file-height="3000" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Ericsson_Maldonado_China_2015.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Marcus_Ericsson" title="Marcus Ericsson">Marcus Ericsson</a> locks up under braking for turn 14.</div></div></div>
<p>After the first pit stops, the Mercedes drivers had to manage their tyres in order to cover from a possible attack by Ferrari, who had an extra set of fresh tyres saved from qualifying. Rosberg however complained over the radio about Hamilton in front of him driving too slow, which caused him to back into the charging Ferraris. He was unable to risk driving too close behind Hamilton, because dispersed air from the back of the car was accelerating tyre degradation. Hamilton was therefore asked by the team to pick up the pace.<sup id="cite_ref-guardian_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guardian-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The top three began to spread out in the second half of the race, and by lap 54 Lewis Hamilton was leading Rosberg by 10.06 seconds, with Vettel a further 12.12 seconds behind Rosberg.<sup id="cite_ref-Lap_charts_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lap_charts-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> However, due to Ferrari's earlier attempt to undercut Rosberg by pitting Vettel early, Räikkönen's tyres were 4 laps fresher than his teammate's, and he closed in on Vettel over the final stint. Having emerged from his final pit stop 4.84 seconds behind Vettel on lap 35, Räikkönen had closed the gap to 1.37 seconds by lap 54.<sup id="cite_ref-Lap_charts_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lap_charts-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> However, the chance of a battle between the Ferrari teammates was extinguished after a transmission failure on <a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a>'s Toro Rosso, who was stranded on the start/finish-straight, brought out the safety car for the final two laps, meaning that the race ended under safety car conditions.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Hamilton_China_2015.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d9/Hamilton_China_2015.jpg/220px-Hamilton_China_2015.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d9/Hamilton_China_2015.jpg/330px-Hamilton_China_2015.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d9/Hamilton_China_2015.jpg/440px-Hamilton_China_2015.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3155" data-file-height="2366" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Hamilton_China_2015.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> on the last lap of the race</div></div></div>
<p>Hamilton crossed the line to win his second race of the season, with Rosberg following him home in 2nd and Vettel completing the podium with 3rd.<sup id="cite_ref-guardian_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guardian-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Pastor Maldonado had an eventful race. On lap 33, while running in seventh position ahead of teammate <a href="/wiki/Romain_Grosjean" title="Romain Grosjean">Romain Grosjean</a>, he missed the pit entry and lost time, dropping several positions. He then proceeded to spin his car on lap 39 as he attempted to make up for lost time, and on lap 48 he had a collision with Jenson Button following a long battle for position, damaging his car and retiring with rear brake failure a few laps later. The stewards later deemed Button to be at fault for the incident.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Chinese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the podium interview, conducted by former <a href="/wiki/Track_and_field" title="Track and field">track and field</a> athlete <a href="/wiki/Edwin_Moses" title="Edwin Moses">Edwin Moses</a>, race winner Lewis Hamilton acknowledged "a fantastic job by the team" and expressed disappointment over the late safety car, calling it "kind of an anti-climax when you have a good race like that".<sup id="cite_ref-press_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-press-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> During the press conference after the race, Nico Rosberg accused his teammate of deliberately slowing down during the middle stint of the race and compromising his race in the process.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton declared, when asked about the radio message telling him to speed up, that he "wasn't controlling his [Nico's] race, I was controlling my own race". Rosberg reacted to that statement by saying: "It's just now interesting to hear from you, Lewis, that you were just thinking about yourself with the pace in front, and necessarily that was compromising my race."<sup id="cite_ref-press_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-press-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton was subsequently backed up by Mercedes non-executive chairman <a href="/wiki/Niki_Lauda" title="Niki Lauda">Niki Lauda</a>, who said: "Sure, everyone drives selfish. [...] What do you think these guys are here to do? I call them egocentric bastards. That is the only way to win and the only way to win the Championship, they are all the same."<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
