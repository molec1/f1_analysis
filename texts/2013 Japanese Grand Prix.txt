<p>The <b>2013 Japanese Grand Prix</b> (formally known as the <b>2013 Formula 1 Japanese Grand Prix</b>)<sup id="cite_ref-formal_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-formal-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Auto_racing" title="Auto racing">motor race</a> that was held on 13 October 2013 at the <a href="/wiki/Suzuka_Circuit" class="mw-redirect" title="Suzuka Circuit">Suzuka Circuit</a> in <a href="/wiki/Suzuka,_Mie" title="Suzuka, Mie">Suzuka</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_calender_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA_calender-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The race was the fifteenth round of the <a href="/wiki/2013_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2013 Formula One season">2013 season</a>, and marked the 39th running of the <a href="/wiki/Japanese_Grand_Prix" title="Japanese Grand Prix">Japanese Grand Prix</a>. The race, contested over 53 laps, was won by <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a> after starting from second on the grid. <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a>, who started on <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>, settled for second after being forced to switch to a three stop strategy, which in the end was not successful.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Reliable_sources/Perennial_sources" title="Wikipedia:Reliable sources/Perennial sources"><span title="The material near this tag may rely on an unreliable source (October 2019)">deprecated source</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
<a href="/wiki/Romain_Grosjean" title="Romain Grosjean">Romain Grosjean</a> took his second podium in succession in third position for <a href="/wiki/Lotus_F1" title="Lotus F1">Lotus F1</a>. This was Red Bull's 14th one-two finish in Formula One.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The result meant that the title was not sealed at Suzuka but Vettel could seal it with 5th place at the next race in India. Meanwhile, Fernando Alonso stretched his lead to 30 points over Kimi Räikkönen but was still 90 behind Vettel's total.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
Räikkönen in turn also increased his lead over Lewis Hamilton to 16 points after the Briton retired from puncture damage after colliding with Vettel on lap one. The result also meant that only Alonso could deprive Vettel of becoming world champion as Hamilton and Räikkönen fell out of contention.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2013_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2013_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Tyre supplier <a href="/wiki/Pirelli" title="Pirelli">Pirelli</a> brought its Orange-banded hard compound tyre as the harder "prime" tyre and the White-banded Medium compound tyre as the softer "option" tyre, as opposed to the previous year where hard and soft selection were provided. <a href="/wiki/Jules_Bianchi" title="Jules Bianchi">Jules Bianchi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Charles_Pic" title="Charles Pic">Charles Pic</a> were each given ten-place grid penalties after stewards found they had been speeding behind the safety car at the previous round in Korea. Both drivers were reprimanded for their actions, and automatically received a ten-place grid penalty as it was their third reprimand of the season.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Free_Practice">Free Practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2013_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Free Practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Free Practice 1 saw <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> drive for <a href="/wiki/Caterham" title="Caterham">Caterham</a>, in place of <a href="/wiki/Charles_Pic" title="Charles Pic">Charles Pic</a>. The session was not without incident, as both <a href="/wiki/Jules_Bianchi" title="Jules Bianchi">Jules Bianchi</a> (<a href="/wiki/Marussia" class="mw-redirect" title="Marussia">Marussia</a>), <a href="/wiki/Giedo_van_der_Garde" title="Giedo van der Garde">Giedo van der Garde</a> (Caterham) both went off at the exit of the second Degner corner. As a result of his accident, Bianchi was forced to sit out of Free Practice 2, while his car was repaired. <a href="/wiki/Pastor_Maldonado" title="Pastor Maldonado">Pastor Maldonado</a> suffered a loose wheel up at Spoon Curve. Williams were later fined €60,000 for failing to attach the wheel properly. <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> set the fastest lap of the session, with teammate <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> behind him.
</p><p>Free Practice 2 saw Pastor Maldonado also going off at the second Degner corner, where he became stuck in the gravel. <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a> lost control of his McLaren going into Spoon, and slid backwards and made heavy contact with the tyre barrier. <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> spun at the second Degner corner, but he was able to continue. <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> spun into the gravel trap at the Dunlop corner and was forced to end his session. This time championship leader <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> finished the session fastest, with teammate <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> behind him.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2013_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In a first, <a href="/wiki/Charles_Pic" title="Charles Pic">Charles Pic</a> was given a drive-through penalty for leaving the pits when the red light was displayed at the pit exit during qualifying. The penalty was to be served during the first five laps of the race.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
