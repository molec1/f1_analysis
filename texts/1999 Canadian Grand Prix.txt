<p>The <b>1999 Canadian Grand Prix</b><sup id="cite_ref-formula1_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-formula1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 13 June 1999 at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_Gilles_Villeneuve" title="Circuit Gilles Villeneuve">Circuit Gilles Villeneuve</a> in <a href="/wiki/Montreal" title="Montreal">Montreal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">Quebec</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>. It was the sixth race of the <a href="/wiki/1999_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="1999 Formula One season">1999 Formula One season</a>. The race was notable for the four crashes that occurred in turn 13 and for the number of times the safety car was deployed. Turn 13, the final chicane, had four separate crashes in which a driver either went wide or spun into the wall. Three of the drivers who crashed there had previously won the Drivers' Championship, leading to the wall becoming known as the "Wall of Champions". The safety car was deployed four times in the race, a record at the time, and was the first F1 race to finish behind the safety car following <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a>'s massive crash with four laps to go.<sup id="cite_ref-f1technical_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1technical-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1999_Canadian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1999_Canadian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Mika_Hakkinen_1999_Canada.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a6/Mika_Hakkinen_1999_Canada.jpg/220px-Mika_Hakkinen_1999_Canada.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="146" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a6/Mika_Hakkinen_1999_Canada.jpg/330px-Mika_Hakkinen_1999_Canada.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a6/Mika_Hakkinen_1999_Canada.jpg/440px-Mika_Hakkinen_1999_Canada.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="426" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Mika_Hakkinen_1999_Canada.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The race was won by <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> for <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>The front runners got off the line cleanly, with <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> maintaining first place, after starting on pole for the first time in 1999, ahead of <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a>. Behind them <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> passed <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> to take fifth place. <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a>, starting ninth, went off the track trying to pass Heinz-Harald Frentzen up the inside into turn one. Trulli spun across turn one, collecting <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> in the process. Trulli and Alesi retired, and the safety car was deployed. <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Wurz" title="Alexander Wurz">Alexander Wurz</a> also retired during the first lap with transmission problems. Barichello limped back to the pits where repairs were made to his car. His was able to resume the race, albeit two laps down on the leader, but ultimately had to retire because of the collision damage after having completed 14 laps.<sup id="cite_ref-autosport_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosport-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:0_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Eddie_Irvine_1999_Canada.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/69/Eddie_Irvine_1999_Canada.jpg/220px-Eddie_Irvine_1999_Canada.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/69/Eddie_Irvine_1999_Canada.jpg/330px-Eddie_Irvine_1999_Canada.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/69/Eddie_Irvine_1999_Canada.jpg/440px-Eddie_Irvine_1999_Canada.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="479" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Eddie_Irvine_1999_Canada.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a> posted his only career fastest lap in this race.</div></div></div>
<p>The safety car returned to the pits at the end of the second lap, having been deployed for only one lap, allowing normal racing to resume with the top six being Michael Schumacher, Häkkinen, Irvine, Fisichella, Coulthard and Frentzen.
</p><p>During the third lap Coulthard passed Fisichella going into turn 13 to retake fourth place. Later that same lap, <a href="/wiki/Ricardo_Zonta" title="Ricardo Zonta">Ricardo Zonta</a> retired after spinning into the wall at that same turn 13. He stopped just off the track, missing his right rear wheel. This brought the safety car out again. Zonta was the first of four drivers to hit the "Wall of Champions" in this race.
</p><p>At the end of lap 7 the safety left the track again, allowing the race to resume normally again. The order remained stable until on lap 15, <a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a> became the second driver to hit the turn 13 wall. He was able to pull off the track in a safe location, and the safety car was not deployed.
</p><p>During lap 24, <a href="/wiki/Pedro_de_la_Rosa" title="Pedro de la Rosa">Pedro de la Rosa</a> came in to the pits for a scheduled stop, but when trying to leave his gearbox failed. This forced him to retire. One lap later <a href="/wiki/Stewart_Grand_Prix" title="Stewart Grand Prix">Stewart</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Herbert" title="Johnny Herbert">Johnny Herbert</a>, from seventh position, was the first driver running in the top ten to make a scheduled pit stop. He returned to the track in tenth place.
</p><p>Michael Schumacher hit the wall at turn 13 on lap 30, giving the lead to <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a>.  Five laps later, <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a> became the fourth and final driver on the day to hit the wall at that turn, bringing the safety car out for the third time. Hill, Schumacher, and Villeneuve were three of the four Formula 1 Drivers' Champions competing in the race who hit the wall.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcsport_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcsport-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The third safety car period, occurring around the halfway point of the race, was used by many drivers to make a scheduled pitstop. Fisichella, Frentzen, <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Diniz" title="Pedro Diniz">Pedro Diniz</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a>, Herbert, Häkkinen,and  Irvine all came into the pits shortly before or after the safety car having being deployed to the circuit.
</p><p>Near the end of lap 38, David Coulthard passed the safety car before entering the pit lane for his scheduled stop. Shortly afterwards <a href="/wiki/Alex_Zanardi" title="Alex Zanardi">Alessandro Zanardi</a> did the same after coming out of the pit lane following his stop trying to prevent being lapped. Both Coulthard and Zanardi also ignored a red light at the end of the pit lane when exiting following their pit stop.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Following the series of pit stops, the top six behind the safety car was Häkkinen, Irvine, Coulthard, Fisichella, <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a> and Ralf Schumacher. Normal racing restarted again on lap 41. Coulthard tried to go around the outside of <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a> into turn one, then the inside on turn two. However they touched, and both spun off. Both were able to rejoin, though Coulthard had to pit at the end of the lap. This dropped them to 8th and 10th place respectively while Fisichella and Frentzen took over third and fourth place with Ralf Schumacher and Diniz following in fifth and sixth.
</p><p>Two laps later Fisichella and Frentzen found themselves trying to lap <a href="/wiki/Luca_Badoer" title="Luca Badoer">Luca Badoer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Panis" title="Olivier Panis">Olivier Panis</a>. However Panis attempted to pass Badoer while approaching turn 13, thus ingoring blue flags being showed to them. Panis was unable to pass before the turn and being on the outside going into it had to yield. This meant he went rather slowly through the turn, forcing Fisichella behind wide which in turn allowed Frentzen to move into third place. On the same lap, <a href="/wiki/Toranosuke_Takagi" title="Toranosuke Takagi">Toranosuke Takagi</a>, already two laps from the lead, slowly entered the pit lane to retire his <a href="/wiki/Arrows_Grand_Prix_International" title="Arrows Grand Prix International">Arrows</a> with transmission problems.
</p><p>During lap 47 Coulthard, Zanardi, Badoer and Panis were all issued 10 second stop and go penalties. Coulthard and Zanardi for ignoring the red light in the pit lane following their pitstops, Badoer and Panis for ignoring blue flags.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The latter three served their penalties on the same lap, while Coulthard followed suit one lap later.
</p><p>Meanwhile, Irvine continued to move back up the order following the incident with Coulthard. By lap 46 he had moved into seventh place. At the start of lap 47 he passed Diniz for sixth place going into turn one. Six laps later he passed Herbert for fifth place going into turn 13, though both drivers had to cut across the grass. Another five lap later he passed Ralf Schumacher on the same spot to take fourth place. In the meantime, on lap 51, Zanardi retired with brake problems.
</p><p>The fourth and final safety car of the race came out on lap 66 after Heinz-Harald Frentzen, running in second place, suffered a brake failure whilst approaching turn three, crashing into the barriers sideways. He was uninjured. The race then finished under safety car conditions, a first for Formula One,<sup id="cite_ref-:0_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> with Mika Häkkinen victorious.<sup id="cite_ref-autosport_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosport-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Eddie Irvine scored his first fastest lap<sup id="cite_ref-chicanef1_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-chicanef1-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> and finished third behind <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a>.
</p>
