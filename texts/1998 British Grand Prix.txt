<p>The <b>1998 British Grand Prix </b>(formally the <b>LI RAC British Grand Prix</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held at the <a href="/wiki/Silverstone_Circuit" title="Silverstone Circuit">Silverstone Circuit</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a> on 12 July 1998. It was the ninth race of the <a href="/wiki/1998_FIA_Formula_One_World_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="1998 FIA Formula One World Championship">1998 FIA Formula One World Championship</a>. The 60-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> driving a <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> car after starting from second position. <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a>, who started from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>, finished second with <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a> third in the other Ferrari. Schumacher's victory was his fourth of the season, and his third consecutive victory having won both the preceding <a href="/wiki/1998_Canadian_Grand_Prix" title="1998 Canadian Grand Prix">Canadian</a> and <a href="/wiki/1998_French_Grand_Prix" title="1998 French Grand Prix">French Grand Prix</a>, but was under controversial circumstances: there was dispute whether he had avoided a stop-and-go penalty by entering the pit lane to serve it on the final lap, crossing the finish line in the pit lane to win the race before reaching his pit box, although the penalty, which was actually merely a time penalty, was later rescinded. This race was held the same day as the <a href="/wiki/1998_FIFA_World_Cup_Final" title="1998 FIFA World Cup Final">1998 FIFA World Cup Final</a> in <a href="/wiki/Saint-Denis,_Seine-Saint-Denis" title="Saint-Denis, Seine-Saint-Denis">Saint-Denis</a>, <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>, but that event took place later in the day from this Grand Prix.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1998_British_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1998_British_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Mika Häkkinen took pole position by nearly half a second from championship rival Michael Schumacher. Jacques Villeneuve took third position whilst Häkkinen's team-mate David Coulthard qualified in fourth. <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> and <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Panis" title="Olivier Panis">Olivier Panis</a> had their qualifying times deleted as they were not able to get out of their cars quick enough during an <a href="/wiki/FIA" class="mw-redirect" title="FIA">FIA</a> safety drill, to practice evacuating the cockpit in case of fire. Ralf Schumacher had spun and stalled his engine early in qualifying and qualified in the spare car, which was set up for his team-mate Damon Hill.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1998_British_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Heavy rain fell during the morning prior to the race. Although the rain stopped before the start, there was a mixture of wet and dry parts on the circuit and as a result all but two cars started on intermediate compound tyres. The two Stewart-Fords decided to start on dry weather tyres.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The first retirement came after 13 laps, when Damon Hill lost control on the damp track whilst battling with Villeneuve for 7th place. After 16 laps, it began to rain again, and many drivers switched to a full wet weather tyre. <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Herbert" title="Johnny Herbert">Johnny Herbert</a> spun and regained the track, but had damaged his car and retired on reaching the pits.
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Coulthard-Silverstone98.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/39/Coulthard-Silverstone98.jpg/220px-Coulthard-Silverstone98.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="125" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/39/Coulthard-Silverstone98.jpg/330px-Coulthard-Silverstone98.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/39/Coulthard-Silverstone98.jpg/440px-Coulthard-Silverstone98.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1024" data-file-height="584" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Coulthard-Silverstone98.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> retires from the race</div></div></div>
<p>David Coulthard, driving on intermediates, spun out on lap 38 whilst passing a backmarker. <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> spun out on the same lap as Coulthard as Barrichello spun out and hit the wall at Club on lap 40. His <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Mercedes_AMG_High_Performance_Powertrains" title="Mercedes AMG High Performance Powertrains">Mercedes</a> team-mate <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> had built up a lead of 49 seconds over second place driver <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> when four laps later he went off the track, did a complete 360 degrees turn before continuing. The incident damaged the front wing of his car and cost him 10 seconds of his lead but following numerous other spins caused by the worsening conditions the safety car was deployed which slowed the cars down, and removed Häkkinen's advantage over Schumacher altogether.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The race restarted on lap 50 and it took only two laps for Häkkinen to make another mistake, which put Schumacher in the lead. The German quickly pulled away from his rival, who was now nursing his McLaren home.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:DC%26MSch_Britain_1998.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e2/DC%26MSch_Britain_1998.jpg/220px-DC%26MSch_Britain_1998.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="124" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e2/DC%26MSch_Britain_1998.jpg/330px-DC%26MSch_Britain_1998.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e2/DC%26MSch_Britain_1998.jpg/440px-DC%26MSch_Britain_1998.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2410" data-file-height="1354" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:DC%26MSch_Britain_1998.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> and <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> side by side</div></div></div>
<p>However, two laps from the finish, Schumacher was issued with a 10-second penalty for passing <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Wurz" title="Alexander Wurz">Alexander Wurz</a> under the safety car on lap 43.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Unsure whether the handwritten notification declared that Schumacher would see 10 seconds being added to his race time or had to serve a 10-second stop-and-go penalty, his team decided to call in him into the pits out of precaution to serve a stop-and-go penalty at the end of the last lap of the race.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> However, in doing so Schumacher crossed the finish line in the pit lane before reaching the Ferrari garage resulting in a dispute on whether he had actually served a stop and go penalty.<sup id="cite_ref-england98_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-england98-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1998_British_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Following the race a dispute arose surrounding the circumstances of Michael Schumacher being issued with and serving his penalty. <sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> His team argued that the penalty should have been issued within 25 minutes of the incident but instead they were informed after 31 minutes. They also argued that the hand-written notification was unclear as to which penalty was actually being issued: a stop-and-go, or a 10-second addition to Schumacher's race time, though the <a href="/wiki/FIA" class="mw-redirect" title="FIA">FIA</a>'s International Court of Appeal later clarified that it was the latter.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The stewards then decided to nevertheless apply the 10-second addition, post-race.<sup id="cite_ref-england98_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-england98-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> However, the added time penalty can only be used to punish an infraction in the last 12 laps of a Grand Prix, and so did not apply here.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The stewards eventually rescinded the penalty altogether.<sup id="cite_ref-england98_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-england98-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>A protest was lodged by <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Mercedes_AMG_High_Performance_Powertrains" title="Mercedes AMG High Performance Powertrains">Mercedes</a> who felt Ferrari cheated by not having Schumacher serve the penalty, but this was rejected by the FIA.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> At the hearing for the protest the International Court of Appeal confirmed that the stewards have made several mistakes in issuing a 10-second time penalty for an incident that happened outside of the last 12 laps of a race while exceeding the allowed time limit for the notification of a penalty having been issued.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>  As a result of their mistakes, the three stewards involved handed in their licenses at an extraordinary meeting of the FIA World Council.<sup id="cite_ref-england98-2_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-england98-2-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
