<p>The <b>2019 Monaco Grand Prix</b> (formally known as the <b>Formula 1 Grand Prix de Monaco 2019</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_racing" title="Formula One racing">Formula One motor race</a> held on 26 May 2019 at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Monaco" title="Circuit de Monaco">Circuit de Monaco</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Street_circuit" title="Street circuit">street circuit</a> that runs through the <a href="/wiki/Monaco" title="Monaco">Principality of Monaco</a>. It was the 6th round of the <a href="/wiki/2019_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2019 Formula One World Championship">2019 Formula One World Championship</a>, the 77th time that the <a href="/wiki/Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="Monaco Grand Prix">Monaco Grand Prix</a> was held, and the 66th time it had been held as a round of the Formula One World Championship since the inception of the series in <a href="/wiki/1950_Formula_One_season" title="1950 Formula One season">1950</a>.
</p><p>The race was won by <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> after starting from pole, with <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a> promoted to second and third place respectively after <a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a> received a penalty, demoting him to fourth place.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2019_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Championship_standings_before_the_race">Championship standings before the race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2019_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Championship standings before the race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Heading into the race <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> held a 7-point lead over teammate <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a> in the Drivers' Championship. In the Constructors' Championship, <a href="/wiki/Mercedes_in_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Mercedes in F1">Mercedes</a> held a 96-point advantage over <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Entrants">Entrants</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2019_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Entrants">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Further information: <a href="/wiki/2019_Formula_One_World_Championship#Entries" title="2019 Formula One World Championship">2019 Formula One World Championship §&#160;Entries</a></div>
<p>The drivers and teams were the same as the season entry list with no additional stand-in drivers for either the race or practice.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> This race saw <a href="/wiki/Alfa_Romeo_in_Formula_One" title="Alfa Romeo in Formula One">Alfa Romeo</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> celebrate his 300th Grand Prix entry.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2019_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>During the first qualifying session, <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a> was blocked on his first flying lap by <a href="/wiki/Antonio_Giovinazzi" title="Antonio Giovinazzi">Antonio Giovinazzi</a> at turn 17. Giovinazzi was later handed a three-place grid penalty. Around halfway through the session, after most drivers had come into the pits, <a href="/wiki/Charles_Leclerc" title="Charles Leclerc">Charles Leclerc</a> had set the sixth fastest time. However, <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> decided not to send him out again in order to save tyres. As other drivers began to improve their lap times, Leclerc fell down the order and was eventually eliminated from Q1 by teammate <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a>, the last driver to cross the line. Ferrari later admitted this was a team mistake.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Vettel's final lap was the fastest of the session.
</p><p>In the second qualifying session, <a href="/wiki/Pierre_Gasly" title="Pierre Gasly">Pierre Gasly</a> impeded <a href="/wiki/Romain_Grosjean" title="Romain Grosjean">Romain Grosjean</a>, and was later given a three-place grid penalty. <a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a> set the fastest time of the session.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> took pole position with a new track record ahead of teammate <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a>. As a result, <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> equalled the record for most front row lockouts, and Hamilton took the record for most pole positions with one constructor. <a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a> qualified in third, his highest qualifying position of the year.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2019_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Before the race started, there was a moment of silence to mark the death of three time world champion, <a href="/wiki/Niki_Lauda" title="Niki Lauda">Niki Lauda</a> who died on 20 May 2019.
</p><p>The opening lap proceeded cleanly, with <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> maintaining his lead from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>. <a href="/wiki/Charles_Leclerc" title="Charles Leclerc">Charles Leclerc</a> began to fight through the field after starting in 15th place. Leclerc had made it up to 12th by lap eight, but touched the inside wall at turn 17 during an overtake attempt on <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a>, sending his <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> into a spin. This contact caused a right-rear tyre puncture. Over the course of the next lap, Leclerc fell to last place as his tyre ripped apart and caused significant damage to the car's floor, spreading debris around the track. After emerging from the pits after a tyre change, he was a lap down on the leaders. A safety car was deployed on lap 11 for the marshals to clear the debris.
</p><p>The leaders made their pit stops at the end of the lap, during the safety car period. <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> entered the pits all within one car length of each other. Verstappen's pit stop was faster than Bottas's, and Verstappen was released alongside, but slightly ahead of Bottas, with the two cars making contact. Bottas had no choice but to yield at the pit exit, losing second place to Verstappen. Bottas was brought into the pits again on the following lap for a switch from medium to hard-compound tyres and for the team to check for damage, emerging in fourth place behind Vettel.
</p><p>Racing resumed on lap 15. On the same lap, <a href="/wiki/Antonio_Giovinazzi" title="Antonio Giovinazzi">Antonio Giovinazzi</a> attempted an overtake on <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a> at turn 17, making contact and sending the <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> into a spin. Kubica's car stopped whilst facing sideways at the exit of the turn, blocking the circuit and forcing Giovinazzi, Hülkenberg, Leclerc, <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a> and <a href="/wiki/George_Russell_(racing_driver)" title="George Russell (racing driver)">George Russell</a> to stop on track and wait for him to move aside so that they could pass Kubica. Leclerc came into the pits on the following lap for a switch to soft-compound tyres, but on his out-lap, Ferrari decided that the floor damage caused by his lap eight incident was too great. Leclerc returned to the pits and retired from the race, continuing his streak of failing to finish his home Grand Prix, after retiring from the <a href="/wiki/2018_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="2018 Monaco Grand Prix">2018 Monaco Grand Prix</a> and from both races at the <a href="/wiki/2017_Monaco_FIA_Formula_2_round" class="mw-redirect" title="2017 Monaco FIA Formula 2 round">2017 Monaco Formula 2 round</a>.
</p><p>On lap 22, Verstappen was issued a five-second time penalty for his unsafe release in the pits ten laps earlier. On lap 26, Giovinazzi was issued a penalty of ten seconds for causing the incident on lap 15. He later served his penalty in the pits. Verstappen spent the rest of the race attempting to overtake Hamilton, who was suffering on worn tyres, in order to build a lead and win the race despite his penalty. On lap 76, Verstappen mounted his only close challenge of the race, on the inside of turn 10. He made contact with the rear of the Mercedes, causing both cars to cut the chicane. Verstappen was unable to get close enough for an overtake on the subsequent laps, and Hamilton crossed the finish line to take victory. Verstappen's penalty saw him demoted from second to fourth, with Vettel and Bottas promoted to second and third respectively. This marked the sixth consecutive victory in 2019 for Mercedes, but the first time that season that the team did not take a 1-2 finish.
</p>
