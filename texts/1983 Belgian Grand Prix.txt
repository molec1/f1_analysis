<p>The <b>1983 Belgian Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held at <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Spa-Francorchamps" title="Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps">Spa-Francorchamps</a> on 22 May 1983. It was the first Belgian Grand Prix to be held at Spa since <a href="/wiki/1970_Belgian_Grand_Prix" title="1970 Belgian Grand Prix">1970</a> and the first on the modern Spa circuit, and was also the sixth race of the <a href="/wiki/1983_FIA_Formula_One_World_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="1983 FIA Formula One World Championship">1983 FIA Formula One World Championship</a>.
</p><p>The 40-lap race was won from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> by <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a>, driving a factory <a href="/wiki/Renault_RE40" title="Renault RE40">Renault</a>. <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Tambay" title="Patrick Tambay">Patrick Tambay</a> finished second in a <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_126C" title="Ferrari 126C">Ferrari</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Cheever" title="Eddie Cheever">Eddie Cheever</a> third in the other Renault. The race marked the debut of local driver and future race winner <a href="/wiki/Thierry_Boutsen" title="Thierry Boutsen">Thierry Boutsen</a>.
</p><p>The first attempt to start the race was waved off.  The field drove around the circuit and lined up again for the second attempt, which was successful.
</p>
