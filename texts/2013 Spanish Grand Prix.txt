<p>The <b>2013 Spanish Grand Prix</b> (formally known as the <b>2013 Formula 1 Gran Premio de España</b>)<sup id="cite_ref-formal_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-formal-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Auto_racing" title="Auto racing">motor race</a> that was held on 12 May 2013 at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Catalunya" class="mw-redirect" title="Circuit de Catalunya">Circuit de Catalunya</a> in <a href="/wiki/Barcelona" title="Barcelona">Barcelona</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_calender_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA_calender-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The race was the fifth round of the <a href="/wiki/2013_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2013 Formula One season">2013 season</a>, and marked the forty-third running of the <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Grand_Prix" title="Spanish Grand Prix">Spanish Grand Prix</a> as a round of the Formula One World Championship, and the twenty-third running at Catalunya.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> took pole position for the race, ahead of his teammate <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ROS_HAM_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ROS_HAM-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> took his thirty-second Grand Prix victory—and his second in his home race—ahead of <a href="/wiki/Lotus_F1" title="Lotus F1">Lotus</a>-<a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> teammate <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> in third. Pole sitter Nico Rosberg finished sixth overall. The most notable overtaking move came on the first lap as Fernando Alonso went round the outside of both Kimi Räikkönen and Lewis Hamilton going into turn 3.<sup id="cite_ref-results_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-results-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> This would prove to be Alonso's final win in Formula 1. Ferrari's strategy of making 4 pit stops was risky but proved to be the best choice as Alonso was able to beat Räikkönen who did one less.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> When the other teams tried to respond to Ferrari's pit stop strategy it was too late for them.
</p>
