<p>The <b>2007 Japanese Grand Prix</b> (officially the <b>XXXIII Fuji Television Japanese Grand Prix</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Auto_racing" title="Auto racing">motor race</a> held on 30 September 2007 at the <a href="/wiki/Fuji_Speedway" title="Fuji Speedway">Fuji Speedway</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oyama,_Shizuoka" title="Oyama, Shizuoka">Oyama, Shizuoka</a>. The 67-lap race was the fifteenth round of the <a href="/wiki/2007_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2007 Formula One season">2007 Formula One season</a>. It was held at the recently revised track in very heavy rain and misty conditions. For the previous 20 years, the <a href="/wiki/Japanese_Grand_Prix" title="Japanese Grand Prix">Japanese Grand Prix</a> had been held at <a href="/wiki/Suzuka_Circuit" class="mw-redirect" title="Suzuka Circuit">Suzuka Circuit</a>, but at this race it returned to Fuji for the first time since <a href="/wiki/1977_Japanese_Grand_Prix" title="1977 Japanese Grand Prix">1977</a>. The race was won by <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>, who also took pole position and the fastest lap of the race. <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> achieved his first podium, by finishing second for the <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a> team, whilst <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> finished in third for <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>. <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a> scored <a href="/wiki/Spyker_F1" title="Spyker F1">Spyker</a>'s first and only point by finishing 9th and being promoted to 8th after the race.
</p><p>As a consequence of the race, Hamilton extended his lead in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">World Drivers' Championship</a> to twelve points over McLaren teammate <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>. Alonso had only been two points behind Hamilton in the standings before he crashed during the race. Räikkönen's third-place finish ensured he remained in mathematical contention to clinch the drivers' title in the remaining two rounds. His Ferrari teammate, <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a>, dropped out of championship contention after finishing the race in sixth position. Massa was now ten points behind Räikkönen. In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">World Constructors' Championship</a>, Ferrari were leading the standings on 170 points. McLaren would have been leading the table on 202 points had they not been given a penalty in the Hungarian Grand Prix, and then disqualified entirely due to the <a href="/wiki/2007_Formula_One_espionage_controversy" title="2007 Formula One espionage controversy">espionage controversy that year</a>. <a href="/wiki/BMW_Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="BMW Sauber">BMW Sauber</a> remained second in the standings, 78 points behind Ferrari, after scoring just two points in the race. Renault ensured that they would finish the year in third, (like BMW Sauber on a total unassailable to any other team), and were 41 points behind.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2007_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2007_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Lewis Hamilton of <a href="/wiki/McLaren_(racing)" class="mw-redirect" title="McLaren (racing)">McLaren</a> led the Drivers' Championship by two points from his team-mate <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>, who was a further 11 points ahead of <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a>. After McLaren's <a href="/wiki/2007_Formula_One_espionage_controversy" title="2007 Formula One espionage controversy">exclusion</a> from the Constructors' Championship, Ferrari had been confirmed as Constructors' Champions, following their <a href="/wiki/2007_Belgian_Grand_Prix" title="2007 Belgian Grand Prix">Belgian Grand Prix</a> one-two. They were 71 points ahead of <a href="/wiki/BMW_Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="BMW Sauber">BMW Sauber</a>.
</p><p>Three <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japanese</a> constructors and two Japanese drivers competed in the race. <a href="/wiki/Toyota_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Toyota F1">Toyota</a> were 6th in the Constructors' Championship, whilst <a href="/wiki/Honda_Racing_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Honda Racing F1">Honda</a> were 8th, behind their own "B-team" <a href="/wiki/Super_Aguri_F1" title="Super Aguri F1">Super Aguri</a>. <a href="/wiki/Takuma_Sato" title="Takuma Sato">Takuma Sato</a> was 15th in the Drivers' Championship for Super Aguri, whilst compatriot <a href="/wiki/Sakon_Yamamoto" title="Sakon Yamamoto">Sakon Yamamoto</a> had yet to score for <a href="/wiki/Spyker_F1" title="Spyker F1">Spyker F1</a>, having debuted at the <a href="/wiki/2007_Hungarian_Grand_Prix" title="2007 Hungarian Grand Prix">Hungarian Grand Prix</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> changed his helmet design for this race, choosing to use the helmet design of former <a href="/wiki/World_Rally_Champion" class="mw-redirect" title="World Rally Champion">World Rally Champion</a> <a href="/wiki/Colin_McRae" title="Colin McRae">Colin McRae</a>, who had died two weeks before the race in a helicopter crash.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2007_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Takuma_Sato_2007_Japan.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f9/Takuma_Sato_2007_Japan.jpg/220px-Takuma_Sato_2007_Japan.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f9/Takuma_Sato_2007_Japan.jpg/330px-Takuma_Sato_2007_Japan.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f9/Takuma_Sato_2007_Japan.jpg/440px-Takuma_Sato_2007_Japan.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1837" data-file-height="1225" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Takuma_Sato_2007_Japan.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>National driver, <a href="/wiki/Takuma_Sato" title="Takuma Sato">Takuma Sato</a>, qualified in twenty-first position.</div></div></div>
<p>Although the weather had dried out by the time qualifying began, the track was still wet and all the drivers went out on wet tyres.
</p><p>Qualifying one saw the two <a href="/wiki/Spyker_F1" title="Spyker F1">Spykers</a> of <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sakon_Yamamoto" title="Sakon Yamamoto">Sakon Yamamoto</a> and the two <a href="/wiki/Super_Aguri_F1" title="Super Aguri F1">Super Aguris</a> of <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Davidson" title="Anthony Davidson">Anthony Davidson</a> and <a href="/wiki/Takuma_Sato" title="Takuma Sato">Takuma Sato</a> eliminated, along with <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Wurz" title="Alexander Wurz">Alexander Wurz</a> in the <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Honda_Racing_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Honda Racing F1">Honda</a>. <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Toyota_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Toyota F1">Toyota</a> also collided with Yamamoto, forcing both drivers out of qualifying.  Schumacher was already through to Q2, but could not set a time in the second session and so qualified 16th.
</p><p>Qualifying two saw the elimination of the two <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renaults</a> of <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> and <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a>, <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vitantonio_Liuzzi" title="Vitantonio Liuzzi">Vitantonio Liuzzi</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso</a> and the Toyotas of <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> as well as Schumacher.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> in the Toro Rosso and <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a> in the <a href="/wiki/BMW_Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="BMW Sauber">BMW Sauber</a> occupied row 5, just behind <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a>, getting his best qualifying of the season in the Honda, and <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a>'s Red Bull. <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> qualified 6th in the Williams, but was penalised ten places for an engine change. This meant that Vettel qualified eighth, the then best qualifying ever for Toro Rosso. <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> qualified 5th for BMW Sauber, and the top four was once again the two <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLarens</a> and two <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferraris</a>. The Ferraris failed to get on the front row, with <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> 3rd and 4th respectively. <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> then pipped his more experienced McLaren teammate <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> to earn the fifth pole position of his career.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2007_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Due to torrential rainfall, the race was started behind the <a href="/wiki/Safety_car" title="Safety car">safety car</a>, which led the field for the first 19 laps.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The Ferraris of Kimi Räikkönen and <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> were forced to pit during the first few laps after starting on standard wet tyres, as opposed to "extreme wet" tyres, which were better-suited to the treacherously wet conditions. Ferrari boss <a href="/wiki/Jean_Todt" title="Jean Todt">Jean Todt</a> later stated that the team were not informed about the requirements prior to the race, although all other teams were.<sup id="cite_ref-f1comrr_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrr-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> During the initial safety car period, the FIA instructed the lapped driver <a href="/wiki/Vitantonio_Liuzzi" title="Vitantonio Liuzzi">Vitantonio Liuzzi</a> to pass the field and catch up to the back of the queue as quickly as he safely could, as a way of gauging whether the track was ready for the race to begin in earnest.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Japan_2007_GP_Toyota.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e5/Japan_2007_GP_Toyota.jpg/220px-Japan_2007_GP_Toyota.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e5/Japan_2007_GP_Toyota.jpg/330px-Japan_2007_GP_Toyota.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e5/Japan_2007_GP_Toyota.jpg/440px-Japan_2007_GP_Toyota.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3264" data-file-height="2448" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Japan_2007_GP_Toyota.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> was the last car to actually finish, and classified thirteenth for the <a href="/wiki/Toyota_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Toyota F1">Toyota</a> team.</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Japan_2007_GP_Fuji_3.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/24/Japan_2007_GP_Fuji_3.jpg/220px-Japan_2007_GP_Fuji_3.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/24/Japan_2007_GP_Fuji_3.jpg/330px-Japan_2007_GP_Fuji_3.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/24/Japan_2007_GP_Fuji_3.jpg/440px-Japan_2007_GP_Fuji_3.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3264" data-file-height="2448" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Japan_2007_GP_Fuji_3.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A view of the wet conditions, from the grandstand, during the race.</div></div></div>
<p>The Ferraris dropped back to 20th and 21st positions respectively following the pit stops, and Massa pitted again on lap 15. When the safety car finally left the circuit, the two McLarens began the race proper at the head of the field, in front of the quick-starting <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso</a> driver had taken third place from <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a>, after the latter and <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a>'s Honda collided. <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> reached fourth place during the first racing lap as a result of the collision,<sup id="cite_ref-f1comrr_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrr-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> despite still suffering from the after-effects of food poisoning, having earlier vomited into his helmet during the first <a href="/wiki/Safety_car" title="Safety car">safety car</a> period.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> A first-corner spin caused <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Wurz" title="Alexander Wurz">Alexander Wurz</a> to collide heavily with <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a>'s Ferrari. Wurz's car sustained heavy damage and he retired on the spot.
</p><p>Later in the race, Fernando Alonso crashed out. The reigning world champion blamed aquaplaning for the accident, which once again brought out the safety car. It was the first time that a McLaren car had failed to finish during the 2007 season. During this safety car period on lap 45, third-placed Sebastian Vettel crashed into the rear of second-placed Webber in the wet conditions, Webber retiring immediately and Vettel returning to the pits to retire. Vettel had earlier became the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_driver_records#For_at_least_one_lap,_youngest_leaders" title="List of Formula One driver records">youngest ever driver to lead a lap of a Formula One race</a> (aged 20 years and 89 days) – his record was subsequently broken by <a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a> (aged 18 years and 228 days) at the <a href="/wiki/2016_Spanish_Grand_Prix" title="2016 Spanish Grand Prix">2016 Spanish Grand Prix</a>. This collision put <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a> in second place, which he managed to hold until the end of the race, despite repeated overtaking attempts by Kimi Räikkönen.  It was Kovalainen's first podium finish. Räikkönen would eventually finish third, ahead of <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> in fourth for <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a>.  This was the first time that two Finnish drivers had both finished on the podium.<sup id="cite_ref-racepress_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-racepress-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On the final lap, <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> prevailed in his battle with BMW driver <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Poland" title="Poland">Pole</a> had previously served a drive through penalty for colliding with Hamilton. Massa finished sixth, and Kubica seventh, behind <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a>'s Renault. <a href="/wiki/Vitantonio_Liuzzi" title="Vitantonio Liuzzi">Vitantonio Liuzzi</a> finished in eighth, scoring the first 2007 championship points for Toro Rosso.<sup id="cite_ref-f1comrr_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1comrr-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> However, these points were later removed when Liuzzi was penalised for overtaking Adrian Sutil under waved yellow flags. This promoted Sutil to eighth, giving him and the Spyker team their first and only F1 points.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2007_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Following the race, <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> received a 10-place penalty for the <a href="/wiki/2007_Chinese_Grand_Prix" title="2007 Chinese Grand Prix">next race</a> in <a href="/wiki/Shanghai" title="Shanghai">Shanghai</a>, <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China" class="mw-redirect" title="People&#39;s Republic of China">China</a>, for causing the collision with <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> which put both drivers out of the race, but his punishment was later reduced to a reprimand when new evidence was presented to the FIA, prompting it to begin an investigation of <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> for dangerous driving and causing the collision himself. Hamilton was leading the race right in front of Webber, before suddenly braking and forcing Webber to follow suit lest he be penalised for overtaking behind the safety car; the collision occurred when Vettel's car ran into the back of Webber. Hamilton might have faced either disqualification or a grid penalty for the next race,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> but the FIA decided not to impose any penalty.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Problems_with_the_circuit">Problems with the circuit</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2007_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Problems with the circuit">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The event was afflicted by poor transportation, poor facilities (including some reserved seats without a view), a lack of organization, and expensive meals that meant a simple lunch-box was sold for 10,000 <a href="/wiki/Japanese_yen" title="Japanese yen">yen</a> (<a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">US$</a>87) at the circuit.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">Transportation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2007_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Transportation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The free practice session on Saturday was abandoned after repeated delays due to <a href="/wiki/Fog" title="Fog">fog</a>, which grounded the medical helicopter.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Only three drivers set a time in four minutes: <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Wurz" title="Alexander Wurz">Alexander Wurz</a> (Williams-Toyota), <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> (Williams-Toyota) and <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> (Toyota).<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Before the race, there were suggestions that the plan of carrying all of the 100,000 or more spectators only by shuttle bus would be impossible. To relieve people's doubts, the circuit announced that there was complete preparation for the race.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> These doubts were realised when, after the qualifying session on Saturday, the shuttle buses could not leave because the road on the east gate of the circuit sank around 16:00. As a result, approximately 20,000 spectators were forced to stay for four hours or more until 21:00.<sup id="cite_ref-Sawadareview070929_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sawadareview070929-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> For the race on Sunday, only the shuttle bus was admitted for spectators as transport.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Refund">Refund</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2007_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Refund">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Japan_2007_GP_Fuji_4.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/90/Japan_2007_GP_Fuji_4.jpg/220px-Japan_2007_GP_Fuji_4.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/90/Japan_2007_GP_Fuji_4.jpg/330px-Japan_2007_GP_Fuji_4.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/90/Japan_2007_GP_Fuji_4.jpg/440px-Japan_2007_GP_Fuji_4.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3264" data-file-height="2448" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Japan_2007_GP_Fuji_4.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>View from controversial 'C' stand.</div></div></div>
<p>On Sunday morning, the circuit announced that they would refund spectators who had bought seats in the reserved seat area on 'C' stand, near the first corner. This was due to the low visibility which meant that the cars on the racing line were not seen at all.<sup id="cite_ref-Sawadareview070930_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sawadareview070930-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> The cost of the tickets, 50,000 <a href="/wiki/Japanese_yen" title="Japanese yen">yen</a> (<a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">US$</a>435), was scheduled to be reimbursed to 7,000 people who were in the stand. The total cost of the refund was expected to be around 350 million <a href="/wiki/Japanese_yen" title="Japanese yen">yen</a> (<a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">US$</a>3 million).<sup id="cite_ref-350_million_yen_will_be_reimbursed_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-350_million_yen_will_be_reimbursed-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> After the race on Sunday, the circuit held a press conference to apologise for problems and announced that they would refund another 85 spectators who did not arrive at the circuit until after the start of the race due to the delay of the shuttle bus. The circuit said that they would reimburse them for all the costs caused by watching the race: the transportation expenses, the hotel charges and the cost of the tickets.<sup id="cite_ref-350_million_yen_will_be_reimbursed_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-350_million_yen_will_be_reimbursed-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Toyota_bias">Toyota bias</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2007_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Toyota bias">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Fuji Speedway prohibited spectators from setting up flags and banners supporting teams and drivers,<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> with the exception of the <a href="/wiki/Toyota_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Toyota F1">Toyota F1</a> team whose owner also owned the circuit.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Therefore, there were very few flags and banners in the event compared with other Grand Prix events.<sup id="cite_ref-Sawadareview070930_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sawadareview070930-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> After the event, Japanese media figures such as <a href="/wiki/Fuji_Television" class="mw-redirect" title="Fuji Television">Fuji TV</a> F1 commentator Jun Imamiya, and Fuji TV F1 commentator and former mechanic for <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton Formula</a> Tetsuo Tsugawa criticised the organization of the circuit. Tsugawa mentioned:
</p>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r960796168">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>…Although I have worked in Formula One for thirty years, this is the first time I have seen a Grand Prix race without seeing fans wave the flag of <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>.  I think this is inexcusable.  What I heard was Fuji Speedway prohibited flags and banners of F1 teams, but I saw a Toyota F1 flag in the stands of the Toyota-owned circuit.  <br />  The track later said the flags were not prohibited, but there was a miscommunication between the race organisers and the staff of the circuit.  However, many fans familiar with Formula One noticed the strangeness of the circuit without the team banners.  Why was this not solved on initial notice?<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>
<p>However, the event was well received by visitors in the paddock.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Despite the problems and criticism, the team principal of <a href="/wiki/Toyota_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Toyota F1">Toyota F1</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tadashi_Yamashina" title="Tadashi Yamashina">Tadashi Yamashina</a> praised the event:
</p>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r960796168"/><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>…I would like to say congratulations to Fuji Speedway for hosting this race for the first time in 30 years. The facilities here are excellent and the race was well organised.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>
<p>In 2009, the Japanese Grand Prix was held again at Suzuka, with the plan to alternate between the two circuits in subsequent years. It was suggested that this could help to prevent future shows of such flagrant parochialism.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> However, Toyota later announced that the Japanese Grand Prix would not be held at Fuji Speedway any more, and the <a href="/wiki/2008_Japanese_Grand_Prix" title="2008 Japanese Grand Prix">2008 Japanese Grand Prix</a> was the last held at the circuit.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lawsuit">Lawsuit</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2007_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Lawsuit">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 16 June 2008, 109 spectators went to <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_District_Court" title="Tokyo District Court">Tokyo District Court</a> and took an action against the circuit to claim total 32 million yen (<a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">US$</a>295,500) in compensation for bad health and missing the race due to the problems with the shuttle buses.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The date of the event predicted near this tag has passed. (January 2018)">needs update</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Classification">Classification</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2007_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Classification">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
