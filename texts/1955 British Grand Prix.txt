<p>The <b>1955 British Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held at <a href="/wiki/Aintree_Motor_Racing_Circuit" title="Aintree Motor Racing Circuit">Aintree</a> on 16 July 1955. It was race 6 of 7 in the <a href="/wiki/1955_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1955 World Championship of Drivers">1955 World Championship of Drivers</a>. British driver <a href="/wiki/Stirling_Moss" title="Stirling Moss">Stirling Moss</a> led a Mercedes 1–2–3–4 domination of the race, to win his first Formula One race narrowly ahead of his illustrious Argentine teammate <a href="/wiki/Juan_Manuel_Fangio" title="Juan Manuel Fangio">Juan Manuel Fangio</a>. Several people, including Moss, believed that the Argentine allowed his British protegé to claim his debut win in front of his home crowd. This was, however, at Moss' inquiry, consistently denied by Fangio, who claimed that Moss "was simply faster that day."
</p><p>Subsequent to the race, the German, Swiss and Spanish Grands Prix were cancelled, in the wake of the <a href="/wiki/1955_Le_Mans_disaster" title="1955 Le Mans disaster">Le Mans disaster</a>. With only one Championship round therefore remaining (the <a href="/wiki/1955_Italian_Grand_Prix" title="1955 Italian Grand Prix">Italian Grand Prix</a> some 2 months later), Fangio's points advantage over Moss was sufficient to secure his third World Drivers' Championship.
</p>
