<p>The <b>2019 French Grand Prix</b> (formally known as the <b>Formula 1 Pirelli Grand Prix de France 2019</b>)<sup id="cite_ref-formal_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-formal-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Auto_racing" title="Auto racing">motor race</a> on 23 June 2019 at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_Paul_Ricard" title="Circuit Paul Ricard">Circuit Paul Ricard</a> in <a href="/wiki/Le_Castellet,_Var" title="Le Castellet, Var">Le Castellet</a>, France.<sup id="cite_ref-formal_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-formal-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The race was the 8th round of the <a href="/wiki/2019_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2019 Formula One World Championship">2019 FIA Formula One World Championship</a>. It was the 88th running of the <a href="/wiki/French_Grand_Prix" title="French Grand Prix">French Grand Prix</a>, and the 60th time the event had been included as a round of the <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_World_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="Formula One World Championship">Formula One World Championship</a> since the inception of the series in <a href="/wiki/1950_Formula_One_season" title="1950 Formula One season">1950</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The race was won by <a href="/wiki/Mercedes_in_Formula_One" class="mw-redirect" title="Mercedes in Formula One">Mercedes</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> ahead of teammate <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Charles_Leclerc" title="Charles Leclerc">Charles Leclerc</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2019_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Entrants">Entrants</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2019_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Entrants">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Further information: <a href="/wiki/2019_Formula_One_World_Championship#Entries" title="2019 Formula One World Championship">2019 Formula One World Championship §&#160;Entries</a></div>
<p>The drivers and teams were the same as the season entry list with no additional stand-in drivers for the race. However, <a href="/wiki/Nicholas_Latifi" title="Nicholas Latifi">Nicholas Latifi</a> drove in the first practice session for <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Championship_standings_before_the_race">Championship standings before the race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2019_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Championship standings before the race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Before the race <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> had 162 points and held a 29-point lead over teammate <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> third with 100 points, followed by <a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a> with 88 points, and then by Vettel's teammate <a href="/wiki/Charles_Leclerc" title="Charles Leclerc">Charles Leclerc</a> in fifth place with 72 points.
</p><p>In the Constructors' Championship standings, <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> led <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> with 295 points and had a 123-point lead having won all the previous races in the season so far. <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a> was third with 124 points and <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> was fourth with 30 points, just 2 points ahead of <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a> with 28 points.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Penalties">Penalties</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2019_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Penalties">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Daniil_Kvyat" title="Daniil Kvyat">Daniil Kvyat</a> was required to start from the back of the grid as he exceeded his quota of power unit components, using a 4th Internal Combustion Unit (ICE), 3rd Motor Generator Unit-Kinetic (MGU-K), 3rd Energy Store (ES), 3rd Control Electronics (CE), 4th Turbocharger (TC) and 4th Motor Generator Unit-Heat (MGU-H). <a href="/wiki/George_Russell_(racing_driver)" title="George Russell (racing driver)">George Russell</a> was required to start from the back of the grid as he exceeded his quota of power unit components, using a 3rd Energy Store (ES) and 3rd Control Electronics (CE).
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Circuit_changes">Circuit changes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2019_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Circuit changes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Following the <a href="/wiki/2018_French_Grand_Prix" title="2018 French Grand Prix">2018 race</a>, teams and drivers expressed concerns about the pit lane entry which saw cars enter the lane aimed directly at the <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> garage. In response the FIA announced that the pit lane entry would be moved to somewhere between turns 14 and 15 and also announced that the track would be resurfaced.
</p><p>The FIA also announced that they had moved to clamp down any potential corner cutting ahead of the French Grand Prix weekend at the Circuit Paul Ricard. The nature of the track, which had prominently been used as a test venue in preceding years, meant that there was a lot of runoff for the drivers if they made a mistake. However, there had been bollards and signs placed around the circuit in order to stop the drivers taking advantage of the runoff area, ensuring they would lose time and rejoin the circuit safely. At turn 2, two yellow bollards had appeared along with some red and white signage to indicate where a driver should rejoin the circuit. The event notes state: "Any driver who fails to negotiate Turn 2 by using the track, and who passes completely to the right of the first fluorescent yellow bollard on the apex of the corner, must keep completely to the right of the fluorescent yellow bollard and re-join the track by driving through the two arrays of blocks in the run-off by passing to the right of the first and to the left of the second." A similar system had been enforced further on in the first sector, at turns 3, 4, and 5.
</p><p>The rules would not strictly apply to free practice sessions, and each case was supposed to be handled and analysed differently depending on the circumstance.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2019)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Free_practice">Free practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2019_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Free practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> set the fastest time in FP1 followed by <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> drivers were third and fourth, with <a href="/wiki/Charles_Leclerc" title="Charles Leclerc">Charles Leclerc</a> ahead of <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a>.
</p><p>In FP2 it was another <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> 1–2 with Bottas leading Hamilton, and then Leclerc leading Vettel in 3–4. Early in the session Hamilton went off the track and joined unsafely, forcing <a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a> off the track when he rejoined. Hamilton was later summoned by the stewards but he received no penalty.

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2019_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_summary">Race summary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2019_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Race summary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The race began cleanly, with <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Sainz_Jr." title="Carlos Sainz Jr.">Carlos Sainz Jr.</a> unsuccessfully attempting to overtake <a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a> and the two <a href="/wiki/Mercedes_in_Formula_One" class="mw-redirect" title="Mercedes in Formula One">Mercedes</a> cars maintaining their lead. On lap 6, <a href="/wiki/George_Russell_(racing_driver)" title="George Russell (racing driver)">George Russell</a> attempted an overtake on teammate <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a> around the outside of turn 10, but was pushed off-track, destroying a polystyrene distance marker. By the following lap, <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> had overtaken both <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLarens</a> to take fifth place.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Antonio_Giovinazzi" title="Antonio Giovinazzi">Antonio Giovinazzi</a> was the first driver to pit, on lap 8, after struggling with degrading soft-compound tyres. On lap 10, <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a> was handed a 5-second time penalty for leaving the track and gaining an advantage on lap 1. The decision was controversial, since Pérez had correctly passed around the penalty bollard after cutting turn 4 as specified by the race director, but had still overtaken <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Albon" title="Alexander Albon">Alexander Albon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Magnussen" title="Kevin Magnussen">Kevin Magnussen</a> to claim 13th place as a result.
</p><p>Verstappen was the first of the leading pack to pit, on lap 21. <a href="/wiki/Charles_Leclerc" title="Charles Leclerc">Charles Leclerc</a> pitted on the following lap, as did <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a> on lap 24, and <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> from the lead on lap 25. Vettel, who was yet to pit, had been far enough behind Hamilton that the Mercedes driver maintained the lead of the race. Vettel pitted on the following lap, emerging behind Verstappen and restoring the order of the top five. <a href="/wiki/Lance_Stroll" title="Lance Stroll">Lance Stroll</a> was the final driver to make their first pit-stop; he did so on lap 40.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Romain_Grosjean" title="Romain Grosjean">Romain Grosjean</a> became the first and only retirement of the Grand Prix on lap 45 with an unspecified problem. A <a href="/wiki/Safety_car#Virtual_safety_car_(VSC)" title="Safety car">virtual safety car</a> was briefly implemented on lap 50 after a bollard had rolled onto the track. Vettel pitted for soft-compound tyres on the penultimate in an attempt to claim the fastest lap point, which he did on the final lap of the race.
</p><p>On the final lap, <a href="/wiki/Lando_Norris" title="Lando Norris">Lando Norris</a>, who had been suffering from hydraulic problems, was passed by <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> before turn 8. Ricciardo ran deep into the corner, and forced Norris off the track whilst rejoining it in turn 9. This allowed <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a> through, with Räikkönen also getting past Ricciardo. Ricciardo then went off-track on the following straight to overtake the <a href="/wiki/Alfa_Romeo_in_Formula_One" title="Alfa Romeo in Formula One">Alfa Romeo</a> for seventh place. Ricciardo was later handed two 5-second penalties after the race, one for rejoining the track unsafely, and one for leaving the track and gaining an advantage. This demoted him to 11th place, outside of the points. The stewards also handed him two penalty points on his Super Licence for "Unsafe rejoining of track" and one point for "Gaining advantage off track".<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> won the race from pole position ahead of teammate <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Charles_Leclerc" title="Charles Leclerc">Charles Leclerc</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
