<p>The <b>1986 Italian Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held at <a href="/wiki/Autodromo_Nazionale_di_Monza" title="Autodromo Nazionale di Monza">Monza</a> on 7 September 1986. The race, contested over 51 laps, was the thirteenth race of the <a href="/wiki/1986_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="1986 Formula One season">1986 Formula One season</a> and was won by <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW11" title="Williams FW11">Williams</a>-<a href="/wiki/Honda_in_Formula_One" title="Honda in Formula One">Honda</a>, with teammate and Drivers' Championship rival <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> second and <a href="/wiki/Stefan_Johansson" title="Stefan Johansson">Stefan Johansson</a> third in a <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_F1/86" title="Ferrari F1/86">Ferrari</a>. With fellow championship rivals <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> both failing to score, Piquet moved into second in the championship, five points behind Mansell.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1986_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>During the second qualifying session at Monza, the <a href="/wiki/Benetton_B186" title="Benetton B186">Benetton</a>-<a href="/wiki/BMW_M12" title="BMW M12">BMW</a> of <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> was recorded as being the fastest car along the start-finish straight, with a top speed of 351.22&#160;km/h (218.24&#160;mph). He was followed by four more BMW-powered cars: teammate <a href="/wiki/Teo_Fabi" title="Teo Fabi">Teo Fabi</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Brabham_BT55" title="Brabham BT55">Brabhams</a> of <a href="/wiki/Derek_Warwick" title="Derek Warwick">Derek Warwick</a> and <a href="/wiki/Riccardo_Patrese" title="Riccardo Patrese">Riccardo Patrese</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Arrows_A8" title="Arrows A8">Arrows</a> of <a href="/wiki/Thierry_Boutsen" title="Thierry Boutsen">Thierry Boutsen</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-gurneyflap_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gurneyflap-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The fastest non-BMW powered car was the <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW11" title="Williams FW11">Williams</a>-<a href="/wiki/Honda_in_Formula_One" title="Honda in Formula One">Honda</a> of <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a>, with a top speed 10&#160;km/h (6&#160;mph) slower than that of Berger.
</p><p>Despite this, Berger could only qualify fourth, while Fabi took his second consecutive pole position. <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> lined up alongside Fabi on the front row, despite being nearly half a second slower in his <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/2" title="McLaren MP4/2">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Techniques_d%27Avant_Garde" title="Techniques d&#39;Avant Garde">TAG</a> and recording a top speed over 16&#160;km/h (10&#160;mph) slower than Berger's.<sup id="cite_ref-gurneyflap_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gurneyflap-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Drivers' Championship leader Mansell was third, ahead of Berger, while Brazilian pair <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> (<a href="/wiki/Lotus_98T" title="Lotus 98T">Lotus</a>-<a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a> (Williams-Honda) made up the third row. The top ten was completed by Warwick, <a href="/wiki/Keke_Rosberg" title="Keke Rosberg">Keke Rosberg</a> in the second McLaren, <a href="/wiki/Michele_Alboreto" title="Michele Alboreto">Michele Alboreto</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_F1/86" title="Ferrari F1/86">Ferrari</a> and Patrese; Alboreto's position was notable as he had missed the first day of qualifying after injuring his arm in a motorcycle crash.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The race saw the debut of local driver <a href="/wiki/Alex_Caffi" title="Alex Caffi">Alex Caffi</a>, deputising for <a href="/wiki/Allen_Berg" title="Allen Berg">Allen Berg</a> at <a href="/wiki/Osella" title="Osella">Osella</a>, as well as the debut of the French <a href="/wiki/Automobiles_Gonfaronnaises_Sportives" title="Automobiles Gonfaronnaises Sportives">AGS</a> team, whose <a href="/wiki/Motori_Moderni" title="Motori Moderni">Motori Moderni</a>-powered <a href="/wiki/AGS_JH21C" title="AGS JH21C">JH21C</a> was driven by another local driver, <a href="/wiki/Ivan_Capelli" title="Ivan Capelli">Ivan Capelli</a>. With the number of entries increased to 27 at both this race and the next race in <a href="/wiki/1986_Portuguese_Grand_Prix" title="1986 Portuguese Grand Prix">Portugal</a>, <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_du_Sport_Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale du Sport Automobile">FISA</a> decided to allow all the cars to start. Caffi was the beneficiary of this decision, starting 27th behind Capelli and Osella teammate <a href="/wiki/Piercarlo_Ghinzani" title="Piercarlo Ghinzani">Piercarlo Ghinzani</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1986_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Following problems at the start of the parade lap, pole position man Teo Fabi was forced to start from the back of the grid and Alain Prost, alongside him on the front row, had to start from the pit lane in the spare car. At the green light, Gerhard Berger took the lead, but on lap 8 lost positions to first Mansell, Piquet and an on-form Alboreto in the Ferrari. Ayrton Senna was out with a broken gearbox at the start. Alboreto looked to be in challenging the Williams duo for the lead having overtaken Rosberg, Arnoux and Berger before spinning at the exit of the first chicane. Like the British Grand Prix, the race became a close fight between the two Williams drivers, but this time Piquet hunted down his teammate British driver Nigel Mansell to take victory. Piquet defeated Mansell in a straight fight, leading the British home by 9.828 seconds. The Brazilian managed to pass Mansell at the Curva Grande to go on and claim his fourth win of the season. Behind, Fabi and Prost had charged from the rear and by lap 12 were running 8th and 9th. Prost was disqualified for changing cars after the start of the parade lap- which was illegal, but his engine blew up a lap after he was flagged anyway. Johansson charged early in the race, passing Rosberg and Arnoux on lap 5 to go on to finish third.
</p>
