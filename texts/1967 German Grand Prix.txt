<p>The <b>1967 German Grand Prix</b> was a motor race for both <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a>  and  <a href="/wiki/Formula_Two" title="Formula Two">Formula Two</a> cars held at the <a href="/wiki/N%C3%BCrburgring" title="Nürburgring">Nürburgring</a> on August 6, 1967. It was race 7 of 11 in both the <a href="/wiki/1967_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1967 World Championship of Drivers">1967 World Championship of Drivers</a> and the <a href="/wiki/1967_International_Cup_for_Formula_One_Manufacturers" class="mw-redirect" title="1967 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers">1967 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers</a>. The 15-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Brabham" title="Brabham">Brabham</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Denny_Hulme" title="Denny Hulme">Denny Hulme</a> after he started from second position. His teammate <a href="/wiki/Jack_Brabham" title="Jack Brabham">Jack Brabham</a> finished second and <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Chris_Amon" title="Chris Amon">Chris Amon</a> came in third.
</p><p>There had been some changes to the track in an attempt to slow the cars down as they approached the pit area. However, it was clear that the cars had developed considerably over 12 months, so the changes had very little effect on the lap times.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix.com_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix.com-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1967_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Entry">Entry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1967_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Entry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A total of 16 <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">F1</a> cars were entered for the event. As with the 1966 event, there were a field of 10 <a href="/wiki/Formula_Two" title="Formula Two">Formula Two</a> cars. Amongst these <a href="/wiki/Formula_Two" title="Formula Two">F2</a> cars number of stars of tomorrow including <a href="/wiki/Jacky_Ickx" title="Jacky Ickx">Jacky Ickx</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jo_Schlesser" title="Jo Schlesser">Jo Schlesser</a> in their <a href="/wiki/Equipe_Matra_Sports" title="Equipe Matra Sports">Matras</a>. Among the field were two wooden-chassis <a href="/wiki/Protos_(constructor)" title="Protos (constructor)">Protos</a>. Apart from the <a href="/wiki/Formula_Two" title="Formula Two">F2</a> entries, the field was much as usual except for a second <a href="/wiki/Lola_Cars" title="Lola Cars">Lola</a>-<a href="/wiki/BMW_in_Formula_One" title="BMW in Formula One">BMW</a> for <a href="/wiki/Hubert_Hahne" title="Hubert Hahne">Hubert Hahne</a>. As this had a 2-litre engine, it was entered as a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">F1</a> car.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix.com_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix.com-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1967_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Jim_Clark" title="Jim Clark">Jim Clark</a> took <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> for <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Team Lotus</a>, in their <a href="/wiki/Cosworth_DFV" title="Cosworth DFV">Cosworth DFV</a> powered <a href="/wiki/Lotus_49" title="Lotus 49">Lotus 49</a>, averaging a speed of 105.598&#160;mph, around 14.189 mile circuit. <a href="/wiki/Jim_Clark" title="Jim Clark">Clark</a> was nearly 10 seconds faster than the next driver, <a href="/wiki/Denny_Hulme" title="Denny Hulme">Denny Hulme</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Brabham_BT24" title="Brabham BT24">Brabham-Repco BT24</a>. Third fastest was set by Ickx in his F2 <a href="/wiki/Equipe_Matra_Sports" title="Equipe Matra Sports">Matra</a>. As this was a Formula Two car, Ickx would have to start behind the main grid. Therefore, alongside Clark and Hulme on the four car front row was the <a href="/wiki/BRM_P115" title="BRM P115">BRM P115</a> of <a href="/wiki/Jackie_Stewart" title="Jackie Stewart">Jackie Stewart</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dan_Gurney" title="Dan Gurney">Dan Gurney’s</a> <a href="/wiki/Eagle_Mk1" title="Eagle Mk1">Eagle-Weslake T1G</a>.  The second <a href="/wiki/Eagle_Mk1" title="Eagle Mk1">Eagle</a> of <a href="/wiki/Bruce_McLaren" title="Bruce McLaren">McLaren</a> headed up the second row, where he was joined by <a href="/wiki/John_Surtees" title="John Surtees">John Surtees</a> in his <a href="/wiki/Honda_RA273" title="Honda RA273">Honda RA273</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jack_Brabham" title="Jack Brabham">Jack Brabham</a> in his <a href="/wiki/Brabham_BT24" title="Brabham BT24">Brabham-Repco BT24</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix.com_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix.com-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1967_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Clark converted his <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> into an early lead, while his <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Team Lotus</a> team-mate <a href="/wiki/Graham_Hill" title="Graham Hill">Graham Hill</a> was pushed from his grid position of 13th, onto some grass, restarting the back of the field, behind the F2 cars. Clark stayed ahead Hulme and Gurney for the first three laps of the race. On the fourth lap, Clark dramatically slowed, his <a href="/wiki/Suspension_(vehicle)" class="mw-redirect" title="Suspension (vehicle)">suspension</a> having buckled, and so ended his race. Hill managed his <a href="/wiki/Lotus_49" title="Lotus 49">Lotus</a> up to tenth before mechanical troubles eventually put him out of the race.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix.com_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix.com-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Immediately Gurney passed Hulme for the lead, while Brabham was third after McLaren retired with a split oil pipe. Ickx continued to impress. He was now up to fifth, behind Stewart. The Scotsman overtook Brabham, only to encounter transmission problems, and so Ickx moved up to fourth. Shortly after this, the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> of <a href="/wiki/Chris_Amon" title="Chris Amon">Chris Amon</a> closed up and passed the F2 <a href="/wiki/Equipe_Matra_Sports" title="Equipe Matra Sports">Matra</a>. By lap 12, Ickx was also out of the race, following the collapse of this front <a href="/wiki/Suspension_(vehicle)" class="mw-redirect" title="Suspension (vehicle)">suspension</a>. On the next lap, the <a href="/wiki/Universal_joint" title="Universal joint">universal joint</a> on a <a href="/wiki/Drive_shaft" title="Drive shaft">driveshaft</a> broke for the race leader, Gurney. Hulme took the lead to win from his team-mate Brabham and fellow <a href="/wiki/Kiwi_(people)" title="Kiwi (people)">Kiwi</a>, Amon.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix.com_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix.com-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1967_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Lap leaders:
<ul><li>Jim Clark (1-3)</li>
<li>Dan Gurney (4-12)</li>
<li>Denny Hulme (13-15)</li></ul></li>
<li>This was the first time F1 was run on the Nürburgring with the Hohenrain chicane installed to slow the cars into the pits and the pit straight.</li>
<li>This race was the first in F1 history to be broadcast for colour television in Germany.</li>
<li>First Championship race since 1962 Monaco GP without a UK driver on the podium.</li>
<li>First (and so far only) World Championship race to feature an all-<a href="/wiki/Australasia" title="Australasia">Australasian</a> podium.</li></ul>
