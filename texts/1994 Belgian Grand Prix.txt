<p>The <b>1994 Belgian Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held on 28 August 1994 at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Spa-Francorchamps" title="Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps">Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spa,_Belgium" title="Spa, Belgium">Spa</a>. It was the eleventh race of the <a href="/wiki/1994_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1994 Formula One World Championship">1994 Formula One World Championship</a>.
</p><p>The 44-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW16" title="Williams FW16">Williams</a>-<a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>. After <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> took the first <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a> team, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> took the chequered flag in his <a href="/wiki/Benetton_B194" title="Benetton B194">Benetton</a>-<a href="/wiki/Ford_Motor_Company" title="Ford Motor Company">Ford</a>, only to be disqualified due to excessive wear on the wooden <a href="/wiki/Skid_block" title="Skid block">skid block</a> underneath his car. Hill was thus awarded the win, his third of the season, with <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> second in a <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/9" title="McLaren MP4/9">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Peugeot" title="Peugeot">Peugeot</a> and Schumacher's teammate <a href="/wiki/Jos_Verstappen" title="Jos Verstappen">Jos Verstappen</a> third.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1994_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1994_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Having deputised for a suspended <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> at <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> at the previous race in <a href="/wiki/1994_Hungarian_Grand_Prix" title="1994 Hungarian Grand Prix">Hungary</a>, <a href="/wiki/Philippe_Alliot" title="Philippe Alliot">Philippe Alliot</a> now returned to his old <a href="/wiki/Larrousse" title="Larrousse">Larrousse</a> team for this race, taking the place of <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Beretta" title="Olivier Beretta">Olivier Beretta</a>. Meanwhile, the cash-strapped <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Lotus</a> replaced <a href="/wiki/Alex_Zanardi" title="Alex Zanardi">Alessandro Zanardi</a> with local driver <a href="/wiki/Philippe_Adams" title="Philippe Adams">Philippe Adams</a>, who brought significant funding to the team.
</p><p>Following the deaths of <a href="/wiki/Roland_Ratzenberger" title="Roland Ratzenberger">Roland Ratzenberger</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> at <a href="/wiki/1994_San_Marino_Grand_Prix" title="1994 San Marino Grand Prix">Imola</a> earlier in the season, a chicane was installed at Eau Rouge to slow the cars.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1994_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Friday qualifying session was held in wet but drying conditions. Towards the end of the session, the <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a> cars were sent out on slick tyres, and <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> duly put his car on provisional pole with teammate <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a> fourth. More rain fell on Saturday, with most of the drivers lapping several seconds slower than the day before, and only <a href="/wiki/Christian_Fittipaldi" title="Christian Fittipaldi">Christian Fittipaldi</a> improving his time. Thus, Barrichello took the first pole position of his career and the first for Jordan.
</p><p>Drivers' Championship leader <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> was second in his <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a> third in his <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a>. After Irvine came <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jos_Verstappen" title="Jos Verstappen">Jos Verstappen</a> in the second Benetton, <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> in the second Williams and the returning Häkkinen, with <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pierluigi_Martini" title="Pierluigi Martini">Pierluigi Martini</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a> completing the top ten.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1994_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Philippe_Adams%27_Lotus_109.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/95/Philippe_Adams%27_Lotus_109.jpg/220px-Philippe_Adams%27_Lotus_109.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="145" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/95/Philippe_Adams%27_Lotus_109.jpg/330px-Philippe_Adams%27_Lotus_109.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/95/Philippe_Adams%27_Lotus_109.jpg/440px-Philippe_Adams%27_Lotus_109.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3570" data-file-height="2358" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Philippe_Adams%27_Lotus_109.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Local driver <a href="/wiki/Philippe_Adams" title="Philippe Adams">Philippe Adams</a> spun his <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Lotus</a> out of the race on lap 16.</div></div></div>
<p>In dry conditions, Barrichello led away from Schumacher and a fast-starting Alesi. Schumacher passed Barrichello at Les Combes, and Alesi soon followed, only for his engine to fail on lap 3. With the Jordans struggling to hold on to track position against faster cars, Hill moved into second, ahead of Coulthard and Häkkinen. <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> in the remaining <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> also retired with engine failure by lap 12. <a href="/wiki/Philippe_Adams" title="Philippe Adams">Philippe Adams</a> who made his debut Grand Prix appearance at his home circuit had soon spun out into the gravel trap five laps after Berger retired.
</p><p>Coulthard overtook teammate Hill during the first round of pit stops, while Barrichello moved back up to second before making his stop. On lap 19, Schumacher spun exiting Fagnes but retained the lead; the following lap, Barrichello spun into retirement at Pouhon and clipped the armco barrier giving his <a href="/wiki/Jordan_194" title="Jordan 194">Jordan 194</a> terminal suspension damage. <a href="/wiki/Martin_Brundle" title="Martin Brundle">Martin Brundle</a> moved into fifth in the second McLaren, before he himself had spun off and clipped the armco like Barrichello by lap 25.
</p><p>When Schumacher and Hill made their second pit stops on lap 28, Coulthard led a lap for the first time in his F1 career. After making his own second stop, he remained ahead of Hill until the Williams team called him in on lap 37 to check his rear wing. He then developed gearbox problems and was passed by Häkkinen, Verstappen and <a href="/wiki/Mark_Blundell" title="Mark Blundell">Mark Blundell</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_Racing" title="Tyrrell Racing">Tyrrell</a>. On lap 40, Coulthard hit the back of Blundell's car going through La Source; both were able to continue, with Coulthard apologising to Blundell afterwards. <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a> was the final retirement with the result of an alternator failure with only 3 laps to go and was classified 14th, leaving 13 runners left in the race up to the finish.
</p><p>Though Hill set the fastest lap of the race on lap 41, Schumacher crossed the finish line some 13 seconds ahead, with Häkkinen a further 51 seconds back and Verstappen, Coulthard and Blundell completing the top six.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1994_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Benetton_B194_bottom2_2019_Michael_Schumacher_Private_Collection.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/db/Benetton_B194_bottom2_2019_Michael_Schumacher_Private_Collection.jpg/220px-Benetton_B194_bottom2_2019_Michael_Schumacher_Private_Collection.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/db/Benetton_B194_bottom2_2019_Michael_Schumacher_Private_Collection.jpg/330px-Benetton_B194_bottom2_2019_Michael_Schumacher_Private_Collection.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/db/Benetton_B194_bottom2_2019_Michael_Schumacher_Private_Collection.jpg/440px-Benetton_B194_bottom2_2019_Michael_Schumacher_Private_Collection.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2560" data-file-height="1707" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Benetton_B194_bottom2_2019_Michael_Schumacher_Private_Collection.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Bottom view of the <a href="/wiki/Benetton_B194" title="Benetton B194">Benetton B194</a>.</div></div></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Michael_Schumacher_Belgian_Grand_Prix.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/57/Michael_Schumacher_Belgian_Grand_Prix.jpg/220px-Michael_Schumacher_Belgian_Grand_Prix.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="145" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/57/Michael_Schumacher_Belgian_Grand_Prix.jpg/330px-Michael_Schumacher_Belgian_Grand_Prix.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/57/Michael_Schumacher_Belgian_Grand_Prix.jpg/440px-Michael_Schumacher_Belgian_Grand_Prix.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3564" data-file-height="2352" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Michael_Schumacher_Belgian_Grand_Prix.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Michael Schumacher celebrating his race win before being disqualified.</div></div></div>
<p>Shortly after the race, excessive wear was found on the wooden <a href="/wiki/Skid_block" title="Skid block">skid block</a> on the underside of Schumacher's car. The skid block was a <a href="/wiki/1994_Formula_One_World_Championship#Technical_changes" title="1994 Formula One World Championship">mandatory requirement</a> on all cars, introduced two races before in <a href="/wiki/1994_German_Grand_Prix" title="1994 German Grand Prix">Germany</a>, to increase ride height and reduce <a href="/wiki/Ground_effect_(cars)" title="Ground effect (cars)">ground effect</a> advantages. Wear on the skid block of up to 1mm was permitted; any greater would make the ride height too low and result in an illegal aerodynamic advantage.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The Benetton team claimed that the excessive wear resulted from Schumacher's spin on lap 19, but the stewards rejected the claim because of the wear pattern. Schumacher was thus disqualified and Hill awarded the victory, with Häkkinen second, Verstappen third, Coulthard fourth, Blundell fifth and <a href="/wiki/Gianni_Morbidelli" title="Gianni Morbidelli">Gianni Morbidelli</a> sixth in the <a href="/wiki/Footwork_Arrows" title="Footwork Arrows">Footwork</a>.
</p><p>Schumacher's lead over Hill in the Drivers' Championship was reduced to 21 points with five races remaining.
