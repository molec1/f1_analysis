<p>The <b>1986 San Marino Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held at <a href="/wiki/Autodromo_Enzo_e_Dino_Ferrari" class="mw-redirect" title="Autodromo Enzo e Dino Ferrari">Imola</a> on 27 April 1986. The race was the third round of the year's World Championship. As with <a href="/wiki/1985_San_Marino_Grand_Prix" title="1985 San Marino Grand Prix">the previous year's event</a>, fuel consumption was a big issue, changing the points finishers in the closing laps.
</p><p>The Autodromo Dino Ferrari had received safety upgrades from the previous year, including slight track modifications after the Variante Alta chicane and extended runoff areas at the Rivazza complex.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> (<a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/2" title="McLaren MP4/2">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Techniques_d%27Avant_Garde" title="Techniques d&#39;Avant Garde">TAG</a>) dominated the race after <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> (<a href="/wiki/Lotus_98T" title="Lotus 98T">Lotus</a>-<a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a>) and Nigel Mansell (<a href="/wiki/Williams_FW11" title="Williams FW11">Williams</a>-<a href="/wiki/Honda_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Honda F1">Honda</a>) retired early, before almost running out of fuel, three corners from the chequered flag. Frantically weaving the car back and forth to slosh the last drops of fuel into the pickup, he managed to keep it running just long enough to creep over the line and win the race (his <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> team mate <a href="/wiki/Keke_Rosberg" title="Keke Rosberg">Keke Rosberg</a> was classified 5th despite running dry 2 laps from the finish, a problem attributed to Rosberg using too much boost). <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a> brought his Williams home in second place only 7.645 seconds behind Prost, while <a href="/wiki/Austrian_people" class="mw-redirect" title="Austrian people">Austrian</a> <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> finished third to score his and the <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a> team's first Formula One podium finish.
</p>
