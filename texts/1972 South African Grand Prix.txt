<p>The <b>1972 South African Grand Prix</b>, formally the <b>Sixth AA Grand Prix of South Africa</b> (<a href="/wiki/Afrikaans" title="Afrikaans">Afrikaans</a>: <i>Sesde AA Suid-Afrikaanse Grand Prix</i><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>), was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at <a href="/wiki/Kyalami" title="Kyalami">Kyalami</a> on 4 March 1972. It was race 2 of 12 in both the <a href="/wiki/1972_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1972 World Championship of Drivers">1972 World Championship of Drivers</a> and the <a href="/wiki/1972_International_Cup_for_Formula_One_Manufacturers" class="mw-redirect" title="1972 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers">1972 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers</a>. The 79-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Denny_Hulme" title="Denny Hulme">Denny Hulme</a> after he started from fifth position. <a href="/wiki/Emerson_Fittipaldi" title="Emerson Fittipaldi">Emerson Fittipaldi</a> finished second for the <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Lotus</a> team and <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Peter_Revson" title="Peter Revson">Peter Revson</a> came in third. 
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1972_South_African_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Entry">Entry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1972_South_African_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Entry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A total of 27 cars were entered for this race. Although there was more than a month between the <a href="/wiki/1972_Argentine_Grand_Prix" title="1972 Argentine Grand Prix">Argentine Grand Prix</a> and South Africa, most cars were transported direct to Kyalami from <a href="/wiki/Aut%C3%B3dromo_Juan_y_Oscar_G%C3%A1lvez" title="Autódromo Juan y Oscar Gálvez">Buenos Aires</a>. There were few changes in the entry.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix1_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Having missed the trip to Argentina due to a clashing <a href="/wiki/Formula_5000" title="Formula 5000">Formula 5000</a> race, <a href="/wiki/Mike_Hailwood" title="Mike Hailwood">Mike Hailwood</a> returned to <a href="/wiki/Surtees" title="Surtees">Team Surtees</a>. <a href="/wiki/Jean-Pierre_Beltoise" title="Jean-Pierre Beltoise">Jean-Pierre Beltoise</a> also missed Buenos Aires, due to legal problems following an accident that had claimed the life of <a href="/wiki/Ignazio_Giunti" title="Ignazio Giunti">Ignazio Giunti</a> in January 1971. <a href="/wiki/Frank_Williams_Racing_Cars" title="Frank Williams Racing Cars">Team Williams</a> ran a second <a href="/wiki/March_Engineering" title="March Engineering">March-Cosworth</a> for rising star <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Pace" title="Carlos Pace">Carlos Pace</a> alongside their regular driver, <a href="/wiki/Henri_Pescarolo" title="Henri Pescarolo">Henri Pescarolo</a>. A new team arrived in South Africa, the <a href="/wiki/Eifelland" title="Eifelland">Team Eifelland Caravans</a> outfit making its first appearance with <a href="/wiki/Rolf_Stommelen" title="Rolf Stommelen">Rolf Stommelen</a> down to drive. As usual for Kyalami, the field was bolstered by local entries: <a href="/wiki/Dave_Charlton" title="Dave Charlton">Dave Charlton</a> in a <a href="/wiki/Lucky_Strike" title="Lucky Strike">Lucky Strike</a>-sponsored <a href="/wiki/Lotus_72" title="Lotus 72">Lotus 72</a>D, and pair of cars from <a href="/wiki/Team_Gunston" title="Team Gunston">Team Gunston</a> for <a href="/wiki/John_Love_(racing_driver)" title="John Love (racing driver)">John Love</a> (<a href="/wiki/Surtees" title="Surtees">Surtees</a> TS9) and <a href="/wiki/William_Ferguson_(racing_driver)" title="William Ferguson (racing driver)">Willie Ferguson</a> (<a href="/wiki/Brabham" title="Brabham">Brabham</a> BT33).<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix1_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1972_South_African_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The reigning World Champion, and winner of the opening round, <a href="/wiki/Jackie_Stewart" title="Jackie Stewart">Jackie Stewart</a> secured <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>, for <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_Racing" title="Tyrrell Racing">Elf Team Tyrrell</a>, averaging a speed of 119.221&#160;mph. Stewart beat <a href="/wiki/Clay_Regazzoni" title="Clay Regazzoni">Clay Regazzoni</a> (<a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Emerson_Fittipaldi" title="Emerson Fittipaldi">Emerson Fittipaldi</a> (<a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Lotus</a>) by a few tenth of a second. <a href="/wiki/Mike_Hailwood" title="Mike Hailwood">Mike Hailwood</a> was next up on the second row, joined by <a href="/wiki/Denny_Hulme" title="Denny Hulme">Denny Hulme</a> in his <a href="/wiki/McLaren_M19A" title="McLaren M19A">McLaren M19A</a>. A pair of <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_312B" title="Ferrari 312B">Ferrari 312B</a>2 were on row three, with <a href="/wiki/Mario_Andretti" title="Mario Andretti">Mario Andretti</a> ahead of <a href="/wiki/Jacky_Ickx" title="Jacky Ickx">Jacky Ickx</a>. They were joined by <a href="/wiki/Fran%C3%A7ois_Cevert" title="François Cevert">François Cevert</a> in the second Tyrrell.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix1_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-racing-reference1_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-racing-reference1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1972_South_African_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The race was held over 79 laps of the <a href="/wiki/Kyalami" title="Kyalami">Kyalami</a> circuit. At the start, <a href="/wiki/Denny_Hulme" title="Denny Hulme">Denny Hulme</a> briefly held the lead before <a href="/wiki/Jackie_Stewart" title="Jackie Stewart">Jackie Stewart</a> moved ahead, meanwhile <a href="/wiki/Clay_Regazzoni" title="Clay Regazzoni">Clay Regazzoni</a> had a bad get away and was back in the midfield. The <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_003" title="Tyrrell 003">Tyrrell 003</a> of Stewart quickly built a lead while the battle for second between Hulme, <a href="/wiki/Emerson_Fittipaldi" title="Emerson Fittipaldi">Emerson Fittipaldi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mike_Hailwood" title="Mike Hailwood">Mike Hailwood</a> detached itself from the rest of the field. The <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">Yardley Team McLaren</a> of Hulme began to overheat and this saw him dropped behind his rivals. Hailwood, a few laps later, was able to pass Fittipaldi to move up to second and began to challenge Stewart for the lead. On lap 28, the rear suspension of Hailwood’s <a href="/wiki/Surtees" title="Surtees">Surtees TS9B</a> failed and he retired from the race. Although this left Fittipaldi to attack Stewart, the Tyrrell driver stayed ahead, only to suffer a gearbox failure on lap 45. Fittipaldi inherited the lead, with Hulme right on his tail. The Brazilian now ran into handling problems and this enable Hulme to regain the lead on lap 57, from which he would go to win. Fittipaldi was still in a strong second place as the gap between him and the third place, <a href="/wiki/Chris_Amon" title="Chris Amon">Chris Amon</a> was large. The gap became even bigger where Amon’s <a href="/wiki/Matra_MS120C" class="mw-redirect" title="Matra MS120C">Matra MS120C</a> developed a serious vibration. This saw him drop down the order and finish 3 laps adrift in 15th. Third place thus went to the second McLaren, of <a href="/wiki/Peter_Revson" title="Peter Revson">Peter Revson</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix1_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-racing-reference1_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-racing-reference1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Hulme won in a time of 1hr 45.49.1mins., averaging a speed of 114.898&#160;mph, and was over 14 seconds ahead of Fititpaldi. Revson was a further 12.7 seconds behind. Only other drivers to complete the full race distance were <a href="/wiki/Mario_Andretti" title="Mario Andretti">Mario Andretti</a> (Ferrari) and <a href="/wiki/Ronnie_Peterson" title="Ronnie Peterson">Ronnie Peterson</a> (March). This victory gave Hulme a six-point the lead in the World Championship.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix1_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix1-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-formula1972_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-formula1972-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-crash1972_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-crash1972-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
