<p>The <b>2012 United States Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>2012 Formula 1 United States Grand Prix</b>)<sup id="cite_ref-formal_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-formal-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_of_the_Americas" title="Circuit of the Americas">Circuit of the Americas</a> in <a href="/wiki/Travis_County,_Texas" title="Travis County, Texas">Travis County</a>, near <a href="/wiki/Austin,_Texas" title="Austin, Texas">Austin, Texas</a> on November 18, 2012. The race, run over fifty-six laps, was the penultimate round of the <a href="/wiki/2012_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2012 Formula One season">2012 championship</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7Dec_calendar_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7Dec_calendar-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> It was the inaugural race at the circuit, and the first time the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Grand_Prix" title="United States Grand Prix">United States Grand Prix</a> had been held since <a href="/wiki/2007_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2007 Formula One World Championship">2007</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> On Sunday a capacity crowd of 117,429 watched the race around the brand new 20-turn circuit.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> started the race from pole position.<sup id="cite_ref-VET_pole_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VET_pole-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> won the race, his last for McLaren before his move to Mercedes, with Vettel finishing second and <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> in third.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
<sup id="cite_ref-HAM_win_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HAM_win-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Vettel's second place gave <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a> enough points to secure their third consecutive <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">World Constructors' Championship</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-WCC_decided_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WCC_decided-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Senna" title="Bruno Senna">Bruno Senna</a> scored his last ever World Championship points at this race.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2012_United_States_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2012_United_States_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After a <a href="/wiki/Circuit_of_the_Americas#Breach_of_contract_and_reinstatement" title="Circuit of the Americas">troubled construction period</a> that saw a stop-work order issued in November 2011,<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> and the threat of the race being removed from the Formula One calendar altogether,<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> construction laid the final layer of tarmac on September 21.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> The circuit passed its final pre-race inspection on September 25, allowing the race to go ahead as planned.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> By comparison, the most-recent additions to the Formula One calendar&#160;– the <a href="/wiki/Korea_International_Circuit" title="Korea International Circuit">Korea International Circuit</a> in <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Buddh_International_Circuit" title="Buddh International Circuit">Buddh International Circuit</a> in India&#160;– were only completed in the days leading up to their inaugural races.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> The event was not the first Formula One Grand Prix in the state of Texas- the one-off <a href="/wiki/1984_Dallas_Grand_Prix" title="1984 Dallas Grand Prix">Dallas Grand Prix</a> was held in July 1984 and the event was run in the extremely high temperatures of a Texas summer.
</p><p>Tyre supplier <a href="/wiki/Pirelli" title="Pirelli">Pirelli</a> provided teams with the medium and hard compounds for the race, with the hard tyre designated the prime compound, and the medium tyre the option compound.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> As a result of this, Pirelli's Paul Hembery predicted that the race would feature one-stop strategies throughout the field.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> The selection of tyres was criticised by teams and drivers for being too conservative and counter-productive to racing,<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> though Pirelli claimed that this was a result of the highly-specific chemical makeup of the tarmac used at circuits hosting Grands Prix.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> After analysing data gathered in free practice on Friday, Pirelli admitted that the soft and super-soft compounds would have been better-suited to the characteristics of the circuit.
</p><p>The race saw the use of a single <a href="/wiki/Drag_Reduction_System" class="mw-redirect" title="Drag Reduction System">Drag Reduction System</a> (DRS) zone, located along the back straight to facilitate overtaking into Turn 12.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> The detection point was located 1,220 feet (372&#160;m) before the apex of the Turn 11 hairpin, with the activation point positioned 1,050 feet (320&#160;m) after the Turn 11 apex, making the total DRS zone some 2,130 feet (649&#160;m) in length.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Free_practice">Free practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2012_United_States_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Free practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Jenson_Button_2012_USA.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ed/Jenson_Button_2012_USA.jpg/220px-Jenson_Button_2012_USA.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="128" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ed/Jenson_Button_2012_USA.jpg/330px-Jenson_Button_2012_USA.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ed/Jenson_Button_2012_USA.jpg/440px-Jenson_Button_2012_USA.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3193" data-file-height="1852" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Jenson_Button_2012_USA.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Jenson Button during the Friday practice. McLaren cars run with <a href="/wiki/Verizon_Communications" title="Verizon Communications">Verizon</a> logos instead of <a href="/wiki/Vodafone" title="Vodafone">Vodafone</a> in North America.</div></div></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> was the fastest driver in both the opening practice sessions, setting a time a second and a half faster than <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> in the Friday morning session, and two seconds faster than title rival <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> The session was run without incident, though drivers reported that there was very little grip around the circuit.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Ma_Qinghua" title="Ma Qinghua">Ma Qinghua</a> was the only reserve driver to take part in the session, replacing <a href="/wiki/Narain_Karthikeyan" title="Narain Karthikeyan">Narain Karthikeyan</a> at <a href="/wiki/HRT_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="HRT F1">HRT</a>. He finished the session in twenty-fourth and last position, ten seconds behind Vettel.
</p><p>Vettel was again fastest in the Friday afternoon session, seven tenths of a second quicker than team-mate <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> and Alonso.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Vettel's running was limited by a water leak that forced him to spend half the session in the pits, while <a href="/wiki/Caterham_F1" title="Caterham F1">Caterham's</a> <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> suffered a puncture when he made contact with <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Scuderia Toro Rosso</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Jean-%C3%89ric_Vergne" title="Jean-Éric Vergne">Jean-Éric Vergne</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Pastor_Maldonado" title="Pastor Maldonado">Pastor Maldonado</a> suffered an engine failure in his <a href="/wiki/Williams_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams F1">Williams</a> late in the session.
</p><p>Vettel completed the trifecta of fastest lap times when he topped the final session on Saturday morning, finishing two and a half tenths of a second faster than Hamilton.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> The hour-long session was marked by a collision between <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Marussia_F1" title="Marussia F1">Marussia</a> of <a href="/wiki/Charles_Pic" title="Charles Pic">Charles Pic</a> that damaged the floor of Pic's car. Pérez escaped without penalty, much to the chagrin of Marussia.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Jean-Éric Vergne also ran into further trouble, breaking his front suspension when he hit a kerb and stopped on the circuit.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2012_United_States_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Qualifying was held over one hour on Saturday afternoon. The session was broken up into three periods of twenty minutes, fifteen minutes and ten minutes respectively. At the end of the first period, Q1, the slowest seven cars were eliminated, and would start the race from the last seven positions on the grid. Any car that failed to qualify <a href="/wiki/107%25_rule" title="107% rule">within 107%</a> of the fastest lap time recorded in Q1 would have failed to qualify, though they would be eligible to enter the race at the discretion of the stewards. At the end of the second period, Q2, the slowest seven cars were again eliminated and would start the race in positions eleven to seventeen. This would leave the ten fastest cars to compete for pole position in the final ten-minute period, Q3.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Q1">Q1</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2012_United_States_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Q1">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>With the surface of the circuit offering very little grip, every team sent their drivers out for extended runs in the first twenty-minute period of qualifying, with the objective of laying down enough rubber on the circuit to improve their overall lap time, and trying to time their final flying runs to take full advantage of the circuit.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/HRT_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="HRT F1">HRT</a> were initially concerned that they would fail to qualify inside the 107% cut-off, and therefore fail to qualify for the race.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> These fears were not realised as they sent <a href="/wiki/Pedro_de_la_Rosa" title="Pedro de la Rosa">Pedro de la Rosa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Narain_Karthikeyan" title="Narain Karthikeyan">Narain Karthikeyan</a> out on the softer option tyres, allowing them to record faster lap times than if they had used the harder prime compound, which most of the front-runners would use in the first period so as to keep a set of soft tyres in reserve. Both de la Rosa and Karthikeyan qualified for the race, filling out the final row of the grid. Karthikeyan's session ended early when he pulled over onto the tarmac run-off at Turn 2, the victim of a mechanical fault. The positioning of his car forced race control to show yellow flags in the first sector, obliging drivers to slow down to avoid an accident.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Caterham_F1" title="Caterham F1">Caterham's</a> <a href="/wiki/Vitaly_Petrov" title="Vitaly Petrov">Vitaly Petrov</a> struggled early in the session with traffic, and spent most of the twenty minutes outside the 107% cut-off. His final flying lap saw him qualify twenty-first for the race, one place ahead of team-mate <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a>. It was only the sixth time in eighteen races that Petrov had out-qualified his team-mate. Caterham later claimed that Karthikeyan's stoppage robbed them of the chance to set faster times, and the net result was that both Caterham cars qualified behind the Marussias of <a href="/wiki/Timo_Glock" title="Timo Glock">Timo Glock</a> and <a href="/wiki/Charles_Pic" title="Charles Pic">Charles Pic</a>, prompting the team to declare that they had now caught Caterham in terms of raw pace.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> was the seventh and final driver eliminated at the end of the first qualifying period. He had spent most of the twenty minutes engaged in a battle to avoid elimination with <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes'</a> <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a>, with Rosberg prevailing when Ricciardo's final lap was compromised by the yellow flags in the first sector. The result meant that a <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Scuderia Toro Rosso</a> was eliminated in the first qualifying period for the tenth time in 2012, though it was only Ricciardo's second elimination.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Q2">Q2</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2012_United_States_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Q2">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The trend towards the circuit becoming faster as more rubber was laid down continued in Q2, with lap times continuing to drop. <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a>, who had recorded the fastest time in Q1, went on to beat his best time by eight tenths of a second in Q2. The faster lap times now meant that the softer option compound was a necessity; predictions from the teams – which would soon prove to be accurate – suggested that it would be impossible to advance to the final period of qualifying using the harder prime tyres.
</p><p>Despite narrowly beating Ricciardo in Q1, Nico Rosberg was unable to improve his time in Q2, and qualified in seventeenth overall. His team later defended the decision to have him compete in an older specification of the <a href="/wiki/Mercedes_F1_W03" title="Mercedes F1 W03">Mercedes F1 W03</a> as they felt it was better-suited to Rosberg's driving style, particularly when it came to heating his tyres.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> The Saubers of Pérez and <a href="/wiki/Kamui_Kobayashi" title="Kamui Kobayashi">Kamui Kobayashi</a> filled out the eighth row of the grid, with the team unable to explain their lack of pace despite early predictions that they could challenge Mercedes for fifth place in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">World Constructors' Championship</a> in Austin and <a href="/wiki/2012_Brazilian_Grand_Prix" title="2012 Brazilian Grand Prix">Brazil</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Jean-Éric Vergne overcame his free practice dramas to qualify fourteenth, two tenths of a second ahead of Pérez and two tenths of a second behind <a href="/wiki/Paul_di_Resta" title="Paul di Resta">Paul di Resta</a>, who was disappointed to be eliminated in Q2 when team-mate <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a> comfortably progressed to Q3. <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> had to settle for twelfth overall when his <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4-27" title="McLaren MP4-27">McLaren MP4-27</a> lost all power late in the period, and he later admitted that he was afraid of getting caught up in what he called a "chaotic first lap".<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Senna" title="Bruno Senna">Bruno Senna</a> was the last driver eliminated in Q2, qualifying in eleventh place in his best qualifying performance since his Q3 appearance in the <a href="/wiki/2012_Hungarian_Grand_Prix" title="2012 Hungarian Grand Prix">Hungarian Grand Prix</a>.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Q3">Q3</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2012_United_States_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Q3">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Sebastian Vettel took pole position with the fastest lap of the weekend to date, setting a time of 1:35.657 on his final flying lap. In spite of this, Lewis Hamilton was closer to Vettel than he had been all weekend, missing pole position by just one tenth of a second. <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> qualified third, ahead of <a href="/wiki/Romain_Grosjean" title="Romain Grosjean">Romain Grosjean</a>, who was later demoted to ninth when he received a five-place grid penalty for an unscheduled gearbox change.<sup id="cite_ref-GRO_5_place_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GRO_5_place-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> qualified fifth, expressing surprise at such a strong qualifying performance when <a href="/wiki/Lotus_F1" title="Lotus F1">his team</a> had been struggling with the lack of grip throughout free practice.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>, who has won the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Grand_Prix" title="United States Grand Prix">United States Grand Prix</a> on five occasions, qualified sixth in his penultimate race before his second retirement from the sport,<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> ahead of former team-mate <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a>. Nico Hülkenberg finished the session in eighth, while <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> struggled throughout qualifying to finish ninth overall. Ferrari were later forced to defend their qualifying tactics after sending Alonso out to complete his final timed run on a set of "scrubbed" – used – tyres.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Pastor_Maldonado" title="Pastor Maldonado">Pastor Maldonado</a> completed the top ten.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-qualifying">Post-qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2012_United_States_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Post-qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Both <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> were summoned to the stewards' office after qualifying. Schumacher had been cited for blocking Alonso during Q2 when the Mercedes driver impeded him at Turn 15. Schumacher escaped without punishment, as he had been on a flying lap of his own at the time, and blocking Alonso had not prevented him from advancing to Q3.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Webber, on the other hand, was called to answer charges of missing the mandatory weight check at the end of qualifying. The stewards accepted that <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a> had made an error and saw that the car was immediately sent to scrutineering once it was realised. Webber escaped with a reprimand, thereby allowing him to keep his grid position for the race.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2012_United_States_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the start, Vettel led from pole position and Webber passed Hamilton before entering the first corner. Alonso made a good start to gain 3 places.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> Vettel opened up a lead over Webber before Hamilton passed the Australian in the DRS zone on the long straight from T11 to T12 on Lap 4. Webber would later retire on Lap 17 when KERS shut down and eventually the alternator failed on the Red Bull car.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> Vettel would pit on Lap 22 but retained the lead over Räikkönen, Hamilton, Massa, and Alonso. Hamilton was able to catch and pass Vettel on Lap 42 in the DRS zone and kept the lead into turn 12. McLaren's Lewis Hamilton would go on to win the race, his last win for McLaren, followed by Red Bull's Sebastian Vettel and Ferrari's Fernando Alonso.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Senna" title="Bruno Senna">Bruno Senna</a> scored his final Formula One point to date.
</p>
