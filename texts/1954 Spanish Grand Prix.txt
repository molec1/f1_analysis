<p>The <b>1954 Spanish Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held on 24 October 1954 at <a href="/wiki/Pedralbes_Circuit" title="Pedralbes Circuit">Pedralbes</a>. It was the ninth and final race in the <a href="/wiki/1954_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1954 World Championship of Drivers">1954 World Championship of Drivers</a>. The 80-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Mike_Hawthorn" title="Mike Hawthorn">Mike Hawthorn</a> after he started from third position. <a href="/wiki/Luigi_Musso" title="Luigi Musso">Luigi Musso</a> finished second for the <a href="/wiki/Maserati_in_motorsport" title="Maserati in motorsport">Maserati</a> team and <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Juan_Manuel_Fangio" title="Juan Manuel Fangio">Juan Manuel Fangio</a> came in third.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_report">Race report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1954_Spanish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Race report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The long-awaited Lancia D50s arrived-using their 90 degree V8 engine as a stiffening aid for the chassis; they were simple but brilliantly designed. Ascari immediately set the fastest practice lap and led from pole.
</p><p>Villoresi in the fellow Lancia retired with brake trouble after just 1 lap and Ascari succumbed to clutch problems after 9 laps. 
</p><p>Various other drivers took the lead and then retired-Schell spun off, Trintignant had mechanical problems and Moss overheated (one of several retirements caused by flying newspaper jamming the radiator ducts). The race boiled down to a duel between Hawthorn and Fangio but the Argentine was losing oil and lost second place to Musso. Hawthorn hung on to take the win with Fangio driving well to claim the final podium position.
</p><p>This proved to be the last major race held at the Pedralbes street circuit. The <a href="/wiki/1955_Le_Mans_disaster" title="1955 Le Mans disaster">Le Mans disaster in 1955</a> meant tighter safety regulations, and the spectator-lined street circuit in the Pedralbes neighborhood of Barcelona was abandoned and has not been used since.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1954_Spanish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><b><a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">Pole position</a></b>: <a href="/wiki/Alberto_Ascari" title="Alberto Ascari">Alberto Ascari</a> 2:18.1</li>
<li>Fastest lap: <a href="/wiki/Alberto_Ascari" title="Alberto Ascari">Alberto Ascari</a> 2:20.4</li>
<li><b>Lap Leaders</b>:
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Harry_Schell" title="Harry Schell">Harry Schell</a> 10 laps (1–2, 10, 13, 15–17, 19, 21, 23) (First race he has led a lap)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Alberto_Ascari" title="Alberto Ascari">Alberto Ascari</a> 7 laps (3–9)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Maurice_Trintignant" title="Maurice Trintignant">Maurice Trintignant</a> 5 laps (11–12, 14, 18, 20) (First race he has led a lap)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Mike_Hawthorn" title="Mike Hawthorn">Mike Hawthorn</a> 58 laps (22, 24–80)</li></ul></li>
<li><b>Shared Drives</b>: Car #22: <a href="/wiki/Toulo_de_Graffenried" title="Toulo de Graffenried">Toulo de Graffenried</a> (30 laps) and <a href="/wiki/Ottorino_Volonterio" title="Ottorino Volonterio">Ottorino Volonterio</a> (27 laps).</li>
<li>Grand Prix debut for: <a href="/wiki/Ottorino_Volonterio" title="Ottorino Volonterio">Ottorino Volonterio</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lancia_in_Formula_One" class="mw-redirect" title="Lancia in Formula One">Lancia</a>.</li>
<li>Last Grand Prix appearance for: <a href="/wiki/Birabongse_Bhanudej" title="Birabongse Bhanudej">Prince Bira</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Swaters" title="Jacques Swaters">Jacques Swaters</a>.</li>
<li>First points and first podium for <a href="/wiki/Luigi_Musso" title="Luigi Musso">Luigi Musso</a>.</li>
<li>The only time that Juan Manuel Fangio finished third in a World Drivers' Championship race.</li></ul>
