<p>The <b>1987 Spanish Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at <a href="/wiki/Circuito_Permanente_de_Jerez" class="mw-redirect" title="Circuito Permanente de Jerez">Jerez</a> on 27 September 1987. It was the thirteenth round of the <a href="/wiki/1987_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="1987 Formula One season">1987 Formula One season</a>. It was the 29th <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Grand_Prix" title="Spanish Grand Prix">Spanish Grand Prix</a> and the second to be held at Jerez. The race was held over 72 laps of the 4.22-kilometre (2.62&#160;mi) circuit for a race distance of 304 kilometres (189&#160;mi).
</p><p>The race was won by British driver <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW11" title="Williams FW11">Williams</a>-<a href="/wiki/Honda_in_Formula_One" title="Honda in Formula One">Honda</a>. It was Mansell's fifth victory of 1987 and the eighth for the Williams team, securing them their second consecutive Constructors' Championship and fourth in all. Frenchman <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> finished second in a <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/3" title="McLaren MP4/3">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Techniques_d%27Avant_Garde" title="Techniques d&#39;Avant Garde">TAG</a>, some 22 seconds behind Mansell, with Swedish teammate <a href="/wiki/Stefan_Johansson" title="Stefan Johansson">Stefan Johansson</a> third. Mansell's teammate and Drivers' Championship rival, <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a>, finished fourth. Prost broke <a href="/wiki/Niki_Lauda" title="Niki Lauda">Niki Lauda</a>'s record with his 55th podium at this race.
</p><p>The win moved Mansell ahead of <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a>, who finished fifth in his <a href="/wiki/Lotus_99T" title="Lotus 99T">Lotus</a>-Honda, into second place in the Drivers' Championship, albeit 18 points behind Piquet with three races remaining.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Martin_Brundle" title="Martin Brundle">Martin Brundle</a>, who finished 11th in his <a href="/wiki/Zakspeed_871" title="Zakspeed 871">Zakspeed</a>, described his drive as "the time I got out the car thinking no human could have done [any] better".<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Summary">Summary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1987_Spanish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Summary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nelson Piquet secured his 24th and final F1 pole position in his Williams-<a href="/wiki/Honda_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Honda F1">Honda</a> with Nigel Mansell completing an all-Williams front row. The race was comfortably won by Mansell who passed Piquet at the end of the first lap and was never headed. The battle for third (then second) was led for much of the time by Ayrton Senna, who like the previous year tried to complete the race without changing tyres. Both Senna and <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Lotus</a> were of the opinion that the <a href="/wiki/Lotus_99T" title="Lotus 99T">99T</a>'s computerised <a href="/wiki/Active_suspension" title="Active suspension">active suspension</a> system would help preserve his tyres throughout the race.
</p><p>Senna had a queue of both <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferraris</a>, Prost's McLaren and <a href="/wiki/Thierry_Boutsen" title="Thierry Boutsen">Thierry Boutsen</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a> behind him, which was joined by Piquet after a long pit-stop. For lap after lap, Senna held off all-comers, similar to <a href="/wiki/Gilles_Villeneuve" title="Gilles Villeneuve">Gilles Villeneuve</a>'s performance in his <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> at <a href="/wiki/Circuito_del_Jarama" title="Circuito del Jarama">Jarama</a> for the <a href="/wiki/1981_Spanish_Grand_Prix" title="1981 Spanish Grand Prix">1981 Spanish Grand Prix</a>. The Lotus-Honda was very fast in a straight line with a low downforce setup, but was slow through Jerez's many twists and turns as a result. Senna's pursuers could not pass him on the long pit straight, and with Jerez generally having a lot of dust and sand off the racing line, they were not able pass him through the corners without losing grip.
</p><p>However, Piquet's similarly powered Williams was able to get by (not before having a spin) followed eventually by Boutsen and Prost as the Brazilian's tyres finally went off. Senna faded to finish fifth, but the battle for second continued between Boutsen and Piquet - Boutsen went out avoiding Piquet who was rejoining the track after having gone off - and then between Piquet and Prost, with Prost getting the better of the Williams driver who also lost third place to McLaren's <a href="/wiki/Stefan_Johansson" title="Stefan Johansson">Stefan Johansson</a> who put in another strong drive. Both Ferraris blew their engines.
</p>
