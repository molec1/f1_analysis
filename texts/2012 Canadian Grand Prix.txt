<p>The <b>2012 Canadian Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>Formula 1 Grand Prix du Canada 2012</b>)<sup id="cite_ref-formal_name_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-formal_name-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race that took place on 10 June 2012 at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_Gilles_Villeneuve" title="Circuit Gilles Villeneuve">Circuit Gilles Villeneuve</a> in <a href="/wiki/Montreal" title="Montreal">Montreal, Quebec</a>, Canada. The 70-lap race was the seventh round of the <a href="/wiki/2012_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2012 Formula One season">2012 Formula One season</a>, and the first of two North American rounds.<sup id="cite_ref-7Dec_calendar_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7Dec_calendar-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> It was the 49th <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Grand_Prix" title="Canadian Grand Prix">Canadian Grand Prix</a>, and the 33rd to be held at the circuit since its début on the calendar in <a href="/wiki/1978_Formula_One_season" title="1978 Formula One season">1978</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> started the race from pole.<sup id="cite_ref-quali_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-quali-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> went on to win his first race of the year, becoming the seventh different driver to win in as many races, a record for a single Formula One season.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2012_Canadian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2012_Canadian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After experimenting with two <a href="/wiki/Drag_Reduction_System" class="mw-redirect" title="Drag Reduction System">Drag Reduction System</a> (DRS) zones with a single activation point in <a href="/wiki/2011_Canadian_Grand_Prix" title="2011 Canadian Grand Prix">2011</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> the <a href="/wiki/FIA" class="mw-redirect" title="FIA">FIA</a> decided that the 2012 race would use a single, shorter DRS zone.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In 2011, the DRS zone was 650 metres from the l'Epingle hairpin to the final chicane, with a secondary zone placed along the main straight. For 2012, the detection point was once again placed after the l'Epingle hairpin, and the DRS zone was shortened to 600 metres, while the secondary zone along the pit straight was removed.<sup id="cite_ref-DRS_points_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DRS_points-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The FIA explained that the DRS zone was shortened because overtaking was "too easy" in 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-DRS_points_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-DRS_points-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/2012_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="2012 Monaco Grand Prix">Monaco Grand Prix</a> saw several teams question the legality of parts used by <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a> on <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_RB8" title="Red Bull RB8">their car</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> and although <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a>'s win went uncontested, rival teams sought clarification on matter.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> One week before the Canadian Grand Prix, the FIA declared the parts used in Monaco to be illegal, forcing the team to change them.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> The team was also forced to change the design of their axles, after FIA Race Director <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Whiting" title="Charlie Whiting">Charlie Whiting</a> felt that holes in the axles contravened the technical regulations.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Like the <a href="/wiki/2011_Canadian_Grand_Prix" title="2011 Canadian Grand Prix">2011 Canadian Grand Prix</a>, tyre supplier Pirelli brought its yellow-banded soft compound tyre as the harder "prime" tyre and the red-banded supersoft compound tyre as the softer "option" tyre.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Threats_from_student_protest_groups">Threats from student protest groups</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2012_Canadian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Threats from student protest groups">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Following a <a href="/wiki/Smoke_bomb" title="Smoke bomb">smoke bomb</a> incident on <a href="/wiki/Montreal_Metro" title="Montreal Metro">Montreal Metro</a> subway, student activists from the <a href="/wiki/Universit%C3%A9_du_Qu%C3%A9bec_%C3%A0_Montr%C3%A9al" title="Université du Québec à Montréal">Université du Québec à Montréal</a> threatened to prevent the race from going ahead as part of <a href="/wiki/2012_Quebec_student_protests" title="2012 Quebec student protests">ongoing demonstrations</a> across <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">Quebec</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Further threats emerged two weeks later, with activists calling for disruptions to the race, which they depicted as a symbol of the "capitalist class",<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> while students from the Faculty of Arts at the Université du Québec à Montréal described the Grand Prix as "[representing] sexist, anti-environmental, elitist and economic values that must be abolished" and suggested disruptions be organized by the <a href="/wiki/Association_pour_une_solidarit%C3%A9_syndicale_%C3%A9tudiante" title="Association pour une solidarité syndicale étudiante">Coalition Large de l'Association pour une Solidarité Syndicale Étudiante</a> (CLASSE) — recognised as the most-radical student protest group.<sup id="cite_ref-natpo_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-natpo-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-UQAM_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UQAM-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> The race was specifically mentioned in chants directed at Premier of Quebec <a href="/wiki/Jean_Charest" title="Jean Charest">Jean Charest</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-UQAM_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UQAM-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After attacking the Formula One website in protest against the <a href="/wiki/2012_Bahrain_Grand_Prix" title="2012 Bahrain Grand Prix">Bahrain Grand Prix</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> hacktivist community <a href="/wiki/Anonymous_(group)" title="Anonymous (group)">Anonymous</a> threatened similar action against the sport to protest the Canadian Grand Prix as a part of <a href="/wiki/Timeline_of_events_associated_with_Anonymous#Operation_Quebec" title="Timeline of events associated with Anonymous">"Operation Quebec"</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> promising to block access to the circuit, light fires within the circuit limits, and foreshadowing attacks on hotels in the city proper.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Anti-capitalist group CLAC also threatened to target <a href="/wiki/Crescent_Street" title="Crescent Street">Crescent Street</a> in <a href="/wiki/Downtown_Montreal" title="Downtown Montreal">downtown</a> <a href="/wiki/Montreal" title="Montreal">Montreal</a> to "disrupt this crass elite at play",<sup id="cite_ref-300_Thursday_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-300_Thursday-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> describing the Grand Prix as "an orgy of cash [that] symbolizes <a href="/wiki/Turbo-capitalism" class="mw-redirect" title="Turbo-capitalism">turbo-capitalism</a>."<sup id="cite_ref-orgy_of_cash_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-orgy_of_cash-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In response to these threats, organisers for the event cancelled the "Open Doors Day" scheduled for the Thursday before the race.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Three hundred protesters gathered at an official pre-race function on the same day, with riot police called in to disperse the crowd.<sup id="cite_ref-300_Thursday_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-300_Thursday-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Montreal police also carried out raids on eighteen homes in the city, arresting eleven people suspected of widespread vandalism.<sup id="cite_ref-orgy_of_cash_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-orgy_of_cash-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Thirty-seven people were arrested on Friday evening when they staged a nude protest in front of tourists.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> A third protest took place on Saturday afternoon, though no arrests were made.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Montreal resident and former <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">World Champion</a> <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a> was highly critical of the protesters, comparing them to rioters who took part in the <a href="/wiki/2011_England_riots" title="2011 England riots">2011 England riots</a> and describing them as "people [who] grew up without ever hearing their parents ever tell them 'no'."<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> After claiming that plans by activists to block the Montreal Metro system on the day of the race could be considered an act of terrorism, Villeneuve claimed he had received "insulting" and "dangerous" e-mails.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Free_practice">Free practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2012_Canadian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Free practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> was the fastest driver in the first practice session, a tenth of a second faster than <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> and two-tenths faster than <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> was forced out of the session early after his team discovered an oil leak in his car, but still finished the session tenth, while <a href="/wiki/Caterham_F1" title="Caterham F1">Caterham</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> crashed at Turn 9 halfway through the session, bringing out the red flag.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Sebastian Vettel was summoned by the stewards after he was observed cutting across the final chicane to avoid <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Senna" title="Bruno Senna">Bruno Senna</a> and guarantee a clear circuit for a flying run, but no action was taken. A <a href="/wiki/HRT_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="HRT F1">HRT</a> mechanic was taken to hospital after he was hit by <a href="/wiki/Pedro_de_la_Rosa" title="Pedro de la Rosa">Pedro de la Rosa</a> in pit lane, but the team reported that he had not suffered any serious injuries.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Hamilton continued his form in the second session, this time leading the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferraris</a> of <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> and <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Jenson Button emerged late in the session, the team having overcome his oil leak, to set the ninth-fastest time; likewise, Caterham managed to rebuild Kovalainen's car in time for him to take part in the session. As in the first session, the second was interrupted by a red flag when <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Senna" title="Bruno Senna">Bruno Senna</a> crashed at the final chicane, destroying one of the experimental rear wings <a href="/wiki/Williams_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams F1">Williams</a> had brought to the race.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Jean-%C3%89ric_Vergne" title="Jean-Éric Vergne">Jean-Éric Vergne</a> became the third driver to crash in free practice, when he went off at the first turn on Saturday morning during his first flying lap, whilst Nico Rosberg suffered from a fuel pick-up problem that cost him an entire hour of running. Vettel finished the session fastest, just six thousandths of a second faster than Fernando Alonso, with Hamilton third.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> was investigated by the stewards for a dangerous pit entry, and was later fined for the incident.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2012_Canadian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:2012_Canadian_Grand_Prix_Sebastian_Vettel_RB8.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5c/2012_Canadian_Grand_Prix_Sebastian_Vettel_RB8.jpg/220px-2012_Canadian_Grand_Prix_Sebastian_Vettel_RB8.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="88" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5c/2012_Canadian_Grand_Prix_Sebastian_Vettel_RB8.jpg/330px-2012_Canadian_Grand_Prix_Sebastian_Vettel_RB8.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5c/2012_Canadian_Grand_Prix_Sebastian_Vettel_RB8.jpg/440px-2012_Canadian_Grand_Prix_Sebastian_Vettel_RB8.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4928" data-file-height="1968" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:2012_Canadian_Grand_Prix_Sebastian_Vettel_RB8.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> qualified on pole position.</div></div></div>
<p>Jean-Éric Vergne was eliminated in Q1, out-qualified by the <a href="/wiki/Caterham_F1" title="Caterham F1">Caterhams</a> of <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vitaly_Petrov" title="Vitaly Petrov">Vitaly Petrov</a>. Petrov was faster than his teammate for most of the Q1 session, but a late lap from Kovalinen — which included a bumpy ride across the kerbs at the final chicane — put him two-tenths of a second clear of the Russian. <a href="/wiki/Pedro_de_la_Rosa" title="Pedro de la Rosa">Pedro de la Rosa</a> outqualified both <a href="/wiki/Marussia_F1" title="Marussia F1">Marussia</a> cars by half a second, with teammate <a href="/wiki/Narain_Karthikeyan" title="Narain Karthikeyan">Narain Karthikeyan</a> within a tenth of a second of <a href="/wiki/Charles_Pic" title="Charles Pic">Charles Pic</a>, completing the final row of the grid. <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> was the fastest driver in the session.
</p><p>The second qualifying period was closely fought, with many drivers vying for a place in Q3 by only a few hundredths of a second, and the exact running order was only resolved in the final few seconds when <a href="/wiki/Pastor_Maldonado" title="Pastor Maldonado">Pastor Maldonado</a> spun at the final chicane and glanced against the outer wall. Maldonado pushed too hard into the chicane. He managed to keep his car from ploughing into the outer wall, but damaged his right rear wheel and suspension and ultimately finished seventeenth. <a href="/wiki/Romain_Grosjean" title="Romain Grosjean">Romain Grosjean</a> narrowly avoided elimination, but his teammate <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> finished twelfth, behind <a href="/wiki/Kamui_Kobayashi" title="Kamui Kobayashi">Kamui Kobayashi</a>. Despite setting the early pace, <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a> qualified thirteenth, with <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> alongside him in fourteenth, a full second quicker than teammate Vergne. <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a> was fifteenth, admitting to having locked up his front tyres at the hairpin on his final flying lap.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2012)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Senna" title="Bruno Senna">Bruno Senna</a> qualified sixteenth overall, narrowly ahead of Maldonado.
</p><p>All ten cars took to the circuit for Q3. Vettel qualified on pole, ahead of <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>. <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> was fourth, ahead of <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> who finished fifth. <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> was sixth in his second appearance in Q3 in as many races, ahead of Grosjean in seventh and <a href="/wiki/Paul_di_Resta" title="Paul di Resta">Paul di Resta</a> eighth. After bettering Nico Rosberg's times in Q1 and Q2, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> finished ninth, half a second behind Rosberg. Onboard footage showed Schumacher visibly frustrated with a slower car on his final flying lap, though no action was taken. Jenson Button completed the grid in tenth, having elected to use the soft tyres as opposed to the super-softs used by everyone else.
</p><p>Following his contact with the wall at the end of his seventeenth placed qualifying performance, the Williams team decided to change Pastor Maldonado's gearbox prior to the race. As a result, Maldonado incurred an automatic five place grid penalty and was demoted to twenty-second position for the start.<sup id="cite_ref-Pastor_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pastor-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2012_Canadian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Button,_Raikkonen,_Kobayashi,_Perez,_Hulkenberg_and_Senna_-_2012_Canadian_Grand_Prix.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2c/Button%2C_Raikkonen%2C_Kobayashi%2C_Perez%2C_Hulkenberg_and_Senna_-_2012_Canadian_Grand_Prix.jpg/220px-Button%2C_Raikkonen%2C_Kobayashi%2C_Perez%2C_Hulkenberg_and_Senna_-_2012_Canadian_Grand_Prix.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="151" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2c/Button%2C_Raikkonen%2C_Kobayashi%2C_Perez%2C_Hulkenberg_and_Senna_-_2012_Canadian_Grand_Prix.jpg/330px-Button%2C_Raikkonen%2C_Kobayashi%2C_Perez%2C_Hulkenberg_and_Senna_-_2012_Canadian_Grand_Prix.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2c/Button%2C_Raikkonen%2C_Kobayashi%2C_Perez%2C_Hulkenberg_and_Senna_-_2012_Canadian_Grand_Prix.jpg/440px-Button%2C_Raikkonen%2C_Kobayashi%2C_Perez%2C_Hulkenberg_and_Senna_-_2012_Canadian_Grand_Prix.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3329" data-file-height="2286" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Button,_Raikkonen,_Kobayashi,_Perez,_Hulkenberg_and_Senna_-_2012_Canadian_Grand_Prix.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> leading a train of cars in the opening stages of the race - he eventually finished sixteenth.</div></div></div>
<p>As required by the qualifying regulations, the top 9 qualifiers all started on the faster red-banded super soft tyres, with 10th placed Jenson Button who did not set a time in Q3 choosing to start on the yellow-banded softs. The remaining drivers had a choice of tyres; Kimi Räikkönen, Nico Hülkenberg, Sergio Pérez, Pastor Maldonado and Pedro de la Rosa opted for the softs, the remainder for the super softs.
</p><p>The race got off to a clean start, with Sebastian Vettel taking the early lead from pole. There were no big changes behind him, as Lewis Hamilton, Fernando Alonso, Mark Webber, Nico Rosberg and Felipe Massa all staying in their respective places. Pastor Maldonado made up a few places after starting 22nd. Massa passed Rosberg for fifth soon after, with the latter soon losing another spot to Paul di Resta. Di Resta was unable to keep the pace of the top 5, and a gap formed up between the first 5 and the rest of the pack.
</p><p>Massa had started closing in on Webber after passing Rosberg, but he then spun and dropped down to 12th, at the tail of the second group led by di Resta. At the head of the field, Vettel built a two-second lead in the first four laps over Hamilton, but then Hamilton slowly started reeling him back in as the super softs slowly began to wear out. Before the first round of pit stops, Hamilton closed right up to Vettel and started to get held up, with Fernando Alonso taking the opportunity to join the leading two. The stops started as early as lap 13 for Massa, and on lap 14 for di Resta and Michael Schumacher. Vettel stayed until lap 16 before pitting, handing the lead to Hamilton who came in on the next lap and rejoined ahead of Vettel, despite a slow pitstop.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Sergio_Perez_Canada_2012.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/64/Sergio_Perez_Canada_2012.jpg/220px-Sergio_Perez_Canada_2012.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="107" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/64/Sergio_Perez_Canada_2012.jpg/330px-Sergio_Perez_Canada_2012.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/64/Sergio_Perez_Canada_2012.jpg/440px-Sergio_Perez_Canada_2012.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4524" data-file-height="2208" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Sergio_Perez_Canada_2012.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>After starting in fifteenth place, <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a> conserved his tyres to finish in third place and take the second podium finish of his career.</div></div></div>
<p>Alonso stayed out two laps longer than Hamilton and, helped by a quick pitstop was able to rejoin in second place, ahead of Hamilton and Vettel and only behind temporary leader Romain Grosjean who was yet to pit. Hamilton immediately attacked him in the DRS zone on the same lap and succeeded to take 2nd place, which became the lead two laps later when Grosjean pitted. Webber was unable to keep up with the leading trio in the first stint and thus, after his stop rejoined behind the yet to stop Sergio Pérez and Kimi Räikkönen, and got held up behind the duo. This left the leading trio of Hamilton, Alonso and Vettel with a gap around 15 seconds to the rest of the field, with the McLaren driver being able to edge out a lead over his two pursuers.
</p><p>On lap 24 Narain Karthikeyan was the first retirement after spinning on turn 1 and pulling over shortly afterwards. Shortly afterwards, his HRT teammate Pedro de la Rosa stopped in the pits with worn-out brakes and didn't re-emerge.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:2012_Canadian_Grand_Prix_Fernando_Alonso_Ferrari_F2012-02.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/58/2012_Canadian_Grand_Prix_Fernando_Alonso_Ferrari_F2012-02.jpg/220px-2012_Canadian_Grand_Prix_Fernando_Alonso_Ferrari_F2012-02.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="119" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/58/2012_Canadian_Grand_Prix_Fernando_Alonso_Ferrari_F2012-02.jpg/330px-2012_Canadian_Grand_Prix_Fernando_Alonso_Ferrari_F2012-02.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/58/2012_Canadian_Grand_Prix_Fernando_Alonso_Ferrari_F2012-02.jpg/440px-2012_Canadian_Grand_Prix_Fernando_Alonso_Ferrari_F2012-02.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4132" data-file-height="2232" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:2012_Canadian_Grand_Prix_Fernando_Alonso_Ferrari_F2012-02.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> led for 17 laps of the race, but his tyres degraded near the end and he quickly lost four places, slipping to fifth.</div></div></div>
<p>Räikkönen and Pérez, running 4th and 5th having started on the soft tyres both had a long first stint as they tried to make the race on a single stop. With Webber being held up behind the duo, this allowed both Grosjean and Nico Rosberg to close up on him. Rosberg made his second stop on lap 40, one lap before Räikkönen made his only stop. Räikkönen rejoined in front of Rosberg, but Rosberg was able to pass him in the DRS zone on the same lap, just as Hamilton did on Alonso earlier. Pérez made his stop on lap 42 and rejoined in between the duo, in 8th place. Michael Schumacher's run of bad luck continued when he left the pits on lap 43 with his DRS flap open was running 9th. He returned to the pits but the team were unable to close the flap, forcing his retirement.
</p><p>Hamilton was able to open up a 4-second lead over Alonso, with Vettel being initially held up by the Spaniard, but unable to keep up with him as the Ferrari driver found the grip in his tyres. As the stint wore on, Alonso was able to pull clear of Vettel and close down Hamilton's lead to just over 2 seconds, and Hamilton decided to make a second stop for new tyres. He rejoined in third, 9.5 seconds behind Vettel and 13 behind Alonso, and just ahead of Webber and Grosjean who were fighting for 4th. Both Alonso and Vettel realized that they do not have enough of a gap to make another stop and rejoin ahead of Hamilton, so they stayed out and attempted to complete the race without stopping again in an attempt to win.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Lewis_Hamilton_Wins_Canada_2012.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/55/Lewis_Hamilton_Wins_Canada_2012.jpg/220px-Lewis_Hamilton_Wins_Canada_2012.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="133" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/55/Lewis_Hamilton_Wins_Canada_2012.jpg/330px-Lewis_Hamilton_Wins_Canada_2012.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/55/Lewis_Hamilton_Wins_Canada_2012.jpg/440px-Lewis_Hamilton_Wins_Canada_2012.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2609" data-file-height="1578" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Lewis_Hamilton_Wins_Canada_2012.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> took the race victory to become the seventh different winner from the opening seven rounds of the 2012 season.</div></div></div>
<p>The battles continued to rage behind the leading trio, as Grosjean started to attack 4th placed Webber. The Red Bull driver decided to make a second pitstop for new tyres and rejoined in 8th place, behind Rosberg and Pérez, but ahead of Räikkönen. Felipe Massa, who was unable to regain the places he lost due to his spin, was now back up to 5th place as he too attempted a one-stopper. That did not work out however, and Rosberg had closed right up to him by lap 55, and brought Pérez, Webber and Räikkönen along. Rosberg attacked Massa on the DRS zone, but overshot the last chicane and had to give the position back. Pérez took advantage of this and was right behind Massa, which meant that he too was able to get the jump on Rosberg when he handed the place back to Massa. He then passed Massa on the same lap, with Rosberg also going through, this time doing the job cleanly. The Brazilian had enough, and pitted for a new set of tyres and rejoined in 10th place, where he stayed till the end.
</p><p>Hamilton on his newer tyres was rapidly gaining on Alonso and Vettel at the front at over a second a lap. As the race entered the latter stages, the tyres of Alonso and Vettel began to totally wear out, and their lap times now dropped by 3 seconds a lap. Hamilton passed Vettel on lap 62, and Alonso two laps later to take the lead, but the tyres of the latter two were so worn that they were vulnerable to the rest of the field. Vettel decided to stop for new tyres on lap 64 and rejoined fifth behind Grosjean and Pérez, who had broken away from the battle between Rosberg, Webber and Räikkönen. Alonso decided to stay out on his totally worn tyres and was passed by Grosjean on lap 66, Pérez one lap later, and a charging Vettel on the penultimate lap. Lewis Hamilton had no trouble reeling off the last 6 laps once he had taken the lead and became the 7th different winner in 7 races. Grosjean and Sergio Pérez achieved their 2nd career podium finishes as they finished second and third respectively. Vettel recovered to 4th, only 2 seconds behind Pérez at the flag. A frustrated Alonso dropped to 5th ahead of Rosberg who had an up and down race finishing 6th, fending off Webber and Räikkönen. Kamui Kobayashi finished a respectable 9th place in the other Sauber with Massa getting the final point in 10th.
</p>
