<p>The <b>1995 Pacific Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>II Pacific Grand Prix</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 22 October 1995 at the <a href="/wiki/Okayama_International_Circuit" title="Okayama International Circuit">TI Circuit</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aida,_Okayama" title="Aida, Okayama">Aida</a>, Japan. It was the fifteenth&#160;round of the <a href="/wiki/1995_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="1995 Formula One season">1995 Formula One season</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pg214-215_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg214-215-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The 83-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a> team after starting from third position. <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a>, who started the Grand Prix from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>, finished second in a <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a> car, with <a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a> third in the other Williams.<sup id="cite_ref-formula1.com-officialresult_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-formula1.com-officialresult-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher's win confirmed him as <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">1995 Drivers' Champion</a>, as Hill could not pass Schumacher's points total with only two races remaining.<sup id="cite_ref-gp-schumi_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gp-schumi-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> This was also the last race for <a href="/wiki/Jean-Christophe_Boullion" title="Jean-Christophe Boullion">Jean-Christophe Boullion</a>.
</p><p>Hill started the race alongside Coulthard on the front row, amidst pressure from the British media for not being "forceful" enough in battles.<sup id="cite_ref-walker-hill_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-walker-hill-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher attempted to drive around the outside of Hill at the first corner, but Hill held Schumacher off as <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a>, driving for <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, got past both on the inside line to take second position. As a result, Hill dropped down to third and Schumacher dropped down to fifth behind <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pg214-215_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg214-215-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher then managed to get past Alesi and Hill during the first of three <a href="/wiki/Pit_stop" title="Pit stop">pit stops</a>. This allowed him, on a new set of <a href="/wiki/Slick_tyre" class="mw-redirect" title="Slick tyre">slick tyres</a>, to close on Coulthard who was on a two-stop strategy. Schumacher opened up a gap of 21&#160;seconds by lapping two&#160;seconds faster per lap than Coulthard, so that when his third&#160;stop came, he still led the race.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix-pacific_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-pacific-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1995_Pacific_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1995_Pacific_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The race, originally scheduled to be held as the third round of the <a href="/wiki/1995_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="1995 Formula One season">season</a> on 16 April 1995, was moved to October as the local infrastructure and communications were badly damaged from the <a href="/wiki/Great_Hanshin_earthquake" title="Great Hanshin earthquake">Great Hanshin earthquake</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix-009_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-009-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-sundaytimes_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sundaytimes-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Heading into the 15th race of the season, <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> was leading the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a> with 82&#160;points; <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a> was second on 55&#160;points, 27&#160;points behind Schumacher. A maximum of 30&#160;points were available for the remaining three races, which meant that Hill could still win the title. Schumacher only needed a fourth-place finish to become Drivers' Champion as, even if Hill won, Schumacher would be more than 20&#160;points ahead of Hill with two races remaining. Behind Hill and Schumacher in the Drivers' Championship, <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> was third on 43&#160;points in a Williams, with <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Herbert" title="Johnny Herbert">Johnny Herbert</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a> both on 40&#160;points.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix-322_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-322-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a>, Benetton were leading on 112&#160;points and Williams were second on 92&#160;points, with a maximum of 48&#160;points available.<sup id="cite_ref-pg214-215_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg214-215-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-grandprix-322_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-322-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> In the two weeks leading up to the race, there was heavy criticism towards Damon Hill, with pundits feeling that Hill had not been "forceful" enough in his battle at the <a href="/wiki/1995_European_Grand_Prix" title="1995 European Grand Prix">European Grand Prix</a> against Schumacher.<sup id="cite_ref-walker-hill_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-walker-hill-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-pg209_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg209-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In an interview leading up to the race, part-time <a href="/wiki/Equipe_Ligier" title="Equipe Ligier">Ligier</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Martin_Brundle" title="Martin Brundle">Martin Brundle</a> said:
</p>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r960796168">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>Damon has to do two things. First, he has to establish himself as the No1 at Williams for next year so the team can give him their full support. Second, he has to re-establish himself as a racer. Maybe he needs to lose a front wheel once or twice to re-establish himself.<sup id="cite_ref-times-oct20_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-times-oct20-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Damon_Hill_juillet_1995.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b6/Damon_Hill_juillet_1995.jpg/170px-Damon_Hill_juillet_1995.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="253" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b6/Damon_Hill_juillet_1995.jpg/255px-Damon_Hill_juillet_1995.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b6/Damon_Hill_juillet_1995.jpg/340px-Damon_Hill_juillet_1995.jpg 2x" data-file-width="341" data-file-height="507" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Damon_Hill_juillet_1995.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a>, who was criticised by pundits leading into the race</div></div></div>
<p>Schumacher, his title-rival, said that Hill made "half-hearted attempts" to overtake, which led to him "getting into trouble".<sup id="cite_ref-pg209_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg209-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The comments were prompted after a series of battles between Hill and Schumacher in previous race meetings, most notably at the <a href="/wiki/1995_Belgian_Grand_Prix" title="1995 Belgian Grand Prix">Belgian Grand Prix</a>, where Hill accused Schumacher of blocking him.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix-belgium_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-belgium-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> At a <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile</a> (FIA) <a href="/wiki/FIA_World_Motor_Sport_Council" title="FIA World Motor Sport Council">World Motor Sport Council</a> meeting on October 19 to discuss driver etiquette, they opted against introducing new rules on the issue. Formula One's <a href="/wiki/Sport_governing_body" class="mw-redirect" title="Sport governing body">governing body</a> emphasised that the <a href="/wiki/International_Sporting_Code" title="International Sporting Code">International Sporting Code</a> would be enforced on the basis that drivers are free to drive as they wish "provided they do not deliberately endanger another driver or repeatedly obstruct him on a straight", following incidents during the year involving Hill and Schumacher.<sup id="cite_ref-pg211_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg211-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Williams were favourites to win the race due to the nature of the track—their <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW17" title="Williams FW17">Williams FW17</a> car was more suited to <a href="/wiki/Downforce" title="Downforce">high-downforce</a> tracks like Aida, and thus had the advantage over Benetton. In an attempt to match the pace of the Williams cars, Benetton introduced a revised <a href="/wiki/Rear_suspension" class="mw-redirect" title="Rear suspension">rear suspension</a> geometry to the <a href="/wiki/Benetton_B195" title="Benetton B195">Benetton B195</a> for the race.<sup id="cite_ref-pg209_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg209-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>There were five driver changes heading into the race. Having been in one of the two Ligier cars since the tenth&#160;race of the season at <a href="/wiki/1995_German_Grand_Prix" title="1995 German Grand Prix">Germany</a>, Martin Brundle was replaced by <a href="/wiki/Aguri_Suzuki" title="Aguri Suzuki">Aguri Suzuki</a> as part of the two sharing the drive for the season.<sup id="cite_ref-times-oct20_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-times-oct20-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-grandprix-r_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-r-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Jan_Magnussen" title="Jan Magnussen">Jan Magnussen</a> was drafted into the <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> team to replace <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> because of the Finn's operation for <a href="/wiki/Appendicitis" title="Appendicitis">appendicitis</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix-hakkinenappendicitis_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-hakkinenappendicitis-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The third driver change was <a href="/wiki/Ukyo_Katayama" title="Ukyo Katayama">Ukyo Katayama</a>'s return to <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_Racing" title="Tyrrell Racing">Tyrrell</a> after missing the European Grand Prix due to a crash at the <a href="/wiki/1995_Portuguese_Grand_Prix" title="1995 Portuguese Grand Prix">Portuguese Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix-katayama_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-katayama-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Gianni_Morbidelli" title="Gianni Morbidelli">Gianni Morbidelli</a> returned to the <a href="/wiki/Footwork_Arrows" title="Footwork Arrows">Footwork</a> team replacing <a href="/wiki/Max_Papis" title="Max Papis">Max Papis</a>, while at <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Racing" title="Pacific Racing">Pacific</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bertrand_Gachot" title="Bertrand Gachot">Bertrand Gachot</a> came back to replace <a href="/wiki/Jean-Denis_D%C3%A9l%C3%A9traz" class="mw-redirect" title="Jean-Denis Délétraz">Jean-Denis Délétraz</a>, both men having driven for these teams at the start of the season.<sup id="cite_ref-pg232_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg232-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Délétraz was replaced as he had not made agreed payment instalments to the Pacific team for the privilege of the drive.<sup id="cite_ref-F1Ypg199_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1Ypg199-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Pacific had originally intended to run local driver <a href="/wiki/Katsumi_Yamamoto" title="Katsumi Yamamoto">Katsumi Yamamoto</a> in place of Délétraz, but he was not granted an <a href="/wiki/FIA_Super_Licence" title="FIA Super Licence">FIA Super Licence</a> and so Gachot retook the seat. Similarly, the <a href="/wiki/Forti" title="Forti">Forti</a> team's plans to replace <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Moreno" title="Roberto Moreno">Roberto Moreno</a> with <a href="/wiki/Hideki_Noda" title="Hideki Noda">Hideki Noda</a> came to nought for the same reason, even though Noda had started three Grands Prix for the <a href="/wiki/Larrousse" title="Larrousse">Larrousse</a> team the previous year.<sup id="cite_ref-R&amp;R_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-R&amp;R-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice_and_qualifying">Practice and qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1995_Pacific_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Practice and qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatright" style="width:40%;&#32;;"><style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r887775652">.mw-parser-output .quotebox{background-color:#F9F9F9;border:1px solid #aaa;box-sizing:border-box;padding:10px;font-size:88%;max-width:100%}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft{margin:0.5em 1.4em 0.8em 0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright{margin:0.5em 0 0.8em 1.4em}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.centered{margin:0.5em auto 0.8em auto}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft p,.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright p{font-style:inherit}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-title{background-color:#F9F9F9;text-align:center;font-size:larger;font-weight:bold}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:before{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" “ ";vertical-align:-45%;line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:after{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" ” ";line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .left-aligned{text-align:left}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .right-aligned{text-align:right}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .center-aligned{text-align:center}.mw-parser-output .quotebox cite{display:block;font-style:normal}@media screen and (max-width:360px){.mw-parser-output .quotebox{min-width:100%;margin:0 0 0.8em!important;float:none!important}}</style>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">"For me it was just a case of going out for my last run and trying to do better just in case Michael improved. But it was so close to the end of the session that I had to be out on the circuit; there wasn't time to see if Michael went quicker, then go out and try for a time if he did."</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style=""><a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a>, commenting on using a new set of <a href="/wiki/Slick_tyres" class="mw-redirect" title="Slick tyres">slick tyres</a> at the end of the session.<sup id="cite_ref-pg209_9-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg209-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>Two practice sessions were held before the race; the first was held on Friday morning and the second on Saturday morning. Both sessions lasted 1&#160;hour and 45&#160;minutes with weather conditions dry throughout.<sup id="cite_ref-atlas-pacificmain_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlas-pacificmain-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher set the fastest time in the first session, posting a lap of 1:16.057, three-tenths of a second quicker than Hill and Coulthard, in second and third places respectively. The <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> cars were fourth and fifth fastest; <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> ahead of <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a>, with McLaren driver <a href="/wiki/Mark_Blundell" title="Mark Blundell">Mark Blundell</a> rounding out the top six.<sup id="cite_ref-pg214-215_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg214-215-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard lapped faster than Schumacher in the second practice session with a time of 1:15.730. Hill was second in the Williams, three-tenths of a second behind Coulthard. <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a> car was fourth, seven-tenths behind Coulthard. He was split by the Ferrari cars who were third and fifth; Alesi in front of Berger. The Benetton cars were sixth and seventh; Schumacher in front of Herbert. <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jean-Christophe_Boullion" title="Jean-Christophe Boullion">Jean-Christophe Boullion</a> were eighth and tenth in the <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a> cars, with <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> ninth in the Jordan, two&#160;seconds off the pace.<sup id="cite_ref-pg214-215_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg214-215-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:David_Coulthard_2007.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d8/David_Coulthard_2007.jpg/170px-David_Coulthard_2007.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="250" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d8/David_Coulthard_2007.jpg/255px-David_Coulthard_2007.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d8/David_Coulthard_2007.jpg/340px-David_Coulthard_2007.jpg 2x" data-file-width="500" data-file-height="734" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:David_Coulthard_2007.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a>, who took pole position in his <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a> car (picture taken in <a href="/wiki/2007_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2007 Formula One World Championship">2007</a>, while driving at <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a>)</div></div></div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/List_of_motorsport_terminology#Q" class="mw-redirect" title="List of motorsport terminology">qualifying session</a> was split into two one-hour sessions; the first was held on Friday afternoon with the second held on Saturday afternoon. The fastest time from either sessions counted towards their final <a href="/wiki/Standing_start" title="Standing start">grid position</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-atlas-pacificmain_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlas-pacificmain-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard clinched his fourth consecutive <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>, in his Williams, with a time of 1:14.013.<sup id="cite_ref-pg214-215_1-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg214-215-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-formula1.com-coulthard1995_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-formula1.com-coulthard1995-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> He was joined on the front row by teammate Hill, who was two-tenths of a second behind.<sup id="cite_ref-pg214-215_1-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg214-215-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher was third in the Benetton, edging closer to the Williams drivers throughout both days of qualifying by steadily reducing the <a href="/wiki/Downforce" title="Downforce">downforce</a> on his car.  His last run, right at the end of the second session, pressured Coulthard into leaving the pit lane to cover the German. The pole position man thus used an additional set of <a href="/wiki/Slick_tyres" class="mw-redirect" title="Slick tyres">slick tyres</a>, meaning that out of the allocation of seven sets of slick tyres as set out by the FIA, he had only two sets of brand-new rubber for the race, whereas Schumacher had the advantage of three sets of new rubber. Hill, concerned about starting from the dirty side of the track (the side of the track that is opposite to the <a href="/wiki/Racing_line" title="Racing line">racing line</a>), had only two sets of brand new tyres for the race.<sup id="cite_ref-pg209_9-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg209-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Berger took fourth despite going off into the <a href="/wiki/Run-off_area" title="Run-off area">gravel</a> late in the second part of qualifying.<sup id="cite_ref-atlas-pacific2ndquali_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-atlas-pacific2ndquali-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Berger's teammate Alesi was fifth, with Irvine completing the top six for his best qualifying position of the season. Rookie Magnussen qualified 12th, only two places behind teammate Blundell, after not making a mistake in either of the two sessions.<sup id="cite_ref-pg210_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg210-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Returning drivers Suzuki, Katayama, Morbidelli and Gachot qualified 13th, 17th, 19th and 24th respectively, with the grid covered by 7.392&#160;seconds.<sup id="cite_ref-pg214-215_1-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg214-215-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1995_Pacific_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The conditions for the race were dry with the <a href="/wiki/Air_temperature" class="mw-redirect" title="Air temperature">air temperature</a> 21&#160;°C (70&#160;°F).<sup id="cite_ref-pg214-215_1-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg214-215-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The drivers took to the track at 09:30&#160;<a href="/wiki/Japan_Standard_Time" title="Japan Standard Time">JST</a> (<a href="/wiki/Greenwich_Mean_Time" title="Greenwich Mean Time">GMT</a> +9) for a 30-minute warm-up session. Both Williams cars maintained their good performance from qualifying; Coulthard had the fastest time of 1:16.831. Hill was third in the other Williams car; Boullion split them in the Sauber for second position. <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Panis" title="Olivier Panis">Olivier Panis</a> completed the top four in a <a href="/wiki/Equipe_Ligier" title="Equipe Ligier">Ligier</a> car, eight-tenths of a second behind Hill.<sup id="cite_ref-pg214-215_1-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg214-215-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Hill drove the spare Williams car along the inside of the start–finish straight in an effort to clean up his grid position on the dusty side of the track.<sup id="cite_ref-pg209_9-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg209-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher finished the session in eighth, despite going off the track, damaging his race car in the process.<sup id="cite_ref-bbc_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> The remote and inaccessible nature of the circuit and the fact that the <a href="/wiki/1995_Japanese_Grand_Prix" title="1995 Japanese Grand Prix">Japanese Grand Prix</a> took place just one week later resulted in a meagre race-day crowd of only 15,000 spectators.<sup id="cite_ref-F1Ypg198_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1Ypg198-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The race started at 14:00&#160;JST. Coulthard, from pole position on the grid, held onto the lead into the first corner. Hill, who started alongside Coulthard, had a bad start. Schumacher attempted to go around the outside of Hill at the first corner, but Hill held Schumacher off. Both drivers ran off the racing line in the process, allowing Alesi through into second place.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix-pacific_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-pacific-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> At the end of lap&#160;one, Coulthard led Alesi by 2.8&#160;seconds, with Hill a further three-tenths back. Berger was fourth, with Schumacher demoted to fifth.<sup id="cite_ref-bbc-1_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc-1-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Bertrand Gachot in the Pacific became the first person to retire from the race with a gearbox problem on lap&#160;two.<sup id="cite_ref-formula1.com-officialresult_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-formula1.com-officialresult-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He was followed by Suzuki and Boullion, who both spun off the track and were unable to continue.<sup id="cite_ref-pg214-215_1-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg214-215-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Boullion blamed his spin on <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Lamy" title="Pedro Lamy">Pedro Lamy</a>, whom he accused of weaving in front of him.<sup id="cite_ref-pg211_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg211-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher passed Berger for fourth position on lap&#160;five, and immediately began closing on Hill in third, who himself was only a few tenths behind Alesi. Schumacher attempted to pass Hill on lap&#160;11 for third place at the <a href="/wiki/Hairpin_bend#Motorsports" class="mw-redirect" title="Hairpin bend">hairpin</a>, but Hill held him off.<sup id="cite_ref-pg214-215_1-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg214-215-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-pg211_12-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg211-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> As Hill and Schumacher were held up by the slower Ferrari cars, Coulthard pulled away by more than a second a lap in the first eight&#160;laps.<sup id="cite_ref-pg210_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg210-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> By lap&#160;18, Coulthard's gap to second place was 14&#160;seconds, and it appeared that he would win the race comfortably.<sup id="cite_ref-pg211_12-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg211-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Michael_Schumacher_1994_San_Marino_Gran_Prix.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2a/Michael_Schumacher_1994_San_Marino_Gran_Prix.jpg/170px-Michael_Schumacher_1994_San_Marino_Gran_Prix.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="259" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/2/2a/Michael_Schumacher_1994_San_Marino_Gran_Prix.jpg 1.5x" data-file-width="200" data-file-height="305" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Michael_Schumacher_1994_San_Marino_Gran_Prix.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>, who was crowned <a href="/wiki/1995_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="1995 Formula One season">1995 Formula One Drivers' Champion</a> as a result of his win (pictured in 1994)</div></div></div>
<p>Alesi, Hill and Schumacher all made pit stops for their first of three&#160;stops on lap&#160;18. The Benetton <a href="/wiki/Pit_stop#F1_pit_crew_roles" title="Pit stop">pitcrew</a> made a quick stop for Schumacher, allowing him to get out ahead of Alesi and Hill.<sup id="cite_ref-pg209_9-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg209-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Hill lost additional time with a sticking refuelling valve, causing his stop to last almost twice as long as Schumacher's.<sup id="cite_ref-pg211_12-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg211-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher exited the pit stop in fourth place (behind Coulthard, and the yet-to-stop Berger and Herbert), with Alesi in seventh place (split from Schumacher by Irvine) and Hill in tenth place (separated from Alesi by Frentzen and Blundell).<sup id="cite_ref-pg214-215_1-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg214-215-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> With Alesi and Hill held up by the slower runners on two-stop strategies in front, Schumacher pulled away and closed in on Coulthard. Blundell made a pit stop on the next lap, and Hill passed Frentzen on lap&#160;22.<sup id="cite_ref-pg214-215_1-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg214-215-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> On the following lap, Alesi passed Irvine at the hairpin; Hill tried to follow Alesi through but his <a href="/wiki/Downforce#Front" title="Downforce">front wing</a> hit the rear of Irvine's car, causing minor damage.<sup id="cite_ref-review_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-review-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-times-oct23-1_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-times-oct23-1-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> Irvine made a pit stop at the end of lap&#160;25, allowing Hill to resume his chase of Alesi unimpeded.<sup id="cite_ref-pg214-215_1-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg214-215-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Like his teammate, Coulthard was scheduled to make three&#160;stops, but his <a href="/wiki/Pit_stop#General_strategy" title="Pit stop">pit strategy</a> was changed to make only two by staying out six&#160;laps longer than originally scheduled, and then taking onboard more fuel than first planned at his first&#160;stop on lap&#160;24.<sup id="cite_ref-pg209_9-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg209-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> As a result of a lighter fuel load for Schumacher because of the different strategies, Schumacher began to consistently lap faster than the Scotsman.<sup id="cite_ref-review_26-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-review-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Schumacher made his second&#160;stop on lap&#160;38, and came out of the pit lane just in front of third-placed Alesi, but over twenty&#160;seconds behind Coulthard. Schumacher immediately began setting <a href="/wiki/Fastest_lap" title="Fastest lap">fastest laps</a> and began to close in on Coulthard once more.<sup id="cite_ref-pg214-215_1-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg214-215-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-review_26-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-review-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Hill managed to move up to third, in front of Alesi, during their second&#160;pit stops on laps&#160;38&#160;and&#160;39 respectively. The Ferrari of Alesi then dropped further back as teammate Berger passed him at the hairpin for fourth position on lap&#160;45.<sup id="cite_ref-pg214-215_1-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg214-215-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard made his second and final stop for new tyres on lap&#160;49; exiting 14&#160;seconds behind Schumacher, who continued to extend the margin between the two. Coulthard was unable to capitalise on the performance advantage offered by the new tyres after the stop due to <a href="/wiki/Racing_flags#Blue_flag" title="Racing flags">lapped traffic</a> getting in his way.<sup id="cite_ref-times-oct23-1_27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-times-oct23-1-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-pg212_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg212-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> The German made his third and final pit stop on lap&#160;60 with a 21-second advantage, exiting in front of Coulthard to lead the race.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix-pacific_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-pacific-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-review_26-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-review-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher opened the gap to 15&#160;seconds, and won the race after 83&#160;laps to secure his eighth victory of the season in a time of 1:48:49.972s.<sup id="cite_ref-pg214-215_1-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg214-215-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher was crowned the 1995 Drivers' Champion as Hill could not catch his points total with two races remaining.<sup id="cite_ref-gp-schumi_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gp-schumi-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He also became the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_driver_records#Other_driver_records" title="List of Formula One driver records">youngest double Drivers' Champion</a> in Formula One history.<sup id="cite_ref-review_26-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-review-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard finished second in his Williams, 14&#160;seconds behind Schumacher, with teammate Hill third. The Ferrari cars of Berger and Alesi were fourth and fifth respectively, but off the pace as Schumacher lapped them both in the closing stages. Berger suffered from a misfiring engine throughout the race.<sup id="cite_ref-pg212_28-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg212-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Herbert took the final point in sixth&#160;place for Benetton, ahead of Frentzen, Panis and Blundell. Throughout the race, Barrichello and Magnussen engaged in a battle for tenth and eleventh positions, with Magnussen keeping Barrichello behind until lap&#160;37 when the Brazilian managed to overtake him into the hairpin. Magnussen finished in tenth place, but Barrichello subsequently retired on lap&#160;67 with an engine problem.<sup id="cite_ref-pg214-215_1-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg214-215-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Magnussen's first race was described as "highly accomplished" by the year's <i><a href="/wiki/Autocourse" title="Autocourse">Autocourse</a></i> annual.<sup id="cite_ref-pg211_12-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg211-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> After his impressive qualifying performance, Irvine was heading for eighth place, but made an unscheduled pit stop on lap&#160;72 and dropped to eleventh. The attrition rate was low, with 17 of the 24 starters finishing the race.<sup id="cite_ref-pg214-215_1-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg214-215-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1995_Pacific_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After the race, it was revealed that Schumacher endured a <a href="/wiki/Transmission_(mechanics)" title="Transmission (mechanics)">downshift</a> problem after his final stop, and that he was lucky to complete the final&#160;lap as warning lights had activated on his steering wheel.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix-pacific_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-pacific-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher praised his pitcrew for doing a "perfect" first&#160;stop which helped him move in front of Alesi and Hill. He said he "never saw anything like this team and its ability to come up with strategies" and that they never made "one mistake this season".<sup id="cite_ref-pg211_12-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg211-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Off-camera while going through <a href="/wiki/Parc_ferm%C3%A9" title="Parc fermé">parc fermé</a>, Schumacher and Hill renewed their argument from the <a href="/wiki/1995_Belgian_Grand_Prix" title="1995 Belgian Grand Prix">Belgian Grand Prix</a> over what degree of blocking was acceptable after their first corner incident.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprix-pacific_5-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprix-pacific-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher told Hill that he was unhappy with Hill's driving throughout the race, most notably during Schumacher's overtaking attempts on lap&#160;one and lap&#160;eleven where Schumacher felt Hill had "brake tested" him.<sup id="cite_ref-times-oct23-1_27-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-times-oct23-1-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> Hill refuted Schumacher's claims, saying:
</p>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r960796168"/><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>Michael wasn't happy with what I did a couple of times in the race and he has told me that he is unhappy with my driving. I find that extraordinary. The situation now is that we are completely free to drive as we like as long as it is not deliberately dangerous, So I drove in that style and he didn't like it. He should have no complaints&#160;... somehow or other, when we got into the braking area at the end of the back straight I did something wrong. But I can't see what I did wrong. It seems that there is one rule for him and another for everybody else at times. I just think that either you agree to that, and there should be no complaints, or there are rules and you should stick to them. I think that I am a better, stronger driver this year than I was last year and can build on that for next year. Clearly Michael has an advantage over everyone and if I want to win, then I am going to have to overhaul him.<sup id="cite_ref-pg211_12-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg211-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>
<p>Despite Hill's comments, he endured continued criticism by the British media after the poor performance; speculation brewed that Williams were going to replace him with Frentzen for the 1996 season.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Despite the rumours, Williams team boss <a href="/wiki/Frank_Williams_(Formula_One)" title="Frank Williams (Formula One)">Frank Williams</a> gave Hill "an unequivocal vote of confidence" heading into the next race, the <a href="/wiki/1995_Japanese_Grand_Prix" title="1995 Japanese Grand Prix">Japanese Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pg218_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg218-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher subsequently changed his opinion of the incident after watching video footage prior to the Japanese race and no longer blamed Hill for it.<sup id="cite_ref-pg218_30-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg218-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During an interview Coulthard, who finished second, revealed that it was his decision to change to a two-stop strategy from a three-stop strategy, telling the Williams pitcrew to delay his stop. Afterwards, he said that in hindsight he would have stayed on a three-stop strategy, and wished he could "blame someone else for this decision, but I can't".<sup id="cite_ref-pg211_12-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pg211-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The 1995 race was the last held at the Aida circuit, and the last Formula One race to date held under the Pacific Grand Prix banner, with the manager of the <a href="/wiki/Okayama_International_Circuit" title="Okayama International Circuit">TI Circuit</a> unable to keep the venue financially profitable.<sup id="cite_ref-sundaytimes_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sundaytimes-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-1996calendar_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1996calendar-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
