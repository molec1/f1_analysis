<p>The <b>1997 Argentine Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at <a href="/wiki/Aut%C3%B3dromo_Juan_y_Oscar_G%C3%A1lvez" title="Autódromo Juan y Oscar Gálvez">Autódromo Oscar Alfredo Gálvez</a> in <a href="/wiki/Buenos_Aires" title="Buenos Aires">Buenos Aires</a>, <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a> on 13 April 1997. It was the third race of the <a href="/wiki/1997_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1997 Formula One World Championship">1997 Formula One World Championship</a>, and the 600th World Championship Grand Prix.
</p><p>The 72-lap race was won from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> by <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW19" title="Williams FW19">Williams</a>-<a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>. <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a> finished second in a <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_F310" title="Ferrari F310">Ferrari</a>, while <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a>, in only his third F1 race, finished third in a <a href="/wiki/Jordan_197" title="Jordan 197">Jordan</a>-<a href="/wiki/Peugeot" title="Peugeot">Peugeot</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Summary">Summary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1997_Argentine_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Summary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-Race">Pre-Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1997_Argentine_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Pre-Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Most of the talk before the grand prix was about <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a> and their poor starts to the season. Despite going out of business, <a href="/wiki/Mastercard_Lola" class="mw-redirect" title="Mastercard Lola">Lola</a> Team Principal <a href="/wiki/Eric_Broadley" title="Eric Broadley">Eric Broadley</a> was confident that the team could be up and running again by the <a href="/wiki/San_Marino_Grand_Prix" title="San Marino Grand Prix">San Marino Grand Prix</a>, albeit with a new main sponsor. The <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_Racing" title="Tyrrell Racing">Tyrrell</a> team also caused a stir after arriving at the grand prix with four new wings on the car, two on the nose cone, the other two alongside the drivers head. They resembled <a href="/wiki/X-wing" class="mw-redirect" title="X-wing">x-wings</a> and this was soon used as their nickname. The practice session bought no surprises with the two Williams cars first and second.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1997_Argentine_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As with the practice session, both Williams were once again on the front row, with <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a> on pole. The first real surprise of the weekend was the performance of <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Panis" title="Olivier Panis">Olivier Panis</a> in the Prost, who managed to qualify third on the grid. Another strong performance was the fifth place of Rubens Barrichello in the <a href="/wiki/Stewart_Grand_Prix" title="Stewart Grand Prix">Stewart</a>. As expected, both McLarens qualified low down the field, complaining of poor handling on the bumpy surface. The slowest qualifier Pedro Diniz's lap time would have still been comfortably fast enough to have put him on pole for the previous year's race.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1997_Argentine_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As the red lights went out, Jacques Villeneuve cleanly navigated the first corner and began to pull away from the other front runners. Michael Schumacher (who was unsighted by oil from Frentzen's car) almost collided with Panis, but the Frenchman moved off line to drop a few places rather than to collide with the Ferrari. Going into the first corner, Michael Schumacher was alongside his teammate Irvine and Rubens Barrichello. However, Michael then understeered and crashed into the back of Barrichello's car causing the Brazilian to spin and getting himself to retire. Barrichello would eventually change his front nose and get going again. But in the melee, Coulthard crashed into the back of Ralf Schumacher's Jordan, ripping of his front wheel and putting him out of the race. The track was unpassable with the back of the field taking to the grass to get round the incident, so the <a href="/wiki/Safety_Car" class="mw-redirect" title="Safety Car">Safety Car</a> was called out. At the restart after 4 laps, Villeneuve once again pulled away from Frentzen and Panis, but Frentzen's race ended on lap 6 with throttle problems. The chance of victory for <a href="/wiki/Prost_Grand_Prix" title="Prost Grand Prix">Prost</a> ended on lap 18, also with throttle problems. On lap 24 the two Jordans collided with <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> retiring from the race. Hill, who was up to 4th at one point, retired with engine failure on lap 33. Although in the final few laps Irvine closed the gap to Villeneuve, the Canadian held tight and went on to win his sixth career grand prix. Irvine finished second, the highest finish of his career at that point, with <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> scoring his first podium in third.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1997_Argentine_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On the podium the organisers mistakenly flew the <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Ireland" title="Flag of Ireland">Irish tricolour</a> for Irvine, instead of the <a href="/wiki/Union_Jack" title="Union Jack">Union Jack</a> that was usually flown for Northern Irish drivers. Although Irvine identifies himself as Irish despite holding a British passport, his parents' house in <a href="/wiki/County_Down" title="County Down">County Down</a> was targeted by <a href="/wiki/Ulster_loyalists" class="mw-redirect" title="Ulster loyalists">loyalists</a> as they felt they had been betrayed by the Ferrari driver. Irvine subsequently asked for a white flag with a shamrock to be used if he secured another podium finish; however, the FIA insisted that the Union Jack be used in future.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
