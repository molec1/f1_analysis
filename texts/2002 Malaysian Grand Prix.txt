<p>The <b>2002 Malaysian Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 17 March 2002 at the <a href="/wiki/Sepang_International_Circuit" title="Sepang International Circuit">Sepang International Circuit</a> and was the second round of the <a href="/wiki/2002_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2002 Formula One season">2002 Formula One season</a>. The Grand Prix is notable for the first lap collision between <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a> and <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari's</a> <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>, which led to the <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">Colombian</a> being given a drive through penalty. This decision led to much criticism for the <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysian</a> stewards, with the <i>victim</i> of the incident, Schumacher, commenting that the decision was "overly harsh" on Montoya.
</p><p>The Grand Prix was won by the Williams-<a href="/wiki/BMW_in_Formula_One" title="BMW in Formula One">BMW</a> of <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">German</a> <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a>, who commented that it was "almost a perfect race". Ralf was followed by his teammate, Juan Pablo Montoya and following them in third was Ralf's brother, Michael Schumacher.
</p><p>The race was followed by a decision by the <a href="/wiki/FIA" class="mw-redirect" title="FIA">FIA</a> to give stewards more powers over the punishments of drivers who are involved in incidents which are deemed "avoidable".
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2002_Malaysian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-Race">Pre-Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2002_Malaysian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Pre-Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice">Practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2002_Malaysian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The Friday morning session saw the two <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferraris</a> of <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> fastest, the closest car to the Ferraris was <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a>, who was over one and a half seconds slower. <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> got the better of Ferrari for the second session with <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> fastest and his teammate <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> was just over half a second behind him, while the Ferrari of Schumacher was over a second slower than Räikkönen's McLaren in third.
</p><p>Like the Friday sessions, the Saturday sessions were split into two separate sessions, although both sessions were held during the morning. The Ferraris were fastest in the first session, although this time the closest non-Ferrari driver, which was Räikkönen, was under a second behind them. The <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> of <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Montoya</a> was fastest in the final practice session before qualifying with Räikkönen second fastest and Montoya's teammate, Ralf Schumacher, third fastest.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2002_Malaysian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Qualifying saw Michael Schumacher take his first <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> of the <a href="/wiki/2002_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2002 Formula One season">2002 season</a>, with only the next three drivers being under a second slower than Schumacher's time, the closest being Juan Pablo Montoya who was under a quarter of a second slower. Schumacher's teammate, Barrichello and Montoya's teammate, <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a>, were third and fourth respectively.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2002_Malaysian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>Before the <a href="/wiki/Parade_lap" title="Parade lap">parade lap</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Arrows_Grand_Prix_International" title="Arrows Grand Prix International">Arrows</a> of <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a> stalled on the grid for the second time in succession. The <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">German</a> got his car running and managed retain his grid position.
</p><p>The start of the race saw Michael Schumacher immediately cut Montoya, making the <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">Colombian</a> brake and avoid a collision, this allowed Barrichello to get alongside Montoya coming into the first corner. Montoya however managed to out-brake the <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazilian</a> into the first corner and thus was alongside Schumacher going through the corner. As the <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> and <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> travelled through the corner, Schumacher's Ferrari started to experience <a href="/wiki/Understeer" class="mw-redirect" title="Understeer">understeer</a> and went into Montoya's car, taking the Ferrari front wing off. The incident left Montoya going wide and going off the track for a brief moment.
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Montoya_Schumacher_Malaysia2002.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/8/83/Montoya_Schumacher_Malaysia2002.jpg/200px-Montoya_Schumacher_Malaysia2002.jpg" decoding="async" width="200" height="130" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/8/83/Montoya_Schumacher_Malaysia2002.jpg/300px-Montoya_Schumacher_Malaysia2002.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/8/83/Montoya_Schumacher_Malaysia2002.jpg 2x" data-file-width="391" data-file-height="255" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Montoya_Schumacher_Malaysia2002.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>On board with <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a> during his first lap collision with <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>. The graphics in the bottom left show the <a href="/wiki/Revolutions_per_minute" title="Revolutions per minute">RPM</a> the <a href="/wiki/BMW_in_Formula_One" title="BMW in Formula One">BMW</a> engine is producing.</div></div></div>
<p>This left Barrichello in the lead with Schumacher Ferrari, minus a front-wing, in second place and his brother <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf</a> in third. Michael Schumacher came into the pits for a new nose, promoting Ralf to second. The second lap saw a collision between the two <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordans</a> of <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> and <a href="/wiki/Takuma_Sato" title="Takuma Sato">Takuma Sato</a>, when the latter ran into the back of the former. "I am really sorry about what happened," said Sato after the race, "I apologized to Giancarlo immediately after the race and he took it very well which I think says a lot of good things about his character" he added.<sup id="cite_ref-GP_Encyclopedia_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GP_Encyclopedia-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> On lap 7, Montoya was given a drive through penalty which cost the Colombian 20 seconds to Barrichello's lead, it was the first drive through penalty in Formula One, as the penalty was added to the list of penalties for the stewards.<sup id="cite_ref-GP_Encyclopedia_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GP_Encyclopedia-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The race at the front was between Barrichello; Ralf Schumacher and the two <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLarens</a> of Räikkönen and <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a>. The two McLarens soon dropped out, both retirements were due to an engine failure. This left the Ferrari and the Williams to battle for the lead, albeit on different strategies, with Barrichello on two stops and Ralf on one-stop. Barrichello was 21 seconds ahead but rejoined 5 seconds behind after his second stop on lap 35. He then closed in on Ralf at a second a lap, and was just 1.2 seconds behind when his engine failed on lap 39, leaving Ralf over 30 seconds ahead of the field with <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> second, with Montoya and Schumacher third and fifth in their recovery drives, with <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> between them.
</p>

The <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">German</a> described his win in Malaysia as almost "a perfect race".<sup id="cite_ref-GP_Encyclopedia_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GP_Encyclopedia-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup></div></div></div>
<p>Montoya got past Button, despite the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">Brit's</a> defensive driving and moved up into second behind his teammate. The Williams pair went on to finish first and second, making it the first Williams 1-2 since the <a href="/wiki/1996_Portuguese_Grand_Prix" title="1996 Portuguese Grand Prix">1996 Portuguese Grand Prix</a>.
</p><p>Button, who was now demoted to third, look on course to take his first podium finish of his F1 career, but a suspension problem gave the Ferrari of Michael Schumacher the last podium finish, leaving Button to take fourth place, his first points scoring finish for his new team, <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>. The final two points positions are taken by the two <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Saubers</a> of <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Heidfeld</a> and <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Massa</a>; it was the first points scoring finish for Massa.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-Race">Post-Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2002_Malaysian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Post-Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The decision to penalise Montoya was met with a host of criticism. The head steward at the event, Nazir Hoosein, had a history of making strange decisions, as he was head steward at the <a href="/wiki/1998_British_Grand_Prix" title="1998 British Grand Prix">1998 British Grand Prix</a>, where Michael Schumacher won the race in the pitlane, which led to Hoosein losing his license to be a steward for a year.<sup id="cite_ref-GP_Encyclopedia_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GP_Encyclopedia-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Michael Schumacher later commented that he thought it was "overly harsh" on Montoya for the incident,<sup id="cite_ref-GP_Encyclopedia_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GP_Encyclopedia-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> while Montoya himself commented that it was "very unfair" and that he "gave Michael room" and commented that Schumacher "understeered and touched him" before adding that "it was a racing incident".<sup id="cite_ref-GP_Encyclopedia_2-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GP_Encyclopedia-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Championship wise, Michael Schumacher's lead in the Drivers' Championship decreased by two points while the Williams team moved up to take the lead in the Constructors' Championship with 22 points to Ferrari's 14.
</p>
