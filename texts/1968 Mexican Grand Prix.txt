<p>The <b>1968 Mexican Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at the <a href="/wiki/Aut%C3%B3dromo_Hermanos_Rodr%C3%ADguez" title="Autódromo Hermanos Rodríguez">Ciudad Deportiva Magdalena Mixhuca</a> on November 3, 1968. It was race 12 of 12 in both the <a href="/wiki/1968_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1968 World Championship of Drivers">1968 World Championship of Drivers</a> and the <a href="/wiki/1968_International_Cup_for_Formula_One_Manufacturers" class="mw-redirect" title="1968 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers">1968 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers</a>.
</p><p>This race was to determine the World Drivers' Championship, contested between Britons Graham Hill in the <a href="/wiki/Lotus_49" title="Lotus 49">Lotus 49</a>B-<a href="/wiki/Ford_Motor_Company" title="Ford Motor Company">Ford</a> and Jackie Stewart in the <a href="/wiki/Matra_MS10" title="Matra MS10">Matra MS10</a>-Ford, and defending champion, New Zealander <a href="/wiki/Denny_Hulme" title="Denny Hulme">Denny Hulme</a> in the <a href="/wiki/McLaren_M7A" title="McLaren M7A">McLaren M7A</a>-Ford. The race was moved back a week so as not to clash with the <a href="/wiki/1968_Summer_Olympics" title="1968 Summer Olympics">Mexico City Summer Olympics</a>, which ended on October 26.
</p><p>Hulme started with a mathematical chance of becoming world champion, but his McLaren broke a rear suspension member early, crashed, and caught fire.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Jo_Siffert" title="Jo Siffert">Jo Siffert</a> took the lead, but had to pit with a broken throttle cable.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>  Stewart fell back with when his engine started to misfire, his car's handling began going off, and had a fuel-feed problem.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Hill won this race and his second Drivers' Championship, after Stewart fell back to seventh after an engine problem with his Matra.<sup id="cite_ref-Kettlewell,_p.1332_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kettlewell,_p.1332-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Mexican government's effort to curb civil unrest led to a switch from military police to unarmed policemen and track marshals for crowd control; by race end, spectators were encroaching on the track itself.<sup id="cite_ref-Kettlewell,_p.1332_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kettlewell,_p.1332-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> This was one reason for the ultimate cancellation of future Mexican <i>Grands Prix</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Kettlewell,_p.1332_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kettlewell,_p.1332-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1968_Mexican_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">Pole position</a>: Jo Siffert - 1:45.22</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Fastest_lap" title="Fastest lap">Fastest lap</a>: Jo Siffert - 1:44.23 on lap 52</li>
<li>Coming into this race, 3 drivers had a chance to win the title, making Hill the only driver to be involved in two 3-way battles.
<ul><li>Graham Hill (39pts) needed either
<ul><li>A podium place, with Stewart 2nd or lower</li>
<li>4th or 5th, with Hulme 2nd or lower and Stewart 3rd or lower</li>
<li>6th, with Hulme 2nd or lower and Stewart 4th or lower</li>
<li>Hulme 2nd or lower and Stewart 5th or lower</li></ul></li>
<li>Jackie Stewart (36pts) needed either
<ul><li>1st</li>
<li>2nd, with Hulme 3rd or lower and Hill 4th or lower</li>
<li>3rd, with Hulme 2nd or lower and Hill 6th or lower</li>
<li>4th, with Hulme 3rd or lower and Hill 7th or lower</li></ul></li>
<li>Denny Hulme (33pts) needed
<ul><li>1st with Hill 4th or lower</li></ul></li></ul></li>
<li>This was Jackie Oliver's first podium finish.</li></ul>
