<p>The <b>1994 German Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held on 31 July 1994 at the <a href="/wiki/Hockenheimring" title="Hockenheimring">Hockenheimring</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hockenheim" title="Hockenheim">Hockenheim</a>. It was the ninth race of the <a href="/wiki/1994_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1994 Formula One World Championship">1994 Formula One World Championship</a>.
</p><p>The 45-lap race was won from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> by Austrian driver <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_412_T1" title="Ferrari 412 T1">Ferrari</a>. Berger achieved the first victory for the Ferrari team since the <a href="/wiki/1990_Spanish_Grand_Prix" title="1990 Spanish Grand Prix">1990 Spanish Grand Prix</a>, some 59 races previously. In a race of high attrition, including eleven retirements on the opening lap, only eight cars finished, with French drivers <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Panis" title="Olivier Panis">Olivier Panis</a> and <a href="/wiki/%C3%89ric_Bernard" title="Éric Bernard">Éric Bernard</a> second and third respectively in their <a href="/wiki/Ligier_JS39" title="Ligier JS39">Ligier</a>-<a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renaults</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1994_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The fast Hockenheim circuit had been modified from the year before, with the fast Senna chicane being made slower.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferraris</a> qualified first and second, with <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> in <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a> lining up alongside.
</p><p>The race was notable for its high rate of attrition, with 11 retirements on the opening lap. Within ten seconds of the start <a href="/wiki/Alex_Zanardi" title="Alex Zanardi">Alessandro Zanardi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Andrea_de_Cesaris" title="Andrea de Cesaris">Andrea de Cesaris</a> tangled towards the back of the pack, taking out both <a href="/wiki/Michele_Alboreto" title="Michele Alboreto">Michele Alboreto</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pierluigi_Martini" title="Pierluigi Martini">Pierluigi Martini</a> before even reaching the first corner. <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> and <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> then tangled going into the first corner, the Finn sliding in front of a group of cars into the wall on the outside of the circuit while the Scot continued to the pits to replace his front wing. <a href="/wiki/Mark_Blundell" title="Mark Blundell">Mark Blundell</a> braked hard to avoid the McLaren only to be hit from behind by <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a>, while <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> had nowhere to go but the gravel. In the melee behind this incident, <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Herbert" title="Johnny Herbert">Johnny Herbert</a> and <a href="/wiki/Martin_Brundle" title="Martin Brundle">Martin Brundle</a> tangled, the Lotus spinning to a halt while the second McLaren continued. <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a> was also pushed into the gravel and managed to continue, but stopped towards the end of the lap with broken suspension and a punctured tyre. <a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a> also damaged his suspension in a first-lap contact with <a href="/wiki/Ukyo_Katayama" title="Ukyo Katayama">Ukyo Katayama</a> and, as a result, circulated outside the points for the remainder of the race. This result would have significant consequences for Hill at the end of the season. In a race where the 12th and 14th placed Ligiers finished on the podium, and the remaining points going to customer Ford HB engined teams on a circuit where power was a distinct advantage; the best Ford HB time in qualifying some 3.235 off pole position, it is clear that any points finish for Hill could have led to a very different result in the Drivers World Championship. Meanwhile <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a> had gotten away unscathed, having qualified second, only for his Ferrari to stop with electrical problems on the run to the first chicane.
</p><p>It was a bad weekend for the Benetton team. After the first lap mayhem, Schumacher went on to take on the leading Ferrari of Gerhard Berger but fell away with engine problems very quickly. Benetton driver Jos Verstappen also came into the pits; while refuelling, some fuel was accidentally sprayed onto the hot bodywork of the car, and a few seconds later the fuel ignited and Verstappen's car was engulfed in a ball of flames. The Dutchman escaped the incident with burns around his eyes, as he had his visor up during the pit stop. No other crew members or any persons were injured severely or killed.
</p><p>As well as Ferrari winning its first and only race of the 1994 Formula One season, the race was especially good for Ligier with <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Panis" title="Olivier Panis">Olivier Panis</a> finishing second and <a href="/wiki/%C3%89ric_Bernard" title="Éric Bernard">Éric Bernard</a> coming home third.
</p>
