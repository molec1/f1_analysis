<p>The <b>1959 French Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at <a href="/wiki/Reims-Gueux" title="Reims-Gueux">Reims</a> on 5 July 1959. It was race 4 of 9 in the <a href="/wiki/1959_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1959 World Championship of Drivers">1959 World Championship of Drivers</a> and race 3 of 8 in the <a href="/wiki/1959_International_Cup_for_Formula_One_Manufacturers" class="mw-redirect" title="1959 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers">1959 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers</a>. It was the 37th <a href="/wiki/French_Grand_Prix" title="French Grand Prix">French Grand Prix</a> and the twelfth to be held at the <a href="/wiki/Reims" title="Reims">Reims</a> highway circuit and the fourth to be held on the longer and faster 8.348&#160;km layout. The race was held over 50 laps of the eight kilometre circuit for a race distance of 417 kilometres.
</p><p>The race was won by British driver <a href="/wiki/Tony_Brooks_(racing_driver)" title="Tony Brooks (racing driver)">Tony Brooks</a> driving a <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_246_F1" title="Ferrari 246 F1">Ferrari 246 F1</a>. Brooks dominated the race, leading all 50 laps and winning by 27 seconds over his American <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Scuderia Ferrari</a> team mate <a href="/wiki/Phil_Hill" title="Phil Hill">Phil Hill</a>. Brooks said after the race a sticking throttle in the closing laps made it more difficult than the result seemed. Australian driver <a href="/wiki/Jack_Brabham" title="Jack Brabham">Jack Brabham</a> was over a minute behind in third position driving a <a href="/wiki/Cooper_T51" title="Cooper T51">Cooper T51</a> for the factory <a href="/wiki/Cooper_Car_Company" title="Cooper Car Company">Cooper</a> racing team after stopping to get new goggles as the circuit broke up.
</p><p>Race day was very hot, to the point where the bitumen started to melt. Race cars were dislodging aggregate stones as the race went on causing American <a href="/wiki/Masten_Gregory" title="Masten Gregory">Masten Gregory</a> to retire with cuts to his face, and <a href="/wiki/Graham_Hill" title="Graham Hill">Graham Hill</a> to retire his <a href="/wiki/Lotus_16" title="Lotus 16">Lotus 16</a> after his radiator was holed.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Stirling_Moss" title="Stirling Moss">Stirling Moss</a> was disqualified from eighth position after receiving a push-start in his <a href="/wiki/British_Racing_Partnership" title="British Racing Partnership">British Racing Partnership</a> entered <a href="/wiki/BRM_P25" title="BRM P25">BRM P25</a>. Moss had pushed his car hard trying to overcome a failing gearbox, claiming a new lap record. <a href="/wiki/Jean_Behra" title="Jean Behra">Jean Behra</a> too pushed hard in his <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_246_F1" title="Ferrari 246 F1">Ferrari 246 F1</a>, climbing into third racing against no less than four team mates at this race.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Behra's engine broke under his charge and the Frenchman had a heated discussion with team manager <a href="/w/index.php?title=Romolo_Tavoni&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Romolo Tavoni (page does not exist)">Romolo Tavoni</a> which ended with Behra punching Tavoni. It would be Behra's last race for Ferrari, with the Frenchman being fired for the assault.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The win was the first of the season for Scuderia Ferrari and moved Brooks into second place overall, five points behind Brabham. Hill's second position moved him into third in the championship.
</p>
