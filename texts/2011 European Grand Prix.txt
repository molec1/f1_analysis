<p>The <b>2011 European Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>LV Grand Prix of Europe</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held on 26 June 2011 at the <a href="/wiki/Valencia_Street_Circuit" title="Valencia Street Circuit">Valencia Street Circuit</a> in <a href="/wiki/Valencia,_Spain" class="mw-redirect" title="Valencia, Spain">Valencia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>. The race, which was the eighth round of the <a href="/wiki/2011_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2011 Formula One season">2011 Formula One season</a>, was won by defending world drivers' champion and championship leader <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a>, who was driving a <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a> car. Vettel, who started from pole position, also recorded the fastest lap of the race on lap 53, giving him a <a href="/wiki/Hat-trick#Motor_racing" title="Hat-trick">hat-trick</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> After a race-long battle, <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> and Vettel's teammate <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> finished the race in second and third respectively. The race was notable for having the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_race_records" title="List of Formula One race records">fewest retirements and the most finishers</a> ever in a Formula One Grand Prix.
</p><p>As a consequence of the race, Vettel extended his lead in the Drivers' Championship standings to 77 points over <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> and Webber. By finishing first and third, Red Bull extended their Constructors' Championship standings lead to 89 points over nearest rivals <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_European_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_European_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the week leading up to the <a href="/wiki/2011_Spanish_Grand_Prix" title="2011 Spanish Grand Prix">Spanish Grand Prix</a>, FIA race director <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Whiting" title="Charlie Whiting">Charlie Whiting</a> announced that the Canadian Grand Prix would see the introduction of a secondary <a href="/wiki/Drag_reduction_system" title="Drag reduction system">drag reduction system</a> (DRS) activation zone.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The plan for a secondary DRS zone would be continued at the European Grand Prix, with the first zone incorporating the long back straight on the approach to Turn 12, and the second zone using the stretch from Turns 14 to 17. Future use of a second DRS zone would depend on its success in Montreal and Valencia.
</p><p>The FIA originally announced a ban on the off-throttle blown diffuser concept to be introduced at the <a href="/wiki/2011_British_Grand_Prix" title="2011 British Grand Prix">British Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> While the planned date for the ban was kept in effect, extra limitations were introduced for the race in Valencia.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> This ban prohibits teams from running "extreme" engine maps in qualifying before reverting to a "safer" map for the race. The ban effectively means that teams are forced to run the same engine maps in both qualifying and the race.
</p><p>The European Grand Prix also marked the race debut of Formula One tyre supplier <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_tyres" title="Formula One tyres">Pirelli</a>'s medium-specification tyre compound.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The medium compound tyre was selected as the white-banded harder "prime" tyre and the yellow-banded soft compound as the softer "option" compound.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>This race was scheduled to be the last where off-throttle blown-diffusers were used; however the ban did not continue after the <a href="/wiki/2011_British_Grand_Prix" title="2011 British Grand Prix">British Grand Prix</a> and proved to be temporary. In <a href="/wiki/2011_German_Grand_Prix" title="2011 German Grand Prix">Germany</a> all the teams returned to use what was commonly referred to as Valencia specification, or Valencia-spec diffusers. The concept was eventually banned for <a href="/wiki/2012_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2012 Formula One season">2012</a>.
</p><p>In <a href="/wiki/2011_Canadian_Grand_Prix" title="2011 Canadian Grand Prix">Canada</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> had lengthened his Drivers' Championship lead to 60 points. He had 161 points, and nearest title contenders <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a>, winner of the last Grand Prix, <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a> teammate <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> and the second <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> of <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> had 101, 94 and 85 respectively in second, third and fourth positions. <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> had crashed out in <a href="/wiki/2011_Canadian_Grand_Prix" title="2011 Canadian Grand Prix">Montreal</a>, likewise Hamilton, and stayed on 69 points in fifth. Red Bull led the Constructors' table with 255 points, McLaren were in second with 186 and Ferrari were in third with 101. <a href="/wiki/Lotus_Renault_GP" class="mw-redirect" title="Lotus Renault GP">Renault</a> were fourth with 60 and <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> were fifth with 52.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice">Practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_European_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the first free practice session, <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> was the fastest car on track by eight tenths of a second ahead of <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault's</a> <a href="/wiki/Vitaly_Petrov" title="Vitaly Petrov">Vitaly Petrov</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> for <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> made it four different cars in the top four. <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a>, in the second Renault, completed the top five, ahead of <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a>. Championship leader <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> was down in sixteenth place, two and half seconds off the pace of Webber. <a href="/wiki/Karun_Chandhok" title="Karun Chandhok">Karun Chandhok</a>, who filled in for <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> at <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus_(2010%E2%80%9311)" title="Team Lotus (2010–11)">Lotus</a> was the only of the 24 drivers not to set a time. <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a> filled in for <a href="/wiki/Paul_di_Resta" title="Paul di Resta">Paul di Resta</a> at <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a> and <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> took <a href="/wiki/Narain_Karthikeyan" title="Narain Karthikeyan">Narain Karthikeyan</a>'s seat at <a href="/wiki/HRT_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="HRT F1">HRT</a>.
</p><p>Alonso was the fastest man in the second session, once again narrowly followed by Hamilton. Vettel had moved himself up into third for this session, and fellow <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">German</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>, was fourth. The remaining <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a>, <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> and the two <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renaults</a> completed the top ten places. <a href="/wiki/Jaime_Alguersuari" title="Jaime Alguersuari">Jaime Alguersuari</a> was the only driver not to set a time in the session. Trulli, di Resta and Karthikeyan all returned to their seats for the session. In the sole Saturday session, Vettel set the quickest time ahead of Alonso, Massa, Webber, Button, <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a>, Hamilton, Schumacher, Petrov and then Heidfeld.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_European_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> took his seventh pole position from eight races in 2011, closely followed by Red Bull teammate, <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a>, who completed the front row. The second and third fastest qualifying teams followed – the <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLarens</a> of <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> (third) and <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> (sixth) split by the two <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferraris</a> of <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> ahead of teammate <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> cars were seventh and eighth with <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> in front of <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>. After that, the <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renaults</a> and <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force Indias</a> fought to make the top 10 – one of each going through – they were followed by a similar battle between the <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Saubers</a> and <a href="/wiki/Williams_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams F1">Williams</a> cars. <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9bastien_Buemi" title="Sébastien Buemi">Sébastien Buemi</a> was the one <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso</a> in Q2, ending up seventeenth, with teammate <a href="/wiki/Jaime_Alguersuari" title="Jaime Alguersuari">Jaime Alguersuari</a> eighteenth. The back three rows on the grid were filled up by the new teams – <a href="/wiki/Narain_Karthikeyan" title="Narain Karthikeyan">Narain Karthikeyan</a> qualifying last and almost a second behind <a href="/wiki/Vitantonio_Liuzzi" title="Vitantonio Liuzzi">Vitantonio Liuzzi</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_European_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The race was very hot, the average temperature around 47&#160;°C (117&#160;°F).
</p><p>At the start <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> led easily from pole, with teammate <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> behind. <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> had a good start from fifth, driving straight between and past <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> at Turn 1; but because Massa looked down the outside of Webber at Turn 2, Alonso could cleverly get back in front of Massa on the inside of Turn 3. Bad starts from the <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLarens</a> meant Hamilton dropped from third to fifth, and <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> dropped from sixth to seventh – behind <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a>. Button later retook sixth place from Rosberg a few laps later into Turn 2. Before the pit stops the order was now Vettel, Webber, Alonso, Massa, Hamilton, Button, Rosberg, Schumacher (the four fastest cars in the top eight spots).
</p><p>It seemed like the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferraris</a> had made great improvements since <a href="/wiki/2011_Canadian_Grand_Prix" title="2011 Canadian Grand Prix">Canada</a> because their pace was at least equal to the McLarens, and significantly faster at some stages of the race. The McLarens had quiet races, Button stuck in sixth, while Hamilton used the undercut to get past Massa in the pit stops for fourth, where he would finish the race.
</p><p>Fernando Alonso engaged in a race long battle with Mark Webber for second while Vettel stormed away into the distance. Early on in the race Alonso overtook Webber in the first <a href="/wiki/Drag_Reduction_System" class="mw-redirect" title="Drag Reduction System">DRS</a> zone, on the inside of Turn 12. However, in the pit stops, great <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a> strategy got Webber back in front. Ferrari later used the pit stops to get Alonso back in front of Webber. A radio message to Webber, telling him to go slower due to gearbox troubles ensured Alonso's second place.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> finished a disappointing 17th after a misjudgement from him, when he exited the pits, where he ploughed his front wing into the side of <a href="/wiki/Vitaly_Petrov" title="Vitaly Petrov">Vitaly Petrov</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a>. This meant he had to pit again the following lap demoting him to 17th, where he stayed on a circuit notoriously difficult to overtake on. Although, Schumacher admitted after the race that seventh and eighth was the maximum for the two <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> cars. <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a> did a respectable job of preserving his tyres so he only had to pit once, he finished eleventh after starting in sixteenth. His <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a> teammate, <a href="/wiki/Kamui_Kobayashi" title="Kamui Kobayashi">Kamui Kobayashi</a>, finished in sixteenth place, the first time in <a href="/wiki/2011_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2011 Formula One World Championship">2011</a> he did not finish in the top ten. For the second race in a row <a href="/wiki/Jaime_Alguersuari" title="Jaime Alguersuari">Jaime Alguersuari</a> justified his position in Toro Rosso by finishing eighth. He did this, even though he started eighteenth. Another driver on a good race was Mark Webber, calling it his "best race this year". Yet, he was still beaten by his teammate Vettel, who took his sixth victory from eight races.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_European_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After the race both McLaren drivers told the media that there was work to be done, particularly improvements in the aerodynamics of their car.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The European Grand Prix had the most classified finishers (24) beating the record set in the <a href="/wiki/2011_Chinese_Grand_Prix" title="2011 Chinese Grand Prix">2011 Chinese Grand Prix</a>. The Grand Prix is notable as it was only the fourth World Championship Grand Prix where there were no retirements, the others being the <a href="/wiki/1961_Dutch_Grand_Prix" title="1961 Dutch Grand Prix">1961 Dutch Grand Prix</a> (fifteen starters), the <a href="/wiki/2005_United_States_Grand_Prix" title="2005 United States Grand Prix">2005 United States Grand Prix</a> (six starters) and the <a href="/wiki/2005_Italian_Grand_Prix" title="2005 Italian Grand Prix">2005 Italian Grand Prix</a> (twenty starters).
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Valencia_Street_Circuit" title="Valencia Street Circuit">track</a> was criticised by former Formula One driver <a href="/wiki/Martin_Brundle" title="Martin Brundle">Martin Brundle</a> after the race, claiming its nature makes it difficult for drivers to overtake making the race boring.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Former Formula One commentator Murray Walker claimed "the race was the least eventful race of an action packed season".<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> 
</p>
