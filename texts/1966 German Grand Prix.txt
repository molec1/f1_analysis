<p>The <b>1966 German Grand Prix</b> was a mixed <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> and <a href="/wiki/Formula_Two" title="Formula Two">Formula Two</a> motor race held at the <a href="/wiki/N%C3%BCrburgring" title="Nürburgring">Nürburgring Nordschleife</a> on 7 August 1966. It was race 6 of 9 in both the <a href="/wiki/1966_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1966 World Championship of Drivers">1966 World Championship of Drivers</a> and the <a href="/wiki/1966_International_Cup_for_Formula_One_Manufacturers" class="mw-redirect" title="1966 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers">1966 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers</a>. It was the 28th <a href="/wiki/German_Grand_Prix" title="German Grand Prix">German Grand Prix</a> and the 22nd to be held at the Nordschleife. It was held over 15 laps of the 22 kilometre circuit for a race distance 342 kilometres.
</p><p>The race was won by 1959 and 1960 World Champion <a href="/wiki/Jack_Brabham" title="Jack Brabham">Jack Brabham</a> driving his <a href="/wiki/Brabham_BT19" title="Brabham BT19">Brabham BT19</a>, his fourth win in succession. Brabham won by 43 seconds over the <a href="/wiki/Cooper_T81" title="Cooper T81">Cooper T81</a> driven by 1964 World Champion <a href="/wiki/John_Surtees" title="John Surtees">John Surtees</a>. Surtees' Austrian teammate <a href="/wiki/Jochen_Rindt" title="Jochen Rindt">Jochen Rindt</a> finished third. The first Formula Two driver to finish was French driver <a href="/wiki/Jean-Pierre_Beltoise" title="Jean-Pierre Beltoise">Jean-Pierre Beltoise</a> in eighth driving a <a href="/wiki/Equipe_Matra_Sports" title="Equipe Matra Sports">Matra Sports</a> entered <a href="/wiki/Matra_MS5" title="Matra MS5">Matra MS5</a>.
</p><p>The race also saw the death of British driver <a href="/wiki/John_Taylor_(racing_driver)" title="John Taylor (racing driver)">John Taylor</a> after a collision with <a href="/wiki/Jacky_Ickx" title="Jacky Ickx">Jacky Ickx</a>.
</p><p>Brabham had collected 39 points, more than double his nearest rival, BRM driver <a href="/wiki/Graham_Hill" title="Graham Hill">Graham Hill</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_report">Race report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1966_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Race report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Another wet track provided a duel between <a href="/wiki/Jack_Brabham" title="Jack Brabham">Jack Brabham</a> and <a href="/wiki/John_Surtees" title="John Surtees">John Surtees</a> for the whole race. It was not until the Cooper's clutch failed two laps from the end that the Australian's win was guaranteed. <a href="/wiki/Jim_Clark" title="Jim Clark">Jim Clark</a>, despite qualifying on pole, made a mistake and spun into a ditch after using the wrong tyres. In a far more serious crash, the <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_Racing" title="Tyrrell Racing">Tyrrell</a>-entered Matra MS5 of <a href="/wiki/Jacky_Ickx" title="Jacky Ickx">Jacky Ickx</a> and the privately-entered <a href="/wiki/Brabham" title="Brabham">Brabham</a> BT11 of <a href="/wiki/John_Taylor_(racing_driver)" title="John Taylor (racing driver)">John Taylor</a> crashed near the bridge between Quiddelbacher and Flugplatz. Taylor was badly burned in the accident and succumbed to his injuries four weeks later.
</p><p>This would be the last Formula One race on the original Nürburgring Nordschleife before the Hohenrain chicane was added to slow the cars coming into the pits.
</p>
