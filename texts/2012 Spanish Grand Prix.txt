<p>The <b>2012 Spanish Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race that was held on 13 May 2012 at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Catalunya" class="mw-redirect" title="Circuit de Catalunya">Circuit de Catalunya</a> in <a href="/wiki/Barcelona" title="Barcelona">Barcelona</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7Dec_calendar_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7Dec_calendar-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> It was the fifth round of the <a href="/wiki/2012_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2012 Formula One season">2012 season</a> and the twenty-second time the Spanish Grand Prix been held at the circuit. The race was supported by the <a href="/wiki/2012_Catalunya_GP2_and_GP3_Series_rounds" title="2012 Catalunya GP2 and GP3 Series rounds">GP2 and GP3 Series</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> initially qualified on pole, but he was excluded from the results for a technical infringement, and <a href="/wiki/Williams_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams F1">Williams'</a> <a href="/wiki/Pastor_Maldonado" title="Pastor Maldonado">Pastor Maldonado</a> inherited pole.<sup id="cite_ref-HAM_EX_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HAM_EX-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Maldonado went on to win the race, his team's first victory since the <a href="/wiki/2004_Brazilian_Grand_Prix" title="2004 Brazilian Grand Prix">2004 Brazilian Grand Prix</a> and the first ever victory by a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_drivers_from_Venezuela" title="Formula One drivers from Venezuela">Venezuelan Formula One driver</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-MAL-ALO-RAI_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MAL-ALO-RAI-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> finished second, ahead of <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> in third.
</p><p>As of 2020<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=2012_Spanish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup>, this is the most recent race won by a driver from the <a href="/wiki/Americas" title="Americas">Americas</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2012_Spanish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2012_Spanish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The race was preceded by a four-day test at the <a href="/wiki/Mugello_Circuit" title="Mugello Circuit">Mugello Circuit</a> in <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>, starting on 1 May.<sup id="cite_ref-Testing_back_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Testing_back-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The test saw teams introduce major aerodynamic upgrades ahead of the race.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Following criticism over the extreme sensitivity of their tyre compounds,<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> tyre supplier <a href="/wiki/Pirelli" title="Pirelli">Pirelli</a> announced that they would be providing teams with the silver-banded hard compound and yellow-banded soft compound for the Spanish Grand Prix, as opposed to the soft and medium tyres they had supplied for previous races in the season.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Drag_Reduction_System" class="mw-redirect" title="Drag Reduction System">Drag Reduction System</a> (DRS) zone was placed in a similar position to the <a href="/wiki/2011_Spanish_Grand_Prix" title="2011 Spanish Grand Prix">2011</a> race. Race officials elected to place it on the start-finish straight to allow for overtaking into the first corner complex however, it was 50 metres longer than the previous year. The DRS detection point was positioned at the safety car line before the final turn, with the activation point located on the start/finish control line, creating a DRS zone of 830 metres.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Alexander_Rossi_(racing_driver)" class="mw-redirect" title="Alexander Rossi (racing driver)">Alexander Rossi</a> replaced <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> at <a href="/wiki/Caterham_F1" title="Caterham F1">Caterham</a> for the first Friday practice session.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> In the same session, <a href="/wiki/Dani_Clos" title="Dani Clos">Dani Clos</a> replaced <a href="/wiki/HRT_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="HRT F1">HRT's</a> <a href="/wiki/Narain_Karthikeyan" title="Narain Karthikeyan">Narain Karthikeyan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jules_Bianchi" title="Jules Bianchi">Jules Bianchi</a> replaced <a href="/wiki/Paul_di_Resta" title="Paul di Resta">Paul di Resta</a> at <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a> and <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a> replaced <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Senna" title="Bruno Senna">Bruno Senna</a> at <a href="/wiki/Williams_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams F1">Williams</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2012_Spanish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Q1">Q1</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2012_Spanish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Q1">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The first qualifying session of the <a href="/wiki/2012_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2012 Formula One season">2012 Formula One season</a> to be held in Europe started at 14:00 local time under bright sunshine. The ambient temperature was 28&#160;°C (82&#160;°F) while the track temperature was a warm 41&#160;°C (106&#160;°F) with wind speed a potential problem at 2.7&#160;m/s.
</p><p>The first knockout session began with the drivers apprehensive about going out first due to airborne pollen and vegetation making the track dirty. In fact no car went on the track for the first four minutes of the twenty-minute session. Finally <a href="/wiki/Paul_di_Resta" title="Paul di Resta">Paul di Resta</a> broke the deadlock and, like with many previous qualifying sessions, was the first onto the track choosing to use the hard compound tyres. The two <a href="/wiki/Lotus_F1_Racing" class="mw-redirect" title="Lotus F1 Racing">Lotus</a> cars followed and after ten minutes sixteen drivers had set timed laps. After the first phase of the session, where drivers set initial times and then pit, <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> was the fastest on the hard compound tyre with a 1:23.989. <a href="/wiki/Pastor_Maldonado" title="Pastor Maldonado">Pastor Maldonado</a> quickly eclipsed that time by six tenths of a second on the soft compound tyre with just over six minutes remaining in the session.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> complained on the team radio that he had "massive understeer" and that his rear was "still not stable". The two <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a> cars waited until five minutes remained until they left their respective garages. Both, not even bothering using a set of hard tyres, went straight onto the quicker softs. Using the hard tyres at the start of Q1 had been common practice for the faster teams in the previous four races but due to how close the field was predicted to be, tyre preservation took precedence.
</p><p>During the last two minutes of the session the all the drivers frantically set times as the qualifying positions changed very quickly. Hamilton went out again, this time on a set of soft tyres and set the fastest time in the 1:22s however, in hindsight he did not need to go for another run as he would have reached Q2 with his hard tyre time. As the session ended <a href="/wiki/Jean-%C3%89ric_Vergne" title="Jean-Éric Vergne">Jean-Éric Vergne</a> was again in danger of being eliminated in the first session for the fourth time in a row, lying in seventeenth. <a href="/wiki/Williams_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams F1">Williams</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Senna" title="Bruno Senna">Bruno Senna</a> was behind him and in the knockout zone along with the usual backrunners (<a href="/wiki/Caterham_F1" title="Caterham F1">Caterham</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marussia_F1" title="Marussia F1">Marussia</a> and <a href="/wiki/HRT_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="HRT F1">HRT</a>) when the time ran out on the session. However, Senna was still on a 'hot' lap had the opportunity to progress to the next session. Senna on the soft tyres had made a mistake on his previous lap in the braking zone of turn 10 which resulted in a front left tyre lock up and going wide of the apex. His sector times were close to seeing him progress but on turn 12 he pushed too hard lost control of the rear of the car, ending up in the gravel trap and leaving him eliminated from further qualifying.
</p><p>Behind Senna was <a href="/wiki/Vitaly_Petrov" title="Vitaly Petrov">Vitaly Petrov</a> who out-qualified teammate <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> for the first time this season. The Caterhams were comfortably (a full second) ahead of the Marussias. <a href="/wiki/Charles_Pic" title="Charles Pic">Charles Pic</a> was the faster of the two and out-qualified his much more experienced teammate, <a href="/wiki/Timo_Glock" title="Timo Glock">Timo Glock</a>, for the second time this season. Spaniard <a href="/wiki/Pedro_de_la_Rosa" title="Pedro de la Rosa">Pedro de la Rosa</a> in the first Hispania car qualified in 23rd while his teammate <a href="/wiki/Narain_Karthikeyan" title="Narain Karthikeyan">Narain Karthikeyan</a> was more than three seconds behind him and more importantly outside the 107% time, set by Hamilton, that permitted him to race.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Q2">Q2</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2012_Spanish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Q2">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Similarly with the first session, fifteen-minute Q2 period began with no cars venturing onto the track until nearly three minutes in, again due to pollen in the air. <a href="/wiki/Kamui_Kobayashi" title="Kamui Kobayashi">Kamui Kobayashi</a> was the first to set a timed lap followed by a host of other cars. Most drivers on their early first runs went on used (from Q1) soft tyres to post a time on the timesheet. Button continued to have problems with his car balance stating "still struggling with the rears" to his engineer. Early on, Hamilton led <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> and Button followed by <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> and defending world champion <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a>.
</p><p>With five minutes remaining, only four cars were out on the track – the two Lotus and <a href="/wiki/Toro_Rosso" class="mw-redirect" title="Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso</a> cars – who were the only drivers not to set a timed lap for Q2. The Lotus cars went second and third respectively on their only timed laps.
</p><p>The second phase of Q2 began with two minutes remaining in the session and the only two cars not to go back out were Hamilton, sitting in first on a 1:22.465, and Webber in fourth on 1:22.977. Webber could only watch as <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>, Vettel, Button, Kobayashi, <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pastor_Maldonado" title="Pastor Maldonado">Pastor Maldonado</a> all posted quicker times than him and pushed him out of the top ten shootout. Webber (twelfth) along with Button (eleventh) were the surprise eliminations of Q2 as Williams driver Maldonado posted the final – and fastest – time of the Q2 session to eliminate Button from qualifying. It was the first time in 21 races (<a href="/wiki/2011_Chinese_Grand_Prix" title="2011 Chinese Grand Prix">2011 Chinese Grand Prix</a>) that Webber had failed to make the top 10. It was also the first time since the <a href="/wiki/2011_Belgian_Grand_Prix" title="2011 Belgian Grand Prix">2011 Belgian Grand Prix</a> that Button had failed to reach Q3.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> posted the slowest time in Q2 and took seventeenth place in a disappointing performance. Ahead of him was <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso</a> duo <a href="/wiki/Jean-%C3%89ric_Vergne" title="Jean-Éric Vergne">Jean-Éric Vergne</a> (fifteenth) and <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> (sixteenth) in a car that clearly lacked something on its rivals. Along with Toro Rosso, <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a> looked like a midfield team for the Spanish race as <a href="/wiki/Paul_di_Resta" title="Paul di Resta">Paul di Resta</a> led German teammate <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a> into thirteenth and fourteenth respectively.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Sauber_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber F1">Sauber</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Kamui_Kobayashi" title="Kamui Kobayashi">Kamui Kobayashi</a> stopped on track at the conclusion of Q2 due to a hydraulic leak,<sup id="cite_ref-formula2_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-formula2-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> and therefore could not take part in Q3. The unpredictability of this season was further highlighted with less than a second separating second to seventeenth position.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Q3">Q3</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2012_Spanish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Q3">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In contrast to the previous two sessions, the remaining Red Bull – <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> – was waiting for a few moments at the end of the pit lane for the lights to go green for the ten-minute session, and was the first to run. He began a timed lap but had slower than usual pace meaning he would simply set sector times to allow him to be ahead of <a href="/wiki/Kamui_Kobayashi" title="Kamui Kobayashi">Kamui Kobayashi</a> (who could not run in Q3) and anyone else who would decide to not set a time in a bid to save tyres for the race. This also gave Vettel the option of being able to choose which tyre compound he wanted to start the race on. <a href="/wiki/Mercedes_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Mercedes F1">Mercedes</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> also only set sector times without actually completing a timed lap in order to be ahead of Kobayashi on the grid.
</p><p>The sole remaining <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> of <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> posted the fastest time with over five minutes remaining. Not much happened for the entire session as only 'banker' laps were set as a benchmark for the final two frantic minutes of action. Vettel went out on track for another lap with under two minutes left, but later decided to pit without a lap time as he thought of saving tyres for the race and that maybe he did not have the pace of the top five cars.<sup id="cite_ref-formula2_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-formula2-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As the session ended, most of the cars were still on 'hot' laps so a frantic finish to the session was a high probability. Alonso took pole first as <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> followed, second quickest at the time. The only running Sauber of <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a> crossed the line third fastest only to be squeezed down the order by the other Lotus of Frenchman <a href="/wiki/Romain_Grosjean" title="Romain Grosjean">Romain Grosjean</a> who claimed second place from Räikkönen. Following this <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">Venezuelan</a> <a href="/wiki/Pastor_Maldonado" title="Pastor Maldonado">Pastor Maldonado</a> took pole from Alonso by 0.017s only for his pole time to be beaten by <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>. Soon after setting the fastest time, Hamilton who had never been on pole in Barcelona, was told to stop his car before he could make it back to the pit lane.
</p><p>Hamilton was more than half a second clear of the Williams of Maldonado in second. Completing the top three was Ferrari's Alonso – it was the first time since the <a href="/wiki/2011_British_Grand_Prix" title="2011 British Grand Prix">2011 British Grand Prix</a> that he qualified (excluding penalties) in the top three – followed by the Grosjean-led Lotus cars seven tenths slower than Hamilton's time. Pérez and Rosberg rounded out the last of the drivers to complete timed laps in the Q3 session, placing sixth and seventh respectively. Vettel, Schumacher and Kobayashi did not set a lap time and thus had the option of choosing to start the race on either the hard or soft compound tyres. Of particular note was the difference in the top three drivers to their teammates. At McLaren, Hamilton was first yet Button was eleventh; at Williams, Maldonado was second and Senna was eighteenth; while at Ferrari Alonso was third yet teammate Massa was seventeenth.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-qualifying">Post-qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2012_Spanish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Post-qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>After the session <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> was excluded from the results of the qualifying session due to insufficient fuel aboard his car. Over the radio his team told him to pull over immediately, but there was not enough fuel in the car to enable it to return the pits under its own power. It also did not have the one-litre fuel sample required for testing by the FIA after qualifying. This meant that Hamilton would start from the back of the grid under article 6.6.2 of the FIA Formula One Technical Regulations.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> It was also the first time that Hamilton had started at the very back of the grid. At the <a href="/wiki/2009_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="2009 Monaco Grand Prix">2009 Monaco Grand Prix</a> Hamilton was due to start from the back due to a gearbox penalty but with <a href="/wiki/Timo_Glock" title="Timo Glock">Timo Glock</a> starting from pit lane, Hamilton avoided starting last.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On review of qualifying, Mark Webber thought the track would only improve by 2–3 tenths of a second by the end of the Q2 session, but the actual improvement was significantly greater. Webber later said that he "underestimated" other cars' speed and that the team had been "caught with our pants down". He also said that he was "happy" with his lap, also stating "it's the way it goes sometimes".<sup id="cite_ref-formula2_18-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-formula2-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Pastor_Maldonado" title="Pastor Maldonado">Pastor Maldonado</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a> both achieved their best ever qualifying results. Maldonado's previous best was seventh, set at the <a href="/wiki/2011_British_Grand_Prix" title="2011 British Grand Prix">2011 British Grand Prix</a>, and his second place in Spain was elevated to pole position on the grid after Hamilton was excluded from qualifying. Pérez's previous best was eighth, achieved at both of the previous two grands prix, in Bahrain and China. His sixth place was elevated to fifth on the grid due to Hamilton's exclusion. Maldonado's pole position was the only one that he took during his Formula One career.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2012_Spanish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The first European race of the 2012 season began at 12:00 <a href="/wiki/UTC" class="mw-redirect" title="UTC">UTC</a> (14:00 local) under cloudy conditions. The ambient temperature was 22&#160;°C (72&#160;°F) while the track temperature was 35&#160;°C (95&#160;°F), 6&#160;°C (11&#160;°F) degrees cooler than the Saturday qualifying. The wind speed was still around the 2.5&#160;m/s mark from the previous days qualifying.
</p><p>Despite all but the top seven drivers having the option of starting on either compound tyre, all of the grid choose to start on the faster but higher degrading yellow banded soft ‘option’ tyre.  As the five red lights went out <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> got a great start as the <a href="/wiki/Williams_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams F1">Williams</a> of <a href="/wiki/Pastor_Maldonado" title="Pastor Maldonado">Pastor Maldonado</a> attempted to push him to the dirty side of the track. Maldonado could not match the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>'s start and aggressiveness and was beaten to the first turn by Alonso who (like <a href="/wiki/2011_Spanish_Grand_Prix" title="2011 Spanish Grand Prix">2011</a>) led his home Grand Prix from the first turn. Finn <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> led <a href="/wiki/Lotus_F1_Team" class="mw-redirect" title="Lotus F1 Team">Lotus</a> teammate <a href="/wiki/Romain_Grosjean" title="Romain Grosjean">Romain Grosjean</a> while 5th to the first turn was <a href="/wiki/Sauber_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber F1">Sauber</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a>. The two <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> followed Pérez with <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a>'s <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> rounding out the top 8. Red Bull teammate <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> had another poor start from the grid dropping places to both <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force Indias</a>, the other Ferrari of <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jean-%C3%89ric_Vergne" title="Jean-Éric Vergne">Jean-Éric Vergne</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso</a>, leaving him in 15th by turn 1.
</p><p>Turn three saw Grosjean and Pérez make contact just enough for Pérez to receive a left rear puncture and struggle to avoid the gravel trap. <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>, starting from 24th, was 20th by the end of lap 1 while Vergne and Massa made great starts after promoting themselves to 10th and 11th respectively.
</p><p>Pérez, limping to the pits, chose to fit hard compound tyres, which proved to be quicker (at the time) than the soft tyres used by the rest of the field. <a href="/wiki/Marussia_F1" title="Marussia F1">Marussia F1</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Charles_Pic" title="Charles Pic">Charles Pic</a> also had a bad start, going into a spin at the rear of the field, but was able to recover and continue the race.
</p><p>The two Red Bull drivers, Mark Webber and Sebastian Vettel, made very early pit stops on laps 7 and 8 respectively, switching to the harder tyres. After lap 9 the Red Bull and Sauber teams were the only cars to have pitted, all choosing hard compound tyres. Sauber was thought to pit <a href="/wiki/Kamui_Kobayashi" title="Kamui Kobayashi">Kamui Kobayashi</a> in order to cover the early-stopping Red Bulls.  At this stage the Hamilton was charging his way through the field while the three at the front (Alonso, Maldonado, and Räikkönen) were building a twelve-second gap to 4th-placed Grosjean.
</p><p>Shortly afterwards, the majority of the other drivers made their first scheduled pit stop, with Lotus, Mercedes and Force India the only front running teams to opt for the soft compound tyre again. On lap 13, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> hit <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Senna" title="Bruno Senna">Bruno Senna</a> from behind after misjudging his braking, forcing both of them out of the race. Hamilton, who was using a two-stop strategy, was running as high as 4th place when he finally pitted on lap 15. On the exit of his pit box, Hamilton appeared to run over something. Replays suggested that he could have hit a used tyre that a mechanic was holding. Mark Webber ran into problems on lap 17, losing several places from 13th place before pitting for the second time, where he was fitted with a new nose cone and a new set of hard tyres. Crucially for the Australian, before he lost front wing performance he led a train of six cars all close behind each other.
</p><p>The next round of pit stops occurred between laps 23 and 28. Come lap 25, Maldonado's stop proved to be faster, and he ‘undercut’ Alonso from the pits to emerge ahead, taking the lead when Räikkönen pitted. Alonso pitted on lap 27 and prior to the pit stop was shown waving his hand in disgust at Charles Pic for not allowing the faster Ferrari to pass the lapped Marussia. <a href="/wiki/Narain_Karthikeyan" title="Narain Karthikeyan">Narain Karthikeyan</a> stopped at the end of the pit lane on track during lap 25, one of his wheels apparently not correctly fitted after his pit stop.
</p><p>Soon after, Sebastian Vettel and Felipe Massa were both handed drive through penalties for failing to slow for a yellow flag warning, presumably for the flags shown at the turn 1 Schumacher/Senna incident.
</p><p>After the Lotus cars switched back from soft to hard tyres, their pace was visibly more competitive with the leaders and it became clear a strategic error had occurred. Lap 34 saw a daring move from the Japanese driver Kobayashi on 2009 world champion Button where he out braked Button on the inside of turn 5 and gently nudged him further out of his way. Sky Sports commentator Martin Brundle said “you know when you’ve been KK’d” referring to Kobayashi’s initials and his attacking overtaking style.
</p><p>At this stage of the race, Maldonado, Alonso or Räikkönen looked to be the only drivers with a realistic chance of winning the Grand Prix. Webber, after his front wing replacement, was stuck behind <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a> in the second Force India and could not find enough straight line speed to pass him under DRS. Many cars hit the rev limiter down the front straight early and relied on delayed braking to complete a pass.
</p><p>Hamilton made his second and final pit stop from 7th place on lap 36, rejoining the race in 14th place. Soon afterwards, Charles Pic withdrew from the race with mechanical problems, having just been handed a drive through penalty for ignoring blue flags for the Alonso incident. The final retirement occurred on lap 40, when a botched pit stop and transmission problems ended Sergio Pérez's race.
</p><p>At the head of the field, Alonso was making steady inroads into Maldonado's lead, cutting it to 6 seconds by lap 38. On Maldonado’s final pit stop, a slight delay occurred when there was trouble with the left rear wheel. This delay cost him 2-3 valuable seconds and, it was thought at the time, perhaps the race. Unusually, the other Red Bull, Vettel, also got his front wing changed on lap 44. Following his final pit stop  (lap 49), Räikkönen also began to make up ground on the two leaders.
</p><p>After his final pit stop, Räikkönen was 22.809 seconds down on the leader but fresher tyres meant that he would have a speed advantage for the last 16 laps. Alonso eventually caught the back of Maldonado but did not have the ability to overtake him. Maldonado was clear on his focus of tyre preservation. The gap hovered around one second for some time until Alonso started dropping off.
</p><p>Räikkönen was consistently gaining one second a lap on the leaders and his team thought that they would be in the best position for the win when Maldonado and Alonso’s tyres hit the performance ‘cliff’. Meanwhile, Kobayashi took 5th from Rosberg at turn 10 under brakes and Webber was still stuck right behind the Force India of Hülkenberg. All the while a fast Vettel passed Hamilton and Rosberg in the closing stages to eventually claim 6th behind the sole remaining Sauber.
</p><p>It appeared that the more experienced Alonso would eventually regain the lead, but Maldonado withstood the pressure and eventually Alonso decided to settle for second, rather than risk losing any more traction on his tyres. Räikkönen's late surge proved insufficient to take second place. The win for Maldonado was his first in Formula One and eclipsed his previous best result - 8th in <a href="/wiki/2012_Chinese_Grand_Prix" title="2012 Chinese Grand Prix">China</a>. In fact it was only his third ever points scoring finish in 24 races. It was also <a href="/wiki/Williams_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams F1">Williams</a> best result since the <a href="/wiki/2004_Brazilian_Grand_Prix" title="2004 Brazilian Grand Prix">2004 Brazilian Grand Prix</a>.
</p><p>Räikkönen's teammate Romain Grosjean finished 4th, driving a steady race after the first lap incident with Pérez. Kamui Kobayashi and Sebastian Vettel overtook Nico Rosberg late in the race for 5th and 6th places respectively. Lewis Hamilton finished a meritorious 8th after starting from the back of the grid, with Jenson Button and Nico Hülkenberg (who had kept Mark Webber (11th) behind from lap 22 until the end) taking the final points. It was also <a href="/wiki/Kamui_Kobayashi" title="Kamui Kobayashi">Kamui Kobayashi</a>'s equal best result of his Formula One career, matching his previous best of 5th from the <a href="/wiki/2011_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="2011 Monaco Grand Prix">2011 Monaco Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2012_Spanish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After the race, the stewards gave <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> a five-place grid penalty at the next race, <a href="/wiki/2012_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="2012 Monaco Grand Prix">Monaco</a>, after he crashed into <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Senna" title="Bruno Senna">Bruno Senna</a> and caused them both to retire.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> This penalty was applied in accordance with article 16.1 of the FIA Formula One Sporting Regulations.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Pit_lane_fire">Pit lane fire</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2012_Spanish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Pit lane fire">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>

Pit crews from several teams were able to bring the blaze under control.<sup id="cite_ref-fire_pictures_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fire_pictures-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Thirty-one people were injured,<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> with seven transferred to local hospitals.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> All were later released.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Early accounts surfaced suggesting that the fire was caused by fuel that exploded while being prepared for a routine post-race inspection.<sup id="cite_ref-90_mins_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-90_mins-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Photographs taken at the scene showed Senna's car as being the point of origin of the fire, which ignited when a fuel rig used to drain the car started leaking,<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> while other reports suggested that a spark from the KERS unit initiated the blaze.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Senna's <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW34" title="Williams FW34">FW34</a> was damaged as a result;<sup id="cite_ref-fire_pictures_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fire_pictures-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Maldonado's car was not in the garage at the time.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Teams were reported as lending equipment to Williams for the <a href="/wiki/2012_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="2012 Monaco Grand Prix">Monaco Grand Prix</a> to replace everything that was lost in the fire.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Maldonado rescued his twelve-year-old cousin Manuel from the fire, as he had a broken foot.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
