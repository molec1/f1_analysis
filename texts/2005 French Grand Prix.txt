<p>The <b>2005 French Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 3 July 2005 at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Nevers_Magny-Cours" title="Circuit de Nevers Magny-Cours">Circuit de Nevers Magny-Cours</a> near <a href="/wiki/Magny-Cours" title="Magny-Cours">Magny-Cours</a> in <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>. It was the tenth race of the <a href="/wiki/2005_FIA_Formula_One_World_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="2005 FIA Formula One World Championship">2005 FIA Formula One World Championship</a>. The 70-lap race was won from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> by <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/Renault_R25" title="Renault R25">Renault</a>, with Drivers' Championship rival <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> finishing second in a <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4-20" title="McLaren MP4-20">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Mercedes_AMG_High_Performance_Powertrains" title="Mercedes AMG High Performance Powertrains">Mercedes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> third in a <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_F2005" title="Ferrari F2005">Ferrari</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2005_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2005_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The French Grand Prix was the tenth race of the 2005 season and after the controversial <a href="/wiki/2005_United_States_Grand_Prix" title="2005 United States Grand Prix">United States Grand Prix</a> at Indianapolis two weeks previously, Formula One returned to Europe for the busiest month in its 56-year history, with Magny-Cours being the first of four races to be held in the five weekends of July.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice_and_qualifying">Practice and qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2005_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Practice and qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the start of the weekend, <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> and <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a> appeared to be the fastest teams, topping the time sheets for both practice sessions on Friday. However, <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> suffered an <a href="/wiki/Engine" title="Engine">engine</a> failure, using a new-spec <a href="/wiki/Mercedes_AMG_High_Performance_Powertrains" title="Mercedes AMG High Performance Powertrains">Mercedes</a> <a href="/wiki/V10_engine" title="V10 engine">V10</a>, and was forced to drop ten grid positions as a result. Renault set the two fastest times in Saturday practice 1 by over one second, but only nine cars ran in the session. <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> was quickest in the final practice session before qualifying, with Räikkönen was close behind.
</p><p>The qualifying session ran in markedly cool conditions than expected but the <a href="/wiki/Michelin" title="Michelin">Michelin</a> tyres still had the advantage over the <a href="/wiki/Bridgestone" title="Bridgestone">Bridgestones</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-qualifyingreport_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualifyingreport-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> scored his second consecutive <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Toyota_Racing_(Formula_One_team)" title="Toyota Racing (Formula One team)">Toyota</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> alongside him on the front row. Räikkönen's penalty dropped him from third to 13th, thus promoting <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> to third. The <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Saubers</a> of <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a> were 10th and 11th respectively (subsequently promoted to 9th and 10th), while <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a>, who had struggled all weekend despite several new <a href="/wiki/Aerodynamics" title="Aerodynamics">aerodynamic</a> updates to the car, were 13th and 14th. The <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordans</a> and <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardis</a> filled the back two rows. The session was incredibly close as the top nine drivers were covered by less than a second.<sup id="cite_ref-qualifyingreport_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualifyingreport-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2005_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The start of the race saw Alonso speed away quickly, leaving Trulli to fend off Michael Schumacher through the first few corners, while <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> leap-frogged <a href="/wiki/Takuma_Sato" title="Takuma Sato">Takuma Sato</a> into fourth place. Räikkönen was able to pass both <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> to move into 11th position by the end of the first lap. By the second lap, Alonso had extended his lead to nearly three seconds, while Räikkönen moved up another position to tenth by passing Villeneuve. <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Christian_Klien" title="Christian Klien">Christian Klien</a> became the first retirement of the race on lap 2 due to a lack of fuel pressure. Alonso continued pulling away from the pack setting consecutive fastest laps.
</p><p>Barrichello was the first of the front-runners to stop; from fourth position. The following lap both Trulli and Michael Schumacher pitted allowing Schumacher to emerge ahead having been stuck behind Trulli's Toyota since the beginning. Alonso finally pitted on lap 20 while leading by nearly 30 seconds allowing him to rejoin without losing a position. The pitstop sequence allowed the two long-running McLarens into 2nd and 3rd positions with <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a> ahead of Räikkönen. Both McLaren drivers continued their first stints before Montoya pitted on lap 25 rejoining ahead of Michael Schumacher to take third. Räikkönen stayed out for three more laps and finally pitted on lap 28. Those laps did make the difference and he came out ahead of Montoya.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Patrick_Friesacher" title="Patrick Friesacher">Patrick Friesacher</a> became the second retirement when he stopped on lap 34. On lap 37, <a href="/wiki/Christijan_Albers" title="Christijan Albers">Christijan Albers</a> crashed his <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a> heavily after spinning in turn 2. Alonso stopped for the second time on lap 41 rejoining with a 14-second margin to the second-placed Räikkönen. Meanwhile, Montoya began to struggle and ultimately retired from third position on lap 46 with a <a href="/wiki/Hydraulics" title="Hydraulics">hydraulics</a> failure. This allowed Michael Schumacher to move into the final podium position, which he held after his final pitstop on lap 51. Williams' dismal day continued as <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> pitted complaining that his car was impossible to drive.  With all but the top four cars lapped, Sato ran wide briefly into the gravel at Estoril corner, which cost him 10th position to <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a>. Fisichella stalled as he attempted to leave his pit box on lap 58. Renault mechanics had to restart his engine costing him two valuable positions.
</p><p>With eight laps remaining, Alonso lapped fourth-placed <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a>, leaving only the top three drivers on the lead lap. Alonso crossed the finish line to take his fifth and Renault's sixth win of the season. Räikkönen finished 11.8 seconds behind Alonso, ensuring that the gap in the championship standings increased by only two points, while Michael Schumacher picked up a timely podium for Ferrari, allowing him to stay in the championship hunt as well. Button came in fourth to score <a href="/wiki/British_American_Racing" title="British American Racing">BAR</a>'s first points of the year, with Trulli, Fisichella, Ralf Schumacher and Villeneuve completing the top eight.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Friday_drivers">Friday drivers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2005_French_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Friday drivers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The bottom six teams in the <a href="/wiki/2004_FIA_Formula_One_World_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="2004 FIA Formula One World Championship">2004 Constructors' Championship</a> were entitled to run a third car in free practice on Friday. These drivers drove on Friday but did not compete in qualifying or the race.
</p><p>This was the last appearance for <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Panis" title="Olivier Panis">Olivier Panis</a>.
</p>
