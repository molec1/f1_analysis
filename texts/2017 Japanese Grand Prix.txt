<p>The <b>2017 Japanese Grand Prix</b> (formally known as the <b>2017 Formula 1 Japanese Grand Prix</b>)<sup id="cite_ref-formal_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-formal-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_racing" title="Formula One racing">Formula One motor race</a> held on 8 October 2017 at the <a href="/wiki/Suzuka_Circuit" class="mw-redirect" title="Suzuka Circuit">Suzuka International Racing Course</a> in <a href="/wiki/Suzuka,_Mie" title="Suzuka, Mie">Suzuka</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Mie_Prefecture" title="Mie Prefecture">Mie Prefecture, Japan</a>. The race was the sixteenth round of the <a href="/wiki/2017_FIA_Formula_One_World_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="2017 FIA Formula One World Championship">2017 FIA Formula One World Championship</a> and marked the forty-third running of the <a href="/wiki/Japanese_Grand_Prix" title="Japanese Grand Prix">Japanese Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-prov._calendar_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prov._calendar-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The 2017 event was the thirty-third time that the race has been run as a World Championship event since the inaugural season in <a href="/wiki/1950_Formula_One_season" title="1950 Formula One season">1950</a>, and the twenty-ninth time that a World Championship round had been held at Suzuka. This would also prove to be the last Grand Prix for <a href="/wiki/Jolyon_Palmer" title="Jolyon Palmer">Jolyon Palmer</a>, he was replaced by <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Sainz_Jr" class="mw-redirect" title="Carlos Sainz Jr">Carlos Sainz Jr</a> for the rest of the 2017 season.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> entered the round with a thirty-four-point lead over <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari's</a> <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> in the World Drivers' Championship. Hamilton's teammate <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a> sat third, a further twenty-five points behind. In the World Constructors' Championship, Mercedes held a lead of one hundred and eighteen points over Ferrari, with <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a> a further one hundred and fifteen points behind in third place.
</p>
