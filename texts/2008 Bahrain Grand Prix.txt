<p>The <b>2008 Bahrain Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>V Gulf Air Bahrain Grand Prix</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 6 April 2008 at the <a href="/wiki/Bahrain_International_Circuit" title="Bahrain International Circuit">Bahrain International Circuit</a>, in <a href="/wiki/Sakhir" title="Sakhir">Sakhir</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bahrain" title="Bahrain">Bahrain</a>. It was the third race of the <a href="/wiki/2008_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2008 Formula One season">2008 Formula One season</a>. The 57-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> team. <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> was second in the other Ferrari, and <a href="/wiki/BMW_Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="BMW Sauber">BMW Sauber</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a> was third.
</p><p>The race began with Kubica in <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> alongside Massa; <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>, the eventual <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Champion</a>, started from third, alongside Räikkönen. Kubica was passed by Massa into the first corner, and then by Räikkönen on the third lap. The Ferraris dominated at the front of the race, leading to their 1–2 finish. Hamilton had a slow start after almost <a href="/wiki/Stall_(engine)" title="Stall (engine)">stalling</a> on the grid, and dropped back to ninth. The McLaren driver ran into the back of <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a> a lap later, breaking off the McLaren's <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_car#Aerodynamics" title="Formula One car">front wing</a> and dropping Hamilton to the back of the field.
</p><p>Kubica's strong finish promoted BMW Sauber to the lead in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a>, after BMW driver <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> finished fourth. Ferrari and McLaren trailed, one and two <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Championship_points_scoring_systems" title="List of Formula One World Championship points scoring systems">points</a> behind, respectively. Räikkönen took the lead in the Drivers' Championship, with 19&#160;points, three points ahead of Heidfeld and five ahead of Hamilton, Kubica and Kovalainen, with 15&#160;races remaining in the season.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2008_Bahrain_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Grand Prix was contested by 22&#160;drivers, in 11 teams of two.<sup id="cite_ref-classification_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-classification-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The teams, also known as "<a href="/wiki/Formula_One#Constructors" title="Formula One">constructors</a>", were <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, <a href="/wiki/McLaren_(racing)" class="mw-redirect" title="McLaren (racing)">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_HighPerformanceEngines" class="mw-redirect" title="Mercedes-Benz HighPerformanceEngines">Mercedes</a>, <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a>, <a href="/wiki/Honda_Racing_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Honda Racing F1">Honda</a>, <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a>, <a href="/wiki/BMW_Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="BMW Sauber">BMW Sauber</a>, <a href="/wiki/Toyota_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Toyota F1">Toyota</a>, <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a>, <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso</a> and <a href="/wiki/Super_Aguri" class="mw-redirect" title="Super Aguri">Super Aguri</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-classification_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-classification-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_tyres" title="Formula One tyres">Tyre</a> supplier <a href="/wiki/Bridgestone" title="Bridgestone">Bridgestone</a> brought two different tyre compounds to the race; the softer of the two marked by a single white stripe down one of the grooves.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Prior to the race, McLaren driver <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> led the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a> with 14&#160;<a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Championship_points_scoring_systems" title="List of Formula One World Championship points scoring systems">points</a>, and Ferrari driver <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> was second with 11&#160;points. Behind Hamilton and Räikkönen in the Drivers' Championship, <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> was third, also with 11&#160;points, in a BMW Sauber, and Hamilton's McLaren teammate <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> was fourth with 10&#160;points. Heidfeld's teammate <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a> was fifth with eight points.<sup id="cite_ref-pointsFIA_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pointsFIA-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a>, McLaren–Mercedes were leading with 24&#160;points, five points ahead of BMW Sauber. Ferrari were third with 11&#160;points.<sup id="cite_ref-pointsFIA_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pointsFIA-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:LewisHamilton_2_2007_amk.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7f/LewisHamilton_2_2007_amk.jpg/220px-LewisHamilton_2_2007_amk.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="330" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7f/LewisHamilton_2_2007_amk.jpg/330px-LewisHamilton_2_2007_amk.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7f/LewisHamilton_2_2007_amk.jpg/440px-LewisHamilton_2_2007_amk.jpg 2x" data-file-width="900" data-file-height="1350" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:LewisHamilton_2_2007_amk.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a></div></div></div>
<p>Ferrari dominated the <a href="/wiki/2008_Malaysian_Grand_Prix" title="2008 Malaysian Grand Prix">previous round in Malaysia</a>, where <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> had claimed <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>, and led his teammate Räikkönen in second place through the opening stages of the race, before spinning off and retiring midway through.<sup id="cite_ref-malgpreport_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-malgpreport-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Räikkönen went on to win the race, and expressed his optimism about Bahrain: "I have finished third in three successive Grands Prix in Bahrain. Time and again something has gone wrong. Sakhir is one of those circuits where I really want to win. Finally."<sup id="cite_ref-KRhope_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-KRhope-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the opening two races, Massa came under fire from the press for two errors that left him without points: a collision with Red Bull driver <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> <a href="/wiki/2008_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="2008 Australian Grand Prix">in Australia</a> and his spin at Malaysia.<sup id="cite_ref-malgpreport_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-malgpreport-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-tshtshmassa_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tshtshmassa-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Massa promised that the first two races would not be indicative of the rest of the season: "It was not the start to the season that I wanted, but there are still 16&#160;races to go and 160&#160;points up for grabs. In the next few races I plan to get back all the points I have lost in the opening two rounds."<sup id="cite_ref-mesomassa_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mesomassa-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Hamilton won the opening race in Australia, but managed to finish only fifth after a qualifying penalty and a botched <a href="/wiki/Pit_stop" title="Pit stop">pit stop</a> in Malaysia.<sup id="cite_ref-malgpreport_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-malgpreport-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The season began well for Kubica, as he qualified second in Australia and finished second in Malaysia. Kubica predicted his team could maintain their momentum into the third race: "I'm confident that we can be very competitive here as well."<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>A week before the start of the Grand Prix weekend, the <i><a href="/wiki/News_of_the_World" title="News of the World">News of the World</a></i> alleged that <a href="/wiki/Max_Mosley" title="Max Mosley">Max Mosley</a>, the president of Formula One's <a href="/wiki/Sport_governing_body" class="mw-redirect" title="Sport governing body">governing body</a>, the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile</a> (FIA), had engaged in sexual acts with five <a href="/wiki/Prostitute" class="mw-redirect" title="Prostitute">prostitutes</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> While Mosley denied the allegations, he cancelled his scheduled appearance at the Bahrain Grand Prix.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Several teams condemned Mosley's alleged actions and asked for his resignation, and while for a time the controversy threatened to overshadow the race, Mosley eventually retained his position and successfully sued the <i>News of the World</i> for the report.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice">Practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2008_Bahrain_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Felipe_Massa_2008_Algarve.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8c/Felipe_Massa_2008_Algarve.jpg/220px-Felipe_Massa_2008_Algarve.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="192" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8c/Felipe_Massa_2008_Algarve.jpg/330px-Felipe_Massa_2008_Algarve.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8c/Felipe_Massa_2008_Algarve.jpg/440px-Felipe_Massa_2008_Algarve.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3134" data-file-height="2732" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Felipe_Massa_2008_Algarve.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a></div></div></div>
<p>Three practice sessions were held before the Sunday race—two on Friday, and a third on Saturday. The Friday morning and afternoon sessions each lasted 90&#160;minutes. The third session was held on Saturday morning and lasted an hour.<sup id="cite_ref-sporting_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sporting-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> The Ferraris outpaced the other teams in the first session on a dusty track surrounded by the <a href="/wiki/Sand_dune" class="mw-redirect" title="Sand dune">sand dune</a> desert of <a href="/wiki/Sakhir" title="Sakhir">Sakhir</a>. Massa's time of 1:32.233 was quicker than Räikkönen's, who was slowed down by an early excursion across the sand, requiring a <a href="/wiki/Pit_stop" title="Pit stop">pit stop</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-prac1_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prac1-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> of Williams, Hamilton, McLaren driver <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a>, Williams driver <a href="/wiki/Kazuki_Nakajima" title="Kazuki Nakajima">Kazuki Nakajima</a> and Kubica rounded out the top seven.<sup id="cite_ref-prac1_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prac1-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> In the second session, Hamilton lost control of his car and slid sideways into a wall.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton emerged unharmed from the collision, but his McLaren suffered significant damage.<sup id="cite_ref-prac2_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prac2-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Except for the crash, the second session ended like the first: once again, Massa led Räikkönen to Ferrari 1–2, ahead of Kovalainen, Hamilton and Kubica.<sup id="cite_ref-prac2_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prac2-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> The third session was again held on a dusty track, where Rosberg was quickest with a time of 1:32.521.<sup id="cite_ref-prac3_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prac3-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Massa took second, ahead of Red Bull driver <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a>, Toyota driver <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a>, <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> of Red Bull, Nakajima and Kubica. Räikkönen was ninth quickest, and Hamilton 18th.<sup id="cite_ref-prac3_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prac3-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2008_Bahrain_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The qualifying session on Saturday afternoon was split into three&#160;parts. The first part ran for 20&#160;minutes, and cars that finished the session 17th position or lower were eliminated from qualifying. The second part of the qualifying session lasted 15&#160;minutes and eliminated cars that finished in positions&#160;11 to 16. The final part of the qualifying session determined the positions from first to tenth, and decided pole position. Cars which failed to make the final session could refuel before the race, so ran lighter in those sessions. Cars which competed in the final session of qualifying were not allowed to refuel before the race, and as such carried more fuel than in the previous sessions.<sup id="cite_ref-sporting_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sporting-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatleft" style="width:28%;&#32;;"><style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r887775652">.mw-parser-output .quotebox{background-color:#F9F9F9;border:1px solid #aaa;box-sizing:border-box;padding:10px;font-size:88%;max-width:100%}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft{margin:0.5em 1.4em 0.8em 0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright{margin:0.5em 0 0.8em 1.4em}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.centered{margin:0.5em auto 0.8em auto}.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatleft p,.mw-parser-output .quotebox.floatright p{font-style:inherit}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-title{background-color:#F9F9F9;text-align:center;font-size:larger;font-weight:bold}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:before{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" “ ";vertical-align:-45%;line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox-quote.quoted:after{font-family:"Times New Roman",serif;font-weight:bold;font-size:large;color:gray;content:" ” ";line-height:0}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .left-aligned{text-align:left}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .right-aligned{text-align:right}.mw-parser-output .quotebox .center-aligned{text-align:center}.mw-parser-output .quotebox cite{display:block;font-style:normal}@media screen and (max-width:360px){.mw-parser-output .quotebox{min-width:100%;margin:0 0 0.8em!important;float:none!important}}</style>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">I am very happy. I missed the chance to take pole position in Australia, but this time it worked out well ... We knew before the season that the car was good due to the results of the computer simulation and the wind tunnel work. Finally it has paid off that we never gave up working hard. I want to thank the entire team who have worked so hard over the last months. We will now study the data and prepare for tomorrow. A long race lies ahead of us.</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style=""><a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a>, following the third qualifying session.<sup id="cite_ref-qualquotes_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualquotes-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>Kubica clinched the first pole position of his career with a time of 1:33.096.<sup id="cite_ref-qualresults_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualresults-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Massa qualified less than 0.03&#160;seconds behind the BMW and joined Kubica on the front row of the <a href="/wiki/List_of_motorsport_terminology#G" class="mw-redirect" title="List of motorsport terminology">grid</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-qualresults_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualresults-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton took third place, using his team's spare <a href="/wiki/Chassis" title="Chassis">chassis</a>; Räikkönen was next quickest, and despite being critical of his car's <a href="/wiki/Racing_setup" title="Racing setup">set-up</a> was confident in its racing ability.<sup id="cite_ref-qualquotes_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualquotes-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-bbcqual_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcqual-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Kovalainen would line up fifth on the grid alongside Heidfeld, who had trouble maximising performance from his tyres.<sup id="cite_ref-qualquotes_21-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualquotes-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Trulli took seventh place, ahead of Rosberg and Honda driver <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-qualresults_22-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualresults-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Renault driver <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> was the last driver to make the third session; Webber missed out on the top ten by 0.009&#160;seconds and would start the race in 11th position.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportqual_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportqual-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Button's teammate <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> took 12th place after a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_car#Transmission" title="Formula One car">gearbox</a> problem interrupted his second session laps, ahead of <a href="/wiki/Timo_Glock" title="Timo Glock">Timo Glock</a> of Toyota, <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet_Jr." title="Nelson Piquet Jr.">Nelson Piquet</a> of Renault and Toro Rosso driver <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9bastien_Bourdais" title="Sébastien Bourdais">Sébastien Bourdais</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-qualquotes_21-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualquotes-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Nakajima was the slowest of the second session drivers, and took 16th.<sup id="cite_ref-qualresults_22-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualresults-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard qualified 17th, ahead of <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> of Force India.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportqual_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportqual-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Toro Rosso driver <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> took 19th, and blamed his set-up: "I felt it was more a case of the car driving me than me driving the car."<sup id="cite_ref-qualresults_22-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualresults-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Fisichella's teammate <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a> qualified 20th, ahead of <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Davidson" title="Anthony Davidson">Anthony Davidson</a> of Super Aguri. Davidson's teammate <a href="/wiki/Takuma_Sato" title="Takuma Sato">Takuma Sato</a> spun out and crashed into the barriers. Sato's accident damaged his <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_car#Aerodynamics" title="Formula One car">rear wing</a> and <a href="/wiki/Suspension_(vehicle)" class="mw-redirect" title="Suspension (vehicle)">suspension</a> and left him unable to continue in the session.<sup id="cite_ref-autosportqual_24-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosportqual-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2008_Bahrain_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a href="/wiki/File:First_lap_2008_Bahrain.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/64/First_lap_2008_Bahrain.jpg/300px-First_lap_2008_Bahrain.jpg" decoding="async" width="300" height="130" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/64/First_lap_2008_Bahrain.jpg/450px-First_lap_2008_Bahrain.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/64/First_lap_2008_Bahrain.jpg/600px-First_lap_2008_Bahrain.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3101" data-file-height="1345" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:First_lap_2008_Bahrain.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> crosses the sand during the first lap of the race</div></div></div>
<p>The weather and conditions on the grid were before dry for the race. The <a href="/wiki/Air_temperature" class="mw-redirect" title="Air temperature">air temperature</a> was 29&#160;°C (84&#160;°F) with signs of a breeze which could blow sand onto the track and impede the cars' grip.<sup id="cite_ref-commbbc_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-commbbc-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Massa got the best start of the frontrunners off the line, as he passed Kubica into the first corner to take the lead.<sup id="cite_ref-fiaracefacts_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiaracefacts-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton's poor start caused his anti-<a href="/wiki/Stall_(engine)" title="Stall (engine)">stall</a> system to kick in, and he was passed by six drivers to fall back to ninth.<sup id="cite_ref-gpreport_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpreport-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> Räikkönen benefited from Hamilton's start by moving up to third, ahead of Kovalainen, Trulli and Heidfeld.<sup id="cite_ref-fiaracefacts_26-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiaracefacts-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> As Massa extended his lead at the front of the race, Hamilton, who trailed Alonso, collided with the back of the Renault, knocking the McLaren's <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_car#Aerodynamics" title="Formula One car">front wing</a> off the car.<sup id="cite_ref-fiaracefacts_26-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiaracefacts-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-hamiltonwing_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hamiltonwing-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Suffering handling difficulties, Hamilton returned to the pit-lane for a new nose section, and rejoined in 18th place.<sup id="cite_ref-fialapchart_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fialapchart-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Räikkönen took second place when he passed Kubica on lap three; Heidfeld took fourth when he passed Trulli and Kovalainen in separate manoeuvres.<sup id="cite_ref-fiaracefacts_26-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiaracefacts-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Further down the field, Vettel retired from the race on the first lap after twice colliding with other cars; Button, Sutil and Coulthard pitted to repair early damage.<sup id="cite_ref-fiaracefacts_26-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiaracefacts-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-gpreport_27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpreport-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>By lap&#160;10, Massa had opened his lead over Räikkönen to 4.4&#160;seconds, ahead of Kubica, Heidfeld, Kovalainen and Trulli.<sup id="cite_ref-fiaracefacts_26-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiaracefacts-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fialapchart_29-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fialapchart-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Kubica was the first of the frontrunners to pit, on lap&#160;17.<sup id="cite_ref-pitsummary_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pitsummary-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Räikkönen and Trulli followed on lap&#160;20; Massa pitted from the lead one lap later.<sup id="cite_ref-pitsummary_30-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pitsummary-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Following the first round of pit stops, the gap between the Ferraris was 5.4&#160;seconds, however by lap&#160;31 Räikkönen had closed to within four seconds of his teammate.<sup id="cite_ref-fiaracefacts_26-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiaracefacts-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Massa held a 3.6&#160;second lead over Räikkönen when the two Ferraris pitted for the final time on laps 39&#160;and 38, respectively, and Massa retained his lead into the final <a href="/wiki/List_of_motorsport_terminology#S" class="mw-redirect" title="List of motorsport terminology">stint</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-fiaracefacts_26-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fiaracefacts-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-pitsummary_30-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pitsummary-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Kubica pitted on lap&#160;41, Heidfeld on lap&#160;45, and Kovalainen on lap&#160;47.<sup id="cite_ref-pitsummary_30-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pitsummary-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Coulthard and Button collided on lap&#160;18 when Button attempted to pass the Red Bull on the inside at turn eight; the Honda lost its front wing and retired a lap later after two pit stops.<sup id="cite_ref-commbbc_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-commbbc-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton continued his climb back through the field; he moved from 18th, passing Piquet, Davidson, Sutil and Bourdais in separate manoeuvres, to sit in 14th by the time he pitted on lap&#160;31.<sup id="cite_ref-fialapchart_29-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fialapchart-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-pitsummary_30-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pitsummary-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Piquet retired on lap&#160;42 with <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_car#Transmission" title="Formula One car">transmission</a> failure, requiring a gearbox change before the next race.<sup id="cite_ref-commbbc_25-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-commbbc-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Massa took his first win of the season when he crossed the line at the end of the 57th lap, 3.3&#160;seconds ahead of the second-placed Räikkönen.<sup id="cite_ref-classification_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-classification-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Kubica took third, ahead of his teammate Heidfeld, and Kovalainen, who set the fastest lap of the race on lap&#160;49, with a time of 1:33.193, despite being slower than the frontrunners for much of the race.<sup id="cite_ref-classification_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-classification-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-f1byteam_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1byteam-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Trulli, Webber, Glock and Alonso rounded out the top ten, after Glock's Toyota held off a quick Alonso late in the race.<sup id="cite_ref-classification_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-classification-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-f1byteam_31-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1byteam-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello and Fisichella finished strongly, ahead of Hamilton, who managed only 13th.<sup id="cite_ref-classification_4-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-classification-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Nakajima, Bourdais, Davidson and Sato took the next four places; Coulthard and Sutil finished last on track after their respective crashes demoted them to the back of the field.<sup id="cite_ref-classification_4-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-classification-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Vettel, Button and Piquet were the three retirements from the race.<sup id="cite_ref-classification_4-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-classification-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2008_Bahrain_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatright" style="width:35%;&#32;;"><link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r887775652"/>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">It didn't change anything starting second because we had a good a start. I think Robert [Kubica] didn't have a good start and I passed him. Then the race was pretty difficult in the beginning because there was a lot of oil on the track. We almost went off, me and Robert, and it was pretty difficult. During the race the car was just very good. I didn't push completely to the limit because I saw the gap increasing and then Kimi [Räikkönen] was behind but I could manage to keep the gap to him and the strategy was perfect as well.</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style=""><a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a>, speaking after the race.<sup id="cite_ref-pressconference_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconference-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>The top three finishers appeared on the <a href="/wiki/Podium#Usage_in_motorsport" title="Podium">podium</a> and in the subsequent <a href="/wiki/Press_conference" title="Press conference">press conference</a>, where Massa appeared relieved: "For sure the race was pretty difficult because I didn't want to make any mistakes. I didn't push as much either, just tried to bring the car home and just controlling the pace as well."<sup id="cite_ref-pressconference_32-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconference-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Massa said that he struggled with grip early, owing to oil in the middle sector of the course.<sup id="cite_ref-pressconference_32-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconference-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Räikkönen's second place promoted him to the lead in the Drivers' Championship, and he expressed his optimism about future races:
</p>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r960796168">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>The whole weekend has been pretty difficult, one of those things when we cannot really get the car right ... We are leading the Championship which is the main thing and we know that we have the speed once we get everything right. The race was quite difficult but anyhow I am happy with second.<sup id="cite_ref-pressconference_32-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconference-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>
<p>Kubica said that his poor start was due to <a href="/wiki/Wheelspin" title="Wheelspin">wheelspin</a> off the line, and the presence of oil on the track impeded the performance of his car, leading to Räikkönen's pass: "But anyway I think it was a good result: third and fourth for the team and leading the Constructors' Championship, so it was good weekend."<sup id="cite_ref-pressconference_32-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconference-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Kimi_Raikkonen_2008_(square).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/40/Kimi_Raikkonen_2008_%28square%29.jpg/220px-Kimi_Raikkonen_2008_%28square%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="220" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/40/Kimi_Raikkonen_2008_%28square%29.jpg/330px-Kimi_Raikkonen_2008_%28square%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/40/Kimi_Raikkonen_2008_%28square%29.jpg/440px-Kimi_Raikkonen_2008_%28square%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="964" data-file-height="964" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Kimi_Raikkonen_2008_(square).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a></div></div></div>
<p>Hamilton accepted responsibility for his near stall on the grid: "I hadn't hit the switch early enough and therefore we were not in the launch map and went straight into anti-stall, and when everyone else was in their launch mode, I wasn't."<sup id="cite_ref-gpreport_27-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gpreport-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> Renault dismissed suggestions that Alonso had <a href="/wiki/Brake_test" title="Brake test">brake tested</a> Hamilton in the incident that led to the McLaren's wing breaking off.<sup id="cite_ref-renaultdefence_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-renaultdefence-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Pat_Symonds" title="Pat Symonds">Pat Symonds</a>, Renault's head of engineering, said his team's <a href="/wiki/Telemetry" title="Telemetry">telemetry</a> indicated Alonso was on full throttle down the straight, and had not touched the brakes: "I think all I can say from our side is that there is no blame attributable to Fernando, which is what some of the speculation might be."<sup id="cite_ref-renaultdefence_33-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-renaultdefence-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> McLaren F1 <a href="/wiki/Chief_executive_officer" title="Chief executive officer">CEO</a> <a href="/wiki/Martin_Whitmarsh" title="Martin Whitmarsh">Martin Whitmarsh</a> said that Hamilton's front wing had broken seconds before the impact, and that the resulting <a href="/wiki/Downforce" title="Downforce">downforce</a> reduction had sucked Hamilton into Alonso's <a href="/wiki/Drafting_(aerodynamics)" title="Drafting (aerodynamics)">slipstream</a> faster than expected.<sup id="cite_ref-hamiltonwing_28-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hamiltonwing-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> However, photos indicate that the front wing on the McLaren could have broken even earlier when Hamilton nudged a car (believed to be Alonso) on the opening lap.<sup id="cite_ref-hamiltonwing_28-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hamiltonwing-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> The later incident left Alonso with damage to the back of the car, impairing his attempts to pass Glock late in the race.<sup id="cite_ref-renaultsunday_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-renaultsunday-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Räikkönen's second-placed finish gave him a three-point lead over Heidfeld in the Drivers' Championship, with 19&#160;points, ahead of Heidfeld with 16, followed by Hamilton, Kubica and Kovalainen, each with 14&#160;points.<sup id="cite_ref-pointsFIA_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pointsFIA-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> BMW Sauber's strong performance gave them the lead in the Constructors' Championship, with 30&#160;points, ahead of Ferrari with 29&#160;points and McLaren with 28&#160;points.<sup id="cite_ref-pointsFIA_6-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pointsFIA-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
