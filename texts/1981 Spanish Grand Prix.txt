<p>The <b>1981 Spanish Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 21 June 1981 at the <a href="/wiki/Circuito_Permanente_del_Jarama" class="mw-redirect" title="Circuito Permanente del Jarama">Circuito Permanente del Jarama</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jarama" title="Jarama">Jarama</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Summary">Summary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1981_Spanish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Summary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The 1981 Spanish Grand Prix featured the second closest finish ever of a Formula One race: after <a href="/wiki/Gilles_Villeneuve" title="Gilles Villeneuve">Gilles Villeneuve</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, the four following cars finished in just 1.24 seconds. This was Villeneuve's last victory, often regarded as his tactical masterpiece.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>There were some changes for this race: <a href="/wiki/Eliseo_Salazar" title="Eliseo Salazar">Eliseo Salazar</a> had left <a href="/wiki/March_Engineering" title="March Engineering">March</a> to join <a href="/wiki/Ensign_(racing_team)" class="mw-redirect" title="Ensign (racing team)">Ensign</a>, replacing <a href="/wiki/Marc_Surer" title="Marc Surer">Marc Surer</a>. Also, <a href="/wiki/John_Player_Special" class="mw-redirect" title="John Player Special">John Player Special</a> sponsorship and livery returned to <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Team Lotus</a> after a 2-year hiatus.
</p><p>The pole went to <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Laffite" title="Jacques Laffite">Jacques Laffite</a> on his <a href="/wiki/Equipe_Ligier" title="Equipe Ligier">Ligier</a>-<a href="/wiki/Equipe_Matra_Sports" title="Equipe Matra Sports">Matra</a> with the two <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a>-<a href="/wiki/Ford_Motor_Company" title="Ford Motor Company">Cosworth</a> of <a href="/wiki/Alan_Jones_(Formula_1)" class="mw-redirect" title="Alan Jones (Formula 1)">Alan Jones</a> and <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Reutemann" title="Carlos Reutemann">Carlos Reutemann</a> second and third ahead of <a href="/wiki/John_Watson_(racing_driver)" title="John Watson (racing driver)">John Watson</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Team_McLaren" class="mw-redirect" title="Team McLaren">McLaren</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Alfa_Romeo_(Formula_One)" class="mw-redirect" title="Alfa Romeo (Formula One)">Alfa Romeo</a> of <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Giacomelli" title="Bruno Giacomelli">Bruno Giacomelli</a>. Gilles Villeneuve was seventh.
</p><p>Race day was unusually hot. At the beginning of the race Jones and Reutemann went into the lead, as Laffite made a poor start. Villeneuve jumped into third place at the first corner, damaging Prost's front wing as he took the position. At the end of the first lap Villeneuve pulled out of Reutemann's slipstream and took second place. Jones began to build a lead but on lap 14 he went off the track, when he was 10 seconds ahead of the Canadian.
</p><p>This left Villeneuve with Reutemann on his tail. Behind them Watson, Laffite and <a href="/wiki/Elio_de_Angelis" title="Elio de Angelis">Elio de Angelis</a> began to close on the dueling leaders. Reutemann was having some trouble with his gearbox and when Laffite arrived behind him there was little the Argentinian could do to stop him from overtaking. Reutemann would later drop behind Watson. The five front-runners became a train of cars, packed together for the remaining laps of the race.
</p><p>Villeneuve used the power of his Ferrari engine on the straight to gain a little margin and not get overtaken by his rivals, but in the corners they were all over him. Many times Laffite pulled alongside the Canadian as they went out a corner but the Ferrari would stay ahead as the horsepower kicked in. The five remained locked together right to the flag, crossing the line covered by just 1.24 seconds to record the second-closest race in the history of Formula One.
</p><p>This would be the last Spanish Grand Prix at Jarama, owing to criticism of the track being too narrow for modern Formula One, the unpleasant conditions and the small crowd<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> (the small turn-out was probably due to the backlash of the <a href="/wiki/1980_Spanish_Grand_Prix" title="1980 Spanish Grand Prix">previous year's race</a> not being counted as a World Championship race, the announcement was made on the weekend itself); and the last Spanish Grand Prix until the <a href="/wiki/1986_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1986 Formula One World Championship">1986</a> season, when it would be held at the newly built <a href="/wiki/Circuito_de_Jerez" title="Circuito de Jerez">Jerez circuit</a> in the south of the country.
</p>
