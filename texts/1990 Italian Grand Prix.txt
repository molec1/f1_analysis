<p>The <b>1990 Italian Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>Coca-Cola 61° Gran Premio d'Italia</b><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 9 September 1990 at <a href="/wiki/Autodromo_Nazionale_di_Monza" title="Autodromo Nazionale di Monza">Monza</a>. It was the twelfth race of the <a href="/wiki/1990_FIA_Formula_One_World_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="1990 FIA Formula One World Championship">1990 FIA Formula One World Championship</a>. The race was the 60th <a href="/wiki/Italian_Grand_Prix" title="Italian Grand Prix">Italian Grand Prix</a> and the 55th to be held at Monza.
</p><p>The 53-lap race was won by Brazilian driver <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/5" title="McLaren MP4/5">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Honda_in_Formula_One" title="Honda in Formula One">Honda</a>. Senna took <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>, led every lap and set the fastest race lap, thus achieving a <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_driver_records#Grand_Slam" title="List of Formula One driver records">Grand Slam</a>. Senna's Driver's Championship rival, Frenchman <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a>, finished second in his <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_641" title="Ferrari 641">Ferrari</a>, some six seconds behind, with Senna's Austrian teammate <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> third.
</p><p>The win enabled Senna to extend his lead over Prost in the Drivers' Championship to 16 points with four races remaining.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1990_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-qualifying_report">Pre-qualifying report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1990_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Pre-qualifying report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Friday morning pre-qualifying session at Monza followed a similar pattern to the previous race at <a href="/wiki/1990_Belgian_Grand_Prix" title="1990 Belgian Grand Prix">Spa</a>, with the same four drivers progressing to the main qualifying sessions. <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Grouillard" title="Olivier Grouillard">Olivier Grouillard</a> was fastest again in the <a href="/wiki/Osella" title="Osella">Osella</a>, his tenth pre-qualification from twelve attempts. <a href="/wiki/Bertrand_Gachot" title="Bertrand Gachot">Bertrand Gachot</a> achieved his best pre-qualifying position of the season so far in second place in the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Coloni" title="Scuderia Coloni">Coloni</a>, his second success at this stage. The <a href="/wiki/Automobiles_Gonfaronnaises_Sportives" title="Automobiles Gonfaronnaises Sportives">AGS</a> cars both pre-qualified again in third and fourth, with <a href="/wiki/Gabriele_Tarquini" title="Gabriele Tarquini">Gabriele Tarquini</a> outpacing <a href="/wiki/Yannick_Dalmas" title="Yannick Dalmas">Yannick Dalmas</a> this time.
</p><p>Therefore the same three cars missed out on pre-qualification, namely the two <a href="/wiki/EuroBrun" title="EuroBrun">EuroBruns</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Life_Racing_Engines" title="Life Racing Engines">Life</a>. Fifth again was the EuroBrun of <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Moreno" title="Roberto Moreno">Roberto Moreno</a>, just under six tenths of a second slower than Dalmas, although <a href="/wiki/Claudio_Langes" title="Claudio Langes">Claudio Langes</a> was nearly 6.5 seconds further adrift in the sister car. <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Giacomelli" title="Bruno Giacomelli">Bruno Giacomelli</a> was 20 seconds further behind in the Life, having only managed two laps at the team's home event before a substantial engine failure. It was the last appearance for their unusual, but hugely underdeveloped <a href="/wiki/W12_engine" title="W12 engine">W12 engine</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Walker_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Walker-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1990_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_report">Race report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1990_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Race report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Near the end of the first lap, <a href="/wiki/Derek_Warwick" title="Derek Warwick">Derek Warwick</a> ran wide at the Parabolica, his <a href="/wiki/Lotus_102" title="Lotus 102">Lotus</a> hitting the guard rail at around 140&#160;mph (230&#160;km/h) and flipping upside down. Warwick clambered out of the car unhurt.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The race was stopped on the second lap and restarted over the original distance, with Warwick taking the spare car and eventually retiring with a clutch failure. Jean Alesi became the first retirement as he went off Variante del Rettifilo on lap 5 and hit the wall, as the Lotus of <a href="/wiki/Martin_Donnelly_(racing_driver)" title="Martin Donnelly (racing driver)">Martin Donnelly</a> had a dramatic engine failure at the start of lap 14. And Alboreto in the Arrows spun off at Ascari with only 1 lap to go.
</p>
