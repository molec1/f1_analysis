<p>The <b>1982 Canadian Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at <a href="/wiki/Circuit_Gilles_Villeneuve" title="Circuit Gilles Villeneuve">Circuit Gilles Villeneuve</a> on 13 June 1982. It was the eighth race of the <a href="/wiki/1982_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1982 Formula One World Championship">1982 Formula One World Championship</a>. This was the first Canadian Grand Prix to be held in June, the organisers having moved the race from the autumn to allow for warmer weather;<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> it has been held in June ever since.
</p><p>The 70-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/Brabham_BT50" title="Brabham BT50">Brabham</a>-<a href="/wiki/BMW_in_Formula_One" title="BMW in Formula One">BMW</a>. It was the first Formula One victory for a BMW-engined car, but the only victory of the season for defending Drivers' Champion Piquet. Team-mate <a href="/wiki/Riccardo_Patrese" title="Riccardo Patrese">Riccardo Patrese</a> finished second in an older <a href="/wiki/Brabham_BT49" title="Brabham BT49">Brabham</a>-<a href="/wiki/Ford_Motor_Company" title="Ford Motor Company">Ford</a>, with <a href="/wiki/John_Watson_(racing_driver)" title="John Watson (racing driver)">John Watson</a> third in a <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/1" title="McLaren MP4/1">McLaren</a>-Ford.
</p><p>The race was marred by the death of Italian driver <a href="/wiki/Riccardo_Paletti" title="Riccardo Paletti">Riccardo Paletti</a>, in only his second F1 race start. At the start, the lights took an unusually long time to turn to green. During this time, <a href="/wiki/Didier_Pironi" title="Didier Pironi">Didier Pironi</a>, who had the pole position, stalled the engine of his <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>. Pironi lifted his hand to signal the problem just as the lights switched to green, which was too late to abort the start. The other cars swerved across the track, trying to squeeze past Pironi's stationary car.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>  <a href="/wiki/Raul_Boesel" title="Raul Boesel">Raul Boesel</a> just clipped the back left of the Ferrari, spinning his <a href="/wiki/March_Engineering" title="March Engineering">March</a> into the path of <a href="/wiki/Eliseo_Salazar" title="Eliseo Salazar">Eliseo Salazar</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jochen_Mass" title="Jochen Mass">Jochen Mass</a>. Salazar, Boesel and Mass suffered minor impacts but it looked as if everyone had passed the Ferrari without serious consequences.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> However, Paletti could not react in time and slammed into the rear of the stranded Ferrari at 180&#160;km/h (around 110&#160;mph), catapulting it into the path of <a href="/wiki/Geoff_Lees_(racing_driver)" title="Geoff Lees (racing driver)">Geoff Lees</a>. The Osella's nose was crushed in severely.
</p><p>Due to the force of the severe impact, Paletti sustained heavy chest injuries and was lying unconscious in his car, wedged against the steering wheel. Didier Pironi and <a href="/wiki/Sid_Watkins" title="Sid Watkins">Sid Watkins</a>, the <a href="/wiki/FIA" class="mw-redirect" title="FIA">FIA</a>'s head doctor, were on the scene to stabilise and assist Paletti. As Watkins climbed over the wreckage of the Osella, the petrol from the fuel tank ignited, enveloping the car in a wall of fire. When the fire was finally put out, the injured Paletti was without a pulse. It took the rescue workers 25 minutes to cut him out safely from his wrecked car, as the sparks caused by the cutting equipment threatened to re-ignite the petrol on the track. He was flown by a medical helicopter to the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Victoria_Hospital,_Montreal" title="Royal Victoria Hospital, Montreal">Royal Victoria Hospital</a>, where he died soon after arriving. His mother was watching from the stands, where they were to celebrate his 24th birthday later that week. Paletti suffered a torn aorta as well as fractures to both legs.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> According to track doctor Dr. Jacques Bouchard his pupils were already dilated when medical personnel arrived and that the extended extraction time made no difference to his chances of survival.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Paletti was the last driver to be killed during a Formula One race weekend until <a href="/wiki/Roland_Ratzenberger" title="Roland Ratzenberger">Roland Ratzenberger</a> at the <a href="/wiki/1994_San_Marino_Grand_Prix" title="1994 San Marino Grand Prix">1994 San Marino Grand Prix</a>, and the last to die in a Formula One car until <a href="/wiki/Elio_de_Angelis" title="Elio de Angelis">Elio de Angelis</a> lost his life while testing for Brabham at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_Paul_Ricard" title="Circuit Paul Ricard">Circuit Paul Ricard</a> in France in 1986.
</p>
