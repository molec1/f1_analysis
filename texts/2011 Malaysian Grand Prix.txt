<p>The <b>2011 Malaysian Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>2011 Formula 1 Petronas Malaysia Grand Prix</b>)<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 10 April 2011 at the <a href="/wiki/Sepang_International_Circuit" title="Sepang International Circuit">Sepang International Circuit</a> in <a href="/wiki/Selangor" title="Selangor">Selangor</a>, <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a>. It was the second round of the <a href="/wiki/2011_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2011 Formula One season">2011 Formula One season</a>. The 56-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> after starting from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>. <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> finished second in a <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> completed the podium for <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a>, in third place.<sup id="cite_ref-Race_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Race-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As a consequence of the race, Vettel extended his lead in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">World Drivers' Championship</a> to 24&#160;points over Button. Button's McLaren teammate <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>, who finished eighth in Malaysia, fell 4&#160;points behind Button in third, tied on points with <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> who finished fourth in the race. In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">World Constructors' Championship</a>, Red Bull's 37-point haul from the weekend allowed them to extend the championship lead over McLaren to 24&#160;points, with <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> a further 12&#160;points behind in third position.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Malaysian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Malaysian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Ferrari_150_Italia_side_fin_2011_Malaysia.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/60/Ferrari_150_Italia_side_fin_2011_Malaysia.jpg/220px-Ferrari_150_Italia_side_fin_2011_Malaysia.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="144" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/60/Ferrari_150_Italia_side_fin_2011_Malaysia.jpg/330px-Ferrari_150_Italia_side_fin_2011_Malaysia.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/60/Ferrari_150_Italia_side_fin_2011_Malaysia.jpg/440px-Ferrari_150_Italia_side_fin_2011_Malaysia.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2560" data-file-height="1672" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Ferrari_150_Italia_side_fin_2011_Malaysia.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> were celebrating the 150th anniversary of the <a href="/wiki/Italian_unification" title="Italian unification">unification of Italy</a></div></div></div>
<p>After failing to qualify in <a href="/wiki/2011_Australian_Grand_Prix#Qualifying" title="2011 Australian Grand Prix">Melbourne</a> when they were forced to use the front wing from their <a href="/wiki/Hispania_F110" title="Hispania F110">2010 car</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hispania_Racing" class="mw-redirect" title="Hispania Racing">Hispania Racing</a> arrived at the circuit confident of being able to qualify for the race because their <a href="/wiki/Hispania_F111" title="Hispania F111">2011 specification</a> front wing satisfactorily completed its mandatory crash tests.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Several teams made driver changes for the first practice session. <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a> once again substituted for <a href="/wiki/Paul_di_Resta" title="Paul di Resta">Paul di Resta</a> at <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a>, whilst <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> drove in the place of <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9bastien_Buemi" title="Sébastien Buemi">Sébastien Buemi</a> at <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Scuderia Toro Rosso</a>, having previously driven in the place of <a href="/wiki/Jaime_Alguersuari" title="Jaime Alguersuari">Jaime Alguersuari</a> in <a href="/wiki/2011_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="2011 Australian Grand Prix">Melbourne</a>. <a href="/wiki/2011_GP2_Series_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2011 GP2 Series season">GP2</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Davide_Valsecchi" title="Davide Valsecchi">Davide Valsecchi</a> also took part, replacing <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> at <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus_(2010%E2%80%9311)" title="Team Lotus (2010–11)">Lotus</a>.
</p><p>Tyre supplier <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_tyres" title="Formula One tyres">Pirelli</a> brought its silver-banded hard compound tyre as the harder "prime" tyre and the yellow-banded soft compound as the softer "option" compound. This was the same tyre selection that Bridgestone had chosen to bring to the Malaysian Grand Prix for the past two years.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The high temperatures in <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a> and the abrasive surface of the Sepang circuit led to tyre supplier <a href="/wiki/Pirelli" title="Pirelli">Pirelli</a> making predictions of high wear rates throughout qualifying and the race to the extent that pit stop strategy would be the deciding factor in the outcome.<sup id="cite_ref-lap_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lap-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The tyre manufacturer also supplied an experimental harder compound of rubber for use during free practice with a view to introducing the tyre for the <a href="/wiki/2011_Turkish_Grand_Prix" title="2011 Turkish Grand Prix">Turkish Grand Prix</a>, but rejected it following feedback from the teams.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Shortly after qualifying, the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">FIA</a> moved the pole position slot from the left- to the right-hand side of the circuit.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The pole slot had traditionally been on the left-hand side of the circuit because it offered the optimal racing line into the tight first corner, but was moved because of the build-up of marbles from the Pirelli tyres off the racing line, which runs over the right-hand side of the circuit.
</p><p>Before the race, winner of <a href="/wiki/2011_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="2011 Australian Grand Prix">race one</a> in <a href="/wiki/Melbourne_Grand_Prix_Circuit" class="mw-redirect" title="Melbourne Grand Prix Circuit">Melbourne</a> - <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> - led the Drivers' Championship with the 25 points he gained for winning the only race thus far in <a href="/wiki/2011_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2011 Formula One season">2011</a>. The rest of the standings were as the drivers had finished at that race - <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> on 18, <a href="/wiki/Vitaly_Petrov" title="Vitaly Petrov">Vitaly Petrov</a> on 15 etc.
</p><p>In the Constructors' Championship, <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a> lead with 35 points, <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> were second with 26 (9 points behind), and <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> were a further 8 behind McLaren on 18 points. Renault were in fourth, just behind on 15 points. <a href="/wiki/Toro_Rosso" class="mw-redirect" title="Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso</a> on 4, and <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a> on 3, completed the points scoring teams.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Free_practice">Free practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Malaysian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Free practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Jerome_d%27Ambrosio_suffered_an_accident_2011_Malaysia_FP1.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2f/Jerome_d%27Ambrosio_suffered_an_accident_2011_Malaysia_FP1.jpg/220px-Jerome_d%27Ambrosio_suffered_an_accident_2011_Malaysia_FP1.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="135" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2f/Jerome_d%27Ambrosio_suffered_an_accident_2011_Malaysia_FP1.jpg/330px-Jerome_d%27Ambrosio_suffered_an_accident_2011_Malaysia_FP1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2f/Jerome_d%27Ambrosio_suffered_an_accident_2011_Malaysia_FP1.jpg/440px-Jerome_d%27Ambrosio_suffered_an_accident_2011_Malaysia_FP1.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2560" data-file-height="1576" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Jerome_d%27Ambrosio_suffered_an_accident_2011_Malaysia_FP1.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/J%C3%A9r%C3%B4me_d%27Ambrosio" title="Jérôme d&#39;Ambrosio">Jérôme d'Ambrosio</a> suffered a catastrophic suspension failure during the first free practice session</div></div></div>
<p>The first practice session was run in humid but dry conditions, despite forecasts of heavy rain. <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> was the fastest driver in the first session, over a second and a half faster than <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> and two seconds faster than <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>. In the second <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a> <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_RB7" title="Red Bull RB7">car</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> finished seventeenth overall, four seconds off Webber's pace; however, with comments from team principal <a href="/wiki/Christian_Horner" title="Christian Horner">Christian Horner</a> in the build-up to the event suggesting that the unraced KERS device would be essential, Vettel's program was focused on developing the KERS unit rather than outright pace. Overall lap times were considerably slower at the circuit than in <a href="/wiki/2010_Malaysian_Grand_Prix" title="2010 Malaysian Grand Prix">2010</a>, with Webber's fastest time over two and a half seconds slower than <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>'s time in the same session a year previously.<sup id="cite_ref-lap_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lap-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Pastor_Maldonado%27s_crash_2011_Malaysia_FP2_1.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/85/Pastor_Maldonado%27s_crash_2011_Malaysia_FP2_1.jpg/220px-Pastor_Maldonado%27s_crash_2011_Malaysia_FP2_1.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="93" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/85/Pastor_Maldonado%27s_crash_2011_Malaysia_FP2_1.jpg/330px-Pastor_Maldonado%27s_crash_2011_Malaysia_FP2_1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/85/Pastor_Maldonado%27s_crash_2011_Malaysia_FP2_1.jpg/440px-Pastor_Maldonado%27s_crash_2011_Malaysia_FP2_1.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1600" data-file-height="677" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Pastor_Maldonado%27s_crash_2011_Malaysia_FP2_1.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Pastor_Maldonado" title="Pastor Maldonado">Pastor Maldonado</a> ran wide at the pit entry and hit the wall during second practice.</div></div></div>
<p>The session was scattered with incidents; <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Racing" title="Virgin Racing">Virgin</a> driver <a href="/wiki/J%C3%A9r%C3%B4me_d%27Ambrosio" title="Jérôme d&#39;Ambrosio">Jérôme d'Ambrosio</a> suffered a mechanical fault that caused his right-front suspension to fail. The team were unable to repair the problem in time for the second practice session. Further down the pit lane, <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a> experienced two similar problems, first with <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a>, whose front-right tyre locked in place, and he was able to limp back to the garage. <a href="/wiki/Vitaly_Petrov" title="Vitaly Petrov">Vitaly Petrov</a> was sidelined with a mechanical failure for most of the first session, and spun off at turn 9 when he experienced a similar problem to Heidfeld. Both Renaults spent most of the second session in the pits while the team investigated the problems, which was discovered to have come from a material fault in the suspension uprights, but they were limited to just a handful of laps overall.
</p><p>Webber once again topped the second practice session, but his margin to <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> was just five thousandths of a second. <a href="/wiki/Pastor_Maldonado" title="Pastor Maldonado">Pastor Maldonado</a> set the fastest time early in the session, but damaged his <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW33" title="Williams FW33">Williams</a> when he spun into a wall in the pit lane entry and slipped down the order. <a href="/wiki/Vitantonio_Liuzzi" title="Vitantonio Liuzzi">Vitantonio Liuzzi</a> also encountered trouble, stopping on the circuit after bouncing over a kerb, but he was able to get his car going once more. The <a href="/wiki/Hispania_Racing" class="mw-redirect" title="Hispania Racing">Hispania</a>'s new front wing worked as expected, with Liuzzi able to set times within 107% of the leader's benchmark in both practice sessions.
</p><p>Hamilton came first in the final practice session, ahead of Webber and Button. The session was free of incidents, aside from several spins by <a href="/wiki/Paul_di_Resta" title="Paul di Resta">Paul di Resta</a>, while Liuzzi was held up in the pits for most of the session with a mechanical fault and only took to the circuit in the last fifteen minutes.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Malaysian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Narain_Karthikeyan_2011_Malaysia_Qualify.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/96/Narain_Karthikeyan_2011_Malaysia_Qualify.jpg/220px-Narain_Karthikeyan_2011_Malaysia_Qualify.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/96/Narain_Karthikeyan_2011_Malaysia_Qualify.jpg/330px-Narain_Karthikeyan_2011_Malaysia_Qualify.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/96/Narain_Karthikeyan_2011_Malaysia_Qualify.jpg/440px-Narain_Karthikeyan_2011_Malaysia_Qualify.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1600" data-file-height="1202" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Narain_Karthikeyan_2011_Malaysia_Qualify.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Narain_Karthikeyan" title="Narain Karthikeyan">Narain Karthikeyan</a> completed the first full grid of the year but both HRT cars failed to finish the race</div></div></div>
<p>The qualifying session was closely fought among the Red Bull and McLaren drivers, with the difference between pole and second place just one tenth of a second, as opposed to the eight tenths seen in Melbourne.
</p><p>The first session was interrupted when <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9bastien_Buemi" title="Sébastien Buemi">Sébastien Buemi</a>'s Toro Rosso shed part of its sidepod while Buemi was on a flying lap. There was no internal damage to the car, and Buemi was able to return to the pits with the internal workings of the car showing. The session was red-flagged for several minutes while marshalls retrieved the bodywork. Buemi's car was inspected by the FIA and cleared to return to the circuit, though the exact cause of the problem remained unknown.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Because of the incident involving Buemi, several teams were forced to abort flying laps, including the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferraris</a> of <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>, who had been in the process of setting flying laps on the hard compound tyres. When the session restarted, the team were forced to sacrifice one set of soft tyres for both cars. By the end of the session, both <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus_(2010%E2%80%9311)" title="Team Lotus (2010–11)">Lotuses</a> were eliminated, a clear two seconds faster than the <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Racing" title="Virgin Racing">Virgins</a> of Glock and d'Ambrosio. Both <a href="/wiki/Hispania_Racing" class="mw-redirect" title="Hispania Racing">Hispanias</a> were also eliminated, but comfortably made the grid as <a href="/wiki/Narain_Karthikeyan" title="Narain Karthikeyan">Narain Karthikeyan</a> in twenty-fourth position was a second inside the 107% margin. <a href="/wiki/Pastor_Maldonado" title="Pastor Maldonado">Pastor Maldonado</a> was also eliminated, qualifying in eighteenth place.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Sebastien_Buemi_2011_Malaysia_FP2_1.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e1/Sebastien_Buemi_2011_Malaysia_FP2_1.jpg/220px-Sebastien_Buemi_2011_Malaysia_FP2_1.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="92" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e1/Sebastien_Buemi_2011_Malaysia_FP2_1.jpg/330px-Sebastien_Buemi_2011_Malaysia_FP2_1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e1/Sebastien_Buemi_2011_Malaysia_FP2_1.jpg/440px-Sebastien_Buemi_2011_Malaysia_FP2_1.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2560" data-file-height="1075" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Sebastien_Buemi_2011_Malaysia_FP2_1.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9bastien_Buemi" title="Sébastien Buemi">Sébastien Buemi</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Toro_Rosso_STR6" title="Toro Rosso STR6">Toro Rosso</a> shed part of its sidepod during qualifying.</div></div></div>
<p>The second qualifying period saw <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> top the timing sheets, the first person to set a lap time under 1:36.000 all weekend. Several teams, most notably <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a> elected to do just one run late in the session, with both Petrov and Heidfeld easily advancing to the third and final session. A late lap from <a href="/wiki/Kamui_Kobayashi" title="Kamui Kobayashi">Kamui Kobayashi</a> saw him qualify for the final session at the expense of <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>, who was demoted to eleventh place for the second race in a row. Buemi recovered from his earlier scare to qualify twelfth, with teammate <a href="/wiki/Jaime_Alguersuari" title="Jaime Alguersuari">Jaime Alguersuari</a> in thirteenth. <a href="/wiki/Paul_di_Resta" title="Paul di Resta">Paul di Resta</a> out-qualified <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a> by placing fourteenth, followed by <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a> in the second <a href="/wiki/Williams_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams F1">Williams</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a> respectively, with Sutil finishing seventeenth overall.
</p><p>The third period began with the remaining ten drivers divided into two distinct groups: the Red Bull and McLaren drivers, who all set two flying laps, and everyone else, only going out once, many with the hope of saving an extra set of tyres for the race. <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> set the early benchmark with a time of exactly 1:35.000, followed by the Red Bulls and <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a>. <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> was the next driver to set a time, but he was over half a second off Hamilton's pace. The top four drivers emerged again to set their final times, with each driver getting progressively faster. Jenson Button improved on his initial time, but remained fourth. <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> bested him, but was still two tenths of a second off Hamilton's time. Hamilton himself improved in kind, and looked as if he had secured pole position before <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> crossed the line, a tenth of a second faster than Hamilton. With Vettel first and Hamilton second, followed by Webber, Button and Alonso, the top five drivers lined up in the same order as they did in Melbourne. <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> qualified in sixth place for Renault, followed by Massa in the second Ferrari and teammate Petrov in the second Renault in eighth. <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> and Kamui Kobayashi completed the top ten.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Lewis_Hamilton_and_Fernando_Alonso_2011_Malaysia_2.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c6/Lewis_Hamilton_and_Fernando_Alonso_2011_Malaysia_2.jpg/220px-Lewis_Hamilton_and_Fernando_Alonso_2011_Malaysia_2.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="156" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c6/Lewis_Hamilton_and_Fernando_Alonso_2011_Malaysia_2.jpg/330px-Lewis_Hamilton_and_Fernando_Alonso_2011_Malaysia_2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c6/Lewis_Hamilton_and_Fernando_Alonso_2011_Malaysia_2.jpg/440px-Lewis_Hamilton_and_Fernando_Alonso_2011_Malaysia_2.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2560" data-file-height="1817" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Lewis_Hamilton_and_Fernando_Alonso_2011_Malaysia_2.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Lewis Hamilton and Fernando Alonso battle during the race.</div></div></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Malaysian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In what would be a dominant race for the 2010 World Champion, Vettel made a clean break from the lights and quickly began developing a strong lead from the rest of the pack. The other big winner was Nick Heidfeld, who was able to move from sixth to second at the start. By lap 9 Vettel had built a 5.2 second lead over his fellow German,<sup id="cite_ref-bbc_race_as_it_happened_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc_race_as_it_happened-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> and was able to comfortably bring his car home in first in spite of KERS problems that plagued the second half of his race.<sup id="cite_ref-Race_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Race-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> while Pastor Maldonado retired with a misfire. Barrichello was the next retirement with a gearbox problem, which they later traced to the hydraulics. On lap 24, the Sauber of <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a> was struck by a piece of debris, possibly from an unidentified car. The debris damaged the front wing and floor of the Sauber before passing through both the chassis and the protective <a href="/wiki/Zylon" title="Zylon">Zylon</a> panel, and hitting the <a href="/wiki/Electronic_control_unit" title="Electronic control unit">ECU</a>. The ECU was rendered inactive, stopping the car. The debris then passed out of the car by smashing through the sidepod.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Jarno Trulli slid across the gravel before retiring with a clutch failure on lap 31.
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Jenson_Button_2011_Malaysia_FP3.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a8/Jenson_Button_2011_Malaysia_FP3.jpg/220px-Jenson_Button_2011_Malaysia_FP3.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="127" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a8/Jenson_Button_2011_Malaysia_FP3.jpg/330px-Jenson_Button_2011_Malaysia_FP3.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a8/Jenson_Button_2011_Malaysia_FP3.jpg/440px-Jenson_Button_2011_Malaysia_FP3.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1600" data-file-height="923" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Jenson_Button_2011_Malaysia_FP3.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> finished in second position, for his first podium of the year</div></div></div>
<p>The Hispanias of Karthikeyan and Liuzzi were withdrawn by the team for "safety reasons".<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Karthikeyan experienced an abnormal spike in water temperature on lap 15, whilst Liuzzi's car was subject to vibrations from the rear wing. Although neither fault was terminal, the team elected to retire the cars rather than risk further damage.
</p><p>Behind Vettel, the McLarens of Lewis Hamilton and Jenson Button fought with Fernando Alonso for position, Button coming out on top and ultimately taking his first podium of the season, ahead of Nick Heidfeld who finished third, whilst both Hamilton and Alonso were penalised for their actions on track.<sup id="cite_ref-Race_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Race-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Further back, D'Ambrosio pulled off the track with an electronics failure, and later Petrov crashed out from eighth place.
</p>
