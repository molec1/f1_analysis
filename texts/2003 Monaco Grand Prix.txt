<p>The <b>2003 Monaco Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>LXI Grand Prix de Monaco</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 1 June 2003 at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Monaco" title="Circuit de Monaco">Circuit de Monaco</a>. It was the seventh race of the <a href="/wiki/2003_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2003 Formula One season">2003 Formula One season</a>. The 78-lap race was won by Juan Pablo Montoya in a <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> car from a third position start. Kimi Räikkönen finished second driving for <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>, with Michael Schumacher third in a <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>.
</p><p>There were no recorded on track overtakes during this race, one of the very few occasions in Formula One history where this has occurred. The other three races where this happened were the controversial <a href="/wiki/2005_United_States_Grand_Prix" title="2005 United States Grand Prix">2005 United States Grand Prix</a>, <a href="/wiki/2009_European_Grand_Prix" title="2009 European Grand Prix">2009 European Grand Prix</a> and the <a href="/wiki/2017_Russian_Grand_Prix" title="2017 Russian Grand Prix">2017 Russian Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2003_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2003_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Grand Prix was contested by 20 drivers, in ten teams of two.<sup id="cite_ref-entrants_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-entrants-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The teams, also known as <a href="/wiki/Formula_One#Constructors" title="Formula One">constructors</a>, were <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a>, <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>, <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jaguar_Racing" title="Jaguar Racing">Jaguar</a>, <a href="/wiki/British_American_Racing" title="British American Racing">BAR</a>, <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Toyota_Racing_(Formula_One_team)" title="Toyota Racing (Formula One team)">Toyota</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-entrants_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-entrants-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Before the race, McLaren driver <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> led the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a> with 40 points; Ferrari driver <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> was second on 38 points. Behind them in the Drivers' Championship, <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> was third on 26 points in the other Ferrari, with <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> and <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> on 25 and 23 points respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-championships_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-championships-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a>, Ferrari were leading with 64 points and McLaren were second on 63 points, with Renault third on 35 points.<sup id="cite_ref-championships_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-championships-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice_and_qualifying">Practice and qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2003_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Practice and qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Four practice sessions were held before the Sunday race—Two on Thursday, and two on Saturday. The Thursday morning and afternoon sessions each lasted an hour. The third and final practice sessions were held on Saturday morning and lasted 45 minutes.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Jaguar driver <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> set the fastest time in the first session, posting a lap of 1:16.373, one-tenth of a second quicker than <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> and Coulthard, in second and third places respectively. The Renault cars were fourth and fifth fastest; Alonso ahead of <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a>, with Michael Schumacher rounding out the top six.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/List_of_motorsport_terminology#Q" class="mw-redirect" title="List of motorsport terminology">qualifying session</a> was run as a one-lap session and took place on Thursday and Saturday afternoon. The cars were run one at a time; the Thursday running order was determined with the Championship leading heading out first. The Saturday running order was determined by times set in Friday afternoon qualifying with the fastest heading out last and the slowest running first. The lap times from the Thursday afternoon session did not determine the grid order.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Jenson Button suffered a heavy crash during Saturday morning practice and his resulting injuries meant that he took no further part in the weekend.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
