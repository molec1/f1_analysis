<p>The <b>1982 Austrian Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 15 August 1982 at the <a href="/wiki/%C3%96sterreichring" class="mw-redirect" title="Österreichring">Österreichring</a> in <a href="/wiki/Spielberg,_Styria" title="Spielberg, Styria">Spielberg, Styria</a>, <a href="/wiki/Austria" title="Austria">Austria</a>. The race, contested over 53 laps, was the thirteenth race of the <a href="/wiki/1982_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="1982 Formula One season">1982 Formula One season</a> and was won by <a href="/wiki/Elio_de_Angelis" title="Elio de Angelis">Elio de Angelis</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/Lotus_91" title="Lotus 91">Lotus</a>-<a href="/wiki/Ford_Motor_Company" title="Ford Motor Company">Ford</a>. It was the first win for Lotus since the <a href="/wiki/1978_Dutch_Grand_Prix" title="1978 Dutch Grand Prix">1978 Dutch Grand Prix</a>. De Angelis held off <a href="/wiki/Keke_Rosberg" title="Keke Rosberg">Keke Rosberg</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW08" title="Williams FW08">Williams</a>-Ford to win by just 0.05 seconds, or less than half a car length. <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Laffite" title="Jacques Laffite">Jacques Laffite</a> finished third in a <a href="/wiki/Ligier_JS19" title="Ligier JS19">Ligier</a>-<a href="/wiki/Equipe_Matra_Sports" title="Equipe Matra Sports">Matra</a>, one lap behind.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1982_Austrian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A <a href="/wiki/Chicane" title="Chicane">chicane</a> had been added at the entrance to the pits earlier in the year. <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Brabham" title="Brabham">Brabham</a> led into the first corner from pole position, while <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> passed Piquet's team-mate <a href="/wiki/Riccardo_Patrese" title="Riccardo Patrese">Riccardo Patrese</a> for second. Further back, there was a collision which eliminated the two <a href="/wiki/Alfa_Romeo_in_Formula_One" title="Alfa Romeo in Formula One">Alfa Romeos</a> of <a href="/wiki/Andrea_de_Cesaris" title="Andrea de Cesaris">Andrea de Cesaris</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Giacomelli" title="Bruno Giacomelli">Bruno Giacomelli</a>, as well as the Williams of <a href="/wiki/Derek_Daly" title="Derek Daly">Derek Daly</a>. Prost's advantage over Patrese lasted only a few corners before the Italian re-passed him. On lap 2, Patrese took the lead from Piquet and the two Brabhams immediately began to pull away on their half-full tanks.
</p><p>Shortly before half distance, Piquet made the first planned mid-race fuel and tyre pit stop in modern F1 history. He rejoined in fourth place, behind Prost and <a href="/wiki/Elio_de_Angelis" title="Elio de Angelis">Elio de Angelis</a>, and just ahead of <a href="/wiki/Keke_Rosberg" title="Keke Rosberg">Keke Rosberg</a>. Shortly afterwards, Patrese made his stop, having built up a sufficient lead to rejoin the race still in first place. However, the Italian suffered an engine failure three laps later, leaving Prost to inherit a comfortable lead from de Angelis.
</p><p>Piquet was back up to third but was now in trouble, unable to make any inroads into the leaders and struggling to stay ahead of Rosberg. On lap 32, the Brazilian retired with an electrical failure. Free of the Brabham, Rosberg began closing in on de Angelis, who at this point was ten seconds ahead.
</p><p>Prost was leading by half a minute when on lap 49, five from the end, his Renault suffered a mechanical failure. This left de Angelis and Rosberg – neither of whom had won a Grand Prix before – battling for the lead. At the start of the last lap de Angelis was 1.6 seconds ahead. Rosberg closed rapidly on the final tour and was right on the Lotus's gearbox heading into the final corner, the Rindt Kurve. De Angelis calmly defended the inside line, but slid wide on the exit of the corner. Rosberg dived inside on the home straight, only to come up short by 0.050 seconds, or less than half a car length.
</p><p>De Angelis joyfully celebrated his maiden win, while Rosberg had nonetheless boosted his Driver's Championship chances, moving into second place ahead of <a href="/wiki/John_Watson_(racing_driver)" title="John Watson (racing driver)">John Watson</a>, who had suffered an engine failure. <a href="/wiki/Equipe_Ligier" title="Equipe Ligier">Ligier</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Laffite" title="Jacques Laffite">Jacques Laffite</a> completed the podium, coming home a lap down, while <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Tambay" title="Patrick Tambay">Patrick Tambay</a> was fourth in the sole <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, having recovered from a puncture early in the race. Watson's <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> team-mate <a href="/wiki/Niki_Lauda" title="Niki Lauda">Niki Lauda</a> was fifth, with <a href="/wiki/Mauro_Baldi" title="Mauro Baldi">Mauro Baldi</a> taking the final point in his <a href="/wiki/Arrows_Grand_Prix_International" title="Arrows Grand Prix International">Arrows</a>.
</p><p>This was the 150th World Championship race victory for the <a href="/wiki/Cosworth_DFV" title="Cosworth DFV">Cosworth DFV</a> engine. However, it was also to be the last hailed by <a href="/wiki/Colin_Chapman" title="Colin Chapman">Colin Chapman</a>'s famous act of throwing his cloth cap into the air after a Lotus victory, as Chapman would die four months later.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> It was also the last win by a Chapman-built Lotus, and the team's last win until the <a href="/wiki/1985_Portuguese_Grand_Prix" title="1985 Portuguese Grand Prix">1985 Portuguese Grand Prix</a>.
</p>
