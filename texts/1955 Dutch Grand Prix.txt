<p>The <b>1955 Dutch Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held at <a href="/wiki/Circuit_Zandvoort" title="Circuit Zandvoort">Zandvoort</a> on June 19, 1955. It was race 5 of 7 in the <a href="/wiki/1955_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1955 World Championship of Drivers">1955 World Championship of Drivers</a>. The 100-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Juan_Manuel_Fangio" title="Juan Manuel Fangio">Juan Manuel Fangio</a> after he started from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>. His teammate <a href="/wiki/Stirling_Moss" title="Stirling Moss">Stirling Moss</a> finished second and <a href="/wiki/Maserati_in_motorsport" title="Maserati in motorsport">Maserati</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Luigi_Musso" title="Luigi Musso">Luigi Musso</a> came in third.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_report">Race report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1955_Dutch_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Race report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Despite a track made slippery by continuous drizzle, the record crowd was treated to some outstanding  driving as the masters slid their machines through the sand-dunes. Fangio and Moss again took up the lead with Musso's Maserati in pursuit. Kling and Behra were chasing furiously until the German spun into the sand and retired. Mieres then took up the challenge, passing Behra and closing the gap.
</p><p>However, Musso was too far ahead and was even catching the Mercedes pair who were having to go at full pelt to keep ahead. It was only when he spun off and dropped back that they could relax slightly.
</p><p>It was another impressive 1-2 for them, despite Moss sustaining a smoking engine late on in the race. Musso had given them a hard challenge and Fangio was the first to congratulate him on his podium finish.
</p>
