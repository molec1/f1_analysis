<p>The <b>1952 Belgian Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_Two" title="Formula Two">Formula Two</a> race held on 22 June 1952 at <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Spa-Francorchamps" title="Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps">Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps</a>. It was race 3 of 8 in the <a href="/wiki/1952_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1952 World Championship of Drivers">1952 World Championship of Drivers</a>, in which each Grand Prix was run to Formula Two rules rather than the Formula One regulations normally used.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1952_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Maserati_in_motorsport" title="Maserati in motorsport">Maserati</a>'s new <a href="/wiki/Maserati_A6GCM" title="Maserati A6GCM">A6GCM</a> was still not ready, and, to compound this, their lead driver <a href="/wiki/Juan_Manuel_Fangio" title="Juan Manuel Fangio">Juan Manuel Fangio</a> had suffered back injuries at the non-championship <a href="/wiki/Monza_Grand_Prix" title="Monza Grand Prix">Monza Grand Prix</a>. This meant that <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> were once again favoured for success in the race, with their driver lineup consisting of <a href="/wiki/Alberto_Ascari" title="Alberto Ascari">Alberto Ascari</a> (in place of <a href="/wiki/Andr%C3%A9_Simon_(racing_driver)" title="André Simon (racing driver)">André Simon</a>), <a href="/wiki/Giuseppe_Farina" title="Giuseppe Farina">Nino Farina</a> and <a href="/wiki/Piero_Taruffi" title="Piero Taruffi">Piero Taruffi</a>. There were also two privateer Ferrari entries: local driver <a href="/wiki/Charles_de_Tornaco" title="Charles de Tornaco">Charles de Tornaco</a> of <a href="/wiki/Ecurie_Francorchamps" title="Ecurie Francorchamps">Ecurie Francorchamps</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Louis_Rosier" title="Louis Rosier">Louis Rosier</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Gordini" title="Gordini">Gordini</a> team expanded their lineup to include Belgian driver <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Claes" title="Johnny Claes">Johnny Claes</a>, alongside <a href="/wiki/Jean_Behra" title="Jean Behra">Behra</a>, <a href="/wiki/Robert_Manzon" title="Robert Manzon">Manzon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Birabongse_Bhanudej" title="Birabongse Bhanudej">Bira</a>. American <a href="/wiki/Robert_O%27Brien_(auto_racer)" class="mw-redirect" title="Robert O&#39;Brien (auto racer)">Robert O'Brien</a> also drove a <a href="/wiki/Simca-Gordini" class="mw-redirect" title="Simca-Gordini">Simca-Gordini</a> for this race. <a href="/wiki/Hersham_and_Walton_Motors" title="Hersham and Walton Motors">HWM</a> also recruited a pair of Belgian drivers — <a href="/wiki/Paul_Fr%C3%A8re" title="Paul Frère">Paul Frère</a> and <a href="/wiki/Roger_Laurent" title="Roger Laurent">Roger Laurent</a> — who raced alongside regular drivers <a href="/wiki/Peter_Collins_(racing_driver)" title="Peter Collins (racing driver)">Peter Collins</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lance_Macklin" title="Lance Macklin">Lance Macklin</a>. <a href="/wiki/Stirling_Moss" title="Stirling Moss">Stirling Moss</a> switched from HWM to <a href="/wiki/English_Racing_Automobiles" title="English Racing Automobiles">ERA</a> for this race. A handful of other privateer entrants also took part, including future World Champion <a href="/wiki/Mike_Hawthorn" title="Mike Hawthorn">Mike Hawthorn</a>, who made his debut in a <a href="/wiki/Cooper_Car_Company" title="Cooper Car Company">Cooper</a>-<a href="/wiki/Bristol_Cars" title="Bristol Cars">Bristol</a>.
</p><p>Ascari headed an all-Ferrari front row, with teammates Farina and Taruffi in second and third, respectively. The Gordinis of Manzon and Behra made up row two, while the third row consisted of Hawthorn, <a href="/wiki/Ken_Wharton" title="Ken Wharton">Ken Wharton</a> (in a <a href="/wiki/Frazer-Nash" class="mw-redirect" title="Frazer-Nash">Frazer-Nash</a>), and Frère, who was the highest qualifier of the five Belgian drivers on the grid.
</p><p>Taruffi started badly in the rain, dropping to ninth by the end of the first lap, while Behra overhauled the two leading Ferraris to take the lead of the race. Moss also started well, before his car broke down half way through the first lap. Behra's lead was short-lived, as both Ascari and Farina overtook him on the second lap, subsequently holding first and second for the remainder of the race. Behra dropped to fourth when the recovering Taruffi passed him on lap 13. On the following lap Taruffi spun at Malmédy and Behra hit him, causing both to retire. Manzon overtook Hawthorn to assume what was now third place. Despite suffering from fuel leakage problems, Hawthorn was able to maintain fourth place until the end of the race. His fellow debutant Paul Frère also finished in the points, in fifth.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Ascari's win (with fastest lap), and Taruffi's retirement, meant that the two now shared the lead of the Championship, on nine points each. <a href="/wiki/1952_Indianapolis_500" title="1952 Indianapolis 500">Indianapolis 500</a> winner <a href="/wiki/Troy_Ruttman" title="Troy Ruttman">Troy Ruttman</a> was in third, while Farina's second-place finish raised him to fourth in the standings, three points adrift of the joint Championship leaders.
</p>
