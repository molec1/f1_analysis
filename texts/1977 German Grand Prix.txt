<p>The <b>1977 German Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at <a href="/wiki/Hockenheimring" title="Hockenheimring">Hockenheimring</a> on 31 July 1977. It was the eleventh race of the 1977 World Championship of F1 Drivers and the 1977 International Cup for F1 Constructors.
</p><p>The German Grand Prix was moved to Hockenheim following <a href="/wiki/Niki_Lauda" title="Niki Lauda">Niki Lauda</a>'s near-fatal accident at the dangerous <a href="/wiki/N%C3%BCrburgring" title="Nürburgring">Nürburgring</a> in <a href="/wiki/1976_German_Grand_Prix" title="1976 German Grand Prix">1976</a>. This was the second time the race was held at Hockenheim, the first being in <a href="/wiki/1970_German_Grand_Prix" title="1970 German Grand Prix">1970</a>.
</p><p>The 47-lap race was won by Lauda, driving a <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_312T" title="Ferrari 312T">Ferrari</a>. <a href="/wiki/Jody_Scheckter" title="Jody Scheckter">Jody Scheckter</a> finished second in a <a href="/wiki/Wolf_WR1" title="Wolf WR1">Wolf</a>-<a href="/wiki/Ford_Motor_Company" title="Ford Motor Company">Ford</a>, having started from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>, while <a href="/wiki/Hans-Joachim_Stuck" title="Hans-Joachim Stuck">Hans-Joachim Stuck</a> was third in a <a href="/wiki/Brabham_BT45" title="Brabham BT45">Brabham</a>-<a href="/wiki/Alfa_Romeo_in_Formula_One" title="Alfa Romeo in Formula One">Alfa Romeo</a>.
</p><p>During the race, <a href="/wiki/Team_Penske#Formula_One" title="Team Penske">Penske</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Hans_Heyer" title="Hans Heyer">Hans Heyer</a> participated despite not qualifying.  Mechanical issues forced Meyer to retire on lap 9, which caused his participation to be discovered.  He was disqualified, making him the only driver to not qualify, not finish and be disqualified in a single race.<sup id="cite_ref-Heyer_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Heyer-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1977_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In qualifying, <a href="/wiki/Jody_Scheckter" title="Jody Scheckter">Jody Scheckter</a> took his first pole of the season, ahead of <a href="/wiki/John_Watson_(racing_driver)" title="John Watson (racing driver)">John Watson</a> and then <a href="/wiki/Niki_Lauda" title="Niki Lauda">Niki Lauda</a> who headed the second row. The start of the race was given by a green flag due to red/green lights being damaged by a service vehicle, which as a result caused an accident near the back of the grid between <a href="/wiki/Alan_Jones_(racing_driver)" title="Alan Jones (racing driver)">Alan Jones</a> and <a href="/wiki/Clay_Regazzoni" title="Clay Regazzoni">Clay Regazzoni</a> (putting both drivers out on the spot).<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Scheckter kept the lead at the first corner with both Watson and Lauda keeping their positions. Watson put pressure on Scheckter until his engine failed on the eighth lap, giving second to Lauda who passed Scheckter soon after and began to pull away. Scheckter battled for second with <a href="/wiki/James_Hunt" title="James Hunt">James Hunt</a> until the defending champion retired with an engine failure which was caused by a broken fuel pump on lap 33, thus giving third to Watson's teammate and home driver <a href="/wiki/Hans-Joachim_Stuck" title="Hans-Joachim Stuck">Hans-Joachim Stuck</a>. That was how it stayed to the end; Lauda winning from Scheckter and Stuck, whilst the rest of the points were rounded-out with Lauda's teammate <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Reutemann" title="Carlos Reutemann">Carlos Reutemann</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vittorio_Brambilla" title="Vittorio Brambilla">Vittorio Brambilla</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Surtees" title="Surtees">Surtees</a> and <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Tambay" title="Patrick Tambay">Patrick Tambay</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Ensign_Racing" title="Ensign Racing">Ensign</a>.
