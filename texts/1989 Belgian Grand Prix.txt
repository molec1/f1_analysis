<p>The <b>1989 Belgian Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Spa-Francorchamps" title="Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps">Spa-Francorchamps</a> on 27 August 1989. It was the eleventh race of the <a href="/wiki/1989_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1989 Formula One World Championship">1989 Formula One World Championship</a>.
</p><p>The 44-lap race was won from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> by Brazilian driver <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/5" title="McLaren MP4/5">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Honda_in_Formula_One" title="Honda in Formula One">Honda</a>. Senna finished just ahead of French teammate <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a>, with Englishman <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> third in a <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_640" title="Ferrari 640">Ferrari</a>. The win, Senna's fifth of the season, moved him to within 11 points of Prost in the Drivers' Championship.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1989_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-qualifying_report">Pre-qualifying report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1989_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Pre-qualifying report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the Friday morning pre-qualifying session, an <a href="/wiki/Onyx_(racing_team)" class="mw-redirect" title="Onyx (racing team)">Onyx</a> topped the time sheets for the fifth Grand Prix in succession. <a href="/wiki/Stefan_Johansson" title="Stefan Johansson">Stefan Johansson</a> was again comfortably fastest by over a second, and his team-mate <a href="/wiki/Bertrand_Gachot" title="Bertrand Gachot">Bertrand Gachot</a> also pre-qualified in third. The other two pre-qualifiers were the <a href="/wiki/Larrousse" title="Larrousse">Larrousse</a>-<a href="/wiki/Lola_Cars" title="Lola Cars">Lola</a> cars of <a href="/wiki/Michele_Alboreto" title="Michele Alboreto">Michele Alboreto</a> in second, and <a href="/wiki/Philippe_Alliot" title="Philippe Alliot">Philippe Alliot</a> in fourth.
</p><p>Those failing to proceed to the main qualifying sessions included both <a href="/wiki/Osella" title="Osella">Osellas</a>; <a href="/wiki/Nicola_Larini" title="Nicola Larini">Nicola Larini</a> and <a href="/wiki/Piercarlo_Ghinzani" title="Piercarlo Ghinzani">Piercarlo Ghinzani</a> close together in fifth and sixth. <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Moreno" title="Roberto Moreno">Roberto Moreno</a> was seventh in his <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Coloni" title="Scuderia Coloni">Coloni</a>, with the <a href="/wiki/Automobiles_Gonfaronnaises_Sportives" title="Automobiles Gonfaronnaises Sportives">AGS</a> of <a href="/wiki/Gabriele_Tarquini" title="Gabriele Tarquini">Gabriele Tarquini</a> eighth. The struggling <a href="/wiki/Zakspeed" title="Zakspeed">Zakspeed</a> drivers were ninth and tenth, <a href="/wiki/Bernd_Schneider_(racing_driver)" title="Bernd Schneider (racing driver)">Bernd Schneider</a> marginally faster than <a href="/wiki/Aguri_Suzuki" title="Aguri Suzuki">Aguri Suzuki</a>. The other AGS of <a href="/wiki/Yannick_Dalmas" title="Yannick Dalmas">Yannick Dalmas</a> was next, ahead of <a href="/wiki/Gregor_Foitek" title="Gregor Foitek">Gregor Foitek</a>, driving the older <a href="/wiki/EuroBrun_ER188" title="EuroBrun ER188">EuroBrun ER188B</a> after the new car had not been successful. Foitek quit the team after this Grand Prix, to be replaced by their 1988 driver <a href="/wiki/Oscar_Larrauri" title="Oscar Larrauri">Oscar Larrauri</a>. Bottom of the time sheets was the second Coloni of debutant <a href="/wiki/Enrico_Bertaggia" title="Enrico Bertaggia">Enrico Bertaggia</a>, who was unable to put a proper timed lap together. He was replacing <a href="/wiki/Pierre-Henri_Raphanel" title="Pierre-Henri Raphanel">Pierre-Henri Raphanel</a>, who had left Coloni to join <a href="/wiki/Rial_Racing" title="Rial Racing">Rial</a> after <a href="/wiki/Volker_Weidler" title="Volker Weidler">Volker Weidler</a> had quit the team.<sup id="cite_ref-Walker_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Walker-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying_report">Qualifying report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1989_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Qualifying report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Ayrton_Senna_1989_Belgian_GP.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/77/Ayrton_Senna_1989_Belgian_GP.jpg/220px-Ayrton_Senna_1989_Belgian_GP.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="150" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/77/Ayrton_Senna_1989_Belgian_GP.jpg/330px-Ayrton_Senna_1989_Belgian_GP.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/77/Ayrton_Senna_1989_Belgian_GP.jpg/440px-Ayrton_Senna_1989_Belgian_GP.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1800" data-file-height="1225" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Ayrton_Senna_1989_Belgian_GP.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Ayrton Senna gained pole position ahead of his teammate.</div></div></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> took his eighth pole position of the season by nearly six-tenths of a second from <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> teammate <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a>, with almost a further second back to <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> in third. The two <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> were fourth and fifth with <a href="/wiki/Thierry_Boutsen" title="Thierry Boutsen">Thierry Boutsen</a>, in his home race, ahead of <a href="/wiki/Riccardo_Patrese" title="Riccardo Patrese">Riccardo Patrese</a>, followed by <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> in the second Ferrari. <a href="/wiki/Alessandro_Nannini" title="Alessandro Nannini">Alessandro Nannini</a> was seventh in the <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a>, over two seconds behind Mansell, and the top ten was completed by <a href="/wiki/Stefano_Modena" title="Stefano Modena">Stefano Modena</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Brabham" title="Brabham">Brabham</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maur%C3%ADcio_Gugelmin" title="Maurício Gugelmin">Maurício Gugelmin</a> in the <a href="/wiki/March_Engineering" title="March Engineering">March</a> and <a href="/wiki/Derek_Warwick" title="Derek Warwick">Derek Warwick</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Arrows_Grand_Prix_International" title="Arrows Grand Prix International">Arrows</a>.
</p><p>The major talking point, however, was the failure of both <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a> and <a href="/wiki/Satoru_Nakajima" title="Satoru Nakajima">Satoru Nakajima</a> to qualify, the first time in the <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Lotus</a> team's history that neither of its cars had qualified.
</p><p>All the times were determined in the second qualifying session, after the first had taken place in wet conditions.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1989_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_report">Race report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1989_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Race report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:1989_Belgian_GP_race_start_02.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ee/1989_Belgian_GP_race_start_02.jpg/220px-1989_Belgian_GP_race_start_02.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="159" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ee/1989_Belgian_GP_race_start_02.jpg/330px-1989_Belgian_GP_race_start_02.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ee/1989_Belgian_GP_race_start_02.jpg/440px-1989_Belgian_GP_race_start_02.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1800" data-file-height="1301" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:1989_Belgian_GP_race_start_02.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> ahead of <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> at the race start. Behind, <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thierry_Boutsen" title="Thierry Boutsen">Thierry Boutsen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> running wide.</div></div></div>
<p>The race was delayed for some time due to the wet conditions. At the start, Senna led the way from Prost and Berger. Mansell managed to pass both Williams on the grass on the run to La Source hairpin to claim fourth position by the first corner. <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Herbert" title="Johnny Herbert">Johnny Herbert</a> retired when he spun off on lap 4 in his first race for <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_Racing" title="Tyrrell Racing">Tyrrell</a>. <a href="/wiki/Ren%C3%A9_Arnoux" title="René Arnoux">René Arnoux</a> then retired in the pit lane on lap 5 after a collision with the Lola of <a href="/wiki/Philippe_Alliot" title="Philippe Alliot">Philippe Alliot</a> at La Source. Early on, Berger pressured Prost but was unable to find a way through. Senna comfortably led from start to finish in conditions similar to those in which he won his first Belgian Grand Prix in 1985. Behind him, Berger suffered his tenth consecutive retirement of the season when he spun out on lap 10, leaving Prost to fend off a charging Mansell who, in his efforts to pass the McLaren, made a number of unorthodox moves at the exit of La Source in an attempt to gain a better run at the McLaren through Eau Rouge and on to the long uphill straight. Senna eased up in the last few laps, allowing Prost and Mansell to finish within two seconds of him. Boutsen finished fourth in his 100th race. <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Herbert" title="Johnny Herbert">Johnny Herbert</a>, in his first race for <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_Racing" title="Tyrrell Racing">Tyrrell</a> (replacing <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a> who was busy competing in the F3000 championship), said during the BBC commentary that in order to see the car in front it was necessary to press the helmet visor against the lens of the rear-facing visibility light from the car in front. Unusually, <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Cheever" title="Eddie Cheever">Eddie Cheever</a> received the black and white flag for 'unsportsmanlike behaviour' for his alleged baulking of Mansell.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
