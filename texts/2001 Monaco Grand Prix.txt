<p>The <b>2001 Monaco Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>LIX Grand Prix de Monaco</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Monaco" title="Circuit de Monaco">Circuit de Monaco</a> in <a href="/wiki/La_Condamine" title="La Condamine">La Condamine</a> and <a href="/wiki/Monte_Carlo" title="Monte Carlo">Monte Carlo</a> on 27 May. It was the seventh race of the <a href="/wiki/2001_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2001 Formula One World Championship">2001 Formula One World Championship</a> and the 59th running of the <a href="/wiki/Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="Monaco Grand Prix">event</a>.  <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> won the 78-lap race for the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> team. His teammate <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> finished second with <a href="/wiki/Jaguar_Racing" title="Jaguar Racing">Jaguar</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a> third.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a>, who qualified in <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> by setting the fastest lap in qualifying, <a href="/wiki/Stall_(engine)" title="Stall (engine)">stalled</a> at the start of the <a href="/wiki/Parade_lap" title="Parade lap">formation lap</a> because of an electronic <a href="/wiki/Launch_control_(automotive)" title="Launch control (automotive)">launch control system</a> fault, leading to Schumacher inheriting his position. Schumacher maintained the lead in the first laps with <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> second and Barrichello third. Häkkinen responded to Schumacher's pace before he lost second to Barrichello on lap 13 because his car pulled to the right. Schumacher continued to lead until he made a <a href="/wiki/Pit_stop" title="Pit stop">pit stop</a> on the 55th lap, relinquishing it to Barrichello for four laps. He then retook it and held it to claim his fourth victory of the season, his fifth at the Monaco Grand Prix, and the 48th of his career.
</p><p>As a consequence of the race, Michael Schumacher extended his lead in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a> from four to twelve <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Championship_points_scoring_systems" title="List of Formula One World Championship points scoring systems">points</a> over Coulthard. Barrichello maintained third and <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> remained in fourth after he retired late in the event. In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a>, Ferrari extended their lead over <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> to 32 points. <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a> kept third and fourth and <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a> maintained fifth with ten races remaining in the season.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2001_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The 2001 <a href="/wiki/Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="Monaco Grand Prix">Monaco Grand Prix</a> was the 7th of the 17 races in the <a href="/wiki/2001_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2001 Formula One World Championship">2001 Formula One World Championship</a> and the 59th edition of the event. It was held at the 19-turn 3.370&#160;km (2.094&#160;mi) <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Monaco" title="Circuit de Monaco">Circuit de Monaco</a> between <a href="/wiki/La_Condamine" title="La Condamine">La Condamine</a> and <a href="/wiki/Monte_Carlo" title="Monte Carlo">Monte Carlo</a> on 27 May.<sup id="cite_ref-CF1Result_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CF1Result-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Before the race, <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> led the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a> with 42 <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Championship_points_scoring_systems" title="List of Formula One World Championship points scoring systems">points</a>, ahead of <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>'s <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> in second with 38 and the second Ferrari of <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> in third with 18. <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> of <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> was fourth with 12 points, and <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> was fifth with 8 points.<sup id="cite_ref-points_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a>, Ferrari led with 60 points, McLaren and Williams were second and third with 42 and 18 points, as Jordan and Sauber contended for fourth place.<sup id="cite_ref-points_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Michael Schumacher had won three of the six preceding races of and took <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> in five of them. He was optimistic about his prospects of equalling <a href="/wiki/Graham_Hill" title="Graham Hill">Graham Hill</a>'s record of five Monaco Grand Prix victories, "Traditionally, I have always gone well in Monte Carlo. On top of that, we have a great car this year and so I think we will be very competitive in our fight with McLaren for pole position and the win."<sup id="cite_ref-AF1SchumiPreview_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1SchumiPreview-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard, the <a href="/wiki/2000_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="2000 Monaco Grand Prix">2000 winner of the race</a>, said he wanted to repeat his success from the year before and saw an opportunity to take the lead of the Drivers' Championship.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1SchumiPreview_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1SchumiPreview-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Jaguar_Racing" title="Jaguar Racing">Jaguar</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a> said he was optimistic for Monaco because of his finishing record at the track, "I have a good track record at this place, with a second, a third and last year fourth place to my name. I am reasonably optimistic about our chances this weekend."<sup id="cite_ref-SpeedWedNotes_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SpeedWedNotes-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Following the <a href="/wiki/2001_Austrian_Grand_Prix" title="2001 Austrian Grand Prix">Austrian Grand Prix</a> two weeks prior, where four cars <a href="/wiki/Stall_(engine)" title="Stall (engine)">stalled</a> at the start because of <a href="/wiki/Launch_control_(automotive)" title="Launch control (automotive)">electronic launch control systems</a> failures, concerns were voiced by the technical director of Williams <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Head" title="Patrick Head">Patrick Head</a> and Jordan's <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> over driver safety because of the possibility of stranded vehicles and a major accident.<sup id="cite_ref-LESLaunchControl_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LESLaunchControl-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Others differed with Coulthard saying that launch control could help make faster getaways,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> and Michael Schumacher predicted that there would be no repeat of cars being unable to start, noting that McLaren driver <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> stalled at the <a href="/wiki/2001_Brazilian_Grand_Prix" title="2001 Brazilian Grand Prix">Brazilian Grand Prix</a> without launch control.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Max_Mosley" title="Max Mosley">Max Mosley</a>, the president of the series' governing body, the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile</a> (FIA), advised teams to switch off their launch control systems if they expressed concerns over its reliability; he ruled out banning the aid for the Monaco race.<sup id="cite_ref-LESLaunchControl_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LESLaunchControl-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Jordan subsequently deactivated launch control on their <a href="/wiki/Jordan_EJ11" title="Jordan EJ11">EJ11s</a> and opted for a manual system due to the Circuit de Monaco's narrow characteristic.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Although the FIA warned of "severe penalties" for teams who did not correctly set-up the systems, it allowed drivers to rehearse their starts at the conclusion of Friday's practice sessions.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Due to the configuration of the Circuit de Monaco, with its low average speed and abundance of low-speed corners, allied to the low-<a href="/wiki/Grip_(auto_racing)" title="Grip (auto racing)">grip</a> nature of the public road surface, the teams all set their <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_car" title="Formula One car">cars</a> up to produce the maximum amount of <a href="/wiki/Downforce" title="Downforce">downforce</a> and mechanical grip possible.<sup id="cite_ref-FIAFP12_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAFP12-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Prost_Grand_Prix" title="Prost Grand Prix">Prost</a> introduced new front and rear wings, undertray and rear crash structure to <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a>'s car. <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a> fitted a revised aerodynamic package with new front and rear wings and <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_motorsport_terms#S" title="Glossary of motorsport terms">sidepods</a> to its <a href="/wiki/Benetton_B201" title="Benetton B201">B201s</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FIAFP12_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAFP12-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Jaguar ran with a revised rear crash structure to match its new <a href="/wiki/Diffuser_(automotive)" title="Diffuser (automotive)">diffuser</a> and new rear wing components and undertray from Saturday's practice sessions.<sup id="cite_ref-SpeedWedNotes_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SpeedWedNotes-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FIAFP12_11-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAFP12-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Arrows debuted an elevated nose wing positioned vertically on two uprights over the top of <a href="/wiki/Jos_Verstappen" title="Jos Verstappen">Jos Verstappen</a>'s front wing aimed at increasing the maximum amount of downforce available. Jordan introduced a similar modification on Trulli's car; their design was positioned in front of the driver. The devices were examined after Thursday's practice sessions by the FIA technical delegate <a href="/wiki/Jo_Bauer" title="Jo Bauer">Jo Bauer</a>, who believed they were an infringement of Formula One regulations.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-unusualfrontwings_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-unusualfrontwings-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Jordan and <a href="/wiki/Arrows_Grand_Prix_International" title="Arrows Grand Prix International">Arrows</a> were required to remove the devices for safety reasons, and the stewards warned the two teams that they risked exclusion from the Grand Prix if they were used again.<sup id="cite_ref-unusualfrontwings_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-unusualfrontwings-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice">Practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2001_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Four practice sessions were held before the Sunday race—two on Thursday, and two on Saturday. The Thursday morning and afternoon sessions each lasted an hour. The third and final practice sessions on Saturday morning ran for 45 minutes.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Both of Thursday's sessions took place in dry and warm weather conditions.<sup id="cite_ref-FIAFP12_11-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAFP12-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> In the first practice session, Michael Schumacher set the fastest lap of 1 minute and 21.577 seconds, 0.827 seconds faster than Coulthard in second. The two Jordan cars of Trulli and <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a>, Barrichello, Häkkinen, <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Panis" title="Olivier Panis">Olivier Panis</a> of <a href="/wiki/British_American_Racing" title="British American Racing">British American Racing</a> (BAR), Ralf Schumacher, Irvine and Alesi rounded out the session's top ten drivers.<sup id="cite_ref-AtlasFP1_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AtlasFP1-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> On a dirty track, <a href="/wiki/Pedro_de_la_Rosa" title="Pedro de la Rosa">Pedro de la Rosa</a> damaged the front left corner of his Jaguar by hitting a barrier at Portier turn, and <a href="/wiki/Tarso_Marques" title="Tarso Marques">Tarso Marques</a> spun his <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a> car 180 degrees backwards into a wall at the entry to La Rascasse corner.<sup id="cite_ref-AtlasFP1_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AtlasFP1-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-AutosportFP1_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportFP1-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Since <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_motorsport_terms#S" title="Glossary of motorsport terms">spare cars</a> could not be driven until qualifying on Saturday, neither driver returned to the track for the rest of the session.<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportFP1_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportFP1-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Mika_Haekkinen_2006.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/53/Mika_Haekkinen_2006.jpg/170px-Mika_Haekkinen_2006.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="230" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/53/Mika_Haekkinen_2006.jpg/255px-Mika_Haekkinen_2006.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/53/Mika_Haekkinen_2006.jpg/340px-Mika_Haekkinen_2006.jpg 2x" data-file-width="549" data-file-height="743" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Mika_Haekkinen_2006.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> <i>(pictured in 2006)</i> set the fastest lap time in practice in the third session on Saturday morning.</div></div></div>
<p>Häkkinen led the second session with the day's fastest lap at 1 minute and 19.853 seconds, with Michael Schumacher, Ralf Schumacher, Barrichello, Trulli, Coulthard, Frentzen, Alesi, BAR's <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a> and Williams' <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a> in positions two through ten.<sup id="cite_ref-SpeedvisionFriday_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SpeedvisionFriday-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard struck a barrier at Tabac corner and broke his McLaren's right-front <a href="/wiki/Tie_rod" title="Tie rod">track rod</a>, which was replaced in the <a href="/wiki/Pit_stop" title="Pit stop">pit lane</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FIAFP12_11-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAFP12-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> spun his Minardi into a wall at La Rascasse turn and damaged its rear suspension, ending his session early. With 15 minutes to go, Montoya damaged his car's rear at La Rascasse turn, and the session was stopped for four minutes as <a href="/wiki/Motorsport_marshal" title="Motorsport marshal">track marshals</a> extricated him.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> His teammate Ralf Schumacher crashed at the Swimming Pool complex with the front left of his car. Two track marshals retrieving Schumacher's front wing narrowly avoided being hit by <a href="/wiki/Enrique_Bernoldi" title="Enrique Bernoldi">Enrique Bernoldi</a>'s Arrows car. Irvine spun at the Novelle Chicane and Michael Schumacher swerved to avoid contact with his car.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After taking Friday off—a feature of the event timetable unique to Monaco—<sup id="cite_ref-SpeedvisionFriday_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SpeedvisionFriday-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;n 1&#93;</a></sup> the drivers returned to action on Saturday in clear weather. Ralf Schumacher complained of head and neck pain following his crash in the second session and was treated by his team's physiotherapist before being ordered to rest until Saturday.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Nevertheless, he was quickest in the third practice session with a lap of 1 minute and 21.036 seconds, ahead of Villenueve, Coulthard, Michael Schumacher, Sauber's <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a>, Irvine, Alesi, Frentzen, Panis and Trulli.<sup id="cite_ref-AtlasFP3_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AtlasFP3-23">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> 13 minutes into the session, Verstappen's engine failed, and laid oil at La Rascasse corner,<sup id="cite_ref-AtlasFP3_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AtlasFP3-23">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> prompting a quarter of an hour stoppage for marshals to clear it with sand.<sup id="cite_ref-F1NetFP3_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1NetFP3-24">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Luciano_Burti" title="Luciano Burti">Luciano Burti</a>'s Prost car suffered a mechanical failure that sent him veering into the Sainte Devote tyre wall and narrowly avoided hitting multiple stationary <a href="/wiki/Vehicle_recovery" title="Vehicle recovery">recovery vehicles</a> with three minutes remaining. Burti was unhurt.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The delay to dry the oil at La Rascasse turn delayed the start of the fourth session by 10 minutes.<sup id="cite_ref-F1NetFP3_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1NetFP3-24">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Once underway, Häkkinen was fastest with a 1 minute and 18.282 seconds lap, followed by Michael Schumacher, Ralf Schumacher, Coulthard, Irvine, Trulli, Montoya, Barrichello, Alesi and Frentzen. Alonso spun 180 degrees at the Loews <a href="/wiki/Hairpin_turn" title="Hairpin turn">hairpin</a> and blocked the track. Frentzen stopped into Casino Square corner and track marshals extricated his car. Räikkönen and Bernoldi had separate crashes at the Swimming Pool complex and La Rascasse corner; Bernoldi's front wing got lodged in Häkkinen's front suspension. <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> spun at La Racasse and blocked the circuit as track marshals extricated his Benetton car.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2001_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:David_Coulthard_2007.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d8/David_Coulthard_2007.jpg/170px-David_Coulthard_2007.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="250" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d8/David_Coulthard_2007.jpg/255px-David_Coulthard_2007.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d8/David_Coulthard_2007.jpg/340px-David_Coulthard_2007.jpg 2x" data-file-width="500" data-file-height="734" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:David_Coulthard_2007.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> <i>(pictured in 2007)</i> took the 12th pole position of his career and his second of the season.</div></div></div>
<p>Saturday's afternoon one hour qualifying session saw each driver was limited to twelve laps, with the <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_motorsport_terms#G" title="Glossary of motorsport terms">grid</a> order decided by their fastest laps. During this session, the <a href="/wiki/107%25_rule" title="107% rule">107% rule</a> was in effect, which necessitated each driver to set a time within 107 per cent of the quickest lap to qualify for the race.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Conditions were warm and sunny for qualifying.<sup id="cite_ref-monacogp_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-monacogp-27">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard broke the unofficial track lap record set by Frentzen in <a href="/wiki/1997_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="1997 Monaco Grand Prix">1997</a>, and took his second <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> of the season, and the 12th of his career with a time of 1 minute and 17.430 seconds.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> He was joined on the grid's front row by Michael Schumacher who was 0.201 seconds slower after aborting his first timed lap because Bernoldi caught him off guard exiting the tunnel and he lost a tenth of a second by glancing the left-hand side at Le Portier turn.<sup id="cite_ref-SVQuali_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SVQuali-29">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-AutosportQuali_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportQuali-30">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Häkkinen changed his <a href="/wiki/Racing_setup" title="Racing setup">car's set-up</a> for a final timed run but it gave him excess <a href="/wiki/Understeer_and_oversteer" title="Understeer and oversteer">understeer</a> and a lack of front grip, leaving him third.<sup id="cite_ref-SVQuali_29-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SVQuali-29">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Fourth-placed Barrichello worked with his engineers following the final practice session to improve his car's performance; he <a href="/wiki/Flat_spot" title="Flat spot">flat-spotted</a> his front-right tyre at Sainte Devote turn. Ralf Schumacher was the best of the <a href="/wiki/Michelin" title="Michelin">Michelin</a> tyre runners in fifth after <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> of the Benetton team impeded his final timed lap.<sup id="cite_ref-SVQuali_29-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SVQuali-29">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-SatQuotes_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SatQuotes-31">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Sixth-placed Irvine was delayed on his third timed lap and braked early for the Novelle Chicane.<sup id="cite_ref-SatQuotes_31-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SatQuotes-31">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Montoya was seventh in his first appearance at Monaco since the <a href="/wiki/1998_International_Formula_3000_Championship" title="1998 International Formula 3000 Championship">1998 International Formula 3000 Championship</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportQuali_30-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportQuali-30">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Trulli secured eighth from losing about two-tenths of a second due to a slower car baulking him. Villeneuve qualified ninth and Fisichella in tenth added more front wing angle to create a balanced car.<sup id="cite_ref-SatQuotes_31-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SatQuotes-31">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Alesi was the fastest driver not to qualify in the top ten;<sup id="cite_ref-SVQuali_29-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SVQuali-29">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> he improved on each of his timed laps and made contact with a barrier in the tunnel. Panis in 12th went too quickly at the Piscine chicane on his last lap.<sup id="cite_ref-SatQuotes_31-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SatQuotes-31">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Frentzen in 13th spun at Portier corner before the entrance of the tunnel, and he abandoned his car because the constricting barriers left him with little room to recover.<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportQuali_30-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportQuali-30">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> De La Rosa was 14th, followed by Räikkönen in 15th, who encountered an abundance of traffic and a waved <a href="/wiki/Racing_flags#Yellow_flag" title="Racing flags">yellow flag</a> on his final timed lap. His Sauber teammate Heidfeld had an unrectifiable electronic throttle problem on his second timed lap and he took the team's spare car to qualify 16th. Balance and braking problems and traffic left Button in 17th. Alonso began ahead of both the Arrows cars and Burti's Prost in 18th. Marques, 22nd, had excess oversteer.<sup id="cite_ref-SatQuotes_31-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SatQuotes-31">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Warm-up">Warm-up</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2001_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Warm-up">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A 30-minute warm-up session was held on Sunday morning in dry weather.<sup id="cite_ref-monacogp_27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-monacogp-27">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard led the session with a 1 minute and 20.944 seconds lap, ahead of his teammate Häkkinen in second. The Ferrari cars of Michael Schumacher and Barrichello, Frentzen, Ralf Schumacher, Irvine, Verstappen, de la Rosa and Fisichella followed in the top ten. While the session passed relatively peacefully, Marques crashed into an Armco metal barrier at Anthony Noghes corner.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2001_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The race took place in the afternoon from 14:00 local time. The weather was dry and sunny, with the air temperature 23&#160;°C (73&#160;°F) and a track temperature between 36–39&#160;°C (97–102&#160;°F).<sup id="cite_ref-monacogp_27-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-monacogp-27">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Several teams began on the soft compound tyres; a one-stop strategy was determined to be ideal, with some drivers making their first pit stops early to advance their position and others stopping later because of a similar performance level.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> At the start of the <a href="/wiki/Parade_lap" title="Parade lap">formation lap</a>, an electronic launch control software glitch caused Coulthard's engine to stall,<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> prompting the McLaren mechanics to restart his engine; he was required to begin from the back of the grid. His teammate Häkkinen was stuck behind him; he drove away before the final car passed him and was allowed to start from third place.<sup id="cite_ref-FIARace_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIARace-36">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher made a clean start to lead the field into Sainte Devote urn, ahead of Häkkinen and Barrichello.<sup id="cite_ref-FIARace_36-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIARace-36">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> Montoya passed Irvine for fifth when the latter ran wide attempting to pass Ralf Schumacher on the left at Sainte Devote corner.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-37">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Behind the two, Verstappen lost control of his car and made contact with Burti. Verstappen fell to 20th as Burti had one of his endplates removed.<sup id="cite_ref-BBCLaps_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCLaps-38">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Barrichello_2002.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/36/Barrichello_2002.jpg/170px-Barrichello_2002.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="298" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/36/Barrichello_2002.jpg/255px-Barrichello_2002.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/36/Barrichello_2002.jpg/340px-Barrichello_2002.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1128" data-file-height="1975" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Barrichello_2002.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> <i>(pictured in 2002)</i> finished in second place</div></div></div>
<p>On the run to the exit of Portier corner,<sup id="cite_ref-F1COMRace_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1COMRace-39">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> Heidfeld and Bernoldi connected,<sup id="cite_ref-SVRace_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SVRace-40">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> causing Heidfeld to be launched over one of Bernoldi's wheels, and sending him straight into a barrier, making him the race's first retirement on lap one.<sup id="cite_ref-GPRace_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPRace-41">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> At the first lap's conclusion, Michael Schumacher led Häkkinen by 1.5 seconds;<sup id="cite_ref-BBCLaps_38-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCLaps-38">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> the duo were followed by Barrichello, Ralf Schumacher, Montoya and Irvine.<sup id="cite_ref-F1COMRace_39-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1COMRace-39">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> Montoya recorded the race's <a href="/wiki/Fastest_lap" title="Fastest lap">fastest lap</a> at that point as he attempted to overtake his teammate Ralf Schumacher. On lap two, Burti fell to 20th when Verstappen and Coulthard passed him. Up front, Michael Schumacher extended his gap over Häkkinen to 1.6 seconds at the start of lap three.<sup id="cite_ref-FIARace_36-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIARace-36">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> That lap, Montoya shifted down a gear, slowed and collected understeer.<sup id="cite_ref-SVRace_40-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SVRace-40">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> This put him wide at the entrance of the Swimming Pool complex and hit a barrier. This moved Irvine and Trulli to fifth and sixth respectively. Further back, Marques was passed by Verstappen for 17th and Burti entered the pit lane to replace his damaged front wing. Marques lost a further position through a pass by Coulthard for 18th place on lap four.<sup id="cite_ref-FIARace_36-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIARace-36">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-F1COMRace_39-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1COMRace-39">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Michael Schumacher increased his lead over Häkkinen by another six-tenths of a second on the fifth lap with the latter under pressure from Barrichello and Ralf Schumacher. Häkkinen then went slightly faster to lower the gap to 2.5 seconds and set a new fastest lap under 1 minute and 23 seconds to be 1.6 seconds adrift on the eighth lap, to which Michael Schumacher responded with his own fastest lap.<sup id="cite_ref-BBCLaps_38-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCLaps-38">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> That lap, Verstappen overtook his teammate Bernoldi for 16th and the latter now had Coulthard behind him.<sup id="cite_ref-FIARace_36-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIARace-36">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-LapChart_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LapChart-42">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> On lap 10,<sup id="cite_ref-FIARace_36-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIARace-36">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello began to drop back with a <a href="/wiki/Cramp" title="Cramp">cramp</a> in his right foot, and was advised over the radio by Ferrari's technical director <a href="/wiki/Ross_Brawn" title="Ross Brawn">Ross Brawn</a> to consume more water and move his toes, causing Barrichello to brake earlier than expected.<sup id="cite_ref-SVRace_40-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SVRace-40">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-GPRace_41-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPRace-41">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> Two laps later, Coulthard attempted a unsuccessful pass on Bernoldi at Tabac corner.<sup id="cite_ref-F1COMRace_39-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1COMRace-39">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> Häkkinen's car began to pull to the right on lap 13,<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportRace_43-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportRace-43">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> and Barrichello overtook him at the exit of the tunnel for second.<sup id="cite_ref-BBCLaps_38-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCLaps-38">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> He entered the pit lane for a 50-second pit stop on the 14th lap to have his car's suspension <a href="/wiki/Overhead_valve_engine" title="Overhead valve engine">push rods</a> checked by his mechanics. No damage was found.<sup id="cite_ref-F1COMRace_39-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1COMRace-39">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-LapChart_42-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LapChart-42">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:0_44-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-44">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> On the 14th lap, Panis retired with a <a href="/wiki/Steering" title="Steering">steering</a> problem, and Häkkinen did the same two laps later because his problem persisted.<sup id="cite_ref-BBCLaps_38-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCLaps-38">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-SVRace_40-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SVRace-40">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the meantime, Verstappen got ahead of Button for 12th on lap 15. Button retook the position temporarily soon after as Verstappen re-passed him. Coulthard continued to duel Bernoldi for position but still could not effect a pass.<sup id="cite_ref-F1COMRace_39-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1COMRace-39">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-LapChart_42-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LapChart-42">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> On the 18th lap, tenth-placed de la Rosa had an hydraulics issue that deprived him control of his <a href="/wiki/Transmission_(mechanics)" title="Transmission (mechanics)">gearbox</a> and throttle.<sup id="cite_ref-SVRace_40-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SVRace-40">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-GPRace_41-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPRace-41">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> He pulled off at the side of the track to retire on the next lap.<sup id="cite_ref-FIARace_36-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIARace-36">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> On lap 26,<sup id="cite_ref-F1COMRace_39-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1COMRace-39">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> Burti, who had a long brake pedal, missed the Sainte Devote corner braking point. He stopped away from the barrier and he retired when his reverse gear failed.<sup id="cite_ref-SVRace_40-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SVRace-40">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard tried again to overtake Bernoldi on the next lap but the latter defended his position. That allowed Michael Schumacher to lap Coulthard, who could not use the situation to pass Bernoldi.<sup id="cite_ref-F1COMRace_39-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1COMRace-39">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> On lap 31, Trulli retired from fifth at La Rascasse turn with flames coming from the rear of his car due to a loss of engine hydraulic pressure.<sup id="cite_ref-BBCLaps_38-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCLaps-38">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-SVRace_40-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SVRace-40">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> That elevated Fisichella to sixth.<sup id="cite_ref-FIARace_36-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIARace-36">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> As Coulthard allowed Barrichello to lap him on lap 32, he sought to use the opportunity to pass Bernoldi but he could not do so.<sup id="cite_ref-BBCLaps_38-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCLaps-38">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Two laps later, Fisichella entered Sainte Devote corner too fast, and struck a barrier with his rear wheel. He nonetheless continued in sixth, ahead of Alesi,<sup id="cite_ref-FIARace_36-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIARace-36">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-BBCLaps_38-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCLaps-38">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> because his car's suspension was undamaged.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_44-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-44">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Michael_Schumacher-I%27m_the_man_(cropped).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/Michael_Schumacher-I%27m_the_man_%28cropped%29.jpg/170px-Michael_Schumacher-I%27m_the_man_%28cropped%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="230" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/Michael_Schumacher-I%27m_the_man_%28cropped%29.jpg/255px-Michael_Schumacher-I%27m_the_man_%28cropped%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/Michael_Schumacher-I%27m_the_man_%28cropped%29.jpg/340px-Michael_Schumacher-I%27m_the_man_%28cropped%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1260" data-file-height="1704" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Michael_Schumacher-I%27m_the_man_(cropped).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> <i>(pictured in 2005)</i> won his fourth race of the season, his fifth at the Monaco Grand Prix and the 48th of his career.</div></div></div>
<p>Michael Schumacher set a new official lap record of 1 minute and 20.770 seconds on the 38th lap. He further improved it to a time of 1 minute and 20.422 seconds four laps later, extending his lead over his teammate Barrichello to 18.3 seconds.<sup id="cite_ref-LapChart_42-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LapChart-42">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> On lap 43,<sup id="cite_ref-BBCLaps_38-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCLaps-38">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Fisichella had an hydraulic failure that caused his gearbox to fail,<sup id="cite_ref-SVRace_40-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SVRace-40">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-GPRace_41-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPRace-41">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> and he crashed into the tyre wall at Sainte Devote, removing the front left wheel from his car.<sup id="cite_ref-GPRace_41-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPRace-41">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-AutosportRace_43-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportRace-43">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> Bernoldi made a pit stop at the end of the following lap, allowing Coulthard to lap 4.5 seconds faster than before.<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportRace_43-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportRace-43">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> He took advantage of Alonso being lapped by Villeneuve to pass the latter for ninth on lap 48.<sup id="cite_ref-FIARace_36-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIARace-36">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-F1COMRace_39-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1COMRace-39">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> Two laps later, Frentzen in seventh understeered at the apex and he drifted into a left-hand Armco metal barrier exiting the tunnel at 270&#160;km/h (170&#160;mph). He slid along the barrier before stopping at the Novelle Chicane and clambered out of his car unhurt.<sup id="cite_ref-GPRace_41-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPRace-41">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-AutosportRace_43-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportRace-43">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> Alesi entered the pit lane on lap 51, and returned to the race still in sixth. Four laps later, Michael Schumacher made his pit stop from the lead, promoting his teammate Barrichello into first.<sup id="cite_ref-FIARace_36-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIARace-36">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> Further down the order, Alonso retired with a failed gearbox on lap 57.<sup id="cite_ref-GPRace_41-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPRace-41">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-LapChart_42-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LapChart-42">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> Ralf Schumacher retired from third position in the pit lane on the next lap with an electrical fault that switched off his engine.<sup id="cite_ref-FIARace_36-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIARace-36">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-AutosportRace_43-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportRace-43">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Irvine was promoted to third, Villeneuve fourth, Alesi fifth and Coulthard sixth. Barrichello made his pit stop from first position on lap 60, returning the lead to his teammate Michael Schumacher. That lap, Irvine made his pit stop and retained third place.<sup id="cite_ref-FIARace_36-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIARace-36">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> Then, Marques' <a href="/wiki/Drive_shaft" title="Drive shaft">driveshaft</a> broke and he drove onto the <a href="/wiki/Run-off_area" title="Run-off area">run-off area</a> at the Novelle Chicane to retire.<sup id="cite_ref-BBCLaps_38-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCLaps-38">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-GPRace_41-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPRace-41">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> Six laps later, Coulthard made his pit stop and emerged in front of Button in sixth.<sup id="cite_ref-F1COMRace_39-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1COMRace-39">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> He then set the race's overall fastest lap at 1 minute and 19.424 seconds on the 68th lap as he drew closer to Alesi in fifth.<sup id="cite_ref-GPRace_41-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPRace-41">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> Prost reacted by calling Alesi into the pit lane on the next lap for new tyres to better his defence of fifth. However, Alesi was delayed and he rejoined the race behind Coulthard in sixth.<sup id="cite_ref-FIARace_36-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIARace-36">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher led the last nine laps to take his fourth victory of the season, his fifth at Monaco and the 48th of his career,<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> by 0.431 seconds over Barrichello in a staged finish.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;n 2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:1_37-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-37">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-AutosportRace_43-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportRace-43">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> Irvine took Jaguar's first podium in third,<sup id="cite_ref-JaguarRace_48-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JaguarRace-48">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> ahead of the faster Villeneuve in fourth.<sup id="cite_ref-FIARace_36-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIARace-36">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard finished a lap behind in fifth and Alesi took sixth. Button, Verstappen and Bernoldi followed in positions seven to nine.<sup id="cite_ref-CF1Result_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CF1Result-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Räikkönen was five laps down and the last finisher in tenth after an early race pit stop to rectify a wheel sensor failure and he had no <a href="/wiki/Traction_control_system" title="Traction control system">traction control</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-GPRace_41-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPRace-41">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> The attrition rate was high with 12 of the 22 drivers not finishing the race.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2001_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The top three drivers appeared in <a href="/wiki/Rainier_III,_Prince_of_Monaco" title="Rainier III, Prince of Monaco">Prince Rainier III of Monaco</a>'s royal box to collect their trophies and appeared in the subsequent <a href="/wiki/News_conference" class="mw-redirect" title="News conference">press conference</a> to speak to the media.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_14-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher said he felt little emotion on the podium because he took "a very straightforward win" and that finishing the race was the most important aspect because of the circuit's narrow characteristic, "Although it was an easy drive, it was still hard to some degree, because we were still doing reasonably fast lap times. I don't know what it so special."<sup id="cite_ref-PressConf_50-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PressConf-50">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello stated his cramp disappeared after his pit stop, "I was asking God to give me a chance to race because the car was brilliant. I never had a car as good as this one of today. But as Michael was saying, I had to save so much because I couldn't do anything else."<sup id="cite_ref-PressConf_50-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PressConf-50">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> Irvine spoke of his delight to finish third and stated his hope to continue Jaguar's form into the <a href="/wiki/2001_Canadian_Grand_Prix" title="2001 Canadian Grand Prix">Canadian Grand Prix</a>, "It's good for all the guys in the team. Everyone's been working very hard. We haven't been getting very far to be honest until these new aerodynamics arrived on Saturday so it's good for the guys in factory."<sup id="cite_ref-PressConf_50-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PressConf-50">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Enrique_Bernoldi_2007_Curitiba.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f7/Enrique_Bernoldi_2007_Curitiba.jpg/170px-Enrique_Bernoldi_2007_Curitiba.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="195" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f7/Enrique_Bernoldi_2007_Curitiba.jpg/255px-Enrique_Bernoldi_2007_Curitiba.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f7/Enrique_Bernoldi_2007_Curitiba.jpg/340px-Enrique_Bernoldi_2007_Curitiba.jpg 2x" data-file-width="800" data-file-height="919" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Enrique_Bernoldi_2007_Curitiba.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Enrique_Bernoldi" title="Enrique Bernoldi">Enrique Bernoldi</a> <i>(pictured in 2007)</i> claimed that McLaren threatened to shorten his career after delaying the faster Coulthard for 35 laps.</div></div></div>
<p>Bernoldi, who battled Coulthard for 35 laps, alleged that the McLaren team principal <a href="/wiki/Ron_Dennis" title="Ron Dennis">Ron Dennis</a> and the director of motorsport for Mercedes-Benz <a href="/wiki/Norbert_Haug" title="Norbert Haug">Norbert Haug</a> had approached him and threatened to shorten his career if a similar scenario occurred in the future, a claim that Dennis denied, "It was quite a while after the race when I talked to him and I was cool, calm, and collected and I was not angry. I just told him that in my opinion it was unsporting behaviour."<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> An audio tape released to the press by McLaren on 11 June vindicated Dennis; according to transcribers he said to Bernoldi, "that was not sporting and you shouldn't carry on like that."<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> The Arrows team principal <a href="/wiki/Tom_Walkinshaw" title="Tom Walkinshaw">Tom Walkinshaw</a> rejected a claim by Dennis that Bernoldi would not allow Coulthard to pass him for television exposure. Coulthard spoke of his belief that Bernoldi was over defensive and said he would raise the issue at the next <a href="/wiki/Grand_Prix_Drivers%27_Association" title="Grand Prix Drivers&#39; Association">Grand Prix Drivers' Association</a> meeting, "We agreed not to move once a driver has made his move. You make your decision – and up the hill a couple of times I was coming to get my wheels alongside him and he moved over."<sup id="cite_ref-BBCDCBER_53-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCDCBER-53">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> Bernoldi argued he was driving a routine race and was on the circuit to better his driving ability.<sup id="cite_ref-BBCDCBER_53-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCDCBER-53">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After the race, the FIA examined several <a href="/wiki/Bridgestone" title="Bridgestone">Bridgestone</a> tyres for possible illegal wear. It discovered nothing abnormal and the result of the race was declared final.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Jackie_Stewart" title="Jackie Stewart">Jackie Stewart</a>, the three-time Formula One World Champion and Jaguar team founder, praised Irvine's performance and the driver's maturity, "He practised well, he qualified well and he raced well. You couldn't ask for more. He proved himself here by driving in a very controlled way so he was ready to take advantage of opportunities and to resist any pressure from Jacques (Villeneuve) at the end."<sup id="cite_ref-JaguarRace_48-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JaguarRace-48">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> Alesi earned his first points finish since coming sixth in the <a href="/wiki/1999_Japanese_Grand_Prix" title="1999 Japanese Grand Prix">1999 Japanese Grand Prix</a> and the Prost team's first since Trulli's second-place result at the <a href="/wiki/1999_European_Grand_Prix" title="1999 European Grand Prix">1999 European Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ProstAlesi_55-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ProstAlesi-55">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> He declared the result "a great thing" for himself and Prost and said that he predicted similar results in the future, "I totally enjoyed my race. But towards the end, when I felt the car vibrating in the tunnel, I was really worried. It was a <a href="/wiki/Flat_tire" title="Flat tire">flat tyre</a> and I had to go back to the pits to change it, which cost me the fifth place points. What is really important today is that I scored the first point for the team this year – or last."<sup id="cite_ref-ProstAlesi_55-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ProstAlesi-55">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The result increased Michael Schumacher's lead in the Drivers' Championship to 12 points over Coulthard. Barrichello consolidated third position as Ralf Schumacher's non-finish kept him in fourth. Heidfeld was in fifth place.<sup id="cite_ref-points_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> In the Constructors' Championship, Ferrari increased their advantage over McLaren to 32 points. Williams and Jordan continued to occupy third and fourth positions and Sauber were fifth with ten races remaining in the season.<sup id="cite_ref-points_3-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
