<p>The <b>1966 Dutch Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at <a href="/wiki/Circuit_Zandvoort" title="Circuit Zandvoort">Zandvoort</a> on July 24, 1966. It was race 5 of 9 in both the <a href="/wiki/1966_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1966 World Championship of Drivers">1966 World Championship of Drivers</a> and the <a href="/wiki/1966_International_Cup_for_Formula_One_Manufacturers" class="mw-redirect" title="1966 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers">1966 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers</a>. The race was the 16th <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Grand_Prix" title="Dutch Grand Prix">Dutch Grand Prix</a> since it was first held in 1948. It was held over 90 laps of the four kilometre circuit for a race distance of 382 kilometres.
</p><p>The race was the third in succession to be won by Australian driver, 1959 and 1960 world champion, <a href="/wiki/Jack_Brabham" title="Jack Brabham">Jack Brabham</a> in his <a href="/wiki/Brabham_BT19" title="Brabham BT19">Brabham BT19</a>. Brabham lapped the field on his way to his second Dutch Grand Prix victory to add to his win in <a href="/wiki/1960_Dutch_Grand_Prix" title="1960 Dutch Grand Prix">1960</a>. British driver, 1962 world champion <a href="/wiki/Graham_Hill" title="Graham Hill">Graham Hill</a> finished second in his <a href="/wiki/BRM_P261" title="BRM P261">BRM P261</a>, himself a lap ahead of the rest of the field. Reigning world champion <a href="/wiki/Jim_Clark" title="Jim Clark">Jim Clark</a> took his first podium finish of the year in his <a href="/wiki/Lotus_33" title="Lotus 33">Lotus 33</a>, after duelling with the Brabhams in the early stages, even leading the race and pulling away before suffering a water pump failure that set him back two laps.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Brabham's win expanded his points lead to 16 points over Hill, with Hill's BRM teammate <a href="/wiki/Jackie_Stewart" title="Jackie Stewart">Jackie Stewart</a> two points further away.
</p>
