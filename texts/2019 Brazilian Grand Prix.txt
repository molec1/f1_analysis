<p>The <b>2019 Brazilian Grand Prix</b> (formally known as the <b>Formula 1 Heineken Grande Prêmio do Brasil 2019</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race that was held on 17 November 2019 at the <a href="/wiki/Aut%C3%B3dromo_Jos%C3%A9_Carlos_Pace" title="Autódromo José Carlos Pace">Autódromo José Carlos Pace</a> in <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo" title="São Paulo">São Paulo</a>, Brazil. The race was the twentieth and penultimate round of the <a href="/wiki/2019_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2019 Formula One World Championship">2019 Formula One World Championship</a> and marked the 48th running of the <a href="/wiki/Brazilian_Grand_Prix" title="Brazilian Grand Prix">Brazilian Grand Prix</a> and the 47th time that the race has been run as a World Championship event since the inaugural season in <a href="/wiki/1950_Formula_One_season" title="1950 Formula One season">1950</a>.
</p><p>The race was won by <a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a> with <a href="/wiki/Pierre_Gasly" title="Pierre Gasly">Pierre Gasly</a> and <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Sainz_Jr." title="Carlos Sainz Jr.">Carlos Sainz Jr.</a> taking their first podiums in second and third respectively.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2019_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a> maintained his lead down into the first corner, while just behind, <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> overtook <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> for second. Apart from a few minor changes in the midfield, and <a href="/wiki/Charles_Leclerc" title="Charles Leclerc">Charles Leclerc</a> steadily making upwards progress from fourteenth on the grid, the order remained generally stable for the first few laps. On lap 9 <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> attempted an overtake on <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Magnussen" title="Kevin Magnussen">Kevin Magnussen</a>, but Ricciardo locked his tyres, causing a collision which resulted in Magnussen going into a spin and Ricciardo suffering damage to his front wing. Ricciardo was later given a five-second time penalty for the incident. By lap 11 Leclerc had made his way up to sixth place.
</p><p>By lap 18 the soft tyres were generally running out of life with <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a> the first to stop the following lap. On lap 20 <a href="/wiki/Mercedes_in_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Mercedes in F1">Mercedes</a> pitted Hamilton, hoping to <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_motorsport_terms#U" title="Glossary of motorsport terms">undercut</a> Verstappen. The next lap, <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a> pitted Verstappen to try to cover off Hamilton. The pit stop broke the record for fastest pit stop with a time of 1.82 seconds, beating the previous time of 1.88 seconds which was made during the <a href="/wiki/2019_German_Grand_Prix" title="2019 German Grand Prix">2019 German Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> As Verstappen exited the pits the <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> of <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a> pulled out in front of him, forcing the Red Bull to take evasive action; this resulted in Verstappen losing time and falling behind Hamilton. Verstappen and Hamilton quickly passed Leclerc, before Verstappen overtook Hamilton into turn 1. The two then passed <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Albon" title="Alexander Albon">Alexander Albon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a>, and Vettel, who all pitted. At this point in the race, the cars outside the top six ran: <a href="/wiki/Pierre_Gasly" title="Pierre Gasly">Pierre Gasly</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Antonio_Giovinazzi" title="Antonio Giovinazzi">Antonio Giovinazzi</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a> rounding out the top ten.
</p><p>Over the next dozen of laps the wind started to pick up in speed. On lap 42 Bottas pitted for hard tyres, with his teammate, Hamilton, pitting two laps later to go onto the mediums. On lap 45 Verstappen pitted to prevent the undercut attempt from Hamilton. Further back, Bottas was unable to find a way past the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> of Leclerc. Bottas retired on lap 52 with a hydraulics problem. His stopped car was adjudged to need a <a href="/wiki/Safety_car" title="Safety car">safety car</a> to retrieve, initiating a round of pit stops. Hamilton was told to do the opposite of Verstappen, and so Hamilton stayed out whereas Verstappen pitted.
</p><p>Further back, Leclerc and <a href="/wiki/George_Russell_(racing_driver)" title="George Russell (racing driver)">George Russell</a> also pitted and any lapped cars were allowed to unlap themselves forcing the safety car to stay out for another couple of laps. At the restart, the order of the top ten was as follows: Hamilton, Verstappen, Vettel, Albon, Leclerc, Gasly, <a href="/wiki/Romain_Grosjean" title="Romain Grosjean">Romain Grosjean</a>, <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Sainz_Jr." title="Carlos Sainz Jr.">Carlos Sainz</a>, Räikkönen and Giovinazzi. Verstappen managed to overtake Hamilton into turn 1. Further back, the other <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a> of Albon overtook Vettel for third, while Grosjean in the <a href="/wiki/Haas_F1_Team" title="Haas F1 Team">Haas</a> started to have problems with his car, causing him to drop back. On lap 66 Leclerc overtook Vettel in turn 1, but Vettel, on the back straight, tried to take the position back. The two collided, with Leclerc suffering a broken suspension and Vettel getting a puncture. Both Ferraris retired from the race with terminal damage, and the safety car was called out again.
</p><p>Mercedes then pitted Hamilton, still on lap 66, onto fresh soft tyres. This put him in line behind the safety car in fourth position, ahead of the <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> of Sainz, but behind the Red Bulls of Verstappen and Albon, and the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso</a> of Gasly. During the safety car, <a href="/wiki/Lance_Stroll" title="Lance Stroll">Lance Stroll</a> retired with front suspension damage. At the race restart, on lap 70, Hamilton passed Gasly, but later made contact with Albon while trying to pass him at turn 10. Albon was sent into a spin and was forced to rejoin the field in fifteenth position. Hamilton, too, was slowed by the contact, allowing Gasly to pass into second place. Verstappen came home to win his third race of the season. Six seconds behind him, Gasly beat Hamilton for second place by 0.062 seconds. Sainz, two seconds behind them both, finished fourth.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post_race">Post race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2019_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Post race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:F%C3%B3rmula_1_%E2%80%93_Grande_Pr%C3%AAmio_do_Brasil_de_F1_2019_(49080565726).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e0/F%C3%B3rmula_1_%E2%80%93_Grande_Pr%C3%AAmio_do_Brasil_de_F1_2019_%2849080565726%29.jpg/220px-F%C3%B3rmula_1_%E2%80%93_Grande_Pr%C3%AAmio_do_Brasil_de_F1_2019_%2849080565726%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e0/F%C3%B3rmula_1_%E2%80%93_Grande_Pr%C3%AAmio_do_Brasil_de_F1_2019_%2849080565726%29.jpg/330px-F%C3%B3rmula_1_%E2%80%93_Grande_Pr%C3%AAmio_do_Brasil_de_F1_2019_%2849080565726%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e0/F%C3%B3rmula_1_%E2%80%93_Grande_Pr%C3%AAmio_do_Brasil_de_F1_2019_%2849080565726%29.jpg/440px-F%C3%B3rmula_1_%E2%80%93_Grande_Pr%C3%AAmio_do_Brasil_de_F1_2019_%2849080565726%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1280" data-file-height="853" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:F%C3%B3rmula_1_%E2%80%93_Grande_Pr%C3%AAmio_do_Brasil_de_F1_2019_(49080565726).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pierre_Gasly" title="Pierre Gasly">Pierre Gasly</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> (subsequently penalised) on the podium.</div></div></div>
<p>As the final lap of the race was being run, a stewards investigation was announced concerning the Hamilton–Albon incident on lap 70. The podium ceremony was run with Verstappen, Gasly, and Hamilton, but with the investigation ongoing for Hamilton. The stewards summoned Hamilton and Albon and Hamilton was given a five-second time penalty which dropped him from third to seventh. His penalty promoted Sainz to third, representing Sainz's first podium finish (although Sainz was not present at the podium ceremony), McLaren's first since the <a href="/wiki/2014_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="2014 Australian Grand Prix">2014 Australian Grand Prix</a> and also setting a new record for the youngest combined podium age, beating the <a href="/wiki/2008_Italian_Grand_Prix" title="2008 Italian Grand Prix">2008 Italian Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The race was the first since the <a href="/wiki/1994_German_Grand_Prix" title="1994 German Grand Prix">1994 German Grand Prix</a> in which two drivers scored their first podiums in Formula One.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The race also marked the first time in the turbo-hybrid era, introduced in <a href="/wiki/2014_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2014 Formula One World Championship">2014</a>, and the first time since the <a href="/wiki/2013_United_States_Grand_Prix" title="2013 United States Grand Prix">2013 United States Grand Prix</a>, that the final podium trio did not feature a <a href="/wiki/Mercedes_in_Formula_One" class="mw-redirect" title="Mercedes in Formula One">Mercedes</a> or <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> driver.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> This race also meant that all 3 drivers on the podium are graduates of the <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Junior_Team" title="Red Bull Junior Team">Red Bull Junior Team</a> programme.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>. <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Sainz_Jr" class="mw-redirect" title="Carlos Sainz Jr">Carlos Sainz Jr</a>'s podium finish meant that he was first <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_drivers_from_Spain" title="Formula One drivers from Spain">Spanish driver</a> to step on the podium since <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> took his most recent podium finish at the <a href="/wiki/2014_Hungarian_Grand_Prix" title="2014 Hungarian Grand Prix">2014 Hungarian Grand Prix</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
