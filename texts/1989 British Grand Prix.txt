<p>The <b>1989 British Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>XLII Shell British Grand Prix</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at <a href="/wiki/Silverstone_Circuit" title="Silverstone Circuit">Silverstone</a> on 16 July 1989. It was the eighth race of the <a href="/wiki/1989_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1989 Formula One World Championship">1989 Formula One World Championship</a>.
</p><p>The 64-lap race was won by Frenchman <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/5" title="McLaren MP4/5">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Honda_in_Formula_One" title="Honda in Formula One">Honda</a>, after he started from second position. Prost's Brazilian teammate, <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a>, took <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> and led until he spun off on lap 12. Local driver <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> finished second in a <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_640" title="Ferrari 640">Ferrari</a>, with Italian <a href="/wiki/Alessandro_Nannini" title="Alessandro Nannini">Alessandro Nannini</a> third in a <a href="/wiki/Benetton_B189" title="Benetton B189">Benetton</a>-<a href="/wiki/Ford_Motor_Company" title="Ford Motor Company">Ford</a>. This marked the last win for a Honda powered car at Silverstone until <a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a> won the <a href="/wiki/70th_Anniversary_Grand_Prix" title="70th Anniversary Grand Prix">70th Anniversary Grand Prix</a> in 2020.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1989_British_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-qualifying_report">Pre-qualifying report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1989_British_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Pre-qualifying report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>For the second Grand Prix in a row, the <a href="/wiki/Onyx_Grand_Prix" title="Onyx Grand Prix">Onyx</a> of <a href="/wiki/Bertrand_Gachot" title="Bertrand Gachot">Bertrand Gachot</a> topped the time sheets in Friday morning's pre-qualifying session. It was Gachot's second pre-qualifying success. Two tenths of a second slower in second place was <a href="/wiki/Nicola_Larini" title="Nicola Larini">Nicola Larini</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Osella" title="Osella">Osella</a>, pre-qualifying for the third time this season. The other two to go through to the main qualifying sessions were the <a href="/wiki/Brabham" title="Brabham">Brabham</a> pairing of <a href="/wiki/Stefano_Modena" title="Stefano Modena">Stefano Modena</a> and <a href="/wiki/Martin_Brundle" title="Martin Brundle">Martin Brundle</a>. This was the team's last pre-qualifying session of the season, as their five points in <a href="/wiki/1989_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="1989 Monaco Grand Prix">Monaco</a> were enough to enable them to avoid having to pre-qualify for the rest of the season. Modena had pre-qualified at all eight events so far in 1989, and Brundle six, with both drivers qualifying for all of those races. 
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Stefan_Johansson" title="Stefan Johansson">Stefan Johansson</a> narrowly missed out in fifth place in the second Onyx, his fourth failure this season. <a href="/wiki/Alex_Caffi" title="Alex Caffi">Alex Caffi</a> was sixth in the <a href="/wiki/Dallara" title="Dallara">Dallara</a>, only his second failure, but Dallara were another team who would avoid pre-qualifying for the rest of the season, having scored eight points.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Gregor_Foitek" title="Gregor Foitek">Gregor Foitek</a> was seventh in the sole <a href="/wiki/EuroBrun" title="EuroBrun">EuroBrun</a>, still only able to pre-qualify once this season, which was once more than <a href="/wiki/Piercarlo_Ghinzani" title="Piercarlo Ghinzani">Piercarlo Ghinzani</a>, eighth in the second Osella. Ninth was <a href="/wiki/Yannick_Dalmas" title="Yannick Dalmas">Yannick Dalmas</a>, who had replaced <a href="/wiki/Joachim_Winkelhock" title="Joachim Winkelhock">Joachim Winkelhock</a> at <a href="/wiki/Automobiles_Gonfaronnaises_Sportives" title="Automobiles Gonfaronnaises Sportives">AGS</a>, and for the rest of the season, both AGS cars would have to take part in pre-qualifying sessions. The <a href="/wiki/Zakspeed" title="Zakspeed">Zakspeed</a> pairing of <a href="/wiki/Bernd_Schneider_(racing_driver)" title="Bernd Schneider (racing driver)">Bernd Schneider</a> and <a href="/wiki/Aguri_Suzuki" title="Aguri Suzuki">Aguri Suzuki</a> were tenth and twelfth, with no success at this stage since the first race of the season. <a href="/wiki/Pierre-Henri_Raphanel" title="Pierre-Henri Raphanel">Pierre-Henri Raphanel</a> was eleventh in his <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Coloni" title="Scuderia Coloni">Coloni</a>, and from here on, both Colonis would have to take part in pre-qualifying. At the bottom of the time sheets was the <a href="/wiki/Rial_Racing" title="Rial Racing">Rial</a> of <a href="/wiki/Volker_Weidler" title="Volker Weidler">Volker Weidler</a>, his eighth consecutive failure to pre-qualify, but <a href="/wiki/Christian_Danner" title="Christian Danner">Christian Danner</a>'s three points at <a href="/wiki/1989_United_States_Grand_Prix" title="1989 United States Grand Prix">Phoenix</a> meant both cars would avoid pre-qualifying for the rest of the season.<sup id="cite_ref-Walker_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Walker-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying_report">Qualifying report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1989_British_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Qualifying report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>McLaren took control of qualifying with <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> ahead of <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a>. Having announced before the previous race in <a href="/wiki/1989_French_Grand_Prix" title="1989 French Grand Prix">France</a> that he would be leaving McLaren at the end of the season, Prost now announced that he would be joining <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> in <a href="/wiki/1990_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1990 Formula One World Championship">1990</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Derek_Warwick" title="Derek Warwick">Derek Warwick</a> returned after missing the French Grand Prix, qualifying 19th. Still in pain from his karting accident, he had a special seat fitted in his <a href="/wiki/Arrows_Grand_Prix_International" title="Arrows Grand Prix International">Arrows</a>. <a href="/wiki/Maur%C3%ADcio_Gugelmin" title="Maurício Gugelmin">Maurício Gugelmin</a> qualified sixth, but his <a href="/wiki/March_Engineering" title="March Engineering">March</a> developed a water-related problem moments before the race, forcing him to start from the pit lane.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1989_British_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_report">Race report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1989_British_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Race report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Prost beat Senna off the line, but the Brazilian was later on the brakes and re-took the lead going into Copse Corner. They were followed by the Ferraris of <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a>. At the end of lap 4, Berger pulled into the pits with electrical problems, rejoining the race some laps later. On lap 5 the race order was Senna, Prost, Mansell, the <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> pair of <a href="/wiki/Thierry_Boutsen" title="Thierry Boutsen">Thierry Boutsen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Riccardo_Patrese" title="Riccardo Patrese">Riccardo Patrese</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Alessandro_Nannini" title="Alessandro Nannini">Alessandro Nannini</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a>.
</p><p>Senna spun out on lap 12 going into Becketts due to gear selection problems, handing Prost the lead from Mansell. Patrese overtook teammate Boutsen for third, before suffering a huge accident on lap 20 when a burst radiator sprayed water onto his rear wheels, causing him to spin off into the tyre barrier at Club Corner. With Prost and Mansell pulling away, <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a> moved into third in his <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Lotus</a> on lap 23, ahead of Boutsen, <a href="/wiki/Philippe_Alliot" title="Philippe Alliot">Philippe Alliot</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Larrousse" title="Larrousse">Larrousse</a>-<a href="/wiki/Lola_Cars" title="Lola Cars">Lola</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_Racing" title="Tyrrell Racing">Tyrrell</a>. Boutsen dropped back with a rear puncture while Alesi spun off at Club Corner on lap 29, at which point Prost led Mansell by 3.2 seconds, followed by Piquet, Nannini, Gugelmin and Alliot.
</p><p>Alliot retired with an engine failure on lap 40. On lap 42, Mansell developed a puncture on his right front tyre, forcing him to pit. Prost was then delayed during his own pit stop for fresh tyres, but retained a healthy lead over Mansell. Gugelmin retired from fifth with a gearbox failure on lap 55, before Nannini passed Piquet for third on lap 56, pulling away in the closing laps.
</p><p>At the chequered flag, Prost was 19 seconds ahead of Mansell, with Nannini a further 29 seconds back and Piquet the last driver on the lead lap. The <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardis</a> of <a href="/wiki/Pierluigi_Martini" title="Pierluigi Martini">Pierluigi Martini</a> and <a href="/wiki/Luis_P%C3%A9rez-Sala" title="Luis Pérez-Sala">Luis Pérez-Sala</a> finished fifth and sixth, scoring three points which prevented the team from being relegated to the pre-qualifying sessions for the second half of 1989 (and kept the <a href="/wiki/Onyx_Grand_Prix" title="Onyx Grand Prix">Onyx</a> team in these sessions, despite their two points for <a href="/wiki/Stefan_Johansson" title="Stefan Johansson">Stefan Johansson</a>'s fifth place at the previous race in France). This was Sala's only point in Formula One.
</p>
