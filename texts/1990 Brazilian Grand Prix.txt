<p>The <b>1990 Brazilian Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at <a href="/wiki/Aut%C3%B3dromo_Jos%C3%A9_Carlos_Pace" title="Autódromo José Carlos Pace">Interlagos</a>, <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Paulo" title="São Paulo">São Paulo</a> on 25 March 1990. It was the second race of the <a href="/wiki/1990_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1990 Formula One World Championship">1990 Formula One World Championship</a>. It was also the first <a href="/wiki/Brazilian_Grand_Prix" title="Brazilian Grand Prix">Brazilian Grand Prix</a> to be held at Interlagos since <a href="/wiki/1980_Brazilian_Grand_Prix" title="1980 Brazilian Grand Prix">1980</a>, following the renovation and shortening of the circuit and the ascendancy of São Paulo driver <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a>.
</p><p>The 71-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_641" title="Ferrari 641">Ferrari</a>. Senna took <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> in his <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/5" title="McLaren MP4/5">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Honda_in_Formula_One" title="Honda in Formula One">Honda</a> and led until he collided with <a href="/wiki/Satoru_Nakajima" title="Satoru Nakajima">Satoru Nakajima</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_018" title="Tyrrell 018">Tyrrell</a>-<a href="/wiki/Ford_Motor_Company" title="Ford Motor Company">Ford</a>, allowing Prost through. Prost took his 40th Grand Prix victory, and his sixth and final Brazilian win, with Senna's teammate <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> second and Senna himself recovering to third.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1990_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The rebuilt <a href="/wiki/Aut%C3%B3dromo_Jos%C3%A9_Carlos_Pace" title="Autódromo José Carlos Pace">Interlagos</a> circuit, which hosted the race for the first time since <a href="/wiki/1980_Brazilian_Grand_Prix" title="1980 Brazilian Grand Prix">1980</a>, had been dramatically altered. The track had been shortened by 3.5&#160;km and lost many fast sweepers and the Retao straight, which had allowed Formula One drivers to use full throttle for 20 seconds.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1990_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-qualifying_report">Pre-qualifying report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1990_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Pre-qualifying report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the Friday morning pre-qualifying session, the <a href="/wiki/Larrousse" title="Larrousse">Larrousse</a>-<a href="/wiki/Lola_Cars" title="Lola Cars">Lola</a> cars secured a 1–2, with <a href="/wiki/%C3%89ric_Bernard" title="Éric Bernard">Éric Bernard</a> a couple of tenths of a second faster than his team-mate <a href="/wiki/Aguri_Suzuki" title="Aguri Suzuki">Aguri Suzuki</a>. Five thousandths of a second behind Suzuki in third was <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Grouillard" title="Olivier Grouillard">Olivier Grouillard</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Osella" title="Osella">Osella</a>. The other pre-qualifier was <a href="/wiki/Yannick_Dalmas" title="Yannick Dalmas">Yannick Dalmas</a> in his <a href="/wiki/Automobiles_Gonfaronnaises_Sportives" title="Automobiles Gonfaronnaises Sportives">AGS</a>, the first time the Frenchman had progressed to the main qualifying sessions this season. Dalmas edged out his team-mate <a href="/wiki/Gabriele_Tarquini" title="Gabriele Tarquini">Gabriele Tarquini</a>, who was fifth, the fastest runner to drop out at this stage.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Roberto_Moreno" title="Roberto Moreno">Roberto Moreno</a> also missed out in sixth in an eventful session. His <a href="/wiki/EuroBrun" title="EuroBrun">EuroBrun</a> suffered an ignition problem just 200 metres after leaving the pitlane and the team decided to let Moreno use the car of his team-mate <a href="/wiki/Claudio_Langes" title="Claudio Langes">Claudio Langes</a>, who at that stage had the sixth best time. Moreno promised Langes he would return the car for the last 10 minutes of the session. Moreno managed a fast lap that placed him at the top of the pre-qualifying table at that point, with a time of 1:25.763. But as he attempted a second fast lap straight afterwards, the car stopped on the track, because the team had miscalculated the fuel quantity needed for two fast laps and the EuroBrun ran out of fuel. Moreno's hope of progressing to qualifying proper ended as the track dried up, and in the final minutes of the session he was bumped down to sixth place. Langes never got back the car, and was thirteen seconds adrift of Moreno's time in eighth place.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Bertrand_Gachot" title="Bertrand Gachot">Bertrand Gachot</a> struggled badly again in the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Coloni" title="Scuderia Coloni">Coloni</a>, faster than Langes but still ten seconds off Bernard's pace in seventh. The Coloni's <a href="/wiki/Subaru_1235" title="Subaru 1235">Subaru 1235</a> engine, built by <a href="/wiki/Motori_Moderni" title="Motori Moderni">Motori Moderni</a>, was proving to be overweight, underpowered and fragile.<sup id="cite_ref-Walker_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Walker-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Bottom of the time sheets was <a href="/wiki/Gary_Brabham" title="Gary Brabham">Gary Brabham</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Life_Racing_Engines" title="Life Racing Engines">Life</a>, failing to post a time at all. The car's engine broke a <a href="/wiki/Connecting_rod" title="Connecting rod">connecting rod</a> after a quarter of a lap, leaving Brabham and his manager to question the Australian's future at the team.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Brabham later stated that he had been uncertain his team would even be present at Interlagos, until he saw the car in the pit garage.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1990_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_report">Race report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1990_Brazilian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Race report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After qualifying, <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Thierry_Boutsen" title="Thierry Boutsen">Thierry Boutsen</a>, himself third on the grid, predicted that the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferraris</a> on the third row of the grid would be the cars to beat, citing their <a href="/wiki/Semi-automatic_transmission" title="Semi-automatic transmission">semi-automatic transmission</a> and its paddle shift which allowed both Alain Prost and <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> to keep their hands on the wheel around the bumpy turns at the back of the circuit
</p><p>At the first corner, <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alessandro_Nannini" title="Alessandro Nannini">Alessandro Nannini</a> and <a href="/wiki/Andrea_de_Cesaris" title="Andrea de Cesaris">Andrea de Cesaris</a> tangled, eliminating de Cesaris and requiring the <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a> driver to stop for a new nosecone. On lap eight Boutsen passed Berger for second place, and Prost took the <a href="/wiki/V12_engine" title="V12 engine">V12</a> Ferrari past the <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> driver on lap 17. Mansell pitted on lap 27 for new tyres and also to inspect a broken rollbar, rejoining in 9th place.
Boutsen's pit stop on lap 30 went disastrously wrong. With failing brakes, and a tricky bump in the pitlane, the Williams was unable to stop and crashed into some of his mechanics and the wheel and tyre equipment stacked outside the garage. This required a new nosecone and when he rejoined, he was down in 11th position.
</p><p>Prost was piling on the pressure, and by lap 35 he had climbed to second within 10 seconds of Senna, and was now ahead of <a href="/wiki/Riccardo_Patrese" title="Riccardo Patrese">Riccardo Patrese</a>, Berger and <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a>. When Senna came up to lap former <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Lotus</a> teammate <a href="/wiki/Satoru_Nakajima" title="Satoru Nakajima">Satoru Nakajima</a>, there was contact and the McLaren had to pit for a new nosecone. He rejoined and challenged hard, but the reduced downforce levels made the car difficult to drive. On lap 66, Patrese retired with a broken oil cooler.
</p><p>Prost took his 40th victory, and his first for Ferrari, from Berger and the recovering Senna. Mansell finished an excellent fourth, having driven through the field with a broken rollbar. Boutsen finished a creditable fifth and Piquet claimed the final point in front of his home crowd after passing Alesi – who was suffering severe tyre wear after attempting to run non-stop on his <a href="/wiki/Pirelli" title="Pirelli">Pirellis</a> – on the last lap.
</p>
