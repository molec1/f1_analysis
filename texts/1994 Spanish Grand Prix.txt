<p>The <b>1994 Spanish Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 29 May 1994 at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Catalunya" class="mw-redirect" title="Circuit de Catalunya">Circuit de Catalunya</a>, <a href="/wiki/Barcelona" title="Barcelona">Barcelona</a>. It was the 36th <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Grand_Prix" title="Spanish Grand Prix">Spanish Grand Prix</a> and the fourth to be held at the Circuit de Catalunya, and the fifth race of the <a href="/wiki/1994_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1994 Formula One World Championship">1994 Formula One World Championship</a>.
</p><p>The 65-lap race was won by Englishman <a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW16" title="Williams FW16">Williams</a>-<a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>. It was the first victory of the season for Hill and the Williams team, who were still recovering from the death of <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> at <a href="/wiki/1994_San_Marino_Grand_Prix" title="1994 San Marino Grand Prix">Imola</a> four weeks previously. Hill won by 24 seconds from German driver and championship leader <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>, whose <a href="/wiki/Benetton_B194" title="Benetton B194">Benetton</a>-<a href="/wiki/Ford_Motor_Company" title="Ford Motor Company">Ford</a> was stuck in fifth gear for most of the race. Another Englishman, <a href="/wiki/Mark_Blundell" title="Mark Blundell">Mark Blundell</a>, finished third in a <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_022" title="Tyrrell 022">Tyrrell</a>-<a href="/wiki/Yamaha_Motor_Company" title="Yamaha Motor Company">Yamaha</a>, which would turn out to be the final podium finish for the Tyrrell team.
</p><p>The Grand Prix was additionally notable for the season-ending crash of debutant Italian driver <a href="/wiki/Andrea_Montermini" title="Andrea Montermini">Andrea Montermini</a> in his <a href="/wiki/Simtek_S941" title="Simtek S941">Simtek S941</a> on the front straight. Montermini, elevated from test driver status after the death of <a href="/wiki/Roland_Ratzenberger" title="Roland Ratzenberger">Roland Ratzenberger</a> at the San Marino Grand Prix crashed heavily into the pit wall. It also marked the Formula One debut of Scottish driver <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a>, replacing Senna for <a href="/wiki/Williams_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams F1">Williams</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1994_Spanish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-race">Pre-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1994_Spanish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Pre-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>This was the first race for the newly formed <a href="/wiki/Grand_Prix_Drivers_Association" class="mw-redirect" title="Grand Prix Drivers Association">Grand Prix Drivers Association</a> (GPDA). Several top level names, including Michael Schumacher, Damon Hill, and Gerhard Berger, were instrumental in the setup and running of the GPDA, and they took the decision to install a temporary chicane before the Nissan corner.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> This was an attempt to improve safety, as well as limit speeds around the track, as the Nissan corner was generally taken at near flat-out speeds.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1994_Spanish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> took the second pole position in succession and the second of his career. <a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a> also lined up on the front row, his time had been beaten by Schumacher's by over half a second, and he was only one thousandth of a second ahead of <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a>. The pair's respective Williams and <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> teammates lined up eighth and ninth, McLaren's <a href="/wiki/Martin_Brundle" title="Martin Brundle">Martin Brundle</a> on the fourth row in front of a disappointed David Coulthard for Williams. Schumacher's <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a> teammate – <a href="/wiki/JJ_Lehto" title="JJ Lehto">JJ Lehto</a> – had done a bit better for himself and lined up fourth. <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a> qualified in fifth, followed by the two <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferraris</a>. <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_Racing" title="Tyrrell Racing">Tyrrell</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Ukyo_Katayama" title="Ukyo Katayama">Ukyo Katayama</a> was the other driver in the top ten, while at the back <a href="/wiki/Andrea_Montermini" title="Andrea Montermini">Andrea Montermini</a> crashed heavily on the Simtek previously driven by Roland Ratzenberger and broke his ankles. Obviously he failed to set a timed lap in the second session and failed to qualify.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1994_Spanish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Olivier_Beretta" title="Olivier Beretta">Olivier Beretta</a> retired when his Larousse-Ford's engine failed on the formation lap. Schumacher led from pole position at the start of the race whilst Barrichello and Berger collided at the first corner. Neither driver retired as a direct result of the collision, although both did eventually, but Berger was forced to run across the grass and lost places and would eventually retire on lap 28 with gearbox problems.
Coulthard climbed up to fifth place from his ninth place starting spot, but his car stalled in the pits on lap 16. Despite retiring from twelfth place on lap 32 with electrical problems, he described his race as a "good debut overall".<sup id="cite_ref-SGP94_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SGP94-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> While <a href="/wiki/Williams_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams F1">Williams</a> notched up their first victory of the season, and first after the <a href="/wiki/Death_of_Ayrton_Senna" title="Death of Ayrton Senna">death of</a> <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a>, championship leader <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> finished a strong second, despite being stuck in fifth gear for most of the race.<sup id="cite_ref-SGP94_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SGP94-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Knowing that he had a major problem, he managed to make a pitstop (and get away from the pit stop in 5th gear), and as the race unfolded gave nothing to the leading Hill's pace. He had to change his driving style to find new trajectories and corner apexes, and his past experience as a <a href="/wiki/Group_C" title="Group C">World Sports Car</a> driver helped him to do so.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Meanwhile Rubens Barrichello retired after he spun off near the pit entry on lap 40 and Schumacher was once again able to make a pitstop and not stall the car. After the pit stops, <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> was in third place for McLaren behind Hill and Schumacher, having temporarily been in front of Schumacher earlier in the race. Häkkinen's engine failed as did JJ Lehto's Benetton-Ford engine 5 laps after Hakkinen on lap 54, though granting the place to Hakkinen's teammate <a href="/wiki/Martin_Brundle" title="Martin Brundle">Martin Brundle</a>. Brundle then retired himself after a transmission explosion from the back of his car at the first corner by lap 60 and was classified 11th. <a href="/wiki/Mark_Blundell" title="Mark Blundell">Mark Blundell</a>, for Tyrrell, completed the podium celebrations, scoring his 3rd and last podium of his career.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1994_Spanish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>After the race, the <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a> team were very emotional with Hill's win.
</p>

Everyone at Williams has been through a terrible time. This victory must go to them and to all the fans of <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> who I met in Brazil. They wanted Williams to be successful. It was important to do well in this race and to win it is better than I expected to do.</p><div class="templatequotecite">—&#8201;<cite><a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a> speaking about Senna, Transcript of recording from <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://web.archive.org/web/20070221054652/http://www.gpracing.net192.com/races/reports/553.cfm">Grand Prix Racing</a>.</cite></div></blockquote>
