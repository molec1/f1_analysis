<p>The <b>2013 Italian Grand Prix</b> (formally known as the <b>2013 Formula 1 Gran Premio d'Italia</b>)<sup id="cite_ref-formal_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-formal-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Auto_racing" title="Auto racing">motor race</a> held on 8 September 2013 at the <a href="/wiki/Autodromo_Nazionale_di_Monza" title="Autodromo Nazionale di Monza">Autodromo Nazionale di Monza</a> in <a href="/wiki/Monza" title="Monza">Monza</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA_calender_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA_calender-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The race was the 12th round of the <a href="/wiki/2013_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2013 Formula One season">2013 season</a>, and the 83rd running of the <a href="/wiki/Italian_Grand_Prix" title="Italian Grand Prix">Italian Grand Prix</a>.
</p><p>Prior to the weekend, <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a> revealed that <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> will be driving alongside <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> in 2014, taking over from <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a>, who is moving to the <a href="/wiki/Porsche" title="Porsche">Porsche</a> Sportscar programme.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Furthermore, <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> announced two days after the race that he will leave <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> at the end of <a href="/wiki/2013_Formula_One_Season" class="mw-redirect" title="2013 Formula One Season">2013</a> after eight years with the team.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> On the next day, the Scuderia announced that former Ferrari driver and <a href="/wiki/2007_Formula_One_Season" class="mw-redirect" title="2007 Formula One Season">2007</a> champion <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> will take Massa's place.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2013_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Tyre supplier Pirelli brought its hardest two compounds, with the orange-banded hard compound tyre being the harder "prime" tyre, and the white-banded medium compound tyre as the softer "option" tyre. This is the same tyre allocation as for the <a href="/wiki/2012_Italian_Grand_Prix" title="2012 Italian Grand Prix">2012 Italian Grand Prix</a>. <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a> looked strong in all three practice sessions prior to qualifying. The two biggest threats to a Vettel pole position, apart for Vettel's teammate <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a>, were <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> and, quite surprisingly, <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>, who had strong pace on a single lap Saturday morning, an area where Ferrari struggled throughout the season. Three-time 2013 pole-sitter <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> had really poor mileage in the final practice session due to overheating<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> and was unlikely to fight for pole because of this.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Q1">Q1</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2013_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Q1">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Drivers eliminated in the first qualifying session were both drivers from <a href="/wiki/Caterham_F1" title="Caterham F1">Caterham</a> and <a href="/wiki/Marussia_F1" title="Marussia F1">Marussia</a> as well as <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a> of <a href="/wiki/Williams_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams F1">Williams</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Esteban_Guti%C3%A9rrez" title="Esteban Gutiérrez">Esteban Gutiérrez</a>. Several drivers were out on track early on option tyres, including 2007 champion <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jean-%C3%89ric_Vergne" title="Jean-Éric Vergne">Jean-Éric Vergne</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Q2">Q2</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2013_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Q2">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The second qualifying session was the stage of a few surprises. First, both <a href="/wiki/Lotus_F1" title="Lotus F1">Lotus</a> drivers were eliminated at the end of the 15 minutes session. Qualifying has never been the strength of the <a href="/wiki/Lotus_E21" title="Lotus E21">E21</a> this season, especially on the lowest downforce track of the season. The biggest disappointment of the session was the elimination of Hamilton's <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a>. The five-time 2013 pole-sitter had an off at the exit of Curva Parabolica on his first flying lap and was impeded by <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a> on his second attempt at the entrance of the same turn. The German was later handed a three-place grid penalty for the race.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> It was the first time that Hamilton failed to make a Q3 appearance since the <a href="/wiki/2010_Malaysian_Grand_Prix" title="2010 Malaysian Grand Prix">2010 Malaysian Grand Prix</a>. As a result, Hamilton's streak of consecutive Q3 appearances ended at 66.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Q3">Q3</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2013_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Q3">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Both Ferraris and Red Bulls were out early as well as Rosberg's Mercedes. The red cars were using their towing tactic, which they practiced a lot during free practice. Vettel was the fastest man of that group with a time of 1:23.859. All ten cars then went out on track, either for a second run or for a first. Rosberg and Ricciardo were out first in the second wave as they opted for a two-flying lap run. Webber improved his time on his second run, but stayed second behind Vettel while the German also improved his fastest time to 1:23.755. <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a> set up for a massively impressive third place, especially with his teammate early exit in Q1. Ferrari's towing tactic did not do any good as the red cars could only manage fourth and fifth, with <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> in front of Alonso. Rosberg took a respectable sixth, considering his misfortune of Saturday morning. Ricciardo again showed great qualifying pace with seventh despite running wide at turn six. The <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> duo could only manage eight and ninth, with <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a> in front, while Vergne had to settle for tenth with an off that was a near carbon copy of Hamilton's in Q2 at Curva Parabolica.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2013_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>At the start, <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> led the race from pole position with <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> jumping up into second position, ahead of <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> and teammate <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>. Further down the order, <a href="/wiki/Lotus_F1" title="Lotus F1">Lotus</a>' <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> was forced to put his left wheels on the grass to avoid <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a> on the approach of Variante del Rettifilo, preventing the Finn to brake in time and to avoid running in the back of the Mexican's <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>. Räikkönen had to pit for a new front wing at the end of the first lap. More drama unfolded during the first lap as <a href="/wiki/Paul_di_Resta" title="Paul di Resta">Paul di Resta</a> ran into the back of <a href="/wiki/Romain_Grosjean" title="Romain Grosjean">Romain Grosjean</a>'s Lotus.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The Scot lost his front left wheel and retired his car on the spot, while Grosjean's car was left unscathed by the incident. On lap three, Alonso made his move on Webber at Variante della Roggia for third place and the Spaniard later passed his teammate at Variante del Rettifilo on lap eight for second place.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> picked up a slow puncture early on in the race as well as radio problems.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> This meant that his team could not tell him about the tire problem and had to communicate with him with the help of pit boards throughout the rest of the race. Hamilton's one stop strategy became a two-stopper because of the slow puncture and rejoined the track almost at the back of the grid. Further ahead, <a href="/wiki/Jean-%C3%89ric_Vergne" title="Jean-Éric Vergne">Jean-Éric Vergne</a> was on his way to a points finish until his transmission failed on lap fifteen.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Later on, Webber managed to leapfrog Massa during the pit stops. The impressive <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a> managed to stay in front of <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> for a net fifth place, but the recovering Hamilton passed both Germans at Curva Biassono and at Variante del Rettifilo respectively, though the Briton had to stop again because of his earlier tire problem. Räikkönen was also recovering from his first-lap clash with Pérez, taking advantage of a battle between <a href="/wiki/Esteban_Guti%C3%A9rrez" title="Esteban Gutiérrez">Esteban Gutiérrez</a> and <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a>, although Hamilton passed the three of them after his second pit stop. The Briton then quickly caught the McLaren duo and overtook them almost as quickly. Räikkönen also overtook Pérez and went after <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> for tenth and the last championship point.
</p><p>At the end of his 52nd lap, Sutil pulled into the Force India garage and retired with a brake problem. He was classified 16th, as he had completed 90% of the winner's race distance. A lap later, Sebastian Vettel crossed the start/finish line to win the 2013 Italian Grand Prix, his sixth win of the season, ahead of Alonso and teammate Webber. Massa drove to a respectable fourth, while Hülkenberg successfully beat Rosberg to the flag for a strong fifth place. <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> took advantage of the great top speed of his <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso</a> to successfully fight off Grosjean for a solid seventh. Hamilton recovered from his slow puncture to pick up ninth, while Button managed to hold Räikkönen behind him for tenth.
</p>
