<p>The <b>2003 Spanish Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>XLV Gran Premio Marlboro de España</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 4 May 2003 at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Barcelona-Catalunya" title="Circuit de Barcelona-Catalunya">Circuit de Catalunya</a> in Barcelona, Spain. It was the fifth round of the <a href="/wiki/2003_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2003 Formula One season">2003 Formula One season</a> and the forty-fifth <a href="/wiki/Spanish_Grand_Prix" title="Spanish Grand Prix">Spanish Grand Prix</a>, and also the last held on this layout of the circuit. The 65-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> in a <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> car after starting from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>. <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> finished second driving for the <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a> team with <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> third in the other Ferrari.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2003_Spanish_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>This was the first double retirement of the season for <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>.</li>
<li>Ferrari debuted their new car, the F2003-GA at this race. GA was added to the cars name as a tribute to <a href="/wiki/Gianni_Agnelli" title="Gianni Agnelli">Gianni Agnelli</a>, head of Fiat, who died shortly before the car's unveiling.</li>
<li>First points: <b><a href="/wiki/Cristiano_da_Matta" title="Cristiano da Matta">Cristiano da Matta</a></b>.</li>
<li>Only point: <b><a href="/wiki/Ralph_Firman" title="Ralph Firman">Ralph Firman</a></b>.</li></ul>
