<p>The <b>2009 Belgian Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>LXV ING Belgian Grand Prix</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 30 August 2009 at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Spa-Francorchamps" title="Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps">Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps</a> near the town of <a href="/wiki/Spa,_Belgium" title="Spa, Belgium">Spa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">Belgium</a>. It was the 12th race of the <a href="/wiki/2009_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2009 Formula One season">2009 Formula One season</a>. The race was contested over 44 laps, with an overall race distance of 308.2&#160;km (191.5&#160;mi). The race was won by <a href="/wiki/2007_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2007 Formula One season">2007</a> world champion <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> of <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> after starting from sixth on the grid.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> of <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a> came second after starting from the team's first and only pole position. <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> came third after starting from eighth on the grid.
</p><p>It was Räikkönen's first Grand Prix victory since the <a href="/wiki/2008_Spanish_Grand_Prix" title="2008 Spanish Grand Prix">2008 Spanish Grand Prix</a> and Ferrari's only victory of the season. Fisichella's second position gave Force India their first ever championship points and their first podium. This race also marks Fisichella's final podium and last points in <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a>, before he became a <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> test driver in <a href="/wiki/2010_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2010 Formula One World Championship">2010</a>, as well as Räikkönen's 18th and last Grand Prix victory until the <a href="/wiki/2012_Abu_Dhabi_Grand_Prix" title="2012 Abu Dhabi Grand Prix">2012 Abu Dhabi Grand Prix</a> and last for Ferrari until the <a href="/wiki/2018_United_States_Grand_Prix" title="2018 United States Grand Prix">2018 United States Grand Prix</a>. This was the last race for <a href="/wiki/Luca_Badoer" title="Luca Badoer">Luca Badoer</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2009_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2009_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> led the World Championship entering the Belgian race weekend despite not scoring a podium since <a href="/wiki/2009_Turkish_Grand_Prix" title="2009 Turkish Grand Prix">early June</a>. He had an 18-point lead over <a href="/wiki/Brawn_GP" title="Brawn GP">Brawn GP</a> teammate <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a>. <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> was 2½ points behind Barrichello in third and was 4½ points ahead of <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a> teammate <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a>. The respective recoveries of <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> and <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> ensured that the last two World Champions, <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>, had scored podiums in the two races leading up to Belgium. Brawn GP led Red Bull Racing by 27½ points in the Constructors' Championship. The next team, Ferrari, were 52½ points behind Red Bull and 80 points behind Brawn. Ferrari's Kimi Räikkönen, who had previously won the event three times, was the only former winner entered in the race.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice_and_Qualifying">Practice and Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2009_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Practice and Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Rain affected the first practice session of the weekend. Only 10 cars got dry lap times in while the rest of the field posting lap times in the wet. <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> posted the fastest lap followed by Button. <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> was third followed by the two <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rossos</a>. <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> posted the fastest time off the wet runners followed by <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a>. Neither Vettel nor Hamilton posted lap times.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The second session stayed dry throughout with Hamilton topping the session. <a href="/wiki/Timo_Glock" title="Timo Glock">Timo Glock</a> was second and Räikkönen third, the top three cars were separated by less than 0.7s. Fisichella kept his pace for Force India as he came sixth. This was one of the closest sessions in the history of Formula One with 18 of the 20 cars coming within less than a second from the leader. Only Rosberg and <a href="/wiki/Luca_Badoer" title="Luca Badoer">Luca Badoer</a> were more than a second away from Hamilton's time. Both Brawns struggled coming only 17th and 18th.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> This session may have seen another indication that <a href="/wiki/BMW_in_Formula_One" title="BMW in Formula One">BMW Sauber</a> have greatly improved their car after <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> topped the session. Another <a href="/wiki/Toyota_Racing_(Formula_One_team)" title="Toyota Racing (Formula One team)">Toyota</a> came second with Trulli and Force India's <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a> came third. Hamilton took 9th place with Button rounding off the top ten. Red Bull's problems with the <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a> engine continued as Webber's engine had a major problem. This gave Red Bull-Renault their third engine failure in a week.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The first session would see five cars drop out and decide the final five grid positions. Badoer qualified 20th for the second race in a row, after crashing at the end of the session and bringing out the yellow flag, causing many drivers to slow down.<sup id="cite_ref-Qualy5_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Qualy5-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Fisichella topped the session with Trulli finishing just behind him. The other four drivers to be knocked out at the end of the session were Renault's <a href="/wiki/Romain_Grosjean" title="Romain Grosjean">Romain Grosjean</a> (P19), <a href="/wiki/Williams_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams F1">Williams</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Kazuki_Nakajima" title="Kazuki Nakajima">Kazuki Nakajima</a> (P18) and the two Toro Rossos of <a href="/wiki/Jaime_Alguersuari" title="Jaime Alguersuari">Jaime Alguersuari</a> (P17) and <a href="/wiki/S%C3%A9bastien_Buemi" title="Sébastien Buemi">Sébastien Buemi</a> (P16).<sup id="cite_ref-Qualy_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Qualy-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The second session would again see another five cars drop out to decide grid positions 11–15. The session was topped by the Toyota of Jarno Trulli, while BMW Sauber's <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a> was second with Red Bull's Vettel coming third. Also significant in the second part of qualifying was the elimination of Brawn's Jenson Button who could only manage P14. The two McLarens of <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a> (P15) and Lewis Hamilton (P12) were also knocked out along with the second Renault of Alonso (P13) and Force India's Adrian Sutil (P11).<sup id="cite_ref-Qualy_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Qualy-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Fisichella_Belgium_2009_(cropped).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/45/Fisichella_Belgium_2009_%28cropped%29.jpg/220px-Fisichella_Belgium_2009_%28cropped%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="128" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/45/Fisichella_Belgium_2009_%28cropped%29.jpg/330px-Fisichella_Belgium_2009_%28cropped%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/45/Fisichella_Belgium_2009_%28cropped%29.jpg/440px-Fisichella_Belgium_2009_%28cropped%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="546" data-file-height="317" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Fisichella_Belgium_2009_(cropped).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> claimed the maiden pole position for <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>The final session would see the remaining 10 grid positions be decided. Williams's new aero package was not working as they would like as Rosberg qualified P10. The two Red Bulls of Webber (P9) and Vettel (P8) seemed to struggle in conditions favouring their cars. Glock took P7 with Räikkönen, driving the only <a href="/wiki/Kinetic_energy_recovery_system" title="Kinetic energy recovery system">KERS</a> car in the top 10, coming in sixth. The two BMW Saubers' new updates seemed to be lifting the whole team as Kubica (P5) and Heidfeld (P3) achieved their best qualifying positions of the season. The only Brawn in the final session, that of Rubens Barrichello, took P4. One of the biggest surprises on an already unpredictable track, Force India took their very first pole position. It was Fisichella's first pole position since the <a href="/wiki/2006_Malaysian_Grand_Prix" title="2006 Malaysian Grand Prix">2006 Malaysian Grand Prix</a>. Trulli took second place.<sup id="cite_ref-Qualy_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Qualy-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2009_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Jenson_Button_2009_Belgium.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ee/Jenson_Button_2009_Belgium.jpg/220px-Jenson_Button_2009_Belgium.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="109" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ee/Jenson_Button_2009_Belgium.jpg/330px-Jenson_Button_2009_Belgium.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ee/Jenson_Button_2009_Belgium.jpg/440px-Jenson_Button_2009_Belgium.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1768" data-file-height="875" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Jenson_Button_2009_Belgium.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> failed to complete the first lap, his only failure to score points in the 2009 campaign.</div></div></div>
<p>As the race started everyone seemed to make a good start apart from Rubens Barrichello, who almost stalled his car, causing him to drop to the back of the field. At Les Combes, at the top of the hill, a crash caused the safety car to come out.<sup id="cite_ref-Race_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Race-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Romain Grosjean ran into the back of Jenson Button who in turn spun 180° and collided with Grosjean for a second time, causing them to both retire. Lewis Hamilton backed off after seeing the incidents ahead of him, which caused Jaime Alguersuari to run into him; both crashed into the barrier and retired.<sup id="cite_ref-Race_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Race-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The safety car came in on lap 5 with Fisichella still leading and Räikkönen right behind him. Räikkönen closed up to the Force India driver through the Eau Rouge corner and passed him on the way into Les Combes. The main bulk of the first round of stops came in around lap 14. Mark Webber was released in front of Nick Heidfeld and was handed a drive through penalty, severely affecting his race.<sup id="cite_ref-Race_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Race-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Kimi_Raikkonen_2009_Belgium.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4f/Kimi_Raikkonen_2009_Belgium.jpg/220px-Kimi_Raikkonen_2009_Belgium.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="103" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4f/Kimi_Raikkonen_2009_Belgium.jpg/330px-Kimi_Raikkonen_2009_Belgium.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4f/Kimi_Raikkonen_2009_Belgium.jpg/440px-Kimi_Raikkonen_2009_Belgium.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1204" data-file-height="565" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Kimi_Raikkonen_2009_Belgium.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> achieved Ferrari's only win of 2009.</div></div></div>
<p>After the first stop Fisichella closed to within one second of Räikkönen. Jarno Trulli retired from the race on lap 22; after starting on the front row he had dropped to the back of the field, having pitted after a first lap collision. Fernando Alonso retired on lap 27 with a suspected wheel problem after a first corner collision with Adrian Sutil. Räikkönen and Fisichella pitted for the second time on the same lap and it was the Finn who emerged in front and kept his lead.<sup id="cite_ref-Race_7-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Race-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Vettel recorded the fastest lap of the race with a late push to try to catch the leaders but it was to no avail as Räikkönen took the chequered flag for the first time this season with Fisichella scoring Force India's first-ever points with a second-place finish. Vettel finished third, followed by the two BMW Saubers and Heikki Kovalainen finishing sixth. After dropping to the back of the field at the start, Rubens Barrichello came in seventh with Nico Rosberg picking up the final point in eighth, finishing his seven race points streak. Barrichello's car was smoking from the back for the last few laps and the car caught fire in parc fermé. Webber finished ninth for the second race in a row and scored no points.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2009_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Podium_2009_Belgium.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/be/Podium_2009_Belgium.jpg/220px-Podium_2009_Belgium.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="237" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/be/Podium_2009_Belgium.jpg/330px-Podium_2009_Belgium.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/be/Podium_2009_Belgium.jpg/440px-Podium_2009_Belgium.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2137" data-file-height="2298" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Podium_2009_Belgium.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The post-race podium ceremony. From left to right: <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Stefano_Domenicali" title="Stefano Domenicali">Stefano Domenicali</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>With Button failing to score, his championship lead became smaller for the second race in a row as his teammate Barrichello closed the gap by two points. Vettel's third place moved him ahead of his teammate Webber.<sup id="cite_ref-Standings_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Standings-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Raikkonen's win further extended Ferrari's lead in the constructors' championship over McLaren, but his teammate Luca Badoer was again under scrutiny after finishing last for the second time: reports came out of the paddock that second place man Fisichella would replace him at Ferrari for the <a href="/wiki/2009_Italian_Grand_Prix" title="2009 Italian Grand Prix">Italian Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> On 31 August Force India team principal <a href="/wiki/Vijay_Mallya" title="Vijay Mallya">Vijay Mallya</a> reported that Fisichella would not be driving for <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> at <a href="/wiki/Autodromo_Nazionale_Monza" class="mw-redirect" title="Autodromo Nazionale Monza">Monza</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> However, on 3 September Fisichella was released from his contract at Force India and joined Ferrari until the end of the season.<sup id="cite_ref-Fisichella_Ferrari_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Fisichella_Ferrari-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Force India announced on 7 September that <a href="/wiki/Vitantonio_Liuzzi" title="Vitantonio Liuzzi">Vitantonio Liuzzi</a>, the team's test driver, would replace Fisichella for the remaining five races of the season.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 4 September, <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a> were summoned to a meeting by Formula One's governing body, the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">FIA</a> with the allegations of race fixing during the <a href="/wiki/2008_Singapore_Grand_Prix" title="2008 Singapore Grand Prix">2008 Singapore Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Race_Fix_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Race_Fix-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Renault faced the possibility of expulsion from the championship<sup id="cite_ref-Race_Fix_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Race_Fix-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> as <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> were in the <a href="/wiki/2007_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2007 Formula One World Championship">2007</a> Formula One season. During the <a href="/wiki/FIA_World_Motor_Sport_Council" title="FIA World Motor Sport Council">FIA World Motor Sport Council</a> meeting later that month, Renault admitted that <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet_Jr." title="Nelson Piquet Jr.">Nelson Piquet Jr.</a> had deliberately crashed under the instructions of Renault team principal <a href="/wiki/Flavio_Briatore" title="Flavio Briatore">Flavio Briatore</a> and chief engineer <a href="/wiki/Pat_Symonds" title="Pat Symonds">Pat Symonds</a>, in the hope of helping Alonso win. The Renault team were handed a disqualification from F1, which was suspended for two years pending any further rule infringements. Briatore was banned from all FIA-sanctioned events for life, while Symonds was banned for five years.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
