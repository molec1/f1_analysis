<p>The <b>1984 European Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held at <a href="/wiki/N%C3%BCrburgring" title="Nürburgring">Nürburgring</a> on 7 October 1984. It was the fifteenth race of the <a href="/wiki/1984_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1984 Formula One World Championship">1984 Formula One World Championship</a>. It was also the first Formula One race to be held at the Nürburgring since 1976, and the first to be run on the new 4.54&#160;km (2.82&#160;mi) GP-Strecke circuit, rather than the historic 20.892&#160;km (12.982&#160;mi) Nordschleife, which after new pit facilities and layout modifications had been shortened so that both circuits could be used at the same time.
</p><p>The 67-lap race was won by Frenchman <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Techniques_d%27Avant_Garde" title="Techniques d&#39;Avant Garde">TAG</a>. Italian <a href="/wiki/Michele_Alboreto" title="Michele Alboreto">Michele Alboreto</a> finished second in a <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_126C" title="Ferrari 126C">Ferrari</a>, with Brazilian <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a> third in a <a href="/wiki/Brabham_BT53" title="Brabham BT53">Brabham</a>-<a href="/wiki/BMW_in_Formula_One" title="BMW in Formula One">BMW</a>, having started from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>.
</p><p>Prost's Austrian teammate <a href="/wiki/Niki_Lauda" title="Niki Lauda">Niki Lauda</a>, who nearly lost his life in the <a href="/wiki/1976_German_Grand_Prix" title="1976 German Grand Prix">last F1 race at the Nürburgring in 1976</a>, finished fourth. With the reallocation of the disqualified <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_Racing" title="Tyrrell Racing">Tyrrell</a> team's points two days after the race and Prost thus gaining an extra point, Lauda's lead in the Drivers' Championship was reduced to 3.5 points with one race remaining.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1984_European_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>During the race morning warm-up session, <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> spun his <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/2" title="McLaren MP4/2">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Techniques_d%27Avant_Garde" title="Techniques d&#39;Avant Garde">TAG</a> and hit a course car parked at the side of the circuit.
</p><p>Young Brazilian driver <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> triggered a first corner accident which took out the cars of <a href="/wiki/Keke_Rosberg" title="Keke Rosberg">Keke Rosberg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marc_Surer" title="Marc Surer">Marc Surer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Piercarlo_Ghinzani" title="Piercarlo Ghinzani">Piercarlo Ghinzani</a>. Senna's <a href="/wiki/Toleman_TG184" title="Toleman TG184">Toleman</a> (which had started 12th) had run into the back of Rosberg's <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW09" title="Williams FW09">Williams</a> under braking at the end of the main straight which caused the accident. Rosberg had started fourth (after blowing his <a href="/wiki/Honda_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Honda F1">Honda</a> engine coming out of the final corner of his qualifying lap), but was slow off the line as his engine had suddenly developed a misfire.
</p><p>After qualifying second on the grid, Alain Prost won the race in his McLaren from the <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_126_C" class="mw-redirect" title="Ferrari 126 C">Ferrari</a> of <a href="/wiki/Michele_Alboreto" title="Michele Alboreto">Michele Alboreto</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Brabham_BT53" title="Brabham BT53">Brabham</a>-<a href="/wiki/BMW_M12" title="BMW M12">BMW</a> of defending World Champion <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a>, with both the Ferrari and Brabham running out of fuel as they crossed the finish line. When they got out of their cars which stopped at the pit exit, Alboreto and Piquet raised their arms to each other in a gesture of frustration at <a href="/wiki/FIA" class="mw-redirect" title="FIA">FISA</a>'s 220 litre fuel limit for turbos which had reduced races to economy runs.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Niki_Lauda" title="Niki Lauda">Niki Lauda</a>, who had almost lost his life in a fiery crash during the 1976 German GP, started 15th and finished 4th in his McLaren. In stark contrast to the lack of safety of the Nordschleife, Lauda gave the new GP-Strecke the thumbs up as a very safe Grand Prix circuit, saying that it was "the perfect place to hold a Grand Prix".
</p>
