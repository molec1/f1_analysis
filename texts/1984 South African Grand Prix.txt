<p>The <b>1984 South African Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held at <a href="/wiki/Kyalami" title="Kyalami">Kyalami</a> on 7 April 1984. It was race 2 of 16 in the <a href="/wiki/1984_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1984 Formula One World Championship">1984 Formula One World Championship</a>. The 75-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Niki_Lauda" title="Niki Lauda">Niki Lauda</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/2" title="McLaren MP4/2">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Techniques_d%27Avant_Garde" title="Techniques d&#39;Avant Garde">TAG</a>, with teammate <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> second and <a href="/wiki/Derek_Warwick" title="Derek Warwick">Derek Warwick</a> third in a <a href="/wiki/Renault_RE50" title="Renault RE50">Renault</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1984_South_African_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1984_South_African_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Qualifying saw reigning World Champion <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a> take the first of an eventual nine <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole positions</a> for 1984 in his <a href="/wiki/Brabham_BT53" title="Brabham BT53">Brabham</a>-<a href="/wiki/BMW_in_Formula_One" title="BMW in Formula One">BMW</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Keke_Rosberg" title="Keke Rosberg">Keke Rosberg</a> alongside him on the front row in his <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW09" title="Williams FW09">Williams</a>-<a href="/wiki/Honda_in_Formula_One" title="Honda in Formula One">Honda</a>. On the second row were <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Lotus</a> and <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Tambay" title="Patrick Tambay">Patrick Tambay</a> in the factory <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>, while on the third row were <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> in the <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> and <a href="/wiki/Teo_Fabi" title="Teo Fabi">Teo Fabi</a> in the second Brabham. The top ten was completed by <a href="/wiki/Elio_de_Angelis" title="Elio de Angelis">Elio de Angelis</a> in the second Lotus, <a href="/wiki/Niki_Lauda" title="Niki Lauda">Niki Lauda</a> in the second McLaren, <a href="/wiki/Derek_Warwick" title="Derek Warwick">Derek Warwick</a> in the second Renault and <a href="/wiki/Michele_Alboreto" title="Michele Alboreto">Michele Alboreto</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Cosworth" title="Cosworth">Cosworth</a>-powered <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_Racing" title="Tyrrell Racing">Tyrrells</a> and <a href="/wiki/Arrows_Grand_Prix_International" title="Arrows Grand Prix International">Arrows</a> struggled, with <a href="/wiki/Thierry_Boutsen" title="Thierry Boutsen">Thierry Boutsen</a> coming 27th and last in his Arrows and thus failing to qualify.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1984_South_African_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the Sunday morning warm-up session <a href="/wiki/Piercarlo_Ghinzani" title="Piercarlo Ghinzani">Piercarlo Ghinzani</a>, who had qualified 20th in his <a href="/wiki/Osella" title="Osella">Osella</a>, crashed heavily at the fast left-hand Jukskei Sweep, the car bursting into flames. Ghinzani escaped with minor burns on his hands, and subsequently withdrew from the race, thus promoting Boutsen to the last grid spot.
</p><p>At the start, Piquet almost stalled on the grid and was passed by Rosberg and Mansell. The Lotus then faltered and Mansell fell down the order, putting Piquet back up to second by the first corner. At the end of lap 1, Piquet passed Rosberg for the lead, before teammate Fabi moved into second on lap 2. Lauda had made a good start to run fourth; he then overtook Rosberg on lap 4 and Fabi on lap 10. Both Brabhams then hit trouble, needing new tyres before retiring with <a href="/wiki/Turbocharger" title="Turbocharger">turbo</a> failures, Fabi on lap 19 and Piquet on lap 30.
</p><p>From there, Lauda controlled the race and went on to win easily, with Prost over a minute behind in second and the only other driver on the lead lap. Warwick gained his first Formula One podium by finishing third, while completing the top six were <a href="/wiki/Riccardo_Patrese" title="Riccardo Patrese">Riccardo Patrese</a> (<a href="/wiki/Alfa_Romeo_184T" title="Alfa Romeo 184T">Alfa Romeo</a>), <a href="/wiki/Andrea_de_Cesaris" title="Andrea de Cesaris">Andrea de Cesaris</a> (<a href="/wiki/Ligier_JS23" title="Ligier JS23">Ligier</a>-Renault) and, gaining his first ever World Championship point, Ayrton Senna in his <a href="/wiki/Toleman_TG183" title="Toleman TG183">Toleman</a>-<a href="/wiki/Hart_Racing_Engines" title="Hart Racing Engines">Hart</a>.
</p>
