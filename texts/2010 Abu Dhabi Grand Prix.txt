<p>The <b>2010 Abu Dhabi Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>Etihad Airways Abu Dhabi Grand Prix</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 14 November 2010 at the <a href="/wiki/Yas_Marina_Circuit" title="Yas Marina Circuit">Yas Marina Circuit</a> on Yas Island, an island on the outskirts of <a href="/wiki/Abu_Dhabi" title="Abu Dhabi">Abu Dhabi</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_Arab_Emirates" title="United Arab Emirates">United Arab Emirates</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> It was the nineteenth and last round of the <a href="/wiki/2010_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2010 Formula One season">2010 Formula One season</a>. The 55-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> after starting from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>. <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> finished second in a <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>, and teammate <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> completed the podium, in third place.
</p><p>A second race win in succession – as well as a second consecutive Abu Dhabi Grand Prix win – for Vettel, coupled with other championship contenders Hamilton, <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> finishing second, seventh and eighth respectively, allowed him to become the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_driver_records#Youngest_World_Drivers&#39;_Championship_winners" title="List of Formula One driver records">youngest World Drivers' Champion</a>, at the age of 23&#160;years, 134&#160;days.<sup id="cite_ref-BBCAbu_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCAbu-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Button's third place allowed him to finish fifth in the championship standings, having been eliminated from championship contention at the previous race in <a href="/wiki/2010_Brazilian_Grand_Prix" title="2010 Brazilian Grand Prix">Brazil</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Due to injuries sustained in a rally crash prior to the <a href="/wiki/2011_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2011 Formula One World Championship">2011 Formula One season</a>, this was the last Formula One race for <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a> until his return in 2019. This was also the last Grand Prix for <a href="/wiki/Lucas_di_Grassi" title="Lucas di Grassi">Lucas di Grassi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Christian_Klien" title="Christian Klien">Christian Klien</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_Abu_Dhabi_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_Abu_Dhabi_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Heading into the final race of the season, <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> was leading the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a> with 246&#160;<a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Championship_points_scoring_systems" title="List of Formula One World Championship points scoring systems">points</a>; <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a> drivers <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> were second and third on 238&#160;points and 231&#160;points respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-permutations_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-permutations-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> was also in mathematical contention on 222&#160;points, 24 behind Alonso with a maximum of 25 points available for the final race.<sup id="cite_ref-permutations_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-permutations-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> As a result, this was the first occasion that four drivers still had a chance of winning the Drivers' Championship at the final event of the season.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a>, <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a> had already secured their first championship title with a total of 469&#160;points,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> 48&#160;clear of second-placed <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> with a maximum of 43&#160;points available. McLaren were themselves not confirmed in second place in the championship, with a 32-point advantage over <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>.
</p>

<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">To interfere with the drivers was never a possibility for us. The whole world condemned Ferrari after what they did in Hockenheim, but we have turned out as idiots because we did not act in this way. We never even thought about it as long as both our drivers remain in the hunt for the championship. It is not assured that Alonso will always be the lucky one. <p><cite class="left-aligned" style=""><a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a> team owner <a href="/wiki/Dietrich_Mateschitz" title="Dietrich Mateschitz">Dietrich Mateschitz</a>, on team orders.<sup id="cite_ref-dietrich_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dietrich-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>Much of the media coverage occurring before the event was centred upon Red Bull, and the possibility of team orders being used to influence the result of the championship – if the team was in the position to do so – as had been used by Ferrari at the <a href="/wiki/2010_German_Grand_Prix" title="2010 German Grand Prix">German Grand Prix</a>. Ferrari used radio transmissions to allow Fernando Alonso to overtake teammate <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> for the race victory and gain seven extra points towards the Drivers' Championship. Team owner <a href="/wiki/Dietrich_Mateschitz" title="Dietrich Mateschitz">Dietrich Mateschitz</a> and team principal <a href="/wiki/Christian_Horner" title="Christian Horner">Christian Horner</a> both stated that Red Bull would not impose any team orders in order to benefit one of its drivers in a bid to stop Alonso from winning the championship.<sup id="cite_ref-dietrich_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dietrich-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Sebastian Vettel himself had stated that he would help his teammate win the championship if required,<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> having trailed Mark Webber by seven points heading into the weekend.
</p><p>After 242 races since their full-time return in <a href="/wiki/1997_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1997 Formula One World Championship">1997</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> tyre supplier <a href="/wiki/Bridgestone" title="Bridgestone">Bridgestone</a> bowed out of Formula One, having been the sport's single tyre supplier since <a href="/wiki/Michelin" title="Michelin">Michelin</a> pulled out after the <a href="/wiki/2006_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2006 Formula One World Championship">2006</a> season.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Pirelli" title="Pirelli">Pirelli</a> took over the tyre contract in <a href="/wiki/2011_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2011 Formula One World Championship">2011</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> returning to Formula One for the first time since <a href="/wiki/1991_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1991 Formula One World Championship">1991</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice_and_qualifying">Practice and qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_Abu_Dhabi_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Practice and qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Alonso_abu_dabi_2010.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5e/Alonso_abu_dabi_2010.jpg/220px-Alonso_abu_dabi_2010.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="163" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5e/Alonso_abu_dabi_2010.jpg/330px-Alonso_abu_dabi_2010.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5e/Alonso_abu_dabi_2010.jpg/440px-Alonso_abu_dabi_2010.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3849" data-file-height="2847" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Alonso_abu_dabi_2010.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> only required a top-two placing to win the title without having to rely on other results. He placed in the top six in each of the three free practice sessions, and qualified third for the race.</div></div></div>
<p>Three practice sessions were held before the race; the first on Friday afternoon and the second on Friday evening. Both sessions lasted 90&#160;minutes. The third session was held on Saturday afternoon and lasted an hour.<sup id="cite_ref-sporting_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sporting-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> All three sessions were held in dry conditions, although the first session was held on a damp but drying track after a pre-session downpour which had not been anticipated.<sup id="cite_ref-prac1_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prac1-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-prac2_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prac2-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Vettel was quickest with a time of 1:42.760 in the first session, just over six-tenths of a second faster than Hamilton. Button was another four-tenths off Hamilton's pace, followed by Webber, <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a>, and Alonso. <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> was seventh, the only other driver to be within 1.5 seconds of Vettel's time.<sup id="cite_ref-prac1_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prac1-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> In the second practice session, Hamilton was fastest with a time of 1:40.888, around a quarter of a second quicker than second-placed Vettel. Alonso took third place, ahead of Webber, Kubica and <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-prac2_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prac2-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher was only quick enough for eleventh position in the session, marginally slower than his <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> teammate <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a>. The Saturday afternoon session was held on a much warmer track, which reached temperatures as high as 43&#160;°C (109&#160;°F),<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> compared with 29&#160;°C (84&#160;°F) and 31&#160;°C (88&#160;°F) track temperatures for the Friday sessions.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Vettel was again quickest, posting a time of 1:40.696, narrowly faster than teammate Webber.<sup id="cite_ref-prac3_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prac3-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton, Alonso, Button and <a href="/wiki/Vitaly_Petrov" title="Vitaly Petrov">Vitaly Petrov</a> rounded out the top six positions.<sup id="cite_ref-prac3_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-prac3-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Vettel_abu_dabi_2010.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/69/Vettel_abu_dabi_2010.jpg/220px-Vettel_abu_dabi_2010.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="199" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/69/Vettel_abu_dabi_2010.jpg/330px-Vettel_abu_dabi_2010.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/69/Vettel_abu_dabi_2010.jpg/440px-Vettel_abu_dabi_2010.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3050" data-file-height="2757" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Vettel_abu_dabi_2010.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> set the fastest time in the last two parts of the qualifying session, and started from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>. His tenth pole position of the season enabled his <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a> team to tie <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>'s single-season record of 15 pole positions, set during the <a href="/wiki/1988_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1988 Formula One World Championship">1988</a> season.<sup id="cite_ref-RBRpole_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RBRpole-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup></div></div></div>
<p>The qualifying session held on Saturday evening was split into three&#160;parts. The first part ran for 20&#160;minutes and eliminated the cars from qualifying that finished the session 18th or lower. The second part of qualifying lasted 15&#160;minutes and eliminated cars that finished in positions&#160;11 to 17. The final part of qualifying determined the positions from first to tenth, and decided <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>. Cars which competed in the final session of qualifying were not allowed to change tyres before the race, and as such started the race on the tyres that they set their quickest lap on.<sup id="cite_ref-sporting_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sporting-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatright" style="width:31%;&#32;;"><link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r887775652"/>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">I'm not rapped to be fifth on the grid, it's disappointing, but there's still a long way to go tomorrow – the fat lady hasn't sung yet. I would like to have been further up, but I couldn't get the pace. We need to be there at the end of tomorrow's race and a lot can still happen; I haven't helped things today, but the championship's not decided today. I didn't seem to have the grip the other guys had and I need to have a look where I was losing time. We will just have to do the best job we can tomorrow, it's a long race – we're still in the hunt and that's the main thing.</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style=""><a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a>, following the third qualifying session, in which he qualified the farthest back of the four title contenders, in fifth.<sup id="cite_ref-qualquotes_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualquotes-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>Vettel clinched his tenth <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> for the season, with a time of 1:39.394. As a result, Vettel became only the seventh driver to record ten pole positions in a season,<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> and took his team's 15th pole of the season, equalling <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>'s tally from the <a href="/wiki/1988_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1988 Formula One World Championship">1988</a> season.<sup id="cite_ref-RBRpole_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RBRpole-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> He was joined on the front row of the grid by Hamilton.<sup id="cite_ref-qualautosport_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualautosport-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-qualf1_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualf1-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Alonso qualified third, though he was happy with his grid position having outqualified Webber.<sup id="cite_ref-qualquotes_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualquotes-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Button qualified fourth, four-tenths of a second behind Vettel, and Webber qualified fifth. Massa, <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a>, Schumacher, Rosberg and Petrov rounded out the top ten. Kubica only managed 11th – missing out on the top ten for the only time in <a href="/wiki/2010_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2010 Formula One World Championship">2010</a> – having struggled with overall grip in the session.<sup id="cite_ref-qualquotes_22-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualquotes-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Kamui_Kobayashi" title="Kamui Kobayashi">Kamui Kobayashi</a> was best of the <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Saubers</a> in 12th, ahead of the German trio of <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Vitantonio_Liuzzi" title="Vitantonio Liuzzi">Vitantonio Liuzzi</a> qualifying 16th.<sup id="cite_ref-qualautosport_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualautosport-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> The rest of the grid was made up by the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rossos</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus_(2010%E2%80%9311)" title="Team Lotus (2010–11)">Lotuses</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Racing" title="Virgin Racing">Virgins</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Hispania_Racing" class="mw-redirect" title="Hispania Racing">Hispanias</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-qualf1_26-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualf1-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_Abu_Dhabi_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Schumacher_abu_dabi_2010.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/60/Schumacher_abu_dabi_2010.jpg/220px-Schumacher_abu_dabi_2010.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="146" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/60/Schumacher_abu_dabi_2010.jpg/330px-Schumacher_abu_dabi_2010.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/60/Schumacher_abu_dabi_2010.jpg/440px-Schumacher_abu_dabi_2010.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4288" data-file-height="2848" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Schumacher_abu_dabi_2010.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>'s spin and resultant collision with <a href="/wiki/Vitantonio_Liuzzi" title="Vitantonio Liuzzi">Vitantonio Liuzzi</a> brought out the safety car on the first lap.</div></div></div>
<p>The conditions on the grid were dry before the race, the <a href="/wiki/Air_temperature" class="mw-redirect" title="Air temperature">air temperature</a> at 29&#160;°C (84&#160;°F),<sup id="cite_ref-BBCRace_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCRace-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> track temperature at 33&#160;°C (91&#160;°F);<sup id="cite_ref-BBCRace_27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCRace-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-commautosport_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-commautosport-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> and no rain expected.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> The race's formation lap commenced at 17:00&#160;<a href="/wiki/Time_in_the_United_Arab_Emirates" title="Time in the United Arab Emirates">local time</a> (<a href="/wiki/Coordinated_Universal_Time" title="Coordinated Universal Time">UTC</a>+4), with all 24 drivers getting away cleanly. Vettel retained his pole position lead into the first corner, followed by Hamilton, with Button moving into third after Alonso made a slow start. At turn six, Schumacher tried to pass teammate Rosberg around the outside, while battling for eighth position. Schumacher spun his Mercedes,<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> which meant he faced the rest of the oncoming field on track and was collected by Liuzzi, causing both drivers to retire on the spot and the <a href="/wiki/Safety_car#Formula_One" title="Safety car">safety car</a> to be deployed.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> In order to aid any losses that a pit stop may have cost under normal racing conditions, six drivers – Rosberg, Petrov, <a href="/wiki/Jaime_Alguersuari" title="Jaime Alguersuari">Jaime Alguersuari</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Senna" title="Bruno Senna">Bruno Senna</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lucas_di_Grassi" title="Lucas di Grassi">Lucas di Grassi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Christian_Klien" title="Christian Klien">Christian Klien</a><sup id="cite_ref-commautosport_28-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-commautosport-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> – made <a href="/wiki/Pit_stop#Formula_One" title="Pit stop">pit stops</a> at the end of lap one,<sup id="cite_ref-pitstops_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pitstops-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> and rejoined the circuit between 17th and 22nd. Racing resumed at the end of lap five when the safety car pulled into the pit-lane. The top five remained static until the end of lap eleven.
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:WebberAlonsoGPAbuDabi2010.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/97/WebberAlonsoGPAbuDabi2010.jpg/220px-WebberAlonsoGPAbuDabi2010.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="146" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/97/WebberAlonsoGPAbuDabi2010.jpg/330px-WebberAlonsoGPAbuDabi2010.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/97/WebberAlonsoGPAbuDabi2010.jpg/440px-WebberAlonsoGPAbuDabi2010.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3834" data-file-height="2553" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:WebberAlonsoGPAbuDabi2010.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Early pit-stops by <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> did not help their championship chances, as progress up the order was slower than expected.</div></div></div>
<p>Webber – who brushed the armco with the right-hand side of his car three laps previously<sup id="cite_ref-commautosport_28-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-commautosport-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> – pitted after struggling for grip on the super-soft, option tyres.<sup id="cite_ref-pitstops_32-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pitstops-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> He rejoined the circuit 16th, and behind the early-stopping trio of Rosberg, Petrov and Alguersuari. Four laps later, Alonso also brushed the Turn 19 barrier before pitting.<sup id="cite_ref-pitstops_32-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pitstops-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> He rejoined just in front of Webber, who had moved up the field steadily, and was running 13th – and set the race's fastest lap at the time – by the time Alonso pitted. Vettel and Hamilton continued to set quicker lap times than their championship rivals, despite being on heavily worn option tyres compared to the fresher, hard tyres of Alonso and Webber. That pairing caught up with Petrov towards the end of lap&#160;18, a battle that would go on until the race's conclusion, with the Renault driver having already stopped for tyres. Five laps later, Alonso tried to overtake Petrov under braking for Turn 11 but had to back out of the manoeuvre, narrowly avoiding the back of the Renault. On the same lap, Hamilton pitted from second place.<sup id="cite_ref-commautosport_28-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-commautosport-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-pitstops_32-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pitstops-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Vettel pitted from the lead on the next lap,<sup id="cite_ref-pitstops_32-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pitstops-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> and rejoined the circuit comfortably ahead of Hamilton, but in second place behind Button, who had still to stop on track. Hamilton had filtered back into the race in fifth position, behind Kubica and Kobayashi who were running similar strategies to Button out front.<sup id="cite_ref-commautosport_28-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-commautosport-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> After passing Kobayashi, Hamilton closed in behind Kubica but found it hard to pass the Renault, akin to what Alonso had been suffering behind Petrov. Despite having nearly 40&#160;laps on the harder tyre, Kubica's pace was still of a substantial level to hold off the advances of Hamilton.
</p><p>Button pitted on lap&#160;39 to release Vettel back into the lead,<sup id="cite_ref-pitstops_32-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pitstops-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> holding a lead of nearly twelve seconds over Kubica.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Button rejoined in fourth place behind Kubica and Hamilton, with Kubica ever increasing his margin over Petrov and Alonso, who were running inside the points again in seventh and eighth respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-commautosport_28-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-commautosport-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Kubica finally pitted on lap&#160;46 from second place,<sup id="cite_ref-pitstops_32-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pitstops-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> and came back on track in sixth place, crucially ahead of teammate Petrov, Alonso and Webber; the latter pair seeing their championship chances dwindling with every passing lap. In clean air, Hamilton set the race's fastest lap on lap 47 – 1:41.274 – as he closed slightly on Vettel.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> No further passing attempts were made on track, and thus, Vettel led the race to the end, to take his fifth win of the season.<sup id="cite_ref-BBCAbu_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCAbu-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The McLarens of Hamilton and Button completed the podium ahead of Rosberg, the Renaults of Kubica and Petrov, Alonso and Webber. Rounding out the points were Alguersuari and Massa, the Ferrari driver having never got ahead of his rival in 40 laps of battling. With the poor results of Alonso and Webber, Vettel claimed the championship to become the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions#Youngest_Drivers&#39;_Champion" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">youngest Championship winner</a> in the sport<sup id="cite_ref-BBCAbu_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCAbu-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and became the first driver to simultaneously lead the championship for the first time, and as a result win the championship since <a href="/wiki/James_Hunt" title="James Hunt">James Hunt</a> in <a href="/wiki/1976_Formula_One_season" title="1976 Formula One season">1976</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Autosport_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Autosport-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> His championship-winning margin was four points over Alonso,<sup id="cite_ref-Autosport_36-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Autosport-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> with Webber a further ten points behind in third.<sup id="cite_ref-Autosport_36-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Autosport-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2010_Abu_Dhabi_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Hamilton_abu_dabi_2010.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2c/Hamilton_abu_dabi_2010.jpg/220px-Hamilton_abu_dabi_2010.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="146" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2c/Hamilton_abu_dabi_2010.jpg/330px-Hamilton_abu_dabi_2010.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2c/Hamilton_abu_dabi_2010.jpg/440px-Hamilton_abu_dabi_2010.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4288" data-file-height="2848" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Hamilton_abu_dabi_2010.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>'s record for being the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions#Youngest_Drivers&#39;_Champion" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">youngest Championship winner</a> in <a href="/wiki/2008_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2008 Formula One World Championship">2008</a> was broken by <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a>. At 23&#160;years, 134&#160;days, Vettel broke Hamilton's record by 166 days.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup></div></div></div>
<div class="quotebox pullquote floatleft" style="width:33%;&#32;;"><link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r887775652"/>
<div class="quotebox-quote left-aligned" style="">After the race it is always very easy to see the best strategy. As I said now you need to cover from someone and if we didn't stop I think Webber probably would have overtaken us, if we stopped we cover from Webber but let Petrov and Rosberg in front so it was a very difficult call I think. It was obviously a tough difficult race. I lost a position at the start and then when the first safety car came out Petrov and Rosberg came in so... We saw some problems with the soft tyres, Webber pitted and then we tried to cover from him. When you cover someone probably you give something away to the others. So it was then a choice between covering Vettel or Petrov and Rosberg, so we covered Webber and then it was difficult to overtake Petrov. The Renault is very quick on the top speed so it was a frustrating race behind him. But this is a sport, this is motor racing. Sometimes you win, sometimes you lose.</div>
<p><cite class="left-aligned" style="">Championship runner-up <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>, summing up his race and his chances of his third championship disappearing.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup></cite>
</p>
</div>
<p>The top three finishers appeared on the <a href="/wiki/Podium#Usage_in_motorsport" title="Podium">podium</a> and in the subsequent <a href="/wiki/Press_conference" title="Press conference">press conference</a>. Vettel's victory had left him "speechless", and expressed his delight at winning the Championship.<sup id="cite_ref-pressconference_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconference-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> He also stated:
</p>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r960796168">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>To be honest I didn't know anything until I crossed the chequered flag. The last 10 laps I was wondering as my race engineer every lap was trying to give some advice and trying to help me carry the car home. I was thinking why is this guy nervous, we must be in a bloody good position. Then crossing the line he came on the radio very silently and said 'it is looking good, but we have to wait until the cars finish'. I was thinking 'what does he mean' and I hadn't seen the screens. I just wanted to make sure, not get any distraction, just focus on myself. Then he came on the radio and screams at me that we have won the World Championship.<sup id="cite_ref-pressconference_39-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconference-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>
<p>Hamilton praised his team for all their work during the season, stating that "we pushed very hard throughout the year, so a big thank to all the guys back home at the factory for not giving up. Next year will be a better year."<sup id="cite_ref-pressconference_39-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconference-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> Button echoed Hamilton's comments about the work of his McLaren team, and summed up his first season with the team: "I would like to say a big thank you to the team. This is my first year with the team and I have had a great season. I really feel at home and I think the experiences we have gained this year, Lewis and myself, with working closely with the engineers and everyone in the Vodafone McLaren Mercedes team means we can really build on this season for next year."<sup id="cite_ref-pressconference_39-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconference-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
