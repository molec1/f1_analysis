<p>The <b>1954 British Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held at <a href="/wiki/Silverstone_Circuit" title="Silverstone Circuit">Silverstone</a> on 17 July 1954. It was race 5 of 9 in the <a href="/wiki/1954_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1954 World Championship of Drivers">1954 World Championship of Drivers</a>. The 90-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Froil%C3%A1n_Gonz%C3%A1lez" title="José Froilán González">José Froilán González</a> after he started from second position. His teammate <a href="/wiki/Mike_Hawthorn" title="Mike Hawthorn">Mike Hawthorn</a> finished second and <a href="/wiki/Maserati_in_motorsport" title="Maserati in motorsport">Maserati</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Onofre_Marim%C3%B3n" title="Onofre Marimón">Onofre Marimón</a> came in third.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_report">Race report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1954_British_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Race report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A huge crowd turned out at Silverstone to see if Mercedes could repeat their Reims rout. In the end, just two silver cars arrived (for Fangio and Kling). In contrast, Maserati had nine cars, whilst Ferrari had three for the experienced trio of Hawthorn, Gonzalez and Trintignant. Fangio set Silverstone's fastest ever lap, breaking the 100&#160;mph barrier with a lap of 100.35&#160;mph. It was Gonzalez who led away and held the lead until the flag. Behind him, Fangio passed Hawthorn for second but after colliding several times with oil drums in a difficult handling car, he dropped to fourth. Moss took over the position but retired with rear axle problems, leaving Hawthorn to follow home for a Ferrari 1–2 and young Onofre Marimón to take his second (and last) podium place.
</p>
