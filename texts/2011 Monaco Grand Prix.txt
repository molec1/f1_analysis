<p>The <b>2011 Monaco Grand Prix</b>, formally the <b>Formula 1 Grand Prix de Monaco 2011</b>,<sup id="cite_ref-Monaco_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Monaco-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> was held on 29 May 2011 at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Monaco" title="Circuit de Monaco">Circuit de Monaco</a> in <a href="/wiki/Monte_Carlo" title="Monte Carlo">Monte Carlo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Monaco" title="Monaco">Monaco</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Sixth round of the <a href="/wiki/2011_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2011 Formula One season">2011 Formula One season</a>, the 78&#160;lap race was won by the championship leader, <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> who started from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>. <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> was second and <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> third.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Winner Vettel extended his lead in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">World Drivers' Championship</a> to 58&#160;points over <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> who was sixth. <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> finished fourth and maintained third place in the championship, six points behind Hamilton in third and three ahead of Button. In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">World Constructors' Championship</a>, Red Bull extended its lead over McLaren to 61 points, with Ferrari being a further 68&#160;points behind.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Many of the drivers were initially concerned that the <a href="/wiki/Drag_Reduction_System" class="mw-redirect" title="Drag Reduction System">Drag Reduction System</a> (DRS) would prove to be unsafe on the narrow streets of the principality, and so lobbied to have the device banned for the duration of the weekend. Some elements of the paddock, including <a href="/wiki/Williams_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams F1">Williams</a> objected to the ban, and use of the device was retained, with the DRS activation zone encompassing the front straight of the circuit, prompting <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> to comment that he did not think the 300 metres (980&#160;ft) of space allowed for the DRS would promote much overtaking. Following increased pressure from the drivers and FOTA, the FIA agreed to ban the use of the DRS in the tunnel for free practice and qualifying.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Tyre supplier <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_tyres" title="Formula One tyres">Pirelli</a> brought its yellow-banded soft compound tyre as the harder "prime" tyre and the red-banded super-soft compound as the softer "option" compound, as opposed to the previous year where Bridgestone brought the medium compound as the "prime" tyre. The Monaco grand prix marked the debut of the Pirelli red-banded super-soft compound.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the build-up to the race, <a href="/wiki/Hispania_Racing" class="mw-redirect" title="Hispania Racing">Hispania Racing</a> team principal <a href="/wiki/Colin_Kolles" title="Colin Kolles">Colin Kolles</a> suggested that the team would submit an official protest to the FIA over the use of off-throttle blown diffusers.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Hispania had planned for an upgrade that would include the device for the <a href="/wiki/2011_Spanish_Grand_Prix" title="2011 Spanish Grand Prix">Spanish Grand Prix</a> before the FIA declared off-throttle blown diffusers to be a moveable aerodynamic device, and therefore illegal. Hispania then proceeded to abandon the planned upgrade. However, the FIA reversed their decision shortly before the Spanish Grand Prix, instead nominating to discuss the matter with the sport's technical working group. Kolles declared that his team believed the off-throttle blown diffuser concept to be illegal, and was considering a protest before the Monaco Grand Prix.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> were first and second at the previous Grand Prix, the week before in <a href="/wiki/2011_Spanish_Grand_Prix" title="2011 Spanish Grand Prix">Spain</a>; and although they were also first and second in the Drivers' Championship at this point in the season - the gap was not as close as it was in the Grand Prix. Vettel's 4 wins from 5 races had given him 118 points, 41 more than Hamilton's 77 points. Third in the standings was <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a>, 10 behind on 67, and <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> was 6 behind him on 61. <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> was fifth with 51 points - less than half Vettel's total.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a> led the Constructors' Championship with 185 points and <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> were second on 138 points (47 behind). <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> were third on 75 points and the battle for fourth raged on between <a href="/wiki/Lotus_Renault_GP" class="mw-redirect" title="Lotus Renault GP">Renault</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes GP</a> - who were on 46 and 40 points respectively. Before this race, <a href="/wiki/Williams_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams F1">Williams</a> had had the worst start to a season in their history and were yet to score any points.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:2011_Monaco_GP_Virgin_2.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/76/2011_Monaco_GP_Virgin_2.jpg/220px-2011_Monaco_GP_Virgin_2.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="152" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/76/2011_Monaco_GP_Virgin_2.jpg/330px-2011_Monaco_GP_Virgin_2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/76/2011_Monaco_GP_Virgin_2.jpg/440px-2011_Monaco_GP_Virgin_2.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2848" data-file-height="1962" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:2011_Monaco_GP_Virgin_2.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/J%C3%A9r%C3%B4me_d%27Ambrosio" title="Jérôme d&#39;Ambrosio">Jérôme d'Ambrosio</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Virgin_MVR-02" title="Virgin MVR-02">Virgin MVR-02</a> was the slowest car in qualifying</div></div></div>
<p>In the third part of qualifying, <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a> crashed after exiting the tunnel, an accident similar to <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a>'s in the earlier free practice session. However, Pérez was not so lucky as he smashed into the barrier used to separate the track and escape road, and qualifying had to be red flagged for more than half an hour, while Pérez was extricated from the car and the barrier was repaired.  This meant that in the 2:26 time left, none of the remaining nine cars could improve their times due to the lack of heat and performance in their tyres. That meant that Vettel, who already had set the second fastest qualifying time in Monaco history, and Button who had set their times earlier in the session were better off than drivers who did not. Pérez's impact with the barrier was similar to Sauber driver <a href="/wiki/Karl_Wendlinger" title="Karl Wendlinger">Karl Wendlinger</a>'s crash in the run-up to the <a href="/wiki/1994_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="1994 Monaco Grand Prix">1994 Monaco Grand Prix</a>. As expected, the improved safety regulations resulted in Pérez's injuries being less severe, as he suffered a concussion and a sprained thigh.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Neither Hispania driver was able to record a lap time during the qualifying session; Liuzzi due to damage sustained during the earlier practice session, and Karthikeyan due to a gearbox and a rear suspension failure. However, in line with the <a href="/wiki/107%25_rule" title="107% rule">107% rule</a> the team were allowed to enter the race due to posting competitive times during earlier free practice.<sup id="cite_ref-HRT_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HRT-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Kamui_Kobayashi" title="Kamui Kobayashi">Kamui Kobayashi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jaime_Alguersuari" title="Jaime Alguersuari">Jaime Alguersuari</a> had also been called to the stewards' room after Kobayashi slowed down whilst coming into the pits, blocking Alguersuari who was on a flying lap in the first part of the session.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2011_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:2011_Monaco_GP_Sebastien.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4a/2011_Monaco_GP_Sebastien.jpg/220px-2011_Monaco_GP_Sebastien.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="146" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4a/2011_Monaco_GP_Sebastien.jpg/330px-2011_Monaco_GP_Sebastien.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4a/2011_Monaco_GP_Sebastien.jpg/440px-2011_Monaco_GP_Sebastien.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3531" data-file-height="2345" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:2011_Monaco_GP_Sebastien.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> won the race by a slim margin after being pressured all race by <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a></div></div></div>
<p>Vettel won the start from pole and led Button to the first corner, with Alonso getting around a slow starting Webber for third. Hamilton started ninth in the position vacated by Pérez but made little progress on the opening lap. Vettel went 2.4 seconds ahead of Button by the end of the first lap.<sup id="cite_ref-bbc_monaco_as_it_happened_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc_monaco_as_it_happened-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher's anti-stall kicked in at the start  dropping him to tenth at first corner, but he retook ninth from Hamilton at Grand-Hotel hairpin. Vettel continued to build a strong lead but his pit stop on lap 16 was slow and he was fitted with hard compound tyres due to a radio communication error. Alonso briefly led then but when he pitted on Lap 17 Button went to the lead. He stayed there until Lap 33 when he pitted and Vettel once again resumed the lead.
</p><p>On lap 30 Glock's rear left suspension broke, so there were yellow flags for a brief period while they removed his car.<sup id="cite_ref-bbc_monaco_gp_highlights_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc_monaco_gp_highlights-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:2011_Monaco_GP_Button_2.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/82/2011_Monaco_GP_Button_2.jpg/220px-2011_Monaco_GP_Button_2.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/82/2011_Monaco_GP_Button_2.jpg/330px-2011_Monaco_GP_Button_2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/82/2011_Monaco_GP_Button_2.jpg/440px-2011_Monaco_GP_Button_2.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3791" data-file-height="2847" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:2011_Monaco_GP_Button_2.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> finished a close third after the top three battled towards the end of the Grand Prix</div></div></div>
<p>An incident between Hamilton and Massa caused Massa to crash in the tunnel, bringing out the first safety car. At the same time Schumacher's car suffered an airbox fire as he was about to pit,<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> and he retired in the pit entrance forcing a safety car period while they cleared both cars.<sup id="cite_ref-bbc_monaco_gp_highlights_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc_monaco_gp_highlights-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Vettel found himself leading behind the safety car and when racing resumed a battle between Vettel, Alonso and Button began. Vettel held the two former world champions behind him, despite being on tyres that had lasted since lap 16. As Button and Alonso fought for second place, the race looked set for an exciting climax, when an accident occurred involving Hamilton, Sutil, Alguersuari and Petrov. Sutil had hit the barrier on the previous corner, causing a right-rear puncture. Hamilton braked as Sutil lost control, and Alguersuari then ran into the back of Hamilton, damaging Hamilton's rear wing. Alguersuari hit the barrier, causing Petrov to do the same<sup id="cite_ref-bbc_monaco_gp_highlights_13-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc_monaco_gp_highlights-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> and both cars were out of the race, with Petrov being briefly trapped in his car. This brought out the safety car again and on lap 72 the race was <a href="/wiki/List_of_red-flagged_Formula_One_races" title="List of red-flagged Formula One races">red flagged</a>, with the cars stopping on the grid at lap 72. During the red flag period, teams were allowed to change tyres and work on cars (unlike most motorsport, where tyre changes are not permitted during the red flag unless it is for a change of weather conditions). This worked to the advantage of Vettel, Alonso, and Hamilton. Vettel and Alonso had heavily used tyres, while Hamilton most likely would not have been able to finish the race with the damaged rear wing that required almost the entire red flag period to repair.
</p><p>After Petrov was extracted from his car and the track cleared, the race resumed still behind the safety car, which went in at the end of lap 73. On lap 74 there was a brief yellow flag period due to an incident between Hamilton and Maldonado, ending Maldonado's race with a broken front suspension. Vettel took the chequered flag, extending his lead in the world championship to 58 points. Vettel was closely followed by Alonso in second, with Button taking the final podium spot.<sup id="cite_ref-bbc_monaco_as_it_happened_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbc_monaco_as_it_happened-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Hamilton had a difficult day, being penalised for causing two avoidable accidents, one involving Massa (for which he was given a drive-through penalty), and another with Maldonado for which he received a post-race 20 second penalty which did not affect his sixth placing. In an interview with <a href="/wiki/BBC_Sport" title="BBC Sport">BBC Sport</a>, Hamilton criticised the stewards after the race, saying "Out of six races, I've been to the stewards five times. It's a joke, it's an absolute frickin' joke."<sup id="cite_ref-autosport_hamilton_slates_stewards_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-autosport_hamilton_slates_stewards-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> He later apologised.
</p>
