<p>The <b>2002 Hungarian Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at <a href="/wiki/Hungaroring" title="Hungaroring">Hungaroring</a> on 18 August 2002. It was the thirteenth race of the <a href="/wiki/2002_FIA_Formula_One_World_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="2002 FIA Formula One World Championship">2002 FIA Formula One World Championship</a>, and the last race on the original Hungaroring layout, before the circuit was modified in <a href="/wiki/2003_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2003 Formula One World Championship">2003</a>.
</p><p>The 77-lap race was won by Brazilian driver <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_F2002" title="Ferrari F2002">Ferrari</a>. Barrichello's German teammate <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> finished second, thus enabling Ferrari to clinch their fourth consecutive Constructors' Championship. Schumacher's brother <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf</a> finished third in a <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW24" title="Williams FW24">Williams</a>-<a href="/wiki/BMW_in_Formula_One" title="BMW in Formula One">BMW</a>.
</p><p>British driver <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Davidson" title="Anthony Davidson">Anthony Davidson</a> made his F1 debut at this race, replacing Malaysia's <a href="/wiki/Alex_Yoong" title="Alex Yoong">Alex Yoong</a> at <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a>. Yoong had been sent to a "qualifying training" testing programme, having failed to qualify three times in 2002 before this race. The <a href="/wiki/Arrows_Grand_Prix_International" title="Arrows Grand Prix International">Arrows</a> team did not arrive due to ongoing financial troubles.
</p>
