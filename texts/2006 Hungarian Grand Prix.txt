<p>The <b>2006 Hungarian Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 6 August 2006 at the <a href="/wiki/Hungaroring" title="Hungaroring">Hungaroring</a>, <a href="/wiki/Budapest" title="Budapest">Budapest</a>. It was the 13th race of the <a href="/wiki/2006_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2006 Formula One season">2006 Formula One season</a>. <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> won the race driving a <a href="/wiki/Honda_in_Formula_One" title="Honda in Formula One">Honda</a>, the first victory of his career, the first race win for a <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">British</a> driver since <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> won the <a href="/wiki/2003_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="2003 Australian Grand Prix">Australian Grand Prix</a> three years previously, and the first by an <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">Englishman</a> since <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Herbert" title="Johnny Herbert">Johnny Herbert</a> won the <a href="/wiki/1999_European_Grand_Prix" title="1999 European Grand Prix">1999 European Grand Prix</a> nearly seven years previously, in similarly changeable weather circumstances. <a href="/wiki/Pedro_de_la_Rosa" title="Pedro de la Rosa">Pedro de la Rosa</a> finished second for <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren-Mercedes</a>, the only podium finish of his career, and <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> finished third, giving <a href="/wiki/BMW_in_Formula_One" title="BMW in Formula One">BMW Sauber</a> their first ever podium.
</p><p>It was the first win for Honda as a constructor since <a href="/wiki/John_Surtees" title="John Surtees">John Surtees'</a> victory in the <a href="/wiki/1967_Italian_Grand_Prix" title="1967 Italian Grand Prix">1967 Italian Grand Prix</a> 39 years prior, the first win for a Honda engine since <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Mugen-Honda" class="mw-redirect" title="Mugen-Honda">Mugen-Honda</a>-powered <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a> triumphed in the <a href="/wiki/1999_Italian_Grand_Prix" title="1999 Italian Grand Prix">1999 Italian Grand Prix</a>, 7 years and 119 races earlier and the first win for a non-European constructor since <a href="/wiki/Jody_Scheckter" title="Jody Scheckter">Jody Scheckter</a> won with Canadian team <a href="/wiki/Walter_Wolf_Racing" title="Walter Wolf Racing">Wolf</a> in the <a href="/wiki/1977_Canadian_Grand_Prix" title="1977 Canadian Grand Prix">1977 Canadian Grand Prix</a>. The race would also prove to be Honda's only win in their second stint in Formula One as a full constructor, ending in late 2008 after Honda decided to pull out of F1 after the Global Financial Crisis.
</p><p>It was the last victory for a Honda-powered car in Formula One until <a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a> won the <a href="/wiki/2019_Austrian_Grand_Prix" title="2019 Austrian Grand Prix">2019 Austrian Grand Prix</a> for Red Bull. It was also the last victory for <a href="/wiki/Honda_Racing_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Honda Racing F1">Honda</a> as a full constructor entry in Formula One to date. It was also last-ever all-<a href="/wiki/Michelin" title="Michelin">Michelin</a> podium to date.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2006_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2006_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>During Friday practice <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> was given a two-second qualifying penalty by the stewards for dangerous driving and overtaking under a yellow flag condition.<sup id="cite_ref-alonsopenalty_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-alonsopenalty-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> This meant that two seconds would be added to his times in each part of qualifying. <a href="/wiki/Christijan_Albers" title="Christijan Albers">Christijan Albers</a> was given a ten-place grid penalty for an engine change, his second in as many weekends. <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> received the same penalty after having an engine change when his let go in the final practice session.<sup id="cite_ref-buttonalberspeanlty_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-buttonalberspeanlty-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In a similar situation to Alonso's penalty, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> was given a two-second penalty for overtaking <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a> and Alonso under "red flag" conditions at the end of Saturday's last free practice session. The decision left Schumacher "fuming," with him saying "I blame myself partly for what did happen, but I didn't expect this kind of penalty."<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2006_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In qualifying, <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> achieved pole position with a time of 1:19.599 seconds. <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> qualified second, with <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> starting the race in third.<sup id="cite_ref-quali_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-quali-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2006_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Buttonwin.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0a/Buttonwin.jpg/200px-Buttonwin.jpg" decoding="async" width="200" height="133" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0a/Buttonwin.jpg/300px-Buttonwin.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0a/Buttonwin.jpg/400px-Buttonwin.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1600" data-file-height="1064" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Buttonwin.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Button returns to the pit lane having won the race.</div></div></div>
<p>The track was wet at the start of the race, making it the first ever wet <a href="/wiki/Hungarian_Grand_Prix" title="Hungarian Grand Prix">Hungarian Grand Prix</a>. Polesitter <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> took the lead early on. Alonso and Schumacher made their way through the field with Schumacher up into 6th place from 11th into the first corner, and Alonso climbing from 15th place with a spectacular first hard-fought lap. He went on to pass Schumacher on the outside of turn 5 after a straight fight for several laps and reached 3rd place. He then took the lead after the McLarens of <a href="/wiki/Pedro_de_la_Rosa" title="Pedro de la Rosa">Pedro de la Rosa</a> and Räikkönen pitted. Bridgestone wet-tyres used to dictate the field in non-dry conditions but today it seemed a one-off for the Japanese rubber. All Bridgestone-drivers, including the Ferraris, were seen struggling and seriously down on pace compared to their competitors. Schumacher fell right back in the clutches of <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> and lost his front wing battling for 5th place, hitting the Italian mid-corner fighting off snap-oversteer. This forced the German to pit, going a lap down. Soon after Jenson Button overtook Massa, Fisichella and Schumacher in just under the space of 2 laps. Räikkönen struggled on his second set of tyres and ended up crashing into the back of <a href="/wiki/Vitantonio_Liuzzi" title="Vitantonio Liuzzi">Vitantonio Liuzzi</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Toro_Rosso" class="mw-redirect" title="Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso</a>, vaulting the car and bringing out the <a href="/wiki/Safety_car" title="Safety car">safety car</a>. Alonso then pitted, allowing Schumacher to get back on the lead lap. Another beneficiary of the safety car was Jenson Button, who decided not to pit during the safety car period and climbed up to 2nd place behind Alonso. After the period was over Button began to challenge Alonso, but soon had to pit for fuel. Alonso led, but after a pitstop for dry tyres his right-rear wheel nut detached, causing the Spaniard to lose control and crash. Button inherited the lead and was never challenged from then on. Schumacher made his way up to 2nd by staying on intermediate tyres as others around him pitted for dry weather ones, but this gamble backfired as the cars on drys caught him in the final laps. Schumacher defended his position (including controversially cutting a chicane on consecutive laps without penalty<sup id="cite_ref-SchumacherCut_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SchumacherCut-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>), but Pedro de la Rosa and <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> both ultimately passed him. Whilst he was being overtaken Schumacher banged wheels with Heidfeld, damaging his Ferrari's suspension and forcing him out of the race three laps short of the finish.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Pedro_de_la_Rosa_Hungaroring.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ae/Pedro_de_la_Rosa_Hungaroring.jpg/220px-Pedro_de_la_Rosa_Hungaroring.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ae/Pedro_de_la_Rosa_Hungaroring.jpg/330px-Pedro_de_la_Rosa_Hungaroring.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ae/Pedro_de_la_Rosa_Hungaroring.jpg/440px-Pedro_de_la_Rosa_Hungaroring.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2576" data-file-height="1932" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Pedro_de_la_Rosa_Hungaroring.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Pedro_de_la_Rosa" title="Pedro de la Rosa">Pedro de la Rosa</a> scored the first (and only) podium finish of his career in second position.</div></div></div>
<p>Button won the race despite beginning in 14th place through relentless driving with de la Rosa scoring his first podium with second place, and Heidfeld getting <a href="/wiki/BMW_in_Formula_One" title="BMW in Formula One">BMW Sauber</a>'s first ever podium with 3rd.<sup id="cite_ref-buttonwin_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-buttonwin-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Debutant driver <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a> finished in seventh place and scored two points, but was later disqualified as his car was underweight due to excessive tire wear.<sup id="cite_ref-kubica_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kubica-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> This coincidentally meant that Michael Schumacher earned a point despite not finishing, as he was elevated to 8th place in the final results.<sup id="cite_ref-kubica_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kubica-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
