<p>The <b>1968 Canadian Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_Mont-Tremblant" title="Circuit Mont-Tremblant">Circuit Mont-Tremblant</a> in <a href="/wiki/Mont-Tremblant,_Quebec" class="mw-redirect" title="Mont-Tremblant, Quebec">St. Jovite</a>, <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">Quebec</a>, Canada on September 22, 1968. It was race 10 of 12 in both the <a href="/wiki/1968_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1968 World Championship of Drivers">1968 World Championship of Drivers</a> and the <a href="/wiki/1968_International_Cup_for_Formula_One_Manufacturers" class="mw-redirect" title="1968 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers">1968 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers</a>. The 90-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Denny_Hulme" title="Denny Hulme">Denny Hulme</a> after starting from sixth position. Hulme's teammate <a href="/wiki/Bruce_McLaren" title="Bruce McLaren">Bruce McLaren</a> finished second and <a href="/wiki/British_Racing_Motors" title="British Racing Motors">BRM</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Rodr%C3%ADguez_(racing_driver)" title="Pedro Rodríguez (racing driver)">Pedro Rodríguez</a> came in third.
</p><p>After the success of the <a href="/wiki/1967_Canadian_Grand_Prix" title="1967 Canadian Grand Prix">1967 Canadian Grand Prix</a>, the event was given a place on the 1968 calendar, but was moved from <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Tire_Motorsport_Park" title="Canadian Tire Motorsport Park">Mosport Park</a> to the Circuit Mont-Tremblant.<sup id="cite_ref-Grandprix.com_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Grandprix.com-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Attention centred on the battle for the Drivers' Championship, with <a href="/wiki/Graham_Hill" title="Graham Hill">Graham Hill</a> leading on 30 points, closely followed by <a href="/wiki/Jacky_Ickx" title="Jacky Ickx">Jacky Ickx</a> on 27, <a href="/wiki/Jackie_Stewart" title="Jackie Stewart">Jackie Stewart</a> on 26 and defending champion <a href="/wiki/Denny_Hulme" title="Denny Hulme">Denny Hulme</a> on 24.<sup id="cite_ref-Grandprix.com_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Grandprix.com-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1968_Canadian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Entry">Entry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1968_Canadian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Entry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A total of 22 cars were entered for this event, the first of three races in the Americas.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Dan_Gurney" title="Dan Gurney">Dan Gurney</a> was present in a third <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">Bruce McLaren Motor Racing</a> prepared <a href="/wiki/McLaren_M7A" title="McLaren M7A">McLaren M7A</a>, although entered by his <a href="/wiki/All_American_Racers" title="All American Racers">Anglo American Racers</a> team, having given up on his <a href="/wiki/Anglo_American_Racers" class="mw-redirect" title="Anglo American Racers">Eagle</a>-<a href="/wiki/Weslake" title="Weslake">Weslake</a> project. Despite this, there was an <a href="/wiki/Eagle_Mk1" title="Eagle Mk1">Eagle T1F</a> in Quebec, in the hands of local <a href="/wiki/1968_Continental_Championship_season" class="mw-redirect" title="1968 Continental Championship season">Formula A/5000</a> driver, <a href="/wiki/Al_Pease" title="Al Pease">Al Pease</a>. Another local driver from this series, <a href="/wiki/Bill_Brack" title="Bill Brack">Bill Brack</a>, appeared in <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Team Lotus</a>'s third car. Meanwhile, <a href="/wiki/British_Racing_Motors" title="British Racing Motors">BRM</a> ran only one car for <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Rodr%C3%ADguez_(racing_driver)" title="Pedro Rodríguez (racing driver)">Pedro Rodríguez</a>. <a href="/wiki/Equipe_Matra_Sports" title="Equipe Matra Sports">Matra Sports</a> expanded their operation to two cars, with <a href="/wiki/Henri_Pescarolo" title="Henri Pescarolo">Henri Pescarolo</a> joining <a href="/wiki/Jean-Pierre_Beltoise" title="Jean-Pierre Beltoise">Jean-Pierre Beltoise</a>, while <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Servoz-Gavin" title="Johnny Servoz-Gavin">Johnny Servoz-Gavin</a> drove a second car prepared by <a href="/wiki/Matra_International" class="mw-redirect" title="Matra International">Matra International</a>, following his second place at the previous race in <a href="/wiki/1968_Italian_Grand_Prix" title="1968 Italian Grand Prix">Italy</a>. Alongside <a href="/wiki/Jackie_Stewart" title="Jackie Stewart">Jackie Stewart</a>, this made four <a href="/wiki/Equipe_Matra_Sports" title="Equipe Matra Sports">Matras</a> in the field.<sup id="cite_ref-Grandprix.com_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Grandprix.com-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1968_Canadian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>With his <a href="/wiki/Repco" title="Repco">Repco</a> engine finally beginning to work, <a href="/wiki/Jochen_Rindt" title="Jochen Rindt">Jochen Rindt</a> secured <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Brabham" title="Brabham">Brabham Racing Organisation</a> in their <a href="/wiki/Brabham" title="Brabham">Brabham</a>-<a href="/wiki/Repco" title="Repco">Repco</a> BT26, with an average speed of 101.711&#160;mph. He was joined on the front row by <a href="/wiki/Chris_Amon" title="Chris Amon">Chris Amon</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_312" title="Ferrari 312">Ferrari 312</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jo_Siffert" title="Jo Siffert">Jo Siffert</a> in <a href="/wiki/Rob_Walker_Racing_Team" title="Rob Walker Racing Team">Rob Walker</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Lotus_49" title="Lotus 49">Lotus 49</a>B. The fastest McLaren was that of Dan Gurney, qualifying on the second row, alongside the works Lotus of <a href="/wiki/Graham_Hill" title="Graham Hill">Graham Hill</a>, while the third row featured both of the Bruce McLaren Motor Racing entered McLaren M7As of <a href="/wiki/Denny_Hulme" title="Denny Hulme">Denny Hulme</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bruce_McLaren" title="Bruce McLaren">Bruce McLaren</a>, sandwiching the sole <a href="/wiki/Honda_RA301" title="Honda RA301">Honda RA301</a> of <a href="/wiki/John_Surtees" title="John Surtees">John Surtees</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Grandprix.com_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Grandprix.com-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-racing-reference1_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-racing-reference1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During qualifying, <a href="/wiki/Jacky_Ickx" title="Jacky Ickx">Jacky Ickx</a>'s hopes of winning the World title ended when he crashed his Ferrari 312, after the throttle stuck open. As a result, Ickx suffered a broken leg.<sup id="cite_ref-Grandprix.com_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Grandprix.com-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1968_Canadian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The race was held over 90 laps of the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_Mont-Tremblant" title="Circuit Mont-Tremblant">Circuit Mont-Tremblant</a>, taking place in sunny conditions, with the Ferrari of Chris Amon leading straight from the off, with Jo Siffert chasing him. Then followed Jochen Rindt, Dan Gurney and Graham Hill. The positions at the front remained stable, with John Surtees retiring from eighth place with gearbox troubles. On lap 14, Hill managed pass Gurney, and 12 laps later, the American dropped away with a broken radiator. An oil leak accounted for Siffert, on lap 29, and so Rindt was second, although he too retired shortly afterwards with an engine failure. This promoted Hill to second. That too was short-lived, as he soon dropped behind the McLarens of Denny Hulme and Bruce McLaren because of a serious vibration issues. As Hill's Lotus 49B gradually fell back, he was overtaken by Pedro Rodríguez and Johnny Servoz-Gavin. A few laps later (lap 71), the Matra of Servoz-Gavin had spun out and Hill was back up to fifth.<sup id="cite_ref-Grandprix.com_2-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Grandprix.com-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-racing-reference1_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-racing-reference1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>While all this was going on, Amon seemed to have everything under control for the first 72 laps of the race, however, on the 73rd lap, his legendary bad luck struck when his Ferrari's transmission failed. This gave McLaren a 1-2 victory with Rodríguez grabbing third for BRM. Hulme won in a time of 2hr 27.11.2mins., averaging a speed of 97.799&#160;mph, and was a full lap ahead of his team-mate.<sup id="cite_ref-Grandprix.com_2-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Grandprix.com-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-racing-reference1_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-racing-reference1-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The result put reigning World Champion Hulme level on points with Hill, with two races remaining.<sup id="cite_ref-Grandprix.com_2-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Grandprix.com-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
