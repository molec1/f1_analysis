<p>The <b>1957 Pescara Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a>, held on 18 August 1957, at the <a href="/wiki/Pescara_Circuit" title="Pescara Circuit">Pescara Circuit</a> near <a href="/wiki/Pescara" title="Pescara">Pescara</a> in <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>. It was race 7 of 8 in the <a href="/wiki/1957_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1957 World Championship of Drivers">1957 World Championship of Drivers</a>. The race, which was the only Formula One World Championship race at the track, was held at the longest ever circuit to stage a Formula One World Championship Grand Prix:<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> the track is 25.8&#160;km (16 miles) long and is now part of the SR16bis on the coast of Pescara. It was also the first of the two consecutive Italian races, and after the subsequent <a href="/wiki/1957_Italian_Grand_Prix" title="1957 Italian Grand Prix">race at Monza</a> was complete, it became the first time that two Formula One World Championship races had been held in the same country in the same year.
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Pescara-F1-1957.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8e/Pescara-F1-1957.jpg/220px-Pescara-F1-1957.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="114" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8e/Pescara-F1-1957.jpg/330px-Pescara-F1-1957.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8e/Pescara-F1-1957.jpg/440px-Pescara-F1-1957.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1000" data-file-height="520" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Pescara-F1-1957.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Start of the Pescara Grand Prix</div></div></div>
<p>The event took place exceptionally due to the sudden cancellation of the <a href="/wiki/Belgian_Grand_Prix" title="Belgian Grand Prix">Belgium</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Grand_Prix" title="Dutch Grand Prix">Dutch Grand Prix</a>. The temporary public road circuit used for this race was located near the town of Pescara. The Pescara circuit was renowned at the time as one of the most difficult and dangerous routes for motor racing; it became popular because of the automobile race <a href="/wiki/Coppa_Acerbo" title="Coppa Acerbo">Coppa Acerbo</a>, held there since 1924.
</p><p>The circuit was so dangerous that <a href="/wiki/Enzo_Ferrari" title="Enzo Ferrari">Enzo Ferrari</a>, a man not known for compassion for his drivers, did not send his team to the race out of fear for his drivers' safety, coupled with safety fears following the <a href="/wiki/Mille_Miglia#The_end" title="Mille Miglia">Mille Miglia accident</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2015)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> However, he was persuaded by <a href="/wiki/Luigi_Musso" title="Luigi Musso">Luigi Musso</a> to enter a single <a href="/wiki/Lancia_D50" title="Lancia D50">Ferrari 801</a> as Musso wanted to secure the Italian drivers' championship<sup id="cite_ref-MS_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MS-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> (he would then retire on lap 10 with a split oil tank).<sup id="cite_ref-MS_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MS-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Furthermore, approval to run at the circuit was reluctantly granted by <a href="/wiki/ANAS" title="ANAS">ANAS</a> after eleven people had been killed in an <a href="/wiki/Mille_Miglia#The_end" title="Mille Miglia">accident</a> during the <i>Mille Miglia</i> race just three months before.
</p><p>A unique incident occurred when Jack Brabham's F2 Cooper was running short of fuel far from the pits and he pulled into a roadside petrol station and topped up.
</p><p>The race drew a crowd in excess of 200,000 spectators.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1957_Pescara_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>This was a race where some drivers qualified 20 seconds ahead of others. The biggest difference was pole sitter Juan Manuel Fangio's time of 9:44.6 compared to Jack Brabham's time of 11:35.2, almost 2 minutes off Fangio's time. This was because many of the drivers on the grid had never driven at Pescara before. Also, Brabham's Cooper was a Formula 2 car with a 1.5-litre engine and Pescara was a "power" circuit.</li></ul>
