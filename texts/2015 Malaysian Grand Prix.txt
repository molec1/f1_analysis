<p>The <b>2015 Malaysian Grand Prix</b>, formally titled the <b>2015 Formula 1 Petronas Malaysia Grand Prix</b>, was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Auto_racing" title="Auto racing">motor race</a> that was held on 29 March 2015 at the <a href="/wiki/Sepang_International_Circuit" title="Sepang International Circuit">Sepang International Circuit</a> in <a href="/wiki/Kuala_Lumpur" title="Kuala Lumpur">Kuala Lumpur</a>, <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-fom_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fom-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> It was the seventeenth running of the <a href="/wiki/Malaysian_Grand_Prix" title="Malaysian Grand Prix">Malaysian Grand Prix</a> as a World Championship event.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> came into the race leading the Drivers' championship by seven points over teammate <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a>, followed closely by <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a>, a further three points adrift. <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> entered with a twenty-eight-point lead over <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> in the Constructors' championship campaign.
</p><p>Hamilton secured pole position in a rain-soaked qualifying session, the 40th pole position in his career. Vettel won the race, having started from second place on the grid. It was the 40th victory of his career, and the first victory for Ferrari since the <a href="/wiki/2013_Spanish_Grand_Prix" title="2013 Spanish Grand Prix">2013 Spanish Grand Prix</a>.
This was the first time since the <a href="/wiki/2006_United_States_Grand_Prix" title="2006 United States Grand Prix">2006 United States Grand Prix</a> that neither McLaren was classified.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Malaysian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Malaysian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The race, held on 29 March 2015 with practice and qualifying sessions on the two preceding days,<sup id="cite_ref-fom_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fom-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> was the seventeenth running of the <a href="/wiki/Malaysian_Grand_Prix" title="Malaysian Grand Prix">Malaysian Grand Prix</a>, which had been part of the <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_World_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="Formula One World Championship">Formula One World Championship</a> without interruption since <a href="/wiki/1999_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1999 Formula One World Championship">1999</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> A race sanctioned by the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile</a>, motorsport's governing body, had been held in Malaysia since the 1960s, with the first editions being run in <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a>, then part of the Malaysian Federation, before moving to the <a href="/wiki/Shah_Alam_Circuit" title="Shah Alam Circuit">Shah Alam Circuit</a>. With the arrival of the Formula One Grand Prix in 1999, the race was moved to the purpose-built <a href="/wiki/Sepang_International_Circuit" title="Sepang International Circuit">Sepang International Circuit</a>, where all editions have since been held.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> For sponsorship reasons, the race's official name was <i>2015 Formula 1 Petronas Malaysia Grand Prix</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-fom_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fom-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Going into the weekend, <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> was leading the Drivers' Championship, having won the first race ahead of his teammate <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a>. He therefore had 25 points and held a seven-point advantage over Rosberg, with Vettel another three points behind.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> In the constructors' standings, <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> was on top, haven taken the maximum available 43 points from their 1–2 finish in <a href="/wiki/2015_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="2015 Australian Grand Prix">Melbourne</a>. <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> were second on 15 points, followed by <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a> with 14.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Lewis_Hamilton_2015_Malaysia_FP3_1.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b3/Lewis_Hamilton_2015_Malaysia_FP3_1.jpg/220px-Lewis_Hamilton_2015_Malaysia_FP3_1.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b3/Lewis_Hamilton_2015_Malaysia_FP3_1.jpg/330px-Lewis_Hamilton_2015_Malaysia_FP3_1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b3/Lewis_Hamilton_2015_Malaysia_FP3_1.jpg/440px-Lewis_Hamilton_2015_Malaysia_FP3_1.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1600" data-file-height="1066" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Lewis_Hamilton_2015_Malaysia_FP3_1.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The dominance of <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> (pictured during practice) was a major talking point before the race.</div></div></div>
<p>Mercedes arrived at the second race of the season with maximum points from <a href="/wiki/2015_Australian_Grand_Prix" title="2015 Australian Grand Prix">Australia</a>, having finished first and second with third placed Sebastian Vettel half a minute down. Following their dominant performance, many commentators voiced fears that the sport might become even more one-sided and boring over the course of the 2015 season than it had been the previous year.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a>'s team principal <a href="/wiki/Christian_Horner" title="Christian Horner">Christian Horner</a> called for the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">FIA</a> to step in and apply rule changes that would level the competition, stating: "The FIA, within the rules, have an equalisation mechanism and that needs to be looked at."<sup id="cite_ref-15_March_2015_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15_March_2015-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Red Bull subsequently threatened to leave the sport, should no measures be taken.<sup id="cite_ref-telegraph1_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-telegraph1-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> While many commentators pointed out that Red Bull had enjoyed their own share of dominance in the prior years,<sup id="cite_ref-telegraph1_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-telegraph1-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Formula One supremo <a href="/wiki/Bernie_Ecclestone" title="Bernie Ecclestone">Bernie Ecclestone</a> voiced understanding for the team, saying: "There is a rule that I think [former president] <a href="/wiki/Max_Mosley" title="Max Mosley">Max</a> [Mosley] put in when he was there that in the event... that a particular team or engine supplier did something magic - which Mercedes have done - the FIA can level up things. They [Mercedes] have done a first class job which everybody acknowledges. We need to change things a little bit now and try to level things up a little bit."<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Mercedes' executive director <a href="/wiki/Toto_Wolff" title="Toto Wolff">Toto Wolff</a> reacted to the calls by urging his rivals to "get your fucking head down and work to sort it out".<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> also raised doubt over the practicality of rule changes, saying "There's nothing really to ban because it doesn't look like Mercedes are doing anything other teams aren't doing."<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Following criticism voiced by Red Bull, the future of engine supplier <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a> was also a matter of debate. While Renault contemplated exiting the sport, another scenario that received media attention was a possible buy-out of the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso</a> team.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> The discussion about the future of the sport was additionally fuelled by the news that the <a href="/wiki/German_Grand_Prix" title="German Grand Prix">German Grand Prix</a> would be dropped from the 2015 calendar, due to a lack of financing.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> The exclusion of one of motorsport's most traditional events caused many to raise the question whether the same fate might await other traditional venues such as <a href="/wiki/Autodromo_Nazionale_Monza" class="mw-redirect" title="Autodromo Nazionale Monza">Monza</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> returned from his pre-season injury to take back his seat at McLaren, which had been filled by <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Magnussen" title="Kevin Magnussen">Kevin Magnussen</a> at the previous race in Australia.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Also returning was <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a>, who missed the previous race due to a back injury sustained during qualifying.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Marussia_F1" title="Marussia F1">Manor Marussia</a>, who were able to field their cars for the first time in Malaysia,<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> were under criticism after they failed to run their cars at the Australian Grand Prix, with Ecclestone saying they would need to pay for their travel expenses, which are usually covered by the commercial rights holder.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As in the previous two years, <a href="/wiki/Pirelli" title="Pirelli">Pirelli</a> announced they would be supplying teams with the orange-banded hard compound tyre as the prime selection and the white-banded medium compound as the option selection for the event. The event also made use of Pirelli's two wet weather tyres, the green-banded intermediate and blue-banded full wet tyres.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Free_practice">Free practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Malaysian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Free practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Fernando_Alonso_2015_Malaysia_FP1.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b7/Fernando_Alonso_2015_Malaysia_FP1.jpg/220px-Fernando_Alonso_2015_Malaysia_FP1.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="110" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b7/Fernando_Alonso_2015_Malaysia_FP1.jpg/330px-Fernando_Alonso_2015_Malaysia_FP1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b7/Fernando_Alonso_2015_Malaysia_FP1.jpg/440px-Fernando_Alonso_2015_Malaysia_FP1.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2560" data-file-height="1280" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Fernando_Alonso_2015_Malaysia_FP1.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> returned to the grid after missing the first race in Australia.</div></div></div>
<p>Per the regulations for the 2015 season, three practice sessions were held, two 90-minute sessions on Friday and another one-hour session before qualifying on Saturday.<sup id="cite_ref-pracreg_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pracreg-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Nico Rosberg set the fastest time in the first practice session on Friday morning, while championship leader Lewis Hamilton was sidelined due to engine problems.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> He recovered to take fastest time in the second session of the day, while Ferrari proved that they would be closer to their rivals in Sepang, coming within 0.4 seconds in both free practice sessions.<sup id="cite_ref-fp2_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fp2-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Fernando Alonso made his debut in the new McLaren, finishing 14th and 16th in the two Friday sessions respectively, outpacing teammate Jenson Button both times.<sup id="cite_ref-fp2_31-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fp2-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Also debuting during free practice were Manor-Marussia, with both cars struggling to meet the time limit of <a href="/wiki/107%25_rule" title="107% rule">107%</a>. <a href="/wiki/Raffaele_Marciello" title="Raffaele Marciello">Raffaele Marciello</a> filled in for <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a>-regular <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Nasr" title="Felipe Nasr">Felipe Nasr</a> during the first session.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During the third practice session on Saturday morning, both Mercedes drivers were once again fastest, with Rosberg setting the fastest time, while Ferrari was about half a second behind.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Malaysian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Max_Verstappen_2015_Malaysia_Q2.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/db/Max_Verstappen_2015_Malaysia_Q2.jpg/220px-Max_Verstappen_2015_Malaysia_Q2.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="110" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/db/Max_Verstappen_2015_Malaysia_Q2.jpg/330px-Max_Verstappen_2015_Malaysia_Q2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/db/Max_Verstappen_2015_Malaysia_Q2.jpg/440px-Max_Verstappen_2015_Malaysia_Q2.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2560" data-file-height="1280" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Max_Verstappen_2015_Malaysia_Q2.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a>, pictured here during the wet qualifying session, became the youngest driver to score points in Formula One.</div></div></div>
<p>Qualifying consisted of three parts, 18, 15 and 12 minutes in length respectively, with five drivers eliminated from competing after each of the first two sessions.<sup id="cite_ref-pracreg_29-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pracreg-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> The first part of qualifying (Q1) got under way in the dry and sunny conditions; this was not the conditions that would favour McLaren as they were still off the pace of the midfield although they made progress. Jenson Button along with his returning teammate Fernando Alonso were separated by only a tenth of a second, but were 17th and 18th on the grid. Joining them were the Manor-Marussia cars of <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Merhi" title="Roberto Merhi">Roberto Merhi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Will_Stevens" title="Will Stevens">Will Stevens</a>; Merhi recorded a time that was almost five seconds slower than Alonso, and Stevens failed to take to the circuit, while Felipe Nasr also failed to make it out of Q1, as he struggled for balance in his Sauber.<sup id="cite_ref-f1fanqual_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1fanqual-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Clouds built up at the west of the circuit before Q2 and there was a traffic jam in the pit lane. A scramble for position started to be evident and only one driver&#160;– Nico Rosberg&#160;– managed a clean lap without traffic, as the rest of the field dealt with the thunderstorm that was warned to arrive during Q2 and started tripping up on each other. The biggest casualty of the session was Ferrari's <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> who got held up by <a href="/wiki/Marcus_Ericsson" title="Marcus Ericsson">Marcus Ericsson</a>. Räikkönen recorded a lap of 1:42.173, which was not fast enough to get into Q3 as rain started soon after. Joining Räikkönen were <a href="/wiki/Pastor_Maldonado" title="Pastor Maldonado">Pastor Maldonado</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force Indias</a> of <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a> and the Toro Rosso of <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Sainz_Jr." title="Carlos Sainz Jr.">Carlos Sainz Jr.</a>, as they could not improve on their original lap times due to the conditions.<sup id="cite_ref-f1fanqual_35-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1fanqual-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After a delay of half an hour, the cars returned to the circuit, with the majority of the remaining ten drivers on intermediate tyres, while <a href="/wiki/Romain_Grosjean" title="Romain Grosjean">Romain Grosjean</a>, <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> and Valtteri Bottas initially surveyed the circuit on wets before switching to the intermediates. Ultimately, Hamilton took <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> by 0.074 seconds ahead of Vettel, with Rosberg third. They were followed by the Red Bull cars of <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Daniil_Kvyat" title="Daniil Kvyat">Daniil Kvyat</a>, while Toro Rosso's young driver <a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a> took sixth place. The rest of the top ten was completed by Massa, Grosjean, Bottas and Ericsson.<sup id="cite_ref-f1fanqual_35-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1fanqual-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> It was the 40th pole position of Hamilton's career.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> After qualifying, Romain Grosjean was found to have left the pit lane in a different position to the one he left the garage, and so he received a two place grid penalty dropping him to tenth on the grid.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Malaysian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Safety_Car_with_Sebastian_Vettel_2015_Malaysia.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6d/Safety_Car_with_Sebastian_Vettel_2015_Malaysia.jpg/220px-Safety_Car_with_Sebastian_Vettel_2015_Malaysia.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="330" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6d/Safety_Car_with_Sebastian_Vettel_2015_Malaysia.jpg/330px-Safety_Car_with_Sebastian_Vettel_2015_Malaysia.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6d/Safety_Car_with_Sebastian_Vettel_2015_Malaysia.jpg/440px-Safety_Car_with_Sebastian_Vettel_2015_Malaysia.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1067" data-file-height="1600" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Safety_Car_with_Sebastian_Vettel_2015_Malaysia.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>An early safety car phase proved to be a decisive factor in the race's outcome.</div></div></div>
<p>Temperatures on race day were very hot, with the track temperature at 61&#160;°C (142&#160;°F). Rain was possible, though less than in previous years, also due to the earlier starting time.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> Williams were hoping for a dry race, since qualifying showed they were still off the pace in wet conditions.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> Will Stevens, who missed qualifying due to a fuel system problem, was ultimately unable to participate in the race.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> Approximately 44,611 people attended the race.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At the start, Lewis Hamilton got away well, while Nico Rosberg challenged Sebastian Vettel for second place, but the Ferrari was able to come out on top. Both Pastor Maldonado and Kimi Räikkönen suffered punctures on lap one after contact with Valtteri Bottas and Felipe Nasr respectively<sup id="cite_ref-winlose_44-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-winlose-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> and had to come in for a new set of tyres. Vettel was able to stay with the Mercedes of Hamilton over the first few laps. On lap four, Marcus Ericsson braked too late and spun in turn one, retiring from the race and bringing out the <a href="/wiki/Safety_car" title="Safety car">safety car</a>. All but seven cars went into the pits to change tyres, including both Mercedes drivers, while Sebastian Vettel stayed out. When racing resumed on lap seven, Vettel led Nico Hülkenberg, Romain Grosjean, Carlos Sainz Jr. and Sergio Pérez, with Hamilton the first of the cars who made pit stops right behind.<sup id="cite_ref-ticker_46-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ticker-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Both Mercedes cars now made their way through the field, with Hamilton overtaking Hülkenberg for second position on the end of lap ten, now ten seconds down on race leader Vettel. Rosberg reached third position on lap 14, while Hamilton was unable to make significant gains on Vettel in front. The cars that had not used the safety car phase for their stops began to change tyres on lap 16, while Maldonado was handed a ten-second penalty for exceeding the safety car time. Meanwhile, the Red Bull cars showed signs of brake problems with brake dust exiting the wheels at the major braking points on both cars. Vettel came in for his first stop on lap 18, handing the lead back to Hamilton. Vettel rejoined in third place and began to close rapidly on second-placed Rosberg. Vettel made good use of his fresher tyres, overtaking Rosberg on lap 22 and setting out to do the same on Hamilton, shortly before the latter went into the pits for his second pit stop on lap 24. Meanwhile, Fernando Alonso retired due to engine problems on lap 21 while running in ninth place. With deteriorating front wing damage, Daniel Ricciardo let his teammate Daniil Kvyat through into eleventh place on the beginning of lap 26,<sup id="cite_ref-ticker_46-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ticker-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> only for Kvyat to be sent in a spin by Hülkenberg, who ran wide and hit the Red Bull in turn two. Both drivers were able to continue.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> The other Force India of Pérez sent Romain Grosjean's <a href="/wiki/Lotus_F1" title="Lotus F1">Lotus</a> into a spin five laps later. Both Force India drivers were handed a ten-second penalty for their actions.<sup id="cite_ref-ticker_46-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ticker-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Red_Bull_duo_2015_Malaysia_Race_1.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/86/Red_Bull_duo_2015_Malaysia_Race_1.jpg/220px-Red_Bull_duo_2015_Malaysia_Race_1.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/86/Red_Bull_duo_2015_Malaysia_Race_1.jpg/330px-Red_Bull_duo_2015_Malaysia_Race_1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/86/Red_Bull_duo_2015_Malaysia_Race_1.jpg/440px-Red_Bull_duo_2015_Malaysia_Race_1.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1600" data-file-height="1067" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Red_Bull_duo_2015_Malaysia_Race_1.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Both <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bulls</a> suffered from brake issues. Brake dust can be seen here blowing out from the brake discs.</div></div></div>
<p>Vettel made a pit stop for a second and final time on lap 38, handing the lead back to Hamilton, who came in for his final stop a lap later, emerging about twelve seconds behind Vettel in third place. His teammate Rosberg made a pit stop on lap 41, elevating Hamilton to second, who now started to close on Vettel. Jenson Button retired from the race on lap 40 due to a turbo failure,<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> while Maldonado's bad day turned worse when he had to park his car suffering brake problems.<sup id="cite_ref-winlose_44-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-winlose-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton was unable to overcome the gap between himself and Vettel, leaving Vettel to take his 40th victory, the first since the <a href="/wiki/2013_Brazilian_Grand_Prix" title="2013 Brazilian Grand Prix">2013 Brazilian Grand Prix</a> and his first with Ferrari.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> Bottas was able to overtake his teammate Felipe Massa on the final lap, after both Williams drivers had an uneventful race to finish fifth and sixth respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-ticker_46-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ticker-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> It was Ferrari's first win since the <a href="/wiki/2013_Spanish_Grand_Prix" title="2013 Spanish Grand Prix">2013 Spanish Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2015_Malaysian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Podium_2015_Malaysia_2.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f1/Podium_2015_Malaysia_2.jpg/220px-Podium_2015_Malaysia_2.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f1/Podium_2015_Malaysia_2.jpg/330px-Podium_2015_Malaysia_2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f1/Podium_2015_Malaysia_2.jpg/440px-Podium_2015_Malaysia_2.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2560" data-file-height="1707" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Podium_2015_Malaysia_2.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The podium after the race</div></div></div>
<p>Sebastian Vettel and Ferrari reacted emotionally to the victory, with Vettel unable to hold back tears on the podium.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup> Speaking about the experience as a Ferrari driver, he told <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Jordan" title="Eddie Jordan">Eddie Jordan</a> during the podium interview:
</p>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r960796168">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>I remember when the gate opened in Maranello it was like a dream coming true. I remember the last time I was there was as a young kid watching <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael [Schumacher]</a> over the fence driving around in the Ferrari and now I'm driving that very red car. It's incredible. The day today, the race, really spot on, the whole team was there, great strategy, great pace, we beat them fair and square [...].<sup id="cite_ref-fia_53-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fia-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>
<p>Second placed Lewis Hamilton conceded that Ferrari and Vettel "did a fantastic job this weekend" and that he had not expected them to be as quick as they were.<sup id="cite_ref-fia_53-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fia-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Ferrari team shared Vettel's enthusiasm, as they could be seen singing along to the Italian national anthem during the podium ceremony, led by team principal <a href="/wiki/Maurizio_Arrivabene" title="Maurizio Arrivabene">Maurizio Arrivabene</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup> He later admitted he cried after receiving a congratulatory text message from Michael Schumacher's manager on behalf of his family.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup> Comparisons between Vettel and Schumacher were omnipresent in the following days, with commentators pointing out that it took Vettel merely two races for his first win with the Scuderia, while Schumacher achieved a victory only in his <a href="/wiki/1996_Spanish_Grand_Prix" title="1996 Spanish Grand Prix">seventh race</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-senna_56-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-senna-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> During the post-race press conference, Vettel expressed hope to emulate Schumacher's success at Ferrari and win the world championship.<sup id="cite_ref-fia_53-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fia-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The result was viewed as a welcome rebuttal of the fears of monotony voiced after the Australian Grand Prix. German newspaper <i><a href="/wiki/S%C3%BCddeutsche_Zeitung" title="Süddeutsche Zeitung">Süddeutsche Zeitung</a></i> declared Vettel's win was "the best the flagging sport could have hoped for".<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>Controversy arose when Force India's Bob Fernley accused Manor Marussia of intentionally only running one car during qualifying and the race and urged the FIA to investigate the matter.<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup> Manor rejected these claims, with sporting director <a href="/wiki/Graeme_Lowdon" title="Graeme Lowdon">Graeme Lowdon</a> saying "I can guarantee you if that car could have moved, it would have raced".<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Finishing seventh, Max Verstappen became the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_driver_records#Youngest_drivers_to_score_points" title="List of Formula One driver records">youngest driver to score World Championship points</a>. Verstappen was quoted saying that it had been a "good day", while his father, former Formula One driver <a href="/wiki/Jos_Verstappen" title="Jos Verstappen">Jos Verstappen</a>, commented: "I'm very pleased for what he has done today. I'm happy that he finished the race and the way he raced, he deserved it. I'm very proud."<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As a result of the race, Vettel moved within three points of Hamilton in the Drivers' Championship, on 43 to 40 points. Rosberg followed in third with 33 points, 13 ahead of <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup> In the Constructors' Championship, Mercedes retained their lead at now 76 points, 24 clear of Ferrari, with <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> following in third on 30 points.<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
