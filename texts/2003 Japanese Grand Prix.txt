<p>The <b>2003 Japanese Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>XXIX Fuji Television Japanese Grand Prix</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 12 October 2003 at the <a href="/wiki/Suzuka_Circuit" class="mw-redirect" title="Suzuka Circuit">Suzuka Circuit</a>, <a href="/wiki/Suzuka,_Mie" title="Suzuka, Mie">Suzuka</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mie_Prefecture" title="Mie Prefecture">Mie</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. It was the sixteenth and final round of the <a href="/wiki/2003_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2003 Formula One season">2003 Formula One season</a> as well as the 29th <a href="/wiki/Japanese_Grand_Prix" title="Japanese Grand Prix">Japanese Grand Prix</a>. The 53-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> driving for <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> after starting from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>. <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a>, who started the race from eighth position, finished second in a <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> car, with <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> third in the other McLaren.
</p><p>Barrichello's win saw Ferrari clinch their 13th Constructors Championship title, the team's fifth title in a row, with Barrichello's team-mate <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> finishing eighth to secure his record-breaking 6th World Driver's Championship surpassing the record set by <a href="/wiki/Juan_Manuel_Fangio" title="Juan Manuel Fangio">Juan Manuel Fangio</a> in 1957. This was also Schumacher's fourth consecutive World Driver's title.<sup id="cite_ref-grandprixcomracenotes_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-grandprixcomracenotes-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2003_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2003_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Heading into the final race of the season, <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> was leading the World Driver's Championship standings with 92 points; <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> was second on 83 points, 9 points behind Schumacher. A maximum of 10 points were available, which meant that Räikkönen could still win the title. Schumacher only needed an eighth-place finish to become Driver's Champion even if Räikkönen won the race. Räikkönen needed to win and Schumacher not to score a single point in order for him to become Driver's Champion.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>There was one driver change heading into the race. Having been a driver for the <a href="/wiki/British_American_Racing" title="British American Racing">BAR-Honda</a> team for most of the season, <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a> pulled out of the Grand Prix after asking to be released by the team and was replaced by the team's test driver <a href="/wiki/Takuma_Sato" title="Takuma Sato">Takuma Sato</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Sato had been confirmed as a driver for the team for the 2004 season in the days running up to the race and would drive alongside <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notes">Notes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2003_Japanese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Notes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Takuma_Sato" title="Takuma Sato">Takuma Sato</a> finished 6th in his one and only race of the season.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> spun 3 times in this race, once with his brother Michael.</li>
<li>This was the last time to date that the <a href="/wiki/Japanese_Grand_Prix" title="Japanese Grand Prix">Japanese Grand Prix</a> was the final round of the season.</li>
<li>Last race for <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jos_Verstappen" title="Jos Verstappen">Jos Verstappen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Justin_Wilson_(racing_driver)" title="Justin Wilson (racing driver)">Justin Wilson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nicolas_Kiesa" title="Nicolas Kiesa">Nicolas Kiesa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ralph_Firman" title="Ralph Firman">Ralph Firman</a>.</li>
<li>This was <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a>'s final podium until the <a href="/wiki/2006_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="2006 Monaco Grand Prix">2006 Monaco Grand Prix</a>.</li></ul>
