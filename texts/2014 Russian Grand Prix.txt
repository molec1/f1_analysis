<p>The <b>2014 Russian Grand Prix</b> (formally known as the <b>2014 Formula 1 Russian Grand Prix</b>; Russian: <span lang="ru">Гран-при России 2014 года</span>, <small>romanized:&#160;</small><i lang="ru-Latn" title="Russian-language romanization">Gran-pri Rossii 2014 goda</i>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Auto_racing" title="Auto racing">motor race</a> held on 12 October 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The fifty-three lap race was held at the <a href="/wiki/Sochi_Autodrom" title="Sochi Autodrom">Sochi Autodrom</a>, a brand new circuit built on the site of the <a href="/wiki/2014_Winter_Olympics" title="2014 Winter Olympics">2014 Winter Olympics</a> in the city of <a href="/wiki/Sochi" title="Sochi">Sochi</a> in <a href="/wiki/Krasnodar_Krai" title="Krasnodar Krai">Krasnodar Krai</a>, <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a>.
</p><p>The race was the sixteenth round of the <a href="/wiki/2014_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2014 Formula One season">2014 season</a>, following on from the <a href="/wiki/2014_Japanese_Grand_Prix" title="2014 Japanese Grand Prix">Japanese Grand Prix</a> at <a href="/wiki/Suzuka_Circuit" class="mw-redirect" title="Suzuka Circuit">Suzuka</a> one week previously, and preceding the <a href="/wiki/2014_United_States_Grand_Prix" title="2014 United States Grand Prix">United States Grand Prix</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_of_the_Americas" title="Circuit of the Americas">Circuit of the Americas</a>. The race marked the first time that the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Grand_Prix" title="Russian Grand Prix">Russian Grand Prix</a> had been held in <a href="/wiki/1914_Grand_Prix_season" title="1914 Grand Prix season">a century</a>, and was also the first time the Russian Grand Prix was run as a round of the Formula One World Championship since the championship was formed in <a href="/wiki/1950_Formula_One_season" title="1950 Formula One season">1950</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> in a <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> won the race after starting from pole position and leading every lap. His teammate <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> finished second, after working his way up from the back of field after having to make an unscheduled pit stop on the first lap. <a href="/wiki/Williams_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams F1">Williams</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a> completed the podium, having set the fastest lap&#8212;and a new lap record&#8212;on the final lap of the race. Following <a href="/wiki/Jules_Bianchi" title="Jules Bianchi">Jules Bianchi</a>'s serious accident in the <a href="/wiki/2014_Japanese_Grand_Prix" title="2014 Japanese Grand Prix">Japanese Grand Prix</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marussia_F1" title="Marussia F1">Marussia</a> entered a single car for <a href="/wiki/Max_Chilton" title="Max Chilton">Max Chilton</a>, leaving the grid with twenty-one cars. The race ultimately proved to be Marussia's last of the season, as the team went into <a href="/wiki/Administration_(law)" title="Administration (law)">administration</a> ahead of <a href="/wiki/2014_United_States_Grand_Prix" title="2014 United States Grand Prix">the next race</a> in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-Marussia_return_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Marussia_return-8">&#91;N 1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The result secured the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">World Constructors' Championship</a> for <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> with three races remaining in the season, while Hamilton extended his <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">World Drivers' Championship</a> lead over Rosberg to seventeen points. Bottas's podium allowed him to overtake <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> in the drivers' standings.
</p>

<p>With <a href="/wiki/Sochi_Autodrom" title="Sochi Autodrom">the circuit</a> being built on the site of the <a href="/wiki/2014_Winter_Olympics" title="2014 Winter Olympics">2014 Winter Olympics</a>, the <a href="/wiki/International_Olympic_Committee" title="International Olympic Committee">International Olympic Committee</a> voiced concerns that construction would interrupt preparations for the Olympics, and so were given the power to postpone the inaugural Russian Grand Prix until the <a href="/wiki/2015_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2015 Formula One season">2015 season</a> if preparations for the race interfered with the Winter Olympics.<sup id="cite_ref-IOC_warns_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IOC_warns-10">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> However, the Olympic Games started without interruption, and the IOC did not exercise their power. FIA Race Director <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Whiting" title="Charlie Whiting">Charlie Whiting</a> inspected the circuit in the week before the <a href="/wiki/2014_Belgian_Grand_Prix" title="2014 Belgian Grand Prix">2014 Belgian Grand Prix</a> and gave it the FIA's final seal of approval, allowing the race to go ahead.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In September 2014, the circuit hosted a round of the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Touring_Car_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="Russian Touring Car Championship">Russian Touring Car Championship</a> as preparation for the Grand Prix.<sup id="cite_ref-russian_touring_cars_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-russian_touring_cars-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversy">Controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2014_Russian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Vladimir_Putin_and_Bernie_Ecclestone_46776.jpeg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/03/Vladimir_Putin_and_Bernie_Ecclestone_46776.jpeg/220px-Vladimir_Putin_and_Bernie_Ecclestone_46776.jpeg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/03/Vladimir_Putin_and_Bernie_Ecclestone_46776.jpeg/330px-Vladimir_Putin_and_Bernie_Ecclestone_46776.jpeg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/03/Vladimir_Putin_and_Bernie_Ecclestone_46776.jpeg/440px-Vladimir_Putin_and_Bernie_Ecclestone_46776.jpeg 2x" data-file-width="650" data-file-height="434" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Vladimir_Putin_and_Bernie_Ecclestone_46776.jpeg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Russian President <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Putin" title="Vladimir Putin">Vladimir Putin</a> (foreground, left) and <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_Administration" class="mw-redirect" title="Formula One Administration">Formula One Administration</a> CEO <a href="/wiki/Bernie_Ecclestone" title="Bernie Ecclestone">Bernie Ecclestone</a> (foreground, right), spectating during the Grand Prix.</div></div></div>
<p>Following the shooting down of <a href="/wiki/Malaysia_Airlines_Flight_17" title="Malaysia Airlines Flight 17">Malaysia Airlines Flight 17</a> over eastern <a href="/wiki/Ukraine" title="Ukraine">Ukraine</a> in July 2014 and amid allegations of Russian involvement in the incident and the <a href="/wiki/2014_Russian_military_intervention_in_Ukraine" class="mw-redirect" title="2014 Russian military intervention in Ukraine">Russian military intervention in the country</a>, there were calls from the British <a href="/wiki/Conservative_Party_(UK)" title="Conservative Party (UK)">Conservative Party</a> for Formula One to abandon the race as part of sanctions placed on the Russian government,<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> as the race was established with financial support from the government.<sup id="cite_ref-Russia_in_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Russia_in-14">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Similar suggestions were made from British and German parliamentarians over Russia's hosting of the <a href="/wiki/2018_FIFA_World_Cup" title="2018 FIFA World Cup">2018 FIFA World Cup</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>When responding to questions about the race—and humanitarian concerns over the revival of the <a href="/wiki/European_Grand_Prix" title="European Grand Prix">European Grand Prix</a> in <a href="/wiki/Azerbaijan" title="Azerbaijan">Azerbaijan</a> given <a href="/wiki/Human_rights_in_Azerbaijan" title="Human rights in Azerbaijan">Azerbaijan's human rights record</a>—<a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a> team principal <a href="/wiki/Christian_Horner" title="Christian Horner">Christian Horner</a> refused to offer any kind of judgement, pointing out that it was the responsibility of the <a href="/wiki/FIA" class="mw-redirect" title="FIA">FIA</a> to monitor the situation and act accordingly;<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> a position supported by <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> director <a href="/wiki/Toto_Wolff" title="Toto Wolff">Toto Wolff</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Sirotkin_FP1_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sirotkin_FP1-18">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Bernie_Ecclestone" title="Bernie Ecclestone">Bernie Ecclestone</a>, CEO of <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_Administration" class="mw-redirect" title="Formula One Administration">the sport's commercial rights holder</a>, publicly stated that he had "no doubts" about the race taking place in the wake of the crash,<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> and distanced the sport from ongoing political debates.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> With Russia facing increased economic sanctions from the European Union and United States,<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> deputy Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Dmitry_Kozak" title="Dmitry Kozak">Dmitry Kozak</a> noted that the Grand Prix would not be affected in the event such sanctions were implemented,<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> and the race went ahead as scheduled. Both <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Putin" title="Vladimir Putin">Vladimir Putin</a> and Dmitry Kozak were in attendance, with Putin presenting the race winner's trophy.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Support_events">Support events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2014_Russian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Support events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/2014_Sochi_GP2_and_GP3_Series_rounds" title="2014 Sochi GP2 and GP3 Series rounds">2014 Sochi GP2 Series round</a> and <a href="/wiki/2014_Sochi_GP2_and_GP3_Series_rounds#GP3_Series_classificstion" title="2014 Sochi GP2 and GP3 Series rounds">2014 Sochi GP3 Series round</a></div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/GP2_Series" title="GP2 Series">GP2</a> and <a href="/wiki/GP3_Series" title="GP3 Series">GP3 Series</a>—a pair of feeder championships for drivers preparing for Formula One—also made <a href="/wiki/2014_Sochi_GP2_and_GP3_Series_rounds" title="2014 Sochi GP2 and GP3 Series rounds">their début</a> at the circuit, racing in support of the Grand Prix as the penultimate round of the <a href="/wiki/2014_GP2_Series_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2014 GP2 Series season">2014 GP2</a> and <a href="/wiki/2014_GP3_Series_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2014 GP3 Series season">GP3 championships</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-GP2_calendar_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GP2_calendar-25">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2014_Russian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-event">Pre-event</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2014_Russian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Pre-event">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Marussia_entry">Marussia entry</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2014_Russian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Marussia entry">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p><a href="/wiki/Marussia_F1" title="Marussia F1">Marussia</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Jules_Bianchi" title="Jules Bianchi">Jules Bianchi</a> suffered <a href="/wiki/Diffuse_axonal_injury" title="Diffuse axonal injury">serious head injuries</a> at the <a href="/wiki/2014_Japanese_Grand_Prix" title="2014 Japanese Grand Prix">Japanese Grand Prix</a> when he crashed into a tractor trying to recover <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a> under yellow-flag conditions, ruling him out of the Russian Grand Prix.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Per FIA regulations, the team entered two cars, with <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Rossi_(racing_driver)" class="mw-redirect" title="Alexander Rossi (racing driver)">Alexander Rossi</a>, their testing and reserve driver, being named as Bianchi's replacement for the event.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>  However, before Friday's practice sessions and with FIA approval, the team opted not to run the second <a href="/wiki/Marussia_MR03" title="Marussia MR03">Marussia MR03</a> car and <a href="/wiki/Max_Chilton" title="Max Chilton">Max Chilton</a> was their sole representative, leaving the grid with twenty-one cars.  As a sign of respect to Bianchi and his family, the team built up and placed his car in his side of the garage and had his livery placed on the walls for the duration of the weekend.<sup id="cite_ref-marussia_1_car_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-marussia_1_car-29">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h5><span class="mw-headline" id="Tributes_to_Jules_Bianchi">Tributes to Jules Bianchi</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2014_Russian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Tributes to Jules Bianchi">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h5>
<p>There were several tributes at this Grand Prix to show support for Bianchi:<sup id="cite_ref-Bianchi_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bianchi-31">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Reliable_sources/Perennial_sources" title="Wikipedia:Reliable sources/Perennial sources"><span title="The material near this tag may rely on an unreliable source (November 2019)">deprecated source</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<ul><li>Marussia adopted a "#JB17" livery on the cockpit sides of its sole participating car;</li>
<li>Drivers wore a sticker on their helmets saying "<i><i lang="fr" title="French language text">Tous avec Jules</i> #17</i>",<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> and held a one-minute silence just before the race.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Penalties">Penalties</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2014_Russian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Penalties">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p><a href="/wiki/Pastor_Maldonado" title="Pastor Maldonado">Pastor Maldonado</a> incurred a ten-place penalty at the <a href="/wiki/2014_Japanese_Grand_Prix" title="2014 Japanese Grand Prix">Japanese Grand Prix</a> for exceeding his quota of five engine components for the season.<sup id="cite_ref-MAL_pen_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MAL_pen-35">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> However, as he qualified seventeenth,<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> he was unable to take the full penalty as doing so would move him past twenty-second and last place on the grid. Under new rules introduced for the <a href="/wiki/2014_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2014 Formula One season">2014 season</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-further_changes_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-further_changes-38">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> the remainder of the penalty was carried over to the Russian Grand Prix, automatically giving him a five-place penalty.<sup id="cite_ref-MAL_HUL_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MAL_HUL-39">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Tyres">Tyres</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2014_Russian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Tyres">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Tyre supplier <a href="/wiki/Pirelli" title="Pirelli">Pirelli</a> announced that they would be providing teams with their medium-compound tyre as a "prime" tyre and the soft-compound as the "option" tyre for the Grand Prix to cope with the brand-new surface and to tolerate the high lateral loads placed on the tyre, particularly through turn 4,<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-200kph_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-200kph-41">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> an elongated constant-radius corner with estimated speeds of 200&#160;km/h (120&#160;mph) that stands out as the longest corner on the 2014 Formula One calendar.<sup id="cite_ref-200kph_41-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-200kph-41">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Drag_reduction_system">Drag reduction system</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2014_Russian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Drag reduction system">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Two <a href="/wiki/Drag_reduction_system" title="Drag reduction system">drag reduction system</a> (DRS) zones were introduced for the race. The detection point for the first was located on the entry to turn 1, with the activation zone placed on the apex of the corner. The second detection point was positioned along the circuit's back straight, with the activation zone encompassing turns 12 and 13.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Free_practice">Free practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2014_Russian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Free practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In accordance with the  <a href="/wiki/2014_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2014 Formula One World Championship">2014</a> regulations, three free practice sessions were held. Two one and a half-hour sessions on Friday and another one-hour session on Saturday morning. Per the regulations the teams were allowed to replace one of their regular drivers with a test and reserve driver during the first two sessions.<sup id="cite_ref-pracreg_43-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pracreg-43">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup>
<a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> set the fastest time in the first free practice session, seven hundredths of a second ahead of teammate <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>, and two tenths of a second faster than <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-fp1_times_44-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fp1_times-44">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> Elsewhere, <a href="/wiki/Williams_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams F1">Williams</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a> carried out limited running after the tyre blankets designed to keep his tyres at the optimal operating temperature were found to have failed, damaging his tyres. Russian driver <a href="/wiki/Sergey_Sirotkin_(racing_driver)" title="Sergey Sirotkin (racing driver)">Sergey Sirotkin</a> made his Formula One début, driving for <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a> in the place of <a href="/wiki/Esteban_Guti%C3%A9rrez" title="Esteban Gutiérrez">Esteban Gutiérrez</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Sirotkin_FP1_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sirotkin_FP1-18">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> He finished the session seventeenth overall, two and a half seconds slower than Rosberg and four tenths of a second behind Sauber teammate <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a>. <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Merhi" title="Roberto Merhi">Roberto Merhi</a> also took part, driving in the place of <a href="/wiki/Caterham_F1" title="Caterham F1">Caterham</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Kamui_Kobayashi" title="Kamui Kobayashi">Kamui Kobayashi</a> in his third appearance of the season for the team.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Hamilton led the way in the second free practice session, finishing eight tenths of a second ahead of <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Magnussen" title="Kevin Magnussen">Kevin Magnussen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>. Rosberg finished fourth, though he made a mistake on his final flying lap.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> Hamilton was again fastest in the third practice session, three tenths of a second quicker than Rosberg, with Bottas in third.<sup id="cite_ref-fp3_times_48-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fp3_times-48">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> Several drivers encountered trouble whilst simulating a qualifying lap, most notably Magnussen, who damaged his suspension after running wide over a kerb; and Hamilton, who spun at the penultimate corner, narrowly avoiding the wall. <a href="/wiki/Pastor_Maldonado" title="Pastor Maldonado">Pastor Maldonado</a> was unable to set a lap time after his <a href="/wiki/Kinetic_energy_recovery_system" title="Kinetic energy recovery system">energy recovery system</a> failed. Aided by practice and qualifying sessions from the <a href="/wiki/GP2_Series" title="GP2 Series">GP2</a> and <a href="/wiki/GP3_Series" title="GP3 Series">GP3 Series</a> support categories, the track evolved to the point where drivers were setting lap times three and a half seconds quicker ahead of qualifying than they had been at the start of the weekend.<sup id="cite_ref-fp1_times_44-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fp1_times-44">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-fp3_times_48-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fp3_times-48">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Following the first day's running, officials made several changes to the circuit, including the installation of speed bumps in the turn 2–3 run-off area to discourage drivers from deliberately running wide to carry more speed into turn 4, a practice that had been observed during the free practice and qualifying sessions for the GP2 and GP3 races.<sup id="cite_ref-changes_49-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-changes-49">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> The pit lane speed was also revised from 80&#160;km/h (50&#160;mph) to 60&#160;km/h (37&#160;mph) to address concerns over the narrow pit entry and its tight corners.<sup id="cite_ref-changes_49-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-changes-49">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2014_Russian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Nico_Rosberg_Russian_Grand_Prix_2014.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/Nico_Rosberg_Russian_Grand_Prix_2014.jpg/220px-Nico_Rosberg_Russian_Grand_Prix_2014.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/Nico_Rosberg_Russian_Grand_Prix_2014.jpg/330px-Nico_Rosberg_Russian_Grand_Prix_2014.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/Nico_Rosberg_Russian_Grand_Prix_2014.jpg/440px-Nico_Rosberg_Russian_Grand_Prix_2014.jpg 2x" data-file-width="650" data-file-height="434" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Nico_Rosberg_Russian_Grand_Prix_2014.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> secured their fifteenth pole position in sixteen Grands Prix and their eighth front-row lock-out of the season in Sochi.</div></div></div>
<p>Normally qualifying consists of three parts, 18, 15 and 12 minutes in length respectively, with six drivers eliminated from competing after each of the first two sessions.<sup id="cite_ref-pracreg_43-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pracreg-43">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> However, with <a href="/wiki/Marussia_F1" title="Marussia F1">Marussia</a>'s permission to run a single car for the race, the qualifying procedure was revised, with five drivers—instead of the usual six—eliminated at the end of the first part of qualifying (Q1).<sup id="cite_ref-quali_50-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-quali-50">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> topped the first period, going under 1:39.000 for the first time all weekend. <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> was a close second, with <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a> the only other driver within a second of Hamilton's lap time. <a href="/wiki/Marcus_Ericsson" title="Marcus Ericsson">Marcus Ericsson</a> was eliminated in seventeenth place, once again out-qualifying teammate <a href="/wiki/Kamui_Kobayashi" title="Kamui Kobayashi">Kamui Kobayashi</a>, who finished nineteenth. After struggling with another engine fault, <a href="/wiki/Pastor_Maldonado" title="Pastor Maldonado">Pastor Maldonado</a> could only set a time good enough for twentieth, out-qualifying the sole Marussia of <a href="/wiki/Max_Chilton" title="Max Chilton">Max Chilton</a>. <a href="/wiki/Williams_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams F1">Williams</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> proved to be a shock elimination, struggling with a fuel flow issue that left him down on power; he was recorded going through the speed trap on the approach to turn 2 some 23&#160;km/h (14&#160;mph) slower than Bottas, the fastest driver through the speed trap, and qualified eighteenth for his first Q1 elimination since the <a href="/wiki/2014_British_Grand_Prix" title="2014 British Grand Prix">British Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-quali_50-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-quali-50">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Hamilton, Rosberg and Bottas once again led the way in Q2, which saw several drivers in a close fight to avoid elimination. Having struggled with a lack of pace over the course of the weekend, <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> missed out on a Q3 berth by a tenth of a second. He was followed by the <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force Indias</a> of <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a> in twelfth and <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a> in thirteenth, while <a href="/wiki/Esteban_Guti%C3%A9rrez" title="Esteban Gutiérrez">Esteban Gutiérrez</a> out-qualified <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a> to give the <a href="/wiki/Sauber_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber F1">Saubers</a> fourteenth and fifteenth. <a href="/wiki/Romain_Grosjean" title="Romain Grosjean">Romain Grosjean</a> was the final driver eliminated in Q2 despite having improved upon his Q1 time.<sup id="cite_ref-quali_50-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-quali-50">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Mercedes drivers continued to dominate in the final twelve-minute period, but the rapid evolution of the circuit came to an abrupt halt after the first timed laps, and neither Hamilton nor Rosberg were able to improve their times, leaving Hamilton with provisional pole. Despite the lap times dropping off, Valtteri Bottas was able to best Hamilton's time through the first two sectors, and maintained a pace that suggested he could steal a maiden pole position until he made an unforced error in the final corner. <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> finished fourth, with <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Magnussen" title="Kevin Magnussen">Kevin Magnussen</a> proving <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>'s newfound performance was no accident in sixth. <a href="/wiki/Daniil_Kvyat" title="Daniil Kvyat">Daniil Kvyat</a> secured a career-best fifth place in his home Grand Prix, while <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> out-qualified teammate Vettel for the eleventh time in 2014 with seventh. <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> endured their most difficult qualifying session since the British Grand Prix,  with <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> closely matched, but over two seconds behind Hamilton. <a href="/wiki/Jean-%C3%89ric_Vergne" title="Jean-Éric Vergne">Jean-Éric Vergne</a> finished tenth overall, ensuring two <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rossos</a> in the top ten.<sup id="cite_ref-quali_50-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-quali-50">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-qualifying">Post-qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2014_Russian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Post-qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p><a href="/wiki/Kevin_Magnussen" title="Kevin Magnussen">Kevin Magnussen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a> received five-place grid penalties for gearbox changes,<sup id="cite_ref-MAL_HUL_39-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MAL_HUL-39">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-MAG_penalty_52-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MAG_penalty-52">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> demoting them to eleventh and seventeenth place respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-quali_50-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-quali-50">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Pastor_Maldonado" title="Pastor Maldonado">Pastor Maldonado</a> qualified in twentieth which became twenty-first once his carry-over penalty was applied. Maldonado and <a href="/wiki/Max_Chilton" title="Max Chilton">Max Chilton</a> also received a five-place penalty for a gearbox change.<sup id="cite_ref-quali_50-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-quali-50">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-CHI_MAL_53-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CHI_MAL-53">&#91;N 2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2014_Russian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:F1_Grand_Prix_Russia_2014_start_lane.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/22/F1_Grand_Prix_Russia_2014_start_lane.jpg/220px-F1_Grand_Prix_Russia_2014_start_lane.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="124" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/22/F1_Grand_Prix_Russia_2014_start_lane.jpg/330px-F1_Grand_Prix_Russia_2014_start_lane.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/22/F1_Grand_Prix_Russia_2014_start_lane.jpg/440px-F1_Grand_Prix_Russia_2014_start_lane.jpg 2x" data-file-width="960" data-file-height="540" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:F1_Grand_Prix_Russia_2014_start_lane.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The cars lining up on the starting grid.</div></div></div>
<p>At the start of the race, <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> attempted to out-brake <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> into turn 2, but locked both of his front wheels, running wide and creating a flat spot on both tyres. After returning the position to Hamilton, he pitted at the end of the lap, as the flat spots would create a vibration in the car given the high speeds and lateral loading on the tyres. He immediately changed to the harder Prime compound, with the team switching to a strategy that called for him to do the next fifty-two laps of the race on a single set of tyres. Rosberg took advantage of the pit strategies of other drivers and the straight-line speed of <a href="/wiki/Mercedes_F1_W05_Hybrid" title="Mercedes F1 W05 Hybrid">his car</a> to gradually work his way through the field. Behind him, <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> attempted to replicate his strategy, also pitting on the first lap, but switching to the softer Option compound. He was less successful that Rosberg, hampered by slower mid-field drivers.
</p><p>At the front of the field, <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a> was able to keep up with Hamilton during the early stages of the race, but Hamilton was able to gradually build up a forty-second lead by the time of the first&#8212;and only&#8212;round of pit stops, giving him enough of a buffer to pit without losing the lead, even when faced with the lowered speed limit and lengthy pit lane of the <a href="/wiki/Sochi_Autodrom" title="Sochi Autodrom">Sochi Autodrom</a>. Hamilton was unchallenged throughout the race, ultimately winning by thirteen seconds ahead of Rosberg following his recovery and giving the team their ninth one-two finish of <a href="/wiki/2014_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2014 Formula One season">the season</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup> Bottas finished in third, his fifth podium of the season, having been overtaken by Rosberg on track. Despite having fresher tyres and setting a series of laps among the fastest in the race&#8212;including the fastest lap and the official lap record&#8212;he was unable to catch Rosberg in the final laps.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> drivers <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Magnussen" title="Kevin Magnussen">Kevin Magnussen</a> finished fourth and fifth, giving <a href="/wiki/Mercedes_AMG_High_Performance_Powertrains" title="Mercedes AMG High Performance Powertrains">Mercedes-powered</a> cars the top five places in the final standings. McLaren attempted to adjust Button's strategy to place ahead of Rosberg after his pit stop, but were powerless to prevent Rosberg from overtaking. Magnussen spent the early phase of the race in a strategy battle with <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>, <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a>, which he ultimately won. Alonso finished in sixth, the first non-Mercedes-powered driver across the line, having spent most of the race fending off Ricciardo. After spending most of the weekend struggling with an under-powered and under-performing car, Vettel secured four World Championship points with eighth place. <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a> completed the top ten.
</p><p>Felipe Massa's attempt at replicating Rosberg's strategy failed, leaving him outside the points in eleventh place, ahead of <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a>. Despite qualifying in fifth and tenth, <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso</a> drivers <a href="/wiki/Daniil_Kvyat" title="Daniil Kvyat">Daniil Kvyat</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jean-%C3%89ric_Vergne" title="Jean-Éric Vergne">Jean-Éric Vergne</a> finished in fourteenth and thirteenth place respectively, having been forced to race conservatively in the face of high fuel consumption and a predicted 12&#160;°C (22&#160;°F) drop in temperature in the final stages of the race, which never eventuated. <a href="/wiki/Esteban_Guti%C3%A9rrez" title="Esteban Gutiérrez">Esteban Gutiérrez</a> led <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a> teammate <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a> across the line one lap down with the latter's race having been disrupted by contact with <a href="/wiki/Romain_Grosjean" title="Romain Grosjean">Romain Grosjean</a> at turn 2 for which Grosjean was punished with a five-second stop/go penalty. Grosjean ultimately finished seventeenth ahead of <a href="/wiki/Pastor_Maldonado" title="Pastor Maldonado">Pastor Maldonado</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Marcus_Ericsson" title="Marcus Ericsson">Marcus Ericsson</a> the final classified finisher in nineteenth place, two laps behind Hamilton.<sup id="cite_ref-ticker_55-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ticker-55">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The race saw two retirements, with <a href="/wiki/Kamui_Kobayashi" title="Kamui Kobayashi">Kamui Kobayashi</a> forced out on lap twenty-one with what the team described as a brake issue, though Kobayashi later suggested that <a href="/wiki/Caterham_F1" title="Caterham F1">Caterham</a> had deliberately retired the car to avoid damaging its power unit ahead of the <a href="/wiki/2014_United_States_Grand_Prix" title="2014 United States Grand Prix">next race</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup> He also reported that the team had found damage to a suspension wishbone following free practice 3, and that with no replacement part available, the team had repaired the damage by fusing the wishbone together with carbon, a solution that Kobayashi felt was unsafe.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Marussia_F1" title="Marussia F1">Marussia's</a> difficult race lasted just nine laps, with <a href="/wiki/Max_Chilton" title="Max Chilton">Max Chilton</a> reporting an unusual vibration in his front-left suspension, and the team elected to retire the car rather than risk a suspension failure.<sup id="cite_ref-ticker_55-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ticker-55">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2014_Russian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Hamilton_and_Putin.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/30/Hamilton_and_Putin.jpg/220px-Hamilton_and_Putin.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/30/Hamilton_and_Putin.jpg/330px-Hamilton_and_Putin.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/30/Hamilton_and_Putin.jpg/440px-Hamilton_and_Putin.jpg 2x" data-file-width="650" data-file-height="434" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Hamilton_and_Putin.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> accepting the race winner's trophy from Russian president <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Putin" title="Vladimir Putin">Vladimir Putin</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>With twenty-five points for first place, <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> extended his <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">World Drivers' Championship</a> lead over <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> by seven points to carry a seventeen-point margin into the <a href="/wiki/2014_United_States_Grand_Prix" title="2014 United States Grand Prix">United States Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-c&#39;ship_58-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-c&#39;ship-58">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> By out-scoring title rivals <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a>, the result also secured the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">World Constructors' Championship</a> for <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a>, their first title as a Formula One constructor. Hamilton dedicated the win to Jules Bianchi.<sup id="cite_ref-Bianchi_31-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bianchi-31">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a>'s podium finish elevated him from sixth to fourth in the drivers' standings,  overtaking <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>. The result also allowed <a href="/wiki/Williams_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams F1">Williams</a> to consolidate their hold on third place in the constructors' standings, ahead of <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>. Further down the order, fourth and fifth places for <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> and minor points placings for <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a> saw the British team overtake their rivals for fifth place in the standings.<sup id="cite_ref-c&#39;ship_58-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-c&#39;ship-58">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Both Marussia and <a href="/wiki/Caterham_F1" title="Caterham F1">Caterham</a> went into <a href="/wiki/Administration_(law)" title="Administration (law)">administration</a> after the race, citing financial difficulties.<sup id="cite_ref-Marussia_close_59-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Marussia_close-59">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup> Although Caterham returned to compete in the <a href="/wiki/2014_Abu_Dhabi_Grand_Prix" title="2014 Abu Dhabi Grand Prix">Abu Dhabi Grand Prix</a>, the team collapsed shortly thereafter. Marussia faced a similar fate before a last-minute deal with an investor rescued the team, and they returned to the Formula One grid in <a href="/wiki/2015_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2015 Formula One season">2015</a>, albeit registered as a British, rather than Russian competitor.<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Accolades">Accolades</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2014_Russian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Accolades">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In December 2014, the race was awarded with the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_Promotional_Trophy_winners#Race_Promoters.27_Trophy" title="List of Formula One Promotional Trophy winners">Formula One Promotional Trophy</a>. <a href="/wiki/Dmitry_Kozak" title="Dmitry Kozak">Dmitry Kozak</a> received the award from <a href="/wiki/Bernie_Ecclestone" title="Bernie Ecclestone">Bernie Ecclestone</a> in a special ceremony at the <a href="/wiki/Sochi_Autodrom" title="Sochi Autodrom">circuit</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
