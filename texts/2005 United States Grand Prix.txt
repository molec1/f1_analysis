<p>The <b>2005 United States Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on June 19, 2005, at the <a href="/wiki/Indianapolis_Motor_Speedway" title="Indianapolis Motor Speedway">Indianapolis Motor Speedway</a> and was the ninth race of the <a href="/wiki/2005_FIA_Formula_One_World_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="2005 FIA Formula One World Championship">2005 FIA Formula One World Championship</a>. The event is one of the most controversial <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor races in modern history. Out of the 20 cars that entered the race, only the six cars from the teams using <a href="/wiki/Bridgestone" title="Bridgestone">Bridgestone</a> tyres (<a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a>) competed. The remaining fourteen entrants, all using <a href="/wiki/Michelin" title="Michelin">Michelin</a> tyres, completed the parade lap, but retired to the pits before the race started.
</p><p>Following several tyre failures before the race, which caused major accidents for <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Toyota_Racing_(Formula_One_team)" title="Toyota Racing (Formula One team)">Toyota</a> during Friday practice and Ricardo Zonta in the other Toyota, Michelin advised its seven customer teams that without a reduction in speed in Turn 13, the tyres provided for the race would only be safe for 10 laps. Michelin had been providing working tyres for the race since 2001. The situation was worsened by the 2005 Formula One rules, which forbade tyre changes during the race, and a repave of the oval portion of the course after the <a href="/wiki/2004_Brickyard_400" title="2004 Brickyard 400">2004 Brickyard 400</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile</a> (FIA), the sport's governing body, refused a compromise proposal from Michelin to allow a <a href="/wiki/Chicane" title="Chicane">chicane</a> to be installed, maintaining that such rule changes would be grossly unfair to the Bridgestone-shod teams, who had come prepared with properly working tyres, and that a last-minute change to the track layout would be dangerous in case of crashes. The Michelin teams, unable to come to a compromise with the FIA, decided not to participate. It was later stated that the Michelin-shod teams could have potentially exposed themselves to criminal liability under <a href="/wiki/Indiana" title="Indiana">Indiana</a> state law had they competed.<sup id="cite_ref-BBC20050722_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC20050722-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> It was also thought that since Bridgestone also provided tyres to the two <a href="/wiki/INDYCAR" class="mw-redirect" title="INDYCAR">Indy Racing League</a> series (<a href="/wiki/IndyCar_Series" title="IndyCar Series">IndyCar Series</a> and <a href="/wiki/Indy_Lights" title="Indy Lights">Infiniti Pro Series</a>) via its <a href="/wiki/Firestone_Tire_and_Rubber_Company" title="Firestone Tire and Rubber Company">Firestone</a> brand, and had encountered issues during testing that caused them to return with different IndyCar tyres,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> it understood better the loads the Formula One tyres would be under. Thus, Bridgestone was able to provide a proper tyre.
</p><p>Of the six competitors, Ferrari's <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> was the eventual winner, with his teammate <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> finishing second. The result significantly boosted Schumacher's championship standing, placing him third overall—no driver above him in driver championship points took part in the race.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The final race result was the lowest number of finishing entries ever seen in a major <a href="/wiki/Open-wheel_racing" class="mw-redirect" title="Open-wheel racing">open-wheel</a> motorsports event at the Indianapolis Motor Speedway since the institution of the <a href="/wiki/Indianapolis_500" title="Indianapolis 500">500-Mile Race</a> (surpassing the previous record low of seven finishers in <a href="/wiki/1966_Indianapolis_500" title="1966 Indianapolis 500">1966</a>, a race marred by a major first lap accident that eliminated a full third of the starting field). The situation created enormous negative publicity for the sport of Formula One, especially in the United States, a market in which Formula One had struggled to establish itself over the preceding twenty years; some even called the race "<a href="/wiki/List_of_scandals_with_%22-gate%22_suffix" class="mw-redirect" title="List of scandals with &quot;-gate&quot; suffix">Indygate</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2005_United_States_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The qualifying session for the United States Grand Prix was held on June 18. <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> gained the <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> by posting the fastest lap time, 1:10.625. Trulli, driving for <a href="/wiki/Toyota_Racing_(Formula_One_team)" title="Toyota Racing (Formula One team)">Toyota</a>, was the team's first driver to win the pole position for a Formula One race.<sup id="cite_ref-pole_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pole-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The second-place qualifier was <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a>, who ran a lap of 1:10.694. <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a>, <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> were the next three fastest qualifiers. <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a>, who led the season point standings, qualified in sixth. The rest of the top 10 consisted of <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a>, <a href="/wiki/Takuma_Sato" title="Takuma Sato">Takuma Sato</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pole_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pole-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-race_controversy">Pre-race controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2005_United_States_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Pre-race controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Ralf_Schumacher_cropped.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d5/Ralf_Schumacher_cropped.jpg/220px-Ralf_Schumacher_cropped.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="275" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d5/Ralf_Schumacher_cropped.jpg/330px-Ralf_Schumacher_cropped.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d5/Ralf_Schumacher_cropped.jpg/440px-Ralf_Schumacher_cropped.jpg 2x" data-file-width="456" data-file-height="570" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Ralf_Schumacher_cropped.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Ralf Schumacher at an autograph session before the practice session where he crashed in Turn 13.</div></div></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Michelin_tyre_failures">Michelin tyre failures</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2005_United_States_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Michelin tyre failures">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>During the afternoon's practice session on Friday, June 17, 2005, <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a>, driving for <a href="/wiki/Toyota_Racing_(Formula_One_team)" title="Toyota Racing (Formula One team)">Toyota</a>, crashed badly in Turn 13 of the <a href="/wiki/Indianapolis_Motor_Speedway" title="Indianapolis Motor Speedway">Indianapolis Motor Speedway</a> road course, as a result of a left-rear tyre failure. He was unable to continue racing, and was replaced by the team's <a href="/wiki/Test_driver" title="Test driver">test driver</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ricardo_Zonta" title="Ricardo Zonta">Ricardo Zonta</a>, who himself suffered a left-rear tyre failure during practice as well,<sup id="cite_ref-:0_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> for the rest of the weekend. Schumacher had crashed in the same spot as a result of a tyre failure the <a href="/wiki/2004_United_States_Grand_Prix#Race" title="2004 United States Grand Prix">previous year</a>, while driving for the <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> team.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Turn 13 on the <a href="/wiki/Indianapolis_Motor_Speedway" title="Indianapolis Motor Speedway">Indianapolis Motor Speedway</a> road course is a high-speed banked turn, unique in Formula One racing, which causes a greater than usual <a href="/wiki/Circle_of_forces" title="Circle of forces">tyre loading</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> On June 18, Michelin reported that it did not understand why the tyres it had provided for its seven customer teams&#160;– <a href="/wiki/British_American_Racing" title="British American Racing">BAR</a>, <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>, <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a>, <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a>, Toyota, and Williams&#160;– had failed in this turn, and announced its intention to fly in tyres of a different specification from its <a href="/wiki/Clermont-Ferrand" title="Clermont-Ferrand">Clermont-Ferrand</a> headquarters.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The replacement tyres flown in, which were of the type used in the <a href="/wiki/2005_Spanish_Grand_Prix" title="2005 Spanish Grand Prix">Spanish Grand Prix</a> earlier that year, turned out to have the same problem when tested.<sup id="cite_ref-Stoddart&#39;s_Comments_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Stoddart&#39;s_Comments-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Telegraph_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Telegraph-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Correspondence_between_Michelin_and_the_FIA">Correspondence between Michelin and the FIA</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2005_United_States_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Correspondence between Michelin and the FIA">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In a letter to FIA Race Director <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Whiting" title="Charlie Whiting">Charlie Whiting</a> dated Saturday, June 18, Michelin representatives <a href="/wiki/Pierre_Dupasquier" title="Pierre Dupasquier">Pierre Dupasquier</a> and Nick Shorrock revealed that they did not know the cause of the Toyota tyre failures, and unless the cars could be slowed down in Turn 13, they could not guarantee the tyres' safety for more than 10 laps.<sup id="cite_ref-Telegraph_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Telegraph-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
Whiting replied on Sunday, June 19, expressing his surprise that Michelin had not brought suitable tyres, suggesting that the teams should limit their drivers to the maximum safe speed specified by Michelin in Turn 13. He also addressed several solutions which had been proposed by the teams, insisting that use of the new specification tyres flown in overnight would be "a breach of the rules to be considered by the stewards", and the placement of a <a href="/wiki/Chicane" title="Chicane">chicane</a> in the turn was "out of the question"&#160;– the race would not be sanctioned by the <a href="/wiki/FIA" class="mw-redirect" title="FIA">FIA</a> (making it a non-championship race) if the track layout was changed. He deemed the Michelin teams' proposals to be "grossly unfair" to the <a href="/wiki/Bridgestone" title="Bridgestone">Bridgestone</a> teams.<sup id="cite_ref-letters_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-letters-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template noprint noexcerpt Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:NOTRS" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:NOTRS"><span title="fansite (March 2017)">better&#160;source&#160;needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Tyre_carts_on_grid_at_USGP_2005.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/de/Tyre_carts_on_grid_at_USGP_2005.jpg/220px-Tyre_carts_on_grid_at_USGP_2005.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="117" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/de/Tyre_carts_on_grid_at_USGP_2005.jpg/330px-Tyre_carts_on_grid_at_USGP_2005.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/de/Tyre_carts_on_grid_at_USGP_2005.jpg/440px-Tyre_carts_on_grid_at_USGP_2005.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3008" data-file-height="1603" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Tyre_carts_on_grid_at_USGP_2005.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Tire" title="Tire">Tyre</a> issues caused the race to be run with only six competitors.</div></div></div>
<p>In a second letter, also dated June 18, Dupasquier and Shorrock confirmed that they would not permit their teams to race on the Michelin tyres used during qualification without changes to the circuit, and reiterated their request to slow down Turn 13. Whiting's brief reply maintained that no such change would be permitted, and gave the teams the choice of limiting speeds through Turn 13, using tyres of a different specification to those used in qualifying, subject to a penalty, or changing tyres repeatedly, which would have been permitted if a driver's safety were at issue.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template noprint noexcerpt Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:NOTRS" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikipedia:NOTRS"><span title="fansite (March 2017)">better&#160;source&#160;needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Attempts_at_compromise">Attempts at compromise</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2005_United_States_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Attempts at compromise">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Alonso_(Renault)_qualifying_at_USGP_2005.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/be/Alonso_%28Renault%29_qualifying_at_USGP_2005.jpg/220px-Alonso_%28Renault%29_qualifying_at_USGP_2005.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="97" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/be/Alonso_%28Renault%29_qualifying_at_USGP_2005.jpg/330px-Alonso_%28Renault%29_qualifying_at_USGP_2005.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/be/Alonso_%28Renault%29_qualifying_at_USGP_2005.jpg/440px-Alonso_%28Renault%29_qualifying_at_USGP_2005.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1162" data-file-height="514" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Alonso_(Renault)_qualifying_at_USGP_2005.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Alonso qualifying for the race</div></div></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Paul_Stoddart" title="Paul Stoddart">Paul Stoddart</a>, then owner of <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a>, a team using Bridgestone tyres, published an account on Wednesday, June 22, of the events leading up to the race. Stoddart recorded a meeting around 10:00am on the day of the race, to which Speedway president <a href="/wiki/Tony_George" title="Tony George">Tony George</a>, two senior Michelin representatives, <a href="/wiki/Bernie_Ecclestone" title="Bernie Ecclestone">Bernie Ecclestone</a> (president and <a href="/wiki/CEO" class="mw-redirect" title="CEO">CEO</a> of <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_Management" class="mw-redirect" title="Formula One Management">Formula One Management</a> and <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_Administration" class="mw-redirect" title="Formula One Administration">Formula One Administration</a>), the team principals, and the teams' Michelin technical representatives were summoned. All invited were present except <a href="/wiki/Jean_Todt" title="Jean Todt">Jean Todt</a>, Team Principal of <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Scuderia Ferrari</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Stoddart&#39;s_Comments_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Stoddart&#39;s_Comments-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The Michelin representatives stated their position that the tyres provided to the teams could not safely complete the race distance, and requested that the Bridgestone teams, represented by Stoddart and <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Colin_Kolles" title="Colin Kolles">Colin Kolles</a>, permit the installation of a chicane in Turn 13. Those present discussed and agreed to reject the FIA's solution of speed-limiting the Michelin cars in the turn because of the potential for accidents. They likewise dismissed the possibility of making pit stops every ten laps, resolved that a chicane was the best solution, and instructed several technical representatives  to prepare plans for its installation. Bernie Ecclestone offered to consult Todt, who had not come to the meeting, and the president of the FIA, <a href="/wiki/Max_Mosley" title="Max Mosley">Max Mosley</a>, who was not present at the race, and reconvene the meeting when he had responses.<sup id="cite_ref-Stoddart&#39;s_Comments_12-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Stoddart&#39;s_Comments-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Ecclestone returned at about 10:55 to inform the group that Todt had refused to agree to the chicane, maintaining that it was an FIA and a Michelin problem and not his. By the time Stoddart's account of the meeting was published, Todt had already denied that he had ever been consulted, but stated that, if asked, he would not have agreed to the chicane.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Furthermore, Ecclestone reported that Mosley had "stated that if any attempts were made to alter the circuit, he would cancel the Grand Prix forthwith".<sup id="cite_ref-Stoddart&#39;s_Comments_12-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Stoddart&#39;s_Comments-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Team_principals.27_plan"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Team_principals'_plan">Team principals' plan</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2005_United_States_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Team principals&#039; plan">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Zonta_(Toyota)_qualifying_at_USGP_2005.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b2/Zonta_%28Toyota%29_qualifying_at_USGP_2005.jpg/220px-Zonta_%28Toyota%29_qualifying_at_USGP_2005.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="84" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b2/Zonta_%28Toyota%29_qualifying_at_USGP_2005.jpg/330px-Zonta_%28Toyota%29_qualifying_at_USGP_2005.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b2/Zonta_%28Toyota%29_qualifying_at_USGP_2005.jpg/440px-Zonta_%28Toyota%29_qualifying_at_USGP_2005.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1831" data-file-height="697" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Zonta_(Toyota)_qualifying_at_USGP_2005.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>As <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> was injured, <a href="/wiki/Ricardo_Zonta" title="Ricardo Zonta">Ricardo Zonta</a> qualified the <a href="/wiki/Toyota_Racing_(Formula_One_team)" title="Toyota Racing (Formula One team)">Toyota</a> instead of him.</div></div></div>
<p>The group, according to Stoddart, continued to propose alternative solutions, including "a non-championship race, or a race in which the Michelin teams could not score points, and even a race whereby only the Michelin teams used the new chicane", but eventually agreed that the best option was to install the chicane and run a non-championship race, without Ferrari if necessary.<sup id="cite_ref-non-FIA_USGP_plan_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-non-FIA_USGP_plan-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> To ignore the FIA's instructions and carry on the race would have resulted in the FIA's withdrawing its staff, so the group appointed delegates to fill the various offices, including a race director to replace Charlie Whiting and a safety car driver to replace <a href="/wiki/Bernd_Mayl%C3%A4nder" title="Bernd Mayländer">Bernd Mayländer</a>. The team principals were instructed to convey to their teams and drivers that, in the absence of FIA scrutineers and equipment, the technical rules could not be enforced, and that they were to conduct themselves honourably and in the interest of an entertaining race.<sup id="cite_ref-non-FIA_USGP_plan_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-non-FIA_USGP_plan-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>They proceeded to summon the twenty drivers and present their plan. Of the drivers' opinions, Stoddart writes: "While I cannot testify that each and every driver agreed with what we were proposing, what I can say with certainty is that no driver disagreed." The Ferrari drivers expressed no opinion in the matter, leaving the decision to Todt, who was not present. The nine team principals who were present then resolved that, unless they and the FIA could come to a decision in the best interest of the sport, they would not participate in the race.<sup id="cite_ref-non-FIA_USGP_plan_17-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-non-FIA_USGP_plan-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:GrandPrix_Circuit_US_2005_Turn13.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4f/GrandPrix_Circuit_US_2005_Turn13.svg/220px-GrandPrix_Circuit_US_2005_Turn13.svg.png" decoding="async" width="220" height="184" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4f/GrandPrix_Circuit_US_2005_Turn13.svg/330px-GrandPrix_Circuit_US_2005_Turn13.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4f/GrandPrix_Circuit_US_2005_Turn13.svg/440px-GrandPrix_Circuit_US_2005_Turn13.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="525" data-file-height="440" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:GrandPrix_Circuit_US_2005_Turn13.svg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Turn 13, the centre of the controversy</div></div></div>
<p>After a short break, the group gathered again in Ecclestone's office to find Renault team principal <a href="/wiki/Flavio_Briatore" title="Flavio Briatore">Flavio Briatore</a> on the phone with Max Mosley. Mosley had apparently rejected all of their proposals, and it was stated that "Mosley had informed Mr. Martin, the FIA's most senior representative in the USA, that if any kind of non-championship race was run, or any alteration made to the circuit, the US Grand Prix, and indeed, all FIA-regulated motorsport in the US, would be under threat".<sup id="cite_ref-Stoddart&#39;s_Comments_12-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Stoddart&#39;s_Comments-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> On the same day that Stoddart's version of events was published, the FIA issued a statement denying that Mosley had made the reported threat, or that any such conversation had taken place.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Having exhausted their options, the Michelin team principals, Stoddart, and Bernie Ecclestone&#160;– but not Jordan's Colin Kolles&#160;– discussed whether their cars should proceed to the grid, and decided that they should participate in the formation lap but that they could not race.<sup id="cite_ref-Stoddart&#39;s_Comments_12-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Stoddart&#39;s_Comments-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Stoddart asked Kolles if he would be allowing his cars to take part and  was informed that Jordan would indeed be racing, despite having previously agreed not to. Stoddart was then approached by a Bridgestone representative and told that Bridgestone wanted him to race; he has also stated that given his "current relationship with Mr Mosley, [he] felt certain heavy sanctions would follow if [he] did not [race]." Stoddart too decided to allow his drivers to start, but reported that he would retire them if the Jordans did not finish the race.<sup id="cite_ref-non-FIA_USGP_plan_17-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-non-FIA_USGP_plan-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_report">Race report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2005_United_States_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Race report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Schumacher_(Ferrari)_in_practice_at_USGP_2005.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9d/Schumacher_%28Ferrari%29_in_practice_at_USGP_2005.jpg/220px-Schumacher_%28Ferrari%29_in_practice_at_USGP_2005.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="86" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9d/Schumacher_%28Ferrari%29_in_practice_at_USGP_2005.jpg/330px-Schumacher_%28Ferrari%29_in_practice_at_USGP_2005.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9d/Schumacher_%28Ferrari%29_in_practice_at_USGP_2005.jpg/440px-Schumacher_%28Ferrari%29_in_practice_at_USGP_2005.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1798" data-file-height="700" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Schumacher_(Ferrari)_in_practice_at_USGP_2005.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Ferrari's <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> (here pictured in qualifying) took his first, and only, win of the season.</div></div></div>
<p>At the start of the race, all the cars lined up on the grid per <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_regulations#Race_procedure" title="Formula One regulations">FIA race procedure</a>. As Charlie Whiting signalled the green light to start the formation lap, a full field of twenty cars set off as normal for a single lap before forming the starting grid. At the banked Turn 13, the entrance to the pit lane (and the turn that was the centre of the controversy), all teams that ran Michelin tyres returned to their pits, leaving just six cars from the three Bridgestone teams (<a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a>) to start the race.<sup id="cite_ref-crashreport_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-crashreport-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The move by the teams, to come to the grid and then pull out after the formation lap into the pits, infuriated the fans, who did not know about the plan.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-strangest_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-strangest-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio" title="BBC Radio">BBC Radio</a> broadcaster Maurice Hamilton said of the event, "Without question, it was the strangest race I commentated on in F1."<sup id="cite_ref-strangest_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-strangest-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Because of the retirement of the drivers who qualified ahead of them, Michael Schumacher and his Ferrari teammate, Barrichello, were the foremost starters, though using the grid positions they had qualified in; the pair were followed by <a href="/wiki/Tiago_Monteiro" title="Tiago Monteiro">Tiago Monteiro</a> and <a href="/wiki/Narain_Karthikeyan" title="Narain Karthikeyan">Narain Karthikeyan</a>, both driving for Jordan. Rounding out the remaining field of six were <a href="/wiki/Christijan_Albers" title="Christijan Albers">Christijan Albers</a> and <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Friesacher" title="Patrick Friesacher">Patrick Friesacher</a> of Minardi. Schumacher retained the lead when the race started, and the only changes in positioning came when Albers overtook Karthikeyan but lost the position again later on in the race.<sup id="cite_ref-Ferrari_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ferrari-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> The two Ferrari drivers quickly built a significant lead over their rivals. By lap 10, many of the estimated 100,000<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>–130,000<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> attendees had begun to leave the grandstands. Thousands of fans were reported to have gone to the Indianapolis Motor Speedway ticket office to demand refunds, and police were called to keep the peace.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Boos were heard throughout the race, and some upset fans threw beer cans and water bottles on the track.<sup id="cite_ref-boos_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-boos-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The race was a story of pit strategy, as the only passing on the circuit was of lapped traffic. Albers was the only car to run a three pit stop race, as all other drivers chose to stop only twice. The only lead changes came on lap 26, as Schumacher's 32-second stop gave Barrichello the lead, and on lap 51, as Schumacher turned in the quickest pit stop at 23.615 seconds, giving him enough time to exit pit lane at the same time as Barrichello, with the result of forcing Barrichello into the grass of Turn One. After this incident, which was not investigated by race officials, both Ferrari drivers were reminded over their radios not to crash out of the race, and they both settled into a slower pace, comfortably ahead of the rest of the field.<sup id="cite_ref-crashreport_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-crashreport-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Schumacher ended up with the victory, ahead of Barrichello. Monteiro and Karthikeyan, more than a lap down, finished in a distant third and fourth, and Albers and Friesacher brought up the rear, two laps down.<sup id="cite_ref-gear_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gear-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-analysis_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-analysis-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> All four of the drivers for Jordan and Minardi scored their first points in Formula One at this race.<sup id="cite_ref-analysis_28-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-analysis-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Karthikeyan's points were the first for an Indian driver in Formula One.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> This was also the final race at which the Minardi team tallied points.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At the podium ceremony, at which none of the scheduled dignitaries were present, all Ferrari team members quietly accepted their awards, and quickly exited. Monteiro stayed behind to celebrate his first podium finish, and the first for a Portuguese driver.<sup id="cite_ref-crashreport_19-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-crashreport-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">Aftermath</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2005_United_States_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Aftermath">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Blame_Mosley_banner.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6f/Blame_Mosley_banner.jpg/220px-Blame_Mosley_banner.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="153" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6f/Blame_Mosley_banner.jpg/330px-Blame_Mosley_banner.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6f/Blame_Mosley_banner.jpg/440px-Blame_Mosley_banner.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2630" data-file-height="1835" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Blame_Mosley_banner.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Disgruntled fans blaming <a href="/wiki/FIA" class="mw-redirect" title="FIA">FIA</a> president <a href="/wiki/Max_Mosley" title="Max Mosley">Max Mosley</a> for the events of the race</div></div></div>
<p>The win, Schumacher's only victory of 2005, moved him from fifth to third in the drivers championship.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Alonso and Räikkönen remained first and second in the championship standings, on 59 and 37 points respectively, while Schumacher moved up to 34 points.<sup id="cite_ref-Ferrari_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ferrari-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> With his second-place finish, Barrichello went into fourth in the drivers championship, on 29 points, and Trulli dropped to fifth with 27 points.<sup id="cite_ref-Ferrari_22-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ferrari-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> The Ferrari team moved into joint second in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a>, matching McLaren with 63 points; both teams trailed Renault, which retained the lead on 76 points. Both Jordan and Minardi scored points, moving out of a tie with BAR-Honda at the bottom of the constructor standings.<sup id="cite_ref-analysis_28-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-analysis-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> The results of the United States Grand Prix were overshadowed by the withdrawal of the Michelin-shod teams, and by the inability to find a solution which would have allowed them to race.<sup id="cite_ref-strangest_21-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-strangest-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-gear_27-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gear-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Bernie Ecclestone, in answer to a question by <a href="/wiki/ITV_(TV_network)" title="ITV (TV network)">ITV</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Martin_Brundle" title="Martin Brundle">Martin Brundle</a> in an interview just before the start of the race, described the future of Formula One in the United States and the future of Michelin in the sport as "not good". He also said that the "incident's not the fault of the teams, to be honest with you."<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The race was labelled a farce, and <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> said, "It throws into doubt the future of the race in US".<sup id="cite_ref-Telegraph_13-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Telegraph-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Associated_Press" title="Associated Press">Associated Press</a> writer Stephen Wade pointed to the boycott as an extension of previous disagreements between the teams and Max Mosley, which had led to the threatened creation of a <a href="/wiki/Grand_Prix_World_Championship" title="Grand Prix World Championship">rival series</a> as an alternative to Formula One.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">The Guardian</a>'</i>s Richard Williams considered the prior disputes a factor in the failure to reach a compromise and felt that the events at this race had increased the risk of a complete rupture.<sup id="cite_ref-boos_26-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-boos-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Minardi boss Paul Stoddart said immediately after the race that nine teams&#160;– all but Ferrari&#160;– agreed not to race, and had Jordan not reversed its decision at the last minute, Minardi would also have <a href="/wiki/Boycott" title="Boycott">boycotted</a> the race.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> In his later, lengthier, statement, he indicated that although it had been Michelin's failure to provide a reliable tyre which had initiated the events, he laid the full blame for the failure to reach some accommodation (which would have allowed a race to happen, for the benefit of the many fans who had paid considerable money for travel and tickets) at the feet of Max Mosley and the FIA, with a small share of the blame going to what he characterized as the obstructionist Ferrari team leader, Jean Todt. He furthermore called for Mosley's resignation.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="FIA.27s_reaction"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="FIA's_reaction">FIA's reaction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2005_United_States_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: FIA&#039;s reaction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The following day, the FIA published a justification of its refusal to permit a change in tyres or the installation of a chicane. It contended that Formula One operated under "clear rules" which could not be altered when a team "brings the wrong equipment to a race." It further claimed that a chicane would have resulted in the race being run on a track that had been significantly altered "without following any of the modern safety procedures," which would have exposed FIA to significant legal liability in the event of an accident.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The FIA also summoned the seven Michelin-shod teams before the <a href="/wiki/World_Motor_Sport_Council" class="mw-redirect" title="World Motor Sport Council">World Motor Sport Council</a> at their headquarters in France, for a hearing on June 29, to explain their failure to participate, by which they had presumably violated the terms of the <a href="/wiki/Concorde_Agreement" title="Concorde Agreement">Concorde Agreement</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> It later published copies of the letters sent to each team "in the interests of transparency".<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup>
They were charged with violating article 151c of the <a href="/wiki/International_Sporting_Code" title="International Sporting Code">International Sporting Code</a>, which refers to <i>acts prejudicial to the interests of competition or motorsport generally.</i><sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> Specifically, it was charged that they had:
</p>
<ul><li><i>Failed to ensure availability of suitable tyres for the race.</i></li>
<li><i>Wrongfully refused to allow cars to start the race.</i></li>
<li><i>Wrongfully refused to allow cars to race subject to speed restrictions at one corner, which was safe for such tyres available.</i></li>
<li><i>Combined with other teams to make a demonstration damaging to the image of Formula 1 by pulling into the pits immediately before the start of the race.</i></li>
<li><i>Failed to notify the stewards of intention not to race.</i></li></ul>
<p>On June 22 the FIA produced a press release from Max Mosley, in the form of a question-and-answer session, in an effort to clarify the FIA's stand on the controversy.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> In it Mosley drew an analogy to a hypothetical situation where the engines from one manufacturer had oil starvation problems due to high lateral loading in one corner, and pointed out that those cars would simply have been forced to run slower as a result. He reiterated that the reason for not installing the chicane was purely that it had never been tested and was thereby deemed unsafe. He pointed out that the alternatives that the FIA suggested were feasible, and wondered why the teams did not use the pitlane as an alternative, especially when, with only six Bridgestone cars, the Michelin teams could still compete for the points scoring seventh and eighth places.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On June 29 the FIA World Motor Sport Council found the teams guilty of not being in possession of suitable tyres, "but with strong mitigating circumstances," and failure to allow their cars to start the race. The teams were found not guilty of the other three counts. The punishment was not decided, and was not to have been announced until September 14.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On July 22, the FIA World Motor Sport Council voted to overturn its previous decision, and exonerated the Michelin teams of all charges. The decision was due to "evidence previously submitted to the FIA Senate."<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> According to BBC Sport, Dennis and Red Bull principal <a href="/wiki/Christian_Horner" title="Christian Horner">Christian Horner</a> told Mosley and the FIA Senate that <a href="/wiki/Indiana" title="Indiana">Indiana</a> state law left the Michelin teams no option but to pull out of the race. They contended that had they competed, they potentially faced criminal charges for knowingly putting others at risk even if no accident actually occurred.<sup id="cite_ref-BBC20050722_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBC20050722-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
Horner later stated in an interview that whilst it was "incredibly difficult" pulling out of the race, "there were things going on that were bigger than the sport that day".<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Compensation">Compensation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2005_United_States_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Compensation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On June 28, Michelin announced that it would offer compensation to all race fans who had purchased tickets for the Grand Prix.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> The company planned to issue refund cheques through the Speedway ticket office for the price of all tickets for the race by the end of September. Additionally, Michelin purchased 20,000 tickets for the <a href="/wiki/2006_United_States_Grand_Prix" title="2006 United States Grand Prix">2006 United States Grand Prix</a> to be distributed to spectators who attended the 2005 race.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In addition to the refunded tickets, there was some discussion about holding a second, non-championship race at Indianapolis. On July 2, at the <a href="/wiki/2005_French_Grand_Prix" title="2005 French Grand Prix">2005 French Grand Prix</a>, <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> team principal <a href="/wiki/Ron_Dennis" title="Ron Dennis">Ron Dennis</a> suggested that an additional race could be held at the American circuit after the last official race of the season, in <a href="/wiki/2005_Chinese_Grand_Prix" title="2005 Chinese Grand Prix">Shanghai</a>. The teams had, apparently, already discussed the idea with <a href="/wiki/Bernie_Ecclestone" title="Bernie Ecclestone">Bernie Ecclestone</a>. But the next day <a href="/wiki/Tony_George" title="Tony George">Tony George</a> dismissed the possibility: "There will be no race at Indianapolis Motor Speedway this fall."<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At the 2005 <a href="/wiki/Champ_Car_World_Series" title="Champ Car World Series">Champ Car World Series</a> <a href="/wiki/Grand_Prix_of_Cleveland" title="Grand Prix of Cleveland">Grand Prix of Cleveland</a>, held one week after the US Grand Prix, free admission was granted to all bearers of ticket stubs of the US Grand Prix.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Friday_drivers">Friday drivers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2005_United_States_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Friday drivers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Teams that were not in the top four of the <a href="/wiki/2004_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2004 Formula One season">2004 Constructors' Championship</a> standings were entitled to run a third car in free practice on Friday.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> These drivers drove on Friday but did not compete in qualifying or the race,<sup id="cite_ref-pole_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pole-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-raceresult_31-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-raceresult-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> with the exception of Ricardo Zonta, who replaced Ralf Schumacher after his practice accident.
