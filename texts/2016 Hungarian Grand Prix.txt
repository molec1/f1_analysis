<p>The <b>2016 Hungarian Grand Prix</b> (formally known as the <b>Formula 1 Magyar Nagydíj 2016</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Auto_racing" title="Auto racing">motor race</a> that was held on 24 July 2016 at the <a href="/wiki/Hungaroring" title="Hungaroring">Hungaroring</a> in <a href="/wiki/Mogyor%C3%B3d" title="Mogyoród">Mogyoród</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hungary" title="Hungary">Hungary</a>. It was the eleventh round of the <a href="/wiki/2016_FIA_Formula_One_World_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="2016 FIA Formula One World Championship">2016 FIA Formula One World Championship</a> and the 32nd running of the <a href="/wiki/Hungarian_Grand_Prix" title="Hungarian Grand Prix">Hungarian Grand Prix</a>, and 31st time it had been held as a round of the World Championship.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> entered the round with a one-point lead over teammate <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> in the Drivers' Championship while Mercedes led in the Constructors' Championship ahead of <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>. The race was won by Hamilton, with Rosberg and <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> completing the podium, meaning Hamilton took over the lead in the Drivers' Championship after the race.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2016_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the week before the race, the <a href="/wiki/FIA" class="mw-redirect" title="FIA">FIA</a> revised the rules governing pit-to-car communications following criticism from the teams in light of a penalty given to <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> at the <a href="/wiki/2016_British_Grand_Prix" title="2016 British Grand Prix">British Grand Prix</a> for receiving assistance outside those allowed under the regulations; and in the aftermath of <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a>'s brake failure and subsequent retirement from the <a href="/wiki/2016_Austrian_Grand_Prix" title="2016 Austrian Grand Prix">Austrian Grand Prix</a>, which <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a> was particularly critical of in light of the safety implications arising from Pérez's accident.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The rules, first introduced during the <a href="/wiki/2015_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2015 Formula One World Championship">2015</a> season, were intended to crack down on driver coaching—stating that a competitor must drive the car "alone and unaided"—but allowing teams the scope to alert drivers to the imminent failure of components.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Under the revisions introduced for the Hungarian Grand Prix, a car must pit or be retired immediately if an issue arises that is deemed serious enough to warrant intervention from the team.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The revised regulations were poorly received, with <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> being particularly critical during interviews.
</p><p>After introducing "baguette" kerbs at the Austrian Grand Prix as a means of policing track limits and prevent drivers from deliberately running wide to gain an advantage, the Hungarian Grand Prix saw the introduction of electronic monitoring at selected corners, with pressure-sensitive plates placed under kerbs to detect cars running wide, with the system tied to the car transponders to detect when a driver had run too wide.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
