<p>The <b>1965 Mexican Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held at <a href="/wiki/Autodromo_Hermanos_Rodriguez" class="mw-redirect" title="Autodromo Hermanos Rodriguez">Ciudad Deportiva Magdalena Mixhuca</a> in <a href="/wiki/Mexico_City" title="Mexico City">Mexico City</a> on October 24, 1965. It was race 10 of 10 in both the <a href="/wiki/1965_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1965 World Championship of Drivers">1965 World Championship of Drivers</a> and the <a href="/wiki/1965_International_Cup_for_Formula_One_Manufacturers" class="mw-redirect" title="1965 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers">1965 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers</a>. The race was won by <a href="/wiki/Richie_Ginther" title="Richie Ginther">Richie Ginther</a>, who took his first victory, first for the <a href="/wiki/Honda_Racing_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Honda Racing F1">Honda</a> team and, excluding the Indianapolis 500, first win for the non-European team, after leading for the entire race. The <a href="/wiki/Brabham" title="Brabham">Brabham</a>-<a href="/wiki/Coventry_Climax" title="Coventry Climax">Climax</a> of <a href="/wiki/Dan_Gurney" title="Dan Gurney">Dan Gurney</a> finished the race second and the <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Lotus</a>-<a href="/wiki/Coventry_Climax" title="Coventry Climax">Climax</a> of <a href="/wiki/Mike_Spence" title="Mike Spence">Mike Spence</a> completed the podium.   
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_report">Race report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1965_Mexican_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Race report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Mexican Grand Prix provided a host of new records - the last race of the 1500cc era was the only one of the season not to be won by a British-powered car and also the only race not to be won by a British driver. <a href="/wiki/Honda_Racing_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Honda Racing F1">Honda</a>'s testing proved to be of benefit as <a href="/wiki/Jim_Clark" title="Jim Clark">Jim Clark</a> and <a href="/wiki/Graham_Hill" title="Graham Hill">Graham Hill</a> dropped out with engine problems and <a href="/wiki/Jackie_Stewart" title="Jackie Stewart">Jackie Stewart</a> retired with clutch problems to leave <a href="/wiki/Dan_Gurney" title="Dan Gurney">Dan Gurney</a> as the only challenger to <a href="/wiki/Richie_Ginther" title="Richie Ginther">Richie Ginther</a>. <a href="/wiki/Lorenzo_Bandini" title="Lorenzo Bandini">Lorenzo Bandini</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Rodr%C3%ADguez_(racing_driver)" title="Pedro Rodríguez (racing driver)">Pedro Rodríguez</a> collided at the hairpin, leaving Ginther to stroll home for Honda's first, and his only, <i>Grand Prix</i> win. <a href="/wiki/Goodyear_Tire_and_Rubber_Company" title="Goodyear Tire and Rubber Company">Goodyear</a> also bowed out of Formula One in winning form. Gurney and <a href="/wiki/Mike_Spence" title="Mike Spence">Mike Spence</a> took the other podium places, with <a href="/wiki/Jo_Siffert" title="Jo Siffert">Jo Siffert</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ronnie_Bucknum" title="Ronnie Bucknum">Ronnie Bucknum</a> and <a href="/wiki/Richard_Attwood" title="Richard Attwood">Richard Attwood</a> completing the points scorers.
</p>
