<p>The <b>2006 British Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 11 June 2006 at the <a href="/wiki/Silverstone_Circuit" title="Silverstone Circuit">Silverstone Circuit</a> in Northamptonshire, England. The 60-lap race was the eighth round of the <a href="/wiki/2006_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2006 Formula One season">2006 Formula One season</a>.
</p><p>Ticket sales were rather slow; the race was scheduled far earlier than normal, and local <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> had a rather poor season the previous year. When the race sold out in 2005, Button had been coming off one of his best years. Also, the weekend clashed with <a href="/wiki/2006_FIFA_World_Cup#Group_B" title="2006 FIFA World Cup">England's first World Cup game</a>. <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a> and <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a> both scored their final World Championship points by finishing in eighth and sixth respectively.
</p><p>Button had a very poor qualifying run and started the race 19th; after a blinding first few laps, Jenson's engine caught fire on lap 9, due to an oil leak that also caused the car to spin out of the race. Scotsman <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> also had a poor race suffering from understeer.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> became the first Spanish driver and the youngest driver (24 years, 10 months, 13 days) to get a <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_driver_records#Hat_Trick_(pole,_win_&amp;_fastest_lap_in_same_race)" title="List of Formula One driver records">hat trick</a> (pole position, winning and fastest lap in the same race). He fell one lap short of clinching a <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_driver_records#Grand_Chelem" title="List of Formula One driver records">Grand Chelem</a> (leading every lap, he would finally achieve this at the <a href="/wiki/2010_Singapore_Grand_Prix" title="2010 Singapore Grand Prix">2010 Singapore Grand Prix</a>).
</p><p>This race also featured the first ever pit stop to have involved a woman, during a <a href="/wiki/Midland_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Midland F1">Midland F1</a> pit stop for <a href="/wiki/Tiago_Monteiro" title="Tiago Monteiro">Tiago Monteiro</a>, <a href="/wiki/ITV_Sport" title="ITV Sport">ITV-F1</a>'s then pit-lane reporter <a href="/wiki/Louise_Goodman" title="Louise Goodman">Louise Goodman</a> was the left rear tyre changer.
</p><p>The only other noticeable incident of the race happened on the first lap, when Scott Speed pushed Ralf Schumacher's Toyota right in the path of Mark Webber at the entrance on the Hangar Straight. Schumacher and Webber retired on the spot, while Speed crawled to the pits and drove straight into the garage at the end of lap 1.
</p>
