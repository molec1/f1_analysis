<p>The <b>1990 Portuguese Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 23 September 1990 at <a href="/wiki/Aut%C3%B3dromo_do_Estoril" class="mw-redirect" title="Autódromo do Estoril">Autódromo do Estoril</a>. It was the thirteenth race of the <a href="/wiki/1990_FIA_Formula_One_World_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="1990 FIA Formula One World Championship">1990 FIA Formula One World Championship</a>. The race was the 19th <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_Grand_Prix" title="Portuguese Grand Prix">Portuguese Grand Prix</a> and the seventh to be held at Estoril. It was scheduled to be held over 71 laps of the 4.35-kilometre (2.70&#160;mi) circuit but was stopped after 61 laps, a race distance of 265.35 kilometres (164.88&#160;mi), after an accident involving the <a href="/wiki/Arrows_A11" title="Arrows A11">Arrows</a> of <a href="/wiki/Alex_Caffi" title="Alex Caffi">Alex Caffi</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Lola_LC90" title="Lola LC90">Lola</a> of <a href="/wiki/Aguri_Suzuki" title="Aguri Suzuki">Aguri Suzuki</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> took <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> in his <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_641" title="Ferrari 641">Ferrari</a> and was leading when the race was stopped, thus taking his only victory of the season and his last for the Ferrari team. Mansell finished 2.8 seconds ahead of Drivers' Championship leader <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> in the <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/5" title="McLaren MP4/5">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Honda_in_Formula_One" title="Honda in Formula One">Honda</a>, who in turn finished 1.3 seconds ahead of rival <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> in the other Ferrari. Senna thus extended his lead over Prost to 18 points with three races remaining.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1990_Portuguese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-qualifying_report">Pre-qualifying report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1990_Portuguese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Pre-qualifying report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Friday morning pre-qualifying session produced a similar result to the previous two races, with the same four cars pre-qualifying, albeit in a slightly different order. <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Grouillard" title="Olivier Grouillard">Olivier Grouillard</a> was fastest for the third time in a row in the <a href="/wiki/Osella" title="Osella">Osella</a>, half a second ahead of the <a href="/wiki/Automobiles_Gonfaronnaises_Sportives" title="Automobiles Gonfaronnaises Sportives">AGS</a> of <a href="/wiki/Yannick_Dalmas" title="Yannick Dalmas">Yannick Dalmas</a>. Now a regular pre-qualifier after the disastrous period with the <a href="/wiki/Subaru_1235" title="Subaru 1235">Subaru</a> engine, <a href="/wiki/Bertrand_Gachot" title="Bertrand Gachot">Bertrand Gachot</a> was third fastest in the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Coloni" title="Scuderia Coloni">Coloni</a>, nearly a second ahead of <a href="/wiki/Gabriele_Tarquini" title="Gabriele Tarquini">Gabriele Tarquini</a> in the other AGS.<sup id="cite_ref-Walker_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Walker-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>This left the usual trio missing out on pre-qualification. The two <a href="/wiki/EuroBrun" title="EuroBrun">EuroBruns</a> of <a href="/wiki/Robert_Moreno" title="Robert Moreno">Robert Moreno</a> and <a href="/wiki/Claudio_Langes" title="Claudio Langes">Claudio Langes</a> were again fifth and sixth, with Langes more than two seconds slower than his team-mate as the team began to lose touch with their rivals. The <a href="/wiki/Life_Racing_Engines" title="Life Racing Engines">Life</a> team had finally abandoned their unreliable and vastly underpowered <a href="/wiki/W12_engine" title="W12 engine">W12 engine</a>, and had fitted the L190 with a well-used <a href="/wiki/Judd_(engine)" title="Judd (engine)">Judd</a> CV V8. It had taken ten days to modify the chassis, and the car was now 80kg lighter. But the engine cover now did not fit properly, and it flew off on <a href="/wiki/Bruno_Giacomelli" title="Bruno Giacomelli">Bruno Giacomelli</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Shakedown_(testing)" title="Shakedown (testing)">shakedown</a> lap, halfway through the session. It was slightly damaged and the team had no replacement, so no further running was possible.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p><a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_Racing" title="Tyrrell Racing">Tyrrell</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Satoru_Nakajima" title="Satoru Nakajima">Satoru Nakajima</a> qualified 20th but withdrew due to <a href="/wiki/Influenza" title="Influenza">influenza</a> after crashing in the Sunday morning warm-up session. The grid was adjusted accordingly, although only 25 drivers started as no non-qualifier was reinstated.

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1990_Portuguese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_report">Race report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1990_Portuguese_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Race report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the start, with both Ferraris on the front row (Mansell on pole, <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> second), Mansell got too much wheelspin and slid across the track towards Prost, who had to avoid being shunted into the pitwall. All of this allowed the <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLarens</a> of <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> to slip past Mansell and Prost to make it a McLaren 1–2 with Senna leading into the first corner. After all the mid race tyre stops, it was Senna who emerged as the race leader, followed by Mansell, Berger, and Prost. A few laps later Mansell had caught Senna and passed him into turn one (Senna letting him through, unlike the year before when they collided).
</p><p>On lap 52, race leader <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> was attempting to lap the <a href="/wiki/Ligier_JS33B" class="mw-redirect" title="Ligier JS33B">Ligier JS33B</a> of <a href="/wiki/Philippe_Alliot" title="Philippe Alliot">Philippe Alliot</a> when the Frenchman stuck to the racing line by driving inches in front of Mansell into the inside of "Curva 2" corner. However, the right rear wheel of his car hit the left front of Mansell's Ferrari and Alliot was sent spinning into the <a href="/wiki/Armco_barrier" class="mw-redirect" title="Armco barrier">Armco barrier</a>. Mansell came out unscathed from this incident, and eventually won the race which was stopped early due to an accident between Suzuki and Caffi.
</p><p>Ferrari announced that <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a> would partner <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> for 1991. Alesi was reported to have already signed a contract with Williams, but changed his mind when Ferrari showed an interest, feeling that this would give him a better chance of winning a championship.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2009)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
