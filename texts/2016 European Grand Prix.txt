<p>The <b>2016 European Grand Prix</b> (formally known as the <b>2016 Formula 1 Grand Prix of Europe</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Auto_racing" title="Auto racing">motor race</a> that was held on 19 June 2016 at the <a href="/wiki/Baku_City_Circuit" title="Baku City Circuit">Baku City Circuit</a> in <a href="/wiki/Baku" title="Baku">Baku</a>, <a href="/wiki/Azerbaijan" title="Azerbaijan">Azerbaijan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-calendar_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-calendar-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The race was the eighth round of the <a href="/wiki/2016_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2016 Formula One season">2016 season</a>, and marked the twenty-third running of the <a href="/wiki/European_Grand_Prix" title="European Grand Prix">European Grand Prix</a> as a round of the Formula One World Championship. It was the first time that the race has been held at the circuit and the first time that a Grand Prix has been held in Azerbaijan.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> entered the round holding a nine-point lead in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">World Drivers' Championship</a> ahead of teammate <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>. Their team, <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a>, came in leading <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> by seventy-six points in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">World Constructors' Championship</a>. Rosberg went on to win the race—his first race win since the <a href="/wiki/2016_Russian_Grand_Prix" title="2016 Russian Grand Prix">Russian Grand Prix</a>—and extended his championship lead to twenty-four points. The result also marked Rosberg's second career <a href="/wiki/Grand_Slam_(Formula_One)" class="mw-redirect" title="Grand Slam (Formula One)">Grand Slam</a>. <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> finished second, with <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a> completing the podium. In the Constructors' Championship, Mercedes extended their lead over Ferrari to eighty-one points.
</p><p>Valtteri Bottas recorded the highest ever clocked speed in an official Formula One session, at 378 kilometres per hour (235&#160;mph) during qualifying, breaking the previous record of 369.6 kilometres per hour (229.7&#160;mph), set by <a href="/wiki/Ant%C3%B4nio_Pizzonia" title="Antônio Pizzonia">Antônio Pizzonia</a> at <a href="/wiki/Autodromo_Nazionale_Monza" class="mw-redirect" title="Autodromo Nazionale Monza">Monza</a> during the <a href="/wiki/2004_Italian_Grand_Prix" title="2004 Italian Grand Prix">2004 Italian Grand Prix</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2016_European_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2016_European_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Going into the weekend, <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> led the Drivers' Championship with 116 points, nine ahead of teammate <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>. <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> followed in third with 78 points, six clear of <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a>, followed by <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> with 69.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In the Constructors' standings, <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a> led the field with 223 points, ahead of <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> with 147. Third was <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull Racing</a> with 130 points, with <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> a further 49 points behind.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations">Preparations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2016_European_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Preparations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Barriers_in_Baku_for_Formula_1_Grand_Prox_2016.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ae/Barriers_in_Baku_for_Formula_1_Grand_Prox_2016.jpg/220px-Barriers_in_Baku_for_Formula_1_Grand_Prox_2016.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="123" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ae/Barriers_in_Baku_for_Formula_1_Grand_Prox_2016.jpg/330px-Barriers_in_Baku_for_Formula_1_Grand_Prox_2016.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ae/Barriers_in_Baku_for_Formula_1_Grand_Prox_2016.jpg/440px-Barriers_in_Baku_for_Formula_1_Grand_Prox_2016.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3264" data-file-height="1832" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Barriers_in_Baku_for_Formula_1_Grand_Prox_2016.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Barriers erected in the streets of Baku for the race</div></div></div>
<p>Plans to hold a Formula One race in Azerbaijan were first announced by <a href="/wiki/Bernie_Ecclestone" title="Bernie Ecclestone">Bernie Ecclestone</a> – the sport's commercial rights holder – in May 2014, when he declared that the race in Baku would replace the <a href="/wiki/Korean_Grand_Prix" title="Korean Grand Prix">Korean Grand Prix</a> for the <a href="/wiki/2015_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2015 Formula One season">2015 season</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The government of Azerbaijan confirmed the plans in July of the same year, declaring that the race would be held under the title <a href="/wiki/European_Grand_Prix" title="European Grand Prix">Grand Prix of Europe</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> At the same time, the organisers announced that the debut in Baku would be delayed until <a href="/wiki/2016_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2016 Formula One World Championship">2016</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Hermann_Tilke" title="Hermann Tilke">Hermann Tilke</a>, the designer of the street circuit, released first pictures of the planned venue in October 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Even though the race faced criticism by human rights activists, citing the government's attitude towards civil liberties, Ecclestone gave the go-ahead for the race in April 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> The Grand Prix in Baku was the second to be held in the former <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a>, following the <a href="/wiki/Russian_Grand_Prix" title="Russian Grand Prix">Russian Grand Prix</a> at <a href="/wiki/Sochi" title="Sochi">Sochi</a>, first held in <a href="/wiki/2014_Russian_Grand_Prix" title="2014 Russian Grand Prix">2014</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The circuit passed its final inspection in May 2016 with FIA Race Director <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Whiting" title="Charlie Whiting">Charlie Whiting</a> declaring his satisfaction that the circuit would be ready in time for the race.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> The circuit attracted some criticism from drivers who highlighted a lack of run-off in some corners and the tight pit entry positioned at one of the fastest points on the circuit.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Following problems with loose <a href="/wiki/Curb" title="Curb">kerbs</a> in free practice, changes were made in turns 6 and 12, where the kerbs were replaced by painted markings on the track.<sup id="cite_ref-kerbs_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kerbs-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Further revisions were made to the pit entry ahead of qualifying, extending the white line denoting the entry lane to allow drivers more time to react to cars entering the pit.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Tyres">Tyres</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2016_European_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Tyres">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>After providing teams with the ultrasoft compound for the <a href="/wiki/2016_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="2016 Monaco Grand Prix">Monaco</a> and <a href="/wiki/2016_Canadian_Grand_Prix" title="2016 Canadian Grand Prix">Canadian Grands Prix</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> tyre supplier <a href="/wiki/Pirelli" title="Pirelli">Pirelli</a> made the supersoft, soft and medium compounds available for the race despite the ultrasoft compound being specifically designed for <a href="/wiki/Street_circuit" title="Street circuit">street circuits</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Pirelli explained the decision not to provide the ultrasoft tyres as being a result of a lack of available performance data on the circuit,<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> while most teams opted for a full complement of soft and supersoft tyres and taking only the minimum number of medium compound tyres dictated by the rules.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> In preparing for the race, Pirelli acknowledged concerns about the circuit's high speed straights and direction changes causing "standing waves" over longer runs, a phenomenon whereby the energy passing through the wheel is strong enough to shift the build of the tyre, compromising its rigidity and increasing the likelihood of tyre failures.<sup id="cite_ref-standing_waves_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-standing_waves-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Further concerns were raised about the impact of the temporary surface used to protect the cobblestones through the <a href="/wiki/Old_City_(Baku)" title="Old City (Baku)">Old City</a> section on the tyres, amid fears that predicted high temperatures throughout the weekend would cause this surface to break up.<sup id="cite_ref-standing_waves_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-standing_waves-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Following the first free practice session, it was found that the metal bolts used to anchor segments of kerbing to the roadway had started working loose and had been cutting into the rubber of the tyres, necessitating minor modifications to the circuit in order to correct the issue.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Drag_reduction_system">Drag reduction system</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2016_European_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Drag reduction system">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The race saw the use of two <a href="/wiki/Drag_reduction_system" title="Drag reduction system">drag reduction system</a> (DRS) zones, with the first positioned along the main straight and the second located on the parallel back straight between turns 2 and 3.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Support_events">Support events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2016_European_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Support events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The circuit also hosted the <a href="/wiki/2016_Baku_GP2_Series_round" title="2016 Baku GP2 Series round">third round</a> of the <a href="/wiki/2016_GP2_Series" title="2016 GP2 Series">2016 GP2 Series</a> championship as a supporting event.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversies">Controversies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2016_European_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Controversies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Following the initial announcement of its revival, the race was subject to criticism in light of Azerbaijan's <a href="/wiki/Human_rights_in_Azerbaijan" title="Human rights in Azerbaijan">human rights record</a> as the race was established with the financial support of the Azerbaijani government.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> On 7 June 2016, the organisation Sport for Rights called for Bernie Ecclestone to speak out about the human rights situation in the country, having written an open letter to him to which Ecclestone had not replied. Sport for Rights made it clear that they did not call for a cancellation of the race, but instead urged the sport to use the race to promote these issues in public.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Ecclestone replied a week later, saying that Formula One had "a clear conscience" on human rights and added: "I tell you what we ought to do. As far as we are concerned, not have any races where there is corruption in the country. Can you tell me where we are going to be racing?"<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>With the race scheduled for 19 June, the event conflicted with the conclusion of the <a href="/wiki/2016_24_Hours_of_Le_Mans" title="2016 24 Hours of Le Mans">2016 24 Hours of Le Mans</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a>, who won the <a href="/wiki/2015_24_Hours_of_Le_Mans" title="2015 24 Hours of Le Mans">2015 race</a> with <a href="/wiki/Porsche_919_Hybrid" title="Porsche 919 Hybrid">Porsche</a> would not be able to return and defend his title, leading to accusations that <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_Management" class="mw-redirect" title="Formula One Management">Formula One Management</a> had deliberately scheduled the race to conflict with Le Mans and prevent Formula One drivers from participating.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> In May 2016, the Azerbaijan government decided to abandon the use of <a href="/wiki/Daylight_saving_time" title="Daylight saving time">daylight saving time</a>, prompting event organisers to change the start time of the race. The revised start time negated the clash between the start of the Grand Prix and the race in Le Mans.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Free_practice">Free practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2016_European_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Free practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Baku-F1-Street-Circuit-Openstreetmaps-rev1.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/76/Baku-F1-Street-Circuit-Openstreetmaps-rev1.png/220px-Baku-F1-Street-Circuit-Openstreetmaps-rev1.png" decoding="async" width="220" height="124" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/76/Baku-F1-Street-Circuit-Openstreetmaps-rev1.png/330px-Baku-F1-Street-Circuit-Openstreetmaps-rev1.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/76/Baku-F1-Street-Circuit-Openstreetmaps-rev1.png/440px-Baku-F1-Street-Circuit-Openstreetmaps-rev1.png 2x" data-file-width="1100" data-file-height="620" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Baku-F1-Street-Circuit-Openstreetmaps-rev1.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Layout of the Baku City Circuit around the old city part (left) and modern Baku area (right)</div></div></div> 
<p>Per the regulations for the <a href="/wiki/2016_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2016 Formula One World Championship">2016</a> season, two 90-minute practice sessions were held on Friday and another 60-minute session was held before qualifying on Saturday.<sup id="cite_ref-pracreg_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pracreg-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> In the first ever session on the new track on Friday morning, Lewis Hamilton set the fastest time at 1:46.435. He was followed by his Mercedes teammate Rosberg almost four-tenths of a second back, with <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a> third for <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a>, also within one second of Hamilton. <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> was an encouraging fourth for <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>. Both Bottas and Alonso set their fastest times on the soft rather than the supersoft tyre compound used by the Mercedes drivers. Several drivers got caught out on the new track, the first being <a href="/wiki/Esteban_Guti%C3%A9rrez" title="Esteban Gutiérrez">Esteban Gutiérrez</a>, who was forced into the run-off area at turn 15. Hamilton also had an incident, touching the barrier at turn 3 and flat-spotting his tyres later on the lap. The biggest incident occurred when <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> crashed his <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_RB12" title="Red Bull RB12">Red Bull RB12</a> at the exit of turn 15, losing one of his rear wheels and causing an eleven-minute <a href="/wiki/Red_flag_(racing)" class="mw-redirect" title="Red flag (racing)">red flag</a> period.<sup id="cite_ref-fp1_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fp1-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Hamilton was again fastest in the second session on Friday afternoon, edging out Rosberg by almost seven-tenths of a second. Rosberg's running was limited as he coasted to a halt on track with twenty minutes of the session left to go due to losing drive. Ferrari was not competitive in eighth and thirteenth place for Vettel and Räikkönen respectively, with both cars suffering from problems with their energy recovery unit. The top five cars were powered by Mercedes, as <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a> led the charge behind Mercedes in third, ahead of Bottas and <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a>. Fernando Alonso managed eleventh place, but had to spend considerable time in the garage while his team worked on his car. <a href="/wiki/Marcus_Ericsson" title="Marcus Ericsson">Marcus Ericsson</a> also had limited track time as a problem with his exhaust pipe forced him to miss the early part of the session. With loose <a href="/wiki/Curb" title="Curb">kerbs</a> detected during the GP2 qualifying session, drivers were instructed to stay clear of the kerbs at turn 6. Once more, many drivers had minor incidents on track, with some having to take to the run-off area at turn 3, while Gutiérrez touched the barrier at turn 8. <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Sainz_Jr." title="Carlos Sainz Jr.">Carlos Sainz Jr.</a> spun at the first corner, but nevertheless finished the session in sixth, the fastest runner without a Mercedes power unit.<sup id="cite_ref-fp2_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fp2-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the third session on Saturday morning, Hamilton topped the timesheets once again, setting a time of 1:44.352, a quarter of a second clear of teammate Rosberg. Hülkenberg was third for <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a> ahead of Ricciardo and Vettel. The session saw several incidents: Bottas damaged the floor of his car when he ran over a drain cover on his installation lap and was forced to miss the rest of the session; <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> made contact with the wall, but was later able to rejoin practice; Räikkönen's track time was limited as well due to a loss of power; and a red flag period occurred right after the <a href="/wiki/Racing_flags#The_chequered_flag" title="Racing flags">chequered flag</a> fell as Pérez crashed at turn 15.<sup id="cite_ref-fp3_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fp3-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2016_European_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Bottas_Baku_2016.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b6/Bottas_Baku_2016.jpg/220px-Bottas_Baku_2016.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="124" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b6/Bottas_Baku_2016.jpg/330px-Bottas_Baku_2016.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b6/Bottas_Baku_2016.jpg/440px-Bottas_Baku_2016.jpg 2x" data-file-width="800" data-file-height="450" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Bottas_Baku_2016.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>In qualifying, <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a> reached the highest speed ever recorded for a Formula One car, at 378&#160;km/h (235&#160;mph).</div></div></div>
<p>The first period of qualifying (Q1) ran for eighteen minutes and set positions seventeen to twenty-two on the grid.<sup id="cite_ref-pracreg_33-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pracreg-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Nico Rosberg was the fastest at the end of the session, which saw <a href="/wiki/Manor_F1_Team" class="mw-redirect" title="Manor F1 Team">MRT</a> drivers <a href="/wiki/Rio_Haryanto" title="Rio Haryanto">Rio Haryanto</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pascal_Wehrlein" title="Pascal Wehrlein">Pascal Wehrlein</a> eliminated in seventeenth and eighteenth place, their best qualifying performance of the season. <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> qualified nineteenth, after running wide at turn 15 on his first flying lap and aborting his second after encountering traffic. <a href="/wiki/Marcus_Ericsson" title="Marcus Ericsson">Marcus Ericsson</a> finished twentieth overall, ahead of the <a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renaults</a> of <a href="/wiki/Kevin_Magnussen" title="Kevin Magnussen">Kevin Magnussen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jolyon_Palmer" title="Jolyon Palmer">Jolyon Palmer</a>, who struggled with a lack of power on the circuit's long straights.<sup id="cite_ref-qualireport_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualireport-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The second qualifying period was fifteen minutes in length, and was used to set positions eleven through to sixteen.<sup id="cite_ref-pracreg_33-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pracreg-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> It was once again topped by Nico Rosberg, who was the first person to set a time under 1:43.000 all weekend. Meanwhile, Lewis Hamilton locked his brakes on the approach to turn 7, damaging his race tyres. Hamilton narrowly avoided elimination, setting a time good enough to proceed on his last attempt. <a href="/wiki/Romain_Grosjean" title="Romain Grosjean">Romain Grosjean</a> was eliminated in eleventh, having overcome issues with his brake balance. <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a> was a surprise elimination in twelfth, having run consistently at the front of the field throughout the weekend. His elimination was attributed to a miscommunication between himself and his engineer over the amount of fuel he had on board. <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Sainz_Jr." title="Carlos Sainz Jr.">Carlos Sainz Jr.</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> finished thirteenth and fourteenth to fill the seventh row of the grid, ahead of Gutiérrez.<sup id="cite_ref-qualireport_37-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualireport-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Nico_Rosberg_2016_British_GP.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/46/Nico_Rosberg_2016_British_GP.jpg/220px-Nico_Rosberg_2016_British_GP.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="118" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/46/Nico_Rosberg_2016_British_GP.jpg/330px-Nico_Rosberg_2016_British_GP.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/46/Nico_Rosberg_2016_British_GP.jpg/440px-Nico_Rosberg_2016_British_GP.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2252" data-file-height="1204" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Nico_Rosberg_2016_British_GP.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> <i>(pictured at the <a href="/wiki/2016_British_Grand_Prix" title="2016 British Grand Prix">British Grand Prix</a>)</i> started from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> and went on to win the race for <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_in_Formula_One" title="Mercedes-Benz in Formula One">Mercedes</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>The third and final session was twelve minutes long.<sup id="cite_ref-pracreg_33-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pracreg-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a> set the early pace<sup id="cite_ref-qualireport_37-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualireport-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> before Hamilton made a mistake, also impeding Rosberg's first flying lap.<sup id="cite_ref-qualibbc_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualibbc-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> Rosberg surpassed Pérez's lap time to claim provisional pole while Hamilton crashed out, clipping the barrier on the inside of turn 10 and breaking his front suspension.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> The session was immediately red-flagged while Hamilton's car was cleared away, and resumed with just two minutes remaining. This left eight drivers – with the exception of the crashed Hamilton and Rosberg, who elected not to go out – to fight for positions on the grid. <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Ricciardo" title="Daniel Ricciardo">Daniel Ricciardo</a> led the column of cars out and set a lap time good enough for third place; <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> matched his lap time to the thousandth of a second, and was classified fourth, as Ricciardo had set his lap time first. <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> finished fifth, ahead of <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a>. <a href="/wiki/Daniil_Kvyat" title="Daniil Kvyat">Daniil Kvyat</a> out-qualified teammate Sainz in seventh, the first time that he had out-qualified a teammate in 2016. <a href="/wiki/Valtteri_Bottas" title="Valtteri Bottas">Valtteri Bottas</a> overcame the damage to his car and lack of running during free practice to finish eighth alongside Verstappen, the two having been involved in a series of on-track altercations throughout qualifying. Hamilton finished tenth, with his time of over two minutes having been recorded when he took to the escape road on his first flying lap.<sup id="cite_ref-qualireport_37-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualireport-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> During qualifying, Bottas reached the highest ever recorded speed in an official Formula One session, at 378&#160;km/h (235&#160;mph),<sup id="cite_ref-stats_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-stats-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> exceeding the previous record of 369.6&#160;km/h (229.7&#160;mph) set by <a href="/wiki/Ant%C3%B4nio_Pizzonia" title="Antônio Pizzonia">Antônio Pizzonia</a> at the <a href="/wiki/2004_Italian_Grand_Prix" title="2004 Italian Grand Prix">2004 Italian Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-qualifying">Post-qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2016_European_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Post-qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Sergio Pérez was demoted to seventh with the application of a gearbox penalty, following a change made necessary after he hit the barrier in turn 15 during the last practice session. This promoted Ricciardo, Vettel, Räikkönen, Massa, and Kvyat up the grid in the process.<sup id="cite_ref-perez_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-perez-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> Sainz and Magnussen were also demoted, with Magnussen having to start from the pit lane.<sup id="cite_ref-StartingGrid_43-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-StartingGrid-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> Lewis Hamilton was given permission to change his front left tyre ahead of the race. Under the regulations, a driver who qualifies in the top ten must start the race on the set of tyres they used to record their best time in the second part of qualifying. However, Hamilton's front left tyre, which belonged to the set of tyres that he subsequently used to set his fastest time during his next flying lap, was deemed to be too damaged following his lock-up going into turn seven, necessitating its change on safety grounds.<sup id="cite_ref-qualibbc_38-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-qualibbc-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2016_European_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Start_backshot_Baku_2016.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/38/Start_backshot_Baku_2016.jpg/220px-Start_backshot_Baku_2016.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="124" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/38/Start_backshot_Baku_2016.jpg/330px-Start_backshot_Baku_2016.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/38/Start_backshot_Baku_2016.jpg/440px-Start_backshot_Baku_2016.jpg 2x" data-file-width="800" data-file-height="450" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Start_backshot_Baku_2016.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The start of the race</div></div></div>
<p>At the start of the race, Rosberg retained his lead ahead of Ricciardo, followed by Vettel and Räikkönen. <a href="/wiki/Sergio_P%C3%A9rez" title="Sergio Pérez">Sergio Pérez</a> gained two places and was fifth, while <a href="/wiki/Esteban_Guti%C3%A9rrez" title="Esteban Gutiérrez">Esteban Gutiérrez</a> damaged his front wing when he made contact with the back of <a href="/wiki/Nico_H%C3%BClkenberg" title="Nico Hülkenberg">Nico Hülkenberg</a>'s car. Hamilton remained tenth until he overtook Kvyat for ninth place on lap four. One lap later, both Bottas and Hamilton seized an opportunity to overtake <a href="/wiki/Max_Verstappen" title="Max Verstappen">Max Verstappen</a>, as he ran wide into turn two. As a reaction, Red Bull decided to change tyres on Verstappen's car on the following lap. Another lap later, Ricciardo followed suit. At Red Bull's sister team <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso</a>, Daniil Kvyat retired on lap eight. On lap ten, Hamilton overtook Bottas for fourth place, but was more than twenty seconds behind teammate Rosberg. He went into the pit lane for a tyre change on lap 15. <a href="/wiki/Force_India" title="Force India">Force India</a> reacted two laps later and brought Pérez in and he emerged back from the pit lane just ahead of Hamilton. Meanwhile, the order at the front was Rosberg, Vettel, Bottas, Ricciardo and Räikkönen. On lap 18, Räikkönen took fourth place by overtaking Ricciardo on the start/finish straight. Pérez moved ahead of Massa into seventh on lap 20, and Hamilton followed through one lap later, while Bottas pitted and returned to the track in ninth place.<sup id="cite_ref-ticker_45-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ticker-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Second placed Sebastian Vettel came into the pit lane at the end of lap 21 and dropped to third behind Ricciardo. Race leader Rosberg made his stop one lap later, retaining the lead. On lap 23, both Pérez and Hamilton passed Ricciardo, who came in for his second tyre change at the end of the lap and dropped back to 13th place. Aided by pit stops in front of him and a timely overtake on <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Sainz_Jr." title="Carlos Sainz Jr.">Carlos Sainz Jr.</a> on lap 27, he moved back into ninth shortly thereafter. Two laps later, Räikkönen let teammate Vettel pass him for second place, albeit 18 seconds down on race leader Rosberg. Starting by lap 32, Hamilton suffered from problems with his car, being stuck in a wrong engine mode.<sup id="cite_ref-mercengine_46-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mercengine-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> Due to the regulations stating that drivers had to drive the vehicles "unaided", the team was not allowed to help him. Another lap later, the race ended for Sainz due to a suspension problem. On lap 42, <a href="/wiki/Pascal_Wehrlein" title="Pascal Wehrlein">Pascal Wehrlein</a> retired as well with brake failure. By lap 44, Hamilton had sorted out his issues and set the fastest lap of the race while in fifth place. In front of him the order was Rosberg, Vettel, Räikkönen and Pérez.<sup id="cite_ref-ticker_45-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ticker-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> One lap later, <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> retired in the pit lane due to gearbox issues,<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> after having been passed by multiple drivers in the preceding laps.<sup id="cite_ref-ticker_45-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ticker-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As Rosberg was comfortably in the lead, still 18 seconds ahead of Vettel, Verstappen took ninth place from Massa going into turn one on lap 46. Red Bull teammate Ricciardo also gained a place, at Hülkenberg's expense, at the same spot three laps later. With Räikkönen facing a five-second time penalty for crossing the pit entry line, Pérez behind him would not have to pass him to achieve third place as long as he stayed within five seconds of him. Pérez nevertheless made the move on the final lap to finish in the final podium position. Meanwhile, Nico Rosberg crossed the line to win the Grand Prix, more than 16 seconds ahead of Sebastian Vettel.<sup id="cite_ref-ticker_45-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ticker-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2016_European_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Lewis_Hamilton_2016_Malaysia_1.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6f/Lewis_Hamilton_2016_Malaysia_1.jpg/220px-Lewis_Hamilton_2016_Malaysia_1.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="330" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6f/Lewis_Hamilton_2016_Malaysia_1.jpg/330px-Lewis_Hamilton_2016_Malaysia_1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6f/Lewis_Hamilton_2016_Malaysia_1.jpg/440px-Lewis_Hamilton_2016_Malaysia_1.jpg 2x" data-file-width="800" data-file-height="1200" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Lewis_Hamilton_2016_Malaysia_1.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> <i>(pictured at the <a href="/wiki/2016_Malaysian_Grand_Prix" title="2016 Malaysian Grand Prix">Malaysian Grand Prix</a>)</i> criticised team radio rules after being unable to fix a problem on his car for twelve laps.</div></div></div>
<p>At the podium interview, conducted by former team owner <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Jordan" title="Eddie Jordan">Eddie Jordan</a>, Rosberg hailed an "amazing weekend" for himself, saying that he was "ecstatic" about the victory, but refused to answer questions concerning the outcome of the World Championship. Second-placed Vettel praised the track and his team's effort in preparing the car after difficulties in Friday's free practice. Sergio Pérez emphasised his recovery after his gearbox penalty, which put him back five places on the starting grid and the satisfaction of overtaking Räikkönen on the last lap of the race. During the following press conference, Vettel expressed surprise at the fact that no <a href="/wiki/Safety_car" title="Safety car">safety car</a> period had emerged over the course of the race.<sup id="cite_ref-pressconf_48-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pressconf-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Fifth-placed Hamilton, who had been stuck in a wrong engine mode for twelve laps<sup id="cite_ref-mercengine_46-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mercengine-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> and was at first unable to resolve the issue without the help of his team, attacked the revised radio rules, stating: "The rule needs to be looked at again because it is a technical issue." He stressed that the ban made the sport more dangerous. Several drivers, such as Fernando Alonso and Sebastian Vettel, joined him in his criticism.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> It transpired after the race that Rosberg had had the same problem, a couple of laps after it occurred on Hamilton's car, but was able to solve it faster than his teammate.<sup id="cite_ref-mercengine_46-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mercengine-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Following his pit lane entry violation during the race, which handed him a five-second time penalty, Kimi Räikkönen was furthermore given two penalty points to his <a href="/wiki/FIA_Super_Licence" title="FIA Super Licence">licence</a>, bringing him to five overall.<sup id="cite_ref-räikkönen_50-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-räikkönen-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As a result of the race, Rosberg extended his lead in the Drivers' Championship to 24 points over Hamilton, with Vettel a further 21 points behind in third.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> In the Constructors' standings, Mercedes retained their lead with 258 points, followed by Ferrari on 177 and Red Bull with 140 points.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
