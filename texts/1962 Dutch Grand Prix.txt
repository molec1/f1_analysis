<p>The <b>1962 Dutch Grand Prix</b> was the eleventh time the <a href="/wiki/Dutch_Grand_Prix" title="Dutch Grand Prix">Dutch Grand Prix</a> (or Grote Prijs van Nederland) motor race was held. The race also held the honorary designation of the 22nd <a href="/wiki/European_Grand_Prix" title="European Grand Prix">European Grand Prix</a>. It was run to <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> regulations on 20 May 1962 as race 1 of 9 in both the <a href="/wiki/1962_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1962 World Championship of Drivers">1962 World Championship of Drivers</a> and the <a href="/wiki/1962_International_Cup_for_Formula_One_Manufacturers" class="mw-redirect" title="1962 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers">1962 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers</a>. It was held over 80 laps of the compact 2.6 mile <a href="/wiki/Circuit_Park_Zandvoort" class="mw-redirect" title="Circuit Park Zandvoort">Circuit Park Zandvoort</a> for a race distance of just over 200 miles.
</p><p>It was won by British driver <a href="/wiki/Graham_Hill" title="Graham Hill">Graham Hill</a> driving a <a href="/wiki/BRM_P57" title="BRM P57">BRM P57</a>. It was the first Grand Prix victory for the future dual-World Champion and the second time a <a href="/wiki/British_Racing_Motors" title="British Racing Motors">BRM</a> driver had won the race after <a href="/wiki/Jo_Bonnier" title="Jo Bonnier">Jo Bonnier</a> in <a href="/wiki/1959_Dutch_Grand_Prix" title="1959 Dutch Grand Prix">1959</a>. Hill finished over 27 seconds ahead of <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Team Lotus</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Trevor_Taylor_(racing_driver)" title="Trevor Taylor (racing driver)">Trevor Taylor</a> driving a <a href="/wiki/Lotus_24" title="Lotus 24">Lotus 24</a>. The reigning World Champion, Ferrari's <a href="/wiki/Phil_Hill" title="Phil Hill">Phil Hill</a> (<a href="/wiki/Ferrari_156" class="mw-redirect" title="Ferrari 156">Ferrari 156</a>) completed the podium.
</p><p>The race provided an indication of the season to come as the long-maligned <a href="/wiki/British_Racing_Motors" title="British Racing Motors">British Racing Motors</a> organisation were on their way to their first and ultimately only constructor's championship. It also signalled Hill's own rise in the sport, having only stood on the podium once before, at the same circuit two years previously. He would win three more races this year and be crowned World Champion.
</p>
