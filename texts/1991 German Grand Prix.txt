<p>The <b>1991 German Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at <a href="/wiki/Hockenheimring" title="Hockenheimring">Hockenheimring</a> on 28 July 1991. It was the ninth round of the <a href="/wiki/1991_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="1991 Formula One season">1991 Formula One season</a>. The 45-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> after he started from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>. His teammate <a href="/wiki/Riccardo_Patrese" title="Riccardo Patrese">Riccardo Patrese</a> finished second with <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a> third. This was the first German Grand Prix to be held after the <a href="/wiki/German_reunification" title="German reunification">German reunification</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-race">Pre-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1991_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Pre-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ayrton Senna spent a night in a hospital in Mannheim after crashing during pre-race testing a week earlier. Senna suffered a tyre failure at the end of a long straight, causing the car to launch into the air and turn over several times. The McLaren-Honda went 15 feet into the air and was destroyed in the crash, according to witnesses. After regaining consciousness, Senna was taken to hospital with neck and chest bruising.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>There were two changes to the entry list, the first was at Lotus where <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Herbert" title="Johnny Herbert">Johnny Herbert</a> was replaced by young German <a href="/wiki/Michael_Bartels" title="Michael Bartels">Michael Bartels</a> because of the former's <a href="/wiki/Formula_Nippon" class="mw-redirect" title="Formula Nippon">Japanese Formula 3000</a> commitments, and the second was at <a href="/wiki/Footwork_Arrows" title="Footwork Arrows">Footwork</a> where <a href="/wiki/Alex_Caffi" title="Alex Caffi">Alex Caffi</a> was back in action after his road accident. Elsewhere <a href="/wiki/Satoru_Nakajima" title="Satoru Nakajima">Satoru Nakajima</a> announced he would retire at the end of the year.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1991_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-qualifying_report">Pre-qualifying report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1991_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Pre-qualifying report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The participants in the Friday morning pre-qualifying sessions were reshuffled prior to this event, the season having reached its mid-point. <a href="/wiki/BMS_Scuderia_Italia" title="BMS Scuderia Italia">Scuderia Italia (Dallara)</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a> had scored points, and were thus relieved of the requirement to pre-qualify, and could automatically join the rest of the field in the main qualifying sessions from here on. By virtue of <a href="/wiki/Nicola_Larini" title="Nicola Larini">Nicola Larini</a>'s seventh place finish at the first round in <a href="/wiki/1991_United_States_Grand_Prix" title="1991 United States Grand Prix">Phoenix</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Modena_(racing_team)" title="Modena (racing team)">Modena</a> team were also lifted out of pre-qualifying, despite struggling in the sessions at recent Grands Prix.<sup id="cite_ref-Walker_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Walker-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Taking their places during the Friday morning sessions were <a href="/wiki/Brabham" title="Brabham">Brabham</a>, <a href="/wiki/Automobiles_Gonfaronnaises_Sportives" title="Automobiles Gonfaronnaises Sportives">AGS</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Footwork_Arrows" title="Footwork Arrows">Footwork</a>, who had all failed to score points so far in 1991, or match Modena Lambo's seventh place finish at any race. <a href="/wiki/Fondmetal" title="Fondmetal">Fondmetal</a> and <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Coloni" title="Scuderia Coloni">Coloni</a> were also still required to pre-qualify.
</p><p>Here at Hockenheim, the fastest pre-qualifier was <a href="/wiki/Martin_Brundle" title="Martin Brundle">Martin Brundle</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Brabham_BT60" title="Brabham BT60">Brabham BT60Y</a>. He was over a second faster than the <a href="/w/index.php?title=AGS_JH25&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="AGS JH25 (page does not exist)">AGS JH25B</a> of <a href="/wiki/Gabriele_Tarquini" title="Gabriele Tarquini">Gabriele Tarquini</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Michele_Alboreto" title="Michele Alboreto">Michele Alboreto</a> just a tenth behind in the <a href="/wiki/Footwork_FA12" title="Footwork FA12">Footwork FA12C</a>, despite gearbox problems. The fourth pre-qualifier was the other Brabham of <a href="/wiki/Mark_Blundell" title="Mark Blundell">Mark Blundell</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Walker_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Walker-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The four entrants missing out included Fondmetal driver <a href="/wiki/Olivier_Grouillard" title="Olivier Grouillard">Olivier Grouillard</a>, who suffered an engine failure and finished fifth fastest, ahead of the second Footwork of <a href="/wiki/Alex_Caffi" title="Alex Caffi">Alex Caffi</a>, who had returned to the cockpit after missing four races. The second AGS of Italian <a href="/wiki/Fabrizio_Barbazza" title="Fabrizio Barbazza">Fabrizio Barbazza</a> was seventh, nearly a second ahead of regular backmarker <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Chaves" title="Pedro Chaves">Pedro Chaves</a> for the cash-strapped Coloni team.<sup id="cite_ref-Walker_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Walker-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying_report">Qualifying report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1991_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Qualifying report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In Saturday practice <a href="/wiki/%C3%89rik_Comas" title="Érik Comas">Érik Comas</a> had a massive accident in his <a href="/wiki/Equipe_Ligier" title="Equipe Ligier">Ligier</a>. The French driver was unhurt, but it raised questions about the safety of the second chicane. In Qualifying, <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> took pole from title rival <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a>. Gerhard Berger was third, followed by <a href="/wiki/Riccardo_Patrese" title="Riccardo Patrese">Riccardo Patrese</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Andrea_de_Cesaris" title="Andrea de Cesaris">Andrea de Cesaris</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a>, <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Moreno" title="Roberto Moreno">Roberto Moreno</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Pierluigi_Martini" title="Pierluigi Martini">Pierluigi Martini</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a>, taking full advantage of his Ferrari engine around the high speed circuit.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1991_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_report">Race report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1991_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Race report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On Sunday, a couple of hours before the race, there was a FIA driver's meeting and Senna requested to race director <a href="/wiki/Roland_Bruynseraede" title="Roland Bruynseraede">Roland Bruynseraede</a> that the tyre walls at the chicanes be replaced with traffic cones because of the possibly of hitting the tyres and rolling; that happened to him during qualifying for the <a href="/wiki/1991_Mexican_Grand_Prix" title="1991 Mexican Grand Prix">Mexican Grand Prix</a>, and this heated up when FIA president <a href="/wiki/Jean-Marie_Balestre" title="Jean-Marie Balestre">Jean-Marie Balestre</a>, Senna and a few other drivers had a brief argument over the regulations involving safety. Balestre then instigated a democratic vote, and the vote went towards removing the tyre walls and replacing them with traffic cones.
</p><p>At the start of the race, Mansell made a great start while Berger slotted into second ahead of team-mate Senna, with Prost, Patrese, and Alesi rounding out the top six. At the back Mark Blundell spun but continued, although Larini spun off into retirement in his attempt to avoid the Brabham. Berger made a bad pit-stop and fell back to tenth, while Prost started to reel in Senna. Mansell was running away at the front and when he pitted for tyres he dropped just behind Alesi, but did not waste time in changing the situation and passed Alesi two laps later to re-take the lead. While Mansell was surging away, a tremendous battle developed for third place between Senna, Prost, and Patrese, with Riccardo beating both men before setting off after Alesi. Senna and Prost continued to squabble over fourth and the major talking point came on lap 37 when Prost attempted to pass Senna going into the first chicane. Prost was faster and tried to go around the outside, Senna would not give way and Prost went off and proceeded to stall the engine. Prost blamed Senna and said he would not be so forgiving the next time while Senna accused Prost of complaining for the sake of complaining.  Prost's comments would earn him a one-race suspended ban, while the FIA ordered a sit-down meeting between the two men at the next race. Meanwhile, Mansell cruised to his third straight win, leading home Patrese, Alesi, Berger, de Cesaris, and Gachot, Senna having run out of fuel on the last lap for the second straight race and being classified seventh, allowing Mansell to close to within eight points of Senna in the drivers championship.
</p>
