<p>The <b>1989 Italian Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at <a href="/wiki/Autodromo_Nazionale_di_Monza" title="Autodromo Nazionale di Monza">Monza</a> on 10 September 1989. It was the twelfth race of the <a href="/wiki/1989_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="1989 Formula One season">1989 Formula One season</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> took his fourth and final win of the season after <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> teammate and pole-sitter <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> suffered an engine failure with nine laps to go, thus extending the Frenchman's lead over the Brazilian in the Drivers' Championship to 20 points. Knowing that he was moving to <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> for 1990, Prost dropped his winners' trophy from the podium into the crowd, to the severe disapproval of his team manager <a href="/wiki/Ron_Dennis" title="Ron Dennis">Ron Dennis</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a>, the man Prost would be replacing at Ferrari, recorded his first finish of the season with second, followed by the two <a href="/wiki/Williams_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Williams F1">Williams</a> of <a href="/wiki/Thierry_Boutsen" title="Thierry Boutsen">Thierry Boutsen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Riccardo_Patrese" title="Riccardo Patrese">Riccardo Patrese</a>. The final points went to <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_Racing" title="Tyrrell Racing">Tyrrell</a> and <a href="/wiki/Martin_Brundle" title="Martin Brundle">Martin Brundle</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Brabham" title="Brabham">Brabham</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-race">Pre-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1989_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Pre-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Before the Italian Grand Prix, Alain Prost was confirmed as a Ferrari driver from 1990. To the <a href="/wiki/Tifosi" title="Tifosi">Tifosi</a> who had previously seen Prost as the villain, he was now the hero of the crowd. It mattered not that he was still driving for <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>, he was a confirmed Ferrari driver now and was treated as such (in previous years he had been jostled, jeered, and even pelted with tomatoes by some of the Tifosi for daring to beat the Ferraris).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Prost's announcement allowed <a href="/wiki/Frank_Williams_(Formula_One)" title="Frank Williams (Formula One)">Frank Williams</a> to re-sign Riccardo Patrese for 1990 (Prost had offers from both Ferrari and Williams). <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Lotus</a> also announced at the Grand Prix that they would be using the <a href="/wiki/Lamborghini_V12_engine" class="mw-redirect" title="Lamborghini V12 engine">Lamborghini V12 engine</a> in 1990. They also confirmed that their 1990 drivers would be <a href="/wiki/Derek_Warwick" title="Derek Warwick">Derek Warwick</a> and the teams test driver <a href="/wiki/Martin_Donnelly_(racing_driver)" title="Martin Donnelly (racing driver)">Martin Donnelly</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1989_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-qualifying_report">Pre-qualifying report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1989_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Pre-qualifying report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>For the first time in six Grands Prix, <a href="/wiki/Onyx_(racing_team)" class="mw-redirect" title="Onyx (racing team)">Onyx</a> were not fastest in pre-qualifying. That credit went to <a href="/wiki/Larrousse" title="Larrousse">Larrousse</a>-<a href="/wiki/Lola_Cars" title="Lola Cars">Lola</a>, as their cars were first and second on Friday morning, with <a href="/wiki/Philippe_Alliot" title="Philippe Alliot">Philippe Alliot</a> ahead of <a href="/wiki/Michele_Alboreto" title="Michele Alboreto">Michele Alboreto</a>. Third was <a href="/wiki/Nicola_Larini" title="Nicola Larini">Nicola Larini</a> in the Osella, with <a href="/wiki/Bertrand_Gachot" title="Bertrand Gachot">Bertrand Gachot</a>'s Onyx fourth. Gachot's team-mate <a href="/wiki/Stefan_Johansson" title="Stefan Johansson">Stefan Johansson</a> missed out in fifth.
</p><p>Sixth was the <a href="/wiki/Automobiles_Gonfaronnaises_Sportives" title="Automobiles Gonfaronnaises Sportives">AGS</a> of <a href="/wiki/Gabriele_Tarquini" title="Gabriele Tarquini">Gabriele Tarquini</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Moreno" title="Roberto Moreno">Roberto Moreno</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Coloni" title="Scuderia Coloni">Coloni</a> seventh. Eighth was Larini's Osella team-mate <a href="/wiki/Piercarlo_Ghinzani" title="Piercarlo Ghinzani">Piercarlo Ghinzani</a>, his eleventh pre-qualifying failure this season. As at the previous meeting, the <a href="/wiki/Zakspeed" title="Zakspeed">Zakspeeds</a> were ninth and tenth, <a href="/wiki/Bernd_Schneider_(racing_driver)" title="Bernd Schneider (racing driver)">Bernd Schneider</a> again ahead of <a href="/wiki/Aguri_Suzuki" title="Aguri Suzuki">Aguri Suzuki</a>. Argentine <a href="/wiki/Oscar_Larrauri" title="Oscar Larrauri">Oscar Larrauri</a> had returned to <a href="/wiki/EuroBrun" title="EuroBrun">EuroBrun</a> for whom he raced in 1988, replacing Swiss driver <a href="/wiki/Gregor_Foitek" title="Gregor Foitek">Gregor Foitek</a>, but with no improvement down in eleventh. Twelfth was the other AGS of <a href="/wiki/Yannick_Dalmas" title="Yannick Dalmas">Yannick Dalmas</a>, ahead only of the second Coloni of <a href="/wiki/Enrico_Bertaggia" title="Enrico Bertaggia">Enrico Bertaggia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Walker_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Walker-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying_report">Qualifying report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1989_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Qualifying report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Predictably, the <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/5" title="McLaren MP4/5">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Honda_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Honda F1">Honda</a> of <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> was the fastest in qualifying for his 38th career pole position. Second was a surprise as Ferrari's Gerhard Berger joined Senna on the front row, whilst his teammate <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> was third. Prost was only 4th, some 1.79 seconds slower than his Brazilian teammate, publicly complaining all weekend of a down on power engine compared to Senna's.
</p><p>Prost's claims that Honda were favouring Senna were refuted by both Honda and McLaren boss Ron Dennis. However the Frenchman had allies in former Honda drivers Mansell and <a href="/wiki/1982_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1982 Formula One World Championship">1982</a> World Champion <a href="/wiki/Keke_Rosberg" title="Keke Rosberg">Keke Rosberg</a> who also told the press that when it became known they would not be driving Honda powered cars any more that their engines did not work as well or have as much power as was previous. <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> commentator <a href="/wiki/Murray_Walker" title="Murray Walker">Murray Walker</a> also reported during the race itself that Prost was over 7&#160;mph (11&#160;km/h) slower than Senna through the speed trap in the race morning warm-up session despite the two cars running similar wing settings.
</p><p>Senna's time of 1:23.720 in his <a href="/wiki/V10_engine" title="V10 engine">V10</a> McLaren-Honda was 2.254 seconds faster than he had been <a href="/wiki/1988_Italian_Grand_Prix" title="1988 Italian Grand Prix">a year earlier</a> in the turbocharged <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/4" title="McLaren MP4/4">McLaren MP4/4</a>. His time was only 0.26 seconds shy of the fastest ever lap of the Monza circuit set by <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a> in a <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW11" title="Williams FW11">Williams</a>-Honda in qualifying for the <a href="/wiki/1987_Italian_Grand_Prix" title="1987 Italian Grand Prix">1987 race</a> when the turbo engines were developing some 300&#160;bhp (224&#160;kW; 304&#160;PS) more than the <a href="/wiki/Naturally_aspirated_engine" title="Naturally aspirated engine">naturally aspirated engines</a> of 1989.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Eddie_Cheever" title="Eddie Cheever">Eddie Cheever</a>, who had finished 3rd in the 1988 race, failed to qualify his <a href="/wiki/Arrows_A11" title="Arrows A11">Arrows</a>-<a href="/wiki/Cosworth_DFR" class="mw-redirect" title="Cosworth DFR">Ford</a>. It was the second time in 1989 that the American had failed to qualify for a race. His teammate Derek Warwick qualified 16th. The <a href="/wiki/Brabham_BT58" title="Brabham BT58">Brabham</a>-<a href="/wiki/Judd_(engine)#CV" title="Judd (engine)">Judd</a> of <a href="/wiki/Stefano_Modena" title="Stefano Modena">Stefano Modena</a> was excluded from the meeting when his car was found to be underweight. This promoted the <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a> of <a href="/wiki/Luis_P%C3%A9rez-Sala" title="Luis Pérez-Sala">Luis Pérez-Sala</a> onto the grid.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1989_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_report">Race report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1989_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Race report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As the grid was in the process of forming up before the start, the McLaren team transferred the settings from Senna's car to Prost's in the hope of curing its handling problem (Prost had actually been more than 2 seconds slower in the race morning warm up than Senna). Prost would later say that while handling and grip were significantly improved, the down on power engine remained and despite the same wing settings he still could not match his teammate for straight line speed.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Senna led from the start and built up a small lead over Berger, while Mansell (whose 'development' <a href="/wiki/V12_engine" title="V12 engine">V12 engine</a> was not revving right) and especially Prost struggled to stay in touch. Indeed, in the early stages of the race Prost, who was getting used to his cars new set up, was having a hard time holding off the V10 <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW12" title="Williams FW12">Williams</a>-<a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a> of <a href="/wiki/Thierry_Boutsen" title="Thierry Boutsen">Thierry Boutsen</a>, though the BBC's <a href="/wiki/James_Hunt" title="James Hunt">James Hunt</a> explained that Prost was using a harder set of "B" compound <a href="/wiki/Goodyear_Tire_and_Rubber_Company" title="Goodyear Tire and Rubber Company">tyres</a> in the hope of not having to pit during the race.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Emanuele_Pirro" title="Emanuele Pirro">Emanuele Pirro</a> was the races first retirement, the transmission in his <a href="/wiki/Benetton_B189" title="Benetton B189">Benetton</a>-<a href="/wiki/Cosworth_DFR" class="mw-redirect" title="Cosworth DFR">Ford</a> not lasting a single lap. Only just longer was the <a href="/wiki/Lola_LC89" title="Lola LC89">Lola</a>-Lamborghini of <a href="/wiki/Philippe_Alliot" title="Philippe Alliot">Philippe Alliot</a>. The V12 powered Lolas of Alliot and <a href="/wiki/Michele_Alboreto" title="Michele Alboreto">Michele Alboreto</a> had easily been the fastest in pre-qualifying, and Alliot went on to qualify a fine 7th (ahead of the Benetton's) before spinning into the sand trap at <a href="/wiki/Alberto_Ascari" title="Alberto Ascari">Ascari</a> on just his second lap.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Alessandro_Nannini" title="Alessandro Nannini">Alessandro Nannini</a>'s Benetton lost its brakes on lap 33 putting him into retirement, while Nigel Mansell suffered gearbox failure on lap 41. Through all of this, Senna was still comfortably in the lead with Prost having fought his way past Berger into second following Mansell's retirement. Prost passed Berger in front of the pits and the main grandstand and this saw the unusual sight of the Tifosi cheering when a McLaren passed a Ferrari. It must be remembered though that Prost was a confirmed Ferrari driver for 1990 while Berger, the hero of the 1988 race, was leaving the Scuderia to take Prost's seat at McLaren. The Williams' pair of Boutsen and Patrese, having found the limits of the older FW12 model, were circulating in 4th and 5th places but at no stage threatened the leaders.
</p><p>On lap 44 the roar of the crowd told the story as the V10 Honda in Senna's McLaren comprehensively blew up going into the Parabolica, dumping its oil onto the rear tyres and sending Senna into a gentle spin and retirement, handing Prost the lead which he held to win his first Italian Grand Prix since <a href="/wiki/1985_Italian_Grand_Prix" title="1985 Italian Grand Prix">1985</a>. Berger finished second for not only his first points of the season but indeed his first race finish while Boutsen came home third. Despite Prost's engine complaints, he still managed to set the fastest lap of the race on lap 43.
</p><p>Prost's win restored his 20-point championship lead over Senna with only four races remaining in the season. However, due to the "Best 11" scoring system, Prost had now scored major points in 11 races while Senna could still score from all four remaining races. This meant that unless Prost won races, he would be able to score only a small number of points for the remainder of the season while Senna could conceivably score a maximum of 36 points (and win the championship) if he won the last four rounds.
</p><p>McLaren-Honda won the Constructors' Championship with four races left.
</p>
