<p>The <b>1968 Monaco Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Monaco" title="Circuit de Monaco">Monte Carlo Circuit</a> on 26 May 1968. It was race 3 of 12 in both the <a href="/wiki/1968_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1968 World Championship of Drivers">1968 World Championship of Drivers</a> and the <a href="/wiki/1968_International_Cup_for_Formula_One_Manufacturers" class="mw-redirect" title="1968 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers">1968 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers</a>. The race was won by <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Lotus</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Graham_Hill" title="Graham Hill">Graham Hill</a>, who started from pole position. <a href="/wiki/Richard_Attwood" title="Richard Attwood">Richard Attwood</a>, driving for <a href="/wiki/BRM" class="mw-redirect" title="BRM">BRM</a>, gained second place and fastest lap, while <a href="/wiki/Lucien_Bianchi" title="Lucien Bianchi">Lucien Bianchi</a> finished in third position in a Cooper, in what was to be these two drivers' only podium finishes.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1968_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1968_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Following the fatal accident of <a href="/wiki/Lorenzo_Bandini" title="Lorenzo Bandini">Lorenzo Bandini</a> <a href="/wiki/1967_Monaco_Grand_Prix" title="1967 Monaco Grand Prix">a year earlier</a>, the track was altered with the harbour chicane being tightened and the race shortened by 20 laps. <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> still chose not to attend the race since they felt the safety measures to be insufficient.<sup id="cite_ref-gp_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gp-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> While <a href="/wiki/Graham_Hill" title="Graham Hill">Graham Hill</a> stated <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Lotus</a> were still "in despair" over the losses of <a href="/wiki/Jim_Clark" title="Jim Clark">Jim Clark</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mike_Spence" title="Mike Spence">Mike Spence</a>, the team nevertheless introduced their new <a href="/wiki/Lotus_49B" class="mw-redirect" title="Lotus 49B">Lotus 49B</a> for the race. This race was the first race where wings were used on an F1 car since the B specifications introduced a wedge shape and a front wing. 1968 would prove to be the season in which wings became a common place on Formula One cars. <a href="/wiki/Jackie_Stewart" title="Jackie Stewart">Jackie Stewart</a> was still sidelined by his wrist injury and was replaced by <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Servoz-Gavin" title="Johnny Servoz-Gavin">Johnny Servoz-Gavin</a>, after Ferrari refused to allow <a href="/wiki/Chris_Amon" title="Chris Amon">Chris Amon</a> to enter for the Matra International team.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Richard_Attwood" title="Richard Attwood">Richard Attwood</a> was promoted from <a href="/wiki/Reg_Parnell_Racing" title="Reg Parnell Racing">Reg Parnell Racing</a> to the <a href="/wiki/BRM" class="mw-redirect" title="BRM">BRM</a> works team after Mike Spence's replacement, <a href="/wiki/Chris_Irwin" title="Chris Irwin">Chris Irwin</a>, suffered career-ending head injuries at the <a href="/wiki/1000km_N%C3%BCrburgring" class="mw-redirect" title="1000km Nürburgring">1000km Nürburgring</a> endurance race. <a href="/wiki/Brian_Redman" title="Brian Redman">Brian Redman</a>, who had produced a solid performance in the <a href="/wiki/1968_Spanish_Grand_Prix" title="1968 Spanish Grand Prix">previous race</a> by finishing third, was racing at the <a href="/wiki/1000_km_Spa" class="mw-redirect" title="1000 km Spa">1000 km Spa</a> and was therefore replaced at <a href="/wiki/Cooper_Car_Company" title="Cooper Car Company">Cooper</a> by <a href="/wiki/Lucien_Bianchi" title="Lucien Bianchi">Lucien Bianchi</a>. With the <a href="/wiki/1968_Indianapolis_500" title="1968 Indianapolis 500">Indianapolis 500</a> taking place just five days after the grand prix, <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren's</a> <a href="/wiki/Denny_Hulme" title="Denny Hulme">Denny Hulme</a> was busy flying back and forth to attend both the Monaco Grand Prix as well as qualifying at Indianapolis.<sup id="cite_ref-gp_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gp-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>With France in <a href="/wiki/May_1968_events_in_France" class="mw-redirect" title="May 1968 events in France">political unrest</a>, the race organizers borrowed power generators from a local film production company in order to ensure that the tunnel would stay illuminated even in case of a power outage.<sup id="cite_ref-espn_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-espn-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The race length was reduced from the traditional 100 laps / 315 km to 80 laps / 250 km, which has continued to do so as of 2017.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1968_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>During qualifying, <a href="/wiki/Graham_Hill" title="Graham Hill">Graham Hill</a>, a previous three time-winner at Monaco, set the pace and secured <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> 0.6 seconds ahead of the strong <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Servoz-Gavin" title="Johnny Servoz-Gavin">Johnny Servoz-Gavin</a>. The Frenchman proved once more the <a href="/wiki/Matra_MS10" title="Matra MS10">Matra MS10</a>, which had recorded the <a href="/wiki/Fastest_lap" title="Fastest lap">fastest lap</a> for <a href="/wiki/Jean-Pierre_Beltoise" title="Jean-Pierre Beltoise">Jean-Pierre Beltoise</a> in <a href="/wiki/1968_Spanish_Grand_Prix" title="1968 Spanish Grand Prix">Spain</a>, was highly competitive.<sup id="cite_ref-gp_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gp-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1968_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Johnny_Servoz-Gavin" title="Johnny Servoz-Gavin">Johnny Servoz-Gavin</a> took the lead from <a href="/wiki/Graham_Hill" title="Graham Hill">Hill</a> at the start, while <a href="/wiki/Bruce_McLaren" title="Bruce McLaren">Bruce McLaren</a> took out the other <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Lotus</a> of <a href="/wiki/Jackie_Oliver" title="Jackie Oliver">Jackie Oliver</a> at the chicane on the first lap. Servoz-Gavin was struck by bad luck on lap 3 when he suffered a drive shaft failure and crashed. This set the tone for the rest of the race, when after a series of accidents and mechanical failures, only five cars finished the race, with everyone from 3rd-place finishing at least four laps down on eventual winner Hill, who cemented his reputation as "Mr. Monaco"<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> by taking his fourth win in the principality. It was however a close finish, with <a href="/wiki/BRM" class="mw-redirect" title="BRM">BRM</a> replacement <a href="/wiki/Richard_Attwood" title="Richard Attwood">Richard Attwood</a> surprising by finishing just 2 seconds behind the Englishman. Even though Hill broke the Monaco lap record three times during the race, it was Attwood who ultimately recorded <a href="/wiki/Fastest_lap" title="Fastest lap">fastest lap</a>, the only one of his career.<sup id="cite_ref-espn_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-espn-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> This would also be his one and only podium finish at a Formula One race, just as for Belgian <a href="/wiki/Lucien_Bianchi" title="Lucien Bianchi">Lucien Bianchi</a>, who finished third.
</p><p>Disaster would strike Formula One again just two weeks later as fourth-placed <a href="/wiki/Ludovico_Scarfiotti" title="Ludovico Scarfiotti">Ludovico Scarfiotti</a> was killed during the Rossfeld hillclimb event.<sup id="cite_ref-gp_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gp-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
