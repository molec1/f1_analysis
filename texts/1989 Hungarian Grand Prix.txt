<p>The <b>1989 Hungarian Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at <a href="/wiki/Hungaroring" title="Hungaroring">Hungaroring</a> on 13 August 1989. It was the tenth race of the <a href="/wiki/1989_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="1989 Formula One World Championship">1989 Formula One World Championship</a>.
</p><p>The 77-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_640" title="Ferrari 640">Ferrari</a>. After qualifying only 12th, Mansell charged through the field and took the lead with an opportunistic overtaking manoeuvre on <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> in the <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4/5" title="McLaren MP4/5">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Honda_in_Formula_One" title="Honda in Formula One">Honda</a> as the two were lapping <a href="/wiki/Stefan_Johansson" title="Stefan Johansson">Stefan Johansson</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Onyx_Grand_Prix" title="Onyx Grand Prix">Onyx</a>-<a href="/wiki/Ford_Motor_Company" title="Ford Motor Company">Ford</a>. Senna finished 26 seconds behind Mansell, with <a href="/wiki/Thierry_Boutsen" title="Thierry Boutsen">Thierry Boutsen</a> third in a <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW12" title="Williams FW12">Williams</a>-<a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>.
</p><p>Senna's teammate and Drivers' Championship rival, <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a>, finished fourth, meaning that his lead over Senna in the championship was reduced to 14 points.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1989_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Pre-qualifying_report">Pre-qualifying report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1989_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Pre-qualifying report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the Friday morning pre-qualifying session, an <a href="/wiki/Onyx_(racing_team)" class="mw-redirect" title="Onyx (racing team)">Onyx</a> topped the time sheets for the fourth Grand Prix in succession. <a href="/wiki/Stefan_Johansson" title="Stefan Johansson">Stefan Johansson</a> was comfortably fastest, and his team-mate <a href="/wiki/Bertrand_Gachot" title="Bertrand Gachot">Bertrand Gachot</a> also pre-qualified in fourth. Both drivers had re-signed with Onyx for 1990. For the first time this season, <a href="/wiki/Piercarlo_Ghinzani" title="Piercarlo Ghinzani">Piercarlo Ghinzani</a> went through to the main qualifying sessions, in second place. For the third time this season, and for the first time since the <a href="/wiki/1989_United_States_Grand_Prix" title="1989 United States Grand Prix">US Grand Prix</a>, he outpaced his <a href="/wiki/Osella" title="Osella">Osella</a> team-mate <a href="/wiki/Nicola_Larini" title="Nicola Larini">Nicola Larini</a>, who missed out in fifth position. The <a href="/wiki/Larrousse" title="Larrousse">Larrousse</a>-<a href="/wiki/Lola_Cars" title="Lola Cars">Lola</a> of <a href="/wiki/Michele_Alboreto" title="Michele Alboreto">Michele Alboreto</a> was the other pre-qualifier in third, the Italian suffering from a cracked rib. His team-mate <a href="/wiki/Philippe_Alliot" title="Philippe Alliot">Philippe Alliot</a> was down in sixth, the first time either he or a Larrousse had failed to pre-qualify.<sup id="cite_ref-Walker_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Walker-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Automobiles_Gonfaronnaises_Sportives" title="Automobiles Gonfaronnaises Sportives">AGS</a> cars of <a href="/wiki/Yannick_Dalmas" title="Yannick Dalmas">Yannick Dalmas</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gabriele_Tarquini" title="Gabriele Tarquini">Gabriele Tarquini</a> were seventh and ninth respectively, while <a href="/wiki/Zakspeed" title="Zakspeed">Zakspeed</a> drivers <a href="/wiki/Bernd_Schneider_(racing_driver)" title="Bernd Schneider (racing driver)">Bernd Schneider</a> and <a href="/wiki/Aguri_Suzuki" title="Aguri Suzuki">Aguri Suzuki</a>, still hampered by their underpowered <a href="/wiki/Yamaha_Motor_Company" title="Yamaha Motor Company">Yamaha</a> engines, were eighth and twelfth. <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Moreno" title="Roberto Moreno">Roberto Moreno</a> was tenth in the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Coloni" title="Scuderia Coloni">Coloni</a>, while his team-mate <a href="/wiki/Pierre-Henri_Raphanel" title="Pierre-Henri Raphanel">Pierre-Henri Raphanel</a> was unable to post a representative time and was bottom of the time sheets in his last appearance for the team. <a href="/wiki/Gregor_Foitek" title="Gregor Foitek">Gregor Foitek</a> was still unable to pre-qualify the new <a href="/wiki/EuroBrun" title="EuroBrun">EuroBrun</a> car, and was eleventh fastest.<sup id="cite_ref-Walker_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Walker-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying_report">Qualifying report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1989_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Qualifying report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Riccardo_Patrese" title="Riccardo Patrese">Riccardo Patrese</a> took a surprise <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> in his <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a>-<a href="/wiki/Renault_in_Formula_One" title="Renault in Formula One">Renault</a>, the first and only non-<a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> pole of the season, beating <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a> by three-tenths of a second. In another surprise, <a href="/wiki/Alex_Caffi" title="Alex Caffi">Alex Caffi</a> took third in his <a href="/wiki/Dallara" title="Dallara">Dallara</a>, just six-tenths behind Senna, with <a href="/wiki/Thierry_Boutsen" title="Thierry Boutsen">Thierry Boutsen</a> fourth in the second Williams. Drivers' Championship leader <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> was fifth in the second McLaren, with <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> sixth in the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>. The top ten was completed by <a href="/wiki/Alessandro_Nannini" title="Alessandro Nannini">Alessandro Nannini</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stefano_Modena" title="Stefano Modena">Stefano Modena</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Brabham" title="Brabham">Brabham</a>, <a href="/wiki/Derek_Warwick" title="Derek Warwick">Derek Warwick</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Arrows_Grand_Prix_International" title="Arrows Grand Prix International">Arrows</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pierluigi_Martini" title="Pierluigi Martini">Pierluigi Martini</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> could only manage 12th in the second Ferrari, nearly seven-tenths behind teammate Berger and over two seconds behind Patrese, and later complained of traffic.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1989_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_report">Race report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1989_Hungarian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Race report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At the start of the race, Patrese, Senna and Caffi maintained their grid order into turn 1, while Boutsen lost out to Prost as Berger passed both of them. Further back, Mansell made a good start, rising to 8th at the first corner. It soon became clear, however, that Caffi was struggling, the Dallara unable to replicate the speed it had shown in qualifying. Before long he had been passed by both Berger and Prost, and was holding up a train of cars consisting of Boutsen, Nannini, Mansell and Warwick.
</p><p>Nannini exited the train when he pulled in to change tyres. This promoted Mansell to 7th, which he quickly turned into 5th by passing Boutsen and Caffi in quick succession. He then set about closing the 17-second gap to the leaders, and was promoted to 4th when Berger pitted for tyres. Having caught up to the leading group, Mansell passed Prost for 3rd. Patrese's Williams then began to develop a problem with a holed radiator, which slowed him and bunched up the leading group. Eventually, Patrese's holed radiator became so bad that both Senna and Mansell were able to pass him in the space of a few corners. Patrese retired from the race shortly afterwards.
</p><p>Mansell now began to pressure Senna, clearly faster but unable to pass due to the extra power of the McLaren's <a href="/wiki/Honda_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Honda F1">Honda</a> engine. Meanwhile, Prost pitted for tyres and rejoined 6th, while Berger only inherited 3rd briefly before he retired with gearbox problems, leaving Senna and Mansell on their own. Eventually, the pair came up to lap <a href="/wiki/Stefan_Johansson" title="Stefan Johansson">Stefan Johansson</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Onyx_(racing_team)" class="mw-redirect" title="Onyx (racing team)">Onyx</a>. Senna caught him at an awkward moment, just at the accelerating zone out of turn 3. The Brazilian uncharacteristically hesitated, briefly lifting off, and this allowed Mansell to draw alongside as they went past Johansson and then use the Ferrari's greater momentum to surge past Senna and take the lead. After that, Mansell had an unchallenged run to the flag, beating Senna by nearly 26 seconds, with Boutsen completing the podium. Prost overtook <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Cheever" title="Eddie Cheever">Eddie Cheever</a>'s Arrows for 4th on the final lap, while <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Lotus</a> rounded off the points scorers.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Many of the leading cars had problems with tyre vibrations-both Senna and Mansell complained about this, whilst Prost also had difficulties after picking up debris whilst going offline to avoid Patrese's oil.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
