<p>The <b>2001 Belgian Grand Prix</b> (officially the <b>LIX Foster's Belgian Grand Prix</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Spa-Francorchamps" title="Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps">Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps</a> in <a href="/wiki/Stavelot" title="Stavelot">Stavelot</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wallonia" title="Wallonia">Wallonia</a> on 2 September 2001. It was the 14th round of the <a href="/wiki/2001_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2001 Formula One World Championship">2001 Formula One World Championship</a> and the 48th edition of the <a href="/wiki/Belgian_Grand_Prix" title="Belgian Grand Prix">event</a> as part of the series. <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> driver and <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Champion</a> <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> won the 36-lap race from a third position start. <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> finished in second for <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> with <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> third.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a> team won the <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> by recording the fastest lap in qualifying; he stalled on the <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_motorsport_terms#G" title="Glossary of motorsport terms">grid</a> and forfeited pole. His teammate <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> lost the lead to Michael Schumacher into Les Combes turn. Michael Schumacher led the next four laps before the race was stopped for an accident involving <a href="/wiki/Jaguar_Racing" title="Jaguar Racing">Jaguar</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a> and <a href="/wiki/Luciano_Burti" title="Luciano Burti">Luciano Burti</a> of <a href="/wiki/Prost_Grand_Prix" title="Prost Grand Prix">Prost</a> on lap five. The race was declared null and void and recommenced with a revised distance of 36 laps. Michael Schumacher led every lap of the restarted race to take his fifth victory in Belgium, his eighth win of the season and overtook the four-time world champion <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a>'s all-time record of career victories with his 52nd.
</p><p>Burti was kept in hospital with facial bruising and a concussion until 10 September; his accident helped enhance helmet safety. The race result allowed Coulthard to further his Drivers' Championship advantage over the other Ferrari of <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> by four <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">points</a> in second position. Barrichello in turn moved another two points clear of fourth-placed Ralf Schumacher. In the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a>, McLaren in second moved further ahead of Williams in third by nine points as <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a> passed <a href="/wiki/British_American_Racing" title="British American Racing">British American Racing</a> (BAR) for fifth with three races left in the season.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2001_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The 2001 Belgian Grand Prix was the 14th of the 17 races in the <a href="/wiki/2001_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2001 Formula One World Championship">2001 Formula One World Championship</a> and the 48th running of the <a href="/wiki/Belgian_Grand_Prix" title="Belgian Grand Prix">event</a> as part of the series. It took place at the 21-turn 6.968&#160;km (4.330&#160;mi) <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Spa-Francorchamps" title="Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps">Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps</a> in <a href="/wiki/Stavelot" title="Stavelot">Stavelot</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wallonia" title="Wallonia">Wallonia</a> on 2 September.<sup id="cite_ref-CF1Race_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CF1Race-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> While the track's length has been reduced from 14&#160;km (8.7&#160;mi) for safety reasons, it is still the longest in Formula One. It contains a number of fast and long sweeping corners along with a high degree of gradient change, which puts a high amount of <a href="/wiki/G-force" title="G-force">g-force</a> on the driver.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-SVPreview_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SVPreview-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Before the race, both the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a> and <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">Constructors' Championship</a> were already settled, with <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> having claimed both titles in the preceding <a href="/wiki/2001_Hungarian_Grand_Prix" title="2001 Hungarian Grand Prix">Hungarian Grand Prix</a>, with their nearest rivals <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> too many <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Championship_points_scoring_systems" title="List of Formula One World Championship points scoring systems">points</a> behind to be able to catch them.<sup id="cite_ref-points_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Several positions in the standings were undecided. In the battle for second place, <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> of McLaren was five points in front of the second Ferrari of <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> in third and a further two ahead of <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a>' <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> in fourth.<sup id="cite_ref-points_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In the Constructors' standings, McLaren in second were 13 points ahead of Williams, while <a href="/wiki/Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="Sauber">Sauber</a> and <a href="/wiki/British_American_Racing" title="British American Racing">British American Racing</a> (BAR) contended for fourth.<sup id="cite_ref-points_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Although a crash during testing at the <a href="/wiki/Mugello_Circuit" title="Mugello Circuit">Mugello Circuit</a> left him with a sore neck, doctors deemed Michael Schumacher fit to compete in Belgium.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> He stated that he would be unwilling to be concentrated in his driving, and did not say when he felt he would surpass four-time world champion <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a>'s all-time record of 51 race victories, which he equalled in Hungary.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard said he set himself the objective of winning the season 's final four races to consolidate second in the Drivers' Championship. Barrichello stated he had also targeted second and his teammate Michael Schumacher promised to help him, saying, "For me the season has just started."<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Several teams modified their <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_car" title="Formula One car">cars</a> to suit the mid-<a href="/wiki/Downforce" title="Downforce">downforce</a> <a href="/wiki/Racing_setup" title="Racing setup">setup</a> of the Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps.<sup id="cite_ref-SVPreview_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SVPreview-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Williams debuted a lighter and stiffer chassis by 3&#160;kg (6.6&#160;lb) for driver Ralf Schumacher with a revised aerodynamic package for better airflow to the <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW23" title="Williams FW23">FW23</a>. Ferrari introduced a new front wing to the <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_F2001" title="Ferrari F2001">F2001</a> that was built upon a set of solutions tested in the previous race and adapted to suit the layout of the track. The <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Grand_Prix" title="Jordan Grand Prix">Jordan</a> team reverted to the aerodynamic configuration they used for the <a href="/wiki/Jordan_EJ11" title="Jordan EJ11">EJ11</a> at the preceding Hungarian Grand Prix. <a href="/wiki/Prost_Grand_Prix" title="Prost Grand Prix">Prost</a> adopted a new rear <a href="/wiki/Suspension_(vehicle)" class="mw-redirect" title="Suspension (vehicle)">suspension</a> geometry with a deformable structure and a new <a href="/wiki/Drive_shaft" title="Drive shaft">semi-axle</a> structure, characterised by a large extractor profile. <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arrows_Grand_Prix_International" title="Arrows Grand Prix International">Arrows</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jaguar_Racing" title="Jaguar Racing">Jaguar</a> carried over technical innovations developed for use in Hungary to Belgium, while <a href="/wiki/Minardi" title="Minardi">Minardi</a> manufactured a new chassis, the <a href="/wiki/Minardi_PS01" title="Minardi PS01">PS01B</a>, which featured a revised rear crash structure and <a href="/wiki/Transmission_(mechanics)" title="Transmission (mechanics)">gearbox</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Practice">Practice</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2001_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Practice">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Four practice sessions were scheduled before the Sunday race, two each on Friday and Saturday. The Friday morning and afternoon sessions lasted an hour; the third and fourth sessions, on Saturday morning, lasted 45 minutes.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The Friday practice sessions were held in cool and overcast weather conditions that turned to a downpour in the afternoon, causing several drivers to <a href="/wiki/Aquaplaning" title="Aquaplaning">aquaplane</a> on the wet track.<sup id="cite_ref-FIAFriday_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAFriday-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> In the first practice session, Michael Schumacher was fastest with a lap of 1 minute, 48.655 seconds, followed by Jordan's <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a>, Barrichello, <a href="/wiki/Mika_H%C3%A4kkinen" title="Mika Häkkinen">Mika Häkkinen</a> of McLaren, Ralf Schumacher, <a href="/wiki/Jean_Alesi" title="Jean Alesi">Jean Alesi</a> in the second Jordan, the Saubers of <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a>, <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a> of Williams and <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a>'s Benetton.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1FP1_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1FP1-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard lost control of his car by running wide onto sodden dirt, hitting a <a href="/wiki/Curb" title="Curb">kerb</a> at the exit of Les Fagnes corner,<sup id="cite_ref-F1NetFP1_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1NetFP1-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> a right-hander taken at 160&#160;km/h (99&#160;mph) and collided with a left-hand side metal barrier head-on, tearing the right-front wheel from his car's tethers.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1FP1_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1FP1-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> His damaged car skidded backwards along the wall before stopping. Coulthard clambered out of the car unhurt; he missed the second practice session while it was repaired.<sup id="cite_ref-FIAFriday_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAFriday-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-F1NetFP1_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1NetFP1-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Michael Schumacher recorded the day's quickest lap in the second session at 1 minute, 48.655 seconds. Trulli did not improve his lap but duplicated his first session result in second. Barrichello remained in third, with Fisichella was fourth-fastest, ahead of Häkkinen and his compatriot Räikkönen. Ralf Schumacher, Jaguar driver <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Irvine" title="Eddie Irvine">Eddie Irvine</a>, Alesi and Benetton's <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> rounded out the top ten fastest drivers.<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportFP2_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportFP2-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Rain fell came 16 minutes in and eased five minutes later.<sup id="cite_ref-F1TodayFP2_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1TodayFP2-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Button hit an armco metal barrier along the <a href="/wiki/Pit_stop" title="Pit stop">pit lane</a> exiting the Bus Stop <a href="/wiki/Chicane" title="Chicane">chicane</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FIAFriday_11-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAFriday-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-AutosportFP2_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportFP2-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> As Michael Schumacher was about to overtake <a href="/wiki/Pedro_de_la_Rosa" title="Pedro de la Rosa">Pedro de la Rosa</a>'s Jaguar going downhill to Eau Rouge corner, he could not see him due to spray reducing visibility and he hit the rear of de la Rosa's car. His front wing was broken and de la Rosa's left-rear wheel was removed.<sup id="cite_ref-FIAFriday_11-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAFriday-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-F1TodayFP2_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1TodayFP2-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Montoya lost control of his car on a wet kerb and he damaged it after a spin into a tyre wall out of Les Combes corner.<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportFP2_14-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportFP2-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-F1TodayFP2_15-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1TodayFP2-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The third practice session was delayed for two hours due to fog, rain showers and low clouds reducing visibility. Race organisers later cancelled it for safety reasons because the emergency medical helicopters were grounded in <a href="/wiki/Li%C3%A8ge" title="Liège">Liège</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1Cancel_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1Cancel-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> When the sky cleared at 10:00 local time,<sup id="cite_ref-AF1Cancel_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1Cancel-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> it was agreed by the Grand Prix organisers that a single 45-minute session would be held without practice starts to prevent a delay in television coverage for qualifying.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1FP4_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1FP4-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Williams led the session with Montoya fastest with a 1-minute, 47.974 seconds lap and Ralf Schumacher in second.  Häkkinen, Coulthard. Barrichello, Fisichella, Irvine, BAR driver <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Villeneuve" title="Jacques Villeneuve">Jacques Villeneuve</a>, Button and Alesi completed the top ten ahead of qualifying. Several drivers ran off the slippery track surface during the session. De La Rosa, Irvine and Häkkinen went wide into the grass at the Bus Stop chicane, while <a href="/wiki/Heinz-Harald_Frentzen" title="Heinz-Harald Frentzen">Heinz-Harald Frentzen</a> of the Prost team and Button ran off at Les Combes turn.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1FP4_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1FP4-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2001_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Juan_Pablo_Montoya_Autosport_International_2001.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/77/Juan_Pablo_Montoya_Autosport_International_2001.jpg/170px-Juan_Pablo_Montoya_Autosport_International_2001.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="255" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/77/Juan_Pablo_Montoya_Autosport_International_2001.jpg/255px-Juan_Pablo_Montoya_Autosport_International_2001.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/77/Juan_Pablo_Montoya_Autosport_International_2001.jpg/340px-Juan_Pablo_Montoya_Autosport_International_2001.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1180" data-file-height="1772" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Juan_Pablo_Montoya_Autosport_International_2001.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a> earned the second <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> of his career after trading the fastest lap with his Williams teammate <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> in the final five minutes of qualifying.</div></div></div>
<p>Saturday afternoon's one-hour qualifying session saw drivers limited to twelve laps, with the starting order decided by their fastest laps. During this session the <a href="/wiki/107%25_rule" title="107% rule">107% rule</a> was in effect, requiring each driver to remain within 107 per cent of the fastest lap time to qualify for the race.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Heavy rain at the start meant the field stayed in their garages for 26 minutes before cars on <a href="/wiki/Rain_tyre" title="Rain tyre">intermediate tyres</a> ventured onto the track. By then, sunny weather allowed the track to progressively dry and cars were fitted with dry tyres in the last five minutes.<sup id="cite_ref-FIASaturday_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIASaturday-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-SpeedvisionRace_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SpeedvisionRace-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Williams were the first team to switch their cars onto the dry tyres.<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportQuali_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportQuali-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Montoya traded the fastest lap with his teammate Ralf Schumacher and took the second <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> of his career with a 1-minute, 52.072 seconds lap, with Ralf Schumacher second.<sup id="cite_ref-SpeedvisionRace_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SpeedvisionRace-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher in third drove the spare Ferrari before switching to his race car when the track dried. Frentzen was hesitant to switch to the dry tyres until his team owner Alain Prost convinced him to do so and qualified fourth. Barrichello, fifth, used the intermediate tyres throughout.<sup id="cite_ref-FIASaturday_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIASaturday-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Villeneuve ran conservatively because he feared making an error and took sixth. Häkkinen qualified in seventh, bemoaning his conservative driving style due to the timing of the switch from intermediate to dry tyres.<sup id="cite_ref-SatQuotes_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SatQuotes-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Eighth-placed Fisichella reported that the feel of his car was adequate in changeable weather.<sup id="cite_ref-SatQuotes_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SatQuotes-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard fell from third to ninth in qualifying's final seconds due to his team telling him there was insufficient time to have the dry tyres fitted to his car and traffic slowed him.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> De La Rosa, tenth, experienced a loss in <a href="/wiki/Oil_pressure" title="Oil pressure">oil pressure</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-FIASaturday_19-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIASaturday-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Panis in 11th was affected by an electrical fault that a change of <a href="/wiki/Steering_wheel" title="Steering wheel">steering wheel</a> failed to rectify; his third timed lap was set too late to improve his position.<sup id="cite_ref-FIASaturday_19-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIASaturday-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Räikkönen was 6th in the final ten seconds until a reoccurring transmission fault left him in 12th. Alesi chose not to switch to the dry tyres and was 13th.<sup id="cite_ref-SatQuotes_22-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SatQuotes-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Heidfeld in 14th had the same transmission fault as his teammate Räikkönen and yellow flags were waved when he stopped at Burneville corner.<sup id="cite_ref-FIASaturday_19-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIASaturday-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> An excessive amount of <a href="/wiki/Understeer_and_oversteer" title="Understeer and oversteer">oversteer</a> left Button in 15th.<sup id="cite_ref-FIASaturday_19-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIASaturday-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-SpeedvisionRace_20-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SpeedvisionRace-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> A lack of time to switch from the intermediate to dry tyres restricted Trulli to 16th. Irvine in 17th switched to the spare Jaguar because the <a href="/wiki/Exhaust_system" title="Exhaust system">exhaust</a> on his car broke and the spare had excess understeer. 18th-placed <a href="/wiki/Luciano_Burti" title="Luciano Burti">Luciano Burti</a> of Prost was delayed for half a minute because of a tyre pressure adjustment error that prevented him from improving his best lap. <a href="/wiki/Jos_Verstappen" title="Jos Verstappen">Jos Verstappen</a> was 19th in the faster Arrows car. Irvine slowed him and a plan to do two timed laps did not come to fruition as he crossed the start/finish line three seconds after qualifying ended. Minardi's <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> started 20th due to him being on intermediate tyres at qualifying's end. The Arrows and Minardi formation continued on the eleventh row with <a href="/wiki/Enrique_Bernoldi" title="Enrique Bernoldi">Enrique Bernoldi</a> 21st after he lost time because he was called to the <a href="/wiki/Truck_scale" title="Truck scale">weighbridge</a> with five minutes left and he ran out of fuel at the Bus Stop chicane. <a href="/wiki/Tarso_Marques" title="Tarso Marques">Tarso Marques</a> took 22nd after Ralf Schumacher slowed him and his car's rear suspension buckled on his fourth timed lap.<sup id="cite_ref-SatQuotes_22-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SatQuotes-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-StatsQuali_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-StatsQuali-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-qualifying">Post-qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2001_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Post-qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Verstappen, Alonso, Bernoldi and Marques failed to lap within the 107 per cent limit; all four drivers were allowed to start the race because the stewards deemed that there had been "exceptional circumstances" owing to the changeable track conditions and also because they lapped within the limit during free practice.<sup id="cite_ref-Annual107_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Annual107-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Afterwards, the McLaren team principal <a href="/wiki/Ron_Dennis" title="Ron Dennis">Ron Dennis</a> lodged an official protest to the world governing body of motorsport, the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile</a> (FIA), to clarify if the regulation barring drivers on improving their lap times under yellow flag conditions was consistent after 17 competitors went faster with yellow flags to tend to Heidfeld's car.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> At a meeting of the race stewards eight hours later, data, information and oral arguments were reviewed and Dennis' protest was rejected. McLaren did not appeal the decision.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Warm-up">Warm-up</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2001_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Warm-up">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A 30-minute warm-up session was held on Sunday morning in cloudy and dry weather.<sup id="cite_ref-GF1Review_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF1Review-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> All drivers fine-tuned their race setups against the weather conditions of the time, and set laps in their spare cars.<sup id="cite_ref-FIAWarmUp_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAWarmUp-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher was fastest with a 1-minute, 49.495 seconds lap, followed by Häkkinen, Räikkönen, Ralf Schumacher, Irvine, Frentzen, Montoya, Trulli, Heidfeld and Barrichello.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1WarmUp_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1WarmUp-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Trulli removed his car's front wing and <a href="/wiki/Bargeboard_(aerodynamics)" title="Bargeboard (aerodynamics)">bargeboards</a> by running wide onto some bumps at Les Combes turn. He was unable to steer and stopped on a trackside wall. Alonso lost control of his car exiting Stavelot corner and destroyed its left-hand corner in a collision with a inside wall.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1WarmUp_30-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1WarmUp-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> He ricocheted to the right; Alonso exited the car unhurt with aid from track marshals,<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> as Marques stopped to check if his teammate was unhurt.<sup id="cite_ref-AF1WarmUp_30-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1WarmUp-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Alonso was transported to the track's medical centre for a precautionary check-up. He was passed fit to compete in the race and used the spare Minardi car.<sup id="cite_ref-FIAWarmUp_29-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIAWarmUp-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-AF1WarmUp_30-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1WarmUp-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2001_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="Nullified_race_and_Burti.27s_accident"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Nullified_race_and_Burti's_accident">Nullified race and Burti's accident</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2001_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Nullified race and Burti&#039;s accident">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The race took place in the afternoon from 14:00 local time.<sup id="cite_ref-GF1Review_28-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF1Review-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> The weather at the start was overcast and some light rain fell on the track 11 minutes before the <a href="/wiki/Parade_lap" title="Parade lap">formation lap</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-F1LiveRace_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1LiveRace-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> The air temperature was between 12 to 16&#160;°C (54 to 61&#160;°F) and the track temperature was 17&#160;°C (63&#160;°F);<sup id="cite_ref-GF1Review_28-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GF1Review-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ITVRace_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ITVRace-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> weather forecasts on the day of the race predicted the possibility of heavy rain in its latter stages.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Before the formation lap was about to commence, Frentzen and Marques <a href="/wiki/Stall_(engine)" title="Stall (engine)">stalled</a> because of gearbox problems.<sup id="cite_ref-SunQuotes_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SunQuotes-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FIARace_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIARace-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Both drivers were ordered to start from the back of the <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_motorsport_terms" title="Glossary of motorsport terms">grid</a>, causing the start to be aborted, and the number of laps reduced from 44 to 43.<sup id="cite_ref-F1ComRace_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1ComRace-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> At the start of the second formation lap,<sup id="cite_ref-F1NetRace_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1NetRace-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> Montoya was stationary as he engaged first gear. He forfeited pole position and began from the rear of the grid.<sup id="cite_ref-GPComRace_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPComRace-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> His teammate Ralf Schumacher led on the approach to Les Combes corner as he locked his brakes.<sup id="cite_ref-F1ComRace_37-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1ComRace-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello kept third as Fisichella moved to fourth. Going uphill into Les Combes corner, Michael Schumacher pulled out of Ralf Schumacher's <a href="/wiki/Drafting_(aerodynamics)" title="Drafting (aerodynamics)">slipstream</a> and made a pass around the left for first place. Villeneuve fell behind the McLarens at the same time.<sup id="cite_ref-ITVRace_33-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ITVRace-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FIARace_36-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIARace-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Luciano_Burti_2006_Curitiba.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/86/Luciano_Burti_2006_Curitiba.jpg/170px-Luciano_Burti_2006_Curitiba.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="240" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/86/Luciano_Burti_2006_Curitiba.jpg/255px-Luciano_Burti_2006_Curitiba.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/86/Luciano_Burti_2006_Curitiba.jpg/340px-Luciano_Burti_2006_Curitiba.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1538" data-file-height="2168" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Luciano_Burti_2006_Curitiba.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Luciano_Burti" title="Luciano Burti">Luciano Burti</a> <i>(pictured in 2006)</i> was involved in an impact with a tyre wall at Blanchimont corner on lap five.</div></div></div>
<p>Button moved from 13th to ninth by the conclusion of the first lap, while Villeneuve fell four positions over the same distance. At the close of the first lap, Michael Schumacher led Ralf Schumacher by 2.1 seconds. Barrichello was a further six-tenths of a second behind Ralf Schumacher in third, while Fisichella in fourth was 2.2 seconds adrift of Barrichello.<sup id="cite_ref-LapChart_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LapChart-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> Over the next three laps, Michael Schumacher pulled away from the rest of the field, extending his advantage to 6.3 seconds. In the meantime, Alesi overtook de la Rosa and Panis to advance to 16th as Montoya passed Verstappen for 16th.<sup id="cite_ref-FIARace_36-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIARace-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> On lap three, Fisichella lost fourth to Häkkinen into Les Combes corner, and a driver error at the exit to Rivage turn lost him fifth to Coulthard on the outside into Pouhon corner. Further back, Räikkönen braked later than Villenueve at the Bus Stop chicane to move into seventh.<sup id="cite_ref-ITVRace_33-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ITVRace-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-F1ComRace_37-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1ComRace-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-StatsF1Race_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-StatsF1Race-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> Trulli passed Irvine for 14th.<sup id="cite_ref-FIARace_36-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIARace-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> On the same lap, Alonso retired with a broken gearshaft.<sup id="cite_ref-SunQuotes_35-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SunQuotes-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-BBCLapwatch_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCLapwatch-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> On the fourth lap, Alesi passed Button for ninth, as Heidfeld and Trulli demoted Panis from 12th to 14th.<sup id="cite_ref-FIARace_36-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIARace-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Irvine lost another position when Montoya passed him for 15th.<sup id="cite_ref-F1ComRace_37-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1ComRace-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As Burti was about to pass Irvine on the inside at Blanchimont corner on lap five, Irvine was caught off guard and stayed on the <a href="/wiki/Racing_line" title="Racing line">racing line</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-F1LiveRace_32-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1LiveRace-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> putting Burti's left-hand wheels onto the grass.<sup id="cite_ref-SVRace_43-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SVRace-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> Burti's right-front wheel and Irvine's Jaguar made contact.<sup id="cite_ref-F1LiveRace_32-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1LiveRace-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> removing Burti's front wing and downforce.<sup id="cite_ref-GPComRace_39-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPComRace-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> Irvine drove over the Prost's front wing and destroyed it.<sup id="cite_ref-SVRace_43-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SVRace-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> Burti was sent spearing across the gravel trap and into a four-deep tyre barrier at 150&#160;mph (240&#160;km/h).<sup id="cite_ref-GPComRace_39-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPComRace-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> The front of Burti's full-face crash helmet broke as it took the full force of the 111&#160;<i>g<sub>0</sub></i> (1,090&#160;m/s<sup>2</sup>) impact.<sup id="cite_ref-BurtiHelmetCrash_44-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BurtiHelmetCrash-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-AutosportFeb19_45-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportFeb19-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> Further injury to Burti was prevented because a head and neck restraint absorbed the impact.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Irvine went off the track with the right-hand wheels removed from his Jaguar.<sup id="cite_ref-CBCSep01_47-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CBCSep01-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> He exited his car to assist the track marshals in removing the tyres pinned to Burti and released his car from the barrier.<sup id="cite_ref-GPComRace_39-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPComRace-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> Räikkönen stopped with a transmission failure as Frentzen and Marques made pit stops.<sup id="cite_ref-FIARace_36-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIARace-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Safety_car" title="Safety car">safety car</a> was deployed before the race was stopped. All the remaining drivers went on the starting grid to await the restart.<sup id="cite_ref-F1LiveRace_32-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1LiveRace-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Meanwhile, the FIA medical delegate <a href="/wiki/Sid_Watkins" title="Sid Watkins">Sid Watkins</a> arrived to direct the extraction of Burti from his car. Burti was transported to the circuit's medical centre by ambulance with no emergency treatment.<sup id="cite_ref-F1NetRace_38-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1NetRace-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> After a medical inspection, he was flown by helicopter to the Centre hospitalier universitaire de Liège in a conscious and stable condition with a serious concussion.<sup id="cite_ref-FIARace_36-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIARace-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Restarted_race">Restarted race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2001_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Restarted race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:David_Coulthard_2009.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/84/David_Coulthard_2009.jpg/170px-David_Coulthard_2009.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="227" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/84/David_Coulthard_2009.jpg/255px-David_Coulthard_2009.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/84/David_Coulthard_2009.jpg/340px-David_Coulthard_2009.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1536" data-file-height="2048" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:David_Coulthard_2009.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a> <i>(pictured in 2009)</i> finished in second to consolidate second in the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">Drivers' Championship</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>According to series regulations, the first four laps were declared null and void, and the starting order was determined from the finishing order of the first four laps,<sup id="cite_ref-FIARace_36-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIARace-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> revising the distance to 36 laps and reducing the field to 18 cars.<sup id="cite_ref-STRace_48-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-STRace-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> The race restarted at 14:45 local time.<sup id="cite_ref-ITVRace_33-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ITVRace-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Ralf Schumacher's Williams was not lowered from a <a href="/wiki/Jack_(device)" title="Jack (device)">jack</a> at the start of the third formation lap because his mechanics were immersed on replacing a rear wing beam for safety reasons after Montoya's front wing broke. He avoided incurring a ten-second stop-and-go penalty and was required to start from the back of the grid.<sup id="cite_ref-GPComRace_39-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPComRace-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-CrashRace_49-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CrashRace-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-WilliamsError_50-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WilliamsError-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> Fisichella made a fast start from sixth to pass the McLarens and Barrichello,<sup id="cite_ref-FIARace_36-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIARace-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> but not Michael Schumacher for first into the La Source <a href="/wiki/Hairpin_turn" title="Hairpin turn">hairpin</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-CrashRace_49-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CrashRace-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> Barrichello maintained third as Button moved to fourth. Coulthard was briefly ahead of Häkkinen before his teammate passed. Cresting a hill, both McLarens overtook Button as the BARs made contact and briefly drove off the circuit.<sup id="cite_ref-FIARace_36-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIARace-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On the lap, after Verstappen hit him, Heideld attempted to pass de la Rosa on the left at the La Source hairpin. The two made contact with Heidfeld retiring immediately with front wing and suspension damage. De la Rosa entered the pit lane the lap after with car damage.<sup id="cite_ref-SunQuotes_35-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SunQuotes-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-GPComRace_39-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPComRace-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> Up front, Michael Schumacher began to pull away from the rest of the field, increasing his lead over Fisichella to five seconds by the third lap.<sup id="cite_ref-FIARace_36-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIARace-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> One lap prior, Montoya's car pulled to one side though he overtook four cars before he retired from an engine failure.<sup id="cite_ref-ITVRace_33-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ITVRace-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-SunQuotes_35-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SunQuotes-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> On lap three, Trulli overtook Button for sixth. Button lost seventh to Alesi on the following lap. Michael Schumacher further extended his advantage to 14 seconds over Fisichella by lap six as Coulthard challenged the Benetton. Further back, Villeneuve passed Button for eighth. The first round of pit stops began at the conclusion of the lap when Trulli entered the pit lane. After his pit stop on lap seven, Panis crossed the white line to denote the pit lane exit, an infraction of the sporting regulations which entailed a ten-second stop-and-go penalty.<sup id="cite_ref-FIARace_36-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIARace-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Barrichello made his first stop on the ninth lap. Michael Schumacher led the rest of the field by 20 seconds when he entered the pit lane for the first time on the next lap. He rejoined the race in second. Fisichella led before his own stop at the conclusion of the lap. He retained second place. Panis took his ten-second stop-and-go-penalty on the 12th lap. After the pit stops, the Grand Prix settled into a rhythm with Michael Schumacher further extending his advantage over Fisichella to 24.8 seconds by lap 15.<sup id="cite_ref-BBCLapwatch_42-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BBCLapwatch-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> At the rear of the field, Marques made an unscheduled visit to the pit lane for repairs to his suspension.<sup id="cite_ref-SunQuotes_35-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SunQuotes-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-FIARace_36-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIARace-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Two laps later, Michael Schumacher lost concentration as he steered his Ferrari to the left at Stavelot turn and ran wide after missing the apex of the corner. He rejoined the track without losing the lead. His teammate Barrichello hit a trackside <a href="/wiki/Bollard" title="Bollard">bollard</a> entering the Bus Stop chicane on lap 17.<sup id="cite_ref-FIARace_36-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIARace-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-GPComRace_39-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPComRace-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> His car lost front downforce from removing his front wing and he completed a full lap at reduced speed after missing the pit lane entry. Barrichello rejoined the race in ninth.<sup id="cite_ref-ITVRace_33-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ITVRace-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-F1ComRace_37-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1ComRace-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-CrashRace_49-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CrashRace-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Michael_Schumacher-I%27m_the_man_(cropped).jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/Michael_Schumacher-I%27m_the_man_%28cropped%29.jpg/170px-Michael_Schumacher-I%27m_the_man_%28cropped%29.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="230" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/Michael_Schumacher-I%27m_the_man_%28cropped%29.jpg/255px-Michael_Schumacher-I%27m_the_man_%28cropped%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/Michael_Schumacher-I%27m_the_man_%28cropped%29.jpg/340px-Michael_Schumacher-I%27m_the_man_%28cropped%29.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1260" data-file-height="1704" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Michael_Schumacher-I%27m_the_man_(cropped).jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> <i>(pictured in 2005)</i> took his eighth victory of the season and he broke the all-time wins record held by <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> with the 52nd of his career.</div></div></div>
<p>On lap 18, while under pressure from Ralf Schumacher, Button struck a bollard on a kerb going into the Bus Stop Chicane. The contact removed his front wing and the lack of downforce sent him into a barrier beside the track.<sup id="cite_ref-F1ComRace_37-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1ComRace-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-GPComRace_39-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPComRace-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> Trulli entered the pit lane on the following lap to begin the second round of pit stops. His teammate Alesi followed on lap 20.<sup id="cite_ref-FIARace_36-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIARace-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Fisichella was six-tenths of a second ahead of Coulthard in third by the time of his second stop on lap 23. Fisichella's car sprayed oil on Coulthard, causing the McLaren driver to clean his <a href="/wiki/Visor" title="Visor">visor</a> on the main <a href="/wiki/Straight_(racing)" title="Straight (racing)">straights</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ITVRace_33-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ITVRace-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-GPComRace_39-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPComRace-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard made his stop the lap after, and emerged in third behind Fisichella.<sup id="cite_ref-ITVRace_33-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ITVRace-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher led by 43.6 seconds by the time of his second pit stop at the end of lap 25 and he retained the lead.<sup id="cite_ref-LapChart_40-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LapChart-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> On lap 26, Coulthard steered left to attempt an unsuccessful overtake on Fisichella.<sup id="cite_ref-F1ComRace_37-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1ComRace-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Häkkinen ran wide entering the Bus Stop chicane on lap 27, rejoining the track through the grass.<sup id="cite_ref-F1LiveRace_32-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1LiveRace-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> On the next lap, Fisichella lost momentum while lapping Bernoldi, and Coulthard steered left to pass but Fisichella defended at the La Source hairpin.<sup id="cite_ref-ITVRace_33-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ITVRace-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-F1ComRace_37-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1ComRace-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-GPComRace_39-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPComRace-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard tried again by slipstreaming Fisichella in Eau Rouge corner and passed him on the right for second.<sup id="cite_ref-F1LiveRace_32-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1LiveRace-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On lap 29, Ralf Schumacher overtook Villeneuve for eighth.<sup id="cite_ref-FIARace_36-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIARace-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Three laps later, Barrichello passed Alesi on the straight towards Les Combes corner for sixth.<sup id="cite_ref-ITVRace_33-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ITVRace-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-F1ComRace_37-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1ComRace-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Jordan lost one of its EJ11s on the lap when the fifth-placed Trulli drove to the side of the track with oil smoke billowing from the rear of his car.<sup id="cite_ref-F1LiveRace_32-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1LiveRace-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-GPComRace_39-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GPComRace-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> In the final three laps, Ralf Schumacher attempted to pass Alesi five times and was unsuccessful each time.<sup id="cite_ref-F1ComRace_37-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1ComRace-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Up front, Coulthard reduced Michael Schumacher's lead by a second per lap,<sup id="cite_ref-F1NetRace_38-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-F1NetRace-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> as the latter slowed,<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> but Schumacher took his fifth win at the Circuit de-Spa Francorchamps to equal the triple world champion <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a>'s record number of wins at the track.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> It was his eighth victory of 2001 and the 52nd of his career, overtaking the four-time world champion Alain Prost's all-time record of 51 wins.<sup id="cite_ref-STRace_48-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-STRace-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard followed 10.098 seconds later in second and Fisichella finished a season-high third. Häkkinen took fourth, Barrichello fifth and Alesi sixth. The final finishers were Ralf Schumacher, Villenueve, Frentzen, Verstappen, Panis, Bernoldi and Marques.<sup id="cite_ref-STRace_48-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-STRace-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Post-race">Post-race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2001_Belgian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Post-race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The top three drivers appeared on the podium to collect their trophies and spoke to the media in a later press conference.<sup id="cite_ref-regulations_10-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-regulations-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Michael Schumacher said he slowed to be conservative, "For whatever reason, I thought I had the chance to open up the gap for an extra pit and we didn't know whether they would do so I just wanted to have that safety margin because I was faster than Giancarlo and David couldn't get by I was able to open up such a lead."<sup id="cite_ref-AF1PressConf_54-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1PressConf-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup> He said that his 52nd win had no priority, saying, "Actually I'm very delighted about this but I will be much more delighted sitting one day on the sofa, retired, and I have a cigar and beer in hand and think about it."<sup id="cite_ref-AF1PressConf_54-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1PressConf-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup> Coulthard admitted his surprise over Fisichella's pace. He said he was pleased to finish second and increase his Drivers' Championship advantage over Barrichello, "Given the weekend I've had, I'm obviously very happy to have got second place, hoping that Luciano is 100 per cent and able to do the next race, and then we can all feel it's been a positive weekend."<sup id="cite_ref-AF1PressConf_54-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1PressConf-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup> Fisichella attributed his third-place result to the new aerodynamic package on his Benetton that gave him improved <a href="/wiki/Grip_(auto_racing)" title="Grip (auto racing)">grip</a> and handling, "We did a very good job. I did two amazing starts, especially the second one from sixth position to second, and I'm happy. It's a great day, and I'd like to say thank you to the team."<sup id="cite_ref-AF1PressConf_54-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AF1PressConf-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Giancarlo_Fisichella_2008_2.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/73/Giancarlo_Fisichella_2008_2.jpg/170px-Giancarlo_Fisichella_2008_2.jpg" decoding="async" width="170" height="238" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/73/Giancarlo_Fisichella_2008_2.jpg/255px-Giancarlo_Fisichella_2008_2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/73/Giancarlo_Fisichella_2008_2.jpg/340px-Giancarlo_Fisichella_2008_2.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2025" data-file-height="2834" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Giancarlo_Fisichella_2008_2.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> <i>(pictured in 2008)</i> finished third for <a href="/wiki/Benetton_Formula" title="Benetton Formula">Benetton</a>, his best result of the season.</div></div></div>
<p>After the race, the stewards investigated the collision between Irvine and Burti on lap five by reviewing television footage and listening to Irvine's oral explanation. They took no further action and deemed it "a racing incident", with neither driver in particular to blame.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup> It was the second major accident Burti had sustained in more than a month as he barrel rolled twice after colliding with the rear of Michael Schumacher's car at the start of the <a href="/wiki/2001_German_Grand_Prix" title="2001 German Grand Prix">German Grand Prix</a> in July 2001.<sup id="cite_ref-CBCSep01_47-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CBCSep01-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> The accident was a talking point for days after the race, with Alain Prost noting that Burti's impact would have been fatal about ten years earlier, and former driver <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Laffite" title="Jacques Laffite">Jacques Laffite</a> praised the strength of the modern Formula One car's <a href="/wiki/Monocoque" title="Monocoque">monocoque</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-BurtiAF101_56-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BurtiAF101-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> The Jaguar team principal <a href="/wiki/Niki_Lauda" title="Niki Lauda">Niki Lauda</a> told the press that he was unhappy with the inaction and conduct of the track marshals, "The marshals were not operating properly. Eddie had to take over and basically tell them what to do. He was really part of the rescue there, which normally drivers shouldn't do but he had to do it, since they didn't seem to have an idea what they need to do."<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup> Irvine said afterwards that he had not seen Burti attempting a pass on the outside of him. He spoke of his relief that Burti was conscious.<sup id="cite_ref-CBCSep01_47-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CBCSep01-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Burti sustained facial and head bruising and he underwent a <a href="/wiki/Neuroimaging" title="Neuroimaging">brain scan</a>, which revealed a head injury though no swelling of the brain.<sup id="cite_ref-BurtiAF101_56-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BurtiAF101-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Gary_Hartstein" title="Gary Hartstein">Gary Hartstein</a>, a member of the FIA's medical delegation, predicted that Burti would take up to two months to complete a full recovery.<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup> Burti underwent further tests and was placed in an <a href="/wiki/Induced_coma" title="Induced coma">induced coma</a> until he left intensive care on 4 September.<sup id="cite_ref-BurtiHelmetCrash_44-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BurtiHelmetCrash-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup> He was discharged from hospital six days later and returned to Monaco to continue his recuperation.<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup> Burti was replaced by the Czech <a href="/wiki/International_Formula_3000" title="International Formula 3000">International Formula 3000</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Tom%C3%A1%C5%A1_Enge" title="Tomáš Enge">Tomáš Enge</a> for the rest of the season.<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup> He stated in subsequent interviews that he has no recollection of the crash.<sup id="cite_ref-BurtiHelmetCrash_44-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BurtiHelmetCrash-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-BurtiCondition_62-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BurtiCondition-62">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup> The effects of the accident caused Burti to suffer from seizures and amnesia for the next four months, both of which required medication.<sup id="cite_ref-BurtiCondition_62-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BurtiCondition-62">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup> The FIA analysed the accident to enhance safety and outlawed the creation of holes in helmets that allowed drinking tubes and radio communication wires to pass through.<sup id="cite_ref-BurtiHelmetCrash_44-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BurtiHelmetCrash-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> The incident was used as a case study in the development of the <a href="/wiki/Halo_(safety_device)" title="Halo (safety device)">halo</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup> In an interview with <i><a href="/wiki/Autosport" title="Autosport">Autosport</a></i> in 2019, Burti attributed his survival to vehicle and circuit safety improvements made after Ayrton Senna's <a href="/wiki/Death_of_Ayrton_Senna" title="Death of Ayrton Senna">fatal accident</a> at the <a href="/wiki/1994_San_Marino_Grand_Prix" title="1994 San Marino Grand Prix">1994 San Marino Grand Prix</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-AutosportFeb19_45-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AutosportFeb19-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Frank_Williams_(Formula_One)" title="Frank Williams (Formula One)">Frank Williams</a>, the team principal of the Williams squad, stated that they made an error in keeping Ralf Schumacher's car on a jack at the start of the third formation lap and that nobody could be blamed for it.<sup id="cite_ref-WilliamsError_50-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WilliamsError-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> Button said of his accident that caused him to retire from the race on lap 18, "The car was fine when I turned left, but it didn't want to turn right because the wing had fallen under my wheels, and so I hit the wall."<sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64">&#91;64&#93;</a></sup> The result left Coulthard nine points in front of Barrichello in the battle for second while the latter moved a further two points clear of Ralf Schumacher.<sup id="cite_ref-points_5-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In the Constructors' Championship, McLaren moved further away from Williams by another nine points, while Jordan moved past BAR for fifth with three races left in the season.<sup id="cite_ref-points_5-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-points-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
