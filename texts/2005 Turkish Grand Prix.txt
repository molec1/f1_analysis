<p>The <b>2005 Turkish Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 21 August 2005 at <a href="/wiki/Istanbul_Park" class="mw-redirect" title="Istanbul Park">Istanbul Park</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tuzla,_Istanbul" title="Tuzla, Istanbul">Tuzla</a>, <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>. It was the fourteenth race of the <a href="/wiki/2005_FIA_Formula_One_World_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="2005 FIA Formula One World Championship">2005 FIA Formula One World Championship</a> and the inaugural Turkish Grand Prix.
</p><p>The 58-lap race was won by Finnish driver <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/McLaren_MP4-20" title="McLaren MP4-20">McLaren</a>-<a href="/wiki/Mercedes_AMG_High_Performance_Powertrains" title="Mercedes AMG High Performance Powertrains">Mercedes</a>. Räikkönen took his fifth victory of the season after starting from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> and leading every lap. Räikkönen's Colombian teammate, <a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a>, ran second until he collided with a backmarker and then ran wide in the closing laps, allowing Spaniard <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> through in his <a href="/wiki/Renault_R25" title="Renault R25">Renault</a>.
</p><p>With five races remaining, Alonso led the Drivers' Championship by 24 points from Räikkönen, while Renault's lead over McLaren in the Constructors' Championship was nine points.
</p>

<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> collided on lap 14. The rear suspension of Schumacher's Ferrari was damaged, but after repairs he rejoined the race to improve his qualifying slot for the <a href="/wiki/2005_Italian_Grand_Prix" title="2005 Italian Grand Prix">next race in Italy</a> by three positions. Webber's Williams lost the nose cone.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> lost the front wing and <a href="/wiki/Bargeboard_(aerodynamics)" title="Bargeboard (aerodynamics)">bargeboard</a> from his Sauber in a first-corner collision with the Williams of former teammate <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a>. After replacements were fitted, Massa continued racing until his engine failed on lap 29.</li>
<li>Both Williams cars retired from the race due to right-rear tyre failures - Webber on lap 21 and Heidfeld on lap 30.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Juan_Pablo_Montoya" title="Juan Pablo Montoya">Juan Pablo Montoya</a> was running second on the penultimate lap when he clashed with the Jordan of <a href="/wiki/Tiago_Monteiro" title="Tiago Monteiro">Tiago Monteiro</a>, damaging his <a href="/wiki/Diffuser_(automotive)" title="Diffuser (automotive)">diffuser</a>. On the next lap Montoya ran straight ahead at Turn 8, allowing <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> to pass him.</li>
<li>Montoya's fastest lap was over two seconds faster than his teammate's qualifying time.</li></ul>
