<p>The <b>2006 Canadian Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 25 June 2006 at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_Gilles_Villeneuve" title="Circuit Gilles Villeneuve">Circuit Gilles Villeneuve</a> in <a href="/wiki/Montreal" title="Montreal">Montreal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">Quebec</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>. The 70-lap race was the ninth round of the <a href="/wiki/2006_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2006 Formula One season">2006 Formula One season</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2006_Canadian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Polesitter <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> took his sixth win of the season and increased his championship lead over <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>, who finished second after passing <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> with two laps remaining when the Finn ran wide. Räikkönen subsequently finished in third position, completing the podium for the <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> team. Alonso had now taken six wins and three second places from the first nine races.
</p><p>There were problems at the start of the race when the two Midland cars collided at the tight hairpin on Lap 1, before Juan Pablo Montoya and Nico Rosberg collided on Lap 2 (which necessitated bringing out the safety car). Rosberg retired, whilst Montoya continued until he collided with a wall later in the race. Schumacher was stuck behind <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> for a large proportion of the race before finally managing to overtake him, although Alonso had gained a considerable lead over him by this point.
</p><p>With just a handful of laps left the safety car was out for a second time when home-town hero Jacques Villeneuve crashed into the wall due to brake failure. Räikkönen, who was second in the snake of cars behind the safety car, was unable to take advantage of the closed gap to Alonso due backmarkers in front of him. He eventually lost the position to Schumacher.
</p>
