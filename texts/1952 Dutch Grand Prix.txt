<p>The <b>1952 Dutch Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_Two" title="Formula Two">Formula Two</a> race held on 17 August 1952 at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_Zandvoort" title="Circuit Zandvoort">Circuit Zandvoort</a>. It was race 7 of 8 in the <a href="/wiki/1952_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1952 World Championship of Drivers">1952 World Championship of Drivers</a>, in which each Grand Prix was run to Formula Two rules rather than the Formula One regulations normally used. The 90-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Alberto_Ascari" title="Alberto Ascari">Alberto Ascari</a> after he started from <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a>. His teammates <a href="/wiki/Giuseppe_Farina" title="Giuseppe Farina">Giuseppe Farina</a> and <a href="/wiki/Luigi_Villoresi" title="Luigi Villoresi">Luigi Villoresi</a> finished in second and third places.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_report">Race report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1952_Dutch_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Race report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<p><a href="/wiki/Luigi_Villoresi" title="Luigi Villoresi">Luigi Villoresi</a>, absent from the World Championship since the final round of the <a href="/wiki/1951_Formula_One_season" title="1951 Formula One season">1951 season</a>, returned to the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> lineup for the Dutch Grand Prix, replacing <a href="/wiki/Piero_Taruffi" title="Piero Taruffi">Piero Taruffi</a>, alongside regulars <a href="/wiki/Giuseppe_Farina" title="Giuseppe Farina">Nino Farina</a> and <a href="/wiki/Alberto_Ascari" title="Alberto Ascari">Alberto Ascari</a>, the latter of which had clinched the Drivers' Championship title <a href="/wiki/1952_German_Grand_Prix" title="1952 German Grand Prix">two weeks previously</a>. <a href="/wiki/Charles_de_Tornaco" title="Charles de Tornaco">Charles de Tornaco</a> also drove a Ferrari at Zandvoort, on behalf of the <a href="/wiki/Ecurie_Francorchamps" title="Ecurie Francorchamps">Ecurie Francorchamps</a> team. <a href="/wiki/Gordini" title="Gordini">Gordini</a> entered the same three drivers from the previous event, the French trio of <a href="/wiki/Jean_Behra" title="Jean Behra">Behra</a>, <a href="/wiki/Robert_Manzon" title="Robert Manzon">Manzon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Maurice_Trintignant" title="Maurice Trintignant">Trintignant</a>, while Belgian driver <a href="/wiki/Paul_Fr%C3%A8re" title="Paul Frère">Paul Frère</a> drove an Ecurie Belge-entered <a href="/wiki/Simca-Gordini" class="mw-redirect" title="Simca-Gordini">Simca-Gordini</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Hersham_and_Walton_Motors" title="Hersham and Walton Motors">HWM</a> team partnered Britons <a href="/wiki/Lance_Macklin" title="Lance Macklin">Lance Macklin</a> and <a href="/wiki/Duncan_Hamilton_(racing_driver)" title="Duncan Hamilton (racing driver)">Duncan Hamilton</a> with the local driver <a href="/wiki/Dries_van_der_Lof" title="Dries van der Lof">Dries van der Lof</a>. The only other Dutch driver on the grid was <a href="/wiki/Jan_Flinterman" title="Jan Flinterman">Jan Flinterman</a>, who took part in a <a href="/wiki/Maserati_in_motorsport" title="Maserati in motorsport">Maserati</a> for Escuderia Bandeirantes alongside <a href="/wiki/Chico_Landi" title="Chico Landi">Chico Landi</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gino_Bianco" title="Gino Bianco">Gino Bianco</a>. The works Maserati team were once again absent from the grid, following an unsuccessful appearance in Germany. The field was completed by the <a href="/wiki/Connaught_Engineering" title="Connaught Engineering">Connaught</a> of <a href="/wiki/Ken_Downing" title="Ken Downing">Ken Downing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mike_Hawthorn" title="Mike Hawthorn">Mike Hawthorn</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Cooper_Car_Company" title="Cooper Car Company">Cooper</a>-<a href="/wiki/Bristol_Cars" title="Bristol Cars">Bristol</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ken_Wharton" title="Ken Wharton">Ken Wharton</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Frazer-Nash" class="mw-redirect" title="Frazer-Nash">Frazer-Nash</a> and <a href="/wiki/Stirling_Moss" title="Stirling Moss">Stirling Moss</a> in an <a href="/wiki/English_Racing_Automobiles" title="English Racing Automobiles">ERA</a>.
</p><p>The Ferraris once again dominated qualifying, with Ascari taking his fourth pole position of the season, ahead of Farina in second. Mike Hawthorn shone in practice, gaining a front-row start for his little Cooper-Bristol, relegating Villoresi's Ferrari to the second row of the grid. Trintignant's Gordini completed row two, while his teammates Behra and Manzon were joined on the third row by Wharton in the sole Frazer-Nash.
</p><p>Hawthorn fought valiantly with the Ferraris for five laps before they resumed their usual formation. Ascari led Farina and Villoresi home in another Ferrari procession, with Hawthorn gaining fourth place, two laps behind the Ferrari trio. This was Ascari's fifth consecutive victory (along with a fifth consecutive fastest lap), and his seventh victory in total, breaking <a href="/wiki/Juan_Manuel_Fangio" title="Juan Manuel Fangio">Fangio</a>'s record for the most World Championship race wins. The Gordinis of Manzon and Trintignant finished a further lap behind Hawthorn, taking fifth and sixth place, respectively. Stirling Moss got up as high as seventh in the ERA before having to retire.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Farina's podium finish took him to second place in the Drivers' Championship standings, overtaking the absentee Taruffi. Swiss driver <a href="/wiki/Rudi_Fischer" title="Rudi Fischer">Rudi Fischer</a>, also not present at the Dutch Grand Prix, remained in fourth, while Mike Hawthorn's result took him to fifth in the standings, level on points with Fischer.
