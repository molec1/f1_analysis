<p>The <b>1962 British Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held at <a href="/wiki/Aintree_Motor_Racing_Circuit" title="Aintree Motor Racing Circuit">Aintree</a> on 21 July 1962. It was race 5 of 9 in both the <a href="/wiki/1962_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1962 World Championship of Drivers">1962 World Championship of Drivers</a> and the <a href="/wiki/1962_International_Cup_for_Formula_One_Manufacturers" class="mw-redirect" title="1962 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers">1962 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers</a>. This was the last race at Aintree. From <a href="/wiki/1963_British_Grand_Prix" title="1963 British Grand Prix">1963</a> onwards, the race would be held at <a href="/wiki/Silverstone_Circuit" title="Silverstone Circuit">Silverstone</a>. Scotsman <a href="/wiki/Jim_Clark" title="Jim Clark">Jim Clark</a> dominated the race, driving a <a href="/wiki/Lotus_25" title="Lotus 25">Lotus 25</a>. It was considered a power track,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (March 2017)">clarification needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> benefitting the light and powerful Lotus and Lola cars in particular. <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> were still sidelined due to the Italian metal workers' strike but managed to send one car for <a href="/wiki/Phil_Hill" title="Phil Hill">Phil Hill</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-IMS962_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IMS962-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1962_British_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>It was not a particularly exciting race, with Clark leading from start to finish and fairly large gaps between the cars.<sup id="cite_ref-IMS962_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IMS962-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Out of the twentyone starters, six still had four-cylinder engines while the rest were multi-cylinder cars. These cars were in a sort of class of their own, with <a href="/wiki/Jackie_Lewis" title="Jackie Lewis">Jackie Lewis</a> finishing best of the four-cylinder cars.<sup id="cite_ref-IMS32_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IMS32-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/John_Surtees" title="John Surtees">John Surtees</a>' Lola started and finished in second place, in spite of having lost second gear on the tenth lap. <a href="/wiki/Dan_Gurney" title="Dan Gurney">Dan Gurney</a> was hopeful after winning in <a href="/wiki/1962_French_Grand_Prix" title="1962 French Grand Prix">France</a> and at the non-championship <a href="/wiki/1962_Solitude_Grand_Prix" title="1962 Solitude Grand Prix">Solitude race</a> in the preceding two weeks. He began the race in third but had a slipping clutch and slid steadily down the field, ending in ninth position.<sup id="cite_ref-IMS18_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IMS18-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> His teammate <a href="/wiki/Jo_Bonnier" title="Jo Bonnier">Jo Bonnier</a> had to retire with a broken transmission. <a href="/wiki/Bruce_McLaren" title="Bruce McLaren">Bruce McLaren</a> (Cooper) passed Gurney after twelve laps and finished in third.<sup id="cite_ref-IMS18_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IMS18-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Graham_Hill" title="Graham Hill">Graham Hill</a> pushed his BRM hard but had to settle for fourth, and was threatened by <a href="/wiki/Jack_Brabham" title="Jack Brabham">Jack Brabham</a> in the closing stages. Brabham, however, had burns on his right foot since lap 40 and was in a lot of pain. He finished fifth, ahead of <a href="/wiki/Tony_Maggs" title="Tony Maggs">Tony Maggs</a>' Cooper.<sup id="cite_ref-IMS962_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IMS962-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Brabham had hoped to start in his own car after his mechanics had worked days and nights, but at 3 am the morning before the race it was discovered that they had been supplied with the wrong exhaust system. The <a href="/wiki/Brabham_BT3" title="Brabham BT3">Brabham BT3</a> had to wait until <a href="/wiki/1962_German_Grand_Prix" title="1962 German Grand Prix">Nürburgring</a> two weeks later to make its first appearance.<sup id="cite_ref-IMS33_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IMS33-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Innes_Ireland" title="Innes Ireland">Innes Ireland</a> had perhaps the day's biggest disappointment. After having qualified his <a href="/wiki/Coventry_Climax" title="Coventry Climax">Climax</a>-engined <a href="/wiki/British_Racing_Partnership" title="British Racing Partnership">UDT/Laystall</a> <a href="/wiki/Lotus_24" title="Lotus 24">Lotus 24</a> on the first row, the gear shifter bent on his first lap, leaving him with only three gears. He had to pit immediately after start and finished last, after having spent several laps repairing the car.<sup id="cite_ref-IMS962_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IMS962-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Trevor_Taylor_(racing_driver)" title="Trevor Taylor (racing driver)">Trevor Taylor</a> was anxious to have a good race after several recent collisions and started well. Soon, however, he had to pit with a loose carburettor, and only managed an eighth place in his <a href="/wiki/Lotus_24" title="Lotus 24">Lotus 24</a>. Phil Hill's Ferrari, meanwhile, was totally outclassed by the British cars in spite of its new six-speed gearbox. He never ran higher than tenth and had to retire with ignition troubles.<sup id="cite_ref-IMS962_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IMS962-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
