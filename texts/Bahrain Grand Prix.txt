<p>The <b>Bahrain Grand Prix</b> (<a href="/wiki/Arabic_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Arabic language">Arabic</a>: <span lang="ar" dir="rtl">جائزة البحرين الكبرى</span>&#8206;, currently officially known as the <b>Gulf Air Bahrain Grand Prix</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> Championship race in <a href="/wiki/Bahrain" title="Bahrain">Bahrain</a> currently sponsored by <a href="/wiki/Gulf_Air" title="Gulf Air">Gulf Air</a>. The first race took place at the <a href="/wiki/Bahrain_International_Circuit" title="Bahrain International Circuit">Bahrain International Circuit</a> on 4 April 2004. It made history as the first Formula One Grand Prix to be held in the <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a>, and was given the award for the "Best Organised Grand Prix" by the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">FIA</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-BNA501343_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BNA501343-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The race has usually been the third race of the Formula One calendar. However, in the <a href="/wiki/2006_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2006 Formula One World Championship">2006</a> season, Bahrain swapped places with the traditional opener, the <a href="/wiki/Australian_Grand_Prix" title="Australian Grand Prix">Australian Grand Prix</a>, which was pushed back to avoid a clash with the <a href="/wiki/2006_Commonwealth_Games" title="2006 Commonwealth Games">Commonwealth Games</a>. In 2010, Bahrain staged the opening race of the <a href="/wiki/2010_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2010 Formula One World Championship">2010</a> season and the cars drove the full 6.299&#160;km (3.914&#160;mi) "Endurance Circuit" to celebrate F1's 'diamond jubilee'.
</p><p>The 2011 Grand Prix, due to be held on 13 March, was cancelled on 21 February due to the <a href="/wiki/Bahraini_protests_of_2011" class="mw-redirect" title="Bahraini protests of 2011">2011 Bahraini protests</a><sup id="cite_ref-Bahrain_Grand_Prix_called_off_due_to_protests_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bahrain_Grand_Prix_called_off_due_to_protests-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> after drivers including <a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a> and <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a> had protested.<sup id="cite_ref-online.wsj.com_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-online.wsj.com-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Human rights activists called for a cancellation of <a href="/wiki/2012_Bahrain_Grand_Prix" title="2012 Bahrain Grand Prix">2012 race</a> due to reports of human rights abuses committed by the Bahraini authorities.<sup id="cite_ref-Guardianjan/30/bahrain-grand-prix_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Guardianjan/30/bahrain-grand-prix-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Team personnel also voiced concerns about safety,<sup id="cite_ref-Guardian2012/apr/09/formula-one-bahrain-grand-prix_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Guardian2012/apr/09/formula-one-bahrain-grand-prix-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> but the race, nonetheless, was held as planned on 22 April 2012.
</p><p>In 2014, to commemorate the tenth anniversary of the first staging of the Bahrain Grand Prix, the race was held as a night event under floodlights.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> In so doing it became the second Formula One night race after the <a href="/wiki/Singapore_Grand_Prix" title="Singapore Grand Prix">Singapore Grand Prix</a> in 2008. Bahrain's inaugural night event was won by <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>. Subsequent races have also been night races.
</p><p>Shortly after the Formula One February 2014 testing, the first corner of the track was renamed after seven-time champion German driver <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> in honour of his achievements and also in support after he suffered an almost fatal skiing accident late December 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bahrain_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Bahrain_International_Circuit_back_straight.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f0/Bahrain_International_Circuit_back_straight.jpg/220px-Bahrain_International_Circuit_back_straight.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="171" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f0/Bahrain_International_Circuit_back_straight.jpg/330px-Bahrain_International_Circuit_back_straight.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f0/Bahrain_International_Circuit_back_straight.jpg/440px-Bahrain_International_Circuit_back_straight.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3596" data-file-height="2793" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Bahrain_International_Circuit_back_straight.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The Bahrain International Circuit in 2010</div></div></div>
<p>The building of the <a href="/wiki/Bahrain_International_Circuit" title="Bahrain International Circuit">Bahrain International Circuit</a> in Sakhir was started in 2002 with high domestic interest about the project as it gave a future to the next generation of Bahraini racers. Bahrain had fought off fierce competition from elsewhere in the region to stage a F1 race, with <a href="/wiki/Egypt" title="Egypt">Egypt</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lebanon" title="Lebanon">Lebanon</a> and the <a href="/wiki/United_Arab_Emirates" title="United Arab Emirates">United Arab Emirates</a> all hoping for the prestige of hosting a Formula One Grand Prix. By the completion of the project, the circuit became the centre of motor sport in the <a href="/wiki/Persian_Gulf" title="Persian Gulf">Persian Gulf</a>, as it held many other races such as drag races, GT races, Formula 3 races and the Australian V8 Supercar series.<sup id="cite_ref-Pictures_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pictures-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The first race was in <a href="/wiki/2004_Bahrain_Grand_Prix" title="2004 Bahrain Grand Prix">2004</a>. The race was won by German driver <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a> for Ferrari. <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> won the second Bahrain Grand Prix for Renault in <a href="/wiki/2005_Bahrain_Grand_Prix" title="2005 Bahrain Grand Prix">2005</a>, and then became the first repeat winner of the Middle Eastern race in <a href="/wiki/2006_Bahrain_Grand_Prix" title="2006 Bahrain Grand Prix">2006</a> (again for Renault), after a thrilling race-long battle with Michael Schumacher.
In <a href="/wiki/2007_Bahrain_Grand_Prix" title="2007 Bahrain Grand Prix">2007</a> and <a href="/wiki/2008_Bahrain_Grand_Prix" title="2008 Bahrain Grand Prix">2008</a>, Brazilian <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> won the race for <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>. <a href="/wiki/2009_Bahrain_Grand_Prix" title="2009 Bahrain Grand Prix">2009</a> saw <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> win for <a href="/wiki/Brawn_GP" title="Brawn GP">Brawn GP</a>. After his <a href="/wiki/2010_Bahrain_Grand_Prix" title="2010 Bahrain Grand Prix">2010</a> triumph, Alonso became the first three-time winner.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA2012_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA2012-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The 2010 race saw a new circuit configuration being used for the Grand Prix. It used the "Endurance Circuit" layout, extending the lap length to 6.299&#160;km (3.914&#160;mi).<sup id="cite_ref-2010_layout_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2010_layout-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The new track turns left shortly after Turn 4, the right-hander at the top of the hill following the first sequence of turns. There is then a sequence of five turns before the cars head back to the original circuit. Then comes a left-right kink before a tight hairpin returns the cars onto the main track.<sup id="cite_ref-2010_layout_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2010_layout-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The track would have reverted to its original layout for the <a href="/wiki/2011_Bahrain_Grand_Prix" title="2011 Bahrain Grand Prix">2011</a> race,<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> and did so for the <a href="/wiki/2012_Bahrain_Grand_Prix" title="2012 Bahrain Grand Prix">2012</a> race.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA2012_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA2012-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2011_cancellation">2011 cancellation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bahrain_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 2011 cancellation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/2011_Bahrain_Grand_Prix" title="2011 Bahrain Grand Prix">2011 Bahrain Grand Prix</a></div>
<p>On 21 February 2011, it was announced that the <a href="/wiki/2011_Bahrain_Grand_Prix" title="2011 Bahrain Grand Prix">2011 Bahrain Grand Prix</a> scheduled for 13 March was cancelled due to the <a href="/wiki/Bahraini_protests_of_2011" class="mw-redirect" title="Bahraini protests of 2011">2011 Bahraini protests</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Bahrain_Grand_Prix_called_off_due_to_protests_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bahrain_Grand_Prix_called_off_due_to_protests-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> On 3 June, FIA decided to reschedule the race for 30 October. World champion racer <a href="/wiki/Damon_Hill" title="Damon Hill">Damon Hill</a> called on Formula One not to reschedule saying that if the race went ahead "we will forever have the blight of association with repressive methods to achieve order".<sup id="cite_ref-Cary_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cary-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> And <a href="/wiki/Bernie_Ecclestone" title="Bernie Ecclestone">Bernie Ecclestone</a> told the BBC in an interview: "Hopefully there'll be peace and quiet and we can return in the future, but of course it's not on. The schedule cannot be rescheduled without the agreement of the participants – they're the facts."<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> A week after its decision to reschedule the race, Formula One announced the cancellation of the race for 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-online.wsj.com_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-online.wsj.com-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2012_controversy">2012 controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bahrain_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2012 controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/2012_Bahrain_Grand_Prix" title="2012 Bahrain Grand Prix">2012 Bahrain Grand Prix</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:242px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Bahrain_uprising_graffiti_in_Barbar_(12).JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/Bahrain_uprising_graffiti_in_Barbar_%2812%29.JPG/240px-Bahrain_uprising_graffiti_in_Barbar_%2812%29.JPG" decoding="async" width="240" height="100" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/Bahrain_uprising_graffiti_in_Barbar_%2812%29.JPG/360px-Bahrain_uprising_graffiti_in_Barbar_%2812%29.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/Bahrain_uprising_graffiti_in_Barbar_%2812%29.JPG/480px-Bahrain_uprising_graffiti_in_Barbar_%2812%29.JPG 2x" data-file-width="3734" data-file-height="1552" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Bahrain_uprising_graffiti_in_Barbar_(12).JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A graffiti in Bahrain village</div></div></div>
<p>Human rights activists called for a cancellation of the <a href="/wiki/2012_Bahrain_Grand_Prix" title="2012 Bahrain Grand Prix">2012 Bahrain Grand Prix</a>, which took place on 22 April, because of reports of ongoing use of excessive force by authorities and torture in detention.<sup id="cite_ref-Guardianjan/30/bahrain-grand-prix_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Guardianjan/30/bahrain-grand-prix-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> That includes the killing of activist Salah Abbas Habib during a demonstration on the eve of the Grand Prix,<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> as well as the earlier fatal shooting of photojournalist Ahmed Ismael Hassan al-Samadi, who was covering a protest against the Bahrain Grand Prix.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 9 April 2012, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">The Guardian</a></i> reported that according to an unnamed leading member of one of the teams who said his views were representative, "the Formula One teams want the sport's governing body to cancel – or at least postpone – the Bahrain Grand Prix ..., because of increasing safety concerns amid ongoing protests in the kingdom ... I feel very uncomfortable about going to Bahrain. If I'm brutally frank, the only way they can pull this race off without incident is to have a complete military lockdown there. And I think that would be unacceptable, both for F1 and for Bahrain. But I don't see any other way they can do it".<sup id="cite_ref-Guardian2012/apr/09/formula-one-bahrain-grand-prix_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Guardian2012/apr/09/formula-one-bahrain-grand-prix-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In that context, <a href="/wiki/Anonymous_(group)" title="Anonymous (group)">Anonymous</a> launched on 21 April 2012 the operation opBahrain, threatening the Formula 1 representatives of a <a href="/wiki/Cyberattack" title="Cyberattack">cyberattack</a> in case they go on with Bahrain Grand Prix. Hours later, Anonymous hackers took down the f1-racers.net website after launching a <a href="/wiki/Distributed_denial-of-service" class="mw-redirect" title="Distributed denial-of-service">distributed denial-of-service</a> attack on it.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The 2012 Grand Prix reverted to using the 15-corner Grand Prix Circuit configuration last used in 2009, instead of the Endurance Circuit configuration used in 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-FIA2012_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FIA2012-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Continuing_controversy">Continuing controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bahrain_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Continuing controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Since the global media attention over the large scale demonstrations in 2011 and 2012, there have been continual reports from human rights groups about abuses and jailings in Bahrain relating to F1 protests.  Among them are photographer Ahmed Humaidan, who was one of about 30 people jailed for roles in the 2012 protest,<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> and activist Najah Ahmed Yousif, who is in prison, and has been physically and sexually abused, for criticising the Bahrain F1 on social media.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Rights organisations continue to criticise the <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_Group" title="Formula One Group">Formula One Group</a> and <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile</a> (FIA) for refusing to follow their own Statement of Commitment to Respect for Human Rights, saying that by not leveraging their position of power to take action against such political crackdown, the F1 organisers are complicit to the dissidents' suffering.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> In 2018, F1 "admitted concern" for Yousif, after continued public and media pressure, however there has been no known follow up since.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2020_postponement">2020 postponement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bahrain_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2020 postponement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/2020_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2020 Formula One World Championship">2020 Formula One World Championship</a></div>
<p>Responding to the <a href="/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic" title="COVID-19 pandemic">COVID-19 pandemic</a>, event organisers initially announced that no spectators would be permitted to attend the race.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> On 13 March the race was indefinitely postponed.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> The race is due to be held on 29 November. It will be followed up by the Sakhir Grand Prix, which will use the Outer Circuit configuration of the <a href="/wiki/Bahrain_International_Circuit" title="Bahrain International Circuit">Bahrain International Circuit</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Characteristics">Characteristics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bahrain_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Characteristics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A characteristic of the course is the large run-off areas, which have been criticised for not punishing drivers who stray off the track. However, they tend to prevent sand getting onto the track and the circuit is regarded as one of the safest in the world.<sup id="cite_ref-Pictures_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pictures-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Although alcoholic beverages are legal in Bahrain, the drivers do not spray the traditional <a href="/wiki/Champagne_(wine)" class="mw-redirect" title="Champagne (wine)">champagne</a> on the podium. Instead, they spray a non-alcoholic <a href="/wiki/Rosewater" class="mw-redirect" title="Rosewater">rosewater</a> drink known as Waard.<sup id="cite_ref-Pictures_8-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pictures-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p><a href="/wiki/Formula_BMW_Asia" class="mw-redirect" title="Formula BMW Asia">Formula BMW Asia</a> supported the Bahrain Grand Prix in 2004, with Hong Kong driver <a href="/wiki/Marchy_Lee" title="Marchy Lee">Marchy Lee</a> winning both rounds.  But the series had logistical problems after the race, when the cars were delayed on their way to <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">Malaysia</a>, missing the next event and had to reschedule the rest of the season.  <a href="/wiki/Formula_BMW_Asia" class="mw-redirect" title="Formula BMW Asia">Formula BMW Asia</a> has not supported the Bahrain Grand Prix since, but the first ever Formula BMW World Final was held in Bahrain. The Porsche Supercup has supported the 2005, 2006, 2007 and 2008 races. The GP2 Series supported the race in 2007 and a celebrity race was held in 2006 with the likes of <a href="/wiki/Simon_Webbe" title="Simon Webbe">Simon Webbe</a> competing.
</p><p>Further support for the 2008 event was provided by the <a href="/wiki/GP2_Asia" class="mw-redirect" title="GP2 Asia">GP2 Asia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Speedcar_Series" title="Speedcar Series">Speedcar Series</a> and a series using Australian built <a href="/wiki/Chevrolet_Lumina" title="Chevrolet Lumina">Chevrolet Luminas</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Visa_waiver">Visa waiver</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Bahrain_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Visa waiver">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Gulf_Cooperation_Council" title="Gulf Cooperation Council">Gulf Cooperation Council</a> citizens and residents, and nationals of 66 countries are eligible for visa on arrival in Bahrain throughout the year. 113 nationalities, including those eligible for visa on arrival, can apply for visas through an online application process.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> For the Grand Prix, Bahraini authorities issue a special F1 visa which allows for multiple entries in a defined two-week period around the event. The visa is available to Grand Prix ticketholders and is free of charge.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
