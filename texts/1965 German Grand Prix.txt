<p>The <b>1965 German Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>XXVII Großer Preis von Deutschland</b>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> <a href="/wiki/Motor_race" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor race">motor race</a> held at <a href="/wiki/N%C3%BCrburgring" title="Nürburgring">Nürburgring</a> on August 1, 1965. It was race 7 of 10 in both the <a href="/wiki/1965_World_Championship_of_Drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="1965 World Championship of Drivers">1965 World Championship of Drivers</a> and the <a href="/wiki/1965_International_Cup_for_Formula_One_Manufacturers" class="mw-redirect" title="1965 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers">1965 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers</a>. The 15-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Jim_Clark" title="Jim Clark">Jim Clark</a>, who in his <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Lotus</a>-<a href="/wiki/Coventry_Climax" title="Coventry Climax">Climax</a>, took pole position, the fastest lap of the race, and led every lap. The victory ensured that Clark won the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Drivers%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Drivers&#39; Champions">World Championship of Drivers</a> with three races left to go. It also meant that Lotus won the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Formula_One_World_Constructors%27_Champions" title="List of Formula One World Constructors&#39; Champions">1965 International Cup for Formula One Manufacturers</a> at the same time. <a href="/wiki/British_Racing_Motors" title="British Racing Motors">BRM</a> driver, <a href="/wiki/Graham_Hill" title="Graham Hill">Graham Hill</a>, finished the race in second position in front of <a href="/wiki/Brabham" title="Brabham">Brabham</a>-<a href="/wiki/Coventry_Climax" title="Coventry Climax">Climax</a> driver, <a href="/wiki/Dan_Gurney" title="Dan Gurney">Dan Gurney</a>, who completed the podium by finishing third.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_report">Race report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1965_German_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Race report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Graham Hill could still theoretically overhaul Clark for the championship. However Clark became Champion with a masterful performance, leading from pole to the flag and setting fastest lap, to gain maximum points with 3 Grand Prix still to be run. Behind him, the rest of the pack had all sorts of mechanical problems-Surtees had gear selection problems, Stewart bent a wishbone, Hulme punctured his fuel tank when his seat worked loose and Amon had transistor problems despite borrowing two transistor boxes. Clark was duly crowned as champion at the start of August, the earliest the championship had been won until 2002, when Michael Schumacher obtained his 5th title on July 21.
</p>
