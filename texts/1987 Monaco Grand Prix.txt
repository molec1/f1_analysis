<p>The <b>1987 Monaco Grand Prix</b> (formally the <b>45e Grand Prix de Monaco</b><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>) was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 31 May 1987 at the <a href="/wiki/Circuit_de_Monaco" title="Circuit de Monaco">Circuit de Monaco</a>, <a href="/wiki/Monte_Carlo" title="Monte Carlo">Monte Carlo</a>. It was the fourth race of the <a href="/wiki/1987_FIA_Formula_One_World_Championship" class="mw-redirect" title="1987 FIA Formula One World Championship">1987 FIA Formula One World Championship</a>.
</p><p>The 78-lap race was won by <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a>, driving a <a href="/wiki/Lotus_99T" title="Lotus 99T">Lotus</a>-<a href="/wiki/Honda_in_Formula_One" title="Honda in Formula One">Honda</a>. It was the first of an eventual six wins for the Brazilian driver at Monaco. Compatriot <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a> was second in a <a href="/wiki/Williams_FW11" title="Williams FW11">Williams</a>-Honda, with Italian <a href="/wiki/Michele_Alboreto" title="Michele Alboreto">Michele Alboreto</a> third in a <a href="/wiki/Ferrari_F1/87" title="Ferrari F1/87">Ferrari</a>.
</p><p>The win promoted Senna to second in the Drivers' Championship, three points behind <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> driver <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Race_summary">Race summary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1987_Monaco_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Race summary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Traditionally the number of competitors permitted for the Monaco Grand Prix was lower than at all other races, due to the tight and twisty nature of the Monte Carlo circuit. Originally 16, it was later increased to 20. For 1987, however, it was increased to a full grid of 26. According to <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_du_Sport_Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale du Sport Automobile">FISA</a>, this move was made in order to bring the race into line with the other races on the F1 calendar, but there were cynical views that it was made in order to reduce the number of non-qualifiers to appease team sponsors. There was widespread concern about the results of overcrowding on the track and the speed difference of various cars.
</p><p>During the practice session, <a href="/wiki/Michele_Alboreto" title="Michele Alboreto">Michele Alboreto</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> tangled with <a href="/wiki/Christian_Danner" title="Christian Danner">Christian Danner</a>'s slow-moving <a href="/wiki/Zakspeed" title="Zakspeed">Zakspeed</a> on the uphill section after the Ste-Devote corner. Alboreto's car was thrown in the air and caught fire, but landed back on the track. FISA blamed Danner for the accident and took the decision to disqualify him from the weekend, the first such event in the history of the Formula One World Championship. There were widespread objections throughout the paddock, particularly as there were several other practice accidents and it was felt that Danner had no more to blame than any other driver involved in these accidents.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Alboreto himself believed that Danner was not to blame for the accident.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Nigel_Mansell" title="Nigel Mansell">Nigel Mansell</a> took <a href="/wiki/Pole_position" title="Pole position">pole position</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Williams_Grand_Prix_Engineering" title="Williams Grand Prix Engineering">Williams</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Ayrton_Senna" title="Ayrton Senna">Ayrton Senna</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Team_Lotus" title="Team Lotus">Lotus</a> alongside on the front row and <a href="/wiki/Nelson_Piquet" title="Nelson Piquet">Nelson Piquet</a> third in the other Williams. At the start, Mansell led away from Senna, Piquet, Alboreto and <a href="/wiki/Alain_Prost" title="Alain Prost">Alain Prost</a> in the <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>. Mansell led until lap 30 when he retired with a loss of turbo boost; Senna then led for the remainder of the race; despite making a pit stop for tyres.
</p><p>Senna eventually won by 33 seconds from Piquet. Prost was running third when his engine failed with three laps to go, promoting Alboreto to the final podium position. <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Berger" title="Gerhard Berger">Gerhard Berger</a> finished fourth in the other Ferrari, with the top six completed by the first two <a href="/wiki/Naturally_aspirated_engine" title="Naturally aspirated engine">naturally-aspirated</a> finishers, <a href="/wiki/Jonathan_Palmer" title="Jonathan Palmer">Jonathan Palmer</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Tyrrell_Racing" title="Tyrrell Racing">Tyrrell</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ivan_Capelli" title="Ivan Capelli">Ivan Capelli</a> in the <a href="/wiki/March_Engineering" title="March Engineering">March</a>.
</p><p>Senna's victory was the first for a car with <a href="/wiki/Active_suspension" title="Active suspension">active suspension</a>.
</p>
