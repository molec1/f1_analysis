<p>The <b>2007 Italian Grand Prix</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> motor race held on 9 September 2007 at <a href="/wiki/Autodromo_Nazionale_di_Monza" title="Autodromo Nazionale di Monza">Autodromo Nazionale di Monza</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a>. It was the thirteenth race of the <a href="/wiki/2007_Formula_One_season" class="mw-redirect" title="2007 Formula One season">2007 Formula One season</a>.
</p><p>Going into the race, <a href="/wiki/McLaren_(racing)" class="mw-redirect" title="McLaren (racing)">McLaren</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> held a 5-point lead over team-mate <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> in the Drivers' Championship, whilst McLaren held an 11-point lead over <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> in the Constructors' Championship. Ferrari were expected to do well at home after a one-two finish in the <a href="/wiki/2007_Turkish_Grand_Prix" title="2007 Turkish Grand Prix">Turkish GP</a> and <a href="/wiki/Michael_Schumacher" title="Michael Schumacher">Michael Schumacher</a>'s victory at Monza <a href="/wiki/2006_Italian_Grand_Prix" title="2006 Italian Grand Prix">the previous year</a>.  However, McLaren dominated the traditional test session prior to the Grand Prix, with Alonso topping the time sheets on two of the four days and Hamilton and <a href="/wiki/Pedro_de_la_Rosa" title="Pedro de la Rosa">Pedro de la Rosa</a> fastest on the other days.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> took his  fourth win of the season, leading home the fourth one-two finish of  the season for <a href="/wiki/McLaren_(racing)" class="mw-redirect" title="McLaren (racing)">McLaren</a> as <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> finished second. It was <a href="/wiki/McLaren_(racing)" class="mw-redirect" title="McLaren (racing)">McLaren</a>'s eighth victory at <a href="/wiki/Autodromo_Nazionale_di_Monza" title="Autodromo Nazionale di Monza">Monza</a>  and <a href="/wiki/Mercedes-Benz_HighPerformanceEngines" class="mw-redirect" title="Mercedes-Benz HighPerformanceEngines">Mercedes</a>'  fourth, while Alonso  became the first <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spaniard</a>  to win the <a href="/wiki/Italian_Grand_Prix" title="Italian Grand Prix">Italian Grand Prix</a>. It turned out to be his last win for McLaren, and McLaren's last 1-2 finish until the <a href="/wiki/2010_Chinese_Grand_Prix" title="2010 Chinese Grand Prix">2010 Chinese Grand Prix</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Report">Report</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2007_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Report">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Qualifying">Qualifying</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2007_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Qualifying">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The two <a href="/wiki/Spyker_F1" title="Spyker F1">Spykers</a> of <a href="/wiki/Adrian_Sutil" title="Adrian Sutil">Adrian Sutil</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sakon_Yamamoto" title="Sakon Yamamoto">Sakon Yamamoto</a> were both eliminated in Q1, along with <a href="/wiki/Vitantonio_Liuzzi" title="Vitantonio Liuzzi">Vitantonio Liuzzi</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Toro_Rosso" title="Scuderia Toro Rosso">Toro Rosso</a>, <a href="/wiki/Takuma_Sato" title="Takuma Sato">Takuma Sato</a> of <a href="/wiki/Super_Aguri_F1" title="Super Aguri F1">Super Aguri</a>, and, surprisingly, the <a href="/wiki/Toyota_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Toyota F1">Toyota</a> of <a href="/wiki/Ralf_Schumacher" title="Ralf Schumacher">Ralf Schumacher</a> and <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a>.
</p><p>In the second session, both the Toro Rosso of <a href="/wiki/Sebastian_Vettel" title="Sebastian Vettel">Sebastian Vettel</a> and <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Davidson" title="Anthony Davidson">Anthony Davidson</a>'s Super Aguri, who had done very well to make the session, were eliminated. So were <a href="/wiki/Giancarlo_Fisichella" title="Giancarlo Fisichella">Giancarlo Fisichella</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Wurz" title="Alexander Wurz">Alexander Wurz</a> of <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Honda_Racing_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Honda Racing F1">Honda</a> of <a href="/wiki/Rubens_Barrichello" title="Rubens Barrichello">Rubens Barrichello</a> and Coulthard's Red Bull teammate <a href="/wiki/Mark_Webber" title="Mark Webber">Mark Webber</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> had done excellently to put the Honda in the top 10, and he qualified tenth, on the fifth row with <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a>'s Toyota. <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> qualified his Williams eighth, alongside the second Renault of <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a>, who had put in a brilliant performance. <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a> took 6th in the <a href="/wiki/BMW_Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="BMW Sauber">BMW Sauber</a>, while his teammate <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> broke the domination of the top 4 by <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> and <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a>, beating the Scuderia's <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a> to 4th. However, it was service as normal at the very front, with Räikkönen's teammate <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a> taking 3rd, and the two McLarens of <a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> locking out the front row, with the Spaniard just pipping his younger teammate to pole by 0.037 of a second.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Race">Race</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2007_Italian_Grand_Prix&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Race">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The top three held station from the start, but <a href="/wiki/Kimi_R%C3%A4ikk%C3%B6nen" title="Kimi Räikkönen">Kimi Räikkönen</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> jumped <a href="/wiki/Nick_Heidfeld" title="Nick Heidfeld">Nick Heidfeld</a> in the <a href="/wiki/BMW_Sauber" class="mw-redirect" title="BMW Sauber">BMW Sauber</a> and moved into fourth. <a href="/wiki/Jarno_Trulli" title="Jarno Trulli">Jarno Trulli</a> was a big loser in the <a href="/wiki/Toyota_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Toyota F1">Toyota</a>, slipping from 9th to 13th, while the big winner was <a href="/wiki/David_Coulthard" title="David Coulthard">David Coulthard</a>, who went from 20th to 15th in the <a href="/wiki/Red_Bull_Racing" title="Red Bull Racing">Red Bull</a>.
</p><p>On lap 2, however, Coulthard crashed at Curva Grande, completely negating the influence of his brilliant start and with his car in a dangerous position the crash forced the safety car to be deployed. This allowed the <a href="/wiki/Super_Aguri_F1" title="Super Aguri F1">Super Aguri</a> of <a href="/wiki/Anthony_Davidson" title="Anthony Davidson">Anthony Davidson</a> up into 15th.
</p><p>The race's only other retirement was Räikkönen's teammate <a href="/wiki/Felipe_Massa" title="Felipe Massa">Felipe Massa</a>, who retired with a suspension failure on lap 11 after running in third place. This allowed the Finn to move into third and <a href="/wiki/Nico_Rosberg" title="Nico Rosberg">Nico Rosberg</a> to move into the points in his <a href="/wiki/WilliamsF1" class="mw-redirect" title="WilliamsF1">Williams</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Lewis_Hamilton" title="Lewis Hamilton">Lewis Hamilton</a>, running second in the <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a>, suffered a puncture on lap 19, and dropped down to 6th behind Räikkönen, the two BMW Saubers of Heidfeld and <a href="/wiki/Robert_Kubica" title="Robert Kubica">Robert Kubica</a> and <a href="/wiki/Heikki_Kovalainen" title="Heikki Kovalainen">Heikki Kovalainen</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Renault_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Renault F1">Renault</a>. Räikkönen then took the lead as Hamilton's teammate <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Alonso" title="Fernando Alonso">Fernando Alonso</a> pitted from the lead on lap 21.
</p><p>A recovering Hamilton moved back to third place behind Räikkönen and Alonso after the two BMW Saubers and Kovalainen pitted, but as the Ferrari man emerged from his pitstop, Hamilton was still behind. He made a daring move down the inside of turn one to recover second place, more or less confirming a McLaren 1-2. Alonso retained the lead.
</p><p>There were no further changes at the front, and Alonso led home by six seconds from Hamilton, with Räikkönen completing the podium. Heidfeld finished in fourth just four seconds ahead of his teammate Kubica, with Rosberg still in close contention in sixth. Kovalainen was seventh, and <a href="/wiki/Jenson_Button" title="Jenson Button">Jenson Button</a> rounded out the points in a rare foray into the points paying positions for the <a href="/wiki/Honda_Racing_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Honda Racing F1">Honda</a> team.
</p>
